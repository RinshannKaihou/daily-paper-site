> **一句话总结：** 蚂蚁集团 + 人民大学团队首次将“零 RL”（不带任何 SFT 数据，直接从预训练 base 模型启动 RLVR）扩展到 1 万亿参数 MoE 模型（Ling-2.5-1T-Base，激活参数 63B），通过裁剪重要性采样、训练-推理 logit 比率修正、混合精度控制等“极简”改造，在 AIME 2026 取得 84.2%（一阶段）/ 93.2%（二阶段 Yarn=2）的 pass@1，并自发涌现拟人化、结构化、自验证、并行推理与“上下文焦虑”等行为，在 CoT 可读性、可复现性、效率三个维度上击败 GLM-5.1、Kimi K2.6、MiniMax M2.7、Qwen3.5-397B 等强基线。

> **TL;DR:** A 16-author team from Ant Group, Renmin University of China, Tsinghua, and Zhejiang U applies "zero RL" (RLVR directly on a pretrained base model, no SFT) to a 1-trillion-parameter MoE base (Ling-2.5-1T-Base, 63B activated), using a minimalist four-stage pipeline (clipped importance sampling + training-inference ratio correction + mixed-precision FP32 at attention/LM-head + self-distillation + tier-based training). On seven math benchmarks, the Second-Stage RL variant reaches AIME 2024 / 2025 / 2026 = 94.1 / 92.3 / 93.2 (pass@1, 64-run avg), closing much of the gap to frontier models (GPT-5.5: 98.3, Gemini 3.1 Pro: 98.2, Qwen3.7-Plus: 97.0 on AIME 2026). Critically, the 1T model spontaneously develops structured formatting, self-verification, parallel reasoning, anthropomorphic traces, and "context anxiety" — behaviors that on the 104B sibling required hand-crafted reward shaping — empirically validating the bitter lesson: scale + simple methods beat engineered heuristics.

## 核心贡献

- **首次在 1 万亿参数规模验证 Zero RL**：将 RLVR（不依赖任何 SFT CoT 数据，直接从预训练 base 模型启动强化学习）成功扩展到 1T MoE（激活 63B），训练稳定超过数千步。关键启示是：1T 规模下保持稳定只需要少量“极简”改造，无需复杂算法工程。
- **提出四阶段渐进式训练流水线**：(1) 一阶段 RL（token-level loss + KL 罚 + 裁剪重要性比 + 训练-推理 logit 比率修正，扩大响应窗口 4k→64k）激励推理；(2) 自蒸馏压缩 CoT 并抹掉训练-推理引擎的累积数值误差；(3) 二阶段 RL（切换 sample-level loss、移除 KL）持续优化；(4) 三阶段 RL 引入 tier-based 训练（Low 4k / Medium 16k / High 64k）赋予模型自适应推理深度。
- **三维度 CoT 质量评估框架**：除最终答案正确率外，提出 Comprehensibility（LLM-as-Judge 配对胜率）、Reproducibility（蒸馏到弱模型后的下游增益）、Efficiency（正确解的平均 token 数），为推理过程本身提供诊断信号。
- **三条经验性“bitter lesson”发现**：(a) 规模决定能力上限——1T 在样本效率与性能天花板全面碾压 104B sibling；(b) RL 是“先 discovery 后 sharpening”的两阶段现象（pass@1024 早期增长后饱和，pass@1 持续上升）；(c) 高级认知策略在 1T 上自发涌现（结构化、自验证、并行推理、拟人化、上下文焦虑），使人工启发式（在 104B 上仍必须手工设计 reward 才能引导）变得多余。
- **基础设施优化**：FP32 仅作用于 attention softmax 与 LM head（其余 BF16）以消除指数放大误差；针对 MLA 层用 all-to-all CP、针对 Lightning Attention 用 AllGather CP 替代 ring attention，打破顺序通信瓶颈且数学等价。

## Key Contributions

- **First 1-trillion-parameter Zero-RL demonstration**: Successfully runs RLVR starting directly from a pretrained MoE base (Ling-2.5-1T-Base, 63B activated) with no supervised CoT data, and keeps training stable for thousands of steps. The headline engineering insight is that only a handful of "minimal" modifications (clipped importance ratio, training-inference logit ratio correction, targeted FP32 at attention/LM-head, KL anchor) are needed at 1T scale.
- **Four-stage progressive pipeline**: First-stage RL (token-level loss + KL + clipped IS + ratio correction, curriculum window 4k→64k) elicits reasoning; self-distillation compresses CoTs and resets accumulated train/inference numerical drift; second-stage RL (sample-level loss, no KL) sustains improvement; third-stage RL introduces tier-based (Low 4k / Medium 16k / High 64k) training for adaptive reasoning depth.
- **Three-axis CoT-quality evaluation framework**: Beyond final-answer accuracy, proposes Comprehensibility (LLM-as-judge pairwise win-rate), Reproducibility (downstream gain after distilling traces into a weaker student), and Efficiency (avg token count of correct traces).
- **Three empirical validations of the bitter lesson at scale**: (a) model capacity dictates both ceiling and sample efficiency — the 1T dominates the 104B sibling; (b) Zero RL is a sequential discovery→sharpening phenomenon (pass@1024 saturates early while pass@1 keeps climbing); (c) advanced cognitive behaviors (structured formatting, self-verification, parallel reasoning, anthropomorphism, "context anxiety") emerge spontaneously at 1T, making the hand-crafted reward shaping that was necessary at 104B redundant.
- **Infrastructure optimizations**: FP32 only at attention softmax and LM head (rest BF16) to kill exponentiation-amplified error; MLA layers use all-to-all CP and Lightning Attention layers use AllGather CP to replace sequential ring attention — mathematically equivalent, much lower latency.

## Methodology

**Base models.** Two pretrained MoE models with no SFT: Ling-2.5-1T-Base (1T total / 63B activated) and Ling-2.5-flash-Base (104B / 7.4B activated).

**Four-stage pipeline (the methodological core).**

1. **First-Stage RL (Reasoning Elicitation).** A clipped importance-sampling policy gradient (CISPO-style; MiniMax 2025) is used so that gradients still flow for every token instead of being zeroed out by PPO clipping — critical for amplifying the low-probability reasoning tokens that a base model almost never emits. To kill the train/inference logit mismatch between Megatron (training) and SGLang (rollout), the numerator of the importance ratio is replaced with the actual training-engine logits `πM_θ(o_{i,t} | q, o_{i,<t})`, while the denominator uses the inference-engine rollout policy `πS_{θ_old}` (Eq. 2–3). Only an upper clip `ε_high = 5.0` is applied (no lower bound, to keep exploration). A KL penalty to a reference model (`β = 1e-4`, K3 divergence, reference refreshed every 400 steps) and a **token-level loss** (sum over all tokens, not length-normalized) jointly force the model to produce long reasoning chains. The response window is expanded 4k → 8k → 16k → 32k → 64k every 800 steps.

2. **Self-Distillation (Compression & Stabilization).** Sample multiple rollouts per query from the first-stage expert, pick the shortest correct trace, then prompt the model to self-evaluate and remove redundant segments. Fine-tune the original base model on this compressed corpus (3 epochs, 64k seq len, lr 7e-5). This both shortens outputs and wipes out the accumulated train↔inference numerical drift before further RL.

3. **Second-Stage RL (Sustained Optimization).** Same clipped IS + ratio correction, but switches to **sample-level loss** (length-normalized; Eq. 7) to stop the unbounded length growth token-level loss induces, and **removes the KL penalty** (the distilled checkpoint is already a strong anchor).

4. **Third-Stage RL (Adaptive Reasoning Depth).** Partitions prompts into Low/Medium/High difficulty tiers, each with a different max token budget (4k/16k/64k) and system prompt `p_k`. Rollout is conditioned on `(p_k, q)`, teaching the model to allocate compute adaptively; at inference, users select the tier via system prompt.

**Infrastructure (§3.2).** Mixed-precision: model body in BF16, but attention softmax and LM head in FP32 — because the importance ratio exponentiates logits, so any rounding error there blows up the ratio. Context parallelism: MLA layers use all-to-all CP (low-rank latent → small comm volume); Lightning Attention layers use a single AllGather on the fixed-size KV state.

**Reward.** `r = r_acc + r_format`. `r_format` ∈ {0,1} requires closed `<think>…</think><answer>…</answer>` tags followed by EOS (Format B) — they show the looser Format A degenerates into infinite trailing garbage that inflates length without reward gain. `r_acc` is rule-based early and is replaced by an LLM-as-judge (Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Instruct) for harder later-stage problems whose answers need semantic comparison.

**Setup.** 320× H200 GPUs; Areal (Fu et al. 2025) orchestrates the Megatron/SGLang hybrid; Adam (β1=0.9, β2=0.999), constant lr 2e-6, weight decay 0.01, G=16 rollouts per question at temperature 1.0. Evaluation: pass@1 averaged over 64 runs at T=0.6, top-k=0.95, on AIME 2024/2025/2026, HMMT Feb 2025 / Nov 2025 / Feb 2026, and IMOAnswerBench.

## Results & Highlights

- **Headline benchmark (Table 1).** Ring-2.5-1T-Zero (Second-Stage RL, Yarn=2) reaches **AIME 2024 = 94.1%, AIME 2025 = 92.3%, AIME 2026 = 93.2%** pass@1 — vs. the 104B Ring-2.5-flash-Zero's 71.2 / 63.5 / 65.3 at the same first stage, i.e. the 1T model is **~18–28 points higher** purely from parameter scaling under an identical pipeline.
- **First-stage-only Zero RL is already strong.** Ring-2.5-1T-Zero after only First-Stage RL (no distillation, no second stage) hits **84.2% on AIME 2026 and 89.1% on AIME 2024**, demonstrating that advanced math reasoning can bootstrap directly from a base model with no SFT.
- **Multi-stage monotonic gains.** AIME 2026 evolves: First-Stage RL 84.2 → Self-Distillation 88.1 → Second-Stage RL 92.5 → Second-Stage RL (Yarn=2) 93.2. IMOAnswerBench climbs 59.3 → 63.8 → 72.7 → 75.5 across the same progression.
- **Still trails frontier models.** On AIME 2026, the best Ring variant (93.2) lags GPT-5.5 (98.3), Gemini 3.1 Pro (98.2), Qwen3.7-Plus (97.0), Kimi K2.6 (96.4), Claude Opus 4.8 (95.7), GLM-5.1 (95.3) — competitive but not SOTA.
- **Adaptive tiers trade off cost vs accuracy.** Third-Stage RL High (TT=128k, IT≈20.8k tokens) reaches AIME 2026 = 91.4%; Medium (TT=16k, IT≈8k) reaches 90.8% at <40% of the tokens; Low (TT=4k, IT≈2.3k) reaches 68.8%.
- **CoT Efficiency (Fig. 3c).** On the subset of AIME problems solved by all five models, Ring-2.5-1T-Zero averages **6,368 tokens** — less than half of GLM-5.1 (17,220), MiniMax M2.7 (16,627), Qwen3.5-397B (14,115), Kimi K2.6 (16,292).
- **CoT Reproducibility (Fig. 3b).** Distilling Ring-Zero traces into Qwen2.5-32B gives **78.4 vs. 72.6** for DeepSeek-R1 distillation (+5.8 points), and into Llama3.3-70B gives **74.5 vs. 70.0** (+4.5) — despite using only **100K samples vs. DeepSeek-R1's 800K**.
- **CoT Comprehensibility (Fig. 3a).** LLM-as-judge pairwise win-rates over GLM-5.1, Kimi K2.6, MiniMax M2.7, and Qwen3.5-397B on all 90 AIME 2024–2026 problems are dominant for Ring-Zero.
- **Ablation: ratio correction (Fig. 6).** The baseline (SGLang-only logits) collapses at ~800 steps; +IcePop survives to ~2700 then fails; the proposed Megatron-numerator/SGLang-denominator ratio trains stably to 3000+ steps.
- **Ablation: KL penalty (Fig. 5).** Without KL, the train/inference log-prob gap diverges, entropy collapses, and reward crashes; with KL, all metrics stay healthy.
- **Algorithm comparison (Fig. 4).** CISPO > DAPO > GRPO in both speed of reward growth and in instability; GSPO preserves entropy but barely grows sequence length and is ill-suited to bootstrap reasoning from scratch.
- **Length inertia (Fig. 10c).** Even for problems the model already solves perfectly on the first try, response length grows monotonically with training — confirming that token-level loss incentivizes a "lazy" verbosity shortcut.
- **Scale effect (Fig. 10a).** After 3600 steps, Ring-2.5-1T-Zero-I hits 89.06% / 83.28% on AIME 2024/2025; the 104B flash hits only 71.72% / 63.54% even after 5200 steps.

## Authors & Credibility

Sixteen authors from **Gaoling School of AI, Renmin University of China** (Wayne Xin Zhao's group, well-known for the "A Survey of Large Language Models"), **Ant Group** (Zhiqiang Zhang, Jun Zhou — Ant's research arm, which also released the Ling-2.5 / Ling series the base model comes from), **Tsinghua University**, and **Zhejiang University**. Corresponding authors are Wayne Xin Zhao (RUC) and Zhiqiang Zhang (Ant). Two equal-contribution first authors (Tang, Cao). The work builds directly on the authors' own prior Ling-2.5 release (Team and AI, 2025) and on the Areal RL framework (Fu et al. 2025), so they have hands-on access to the 1T base weights, the RL infra, and the compute (320× H200). Credibility on the engineering claims is high; credibility on the SOTA-comparison claims is tempered by the use of self-reported numbers for proprietary baselines and an LLM-as-judge whose own model (Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Instruct) overlaps the Qwen baselines.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Genuinely novel as the *first published* 1T-parameter Zero-RL study. The individual components (GRPO from DeepSeek-R1, clipped IS from MiniMax-M1/CISPO, token→sample-level loss switching, self-distillation) are not new; the contribution is the integration recipe at 1T plus the discovery-vs-sharpening analysis (pass@1024 vs pass@1) and the spontaneous-emergence observations. The "bitter lesson" framing is rhetorical but the 104B-needs-hand-crafted-reward vs 1T-doesn't comparison is a real finding.
- **Rigor**: Strong on ablations (RL algorithm comparison, KL on/off, ratio-correction variants, format reward, learning rate, rollout count, loss reduction, window size, length inertia, pass@1024 tracking). **However, every ablation is run only on the 104B flash model**, explicitly "due to computational resource constraints" — so the central claim that these fixes are what make 1T stable is supported by analogy, not by direct 1T ablation. The pass@1024 result is on AIME 2024 only.
- **Baselines**: Mixed. The headline math table omits DeepSeek-R1 / R1-Zero for every benchmark except AIME 2024 (77.9 / 79.8), so the most natural prior Zero-RL baseline is missing on 6 of 7 benchmarks. Frontier-model rows are missing for AIME 2024, AIME 2025, and HMMT Feb 2025, making head-to-head comparison partial. The CoT-quality comparisons use an LLM-as-judge (proprietary prompt in Appendix B) against GLM-5.1 / Kimi K2.6 / MiniMax M2.7 / Qwen3.5-397B — judge bias is plausible, especially because the reward function's own correctness judge is also a Qwen model.
- **Reproducibility**: Low to moderate. Base model (Ling-2.5-1T-Base) is from the authors' prior work and the training data is proprietary; the paper does not release weights, data, or code. Hyperparameters are listed in enough detail to reproduce *if* one has 320 H200s and the base weights. The tier-based "negative transfer" finding is acknowledged.
- **Significance**: High for the open community because it lowers the perceived engineering barrier to 1T Zero RL ("just CISPO + ratio correction + FP32-at-softmax/LM-head"). The discovery-vs-sharpening resolution and the five emergent behaviors are likely to be cited heavily regardless of the absolute scores.
- **Red flags / overclaims**:
  - "Competitive performance comparable to frontier models" is generous — on AIME 2026, 93.2 vs GPT-5.5's 98.3 is a 5-point gap, and the paper is behind on every benchmark where a frontier number is reported.
  - The Third-Stage RL "High" mode (91.4 on AIME 2026) **regresses** vs Second-Stage RL peak (93.2) — the adaptive-depth stage actually hurts the top-tier mode. The authors attribute this to "lack of ultra-long data" and "negative transfer," but this means their headline adaptive-depth contribution underperforms the simpler Second-Stage variant.
  - Reward correctness for hard problems is judged by Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Instruct, which is closely related to the Qwen3.5-397B baseline used in CoT-quality comparisons — a soft circularity.
  - "Spontaneously develops … anthropomorphism" (use of "brain fart," "shit," "wing it," "genius," "jk") is more accurately described as surfacing of internet-forum artifacts from pretraining rather than a cognitively meaningful emergent behavior; the paper half-acknowledges this ("corpus-induced artifacts") but still lists it as an "advanced cognitive strategy."

## Strengths

- First credible empirical map of Zero RL dynamics at 1T parameters; the discovery→sharpening analysis (pass@1024 vs pass@1) is a clean, falsifiable contribution to a live debate (Yue et al. vs. Liu et al.).
- Engineering recipe is *simple* and well-motivated: each fix is tied to a specific failure mode (logit mismatch → ratio correction; exponentiation error → FP32 at softmax/LM-head; length inertia → token→sample-level loss switch via self-distillation).
- CoT-quality framework (Comprehensibility / Reproducibility / Efficiency) is a useful, reusable lens that goes beyond accuracy — the efficiency number (6,368 tokens vs. 14k–17k for baselines) and the 8× sample-efficiency win in distillation (100K vs. 800K) are striking.
- Honest about several weaknesses (length inertia, negative transfer in tier training, long-tail data inefficiency, pretrained priors as a hard ceiling).
- Rich qualitative case studies of the five emergent behaviors make the paper readable and give other researchers concrete phenomena to look for.

## Limitations

- **No ablations on the 1T model itself** — all ablations are on the 104B flash, so it is unproven that the same fixes are necessary (or sufficient) at 1T.
- **Proprietary base model, data, and weights** — none of Ling-2.5-1T-Base, the curated RL corpus, or the final Ring-2.5-1T-Zero checkpoint is released. Independent reproduction is essentially impossible.
- **Behind frontier models on every comparable benchmark** (5.1 points behind GPT-5.5 on AIME 2026); the "competitive" framing oversells the result.
- **Third-Stage RL (tier-based adaptive depth) regresses** vs. the Second-Stage peak, undermining the paper's headline multi-stage contribution.
- **LLM-as-judge circularity**: reward uses a Qwen-family judge; CoT-quality baselines include Qwen3.5-397B. Judge bias is not controlled for.
- **Single domain (math)**: no code, science, or agentic benchmarks; the "bitter lesson" generalization is untested outside math reasoning.
- **Context window capped at 64k (training)**; the authors themselves flag this as a hard limit on further capability and call for longer-window training.
- **"Anthropomorphism" is overstated** — it is largely pretraining-corpus leakage (profanity, forum slang), not a learned cognitive strategy.
- **Compute footprint (320× H200)** makes the recipe inaccessible to most academic groups, somewhat undercutting the "lowered engineering barriers" conclusion.

## Relevance

对关注 LLM 推理、RLVR、大模型训练动力学的研究者高度相关：这是首篇把 Zero RL 推到 1T 规模的工作，给出的极简稳定化配方（裁剪重要性采样 + 训练-推理 logit 比率修正 + FP32-at-softmax/LM-head + 自蒸馏 + token→sample-level loss 切换）以及 discovery→sharpening 两阶段分析可直接迁移到自研万亿参数基座的推理后训练。对关心测试时算力分配（tier-based adaptive depth）与 CoT 质量评估（可读性 / 可蒸馏性 / 效率）的读者也有方法学参考价值，但其单领域（数学）、未开源权重/数据、且绝对分数仍落后 SOTA ~5 点，应作为规模化现象学参考而非 SOTA 基线。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the ratio-correction (Megatron numerator / SGLang denominator) and FP32-at-softmax/LM-head fixes remain necessary at 1T, or does the 104B ablation overstate their importance? A direct 1T ablation is the obvious missing experiment.
- The discovery→sharpening transition is tracked only via AIME 2024 pass@1024. Does the same two-phase pattern hold on code, science, or agentic tasks, or is it math-specific?
- The Third-Stage RL "High" mode regresses vs. Second-Stage RL. Would a non-joint training schedule (separate LoRA / separate experts per tier, or a router-gated MoE on top) avoid the negative transfer?
- How much of the "anthropomorphism" / profanity emergence is pretraining-corpus leakage that would vanish under a cleaner base-model pretraining mix? Is it predictive of capability, or just noise?
- The efficiency win (6,368 tokens vs. 14k–17k) is measured only on mutually-solved problems. Does the token advantage survive on problems only Ring solves, or on problems only baselines solve?
- The reward's late-stage correctness judge is Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Instruct, which is closely related to one of the CoT-quality baselines (Qwen3.5-397B). How sensitive are the headline accuracy numbers to the choice of judge?
- pass@1024 saturates early — does that mean RL has truly hit a pretrained-prior ceiling, or would a longer context window (128k+) re-open the discovery phase?
- The paper claims hand-crafted reward shaping is unnecessary at 1T. Does this generalize to non-math domains (code verification, tool use) where the reward signal is much sparser?
