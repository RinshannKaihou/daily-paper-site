> **一句话总结：** EvoQuant 把"量化策略改进"建模成有证据约束的程序演化问题：用 DeepSeek-R1 诊断失效模式、分层生成可控编辑，再通过 OOS 感知的硬门+自适应晋升规则做验证，在 4 个 A 股 + 3 个比特币策略上把平均测试 Sharpe 从 -0.298 提升到 0.538，最佳策略相对提升 199%，且在 2 倍手续费、滚动 walk-forward 下仍保持正收益。

> **TL;DR:** EvoQuant reframes LLM-driven quant strategy improvement as verifier-guided program evolution: it parses a user strategy into a typed "strategy genome", uses DeepSeek-R1 to diagnose failure modes and propose hierarchical edits, then admits a candidate only after it survives out-of-sample-aware hard gates and an adaptive promotion score. Across 7 representative strategies (4 A-share equity, 3 Bitcoin) on AKShare daily data (2020–2025), average test Sharpe rises from -0.298 to 0.538; the best strategy (Bitcoin Oversold Reversal) gains +1.516 Sharpe (from -0.838 to 0.679) and a 199% relative lift; ablations show adaptive promotion is the linchpin (removing it collapses Sharpe uplift from 0.982 to 0.128 on the A-share Volume Breakout task) and gains survive 2x fee/slippage (0.700 uplift) and rolling walk-forward windows.

## 核心贡献

- **证据约束的策略演化范式：** 首次把"已有部署量化策略的改进"形式化为带证据约束的程序演化——将用户策略解析成可编辑、可追溯的 typed strategy genome（信号/风险/仓位/进场/出场分层 + provenance），编辑必须满足非先验性与可执行性约束才被接受。
- **LLM 驱动的诊断–生成–验证闭环：** 用 DeepSeek-R1 做 bottleneck 诊断（风险规则 alpha 截断、regime 错配、验证-OOS 衰减等），按 repair→bridge→redesign→family-migration 分层生成 edit plan；OOS-aware verifier 用"最低交易数 + 最大回撤上限 + drift 上限"硬门筛除投机性候选。
- **自适应晋升 + 经验蒸馏：** 评分 Q(c) 把 val 改进与 OOS 审计分离，ADOPT/INCUBATE/REJECT 三档决策；memory 模块把成功范式与失败原因（交易稀疏、OOS 崩塌、过度 drift）写回知识库，实现持续自改进。
- **跨市场长周期实证：** 在 A 股 30 只随机抽样股票×4 策略族 + 3 个比特币策略上做主实验、消融、2 倍手续费 stress、双窗口 walk-forward，并报告 115/120 的非退化率与拒绝原因分布，而非只给最优解。

## Key Contributions

- **Evidence-constrained strategy evolution.** Formulates quantitative strategy improvement as program evolution from a user-provided strategy, with a typed genome supporting localized, executable, provenance-preserving edits rather than unconstrained generative search.
- **LLM-enabled verifier-guided optimization protocol.** A unified auditable loop combining LLM bottleneck diagnosis, hierarchical candidate synthesis (parameter repair → bridge → redesign → family migration), out-of-sample-aware verification, adaptive promotion (ADOPT/INCUBATE/REJECT), and distillation of reusable memory cases.
- **Mitigation of backtest overfitting.** Logs rejected candidates with failure reasons, enforces minimum trade counts on both validation and OOS splits, tracks validation→OOS decay (δ_oos) and market-regime sensitivity, and reports non-degradation (115/120 tasks) instead of only the best discovered strategy.
- **Long-horizon cross-market evaluation.** Provides before/after comparisons, component ablations, transaction-cost stress tests, and rolling walk-forward validation across both A-share and Bitcoin markets.

## Methodology

EvoQuant casts strategy optimization as verifier-guided program evolution with four modules:

1. **Strategy ingestion & representation (Module 1).** Raw code/rule text is parsed into an AST, tagged with trading semantics (signal, entry, risk, position sizing, exit), factored into editable layers, and recompiled into a typed *strategy genome* g = (g_sig, g_risk, g_pos, g_entry, g_exit, ξ), where ξ stores provenance, strategy family, parent IDs, and mutation history. Any edit must declare its target layer, satisfy non-anticipativity/exposure constraints, and recompile to executable semantics.

2. **Baseline evaluation & evidence construction (Module 2).** The evaluator loads real daily market data (AKShare), builds chronological train/val/test splits, and runs the incumbent under identical execution assumptions to produce an evidence pack E(g) = {M_X, PX_trade, PX_regime} ∪ {δ_oos, C_conc, H_risk} across X ∈ {train, val, test}, capturing Sharpe, return, MDD, trade count, win rate, trade sparsity, holding-period and return-concentration distributions, regime sensitivity, validation→OOS degradation, and abnormal risk phenomena.

3. **Diagnostic–generative optimization loop (Module 3).**
   - *Diagnosis & search plan:* the LLM turns the evidence pack into bottleneck diagnosis z_t and a plan p_t = (o_t, ℓ_t, O_t, F_t, K_t) specifying round objective, search level, allowed operators, forbidden shortcuts, and candidate budget. Failure modes include risk-rule alpha truncation, regime mismatch, and validation decay.
   - *Controlled candidate generation:* the prompt assembles current genome + compressed EvidencePack + diagnosis + retrieved memory cases; the LLM returns an edit plan (target layer, operation, patch, mechanism rationale, expected metric movement, safety constraints). Acceptance requires the typed edit contract c_{t,i} = Δ_{t,i}(g_t), Δ ∈ T(p_t, M_t), dG(g_t, c) ≤ ε_{ℓ_t}, I(c)=1, where dG is semantic drift and I checks executable/financial invariants. Parameter repair tunes lookback/threshold/stop-width/position cap; bridge edits modify filters/exits/signal combos; redesign rewrites the strategy graph; family migration shifts to a nearby strategy family after repeated local failure.
   - *OOS-aware verification, promotion, memory:* hard gates G(c)=1 ⟺ N_val(c) ≥ n_min, N_oos(c) ≥ n_min, MDD_oos(c) ≤ d̄, dG(g_t,c) ≤ ε_{ℓ_t}, I(c)=1, remove non-executable/zero-trade/drifting/high-drawdown candidates before soft scoring Q(c) = w_v·ΔS_val + w_o·ΔS_oos + w_r·ΔR_oos − λ_d·P_dd − λ_g·P_gap − λ_s·P_stress, where penalties cover OOS drawdown expansion, val/OOS discrepancy, and lightweight stress probes (fee/slippage, one-bar delay, parameter jitter). The promotion engine then routes candidates to ADOPT/INCUBATE/REJECT and persists diagnosis, plan, edit, evidence, decision, and failure reason into memory for retrieval and blacklisting.

4. **Iterative refinement & output (Module 4).** Tracks g* = argmax Q_audit(g) over adopted+incubated history; escalates from local repair → bridge → redesign → family migration on repeated failure; stops when budget exhausted, audit score converges, or no level passes verification. Outputs optimized code g*, pre/post backtest report, and full trajectory (diagnoses, edit plans, promotion decisions, memory).

**LLM backend:** DeepSeek-R1 for all inference; each run lasts 20 iterations.

## Results & Highlights

- **Average Sharpe lift across all 7 strategies: −0.298 → 0.538** (best-performing strategy achieves a 199% relative improvement), with average annual return uplift of 13.38 percentage points on the A-share portfolios.
- **A-share portfolio results (Table 1, test set):**
  - Bollinger Reversion: Sharpe 0.351 → 0.966 (+0.615), annual return 4.89% → 9.02%.
  - EMA-RSI Trend: Sharpe −0.465 → 0.367 (+0.832), return 1.64% → 16.83%, MDD 14.36% → 10.41% (−3.95 pp).
  - Low-Vol Quality: Sharpe −0.500 → 0.386 (+0.885), return 1.58% → 17.86%.
  - Volume Breakout: Sharpe −0.493 → 0.489 (+0.982), return −2.21% → 15.74% (+17.95 pp).
- **Bitcoin results (Table 1):** MACD-RSI-Bollinger Sharpe −1.118 → 0.265 (+1.383, flips positive); Oversold Reversal −0.838 → 0.679 (+1.516, flips positive); ATR Trend Breakout −1.536 → −1.050 (+0.486, stays negative). All three reduce MDD by 2.05–9.68 pp.
- **Ablation on A-share Volume Breakout (Table 2):** Full system Sharpe ∆ = 0.982. Removing the promotion engine collapses it to 0.128; Simple-threshold promotion gives only 0.053; Parameter-only edits 0.274; Random-mutation (no diagnosis) 0.645; w/o rule diagnosis 0.693; w/o memory 0.782 — confirming diagnosis-conditioned generation, adaptive promotion, and memory each contribute materially.
- **Robustness (Table 3):** Under 2× fee/slippage, mean Sharpe uplift remains 0.700 (not a friction artifact); under an alternative chronological split, 0.368; rolling walk-forward 2020–2023 window 0.370, 2021–latest window 0.545 — performance weakens without collapsing.
- **Generalization evidence:** Across all 120 A-share stock×strategy tasks (seed 42), the per-symbol Sharpe uplift distribution is right-skewed with 115/120 non-degradation rate; the most frequent verifier rejection reason is sparse OOS trade coverage, evidencing active filtering rather than selection among many degrees of freedom.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine. The framing of strategy *improvement* (not factor discovery) as evidence-constrained program evolution with typed genome edits, explicit drift budgets, and an ADOPT/INCUBATE/REJECT promotion protocol is more disciplined than most LLM-trading-agent papers, and directly engages with the backtest-overfitting literature (White 2000; Bailey et al. 2015; Jacquier et al. 2025).
- **Rigor — strong points:** (i) Chronological splits with explicit δ_oos tracking; (ii) ablation that isolates the promotion engine as decisive (0.982 → 0.128 without it); (iii) 2× fee/slippage stress and rolling walk-forward rather than a single lucky split; (iv) reporting 115/120 non-degradation and rejection-reason distribution (most rejections = sparse OOS trades) instead of cherry-picking the best run.
- **Rigor — weak points and red flags:**
  - **Weak / absent external baselines.** The paper compares against its own ablations (Simple-threshold, Parameter-only, Random-mutation) but does not benchmark against any prior LLM quant agent (R&D-Agent-Quant, AlphaForge, AlphaAgent) or a classical AutoML/evolutionary search baseline (Optuna, AlphaEvolve). "Improvement over random mutation" is a low bar; the absence of a strong programmatic-search baseline is the biggest gap.
  - **Single LLM, no LLM sensitivity analysis.** All inference uses DeepSeek-R1; no cross-model robustness (e.g., GPT-4o, Claude, Qwen) is reported, so it is unclear how much performance depends on R1's reasoning style.
  - **Asset universe limitations.** A-share evaluation uses *30 randomly sampled available symbols* rather than a fully investable historical universe with delisting/survivorship reconstruction — a known bias the authors acknowledge but that bounds financial interpretability, especially for the "Low-Vol Quality" strategy where survivorship matters most.
  - **Cost model is simplified.** Only fee/slippage are stressed; liquidity, market impact, borrow costs, and corporate actions are absent, so the live-tradeability claim is not supported.
  - **ATR Trend Breakout stays negative** (−1.050 Sharpe) even after optimization, and the appendix shows the optimized ATR Trend Breakout underperforms the original in the test window — a partial failure that the main text soft-pedals.
  - **20 iterations, single seed for the headline 115/120 number** (seed 42); variance across seeds is not reported, so the stability of the non-degradation rate is unverified.
- **Reproducibility:** Mixed. Public data + open LLM + promised anonymous code are favorable; but the typed-genome parser, the exact hard-gate thresholds (n_min, d̄, ε_{ℓ_t}), and the Q(c) weights (w_v, w_o, w_r, λ_d, λ_g, λ_s) are described qualitatively rather than fully specified, which would hamper exact replication.
- **Significance:** If the gains hold against a real programmatic-search baseline and a multi-seed study, the verifier-gated promotion protocol is a meaningful methodological contribution for LLM-driven quant research; until then it is a promising but under-benchmarked prototype.

## Strengths

- Principled formulation as constrained program evolution with provenance, drift budgets, and explicit edit contracts.
- Strong, transparent ablation isolating adaptive promotion as the decisive component.
- Cross-market evaluation (A-share + Bitcoin) with stress tests and walk-forward windows rather than a single in-sample cherry pick.
- Honest reporting of 115/120 non-degradation and rejection-reason breakdown, including the 5/120 degradations.
- Acknowledges survivorship bias, simplified execution, and single-LLM limitations in a dedicated Limitations section.

## Limitations

- No comparison against prior LLM quant agents or classical programmatic alpha search (Optuna, AlphaEvolve, AlphaForge, R&D-Agent-Quant).
- 30 randomly sampled A-share symbols, not a survivorship-corrected investable universe.
- Single LLM (DeepSeek-R1), no model-sensitivity study; headline non-degradation rate from a single seed.
- Simplified cost model — no market impact, liquidity, borrow, or corporate-action handling.
- One of three Bitcoin strategies (ATR Trend Breakout) remains negative-Sharpe post-optimization and underperforms in the appendix's test-window return curve.
- Code released only under anonymous link; exact verifier thresholds and scoring weights not fully specified in the paper.

## Relevance

对关注「LLM agents + 金融/量化交易」方向高度相关：EvoQuant 提供了一套可审计、抗回测过拟合的 verifier-guided 改进闭环，与 LLM agent 自我反思、工具增强、记忆蒸馏等机制直接呼应，其 typed-genome + OOS 硬门 + 拒绝日志的设计思路对构建可信的自主量化研究 Agent 具有方法学参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does EvoQuant perform when the candidate-generation and diagnosis LLM is swapped (e.g., GPT-4o, Claude, Qwen-3) — is the promotion protocol robust to the LLM, or is DeepSeek-R1's reasoning style load-bearing?
- What happens against a strong programmatic-search baseline (Optuna TPE, AlphaEvolve, CMA-ES) given the same 20-iteration budget on the genome, rather than only against random mutation and ablations?
- Does the 115/120 non-degradation rate survive a multi-seed study (e.g., seeds 0–9), and how wide is the per-seed Sharpe-uplift variance?
- Would adding a survivorship-corrected, delisting-aware A-share investable universe (CSI 300/500 historical constituents) materially change the Low-Vol Quality and EMA-RSI Trend results?
- How sensitive is the adaptive promotion threshold to the n_min trade-count floor and the MDD cap d̄ — is there a regime where the verifier becomes either too permissive or overly conservative?
- Can the distilled memory cases transfer across strategy families or markets (e.g., cases learned on A-share Volume Breakout reused for Bitcoin ATR Trend Breakout), and does cross-family transfer beat family-specific memory?
