Sparse Inter-Layer Dependencies of Transformer FFN Neurons

Johannes Knittel · Hanspeter Pfister — Harvard University

2026-07-15 arxiv.org/abs/2607.11990 · GPT-2 S/M/L/XL Qwen2.5-0.5B MiniPile
Key Finding

Each Transformer FFN neuron is driven by a sparse subset of upstream components — a training-free attribution method shows that retaining just ~5% of them reconstructs FFN activations with high fidelity, beneath the model's dense parameterization.

Key Metric
5%

of upstream components retained → FFN pre-activation AUROC > 0.9 across GPT-2 S/M/L/XL & Qwen2.5-0.5B. Compound sparsity shrinks the effective fan-in to roughly 0.5%.

nominal 10% × ~5% active ≈ 0.5% contributing
Why This Matters

Locates each FFN neuron's true upstream drivers from native weights alone — no SAE/transcoder training — a practical on-ramp for circuit-level interpretability and sparse model design.

Critical Edge

Advertised inference-efficiency gains are not backed by any efficiency experiment — only perplexity under mean-ablation. Per-neuron masks do not directly yield a static sparse execution plan.

Methodology / pipeline

training-free · native-weight attribution

A GPT-style FFN neuron reads the residual stream as a dense linear sum of all upstream outputs. The paper asks whether that dense sum is actually driven by a sparse subset — and tests it causally.

Results & Highlights

fidelity · compound sparsity · structure

High fidelity at low sparsity

At 10% retained and above, patched pre-activations track actual ones with strong Pearson & high AUROC. Even at 5%, most layers hold AUROC > 0.9 (Fig. 2).

Layer 1 is sparse-friendly

Fidelity degrades only mildly with just 1% retained in the first layer — pre-activations there are dominated by token & positional embeddings.

Compound sparsity is severe

Nominal 10% × ~5% active neurons ≈ 0.5% effectively contributing. Percentile-ablation curves "nearly coincide" with the unconstrained score.

Relevance dominates baselines

Random selection degrades perplexity at 25% masked; relevance selection barely moves it. Variance-only ranking is markedly weaker — geometric alignment carries the signal.

Weight alignment often suffices

For GPT-2 S/L/XL, ranking by dot-products alone nearly matches the full score; GPT-2 M & Qwen2.5 benefit from the variance term.

Structured dependency graphs

Locality (adjacent layers dominate), hub upper layers, and a notably silent first attention layer in GPT-2 M/XL & Qwen2.5-0.5B.

Strengths
  • Training-free, operates directly on native weights — no SAE/transcoder overhead, scales to every neuron.
  • Clean decomposition (Eq. 5–7) makes the geometric intuition — FFN keys align with upstream output directions — explicit and testable.
  • Cross-architecture: includes a modern RMSNorm + rotary + gated-SiLU model (Qwen2.5), with Appendix A documenting the gated-FFN adaptation.
  • Honest framing: sparsity as upper bounds; sufficiency rather than necessity.
  • Surprising structural findings — locality, hub layers, silent first attention layer — concrete starting points for circuit work.
  • Proper disjoint train/test subsets and three selection modes for the perplexity experiments.
Limitations & Red Flags
  • Local, non-propagating neuron-level patching; perplexity is aggregate and can hide input-specific drift.
  • Establishes sufficiency, not necessity — sparse subsets are not proven minimal or required.
  • Caps at 1.5B params; no instruction-tuned models; behaviour at 7B+/70B+ unknown.
  • No comparison to SAEs, transcoders, AttnLRP, or Ferrando & Voita (2024) on the same metric.
  • Efficiency claims speculative — no inference-time / FLOPs / memory measurements; per-neuron masks aren't a static plan.
  • For GPT-2 S/L/XL, weight alignment alone matches the full score — the variance term is partially redundant there.
  • Qwen2.5 markedly worse (25%–15% vs 5%–15%): modern gated FFNs may be less sparse under this method.

Open Questions / for further work

scale · baselines · interventions · use
Does the sparsity persist — or shrink — at 7B–70B scale and in instruction-tuned / RLHF models, where the residual stream may be more superposed?
How does it compare quantitatively against SAEs, transcoders, AttnLRP, and Ferrando & Voita's information-flow routes on the same fidelity metric?
Can per-neuron masks consolidate into a single input-agnostic sparse execution plan with real speedups — vs. Deja Vu-style contextual sparsity?
What happens under stronger interventions (resample / counterfactual noise) — does the high fidelity survive, or does mean-ablation hide dependencies?
Does the "silent first attention layer" replicate in modern architectures, and what does it imply about that layer's role?
Can the sparse pathways drive targeted distillation / pruning that beats magnitude baselines on downstream tasks, not just perplexity?
