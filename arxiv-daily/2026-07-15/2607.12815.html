§ 02

How they asked it

observational debate → causal necessity test

Visual Access Sweep

For each sweep point (t_cutoff, ℓ_cutoff), additively mask attention from generated-token queries to image-token keys — post-softmax zeroing outside the rectangle {ℓ ≤ ℓ_cutoff, t ≤ t_cutoff}. Only the visual channel dies; residual-stream-propagated info survives.

Define the Visual Access Boundary (VAB)

The minimal access pair (ℓ*, τ*) preserving accuracy within tolerance ϵ=0.05. Three criteria: Direct ℓ*D, CoT-own-max ℓ*CoT, Direct-anchored ℓ*DA — with shifts Δℓ*own and Δℓ*DA.

Controlled tasks that resist shortcuts

(a) CLEVR-style synthetic attribute-counting over 5 classes — color, shape, location, angle, size — holding COUNT fixed while varying perceptual difficulty. (b) GQA-balanced yes/no real-image subset (100 samples) for external validity.

Triangulate the bottleneck

Three orthogonal readout diagnostics: multi-object perceptual readout (predicts per-attribute CoT gain), probe-vs-decode (linearly recoverable yet undecodable?), and symbolic-attribute oracle bypass (text-supplied ground-truth attributes, no image).

Controls & robustness

Null-sink control (rule out attention redistribution), late-layer query-text-block control (rule out query-mediated re-reading), ϵ-sensitivity over {0.03, 0.05, 0.07, 0.10}, plus a Llama-3.2-11B-Vision cross-attention pilot.

Novelty
The key move: treat "does CoT need the image?" as a causal-necessity question via two-axis attention masking — superseding prior observational work that conflates "low attention" with "unused."
§ 03

What the sweep found

finite boundary · readout-limited · oracle-restorable
FINDING / 01

A finite VAB exists under both Direct and CoT across all 6 prefix-fusion configs. Accuracy holds near baseline while a broad upper-layer region is blocked, then drops sharply — independent of CoT.

FINDING / 02

CoT does not proportionally expand image access: generation length grows ~50× while |Δℓ*DA| ≤ 2 on Qwen2.5-VL-32B & InternVL3-14B/38B (color & shape: Δℓ*DA=0).

FINDING / 03

VAB is not a synthetic artifact. On GQA real images, Direct 0.84 (ℓ*=40) vs CoT 0.80 ((ℓ*,τ*)=(28,≈32)) — finite boundary without CoT gain, decoupling the two phenomena.

FINDING / 04

At 3B, color (readout 0.713) gains +0.250 from CoT while angle (readout 0.245) gains only +0.030 — readout accuracy tracks ΔCoT almost monotonically.

FINDING / 05

Oracle bypass restores CoT gains uniformly. With no image and ground-truth attributes as text, even hard attributes (angle/size/location) gain consistently — counting is not the limiter once symbols are supplied.

FINDING / 06

Robust to ϵ. Under direct-anchored criterion on 32B, |Δℓ*DA| ≤ 1 for color & shape across ϵ ∈ {0.03, 0.05, 0.07, 0.10}.

§ 04

Strengths vs. limitations

honest scoping · the authors flag most of these themselves

Strengths

  • Causal, not correlational. Converts an observational debate into a necessity test via attention masking.
  • Triangulated diagnostics. Probe / decode / oracle — three orthogonal measures of the same bottleneck.
  • Cross-everything generalization. Qwen vs InternVL · 3B–38B · prefix vs cross-attention · synthetic vs GQA.
  • Clean decoupling. GQA shows VAB exists without CoT gain — separating boundary from benefit.
  • Honest discussion. Explicit list of untested regimes; three competing mechanism accounts left open.
  • Immediately usable. Routing heuristic with quantified accuracy / cost trade-off.

Limitations & red flags

  • Narrow task coverage. Only counting + binary GQA; iterative-inspection tasks (where active perception helps) deliberately excluded.
  • Tiny GQA sample. n=100, single seed, single Qwen2.5-VL-32B checkpoint — weak real-image evidence.
  • VAB mechanism under-determined. Could be early binding, residual propagation, or front-loaded extraction; no residual-stream intervention.
  • One-sided Llama pilot. Direct prompting "did not reliably follow option-key format" — protocol mismatch weakens cross-architecture claim.
  • Selection effect in headline. "|Δℓ*DA| ≤ 2" holds only where the target is attainable; N/A in several 3B/7B/8B cells.
  • Untuned routing threshold. 0.5 cutoff validated on only two Qwen2.5-VL checkpoints; probe-vs-decode on one model only.

§ 05

Open questions

where the boundary framework breaks next
Q.01

Does the VAB shift on tasks that genuinely need iterative inspection — multi-hop spatial reasoning, dense counting, chart QA — where MemVR / Look-Back are reported to help?

Q.02

What does a residual-stream intervention (patching image-derived hidden states per layer) reveal about which mechanism — early binding vs residual propagation vs front-loaded extraction — actually produces the VAB?

Q.03

Is the probe-vs-decode gap a trainable deficiency? Can perception-focused SFT or RL close it for hard attributes and unlock CoT gains without an oracle?

Q.04

Does VAB transfer to video VLMs, where temporal image-token access adds a third axis?

Q.05

How does the VAB interact with visual-token compression / merging — does pruning beyond the VAB layer come "for free"?

Q.06

Could readout-routing be learned rather than thresholded, and does it generalize beyond Qwen2.5-VL to InternVL3 and families where readout is unmeasured?
