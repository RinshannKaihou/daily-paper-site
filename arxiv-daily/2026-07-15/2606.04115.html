Methodology

dMX rests on three observations: floating-point quantization stays well-defined when exponent/mantissa widths are treated as real-valued parameters; deployment still requires collapsing onto the discrete, hardware-supported MXFP set; and gradients propagate w.r.t. bit-width as with any learnable parameter — previously used for integer quantization (FracBits, SDQ, BSQ) but never for floating-point / MX.

Continuous FP parameterization

The whole family collapses to MXFP(β) = E(2+β)M(1+β): β=2 → MXFP8 (E4M3), β=0 → MXFP4 (E2M1), intermediate → MXFP6-like. Tying weights & activations → one scalar per layer. Gradients derived across normal / subnormal / clipped regimes (Appendix A).

Temperature annealing

A sigmoid β = 2 / (1+e^(−T·(β̂/2−0.5))) is near-linear at low T (free exploration) and sharpens to a step at β=1 as T grows — pushing offsets cleanly onto MXFP4 / MXFP6 / MXFP8 endpoints, closing the train/inference gap.

Target-aware regularization

Either simple scaling R_s = λ·b̄ or target-aware R_t = max(0, λ·(b̄_current − b̄_target)), with b̄ as simple or tensor-size-weighted average. The target-aware form yields denser, more predictable operating points.

PTQ training setup

3200 FineWeb samples, batch 8, 400 steps (800 for 3B–8B), jointly optimizing rotations + bit-widths in a SpinQuant-like infrastructure, quantized via Brevitas. Baselines share identical calibration with bit-widths frozen.

Strengths & Limitations

+ Strengths

  • First principled gradient framework for MXFP mixed precision — clean one-parameter-per-layer design mapping exactly onto legal hardware formats.
  • Well-engineered annealing. Continuous forward + temperature anneal solves the FP4↔FP8 oscillation; the ROUND+STE ablation gives direct evidence it matters.
  • Practitioner-facing knob. Target-aware regularization exposes a desired average bit-width rather than an opaque λ.
  • Honest reporting — per-layer sensitivity tables, candid discussion of the high-target-bit-width failure and model-specific underperformance, plus a real 8B scaling appendix.
  • Cheap PTQ cost — 400 calibration steps keeps it firmly in the post-training regime.

− Limitations

  • Thin baselines. Only uniform MXFP and a KL heuristic on one model — no HAWQ-V3, APTQ, MicroMix, or integer mixed-precision ported to the same setting.
  • Hyper-parameter sensitivity (T_ratio, schedule shape, λ) is largely asserted, not systematically swept.
  • No hardware validation. Only weights+activations; no latency / throughput / memory on real MX-capable silicon — average bit-width is a coarse proxy.
  • Narrow evaluation. PTQ-only on ≤8B dense models; MoE (acknowledged future work) and instruct-tuned chat evals absent.
  • High target widths unreliably hit, and per-model retuning is sometimes required (explicitly needed for 3B–8B).
  • No public code-release commitment stated in the paper body.

Questions for Further Exploration

How does dMX compare against integer-domain differentiable methods (BSQ, FracBits, HMQ) when all are ported to the same MX hardware and the same average-bit-width budget?

Can the single-offset parameterization extend to a richer format set (E5M2, INT, non-uniform MX) without losing the smooth-annealing property?

What is the real end-to-end latency / throughput / memory delta on production MX-capable accelerators, beyond the average-bit-width proxy?

Does the learned ranking (down / v proj most sensitive) align with Hessian-spectrum or outlier-structure rankings from HAWQ / SpinQuant, and is it stable across seeds?

Does the framework transfer cleanly to mixture-of-experts models, where per-expert sensitivity varies dramatically, and to chat models on MT-Bench / AlpacaEval?

Would a learned per-layer temperature schedule, or a budget-aware Lagrangian replacing the max-penalty, improve convergence to target bit-width at the high end?

Authors & Credibility

Giuseppe Franco · Ian Colbert · Pablo Monteagudo-Lago · Felix Marty · Nicholas Fraser

All five are at AMD — a co-author of the OCP MX specification cited as the format standard, and maintainer of Brevitas (the quantization library used). Strong credibility on both hardware-format constraints and tooling. arXiv preprint (v2, 14 Jul 2026), not yet peer-reviewed.

Relevance

For researchers tracking low-bit LLM deployment — especially MXFP / OCP float formats and mixed-precision allocation — dMX offers a directly reusable differentiable framework. The continuous-β + anneal idea and the down/v proj sensitivity finding are valuable for quantization-algorithm design and hardware co-optimization; a worthwhile close-read in this direction for 2026.
