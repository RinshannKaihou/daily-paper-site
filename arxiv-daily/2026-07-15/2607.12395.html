Ring-Zero: scaling Zero RL to a trillion parameters

A 1-trillion-parameter MoE base, trained with reinforcement learning from scratch — no SFT data — needs only a handful of "minimal" engineering fixes to stay stable, and then spontaneously develops structured, self-verifying reasoning.

Xinyu Tang, Gangqiang Cao et al. · Gaoling School of AI, Renmin University of China · Ant Group · Tsinghua · Zhejiang U · 16 authors · builds on the authors' own Ling-2.5-1T-Base (1T total / 63B activated).

Key Metric
93.2%
AIME 2026 pass@1 · Second-Stage RL (Yarn=2)
First-Stage-only Zero RL already hits 84.2% · CoT averages 6,368 tokens — less than half of GLM-5.1, Kimi K2.6, Qwen3.5-397B.
Why This Matters

First credible empirical map of Zero-RL dynamics at 1-trillion-parameter scale — a simple, well-motivated stabilization recipe that other teams can transfer directly to proprietary trillion-param bases.

Bitter lesson, empirically staged: scale + minimal fixes beat engineered heuristics.

Critical Edge

Every ablation runs only on the 104B sibling — "due to computational resource constraints" — so the central claim that these fixes are what keep 1T stable rests on analogy, not direct evidence.

No weights, data, or code released; reproduction essentially impossible.

01

Methodology — a four-stage minimalist pipeline

Stage I

Reasoning Elicitation

Clipped importance sampling (CISPO) keeps gradients alive for low-prob reasoning tokens. Token-level loss + KL anchor force long chains; window grows 4k→64k.

ε_high=5.0 · β=1e-4 · K3 divergence
Stage II

Self-Distillation

Sample many rollouts, keep the shortest correct trace, prompt self-pruning. Fine-tune the base on the compressed corpus to kill accumulated train↔inference numerical drift.

3 epochs · seq 64k · lr 7e-5
Stage III

Sustained Optimization

Same clipped IS + ratio correction, but switches to sample-level loss (length-normalized) to stop unbounded growth, and removes KL — the distilled checkpoint is already a strong anchor.

Stage IV

Adaptive Depth

Tier-based training on Low / Medium / High difficulty prompts with budgets 4k / 16k / 64k, teaching the model to allocate compute per problem.

tier-conditioned rollout

Train / Inference ratio correction

Numerator uses Megatron training logits, denominator uses SGLang rollout logits — kills the logit mismatch that collapses baseline training at ~800 steps and lets IcePop survive only to ~2700; the corrected ratio is stable past 3000.

Mixed precision & context parallelism

Body in BF16, but attention softmax + LM head in FP32 — because the importance ratio exponentiates logits, rounding error there blows up. MLA layers use all-to-all CP; Lightning Attention uses AllGather CP — mathematically equivalent, lower latency.

03

Strengths & limitations, side by side

＋ Strengths

  • First credible empirical map of Zero-RL dynamics at 1T — the pass@1024 vs pass@1 "discovery → sharpening" analysis is a clean, falsifiable contribution to a live debate.
  • Engineering recipe is simple and well-motivated — each fix is tied to a specific failure mode (logit mismatch → ratio correction; exponentiation error → FP32; length inertia → token→sample loss switch).
  • CoT-quality framework (Comprehensibility / Reproducibility / Efficiency) is reusable and goes beyond accuracy — 6,368 tokens and an 8× sample-efficiency win are striking.
  • Honest about weaknesses — length inertia, negative transfer in tier training, long-tail data inefficiency, pretrained-prior ceiling are all acknowledged.
  • Rich qualitative case studies of the five emergent behaviors give other researchers concrete phenomena to look for.

− Limitations

  • No ablations on the 1T model itself — all ablations run on the 104B flash, so it is unproven that the same fixes are necessary (or sufficient) at 1T.
  • Proprietary base, data, weights — none of Ling-2.5-1T-Base, the RL corpus, or the final checkpoint is released. Independent reproduction is essentially impossible.
  • Behind frontier on every comparable benchmark (5.1 pts behind GPT-5.5 on AIME 2026); the "competitive" framing oversells the result.
  • Third-Stage RL (adaptive depth) regresses vs the Second-Stage peak (91.4 vs 93.2) — the headline multi-stage contribution underperforms the simpler variant.
  • LLM-as-judge circularity — reward uses a Qwen-family judge; CoT-quality baselines include Qwen3.5-397B. Bias is not controlled for.
  • Single domain (math) — no code, science, or agentic benchmarks; the "bitter lesson" generalization is untested outside math.
  • Compute footprint of 320× H200 makes the recipe inaccessible to most academic groups, undercutting the "lowered barriers" conclusion.
04

Open questions for further work

Q1.Do the ratio-correction and FP32-at-softmax/LM-head fixes remain necessary at 1T, or does the 104B ablation overstate their importance? A direct 1T ablation is the obvious missing experiment.
Q2.The discovery→sharpening transition is tracked only via AIME 2024 pass@1024. Does the same two-phase pattern hold on code, science, or agentic tasks — or is it math-specific?
Q3.Third-Stage "High" regresses vs Second-Stage. Would a non-joint schedule (separate LoRA / experts per tier, or a router-gated MoE on top) avoid the negative transfer?
Q4.How much of the "anthropomorphism" / profanity is pretraining-corpus leakage that would vanish under a cleaner pretraining mix? Is it predictive of capability, or just noise?
Q5.The efficiency win is measured only on mutually-solved problems. Does the token advantage survive on problems only Ring solves, or only baselines solve?
Q6.pass@1024 saturates early — has RL truly hit a pretrained-prior ceiling, or would a longer context window (128k+) re-open the discovery phase?
