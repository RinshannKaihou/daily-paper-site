> **一句话总结：** 作者提出 Visual Access Sweep 这一因果干预方法，沿层深和生成时间掩蔽生成 token 对图像 token 的注意力，定义 Visual Access Boundary (VAB)；在 Qwen2.5-VL 与 InternVL3 六种配置上发现 CoT 尽管生成长度约 50 倍，其 VAB 层与无 CoT 边界相差不超过两层，表明 CoT 的增益不来自延长对图像的直接访问，而是受限于模型能否将视觉属性"读出"为可用符号。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces **Visual Access Sweep**, a causal intervention that masks attention from generated-token queries to image-token keys along layer depth and generation time, defining the **Visual Access Boundary (VAB)** as the minimal access region preserving accuracy. Across six Qwen2.5-VL (3B/7B/32B) and InternVL3 (8B/14B/38B) configurations on synthetic attribute-counting and GQA yes/no tasks, CoT produces ~50× longer generations yet its VAB layer stays within ≤2 layers of the Direct boundary (e.g., Qwen2.5-VL-32B color counting: ℓ*_D=43, ℓ*_DA=43, Δℓ*_DA=0; GQA Direct 0.84 vs CoT 0.80), and CoT gains are instead predicted by perceptual readout accuracy (Pearson r up to +0.92 at 3B) and restored uniformly by a symbolic-attribute oracle bypass — placing the bottleneck at readout rather than reasoning or continued image access.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **Visual Access Sweep** 因果干预范式：沿"层深度 × 生成时间"两轴，对生成 token 查询到图像 token 键的注意力进行加性掩蔽（post-softmax 置零），将"VLM 是否还需要直接看图像"这一观察性问题转为可检验的因果问题。
- 定义 **Visual Access Boundary (VAB)** 作为保持任务精度（容差 ϵ=0.05）所需的最小访问区域；区分 Direct 边界 ℓ*_D、CoT 自身上限边界 ℓ*_CoT 与 Direct-anchored 边界 ℓ*_DA 三种判据。
- 在 6 个模型配置（Qwen2.5-VL 3B/7B/32B + InternVL3 8B/14B/38B）+ Llama-3.2-11B-Vision 跨注意力试点 + GQA 真实图像扩展上，证实存在有限的 VAB；CoT 并不按生成长度等比例扩张所需的直接图像访问区域。
- 通过 **multi-object perceptual readout** + **probe-vs-decode** + **symbolic-attribute oracle bypass** 三组诊断，论证 CoT 增益的真正瓶颈在于"视觉属性能否被模型自身读出为可用符号"，而非计数推理或编码本身。
- 提出实用启发式：基于属性级 readout 阈值 (0.5) 做 CoT 路由，可在大约一半输出 token 成本下恢复 75–87% 的 CoT 增益。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces **Visual Access Sweep**: a two-axis causal intervention that additively masks (post-softmax zeroing) attention from generated-token queries to image-token keys across (layer depth ℓ_cutoff, generation time t_cutoff), converting the observational "does CoT need the image?" question into a necessity test.
- Formalizes the **Visual Access Boundary (VAB)** as the minimal access pair (ℓ*, τ*) preserving accuracy within tolerance ϵ=0.05; provides three criteria — Direct ℓ*_D, CoT-own-max ℓ*_CoT, and Direct-anchored ℓ*_DA — with explicit shift metrics Δℓ*_own and Δℓ*_DA.
- Demonstrates finite VABs under both Direct and CoT prompting across 6 prefix-fusion configurations (Qwen2.5-VL 3B/7B/32B; InternVL3 8B/14B/38B), plus a cross-attention pilot on Llama-3.2-11B-Vision and a real-image GQA yes/no extension — separating the existence of a visual-access boundary from whether CoT improves accuracy.
- Isolates the bottleneck to **perceptual readout** by combining (a) multi-object readout as a predictor of per-attribute CoT gain, (b) a single-object linear probe-vs-decode gap showing attributes can be linearly recoverable yet undecodable by the model, and (c) an oracle bypass supplying ground-truth attributes as text, which uniformly restores CoT gains even for hard attributes like angle/size/location.
- Offers a practical **selective CoT routing** heuristic that uses an attribute-level readout threshold to recover most of always-CoT accuracy at roughly half the token cost.

## Methodology

The work targets decoder-only prefix-fusion VLMs (image tokens concatenated as a prefix to text tokens, processed by standard self-attention — not a separate cross-attention module). The core intervention is:

1. **Visual Access Sweep.** For each sweep point (t_cutoff, ℓ_cutoff), direct attention from generated-token queries to image-token keys is masked (M^ℓ_ij = −∞, yielding zero post-softmax weight) everywhere outside the rectangle {ℓ ≤ ℓ_cutoff, t ≤ t_cutoff}. Sweeping traces how much direct image-token access is needed to preserve performance. Crucially, this masks only one channel — visual information already propagated into hidden states via the residual stream is unaffected.

2. **Visual Access Boundary (VAB).** Given full-access accuracy A^p_full and intervention accuracy A^p(ℓ, τ), three accuracy drops are defined against tolerance ϵ=0.05:
   - Δ_D: Direct boundary ℓ*_D.
   - Δ_CoT: CoT-own-max boundary ℓ*_CoT (preserves CoT's own ceiling).
   - Δ_DA: Direct-anchored boundary ℓ*_DA (CoT under intervention reaches Direct's full-access target).
   Shifts Δℓ*_own = ℓ*_CoT − ℓ*_D and Δℓ*_DA = ℓ*_DA − ℓ*_D are reported.

3. **Tasks.** (a) Controlled synthetic attribute-counting scenes (CLEVR-style) with 5 attribute classes (color, shape, location, angle, size), holding the reasoning operation (COUNT) fixed while varying perceptual difficulty — by design resisting shortcuts via parametric knowledge or dataset bias. (b) A GQA-balanced yes/no real-image subset (100 samples, 53 no / 47 yes) to test external validity.

4. **Readout diagnostics.** Multi-object perceptual readout (model fills in the queried attribute for every object given textual scaffolding for the rest); single-object linear probe-vs-decode on Qwen2.5-VL-32B final-layer hidden states; symbolic-attribute oracle bypass on Qwen2.5-VL-3B with no image input, supplying ground-truth attributes serialized as text under minimal vs full oracle conditions.

5. **Controls.** Null-sink control (redirect masked attention mass to a dummy non-visual sink), late-layer query-text-block control (rule out query-mediated re-reading), and ϵ-sensitivity across {0.03, 0.05, 0.07, 0.10}. Compute: 3× NVIDIA H100 NVL.

## Results & Highlights

- **Finite VAB exists under both Direct and CoT across all 6 prefix-fusion configurations.** Performance stays near full-access baseline while a broad upper-layer region is blocked, then drops sharply when intervention reaches earlier layers — independent of CoT.
- **CoT does not proportionally expand direct image access.** Across 12 (family, scale, task) settings, |Δℓ*_own| stays within 9 layers while generation length increases ~50×; the direct-anchored criterion gives |Δℓ*_DA| ≤ 2 on Qwen2.5-VL-32B and InternVL3-14B/38B (Qwen2.5-VL-32B color: ℓ*_D=43, ℓ*_DA=43, Δℓ*_DA=0; shape: ℓ*_D=42, ℓ*_DA=42, Δℓ*_DA=0).
- **VAB is not specific to synthetic stimuli.** On the GQA-derived real-image yes/no task (Qwen2.5-VL-32B), Direct full accuracy = 0.84 (ℓ*=40) and CoT full accuracy = 0.80 ((ℓ*, τ*)=(28, ≈32)); both exhibit finite VAB despite CoT not improving over Direct — consistent with reports that longer VLM reasoning can weaken perceptual grounding.
- **CoT gain tracks perceptual readout accuracy.** On Qwen2.5-VL, Pearson r between per-attribute readout and ΔCoT is +0.92 (3B), +0.88 (7B), +0.51 (32B, where three of five attributes saturate above readout 0.77); Spearman ρ = +1.00 at 3B and 7B. At 3B, color (readout 0.713) gains +0.250 from CoT while angle (readout 0.245) gains only +0.030.
- **Probe-vs-decode gap localizes the bottleneck.** On Qwen2.5-VL-32B single-object: angle Probe 0.954 vs Decode 0.383 (gap +0.571); location 0.971 vs 0.312 (+0.658); size 0.892 vs 0.283 (+0.608); color and shape have zero gap (1.000 / 1.000). Hard attributes are linearly decodable from hidden states but the model cannot itself output them.
- **Oracle bypass restores CoT gains uniformly.** On Qwen2.5-VL-3B with no image input and ground-truth attributes serialized as text, CoT gains become consistent across all attributes (including angle/size/location that showed weak gains under image input), confirming the counting operation is not the limiter once attributes are supplied as symbols.
- **ϵ-sensitivity check is robust.** Under the direct-anchored criterion on Qwen2.5-VL-32B, |Δℓ*_DA| ≤ 1 for color and shape counting across ϵ ∈ {0.03, 0.05, 0.07, 0.10}.
- **Practical routing heuristic.** Adaptive CoT (readout threshold 0.5) recovers 86.7% (3B) and 75.6% (32B) of the Always-Direct→Always-CoT gain at ~46–50% of the Always-CoT token cost.

## Authors & Credibility

All six authors (Hiroto Osaka, Shohei Taniguchi, Gouki Minegishi, Kai Yamashita, Masahiro Suzuki, Yutaka Matsuo) are at **The University of Tokyo**, with Yutaka Matsuo being a well-known researcher in LLM/multimodal learning. The work is positioned as a Preprint (arXiv:2607.12815v1, 14 Jul 2026). Methodologically, the paper follows good mechanistic-interpretability hygiene: pre-registered-style controlled synthetic tasks, causal intervention rather than correlational attention-magnitude reading, multiple controls (null-sink, query-text-block, ϵ-sensitivity), and a real-image GQA extension plus a cross-attention pilot on Llama-3.2-Vision for external validity. All sweeps run on 3× NVIDIA H100 NVL with reproducible seeds (e.g., GQA shuffle seed 0).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely novel framing. The key conceptual move — treating "does CoT need the image?" as a causal-necessity question via two-axis attention masking rather than reading off attention-magnitude decay — is a clean contribution that supersedes prior observational work (Tong et al. 2024; Rahmanzadehgervi et al. 2024; Kang et al. 2025) which conflates "low attention" with "unused."
- **Rigor:** Strong. Three boundary criteria (Direct / CoT-own-max / Direct-anchored) are carefully distinguished; ϵ-sensitivity across {0.03, 0.05, 0.07, 0.10}; null-sink and query-text-block controls address the two most obvious artifacts (attention redistribution; query-mediated re-reading). The probe-vs-decode-vs-oracle triad cleanly separates latent encoding, decoded availability, and counting-operation benefit.
- **Reproducibility:** Reasonably high. Public base models (Qwen2.5-VL, InternVL3, Llama-3.2-Vision), procedural CLEVR-style scene generation, GQA balanced split, verbatim prompt strings in Table 4, and explicit probe protocol (Table 13). Main ambiguity: the controlled scenes' generator parameters are described (Appendix A.1) but full generation code release is not promised.
- **Significance:** Conceptually important for test-time-scaling research — gives a mechanistic reason *why* longer CoT fails to help perception-heavy VQA (Lu et al. 2024; Liu et al. 2024b; Kancheti et al. 2026): the bottleneck is readout, not reasoning length. Practically actionable via the readout-routing heuristic.
- **Red flags / honest caveats:**
  - **Task coverage is narrow.** Only counting (synthetic) and binary yes/no (GQA) are studied; the authors explicitly exclude "tasks deliberately designed to require iterative visual inspection" — which is precisely where active-perception methods (MemVR, Look-Back, Visual Perception Token) would matter, so the claim "ordinary CoT does not by itself close the perception-reasoning loop" is only validated in regimes that do not require iterative inspection.
  - **GQA sample size is tiny (n=100, single seed).** The 0.84 vs 0.80 Direct/CoT gap is on 100 yes/no items — too small to support strong claims about real-image CoT, and only a single Qwen2.5-VL-32B checkpoint is used.
  - **VAB mechanism is under-determined.** The paper itself concedes the same VAB pattern could arise from early attribute binding, residual-stream propagation, or front-loaded perceptual extraction in early CoT tokens — Visual Access Sweep alone cannot distinguish these. No intervention on the residual stream is performed.
  - **Llama-3.2-Vision pilot is one-sided.** Only CoT-side sweep reported because Direct prompting "did not reliably follow the option-key answer format" — a protocol mismatch that weakens the cross-architecture generality claim.
  - **Direct-anchored target is frequently unreachable** (N/A in several Qwen2.5-VL-3B/7B and InternVL3-8B cells of Table 8), so the headline "|Δℓ*_DA| ≤ 2" holds only on the larger models where the target is attainable — a selection effect the paper acknowledges but readers may miss.
  - **Angle is a limiting case** (chance-level readout, VAB ill-defined), used as a boundary case rather than main evidence — appropriately flagged.

## Strengths

- Causal-intervention design that converts an observational debate into a necessity test.
- Three orthogonal readout diagnostics (probe / decode / oracle) that triangulate the bottleneck rather than relying on any single measure.
- Cross-family (Qwen vs InternVL), cross-scale (3B–38B), cross-fusion (prefix vs cross-attention), and cross-domain (synthetic vs GQA) generalization checks.
- Clean separation between "VAB exists" and "CoT improves accuracy" — the GQA result shows VAB without CoT gain, decoupling the two phenomena.
- Honest scoping in the Discussion (explicit list of regimes not tested; three competing mechanism accounts left open).
- Practical, immediately usable routing heuristic with quantified accuracy/cost trade-off.

## Limitations

- Task scope limited to attribute counting and binary GQA; no benchmarks requiring genuine iterative visual inspection (e.g., multi-step spatial reasoning, fine-grained counting of dozens of objects, chart/doc understanding).
- GQA extension uses only 100 images on a single model — insufficient statistical power and no cross-model real-image evidence.
- VAB does not identify the underlying mechanism (early binding vs residual stream vs front-loaded extraction); additional interventions on the residual stream and on the temporal distribution of perceptual extraction would be needed.
- Probe-vs-decode gap is reported only on Qwen2.5-VL-32B single-object; not cross-validated across families.
- All analyses use frozen, off-the-shelf checkpoints; no test of whether the VAB shifts after perception-focused fine-tuning or RL.
- Selective-routing heuristic threshold (0.5) is admittedly untuned and validated on only two Qwen2.5-VL checkpoints.

## Relevance

对研究 VLM 推理机制、test-time scaling 与 CoT 可解释性的方向高度相关：本文提供了一套可直接复用的因果干预工具（Visual Access Sweep）来检验"CoT 是否真的在用图像"，并指出感知读出（perceptual readout）才是 CoT 增益的瓶颈，这对设计更高效的推理时策略、active-perception 模块以及视觉token 压缩/路由都具有直接启发。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the VAB shift on tasks that *genuinely* require iterative visual inspection (e.g., multi-hop spatial reasoning, dense counting, chart QA), where active-perception methods like MemVR or Look-Back are reported to help?
- What does a residual-stream intervention (e.g., patching image-derived hidden states at specific layers) reveal about which of the three competing mechanism accounts (early binding / residual propagation / front-loaded extraction) actually produces the VAB?
- Is the probe-vs-decode gap a *trainable* deficiency — i.e., can perception-focused SFT or RL close the gap for hard attributes (angle/location/size) and thereby unlock CoT gains without an oracle?
- Does the VAB framework transfer to video VLMs, where temporal image-token access adds a third axis?
- How does the VAB interact with visual-token compression/merging schemes (e.g., token pruning) — does pruning beyond the VAB layer come "for free"?
- Could the readout-routing heuristic be learned rather than thresholded, and does it generalize beyond Qwen2.5-VL to InternVL3 and other families where readout accuracy is not measured?
