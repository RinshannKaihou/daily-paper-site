Generalization Boundaries of Fine-Tuned Small Language Models for Graph Structural Inference

arXiv: 2604.18092

Date: 2026-04-21

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18092

⚠️ Abstract-only summary

一句话总结： 本文系统研究了微调后的小语言模型（3-4B参数）在图结构推理任务上的泛化边界，发现模型在图尺寸和图族分布两个轴上均展现出强序数一致性，但不同架构有独特的退化模式。
TL;DR: Systematically investigates generalization boundaries of fine-tuned 3-4B SLMs on graph structural inference along two axes: graph size (beyond training range) and graph family distribution (held-out random graph families). Finds that models maintain strong ordinal consistency and continue ranking graphs by structural properties even on substantially larger inputs, with architecture-specific degradation profiles.

核心贡献

  • 系统研究了微调小语言模型在图结构推理上的两个泛化轴：图尺寸和图族分布
  • 使用3个3-4B参数的指令微调模型和2种图序列化格式进行控制实验
  • 发现模型在跨图族和超训练范围的图上均保持强序数一致性
  • 揭示了不同模型架构的退化模式差异

Key Contributions

  • Systematic evaluation of fine-tuned SLM generalization on graph structural inference
  • Controlled experiments with three 3-4B instruction-tuned models and two serialization formats
  • Finding that models maintain ordinal consistency across structurally distinct graph families
  • Identification of architecture-specific degradation profiles when scaling beyond training range

Methodology

Controlled experimental setup using three instruction-tuned models (3-4B parameters) with two graph serialization formats. Evaluates on graphs substantially larger than training range and across held-out random graph families. Also assesses domain-learning on real-world graph benchmarks.

Results & Highlights

  • Fine-tuned models maintain strong ordinal consistency across structurally distinct graph families
  • Models continue to rank graphs by structural properties on inputs substantially larger than training range
  • Different architectures show distinct degradation profiles when generalizing
