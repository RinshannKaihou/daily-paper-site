Generalization Boundaries of Fine-Tuned Small Language Models for Graph Structural Inference

arXiv: 2604.18092

Date: 2026-04-21

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18092

一句话总结： 本文系统刻画了LoRA微调的3-4B小语言模型（Llama-3.2-3B、Qwen2.5-3B、Phi-4-mini）在图结构属性估计上的泛化边界，发现邻接表编码在跨分布迁移、尺寸外推（n=20-30训练到n=150）和真实图域上始终优于边表编码，且序一致性（Spearman ρ）比数值精度（R²）衰减更平缓，模型在跨图族迁移上保持ρ>0.88、尺寸外推保持ρ>0.74。
TL;DR: Systematically maps the generalization boundaries of LoRA-fine-tuned small language models (Llama-3.2-3B, Qwen2.5-3B, Phi-4-mini, all 3-4B) for graph property estimation across two axes: cross-distribution transfer (held-out random graph family) and size generalization (trained on n=20-30, tested up to n=150), plus real-world graph benchmarks (ENZYMES, IMDB-BINARY, NCI1, ogbg-moltox21). Adjacency-list serialization consistently beats edge-list across all conditions and the gap widens at larger sizes; all models maintain Spearman ρ>0.88 on held-out families and ρ>0.74 up to n=150; on real-world graphs ρ ranges 0.685–0.948. Ordinal consistency (Spearman ρ) degrades far more gracefully than magnitude accuracy (R²), revealing a dissociation between comparative structural reasoning and calibrated numerical estimation.

核心贡献

  • 系统刻画了微调SLM在图结构推理上的两条泛化轴：跨图族分布迁移（用2族训练、留1族测试）和尺寸外推（n=20-30训练到n=150），并在真实图域上评估领域学习能力。
  • 发现邻接表编码(Adj)始终优于边表编码(Edge)，且优势随图尺寸增大而扩大——这是跨所有条件最一致的现象，支持"节点分组表示更利于transformer注意力局部聚合"的假说。
  • 揭示序一致性与数值精度的解离：Spearman ρ在分布偏移下保持较高，而R²（数值方差解释）急剧下降，表明比较性结构推理与校准数值估计依赖部分不同的学习表示。
  • 给出架构特异性的退化轮廓：Phi-4-mini近单调平滑退化，Qwen2.5-3B呈"高原平台"模式（中等尺寸后稳定），Llama-3.2-3B波动较大，说明架构选择对尺寸泛化至关重要。

Key Contributions

  • Maps two complementary generalization axes for fine-tuned SLM graph property estimators: cross-distribution transfer (train on 2 of 3 random graph families ER/BA/WS, test on the held-out third) and size generalization (train on n=20-30, evaluate up to n=150), plus real-world domain learning on ENZYMES, IMDB-BINARY, NCI1, ogbg-moltox21.
  • Establishes adjacency-list serialization as a persistent, cross-condition advantage over edge-list encoding, with the gap widening at larger graph sizes — interpreted as a locality benefit for transformer attention aggregation.
  • Documents an ordinal/cardinal dissociation: Spearman rank correlation degrades gracefully under distribution shift while R² collapses sharply in several edge-list transfer conditions, indicating comparative structural reasoning is more robust than calibrated magnitude estimation.
  • Identifies architecture-specific degradation profiles (smooth monotonic for Phi-4-mini, plateau for Qwen2.5-3B, volatile for Llama-3.2-3B), arguing that single-point OOD evaluation misses the full degradation trajectory.

Methodology

Models. Three instruction-tuned SLMs in the 3-4B class: Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, Phi-4-mini-Instruct. All fine-tuned with LoRA: scaling α=32, dropout 0.05; rank r=32 for synthetic graphs, r=16 for real-world graphs (to mitigate overfitting on smaller datasets). Training: sequence length 2048, batch size 2 with gradient accumulation 8, cosine LR schedule with warmup ratio 0.03, LR 2×10⁻⁴, 10 epochs. Hardware: single NVIDIA RTX 3090 GPU.

Graph serialization. Two formats, both with explicit (n, m) header: Adjacency-list (Adj, node-wise neighbor grouping) and Edge-list (Edge, ordered edge pairs without grouping). The formats differ only in token organization, isolating locality effects from information content.

Synthetic data. TinyGraphEstimator dataset: undirected, unweighted, connected graphs from three random graph models — Erdős–Rényi (ER), Barabási–Albert (BA), Watts–Strogatz (WS); node counts uniformly sampled from n∈[20,30]. 1,200 training + 120 test graphs (evenly across families). Cross-distribution: train on 2 families, test on held-out third (800 train, 400 validation per condition). Size generalization: train on full set, test on additionally generated graphs up to n=150.

Real-world data. TUDataset (ENZYMES biological, IMDB-BINARY social, NCI1 chemical) and OGB (ogbg-moltox21 molecular); converted to unweighted undirected topology; 80/10/10 train/val/test split per dataset.

Metrics. Three complementary metrics macro-averaged across 12 graph properties (min/mean/max/std of degree; density; average clustering coefficient; transitivity; triangle count; average shortest path length; diameter; chromatic number; global efficiency): (1) Spearman rank correlation ρ (primary, ordinal consistency); (2) NRMSE_range (scale-independent magnitude error); (3) R² (variance explained; negative = below constant-mean predictor).

Results & Highlights

  • In-distribution baseline (Table 1, n=20-30): All fine-tuned models achieve Spearman ρ>0.984 and R²>0.966 under adjacency-list encoding; Qwen2.5-3B is best (ρ=0.987, R²=0.972). Zero-shot baselines are near-random: ρ 0.145–0.261, R² negative (e.g., Llama-3.2-3B Adj zero-shot R²=−1.870). Adj consistently beats Edge across all models/metrics.
  • Cross-distribution transfer (Table 2, adjacency-list): All models achieve Spearman ρ 0.888–0.956 across the three held-out families; R² 0.469–0.798 (consistently positive). Phi-4-mini is strongest overall (ρ 0.913/0.952/0.956, R² 0.733/0.611/0.798). Edge-list R² collapses severely in ER+BA→WS (0.231–0.291) and ER+WS→BA (0.063–0.263), with Qwen2.5-3B NRMSE_range jumping to 0.455 (more than 2× the adjacency-list value) — the clearest ordinal/cardinal dissociation.
  • Size generalization (Table 3, adjacency-list, n up to 150): Phi-4-mini degrades nearly monotonically from ρ=0.984 (training range) to 0.749 at n=140-150; Qwen2.5-3B plateaus at 0.847–0.888 after an initial decline and stays ≥0.847 to n=150; Llama-3.2-3B is volatile, dipping to ρ=0.573 at n=130-140 then partially recovering to 0.755. Edge-list degrades faster: Qwen2.5-3B 0.977→0.596, Phi-4-mini 0.975→0.647, Llama-3.2-3B 0.975→0.658 at the largest size.
  • Real-world domain learning (Table 4, adjacency-list): Spearman ρ ranges 0.685 (Phi-4-mini on NCI1) to 0.948 (Llama-3.2-3B on ogbg-moltox21); R² 0.480–0.931. On ogbg-moltox21, Llama-3.2-3B reaches ρ=0.948, R²=0.931; on IMDB-BINARY, Qwen2.5-3B reaches ρ=0.940, R²=0.824. NCI1 is the weakest domain (ρ as low as 0.685).
  • Representation gap is domain-dependent: On ENZYMES (biological, irregular topology) the Adj–Edge gap is 0.161 for Llama-3.2-3B (ρ 0.894 vs 0.733), substantially larger than any synthetic gap; on IMDB-BINARY (regular social graphs) the gap narrows (Qwen2.5-3B: 0.940 vs 0.912).
  • Per-property locality gradient: Node-level and local-structural properties are estimated reliably; global combinatorial properties (especially chromatic number) fail across all models — a genuine boundary of inference.

Critical Assessment

  • Single-author, single-GPU study with no code release. Reproducibility rests entirely on the described protocol; the absence of a public code repository or fixed data split metadata makes independent verification difficult, and the TinyGraphEstimator dataset dependency (cite [4]) is the load-bearing data source without a checksum or version.
  • Size generalization relies exclusively on Spearman ρ. The author explicitly acknowledges this as a limitation: magnitude-based metrics are not comparable across size bins due to shifting property distributions, so the reported graceful degradation may mask much faster collapse of absolute prediction accuracy — a materially important caveat given the ordinal/cardinal dissociation observed elsewhere.
  • Evaluation scope is narrow on graph classes. All experiments use undirected, unweighted, connected graphs; directed, weighted, and multi-component graphs are untested, and the claimed carry-over is asserted by argument rather than evidence.
  • The 3-4B scale ceiling is not challenged. Whether the adjacency-list advantage and the architecture-specific degradation profiles hold for larger or smaller models is an explicit open question; the conclusions are bounded to the 3-4B class.
  • No statistical significance testing. Results are single-run point estimates per condition; with volatile behavior like Llama-3.2-3B's ρ=0.573 dip at n=130-140, it is unclear whether that dip is a stable architecture property or a seed/draw artifact.

Strengths

  • Clean, controlled experimental design: the two serialization formats differ only in token organization, isolating locality effects; the cross-distribution setup (train on 2 families, test on the held-out third) is a principled OOD test.
  • Three genuinely complementary metrics (Spearman ρ, NRMSE_range, R²) that together expose the ordinal/cardinal dissociation — a substantive finding rather than a single-number leaderboard.
  • Honest scoping: the paper explicitly states where models fail (chromatic number, magnitude under edge-list) and frames the contribution as mapping boundaries ("where they stop working") rather than claiming universal competence.
  • Real-world benchmarks (TUDataset, OGB) complement the synthetic experiments, strengthening external validity.

Limitations

  • Size generalization conclusions are ordinal-only; absolute prediction accuracy at large n is unmeasured and likely worse than the Spearman trajectory suggests.
  • Graph class coverage is restricted to undirected/unweighted/connected; generalization to directed, weighted, or disconnected graphs is unverified.
  • Scale limited to 3-4B models; no evidence the findings transfer to larger or smaller architectures.
  • No code release and no statistical significance testing; volatile per-bin results (Llama dip at n=130-140) may not be reproducible across seeds.
  • Single author and single GPU (RTX 3090) constrain the breadth of the sweep; only 120 test graphs for the in-distribution evaluation.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How rapidly does absolute (magnitude) prediction accuracy degrade with graph size, and can a size-conditioned calibration restore R² at large n?
  • Does the adjacency-list advantage persist for directed/weighted/multi-component graphs, or does it reverse when edge directionality carries critical information?
  • Are the architecture-specific degradation profiles (Phi smooth, Qwen plateau, Llama volatile) causal consequences of tokenization, attention head specialization, or pretraining data composition?
  • Would ensembling the three models recover the volatile Llama-3.2-3B dip at n=130-140, or is the dip a systematic structural blind spot?
  • Can the ordinal/cardinal dissociation be exploited — e.g., use SLMs only for ranking/comparison tasks while delegating magnitude estimation to classical algorithms?
