> **一句话总结：** 本文通过将 SwiGLU 块中的五个线性层分解为残差轴读取器（qkv, w1, w3）和块内生成器（o_proj, w2），发现 W4A4 量化误差主要来自读取器的激活异常值，提出 Depth Registers + 寄存器幅度铰链损失（DR+sink）在训练时抑制读取器激活异常，将朴素 W4A4 的 PPL 从 1727 降至 39.9（配合 SmoothQuant），并证实正交旋转无法约束 SwiGLU 双线性乘积尾部分布。

> **TL;DR:** This paper decomposes the five trainable linear layers in SwiGLU blocks into residual-axis readers (qkv, w1, w3 -- ingesting RMSNorm of the residual stream) and block-internal generators (o_proj, w2 -- ingesting softmax-V aggregate and SwiGLU bilinear product). It shows that W4A4 quantization error is dominated by reader activation outliers, and proposes Depth Registers with a register-magnitude hinge loss (DR+sink) to suppress reader kurtosis at training time, reducing naive W4A4 PPL from 1727 to 119 (14x), further to 39.9 with SmoothQuant. Crucially, Full QuaRot's orthogonal rotation does not close the remaining gap to FP16, confirming the prediction that bilinear SwiGLU tails resist rotation-based smoothing.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 SwiGLU 块的**读取器/生成器分解（Reader/Generator Decomposition）**：将五个可训练线性层按输入来源分为残差轴读取器（qkv, w1, w3，读取 RMSNorm 处理后的残差流）和块内生成器（o_proj, w2，读取 softmax-V 聚合和 SwiGLU 双线性乘积），从理论上证明残差轴幅度控制可紧密约束读取器，但 w2 的双线性输入仅受因子界的平凡乘积约束。
- 提出 **Depth Registers + 寄存器幅度铰链损失（DR+sink）**：一种训练时干预手段，通过在残差流中添加可学习的深度寄存器向量并施加铰链损失以约束其幅度，在不影响 FP16 PPL 和零样本能力的前提下，将读取器激活峰度（kurtosis）从约 35.9 压缩至 2.85。
- 通过**跳跃线性消融（skip-linear ablation）**定量分解 W4A4 损伤预算：在 300M 参数模型上，当所有读取器保持 FP16 时，W4A4 的 PPL 下限约为 31，且 Delta-remove(w2) 占据主导地位，确认 w2 是量化瓶颈。
- 实验验证**正交旋转无法约束 SwiGLU 双线性尾部**：Full QuaRot（添加逐头值 Hadamard 和在线 w2 输入旋转）仍未能消除 FP16 与 W4A4 之间的差距，直接验证了理论预测。
- 提出**过量 NLL（Excess NLL）**作为 PPL 的替代指标，能更好地捕捉非加性量化损伤。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the **Reader/Generator Decomposition** of SwiGLU block linear layers: readers (qkv, w1, w3) ingest RMSNorm of the residual stream and are bounded by residual-axis magnitude control; generators (o_proj, w2) ingest block-internal quantities whose bilinear products are only bounded by trivial product-of-factor bounds, making them fundamentally harder to smooth via magnitude control alone.
- Proposes **Depth Registers with register-magnitude hinge loss (DR+sink)**: a training-time intervention that appends learnable depth register vectors to the residual stream and constrains their magnitude via a hinge loss, collapsing reader activation kurtosis (e.g., qkv: 35.9 -> 2.85) while leaving generator kurtosis essentially unchanged (w2: 1921 -> 1860), at matched FP16 PPL and zero-shot capacity.
- Develops the **skip-linear ablation** methodology for W4A4 damage budgeting: selectively holding individual linear layers at FP16 while quantizing all others to W4A4, revealing that readers dominate the quantization error and that the floor (all readers at FP16) is approximately 31 PPL with w2 as the dominant bottleneck.
- Empirically confirms that **orthogonal rotation cannot bound the bilinear SwiGLU tail**: Full QuaRot (per-head value Hadamard plus online w2-input rotation) fails to close the FP16 gap, directly testing and validating the paper's core theoretical prediction about bilinear product tail behavior.
- Introduces **Excess NLL** as a diagnostic metric that captures non-additive quantization damage more faithfully than PPL, showing the reader-rescued W4A4 residue is flat at approximately 0.28 nats across three matched checkpoints.

## Methodology

**Controlled Setting:** A 300M-parameter SwiGLU decoder-only language model trained on 5B tokens of FineWeb-Edu, providing a reproducible sandbox for W4A4 quantization analysis.

**Reader/Generator Decomposition (Section 3):**
- Residual-axis readers (qkv, w1, w3): These layers receive as input the RMSNorm of the accumulated residual stream. Their activation distributions are governed by the residual stream magnitude, which can be controlled.
- Block-internal generators (o_proj, w2): o_proj receives the softmax-V aggregate (attention output); w2 receives the SwiGLU bilinear product (silu(xW1) * xW3). The bilinear nature of w2's input means that orthogonal rotations of the individual factors (W1, W3) cannot bound the tail of the element-wise product distribution.

**Depth Registers (Section 4):**
- Append learnable register vectors to the residual stream at each depth (analogous to vision transformers' register tokens, but applied for magnitude control rather than attention sink mitigation).
- Apply a register-magnitude hinge loss that penalizes register magnitudes exceeding a threshold, effectively suppressing outlier activations in the residual stream.
- The intervention is training-time only; at inference, the registers are part of the model but contribute negligible overhead.

**Skip-Linear Ablation (Section 5):**
- For each of the five trainable linears, hold that layer at FP16 while quantizing all others to W4A4, measuring the resulting PPL.
- This decomposes the total W4A4 damage into per-layer contributions, revealing which input-activation sites dominate.

**Comparison Methods:**
- SmoothQuant: post-hoc activation smoothing via per-channel weight rescaling.
- Per-Linear QuaRot: per-linear-layer Hadamard rotation applied post-hoc.
- Full QuaRot: adds per-head value Hadamard and online w2-input rotation to Per-Linear QuaRot.
- AWQ: activation-aware weight quantization.

## Results & Highlights

**Main Quantization Results (PPL on validation set):**
- FP16 baseline: 23.6 PPL
- Naive round-to-nearest W4A4: 1727 PPL (catastrophic collapse)
- DR+sink W4A4: 119 PPL (14x reduction from naive)
- DR+sink + SmoothQuant W4A4: 39.9 PPL
- Per-Linear QuaRot W4A4: 25.5 / 25.4 PPL (nearly matches FP16)
- Full QuaRot W4A4: 26.0 PPL (does NOT improve over Per-Linear, confirming rotation cannot help w2)

**Kurtosis Analysis:**
- DR+sink collapses reader kurtosis: qkv from 35.9 to 2.85; w1 and w3 similarly reduced
- Generator kurtosis barely changes: w2 from 1921 to 1860 (essentially unaffected)
- This asymmetric effect confirms the reader/generator decomposition's prediction

**Skip-Linear Ablation (Table 3):**
- Holding qkv at FP16 provides the largest single-layer rescue
- Holding all three readers at FP16 yields a floor of approximately 31 PPL
- Delta-remove(w2) dominates the residual damage, confirming w2 as the primary W4A4 bottleneck

**Excess NLL Diagnostic:**
- The reader-rescued W4A4 residue is approximately 0.28 nats across three matched checkpoints
- This flat residue indicates that the remaining damage is systematic and concentrated in the generator axis

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (Moderate-High):** The Reader/Generator decomposition is conceptually clean and provides a useful vocabulary for analyzing SwiGLU quantization. The insight that bilinear products resist orthogonal rotation is mathematically elementary but diagnostically powerful. Depth Registers as a training-time intervention are not entirely novel (register tokens in vision transformers, attention sinks in LLMs), but the specific application to activation outlier control with a hinge loss is new. The skip-linear ablation methodology is a straightforward but effective diagnostic tool.

**Rigor (Moderate-High):** The paper provides norm-based arguments for why readers are bounded and generators are not, and backs this up with kurtosis measurements and skip-linear ablations. However, the theoretical arguments are "elementary norm arguments" rather than formal theorems with provable guarantees. The experimental methodology is sound within its scope but limited to a single model and seed.

**Reproducibility (Moderate):** The 300M model trained on 5B tokens of FineWeb-Edu is small enough to be reproducible in principle, but no code is released. The single-seed setting means variance is unknown. The paper provides sufficient methodological detail (DR+sink formulation, skip-linear protocol) for independent reproduction.

**Significance (Moderate):** The diagnostic value of the paper is high -- it provides a clear framework for understanding *where* W4A4 fails in SwiGLU models and *why* existing rotation-based methods cannot fully close the gap. However, as the authors acknowledge, the work is a probe rather than a solution. The practical impact is limited by: (1) 300M scale only; (2) no demonstration at 7B+ where W4A4 is actually relevant for deployment; (3) the best W4A4 results (DR+sink + SmoothQuant at 39.9 PPL) are still far from FP16 (23.6), and Per-Linear QuaRot (25.5) is a post-hoc method that does not require training-time intervention.

**Red flags:**
- Single seed, single model, single scale: no variance estimates and no evidence of generalization beyond 300M.
- The paper does not isolate the effect of the reader/generator partition from the hinge loss -- the DR+sink intervention conflates both.
- Per-Linear QuaRot (a post-hoc method) nearly matches DR+sink on the reader axis (25.5 vs. 39.9 PPL), raising the question of whether Depth Registers are necessary at all.
- The w2 bottleneck is identified but not resolved; the paper effectively shows that W4A4 on SwiGLU is still an open problem for the generator axis.

## Strengths

- The Reader/Generator decomposition provides an elegant and memorable framework for analyzing quantization error sources in SwiGLU transformers.
- Exceptional intellectual honesty: the paper explicitly scopes its claims, acknowledges what it does not solve, and positions itself as a diagnostic probe rather than a deployment-ready solution.
- The skip-linear ablation is a clean experimental design that directly quantifies per-layer W4A4 damage contributions.
- The confirmation that Full QuaRot's orthogonal rotation cannot help w2 is a negative result with genuine theoretical import: it rules out a natural class of post-hoc smoothing methods for the bilinear SwiGLU tail.
- The excess NLL metric provides a more principled alternative to PPL for measuring non-additive quantization damage.
- Well-structured paper with clear figures (especially Figure 1 illustrating the decomposition) and comprehensive tables.

## Limitations

- **Scale limitation:** All experiments are on a 300M-parameter model trained on 5B tokens. It is unknown whether the reader/generator decomposition insights transfer to 7B+, 70B+, or larger models where W4A4 is practically relevant.
- **Single seed:** No variance estimates; the 119 PPL for DR+sink or 39.9 for DR+sink + SmoothQuant could fluctuate significantly across seeds.
- **Conflated interventions:** The DR+sink method combines depth registers (the partition) with the hinge loss (the magnitude constraint), and the paper does not ablate these separately. It is unclear whether the registers alone, or the hinge alone, would achieve similar results.
- **No solution for w2:** The paper identifies w2 as the primary W4A4 bottleneck but offers no remedy. The generator axis problem remains open.
- **Training-time requirement:** DR+sink requires modifying the training process, limiting applicability to pre-trained models. Per-Linear QuaRot (post-hoc) achieves better PPL, undermining the practical motivation for DR+sink.
- **No comparison with mixed-precision schemes:** The paper studies uniform W4A4 but does not compare against W4A8 or mixed W4A4/W8A8 strategies that are more commonly used in practice.

## Relevance

本文与 LLM 量化研究高度相关，通过读取器/生成器分解框架系统揭示了 SwiGLU 模型中 W4A4 量化的误差来源结构。其核心发现——残差轴读取器的激活异常可通过训练时干预抑制，而 w2 生成器的双线性输入尾部分布无法通过正交旋转约束——为理解 Transformer FFN 层的量化瓶颈提供了重要的理论和实验依据。该工作虽然仅在 300M 规模上验证，但其诊断框架对大规模模型的量化研究具有直接的指导意义，特别是对当前广泛采用的 SwiGLU 架构（如 LLaMA 系列）的低比特量化部署。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the Reader/Generator decomposition scale to 7B and 70B models? Do the kurtosis patterns and skip-linear ablation rankings transfer to larger scales where W4A4 is practically relevant?
- Can the DR+sink intervention be separated from the hinge loss in a principled way? What is the individual contribution of depth registers versus the magnitude constraint?
- If w2 is the primary bottleneck, what alternative approaches beyond orthogonal rotation could address the bilinear SwiGLU tail? For example, could learnable non-orthogonal transforms, tailored clipping, or mixed-precision (W8 for w2, W4 elsewhere) resolve the generator axis problem?
- How does the reader/generator decomposition interact with more recent quantization methods such as GPTQ, AQLM, or QuIP# that go beyond simple round-to-nearest?
- Is the flat excess NLL residue (~0.28 nats) observed across matched checkpoints a universal property of reader-rescued W4A4, or is it specific to this model/training regime?
- Could the Depth Registers approach be combined with quantization-aware training (QAT) rather than post-training quantization to jointly address both reader and generator axis challenges?
