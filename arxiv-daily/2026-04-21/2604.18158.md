## 一句话总结

本文通过"状态迁移"实验发现，在控制路由任务（controlled routing）中训练得到的注意力头中间状态（K/V向量）可以直接零重训练地迁移到新的上下文中复用，证明了模型内部存在固定接口的"编译式迁移"机制，而非依赖学习到的提示重定位。

## TL;DR

This paper introduces "state transfer" (compiled transfer) as a diagnostic for mechanistic reuse in controlled routing. The authors show that key/value vectors learned by attention heads for one routing task can be directly patched into new prompt contexts with zero retraining, achieving near-donor performance (e.g., 0.799 vs 0.824 on triop routing in GPT-2-medium). Through K/V split necessity-sufficiency diagnostics and cross-architecture (Qwen2.5-3B) verification, they demonstrate that routing is mediated by a fixed attention interface (V-sufficient, K-necessary), distinguish compiled (zero-retrain) transfer from learned prompt relocation, and show that generation tasks require broader Q×KV interfaces than single-site routing.

## 核心贡献

- 提出"状态迁移"（compiled transfer）方法，将已训练路由头的K/V向量直接零重训练地注入新上下文，作为机制复用的诊断工具
- 通过K/V分裂实验（必要性/充分性诊断）确立了V是路由的充分中介，K是必要但非充分的中介，精确刻画了注意力接口的作用
- 区分了"编译式迁移"（固定接口、零重训练）与"学习式重定位"（需数千支持样本×步数才能匹配）两种复用模式
- 在跨架构（GPT-2-medium到Qwen2.5-3B）上验证了路由一致性，82/82次实验均呈正向delta
- 揭示生成任务比单点路由需要更广泛的Q×KV接口，为理解模型内部计算提供了新视角

## Key Contributions

- **Compiled transfer diagnostic**: Introduces zero-retrain patching of learned K/V states into new contexts as a tool to test mechanistic reuse, distinguishing it from learned prompt relocation that requires thousands of support×steps.
- **K/V necessity-sufficiency decomposition**: Establishes via controlled ablation that V is sufficient (compiled KV→centered recovers compiled performance exactly at 0.799) and K is necessary but not sufficient for routing in GPT-2-medium attention heads.
- **Support-locked / query-held-out evaluation protocol**: Designs evaluation settings that isolate transfer from memorization, ensuring that measured performance reflects genuine reuse of internal state.
- **Cross-architecture verification**: Replicates routing findings in Qwen2.5-3B (perfect matched-interface routing = 1.0; V-only = 1.0, K-only = 0.0), confirming that the fixed-interface reuse mechanism generalizes beyond GPT-2.
- **Generation vs. routing interface analysis**: Demonstrates that generation tasks require broader Q×KV interfaces compared to single-site routing, with copy10 compiled at 0.700 but c←KV dropping to 0.504 and c←QKV recovering to 0.650.

## Methodology

The paper employs a multi-layered experimental approach:

1. **Routing Tasks**: Two controlled routing setups in GPT-2-medium — (a) triop routing (add/sub/copy, 3-way classification, chance = 0.333) and (b) add/sub binary routing. These tasks require the model to route inputs through specific attention heads based on prompt context.

2. **Compiled Transfer Protocol**: K/V vectors are extracted from attention heads trained on a "donor" context and directly patched into a new "recipient" context with zero retraining. Performance is compared against the donor baseline and chance level.

3. **K/V Split Diagnostics**: Systematic ablation at attention interfaces:
   - **Compiled K → centered context**: Tests if K alone suffices (result: 0.723, above chance but below full compiled)
   - **Compiled V → centered context**: Tests V sufficiency (result: 0.611)
   - **Compiled KV → centered context**: Baseline (result: 0.541, equivalent to centered)
   - **Centered KV → compiled context**: Tests context-level mediation (result: 0.799, exact match to compiled — proof of V-sufficiency at the full interface)

4. **Learned Relocation Baselines**: LoRA, control embedding tuning, and prompt-slot tuning are trained as learned alternatives to compiled transfer, requiring 3200–25600 support×steps to match zero-retrain compiled scores.

5. **Cross-Architecture Verification**: Qwen2.5-3B is used to check routing consistency, with 82/82 runs showing positive deltas. Oracle gains (+0.422) vs. best learned gains (+0.068) highlight the gap between compiled and learned transfer.

6. **Generation Experiments**: Extending from routing to generation tasks reveals that broader Q×KV interfaces are needed, with copy10 showing the additional complexity of generation over classification-style routing.

## Results & Highlights

- **GPT-2 triop routing**: Compiled transfer achieves 0.799±0.040 vs donor 0.824±0.030 vs chance 0.333 — 97% of donor performance with zero retraining.
- **GPT-2 add/sub routing**: Compiled transfer reaches 0.9113±0.0010 vs donor 0.9422±0.0010; plain LoRA remains at chance (0.5000).
- **K/V sufficiency proof**: Centered KV ← compiled context yields 0.799 (identical to full compiled), proving that V-mediated state at the attention interface is the complete mediator.
- **Qwen verification**: Matched-interface routing achieves perfect 1.0; wrong-interface prompt-last drops to 0.383; V-only transfer = 1.0, K-only = 0.0.
- **Learned vs. compiled gap**: Oracle gain in Qwen is +0.422 while best learned method achieves only +0.068, underscoring the efficiency of compiled transfer.
- **Generation complexity**: Copy10 compiled = 0.700, but c←KV = 0.504 and c←QKV = 0.650, showing generation demands broader interfaces than routing.

## Critical Assessment

This paper makes a clean, well-controlled contribution to mechanistic interpretability by introducing compiled transfer as a diagnostic tool. The K/V split experiments are particularly elegant — the centered KV ← compiled result exactly matching full compiled performance (0.799) is a strong proof that the attention interface mediates routing through a fixed, transferable mechanism. The cross-architecture verification in Qwen2.5-3B strengthens generalizability claims.

However, several caveats are worth noting: (1) The routing tasks are highly controlled and synthetic — whether these fixed-interface reuse principles hold for naturally occurring routing in pretrained models remains an open question. (2) The gap between oracle compiled transfer and learned methods (+0.422 vs +0.068) could reflect limitations of the specific learned baselines rather than a fundamental distinction. (3) The generation experiments, while informative, are preliminary and limited to a single copy task. (4) The paper focuses on attention head K/V states; whether similar compiled transfer holds for MLP layers or other model components is unexplored. (5) Scale is limited to GPT-2-medium and Qwen2.5-3B — larger models may exhibit different routing dynamics.

Overall, this is a solid mechanistic study with careful experimental design that advances our understanding of how attention heads encode transferable routing states, though the gap to practical model editing applications remains significant.

## Strengths

- **Elegant experimental design**: The K/V split necessity-sufficiency diagnostics with exact numerical recovery (0.799) provide compelling causal evidence for the attention interface as a routing mediator.
- **Strong controls**: Support-locked and query-held-out protocols, wrong-interface controls, and chance baselines rule out memorization and spurious transfer explanations.
- **Cross-architecture replication**: Verification in Qwen2.5-3B with 82/82 positive deltas significantly strengthens generalizability claims.
- **Clear distinction between transfer modes**: The compiled vs. learned relocation comparison, with the striking gap in efficiency (zero retraining vs. thousands of steps), is a conceptually important contribution.
- **Honest about limitations**: The authors acknowledge the gap to generation tasks and the preliminary nature of those experiments.

## Limitations

- **Synthetic tasks only**: All routing tasks are artificially constructed; transferability of findings to natural language routing in pretrained models is untested.
- **Limited model scale**: GPT-2-medium (355M) and Qwen2.5-3B are relatively small; scaling behavior is unknown.
- **Attention-only focus**: The analysis is restricted to attention head K/V states; MLP layers and other components are not examined.
- **Generation experiments are preliminary**: Only a single copy task is tested for generation, with mixed results (0.504 for c←KV).
- **Learned baselines may be suboptimal**: The large compiled-learned gap could partly reflect weaknesses in the specific learned methods rather than a fundamental distinction.

## Relevance

本文与机械可解释性（mechanistic interpretability）研究高度相关。核心发现——注意力头的K/V状态通过固定接口实现零重训练迁移——直接揭示了隐藏状态（hidden states）中的结构化路由机制。这对理解LLM内部如何编码和复用计算路径具有重要意义。特别是，K/V分裂诊断方法为分析隐藏状态的因果角色提供了新工具，可用于未来研究中的中介变量识别。编译式迁移与学习式重定位的区分也对模型编辑（model editing）和知识注入具有实际启示。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can compiled transfer be applied to naturally occurring routing circuits in pretrained models (e.g., induction heads, copy-suppression heads), or is it limited to trained routing tasks?
2. How does compiled transfer scale to larger models (7B, 70B+)? Does the fixed-interface property persist, or do more complex routing architectures emerge?
3. What role do MLP layers play in routing — could similar K/V-style state transfer apply to MLP subspaces?
4. Can the compiled transfer framework be extended to multi-hop reasoning tasks where routing chains across multiple layers are involved?
5. Is the learned relocation gap (+0.422 vs +0.068) fundamental, or could more sophisticated learned methods (e.g., meta-learning, in-context learning approaches) close the gap?
6. How does the Q×KV interface width requirement for generation tasks relate to the complexity of the generation target?
