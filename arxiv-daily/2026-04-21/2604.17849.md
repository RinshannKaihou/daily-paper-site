## 一句话总结

本文系统性地研究了计算机使用智能体（CUA）在任务执行中的不可靠性来源，将其分解为执行随机性、任务歧义性和规划变异性三个因素，并通过在OSWorld上的大量实验表明：指令澄清是最有效的可靠性提升手段（Kimi的McNemar b-c值达100），而确定性解码的效果因模型而异，整体揭示了当前CUA在可靠完成真实计算机任务方面仍存在显著差距。

## TL;DR

This paper systematically investigates why computer-use agents (CUAs) fail unreliably by decomposing unreliability into three factors: stochasticity during execution, ambiguity in task specification, and planning variability. Evaluating six models on OSWorld with Pass^k metrics and statistical tests (McNemar, Wilcoxon), the authors find that deterministic decoding is insufficient alone, environment perturbations significantly hurt frontier models, instruction clarification is the most effective intervention, and all models show substantial Pass^1-to-Pass^3 reliability drops (e.g., Claude Sonnet 4.6 from 0.702 to 0.612).

## 核心贡献

- **提出了CUA可靠性的形式化框架**：将计算机使用智能体的任务执行建模为POMDP（部分可观测马尔可夫决策过程），并明确区分"成功率"与"可靠性"——可靠性定义为所有执行都成功的概率，而非单次成功的概率。
- **引入三种可靠性评估指标**：Pass^k（多次运行全部成功的概率）、McNemar检验（衡量可靠性状态转换的统计显著性）、Wilcoxon符号秩检验（评估一致性改进的统计显著性），为CUA可靠性评估提供了严谨的统计工具。
- **系统性地分解不可靠性来源**：将不可靠性归因于三个因素——执行随机性（temperature采样）、任务歧义性（指令不明确）、规划变异性（推理链不稳定），并分别设计了针对性的干预实验。
- **全面评估六种主流模型**：涵盖GPT-5、Claude Sonnet 4.6、Kimi 2.5、Qwen-3VL-8B、OpenCUA和UI-TARS-1.5-7B，揭示了不同规模和架构模型在可靠性上的显著差异。
- **发现指令澄清是最有效的可靠性干预**：Retry(Clarify)策略在所有模型上均显著提升可靠性，Kimi的b-c值高达100，GPT-5达68，远超其他干预手段。

## Key Contributions

- **Formal reliability framework for CUAs**: Models CUA task execution as a POMDP M = (S, O, A, T, I, R) and distinguishes reliability (probability that ALL executions succeed) from success rate (probability that at least one execution succeeds), using the Pass^k metric: Pass^k = E_x[C(c_x, k) / C(n, k)].
- **Three statistical metrics for reliability evaluation**: Pass^k for measuring success probability under repeated runs, McNemar's test (chi^2 = (b-c)^2/(b+c)) for detecting reliability transitions between interventions, and Wilcoxon signed-rank test for assessing consistency improvements across task instances.
- **Systematic decomposition of unreliability factors**: Identifies and empirically validates three distinct sources: (1) stochasticity during execution from temperature-based sampling, (2) ambiguity in task specification from underspecified instructions, and (3) planning variability from inconsistent reasoning chains.
- **Comprehensive evaluation of six frontier models**: Tests GPT-5 (S3), Claude Sonnet 4.6, Kimi 2.5, Qwen-3VL-8B-Instruct, OpenCUA, and UI-TARS-1.5-7B on OSWorld, revealing that even the best model (Claude Sonnet 4.6) drops from Pass^1=0.702 to Pass^3=0.612.
- **Identification of instruction clarification as the most effective intervention**: The Retry(Clarify) strategy consistently improves reliability across all models, with McNemar b-c values reaching 100 for Kimi and 68 for GPT-5, substantially outperforming deterministic decoding, environment perturbation defenses, and plan refinement.

## Methodology

The paper formulates CUA task execution as a POMDP and introduces three complementary reliability metrics. **Pass^k** measures the probability that all k sampled executions of a task succeed, computed as E_x[C(c_x, k) / C(n, k)] where c_x is the number of successful runs for task x out of n total runs. The **McNemar test** uses a paired contingency table (pass-to-fail transitions b vs. fail-to-pass transitions c) with chi^2 = (b-c)^2/(b+c) to detect whether an intervention systematically changes reliability. The **Wilcoxon signed-rank test** assesses whether consistency improvements are statistically significant across task instances.

The authors evaluate six models on OSWorld, a benchmark requiring real desktop interactions (file management, application usage, web browsing). They run each task multiple times (n=5 for Pass^k computation, k=1,3,5) to measure reliability degradation from single-run to repeated-run settings.

Three categories of interventions are tested:
1. **Stochasticity reduction**: Deterministic decoding (temperature=0), strategy determinism (fixed action templates), and environment perturbation (cosmetic changes like wallpaper, font size, icon position).
2. **Ambiguity resolution**: Instruction clarification (LLM-generated questions + ground-truth answers), user simulator feedback (model-generated questions + model-generated answers during execution), and combined retry-with-clarification strategies.
3. **Planning stabilization**: Plan extraction (separate planning step before execution) and plan refinement (iterative improvement of extracted plans over multiple rounds).

## Results & Highlights

- **Reliability is a real problem**: All models show substantial Pass^1-to-Pass^3 drops. Claude Sonnet 4.6 (best baseline): 0.702 -> 0.612. GPT-5: 0.522 -> 0.395. Kimi 2.5: 0.500 -> 0.369. Even frontier models lose 10-13% reliability going from single to triple runs.
- **Deterministic decoding is model-dependent and insufficient**: Qwen-3VL-8B actually regresses with temperature=0 (b-c=-2), while OpenCUA (b-c=28) and UI-TARS (b-c=16) improve. This suggests stochasticity is not uniformly the problem — some models benefit from exploration.
- **Environment perturbations hurt frontier models**: Cosmetic changes (wallpaper, icon position, font size) significantly degrade performance. Claude drops with b-c=-20, indicating over-reliance on visual memorization rather than genuine UI understanding.
- **Instruction clarification is the most effective intervention**: Retry(Clarify) yields the largest reliability gains across all models. Kimi achieves b-c=100, GPT-5 b-c=68, meaning these models transition overwhelmingly from fail to pass when given clarified instructions. User simulator feedback during execution also helps but less consistently.
- **Plan refinement shows moderate gains**: Extracting and refining plans helps GPT-5 (improving across iterations) but shows mixed results for Claude and Kimi, suggesting planning variability is less tractable than ambiguity.
- **Small open models can rival frontier models with interventions**: With Retry(Clarify), Kimi 2.5 achieves comparable reliability to Claude Sonnet 4.6, suggesting that reliability bottlenecks are partly addressable without larger models.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are from UC Santa Barbara and the startup Similar (eric@similar.ai). Gonzalo Gonzalez-Pumariega and Saaket Agashe are researchers focused on AI agents and evaluation. Xin Eric Wang is a faculty member at UCSB working on vision-language models and embodied AI. The work benefits from access to multiple frontier models (GPT-5 S3, Claude Sonnet 4.6, Kimi 2.5) and the well-established OSWorld benchmark. The statistical methodology (McNemar, Wilcoxon) is appropriate and strengthens the empirical claims. The paper is transparent about limitations, including OSWorld-centric evaluation and the constraint of analyzing reliability at the episode level rather than individual actions.

## Critical Assessment

The paper makes a valuable and timely contribution by formalizing the reliability problem for CUAs, but several aspects warrant scrutiny:

**Statistical power concerns**: With n=5 runs per task and a subset of 69 OSWorld tasks, the sample sizes for McNemar tests can be small. McNemar's test requires sufficient b+c counts, and with only 69 tasks, some comparisons may lack statistical power despite reported significance.

**Intervention confounds**: The Retry(Clarify) strategy conflates instruction improvement with simple retry (more attempts). The paper does not fully disentangle whether the gains come from clarified instructions or from the increased opportunity to succeed. A Retry(Original) baseline with the same number of attempts but original instructions would strengthen the causal claim.

**Generalizability beyond OSWorld**: OSWorld is a Ubuntu-based desktop environment. Whether these findings transfer to web-only agents, mobile agents, or Windows/macOS environments remains an open question. The authors acknowledge this but it limits the scope of conclusions.

**Ground-truth clarification is unrealistic**: The instruction clarification intervention uses ground-truth answers to model-generated questions. In practice, users would provide the answers, introducing potential miscommunication. The user simulator experiment partially addresses this but uses the same model to both ask and answer questions, creating a circular evaluation.

**Missing cost analysis**: The paper does not report computational costs or latency impacts of interventions. Plan refinement with multiple iterations and retry strategies could be prohibitively expensive in production settings. A cost-effectiveness analysis would strengthen practical recommendations.

## Strengths

- Rigorous formal framework distinguishing reliability from success rate, with clear mathematical definitions (POMDP formulation, Pass^k metric).
- Three-factor decomposition of unreliability provides a structured understanding that can guide future research.
- Comprehensive evaluation across six models spanning frontier (GPT-5, Claude) and smaller open models (Qwen-3VL-8B, UI-TARS).
- Appropriate use of statistical tests (McNemar, Wilcoxon) rather than relying solely on aggregate metrics.
- Practical findings: instruction clarification is a low-cost, high-impact intervention that can be immediately adopted.

## Limitations

- Evaluation is limited to OSWorld (Ubuntu desktop), with unknown generalizability to web, mobile, or other desktop environments.
- Small sample sizes (69 tasks, 5 runs) may limit statistical power for some comparisons.
- The ground-truth clarification setup is unrealistic for deployment; user-provided clarifications would introduce additional noise.
- No analysis of computational cost or latency trade-offs for the proposed interventions.
- The POMDP formulation, while elegant, is not directly operationalized — the transition and observation functions are not explicitly modeled or learned.
- Plan variability experiments are less conclusive than the ambiguity experiments, suggesting the decomposition may not be equally tractable across all factors.

## Relevance

本文与ML研究中的智能体可靠性和鲁棒性评估高度相关。计算机使用智能体（CUA）是当前AI智能体研究的热点方向，本文提出的Pass^k指标和三因素不可靠性分解框架为智能体评估提供了新的方法论。指令澄清作为最有效的干预手段这一发现，对实际部署AI智能体系统具有直接的指导意义。研究还揭示了当前前沿模型在真实计算机任务中仍存在显著的可靠性差距，这对智能体安全性和可信赖性研究具有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How do the reliability findings transfer to web-only agents (e.g., WebArena, VisualWebArena) or mobile agents (e.g., AndroidWorld)?
2. Can the Pass^k metric be efficiently approximated with fewer than 5 runs per task to reduce evaluation cost?
3. What is the cost-effectiveness trade-off between instruction clarification and simply increasing model scale for reliability gains?
4. How would human-provided (rather than ground-truth) clarifications affect the intervention effectiveness, considering potential user errors or misunderstandings?
5. Can the three-factor decomposition be used to build a predictive model of which tasks are likely to be unreliable for a given CUA?
6. Do the environment perturbation findings suggest that CUAs are memorizing visual layouts rather than understanding UI semantics, and if so, how can this be addressed in training?
7. What is the relationship between per-action reliability and episode-level reliability — can action-level interventions compound to produce larger episode-level gains?
