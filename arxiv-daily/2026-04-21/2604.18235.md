> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 CalibAdv 方法，通过在 GRPO 训练深度搜索智能体时精细校准优势值（advantage）——对中间步骤进行软惩罚缩放、对最终答案步骤重平衡正负优势、以及解耦特殊标记与优势信号——有效缓解了正确中间步骤被错误惩罚和负优势主导导致的训练崩溃问题，在三个模型七个基准上平均取得 11.80% 的 F1 相对提升。

> **TL;DR:** CalibAdv calibrates the advantage signal in GRPO for training deep search agents by (1) softly penalizing intermediate steps based on document-retrieval correctness, (2) rebalancing positive/negative advantages at the final answer step, and (3) decoupling special tokens from advantage signals. It yields 11.80% average relative F1 improvement across three models and seven benchmarks while preventing training collapse.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示了 GRPO 训练深度搜索智能体的两大问题**：(1) 中间步骤正确性（如有效检索）与最终奖励信号之间的严重不匹配，导致大量正确的中间步骤被错误惩罚；(2) 正负优势值失衡导致训练不稳定，甚至出现灾难性语言能力崩溃。
- **提出 CalibAdv 优势校准框架**，包含三个互补组件：中间步骤软优势惩罚、最终答案步骤优势重平衡、以及特殊标记与优势信号解耦。
- **设计无标注成本的银文档代理（silver document proxy）**，通过比较错误 rollout 和正确 rollout 的检索文档集合，自动判断中间步骤的正确性，无需额外人工标注或外部 LLM。
- **在三个骨干模型和七个多跳 QA 基准上验证**，平均 F1 相对提升 11.80%，并完全避免了灾难性训练崩溃。
- **深入分析了训练崩溃的机理**：负优势主导导致模型熵上升、PPL 升高，最终退化为乱码和词级别重复。

## Key Contributions

- **Identifies two fundamental issues in applying GRPO to deep search**: (1) a substantial mismatch between intermediate step correctness and the reward signal, causing correct retrieval steps to be penalized when the final answer is wrong; (2) training instability and catastrophic collapse driven by dominance of negative advantages.
- **Proposes CalibAdv**, a three-component advantage calibration method: soft advantage penalization for intermediate steps, advantage rebalance for the final answer step, and decoupling of special tokens from advantage signals.
- **Introduces the silver document proxy** for step-level correctness estimation without additional annotations or external LLMs, by comparing documents retrieved by incorrect vs. correct rollouts within the same group.
- **Achieves 11.80% average relative F1 improvement** across three backbone models (Qwen2.5-3B-Base, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, Llama3.2-3B-Instruct) and seven multi-hop QA benchmarks, while preventing catastrophic training collapse.
- **Provides a detailed mechanistic analysis of training collapse**: tracks entropy, token probabilities, PPL, and the negative-to-positive advantage ratio, showing that sustained negative advantage dominance drives entropy up and leads to complete loss of natural language generation capability.

## Methodology

CalibAdv operates within the standard GRPO training loop for deep search agents and modifies the advantage assignment at three levels:

1. **Soft Advantage Penalization for Intermediate Steps**: For each penalized intermediate step (where the final answer is wrong), a correctness score `c_s = |D_step ∩ D_silver| / |D_step|` is computed, where `D_step` is the set of documents retrieved by that step and `D_silver` is the union of documents retrieved by correct rollouts for the same question. The negative advantage is then scaled: `Adv_calibrated(s) = c_s * Adv(s)` for `Adv(s) < 0`. Steps retrieving more silver documents receive less penalty.

2. **Advantage Rebalance for Final Answer Step**: The final answer step often exhibits severe imbalance with negative advantages dominating. To counteract this, positive advantages in the answer step are up-scaled by a factor `λ` (set to 1.5 in all experiments): `Adv_calibrated(s) = λ * Adv(s)` for `Adv(s) > 0`.

3. **Decoupling Special Tokens from Advantage Signals**: The `<think/>` token is prepended to the prompt rather than generated by the model, and no advantage is assigned to it. This prevents format collapse when negative advantages dominate and the model learns to avoid generating structural tokens.

Training uses a batch size of 512, learning rate of 5e-7, one epoch for Qwen2.5-7B and Llama3.2-3B, and two epochs for Qwen2.5-3B. Experiments are conducted on 8 NVIDIA H20 GPUs.

## Results & Highlights

- **Overall performance**: CalibAdv achieves an average 11.80% relative F1 improvement over standard GRPO across three models and seven benchmarks (2WikiMultiHopQA, Bamboogle, HotpotQA, MuSiQue, StrategicalQA, BeerQA, Brew).
- **Training stability**: CalibAdv completely avoids catastrophic training collapse that plagues standard GRPO, maintaining low PPL and proper output formatting throughout training.
- **Superiority over baselines**: Outperforms SimpleTIR (only delays collapse), LLD (limits exploration), MT-GRPO (requires tree-structured sampling), GiGPO (cannot address mis-penalization), and StepSearch (requires sub-question annotations).
- **Ablation study**: Each component contributes positively. Prepending the think token prevents format collapse (format score: 0.30 → 1.00). Soft penalization improves F1 from 42.34 to 45.74. Advantage rebalance further improves F1 to 48.96 and eliminates high-PPL outputs entirely.
- **Silver document proxy reliability**: 89% human-verified and 83% LLM-verified agreement for steps with `c_s = 1`. A replacement experiment using DeepSeek-V3.2 for scoring shows negligible performance difference (56.70 vs. 56.74) while adding 67% wall-clock time and 200% GPU cost.
- **Hyperparameter robustness**: λ in range [1.0, 2.0] consistently outperforms standard GRPO, with λ = 1.5 being optimal.

## Critical Assessment

The paper addresses a genuinely important and practical problem — training instability in GRPO for multi-turn reasoning agents. The proposed solution is well-motivated by empirical observations (entropy dynamics, PPL tracking, advantage ratio analysis). However, several aspects warrant scrutiny:

- **Generality beyond deep search**: The silver document proxy is specifically designed for retrieval-augmented settings where document overlap can serve as a correctness proxy. It is unclear how well CalibAdv generalizes to other GRPO applications (e.g., mathematical reasoning, code generation) where no such document-based signal exists.
- **Simplicity vs. novelty**: The three components — scaling negative advantages by a correctness score, up-scaling positive advantages, and prepending a token — are individually straightforward. The primary contribution lies in the careful analysis and combination rather than in any single novel technique.
- **Evaluation scope**: All benchmarks are multi-hop QA tasks. While this is the target domain, testing on more diverse RLVR tasks (math, code) would strengthen the claim that advantage calibration is broadly necessary.
- **The λ hyperparameter**: Although robustness analysis is provided (λ ∈ [1.0, 2.0] works), the optimal value of 1.5 was likely tuned on these specific benchmarks. Whether this transfers to other tasks or models is not established.

## Strengths

- **Thorough empirical analysis** of training collapse dynamics, including tracking entropy, PPL, token probabilities, and advantage ratios across training steps.
- **Principled diagnosis → treatment approach**: The paper first carefully analyzes why collapse happens (negative advantage dominance → entropy increase → language degradation) before proposing targeted solutions.
- **No additional inference or annotation cost**: The silver document proxy uses information already available during training (retrieved documents from correct rollouts), making the method practical.
- **Strong experimental coverage**: Three backbone models from different families, seven benchmarks, and six baselines provide a comprehensive evaluation.
- **Complete ablation study** with both quantitative metrics and training dynamics visualizations, clearly showing the contribution of each component.

## Limitations

- **Domain specificity**: The silver document proxy is limited to retrieval-based tasks. Extending CalibAdv to tasks without retrieval (e.g., pure reasoning, code generation) would require designing alternative step-level correctness signals.
- **Limited to outcome-level rewards**: The method still relies on outcome-level reward signals for the final answer. While it softens the penalty for intermediate steps, it does not provide truly dense process-level rewards.
- **Small model sizes only**: Experiments are conducted on 3B and 7B models. Whether the findings and solutions scale to larger models (70B+) remains unexplored.
- **Single-domain evaluation**: All benchmarks are multi-hop question answering. The training collapse analysis may not fully transfer to other GRPO application domains.
- **Heuristic correctness proxy**: While empirically validated, the silver document overlap metric is a heuristic. There may be cases where documents overlap but the step is not genuinely helpful, or conversely, helpful steps that retrieve different documents.

## Relevance

本文对 GRPO 训练稳定性的深入分析直接回应了 LLM 训练中的核心痛点——负优势信号主导导致的训练崩溃问题。CalibAdv 通过精细校准优势值来维持训练稳定性的思路，对任何使用 GRPO 或类似 RL 方法训练多轮推理智能体的场景都具有参考价值，特别是在需要平衡探索与利用的 LLM 强化学习训练中。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How would CalibAdv perform on non-retrieval RLVR tasks such as mathematical reasoning or code generation, where no silver document proxy is available?
2. Can the advantage rebalancing mechanism (λ scaling) be made adaptive during training rather than fixed, e.g., dynamically adjusting based on the observed positive/negative advantage ratio?
3. Does CalibAdv's analysis of training collapse dynamics generalize to other RL algorithms like PPO or REINFORCE when applied to multi-turn settings?
4. What happens when scaling to larger models (70B+)? Does the negative advantage dominance problem persist, diminish, or intensify?
5. Could the silver document proxy idea be extended to a general "step quality estimator" using learned models rather than document overlap, enabling broader applicability?
6. How does CalibAdv interact with other training stabilization techniques such as KL penalties, learning rate schedules, or reward shaping?
