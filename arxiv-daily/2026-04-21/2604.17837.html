Polysemantic Experts, Monosemantic Paths: Routing as Control in MoEs

arXiv: 2604.17837

Date: 2026-04-21

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17837

一句话总结： 本文提出一种无需参数的残差流分解方法，将MoE模型每层的隐藏状态正交拆分为"控制信号"（驱动路由决策）和"内容通道"（路由不可见），发现在6种MoE架构中控制信号逐层旋转而内容累积，使得单个专家虽多义但专家路径（trajectory）具有单义性——按语义功能聚类跨语言跨表面形式的token，从而证明MoE的可解释性基本单元是轨迹而非专家。
TL;DR: A parameter-free SVD-based decomposition splits each MoE layer's residual stream into a router-visible control signal and an orthogonal router-blind content channel. Across 6 MoE architectures, the control signal rotates layer-to-layer while content accumulates, forcing compositional specialization: individual experts are polysemantic, but expert trajectories become monosemantic, clustering tokens by semantic function across languages and surface forms.

核心贡献

  • 提出了一种基于SVD的无参数因果分解方法，将MoE模型每层的残差流正交分解为路由可见的控制信号和路由不可见的内容通道，数学上保证只有控制信号能影响专家选择。
  • 发现在6种MoE架构中存在一致的旋转计算策略：路由器放大已有高激活维度（信号放大，rho~0.6），控制信号逐层剧烈变化而内容通道跨层稳定累积，专家将内容转化为下一层的控制信号。
  • 揭示了专家路径的单义性：单个专家虽多义，但多层专家路径按语义功能聚类token，跨语言跨表面形式。控制子空间的聚类比内容子空间多4倍的唯一token种类，证明路径是MoE可解释性的自然单元。
  • 同一个token（如":"）在不同语境下（类型注解、引言冒号、时间分隔符）走完全不同的专家轨迹，证明路由基于语义功能而非表面形式。

Key Contributions

  • Introduces a parameter-free causal decomposition of the residual stream in MoE models, using SVD of the routing matrix to separate hidden states into orthogonal router-visible (control) and router-blind (content) channels with mathematical guarantees.
  • Discovers a rotating computational strategy conserved across 6 MoE architectures (7B-106B params): routers amplify already-dominant dimensions (rho ~ 0.6), control signals rotate sharply between layers while content accumulates stably, and each expert transforms content into the next layer's control signal.
  • Demonstrates that expert trajectories are monosemantic: while individual experts remain polysemantic, multi-layer expert paths cluster tokens by semantic function across languages and surface forms. Control-subspace clusters exhibit 4x more unique token IDs than content clusters at equal interpretability.
  • Shows that the same surface-form token (e.g., ":") follows distinct trajectories depending on contextual function (type annotation, introductory colon, time separator), proving routing is driven by semantic role, not surface form.

Methodology

The paper develops its analysis in three stages:

1. Causal Decomposition (Section 2): The routing decision at layer l is s_l = R_l^T h_l. Since this is a linear function, the router can only access information in the row space of R_l. The authors compute the SVD of R_l to obtain right-singular vectors V_l, then project h_l into: (a) h_l^vis = V_l V_l^T h_l (router-visible, the control signal) and (b) h_l^blind = (I - V_l V_l^T) h_l (router-blind, the content channel). By construction, R_l^T h_l^blind = 0, meaning the content channel cannot causally influence routing.

2. Computational Organization Analysis (Section 3): Across 6 MoE architectures (Qwen3-30B-A3B, GPT-OSS-20B, GLM-4.5-Air, OLMoE, Granite-4-Tiny, DeepSeek-v2-Lite), the authors: (a) measure correlation between per-dimension hidden-state magnitude and router-weight magnitude to establish signal amplification; (b) train logistic regression probes on top-2% dimensions vs random 2% to validate causal sufficiency; (c) probe the two channels at current vs next layer to demonstrate control rotation and content accumulation; (d) probe both channels for surface features (language, token ID, position).

3. Trajectory Analysis (Section 4): Using ~10M multilingual tokens (EN/ZH/ES), the authors extract top-1 expert paths over contiguous mid-layers (e.g., layers 8-16 for gpt-oss-20b). Tokens sharing identical paths form semantically coherent clusters that cross language boundaries. They validate the source by independently clustering tokens in each subspace (k-means, k=32) across layers and measuring lexical diversity: control clusters average 4.3 unique token IDs vs 1.1 for content and 1.4 for full representations.

Results & Highlights

  • Routers amplify, not integrate: Router weights concentrate on dimensions with highest activation magnitude (rho ~ 0.6 across all models). Probes on just the top 2% of dimensions predict top-1 expert with 38-78% accuracy; random 2% subsets achieve 1-4%.
  • Control rotates, content accumulates: h_l^vis predicts current-layer expert at ~99% but carries ~35% information about next layer. h_l^blind is the strongest predictor of next-layer routing (~65-75%). Cross-layer cosine similarity confirms: control is ephemeral, content is stable.
  • Surface features bypass routing: Language, token identity, and position are encoded primarily in the content channel, especially at middle layers. Routing does not read these features.
  • Paths cluster by meaning: Semantically coherent clusters include: {exhibition, Exhibition, Showcase, zhan, zhanlan}; {overseas, haiwai, jingwai}; {cholesterol, sugar, xuezhi, grasa} across three languages.
  • Control subspace is the source: k-means clustering in the control subspace yields 4.3 unique tokens per cluster vs 1.1 for content and 1.4 for full h_l, confirming that control encodes semantic function while content preserves lexical identity.
  • Colon token case study: The token ":" follows three visually distinct expert trajectories in Qwen3-30B-A3B depending on whether it serves as a Python type annotation, an introductory colon, or a time separator.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (High): The SVD-based decomposition of the residual stream into control vs content channels for MoE analysis is, to our knowledge, entirely novel. The finding that expert trajectories (not individual experts) are the natural unit of interpretability reframes how MoE computation should be understood. The observation that routing bypasses surface features and instead encodes abstract semantic function resolves several puzzles in prior work.
  • Rigor (High): The decomposition is derived from first principles (linearity of routing), validated with probe experiments, and tested across 6 diverse architectures spanning 7B-106B parameters. The causal claim is mathematically guaranteed, not statistical. The trajectory analysis uses ~10M multilingual tokens. Appendixes provide full derivations, model details, and per-layer breakdowns.
  • Reproducibility (Moderate-High): The decomposition itself is straightforward to implement (SVD of routing matrix + projection). All models are publicly available. No custom training or fine-tuning is required. However, the automated interpretability scoring for clusters is not fully specified, and no public code repository is linked.
  • Significance (High): This work provides a principled framework for understanding MoE computation and directly challenges the "expert as unit" paradigm. The control/content decomposition has immediate implications for model steering, pruning, and intervention. The trajectory perspective could reshape how the community approaches MoE interpretability.
  • Red flags: (1) The path analysis focuses on top-1 expert only; top-k routing (common in practice) may yield different trajectory structures. (2) The monosemanticity claim for paths is evaluated via cluster coherence and lexical diversity, not via downstream task performance or causal intervention. (3) The paper does not demonstrate that perturbing the control signal actually steers model behavior (acknowledged as open question). (4) The trajectory analysis is limited to mid-layers and one model for the detailed clustering; generalization across all layers and all models is not fully shown.

Strengths

  • Elegant, parameter-free decomposition grounded in the linearity of routing -- no training, no fitting, no assumptions beyond the architecture itself.
  • Comprehensive evaluation across 6 diverse MoE architectures, making the findings likely to reflect general principles rather than model-specific artifacts.
  • The rotating control signal mechanism provides a clean mechanistic explanation for why expert paths become monosemantic despite individual expert polysemanticity.
  • Strong multi-lingual evidence (EN/ZH/ES) demonstrating that paths cluster by semantic function across language boundaries.
  • The colon token case study (Figure 1) is a compelling, intuitive demonstration that routing is context-dependent, not token-dependent.
  • The paper clearly identifies what it proves (mathematical guarantee of the decomposition) vs. what it hypothesizes (implications for neural control).

Limitations

  • Top-1 routing only: The analysis considers only the top-1 expert per layer. Most MoE models activate k>1 experts (k=4-8 in the studied models), and the behavior of secondary experts may alter or dilute the trajectory structure.
  • No causal intervention: The paper demonstrates that the control signal causally drives routing, but does not show that perturbing the control signal changes downstream model behavior. The orthogonality of control and content is at the routing level; whether it holds through expert computation is unverified.
  • Mid-layer focus: The detailed trajectory analysis is conducted on mid-layers (layers 8-16 of 24 for gpt-oss-20b). Early and late layers may exhibit different patterns.
  • Cluster evaluation is indirect: Monosemanticity is assessed via lexical diversity (unique token IDs per cluster) and qualitative inspection of example clusters, not via rigorous automated scoring or task-based evaluation.
  • Scalability of path analysis: Exact path matching becomes infeasible with many layers and many experts; the exponential number of possible paths is not addressed.
  • No comparison with dense model baselines: The paper speculates that the state-instruction duality extends to dense models but provides no evidence.

Relevance

本文与机制可解释性（mechanistic interpretability）研究高度相关，提出了一种数学上精确的MoE残差流因果分解框架，并将MoE的可解释性基本单元从单个专家重新定义为专家轨迹（trajectory），对理解MoE模型内部计算组织和设计模型干预方法具有重要意义。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can perturbations to the control signal (h_l^vis) reliably steer expert selection and downstream generation without corrupting the content channel, as the decomposition suggests?
  • How do secondary experts (top-2 through top-k) contribute to the trajectory structure? Do they follow the same paths, or create parallel computational streams?
  • Does the control/content decomposition survive fine-tuning, RLHF, or other post-training modifications?
  • Can the trajectory perspective be used to identify and prune redundant experts, or to design more efficient MoE architectures with built-in compositional specialization?
  • Is there an analogous decomposition for dense transformer models where the "routing decision" is implicit in attention patterns?
  • How stable are trajectories across different datasets, domains, and prompt distributions?
