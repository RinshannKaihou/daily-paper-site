MM-JudgeBias: A Benchmark for Evaluating Compositional Biases in MLLM-Based Judgment

arXiv: 2604.18164

Date: 2026-04-21

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18164

一句话总结

本文提出了 MM-JudgeBias 基准，通过系统定义 9 种跨 3 个维度（完整性、一致性、鲁棒性）的组合偏差类型，并设计 Bias-Deviation (BD) 和 Bias-Conformity (BC) 两个互补指标，全面评估了 26 个多模态大语言模型作为评判者时的组合推理偏差，揭示了当前 MLLM 在多模态评判任务中普遍存在的严重偏差问题。

TL;DR

The paper introduces MM-JudgeBias, a benchmark of 1,804 multimodal evaluation samples covering 4 task types and 12 domains, designed to systematically measure compositional biases in MLLM-as-a-Judge systems. The authors define 9 bias types across 3 dimensions (Integrality, Congruity, Robustness) and propose two complementary metrics: Bias-Deviation (BD) for sensitivity to semantic disruptions and Bias-Conformity (BC) for stability under semantic-preserving perturbations. Evaluation of 26 MLLMs reveals that all models exhibit significant compositional biases, particularly in image neglect and image-misalignment, and that model scale does not linearly correlate with compositional integrity. Accepted at ACL 2026 Main.

核心贡献

  1. 系统性定义多模态评判中的组合偏差（Compositional Bias）：首次将多模态大模型作为评判者时的偏差问题形式化定义为组合偏差——即评判者在整合查询（Query）、图像（Image）、回复（Response）多个组件时无法正确推理的系统性倾向。将偏差归纳为 3 个维度、9 种类型：
  • 完整性维度（Integrality）：Query-Neglect（忽略查询）、Image-Neglect（忽略图像）、Response-Dominance（回复主导）
  • 一致性维度（Congruity）：Image-Misalignment（图像不一致）、Query-Misalignment（查询不一致）、Response-Misalignment（回复不一致）
  • 鲁棒性维度（Robustness）：Visual-Distortion（视觉扭曲）、Caption-Redundancy（描述冗余）、Style-Variation（风格变化）
  1. 构建 MM-JudgeBias 基准数据集：从 29 个源基准中精选 1,804 个多模态评估样本，覆盖 4 种任务类型和 12 个领域类型，并为每种偏差类型设计了专门的数据构建流程。
  1. 提出两个互补的评估指标：
  • Bias-Deviation (BD)：衡量模型对语义破坏性修改的敏感度，即模型能否检测到关键组件被篡改
  • Bias-Conformity (BC)：衡量模型在语义保持的扰动下（如视觉扭曲、风格变化）的评分稳定性
  1. 大规模实证评估：对 26 个 MLLM 进行了全面评估，包括闭源模型（Gemini、GPT、Claude 系列）、开源模型（Qwen-VL、InternVL 系列）和专用评判模型（Prometheus-Vision、LLaVA-Critic），揭示了模型规模与组合完整性之间不存在线性相关性等关键发现。

Key Contributions

  1. Formal definition of Compositional Bias in MLLM-as-a-Judge: The paper provides the first systematic formalization of bias in multimodal judgment as "compositional bias" — the systematic tendency where a judge fails to correctly integrate and reason over multiple components (query, image, response). This is organized into 3 dimensions with 9 specific bias types spanning Integrality (Query-Neglect, Image-Neglect, Response-Dominance), Congruity (Image-Misalignment, Query-Misalignment, Response-Misalignment), and Robustness (Visual-Distortion, Caption-Redundancy, Style-Variation).
  1. MM-JudgeBias benchmark dataset: A carefully curated benchmark of 1,804 multimodal evaluation samples sourced from 29 existing benchmarks, spanning 4 task types (VQA, image captioning, visual reasoning, multimodal dialogue) and 12 domain types, with dedicated construction pipelines for each bias type.
  1. Two complementary evaluation metrics: Bias-Deviation (BD) measures whether the judge detects semantically disruptive modifications to key components (higher BD = better sensitivity); Bias-Conformity (BC) measures scoring stability under semantic-preserving perturbations such as visual distortion or style variation (higher BC = more robust).
  1. Large-scale empirical evaluation of 26 MLLMs: Comprehensive evaluation covering closed-source models (Gemini-2.5-Pro/Flash, GPT-4o/4.1, Claude-Opus-4.5/Sonnet-4), open-source models (Qwen-VL-2.5/3, InternVL-3), and specialized critic models (Prometheus-Vision, LLaVA-Critic), revealing critical insights about compositional integrity in current MLLM judges.

Methodology

Task Formulation

The MLLM-as-a-Judge task is formulated under a pointwise scoring paradigm, where the judge model receives a triplet (query, image, response) and outputs a score on a 1-10 scale. Compositional bias is defined as the systematic deviation in judgment quality when the model fails to properly integrate all three components.

Bias Taxonomy (3 Dimensions, 9 Types)

  • Integrality Dimension: Tests whether the judge properly utilizes all input components. Query-Neglect and Image-Neglect test if removing/modifying the query or image changes the score; Response-Dominance tests if the judge relies primarily on response quality while neglecting multimodal context.
  • Congruity Dimension: Tests whether the judge detects mismatches between components. Image-Misalignment pairs the response with a semantically unrelated image; Query-Misalignment uses a mismatched query; Response-Misalignment swaps the response with an irrelevant one.
  • Robustness Dimension: Tests stability under semantic-preserving perturbations. Visual-Distortion applies image-level noise/compression; Caption-Redundancy adds redundant text descriptions; Style-Variation changes the linguistic style of the response.

Metrics

  • Bias-Deviation (BD): Computed as the expected score difference between original and semantically disrupted samples. A higher BD indicates the model successfully detects the disruption, reflecting better compositional integrity.
  • Bias-Conformity (BC): Computed as 1 minus the expected score difference between original and semantically preserved samples. A higher BC indicates the model is stable under perturbation, reflecting robustness.

Data Construction Pipeline

The benchmark construction involves: (1) selecting high-quality source samples from 29 existing multimodal benchmarks, (2) filtering for suitability across bias types, (3) generating perturbed variants using both automated pipelines and human verification, and (4) quality control through human annotation to ensure label accuracy.

Results & Highlights

  1. Universal compositional bias: All 26 evaluated MLLMs exhibit significant compositional biases across multiple bias types, with no model achieving consistently high BD and BC scores across all dimensions.
  1. Image-related biases are the most severe: Models are particularly susceptible to Image-Neglect (failing to properly utilize visual information) and Image-Misalignment (failing to detect when a response is paired with an unrelated image), suggesting that current MLLMs still struggle with deep visual grounding in judgment tasks.
  1. Scale does not guarantee compositional integrity: Larger models do not consistently outperform smaller ones on compositional bias metrics. Some smaller specialized models outperform larger general-purpose models on specific bias types.
  1. Reasoning mode improves reliability: Enabling reasoning/chain-of-thought capabilities generally improves BD scores (better detection of disruptions) but can sometimes reduce BC scores (less stable under perturbation), indicating a trade-off.
  1. Top-performing models: Gemini-3-Pro and Claude-Opus-4.5 demonstrate notably strong performance on specific bias types, particularly in integrality-related measures.
  1. Specialized critic models show promise but limitations: Prometheus-Vision and LLaVA-Critic show improvements over their base models in certain bias dimensions, but underlying base model capabilities remain a bottleneck.
  1. Asymmetric evaluation tendencies: Models tend to exhibit asymmetric biases — for example, being more sensitive to query modifications than to image modifications, suggesting inherent modality preference in evaluation.

Critical Assessment

The paper makes a significant contribution to the growing field of MLLM-as-a-Judge evaluation by identifying and formalizing a critical but previously underexplored failure mode — compositional bias. The strength of the work lies in its systematic approach: rather than ad-hoc bias detection, it provides a principled taxonomy with clear formal definitions, complementary metrics, and a carefully constructed benchmark.

However, the scope is limited to pointwise scoring (1-10 scale) and does not cover pairwise or listwise evaluation paradigms. The benchmark focuses on vision-language modality, leaving other multimodal settings (audio, video) unexplored. The perturbation-based construction methodology, while practical, may not capture all real-world failure modes of MLLM judges. Additionally, the metrics (BD and BC) capture aggregate tendencies but may miss nuanced, context-dependent biases that vary across specific domains or task types.

Strengths

  1. Principled and comprehensive bias taxonomy: The 3-dimension, 9-type taxonomy is well-organized, covering the key failure modes of compositional reasoning in multimodal judgment.
  2. Complementary metric design: The BD/BC pair provides both sensitivity and stability perspectives, offering a more complete picture than a single metric could.
  3. Large-scale evaluation: Testing on 26 diverse MLLMs provides strong empirical grounding and makes the findings broadly applicable.
  4. Practical impact: The benchmark directly addresses a critical gap in MLLM-as-a-Judge reliability, with clear implications for model selection and deployment in real-world evaluation systems.
  5. Clear formalization: The mathematical formulation of compositional bias and the metrics is clean and reproducible.

Limitations

  1. Scope limited to pointwise scoring: The benchmark only covers the pointwise evaluation paradigm (single response scoring), not pairwise comparison or listwise ranking, which are also widely used in practice.
  2. Vision-language only: The focus on image-text pairs means the findings may not generalize to other multimodal settings (video, audio, multi-image).
  3. Perturbation-based construction: The benchmark relies on specific perturbation strategies to create biased samples, which may not fully represent the diversity of real-world compositional failures.
  4. Limited analysis of mitigation strategies: The paper primarily diagnoses the problem but does not explore systematic approaches to reduce compositional bias in MLLM judges.
  5. Static benchmark: As MLLMs evolve rapidly, the specific perturbation strategies and bias magnitudes may become outdated, requiring ongoing maintenance.

Relevance

本文对多模态大语言模型作为自动评判者（MLLM-as-a-Judge）的可靠性研究具有重要意义。随着 MLLM 被越来越多地用于自动评估生成内容的质量，理解其评判偏差变得至关重要。本文提出的组合偏差框架不仅适用于评估场景，也为理解 MLLM 的多模态推理能力提供了新视角。研究结果表明，即使最先进的模型在整合多模态信息进行评判时仍存在显著缺陷，这对自动评估系统的设计和部署提出了重要警示。该工作与 RLAIF、自动化评测、多模态对齐等研究方向紧密相关。
