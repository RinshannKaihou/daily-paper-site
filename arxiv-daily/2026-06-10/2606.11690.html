Why This Matters
Self-hosting break-even is a surface in (λ, L, H) space — not a single token threshold. Organizations planning LLM budgets using any of 15+ surveyed calculators are systematically underestimating self-hosted cost by 1/U.
⚠ Critical Edge
Single author, no peer review. Only n=3 model architectures tested — insufficient for strong generalization. The 36.3× headline is a cherry-picked extreme (λ=1 rps); practically relevant enterprise range is 2.5–24×. I/O ratio fixed at 512:256.
Methodology

All 15+ surveyed public calculators (Helicone, LiteLLM, YourGPT, etc.) treat GPU utilization as a fixed input. The paper's key insight: utilization is a dependent variable driven by offered request rate λ via Little's Law (N ≈ λW).

C_eff = P_GPU × 10⁶ / (3600 × Θ_achieved(λ, L))
error = C_eff / C_naive = 1 / U

42 benchmark runs on H100 NVL 96GB, sweeping λ ∈ {1, 5, 10, 25, 50, 100, 200} rps across Llama 3.1 8B, Qwen3-30B-A3B, and Mixtral 8x7B. Cross-validated on 56 runs over A100 PCIe.

Poisson arrivals, synthetic 512-input/256-output tokens, no prefix caching, no speculative decoding — conservative upper bounds on cost dispersion. Total hardware cost: under $65.

Strengths
  • Addresses a real blind spot: every named public calculator treats utilization as fixed.
  • Memorable formula: C_eff/C_naive = 1/U — simple, actionable, directly usable.
  • Hardware-conditional FP8 finding: dense FP8 advantage inverts on A100 PCIe without native FP8 tensor cores.
  • Under $65 to replicate: unusually low barrier for a systems paper.
  • Open-source tool: vllm-cost-meter attaches to live vLLM Prometheus metrics, MIT licensed.
  • Crossover-as-surface framing: refutes the common "break-even above X tokens/month" heuristic.
Limitations
  • Single author, no peer review: experimental design gaps possible.
  • n=3 architectures: dense, sparse, ultra-sparse — insufficient for strong generalization.
  • Fixed 512:256 I/O ratio: long-context or summarization workloads may differ significantly.
  • Poisson only: burstier CV>2 traffic may amplify the penalty further.
  • H100 NVL + A100 PCIe only: H200, MI300X, TPU v5 uncharacterized.
  • On-demand pricing only: spot pricing (0.3–0.7×) shifts absolute crossover points significantly.
Open Questions
  1. How does the cost model extend to bursty workloads (Gamma/Weibull inter-arrival, CV > 1)?
  2. Can the framework produce SLO-aware crossover surfaces that account for TTFT_p99 and TPOT_p99 constraints simultaneously?
  3. Does the active-parameter-dominance finding (Qwen3-30B-A3B FP8 cheaper than Llama 8B FP8 at saturation) hold for denser sparsity ratios?
  4. How should multi-replica and auto-scaling deployments be modeled — the current framework assumes fixed hardware allocation?
  5. What is the precise hardware-capability threshold (native FP8 tensor cores) that determines whether FP8 benefits dense vs. MoE more?
