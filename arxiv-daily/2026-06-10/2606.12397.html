Methodology
Linear-Algebraic Router

By the Eckart-Young theorem, the best one-vector summary of an expert matrix is its principal singular vector — the direction maximizing the Rayleigh quotient. Conventional routers impose no constraint forcing their rows toward this direction, leaving a theoretical gap between the router and the expert it represents.

MPI closes that gap with a 2-step Power-then-Retract update applied to each router row R[i] against its expert gate weight W_g^i:

R̂[i] = R[i] · W_g^i · (W_g^i)^T   →   R'[i] = C · R̂[i] / ‖R̂[i]‖₂ ,   C = C'/√N
  1. Power step rotates the router row toward the dominant singular vector of the expert's gate weight matrix.
  2. Retraction renormalizes with a scale-invariant constant C' = 2 (default), decoupling routing-logit scale from expert count N.
  3. One iteration suffices — more iterations cost ~5% throughput and actually hurt downstream accuracy by ~1.4 pp.

The full modification is a 2-line change in the MoE forward pass (one matmul + F.normalize), compatible with Megablocks, and the paper proves the update is equivalent to steepest ascent on the spherical manifold under a minimal-update principle.

Strengths
Why it works
  • Theoretically principled — connects router design to Rayleigh quotient, power method, and manifold optimization with verifiable math.
  • Practically minimal — 2 lines of code, 0.2% training overhead, zero inference overhead.
  • Optimizer-agnostic — consistent gains across AdamW, AdamH, Muon, MuonH.
  • Honest ablations — reports the failure mode of over-iterating, building trust in the analysis.
  • Plug-in compatible — composes with z-loss (+0.68 pp) and Sigmoid activation.
Limitations
Caveats
  • Validation stops at 11B / 350B tokens — frontier-scale (100B+) behavior is unknown.
  • No direct head-to-head with other router improvements (expert choice, capacity factor, etc.) at matched compute.
  • Improved load balancing is tentatively attributed to retraction; mechanism deferred to future work.
  • Only FineWeb-Edu / English pretraining data studied — multilingual and domain-specific behavior untested.
  • Choice of W_g over W_p or W_o has only marginal empirical support and no theoretical justification.
Questions for Further Exploration
Open ends
  1. Does MPI improve expert specialization, or only routing accuracy over a fixed expert distribution?
  2. How does the method scale to N = 256 or N = 1024 experts, where the retraction constant C = C'/√N becomes very small?
  3. Can the power iteration be applied to combined expert matrices (e.g., W_g ⊗ W_o) for further alignment?
  4. How does MPI interact with expert merging or pruning, where router rows directly identify redundant experts?
  5. At trillion-token scales, does the 1.04× convergence advantage compound or saturate?
