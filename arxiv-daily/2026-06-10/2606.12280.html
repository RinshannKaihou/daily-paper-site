INT8 W8A8 quantization of Ideogram 4.0 matches FP8 image quality on Ampere GPUs — no FP8 tensor cores required.

arxiv.org/abs/2606.12280 ↗ | Deep Gandhi · Ali Asaria · Tony Salomone | Transformer Lab
Key Metric
+3.57
CLIP score gain: Q4_K vs NF4 at identical 10.4 GB on-disk size (CI [+2.65, +4.51], excludes zero). Pareto winner on quality–memory frontier.
Why This Matters
INT8 + GGUF Q4_K bring FP8-quality diffusion to consumer RTX 3090s — and the recipes transfer directly to FLUX and SD3-class architectures.
Critical Edge
INT8 is 6–12% slower than FP8 and NF4 on Ampere — quality is preserved but speed is deferred until a fused INT8 GEMM kernel exists.
Methodology
A
Target: 9.3B dual-branch flow-matching DiT
Ideogram 4.0 — 34-layer single-stream backbone, two separate weight copies (cond + uncond). 211 linear layers per branch quantized; VAE and Qwen3-VL-8B text encoder left at published precision.
B
INT8 W8A8 recipe
Per-channel static weight INT8 + per-token dynamic activation INT8 + SmoothQuant (α=0.5). Mixed-precision protection: top-17 fragility-ranked FFN down-projections kept in bf16 (~8% of linears).
C
Activation fragility profiling
Per-linear sweep of max-abs / std / kurtosis over 8 calibration prompts across denoising steps. Fragility score = kurtosis × max-abs. Ranking is trajectory-stable (Spearman 0.930 early vs late steps).
D
GGUF Q4_K — custom encoder
No public Q4_K encoder existed for DiTs. Authors implement NumPy Q4_K superblock quantizer + Torch dequant kernel, verified bit-exact against reference decoder.
E
Evaluation protocol
200-prompt quality benchmark (PartiPrompts, stratified) + 100-prompt OCR benchmark (63 machine-checkable). PickScore, CLIPScore, EasyOCR NED. 10,000-sample paired bootstrap 95% CIs throughout.

Strengths & Limitations
Strengths
  • Statistically honest: paired bootstrap CIs (n=10,000) throughout main results — exemplary for a PTQ paper.
  • Sharp ablation: N=17 protection knee is a concrete, reproducible engineering guideline.
  • Novel OCR protocol: per-category quantization analysis with machine-checkable EasyOCR targets — reusable for the field.
  • Toolchain gap filled: custom Q4_K GGUF encoder for DiTs released publicly.
  • Transparent about limits: explicitly states INT8 is not yet a speed or memory win on Ampere.
  • Weights released: HuggingFace under non-commercial license (transformerlab/ideogram-4-int8-w8a8).
Limitations
  • No memory win: INT8 is 18.6 GB — same footprint as FP8. Advantage deferred to fused kernel.
  • INT8 is slower: ~6–12% behind FP8 and NF4; torch.compile crashes on INT8 dequant kernel.
  • Single seed (1000): bootstrap CIs are over prompts, not seeds — seed-level variance uncharacterized.
  • Selection/eval overlap: N=17 cutoff chosen on 50-prompt slice that is a subset of the 200-prompt benchmark.
  • No independent hardware replication of latency numbers.
  • Architecture scope: validated only on Ideogram 4.0; transfer to FLUX/SD3 is asserted but not demonstrated.
Questions for Further Exploration
  • Q1 Does the N=17 FFN down-projection protection threshold transfer to other single-stream DiT architectures (FLUX.1, SD3-Medium), or is the sharp knee specific to Ideogram 4.0's training regime?
  • Q2 What latency reduction is achievable once a fused Ampere INT8 GEMM (CUTLASS/ViDiT-Q-style) is implemented? The quality substrate is established — the speed payoff is the natural next experiment.
  • Q3 How sensitive is final quality to calibration set size and diversity? The fragility profiling uses only 8 prompts — edge-case content (complex multilingual text, fine-grained textures) may shift rankings.
  • Q4 Does the INT8 OCR NED advantage over NF4 hold for non-Latin scripts (Chinese, Arabic, Devanagari), where glyph complexity is higher and quantization-induced blurring may be more damaging?
  • Q5 Could step-caching methods (TACache for flow matching) compose with the INT8 recipe without quality regression, making Q4_K's slower Torch dequant path competitive on latency?
  • Q6 Would a learned or Hessian-based layer selection criterion yield a smaller protected set with equivalent or better quality compared to the kurtosis × max-abs fragility score?
