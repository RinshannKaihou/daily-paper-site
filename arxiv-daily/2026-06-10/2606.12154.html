Remove the large model from runtime entirely — get 8.4× speedup and 131 tok/s on an 8 GB laptop

The Brain That Goes Quiet: Serving a Large Model's Knowledge at 131 Tokens per Second on an 8 GB Laptop by Removing the Large Model from the Runtime Path ↗
Myeong Jun Jo ANIMA Research · Independent
Key Metric
8.4×
latency reduction: 4,465ms → 518ms
throughput: 15.7 → 131.4 tok/s end-to-end (8GB GPU)
Why This Matters
The bottleneck was never the model's intelligence — it was the cost of asking. Offline harvesting + runtime silence enables 128k context for free on consumer hardware that couldn't serve 35B models at all.
⚠ Critical Edge
Closed-world only — refuses rather than improvises. Internal structured format not disclosed; the envelope-fidelity finding (most interesting result) can't be directly reproduced. BM25 routing collapses on colloquial phrasing. Single user; concurrency unmeasured.
Methodology — The Horsehair System
OFFLINE (brain works at night)
35B model reads source docs → extracts verified answers
→ structured knowledge store (each item: trigger + facts)
Verification gate: 10/10 corrupted blocked, 6/6 passed
RUNTIME (brain goes silent)
Query → BM25 router (2.4ms median, 563 entries)
→ deterministic renderer (zero model invocation)
→ MiniCPM5-1B 4-bit streams answer to user

Three different 35B-class models (Qwen3.6-35B-A3B, Gemma4-26B-A4B, GLM-4.7-Flash) all showed 4–7s end-to-end latency, confirming the bottleneck is structural — the act of invocation — not any specific model.

Hardware: RTX 4060 Laptop, 8GB VRAM, via ik_llama.cpp. Context from 4k to 128k tokens costs zero decode speed; VRAM grows ~22 MiB/1k.

Envelope Fidelity — Surprising Finding

Identical content, different packaging formats. Small model (1B) fidelity is entirely determined by the answer's wrapping format, not its content:

marker-line format
9/9
exact matches
Korean-field quoted
3/9
exact matches
JSON field
0/9
exact matches
quoted string
0/9
exact matches

The format was originally chosen for speed and auditability — not fidelity. The finding that it also preserves facts is accidental and generalizable: for small-model relay systems, answer representation format is at least as important as retrieval quality.

Note: 236-token repetitive answers collapse to 0.00 regardless of format. Non-repetitive answers up to 232 tokens survive fully.

Strengths
  • Honest writing: Section 7 documents three real failures; author states what novelty is and isn't claimed.
  • Envelope-fidelity finding: generalizable, unexpected, and practically important for RAG system design.
  • Context-length is free: 128k context window at zero speed penalty on 8GB.
  • Speed trilemma framing: speed-fidelity-auditability cleanly articulated as a practitioner design space.
  • Co-residency failure warning: large model "staying quiet" silently degrades into co-generation — critical pitfall documented.
  • Three metrics reported separately: E2E throughput, decode rate, and TTFT — with explicit explanation of why they differ.
Limitations
  • Closed-world only: no novel composition, inference, or open-ended QA. Refuses rather than improvises.
  • Vocabulary-dependent routing: 92.8% BM25 top-1 is on document-vocabulary queries; colloquial phrasing degrades it.
  • 17-document scale only: scaling to thousands of documents with millions of entries is unvalidated.
  • Format undisclosed: the envelope-fidelity result (best finding) cannot be directly reproduced.
  • Single user, single machine: concurrency behavior completely unmeasured.
  • Offline harvest cost unknown at scale: 17 documents is fast; thousands is not characterized.
Open Questions
  1. Why does marker-line format produce 9/9 exact matches while JSON produces 0/9 for identical content — tokenization artifact, training data effect, or something else? Does this generalize to other 1B-class models?
  2. What is the BM25 accuracy degradation curve as query phrasing becomes more colloquial? At what distance from document vocabulary does the confidence gate stop being effective?
  3. How does the system generalize to more ambiguous source types (meeting transcripts, email threads, informal notes)?
  4. What is the mechanism behind co-residency degrading into co-generation (Section 7) — is the large model invoked by the runtime framework, or some other coupling?
  5. How does this compare to a heavily quantized (2–4 bit) 35B model at the same hardware budget in per-query latency and coverage?
