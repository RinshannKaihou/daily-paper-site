Key Finding · arXiv 2606.11961

ICL hits an immovable categorical ceiling; LoRA breaks it only by crossing into memorization.

Categorical Prior Lock-in: Why In-Context Learning Fails for Structured Data / Pelusi · Braghin · Trombetta / 2026-06-10 / 13 pp. · ACM submission
in-context learning tabular synthesis LoRA LLM failure modes privacy / memorization
Key Metric
0.86↘
DCR ratio for Qwen LoRA 10% (0.8655) — every LoRA config drops below the 1.0 memorization threshold (0.8609 at 50%, 0.9512 Mistral 10%).
Why This Matters
Locally-deployed 7B models can't synthesize privacy-sensitive tabular data via ICL alone — and the LoRA "fix" silently memorizes training rows.
Critical Edge
Single credit-card dataset, 7B scale only. Mistral LoRA 50% collapses 100% of outputs, mechanism uninvestigated.

Methodology

方法
  • DatasetPublic credit-card transaction log, 7 numerical + 4 categorical features. job has 494 labels (max label 0.75%); fraud prevalence 0.58%.
  • ModelsQwen2.5-7B-Instruct & Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3 — chosen for local deployment under data-residency constraints.
  • StrategiesZero-shot, 1/5/10-shot ICL, LoRA at 10% & 50% data exposure. T=0.8, top-p=0.95, rep-penalty 1.05; 10,000 valid records per config.
  • MetricsError rate, per-feature TVD (50-bin), Pearson matrix error, DCR ratio, exact-copy rate, fraud prevalence.
  • GeneralizeZipf (α=0.5) synthetic categoricals at cardinality 50 / 100 / 500, 30 runs each — confirms lock-in is scale-invariant.

Strengths

优势
  • Single, well-characterized real-world dataset with interpretable metrics.
  • Honest disclosure of LoRA's costs (memorization, structural collapse) — no advocacy.
  • Cardinality scaling experiment (Fig. 2) makes the lock-in claim scale-invariant across 50/100/500 labels.
  • DCR + exact-copy rate cleanly separates distributional proximity from literal memorization.
  • Public code (v1.0.0), fixed seeds, fully described pipeline — directly reproducible.

Limitations

局限
  • Single dataset; the 494-label occupation field and spatial coordinates are unusually challenging.
  • 7B-scale only — frontier models with stronger instruction following may behave differently.
  • No constrained decoding, RAG, or intermediate adaptation strategies tested.
  • No membership-inference or attribute-inference attacks; privacy threat model undercharacterized.
  • Mistral LoRA 50% collapse is documented but not explained.
  • TVD is marginal-only; no Train-on-Synthetic / Test-on-Real utility measurement.

Questions for Further Exploration

待探索
  1. Does lock-in diminish at 34B / 70B / frontier scale, and what is the effective threshold where ICL begins to override pre-training categorical priors?
  2. Would constrained decoding (vocabulary restricted to the dataset's categorical label set) bypass lock-in, or is the failure upstream of token sampling in the probability distribution itself?
  3. Does the near-zero numerical TVD at 10-shot translate to acceptable Train-on-Synthetic / Test-on-Real classifier performance, making categorical lock-in less critical in practice?
  4. Can the Mistral LoRA 50% collapse be attributed to a specific training hyperparameter, and is it preventable with constrained output formatting during fine-tuning?
  5. How does DCR-based memorization risk compare to formal differential-privacy guarantees — is DCR < 1.0 a meaningful privacy boundary or an overly conservative proxy?
