Key Metric
90.3pp
Absence-TPR gap on Qwen3-4B:
RAG 99.0% vs gamma-LoRA 8.7%. Reverse asymmetry on presence-TPR: LoRA 73.7% vs RAG 35.3%.
Why this matters
Hands every team building personalized LLMs an experimental protocol for choosing parametric memory vs retrieval — and warns that heavier RLHF widens the calibration gap rather than closing it.
Critical Edge
The headline behavioral result does not replicate on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (Δ +0.003 nat/tok). The style claim is recipe-specific to Qwen3-4B; the n=50 synthetic corpus is also generated by one of the preference judges.
§01

Methodology

实验方法

Four substrates, 50 personae, three probes

All substrates evaluated on the same 50 synthetic personae, each with a 30-turn conversation history. Primary base model is Qwen3-4B-Instruct; Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct and Mistral-7B-v0.3 provide cross-model replication.

Three probes target different memory axes independently: behavioral (next-token log-likelihood on held-out continuations + blind LLM-judge preference at n=750 pairings); factual calibration (12 controlled yes/no probes × 50 personae × 4 configs = 2,400 records, presence and absence measured separately); and real-data transfer (LaMP-3 product rating, exact-match accuracy).

Substrates compared

  • B_nohistBase model, no user history — negative control.
  • C_ragBGE-large top-K=5 retrieval + fixed abstain clause.
  • C_loraPer-user gamma-LoRA, rank 64–128, q/k/v/o, 20 epochs.
  • C_lora_calibgamma-LoRA + same abstain prompt — isolates prompting.

§03

Strengths & Limitations

优势与局限

Strengths

  • Clean three-axis decomposition of a muddled aggregate metric; the 2×2 "no Pareto winner" figure anchors the paper.
  • Explicit §1.3 "What this paper does not claim" — unusually honest scoping for a single-author preprint.
  • Pre-registered falsifiers and substrate-controlled ablation C_lora_calib guard the main confound.
  • Causal band-zero intervention with layer- and projection-specificity controls goes beyond correlation.
  • Code and synthetic corpus released; compute is accessible (~14 GPU-hours on a single L40S or H100).
  • The diagnosed LaMP-3 negative is itself a contribution — null result with a named cause.

Limitations

  • Style advantage does not replicate cross-model — Qwen3-4B-specific.
  • Synthetic-corpus circularity: Claude Sonnet 4.6 generates the 50 personae and is also one of the preference judges.
  • n = 50 personae, n = 3 model families; no held-out human evaluation at scale (preliminary n=30 only).
  • Partial causal specificity: v_proj L21–35 carries ~57% of the absence effect; head-within-band isolation is future work.
  • Single LoRA recipe (rank 64–128, 20 epochs); prefix tuning, IA3, full FT not compared as substrates.
  • No privacy analysis on per-user LoRA adapters (differential privacy / membership inference).
§04

Questions for Further Exploration

待探索问题
  1. Does the style advantage of gamma-LoRA recover on Llama-3.1-8B with a different recipe (e.g., the KL anchor that fixes LaMP-3)?
  2. Is the alignment tax directional with model scale — heavier RLHF → larger calibration gap — across checkpoints of the same base?
  3. Does the synthetic-corpus circularity inflate the behavioral preference estimate? A held-out human A/B at larger n would settle it.
  4. Can the q_proj L21–35 intervention be used constructively — zeroing the band only for absence-type queries at inference as a lightweight hybrid?
  1. How does gamma-LoRA compare to external memory systems (Mem0, MemGPT) on the three-axis framework? Deferred to Appendix B.4.
  2. Does the absence-calibration gap close under explicit negative-evidence training (counter-examples in the fine-tune mix)?
  3. How does the substrate asymmetry behave under retrieval contamination — when facts ARE in the history but the RAG index drops them?
  4. Does the q_proj top-band localization survive at larger model scales (≥70B), or does it migrate to deeper layers?
