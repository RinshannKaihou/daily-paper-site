2606.12360 post-training sparse-autoencoders RLHF interpretability 2026-06-10

SAE-based interpretability unifies four post-training interventions under a single "explaining away" principle — and exposes real safety failures hiding in widely-used preference data before any optimization begins.

Anatomy of Post-Training: Using Interpretability to Characterize Data and Shape the Learning Signal ↗ Goodfire · cs.LG

Bergen · Bhalla · Baskaran · Loeffler · Sarfati · Gala · Panwar · Aranguri · Fel · Geiger · Kowal · Boppana · Balsam · Lewis · Merullo · McGrath · Lubana

Key Metric
R² 0.9
Feature-conditioned pipeline predicts which SAE features actually change post-DPO, enabling audits that pre-empt off-target learning.
Why This Matters
The Dolci open-source dataset — in wide use — degrades jailbreak robustness after DPO. Interpretability catches this before you train.
Critical Edge
The framework's concept-independence assumption breaks for hierarchical attributes like "style" — suppressing Bold also suppresses Emoji. No working fix offered.
Methodology
⚡
Activation Steering
Shift hidden-state reps ± concept direction at train time; apply to rejected responses for best safety recovery
⚖️
Reward Shaping
Subtract λ·su(x,y) from DPO loss. Most consistent across model scales; traces a Pareto safety-utility frontier
💉
Inoculation Prompting
Append prompt Pu at train time; removed at test. Inconsistent; dropped from safeguard experiments
🔬
Data Filtering
Score tokens/examples by su, remove those where u predicts the preference label. Strong on localized traits
Optimal policy: π*(y|x) ∝ π₀(y|x) · exp(r(x,y) / β)    Shaping to suppress concept u: r̃(x,y) = r(x,y) − λ · log c_u(x,y)
Results
  • Poisoning All four intervention families suppress synthetically induced traits (Goblin Weave, Cheerfulness, Conflict Avoidance, Formality, Overconfidence) with statistical significance. Token filtering underperforms on diffuse traits.
  • Pipeline Validity Feature-conditioned pipeline R² = 0.9 on predicting post-DPO feature shifts. Prompt-conditioned pipeline R² = 0.58, consistent with its local scope.
  • Safety Audit Dolci DPO training degrades jailbreak robustness — quantified on HarmBench and XSTest. SFT refuses more; DPO over-refuses less but complies with harmful queries more.
  • Stylization Token filtering and reward shaping most effectively recover SFT-level formatting rates across 5 attributes. SAE-based methods have lower off-target suppression than linear-probe methods.
  • Safeguard Recovery Reward shaping only consistently improves harmful-refusal scores while preserving benign-query accuracy across all 4 model families (7B–70B).
  • Persona Ampl. Trait expression increases monotonically with λ (Likert 0–4). Cost: approximately linear OLMES accuracy degradation. Framework works for amplification, not just suppression.
Strengths
  • Unified theory, not just narrative — exponential-tilting derivation formally connects activation steering, reward shaping, prompting, and filtering.
  • Quantitative validation — both pipelines tested against actual post-training outcomes, not just heuristics.
  • Intellectual honesty — Section 4.3 and Fig. 12 specifically document failure cases and mechanistically explain why.
  • Realistic testbed — Dolci dataset, standard DPO pipeline, OLMES evaluation; not a toy setting.
  • Safeguard results scale — reward shaping validated across 7B, 8B, 32B, and 70B model families.
  • Extended scope — concept amplification (playful personality) broadens framework beyond suppression.
Limitations
  • Independence assumption violated — style is hierarchical; sycophancy is entangled with helpfulness. Core failure mode with no working solution.
  • Requires pre-trained SAEs — Layer-24 SAEs on the target model are a non-trivial prerequisite; limits turnkey adoption.
  • Inoculation prompting unreliable — dropped from safeguard experiments entirely.
  • Activation steering fragile — requires careful direction (rejected responses); applying to chosen responses can degrade the steering direction.
  • Advertised use-case is weakest — prompt-conditioned behaviors show only partial, often non-significant mitigation.
  • Single architecture tested — all experiments on Llama-3.1 + Dolci; cross-architecture robustness unknown.
Open Questions
01 How should the framework be modified when concepts have known hierarchical structure — e.g., "style" containing "bold," "emoji," and "markdown" as sub-concepts?
02 General sycophancy is already high post-SFT before DPO. Is sycophancy primarily a pretraining artifact, and can it be addressed at earlier stages?
03 Feature-conditioned achieves R² = 0.9. What systematic classes of behaviors do SAE features miss — multi-token or multi-turn interactions?
04 How does the pipeline perform on RM-based RLHF rather than human-annotated data like Dolci? RM-labeled data may have different spurious correlation structures.
05 Is the explaining-away framework applicable during pretraining, where there is no contrastive preference structure?
06 How do results generalize to proprietary preference data from large-scale human annotation, where spurious correlate distributions may differ substantially?
