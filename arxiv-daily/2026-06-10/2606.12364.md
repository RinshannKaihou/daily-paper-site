> **一句话总结：** 本文在代码预训练、Transformer 蒸馏和时序基础模型三类复杂依赖任务上系统对比 xLSTM、Mamba-2 和 Gated DeltaNet，发现 xLSTM 全面领先，并通过统一形式化框架揭示其优势源于独立门控带来的"累加（accumulation）"与"有限状态追踪（finite-state tracking）"的同时支持。

> **TL;DR:** A head-to-head empirical comparison of xLSTM, Mamba-2, and Gated DeltaNet across code pre-training, Transformer-to-subquadratic distillation, and time-series foundation model pre-training shows xLSTM wins nearly every setting. A unified linear-attention formulation traces the advantage to two primitive capabilities — accumulation and finite-state tracking — that xLSTM uniquely combines via its decoupled input/forget gating scheme.

## 核心贡献

- 首次在超出标准英语网页预训练范围的三类复杂任务（代码预训练、大模型蒸馏、时序基础模型）上对 xLSTM、Mamba-2 和 Gated DeltaNet 进行严格的头对头比较，xLSTM 在几乎所有实验中胜出
- 提出将三种架构统一至线性注意力框架的形式化描述，以门控机制、归一化和覆写方式为分析维度，将架构差异归因于两个基础能力：**累加**（accumulation）和**有限状态追踪**（finite-state tracking）
- 设计受控合成任务（Majority 计数、Parity 奇偶校验等）验证上述假设，结果与理论预测完全吻合：只有 xLSTM[1:1] 能在两类任务上同时实现长度泛化
- 揭示 Mamba-2 因输入门/遗忘门耦合导致计数能力受限；Gated DeltaNet 因显式覆写机制有助于状态替换但损害累加能力

## Key Contributions

- **First systematic comparison** of xLSTM, Mamba-2, and Gated DeltaNet on complex-dependency domains (code + time series), going beyond the standard English-web benchmarks where architecture differences wash out
- **Unified linear-attention formulation** expressing all three models through a common notation of input/forget gates, normalization, and overwriting mechanisms, enabling principled mechanistic comparison
- **Mechanistic hypothesis and validation**: architectures differ primarily on two primitives — accumulation (counting-like unbounded length extrapolation) and finite-state tracking (ordered discrete state updates); controlled synthetic tasks confirm xLSTM[1:1] is the only model combining both
- **Practical design guidance**: hybrid m-heavy xLSTM[m:s] ratios (e.g., [7:1] for code, [3:1] for time series) use the efficient linear-attention accumulation block as the dominant component while adding a smaller recurrent component for state tracking

## Methodology

**Experiment 1 — Code-focused Language Model Pre-training (Section 2.1).**
400M-parameter inter-layer hybrid models are trained from scratch using `lm-engine`. All three models use 24 layers with 3 self-attention layers fixed; the tested subquadratic operator fills the remaining layers. Training data: Nemotron-CC-Code-v1 at 20B and 100B tokens, and a Nemotron-CC-Code-v1 + FineWeb-Edu mixture at 20B tokens. Primary metric: HumanEval pass@k for k ∈ {2, 8, 16, 64}. Secondary: HellaSwag, PIQA, ARC-Easy, ARC-Challenge, WinoGrande.

**Experiment 2 — Transformer Distillation (Section 2.2).**
Linearization-style knowledge distillation using Qwen3-4B-Instruct as teacher. Every multi-head attention block is replaced with an intra-layer hybrid: the tested linear-attention operator runs in parallel with sliding-window attention (window 512, 4 sink tokens), fused by a learned data-dependent gate. Stage I: per-layer MSE alignment with teacher attention outputs. Stage II: 0.9 CE + 0.1 KL with top-k=256 sparse teacher distribution over 10,000 steps. xLSTM[1:0] (mLSTM) and Gated DeltaNet are compared as drop-in matrix-state operators; Mamba-2 is excluded because its tied gates prevent reuse of teacher Q/K/V projections. Evaluated on HumanEval, HumanEval+, MBPP, MBPP+.

**Experiment 3 — Time-Series Foundation Model Pre-training (Section 2.3).**
Protocol of Auer et al. (2025) is followed, varying only the sequence mixer. Models trained at 1M, 4M, 10M, 40M, 80M parameters. Evaluated zero-shot on GIFT-Eval (heterogeneous forecasting benchmark spanning multiple domains and frequencies). Metrics: MASE and CRPS aggregated by geometric mean.

**Mechanistic Analysis (Section 3).**
A unified recurrence notation expresses all three models as variations of linear attention with gating. Key distinctions:
- xLSTM[1:0] (mLSTM): independent input gate (exponential, softmax-like over time) and forget gate (sigmoid); enables flexible down-weighting/overwriting of old values
- Mamba-2: tied input and forget gates (derived from zero-order hold discretization of SSMs); behaves like a GRU and inherits counting limitations
- Gated DeltaNet: independent gates like xLSTM but adds an explicit orthogonal projection overwrite (`I − k_t⊗k_t/‖k_t‖²`), which helps retrieval but interferes with accumulation

**Synthetic Validation (Section 4).**
Models trained at sequence length 128, evaluated at 128, 512, 2048. Counting tasks: A^n B^n, A^n B^n C^n, Majority. State-tracking tasks: Parity, Modular Arithmetic (Z_5), word-problem evaluation in symmetric group S_3.

## Results & Highlights

**Code Pre-training:**
- xLSTM[7:1] leads at every pass@k and every training configuration
- At pass@64 / 20B tokens: xLSTM improves over next-best by **+1.43 points**; at 100B tokens gap narrows to +0.90 points
- Runner-up on code-only corpus: Gated DeltaNet; on mixed corpus: Mamba-2 is competitive
- On reasoning/commonsense benchmarks (HellaSwag, PIQA, ARC-*), xLSTM leads aggregate but margins are smaller (under 0.1 HumanEval points at 20–100B tokens)

**Distillation (pass@1):**
| Student | HumanEval | HumanEval+ | MBPP | MBPP+ | Avg. |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Qwen3-4B-Instruct (teacher) | 0.914 | 0.835 | 0.708 | 0.847 | 0.826 |
| xLSTM[1:0] | **0.831** | **0.764** | **0.689** | 0.788 | **0.768** |
| Gated DeltaNet | 0.802 | 0.739 | 0.677 | **0.802** | 0.755 |
| Gated DeltaNet [−1,1] | 0.813 | 0.745 | 0.671 | 0.796 | 0.756 |

**Time-Series (GIFT-Eval):**
- xLSTM[3:1] leads from 1M to 40M parameters on both MASE and CRPS
- At 10M parameters: xLSTM reaches 0.733 MASE / 0.508 CRPS vs. Mamba-2's 0.767 / 0.525
- At 80M parameters, models nearly converge; Mamba-2 leads CRPS by 0.005

**Synthetic Tasks:**
- Mamba-2: A^n B^n accuracy drops from 1.000 (length 128) to 0.241 (length 2048); Parity never exceeds 0.352
- Gated DeltaNet default: solves easiest counting variant but degrades to 0.268 on Majority at length 2048; never solves state tracking
- Gated DeltaNet [−1,1]: recovers Parity and S_3 in-distribution but partial length generalisation (Parity drops to 0.472 at length 2048)
- xLSTM[1:0]: length-generalises on every counting task (0.892 on A^n B^n, 0.763 on Majority at length 2048) but fails state tracking
- xLSTM[1:1]: **solves all three state-tracking tasks and retains useful counting extrapolation** — the only model combining both primitives

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. The empirical benchmark alone would be incremental, but the unified formulation and mechanistic decomposition into "accumulation vs. finite-state tracking" is a genuine conceptual contribution. The synthetic task design cleanly isolates the two primitives.
- **Rigor:** The matched-recipe design is methodologically sound — same lm-engine, same data, same training budget, only the operator changes. Parameter counts are matched in the time-series sweep. However, the code pre-training comparison is limited to 400M parameters; behavior at 1B+ is untested. The distillation experiment wisely excludes Mamba-2 for principled reasons, but this narrows the comparison.
- **Reproducibility:** Implementation relies on `lm-engine` and the linearization recipe of Hauzenberger et al. (2026). Neither paper nor arXiv listing mentions public code or model releases. NXAI has released xLSTM code in the past but this is not confirmed here.
- **Significance:** High if the mechanistic interpretation holds broadly. The claim that xLSTM's advantage comes from combining accumulation and state tracking — rather than from scale or data curation — is a falsifiable hypothesis that future work can test. The finding also provides actionable guidance: use m-heavy xLSTM[m:s] for complex-dependency tasks.
- **Red flags:**
  1. Clear conflict of interest: the xLSTM team benchmarks xLSTM and wins. Independent replication is essential.
  2. Benchmark selection (code + time series) may favor xLSTM's particular inductive biases; results on long-document NLP or retrieval-heavy tasks are absent.
  3. Code experiments are 400M only; scaling behavior is shown only for time series.
  4. Mamba-2 is excluded from the distillation experiment, reducing the three-way comparison to two models in that setting.
  5. The gap between architectures narrows at 80M parameters in time-series and at 100B tokens in code, raising questions about whether advantages persist at production scale.

## Strengths

- Moves beyond leaderboard reporting to provide a mechanistic, falsifiable explanation of performance differences
- Unified linear-attention formulation is clean and mathematically precise, bridging SSM and linear-attention notation traditions
- Synthetic task suite is well-designed: Majority (accumulation), Parity/S_3 (state tracking), and counting variants cleanly isolate the two primitives
- Three independent evaluation settings (pre-training, distillation, TSFM) all point in the same direction, strengthening the empirical case
- Explicitly discusses failure modes of competing models (Mamba-2 GRU-like limitation, Gated DeltaNet overwrite interference)
- Hybrid architecture design rationale (xLSTM[7:1], xLSTM[3:1]) is directly actionable

## Limitations

- **Conflict of interest** is the dominant concern: all authors are the xLSTM development team
- Code pre-training experiments are limited to 400M parameters; larger-scale behavior is unknown
- Mamba-2 excluded from distillation (for valid reasons, but limits generalizability of that experiment)
- Performance gaps narrow at large scale (80M TSFM, 100B code tokens), potentially vanishing at 1B+ models
- No inference speed or memory footprint comparison — critical for practical deployment decisions
- RWKV, RetNet, Griffin, and other subquadratic families are not included
- No public model/code release mentioned

## Relevance

对研究高效序列建模和 Transformer 替代架构的读者具有很高参考价值：统一形式化框架和"累加 vs. 有限状态追踪"的双原语分解，为理解和设计次二次方架构提供了清晰的理论透镜，在代码生成和时序预测两个实用域的实证结果也提供了直接的部署参考。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Do the performance advantages of xLSTM persist at 1B–7B parameter scale, or do architectural differences wash out as seen at 80M in the time-series experiments?
2. How do the three architectures compare on retrieval-heavy and long-document NLP tasks, where Gated DeltaNet's explicit overwrite mechanism might be advantageous?
3. Can the mechanistic analysis extend to other subquadratic families (RWKV, RetNet, Griffin) using the same unified formulation?
4. What are the wall-clock training and inference throughput differences between architectures at matched parameter counts?
5. Is the advantage of xLSTM[1:1] on synthetic state-tracking tasks preserved at very long sequences (>10K tokens) beyond the 2048 evaluation length tested?
6. Are model checkpoints or training code publicly released, enabling independent replication of the key results?
