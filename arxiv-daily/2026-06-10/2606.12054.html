Isotropic Gaussian noise, one forward pass per step, is enough.

Simplicity Suffices for Parameter Noise Injection in Stochastic Gradient Descent

why this matters

A clean, single-pass batching trick for per-example weight noise — and empirical evidence that sophisticated noise schedules pay nothing over a constant-variance Gaussian on three standard benchmarks.

critical edge

The ENSGD identity is proved only for fully-connected layers; transfer to convolutions or transformers is unproven, and ImageNet / LLM fine-tuning is untested.

Methodology 核心方法

Optimize the smoothed loss LSZ(θ) = EZ[LS(θ − Z)] by injecting per-example weight perturbations without breaking the batched matmul.

The trick — Theorem 1

For a linear layer Lk(X) = σ(X Wk), applying the same Z to every example couples them. Inject noise into the output and rescale:

x · Z  ≡  𝒩d(0, σ² ‖x‖² · I)  ⟶  ψi = 𝒩d(0, σ²(‖xi‖² + 1) · I)

Each ψi draws element-wise in parallel → the batch remains a single matrix multiply, with every example uniquely perturbed.

Four noise parameterizations, head-to-head

  • Isotropic — constant σ across params and steps.
  • Moving — σ scaled by EMA of last 5 gradient magnitudes.
  • SqGrad — σ scaled by Adam's second moment, then normalized.
  • InvSqGrad — σ scaled by the inverse of Adam's second moment, then normalized.

σ tuned via validation on a log-uniform grid σ ∈ [0.00125, 0.08]; results = best mean test 0-1 loss over 5 seeds.

Strengths 优点

  • Clean theoretical result (Theorem 1) with a direct algorithmic payoff.
  • Honest scoping — authors flag conv layers and larger benchmarks as future work.
  • "Simplicity suffices" is actionable for practitioners tuning noise schedules.
  • Cross-domain coverage: vision (CIFAR-100) and text (AG News, IMDB).
  • No overclaiming: conclusion stays inside parameter noise for SGD, not gradient noise or other regularizers.

Limitations 局限

  • Architectures tested: small CNN (width 120) and MLPs — no transformer / ViT / LLM fine-tune.
  • ENSGD identity proved only for fully-connected layers; conv extension deferred.
  • Single image dataset, two text datasets; text gains ≤ 0.11 pp because TF-IDF landscapes are already smooth.
  • No theoretical account of why isotropic suffices — purely empirical.
  • No code released; reproducibility relies on written descriptions alone.
  • Comparison restricted to Gaussian families — Laplacian / heavy-tailed variants not evaluated.

Questions for further exploration 延伸问题

  1. Does the isotropic-is-sufficient conclusion hold for transformer architectures (ViT on ImageNet, GPT fine-tuning) where the loss landscape geometry is qualitatively different?
  2. Can Theorem 1 be extended to convolutional layers with weight sharing, and what is the equivalent distributional identity?
  3. How does ENSGD compare to sharpness-aware minimization (SAM) — also perturbs parameters, optimizes a different objective. Cheaper approximation?
  4. Why does InvSqGrad perform substantially worse on CIFAR-100 (0.6456 vs 0.6241) yet comparably on text — is this Adam's second-moment normalization fighting the per-param scaling?
  5. If noise were injected at the gradient level (perturbed-gradient descent) rather than the parameter level, do the conclusions survive given the theoretical equivalence under certain conditions?
