> **一句话总结：** 本文通过六项严格控制（OOD训练、多裁判、简单基线、混淆因子、自举置信区间、随机种子方差）系统评估15种解码时真实性方法，发现在指令微调LLM上所有token级方法均无统计显著增益，简单CoT提示反而以+5.6–19pp稳居最优。

> **TL;DR:** This paper runs a rigorous six-control evaluation of 15 decoding-time truthfulness methods (DoLA, ITI, contrastive decoding, learned logit adapters, CoT, self-critique) across 5 models and 3 benchmarks, finding that previously reported gains of 10–30pp shrink to near zero or negative under strict controls on instruction-tuned LLMs, while training-free chain-of-thought prompting remains the only robust winner.

## 核心贡献

- 提出六控制评估框架（C1–C6），分别针对六种常见失效模式：训练集污染、裁判选择偏差、缺乏简单基线、混淆因子（长度/拒绝率）、统计噪声、随机种子方差
- 在5个模型（1B–70B，Llama和Qwen两个系列）、3个基准（TruthfulQA、HaluEval QA、TriviaQA）、15种方法上完成120+受控实验，是迄今最系统的对比研究
- 发现单一评估协议变化（如从标准协议切换到严格协议）可将同一方法的表现从+7.5pp逆转为−2.0pp，量化了五类评估敏感性的具体影响
- 发布七点评估清单，为未来真实性研究提供可执行的方法论标准
- 确认CoT提示是在指令微调模型上唯一稳健有效的方法，且增益来源于激活推理而非响应长度

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a **six-control evaluation framework** addressing contamination (C1), judge bias (C2), missing baselines (C3), confound controls (C4), bootstrap CIs (C5), and seed variance (C6) — each shown to independently change the sign or magnitude of results
- Demonstrates that the +7.5pp apparent gain of a learned logit adapter (TRACE-LD) collapses to −2.0pp (p=0.23) under strict controls on TruthfulQA (N=817), and no token-level method achieves statistical significance
- Shows that **benchmark contamination alone inflates gains 6×** (27.2% overlap → +7.5pp; OOD training → +1.0pp; dose-response confirms the effect is memorization, not domain match)
- Identifies **judge choice as a sign-reversal factor**: Llama-3.3-70B scores TRACE-LD at −3pp relative to greedy; Qwen-235B scores it at −21pp; prompt phrasing alone shifts absolute scores by 7pp on identical outputs
- Establishes that **simple temperature tuning (T=0.3, +2.0pp) matches all learned methods**, making them appear trivially useful unless compared against this zero-cost baseline
- Provides mechanistic analysis of token-level intervention failure: TRACE-LD changes 15.3% of token positions but gate averages α=0.997 (nearly always fully active), indicating diffuse untargeted perturbation; 43-configuration ablation shows downstream %Both stays within a 4pp band regardless of hyperparameters
- Confirms **self-critique and CoT are the only methods with consistent positive effects** (+3.1pp and +5.6–19pp respectively across benchmarks), with CoT's gain confirmed to stem from reasoning engagement, not response length (concise CoT at 245 chars vs. greedy at 554 chars still gains +14.0pp)
- Releases a **seven-point evaluation checklist** for future truthfulness research

## Methodology

**Experimental scope.** Five models: Llama-3-8B-Instruct (61% TruthfulQA baseline), Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct (76%), Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct, Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct (API), and Llama-3-8B base. Three benchmarks: TruthfulQA (N=817, resistance to common misconceptions), HaluEval QA (N=500, knowledge-grounded hallucination), TriviaQA (N=500, open-domain factual recall). Fifteen methods in four categories: (i) simple decoding — temperature and top-p sweeps; (ii) prior methods — DoLA (7 layer configs), ITI (α∈{0.5,1.0,5.0}), contrastive decoding (α∈{0.1–2.0}); (iii) learned adapters — TRACE-LD (2.3M params), HiddenSteer, LoRA (6.8M); (iv) deliberative — CoT, self-critique, and five ablation variants.

**Six controls in detail.**
- **C1 (OOD training):** Learned adapters trained on HaluEval QA (512 examples, 0% TruthfulQA overlap). Contamination can inflate gains 6×; dose-response table shows 27% overlap → −1.5pp, 0% overlap → +1.0pp.
- **C2 (Multiple judges):** Llama-3.3-70B and Qwen-235B, with 3 prompt variants each. Judge choice can reverse the sign of improvements; prompt phrasing alone shifts absolute scores by 7pp.
- **C3 (Simple baselines):** Temperature and top-p sweeps as zero-cost references. T=0.3 achieves +2.0pp, matching all learned adapters.
- **C4 (Confound controls):** Length-matched and non-refusal subsets evaluated separately. TRACE-LD's +1.0 advantage drops to +0.5 on length-matched responses and reverses to −2.2pp on non-refusal subsets.
- **C5 (Bootstrap CIs):** 95% CIs from 10k resamples. Bootstrap CI for TRACE-LD minus greedy contains zero; only self-critique reaches p<0.05 at full scale.
- **C6 (Seed variance):** 3 evaluation seeds establish ±1.3% noise floor, matching the largest token-level improvements observed.

**Deliberative method analysis.** Five CoT/self-critique variants tested on N=200 TruthfulQA with Llama-8B: single-pass CoT (73.0%, +12pp), critique→revise (73.5%, +12.5pp), rewrite w/o critique (73.5%, +12.5pp), answer→explain (59.5%, −1.5pp), verify-only (8.0%, catastrophic failure). Single-pass CoT matches 2-pass self-critique, and rewriting without explicit critique matches full critique-then-revise — confirming the gain comes from engaging reasoning, not the multi-pass mechanism or explicit self-criticism.

## Results & Highlights

**Main finding — token-level methods fail under strict controls:**
- On full TruthfulQA (N=817), no token-level method achieves statistically significant improvement. The greedy baseline is 61.1% [57.8, 64.4]. T=0.3 yields +2.0pp, matching all learned methods.
- TRACE-LD's apparent +1.0 at N=100 reverses to **−2.0pp (p=0.23, 72 wins vs. 88 losses)** at full scale.
- DoLA (best of 7 configs): −1.0pp. Contrastive decoding (α=0.5): −2.0pp. ITI (best): −2.0pp. LoRA (6.8M): −10.0pp.
- Power analysis: MDE = 4.8pp at 80% power, meaning effects of ±2pp cannot be reliably detected at typical sample sizes.

**Contrastive decoding tradeoff:** Increasing α from 0.1 to 2.0 raises %Truthful from 84% to 93% but drops %Informative from 76% to 42%; net %Both falls from 66% to 40%. Being more cautious does not equal being more factual.

**Cross-benchmark results (Table 6):**
- On Llama-3-8B: TRACE-LD shows consistent degradation across all three benchmarks (−1.4 to −5.8pp); CoT improves all three (+5.6 to +19.4pp).
- On Qwen2.5-7B: TRACE-LD mildly positive on TruthfulQA (+3.9) but neutral-to-negative on HaluEval (−0.5) and TriviaQA (−0.8). Self-critique: dramatic gains on HaluEval (+30.5pp) but near-zero on TriviaQA (−0.3pp). No single method is universally effective — effects are regime-dependent rather than method-dominant.

**Self-critique scaling:** +6pp (1B), +3pp (8B), +6pp (7B), +13pp (70B). Larger models benefit more from deliberative reasoning, but CoT magnitude varies with prompt-benchmark alignment and inversely with model strength (+16pp at 63% baseline, +2pp at 90% baseline).

**Seven-point checklist (Table 8):** (1) Report train/eval overlap; (2) multi-judge agreement; (3) ≥2 judge prompt variants; (4) beat T/top-p sweeps under matched conditions; (5) length-matched and non-refusal subsets; (6) paired bootstrap CIs ≥1,000 resamples; (7) seed variance ≥3 seeds.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The paper's contribution is methodological rather than technical — it does not propose a new method but systematically audits existing ones. This is genuinely valuable and underrepresented in the field. The six-control framework is well-structured and each control is motivated by a specific failure mode with quantitative evidence.

**Rigor:** Commendably rigorous for a single-author study. The dose-response contamination experiment (Table 4), 43-configuration adapter ablation (Fig. 2), 10k-resample bootstrap CIs, seed variance measurement, and multi-judge evaluation are all methodologically sound. The finding that the same protocol change converts +7.5pp to −2.0pp is striking and well-documented.

**Reproducibility:** Good in principle. Feature extraction, training procedures, and evaluation protocols are described in sufficient detail. However: DoLA and ITI are reimplementations (not official code), which introduces implementation fidelity as a threat the paper honestly acknowledges. The seven-point checklist is released as a community artifact.

**Significance:** High. The paper makes a concrete, falsifiable case that the 10–30pp gains previously reported for token-level truthfulness methods were largely measurement artifacts on base models that do not transfer to modern instruction-tuned models. If the findings hold up, this reframes a substantial body of prior work and shifts the research agenda toward reasoning-based approaches.

**Red flags:**
- Single-author, independent researcher — no institutional affiliation or co-author cross-check
- Only two model families (Llama and Qwen) at 1B–70B; other architectures (Mistral, Gemma, Phi) are untested
- LLM-as-judge evaluation is used throughout despite the paper identifying judge bias as a key sensitivity — multi-judge mitigation helps but does not eliminate the problem
- N=200 for deliberative method analysis (Table 7) is underpowered given MDE=4.8pp at N=200, 80% power; some conclusions in Section 4.5 should be treated as exploratory
- CoT's effectiveness is partly confounded by prompt-benchmark alignment (the paper acknowledges this), meaning the practical recommendation to "use CoT" is model- and context-dependent

## Strengths

- Genuinely the most comprehensive controlled evaluation of decoding-time truthfulness methods on instruction-tuned models to date (120+ experiments, 5 models, 3 benchmarks, 15 methods)
- Each of the six controls is demonstrated quantitatively to change conclusions, not just argued theoretically
- The contamination dose-response experiment (Table 4) is elegant and decisive
- Honest about scope limitations and threats to validity in the Discussion
- The seven-point checklist is a practical, citable contribution for reviewers and authors
- Concise CoT ablation (245 chars vs. 554 chars greedy, still +14pp) cleanly rules out response-length as a confound

## Limitations

- Scope limited to two model families; generalization to other architectures unverified
- All evaluation is LLM-as-judge; human evaluation is absent, and the paper's own evidence shows judge choice can reverse conclusions
- Deliberative method results (Section 4.5) are largely from N=200 subsets, below the paper's own MDE threshold for reliable detection
- No analysis of why CoT degrades at high-baseline models (+2pp at 90%) — this is an important practical boundary condition left uncharacterized
- TRACE-LD and other adapters are evaluated only at their published configurations; the search over learned adapter architectures may not be exhaustive
- The paper does not investigate hybrid approaches (token-level + reasoning), which the Discussion flags as future work but does not explore

## Relevance

本文直接挑战了解码时幻觉抑制方法（DoLA、ITI、对比解码）在现代指令微调模型上的有效性假设，并以严格受控实验表明CoT提示是目前最稳健的无训练方案，对研究LLM可靠性、评估方法论以及推理增强的研究者具有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Do these findings generalize to other model architectures (Mistral, Gemma, Phi) and to larger frontier models (70B+ instruction-tuned), or is the "alignment ceiling" effect specific to Llama/Qwen families?
2. The paper finds CoT gains inversely correlate with model capability at high baselines (+2pp at 90%). Is there a principled explanation — does alignment training subsume the reasoning benefit that CoT provides?
3. Given that TRACE-LD changes 15.3% of token positions but with gates near 1.0, what architectural change (e.g., position-specific gating, factuality-targeted attention heads) would be needed for targeted logit intervention to work on aligned models?
4. The contamination dose-response shows that even 5% overlap has a near-zero effect (+0pp) while 27% overlap gives +7.5pp — is there a threshold effect, or is the relationship smooth? This has direct implications for data curation in truthfulness research.
5. How does the six-control framework perform when applied retrospectively to published results (e.g., the original DoLA or ITI papers) — can the full gap between reported and strict-control performance be accounted for by the five identified sensitivities?
6. CoT magnitude varies with prompt-benchmark alignment. Can prompt selection be automated or learned to make CoT gains more reproducible across deployment contexts?
7. The paper tests self-critique only in the 2-pass "critique then revise" configuration — would a learned critic model (trained on human feedback) outperform self-critique on instruction-tuned models in the same strict evaluation regime?
