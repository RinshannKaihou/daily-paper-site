> **一句话总结：** 将用户侧记忆分解为行为一致性、事实存在、事实缺失三轴，发现 per-user gamma-LoRA 在风格上胜出（+47pp）但在缺失校准上严重落后于 RAG（−90pp），且注意力层 21–35 的 q_proj 单元在同一方向上因果性地承载这两种相反效果；更强的 RLHF 会放大而非修复这一不对称性（"对齐税"）。

> **TL;DR:** User-side LLM memory is not one problem. This paper decomposes it into three orthogonal axes — behavioral style, factual presence, and factual absence — and shows that per-user gamma-LoRA and BGE-large RAG each win exactly one axis while losing the other. The q_proj cells in attention layers 21–35 causally mediate both effects in opposite directions. Heavier RLHF (Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct) amplifies the absence-calibration gap rather than healing it. LaMP-3 failures are diagnosed as instruction-following collapse rather than substrate failure. Substrate routing is better framed as text classification than calibration.

## 核心贡献

- **三轴诊断框架**：将"个性化"这一单一指标分解为行为一致性（风格/语气）、事实存在（能否回忆历史中的事实）、事实缺失（缺少事实时能否正确弃权）三轴，揭示聚合指标掩盖了方向相反的失败模式。同一底层在行为上 +47pp、在缺失校准上 −90pp，可以同时发生在同 50 个用户身上。
- **底层不对称性实证**：gamma-LoRA（per-user LoRA rank 64–128，仅 q/k/v/o 注意力投影）在行为风格上胜出（59.8% vs 40.2% 盲选偏好，Δ+0.473 nat/tok），RAG 在事实缺失 TPR 上胜出（99.0% vs 8.7%，差距 90.3pp）。
- **因果机制**：归零 q_proj 层 21–35（"顶带"）的 lora_A 权重使缺失探针 TPR 从 40.3% 提升至 73.3%（+33pp），同时使存在探针 TPR 从 42.0% 下降至 22.3%（−20pp）；层控制（L5–19）和投影控制（L21–35 v_proj）确认特异性。
- **对齐税**：在 Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct 上，行为优势从 Δ+0.413 崩溃至 +0.003 nat/tok（基本归零），而缺失校准差距从 45.7pp 扩大至 79.0pp；Mistral-7B-v0.3 复现方向（n=3，2/3 模型一致）。
- **LaMP-3 失败诊断**：9×2 消融矩阵将 main_acc 从 31.5% 的失败溯源为指令遵循崩溃（20.5% 输出违反 1–5 整数约束），而非底层机制失败；eval-time logit mask 在所有 8 个训练方案上将 main_acc 恢复至 ≥0.995。最佳训练时修复（KL 锚点）在 Llama-3.1-8B 上逐 bit 复现（probe2 0.655 = 0.655，r=0.78，p<0.0001，n=50）。
- **路由即分类**：110M DistilBERT 仅凭问题文本（不使用 logit）即超越所有基于 logit 的路由器（Llama F1: 0.718 vs hybrid_logits 0.569，+14.9 F1，p=4e-4）；TF-IDF 单独即可捕获主要收益，说明路由是问题类型分类问题而非校准问题。

## Key Contributions

- **Three-axis diagnostic framework** for user-side memory: behavioral consistency, factual presence, factual absence. A single aggregate personalization score can show +47pp on one axis and −90pp on another simultaneously, making top-line metrics uninformative about substrate-level decisions.
- **Substrate asymmetry finding** with pre-registered falsifiers: gamma-LoRA wins behavioral style (blind pref 59.8% CI [56.5,63.1], n=750 pairings; Δ+0.473 nat/tok on held-out user continuations); RAG wins absence calibration (99.0% vs 8.7% absence-TPR, a 90.3pp gap). No single substrate is Pareto-superior.
- **Causal mechanism** via band-zero intervention: zeroing q_proj weights at layers 21–35 raises absence-TPR by +33pp and lowers presence-TPR by −20pp on the same 50 personae, with layer and projection specificity controls confirming localization.
- **Alignment tax**: on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (heavier RLHF), parametric memory's behavioral advantage collapses to noise (+0.003 nat/tok) while its absence-calibration gap widens by 33pp (+45.7 → +79.0). Pattern holds directionally on Mistral-7B-v0.3 (n=3, 2-of-3 consistent).
- **Diagnosed negative on LaMP-3**: failure of gamma-LoRA to beat a majority baseline (31.5% vs 59.5%) is instruction-following collapse, not substrate failure; a 9-condition mitigation sweep isolates the cause; both eval-time and training-time fixes replicate cross-model.
- **Routing-as-classification**: DistilBERT on question text alone beats all logit-based routers by +14.9 F1 on Llama; the logit signal is redundant for substrate selection.
- Code and synthetic corpus released at https://github.com/EpistemicaLab/substrate-asymmetry-memory.

## Methodology

**Experimental configurations (4 substrates compared on same 50 personae):**
- `B_nohist`: base model, no user history (negative control)
- `C_rag`: BGE-large-en-v1.5 dense top-K=5 retrieval over user history with a fixed abstain clause
- `C_lora`: per-user gamma-LoRA (rank 64–128) trained on the four attention projections (q/k/v/o) for 20 epochs with assistant-only loss mask
- `C_lora_calib`: gamma-LoRA + same abstain prompt clause at inference (substrate-controlled ablation)

**Base models:** Qwen3-4B-Instruct (primary), Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct and Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3 (cross-model replication). Total compute: ~14 GPU-hours on a single L40S or H100.

**Three evaluation probes:**
1. *Behavioral memory (§4.1)*: next-token log-likelihood on 50 WritingPrompts held-out user continuations + blind LLM-judge preference at n=750 pairings (3-template judge from two vendors: Sonnet 4.6, Opus 4.8, Nova Premier).
2. *Factual calibration (§4.2)*: 12 controlled yes/no probes × 50 synthetic personae × 4 configs = 2,400 records. Presence probes ask about facts stated in user history; absence probes ask about facts deliberately omitted. TPR measured separately for each.
3. *Real-data transfer (§4.3)*: LaMP-3 product-rating task (50 users; exact-match rating accuracy).

**Synthetic corpus construction:** 50 personae generated by Claude Sonnet 4.6 conditioned on persona-template prompts (writer / professional / hobbyist), each with 30-turn conversation history; the same generator produces presence/absence probes. Probe generation is substrate-blind. Base model contamination measured at 6.50% (3.33% presence, 9.67% absence) over 600 probes.

**Causal mechanism (§5):** Per-persona Frobenius norm of gamma-LoRA weight changes summed over q_proj layers 21–35 ("top band") correlates with presence-TPR (r=+0.41, p=0.0017) and absence-TPR (r=−0.49, p=0.0001). Causal intervention: zero lora_A weights for q_proj at layers 21–35, re-evaluate. Specificity controls: layer control (L5–19 q_proj) and projection control (L21–35 v_proj).

**LaMP-3 mitigation sweep (§4.3.2):** 9 training-time arms × 2 eval conditions (free decoding vs {1..5} logit mask). Arms span schedule (epoch), architecture (rank, IA3, partial proj), loss (rating-token ×10, KL anchor, Alpaca anchor), and data (Claude paraphrase augmentation) families.

**Routing baselines (§4.6):** hybrid_logits (logistic over both substrates' next-token entropy and top-1 margin) vs P(True) [Kadavath'22], self-consistency k=5 [Wang'23], Adaptive-RAG TF-IDF [Jeong'24], and Adaptive-RAG DistilBERT. Evaluation on n=20 personae / 240 rows with 5k-bootstrap CIs and paired permutation tests.

## Results & Highlights

**Behavioral style (§4.1):**
- gamma-LoRA: +0.473 nat/tok over no-history baseline; 59.8% blind-pref win (CI [56.5, 63.1]), n=750 pairings; structural on 50/50 personae and 240/250 records.
- RAG: essentially a no-op (+0.060 nat/tok).
- Cross-model: style lift does NOT replicate on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (Δ +0.003 nat/tok) — style result is recipe-specific to Qwen3-4B.

**Factual calibration (§4.2):**

| Config | Presence TPR | Absence TPR | F1 |
|---|---|---|---|
| B_nohist | 3.3% | 9.7% | 0.050 |
| C_rag (abstain) | 35.3% | **99.0%** | **0.521** |
| C_lora | 73.7% | 8.7% | 0.156 |
| C_lora_calib (LoRA + abstain) | 70.7% | 53.3% | 0.611 |

- The substrate-controlled gap (C_rag − C_lora_calib) is 45.7pp on Qwen and **+79.0pp on Llama** (strengthens, not heals).
- gamma-LoRA wins present-fact recall (73.7% vs 35.3%) — exactly the inverse asymmetry.

**LaMP-3 (§4.3):**
- gamma-LoRA: 31.5% vs majority baseline 59.5% (−28pp). Failure cause: 20.5% of outputs violate integer 1–5 constraint (mostly "x/5" fractions and ~8% off-topic tail). Residual paired-probe ceiling: 0.605–0.660, σ=0.018, recipe-invariant.
- Logit mask alone (arm H): main_acc 0.315 → **1.000**, probe2 0.600; applies across all 8 training recipes.
- Best training-time fix (KL anchor, arm D): main_acc 1.000, probe2 **0.660**; replicates bit-identically on Llama (probe2 0.655 = 0.655, r=0.78, p<0.0001).

**Causal intervention (§5.2):**
- Zero L21–35 q_proj band: absence-TPR 40.3% → 73.3% (**+33pp**); presence-TPR 42.0% → 22.3% (**−20pp**). Both per-persona sign-tests p<1e-4.
- Layer control (L5–19 q_proj): Δabs −3.0 / +2.0pp (negligible).
- Projection control (L21–35 v_proj): Δabs +18.7 / −12.0pp (~57% of target magnitude on absence; partial specificity).

**Routing (§4.6):**

| Router | Qwen F1 | Llama F1 |
|---|---|---|
| hybrid_logits (baseline) | 0.579 | 0.569 |
| P(True) | 0.491 | 0.078 |
| Self-consistency k=5 | 0.479 | 0.690 |
| Adaptive-RAG TF-IDF | 0.638 | 0.709 |
| **Adaptive-RAG DistilBERT** | **0.660** | **0.718** |

- DistilBERT beats hybrid_logits by +8.1 F1 on Qwen and +14.9 F1 on Llama (p=4e-4). P(True) collapses on Llama (99.2% routed to LoRA, F1=0.078).

**Alignment tax summary:** Qwen3-4B (lighter RLHF) → Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (heavier RLHF): style Δ collapses (+0.413 → +0.003), absence gap widens (+45.7 → +79.0pp). Directionally consistent on Mistral-7B-v0.3 (2/3 models). Pre-registered prediction: heavier post-training → larger calibration gap, smaller style gap.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High for the framing. Decomposing "personalization" into three orthogonal axes is a clean and necessary contribution — existing benchmarks (LaMP, PERSOMA, UQABench) aggregate across heterogeneous task types and obscure the asymmetry. The "alignment tax on parametric user-memory" is a counter-intuitive and potentially important finding if replicated at scale. The routing-as-classification insight is simple but actionable.

**Rigor:** Strong for a single-author preprint. Key strengths: pre-registered falsifiers, substrate-controlled comparisons (C_rag vs C_lora_calib to isolate prompting from substrate), layer and projection specificity controls for the causal claim, 3-vendor LLM judge with inter-rater statistics (Cohen's κ reported), and bit-identical cross-model replication of the KL-anchor fix. Key weaknesses: the causal claim is a band-zero intervention, not a full mechanistic circuit (head-within-band isolation is explicitly left as future work); v_proj carries ~57% of the absence effect, weakening the specificity of the q_proj attribution.

**Reproducibility:** Good. Code and corpus are released. Compute is modest (14 GPU-hours). All hyperparameters and templates are reported. The judge specification uses Claude Sonnet 4.6 via AWS Bedrock — exact prompt templates are in appendix but API access is required to reproduce the behavioral probe at n=750 scale. The 50-user corpus scale is small; generalization to larger, real-user corpora is unverified.

**Significance:** High practical relevance. The finding that aggregate personalization metrics misclassify instruction-following collapse as substrate failure (§4.3) is immediately applicable to any team running LaMP-style evaluations. The routing-as-classification result challenges a class of calibration-based routing papers. The alignment-tax finding, if it scales, has implications for RLHF recipe design in personalization-heavy deployments.

**Red flags:**
- The style result (behavioral advantage of gamma-LoRA) does NOT replicate on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct — this is partially buried in §4.5 and §6 but is a meaningful qualification: the headline behavioral claim is recipe/model-specific to Qwen3-4B.
- n=50 personae throughout; the corpus is synthetic and generated by Claude Sonnet 4.6 — the same model used as one of the preference judges, creating a potential circularity (the synthetic data distribution could favor Sonnet's own stylistic preferences).
- The LaMP-3 "main_acc → 1.000 with logit mask" result is ceiling performance on a constrained output task — this is not a general recovery; it simply forces valid integer output. The residual probe2 ceiling of 0.605–0.660 is more informative but is attributed to task structure (4 in-context preference pairs), not recipe.
- Alignment-tax claim rests on n=3 model families (Qwen, Llama, Mistral) at fixed model sizes; the confound between RLHF intensity and model size / training data is acknowledged but not fully controlled.

## Strengths

- Clean conceptual decomposition of a muddled aggregate metric; the 2×2 "no Pareto winner" figure (Figure 1) is a strong visual summary.
- Explicit §1.3 ("What this paper does not claim") is unusually honest and sets appropriate scope.
- Pre-registered falsifiers and substrate-controlled ablations (C_lora_calib) guard against the most obvious confounds.
- Causal intervention (band-zero) with specificity controls goes meaningfully beyond correlation.
- Code and corpus released; compute is accessible (single L40S/H100, ~14 GPU-hours).
- The LaMP-3 "honest negative" is a methodologically valuable contribution: a null result with a diagnosed cause is more useful than an unexplained failure.
- Routing result is immediately actionable: TF-IDF alone captures most of the gain over logit-based methods.

## Limitations

- **Style result does not replicate cross-model**: the behavioral advantage of gamma-LoRA collapses on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct — the headline behavioral claim is Qwen3-4B-specific.
- **Synthetic corpus circularity**: 50-user corpus generated by Claude Sonnet 4.6, which is also one of the LLM judges for behavioral preference. Stylistic biases may be shared.
- **Small n**: n=50 personae throughout; n=3 model families for alignment-tax claim. Effect sizes are large enough to clear this bar at n=50, but generalization to diverse real users is unverified.
- **Partial causal specificity**: q_proj L21–35 is the primary band, but v_proj L21–35 carries ~57% of the absence effect; head-within-band isolation is left as future work.
- **Single LoRA rank and training recipe**: gamma-LoRA is rank 64–128, trained for 20 epochs with assistant-only loss mask — other parameter-efficient adaptation methods (prefix tuning, IA3, full fine-tuning) are not systematically compared as substrates.
- **LaMP-3 scope**: only LaMP-3 (product rating) tested as real-data probe; other LaMP tasks and other personalization benchmarks not evaluated.
- **No privacy analysis**: per-user LoRA adapters store user history in weights — differential privacy or membership inference considerations are not addressed.

## Relevance

本文对构建个性化 LLM 系统（如用户自适应对话代理、个人写作助手）的研究具有直接指导意义：其三轴诊断框架为评估参数记忆与检索增强的权衡提供了明确的实验协议，"对齐税"发现也提醒在 RLHF 训练中需专门考量用户侧记忆的校准能力。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the style advantage of gamma-LoRA recover on Llama-3.1-8B if a different training recipe is used (e.g., the KL anchor that fixes LaMP-3)? The cross-model collapse of style suggests the advantage is training-recipe-sensitive, not substrate-fundamental.
2. Is the alignment tax directional with model scale (larger = worse calibration gap)? The pre-registered prediction (heavier post-training → larger gap) is tested at n=3 fixed-family models; a systematic scaling study across checkpoints of the same base would be more conclusive.
3. Does the synthetic-corpus circularity (Claude generates data + judges) inflate the behavioral preference estimate? A held-out human evaluation at larger n (the paper has a preliminary n=30 human A/B at 79.3% LoRA share) would strengthen the behavioral claim.
4. Can the q_proj L21–35 band intervention be used constructively — e.g., selectively zeroing the band only for absence-type queries at inference time as a lightweight hybrid mechanism?
5. How does gamma-LoRA compare to external memory systems (Mem0, MemGPT) on the three-axis framework? The paper defers this to Appendix B.4 and flags it as future work — but it is the most practically relevant comparison for production deployments.
