Key Finding

Lagrangian mechanics can deductively derive interpretable ML architectures from three formal symmetries

The Standard Interpretable Model: A general theory of interpretable machine learning to deductively design interpretable methods using Lagrangian mechanics

Pietro Barbiero Giovanni De Felice Mateo Espinosa Zarlenga Francesco Giannini Filippo Bonchi Mateja Jamnik Giuseppe Marra Ruggero Noris
Key Metric
>0.8
Symmetry II violation score for Qwen2.5 chain-of-thought explanations (0 = perfect, 1 = maximally violated). Also: only ~500 of 33,000 concepts (~1.5%) suffice to drive Steerling-8B predictions below the 0.1 violation floor.
Why This Matters

A unified theory exposes CoT as formally unfaithful and proves standard metrics like MAE are invalid proxies for interpretability.

SIM replaces ad-hoc interpretability desiderata with a principled, falsifiable framework — any method can now be formally certified or indicted.

Critical Edge

The three premises are human choices, not derived axioms: the claim that SIM is a general theory must be read knowing it only covers formal, symbolic user models — extension to non-symbolic or multi-stakeholder settings is unproven and left as future work.

Three Interpretability Symmetries
Symmetry I
Concept Invariance

The model's concept scores must induce the same human preorder up to any order-preserving reparametrisation. A model that fits scores precisely while reversing their semantic ordering violates this symmetry — making MAE an invalid proxy.

χ(cₜ) = χ(𝔤ₜ ∘ cₜʷ) for all strictly monotone 𝔤ₜ
Symmetry II
Prediction–Concept Dependency

Task predictions must depend on inputs exclusively through the shared concept map. Prediction gradients must lie in the column space of the concept Jacobian. CoT explanations in Qwen2.5 score >0.8 — near maximal violation.

im(∇ᴫf) ⊆ col(∇ᴫc)
Symmetry III
Bounded Reasoning

The concept-to-prediction formula must lie in the kernel of a user-specified admissibility operator Kˆ, bounding complexity to what the target entity can reason about. Increasing λ₃ converges training toward simpler hypotheses.

Kˆᔓʰ⁽ʷ(ɸ(c(z;θϲ);θɸ), …) = 0
Six-Step Pipeline
01
Interpretability Premises

User specifies a target entity h with a fixed symbol vocabulary, concept maps, and bounded-reasoning operator. Interpretability is explicitly user-relative — different premises yield different but internally consistent theories.

02
Interpretability Symmetries

Premises are formalised as group invariances over input space. Three symmetries emerge: semantic ordering (I), gradient subspace alignment (II), hypothesis admissibility (III).

03
Operational Constraints

Each symmetry is translated into a testable constraint q(z) ≤ 0 — monotonicity hinge loss (I), gradient subspace alignment (II), kernel membership (III).

04
Lagrangian

A single Lagrangian L = T − V encodes all three constraints with multipliers λ₁, λ₂, λ₃. Equations of motion directly yield gradient-based training rules via least action.

05
Trajectories — Phase II

Apply least action, discretise: a momentum-based update penalises constraint violations locally during training. Approximate, local satisfaction only.

SOFT — optimisation
06
Architecture IV — Phase III

Constraints compiled directly into model structure. Global, by-construction satisfaction. Subsumes CBMs, SENNs, ProtoPNets, decision trees, feature attribution as special cases.

HARD — architectural
Results & Highlights
  • S1
    MAE is not interpretability. DNN minimising MAE achieves low absolute error but highest Constraint I violation rate — it routinely reverses human-induced semantic preorders.
  • S2
    Architectural compilation is global. DNN+A (Phase III) enforces exact gradient subspace alignment by construction; DNN+L (Phase II) achieves alignment only near the constrained training point.
  • S3
    CoT explanations are formally unfaithful. Qwen2.5 scores consistently >0.8 on Sym-II violation — prediction Jacobian ∇f nearly orthogonal to CoT explanation Jacobian ∇c.
  • VLM
    VLM concept semantics are broken. CLIP, Moondream2, and Qwen2 produce asymmetric pairwise rankings for "red intensity" on MNIST — violating basic consistency. Fixed without fine-tuning via ordered prototypes.
  • S8B
    ~500 concepts drive 33,000. In Steerling-8B, only ~1.5% of 33,000 supervised concepts are needed to drop Symmetry II violation below 0.1 — confirming genuine concept-mediated prediction.
Strengths / Limitations
Substantive formalism: Lagrangian, equations of motion, and variational optimisation are genuine mathematical tools — not metaphor. Architecture IV (eq. 16) is fully explicit.
Falsifiability first-class: Section 6.1 defines analytical, empirical, and epistemic falsification axes — rare commitment for a theory paper.
Actionable software: PyTorch Concepts (PyC) provides ConceptBottleneckModel, ConceptEmbeddingModel, SteerlingModel with built-in intervention support.
Immediate diagnostics: CoT unfaithfulness and VLM concept failure are both quantifiable, actionable findings for practitioners today.
Jacobian cost: Constraint II (Sym-II) scales quadratically with representation dimension — potentially prohibitive for large models without approximation; tractability analysis absent.
Premises are still choices: The three symmetries are derived from user-defined premises, not deeper axioms. Exhaustiveness is openly unresolved (Sec. 6.3).
Scope gaps: Stochastic/temporal models, transformer-scale Phase III compilation, and multi-stakeholder settings are all left as future work.
Insider evaluation: Authors assess their own prior models (Steerling, CEM) using their own framework — independent replication of comparative claims is still needed.

Open Questions
  • Q1 Can the derivation pipeline (premises → symmetries → constraints → architectures) be automated via LLM-assisted symbolic derivation or program synthesis?
  • Q2 How does SIM handle multi-stakeholder interpretability, where different target entities h₁, h₂ induce conflicting symmetry requirements on the same model?
  • Q3 For transformer-scale models, can Constraint II (Jacobian alignment) be approximated efficiently enough for use as a training signal, or only as a post-hoc diagnostic?
  • Q4 Does satisfying all three symmetries by architectural compilation (Phase III) incur a systematic capacity penalty relative to unconstrained models?
  • Q5 CoT fails Sym-II in Qwen2.5 — do any existing LLM explanation methods (attribution-based, attention-based) satisfy Symmetry II, and at what accuracy cost?
