Key Finding

Embedding-space clustering selects better LLM outputs — no training needed — extending self-consistency to any open-ended task.

Agreement in Representation Space for Open-Ended Self-Consistency

Why It Matters
Replaces expensive LLM-judge calls with cheap embedding clustering that scales monotonically with sample count — unlike USC and SCe which plateau or regress.
Critical Edge
The full EBA clustering step barely beats simply picking the sample nearest to the mean embedding — raising the question of whether agglomerative clustering adds anything beyond a simpler centroid baseline.
EBA Pipeline
01 Sample N generations from LLM at T=0.7, top_p=1.0. Pool of 512; bootstrap 50 subsets per reported result.
02 Embed each generation via φ(·) — external (Qwen-Embedding-8B / Gemma) or the LLM's own last hidden token (no extra model needed).
03 Cluster embeddings: agglomerative hierarchical, average linkage, cosine distance. Number of clusters chosen automatically via Silhouette analysis.
04 Select the generation closest to the centroid of the dominant (largest) cluster as final output.

Sharpest Findings
Centroid proximity experiment: Nearest-to-global-centroid ≈ EBA across all n. Farthest-from-centroid degrades ~20 pp at n=256 on MATH500 — the paper's strongest analytical result.
Native hidden reps: Only 1–2 pp below external embedder, still substantially above random — practical cost-saving deployment path.
Full-gen vs. extracted-answer EBA: Full-generation clustering consistently underperforms; embeddings are sensitive to reasoning trace variation even when final answers agree.
Classical SC unification: On MATH500 extracted answers, EBA scaling curve matches majority voting exactly — EBA is a continuous-domain generalization of classical SC.
Strengths & Limitations
Strengths
+Training-free, model-agnostic, no extra LLM calls beyond sample generation.
+Native hidden-rep variant needs no external embedder — reduces deployment cost.
+Silhouette-based auto cluster count: zero hyperparameter tuning.
+Geometric hypothesis is falsifiable — centroid ablation provides direct empirical confirmation.
+Agreement signal scales monotonically with n — superior to USC/SCe in high-compute regimes.
+Code released, all datasets public, bootstrapping strategy noise-reducing and reproducible.
Limitations
−Clustering ≈ centroid selection: marginal value of agglomerative step is unclear.
−Embedding geometry ≠ semantic equivalence (anisotropy, high-dim cone concentration).
−No MBR comparison — minimum Bayes risk decoding is the natural prior work, absent from evaluation.
−Summarization Rouge-1 gain (+1 pt) is small and the metric under-rewards paraphrastic quality.
−Only Llama + Qwen tested — both transformer, instruction-tuned; robustness claim is narrow.
−Dialogue, long-form generation, agentic reasoning not evaluated; sensitive to reasoning trace variation.
Open Questions
Q1
How does EBA compare to minimum Bayes risk decoding — the most natural prior work omitted from the paper?
Q2
Since nearest-to-global-centroid nearly matches EBA — is the clustering step necessary at all? Is the real contribution simply "use mean embedding as quality proxy"?
Q3
Can the centrality signal be calibrated into a confidence score for downstream abstention or routing to a stronger model?
Q4
Does geometric-quality correlation persist under genuinely multimodal answer distributions — multiple correct but structurally distinct code solutions?
Q5
Would a domain-fine-tuned embedding space (code, math semantic equivalence) improve cluster separability and boost quality further?
Q6
How does EBA interact with chain-of-thought diversity — could diversity-promoting sampling improve cluster separability?
