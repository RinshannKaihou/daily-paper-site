Key finding

A pre-registered three-channel monitor catches 86.6% of distributional shifts in deployed LLM safety classifiers.

一句话总结：针对已部署的LLM安全分类器（DeBERTa、Llama Guard 3、ShieldGemma、Text-Moderation），提出三通道在线监控系统并在检测到偏移后通过加权共形预测层自适应恢复目标错误率，800单元格预注册实验中实现 86.6% 有效检测率（平均延迟 39.5 步）。

Key Metric
86.6%
Valid detection across 800 factorial cells (4 classifiers × 5 shifts × 20 seeds × 2 window sizes). Mean latency 39.5 steps at w=100. 693/800 detections; Wilson 95% CI [0.841, 0.888].
Why this matters
Deploying LLM safety classifiers without shift monitoring yields silently degraded moderation. This work supplies the first pre-registered, end-to-end monitoring-plus-recovery pipeline — and explicitly diagnoses when its recovery layer fails, a rarity for production safety tooling.
Critical Edge
Weighted conformal adaptation collapses for 3 of 4 classifiers. Logistic density-ratio estimation achieves near-perfect source/target separability in high-dimensional embeddings, clipping every weight to floor — recovery becomes a formula artifact, not adaptation. PCA-32 partially restores it; unvalidated on adversarial shifts.
01

Methodology

方法

Stream of (text, score, embedding) tuples → three parallel detectors → alarm → optional weighted conformal recalibration.

  • KS detector (primary). Sliding window w=100/200 on score CDFs against frozen reference (n_ref=500). Threshold via empirical null of N_cal=50 streams. Best for strong, abrupt shifts.
  • Confidence sequence (CS). Growing-window adaptive betting test (Waudby-Smith & Ramdas 2024). 100% detection, 0% FAR on 40 null streams. 97% vs KS 43% at 30% mixing.
  • MMD detector. Unbiased MMD² on penultimate-layer embeddings (Gaussian kernel, median bandwidth), threshold from 1000-permutation bootstrap. 120/120 detection; latency = w.
  • Conformal abstention. Split-conformal; on alarm, logistic density-ratio weights w̃_i clipped to [1/C, C], C=10, recompute weighted quantile q̃_w. Abstain when prediction set size ≠ 1.

Encoder/decoder crossover:

  • Encoders (DeBERTa, Text-Mod): fast on paraphrase/code-switch (26–37 steps), slow on adversarial suffix.
  • Decoders (Llama Guard, ShieldGemma): reverse — fast on adversarial suffix (26–29), slow on paraphrase (64–97).

Operational guidance

部署建议

Four findings you can act on without re-reading the paper.

  • No uniform config. Per-classifier monitoring profile required — FAR varies 5× across classifiers at identical calibration.
  • Channel by mixing regime. KS for strong/abrupt; CS (not KS) as primary when contamination ≤30%; MMD as backstop for any shift.
  • PCA-32 before density ratios. Cheap fix that breaks logistic separability; restores Llama Guard +33pp coverage on temporal.
  • Detect adversarial via disagreement. GCG suffixes make Llama Guard more alarmed (mean 0.78) while evading DeBERTa — disagreement itself is a signal.

Strengths

  • Pre-registered factorial (git-anchored) + 21-assertion verification — gold-standard empirical rigor.
  • Importance-weight collapse diagnosed with mechanistic hypothesis and empirical test (PCA ablation).
  • Encoder/decoder crossover is actionable for production deployment.
  • Three-channel architecture with principled, regime-specific detector roles.
  • Transparent failure analysis: 107 invalid cells decomposed by mode; deviations logged.
  • Public code release with reproducible fine-tuning scripts.

Limitations

  • Single author, no institutional affiliation — limited peer validation of implementation.
  • Conformal adaptation fails for 3 of 4 classifiers; PCA-32 fix validated on only 2 of 5 shift types.
  • DeBERTa fails adversarial detection in 38/40 seed-window combinations in Regime C.
  • Regime C corpus = 22 GCG examples; adversarial claims are classifier-level, not cell-level.
  • All classifiers binary; multi-label safety taxonomies not covered.
  • 33.5% residual variance in detection latency unexplained; n=4 mechanistic hypothesis is suggestive only.
  • No analysis of abstention rate under the conformal layer — operational costs unmodeled.
05

Questions for further exploration

延伸问题
  1. Is DeBERTa-v3-large's non-collapse a function of scale (304M vs 86M), objective (binary vs 9-class), or dimensionality (1024-d)? A controlled ablation inside the DeBERTa-v3 family could isolate it.
  2. Would non-parametric density-ratio estimators (Telescoping DRE; KLIEP / uLSIF) avoid the separability collapse without requiring PCA — and at what variance cost?
  3. Real production safety-violation streams typically mix at well below 50%. If so, the CS detector — not KS — should be the primary channel. Has anyone measured this?
  4. GCG suffixes that evade DeBERTa raise Llama Guard's mean score to 0.78. Can cross-classifier disagreement be lifted into a direct adversarial detector, independent of the shift framework?
  5. The 50-step ramp sweep is one (DeBERTa × paraphrase) condition. A full mixing × ramp-rate interaction is needed to characterize the practical operating envelope.
  6. The conformal abstention criterion produces both empty sets (size 0) and ambiguous sets (size ≥2). What are the operational rates of each, and is a fallback policy needed for size-0 under severe shift?
