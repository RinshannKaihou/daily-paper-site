LLM judges create a novelty mirage — they rate model-written research questions as more novel than the questions real authors actually pursued.

Soumitra Sinhahajari · Navonil Majumder · Soujanya Poria / DeCLaRe Lab, NTU Singapore / 10 Jun 2026

On the Limits of LLM-as-Judge for Scientific Novelty Assessment  ·  RQ-Bench

Key Metric 关键指标
22%
expert–llm agreement · non-obviousness
On 50 sampled instances, two domain experts agree on novelty 60% of the time. But when the same pairs are judged by gemini-3.1-pro, agreement collapses to 22%. The LLM judge is not just inaccurate — it is anti-correlated with the human sense of what makes a question novel.
Why This Matters 为什么重要
Any AI-for-science pipeline that screens ideas by LLM-judged novelty is rewarding narrow gap-filling language, not the kind of broad, ambitious questions that move a field.
Critical Edge 关键边界
The reference set is "what authors pursued," not "what is globally most novel" — and the human study rests on only two experts across 50 samples.

Methodology

方法

RQ-Bench Construction

Source
~200K arXiv CS papers · 2025–01 → 2026–04
Filter
top 10% by citation · 13 sub-fields · LaTeX + indexed
Yield
746 source papers → 1,434 author-anchored RQs
Verif.
gemini-3.1-pro 98% · human 50/50 approved
  1. qwen3.5-27b-thinking Influential citation ID — classify each citation as inspiration / competitor / irrelevant from local context.
  2. gemini-3.1-pro Idea & contribution extraction from the source paper.
  3. gemini-3-flash Gap identification grounded in cited work & target contributions.
  4. gemini-3.1-pro RQ synthesis → 1.92 RQs/paper, 2,464 RQ–citation links, 3,151 gap statements.

Evaluation Framework

Generators
qwen3-30b · deepseek-v4-pro · gemma-4-31b · gpt-oss-20b · gpt-5.5 · gemini-3.1-pro
Judges
gemini-3.1-pro · deepseek-v4-pro · max-reasoning, greedy
Settings
Standalone + Comparative + Human expert
  1. Novelty K = {Orig, Gap, NonObs} scored 0–3 on 3 dimensions.
  2. Narrowness N = {SrcBound, DiagFrame} scored 0–3, lower = broader.
  3. Author-anchor Overlap@5 0–4, how closely best-of-5 matches the author's direction.
  4. Pass@5 candidates per instance; reported as win / tie / lose.
  5. Human expert blind study: 50 instances, cs.CL & cs.LG, non-obviousness only.

§03

Strengths & Limitations

优势 & 局限

Strengths / 优势

  • Principled design — three evaluation settings (standalone, comparative, human) triangulate the same diagnosis.
  • Concretely falsifiable claim — the narrowness dimension + revised-prompt ablation turn "LLMs get novelty wrong" into a measurable, repeatable test.
  • Public artifact — RQ-Bench (1,434 RQs) released at declare-lab/rq-bench with full statistics.
  • Honest framing — low author-anchor overlap is read as "models generate alternatives," not "models fail."
  • Well-matched pipeline — appropriate LLM at each of the four construction stages; both automated and human verification of RQ quality.

Limitations / 局限

  • Domain coverage — restricted to CS sub-fields on arXiv; biology, chemistry, social science unverified.
  • Expert scale — two domain experts over 50 samples is a thin human study.
  • Benchmark-construction bias — the "ground-truth" RQs are themselves produced by Gemini-3.1-Pro; its biases propagate into the reference set.
  • Mechanism unexplained — the paper shows LLMs reward narrow language, but not why; no judge-side prompt ablation on explicit narrowness rubrics.
  • Static snapshot — cutoff April 2026; future models may memorize some source content.
§04

Questions for Further Exploration

待探索问题

Open questions the paper does not answer

  1. Can an explicit narrowness rubric in the judge prompt close the expert–LLM gap — or is the misalignment too deep to fix via prompting alone?
  2. Does the novelty mirage persist for full idea evaluation (method + feasibility + experiments), or is it specific to the research-question level?
  3. Would a judge fine-tuned on human expert novelty annotations generalize better than prompted frontier models?
  4. How much of the expert preference is hindsight bias from knowing the published outcome? Could a masked expert study disentangle this?
  5. Does the mirage magnitude vary across sub-fields, and does it correlate with how well the generator was trained on that sub-field's papers?
