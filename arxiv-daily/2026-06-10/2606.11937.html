Headline finding

HPX asynchronous tasks beat OpenMP by up to 26% on a 128-core AMD node.

A unified, reproducible benchmark of four parallelization variants across OpenMP/GCC, OpenMP/LLVM, and HPX for right-looking tiled Cholesky decomposition — with a clean, isolated measurement of per-task runtime overhead.

From Fork-Join to Asynchronous Tasks: Parallelizing Tiled Cholesky Decomposition with OpenMP and HPX ↗ Strack · Van Craen · Pflüger  ·  IPVS, University of Stuttgart
Key metric · per-task overhead
3.8×
HPX ≈ 2 μs/task  ·  GCC OpenMP ≈ 7.6 μs/task
Why this matters
For HPC runtime developers, the 3.8× task-overhead gap is a concrete, actionable datum. Collapsed fork-join is a cheap, practical win before committing to full AMT — and on small problems, fork-join can still beat OpenMP async tasking.
Critical edge
All numbers come from a single dual-socket AMD Zen 2 platform; generalization to Intel Xeon, ARM Neoverse, or accelerators is unvalidated. Cross-runtime gaps also conflate scheduling policy with raw overhead.
01

Methodology

方法

The benchmark targets the right-looking tiled Cholesky on a symmetric positive-definite matrix partitioned into M×M square tiles. Each outer iteration runs four BLAS kernels:

  • POTRFIn-place Cholesky on the diagonal tile AJ,J.
  • TRSMTriangular solve updating column tiles AI,J, I > J.
  • SYRKSymmetric rank-k update on diagonal trailing tiles.
  • GEMMGeneral matrix-matrix multiply on off-diagonal trailing tiles.

Four parallelization variants, each implemented in both OpenMP and HPX, isolate the effect of tasking, scheduling, and barrier semantics:

VariantMechanismCost / benefit
Fork-join#pragma omp parallel for on outer loops; only outer submatrix-update is parallelized.Cheapest; poor at fine tile counts.
Fork-join collapsedcollapse(2) fuses SYRK + GEMM, exposing both dimensions.Near tasking performance at zero programming cost.
Task synchronousEach BLAS call is a task, but taskwait barriers mirror fork-join sync points.Isolates task creation cost from scheduling benefit.
Task asynchronousExplicit dependencies (task depend / hpx::dataflow); no global barriers.Full AMT benefit; runtime schedules as soon as inputs are ready.

Hardware: 2× AMD EPYC 7742 (Zen 2), 128 threads, workers pinned one-per-physical-core. Compilers: GCC 14.2, LLVM/Clang 22.1. HPX 1.11.0 + jemalloc. BLAS: OpenBLAS 0.3.28 sequential inside tasks. Metric: median of 20 runs; task overhead isolated via no-op BLAS substitution.

Strengths & Limitations

评估

Strengths 优势

  • Systematic, reproducible, openly available benchmark with full configuration disclosure (MIT, constracktor/Cholesky-Bench).
  • Clean isolation of per-task overhead via no-op BLAS substitution — a rare, important methodological step.
  • Nuanced finding that collapsed fork-join is a cheap, practical win before committing to full AMT.
  • GCC vs LLVM comparison surfaces a real-world collapse(2) scheduling pitfall practitioners need to know.
  • Honest single-platform disclosure and a clear future-work roadmap (Intel, ARM, RISC-V, TTG, StarPU).

Limitations 局限

  • Single hardware platform (dual-socket AMD Zen 2) — generalization to Intel, ARM Neoverse, or accelerators is unvalidated.
  • Single algorithmic variant (right-looking); left-looking and top-looking Cholesky may behave differently.
  • HPX adoption cost (C++ futures API, jemalloc, separate build system) is not reflected in runtime numbers.
  • Only shared-memory parallelism; HPX's distributed-memory backends (MPI, FleCSI, Legion) are out of scope.
  • Other AMT runtimes (StarPU, TTG, PaRSEC) not in the same framework; cross-AMT comparison deferred.
04

Questions for further exploration

待探索
  1. Does the ~3.8× task-overhead advantage of HPX over OpenMP hold on Intel Xeon (Sapphire Rapids) or ARM Neoverse V2, where NUMA topology and cache coherence differ substantially?
  2. Would left-looking or top-looking Cholesky — which expose more regular task graphs — reduce the advantage of async tasking over collapsed fork-join?
  3. Can OpenMP's task overhead be pushed down to HPX-level (~2 μs) via OpenMP 5.x detached tasks or LLVM-specific optimizations, or is it a fundamental fork-join design consequence?
  4. How does Cholesky-Bench scale in a distributed-memory setting with HPX's MPI or FleCSI backend — does the AMT advantage amplify or diminish as inter-node communication enters?
  5. GPRat uses HPX tiled Cholesky in a real application — what is the end-to-end GPRat speedup from async HPX tasking, compared to the 26% microbenchmark figure?
