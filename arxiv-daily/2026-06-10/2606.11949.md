> **一句话总结：** 针对已部署的LLM安全分类器（DeBERTa、Llama Guard 3、ShieldGemma、Text-Moderation），提出了一套三通道在线监控系统（滑动窗口KS检测器 + 置信序列 + MMD嵌入检测器），并在检测到偏移后通过加权共形预测层自适应恢复目标错误率，在800单元预注册析因实验中实现86.6%有效检测率（平均延迟39.5步），同时揭示了高维嵌入空间中密度比估计近乎完美分离导致共形自适应对大多数分类器失效的根本性失败模式。

> **TL;DR:** A pre-registered three-channel online monitor (sliding-window KS on classifier scores, growing-window confidence sequences, MMD on embeddings) for distributional shift in deployed LLM safety classifiers, combined with a weighted conformal abstention layer triggered on detection. Achieves 86.6% valid detection across 800 factorial cells (4 classifiers × 5 shift types × 20 seeds × 2 window sizes), mean latency 39.5 steps, 2–10% FAR. Crucially identifies and diagnoses importance-weight collapse in high-dimensional generative model embeddings as a near-universal failure mode for conformal adaptation, with PCA-32 as a partial fix.

## 核心贡献

- **三通道在线监控架构**：主通道为滑动窗口KS检测器（对分类器分数CDF进行监控），辅以置信序列（CS）增长窗口检测器（在低混合比场景下性能优于KS，30%混合时CS检出率97% vs KS 43%）和MMD嵌入检测器（提供即时二元告警，延迟=窗口长度w）。
- **加权共形弃权层**：偏移检测后激活，通过逻辑回归密度比估计对校准集非符合分数重加权，动态调整决策阈值以恢复目标错误率ε=0.1，无需新标注数据。
- **密度比崩溃的诊断与修复**：首次系统记录高维安全分类器嵌入（4096维Llama Guard、3584维ShieldGemma）中逻辑密度比估计达到近乎完美的源/目标可分性，导致所有校准权重被截断至floor（ESS≈300），使共形自适应完全退化为固定+0.10残差。PCA降至32维打破崩溃，为Llama Guard恢复33 pp、ShieldGemma恢复21 pp覆盖率（时序偏移条件下）。
- **预注册析因设计**：800单元格全因素实验（包含所有超参数承诺记录于git commit be630f3），配备21条断言验证脚本，是同类安全分类器研究中罕见的严格评估设计。
- **编码器/解码器架构分裂发现**：判别式编码器（DeBERTa、Text-Moderation）对语义改写/组合偏移检测快（26–37步），对对抗后缀慢；生成式解码器（Llama Guard、ShieldGemma）模式相反——对对抗后缀快（26–29步），对改写慢（64–97步）。此交互对任何单分类器研究均不可见，呼吁针对每个分类器维护独立监控档案。
- **方差分解**：双因素固定效应ANOVA显示分类器（η²=0.243）与偏移类型（η²=0.237）对检测延迟方差贡献几乎相等，交互效应（η²=0.185）也实质性显著（全部p<0.001），残差占33.5%。

## Key Contributions

- **Three-channel monitoring architecture**: Primary channel is a sliding-window one-sample KS detector on classifier score CDFs (frozen reference, w=100 or 200). Secondary channel is a growing-window confidence sequence (CS) with the time-uniform guarantee of Waudby-Smith & Ramdas (2024), achieving 100% detection with 0% FAR on reference-only streams. Tertiary channel is an MMD detector on penultimate-layer embeddings (Gaussian kernel, bandwidth fixed at calibration-time median, threshold via 1000-permutation bootstrap null), providing immediate binary detection at latency=w for all 120/120 tested streams.
- **Weighted conformal abstention layer**: Estimates density ratios w_i = p_target(x_i)/p_source(x_i) via logistic regression on source vs. target embeddings (Tibshirani et al. 2019), clipped to [1/C, C] with C=10, then recomputes the conformal quantile as a weighted quantile. Abstains when prediction set size ≠ 1. Targets ε=0.1 false error rate without new labels.
- **Importance-weight collapse diagnosis**: In all three shift types for Text-Moderation, Llama Guard, and ShieldGemma: logistic regression achieves perfect source/target separability, assigning P̂(target|x)≈0 to all calibration points and clipping all weights to floor 1/C=0.1 (ESS≈300 = ncal). The residual ≤+0.10 recoveries at ESS≈300 are explained by the test-point term w(X_{n+1})=1.0 shifting the effective quantile from 90.3% to 93.3% — a formula artifact, not genuine adaptation. PCA to 32 dimensions retains 82–91% of embedding variance while breaking logistic separability, enabling ESS reductions to 19.6 (ShieldGemma) and meaningful recovery.
- **Pre-registered factorial evaluation**: Full factorial 4×5×20×2=800 cells, all hyperparameters committed before execution (git commit be630f3), verified by scripts/verify_paper_numbers.py (21 assertions, all passing). Code and raw results at https://github.com/junwenleong/safety-classifier-shift-monitor.
- **Encoder/decoder detection crossover**: Encoders (DeBERTa, Text-Moderation) detect paraphrase/code-switch/compositional shifts fast (26–37 steps) but are slow on adversarial suffix (25–37 steps); generative decoders (Llama Guard, ShieldGemma) show the reverse: slow on paraphrase (64–97 steps), fast on adversarial suffix (26–29 steps). The null score distribution geometry (encoder σ≈0.07–0.09 vs decoder σ≈0.14) is the proposed mechanism: tighter boundaries → faster detection for surface-level perturbations; adversarial suffixes disrupt the generation distribution more visibly than a classification head.
- **Variance decomposition**: Two-way fixed-effects ANOVA on 693 valid detections. Classifier (η²=0.243 [0.205, 0.291]), shift type (η²=0.237 [0.193, 0.293]), interaction (η²=0.185), residual 0.335. Top interactions: DeBERTa×adversarial-suffix (+21.6 steps above expected), ShieldGemma×paraphrase (+19.9), Llama Guard×code-switch (+17.9), Llama Guard×adversarial-suffix (−16.0).

## Methodology

**System inputs**: A stream of tuples (x_t, s_t, r_t): input text, unsafe-class probability s_t ∈ [0,1], and penultimate-layer representation r_t ∈ ℝ^d.

**KS Detector (primary, §3.3)**: Maintains sliding window of w most recent scores. Computes one-sample KS statistic D_w = sup_x |F̂_w(x) − F̂_ref(x)| against a frozen reference CDF built from n_ref=500 in-distribution records. The alarm wraps a sliding-window Hoeffding bound confidence sequence and is calibrated empirically: N_cal=50 negative-control streams establish the 97th percentile of maximum observed statistic as the alarm threshold. Alarms suppressed during warmup period of w steps. Window sizes w=100 and w=200 evaluated; w=100 detects 7 steps faster on average but has slightly higher FAR.

**MMD Detector (secondary, §3.4)**: Sliding window of w embeddings vs. frozen reference embeddings. Unbiased MMD² with Gaussian kernel (bandwidth = calibration-time median heuristic). Threshold via 1000-permutation bootstrap at (1−α) quantile. Provides complementary detection: answers *whether* embedding geometry has changed (binary, immediate), while KS answers *how quickly* the score distribution diverges (graded severity).

**Confidence Sequence Detector (tertiary, §6.3)**: Growing-window adaptive betting confidence sequence (Waudby-Smith & Ramdas 2024). Time-uniform guarantee: no false alarm at any point across 40 null streams. 100% detection (120/120) vs KS 100/120 on same subset. Latency ~2× slower than KS (e.g., DeBERTa×paraphrase: CS 57.2 vs KS 25.1 steps). At low mixing (30% contamination): CS 97% vs KS 43% detection; at 50%+, both detect reliably but KS is faster.

**Conformal Abstention Layer (§3.9)**:
- Unweighted mode: Standard split-conformal prediction (Vovk et al. 2005), threshold q̂ at ⌈(1−ε)(n+1)⌉/n-th quantile of nonconformity scores α_i = 1 − f(x_i)_y_i.
- Weighted-on-alarm mode (Tibshirani et al. 2019): After shift alarm, estimates w_i = p_target(x_i)/p_source(x_i) via logistic regression on source vs. target embeddings; computes weighted quantile q̃_w = inf{q: Σ_{i: α_i≤q} w̃_i ≥ 1−ε}.

**Four Classifiers Evaluated** (Table 1):
- DeBERTa-v3-large: 304M params, 1024-d embeddings, fine-tuned on WildGuardMix (binary)
- Text-Moderation (KoalaAI): DeBERTa-v3-base, 86M, 768-d, fine-tuned on WildGuardMix (9-class)
- Llama Guard 3: 8B decoder-only LLM, 4096-d embeddings, proprietary training data
- ShieldGemma: 9B decoder-only LLM, 3584-d embeddings, proprietary training data

**Five Shift Conditions** (Table 2):
- Paraphrase: GPT-4o paraphrasing of harmful prompts (organic rephrasing threat model)
- Code-switch: Multilingual transliteration (non-English users)
- Compositional: Multi-turn concatenation into long contexts (context-window attacks)
- Temporal: Real jailbreaks from public red-team databases — lmsys/toxic-chat (39%), JailbreakBench (34%), ChatGPT-Jailbreak-Prompts (27%) (emerging harm categories)
- Adversarial suffix: GCG-optimized suffixes from Zou et al. 2023 (automated red-teaming)

**False Alarm Rate Calibration**: Empirical null simulation with N_cal=50 streams, alarm threshold set at 97th percentile of maximum observed statistics. Empirical FARs: Text-Moderation 2.0%, Llama Guard 3.0%, ShieldGemma 8.5%, DeBERTa 9.5%.

## Results & Highlights

**RQ1 — Detection Performance**:
- Overall valid detection rate: 86.6% (693/800; 95% Wilson CI [0.841, 0.888])
- Raw TPR: 91.5% overall (DeBERTa 87%, Text-Mod 91.5%, Llama Guard 95%, ShieldGemma 92.5%)
- Valid detection (requires clean negative control): DeBERTa 80.5%, Text-Mod 90%, Llama Guard 92%, ShieldGemma 84%
- Mean latency overall: 39.5 steps at w=100; 45.4 steps at w=200 (7-step average advantage for smaller window)
- Full detection rate matrix (Table 6): Code-switch is easiest for Llama Guard (100%), temporal easiest for Llama Guard/ShieldGemma (~80%), adversarial suffix hardest for DeBERTa (68%)
- Regime B (real temporal jailbreaks): 85% detection (17/20 seeds) on DeBERTa×Llama Guard; Llama Guard 10/10, DeBERTa 7/10
- Regime C (adversarial success — 22 GCG examples): Cross-classifier anomaly detection on Llama Guard (14/40 seed-window combinations, TOWARD_UNSAFE direction); DeBERTa fails 38/40 (GCG pushes scores to safe region)

**RQ2 — Conformal Adaptation** (Table 4, n=200 post-shift examples):
- DeBERTa temporal: Gap=0.085, Recovery=+0.160, ESS=88
- DeBERTa paraphrase: Gap=0.515, Recovery=+0.390, ESS=46
- DeBERTa adversarial suffix: Gap=0.325, Recovery=+0.020, ESS=206
- Text-Moderation: All three shift types, ESS≈300, Recovery ≤+0.020
- Llama Guard temporal: Gap=0.350, Recovery=+0.100, ESS=300
- ShieldGemma temporal: Gap=0.225, Recovery=+0.075, ESS=300
- After PCA-32: Llama Guard temporal ESS drops 300→19.6 (coverage 0.555→0.885, +33 pp); ShieldGemma temporal ESS 300→85 (coverage 0.690→0.895, +21 pp)

**RQ3 — Variance Decomposition** (Table 5, 693 valid detections):
- Classifier: η²=0.243 [0.205, 0.291], p<0.001
- Shift type: η²=0.237 [0.193, 0.293], p<0.001
- Classifier×Shift: η²=0.185, p<0.001
- Residual: η²=0.335
- Seed-clustered bootstrap CIs (Table 8): All exclude zero with wide margins; conclusions robust to within-seed correlation

**Detection Channel Comparison** (§6.3):
- KS (w=100): Best for strong, abrupt shifts; fastest detection
- CS (growing-window): Best for weak/gradual shifts (low mixing ≤30%); 100% detection, 0% FAR, but ~2× slower
- MMD: Immediate binary alarm (latency=w) for all shifts; complementary role as backstop; 120/120 detection, FAR controlled (0–10%)

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: Moderate-to-good. The combination of sequential testing + conformal prediction for deployment monitoring is not new (Podkopaev & Ramdas 2022; Prinster et al. 2025 at ICML). The specific application to LLM safety classifiers, and especially the systematic diagnosis of density-ratio collapse as a near-universal failure mode in generative model embeddings with a concrete PCA mitigation, is the most original contribution. The encoder/decoder detection crossover finding is genuinely novel and practically important. The pre-registered factorial design applied to classifier monitoring is methodologically distinctive — the authors are correct that no prior work analyzes classifier×shift interactions this way.

**Rigor**: Unusually high for a single-author applied ML paper. Pre-registration with git-commit anchoring, Wilson Score CIs, BCa bootstrap (10,000 resamples), Holm-Bonferroni correction for 8 highlighted pairwise comparisons (weakest adjusted p=0.044), permutation tests (1,000–10,000), seed-clustered bootstrap for variance decomposition robustness — these are not pro forma additions. The distinction between raw TPR (91.5%) and valid detection (86.6%) is carefully maintained and explained.

**Reproducibility**: High. Code, configurations, pre-registration document, and raw results are publicly available at https://github.com/junwenleong/safety-classifier-shift-monitor. YAML config files version-control all hyperparameters. Fine-tuning scripts reproduce encoder checkpoints from WildGuardMix with fixed seeds (~2 hours on Apple Silicon). The verification script confirms paper statistics against raw data.

**Significance**: Practically important for teams operating content moderation or safety systems in production. The key actionable findings are: (1) a uniform monitoring configuration will systematically miscalibrate — per-classifier profiles are necessary; (2) importance-weight collapse is near-universal in generative model embeddings and defeats standard weighted conformal prediction; (3) PCA-32 before density ratio estimation is a cheap, effective fix for this specific failure mode; (4) KS vs CS selection should depend on expected mixing proportion.

**Red flags**:
- Single author, independent researcher: no peer review of implementation prior to posting.
- The Regime C (GCG adversarial) corpus is only 22 examples — all Llama Guard detections are replications of a single adversarial token pattern, not independent shift events. The paper is appropriately candid about this limitation.
- DeBERTa fails adversarial detection in 38/40 seed-window combinations in Regime C — the monitor provides no signal for the classifier it is most meant to protect in this condition. This is a significant gap for adversarial deployment settings.
- PCA-32 validation for conformal recovery is demonstrated only on temporal and paraphrase shifts; the paper explicitly notes it has not been validated on adversarial suffix shifts.
- The conformal abstention criterion (abstain when |C(x)| ≠ 1) means the system can produce very high abstention rates under shift, which has operational costs not analyzed in the paper.
- All four classifiers are binary; multi-category safety taxonomies may exhibit category-specific shift patterns invisible to a scalar score monitor.
- False alarm rates vary 5× across classifiers (2.0% to 9.5%) despite identical calibration procedures — per-classifier threshold tuning is implied but not evaluated.

## Strengths

- Pre-registered factorial design with git-commit anchoring and automated statistical verification — the gold standard for empirical rigor in applied ML
- Systematic diagnosis of importance-weight collapse with a mechanistic hypothesis (high-dimensional separability), an empirical test (PCA ablation), and a concrete fix
- Encoder/decoder crossover finding provides actionable, architecture-specific guidance for production deployment
- Three-channel architecture with principled operational profiles for each detector (KS for speed, CS for weak shifts, MMD for immediate binary backstop)
- Transparent reporting: false alarm rates, failure analysis (107 invalid cells decomposed by failure mode), compute requirements, and deviations from pre-registration all explicitly documented
- Public code release with reproducible fine-tuning scripts

## Limitations

- Single author, no institutional affiliation — limits peer validation of implementation and reduces confidence in error-free execution of a complex pipeline
- Conformal adaptation fails for 3 of 4 classifiers in all shift conditions; PCA-32 fix is validated on only 2 of 5 shift types
- Regime C (adversarial) corpus too small (22 examples) for per-cell detection rate estimates; adversarial robustness claims are classifier-level, not cell-level
- DeBERTa fails detection on its own adversarial attack condition — the most security-relevant scenario — in 38/40 cells
- All classifiers are binary; multi-label safety systems not covered
- FAR calibration uses homogeneous WildGuardMix negative controls; production streams with higher natural variance may require larger or more diverse calibration sets
- No analysis of abstention rate under the conformal layer — high abstention rates may be operationally unacceptable
- 33.5% residual variance in detection latency remains unexplained; the mechanistic hypothesis (null score distribution geometry) is based on n=4 classifiers and should be treated as suggestive

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. The paper identifies DeBERTa-v3-large as the only non-collapsing classifier under weighted conformal prediction. Is this a function of model scale (304M vs 86M), binary vs multi-class pretraining objective, or embedding dimensionality (1024-d)? A controlled ablation between DeBERTa-v3-large and Text-Moderation (both DeBERTa-v3 family, same training data) would directly address the scale vs objective hypothesis.
2. Would non-parametric density ratio estimators (Telescoping DRE, Rhodes et al. 2020; or kernel-based KLIEP/uLSIF) avoid the separability collapse without requiring PCA, and what is the tradeoff in estimation variance?
3. The CS growing-window detector achieves 97% detection at 30% mixing vs KS's 43%. What is the practical mixing proportion in real production safety violations — if it is typically below 50%, CS should be the primary rather than supplementary detector.
4. The GCG adversarial suffixes that successfully evade DeBERTa make Llama Guard *more* alarmed (post mean score 0.78). Can this cross-classifier disagreement signal be used as a direct adversarial attack detector, independent of the distributional shift framework?
5. How does detection latency scale with the severity (mixing proportion) and onset rate of the shift? The paper provides a 50-step ramp sweep for DeBERTa×paraphrase, but this is a single condition — a full mixing × ramp interaction would better characterize the practical operating envelope.
6. The conformal abstention criterion (abstain when |C(x)| ≠ 1) produces prediction sets of size 0 (empty, refuse to classify) as well as size 2 (abstain due to ambiguity). What are the operational rates of each, and are they distinguishable? Empty sets under severe shift may require a fallback policy not discussed in the paper.
