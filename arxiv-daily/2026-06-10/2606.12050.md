> **一句话总结：** 本文在ODE场景下为PINN近似推导了首个可计算的后验双侧误差界——下界基于局部强单调性条件（Theorem 1），上界基于比全局Lipschitz更弱的单侧Lipschitz条件（Theorem 3），在96维刚性线性系统和非线性旋转增长系统上验证了其紧性与实用性，并提出以上界证书作为辅助正则化项的两阶段训练策略。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides the first computable a posteriori lower bounds for PINN prediction errors in ODEs (alongside sharpened upper bounds), constructs genuine two-sided error enclosures verified on nonlinear and high-dimensional stiff systems, and shows that the upper certificate can serve as an effective training regularizer — reducing final error by nearly 8x over standard PINN continuation on an unstable oscillatory system.

## 核心贡献

- **首个可计算ODE-PINN误差后验下界**：基于向量场在认证状态空间域 $D$ 上的局部强单调性条件（$\ell_D > 0$），下界形式为 $\|e(t)\| \ge e^{\ell_D t}\|e(0)\| - \int_0^t e^{\ell_D(t-s)}\delta(s)\,ds$，仅依赖PINN残差和初始误差，无需真实解。
- **局部化上界（弱化假设）**：在单侧Lipschitz条件（$\mu_D$）而非全局Lipschitz条件（$L_D$）下推导上界，对于具有大旋转部分的系统（如 $\beta J$ 分量），$\mu_D \ll L_D$，上界显著更紧（实验中上界从 $2.27\times10^1$ 压缩至 $6.01\times10^{-2}$）。
- **线性系统闭合公式**：对LTI系统 $\dot{x}=Ax$，一侧系数精确为系统矩阵对称部分的极端特征值 $m(A)=\lambda_{\min}(\text{sym}\,A)$、$M(A)=\lambda_{\max}(\text{sym}\,A)$，给出双侧误差的显式表达式。
- **符号残差有限探针下界证书（Theorem 2）**：针对硬约束PINN（$e(0)=0$）导致标量下界退化为零的问题，利用坐标单位向量组成探针集 $P$，通过 $\|e(t)\| \ge \max_{p\in P}|p^\top e^{At}e(0) - \int_0^t p^\top e^{A(t-s)}R(s)\,ds|$ 恢复非平凡下界信息。
- **双阶段证书指导训练**：上界证书满足辅助标量ODE $\dot{z}_c = cz_c + \delta_\theta$（Theorem 7），将其均值或终端值作为正则化项加入第二阶段训练损失，在不稳定振荡系统上最终误差降至 $5.68\times10^{-4}$，远优于标准续训的 $4.43\times10^{-3}$。

## Key Contributions

- **First computable a posteriori lower bounds for PINN errors in ODEs** under a localized strong monotonicity condition: `||e(t)|| >= exp(ℓ_D t)||e(0)|| - ∫exp(ℓ_D(t-s))δ(s)ds`. Valid on any certified convex domain D where both exact and PINN trajectories remain.
- **Localized upper bounds under one-sided Lipschitz continuity**, strictly weaker than Lipschitz continuity (Theorem 4 proves the converse fails; `f(x)=-x^3` is globally upper one-sided Lipschitz with μ=0 but not Lipschitz). For the rotation system, this reduces the upper certificate from ~22.7 (Lipschitz) to ~0.06 (one-sided).
- **Explicit LTI/LTV eigenvalue formulas** (Theorems 5, Corollary 1): sharp constants are `m(A) = λ_min(sym A)` and `M(A) = λ_max(sym A)`, with integrating-factor generalizations for time-varying `A(t)`.
- **Signed-residual finite-probe lower certificate** (Theorem 2) for linear systems with hard IC enforcement: recovers informative lower bounds when `e(0)=0` collapses the scalar certificate. On a 96-dimensional stiff system, gives a lower bound of `7.94×10⁻⁹`, about 66.5% of the true error `1.19×10⁻⁸`.
- **RK4-based numerical post-processing** with explicit remainder bounds (Theorem 6, Corollary 2): certification integrals are solved as scalar auxiliary IVPs via RK4, with fully computable O(h⁴) error guarantees.
- **Certificate-informed two-stage training** (Section 5, Theorem 7–8): the upper certificate is the unique solution of an auxiliary scalar ODE and serves as a training regularizer; proven to be no larger than the Lipschitz-based regularizer when one-sided constants are available.

## Methodology

**Problem setting.** The paper addresses the IVP $\dot{x}(t)=f(t,x(t))$, $x(0)=x_0$, approximated by a PINN $\hat{x}(t) = \hat{\varphi}_{\theta^\star}(t,x_0)$. All analysis is on a certified convex domain $D \subset \mathbb{R}^n$ where both trajectories remain. The residual is $\mathcal{R}_\varphi(t) = \dot{\hat{x}}(t) - f(t,\hat{x}(t))$, computable via automatic differentiation.

**Three certified coefficients** govern the bounds:
- $\ell_D = \inf_{(t,\xi)\in\mathbb{T}\times D} \lambda_{\min}(\text{sym}(J_x f))$ — lower one-sided Lipschitz (strong monotonicity when positive)
- $\mu_D = \sup \lambda_{\max}(\text{sym}(J_x f))$ — upper one-sided Lipschitz
- $L_D = \sup \|J_x f\|_2$ — Lipschitz constant

Key insight: $\mu_D \le L_D$ always, with strict inequality for non-normal vector fields (e.g., those with large skew-symmetric components like rotations). Upper bounds based on $\mu_D$ are therefore always at least as tight as Lipschitz-based bounds.

**Lower bound (Theorem 1).** By differentiating $\|e(t)\|$ and applying the strong monotonicity inequality and Cauchy-Schwarz, one obtains $\frac{d}{dt}\|e(t)\| \ge \ell_D\|e(t)\| - \delta(t)$, which after integrating yields the closed-form lower certificate. The certificate is nontrivial precisely when $\underline{E}_\text{loc,raw}(t) = e^{\ell_D t}\|e(0)\| - \int_0^t e^{\ell_D(t-s)}\delta(s)\,ds > 0$.

**Upper bound (Theorem 3).** The same energy argument with the upper one-sided Lipschitz gives $\frac{d}{dt}\|e(t)\| \le \mu_D\|e(t)\| + \delta(t)$, yielding $\|e(t)\| \le e^{\mu_D t}\|e(0)\| + \int_0^t e^{\mu_D(t-s)}\delta(s)\,ds$.

**Finite-probe lower certificate (Theorem 2).** For linear systems, the exact error satisfies $e(t) = e^{At}e(0) - \int_0^t e^{A(t-s)}R(s)\,ds$. For any unit vector $p$, $|p^\top e(t)| \le \|e(t)\|$ by Cauchy-Schwarz. Taking the max over a finite probe set $P$ (e.g., coordinate unit vectors) gives a computable lower bound without requiring $\|e(0)\| > 0$.

**RK4 post-processing.** All certification integrals reduce to the scalar auxiliary IVP $\dot{z}=az+\delta(t)$, $z(0)=0$. Theorem 6 provides a computable O(h⁴) global remainder bound $E^\alpha_\text{RK4}(T_\text{cert}, N) = \frac{\Xi_a(T_\text{cert})}{90}K_5(T_\text{cert})h^4$, enabling fully rigorous (or "conditionally rigorous" if $D_q$ are estimated numerically) certificates.

**Certificate-informed training (Section 5).** The upper certificate $U_c^\theta(t) = e^{ct}\|x_0 - \hat{x}_\theta(0)\| + \int_0^t e^{c(t-s)}\delta_\theta(s)\,ds$ solves the auxiliary ODE $\dot{z}^\theta_c = cz^\theta_c + \delta_\theta$, $z^\theta_c(0) = \|x_0 - \hat{x}_\theta(0)\|$ (Theorem 7). The stage-2 training loss is $\mathcal{L}_\text{stage2}(\theta) = \mathcal{L}_\text{PINN}(\theta) + \eta \mathcal{L}^\text{up}_\text{cert}(\theta)$, where $\mathcal{L}^\text{up}_\text{cert}$ is either the mean or terminal value of $z^\theta_c$. Lower certificates are kept as post-training diagnostics only — minimizing the lower certificate could be gamed by maximizing $\|e(0)\|$.

## Results & Highlights

**Experiment 1: Nonlinear radial-growth plus rotation system** (2D, $\dot{x} = (1+\alpha\|x\|^2)x + \beta Jx$, $\alpha=0.4$, $\beta=6$, $T=1$).
- The skew-symmetric rotation part $\beta J$ inflates the Lipschitz constant to $L_{B_R} = 1 + 3\alpha R^2 + |\beta|$ but does not affect the one-sided constants ($\ell_{B_R}=1$, $\mu_{B_R} = 1+3\alpha R^2$).
- At $t=1$: true error $3.86\times10^{-2}$, global lower certificate $3.30\times10^{-2}$, global one-sided upper certificate $6.01\times10^{-2}$, global Lipschitz upper ~$2.27\times10^1$ (off plot scale).
- The two-sided band $[3.30, 6.01]\times10^{-2}$ tightly localizes the true error; the Lipschitz certificate is uninformative.

**Experiment 2: 96-dimensional stiff diagonal linear system** (stiffness ratio $10^4$, $T=1$).
- $m(A) = -10^4$, $M(A) = -1$, $\|A\|_2 = 10^4$: dissipative system, no positive strong monotonicity lower bound from scalar certificate.
- Finite-probe signed-residual certificate (Theorem 2) gives lower bound $7.94\times10^{-9}$, approximately 66.5% of the true error $1.19\times10^{-8}$.
- One-sided upper certificate $6.51\times10^{-5}$ remains finite and plotable; Lipschitz-based upper certificate ($\propto\|A\|_2 = 10^4$) overflows the plot scale entirely.

**Experiment 3: Certificate-informed training on unstable oscillatory system** ($A = \begin{pmatrix}0.35 & -20 \\ 20 & 0.35\end{pmatrix}$, $M(A)=0.35$, $\|A\|_2=20.003$).
- Three stage-2 branches: standard continuation, one-sided upper-certificate training ($U_{M(A)}$), Lipschitz upper-certificate training ($U_{\|A\|_2}$).
- Final true errors: one-sided trained $5.68\times10^{-4}$, standard $4.43\times10^{-3}$, Lipschitz $9.79\times10^{-3}$.
- One-sided final upper certificate $2.26\times10^{-2}$; Lipschitz-based final upper certificate ~$5.70\times10^7$ — demonstrating that using the correct (sharper) constant in training is critical.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High within its scope. Lower bounds for PINN errors have been explicitly identified as an open problem in the prior literature ([1], [12]); this paper is the first to close it for ODEs. The weakening of upper-bound assumptions from Lipschitz to one-sided Lipschitz is a genuine and quantitatively significant improvement (demonstrated concretely with a factor-of-400 gap in the rotation example). The finite-probe lower certificate for linear hard-IC systems is a principled and novel repair.

**Rigor:** The paper is mathematically rigorous throughout. All main results (Theorems 1–8, Lemmas 1–2, Corollaries 1–2) carry complete proofs. Assumptions are stated with precision; admissible vs. sharp constants are carefully distinguished. The only caveat is Remark 11, which correctly acknowledges that the RK4 remainder becomes "conditionally rigorous" if the derivative suprema $D_q(T_\text{cert})$ are estimated numerically rather than certified analytically.

**Reproducibility:** The framework depends only on the trained PINN, its residual, and the local system constants — no exact solution required. The auxiliary RK4 ODE reduces the certification pipeline to standard numerics. However, no code repository is linked in the paper, and readers must implement the full pipeline from the theoretical description. The examples (radial-growth system, 96d stiff diagonal, unstable oscillator) are well-specified enough to reproduce.

**Significance:** Moderate-to-high for verified scientific ML on ODEs. The two-sided certificate is a genuine step toward certifiable deployment of PINNs in safety-critical settings. The practical impact is currently limited by the ODE scope and the need for verified local constants, but the Hilbert-space analogy (Remark 6) and the authors' stated future work on PDE extension suggest a natural roadmap.

**Red flags:**
- Scope is restricted to ODEs; PDE coverage (far more common in PINN applications) is stated as future work.
- The scalar lower certificate is uninformative for dissipative systems ($\ell_D \le 0$), which includes many physically relevant systems (stable equilibria, stiff relaxation). The finite-probe repair only works for linear systems; for nonlinear hard-IC PINNs, "nontrivial lower repair would require additional assumptions or observables... and is left for future work."
- Band widths $\bar{E}(t) - \underline{E}(t)$ grow exponentially with time in expanding regimes; long-horizon certification may be impractical.
- The certified domain D must contain both the exact and PINN trajectories — certifying this containment is itself a non-trivial precondition, discussed briefly but not fully resolved.

## Strengths

- Fills a concrete and previously acknowledged gap: rigorous a posteriori lower bounds for PINNs.
- The weakening from Lipschitz to one-sided Lipschitz for upper bounds is quantitatively impactful, not merely a theoretical nicety — demonstrated with a factor >300x improvement in the rotation example.
- All certification quantities are computable from the trained network alone (residual + local system constants); no access to the true solution needed.
- Three qualitatively different numerical examples cover genuinely distinct regimes: expansive nonlinear, high-dimensional stiff linear, and unstable oscillatory with certificate-guided training.
- The finite-probe lower certificate preserves informativeness in the hardest case (dissipative, hard-IC) and achieves 66.5% of the true error in the 96d experiment.
- Honest delineation of when each certificate is and is not informative (Remark 4, Remark 11, Section 7 limitations).

## Limitations

- **ODE-only**: Extension to PDEs requires accounting for spatial operators, boundary conditions, and semigroup stability theory — acknowledged as future work.
- **Scalar lower certificate uninformative for dissipative systems** ($\ell_D \le 0$): the repair (Theorem 2) is limited to linear systems; nonlinear hard-IC PINNs lack a general lower repair.
- **Local constants must be certified**: computing tight, verified $\ell_D$, $\mu_D$ requires bounding the symmetric part of the Jacobian on D, which itself may require rigorous arithmetic or sum-of-squares methods — not discussed.
- **Certified domain containment**: verifying that PINN trajectory stays in D throughout $\mathbb{T}$ is a precondition, but no algorithm for this check is provided.
- **Exponential growth of band width**: in expansive regimes, certified bands may widen rapidly with T, limiting long-horizon utility.
- **Conservative residual majorants**: the RK4 error remainder and the residual majorant $\delta(t) \ge \|\mathcal{R}_\varphi(t)\|$ introduce conservatism; sharper majorants are listed as future work.
- No open-source code is mentioned.

## Relevance

本文针对物理信息神经网络在求解常微分方程时的可靠性认证问题，提出了首个双侧后验误差界框架，对于关注科学机器学习的可验证性、安全关键部署或数值分析的研究者具有直接参考价值；由于当前工作仅覆盖ODE（不含PDE），且下界在耗散系统中存在退化问题，与主要研究偏微分方程或广义神经网络泛化理论的方向关联性有限。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. For nonlinear hard-IC PINNs with dissipative dynamics ($\ell_D \le 0$), what additional structural assumptions (e.g., adjoint-remainder bounds, Hamiltonian structure, Lyapunov functions) would enable a nontrivial lower certificate analogous to the finite-probe repair for linear systems?
2. Can the one-sided Lipschitz constant $\mu_D$ be certified rigorously (e.g., via sum-of-squares or interval arithmetic over the Jacobian's symmetric part) at acceptable computational cost for nonlinear systems of practical dimension?
3. How does the certified band width scale with integration horizon $T$ for typical PINN applications in, e.g., climate or molecular dynamics simulation, and at what $T$ does the certificate become too conservative to be informative?
4. The Hilbert-space PDE analogue (Remark 6) is presented conceptually; which class of parabolic or hyperbolic PDEs can be handled first, and does the semigroup coercivity assumption translate to a practically checkable condition?
5. Does the two-stage certificate-informed training improve sample efficiency (i.e., reach a given accuracy with fewer collocation points), or only improve final accuracy given a fixed training budget?
