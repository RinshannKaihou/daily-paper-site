> **一句话总结：** 本文在信用卡交易表格数据合成场景下系统实验发现，LLM的ICL对高基数类别列存在"类别先验锁定"硬性天花板（TVD≈1.0），而LoRA微调虽可突破该限制，但会使DCR比值降至1.0以下并产生结构化输出不稳定（Mistral-7B 50%数据微调时100%生成失败）。

> **TL;DR:** ICL for tabular data generation can steadily improve numerical column fidelity with more examples but hits an immovable ceiling on high-cardinality categorical columns — TVD stays near 1.0 regardless of shot count. LoRA fine-tuning breaks through this ceiling but crosses the memorization boundary (DCR < 1.0) and, in the worst case, completely destabilizes structured output generation.

## 核心贡献

- **命名并刻画"类别先验锁定"失效模式：** ICL即便使用10个样本，高基数类别列（如含494个标签的`job`字段）的TVD仍接近1.0，而数值列TVD随样本数单调下降至0.135，两者呈鲜明不对称。
- **首次系统量化ICL与LoRA在结构可靠性、分布保真度、特征相关性保持和隐私风险四个维度上的权衡：** 发现它们并非连续的"调节旋钮"，而是在分布类型上存在质的跳跃。
- **隐私风险量化：** 所有LoRA配置的DCR比值均低于1.0（Qwen 10%: 0.8655，50%: 0.8609），且少数类欺诈类别再现率极低（0.12%~0.14% vs. 真实0.58%），揭示了微调在分布拟合与记忆化之间的固有张力。
- **Mistral-7B LoRA 50%配置完全失效：** 在16962次尝试中仅生成1000条有效记录，错误率100%，为结构化输出崩溃提供了一个极端案例。

## Key Contributions

- **Categorical prior lock-in as a named failure mode:** Across both Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct and Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3, categorical TVD for the high-cardinality `job` field (494 distinct labels, highly imbalanced) remains near 1.0 under all ICL configurations (0 to 10 examples), while numerical TVD improves monotonically. The failure propagates to inter-feature dependencies and minority class omission.
- **Four-dimensional evaluation framework:** Structural reliability (error rate), distributional fidelity (TVD per feature, aggregate), inter-feature correlation preservation (Pearson matrix error), and privacy/memorization risk (DCR Ratio + exact copy rate) — applied consistently across zero-shot, 1/5/10-shot ICL, and LoRA (10%/50% data exposure).
- **LoRA memorization quantification:** All LoRA configurations fall below the DCR = 1.0 memorization threshold. Notably, the exact copy rate for Qwen2.5-7B actually decreases from 0.10% to 0.05% with more exposure — showing distributional proximity and literal duplication are distinct phenomena.
- **Structural collapse under fine-tuning:** Mistral-7B LoRA at 50% data exposure generates 0 valid records out of 1000 attempts (100% error rate), a concrete demonstration of output format destabilization from fine-tuning.

## Methodology

**Dataset.** A publicly available credit card transaction log with binary fraud labels. Each record has 7 numerical features (transaction amount `amt`, cardholder/merchant geographic coordinates, city population `city_pop`, Unix timestamp `unix_time`) and 4 categorical features (`category`, `gender`, `job`, `state`). The `job` field has 494 distinct labels with a maximally imbalanced distribution (most common label: 0.75% of records). Geographic coordinates are strongly correlated (r > 0.99 between cardholder and merchant coordinates). Only 0.58% of transactions are fraudulent, making minority class reproduction a key stress test.

**Models.** Two open-weight, instruction-tuned 7B decoder-only transformers: Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct and Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3, selected for local deployment under data residency constraints.

**Generation strategies.** Three paradigms tested across all configurations:
- Zero-shot ICL (schema prompt only)
- Few-shot ICL: 1, 5, or 10 in-context example records
- LoRA fine-tuning: trained on 10% or 50% of real data, then prompted zero-shot

**Generation pipeline.** Records generated one at a time via temperature sampling (T=0.8, top-p=0.95, repetition penalty=1.05). A record is valid if it parses as well-formed JSON with all required fields and correct type signatures. Invalid records are discarded and regenerated until 10,000 valid records are collected.

**Evaluation metrics.**
- *Structural reliability:* Error rate = fraction of generation attempts rejected by the validation pipeline.
- *Distributional fidelity:* Total Variation Distance (TVD) computed per feature between synthetic and real marginal distributions (continuous features discretized into 50 equal-width bins).
- *Privacy/memorization:* DCR Ratio — average distance from each synthetic record to its nearest real neighbor, normalized by the average nearest-neighbor distance within the real dataset. Below 1.0 indicates proximity to memorization. Also reports exact copy rate.
- *Inter-feature correlation:* Maximum and total absolute Pearson correlation error across the 7 numerical features + fraud label.
- *Fraud class reproduction:* Prevalence of `is_fraud=1` in synthetic output vs. 0.58% baseline.

The experiment also tests cardinality scaling: TVD is evaluated on synthetic Zipf-distributed (α=0.5) categorical features at cardinalities 50, 100, and 500, averaged over 30 runs, to confirm lock-in is a general ICL property rather than dataset-specific.

## Results & Highlights

**Structural reliability (Table 1).**
- Qwen2.5-7B is highly reliable: zero-shot ICL error rate 1.17%, dropping to 0% at 5-shot. Both LoRA configs at 0%.
- Mistral-7B under ICL: error rates 0.89%–3.59%, acceptable. LoRA 10%: 41.04% error rate. LoRA 50%: 100% error (complete collapse; excluded from further analysis).

**Numerical distributional fidelity (Table 2).**
- `amt` is well-reproduced by both models across all strategies (TVD < 0.1).
- Geographic features are the hardest numerical columns for ICL — Qwen's longitude TVD stays at 0.2937 even at 10 examples; only LoRA brings it below 0.10.
- `unix_time` shows model-specific anomalies: Mistral achieves near-zero TVD at zero-shot (0.0005) not from genuine learning but from a pre-training default that happens to match the dataset's density peak — TVD actually worsens at 5-shot (0.1986), exposing the coincidence.

**Categorical distributional fidelity (Table 3).**
- `job` TVD: Qwen ICL ranges 0.9840–1.000 regardless of shot count. LoRA 10% reduces it to 0.1551, LoRA 50% to 0.1430 — still above the 0.10 threshold.
- `gender` is well-handled by ICL for Mistral (TVD ≈ 0.001–0.345) but not for Qwen (0.408–0.452 under ICL, dropping to 0.0012–0.0059 under LoRA).
- `state` and `category` show similar patterns: ICL leaves large residual TVD; LoRA brings both below 0.06 for Qwen.

**Aggregate TVD (Table 4).**
- Qwen ICL: numerical TVD 0.489→0.215 (zero-shot to 10-shot); categorical TVD oscillates 0.589–0.643 with NO directional improvement. LoRA 50% collapses categorical TVD to 0.061 — a discontinuous jump, not a gradual improvement.
- The ICL→LoRA transition for categorical fidelity is qualitative, not quantitative.

**Cardinality scaling (Fig. 2).** TVD on synthetic Zipf-distributed categoricals at 50/100/500 labels: for Qwen, all curves (1/5/10-shot) are virtually identical regardless of cardinality — confirming lock-in is invariant to shot count and cardinality within this range.

**Fraud class reproduction (Sec. 5.4).** Neither model produces a single fraudulent transaction under any ICL configuration (complete minority-class failure). LoRA partially restores the fraud class: Qwen 10% produces 12 fraudulent records (0.12%), Qwen 50% produces 14 (0.14%), Mistral 10% produces 5 (0.05%) — all still well below the real 0.58%.

**Inter-feature correlation preservation (Table 6).**
- Zero-shot: geographic correlations collapse to near-zero in synthetic output (max pairwise error 0.40, total error 4.34).
- 10-shot: total error halves to 2.26, but geographic clustering around urban centers persists.
- LoRA 50%: total error drops to 0.82 (5x reduction). Remaining worst pair is `amt/is_fraud` (0.15), reflecting the fraud underrepresentation.

**Privacy/memorization (Table 5).**
- All ICL configurations: DCR well above 1.0 (range 1.81–37.3), no exact copies (except one isolated 0.10% case at Qwen 10-shot).
- All LoRA configurations: DCR below 1.0 (Qwen: 0.8655 at 10%, 0.8609 at 50%; Mistral: 0.9512 at 10%). The memorization signal saturates early and does not scale with training volume.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate. The observation that ICL cannot override pre-training priors is consistent with existing ICL theory literature. The value here is the controlled tabular setting, the specific naming and quantification of the categorical ceiling, and the integrated privacy analysis. The contribution is more diagnostic/empirical than mechanistic.
- **Rigor:** Reasonable for the scope. Using two architecturally distinct 7B models with different training data is a meaningful design choice. However, conclusions are confined to 7B models; scaling behavior is unknown and likely important (frontier models with stronger instruction following may behave differently). The Zipf cardinality experiment is a useful generalization check.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong positive signals — open-weight models, public dataset, public code with tagged release, fixed random seed, fully described pipeline. The paper is reproducible by practitioners.
- **Significance:** High practical relevance for privacy-preserving tabular data synthesis (a real enterprise use case, especially under data residency constraints). The specific finding that ICL and LoRA occupy fundamentally different distributional regimes — not a continuum — is actionable for system designers.
- **Red flags:**
  - Single dataset (credit card transactions). The categorical features in this dataset (job with 494 labels, geographic coordinates) are unusually challenging. Results may overstate the generality of lock-in for datasets with lower cardinality or less domain specificity.
  - TVD is a marginal statistic only. The authors acknowledge this and add correlation analysis, but joint distributional fidelity (e.g., via classifier-based metrics or FID analogues) is absent.
  - The mechanism of LoRA structural collapse in Mistral is unexplained. The 100% failure at 50% exposure is a striking result deserving deeper investigation.
  - No ablation on prompt format, system prompt, or quantization — variables that practitioners would naturally tune.

## Strengths

- Clear experimental design with a single, well-characterized real-world dataset and interpretable metrics.
- Honest disclosure of LoRA's costs (memorization, structural instability) without advocating it as a simple fix.
- The cardinality scaling experiment (Fig. 2) showing that all ICL shot counts produce virtually identical TVD curves is particularly compelling evidence for the lock-in claim.
- Privacy analysis using both DCR ratio and exact copy rate correctly distinguishes distributional proximity from literal memorization.
- Public code release enables direct replication and extension.

## Limitations

- Single dataset; highly domain-specific categorical structure (494-label occupation field, geographic coordinates with spatial correlation) may not generalize to lower-cardinality or semantically simpler tabular domains.
- Only 7B-scale models; frontier models (e.g., GPT-4o, Llama-3 70B) with stronger instruction following and richer pre-training may partially overcome lock-in.
- No retrieval-augmented generation, constrained decoding (which could enforce categorical distributions), or other intermediate adaptation strategies are tested.
- Memorization is measured via DCR and exact copies only; membership inference attacks or attribute inference are not evaluated, leaving the full privacy threat model undercharacterized.
- The structural collapse in Mistral-7B LoRA 50% is documented but not explained — the failure mechanism (catastrophic forgetting of the JSON schema, gradient instability, learning rate sensitivity) is unaddressed.
- TVD measures marginal distributions only; no classifier-based utility metric (e.g., Train-on-Synthetic, Test-on-Real accuracy) is reported to connect distributional fidelity to downstream ML impact.

## Relevance

本文对于研究LLM在隐私敏感、分布偏移场景下的结构化数据合成能力具有直接参考价值，尤其是对于需要在本地部署（数据驻留约束）条件下权衡ICL与参数微调策略的应用研究者。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does categorical prior lock-in diminish at larger scales (34B, 70B, frontier models)? If it is scale-dependent, what is the effective threshold where ICL begins to override pre-training categorical priors?
2. Would constrained decoding (e.g., enforcing a vocabulary restricted to the dataset's categorical label set) bypass lock-in, or is the failure upstream of token sampling in the probability distribution itself?
3. What is the actual downstream ML utility impact? Does the near-zero TVD on numerical features achieved by 10-shot ICL translate to acceptable Train-on-Synthetic/Test-on-Real classifier performance, making categorical lock-in less critical in practice?
4. Can the Mistral-7B LoRA structural collapse at 50% be attributed to a specific training hyperparameter (learning rate, rank, number of steps), and is it preventable with constrained output formatting during fine-tuning?
5. How does the DCR-based memorization risk compare to formal differential privacy guarantees? Is the sub-1.0 DCR threshold a meaningful privacy boundary or an overly conservative proxy?
