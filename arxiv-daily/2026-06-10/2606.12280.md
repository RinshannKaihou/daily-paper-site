> **一句话总结：** 来自 Transformer Lab 的工程师对 Ideogram 4.0（9.3B 双分支流匹配 DiT）实施 INT8 W8A8 量化（SmoothQuant + 17 层 FFN down-projection 混合精度保护），在 Ampere RTX 3090 上以统计显著的方式与 FP8 质量持平并超越 NF4（+1.9 CLIP），同时发现 GGUF Q4_K 以相同磁盘占用 Pareto 优于 NF4；量化权重已在 Hugging Face 公开发布。

> **TL;DR:** Transformer Lab quantizes Ideogram 4.0 (a 9.3B dual-branch flow-matching DiT) to INT8 W8A8 for Ampere RTX 3090s that lack FP8 tensor cores. With SmoothQuant (α=0.5) and mixed-precision protection of 17 FFN down-projection layers (~8% of linears), the recipe matches FP8 quality on a 200-prompt benchmark (paired bootstrap CIs include zero on Pick and CLIP) and beats NF4 by +1.9 CLIP. GGUF Q4_K is the Pareto winner on the quality-memory frontier at equal on-disk size to NF4. Quantized weights are released on Hugging Face under the model's non-commercial license.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 INT8 W8A8 量化方案（逐通道权重 + 逐 token 动态激活 + SmoothQuant α=0.5），在 200-prompt 配对引导区间实验中与 FP8 质量无统计显著差异（Pick Δ=−0.013，CI [−0.12,+0.09]；CLIP Δ=−0.125，CI [−0.73,+0.47]），同时超越 NF4 +1.9 CLIP（CI [+1.21,+2.64]，不含零）。
- 发现 FFN down-projection 层（`feed_forward.w2`）是扩散 Transformer 中量化最脆弱的层集合：其激活峰态 × max-abs 脆弱评分约为次高层的 10 倍，保护仅 17 层（≈8% 线性层，≈1.5 GB bf16 额外开销）即可闭合几乎全部质量缺口（N=17 时 CLIP 17.42，N=8 时骤降至 16.01）。
- 首次对该模型类别进行逐类别 OCR 量化分析：使用 EasyOCR 精确匹配准确率和归一化编辑距离（NED），确认文字清晰度在量化后得到保留（INT8 OCR NED 0.704，与 FP8 的 0.715 相当，优于 NF4 的 0.760）。
- GGUF Q4_K（4.5 bpw，10.4 GB）在相同磁盘尺寸下超越 NF4：Pick +0.96（CI [+0.77,+1.15]），CLIP +3.57（CI [+2.65,+4.51]），是质量-显存 Pareto 最优点；Q8_0（8.5 bpw）质量中性。
- 量化权重已在 Hugging Face 以 Ideogram 4.0 非商业研究许可公开发布（INT8 W8A8 和 GGUF Q4_K 各一份）。

## Key Contributions

- **INT8 W8A8 recipe** matching FP8 quality on Ampere (no FP8 tensor cores): per-channel static weights + per-token dynamic activations + SmoothQuant (α=0.5) + mixed-precision protection of N=17 high-fragility FFN down-projections.
- **Activation fragility profiling**: per-block sweep of max-abs / std / kurtosis over 8 calibration prompts across denoising steps, ranked by mean kurtosis × max-abs. Ranking is trajectory-stable (Spearman 0.930 between early- and late-step halves), justifying a static profile.
- **Per-category OCR evaluation under quantization**: first such analysis for this model class, using EasyOCR exact-match and NED on 100 text-rendering prompts (63 with machine-checkable targets).
- **GGUF Q4_K for diffusion transformers**: custom NumPy Q4_K superblock quantizer + Torch dequantization kernel (verified bit-exact against reference decoder), since no public Q4_K encoder for DiTs existed.
- **Precise limitation accounting**: INT8 gains are quality-preserving, not yet a speed or memory win on Ampere — the paper explicitly states this and positions a fused Ampere INT8 GEMM (CUTLASS/ViDiT-Q-style) as the required next step.

## Methodology

**Model architecture.** Ideogram 4.0 is a 9.3B flow-matching DiT with a single-stream 34-layer backbone, distributed for classifier-free guidance as two separate-weight copies (conditional + unconditional branch, ~9.3 GB each at FP8, 211 linear layers × 2). It is conditioned by a Qwen3-VL-8B text encoder; the VAE and text encoder are left at published precision throughout.

**Target hardware.** A SkyPilot cluster of 2×RTX 3090 (24 GB, Ampere). Ampere has fast native INT8 but no FP8 tensor cores; the published FP8 checkpoint runs by dequantizing to bf16 each forward at 172.9 s/image (~5% slower than NF4 at 164.5 s/image).

**INT8 W8A8 quantization.**
- Scope: all 211 linear layers of each DiT branch; text encoder and VAE excluded.
- Weights: per-channel (per-output-row) INT8, static.
- Activations: per-token dynamic INT8 scaling, absorbing timestep dependence without a static calibration table.
- SmoothQuant (α=0.5): smoothing vector λ_j = s_j^α / max_i |W_ij|^{1−α} migrates activation outliers into weights.
- Calibration set: 128 prompts (disjoint from evaluation); 8-prompt fragility profiling subset, also disjoint.
- Mixed-precision protection: top-N fragile layers kept in bf16 (no activation quantization). N=17 chosen via a protection-size ladder on a 50-prompt slice; this covers FFN down-projections plus the time-embedding output.

**Activation fragility profiling.** Per linear in the conditional DiT branch, records max-abs, std, kurtosis over 8 denoising steps across 8 prompts. Fragility score = mean kurtosis × max-abs. The ranking is trajectory-stable (Spearman 0.930), so a small-budget early sweep is sufficient. FFN down-projections dominate (~10× the runner-up score), consistent with module-type findings in prior work.

**GGUF quantization.** Each DiT branch serialized independently to Q8_0 (8.5 bpw, round-to-nearest) and Q4_K (4.5 bpw, k-quants superblock method). Because no existing GGUF toolchain provides a Q4_K encoder for DiTs, the authors implement a NumPy Q4_K superblock quantizer and Torch dequantization kernel, verified bit-exact against a reference decoder.

**Evaluation protocol.**
- Three disjoint prompt sets from PartiPrompts with deterministic seeds: calibration (n=128), quality benchmark (n=200, category-stratified, no text prompts), text benchmark (n=100, 63 with machine-checkable OCR targets).
- Identical generation tuple for all variants: seed 1000, 48 steps, 1024×1024, V4_QUALITY_48 preset.
- Standalone quality: PickScore and CLIPScore (in-house; no cross-paper absolutes).
- Text quality: EasyOCR exact-match accuracy and normalized edit distance (NED, lower is better).
- Reference fidelity: PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS (AlexNet) vs. FP8 images at same seed.
- Statistics: 10,000-sample paired per-prompt bootstrap 95% CI.

## Results & Highlights

**Headline numbers (Table 1, 200-prompt quality benchmark):**

| Variant | Pick | CLIP | OCR NED | s/img | Size (GB) | Weights |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| FP8 (ref) | 18.97 | 17.54 | 0.715 | 172.9 | 18.6 | 8-bit (deq.) |
| NF4 | 18.50 | 15.53 | 0.760 | 164.5 | ~10.4 | 4-bit W |
| INT8 (ours) | **18.96** | **17.42** | **0.704** | 184–185 | 18.6 | 8-bit W+A |
| Q4_K (ours) | 19.46 | 19.10 | 0.62–0.73 | 203.3 | 10.4 | 4.5-bit W |

- **INT8 vs FP8**: Pick Δ=−0.013 (CI [−0.12,+0.09]), CLIP Δ=−0.125 (CI [−0.73,+0.47]) — both CIs include zero. INT8 is statistically indistinguishable from FP8.
- **INT8 vs NF4**: Pick Δ=+0.46 (CI [+0.33,+0.59]), CLIP Δ=+1.90 (CI [+1.21,+2.64]) — both exclude zero. Robust improvement.
- **Q4_K vs NF4** (at equal disk size): Pick +0.96 (CI [+0.77,+1.15]), CLIP +3.57 (CI [+2.65,+4.51]) — Pareto winner.
- **Q8_0 vs baseline**: Pick 18.713 vs ref 18.714 on 50-prompt subset — quality neutral.

**Reference fidelity (Table 2, PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS vs FP8):**
INT8 and NF4 are in the same perceptual-distance band. INT8 passes the PSNR≥21 "matches 16-bit" threshold (21.42); both INT8 and NF4 straddle the LPIPS≤0.30 line. The key finding: INT8 deviates from FP8 by about the same pixel distance as NF4, yet INT8 preserves standalone quality (Pick/CLIP) while NF4 loses −1.89 CLIP.

**Ablation (Table 3, 50-prompt slice, CLIP):**
- Naive W8A8, no smoothing, no protection: CLIP 14.30
- + SmoothQuant only (α=0.5): CLIP 16.56
- + Protection only (top-17): CLIP 16.81
- + Both (final recipe): CLIP 17.54
- Protection ladder N=8: CLIP 16.01; N=17: CLIP 17.42 — sharp knee of −1.41 CLIP over 9 layers.
- Protection is the dominant lever (78–92% of the recovery); the two effects are sub-additive.

**OCR.** INT8 OCR NED 0.704 is on par with FP8 (0.715) and ahead of NF4 (0.760). Exact-match accuracy is near zero for all variants — a property of the base model on text prompts, not quantization. Fine secondary lettering is the most quantization-fragile content; main headline text survives in all variants.

**Latency.** INT8 runs at ~184–185 s/image — ~6% slower than FP8 (172.9) and ~12% slower than NF4 (164.5), because no fused INT8 GEMM exists in the ideogram4 stack (torch.compile crashes on the INT8 dequant kernel and on the FP8 kernel). Q4_K runs at 203.3 s/image via a Torch dequant path.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Low-to-moderate on the quantization methodology side — SmoothQuant, per-token dynamic activations, and GGUF k-quants are all established tools. The genuine novelties are: (1) applying and evaluating these on a dual-branch 9.3B commercial DiT at this scale, (2) the per-category OCR-under-quantization evaluation protocol, and (3) the custom Q4_K encoder for DiTs. This is a strong applied engineering paper, not a methods paper.

**Rigor:** Above average. Paired bootstrap CIs (n=10,000 resamples) throughout the main results are exemplary for a PTQ paper. The ablation is specific, has a sharp quantitative finding (the N=17 knee), and cleanly separates the contributions of smoothing vs. protection. The paper is also honest about where its statistics are weaker: the ablation and Q8_0 numbers use 50-prompt subsets without error bars, and the protection cutoff N=17 is chosen on data that overlaps with the evaluation benchmark (acknowledged as a limitation).

**Reproducibility:** Partial. The methodology (SmoothQuant + fragility profiling + GGUF) is fully described and the quantized weights are released. However, the full-precision source weights are not public, so independently verifying the quantization pipeline from scratch requires model access. Latency numbers come from a single cluster and are not replicated on independent hardware.

**Significance:** Practically high for the community. The GGUF Q4_K recommendation is immediately actionable on consumer hardware. The INT8 recipe documents a quality-preserving substrate for a future fused Ampere kernel, providing a concrete roadmap. The OCR evaluation protocol is a reusable contribution for the field.

**Red flags:**
- Single seed (1000) for all quality evaluations. Bootstrap CIs are over prompts, not seeds, so seed-specific variance is uncharacterized.
- Protection cutoff N=17 is selected on a 50-prompt slice that is a subset of the 200-prompt benchmark — a mild selection/evaluation overlap.
- No independent hardware replication of latency numbers.
- CLIP/Pick scores are weak judges for fine typography; the OCR probe partially compensates, but ImageReward/HPSv2 would strengthen text claims (acknowledged).
- Comparison is against published FP8/NF4 variants only, not against academic PTQ methods (GPTQ, AWQ, ViDiT-Q) — this is deliberate (tuned recipe vs. method bake-off) but limits positioning relative to the literature.

## Strengths

- Statistically honest throughout: paired bootstrap CIs rather than point estimates, and explicit scope on where statistics are weaker.
- Transparent about what INT8 does and does not deliver: quality preserved, memory not reduced vs. FP8, speed not yet improved on Ampere without a fused kernel.
- Novel OCR evaluation per category, with a self-implemented EasyOCR pipeline on machine-checkable targets — a reusable contribution.
- Custom Q4_K GGUF encoder for DiTs fills a real toolchain gap and is released.
- Sharp, actionable ablation result (protect 17 FFN down-projections; the protection-size knee is a concrete engineering guideline).
- Quantized weights publicly released on Hugging Face under the model's existing license.

## Limitations

- No memory reduction for INT8 vs FP8 on Ampere: INT8 weights are 8-bit (18.6 GB total) — same footprint as FP8 (which dequantizes to bf16 on-the-fly). Memory win is deferred until a fused INT8 GEMM is available.
- INT8 is actually slower than FP8 and NF4 (~6–12%) due to the lack of fused INT8 GEMM in the ideogram4 stack; torch.compile fails.
- Single-seed evaluation; seed-level variance not reported.
- Methodology generalizes in principle but is validated only on this specific architecture and hardware.
- Reference baseline is FP8, not BF16/FP32 (no public BF16 release exists); "fidelity to FP8" is a proxy for full-precision fidelity.
- Q4_K reference fidelity (PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS) is measured on a 50-prompt slice only, not the full 200-prompt benchmark.

## Relevance

本文对关注大规模扩散模型高效推理与消费级部署的研究者高度相关：INT8 W8A8 配合 SmoothQuant 和 FFN down-projection 保护的工程方案，以及 GGUF Q4_K 的 Pareto 优势，可直接迁移至 FLUX、SD3 等类似单流 DiT 架构；逐类别 OCR 评估协议也为量化评测提供了新的方法论参考。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does the N=17 FFN down-projection protection threshold transfer to other single-stream DiT architectures (FLUX.1, SD3-Medium)? Is the sharp knee a general property of this module type or specific to Ideogram 4.0's training regime?
2. When a fused Ampere INT8 GEMM (CUTLASS/ViDiT-Q-style) is implemented, what latency reduction is achievable? The paper establishes the quality substrate — the speed payoff is the natural next experiment.
3. The fragility profiling uses only 8 prompts over a few denoising steps. How sensitive is the final quality to calibration set size and diversity, particularly for edge-case content (complex multilingual text, fine-grained textures)?
4. Does the OCR NED advantage of INT8 over NF4 hold for non-Latin scripts (Chinese, Arabic, Devanagari), where glyph complexity is higher and quantization-induced blurring may be more damaging?
5. Could step-caching methods (TACache for flow matching) compose with the INT8 recipe without quality regression, and would this combination make Q4_K's slower Torch dequant path competitive?
6. The protection strategy selects layers by activation fragility score (kurtosis × max-abs). Would a learned or task-specific selection criterion (e.g., sensitivity-based, Hessian-based) yield a smaller protected set with equivalent or better quality?
