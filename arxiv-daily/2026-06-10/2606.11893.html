Why this matters

Brain signals become an active guidance source, not a passive correlation target — they add information orthogonal to language-only supervision, advancing brain-inspired AI from description to function.

Critical edge

NARI fails on 2 of 6 models (Qwen2-7B, Mistral-7B) for lack of incorrect examples; applicability to near-ceiling frontier models is explicitly deferred. Only 10 fMRI subjects.

Methodology 方法

fMRI source

OpenNeuro ds003076 (Lytle et al., 2020) — 10 healthy adults solving 70 deductive logic problems during scan. Two task types: syllogistic (set-inclusion) and transitive (linear ordering), both with pseudowords to strip lexical priors. ROI focus: fronto-parietal reasoning, MD network, language network.

Neural-predictivity metric

Per-layer RidgeCV → voxel prediction → ceiling-normalized Pearson r (ceiling from inter-subject neural predictivity, ≈0.26–0.30).

NARI — inference-time intervention

Constrained gradient ascent on the cosine-similarity objective S = Sim(W·r, v), with the intercept dropped to encode a systematic shift. Subject-wise directions are aggregated and applied as an additive offset to attention-module outputs at middle layers (¼–¾ depth).

NARF — representation fine-tuning

LoRA on attention modules (middle layers). MSE loss pulls representations toward NARI-successful targets; a regularizer anchors originally-correct cases. λ = 1, lr = 1e-6, 100 epochs; best model by validation across all premise permutations.

Combined objective

L = λnarf · (rep. similarity to fMRI) + CE(token, label), with λnarf = 0.1. The neural term targets only currently-incorrect cases — corrective, not global.

Strengths 优势

  • Truly interdisciplinary — proper neuroscience methodology (ceiling normalization, region specificity, GLMSingle) combined with 10-model LLM evaluation.
  • Scale — 10 LLMs across 4 families (1.5B–72B) is unusual for a brain-grounded paper.
  • External validity — second dataset (HCP Relational Processing) with a different stimulus modality.
  • Mechanistic evidence — trajectory & PCA analyses show NARF yields more discriminative reasoning paths, not just higher accuracy.
  • Clean stimuli — pseudowords strip lexical confounds from the brain–LLM comparison.
  • Open code & data — github.com/pkuxmq/Brain-guided_LLM + OpenNeuro ds003076.

Limitations 局限

  • 10 fMRI subjects — standard for cognitive neuroimaging, but small for estimating "brain directions"; subject variability is acknowledged and uncorrectable here.
  • Task scope — syllogistic + transitive only; inductive / abductive / causal / math are future work. BOLD's slow hemodynamics limit fast-CoT guidance.
  • Deployment cost — full pipeline needs new task-fMRI; distilling brain directions into computational proxies is open.
  • Near-ceiling models — NARI fails for Qwen2-7B & Mistral-7B (insufficient errors); frontier models not tested.
  • FOLIO framing — 13.2% reported as max-over-5-runs, not mean (a more optimistic framing).
  • No RLHF / DPO interaction — behavior under post-trained models is untested.

Questions for further exploration 待探索

01

Can brain-derived steering directions be approximated without fMRI — e.g., by contrastive training on correct vs. incorrect reasoning traces?

02

How do neural-predictivity and NARI/NARF effectiveness change after RLHF or DPO, where reasoning representations may already be more structured?

03

Subject-coupling varies — which properties of a subject's neural patterns make their fMRI data most useful for guiding a given model? Is there a model–brain pairing effect?

04

Could MEG / EEG (higher temporal resolution) enable finer-grained guidance for multi-step CoT, where intermediate steps have distinct temporal signatures?

05

Can the framework extend to other capabilities with known neural substrates — theory of mind, causal inference, spatial reasoning?

06

The fine-tuning uses ~70 fMRI problems × 10 subjects. Does the observed generalization reflect the pseudoword design rather than truly general reasoning gains?
