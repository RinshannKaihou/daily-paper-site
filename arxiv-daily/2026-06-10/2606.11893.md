> **一句话总结：** 本文利用任务fMRI大脑激活信号，通过推理时表示干预（NARI）和参数微调（NARF）直接引导LLM内部表示，在演绎推理任务上实现最高13%的绝对准确率提升，在10个规模1.5B–72B的LLM中均有效，将LLM-大脑对齐研究从相关性描述推进至因果性功能增强。

> **TL;DR:** LLM internal representations partially align with fMRI activity in fronto-parietal deductive reasoning regions (~76% of explainable variance at aggregate level, dropping to ~27% within reasoning subtypes). Leveraging this partial alignment, the authors introduce two brain-guided methods — NARI (Neural Activation guided Representation Intervention, applied at inference time) and NARF (Neural Activation guided Representation Fine-tuning, applied during training) — that steer LLM representations along directions derived from the joint structure of model and brain representations. Across 10 LLMs (1.5B–72B), these methods yield up to 13% absolute accuracy gains on deductive reasoning, transfer across reasoning types, generalize to out-of-distribution problems, and provide complementary gains on top of language-only supervision, establishing brain signals as an active guidance source rather than a passive correlation target.

## 核心贡献

- **LLM-大脑表示对齐量化**：利用神经预测性指标（ceiling-normalized Pearson相关），系统分析10个开源LLM对fronto-parietal推理脑区任务fMRI信号的解释力，发现聚合层面约76%的可解释方差可由LLM表示预测，但特定推理类型内部的预测性下降至约27%，揭示对齐与分歧并存。
- **NARI——推理时表示干预**：在推理阶段，将答错问题的LLM表示沿由模型-大脑联合结构导出的梯度方向（去除截距项以引入系统性偏移）做受约束梯度上升，使表示进入正确答案子空间，实现100%的目标翻转成功率（在被测6个模型上）。
- **NARF——表示级参数微调**：将NARI成功案例产生的干预方向作为微调目标，通过MSE损失驱动中间层表示向大脑引导的目标靠拢，并辅以正则化保持原始正确案例的表示，使泛化能力从问题内扩展到新问题、新前提顺序和更多前提设定。
- **与语言监督正交的增益**：NARF与标准交叉熵语言标签监督结合后，在10个LLM上平均带来2.2%的额外准确率提升（范围0.5–6.4%），且在FOLIO等独立数据集上对推理类型（如命题推理）的泛化提升最高达13.2%（Mistral-7B-Instruct），证明大脑信号提供了语言监督之外的独立学习信号。
- **方法论突破**：从描述性对齐（相关分析）推进到功能性引导（直接提升任务性能），为神经科学驱动AI改进提供了可操作框架。

## Key Contributions

- **Systematic LLM-brain alignment quantification:** Using a neural-predictivity metric (ceiling-normalized cross-validated ridge regression + Pearson correlation), demonstrates that 10 open-source LLMs explain ~76% of ceiling-normalized explainable variance in fronto-parietal reasoning regions across combined syllogistic + transitive trials, but only ~27% within each specific reasoning type. This regional specificity (deductive reasoning > MD network > language network) indicates LLMs capture reasoning-related patterns beyond pure language processing.
- **NARI — inference-time representation intervention:** A constrained gradient ascent procedure that steers incorrectly-answered LLM representations along directions derived from the joint model-brain representational structure (gradient of cosine similarity to fMRI voxels, excluding intercept). Achieves 100% correction success rate on targeted incorrect cases across 6 tested mid-scale models; the generalized direction transfers to new out-of-set problems.
- **NARF — neural activation guided representation fine-tuning:** Fine-tunes attention module parameters (LoRA, middle layers 1/4–3/4 depth) via MSE loss toward NARI-successful intervention targets, with regularization on originally-correct cases. Enables generalization to new problems, permuted premise orders, more-premise settings, and propositional reasoning (a distinct reasoning type with a different neural basis).
- **Complementary gains over language supervision:** Combining NARF with cross-entropy language supervision yields a statistically significant average 2.2% gain (range 0.5–6.4%) across 10 LLMs on test problems with all premise-order permutations, validated on both generated test sets and the independent FOLIO dataset.
- **Extension to HCP relational processing:** NARI and NARF generalize to the HCP Relational Processing Task (a different fMRI dataset and stimulus modality), validating that the approach is not tied to one experimental paradigm.

## Methodology

**fMRI Dataset:** OpenNeuro ds003076 (Lytle et al., 2020) — 10 healthy adults (mean age 25.3 ± 3.4 years, originally 17 subjects; 7 excluded for missing data or poor MRI quality). Participants solved 70 deductive logic problems during fMRI: 2 types — syllogistic reasoning (set-inclusion with monosyllabic pseudowords, e.g., "All blons are pink; All pink things are young; Ken is a blon — Ken is pink?") and transitive reasoning (linear ordering with syllable-name pseudowords). Each trial: 3 premises presented sequentially (2s interval), 6s response window, binary True/False judgement. Pseudowords minimize semantic confounds. Brain regions: fronto-parietal reasoning ROIs (IFG, MeFG, MFG bilateral, PG bilateral, PPC bilateral, BG left), MD network, and core language network, with participant-specific functional localization.

**Neural Predictivity Metric:** (1) Localize top-N model units responsive to reasoning (contrasting reasoning vs. reading conditions, layer-wise); (2) extract representations from attention outputs and layer outputs at last token; (3) fit 5-fold cross-validated RidgeCV to predict voxel activations from model representations; (4) compute Pearson correlation on held-out 20%; (5) ceiling-normalize by upper-bound derived from inter-subject neural predictivity. Ceiling values: ~0.257–0.299 for reasoning regions, ~0.248–0.287 for MD, ~0.253–0.293 for language.

**NARI (Inference-time Intervention):** For each subject and layer, fit a ridge regression mapping W_sub, b_sub from model representations r to fMRI voxels v_sub. The intervention objective is S = Sim(W_sub · r, v_sub). The gradient g(x) = W_sub^T ∇_{W_sub·x} S defines the steering direction (intercept b_sub dropped to incorporate systematic representational shift). Representations are updated via constrained gradient ascent (Adam optimizer, bounded within ασ^l of original, α grid-searched in [0.1, 1.0], up to 200 steps, verified every 5 steps). Subject-wise aggregation produces a generalized direction applied as additive offset during forward pass to new problems. Intervention applied to attention-module outputs at middle layers (1/4–3/4 depth).

**NARF (Fine-tuning):** Uses successful NARI cases Q_train = {(q, {Δr^{q,l}})} as training targets. Loss per layer l: L^l = Σ_{(q,Δr^{q,l})∈Q_train} ½‖r^{q,l} − (r^{q,l}_{ori} + Δr^{q,l})‖² + λ Σ_{q∈Q_reg} ½‖r^{q,l} − r^{q,l}_{ori}‖². LoRA applied to attention modules, middle layers. λ=1 for most models; learning rate 1e-6; 100 epochs; best model by validation. All premise permutations enumerated during validation and testing to prevent order-sensitivity overfitting.

**Combined NARF + Language Supervision:** Minimizes hybrid objective L = λ_narf · (representation similarity to fMRI) + CE(output token, label), with λ_narf=0.1 for most models. Critically, the neural guidance term targets only cases the model currently answers incorrectly, providing corrective representational guidance rather than global alignment.

**Models evaluated:** Qwen2-1.5B/7B/72B-Instruct, Qwen3-4B, Llama2-7B-Chat, Llama3-8B/3.3-70B-Instruct, Mistral-7B-Instruct, Phi-4-mini-Instruct, Gemma2-9B-it. Model accuracy range: 71.4% (Llama2-7B-Chat) to 97.1% (Llama3.3-70B-Instruct), bracketing human average (86.4%).

## Results & Highlights

**Alignment findings:**
- LLMs explain ~76% of ceiling-normalized variance in deductive reasoning regions (aggregate across syllogistic + transitive); significantly higher than MD regions and language regions (p < 0.001 for both comparisons).
- Within-type predictivity drops to ~27%, significantly above untrained model baselines (p < 0.001), and regionally specific (deductive reasoning ≥ language ≥ MD for syllogistic; p = 0.001 for deductive vs. MD, p = 0.009 for deductive vs. language in transitive).
- PCA analysis shows LLM and fMRI representations partially separate syllogistic from transitive problems, reconciling the high cross-type predictivity with lower within-type values.
- Middle layers and attention outputs show higher neural alignment than final layers.

**NARI results:**
- 100% targeted correction success rate on 6 tested mid-scale models (Qwen2-1.5B/7B, Mistral-7B, Llama2-7B-Chat, Llama3-8B, Phi-4-mini) across both syllogistic and transitive problems.
- True fMRI signals substantially outperform Random Signals (model geometry only) and Random Directions baselines across all perturbation ranges.
- Excluding the intercept term improves direction quality (captures systematic shift between model and brain representations).
- Generalized direction transfers to out-of-set problems for Qwen2-1.5B, Llama2-7B-Chat, Llama3-8B, Phi-4-mini (two models fail due to too few incorrect examples for reliable direction estimation).
- NARI also works on the thinking model DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B, successfully modifying chain-of-thought reasoning trajectories.

**NARF results:**
- Robust generalization to permuted premise orders and more-premise problems (4–6 premises), indicating true generalization beyond pattern matching.
- Improvements persist across reasoning subtypes and extend to propositional reasoning (modus ponens, modus tollens, disjunction elimination — a distinct reasoning type with separate neural basis in the brain).
- Ablation confirms that the systematic shift component is important but must be combined with structural alignment.

**Combined NARF + language supervision:**
- 2.2% average accuracy gain across 10 LLMs (range 0.5–6.4%); statistically significant (paired t-tests).
- On FOLIO dataset: Mistral-7B-Instruct improves by 13.2% (considering max over 5 runs: 83.7% vs. 70.5% for language-only fine-tuning).
- Representational analysis: language supervision mainly shapes final-layer outputs; NARF induces more discriminative reasoning trajectories throughout the network, yielding clearer separation of correct vs. incorrect logics.
- Human fMRI targets outperform label-based representations as fine-tuning guidance (ablation in Sec. S11.2).

**HCP generalization:**
- NARI and NARF both generalize to HCP Relational Processing Task (different dataset, image-description stimulus modality), validating pipeline generality.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The conceptual advance from passive alignment measurement to active functional guidance is genuine and well-motivated. Brain-signal-guided representation engineering for LLMs has no direct precedent at this scale (10 models, multiple task types). The two-level deployment (NARI at inference, NARF for training) is practically useful.
- **Rigor:** Solid for the central claims. Statistical comparisons use paired t-tests with appropriate corrections; ablations are thorough (Random Signals, Random Directions, intercept ablation, region specificity). Experiments repeated 5 times with random seeds. However, the fMRI dataset is small: only 10 subjects, which limits statistical power and could mean the "generalized" brain directions are driven by a handful of subjects with high model-subject coupling.
- **Reproducibility:** Good in principle — fMRI dataset and code are public. In practice, the pipeline requires fMRI data acquisition and preprocessing expertise (MATLAB, SPM12, GLMSingle), which is a non-trivial barrier. The generated test datasets are available in the code repository.
- **Significance:** The orthogonality of neural gains to language supervision is the most practically important finding — it means brain signals genuinely add information. The 13.2% improvement on FOLIO (Mistral-7B) is impressive, though it should be noted this is the maximum over 5 runs, not the mean.
- **Red flags / caveats:**
  - The fMRI dataset covers only 70 problems and 10 subjects for a fundamentally noisy imaging modality. The ~27% within-type predictivity is modest, and the "76% aggregate" number is ceiling-normalized, so the absolute correlation is lower.
  - NARI achieves 100% targeted correction on the training problems, but the transfer experiments show failure for 2 of 6 models (Qwen2-7B, Mistral-7B), attributed to "too few incorrect examples." This is a practical limitation: models that are already highly accurate may not benefit from NARI.
  - The fine-tuning experiments (NARF) use very few training examples (70 fMRI problems × 10 subjects), which is a severe data constraint — the generalization observed may reflect the pseudoword design rather than truly general reasoning improvements.
  - No experiment tests whether the benefits persist after standard RLHF-style post-training, or whether the intervention directions change under different data distributions.
  - The paper primarily targets mid-scale models (1.5B–70B) with measurable errors; applicability to frontier models with near-ceiling accuracy is explicitly deferred.

## Strengths

- Genuinely interdisciplinary: rigorous neuroscience methodology (ceiling normalization, region specificity, GLMSingle preprocessing) combined with large-scale LLM evaluation.
- Scale of LLM evaluation (10 models across 4 families, 1.5B–72B) is unusual for a neuroscience-grounded paper and strengthens generality claims.
- Two-dataset validation (OpenNeuro deductive reasoning + HCP relational processing) with different stimulus modalities is a meaningful external validity check.
- Representation-level fine-tuning is philosophically and practically distinct from behaviour-level supervision — the mechanistic analysis (trajectory discrimination, PCA of representations) supports the claimed mechanism.
- Pseudoword-based stimuli design minimizes confounds from lexical/semantic prior knowledge, making the brain-LLM comparison cleaner.
- Code and data are publicly available, facilitating replication.

## Limitations

- **Small fMRI cohort:** 10 subjects is standard for cognitive neuroimaging but creates uncertainty in the estimated "brain directions." Subject variability is acknowledged (higher-performing subjects yield better directions), but cannot be overcome with this dataset size.
- **Task scope:** Focus is exclusively on deductive reasoning (syllogistic + transitive). Extension to inductive, abductive, causal, or mathematical reasoning is future work. fMRI's slow hemodynamic response (BOLD) also limits applicability to fast chain-of-thought reasoning.
- **Practical deployment barrier:** The full pipeline requires task-fMRI acquisition for new tasks, which is expensive, time-consuming, and not scalable. Distilling brain-derived directions into purely computational proxies is an open problem.
- **Near-ceiling models:** The framework provides less benefit to models already achieving high accuracy (NARI fails for Qwen2-7B and Mistral-7B due to insufficient errors); frontier models (GPT-4 class) are not evaluated.
- **FOLIO result reporting:** The 13.2% gain on FOLIO is reported as "max performance under five runs" rather than mean, which is a more optimistic framing; mean gains are not explicitly stated for this dataset.
- **Temporal resolution:** fMRI aggregates over slow hemodynamic responses; MEG/EEG could provide finer-grained guidance for multi-step reasoning, but are not explored here.

## Relevance

该论文将认知神经科学的fMRI信号直接引入LLM表示工程，从相关性测量推进到功能性增强，为研究LLM内部机制、推理鲁棒性和脑启发AI设计提供了重要的方法论参考，尤其对关注LLM表示可解释性、推理泛化及神经科学与AI交叉领域的研究者具有高度价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the brain-derived steering directions be approximated without actual fMRI data — e.g., by identifying synthetic "brain-like" activations through purely computational means (e.g., contrastive training on correct vs. incorrect reasoning traces)?
2. How does neural predictivity and NARI/NARF effectiveness change for models that have undergone RLHF or DPO post-training, where reasoning representations may already be more structured?
3. The authors note that model-subject coupling varies — which properties of a human subject's neural patterns make their fMRI data most useful for guiding a specific model? Is there a model-brain pairing effect?
4. Would MEG or EEG data (higher temporal resolution) enable more fine-grained guidance for multi-step reasoning, including chain-of-thought models where intermediate reasoning steps have distinct temporal signatures?
5. Can the framework generalize to other cognitive capabilities with known neural substrates — theory of mind, causal inference, spatial reasoning — where specialized brain networks have been identified?
