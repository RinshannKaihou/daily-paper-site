Under B-bit quantization, perfect top-k retrieval requires Bd = Ω(k ln N) — a corpus-dependent bound.

What Limits Does Quantization Place on Dense Top-k Retrieval? A Theoretical Study

Koki Okajima · Tsukasa Yoshida | NTT, Inc.
dense retrieval vector quantization embedding theory vector databases
Key Metric
B* = O(ln ln N) B* ≈ 4.5 at N=10⁸, k=128. At B=4, k=128, N>10⁶ forces d > 384 (MiniLM reference).
Why This Matters
Vector-database engineers can now size embedding dimension and precision jointly against a theoretical floor — the (B, d) trade-off is no longer empirical guesswork at billion-scale corpora.
Critical Edge
Bounds are for perfect top-k only — real systems target approximate recall, and no matching achievability is given; B* is O(ln ln N), not tight.

Methodology方法

I
Existence indicatorAn indicator I ∈ {0,1}: 1 iff some (corpus, query) embedding pair realises every k-subset as the inner-product top-k. I=0 is a hard impossibility — no training or optimisation recovers it.
II
First-moment counting (Prop. 1)Bound admissible pairs by |X|N+M·M−M; once M ln M > (N+M) ln |X|, the indicator is forced to 0.
III
Cardinality bound (Lemma 1)For ℓ₂-normalised B-bit uniform scalar grids: |Sd,B| ≤ exp[d(B ln 2 − ½ ln d + ½ ln 2πe)] via a Gaussian-integral comparison.
IV
Lambert W analysis (Lemma 2)Optimise t = d/2 and extract the explicit (B, d) regime boundaries — the log-I minimum is solved by W−1.
V
Quantization modelΛB partitions [−1, 1] into 2B equal subintervals; Sd,B = {x ∈ ΛBd : ‖x‖₂ ≤ 1}. Matches FAISS QT_B_uniform on ℓ₂-normalised embeddings.

Strengths优势

  • Self-contained proofs — Theorem 1 is a one-page counting argument.
  • Complete (B, d) picture — three regimes, not a single bound.
  • Reconciles prior O(k) work — vacuous at B → ∞, complementary not contradictory.
  • Targets FAISS QT_B_uniform — actionable for practitioners.
  • Realistic numerics — k=16..128, N=10³..10⁸, d=384 reference line.

Limitations局限

  • Perfect retrieval only — ε-recall is left to future work.
  • Scalar quantisation only — PQ, RQ, learned codebooks not covered.
  • No achievability — lower bounds are not matched; tightness open.
  • B* is O(ln ln N) — the hidden constant is unknown.
  • Inner-product scoring only — L2 / asymmetric quantisation unaddressed.
  • Worst-case framing — every k-subset must be realisable.

Questions for Further Exploration问题

1
Achievability? Does the second-moment method give Bd = Θ(k ln N), or is the first-moment bound loose?
2
Approximate top-k? Allowing an ε fraction of k-subsets to be unrealisable — does regime (i) relax into a more practical threshold?
3
Product quantisation? A PQ code with M sub-quantizers of B bits has 2BM codewords — does Theorem 1 with Bd → BM give the right bound?
4
Asymmetric quantisation? Standard in FAISS IVFADC and ScaNN — full-precision queries, quantised documents. Does a similar bound hold?
5
Empirical B*? Is the threshold observable in recall-vs-bits curves on BEIR or MS-MARCO with QT_4 vs QT_8?
6
Over-dimensioning? Regime (iii) says very high d hurts. Is this phase transition visible in production recall?
