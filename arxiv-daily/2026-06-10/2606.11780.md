> **一句话总结：** 本文用第一矩计数法严格证明：在每坐标 B 比特量化下，对 N 文档语料库实现完美 top-k 检索所需的嵌入维度满足 Bd = Ω(k ln N)，并进一步揭示了 ℓ₂ 归一化均匀量化下存在临界精度阈值 B* = O(ln ln N)，低于该阈值无论维度多大均无法保证完美检索。

> **TL;DR:** Using the first-moment method from probabilistic combinatorics, this paper proves that under B-bit-per-coordinate quantization, perfectly realizing all top-k retrieval sets over an N-document corpus requires Bd = Ω(k ln N) — overturning the corpus-independent O(k) dimension bound that only holds at infinite precision. For ℓ₂-normalized uniform scalar quantization (the FAISS scalar quantizer model), a hard precision floor B* = O(ln ln N) is identified below which no dimension suffices, along with a surprising upper ceiling on feasible dimension at fixed precision.

## 核心贡献

- **颠覆无限精度结论**：Weller et al. (ICLR 2026) 和 Wang et al. (2026) 证明了实数空间中 d = 2k+1 = O(k) 维就足够，与语料库大小 N 无关；本文证明该结论在有限精度下不成立。
- **有限字母表不可能性定理（Theorem 1）**：对任意 2^{Bd} 点字母表，完美 top-k 检索要求 Bd = Ω(k ln N)，精度 B 与维度 d 之间存在对称的权衡关系。
- **均匀量化下的精确刻画（Theorem 2）**：针对 ℓ₂ 归一化 B-bit 均匀标量量化（对应 FAISS QT_B_uniform 族），识别出三个区间：(i) 亚临界精度 B ≤ B* 时完全不可能；(ii) 精度适中时维度下界；(iii) 维度过高反而失效的上界。
- **精度阈值 B* = O(ln ln N)**：对于常数 k，B* 仅以双对数速度增长，意味着在极大语料库下即使很小的精度不足也无法通过增加维度来补救。
- **数值验证**：在 B=4、k=128 时，N > 10^6 的语料库所需维度超过 d=384（标准密集检索器），在 N > 2.5×10^4 时 4-bit 量化已越过 B* 阈值。

## Key Contributions

- **Finite-alphabet impossibility (Theorem 1)**: For any 2^{Bd}-point alphabet, perfect top-k retrieval over N documents requires Bd = Ω(k ln N). This is proven via the **first-moment method**: the number of admissible configurations (bounded by |X|^{N+M}) must exceed the number of k-subsets M = C(N,k) ≥ (N/k)^k, forcing Bd to grow with k ln N.
- **Uniform quantization impossibility (Theorem 2)**: Specializing to ℓ₂-normalized B-bit uniform mid-point scalar quantization (FAISS QT_B_uniform model), three impossibility regimes are identified: (i) sub-critical precision B ≤ B*, (ii) precision-limited dimension lower bound when B = (1+δ)B*, and (iii) a high-dimension upper bound where the norm-constrained grid Sd,B becomes too sparse.
- **Hard precision floor B* = O(ln ln N)**: Below B*, increasing dimension cannot compensate — no finite-precision configuration can realize all top-k patterns, regardless of d.
- **Reconciliation with prior O(k) bounds**: Both prior results assume B → ∞; in that limit, the new bounds (1) and (3) become vacuous and impose no constraint. The results are complementary, not contradictory.
- **Practical benchmark**: For B=4, k=128, the necessary dimension exceeds d=384 (MiniLM) for N > 10^6; the B* threshold is crossed for N > 2.5×10^4 at k=128, but B* ≈ 3–4.5 across all k for N up to 10^8, meaning marginally increasing precision avoids the hard floor.

## Methodology

**Problem formalization.** The paper defines an *existence indicator* I ∈ {0,1} that equals 1 iff there exist corpus embeddings {u_i ∈ X}^N and query embeddings {v_S ∈ X} such that for every k-subset S, the inner-product top-k of {u_i} under query v_S is exactly S. I = 0 is a hard impossibility — no training procedure or optimization can overcome it.

**First-moment counting argument (Proposition 1).** The number of admissible (corpus, query) pairs is upper bounded by |X|^{N+M} · M^{-M}, yielding:
```
I ≤ exp[−M ln M + (N + M) ln |X|]
```
Once M ln M > (N+M) ln |X|, the exponent is negative, forcing I = 0. Since M = C(N,k) ≥ (N/k)^k grows polynomially in N of degree k, while ln |X| = Bd ln 2 is fixed, the budget Bd must scale as Ω(k ln N).

**Proof of Theorem 1.** Direct application of Proposition 1 with |X| = 2^{Bd} and lower bounds M ≥ (N/k)^k and M/(N+M) ≥ 1 − 2/N.

**Proof of Theorem 2.** Requires first bounding |S_{d,B}| — the size of the ℓ₂-norm-constrained grid. Lemma 1 gives:
```
|S_{d,B}| ≤ exp[d(B ln 2 − ½ ln d + ½ ln(2πe))]
```
This is proven by bounding the generating function Σ_{x∈Λ_B} e^{−tx²} ≤ 2^B √(π/t) via a Gaussian integral comparison, then optimizing t = d/2. Combining with Proposition 1, the log-I bound becomes a function of d with a minimum at d/(2πe·4^B) satisfying d/(2πe·4^B) · ln[d/(2πe·4^B)] = −1/e, solved via the Lambert W function. Lemma 2 provides a closed-form lower bound on W_{-1} to extract the explicit regime boundaries.

**Quantization model.** The uniform mid-point quantizer Λ_B partitions [−1,1] into 2^B equal subintervals of width 2^{1−B}. Embeddings lie in S_{d,B} = {x ∈ Λ_B^d : ‖x‖₂ ≤ 1}, modeling FAISS QT_B_uniform with ℓ₂-normalized embeddings.

## Results & Highlights

- **Main bound (Theorem 1)**: Bd = Ω(k ln N) for any finite-alphabet embedding. The bound is symmetric in B and d: halving precision at fixed N doubles the required dimension, and vice versa.
- **Precision floor (Theorem 2(i))**: B* is defined by B* ln 4 = ln ln M − ln π + ln[M/(N+M)], which is O(ln ln N) for k = O(1). For N = 10^8, k = 128: B* ≈ 4.5; for k=16: B* ≈ 2.7. Standard 4-bit quantization is thus near or below this threshold for large k at web-scale corpora.
- **Dimension bounds (Theorem 2(ii,iii))**: Above B*, feasible d lies in a window [d_min, d_max]. Notably, d_max = 2e[k ln N + k − k ln k]^{1+δ} is so large (exceeding 4300 for typical parameters) that it poses no practical constraint for standard models (which rarely exceed d=4096).
- **Numerical illustration**: At B=4, k=128, N=10^6: required d from Theorem 1 ≈ 300+, consistent with or exceeding d=384. The B* threshold at N=2.5×10^4 for k=128 shows the hard floor emerges earlier than expected. The right panel of Figure 2 shows B* ≈ 3–4.5 across all k values for N up to 10^8.
- **Upper dimension ceiling**: In regime (iii), as d grows, the unit ball's volume shrinks relative to the [-1,1]^d cube, so S_{d,B} admits too few vectors to realize all C(N,k) top-k patterns — a counterintuitive result that over-dimensioning can be as harmful as under-dimensioning.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: High for its niche. The paper identifies and fills a genuine gap: all prior retrieval capacity theory assumed infinite precision, and no prior work had asked what quantization costs theoretically. The precision floor B* = O(ln ln N) is a crisp, non-obvious result.

**Rigor**: Strong. The proof of Theorem 1 is short and tight. The proof of Theorem 2 is more involved (requiring Lemma 1's cardinality bound and Lemma 2's Lambert W bound) but is self-contained and verifiable. The authors correctly flag that B* is not claimed tight (only O(ln ln N), not Θ(ln ln N)), and that the upper dimension bound in regime (iii) is mild.

**Reproducibility**: As a theory paper, the main results are reproducible via proof-checking. The numerical figures (Figures 1 and 2) are simple to recompute from the closed-form bounds in Theorems 1 and 2.

**Significance**: Moderate-to-high for retrieval theory; moderate for practitioners. The key practical message — that quantization at fixed precision requires embedding dimension to grow logarithmically with corpus size — is mild in practice (ln N ≈ 14 for N=10^6), but the hard precision floor B* is more alarming: systems using B=4 with k≥128 and N≥25,000 may already be in the infeasible zone for perfect retrieval. However, the paper's own numerical analysis suggests that slightly increasing B (e.g., to B=5 or B=8) easily avoids this threshold, softening the alarm.

**Red flags**:
- The analysis is for *perfect* top-k retrieval (every k-subset must be realizable). Real systems target approximate retrieval (e.g., recall@10 ≥ 95%), so the hard impossibility thresholds may not translate directly to degradation in practice.
- Theorem 2 applies specifically to uniform scalar quantization with shared range [−1,1]. Product quantization, residual quantization, and learned codebooks are not covered (though Theorem 1 applies as a fallback for any finite alphabet).
- No matching upper bound (achievability result) is given — the precision floor B* and the dimension lower bound are not shown to be tight. The second moment method is suggested as a path forward but not pursued.
- The paper is an arXiv preprint without peer review at time of summary.

## Strengths

- Clean, self-contained theoretical contribution with fully explicit proofs.
- The first-moment method is used elegantly: the core argument is essentially a one-page counting proof.
- The three-regime classification (sub-critical, precision-limited, high-dimension) provides a complete picture of the (B, d) parameter space rather than a single bound.
- Directly and explicitly reconciles with the prior O(k) result (Theorem 1 is vacuous at B → ∞), showing intellectual honesty.
- The paper explicitly targets the FAISS scalar quantizer family, making the results actionable for practitioners using FAISS.
- Numerical figures are concrete and use realistic parameter values (k=16 to 128, N=10^3 to 10^8, d=384 reference line from MiniLM).

## Limitations

- **Perfect retrieval only**: The impossibility results are for zero-error top-k retrieval. Extending to approximate retrieval (ε-recall) is explicitly listed as future work and would likely relax regime (i) of Theorem 2.
- **Scalar quantization only for Theorem 2**: Product quantization (PQ), residual quantization (RQ), and learned codebooks are not covered. Theorem 1 remains valid for PQ (treating a PQ code as a finite alphabet), but the tighter bounds of Theorem 2 do not extend.
- **No achievability**: The lower bounds are not matched by explicit finite-precision constructions. Whether the bounds are tight is unknown.
- **B* not tight**: The precision floor is only O(ln ln N); the exact constant hidden in the big-O is not determined.
- **Inner-product scoring only**: The analysis uses inner product (equivalently cosine similarity on the sphere) as the relevance score. Whether similar bounds hold for L2 distance or other metrics is not addressed.
- **Worst-case framing**: The bound requires *every* k-subset to be realizable, a much stronger requirement than needed in any practical retrieval task where query distribution is non-uniform.

## Relevance

本文为向量数据库与密集检索系统（包括基于 RAG 的 AI 应用）中的量化决策提供了理论基础，对关注大规模检索（BEIR、MS-MARCO、RAG pipeline）效率与精度权衡的研究者具有参考价值——尤其是在为十亿级语料库选择嵌入维度和量化精度时，本文的理论下界可作为设计约束的参考依据。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can a matching upper bound (achievability) be established? Does the second moment method yield Bd = Θ(k ln N), or is the first-moment bound loose?
2. What is the analogous bound under approximate top-k retrieval (e.g., allowing a fraction ε of k-subsets to be unrealizable)? This would likely relax regime (i) and give a more practically relevant threshold.
3. Does the analysis extend to product quantization? A PQ code with M sub-quantizers of B bits each has a 2^{BM}-point codebook — does Theorem 1 with Bd replaced by BM give the right bound?
4. Are there analogous results for asymmetric quantization (queries stored at full precision, documents quantized), which is standard in FAISS IVFADC and ScaNN?
5. Can the B* threshold be empirically observed in retrieval recall-vs-bits curves (e.g., on BEIR or MS-MARCO with FAISS QT_4 vs QT_8) as a phase transition in recall@k performance?
6. The upper dimension ceiling in regime (iii) is counterintuitive — does this mean that extremely high-dimensional quantized embeddings are *worse* than moderate-dimensional ones for a fixed bit budget? Is this observable in practice?
