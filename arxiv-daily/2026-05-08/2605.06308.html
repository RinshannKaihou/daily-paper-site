Measuring Black-Box Confidence via Reasoning Trajectories: Geometry, Coverage, and Verbalization

arXiv: 2605.06308

Date: 2026-05-08

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06308

一句话总结： 本文将链式推理（CoT）过程嵌入为滑动窗口轨迹，通过测量轨迹在嵌入空间中对外部答案锚点的收敛程度，提出了一种无需访问logits或隐层状态的黑盒置信度评估方法（C+G+V），在6/6评测设置下，以K=4样本的代价超越了K=8自洽投票的AUC（中位数提升+0.075）。
TL;DR: This paper proposes a black-box confidence scoring method for chain-of-thought (CoT) LLM reasoning that embeds reasoning traces as sliding-window trajectories and measures their geometric convergence toward external answer anchors via a one-parameter softmax — no logits, hidden states, or supervised calibrators needed. Fusing this Geometry (G) channel with a Coverage (C) channel (whether the correct hypothesis was ever proposed) and a Verbalization (V) channel (the model's own self-rated confidence) into C+G+V at K=4 samples Pareto-improves self-consistency at K=8 (median ΔAUC=+0.075, p=7.1×10⁻⁹) across MedQA-USMLE, GPQA Diamond, and MMLU-Pro with Gemini 3.1 Pro and Claude Sonnet 4.6 — while cutting per-question API cost by roughly 45–55%. The key mechanistic finding is that CoT trajectories show a consistent late-stage commitment signature: the penultimate reasoning window carries the strongest correctness signal, and the final window on GPQA actually inverts (positive slope), revealing that answer verbalization re-orients trajectories toward the model's committed pick regardless of correctness.

Key Contributions

  • Trajectory-Geometry confidence score (G): Embeds a single CoT as overlapping sliding-window segments, scores their convergence toward fixed external answer anchors via a one-parameter softmax, and achieves single-feature selective-prediction AUC of 0.71–0.82 without logits, hidden states, or training labels beyond cross-validation folds.
  • Late-trace commitment signature: The fitted softmax slope is deepest-negative (most informative) at the penultimate window across all six benchmark-reasoner combinations; the final window inverts sign on GPQA Diamond (+22.1 for Gemini, +23.7 for Claude), providing a falsifiable mechanistic fingerprint of answer commitment before literal verbalization.
  • Three-channel decomposition (C+G+V): Decomposes black-box confidence into Coverage (reachability prior from a proposer/judge regime), Geometry (within-trace spatial commitment), and Verbalization (model's self-rated confidence). Likelihood-ratio tests confirm C and G carry independent signal in 18/18 and 16/18 settings; V adds residual signal in only 6/18.
  • Pareto improvement over self-consistency: C+G+V at K=4 beats SC@8 in 6/6 closed-set settings (median ΔAUC=+0.075, Stouffer z=5.67, p=7.1×10⁻⁹) at ~45–55% lower API cost; a fixed-pick control (+0.060) and E5 cross-embedder replication rule out answer-switching and single-vendor artifacts.
  • Structural ceiling and V asymmetry: Verbalization is the only channel that does not pathologically rank "no-hypothesis-correct" traces above "wrong-in-set" traces, surfacing a fundamental structural ceiling of geometry-only approaches and motivating conditional use of V based on per-setting calibration tests.

Methodology

Problem Setting

The authors target black-box confidence estimation for chain-of-thought (CoT) reasoning accessed through text-only APIs (no logits, no hidden states). The dominant baseline — self-consistency (SC), i.e., majority voting over K sampled CoTs — is linearly expensive in K, produces discrete k/K confidence with low resolution at small K, and ignores the internal geometry of each trace.

Trajectory Construction

Given a multiple-choice question q, K=4 CoTs are sampled at temperature T=0.7 with the option list withheld from the generator (blinded protocol). Each CoT is embedded as a sequence of overlapping sliding windows (window size W=30 words, stride S=15) using a fixed embedding model (primary: gemini-embedding-001, p=3072 dimensions). The query embedding is prepended as the trajectory origin.

Geometric Scoring (G)

Per-option anchor embeddings are taken from the official benchmark option texts. The trajectory's penultimate window embedding is compared to each anchor via cosine distance. A one-parameter softmax with inverse-temperature β (fit by maximum likelihood on training folds) converts distances to a probability:

GEO(q) = exp(β̂ · D_ŷ) / Σⱼ exp(β̂ · D_j)

A strongly negative β̂ means closer anchors are more likely to be correct. The penultimate window is chosen a priori to avoid the terminal verbalization regime; the window-position sweep validates this choice empirically.

Composite and Auxiliary Features

GEO is fused (via logistic regression with 5-fold CV) with four auxiliary features computed from the same K samples: STR (string consensus), SEM (mean pairwise cosine among condensed answers), VOL (trajectory volatility), and ENS (cross-model agreement between Gemini and Sonnet picks).

Three-Channel Open-Ended Regime (C+G+V)

Moving beyond closed-set MCQ, the authors introduce a proposer/judge pipeline where candidate answers are generated rather than given:

  • Coverage (C): A judge LLM (GPT-5-mini, swapped with Claude Sonnet 4.6 for robustness) scores whether the correct answer appears among K proposed hypotheses — a question-difficulty prior.
  • Geometry (G): Same as above, but scored against LLM-generated committed-rationale anchors.
  • Verbalization (V): The generator is shown its own CoT and asked to rate confidence 0–100; mean, std, and min over K responses form the feature bundle.

Each channel is fit separately under 5-fold logistic regression, then the three channel scores are fused by a second 5-fold logistic layer to prevent leakage.

Evaluation Protocol

Selective-prediction AUC (AUROC of abstention under accuracy-coverage tradeoff) is the primary metric. Benchmarks: MedQA-USMLE (1,273 4-option medical questions), GPQA Diamond (198 graduate-level science questions), MMLU-Pro (pilot, 200 questions). Reasoners: Gemini 3.1 Pro, Claude Sonnet 4.6, Llama 3.3 70B (open-weight third family). Robustness checks include: E5 cross-embedder replication, committed-rationale anchor ablation, jargon-masking controls, judge-family swap, and window-position sweeps.

  • Penultimate window universality: The k=−2 window is the deepest-negative (most calibrated) in all 6 benchmark-reasoner combinations. The k=−1 (final) window inverts only on GPQA Diamond, not on MedQA or MMLU-Pro, localizing the verbalization flip to a high-difficulty domain where model accuracy is ~50%.
  • Anchor ablation: Condensed-answer and reasoning-tail anchors yield near-chance GEO AUC (0.32–0.48); only external dataset-option text anchors yield strong discrimination (0.71–0.75), providing a structural lesson that anchors derived from the model's own output are informationally redundant.
  • Cost reduction: G+C+V@4 is 42–55% cheaper per question than SC@10 at May-2026 API prices (e.g., MedQA/Claude: $0.0294 vs. $0.0607).
  • Verbalization asymmetry: AUC(V) correlates with reasoner accuracy (Spearman ρ=+0.71); V adds independent signal in only 6/18 open-ended settings. The incremental value is largest when verbalizations are internally stable (low dispersion) and the reasoner is already competent.
  • Judge swap robustness (Cohen's κ=0.82): Swapping the coverage judge from GPT-5-mini to Claude Sonnet 4.6 leaves G-only AUC unchanged (|Δ|≤0.013) and shifts C-only AUC by at most ±0.02, confirming geometry is genuinely orthogonal to judge identity.
  • Llama 3.3 70B replication: β̂ remains strongly negative across all three benchmarks for the open-weight model; GEO AUC degrades to near-chance on GPQA (where Llama accuracy collapses to 0.33), validating that the geometric channel tracks model capability faithfully.

The application motivation (clinical decision support via MedQA) is coherent with the Novo Nordisk affiliation.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: Moderately high. The idea of treating CoT as a geometric trajectory in embedding space and scoring convergence toward external anchors is genuinely novel. Prior work (Lin et al., 2023) embeds clouds of completed responses; this paper embeds windows of a single trace against fixed external anchors. The C+G+V decomposition is a clean conceptual contribution. However, the individual components (embedding-based uncertainty, verbalized confidence, coverage via majority vote) are not new in isolation.

Rigor: High. The paper commits to a priori design choices (penultimate window, blinded options), then validates them empirically. Multiple falsification checks are reported: the Bayesian posterior consistency check (Eq. 5 verified across 6 combinations), the judge-swap experiment (κ=0.82), the E5 cross-embedder replication, and the fixed-pick control to isolate calibration from answer-switching. The bucket-separation analysis (Table 19) honestly exposes the structural ceiling of geometry-only approaches. Statistical testing (Stouffer pooled z, bootstrap CIs) is appropriate and consistently reported.

Reproducibility: Moderate. All three frontier models used (Gemini 3.1 Pro, Claude Sonnet 4.6, GPT-5-mini for judging) are commercial APIs with unpredictable versioning and availability. The open-weight Llama 3.3 70B replication is helpful. No code repository is linked in the arxiv preprint. The hyperparameter space is small (W=30, S=15, K=4), which aids replication, but reproducing all 18-setting results at full scale would require substantial API spend (~3× what the paper reports using).

Significance: High for practitioners deploying CoT reasoning through black-box APIs, particularly in high-stakes domains. The 45–55% cost reduction at better AUC is a compelling practical result. The mechanistic finding about the penultimate window commitment signature could influence how CoT pipelines are designed and monitored. The structural-ceiling finding (bucket-(iii) traces are systematically mis-ranked by all embedding-based scores) is a genuine warning for those relying solely on geometric confidence.

Red flags:

  • GPQA Diamond retains only N=117 questions for Gemini (after condensation filtering), making per-combination conclusions from that cell less reliable; the authors acknowledge this and interpret it jointly with the Sonnet cell.
  • The Coverage channel is judge-mediated — its signal partially reflects judge LLM priors, not pure reasoner reachability. The authors bound this with a judge-swap but acknowledge a full multi-judge audit remains future work.
  • Verbalization's "residual signal in 6/18" framing is honest but could be spun; the deployment guidance ("gate V on setting-level calibration tests") is practical but adds pipeline complexity.
  • The paper references "Martell et al., 2026" for prior self-consistency work — this is the authors' own concurrent/forthcoming work, creating a self-citation loop that makes the baseline comparison harder to verify independently.

Strengths

  • Clean three-way decomposition of black-box confidence with mechanistic interpretability (geometric commitment, coverage failure, metacognitive self-report).
  • Consistently falsifiable claims: a priori design choices validated post-hoc, Bayesian posterior consistency verified algebraically, judge identity shown not to contaminate G channel.
  • Cost-efficiency framing is concrete (Table 14 with exact dollar figures) and practically actionable.
  • Structural ceiling analysis honestly documents what geometry cannot do (rank "no-path-to-gold" vs. "wrong-in-set" traces), strengthening rather than undermining the paper's contribution.
  • Three model families across two API providers and one open-weight model substantially de-risks vendor-artifact explanations.

Limitations

  • Evaluated on English multiple-choice benchmarks only; non-English, non-text-only (e.g., code, math with formal notation), or generative (open-ended answer) settings are not validated.
  • The Coverage channel depends on an LLM judge and inherits its biases; the paper provides a swap check but not a human-adjudicated ground truth.
  • No code release noted; the pipeline has multiple interacting components (embedder, proposer, judge, verbalizer) that could introduce subtle bugs in reimplementation.
  • The open-ended regime's budget advantage partly depends on avoiding extra self-consistency samples; the comparison to SC@8 is fair given Stouffer z but the panel is not fully crossed (not all 18 open-ended settings have the same SC@8 baseline reported).
  • Weaker models (Llama on GPQA) degrade gracefully to near-chance GEO AUC, which is mechanistically consistent but means the method is uninformative precisely when cheap models are used on hard tasks.

Relevance

本文直接针对通过黑盒API部署Chain-of-Thought推理模型时的置信度校准问题，提出了一套轻量、可解释的三通道框架，对需要在医疗、科学推理等高风险场景中评估LLM可靠性的研究者和工程师具有直接参考价值；几何轨迹信号的倒数第二窗口提交特征也为理解LLM推理动态提供了新的机制性视角。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the penultimate-window commitment signature hold for open-ended generation tasks (summarization, code, math proof steps) where there is no fixed option anchor set?
  2. Can trajectory geometry serve as an online monitoring signal during streaming CoT generation — i.e., can you detect low-confidence reasoning before the model finalizes its answer?
  3. How does the method behave under adversarial prompting or when the CoT is deliberately unfaithful to the model's underlying computation (cf. Turpin et al., 2023)?
  4. The Coverage channel is currently mediated by an LLM judge — would retrieval-augmented or formal-verifier-based coverage checking produce a less biased prior?
  5. Given that GEO AUC tracks model capability (degrading for Llama on hard GPQA), could geometric trajectory analysis be repurposed as a difficulty estimator or as a routing signal for model cascades?
  6. Does the three-phase trajectory shape (terminal flip → penultimate trough → early attenuation) persist in models trained with reinforcement learning from human feedback vs. supervised fine-tuning alone?
