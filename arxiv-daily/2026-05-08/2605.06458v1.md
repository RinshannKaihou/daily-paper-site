> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一种基于广义特征值分解的对比敏感度分解框架，将大语言模型（LLM）中间层激活分解为语义不变子空间与表面形式"干扰"子空间，并利用不变子空间投影在9个异构LLM上实现零样本模型归因，准确率超过92%。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces a local geometric framework for analyzing how large language models encode semantic invariance. The core method — contrastive sensitivity decomposition via a generalized eigenvalue problem — separates each layer's hidden representations into a semantic-invariant subspace (stable under paraphrasing) and a nuisance subspace (surface-form variation). Experiments on nine transformer models (4B–16B parameters, diverse families) show that: (1) invariant structure concentrates in mid-to-late layers; (2) semantic displacement lies geometrically orthogonal to the nuisance subspace; (3) causal interventions confirm the invariant component controls model output semantics; and (4) invariant zone signatures enable zero-shot model attribution at >92% accuracy across base, fine-tuned, and knowledge-distilled model variants. The paper is primarily a mechanistic/representational analysis contribution, not a training method, and the dataset is intentionally small and controlled (410 prompts).

---

## 核心贡献

- **几何框架**：将LLM的语义不变性形式化为流形切空间的分解问题，区分"语义方向"与"干扰方向"，提供了一个有理论依据的局部几何视角。
- **对比敏感度分解（CSD）**：通过构建语义保持扰动（SP）与语义改变扰动（SC）的协方差矩阵，求解广义特征值问题，数据驱动地识别每一层的不变子空间，无需监督标注。
- **不变区域局部化**：实验证明不变特征集中出现在中后期层，广义特征值谱随深度稳定增长，为层选择提供了原则性依据。
- **因果验证**：通过直接干预隐藏状态（替换不变/干扰分量），因果地验证了不变子空间对模型输出语义的控制作用，而干扰子空间干预则输出几乎不变。
- **零样本模型归因**：利用不变区域表示作为"模型签名"，在9个不同LLM族（包括微调和知识蒸馏版本）上实现零样本归因，准确率普遍超过92%，比PCA基线高出约4–7个百分点。

## Key Contributions

- **Geometric formalization of semantic invariance**: Models paraphrastic robustness as a local manifold property, decomposing the tangent space T_z M into semantic and nuisance directions, grounding empirical observations in differential geometry.
- **Contrastive Sensitivity Decomposition (CSD)**: A data-driven method that solves a generalized eigenvalue problem contrasting semantic-preserving vs. semantic-changing covariance matrices to identify invariant subspace directions at each layer — no supervision required.
- **Layer-wise invariant zone localization**: Demonstrates that eigenvalue spectra grow monotonically with depth and plateau in mid-to-late layers, providing a principled criterion for selecting layers with strong invariant structure.
- **Causal validation via representation intervention**: Directly patches the invariant or nuisance component of hidden states and propagates through remaining layers; invariant-component swaps produce large KL divergence for semantically different donors (~0.34–0.43) but near-zero for paraphrases (~0.04); nuisance swaps produce non-selective output changes regardless of semantic alignment.
- **Zero-shot model attribution**: Invariant zone signatures serve as compact latent fingerprints enabling >92% attribution accuracy across base, fine-tuned, and distilled variants of nine diverse LLMs, outperforming PCA baselines consistently.

## Methodology

The paper builds on the **manifold hypothesis**: transformer hidden states lie on a low-dimensional semantic manifold M ⊂ R^d. At any point z on the manifold, the tangent space T_z M decomposes (approximately) as:

> T_z M ≈ T_z^semantic ⊕ T_z^nuisance

Paraphrasing moves representations along nuisance directions while semantic identity is preserved in the complementary invariant subspace.

**Contrastive Sensitivity Decomposition (Section 3.2):**  
For each input x and layer ℓ, two perturbation families are constructed:
- **SP (semantic-preserving)**: paraphrases that rephrase while preserving meaning.
- **SC (semantic-changing)**: variants with different semantic content.

Finite-difference feature changes Δz^SP and Δz^SC are computed from layer activations. The method then forms empirical covariance matrices S^SP and S^SC and solves the generalized eigenvalue problem:

> S^SC v = λ S^SP v

Eigenvectors v with large λ define directions that vary strongly under semantic change but are stable under paraphrasing — these are the **invariant directions**. The top-k eigenvectors form the invariant subspace basis U^inv, and the invariant representation is obtained by projection: z_inv = U^inv U^inv^T z.

**Invariant Zone Selection:** Layers are selected based on large leading generalized eigenvalues, identifying depth regions where semantic-nuisance separation is strongest (empirically mid-to-late layers).

**Zero-Shot Model Attribution (Section 3.3):** For a test model, the invariant zone representation s_t(x) is computed and matched against reference model signatures s_m(x) via cosine similarity. Attribution selects the closest-matching reference: m̂ = argmax_m Sim(s_t(x), s_m(x)).

**Dataset:** 410 prompts across 10 thematic groups (Cats, France, Python, Photosynthesis, Democracy, Einstein, Climate Change, Calculus, Meditation, Bread), each with 20 SP and 20 SC variants. Strictly separated discovery/validation subsets prevent data leakage.

**Models:** Nine instruction-tuned models from diverse families: Mistral-Instruct (7B), Llama-3-Instruct (8B), Gemma-IT (7B), Qwen2.5-Instruct (7B), GLM-4-Chat (9B), DeepSeek-MoE-Chat (16B), InternLM2-Chat (7B), Phi-4-Mini (4B), Falcon3-Instruct (7B).

## Results & Highlights

**Invariant Zone Localization:**  
Leading generalized eigenvalues increase steadily with depth across all nine models. Invariant zones emerge most strongly in mid-to-late layers, confirming that semantic abstraction concentrates progressively deeper in the network hierarchy.

**Tangent-Direction Validation (Section 4.3):**  
The nuisance projection energy E_nuis (fraction of semantic displacement energy aligned with nuisance directions) is consistently ~0.01–0.02 across all models and groups. This near-zero value confirms that semantic displacement between concepts lies largely orthogonal to the dominant nuisance subspace — a key geometric prediction of the framework.

**Causal Intervention (Section 4.4 and Appendix A):**  
- Within-group SP invariant swaps: KL ≈ 0.036–0.052 (minimal output change).  
- Within-group SC invariant swaps: KL ≈ 0.337–0.427 (substantial output change).  
- Between-group invariant swaps: KL ≈ 0.974–0.986 (large, consistent shifts across all models).  
- Nuisance swaps: SP and SC curves are indistinguishable, confirming the nuisance subspace does not selectively encode semantic identity.

**Model Attribution (Section 4.5):**  
Attribution accuracy exceeds 92% for base models across all nine model families, with only 2–4% degradation under fine-tuning or knowledge distillation. The invariant zone signatures used for attribution are computed from original base models and applied without re-estimation to fine-tuned/distilled variants.

**Comparison vs. PCA Baseline (Appendix D):**  
Invariant representation Z_inv outperforms PCA by 4–7 percentage points across all settings and model families. PCA nuisance projection energy is ~0.3–0.7, while the proposed method achieves ~10^-3, demonstrating vastly better semantic-nuisance separation.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:**  
The generalized eigenvalue formulation for separating semantic vs. nuisance variation is a meaningful contribution, going beyond prior probing/RSA work by providing an explicit, actionable decomposition. However, the core intuition (paraphrases are nearby in latent space, different meanings are far apart) is not novel, and the manifold hypothesis framing adds theoretical veneer without enabling new theoretical guarantees. The attribution application is a natural downstream use case rather than a fundamentally new contribution.

**Rigor:**  
The experimental design is thoughtful: strict train/test splits for subspace discovery vs. evaluation, ablations over subspace dimension k, comparisons against PCA baseline, and causal intervention rather than mere correlation. The causal intervention protocol is the strongest part of the paper. However, the dataset is small (410 prompts, 10 topics), hand-constructed, and topically limited — the authors acknowledge this and frame it appropriately, but it limits the scope of generalization claims.

**Reproducibility:**  
The paper describes the generalized eigenvalue formulation, dataset construction protocol, and intervention procedure in sufficient detail to re-implement. The dataset examples in Appendix E are helpful. However, no code or dataset release is mentioned, which will hamper direct replication.

**Significance:**  
The connection to model attribution (fingerprinting via internal representations rather than output-level artifacts) is practically significant, particularly as a training-free, zero-shot approach that survives fine-tuning and distillation. The geometric interpretation of semantic invariance provides a cleaner conceptual vocabulary for mechanistic interpretability.

**Red Flags:**  
- The dataset is very small (410 prompts) and covers only 10 narrow thematic groups. Whether the framework generalizes to open-ended generation, long-form text, or truly diverse semantic neighborhoods is untested.  
- The nuisance subspace is acknowledged to be imperfectly disentangled (Fig. 5, Appendix B), with nuisance swaps still producing non-negligible output changes even for SP donors. This partial entanglement is not fully resolved.  
- Attribution is evaluated only at the model-identity level (which of 9 models generated a representation), not at finer levels (e.g., distinguishing fine-tuned variants from each other). The 9-way classification task with 7B-scale models sharing similar architectures may be an easier setting than real-world scenarios.  
- No statistical significance tests or confidence intervals are reported for attribution accuracy.

---

## Strengths

- Clear geometric framework with formal definitions that tightly motivate the empirical methodology.
- Causal intervention experiments provide considerably stronger evidence than correlational analysis alone.
- Evaluated across nine diverse model families and scales (4B–16B), avoiding single-architecture artifacts.
- Results are consistent and stable across subspace dimension k ∈ {1, 10, 32}, suggesting the invariant structure is a genuine intrinsic property rather than a tuning artifact.
- Comparison with PCA baseline (Appendix D) is quantitatively decisive, with ~300x lower nuisance projection energy.
- Careful data split design prevents discovery/evaluation leakage.

---

## Limitations

- Small, manually constructed dataset (410 prompts, 10 topics) limits generalization; no evaluation on natural corpora or long-form generation.
- The nuisance subspace is not perfectly disentangled — it retains residual semantic information, limiting interpretability of the two components as clean orthogonal factors.
- No formal theoretical analysis of when the tangent-space decomposition holds or what conditions are sufficient for the generalized eigenvalue approach to recover the true invariant directions.
- Attribution accuracy is reported for model-family identification, not for fine-grained variant discrimination (e.g., distinguishing two fine-tunes of the same base model).
- Preprint only — no peer review. Code and dataset are not released, limiting reproducibility.
- The framework is local (per semantic group, per input), so computing invariant zones at inference time requires constructing perturbation families — computationally non-trivial for deployment.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the contrastive sensitivity decomposition scale to open-domain, long-form text without manually curated SP/SC perturbation pairs? What automated paraphrase generation strategies would work?
2. Does invariant zone geometry transfer across model families (e.g., can a Mistral-derived invariant subspace meaningfully align with a Gemma subspace), and if so, what does this imply about universal semantic representations?
3. The nuisance subspace retains residual semantic information — is this a fundamental limit of linear decomposition, or could a nonlinear decomposition (e.g., via contrastive learning objectives) achieve true orthogonality?
4. How sensitive is attribution accuracy to adversarial paraphrasing or deliberate attempts to make one model's representations resemble another's (e.g., via representation engineering)?
5. The framework identifies invariant zones in specific depth ranges — do these ranges correspond to known functional layers (e.g., factual recall layers, reasoning layers) identified by other mechanistic interpretability methods?
6. Could the invariant subspace be used as a training signal (e.g., as a regularizer) to explicitly encourage semantic invariance, and would this improve downstream robustness?
