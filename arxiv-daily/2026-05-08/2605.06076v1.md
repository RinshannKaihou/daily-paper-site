> **一句话总结：** 通过追踪监督微调过程中 Transformer 电路的动态演化，本文证明了基于当前参数快照的静态机制定位存在严重的"时间滞后"缺陷——Attention 主导的技能电路会自由迁移，使静态定位在实践中等价于随机定位，并提出"预测性定位"作为未来方向。

> **TL;DR:** The widely-used "locate-then-update" paradigm for LLM post-training assumes that mechanistic circuits identified from current model parameters can guide future parameter updates. This paper shows that assumption is fundamentally broken: transformer circuits undergo continuous "free evolution" during fine-tuning, with attention-dominated skill circuits migrating drastically while MLP-dominated knowledge circuits stay relatively stable. As a result, static mechanistic localization performs no better than random parameter selection for skill-centric tasks, and its apparent success in knowledge editing / unlearning is an artifact of MLP circuits being intrinsically resistant to migration. The paper proposes three new metrics (Circuit Distance, Circuit Stability, Circuit Conflict) to quantify this dynamic and calls for "predictive" localization methods that anticipate future circuit states.

---

## 核心贡献

- **揭示自由演化现象**：系统证明了在无定位约束的监督微调中，Transformer 电路会自发发生结构迁移（"自由演化"），注意力头（Attention）主导的任务电路迁移幅度远大于 MLP 电路。
- **提出三项电路演化度量**：Circuit Distance（CD，衡量关键组件迁移程度）、Circuit Stability（CS，衡量任务机制学习稳定性）、Circuit Conflict（CC，基于 CNF 逻辑电路量化多任务间的机制冲突）。
- **解构"有效性幻觉"**：论证现有机制定位方法（如 ROME/MEMIT）的表面成功源自 MLP 电路本身的低迁移率，而非定位策略的真实有效性；在注意力主导的技能任务上，机制定位与随机定位无显著差异。
- **提出预测性定位框架**：以"未来机制定位"为概念验证，表明使用微调后参数提取的电路进行定位可同时提升目标任务和泛化任务性能，为后续研究指明方向。

---

## Key Contributions

- **Free Evolution discovery**: The first systematic demonstration that transformer circuits spontaneously shift during SFT, even when no localization is applied. Attention circuits migrate far more than MLP circuits across all tested tasks and models (Mistral-7B and LLaMA3-8B).
- **Three novel diagnostic metrics**: Circuit Distance (CD), Circuit Stability (CS), and Circuit Conflict (CC), providing a rigorous framework to measure circuit dynamics along migration, mastery, and cross-task interference dimensions.
- **Deconstruction of "illusion of effectiveness"**: Mechanistic Localization performs statistically indistinguishably from Random Localization on skill-centric (Attention-dominated) tasks. Its empirical wins on knowledge editing and unlearning benchmarks are explained by MLP circuits' natural resistance to migration rather than any real guidance from the static circuit map.
- **Predictive Localization as a forward-looking paradigm**: A preliminary "Future Localization" oracle experiment validates that forecasting post-update circuits substantially improves both task accuracy and pervasiveness preservation, charting a concrete research agenda.

## Methodology

The paper frames LLM post-training as a multi-objective optimization over a target dataset D_t and a pervasiveness dataset D_p. The "Locate-then-Update" paradigm first identifies a critical component set C via circuit discovery (automated EdgePruning on a Logical Circuit Framework), then freezes all other parameters and fine-tunes only C.

**Circuit Discovery** employs a causal-interventional circuit discovery method that finds a minimal subgraph C ⊂ G preserving the model's task-relevant output distribution, providing stronger causal grounding than gradient/magnitude methods or vocabulary probing.

**Three evaluation metrics** are defined:

- *Circuit Distance (CD)*: Manhattan distance over causal edge effects between an initial circuit G^θ and an updated circuit G^θ', normalized by the empirical range. Tracks how much critical pathways physically relocate after parameter updates.
- *Circuit Stability (CS)*: Expected Spearman rank correlation between circuits derived from two i.i.d. subsets of D_t at a given parameter state θ'. High CS indicates the model has consolidated stable knowledge of the mechanism; low CS indicates unsettled internal representations.
- *Circuit Conflict (CC)*: Circuit overlap between target and pervasiveness task circuits, formalized as the UNSAT Core size of a Boolean satisfiability problem constructed from the CNF representations of both circuits. Strictly quantifies mechanistic interference beyond naive set intersection.

**Experimental setup**: Two SFT pipelines—Free Evolution (full-parameter SFT as baseline) vs. Localization Evolution (Mech or Random strategy)—are run on Mistral-7B and LLaMA3-8B across 15 datasets spanning skill-centric tasks (e.g., Arithmetic, Induction, Bool) and knowledge-centric tasks (e.g., WMDP-Bio, Gender, SST2), with OpenBookQA as the pervasiveness task. SFT tracked over 10 epochs × 20 observation steps; results reported under tight 95% CI across 5 seeds.

## Results & Highlights

- **Free Evolution is universal**: All tasks exhibit significant CD throughout SFT (RQ1-a confirmed). Even tasks already mastered at >85% accuracy undergo drastic circuit migration.
- **Attention vs. MLP divergence**: Attention circuits consistently show higher CD than MLP circuits. MLP stability (CS) substantially exceeds Attention stability, consistent with MLPs acting as dense associative stores that resist migration.
- **Five factors modulating circuit evolution** (Table 2): task type (skill vs. knowledge), degree of pervasiveness co-optimization, dataset size, conflict proportion, and initial task mastery.
- **Localization ≈ Random for skill tasks**: On the Arithmetic task (Attention-dominated), the Mech strategy yields no statistically significant advantage over Random localization in target or pervasiveness accuracy. Circuit Conflict data corroborate this—localization paradoxically increases CD and destabilizes CS.
- **Illusion confirmed on unlearning benchmark** (Table 3): On WMDP-Bio (MLP-dominated), Mech outperforms Random because MLPs inherently resist migration, not because the circuit map is accurate. On the Induction task (Attention-dominated), Mech offers negligible improvement over Random.
- **Future Localization oracle** (Figure 4): Using the post-SFT circuit as a surrogate for the current circuit substantially outperforms both Mech and Random strategies in task and pervasiveness accuracy, validating the "foresight" hypothesis.
- **Gradient-based localization** (Appendix F): Components localized via gradient signals exhibit markedly superior CD performance, suggesting gradient-based approximation of future circuit states as a promising direction.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: High. The paper asks a genuinely important and underexplored question—whether static mechanistic snapshots can reliably guide dynamic post-training—and provides the first systematic empirical treatment of circuit evolution during SFT. The "illusion of effectiveness" thesis is a meaningful contribution to the mechanistic interpretability critique literature.

**Rigor**: Moderate-to-high. The three metrics are formally defined and the experimental setup controls for parameter count between Mech and Random strategies. The use of five random seeds with tight confidence intervals is commendable. However, the paper relies heavily on two models (Mistral-7B, LLaMA3-8B), and scalability to larger models is not demonstrated. The circuit extraction method (EdgePruning) is computationally intensive and may introduce its own approximation errors that affect CD/CS measurements.

**Reproducibility**: Supported by open-source code. Experimental details are deferred substantially to appendices (A–G), making the main paper harder to replicate independently. Dataset and hyperparameter details in Appendix B are necessary reading.

**Significance**: High for the mechanistic interpretability and model editing communities. The finding that Mechanistic Localization is indistinguishable from Random Localization for skill-centric tasks is a strong negative result that should reshape how practitioners evaluate "locate-then-update" claims.

**Red flags**:
- The "Future Localization" oracle is not a deployable method—it is a post-hoc upper bound used to validate a hypothesis, not a concrete proposal. The paper's call for "predictive localization" remains aspirational.
- The Attention vs. MLP dichotomy is presented somewhat categorically; real circuits are mixed, and the degree to which tasks are cleanly separable into "Attention-dominated" vs. "MLP-dominated" may be model-specific.
- No large-scale RL post-training (RLHF/GRPO) experiments are included; the paper notes conclusions hold for knowledge editing but the RL regime is left as future work.
- The Circuit Conflict metric relies on SAT solving (UNSAT Core), which can be slow and whose sensitivity to circuit size thresholds is not fully characterized.

---

## Strengths

- Clear and falsifiable research questions (RQ1-a, RQ1-b, RQ1-c, RQ2-a, RQ2-b) with dedicated experiments for each.
- Three new metrics that are independently useful beyond the paper's specific claims.
- The "illusion of effectiveness" analysis is intellectually courageous—it challenges the validity of a growing body of published work rather than merely extending it.
- The five-factor ablation (Table 2) provides actionable insight into when circuits evolve most aggressively.
- Future Localization oracle cleanly bounds the potential of predictive approaches.

---

## Limitations

- Experiments are limited to models under 10B parameters (Mistral-7B, LLaMA3-8B); the free-evolution dynamics may differ at larger scales where MLP layers dominate even more.
- Circuit extraction via EdgePruning is computationally expensive; scalability to longer contexts or larger models is unaddressed.
- "Predictive Localization" is advocated but no concrete algorithm is proposed—the paper ends at diagnosing the problem rather than solving it.
- The paper focuses exclusively on SFT; how the findings extend to RLHF, DPO, or parameter-efficient methods (LoRA) is mentioned only in passing.
- Circuit Conflict's reliance on SAT solving introduces both a computational bottleneck and a potential sensitivity to the CNF formulation chosen.

---

## Relevance

本文对大模型后训练（如知识编辑、模型遗忘）中广泛使用的"先定位再更新"范式提出了根本性质疑，揭示了静态机制定位的时间滞后缺陷，对研究 LLM 可解释性与高效微调策略的读者具有直接参考价值；尤其适合关注机制可解释性在实际后训练场景中适用边界的研究者。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can gradient signals computed at the current parameter state effectively approximate the future circuit, providing a practical "predictive localization" algorithm without requiring oracle access to post-SFT parameters?
2. How do these circuit evolution dynamics scale with model size? Does the Attention-MLP migration asymmetry hold for 70B+ parameter models where MLP layers are proportionally even larger?
3. For LoRA and other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, does constraining updates to low-rank subspaces suppress free evolution, or does the same migration occur within the LoRA subspace?
4. The paper shows Mechanistic Localization ≈ Random Localization for skill-centric tasks. Are there task/domain combinations where the circuit map is sufficiently stable that static localization genuinely outperforms random selection?
5. Circuit Conflict is measured via UNSAT Core of CNF representations—how sensitive are the reported CC values to the SAT solver's approximation tolerance and the sparsity threshold used in circuit extraction?
6. Given that the "illusion of effectiveness" is linked to MLP-dominated tasks, how should practitioners design post-training evaluations to distinguish genuine mechanistic guidance from coincidental MLP stability?
