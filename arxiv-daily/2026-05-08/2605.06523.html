On the Implicit Reward Overfitting and the Low-rank Dynamics in RLVR

arXiv: 2605.06523

Date: 2026-05-08

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06523

一句话总结： 本文通过"周期性秩-1替换"实验揭示 RLVR 存在隐式奖励过拟合现象——模型的推理提升几乎完全集中在权重更新的秩-1分量中，而非秩-1的分量在训练集上虚高奖励但不贡献泛化，且奇异值谱呈"领先尖峰+重尾"的普遍规律，LoRA 输出侧对秩-1子空间的对齐远强于输入侧。
TL;DR: This paper empirically and theoretically demonstrates that RLVR (Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards, e.g., GRPO/DAPO/GSPO) suffers from implicit reward overfitting: the reasoning gains are almost entirely captured by the rank-1 component of the weight update matrix ΔW, while the remaining non-rank-1 components inflate training rewards without improving test generalization. Using a diagnostic tool called Periodic Rank-1 Substitution — periodically discarding all but the rank-1 component of ΔW during training — the authors show that test accuracy is nearly matched with far lower training reward. They further characterize the singular spectrum of RLVR updates (universal "leading spike + heavy tail" shape) and explain a geometric asymmetry in LoRA training: the output-side adapter aligns strongly with the rank-1 left singular subspace while the input side does not. Non-rank-1 components, though irrelevant to reasoning, preserve world knowledge, instruction-following, and safety. This is a mechanistic analysis paper, not a training recipe; it does not definitively solve the overfitting problem but provides compelling diagnostic evidence and theoretical grounding.

  • 刻画三大低秩动态特性：(1) RLVR 的推理能力几乎全部集中于秩-1分量；(2) ΔW 的奇异值谱呈普遍的"领先尖峰+准线性衰减重尾"分布，且该形态在训练初期即出现并持续稳定；(3) 非秩-1分量并非随机噪声——它们维系模型的世界知识、指令遵从与安全性，防止类灾难性遗忘的退化。
  • 揭示 LoRA 训练的几何不对称性：从实验与理论（梯度分解）两个角度证明，LoRA 的输出侧矩阵 A 与左奇异向量子空间强对齐，而输入侧矩阵 B 因上下文特征的复杂分布性难以对齐，从而解释了为何 LoRA 的秩-1近似能够有效捕获 RLVR 的推理方向。
  • 提供统一的理论框架：将 GRPO、DAPO、GSPO 等主流 RLVR 方法统一归结为"带符号加权的监督微调"，梯度本质是秩-1矩阵的加权平均，从理论上支持秩-1主导现象。

Key Contributions

  • Implicit reward overfitting diagnosis: Using Periodic Rank-1 Substitution (PRS) — resetting the base model every 10 steps to keep only the rescaled rank-1 component of ΔW — the authors show that on a significant fraction of datasets, PRS matches full RLVR test performance with substantially lower training reward, indicating that non-rank-1 components overfit the reward signal rather than encode generalizable reasoning.
  • Three universal properties of RLVR weight updates: (1) Reasoning improvement is almost exclusively encoded in the rank-1 component. (2) The singular spectrum of ΔW universally follows a "leading spike + quasi-linear heavy tail" pattern across all linear layers and throughout training. (3) Non-rank-1 components are functionally meaningful: they preserve out-of-domain capabilities (instruction-following on IF-Eval, world knowledge on MMLU/MMLU-Pro, and safety on SafetyBench), preventing the catastrophic capability degradation that rank-1-only models exhibit.
  • Geometric asymmetry in LoRA dynamics: Empirically (via Frobenius cosine similarity and principal angles across layers) and theoretically (via gradient decomposition), the paper proves that the output-side LoRA matrix A aligns strongly with the left singular subspace of ΔW^(1), while the input-side matrix B does not. This asymmetry arises because output gradients are compressed to a scalar direction while input activations are context-dependent and directionally scattered.
  • Unified gradient view of RLVR algorithms: An appendix shows GRPO, DAPO, and GSPO all reduce to signed weighted SFT in gradient form, and the token-level gradient to a weight matrix is always a rank-1 matrix (δx^T), providing a theoretical basis for why rank-1 dominance should be expected.

Methodology

The paper is primarily an empirical + analytical study, not a new training algorithm. The core methodology proceeds in three stages:

Stage 1 — Periodic Rank-1 Substitution (PRS)

At every 10 training steps, the authors perform SVD on the accumulated weight update ΔW = W_trained − W_base, extract the rank-1 approximation ΔW^(1) = σ₁u₁v₁ᵀ, rescale it by the Frobenius norm ratio ‖ΔW‖_F / ‖ΔW^(1)‖_F to preserve update magnitude, and add it back to the original base model to form the new starting point. Training then resumes from this rank-1-only checkpoint. This cleanly separates the rank-1 reasoning component from higher-rank components at each cycle, preventing non-rank-1 updates from accumulating over training.

Experiments are run on Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct (Countdown-3to4 dataset) and Llama3.1-8B-Instruct (GSM8K), using GRPO as the RL algorithm, with training on a single RTX Pro 6000 GPU (6 hours for Qwen experiment) and H100 GPU (20 hours for Llama experiment).

Stage 2 — Out-of-domain capability evaluation

To test whether non-rank-1 components preserve general capabilities, the authors apply a one-time rank-1 extraction to a broader set of RL-tuned models (Qwen2.5-7B-R1, DeepMath-Zero-7B, Qwen2.5-32B-DAPO, Qwen3-8B-DAPO-Math, Llama3.1-8B-Thinking-R1) and measure performance on IF-Eval (instruction following), MMLU, MMLU-Pro (world knowledge), and SafetyBench (safety alignment). Evaluation uses VLLM on a single H100 GPU.

Stage 3 — Spectral analysis and LoRA alignment

SVD is applied to ΔW for all linear layers of RLVR-trained models at multiple training checkpoints (steps 10–80) to characterize the universal spectral pattern. For the LoRA alignment analysis, rank-1 LoRA adapters are trained on the same dataset; alignment is measured via Frobenius cosine similarity (global) and principal angles (left and right subspaces separately) between ΔW_LoRA and ΔW^(1).

The theoretical analysis derives gradients for rank-1 LoRA explicitly, showing ∂L/∂a ∝ g (output gradient direction) while ∂L/∂b ∝ x (input activation direction), proving the asymmetry from first principles.

Results & Highlights

  • PRS vs. Full GRPO on test accuracy: On Countdown-3to4 with Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, PRS achieves comparable test-set accuracy to full GRPO despite training reward being significantly lower. On GSM8K with Llama3.1-8B-Instruct, a similar pattern holds. The training-reward/test-accuracy gap is the central empirical finding.
  • Safety degradation from rank-1 extraction: After one-time rank-1 extraction, SafetyBench accuracy drops noticeably for most models (e.g., Qwen2.5-7B-R1: 77.41 → 73.22; Llama3.1-8B-Thinking-R1: 45.22 → 66.14 — the latter anomaly is explained by Llama3.1-8B-Thinking-R1 being trained via cold-start SFT + GRPO + CoT distillation, so its rank-1 component encodes more than pure RLVR reasoning). Out-of-domain knowledge (MMLU, MMLU-Pro, IF-Eval) also degrades consistently post rank-1 extraction.
  • Universal spectral pattern: Across all tested models and all linear layers, the singular spectrum of ΔW shows a large σ₁ followed by quasi-linearly decaying σ₂, σ₃, … This pattern is stable from very early in training (step 10) and persists.
  • LoRA output-side alignment: The left principal angle between LoRA's A matrix and the left singular subspace of ΔW^(1) is consistently smaller (closer alignment) than the right principal angle across all 28 layers of Qwen2.5-7B, confirming the geometric asymmetry.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: Moderate-to-high. The core observation — that RLVR's reasoning gains concentrate in rank-1 — builds on a prior result (Cai et al. 2025, arXiv:2510.00553), but this paper is the first to name the phenomenon "implicit reward overfitting," operationalize it via PRS, and characterize the three-pronged picture (overfitting + spectral shape + LoRA asymmetry) in a unified analysis. The PRS diagnostic is genuinely creative.

Rigor: Moderate. The experiments are reproducible in principle (hyperparameters fully reported, code infrastructure standard GRPO). However, the "implicit reward overfitting" framing relies on comparing training reward trajectories to test accuracy, and the gap observed may partly reflect well-known reward hacking rather than a new phenomenon. The claim that PRS "effectively mitigates implicit reward overfitting" is only supported on two datasets; whether it is a practical training improvement or merely a diagnostic probe is not resolved. The paper explicitly does not propose a cure.

Reproducibility: Reasonable. Experiments require 6–20 GPU hours on professional-grade cards (RTX Pro 6000, H100 80GB), which is accessible for academic groups. Full hyperparameters and dataset references are given. However, no code repository is linked.

Significance: High for the mechanistic understanding community. If the findings hold at scale (>32B parameters, architectures beyond Qwen/Llama), they would provide a principled explanation for the pass@k degradation phenomenon and guide LoRA rank selection and RLVR training design.

Red flags:

  • Experiments are limited to 7B–32B models on math-focused datasets; generalization to other domains and tasks is assumed but not tested.
  • The "implicit reward overfitting" diagnosis depends on comparing two single training runs per setting — statistical noise is not fully characterized.
  • The Llama3.1-8B-Thinking-R1 anomaly (rank-1 extraction helps rather than hurts) is explained post-hoc and slightly undermines the universality claim.
  • Non-rank-1 components maintaining safety/knowledge does not necessarily mean they are "overfitting the reward" — an alternative interpretation is that they serve a legitimate regularization role. The paper acknowledges this but does not fully disentangle the two.

Strengths

  • Elegant diagnostic methodology (PRS) that isolates the rank-1 component during training, not just post-hoc.
  • The three properties (overfitting, spectral shape, LoRA asymmetry) are presented with consistent supporting evidence across multiple experimental settings and models.
  • Theoretical derivation of LoRA gradient asymmetry is clean, first-principles, and non-trivial.
  • Honest about limitations: the paper explicitly states it cannot observe behavior at larger scales and does not claim to have solved the problem.
  • Unified appendix showing GRPO/DAPO/GSPO are all signed weighted SFT is a useful contribution to the community's understanding.
  • The counterexample (Llama3.1-8B-Thinking-R1) is analyzed rather than swept under the rug.

---

Limitations

  • Compute constraints prevent experiments beyond 32B parameters; whether the rank-1 dominance and overfitting patterns hold for frontier-scale models (70B+) is unknown.
  • Only mathematical reasoning datasets (Countdown-3to4, GSM8K) are used for the core PRS experiments; other reasoning domains (code, science, commonsense) are not tested.
  • No new training algorithm or practical mitigation strategy is proposed; PRS is a diagnostic, not a fix.
  • The relationship between implicit reward overfitting (as defined here) and the well-studied reward hacking / entropy collapse literature is discussed only briefly.
  • Architecture diversity is limited to Qwen2.5 and Llama3.1 families; findings may not transfer to architectures with significantly different attention or MLP designs.
  • No code or model checkpoints are released, limiting direct reproducibility for the community.

本文深入分析了 RLVR 训练中权重更新的低秩结构与隐式过拟合现象，对理解大语言模型强化学习的本质机制、LoRA 在 RL 中的行为以及如何设计更高效的推理训练范式具有直接参考价值，适合关注 LLM 对齐与高效微调的研究者阅读。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the rank-1 dominance of reasoning updates hold at 70B+ parameter scale, or does the effective rank of the reasoning component grow with model size?
  2. Can PRS be turned into a practical training algorithm (e.g., with a soft rank-1 regularizer on ΔW) that simultaneously improves generalization and preserves non-reasoning capabilities?
  3. The paper shows LoRA output-side aligns with left singular vectors — does this imply that LoRA rank > 1 is largely wasted capacity in RLVR, and would rank-1 LoRA suffice for all RLVR tasks?
  4. How does the spectral pattern (leading spike + heavy tail) relate to the entropy collapse phenomenon identified in concurrent work? Are they two sides of the same mechanistic coin?
  5. The safety degradation after rank-1 extraction is significant — does this mean that safety alignment in RLVR is being stored in the non-rank-1 subspace as a form of "implicit regularization," and if so, can this be exploited for more targeted alignment?
  6. For domains beyond mathematics (e.g., code generation, scientific reasoning), does RLVR still exhibit the same singular spectral shape and rank-1 concentration of task-specific improvement?
