Topological Signatures of Grokking

arXiv: 2605.06352

Date: 2026-05-07

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06352

一句话总结： 本文使用持续同调（Persistent Homology）对神经网络在模块化算术任务上发生"grokking"现象时的表示空间进行拓扑分析，发现泛化阶段伴随 H₁ 持续性的显著跃升，揭示了循环拓扑结构的涌现是 grokking 的可测量特征。
TL;DR: This paper applies persistent homology (a tool from topological data analysis) to the learned embedding representations of transformers and MLPs trained on modular arithmetic, and identifies a sharp, reproducible increase in first-homology (H₁) persistence as a topological signature of grokking — one that tracks generalization rather than memorization, disappears under label corruption, and complements (but is not redundant with) Fourier analysis and local intrinsic dimension diagnostics.

---

核心贡献

  • 拓扑 grokking 特征：在模块化加法任务上，跨多个模数（p = 113, 149, 197）和训练比例（α = 0.2–0.3），一致发现 H₁ 最大持续性和总持续性在泛化阶段出现锐利跃升，且伴随持续图中主导长寿命一维环的涌现。
  • 跨架构一致性：Transformer 和 MLP 上均观察到相同的拓扑信号，排除了架构特异性的可能。
  • 泛化而非记忆：标签置换消融实验表明，拓扑转变与泛化绑定——当 grokking 失败时（P_frac ≥ 20%），PH 信号消失，证明该指标不是优化动态或记忆的副产品。
  • 与现有方法的互补性：LID（局部内在维度）在 grokking 时下降（压缩），PH 在 grokking 时上升（结构涌现）；两者捕捉同一相变的不同侧面，而 Fourier 分析则提供频域视角，三者互补。
  • 时序延迟发现：交叉相关分析显示测试精度提升先于拓扑转变约 1000 训练步，暗示 grokking 是"功能发现在前、几何重组在后"的两阶段过程。

Key Contributions

  • Topological grokking signature: A sharp, reproducible increase in H₁ maximum and total persistence is identified across moduli (p = 113, 149, 197) and training fractions (α = 0.2–0.3), with persistence diagrams showing emergence of a dominant long-lived 1-cycle consistent with the cyclic geometry of modular arithmetic.
  • Architecture-agnostic finding: The same topological transition occurs in both transformers and MLPs, ruling out attention-specific explanations.
  • Generalization-specific signal: Label permutation ablations confirm the PH signature tracks generalization — it disappears when grokking fails (P_frac ≥ 20%), dissociating the signal from generic memorization or training dynamics.
  • Complementary diagnostic: PH captures global loop structure (rising at grokking), LID captures local dimensionality (falling at grokking), and Fourier analysis captures dominant frequency components — together they form a coherent multi-perspective account of the representational phase transition.
  • Temporal ordering: Cross-correlation analysis reveals test accuracy improvements precede the topological transition by ~1,000 training steps, suggesting a two-stage grokking: functional generalization, then geometric reorganization.

Methodology

Task setup. The authors study modular addition: given a, b ∈ {0, ..., p−1}, predict (a+b) mod p. Three moduli (p = 113, 149, 197) and three training fractions (α = 0.2, 0.25, 0.3) are crossed in a factorial design, with five random seeds each.

Architectures. Two models are evaluated:

  1. A two-layer transformer encoder (d_model = 128, 4 attention heads, MLP width 256, GELU) with pre-layer norm, using only the two input tokens.
  2. A three-hidden-layer MLP (d_embed = 128, width 512, GELU) processing concatenated embeddings of a and b.

Both are trained with AdamW (lr = 3×10⁻³, weight decay 0.1) for 60,000 steps with batch size 512, checkpointed every 500 steps.

Topological analysis. At each checkpoint, Vietoris–Rips persistent homology is computed (via RIPSER) on point clouds derived from (i) the token embedding matrix rows and (ii) the second-token hidden states after each encoder layer. Point clouds are centered and normalized before PH computation. Two scalar summaries are extracted: H₁ maximum persistence (the longest-lived loop) and H₁ total persistence (sum of all loop lifetimes).

Comparison baselines. Fourier analysis (following Nanda et al.) measures restricted/excluded accuracy over dominant Fourier frequencies. Local intrinsic dimension (LID) is estimated with the TwoNN estimator on 2,000 subsampled test-set hidden states.

Ablations. Label permutation at fractions P_frac ∈ {0%, 1%, 5%, 10%, 20%, 100%} is used to corrupt the modular structure and test whether PH signals are generalization-specific. Control experiments on MNIST (varying weight initialization scale) test generalizability beyond cyclic-structure tasks.

Results & Highlights

Transformer on modular arithmetic (p = 197): H₁ max persistence rises sharply from a baseline of ~0.075–0.08 to 0.20–0.25 at the grokking step, while H₁ total persistence rises from ~20 to 30–50. The grokking step shifts with training fraction (α = 0.3 generalizes at steps 15,000–20,000; α = 0.2 at steps 35,000–45,000), and the PH transition tracks this shift precisely. LID simultaneously drops from ~20–25 to ~5, providing the complementary compression picture. Results replicate across p = 113 and p = 149.

MLP on modular arithmetic: Embedding-layer H₁ max rises from ~0.08 to 0.29–0.35. A layer-wise pattern emerges: H₁ max increases further at layer 3 (0.4–0.5), but H₁ total shows layer-dependent behavior — for smaller primes it decreases at deeper layers (consolidation into fewer stronger cycles), while for p = 197 it increases modestly, consistent with the larger group sustaining more distributed topological structure.

Ablation (label permutation): For P_frac ≤ 10%, grokking occurs and PH signals are strong: H₁ max correlates positively with test accuracy (Spearman ρ up to 0.81 at layer 1), and H₀ measures correlate negatively (ρ as low as −0.91). At P_frac ≥ 20%, models fail to generalize and all PH–accuracy correlations collapse near zero.

Temporal dynamics: Cross-correlation analysis finds a consistent ~1,000-step lag in which test accuracy changes precede PH changes for transformers. MLPs show near-zero lag, suggesting simultaneous reorganization.

MNIST control: No sharp topological transition is observed. H₁ max increases gradually without a clear inflection; H₁ total rises early then declines. This confirms that the clean PH signature depends on the presence of underlying global cyclic structure in the task.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: The application of persistent homology specifically to characterize grokking is claimed as a first by the authors, and this appears credible — prior TDA work on neural networks (Watanabe & Yamana, Ballester et al.) does not address grokking. The finding that PH captures global loop structure that LID misses is a genuine methodological contribution. However, the claim is somewhat narrow: the result confirms (in a new language) what Nanda et al. already showed via Fourier analysis — that modular arithmetic grokking coincides with emergent circular/cyclic structure in representations. The topological lens makes this precise and multi-scale, but does not overturn or substantially revise the prior picture.

Rigor: The experimental design is sound. Multiple seeds (5), multiple primes, multiple training fractions, and a label-corruption ablation form a reasonably thorough empirical package. The use of Spearman correlations with significance testing (p < 0.05 bolded) is appropriate for the ordinal relationships studied. The cross-correlation analysis for temporal ordering is a nice addition that goes beyond many comparable papers.

Reproducibility: High. Hardware and software environment are specified (WSL2, RTX 3070 Laptop, AMD Ryzen 5800H), compute times are given (8–10 min per model, 2 min PH analysis), fixed seeds are used. However, no code repository is linked in the preprint — code availability is unclear.

Significance: Moderate. The paper adds a new diagnostic lens with genuine geometric interpretability, but the scope is limited to tasks with known global cyclic structure. The MNIST experiments honestly expose this limitation. The paper does not advance a causal or mechanistic account of why grokking happens — it characterizes the geometric correlate of grokking, not its cause.

Red flags:

  • The MNIST results are notably diffuse and the paper's framing somewhat undersells this as a "limitation" when it is in fact a substantial constraint on generalizability — the method appears task-geometry-specific.
  • The paper makes no claim about whether PH could be used to predict or accelerate grokking (only to detect it post hoc), limiting practical impact.
  • No code is linked; the TDA community expects reproducible pipelines.

Strengths

  • Clean, honest experimental design with multiple control conditions (label permutation, MNIST, multiple architectures and primes).
  • Genuine TDA expertise in the author team ensures methodological correctness.
  • The temporal ordering analysis (CCF) is a relatively underexplored dimension that adds real scientific value.
  • Results are consistent and reproducible across seeds and settings in the regime where the method is applicable.
  • Comparison with LID and Fourier analysis provides useful triangulation rather than cherry-picking the most favorable method.

---

Limitations

  • The topological signature is highly task-specific: it works cleanly for modular arithmetic (cyclic group structure) but not for MNIST. Generalizability to other tasks is an open question.
  • PH provides a descriptive geometric summary, not a mechanistic or causal explanation of grokking. The paper cannot explain why neural networks grok, only that a specific topological reorganization accompanies it.
  • No code repository is provided, making full reproduction dependent on re-implementing from description.
  • The study is limited to small-scale models (2-layer transformer, 3-layer MLP) trained on toy algorithmic tasks. Whether these signatures appear in larger models or more naturalistic tasks is untested.
  • The ~1,000-step temporal lag between accuracy improvement and PH transition suggests PH is a lagging indicator — not useful for early detection of grokking.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can PH signatures be used as a predictive or interventional tool — e.g., can enforcing topological regularization accelerate grokking, as the sphere-constraint paper (Yıldırım, 2026) suggests for geometric inductive biases?
  2. Do similar H₁ persistence signatures appear during grokking in larger-scale language models trained on more structured linguistic or mathematical tasks?
  3. The ~1,000-step lag between accuracy gain and topological reorganization is intriguing — what mechanism underlies this geometric consolidation phase, and is it related to the "weight norm compression" or "subnetwork competition" accounts of grokking?
  4. Can PH applied to attention patterns (rather than token embeddings) reveal complementary information about the circuits involved?
  5. The MLP encodes topological structure more in deeper layers than the transformer; what does this say about the role of attention in enabling early representational organization?
  6. Is there a minimal algebraic condition on the training task that predicts whether PH will produce an interpretable grokking signature (e.g., tasks with abelian group structure vs. tasks without)?
