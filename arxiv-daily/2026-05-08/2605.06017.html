Matrix-Decoupled Concentration for Autoregressive Sequences: Dimension-Free Guarantees for Sparse Long-Context Rewards

arXiv: 2605.06017

Date: 2026-05-08

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06017

一句话总结： 本文提出矩阵解耦集中度（MDC）框架，通过精确的因果解析矩阵与目标敏感度向量的矩阵-向量乘积来编码依赖结构，从根本上消除了自回归LLM稀疏序列奖励评估中经典界限存在的 O(N) 方差代理膨胀问题，实现了与序列长度无关的 O(1) 维度自由界限。
TL;DR: This paper introduces the Matrix-Decoupled Concentration (MDC) framework, a new McDiarmid-type inequality for dependent sequences arising in autoregressive LLMs. Unlike classical approaches that suffer from "scalar collapse" — where the dependency structure is compressed to a scalar and artificially inflates the variance proxy to O(N) for sparse terminal rewards — MDC encodes dependencies as an exact causal interdependence matrix H and computes the variance proxy via the resolvent-sensitivity product ||Γc||₂². This preserves coordinate-wise sparsity of the reward function, yielding a dimension-free O(1) bound for long-context generation. The framework also recovers optimal constants for Markov chains and gives order-optimal O(N) bounds for causal trees, areas where classical methods either diverge or require inapplicable global conditioning. Worth reading if you care about the theoretical foundations of RLHF/sequence-level evaluation, or formal guarantees for long-context LLMs.

核心贡献

  • 矩阵解耦集中度（MDC）框架：将逐步条件依赖关系编码为因果相互依赖矩阵 H，并通过因果解析矩阵 Γ = (I − H)⁻¹ 与敏感度向量 c 的精确矩阵-向量乘积确定方差代理，从代数上防止标量坍塌。
  • 维度自由的稳定性保证：对于稀疏终端奖励（c = (0,...,0,c_N)），MDC 给出与序列长度 N 无关的 O(1) 方差代理，而经典方法产生 O(N) 膨胀，从理论上证明了长上下文推理的稳定性。
  • 对 Markov 链的最优常数恢复：MDC 自然恢复 Marton 最优传输方法的 (1−α)⁻² 方差乘子，无需测度张量化或严格凸性假设。
  • 因果树的阶最优界限：利用图 d-分离，MDC 在经典空间方法（依赖全局 Gibbs 条件化，与自回归因果过滤根本不相容）无法应用的情况下，给出 O(N) 阶最优界限。
  • 严格因果框架：MDC 的构造严格尊重序列生成的因果顺序，不要求对未来的信息，从根本上解决了空间耦合方法与自回归解码之间的因果结构不匹配问题。

Key Contributions

  • The MDC Framework (Theorem 3.1): A sharp McDiarmid-type inequality for arbitrary finite-valued dependent sequences where the variance proxy is governed strictly by ||Γc||₂², the squared ℓ₂-norm of the matrix-vector product between the causal resolvent Γ = (I − H)⁻¹ and the sensitivity vector c. This is the central technical object that prevents scalar collapse.
  • Dimension-Free Bound for Sparse LLM Rewards (Proposition 5.2): For any autoregressive sequence satisfying the sub-critical causal influence condition, a sparse terminal reward function (sensitivity only at the final token) satisfies a concentration bound with variance proxy c²_N(1−α)⁻², which is strictly independent of context length N.
  • Recovery of Optimal Markov Chain Constants (Proposition 4.1): MDC natively recovers the optimal (1−α)² transport constant for contracting Markov chains without requiring the metric tensorization steps forced on classical optimal transport approaches (Marton, Samson).
  • Order-Optimal Bounds for Causal Trees (Proposition 4.3): By exploiting topological d-separation, MDC establishes optimal O(N) concentration for tree-structured causal dependencies where one-dimensional linearization methods inflate constants to O(N²) or diverge.
  • Identification and Resolution of Two Bottlenecks: The paper formalizes "scalar collapse" (caused by sub-multiplicative norm decoupling) and "causal structure mismatch" (caused by global Gibbs conditioning in spatial methods) as the two fundamental obstacles in existing frameworks, then resolves both simultaneously.

Methodology

The MDC framework is built on three interlocking components:

1. The Causal Interdependence Matrix H. Given transition kernels {p_i(· | x_{1:i−1})}_{i=1}^N for the autoregressive sequence, the author defines H ∈ ℝ^{N×N} where each strictly upper-triangular entry H_{i,j} (i < j) bounds the maximum total variation distance between the transition kernels of X_j caused by altering the single state X_i, conditioning on all intervening states. By the causal structure, H is strictly upper-triangular and entry-wise non-negative, making (I − H) invertible via a finite Neumann series. The zero entries of H exactly encode conditional independences (e.g., Markov property, d-separation).

2. The Causal Resolvent Γ = (I − H)⁻¹. This matrix aggregates all directed causal paths (of any length) between positions. Its entry Γ_{i,j} = Σ_{r=0}^{N-1} (H^r)_{i,j} sums contributions from paths of length r. For causal trees, Γ_{i,j} = α^{d(i,j)} (where d(i,j) is ancestor path length), precisely encoding tree topology.

3. Variance Proxy ||Γc||₂². The key algebraic insight is that the exact matrix-vector multiplication Γc (rather than a scalar spectral norm) retains the zero structure of the sensitivity vector c. For a sparse reward with c = (0,...,0,c_N)^T, the product Γc isolates only the N-th column of Γ, contributing only terms where i is an ancestor of the terminal token. This algebraic preservation prevents the classical inflation from ||Γ||∞² ||c||∞² which treats all coordinates as uniformly sensitive.

Proof strategy (Theorem 3.1): The proof proceeds via (i) Doob martingale decomposition of f(X) − E[f(X)]; (ii) vectorized path coupling to bound the conditional oscillation δ_k(x) of each martingale difference Δ_k, establishing δ_k(x) ≤ (Γc)_k through the recursive inequality (I − H^T)v ≤ e_k and monotone left-multiplication by Γ^T; (iii) applying Hoeffding's Lemma to each bounded martingale difference and chaining the exponential moment bounds.

Results & Highlights

  • Main theorem (Theorem 3.1): P(|f(X) − E[f(X)]| ≥ t) ≤ 2 exp(−2t²/||Γc||₂²), strictly determined by the exact resolvent-sensitivity product.
  • Scalar collapse quantified: Classical sub-multiplicative bounds give σ² ≤ N||Γ||∞² ||c||∞², which evaluates to O(N) for sparse rewards. The figure in Section 5.2 shows the classical bound (red dashed) growing linearly with context length N while the MDC theoretical bound (blue) remains constant and the exact MDC proxy for sliding-window attention (green) is also flat.
  • Markov chains (Proposition 4.1): MDC recovers the exact (1−α)⁻² constant from Marton [12], now valid for L₂ geometries without metric conversion artifacts.
  • Causal trees (Proposition 4.3): For a sub-critical tree (αD < 1) with uniform Lipschitz constants, ||Γ1_N||₂² ≤ N/(1−αD)², giving P(|S_N − E[S_N]| ≥ t) ≤ 2 exp(−2t²(1−αD)²/N).
  • LLM application (Proposition 5.2): For sparse terminal reward with sensitivity vector c = (0,...,0,c_N)^T under sub-critical causal influence (||H||_1 ≤ α < 1), the variance proxy is c_N²(1−α)⁻² — a constant independent of N. This is the formal statement that long-context LLMs with sub-critical attention are theoretically stable.
  • Independence as a special case: When H = 0, Γ = I, and the MDC bound reduces exactly to the classical McDiarmid inequality ||c||₂², confirming no structural loss for independent sequences.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: High. The paper correctly identifies a genuine gap in existing concentration inequality literature for autoregressive sequences: the incompatibility of spatial methods with causal filtrations and the systematic scalar collapse in martingale graph methods. The matrix-resolvent formulation and the algebraic insight that exact matrix-vector multiplication preserves sparsity are new and non-trivial. The unified treatment of Markov chains, causal trees, and LLMs under one framework is a meaningful contribution.

Rigor: High for the theoretical claims. The main theorem proof is complete in the appendix and the algebraic steps are careful. The sub-critical causal influence condition (Condition 5.1) is a genuine mathematical assumption, and the paper is appropriately explicit about it. The paper does not overstate its results.

Reproducibility: Moderate. This is a pure theory paper; the numerical figure (Figure 1) is simple to reproduce given the stated parameters (sliding window size W=5, α=0.8). However, there is no code, and applying MDC to a real LLM would require computing or bounding H, which the paper does not demonstrate empirically.

Significance: Meaningful for the theory of sequence-level evaluation in LLMs and RLHF. The dimension-free O(1) guarantee for sparse long-context rewards is the kind of result that could eventually underpin sample complexity bounds for RLHF training or formal stability certificates for long-context inference. The practical impact depends heavily on whether the sub-critical causal influence assumption holds for real transformer attention patterns — this is discussed qualitatively (sliding window, linear attention) but not verified.

Red Flags:

  • Single-author preprint with no peer review yet.
  • The sub-critical condition ||H||_1 ≤ α < 1 is a non-trivial assumption whose validity for dense standard transformer self-attention (not just sliding window) is not established. The paper acknowledges this in the future work section.
  • The framework handles worst-case H_{i,j} (a quenched L∞ bound). The paper itself notes in the future work section that relaxing to an "annealed L¹ bound" would vastly expand applicability, implicitly acknowledging the current bound may be conservative for non-uniformly mixing sequences.
  • No experiments or empirical validation are provided, making the practical relevance to real LLMs purely conjectural at this stage.
  • The "Markov random fields / beyond directed time" limitation is explicitly acknowledged — MDC relies intrinsically on the unidirectional arrow of time and does not extend to undirected or bidirectional dependency graphs.

Strengths

  • Cleanly identifies and formally names two distinct bottlenecks (scalar collapse and causal structure mismatch) that existed implicitly in the prior literature.
  • The causal resolvent Γ = (I − H)⁻¹ is an elegant algebraic object that subsumes path-counting in graphical models while remaining computable via a finite Neumann series.
  • Dimensional analysis is explicit throughout: the paper tracks exactly where O(N) inflation enters classical bounds and where MDC prevents it.
  • The recovery of known optimal constants (Marton's (1−α)² for Markov chains) without requiring their original proof techniques is a strong sanity check.
  • The connection to d-separation (Pearl) grounds the matrix sparsity in well-understood graphical model theory.

Limitations

  • The theory is bounded-differences (Hoeffding-type); no Bernstein/Freedman-type variance-adaptive bounds are established, which could be significantly tighter for highly sparse transitions.
  • Verifying or bounding H for real large-scale LLMs is non-trivial and not addressed. The paper is fully theoretical without empirical evaluation.
  • The framework assumes finite state spaces (discrete token vocabularies), with no extension to continuous or mixed distributions.
  • Extensions to undirected graphical models (Markov random fields) are explicitly left as future work; the strictly causal (lower-triangular) structure is essential to the current approach.
  • The worst-case (quenched) definition of H_{i,j} may lead to conservative bounds when the actual coupling between tokens is intermittent rather than persistent.

Questions for Further Exploration

  2. How does the MDC resolvent Γ relate to the attention weight matrices in practice — is there a natural approximation of H from attention scores or hidden state Jacobians?
