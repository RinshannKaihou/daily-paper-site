PACZero: PAC-Private Fine-Tuning of Language Models via Sign Quantization

arXiv: 2605.06505

Date: 2026-05-08

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.06505

一句话总结： PACZero 通过对子集聚合零阶梯度进行符号量化，在大语言模型微调中实现了 PAC 隐私保证，其 PACZero-ZPL 变体在 I=0（完美成员推断抵抗）条件下，于 SST-2 OPT-1.3B 全参数微调中达到 88.99±0.91% 的准确率，距非隐私基线 MeZO 仅差约 2.1 个百分点。
TL;DR: PACZero is a family of PAC-private zeroth-order (ZO) optimization mechanisms for LLM fine-tuning that sidesteps the utility collapse plaguing differentially-private ZO methods in high-privacy regimes. It aggregates per-sample ZO scalars over M=128 random subsets, sign-quantizes each subset mean, and releases a single bit per step. When all subsets agree on the sign (unanimity, ~34–45% of SST-2 steps), the release is information-theoretically free; on disagreement steps, noise is calibrated to an exact binary-channel mutual information budget rather than worst-case sensitivity. The result is two variants: PACZero-MI (budgeted MI) and PACZero-ZPL (zero MI, perfect MIA resistance at I=0), with PACZero-ZPL reaching 88.99±0.91% on SST-2 OPT-1.3B full fine-tuning — within 2.1pp of the non-private MeZO baseline and at an MIA-resistance level that no published DP-ZO accountant can achieve at finite per-step noise. If you work on private LLM fine-tuning and care about the high-privacy regime where DP-SGD utility collapses, this paper is worth reading.

Key Contributions

  • PAC-private ZO mechanism family: Introduces PACZero, which replaces DP's per-record sensitivity clipping with PAC Privacy's mutual-information (MI) calibration over a subset-distribution model of the training corpus, enabling useful high-privacy fine-tuning of LLMs without backpropagation.
  • Zero-cost unanimity steps: When the sign of the secret subset's ZO scalar agrees with every candidate subset (~34–45% of training steps on SST-2), the release contributes zero conditional MI, providing gradient signal at literally zero privacy budget cost — a structural property absent from all DP-ZO approaches.
  • Exact binary-channel MI calibration (PACZero-MI): Replaces the Gaussian variance upper bound used by canonical PAC Privacy with the exact MI of the noised binary release, computed via Gauss-Hermite quadrature plus bisection. This closes an unbounded calibration gap as budgets tighten, producing a flat accuracy plateau across four decades of MI budget (10^{-4} to 0.68 nats).
  • Zero privacy loss variant (PACZero-ZPL): By releasing a uniform U({-1,+1}) coin flip on disagreement steps, the mechanism achieves I(S*; Y_{1:T}) = 0 for every T — perfect MIA-prior resistance — while still making progress via unanimity steps. This is the first iterative LLM fine-tuning mechanism with this guarantee.
  • Comprehensive empirical evaluation: Tested on SST-2 (accuracy) and SQuAD (F1) across OPT-1.3B and OPT-6.7B in both LoRA and full-parameter tracks, with ablations on MI budget, clip norm, LoRA rank, unanimity rates, and mechanism decomposition.

Methodology

Setup. PACZero operates in the zeroth-order (ZO) fine-tuning setting: a single perturbation direction z ~ N(0, I_d) is shared across all training samples, and per-sample gradients are approximated as finite-difference scalars g_i = [ℓ_i(θ+μz) - ℓ_i(θ-μz)] / (2μ). Since z is data-independent, privatizing the update reduces to privatizing a scalar, not a d-dimensional vector.

PAC Privacy instantiation. The training corpus of N examples is partitioned into M=128 random subsets S_1, ..., S_M (each example appears in M/2 subsets). The secret dataset S = S_{j} is drawn uniformly at random from these M subsets. Membership inference attacks (MIA) target whether a record x_i is in S*; the prior MIA success rate is exactly 1/2 under this construction.

Per-step mechanism. At each step t:

  1. Compute per-sample ZO scalars g_i and clip them for stability.
  2. Aggregate to M subset means g_m and sign-quantize: s_m = sign(g_m) ∈ {-1, +1}.
  3. Compute posterior agreement probability q_t^+ = Σ_m p_t[m] · 1[s_m = +1].
  4. Unanimity branch (q_t^+ ∈ {0,1}): release Y_t = s_{j*} deterministically (zero MI, zero noise, zero budget cost).
  5. Disagreement branch (q_t^+ ∈ (0,1)):
  • PACZero-MI: release Y_t = sign(s_{j*} + N(0, σ_t^2)) where σ_t is determined by exact BinaryMI^{-1}(q_t^+, β_t) to meet per-step MI budget β_t; update Bayesian posterior p_{t+1} on observed noisy release.
  • PACZero-ZPL: release a uniform coin flip Y_t ~ U({-1,+1}), independent of S* and z_t; posterior stays fixed.
  1. Update model parameters: θ_{t+1} ← (1 - η_t λ)θ_t - η_t Y_t z_t.

MI budget management (PACZero-MI). PACZero-MI uses adaptive budget allocation: β_t = max(0, MI_tot - MI_used^{(t-1)}) / (T - t + 1), re-spending unspent budget from unanimity steps uniformly over remaining steps. This provably satisfies I(S*; Y_{1:T}) ≤ MI_tot (Theorem 3).

Privacy proofs. Lemma 2 establishes per-step MI: zero on unanimity, exactly β_t^used on disagreement (by construction of the inverse). Theorem 3 proves the end-to-end guarantee via the adaptive composition framework. PACZero-ZPL's I=0 guarantee follows immediately from per-step independence (Theorem 5).

Key insight. Sign quantization before noise calibration creates frequent unanimity steps where the posterior-weighted majority collapses: the released bit is a deterministic function of public history, carrying zero conditional MI. This "structural free lunch" is the mechanism's core advantage over DP-ZO methods, which must add noise proportional to worst-case per-record sensitivity regardless of data stability.

Results & Highlights

SST-2 headline (Table 1):

  • PACZero-ZPL at I=0: 88.99±0.91% (FT 1.3B), 90.52±1.43% (FT 6.7B) — within 2.1pp of non-private MeZO baseline (91.1% FT).
  • PACZero-MI at MI=0.33 nats (matched to DP ε≈2): 89.51±1.12% (FT 1.3B), exceeds matched DPZero K=1 by +2.9pp.
  • At matched MI budgets, PACZero-MI is statistically equivalent to or above the best PACZero-ZPL cell.

The DP-ZO cliff (Table 3):

  • DPZero K=1 FT remains at chance accuracy (~50%) for every ε < 1 at T=20,000 steps.
  • DP-AggZO K=64 FT first achieves above-chance accuracy only at ε=0.5 (65.94%), and only reaches 88.42% at ε=2.
  • PACZero-ZPL at I=0 matches the strongest in-house DP cell (88.42%) at ε=2, demonstrating utility in a privacy regime no DP-ZO accountant can enter at finite noise.

Tight-MI plateau (Table 2): PACZero-MI test accuracy on SST-2 FT OPT-1.3B spans only 86.47–89.56% across four decades of nominal MI (10^{-4} to 0.68 nats, T=1000), a 3.09pp range. DP-ZO accuracy collapses steeply as ε tightens — a qualitatively different behavior.

SQuAD results: PACZero-MI trails DPZero K=1 by 6–13 F1 on FT cells (optimization protocol mismatch: DPZero uses T=20,000, PACZero-MI uses T=1,000). PACZero-ZPL still achieves nontrivial F1 (62.25±1.33 at 1.3B, 72.12 at 6.7B). The lower unanimity rate on SQuAD (~25–33% vs. 34–45% on SST-2) explains the larger utility gap.

Unanimity rates: SST-2 FT: ~34–45%; SST-2 LoRA: ~38–48%; SQuAD FT: ~25–33%; SQuAD LoRA: ~8–11%. The unanimity fraction is stable across MI budget levels, confirming it is a property of the loss landscape rather than the privacy mechanism.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: High. The application of sign quantization to create "unanimity" steps with zero conditional MI is a genuinely novel structural insight. Combining PAC Privacy with iterative ZO fine-tuning addresses an open problem explicitly noted in prior PAC Privacy work. The exact binary-channel MI calibration (replacing variance upper bounds) is a clean technical contribution.

Rigor: Strong for the privacy claims. Theorem 3 and 5 are properly proven with deferred full proofs. The adaptive composition framework (from Zhu et al., 2026) is correctly applied. The empirical comparisons are carefully matched at the MIA-prior level rather than conflating DP-ε with MI nats directly, and the paper is transparent about the semantic gap between PAC Privacy and DP.

Reproducibility: Good. Code is released. Appendices provide per-seed values (Tables 4–5), per-cell hyperparameter recipes (Table 7), learning-rate sweeps (Table 8), clip ablations (Table 9), and GPU-hour counts (Tables 20–21). The DP-AggZO parity check (Appendix D) validates the baseline reproduction.

Significance: Meaningful for the private fine-tuning community. The paper provides the first practical, iterative LLM fine-tuning mechanism achieving I=0 MIA resistance with usable utility. The flat MI plateau finding challenges the conventional wisdom that tighter privacy always costs more accuracy.

Red flags and caveats:

  1. PAC Privacy ≠ DP: The paper is explicit about this but it bears emphasis for readers. PAC Privacy bounds MI over a subset-distribution model; it does NOT provide DP's worst-case neighbor guarantee. PACZero-ZPL is not (ε, δ)-DP for any finite ε. "DP ε=0" annotations in tables are numerical reference points only, not claims about DP guarantees.
  2. SQuAD protocol mismatch: The 6–13 F1 gap on SQuAD FT vs. DPZero K=1 is confounded by the 20× step-count difference (T=20,000 for DPZero vs. T=1,000 for PACZero). This is acknowledged but weakens the SQuAD comparison.
  3. Scale limitations: All experiments use OPT-1.3B and OPT-6.7B. Whether unanimity rates (which drive PACZero-ZPL utility) generalize to Llama/Gemma/Mistral or to token-level generation tasks is an open question noted in the paper.
  4. Evaluation scope: Results are on two benchmarks (SST-2, SQuAD) and two models. Pre-training is explicitly out of scope. The M=128 subset construction with |U|=1000 fits fine-tuning corpora only.
  5. PACZero-ZPL "progress" mechanism: Learning at I=0 depends entirely on unanimity steps (~34–45% on SST-2). On tasks with lower unanimity (SQuAD LoRA: ~8–11%), utility is substantially reduced without any tuneable privacy-utility knob.

Strengths

  • Cleanly motivated: identifies a root cause (worst-case sensitivity in DP) and proposes a structurally different fix (subset aggregation + sign quantization + MI calibration) rather than incremental tuning.
  • The unanimity insight is elegant and independently verifiable: the paper provides mechanism decomposition ablations (Appendix K, Table 12) confirming the gain comes from sign quantization, not subset aggregation alone.
  • Transparent about the PAC vs. DP distinction throughout; does not oversell privacy guarantees.
  • Adaptive MI budget allocation is simple (one-liner, Eq. 6) and provably correct within the adaptive composition framework.
  • Comprehensive appendices with ablations, per-seed data, and implementation details supporting reproducibility.

---

Limitations

  • No results on models beyond OPT family; generalization of unanimity rates to other architectures is unknown.
  • SQuAD results are not cleanly competitive due to protocol mismatch; the paper acknowledges this but the comparison remains unsatisfying.
  • PACZero-ZPL provides no privacy-utility knob: once unanimity rate drops (e.g., on generative tasks or LoRA), utility degrades with no mechanism to recover it without reintroducing MI cost.
  • The PAC Privacy guarantee is over the subset-distribution D = Unif{S_1,...,S_M}, not over arbitrary datasets. The paper's I=0 claim applies within this specific adversarial model; it does not rule out other forms of leakage outside this model.
  • The proposed hybrid budget-then-ZPL schedule (Section 6 future work) is not yet implemented or evaluated; it may be the practical sweet spot but remains speculative.
  • Compute costs are moderate but non-trivial: per-cell GPU hours are reported in Appendix N; the M=128 subset forward passes add overhead relative to standard ZO.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How do unanimity rates behave on instruction-following or token-level generation tasks (e.g., summarization, code completion), where the loss landscape may be substantially different from SST-2 classification?
  2. Can the hybrid MI-then-ZPL schedule (spend MI budget to concentrate the posterior, then switch to ZPL) be formalized with convergence guarantees under the adaptive composition framework?
  3. Does the flat MI plateau (four decades of budget yielding <3.1pp accuracy variation) hold for larger models (>7B parameters) and other architectures (Llama-3, Mistral)?
  4. PAC Privacy bounds MI under a balanced subset distribution with |U|=1000. How sensitive are the utility and privacy guarantees to the choice of M, |U|, and the subset construction when fine-tuning corpora are much larger (>100K examples)?
  5. The paper shows PACZero-MI outperforms PACZero-ZPL on SQuAD. Is there a task-dependent threshold on unanimity rate below which PACZero-ZPL is not viable as a standalone mechanism?
  6. How does PACZero interact with privacy amplification by subsampling, which is a key tool in DP-SGD? Can subsampling be incorporated without violating the subset-distribution model underlying PAC Privacy?
  7. The mechanism decomposition (Appendix K) shows sign quantization accounts for +2.3pp at T=2000. How does this interact with the choice of perturbation scale μ and whether the ZO gradient approximation is in a reliable regime?
