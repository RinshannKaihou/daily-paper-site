Decodable but Not Corrected by Fixed Residual-Stream Linear Steering: Evidence from Medical LLM Failure Regimes

arXiv: 2605.05715

Date: 2026-05-08

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05715

一句话总结： 在医学QA任务中，LLM的"过度思考"失败模式在隐藏状态中可被线性探针以71.6%的精度解码，但29种配置的固定线性激活引导均无法将其纠正（Δ≈0），原因在于该失败信号与任务关键计算方向高度纠缠（特异性比仅0.119），尽管同一信号可用于事后选择性弃权（AUROC=0.610）。
TL;DR: This paper investigates whether linearly decodable failure signals in LLM hidden states can be leveraged to correct those failures — specifically targeting "Overthinking" (OT), a regime where models answer correctly under resampling but fail via extended chain-of-thought reasoning on medical QA. The OT signal is reliably decodable (71.6% balanced accuracy, p≈10⁻¹⁶, robust across two architectures and one additional domain), but five families of fixed linear activation steering covering 29 configurations all produce near-zero accuracy delta (Δ≈0), while non-targeted shared-direction steering and concept erasure actively damage accuracy (−12.1pp and −3.6pp respectively). The core diagnosis is representational entanglement: the OT direction shares 88% of its variance with task-relevant computation (specificity ratio 0.119), making any fixed linear perturbation along it a collateral attack on the reasoning it is supposed to fix. The same decodable structure does, however, support post-generation selective abstention (held-out AUROC=0.610, outperforming all five uncertainty baselines), showing that reading representations is operationally useful even when writing to them is not.

核心贡献

  • 识别并验证"过度思考"（Overthinking, OT）行为区制：OT定义为模型在重采样下能正确回答（≥60%正确率），但在扩展CoT推理中失败（>200 token）的稳定行为模式，经过长度回归、提示变体、专家标注（94%一致性，Jaccard≥0.81）验证，是真实的生成时区制而非问题难度伪影。
  • 量化"可解码–可纠正"差距：OT在Llama-3.1-8B第17层以81.5%单向准确率线性可解码（跨架构Qwen2.5-7B和跨领域MMLU-STEM验证），但29种固定线性引导配置全部产生Δ≈0，两组证据明确分离了"可读"与"可写"两种能力。
  • 提供表示纠缠的几何证据：OT特异性比仅0.119（对比拒绝方向的0.999），88%的对比信号与所有失败模式共享；概念擦除（LEACE）证明该纠缠是因果性的而非仅相关性（方向特定损害 vs. 随机方向+0.3pp）；跨架构引导子空间在主要引导层16-17处退化至0.67/0.65。
  • 将可解码结构转化为选择性弃权的实用价值：同一隐藏状态结构支持事后可靠性估计，第21层探针在持出测试集上AUROC=0.610，超越全部5种不确定性基线（ΔAUROC=0.041, p=0.009），在60%覆盖率下准确率提升+5.5pp。
  • 初步证据表明非线性干预可部分弥合差距：学习的非线性MLP（4096→64→4096）获得Δ=+2.8pp（p=0.025），提示纠缠是程度问题而非绝对障碍，为未来实例自适应方法指明方向。

Key Contributions

  • Behavioral regime identification and validation of Overthinking (OT): OT is operationalized as a sampling-level pattern (≥60% correct across resampled traces, but incorrect in long-reasoning traces >200 tokens) and validated as a generation-time construct stable under length regression, prompt perturbation, and expert annotation (94% expert agreement, Jaccard ≥0.81, 100% within-question purity).
  • Empirical quantification of the classification-correction gap: The OT signal is linearly decodable at 71.6% balanced accuracy (p≈10⁻¹⁶) with cross-architecture (Qwen2.5-7B) and cross-domain (MMLU-STEM) generalization, yet all 29 fixed linear steering configurations yield Δ≈0 — cleanly separating the capacity to read representations from the capacity to write to them.
  • Geometric diagnosis of representational entanglement: The OT contrastive direction has specificity ratio 0.119 (vs. 0.999 for refusal), meaning 88% of its variance is shared with task-relevant directions; LEACE concept erasure provides causal evidence (direction-specific damage −3.6pp vs. random +0.3pp); cross-domain subspace alignment degrades precisely at the steering intervention layers.
  • Operational reuse for post-generation selective abstention: A single-forward-pass correctness probe (layer 21) achieves held-out AUROC=0.610, outperforming all five uncertainty baselines, with +5.5pp accuracy gains at 60% coverage — demonstrating that decodable structure has operational value for reliability estimation even when steering fails.
  • Partial gap closure via learned non-linear intervention: A non-linear residual MLP achieves Δ=+2.8pp (p=0.025), providing preliminary evidence that the entanglement is partial rather than absolute, and pointing toward instance-adaptive methods as the most promising direction forward.

Methodology

Dataset and models. Experiments center on MedQA (10,178 training / 1,273 test questions) evaluated on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct as the primary model and Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct for cross-architecture validation. An additional cross-domain check uses MMLU-STEM (300 questions, 18 subjects).

Failure annotation. Each question receives 10 sampled traces (T=0.8). OT is heuristically labeled when ≥60% of traces are correct but incorrect traces exceed 200 tokens. Remaining failures are classified into Knowledge Deficit (KD) and Reasoning Chain Break (RCB) via Claude Haiku in an LLM-as-judge paradigm. Domain expert validation (two blinded 4th-year clinical students, n=500 gold set) confirms 94% OT agreement (Jaccard ≥0.81).

Hidden state extraction. Last-token hidden states at all 32 layers (Llama) or 28 layers (Qwen) are extracted in fp32 precision from 12,000 traces (2,000 per failure mode + 6,000 matched correct traces), yielding tensors of shape (12000, 32, 4096) or (12000, 28, 3584).

Geometric analysis. Mean-difference contrastive vectors are computed per layer per mode. A specificity ratio metric quantifies how much of each contrastive vector is mode-specific versus shared across all modes. Binary OT-vs.-non-OT probes use PCA-50 features with logistic regression (L2, LBFGS). Permutation tests (10,000 shuffles) validate the low specificity finding is not an artifact of set-subset structure.

Steering experiments. Five method families are tested (29 additive configurations total):

  1. Contrastive steering — adding mode-specific or uniform shared contrastive vectors
  2. Probe-guided steering — using probe weight vectors as intervention directions
  3. Multi-layer steering — applying vectors simultaneously at 1, 3, or 5 layers
  4. Rank-k subspace steering — SVD-constructed rank-{1,3,5} bases from stacked direction matrices
  5. Prompt-end steering — vectors extracted from prompt-end hidden states (causally independent of the response)

Vectors are applied via forward hooks adding α·v to the residual stream at target layers (α∈{0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 3.0}). Equivalence testing (TOST, ±2.5pp margin) formalizes the null result. Mean-difference concept erasure (LEACE) is additionally tested.

Selective abstention. A binary correctness probe (layer 21, selected via held-out AUROC scan over 32 layers on a 70/30 split) is evaluated on the full test set for selective abstention via coverage-accuracy curves.

Results & Highlights

Decodability. Binary OT-vs.-non-OT classification achieves 71.6% balanced accuracy (AUROC=0.672, majority baseline=66.7%, p≈10⁻¹⁶). The OT probe peaks at layer 17 (81.5%), with cross-architecture transfer to Qwen (46.9% three-way at layer 18) and cross-domain transfer to MMLU-STEM (61.4% zero-shot balanced accuracy, z=6.15, p<10⁻⁹).

Steering null result. All 29 fixed linear steering configurations produce Δ≈0 on n=1,273 test questions. Mode-specific steering: Δ=−0.2pp (95% CI: [−2.8, +2.4]pp); TOST equivalence p=0.039 (within ±2.5pp). The joint evidence across all 29 configurations is decisive: were the true effect ≥2.5pp, observing all nulls simultaneously would occur with probability <10⁻¹⁵. Uniform shared-direction steering severely damages accuracy (Δ=−12.1pp). Qwen cross-architecture steering also yields all Δ∈[−0.9,+0.8]pp (all p>0.05).

Representational entanglement evidence. OT specificity ratio = 0.119 (Llama), 0.152 (Qwen), vs. refusal specificity 0.999 — a 4.7× gap. Contrastive vectors have average pairwise cosine similarity 0.820 across failure modes. Concept erasure of OT direction: Δ=−3.6pp (p=0.01, McNemar), while 10 random erasures produce Δ=+0.3pp (σ=1.8pp) — confirming direction specificity. The per-instance probe-steering correlation is r=−0.002 (p=0.97), showing probe confidence and steering effect are completely uncorrelated.

Cross-domain subspace degradation. PCA-3 cosine between MedQA and MMLU-STEM OT signals is 0.87–0.96 at most layers but drops to a local trough of 0.67/0.65 at the primary steering layers 16–17, consistent with the shared subspace degrading precisely at the intervention point.

Selective abstention. Correctness probe achieves held-out AUROC=0.610 (training: 0.716; 5-fold CV: 0.721±0.005), exceeding all five uncertainty baselines (ΔAUROC=0.041, p=0.009). At 60% coverage: +5.5pp accuracy gain; at 70% coverage: comparable gains with lower abstention cost. At 60% on balanced training: +14.0pp above the 50% balanced baseline.

Non-linear MLP partial closure. A residual MLP (4096→64→4096) achieves Δ=+2.8pp (p=0.025), 140 corrections vs. 104 damages (ratio 1.35:1), with 50.6% centroid-distance reduction but damage rate remaining at 8.2% — consistent with partial rather than complete disentanglement.

Self-consistency comparison. Majority vote at 10 traces (MV@10) achieves 72.7% (+7.4pp, p<10⁻¹⁰), correcting OT errors while fixing only 18.6% of non-OT errors (+10.6pp on training data) — solvable at 10× cost. Best-of-N probe selection at k=3 achieves 70.6% vs. MV@3's 69.2% (+1.4pp; p=0.17), suggesting complementary signal at comparable cost.

Credibility signals:

  • Cross-architecture (Qwen2.5-7B) and cross-domain (MMLU-STEM) replication of key findings.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: The classification-correction gap is now documented across multiple concurrent works (Wu et al. 2025; Basu et al. 2026; Wang et al. 2026; Sanyal et al. 2025; Cox et al. 2026). The paper's claim to novelty is providing mechanistic geometric characterization why steering fails in a specific domain — particularly the specificity ratio quantification, the concept-erasure causal test, and the cross-domain subspace degradation evidence. This is a genuine contribution over prior gap-documentation work, though the field is moving quickly and simultaneous independent discoveries dilute uniqueness.

Rigor: Very high. The statistical methodology is among the most careful seen in activation-steering literature — TOST equivalence testing (not just null-hypothesis significance), Holm correction, permutation tests for the specificity claim, multiple pre-registered validity threats with targeted controls. The use of n=1,273 for the primary test and conservative equivalence margins (±2.5pp, motivated by clinical trial conventions) is appropriate.

Reproducibility: High in principle. All models are publicly available (Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct), data is public (MedQA, MMLU-STEM), hyperparameters and implementation details are fully specified in appendices. Compute requirement is approximately 4 hours per steering configuration on an A10G GPU (24GB). Code promised upon publication — a minor concern is that "upon publication" is conditional.

Significance: High for the mechanistic interpretability and activation-steering communities. The specificity ratio as a pre-intervention diagnostic (with the 4.7× gap between OT and refusal as a predictive calibration point) is actionable and transferable. The selective abstention result — AUROC 0.610 from a single forward pass — is a concrete operational finding with deployment relevance.

Red flags:

  • Single author from Amazon; no external validation of the experimental pipeline by independent researchers (mitigated by the careful controls, but not eliminated).
  • The 60% correct-rate threshold for OT definition is operationally motivated but somewhat arbitrary; the paper shows Jaccard ≥0.81 under threshold sweeps (50–70%), which is reassuring but not fully exhaustive.
  • Qwen concept erasure experiments were not conducted (explicitly acknowledged as a limitation), leaving the Qwen specificity ratio of 0.414 (vs. Llama 0.119) unexplained — possibly a qualitatively different encoding.
  • The non-linear MLP result (Δ=+2.8pp) is statistically significant but modest and clinically non-equivalent (TOST p=0.57); it is presented appropriately as preliminary, but the gap to strong correction (10× cost self-consistency achieves +7.4pp) remains large.
  • Temperature mismatch between annotation (T=0.8) and steering evaluation (T=0.1) is a genuine confound, though same-temperature evaluation actually produces larger damage (Δ=−6.7pp), directly ruling out temperature as an explanation for the null.
  • The paper does not test MedPrompt, budget forcing, or other inference-time compute scaling strategies that might address OT differently.

Strengths

  • Unusually careful and honest statistical methodology with TOST equivalence testing and systematic confound controls.
  • The specificity ratio is a novel, quantitative, and actionable pre-intervention diagnostic that other researchers can apply to new domains.
  • Clean conceptual contribution: the "reading vs. writing" dissociation is clearly articulated and supported by three converging lines of evidence (low specificity, concept erasure damage, probe-steering uncorrelation).
  • Positive control (refusal steering) demonstrates that the null result is domain-specific failure, not infrastructure failure — a methodological best practice that many steering papers skip.
  • The selective abstention finding provides genuine practical value from the same experimental setup, turning a negative result into an actionable positive finding.
  • Explicit acknowledgment of alternative interpretations (OT direction as causally necessary for correct reasoning vs. representational superposition) shows intellectual honesty.
  • Expert clinical annotation with proper inter-annotator reliability measurement and boundary robustness analysis.

---

Limitations

  • Scale: evaluated only on 7–8B parameter models; higher-scale models likely have lower OT prevalence, and the geometry of superposition may differ substantially.
  • Scope: primary results are from MedQA medical QA; MMLU-STEM confirms the pattern but broader domain coverage is needed to establish full generality.
  • Untested intervention families: full oblique LEACE projector, DAS (Geiger et al. 2024), ReFT (Wu et al. 2024), K-CAST (Valentino et al. 2026), path patching, parameter-efficient fine-tuning — the negative result is specific to fixed-direction residual-stream linear interventions.
  • Qwen concept erasure experiments not conducted, leaving the architecture-specific geometry question partially open.
  • The "Overthinking" label is behavioral, not mechanistic — the paper explicitly disclaims making claims about internal cognitive mechanisms, but this limits interpretive depth.
  • The non-linear MLP result, while promising, uses only a 64-dimensional bottleneck trained on 3,200 examples; full-scale learned interventions (DAS-style) remain untested.
  • The 60% majority-correct threshold for OT is definitional by construction, making the 100% MV@10 correction rate a tautology rather than an independent empirical finding.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can instance-adaptive methods (K-CAST, DAS, representation fine-tuning) fully close the gap by learning to disentangle the shared subspace, given the MLP's partial success at 50.6% centroid-distance reduction?
  1. Does the specificity ratio correlate predictively with steering success across other behavioral domains (factual recall, math reasoning, sycophancy), or is there a threshold value above which steering reliably works?
  1. Why does Qwen2.5-7B show substantially higher OT specificity (0.414 vs. 0.119) despite both models failing to steer? Does this reflect a qualitatively different representational geometry, and would concept erasure on Qwen produce different results?
  1. The OT signal is generated during extended reasoning (last-token probing at 71.6% vs. prompt-end probing at 54.4%). Can token-level or prefix-conditional interventions applied early in the reasoning chain — before the failure mode accumulates — succeed where post-hoc fixed steering fails?
  1. At larger model scales (70B+) where OT prevalence may be lower, does the failure mode's representational geometry become more separable (higher specificity), potentially making linear steering viable?
  1. Could the selective abstention capability (AUROC=0.610) be operationalized in a production medical QA system, and what accuracy-coverage tradeoff is clinically acceptable given the cost of additional sampling?
