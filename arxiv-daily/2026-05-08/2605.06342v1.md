> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 SKOP（Key-Orthogonal Projections 引导方法），通过将引导向量投影到与"关键差异方向"正交的子空间，在保留 95% 以上原始引导效果的同时，将模型实用能力损失降低 5–7 倍，并首次实现了在长上下文检索任务中有效的激活引导。

> **TL;DR:** SKOP is a query-space activation steering method for LLMs that identifies "attention rerouting" — the mechanism by which steering vectors shift model attention away from contextually critical "focus tokens" toward less informative "tail tokens" — as the root cause of utility degradation during steering. By projecting steering vectors to remove components that drive harmful focus-to-tail attention shifts, and applying this correction selectively to high-risk attention heads, SKOP achieves the best steering-utility trade-off among tested methods on LLaMA-3.1-8B and Gemma-2-9B: it retains over 95% of vanilla steering efficacy while reducing utility degradation by 5–7x, and is the only method that remains effective in long-context (up to 16K token) retrieval settings where prior approaches break down.

## 核心贡献

- **注意力重路由诊断**：首次将"注意力重路由"（attention rerouting）明确识别为 query-space 激活引导中引导效果与实用能力之间权衡的根本机制——引导向量改变了 query-key 对齐方式，使注意力从少量关键"焦点 token"转移到大量低信息量的"尾部 token"。
- **SKOP 方法**：提出三阶段方法：（1）利用小型校准集（250 个样本即可）刻画每个注意力头的"关键差异方向"；（2）通过投影将引导向量中驱动焦点→尾部注意力转移的成分去除；（3）仅对最易发生重路由的高风险注意力头应用该投影，避免对安全头施加不必要的约束。
- **最优引导-实用权衡**：在多个 steering 基准上，SKOP 在所有已测方法中取得最佳综合排名，实用能力损失降低 5–7 倍，同时保留 95% 以上的引导强度。
- **长上下文有效引导**：在 RULER NIAH 长上下文检索基准上（1K–16K token），SKOP 是唯一能在 steering 的同时维持稳健检索准确率的方法，填补了激活引导在长上下文场景下的空白。

## Key Contributions

- **Mechanistic diagnosis of attention rerouting:** The paper shows formally and empirically that query-space steering changes relative query-key scores, shifting attention mass from a small, stable "focus set" of tokens (which together capture ~80% of attention mass on fewer than 20% of tokens) toward a larger "tail set." This focus-to-tail rerouting grows monotonically with steering strength and is identified as the primary driver of utility degradation.
- **SKOP algorithm:** A three-stage method that (1) collects key-difference vectors between focus and tail tokens from a small calibration set, (2) constructs a projector onto the subspace orthogonal to the dominant key-difference directions (using top-p eigenvectors of the second-moment matrix of key-difference vectors), and (3) selectively applies projection only to high-risk heads ranked by a Rayleigh-quotient-based risk score.
- **Demonstrated efficiency:** The calibration set needs only ~250 examples and is robust to domain mismatch, making SKOP practical with minimal overhead.
- **Long-context activation steering:** First demonstration that activation steering can work robustly in long-context retrieval (needle-in-a-haystack tasks up to 16K tokens), a setting where all prior methods fail.
- **Outperforms fine-tuning baseline:** SKOP matches vanilla query-space steering efficacy and substantially outperforms LoRA on steering while avoiding LoRA's monotonic utility degradation with more training data.

## Methodology

The paper operates on decoder-only transformers and focuses on query-space steering, where a steering vector **r**_q is added to query activations at each layer and head. This modifies attention logits by an additive perturbation term δ_ij = λ⟨**r**_q, **k**_j⟩/√d', which drives attention rerouting when the inner product between the steering vector and key differences between focus and tail tokens is nonzero.

**SKOP proceeds in three stages:**

1. **Characterising utility-critical key differences (Sec. 5.1):** Using a calibration set D_util, for each attention head (ℓ, h) and decoding step t, the algorithm identifies the focus set H_t (minimal token set capturing fraction τ_high = 0.8 of attention mass) and tail set L_t (complement). Key-difference vectors Δk_ij = k_i - k_j for i ∈ H, j ∈ L are collected and summarised by their second-moment matrix Σ_Δk.

2. **Projection onto the key-difference subspace (Sec. 5.2):** The expected squared perturbation on the focus-to-tail score gap under steering is r_q^T Σ_Δk r_q. The SKOP projector P_Δk = I - U_Δk U_Δk^T removes the top-p eigenvectors of Σ_Δk from the steering vector, where p is chosen so that the retained eigenvectors account for fraction γ_energy = 0.9 of the total rerouting energy. The projected steering vector r̃_q = P_Δk r_q replaces the original.

3. **Selective application to high-risk heads (Sec. 5.3):** A Rayleigh-quotient risk score R^(ℓ,h) = (r_q^T Σ_Δk r_q) / (||r_q||² + ε) ranks heads by their susceptibility to harmful rerouting. Projection is applied only to the top-k% (20%) of heads by this score. This avoids suppressing steering capacity in benign heads where rerouting is not an issue.

**Key insight distinguishing SKOP from full attention invariance:** Enforcing full invariance (projecting onto the null space of K_c) suppresses steering entirely, since rerouting is both the mechanism of harm and a carrier of steering signals. SKOP's selective approach preserves the steering-carrying rerouting while eliminating the utility-harming component.

## Results & Highlights

- **Steering-utility trade-off (Table 1, Fig. 4, LLaMA-3.1-8B):** SKOP achieves the best average rank (2.69) across all steering and utility benchmarks. It attains steering scores of 2.51 / 2.10 / 3.19 on Power / Wealth / Corrigibility and TQA score of 65.9, while achieving utility scores of 25.0 (IFBench), 65.0 (ARC), 65.2 (HellaSwag), 66.6 (GSM8K). The nearest competitor SADI ranks 3.38, and all vanilla steering methods show utility scores 50-75% below baseline.
- **Utility preservation:** Vanilla query-space steering induces large negative ΔM (focus-set mass drops) in 31% of (head, step) pairs at λ=4.0; SKOP reduces this by 3-10x across thresholds.
- **Steering strength robustness (Fig. 5):** Vanilla steering utility degrades monotonically and severely as λ increases. SKOP maintains strong utility preservation across all λ values tested, with only modest reductions in peak steering score.
- **Long-context results (Table 2, RULER NIAH):** At 16K tokens, DISCO-Q formatting compliance drops to 36.4% and retrieval accuracy degrades significantly beyond 4K tokens. SKOP maintains 71.6% formatting compliance at 16K and 88.2% retrieval accuracy, closely tracking the unsteered baseline (97.8%).
- **Calibration robustness:** As few as 250 calibration examples suffice, and single-domain calibration sets yield comparable trade-offs to mixed defaults, suggesting SKOP exploits stable geometric structure of the model rather than statistical properties of calibration data.
- **Results replicate on Gemma-2-9B-IT** with similar trends (reported in Appendix E.1).

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. While activation steering and attention-space steering are established, the identification of focus-to-tail attention rerouting as the specific causal mechanism of utility degradation — and the selective projection approach to address it — is a genuinely new contribution. The long-context steering result is presented as a first.

**Rigor:** Generally strong. The theoretical derivation (Eq. 6-7) clearly isolates the rerouting perturbation term. Empirical claims are backed by quantitative tables and figures across two model families and multiple baselines. Hyperparameter sensitivity is analyzed (Appendix E.3). The 250-sample calibration robustness claim is substantiated.

**Reproducibility:** Moderate concern. The calibration set construction, risk-head selection, and projection procedure are described in enough detail to reimplement, but code is not mentioned as being released. The paper references appendix sections (B.1, B.2, C.3, D.1, D.2) for full algorithmic and dataset details, which are present in the PDF. Experiments on Gemma are relegated to appendix, limiting direct verification from the main text.

**Significance:** High for the activation steering community. Practical inference-time LLM control without retraining is an important problem; reducing utility degradation 5-7x is a meaningful improvement. Long-context steering is a new capability.

**Red flags:**
- The method is explicitly limited to query-space steering with mean-difference vectors; extension to residual-stream steering is left as future work, limiting generality.
- Steering scores are evaluated using an LLM judge, which introduces potential reliability concerns (though this is standard practice in the field).
- The utility calibration set must be constructed upfront; while 250 samples is small, the assumption that a suitable utility dataset is available may not hold in all deployment scenarios.
- No theoretical guarantee on worst-case utility degradation; empirical results could vary with different model architectures or steering tasks not tested.

## Strengths

- Clear mechanistic story: the paper not only proposes a fix but explains *why* utility degrades (attention rerouting) and *why* full invariance fails (suppresses steering entirely), framing SKOP as a principled middle ground.
- Practical overhead is low: calibration requires only 250 examples with no gradient computation; projection adds minimal inference cost.
- Extensive evaluation: 7 baselines, 2 model families, 4 steering behaviors, 4 utility benchmarks, and long-context tasks.
- The low-rank structure of Σ_Δk (empirically observed) is a useful finding that both motivates the approach and limits computational cost.
- Results are consistent across Gemma-2 and LLaMA-3.1, suggesting the findings are not model-specific artifacts.

## Limitations

- **Scope restricted to query-space steering:** Residual-stream steering (CAA-style) cannot be cleanly decomposed into the single rerouting term that SKOP exploits. Extending the analysis to broader steering families is left as future work.
- **Slight reduction in peak steering score:** SKOP achieves marginally lower maximum steering scores than vanilla query-space methods; this tradeoff is favorable overall but may matter in safety-critical refusal steering where maximum efficacy is paramount.
- **Requires a utility calibration set:** Practitioners must supply a domain-appropriate set of utility examples; the paper shows cross-domain robustness but does not address cases where no utility proxy is available.
- **No code release mentioned:** Reproducibility depends on re-implementing from the paper's description.
- **LLM judge for steering evaluation:** Automated judge quality may vary; the paper uses the same judge as prior work but does not independently validate judge reliability.
- **Experiments on open-weight models only:** Applicability to proprietary or black-box models (where internal activations are not accessible) is not addressed.

## Relevance

本文对 LLM 激活引导（activation steering）领域有直接贡献，从机制层面解释了引导效果与模型实用能力之间的权衡，对研究 LLM 可解释性、推理时行为控制以及长上下文模型能力的研究者具有较高参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the SKOP framework extend to residual-stream steering, where the rerouting effect cannot be cleanly isolated to a single term? What approximation would be needed?
2. How does SKOP interact with grouped-query attention (GQA) used in many modern LLMs (briefly mentioned but not fully analyzed)?
3. Does the stable, low-rank structure of the key-difference subspace (Σ_Δk) vary meaningfully across model scales, and does this affect how many calibration samples are needed at larger scales?
4. Could SKOP's focus-preservation objective be incorporated directly into the optimization of steering vectors (end-to-end), rather than as a post-hoc projection step?
5. In safety-critical refusal steering scenarios, is the ~5% reduction in peak steering efficacy introduced by SKOP acceptable, or does it create exploitable gaps?
6. How does SKOP perform when the steering behavior conflicts with the model's default attention patterns (e.g., steering toward behaviors that require attending to tokens the model normally ignores)?
