Leverage Is Not Reach: A Control-Window Law for Single-Neuron Steering in Language Models

arXiv: 2606.19831

Date: 2026-06-19

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19831

一句话总结： 论文提出"控制窗口律"（control-window law），把单个 FFN 神经元干预对语言模型行为的可控性归约为一个无量纲坐标 cos θ_v，证明"杠杆（leverage，能否扰动内部状态）"与"触及（reach，是否产生连贯的行为改变）"是两回事；据此预算标定的剂量既能预测塌缩顺序、又能以前向-only 对比筛选发现梯度归因遗漏的行为门控（含越狱 pivot）。
TL;DR: A budget-normalized "control-window law" reduces single-neuron steering of LMs to one control coordinate, the alignment cos θ_v between residual stream and write direction, which follows a universal saturation curve; coherent control exists iff a behavior trigger lies below a weights-predictable collapse ceiling, and this same coordinate explains why gradient attribution anti-predicts control and why intervention success on refusal is typed (coherent bypass vs. strict-actionable reach) rather than scalar.

核心贡献

  • 单坐标控制律：把任何单神经元剂量效应归约到一个标量控制坐标 cos θ_v = g/√(1+g²)（g = t/B），在六种架构上与该普适饱和曲线匹配到小数点后两位；控制与塌缩是该坐标上的两个阈值，连贯控制当且仅当行为触发阈值低于塌缩天花板。
  • 可预测的塌缩天花板：天花板尺度可仅由权重 + 一次通用前向 pass 算出（B = ‖r_L‖/‖v‖，系数 m* 在 Qwen/Llama 系为 1.4–1.7，Gemma 近秩一残差为 0.31）；在 15 个留出神经元上预测天花板的平均绝对误差 0.14（bulk 层约 0.07），开/闭判决在 11/15 上成立（对比 10/15 多数基线）。
  • 闭窗口的三种机制：几何性闭合（天花板过低）、归一化限幅闭合（post-FFN norm 截断单神经元可达范围，Gemma-2/3）、深度解耦闭合（write 太晚无法传播）。
  • "杠杆≠触及"：局部梯度归因与真实控制反向相关——真正的控制器沿 readout 轴的投影几乎为零、一阶梯度过小；前向-only 对比筛选可在相同算力下恢复梯度排名最低的 4/4 控制器（梯度法 0/4）。
  • 类型化（typed）而非标量的可控性：在越狱这一最难的场景上区分"连贯绕过"与"严格可操作触及"；6 个被审计 Llama pivot 中仅 3 个真正达到可操作触及，且仅在更晚的 rollout horizon 上出现。
  • 测量纪律纠错：固定幅值 steering 会把可控神经元误报为破坏性；预算标定剂量 + 五条测量约定（签名分支、rollout horizon、脚本匹配连贯门、安全内容门）纠正记录。

Key Contributions

  • A control-window law on one coordinate. Any dose's effect reduces to a single control coordinate cos θ_v (alignment between residual and write direction) following the universal drive cos θ_v = g/√(1+g²), matched to two decimals across six architectures; control and collapse are thresholds on this coordinate, with coherent control iff cos θ_v^trig(τ) < cos θ_v^coll.
  • Prospective, weights-predictable ceiling + three closed-window mechanisms. Ceiling scale is derived from residual geometry (coherence budget B = ‖r_L‖/‖v‖ and family collapse coefficient m*), validated on 15 held-out neurons (MAE 0.14, ~0.07 in bulk layers, 11/15 committed verdicts). Closed windows arise from (i) geometric low ceiling, (ii) post-FFN normalization capping reach (Gemma-2/3), or (iii) decoupling at extreme depth.
  • Generality across behavior classes. The same coordinate governs language routing (Qwen3-4B L25/F347: English→Chinese), arithmetic-operator choice (Llama-3.1-8B L19/F13312: +→×), the published refusal pivot (Llama L11/F4258), and a task-framing gate (Qwen3.5-2B L8/F3742: locks responses to "mathematically impossible"); benign cases use deterministic, judge-free oracles.
  • Leverage is not reach. Local gradient attribution anti-predicts control because true controllers write off the readout axis (near-zero first-order gradient); a forward-only contrastive screen recovers controllers the gradient ranks lowest (4/4 vs 0/4 on matched compute).
  • Typed, not scalar, intervention success on refusal. Coherent bypass (fluent, on-task, non-refusal) separates from strict-actionable reach (operationally specific content); only 3/6 audited Llama pivots show genuine actionable reach and only at later rollout horizons.
  • A measurement discipline that corrects the record. Budget-calibrated dosing rescues controllable neurons that fixed-magnitude steering mislabels destructive, and unifies super weights, refusal neurons, and arithmetic/framing gates as points on one window.

Methodology

  • Setting. Intervention injects a scalar multiple t of a neuron's down-projection column v into the residual at layer L: r(t) = r_L + t·v. Because the write direction is empirically near-orthogonal to the residual, the normalized readout discards magnitude and the only surviving quantity is the rotation, summarized by cos θ_v = cos ∠(r_L + tv, v̂).
  • Universal drive. The dose reduces to the dimensionless coupling g = t·‖v‖/‖r_L‖ = t/B, where B (the coherence budget) is the single-neuron FFN-to-skip ratio; the drive cos θ_v = g/√(1+g²) holds to two decimals across six architectures (Qwen2.5/3, Qwen3.5-2B, Llama-3.1-8B, Gemma-2/3, etc.), confirming both orthogonality and linear-add assumptions.
  • Collapse ceiling. Output degenerates at t = m·B; the ceiling cos θ_v^coll = m/√(1+m²) with m ∈ [1.37, 1.65] for Qwen/Llama and ≈0.31 for the near-rank-one Gemma residual. m* is set by the residual's participation ratio (effective dimensionality) with weak depth dependence — a small family-level correction, not a per-neuron fit.
  • Trigger. Behavior flips at cos θ_v^trig(τ), a rollout-time boundary: ~0.3 for token-local mode switches, ~0.45 for refusal, ~0.6 for task-framing. Predicted only from behavior class + horizon, not weights (an explicit asymmetry acknowledged as open).
  • Discovery. Candidate controllers found by forward-only contrastive activation ranking on a small prompt set, then verified by dosing — no gradients or activation patching. Reuses published anchors (Yu et al., Kazemi et al., Feucht et al.).
  • Measurement contract (5 conventions). Budgeted dose, signed controlling branch, behavior-specific rollout horizon, script-matched coherence gate (distinct-bigram / character diversity), and for safety a content gate separating non-refusal from strict-actionable reach.
  • Falsifier (pre-registered). Law falsified by a neuron whose trigger < predicted ceiling on the controlling branch under a budgeted dose ladder yet shows no coherent control, or whose output collapses far below the family/depth-calibrated ceiling. Across 15 held-out neurons none met the falsifier.

Results & Highlights

  • Drive universality: cos θ_v = g/√(1+g²) matched to two decimal places across six architectures and full dose sweeps; baseline alignment ≈0 except for a few high-activation neurons (L11/F4258 at 0.36, L20/F1378 at 0.24).
  • Prospective ceiling prediction: MAE 0.14 overall (95% CI 0.09–0.23), ~0.07 within the network bulk (relative depth < 0.8); 11/15 committed open-or-closed verdicts correct vs a 10/15 majority baseline.
  • Concrete gates surfaced: L25/F347 (Qwen3-4B, EN→CN), L19/F13312 (Llama-3.1-8B, +→×), L11/F4258 (Llama-3.1-8B, refusal), L8/F3742 (Qwen3.5-2B, "mathematically impossible" framing).
  • Attribution anti-correlation: forward-only contrastive screen recovers 4/4 controllers that gradient ranks lowest; gradient-ranked neurons are collapse-prone.
  • Refusal typed: of 6 audited Llama pivots, only 3 show genuine strict-actionable reach, and only at later rollout horizons; coherent bypass (e.g., L15/F9635: 0.62 bypass vs 0.00 actionable) does not imply actionable reach — non-overlapping CIs separate e.g. L10/F1084 (0.75 actionable) from L15/F9635 (0.17).
  • Closed-window explanations: geometric close, drive-capped close under post-FFN norm (Gemma-2/3), decoupled close at extreme depth — three failure modes, not violations.
  • Super weights: behave as scale-setters / prompt-affordance amplifiers, not single-neuron gates (ablation collapses the model; selects no behavior).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High and specific. The framing of single-neuron steering as a pharmacological dose-response with a predictable collapse ceiling, and the leverage-vs-reach distinction that inverts gradient attribution, are genuinely new relative to activation-steering and representation-engineering literature. Unifying super weights, refusal neurons, arithmetic gates, and framing gates on one coordinate is a clean abstraction.
  • Rigor: Strong on the ceiling (weights-derived, MAE quantified with CIs, pre-registered falsifier, branch-resolved signed windows to avoid spuriously low ceilings). Weaker and self-admittedly so on the trigger: "a fully first-principles trigger value remains open," and trigger values are measured not predicted from weights — the law is asymmetric (constrains collapse tightly, control loosely).
  • Reproducibility: Mixed. Models are public (Qwen2.5/3, Llama-3.1-8B, Gemma-2/3) and neuron naming convention (L/F index) is precise; the contrastive discovery primitive is forward-only and cheap. However, the refusal/safety content gate uses an LLM judge with "register-aware" scoring whose bar is not fully pinned down in the main text (Appendix E), and the curated controller set is hand-selected to state and test the law rather than sampled.
  • Significance: Substantial for mechanistic interpretability and safety auditing. The typed bypass-vs-actionable-reach finding is a concrete, falsifiable correction to naive "single neuron = jailbreak" claims, and the forward-only controller screen is a practical recipe.
  • Red flags / concerns:
  • Sample size. 15 held-out neurons is small; 11/15 verdicts is only barely above the 10/15 majority baseline — the prospective edge is thin and the paper itself notes finer rate differences (0.88 vs 0.75 actionable) fall within CIs.
  • Selection. Controllers are "curated ... chosen to state and test the law," introducing potential selection bias; model-scale validation is deferred to a companion paper not included here.
  • Trigger theory gap. The more safety-relevant edge (trigger) is not predicted from first principles, so the law's predictive power on the control side is the weakest link exactly where it matters most (refusal).
  • Single author, no code/data link in abstract. A claim of this breadth normally benefits from independent replication; no public repo is referenced in the front matter.
  • Closed-window "decoupled close" exception is recorded by pre-registered criterion rather than an open-window failure — defensible, but it does carve out an unfalsifiable escape hatch for neurons that simply do nothing.

Strengths

  • Clean, falsifiable one-coordinate theory with a pre-registered falsifier and quantified MAE with confidence intervals.
  • Universality of the drive curve across six architectures is a striking, easily-checked regularity.
  • The leverage-vs-reach inversion of gradient attribution is both theoretically motivated and constructively useful (4/4 vs 0/4 controller recovery).
  • Typed refusal analysis (bypass ≠ actionable reach) is a meaningful safety contribution that avoids the common overclaim that any single-neuron flip is a jailbreak.
  • Honest about the law's tautology risk and the open trigger-theory problem.

Limitations

  • Small held-out set (15 neurons); prospective edge over majority baseline is narrow.
  • Trigger is measured, not weights-predicted — the law is asymmetric and weaker on the safety-critical edge.
  • Curated controller selection risks bias; model-scale validation deferred to an unreleased companion paper.
  • Safety content gate depends on an LLM judge whose calibration lives mostly in the appendix.
  • Post-FFN-norm equivalence break (Gemma) is handled as a special case, limiting universality claims for that architecture family.
  • Single author; no cited public code release in the front matter.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can the trigger cos θ_v^trig(τ) be predicted from weights/curvature rather than measured, closing the acknowledged asymmetry and making the law fully first-principles?
  • Does the control-window law hold at model scale (thousands of neurons) and under compositional multi-neuron dosing (K>1), where the single-write orthogonality assumption may break?
  • How stable is the coherence budget B and collapse coefficient m* across training checkpoints — could drift in B serve as a training-stability / SDC observable?
  • Is the gradient-attribution anti-prediction specific to first-order gradients, or do second-order (curvature β) coefficients recover the true controllers?
  • Does the typed bypass-vs-actionable-reach separation generalize beyond Llama-3.1-8B to frontier closed models where single-neuron audits are harder?
  • Could the forward-only contrastive screen be turned into an automated safety audit that flags single-neuron jailbreak vulnerabilities pre-deployment?
