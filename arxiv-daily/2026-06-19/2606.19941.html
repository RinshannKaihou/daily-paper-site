Compositionality Emerges in a Narrow Depth-Connectivity Regime: Architecture Constraints and Solution Manifolds

arXiv: 2606.19941

Date: 2026-06-19

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19941

一句话总结： 该论文发现神经网络的组合性内部结构只在一个狭窄的"连接-深度甜区"中涌现，提出了相似性剪枝（SP）与基于 PNG 压缩率的深度预测器来定位该区域，并用组合稀疏性、体积比与 Welch 干扰界的理论解释为何组合性解只在该甜区可达。
TL;DR: Compositionality in MLPs is not a generic outcome of SGD; it only emerges in a narrow sweet-spot regime along the connectivity axis (which specific sparse edges survive, not the sparsity level) and the depth axis (a target-dependent optimal depth L*). The authors release EMC2-Bench, propose Similarity-based Pruning (SP) and a PNG-compression-ratio depth predictor to find the regime, and explain it via a volume-ratio argument (Prop. 5.1–5.2) plus a Welch-bound feature-interference analysis.

核心贡献

  • 将"涌现式组合性"形式化为一个结构可达性问题：组合性是深度与连接性交互决定的，而非任一单独因素决定。
  • 发布 EMC2-Bench：一个基于权重扰动 + VLM 判定的组合性评测套件，对每个非零权重施加扰动 δ 并询问 VLM 输出图像是否仍为目标概念，定义组合性分数为通过语义不变性测试的权重占比。
  • 提出 相似性剪枝（SP）：在神经元层级逐层剪枝，去除同层内余弦相似度高于阈值 τ 的冗余神经元，并用迭代"剪枝-再训练"恢复子网络。在相同参数预算下，SP 是唯一获得非零组合性分数的方法（Lottery Ticket、Wanda、LLM-Pruner 均为 0）。
  • 提出 目标复杂度深度预测器：用目标图像的 PNG 压缩率作为图像复杂度的轻量代理，线性拟合预测最优组合性深度，并在 ±6 层容差带内定位甜区。
  • 给出理论框架：基于组合稀疏性（compositional sparsity）、体积比论证（Prop. 5.1 宽度稀释、Prop. 5.2 深度非单调性）和特征干扰界（Welch bound），证明组合性解的体积比 ρ(W,L) 在 (W_min, L*) 角点处达峰、向各方向衰减。
  • 通过三条互补机制解释 SGD 为何能落入甜区：强制唯一性（forced uniqueness，ρ→1）、盆地平坦性（compositional Hessian trace ≤ FER Hessian trace）、梯度信号清晰度。

Key Contributions

  • Reframes emergent compositionality as a structural reachability problem jointly governed by depth and connectivity, mapped empirically.
  • EMC2-Bench: a perturbation-based evaluation suite that queries a calibrated VLM under weight sweeping to quantify compositionality as the fraction of non-zero weights whose perturbation preserves target semantics.
  • Similarity-based Pruning (SP): a neuron-level iterative prune-retrain rule removing within-layer redundant neurons (cosine similarity > τ). SP is the only method recovering non-zero compositional scores; LTH, Wanda, and LLM-Pruner all score 0 at matched parameter counts.
  • Target-complexity depth predictor: uses the PNG compression ratio of the target image as a lightweight complexity proxy, linearly fitted to the empirically optimal depth, narrowing depth search to a ±6-layer band.
  • Theory: volume-ratio argument (Prop. 5.1 width dilution ρ = O(W^{-P(L-1)}); Prop. 5.2 depth non-monotonicity peaking at L* = ⌈log2(P+1)⌉) plus a Welch-bound feature-interference reduction showing compositional sparsity lowers the unavoidable interference barrier for monosemanticity.

Methodology

Setting. The authors restrict the study to fully-inspectable MLPs that take pixel-coordinate inputs (x, y, d=x²+y², 1) and predict (h, s, v), converted to RGB. The reference point of comparison is Picbreeder/NEAT CPPN networks (which yield unified factored representations, UFR) versus the same networks ported to dense MLPs and retrained by SGD (which yield fractured entangled representations, FER).

Measurement (EMC2-Bench). For every non-zero weight w, create w' = w + δ, run the network, and query a VLM judge ("Does the image clearly illustrate ?"). The compositional score is N_comp / N_{≠0}, the fraction of weights whose perturbation still yields an affirmative VLM judgment.

Similarity-based Pruning (SP). Per layer, compute pairwise cosine similarity of neuron feature maps; build redundancy pairs P_i = {(k,j) : S_{k,p(k)} > τ} where p(k) is the most similar neuron to k; randomly drop one of each pair by zeroing both incoming and outgoing weights; fine-tune without re-initialization; repeat.

Depth predictor. PNG compression ratio CR(x) = Size_PNG / Size_raw serves as a complexity heuristic; a linear regressor on Picbreeder artifacts predicts the optimal depth for novel targets, then a small local sweep refines it.

Theory. Adopts the compositional-sparsity framework (Dauphin/Poggio et al. 2025). Defines compositional volume ratio ρ(W,L) = vol(M^comp_ε) / vol(M_ε) and proves it concentrates at (W_min, L*) and decays in every direction. Adds three SGD-bias mechanisms (forced uniqueness at the sweet spot, basin flatness via Hessian trace, gradient clarity) and a Welch-bound argument that compositional sparsity reduces unavoidable feature interference, linking sparsity to geometric monosemanticity.

Results & Highlights

  • Connectivity matters qualitatively, not quantitatively. At identical parameter budgets, SP recovers non-zero compositional scores (e.g., Skull 0.05→0.18, Butterfly 0.07→0.28, Apple 0.07→0.24 as optimizer quality improves from Adam to Muon with NS step=20), while Lottery Ticket / Wanda / LLM-Pruner all score exactly 0.
  • Optimizer matters. Muon (more Newton-Schulz steps) achieves lower training loss and correspondingly higher compositional scores than Adam at unchanged capacity — better minima produce stronger compositionality.
  • Depth is non-monotonic and target-dependent. Compositional score vs depth offset ∆d ∈ {−3,…,3} shows a clear peak at ∆d=0 that drops sharply on both sides, and the peak location differs across artifacts (skull, butterfly, apple, muon).
  • Out-of-domain transfer. SP + heuristic depth search produces monosemantic intermediate feature maps with vertical symmetry, meaningful weight-sweeping output changes, and a depth-ablation peak near the predicted depth, on images whose underlying decomposition is unknown a priori.
  • Multi-target failure. Fitting three targets in a single MLP with identical pruning destroys monosemanticity, consistent with Prop. 5.2's prediction that no single (W,L) maximizes ρ across heterogeneous L*_i.
  • Spatial compositionality is an open boundary. ρ collapses by orders of magnitude as the number of primitives P grows, explaining why single-object compositionality does not extend to multi-object scenes.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. Reframing compositionality as an architecture-regime (depth × connectivity) reachability problem, with a concrete volume-ratio characterization of why the regime is narrow, is a genuine conceptual advance over the prior FER literature which only documented the failure.
  • Rigor: Moderate-to-good. The two propositions are stated cleanly and make non-vacuous predictions (ρ concentrates at (W_min, L*), decays polynomially in width and exponentially in excess depth). However, the width bound's denominator W^{PL} is acknowledged as a coarse upper bound (quotienting by permutation symmetry SW^L would tighten it but is not done), and the three SGD-bias "mechanisms" decrease in formal strength — only Mechanism 1 is a near-proof; Mechanisms 2–3 are qualitative with Hessian-trace inequality stated without derivation in the main text.
  • Reproducibility: Mixed. EMC2-Bench relies on a "calibrated VLM judge," whose identity, calibration procedure, and judge-prompt sensitivity are not pinned down in the abstract/intro and could be a major reproducibility hazard. SP and the depth predictor are fully specified and easy to reimplement.
  • Significance: Potentially high for mechanistic interpretability and architecture search if the narrow-regime claim generalizes; currently limited to tiny MLPs on CPPN-style image targets.
  • Red flags:
  • VLM-as-judge evaluation introduces an uncontrolled, likely noisy measurement layer; the authors concede "this metric relies on a VLM evaluator and is not a perfect measure of semantic invariance."
  • Scope is restricted to MLPs; the authors explicitly exclude CNNs and Transformers because "the existence and characterization of compositional solutions in those architectures remain unclear," so claims do not directly transfer to LLMs.
  • Picbreeder reference artifacts are themselves human-curated synthetic outcomes, which the authors flag, weakening the "ground truth compositional" baseline.
  • Absolute compositional scores are low even for SP (0.05–0.28), only dramatic relative to baselines at exactly 0.
  • Depth predictor has "substantial variance" even under a refit line, and needs a ±6-layer correction band — practical but imprecise.

Strengths

  • Couples a clean empirical intervention (SP, depth sweep) with a non-vacuous theoretical explanation grounded in approximation theory.
  • EMC2-Bench provides a concrete, if imperfect, operational definition of "compositionality" that many prior papers lacked.
  • Honest about limitations: explicitly notes VLM-judge imperfection, predictor noise, and the multi-target / spatial-compositionality failure modes.
  • Actionable: SP and the PNG-ratio predictor are simple to apply and not tied to Picbreeder.

Limitations

  • MLP-only; no Transformer/CNN/LLM experiments, so the headline claim is confined to small CPPN-style image-synthesis networks.
  • Evaluation depends on an uncharacterized VLM judge; robustness of the compositional score to judge choice is not established in the main text.
  • Absolute scores are low; the contrast with baselines at 0 may partly reflect that baselines were not tuned for this unusual objective.
  • Theory assumes a balanced binary composition tree and bounded arity; realism of these assumptions for natural tasks is not argued.
  • The volume-ratio bound is loose (ignores permutation symmetry), so the predicted sharpness of the sweet spot is qualitative rather than tight.

Relevance

与"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"以及"ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations"方向高度契合：它从表示内部结构与组合稀疏性的几何角度，定量解释了单语义/组合性表示何时可达，并通过 Hessian 平坦性与特征干扰界给出了为何 SGD 在某些架构下"静默地"收敛到破碎解的机制，对可解释性与稳健泛化的研究有直接启发意义。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How sensitive is the EMC2-Bench compositional score to the choice and calibration of the VLM judge? Would a human study or multiple judges agree?
  • Does the narrow (W_min, L*) sweet spot persist for Transformer attention heads or SAE latents at LLM scale, or is it an artifact of CPPN-style MLPs?
  • Can the volume-ratio bound be tightened by quotienting the layerwise permutation symmetry S_{W^L}, and would a tighter bound sharpen the predicted regime width?
  • The depth predictor (PNG compression ratio) is a strikingly simple proxy — does it survive on natural images with backgrounds, or only on Picbreeder-style synthetic targets?
  • Can the three SGD-bias mechanisms (forced uniqueness, basin flatness, gradient clarity) be tested independently — e.g., does explicitly flattening the Hessian recover compositionality outside the sweet spot?
  • Does the multi-target failure (single network, heterogeneous L*_i) predict specific circuits of "routing through different effective depths" that could be observed in real LLMs?
  • For spatial compositionality, the authors suggest multi-view/depth/motion signals as inductive biases — what is the minimal such signal that restores ρ to a usable level?
