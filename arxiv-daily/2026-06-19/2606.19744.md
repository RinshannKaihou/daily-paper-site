> **一句话总结：** 本文系统研究了顺序应用 DPO（直接偏好优化）对先前已学偏好目标的影响，发现在 Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct + LoRA 设置下并不存在统一的"遗忘"模式——后训练目标对先前偏好的影响从部分退化、保持稳定、配对级重分布到正向迁移都可能发生，取决于目标间的关系、信号强度和训练顺序；而机制性诊断（梯度余弦与适配器位移）显示 Stage-2 梯度与适配器更新都与前一目标近正交，说明"梯度直接对抗"并非遗忘的主因。

> **TL;DR:** A systematic empirical study of sequential Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct with LoRA across four preference settings (HH-RLHF, HelpSteer2, PKU-SafeRLHF, UltraFeedback) shows that later training does NOT uniformly degrade earlier preferences — effects range from partial degradation to stability, pair-level redistribution, or positive transfer, depending on objective relationship, signal strength, and training order. Stage-2 gradients are near-orthogonal (cosine ≈ 0, angle ≈ 90°) to the previous objective and adapter movement is sideways (4.5–16.1% of Stage-1 norm), suggesting gradient opposition is NOT the primary driver of forgetting.

## 核心贡献

- 提供了顺序 DPO 对齐的系统实证研究，覆盖四种目标关系设置（分布冲突、多属性交互、强安全信号、兼容目标），证明偏好变化谱系从双向遗忘到正向迁移，由目标关系和信号强度而非顺序训练本身决定。
- 引入偏好优化中的配对级策略边际分析（pair-level policy-margin analysis），通过四分位分解（quartile decomposition）跟踪逐样本边际变化，揭示了被聚合奖励边际和相对偏好准确率掩盖的异质重分布。
- 提供基于梯度冲突和适配器运动的机制性诊断：在所有 8 个排序中，偏好变化均发生在更新方向近正交的情况下，提示间接机制（漂移、信号失衡、间接参数运动）而非直接梯度对抗。
- 给出对顺序对齐流水线的设计启示：未来方法应显式考虑目标兼容性与信号强度，而非假设后训练目标均匀影响先前偏好。

## Key Contributions

1. **Systematic empirical study of sequential alignment via DPO** across four preference settings, showing preference change spans a spectrum from bidirectional forgetting to positive transfer determined by objective relationship and signal strength — not by sequential training alone.
2. **Pair-level policy-margin analysis** for preference optimization, tracking per-example margin changes across checkpoints with quartile decomposition, revealing heterogeneous redistribution hidden by aggregate reward margins and relative preference accuracy.
3. **Mechanistic diagnostics** via gradient-conflict cosine similarity and LoRA adapter movement, showing preference changes occur despite near-orthogonal update directions across all studied settings (gradient cosine −0.007 to +0.028; adapter directional cosine ≈ 0). This suggests indirect mechanisms rather than direct gradient opposition — interpreted as suggestive, since it is restricted to LoRA-parameter space.

## Methodology

- **Base model & adaptation:** Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (≈8.1B params), frozen base weights with LoRA adapters (rank r=32, α=128, dropout 0.05, applied to q/k/v/o/up/down/gate projections; 83.9M trainable params ≈ 1.03%). Run in bfloat16 with gradient checkpointing on a single 48GB GPU. LoRA enables exact per-stage adapter checkpointing.
- **Training objective:** DPO (Rafailov et al., 2023). In sequential training each stage resumes from the previous adapter checkpoint, and the Stage-2 reference policy is the final Stage-1 adapter (mirrors practical sequential fine-tuning).
- **Evaluation reference is fixed at the base model** throughout — distinct from the shifting training reference — ensuring cross-stage margin comparisons are on a consistent scale.
- **Four preference settings / datasets (both orderings A→B and B→A in each):**
  - *Distributional conflict* — HH-RLHF (helpful-base vs harmless-base). Safety–helpfulness shift; <1% prompt overlap (0.86%), no flipped-label pairs → distributional rather than adversarial conflict.
  - *Multi-attribute interaction* — HelpSteer2 (verbosity vs coherence). Fine-grained attributes that can trade off.
  - *Strong safety signal* — PKU-SafeRLHF (harmless vs helpful) where harmlessness provides a much stronger signal.
  - *Compatible objectives* — UltraFeedback (instruction-following vs honesty). Overlapping response-quality behaviours.
- **Aggregate metrics:**
  - *DPO-relative reward margin* r_θ = β[Δ_θ − Δ_ref] (mean over pairs); positive ⇒ policy widens chosen-over-rejected gap vs reference.
  - *Relative preference accuracy* = (1/|D|) Σ [1[r_i>0] + ½·1[r_i=0]]; 0.5 is the neutral baseline.
- **Pair-level analysis:** length-normalised policy margin m_i = log π_θ(y_w|x) − log π_θ(y_l|x) (mean per-token log-prob difference). Per-pair change Δ_i = m_i^(1) − m_i^(2); positive ⇒ degradation, negative ⇒ improvement.
- **Quartile decomposition:** sort pairs by Stage-1 margin into 4 groups; Q1 = hard/low-confidence, Q4 = easy/high-confidence; report mean margin change per quartile to localise where change concentrates.
- **Mechanistic diagnostics:**
  - *Gradient cosine similarity* between live Stage-2 DPO gradient and a diagnostic Stage-1-objective gradient on a fixed reference batch (over trainable LoRA params); −1 = opposition, 0 = orthogonality, +1 = alignment.
  - *Adapter movement* = ‖S2 − S1‖/‖S1‖ (relative displacement in raw LoRA params) and cos(S1, S2 − S1) (directional alignment). Used as supporting evidence because raw LoRA space is not invariant to rescaling.
- Evaluation is deterministic (log-probabilities over fixed chosen/rejected responses, not generations).

## Results & Highlights

- **Aggregate (Table 1) — no uniform catastrophic forgetting:**
  - *HH-RLHF:* measurable partial degradation. harmless→helpful: harmless margin 0.925 → 0.707 (−23.6%), helpful accuracy recovers 38.1% → 57.8%. helpful→harmless: helpful margin 0.731 → 0.228 (−68.8%), harmless accuracy recovers 41.1% → 59.7%. Prior objective stays above neutral baseline (61.4%, 55.4%) → partial, not catastrophic.
  - *HelpSteer2:* smaller changes; verbosity→coherence: verbosity 4.048 → 3.776 (−6.7%); coherence→verbosity: coherence 0.925 → 0.720 (−22.2%). Verbosity produces much larger margins than coherence.
  - *PKU-SafeRLHF:* harmless produces very large margins (Stage-1 14.071 vs 0.925 in HH-RLHF). harmless does NOT degrade after helpful training (14.071 → 16.930, accuracy 95.9%); in helpful→harmless the helpful accuracy drops 67.3% → 57.4% (−9.9pp) even though DPO-relative margin rises 0.622 → 1.230 (margin and accuracy move oppositely).
  - *UltraFeedback:* no aggregate forgetting in either ordering — strong positive transfer. IF 8.195 → 15.114, honesty reaches 13.572; honesty 5.331 → 13.114, IF reaches 15.745. After Stage-1 IF training alone, honesty margin is already 7.471 (84.3% accuracy), indicating substantial baseline compatibility.
- **Pair-level (Table 2) — heterogeneous redistribution:** mean Δ hides substantial heterogeneity. E.g., HH-RLHF helpful→harmless mean Δ +0.042 with only 39.3% pairs improving (broad degradation); UltraFeedback IF→honesty mean Δ −0.314 with 80.8% improving.
- **Quartile decomposition (Figure 2) — selective, not uniform:**
  - HH-RLHF helpful→harmless: degradation concentrated in Q4 (easy/high-confidence pairs).
  - PKU-SafeRLHF harmless→helpful: all quartiles improve, monotonically growing Q1→Q4 (high-confidence harmless pairs reinforced).
  - UltraFeedback: improvement concentrated in Q4 for both orderings (IF→honesty Q3 −0.240, Q4 −0.805; honesty→IF Q3 −0.234, Q4 −1.027) — opposite direction to HH-RLHF helpful→harmless where Q4 loses most.
- **Mechanistic (Table 3) — near-orthogonality throughout:** gradient cosine −0.007 to +0.028 (angles 88.4°–90.4°); no systematic opposition. Adapter relative movement only 4.5–16.1% of Stage-1 norm, with directional cosine ≈ 0 (θ ≈ 90°) → later training moves "sideways" in LoRA space, never reverses Stage-1 adapter. Largest cosine +0.028 (88.4°) appears in PKU helpful→harmless, the same run where helpful margin rises → slight alignment, not opposition. Evidence is more consistent with distributional drift, signal imbalance, or indirect parameter movement than direct gradient opposition.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate):** The "beyond uniform forgetting" framing is a useful conceptual reframing, and the pair-level/quartile analysis for *preference* optimisation (as opposed to multiple-choice knowledge benchmarks) is a genuinely finer evaluation granularity. The general observation that sequential fine-tuning causes non-uniform interference is well established in continual learning, so the conceptual novelty is incremental; the most novel element is the mechanistic orthogonality finding.
- **Rigour (Good, with caveats):** Fixed base-model evaluation reference, both orderings per setting, deterministic log-prob evaluation, and three complementary diagnostic levels (aggregate / pair-level / mechanistic) are methodologically sound. However, single base model (Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct) and single adaptation method (LoRA) limit generalisation. The authors explicitly caveat the mechanistic evidence as "suggestive rather than causal" because (a) gradients are measured only over LoRA params on a fixed reference batch, and (b) raw LoRA parameter space is not rescaling-invariant — a candid and important limitation.
- **Reproducibility (Good):** Standard open model, explicit LoRA config (r=32, α=128, dropout 0.05, target projections), public datasets, deterministic log-prob evaluation. Hyperparameters are reported in Appendix A. With held-out eval pairs and orderings specified, reproduction is feasible; full code release not stated in the body.
- **Significance (Moderate):** Practically useful for practitioners building multi-objective alignment pipelines and for training-stability monitoring. The orthogonality result is intellectually interesting and could reframe how the community thinks about interference. It is an analysis paper, not a method paper, so direct capability impact is limited.
- **Red flags / uncertainties:**
  - Single model + single adapter method — LoRA's low-rank subspace may itself induce the orthogonality (a confound the authors acknowledge).
  - Four hand-constructed settings may not represent the full diversity of real-world multi-objective alignment.
  - Sample sizes for pair-level analysis vary (422–1000 pairs), and the abstract/body do not report seeds or confidence intervals, so statistical significance of smaller Δ values is unclear.
  - "Strong safety signal" and "compatible objectives" show near-zero forgetting, which somewhat narrows the regimes where the central thesis (non-uniform change) is most dramatic.

## Strengths

- Goes beyond aggregate win-rate/accuracy to pair-level and quartile analysis — genuinely more informative evaluation granularity for preference data.
- Mechanistic diagnostic directly tests and challenges the intuitive "competing gradients" explanation for forgetting.
- Fixed base-model evaluation reference is the right methodological choice for cross-stage comparability.
- Candid about limitations (LoRA-only, suggestive-not-causal evidence, four settings only).
- Practical, actionable guidance for sequential alignment pipeline design.

## Limitations

- Single base model (Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct) and single adaptation method (LoRA); generalisation to full fine-tuning, other model families/scales, and other alignment objectives (KTO, IPO, SimPO, PPO-RLHF) untested.
- Four hand-constructed preference settings may not capture the diversity of real-world multi-objective alignment.
- LoRA's low-rank constraint may itself produce the near-orthogonality, confounding the mechanistic conclusion.
- No seeds, variance, or significance reporting for the smaller Δ values.
- Mechanistic gradient analysis uses a single fixed reference batch and only LoRA params — does not capture full-model or activation-level dynamics.

## Relevance

与"LLM 训练稳定性与可观测性"及"LLM 隐藏状态与可解释性"方向高度相关：该研究通过配对级边际、四分位分解与梯度/适配器正交性诊断，揭示了顺序对齐训练中非均匀干扰的细粒度机制，为多目标对齐流水线的训练稳定性监控（用 policy margin 作为实时信号）以及表示/参数层面的失效模式分析提供了具体的实证与方法参考。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the near-orthogonality findings hold for full fine-tuning, or are they partly an artefact of LoRA's low-rank adapter subspace? A controlled full-FT vs. LoRA comparison at matched capacity is the cleanest test.
- How do results generalise across model scales (1B → 70B) and families beyond Llama-3? Larger models may have richer gradient geometry.
- Would other alignment objectives (KTO, IPO, SimPO, PPO-RLHF) show the same non-uniform forgetting patterns when applied sequentially?
- Can pair-level policy margins serve as a real-time training-stability monitoring signal to detect interference during sequential alignment?
- Is there a principled objective-ordering rule (given compatibility and signal strength) that minimises destructive interference — and does gradient orthogonality predict safe ordering?
- The PKU-SafeRLHF case where margin and accuracy move oppositely: which is the more faithful indicator of preference retention, and when should practitioners distrust aggregate margins?
- Could activation-level / representational diagnostics (not just parameter-space) reveal interference that LoRA-gradient orthogonality misses?
