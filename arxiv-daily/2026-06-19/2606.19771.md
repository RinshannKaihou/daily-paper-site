> **一句话总结：** 提出 ICT（Independent Combinatorial Tokens）框架，用 token logit 分布与组内平均分布的 Jensen-Shannon 散度选出最具分布独特性的前 10% token 进行稀疏梯度更新，理论上同时调节 Shannon 熵与二阶 Rényi 熵以避免熵塌缩/爆炸，在 Qwen2.5（0.5B/1.5B/7B）上较 GRPO、20-Entropy、STAPO 平均 pass@4 提升 4.37%/4.98%/4.38%，单基准最高增益达 14.9%。

> **TL;DR:** ICT reformulates RLVR from scalar entropy control to token-level distributional selection: it scores each token by Jensen-Shannon divergence of its logit distribution from the group-average distribution and updates only the top 10% most distributionally unique tokens, simultaneously reducing Shannon entropy and bounding second-order Rényi entropy to prevent both entropy collapse and explosion, yielding consistent pass@4 gains over GRPO, 20-Entropy, and STAPO on Qwen2.5 0.5B/1.5B/7B across seven benchmarks.

## 核心贡献

- 诊断出 RLVR 中基于 Shannon 熵控制的二分矛盾：均匀 token 更新（GRPO）导致熵塌缩与过早收敛，而仅在高熵 token 上更新（20-Entropy 类方法）导致熵爆炸与无方向探索。论文指出 Shannon 熵只是不确定性的标量代理，分布结构不同也可能有相同 Shannon 熵（Figure 1）。
- 提出 Independent Combinatorial Tokens（ICT）框架，将优化焦点从标量不确定性转移到 token logits 的分布性质：用 Jensen-Shannon 散度衡量每个 token 的 logit 分布相对"组内平均分布"Pavg 的偏离，选出分布独特性最强的 token 作为推理链上的关键分支点。
- 给出基于二阶 Rényi 熵（碰撞概率/策略纯度 β(π)）与 Shannon 熵的双重理论分析：推导出更新单个 token 时 ΔH2 ≈ -2Δθ·π(a*)·(π(a*)/β(π) - 1)，证明存在熵分叉——更新 π(a)>β(π) 的高置信 token 使熵下降（塌缩区），更新 π(a)<β(π) 的低置信 token 使熵上升（爆炸区）；而独特 token 恰好处于两区交界，高/低熵 token 在选中的独特 token 中比例约为 1:1（实验验证 GSM8K 1.03、MATH 0.99）。
- 提出 Sparse-GRPO 目标，仅在 mask M 限定的独特 token 上做归一化更新；配合 warm-up 机制（前 Θ 步全 token 更新，之后开启稀疏选择器）。
- 实证：仅在 Qwen2.5（0.5B/1.5B/7B）上更新前 10% 独特 token，跨 7 个基准（GSM8K、Math500、MMLU-Stem、GPQA、AIME23/24/25）相对 GRPO/20-Entropy/STAPO 平均 pass@4 提升 4.37%/4.98%/4.38%，单基准最高增益 14.9%（MMLU-Stem 0.5B P@4：65.40 vs 20-Entropy 50.50）。该收益随模型规模增大而增强。

## Key Contributions

- Diagnoses the dichotomy in RLVR entropy control: uniform token updates (GRPO) cause entropy collapse and premature convergence, while high-entropy-only updates (20-Entropy style) cause entropy explosion and directionless exploration. Argues that Shannon entropy is merely a scalar proxy for uncertainty — distinct distributions can share identical Shannon entropy (Figure 1).
- Introduces the Independent Combinatorial Tokens (ICT) framework, shifting optimization from scalar uncertainty to the distributional structure of token logits: it uses Jensen-Shannon divergence between each token's logit distribution and the group-average distribution Pavg to identify distributionally unique tokens as critical branching points.
- Provides a dual-entropy theory grounded in second-order Rényi entropy (collision probability / strategy purity β(π)) and Shannon entropy. Derives the entropy-bifurcation result ΔH2 ≈ -2Δθ·π(a*)·(π(a*)/β(π) − 1): updates with π(a)>β(π) decrease entropy (collapse regime), updates with π(a)<β(π) increase entropy (explosion regime), while unique tokens sit at the boundary. Empirically verifies ~1:1 high-/low-entropy ratio among selected unique tokens (GSM8K 1.03, MATH 0.99).
- Proposes the Sparse-GRPO objective, normalizing updates only over the active mask of unique tokens, plus a warm-up mechanism (full-token updates for the first Θ steps, then the sparse selector activates).
- Empirically, updating only the top 10% of unique tokens on Qwen2.5 (0.5B/1.5B/7B) yields average pass@4 improvements of 4.37%/4.98%/4.38% over GRPO/20-Entropy/STAPO across seven benchmarks (GSM8K, Math500, MMLU-Stem, GPQA, AIME23/24/25), with a maximum single-benchmark gain of 14.9% (MMLU-Stem 0.5B P@4: 65.40 vs 20-Entropy 50.50). Gains scale with model size.

## Methodology

**Backbone & setting.** ICT builds on GRPO via the VeRL framework, trained on GSM8K and MATH training sets, evaluated on GSM8K, Math500, MMLU-Stem, GPQA, AIME23/24/25. All baselines (GRPO, 20-Entropy, STAPO) share identical loss, optimizer, and recipe; only the update mask differs. Results are means over 5 random seeds.

**Distributional selector.** For each generated rollout i at position t, ICT computes the per-position group-average distribution Pavg(·|t) = (1/G)·Σ_j softmax(L_{j,t}) across the G rollouts in a GRPO group, then scores token uniqueness as u_{i,t} = D_JS(softmax(L_{i,t}) ∥ Pavg(·|t)). A binary mask M_{i,t} = 1[u_{i,t} ≥ Percentile({u_{i,τ}}, k)] retains the top k-percentile (k=10) of uniqueness scores.

**Sparse-GRPO objective.** L_JS-GRPO(θ) = E[ (1/G) Σ_i (1/Σ_t M_{i,t}) Σ_t M_{i,t}·Ψ_{i,t}(θ) ], where Ψ_{i,t} is the standard GRPO per-token objective (clipped ratio + KL penalty). A warm-up of Θ steps uses M=1 everywhere before the sparse selector activates.

**Theory.** Defines strategy purity β(π) = Σ_a π(a)² (the collision probability) and the squared policy p2(a) ∝ π(a)². The Rényi-2 gradient is ∇_θ H2 = −2 E_{a∼p2}[∇_θ log π_θ(a)], giving the first-order entropy-change formula ΔH2 ≈ −2Δθ_{s,a*} π(a*)(π(a*)/β(π) − 1) for an update to a single selected token a*. This yields the entropy bifurcation: high-confidence tokens (π>β) decrease entropy, low-confidence tokens (π<β) increase entropy. The paper additionally proves homogeneity of the two entropy measures: for dominant tokens with π(a*) > e^{−1}, sgn(∂H1/∂π(a*)) = sgn(∂H2/∂π(a*)) = −1, so reinforcing dominant tokens lowers both entropies. Selected unique tokens straddle the β(π) boundary, producing balanced entropy dynamics.

**Compute note.** JS divergence over the full vocabulary is computed via parallel batch processing on logit vectors; the authors claim negligible overhead relative to the backward-pass savings from the 90% sparse mask. Experiments run on a V100 cluster.

## Results & Highlights

- **Average Total (Avg P@4 across 7 benchmarks) improvements over the best baseline:** +3.38 (0.5B), +4.31 (1.5B), +3.94 (7B) percentage points; over the Qwen2.5 base models: +6.28/5.12/4.16 points respectively.
- **pass@4 vs pass@1 differential:** pass@4 gains consistently exceed pass@1 gains (0.5B: +3.37 vs +2.37; 1.5B: +4.98 vs +3.64; 7B: +4.38 vs +3.50), indicating ICT generates more diverse correct reasoning trajectories rather than redundant ones — evidence for enhanced exploration.
- **Max single-benchmark gain 14.9%** comes from MMLU-Stem 0.5B P@4 (65.40 vs 20-Entropy 50.50).
- **Cross-domain generalization:** trained only on math (Math500/GSM8K), gains transfer to GPQA, whereas 20-Entropy degrades on non-math benchmarks despite strong math-only numbers.
- **Sparsity ablation (Qwen2.5-1.5B, GSM8K):** 10%-unique (P@1 66.23 / P@4 85.31) > 30%-unique (64.45/83.76) > 20%-unique (63.37/83.31) > 90%-frequent (60.62/85.08) > GRPO full (57.45/78.45). The reward trajectory peaks at 10%, consistent with a sharp inflection point in the JS-uniqueness distribution near the 10% threshold. Updating only the bottom 90% non-unique tokens yields little improvement, supporting the "less is more" thesis.
- **Composition check:** among selected unique tokens the high-/low-entropy ratio is ≈1:1 (GSM8K 1.03, MATH 0.99), matching the balanced entropy-dynamics prediction.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. The reframing of RLVR instability as a distributional-selection problem (JS divergence of token logits vs. group average) rather than scalar entropy regularization is clean and implementable. Token-importance selection for RLVR is an active area (critical-token methods, DAPO, STAPO), so the conceptual delta is "use JS divergence against the GRPO group-average instead of per-token Shannon entropy." The dual Shannon+Rényi entropy analysis is a useful, well-executed theoretical lens, though entropy-based token weighting has substantial precedent.
- **Rigor:** Reasonably strong. The entropy-bifurcation derivation is explicit and reproducible (Eq. 6, Appendix A.2). Results are means over 5 random seeds, and all baselines share the identical optimizer/loss/recipe — only the mask differs, a clean controlled comparison. Caveat: the first-order Taylor approximation explicitly assumes small learning rates and acknowledges that AdamW momentum and large advantage spikes can break it; this is honestly disclosed.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Model family, sizes, benchmarks, baselines, k=10 threshold, warm-up mechanism, and the exact selector formula (Eq. 9–11) are all given. The group-average distribution construction (per-position average over G rollouts) is unambiguous. Minor gaps: exact warm-up length Θ, learning rate, and full V100 hyperparameters are relegated to the appendix; paired significance tests across seeds are deferred to future work.
- **Significance:** Practically meaningful if the gains are compute-neutral. The mean gain (~4.4–5.0 pts Total Avg P@4) is solid and consistent across three model sizes and seven benchmarks, and the cross-domain transfer to GPQA is a positive signal. Significance is tempered by the uneven per-benchmark distribution (see red flags).
- **Red flags:**
  (1) The 14.9% headline maximum is concentrated in a single cell (MMLU-Stem 0.5B, vs the weakest 20-Entropy baseline); across AIME-series benchmarks some cells show ICT near or below STAPO (e.g., AIME25 7B P@4: ICT 17.17 vs STAPO 13.33 — ICT ahead, but AIME23 7B P@4: ICT 10.83 vs STAPO 10.00 is a small margin, and AIME24 1.5B P@1 ICT 16.40 is actually below STAPO 16.65). The mean-vs-max ~3x gap means a few strong cells drive the headline.
  (2) "Top 10% of unique tokens" depends on a per-step percentile over a single generation's T tokens — short generations have small absolute counts, which is not discussed.
  (3) The fixed k=10 is selected by observing an inflection in the same data used to evaluate, a mild selection-on-test concern; the ablation is only on 1.5B/GSM8K.
  (4) "Negligible overhead" for full-vocabulary JS divergence is asserted without wall-clock numbers.
  (5) The 4.58% figure in the abstract is the average of the three per-baseline deltas (4.37/4.98/4.38), not the average across benchmarks — easy to misread.

## Strengths

- Clean conceptual contribution: collapses two failure modes (entropy collapse vs. explosion) into a single, principled distributional-selection mechanism with a clear information-theoretic motivation (self-information inversely proportional to probability).
- Both theory and experiments are provided; the entropy-bifurcation result and the 1:1 high/low-entropy composition check are concrete predictions that are empirically validated.
- Strong controlled comparison: only the update mask differs across methods; all other components (loss, optimizer, KL coefficient, recipe) are held identical.
- Compute-attractive: 90% of gradients are masked during backward, and the method is a drop-in modification to standard GRPO.
- Scales across three model sizes (0.5B/1.5B/7B), mitigating size-specific artifact concerns, and shows cross-domain transfer from math training to GPQA.

## Limitations

- **Fixed token ratio:** k=10 is global and static; the authors themselves flag adaptive/dynamic selection as future work.
- **Outlier-driven headline:** the 14.9% max is a single benchmark cell; per-benchmark variance is non-trivial and a few cells are below STAPO.
- **First-order approximation:** the central theory assumes infinitesimal updates; AdamW momentum and advantage spikes can invalidate it (honestly acknowledged but not quantified).
- **Overhead unmeasured:** "negligible" full-vocabulary JS cost is asserted, not benchmarked with wall-clock/FLOPs.
- **Statistical reporting:** means over 5 seeds only; no standard deviations or paired significance tests reported (deferred to future work).
- **Single metric family:** only pass@1/pass@4 reported; no pass@8, no consistency-of-correct-answer or diversity-of-reasoning-paths analysis despite the exploration framing.
- **Narrow training data:** trained only on GSM8K+MATH; broader domains and other model families (Llama, Mistral) are left to future work.
- **Selector ablation only on one config:** the 10/20/30% sweep is run only on Qwen2.5-1.5B/GSM8K, so transferability of the threshold is under-tested.

## Relevance

直接契合「LLM 训练稳定性与可观测性」与「LLM 推理评测」两条核心兴趣：ICT 针对 RLVR 训练中熵塌缩与爆炸这一典型不稳定性失效模式，给出基于 token 级 logit 分布偏差（JS 散度）的可解释、可监测选择机制，并用熵分叉理论与多基准 pass@1/pass@4 量化训练漂移与推理质量，对监控训练动态、诊断 token 级更新异常、评估推理输出多样性具有方法论与可观测性参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does performance change if Pavg is computed against a running global reference distribution rather than the per-group average, and which better captures "distributional uniqueness"?
- Is the 10% threshold stable across model families (Llama, Mistral), generation lengths, and non-math domains, or does it require per-config tuning?
- What are the wall-clock and memory costs of the full-vocabulary JS divergence computation relative to the backward-pass savings from the 90% mask?
- Can the entropy-bifurcation signal (π(a) vs. β(π)) be exposed as a live training monitor for early detection of collapse/explosion, supporting the "training stability & observability" angle?
- Do gains compose with other RLVR stabilizers (DAPO dynamic sampling, clip-higher, process supervision) or are they redundant?
- How does ICT affect pass@8 and reasoning-path diversity (not just pass@4), given the exploration-centric framing?
- Per-benchmark: which benchmarks drive the mean, and does the method hold on the hardest cells (AIME24/25) or mostly on easier/commonsense ones?
- Does the first-order approximation's failure mode (large advantages + AdamW momentum) manifest as instability in practice, and is there a detectable signature?
