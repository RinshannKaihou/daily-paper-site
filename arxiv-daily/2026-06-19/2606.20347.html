Critical Percolation as a Synthetic Data Model for Interpretability

arXiv: 2606.20347

Date: 2026-06-19

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20347

一句话总结： 作者将临界均值场渗流（critical mean-field percolation）构造成一类解析可控、无需调参的合成数据，用以替代缺乏层次结构的现有可解释性"玩具数据"，并给出近线性时间的层次采样算法；探测实验表明网络激活中可线性解码其 ground-truth 层次潜变量。
TL;DR: The authors turn critical mean-field percolation theory into a synthetic interpretability dataset whose clusters are sparse, low-dimensional fractals (fractal dimension D=4) with a power-law size distribution (exponent τ=5/2) and a taxonomic latent hierarchy; an almost-linear-time O(n·α(n)) "cyclic coalescent" sampler generates the data, and linear probing shows a residual MLP trained on it linearly encodes the ground-truth latents. Published at the ICML 2026 Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop as a proof-of-concept.

核心贡献

  • 提出一类基于临界均值场渗流簇的合成数据模型，同时具备自然数据的多重统计性质：稀疏性、自相似（分形）、幂律尺寸分布（τ=5/2）、低本征维（分形维 D=4）与分类学层次结构。
  • 将目标值建模为二元潜变量森林上的归一化求和（Eq. 4），层次潜变量对应数据空间中的"键"，天然形成 set-inclusion 式的分类学层级（区别于 PCFG 式组合层级）。
  • 利用渗流簇 ↔ 均匀随机标号树 ↔ 可加聚变（additive coalescence, Pitman 1999）三者间的映射，提出cyclic coalescent 算法，借助 union-find 在 O(n·α(n)) 时间内联合采样随机树及其层次潜变量分解。
  • 用此算法开发了可缩放的合成数据生成流程（迭代式优先连接生成功率律簇 + 每簇独立 cyclic coalescent + 分支随机游走嵌入）。
  • 实证：在渗流数据上训练的残差 MLP 中，ground-truth 潜变量可被线性探测解码；并提出 figure-of-merit FOM = s_latent/√s_cluster，发现 per-latent MSE 随 FOM 呈幂律趋势，且 FOM 比潜变量"深度"更能刻画其统计重要性。
  • 指出一个有意义的负面发现：各 MLP block 之间探测 MSE 几乎无逐层变化趋势，提示网络并未以"逐层累加层次和"的方式组织潜变量。

Key Contributions

  • A synthetic data family grounded in critical mean-field percolation that simultaneously captures sparsity, self-similarity (fractality, D=4), power-law size statistics (τ=5/2), low intrinsic dimensionality, and taxonomic (set-inclusion) hierarchical structure — a combination missing from current interpretability toy datasets.
  • A hierarchical latent-variable construction over percolation clusters, where latents correspond to bonds in data space and targets are normalized sums along latent paths (Eq. 4); this is complementary to PCFG-style compositional hierarchies.
  • A novel cyclic coalescent algorithm (Algorithm 1) built on Pitman's additive-coalescence/random-tree mapping, implemented with union-find to jointly sample a uniform random labeled tree and its binary merger hierarchy in almost-linear O(n·α(n)) time (α = inverse Ackermann).
  • A scalable synthetic-dataset generator combining iterative preferential attachment (p' = 1/3, derived for critical mean-field) for cluster sizes, per-cluster cyclic coalescent, and hierarchical branching-random-walk embedding.
  • Empirical demonstration via linear probing that a residual MLP (transformer-FFN-like, 4× expansion, ReLU) trained on percolation data linearly encodes the ground-truth latents; introduces FOM = s_latent/√s_cluster and shows per-latent probe MSE follows a power-law in FOM.
  • A notable negative result: no interpretable layerwise progression across MLP blocks, suggesting the network does not organize latents as a cumulative hierarchy across layers.

Methodology

Data model (Sec. 2). The unit cube [0,1)^d is discretized into a hypercubic lattice Λ of linear size L. Each site is occupied i.i.d. with probability p, giving a random subset S. Empirical datasets are drawn uniformly from S. Three assumptions make the model tractable:

  • Mean-field: d ≫ d_u = 6 (upper critical dimension), so the Bethe lattice approximation applies with percolation threshold p_c = 1/(z−1) = 1/(2d−1), z = 2d.
  • Criticality: p = p_c, yielding pure power-law cluster-size distribution n_s = s^{−τ} with τ = 5/2, σ = 1/2, and fractal dimension D = 4.
  • Hierarchy: targets follow a binary forest F of latent variables; for each mean-field bond merging clusters C_1, C_2 a fresh latent is created as the parent of their topmost latents. The target at point x is y_x = (1/√(1+d_x)) Σ z_i^{(x)} (Eq. 4), a normalized sum over the latent path of depth d_x. Latent values z_i ~ N(0,1).

Cyclic coalescent sampler (Sec. 3). Direct high-dimensional percolation simulation is intractable, so the authors exploit the equivalence critical-mean-field-percolation-cluster ↔ uniform-random-labeled-tree ↔ additive-coalescent-merger-process (Aldous; Pitman 1999). Their cyclic coalescent variant arranges initial singleton blocks in a uniformly random cycle; at each of n−1 steps, a uniformly drawn node u selects its block's cyclic successor A' and a random node v ∈ A', adds edge {u,v} to the tree, creates a new merger-tree node, and merges blocks. A union-find with union-by-rank and path-halving gives O(n·α(n)) total time. Correctness is proven in Appendix E.2.

Dataset generation (Sec. 4). Cluster sizes are produced by iterative preferential attachment (create new cluster w.p. 1/3, else grow a size-proportional cluster — p'=1/3 derived in App. D). Each cluster independently runs the cyclic coalescent to get a tree + latent hierarchy. Nodes are embedded via a branching random walk that follows the latent tree, giving consistent geometry independent of iteration count. Two datasets were generated: one-cluster (2×10^5 points, single cluster) and multi-cluster (2×10^6 points, clusters with ≥500 points retained).

Network & probing (Sec. 5). A residual MLP mimicking a transformer FFN block (x' = x + W_2 σ(W_1 x + b_1) + b_2): one-cluster uses d_model=256, L=3 blocks; multi-cluster uses d_model=512, L=3 blocks; trained with AdamW + cosine LR decay. Baselines are Ridge regression (to confirm nonlinearity) and 1-NN (best practical estimate). Linear probes (Alain & Bengio 2016) are fit from activations to latent values z_i, stratified by depth, excluding latents with s_latent < 150. Per-latent MSE is reported, along with a figure of merit FOM = s_latent/√s_cluster.

Results & Highlights

  • Task learnability (Table 1): MLP R² = 0.94 (one-cluster) / 0.88 (multi-cluster); Ridge R² = 0.51 / 0.39 (confirms task is genuinely nonlinear); 1-NN R² = 0.94 / 0.86 (MLP matches the best practical estimate).
  • Linear decodability (Fig. 4): Ground-truth latents are linearly decodable from MLP activations; hidden-layer activations give lower probe MSE than the residual stream, and all layers beat raw input. The probe-vs-input gap shrinks with increasing FOM, suggesting coarse-grained (high-FOM) latents are partly recoverable from input geometry alone.
  • Power-law in FOM: Per-latent MSE scales as a power law in FOM (slopes ≈ −0.64 one-cluster, −0.20 multi-cluster), and FOM explains latent importance better than depth — Fig. 5 shows no power-law trend versus depth alone.
  • No layerwise progression: Probe MSE is nearly constant across MLP blocks, with no interpretable cross-layer pattern — a negative result implying the network does not implement an obvious "hierarchical sum across depth" structure.
  • Scalable & tuning-free generation: Because statistical properties are pinned by critical exponents (τ=5/2, D=4), the model requires no hyperparameter tuning; the O(n·α(n)) sampler enables arbitrary-scale data.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Genuinely distinctive. Using critical percolation theory as a first-principles prior for synthetic interpretability data — uniting sparsity, fractality, power laws, low intrinsic dimension, and taxonomic hierarchy in one analytically tractable object — is more principled than the usual Gaussian/blobs/spirals or even recent activation-model benchmarks (Chanin & Garriga-Alonso 2026). The cyclic-coalescent O(n·α(n)) sampler is a clean, non-trivial algorithmic contribution.
  • Rigor: The mathematical scaffolding (Bethe lattice, Aldous/Pitman mappings, inverse-Ackermann union-find, proven correctness in App. E.2) is solid and appropriately cited. Experiments are internally consistent and include sensible baselines (Ridge for nonlinearity, 1-NN as an oracle). However, the empirical scope is narrow: one architecture (residual MLP), regression only, two datasets, no comparison to existing synthetic interpretability benchmarks, and no SAE/circuit methods evaluated.
  • Reproducibility: Good signals — tuning-free model, explicit hyperparameter tables (App. B), claimed code release. The generator's reliance on standard combinatorial objects (union-find, random trees) should make reimplementation feasible.
  • Significance: Currently moderate and aspirational. The paper itself repeatedly frames the work as a "proof of concept" and a "testbed proposal." Its real value is contingent on a future demonstration that percolation data discriminates between interpretability methods (e.g., reproduces feature splitting / absorption pathologies) — which is explicitly left as future work. As it stands, the headline claim ("latents are linearly decodable") is a relatively weak baseline that many datasets satisfy.
  • Red flags: None severe, but worth flagging — (1) workshop/proof-of-concept status; (2) linear decodability is a low bar and the paper acknowledges probing is correlational, not causal; (3) no head-to-head with competing synthetic-data models; (4) the model currently handles only vectorized regression data (no tokens/classification), limiting immediate LLM relevance.

Strengths

  • Unifies five natural-data properties (sparsity, self-similarity, power-law, low intrinsic dimension, taxonomic hierarchy) in a single, parameter-free (critical-exponent-pinned) model.
  • Elegant, provably-correct, near-linear-time sampler via the cyclic coalescent — practical for arbitrary-scale generation.
  • Clean experimental design with appropriate baselines and an informative diagnostic (FOM) that outperforms naive "depth."
  • Honest reporting of a negative result (no layerwise latent progression) rather than overclaiming.
  • Authors are candid about limitations and explicitly position the work as foundational tooling for future interpretability studies.

Limitations

  • Correlational, not causal: Linear probes show association only; the authors note causal interventions (à la ROME/activation patching) are needed to validate interpretability hypotheses.
  • Narrow empirical scope: Single residual-MLP architecture, regression-only, no SAE/circuit/feature-splitting evaluations — the central selling point (a testbed for interpretability methods) is not yet demonstrated.
  • Missing realism: The model assumes data is populated randomly, does not capture compositional (part-whole) feature relationships, and supports only vectorized regression — no tokenized text or classification.
  • No baselines vs. other synthetic models: Not compared quantitatively against Chanin & Garriga-Alonso (2026), Bussmann et al. (2025), or Costa et al. (2025) hierarchical-activation models.
  • Mean-field regime only: Generalizing to finite-dimensional or supercritical percolation (needed for cyclic/overlapping compositional concepts) is open.

Relevance

与"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability（机制可解释性、潜变量结构、probing、SAE、表征几何）"方向高度相关：它提供了一个 ground-truth 层次潜变量已知的合成数据源，可用来检验 SAE 特征分裂/吸收、层次潜特征以及表征几何等方法是否真能恢复数据中预设的多尺度结构，是评估可解释性工具可靠性的有用基准候选。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does percolation data reproduce SAE pathologies (feature splitting, absorption, composition) as a function of SAE width — i.e., does the testbed actually separate interpretability methods?
  • How do probe results change with architecture (real transformer blocks, attention) and with tokenized/classification variants of the task?
  • Is the "no layerwise progression" finding robust, or does it reflect that a 3-block residual MLP is too shallow to expose hierarchical accumulation?
  • Can the cyclic coalescent be extended to supercritical percolation to model overlapping/compositional concepts, and would that yield cyclic latents recoverable by SAEs?
  • Does the FOM = s_latent/√s_cluster power-law in probe MSE have a theoretical explanation rooted in the percolation critical exponents?
  • How do percolation-trained representations compare (via representational similarity) to those trained on real hierarchical data, and what does that imply about transferability of conclusions?
