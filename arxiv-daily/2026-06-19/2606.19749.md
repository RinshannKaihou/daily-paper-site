> **一句话总结：** 本文系统评测了两套开源（OpenAIReview、'coarse）、一套商用（Reviewer3）加一个 zero-shot 基线共四类智能审稿系统，在六个前沿/高效 LLM 上跨 ICLR/NeurIPS 真实论文与八领域扰动基准进行对比，发现最佳配置（OpenAIReview + GPT-5.5）在论文质量判别上达 83% 成对准确率、能检出 71.6% 注入错误，但仍有大量误报和过度挑刺，并揭示了不同模型捕获的是互补的错误。

> **TL;DR:** The first head-to-head benchmark of publicly usable agentic peer-review systems (OpenAIReview, 'coarse, Reviewer3, zero-shot) across six LLMs on real ICLR/NeurIPS papers plus a new 8-domain perturbation benchmark. Best config (OpenAIReview + GPT-5.5) reaches 83.0% pairwise accuracy at tracking paper quality and 71.6% recall on injected errors; the union of all six models hits 83.3% recall, indicating complementary errors worth exploiting in future harness design.

## 核心贡献

- 首次对**可直接运行**的第三方智能审稿系统（OpenAIReview、'coarse、Reviewer3 加 zero-shot 基线）进行**整体管线**对比评测，而非只替换底层 LLM 的提示词。
- 提出**质量代理信号相关性研究**：在真实 ICLR/NeurIPS 2021–2022 论文上，所有系统在四个质量代理（社区引用、会议奖项、审稿分数、复合指标）上的成对判别准确率均高于随机，最佳为 OpenAIReview + GPT-5.5 的 0.83，且信号随模型能力增强而增强。
- 构建跨 8 个 arXiv 学科、4 类错误（表面数学编辑、虚假声明、推理错误、实验设计错误）的**扰动注入基准**，提供带已知 ground-truth 的错误检出能力度量；最佳配置检出 71.6%，六个模型并集达 83.3%。
- 部署 OpenAIReview 公网工具，收集 1,100 篇论文的 1,360 次真实用户反馈，投票倾向正面（点赞:点踩 = 1.44:1），主要缺点是**精度不足（误报与琐碎挑刺）**。
- 人类审稿人与 AI 在段落层面互补（Jaccard 0.23）：人类聚焦论文级/新颖性批评，AI 聚焦表面/声明/实验/数学技术细节，支持 AI 作为人类审稿补充而非替代的定位。

## Key Contributions

- **First whole-system head-to-head benchmark** of publicly usable agentic reviewers (OpenAIReview, 'coarse, Reviewer3) plus a zero-shot baseline, scoring each as an end-to-end pipeline rather than swapping the underlying LLM.
- **Quality-proxy correlation study** on real ICLR/NeurIPS papers: every system discriminates low- from high-quality papers above chance across four proxies (citations, awards, review scores, composite); best is OpenAIReview + GPT-5.5 at 0.83, with signal scaling in model strength.
- **New 8-domain perturbation benchmark** with four error categories (surface math edits, false claims, faulty reasoning, experimental-design errors) providing known ground truth; a five-stage extract→generate→validate→verify→inject pipeline produces genuine, well-formed errors (manual audit: 82.5% valid).
- **Complementarity finding**: backend models catch largely disjoint errors, so the six-model union reaches 83.3% recall (+11.7 points over the best single model), pointing to better harness design as the main lever.
- **Real-world deployment evidence**: a public OpenAIReview web tool collected 1,360 reviews of 1,100 papers, with likes outnumbering dislikes 1.44:1 and 4.9% of comments marked resolved; main weakness is **comment precision** (false positives + trivial nitpicks = ~71% of downvotes).

## Methodology

The paper evaluates four review methods — zero-shot, OpenAIReview, 'coarse, and the closed Reviewer3 — each backed (except Reviewer3) by one of six LLMs: two frontier (GPT-5.5, Claude Opus 4.7) and four efficient (DeepSeek-V4-Flash, Qwen3.6-35B-A3B, Gemini-3.1-Flash-Lite, Grok-4.1-Fast), all via OpenRouter without reasoning mode. OpenAIReview splits a paper into passages, reviews each against a running summary + neighbors, and merges comments, enabling long-range inconsistency detection without one giant prompt.

**Study 1 (quality tracking):** 30 high- vs 30 low-quality ICLR/NeurIPS 2021–2022 papers per proxy (197 unique). Metric = pairwise accuracy on whether the low-quality paper draws more comments, with ties scored 0.5, plus comment-volume delta and severity stratification (Major/Moderate/Minor). 95% CIs from cluster bootstrap over papers.

**Study 2 (perturbation):** 74 papers across 8 arXiv subject classes; a deterministic extractor finds candidate spans (equations, theorems, proofs, claim/experimental paragraphs), a generator LLM produces replacement LaTeX for an admitted error type, then structural validation, a checklist verifier (to exclude mere typos), and injection yield one corrupted paper. Detection requires fuzzy substring match of the perturbed span in a comment quote above threshold τ, confirmed by an LLM judge; recall is the fraction of injected errors detected.

**Study 3 (deployment):** OpenAIReview with Claude Opus 4.6 backend exposed as a public web app; users like/dislike, resolve, or comment on each review. Downvoted comments were classified by an LLM judge with manual validation.

## Results & Highlights

- **Quality tracking:** OpenAIReview + GPT-5.5 leads at 0.83 overall (CI [0.73, 0.91]) and Major-tier 0.78 / Moderate 0.91; Reviewer3 0.80, zero-shot+DeepSeek-V4-Flash 0.80, 'coarse 0.66. All proxies ≥ 0.60; signal scales with model strength.
- **Error recall:** OpenAIReview + GPT-5.5 best single config at 71.6% (CI [65.6, 76.9]); zero-shot GPT-5.5 already at 59.8%. OpenAIReview's largest gains over zero-shot are on prose-level errors (faulty reasoning 36.8%→68.4%); surface math-token recall barely moves (45.8%→47.3%). 'coarse and Reviewer3 underperform zero-shot overall, likely because they prioritize 5–10 high-priority comments rather than exhaustive enumeration.
- **Complementarity:** six-model union reaches 83.3% recall (CI [79.7, 86.9]), +11.7 points; humans and AI Jaccard 0.23 at paragraph level; humans own 80% of paper-level/novelty critiques, AI over-indexes on surface/claim/experimental/formal-math.
- **Deployment:** 1.44:1 up/down vote ratio; 4.9% of comments resolved; ~71% of downvotes were false positives (38.5%), trivial nitpicks (17.3%), or unreasonable detail asks (15.2%); OCR/parsing artifacts another 6.4%.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: Moderate-High.** Perturbation-based review evaluation has NLP lineage (FLAWS, SPECS are concurrent); the differentiators — by error type across six LLMs, head-to-head third-party systems, and a real deployment — are genuinely new and timely. The complementarity-by-ensemble observation is somewhat expected but quantified carefully.
- **Rigor: Moderate-High.** Cluster-bootstrap CIs, four proxies, severity stratification, a manual audit of perturbation validity (82.5%), and a validated fuzzy-match threshold τ strengthen claims. However, several proxies are small (n=60) and noisy, frontier models run only on 74/24-paper subsets due to cost, and pairwise-accuracy on comment counts is a coarse metric that misses "one fatal flaw" reviews.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate.** OpenAIReview and 'coarse are open-source; Reviewer3 is closed (treated as a fixed black box). Perturbation pipeline is described in detail, but the generator LLM and exact generator prompts, candidate pools, and full per-subtype tables are largely relegated to appendices. Deployment data is observational and not publicly released.
- **Significance: High** for the meta-science and peer-review community; provides the first rigorous, current-model snapshot of a fast-moving product space and a reusable perturbation benchmark.
- **Red flags:** (1) Authors built OpenAIReview, the winning system — results consistently favor it on every error category, and the deployment only tests OpenAIReview, so claims could be self-serving; no non-author system won. (2) "Quality" proxies (citations, awards, review scores) are explicitly noisy and only loosely track true quality; conflating "more comments" with "worse paper" can reward verbosity. (3) Reviewer3 and 'coarse optimize for editorial prioritization (5–10 comments) rather than exhaustive enumeration, so recall is a somewhat unfair axis for them — the authors flag this but still report it head-to-head. (4) Frontier-model numbers rest on a 24-paper subset, limiting statistical power for the headline 71.6% recall figure.

## Strengths

- Evaluates whole systems authors can actually run today, not academic prototypes, across a broad LLM sweep.
- Two complementary evaluation axes (quality-correlation + ground-truth perturbation recall) plus a real public deployment triangulate the same conclusion.
- Honest about error rates and failure modes; the precision problem (false positives, nitpicks) is foregrounded rather than buried.
- Quantified complementarity between models and between humans/AI gives concrete design direction (ensemble harnesses, AI-for-enumeration + human-for-novelty).

## Limitations

- Best single recall (71.6%) leaves substantial undetected error headroom; the 83.3% union is an oracle ensemble, not a deployable single system.
- Quality proxies are small and noisy; "more comments = worse paper" assumption may reward chattier systems and penalize concise fatal-flaw reviews.
- Reviewer3 and 'coarse's editorial-prioritization design is misaligned with exhaustive-recall evaluation, disadvantaging them on this metric.
- Perturbation benchmark covers theoretical/empirical ML-adjacent fields; generalization to purely humanities or large-scale experimental science is untested.
- Only OpenAIReview is deployed and evaluated for user feedback; no comparable real-user data for competitors.
- Authorship overlap with the leading system creates an incentive-conflict surface.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can a single harness dynamically route each passage to the backend model most likely to catch its error type, approaching the 83.3% oracle-union recall at single-system cost?
- How much of the OpenAIReview advantage is the running-summary mechanism versus prompt engineering, and does it hold on longer (20+ page) papers where context fragmentation is worse?
- Does the "more comments = lower quality" signal survive a balanced control where high- and low-quality papers are matched on length, field, and equation density?
- What is the false-positive rate (precision), not just recall, of each system on the perturbation benchmark, and does improving precision cost recall?
- Could the complementarity between humans (novelty/scope) and AI (technical enumeration) be formalized into a human-in-the-loop protocol that measurably improves review reliability under the "review death spiral" capacity model cited?
