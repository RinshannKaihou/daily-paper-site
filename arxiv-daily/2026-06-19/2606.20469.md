> **一句话总结：** 本文将"极小点尖锐度"重新定义在 Fisher 信息矩阵诱导的统计流形黎曼几何上，定义 $S_R(\theta^*)=\mathrm{tr}(G(\theta^*)^{-1}\nabla^2 L(\theta^*))$ 并证明其在保持网络功能的参数重标定下严格不变（正面回应 Dinh 等人的批评），通过将 mini-batch SGD 噪声建模为协方差 $\propto$ FIM 的 SDE 导出平稳分布 $\pi^*\propto\exp(-(B/\eta)L)$，证明 SGD 在 Riemannian 平坦极小处指数级集中，并用 $S_R$ 显式控制的 PAC-Bayes 界把几何偏置与测试误差严格联系起来。

> **TL;DR:** The paper replaces the Euclidean loss Hessian with the Fisher Information Matrix as a Riemannian metric, defines a reparametrization-invariant sharpness $S_R=\mathrm{tr}(G^{-1}\nabla^2 L)$, proves it resolves the Dinh et al. critique, derives the mini-batch SGD SDE stationary distribution (gradient noise $\propto$ FIM) to show exponential concentration on Riemannian-flat minima, and links the bias to test error via a PAC-Bayes bound controlled explicitly by $S_R$; MNIST/CIFAR-10 experiments confirm $S_R$ tracks generalization and scales as $B/\eta$ where Euclidean sharpness does not.

## 核心贡献

- **Riemannian sharpness $S_R$**：以 Fisher 信息矩阵 $G(\theta)$ 作为度量张量，定义 $S_R(\theta^*)=\mathrm{tr}(G(\theta^*)^{-1}\nabla^2 L(\theta^*))$，把尖锐度变成统计流形上的几何量（Eq. 6）。
- **Lemma 1 不变性证明**：利用 FIM 与 Hessian 在临界点处作为 $(0,2)$ 张量的变换规则 $G\mapsto J^{-\top}GJ^{-1}$、$\nabla^2L\mapsto J^{-\top}\nabla^2L\,J^{-1}$ 及 trace 的循环性，证明 $S_R$ 在任意光滑且保持网络功能的重标定下严格不变，正面回应 Dinh et al. (2017) 对欧氏尖锐度的批评。
- **Theorem 1（SGD 隐式偏置）**：在 Assumption 1 (smoothness + FIM PD)、Assumption 2 (interpolation/零训练损失)、Assumption 3 (Fisher 噪声结构 $\mathrm{Cov}[\nabla\tilde L]=\tfrac{\eta}{B}G$)、Assumption 4 (barrier 分离) 下，将 mini-batch SGD 写成 Itô SDE $d\theta=-\nabla L\,dt+\sqrt{\tfrac{\eta}{B}}\,G^{1/2}dW_t$，其局部平稳分布 $\pi^*(\theta)\propto\exp(-\tfrac{B}{\eta}L(\theta))$；经 Laplace 近似得到 basin 概率质量 $\propto\sqrt{\det G/\det\nabla^2L}$，由 AM-GM 不等式推出 SGD 偏向 $S_R$ 更小的极小。
- **Corollary 2（PAC-Bayes 界）**：对 $(\lambda,G)$-flat 极小，构造 $Q=\mathcal N(\theta^*,\sigma^2I)$、$P=\mathcal N(\theta_0,\sigma_0^2I)$，导出 $L_{test}-L_{train}\le\sqrt{\tfrac{1}{2n}(\tfrac{1}{S_R}+\tfrac{d^2}{\sigma_0^2})\log\tfrac{1}{\delta}}$（Eq. 23），界随 $S_R$ 减小而收紧。
- **Assumption 3 的实证拆解**：把"梯度噪声协方差 $\propto$ FIM"拆成方向性 claim（$\hat\Sigma$ 与 $\hat G$ 的 cosine 对齐）和标量 claim（比例恰好为 $\eta/B$）；MNIST/CIFAR-10 上 cosine ≈ 1.000，标量 claim 在 MNIST 上成立、在 CIFAR-10 小 batch 下偏差较大。
- **Theorem 2（鲁棒化）**：提出 Assumption 5（$\mathrm{Cov}[\nabla\tilde L]=\alpha(\theta)G(\theta)$），证明主结论对 $\alpha$ 不变——因为 $\alpha$ 只出现在 basin 质量的公共前因子 $(2\pi\alpha)^{d/2}$ 中。
- **Adam 扩展**：引入 Adam 自身度量下的 $S_R^{Adam}=\mathrm{tr}(G_Adam^{-1}H)$, $G_Adam=\mathrm{diag}(v_t)$，把 SGD/FIM 结论推广为"任何预条件优化器都偏向自身度量下的平坦极小"。
- **实验**：MNIST (3 层 MLP, hidden 256) 与 CIFAR-10 (TinyCNN)，扫 $B\in\{32,128,512\}$、$\eta\in\{0.001,0.01,0.05,0.1\}$、SGD/Adam、3 seeds。验证 $S_R\propto B/\eta$（Theorem 1 预测）并显示 $S_R$ 与测试准确率的相关性强于欧氏 $S_E$。

## Key Contributions

- **Definition of Riemannian sharpness** $S_R(\theta^*)=\mathrm{tr}(G(\theta^*)^{-1}\nabla^2 L(\theta^*))$ using the FIM as the Riemannian metric, making sharpness a geometric quantity on the statistical manifold rather than a coordinate-dependent number (Eq. 6).
- **Lemma 1 (invariance proof)**: $S_R$ is provably invariant under any smooth, function-preserving reparametrization, via the tensor transformation laws $G\mapsto J^{-\top}GJ^{-1}$, $\nabla^2L\mapsto J^{-\top}\nabla^2L\,J^{-1}$ and the cyclic property of trace; this directly answers the Dinh et al. (2017) reparametrization critique of Euclidean (Hessian-trace / max-eigenvalue) flatness.
- **Theorem 1 (SGD implicit bias)**: Under smoothness + interpolation + Fisher-noise ($\mathrm{Cov}[\nabla\tilde L]=\tfrac{\eta}{B}G$) + barrier-separation assumptions, mini-batch SGD is an Itô SDE $d\theta=-\nabla L\,dt+\sqrt{\tfrac{\eta}{B}}\,G^{1/2}dW_t$ whose local stationary density is $\pi^*(\theta)\propto\exp(-\tfrac{B}{\eta}L(\theta))$; a Laplace approximation gives basin mass $\propto\sqrt{\det G/\det\nabla^2L}$, and AM-GM shows SGD asymptotically favors minima with smaller $S_R$.
- **Corollary 2 (PAC-Bayes bound)**: For a $(\lambda,G)$-flat minimum with Gaussian posterior $Q=\mathcal N(\theta^*,\sigma^2I)$ and data-free prior $P=\mathcal N(\theta_0,\sigma_0^2I)$, $L_{test}-L_{train}\le\sqrt{\tfrac{1}{2n}(\tfrac{1}{S_R}+\tfrac{d^2}{\sigma_0^2})\log\tfrac{1}{\delta}}$ (Eq. 23), decreasing in $S_R$ — formally linking flatness to test performance.
- **Empirical decomposition of Assumption 3**: Separates the directional claim (cosine alignment between diagonal $\hat\Sigma$ and $\hat G$) from the scalar claim (proportionality constant $=\eta/B$). Cosine $\approx 1.000$ across all configs; scalar claim holds on MNIST but degrades on CIFAR-10 at small batch.
- **Theorem 2 (robustness)**: Introduces the empirically supported weaker Assumption 5 ($\mathrm{Cov}[\nabla\tilde L]=\alpha(\theta)G(\theta)$) and proves the flat-minima bias is invariant to $\alpha$, because $\alpha$ appears only in the basin-mass prefactor $(2\pi\alpha)^{d/2}$ that cancels in pairwise comparisons.
- **Adam extension**: Defines $S_R^{Adam}=\mathrm{tr}(G_{Adam}^{-1}H)$ with $G_{Adam}=\mathrm{diag}(v_t)$, generalizing the result to "any preconditioned optimizer is biased toward minima flat under its own metric tensor."
- **Experiments on MNIST (3-layer MLP) and CIFAR-10 (TinyCNN)**: sweep $B$, $\eta$, SGD/Adam, 3 seeds; confirm the $S_R\propto B/\eta$ prediction and show $S_R$ correlates with generalization where Euclidean $S_E$ does not.

## Methodology

The paper builds on three layers, plus a self-critique.

**(1) Geometry.** Instead of measuring curvature in parameter space via $\mathrm{tr}(\nabla^2L)$ or $\lambda_{\max}(\nabla^2L)$ (Dinh-invariant-breaking Euclidean sharpness $S_E$), the authors endow $\mathbb R^d$ with the FIM metric $G(\theta)_{ij}=\mathbb E_{x,y}[\partial_{\theta_i}\log p\cdot\partial_{\theta_j}\log p]$ (Eq. 5) and define $S_R=\mathrm{tr}(G^{-1}\nabla^2L)$ (Eq. 6). Lemma 1 proves invariance by showing both $G$ and $\nabla^2L$ transform as $(0,2)$ tensors under a function-preserving reparametrization (the Hessian's extra second-derivative term vanishes at a critical point where $\nabla L=0$), so the trace of their product is invariant. Čencov's theorem is cited to justify FIM as the canonical metric.

**(2) Dynamics.** Mini-batch SGD $\theta_{t+1}=\theta_t-\eta\nabla\tilde L(\theta_t)$ is written as $\nabla L+\xi_t$ with Assumption 3 $\mathrm{Cov}[\xi_t]=\tfrac{\eta}{B}G$. The continuous-time Itô limit is $d\theta=-\nabla L\,dt+\sqrt{\tfrac{\eta}{B}}G^{1/2}dW_t$ (Eq. 12). Under the locally-constant-$G$ approximation near a minimum, the Fokker-Planck equation $\partial_t\rho=\nabla\cdot(\rho\nabla L)+\tfrac{\eta}{2B}\nabla\cdot(G\nabla\rho)$ admits detailed balance with stationary density $\pi^*\propto\exp(-\tfrac{B}{\eta}L)$ (Eq. 13). Laplace approximation of a quadratic basin gives mass $\propto(\det H^*)^{-1/2}\cdot\sqrt{\det G^*}$ (Eq. 14), and AM-GM shows smaller $S_R\Rightarrow$ larger mass. Corollary 1 gives the exponential preference ratio $(S_R(\theta_B^*)/S_R(\theta_A^*))^{d/2}$ under an isotropic-eigenvalue simplification.

**(3) Generalization.** PAC-Bayes with data-free Gaussian prior $P=\mathcal N(\theta_0,\sigma_0^2I)$ and posterior $Q=\mathcal N(\theta^*,\sigma^2I)$; KL bounded by $d\sigma^2/(2\sigma_0^2)+C_0$; second-order Taylor plus $(\lambda,G)$-flatness bounds $E_Q[L_{train}]$ by $\lambda\sigma^2\mathrm{tr}(G)/2$; optimizing $\sigma^2$ yields Eq. 23.

**(4) Self-critique & estimation.** The full $G$ is intractable, so all experiments use the diagonal empirical Fisher (Eq. 2, $\hat G_{ii}=\tfrac1{N_b}\sum(\partial_{\theta_i}\log p)^2$ with $y\sim p(\cdot|x;\theta)$ sampled from the model's own predictions) plus Hutchinson's trace estimator (Rademacher probes) for the Hessian diagonal, with Tikhonov damping $\lambda=10^{-6}$: $\hat S_R=\sum_i\hat H_{ii}/(\hat G_{ii}+\lambda)$ (Eq. 4). The authors flag that the diagonal empirical Fisher does **not** transform as a tensor and therefore inherits invariance only approximately (K-FAC would be needed for exact invariance). They also explicitly test Assumption 3's two sub-claims (cosine alignment $\approx 1.000$ everywhere; scalar $\eta/B$ holds on MNIST but less cleanly on CIFAR-10) and introduce Assumption 5 + Theorem 2 to show the main conclusions survive the scalar mismatch.

Experiments: MNIST 3-layer MLP (hidden 256, ReLU, 20 epochs) and CIFAR-10 TinyCNN (30 epochs); ablate SGD vs Adam, $B\in\{32,128,512\}$, $\eta\in\{0.001,0.01,0.05,0.1\}$; 3 random seeds; log $S_R$ and $S_E$ at epochs 5/10/15/20 (MNIST) and 5/10/15/20/25/30 (CIFAR-10).

## Results & Highlights

- **$S_R\propto B/\eta$ scaling (Theorem 1 prediction)**: On MNIST (SGD, $\eta=0.01$), increasing $B$ from 32 to 512 raises $S_R$ from 388 to 71,656 (~185×) while test accuracy drops 0.985→0.977. On CIFAR-10 $S_R$ rises 128,412→802,797 across the same $B$ sweep.
- **Learning-rate scaling**: On MNIST ($B=128$), larger $\eta$ lowers $S_R$ and improves accuracy (e.g., $\eta=0.05$: $S_R=168$, acc 0.985 vs $\eta=0.001$: $S_R=73{,}102$, acc 0.968). On CIFAR-10 the picture reverses at large $\eta$: $\eta=0.1$ gives low $S_R$ but poor accuracy (0.609), because the model fails to converge — an explicit caveat that "$S_R$ is reliable only when the optimizer has converged."
- **Assumption 3 decomposition**: cosine alignment $\hat\Sigma$ vs $\hat G$ is 1.000 (MNIST) and 0.984–0.997 (CIFAR-10); relative Frobenius error $\gamma$ is <0.01 on MNIST but rises to 0.07–0.18 on CIFAR-10 at $B=32$ (small-batch/high-noise regime farthest from the continuous-time limit).
- **SGD vs Adam**: Adam on MNIST reaches $S_R\approx3{,}895$, acc 0.980, comparable to SGD small-batch; on CIFAR-10 Adam's $S_R$ (226,738) is lower than SGD's (386,247) but accuracy is also slightly lower (0.794 vs 0.803). The Adam-adjusted $S_R^{Adam}=\mathrm{tr}(G_{Adam}^{-1}H)$ with $G_{Adam}=\mathrm{diag}(v_t)$ reconciles this by switching to Adam's own preconditioning metric.
- **Reparametrization robustness (§6.5)**: under a function-preserving layer-wise weight rescaling, $S_E$ grows dramatically while $S_R$ remains substantially more stable (but not exactly invariant), because the empirical Fisher is independently re-estimated and does not transform as a tensor — exactly the gap that K-FAC would close.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — Moderate.** FIM-as-metric, "SGD noise $\propto$ Fisher," and PAC-Bayes flat-minima bounds each have substantial prior precedent (Amari 1998; Li/Tai/E 2017; Dziugaite & Roy 2017; Jang et al. 2022; Kristiadi et al. 2023). The authors themselves cite Jang et al. and Kristiadi et al. as already establishing reparametrization-invariant sharpness. The genuine contribution is the **integration** — invariance + SDE stationary distribution + PAC-Bayes bound under one measure — rather than a new primitive.
- **Rigor — Mixed.** The invariance proof (Lemma 1) is clean and correct *for the true FIM*. However: (a) Theorem 1's stationary density $\pi^*\propto\exp(-(B/\eta)L)$ is derived only under a locally-constant-$G$ approximation; the authors admit the *global* stationary distribution carries an extra Jacobian factor (Roberts & Stramer 2002) that they do not analyze. (b) Corollary 1's exponential mass ratio requires an *isotropic eigenvalue assumption* ($\lambda_i(G^{-1}H^*)\approx S_R/d$) that is explicitly acknowledged as a simplification; real spectra are non-uniform. (c) The PAC-Bayes bound has a dimension-dependent constant $d^2/\sigma_0^2$ the authors leave for future tightening. (d) The headline invariance is for the true FIM, while **every experiment uses the diagonal empirical Fisher**, which the authors concede is only approximately invariant and not a tensor under reparametrization.
- **Reproducibility — Plausible but unverified.** Standard architectures (MLP, TinyCNN) on MNIST/CIFAR-10 with documented hyperparameters and 3 seeds; no code release is mentioned in the paper. The diagonal empirical Fisher + Hutchinson estimator are standard and reimplementable. But the load-bearing K-FAC experiments needed to validate *exact* invariance are **not run**, so the central theoretical claim is empirically demonstrated only in its approximate form.
- **Significance — Theoretical clarification, not an algorithm.** The practical takeaway ("flat minima generalize, prefer small batch") is what most practitioners already believe; the work refines the *theory* but delivers no new training method or practically computable generalization certificate that beats existing estimators at scale.
- **Red flags:**
  1. **Theory–practice gap**: the theorem's invariance is for true FIM; experiments use diagonal empirical Fisher — the central selling point is not what is measured.
  2. **Scale**: only MNIST + CIFAR-10 with a 3-layer MLP and a *TinyCNN*; no ImageNet, no transformer, no LLM-scale validation. Claims about "why flat minima generalize" are asserted for deep learning broadly but tested only at toy scale.
  3. **Non-monotonicity on CIFAR-10**: the paper itself admits $S_R$ vs accuracy is not monotone across all learning rates (e.g., $\eta=0.1$ gives low $S_R$ but bad accuracy due to instability), undercutting the clean "$S_R$ tracks generalization" headline.
  4. **Limited baselines**: no head-to-head comparison with other modern reparametrization-invariant complexity measures (path-norm, Fisher-weighted Hessian variants, edge-of-stability curvature) beyond the Euclidean $S_E$ strawman, and no comparison against Jang et al. (2022)'s information-geometric sharpness or Kristiadi et al. (2023)'s Riemannian framework on the same benchmarks.
  5. **AI-tool disclosure + no funding + non-specialist author profile**: not disqualifying, but warrants extra skepticism about whether the proofs have had independent expert verification.

## Strengths

- Clean, correct invariance proof for the true FIM that genuinely answers Dinh et al. within a coherent framework.
- Unifies three usually-separate analyses (geometric invariance, SGD-as-SDE dynamics, PAC-Bayes) under a single measure $S_R$, which prior invariant-sharpness work (Jang, Kristiadi) did not do.
- Unusually honest about its own weaknesses: explicit decomposition and empirical test of Assumption 3, an explicit Theorem 2 showing robustness to the scalar mismatch, and a candid §6.5 on the empirical-FIM invariance gap.
- Quantitative confirmation of the $B/\eta$ scaling prediction on MNIST, going beyond a qualitative "flat = good" story.
- The Adam-adjusted $S_R^{Adam}$ generalization is an elegant, well-motivated extension.

## Limitations

- Headline invariance holds for the true FIM; the diagonal empirical Fisher used in **all** experiments is only approximately invariant (K-FAC needed for exactness) — and K-FAC is never run.
- Experimental scale is MNIST (3-layer MLP) and CIFAR-10 (TinyCNN) only; no modern architectures, no ImageNet, no transformer/LLM-scale validation.
- Theorem 1's stationary distribution is derived only under a locally-constant-$G$ approximation; the global stationary density's Jacobian factor is unanalyzed.
- Corollary 1's exponential mass ratio relies on an isotropic-eigenvalue simplification the authors flag as unrealistic.
- PAC-Bayes bound carries a dimension-dependent constant $d^2/\sigma_0^2$ left untightened; no attempt at a non-vacuous numerical bound is reported.
- $S_R$ is not a monotone predictor of accuracy on CIFAR-10 at large learning rates (instability confound), so the metric is only reliable post-convergence.
- No code release mentioned; no head-to-head comparison against competing invariant-sharpness measures beyond Euclidean $S_E$.

## Relevance

与你研究方向中 **"ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations"** 和 **"LLM Training Stability & Observability"** 高度相关：本文从信息几何与随机过程层面严格刻画了 SGD 的隐式偏置、损失景观曲率与泛化的因果链，可为分析大规模训练中优化轨迹稳定性、batch/lr 选择与泛化表现提供理论度量工具；其 Fisher 度量下的尖锐度也可作为训练监控的候选可观测量，潜在地服务于训练稳定性诊断。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would replacing the diagonal empirical Fisher with **K-FAC** (or a low-rank structured estimator) materially change both the empirical invariance and the generalization-tracking behavior, and is the compute cost justified at LLM scale?
- Does the $S_R$-based PAC-Bayes bound remain predictive of test error at **ImageNet / foundation-model scale**, or does the diagonal-estimator approximation and the $d^2/\sigma_0^2$ constant make it vacuous there?
- How does $S_R$ relate to other reparametrization-invariant complexity measures — **path-norm, Fisher-weighted Hessian, edge-of-stability curvature, implicit-regularization / Sobolev-sensitivity bounds** — and can a unified hierarchy or equivalence be established?
- Can the SDE stationary-distribution analysis be extended beyond SGD to **momentum and adaptive optimizers** (Adam, AdamW, Lion), whose gradient-noise structure is not simply $\propto$ FIM — does the $S_R^{Adam}$ sketch generalize rigorously?
- Could $S_R$ (or its trajectory over epochs) serve as a **practical observable for training monitoring** — e.g., for early stopping, LR scheduling, or detecting when SGD has settled into a genuinely flat basin versus an unstable low-$S_R$ artifact?
- The global stationary distribution under position-dependent $G$ carries a Jacobian factor (Roberts & Stramer 2002); how large is this correction in practice, and does it qualitatively change the flat-minima preference?
