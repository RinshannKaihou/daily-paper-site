> **一句话总结：** 提出 CWE-Trace 框架（834 条手工标注的 Linux 内核样本 + 严格时间切分 + DFI/HDD 两个诊断指标），系统评测 8 个原生 LLM 与 15 个 LoRA 微调变体后发现：数据污染对漏洞检测无可见增益（84% 名义污染样本无可用记忆信号），骨干网络的方向性先验主导微调效果（DFI 从 -85.5 到 +94.8 pp 稳定存在），微调只是移动输出阈值而非改变决策策略——即"校准而无理解"；最强检测仅 52.1%（仅高于随机 2.1 pp），精确 CWE Top-1 低于 1.3%。

> **TL;DR:** CWE-Trace stress-tests 8 vanilla LLMs and 15 LoRA variants on 834 manually curated Linux kernel samples with a strict pre/post-2025 temporal split. Data contamination yields no memorization advantage (84% of nominally contaminated samples carry no usable signal), backbone directional priors dominate fine-tuning (stable DFI from -85.5 to +94.8 pp), and fine-tuning shifts the output threshold without changing the decision policy—"calibration without comprehension." Best detection is only 52.1% (+2.1 pp over chance); exact CWE Top-1 stays below 1.3%.

## 核心贡献

- **新基准与数据集（CWE-Trace）：** 从 417 个 Linux kernel CVE 中手工标注 834 个上下文感知的"漏洞—补丁"样本对，覆盖 74 个 CWE；采用双人评审协议（two-reviewer inspection）过滤非安全改动，保留 L1（单函数）与 L2（多文件/多函数）上下文，超越以往函数孤立抽取。
- **严格时间切分：** PBD（历史集 ≤2024，628 样本）与 LFD（无泄漏集 2025，206 样本）分离，用于区分"泛化 vs 记忆"；并对开源模型固定 HuggingFace commit SHA（两级溯源）以防止后发布更新带来的污染。
- **两个原创诊断指标：** DFI（Directional Failure Index）度量模型的偏执/怀疑方向性偏差；HDD（Hierarchical Distance and Direction）在 CWE-1000 分类树中度量错误的最短路径距离与方向（exact/shallow/deep/lateral/invalid），可区分"浅层类别识别"与"精确根因理解"。
- **系统化微调研究：** 3 个骨干（Qwen3-4B、DeepSeek-R1-32B、Llama3.1-8B）× 5 个 SOTA 数据集（Devign、LineVul、PrimeVul、MegaVul、VDISC）= 15 个 LoRA 变体，在统一基准与统一三任务目标下评测，隔离"微调改变了什么 vs 由骨干先验决定的部分"。
- **数据质量审计：** 在函数级而非数据集级验证污染样本，发现 PrimeVul/MegaVul 标签冲突率约 75%、污染样本中约 31% 存在 CWE 误分类，从机制上解释了"CVE 级重叠不等于记忆曝光"的零结果。

## Key Contributions

- **CWE-Trace benchmark:** 834 context-aware vulnerable–patched samples from 417 Linux kernel CVEs across 74 CWEs, validated by a two-reviewer consensus protocol, distinguishing CVE-level overlap from true function-level memorization exposure.
- **Leakage-free temporal split:** A pre-2025 historical set (PBD) and a post-cutoff 2025 set (LFD), with model checkpoints pinned to specific HuggingFace SHAs (two-tier provenance) to rule out post-release contamination.
- **Two novel diagnostic metrics:** DFI (Directional Failure Index) reveals stable paranoid-vs-skeptical decision bias; HDD (Hierarchical Distance and Direction) measures shortest-path distance and error direction in the CWE-1000 tree, separating shallow family recognition from precise root-cause understanding.
- **Systematic fine-tuning study:** A 3×5 grid (Qwen3-4B, DeepSeek-R1-32B, Llama3.1-8B × Devign, LineVul, PrimeVul, MegaVul, VDISC) yielding 15 LoRA variants evaluated under matched contamination controls and a unified three-task objective.
- **Function-level data-quality audit:** Quantifies ~75% label-conflict rate in PrimeVul/MegaVul and ~31% CWE misclassification among contaminated samples, mechanistically explaining why CVE-level contamination produces no memorization shortcut.

## Methodology

CWE-Trace extracts vulnerable (pre-fix) and patched (post-fix) code from Linux kernel commits, building manually labeled L1 (single-function) and L2 (multi-function/multi-file) blocks. From 1,020 raw samples, two reviewers validated 628 PBD (≤2024) and 206 LFD (2025) samples, excluding false positives, contextual gaps, and singleton CWEs. CWE-664 dominates both splits (43.3% PBD / 60.2% LFD).

**Three tasks** (all zero-shot, to isolate pretraining/fine-tuning from prompt engineering):
1. **Non-targeted detection (VD/PV):** predict vulnerable/safe/abstain with no hint.
2. **Targeted detection (TVD/TPV):** given a CWE hint (e.g., "check for CWE-416"), test hypothesis verification.
3. **CWE classification (CWE-C):** produce a ranked CWE-1000 candidate list, scored by Top-k accuracy, MRR, RootMicro/RootMacro@1, and HDD.

**Metrics:** Standard Accuracy (abstentions counted as errors), Coverage, Top-k, MRR, Root-Taxonomy Accuracy, HDD (Distance + DirectionGap + direction profile), DFI (signed decision asymmetry), CGR (Contextual Gain Ratio from hints).

**Models:** 8 vanilla LLMs — code-specialized (CodeLlama 7B, StarCoder2 7B, Qwen3-Coder 30B) and general (Mistral v0.3 7B, Llama3.1 8B, DeepSeek-R1 7B-distill, Qwen3 4B, GPT-4.1-mini). Plus 15 LoRA fine-tuned variants.

**Fine-tuning setup:** LoRA rank r=16, α=32, dropout=0 on all attention and feed-forward projections (q,k,v,o,gate,up,down_proj); lr 2e-4, cosine decay, 5% warmup, weight decay 0.01, grad clip 1.0, batch size 64, one epoch, bfloat16. Vulnerable-class oversampling (15–20× for PrimeVul/MegaVul/VDISC). Inference at temperature=0 with SHAs pinned.

## Results & Highlights

- **Detection is near-chance with extreme directional bias.** Overall accuracy clusters at 49–53% (chance=50%). Best vanilla model CodeLlama reaches only 52.1%. The ~50% average hides stark failure modes:
  - DeepSeek-R1: strongly paranoid, DFI +94.8 pp (PBD) / +90.8 pp (LFD) — flags nearly everything vulnerable.
  - GPT-4.1-mini: strongly skeptical, DFI −85.5 pp (PBD) / −89.3 pp (LFD) — predicts "safe" and misses most vulnerabilities.
  - Qwen3-4B: near-total abstention, 36.9% coverage, 11.9% overall.
- **Fine-tuning shifts threshold, not policy.** Qwen3-4B gains +27.5 to +41.5 pp mainly by overcoming abstention (coverage 36.9%→>91%); Llama3.1 is neutral-to-harmful (Devign-FT collapses to 1.3% overall / 4.1% coverage on PBD); DeepSeek-R1 degrades (−0.6 to −29.1 pp on LFD), often amplifying its paranoid prior.
- **Label-space transfer is flat.** Gains on CWEs inside vs outside the fine-tuning label space are nearly identical (e.g., Qwen3-4B on LFD), indicating a global response-policy shift rather than CWE-specific learning.
- **Contamination has no detectable effect.** No consistent advantage for seen-CVE samples; 84% of nominally contaminated samples carry no usable memorization signal because root-cause functions are absent/cross-mapped, and ~31% carry CWE misclassification. 59.62% of PBD CVEs appear in some fine-tuning corpus (MegaVul 54.72%, PrimeVul 33.96%, LineVul 18.11%) yet confer no benefit.
- **Detection and understanding are decoupled.** DeepSeek-R1, the weakest binary detector, gains the most in coarse CWE classification; StarCoder2 leads LFD coarse root-classification at 61.0% Micro@1 (Qwen3-4B 59.2%), but Macro@1 stays at 9.7–19.3% (PBD), showing concentration on dominant root families.
- **Exact CWE understanding is essentially absent.** Exact CWE Top-1 ranking stays below 1.3%.
- **Non-LLM baselines reinforce the context bottleneck.** Semgrep recall is 0.3% (PBD) / 0.0% (LFD); DeepWukong predicts everything as safe; GraphCodeBERT reaches 61.5%/62.5% F1 only on the ≤512-token subset.

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are Arastoo Zibaeirad and Marco Vieira of the University of North Carolina at Charlotte (azibaeir, marco.vieira@charlotte.edu), a group with a documented software-engineering/security-reliability track record (prior EDCC empirical work on combining static analysis with ML). The study is methodologically careful: pinned HuggingFace commit SHAs for reproducibility, bfloat16 with temperature=0, a two-reviewer manual validation protocol, a 3×5 fine-tuning grid, and an explicit threats-to-validity section covering internal/external/construct validity. The paper self-positions against the closest prior contamination-aware benchmark (Ullah et al., 228 samples / 8 CWEs, no fine-tuning) and dataset-level label-noise audits (Croft et al.), claiming to add function-level distribution-shift verification, multi-backbone fine-tuning, and diagnostic decomposition.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate-High):** The "calibration without comprehension" framing is the genuine contribution, and DFI/HDD are useful, transferable behavioral diagnostics. The mechanistic, function-level explanation of the *null* contamination result (absent root-cause functions + 75% label-conflict + 31% CWE misclassification) is a real advance over dataset-level audits. However, the headline finding "LLMs are bad at vulnerability detection" is well-trodden ground; the value lies in the *why* and the rigor, not the direction of the result.
- **Rigor (High):** Strong experimental hygiene — temporal split, pinned SHAs, unified task objectives across datasets, oversampling rationale, conservative temperature=0 justification (observed DFI magnitudes far exceed plausible sampling variance). The 3×5 grid plus 8 vanilla models is a substantial evaluation.
- **Reproducibility (Moderate-High):** Checkpoints, SHAs, hyperparameters, and dataset stats are all reported; the framework is described as language-agnostic and HuggingFace-hosted. The main reproducibility friction is the manual two-reviewer dataset construction (834 samples), which is labor-intensive to recreate exactly; HDD is also tied to a specific CWE-1000 version.
- **Significance (Moderate-High for SE/security; Moderate for general LLM-reliability):** For the vulnerability-detection community it is a clear, well-evidenced negative result that should redirect effort toward better-structured supervision and prior-correcting objectives. As an LLM-evaluation methodology paper, DFI and HDD are reusable beyond security.
- **Red flags / overstated claims:**
  - The claim "current LLMs lack reliable security reasoning ... regardless of fine-tuning strategy" is slightly overstated: only LoRA on 3 mid-scale backbones is tested (acknowledged), and the single API model is GPT-4.1-mini — no true frontier model (GPT-5 / Claude Opus-class) is evaluated. "Current LLMs" is therefore partially an extrapolation.
  - Only zero-shot prompting is used; CoT, few-shot, and RAG are explicitly excluded. Modern security workflows routinely use these, so the practical conclusion is narrower than the abstract implies.
  - Severe class imbalance (CWE-664 at 43–60%) depresses Macro@1 partly by construction; the "narrow family concentration" finding is real but amplified by the distribution.
  - Best detector is CodeLlama 7B (an older code model); framing this as representative of "current LLMs" leans on the weakest evidence.
- **No fabrication/leakage concerns** detected; numbers are internally consistent across abstract, tables, and prose.

## Strengths

- Clean, leakage-aware benchmark design with a genuine post-cutoff 2025 split and SHA-pinned provenance.
- DFI and HDD are principled, parameter-free (HDD depends only on the CWE-1000 graph) diagnostics that expose *how* models fail, not just *that* they fail.
- Honest negative-result framing with a mechanistic explanation, rather than cherry-picked positives.
- Function-level contamination audit reveals actionable dataset-quality problems (75% conflict, 31% misclassification) in widely used corpora.
- Unified three-task protocol across all five fine-tuning datasets isolates the supervision signal from objective differences.

## Limitations

- Only LoRA adaptation on three backbones; full fine-tuning and other PEFT methods untested.
- Zero-shot only — no CoT, few-shot, RAG, tool-use, or agentic workflows, which are standard in deployed security pipelines.
- Single language/ecosystem (C / Linux kernel); cross-language replication (Java, Python, Rust) is left to future work.
- No frontier-scale proprietary model beyond GPT-4.1-mini.
- Manual dataset construction does not scale and is hard to reproduce verbatim; HDD tied to a CWE-1000 version.
- Context blocks do not reconstruct full build environments (includes/macros), which may affect hardware-specific paths.
- DFI magnitude could in principle shrink at non-zero temperature (the authors argue conservatively that it would not erase the effect).

## Relevance

与你的兴趣高度契合：这是一篇典型的 **LLM 推理评测（LLM Inference Evaluation）** 与 **ML 可靠性/失效模式分析** 工作——它系统量化了 LLM 在漏洞检测推理任务上的输出稳定性、方向性偏差与微调后的行为变化，并提供了可迁移的诊断指标（DFI/HDD）。对"评估模型输出一致性、失效模式分析"方向有直接方法论参考价值，尽管其应用域（系统软件安全）偏离你的核心（训练稳定性/可解释性/数值精度）。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the stable directional priors (DFI) reflect a representation-geometry property of the backbone? Could probing/SAE analysis localize the paranoid-vs-skeptical decision circuit, connecting DFI to mechanistic interpretability?
- Would contrastive or DPO-style training on paired vulnerable–patched examples actually correct the threshold shift, or merely move it—i.e., is "calibration without comprehension" a fundamental ceiling for next-token objectives?
- How do frontier models (GPT-5, Claude Opus 4.x) and structured prompting (CoT/RAG) change the picture, given zero-shot was the only regime tested?
- Does the function-level distribution-shift explanation generalize to other contaminated benchmarks (e.g., in math/code reasoning), suggesting a broader "contamination without memorization" phenomenon?
- Can HDD/DFI be adapted as general reliability diagnostics beyond security—e.g., for measuring directional bias in safety refusals or calibration in factual QA?
