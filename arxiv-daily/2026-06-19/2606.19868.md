> **一句话总结：** 本文将 24 种仅靠 API 可观测信号的黑盒 LLM 不确定性估计方法系统归为言语化、采样、解释、多智能体与混合五大类，并在 4 个模型 × 4 个数据集（开放与封闭式 QA）上以 AUROC/ECE/Brier 统一评测；结论是没有单一方法持续占优，但显式对候选答案进行推理/比较（如 VPD、TopK、DiNCo、SteerConf）以及融合多信号的混合方法在多数设定下表现最稳健。

> **TL;DR:** A systematic review + unified benchmark of 24 black-box uncertainty estimation (UE) methods for LLMs (restricted-API, no logits/hidden states), organized into five categories (verbalization-, sampling-, explanation-, multi-agent-, hybrid-based), evaluated across 4 models (Qwen3-4B, Qwen3-30B, DeepSeek-V3.2, GPT-5-mini) × 4 datasets (TriviaQA, HotpotQA, CoQA, TruthfulQA-MC) using AUROC/ECE/Brier. No single method dominates; methods that reason over/compare answer-space candidates and hybrid methods combining multiple signals are generally most robust.

## 核心贡献

- **统一五类分类法**：首次将黑盒 UE 方法系统梳理为言语化（verbalization-based，含数值型与语言型两个子类）、采样（sampling-based，含直接采样、NLI 关系矩阵、连续语义空间、扰动四类）、解释（explanation-based）、多智能体（multi-agent）与混合（hybrid）五大类，弥补现有综述对黑盒场景粒度不足的缺陷。
- **统一评测框架与 24 方法基准**：在统一推理设置（统一 CoT 提示、温度、采样数、NLI 模型 deBERTa-v3-large、嵌入模型 all-MiniLM-L6-v2）下，对 24 种代表性方法在 4 个模型 × 4 个数据集上做横向比较，并从判别力（discrimination）与校准（calibration）两个角度评估。
- **关键实证洞察**：（1）开放式 QA 中，基于 NLI 关系矩阵的采样方法（SelfCheck、Deg、KLE、SNNE）普遍优于纯嵌入相似度方法；（2）采样聚合的言语化方法与混合方法（DiNCo、SteerConf、VPD）综合表现最强；（3）封闭式 QA 中，专门为多选题设计的方法（T3、UF）显著占优。
- **可复现资源**：公开基准数据与统一评测框架，并维护公开项目页 Black-BoxUE-Hub 以汇总相关论文与资源。

## Key Contributions

- **Unified five-category taxonomy**: First systematic organization of black-box UE methods into verbalization-based (numeric + linguistic), sampling-based (direct, NLI-relation-matrix, continuous-semantic-space, perturbation), explanation-based, multi-agent, and hybrid, with finer granularity than prior surveys that mix white-box and black-box.
- **Unified evaluation framework + 24-method benchmark**: All 24 methods compared under a single inference configuration (unified CoT prompting, temperatures, sample counts, NLI model deBERTa-v3-large, embedding model all-MiniLM-L6-v2) across 4 models × 4 datasets, evaluated from both discrimination and calibration angles.
- **Actionable empirical insights**: (1) In open-ended QA, NLI-relation-matrix sampling methods (SelfCheck, Deg, KLE, SNNE) generally outperform pure embedding-similarity methods; (2) sampling-aggregated verbalization and hybrid methods (DiNCo, SteerConf, VPD) are the strongest overall; (3) in closed-ended QA, methods purpose-built for multiple-choice (T3, UF) dominate.
- **Reproducible artifacts**: Releases benchmark data, the unified evaluation framework, and maintains a public Black-BoxUE-Hub project page.

## Methodology

**Problem formulation.** Given input x, a black-box LLM produces a predicted answer y and a set of externally observable signals (verbalized scores, sampled responses, reasoning traces, multi-agent interactions). A UE method outputs either an uncertainty score U(x) or a confidence score C(x, y)∈[0,1]. A good score must satisfy (i) strong ranking/discrimination (Eq.1: C ranks correct answers above incorrect ones) and (ii) calibration (Eq.2: p(y=ŷ | C=q) = q). All method outputs are monotonically transformed to a confidence score for unified comparison.

**Taxonomy (5 categories, 24 methods).** Verbalization-based (CoT, TopK, VPD, Ling, MarConf); sampling-based split into (a) direct/consistency (SE, SelfCheck, SEU, ConvexHull, SINdex, SNNE, LUQ, GU, MDUQ), (b) NLI/graph-spectral (EigV, Deg, Ecc, KLE, SeSE), (c) perturbation (SPUQ, InvE); explanation-based (COTA, T3, PathWeight/PathConv/Topo-UQ/IUQ); multi-agent (Collab, ArgLLMs, DAE, BSDetector); hybrid (UF, SteerConf, DiNCo).

**Experimental setup.**
- Datasets/tasks: TriviaQA, HotpotQA, CoQA (open-ended QA, LLM-as-judge correctness via GPT-5.1) + TruthfulQA (multiple-choice, closed-ended, exact-match correctness). 500 evaluation samples per dataset (after reserving 50 for tuning), fixed random seed.
- Models: Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507, Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct-2507, DeepSeek-V3.2, GPT-5-mini (open + closed, small + large).
- Inference: CoT generation by default; temperature 1.0 with 5 samples for sampling methods; temperature 0.1 otherwise; max 8192 tokens; auxiliary models deBERTa-v3-large-mnli (NLI) and all-MiniLM-L6-v2 (embeddings).
- Metrics: AUROC (discrimination, scale-invariant — primary for sampling methods), ECE (10-bin calibration), and Brier Score (combined calibration + sharpness, penalizes overconfident errors).

## Results & Highlights

- **No single method dominates** across all settings; performance depends on task format, dataset, and model family.
- **Sampling aggregation consistently beats single-pass verbalization** — repeated elicitation yields a more stable uncertainty signal and narrows the gap between CoT/TopK/VPD.
- **Open-ended QA best methods**: NLI-relation-matrix sampling methods (SelfCheck 78.28, Deg 78.18, KLE 78.08, SNNE 78.05 avg AUROC) beat pure embedding methods (SEU 76.54, SINdex 77.07). Overall strongest: **SteerConf** (best overall discrimination), **sampling-aggregated VPD** (best overall calibration), **DiNCo** (strong across all metrics). VPD is strong even single-pass — LLMs already contain useful UE signals elicitable via good prompting.
- **VPD advantages** stem from implicit multi-candidate reasoning within one generation + a "None of the above" option that captures residual uncertainty (helps on hard datasets like HotpotQA).
- **Ling (linguistic verbalization) consistently weak**: natural-language hedging does not map reliably to numeric confidence.
- **Explanation-based & multi-agent methods are relatively weak** in open-ended settings (e.g., COTA's reasoning-chain completeness is insufficient; multi-agent cost without proportional gain).
- **Closed-ended QA (TruthfulQA-MC)**: methods purpose-built for multiple-choice dominate — **T3** (per-option explanation + joint comparison) and **UF** (question-level uncertainty × answer fidelity) — because explicit candidate modeling is highly beneficial when the answer space is known. TopK slightly beats VPD on AUROC here, while VPD wins on calibration (it outputs a normalized distribution).
- **Cross-model**: Qwen3-30B and DeepSeek-V3.2 give higher AUROC; GPT-5-mini weaker on AUROC for some methods (more diverse surface forms weaken similarity-based methods) but good on calibration. Calibration metrics generally decrease (improve) as base accuracy improves.
- **Sample-size effect**: sampling-based methods improve steadily in AUROC as sample count grows; verbalization-based methods fluctuate.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate. The contribution is a survey + unified benchmark, not a new method. Its value is consolidation and the broad 24-method head-to-head under an explicitly black-box framing, which the literature genuinely lacked. Algorithmic novelty is nil, but systematization novelty is real.
- **Rigor**: Strong on experimental control (unified prompts, temperatures, sample counts, NLI/embedding auxiliary models, LLM-judge with stated judge model GPT-5.1, fixed seed, 500 samples). Metrics (AUROC/ECE/Brier) are standard and well-justified; the authors correctly note that AUROC is the fairer comparator for sampling methods because their scores are not natively calibrated to [0,1], while ECE/Brier need post-hoc mapping. Caveats: only 500 samples per dataset limits statistical power; no confidence intervals or significance tests are reported (rankings could be within noise for closely-performing methods); only a single judge model (GPT-5.1) for open-ended correctness adjudication is a single point of failure for the ground-truth labels themselves.
- **Reproducibility**: High in principle — code + data released, framework detailed, public Black-BoxUE-Hub. Real reproducibility risks: API nondeterminism, rate limits, and dependency on proprietary GPT-5-mini/GPT-5.1 judge whose behavior may drift over time.
- **Significance**: High practical relevance — black-box/API-only is the dominant real-world deployment regime, and the actionable guidance (favor candidate-reasoning + hybrid; NLI > embedding similarity; purpose-built methods for MC) is directly useful to practitioners.
- **Red flags**:
  - 2 authors for a 24-method × 4-model benchmark is a large reimplementation effort; faithfulness of each method to its original paper is hard to verify (the authors note simplifications, e.g., DiNCo uses a TopK-like K=5 prompt instead of the original procedure).
  - "No single method dominates" is expected and weak as a headline; the more useful claims (VPD/SteerConf/DiNCo strong; NLI > embedding) need error bars to be trustworthy.
  - Closed-ended evaluation uses only TruthfulQA-MC (one dataset), so the "purpose-built MC methods dominate" conclusion rests on a single data point.
  - Inclusion of as-yet-unpublished methods (arXiv 2025, ICLR 2026, NeurIPS 2025) means some baselines are themselves unvalidated.

## Strengths

- Genuinely unified, fine-grained five-category taxonomy filling a real gap (prior surveys mixed white/black-box).
- Broad, well-controlled empirical comparison: 24 methods, 4 models (open + closed), 4 datasets (open + closed QA), three complementary metrics.
- Thoughtful analysis: explains *why* NLI > embedding (factual consistency vs broad similarity), why VPD works (implicit candidate comparison + "None of the above"), why Ling fails (unreliable numeric mapping), why CoQA/HotpotQA calibration degrades.
- Practitioner-actionable guidance rather than just rankings.
- Releases code, data, and a maintained project hub.

## Limitations

- Survey/benchmark only — proposes no new UE method.
- No reported confidence intervals or significance testing; 500 samples/dataset is modest.
- Single judge model (GPT-5.1) for open-ended ground truth — a potential single point of failure.
- Closed-ended conclusions rest on a single dataset (TruthfulQA-MC).
- Several baselines are themselves recent/unpublished (2025/2026), so some are unvalidated.
- API nondeterminism and proprietary-model drift threaten long-term reproducibility.
- Cost analysis is qualitative ("Computation" column Low/Medium/High in Table 1); no dollar-cost or latency numbers, so cost-normalized method selection is not possible.

## Relevance

与你的"LLM Inference Evaluation"（推理质量、输出一致性、可复现性评测）方向高度契合——本文正是系统量化 LLM 输出可信度与一致性的评测工作；同时与你"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"方向互补：在 logits/hidden states 不可得的黑盒场景下，它系统考察了哪些可观测信号（言语化置信度、采样一致性、NLI 关系、多智能体分歧）最能反映模型内部不确定性，为白盒可解释性研究提供了行为侧的对照基线。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How stable are the rankings under bootstrap confidence intervals / paired significance tests at N=500? Are VPD/SteerConf/DiNCo statistically distinguishable from the next tier?
- Does the NLI > embedding-similarity finding hold when a stronger embedding model (e.g., large instruction-tuned encoder) replaces all-MiniLM-L6-v2, or is it an artifact of the weak encoder?
- How sensitive are verbalization-based methods to prompt phrasing and to the choice of judge model for open-ended correctness?
- Can the closed-ended conclusions (T3/UF dominate) be replicated on additional MC benchmarks (MMLU, ARC, CommonsenseQA) beyond TruthfulQA-MC?
- What is the cost-normalized picture — e.g., AUROC-per-thousand-API-calls — given multi-agent (Collab uses 5+5 agents) and sampling methods are expensive?
- For reasoning/agent settings where the "answer" is a trajectory, how do these UE signals transfer, and can DiNCo-style within-generation candidate comparison extend to step-level uncertainty?
- Could black-box signals (sampling consistency, NLI graphs) be fused with white-box signals (hidden states, SAE features) to outperform either alone?
