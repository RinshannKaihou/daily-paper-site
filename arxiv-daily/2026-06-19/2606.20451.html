SSH-Net: A Deep Neural Network for Predicting Failure Time Distribution Functions under Competing Risks with Application to GPU Data

arXiv: 2606.20451

Date: 2026-06-19

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20451

一句话总结： 提出 SSH-Net，一种结构化的分段危险率深度神经网络，将网络结构显式绑定到数据层级（柜体位置 vs 柜内层级），用分段常数风险函数 + 平滑惩罚的惩罚对数似然损失预测 GPU 等复杂系统在竞争风险下的失效时间分布，在模拟和 Titan 超算 GPU 数据上 Brier/AUC/RMSE 均优于 DeepHit 和 NFG。
TL;DR: SSH-Net is a cause-specific competing-risks survival neural network whose sub-network topology is tied to the hierarchical structure of the input covariates (global cabinet location vs. within-cabinet cage/slot/node), outputs piecewise-constant cause-specific hazards trained with a P-spline-style smoothness penalty on the log-likelihood, and outperforms DeepHit and NFG on Brier score, AUC, and CIF RMSE in both simulation and the Cray XK7 Titan GPU failure dataset.

核心贡献

  • 结构化网络架构：提出 SSH-Net，将网络结构与数据结构显式绑定——为"全局"协变量（柜体空间位置 x_il）和"低层级"协变量（柜内 cage/slot/node x_ic）分别设计子网络 E 与 L，经共享层 S（节点数远小于 p_l+p_c，起降维作用）后送入各失效模式子网络 M_1…M_K。
  • 分段常数风险输出 + 平滑惩罚：以 log(常数风险) 作为网络输出，避免对连续时间离散化；损失为带平滑惩罚（对相邻常数风险差分，D 矩阵 P-spline 风格）的负对数似然，惩罚系数 λ_k = λ*_k × (Σδ_ik)^(1/3) 随失效样本数自适应缩放。
  • 超参数可由领域知识引导：因 J_k（时间分箱数）与"恒定瞬时失效率持续时长"直接对应，研究者可基于系统背景（如 Titan 运行约 8 年 → 70 bins ≈ 41 天恒定）预设合理网格，降低调参成本。
  • 公平的模拟评测设计：用潜在失效时间模型（Min et al. 2025 的 Weibull 设定，非 cause-specific，不偏向 SSH-Net）生成模拟数据，从而可用 RMSE 对比真实 CIF，弥补以往文献只用 Brier/AUC 的不足。
  • GPU 可靠性应用：首次将神经网络竞争风险方法应用于 Titan GPU 数据（19,319 个 GPU，1127 次 OTB、3093 次 DBE 失效），给出 CIF 预测、关键影响因子（cage 位置最显著）与失效类型间相对风险。

Key Contributions

  • A structured, interpretable neural network whose topology mirrors the data hierarchy (separate sub-networks E and L for lower-level vs. global covariates, a small shared layer S for dimensionality reduction, and per-failure-mode sub-networks M_k), which both improves prediction and makes hyperparameter tuning data-informed rather than guess-based.
  • A piecewise-constant cause-specific hazard output with a smoothness-penalized negative log-likelihood loss (D-matrix difference penalty on adjacent hazard bins), avoiding time discretization and controlling overfitting — a combination not used in prior piecewise-hazard survival NNs.
  • A domain-knowledge-guided hyperparameter scheme: bin count J_k and penalty λ_k are anchored to physical interpretation (duration of constant failure rate), shrinking the search grid.
  • A simulation design that enables fair, truth-grounded evaluation: data generated from a latent failure-time model (not cause-specific) so the RMSE of predicted CIFs against the true CIF can be computed, beyond the usual time-dependent Brier/AUC.
  • First application of deep competing-risks survival models to GPU reliability data (Cray XK7 Titan), quantifying cage-level effects and OTB-vs-DBE relative risk.

Methodology

SSH-Net operates under a cause-specific competing-risks framework. For unit i with covariates x_i = (x_ic, x_il), failure type G_i ∈ {1,…,K}, and event indicator δ_ik, it models the cause-specific hazard h(t,k|x_i), assumed piecewise constant over equally-spaced time bins of width Δ_k. The network outputs log(h_{jk|x_i}) for each bin j.

Architecture (Figure 1):

  • Sub-network E (for x_ic, dim p_c) and sub-network L (for x_il, dim p_l): node counts tied to p_c, p_l; two fully-connected layers each to capture main effects + 2nd-order interactions; ReLU activation (avoids vanishing gradient).
  • Shared layer S: deliberately small relative to p_c+p_l, acting as a learned data-reduction bottleneck; Tanh activation (zero-symmetric, reduces bias per Li et al. 2020).
  • Per-mode sub-networks M_1…M_K: node count chosen near the median of the J_k grid (latent variables ↔ bins).
  • Output layers O_1…O_K: constant log cause-specific hazards. Different J_k per failure type adds flexibility and mitigates imbalanced censor rates.

Loss is the negative penalized log-likelihood:

l = − Σ_k Σ_i [ δ_ik log h(t_i,k|x_i) − H(t_i,k|x_i) − λ_k h_ik^T D h_ik ],

where D is the J_k×J_k second-difference (P-spline) penalty matrix and λ_k = λ*_k·(Σδ_ik)^(1/3) scales with observed failures.

Hyperparameters: J_k ∈ {10,20,30,50(,70)}, λ*_k ∈ {0,0.5,1,1.5}, layers in M_k ∈ {1,2,3}; selected via penalized log-likelihood on a validation set. Optimizer AdamW, lr 1e-3, batch 100, 100 epochs, early stop 10.

Baselines: DeepHit (Lee et al. 2018, discrete) and NFG / Neural Fine-Gray (Jeanselme et al. 2023, monotonic CIF). SSH-Net's structure-derived node count fixed at 50 for the Titan data.

Results & Highlights

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty — moderate. Piecewise-constant-hazard survival NNs already exist (Fornili et al. 2014; Kvamme & Borgan 2021), as do competing-risks NNs (DeepHit, NFG, Danks & Yau 2022, Wang & Sun 2022). The genuinely new pieces are (a) the data-structure-tied segmented topology (E/L/S/M_k) and (b) the adjacent-hazard smoothness penalty. Both are reasonable and well-motivated, but each is an incremental combination of established ideas (P-spline penalty + piecewise hazard NN + branched sub-networks) rather than a new paradigm.
  • Rigor — good. The simulation is designed to be fair: data come from a latent failure-time Weibull model (Min et al. 2025), not a cause-specific model, so it does not structurally favor SSH-Net. 100 replicates × 8 scenarios, three complementary metrics, and truth-grounded RMSE strengthen the claims beyond the typical Brier/AUC-only evaluations.
  • Reproducibility — moderate, leaning weak. Hyperparameter grids, optimizer settings (Table 2), and architecture details are reported, and the Titan dataset is public (Ostrouchov et al. 2020). However, no code or model checkpoints are mentioned as released, and some details (exact initialization, random seeds, per-fold test-set sizes) are unspecified.
  • Significance — niche. Validated on a single GPU dataset plus simulation; generalization to other hardware fleets or non-engineering domains is not demonstrated. The engineering contribution (interpretable structure for hierarchical covariates) is the most transferable idea.
  • Red flags / caveats:
  1. Absolute margins are small: Brier gaps vs NFG are often <0.2 (×1e-2); whether they are practically meaningful vs noise is not statistically tested (no CI/significance on the model-comparison differences).
  2. Censoring is extreme (up to 97.4% for OTB), so CIF predictions for the rarer mode rest on few events.
  3. Fairness of comparison is debatable: SSH-Net's node count is fixed at 50 based on its structure, while DeepHit/NFG tune nodes more broadly; DeepHit was run on only 2 of 8 simulation scenarios "because of longer computation time," weakening the head-to-head.
  4. Only time-to-first-failure is modeled (later failures are correlated and excluded), and time-varying covariates are not supported (acknowledged as future work).
  5. The "structure informed by data" claim is only as good as the analyst's knowledge of the hierarchy; on a flat dataset the advantage may vanish.

Strengths

  • Genuinely interpretable architecture that maps sub-networks to physical data hierarchy, aiding both prediction and hyperparameter selection.
  • Smoothness-penalized piecewise-hazard loss elegantly avoids time discretization and overfitting.
  • Strong, fair simulation protocol with truth-grounded RMSE — a methodological improvement over prior competing-risks NN evaluations.
  • Concrete, well-analyzed real-world application with physically meaningful findings (cage/cooling effect, relative-risk timing).
  • Uncertainty quantification via Monte-Carlo dropout adds practical value for reliability decisions.

Limitations

  • No public code release indicated; reproducibility hinges on reimplementation.
  • Only two baselines; newer/stronger competing-risks NNs (e.g., transformer-based Wang & Sun 2022) not compared.
  • Wins over NFG are consistent but small in absolute terms and lack significance testing.
  • Extreme censor rates (especially OTB ~94%) limit confidence on the rarer mode.
  • Restricted to static covariates and first-failure-only; cannot exploit sensor streams or recurrent failures.
  • Single real dataset; external validation on other HPC/hardware fleets is missing.
  • Gaussian-process / spatial-correlation structure for cabinet locations left to future work — the current one-hot + dense-layer treatment may under-model spatial smoothness.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Would replacing the dense one-hot treatment of cabinet location with a Gaussian-process / graph neural layer (as the authors hint) materially close the spatial-smoothness gap, and does it change the OTB/DBE relative-risk timing?
  • Are the small Brier/AUC margins over NFG statistically significant across the 100 replicates, or within Monte-Carlo noise given the extreme censoring?
  • How does SSH-Net behave under time-varying covariates (e.g., live temperature/ ECC counters), which is the realistic regime for SDC detection on production GPU fleets?
  • Can the smoothness-penalized piecewise-hazard loss + structured-segmented topology transfer to LLM training-run anomaly/failure time prediction (e.g., time-to-loss-spike, time-to-node-drop) where hierarchical (rack/node/replica) covariates also exist?
  • Does the method extend beyond first-failure to recurrent competing-risk events, which dominate real GPU operation logs?
  • Is there a principled way to choose the shared-layer size |S|, or is it currently just "smaller than p_l+p_c"?
