> **一句话总结：** 在相同训练条件下系统对比 VLM 的三种视觉信息注入方式（in-context 拼接、gated cross-attention、attention 注入），发现 in-context 注入会让视觉 token 在 LLM 内部经历渐进式语义演化、逐步偏向高频特征并与语言空间收敛，从而在 OCR/视频等细粒度任务上更强，而层间注入的视觉表示则保持离散、低频且与语言空间正交。

> **TL;DR:** Under identical training conditions, the authors compare three VLM integration paradigms (in-context, gated cross-attention, attention-only injection) and reveal a "hidden evolution": in-context visual tokens are progressively reshaped across LLM layers toward high-frequency features and converge with the language space, whereas layer-wise injection stays discontinuous, low-frequency, and orthogonal to language — explaining in-context's edge on OCR/video tasks and showing attention allocation alone does not explain performance.

## 核心贡献

- 首次在**严格控制训练条件**（相同数据混合、优化配方、token 预算、连接器）下，公平对比 in-context 注入（IN-CT）与两类层间注入（LW-GC gated cross-attention、LW-AT attention 注入），把性能差异归因于**集成机制本身**而非数据或规模等混淆因素。
- 提出"**disguised visual context**"概念并给出四层机制性证据：视觉 token 进入 LLM 时是缺乏语言结构的原始信号，其后续演化完全取决于集成范式。
- 通过 **CKA、Fourier 频域分析、PCA 子空间投影、Attention Mass** 四种互补分析，刻画了视觉 token 在各层的演化轨迹、捕获频率特征、与语言空间的对齐程度及生成时的利用方式。
- 关键反直觉发现：**注意力分配本身不足以解释性能差距**——决定性能的是各层视觉表示的"质量"（频率特性与对齐），而非模型在哪些层"看"图像。
- 提出混合集成（IN-CT + LW-AT）探索性实验，证明同时获得高频与低频视觉特征可进一步提升性能（但 token 数翻倍，非实用建议）。

## Key Contributions

- First **controlled apples-to-apples comparison** of the two dominant VLM integration families (in-context vs layer-wise injection), isolating integration as the key variable while fixing data mixtures, optimisation recipe, token budgets, and connector architecture.
- Introduces the framing of visual tokens as **"disguised visual context"** that must be reshaped inside the LLM, and shows the integration paradigm dictates *how* and *whether* this reshaping happens.
- A **four-pronged mechanistic analysis toolkit**: token-level CKA for representational continuity, Fourier amplitude for captured frequency band, 3D-PCA for cross-modal (language-space) alignment, and Attention Mass for generation-time utilisation.
- Establishes that **attention allocation is insufficient to explain performance gaps**; representational quality at each layer is the critical driver — a corrective to prior analysis that equates "where attention flows" with "what the model uses."
- An exploratory **hybrid IN-CT + LW-AT** model combining high- and low-frequency visual features, achieving the best scores but at doubled visual-token cost — positioned as a design hint, not a deployable recipe.

## Methodology

**Three integration paradigms (Fig. 1):**
- **IN-CT (in-context injection):** projected visual tokens concatenated with text at the input (`h0 = [V̂; Ht]`) and processed through all standard transformer blocks, including FFNs.
- **LW-GC (layer-wise gated cross-attention, Flamingo-style):** text-only input; per-layer visual features injected via gated cross-attention + gated FFN, both modulated by `tanh` scalars initialised to zero. Tested both every-layer and every-4-layer insertion.
- **LW-AT (layer-wise attention-only injection, PAL-style):** visual features injected directly into the LLM's existing attention keys/values, explicitly bypassing FFNs.

All paradigms share an identical MLP+GELU connector and an identical number of visual tokens (no Perceiver compression).

**Backbones & data:** Qwen2.5-0.5B and Qwen2.5-3B as LLM backbones, SigLIP2-So400m as vision encoder. Two-stage training: connector pretraining on BLIP-558K, then instruction tuning on LLaVA-NeXT (~700K) or LLaVA-OneVision (~1.6M). An OCR-filtered 500K ablation removes text-centric samples.

**Four analyses:**
1. *Representational evolution* — token-level CKA between layer pairs (CKA is rotation/scale invariant, unlike cosine).
2. *Frequency capture* — relative log amplitude of Fourier-transformed hidden states (high→low = fine detail→global structure).
3. *Language-space alignment* — 3D-PCA of image vs text token hidden states across layers.
4. *Generation-time utilisation* — Attention Mass (summed attention weight from generated token to visual tokens), plus inspection of learned LW-GC gate values.

**Controlled constraint:** all comparisons use a *constant-epoch* (not equal-compute) budget; authors flag this as favourable to IN-CT, since IN-CT processes vision through all FFNs (more compute/epoch) while LW variants trade per-epoch alignment for throughput.

## Results & Highlights

- **Single-image (14 benchmarks):** IN-CT best overall, LW-AT second, LW-GC lags badly — most pronounced on OCR/Chart (e.g., 0.5B OCRBench: IN-CT 1236 vs LW-GC 671; AI2D: IN-CT 53.8 vs LW-GC 7.6). On LLaVA-OV the IN-CT vs LW-AT gap narrows and LW-AT occasionally wins.
- **Multi-image & multi-view (16 benchmarks):** LW-GC competitive only when trained on single-image data; advantage disappears once multi-image data (LLaVA-OV) is added.
- **Video:** same ordering (IN-CT > LW-AT > LW-GC), hypothesised to share the OCR mechanism (cross-token composition).
- **OCR-filtered ablation:** removing text-heavy data hurts IN-CT broadly but selectively helps/hurts layer-wise models — supporting the claim that in-context visual tokens can mutually attend across patches to compose spatially distributed info (e.g., characters spanning patches), which static layer-wise tokens cannot.
- **Four findings:**
  - *Finding 1 (CKA):* IN-CT visual tokens evolve smoothly across layers like text tokens; LW-GC/LW-AT show severe discontinuities (no residual path through the LLM).
  - *Finding 2 (Fourier):* IN-CT progressively shifts to high-frequency (fine detail) in early/middle layers, then drops back to low-frequency in final layers (consolidation); LW-GC/LW-AT stay low-frequency and erratic.
  - *Finding 3 (PCA):* IN-CT visual and text tokens converge into a shared subspace, closing the modality gap by final layers; LW-AT visual representations stay orthogonal to language throughout.
  - *Finding 4 (Attention Mass):* attention allocation is task-dependent and similar between IN-CT and LW-AT, yet IN-CT still wins — so representation *quality*, not attention, drives the gap. LW-GC is gated by fixed learned scalars that don't adapt to input.
- **Language preservation (HellaSwag / IFEVAL):** all paradigms show only mild forgetting; LW-GC best preserves commonsense reasoning, IN-CT/LW-AT better retain instruction-following.
- **Hybrid (IN-CT + LW-AT):** best on most benchmarks (e.g., MuirBench 18.2→, OCRBench 48.8, AI2D 41.1), but doubles visual tokens — exploratory only.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The contribution is not a new architecture but a controlled mechanistic dissection of an under-studied design axis (integration point). The "attention is insufficient — representation quality matters" correction (Finding 4) is a genuinely useful reframing for the interpretability community. The disguised-context framing is memorable but largely repackages known ideas (logit-lens evolution, modality gap) into a comparative narrative.
- **Rigour: Good.** Strong experimental hygiene: fixed data/recipe/token-count/connector, multiple data mixtures, an OCR-filtered ablation to test a specific hypothesis, and four complementary probes (CKA, Fourier, PCA, attention). Caveats (constant-epoch, 0.5B/3B scale) are openly stated.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate-to-Good.** Standard open components (Qwen2.5, SigLIP2, LLaVA-NeXT/OV, BLIP-558K). Connector details and gate initialisation are given; per-layer connectors and exact LW-AT/LW-GC configs are partially deferred to Supplementary. No explicit code-release commitment in the main text (no GitHub link found), which lowers reproducibility confidence.
- **Significance: Moderate.** Practically, the paper mostly confirms the field's drift toward in-context integration and questions the efficiency story of layer-wise methods. The frequency/alignment diagnostics are the most transferable artifacts. Impact depends on whether the analysis toolkit is adopted by others.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - The constant-epoch (not equal-compute) protocol structurally favours IN-CT — the authors acknowledge this but it materially affects the headline "IN-CT wins" framing; a compute-matched comparison could narrow or erase gaps.
  - LW-GC and LW-AT are not trained/tuned with techniques their own cited literature recommends for these paradigms (e.g., dynamic input-conditional gating [74]), so the layer-wise variants may be strawmanned.
  - Scale is limited to 0.5B/3B; whether the frequency/alignment dynamics generalise to 7B+ is asserted, not shown.
  - Fourier "frequency of hidden states" is a proxy whose link to actual image high-frequency content (texture/edges) is asserted, not verified with controlled stimuli.
  - The hybrid experiment doubles tokens and is called impractical — so it mainly serves as confirmatory evidence for Finding 2 rather than a contribution.

## Strengths

- Genuinely fair setup isolating integration as the variable — rare in a literature riddled with confounded comparisons.
- Four complementary lenses (CKA/Fourier/PCA/attention) triangulate the same conclusion from different angles.
- The OCR-filtered ablation is a clever hypothesis-driven test of the "visual tokens need to attend to each other" mechanism.
- Honest about limitations and about the compute asymmetry.

## Limitations

- Constant-epoch protocol favours IN-CT; equal-compute comparison is missing.
- Only 0.5B and 3B backbones — scaling behaviour unverified.
- Layer-wise baselines use arguably suboptimal (static-gated) variants; best-practice dynamic gating not tested.
- Vision-language only; generalisation to audio/sensor modalities (the authors' own PAL domain) is left to future work.
- No public code link in the main text.
- Fourier-amplitude interpretation is correlational; causal linkage to fine-grained task performance is hypothesised, not demonstrated with controlled interventions.

## Relevance

高度契合"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"方向：本文正是机制可解释性工作——用 CKA、频域分析、PCA 子空间投影与 attention 探针刻画视觉 token 在 LLM 各层的表征几何、与语言空间的对齐及信息利用，对理解多模模型内部表征演化与模态鸿沟有直接参考价值；同时也间接关联"LLM Inference Evaluation"（对推理时视觉信息利用的评估）。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Under an **equal-compute** budget (more epochs for the cheaper LW variants), do the IN-CT vs layer-wise gaps persist, shrink, or invert?
- Does adding **input-conditional dynamic gating** (per [74]) to LW-GC/LW-AT recover high-frequency capture and language-space convergence, or is the discontinuity structural?
- Do the Fourier-frequency dynamics replicate at **7B+ scale**, and do they predict OCR/video performance across independent model families (not just Qwen-based)?
- Can the CKA/Fourier/PCA diagnostics be turned into a **training signal or regulariser** (e.g., encouraging smoother evolution or targeted high-frequency injection) rather than just a post-hoc probe?
- Is the "visual tokens must attend to each other" hypothesis causally testable — e.g., via an ablation that selectively blocks cross-patch visual-visual attention in IN-CT?
- How do these integration-driven dynamics transfer to **non-vision modalities** (audio/sensor), where critical frequency content differs?
