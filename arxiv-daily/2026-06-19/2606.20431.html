Sparsity, Superposition, and Forgetting: A Mechanistic Study of Representation Retention in Continual Learning

arXiv: 2606.20431

Date: 2026-06-19

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20431

一句话总结： 本文构建一个可控的合成 toy-world（generator-separator pipeline + 冻结标签规则），用线性表示假说定义表征强度 R、superposition S 与任务 effective rank，借助 SINDy 拟合表征保留动力学 ∂R/∂t = g(R, S, t_since)，发现在 experience replay 下 superposition 随时间上升并在任务边界出现瞬时回落后再恢复，稀疏度越高 superposition 越强但遗忘并不必然发生——只有"低 R、高 S、刚出现"的区域最脆弱。
TL;DR: A controlled synthetic toy-world makes continual-learning forgetting mechanisms measurable: by probing pure-feature embeddings under the linear representation hypothesis, the authors show that higher sparsity increases superposition (with transient boundary dips), that forgetting concentrates in the low-representation-strength/high-overlap regime, and that sparser tasks occupy higher effective rank—nuancing the intuition that "more superposition means more forgetting."

核心贡献

  • 提出 generator-separator 合成 pipeline：固定（冻结）的 Φ: [0,1]^N → R^D 生成信号、Ψ 通过线性规则+softmax 给出标签，特征向量 f 在 [0,1]^N 上稀疏（∥f∥₀ ≤ s），构建可控稀疏度与任务支撑 I_t 的序列任务，绕开真实数据的概念纠缠。
  • 在线性表示假说下形式化定义：表征强度 R(i,t)=∥w_i(t)∥²（纯特征嵌入范数）；superposition S(i,t)=Σⱼ(w_jᵀv_i)²（其他特征向 v_i 的方向投影平方和）；特征保留 F(i,t)=∂R/∂t；任务嵌入的 effective rank（基于奇异值的谱熵）。
  • 用 SINDy（最高 10 阶多项式库、阈值最小二乘、上三角掩码防止未训练特征泄漏）从数据中辨识稀疏微分关系 ∂R/∂t = g(R, S, t_since)，把"重叠是否导致遗忘"转化为可量化的动力学问题。
  • 给出三个 RQ 的实证回答：(RQ1) 越稀疏 superposition 越高且整体随时间上升、任务边界处有瞬时 dip；(RQ2) 遗忘集中在低 R/高 S 区，强表征可缓冲重叠甚至使 F 对 S 的依赖局部反转，t_since 起到"巩固变量"作用；(RQ3) effective rank 随稀疏度上升，且新旧任务在任务到达时刻 rank 出奇地均匀，体现稳定的潜在脚手架。
  • 明确列出 4 项设计性局限（均匀稀疏度、不相交任务支撑、SINDy 多项式族、保留代理 ≠ 端到端准确率）并提供可证伪假设与诊断探针供后续在真实架构上验证。

Key Contributions

  • A controlled synthetic generator-separator toy world with frozen Φ/Ψ and tunable feature sparsity s and disjoint task supports I_t, removing real-data confounds; latent bottleneck m < |I_t| is enforced to induce representational pressure.
  • Formal, linear-representation-hypothesis-grounded quantities: representation strength R(i,t)=∥w_i(t)∥², superposition S(i,t)=Σⱼ(w_jᵀv_i)², feature retention F(i,t)=∂R/∂t, and task effective rank via spectral entropy of embedding-matrix singular values.
  • A SINDy-based identification of the sparse differential relation ∂R/∂t = g(R, S, t_since) using a polynomial library (degree ≤10), standardized inputs, thresholded least squares, and an upper-triangular mask aligned to feature introduction times to prevent leakage from untrained features.
  • Empirical takeaways across three research questions: (RQ1) higher sparsity yields more directional sharing, superposition rises over time with transient boundary dips then recovery; (RQ2) forgetting concentrates in the low-R/high-S regime, with representation strength acting as a moderator and t_since as a consolidation variable; (RQ3) effective rank grows with sparsity and is surprisingly uniform across seen/unseen tasks at arrival time.
  • Falsifiable hypotheses and reusable diagnostics (pure-feature embeddings, overlap metrics, effective-rank traces) plus four by-design limitations (uniform sparsity, disjoint task supports, SINDy polynomial family, retention proxy vs end performance) to bound the scope of conclusions.

Methodology

  • Data generation: Fixed frozen generator Φ: [0,1]^N → R^D (feed-forward) and separator Ψ: [0,1]^N → {1,…,K} (linear rule + softmax). Sparse nonnegative feature vectors f ∈ [0,1]^N with ∥f∥₀ ≤ s; support of size s sampled, i.i.d. amplitudes on [0,1]. Tasks {D_t}_{t=1}^T arrive sequentially, each task t activates a disjoint index set I_t ⊆ [N] (S(f) ⊆ I_t).
  • Model: Encoder E_θ: R^D → R^m and classifier C_θ: R^m → {1,…,K}, with m < |I_t| to force a latent bottleneck. Predictor is x ↦ C_θ(E_θ(x)).
  • Training protocol: Standard experience replay with a bounded buffer B mixing current-task minibatches with past samples; chosen to minimize confounds from stronger regularizers/architectures.
  • Probing: Under the linear representation hypothesis, each ground-truth feature is probed in isolation via the pure-feature signal Φ(e_i), giving the learned direction w_i(t) = E_θ(Φ(e_i)) ∈ R^m at training time t.
  • Single-feature dynamics: R(i,t)=∥w_i(t)∥², unit direction v_i=w_i/∥w_i∥, superposition S(i,t)=Σⱼ(w_jᵀv_i)² over j≠i, retention F(i,t)=∂R/∂t. Hypothesize ∂R/∂t = g(R, S, t_since); identify g via SINDy (polynomial library degree ≤10, thresholded least squares, upper-triangular mask aligned to introduction times).
  • Task-level analysis: Task embeddings via pure-feature stack T^pure_t or data-driven stack; task volume measured by effective rank EffRank(T)=exp(-Σ p_i log p_i) with p_i=σ_i/Σσ_j from embedding singular values. Effective rank is robust to small singular values and captures the number of energetically significant directions.

Results & Highlights

  • RQ1 (sparsity vs superposition): Sparser features exhibit systematically larger S(i,t) (more directional sharing). Superposition rises gradually but shows a pronounced transient dip at each task boundary followed by recovery and continued growth—interpreted as boundary-specific interference + re-alignment, then slow consolidation. Strong-R features stabilize into persistent directions even as S increases; high-R regions are not necessarily low-S, indicating overlap and strength are not strictly antagonistic.
  • RQ2 (overlap vs forgetting): Most-negative retention (largest drops in R) consistently occurs in the low-R, high-S regime—weak overlapped signals are easily overwritten. As R increases, the dependence of F on S becomes less adverse and can locally invert (flat/slightly positive slopes in high-R band). t_since reduces volatility: early after first exposure F is more negative in high-S regions (boundary fragility), later approaching zero/positive as shared directions stabilize.
  • RQ3 (capacity allocation): Both aggregate and per-task effective rank rise with sparsity—sparser signals occupy more latent directions despite greater feature-level overlap. At the moment a new task arrives, its embedding region's effective rank is comparable to previously seen tasks, implying a stable latent scaffold. Small rank contractions at transitions followed by re-spreading mirror feature-level dynamics.
  • Robustness: Appendix variants (Figures 7–12) preserve qualitative conclusions; SINDy coefficient selection varies across runs but the sign structure of dominant interactions (negative dependence on S at low R, moderating with R and t_since) is stable.
  • Net message: "More superposition ⇒ more forgetting" is too coarse—overlap increases vulnerability mainly when representations are weak and fresh; representation strength and capacity allocation are the primary moderators.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. The synthetic toy-world + SINDy-on-retention combination is a fresh, principled lens for CL, but the headline findings (overlap ≠ automatic forgetting; sparsity raises superposition; boundary transients) largely formalize intuitions already present in the superposition and CL literatures. The main new value is making these measurable and falsifiable.
  • Rigor: Decent. Quantities are precisely defined under explicit assumptions; SINDy requires library/threshold choices that the authors themselves flag (L3) as misspecification risks. Stability is supported only by sign-structure checks across a few runs—no full statistical significance reporting or confidence intervals on the fitted dynamical surfaces.
  • Reproducibility: Likely good in principle (synthetic data, frozen Φ/Ψ, standard replay), contingent on code release; the appendix promises Figures 7–12 but the main text does not state whether code/configs are public. The paper explicitly states architecture and sampling choices are in the Appendix.
  • Significance: Indirect for practitioners. This is hypothesis-generating and diagnostic, not a new method or accuracy gain; the authors do not propose a forgetting-mitigation algorithm. Its value is as reusable probes that can be layered onto standard CL benchmarks.
  • Red flags: (i) Toy scope with uniform sparsity and disjoint task supports (L1–L2) may flatter the "sparsity reduces forgetting" story; shared features and heavy-tailed activations could change the picture. (ii) Retention F=∂R/∂t is a proxy, not task accuracy (L4)—claims about "forgetting" are technically about representation-strength erosion, not measured performance loss. (iii) m < |I_t| bottleneck is deliberately strong; transfer to non-bottlenecked or over-parameterized models (typical LLM regime) is untested.

Strengths

  • Cleanly disentangles concept frequency, co-occurrence, and label structure via a frozen generator-separator, making superposition and retention genuinely measurable.
  • Grounds all quantities in the linear representation hypothesis, giving a principled bridge between ground-truth generative features and learned geometric directions.
  • SINDy yields an interpretable, sparse dynamical relation rather than a black-box fit; sign-structure stability is a meaningful robustness check.
  • Explicitly states limitations and frames results as falsifiable hypotheses rather than universal claims.
  • Proposes concrete downstream diagnostics: low-R/high-S regions as early-warning indicators, capacity-aware effective-rank tracking alongside accuracy, replay scheduling to smooth boundary dips.

Limitations

  • Uniform per-feature sparsity and disjoint task supports do not match realistic heterogeneous, heavy-tailed, feature-sharing distributions (L1–L2).
  • SINDy's polynomial library (degree ≤10) may misspecify strongly non-polynomial interactions (L3).
  • Feature retention ∂R/∂t is a proxy for forgetting and is not directly tied to classifier accuracy or standard CL forgetting metrics (L4).
  • No proposed or evaluated mitigation method; latent bottleneck m < |I_t| is a strong design choice whose transfer to large/over-parameterized models is unproven.
  • Generalization of the "overlap is only conditionally harmful" finding beyond the controlled regime is asserted as a hypothesis, not demonstrated.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do the superposition-growth and boundary-dip patterns replicate in transformer representations under realistic continual fine-tuning of LLMs, where the bottleneck m < features does not hold?
  • How do mitigation methods (EWC, larger replay buffers, sparsity-inducing / SAE-style regularization, LoRA) shift the SINDy-identified dynamics and the low-R/high-S vulnerability region?
  • Can low-R/high-S signals serve as early-warning indicators of upcoming task accuracy collapse, and does consolidating weak overlapped features actually reduce downstream forgetting?
  • Does the "sparsity increases effective rank" finding persist under heterogeneous (heavy-tailed) sparsity and overlapping task supports, or does it reverse?
  • How robust is the SINDy-derived sign structure to alternative function classes (rational terms, kernels, neural bases) beyond polynomials?
