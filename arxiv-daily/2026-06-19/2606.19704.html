Beyond Static Leaderboards: Predictive Validity for the Evaluation of LLM Agents

arXiv: 2606.19704

Date: 2026-06-19

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19704

一句话总结： 这是一篇立场论文，论证用基于聚合分数的静态排行榜来评价 LLM 智能体系统性低估了部署场景维度，提出以"预测效度"（in-sample 排名与 out-of-sample 排名的相关性）替代 in-sample 均值作为排序准则，并给出十二层测量框架与三项可证伪的 OOD 判据；证据来自七个既有基准与十四项围绕 AssetOpsBench（MCP 工业智能体）的并行实现研究，但本文本身不开展新的受控实验。
TL;DR: A position paper arguing that aggregate-score leaderboards systematically underspecify deployed-LLM-agent evaluation. It proposes ranking configurations by predictive validity (in-sample vs. out-of-sample rank correlation) instead of in-sample mean, backed by a twelve-tier measurement apparatus consolidated from seven benchmarks plus fourteen parallel implementation studies of the MCP-based AssetOpsBench industrial agent benchmark, and commits to three falsifiable OOD-rank-stability criteria (existing evidence only partially supports the thesis).

核心贡献

  • 提出"预测效度"排序准则：用 in-sample 排名与 out-of-sample 排名之间的相关性（而非 in-sample 均值）来给智能体配置打分，直接服务于部署决策。
  • 十二层测量框架：将七个源基准（AssetOpsBench、MCP-Bench、MCP-Universe、ARE/Gaia2、TaskBench、Exgentic、Condition Insight）加上十四项并行研究的指标整合为 T1–T7 核心能力层（pass-rate 下限、工具调用卫生、规划过程质量、能力轴、成本-效率 Pareto、失败模式分类、可复现性）与 T8–T12 部署扩展层（基础设施、多轮对话、推理模式自适应性、知识增强、证据接地与 judge 无关验证）；并指出没有任何现存基准报告超过四到五层。
  • 三项可证伪的 OOD 判据：Criterion A（分层随机留出，弱偏移）、Criterion B（跨子集 6×6 迁移矩阵，中等偏移）、Criterion C（四类对抗性扰动：改写/标识符重命名/时间窗平移/干扰注入，强偏移）。
  • 聚合分数塌缩维度的实证：给出三组典型案例——推理开关在 clarity 上差 31pp 但整体均分类似；Plan-Execute vs. Supervisor-Specialist 在单轮 Pass1 相近但多轮 turn-2-to-5 延迟差 4.2×；单遍 RAG vs. 多跳 Knowledge-Plugin（50–68% vs. ~90% 准确率，token 膨胀 4.5–10×）。
  • judge 自反性与独立锚点：PHMForge 的 LLM-as-judge Krippendorff α=0.61（远低于人-人 0.74–0.82），并用 ARE/Gaia2（轨迹 DAG oracle，0.99 精度/0.95 召回）与 Condition Insight（CAR 0.68→0.91）证明 judge 无关测量可行。
  • CODS-2025 排名不稳定证据：149 队赛事中，执行轨 public–hidden Spearman ρ=−0.13（n=13，统计上等同于零），规划轨 ρ=0.69（n=20，稳健但仍饱和）。
  • PV 复合打分公式：PV(c) = α·Ȳ_c − β·σ_{Y_c,OOD} − γ·IQR(Y_c)，权重在 Criterion-A 留出集上拟合，但本文不最终确定权重（列为后续研究议程）。
  • 预注册试验设计与领域愿景：给出可执行的预注册试点，以及下一代智能体基准应报告内容的领域级蓝图。

Key Contributions

  • Predictive-validity ranking criterion: rank by the correlation between in-sample and out-of-sample rank rather than in-sample mean, because deployment depends on OOD behavior.
  • Twelve-tier measurement apparatus: consolidates the distinctive metrics of seven benchmarks into seven core tiers (T1–T7) and five deployment-extension tiers (T8–T12) surfaced by fourteen implementation studies; explicitly shows no current leaderboard reports more than four or five tiers.
  • Three falsifiable OOD criteria: (A) held-out stratified split (mild), (B) cross-subset 6×6 transfer matrix (moderate), (C) four-class adversarial perturbation — paraphrase / identifier renaming / time-window shift / distractor injection (strong).
  • Concrete aggregation-collapse cases: per-rubric reasoning sensitivity (31pp clarity gap, equal mean), multi-turn artifact reuse (4.2× latency on turns 2–5), retrieval trade-off (50–68% vs ~90% accuracy with 4.5–10× token inflation).
  • Judge-reflexivity diagnosis + judge-independent anchors: PHMForge LLM-as-judge α=0.61 vs human α∈[0.74,0.82]; ARE/Gaia2 DAG-oracle verifier (0.99/0.95 on 450 trajectories); Condition Insight CAR 0.68→0.91.
  • Direct rank-instability evidence: CODS-2025 (149 teams) public↔hidden Spearman ρ=−0.13 (execution, n=13, 95% CI ≈ [−0.64,+0.45]) and ρ=0.69 (planning, n=20), with public scores saturating (8 unique values across 20 teams).
  • PV score formula and pre-registered pilot: a composite PV(c)=αȲ_c − βσ_OOD − γ·IQR, with weights left to a future controlled study; plus an explicit falsification commitment.

Methodology

This is a synthesis-driven position paper, not an experimental study — the authors explicitly state they run no new controlled experiments. The argument is built from three evidence streams:

  1. Seven source benchmarks/ deployed-system papers (AssetOpsBench, MCP-Bench, MCP-Universe, ARE/Gaia2, TaskBench, Exgentic, Condition Insight) whose distinctive metrics are consolidated into the twelve tiers and mapped in a benchmark×tier coverage matrix (Figure 2).
  2. Fourteen parallel implementation studies of the MCP-based AssetOpsBench industrial-agent benchmark, each varying one architectural axis end-to-end (new asset classes incl. a multi-modal visual extension; orchestration; retrieval strategy; reasoning mode; infrastructure optimization; evaluation methodology — Figure 1). The authors treat their convergence as architectural sensitivity rather than independent replication because the studies share institutional context.
  3. Cross-paper triangulation: findings independently observed by multiple teams under different architectures are taken as stronger inference than any single study.

The benchmark anchor (AssetOpsBench) is justified by four stated criteria: community traction (>1,600 GitHub stars, 230 forks), cross-venue uptake (EMNLP/NeurIPS/AAAI), demonstrated extensibility (substrate of the 149-team CODS-2025 competition and of the 14 studies), and full public availability of scenarios, MCP tooling, and Docker artifacts. The predictive-validity score PV(c)=αȲ_c − βσ_{Y_c,OOD} − γ·IQR(Y_c) is presented as a proposal, with weights deliberately left unfitted and assigned to the future research agenda.

Results & Highlights

  • No single benchmark covers more than 4–5 of the twelve tiers; the deployment-extension tiers (T8–T12) are absent from almost all of them (Figure 2 coverage matrix).
  • CODS-2025 rank instability: planning ρ=0.69 (n=20, robust) but execution ρ=−0.13 (n=13, p=0.71, 95% CI ≈ [−0.64,+0.45]); public scores saturate (8 unique values across 20 teams) while mean private scores fall 11.3 points below public.
  • Exgentic cross-benchmark: rank correlations of 0.32–0.85 across six heterogeneous benchmarks → "architectures optimize for specific task distributions, not robust generalization."
  • Reasoning-on/off (G21): total latency +21.5% (15.08s→18.32s), planning latency +41.9%; clarity +31pp (61→92%), hallucination −7pp, but data-retrieval and agent-sequence correctness unchanged.
  • Retrieval (G3): single-pass RAG 50–68% at 8.9–20s vs. Knowledge-Plugin MCP ~90% at 114–146s with 4.5–10× token inflation; Granite-3-8B on the same plugin pipeline reaches only 60% at 91s.
  • Judge reliability (PHMForge): LLM-as-judge α=0.61 vs human α∈[0.74,0.82].
  • Judge-independent verifiers: ARE/Gaia2 0.99 precision / 0.95 recall on 450 trajectories; Condition Insight CAR 0.68→0.91 via constrained prompting (a prompt-level, not backbone-level, gain).
  • Falsification thresholds committed: position considered well-supported if ρ<0.85 across ≥2 of 3 OOD criteria, top-3 in-sample leaves top-5 OOD in ≥10% of splits, mean↔OOD-variance Pearson ρ>0.2, and PV vs mean top-10 Jaccard overlap <0.85. First condition already partly supported (execution ρ=−0.13).

Authors & Credibility

A 61-author IBM-led team (corresponding author pateldha@us.ibm.com) writing in the cs.AI category, version v1 dated 18 Jun 2026. The lead author and many co-authors are associated with the AssetOpsBench/CODS-2025 line of work (Patel et al., 2025; 2026), giving them deep familiarity with the benchmark they synthesize — but also creating a self-referential incentive: the paper's authority rests heavily on a benchmark and a competition built by the same group, and most of the "fourteen parallel implementation studies" cited (Li et al. 2026a/b/c, Mazeeva, Merchant, Gowda, Kumar, Agarwal, Feng, Bhandari, Jebbouri, Go, Vinod) are co-authored by overlapping subsets of this same author list. The work is positioned at EMNLP/NeurIPS/AAAI-adjacent venues via the anchor benchmark, and the pre-registration and explicit falsification commitments are a genuine credibility signal rare in position papers.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (moderate): The predictive-validity framing borrows directly from psychometrics and from the NLP-leaderboard-critique tradition (Ethayarajh & Jurafsky 2020 on utility functions; Dehghani et al. 2021 "benchmark lottery"; Recht et al. 2019 ImageNet-CIFAR rank shift). The genuinely new contribution is (a) porting it to agentic multi-dimensional measurement and (b) the consolidated twelve-tier apparatus. The rank-instability problem itself is not new.
  • Rigor (mixed): Strong on falsifiability — explicit numeric thresholds and a commitment to retract if they fail. Weak on direct evidence: the headline ρ=−0.13 comes from n=13 with a CI spanning [−0.64,+0.45], which the authors honestly call "suggestive only," yet it still does heavy rhetorical lifting. The PV score's functional form and weights are deliberately unspecified, so it is presently an assertion, not a method.
  • Reproducibility (good-faith, untested): AssetOpsBench is public (scenarios, MCP tools, Docker); pre-registration promised. But the paper itself produces no new artifacts to reproduce — the actual PV experiments are deferred. The "fourteen studies" are the reproducible substrate, not this paper.
  • Significance (potentially high, conditional): If the follow-up controlled study confirms the falsification conditions, the impact on how agent leaderboards are reported would be field-level. As written, it is a well-argued agenda, not a result.
  • Red flags: (1) Heavy circularity — IBM authors evaluate the IBM-anchored benchmark and an IBM-run competition; (2) the "convergent architectural sensitivity" caveat quietly downgrades what could be read as independent triangulation into same-lab replications; (3) the twelve tiers are admitted to be a "working hypothesis" with the orthogonality claim untested; (4) several quantitative contrasts (4.2×, 31pp, 3500×) are reported without per-study CIs in the body text, only "where the source studies provide them"; (5) the ρ=−0.13 headline is statistically near-zero and should not be cited as evidence of negative transfer, only of insufficient positive transfer.

Strengths

  • Intellectual honesty about evidence limits: explicit "too thin to confirm," wide CIs reported, falsification thresholds pre-committed.
  • The twelve-tier coverage matrix (Figure 2) is a genuinely useful diagnostic — it makes visible how much each existing benchmark omits.
  • Three OOD criteria of graded severity (mild/moderate/strong) plus a concrete 6×6 transfer design are operationally usable by other groups.
  • Judge-reflexivity argument is well-motivated and backed by both a negative (α=0.61) and two positive (judge-independent) data points.
  • Position-paper format used appropriately: prescribes a criterion, names what would refute it, and defers the experiment rather than overclaiming.

Limitations

  • No new experiments: every quantitative claim is inherited from cited studies; the central PV claim is unfitted.
  • Small-n rank statistics: the most striking instability number (ρ=−0.13) rests on 13 teams and is statistically indistinguishable from zero.
  • Self-referential evidence base: anchor benchmark, competition, and most cited implementation studies share authors with this paper.
  • Twelve-tier orthogonality is asserted, not measured; tier count ("roughly twelve") is a working hypothesis.
  • PV functional form and α,β,γ weights undefined; the score cannot yet be computed by a third party.
  • Domain narrowness: everything is grounded in industrial asset operations/maintenance (HVAC, compressors, PHM); generalization to coding agents, web agents, or open-ended agents is asserted, not shown.
  • Adversarial-perturbation criterion (C) lacks a perturbation-generation protocol — paraphrase/renaming/time-shift/distractor are named but no generator or equivalence-checking procedure is specified.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Under the committed falsification conditions, does the planning-track ρ=0.69 (which is above the 0.85 threshold's neighborhood) actually undermine the position for well-behaved tracks — i.e., when does predictive validity fail vs. hold?
  • How sensitive is the twelve-tier orthogonality claim to factor analysis on a real multi-benchmark score matrix — would empirical PCA recover ~12 near-orthogonal factors or far fewer?
  • Can the PV score's α,β,γ be fit so that PV-rank strictly dominates mean-rank for deployment decisions, or does the Pareto frontier between quality and OOD-stability make a single scalar ranking fundamentally lossy?
  • Does the judge-reflexivity problem (α=0.61) interact with hidden-state probing — could internal-representation features replace LLM-as-judge with a more stable, mechanistic verifier?
  • How do these OOD-rank-instability findings compare to rank instability in pure inference benchmarks (your "reproducibility of inference results" interest) — is the agent case categorically worse, or just less studied?
  • For self-evolving agents that modify their own architecture over time, is any static leaderboard (even a PV-ranked one) coherent, or does the evaluation target itself drift faster than the measurement?
