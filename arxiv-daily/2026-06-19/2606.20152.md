> **一句话总结：** 本文以线性岭回归探测、跨提示泛化、PCA降维和神经元级干预，系统分析了8个LLM在ASAP++/CSEE（英）与ENEM（葡）三个数据集上对作文质量的内部编码：质量信息线性可解码且随层深渐进涌现（Qwen/Phi约在20%层深处饱和，Llama则持续到最后层），个别"作文评分神经元"与分数强相关并对干预敏感，且这些神经元随作文长度系统性向深层迁移（约200词起开始偏移）。

> **TL;DR:** A mechanistic interpretability study showing that, across 8 instruction-tuned LLMs (Llama-3.1/3.2, Qwen2.5/3, Phi-4; 1B–14B) on English ASAP++/CSEE and Portuguese ENEM, essay quality is linearly decodable from residual-stream final-token hidden states (nonlinear MLP probes give only marginal gains), quality emerges progressively across layers, partially transfers across prompts/rubrics, and is mirrored by individual "essay scoring neurons" whose layer-wise distribution shifts to deeper layers for longer essays (~200 words).

## 核心贡献

- 在**8个指令微调LLM**（Llama-3.1/3.2 1B/3B/8B、Qwen2.5 3B/7B/14B、Qwen3-4B、Phi-4-mini；1B–14B）×**3个数据集**（英文 ASAP++、CSEE；葡萄牙文 ENEM）上，系统应用线性探测、跨提示泛化、PCA 降维、神经元分析四类方法。
- 证明作文质量（总分与各 trait 分数）从最后一层 token 的 residual-stream 表示中**线性可解码**，且 Qwen/Phi 系列约在模型深度 ~20% 处饱和、Llama 系列则稳步提升到末层；大模型在更早的层即编码质量信息。
- 非线性 MLP 探测（256 隐单元）相比线性岭回归仅带来**边际且不一致**的提升，支持线性表示假设。
- **跨提示泛化**：留一提示训练，性能仍显著高于 chance，说明存在跨评分量规共享的线性质量信号；探测对 LLM prompt 策略（Essay / Task / CoT）整体鲁棒（但对长作文 Prompt 8，CoT 反而最差，疑似额外指令引入噪声）。
- **PCA 降维**：低维投影已保留充分 rank-relevant 信息；高维分量对绝对分数校准更重要。
- **"作文评分神经元"**：以与探测方向余弦相似度最高的神经元为单变量探针，其 Spearman 相关高且可跨提示迁移；干预实验（附录 G）显示它们比普通神经元对评分行为更关键。
- **神经元层分布随长度偏移**：短作文（Prompt 3–6）的评分神经元偏早-中层，长作文（1/2/7/8）偏中-后层；细粒度分析（Prompt 8 按100词分箱 + LLM 生成补样本）显示约**200词起**向深层迁移，与人类长文本阅读的工作记忆/整合负担类比。
- 为 AES 引入 CSEE（2025年发布）数据集以缓解潜在训练数据泄漏顾虑。

## Key Contributions

- Systematic probing study on **8 instruction-tuned LLMs** (Llama-3.1/3.2 1B/3B/8B, Qwen2.5 3B/7B/14B, Qwen3-4B-2507, Phi-4-mini; 1B–14B) across **3 datasets** (English ASAP++/CSEE, Portuguese ENEM).
- Demonstrates essay quality (overall + trait scores) is **linearly decodable** from the residual-stream hidden state of the final token; Qwen/Phi saturate at ~20% depth while Llama keeps improving to the last layer; larger models encode quality earlier.
- Nonlinear MLP probes (256 hidden units) yield only **marginal, inconsistent** gains over linear ridge regression — supporting the linear representation hypothesis.
- **Cross-prompt generalization** (leave-one-prompt-out) stays well above chance, indicating shared linear quality signal across rubrics; robustness across LLM prompting strategies (Essay/Task/CoT), though CoT hurts on the long-essay Prompt 8.
- **PCA dimensionality reduction**: low-k projections preserve rank-relevant information; higher dimensions matter more for absolute-score calibration.
- **"Essay-scoring neurons"**: neurons with highest cosine similarity between their input/output weights and the probe direction act as strong single-neuron probes (high Spearman correlation), transfer across prompts, and are more intervention-sensitive than typical neurons (Appendix G).
- **Length-conditioned neuron shift**: scoring neurons for short essays (Prompts 3–6) concentrate in early–middle layers, for long essays (1/2/7/8) in middle–late layers; fine-grained analysis on Prompt 8 (100-word bins, augmented with LLM-generated essays) shows the shift begins at **~200 words**, interpreted via human working-memory/integration load for longer texts.
- Uses CSEE (2025) to mitigate potential training-data leakage concerns.

## Methodology

- **Probe object**: For each essay, extract the hidden state of the **last token** at every layer of the residual stream (matrix A^(l) ∈ R^{n×d_model}). Train **linear ridge regression** probes (closed-form, λ via leave-one-out CV) to predict overall or trait scores.
- **Metric**: Quadratic Weighted Kappa (QWK); 80/20 train/test split per dataset.
- **Nonlinear comparison**: MLP probe `W2·ReLU(W1·x+b1)+b2` with 256 hidden units.
- **Robustness axes**:
  - Cross-prompt generalization — leave-one-prompt-out on ASAP++.
  - LLM prompt variants — Essay-only / Task (instructions+essay) / CoT — on Llama-3.1-8B.
  - Dimensionality reduction — PCA to top-k components, comparing low-k vs full-dim probe QWK and Spearman.
- **Neuron-level analysis**: compute cosine similarity between each neuron's weight vector and the probe-derived feature direction; take the top neurons; project activations onto their weights as single-neuron probes; measure Spearman correlation with ground-truth scores; test cross-prompt transfer.
- **Neuron intervention**: targeted editing/ablation (Appendix G) to test causal involvement in scoring behavior.
- **Neuron distribution by length**: per-prompt layer histogram of top scoring neurons on Llama-8B / Qwen2.5-7B / Qwen2.5-14B; fine-grained 100-word-bin analysis on Prompt 8 augmented with LLM-generated essays.

## Results & Highlights

- Linear probes recover overall **and** trait scores from later-layer activations; **overall scores are predicted more accurately than individual traits** (coarse-grained signal stronger than fine-grained).
- **Family differences**: Qwen/Phi saturate at ~20% of depth and plateau; Llama improves steadily to the final layer — tentatively attributed to training-data-quality differences (citing "textbooks are all you need"-style results).
- Larger models encode quality **earlier** but final-layer peak QWK is only marginally higher than smaller models.
- Nonlinear MLP probes: only marginal/inconsistent QWK improvement ⇒ essay quality is largely linearly decodable.
- Cross-prompt: performance drop vs in-prompt but remains well above QWK=0; quality signal is partly shared across rubrics.
- Prompt robustness: Essay/Task/CoT broadly comparable; CoT converges faster on most prompts but **worst on long-essay Prompt 8** (instruction noise hypothesis).
- PCA: low-k captures rank-relevant info; higher-k improves absolute-score calibration (Spearman rises faster with k than QWK).
- Single-neuron probes: top similarity neurons attain high Spearman correlations and **transfer across prompts**; intervention shows they matter more for scoring than typical neurons.
- Length shift: scoring neurons migrate to deeper layers for longer essays; onset at ~200 words across Llama-8B / Qwen-7B / Qwen-14B; larger models show earlier neuron emergence.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — Moderate.** Linear probing of LM representations is a mature paradigm (Belinkov, Hewitt & Liang, Gurnee & Tegmark); the "linearly decodable feature" claim aligns with the well-known linear representation hypothesis. The genuinely new contributions are: (a) applying this machinery specifically to LLM-based **AES**, (b) the **length-conditioned layer-shift of scoring neurons** (with the 200-word threshold and the human working-memory analogy), and (c) the systematic Llama-vs-Qwen/Phi saturation-pattern observation. Single-neuron interpretability in superposition regimes is not itself new, and the authors acknowledge this limitation honestly.
- **Rigor — Good.** 8 models × 3 datasets × multiple analysis axes; closed-form ridge with leave-one-out CV; concurrent use of QWK and Spearman; PCA control against probe capacity; cross-prompt leave-one-out. However, the main text reports **qualitative trends only** — specific QWK values, confidence intervals, and significance tests are deferred to figures/appendix, so quantitative strength cannot be fully verified from the body.
- **Reproducibility — Medium-High.** Public datasets (ASAP++ via LDC, CSEE, ENEM on GitHub), named instruction-tuned models, standard probing recipes, CSEE deliberately chosen for leakage control. No explicit mention of code release for the probe pipeline or the identified neuron indices; intervention protocol details are in appendix only.
- **Significance — Moderate, indirect.** This is a scientific/interpretability contribution; it does **not** improve AES accuracy. Its value is in (i) validating that LLMs encode structured (not merely superficial-cue) quality representations, and (ii) the neuron-level + length findings, which could enable future steering/controllability work. Direct practical impact on AES deployment is limited absent downstream application.
- **Red flags / caveats:**
  1. **Probing ≠ functional use.** The authors themselves note (citing Gurnee & Tegmark, Ravichander) that linear decodability does not prove the model *uses* the direction for scoring; single-neuron correlations are correlational, and causal evidence is limited to per-neuron intervention in Appendix G (not multi-neuron/circuit-level).
  2. **Superposition caveat.** Single-neuron analysis is acknowledged as inherently limited under feature superposition (Elhage et al. 2022); identified "scoring neurons" may be a small visible slice of a distributed code.
  3. **Neuron selection risk.** Top-k neurons are selected by cosine similarity to the probe direction — circularity/selection bias risk; population-level statistics vs cherry-picking should be checked in the appendix figures.
  4. **Cross-prompt transfer is only "partial"** and the absolute QWK drop is not quantified in the body; practical rubric-generalization utility is unclear.
  5. **Prompt 8 CoT anomaly** is explained post-hoc (instruction noise) without direct evidence.
  6. **Length analysis uses LLM-generated essays** to augment bins — this could confound the neuron-distribution-vs-length result if generated essays differ distributionally from real ones.

## Strengths

- Strong breadth (8 models, 2 languages, 3 datasets) and multi-method triangulation (probing + transfer + PCA + neuron intervention).
- Honest framing of probing limitations and the superposition caveat.
- The CSEE (post-cutoff) inclusion is a thoughtful leakage-control move.
- The length-conditioned neuron shift is a non-obvious, testable, and cognitively-motivated finding with concrete threshold (~200 words).
- The Llama-vs-Qwen/Phi saturation-pattern observation is an interesting empirical lead for future work on training-data effects.

## Limitations

- (Authors' stated) Scale limited to 1B–14B; no GPT-4 / Llama-3.1-70B; only one non-English language (Portuguese); intervention restricted to single neurons, not circuits/SAE features.
- Quantitative effect sizes and significance not reported in the body text.
- Probing decodability ≠ causal functional role.
- Single-neuron interpretability limited under feature superposition.
- Cross-prompt transfer is only partial; absolute performance gaps under-reported.
- Length analysis augmented with LLM-generated essays (potential distributional confound).

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Are the "essay scoring neurons" **causally necessary** (multi-neuron joint ablation produces large QWK drops) or largely redundant under superposition?
- Does the linear quality direction form a **universal** subspace across model families, or is it family/size-specific?
- How does the length-conditioned layer shift interact with known **long-context extrapolation failures** — does it break down past the model's training context length?
- Can the quality direction be used for **steering/controllable AES** (boost coherence, organization) without retraining — and does this transfer to other quality-judgment tasks (summarization, code)?
- What is the relationship between these linearly-decodable directions and **SAE features** — are scoring neurons monosemantic single features or mixtures?
- Does the Llama-vs-Qwen/Phi saturation difference correlate with measurable **training-data quality/composition** signals (textbook-filtered corpora)?
- Could the cross-prompt transfer direction serve as a **prompt-invariant AES feature** for improving cross-rubric generalization in supervised AES?
- Replicate the ~200-word neuron-shift threshold on typologically distant languages (Chinese, Arabic) and on non-essay scoring tasks.
