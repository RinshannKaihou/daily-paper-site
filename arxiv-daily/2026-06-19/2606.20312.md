> **一句话总结：** 针对"冻结"的pose-flow视频异常检测器，提出后处理分数校准方法RPC：在标准化似然分数上叠加一个由关键点置信度门控的最近原型偏差，不改骨干网络、不重训、不动缓存骨架轨迹，在2个backbone×4个数据集(8组设置)上帧级AUROC全部提升(+0.34到+4.49点，平均+2.03点)，每帧仅增加约0.118ms开销。

> **TL;DR:** For a frozen pose-flow video anomaly detector whose backbone, cached skeleton tracks, and evaluation pipeline are all fixed, the authors add a reliability-gated nearest-prototype deviation (measured in the frozen latent space) to the standardized likelihood score. This post-hoc, training-free calibration improves frame-level AUROC in all 8 backbone-dataset pairs (mean +2.03 points), with prototype deviation as the main corrective signal and keypoint confidence gating it only when poses are unreliable (~0.118 ms/frame overhead).

## 核心贡献
- **提出"冻结检测器"适配设定**：将问题重新定义为对已冻结的pose-flow检测器进行分数级校准，把流密度证据、经验性正常模式偏差、姿态观测可靠性三类信息解耦——而非训练新检测器或替换似然分数。
- **设计后处理原型偏差分数**：在冻结隐空间中用K-means拟合正常行为原型，用对角Mahalanobis距离度量测试窗口到最近原型的偏差，标准化后作为对似然分数的**加性修正**(而非替代)。
- **可靠性门控规则**：把关键点置信度作为"观测可靠性"而非"异常证据"，仅门控新增的原型项（g = max(q^γ, ρ)），保留似然分数不被门控——这样不可靠姿态仍可被密度模型判为异常，但不能单凭可能反映姿态误差的原型距离主导最终分数。
- **全面的实证验证**：在STG-NF和DA-Flow两个冻结backbone上、4个数据集(SHTech/UBN及各自的HR人类相关变体)的8组设置上，AUROC全部提升；并附带消融、可靠性分组分析、受控扰动鲁棒性、参数敏感性、运行时与定性案例研究。代码已开源(github.com/iNing10/RPC)。

## Key Contributions
- Formulates **frozen-detector adaptation as a score-level calibration problem** that separates three evidence types: flow density, empirical normal-mode deviation, and pose-observation reliability.
- Introduces a **post-hoc prototype deviation score** (K-means prototypes + diagonal Mahalanobis nearest-prototype distance in the frozen latent space), used as an additive correction to the standardized likelihood score — explicitly not a new generative density model.
- Designs a **reliability-gated calibration rule** that treats keypoint confidence as observation reliability (not anomaly evidence) and gates only the added prototype term, leaving the likelihood signal untouched.
- Empirically demonstrates AUROC gains in all 8 backbone-dataset pairs without retraining, plus ablations, reliability-stratified analysis, controlled-pose-perturbation robustness, sensitivity sweeps, runtime measurement (~0.118 ms/frame), and qualitative case studies. Code released.

## Methodology
RPC operates entirely post-hoc on a frozen pose-flow detector f_θ. The frozen backbone already exposes three quantities per window: a likelihood-derived score ℓ(x) = −log p_θ(x), a latent code z = f_θ(x) (flattened via fixed pooling ϕ into feature h(x), with no trainable projection), and keypoint confidence values C.

1. **Prototype fitting (offline, normal-only):** K-means fits K prototypes {c_k} on normal training features h(xᵢᵗʳ). Per-prototype per-dimension diagonal variances v_{k,m} are estimated (with ε=1e-6 floor; empty clusters fall back to global variance). Prototypes are treated as compact reference statistics, not semantic action classes.
2. **Prototype deviation:** Raw prototype deviation d_jᵗᵉ = min_k Σ_m (h_{j,m}ᵗᵉ − c_{k,m})² / v_{k,m})^(1/2), a scale-aware diagonal Mahalanobis distance.
3. **Standardization:** Both ℓ and d are standardized using means/standard-deviations estimated only from normal training windows (μ_ℓ,σ_ℓ,μ_d,σ_d), placing density and distance on a common normal-training scale so the fusion weight α controls correction strength rather than range compensation.
4. **Reliability gate:** Window reliability q_jᵗᵉ = mean of clipped keypoint confidences; gate g_jᵗᵉ = max((q_jᵗᵉ)^γ, ρ). γ>1 suppresses low-confidence windows; ρ is a floor (set to 0 in all experiments). γ=0 corresponds to ungated fusion.
5. **Final calibrated score:** s_jᵗᵉ = ℓ̂_jᵗᵉ + α·g_jᵗᵉ·d̂_jᵗᵉ. Calibrated window scores are converted to frame-level scores via the frozen backbone's original aggregation rule Agg_bb, then evaluated by frame-level AUROC.
6. **Hyperparameter selection:** Per original dataset, selected once on validation (UBN official split; SHTech via a training-only proxy set built from held-out normal windows + behavior-preserving corruptions + synthetic pseudo-anomalies), then frozen and reused for HR variants. Candidate grid: K∈{2…80}, α∈{0…10}, γ∈{0…16}.

## Results & Highlights
- **Main result (Table 3):** RPC improves over the reproduced likelihood-only baseline in **all 8 backbone-dataset pairs**. Gains: +0.34 (DA-Flow/SHTech) to +4.49 (DA-Flow/UBN-HR), **mean +2.03 points**. Gains are larger on UBN-family (synthetic, more diverse normal behavior) than SHTech (real, baseline already strong).
- **Beats traditional post-hoc baselines** (Gaussian, kNN, OC-SVM, IsolationForest applied to the same frozen features) in every row — confirming that calibrating the original score beats replacing it with a standalone latent detector.
- **Competitive with recent SOTA (Table 4):** STG-NF+RPC reaches 85.89 (SHTech) / 87.56 (SHTech-HR); DA-Flow+RPC reaches 78.54 (UBN) / 79.55 (UBN-HR), positioning favorably among skeleton/pose/trajectory methods and broadly competitive even vs. RGB/multimodal/diffusion/LLM methods — without changing modality or retraining.
- **Ablation (Table 5):** Diagonal Mahalanobis prototype alone gives +1.27 (improves 8/8); linear gate q (γ=1) → +1.91; full RPC → +2.03. Shuffled reliability (+1.24) and inverse reliability (−0.60) controls confirm gate direction matters.
- **Reliability-stratified (Table 6):** Full RPC beats baseline in low/mid/high reliability groups (+3.88/+3.43/+2.51); the *additional* gain over prototype-only is largest at low reliability (+2.36), confirming the gate helps most when poses are unreliable.
- **Robustness (Fig. 3):** Gains over baseline under coordinate jitter (+2.60) and confidence corruption (+1.59); tracking switch near-neutral (+0.27); keypoint dropout is the failure case (−0.61) since it destroys body structure.
- **Sensitivity (Fig. 4):** Stable ranges for K, α, γ — not isolated optima.
- **Runtime:** Average +0.118 ms/frame at test time (prototype bank built offline once).

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty (moderate):** The core idea — fusing a standardized likelihood score with a reliability-gated nearest-prototype Mahalanobis distance — is conceptually simple and draws heavily on known ideas (prototype/memory normal references from GEPC-class work, density-vs-distance distinction from generalized OOD detection, keypoint-confidence-as-reliability from SeeKer). The genuine novelty is narrower: the *frozen-detector, score-only* framing and the conservative design choice to gate only the added term. It is an honest, well-scoped engineering contribution, not a conceptual breakthrough.
- **Rigor (good):** 5-seed mean±std reporting, full ablation ladder, reliability stratification, controlled perturbation study, sensitivity sweeps, and an explicit disclosure of the SHTech proxy-validation construction. Compared against 4 traditional post-hoc baselines with the *same* validation rule (fair).
- **Reproducibility (good with caveats):** Code released; hyperparameter grid fully disclosed; proxy set construction detailed. Caveats: (a) the SHTech proxy set and its pseudo-anomaly generators are themselves a design choice that affects selected K/α/γ and is not independently validated against true anomalies; (b) the "frozen" STG-NF/DA-Flow checkpoints and cached skeleton tracks are reproduced by the authors, so exact numerical reproduction depends on their pose/tracking pipeline, which they themselves note is "expensive, sensitive to implementation details, and difficult to reproduce exactly."
- **Significance (modest but real):** Average gain of +2.03 AUROC points is meaningful in this subfield, and the framing (upgrade cached systems without re-running the expensive pose pipeline) is genuinely practical. However, the gains on the harder real-world SHTech are small (+0.34 to +0.54), and the larger UBN gains come on a synthetic benchmark.
- **Red flags:** None severe, but worth noting: (1) absolute AUROC on UBN-family is still modest (74–80%), i.e., the calibrated detectors remain far from solved on the harder dataset; (2) no statistical significance testing is reported — with std up to ±1.57 on UBN, the per-setting gains overlap with baseline within noise in some cells even if the mean ordering is consistent; (3) self-comparison against reproduced baselines (authors reproduced STG-NF/DA-Flow themselves) introduces a risk that the baseline was slightly under-tuned; (4) keypoint-dropout failure case (−0.61) shows the method can hurt under exactly the degraded-observation regime the reliability gate is supposedly designed for.

## Strengths
- Clean problem framing: explicitly separates the three evidence types and is transparent that the frozen likelihood signal is preserved, not replaced.
- Honest, conservative design: gate acts only on the *added* prototype term, so RPC can never suppress a genuinely anomalous high-likelihood window — a thoughtful safety property for surveillance.
- Thorough empirical work: ablations, reliability stratification, perturbation robustness, sensitivity, runtime, and qualitative case studies all support the claims, with 5-seed statistics.
- Practical value: training-free, ~0.118 ms/frame overhead, works on cached pipelines — directly useful for real deployments where re-running pose extraction is prohibitive.
- Code released.

## Limitations
- **Proxy fidelity:** SHTech hyperparameters rely on synthetic pseudo-anomalies that approximate but cannot cover the full space of real abnormal events; the proxy set is itself an unvalidated design choice.
- **Information ceiling:** RPC operates only on cached skeleton tracks, latent features, scores, and confidence — it cannot recover missing body structure or exploit appearance/object cues that were never cached. Keypoint dropout is a demonstrated failure mode (−0.61).
- **Modest real-world gains:** The largest gains concentrate on the synthetic UBN benchmark; on real SHTech the improvements are within ~0.5 points, where per-seed noise (±0.3–0.5) is comparable.
- **No significance testing:** Claims of "improves in all 8 pairs" are based on mean ordering without formal hypothesis tests or confidence-interval overlap analysis.
- **Baseline reproduction risk:** Authors reproduced the STG-NF/DA-Flow baselines and cached poses themselves; minor under-tuning of baselines would inflate the gains.
- **Backbone-dependent hyperparameters:** K, α, γ differ substantially per dataset/backbone (e.g., K=8 vs 80, α=0.25 vs 4.0), suggesting limited out-of-the-box generalization without per-deployment tuning.

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Would adding formal significance testing (paired bootstrap or DeLong test over the 5 seeds) confirm that the per-setting SHTech gains (+0.34–0.54) are real rather than within noise?
- How sensitive are the results to the *quality of the reproduced baseline* — does RPC still win if the STG-NF/DA-Flow baselines are tuned by their original authors at SOTA settings?
- Can the reliability gate be made adaptive per-keypoint rather than a single scalar q (e.g., per-joint Mahalanobis weighting) to better handle structured keypoint dropout — the current main failure mode?
- Does the "freeze + post-hoc calibration" paradigm transfer to other one-class normalizing-flow detectors (e.g., industrial tabular or audio anomaly detection) where the same density-vs-distance tension arises?
- The proxy-validation construction for SHTech is a generalizable idea for one-class model selection without test labels — how does its quality compare to other synthetic-anomaly generators (e.g., based on diffusion perturbations)?
- Is the +0.118 ms/frame overhead dominated by prototype count K and latent dimensionality — and does it scale linearly, making RPC viable for very large prototype banks or streaming settings?
