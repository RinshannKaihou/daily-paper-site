> **一句话总结：** 本文用正式的心理测量学框架对 56 个指令微调 LLM 施测人格与风险偏好量表（含自评与行为任务），证明模型间"心理画像"的差异 81-90% 来自方向性响应偏差（不分题项内容一律偏向量表某一端），而非真实特质；量表的表面信度几乎完全由其"响应正交性"（反向计分比例）决定，且模型画像可被题项选择人为制造——因此 LLM 的"人格"主要是测量工具的伪影，而非模型本身的真实属性。

> **TL;DR:** Administering a battery of self-report and behavioral psychological instruments (personality + risk preference) to 56 instruction-tuned LLMs alongside large human reference samples, the authors show via a formal psychometric variance decomposition that 81-90% of between-model variance is driven by a directional response bias (vs. 9-16% in humans). Apparent scale reliability is almost entirely predicted by a coined metric, "response orthogonality" (proportion of reverse-keyed items, r = -0.95 in LLMs vs. -0.41 in humans), and the apparent profile of any model can be shifted or manufactured via item selection. Borrowed human instruments therefore lack validity for LLMs.

## 核心贡献

- 将 LLM 心理画像问题正式纳入心理测量学测量模型：`x_ij = m + k_j·(θ_i − m) + b_i + ε_ij`，把响应拆为正交的"潜在特质 θ"与"方向性响应偏差 b"两部分。
- 提出"响应正交性（response orthogonality）"概念——量表中"特质方向"与"偏差方向"相反的题项（反向计分题）比例，并证明它几乎完全决定量表在 LLM 上的表面信度。
- 对 56 个指令微调 LLM（46 开源 + 10 闭源，含 Claude Opus/Opus-4-6、GPT-5.4、Gemini、Grok-4.20、Llama-3.3-70B、Qwen3 等）与人类参照样本（N=20,993 的 IPIP-NEO-300 + N=1,507 的风险偏好量表）同步施测，跨自评量表与行为任务得到一致结论。
- 用前向-反向题项均值相关 ρ(R̄_f, R̄_r) 作为诊断量：人类呈强负相关（-0.69 至 -0.82，特质主导），LLM 呈强正相关（+0.61 至 +0.81，偏差主导），二者方向相反、清晰分离。
- 量化方差份额 π_b：响应偏差在 LLM 间解释 81-90% 方差（人类仅 9-16%）；偏差随能力（参数规模、开源/闭源）下降但不被消除。
- 证明量表跨 29 种工具上 LLM 的题项间相关与反向计分比例的相关达 r = -0.95（近乎确定性），而人类仅 r = -0.41；该梯度在 10 种提示/抽取条件下、在大小模型分组、在否定语消除后均稳健。
- 直接展示模型画像可被前向/反向题项选择"制造"——同一模型在前向题与反向题上得出的特质分数差最大可达 2|b̂|，部分模型（如 TildeOpen-30b、gpt-5.4-nano）几乎完全偏向一端。

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes LLM responding in an explicit measurement model that orthogonally separates latent trait θ from a directional response bias b, making the trait/bias confound measurable.
- Coins and operationalizes "response orthogonality" — the proportion of items where trait direction and bias direction oppose — and shows it almost entirely determines an instrument's apparent reliability on LLMs.
- Runs a large, head-to-head comparison: 56 instruction-tuned LLMs (46 open-weight 1B-70B + 10 proprietary: Claude Opus/Haiku 4.5/4.6, GPT-5.4 family, Gemini 3.1 flash-lite, Grok-4.20, Qwen3.5-plus/flash) vs. large human reference samples (N=20,993 IPIP-NEO-300; N=1,507 risk-preference battery), spanning both self-report and behavioral tasks.
- Provides a simple diagnostic — the forward vs. reverse item-mean correlation ρ(R̄_f, R̄_r) — whose sign cleanly separates humans (negative, trait-driven) from LLMs (positive, bias-driven).
- Quantifies the bias variance share π_b = (1+ρ)/2: 81-90% for LLMs vs. 9-16% for humans; shows bias attenuates with capability but is not eliminated even for the most capable proprietary models.
- Demonstrates a near-deterministic orthogonality-consistency gradient (r = -0.95) across 29 instruments that is robust to 10 prompting/elicitation conditions (fresh vs. in-context, constrained text-gen vs. logit extraction, standard vs. flipped option order), to model size, and to removing negation confounds.
- Shows psychological profiles are unstable and steerable: the forward-only vs. reverse-only trait-score gap equals 2|b̂| and ranges up to 0.99 for some models, so item selection can manufacture a model's apparent profile.

## Methodology

- **Measurement model.** Each response `x_ij = m + k_j(θ_i − m) + b_i + ε_ij`, with keying k_j ∈ {+1,-1}, scale midpoint m, latent trait θ_i, person-level response bias b_i, and item noise ε. Forward/reverse means give orthogonal contrasts: `θ̂ = m + ½(R̄_f − R̄_r)`, `b̂ = ½(R̄_f + R̄_r) − m`. Cross-respondent covariance `Cov(R̄_f, R̄_r) = σ_b² − σ_θ²`, so its sign identifies whether trait or bias dominates. The variance share attributable to bias is `π_b = (1+ρ)/2`.
- **Instruments.** Two batteries chosen for usability/safety relevance: IPIP-NEO-300 (Big Five, 5 instruments, near-balanced keying pr∈[0.40,0.60]) and Frey et al.'s risk-preference battery (24 instruments: SOEP, DOSPERT×6, GABS, PRI, SSSV×4, BARRATT×3, DAST, CARE×3, DM, plus behavioral DFD/LOT/MPL; 6 more conditional-probability tasks). In total 29-30 instruments spanning fully forward-keyed (pr=0) to fully balanced (pr=0.5).
- **LLM panel.** 56 instruction-tuned decoder-only transformers: near-complete HuggingFace enumeration of open-source 1B-70B assistant-tuned models (different sizes within a family counted as separate respondents) + 10 proprietary models via APIs (queried April 15, 2026 for personality, May 27, 2026 for risk).
- **Elicitation.** Reference condition: directly-prompted, fresh context per item, greedy constrained text-generation (outlines 1.2.8) for open models, regex extraction for proprietary; temperature zero. Robustness: factorial crossing of {fresh vs. sequential context} × {constrained text-gen vs. logit extraction} × {standard vs. flipped option order} = 10 conditions; plus a "weighted" teacher-forcing condition over the 1,507 human trajectories.
- **Statistics.** Pearson ρ between forward/reverse means per (sample×trait) cell with Fisher-z 95% CIs; π_b via closed form; cross-instrument Pearson r and Spearman between pr and mean inter-item correlation (r̄) with Fisher-z CIs; Cronbach's α reported alongside; profile instability = mean |T_f − T_r| across instruments (= 2|b̂|).

## Results & Highlights

- **Bias dominates between-model variance.** On Neuroticism (median trait), humans slope downward (ρ = -0.82) along the trait line while LLMs slope upward (ρ = +0.68) along the response-bias line. All Big Five traits replicate: humans -0.69 to -0.82, LLMs +0.61 to +0.81.
- **Variance share.** Response bias accounts for π_b = 81-90% of between-model variance in LLMs vs. 9-16% in humans across the five traits.
- **Bias is robust to degenerate responders.** Excluding 5 models that answered every item identically (Llama-3.2-1B, gemma-3-1b, SmolLM3-3B, Ministral-8B, TildeOpen-30b) attenuates correlations (+0.55 to +0.74) but flips no sign.
- **Capability attenuates, does not eliminate.** |b̂| vs. log params correlates only weakly (-0.14 to -0.25, none significant). Proprietary models are less biased than open-source (significantly on Agreeableness p=.007 and Neuroticism p=.028) but exceed the human median on Openness/Extraversion and still lie outside the human range.
- **Orthogonality nearly determines apparent reliability.** Across 29 instruments, cross-instrument correlation between reverse-keying proportion pr and mean inter-item correlation r̄ is r = -0.95 (95% CI [-0.98, -0.90]) for LLMs vs. r = -0.41 ([-0.68, -0.05]) for humans. Fully-forward instruments yield α = 0.85-0.96 (spuriously "reliable"), while orthogonal instruments collapse to near-zero or negative (one behavioral task α = -0.52).
- **Robust across conditions.** The gradient holds in all 10 prompting conditions (r from -0.83 to -0.95), splits at the 7.5B median size (|Δr| ≤ 0.09), and survives negation removal (BARRATT has 1/30 negated item; IPIP deltas -0.03 to +0.08).
- **Profiles are unstable and steerable.** Forward-only vs. reverse-only profiles diverge by 2|b̂|, ranging from 0.15 (e.g., granite-3.3-8B) to 0.99 (some small/degenerate models); even Claude Opus and GPT-5.4 show substantial instability. Risk instruments are more biased (mean |b| = 0.51) than personality (0.30), plausibly because training data contains more personality-test patterns than risk-task patterns.
- **Bias is not sycophancy/social desirability.** Sycophancy would yield one-sided (acquiescent) bias, but the observed bias is two-sided around zero; social desirability would surface as trait variance, not bias. Bias is stable within models (SI Table S9), bounded by instrument category.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** Most prior LLM-psychometrics work either reports profiles or questions them via prompt-sensitivity/factor-structure arguments. This paper supplies a quantitative, instrument-level decomposition that converts "the profiles may be off" into "81-90% of the variance is bias, and reliability is mechanically predicted by reverse-keying proportion." The diagnostic (forward-reverse mean correlation) and the π_b closed form are simple and reusable.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Large panel (56 LLMs, broad families and sizes), human baselines on identical instruments, formal measurement model with identifiable parameters, extensive robustness battery (10 prompting conditions, logit vs. textgen, size split, negation control, degenerate-responder exclusion, Fisher-z CIs). The honest reporting of α turning negative on orthogonal instruments is striking and on point.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** Code, prompts, cleaned data, and full software spec public on GitHub/OSF; open weights via HuggingFace. Main caveat: proprietary responses (10 models, gathered via commercial API on fixed dates) cannot be re-generated, and the 10-model proprietary subset is acknowledged as too small for standard inference (treated descriptively).
- **Significance: Substantial.** Directly challenges a fast-growing literature that profiles LLMs for "personality", risk, morality, and politics, and a parallel one that uses LLMs as simulated human participants. If the bias share is anywhere near this magnitude, many published "LLM has trait X" claims and reliability coefficients are misleading and not comparable across instruments. The practical recommendation — design or retrofit instruments with response orthogonality, or model bias explicitly — is actionable.
- **Red flags / honest caveats.**
  - The unit-of-analysis choice ("each model = one respondent in default state") excludes within-model variation from steering/persona prompting; the authors defend this but the "model individual" is genuinely contested, so the headline numbers are conditional on that framing.
  - The capability proxy is coarse (params, open vs. proprietary, release date) — no clean capability benchmark or base-vs-instruct comparison, so "bias declines with capability" rests on weak slopes (-0.14 to -0.25, none significant) plus the open/proprietary contrast.
  - π_b is derived from a single ρ per cell; although the model is parsimonious and the diagnostics hold, additional components (item difficulty, extreme/midpoint response style) could reallocate some of the "bias" variance.
  - The "manufactured via item selection" argument, while compelling, also holds to a lesser degree for human instruments (r = -0.41, not 0); the LLM-vs-human contrast is the real finding, not the existence of an orthogonality effect per se.
  - Domain coverage is limited to personality + risk; generalization to values, morality, politics, well-being is asserted but not demonstrated.
  - Five proprietary-model degenerate responders were excluded for one analysis; the panel's behavior on more naturalistic generation settings (temperature > 0) is not tested, though the authors argue the effect size makes a reversal implausible.

## Strengths

- Elegant, parsimonious measurement model with closed-form estimators that any researcher can apply to existing data (the decomposition is "portable to human data").
- Convergent evidence from self-report AND behavioral tasks AND 10 prompting conditions makes a confound-specific-to-one-protocol explanation unlikely.
- Clean diagnostic (sign of ρ(R̄_f, R̄_r)) that is trivial to compute and visually interpretable (Fig. 2).
- Intellectually honest treatment of alternative explanations (sycophancy, social desirability, negation handling, sampling noise, degenerate responders) — each explicitly ruled out or bounded.
- Actionable prescription: either use fully orthogonal instruments, model bias formally, or balance label direction within an instrument (a cheap, untested manipulation the authors flag).

## Limitations

- Only instruction-tuned, post-trained models assessed; base models and the role of specific post-training recipes are not isolated (the authors note post-training is known to reduce human alignment).
- Only two construct domains (personality, risk); extension to values/morality/politics/well-being is left to future work.
- Temperature = 0 only; reported sampling effects on alignment are small but not directly re-tested here.
- Proprietary panel (N=10) too small for inferential tests; the strongest quantitative claims rest on the open-source panel.
- Several conditional-probability tasks (BART, CCT, DFE, AUDIT, FTND, PG) required a special teacher-forced condition and some OOM-driven cell drops, slightly reducing coverage.
- "Response orthogonality" is treated as an instrument property, but in practice reverse-keyed items can differ in content validity even for humans; fully separating "orthogonality" from "reverse-item validity" is not fully resolved.
- The deeper question (instrument problem vs. model problem — do LLMs have stable dispositions at all?) is explicitly left open; no validated LLM-specific instrument is proposed, only a design property.

## Relevance

与你的 "LLM Inference Evaluation（推理评测的输出一致性与可复现性）" 与 "LLM 隐藏状态/可解释性" 方向高度相关：它把"用人类心理量表对模型评测得到看似稳定、高信度的结果"这一现象拆解为"量表反向计分比例 × 模型方向性响应偏差"的机械产物，揭示了被广泛使用的模型评测范式存在系统性测量伪影，对如何设计对模型输出既可靠又可复现、不引入人为偏差的评测协议具有直接方法论警示；其"通过前向/反向题项对照来分离真实信号与偏差"的思路，也与用探测/对照刺激从隐藏状态中分离真实结构 vs. 表面伪影的可解释性工作异曲同工。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the directional response bias correlate more strongly with a specific post-training objective (RLHF helpfulness, safety RL, instruction-tuning format) than with raw size or capability benchmarks? A base-vs-instruct within-family comparison would isolate this.
- Can response orthogonality be computed a priori from an instrument's item keying without running any LLM, enabling a cheap, pre-hoc instrument screening tool for the LLM-eval community?
- Does the orthogonality-consistency gradient recover in non-self-report domains (values, morality, politics), and does it interact with known prompt-sensitivity effects (label language, option order, forced-choice vs. free text)?
- Could latent-state probing of hidden activations recover a genuine, non-artifactual disposition signal where scale-based "trait" scores fail — i.e., is there measurable trait structure that response bias masks but representations preserve?
- Would balancing label direction within an instrument (reverse the scale on only half the items, no content reversal) recover trait signal cheaply, as the authors hypothesize but do not test?
- Is the bias stable under non-greedy decoding (temperature > 0, nucleus sampling), and does the modest human-alignment gain reported elsewhere survive once response orthogonality is controlled?
- How does the per-model response bias |b̂| relate to other measurable LLM properties (sycophancy benchmarks, refusal rates, calibration) — is it a general "response-style" latent factor?
