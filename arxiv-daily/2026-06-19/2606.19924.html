The Tao of Agency: Autotelic AI, Embedded Agency and Dissolution of the Self

arXiv: 2606.19924

Date: 2026-06-19

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19924

一句话总结： 本文是一篇立场/理论综合文章，将"自生目的AI（Autotelic AI）"重构为统一框架 (π, G, µ, Cα, V, b, M)，论证嵌入性（embeddedness）是自生目的能动性的必要非充分条件——因为同一动力学F允许多种Markov-blanket划分，核心难题不是生成目标而是生成并相对化"自我"，并沿量子化、非二元哲学与LLM约束采样智能体三个方向延伸。
TL;DR: A single-author conceptual/position paper that consolidates autotelic AI (agents that discover their own goals) into the framework tuple (π, G, µ, Cα, V, b, M) — a goal-conditioned policy, an endogenous goal distribution, a resource functional, a viability set, a Markov-blanket boundary, and a self-model. It argues embeddedness is necessary but not sufficient because the same dynamics admit many valid partitions (the self is non-unique), so the deepest problem is self-generation/relativization, not goal-generation. Extended via a quantum agent-environment cut, a non-dual contemplative reading, and a concrete LLM-as-constrained-state-machine instantiation.

核心贡献

  • 将分散的自生目的AI脉络（内在动机、资源驱动先验、因果介入式学习、稳态、嵌入性）整合为单一形式化框架 (π, G, µ, Cα, V, b, M)。
  • 逐步论证每一成分"必要但不充分"：内在动机给出 r_int 但不给 G；资源先验给 µ 但不给正当性；因果介入给出 G（可控因子）但 µ 仍待定；稳态（homeostasis）给自指固定点但预设嵌入主体；嵌入性最终在揭示主体边界的同时暴露个体化的非唯一性。
  • 重新定位核心难题：不是"如何生成目标"，而是"如何生成并对目标所归属的自我进行相对化"——智能体必须相信自己的边界才能行动，又必须看穿该边界才能理解。
  • 三个延伸方向：(1) 量子化表述（CPTP map / Lindbladian / 量子MDP），使agent-environment cut成为物理实在，并指出量子测量即介入、Hilbert分解非唯一；(2) 与道家wuji、吠檀多nirguna brahman、Madhyamaka二谛、līlā（神圣游戏）等非二元传统对话；(3) 给出基于LLM的具体工程实例化。
  • LLM实例化：将agent建模为"约束下的随机状态机/继续"——S_t → I_{t+1} ~ P_θ(·|S_t, M_t) → Γ → S_{t+1}，通过可容许性过滤器 Γ(I, S, Cα, V, Π_safety) 实现V（硬安全）、Cα（token/工具/时间预算软约束）、M（持续潜在自模型），更接近"在线控制+约束采样"而非奖励优化。

Key Contributions

  • Consolidated framework: Unifies the autotelic AI literature into the tuple (π, G, µ, Cα, V, b, M) — goal-conditioned policy, endogenous goal distribution, resource functional (LEAST taxonomy: Length, Energy, Approximation, Space, Time), viability set, Markov-blanket boundary, and self-model.
  • Necessary-but-not-sufficient ladder: Shows each ingredient is necessary but insufficient on its own. Critically, intrinsic motivation merely replaces a designer scalar r with another r_int (still no nontrivial G); resource priors smuggle in commitments via the aggregation α; causal-interventional learning yields G (controllable factors) but leaves µ undetermined; homeostasis gives a self-justifying fixed point (r_viab = 1[s ∈ V]) but presupposes a delimited agent.
  • Reframed hard problem: Embeddedness individuates the agent but the same dynamics F admit many valid Markov-blanket partitions (µ_α, b_α, η_α) — each picking a different candidate self with a different V and therefore a different objective. The hardest problem is self-generation and relativization, not goal-generation.
  • "Instrumentally indispensable fiction": Action requires the self-model M to behave as if the boundary is fundamental (T), while accurate modeling requires recognizing T is false — a self-referential paradox resolved by operating at two levels of description (constitutive vs. self-modeling).
  • Three extensions: (a) Quantum — replaces classical kernel with a CPTP/Lindbladian map, where measurement = intervention, Hilbert factorization is non-unique, and the agent must suppress µ-η entanglement to keep the classical MDP operative; (b) Non-dual philosophy — convergences with Daoism, Madhyamaka two-truths, Advaita, and the līlā (divine play) framing where the agent "plays" rather than "optimizes"; (c) LLM instantiation as a constrained state machine.
  • Concrete LLM design: Loop S_t → I_{t+1} ~ P_θ(·|S_t, M_t) → Γ → S_{t+1} with an admissibility filter Γ(I, S, Cα, V, Π_safety) ∈ {0,1}; proposes autotelic-specific evaluation metrics — continuation coherence, boundary-maintenance violation rate, goal-emergence diversity, self-repair capacity.

Methodology

Conceptual/theoretical synthesis (cs.AI position paper), not an empirical benchmark study. Method proceeds by:

  1. Diagnosing the given-objective paradigm: Shows standard MDP/RL/supervised framing treats r (or L) as exogenous; reward misspecification/hacking [12,13] and wireheading [17,18] are symptoms of this paradigm, not problems to be solved on the designer's terms.
  2. Layered ascent: Walks through intrinsic motivation (sec 2) → resource-driven goal priors via LEAST taxonomy and resource functional Cα(g) (sec 3, e.g. Levin complexity, speed prior, logical depth, QKSA) → causal-interventional learning via algorithmic information dynamics ΔK(p,δ) (sec 4) → homeostasis/viability r_viab = 1[s ∈ V] as a self-justifying fixed point.
  3. Embedded agency (sec 5): Adopts Markov-blanket partition (µ, b, η) with conditional independence p(µ,η|b)=p(µ|b)p(η|b); cites results that statistical blankets coincide with physical interaction surfaces [62] and that free-energy minimization induces rather than presupposes the partition [63]. Engages Demski-Garrabrant embedded agency (decision theory, embedded world-models, robust delegation, subsystem alignment) and space-time embedded intelligence [67].
  4. Dissolution of agency (sec 6): Surveys coarse-graining criteria (mutual information under intervention [74], integrated information Φ (IIT) [76], causal macro-regularities [77]) and pairs them with non-dual philosophy (Metzinger self-model [78,79], enactivism [82], Parfit personal identity [83], Taoism [84], Madhyamaka [85]). Consolidates into the (π, G, µ, Cα, V, b, M) tuple.
  5. Extensions (sec 7): Quantum (CPTP/Lindbladian formalism, QKSA, AIXI-q, QAIXI, and proposed milestones QKSQA/EQKSQA/EQKSQAA); philosophy (neutral monism, wuji, līlā); LLM agentic instantiation as constrained continuation with admissibility filter.

No experiments are run; the only quasi-engineering artifact is the LLM loop sketch, which is design-level and explicitly invites empirical work.

Results & Highlights

  • No quantitative results — this is a position/synthesis paper. All "results" are conceptual theorems-in-prose:
  • Intrinsic motivation does not by itself constitute autotelesis (it supplies r_int, not G).
  • Any neutral-looking prior over goals smuggles in representation-level commitments.
  • Embeddedness individuates the agent but reveals non-unique individuation.
  • The consolidated tuple binds the components via joint operation of V (defended), b (bounds V), and M (takes b as fundamental while acting, relativizes it while inquiring).
  • Proposed autotelic evaluation metrics (the closest thing to measurable claims): continuation coherence, boundary-maintenance violation rate, goal-emergence diversity, self-repair capacity — to separate productivity from agency's structural properties.
  • Quantum roadmap: proposes a layered research agenda (QKSQA → EQKSQA → EQKSQAA), explicitly flagged as future work.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High as a synthesis/position piece. The explicit relocation of the hard problem from goal-space to self-space, and the pairing of a quantum cut with non-dual contemplative philosophy within a single autotelic framework, is distinctive. Novelty is in the unification and reframing, not in any single component (Markov blankets, embedded agency, intrinsic motivation are all prior art).
  • Rigor: Mixed. The necessary-but-not-sufficient ladder is argued carefully and each cited formalism (AIXI/KSA, Levin complexity, FEP, embedded agency four-problem framing) is characterized accurately. However, the load-bearing claims — "individuation is non-unique," "the agent must believe its boundary to act and see through it to understand" — are asserted rather than proved; the non-uniqueness leans on [74] (mutual-information coarse-graining) and IIT [76] without a derivation. The "consolidated tuple" is a notational packaging of prior concepts rather than a new formal object.
  • Reproducibility: Low for the theoretical core (expected for a position paper). The LLM instantiation is the only potentially reproducible artifact, and it is described at design/architecture level (filter Γ, budget Cα, self-model M) without implementation, baselines, or code. The proposed evaluation metrics are defined but not operationalized with thresholds or datasets.
  • Significance: Potentially high for the autotelic / open-ended / self-improving agent subfield if the self-generation reframing gains traction and is operationalized; modest-to-nil for the broader empirical ML community (no benchmarks, no models). The quantum roadmap (QKSQA/EQKSQA/EQKSQAA) is speculative and far from any runnable system.
  • Red flags:
  • Single-author grand-unification claims spanning quantum mechanics, Eastern contemplative philosophy, and LLM agents is a classic high-ambition pattern warranting skepticism.
  • Rhetoric-heavy load-bearing sentences ("believe in its own boundary," "see through that boundary," "the agent does not optimize but plays") — evocative but under-formalized.
  • The non-dual/quantum extensions risk being analogical rather than formally operationalized; e.g., the quantum section asserts "the agent must suppress entanglement between µ and η so that the classical MDP framework is operative" without showing this is achievable or measurable.
  • The LLM design is essentially "constrained decoding + admissibility filter + persistent memory," which is a reasonable but not novel agentic pattern dressed in autotelic vocabulary; the mapping from tuple to LLM components is mostly relabeling.
  • No comparison to active alternatives (e.g., other self-modeling agent architectures, open-endedness methods, GOFAI self-reference work beyond Demski-Garrabrant).

Strengths

  • Genuinely integrative: brings intrinsic motivation, resource complexity, causal-interventional learning, homeostasis, embedded agency, and non-dual philosophy into one coherent argument with a unifying tuple.
  • Correct and careful characterization of the cited formal machinery (MDP/AIXI/KSA, LEAST resource taxonomy, Levin/speed priors, FEP, Demski-Garrabrant embedded agency).
  • The necessary-but-not-sufficient ladder is a useful pedagogical and conceptual organizer for the autotelic subfield.
  • The reframe (self-generation, not goal-generation, is the bottleneck) is a non-trivial and arguably correct re-prioritization for self-evolving agent research.
  • Concrete enough to propose an LLM instantiation and autotelic-specific evaluation metrics rather than remaining purely philosophical.
  • Author has a prior track record (QKSA, YAQQ), so this is a continuation of a real research program, not a drive-by.

Limitations

  • No empirical evidence; all claims are conceptual. The LLM instantiation is a design sketch with no implementation, baselines, or measured results.
  • The central non-uniqueness-of-individuation claim is asserted (citing [74,76]) rather than derived; the strength of the whole argument hinges on it.
  • The quantum and non-dual extensions are largely analogical/programmatic; the quantum roadmap (QKSQA/EQKSQA/EQKSQAA) is far from runnable and its utility for near-term AI is unclear.
  • Single-author synthesis underrepresents competing positions in active debates (e.g., critics of the free-energy principle's blanket claims, IIT controversies, embedded-agency skeptics).
  • The LLM design's mapping to the tuple is partly relabeling — "admissibility filter + budget + memory" is a known agentic pattern; the autotelic contribution is the framing, not the mechanism.
  • Autotelic evaluation metrics are defined but not benchmarked; "goal-emergence diversity" and "self-repair capacity" need operationalization to be meaningful.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How is the self-model M concretely realized in the proposed LLM loop, and can "boundary-maintenance violation rate" be measured against existing autonomous-agent benchmarks?
  • Can the non-uniqueness of Markov-blanket individuation be tested empirically — e.g., by running different coarse-grainings on the same dynamical system and observing distinct emergent goal sets?
  • Does the quantum extension add measurable capability over a classical FEP/active-inference agent, or is it purely a re-description? What would falsify the quantum-physicalization claim?
  • If the agent must "relativize its own self," how does the framework address goal-misgeneralization and mesa-optimization — where is the safety-relevant target once the boundary is treated as fiction?
  • What baseline (RLHF agent, intrinsic-motivation agent, open-endedness method) would the proposed LLM instantiation be evaluated against on the four autotelic metrics?
  • How does robust delegation interact with M's self-relativization — can a self-relativizing agent ever certify its successor's objective given the Löbian obstruction cited in [69]?
  • Is there a minimal empirical demo (toy MDP or text-world) that would distinguish an "autotelic" agent from a well-tuned curiosity/empowerment agent?
