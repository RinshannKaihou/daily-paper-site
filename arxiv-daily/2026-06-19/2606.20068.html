Process-Verified Reinforcement Learning for Theorem Proving via Lean

arXiv: 2606.20068

Date: 2026-06-19

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20068

一句话总结： 本文将 Lean 证明助手当作过程级奖励预言机（process oracle）：在每个 tactic 上根据 Lean 的 elaboration 结果给出细粒度的正确/错误信号，结合 first-error propagation 与 first-token credit assignment 融入 GRPO 目标，在 MiniF2F/ProofNet 上取得比纯 outcome-only RL 更稳定且一致的提升。
TL;DR: Instead of relying on a single binary pass/fail signal in RLVR for formal theorem proving, the authors use the Lean proof assistant itself as a symbolic process oracle, parsing each proof into tactics and converting Lean's elaboration (locally sound vs. earliest-failing step) into dense, verifier-grounded tactic-level rewards, combined with outcome rewards inside a GRPO objective via first-error propagation and first-token credit assignment — yielding stable, consistent gains on MiniF2F and ProofNet over outcome-only baselines.

核心贡献

  • 形式化 Lean 的符号反馈：将 Lean 在 proof、tactic 两个层级的符号反馈（全局 pass/fail 函数 g(Y)、逐 tactic 评分函数 φ(Y,T)）形式化为可供 RL 直接使用的标量信号，避免了需要人工标注或学习型 PRM 的依赖。
  • 符号验证器引导的 RL：把 outcome 奖励与 tactic 级过程奖励同时融入 GRPO 风格目标，通过 first-error propagation（一旦某 tactic 出错，其后所有 tactic 视为错误）与 first-token credit（tactic 优势只赋给该 tactic 的首 token）实现稠密且可信的信用分配，无需 value model 或额外 PRM。
  • 理论解释：在附录中给出该方法作为 potential-based reward shaping 的离散近似的数学刻画（将"当前 prefix 能被补全为合法 Lean 证明"的概率作为势函数，错误状态为吸收态、势为 0），为方法提供理论基础。
  • 稳定的基准提升：在 MiniF2F 和 ProofNet 上，对 STP-Lean 与 DeepSeek-Prover-V1.5 两种 backbone，tactic 级训练在多数设置下超过 outcome-only GRPO，且单次 whole-proof 生成（pass@64 59.2%）逼近甚至达到需要大量搜索的树搜索方法（58.5%）。
  • 更广的视角：明确指出符号证明助手不应只在评估时充当 verifier，还能在训练时充当过程级奖励预言机，为"LLM 可扩展性 + 符号验证可靠性"的结合指明方向。

Key Contributions

  • Formalizes Lean's symbolic, tree-structured feedback (outcome g(Y) ∈ {0,1} and per-tactic φ(Y,T) ∈ {1, d1, d2}) into scalar training signals usable directly by an RL algorithm, removing the need for learned PRMs or human step-level annotations.
  • Integrates both outcome-level and tactic-level advantages into a GRPO-style objective using a first-error propagation rule and a first-token credit-assignment strategy, yielding dense, verifier-grounded credit without an auxiliary value model.
  • Provides a mathematical grounding (Appendix J) interpreting the credit assignment as a discrete approximation of potential-based reward shaping, where the potential of a state is the probability its prefix completes to a valid Lean proof and the error state is absorbing with zero potential.
  • Demonstrates that tactic-level supervision consistently matches or surpasses outcome-only GRPO across MiniF2F and ProofNet for both STP-Lean and DeepSeek-Prover-V1.5, and that single-shot whole-proof generation (59.2% pass@64) approaches heavy tree-search baselines (58.5%) at far lower inference compute.
  • Reframes symbolic proof assistants as usable during training (not just at evaluation), opening a path for combining LLM scalability with the soundness of symbolic verification in formal reasoning.

Methodology

The method turns the Lean 4 proof assistant into a training-time process oracle through a three-part pipeline:

  1. Parsing and scoring (formalizing Lean's feedback). For a generated proof Y, Lean produces (i) a global outcome g(Y) = 1 iff the whole proof verifies, else 0; and (ii) per-tactic feedback via info trees and error logs. Proofs are parsed from the AST into an ordered tactic sequence f(Y) = (T_1, …, T_{N(Y)}). A tactic not present in Lean's error log is locally sound under dependent type theory. The per-tactic score is φ(Y, T_k) = 1 if the proof passes; = d1 if the proof fails but the tactic has no error and precedes the first error; = d2 once at or after the first error (j = min{i : T_i contains an error}). Concretely the authors used d1 = −0.05, d2 = −0.1.
  1. Reduction to per-tactic rewards via a tactic-level MDP. The authors define a tactic-level MDP M = (S, A, r, F, m) where states are partial proof prefixes, actions are individual Lean tactics, and transitions are deterministic concatenation (s_{j+1} = s_j ⊕ a_j). Lean feedback affects only the reward r(s, a), not the transition. First-error propagation enforces causal/type-theoretic soundness: because the LLM generates autoregressively, once the first erroneous tactic occurs, all continuations are conditioned on an invalid prefix and are treated as wrong.
  1. Mapping to token-level advantages and integration into GRPO. Outcome advantage A_outcome,i,t is the group-normalized g(Y_i) applied uniformly to all tokens (standard GRPO). Process advantage A_process,i,j = r_process(i,j) − mean(g(Y_1…Y_G)) uses the mean outcome as a difficulty-aware baseline. Crucially, the combined advantage places the tactic advantage only on the first token of each tactic:

A_i,t = A_outcome,i,t + 1{t = first(T_{i,s(i,t)})} · A_process,i,s(i,t),

then fed into the GRPO clipped-ratio objective with a KL term. The rationale: in Lean the first token is the tactic keyword (e.g., intro, apply, have), which determines the proof strategy and constrains subgoals.

The setup avoids value functions / return-based credit, which the authors found led to unstable optimization in the proof setting (a suboptimal tactic that is later "rescued" by high returns would be unsound). The whole pipeline runs on top of standard REPL-based Lean verification with a per-attempt timeout (15s used in main experiments), adding negligible overhead beyond sorting/scoring.

Results & Highlights

  • MiniF2F-Test (whole-proof methods, pass@64): STP-Lean + Ours reaches 59.2% ± 0.5, vs. STP-Lean baseline 56.7%–57.9% (a +2.5pp gain over GRPO's +1.2pp). DeepSeek-Prover-V1.5 + STP + Ours reaches 57.8% ± 0.4.
  • ProofNet-Test: STP-Lean + Ours reaches 19.0% ± 0.3 at pass@64, an improvement of +1.4pp at pass@32 over the baseline; gains are consistent if modest.
  • Reaches search-based baselines without search: single-shot 59.2% pass@64 approaches InternLM2.5-StepProver / DeepSeek-Prover-RL + RMaxTS (58.5%) while avoiding large search-time compute — though the authors explicitly note budgets are not directly comparable.
  • Ablations (Table 2, STP-Lean): outcome-only 57.9% (pass@64); tactic-only 56.8%; outcome+tactic (theirs) 59.2% — only the combination of global objective + local credit improves consistently across both benchmarks. On DeepSeek-Prover, plain GRPO yields no gains on ProofNet-Test and sometimes underperforms the supervised baseline, whereas the combined method is stable.
  • Token-credit ablation (Table 3): first-token placement (57.1/59.2) beats all-tokens, last-token, and entropy-based token selection — matching the semantics that the tactic keyword is the key decision point.
  • Reward-strategy ablation (Table 4): removing first-error propagation, removing the difficulty baseline, or collapsing d1 = d2 all degrade performance — sequential error propagation, difficulty normalization, and differentiated penalties are each necessary.
  • Timeout study (Figure 3): 5s is too short (too few valid signals); 10–30s are strong, with 15s best; 10–15s sometimes beat 30s because discarding over-long proofs biases toward shorter, canonical, better-generalizing proofs.
  • Training dynamics (Figure 2): combined outcome+tactic converges to lower entropy (more decisive policy) without mode collapse — average proof length stays stable, so gains are not from output lengthening.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate-to-high. The core idea — use Lean itself as a process reward oracle rather than a learned PRM — is clean and well-motivated, and the first-error propagation + first-token credit instantiation is specific and principled. The framing of "proof assistants as process oracles during training (not just evaluation)" is a genuinely useful conceptual contribution. The building blocks (GRPO, step-level credit, Shapley/PRM-style ideas) are individually known, so novelty lies in the synthesis and the Lean-specific reduction.
  • Rigor: Reasonable but with caveats. Ablations are thorough for what is tested (token-credit variants, reward-strategy components, timeouts, outcome-only vs. tactic-only vs. combined). Multiple seeds with reported mean ± std are included. However, gains are often small (single-digit pp, sometimes within ~1pp of baselines given the std), and on ProofNet some cells show negligible or no improvement. The paper honestly flags these.
  • Reproducibility: Mixed. The authors claim full details in Section 5.1 and Appendix B (hyperparameters, Lean version, HuggingFace/TRL). However, no public code/data link is visible in the extracted text, and the formal Lean feedback parsing pipeline (info-tree parsing, error-log interpretation) is non-trivial to re-implement without their tooling. Training is on only 10k samples from the 3.26M-proof STP set with a 15s Lean timeout — so absolute numbers depend heavily on this specific, small compute regime.
  • Significance: Solid within its niche. Within formal theorem proving + RL, this is a meaningful, ICLR-accepted contribution. Its broader significance to the general "process reward for reasoning" community is more modest: the technique is Lean-specific and the authors themselves note it has not been extended to long natural-language CoT.
  • Red flags:
  • The headline "consistent improvements" are partly driven by cherry-picked best budgets; on ProofNet pass@64 the improvement over GRPO is essentially zero (19.0% vs 19.0% for STP; 18.5% vs 17.6% for DeepSeek).
  • Fixed tactic penalties d1, d2 are hand-tuned and the authors admit sensitivity across models/datasets — a general advantage estimator is left as future work.
  • No comparison to learned PRMs is provided (justified by data scarcity, but it leaves the central "process oracle" advantage less conclusively demonstrated vs. the strongest alternative).
  • Comparison to tree-search is qualitative ("approaches their accuracy") with an explicit footnote that budgets are not directly comparable — readers should not read 59.2% as beating search.

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated use of an existing symbolic verifier to provide dense, sound per-step rewards — sidesteps the brittleness and annotation cost of learned PRMs.
  • Principled design choices each backed by an ablation (first-token credit, first-error propagation, difficulty baseline, differentiated penalties, timeout).
  • Honest reporting of marginal/negative cases and explicit acknowledgment of limitations.
  • Strong conceptual framing that should generalize the conversation about "verifier-as-process-oracle" beyond Lean.
  • Already peer-reviewed (ICLR 2026), from a reputable lab.

Limitations

  • Lean-specific; does not address natural-language chain-of-thought reasoning where step correctness is not symbolically checkable.
  • Gains are modest and on ProofNet sometimes vanish; statistical significance of the smallest improvements is unclear from reported std.
  • Fixed scalar tactic rewards (d1, d2) with acknowledged cross-model sensitivity; no learned/adaptive advantage estimator.
  • Trained on only 10k samples from STP; absolute numbers are tied to a particular small-compute, non-CoT, 15s-timeout regime.
  • No comparison against learned PRMs; no released code/data referenced in the visible text, raising the practical reproducibility bar.
  • Single-shot whole-proof comparison to tree search is not apples-to-apples (budgets differ), so claims of "approaching search accuracy" should be read cautiously.

Relevance

与"LLM 隐藏状态/可解释性"以及"ML 可靠性与数学基础"两条兴趣线间接相关：它把符号验证器嵌入 RL 训练做稠密、可验证的信用分配，可作为"形式化、可信的过程级奖励信号"在推理可靠性上的参考案例，但并非直接针对训练稳定性、隐藏状态几何或精度误差等核心关注点，相关性中等偏弱。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can the tactic-level process oracle be generalized beyond Lean to semi-formal / natural-language CoT via a separately trained verifier, and would the first-error-propagation principle still hold without strict type-theoretic soundness?
  • How sensitive are the gains to the d1, d2 magnitudes across model scales (e.g., 1.5B vs 7B vs larger)? Is there a principled way to learn or anneal them?
  • Does combining this process-verified RL with light tree search at inference compound, or does the denser training signal already saturate the gains search would provide?
  • Could the potential-based-reward-shaping interpretation (Appendix J) yield a learned potential estimator that replaces the fixed tactic scores and improves cross-dataset robustness?
  • The training used only 10k of 3.26M STP proofs — do the reported advantages persist or widen at larger training-set sizes, or do outcome-only and tactic-level methods converge as data scales?
  • What is the effect of CoT vs. non-CoT generation here, given the authors generate pure Lean without long CoT? Does adding CoT change the relative value of tactic-level credit?
  • How does the method interact with quantization / mixed-precision training of the prover model — relevant to computation-reliability concerns about whether verifier-grounded credit remains stable under low precision?
