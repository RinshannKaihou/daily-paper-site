> **一句话总结：** 本文从量化 bin 的几何不对称出发，揭示非均匀 FP4 格式（E2M1）在 Round-to-Nearest-Even 下存在系统性负向"收缩偏置"（Shrinkage Bias），它随层乘性累积并被 Random Hadamard Transform 放大，是 NVFP4 等 E2M1-based 配方训练不稳定的统一根因；作者据此提出 UFP4——基于 E1M2/INT4 均匀网格、在三个 GEMM 全程启用 RHT、且仅对 dY 用随机舍入的 4-bit 训练配方，在 Dense 1.5B / MoE 7.9B / MoE 124B 长时预训练中持续降低 BF16 相对损失误差。

> **TL;DR:** The paper traces FP4 pretraining instability to a geometric root cause: non-uniform formats like E2M1 (used in NVFP4 on Blackwell/Rubin and AMD MI350) suffer Shrinkage Bias — a systematic negative RTNE rounding error from asymmetric bins — that accumulates multiplicatively across layers and is amplified, not mitigated, by the Random Hadamard Transform (RHT). The authors propose UFP4: an E1M2/INT4-style uniform 4-bit recipe with RHT applied to all three training GEMMs (fwd_y, bwd_dx, bwd_dw) and stochastic rounding restricted to dY, which consistently reduces BF16-relative loss error on Dense 1.5B, MoE 7.9B, and MoE 124B long pretraining runs, and advocate that future accelerators treat uniform grids as first-class FP4 training primitives.

## 核心贡献

- **几何归因（Shrinkage Bias）**：首次将 FP4 训练不稳定归因于非均匀格式（E2M1）量化 bin 的几何不对称——在 RTNE 下，相邻 bin 宽度不等（r_i > l_i）会导致条件期望误差为负（公式推导 −(ℓ_i−r_i)/4 = (q_{i+1}−q_{i−1}−2q_i)/4）。例如 E2M1 在 q=2 处偏置为 −0.125。均匀网格（E1M2/INT4）满足 ℓ_i=r_i，从根本上消除该误差。
- **乘性累积与 RHT 放大**：通过张量正交分解（A=α_A·A+R_A）证明 GEMM 主信号衰减因子 η≈α_A·α_B<1，跨 K 层乘性累积 ∏η_k≈exp(−Σδ_k)；非叶子路径（fwd_y、bwd_dx）的误差会级联放大。同时 RHT 把张量从"动态范围受限"变为"局部分辨率受限"，使张量质量进入 E2M1 最不对称的 bin，从而 ∆SQNR 反而为负——这是 RHT 在 E2M1 上的隐藏陷阱。
- **均匀网格的优势与 RHT 配合**：在真实 MLP 张量上验证，RHT 显著提升 effective bucket ratio（如 linear_fc2/fwd_x 从 0.56 升至 0.97），但对 E2M1 反而降低 SQNR，对 E1M2 提升约 +3.24 dB；RHT 会反转格式排序（旋转前 E2M1 略优，旋转后 E1M2 更优）。
- **UFP4 配方**：基于 E1M2/INT4 均匀网格，对前向 FPROP(fwd_y)、数据梯度 DGRAD(bwd_dx)、权重梯度 WGRAD(bwd_dw) 三个 GEMM 全部施加 RHT，随机舍入（SR）仅用于 dY；block size 1×16，FP32 单层 scale，与 E2M1 基线其他配置保持一致以隔离格式与 RHT 范围的影响。
- **规模验证与 Scaling-law**：在 Dense 1.5B、MoE 7.9B、MoE 124B 长时预训练中，最新 1000 步 BF16 相对损失误差分别从 1.2570%→0.9673%、2.3596%→1.8469%、1.7308%→1.3863%；10M–324M MoE scaling-law 外推显示 E1M2 曲线持续低于 E2M1，且拟合的 FP4-to-BF16 gap 不随 compute 增长。
- **消融与可融合性**：消融表明 full-RHT（vs 无 RHT）降损 0.01123，SR-on-dY 再降 0.00456；范围受限 E2M1（max_fpx=2/3/4）不能替代原生均匀网格；fused RHT+quantization 在 SM90/SM100 上分别是独立 quant. 的 1.06×/1.07× 延迟，远低于 unfused 的 1.62×/1.41×。
- **硬件建议**：呼吁未来加速器（含 HiFloat4/Ascend 960 这类已有均匀 S1P2 元素的系统）将 E1M2/INT4 风格均匀网格作为一等 FP4 训练原语，E2M1 仍保留给离群值重的推理负载。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalizes Shrinkage Bias**: Derives (Eq. 2) that asymmetric RTNE bins in non-uniform formats (E2M1) yield a systematic negative expected rounding error — e.g., −0.125 at q=2 — while uniform grids (E1M2/INT4) eliminate it by construction.
- **Propagation and RHT-exacerbation theory**: Uses an orthogonal tensor decomposition to show GEMM signal attenuation η≈α_A·α_B<1 accumulates multiplicatively across K layers (∏η_k≈exp(−Σδ_k)); proves RHT shifts tensors into E2M1's most asymmetric bins, flipping the grid ranking after rotation.
- **UFP4 recipe**: A uniform E1M2/INT4 4-bit recipe applying RHT to all three training GEMMs and stochastic rounding only to dY, with all other knobs (block size 1×16, single-level FP32 scale, SR scope) matched to the E2M1 baseline to isolate the grid effect.
- **Scale-level validation**: Reports BF16-relative loss error reductions on Dense 1.5B (1.26%→0.97%), MoE 7.9B (2.36%→1.85%), and MoE 124B (1.73%→1.39%); scaling-law extrapolation over 10M–324M MoE shows the advantage persists and the FP4 gap does not grow with compute.
- **Mechanistic ablations**: Full-RHT beats no-RHT by 0.01123; SR-on-dY adds 0.00456; range-restricted E2M1 (max_fpx=2/3/4) cannot emulate a true uniform grid; fused RHT+quant kernels add only ~6–7% latency over standalone quantization on SM90/SM100.
- **Hardware recommendation**: Argues future training accelerators should expose E1M2/INT4-style uniform grids as first-class FP4 data elements alongside E2M1 (which should remain for range-limited workloads).

## Methodology

The paper proceeds in four analytical layers and one experimental layer:

1. **Geometric origin (§3.1)**: For a non-negative normalized codebook G⁺={0=q_0<…<q_n}, the interior of each RTNE bin around q_i has widths ℓ_i=(q_i−q_{i−1})/2 and r_i=(q_{i+1}−q_i)/2. Under locally uniform density, the conditional expected RTNE error is E[ρ_G(t)−t | t∈B_i]=(ℓ_i−r_i)/4 (Eq. 2), so any r_i>ℓ_i (which E2M1 has at spacing transitions like q=2,4) yields negative Shrinkage Bias. Uniform grids satisfy ℓ_i=r_i for all bins and cancel the term.

2. **Systemic impact (§3.2)**: Decomposing quantized operands Ã=α_A·A+R_A with ⟨R_A,A⟩=0 gives Z̃_q=α_A·α_B·AB⊤ + residual noise, so the coherent signal is attenuated by η≈α_A·α_B<1 per GEMM and by ∏η_k across K layers (Eq. 5) — multiplicative, hence non-cancelling. Non-leaf outputs (fwd_y, bwd_dx) cascade this decay. RHT then shifts outlier energy into mid-magnitude mass, where E2M1's asymmetric bins dominate; bucket entropy metric B_eff and ∆SQNR=10·log₁₀(NMSE_I/NMSE_H16) quantify this.

3. **UFP4 design (§4)**: Once RHT moves tensors into the local-resolution-limited regime, the grid should prioritize local magnitude preservation over dynamic range → choose E1M2/INT4. Because the grid is now unbiased, RHT can be safely extended to all three GEMMs (whereas E2M1 recipes restrict RHT to bwd_dw to avoid compounding geometric error). SR is kept only on dY to preserve gradient expectations.

4. **Experiments (§5)** organized as Q1–Q5: (Q1) tensor- and GEMM-level SQNR with/without RHT on real MLP tensors; (Q2) long-run BF16-relative loss on Dense 1.5B / MoE 7.9B / MoE 124B; (Q3) Ling scaling-law protocol over 10M–324M MoE; (Q4) ablation of RHT scope and SR scope, plus a range-restricted E2M1 emulation test; (Q5) fused vs unfused RHT+quantization kernel latency on SM90/SM100. The E2M1 baseline is selected by a controlled configuration search (§B) with all auxiliary settings (block size, scale hierarchy, SR scope) matched.

## Results & Highlights

- **Q1 (single tensor / GEMM)**: On well-behaved linear_fc1/fwd_x, RHT is near-neutral for both formats. On outlier-heavy linear_fc2/fwd_x, RHT reverses the ranking: E2M1 leads before rotation (21.90 vs 19.94 dB) but E1M2 leads after (23.19 vs 20.00 dB), with B_eff rising 0.56→0.97. The inversion persists in GEMM outputs (fwd_y, bwd_dx, bwd_dw): E1M2 preserves/improves SQNR, E2M1 often loses it after rotation (e.g., linear_fc2 fwd_y: E1M2 +2.51 dB vs E2M1 −2.05 dB).
- **Q2 (end-to-end)**: Latest-1000-step BF16-relative LM loss error drops on Dense 1.5B (1.2570%→0.9673%), MoE 7.9B (2.3596%→1.8469%), MoE 124B (1.7308%→1.3863%). Both FP4 recipes still leave a measurable gap to BF16.
- **Q3 (scaling)**: Ling-protocol scaling-law fit (10M@6e17 → 324M@2e20 FLOPs) shows the E1M2 curve stays below E2M1 across measured and extrapolated compute; the fitted FP4-to-BF16 gap decreases with compute.
- **Q4 (ablation)**: Full-RHT vs no-RHT → −0.01123 loss; SR-on-dY under full-RHT → −0.00456. Range-restricted E2M1 (max_fpx=2/3/4) all underperform the E2M1 reference on Dense 1.5B and MoE 7.9B, so E2M1 range clipping cannot substitute for native uniform grids.
- **Q5 (kernel)**: Fused RHT+quantization adds only 1.06× (SM90) and 1.07× (SM100) latency over standalone quantization, versus 1.62×/1.41× unfused — full-RHT training is practically cheap to fuse.
- **Concrete numbers to anchor**: E2M1 bin bias −0.125 at q=2; full-RHT + SR total reduction ~0.01579 on Dense 1.5B; MoE 124B final gap 1.3863% (UFP4) vs 1.7308% (E2M1).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — High.** The framing of FP4 instability as a *geometric* property of asymmetric RTNE bins (Shrinkage Bias), with an exact closed-form (Eq. 2) and a multiplicative-layer-accumulation argument (Eq. 5), is a clean unifying theory rather than an empirical patch. The observation that RHT *reverses* the preferred grid is a genuinely useful and counterintuitive insight.
- **Rigor — Solid.** The tensor-level theory is backed by GEMM-level SQNR diagnostics on real training tensors, controlled ablations isolating grid vs RHT scope vs SR scope, scaling-law extrapolation, and kernel measurements. The controlled E2M1-baseline selection (§B) is a commendable effort to make the comparison fair.
- **Reproducibility — Moderate, hardware-gated.** Two concerns. (1) Current FP4 hardware paths are E2M1-centric, so E1M2 results may rely on emulation/simulation rather than native silicon — the paper does not unambiguously state that all runs execute on real uniform-FP4 hardware. (2) Seed counts, variance bars, and exact training horizons for the 124B run are worth verifying in the appendix; figures show point curves without explicit error bars.
- **Significance — High if the uniform-grid path is realizable.** The actionable hardware recommendation (E1M2/INT4 as a first-class training primitive) is well-motivated and partially supported by emerging hardware (HiFloat4 on Ascend 960). If adopted, it reshapes the FP4 training stack.
- **Red flags / open questions**:
  - The "native vs emulated" status of the E1M2 GEMMs is the single most important caveat; if emulated, memory/throughput claims need qualification.
  - Only LM-loss parity is reported; downstream benchmark quality (e.g., MMLU, GSM8K, human evals) is absent, and loss parity does not guarantee capability parity.
  - The improvement magnitude (e.g., MoE 7.9B: 2.36%→1.85%) is meaningful but still leaves a >1.8% gap to BF16 — whether this matters for downstream tasks is untested.
  - SR restricted to dY is justified empirically by a 0.00456 reduction but the theoretical rationale for *not* applying SR to fwd_y/bwd_dx is thinner than the bias analysis.
  - Vendor alignment risk: if the agenda aligns with promoting a specific grid format, independent replication matters, though the analysis itself is vendor-neutral.

## Strengths

- Provides a principled, geometry-based diagnosis with a closed-form bias term, not an empirical patch.
- Unifies quantization grid geometry, RHT regime shift, and layer-wise accumulation in one coherent theory with two clear "Key Findings".
- Validates at meaningful scale (up to MoE 124B) with scaling-law support and per-GEMM ablations.
- Practical kernel evidence (fused RHT+quant adds only ~6–7% latency) addresses the "is full-RHT too expensive" objection.
- Concrete, actionable recipe (UFP4) plus a clear, narrowly-scoped hardware recommendation.
- Honest about residual gaps: explicitly states both FP4 recipes still leave a measurable BF16 gap.

## Limitations

- Does not clearly state whether E1M2 GEMMs run on shipping uniform-FP4 hardware or via emulation; this is the key practical caveat.
- No downstream task benchmarks — only LM loss is reported.
- Seed counts and variance bars not visible in the main figures.
- Range-restricted E2M1 emulation failing does not rule out other E2M1-side fixes (e.g., adaptive per-block format like MixFP4) that could close part of the gap on existing hardware.
- SR-on-dY-only rationale is empirical more than theoretical.
- The hardware recommendation depends on vendor roadmap adoption outside the authors' control.

## Relevance

与用户关注的 "Computation Reliability & Precision Errors" 及 "LLM Training Stability & Observability" 高度契合：本文直击低精度（FP4）预训练中由量化网格几何引发的系统性精度误差与训练不稳定，给出可解析的数值稳定性诊断与以稳定性为导向的训练配方，并从硬件原语层面提出减少精度误差的路径。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Are the E1M2 GEMMs executed on native uniform-FP4 silicon or emulated, and how does that affect the memory and throughput claims?
- Do downstream benchmark scores (MMLU, reasoning, code) confirm the LM-loss improvement translates to capability parity with BF16?
- How sensitive is the 124B result to RHT seed and Hadamard block size, and what are the run-to-run variances?
- Could adaptive per-block format selection (MixFP4-style) recover most of the uniform-grid benefit on existing E2M1-only hardware?
- Can Shrinkage Bias analysis predict instability a priori for other non-uniform low-bit formats (FP6, MXFP, E3M0 exponent-only)?
- Does SR-on-dY-only generalize across architectures (different attention/MoE routing variants), or is it specific to the tested Ling stack?
- What is the interaction between UFP4 and quantization-aware training / outlier-control methods — are they complementary or redundant?
- Given the residual >1% gap on MoE 7.9B, is there a hybrid BF16-critical-path + FP4-rest design that closes it further?
