> **一句话总结：** ScaffoldAgent 将开放式深度研究 (OEDR) 中的大纲演化建模为受控的结构化决策过程（Expansion/Contraction/Revision 三操作，受信念修正理论 AGM 启发），并用一个组合效用信号（检索相关性+新颖性、结构连贯+平衡-冗余、试生成的引用支持+覆盖-冗余）经 UCB 式节点选择与边际增益终止来动态调度大纲更新，在 DeepResearch Bench 上 RACE Overall 达到 44.70 (Qwen3-32B) / 48.27 (DeepSeek-V3.2)，C.acc. 达 54.32/62.20，均显著优于 STORM、WebWeaver、EDR 等基线。

> **TL;DR:** ScaffoldAgent treats open-ended deep research (OEDR) outline evolution as a structured decision process with three AGM-belief-revision-inspired operations (Expansion/Contraction/Revision), and drives node selection, operation scheduling, and termination with a composite utility signal combining retrieval (relevance + novelty), structure (coherence + balance − redundancy), and trial-generation (citation support + coverage − redundancy). On DeepResearch Bench it reaches 44.70 / 48.27 RACE Overall on Qwen3-32B / DeepSeek-V3.2 with citation accuracy 54.32 / 62.20, beating STORM, WebWeaver, EDR, and StackPlanner.

## 核心贡献

- **将大纲演化建模为结构化决策过程。** 受经典信念修正理论 AGM (Alchourrón et al., 1985) 启发，定义三种受控操作：Expansion（节点过粗时分解为子节点，调用 Search+Reporter）、Contraction（兄弟节点重叠/冗余时合并，仅调用 Reporter 检查连贯性）、Revision（节点证据薄弱或过时时刷新意图与证据，调用 Search+Reporter），从机制上对抗"骨架漂移"（scaffold drift, C1）。
- **效用引导的推理时反馈机制。** 用 Ut = λ1·Uret + λ2·Ustr + λ3·Ugen 作为推理时控制信号（而非梯度训练目标），其中 Uret = wrel·Rel + wnov·Nov，Ustr = wcoh·Coh + wbal·Bal − wred·Red，Ugen = α·ρsup + β·ρcov − γ·ρred。该信号统一驱动节点选择（UCB 式：argmax −ū(v) + c·√(ln Nt / nv)）、操作调度与终止（连续 k 步平均边际增益 ΔU < ε 时停止）。解决"延迟反馈"（C2）。
- **三智能体协同架构。** Outline Agent（中央控制器，维护演化大纲树 Tt=(Vt,Pt,Φt)，选择目标节点并决定操作）、Search Agent（用 Bocha web-search API 检索）、Reporter Agent（试写作与最终报告生成）。
- **跨骨干模型的稳健性。** 在 Qwen3-32B 与 DeepSeek-V3.2 两个骨干上，ScaffoldAgent 在所有 Bench 指标上单调提升（RACE 44.70→48.27, Eff.c. 30.42→51.18, C.acc. 54.32→62.20），而 IRCoT、StackPlanner 等基线随骨干波动剧烈，说明效用引导框架随骨干能力单调扩展、未过拟合某模型。

## Key Contributions

- **Outline evolution as a structured decision process.** Three controlled operations inspired by AGM belief-revision theory: Expansion (split an over-broad node into finer children, calls Search+Reporter), Contraction (merge semantically overlapping sibling nodes, calls Reporter only), Revision (refresh a weakly-supported/outdated node's intent and evidence, calls Search+Reporter but keeps tree size). These directly mitigate scaffold drift (C1).
- **Utility-guided inference-time feedback.** A composite utility Ut = λ1·Uret + λ2·Ustr + λ3·Ugen used as an inference-time control signal (not a training objective). Uret captures relevance + novelty of new evidence; Ustr captures coherence + balance − redundancy of the scaffold; Ugen scores trial-writing citation support + intent coverage − cross-section redundancy. This signal drives UCB-style node selection (argmax −ū(v) + c·√(ln Nt / nv)), operation scheduling, and termination (stop when average marginal gain over k steps < ε), addressing delayed feedback (C2).
- **Three-agent architecture.** Outline Agent (central controller maintaining the evolving tree Tt=(Vt,Pt,Φt)), Search Agent (Bocha web-search API), Reporter Agent (trial writing + final report). Expansion/Revision invoke both Search and Reporter; Contraction invokes only Reporter.
- **Backbone-robust scaling.** On both Qwen3-32B and DeepSeek-V3.2, ScaffoldAgent improves monotonically across every Bench metric, whereas baselines (IRCoT, StackPlanner) fluctuate substantially — evidence the framework scales with backbone capability rather than overfitting.

## Methodology

ScaffoldAgent instantiates OEDR as utility-guided outline optimization. The task formulation treats OEDR as dynamic interaction among three mutually-dependent components — Search (retrieve external evidence), Outline (hierarchical scaffold defining report scope), Report (synthesize coherent long-form text).

**State — Evolving Outline Tree.** Tt = (Vt, Pt, Φt), where each node v has Φt(v) = (hv, Ev, θv): intent hv, supporting evidence Ev, and utility statistics θv = (nv, ū(v)) (visit count, running mean utility). Tree starts from root node derived from query: T0 = Init(q).

**Iteration loop.** Each iteration: (1) Outline Agent selects target node vt via UCB-style rule vt = argmax_v [−ū(v) + c·√(ln Nt / nv)] — first term prioritizes low-utility nodes needing improvement, second term encourages exploration of rarely-visited nodes. (2) Chooses operation ot ∈ {Expansion, Contraction, Revision} based on the node's utility profile (insufficient granularity → Expansion; overlapping siblings → Contraction; weak support → Revision). (3) Executes operation via Update(Tt, vt, ot; πres, πrep), producing new utility Ut and updated statistics (nvt ← nvt+1; ū(vt) updated incrementally). (4) Termination when average marginal gain over a window falls below ε: (1/k)·Σ ΔUi < ε.

**Utility function (three dimensions).**
- Uret = wrel·Rel + wnov·Nov — Rel = semantic relevance of retrieved docs to node intent; Nov = novelty relative to evidence already attached.
- Ustr = wcoh·Coh + wbal·Bal − wred·Red — Coh = consistency among related nodes; Bal = structural balance; Red = redundant sibling branches penalty.
- Ugen = α·ρsup + β·ρcov − γ·ρred — ρsup = citation support; ρcov = alignment between node intent and generated content; ρred = cross-section redundancy. Reporter Agent trial-writes the affected node/subtree and the text is scored.

**Backbones & search.** DeepSeek-V3.2 and Qwen3-32B as LLM backbones; Bocha web-search API.

## Results & Highlights

**DeepResearch Bench (Table 1) — RACE quality + FACT grounding:**
- Qwen3-32B: ScaffoldAgent reaches 44.70 RACE Overall (+2.24 over best baseline IRCoT 42.46); Eff.c. 30.42, C.acc. 54.32. Citation accuracy gap is large — IRCoT C.acc. only 40.04, ReAct 23.78; WebWeaver RACE Overall 41.28 but C.acc. 32.72 (−13.42 vs. ScaffoldAgent under DeepSeek); EDR/STORM below 41 RACE Overall and below 37 C.acc.
- DeepSeek-V3.2: ScaffoldAgent 48.27 RACE Overall (+1.45 over StackPlanner 46.82), ranks first on all five RACE sub-dimensions (Overall/Comprehensiveness/Insight/Instruction-following/Readability). Monotonic improvement across backbones (RACE 44.70→48.27, Eff.c. 30.42→51.18, C.acc. 54.32→62.20).
- Notable: under DeepSeek-V3.2, IRCoT's C.acc. jumps to 75.18 and WebWeaver to 88.35 (very high) but ScaffoldAgent balances both quality and grounding.

**DeepResearch Gym (Table 2) — 6 dimensions (Clarity/Depth/Balance/Breadth/Support/Insight):**
- ScaffoldAgent best average 75.83 (+1.78 over EDR 74.05). Leads on Depth (82.40), Breadth (85.20), Insight (68.80). EDR highest Supportability (89.50) but ScaffoldAgent competitive at 79.80.

**Component ablations (RQ2):**
- Action ablation (DeepSeek-V3.2): w/o Contraction → RACE 48.27→44.72 (−3.55), C.acc. 62.20→57.79. w/o Revision → RACE 43.09 (−5.18, lowest single-action RACE), C.acc. 58.11 (citations stay attributable but underlying arguments weaker). Expansion-only → RACE 41.73/Eff.c. 45.20/C.acc. 59.32, plus non-terminating runs (outline inflates until budget exhausted). Confirms Contraction+Revision are load-bearing.
- Utility ablation: w/o Uret → Eff.c. 51.18→39.42 (−11.76, primarily hurts evidence supply). w/o Ustr → RACE 48.27→44.17 (−4.10, hurts global organization). w/o Ugen → RACE 43.58 (−4.69) and C.acc. 62.20→50.82 (−11.38, largest C.acc. drop). Ugen is most damaging overall.
- Operation mix (n=525): Expansion 59.0%, Contraction 22.9%, Revision 18.1%. Expansion dominates early iterations, then Contraction/Revision take over; all three active throughout.

**Multi-turn follow-ups (RQ3, Table 5, 100 instances):** ScaffoldAgent total 72.60 vs ReAct-GR 59.70 and ReAct-FW 57.76. Localized Revision/Expansion on affected subtree preserves unrelated sections (Comprehension 15.10 vs ReAct-FW 6.60), avoiding content loss and logical drift.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate).** The synthesis is tidy: AGM-inspired Expansion/Contraction/Revision + a three-term utility + UCB node selection + marginal-gain termination. Each ingredient has antecedents (MCTS/UCB, plan-then-write vs. dynamic-outline prior work like WebWeaver/DOME/WriteHERE, retrieval-utility in RAG, self-reflection). The concrete combination and using a trial-generation-anchored utility for inference-time outline control is the genuine contribution, but it is an engineering synthesis rather than a fundamentally new idea.
- **Rigor (Moderate-Good).** Two benchmarks, two backbones, per-dimension breakdowns, action and utility ablations with sensible attribution analysis (e.g., explaining why w/o Revision drops RACE but not C.acc.), and a behavior analysis of operation mix. However: (a) no seed counts, error bars, or significance tests reported — all gains stated as point numbers; (b) many key details (exact weights λ1/λ2/λ3, exploration coefficient c, termination window k and ε, utility sub-term weights) are deferred to appendices not visible in the main text; (c) LLM-as-judge scores (RACE, Gym) are inherently noisy and the paper does not report judge-model variance.
- **Reproducibility (Moderate).** Uses open backbones (DeepSeek-V3.2, Qwen3-32B) and the Bocha API, which is favorable. But the system is a multi-agent loop with many prompts, utility thresholds, and an unbounded iterative loop — exact reproduction without code/prompts is hard. No public code release is mentioned in the main text. The non-terminating Expansion-only runs observed in ablation underscore that the framework's behavior is sensitive to termination tuning.
- **Significance (Moderate-High).** Outline/scaffold management is a real and current bottleneck for deep-research agents. The gains over WebWeaver/EDR/STORM are consistent across two benchmarks and two backbones, and the multi-turn result (72.60 vs 59.70) is a practically meaningful capability. If code is released, this could be a reference architecture.
- **Red flags.** (1) Some baseline numbers are missing or marked "pending" (e.g., Prompt/RAG FACT columns show "–"). (2) DeepSeek-V3.2 IRCoT C.acc. of 75.18 and WebWeaver 88.35 are very high — these are FACT citation-accuracy figures; the fact that ScaffoldAgent (62.20) is *lower* on C.acc. than two baselines on the stronger backbone is underplayed — the paper emphasizes RACE Overall leadership instead. (3) Cost analysis (tokens, retrieval calls, wall-clock) of the iterative outline loop is absent in the visible text; the non-terminating Expansion-only failure hints the loop can be expensive. (4) Utility weights and termination hyperparameters are pushed to appendix, making the main-text results hard to audit for over-tuning. (5) Self-judged utility (especially Ugen via trial writing) risks reward hacking / circularity — not directly addressed.

## Strengths

- Addresses a genuine, currently active bottleneck (dynamic outline / scaffold management) in deep-research agents, not a toy task.
- Clean, well-motivated taxonomy of operations anchored in classical belief-revision theory — more principled than free-form outline rewriting.
- Unifying node selection, operation scheduling, and termination under one utility signal is elegant and operationally concrete.
- Strong ablation discipline: separates action-space ablation from utility-dimension ablation, with per-metric attribution explanations rather than hand-waving.
- Backbone-robustness argument (monotonic improvement across two models while baselines fluctuate) is more convincing than single-model claims.
- Multi-turn follow-up experiment shows a non-trivial capability (localized, non-destructive refinement) that fixed-outline baselines structurally cannot match.

## Limitations

- No statistical significance, error bars, or seed counts reported — all results are single-run point numbers.
- All quality metrics (RACE, FACT, Gym dimensions) are LLM-as-judge; no human evaluation or inter-judge agreement reported in the main text.
- Hyperparameters (utility weights λ, UCB exploration c, termination window k and ε, sub-term weights) deferred to appendix — main text cannot be audited for over-tuning.
- Cost (tokens, number of retrieval calls, latency) of the iterative outline loop is not reported; Expansion-only ablation shows the loop can run to budget exhaustion without Contraction/Revision.
- Self-judged Ugen (trial writing scored by an LLM) creates a circularity/reward-hacking risk that is not explicitly mitigated or discussed.
- On the stronger DeepSeek-V3.2 backbone, two baselines (WebWeaver 88.35, IRCoT 75.18) exceed ScaffoldAgent (62.20) on FACT C.acc.; the paper frames leadership around RACE Overall and downplays this.
- Generalization beyond DeepResearch Bench/Gym and beyond the two backbones is untested.

## Relevance

对关注"智能体 / 多智能体系统与长程检索推理"及"长文/报告生成"方向的研究者高度相关：ScaffoldAgent 直接面向开放式深度研究 (OEDR) 这一当前热点，其"大纲即结构化决策过程 + 三维效用信号驱动 UCB 节点选择、操作调度与边际增益终止"的设计，对 Agentic RAG、规划-检索-生成一体化以及多步检索推理系统的评估与训练均有直接借鉴价值，且其非破坏式多轮增量更新能力契合对话式深度研究助手的实际需求。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What are the exact values of λ1/λ2/λ3, the UCB exploration coefficient c, and the termination window k and ε — and how sensitive are results to each?
- How many seeds were run, and are the headline gaps (e.g., +2.24 RACE Overall on Qwen3-32B) statistically significant given LLM-judge noise?
- What is the token / retrieval-call / wall-clock cost overhead of the dynamic outline loop relative to fixed-outline baselines, and does utility-guided termination recover that cost?
- How is reward-hacking / circularity in self-judged Ugen (trial writing scored by the same backbone family) detected and mitigated?
- Why does ScaffoldAgent trail WebWeaver (88.35) and IRCoT (75.18) on FACT C.acc. under DeepSeek-V3.2 — is the utility signal trading citation precision for breadth/insight?
- Do the results hold on a third backbone (e.g., an OpenAI o-series or a smaller open model) and on a non-English or domain-specific corpus?
- Are DeepResearch Bench and DeepResearch Gym publicly released with stable judge prompts, and how do they compare in difficulty/coverage to other long-form QA benchmarks?
- Is there a direct diagnostic (e.g., an outline-stability / drift metric) showing ScaffoldAgent reduces scaffold drift versus baselines, beyond end-to-end quality gains?
