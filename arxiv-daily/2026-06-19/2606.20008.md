> **一句话总结：** VIMPO 从 KL 正则化 RL 最优性条件推出"策略隐含价值函数"，用终止边界条件 V*(s_T)=0 构造无 critic 的价值损失、用 log-ratio 形式的 TD 优势做 PPO 风格 actor 更新；在 Qwen3-4B 上对 GRPO 在 MATH-500/AIME24/AIME25/OlympiadBench 全面提升，并在 25% 奖励翻转下保持显著优势。

> **TL;DR:** VIMPO is a critic-free RLVR method that derives a policy-implied value function from the optimality conditions of KL-regularized RL on the deterministic-transition MDP of autoregressive generation; it uses the terminal boundary condition V*(s_T)=0 as a parameter-free value target and a closed-form log-ratio TD advantage for a PPO-style actor update, beating GRPO on math reasoning and staying robust under 25% reward flipping.

## 核心贡献

- 从 KL 正则化策略最优性条件（式 4）推导出价值恒等式：每个 token 的策略-参考 log-ratio 等于 Bellman 残差加一个 KL 修正项，且该残差项在 π* 下期望为零（天然零均值，带来训练稳定性）。
- 用终止边界条件 V*(s_T)=0 构造一个**无参数的价值监督目标**（式 7-8）：价值损失只依赖策略-参考 log-ratio 与"组均值中心化的最终奖励"，KL 项通过 stop-gradient 仅作中心化基线，避免通过操纵 KL 直接降低损失。
- 同一推导给出**闭式一步 TD 优势**（式 9）并可做 GAE 风格多步累积（式 10），直接接入 PPO actor 更新，实现 token 级信用分配而无需学习 critic。
- 关键设计：**外部奖励只通过价值损失进入，actor 更新只用策略内部 log-ratio 优势**——把"奖励纳入"与"策略改进"显式解耦。
- 实验在 Qwen3-4B-Base（verl 实现）上对 naive GRPO 与 token-level GRPO 全面提升，AIME25 上提升最显著（17.6→20.8 @avg@32）；在 25% 奖励翻转的噪声条件下仍保持明显优势。

## Key Contributions

- Derives a **central identity** (Eq. 4): the per-token policy/reference log-ratio equals a Bellman residual plus a KL correction, where the residual term is provably zero-mean under π* — a derived, not imposed, stability property.
- Uses the **terminal boundary condition V*(s_T)=0** to build a parameter-free value supervision target (Eq. 7-8): the value loss depends only on policy/reference log-ratios and group-mean-centered final rewards, with the KL term stop-gradient'd so it acts purely as a centering baseline (preventing the loss from being minimized by manipulating full-distribution KL).
- The same derivation yields a **closed-form one-step TD advantage** (Eq. 9) that can be accumulated in GAE form (Eq. 10) and plugged directly into a PPO actor update, enabling token-level credit assignment with no learned critic.
- Architectural choice: **external rewards enter only through the value loss, while the actor update uses only policy-internal log-ratio advantages** — explicitly separating reward incorporation from policy improvement.
- Empirically beats both naive GRPO and token-level GRPO on Qwen3-4B-Base (verl implementation), with the largest gain on AIME 2025 (17.6→20.8 avg@32), and retains a clear advantage under 25% reward flipping.

## Methodology

**Setup (3.1).** Autoregressive generation is modeled as a finite-horizon deterministic-transition MDP: state s_t = (x, a_0,...,a_{t-1}), transition P(s_{t+1}|s_t,a_t)=1. The value is the standard expected-return value (NOT the entropy-regularized soft value of Path Consistency Learning / soft Q-learning), so the authors control how KL enters each objective separately.

**Central identity (3.2).** From the KL-regularized local optimality condition (Eq. 1-2) and the collapsed Bellman equation (Eq. 3), eliminating the log partition yields the backbone identity:

    β·ln(π*(a_t|s_t) / πref(a_t|s_t)) = r(s_t,a_t) + γV*(s_{t+1}) − V*(s_t) + β·KL*(s_t)   (Eq. 4)

Three consequences: (i) a closed-form forward recurrence for V* given any anchor; (ii) the bracket β·ln(π*/πref) − β·KL* is zero-mean under π* (derived stability); (iii) a closed-form one-step TD advantage = β·ln(π*/πref) − β·KL*.

**Value loss (3.3).** Following DPO's strategy, the trainable policy π substitutes for π* in the closed form (Eq. 6). The terminal anchor Vπ(s_T)=0 gives the value objective L_V = ½·Vπ(s_T)² (Eq. 7). For outcome-only rewards (r=0 except terminal), with γ=1 and the group-mean return as the initial-value MC estimate, this unfolds into the operational form (Eq. 8): a sum over tokens of squared [β·log-ratio − β·sg[KL] − (R_final − R̄_final)], centered by the group-mean reward. The KL is stop-gradient'd so it cannot be reduced by manipulating the full-distribution KL.

**Actor update (3.4).** The closed-form advantage Â_t^TD = β·log(πθ/πref) − β·KL_π (Eq. 9) is treated as the advantage estimate; it can be GAE-accumulated (Eq. 10), normalized over valid tokens (Eq. 11), and fed into the negative-clipped PPO surrogate L_A. Total objective: L_VIMPO = L_V + c_A·L_A (Eq. 12). Crucially, the external reward enters ONLY through L_V; the actor advantage is purely policy-internal.

**Algorithm 1** details the training step: sample G rollouts per prompt, compute centered rewards, per-token ρ_t = β·log-ratio, κ_t = β·KL, advantage Â_t = ρ_t − κ_t, then L_V over (ρ_t − sg[κ_t]) − (R^(i) − R̄) and PPO actor loss.

**Hyperparameters (main runs).** β = 5e-4, c_A = 5e-3, held fixed after coarse tuning. Base model Qwen3-4B-Base, implemented in verl, trained on the math subset of Guru. GRPO baselines use token-level loss aggregation with KL reward penalty disabled (DAPO recipe); naive GRPO uses sequence-mean-token-mean loss. Shared hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate) are NOT separately tuned per method. Code: https://github.com/backprop07/VIMPO.

## Results & Highlights

**Clean-reward final accuracy (Table 1, avg@1 for MATH-500/OlympiadBench, avg@32 for AIME):**

| Method | MATH-500 | AIME24 | AIME25 | OlympiadBench | Avg |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Qwen3-4B-Base | 54.0 | 8.6 | 3.6 | 18.0 | 21.1 |
| Naive GRPO | 80.4 | 20.0 | 14.6 | 31.3 | 36.6 |
| GRPO (token-level) | 79.6 | 19.3 | 17.6 | 33.2 | 37.4 |
| **VIMPO** | **81.6** | **21.7** | **20.8** | **34.0** | **39.5** |
| GRPO noisy (25% flip) | 75.2 | 13.0 | 11.7 | 28.9 | 32.2 |
| **VIMPO noisy** | **78.2** | **18.3** | **14.9** | **32.3** | **35.9** |

- VIMPO wins every individual benchmark; the largest clean-reward gain is on **AIME 2025 (17.6→20.8)**, the hardest competition-style set.
- Under 25% reward flipping, both methods degrade but VIMPO's advantage is **larger** on AIME (e.g., AIME24: 13.0→18.3) — supporting the claim that decoupling reward incorporation from policy improvement reduces sensitivity to corrupted labels.
- Learning dynamics (Fig. 3): VIMPO shows stronger late-stage learning; response length initially rises then *decreases* while validation accuracy stays higher than GRPO — gains are not just from longer outputs.
- Ablations (Fig. 5): value-only (c_A=0) learns slower than full VIMPO; the actor update accelerates learning but increases policy/reference divergence (VO KL). High-β/high-c_A over-constrains the update and reduces the actor benefit, keeping VO KL flat but accuracy near the value-only baseline.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** Reframing the critic-vs-no-critic trade-off via a *policy-implied* value (derived from KL-regularized optimality rather than learned) is a genuinely fresh angle. It is conceptually adjacent to DPO/TDPO log-ratio parameterizations and to Path Consistency Learning / soft Q-learning, but applies them to *online RL with rollout rewards* on the deterministic-transition AR MDP — a non-trivial combination, and the zero-mean property of the log-ratio residual is a clean derived result.
- **Rigor: Solid.** The derivation is explicit and each step (Eqs. 1-12) is shown; the zero-mean and closed-form-advantage claims follow directly. The stop-gradient on KL is a sensible design detail with a clear justification (preventing loss minimization via KL manipulation).
- **Reproducibility: Good.** Single base model (Qwen3-4B-Base), standard framework (verl), shared hyperparameters across methods, public code, named benchmarks. The main weakness is that β and c_A are selected by "coarse tuning on training dynamics" — a mild hyperparameter-selection concern, though the same is true of GRPO baselines.
- **Significance: Moderate-to-high within math RLVR.** Gains over a properly implemented token-level GRPO (the recommended configuration) are real but modest on MATH-500 (+2.0) and OlympiadBench (+0.8), larger on AIME24 (+2.4) and AIME25 (+3.2). Whether this generalizes beyond Qwen3-4B, beyond math, and to larger compute is the open question that determines real impact.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - **Single model size (4B) and single domain (math).** No scaling-law evidence; no code/tool-use/multi-turn results.
  - **Advantage uses the current policy's KL vs. reference (κ_t) computed at each state** — cost relative to GRPO (extra forward passes for full-distribution KL over the vocab) is not quantified in the body.
  - The "zero-mean signal" is a property of π*, but training uses πθ ≠ π*; the approximation quality as the policy drifts is not empirically characterized beyond the VO KL proxy.
  - AIME24/AIME25 reported at avg@32 (not avg@1), so headline competition numbers benefit from majority voting; the gap may look different at avg@1.
  - No comparison against strong actor-critic baselines (e.g., VAPO) — only GRPO variants — so the claimed "middle ground" is argued theoretically, not shown against the dense-supervision side.

## Strengths

- Principled, end-to-end derivation from a single optimality condition; no ad-hoc reweighting tricks.
- Clean decoupling of reward incorporation (value loss) from policy improvement (actor update), with a plausible mechanism for noise robustness.
- Removes the critic entirely while still delivering token-level credit assignment — the central promise of the paper, delivered.
- Strongest empirical signal (AIME 2025, noisy rewards) is exactly where better credit assignment should matter most — internally consistent story.
- Honest ablations showing the value-only vs. actor trade-off and the failure mode of over-aggressive coefficients.

## Limitations

- Evaluated only on Qwen3-4B-Base and only on math RLVR; generalization is unproven.
- Gains over the recommended token-level GRPO are modest on some benchmarks (MATH-500, OlympiadBench).
- No actor-critic baseline (VAPO/PPO) comparison, so the "middle ground" is one-sided empirically.
- Compute/memory cost of per-token full-distribution KL (κ_t) is not reported.
- The zero-mean stability property holds exactly only at optimality; behavior far from π* is characterized only indirectly via VO KL.
- Hyperparameters β, c_A tuned on training dynamics, which risks mild overfitting to the evaluation setup.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the policy-implied value recurrence stay numerically stable as πθ drifts far from πref and log-ratios grow (the high-VO-KL regime), and can this be detected as a training anomaly?
- How does VIMPO's loss-spike / gradient-norm profile compare to GRPO and PPO over long runs — does the derived zero-mean property translate into measurably smoother training?
- Can the value loss (which ties log-ratios to centered rewards) be used as an early detector of reward hacking or verifier failure?
- Does the method scale to 30B+ models and to non-verifiable or learned-reward settings, or does it strictly require outcome-verifiable rewards?
- What is the wall-clock and memory overhead of computing per-token full-distribution KL (κ_t) at large vocab size vs. GRPO?
- How does VIMPO compare against a strong actor-critic baseline (VAPO) at matched compute, to validate the claimed "middle ground" empirically?
- Under noisy rewards, is the advantage driven by the value loss or the actor advantage being reward-decoupled — which can be isolated via the c_A ablation under noise?
