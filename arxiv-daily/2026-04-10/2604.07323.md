# Gaussian Approximation for Asynchronous Q-learning

> **一句话总结：** 本文为异步Q-learning的Polyak-Ruppert平均迭代建立了非渐近高维高斯逼近定理，在超矩形集合上证明了 $n^{-1/6} \log^4(nSA)$ 的收敛速率，且维度仅以对数多项式形式出现。

> **TL;DR:** This paper establishes a non-asymptotic high-dimensional Gaussian approximation for Polyak-Ruppert averaged iterates of asynchronous Q-learning with polynomial stepsizes, achieving a convergence rate of $n^{-1/6} \log^4(nSA)$ over hyper-rectangles, with only polylogarithmic dependence on the state-action dimension.

## 核心贡献
- 为异步Q-learning的Polyak-Ruppert平均迭代建立了非渐近高维中心极限定理（CLT），在超矩形集合上以Kolmogorov距离度量达到 $n^{-1/6} \log^4(nSA)$ 的收敛速率
- 证明了一个适用于向量值鞅差序列之和的高维中心极限定理（Theorem 1），该结果不依赖个体求和项的局部特征，具有独立的理论价值
- 推导了异步Q-learning在多项式步长下最后迭代的高阶矩界（Theorem 3），显式追踪了对混合时间、折扣因子和行为策略探索性质的依赖关系
- 通过Poisson方程将马尔可夫噪声问题转化为鞅差序列问题，并利用Stein方法与Lindeberg技巧相结合完成证明

## Key Contributions
- Establishes a non-asymptotic high-dimensional CLT for Polyak-Ruppert averaged iterates of asynchronous Q-learning, bounding the Kolmogorov distance over hyper-rectangles with rate $n^{-1/6} \log^4(nSA)$ (Theorem 2)
- Proves a general high-dimensional CLT for sums of vector-valued martingale differences (Theorem 1) that avoids dependence on local characteristics of individual summands -- an improvement over prior results (e.g., Kojevnikov & Song, 2022)
- Derives high-order moment bounds for the last iterate of asynchronous Q-learning under polynomial stepsizes (Theorem 3), with explicit dependence on mixing time $t_{\text{mix}}$, discount factor $(1-\gamma)^{-1}$, and minimal visitation probability $\mu_{\min}$
- Reduces the Markov noise problem to a martingale framework via the Poisson equation, combining Stein's method with Lindeberg's trick and Rollin's approach for the Gaussian approximation

## Methodology
The authors analyze asynchronous Q-learning under polynomial step sizes $\alpha_k = (k + k_0)^{-\omega}$ with $\omega \in (1/2, 1]$. The core technique involves: (1) decomposing the Q-learning noise via the Poisson equation into a martingale difference component and a Markovian remainder; (2) establishing that the leading linear term $\sqrt{n} \bar{\Delta}_n$ can be approximated by a normalized sum of martingale differences; (3) applying their general martingale CLT (Theorem 1) combined with remainder bounds to obtain the Gaussian approximation rate. The analysis requires uniform geometric ergodicity of the transition-tuple Markov chain (Assumption A2) and a strict optimality gap condition (Assumption A4).

## Results & Highlights
- The optimal step size parameter is $\omega = 2/3$, yielding the best convergence rate $n^{-1/6} \log^4(dn)$ where $d = SA$
- Compared to prior work by Liu (2025) which obtained $O(d^{1/2}/n^{1/6})$ in Wasserstein distance, this paper achieves Kolmogorov distance bounds with only polylogarithmic dependence on dimension $d$, a significant improvement for high-dimensional settings
- The moment bound for the last iterate scales as $E^{1/p}[\|Q_t - Q^*\|_\infty^p] \lesssim \frac{t_{\text{mix}} \sqrt{p \log(dt^2)}}{(1-\gamma)^{5/2} t^{\omega/2} \mu_{\min}^{3/2}}$
- The general martingale CLT (Theorem 1) improves upon Kojevnikov & Song (2022) by removing the requirement that conditional covariance matrices be almost surely deterministic

## Critical Assessment
This paper makes a solid theoretical contribution to the non-asymptotic analysis of Q-learning. The removal of polynomial dimension dependence in favor of polylogarithmic factors is a meaningful advance over prior Wasserstein-distance results. However, the rate $n^{-1/6}$ itself is slower than the $n^{-1/4}$ rates available for linear SA with i.i.d. noise, and the authors acknowledge that improving to $n^{-1/4}$ is a key open problem. The assumptions -- particularly the optimality gap condition (A4) and the uniform geometric ergodicity of the triple chain (A2) -- are somewhat restrictive. The optimality gap assumption requires uniqueness of the optimal policy with a strict gap, which may not hold in many practical MDPs. The paper is purely theoretical with no experimental validation, though this is standard for this type of mathematical work.

## Strengths
- Removes polynomial dependence on the state-action dimension $d = SA$ in the Gaussian approximation rate, replacing it with polylogarithmic factors $\log^4(nSA)$ -- a significant improvement for scalability
- The general martingale CLT (Theorem 1) is a standalone contribution that improves upon existing bounds and does not require local characteristics of individual summands
- Comprehensive moment bounds for the last iterate under polynomial stepsizes, a regime previously not covered in the literature
- Clean proof structure that systematically decomposes the problem: Poisson equation reduction, martingale approximation, remainder control, and covariance estimation

## Limitations
- The convergence rate $n^{-1/6}$ is suboptimal compared to the $n^{-1/4}$ rate achievable in linear SA settings; the authors identify improving to $n^{-1/4}$ as future work
- The optimality gap assumption (A4) is non-trivial and restricts applicability to MDPs with unique strictly optimal policies
- No experimental or numerical validation is provided to illustrate the finite-sample behavior of the bounds
- The bounds involve problem-dependent constants that may be large in practice (e.g., dependence on $t_{\text{mix}}^3$ and $(1-\gamma)^{-2}$)

## Relevance
本文对强化学习中最基础的Q-learning算法建立了严格的高维统计推断理论基础，其高维CLT结果和矩界对于理解Q-learning的有限样本行为、构建置信区间以及设计更可靠的强化学习系统具有重要参考价值。鞅差序列的高维CLT结果也可推广至其他随机逼近算法的理论分析。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Can the $n^{-1/6}$ rate be improved to $n^{-1/4}$ as the authors suggest, using a more refined expansion with weighted sums of Poisson equation terms and careful analysis of the smallest singular values of $P_k$?
- Can the optimality gap assumption (A4) be relaxed, perhaps by developing a Gaussian approximation that holds for a broader class of test sets or by considering the case of multiple optimal policies?
- How would these Gaussian approximation results extend to Q-learning with function approximation (e.g., deep Q-networks), where the state-action space is continuous and high-dimensional?
- Can the multiplier bootstrap procedure be validated non-asymptotically for asynchronous Q-learning based on the Gaussian approximation established in this paper?
