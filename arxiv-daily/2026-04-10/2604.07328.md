# How to sketch a learning algorithm

> **一句话总结：** 提出了一种基于"稳定性"假设和高阶前向模式自动微分的数据删除方案，可以在深度学习设置下以任意精度 epsilon 预测删除训练数据后的模型行为，且预计算和预测开销仅为 poly(1/epsilon) 级别。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces a provably accurate data deletion scheme for deep learning that uses forward-mode automatic differentiation to sketch the Taylor expansion of the training algorithm, achieving vanishing prediction error under a "stability" assumption verified on microgpt.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了"稳定性"(stability) 假设——要求复合函数 f = phi(A) 的高阶导数 F-范数随阶数超线性衰减——作为数据删除的理论基础，该假设不同于先前工作的替代模型假设，与训练强大 AI 模型兼容
- 设计了一种基于前向模式自动微分和随机复方向投影的"局部素描"(local sketching) 方法，可以在不显式构造高阶导数张量的情况下高效估计 Taylor 展开的各项系数
- 在数据删除设置下，预计算仅需 poly(1/epsilon) 倍训练开销，预测仅需 d * poly(1/epsilon) 倍推理开销，存储需求为 poly(1/epsilon) 个模型的空间；且预计算阶段无需知道测量函数 phi
- 在 microgpt（Karpathy 的微型 GPT）上进行了初步实验验证，观察到高阶导数范数以约 2^{-omega(r^{3/2})} 的速度衰减，支持稳定性假设成立

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a "stability" assumption on the composed function f = phi circ A that requires super-linear decay of the Frobenius norms of higher-order derivative tensors, which appears compatible with training powerful AI models and is verified empirically on microgpt
- Develops a novel local sketching method for arithmetic circuits using forward-mode automatic differentiation with random complex tangent directions, avoiding the prohibitive O(pns) storage of full Taylor expansion data
- Achieves the first data deletion scheme for deep learning with vanishing prediction error epsilon, where precomputation costs poly(1/epsilon) times training, prediction costs d * poly(1/epsilon) times inference, and the measurement function phi need not be known during precomputation
- Demonstrates via minimal experiments that the derivative norms of microgpt decay as approximately 2^{-omega(r^{3/2})}, supporting the stability assumption and showing that higher-order approximations outperform linear ones in practice

## Methodology

The paper models training and inference as arithmetic circuits. The key idea is to compute a "local sketch" of the learning algorithm A around the zero downweight vector by sampling random complex unit vectors psi_i and pushing them through A using forward-mode automatic differentiation up to order s = O(log(1/epsilon)). This computes the projected Taylor coefficients (1/r!) f^{(r)}(0)[psi_i^{tensor r}] for each direction and each order, without ever materializing the full derivative tensors. At prediction time, the Faa di Bruno formula is used to push the derivatives of A through the measurement function phi, recovering the exact same sketch that would have been obtained by sketching phi(A) directly. A median-of-means estimator provides exponential concentration for the final prediction.

## Results & Highlights

- Theoretical guarantee: For any epsilon, delta > 0, prediction error is O(epsilon * alpha(4*sqrt(d))) with probability 1 - delta, where alpha is the stability function and d is the number of deletions
- Precomputation complexity: O-tilde(size(A) * log(1/delta) / epsilon^2) operations
- Storage: O-tilde(p * log(1/delta) / epsilon^2), reducible via pseudorandom functions
- Prediction complexity: O-tilde(d * log(1/delta) / epsilon^2 + size(phi) * log(1/delta) / epsilon^3)
- Microgpt experiments show derivative norm decay rate ~2^{-omega(r^{3/2})}, far exceeding the minimum required for stability
- Higher-order (degree 2-3) sketched Taylor approximations significantly outperform linear approximations for deleting 18 training examples from microgpt

## Critical Assessment

The paper makes a bold and technically sophisticated claim: the first data deletion scheme for deep learning with vanishing error. The theoretical framework is elegant, combining Taylor expansion, random projections via the symmetric subspace, and forward-mode AD in a principled way. The stability assumption is well-motivated and plausibly holds in practice, though the experiments are extremely minimal -- only microgpt (a tiny toy model) with a single experimental setup. The paper does not address how stability scales with model size, dataset size, or training duration. The poly(1/epsilon) overhead, while polynomial, could be impractical for demanding accuracy levels. The Faa di Bruno formula step in prediction is acknowledged by the author as potentially inefficient in practice. On balance, this is a highly original theoretical contribution with promising but very early empirical support.

## Strengths

- Elegant theoretical framework that cleanly separates the stability assumption from the algorithmic contribution, making both independently analyzable
- The first data deletion scheme to not require foreknowledge of the measurement function during precomputation while still providing provable error bounds
- Novel use of forward-mode AD for implicit sketching of high-order derivative tensors is technically creative and may have applications beyond data deletion
- Honest and transparent about limitations: the author explicitly states experiments are "extremely minimal" and a "proof of concept"
- The paper is well-written with a clear pedagogical introduction that builds intuition before diving into technical details

## Limitations

- Experiments are limited to microgpt, a toy model; no evidence that stability holds for practical-scale models (e.g., LLaMA, GPT-2)
- The stability assumption, while empirically plausible, has no theoretical justification for why it should hold for deep learning in general
- The poly(1/epsilon) factors, though polynomial, may translate to large constants that make the scheme impractical for high-precision requirements
- Storage of O-tilde(p * log(1/delta) / epsilon^2) requires storing many model-sized vectors, which could be prohibitive for large models
- The error bound scales with alpha(4*sqrt(d)), so the guarantee degrades with the number of deletions d in a way that is not precisely characterized for real-world settings

该论文与训练稳定性和可解释性直接相关。稳定性假设本质上是刻画训练过程对数据扰动的敏感程度，这为理解模型行为的来源提供了新的理论工具。数据删除能力（快速预测删除某些训练数据后的模型行为）是模型可解释性、数据归因和隐私保护的核心基础设施。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the stability assumption hold for practical-scale models (e.g., 7B-parameter language models) trained on large datasets? How does alpha scale with model size and dataset size n?
- Can the median-of-means estimator be replaced by a simpler averaging estimator without sacrificing theoretical guarantees, as the author suspects?
- What is the practical computational cost of the Faa di Bruno step for realistic measurement functions, and can it be avoided or approximated?
- Can the stability assumption be theoretically derived from properties of the learning algorithm (e.g., SGD with small learning rate, noise stability of neural networks)?
