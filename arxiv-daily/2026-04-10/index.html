Reward Model Evaluation

Personalized RewardBench: SOTA Reward Models Peak at 75.94%

Qiyao Ma, Dechen Gao, Rui Cai, Boqi Zhao, Hanchu Zhou, Junshan Zhang, Zhe Zhao

揭示当前最优奖励模型在个性化偏好识别上仅达75.94%准确率，而已知rubric的上限高达97–99%；基准与下游BoN/PPO任务的NDCG相关性达0.92。

SOTA reward models reach only 75.94% on personalized preference pairs, leaving a 20-point gap to oracle accuracy (97–99%); the benchmark predicts downstream BoN/PPO quality with NDCG 0.918–0.927.

The paper constructs Personalized RewardBench from LaMP-QA: chosen and rejected responses match on factual quality but diverge only on adherence to user-specific rubrics (human-validated at 4.84 vs. 1.44 on a 5-point scale). A planner module converts raw interaction history into structured rubric aspects, mitigating the train-test misalignment of naive profile injection. Key finding: scaling does not consistently help — internlm2-20b underperforms its 7b variant; personalization requires inference of user preference, not just capacity.

How to Sketch a Learning Algorithm

Sam Gunn

基于稳定性假设和前向模式自动微分，首次实现深度学习场景下任意精度的可证数据删除，预计算开销仅为poly(1/ε)倍训练成本。

First provably accurate data deletion for deep learning via Taylor sketch with forward-mode AD; poly(1/ε) precomputation overhead under a "stability" assumption verified on microgpt.

Beyond Loss Values: Robust Pruning via Loss Trajectory Alignment

Huaiyuan Qin, Muli Yang, G. J. Goenawan, Kai Wang et al. (A*STAR / NUS / Wuhan)

AlignPrune用样本损失轨迹与干净参考信号的Pearson相关性（DAS）替代瞬时损失值排序，在噪声标签下最高提升6.3%准确率，CVPR 2026 Findings。

AlignPrune replaces loss-value ranking with Dynamic Alignment Score (loss trajectory Pearson correlation), achieving up to +6.3% accuracy under noisy labels with as few as 10 clean reference samples.
