# Personalized RewardBench: Evaluating Reward Models with Human Aligned Personalization

> **一句话总结：** 本文提出Personalized RewardBench，基于LaMP-QA构建用户特定rubric的偏好对，揭示当前SOTA奖励模型在个性化偏好识别上最高仅达75.94%准确率，并证明该基准与BoN和PPO下游任务性能的相关性显著优于已有基准（NDCG达0.9180/0.9265）。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces Personalized RewardBench, a benchmark where chosen/rejected pairs differ only in adherence to user-specific rubrics (not general quality), revealing SOTA reward models peak at 75.94% accuracy while showing NDCG correlations of 0.918 (BoN) and 0.927 (PPO) with downstream performance.

## 核心贡献

- 提出Personalized RewardBench：基于LaMP-QA数据集，利用用户特定rubric aspects构建偏好对，确保chosen和rejected在通用质量（事实性、相关性、有用性）上无显著差异，仅在个性化对齐上有区别，经人工评估验证
- 揭示当前SOTA奖励模型在个性化偏好上的显著缺陷：最佳模型Gemini-3-Flash仅达75.94%准确率，而oracle（已知rubric）可达97-99%，说明差距源于推理用户偏好的能力不足而非数据噪声
- 证明基准与下游任务的强相关性：在BoN和PPO设置下，Personalized RewardBench的NDCG分别达0.9180和0.9265，远超Chatbot Arena-Personalized和PRISM-Personalized基线
- 提出planner模块将用户历史交互转化为结构化rubric aspects，有效缓解了直接注入用户profile导致的训练-测试失配问题

## Key Contributions

- Introduces Personalized RewardBench: constructs preference pairs from LaMP-QA where chosen/rejected responses differ exclusively in user-specific rubric adherence, validated via human evaluation showing both responses maintain high general quality (4.3-4.99/5)
- Exposes a critical personalization gap in SOTA reward models: best performer Gemini-3-Flash reaches only 75.94% accuracy, while an oracle with ground-truth rubrics achieves 97-99%, leaving over 20 percentage points of headroom
- Demonstrates strong downstream correlation: NDCG of 0.918 (BoN) and 0.927 (PPO), substantially outperforming Chatbot Arena-Personalized and PRISM-Personalized baselines on all rank correlation metrics
- Proposes a planner module that converts raw user interaction history into structured rubric aspects, mitigating train-test misalignment from naive profile injection

## Methodology

The benchmark is constructed from LaMP-QA, a personalized QA dataset spanning three subjective domains (Arts & Entertainment, Lifestyle & Personal Development, Society & Culture). Chosen responses are generated by Gemini-3-Flash conditioned on user query, profile, and rubric aspects; rejected responses are generated with the same inputs but instructed to avoid those rubrics. A Contriever-based retriever extracts top-10 historical interactions per user to form profiles. Human evaluation confirms both chosen and rejected responses achieve high scores on factuality, relevance, and helpfulness (4.3+/5), diverging only on personal rubric alignment (chosen: 4.84-4.93, rejected: 1.44-1.49). For downstream validation, the authors use Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct as a lightweight policy and Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct as LLM-as-a-judge with rubric-aware scoring, measuring correlation between benchmark accuracy and BoN/PPO policy quality across four rank correlation metrics.

## Results & Highlights

- Best reward model accuracy: Gemini-3-Flash at 75.94% (Lifestyle domain); worst: PAL at 48.76-51.49%
- Oracle upper bound (Gemini-3-Flash with ground-truth rubrics): 97.78-99.09%, confirming the gap is due to preference inference, not data noise
- Downstream correlation (PPO, NDCG): Personalized RewardBench 0.9265 vs. PRISM-Personalized 0.7029 vs. Chatbot Arena-Personalized 0.6573
- Naive user profile injection degrades performance; planner-based rubric extraction recovers and often surpasses baseline accuracy
- Scaling does not consistently help: internlm2-20b underperforms internlm2-7b; mR3-Qwen3-14B underperforms its 8B variant

## Critical Assessment

The paper addresses a real and timely gap: existing reward model benchmarks conflate general quality with personalization, and the downstream correlation experiment is a genuine methodological strength. However, several concerns merit attention. First, the benchmark is built entirely on LaMP-QA data generated by a single LLM (Gemini-3-Flash), raising questions about whether the preference pairs reflect Gemini-specific artifacts rather than universal personalization challenges. Second, the downstream evaluation uses Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct as judge with rubric-aware prompts -- this is essentially the same signal used to construct the benchmark, creating a circularity risk. Third, the number of reward models in the downstream correlation analysis is small (6 models), which makes rank correlation metrics unstable; NDCG/RBO/Spearman values can shift dramatically with one model swap. The human evaluation, while thorough, scores responses on a 1-5 Likert scale, which may not capture subtle quality differences. Finally, the planner module experiments (Section 4.3) are preliminary and lack ablation on the planner architecture itself.

## Strengths

- Rigorous human evaluation validating that chosen/rejected pairs differ only on personal rubrics, not general quality -- this is a genuine methodological contribution
- Strong downstream correlation results (NDCG > 0.91) that meaningfully outperform existing baselines, establishing the benchmark as a practical proxy
- Clear oracle analysis showing the task is solvable (97-99% accuracy), proving the benchmark measures a real capability gap
- Well-structured paper with comprehensive appendices including case studies, prompt templates, and detailed implementation notes
- The finding that scaling does not consistently improve personalization performance is practically important and counter-intuitive

## Limitations

- Data generation depends entirely on Gemini-3-Flash; preference pairs may encode model-specific stylistic patterns rather than universal personalization signals
- Downstream evaluation uses Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct as judge with rubric-aware prompts -- partially circular with benchmark construction methodology
- Only 6 reward models used in downstream correlation analysis, making rank correlation metrics statistically fragile
- Domains limited to three subjective categories from LaMP-QA; unclear how well findings generalize to technical, medical, or legal domains where personalization also matters
- The planner module analysis is preliminary; no comparison with alternative profile-to-rubric extraction methods
- No analysis of how benchmark performance varies with the number or quality of rubric aspects per query

## Relevance

本文对LLM推理评估领域有直接意义：奖励模型是RLHF的核心组件，而个性化对齐是当前LLM落地的重要方向。该基准揭示了现有奖励模型在捕捉用户偏好上的系统性不足，为开发更精细的个性化奖励信号提供了评估工具和改进方向。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How robust are the preference pairs to generator switching (e.g., using GPT-5.1 or Claude instead of Gemini-3-Flash for data construction)?
- Can the benchmark be extended to multi-turn conversational settings where user preferences evolve over time?
- What is the minimal amount of user history needed for the planner to infer accurate rubric aspects?
- Would training reward models on this benchmark's training split (from LaMP-QA) transfer to improved personalization in real-world RLHF pipelines?
