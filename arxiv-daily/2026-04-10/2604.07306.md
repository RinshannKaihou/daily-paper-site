# Beyond Loss Values: Robust Dynamic Pruning via Loss Trajectory Alignment

> **一句话总结：** 本文提出AlignPrune，一种基于损失轨迹对齐的即插即用模块，通过动态对齐分数(DAS)替代传统基于损失值的排序准则，解决了动态数据剪枝在噪声标签场景下的失效问题，在五个基准数据集上最高提升6.3%的准确率。

> **TL;DR:** AlignPrune replaces loss-value-based sample ranking in dynamic data pruning with a Dynamic Alignment Score (DAS) that correlates each sample's loss trajectory with a clean reference signal, enabling robust pruning under noisy labels.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统性地研究了动态数据剪枝在噪声标签场景下的失效问题，揭示了传统基于损失值的排序准则会错误保留高损失的噪声样本
- 提出动态对齐分数(DAS)，通过计算样本损失轨迹与干净参考集轨迹之间的Pearson相关性，从根本上区分干净样本与噪声样本的学习动态差异
- 设计了即插即用的AlignPrune模块，可直接替换InfoBatch、SeTa等现有动态剪枝方法中的损失排序机制，无需修改模型架构或训练流程
- 在五个基准数据集(CIFAR-100N, CIFAR-10N, WebVision, Clothing-1M, ImageNet-1K)上全面验证，最高提升6.3%准确率，且仅需极少甚至无需干净参考数据

## Key Contributions

- Identifies and formalizes the critical failure mode of loss-based dynamic pruning under noisy labels: noisy samples produce high losses and are mistakenly retained rather than pruned
- Proposes Dynamic Alignment Score (DAS), a trajectory-level correlation metric between per-sample loss histories and a clean reference trajectory, which separates clean from noisy samples by learning dynamics rather than loss magnitude
- Designs AlignPrune as a plug-and-play module that replaces loss-based ranking in existing dynamic pruning frameworks (InfoBatch, SeTa) without modifying model architecture or training pipelines
- Demonstrates that the method works with as few as 10 clean reference samples (0.1% of the original clean set) and remains effective using pseudo-clean subsets estimated from noisy data alone

## Methodology

AlignPrune constructs a loss trajectory vector for each training sample over a sliding window of N recent epochs (default N=25), recording the sequence of training losses over time. A parallel reference trajectory is computed as the average loss over a small clean validation set. The Dynamic Alignment Score (DAS) is defined as the Pearson correlation between the sample's loss trajectory and the reference trajectory: clean samples, whose loss decreases monotonically in sync with the model's learning, yield positive DAS values, while noisy samples with erratic loss patterns yield negative or near-zero scores. This DAS replaces the raw loss value as the ranking criterion in existing dynamic pruning methods such as InfoBatch and SeTa. Samples with lower DAS are preferentially pruned, ensuring that noisy data is filtered out while clean data (including hard-but-clean examples) is retained. The method preserves the unbiased gradient estimation guarantee of the underlying dynamic pruning framework.

## Results & Highlights

- On CIFAR-100N with 30% pruning ratio, AlignPrune + InfoBatch achieves an average accuracy improvement of +2.7% across all noise types, compared to +0.6% for vanilla InfoBatch
- On CIFAR-10N with 50% pruning ratio, AlignPrune + SeTa yields +0.1% average improvement, while vanilla SeTa drops by -1.4%
- Up to 6.3% accuracy improvement over state-of-the-art baselines across all benchmarks
- Effective on large-scale real-world noisy datasets: WebVision (66K images, InceptionResNetV2) and Clothing-1M (1M images, ResNet-50 fine-tuning)
- Scales to ImageNet-1K with modern architectures including ConvNeXt, DeiT, and Swin Transformer
- Maintains competitive performance under clean-label settings, naturally degrading to a proxy for conventional dynamic pruning
- Reduces total training time compared to both vanilla InfoBatch and full-data training due to more efficient sample selection
- Works with only 10 clean reference samples; tolerates up to 30% noise in the reference set with marginal performance drop
- Validated beyond image domain on NEWS text classification task

## Critical Assessment

The paper addresses a genuinely important and practical problem: existing dynamic pruning methods are brittle under label noise because they rank by loss, which conflates hard-but-clean samples with noisy ones. The proposed DAS metric is conceptually simple and well-motivated -- using trajectory correlation rather than instantaneous loss magnitude is an elegant solution. The plug-and-play design with minimal hyper-parameter sensitivity (window size N, correlation function choice) strengthens its practical appeal. However, the method requires a clean or pseudo-clean reference set, which adds a dependency that may be non-trivial in fully unsupervised scenarios, even though the ablation shows robustness down to 0.1% clean data. The theoretical justification is somewhat thin -- while empirical evidence is strong, the paper lacks formal convergence or optimality guarantees for the DAS-based pruning criterion. The 6.3% figure appears in specific high-noise settings and may not represent typical gains. Overall, the work is solid, well-scoped, and fills a clear gap in the dynamic pruning literature.

## Strengths

- Simple and well-motivated core idea: replacing loss values with trajectory-level correlation signals
- True plug-and-play design that integrates seamlessly with existing methods without architecture changes
- Extremely comprehensive evaluation: 5 benchmarks, multiple noise types and rates, 3 pruning ratios, multiple architectures
- Strong ablation studies demonstrating robustness to reference set size, quality, and noise
- Hard vs. noisy sample analysis provides clear insight into why DAS works where loss fails
- Code publicly available, supporting reproducibility
- Validated on non-image domain (text classification), suggesting broader applicability

## Limitations

- Requires at least some form of clean or pseudo-clean reference set, introducing a practical dependency
- The trajectory window requires at least 4 epochs before DAS becomes reliable, limiting applicability in very short training regimes
- Theoretical grounding is limited; no formal proof that DAS-based pruning preserves convergence guarantees
- SeTa collapses on large-scale datasets (WebVision, Clothing-1M), limiting the demonstration to InfoBatch integration for those settings
- Gains under clean-label settings are marginal, suggesting the method adds overhead without clear benefit in noise-free scenarios
- The reliance on Pearson correlation assumes roughly linear temporal relationships in loss dynamics, which may not hold for all training regimes

## Relevance

本文与训练稳定性和损失分析高度相关。它揭示了仅依赖瞬时损失值进行数据筛选的脆弱性，提出通过损失轨迹的时序动态特征来增强训练鲁棒性，对于大规模噪声数据场景下的可靠模型训练具有重要的实践意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can DAS be combined with re-labeling strategies (e.g., SOP+) in an end-to-end framework where the reference set is dynamically updated as the model improves its label corrections?
- How does AlignPrune interact with other sources of training instability beyond label noise, such as distribution shift, adversarial examples, or data duplication?
- What are the theoretical convergence properties of DAS-based pruning, and can the trajectory alignment criterion be derived from first principles rather than empirical motivation?
