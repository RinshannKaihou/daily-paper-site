# Toward a Tractability Frontier for Exact Relevance Certification

> **一句话总结：** 本文证明了在精确相关性认证（exact relevance certification）中，任何满足定理强制闭包定律不变性的可高效检查结构谓词都无法精确刻画易处理性边界——这是一项类似Rice定理的元不可能性结果，同时正面给出了当前易处理族的一个八元素有限原始基。

> **TL;DR:** A meta-impossibility theorem showing that no closure-law-invariant, efficiently checkable structural predicate can exactly characterize the tractability frontier for exact relevance certification. The tractable landscape admits a finite primitive basis of eight mechanisms, but quotient realizability is maximal and an orbit-gap collapse defeats all candidate classifiers.

## 核心贡献
- 证明了精确相关性认证的元不可能性定理：任何满足闭包定律不变性的结构谓词都无法精确分类四个阻梗族（dominant-pair、margin masking、ghost-action、additive/statewise offset）
- 将当前十五个易处理族显式分解为六个核心结构族、四个提升族和五个退化族，并提取出八个原始机制
- 证明优化器商空间的实现性是极大的——任意标记核都可被实现为优化器商，因此商结构本身不能承载易处理性边界
- 核心机制是闭包轨道内的"轨道-间隙矛盾"：通过构造同一闭包轨道内具有不同阻梗状态的两个切片来否定任何不变分类器
- 提供了完整的Lean 4形式化验证，覆盖可实现性构造、通用轨道-间隙论证和族级可容许不可能性定理

## Key Contributions
- A meta-impossibility theorem for exact relevance certification: no closure-law-invariant structural predicate can exactly classify four obstruction families (dominant-pair, margin masking, ghost-action, additive/statewise offset)
- An explicit partition of fifteen tractable families into six core, four lifted, and five degenerate cases, yielding an eight-element primitive basis
- A maximality result for optimizer-quotient realizability: every labeling kernel is optimizer-realizable, so quotient shape alone cannot carry the tractability boundary
- The orbit-gap template: constructing same-orbit disagreements via pair-targeted, action-independent affine witnesses to defeat all closure-invariant classifiers
- Complete Lean 4 formalization covering realizability constructions, the generic orbit-gap argument, and admissible no-go theorems (archived at Zenodo)

## Methodology
The paper develops a two-part theory. On the positive side, it classifies known tractable families into three roles (core mechanisms, regime lifts, degenerate collapses) and shows this partition factors through the role classifier. On the negative side, it identifies a natural admissibility class for tractability predicates (polynomial-time checkability, closure-law invariance, structural extractability, bounded-pattern definability), then constructs explicit orbit-gap witnesses for four structurally distinct obstruction families using action-independent, pair-targeted affine transport. The key insight is that closure-law invariance is forced by correctness (not assumed as an axiom), making the impossibility result universal over all correct classifiers on closure-closed domains.

## Results & Highlights
- **Theorem 4.9 (Closure-Sound Package No-Go):** Any class of slice predicates whose members are closure-law invariant cannot contain a predicate that simultaneously characterizes all four obstruction families. Only closure-law invariance is load-bearing; other admissibility clauses are guardrails.
- **Theorem 5.1 (Realizability Barrier):** Every labeling kernel is optimizer-realizable, meaning quotient structure is maximally expressive and cannot serve as a frontier classifier.
- **Theorem 6.17:** Any correct tractability classifier on a closure-closed domain is automatically closure-invariant, so the no-go applies universally, not just to predicates that advertise invariance.
- **Corollary 4.11 (No Correct Tractability Classifier):** No procedure assigning tractability verdicts to representations can correctly predict tractability across the four obstruction families, regardless of internal feature tracking.
- **Proposition 4.12 (Orbit-Gap Completeness):** Exact classification by closure-law-invariant predicates fails if and only if the target predicate admits an orbit-gap witness. This makes orbit gaps the complete obstruction criterion.
- **Positive side:** Fifteen tractable families reduce to eight primitive mechanisms; five core mechanisms are shown independently indispensable via witness families.
- **Binary pairwise dichotomy:** Within binary pairwise-symmetric regimes, there is no intermediate sparse interaction pattern between unary collapse and complete-pair interaction.

## Critical Assessment
This paper represents a serious and technically demanding contribution to the structural complexity theory of decision problems. The meta-impossibility result is conceptually clean—belonging to the Rice/Arrow tradition of impossibility theorems—and the orbit-gap mechanism is elegant. The Lean 4 formalization is a strong credibility signal. However, the paper is extremely dense and assumes substantial familiarity with the author's prior work (reference [29]); the notation and terminology (optimizer quotient, closure orbits, admissible normalization predicates) are specialized enough to limit accessibility. The four obstruction families, while structurally distinct, all rely on the same pair-targeted affine witness mechanism, which raises the question of whether a fundamentally different invariance package could evade the no-go. The paper acknowledges this as an open problem but does not explore alternatives.

## Strengths
- A clean, universal impossibility theorem whose only load-bearing hypothesis (closure-law invariance) is itself forced by correctness, not assumed
- Complete Lean 4 formalization with transparent claim-to-handle mapping provides strong verification guarantees
- The orbit-gap template (Proposition 4.12) is a complete characterization: orbit gaps are necessary and sufficient for the impossibility
- Systematic positive classification of tractable families with explicit independence witnesses for five core mechanisms
- The realizability barrier (every labeling kernel is realizable) is a simple but powerful negative structural fact
- The paper places its result in a rich intellectual context (Rice's theorem, CSP dichotomy, Arrow's theorem, backdoor tractability)

## Limitations
- Heavy dependence on the author's own prior work [29] for the optimizer-quotient framework; difficult to read as a standalone paper
- The four obstruction families all use the same affine witness mechanism, so the "four structurally distinct" claim may overstate the diversity of the evidence
- The admissibility package's auxiliary clauses (structural extractability, bounded-pattern definability) are acknowledged as non-load-bearing, yet occupy substantial definitional space
- No exploration of whether strengthening the invariance package (e.g., with higher-order closure laws or algebraic invariants beyond affine transport) could yield a positive frontier theorem
- The paper is purely theoretical with no experimental or applied motivation; the practical implications for ML feature selection or interpretability remain speculative
- Extensive use of generative AI tools (Codex, Claude Code, Augment, Kilo, OpenCode) across all stages, though the author claims full intellectual control and no technical claims accepted solely from AI output

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Can a frontier theorem be obtained by imposing stronger invariance (e.g., invariance under nonlinear utility reparameterizations, or under more aggressive coordinate-merging operations) that escapes the current orbit-gap mechanism?
- Are there natural restricted domains (beyond the binary pairwise setting) where the optimizer quotient has nontrivial finite characterization, perhaps exploiting algebraic structure analogous to the CSP polymorphism approach?
- How do the obstruction families and the orbit-gap mechanism interact with approximate (nonzero-distortion) relevance certification, where the optimizer quotient is no longer the unique reference object?
- Can the positive compression result (Proposition 6.19: bounded distinct action profiles reduce to bounded actions) be generalized to a broader algorithmic toolkit for exact certification in practice?
