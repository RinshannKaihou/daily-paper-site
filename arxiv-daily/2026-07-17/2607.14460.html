Methodology

  • Proportional regime. x̄∼N(0,Σ) in ℝd, n samples, α=lim n/d fixed, Σ̂=(1/n)XᵀX; target δ(α)=lim E‖Σ̂−Σ‖₂.
  • Reduction. Rotational invariance rewrites λn(Σ̂−Σ) as maxc[(ξ(c))²−c²], leaving only the spectrum s and scalar c.
  • Upper bound (Thm 1/3). Slepian/Gordon comparison yields closed-form δ̂_u(α)=γ̂_x√ϕ₁(γ̂_x)/√(ϕ₁(γ̂_x)−α).
  • Bilinear-quadratic lower bound (Thm 4). New device — mixed bilinear+quadratic Gaussian process gives the reverse inequality.
  • 2-replica matching (Thm 6–8). Proves upper and lower bounds coincide, so δ(α) is exactly determined, not merely sandwiched.
  • Spectral-norm extension (Thm 9/11). Mirrored Gordon argument pins |λ₁(Σ̂−Σ)|, so the full operator norm matches λn.
02

Results & Highlights

  • Exact closed form. δ(α) as a precise constant — a precise value, not an order bound.
  • Isotropic baseline recovered exactly. Σ=I reduces to the MP leading edge minus ‖Σ‖.
  • Sim = theory at d≈3000. For α=1, s=linspace[0.5,1], δn stabilizes at ≈1.7; "excellent agreement already for d on the order of thousand" (§3.6).
  • Sample-complexity curve. α∈{1.0, 1.5, 2.5} shows monotone decrease (α=2.5 → δ≈1.0); sim tracks theory.
  • Matching verified two ways. Fig. 1 numerical check + Thm 8 analytic proof-by-contradiction (eq. 131).
  • No ML benchmarks — quantitative evidence is RDT-vs-Monte-Carlo agreement plus MP-edge recovery.
03

Strengths

  • First exact characterization of limiting spectral-norm error for non-isotropic centered Gaussians, closed-form in the spectrum.
  • The bilinear-quadratic lower-bounding mechanism is a clean, reusable addition to the RDT toolbox — the conceptual key letting bounds meet.
  • Full proofs, not sketches; line-by-line derivations make the algebra auditable.
  • Honest self-consistency check against the Marchenko–Pastur isotropic limit.
  • Modest problem sizes (d≈3000) already match theory — practically encouraging.
04

Limitations

  • Centered Gaussian only. Heavy-tailed, corrupted, or missing-data regimes deferred — exactly the practically pressing cases.
  • Proportional regime. Strongly over-/under-sampled (α→∞ or α<1) not separately analyzed.
  • Matching partly numerical. Thm 8 proves (118) only for an s=linspace-type ensemble; general spectra rely on a numerical check + Remark 4.
  • No code, no ML experiments. Pure theory with three RDT-vs-sim figures.
  • Operator norm only. Frobenius-norm variant not addressed; no non-asymptotic tail/concentration bounds.
  • arXiv v1, single author, self-citation-heavy grounding in own RDT series — not yet peer-reviewed.
