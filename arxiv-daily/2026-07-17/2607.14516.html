§ 01

Method & Results

◆Methodology

  • AdaptiveEmbeddedRK3Optimizer applies Bogacki–Shampine 3(2) stages to the gradient flow θ̇ = −∇L(θ); the 3rd-order averaged gradient g₃ drives Adam moments.
  • Step controller: ϕ = clip(0.9·err_c^(−1/3), 0.5, 2.0), h ← clip(h·ϕ, [0.1·lr, hmax]), with hmax = 2·lr by default.
  • Per-gradient-evaluation accounting: an s-stage RK step costs s evals; FSAL reuse is credited only in the deterministic full-batch (autonomous) regime.
  • Four experiments on MNIST (784–128–10 MLP): stochastic minibatch, full-batch, repaired-controller ablation, and a pre-registered pSGLD temperature sweep.
  • Fixed-step control at h = 0.1 disentangles adaptivity from the large-effective-step confound.

  • Controller is inert. With defaults, h jumps to hmax on step 1 and pins there on 100% of steps; max error 0.029 sits 3× below the saturation threshold 0.091 (Proposition 1).
  • 100× tolerance sweep is bit-identical: losses 0.000109 / 0.000112 / 0.000112 for ρ ∈ {0.01, 0.1, 1.0} — a 3×10⁻⁶ residual gap from first-step h only.
  • Repaired controller (true reject branch + error on applied map): ~40× lower full-batch loss; a fixed-step control at h=0.1 reaches only 0.186, isolating adaptivity as the mechanism.
  • Win does not transfer. No repaired-RK config reaches 90% test acc; deepest loss (3.8×10⁻⁷) gives only 86.95%. Tuned Adam at lr=0.01 hits 90.30% at 1/3 the cost.
  • Tempering refuted. pSGLD T=0 (90.38%) is the global optimum; every T>0 falls ≥0.6 pt, collapsing to 79.79% at T=10⁻³.
§ 02

Assessment

+Strengths

  • Honest, non-rubberstamp framing: sets out to test an appealing idea and reports a mostly-negative answer with the diagnostic depth to explain why.
  • Exhaustive negative, not anecdote. Proposition 1 saturation condition + 36-config ρ × hmax × h0 sweep convert inertness into a proof-by-cube.
  • Fixed-step control cleanly isolates adaptivity from the large-effective-step confound.
  • Pre-registered temperature-sweep hypotheses (H0/H1 in code before running); paired t-tests at n=10.
  • Actionable reporting standard: accepted-h distribution, rejection rate, non-bit-identical trajectories across ≥2 settings.
  • Full code + JSON release on CPU-deterministic runs — cheap to replicate.

!Limitations & Red Flags

  • Scope: single dataset (MNIST), single architecture (784–128–10 MLP), 1024-example full-batch subset. Author explicit: “methodology transfers, rankings may not.”
  • Seed count: main sweeps n=3; only the secondary test-accuracy observation is chased at n=10.
  • One RK design point (Bogacki–Shampine 3(2)); IMEX / implicit schemes have different failure surfaces, not probed.
  • Baseline tuning asymmetry: modest lr grid for Adam vs h0/ρ grid for RK — a fairer head-to-head could move the picture.
  • Headline fragility: the ~40× win is full-batch, deterministic, fragile to h0; no minibatch-noise evidence (where the author's own diagnosis says the error estimator is variance-dominated).
  • Implicit-regularization observation is post-hoc; minimum-selection mechanism identified only by exclusion — no curvature instrumentation.
