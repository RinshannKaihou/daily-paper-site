03Why This Matters

Provides representation-level, probability-independent evidence on whether LLMs truly possess grammatical knowledge — directly shifting the cognitive-science burden of proof. The boundary-tilting deconfounder and MP_Rep metric transfer to other “is this concept linearly encoded?” studies.

04Critical Edge

No causal intervention is performed — the probes are correlational. A reader should not read the θ direction as proof of the model’s grammaticality computation; deconfounding also covers only named extra-grammatical factors.

01Methodology

θ = μ_gram − μ_ungram  ·  decide by σ(θ·x) > 0.5
fit on 1,600 sentences · 100 subsampling runs · Welch t-test + Holm–Bonferroni
  • Mass-mean probing (Marks & Tegmark 2024) — a single closed-form direction, no black-box classifier.
  • Three regions probed per sentence: Pre-critical (control), Critical (divergence), Final (punctuation).
  • Deconfounding battery: Counter/Favour probability stress, SCaMP plausible-vs-implausible, novel boundary-tilting (subtract θ’s projection onto acceptability direction ϕ).
  • Success threshold ≥5 pp above a label-shuffled null, calibrated to Pre-critical false positives [−2.62%, 1.65%].

02Results & Highlights

0.928 Pearson R between log₁₀(params) and probe-success rate on BLiMP.
+6.89 pp by which MP_Rep overtakes MP_Prob in large models (R=0.791, p<0.001) — after lagging 13.4 pp when small.
3.19 pp — largest Counter-condition drop (OLMo-2-0425-7B); probe tracks grammaticality, not string probability.
4.06 pp — Critical-region drop when removing within-phenomenon probes (cross-phenomenon generalization holds).
61.4% cross-linguistic transfer accuracy vs 66.6% within-language English (5 strongest models, de/fr/ru/he/zh).
11.2% gram→extra-grammatical generalization vs 55.9% gram→gram — the θ direction is grammar-specific.

03Strengths

  • Interpretable probe — θ is one direction, fully reproducible closed-form; no layer tuning, no black box.
  • Principled deconfounding — Counter/Favour, SCaMP plausible/implausible, and the new boundary-tilting all converge: grammaticality survives.
  • Broad coverage — 25 models × 5 families, 6 languages; scaling claims carry weight.
  • Honest reporting — explicitly notes accuracies are lower than Wang et al. (2026) and why (low-complexity probe, no layer tuning).
  • Reusable byproducts — MP_Rep metric and boundary-tilting procedure are drop-in tools for the community.

04Limitations

  • Probe success ≠ calibrated accuracy — a ≥5 pp binary threshold; the headline “94.0%” is a proportion of datasets passing, not mean accuracy.
  • No layer tuning — comparisons vs probability and vs Wang et al. are conservative for the probe side.
  • Deconfounding is partial — boundary-tilting removes only named factors (Truth, Shades, Drive); register, discourse coherence uncontrolled.
  • Final region + English only for tilt and Counter/Favour — Critical-region and multilingual deconfounding open.
  • Cross-linguistic rests on 5 models, phenomena collapsed per language; Mandarin coverage weak.
  • Purely correlational — no activation patching/steering; base models only (no chat-tuned).
