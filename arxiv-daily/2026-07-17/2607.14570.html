Key Finding · ICML 2026 Workshop

An untrained structural monitor catches AI-agent infrastructure sabotage — and rolls it back to zero.

The Information Flow Graph (IFG) monitor diffs the control-flow and data-flow graphs of cdk synth renders, needs no attack-log training, and works both as a post-hoc auditor and a synchronous, pre-deploy safeguard.

Why This Matters

Gives small teams an auditable, deploy-time defense against coding-agent sabotage — without bespoke red-team pipelines.

Critical Edge

IFG can’t see sabotage delivered outside the CDK source diff; its headline 3.5% miss rate is on a favorably pruned attack set.

01

Methodology

STEP 01

Render both sides

Run cdk synth on original and patched CDK source → two CloudFormation templates.

STEP 02

Build the SecurityDelta

CFG/DFG deltas → structured artifact: removed deny statements, new admin roles, broadened scopes, hardening regressions.

STEP 03

Score with a trusted LLM

Feeds main task + SecurityDelta + raw code diff; classifies STRIDE threats; emits a single 1–10 suspicion score.

STEP 04

Act: audit or roll back

Async mode: post-hoc flag. Sync mode: block/revert suspicious infra before execution or merge.

03

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean head-to-head vs the strongest trained monitor (Async Control Round-3 RF), same prompts and benchmark slice.
  • Sync rollback 74.4%→0.0% joint success at no measurable utility cost — the standout result.
  • Mechanism-level interpretability: every flag ties to a concrete graph object (a removed deny, a new admin role), not an opaque score.
  • Untrained & attack-log-free → low adoption barrier; matches the democratization thesis.
  • Transparent scope disclosure in Appendix A — documents the excluded side task rather than hiding it.

Limitations

  • Blind to pipeline-bypass attacks: the excluded check in cdk out directory task scores a flat 1/10 on all 17 successful hits.
  • Trusted model still prompt-injectable; IFG narrows but does not close the adaptive-attack surface (no adaptive eval provided).
  • Single ecosystem (AWS CDK/CloudFormation); Terraform/Pulumi/Helm and non-IaC backdoors untested.
  • No cost/latency accounting for sync mode — the central “democratization” claim rests on this.
  • Small absolute sample: 3.5% FNR = 3 misses of 86, wide CI; trusted-model swapped mid-paper (3.7 Sonnet → Haiku 4.5).

Scope note · Appendix A

One side task smuggles malicious CloudFormation via pre-built cdk.out/ artifacts rather than CDK source edits, so it never appears in the diff or synthesized graph. The authors exclude it (20/180 async episodes; IFG scores 1/10 on all 17 successful) — defensible and transparent, but the headline FNR is computed on a favorably pruned attack set.
