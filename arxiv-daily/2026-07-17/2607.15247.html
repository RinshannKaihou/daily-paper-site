The one-sentence takeaway

AutoSynthesis is a LangGraph multi-agent framework that runs the entire quantitative meta-analysis — retrieval, standardized effect sizes, REML pooling, heterogeneity, publication bias, risk-of-bias, PRISMA report — recovering the human benchmark's effect to within Δg = 0.123.

01

Key Finding

The first end-to-end agentic framework performing the complete meta-analysis workflow — not just screening and extraction — including REML pooling and bias diagnostics.

§ 02

Method

four-phase LangGraph pipeline · ~12 specialized agents · Python 3.11

PRISMA-mapped agent pipeline

PHASE 01
Planning & retrieval
  • planning NL→PRISMA protocol
  • search Boolean queries · arXiv, Semantic Scholar, CrossRef, OSF, PubMed (cap 40/db)
  • paper reading cascading PDF: MinerU→LlamaParse→Mistral OCR→pdftotext
PHASE 02
Eligibility & mapping
  • eligibility "INCLUDE when in doubt"
  • study ID detects independent experiments
  • RQ ID binds each effect to a comparison
PHASE 03
Extraction & validation
  • extractor paper-map first, numbers second
  • validation verbatim grounding, drops hallucinated
  • relevance one winner per study–outcome
PHASE 04
Analysis & report
  • analysis Cohen's d→Hedges' g · REML · I²/τ²/Q · moderators
  • bias Egger · trim-and-fill · leave-one-out · RoB 2 / ROBINS-I
  • report PRISMA flow · forest · funnel · Markdown

Design principles

  • LLM × stats
    LLMs reason; statistics stay deterministic. REML (DerSimonian–Laird init, Viechtbauer iteration), Egger, and trim-and-fill are reimplemented in NumPy/SciPy rather than wrapping metafor — meta-analytic conclusions are fixed once extraction is fixed.
  • Anti-hallucination
    Two-phase extraction builds a structured paper-map (studies, outcomes, hypotheses) before pulling numbers; the validation agent requires verbatim-or-within-rounding grounding and discards unlocatable values as hallucinated.
  • Auditability
    Every PRISMA stage maps to a specialized agent with full decision logs, enabling a "living meta-analysis" — resume from any stage to integrate new studies.
  • Compliance
    PRISMA 2020 flow diagram, cumulative-by-year analysis, leave-one-out sensitivity, and design-appropriate risk-of-bias (RoB 2 for RCTs, ROBINS-I otherwise) — analyses human reviewers frequently skip.

Critique

strengths vs. limitations · open questions · credibility

Strengths vs. Limitations

Strengths
  • Principled LLM/deterministic-statistics separation — REML, Egger, trim-and-fill are custom NumPy/SciPy.
  • Two-phase extractor + verbatim-grounding validation is a genuinely well-designed anti-hallucination pattern.
  • Full PRISMA 2020 compliance: flow diagram, leave-one-out, cumulative MA, RoB by design.
  • Transparent reporting of cost, tokens, calls, decoding params, and full prompts in supplement.
  • Honest "support not replace" framing with a compelling living-meta-analysis use case.
Limitations
  • Single benchmark (n=1); education/psychology use cases advertised but never quantified.
  • Shared authorship with the benchmark team weakens independence of recall/precision corrections.
  • Robustness "across LLM backbones" claimed but zero numbers reported — only gpt-5.4-mini.
  • Retrieval capped at 40/db; one study missed purely due to paywall — biases toward open access.
  • Pooled estimate significant under AutoSynthesis, null under human benchmark — divergence underplayed.
  • No human-in-the-loop evaluation; reproducibility "promised, not delivered" (no repo link).

The sharpest caveat

AutoSynthesis calls LLMs persuasive (p<0.001). The human benchmark calls them not (p=0.530).
The paper frames Δg = 0.123 as "broadly similar" within the Aczel ±0.20 tolerance — yet overlapping CIs still flip the qualitative verdict. Is the gap driven by 3 extra included studies, by effect-size coding, or by retrieval? Which conclusion is closer to ground truth remains open.
Reproducibility gap: "open-source license" and "dedicated GitHub repository" are promised, but no link, commit, or release is provided — the $1.5/run claim cannot be independently verified.

Open questions for further work

  1. How does AutoSynthesis perform on n ≥ 5 benchmarks across clinical medicine (Cochrane / RoB 2), education, and psychology — not just LLM-persuasion?
  2. What is the per-agent error contribution? A confusion-matrix breakdown of eligibility, extraction, and validation would localize where to invest.
  3. How sensitive are pooled estimates to the 40-papers/db retrieval cap and to the cascading PDF parser order (MinerU vs. LlamaParse first)?
  4. Does an open-weight backbone (Llama, Qwen) actually preserve Δg ≤ 0.20, and how do prompts transfer?
  5. Can the framework handle network meta-analysis or multi-continuous-moderator meta-regression beyond binary subgroups?
  6. Would a held-out, never-pretrained-on corpus of unpublished preprints change extraction accuracy, isolating contamination effects?
  7. Is the significant / null discrepancy (p<0.001 vs. p=0.530) a property of included studies, of coding — and which is closer to truth?
