Paper Dashboard · AI-Generated Text Detection

GTCL reframes AI-text detection as discriminating latent-space generation trajectories — beating DeTeCtive on all three benchmarks.

Documents become ordered sequences of overlapping word windows whose embedding first-order differences form a discrete trajectory. A temporally aligned, supervised contrastive loss then separates human from machine — no logits, no watermark, no generator access.

§Methodology

Three stages, one frozen backbone

1
Trajectory representationDocument sliced into N=32 overlapping word windows (M=64, step Q=8). Each encoded by frozen Nomic Embed → trainable Transformer projection → L2-normalized z_i. Matrix X_T ∈ R^{N×D} is the latent trajectory.
2
First-order differencesModel δ_i = z_{i+1} − z_i instead of absolute positions. Suppresses topic/lexical global offsets, isolates the incremental semantic transitions tied to next-token prediction.
3
Time-aligned contrast + k-NNCausal mask (j ≤ i) + exp decay γ=0.2. Hard positives/negatives (Ψ=128), supervised contrastive loss (Khosla). Inference: k-NN (k=9) in the learned space.

Validated before the classifier: a Mann–Whitney U test on four geometry metrics (length, step irregularity, curvature, directional dispersion) shows significant human-vs-AI separation (p<0.05) across all 12 metric×dataset cells.

Weighted F1 tracks accuracy. Largest per-class win: F1(Human) 0.91 vs 0.78 on NYT-AI (+16.67 pts). Weakest baselines collapse on Reviews: TMR 0.47, RADAR 0.53, Binoculars 0.57.

§Strengths

Why it lands

  • Conceptual reframing — static embedding → discrete trajectory of differences — articulated and statistically grounded before any classifier is built.
  • Principled causal structure — the asymmetric, time-decaying similarity injects autoregressive causality directly into the contrastive objective.
  • Balanced detection — F1(Human) and F1(AI) stay within a few points on every dataset, unlike many baselines.
  • Text-only inference — no logits, no watermark, no generator access; maximally deployable.
  • Reproducible — public code, data, hyperparameter tables, ablation, and backbone-swap study.

§Limitations & Risks

What stays unproven

  • Missing component ablations — first-order differencing vs absolute positions, and asymmetric vs symmetric masking, are never isolated. The novelty claims rest on exactly these.
  • Small self-built Reviews set — 1,164 AI samples from only 3 generators, no held-out model; 0.98 may not generalize to in-the-wild AI reviews.
  • Marginal on RAID (+2.47 acc) — on a 1,827-doc / 3-family subset. The full RAID leaderboard is far larger; the "robust under adversarial" claim is undertested.
  • NYT-AI imbalance (2k human / 12k AI) inflates headline accuracy; F1 class gap persists.
  • No leave-one-dataset-out or unseen-LLM protocol — cross-domain generalization is asserted, not demonstrated. Frontier autoregressive and diffusion LMs untested.
  • k-NN scales linearly with training-set size; latency/deployment cost not discussed.
