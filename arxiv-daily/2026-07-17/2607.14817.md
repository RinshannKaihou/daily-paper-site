> **一句话总结：** 本文指出 OOD 检测与主动学习等代理任务与"最小化部署遗憾(regret)"在贝叶斯最优层面并不一致，作者将选择性预测与认知拒绝选项统一为一个带覆盖/风险/遗憾约束的凸组合阈值框架（定理 1），并提出以可达 (Coverage, Risk, Regret) Pareto 曲面的"缺口"取代秩相关作为不确定性解耦的诊断；在 CIFAR-10H、APPA-REAL 及 DCIC 等"稠密人工标注"数据集上的基准测试显示，按 regret 与 Pareto-gap 排序的方法名次与按代理任务排序发生明显倒置（例如 Evidential 在 OOD 上最差却在 CIFAR-10H 的纯遗憾上最好）。

> **TL;DR:** The paper argues that proxy tasks for epistemic uncertainty (OOD detection, active learning) are Bayes-optimal under scores that are mathematically distinct from regret-minimizing selectors. It unifies selective classification and the epistemic reject-option into one constrained framework (Theorem 1: the optimal selector thresholds a convex combination (1-λ)A*+λE* with λ∈[1/2,1]), proposes the Pareto-gap of the (Coverage,Risk,Regret) surface as a disentanglement diagnostic replacing rank correlation, and on dense-annotation datasets (CIFAR-10H, APPA-REAL, DCIC) shows sharp rank inversions — e.g., Evidential Networks rank worst on CIFAR-10 Far-OOD AuROC (0.726) but best on CIFAR-10H CE-loss AuReC (0.0673), while DDU is best at Far-OOD (0.935) but worst on regret (0.0958).

## 核心贡献

- **理论统一框架**：将经典选择性分类（基于总不确定性 T* = A* + E*）与"认知拒绝选项"(epistemic reject-option，仅基于 E*) 统一为一个带覆盖率/风险/遗憾三重约束的优化问题。**定理 1** 证明，三种约束问题 (P_ρ, P_Ri, P_Re) 的最优选择器都是真值 A*_ℓ 与 E*_ℓ 的凸组合阈值 {(1-λ)A* + λE* ≤ τ}，且 λ ∈ [1/2,1]，端点恰好恢复两种已有公式。
- **任务错配的形式化论证**：构造了一个受 Rossellini 函数启发的、有解析解的一维贝叶斯回归沙盒，明确展示 OOD 检测、BALD、Expected Error Reduction、风险-覆盖率与遗憾-覆盖率的最优打分器在固定 80% 覆盖下会拒绝**不同**的输入区域，证明任务间方法排名的不一致并非"训练不好"的伪影。
- **解耦诊断新指标——Pareto-gap**：反驳 Mucsányi et al. (2024) 用 Spearman 秩相关判断解耦失败的做法，证明高秩相关并不意味着功能失败（CIFAR-10H 上 Evidential 的 r_s(Â,Ê)=0.999 却有最低 Pareto-gap=0.0379 与最佳 AuReC=0.0673）。改用可达曲面与最优曲面在 (Coverage, Risk, Regret) 三维空间中的距离（基于 IGD+）作为联合解耦-效用诊断。
- **稠密标注基准上的实证反转**：在 CIFAR-10H、APPA-REAL、DCIC 套件（8 个真实多标注数据集）上用 ResNet-18/ResNet-101 训练 MC-Dropout、Deep Ensembles、DDU、Evidential Networks，实证显示按"纯认知效用(AuReC)"与"Pareto-gap"得出的方法排名与 OOD/主动学习代理任务排名发生实质性倒置。

## Key Contributions

- **Unified constrained-optimization framework.** Selective classification (thresholding T*) and the epistemic reject-option (thresholding E*) are merged into a single family of three constrained problems over selectors C: X→[0,1]. Theorem 1 (proved via KKT conditions in the Appendix) shows the optimum is always a threshold on the convex combination (1-λ)A*_ℓ + λE*_ℓ with λ∈[1/2,1]; λ=1/2 recovers Chow/Geifman-El-Yaniv selective prediction, λ=1 recovers Franc-Paplhám epistemic reject-option.
- **Closed-form demonstration of task misalignment.** A tractable 1-D Bayesian regression sandbox on the Rossellini function y=sin(0.2/(x+0.16)³)+ε makes the Bayes-optimal scorers for OOD detection, BALD, EER, risk-coverage, and regret-coverage all analytically computable; at 80% coverage they reject different subsets of input space, proving proxy-task disagreement is structural, not a training artifact.
- **Pareto-gap as a disentanglement diagnostic.** The authors refute the use of rank correlation (Mucsányi et al. 2024) for judging entanglement: high Spearman correlation between Â and Ê can coexist with optimal joint representational capacity. They instead define the Pareto-gap — the IGD+ distance between a method's achievable (Coverage,Risk,Regret) surface and the oracle surface — as a joint operational-utility diagnostic.
- **Empirical rank-inversion evidence.** Benchmarking MC-Dropout, Deep Ensembles, DDU, and Evidential Networks on dense-annotation datasets shows that decision-theoretic rankings (AuReC, Pareto-gap) disagree substantially with proxy-task rankings (OOD AuROC on OpenOOD, NAUC on OpenML), with pairwise rank inversions between top- and bottom-ranked methods.

## Methodology

**Bayesian/frequentist setup.** The paper works in a fixed-dataset regime with input space X, target space Y, training set D, parametric generative model p(x,y|θ)=p(x)p(y|x,θ), and deployment loss ℓ. It defines three ground-truth uncertainty components:
- Total T*_ℓ(x,D) = E[ℓ(H(x,D),y)]
- Aleatoric A*_ℓ(x) = E[ℓ(h(x,θ),y)] (irreducible, θ-known predictor)
- Epistemic/regret E*_ℓ(x,D) = T*_ℓ − A*_ℓ (reducible error of the deployed model)
with T* = A* + E* by construction. Selectors C: X→[0,1] are evaluated by Coverage ρ(C)=E[C(x)], Risk Ri(C)=E[C(x)T*], Regret Re(C)=E[C(x)E*].

**Theorem 1 (Unified selector).** For any of three constrained problems — maximize coverage subject to risk/regret budgets, minimize risk subject to coverage floor and regret cap, or minimize regret subject to coverage floor and risk cap — the optimal selector takes the form C*(x) = 1{(1-λ)A*_ℓ + λE*_ℓ ≤ τ} with λ ∈ [1/2,1]. The proof applies KKT conditions to each Lagrangian; for learned (mis-calibrated) estimators the linear combination weights are left unconstrained to compensate.

**Divergent Bayes-optimal scorers (Section 4).** The paper formally characterizes why proxies fail:
- OOD detection is optimally solved by the input likelihood ratio s*_OOD(x)=p_ood(x)/p_id(x), independent of ℓ and p*(y|x).
- BALD's information-gain objective equals Bayesian regret E*_log only under NLL loss; any loss-model mismatch breaks the equivalence.
- Expected Error Reduction (EER) is a global acquisition score over the post-update posterior, not pointwise E*; high-E* inputs with low p_te can yield near-zero EER.

**Pareto-gap diagnostic.** The oracle surface S* = {(ρ(C), Ri(C), Re(C)) : C thresholds (1-λ)A*+λE*, λ∈[1/2,1]} is compared to a method's achievable surface S(Â,Ê) = {(ρ,Ri,Re) : C thresholds w₁Â+w₂Ê ≤ τ, w₁,w₂,τ∈R}. The optimal (w₁,w₂,τ) are searched directly on the test set (an oracle upper bound, not a deployment recipe). Distance is measured by modified Inverted Generational Distance (IGD+).

**Empirical benchmark.** Models: MC-Dropout (p=0.1, S=20 passes), Deep Ensembles (M=5), DDU (spectral norm Lipschitz=3.0, GMM density on features), Evidential Networks (Dirichlet outputs via Softplus+1, soft-label evidential CE loss, λ_KL=0). Backbones: ResNet-18 (CIFAR-10H, DCIC) pre-trained on ImageNet, ResNet-101 frozen feature extractor + MLP probe (APPA-REAL). All APPA-REAL and DCIC training uses soft-label distributions. Loss-consistent aleatoric/epistemic components extracted under CE/0-1 loss (CIFAR/DCIC) and squared/absolute error loss (APPA-REAL). Results averaged over 5 seeds. AuReC, excess-AuReC (= AuReC − AuReC_oracle), and n-AuReC (= (AuReC − AuReC_oracle)/(AuReC_random − AuReC_oracle)) isolate ranking quality from base-accuracy differences.

## Results & Highlights

- **Proxy vs. regret rank inversion on CIFAR-10H (Table 1, CE loss).** Evidential Networks — dead last on OOD detection (Near-OOD AuROC = 0.711, Far-OOD AuROC = 0.726 in Table 4) — become the **best** method under pure epistemic regret: AuReC = **0.0673** and ex-AuReC = **0.0183**, vs. DDU which wins Far-OOD (AuROC = **0.935**) but is **worst** on regret (AuReC = **0.0958**). Deep Ensembles win Near-OOD (AuROC = **0.907**) but are mid-pack on regret (AuReC = **0.0752**).
- **Active learning inversion (Tables 5 vs. 1).** On OpenML ID6, Evidential Networks collapse to NAUC = **0.177** (vs. MC-Dropout 0.962 and Deep Ensemble 0.960) — yet the same method dominates epistemic regret on CIFAR-10H (AuReC = 0.0673), confirming the theoretical claim that global error-reduction and pointwise regret operate under different incentives.
- **Rank correlation is blind to operational utility.** On CIFAR-10H (CE), Evidential Networks show a near-perfect learned rank correlation r_s(Â_ℓ, Ê_ℓ) = **0.999** (traditionally labelled "entangled") yet achieve the **lowest** Pareto-gap = **0.0379** and the best AuReC = 0.0673. The ground-truth r_s(A*_ℓ,E*_ℓ) on CIFAR-10H reaches **0.882**, showing that high learned correlation can faithfully reflect the data-generating process, not a method failure.
- **DCIC suite (Table 3).** Across 8 multi-rater image-classification datasets, Deep Ensembles win on **5/8** (CE) and **6/8** (0-1) by lowest n-AuReC; DDU wins on **0/8** and exhibits the worst average n-AuReC (0.967 CE / 0.978 0-1, with one dataset's ground-truth rank correlation dropping to −0.347).
- **APPA-REAL age estimation (Table 2).** Deep Ensembles give the lowest AuReC (squared-error **11.3519**, absolute-error **0.7948**) and the smallest Pareto-gap (**0.1806** / **0.1531**); DDU is worst (AuReC = **20.0456**, n-AuReC = 1.048, even exceeding the random baseline).
- **Closed-form sandbox (Figure 1).** At a fixed 80% coverage budget, the Bayes-optimal rejection regions for OOD, BALD, EER, T*-coverage, and E*-coverage on the Rossellini function are visually disjoint subsets of the input space — direct evidence that "evaluating via OOD or active learning actively optimizes for rejecting a different subset of the data."

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high).** The Theorem 1 unification of selective classification and epistemic reject-option via a single convex-combination threshold is a genuinely new and clean theoretical result; the proof is short and correct. The critique of rank-correlation-based disentanglement (Mucsányi et al. 2024) is well-targeted and empirically backed.
- **Rigor (mixed).** The regret benchmark is carefully designed: oracle evaluation on test-set optimal weights is explicitly framed as a capacity upper bound rather than a deployment recipe, and ex-AuReC/n-AuReC are used to factor out base-accuracy differences. However, **two load-bearing empirical pillars rest on an "Anonymous, 2026" concurrent submission ("probly")**: all OOD (Table 4), selective-classification, and active-learning (Table 5) numbers are adopted from this unreviewed source, and the headline "rank inversion" claim leans on cross-task comparisons that pair those borrowed numbers with the authors' own AuReC numbers.
- **A specific methodological quirk: cross-dataset comparison.** The headline inversion pairs CIFAR-10 OOD AuROC (Table 4) with CIFAR-10H AuReC (Table 1). These are different test distributions, and the paper acknowledges that "evaluations span heterogeneous datasets; different datasets for OOD, active learning, and regret." A within-dataset, within-loss inversion (e.g., Evidential simultaneously best on regret and worst on OOD on the *same* test set) would be far more convincing than the cross-set pairing actually presented.
- **Missing ablations.** No analysis of how the Pareto-gap behaves under controlled miscalibration (e.g., temperature scaling), no sensitivity to the number of ensemble members / dropout passes, and no test of whether Evidential's regret win survives when the KL regularizer λ_KL is turned on (the authors set λ_KL=0, a setting that tends to favour evidential outputs).
- **Reproducibility (moderate).** Codebase is the "probly" framework (under review, not public at submission); backbone, hyperparameters, and seed counts are documented in Appendix C. Reliance on dense-annotation datasets (CIFAR-10H, APPA-REAL, DCIC) is the right call given the uncomputability of true regret on single-label data, but limits the benchmarks to domains where such labels exist.
- **Significance (high for the UQ evaluation subfield).** If the community accepts that OOD and AL are misaligned proxies for epistemic utility, this could reshape how UQ papers report results — but adoption hinges on whether the AuReC/Pareto-gap protocol itself becomes standard.
- **Red flags.** Heavy citation of own concurrent work and an anonymous submission; the Pareto-gap is an oracle metric that cannot be computed in deployment, so its diagnostic value for practitioners (vs. for benchmark authors) is undersold.

## Strengths

- Theorem 1 is elegant, general, and unifies two previously separate frameworks under one convex family indexed by λ∈[1/2,1].
- The closed-form Rossellini sandbox makes the abstract "tasks disagree" claim concrete and visually obvious.
- The Pareto-gap reframes "disentanglement" as a question of *operational* utility rather than statistical independence — a conceptually stronger position than the rank-correlation criterion it replaces.
- Honest framing of the frequentist paradox (true regret is uncomputable without knowing the Bayes-optimal predictor) and a clean workaround via dense-annotation datasets.
- Comprehensive per-dataset tables (Appendix D, Tables 6–13) and 5-seed averaging lend statistical credibility.

## Limitations

- True expected regret requires p*(y|x), so the proposed metrics are benchmarking tools only and **cannot be applied to standard single-label datasets**; this restricts the evaluation regime.
- The oracle Pareto-gap and oracle AuReC are computed by searching optimal weights on the test set — useful for benchmarking capacity but not a deployable selector.
- Ground-truth regret conflates estimation and approximation error; the authors do not separate these two error sources.
- Headline rank inversions rely on cross-task comparisons where OOD, active-learning, and regret are measured on **different datasets**; a within-dataset demonstration would be stronger.
- Proxy-task results (OOD, AL) are imported from an anonymous concurrent submission that readers cannot yet verify.
- The benchmark suite is limited to image classification and tabular regression; NLP and large-scale vision-language settings are not covered.

## Relevance

这篇论文对"不确定性量化(尤其是认知/认知不确定性)的评估方法"提出了根本性的批评与替代方案，对从事选择性预测、reject-option、安全 AI 部署与可靠 LLM/视觉模型研究的方向高度相关——它直接影响如何为带拒绝选项的模型挑选与报告不确定性估计器，以及如何理解 OOD 检测/主动学习作为代理任务的局限性。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the Theorem 1 convex-combination selector remain optimal (or close) under **non-proper losses** such as 0/1 with strong ties, or under structured prediction losses (e.g., sequence-level CTC, ranking NDCG)?
- Can the Pareto-gap be **estimated without oracle access** — e.g., via conformal or split-conformal surrogates — so that it becomes a deployment-time diagnostic rather than a benchmark-only tool?
- How does Evidential Networks' regret win on CIFAR-10H survive when the evidential KL regularizer λ_KL is swept, and when the backbone is scaled beyond ResNet-18?
- Would the OOD-vs-regret rank inversion still hold on a **single shared test set** with both dense human labels and an OOD split, removing the current cross-dataset confound?
- The paper benchmarks 4 UQ families; where do newer methods (DEUP, proper-scoring-rule decompositions, BNN variance decompositions) sit on the Pareto-gap axis?
- The Rossellini sandbox uses NLL-equivalent loss where BALD = E*_log; how does the disagreement pattern shift for losses where even the Bayesian regret score diverges from BALD by construction?
