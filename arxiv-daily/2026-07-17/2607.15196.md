> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"主观风险分解"这一新视角，证明在严格proper loss下对期望主观风险（即反向交叉熵）做偏置-方差-熵分解后，认知/偶然不确定性能自然地以分解项形式涌现，从而把 Gal 的互信息、Sale 的逐标签不确定性、Depeweg 的高斯信息论项等众多既有度量统一为同一原则的特例，并把它与统计学习理论中的近似/估计误差联系起来。

> **TL;DR:** A position/theory paper arguing that epistemic/aleatoric uncertainty measures are not primitives needing axioms, but consequences of higher-level modelling choices. By reverse bias-variance-entropy decomposition of the *expected subjective risk* (the reverse cross-entropy R_q(p)=E_{Y~q}[ℓ(p,Y)]) under any strictly proper loss, the authors recover Gal (2016)'s information-theoretic MI/conditional-entropy terms plus a systematic bias, and derive Sale et al. (2024)'s label-wise and Depeweg et al. (2018)'s Gaussian measures as special cases. They then bridge to statistical learning theory, defining subjective analogues of excess risk / approximation error / estimation error, and prove (Corollary 6) that mutual information is a *systematic under-estimate* of true subjective estimation error. This is a no-benchmark theory paper; the only numbers are illustrative (e.g., on synthetic Bernoulli posteriors MI/EPKL/RMI are reported as 0.07/0.14/0.08 for Beta(5,5), and the EPKL≈2×MI 2nd-order approximation of Theorem 8 degrades from a 2.0× ratio to a 3.6× ratio as posterior variance grows from Beta(5,5) to Beta(0.4,0.4)).

## 核心贡献

- 提出"主观风险分解"作为统一框架：在严格 proper loss 下对期望主观风险 E_θ[R_{q_θ}(p)] 做反向偏置-方差-广义熵分解（Theorem 2），把认知不确定性归为方差项（loss-induced Bregman 方差），把偶然不确定性归为期望广义熵项，外加一个刻画"外部锚定"的系统性偏置项。
- 一次性恢复多个经典提案为特例：Example 1 证明 Gal (2016) 的互信息 + 条件熵分解正是反向交叉熵分解；Example 2 恢复 Depeweg et al. (2018) 的高斯信息论项；Example 3 恢复 Sale et al. (2024) 的逐标签不确定性。附录还覆盖 Deep Ensembles、方差型 vs 熵型方法等多源不确定性。
- 提出"为什么不同 UQ 度量会给出不同排序"的解释：这不是矛盾，而是同一模型上不同 proper loss 的 Hessian 曲率（∇²ϕ 在 simplex 边界附近的增长快慢）造成的几何后果——log/pseudo-spherical loss 边界处曲率爆炸，对"模型把概率质量放在现实认为不靠谱的事件上"敏感，而 Brier loss 边界曲率恒定、不敏感。
- 把 UQ 接入统计学习理论：定义 excess subjective risk、subjective approximation gap（可能为负，故称"gap"而非"error"）与 subjective estimation error（Theorem 4 非负），并证明当假设类一致 q̄_n→p 时主观最优与客观最优模型渐近一致（Proposition 3）。
- 关键诊断结论（Corollary 6）：互信息只是主观估计误差的一部分，因此把 MI 当作"完整"的认知不确定性会系统性低估真正的可约部分——MI 应被解读为认知不确定性的下界，而非完整刻画。
- 在 Theorem 7-8 中给出互信息的"对称/反对称"分解，并证明 EPKL（Expected Pairwise KL）是 MI 的二阶近似（EPKL ≈ 2·MI），补充 Schweighofer et al. (2023) 的实证观察。

## Key Contributions

- A unifying *subjective-risk decomposition* framework: under any strictly proper loss, the expected subjective risk decomposes as bias + (loss-induced Bregman) variance + generalized entropy (Theorem 2). Epistemic uncertainty = variance; aleatoric = expected generalized entropy; the bias term carries the external grounding against reality.
- Recovers a long list of existing measures as special cases of the *same* principle rather than as competing definitions: Gal (2016) information-theoretic MI/conditional-entropy (Example 1, from reverse cross-entropy), Depeweg et al. (2018) Gaussian differential-entropy terms (Example 2), Sale et al. (2024) label-wise uncertainty (Example 3), plus Deep Ensembles and variance-vs-entropy methods in Appendix B.
- Provides a principled explanation for *why* different UQ measures disagree: the disagreement is a direct consequence of loss curvature (∇²ϕ near the simplex boundary), not a contradiction. Log and pseudo-spherical losses blow up at the boundary (hallucination-sensitive); Brier loss has constant curvature (insensitive).
- Bridges UQ with statistical learning theory by defining subjective analogues of excess risk, approximation error (the "subjective approximation gap", which can be negative) and estimation error (non-negative, Theorem 4). Shows asymptotic agreement of subjective and objective best-in-class models when q̄_n → p (Proposition 3).
- Diagnoses a widespread mis-reading of mutual information (Corollary 6): MI captures only the posterior-disagreement component of subjective estimation error and is a *systematic under-estimate* of true epistemic uncertainty — so it should be read as a lower bound, not a complete characterization.
- Proves an asymmetric decomposition of mutual information (Theorem 7) and that EPKL is a 2nd-order approximation to it (Theorem 8: I(Ŷ;Θ) = ½·E_{θ,θ'}[K(q_θ‖q_θ')] + O(ε³)), formalising Schweighofer et al. (2023)'s empirical observation and contrasting with Fishkov et al. (2025)'s "all argument-orderings" enumeration strategy.

## Methodology

The paper is purely theoretical/position. Its machinery is:

1. **Subjective vs objective risk.** Objective risk R_p(z)=E_{Y~p}[ℓ(z,Y)] evaluates a prediction under the true data-generating distribution. Subjective risk R_q(z)=E_{Ŷ~q}[ℓ(z,Ŷ)] (Savage 1971) evaluates the *true* distribution under the model's own belief q. The authors repurpose subjective risk as an evaluation functional against reality rather than as a decision-making framework — answering "how realistic is this model?" instead of "how correct is this model?".

2. **Proper-loss / Bregman machinery.** Under a strictly proper loss, R_q(z) = B_ϕ(q,z) + H_ℓ(q), where ϕ=−H_ℓ is the generator and B_ϕ is the induced (asymmetric) Bregman divergence. Because Bregman divergences admit a bias-variance decomposition (Pfau 2025, Heskes 2026), the *expected* subjective risk admits a reverse bias-variance decomposition (Theorem 2): E_θ[R_{q_θ}(p)] = B_ϕ(q̄,p) + E_θ[B_ϕ(q_θ,q̄)] + E_θ[H_ℓ(q_θ)], where q̄ is the right Bregman centroid (the mixture). The three terms are read as bias / epistemic / aleatoric.

3. **Instantiation by example.** Pick the hypothesis class H and a strictly proper loss, then read off the three terms. Log-loss on categoricals yields Gal's I(Ŷ;Θ) + H(Ŷ|Θ); log-loss on unknown-mean/variance Gaussians yields Depeweg's terms; squared loss on Bernoullis yields Sale et al.'s label-wise uncertainty. The recipe is uniform: "uncertainty terms are decomposition terms, not primitives."

4. **Loss-curvature analysis.** The local geometry of B_ϕ is governed by the Hessian ∇²ϕ(p); for log loss ∇²ϕ scales as 1/p_i (boundary-sensitive, hallucination-prone), whereas Brier's Hessian is constant. This recovers the well-known mode-seeking vs mean-seeking behaviour of forward/reverse KL and links to RLHF / Prior Networks / LLM hallucination empirics.

5. **Learning-theoretic extension.** Define subjective excess risk Δ_subj = E_θ[R_{q_θ}(p) − R_{q_θ}(q_θ)] = E_θ[B_ϕ(q_θ,p)], the subjective best-in-class q*_subj, and decompose Δ_subj into subjective approximation gap + subjective estimation error. Theorem 5 shows the variance component equals Gal's MI under log loss; Corollary 6 derives the under-estimate result.

6. **EPKL–MI analysis.** A 3-point identity yields I(Ŷ;Θ) = ½·E_{θ,θ'}[K(q_θ‖q_θ')] + ½·E_θ[K(q_θ‖q̄) − K(q̄‖q_θ)] (Theorem 7); a perturbation argument shows the symmetric component dominates to 2nd order, so EPKL ≈ 2·MI (Theorem 8).

## Results & Highlights

This is a theory/position paper; there are **no benchmark experiments on real datasets and no baselines**. The quantitative content consists of the theorems above plus an illustrative Figure 2 that numerically demonstrates Theorem 8 (the EPKL ≈ 2·MI 2nd-order approximation) on synthetic Bernoulli posteriors with known analytical MI/EPKL/RMI:

- On a **Beta(5,5) posterior** (low posterior variance, ε small), MI = 0.07, EPKL = 0.14, RMI = 0.08 — here EPKL/MI = 2.0, exactly matching the Theorem 8 prediction EPKL ≈ 2·MI.
- On a **Uniform[0,1] posterior** (moderate variance), MI = 0.28, EPKL = 0.72, RMI = 0.44 — the EPKL/MI ratio grows to ≈2.57, showing the 2nd-order approximation starting to degrade.
- On a **Beta(0.4,0.4) posterior** (high posterior variance, bimodal), MI = 0.50, EPKL = 1.80, RMI = 1.30 — the EPKL/MI ratio reaches 3.6, confirming that EPKL≈2·MI breaks down as ε grows, consistent with the O(ε³) remainder term in Theorem 8.

The *qualitative* headline results, which are the actual contributions of the paper, are:

- Gal (2016)'s epistemic/aleatoric terms arise exactly as the variance and generalized-entropy terms of the reverse cross-entropy decomposition, *plus* a previously-overlooked systematic bias term B_ϕ(q̄,p).
- Sale et al. (2024)'s label-wise uncertainty (Example 3), Depeweg et al. (2018)'s Gaussian information-theoretic terms (Example 2), and (in Appendix B) Deep Ensembles and variance-vs-entropy methods are all recovered by choosing a different strictly proper loss or hypothesis class.
- **Corollary 6 (key diagnostic):** mutual information systematically *under-estimates* the true subjective estimation error — i.e., treating MI as the full epistemic uncertainty misses the additional error from restricting predictions to the hypothesis class. MI is a lower bound, not a complete characterization.
- **Proposition 3:** subjective and objective best-in-class models coincide iff their right Bregman projections onto H coincide; under a consistent predictive mixture (q̄_n → p) the two learning targets are asymptotically aligned.
- Disagreement between entropy-based, variance-based, and divergence-based UQ measures is *explained* by loss curvature rather than treated as contradiction.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high):** The reframing of UQ measures as *consequences* (decomposition terms) rather than *primitives* (axiomatised objects) is genuinely new and timely, given the live community debate (Wimmer et al. 2023, Bickford-Smith et al. 2025, Sale et al. 2026 vs Kirchhof et al. 2025). The bias-term insight — that the literature's AU+EU view implicitly equates total uncertainty with a *de-biased* expected subjective risk (Section 3.5) — is a sharp observation that reframes the additive-TU=AU+EU debate.
- **Rigour (mixed):** Main-text theorems are stated cleanly and Appendix A–C give full proofs built on established Bregman-divergence bias-variance results (Pfau 2025, Heskes 2026). However, several steps rely on *expectations being finite* (a footnote-style assumption in the proof of Theorem 2) — and Section 5.4 itself concedes that non-standard argument orderings (RMI, Modified Bregman information) "can be infinite in some cases," so the well-definedness of the decomposition for heavy-tailed or boundary-placed posteriors is not fully addressed.
- **Reproducibility (good for a theory paper):** All claims are analytic; Figure 2's MI/EPKL/RMI values for Beta(5,5)/U[0,1]/Beta(0.4,0.4) Bernoulli posteriors are closed-form and trivially reproducible. There is no code, but none is strictly needed.
- **Significance (conditional):** The unification is conceptually valuable, but its *practical* impact depends on downstream work that uses the framework to (a) design new UQ measures for specific loss geometries, or (b) operationalise the "MI is a lower bound" warning (Corollary 6) in real selective-prediction / RLHF / OOD pipelines. As presented, it does not yet change practice.
- **Red flags / overclaiming:**
  - The paper's framing occasionally overstates how much it "resolves" — e.g., the Section 5.2 line "We posit that our work has resolved this [epistemic uncertainty being a heuristic concept]" overclaims; the paper provides a *re-interpretation*, not an empirical demonstration that the new view yields better-calibrated estimates in practice.
  - **No empirical validation of the central claim that subjective risk is a better practical UQ target** — specifically, the Section 3.2 motivational claims about hallucination / reverse-KL / Prior-Network connections to LLMs (citing Agarwal 2024, Gu 2024, Malinin & Gales 2019) are used as motivation but never tested in the paper. The hook to RLHF and LLM hallucination is dropped after the motivation section, leaving the most exciting practical implication unfulfilled.
  - The Example catalogue (Examples 1–3 + Appendix B) recovers *familiar* measures; the paper does not yet showcase a *new* measure that the framework makes possible and that prior ad-hoc proposals missed — which would have been a more compelling demonstration of "consequence, not primitive."
  - Comparison with Fishkov et al. (2025) is the closest related work and the differentiator ("we fix modelling+evaluation first and obtain a single decomposition" vs "they enumerate all argument orderings") is articulated only verbally, not via a worked example where the two methodologies give genuinely different recommendations.
  - Theorem 8's 2nd-order approximation is validated only on three hand-picked Bernoulli posteriors; a sweep over more distributions (multinomial, Gaussian mixtures) would have strengthened the claim.

## Strengths

- Provides a clean, single-principle explanation for an otherwise crowded zoo of epistemic/aleatoric definitions (MI, EPKL, RMI, Modified/Reverse-Modified Bregman information, label-wise, Gaussian information-theoretic).
- The bias-variance-entropy reading naturally surfaces a *third* term (systematic bias) that the standard AU+EU view silently drops — a genuinely useful diagnostic.
- The bridge to statistical learning theory (excess / approximation / estimation, Proposition 3, Theorem 4–5, Corollary 6) is the most novel and potentially impactful part; it gives a principled answer to "what is MI actually estimating?" (answer: a lower bound on subjective estimation error).
- Honest, high-quality engagement with the live 2025–2026 debate (Bickford-Smith et al., Jimenez et al., Sale et al., Kirchhof et al.); the discussion section (Section 5) is a genuinely useful map of the field.
- Math is clean and the appendix proofs are complete; notation is consistent once the reader internalises forward vs reverse argument orderings.

## Limitations

- **No empirical experiments** on any real dataset, benchmark, or downstream task — every "result" is either a theorem or a closed-form illustrative number. Readers wanting evidence that the framework improves calibration, OOD detection, or selective prediction will not find it.
- The most exciting practical hook — the connection between subjective risk / reverse-KL and LLM hallucination and RLHF (Section 3.2) — is used only as motivation and never exercised; this is the obvious next paper and its absence is felt.
- The framework currently requires specifying a strictly proper loss and a hypothesis class up front; there is no guidance for *choosing* the loss/geometry in a given modelling scenario, which is exactly the decision a practitioner needs to make. The paper explains *why* measures disagree but does not say *which* one to use.
- The "MI is a lower bound" result (Corollary 6) is theoretically clean but the gap between MI and the true subjective estimation error is not quantified, so practitioners cannot tell when ignoring the gap is safe.
- Several convergence/finite-expectation assumptions are buried in proof footnotes; the boundary behaviour (distributions on the simplex boundary, infinite RMI cases the paper itself flags) is not fully handled.
- As a position paper it does not propose or validate any *new* estimator; its claim to unify existing ones is strong, but the framework's generative power (producing measures no one had thought of) is undemonstrated.

## Relevance

与用户关注的 cs.LG / stat.ML / cs.AI 方向高度相关：不确定性量化（UQ）是可信机器学习的核心议题，本文提供了统一认知/偶然不确定性的理论框架，并明确点出"互信息是认知不确定性的下界"这一警示性结论，对从事贝叶斯深度学习、选择性预测、RLHF 或大模型幻觉研究的读者具有直接的参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the framework generate a *new* UQ estimator (a previously-unseen loss-geometry instantiation) that empirically outperforms MI on standard benchmarks (e.g., OOD detection on CIFAR-10/100 vs SVHN, or selective-prediction AURC on ImageNet)?
- How large is the gap between mutual information and the true subjective estimation error in practice (Corollary 6)? Is it negligible for well-specified deep ensembles, or material for under-trained BMA approximations?
- Can the LLM-hallucination / reverse-KL motivation of Section 3.2 be turned into a concrete subjective-risk-based hallucination detector, and does it beat existing entropy-/margin-based detectors on benchmarks like TruthfulQA or HaluEval?
- The framework assumes strictly proper losses; what happens for non-proper losses (e.g., hinge, 0-1) that are widely used in practice — is there an extension, or does the decomposition simply not apply?
- How does the subjective approximation gap (which can be negative) behave as a function of hypothesis-class capacity H — can it be used as a data-driven model-selection criterion that complements the classical approximation/estimation trade-off?
- Proposition 3 gives asymptotic agreement of subjective and objective best-in-class models; what are finite-sample rates, and can they be connected to PAC-Bayes or posterior-contraction bounds?
