01 / METHOD

PRISM — a diagnostic probe, not a guardrail

single forward pass · threshold τ = 0.5
i
Hidden states
Take last-token hL(x) at every transformer layer for safe / CD / PD examples.
ii
Layer select
Pick layer ℓ* by 5-fold CV accuracy on training set (mid-to-late, e.g. 27/36 for 3B).
iii
Standardize in-fold
StandardScaler fit inside each fold only — no leakage across CV splits.
iv
Logistic probe
LogisticRegression(max_iter=2000, C=1.0) over full hidden state, jointly on CD + PD.
Danger directions are class-mean vectors CDD = μ_CD − μ_safe and PDD = μ_PD − μ_safe. Two angles are reported: θ_md (raw geometry) and θ_pw (decision geometry of two L2 probes). A 200-split random-partition null rules out high-dimensional fitting artifacts. PRISM detects; it does not intervene.

04 / CRITICAL ASSESSMENT

Novel & careful — with bounded evidence

Novelty
A genuinely new framing.
Treating embodied physical danger as a distinct representation-level target — not a subtask of text moderation — is a real conceptual contribution. The CDD/PDD direction analysis with a random-split null substantiates it cleanly.
Rigor
Strong at 3B, observational elsewhere.
Random-split null only completed for Qwen2.5-3B (z=10.09, p<0.0001). Authors explicitly write nulls for 7B/14B/32B "were not completed" — the cross-scale claim is an observed angle pattern, not a tested one.
Baselines
Mixed strength.
Llama Guard 3-1B is near-trivial here (taxonomy mismatch → 0/1000 PD). The "comparable to judge" McNemar result sits at a matched-FPR threshold (τ≈0.029 / 0.35), not PRISM's default τ=0.5 — two different operating points.
Honesty
Refreshingly bounded claims.
"Separable rather than strictly orthogonal"; "PRISM is not a complete embodied-agent safety system"; cross-dataset runs are "replication under matched evaluation," not zero-shot transfer.
05 / LIMITATIONS

What the numbers cannot say

  • →Single-scale null. 7B/14B/32B separability is observational, not statistically tested.
  • →Lexical leakage. SafeAgentBench keyword split leaks ("drop" in 12 safe / 29 CD) — inherited by all SAB numbers.
  • →Operating-point drift. McNemar comparability is at matched-FPR τ, not default τ=0.5.
  • →No zero-shot transfer. Cross-dataset eval uses fold-internal CV; unseen distributions untested.
  • →Self-built benchmark. The 99.6% headline sits on PSB-1K, authored by the same method's authors.
  • →No human validation. PSB-1K "physical danger" labels are not checked against real-world physical consequence.
