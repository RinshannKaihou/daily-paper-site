Key Finding

A training-free, weight-preserving closed-loop LQR over DiT activations steers World Action Models to robustness — up to +41 points on LIBERO-10.

WA-LQR treats a World Action Model's residual stream as a reduced-order linear system and nudges it, per denoising step, toward a contrastive robustness setpoint — no retraining, no weight edits.

r = 0.63
Hinge-loss separability predicts steering success across tasks & models
Key Metric
+41 pts
Peak per-task success-rate gain on LIBERO-10 (Cosmos-Policy, Gaussian-noise). Average gains: +11 to +13 pts.
Why This Matters

Sits at mechanistic interpretability, optimal control, and robot-policy robustness. The separability-as-predictor diagnostic lets you pre-screen any foundation-model policy's amenability to activation steering — before spending compute.

Critical Edge

No comparison against robustness-oriented fine-tuning, RL post-training, wrist-camera augmentation, or any data-augmentation baseline that the related-work section itself names as the dominant alternatives — so the "no retraining" claim is positioned, not cost-matched.

§4–5 · Two parts, one hypothesis

01Methodology

Hypothesis: linearly separable robustness features ⟹ steerable WAM. The paper validates every assumption (low intrinsic dimensionality, local linearity, Jacobian input-invariance) before deploying the controller.

Part 1 · Mechanistic Study §4

Collect DiT residual-stream activations from contrastive forward passes (clean vs. perturbed). Mean-pool over token / spatial / action-chunk. Per (layer ℓ, timestep t): PCA on contrastive directions → project to top-3 PCs → fit linear SVM → report hinge loss as separability (0 = separable, 1 = random). Confirms rank-3 ≈ 99% variance & transition-Jacobian cosine ≈ 1.

Part 2 · Steering §5

ActAdd (open-loop): add averaged contrastive vector at fixed γ. Brittle — peaks at γ=0.1, collapses to 0% at γ≥0.2. WA-LQR (closed-loop): project to rank-64 basis, form reduced linearized dynamics via JVP/VJP (Jacobians never materialized), solve L-step LQR per (t, action-chunk τ) via Riccati. Control penalty r(τ)=min(R_final, R_init·exp(τ/τ_R)) → aggressive on early chunks, negligible later.

  • Training-free & weight-preserving — contrastive directions and linearized Ã, B̃ computed once, reused across LIBERO tasks.
  • Cross-task transfer validated via the Task i → Task j protocol (not train-on-test).

What lands well

03Strengths

  • Cheap inference-time fix — weight-preserving, attractive when retraining a WAM is expensive.
  • Honest negative result on LingBot-VA, plus an explicit separability→steerability predictor for cheap pre-screening.
  • Assumptions are validated, not assumed — variance concentration, local linearity, Jacobian reuse each get their own figure.
  • Cross-task transfer of both contrastive directions and linearized dynamics supports the "training-free" claim.
  • Closed-loop avoids the γ-cliff that kills ActAdd (67.4% @ γ=0.1 → 0% @ γ≥0.2).
Where it frays

04Limitations

  • Narrow evaluation — 3 perturbation types, single benchmark (LIBERO-10); lighting, background, language, and object-layout shifts untested.
  • Architecture-dependent — little benefit on LingBot-VA; not a universal WAM robustness tool.
  • No a-priori transfer predictor — mechanistic analysis must be re-run per setting.
  • Per-scene LQR hyperparameter sweep partially undercuts the "training-free" framing.
  • Open- vs. closed-loop is feature-dependent — ActAdd beats WA-LQR on highly-separable Gaussian noise.
  • 30 trials/cell — several ±8–11% "wins" sit within statistical noise.
