05 · METHODOLOGY

Two systems, one task surface — the contrast is only the harness

  • ASA (baseline): one Streamlit app, one GPT-5 call per turn; full CSV dumped into the prompt; free-form replies; no schema, no judge, no HITL, no persistence, no audit.
  • ASuS (engineered): LangGraph DAG of seven Python nodes over a typed AcademicSupervisionState; SQLite checkpointer; per-node append-only audit log.
  • M1 Matching: Chroma symbolic predicates + cosine over text-embedding-3-small. M2 Report eval: PyMuPDF + two HITL gates. M3 Request analysis. M4 Roadmap: judge → bounded 3× retry at 0.75. M5 Risk: deterministic weighted core, LLM narrates only. M6 Summary · M7 Open requests.
  • Evaluation: blind, hybrid, scenario-based — 10 raters, 5 scenarios, 30 MCQ verdicts + 12 dimension scores across D1–D6; neutral “System A/B” labels; no anchoring set.
  • Adversarial asymmetry: the larger GPT-5 is placed on the unscaffolded side, the smaller GPT-4o-mini on the scaffolded side — the deck is stacked against the authors’ own hypothesis.

06 · RESULTS

Wins are broad, but two are partly definitional

  • All six dimensions won (Table 3); pooled 4.08 vs 1.23.
  • Per-rater: 8/10 significant (Wilcoxon p ∈ [0.023, 0.027]); rater D a clear outlier (1.33 vs 1.17).
  • Inter-rater weak: Fleiss κ = 0.10–0.11 (“slight”); pairwise Cohen κ −0.66…0.97 — almost entirely rater D.
  • Footprint: ASuS ≈ 5,000 LOC vs ASA ≈ 300; much of the harness is domain-independent.
  • Caveat: D3 (consistency) and D6 (constraint adherence) are structurally unwinnable for ASA — no persistence, no schema.

For reliability/auditability-bound workflows, harness engineering can challenge the “bigger model is better” intuition — established here on one low-stakes synthetic domain with an author-constructed weak baseline.

Relevance · caveat: generalization to clinical/legal/production is asserted, not demonstrated.

07 · STRENGTHS

What this paper genuinely gets right

  • Adversarial model asymmetry biases the experiment against the authors’ own hypothesis.
  • Seven named, transferable patterns (P1–P7) with a module-to-pattern map practitioners can lift directly.
  • Unusual transparency — outlier rater, missing calibration, unanalysed hyperparameters, and absent per-pattern ablation are all self-disclosed.
  • Clean separation of generator / judge / deterministic core; risk module is a defensible high-stakes design.
  • Full open release — code, seed CSV, ratings, blind sheets, ablation JSONs, dependency-free stats script.

08 · LIMITATIONS

Where the claim overreaches

  • Straw-man baseline: ASA ≈ “bare LLM call,” not a credible strong-model deployment.
  • Definitional wins: several dimensions are unwinnable for ASA by construction.
  • Small n: 10 raters, 5 scenarios; per-rater p-values carry limited weight.
  • No per-pattern ablation — all seven evaluated as a bundle; marginal contribution of each unknown.
  • No sensitivity analysis on risk weights, 0.85/0.65 thresholds, 0.75 judge pass-score, 3-iteration cap.
  • Live demo protocol limits reproducibility of human judgments; self-evaluation confound in the 2×2 (generator = judge).
