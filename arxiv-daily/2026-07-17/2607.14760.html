Why this matters

An auditable, low-compute edge sensor-integrity gate; its strict paired statistics and explicit scope-declaration protocol transfer to other streaming anomaly detectors.

Critical edge

headline caveat

The headline F1 advantage is not statistically significant (paired-bootstrap CI [−0.041, 0.085] overlaps zero). The defensible claim is paired correctness and false-alarm control — not aggregate F1.

Three invariants: reference safety (alarming frames never enter the window) · episode non-duplication · scene-path reachability (Eq. 12).

Inside the paper

01

Methodology

Grayscale frames It emit yt=(dt, ct, zt, mt) — alarm, cue magnitude, fault class, diagnostic metadata. A sliding window of at most N=12 clean frames holds a scalar reference (µ*, g*) and a 32×32 per-cell mean.

  • Sampled stats: mean every 4th column O(HW/4); gradient proxy every 4th pixel O(HW/16) — avoids aliasing 1-pixel texture into a flat image.
  • Scene-transition path (Eq. 9): mean jump δt>θchg=80 + texture-collapse vs. frozen ref + gradient-change ratio qt≥ρ=0.2.
  • Occlusion path (Eq. 10): gt<0.45·g* and bounded mean shift 20<|µ−µ*|≤45 and µ∈[60,120] — rejects trivial drift and extreme transitions.
  • Negative gates: 32×32 coarse grid rejects structured lighting; mode + rapid-brightness gates suppress scene output for Tsup.

02

Results & highlights

  • Controlled scope (320 seqs): F1 0.800 / BAcc 0.822 @ FPR 0.069 vs histogram difference 0.777 @ 0.575. F1 margin +0.023 [−0.041, 0.085] not resolved; paired correctness better (McNemar p=0.0024).
  • Magnitude-swept Xiph audit (250 seqs, FPR≤0.05): highest normalized partial AUC 0.494 vs gradient collapse 0.422, histogram 0.394.
  • Extended-stress sweep (360 seqs): recall 0.925 @ FPR 0.025 (sensitivity diagnostic, not a held-out point).
  • Onset latency: median & P90 0 frames, max 10, mean 0.64 over 103 positives.
  • Long-negative audit: 0 false alarms in 9.09 camera-hours (VIRAT + CDnet2014); one-sided 95% Poisson <0.33 FA/h. Histogram difference: 46.1 FA/h on CDnet.

UHCTD full pass (4.5M frames): recall 0.021–0.028 at FPR 0.04–0.05 — openly reported as near-zero, since fixed photometric predicates only fire inside the declared envelope.

03

Strengths

  • Genuinely low false-alarm operation, triangulated across four independent regimes (controlled, Xiph, UHCTD decoded, VIRAT+CDnet long-negative) — not pooled.
  • Excellent statistical hygiene: paired McNemar, paired-bootstrap CIs, Clopper–Pearson intervals; honest split of "paired correctness significant" vs "F1 not resolved".
  • Bounded arithmetic and per-frame metadata (µ, g, µ*, g*, δ, L, s, τ) → usable as an embedded gate; 31,400 fps @160×90.
  • The configuration-consistency theorem (Eq. 12) is a small but real formal contribution: a deterministic check that suppressors cannot mask the scene path.
  • Scope honesty is exemplary — states what it does not do (no registration, no learned reference, no moved-camera / mean-preserving defocus) on page 1.
04

Limitations

  • Narrow positive scope by construction: camera rotation/translation, mean-preserving defocus, subtle partial covers are out of scope. Alarms on only 3/80 geometric-shift cases (frame difference: 77/80).
  • Fixed thresholds are camera-dependent: UHCTD camera-disjoint transfer fails in both directions; field use needs per-camera calibration the paper does not provide.
  • Baseline coverage gap: no head-to-head vs strongest end-to-end systems (Saglam & Temizel, Mantini & Shah deep models); UHCTD AlexNet baselines cannot be re-thresholded to FPR≤0.05.
  • No physically executed, continuously annotated real-camera tamper dataset — scripted multi-camera field study remains future work.
  • Headline F1 not statistically significant; two operational false negatives share the same weakness — close-range covers with mean shift outside ∆max.
Verdict: rigorous, honestly-scoped sensor-health subsystem — strong on false-alarm control & statistics, weak on baseline breadth & transfer. arXiv:2607.14760 ↗
