Key finding

Counterexample-guided loop turns plant + specs into neural control policies carrying formal (ε, δ) safety certificates.

An Evolutionary Strategy (Basic Random Search wrapped with an augmented-Lagrangian, multi-scenario solver) and Statistical Model Checking alternate in a refinement loop: every rejected candidate feeds its counterexamples back into the scenario set. The output is a policy with a defensible certificate — with confidence ≥ 1−δ, violation probability is ≤ ε.

02

How the loop works

Γ = (S, Env_S, (r,c), d, H) · Scl = (S, Env_S, πθ)

Problem · exact → relaxed

Maximize a minimum cumulative reward subject to zero cumulative constraint violation across all scenarios. Undecidable for nonlinear dynamics with continuous uncertainty, so relaxed to an (ε, δ)-solution.

Algorithm 1 · main loop

Solve finitization Γ_Σ → call verify(π*, γ*, ε, δ) → on counterexample, add to Σ and iterate → return candidate when none remain. Finite budget guards infinite-refinement.

Algorithm 2 · BRS solver

Perturb θ with antithetic Gaussian noise, evaluate across Σ, estimate direction from fitness deltas, update with AdamW. Average aggregation early, worst-case in refinement at reduced step α′ ≤ α.

Algorithm 3 · direction & constraints

Only Pareto-worst scenarios feed direction estimation to keep cost flat. Multi-scenario constraints handled by augmented-Lagrangian L_ES that internally tunes λ, μ.

Verification · SMC

Monte-Carlo draws fresh scenarios until either a counterexample is found or Pr[violation] ≤ ε holds with confidence 1−δ. Tolerance τ lowers target to γ̂ = γ(1−τ) to absorb eval noise.

Engineering · scaling to high-dim

tanh MLPs, column-normalized Gaussian init, running-mean/std input norm, K-Medoids scenario seeding, OpenMPI parallelism, patience early-stopping, ONNX export for deployment.

! DRL baselines fail SMC safety verification

Every RL Baselines3 Zoo policy (A2C, PPO, SAC, TD3, TQC) fails on Humanoid; only isolated passes occur on easier tasks (ARS/Ant, TD3/Walker2d, PPO·TD3·ARS·TQC/Hopper). Reinforces the well-known fragility of trained RL policies.

+ Zero violations on SafetyVelocity

SMC-ES holds 0 constraint violations across all six tasks. Safe-DRL baselines incur non-trivial violations (PPO-Lag 18.95 on Humanoid, FOCOPS 32.62 on Swimmer, RCPO 20.57 on Humanoid) while still not enforcing zero-violation guarantees.

+ Graceful degradation under noise

Under multiplicative sensor + additive actuation noise (ν=0.01) SMC-ES still yields verified policies: Humanoid 10,978 (vs 12,240 noiseless), Ant 5,766, Walker2d 4,722, InvDoublePendulum 9,359. Only the learning rate needed tuning.

! Scaling hits a communication wall

SafetyHalfCheetahVelocity across 128/256/512/1024 cores: 4.05 / 2.41 / 1.36 / 1.02 h — severe diminishing returns past 512 cores due to parameter-sync overhead, acknowledged as implementation-specific but unresolved.

04

Strengths & limitations

Critical assessment · read both columns
  • Formal, quantitative certificates on neural policies in high-dim continuous control (Humanoid: 376-dim obs).
  • Principled loop — correctness (Proposition 1) is clean and short, inheriting directly from the TII 2025 theorem.
  • Three regimes evaluated — standard MuJoCo, SafetyVelocity, and noise-augmented sim-to-real.
  • Honest reporting of cost and explicit acknowledgement that γ* is an optimistic upper bound on the floor.
  • Graceful robustness — only the learning rate had to drop under synthetic noise.
  • Statistical, not absolute — residual ε violation mass remains; γ* can be optimistic (ε, δ, τ dependent).
  • 2–4 orders more serial CPU-h than GPU-DRL; not viable without a sizable CPU cluster.
  • Asymmetric evaluation framing — verified lower bounds vs baselines' peak last-10% averages; lags on raw reward (Ant 6,505 vs TD7 10,133).
  • Safe-DRL numbers borrowed from Safety Gymnasium, not re-run; those algorithms aren't built for strict safety.
  • No ablations isolating K-Medoids seeding, Pareto-worst subsetting, or the average→worst-case switch.
  • No public artifact at submission (code only on acceptance); sim-to-real is synthetic Gaussian only.
