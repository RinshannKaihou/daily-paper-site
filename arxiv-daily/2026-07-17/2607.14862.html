01 / Methodology

Taming the underdamped Langevin velocity update

Problem. Solve minimize u(θ)=E[U(θ,X)] by sampling the Gibbs measure πβ ∝ exp(−βu) for large β.

tSGHMC update (eqs. 9–10). A tamed position-velocity discretization of underdamped Langevin:

νn+1 = νn − λ[γνn + Hν(θ_n, Xn+1)] + √(2λγβ−1)·ξn+1
θn+1 = θ_n + λνn

with the custom taming coefficient

Hν(θ,x) = mθ + (H(θ,x) − mθ) / √(1 + γ−1|θ|4r)

chosen because it satisfies dissipativity, linear growth, and a convergence-friendly property (Lemmas 14–16) — neutralizing the superlinear-growth blow-up of Euler-discretized SGHMC while keeping mini-batch cost.

Relaxed assumptions. (A2) F is locally Lipschitz/bounded with polynomial growth; G only needs continuity-in-average (may be discontinuous). (A3) Data-dependent strong convexity via a matrix A(x) with smallest eigenvalue m>0 of E[A(X₀)] — no global-Lipschitz, no bounded-data requirement.

Proof mechanism. Moreau-Yosida regularization uMY,ε + auxiliary coupled processes (Z, ζ, r) split the W₂ error into a discretization term (Prop. 4), a contraction (Thm. 3, rate m/2γ), and a regularization error (Lemma 5). Coupling ε=λ1/4, γ=O(ε⁻¹) is where the 1/4 rate is won vs the 1/5 in [33].

03 / Strengths

What lands cleanly

  • Genuine gap-filler. First SGHMC variant at the intersection of taming and stochastic-gradient kinetic Langevin — prior tamed schemes use the expensive exact gradient h, prior SGHMC theory needs global-Lipschitz H.
  • Non-trivial coefficient design. The |θ|4r denominator of Hν is justified by Lemmas 14–16 (dissipativity, linear growth, convergence), not ad hoc.
  • Rigorous, traceable theory. The 1/4-vs-1/5 gain is explicitly traced to γ=O(λ−1/4) replacing [33]'s λ−1/5, and a sharper Prop. 4 (λγ term O(λ3/4) vs O(λ2/5)).
  • Broad applicability. Continuity-in-average for G + data-dependent strong convexity cover CVaR, quantile estimation, ReLU regularization, newsvendor, and online data.
  • Domain-meaningful demos. Operations research + insurance/finance + ML, each with assumption verification.
  • Reproducible. Public GitHub repo, full hyperparameter disclosure (λ grids, β=10¹⁰, γ, r, m), dataset citations.
04 / Limitations

Where the claim is load-bearing

  • No complexity-class win. Õ(ε⁻⁵) ties tKLMC/tOBABO and loses to tSE's Õ(ε⁻⁵/³); 1/4-vs-1/5 is a tighter bound, not a faster algorithm.
  • Single baseline. Only TUSLA is benchmarked — no tKLMC / tSE / tOBABO / Adam comparison despite all appearing in the theory table. Structurally favors a second-order method.
  • Strong-convexity scope. Assumption 3 excludes non-convex deep learning; the lone NN run uses m=10⁻¹⁰ (effectively tiny curvature).
  • Toy-to-small scale. N from 100 to 2,167; d from 3 to 201 — no large-scale or high-dimensional demonstration.
  • Cherry-picked reporting. Wins are qualitative ("smaller RMSE," "reaches 0.03"); few hard RMSE numbers or variance bars in text.
  • Delicate hyperparameter recipe. The ε=λ1/4, γ=O(ε⁻¹) coupling is specific; sensitivity to γ misspecification is not studied empirically.
