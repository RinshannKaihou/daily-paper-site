> **一句话总结：** 本文训练了 180 个人脸识别模型（IResNet-34/50/100 × ArcFace/CosFace/MagFace × 5 个身份数量等级 × 4 个训练时长）进行全因子实验，量化嵌入向量超球面聚类几何中残留的训练集成员推断信号，发现训练身份数量是主导因素（ANOVA η̂p² ≥ .95），在 1K 身份时 PairCos AUC 达 0.90 但随身份增加单调下降，并通过 MLP 融合四类统计量在身份最弱处仍能额外恢复成员信息。

> **TL;DR:** The authors train 180 open-set face recognition models (IResNet-34/50/100 with ArcFace/CosFace/MagFace, crossed with training-identity counts nids ∈ {1K, 10K, 50K, 100K, all} and 5/10/20/40 epochs) to measure how much training-set membership information survives in the hyperspherical cluster geometry of embeddings. They compute four per-identity statistics (pairwise cosine, vMF κ, penalised logit, prototype-CE) and report threshold-classifier AUC on nine benchmarks (LFW, XQLFW, CPLFW, RFW, CFP-FP, CFP-FF, AgeDB, IJB-C, and a same-domain held-out WebFace4M split). Training-identity count dominates (η̂p² ≥ .95), with PairCos AUC dropping from 0.90 at nids=1K to 0.71 at nids=all; cross-domain non-member benchmarks (pose/quality shift) inflate the apparent signal by ~+0.22–0.26 AUC over the same-domain baseline; fusing the four statistics with an MLP beats the best single statistic (e.g., IR-100/ArcFace at nids=50K: 0.86 → 0.92). The work is the largest systematic study of its kind (180 models vs. the prior maximum of 9) and provides concrete guidance for privacy audits.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统量化了开集人脸识别模型嵌入超球面几何中的训练成员信息。在仅用嵌入向量（black-box）或嵌入+训练配置（grey-box）的访问条件下，对 4 个聚类几何统计量进行了成员/非成员可分性分析。
- 规模化的全因子实验设计：训练 180 个模型（3 个 IResNet backbone × 3 个角度间隔损失头 × 5 个训练身份数量等级 × 4 个训练时长），并以 ViT-T/CosFace 验证趋势在非卷积架构上的普适性；对比此前最大研究仅 9 个模型。
- 识别出主导控制因素：训练身份数量 nids 解释了最多的 AUC 方差（η̂p² ≥ .95），训练时长次之（η̂p² ≥ .89），而 backbone 与 loss head 的贡献仅为其十分之一。
- 引入同域留出参考集（held-out WebFace4M split），用以分离训练归因的信号与领域偏移效应，揭示跨域非成员基准（pose、quality）会把领域偏移与训练信号混淆，从而人为抬高成员信号约 +0.22–0.26 AUC。
- 提出统计量融合：用 2 层 MLP（64→32，5 折交叉验证）融合四类统计量，在所有配置上稳定地达到或超过最佳单统计量（nids=50K/100K 时增益最明显，Wilcoxon W=666, p < 10⁻¹⁰），表明嵌入几何携带的成员信息是当前下界估计。

## Key Contributions

- First systematic quantification of training-set membership information retained in the explicit hyperspherical cluster geometry of open-set face recognition embeddings, under both black-box (embeddings only) and grey-box (embeddings plus scale s and margin m) access models, evaluated through four per-identity statistics.
- Large fully-crossed factorial design (3 backbones × 3 angular-margin loss heads × 5 nids levels × 4 epoch counts = 180 models), an order of magnitude beyond the largest prior study (9 models), plus a ViT-T/CosFace configuration confirming the nids trend is not specific to CNNs.
- Identifies that training-identity count nids is the dominant factor (partial η̂p² ≥ .95), followed by training duration (η̂p² ≥ .89); backbone depth and loss head contribute at most η̂p² ≈ .09 and .04 respectively — roughly an order of magnitude smaller.
- Introduces a same-domain held-out WebFace4M reference split to disentangle training-attributable signal from domain shift, and demonstrates that cross-domain non-member benchmarks (pose in CFP-FF→CFP-FP, quality in LFW→XQLFW) inflate the apparent membership AUC by +0.26 and +0.22 respectively, matching or exceeding the training contribution itself at nids=100K.
- Shows that fusing the four statistics with a two-hidden-layer MLP (5-fold CV) consistently matches or surpasses the best single statistic, with the largest gains at the weakest points (nids=50K, 100K), one-sided Wilcoxon W=666, p < 10⁻¹⁰.

## Methodology

The paper measures residual membership signal purely in embedding geometry, framed as identity-level membership inference on open-set face recognition (no logits/loss/weights exposed).

**Access model.** An auditor queries the target FR model f with k ≥ 2 probe images of an identity y, obtains ℓ2-normalised 512-d embeddings, computes per-identity cluster statistics, then sweeps a threshold τ to produce a threshold-classifier ROC and reports its AUC as the membership information of the statistic. Two access levels: black-box (embeddings only) for PairCos and κ; grey-box (embeddings plus s and m) for PenLogit and ProtoCE.

**Four per-identity statistics.**
1. *PairCos* — mean pairwise cosine similarity within the identity (tighter cluster → higher value); this is equivalent to the intra-similarity feature used by Li et al.
2. *vMF concentration κ* — von Mises-Fisher concentration estimated via the Banerjee et al. closed-form MLE on the unit hypersphere; produces identical rankings to cluster inertia since embeddings are ℓ2-normalised.
3. *PenLogit* — mean margin-penalised logit computed via a leave-one-out prototype (requires s, m, i.e. grey-box).
4. *ProtoCE* — surrogate cross-entropy loss with a per-identity class prototype and other evaluation identities as negatives; values are not comparable across benchmarks with different identity pools.

**Experimental setup.** 180-model full factorial grid: backbones IResNet-34/50/100, loss heads ArcFace (s=64, m=0.5) / CosFace (s=64, m=0.4) / MagFace (s=64, per-sample m∈[0.45,0.8]), nids ∈ {1K, 10K, 50K, 100K, all≈205,990}, epochs ∈ {5,10,20,40}. All trained from scratch on WebFace4M (4M images, 205,990 identities), 112×112 InsightFace five-landmark alignment, SGD momentum 0.9, weight decay 5×10⁻⁴, poly-decay LR from 0.1 with 1 warmup epoch, batch 128, FP16, horizontal flip. Held-out same-domain reference: for each model, sample min(nids, 2500) members + 2500 non-members from the unsampled WebFace4M identities.

**Analysis methods.** Type-II ANOVA partial η̂p² to decompose variance across the four factors on N=180 models; Cohen's d′, Spearman ρ, and Kruskal–Wallis ϵ² to measure distributional-shift confounds; probit-scale transform to translate matched-pair effect sizes into AUC changes at a fixed baseline (nids=100K, AUC≈0.71); 2-hidden-layer MLP fusion (64→32, ReLU, BatchNorm, dropout 0.3, Adam η=10⁻³) with 5-fold stratified CV on the same-domain split for statistic fusion.

## Results & Highlights

- **Dominant factor is training-set size.** On the same-domain held-out WebFace4M split, partial η̂p² for nids is .99 (PairCos), .95 (κ), .99 (PenLogit), .95 (ProtoCE); epoch is next (.99/.89/.98/.92). Backbone contributes at most .09 and loss head at most .04 — about an order of magnitude less.
- **Monotonic decrease of membership signal with nids.** PairCos marginal-mean AUC on the same-domain reference: nids=1K → 0.90, 10K → 0.81, 50K → 0.73, 100K → 0.71, all → 0.71. By epoch: ep5=0.69, ep10=0.75, ep20=0.79, ep40=0.85. The signal persists above chance even at competitive verification EER (nids=100K, LFW EER≈0.2%, IJB-C EER≈0.1%).
- **Cross-domain benchmarks inflate the signal.** At nids=100K, the pose contribution (CFP-FF → CFP-FP) translates to an AUC change of +0.26, and the quality contribution (LFW → XQLFW) to +0.22 — both matching or exceeding the training contribution over chance (+0.21). Cohen's d′ for CFP-FP − CFP-FF is −2.27 (PairCos), −2.98 (PenLogit); for XQLFW − LFW it is −1.64 (PairCos), −1.81 (PenLogit). These results confirm that threshold-classifier AUC on any cross-domain benchmark conflates domain shift with membership.
- **Fusion beats the best single statistic.** Same-domain, epoch 40, 5-fold CV: IR-100/ArcFace nids=50K best-single 0.86 → MLP 0.92; nids=100K 0.73 → 0.79; IR-34/CosFace nids=50K 0.79 → 0.88; IR-50/ArcFace nids=100K 0.68 → 0.73. Across all cells the MLP consistently matches or outperforms the best single statistic; one-sided Wilcoxon signed-rank W=666, p < 10⁻¹⁰.
- **Demographic and age confounds are smaller but non-zero.** Kruskal–Wallis ϵ² across the four RFW ethnic groups is ≈0.02 for PairCos/κ/PenLogit but 0.123 (medium) for ProtoCE, so non-member benchmark demographic composition affects the baseline. Age spread (AgeDB) shows only weak correlation; only κ reaches the expected negative association (ρ = −0.26).
- **No single statistic is universally best.** PenLogit ranks first most often at moderate-to-high nids and on pose-/quality-degraded sets; ProtoCE attains the highest AUC on frontal/high-quality sets (CFP-FF, LFW, IJB-C). The best statistic is dataset-dependent.
- **Trend reproduces on ViT-T.** The ViT-T/CosFace block reproduces the nids trend in both EER and membership AUC, so the effect is not specific to convolutional backbones.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The per-identity statistics themselves are not new — PairCos equals the intra-similarity feature of Li et al. and the intra-class features of FACE-AUDITOR. The genuine novelty is the controlled measurement scale, the fully-crossed factorial design (Table 1 notes the largest prior FR study used 9 models without a fully-crossed design), and the addition of the same-domain held-out reference, which exposes a previously under-appreciated confound.
- **Rigour.** Strong: Type-II ANOVA with partial η̂p², matched-pair benchmark design (CFP-FF vs CFP-FP sharing 500 identities; LFW vs XQLFW sharing identities), probit-transform for cross-baseline AUC comparison, and Wilcoxon signed-rank testing of fusion gains. The honesty that "the measured separability is a lower bound on the membership information available from embeddings" is a clear, non-overclaiming statement.
- **Reproducibility.** Most inputs are public and standard (WebFace4M, ArcFace/CosFace/MagFace heads, IResNet backbones, InsightFace alignment, nine standard benchmarks). However, the held-out WebFace4M split uses a "fixed seed" that is not released, the per-identity random partitions are not published, and training 180 models is computationally expensive; without released splits/checkpoints, exact numerical reproduction would be difficult.
- **Significance.** The central actionable result — that cross-domain non-member benchmarks inflate apparent membership signal by ~+0.22–0.26 AUC and that privacy audits should use same-domain held-out references — is a meaningful methodological contribution that future MIA evaluations should adopt.
- **Red flags / overclaiming.** No major overclaiming, but two concerns. (1) *Single training source.* All experiments use WebFace4M only; the authors acknowledge this, and given that comparably large datasets are not publicly available or have been retracted (e.g. MS-Celeb-1M), it is understandable but it limits the generalisability of absolute AUC numbers. (2) *Missing defence ablation.* DP-SGD and knowledge distillation are explicitly listed as out-of-scope future work, so the paper cannot say whether the geometric signal survives common privacy defences — a notable gap for a paper framed around GDPR-style auditing. (3) *Uncontrolled probe count k* varies across benchmarks and is acknowledged to affect the cluster statistics non-uniformly, which complicates cross-benchmark AUC comparisons even within the same model. (4) The fusion MLP is trained on the same model's outputs and evaluated via 5-fold identity CV — but no shadow-model or transferability evaluation is provided, so the practical attack utility of the fused signal against unseen target models is not established.

## Strengths

- Genuinely large-scale and systematic: 180 fully-crossed configurations plus a ViT-T control, far exceeding prior work.
- Honest framing as a measurement study rather than a stronger attack, with explicit lower-bound caveats.
- Same-domain held-out reference is a clean methodological device that quantifies how much prior benchmark-based membership AUCs were inflated by domain shift.
- Standard statistical machinery (ANOVA, partial η̂p², Cohen's d′, Kruskal–Wallis ϵ², Wilcoxon signed-rank) backs every claim, including the negative result that backbone/loss head matter little.
- Practical, deployable guidance for privacy auditors: train on more identities, use same-domain references, and expect inflated AUCs on cross-pose/cross-quality benchmarks.

## Limitations

- Single training data source (WebFace4M); absolute AUC numbers may not generalise to other training corpora.
- No evaluation of defences (DP-SGD, knowledge distillation, unlearning), so the practical mitigation story is incomplete.
- Probe-image count k per identity is not controlled across benchmarks, which affects per-statistic estimates non-uniformly.
- The four statistics capture only cluster geometry; richer models operating on raw embedding vectors could extract more, so all reported AUCs are lower bounds.
- Probit-scale AUC translation assumes approximately Gaussian member/non-member distributions with stable variances — an approximation whose violations are not diagnosed.
- Fusion is validated in an in-distribution CV setup; no shadow-model or cross-model transferability evaluation is provided, so real-world attack utility is not established.

## Relevance

本论文系统量化了人脸识别嵌入几何中的训练成员信息泄漏，对于关注隐私审计、成员推断攻击、GDPR 合规以及人脸识别系统安全性的研究方向高度相关；其方法论贡献（同域留出参考集与跨域混淆分离）对所有在超球面度量空间上训练的表示学习模型（包括对比学习、行人重识别）的成员隐私评估都有借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the geometric membership signal survive DP-SGD or knowledge-distillation-based defences, and at what verification-EER cost?
- Does the nids-dominated trend hold for non-CNN and non-WebFace training corpora (e.g. ViT/Swin backbones trained on Glide/CASIA/Glint360K), given that no comparably large public dataset remains?
- Can a learned fusion classifier transfer across target models (shadow-model paradigm), and how does cross-model transferability degrade with nids?
- Is there a closed-form or learned statistic operating on raw embedding vectors (not just cluster geometry) that recovers substantially more membership information, and does the same-domain AUC still drop to ~0.71?
- Are the per-benchmark demographic and age confounds (Kruskal–Wallis ϵ² = 0.123 for ProtoCE on RFW) large enough to bias fairness audits that use membership AUC as a privacy indicator?
- Can a partial-membership / soft-membership formulation (rather than a binary threshold) extract additional information from the cluster-statistic distributions?
