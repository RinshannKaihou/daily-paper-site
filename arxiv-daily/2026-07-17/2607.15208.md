> **一句话总结：** 本文将"偏差离域化"(delocalization of bias) 现象从过阻尼 Langevin 推广到未经 Metropolis 校正的 HMC 与欠阻尼 Langevin (BAOAB)，证明在弱交互/稀疏交互或高斯目标下，控制任意 K 维边缘的 W2 偏差只需 O(√K) 步 (带 log d 项)，并引入了基于"多变量阻尼 Chebyshev 多项式"的矩阵多项式传播子框架来处理离散积分器带来的新困难。

> **TL;DR:** This theory paper (no experiments) extends the "delocalization of bias" phenomenon — previously shown only for overdamped Langevin (the authors' CPAM 2026 paper [17]) — to unadjusted HMC with the leap-frog integrator and underdamped Langevin (UL) with the BAOAB integrator. Under Assumption 1.1 (α-strongly-convex, β-smooth potentials) plus either weak interactions (Assumption 1.3) or sparse interactions on a graph (Assumption 1.5), the W_{2,ℓ∞} bias of the invariant distribution π_h versus the target π is O(√(β/α)·h·log(2d)) (weak) or O(h·log(2d)·((β/α)·log(2d))^{(n+1)/2}) (sparse), implying that controlling any K-dimensional marginal needs only O(√K) integration steps up to log d terms — versus the d^{-1/4} step-size scaling that adjusted HMC needs for non-vanishing Metropolis acceptance. The main technical innovation is a matrix-polynomial framework built on newly defined "multivariate damped Chebyshev polynomials" (T̃^η_k, Ũ^η_{k-1}) that characterizes the multi-step (q,p)-phase-space propagators of leap-frog and BAOAB and upgrades known ℓ2 contractions to ℓ∞. The UL result holds for all sufficiently large friction (γ > 2√β), which in the γ→∞ limit yields delocalization of bias for the Leimkuhler–Matthews (LM) overdamped integrator as a corollary. Benchmarks/datasets: none — results are theorems; baselines are prior bias scalings (e.g., h ~ d^{-1/2} for BAOAB in [34], h ~ d^{-1/4} for adjusted HMC in Beskos et al. [6]).

## 核心贡献

- **将偏差离域化推广到 HMC 与欠阻尼 Langevin**：证明在强凸光滑 (Assumption 1.1) + 弱交互 (Assumption 1.3) 假设下，未校正 HMC (leap-frog, m h = 1/√(20β), C^(O)=1/50) 和未校正 UL (BAOAB, h ≤ 2(1-η)/√β, C^(O)=1/20) 的不变分布偏差满足 W_{2,ℓ∞}(π_h, π) = O(√(β/α)·h·log(2d))。
- **稀疏交互情形的全新边界**：在图结构稀疏交互 (Assumption 1.5, 邻域增长 s_k ≤ C(k+1)^n) 下给出 W_{2,ℓ∞}(π_h, π) = O(h·log(2d)·((β/α)·log(2d))^{(n+1)/2})；关键含义是控制任意 K 维边缘的 W2 偏差只需 O(√K) 步 (含 log d)，相比过阻尼情形 (h·√(log(2d))) 改善了一个 √(log(2d)) 因子 (从 h√(log d) 到 h log d)。
- **高斯目标的精确刻划 (Example 1.2)**：对 π = N(μ, Σ)，h < 1/√β 时未校正 HMC 的 W_{2,ℓ∞} 偏差 = O(h²·√(β)·log(2d)) = O(h·√(log(2d)))。
- **矩阵多项式传播子框架 (Section 3)**：定义"多变量阻尼 Chebyshev 多项式" T̃^η_k、Ũ^η_{k-1}，其三阶递推 (3.5)/(3.6) 把 leap-frog (η=1) 和 BAOAB (η=e^{-γh}) 的多步 (q,p) 传播子一次性表达为路径依赖的 Hessian 多项式 (Proposition 3.5)；η=1 退化回经典 Chebyshev 多项式 T_k, U_k (Proposition 3.1, 3.2)。
- **从 ℓ2 收缩升级到 ℓ∞ 收缩 (Section 4)**：通过稀疏性 (Lemma C.2: r 个 Hessian 乘积的 (i,j) 支撑在 N_{2r}(i)，每行至多 s_r 个非零项) 将 ℓ2 估计转换为 ℓ∞ 估计 (Proposition 4.5, 4.6)，给出 BAOAB 在多步上加权 ℓ∞ 收缩 |T̃^η_k|_{ℓ∞} ≤ (7√2 + 1)·s_{r_k}·exp(-c(h)·(k-1)/2)。
- **覆盖高摩擦极限并蕴含 LM 积分器的离域化**：UL 结果对 γ > 2√β 全部成立 (与连续时间最优 γ = √(β+α) 在 α≪β 下相差至多因子 2)，γ→∞ 时 BAOAB 退化为过阻尼 LM 积分器，故 LM 也具有离域化性质 (此前未知)。

## Key Contributions

- Extends "delocalization of bias" from the authors' earlier overdamped-Langevin result (CPAM 2026, ref [17]) to the two unadjusted samplers most used in practice — leap-frog HMC and BAOAB underdamped Langevin — overcoming the new difficulty that dynamics live on the full (q,p) phase space rather than on positions alone.
- Two sharp bias bounds under strong log-concavity/smoothness (Assumption 1.1): (i) weak-interaction Theorem 1.4 giving W_{2,ℓ∞}(π_h,π) = O(√(β/α)·h·log(2d)) for both samplers; (ii) sparse-interaction Theorem 1.6 giving O(h·log(2d)·((β/α)·log(2d))^{(n+1)/2}). The key operational takeaway: O(√K) integration steps suffice to control any K-marginal up to log d.
- A matrix-polynomial representation of phase-space propagators (Section 3) based on a new family of "multivariate damped Chebyshev polynomials" (Definition 3.4) governed by the three-term recurrence T̃^η_{k+1} = (1+η)·M_{k+1}·T̃^η_k − η·T̃^η_{k-1}, which simultaneously encodes leap-frog (η=1) and BAOAB (η=e^{-γh}) and degenerates to classical T_k, U_k for Gaussian targets (Propositions 3.1–3.3).
- A bridge from existing ℓ2 contraction theory to ℓ∞ bounds: under weak interactions, propagators are near-diagonal so contraction survives in ℓ∞ (Propositions 4.3, 4.4); under sparse interactions, the Hessian-product support condition (Lemma C.2: r-fold products live on N_{2r}(i)) lets a Cauchy–Schwarz split convert ℓ2 to ℓ∞ with the sparsity factor s_r as the only multiplicative loss (Propositions 4.5, 4.6).
- Practical consequence for friction tuning: the UL bias bound holds for every friction γ > 2√β (matching, up to a factor of 2, the sharp contraction threshold γ > √(β−α) of [40]), and the γ→∞ limit recovers the Leimkuhler–Matthews overdamped integrator as a corollary — establishing delocalization of bias for LM.

## Methodology

The authors prove non-asymptotic bias bounds via a synchronous-coupling argument in the W_{2,ℓ∞} metric introduced in [17] (Equation 1.6; for any K coordinates, W2(μ^(K), ν^(K)) ≤ √K·W_{2,ℓ∞}(μ,ν)). The argument has three ingredients:

1. **Coupling framework (Section 2).** Two chains share momentum refreshments (HMC) or O-step Gaussian noise (BAOAB); the exact chain Y is at stationarity π⊗N(0,I), the approximate chain X uses leap-frog / BAOAB. The position gap is split into a contraction term (a) and a discretization-error term (b) (Equations 2.4, 2.5, 2.12). For weak interactions contraction holds over one outer loop (Proposition 2.1); for sparse interactions it holds over N ~ (β/α)·log d outer loops via multi-step coupling (Proposition 2.2).

2. **Matrix-polynomial propagator analysis (Section 3).** The propagators governing both contraction and discretization-error propagation are expressed as the multivariate damped Chebyshev polynomials of Definition 3.4, with path-dependent Hessian arguments M_i = I − (h²/2)·H_i (H_i = ∫₀¹ ∇²V(τ x_i + (1−τ) y_i) dτ, Equation 3.7). Proposition 3.5 gives the closed form for arbitrary trajectories in both the leap-frog and BAOAB schemes; Proposition 3.2 gives the multi-loop HMC propagator as a product of these polynomials.

3. **ℓ∞ propagator bounds and discretization error (Sections 4–5).** ℓ2 bounds adapt [11, Lemma 19] for HMC (Proposition 4.1) and [34, Theorem 5.1] for the weighted-norm BAOAB scheme (Proposition 4.2). The ℓ∞ bounds are the new contribution: weak interactions make the propagators near-diagonal so ℓ∞ ≲ ℓ2 (Propositions 4.3, 4.4); sparse interactions exploit that r-fold Hessian products are supported on N_{2r}(i) (Lemma C.2) so the ℓ∞/ℓ2 ratio is bounded by √s_r (Propositions 4.5, 4.6). Single-step discretization error is bounded in Propositions 5.1 (HMC, position error O((1/2)·h³·√(C3·log(2d)))) and 5.2 (UL, weighted error growing as exp(l·h·(3C5/(2√β) + C6/(16β^{3/2}) + β)) with E_l ~ (27/64)·C5·h³·l + …). Section 5.3 assembles the iterative inequalities and takes k→∞ to yield the invariant-distribution bias bounds of Theorems 1.4 and 1.6.

The framework is explicitly generalized to other splitting integrators (OBABO, ABOBA), since their core block satisfies a similar three-term recurrence with only bounded boundary prefactors.

## Results & Highlights

The paper reports no experimental results — all contributions are theorems. Concrete quantitative claims, with baseline comparisons:

- **Gaussian target, HMC (Example 1.2):** W_{2,ℓ∞}(π_h, π) = O(h²·√β·log(2d)) = O(h·√(log(2d))) for h < 1/√β. This is dimension-independent up to log d, versus the canonical adjusted-HMC step-size requirement h ~ d^{-1/4} for non-vanishing Metropolis acceptance (Beskos et al. [6], ref [45] for adjusted UL), which forces h to vanish polynomially with the full dimension d.

- **Weak-interaction target, HMC and UL (Theorem 1.4):** W_{2,ℓ∞}(π_h, π) = O(√(β/α)·h·log(2d)) with HMC parameters mh = 1/√(20β), C^(O) = 1/50, and UL parameters h ≤ 2(1−η)/√β, C^(O) = 1/20. By W2(μ^(K),ν^(K)) ≤ √K·W_{2,ℓ∞}, controlling any K-dimensional marginal needs only O(√K) leap-frog / BAOAB steps (up to log d).

- **Sparse-interaction target (Theorem 1.6):** W_{2,ℓ∞}(π_h, π) = O(h·log(2d)·((β/α)·log(2d))^{(n+1)/2}) under the graph-sparsity condition s_k ≤ C(k+1)^n (Assumption 1.5). The authors explicitly note an improvement over their prior overdamped-Langevin result [17] — from h·√(log(2d)) to h·log(2d) — and alignment with the BAOAB Wasserstein-2 bias scaling h·√d of [34].

- **HMC contraction rate (Proposition 2.1):** W_{2,ℓ∞}(ρ_{k+1}, π) ≤ (1 − α/(400β))·W_{2,ℓ∞}(ρ_k, π) + O(h·√(log(2d))) for mh = 1/√(20β) under weak interactions with C^(O) = 1/50.

- **Sparse HMC multi-step contraction (Proposition 2.2):** W_{2,ℓ∞}(ρ_{k+N}, π) ≤ (1 − α/(200β))^{N√d}·W_{2,ℓ∞}(ρ_k, π) + O(∑_{i=0}^{N−1}√s_{r_i}·h·√(2β·log(2d))), with N ~ (β/α)·log d outer loops needed to see ℓ∞ contraction.

- **Friction / step-size conditions:** UL requires h ≤ 2(1−η)/√β (equivalently γ > 2√β), matching the sharp contraction threshold γ > √(β−α) of [40] up to a factor of 2 in the ill-conditioned regime α ≪ β, and covering the full high-friction regime γ → ∞.

- **LM integrator corollary:** in the γ→∞ limit BAOAB reduces to the Leimkuhler–Matthews overdamped integrator, so the same delocalization-of-bias bound applies to LM — a new result not contained in [17].

No quantitative results on real datasets, runtimes, or empirical bias measurements are reported.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely novel extension. The leap from positions-only overdamped Langevin to the (q,p)-phase-space propagators of HMC/BAOAB is non-trivial: the authors cannot reuse the single-step contraction argument of [17] because phase-space propagators mix position and momentum differences, and ℓ∞ is not amenable to the continuous-time differentiation used in [19, 37]. The multivariate damped Chebyshev polynomial framework (Definition 3.4) is the conceptual centerpiece and is broadly reusable — the authors explicitly note applicability to OBABO and ABOBA splittings.
- **Rigor:** High. Every claim in the main text (Theorems 1.4/1.6, Propositions 2.1/2.2/3.5/4.1–4.6/5.1/5.2) is backed by an appendix proof. The sparse-interaction analysis via Lemma C.2 (r-fold Hessian products supported on N_{2r}(i)) is a clean argument for the ℓ2 → ℓ∞ bridge.
- **Reproducibility:** This is a theory paper; reproducibility means reconstructing the proofs, not running code. The authors do not release code or simulation notebooks — which is consistent with the genre, but means the practical claim ("O(√K) steps suffice") is not numerically validated here despite the literature's ample numerical evidence (cited refs [15, 46]) for dimension-free step sizes.
- **Significance:** The result is consequential for two communities: (i) Bayesian statisticians using Stan/PyMC, who conventionally apply MH-adjusted HMC and pay a d^{-1/4} step-size cost — this paper formalizes when skipping adjustment is safe for low-dimensional marginals; (ii) molecular-dynamics practitioners using BAOAB/Andersen thermostats at fixed femtosecond step sizes, who can now interpret marginal accuracy as dimension-free.
- **Red flags / honest caveats:**
  - *No numerical experiments.* The central operational claim — that O(√K) steps give a bounded marginal error in practice — is established only asymptotically; constants are not tight, and there is no worked example showing the bounds are non-vacuous at realistic d (e.g., d=10⁶ for an MD system).
  - *Restrictive assumptions.* The potential must be α-strongly-convex and β-smooth (Assumption 1.1) — excluding the non-log-concave posteriors common in modern Bayesian deep learning. Weak interactions require ‖∇²V^(O)‖_{ℓ∞} ≤ C^(O)·α with C^(O) = 1/50 (HMC) or 1/20 (UL) — quite stringent; sparse interactions require polynomial neighborhood growth s_k ≤ C(k+1)^n, excluding the long-range decaying interactions typical of physical systems. The authors flag both as future work, but a reader should not assume the result applies to their target without checking.
  - *Friction restriction.* The UL result needs γ > 2√β (factor of 2 off the sharp γ > √(β−α) threshold of [40]); underdamped dynamics at lower friction are not covered.
  - *Comparison to [17].* A reader's first question will be "what is genuinely new beyond [17]?" The paper answers this well (matrix-polynomial framework, ℓ2→ℓ∞ upgrade, sparse-interaction improvement from h√(log d) to h·log d), but the framing is incremental on a single line of work.
  - *Missing baseline.* No comparison to the d^{-1/3} Randomized Midpoint Method scaling of [49, 8] in the delocalization setting — that direction is explicitly flagged as out of scope because random integration times break the propagator framework.

## Strengths

- Technically deep and self-contained: the matrix-polynomial framework is a reusable contribution that subsumes Gaussian (Chebyshev) and general (path-dependent Hessian) cases in one construction.
- Operationally useful conclusion: in high dimensions, MH adjustment is provably unnecessary for low-dimensional marginals under weak/sparse interactions — a sharp theoretical statement of a folk wisdom long observed in molecular dynamics.
- Honest about limitations and explicit about which integrators the framework does and does not cover (randomized HMC is named as a non-trivial open case).
- Clean upgrade of the sparse-interaction exponent versus the authors' prior result (from h√(log d) to h·log d), matching the BAOAB W2 bias scaling of [34].
- Corollary that LM (overdamped Leimkuhler–Matthews integrator) exhibits delocalization of bias is a free, clean by-product of the friction-γ analysis.

## Limitations

- Theoretical only: no experiments, no code, no worked numerical example verifying that the constants in O(·) are practical at realistic dimensions.
- Strong-convexity + smoothness (Assumption 1.1) excludes non-log-concave targets, which dominate modern Bayesian ML.
- Interaction assumptions are restrictive: weak interactions need the off-diagonal Hessian bounded by α/50 (HMC) or α/20 (UL); sparse interactions need polynomial neighborhood growth — decaying long-range interactions (as in many physical systems) are not covered.
- Friction regime for UL (γ > 2√β) misses the underdamped low-friction regime; sharpness gap of factor 2 versus [40].
- The propagator framework does not extend to randomized HMC [10, 8] because the per-loop leap-frog count is random; this is explicitly left open.
- No discussion of how to detect, for a given target, whether weak/sparse-interaction assumptions hold — practitioners are left to verify these themselves.

## Relevance

This paper is highly relevant to research on high-dimensional MCMC convergence theory and to the design of unadjusted samplers for Bayesian inference and molecular dynamics — directly addressing when MH adjustment can be safely skipped to avoid the d^{-1/4} step-size bottleneck. For readers interested in scalable sampling, non-asymptotic analysis of HMC/Langevin, or low-dimensional-marginal accuracy in high-dimensional posteriors, it is a natural and important follow-up to the authors' CPAM 2026 work; readers working purely on empirical deep-learning samplers or non-log-concave targets will find the assumptions too restrictive for direct use.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Are the weak-interaction constants (C^(O) = 1/50 for HMC, 1/20 for UL) tight, or can a sharper analysis push them toward the empirical observation that dimension-free step sizes work in much more general settings ([15, 46])?
- Does delocalization of bias extend to non-log-concave targets via reflection-coupling or entropic arguments, perhaps with a different notion of marginal bias?
- Can the multivariate damped Chebyshev framework be adapted to randomized HMC [10, 8] or the Randomized Midpoint Method [49] — both of which currently fall outside the scope of this analysis?
- For molecular-dynamics targets with long-range Coulomb/Lennard-Jones interactions (decaying but non-sparse), what is the correct analogue of the s_k sparsity parameter, and what bias scaling does it imply?
- The paper proves delocalization for the Leimkuhler–Matthews integrator in the γ→∞ limit; does the same hold for other weak-second-order overdamped integrators, and can the LM result be obtained directly without passing through the UL analysis?
- Is there a numerical study (even on Gaussian mixtures or Ising-like sparse-interaction targets) showing that the O(√K)-steps rule gives competitive marginal accuracy against adjusted HMC at fixed compute?
