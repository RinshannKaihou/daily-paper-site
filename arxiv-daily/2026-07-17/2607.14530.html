01Methodology to results ↑

Diagnose two bottlenecks in mHC, then fix each with a structurally decoupled design.

HC generalizes one Transformer residual stream into N parallel streams governed by per-layer mappings: Hpre (aggregate), Hpost (distribute), Hres (mix). mHC stabilizes this via the Birkhoff polytope + Sinkhorn–Knopp — but stops at N=4.

On a 2.5B MoE sweep, mHC N=4→16 cuts loss by only 0.006 for +32% FLOPs. The authors attribute this to two compounding bottlenecks:

  • Information bottleneck. A single layer-output vector is the only write-back shared across all N streams — extra streams become redundant.
  • Cost bottleneck. Input-dependent Hres predicts N² coefficients from an NC state, costing O(N³C).

xHC introduces two coordinated fixes:

  • Temporal feature augmentation. r=3 causal depthwise-1D convs (kernels {4,8,12}) enrich the write-back basis to Kr=4 components, followed by modified Gram–Schmidt orthogonalization. Applied only after MLP — adding it after attention destabilizes training. ~24C extra params per MLP sublayer.
  • Sparse residual-stream architecture. A sigmoid router reads the full N-stream state but updates only k=4 active streams (m=2 fixed + TopK=2 routed). Read stays dense — every layer aggregates all N streams — so cross-layer flow is never broken. Cuts dominant cost from O(N³C) to O(k³C).

For deployment, xHC-Flash shares one routing decision across the Attention+MLP block, removes residual mixing on Attention, and uses fused Triton kernels. xHC-Flash-4sub extends sharing across a two-block group.

02Results ↑ method

Consistent gains across two scales (18B, 28B) and two optimizers (AdamW, Muon).

  • 18B MoE. 12-benchmark mean: vanilla 40.6 → mHC 44.8 → xHC 48.8; loss 1.758 vs 1.776 vs 1.799 at ~+4.1% FLOPs.
  • Per-benchmark (xHC − mHC, 18B): HumanEval +6.1, ARC-C +5.9, BBH +5.8, C3 +5.6, GSM8K +3.9, MMLU +2.5.
  • Scaling laws (4 compute points, 1.7e19–4.0e20 FLOPs). vanilla needs 1.50× and mHC 1.19× xHC's compute for equal fitted loss.
  • Expansion sweep (2.5B). xHC N=4→16: −0.012 loss for +4% FLOPs; mHC: −0.006 for +32%. Twice the gain at one-eighth the marginal cost.
  • Muon compatibility (18B). Muon baseline 43.1 → Muon+xHC 49.9 (+6.8) — not AdamW-specific.
  • xHC-Flash (10B). per-sublayer traffic 73.5C → 51C → 40C (4sub) vs mHC@N=4 34C; val loss unchanged (1.983/1.983/1.984 vs mHC 2.004).

03Strengths to limits ↓

A clean two-bottleneck diagnosis, executed with transparent accounting.

  • Diagnosis is the real contribution. Framing HC saturation as joint information-supply + cubic-cost problem, then decoupling the fixes, is well-motivated and ablation-backed. The Figure-5 ablation where the gain grows with N is exactly the predicted pattern.
  • Sparse-on-residual-stream is a genuinely new sparsity axis — distinct from MoE's expert dimension or sparse attention's token dimension. Dense read + sparse write is elegant; fixed-plus-routed + sigmoid are individually ablated.
  • Honest efficiency accounting. Per-operation memory-traffic decomposition (Table 4), wall-clock numbers, and explicit acknowledgement that their mHC reimplementation overhead (15%) differs from the original paper's (6.7%).
  • Muon compatibility is a real robustness check, not an afterthought — the practical GS-removal + selective-Muon rule shows engineering judgment.
  • Consistent across two scales and two optimizers, reducing the chance the gain is a single-config artifact.

04Limitations ↑ strengths

Under-trained checkpoints, a four-point scaling fit, and a missing head-to-head.

Confounds

  • No comparison vs spending the +3–4% FLOPs on more experts, depth, or width.
  • Absolute throughput rests on authors' own mHC reimplementation (15% vs original 6.7%).

Scale of evidence

  • Models look under-trained (18B MMLU ~57%, HumanEval ~29%).
  • N>4 design space (k, fixed-stream ratio m, router) only swept at 2.5B–10B, not 18B/28B.

Scope

  • No long-context, SFT, instruction-tuning, or RLHF evaluation.
  • Only lm-eval-harness-style multiple-choice / code benchmarks.

Statistical fragility

  • 1.50×/1.19× scaling multipliers from a 4-point fit with fixed irreducible loss E=0.72.
  • Proprietary training data — downstream numbers not independently reproducible.

Open questions: would +4% experts beat xHC? do gains survive mature, compute-optimal training? how does the N-stream state interact with 32K–128K context?
