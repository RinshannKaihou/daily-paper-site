Why this matters

A trustworthy-agent design — knowledge base, skill library, auditable Graph of Trace, and a fabrication-checking Auditor — for an error-sensitive science; a reusable baseline for general scientific agents.

04 · critical edge

Critical edge

Headline ALE & BrainPilotBench results are one unrepeated run per task and configuration; deltas as small as 0.04–0.06 between harnesses cannot be distinguished from noise without repeats and confidence intervals.

single-run · stochastic

Methodology

PI-led orchestration, grounded in curated assets

05 / architecture

The PI is the single user-facing entry point: it decomposes the goal, delegates subtasks, and synthesizes an audited deliverable. A skill-router exposes modular skills on demand — agents fetch candidate descriptions by keyword and load full instructions only when a capability is needed.

Knowledge is built offline: textbooks & papers are OCR'd, chunked into 1,500-char overlapping passages, embedded with BGE-m3 (1,024-dim), and retrieved via vector similarity plus a BGE cross-encoder reranker. Skills are distilled through three pipelines — LLM-authored, paper-to-skill, and repo-to-skill tool derivation.

Results & Highlights

Strong on deterministic deliverables

06 / benchmarks
  • ALE (3 brain tasks). 8–63% of the time and 5–56% of the cost of Codex / Claude Code across matched backbones. T2 scores identical across configs; full credit (1.00) with GPT-5.5 + knowledge.
  • BrainPilotBench-v0 (4 tasks). Tied highest RSC place-cell (1.00), highest BCI IV 2a (0.20), beat matched Claude Code on TOPS-fMRI (0.47 vs 0.41); total $0.504 vs $4.931 (Codex + GPT-5.5).
  • RSC case study. Bayesian position decoder: MAE 16.8 cm on a 90 cm track, position correlation r = 0.646.
  • fMRI tonic-pain. Held-out pooled Pearson r = 0.579 (p = 2.76e-10); chronic-back-pain AUC 0.793 (JP) / 0.699 (UK).
  • BCI IV 2a. EEGNet+TCN (28.7k params): accuracy 0.580 → 0.620, κ 0.440 → 0.493 — a positive trend, not significant (p = 0.107).
  • Sleep-EDF (20-fold LOSO). 237,813-param model: 0.794 acc, 0.725 macro-F1 — above priors, slightly below published DeepSleepNet.

Critical Assessment

Strengths

08
  • Genuinely domain-grounded. 7,233-item KB + 72-skill library with real tool derivations (DeepLabCut, MNE-Python, fMRIPrep, SpikeInterface, pycortex, netneurotools).
  • GoT + Auditor directly target fabrication, drift, and missing intervention points — a defensible design for trustworthy scientific agents.
  • Transparent on negatives: knowledge hurting 14/26 cells, non-significant case stats, the vision-hierarchy citation error, and T3 unreliability are all disclosed.
  • Open system + benchmark with engineered scoring: artifact contracts, hash sealing, no-network containers, two-sided Oracle validation.
  • Cost-efficiency holds where it matters: $0.08 vs $22.53 for matched ALE deliverables is operationally meaningful for budget-constrained labs.

Limitations

09
  • Single-run evaluations on stochastic reasoning models; case-study metrics mostly miss significance; no repeated-measure CIs.
  • Open-ended tasks fail. C. elegans neuron tracking (T3): most configs scored ≤ 0.18 or failed outright.
  • Breadth is thin: only 3 ALE tasks and 4 BrainPilotBench-v0 tasks — authors call for community contribution.
  • Domain knowledge is non-monotone: enabling the KB/skills hurts in 14 of 26 valid cells, with no diagnosis of why.
  • No modeling SOTA. Sleep-EDF and BCI models sit below published references and aren't statistically better than the EEGNet baseline.
  • Auditor scope: checks claim-vs-session-evidence only; statistical validity and scientific interpretation stay human.
