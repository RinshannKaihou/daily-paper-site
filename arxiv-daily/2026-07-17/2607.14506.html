◆ Critical Edge

Experiments are at 4B parameters; the compression budget the bounds require has not been shown achievable at 70B–400B production scale without sacrificing empirical reward.

§ 03Methodology

M.1 How the bound is made tractable

The RLVR objective R(h) = E[r(x,y)] has a generation space combinatorial in sequence length × vocabulary. The authors apply the Gumbel-max theorem to write each token as y_t = argmax_v (τ⁻¹ ℓ + ξ) with hypothesis-independent Gumbel noise ξ. Generation becomes a deterministic function y = g(x, ξ; h) — so all randomness (prompt distribution P₀ and noise P_ξ) is explicit and out of the hypothesis.

Theorem 1 (δ = 0.05) then splits the generalization gap:

R(h) − R̂(h) = ε_ext (prompt) + ε_int (generation)

The intrinsic term is O(1/√(mn)) and shrinks by taking n = Ω(m) at eval time — leaving the bound governed by the extrinsic error and the compression penalty on description length C(h).

M.2 Progressive RLVR pipeline

  • 1 · Train big. Standard-LoRA (rank 64) RLVR teacher, GRPO-style importance sampling, no KL penalty.
  • 2 · Distill tiny. On-policy distillation into a TinyLoRA student (u=16, r=16, compressible to a single scalar) — +10% training reward vs. direct TinyLoRA-RLVR.
  • 3 · Quantize hard. Adapter quantized to k∈{2..64} levels (k=5 ≈ 2.3 bits), KT arithmetic-coded.

The trick: PAC-Bayes charges only the final hypothesis, not the search — high capacity during search, tiny description length in the bound.

§ 04Judgement

+ Strengths

  • First principled, non-vacuous theory for the dominant current post-training paradigm (RLVR) — closes a real gap left open by Lotfi et al. (pre-training only).
  • The Gumbel-max reparameterization cleanly converts a combinatorial-support RL objective into a form compatible with existing compression PAC-Bayes machinery — likely reused.
  • Progressive RLVR is practical and explainable: train big, distill tiny, quantize hard. 84–97% accuracy at 14,796× compression is useful independent of the theory.
  • Transparent about limits (4B scale, in-distribution only) — no deployment-readiness overclaim.
  • Auditable description-length accounting (Appendix C, Table 3) lets a reader verify the numerics.

− Limitations

  • Scale. Only 4B params; untested at 70B–400B, where the required compression may be unattainable without unacceptable accuracy loss.
  • Distribution match required. In-distribution only; OOD needs labeled target data and transfers asymmetrically (sometimes negative).
  • Domain coverage. Four domains, all with binary-or-binarizable verifiable rewards; no continuous-reward RLHF, no multilingual, no open-ended generation. SQL tri-level reward reduced to binary in the headline figure.
  • Single base model. Only Qwen3.5-4B (+ 2B ablation); no Llama / Mistral / Gemma replication.
  • TinyLoRA dependency. The whole framework's tightness rests on a 5-month-old method (arXiv:2602.04118) whose robustness is not independently characterized here.
  • Fixed eval budget. 4,096 × 64 presented as given; no sensitivity analysis. k∈{2..64} is a 10-way search with only 4 bits of meta-prior.
