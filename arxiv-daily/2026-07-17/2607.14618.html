Methodology

Quantizer ↔ compiler co-design · two halves

Accuracy side · Poly-Precision

Activation-aware channel-wise waterfilling

  • Error proxy (Eq. 2): per channel j, bit b ∈ {2,3,4,8,16}: E_b[j] ≈ ||w̃−w||²·E[x²] from 128 WikiText-2 calibration sequences; all five bits in one pass.
  • Fractional waterfill (Eq. 3–5): start all channels at 2-bit, greedily promote top gain G=(E_b−E_b′)/(b′−b) from a max-heap until avg budget B is spent.
  • ISA quanta snap (Eq. 6–7): round channel counts to backend tile quanta (AVX2 int8 → mult. of 32); error-aware reassignment keeps B_real within ~0.016–0.045 b.
  • Bit-specific scaling (Eq. 8): one exponent α_b per bit/layer, power-law scale grid-searched, foldable into packed weights.

Execution side · Model Compiler

DAG-level permutation propagation

  • Permute & cluster (Eq. 9): factor O=A·W=(A·P_Wᵀ)·(P_W·W); only clustered weight materialized, permutation never stored at runtime.
  • Bit-block dispatch: W{2,3,4}A16 → LUT GEMM/GEMV (T-MAC, vqtbl1q/vpshufb); W{8,16}A16 → OpenBLAS SIMD GEMM.
  • Cross-layer merge (Eq. 10): non-linear activations commute with channel perms; rewrite bases into compile-time weight rewrites.
  • Graph planner: static operator DAG, topo pass labels edges, inserts shuffles only at incompatible fan-out / fan-in / boundaries; merges span >1 Transformer block.

Strengths

What lands

  1. Real gap, cleanly diagnosed: inter-layer methods too coarse, intra-layer methods use CPU-hostile 5–7-bit, execution work doesn't propagate across the DAG.
  2. Principled pipeline: {2,3,4,8,16} palette + waterfilling + quanta-match + error-aware reassignment is modular and well-motivated for real SIMD/LUT tiles.
  3. Most original systems bit: DAG-level permutation planning, including cross-block merges (FFN-out → next-attention-in), goes beyond RPTQ/Atom local fusion.
  4. Honest scoping: acknowledges all methods converge at 5–6 b; differentiation is explicitly the tight-budget regime.
  5. Real three-tier CPU eval with RAPL energy — not a single-chip speedup table.

Limitations

What to probe

  1. Weight-only: activations stay A16/A8/A32 — no activation/KV-cache savings; "End-to-End" title slightly oversells scope.
  2. Modeled, not measured: the 70.8% reorder-traffic cut is a byte count, not cache/DRAM traffic; CPU baseline is essentially AWQ-on-T-MAC.
  3. No Hessian justification: first-order activation-weighted Frobenius proxy lacks a perplexity-vs-proxy ablation; GPTQ-style second-order not compared.
  4. Suspiciously strong number: Llama2-13B PolyQ-3 PPL 4.88 vs typical published AWQ-3 ~5.3 — sanity-check whether the AWQ baseline is under-tuned.
  5. Fragile head-to-head: AMQ unsupported on Falcon-H1-3B; downstream tasks reported as "capped" MC accuracy, no seeds or CIs.
  6. No artifact statement: compiler/graph-planner not confirmed open-sourced; reproducibility hinges on unreleased code.
