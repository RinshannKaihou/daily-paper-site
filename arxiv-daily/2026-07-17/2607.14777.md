> **一句话总结：** SEED 提出一种自演化的在线策略蒸馏框架，让策略模型同时充当"演员"和"分析者"，把已完成的在线轨迹转化为自然语言 hindsight skills，再通过在普通上下文与技能增强上下文下对同一批采样动作重新打分得到密集 token 级蒸馏信号，与 GRPO 联合优化；在 ALFWorld/WebShop/Search-QA 上以 Qwen2.5-3B/7B 与 Qwen3-1.7B 为骨干，相对 GRPO 在 ALFWorld 平均成功率提升 14.9-45.9 个点，且仅用 60% 训练数据即可超过 GRPO 全量数据性能。

> **TL;DR:** SEED turns the LLM policy into its own hindsight analyzer: at each RL update the latest checkpoint both collects trajectories and extracts natural-language "hindsight skills" from them, then re-scores the same sampled actions under ordinary vs. skill-augmented contexts to produce a dense token-level on-policy distillation signal that is jointly optimized with GRPO. On ALFWorld (text-embodied), WebShop (web navigation), and a 7-dataset Search-based QA suite (NQ, TriviaQA, PopQA, HotpotQA, 2WikiMultiHopQA, MuSiQue, Bamboogle), SEED beats GRPO on the ALFWorld macro-average by +14.9 to +45.9 points across Qwen2.5-3B/7B and Qwen3-1.7B backbones, beats the strongest static-distillation baseline (SDAR) on 10 of 12 aggregate comparisons, and with only 60% of training data surpasses full-data GRPO (80.7 vs. 75.0 on ALFWorld).

## 核心贡献

- **自演化的在线策略蒸馏（OPD）框架**：首次让同一个策略模型在每个外层迭代中同时扮演（a）与环境交互的演员和（b）分析已完成轨迹、提取自然语言 hindsight skill 的分析者两个角色；策略更新后两者同步刷新，使监督信号与策略共同演化，避免静态教师/技能库的"陈旧"问题。
- **基于技能引发概率位移的密集 token 级蒸馏机制**：对完全相同的采样动作 token，分别用普通上下文（学生）和技能增强上下文（教师）进行重打分，将二者的对数概率差通过 sigmoid 门控映射为置信度门，构造出密集、决策级的 OPD 损失，仅在学生分支流梯度。该损失与基于结果的 GRPO 目标联合优化，弥补了轨迹级稀疏奖励与 token 级策略学习之间的监督鸿沟。
- **推理时零额外开销**：hindsight skills 仅在训练阶段作为"特权监督"出现，部署时移除分析者，无需外部记忆库或额外的技能提示——把行为指导真正"参数化"进了策略。
- **配套的理论分析**：在附录中给出三条命题，分别证明 (i) 在线 hindsight 产生与当前策略访问分布匹配的自适应目标；(ii) 即使所有组内轨迹结果相同（奖励"打平"），OPD 梯度仍非零，提供 token 级差异化信用分配；(iii) 同步刷新分析者可控制辅助梯度的"陈旧度"，给出 β_opd/4-Lipschitz 界。
- **跨三个领域、三个骨干模型的系统实证**：覆盖文本具身交互（ALFWorld）、Web 导航（WebShop）、检索增强问答（7 个 QA 数据集），在 10/12 个聚合对比上取得最佳或并列最佳，并展示明显的样本效率与跨域泛化优势。

## Key Contributions

- **A self-evolving on-policy distillation (OPD) framework** in which the latest policy checkpoint serves simultaneously as the rollout actor and the trajectory analyzer; refreshing the shared checkpoint every outer iteration lets decision-making and hindsight supervision co-evolve, addressing the staleness of fixed teachers or one-shot skill datasets.
- **A skill-induced log-probability-shift distillation mechanism** that re-scores the *same* sampled action tokens under ordinary (student) and skill-augmented (teacher) contexts, maps the detached difference through a sigmoid gate, and turns it into a dense token-level auxiliary loss optimized jointly with GRPO. Gradients flow only through the student branch, so the skill is internalized rather than supplied at inference.
- **Zero inference-time overhead**: hindsight skills act as privileged supervision during training only; at deployment the analyzer is removed, requiring no external memory, retrieved skills, or extra prompts.
- **Theory matching the three design requirements** (on-policy, dense, self-evolving): Proposition 1 shows the OPD gradient is a Monte-Carlo estimate toward a skill-reweighted target on the current policy's own occupancy, with a TV/ Pinsker mismatch bound for stale data; Proposition 2 shows OPD remains non-degenerate (gradient norm equals `Var_{π_k}[w_k(c,v)]`) when group outcomes are tied and the GRPO reward term vanishes; Proposition 3 bounds analyzer-staleness by `β_opd·G/4 · E|Δ_{k,k} − Δ_{k,j}|`, exactly zeroed at rollout when the analyzer is synchronized with the policy.
- **Comprehensive empirical validation** across three long-horizon domains (text-embodied ALFWorld, web-navigation WebShop, search-augmented QA built on the Search-R1 protocol over NQ/TriviaQA/PopQA/HotpotQA/2WikiMultiHopQA/MuSiQue/Bamboogle) and three backbones (Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, Qwen3-1.7B-Instruct), with code released at `github.com/jinyangwu/SEED`.

## Methodology

SEED is a two-stage training recipe layered on top of standard outcome-based RL for multi-turn LLM agents.

**Problem setup.** Long-horizon agentic tasks are cast as POMDPs `(S, A, O, T, Ω, R, γ)` where the policy `a_t ~ π_θ(·|h_t)` emits textual/executable actions from the interaction history. The environment returns a single sparse trajectory-level reward `R(τ)`. The mismatch between trajectory-level outcomes and token-level policy updates is the central problem.

**Stage 1 — Hindsight-skill SFT.** For each task the base policy generates `K0` rollouts; an *external* analyzer (instantiated with GLM-5.2, temperature 0.0, max 4096 tokens) reads each completed trajectory and writes a natural-language hindsight skill `s_τ` capturing reusable workflows (success) or failure-avoidance rules (failure). Only validly-formatted skills are retained, and the base model is SFT-ed to predict the skill from the serialized trajectory. This equips a single checkpoint with the trajectory-analysis capability it will need in Stage 2; the same checkpoint then initializes both the RL actor and the analyzer.

**Stage 2 — Self-evolving OPD.** Each outer iteration freezes the current policy as `π_θ_old`. This snapshot (i) collects a group of `N=8` trajectories per task and (ii) generates one hindsight skill per completed trajectory via the analyzer role `A_θ_old`. Then, *keeping the original sampled actions fixed*, the trainable policy `π_θ` computes two log-probabilities on the same tokens:
- teacher branch: `ℓ^skill = log π_θ(a | H(h, s))` under skill-augmented context,
- student branch: `ℓ^θ = log π_θ(a | h)` under ordinary history.

The detached shift `Δ = sg[ℓ^skill − ℓ^θ]` is passed through a sigmoid gate `g = σ(β_opd·Δ)` (β_opd=5.0). The OPD loss is `L_opd = E[m · g · (sg[ℓ^skill] − ℓ^θ)]`, which is gradient-equivalent to a gate-weighted NLL — promoting tokens whose hindsight skill endorses them. It is combined with the standard group-relative clipped GRPO loss (with KL regularization to `π_ref`) as `L_SEED = L_rl + λ_opd·L_opd` (λ_opd=0.01). After each update the optimized policy becomes the next snapshot, so both the experience distribution and the analyzer capability evolve together. The design builds explicitly on SDAR (Lu et al., 2026a) for the gating formulation.

**Training cost.** 8× NVIDIA A800 80GB GPUs; 150 training steps; batch size 16 (ALFWorld/WebShop) or 128 (Search); learning rate 1e-6; PPO clip 0.2; KL coef 0.01; max prompt 2048 (ALFWorld) / 4096 (WebShop, Search); response 512 tokens; max interaction steps 30/15/4 respectively.

## Results & Highlights

- **ALFWorld (140-task seen split), success rate.** With Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, SEED reaches an ALFWorld macro-average of **91.8%**, versus GRPO **75.0%** (+16.8 pts), Skill-GRPO **60.2%** (+31.6), Skill-GRPO* **80.5%** (+11.3), and the strongest static-distillation baseline SDAR **84.4%** (+7.4). SEED saturates Pick/Look/Clean/Heat at 100.0/100.0/100.0/100.0.
- **ALFWorld cross-backbone deltas.** SEED improves the ALFWorld average over GRPO by **+14.9 to +45.9 points** across Qwen2.5-3B, Qwen2.5-7B (96.1 vs 81.2, +14.9), and Qwen3-1.7B (92.0 vs 46.1, +45.9), with the largest gain on the smallest/most-instruct-weak model.
- **WebShop (128 test episodes).** With Qwen2.5-3B, SEED achieves a task-completion **Score of 88.5** and **Success rate 78.9%**, versus GRPO 79.8 / 63.3 (+8.7 score, +15.6 succ) and SDAR 85.0 / 68.0 (+3.5 / +10.9). Caveat: on Qwen2.5-7B SEED's Success of 78.1% is *below* SDAR's 82.8%, the one place SEED concedes.
- **Search-based QA (51,713 questions across 7 datasets).** With Qwen2.5-3B, SEED reaches Search-Avg **45.7%**, beating GRPO **36.4%** (+9.3), GRPO+OPSD **44.6%** (+1.1), and SDAR **43.4%** (+2.3). On Qwen2.5-7B, Search-Avg is 48.6, the best of all reported methods on that backbone.
- **Sample efficiency (ALFWorld).** Using only **60% of the training data, SEED scores 80.7, exceeding the 75.0 of full-data GRPO**; with 40% of data SEED (58.9) matches GRPO trained on 80% (58.6). Gains over GRPO range 13.4–30.2 points across data fractions.
- **Cross-domain generalization (ALFWorld unseen, 134 tasks).** SEED raises the macro-average from 70.9 (GRPO) to **86.2 (+15.3)**, with per-task-family gains of +35.0 (Heat), +18.3 (Look), +16.5 (Pick), +13.5 (Cool), +11.3 (Pick2), and only −2.9 (Clean).
- **Training dynamics.** By training step 40 SEED already reaches ~57% success while GRPO is near 35%; SEED also reduces mean episode length from ~28 turns to 13 (vs ~16 for GRPO), indicating more direct, goal-directed execution.
- **Ablations (Qwen2.5-3B, ALFWorld avg).** Removing Hindsight-Skill SFT → **86.0 (−5.8)**; removing Self-Evolving OPD → **87.0 (−4.8)**; replacing on-policy skills with a static offline library (the largest degradation) → **84.4 (−7.4)**, evidencing that deriving skills from the *current* policy's own trajectories is the most important design choice.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — moderate, incremental over SDAR.** The OPD loss is explicitly described as "Following SDAR (Lu et al., 2026a)," and the core machinery — re-scoring the same on-policy tokens under ordinary vs. privileged contexts and applying a sigmoid-gated distillation loss — is inherited. The genuinely new ingredient is *using the policy's own freshly-extracted hindsight skills as the privileged context and synchronizing the analyzer with the actor*, plus the three matching theory propositions. This is a clean and well-motivated delta, but it is a delta rather than a new paradigm.
- **Rigor — strong.** Three propositions tie directly to the three design requirements (on-policy occupancy match, dense credit under tied rewards with the exact variance identity `Var_{π_k}[w_k(c,v)]`, analyzer-staleness bound `β_opd·G/4`). The authors are admirably explicit that the theorems establish *informativeness, not correctness* — benefit still depends on the empirical assumption that generated skills positively correlate with action value (Eq. 8). Ablations isolate each component and the largest ablation effect (−7.4) correctly identifies on-policy skill generation as the most important factor.
- **Abstract-vs-paper overclaim on "vision-based agentic tasks".** The abstract states "Extensive experiments on text-based and vision-based agentic tasks," but the main results table and the datasets section (Table 3) cover only *text-based* benchmarks — ALFWorld (text interface), WebShop (text web), and Search-R1-style QA. No VQA/Sokoban/screen-pixel/VLM-backbone results appear in the body or appendices I read. This is the most concrete red flag: the headline claim of vision-based evaluation is not substantiated by the experiments shown.
- **Specific weak spots.** SEED concedes WebShop success rate on Qwen2.5-7B (78.1 vs SDAR 82.8) and the 3B ALFWorld "Pick2" task remains at 70.6, far below the saturated 100.0 on five other families — yet no per-task failure analysis is offered for these specific gaps. The SFT stage is bootstrapped by GLM-5.2 as the *external* analyzer, so SEED is not fully self-contained; the paper does not measure sensitivity to analyzer choice (e.g., would a smaller or open analyzer work?). Skill quality is only illustrated qualitatively (Table 4, two examples per domain) with no human or automated evaluation of skill correctness, which matters because the theory's value-alignment assumption (Eq. 8) rests on skills being behaviorally informative.
- **Cherry-picking risk — low to moderate.** The evaluation suite is the standard one for this subfield and the 7-dataset Search-QA aggregate is dataset-balanced, mitigating pooling artifacts. However, only three RL-friendly benchmarks are used; harder agentic suites (AgentBench, OdysseyArena, Long-Horizon-Terminal-Bench — all cited) are absent, leaving open how SEED behaves on truly long horizons (>30 steps) where hindsight skills may be harder to write.
- **Reproducibility — high.** Algorithm 1, hyperparameter table (Table 5), prompts, and dataset splits are all provided; the main barrier is compute (8× A800) and reliance on the GLM-5.2 API for SFT data construction.
- **Significance — solid for the agentic-RL subfield.** The "policy as its own hindsight analyzer" framing is a useful, deployable recipe and the consistency of gains across three backbones and three domains is convincing for the text-agent regime.

## Strengths

- Principled, self-contained recipe: one model, two roles, refreshed every iteration; no inference-time skill prompts and no external memory — clean deployment story.
- Strong and consistent empirical gains, including on the smallest/weakest backbone (Qwen3-1.7B: +45.9 ALFWorld avg), which is where dense supervision should matter most.
- Convincing sample-efficiency result (60% data SEED > 100% data GRPO) and meaningful cross-domain generalization to the ALFWorld unseen split (+15.3 macro-average).
- Theory appendix does real work: the tied-reward variance identity (Prop. 2) is an elegant justification for why SEED keeps learning when GRPO's reward gradient is exactly zero, a regime that is common early in sparse-reward training.
- Honest framing of what the theory proves (informativeness) vs. what it assumes (skill-value alignment); explicit caveat that monotonic return improvement is not guaranteed.
- Public code, model, prompts, and full hyperparameter table.

## Limitations

- **Vision-based experiments claimed but not delivered in the paper body.** The abstract advertises "vision-based agentic tasks" yet only text-based benchmarks are reported; this gap should be corrected or the missing experiments added.
- **Dependency on GLM-5.2 as the SFT-time external analyzer.** Sensitivity to analyzer choice/size is not studied; the framework's "self-evolving" property holds only *after* an external model has bootstrapped the analyzer capability.
- **No quantitative evaluation of skill quality.** Table 4 shows illustrative skills, but there is no measurement of how often the analyzer produces behaviorally-misleading hindsight, which the theory explicitly flags as the failure mode.
- **Concedes on some cells.** WebShop-Succ on 7B (78.1 < SDAR 82.8) and ALFWorld Pick2 on 3B (70.6) are underexplained.
- **Backbone coverage is Qwen-only.** No Llama, Mistral, or proprietary-model results; generalization across model families is unverified.
- **Horizon length is modest.** Max interaction steps are 30/15/4 for ALFWorld/WebShop/Search — the regime where hindsight skill writing is easy. Behavior on the cited longer-horizon benchmarks (OdysseyArena, Long-Horizon-Terminal-Bench) is not tested.
- **Compute cost.** 8× A800 80GB plus per-iteration double scoring (ordinary + skill-augmented forward passes) doubles policy-evaluation cost relative to vanilla GRPO; the paper does not report wall-clock or token-throughput comparisons.

## Relevance

对关注 LLM 智能体强化学习与稀疏奖励场景下细粒度信用分配的研究非常有价值：SEED 提供了一个可落地、推理零额外开销的"自蒸馏 + hindsight 技能"方案，与 GRPO/PPO 类训练流水线天然兼容，可作为长程 agent 训练、工具使用和检索增强问答系统的通用增强模块，特别契合关注样本效率与跨域泛化的方向。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How sensitive is SEED to the choice of the *external* SFT analyzer (GLM-5.2)? Would a same-family smaller analyzer, or removing Stage-1 SFT and cold-starting the analyzer from the base policy, retain the gains?
- Can the skill-induced gate `g = σ(β_opd·Δ)` be replaced by an *outcome-conditional* gate that down-weights skills extracted from low-return trajectories, to directly enforce the value-alignment covariance in Proposition 1 / Eq. 8?
- Does the abstract's "vision-based agentic tasks" claim hold up — i.e., how does SEED perform on actual VLM backbones (e.g., Qwen2.5-VL) over Sokoban, screen-navigation, or GUI-agent benchmarks where observation tokens are visual?
- What is the wall-clock and token-throughput overhead of SEED vs. GRPO and vs. SDAR, given that each update requires both an ordinary and a skill-augmented forward pass plus an analyzer decoding pass?
- Does the self-evolving loop exhibit any drift or reward-hacking behavior where late-training skills become vacuous tautologies the policy already satisfies, neutralizing `Var_{π_k}[w_k(c,v)]`?
- On truly long horizons (100+ steps, e.g., Long-Horizon-Terminal-Bench, OdysseyArena), does a single natural-language skill suffice, or does SEED need hierarchical / per-segment skills to keep hindsight informative?
