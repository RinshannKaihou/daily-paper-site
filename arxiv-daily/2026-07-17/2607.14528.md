> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 CRTBench（350 个问题族、1,750 道题），通过对同一逻辑问题做受控改写（逆否命题、双重否定、量词重写、否定翻转、被动语态等）来测试 LLM 的逻辑不变性；实验发现 GPT-5.4-mini 虽有 98.9% 单题准确率但仅有 60.3% 族级一致性，推理增强模型 o4-mini 以 96.9% 一致性领先，且显式推理会让 GPT-5.4 在量词推理上从 92% 崩塌到 20%。

> **TL;DR:** The authors introduce CRTBench, a 350-family (1,750-question) benchmark that tests whether frontier LLMs answer consistently across logically equivalent reformulations (contrapositive, double negation, De Morgan, quantifier rewrite, negation flip, passive voice). They expose a striking accuracy-consistency gap: GPT-5.4-mini hits 98.9% base accuracy but only 60.3% family consistency on CRTBench, while reasoning-optimized o4-mini reaches 96.9% consistency. Enabling reasoning effort lifts GPT-5.4-mini from 60.3% to 85.4% consistency, but GPT-5.4 itself stays flat at 86.6% because quantifier-reasoning consistency collapses from 92.0% to 20.0%, revealing a quantifier-specific failure mode of high-effort reasoning.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 CRTBench 基准**：覆盖 350 个问题族、共 1,750 道题目，横跨七类平衡的逻辑推理类别（命题逻辑、三段论、德摩根、量词推理、多跳推理、嵌套否定、条件谬误），每族含 1 道原题 + 4 道受控改写题。
- **用"逻辑等价改写"取代任意复述**：定义了等价保持（逆否命题、双重否定、德摩根、量词重写、被动语态）、答案翻转（否定翻转）、表层控制（被动语态）三类变换，使失败可被精确归因到具体逻辑算子。
- **揭示"准确率 ≠ 一致性"的鸿沟**：GPT-5.4-mini 在 CRTBench 上单题准确率 98.9%，但族级一致性仅 60.3%（38.6pp 缺口）。
- **针对每个变换与每个类别做归因分析**：证明失败集中在否定密集型变换（GPT-5.4-mini 逆否命题 72.4%、双重否定 84.6%）而非表层改写（被动语态 98.7%）。
- **发现推理增强的差异化效应**：推理让 GPT-5.4-mini 一致性 +25.1pp，但对 GPT-5.4 整体无效，因为量词推理族一致性从 92% 崩到 20%（过度的"无法确定"式弃答）。

## Key Contributions

- **A new benchmark (CRTBench)** of 350 question families (1,750 questions) spanning seven balanced reasoning categories: propositional logic, syllogistic reasoning, De Morgan's laws, quantifier reasoning, multi-hop inference, nested negation, and conditional fallacies.
- **Logically grounded transformations** (contrapositive, double negation, De Morgan, quantifier rewrite, negation flip, passive voice) classified into equivalence-preserving, answer-reversing, and surface-control groups, enabling causal attribution of invariance failures.
- **Strict family-consistency metric** requiring a model to answer the base question and all four reformulations correctly, making the gap with base accuracy directly interpretable.
- **Empirical demonstration of the accuracy-consistency gap** on frontier models (GPT-5.4, GPT-5.4-mini, Claude Sonnet 4, Gemini 2.5 Flash, o4-mini), with reasoning-optimized o4-mini the strongest at 96.9% family consistency.
- **A surprising reasoning-ablation finding**: enabling reasoning lifts GPT-5.4-mini substantially but leaves GPT-5.4 unchanged overall because quantifier-reasoning consistency collapses from 92.0% to 20.0% under high reasoning effort — a quantifier-specific over-abstention failure mode.

## Methodology

CRTBench is built around the **question family** unit: a base question plus exactly four reformulations, each applying a specific logical transformation. Transformations are partitioned into three classes:

- **Equivalence-preserving** (expected answer matches base): contrapositive (P→Q ⇔ ¬Q→¬P), double negation (P ⇔ ¬¬P), De Morgan's laws, quantifier rewrite, passive voice.
- **Answer-reversing** (expected answer is the logical complement; "Cannot be determined" stays unchanged): negation flip.
- **Surface control**: passive voice (tests whether failures come from logic vs. surface rephrasing).

Not every transformation applies to every category: contrapositive applies to 290 families, passive voice to 300, De Morgan to 50, quantifier rewrite to 60, while double negation and negation flip apply to all 350.

**Two metrics** are tracked:
- **Base accuracy** — whether the original question is answered correctly.
- **Family consistency** — whether the base and all four reformulations match their respective labels (one wrong answer ⇒ the family is inconsistent).

**Construction & validation** proceeded in two manual stages: first all 350 base questions were audited for logical correctness, label correctness, and grammatical naturalness; then after reformulation generation, each family was re-audited for transformation fidelity, answer-label correctness, and linguistic naturalness. The authors explicitly note they did not collect formal inter-annotator agreement, so this is treated as internal QC rather than a human-naturalness study.

**Evaluation setup**: zero-shot prompting at temperature T=0 (o4-mini does not support temperature control), restricting answers to "Yes"/"No"/"Cannot be determined," extracted via a regex-first heuristic with an UNCLEAR fallback (only 5 such cases, all from Gemini 2.5 Flash). A chain-of-thought variant was also run for ablation. API calls were made in April 2026 using identifiers `gpt-5.4`, `gpt-5.4-mini`, `claude-sonnet-4-20250514`, `gemini-2.5-flash`, `o4-mini`.

## Results & Highlights

- **Accuracy-consistency gap on CRTBench (n=350 families)**: GPT-5.4-mini achieves **98.9% base accuracy but only 60.3% family consistency** (38.6pp gap); GPT-5.4 reaches 98.9% accuracy vs 86.6% consistency; o4-mini is strongest at **98.6% accuracy and 96.9% family consistency**. With n=350, GPT-5.4-mini's 60.3% carries a 95% CI of ±5.1pp.
- **Transformation-specific failures**: On CRTBench, contrapositive is the hardest transformation for GPT-5.4-mini at **72.4% consistency vs 98.7% for passive voice** — a 26.3pp gap between the weakest logical transformation and surface control, vs only 1.7pp for o4-mini. Double negation sits at 84.6% for GPT-5.4-mini but 98.0% for o4-mini.
- **Reasoning ablation on GPT-5.4-mini**: Enabling reasoning effort=high raises family consistency from **60.3% → 85.4% (+25.1pp)** and double-negation consistency from 84.6% → 92.3%, at the cost of base accuracy dropping 98.9% → 96.0%.
- **Reasoning ablation on GPT-5.4**: Family consistency stays flat at **86.6%** with reasoning on, while base accuracy drops 98.9% → 92.9%; per-category, quantifier-reasoning consistency **collapses from 92.0% → 20.0%**, nested negation improves 50.0% → 94.0%, conditional fallacies improve 84.0% → 98.0%, and propositional logic improves 86.0% → 100.0%.
- **Root cause of quantifier collapse**: With reasoning effort=high, GPT-5.4 emits "Cannot be determined" 119 times on the 250 quantifier questions (vs 5 times without reasoning), **all 119 incorrect**, dropping question-level quantifier accuracy from 98.0% (245/250) to 52.4% (131/250). Removing the quantifier category would move GPT-5.4(r) to (99.7% accuracy, 97.7% consistency).
- **CoT has minimal effect**: Chain-of-thought prompting changes family consistency by +1.4pp for GPT-5.4-mini (60.3→61.7%), +0.0pp for GPT-5.4, and +0.0pp for GPT-5.4(r); none significant at α=0.05.
- **Category breakdown (GPT-5.4 zero-shot)**: Nested negation is hardest at **50.0%** family consistency, followed by conditional fallacies (84.0%) and propositional logic (86.0%); De Morgan and multi-hop reach 100.0%.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Alexander Gu** and **Alan Chen**, both at the University of Texas at Austin. The paper is a focused 10-page submission (including appendices) with 6 references.
- The work is positioned explicitly against related benchmarks: LogicBench (correctness-only, no reformulation invariance), FOLIO (correctness on first-order logic), paraphrase-based consistency studies (Lin et al. 2025, Shi et al. 2026), and CoT faithfulness work (Turpin et al. 2023). The differentiation argument is crisp.
- Frontier model access (GPT-5.4, o4-mini, Claude Sonnet 4, Gemini 2.5 Flash) is current as of April 2026. The authors commit to releasing code, data, and model outputs upon acceptance.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Genuinely novel relative to paraphrase-based consistency work — the use of *named logical transformations* (contrapositive, double negation, De Morgan, quantifier rewrite) rather than free-form paraphrases is what enables per-operator failure attribution, which paraphrase work cannot do. The CRTBench design is a real contribution. However, the headline "accuracy ≠ consistency" observation is conceptually familiar (Lin et al. 2025 already documents self-inconsistency on simple tasks); the contribution is the *where* and *why*, not the existence of the gap.
- **Rigor**: Strong on per-transformation and per-category breakdowns, and commendably transparent about confidence intervals (±5.1pp at n=350) and the regex-extraction failure mode (5 UNCLEAR responses, all Gemini). The reasoning-ablation analysis with per-category Table 4 is the paper's strongest piece of evidence and is presented honestly, including the unflattering finding that GPT-5.4 does not improve overall.
- **Weak baselines / comparisons**: The "baseline" comparison is really between OpenAI's own reasoning tiers plus three other frontier models; there is no comparison to *targeted consistency-improvement methods* (e.g., self-consistency sampling, paraphrastic-probing consistency verification from ref [5], or constrained-decoding approaches). It is an evaluation paper, so this is partially defensible, but it leaves the "so what do we do about it" question open.
- **Missing ablations**: (1) No few-shot or multi-template sensitivity analysis — the authors themselves flag that they used a single prompt template, so CRTBench numbers may shift under different framings. (2) No inter-annotator agreement or formal naturalness study, which the authors also admit — claims about linguistic realism should be read conservatively because families are template-generated. (3) No human baseline on the same items, so we cannot tell how the accuracy-consistency gap compares to human logical inconsistency. (4) Only binary/ternary answers (Yes/No/Cannot be determined), so longer-chain reasoning inconsistency is untested.
- **Data quirks**: The transformation coverage is uneven — quantifier rewrite applies to only 60 families and De Morgan to only 50, so the per-transformation numbers for those rows rest on a small n (e.g., GPT-5.4(r)'s 78.3% quantifier-rewrite consistency is over only 60 families, CI is wide). The headline family-consistency metric weights all seven categories equally (50 families each), which is reasonable but arbitrary.
- **Overclaiming risk**: Mostly well-controlled. The conclusion appropriately hedges ("we believe this poses an important question to explore") and explicitly declines to attribute the quantifier collapse to RL fine-tuning vs. structural integration of reasoning. The claim that accuracy is "not enough" is well-supported by the 38.6pp gap on GPT-5.4-mini.
- **Reproducibility**: Reasonable — model identifiers, temperature, prompt templates, and answer-extraction heuristics are all specified; data and outputs are promised upon acceptance. The main reproducibility risk is that GPT-5.4/o4-mini are proprietary and may be silently updated, so the exact numbers may drift over time.

## Strengths

- Clean, logically grounded transformation taxonomy that enables operator-level failure attribution — a real methodological advance over paraphrase-based consistency tests.
- Honest reporting of an unflattering finding: reasoning effort *hurts* GPT-5.4 on quantifier reasoning (92→20%), with a plausible mechanistic explanation (over-abstention — 119 spurious "Cannot be determined" responses).
- Per-category and per-transformation tables make it easy to see exactly where each model breaks.
- Strong frontier-model coverage as of April 2026, including both reasoning-enabled and reasoning-disabled variants of the GPT-5.4 family.
- Confidence-interval reporting at n=350 is a good practice that many evaluation papers skip.

## Limitations

- Benchmark is template-generated and covers only Yes/No/Cannot-be-determined items; does not test open-ended or multi-step reasoning chains where negation composition accumulates.
- Single prompt template, zero-shot only (plus one CoT ablation); no few-shot or template-sensitivity analysis. The authors flag this themselves.
- No formal inter-annotator agreement or standalone human-naturalness evaluation — manual audit served as internal QC only.
- Transformation coverage is unbalanced (De Morgan n=50, quantifier rewrite n=60), so some per-transformation percentages have wide CIs.
- No human baseline and no intervention baseline (self-consistency, constrained decoding, consistency-aware training), so the paper diagnoses the problem without testing fixes.
- Proprietary models may drift, threatening long-term reproducibility of exact numbers.

## Relevance

与本用户对 LLM 评估、逻辑推理鲁棒性、推理模型行为分析的兴趣高度相关：CRTBench 提供了一套可归因的逻辑一致性测试方法，可直接用于评估推理增强（reasoning effort / CoT）在何种条件下反而损害模型的逻辑不变性——尤其是 GPT-5.4 启用推理后量词推理从 92% 崩塌到 20% 的发现，对研究 RL 微调与推理集成的副作用很有启发性。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the quantifier-reasoning collapse under high reasoning effort replicate in open-weight reasoning models (e.g., R1-distill, Qwen3-thinking), or is it specific to OpenAI's GPT-5.4 reasoning integration? If the former, it points to a general failure mode of RL-trained reasoning; if the latter, to an implementation quirk.
- Can consistency-aware fine-tuning (e.g., augmenting training data with contrapositive/double-negation reformulations and enforcing label invariance) close the accuracy-consistency gap *without* inducing the over-abstention failure seen on quantifiers?
- How does the accuracy-consistency gap scale with chain length — does composing two or three logical transformations multiplicatively explode inconsistency beyond what single-transformation CRTBench numbers suggest?
- What is a human baseline on CRTBench? Without it, we cannot tell whether the 38.6pp gap on GPT-5.4-mini is a model pathology or also characteristic of human reasoning under negation load (the paper itself cites psycholinguistic work on human negation processing).
- Is the family-consistency metric overly strict (one wrong out of five ⇒ inconsistent)? A graded metric (e.g., fraction of reformulations answered correctly) might better separate "one slip" from "systematic operator-level failure" and change the model ranking.
