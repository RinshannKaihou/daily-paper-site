03 · Methodology

One scalar quantity — the input-magnitude exponent q of a residual block's velocity field, ‖v(x,t)‖ ≤ c‖x‖ᵠ + b — unifies three perspectives.

  • ODE view. Residual nets are forward-Euler discretizations of Ẋ = v(X,t). Grönwall gives a global bound at q = 1, polynomial at q∈(0,1), and an explicit blow-up time for every q > 1.
  • HJB optimal control. Training maximizes the Hamiltonian; the optimum is bang-bang and saturates the magnitude bound — so optimization cannot dodge supercritical velocities.
  • Discrete bounds. Theorem 3.5: linear / polynomial / geometric growth in depth. Theorem 3.7: sharper costate bounds; costate is conserved at q = 0.
  • Primitive arithmetic. Theorem 4.2 closes the catalogue under serial (q→q₁q₂), parallel (max), Hadamard (q₁+q₂), residual wrap (max(1,q)), normalization wrap (→0).
  • Rectification. L2-normalize the input into Mamba's selective branch and replace SiLU gating with sigmoid → q = 5 to q = 1, no normalization layer.

04 · Results

  • Blow-ups grow with depth. Free-velocity Mamba: 12/24 → 24/24 on ETTm1 (L=3→8); 2/12 → 10/12 on Weather — 48 / 72 total.
  • All four q ≤ 1 Mamba variants fully stable across 288 runs (RMSNorm, Pre-LN, Peri-LN, and the no-norm linear-growth fix).
  • PatchTST stable in all four variants, including free-velocity — because its primitive composition already sits at q = 1. Removing LayerNorm does not destabilize it.
  • Two ETTm1 free-velocity runs finished with MSE > 10¹², ~10 orders above target scale — counted as blow-ups.
  • Accuracy spread among q ≤ 1 variants ≤ 1.0% (Weather) / ≤ 1.5% (ETTm1) of per-cell median — near sampling noise.
  • Numerical depth ceiling at q = 1 (Remark 3.6): D ≈ 17 (FP16), D ≈ 128 (FP32) for c=1.

05 · Strengths

  • Mathematically tight. A single sharp threshold q = 1 with two independent proofs (ODE + HJB) giving both necessity and sufficiency.
  • The bang-bang theorem is the conceptual core: the training optimum lives on the boundary, so the architecture must not admit q > 1 at all.
  • Operational, not just explanatory. Per-block certification reduces to a table lookup — a genuine advance over per-architecture case analysis.
  • Suggests new architectures: the parameter-free Mamba fix (q=5→q=1) is an existence proof for normalization-free design.
  • Honest about limits: Post-LN exclusion, conservative upper bounds, squared-loss caveats, and the unexploited q∈(0,1) interior are all openly disclosed.

06 · Limitations

  • Modest scale. L ∈ {3,8}, ≤2M params, two TSF benchmarks only — no LLM, vision, or deep (L ≥ 32) stacks.
  • Post-LN excluded by assumption (outer normalization breaks additive updates) — a major gap given how common Post-LN is.
  • No code or reproducibility artifacts referenced.
  • Accuracy story is thin: the framework predicts which designs are safe, not which q ≤ 1 design is better (≤1.5% spread).
  • No interaction with modern stabilizers (grad clipping, QK-norm, μP, weight decay) studied.
  • Theorem 4.2 gives upper bounds on q, not exact values — certification is conservative; "sharpness" is for the abstract threshold, not per-block q.
