Evaluating Epistemic Uncertainty: Beyond OOD Detection and Active Learning

Jakub Paplhám · Willem Waegeman · Eyke Hüllermeier · Vojtěch Franc · CTU Prague / Ghent / LMU Munich

Key Finding

OOD detection and active-learning proxies optimize different inputs than regret-minimizing selectors — inverting how we rank epistemic-uncertainty methods.

01 Key Metric
0.0673 CIFAR-10H regret · AuReC (lower = better)
Evidential Networks: best on regret (0.0673) yet dead last on Far-OOD AuROC (0.726). The headline rank inversion.
02 Why This Matters

Reshapes how UQ papers should report results — OOD/AL proxies optimize different inputs than regret, so ranking estimators by them misleads deployment.

03 Critical Edge

Headline inversions rely on cross-task comparisons where OOD, active-learning, and regret are measured on different datasets.

01 / METHODOLOGY

One convex family, three constraints

Selective classification (threshold total T* = A*+E*) and the epistemic reject-option (threshold E*) are merged into three constrained problems over a selector C: X→[0,1]. Theorem 1 proves every optimum is a threshold on the convex combination (1−λ)A*+λE*, λ∈[1/2,1].

  • Rossellini sandbox. A 1-D Bayesian regression with closed-form Bayes-optimal scorers shows OOD, BALD, EER, risk-coverage and regret-coverage reject disjoint input subsets at 80% coverage.
  • Pareto-gap. Replaces Spearman rank-correlation as a "disentanglement" test: the IGD+ distance between a method's reachable (Coverage, Risk, Regret) surface and the oracle surface.
  • Benchmark. MC-Dropout, Deep Ensembles, DDU, Evidential Nets on CIFAR-10H, APPA-REAL, DCIC (8 multi-rater sets). 5-seed averages.

02 / RESULTS

Proxy tasks and regret disagree

  • CIFAR-10H (Table 1). Evidential: worst Far-OOD AuROC 0.726, best regret AuReC 0.0673. DDU inverts: best OOD 0.935, worst regret 0.0958.
  • Active-learning inversion (Tbl 5 vs 1). Evidential collapses on OpenML ID6 (NAUC 0.177 vs MC-Dropout 0.962), yet wins CIFAR-10H regret — global error-reduction ≠ pointwise regret.
  • Rank correlation is blind. Evidential shows rs(Â,Ê)=0.999 ("entangled") yet the lowest Pareto-gap (0.0379). Ground-truth rs on CIFAR-10H is 0.882 — high correlation can be correct, not a failure.
  • DCIC (Tbl 3). Ensembles win 5/8 (CE) and 6/8 (0-1) by n-AuReC; DDU wins 0/8.
  • APPA-REAL age (Tbl 2). Ensembles lowest AuReC (squared 11.35, absolute 0.795); DDU exceeds the random baseline (n-AuReC 1.048).
03 / STRENGTHS

Clean theory, honest framing

  • Theorem 1 is elegant — unifies two separate frameworks under one convex family indexed by λ∈[1/2,1], proved cleanly via KKT.
  • Rossellini sandbox makes "tasks disagree" concrete and visually obvious rather than rhetorical.
  • Pareto-gap reframes disentanglement as operational utility, not statistical independence — a stronger position than the rank-correlation criterion it replaces.
  • Honest about the frequentist paradox — true regret is uncomputable without p*(y|x); workaround via dense-annotation datasets is principled.
  • Per-dataset tables (App. D, Tbl 6–13) plus 5-seed averaging give statistical credibility.
04 / LIMITATIONS

Oracle metrics, borrowed proxies

  • Regret needs p*(y|x) — the metrics are benchmark-only and cannot apply to standard single-label datasets, restricting the evaluation regime.
  • Oracle weights. Pareto-gap and AuReC are computed by searching optimal (w₁,w₂,τ) on the test set — a capacity bound, not a deployable selector.
  • Cross-dataset inversions. OOD (CIFAR-10), active learning (OpenML) and regret (CIFAR-10H) live on different test sets; a within-set inversion would be stronger.
  • Borrowed proxies. OOD/AL numbers (Tbl 4, 5) come from an anonymous concurrent submission ("probly") readers cannot yet verify.
  • Conflated errors. Ground-truth regret mixes estimation and approximation error; not separated.
  • Domain scope. Image classification + tabular regression only — no NLP or vision-language.
