Why This Matters
A reusable, open-source variable-level audit combining causal reasoning, adversarial invariance, and shortcut-learning diagnostics for medical-imaging models.
Critical Edge
Prostate volume is MRI-derived and visually encoded in the same scan, so the −7.61% drop is partly mechanical and somewhat circular as evidence of disease-relevant signal.

02 Methodology

A representation-level sensitivity probe, not a causal-effect estimate.

Both MRI appearance X and ISUP grade Y are treated as observed manifestations of latent tumour pathology U. For each covariate C, train a baseline MRI-only grader, then an adversarially-invariant version that suppresses C; compare with the signed delta ΔM(C).

  • Δ > 0 → C acts as a shortcut; suppression helps.
  • Δ < 0 → C carries disease signal; suppression hurts.
  • Gradient-reversal layer between ProFound encoder and per-covariate adversary head (λ = 1.0). Clinical variables needed only at training.
  • One model trained per covariate to isolate effects.

External PROMIS reproduced the harmful direction: prostate volume −3.15% AUC, binary PI-RADS −2.80%, PSA −1.54% (all p<.001); alcohol-use suppression remained beneficial (+0.47%). Cross-architecture check with ResNet-18: prostate-volume suppression again the largest degradation (−4.97% AUC).

04 Strengths

  • Causal taxonomy turns a vague "is this a confounder?" into a signed, testable delta.
  • Strong hygiene: patient-level splits, 6 seeds, bootstrap CIs, paired bootstrap significance (10k resamples), external validation, secondary backbone.
  • Directional findings consistent across datasets and backbones.
  • Honest framing: explicit this is sensitivity analysis, not causal proof.
  • Open code, open external dataset (PROMIS), released ProFound backbone.

05 Limitations

  • One-variable-at-a-time; interactions and joint suppression left to future work.
  • Continuous covariates discretised into 2–4 bins — may underestimate disease-signal in PSA and volume.
  • Single-centre UCLH training cohort, not publicly releasable.
  • Adversarial weight λ fixed at 1.0 with no sensitivity analysis; no comparison to regression/reweighting baselines.
  • Absolute AUCs (~64–69%) modest vs current SOTA — this is a probing paper, not a performance paper.
  • PROMIS is inference-only and pathway-conditioned, weakening the external test of the shortcut-removal claim.
