Covariate balance fails for long-horizon MDPs.

A formal diagnostic, extended to time-dependent offline RL — and applied to the canonical MIMIC-III sepsis benchmark.

Joshua Spear · Rebecca Pope · Neil J. Sebire
UCL GOS Institute of Child Health · NIHR GOSH UCL BRC

Key Finding

Covariate balance fails on the standard sepsis offline-RL benchmark; clipped and Hajek estimators falsely certify balance at long horizons.

Key Metric
0.481

best test-set Macro-F1, full 25-action sepsis space

xgboost_1_w_optuna · 0.622 train / 0.457 val / 0.481 test — behaviour policy is only moderately learnable.

Why This Matters

Targets the canonical sepsis offline-RL benchmark; any healthcare offline-RL or OPE evaluation must add this negative result to its robustness checklist.

Critical Edge

The empirical study is underpowered: only two seeds are used, and best test Macro-F1 on the full action space is only 0.481 — leaving open whether balance failure reflects confounding or a weakly-specified behaviour-policy model.

Critical Assessment · Rigour

§ 01

Methodology

formal + empirical

The paper pairs a closed-form derivation of time-dependent covariate balance (Section 3) with a diagnostic study on MIMIC-III sepsis (Section 4). Its analytical centrepiece: clipped and Hajek importance-sampling estimators provably degenerate to the unweighted MASD for large t — so they cannot certify balance precisely where it matters most.

Formal layer

Re-derives conditional (MASD_Cond) and weighted (MASD_Weight) balance from Rosenbaum–Rubin and a change-of-measure; extends to the time-dependent MASD_Weight-t via the MDP factorisation p(A₀:t|S₀:t) = ∏ p(Aₜ|Sₜ).

Bias proofs

Closed-form (Eqs. 23–24): for large t, MASD_clipped-t → MASD_UnWeight-t and MASD_Hajek-t → MASD_UnWeight-t. Variance reduction collapses into a false "balanced" reading.

Empirical setup

Komorowski 2018 / mimic_sepsis pipeline · 4-hour buckets (max 18 steps) · 5×5 IV-fluid × vasopressor action space (25 actions). Three custom xgboost propensity models; results on held-out test split.

Diagnostic criteria & the Welsh-vs-Wald choice

Balance is certified if MASD_Weight-t < 0.25 for all timesteps (Cannas & Arpino 2019 heuristic) and/or the derived MASD-DiffWeight-t > 0. The authors deliberately use a Welsh (unequal-variance) denominator √(σ²ₐ′ + σ²ₐ″) rather than the pooled Wald denominator — empirically justified because the variance ratio diverges by the 4th (full) / 5th (fluid) timestep.

§ 02

Results & Highlights

MIMIC-III sepsis · 2 seeds
  1. Propensity model is only moderately learnable.
    Best test Macro-F1 of 0.481 (full action space) and 0.657 (fluid-only); tuning/weighting buys only ~+0.01–0.02 over the unweighted xgboost baseline.
  2. Balance fails the 0.25 MASD threshold across nearly the whole trajectory.
    The propensity model improves over unweighted only for the first 3 train / 2 test timesteps on full sepsis (Figures 10, 19).
  3. Apparent long-horizon "improvement" is a double artefact.
    Vanilla-estimator MASD drifts down only because the variance denominator overflows (selection bias); the unnormalised numerator actually diverges with horizon (Figures 4–5).
  4. Clipped / Hajek estimators give a false positive.
    They appear to certify balance at long horizons (Figures 6–7, 11–12) but, per Eqs. 23–24, they certify "no change from unweighted," not "balance holds."
  5. Effective sample size collapses.
    Minimum action representation is only 3% of total observations (seed 0, train); with 25t trajectory growth this drives the IS variance explosion.
  6. Mitigations help — modestly and non-uniformly.
    Fluid-only extends the balanced window to 4 test timesteps; truncating to horizon 8 (sepsis) / 5 (fluid) yields 3 / 5 positive timesteps but with no confidence intervals.
  7. Train/test gap looks like a sample-size effect.
    Sub-sampling the train set to test-set size recovers the same balanced-timestep count — implying training-set balance may be usable in practice (Figure 24).

Strengths + plus

  • Clean formalisation. Two balance notions plus a time-dependent extension; the clipped/Hajek degeneracy proofs are the centrepiece.
  • Honest about its own negative result — explicitly admits it cannot disentangle confounding from test inappropriateness.
  • Clinically canonical benchmark (Komorowski 2018 / MIMIC-III) on a public preprocessing pipeline.
  • Reports train + held-out test and directly tests the in-sample-optimism concern (Figure 24).
  • Actionable mitigations (smaller action space, shorter horizon) — not just a verdict.

Limitations – caveats

  • Small empirical scale: two seeds, one dataset, one domain. No cancer / HIV / ventilation / glycaemic transfer.
  • Author-built propensity models at only moderate accuracy — balance failure may partly be mis-specification, a confound the paper flags but does not resolve.
  • Internally inconsistent thresholds: reuses the 0.25 / 0.10 heuristics derived under a denominator the paper itself rejects.
  • Overflow handled by imputation, admitted as flawed; no log-domain or robust-arithmetic alternative.
  • No released code; multiple broken cross-references ("appendix section ??") suggest a draft.
  • Fragile truncation claim: a count of positive timesteps over two seeds, no confidence intervals.
