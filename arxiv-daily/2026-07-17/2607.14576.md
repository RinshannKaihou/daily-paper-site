> **一句话总结：** 本文提出残差网络"亚线性增长原理"——每个残差块的输入模长指数 q 满足 ∥v(x,t)∥ ≤ c∥x∥^q + b 且 q ≤ 1 是训练稳定性的尖锐阈值（经 ODE 全局存在性与 HJB 最优控制 bang-bang 论证共同得到充要条件），并通过对架构原语的 q 算术给出可组合的认证程序；据此用无归一化修改把超临界的 Mamba 块从 q=5 降到 q=1，在 ETTm1/Weather 上验证 q≤1 变体零 blow-up，而自由速度 q=5 的 Mamba 在深度 L=8 时全部发散。

> **TL;DR:** The authors propose the *sublinear-growth principle* — a residual block's velocity field must satisfy ∥v(x,t)∥ ≤ c∥x∥^q + b with q ≤ 1 — and prove q = 1 is a sharp, necessary-and-sufficient training-stability threshold using two independent arguments (classical ODE non-explosion, and bang-bang optimal control on the Hamilton–Jacobi–Bellman boundary). An "arithmetic of input-magnitude exponents" over five primitive operations lets one certify q ≤ 1 from a Table-1 catalogue without trial-and-error search; a parameter-free modification drops Mamba from q = 5 to q = 1 with no normalization layer. On the ETTm1 and Weather time-series benchmarks, all four q ≤ 1 Mamba variants survive 288/288 seed-level runs while free-velocity Mamba (q = 5) diverges in 48/72 runs (e.g., 24/24 on ETTm1 at depth L = 8), and PatchTST free-velocity stays stable at q = 1 — dissociating stability from normalization presence.

## 核心贡献

- **提出尖锐稳定性阈值 q ≤ 1**：以"亚线性增长原理" ∥v(x,t)∥ ≤ c∥x∥^q + b 统一描述残差块速度场的输入模长增长，并通过两条独立路径（经典 ODE 全局存在性 + HJB bang-bang 最优控制）证明 q = 1 是必要且充分的稳定阈值。
- **离散残差网络的前向/反向稳定性界**：在 q ≤ 1 下导出深度显式的前向状态界（Theorem 3.5，q=0 线性、q∈(0,1) 多项式、q=1 几何增长）与 OC 最优意义下更紧的共态 Lipschitz 界（Theorem 3.7，q=0 时共态被保持）。
- **架构原语上的 q 算术与认证程序**：Theorem 4.2 给出五个操作（串行复合 q→q₁q₂、并联和 q→max、Hadamard 乘积 q→q₁+q₂、残差包裹 q→max(1,q)、归一化包裹 q→0）；配合 Table 1 原语表把"块是否 q ≤ 1"约简为查表 + 简单算术。
- **超临界架构的无归一化修复**：用线性增长修改（L2 归一化选择性参数路径 + sigmoid 替换 SiLU gating）把 Mamba 块从 q = 5 降到 q = 1，无需任何归一化层，实验证明其与带 RMSNorm 的基线同样稳定。
- **理论与实验共同解耦"模长指数"与"归一化层存在"**：Mamba 自由速度（q=5）发散，PatchTST 自由速度（q=1）稳定，说明稳定性的真正判据是 q 而非是否插入 LayerNorm。

## Key Contributions

- **Sharp stability threshold q ≤ 1** for residual velocity fields, established via two independent proofs: classical ODE non-explosion (global flow on [0, T] for q ≤ 1; explicit finite-time blow-up for every q > 1) and an HJB optimal-control argument showing the training optimum is bang-bang on the admissible-class boundary, so the OC optimum blows up at q > 1 and is safe by construction at q ≤ 1.
- **Depth-explicit forward/backward bounds** for the discrete residual stack (Theorem 3.5 linear / polynomial / geometric in depth for q = 0 / (0,1) / 1; Theorem 3.7 sharper costate bounds at the OC optimum, including costate conservation at q = 0).
- **An arithmetic of input-magnitude exponents under five primitive operations** (Theorem 4.2), together with a Table-1 catalogue of standard primitives (LN/RMSNorm/saturating activations at q = 0; linear layers, Lipschitz activations, attention, MLP at q = 1; selective SSM/Mamba at q = 5), turning block-level certification of q ≤ 1 into inspection rather than trial-and-error search.
- **A parameter-free rectification of the supercritical Mamba block** from q = 5 to q = 1 by L2-normalizing the input into the selective-parameter branch and replacing SiLU gating with sigmoid, with no normalization layer anywhere in the residual stream.
- **Empirical dissociation of the stability criterion from normalization presence**: Mamba free-velocity (q = 5) blows up, PatchTST free-velocity (q = 1) is fully stable, demonstrating that q — not the LayerNorm wrapper — is what governs training stability.

## Methodology

The paper unifies three perspectives under a single scalar quantity, the **input-magnitude exponent q** of a residual block's velocity field, defined by the bound ∥v(x, t)∥ ≤ c ∥x∥^q + b.

1. **ODE view (Section 2).** Residual architectures are treated as forward-Euler discretizations of Ẋ = v(X, t). Classical theory gives a Grönwall-type global bound at q = 1, polynomial growth at q ∈ (0, 1), and an explicit finite-time blow-up time t* = ∥X₀∥^(1−q) / (c(q − 1)) for every q > 1. The discrete recursion inherits super-exponential blow-up within modest depth.

2. **Optimal-control view via HJB (Section 3).** Training is cast as an OC problem over an admissible velocity class U^q_ad. Pointwise maximization of the Hamiltonian yields a closed-form **bang-bang** optimum (Theorem 3.1) that saturates the magnitude bound: ∥v*(X, t)∥ = c∥X∥^q + b. Because the optimum lives on the boundary, optimization cannot dodge dangerous supercritical velocities; at q ≤ 1 the entire admissible class is safe. The Crandall–Lions comparison principle and a discrete Bellman-operator contraction (Propositions 3.3, 3.4) yield HJB well-posedness.

3. **Discrete stability bounds (Section 3.2).** Theorem 3.5 gives depth-explicit forward-state bounds (linear / polynomial / geometric in D). Off-optimum, the cumulative Jacobian obeys ∥J_{i:D}∥₂ ≤ (1 + LΔt)^(D−i) (Eq. 32); at the OC optimum, Theorem 3.7 gives sharper costate bounds via Danskin's identity, including the q = 0 *conserved-costate* result with no depth-amplification factor.

4. **Primitive arithmetic (Section 4).** The set of architectural primitives P is closed under five operations whose q-transformations are explicit (Theorem 4.2): serial composition (q → q₁q₂), parallel sum (q → max), Hadamard product (q → q₁ + q₂), residual wrapping (q → max(1, q)), and normalization wrapping (q → 0). A four-step per-block certification procedure plus a between-block mixed-exponent analysis reduces architectural stability certification to a table lookup.

5. **Experiments (Section 5).** On ETTm1 and Weather time-series forecasting (input window 96, horizons ∈ {96, 192, 336, 720}, residual depths L ∈ {3, 8}, small ≤2M-parameter regime), the authors test five Mamba variants (q = 0 RMSNorm baseline / Pre-LN / Peri-LN, q = 1 linear-growth modification, q = 5 free-velocity) and four PatchTST variants. The linear-growth Mamba modification L2-normalizes the input into the selective-parameter branch (dropping that path from q = 1 to q = 0) and replaces SiLU gating x·σ(x) with sigmoid gating σ(x), eliminating a q = 2 contribution.

## Results & Highlights

- **Mamba free-velocity (q = 5) blow-up rate grows sharply with depth**: 12/24 blow-ups on ETTm1 and 2/12 on Weather at L = 3, rising to 24/24 on ETTm1 and 10/12 on Weather at L = 8 — a total of 48 blow-ups out of 72 free-velocity runs (Table 2).
- **All four q ≤ 1 Mamba variants are completely stable** across 288 seed-level runs in their 64 configuration cells (zero blow-ups for RMSNorm baseline, Pre-LN, Peri-LN, and the no-normalization linear-growth modification at q = 1).
- **PatchTST is fully stable across all 288 runs** in all four variants, including the free-velocity variant that sits at q = 1 by primitive composition — directly demonstrating that removing normalization does not destabilize a block whose exponent is already q = 1.
- **Two Mamba ETTm1 free-velocity runs at L = 3, horizon 192** terminated with finite MSE values exceeding 10¹², roughly ten orders of magnitude above the standardized target scale, and are counted as blow-ups.
- **Forecasting accuracy spread for Mamba q ≤ 1 variants** is within ≤ 1.0% of the per-cell median on Weather and ≤ 1.5% on ETTm1; for PatchTST the spread tightens to ≤ 0.05% on Weather (effectively indistinguishable at plot resolution) and ≤ 0.8% on ETTm1.
- **Free-velocity Mamba (q = 5) on surviving cells is uniformly above the median**, by as much as 4.6% MSE on the smallest Weather configuration.
- **Practical depth limits at q = 1** (Remark 3.6): with Δt = 1 and c = 1, the exponential factor 2^D exceeds the FP16 maximum (≈ 6.5 × 10⁴) at D = 17 and the FP32/BF16 dynamic range (≈ 3.4 × 10³⁸) at D = 128, so even subcritical architectures carry a numerical-range depth ceiling.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** Genuine and timely: prior work (Haber–Ruthotto 2017; Kan et al. 2025 on Pre-LN/Peri-LN via HJB) analyzed stability *per-architecture*; this paper is the first to identify a single scalar criterion q ≤ 1 that spans the full primitive catalogue. The bang-bang OC argument that ties the *training optimum* to the boundary of the admissible class is the sharpest theoretical move — it explains why engineering tricks like careful initialization cannot guarantee stability: the optimum itself saturates the dangerous boundary.
- **Rigor.** High. Theorems are stated precisely with uniform-in-θ hypotheses, proofs are given inline (Cauchy–Schwarz for the bang-bang optimum, Grönwall + concavity for forward bounds, Danskin's identity for costate bounds), and viscosity-solution well-posedness is handled via Crandall–Lions comparison with a careful escape for q ∈ (0, 1) where the global modulus condition fails (coercivity + Lipschitz terminal datum). Caveats are openly flagged: squared loss is *neither* BUC nor globally Lipschitz, so they rely on the forward bound confining the trajectory to a ball and use a BUC/Lipschitz extension (Remark 3.8).
- **Reproducibility.** Moderate-to-good. The Mamba linear-growth modification is described concretely (L2-normalize x_t on the parameter path; replace SiLU gating with sigmoid gating — Figure 2), and the hyperparameters (≤2M params, L ∈ {3, 8}, horizons ∈ {96, 192, 336, 720}, 3 seeds Weather / 6 seeds ETTm1) are specified. However, no code release is mentioned, the precise dataset splits and exact training-step budgets are not tabulated, and the "free velocity" baseline conflates removing RMSNorm with whatever default initialization Mamba ships with — a potentially load-bearing choice that is not ablated.
- **Significance.** Conceptually unifying: it gives a single algebraic explanation for *why* LayerNorm stabilizes (it forces q = 0), *why* FixUp/ReZero/Dynamic Tanh also stabilize (they all reach q ≤ 1 by different routes), and *why* Mamba's multiplicative gating specifically is dangerous (q = 5 from Hadamard products accumulating exponents). The framework is a candidate organizing principle for normalization-free architecture search.
- **Red flags / overclaims.**
  - **Scope limited to additive residual updates.** Remark 4.4 explicitly excludes Post-LN (X_{k+1} = LN(X_k + Δt f(X_k))) because an outer normalization resets the state — meaning the bounds do not directly apply to the original Post-LN transformer, a substantial practical gap.
  - **Theorem 4.2 gives upper bounds on q, not exact values.** The certification procedure is conservative by construction; the authors acknowledge this but it weakens the "sharpness" rhetoric — sharpness is established for the abstract exponent threshold, not for the per-block q estimate.
  - **Experiments are shallow (L ≤ 8) and small (≤2M parameters).** The paper's own Remark 3.6 shows the q = 1 depth ceiling is ~17 (FP16) / ~128 (FP32) for c = 1, but no experiment approaches these depths, so the framework's predictions at LLM-relevant depth (~100 layers, ~10⁹ parameters) are untested. The connection to large-scale language/vision training is asserted, not demonstrated.
  - **The 4.6% MSE degradation on Weather for surviving free-velocity Mamba seeds is a cherry-picked worst case** in a table otherwise dominated by blow-ups; the accuracy comparison is the weakest part of the empirical story.
  - **Strict-interior q ∈ (0, 1)** is flagged as an open design space (Remark 4.5) with no current primitive realizing it — a candid admission that the theoretically sharpest regime is unexploited.
  - The claim that the criterion is "independent of initialization or optimization" (Section 2) is theoretically clean but empirically untested: no initialization sweep is reported.

## Strengths

- Mathematically tight: a single sharp threshold q = 1 with two independent derivations (ODE + HJB), giving both necessity (the OC optimum at q > 1 blows up) and sufficiency (every admissible field is safe at q ≤ 1).
- The bang-bang theorem (Theorem 3.1) is the key conceptual insight: optimization cannot avoid the boundary of the admissible class, so the architecture must not admit q > 1 at all.
- The arithmetic of exponents (Theorem 4.2) makes the theory operational: per-block certification reduces to a table lookup, a genuine advance over per-architecture case analysis.
- The parameter-free Mamba fix (q = 5 → q = 1) is an elegant existence proof that the framework can suggest novel normalization-free architectures, not just re-explain existing ones.
- Honest about limitations: Post-LN exclusion, conservative upper bounds, squared-loss caveats, and the unexplored q ∈ (0, 1) interior are all openly disclosed.

## Limitations

- **Scale and depth of experiments are modest**: L ∈ {3, 8} and ≤2M parameters on two TSF benchmarks only. No LLM, vision, or generative-modeling experiments; no deep (L ≥ 32) stacks where the q = 1 numerical-range ceiling (Remark 3.6) would actually bite.
- **Theoretical scope excludes Post-LN transformers** (outer normalization breaks the additive-update assumption) — a major omission given how common Post-LN is.
- **No code or reproducibility artifacts** are referenced.
- **The accuracy story is thin**: the central empirical claim is about *stability* (blow-up counts), and the MSE differences among the four q ≤ 1 variants are within ≤ 1.5% — close to within-cell sampling noise — so the framework does not (yet) predict which q ≤ 1 design is *better*, only which are *safe*.
- **No interaction with modern stabilizers** (gradient clipping, weight decay, QK-norm, μP) is studied, leaving open how q-based certification composes with engineering safety nets.
- **No comparison to alternative stability theories** (e.g., FixUp's Lipschitz-continuity analysis, NFNets' adaptive gradient clipping) in terms of predictive power on a shared benchmark.

## Relevance

这篇论文为残差网络训练稳定性提供了一个尖锐、可操作的数学判据（输入模长指数 q ≤ 1），对关注神经网络架构设计、Transformer/SSM 稳定性、以及归一化层理论与替代方案（Dynamic Tanh、FixUp、ReZero 等）的研究者高度相关；其"原语算术 + 查表认证"的框架可直接用于指导新型归一化-free 架构（如 Mamba、Hymba 这类混合头模型）的设计与诊断。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the q ≤ 1 criterion remain predictive at LLM scale (L ≈ 100, ~10⁹ parameters), where Remark 3.6's numerical-range ceiling at q = 1 (~128 layers for FP32) starts to bind? Does inserting q = 0 blocks at strategic depths extend the safe depth budget?
- How does the framework extend to **Post-LN transformers** (X_{k+1} = LN(X_k + Δt f(X_k))) and to RMSNorm-pre-norm LLMs, given that the present additive-update analysis explicitly excludes outer-normalization blocks?
- Can a useful **strict-interior primitive at q ∈ (0, 1)** be constructed (Remark 4.5), inheriting the polynomial-in-T forward bound strictly sharper than q = 1? What inductive bias would such a primitive carry?
- How does q-based certification compose with engineering stabilizers (gradient clipping, QK-norm, μP, weight decay)? Are there stabilizers that *effectively* reduce c or q without changing the architecture?
- The accuracy spread among q ≤ 1 variants is within ≤ 1.5% — can the framework be sharpened to predict *which* q ≤ 1 composition is accuracy-optimal, not merely stability-admissible?
- Does the bang-bang OC-optimum prediction (that training drives realized velocities toward the admissible-class boundary) have an empirical signature measurable during real training (e.g., growing effective c)?
- Can the primitive catalogue be extended to cover **discontinuous primitives** (vector quantization, top-k routing in MoE) beyond the straight-through-estimator escape hatch noted in Remark 4.3?
