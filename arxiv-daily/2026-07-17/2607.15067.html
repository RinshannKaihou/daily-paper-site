Methodology

∫ The estimator

Replace WIS's global self-normalizer with a Nadaraya-Watson kernel regression of E[W|S]:

Ĵ_KWIS = (1/n) Σᵢ rᵢ wᵢ · [ (Σⱼ k_h(sᵢ,sⱼ) wⱼ) / (Σⱼ k_h(sᵢ,sⱼ)) ]⁻¹ # bracket = NW estimate of E[W|S=sᵢ]
  • Bandwidth continuum. h→∞ ⇒ collapses to WIS; h→0 ⇒ per-state State-WIS (new).
  • Consistency (Thm 1). Under 9 mild assumptions + Einmahl & Mason (2005), Ĵ → E[R] a.s. when h→0, log(n/aₙ)→∞.
  • Bandwidth selection. K-fold CV on the NW regressor; L-BFGS-B with a hand-derived analytic RBF gradient.

↔ The bandwidth spectrum

VIS, Kernel-WIS, WIS and State-WIS are not rivals — they are points on one bandwidth axis.

State-WIS
h → 0 Kernel-WIS
(CV-selected h) WIS
h → ∞
  • VIS = per-sample independence, but unbounded (no normalizer).
  • WIS = globally self-normalised, bounded, but samples are dependent.
  • Kernel-WIS interpolates: bounded and locally independent.

Strengths & Limitations

+ Strengths

  • Clean conceptual unification of VIS (independence) and WIS (boundedness) via one bandwidth parameter; WIS and State-WIS are honest limiting cases.
  • First formal consistency proof for this estimator, on top of Einmahl & Mason (2005) non-parametric kernel theory.
  • Substantial empirical sweep — 10 datasets × faulty-action sets × 2 temperatures × 3 policy types × oracle/non-oracle — far beyond a toy demo.
  • Striking tail improvement under behaviour-policy misspecification, the practically important regime.
  • Intellectual honesty: reports the continuous-reward failure, the unique-bandwidth overfit, and the tangential CV criterion.

− Limitations

  • Does not uniformly outperform WIS — unresolved estimator-selection problem for the analyst.
  • No comparison against doubly-robust (DM, DR, switch-DR, MRDR), the Alquier et al. (2024) clipping / log-smoothing family, or model-based FQE.
  • Evaluation is entirely semi-simulated on recast supervised-classification datasets with discrete 0/1 reward; only one continuous-reward construction, and it fails.
  • Consistency proof assumes fixed policies and h→0; the practical CV procedure selects a fixed h — the gap is acknowledged but not closed.
  • Hand-derived analytic gradient ties the implementation to the RBF kernel.
  • No tail-bound / asymptotic-normality analysis — only consistency; multiple-comparison correction not applied across the t-test grid.
