> **一句话总结：** GeoDetect 利用视觉-语言预训练模型(VLP)嵌入空间的各向异性结构，理论证明对抗样本会偏离干净流形，并以 LID、k-NN、Mahalanobis、KDE 四种几何度量无需微调即可在 ALBEF/CLIP/TCL 上跨零样本分类与检索任务取得接近 100% AUC 的对抗样本检测性能。

> **TL;DR:** GeoDetect is the first task-agnostic, theoretically grounded adversarial-example (AE) detector for vision-language pre-trained models (VLPs). It exploits the empirically observed anisotropy of VLP embedding spaces (CLIPCNN, CLIPViT, ALBEF, TCL), proves that adversarial perturbations push embeddings into off-manifold regions with larger expected distances to random clean points, and turns four classical geometric scores (LID, k-NN, Mahalanobis, KDE) into a lightweight, fine-tuning-free detector. On zero-shot classification (CIFAR10/100, ImageNet1k, STL10, Food101) and image-text retrieval (Flickr30k, MS-COCO), GeoDetect lifts AUC from the MCM baseline's ~65-93% to near 100% (e.g., CLIPCNN CIFAR10/STL10 reach 100.00 AUC / 0.00 FPR95 vs. MCM's 65.47/95.82), and stays robust under two strong white-box adaptive attacks (different-distribution and selective gradient descent).

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统分析 VLP 嵌入空间的各向异性几何结构：用 isotropy 指标 I₁、I₂ 与 effective rank (ER) 实证证明 CLIP/ALBEF/TCL 等对比学习模型比有监督 ResNet-50 更显著各向异性，为后续理论奠定基础。
- 给出严密的理论分析：在 Anisotropic Covariance 与 Manifold Proximity 假设下推导 Lemma 1（最优对抗嵌入的闭式解 `z'_i* = (Σ+λI)^-1 λz_i`）、Lemma 2（对抗样本偏离流形）和 Theorem 1（AE 到随机干净样本的期望距离严格大于干净样本），从理论上证明几何度量天然适合检测 VLP 中的 AE。
- 提出 GeoDetect：一个模型无关、任务无关、无需微调的检测框架，复用 LID（层内）、k-NN、Mahalanobis、KDE 四种经典几何度量，并融合 fused VLP 的多模态编码器层特征提升多模态攻击检测。
- 全面评测覆盖 aligned 与 fused 两类 VLP，跨越零样本分类和图像-文本检索两大任务，并系统评估 Sepuni / Sepmulti / Co-Attack / SGA 攻击以及两类白盒自适应攻击（不同分布批 + 选择性梯度下降），代码已开源 (https://github.com/AfsanehEB/GeoDetect)。

## Key Contributions

- First systematic characterization of VLP embedding-space anisotropy: uses isotropy measures I₁, I₂ and effective rank (ER) to empirically show CLIP, ALBEF and TCL are far more anisotropic than a supervised ResNet-50, motivating the theory.
- A rigorous theoretical treatment: under an Anisotropic Covariance assumption (Σ ≠ cI) and a Manifold Proximity assumption, the authors derive the optimal adversarial embedding (Lemma 1, `z'_i* = (Σ+λI)^-1 λz_i`), prove AEs leave the clean manifold (Lemma 2), and prove the Expected Distance Gap theorem (`E‖z'_i − z_u‖ > E‖z_i − z_u‖`, γ > 3α), justifying why geometric scores detect AEs.
- GeoDetect itself: a model-agnostic, task-agnostic, fine-tuning-free detector built on four classical geometric metrics (LID computed layer-wise, k-NN, Mahalanobis, KDE) over image or multimodal [CLS] embeddings, with logistic-regression/threshold decision rules; fused-VLP multimodal layers are added as extra features to catch multimodal attacks.
- Broad evaluation across both aligned (CLIPCNN ResNet-50, CLIPViT ViT-B/16) and fused (ALBEF, TCL) VLPs, on zero-shot classification and image-text retrieval, against Sepuni/Sepmulti/Co-Attack/SGA and two adaptive white-box attacks (different-distribution batch and selective gradient descent). Code is public.

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** Given clean image-text pairs `D_c = {(x_i, t_i)}` and adversarial pairs `D_a = {(x'_i, t_i)}` (image only) or `{(x'_i, t'_i)}` (both modalities), GeoDetect extracts image embeddings `z_I = E_I(x)`, text embeddings `z_T = E_T(t)`, and (for fused VLPs) multimodal embeddings `z_M = E_M(z_I, z_T)`. Detection is framed as a binary decision `f((x_i,t_i), {z_j}) = H(Metric(z_i, {z_j}))`, where `{z_j}` is a clean reference minibatch.

**Three-stage pipeline:**
1. *Generation* — produce AEs with Sep-Attack (Sepuni on unimodal embedding, Sepmulti on fused multimodal embedding) and Co-Attack (joint image+text); image attacks use PGD with ℓ∞ ε = 8/255, step 1.25, 10 iterations, BERT-Attack with budget 1 token for text.
2. *Extraction* — compute four geometric scores per query against a clean reference batch: k-NN mean distance, MLE estimate of LID (layer-wise, following Ma et al. ICLR'18), Gaussian KDE (bandwidth 0.1), Mahalanobis distance (μ, Σ estimated on D_c). For fused VLPs the multimodal encoder LID is added as an extra feature.
3. *Detection* — for k-NN/Mahalanobis/KDE use a threshold `H(s_i) = I(s_i > τ)`; for LID train a logistic regression on multi-layer LID features. Data split 80% calibration / 20% evaluation; batch size 128 for CLIP, 64 for ALBEF/TCL, k=100 LID / k=10 k-NN for CLIP, k=40 LID for ALBEF/TCL.

**Theory.** Assumption 1 (anisotropic Σ with σ₁ ≫ σ₂ ≫ … ≫ σ_D) is verified empirically (I₁ lower, I₂ higher, ER lower than supervised ResNet-50). Lemma 1 solves the Lagrangian KL-maximization and gives the closed-form optimal AE. Lemma 2 shows the optimal AE amplifies normal components and suppresses tangent components, yielding `‖z'_i − M‖ ≥ γ > α`. Theorem 1 (γ > 3α) gives the strict expected-distance gap that motivates the geometric metrics. Empirical verification on ImageNet-V2/CLIPCNN shows AEs concentrate energy in low-singular-value directions, have higher PCA reconstruction residuals (top-K=10 PCs), and larger KL divergence along the leading 1–20 PCA components.

## Results & Highlights

- **Zero-shot classification, CLIPCNN (Table 1):** On CIFAR10 and STL10, GeoDetect variants hit **AUC = 100.00 and FPR95 = 0.00**, vs MCM's **65.47 AUC / 82.88 FPR95** (CIFAR10 Sepuni) and **95.82 AUC / 17.92 FPR95** (STL10 Sepuni) — a ~+34 and +4 AUC point gain. On ImageNet1k, GeoDetect-kNN reaches **99.67 AUC / 0.89 FPR95** (Co-Attack) vs MCM's **80.83 AUC / 68.38 FPR95** — roughly a 19-point AUC jump.
- **Zero-shot classification, ALBEF (Table 1):** GeoDetect-Mahalanobis achieves **AUC = 100.00 / FPR95 = 0.00** on CIFAR10, CIFAR100, STL10, Food101 (Sepuni and Co-Attack), whereas MCM scores only **AUC 47.43 / FPR95 98.23** for Sepmulti on CIFAR10 and **AUC 63.03 / FPR95 97.59** on ImageNet1k — MCM essentially fails on the fused multimodal attack Sepmulti.
- **Image-text retrieval (Table 2):** CLIPCNN k-NN reaches **AUC 99.99 / FPR95 0.00** on Flickr30k and **99.97 / 0.00** on MS-COCO (Sepuni); ALBEF k-NN reaches **99.75 / 1.05** and **98.54 / 5.67**, well above what label-free detection previously achieved. Retrieval results for aligned CLIP match classification-level performance.
- **Adaptive attacks (Table 3):** Under different-distribution white-box adaptive attacks on the retrieval task, GeoDetect-LID retains strong discrimination: CLIPCNN LID Co-Attack drops only from **AUC 98.90 (non-adaptive) → 82.81 (adaptive)** on Flickr30k, and ALBEF Sepmulti LID even rises under the adaptive setting (**74.26 → 89.83** on Flickr30k, **79.85 → 92.31** on COCO), demonstrating the geometric signal survives gradient-based evasion.
- **Isotropy / manifold evidence (Fig. 2-3):** VLPs show consistently lower effective rank ER than supervised ResNet-50; adversarial embeddings move more energy into the lowest-singular-value directions, exhibit higher PCA reconstruction residuals (top-10 clean PCs), and produce larger KL divergence along the leading 1–20 PCA components — directly supporting Theorem 1.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Genuine and timely — this is the first task-agnostic AE detector for VLPs, explicitly handling both aligned (CLIP) and fused (ALBEF, TCL) architectures across both classification and label-free retrieval. The theoretical move from "CLIP is anisotropic" (Liang et al. NeurIPS'22, Levi & Gilboa ICML'25) to a closed-form optimal AE and an expected-distance-gap theorem is a clean, non-trivial contribution rather than a re-skinning of the unimodal LID detector (Ma et al. ICLR'18).

**Rigor:** The experimental matrix is unusually thorough: 4 VLPs × 7 datasets × 4 metrics × 3+ attack types plus 2 adaptive settings. The theory is actually proved (Lemmas 1-2 and Theorem 1, with proofs in Appendix B) rather than hand-waved, and Assumptions 1-2 are empirically validated (Figs. 2-3).

**Reproducibility:** Strong — code released, attack budgets and step sizes specified (ε = 8/255, step 1.25, 10 iterations, k=100/40 LID, k=10 k-NN, KDE bandwidth 0.1, batch sizes per model), public checkpoints of ALBEF/TCL used, and 80/20 calibration/eval split documented.

**Concerns / red flags:**
- *Single baseline.* The only non-GeoDetect baseline is MCM, a CLIP-OOD detector that is structurally incompatible with retrieval and that fails badly on Sepmulti (e.g., ALBEF CIFAR10 AUC 47.43). PIP is only compared in Appendix E and only on VQA. The absence of stronger, general-purpose baselines (e.g., the certified-robustness / energy-based / feature-space detectors from the unimodal literature re-implemented for VLPs) inflates the apparent gap.
- *Suspiciously perfect numbers.* AUC = 100.00 with FPR95 = 0.00 appears dozens of times across CLIPCNN/ALBEF on CIFAR10/CIFAR100/STL10/Food101. Such perfection suggests the threat model — adversarial perturbations of ε = 8/255 with the [CLS] embedding as target — produces embeddings that are trivially separable, raising the question of whether the detector is catching the attack's heavy footprint rather than a subtle signal. The harder ImageNet1k numbers (e.g., ALBEF Sepmulti AUC 51.92-81.41) and the adaptive-attack drops (CLIPCNN k-NN Sepuni on Flickr30k collapses 99.99 → 67.82) are more informative and less flattering.
- *Assumption 2's Gaussian / locally-Euclidean reduction* is reasonable but is load-bearing for Lemma 1 and Theorem 1; the paper relies on citations ([27, 68]) rather than a direct empirical check that local VLP neighborhoods are Gaussian enough for the closed form to hold.
- *Adaptive attacks are evaluated only at one ζ = 0.1* and one reference-batch-mismatch setting; a fuller sweep, plus an end-to-end adaptive attack that simultaneously co-optimizes the four metrics, would be more convincing.
- *Missing ablations in the main text* (neighborhood size k, batch size, layer choice, sample availability are deferred to Appendix C), so the main-paper reader cannot judge how fragile the geometric scores are to hyperparameters.

**Significance:** A solid contribution to multimodal-model safety. The "AEs leave the manifold under anisotropic contrastive embeddings" insight is generalizable and likely to influence downstream certified-robustness and OOD-detection work on VLPs/MLLMs. The detector is genuinely lightweight (no fine-tuning, classical scores) and works on retrieval where logits are unavailable — a real practical advantage.

## Strengths

- Theoretically grounded: explicit assumptions, lemmas, theorem, and proofs — not just an empirical observation.
- Model-agnostic and task-agnostic: same framework works for aligned (CLIP) and fused (ALBEF, TCL) VLPs, and for both classification and retrieval (no labels needed for LID/k-NN).
- Fine-tuning-free and computationally cheap — minibatch estimates of LID/k-NN/KDE/Mahalanobis scale to large models.
- Honest reporting under adaptive attacks: the authors do publish the large AUC drops under different-distribution adaptive attacks rather than hiding them.
- Empirical verification of the theory (Figs. 2 and 3) is concrete: effective rank, energy in normal directions, PCA reconstruction error, and per-PCA KL divergence all line up with Theorem 1.
- Public code and standard datasets/models make reproduction feasible.

## Limitations

- Only one main-text baseline (MCM); PIP comparison is VQA-only and relegated to the appendix. General-purpose unimodal detectors (MMD-based, energy, feature-space) are not re-implemented for VLPs.
- Many "perfect" results (AUC = 100, FPR95 = 0) on easier datasets/attacks, indicating the chosen threat setting may be too easy and inflating the headline gains; the harder numbers on ImageNet1k and Sepmulti are far lower (e.g., ALBEF Sepmulti AUC 51.92 on ImageNet1k for k-NN).
- Adaptive-attack budget is narrow (ζ = 0.1, single batch-mismatch setting); a stronger, jointly-adaptive attacker across all four metrics is not evaluated.
- Assumption 2 (Gaussian/local-Euclidean embeddings) is justified by citation rather than by a direct statistical test on VLP neighborhoods; the closed-form optimal AE in Lemma 1 inherits this caveat.
- Text-only perturbations and richer text attacks (beyond 1-token BERT-Attack) are not deeply explored; the focus is overwhelmingly image-side.
- No evaluation on the very latest MLLMs (e.g., instruction-tuned LLaVA-style models); VLPs are limited to CLIP/ALBEF/TCL.
- Per-dataset hyperparameters (k, batch size differ between CLIP and ALBEF/TCL) suggest some tuning was needed, which is glossed over in the main text.

## Relevance

本文对关注多模态/视觉-语言模型鲁棒性与对抗安全、嵌入空间几何分析（各向异性、流形结构、内在维度 LID）以及 CLIP 类模型对抗防御的研究方向高度相关，提供了一个无需微调、可迁移到检索任务的轻量检测框架与可借鉴的理论分析范式。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does GeoDetect perform under stronger, jointly-adaptive white-box attacks that co-optimize all four geometric metrics (and the multimodal LID layers) simultaneously, rather than LID or k-NN alone?
- Does the expected-distance-gap theorem (Theorem 1) still hold for instruction-tuned MLLMs (LLaVA, InternVL, Qwen-VL) whose embeddings are far less contrastively aligned than CLIP/ALBEF?
- Can the perfect AUC numbers on CIFAR10/STL10 be explained by a near-trivial embedding shift at ε = 8/255 — and does the detector survive smaller, more stealthy perturbations (ε = 1/255 or 2/255)?
- Would combining GeoDetect's geometric scores with a logits-/similarity-based signal (MCM, energy) close the remaining gap on the hard ALBEF Sepmulti ImageNet1k case (currently AUC ~52-81)?
- Is the closed-form optimal AE (Lemma 1, `z'_i* = (Σ+λI)^-1 λz_i`) empirically close to the perturbations produced by PGD/Co-Attack, or is it an idealization that the real attacks only loosely approximate?
- Can the framework be extended to certified detection (a provable lower bound on the geometric score gap) rather than empirical AUC only?
