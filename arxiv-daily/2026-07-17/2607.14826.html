Key finding

Interventional Causal Circuits for Safe Robot Action Testing and Failure Recovery
Naren Vasantakumaar · Tom Schierenbeck · Michael Beetz — Inst. for AI, Univ. of Bremen

A trained Joint Probability Tree becomes an interventional causal circuit that pinpoints and one-shot corrects rejected robot plans — no retraining.

A fitted planner is promoted into a Pearl-style causal circuit via an ATEnorm-ordered MdVtree, so every rejected action triggers a backdoor-adjusted diagnosis naming the offending parameter and emitting a single corrective re-attempt — no new data, no simulator rollouts, no retraining.

02 / METHOD

Four pieces, no retraining

1

JPT planning distribution

From 1,742 NEEM execution logs, fit a Joint Probability Tree partitioning the action space into leaves with bounded support — so p(x)=0 outside training is exact, unlike Gaussian models.

2

MdVtree via ATEnorm

Rank cause variables by normalised average treatment effect; impose the order on the JPT as a Marginal-Deterministic Vtree, forcing each cause-partitioning SumUnit to have disjoint child supports.

3

Causal circuit + backdoor adj.

Marginal determinism collapses the interventional distribution to a weighted sum over non-overlapping leaf regions — exact and polytime, built from existing inference primitives. No sampling or integration.

4

Diagnose, then one-shot fix

On rejection, pick the cause variable with lowest ρi(x̂i); constrain it to [v*−δ, v*+δ] next cycle, resample the rest. Non-chaining: a failed fix is discarded — no drift into a committed wrong answer.

04 / TRADE-OFFS

What holds up, what doesn't

Strengths

  • +Reuses an already-fitted planner — no new failure classifier or failure dataset.
  • +Pre-deployment Support Determinism Verification turns "hopefully tractable" into a checked precondition; reports the exact violating SumUnit on failure.
  • +Non-chaining one-shot correction + automatic OOD exclusion prevent drifting into a committed wrong fix or extrapolating beyond support.
  • +Every rejection yields a named cause, observed value, and recommended region — rare among data-driven recovery methods.
  • +Honest two-regime framing instead of a single cherry-picked headline.

Limitations

  • −Single task, single simulator, single robot — 5 mostly-positional vars; no contact dynamics, force, or weight.
  • −No real-robot validation; sim-only, explicitly future work.
  • −Only baseline is plain-JPT blind resampling — no learned feasibility classifier, no VLM-recovery, no soft-score tester.
  • −The 37% headline is recovery from a self-corrupted sampler, not a realistic deployment gain.
  • −ATEnorm assumes additive treatment-effect ranking; may mis-order interacting causes, with no mis-ranking analysis.
