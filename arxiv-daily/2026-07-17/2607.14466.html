Interleaved Noise Injection Improves Clean, Corrupted, and OOD Performance

Matt L. Wiemann · Peter Melchior · Andrew K. Saydjari — Princeton University

A square-wave noise schedule — alternating clean and noisy epochs — lifts clean, corrupted, and OOD accuracy at zero compute cost.

Instead of monotonically decaying noise, the authors toggle it: P clean epochs, then L noisy epochs, repeated — stabilized by a gradient-norm rescaling factor. Theory ties impulse noise to input-space Jacobian regularization and Gaussian noise to curvature penalties, yielding an architecture-specific prescription.

Key metric CIFAR-100-C · WideResNet

52.6% → 36.5% mean corruption error vs AdamW baseline (AugMix + interleaved impulse).

Also: clean error 25.7%→24.3%, structural mCE 47.1%→33.9%. 5 seeds, bootstrapped SE.

Why this matters

A nearly free, add-on training trick with clear input-space regularization theory — worth trying in any pipeline, especially CNNs (impulse) or ViTs (Gaussian).

Critical edge

“Clean-accuracy deltas are small (≤1.4 pp) and largely within or near the bootstrap error of the best baselines.”

Method & Results

MMethodology

Square-wave schedule N(t) = I(t)·σ_t, with I(t)=1 for L noisy epochs every P clean epochs. Two corruption types: impulse (salt-and-pepper, σ_t up to 0.65) and Gaussian.

clean epoch (P=5) noisy epoch (L=1)
  • Theory: 2nd-order Taylor of L(θ,x̃) ⇒ impulse noise ⇒ directional Jacobian regularizer (Cauchy-Schwarz-bounded, weighted by pixel contrast) + sharpness penalty; Gaussian ⇒ uniform curvature penalty.
  • Gradient-norm stabilization: p_t = f·‖∇L(θ_{t−1},x)‖ / ‖∇L(θ_t,x̃)‖, f≈0.4 — rescales noisy updates to the last clean magnitude.
  • Defaults: P=5, L=1, f=0.4, σ=0.65 (impulse). WideResNet/CIFAR-100 from scratch; ResNet50 & ViT-B/16 fine-tuned on ImageNet-1k.

Strengths & Limitations

+Strengths

  • Genuinely zero extra compute — no extra forward/backward passes, unlike SAM (2×), AugMix, VIPAug.
  • Strong statistical protocol — 5 seeds + bootstrapped SE, rare in the augmentation literature.
  • Clean theory tying impulse noise to input-space Jacobian + Hessian regularization, with an explicit Cauchy-Schwarz bound and contrast-weighting reading.
  • Actionable prescription — impulse-for-CNN, Gaussian-for-ViT — backed by saliency/attention visualizations.
  • Additive on top of existing regularizers; practitioners need not choose.
  • Robust hyperparameters — wide plateaus over P and f (Figs. 5–6, Table 6).

!Limitations

  • ImageNet is fine-tuning only from pretrained weights — from-scratch training untested.
  • Dated baseline set — only Cutout (2017), AugMix (2019), SAM (2020), VIPAug (2024); no RandAugment, TrivialAugment, Mixup/CutMix, or adversarial training.
  • Classification only — no detection, segmentation, or non-vision tasks.
  • P=5 chosen by best clean accuracy biases headline numbers; a full sweep would shrink the clean deltas.
  • VIPAug numbers anomalous (underperforms SGD on ResNet50) and uninvestigated.
  • No code release mentioned.
  • Theory gaps: off-diagonal Hessian terms dropped without a residual bound; no theory explains why interleaving beats monotonic decay — that rests on Fig. 2 empirics.
