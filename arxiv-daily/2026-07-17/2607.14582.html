MathCoPilot: An Interactive System for Human-AI Symbiotic Mathematical Research

Key finding

Writing the natural-language proof first doubles Lean strict pass rate — 15.87% → 31.75% — and autoformalization quality beats raw model size.

A human-in-the-loop system whose editable "living proof blueprint" lets a mathematician steer strategy while five Lean-4 agents discharge the details. Benchmarked on a strict FormalMATH subset and two real PDE theorems. Jump to results ↓

Why this matters

A high-quality empirical probe of frontier LLMs — Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT-5.4, Claude Opus 4.7 — on research-grade math, including numerical PDE. Directly informs research agents, formal-math benchmarks, and AI-as-reviewer workflows.

Critical edge

The FormalMATH sample is only 21 problems, with several domains at 0/18 — so the headline "NL doubles FF" should be read with wide error bars, and per-cell deltas are statistically fragile.

01Methodology

Three integrated capabilities: an editable proof graph, a feedback-driven skill orchestrator, and an auto-built personal knowledge base.

  • Living proof blueprint. Strategy is decomposed into a clickable node graph (lemma / case / claim) with NL intent and status. The mathematician points at a node rather than steering through dialogue; AI agents discharge the details. See results ↓.
  • Five-agent loop. Brainstorm → Blueprint → Prover → Formalizer → Aggregation. Hard nodes can be recursed (re-applying the whole pipeline to a single node, to arbitrary depth) — the mechanism behind the recovery experiment.
  • Skill orchestration. A pool of NL-first, formalization-first, knowledge-grounded skills; parses Lean feedback (type mismatch, missing hypothesis, unsolved goal) and switches dynamically.
  • Auto knowledge base. Topic-driven retrieval (Semantic Scholar / arXiv / Mathlib) → dependency-ordered autoformalization → only Lean-verified entries retained; stores both Lean code and NL reasoning.

03Strengths

Where the work is genuinely solid.

  • Honest strict verification. No sorry, no empty proofs — catches an otherwise-inflated Opus number (6/21 → 3/21 on FF).
  • Clean recovery experiment. 9/12 → 10/12 on GPT-5.4 misses proves the interactive blueprint is additive beyond single-pass prompting.
  • Domain-rich case studies. DG methods, Gauss-Radau projection, Bramble-Hilbert — authored by people who know the numerics, not toy algebra.
  • Review > construction. The wrong-proof diagnostic isolates critique as a distinct, easier skill, with concrete planted errors.

04Limitations read with care

What the headline numbers do not say.

  • Tiny FormalMATH sample (n=21) with three domains at 0/18; "NL doubles FF" is a real but noisy headline.
  • PDE "100%" is partly circular. Opus formalizes statements into opaque/axiom blocks, then verifies assembly — deep analysis is offloaded to unproved assumptions.
  • No user study despite the central "symbiotic workflow improves productivity" thesis; deferred to future work.
  • Orchestrator "learning" is asserted, never quantified — no ablation vs. always-NL-first or random skill selection.
  • Artifacts not released: system prompts, agent logic, and the showcased Main.lean ("Lean listing unavailable").
