> **一句话总结：** 作者提出一个轻量、可审计的状态机式摄像头篡改监测子系统，仅用采样亮度/梯度统计与"纯净参考窗口"对比来检测镜头遮挡与突变式场景切换；在 320 条受控序列上达到 0.800 F1 / 0.822 平衡准确率，相比最强基线（直方图差异）配对正确率显著更高（McNemar p=0.0024），并在 9.09 摄像头小时已验证负样本审计中零误报。

> **TL;DR:** A deliberately narrow, auditable streaming camera-tamper detector (no learned models, no registration) uses clean-only sliding luminance/gradient references plus mode/rapid-brightness and coarse-grid structured-lighting gates to flag only two fault signatures: texture-collapsing lens occlusion and abrupt photometric scene transition. On 320 in-scope controlled sequences it reaches 0.800 F1 / 0.822 balanced accuracy, beating the strongest baseline (histogram difference, 0.777 F1) with significantly better paired correctness (McNemar p=0.0024), and on the Xiph magnitude-swept audit it wins the partial-AUC ranking at FPR≤0.05 (0.494 vs. 0.422 for gradient collapse); a 9.09-camera-hour VIRAT+CDnet2014 verified-negative audit records zero false alarms.

## 核心贡献

- **一个"纯净参考"流式状态机**：针对两类被显式声明的视觉传感器故障特征（纹理坍塌式镜头遮挡、突变式光度场景切换）构造可检测的状态机；任意被判为篡改的帧绝不进入参考窗口，避免了基线被污染。
- **实现一致的谓词形式化**：把均值跳变、梯度坍塌、32×32 粗网格、抑制门、单次告警-每-事件（episode）的策略逐项写成可复现的决策谓词（Eq. 9–11、Eq. 13），并为每个告警帧附加诊断元数据 `(µ_t, g_t, µ*_t, g*_t, δ_t, L_t, s_t, τ_t)` 以便现场排查。
- **配置一致性定理**：证明当 `T_sup>0 且 θ_rapid ≤ θ_chg` 时，场景切换路径会被快速亮度抑制窗口在触发帧上确定性屏蔽；给出可达性规则 `T_sup=0 ∨ θ_rapid > θ_chg`。
- **误报受限（false-alarm-constrained）评估协议**：用 5 个配对轻量基线（均值跳变、梯度坍塌、边缘代理、帧差、直方图差异）、定向消融和跨公开数据诊断（Xiph、Bremen IoT、UHCTD、VIRAT、CDnet2014）共同评估；明确把"声明作用域"（in-scope）与"域外/边界诊断"分开，避免不同指标被混为一谈。
- **部署边界诚实标注**：用 4.5M 帧的 UHCTD 全解码诊断和跨摄像头微调实验，明确指出固定光度谓词在 moved-camera/defocus 上几乎失效（UHCTD 内域 0.409 召回 vs 域外 0.016），并指出何时需要切换到配准/标定/学习型参考。

## Key Contributions

- A clean-reference streaming state machine for two explicitly declared vision-sensor fault signatures (texture-collapsing occlusion, abrupt photometric scene transition); frames flagged as tampered never enter the sliding reference, so a sustained occlusion cannot be silently absorbed into the baseline.
- An implementation-faithful formalization of the mean, gradient, coarse-grid, suppression, and episode predicates with per-alarm diagnostic metadata, designed for field triage rather than just classification.
- A configuration-consistency rule (with a short proof in the supplement) showing that the rapid-brightness suppression window deterministically masks the scene-transition path whenever `T_sup>0 ∧ θ_rapid ≤ θ_chg`, yielding the safe condition `T_sup=0 ∨ θ_rapid > θ_chg`.
- A false-alarm-constrained evaluation protocol that pairs the detector against five deployable transparent baselines (mean jump, gradient collapse, edge-reference proxy, frame difference, histogram difference) and separates primary in-scope metrics from boundary/transfer diagnostics so non-comparable scopes are not pooled.
- An honest deployment-boundary analysis on 4.5M UHCTD decoded frames, showing that fixed photometric predicates concentrate recall inside the declared envelope (UHCTD in-scope recall 0.409 vs. 0.016 out of scope), and that camera-disjoint transfer is where calibration or learned references become necessary.

## Methodology

The detector processes grayscale frames `I_t ∈ [0,255]^{H×W}` and emits `y_t = (d_t, c_t, z_t, m_t)` where `d_t` is the alarm, `c_t` is a cue magnitude (not a calibrated posterior), `z_t ∈ {no tamper, occlusion event, scene transition}`, and `m_t` is diagnostic metadata. It maintains a window of at most N=12 frames accepted as clean, holding a scalar reference `(µ*_t, g*_t)` and a per-cell mean on a 32×32 grid.

**Sampled statistics.** Mean is sampled every fourth column on every row (O(HW/4)); the gradient proxy samples every fourth pixel on both axes and takes immediate right/lower neighbor differences (O(HW/16)), deliberately avoiding aliasing a 1-pixel texture into a flat image.

**Two detection paths.**
- *Photometric scene-transition path* (Eq. 9): fires when the inter-frame mean jump `δ_t > θ_chg=80`, combined with a transition in the texture-collapse indicator (tested against the *current frozen* reference) and a gradient-change-ratio check `q_t ≥ ρ_grad=0.2`. An auxiliary luminance route additionally compares against the last accepted clean mean with relative ratio `ρ_lumin=0.8`.
- *Gradient-collapse occlusion path* (Eq. 10): `g_t < θ_occ·g*_t (θ_occ=0.45)` *and* a bounded mean shift `20 < |µ_t − µ*_t| ≤ ∆_max=45` *and* `µ_t ∈ [60,120]`. The lower bound rejects trivial luminance drift; the upper bound and absolute interval reject extreme environmental transitions, so mean-preserving defocus is *not* guaranteed to fire.

**Negative gates.**
- A 32×32 coarse-grid gate (Eq. 11) acts only as a *negative* occlusion reject: it uses cell-change fraction `r_20`, block standard deviation `σ̄_b`, and flat-block fraction `r_flat` to identify structured-lighting changes that satisfy the scalar occlusion predicate but should not alarm.
- The mode gate (day–night flag change) and rapid-brightness gate (`δ_t > θ_rapid`) suppress scene output for `T_sup`; these do not suppress an occlusion that independently satisfies Eq. 10.

**Episode state.** Two binary episode states select per-frame or once-per-episode emission; a clean decision ends the episode and resumes clean-only reference updates; scene decisions precede primary occlusion decisions. Three invariants make the system auditable: reference safety (no alarming frame ever enters references), episode non-duplication, and scene-path reachability (Eq. 12). The standalone C++ implementation runs 31,400 fps at 160×90 and 1,270 fps at 1280×720 on commodity x86-64.

**Evaluation protocol.** Eight background videos × 10 clips × 5 deterministic conditions = 400 sequences at 160×90/48 frames with seed 20260701; cover (24–40 level mean shift) and photometric replacement (95 level shift) are in-scope positives; clean and 70-level illumination step are negatives; an 18°/12%/8% geometric shift is an out-of-scope diagnostic.

## Results & Highlights

- **Controlled primary scope (Table II, 320 in-scope sequences).** The full state machine reaches **F1 0.800 and balanced accuracy 0.822 at FPR 0.069**, versus the strongest baseline histogram difference at **F1 0.777 / BAcc 0.713 at FPR 0.575**, and frame difference at F1 0.750 / FPR 0.656. The state machine's F1 advantage over histogram difference is **0.023 with 95% CI [−0.041, 0.085]** (paired bootstrap, 10,000 resamples), so it is *not* statistically resolved on F1, but paired correctness is significantly better (McNemar 81 vs. 46 discordants, **p=0.0024**).
- **Geometric-shift boundary diagnostic.** The state machine alarms on **3/80 (0.038)** geometric-shift sequences versus **77/80 (0.963)** for frame difference — explicit evidence that moved-camera detection requires a registration cue that purely photometric predicates cannot supply.
- **Magnitude-swept public Xiph audit (250 sequences, FPR≤0.05 budget).** The state machine attains the **highest normalized partial AUC of 0.494**, ahead of gradient collapse (0.422), histogram difference (0.394), edge-reference proxy (0.380), mean jump (0.338), and frame difference (0.272).
- **Extended-stress FPR-constrained sweep (360 sequences).** Reaches **recall 0.925 at FPR 0.025** (111 TP / 6 FP / 234 TN / 9 FN) with `θ_chg=150, θ_occ=0.50`, which the authors treat as a sensitivity diagnostic, not a held-out operating point.
- **Component-targeted ablations.** Removing rapid-brightness suppression raises rapid-brightness false alarms from **0.217 → 0.767** and introduces day–night false alarms at 0.233; the coarse-grid gate suppresses *all* structured-light false alarms that fire without it; clean-only reference maintenance preserves a sustained-cover alarm that all-frame updating silently absorbs.
- **Onset latency.** Median and P90 latency of **0 frames**, maximum 10, mean 0.64 over 103 detected positives.
- **UHCTD full decoded-video pass (4.5M frames).** State machine recall **0.028 (Camera A) / 0.021 (Camera B)** at FPR 0.049/0.039 — far below official AlexNet baselines (recall 0.86–0.88 at FPR 0.28–0.29, which cannot be re-thresholded). Envelope stratification: in-scope covers reach **recall 0.667 (6/9, 95% CI [0.30, 0.93])** versus **0.016 (6/382, [0.006, 0.034])** for out-of-scope events — non-overlapping intervals.
- **Camera-disjoint transfer failure.** A ResNet18 fine-tuned on Camera A reaches recall **0.890 at FPR 0.049** same-camera (F1 0.889 at best-F1) but collapses to **0.000 recall** on Camera B at FPR≤0.05 — calibration does not cross cameras.
- **Long-negative public audit.** Zero false alarms in **9.09 camera-hours** across VIRAT (329 recordings) and four CDnet2014 categories; one-sided 95% Poisson bound <0.33 FA/camera-hour. By contrast histogram difference reaches 46.1 FA/h on CDnet2014.
- **Independent Bremen IoT still-image cross-check.** Occlusion predicate holds normal-image FPR to **0.000–0.009** under untuned camera-disjoint transfer, with recall concentrated on the obstructed-lens class (per-category alarm 0.195–0.386); the three reference-based baselines collapse to FPR 0.15–1.00 in the camera-disjoint direction.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are **Bo Ma** and **Wei Qi Yan** (Auckland University of Technology, New Zealand) and **Jinsong Wu** (Guilin University of Electronic Technology, China); Bo Ma is the corresponding author. The framing is consistent with a sensor-reliability / embedded-analytics research line, citing the self-validating sensor literature (Henry & Clarke 1993) and IoT sensor-fault surveys, not just the camera-tamper niche. The paper is unusually careful about evaluation hygiene: it explicitly distinguishes the prediction `d_t` from the clip-level ground-truth label, requires independent confirmation for true negatives (rather than treating "detector did not alarm" as evidence of negativity), publishes exact McNemar p-values and paired-bootstrap CIs, reports Clopper–Pearson intervals on small cells, and labels the FPR-constrained threshold sweep as a *sensitivity diagnostic* rather than a held-out operating point. The companion supplementary material provides exact predicates, the suppression-consistency proof, all per-method tables, and a disclosure that the primary benchmark's background video is not yet public (with a public-data equivalent in Section V-D).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (moderate, honestly scoped).** The scalar cues (mean, gradient, histogram, frame difference) are individually old; the novelty is the *composition*: clean-only reference maintenance so an active alarm cannot poison the baseline, a coarse-grid *negative* gate against structured lighting, mode/rapid-brightness suppression, and once-per-episode emission. The contribution is closer to a verifiable sensor-health subsystem than a new classifier — and the paper says so plainly.
- **Rigor (high).** The statistical analysis is the strongest part: exact McNemar on discordant pairs, paired-bootstrap F1 CIs, Clopper–Pearson intervals on small UHCTD cells, and an explicit admission that the headline F1 margin (0.023, CI [−0.041, 0.085]) is *not* statistically resolved even though paired correctness is (p=0.0024). Many camera-tamper papers report a single F1 with no test; this one tells you exactly what it can and cannot claim.
- **Baseline fairness (the main weakness).** The five baselines are deliberately chosen to be "transparent, deployable without training" constituent cues — mean jump, gradient collapse, an *admittedly inexact* Sobel edge proxy "not claimed as an exact reproduction of [Lee et al.]", frame difference, and histogram difference. Complete published systems like Saglam & Temizel's adaptive scheme and Mantini & Shah's deep-feature UHCTD models are *not* run head-to-head on the controlled benchmark; UHCTD's released AlexNet predictions (recall 0.86–0.88) are shown only as an external reference and "cannot be re-thresholded to FPR≤0.05" because the release provides hard labels. So the headline "best F1 at much lower FPR" is rigorously established against scalar cues, but not against the strongest end-to-end systems.
- **Cherry-picking / scope hygiene (good).** The paper does *not* pool non-comparable scopes: it separates primary in-scope metrics from out-of-scope boundary diagnostics and labels the magnitude-swept and extended-stress sweeps as sensitivity diagnostics, explicitly warning that "absolute recall is necessarily lower than in Table II" once perturbation magnitudes cross the configured envelope. The UHCTD result is openly reported as *near-zero recall* (0.021–0.028) rather than buried.
- **Missing ablations.** The clean-only reference update and coarse-grid rejection produced *no measurable change* on the 5-condition controlled grid — the authors isolate those design choices with targeted probes instead, but a reader might wish for the negative-grid result to be a primary-table finding rather than a stress-probe one. There is also no ablation isolating the contribution of the auxiliary luminance route on the public-data audits (only on the controlled grid, where disabling it trades recall for the lowest FPR 0.025).
- **Data quirk / red flag.** The primary benchmark uses eight background videos with fixed synthetic magnitudes near the configured operating point (cover mean shift 24–40, scene shift 95, illumination step 70) — a generator–detector coupling that the authors themselves flag as a risk and address with the magnitude-swept audit. The in-scope UHCTD covered cell is 9 events; the in-scope moved cell is only 3 events and the authors warn it "should not be over-read." The supplementary operational pools (Table XIII) are admitted to use surrogate labels and excluded from the main false-positive evidence — a rare and welcome disclosure.
- **Reproducibility.** All evaluations use public data (Xiph, Bremen IoT, UHCTD, VIRAT, CDnet2014) or a fully specified synthetic protocol with fixed seed 20260701; the only unreleased piece is the primary benchmark's background video, for which the public-data Xiph equivalent is provided. Exact predicates, configuration table, and statistical procedures are in the supplement; the release artifact is promised to include scripts reproducing every public-data table and figure.

## Strengths

- Genuinely low false-alarm operation, demonstrated across four independent sampling regimes (controlled synthetic, Xiph public synthetic, real UHCTD decoded video, VIRAT+CDnet2014 long-negative) and triangulated rather than pooled.
- Excellent statistical hygiene: paired McNemar, paired bootstrap CIs, Clopper–Pearson intervals, honest separation of "paired correctness significant" from "F1 margin not resolved."
- Bounded arithmetic (O(HW/4) mean, O(HW/16) gradient, fixed 32² grid) and an auditable per-frame metadata record (Eq. 13) make it usable as a gate in an embedded pipeline; 31,400 fps at 160×90 on commodity CPU.
- The configuration-consistency theorem (Eq. 12) is a small but real formal contribution — it gives a deterministic check that the operator has not configured the suppressors to mask the scene path.
- Scope honesty is exemplary: the paper says *what it does not do* (no registration, no learned reference, no claim for moved-camera or mean-preserving defocus) on the first page and reinforces it in every section.

## Limitations

- **Narrow positive scope by construction.** Pure camera rotation/translation, mean-preserving defocus, and subtle local partial covers are explicitly out of scope; the geometric-shift diagnostic shows the state machine alarms on only 3/80 such cases while a registration-based method would catch them.
- **Fixed thresholds are camera-dependent.** The UHCTD camera-disjoint transfer diagnostic (Table IX) shows thresholds selected on one camera do not transfer reliably to the other in either direction; field deployment needs per-camera calibration that the paper does not provide.
- **Baseline coverage gap.** No head-to-head comparison on the controlled benchmark against the strongest published end-to-end systems (Saglam & Temizel, Mantini & Shah deep models); UHCTD's released AlexNet baselines are referenced externally but cannot be re-thresholded into the FPR≤0.05 budget.
- **No physically executed, continuously annotated real-camera tamper dataset.** The authors explicitly note this is missing — UHCTD injects tampering synthetically and Bremen IoT provides physical tampers only as still images — so a scripted multi-camera field study remains future work.
- **Primary benchmark background video is unreleased** (with a public-data Xiph equivalent as substitute); the controlled evaluation also uses fixed synthetic perturbation magnitudes near the operating point.
- **The headline F1 advantage is not statistically significant** (CI overlaps zero); the paper's defensible claim is on paired correctness and false-alarm control, not aggregate F1.
- **Two confirmed false negatives** in the operational audit both share the same predicate weakness: close-range covers whose mean shift falls outside the bounded `∆_max` interval. The proposed remedy (edge-density terminal-texture floor) is not yet in the production predicate.

## Relevance

对监控视频异常检测、传感器健康监测与流式低误报检测感兴趣时，本文提供了一种可审计、可形式化、低算力的工程化范式，其"纯净参考 + 显式负向门控 + 误报受限评估协议"的设计思路对任何需要在边缘端做实时传感器完整性闸门（sensor-integrity gate）的任务都有借鉴价值，尤其是其严格的统计配对检验与作用域声明方法论可直接迁移到其他流式异常检测工作的评估中。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How would the state machine perform against the actual Saglam & Temizel adaptive scheme and a re-trainable Mantini & Shah deep-feature reference on the *same* 400 controlled sequences at FPR≤0.05, rather than only against scalar constituent cues?
- Can a lightweight registration cue (ORB/SIFT homography-failure) be added as a third positive path *without* exploding clean-clip FPR (the paper's own ORB probe shows 0.50 FPR on clean clips), and would that close the moved-camera gap without breaking the auditable-state budget?
- The camera-disjoint threshold transfer fails on UHCTD; would per-camera percentile calibration (instead of absolute thresholds) recover transferability while preserving the FPR≤0.05 budget?
- The bounded mean-shift interval `∆_max` rejects close-range covers whose mean shift exceeds it — does adding a color-agnostic terminal-texture floor (edge-density / high-frequency energy) recover those events without converting illumination transients into occlusion alarms?
- Is the F1 margin over histogram difference genuinely zero once N is increased beyond 320 sequences, or does the paired-bootstrap CI shrink with a larger controlled sweep? The current CI [−0.041, 0.085] is too wide to settle the headline claim.
- How does the detector behave under adversarial tamper that is *designed* to stay inside the accepted envelope (e.g., a near-uniform occluder whose mean shift is engineered into [20,45] and whose texture fully collapses) — i.e., what is the worst-case recall under an adversary who has read this paper?
