Position / Theory · Uncertainty Quantification

Subjective Risk Decomposition: a new view for uncertainty quantification

Under any strictly proper loss, epistemic and aleatoric uncertainties are not primitives to be axiomatised — they are decomposition terms that fall out of a reverse bias–variance–entropy split of the expected subjective risk.

§ Methodology

The machinery, in six moves.

A purely theoretical position paper. Its engine is the Bregman-divergence bias–variance decomposition (Pfau 2025, Heskes 2026) applied to subjective — not objective — risk.

  1. Flip the risk. Subjective risk Rq(z)=𝔼Ŷ∼q[ℓ(z,Ŷ)] evaluates the true distribution under the model's belief — answering "how realistic is this model?" not "how correct?".
  2. Invoke properness. Under a strictly proper loss, Rq(z) = Bϕ(q,z) + Hℓ(q), splitting into an asymmetric Bregman divergence plus generalized entropy.
  3. Take the expectation. Averaging over θ yields the three-term Theorem 2: bias + variance + entropy, read off as bias / epistemic / aleatoric.
  4. Instantiate by loss. Log-loss on categoricals → Gal (2016); log-loss on unknown-mean Gaussians → Depeweg (2018); squared loss on Bernoullis → Sale (2024).
  5. Read the curvature. ∇²ϕ near the simplex boundary governs disagreement: log loss blows up (hallucination-sensitive), Brier stays flat (insensitive).
  6. Bridge to SLT. Define subjective excess risk and split it into a possibly-negative approximation gap plus a non-negative estimation error.

§ Special cases recovered

Three familiar measures, one derivation each.

Choosing a different strictly proper loss or hypothesis class produces a known UQ proposal as a special case — the "consequence, not primitive" thesis made concrete.

Example 1

Gal (2016) — information-theoretic

Log-loss on categorical posteriors. The variance + entropy terms of the reverse cross-entropy decomposition are exactly I(Ŷ;Θ) + H(Ŷ|Θ).

→ yields MI + conditional entropy, + a bias Bϕ(q̄,p)
Example 2

Depeweg (2018) — Gaussian

Log-loss on unknown-mean/variance Gaussians. The decomposition recovers Depeweg's differential-entropy information-theoretic terms.

→ yields Gaussian differential entropy UQ
Example 3

Sale (2024) — label-wise

Squared (Brier) loss on Bernoullis. Curvature is constant; the variance term specialises to Sale et al.'s per-label uncertainty.

→ yields label-wise uncertainty
Appendix B — also covered

Deep Ensembles and the broader variance-vs-entropy family are shown to fit the same mould, extending the catalogue beyond Examples 1–3.

§ Bridge to statistical learning theory

The most novel move: connecting UQ to approximation–estimation.

Subjective analogues of the classical SLT decomposition give a principled answer to "what is MI actually estimating?" — and the answer is sharper than the field assumes.

Th. 4
Subjective estimation error

Non-negative decomposition component. Under log-loss its variance piece equals Gal's MI (Theorem 5).

Cor. 6
MI is a lower bound

MI captures only the posterior-disagreement piece of subjective estimation error — a systematic under-estimate of true epistemic uncertainty.

Prop. 3
Asymptotic agreement

Subjective and objective best-in-class models coincide when the predictive mixture is consistent, q̄n → p.

§ Critical Assessment

Genuinely new framing — but practice-deficient.

+ Strengths

  • Single-principle unification of MI, EPKL, RMI, Modified/Reverse-Modified Bregman, label-wise, Gaussian info-theoretic.
  • A third term surfaces — the systematic bias Bϕ(q̄,p) that the AU+EU view silently drops. A genuinely useful diagnostic.
  • The SLT bridge is the most impactful part — "MI is a lower bound" reframes a widespread mis-reading.
  • Honest engagement with the live 2025–2026 debate (Bickford-Smith, Jimenez, Sale, Kirchhof). Clean appendix proofs.

− Red flags / overclaiming

  • No empirical validation that subjective risk is a better practical UQ target. The LLM-hallucination / RLHF hook of §3.2 is dropped after motivation.
  • "Resolved" overclaim (§5.2) — the paper offers a re-interpretation, not a demonstration of better calibration in practice.
  • No new estimator the framework enables that prior ad-hoc proposals missed. Unification strong; generative power undemonstrated.
  • Finite-expectation assumptions buried in proof footnotes; boundary/infinite-RMI cases flagged in §5.4 but not handled.
  • Fishkov (2025) comparison — the methodological differentiator is articulated only verbally, not via a worked example.
Relevance · cs.LG / stat.ML / cs.AI UQ is central to trustworthy ML. This paper offers a unifying theoretical frame for epistemic/aleatoric uncertainty and a directly actionable warning — mutual information is a lower bound on epistemic uncertainty — of concrete value to anyone in Bayesian deep learning, selective prediction, RLHF, or LLM-hallucination research.

Open questions the framework invites

  1. Can it generate a new estimator that empirically beats MI on OOD (CIFAR vs SVHN) or selective-prediction AURC on ImageNet?
  2. How large is the MI↔true-estimation-error gap (Corollary 6) in practice — negligible for well-specified ensembles, or material for under-trained BMA?
  3. Can the §3.2 reverse-KL motivation become a concrete subjective-risk hallucination detector on TruthfulQA / HaluEval?
  4. What happens for non-proper losses (hinge, 0-1) — does the decomposition extend, or simply not apply?
  5. Can the (possibly negative) subjective approximation gap serve as a data-driven model-selection criterion?
  6. Finite-sample rates for Proposition 3 — connectable to PAC-Bayes or posterior-contraction bounds?
