M.01

The one-bit experiment

Not a new system — a tightly controlled causal probe. Fix everything, vary one bit: whether the gold answer is shown during chain generation.

1
Fixed backboneFull-parameter SFT of Qwen3-8B, 1 epoch, seq len 10,240, lr 2×10⁻⁵, batch 48, DeepSpeed ZeRO-3. Eval in vLLM thinking mode, 32K-token budget, symbolic answer match. Base = 95.2.
2
Fixed data935 math problems from Nemotron-Cascade-SFT, intersected to problems both arms solve, disjoint from MATH-500. First correct chain kept per problem — both corpora pass the same filter on identical inputs.
3
One interventionBlind prompt: problem only. Leaked prompt: appends gold answer a(q) and asks for a trace that reaches it. Penalty Δ = acc(S_blind) − acc(S_leaked).
4
Mechanism probesAnswer-first rate (AFR), prompt ablations (Derive-first / Final-check / Out-of-band), length-matched control, 4K–32K budget sweep, within-corpus 2×2 difference-in-differences.
5
Cross-model signature∆AFR = AFR_leaked − AFR_blind from a few unlabeled generations, regressed against the observed penalty across 8 thinking models from 4 families. Predictions recorded before training each model.
M.02

The mechanism

Visible answers push the model to state the answer early, then rationalize. The habit propagates into the student's own outputs.

  • Median first-answer position shifts from 0.587 (blind) to 0.297 (leaked) — the conclusion moves to the front of the chain.−0.29
  • Answer-first rate rises +20.8 pp (26.6% → 47.4%); student-output AFR gap survives a correctness-controlled subset at +9.4 pp (KS D=0.291, p < 10⁻³⁴).+20.8pp
  • Length inversion: leaked training chains are shorter, yet the leaked student emits longer test chains while scoring worse — plus a termination deficit (7.4 vs 0.4 failures/100).
  • Prompt ablation (Table 1A) shows the rationalize-toward instruction — not bare visibility — carries ~15 of the 16 points. Derive-first recovers most of it; Out-of-band nearly all.
Why this is causally clean

The length-matched control truncates each blind chain to its matched leaked length and still scores 95.0 — 15.4 points above Leaked. Length is not the cause. The within-corpus DiD nets out problem difficulty by splitting leaked chains at the 0.2 AFR threshold and using matched blind subsets as controls.

Carrier experiment

  • Leaked-Early (438 chains, AFR > 0.2): 64.2 — vs Leaked-Late 86.2. DiD three-seed mean +16.9 (range 12–21, p < 10⁻⁴).DiD +16.9
  • Excising only the single answer-stating line (≈302 chars / 1.4% of each chain) lifts Leaked-Early to 88.8, recovering 24.6 of the 29.8-point gap (83%).83% recovered
  • Prompt ablation: Rationalize-toward loses 16.2; Derive-first loses only 4.3; Final-check 1.3; Out-of-band 0.7.
  • Length-matched blind control still scores 95.0 — 15.4 above Leaked. Length is not the cause.
R.03

Generalization

  • Code domain MBPP+ 68.3 vs 58.8 (−9.5); HumanEval+ 72.8 vs 63.6 (−9.2).−9.5 MBPP+
  • Cross-family teachers: Nemotron −14.8 (94.0 vs 79.2); GLM −10.2 (93.0 vs 82.8).
  • Cross-student (1.7B trained on 8B corpora): −6.9, but σ = 8.9 — a sign claim, not a magnitude.
  • No derivation ⇒ no penalty: MMLU +0.8, GPQA-Diamond −2.6 — both indistinguishable from zero across seeds.
  • Practical payoff: answer-blind self-distillation (95.1) beats gold-data SFT (90.8) at a matched 10,240-token budget.
A.01

Strengths & limitations

Exemplary experimental control with honest soft spots. The headline is firm; many generalization rows support the sign of the effect, not its magnitude.

+ Strengths
  • Exemplary control. The one-bit design isolates a single variable; a length-matched arm closes the most obvious alternative explanation.
  • Actionable mechanism. AFR is a one-line diagnostic computable from unlabeled generations; the carrier experiment pinpoints a single line as 83% of the gap.
  • Cheap screening. ∆AFR (r=0.96, LOFO MAE 2.9) turns an expensive negative result into a pre-training diagnostic.
  • Honest nulls. Reporting MMLU/GPQA where the effect vanishes sharpens rather than weakens the mechanism story.
  • Beats gold-data SFT. Answer-blind self-distillation wins at matched budget — immediate practical value.
  • Reproducibility beyond code. Pinned correctness-critical deps, per-cell manifest, pre-recorded held-out predictions.
! Limitations
  • Single student family for the headline. One-bit uses Qwen3-8B on 935 problems; cross-model fit is n=8 with a wide Fisher-z CI.
  • Many rows are single-seed (cross-family Nemotron/GLM, both length controls, derive-first arm) — sign only, not magnitude.
  • Phi-4-reasoning discarded. Largest signature (∆AFR +45.5) unmeasured; the fit excludes the extreme anchor.
  • Cross-student least stable (σ=8.9, one seed at −0.2).
  • Code & student-output transfers not length-controlled.
  • Post-hoc thresholds (0.2 AFR; 20% first-block window), softened by robustness sweeps.
  • No RL/RLHF setting tested — confined to supervised distillation of thinking models.
Authors' own framing

The work is positioned as a careful empirical/methodological contribution, not a system push. The authors explicitly call their claims "interpolation over the measured range" rather than extrapolation, and acknowledge that several generalization rows rest on single seeds — flagging the cross-student penalty's σ=8.9 in Appendix I. Three training seeds {42, 123, 7777} back every headline number.
