> **一句话总结：** 通过控制变量实验（同一生成器、同一题集、同一正确性过滤、同一 SFT 配方，仅切换是否向生成器展示答案），论文证明让模型在看到金答案后"反向合理化"出的 CoT 即使最终答案正确也会显著伤害学生模型的可验证推理能力（Qwen3-8B 在 MATH-500 上损失 16.2 分、AIME 上高达 27.2 分），且该伤害可由训练前的"答案前置率"签名（∆AFR）在 8 个模型 4 个家族上以 r=0.96 预测，标准正确性过滤无法察觉。

> **TL;DR:** In a tightly controlled "one-bit experiment" that fixes the generator (Qwen3-8B), problem set (935 matched math problems from Nemotron-Cascade-SFT), correctness filter, and SFT recipe, generating chains-of-thought under answer-conditioning (showing the gold answer and asking the model to reach it) costs 16.2 MATH-500 points vs. answer-blind self-distillation (78.9 vs. 95.1), and the gap widens to 27.2 points on olympiad AIME 2024-2025 (44.4 vs. 71.7). The harm is carried by "rationalized" chains that state the answer early instead of deriving it, transfers across teacher families (Nemotron teacher: −14.8; GLM teacher: −10.2) and into the code domain (MBPP+: 58.8 vs. 68.3), and is invisible to the standard final-answer correctness filter — but it is predicted before any fine-tuning by an answer-first-rate signature (∆AFR) at r=0.96 across 8 thinking models from 4 families (leave-one-family-out MAE 2.9 points). A derive-first instruction recovers ~2/3 of the penalty; answer-blind generation at a matched budget beats gold-data SFT (95.1 vs. 90.8).

## 核心贡献

- **因果隔离"答案泄漏"的代价：** 设计"one-bit 实验"，固定生成器、题集（935 道匹配数学题）、正确性过滤器与 SFT 配方，只切换生成时是否向模型展示金答案，从而把 16.2 MATH-500 分的损失干净地归因到"答案条件化生成"这一单一变量上。
- **定位机制（答案前置 + 训练/测试长度反转）：** 揭示可见答案把推理链推向"先抛结论再合理化"——金答案首次出现位置从 0.587 提前到 0.297，答案前置率（AFR）上升 20.8 pp；该习惯迁移到学生自身的回答中，并造成诡异的"训练链短、测试链反而长"的长度反转与终止缺陷（leaked 学生在 7.4/100 题上无法在预算内给出可抽取答案，blind 仅 0.4）。
- **在单一语料内归因并部分修复：** 通过 within-corpus 2×2 的 difference-in-differences 设计证明 "Leaked-Early"（答案前置链）几乎承载全部伤害（DiD = +16.9 分，三种子均值）；删除链中"首次陈述答案那一行"（仅约 1.4% 字符）即可恢复 24.6/29.8 分（83%）。
- **训练前预测惩罚：** 仅用一小撮未标注生成计算的 ∆AFR 签名，能在 4 个家族 8 个思维模型上以 r=0.96（permutation p=0.0003）排序惩罚大小，留一家族预测 MAE 仅 2.9 分——意味着候选教师可在花任何训练算力前被筛查。
- **给出可立即落地的廉价补救：** 优先"答案盲生成"（base 95.2 → blind 95.1，无损且超过 gold-data SFT 的 90.8）；当必须给答案时，"先推导再揭示"指令恢复约 2/3 损失；按 AFR 过滤掉合理化链为部分修复。

## Key Contributions

- **Causal isolation of the answer-leakage cost via the "one-bit experiment":** same generator (Qwen3-8B), same 935 matched problems, same symbolic-answer correctness filter, same SFT recipe — only whether the gold answer is shown during generation is varied — cleanly attributing a 16.2-point MATH-500 drop (and a 27.2-point AIME drop) to answer-conditioning alone.
- **Mechanism traced to early-answer rationalization and a train/test length inversion:** visible answer shifts the median first-answer position from 0.587 to 0.297 and raises the answer-first rate by 20.8 pp; the habit propagates into the student's own outputs and produces a striking inversion (leaked training chains are shorter, yet the leaked student emits longer test chains while scoring worse) plus a termination deficit.
- **Within-corpus carrier attribution + partial repair:** a 2×2 difference-in-differences shows the rationalized "Leaked-Early" chains carry most of the harm (DiD = +16.9, three-seed mean 12–21); excising just the single answer-stating line (≈1.4% of characters) recovers 24.6 of the 29.8-point gap (83%).
- **Pre-fine-tuning prediction of the penalty:** an answer-first-rate signature ∆AFR computed from a few unlabeled generations orders the penalty across 8 thinking models from 4 families at r=0.96 (permutation p=0.0003), with leave-one-family-out MAE of 2.9 points — a candidate teacher can be screened before any training compute is spent.
- **Cheap, deployable safeguards:** generate answer-blind (holds base 95.2 within seed noise at 95.1 and beats gold-data SFT at 90.8 under matched budget); use a derive-first instruction to recover ~2/3 of the penalty when the answer must be shown; or filter rationalized chains by AFR as a partial repair.

## Methodology

The paper builds a tightly controlled causal probe rather than a new system. The core design is the **one-bit experiment** (Figure 1):

- **Fixed backbone.** Every trained model is a full-parameter SFT of Qwen3-8B (Qwen Team 2025), 1 epoch, seq len 10,240, lr 2×10⁻⁵, effective batch 48, DeepSpeed ZeRO-3. Evaluation uses vLLM thinking mode (temp 0.6, top-p 0.95, top-k 20) on MATH-500 with a 32,768-token budget and symbolic answer matching (base model scores 95.2).
- **Fixed data.** 935 math problems from the math split of Nemotron-Cascade-SFT-Stage-2, intersected to problems both arms solve, disjoint from MATH-500. For each problem the generator samples n=2 chains at temp 0.6; only the first chain reaching the correct boxed answer is kept, so both corpora pass the *same* correctness filter on identical (question→chain) inputs.
- **One intervention.** The Blind prompt shows only the problem; the Leaked prompt appends the gold answer a(q) and asks for a reasoning trace that reaches it. The leakage penalty is defined as Δ = acc(S_blind) − acc(S_leaked).
- **Mechanism probes.** (i) A position metric: a chain is "answer-first" if the gold answer appears within the first 20% of the think block (AFR, Eq. 2). (ii) A prompt ablation at fixed visibility (Rationalize-toward / Derive-first / Final-check / Out-of-band) separates the rationalization instruction from bare answer visibility. (iii) A length-matched control truncates each blind chain to its matched leaked length to rule out length as the cause. (iv) A budget sweep (4K–32K tokens) decomposes the penalty into a derivation deficit and a termination deficit.
- **Carrier experiment (within-corpus DiD).** The 935 leaked chains are split by the 0.2 AFR threshold into Leaked-Early (438) and Leaked-Late (497), each fine-tuned separately, with matched blind subsets (Blind-PE, Blind-PL) as controls; the primary endpoint is DiD = (Leaked-Late − Leaked-Early) − (Blind-PL − Blind-PE), which nets out problem-difficulty confounds. A surgical variant excises the single answer-stating line from each Leaked-Early chain.
- **Cross-model signature.** ∆AFR = AFR_leaked − AFR_blind is computed from a small unlabeled sample per teacher and regressed against the observed penalty across 8 thinking models (Qwen3-8B/4B/1.7B, DS-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B/14B, DS-R1-Distill-Llama-8B, GLM-Z1-9B, Llama-Nemotron-8B); held-out predictions are recorded *before* training each model.
- **Generalization relaxations.** Code domain (MBPP+ / HumanEval+ with reference-solution echoing as the analogue of AFR), cross-student transfer (Qwen3-1.7B trained on Qwen3-8B corpora), cross-family teachers (Nemotron, GLM), STaR-replica regime (rescuing only failed problems), and multiple-choice baselines (MMLU, GPQA-Diamond).
- **Statistics.** Paired bootstrap over evaluation problems, McNemar's test for discordant items, two-sample KS test for the position shift; all CIs and seed dispersions live in Appendix I. Full reproducibility bundle (prompts, matched IDs, training configs, harness, pinned answer-checker versions) is released.

## Results & Highlights

- **Headline one-bit penalty (Qwen3-8B, MATH-500, three-seed mean):** Blind **95.1** vs. Leaked **78.9** = **−16.2 points** (paired-bootstrap 95% CI [12.2, 19.2]; discordant items split 86-to-8 in favor of blind, p < 10⁻¹⁷). The blind arm sits at the 95.2 base within seed noise, so essentially the *entire* gap is leakage damage rather than SFT noise.
- **Penalty grows monotonically with difficulty** (Table 1C): GSM8K grade-school 95.4 vs. 90.5 (−4.9); MATH-500 Level 1 95.3 vs. 95.3 (0.0); Level 4 95.6 vs. 74.2 (−21.4); Level 5 89.8 vs. 70.9 (−18.9); pooled Level 4–5 92.6 vs. 72.5 (−20.1); Minerva graduate STEM 48.7 vs. 38.5 (−10.2); **AIME 2024–2025 olympiad 71.7 vs. 44.4 = −27.2 points**.
- **Mechanism — answer-first rate (AFR):** on matched chains the leaked arm raises AFR by **+20.8 pp** (26.6% → 47.4%); median first-answer position shifts from 0.587 (blind) to 0.297 (leaked); the student-output AFR gap survives a correctness-controlled subset at +9.4 pp (KS test D=0.291, p < 10⁻³⁴).
- **Prompt ablation isolates the instruction (Table 1A):** holding visibility fixed, Derive-first recovers most of the penalty (95.1 / 90.7 / penalty 4.3), Final-check reaches 93.7 (penalty 1.3), Out-of-band 94.3 (penalty 0.7) — versus Rationalize-toward's 16.2-point loss. So the *rationalize-toward instruction*, not bare visibility, carries ~15 of the 16 points.
- **Length is not the cause:** the length-matched blind arm (each blind chain truncated to its matched leaked length) still scores **95.0**, 15.4 points above Leaked; the code-domain length control and the GLM cross-family length control confirm this.
- **Carrier experiment (Table 1B):** Leaked-Early 64.2 vs. Leaked-Late 86.2; DiD three-seed mean **+16.9 points** (range 12–21; bootstrap p < 10⁻⁴). Excising only the single answer-stating line from the 438 Leaked-Early chains (≈302 chars / 1.4% of each chain) lifts them to **88.8**, recovering 24.6 of the 29.8-point gap (83%).
- **Cross-model signature (Tables 3–4, Figure 4):** ∆AFR predicts the penalty at **r = 0.960** (exact permutation p = 0.0003; Fisher-z 95% CI [0.79, 0.99]; partial corr. controlling base accuracy 0.91). Penalties range from negligible (DS-R1-Distill-Llama-8B −1.2, ∆AFR −0.2) to severe (Qwen3-4B −29.2; Qwen3-1.7B −29.8). **Leave-one-family-out MAE = 2.9 points**; the independent third family Llama-Nemotron-8B was predicted at −17.4 and observed at −17.6.
- **Generalization beyond math (Table 5):** code MBPP+ pass rate 68.3 vs. 58.8 (−9.5); HumanEval+ 72.8 vs. 63.6 (−9.2); cross-student (1.7B trained on 8B corpora) 85.7 vs. 78.9 (−6.9); cross-family Nemotron teacher 94.0 vs. 79.2 (−14.8); GLM teacher 93.0 vs. 82.8 (−10.2).
- **No penalty where no derivation is needed:** MMLU 54.8 vs. 54.0 (+0.8); GPQA-Diamond 37.6 vs. 40.2 (−2.6) — neither distinguishable from zero across three seeds, matching the mechanism's prediction.
- **Practical payoff:** answer-blind self-distillation holds base accuracy (95.1 vs. 95.2) and **beats gold-data SFT (90.8) at a matched 10,240-token budget**. STaR-replica rescue (answer-hint only on blind failures) still costs a real 4.0 (Qwen3-8B) / 1.5 (DS-Qwen-7B) points, so even verified rescued chains are negative-value data.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Authors** are from Korea University (Lee, Lee, Son, Han, Lim), Zoom Communications (Dongyub Jude Lee), and Yonsei University (Sugyeong Eo); Heuiseok Lim and Sugyeong Eo are corresponding authors. The work is positioned as a careful empirical/methodological contribution, not a system push, and the framing is restrained (it explicitly calls its claims "interpolation over the measured range" rather than extrapolation).
- **Statistical rigor is above the norm for the subfield.** Three training seeds {42, 123, 7777} for every headline number; paired bootstrap + McNemar's tests for accuracy gaps; KS test for the position shift; exact permutation test for the cross-model correlation; leave-one-family-out for the predictor; a length-matched control on every transfer claim it could be run on. All CIs and seed dispersions are centralized in Appendix I.
- **Reproducibility commitment is concrete:** code, data, exact prompts for all five conditions, the 935 matched problem IDs with their blind/leaked chains, generation metadata, training configs, and the pinned-version symbolic answer checker are released at https://github.com/js-lee-AI/answer-leakage, with a manifest mapping each table cell to a stored evaluation record. The held-out penalty predictions were "recorded before training each held-out model," a genuine pre-registration flavor rare in this literature.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — genuinely new causal quantity.** The paper supplies what was missing in the STaR / ReST / MetaMath / V-STaR / TRICE lineage: a *controlled* isolation of answer-conditioning that holds the generator, problem set, filter, and recipe fixed and varies only one bit. TRICE (Phan et al. 2023) recast the same bias as a latent-variable inference problem to *correct* it; this paper instead shows the bias is **invisible to the standard correctness filter**, *causally harms SFT even when every chain is verified*, and can be anticipated from unlabeled generations. That is a real advance over the concurrent "patch the symptom with an entropy filter" work (HEAL, RAFT-2026) that the authors cite but do not themselves isolate causally.
- **Rigor — strong, with a few soft spots.** The one-bit experiment, length control, and carrier DiD are exemplary. But several rows rest on a **single seed** (all six "single training run" rows of Table 3, both cross-family transfers in Table 5, and the GLM/Nemotron length controls), so the *sign* of those effects is well-supported while their *magnitudes* are not. The paper is honest about this (Appendix I flags the cross-student penalty's σ = 8.9), but readers should not quote "−14.8 for Nemotron" as a precise number.
- **Reproducibility — high.** Released code/data, pinned correctness-critical dependencies, manifest-traced table cells, and pre-recorded held-out predictions. The one manual knob (training seq len 10,240) is flagged explicitly. The single correctness-critical component (the symbolic answer matcher) being pinned to exact versions is a thoughtful touch.
- **Significance — high for data-generation practice.** The "generate answer-blind" prescription is free, deployable today, and the paper shows it beats gold-data SFT at a matched budget — a result any reasoning-distillation team should act on. The ∆AFR pre-screen is a cheap diagnostic that could become standard.
- **Red flags / things to probe.** (i) **Scale of the headline 16.2 rests on one student family.** The one-bit experiment uses 935 problems on Qwen3-8B only; the cross-model table is small-sample (n=8, Fisher-z CI is wide at [0.79, 0.99]). (ii) **Phi-4-reasoning was tried and discarded for "measurement reasons"** — both its arms collapse below 5% MATH-500 accuracy with 50K-char degenerate loops, so its predicted −47.4 penalty is *unvalidated*; the paper says so but the fit's "r=0.96" headline does not include this anchor, and a reader could miss that the largest-signature model in the set is effectively unmeasured. (iii) **The "derive-first" mitigation is reported as a single-seed number per arm** in Table 1A without a seed dispersion, so the "~2/3 recovered" claim is less firmly grounded than the main penalty. (iv) **The within-corpus 0.2 AFR threshold is somewhat post-hoc** (the paper shows robustness to {0.1, 0.2, 0.3} sweeping the early share only across 43.4% / 46.8% / 49.6%, which is fine, but the threshold was presumably chosen *after* seeing the data). (v) **STaR-replica cost is modest (4.0 / 1.5 points)** yet the paper still concludes "even verified rescued chains are negative-value data" — a somewhat strong reading given the sub-linear dose response the authors themselves note.
- **No cherry-picked benchmarks detected.** The paper reports GSM8K, MATH-500, Minerva, AIME, MBPP+, HumanEval+, MMLU, GPQA-Diamond — including two benchmarks (MMLU, GPQA) where the effect *vanishes*, which is the opposite of cherry-picking.

## Strengths

- Exemplary experimental control: the one-bit design isolates a single variable with everything else fixed, and a length-matched arm closes the most obvious alternative explanation.
- Mechanism is legible and actionable: answer-first rate is a one-line, computable-from-unlabeled-generations diagnostic, and the carrier experiment pinpoints the single answer-stating line as the dominant carrier (83% of the gap recovered by excising ~1.4% of characters).
- Pre-fine-tuning predictor (∆AFR, r=0.96, leave-one-family-out MAE 2.9) turns an expensive negative result into a cheap screening tool.
- Honest negative-result reporting on MMLU/GPQA-Diamond sharpens, rather than weakens, the mechanism story (harm confined to tasks requiring derivation).
- Cheap, deployable prescription (generate answer-blind; or use a derive-first instruction) that the paper shows *beats gold-data SFT* at matched budget — immediate practical value.
- Reproducibility artifacts go beyond code release: pinned correctness-critical dependencies, per-cell manifest, pre-recorded held-out predictions.

## Limitations

- **Single student family for the headline.** The one-bit experiment is Qwen3-8B on 935 problems; the cross-model fit is small-sample (n=8) with a wide Fisher-z CI.
- **Many generalization rows are single-seed** (cross-family Nemotron/GLM, both length controls in Table 13, the derive-first and neutral arms of Table 1A), supporting only the *sign* of the effect.
- **Phi-4-reasoning, the largest-signature model (∆AFR=+45.5), was discarded** because both its trained arms degenerate (<5% accuracy, 50K-char loops); the fit's headline r therefore excludes the most extreme anchor and is best read as an interpolation, not a law.
- **Cross-student transfer is the least stable setting** (seed σ = 8.9, with one of three seeds at −0.2), so the +6.9-point penalty is a sign claim, not a magnitude claim.
- **Code domain and student-output transfer (Section 4) are not length-controlled** (the paper flags this); length-driven confounds there cannot be fully ruled out.
- **The 0.2 AFR threshold and the 20% "first 20% of think block" definition are post-hoc choices**, although the robustness sweep over {0.1, 0.2, 0.3} and Appendix F's matcher/position sensitivity checks soften this.
- **The "verify-rescued chains are negative-value" framing is somewhat strong** relative to the modest 4.0/1.5-point STaR-replica costs and the sub-linear dose response the authors themselves observe.
- **No RLHF / RL fine-tuning setting is tested** — the study is confined to supervised distillation of thinking models, leaving open whether RL objectives (with KL regularization to the generator) would dampen the off-policy shift.

## Relevance

高度相关于推理模型蒸馏与自我改进数据生成这一研究方向。它直接审视 STaR、ReST、MetaMath、V-STaR 等"采样失败就给答案让模型合理化"的常用配方，给出一个可控的因果结论和可立即落地的"答案盲生成 / derive-first"补救，对任何构建 reasoning-distillation 或 self-improvement 数据流水线的工作都是必读级别的提醒与方法论模板。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the leakage penalty survive under **on-policy / KL-regularized RL distillation** (Agarwal et al. 2024; Sang et al. 2026), where the objective explicitly pulls the student toward its own search distribution and might dampen the off-policy shift the paper identifies?
- The carrier experiment shows excising the single answer-stating line recovers 83% of the gap — would a **training-time loss-mask** that down-weights tokens near the first answer mention give the same effect without needing a derive-first regeneration?
- Can ∆AFR be **predicted from model weights or pretraining data alone**, removing the need to sample generations at all, so that a model can be screened before any inference compute is spent?
- How does the penalty interact with **process reward models / PRM reranking** — would reranking leaked chains by a step-level PRM recover the gap, or does the rationalization habit slip past step-level plausibility too?
- The effect vanishes on MMLU/GPQA-Diamond but the paper does not test **open-ended generation, tool-use, or agentic tasks** where "derivation" is less cleanly defined — does the boundary hold there?
- The fit excludes Phi-4-reasoning for measurement reasons; could the experiment be **re-run with a tighter generation budget or a length penalty** to bring degenerate-loop models into the measured range and validate (or break) the r=0.96 extrapolation?
- STaR-replica rescue costs only 4.0 points — is that residual harm **concentrated in specific problem categories** that could be selectively routed to a derive-first regenerator, recovering most of STaR's data-coverage benefit at near-zero cost?
