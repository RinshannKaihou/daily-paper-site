A —Methodology

A three-stage, fine-tuning-free pipeline built on classical geometric scores.

GeoDetect frames detection as a binary decision against a clean reference minibatch: f((x,t), {z_j}) = H(Metric(z_i, {z_j})). No model weights change; only four scores are computed over image or multimodal [CLS] embeddings.

LID · layer-wise MLE k-NN · mean distance (k=10) Mahalanobis · (μ, Σ on D_c) KDE · Gaussian bw=0.1
  1. Generation. Sep-Attack (Sepuni / Sepmulti) + Co-Attack; PGD ℓ∞ ε = 8/255, step 1.25, 10 iters; BERT-Attack 1 token for text.
  2. Extraction. Four geometric scores per query vs a clean reference batch; fused-VLP multimodal LID added as an extra feature for ALBEF/TCL.
  3. Detection. Threshold H(s)=𝟙(s>τ) for k-NN/Maha/KDE; logistic regression on multi-layer LID. 80/20 calibration/eval split.

Theory · Lemma 1 closed-form optimal adversarial embedding

z′*_i = (Σ + λI)⁻¹ λ z_i

Under anisotropic covariance (σ₁ ≫ σ₂ ≫ … ≫ σ_D) and manifold-proximity assumptions, Lemma 2 shows AEs leave the clean manifold (‖z′−M‖ ≥ γ), and Theorem 1 proves the expected-distance gap γ > 3α — the reason geometric scores separate AEs.

B —Empirical Geometry

Why CLIP-like embeddings are geometrically attackable.

Contrastive VLPs (CLIP, ALBEF, TCL) are far more anisotropic than a supervised ResNet-50 — measured by isotropy I₁, I₂ and effective rank (ER).

  • Adversarial embeddings push energy into the lowest-singular-value directions.
  • Higher PCA reconstruction residuals (top-10 clean PCs).
  • Larger KL divergence along the leading 1–20 PCA components.

Verified on ImageNet-V2 / CLIPCNN — direct empirical support for Theorem 1.

D —Strengths

What it gets right.

  • Theoretically grounded — explicit assumptions, two lemmas, a theorem and proofs, not just an empirical observation.
  • Model- and task-agnostic: same framework for aligned (CLIP) and fused (ALBEF, TCL) VLPs, and for both classification and retrieval.
  • Fine-tuning-free and cheap — minibatch LID/k-NN/KDE/Mahalanobis scale to large models.
  • Honest under adaptive attacks — the authors publish the large AUC drops rather than hiding them.
  • Theory is empirically verified (effective rank, normal-direction energy, PCA residual, per-PCA KL divergence all line up).
  • Public code, standard datasets and checkpoints — reproduction is feasible.

E —Limitations

Where it overclaims or stops short.

  • Only one main-text baseline (MCM); PIP is VQA-only and relegated to the appendix. General-purpose unimodal detectors are not re-implemented for VLPs.
  • Many "perfect" AUC = 100 results — the ε = 8/255 threat setting may be too easy; ImageNet1k / Sepmulti fall to 51–81.
  • Adaptive budget is narrow (ζ = 0.1, single batch-mismatch setting); no jointly-adaptive attacker across all four metrics.
  • Assumption 2 (Gaussian / locally-Euclidean embeddings) justified by citation, not a direct statistical test on VLP neighborhoods.
  • Text-side attacks limited to 1-token BERT-Attack; focus is overwhelmingly image-side.
  • No evaluation on instruction-tuned MLLMs (LLaVA-style); VLPs limited to CLIP/ALBEF/TCL.
