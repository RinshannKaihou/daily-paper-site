A theory-backed one-shot self-distillation mechanism for rectified-flow generators.

03Critical Edge

The headline neural gains come from a deliberately degraded teacher (scale 0.75); benefits shrink as the teacher becomes well-calibrated, so practical impact on production RF models is unproven.

The mechanism — Proposition 1

One scalar parameterizes the entire self-distillation family.

Under ridge regression on frozen interpolation pairs, the self-distilled student lies on an exact affine path between the teacher and the pure-distilled refit. Mixing becomes a one-dimensional search — and the integrated RF risk turns out to be a clean quadratic in ξ.

Ŵsd,λ,ξ = (1 − ξ)·Ŵλ + ξ·Ŵpd,λ   ⇒   ξ★ = −λ·R'(λ) / (2·D(λ)),   sign(ξ★) = −sign(R'(λ))

01
Methodology

Freeze the interpolants, then mix on a line.

  • Setting: rectified flow under flow-matching, X_t = (1−t)X_0 + tX_1, velocity u = X_1 − X_0 regressed on (t, X_t).
  • Linear-in-parameters field v_W(t,x) = Wᵀφ(t,x) — and crucially, the interpolation covariates are frozen once sampled, unlike recursive Reflow.
  • Algorithm 1: train teacher Ŵ_λ on true velocities; train pure-distilled Ŵ_pd,λ on teacher predictions; each only once.
  • Pick ξ three ways — oracle ξ★ = (R−C)/D, GCV plug-in (Eq. 18), or 1-D validation on the affine field. Never refit per ξ.
  • Feeds the risk drop into RF Wasserstein / Euler bounds (Bansal et al. 2026) — the velocity term provably tightens (Prop. 3, Thm. 4).

02
Results & Highlights

Negative mixing recovers modes that pure distillation destroys.

  • CIFAR-10 U-Net RF (teacher ×0.75, NFE=32): FID 284.26 → 30.08, KID 0.3215 → 0.0186, RF risk 0.2435 → 0.1803. Validation picks ξ̂ = −1.225; clipping ξ to [0,1] collapses back to teacher (FID 283.56).
  • Gaussian mixture (λ≈7.5): teacher risk 2.077, pure-distillation worsens to 2.639, GCV-SD → 0.273 at ξ̂ ≈ −6.46. Sliced-W₂ at NFE=32: ~1.18 → 0.091.
  • Fashion-MNIST stress test: validation selects ξ̂ = −2.83, RF risk 0.828 → 0.210, Feature-FD 818.5 → 8.8, conditioning accuracy @NFE=64 0.107 → 0.895.
  • Sign rule (Fig. 3b): constraining ξ to [0,1] returns the teacher whenever the oracle correction is negative — convex distillation cannot fix over-regularized teachers.

03
Strengths

Analytic tractability, rare in generative theory.

  • Closed-form optimal mixing coefficient and an exact quadratic risk curve — a one-dimensional problem with no grid search.
  • Cleanly identifies negative mixing as the operative correction for over-regularized RF teachers, tied to the ridge-risk derivative by a sign rule.
  • One-shot GCV / validation tuning avoids the repeated refits that make naive distillation expensive.
  • Theory plugs directly into standard RF Wasserstein / Euler bounds — the velocity-risk improvement is provably the right quantity to optimize.
  • Honest about scope: guarantee stated for fixed λ on a linear ridge teacher, not global optimality or arbitrary downstream metrics.

04
Limitations

Guarantee is linear-ridge; neural gains rest on an injected fault.

  • Strict-improvement theorem holds only for linear RF with ridge on fixed interpolants; the neural affine family (Eq. 19) is an assumption, not a theorem.
  • Headline neural gains inject teacher miscalibration manually (scale 0.75); Appendix C.3 shows the gain shrinks toward zero as a → 1.
  • The constrained-[0,1] “baseline” is near-tautological (it contains the teacher at ξ=0), overstating novelty.
  • No head-to-head with recursive Reflow + synthetic/real mixing (Zhu et al.) or modern diffusion-distillation methods on shared benchmarks.
  • Fixed-interpolant assumption diverges from Reflow practice; extending to endpoint replacement — and preserving the ridge geometry — is left open.
