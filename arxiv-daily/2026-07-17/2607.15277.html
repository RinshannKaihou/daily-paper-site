§ 01

The Idea

If ICL is conditional inference, then probability identities must hold — with no labels required.

If a language model is really doing conditional inference, then its estimates must compose: ask it about subgroups, weight by priors, sum — and you should recover the population. The authors make that assumption testable by recasting the law of total probability as a family of checkable identities that need no ground truth at all.

Statistical self-consistency — a new evaluation axis

Reference-free and orthogonal to alignment: applies even when labels are expensive or undefined (e.g. the fantasy-combat task has no ground truth and still produces a meaningful score).

The identity under test

For any valid partition P of population X:

P(E | X∈X) = ΣS∈P γ_S · P(E | X∈S)

Two local checks → global control

Split consistency (does a subgroup equal the weighted sum of its children?) and order consistency (“young & employed” = “employed & young”?). Propositions 2, 6, 7 give worst-case bounds tying local checks to global tree consistency.

§ 02

Binary Conditioning Trees

One binary split per level → a valid partition at every depth → statistically equivalent estimators for one aggregate.
Fig. — BCT schematic (ACS running example)

Splits chosen by a regression-tree objective that reduces within-subgroup income variance while keeping priors balanced.

Elicitation via persona prompts

Each subpopulation becomes a concatenated persona string fed to the LLM (using folktexts for ACS). The model returns T̂(S) = P(Y > τ | X ∈ S); priors γ̂ are elicited level-wise, renormalized, averaged.

Distance & tolerance

Discrepancies use the normalized Wasserstein-1 distance on ordered categorical outcomes.

tolerance ε = 0.02 throughout · thresholds τ ∈ {100, 1k, 10k, 20k, 40k, 60k, 80k} USD

Error decomposition (law of total variance)

Refinement moves variance from within-group heterogeneity into cross-group structure. Conditional estimates improve with depth, but LLM-estimated priors degrade — TV distance to survey priors grows monotonically. That trade-off produces the concave aggregation-gain curve.

Order > Split, everywhere

Order consistency is uniformly higher than split consistency — models handle constraint reordering better than probabilistic composition. On the tennis task (Table 3a) no model passes more than half the split checks (max Sonnet 4.6 at 0.50; Qwen3.6 Plus at 0.00).

Necessary, not sufficient

Self-consistency is explicitly a necessary condition. A model could satisfy every check while still being misaligned; conversely, high population-level alignment does not certify subpopulation faithfulness.

Robustness of the macro fallacy

Aggregation gains are positive across GPT-5.4, GPT-4o mini, Opus 4.7, Sonnet 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro, Gemini 3 Flash, Qwen3.6 Plus — and across all seven income thresholds tested.

§ 04

The Macro Fallacy

Asking the model directly for the population estimate is systematically worse than reconstructing it from subgroups.

Fine-grained estimates, recombined, beat the direct answer.

Under the conditional-inference view, the direct root-level estimate and the prior-weighted aggregate over subgroups are statistically equivalent. Empirically they are not. The aggregated estimate is consistently closer to survey reference data — robust across models, thresholds, tasks, and tree structures.

Plausible mechanism (single qualitative example): direct prompting underweights young individuals, causing income to be overestimated. Presented as a mechanism, not yet a systematic decomposition.
01

Explicit tree aggregation

Build the BCT, elicit per-leaf conditionals + priors, recombine. Largest gain; costs more calls.

02

Micro-to-macro prompting

One query: ask the model to enumerate relevant subgroups before giving the population estimate. Near-zero-cost, partially recovers the benefit.

03

Honest scope

Effect is “more model-dependent and less systematic” than explicit aggregation — the authors do not oversell it.

§ 06

Critical Assessment

A memorable empirical regularity with real reproducibility caveats.

+ Strengths

  • Reference-free & orthogonal to alignment — useful precisely where ground truth is expensive or undefined.
  • Clean formal scaffolding — BCTs make “statistically equivalent estimators” concrete, with proven bounds (Props 2, 6, 7).
  • Oracle-prior ablation (Appendix A.1) cleanly isolates conditional error from prior error.
  • Micro-to-macro prompting is a practically useful, near-zero-cost intervention — carefully not oversold.
  • Intellectually honest limitations section; cross-domain validation across 5 tasks.

! Limitations

  • Anachronistic model names (GPT-5.4, Sonnet 4.6, Grok 4.3 …) — no version IDs, API access, or decoding parameters in main text.
  • Single tolerance ε = 0.02 carries every headline number; sensitivity analysis not foregrounded.
  • Main BCT is shallow — only two attributes (age, COW); deeper / higher-arity generalisation asserted, not the empirical thrust.
  • Necessary, not sufficient — internally consistent ≠ faithful conditional inference.
  • Macro-fallacy mechanism presented as one qualitative example, not a systematic decomposition.
  • Order consistency restricted to two-attribute events under an unverified context-independence assumption.
