01

Methodology

1 · Visually-Grounded Atomic Decomposition
A Decomposer D fractures the query into minimal solvable atoms A={A₁…Aₙ}. Perception-first: visual elements are grounded before any logical deduction, binding concepts to deterministic symbols.
2 · DAG Planning + Context Isolation
Atoms form a DAG G=(A,E). Scheduling Aᵢ injects only direct-prerequisite outputs: C(Aᵢ)=∪ M(Aⱼ) — no full history, killing long-context hallucination.
3 · Adaptive Atomic Fission
On a failed atom, F(Aᵢ,trace)→{Aᵢ.1…Aᵢ.k} fractures it into finer sub-atoms prepended to the deque. Hierarchical aggregation M_virtual(Aᵢ)=(1/k)ΣM(Aᵢ.k) preserves global topology — no DAG recompilation.
02

Results

Mean across 6 MLLMs · 7 science benchmarks. Higher is better.

▲ Strengths

  • Principled diagnosis of 3 failure modes of linear agents, each mapped to a concrete architectural response.
  • Context Isolation is cleanly formalized (Eq. 1) and delivers real token efficiency vs OctoTools' context explosion.
  • Adaptive Fission is genuinely novel — a runtime granularity fix that avoids global DAG recompilation.
  • Model-agnostic: gains hold across 6 MLLMs (proprietary + open-weight).
  • Largest gains land on the hardest benchmarks — Olympiad physics, chemistry.

✕ Limitations

  • MathVista / Multi-math margins are within noise; LangChain beats TopoAgent on Qwen3-vl MathVista (85.8 vs 80.5).
  • No ablation on Visually-Grounded Decomposition — one of three core mechanisms is unattributed.
  • No head-to-head with Tree-/Graph-of-Thoughts, the most similar prior work.
  • Hyperparameters (T_max, fission budget, STOP threshold) and prompts underspecified; no code release mentioned.
  • Chemistry is the weakest case yet framed as a domain win; no human/oracle upper bound.
