Delocalization of Bias in Unadjusted HMC & Underdamped Langevin

Yifan Chen — UCLA Mathematics
Xiaoou Cheng  ·  Jonathan Niles-Weed  ·  Jonathan Weare — Courant Institute, NYU
Key Finding

For unadjusted leap-frog HMC and BAOAB underdamped Langevin, controlling the bias of any K-dimensional marginal needs only O(√K) integration steps — up to log d factors.

02Why This Matters

Formalizes exactly when Metropolis–Hastings adjustment can be skipped in high-dimensional MCMC — dodging the d−1/4 step-size bottleneck for low-dimensional marginals.

03Critical Edge

No experiments validate the bound at realistic dimensions; strong-convexity plus restrictive interaction assumptions exclude the non-log-concave targets common in Bayesian deep learning.

§ 01 — THE PAYOFF

Steps to bound a K-marginal, as dimension grows

Drag K (the marginal you care about). Adjusted HMC must shrink its step size as d−1/4 to keep Metropolis acceptance alive, so integration-step count climbs with total dimension. Under delocalization, the step count depends on K, not d.

§ 02 — Methodology

Three ingredients of a non-asymptotic bias bound

1

Synchronous coupling in W2,ℓ∞ §2

Two chains share momentum refreshments (HMC) or O-step noise (BAOAB): exact chain Y ~ π, approximate chain X runs leap-frog/BAOAB. The position gap splits into a contraction term and a discretization-error term.

2

Multivariate damped Chebyshev propagators §3

The phase-space propagators of both integrators are written as one matrix-polynomial family T̃ηk with three-term recurrence — encoding leap-frog (η=1) and BAOAB (η=e−γh) simultaneously, degenerating to classical Chebyshev for Gaussians.

3

ℓ2 → ℓ∞ bridge §4–5

Existing ℓ2 contractions upgrade to ℓ∞: weak interactions make propagators near-diagonal; sparse interactions use that r-fold Hessian products live on N2r(i), costing only a √sr factor via Cauchy–Schwarz.

§ 04 — VERDICT

What's solid, what's missing

Strengths
What lands well
  • +Reusable technical centerpiece. The multivariate damped Chebyshev framework subsumes Gaussian (classical Chebyshev) and path-dependent Hessian cases in one construction; authors flag applicability to OBABO / ABOBA splittings.
  • +Operationally sharp. Formalizes a long-standing folk wisdom of molecular dynamics: MH adjustment is provably unnecessary for low-dimensional marginals under weak/sparse interactions.
  • +Clean sparse improvement. h·√log d → h·log d over the authors' own prior result; free LM-integrator corollary from the friction analysis.
  • +Honest about scope. Explicitly names randomized HMC as a non-trivial open case the propagator framework does not cover.
Limitations
Read before applying
  • −No numerical validation. The O(√K) claim is asymptotic; constants are not tight, and no worked example shows the bounds are non-vacuous at realistic d (e.g. 10⁶ for MD).
  • −Assumptions are restrictive. α-strong-convexity + β-smoothness (Asm 1.1) excludes non-log-concave posteriors typical of Bayesian deep learning.
  • −Interaction bounds are stringent. Weak needs off-diagonal Hessian ≤ α/50 (HMC) / α/20 (UL); sparse needs polynomial neighborhood growth — long-range decaying interactions are not covered.
  • −Friction gap. UL result requires γ > 2√β (factor-2 off the sharp threshold of [40]); underdamped low-friction regime is uncovered.
