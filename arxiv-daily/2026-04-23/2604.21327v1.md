> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示了测试时强化学习（TTRL）中虚假信号的两大来源——中等频率答案形成的歧义区域和GRPO的群体相对优势归一化对低共识样本的不对称放大——并提出DDRL框架（平衡置信感知采样+去偏优势估计+共识驱动离策精调），在Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B上相对ETMR基线实现平均+6.7%的提升。

> **TL;DR:** TTRL uses majority-voted pseudo-labels and GRPO for unsupervised test-time LLM adaptation, but medium-frequency answers create an "ambiguous region" where pseudo-labels are unreliable, and GRPO's normalization disproportionately amplifies these spurious rewards. DDRL mitigates both issues through confidence-aware sampling, fixed-advantage assignment, and an efficient off-policy SFT refinement stage.

## 核心贡献

- 通过实证分析揭示TTRL虚假信号的双重来源：（1）中等采样频率答案的伪标签不可靠性；（2）GRPO归一化在低共识组中对虚假奖励的系统性放大。
- 提出DDRL框架，包含三个互补组件：平衡置信感知采样（BCS）、去偏优势估计（DAE）、基于共识的离策精调（COR）。
- BCS通过频率-正确性相关性筛选高可信正样本（频率最高的K+个）和可靠负样本（频率最低的K−个），避免歧义区域样本进入优化。
- DAE将GRPO的群体相对优势替换为固定标签相关优势：A_i = 1[y=y*] − 1[y≠y*]，从根本上消除归一化引起的放大效应。
- COR是轻量级阶段：从RL策略采样M=128个rollout，收集高共识正样本，进行5 epoch监督微调（3-5分钟），稳定并巩固RL阶段收益，效果优于等计算量的额外在线RL训练。

## Key Contributions

- Empirical and theoretical characterization of the "ambiguous region" — medium-frequency answers where P(Correct|frequency) is highly unstable — as the primary source of TTRL noise. Supported by Bayesian analysis and extended to AIME 1985-2024 validation.
- Theoretical analysis showing GRPO's normalization creates a systematic bias in unsupervised settings: when positive samples are scarce (low consensus), normalized advantages are disproportionately large for exactly the most unreliable signals.
- Three-component DDRL framework addressing noise source (BCS), optimization bias (DAE), and training stability (COR) in a unified pipeline.
- Ablation study confirming complementary effects: BCS alone +2.4-3.2% on most models but slightly hurts Qwen2.5-Base-3B without DAE; DAE consistently recovers and improves across all models; COR provides the final stability boost.
- Training dynamics analysis: DDRL's mean advantage evolves meaningfully (negative → positive on MATH-500), while TTRL's mean advantage remains flat near zero throughout.

## Methodology

**Setting:** Test-time reinforcement learning (TTRL) — adapting a pre-trained LLM at inference time using self-generated rollouts, without ground-truth labels. Pseudo-labels are derived via majority voting over N=64 rollouts per query.

**DDRL Pipeline:**
1. **BCS:** For each query, compute occurrence count c(y_i) for each rollout y_i. Select K+ = min(c(y*), ⌊K/2⌋) positive samples (highest frequency, matching pseudo-label), and K− = K − K+ negative samples (lowest frequency). This filters the ambiguous medium-frequency region.
2. **DAE:** Replace GRPO's normalized advantage A_i = (r_i − mean(r)) / std(r) with fixed A_i = 1[y=y*] − 1[y≠y*]. Decouples advantage magnitude from group statistics.
3. **COR:** After RL training, sample M=128 rollouts per query from the RL-adapted policy, collect consensus-matching samples D_op via rejection sampling, and fine-tune for 5 epochs with supervised cross-entropy.

**Models:** Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B (math-specialized), Qwen2.5-Base-3B (general base), Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (general instruction-tuned).

**Benchmarks:** AIME 2024, AMC, MATH-500. Metric: pass@1 with 16 rollouts at temperature 0.6, top-p=0.95.

**Baselines:** No Adaptation, TTRL (Zuo et al., 2025), ETMR (Liu et al., 2025 — entropy-based trajectory diversity).

## Results & Highlights

- **Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B:** DDRL 52.9 avg vs. ETMR 49.6 (+6.7%), vs. TTRL 45.9 (+15.3%). AIME 2024: 25.0 vs. 21.0 (ETMR), 15.8 (TTRL).
- **Qwen2.5-Base-3B:** DDRL 41.6 avg vs. ETMR 40.9 (+1.7%).
- **Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct:** DDRL 39.8 avg vs. ETMR 37.3 (+6.7%), though DDRL underperforms ETMR on AIME 2024 alone for this model.
- **Off-policy COR vs. extended RL:** COR (5 epochs, 3-5 min) consistently outperforms extended on-policy RL at 1/14 to 1/24 the training time.
- **Preliminary sanity check:** GRPO w/o normalization outperforms standard GRPO on both AIME 2024 (20.6 vs. 15.8) and MATH (75.0 vs. 73.0), validating the normalization-amplification hypothesis.
- **Qwen2.5-Math-7B (additional):** DDRL 65.3 avg vs. TTRL 63.9 — consistent improvement at larger scale.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The insight that medium-frequency answers form a specific "ambiguous region" is well-articulated. The DAE solution (fixed advantages) is simple but effective — its simplicity is both a strength (robust) and a limitation (may leave expressiveness on the table). BCS and COR are engineering-level improvements rather than conceptual breakthroughs. The core finding about GRPO normalization bias is the most intellectually interesting contribution.

**Rigor:** Three models, three benchmarks, full ablation table, training dynamics visualization, Bayesian analysis appendix, sensitivity analysis on refinement size — the experimental protocol is thorough. The Qwen2.5-Base-3B model showing different behavior from the math-specialized model is honestly reported.

**Reproducibility:** Code to be released; implementation uses the verl framework (public). The small-scale off-policy stage (5 min) is a practical plus for reproducibility.

**Significance:** TTRL is an active and rapidly growing area. Identifying GRPO normalization as an amplifier of noisy rewards is a practically important finding that can inform future unsupervised RL algorithm design. The off-policy distillation stage as a more efficient alternative to extended RL is a useful contribution.

**Red flags:** (1) DDRL underperforms ETMR on AIME 2024 for Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (13.3 vs. 16.9) — the authors report this honestly but do not fully explain it. (2) The DAE fixed-advantage scheme is deliberately conservative; more expressive uncertainty-aware advantage formulations are left to future work. (3) Evaluation is limited to mathematical reasoning with well-defined correctness criteria — generalization to open-ended generation (dialogue, creative writing) is explicitly unaddressed. (4) The consensus-based pseudo-label remains unreliable for genuinely hard problems (AIME 2024 pass rates are low), which limits the quality of the COR dataset. (5) Comparison against only two baselines; newer contemporaneous TTRL methods (RESTRAIN, SPINE) appear in the related work but are not compared against.

## Strengths

- Clean diagnosis of TTRL failure modes with both empirical evidence and theoretical grounding.
- Three-component framework with each component addressing a distinct failure mode.
- COR off-policy stage is computationally highly efficient (3-5 min) with consistent gains.
- Ablation study clearly demonstrates the complementary nature of BCS, DAE, and COR.
- Honest reporting of cases where DDRL underperforms baselines.

## Limitations

- Limited to mathematical reasoning with well-defined answer correctness; open-ended tasks are untested.
- The fixed-advantage DAE is conservative; more sophisticated advantage designs may further improve efficiency.
- Pseudo-label reliability is fundamentally bounded by problem difficulty and model capability.
- Qwen2.5-Base-3B shows diminishing returns from BCS alone, suggesting the method's effectiveness depends on the model's baseline reasoning ability.
- GRPO normalization removal is not a fully principled solution — it reduces a symptom rather than addressing the underlying unsupervised pseudo-label quality issue.

## Relevance

本文与用户核心关注领域高度相关：直接涉及**LLM训练稳定性**（GRPO归一化引起的不稳定优化动态）和**推理评估**（测试时适应场景下的pass@1评估）。GRPO优势归一化对虚假信号的放大机制，是LLM强化训练中一种值得关注的系统性可靠性问题。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the "ambiguous region" concept be operationalized as an online signal during training — e.g., dynamically adjusting the confidence-aware sampling threshold based on observed frequency distributions?
2. How does the method perform on non-mathematical tasks with softer correctness criteria, such as code generation where functional correctness is binary but style correctness is not?
3. Is the GRPO normalization problem specific to the unsupervised TTRL setting, or does it also manifest in lightly supervised settings (e.g., when ground-truth labels are available for only a small fraction of queries)?
4. Could the DAE fixed-advantage scheme be extended to incorporate confidence gradations (e.g., advantage proportional to frequency percentile) to recover some of the expressiveness lost by the binary assignment?
5. What is the interaction between problem difficulty distribution and DDRL performance — does DDRL gain most on problems that are at the frontier of the model's capability, or does it help uniformly?
