> **一句话总结：** 本文证明了Transformer在训练时对未见过的token的(un)embedding向量会发生坍缩（cosine相似度趋近1），这是符号推理泛化失败的机制性原因，并通过结合copy attention、高多样性数据和冻结/重初始化embedding三种技术组合解决了该问题。

> **TL;DR:** Decoder-only transformers fail to generalize symbolic reasoning to unseen tokens because the (un)embedding vectors of unseen tokens collapse toward the same direction during training under L2-regularized SGD/AdamW with LayerNorm. The authors prove this theoretically, confirm it in Gemma 3 models, and show that combining copy attention, high symbolic diversity, and frozen or periodically reset embeddings achieves robust generalization.

## 核心贡献

- **理论证明embedding坍缩的必然性**：在带有weight decay和LayerNorm的SGD训练下，未见token的softmax权重（即unembedding向量）必然收缩到几乎相同的方向，用Lemma 4.1给出了精确的收缩上界。
- **揭示已有启发式方法的机制**：用embedding坍缩解释了为什么"active forgetting"（周期性重置embedding）有效——它阻止了坍缩的积累；同样解释了Boix-Adserà等人的单独copy架构改动为何在多个未见变量场景下无效。
- **提出三要素组合方案**：copy attention架构 + 高符号多样性训练数据 + 冻结或周期性重置(un)embedding，实现了对命题逻辑问题中未见token的可靠泛化。
- **在Gemma 3系列中观察到坍缩的实际证据**：99个deliberate unused tokens在各模型规模下均呈现高cosine相似度，并且以collapsed unused tokens作为初始化进行微调比使用正常token慢约10倍。

## Key Contributions

- **Theoretical characterization of collapse**: Under L2-regularized GD/SGD with LayerNorm, the softmax classifier weights for unseen labels provably contract toward each other at every gradient step once the model fits training data (Lemma 4.1). The result holds even as the feature extractor θ changes over time.
- **Mechanistic explanation for active forgetting**: Periodic (un)embedding reinitialization works precisely because it prevents the collapse from accumulating; the paper is the first to identify this causal link.
- **Copy attention head design**: A modified architecture that directly attends over input embeddings (bypassing MLP layers) enables copying unseen tokens from context — this is a key ingredient that, combined with diversity and embedding management, achieves full generalization.
- **Empirical evidence in real LLMs**: In Gemma 3 (1B–27B IT), unused token embeddings have mean cosine similarities of 0.23–0.78 vs. 0.01–0.09 for used tokens; finetuning on downstream tasks with unused tokens converges 10× slower.

## Methodology

**Problem Setting**: Decoder-only transformers with tied embedding/unembedding matrices (WE) are trained on propositional logic problems (Horn clause reasoning) with chain-of-thought. At test time, variable names are replaced by tokens held out during training.

**Theoretical Analysis**: The authors model the final-layer softmax as a standard classifier with feature function ϕ(·|θ). They prove that for any pair of labels not present in training data, the gradient step with learning rate ηt contracts their weight difference by factor (1 − ληt + ηt r² pt), where pt is the maximum probability assigned to unseen labels and r bounds the feature norm. With LayerNorm enforcing bounded features and weight decay λ > 0, collapse is guaranteed once the model fits training data (pt → 0).

**Copy Attention**: For the last transformer block, instead of computing attention over value projections, a dedicated copy head attends over the raw input embeddings XWE. The copy contribution is mixed into the final token prediction: pcopy(·|X) = softmax(layernorm(YL + Z)WE⊤). This shortcut allows copying tokens whose embeddings are indistinguishable to deep MLP layers.

**Experiment Design**: A 10-layer, 60–75M parameter decoder-only model (NanoDO framework) is trained from scratch on procedurally-generated propositional logic problems with vocabulary sizes |P| ∈ {150, 600, 2400, 9600} as a proxy for symbolic diversity. 100 tokens are deliberately held out as the unseen set. Three evaluation conditions: (1) all tokens seen, (2) query unseen, (3) all predicates unseen. Training ablations span: trainable WE, frozen WE, periodic reinit at {100, 1k} steps for {10k, 50k} steps.

**Gemma 3 Analysis**: SVD of (un)embedding matrices and pairwise cosine similarities of unused vs. used token embeddings across 1B, 4B, 12B, 27B instruction-tuned models. Finetuning experiment using 80 predicates encoded as (a) unused tokens, (b) English adjectives, (c) ASCII letters.

## Results & Highlights

- **Standard transformers fail** on all test conditions with unseen tokens regardless of vocabulary diversity.
- **Copy attention alone** generalizes with a single unseen query token (needing |P| ≥ 9600) but fails when all predicates are unseen — explained by cosine similarities of unseen unembeddings approaching 1.0 during training.
- **Frozen embeddings** robustly generalize to all test conditions across all vocabulary sizes, with copy attention providing further accuracy gains or lower diversity thresholds.
- **Active forgetting** (periodic reinit throughout training) achieves similar results to frozen WE with sufficient diversity; temporary active forgetting alone (first 10k steps) fails the standard architecture but succeeds with copy attention even when cosine similarity reaches ~0.9 at test time.
- **Best combination**: frozen/periodically-reset WE + copy attention + |P| ≥ 600 → near-perfect accuracy on all conditions.
- **Gemma 3**: Unused token cosine similarities: 1B→0.78, 4B→0.33, 12B→0.23, 27B→0.24 (vs. used: 0.09, 0.03, 0.02, 0.01). Finetuning with unused tokens reaches 90% in ~5000 steps vs. ~500 steps with adjectives/letters.
- SVD analysis shows instruction-tuning increases top-2 singular values of (un)embedding matrix (suggesting further collapse during IT), most pronounced in 1B.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: High. The embedding collapse phenomenon for unseen tokens has not been explicitly characterized with this theoretical precision before. The mechanistic explanation for active forgetting and the practical combination of fixes are genuine contributions. Builds carefully on prior work (Boix-Adserà et al. 2024, Anand et al. 2025).

**Rigor**: Good theoretical grounding. Lemma 4.1 makes precise assumptions (bounded feature norm, labels absent from training data). The key assumption — that the model fits training data so pt → 0 — is tested empirically. The AdamW case (used in practice) is only handled empirically, not theoretically; this is honestly acknowledged.

**Reproducibility**: High for synthetic experiments — NanoDO library, exact hyperparameters provided, procedural data generation described in detail. Gemma 3 experiments use publicly available weights with specified eval procedures.

**Significance**: Meaningful for interpretability, finetuning practice, and understanding ICL. The finding that unused Gemma 3 tokens are collapsed and slow down finetuning has direct practical implications for anyone using reserved vocabulary slots.

**Red Flags**: (1) All synthetic experiments use small 60–75M parameter models on a narrow domain (propositional logic). Generalizing to modern LLM pretraining at scale requires caution. (2) The "solution" of freezing embeddings degrades C4 validation loss significantly (Figure 10), making it impractical for general pretraining — the authors acknowledge this but don't offer a resolution. (3) The Gemma 3 analysis is observational and correlational; the causal story (collapse → slow finetuning) lacks ablation.

## Strengths

- Theoretical result is clean, non-trivial, and covers the practically important setting (changing features, AdamW approximated empirically).
- Controlled experiments are thorough — 4 embedding management conditions × 2 architectures × 4 vocabulary sizes × 3 evaluation conditions.
- Honest discussion of limitations including the C4 language modeling tradeoff.
- Real-model evidence in Gemma 3 bridges the gap between synthetic and practical settings.

## Limitations

- Experiments confined to propositional logic; no evidence the proposed fixes transfer to diverse NLP tasks or larger-scale pretraining.
- No clean solution for the fundamental tension: frozen embeddings hurt language modeling, trainable embeddings cause collapse.
- Multi-token symbol setting (Appendix F) reveals additional failure modes (length generalization, prefix confusion) that are not addressed.
- The Gemma 3 instruction-tuning collapse analysis is speculative ("plausible that these tokens are rarely seen in IT data, though we cannot definitively verify").

## Relevance

本文直接关联LLM训练稳定性和可解释性方向：embedding坍缩是一种训练过程中的表示退化现象，理解其机制对于设计稳定的预训练流程（尤其是处理reserved/unused tokens的初始化策略）和fine-tuning实践具有重要意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the collapse be prevented while keeping embeddings trainable — for example, via orthogonality regularization or randomized embedding augmentation during pretraining?
2. Does the same collapse dynamics occur for rare (rather than absent) tokens, and does this partially explain LLM failures on low-frequency vocabulary items?
3. How does the severity of collapse scale with model size and training compute — the Gemma 3 data suggests larger models collapse less, but the mechanism is unclear?
4. Can temperature scaling of the (un)embedding norms at test time partially compensate for collapse without architectural changes?
5. What is the impact of embedding collapse on retrieval-augmented settings where novel entity names appear in-context?
