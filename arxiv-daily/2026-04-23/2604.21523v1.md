> **一句话总结：** FOCUS 基准通过对图文理解（I2T）和文本生图（T2I）任务注入可控语义扰动，系统评估四种主流 VLM 评估者（Gemini、GPT、Claude、Qwen）在三种评估范式下的可靠性，发现单答案打分失败率超 50%，配对比较最可靠，且增加推理预算不一致地改善评估质量。

> **TL;DR:** FOCUS is a meta-evaluation benchmark that tests whether VLM-based evaluators (Gemini-3.1-Pro, GPT-5.4, Claude-Opus-4.6, Qwen3.5-397B) can reliably detect deliberate perturbations in image-text outputs. Testing 600 I2T and 750 T2I prompts across three evaluation paradigms (single-answer scoring, pairwise comparison, reference-guided) and four strategies (vanilla, with axes, with reference, with axes+reference), FOCUS finds that pairwise comparison is the most reliable paradigm, single-answer scoring fails in >50% of T2I perturbation cases, and increasing reasoning budget does not consistently improve evaluator accuracy.

## 核心贡献

- **FOCUS 元评估基准**：覆盖 I2T（600 提示，来自 7 个基准）和 T2I（750 提示，来自 7 个基准）两类任务，通过向黄金回答注入语义扰动来测试 VLM 评估者的可靠性。
- **双任务扰动分类体系**：I2T 扰动分为视觉定位（VG）、语义解释（SI）、视觉推理（VR）、长文本生成（LG）四类；T2I 扰动分为视觉保真度（VF）、场景连贯性（SC）、物理合理性（PP）、文字渲染（TR）四类。
- **三种评估范式 × 四种策略的系统性比较**：对单答案打分（SAS）、配对比较（PC）、参考引导打分（RGS）三种范式，分别在有无评估轴（Axes）、有无参考答案（Reference）四种策略下全面测试。
- **实践建议**：明确推荐配对比较（尤其是 Ax+R 策略）作为 VLM 评估的最可靠范式；发现增加推理预算（medium > high）不一致地改善评估质量。
- **跨 VLM 评估者比较**：揭示 Gemini-3.1-Pro 总体表现最佳，Claude-Opus-4.6 表现意外弱于排行榜预期，Qwen3.5-397B 在某些类别具有竞争力。

## Key Contributions

- **FOCUS Meta-Evaluation Benchmark**: A systematic benchmark for evaluating VLM evaluators (not VLMs themselves) using a perturbation-based ground truth. Gold responses are generated by frontier models (Gemini-3.1-Pro for I2T text, Gemini-3-Pro-Image for T2I images), then perturbed by an adversarial generation process followed by human validation using a custom PerturbVal tool.
- **Dual-Task Perturbation Taxonomy**: I2T perturbations cover: Visual Grounding (VG) — errors in identifying visual elements, Semantic Interpretation (SI) — errors in interpreting meaning, Visual Reasoning (VR) — errors in multi-step reasoning from visual inputs, Long-form Generation (LG) — errors in extended text outputs. T2I perturbations cover: Visual Fidelity (VF) — image quality/clarity failures, Scene Coherence (SC) — semantic inconsistencies between prompt and image, Physical Plausibility (PP) — physically impossible content, Text Rendering (TR) — text in images rendered incorrectly.
- **Systematic Paradigm × Strategy Evaluation**: Three evaluation paradigms: Single-Answer Scoring (SAS), Pairwise Comparison (PC), Reference-Guided Scoring (RGS). Four strategies per paradigm: V (vanilla, no extras), Ax (with evaluation axes/criteria), R (with reference answer/image), Ax+R (with both). This 3×4 grid produces 12 evaluation protocols, applied to 4 evaluator VLMs.
- **Human Validation via PerturbVal**: A custom interface for human annotators to validate that generated perturbations are: (a) semantically meaningful errors, not typos/formatting, (b) unambiguously worse than the gold response, (c) non-trivially detectable.
- **Practical Recommendations**: The paper concludes with concrete guidelines for practitioners on which paradigm and strategy to use for different VLM evaluation scenarios.

## Methodology

**Benchmark Construction.**

*I2T arm*: Source 600 prompts from 7 established benchmarks: MMBench, MMDocBench, RealWorldQA, MME, SeedBench, MMVet, and LLaVA-Bench. For each prompt, generate a gold response using Gemini-3.1-Pro (highest available frontier model). Generate perturbed responses by prompting Gemini-3.1-Pro to introduce specific error types (one per perturbation category). Human annotators use PerturbVal to verify perturbation quality.

*T2I arm*: Source 750 prompts from 7 benchmarks: T2I-CoReBench, T2I-ReasonBench, MJBench, GenAI-Bench, EvalProTe, DrawBench, and PartiPrompts. Generate gold images using Gemini-3-Pro-Image. Perturb by generating alternative images that introduce specific error types. Human annotators validate.

**Evaluation Paradigms.**
- *Single-Answer Scoring (SAS)*: Present the evaluator with a prompt, a response (gold or perturbed), and ask for a score (typically 1–5 or 1–10). A reliable evaluator should score gold responses higher than perturbed ones.
- *Pairwise Comparison (PC)*: Present the evaluator with a prompt, the gold response, and the perturbed response (order randomized), and ask which is better. A reliable evaluator should choose the gold response.
- *Reference-Guided Scoring (RGS)*: Present the evaluator with a prompt, a reference gold response (or image), and the response to evaluate, asking for a score relative to the reference.

**Strategies.** Each paradigm is tested with: V (just the prompt + response), Ax (plus a list of evaluation criteria relevant to the task), R (plus the gold reference), Ax+R (all three extras). For SAS and RGS, "R" means providing the gold as explicit reference. For PC, "R" is redundant with the paradigm itself.

**Evaluator VLMs.** Gemini-3.1-Pro, GPT-5.4, Claude-Opus-4.6, Qwen3.5-397B-A17B. Each is also tested at three reasoning budget levels (low/medium/high thinking tokens) where applicable.

**Metrics.** Primary: perturbation detection rate (fraction of gold-vs-perturbed comparisons where the evaluator correctly identifies the gold as better). Secondary: false positive rate (marking correct responses as flawed).

## Results & Highlights

**By Paradigm:**
- Single-Answer Scoring: worst performer; failure rate exceeds 50% for T2I perturbations in multiple categories. The evaluator frequently assigns high scores to perturbed responses.
- Pairwise Comparison: best performer overall; most reliable across all VLMs and perturbation categories.
- Reference-Guided Scoring: middle ground; better than SAS, worse than PC. The reference helps but does not fully close the gap with pairwise comparison.

**By Evaluator VLM:**
- Gemini-3.1-Pro: consistently best across paradigms and perturbation types.
- GPT-5.4: competitive with Gemini, slight edge in Text Rendering (TR) category.
- Claude-Opus-4.6: surprisingly underperforms relative to its leaderboard ranking; particularly weak on T2I evaluation.
- Qwen3.5-397B-A17B: competitive on I2T tasks, weaker on T2I.

**By Strategy:**
- Ax (adding evaluation axes) consistently helps across all paradigms.
- R (adding reference) provides the largest single improvement.
- Ax+R is generally the best strategy; the two additions are complementary.

**Reasoning Budget:**
- Medium reasoning budget frequently outperforms high budget—increasing reasoning tokens beyond a threshold does not improve (and sometimes degrades) evaluation accuracy.
- Evaluators sometimes detect errors in their own justifications during extended reasoning but still assign the same (incorrect) score—a notable failure mode where reasoning and scoring become decoupled.

**Hardest Categories:**
- T2I: Physical Plausibility (PP) and Scene Coherence (SC) are hardest to detect.
- I2T: Long-form Generation (LG) is hardest, likely because errors are diffuse rather than point-specific.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: Meta-evaluation (evaluating evaluators) is an active area, with prior work like LLM-Eval-Bench and PandaLM-Eval. FOCUS's specific contribution is: (a) extending to the T2I modality, (b) using perturbation-based ground truth rather than human preference correlation, (c) systematic comparison of paradigms × strategies in a single study. The perturbation framework is cleaner than preference-correlation-based meta-evaluation.

**Rigor**: The benchmark construction methodology (gold responses from frontier models, human validation via PerturbVal) is carefully designed. The 3×4 paradigm×strategy grid is comprehensive. The finding about reasoning budget (medium > high) is important and non-obvious. However, the use of Gemini-3.1-Pro to generate both gold responses AND perturbed responses introduces a potential conflict of interest that could inflate Gemini's evaluator performance.

**Reproducibility**: High in principle—the benchmark will presumably be released, the prompt templates are in the appendix, and the evaluation protocol is fully specified. However, the frontier model API versions (GPT-5.4, Gemini-3.1-Pro as of paper submission) will drift, making exact replication harder over time.

**Significance**: High for researchers and practitioners using VLMs as automated evaluators—this is increasingly common practice as human evaluation becomes expensive. The finding that single-answer scoring is unreliable above 50% failure rate is a strong warning against a widely used pattern.

**Red Flags**: (1) Gold responses generated by Gemini-3.1-Pro may advantage Gemini as an evaluator (it's evaluating outputs in its own "style"). (2) The paper does not report inter-annotator agreement for the PerturbVal human validation step, leaving quality of ground truth uncertain. (3) Claude-Opus-4.6's poor performance is surprising and warrants investigation—is this a genuine capability gap, a prompt format sensitivity, or a systematic bias in the evaluation framework against Claude's output style? (4) Results are reported as aggregate metrics; per-model per-category tables are in the appendix, making the main paper harder to parse.

## Strengths

- Perturbation-based ground truth is cleaner and more controllable than preference correlation
- Comprehensive paradigm × strategy comparison reveals actionable recommendations
- T2I meta-evaluation is underexplored; this benchmark fills a genuine gap
- The reasoning budget analysis reveals a non-obvious failure mode (reasoning/scoring decoupling)
- Large-scale: 600+750 prompts across 14 source benchmarks, 4 evaluators × 12 protocols

## Limitations

- Potential Gemini self-evaluation advantage: gold responses and perturbations generated by the same model being evaluated
- No inter-annotator agreement statistics for PerturbVal human validation
- All gold responses generated by a single frontier model; does not capture the diversity of real-world VLM outputs
- The perturbation categories are manually defined; a different taxonomy might reveal different patterns
- Does not evaluate open-source evaluator VLMs (only closed-API frontier models)

## Relevance

本文与 LLM/VLM 推理评估可靠性和评估方法论直接相关：FOCUS 揭示了当前广泛使用的单答案打分（SAS）范式的严重可靠性问题，对依赖 VLM 自动化评估的研究（尤其是多模态任务）具有重要警示意义；配对比较优于单打分的发现，对设计更可靠的 LLM 评估系统（包括 inference evaluation pipeline）有直接指导价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Is Gemini-3.1-Pro's superior evaluator performance partly a self-evaluation artifact (evaluating outputs in its own generation style)? A clean test would use human-written gold responses and GPT-perturbed versions to evaluate Gemini.
2. The reasoning/scoring decoupling failure mode (evaluator detects errors in its chain-of-thought but doesn't change the score) suggests that scoring and reasoning are processed by different "mechanisms"—is this a known phenomenon in LLM reasoning?
3. Does the pairwise comparison paradigm generalize to open-ended generation tasks where neither response is objectively correct? Or is it only reliable when ground truth is available?
4. Would ensemble evaluation (average scores from multiple evaluators) improve reliability above the best single evaluator, and is this practical for production evaluation pipelines?
5. The paper finds TR (Text Rendering) hardest for T2I evaluation—does this persist when the evaluator is explicitly instructed to zoom in and inspect text elements, or is it a fundamental multimodal comprehension limitation?
