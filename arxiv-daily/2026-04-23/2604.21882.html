Revisiting Non-Verbatim Memorization in Large Language Models: The Role of Entity Surface Forms

arXiv: 2604.21882v1

Date: 2026-04-23

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21882

一句话总结： 构建RedirectQA数据集（利用Wikipedia重定向信息为同一Wikidata事实三元组关联多种实体表面形式），在13个LLM上证明实体命名方式变化会导致事实QA正确率发生不可忽视的翻转，且别名/缩写形式比拼写变体更容易导致不一致，实体频率比表面形式频率对准确率的预测力更强。
TL;DR: RedirectQA is a new factual QA dataset pairing the same Wikidata triples with multiple categorized entity surface forms (aliases, abbreviations, spelling variants, common errors) via Wikipedia redirect information. Evaluated across 13 LLMs, the paper shows factual prediction outcomes often change when only the entity name changes, inconsistency is category-dependent, and entity-level frequency (not just surface-level frequency) drives factual access — suggesting cross-surface coupling rather than independent per-surface memorization.

核心贡献

  • 构建RedirectQA：将Wikidata事实三元组（主语、关系、宾语）与Wikipedia重定向页面信息结合，为每个实体关联规范表面形式（canonical）和多种重定向表面形式（别名/缩写、拼写变体、典型错误），最终包含30,560个表面实例，14,672个规范+15,888个重定向
  • 设计"一致性"评估框架（canonical-redirect pair），控制事实关系和金标答案不变，仅改变主语实体的表面形式，精确量化表面形式对事实访问的影响
  • 在13个LLM（Pythia、OLMo 2、OpenSciRef、LLaMA 3.1、Qwen 3、GPT-4o-mini）上验证：23.7%的canonical-redirect对（Pythia-12B on PopQA pilot）产生不一致预测
  • 频率分解分析：将实体频率（跨所有表面形式的总出现次数）与表面形式频率（特定表面形式的出现次数）解耦，发现两者均正相关准确率，但在规范表面形式子集中，控制实体频率后表面频率的偏相关接近零或负值
  • 发布RedirectQA数据集（CC BY-SA 4.0），代码通过HuggingFace公开

Key Contributions

  • Introduces RedirectQA: 30,560 surface instances from 14,672 factual triples, with redirect surface forms categorized into 33 categories across 3 broad types: Alternative Names & Abbreviations (8,667 pairs), Spelling Variants (4,928 pairs), Typical Errors (2,884 pairs)
  • Establishes surface-form consistency analysis: for each canonical–redirect pair, computes correctness under both surface forms and classifies as Consistent (both correct or both incorrect) vs. Inconsistent (correctness flips)
  • Demonstrates the inconsistency is category-dependent: Spelling Variants show highest consistency (models robust to minor orthographic changes like diacritics, punctuation); Alternative Names & Abbreviations show substantially lower consistency (aliases, initials most challenging); Typical Errors fall in between
  • Decomposes entity frequency into entity-level (aggregate across all surface forms) and surface-level (specific to the target surface form) frequency components via DBpedia Spotlight entity linking
  • Partial correlation analysis reveals entity frequency maintains significant positive partial correlation with accuracy after controlling for surface frequency; surface frequency partial correlation with accuracy is near zero or negative in the canonical-only subset, suggesting cross-surface coupling dominates for canonical names

Methodology

Dataset construction: (1) Collect factual triples from Wikidata (16 relation types, e.g., occupation), following Mallen et al. (2023) setup; filter to entities with unique English labels and Wikipedia pages, randomly sample 500k triples. (2) Collect Wikipedia redirect information for each entity, manually select 33 frequent redirect categories representing surface-form variation; categorize into Alt./Abbrev., Spell. Var., Typ. Err.; downsample overrepresented categories for class balance (~30k surface instances). (3) Generate question realizations using 2 templates per relation type (original from Mallen et al., and GPT-4o paraphrase), giving 61,120 question realizations total.

Evaluated models: 13 LLMs across 3 transparency tiers: transparent (Pythia 410M/2.8B/6.9B/12B, OpenSciRef 0.4B/1.7B, OLMo 2 1B/7B/13B/32B), open-weight (Qwen 3 30B-A3B-Instruct, LLaMA 3.1 8B-Instruct), proprietary (GPT-4o-mini). 15-shot prompting, temperature 0, alias-aware string matching for evaluation.

Consistency analysis: For each canonical–redirect pair, a pair is "consistent" iff correctness outcome is the same under both surface forms. Analyzed separately for pairs where canonical is answered correctly vs. incorrectly.

Frequency analysis: Entity frequency = total DBpedia-linked mentions; surface frequency = mentions with span exactly matching the surface form. Pearson partial correlations ρ(Ent, Acc | Surf) and ρ(Surf, Acc | Ent) computed separately for overall, canonical-only, and redirect-only subsets. Steiger's Z-test for significance comparison between entity and surface frequency correlations.

Results & Highlights

  • Preliminary (Pythia-12B on PopQA-redirect): 23.7% of 14,489 canonical–redirect pairs show inconsistent predictions
  • Overall consistency (13 LLMs, Figure 2): Non-negligible correctness flips across all model classes; larger models more consistent but not monotonically across all categories
  • Category-wise consistency: Spelling Variants highest; Alternative Names & Abbreviations substantially lower; initialism redirects (e.g., "NYT" for "The New York Times") are especially challenging
  • Reverse pattern: Some entities are answered correctly via redirect but not via canonical surface — canonical Wikipedia title is not always the surface form through which LLMs best access facts
  • Frequency partial correlations (Table 2): For OLMo 2 (1B→32B): ρ(Ent, Acc|Surf) = 0.132→0.064 (all significant); ρ(Surf, Acc|Ent) = 0.049→0.083 (significant at smaller sizes, weaker at larger). In canonical-only subset, ρ(Surf, Acc|Ent) is near zero or negative, while ρ(Ent, Acc|Surf) remains consistently positive
  • High-frequency redirect analysis (Table 6): After excluding extremely low-frequency redirects (f_surf > 10), entity frequency remains significantly associated with accuracy; surface frequency partial correlation becomes smaller and often non-significant for Pythia/OpenSciRef, confirming cross-surface coupling interpretation
  • Entity-linking probe (Table 7): Pythia-12B balanced accuracy on recognizing two surface forms as the same entity = 0.522, near random — models cannot reliably identify surface-form equivalences, yet surface-frequency effects persist

Critical Assessment

Novelty: Solid. The key contribution — using Wikipedia redirects to systematically vary surface forms while holding the underlying fact fixed — is elegant and fills a genuine gap. Prior work on factual memorization typically uses canonical names only and attributes failures to "entity popularity" without disentangling surface effects.

Rigor: Strong. The partial correlation framework properly controls for confounds, the Steiger's Z-test for comparing correlated Pearson correlations is appropriate, and the high-frequency filter ablation (Section 4.4) addresses the concern that redirect results are driven by low-frequency rare forms. Two question templates are used to rule out template sensitivity.

Reproducibility: High — dataset released under CC BY-SA 4.0, models are public or API-accessible, all hyperparameters disclosed. The 8-bit quantization for local inference is noted as a potential source of small performance differences.

Significance: Important for LLM evaluation methodology. The finding that canonical surface forms may actually be harder than some redirect forms challenges the assumption that "canonical = best access path." The entity-frequency dominance over surface-frequency in canonical subsets has implications for how we should estimate "what an LLM knows" — aggregate entity frequency may be a better proxy than surface-specific frequency.

Red flags: (1) English-only — Wikipedia/Wikidata naming conventions have English-specific biases. (2) Redirect categories are manually curated (33 out of many possible) — coverage may miss important surface variation types. (3) The entity-linking quality (DBpedia Spotlight) introduces noise; zero-frequency filtering may remove surface forms the model saw but DBpedia missed. (4) Causal interpretation of frequency effects is not established — correlation doesn't prove the frequency drives memorization.

Strengths

  • Large, well-constructed dataset with controlled surface-form variation and categorial structure
  • Cross-surface coupling finding (entity frequency > surface frequency for canonical forms) challenges dominant assumptions in the factual memorization literature
  • Reverse inconsistency pattern (redirect correct, canonical incorrect) is a genuinely surprising finding with interpretive implications
  • Careful robustness checks: question template paraphrase, high-frequency filter, entity-linking binary probe

Limitations

  • English-only; Wikipedia/Wikidata naming conventions reflect Wikimedia editorial practices, not general language use
  • Does not directly probe internal representations or training dynamics that give rise to surface-dependent access
  • Object-side surface variation and question paraphrasing beyond subject entity are outside scope
  • Dataset covers 16 relation types — coverage and diversity may not generalise to other factual domains (scientific facts, events, etc.)
  • The entity-linking binary probe (balanced accuracy 0.522) suggests the model doesn't explicitly represent surface-form equivalence, but this doesn't explain the mechanism of cross-surface coupling

Relevance

与用户关注的推理评估和ML可靠性方向高度相关：RedirectQA揭示了LLM事实知识访问对实体命名方式的敏感性，这是一种"沉默的"精度退化来源——在部署中用户可能自然使用别名、缩写或轻微拼错的实体名称，导致模型输出不一致，是一种值得关注的推理可靠性问题。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does instruction tuning (SFT or RLHF) systematically improve or degrade surface-form consistency compared to base models, and does the direction differ by category type?
  2. Can surface-form consistency be improved by including diverse surface forms during fine-tuning (data augmentation with redirect aliases), and at what cost to overall accuracy?
  3. What is the internal mechanism of cross-surface coupling — do entities with diverse surface forms develop shared internal entity representations, or is it statistical co-occurrence in training data?
  4. How do surface-form effects interact with retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)? If the retrieved context uses a different surface form than the query, does accuracy improve or degrade?
  5. Do multilingual models show stronger cross-surface consistency for languages with more systematic morphological variation (e.g., German compound words, Finnish case suffixes)?
