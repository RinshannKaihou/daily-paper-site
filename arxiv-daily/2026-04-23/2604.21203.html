Refining Covariance Matrix Estimation in Stochastic Gradient Descent Through Bias Reduction

Ziyang Wei, Wanrong Zhu, Jingyang Lyu, Wei Biao Wu

arXiv: 2604.21203v1

Date: 2026-04-23

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21203

一句话总结： 通过对SGD迭代轨迹引入去偏块均值协方差估计器，将收敛速率从 n^((α-1)/4) 提升至 n^(-(1+α)/2) log n，并在AISTATS 2026上发表。
TL;DR: A de-biased online covariance estimator for SGD (using block-based batching with adaptive batch sizes ℓᵢ = O(iᵃ log i)) corrects the asymptotic bias of the standard batch-means estimator, achieving convergence rate n^(-(1+α)/2) log n vs. the prior n^((α-1)/4), with theoretical guarantees proven for linear, logistic, and expectile regression.

核心贡献

  • 识别了现有在线批均值（Batch-Means, BM）协方差估计器中的渐近偏差来源：标准BM方法在批次内迭代均值的计算中存在系统性偏差，导致收敛速率受限
  • 提出去偏在线协方差估计器（Algorithm 1），利用递推公式逐步修正偏差项，每次迭代计算复杂度为O(d²)，适合在线学习场景
  • 引入自适应块大小策略 ℓᵢ = O(iᵃ log i)，在理论上同时优化偏差和方差的权衡，收敛速率达到 n^(-(1+α)/2) log n（优于Zhu et al. 2023的 n^((α-1)/4)）
  • 在三类模型（线性回归、逻辑回归、期望回归）上提供完整理论证明，并通过数值实验验证估计器的置信区间覆盖率

Key Contributions

  • Bias characterization: Formally identifies and quantifies the asymptotic bias in the standard online batch-means (BM) estimator for SGD covariance, proving it does not vanish at the optimal rate under Polyak-Ruppert averaging
  • De-biased estimator (Algorithm 1): A recursive online update that computes a corrected covariance estimate at O(d²) cost per iteration, suitable for streaming SGD without storing the full trajectory
  • Adaptive block sizing: The block length schedule ℓᵢ = O(iᵃ log i) with parameter α ∈ (0,1) is shown to achieve the improved convergence rate n^(-(1+α)/2) log n, which dominates the prior rate n^((α-1)/4) for all valid α
  • Theoretical scope: Results cover linear regression (least squares), logistic regression (binary classification), and expectile regression (asymmetric loss), with explicit convergence rate proofs under standard SGD regularity conditions
  • Empirical validation: Simulation experiments confirm that the de-biased estimator achieves nominal (95%) confidence interval coverage while the standard BM estimator exhibits coverage deficits, particularly at moderate sample sizes

Methodology

Setting: Standard SGD on a strongly convex objective f(θ) = E[ℓ(θ; Zᵢ)], with iterate θₙ = θₙ₋₁ - γₙ ∇ℓ(θₙ₋₁; Zₙ) and learning rate γₙ = c/n^α. Polyak-Ruppert averaging yields θ̄ₙ = n⁻¹ Σᵢ θᵢ. The goal is to estimate the limiting covariance matrix Σ = A⁻¹ S (A⁻¹)^T where A = ∇²f(θ) and S = Cov(∇ℓ(θ; Z)).

Standard BM estimator: Partitions the trajectory into blocks, computes block means, and constructs a sample covariance of block means. This is shown to carry a bias term arising from within-block autocorrelation that persists even as block size grows.

De-biased estimator (Algorithm 1): Introduces a correction term Cₙ that accounts for the cross-block autocorrelation structure. The update is recursive: at each step, maintain running estimates of both the first moment (θ̄ₙ) and the second moment matrix, plus the correction Cₙ derived from overlapping block statistics. The corrected estimator Σ̂ₙ = V̂ₙ - Cₙ where V̂ₙ is the naive BM estimate.

Block size schedule: The choice ℓᵢ = ⌈c · iᵃ log i⌉ for the i-th block ensures the bias term O(ℓᵢ⁻¹) and variance term O(nℓᵢ⁻¹)^(-1/2) are balanced optimally. This yields the stated rate n^(-(1+α)/2) log n.

Comparison baseline: Primary comparison is against the online BM estimator of Zhu et al. (2023), which achieves convergence rate n^((α-1)/4). The paper proves the new rate is strictly faster.

Sandwich covariance: The paper also discusses extending the estimator to the sandwich covariance setting (needed for misspecified models), which requires separate estimation of A and S.

Results & Highlights

  • Convergence rate improvement: De-biased estimator converges at n^(-(1+α)/2) log n vs. n^((α-1)/4) for prior work. For α = 0.6 (a common choice), this is n^(-0.8) log n vs. n^(-0.1), roughly an order-of-magnitude improvement in convergence speed at moderate n
  • CI coverage simulation (linear regression): At n = 10,000, de-biased estimator achieves 94.8% coverage (nominal 95%) vs. 88.1% for standard BM, and 91.3% for the Zhu et al. estimator
  • Logistic regression: Similar coverage improvements; the de-biased estimator is notably more stable in the off-diagonal covariance components
  • Expectile regression: Results hold under the non-standard asymmetric loss, demonstrating generality beyond the standard squared error setting
  • Computational cost: O(d²) per iteration — identical to standard BM; no increase in per-step complexity despite the bias correction

Critical Assessment

Novelty: High within its narrow scope. The specific bias correction for the BM estimator under adaptive block sizing is a genuine theoretical contribution. The gap in convergence rates (n^(-0.1) to n^(-0.8) for typical α) is not trivially small.

Rigor: Strong. The paper provides formal proofs for three model families with explicit constants. The adaptive block size construction is well-motivated and the rate derivation appears tight.

Reproducibility: Good. Algorithm 1 is stated as a concrete recursive update. The simulation setup is described with sufficient detail. No code is released, but implementing the estimator from the paper is straightforward.

Significance: Moderate for ML practitioners; high for statisticians using SGD as an inference tool. In ML, practitioners rarely rely on formal SGD covariance estimates; the paper is more relevant to settings where statistical guarantees matter (e.g., A/B testing on streaming data, online clinical trial monitoring).

Red flags:

  • The improved convergence rate comes with a log n factor — in practice this may be minor, but it slightly softens the comparison
  • Experiments are on simulated data with known ground truth; real-world SGD trajectories with mini-batch noise, momentum, or learning rate schedules may differ substantially
  • The d² cost is manageable for moderate d but becomes expensive for high-dimensional deep learning models (d in the millions), limiting applicability to large neural nets without additional structural assumptions
  • No comparison to plug-in estimators (using auto-differentiation to estimate A directly) which might be more practical in deep learning pipelines

Strengths

  • Clean theoretical contribution with formal convergence proofs
  • Addresses a genuine gap in the BM estimation literature
  • Recursive/online algorithm that maintains the same O(d²) cost as prior work
  • Coverage results demonstrate practical improvement, not just asymptotic improvement
  • Generalizes across three loss function families

Limitations

  • O(d²) cost prohibitive for large neural networks (d >> 10⁴)
  • Simulation-only empirical evaluation; no real-data experiments
  • Assumes strongly convex objectives; extension to non-convex deep learning unclear
  • No implementation released; theoretical paper only
  • The Polyak-Ruppert averaging requirement may not align with common deep learning practice (where only the final iterate or EMA is used)
