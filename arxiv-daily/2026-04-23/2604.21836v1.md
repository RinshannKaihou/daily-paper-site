> **一句话总结：** 利用广义普鲁克鲁斯分析在单刺激层面量化视觉模型的表示分散度，发现低分散度（模型高度一致）的刺激比高分散度刺激产生高达两倍的视觉-语言跨模态对齐，为柏拉图表示假设提供了更精细的单刺激层面验证。

> **TL;DR:** Using Generalized Procrustes Analysis (GPA) to quantify per-stimulus representational dispersion across vision models, this paper shows that stimuli on which vision models strongly agree (low intra-modal dispersion) yield up to 2× higher cross-modal alignment with language models compared to high-dispersion stimuli, offering a stimulus-level mechanistic lens on representational convergence.

## 核心贡献

- 将广义普鲁克鲁斯分析（GPA）引入神经网络表示对比，首次在单刺激层面量化多个视觉模型的表示分散度
- 提出两种分散度汇总策略：均值残差排序法（对异常模型敏感）和残差空间PCA的PC1得分法（更稳健）
- 发现低分散度刺激与高分散度刺激相比，跨模态（视觉-语言）对齐提升最高达2倍（DINOv2与语言模型配对）
- 通过留一验证（held-out模型）证明刺激层面分散度捕获的是跨模型族共享的属性（r=0.21~0.43, p<0.001）
- 为"柏拉图表示假设"提供了单刺激层面的更严格检验，超越了原工作的全局统计层面

## Key Contributions

- Introduces stimulus-specific dispersion measurement via Generalized Procrustes Analysis (GPA) applied to representations from four ViT variants (MAE, DINOv2, CLIP, CLIP+ft), computing Procrustes residuals r_ij = ||(N_i T_i)_j − (N_joint)_j||₂ for each stimulus j and model i
- Demonstrates robustness of the intra-modal dispersion to cross-modal alignment correspondence across two stimulus selection strategies (mean rank ordering and PCA-based PC1 scoring) and all tested vision-language model pairings
- Provides held-out model validation: dispersion computed from 3 models significantly predicts the 4th model's deviation (Pearson r = 0.21–0.43, Spearman ρ = 0.10–0.29, all p<0.001)
- Ablation shows excluding the top 25% high-dispersion stimuli from the full 1024-sample WIT set increases CKNNA alignment across all 12 LLMs tested, while excluding bottom 25% low-dispersion stimuli decreases it
- Applies to ICLR 2026 Workshop on Representational Alignment (Re-Align)

## Methodology

**Vision models:** Four ViT architectures with distinct training objectives: MAE (vit_huge_patch14_224.mae), DINOv2 (vit_giant_patch14_dinov2.lvd142m, dim 1536), CLIP (vit_huge_patch14_clip_224.laion2b, dim 1280), and CLIP+ft on ImageNet-12K. CLS-token representations extracted from the penultimate transformer block; non-DINOv2 models zero-padded to 1536 dimensions; all normalized to unit Frobenius norm.

**GPA procedure:** Minimizes Σᵢ ||NᵢTᵢ − N_joint||²_F subject to TᵢᵀTᵢ = Iₖ (orthogonal transformations only). This computes the Fréchet mean (barycenter) in Procrustes shape space. Scale normalisation ||Nᵢ||_F = 1 is applied before GPA; orthogonality preserves internal geometry (pairwise distances and angles).

**Stimulus dispersion:** Per-stimulus, per-model residuals r_ij = ||(NᵢTᵢ)_j − (N_joint)_j||₂. Summarised as (1) mean dispersion across models, or (2) PC1 score from PCA on the R ∈ ℝ^{m×M} residual matrix. The PC1 captures 58.21% of variance in the WIT dispersion space.

**Cross-modal alignment:** CKNNA (Centered Kernel k-Nearest Neighbors Alignment) with k=10 neighbors, measuring local representational similarity between selected stimulus subsets for vision models vs. BLOOM (560M–7B), OpenLLaMA (3B–13B), and LLaMA (7B–65B) families. LLM representations: mean-pooled token embeddings from the best-aligning transformer block.

**Dataset:** Wikipedia Image-Text (WIT) dataset, 1024 image-caption pairs. No filtering applied.

## Results & Highlights

- **Low vs. high dispersion alignment gap:** Low-dispersion stimuli achieve CKNNA up to 2× higher than high-dispersion stimuli, most pronounced for DINOv2 paired with larger LLaMA models (e.g., 0.6 vs. 0.3 CKNNA@10)
- **Consistency across selection strategies:** Both mean rank ordering (Fig. 2A-E) and PCA-based PC1 selection (Fig. 2F-J) show the same ordering: low < random < high dispersion yields monotonically decreasing alignment for all 4 vision models and all 12 LLMs
- **Effect magnitude:** The gap between low and high dispersion is smallest for MAE and largest for DINOv2 and CLIP+ft, suggesting models with stronger semantic structure (DINOv2) benefit more from stimulus filtering
- **Held-out validation (Supp. Fig. S2):** Pearson r between 3-model dispersion and held-out model deviation: MAE-H 0.33, DINOv2-G 0.21, CLIP-H 0.43, CLIP+ft-H 0.26 (all p<0.001), confirming dispersion is a shared property across model families
- **Drop analysis (Supp. Fig. S1):** Dropping high-dispersion 25% improves CKNNA above full-set baseline across all 4×12 pairings; dropping low-dispersion 25% consistently lowers it — monotonic effect

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Moderate but focused. GPA residuals as stimulus-level diagnostics are a natural extension — the paper explicitly notes that "stimulus-level residuals are a classical by-product of GPA." The contribution is in the application to neural network representations and the discovery that intra-modal dispersion predicts cross-modal alignment.

**Rigor:** The methodology is sound: GPA is well-grounded in shape theory, CKNNA is an established local alignment measure, and held-out model validation is a strong check for generalizability. However, the paper only tests 1024 WIT stimuli — a relatively small and specific dataset (Wikipedia image-text pairs with potentially unusual semantic properties). The effect size (up to 2×) is compelling but the absolute CKNNA values are not reported with confidence intervals.

**Reproducibility:** Moderate — model names are specified, dataset is public, but code is not released and GPA implementation details (convergence criterion, number of iterations) are not specified.

**Significance:** Meaningful for the representational alignment community. The stimulus-selection methodology could be used as a filter for alignment benchmarks, or to probe what properties drive cross-modal convergence. The paper promises future work to identify common features in low-dispersion stimuli, which is the most scientifically interesting next step.

**Red flags:** (1) Workshop paper — limited space means many details are in figures rather than tables, making quantitative comparison difficult. (2) Only one dataset (WIT 1024). (3) What makes a stimulus "low dispersion" is not characterised — the paper notes it as future work but this is the key missing piece for scientific understanding. (4) The "up to 2×" figure is the maximum case; typical differences may be smaller.

## Strengths

- Principled use of GPA as a metric in Procrustes shape space, with clear geometric interpretation
- Robustness demonstrated across two selection strategies and all tested model pairings (4 vision × 12 LLMs)
- Held-out model validation is a strong generalisability check
- The result directly sharpens the Platonic Representation Hypothesis: convergence is not global but stimulus-dependent

## Limitations

- Only one dataset (1024 WIT samples); unclear whether the effect generalises to other image-text datasets with different semantic distributions
- What semantic or visual properties characterise low-dispersion stimuli is not determined — this limits actionability
- Only ViT architectures tested; convolutional or hybrid architectures might show different dispersion patterns
- CKNNA with k=10 on 1024 stimuli means each stimulus's alignment is estimated from a small neighborhood; statistical power may be limited

## Relevance

与用户关注的可解释性和推理评估方向间接相关：该方法揭示了神经网络表示收敛的刺激层面规律，为理解多模态模型（视觉-语言）内部表示的对齐机制提供了新工具，对设计更鲁棒的评估基准（选择具有共识性的刺激）具有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. What semantic or low-level visual properties characterise low-dispersion stimuli — are they more concrete, more frequent, or more compositionally simple?
2. Would applying this stimulus-filtering methodology to alignment benchmarks (e.g., brain encoding benchmarks) improve the predictive validity of model rankings?
3. How does intra-modal dispersion relate to human perceptual disagreement — do images that humans describe differently in language also show higher dispersion across vision models?
4. Can the GPA framework be extended to directly compute cross-modal dispersion between vision and language spaces without reducing to CKNNA post-hoc?
5. Does the dispersion-alignment relationship hold for larger, more diverse datasets (e.g., COCO, LAION subsets), or is it specific to the encyclopedic, informative nature of Wikipedia captions?
