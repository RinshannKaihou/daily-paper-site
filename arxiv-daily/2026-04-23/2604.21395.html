Supervised Learning Has a Necessary Geometric Blind Spot: Theory, Consequences, and Minimal Repair

arXiv: 2604.21395v1

Date: 2026-04-23

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21395

一句话总结： 通过理论证明ERM监督学习必然在标签相关的干扰方向上保留非零雅可比敏感度（几何盲点），并提出PMH（Perturbation Matching Heuristic）用单个高斯正则化项修复该缺陷，在7个跨模态任务上验证，PGD对抗训练反而使该问题恶化。
TL;DR: This paper proves mathematically that any ERM-trained encoder must retain positive path-length distortion in label-correlated nuisance directions — a "geometric blind spot" that is architecture- and dataset-size-independent. It introduces TDI (Trajectory Deviation Index) as the diagnostic and PMH (Perturbation Matching Heuristic) as a one-term fix, validated across 7 tasks spanning vision, language, and foundation-model scale; adversarial PGD training is shown to actively worsen the blind spot.

核心贡献

  • 定理1（ERM编码器非等距性）：证明任何最小化监督损失的编码器，在标签相关的干扰方向上必然保持非零路径长度失真，且该下界与模型容量、数据集大小无关
  • 统一了四个独立实证发现（非鲁棒预测特征、纹理偏差、腐蚀脆弱性、鲁棒性-精度权衡）为同一几何机制的推论
  • TDI（轨迹偏差指数）：新诊断指标，测量各向同性扰动下的期望路径长度失真，是唯一能检测PGD/PMH分离的指标（CKA、内在维度、Jacobian Frobenius范数均无法检测）
  • PMH：通过添加单个高斯噪声匹配项（$\mathcal{L}_{PMH} = \|\phi(x)-\phi(x+\delta)\|^2, \delta\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\sigma^2I)$）修复几何盲点，是命题5证明的唯一能均匀抑制雅可比矩阵的扰动分布
  • 证明PGD对抗训练虽然降低Jacobian Frobenius范数（2.91 vs. ERM 34.58），但TDI反而更差（1.336 vs. ERM 1.093），揭示了"方向压缩"效应

Key Contributions

  • Theorem 1 (ERM Geometric Incompleteness): Proves any ERM minimiser maintains strictly positive linearised embedding drift D(φ*,σ) ≥ σ²ρ²C(P)/L² > 0 in label-correlated nuisance directions, where ρ is the nuisance-label correlation, L is the decoder Lipschitz constant, and C(P) depends only on the distribution
  • Corollary 4 (Adversarial Training Does Not Break the Bound): Formally proves PGD suppresses Jacobian Frobenius in the adversarial direction but increases anisotropy, worsening clean-input TDI — a falsifiable prediction empirically confirmed
  • Proposition 5 (Gaussian Noise is Uniquely Isotropic): Among all zero-mean distributions, Gaussian noise is the unique minimiser of the encoder Jacobian uniformly in every direction, making PMH the theoretically principled minimal fix
  • Unifies non-robust features (Ilyas et al.), texture bias (Geirhos et al.), corruption fragility (Hendrycks et al.), and robustness-accuracy tradeoff (Tsipras et al.) as corollaries of a single theorem
  • Demonstrates the blind spot worsens monotonically across BERT model scales (66M→340M, blind-spot ratio 0.860→0.742) and is amplified by task-specific ERM fine-tuning (+54%)

Methodology

The theoretical framework works in the Gaussian linear model (Definition 5.1): input x = (s, n) with signal s ~ N(0, I_d_s), nuisance n ~ N(0, I_d_n), label y = ⟨w_s, s⟩ + ρ⟨w_n, n⟩ + ε, with ρ = Corr(n, y) > 0. Theorem 1 is proved via: (1) showing any n-independent predictor pays excess loss ≥ ρ² above Bayes; (2) encoding n forces non-zero off-diagonal Jacobian blocks via Cauchy-Schwarz; (3) linearisation lemma converts this to positive path-length distortion. Corollary 2 extends to all distributions satisfying Definition 5.1 under any proper scoring rule.

The diagnostic TDI(φ, σ) := (1/L) Σ_ℓ E_{x,δ}[||φ^(1:ℓ)(x+δ) − φ^(1:ℓ)(x)||² / E_x[||φ^(1:ℓ)(x)||²]], measured at σ=0.01 (Taylor error < 0.1%), averages expected squared path-length distortion under isotropic perturbation across L layers.

PMH loss: L_PMH = ||φ(x) − φ(x+δ)||², δ ~ N(0, σ²I), added to the task loss with λw(t) weighting (cosine ramp, cap 0.30). σ ∈ [0.05, 0.15], applied at 2-3 intermediate backbone scales. Compute overhead: ~1.3× wall-clock time per epoch.

Experiments span 7 tasks: CIFAR-10 ResNet-18 classification (T01), PROTEINS graph classification (T02), QM9 molecular regression (T03), CIFAR-10 ViT from scratch (T04), Human pose estimation Hourglass CNN (T05), Market-1501 Re-ID ResNet-50 (T06), CheXpert chest X-ray ResNet-50 (T07). Language: BERT fine-tuned on SST-2. Foundation model scale: ImageNet ViT-B/16 (CLIP/DINO/SAM backbone).

Results & Highlights

  • Core mechanistic result (Task 04, CIFAR-10 ViT): PMH achieves TDI@0=0.904 vs. ERM 1.093 vs. PGD 1.336 — PGD is worse than no regularisation at all, confirming Corollary 4. PMH achieves lowest Jacobian Frobenius (8.08) among regularised methods, with PGD achieving 2.91 (suppressed directionally) vs. ERM 34.58.
  • Cross-task headline (Table 4): PMH wins 5/7 tasks on headline robust metrics; B0 (ERM) wins Task 05 (pose), VAT wins Task 06 (Re-ID). Task 03 (QM9) is an exception explained by Theorem 1 — atomic positions are the signal, not nuisance.
  • BERT SST-2: PMH reduces paraphrase drift (synonym CLS displacement) by 76.9% (4.38→1.01) at <1 pp accuracy cost (93.12%→92.43%). TDI reduced 28.7-30.3%.
  • Scale universality: Blind-spot ratio decreases monotonically from 0.860 (DistilBERT-66M) to 0.742 (BERT-large-340M), confirming larger models encode nuisance MORE precisely, not less.
  • Fine-tuning hierarchy: ERM fine-tuning increases blind-spot drift 54% relative to pretrained baseline (0.0244→0.0375); PMH repairs it 11× (→0.0033).
  • ImageNet ViT-B/16: PMH fine-tuning (24 epochs) reduces TDI@0 by 23.9% while increasing intra-class representation distance +64%.
  • Corruption robustness (Table 5): TDI ranking (B0>VAT>PMH) correctly predicts robustness ranking across all Gaussian noise levels; PMH generalises to non-Gaussian corruptions (blur, brightness, contrast) within 0.5 pp of E1_no_PMH, dominating VAT and B0.
  • Adversarial training (Task 04): PGD achieves highest clean accuracy L6 probe retention (0.948 at σ=0.1) but has TDI floor 1.336 — Jac. Fro 2.91 (lowest) yet worst isotropic geometry.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: High. The theoretical necessity result (not just existence) distinguishes this from Jacobian regularisation literature (CAE, DAE, [9-11]). The uniqueness result for Gaussian perturbations (Proposition 5) is a clean one-line proof with real implications. TDI as a diagnostic is the most novel practical contribution.

Rigor: The proofs are for the Gaussian linear model, which is an idealisation. Corollary 2 extends to general correlated-nuisance distributions, but the quantitative bound involves C(P) which is distribution-specific and difficult to estimate. The claim that "every model trained on any distribution satisfying Definition 5.1" must have a blind spot is only as strong as how many real-world datasets satisfy that definition.

Reproducibility: Good — code released, seed disclosed, FGSM non-determinism correction noted (cudnn.benchmark), per-task full tables in appendix. Task 03 failure is discussed honestly.

Significance: Potentially high for the ML robustness community. The PGD insight (that adversarial training worsens isotropic geometry while improving worst-case geometry) is counter-intuitive and practically important. The RLHF implication (format signals, verbosity preferences, writing register are label-correlated nuisances that ERM must encode) is speculative but interesting.

Red flags: (1) Single-author preprint — no peer review yet. (2) Theorem 1 is an existence result: it identifies which directions must be sensitive but does not predict their magnitude per-model. (3) The "four consequences" framing may overstate unification — texture bias and adversarial vulnerability have distinct causes that Theorem 1 only partially explains. (4) Task 05 (pose estimation) and Task 07 (chest X-ray) results are mixed, suggesting PMH is not universally optimal.

Strengths

  • Clean, falsifiable mathematical theorem with a one-line proof of the uniqueness result (Proposition 5)
  • TDI detects the PGD/ERM dissociation that Jacobian Frobenius norm, CKA, and intrinsic dimension all miss — a genuine diagnostic gap filled
  • PMH requires no architectural changes and adds only ~1.3× compute overhead
  • The fine-tuning hierarchy experiment (pretrained→ERM→PMH) is particularly compelling for practitioners

Limitations

  • All theoretical results are for Gaussian linear model; extension to general distributions requires C(P) estimation which is practically non-trivial
  • PMH training requires σ_train to be tuned to match deployment shift level (T-alignment principle); poor σ_train choice costs 17× more than over-suppression
  • Task 03 (molecular regression) is a confirmed exception: when signal and nuisance are geometrically aligned (3D positions), PMH targets signal directions
  • Re-ID task (Task 06) shows VAT outperforming PMH on average shift — adversarial smoothing is more suitable when nuisance (occlusion) is spatially correlated
  • The RLHF implication is speculative and not directly tested

Relevance

与用户关注的LLM训练稳定性和可解释性方向高度相关：定理1从数学上证明ERM训练（包括RLHF）必然编码标签相关的干扰方向（格式信号、冗长偏好等），这直接解释了LLM在语义不变的改写下产生不一致输出的根本原因；PMH提供了一个原理性修复方案，对理解LLM训练过程中的表示几何具有重要意义。

Questions for Further Exploration
