SpatialWorld: Benchmarking Interactive Spatial Reasoning of Multimodal Agents in Real-World Tasks

arXiv: 2606.09669

Date: 2026-06-08

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09669

一句话总结： SpatialWorld 提出一个跨 8 个异构模拟器、含 760 个人工标注任务的统一交互式空间推理基准，强制智能体仅凭第一人称 RGB 观测进行闭环决策；评测 15 个前沿 MLLM 后发现最强模型 GPT-5 的平均任务成功率仅 17.4%，揭示了当前多模态智能体在主动探索与长程规划上的根本性短板。
TL;DR: SpatialWorld is a unified, simulator-agnostic benchmark of 760 human-annotated interactive spatial-reasoning tasks spanning 8 heterogeneous 3D backends (AI2-THOR, ProcTHOR, VirtualHome, CARLA, EmbodiedCity, plus multi-agent and digital-game variants). Under vision-only POMDP conditions, the strongest model GPT-5 reaches only 17.4% average Task Success Rate versus the best open-source model Qwen-3.5-397B-A17B at 14.1%; a clear TSR-vs-efficiency mismatch and large domain-specific ranking swings expose deep bottlenecks in active exploration and long-horizon planning.

核心贡献

  • 构建了首个统一跨平台的交互式空间推理基准 SpatialWorld，将 8 个异构模拟后端（AI2-THOR、ProcTHOR、VirtualHome、CARLA、EmbodiedCity、Multi-AI2THOR、Multi-ProcTHOR、3D 游戏如 Block3D/Snake3D/Rubik）封装在共享的观测-动作接口下，消除模拟器耦合干扰。
  • 强制纯视觉、第一人称的 POMDP 范式：智能体每步仅接收一张自我中心 RGB 截图与自然语言目标，禁止任何特权状态（深度图、语义分割、全局地图、关节角等），通过文本动作空间（Navigation / Viewpoint / Interaction / Task-Control）输出高层决策。
  • 提供执行式验证而非轨迹匹配：每个任务配备人工验证初始状态、参考轨迹与终端状态校验器，并以 TSR（任务成功率）与 SE（步效率）双指标度量，能区分"成功"与"低效试错"。
  • 对 15 个前沿 MLLM（含 GPT-5、GPT-5.4、Gemini-3.1-Pro、Qwen-3.5-397B-A17B 等）进行系统评测，揭示成功率低、效率与成功率脱钩、领域排名剧烈波动三大核心发现。

Key Contributions

  • A unified cross-platform benchmark wrapping 8 heterogeneous simulators (AI2-THOR, ProcTHOR, VirtualHome, CARLA, EmbodiedCity, Multi-AI2THOR, Multi-ProcTHOR, and lightweight 3D games like Block3D/Snake3D/Rubik) behind one observation/action interface, removing per-simulator coupling bias that confounds prior embodied benchmarks.
  • A strict vision-only POMDP protocol: agents receive only an egocentric RGB screenshot and a natural-language goal per step, with no privileged state (depth, segmentation, global map, joint angles), and emit high-level text actions across four categories (Navigation, Viewpoint & Posture, Interaction, Task-Control & Coordination).
  • Execution-based verification (terminal-state checkers, not trajectory matching), with each task paired with a human-validated initial state, a reference trajectory, and a task-specific verifier, plus dual TSR/SE metrics that separate genuine competence from trial-and-error.
  • A systematic evaluation of 15 state-of-the-art MLLMs exposing that the best model (GPT-5) achieves only 17.4% average TSR, that TSR and Step Efficiency are decoupled, and that model rankings swing substantially across domains.

Methodology

SpatialWorld formalizes each task as a partially observable Markov decision process ⟨S, O, A, T, Ω, R⟩. At step t the agent receives a natural-language goal g and a raw egocentric RGB observation o_t (strictly no privileged signals such as depth, semantic maps, or global coordinates). Given history H_t, the MLLM policy π_θ predicts the next high-level action a_t ∈ A. The simulator executes a_t, transitions the hidden state s_{t+1} ~ T(·|s_t, a_t), and renders o_{t+1} ~ Ω(·|s_{t+1}). Interaction ends when the agent emits EndTask or exhausts the step budget.

The architecture has five modular components: (i) Environment initialization (either direct state loading or sequential execution of an init_actions list), (ii) a unified Observation Interface delivering one egocentric RGB frame at native resolution, (iii) a unified Action Space A with four categories — Navigation (Move), Viewpoint & Posture (Rotate/Tilt/ChangePosture), Interaction (Pick/Place/ChangeState/Manipulate), Task-Control & Coordination (EndTask/Communicate), (iv) an Action-to-Code mapping that translates unified primitives into simulator-specific calls (e.g., VirtualHome [walkforward], CARLA vehicle.apply_control, ProcTHOR controller.step(action="MoveAhead")), and (v) Verification via task-specific terminal-state checkers.

The benchmark contains 760 human-annotated tasks across 6 scenario categories: Daily (350 tasks), Work (59), Entertainment (173), Travel (132), Social Collaboration (46). Three complexity levels — Navigation, Interaction, and Hybrid (Navigation–Interaction) — are tagged per task. Data construction follows a three-stage pipeline: (i) task design (annotators define instructions and initial state), (ii) human execution (annotators solve tasks, recording ground-truth terminal state and reference action sequence), (iii) verification (expert reviewers cross-check feasibility, instruction clarity, and evaluator correctness, with inter-annotator cross-checking).

Evaluation settings: temperature τ = 1.0, history window w = 30 turns, step budget per task = 2g + 10 (where g is the golden action count). Primary metric TSR = (1/N) Σ 1[V^(i)(s_T^(i))=1]; efficiency metric SE = (1/|S|) Σ_{i∈S} (n_i*/n_i), the ratio of golden-step count to actual agent steps over successful tasks S. Fifteen models are evaluated via official APIs / open weights with no task-specific fine-tuning: Qwen2.5-VL-72B, Qwen3-VL-30B-A3B, Qwen3-VL-235B-Instruct, Qwen3-VL-235B-Thinking, Qwen-3.5-397B-A17B, GLM-4.5V, GLM-4.6V, Kimi-VL-A3B, Kimi-K2.5, Gemini-2.5-Pro, Gemini-3-Flash, Gemini-3.1-Pro, GPT-5, GPT-5.4, and Doubao-2.0-Lite.

Results & Highlights

  • Overall TSR ceiling is low: the strongest model GPT-5 reaches an average Task Success Rate of only 17.4% across all 760 tasks, while the leading open-source model Qwen-3.5-397B-A17B reaches 14.1% — far from human-level reliability.
  • Physical-domain gap: on the physical (embodied) subset, GPT-5 attains only 14.4% Physical Overall TSR and Qwen-3.5-397B-A17B 12.2%; successes cluster in short-horizon operations (e.g., turning on a device).
  • TSR–efficiency mismatch: Kimi-K2.5 and GPT-5.4 have comparable Physical Overall TSRs (9.2% vs. 6.6%), yet GPT-5.4 achieves a higher Step Efficiency (0.569 vs. 0.486), indicating Kimi-K2.5 relies on far more redundant trial-and-error actions to reach the same goals.
  • Complexity-mode difficulty: Navigation–Interaction hybrid tasks are far harder (4.2% mean TSR) than Interaction-only tasks (50.2% mean TSR); GPT-5 dominates Interaction (69.4% TSR) and the combined mode (12.1%), while the Gemini series leads Navigation (8.6%).
  • Indoor vs. outdoor split: GPT-5 (14.1%) and Qwen-3.5-397B-A17B (13.7%) lead indoors; Gemini-3-Flash (9.0%) and GPT-5 (8.3%) lead outdoors — GPT-5's indoor edge suggests fine-grained object grounding, while Gemini's outdoor edge reflects long-horizon macro navigation.
  • Multi-agent difficulty: GPT-5 reaches 34.8% pooled Social TSR, but Multi-ProcTHOR remains very hard (best models only 5.9%), versus Multi-AI2THOR where object-centric routines are solved more often.

Critical Assessment

  • Headline numbers rest on small per-cell task counts. Several breakdowns use very few tasks (e.g., Social Collaboration has only 46 tasks total, Multi-ProcTHOR only 17), so per-environment TSR values like the 5.9% Multi-ProcTHOR ceiling and the 39.0% Digital lead for Gemini-3.1-Pro are computed over small samples and carry high variance — the paper reports point estimates without confidence intervals.
  • Single-run, temperature-1.0 evaluation. All main TSR/SE numbers come from one trajectory per model per task at τ=1.0; although the temperature ablation (Fig. 7a) shows marginal sensitivity, the absence of multi-seed variance estimates means leaderboard gaps of 1–2 percentage points (e.g., GPT-5 14.4% vs Qwen-3.5 12.2% Physical Overall) are not shown to be statistically significant.
  • Step Efficiency metric is only comparable at similar TSR. The authors themselves note SE is "informative for differentiating models with comparable TSR; large TSR gaps render it incomparable," yet SE is still reported side-by-side in the main table, which can mislead readers into cross-model efficiency comparisons that the metric does not support.
  • Digital-game near-zero scores confound reasoning vs. interface difficulty. Rubik and Block3D near-zero results may reflect the difficulty of expressing multi-step geometric transformations through the text action interface rather than a pure spatial-reasoning deficit, but the paper does not run an ablation isolating interface friction from cognitive bottleneck.

Strengths

  • Genuinely simulator-agnostic design: a single text action interface and one egocentric-RGB observation contract span 8 very different backends, enabling apples-to-apples comparison that prior embodied benchmarks (ALFRED, VLABench, EmbodiedBench) cannot provide.
  • Factored complexity via the digital-game suite strips away photorealistic priors, cleanly isolating abstract geometric/topological reasoning from scene semantics.
  • Execution-based terminal-state verification avoids the brittleness of trajectory-matching and accommodates open-ended exploration.
  • Comprehensive 15-model sweep including the newest proprietary and open families, with rich ablations on temperature, history window, resolution, field of view, and continuous vs. discrete action parameterization.

Limitations

  • 760 tasks is modest in absolute size compared to large-scale VQA benchmarks (3DSRBench 2,772; SpatialScore 5,025), limiting statistical power in fine-grained sub-category analysis.
  • All environments are simulated 3D scenes; real-world physical transfer is not assessed, so the "real-world" framing in the title is aspirational.
  • Vision-only constraint, while principled, may systematically disadvantage models trained with depth/proprioception, conflating protocol purity with capability gaps.
  • No fine-tuning or agent-specialization baselines are included — all models are off-the-shelf, so the benchmark measures zero-shot competence but not the ceiling after adaptation.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • The paper ablates temperature (Fig. 7a) but only on a subset — would multi-seed (e.g., 5-run) TSR with confidence intervals change the GPT-5 vs Qwen-3.5-397B-A17B ranking, given their 2.2 pp Physical-Overall gap?
  • The digital-game near-zero scores (Rubik, Block3D) could reflect text-action-interface friction rather than pure reasoning failure: how would an ablation that gives models a structured coordinate-state observation (still vision-free) change these numbers?
  • Multi-ProcTHOR's 5.9% ceiling is reported on only 17 tasks — is the procedural-layout difficulty a generalization failure or an artifact of sample size and verifier strictness?
  • The paper cites Embodied4c [56] arguing that embodied benchmarks should "measure what matters" — could SpatialWorld's terminal-state verifiers be combined with Embodied4c-style progress metrics to diagnose which sub-skill (localization, planning, or motor execution) fails first?
