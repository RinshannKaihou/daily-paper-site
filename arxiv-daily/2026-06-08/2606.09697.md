> **一句话总结：** PsychoSafe 将 LLM 拒绝行为重构为基于循证心理干预（心理急救、动机访谈等）的结构化支持性沟通，构建 8,019 条 prompt-response 语料覆盖五大风险域；在 Qwen-3.5-27B 上，PsychoSafe 系统提示将整体拒绝质量提升 28.1%（外部资源转介 +46.8%、心理 grounding +34.8%），LoRA 微调实现近乎完美的拒绝率但牺牲了相关性（-25%），且在域外对抗样本上泛化有限。

> **TL;DR:** PsychoSafe reframes LLM refusal as structured supportive communication grounded in evidence-based psychological intervention. An 8,019 prompt-response corpus spans five risk domains (suicide/self-harm, sexual crimes, substance use, weapons, violence). On Qwen-3.5-27B, PsychoSafe prompting beats a generic "helpful assistant" baseline by +28.1% overall refusal quality on a 500-prompt validation set (LLM-judge = Qwen-3.5-397B-A17B, κ=0.61 vs human), with +46.8% on external resource referral and +34.8% on psychological grounding; LoRA fine-tuning reaches near-100% refusal/referral but drops relevance by up to -25.3%. On SORRY-Bench (decontaminated 403 prompts), the fine-tuned model cuts compliance to 0.0% (vs base 17.1%), but on XSTest adversarial contrast prompts its safety refusal falls to 17.0% (vs base 59.0%), exposing limited out-of-domain generalization.

## 核心贡献
- 提出 **PsychoSafe** 框架：将 LLM 拒绝从二元分类重构为基于循证心理干预（心理急救、动机访谈、安全计划、QPR 等）的结构化四段式支持性沟通（确认+温和拒绝 → 个性化自助步骤 → 专业资源转介 → 希望性结语）。
- 构建 **8,019 条 prompt-response 语料**，覆盖五大心理敏感风险域（自杀自伤 2,578、性犯罪 326、物质滥用 1,998、武器 1,740、暴力 1,377），通过 sentence-t5-large + UMAP + HDBSCAN 对 16 个安全基准的 54,109 条 prompt 进行风险标签语义聚类得到统一分类。
- 提供两种互补的**诱导策略**：推理时的 PsychoSafe 系统提示（in-context learning）与在推理轨迹（GPT-OSS-120B 蒸馏）上的 LoRA 监督微调，二者在 Qwen-3.5-27B 同一基座上直接对比。
- 建立经**人类评分校准的 LLM-as-Judge 评测**（4 项准则：拒绝、心理 grounding、外部资源、相关性），并通过 SORRY-Bench、XSTest、HellaSwag、MMLU 验证安全性与通用能力保留。

## Key Contributions
- The PsychoSafe framework, reframing LLM refusal as structured supportive communication grounded in validated psychological intervention strategies (Psychological First Aid, Mental Health First Aid, QPR, Safety Planning, Motivational Interviewing), structured into a four-part response (acknowledgment + gentle refusal → personalized self-help step → professional resource referral → hopeful closing).
- An 8,019 prompt-response corpus spanning five psychologically salient risk domains, built by harmonizing 16 public safety benchmarks (54,109 prompts) via sentence-t5-large + UMAP + HDBSCAN semantic clustering of heterogeneous risk taxonomies.
- Two complementary elicitation strategies on the same base model (Qwen-3.5-27B): a psychologically-informed system prompt (in-context learning) and LoRA supervised fine-tuning on reasoning traces distilled from GPT-OSS-120B.
- A human-calibrated LLM-as-Judge evaluation (4 criteria: Refusal, Psychological Grounding, External References, Relevance), with the selected judge (Qwen-3.5-397B-A17B) achieving Cohen's κ=0.61 against a human expert, complemented by SORRY-Bench, XSTest, HellaSwag, and MMLU.

## Methodology
**Risk taxonomy harmonization (§2.1).** The authors build on the Röttger et al. (2025) dataset collection, selecting 16 publicly available, academically licensed benchmarks with safety-relevant prompts + responses + risk labels, yielding 54,109 potentially harmful prompts. Risk labels are enriched with three taxonomies (Vidgen et al. 2024; Ghosh et al. 2025; Inan et al. 2023) and harmonized via semantic clustering: sentence-t5-large embeddings → UMAP (n_neighbors=30, min_dist=0.0, n_components=5) → HDBSCAN (min_cluster_size=7, min_samples=1, cluster_selection_epsilon=20), with a grid search over 672 parameter combinations to find a stable region targeting ~15–20 coherent clusters. Cluster names are derived via TF-IDF over uni/bigrams. Five risk clusters are selected against five criteria (specificity, brief-intervention availability, clinical correspondence, acute/escalating risk, disproportionate effect on vulnerable populations): Suicide & Self-Harm, Sexual Crimes, Substance Use, Weapons, Violence.

**Psychological intervention design (§2.2).** Refusal design draws on brief intervention frameworks for high-risk, time-constrained interactions. Suicide/self-harm uses Psychological First Aid (Ruzek et al. 2007), Mental Health First Aid (Kitchener & Jorm 2008), QPR (Aldrich et al. 2018), Safety Planning (Stanley & Brown 2012). Substance use uses Motivational Interviewing (Miller 1983), 5A's/5R's (WHO 2014), SOBER (Brody 1982). Violence/weapons/sexual crimes emphasize Green Dot Bystander Intervention and Motivational Interviewing to interrupt harmful action and redirect. All refusals follow a shared four-part template. Fourteen hand-crafted exemplar responses are written (generic at instance level, specific at the psychological-mechanism level), reviewed by a psychology expert for appropriateness, clarity, and non-escalatory tone.

**Dataset (§2.3).** 8,019 prompt-response pairs derived by mapping collectable prompts to the five risk clusters; each entry stores prompt, psychologically-informed refusal, risk cluster, and provenance.

**Elicitation (§3).** (1) In-context learning: a single system prompt (Appendix B) encoding the full framework — four-part structure, cluster-specific strategies, helplines (US/UK), personalization guidelines (mirror language register, reference situation, vary phrasing), multi-cluster prioritization (suicide first), and normal-assistant behavior outside targeted domains. (2) Supervised fine-tuning: reasoning traces generated by GPT-OSS-120B given input + desired output; LoRA on all attention and feed-forward projection layers of Qwen-3.5-27B (rank r=1, α=32, no dropout), cross-entropy loss on reasoning traces + human-crafted responses (not user prompts), 5 epochs, max seq len 4096, batch size 4, AdamW 8-bit, peak LR 1×10⁻⁴ cosine decay with 100 warmup steps.

**Evaluation (§4).** Four combinations (base/SFT × v0/v1 prompts) on a 500-example stratified validation set (100 per cluster). LLM-as-Judge over 4 criteria; judge selected among Mistral Large 3 675B Instruct, Qwen-3.5-397B-A17B, GLM-4.7 by Cohen's κ vs a human expert on 50 answers (25 examples × 2 prompts). Additional benchmarks: SORRY-Bench (440 unsafe instructions, 19 linguistic mutations, decontaminated to 403), XSTest (250 safe + 200 unsafe contrast), HellaSwag, MMLU (via LM Evaluation Harness).

## Results & Highlights
- **Overall refusal quality (500-prompt validation set, judge Qwen-3.5-397B-A17B):** PsychoSafe prompting (v1 on base Qwen-3.5-27B) reaches 92.0% normalized average vs 71.9% for the generic v0 baseline — a +28.1% relative improvement; the largest gains are External References +46.8% (64.8%→95.2% yes-rate) and Psychological Grounding +34.8% (C2 mean 3.38→4.56 on 1–5 scale).
- **Fine-tuning trade-off:** LoRA SFT reaches near-perfect Refusal (100.0% v0 / 99.8% v1) and External References (99.8% / 99.1%), but Relevance drops up to −25.3% (C4 mean 3.90→3.37), yielding an overall of 82.7% (v0) / 82.0% (v1) — below the prompt-only v1 (92.0%).
- **Judge-human agreement:** Qwen-3.5-397B-A17B selected as judge with Cohen's κ=0.61 vs human expert, beating GLM-4.7 (κ=0.58) and Mistral Large 3 675B Instruct (κ=0.56) on 50 evaluated answers.
- **SORRY-Bench (decontaminated 403 base prompts, compliance %, lower is safer):** base Qwen-3.5-27B 17.1% → PsychoSafe fine-tuned 0.0%; across 19 linguistic mutations the fine-tuned model holds 0.0% vs base 25.4% (mut-avg).
- **XSTest (over-refusal ↓ / safety ↑):** the fine-tuned model over-refuses less than base on benign prompts (3.6% vs 13.2% default) — ruling out indiscriminate refusal — but its safety refusal on adversarial contrast prompts collapses to 17.0% vs base 59.0%, while the v1 prompt on base raises safety to 78.5% but also raises over-refusal to 24.0%.
- **General capabilities (HellaSwag/MMLU):** HellaSwag roughly unchanged (slight +3 pp for SFT); MMLU drops ~4 pp for SFT and ~5 pp HellaSwag for the v1 prompt — a modest capability trade-off the authors deem acceptable in safety-critical settings.

## Critical Assessment
- **The headline +28.1% is an LLM-judge measurement, not a human outcome study.** The judge (Qwen-3.5-397B-A17B) was calibrated against a single human expert on only 50 answers (κ=0.61, "substantial" but not "almost-perfect" agreement), and the assistant being evaluated (Qwen-3.5-27B) is from the same model family as the judge — a known self-preference bias source (Wataoka et al. 2024, which the paper itself cites). The 500-prompt validation scores are therefore judge-dependent and not a measure of real-user benefit.
- **Fine-tuning trades relevance for refusal: a documented schema-overgeneralization failure.** The SFT model's Relevance drops −25.3% because it applies a generic crisis-intervention template too broadly — the authors' own error analysis (§4.1) finds it "frames factual, ambiguous, or benign prompts as acute crisis scenarios" and "misreads victim/witness situations as perpetrator-intent scenarios." This means the near-100% refusal rate is partly a false-positive artifact, not targeted safety.
- **Out-of-domain generalization is weak and the XSTest safety collapse is serious.** The fine-tuned model's safety refusal on XSTest adversarial contrast prompts falls to 17.0% (vs base 59.0%) — i.e., it complies with 83% of unsafe prompts outside its five training domains. This directly undercuts the SORRY-Bench near-zero compliance, which the authors acknowledge but which limits deployment to the five in-domain clusters.
- **Single-turn, English-only, US/UK-centric resources.** All validation is single-turn; multi-turn behavior, cross-lingual generalization, and real-user effects are explicitly open. The built-in helplines are tailored to the US and UK, so the resource-referral gains do not transfer to other locales.

## Strengths
- Principled grounding in validated, citable psychological intervention frameworks (PFA, MHFA, QPR, Safety Planning, Motivational Interviewing) rather than ad-hoc refusal templates — a genuine bridge between alignment research and clinical psychology.
- Rigorous judge selection: three large candidate judges benchmarked against a human expert via Cohen's κ, with the same-family bias explicitly addressed.
- Honest, multi-axis evaluation: not just refusal rate but psychological grounding, external referral, and relevance, plus the standard SORRY-Bench/XSTest/HellaSwag/MMLU battery and a qualitative error analysis explaining the relevance drop.
- The four-part structured template is reusable and the 8,019-pair corpus + LoRA recipe are released, lowering the barrier to domain-specific safety-tuning.

## Limitations
- The intervention strategies were designed for human–human settings and are abstracted into short written responses; the authors explicitly state PsychoSafe is "not therapy, diagnosis, or crisis management."
- Domain coverage is intentionally narrow (five clusters); many harmful domains are not amenable to short psychological intervention and are out of scope.
- English-only with US/UK-centric resources; broader language and resource coverage is future work.
- Single-turn validation only; multi-turn behavior, adversarial robustness, cross-lingual generalization, and real-user effects remain open.
- Dual-use risk: empathetic refusal behavior could be misused to create unwarranted perceptions of therapeutic authority in non-clinically-validated systems.

## Relevance
本文与"**LLM Inference Evaluation**"相关：PsychoSafe 提出了一套多维度的拒绝质量评测方法（4 项准则 + 经人类校准的 LLM-as-Judge + SORRY-Bench/XSTest 对比），是 LLM 安全行为评估的方法学案例。其核心关注点（拒绝行为的可靠性与一致性、域外泛化失败）也触及"**ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations**"中关于模型行为可靠性的议题。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- The judge and the assistant are both Qwen-3.5 family models; the paper cites Wataoka et al. (2024) on self-preference bias but calibrated against only one human expert on 50 answers (κ=0.61) — would a multi-annotator, cross-family-judge study change the +28.1% headline?
- The SFT model's XSTest safety collapse to 17.0% (vs base 59.0%) shows it complies with 83% of out-of-domain unsafe contrast prompts; would diversifying the fine-tuning data beyond the five clusters recover adversarial safety without sacrificing in-domain refusal quality?
- The relevance drop (−25.3%) stems from schema overgeneralization (crisis template applied to benign/ambiguous prompts); would a training strategy that includes explicit negative examples (non-crisis prompts that should NOT trigger the template) improve selectivity?
- The intervention frameworks (PFA, MHFA, QPR) are validated for human–human settings — the paper flags this as a limitation, but does it cite any evidence that short written AI refusals can approximate the de-escalation efficacy of human-delivered brief interventions?
