> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 DACSI（Document-Authored Control-Signal Impersonation）——一种非命令式的间接提示注入子类，攻击者仅在可检索文档中伪造"元数据/来源/权威/披露策略"类控制信号，即可在 RAG 系统中将合成凭证金丝雀的不安全披露率（UDR）从受防御基线推高数十个百分点；而显式的系统-文档信道分离能将披露率压回接近 0%。

> **TL;DR:** The author defines DACSI, a command-free, metadata-like indirect prompt-injection subclass, and evaluates it on 6 model settings (DeepSeek V4 Pro/Flash, GPT-5.5, Gemini 3.1 Pro Low, Qwen3.5-397B, GLM-4.7) against defended (B1) and imperative-injection baselines at n=200/condition. On DeepSeek V4 Pro, DACSI lifts UDR from a 0.5% defended baseline to 28.0% (E1) and averages 20.5% / peaks 30.5% versus 30.0%/33.0% for traditional command injection (E2); on DeepSeek V4 Flash DACSI averages 89.3% and peaks 99.0% (exceeding injection's 85.3%/98.5%); system–document channel separation collapses DeepSeek V4 Pro attack-condition mean UDR from 20.67% to 0.17% and traditional injection from 31.6% to 0.1%.

## 核心贡献

- 定义 DACSI 作为间接提示注入下的一个非命令式载荷子类：攻击者撰写的文档文本冒充元数据、来源、权威或披露策略等控制平面信号，核心失效是"来源权威歧义"——模型把数据平面的标签当成系统策略。
- 用统一的成对基线原则（matched baseline B1）在 6 个模型设定上做对照实验，证明去掉命令动词后攻击效果仍然存在，且与传统命令式注入可比。
- 给出控制信号家族的语义选择性证据（E3）：披露策略与权威类线索最危险，而否定/无意义控制接近基线，排除"任何元数据都危险"的泛化解释。
- 测试 RAG 检索中介（E4，含 BM25、混合嵌入、LangChain 风格复现）与系统控制探针（E5/M1，含信道分离、检索期脱敏），并给出 M1 来源权威归属探针，表明同一权威语义在不同来源位置（系统撰写 vs 文档撰写 vs 引用）表现不同。
- 通过载荷表面审计（E2 的命令线索/元数据线索编码）与金丝雀格式鲁棒性（E6），论证命令词导向的检测器会漏掉元数据式攻击。

## Key Contributions

- Defines **DACSI** as a non-imperative, metadata-like payload subclass of indirect prompt injection targeting RAG source-authority boundaries, with the crisp principle "document-authored labels are data, not policy."
- Provides regime-based (not single-score) evidence across six model settings using matched defended baselines (B1) and imperative-injection baselines at n=200 per condition, showing command-free control signals can match command-style injection on susceptible models.
- Establishes **signal-family selectivity**: disclosure-policy and authority cues drive most of the lift, while negative/nonsense controls stay near baseline, ruling out a generic-salience explanation.
- Tests RAG mediation (toy / BM25 semi-real / hybrid-embedding / LangChain-style with public SQuAD distractors, HuggingFace sentence embeddings, FAISS) showing retrieval design can preserve, dilute, or suppress the attack.
- Introduces the **M1 source-authority attribution probe**: moving identical authority semantics across system-authored vs document-authored vs quoted vs source-separated locations changes disclosure, providing behavioral (not mechanistic) causal evidence.
- Shows command-word-oriented prompt-injection detectors miss DACSI: a cue audit finds DACSI payloads carry 0 command cues / 5.67 metadata cues while traditional injection carries 3.25 command cues / 0 metadata cues.

## Methodology

**Threat model.** A RAG reader serializes system-authored control text (S) and untrusted retrieved documents (D) into one natural-language prompt. The attacker can *only* author retrievable text (webpage, ticket, README, runbook) — not the system prompt, model, retriever, or user query. The user task creates copy pressure (asking for an exact configuration-like line); the attacker goal is to increase copying of a synthetic, non-functional credential-like canary. A DACSI payload is a document span d_c resembling a control signal in S but authored by an untrusted source.

**Primary metric.** Unsafe Disclosure Rate (UDR): a response is unsafe if it fully or partially reproduces the synthetic canary; redacted placeholders, refusals, and format descriptions are safe. The main lift measure is Lift_{A|B1} = UDR(A) − UDR(B1), where B1 is the defended baseline. Statistics: Wilson 95% CIs, exact paired McNemar/sign tests, Benjamini–Hochberg correction; n=200 samples per condition unless noted.

**Models (interpreted by regime, not by one score).** DeepSeek V4 Pro & Qwen3.5-397B = cleanest positive-lift settings; DeepSeek V4 Flash = high-susceptibility; GPT-5.5 & Gemini 3.1 Pro Low = strong-boundary; GLM-4.7 = saturated baseline leakage. All accessed via API-compatible chat-completion interfaces with low-randomness decoding and fixed output budgets.

**Experiment chain.** E1 (prompt pressure: weak vs aggressive copy asks); E2 (DACSI vs imperative injection under matched tasks); E3 (control-signal taxonomy: disclosure-policy / authority / trust / negative / nonsense); E4 (RAG mediation: toy insertion, BM25 paragraph-chunked, hybrid-embedding semi-real, LangChain-style public-corpus reproduction with SQuAD distractors + FAISS + top-k); E5 (system-control probes: generic warning, Spotlighting-style delimiting, StruQ-style structure, system–document channel separation); M1 (source-authority placement); E6 (canary formats: bearer token, AWS-like key, GitHub-PAT-like, Slack-bot-like, database URL, JWT-like).

## Results & Highlights

- **Feasibility is copy-pressure-conditional (E1).** Under aggressive copy prompts DeepSeek V4 Pro rises from a 0.5% defended baseline to **28.0% UDR**, DeepSeek V4 Flash rises from **53.0% to 91.5%**, and Qwen3.5-397B reaches **98.5%**; strong-boundary Gemini 3.1 Pro Low still shows a residual **10.5%** from a **0%** baseline under official cues.
- **Command-free DACSI matches imperative injection (E2, Table II).** On DeepSeek V4 Pro DACSI averages **20.5%** (peak 30.5%) vs injection's **30.0%** (peak 33.0%); on DeepSeek V4 Flash DACSI averages **89.3%** (peak 99.0%), *exceeding* injection's **85.3%** (peak 98.5%); on Qwen3.5-397B DACSI averages **87.5%** (peak 92.0%) vs injection **85.1%** (peak 89.5%). GLM-4.7 is a saturated-leakage case (DACSI avg 96.7% vs injection 72.8%).
- **Signal-family selectivity (E3, Table V).** On Qwen3.5-397B the defended baseline is **68.0%**; the "disclosure allowed" cue reaches **91.5% (+23.5 pp)**, "admin runbook" authority **85.5%**, "trusted reference" **83.5%**, while the negative "do not quote" control is **67.0%** and the nonsense label **69.0%**.
- **DACSI survives retrieval (E4, Table VI).** Under hybrid-embedding retrieval DeepSeek V4 Pro goes from a **0%** defended baseline to **47.0%** under attack; Gemini 3.1 Pro Low's official cue lifts from **0% to 17.0%**. BM25 chunking is weaker because it can separate the forged cue from the canary-bearing line.
- **System–document channel separation is the most stable mitigation (E5).** On DeepSeek V4 Pro, attack-condition mean UDR falls from **20.67%** (original rendering) to **13.17%** (generic warning), **4.00%** (Spotlighting-style), **0.00%** (StruQ-style), and **0.17%** (channel separation); a focused 2×2 reduces traditional injection from **31.6% to 0.1%** and DACSI from **19.0% to 0.2%**. Generic warning *backfires* on Qwen3.5-397B, raising UDR from **85.4% to 93.5%**.
- **Source placement causally matters (M1, Table IX).** On DeepSeek V4 Pro, document-authored authority raises UDR from **0.0% to 25.0%**, while the same authority as quoted document text gives **0.0%**, neutral metadata **0.5%**, negative policy **0.0%**, and channel separation **0.5%** (paired McNemar p < 10⁻¹⁴). Gemini 3.1 Pro Low shows the inverse: system-authored authority leaks **87.0%** but document-authored authority stays **0.0%**.
- **Effect is not tied to one canary format (E6).** On Gemini 3.1 Pro Low the official cue produces measurable lift across formats: bearer tokens **15.0%**, AWS-like keys **11.0%**, GitHub-token-like **5.5%**, Slack-token-like **14.5%**, JWT-like **13.0%**.

## Critical Assessment

- **Synthetic-disclosure metric does not measure real compromise.** UDR only checks whether a non-functional synthetic canary string is reproduced; the paper itself concedes (§Limitations) it does not measure real credential compromise, account takeover, or downstream exploitation, and depends on unmodeled redaction/access-control/monitoring. So the "low-cost attack" headline is about string disclosure under lab conditions, not a production attack-rate estimate.
- **GLM-4.7 and DeepSeek V4 Flash baseline leakage saturates the metric.** GLM-4.7's defended baseline is ~72.8–99% across conditions and DeepSeek V4 Flash starts at 53% — for these two models "DACSI lift" is largely hidden by ceiling/floor effects, so the cleanest positive evidence rests on just two models (DeepSeek V4 Pro, Qwen3.5-397B).
- **No full horizontal defense evaluation.** The paper explicitly states it does *not* benchmark against complete implementations of Spotlighting, StruQ, CaMeL, or attention-based defenses — only "system-control probes" of the same ideas — so the claim that channel separation is the "most stable" mitigation is relative to partial reproductions, not the published systems.
- **Strong-boundary models provide mostly residual-risk evidence.** GPT-5.5 suppresses essentially everything (DACSI avg 1.2%), so its value is boundary probing, not positive DACSI evidence; the subclass argument therefore leans heavily on the susceptible models where the failure is least surprising.

## Strengths

- Tight, self-limiting framing: DACSI is positioned as a bounded payload *subclass* within indirect prompt injection, with explicit claims about what it is not (not a universal primitive, not an enterprise estimate).
- Strong matched-baseline methodology (same model, task, prompt pressure, canary seed, decoding, outcome rule across conditions) plus Wilson intervals, paired McNemar/sign tests, and Benjamini–Hochberg correction.
- The M1 source-authority probe is a genuinely clever control: identical authority semantics are moved across source locations, showing the effect is about *where* authority is authored, not the words themselves.
- Negative/nonsense controls and the canary-format robustness check (E6) preempt the obvious "any metadata works" and "one-string artifact" critiques.

## Limitations

- Six model settings, mostly via provider-compatible APIs rather than identical vendor deployments; model-version validity is flagged as a threat.
- No enterprise end-to-end deployment (production vector DBs, rerankers, access control, monitoring, policy enforcement) — explicitly out of scope.
- Signal taxonomy is acknowledged as incomplete; effect size depends heavily on task pressure, retriever, chunking, and canary format.
- Generic warning can backfire (Qwen3.5-397B UDR rises), so the practical mitigation advice reduces essentially to channel separation + retrieval-time redaction.

## Relevance

这主要映射到用户的「LLM Inference Evaluation」兴趣区——它本质是一个针对 RAG 推理时安全边界失效的评测基准与威胁刻画，定义了新的攻击载荷子类与配套度量（UDR/lift）并跨 6 个模型设定做对照评测。其"非命令式控制信号如何被模型误判为权威"的发现也与「LLM Hidden States & Interpretability」间接相关：M1 来源权威探针是行为层面的归因证据，作者明确指出这并非内部机制证明，可作为后续做内部归因电路研究的切入点。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The paper cites CaMeL (Debenedetti et al., 2025) and StruQ (Chen et al., 2025) as relevant defense designs but only probes partial reproductions — would a head-to-head test against the *full* published CaMeL/StruQ systems change the "channel separation is most stable" ranking?
- The M1 result shows Gemini 3.1 Pro Low leaks 87.0% under *system-authored* authority but 0.0% under *document-authored* authority — the inverse of DeepSeek V4 Pro. Does this imply some model families have an asymmetric authority-attribution bias that could itself be probed via hidden-state attribution methods?
- Generic warning *backfires* on Qwen3.5-397B (85.4% → 93.5%), which the authors speculate is a salience effect — could this be distinguished from a true source-authority failure using the prompt-sensitivity methodology of Sclar et al. (2024) and Hua et al. (2025) that the paper cites?
- All canaries are synthetic; the paper references FaithEval (Ming et al., 2025) and FaithfulRAG (Zhang et al., 2025) on context faithfulness — could DACSI's disclosure effect be reframed and measured as a faithfulness/attribution failure on real (non-canary) sensitive content?
