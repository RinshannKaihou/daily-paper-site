NutriMLLM: Multimodal Large Language Models for Dietary Micronutrient Analysis

["Runze Yan", "Minxiao Wang", "Jiaying Lu", "Darren Liu", "Xiao Hu", "Hanqi Luo"]

arXiv: 2606.08948

Date: 2026-06-08

Tags: ["MLLM", "Nutrition", "Healthcare", "Synthetic Data"]

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08948

一句话总结： 本文先用四组件评测框架证明 GPT-5/Gemini 3/Claude Sonnet 4.5 等主流多模态大模型在 65 种膳食微量营养素估算上不可靠（在 ASA24 图像上 SMAPEadj 高达 118–138%），再用 NHANES 十年 24 小时膳食回顾驱动的合成图文（约 110 万三元组，Z-Image-Turbo + FLUX.1-dev 双生成器）LoRA 微调 Qwen3-VL(2B/4B/8B/30B) 与 GLM-4.6V-Flash 得到 NutriMLLM，使其在真实食物图像上 30B 版本的 SMAPEadj（ASA24 109.8%、SNAPMe 91.2%）反超三大闭源模型。
TL;DR: The authors show leading MLLMs are unreliable at comprehensive 65-nutrient estimation — on ASA24 images mean SMAPEadj is 118.0% (Claude Sonnet 4.5) to 137.9% (GPT-5), and Qwen3-VL-2B abstains on ~80% of micronutrient samples. They then build a ~1.1M synthetic image–description–nutrient corpus from a decade (2013–2023) of NHANES 24-hour recalls rendered with Z-Image-Turbo + FLUX.1-dev, LoRA-fine-tune Qwen3-VL (2B/4B/8B/30B) and GLM-4.6V-Flash into NutriMLLM, and the largest variant NutriMLLM(Qwen3-VL-30B) reaches SMAPEadj 109.8% on ASA24 and 91.2% on SNAPMe — beating GPT-5 (137.9% / 102.6%), Gemini 3 (125.5% / 95.7%) and Claude Sonnet 4.5 (118.0% / 94.7%).

核心贡献

  • 提出四组件评测框架（NRR 拒答率、HR 幻觉率、UPR=NRR+HR 不可用率、SMAPEadj 拒答惩罚后数值误差），首次系统量化主流 MLLM 在全 65 种营养素（远超传统只评 4 种宏量营养素）估算上的两类失败：拒答与幻觉。
  • 诊断出失败根因是"营养知识缺失"而非视觉感知弱——文本输入（FNDDS 单食物名）下 SMAPEadj 仍很高（GPT-5 75.2%、Gemini 3 49.2%），说明模型即便没有视觉负担也估算不准。
  • 构建约 110 万张"图像-描述-65 营养素标签"合成语料（NHANES 2013–2023 真实膳食回顾 + 模拟光照/角度/风格，用两个开源文生图模型 Z-Image-Turbo 与 FLUX.1-dev 渲染），是迄今最大的带完整微量营养素标注的合成食物图像语料，承诺发表时公开。
  • 训练出 NutriMLLM 家族（Qwen3-VL 2B/4B/8B/30B + GLM-4.6V-Flash），在真实食物图像上把 UPR 压到接近 0，30B 版本准确率反超三大闭源模型。

Key Contributions

  • A four-component evaluation framework (Non-Response Rate, Hallucination Rate, Unusable Prediction Rate, abstention-adjusted SMAPE) giving the first systematic validation of MLLMs for comprehensive (65-nutrient) estimation, versus the 4-macronutrient ceiling of prior work.
  • A diagnosis that the micronutrient failure is fundamentally a knowledge deficit, not a perception deficit: errors persist and are large even on text-only single-food-name inputs (FNDDS), so better image encoders alone cannot fix it.
  • A ~1.1M synthetic image–description–nutrient-triplet corpus built from a decade of NHANES 24-hour recalls (2013–2023) with FNDDS-derived complete 65-nutrient labels, rendered by two open-weight text-to-image models (Z-Image-Turbo + FLUX.1-dev) to avoid single-generator style bias — the largest such corpus, planned for public release.
  • NutriMLLM, the first family of vision-language models specialized for comprehensive dietary micronutrient estimation, obtained purely via recall-driven synthetic LoRA supervision, spanning 2B–30B parameters and matching/exceeding proprietary frontier MLLMs on real food images.

Methodology

Evaluation datasets (§3.1, four independent, none used in training). ASA24 Portion Size Image Database (US NCI, >17,000 images, controlled portion series, full 65-nutrient FNDDS ground truth — tests portion-size estimation). SNAPMe (USDA, 3,311 mobile-phone photos by 95 free-living participants, 65-nutrient labels via linked ASA24 records — tests in-the-wild generalization). NutriBench (11,857 natural-language meal descriptions from 11 countries, 4 macronutrient labels — tests text-meal competence). FNDDS (USDA single-food-name → 65-nutrient QA probe — pure nutrient-knowledge test, no perception/portion confound).

Models (§3.2). Proprietary baselines: GPT-5, Gemini 3, Claude Sonnet 4.5 (API only, not fine-tuned). Open-weight baselines/backbones: Qwen3-VL at 2B/4B/8B/30B and GLM-4.6V-Flash (9B). Traditional supervised baselines: BERT+regression head (text side) and ViT+regression head (image side), both fine-tuned on the same synthetic corpus to isolate the MLLM-backbone contribution. All MLLMs queried with identical Prompt 2 (Appendix E).

Synthetic corpus (§3.3). NHANES 24HRs 2013–2023: each recalled food carries structured fields (name+cooking method, portion, eating occasion, time, source) plus its complete FNDDS 65-nutrient profile. Fields augmented with simulated photographic variables (lighting, camera angle, style) into a text prompt rendered by two open-weight text-to-image models (Z-Image-Turbo and FLUX.1-dev) — no API cost, vs ~$0.134/image for Nano Banana Pro. Yields ~1.1M image–description–nutrient triplets. Only the image is synthetic; labels are real FNDDS ground truth.

LoRA fine-tuning (§3.4). Each backbone fine-tuned on both image and text inputs paired with Prompt 2. Supervision target = a step-by-step reasoning trace (identify food, state portion) followed by the complete 65-nutrient profile as JSON. Every label is complete (no na), so NutriMLLM is never trained to abstain — any na at inference is a spontaneous response to the prompt's abstention option, not learned behaviour.

Evaluation protocol (§3.5). Same prompt, output format, and sampled items across all models (proprietary via API, open-weight locally). Metrics: NRR (fraction abstained), HR (fraction of values outside the per-nutrient 99.5th-percentile plausible range), UPR = NRR + HR, and SMAPEadj (numerical accuracy penalised for missing predictions). Full definitions in Appendix A. Best checkpoint = epoch 3 (Figure C3 balances UPR and SMAPEadj).

Results & Highlights

  • All general MLLMs fail on micronutrients. On ASA24 images, mean SMAPEadj across 65 nutrients ranges 118.0% (Claude Sonnet 4.5) → 125.5% (Gemini 3) → 133.3% (Qwen3-VL-30B) → 137.9% (GPT-5) → 169.7% (GLM-4.6V-Flash) → 180.4% (Qwen3-VL-2B). Qwen3-VL-2B abstains on >55% of macronutrient and 75–100% of micronutrient samples.
  • Failure is knowledge, not vision. On text-only FNDDS, mean SMAPEadj is 49.2% (Gemini 3), 54.8% (Claude Sonnet 4.5), 75.2% (GPT-5) — far better than their 118–138% on ASA24 images, but still large; Qwen3-VL-30B is 102.8% on FNDDS vs 133.3% on ASA24. The deficit persists even without perception load.
  • NutriMLLM collapses coverage failures. Median UPR across 65 nutrients drops on ASA24 from ~78%→~15% (2B), ~80%→~5% (4B), ~17%→~0% (8B), ~60%→~3% (GLM-4.6V-Flash); NutriMLLM(Qwen3-VL-30B) reaches near-zero NRR/HR across the panel, surpassing all three proprietary MLLMs in absolute coverage.
  • NutriMLLM beats frontier proprietary models on accuracy. NutriMLLM(Qwen3-VL-30B) SMAPEadj on ASA24 = 109.8% (vs GPT-5 137.9%, Gemini 3 125.5%, Claude Sonnet 4.5 118.0%); on SNAPMe = 91.2% (vs 102.6%, 95.7%, 94.7%). It also beats the ViT+regression baseline (150.3% ASA24 / 145.5% SNAPMe) trained on identical supervision — isolating the MLLM architectural advantage.
  • Scaling is monotonic. On NutriBench, mean UPR across 4 macronutrients drops 3.2% (2B) → 1.5% (4B) → 0.6% (8B) → 0.3% (30B); mean SMAPEadj drops 102% → 68% → 55% → 49%.
  • Multi-generator ablation matters. Training on the union of Z-Image-Turbo + FLUX.1-dev beats either alone (mean SMAPEadj ~100% vs 109–110% on SNAPMe) — the larger gain on the independent SNAPMe photos shows it improves real-image generalization, not just in-sample fit.
  • Smallest variant gets the biggest practical fix. NutriMLLM(Qwen3-VL-2B) cuts UPR from 37.5–39.2% to 2.6–4.4% on NutriBench macronutrients and median UPR from ~78% to ~15% on ASA24 — restoring on-device viability.
  • Beats BERT baseline head-to-head. On NutriBench, NutriMLLM(Qwen3-VL-30B) leads BERT (same synthetic supervision) on all 4 nutrients for both UPR and SMAPEadj (energy 47.4 vs 56.6, protein 48.9 vs 77.5, carbohydrate 46.3 vs 65.0, total fat 54.8 vs 76.1).

Critical Assessment

  • Headline SMAPEadj values remain very large (>90% even for the best model). A SMAPEadj of 91–110% means typical per-nutrient errors are on the order of the ground-truth value itself, so despite "beating GPT-5" the absolute clinical reliability for individual micronutrient estimation is still weak — the paper frames this as "tractable engineering problem" but does not benchmark against a clinical-adequacy threshold (e.g. whether estimates correctly classify deficiency status).
  • Evaluation sets are small or narrow. SNAPMe has only 3,311 photos from 95 participants and ASA24 is a controlled portion series (fixed food, varied portion) — so the in-the-wild generalization claim rests on a small, US-centric sample, and population/ cuisine diversity outside NHANES/FNDDS coverage (e.g. non-US diets) is not tested.
  • Synthetic-image realism is only qualitatively assessed. Figure 7 visually compares six text-to-image generators but no quantitative measure of synthetic-vs-real image gap (FID, portion-accuracy of generated food) is reported, leaving open how much of the residual error comes from the sim-to-real gap rather than model capacity.
  • The abstention framing is somewhat circular. NutriMLLM is "never trained to abstain" (all labels complete), so its near-zero NRR is partly structural — a model trained only on complete labels will tend to always answer. The paper argues the SMAPEadj distribution does not inflate (so it is not guessing), but it does not compare against a calibration- or uncertainty-aware variant that is allowed to abstain, which would be the fairer test of "genuine knowledge."

Strengths

  • Genuinely novel task scope: first comprehensive 65-nutrient (vs 4-macronutrient) MLLM evaluation and first specialized models, closing a real clinical gap.
  • Rigorous, fair comparison protocol: identical prompt, identical evaluation items, same synthetic supervision for ViT/BERT baselines to isolate the backbone contribution, and ablations for generator choice and checkpoint selection.
  • Honest, sharp diagnosis (knowledge deficit not perception deficit) backed by the text-only FNDDS control.
  • Practical reproducibility: ~1–2 days on a single GPU node, all open-weight ingredients, planned public data/code/weights release.

Limitations

  • Absolute accuracy is still coarse (SMAPEadj ~90–110% for the best variant) — useful for surveillance/triage, not yet for individual clinical dosing decisions.
  • Synthetic training images and US-centric NHANES/FNDDS coverage may not transfer to global cuisines or non-recalled foods.
  • No uncertainty/calibration mechanism — NutriMLLM always answers, so over-confident wrong values ("hallucination") can still propagate into clinical advice, exactly the failure mode the paper flags as most insidious.
  • Proprietary baselines cannot be fine-tuned, so the comparison is "fine-tuned open-weight vs zero-shot proprietary," which favours NutriMLLM; a fine-tuned proprietary variant (if available) is not considered.

Relevance

这篇工作主要映射到用户的「LLM Inference Evaluation」兴趣区——它贡献了一套面向多模态 LLM 的、覆盖 65 种营养素的四组件评测框架（NRR/HR/UPR/SMAPEadj），并把"拒答"和"幻觉"作为一类评测维度。其次它也触及「LLM Hidden States & Interpretability」的外延：通过控制文本 vs 图像输入来定位失败根因（知识缺失 vs 感知缺失），是一种黑盒归因式的诊断方法。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Could adding an explicit abstention/calibration objective during fine-tuning (e.g. training on selectively-masked labels or adding an uncertainty head) let NutriMLLM abstain calibratedly on hard nutrients, rather than always answering — which the paper itself flags as the most insidious failure mode?
  • The multi-generator ablation shows Z-Image-Turbo + FLUX beats either alone; does adding even one proprietary generator (e.g. Nano Banana Pro) on a subset further close the sim-to-real gap, and is the gain worth the ~$0.134/image cost at 1.1M scale?
  • SMAPEadj is still ~90–110% for the best model — what is the per-nutrient breakdown of which micronutrients remain hardest after fine-tuning, and do they correlate with the long-chain fatty acids (SFA 12:0, PFA 18:4) where the paper notes residual spikes?
  • The diagnosis relies on text-only FNDDS as a perception-free control; would a within-model probe (e.g. linear probe on NutriMLLM hidden states for each nutrient value) give a sharper, mechanistic readout of which nutrients are "known" vs "guessed," complementing the SMAPEadj distribution argument?
