> **一句话总结：** GD-MIL 在 H&E 全切片图像的多实例学习编码器中加入梯度反转的 Gleason 分级对抗分支，使切片表征与分级解耦后再与临床变量晚期融合，在 TCGA-PRAD 上达到 C-index 0.704，显著优于临床 Cox 基线（+0.029, p=0.0005）和最强纯影像模型（+0.062, p=0.039）。

> **TL;DR:** On TCGA-PRAD (487 patients, 101 BCR events), GD-MIL — a gated-attention MIL encoder with a gradient-reversal grade adversary late-fused with clinical variables — reaches C-index 0.704, beating the clinical Cox baseline (0.687, Δc=+0.029, p=0.0005) and the best imaging-only model Virchow2+ABMIL (0.639, Δc=+0.062, p=0.039); a median risk split gives log-rank p<0.0001 with a 50-percentage-point 5-year BCR-free survival gap.

## 核心贡献

- 构建了严格的 TCGA-PRAD 评测协议：5 折交叉验证 × 5 个随机种子，严格 out-of-fold 评分，消除用评测折做 checkpoint 选择导致的泄漏
- 系统性基准：证明特征提取器质量是性能主导因素（ResNet50→UNI2-h 带来 ≈0.06 C-index 提升），而 MIL 聚合器选择影响可忽略（四种聚合器仅 0.017 跨度）
- 提出 GD-MIL：通过梯度反转分级对抗鼓励切片表征对 Gleason 分级不变，再与临床变量晚期融合，达到 C-index 0.704 并显著超越临床基线与所有纯影像模型

## Key Contributions

- A leakage-free evaluation protocol on TCGA-PRAD with strict out-of-fold scoring over 5-fold CV repeated across 5 seeds, where the outer test fold is never used for model selection
- A systematic benchmark showing feature extractor quality dominates (ResNet50 0.566 vs UNI2-h up to 0.639) while MIL aggregator choice is negligible (ABMIL/CLAM/TransMIL/PatchGCN span only 0.615–0.632)
- GD-MIL, a gated-attention MIL encoder trained with a gradient-reversal grade adversary (λ=0.5) that disentangles grade from the slide representation before late fusion with clinical variables

## Methodology

**Data & setup.** TCGA-PRAD cohort, 487 patients with 101 BCR events (20.7%), median follow-up 559 days. H&E WSIs are segmented by an HSV filter and tiled at ≈1.0 µm/px into 256×256 px tiles (up to 2000 per slide). Tile features are extracted by three frozen encoders without stain normalization or augmentation: UNI2-h (d=1536), Virchow2 (d=1280), ResNet50 (d=512). A linear projection maps each tile to h_i ∈ R^256.

**Gated-attention MIL backbone.** ABMIL-style gated attention with a tanh relevance gate and σ inclusion gate (V,U ∈ R^128×256, w ∈ R^128) produces normalized per-tile weights; the attention-weighted sum is layer-normalized into slide representation z ∈ R^256.

**Grade-disentanglement adversary.** A two-layer MLP grade predictor ψ attaches to z through a gradient reversal layer (reversal coefficient λ=0.5) that negates gradients during backprop so the encoder suppresses ISUP-grade-predictive information. Combined objective: L = L_cox(r_img) + λ‖ĝ−g‖², where g is standardized ISUP grade. The resulting grade-disentangled imaging risk r_img is concatenated with clinical variables (grade group, T-stage, age) and fed to a late-fusion ℓ2-penalized Cox model.

**Evaluation.** 5-fold stratified CV; within each fold a 15% inner validation split is reserved for early stopping and the outer test fold is never used for selection. Each patient gets a single out-of-fold risk prediction; C-index computed over all 487 OOF predictions, mean±std across 5 seeds. Significance via paired bootstrap (2000 patient-level resamples). Training: Adam (lr 3e-4, weight decay 1e-5), gradient clipping at norm 1.0, ℓ2 penalization 0.1 for Cox/fusion.

## Results & Highlights

- **C-index vs clinical baseline:** GD-MIL reaches **0.704 ± 0.003** versus the clinical Cox model on grade/T-stage/age at **0.687 ± 0.005** (Δc=+0.029, p=0.0005, 95% CI [+0.015, +0.046]) on TCGA-PRAD; GD-MIL is the only method whose full bootstrap CI [0.643, 0.752] lies entirely above the clinical baseline.
- **C-index vs best imaging-only:** Best imaging-only model Virchow2+ABMIL is **0.639 ± 0.007**; GD-MIL beats it by Δc=+0.062 (p=0.039). ResNet50+ABMIL is only **0.566**, near random, and no imaging-only model significantly beats the clinical Cox (p=0.11–0.31).
- **Grade-disentanglement ablation:** GD-MIL gains **+0.085** over plain ABMIL+UNI2-h (0.619→0.704, p=0.003), isolating the contribution of the adversary beyond merely using a foundation model.
- **Survival stratification:** A median risk split yields log-rank p<0.0001, with high-risk (n=244, 76 events, 31.1%) vs low-risk (n=243, 25 events, 10.3%) groups showing a ~50-percentage-point gap in 5-year BCR-free survival (~20% vs ~70%).

## Critical Assessment

The clinical-baseline comparison is weakened by omitting pre-operative PSA and surgical margin status — both well-established prognostic variables — which makes the imaging-vs-clinical head-to-head somewhat favorable to GD-MIL since its "clinical" comparator is deliberately stripped. Grade disentanglement is enforced as a training-time adversary (λ=0.5) but never formally verified at inference: the paper itself notes that measuring residual grade information via mutual information or probing classifiers would be needed, so it is unconfirmed how grade-invariant z actually is. Evaluation is single-cohort (TCGA-PRAD only) with no external/institutional validation, and the modest effect size (Δc=+0.029) rests on 101 events; the claim of prognostic signal beyond grade is therefore suggestive rather than confirmed. The attention-map analysis is illustrative on a single case (TCGA-2A-A8W3) without pathologist-annotated regions of interest.

## Strengths

- Leakage-free protocol with strict OOF scoring and seed-level variance reporting — directly addresses the checkpoint-selection leakage the authors flag in prior work
- Clean isolation of the adversary's contribution (+0.085 over plain ABMIL+UNI2-h, p=0.003)
- Practitioner-relevant benchmark finding: feature extractor quality ≫ aggregator choice
- Code and all baselines publicly released

## Limitations

- Single cohort, no external validation; transfer across scanners/institutions untested
- Clinical feature set excludes PSA and surgical margin status
- Grade disentanglement verified only qualitatively, not via MI/probing
- Tile cap of 2000 may discard informative tissue on large specimens

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would adding pre-operative PSA and surgical margin status to the clinical fusion erode GD-MIL's Δc=+0.029 advantage, since the paper omits these known-prognostic variables from the comparator?
- Can the gradient-reversal grade adversary be quantitatively verified at inference using probing classifiers (as the paper itself suggests) to confirm how grade-invariant z actually is?
- Does the "feature extractor dominates, aggregator negligible" finding (ResNet50 0.566 vs UNI2-h 0.619–0.632) hold on an external cohort, or is it specific to TCGA-PRAD's 101 events?
