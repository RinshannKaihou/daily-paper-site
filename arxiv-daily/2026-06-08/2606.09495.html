ContextShift: A Controlled Benchmark for Context Dependence in Object Detection

["Dan Zlotnikov", "Alex Lazarovich", "Ohad Ben-Shahar"]

arXiv: 2606.09495

Date: 2026-06-08

Tags: ["CV", "Object-Detection", "Robustness", "Benchmark", "Context"]

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09495

一句话总结： ContextShift 在 COCO 2017 上构建受控基准，通过几何变换与背景替换系统操控物体-上下文关系（含基于 NPMI 的连续兼容性轴），发现五种主流检测器在上下文变化下呈现一致的"预测抑制"失效模式（漏检最高增 227%、预测数下降 44%），且性能沿兼容性轴呈非单调 U 型。
TL;DR: ContextShift is a controlled robustness benchmark built on COCO 2017 val (4,952 base images → 131,885 manipulated samples) that isolates context via geometric transforms and synthetic/natural background substitution with a continuous NPMI compatibility axis; across 5 detectors (Faster R-CNN, YOLO26M, Deformable DETR, RF-DETR, D-FINE) it finds a consistent prediction-suppression failure mode (FN up to +227%, predictions down −44%, FP stable/declining), and shows context-aware augmentation recovers performance with every augmented YOLO variant beating the dataset-only baseline on both clean and manipulated images.

核心贡献

  • 提出 ContextShift 受控基准与数据集：从 COCO 2017 出发，通过隐式（几何变换）与显式（合成/自然背景替换）操控系统性改变物体-上下文关系，同时保留物体外观
  • 识别出一一致的失效模式——"预测抑制"：漏检增加、预测数下降、误检稳定或下降；且根因是有效检测候选形成减少，而非置信度降低
  • 发现性能沿 NPMI 兼容性轴呈非单调 U 型：中间兼容性处最优，两极端退化（低兼容=上下文缺失；高兼容=未满足的特异性）
  • 证明该抑制行为在未篡改的真实 COCO 图像上也成立（非合成管线伪影），并提供基于上下文感知增强的缓解概念验证

Key Contributions

  • ContextShift, a controlled, fully reproducible benchmark + 131,885-sample dataset that isolates context as an independent variable while preserving object appearance, with a continuous NPMI-based object–background compatibility axis
  • Identification of a consistent cross-architecture failure mode — prediction suppression via reduced candidate formation (not confidence degradation) — replicated across 5 diverse detectors (Faster R-CNN, YOLO26M, Deformable DETR, RF-DETR, D-FINE)
  • Demonstration of non-monotonic U-shaped performance along the NPMI compatibility axis (peak at mid-range, degradation at both extremes via distinct mechanisms)
  • Real-image validation showing the suppression/U-shape persists on unmodified COCO val2017 (36,335 object–context pairs), ruling out composition-pipeline artifacts
  • A proof-of-concept mitigation using context-aware augmentation where every augmented YOLO variant outperforms the dataset-only baseline on both clean and manipulated data

Methodology

Benchmark design. Treats context as an independent variable. Each image x=(O,c), where O=(o,a) objects+annotations and c context. Manipulation functions f_manip(x)=x'=(O',c'). Two families: (i) implicit — geometric transforms (rotation, scaling [shrink/enlarge], translation) applied to a single object while c'=c, with the object's original footprint reconstructed via LaMa inpainting; (ii) explicit — background replaced while O'=O, either with synthetic patterns (solid color, gradient, low-frequency noise) or natural backgrounds sampled from Places365.

Compatibility axis. An |O|×|P| compatibility matrix between 80 COCO classes and 365 Places365 scene categories computed via Normalized Pointwise Mutual Information (NPMI, bounded [−1,1]), estimated from ~4.6M YOLO-World detections across Places365 at confidence 0.25. Detector-consistency check: Pearson correlation between the precomputed prior and each detector's own co-occurrence estimates is 67.7–78.1%.

Dataset. From 4,952 COCO 2017 val images → 131,885 manipulated samples across 38 folders. Implicit+synthetic variants materialized as discrete datasets; compatibility-driven natural background substitution done in-pipeline over K=30 compatibility bins to avoid combinatorial explosion.

Evaluated detectors. Faster R-CNN, YOLO26M, Deformable DETR, RF-DETR, D-FINE — spanning two-stage, one-stage, and transformer-based paradigms. Fixed inference: confidence threshold τ=0.25, model-default NMS, fixed input resolution, no TTA. Metrics: AP@0.5, rAUC (relative area under severity curve normalized by clean baseline), and behavioral metrics ∆FN/img, ∆FP/img, ∆pred/img. Natural-background eval restricted to single-object (|O|=1) images, 30 COCO classes.

Mitigation. YOLO retrained on COCO train2017 + ~11k manipulated images (~9%): compatibility-based bg_swap plus four geometric transforms. Evaluated on a held-out 990-image split of COCO val2017 under all five context shifts.

Results & Highlights

  • Implicit manipulation — rotation is worst: Rotation yields the lowest robustness (rAUC=0.57) with ∆pred/img=−30.2% and ∆FN/img=+93.5%; shrinkage produces the largest FN increase (∆FN/img=+106.2%, rAUC=0.81); translation is mildest (rAUC=0.94, ∆pred/img=−9.3%); enlargement slightly improves (rAUC=1.05). Mean across 5 detectors, on manipulated objects.
  • Natural background substitution — suppression: Faster R-CNN shows the worst false-negative explosion (∆FN/img=+227.1%) with AP rAUC=0.89 and ∆pred/img=−34.9%; D-FINE ∆FN/img=+151.5% (rAUC=0.87); across all 5 models FP declines (−33.2% to −60.8%) while predictions drop −31.2% to −44.2%.
  • Real-image U-shape: On unmodified COCO val2017 quintiles (n≈7,267/bin), performance peaks at Q3 (NPMI≈−0.019) and degrades at both extremes: Q1 impairs AP by −23.2% to −35.1% with +29.0% to +66.0% FN; Q5 degrades AP by −6.7% to −13.9% with +8.0% to +23.0% FN (relative to Q3).
  • Candidate-formation root cause: Detection candidate existence (IoU≥0.5) drops far more than mean confidence — Q1 ∆E=−22.5% vs ∆S=−3.34%; even in compatible contexts Q5 ∆E=−9.9%, confirming suppression persists beyond mismatched scenes.
  • Mitigation: Every augmented YOLO variant beats the dataset-only baseline — AP@0.5 improves by 1.1–2.6%, FN/img drops by 8.9–21.1%, and total prediction volume rises by 22–36% — on both clean and manipulated images (990-image held-out split).

Critical Assessment

The natural-background evaluation is restricted to single-object (|O|=1) images over only 30 COCO classes (deliberately excluding food items and small accessories) to make NPMI meaningful — so the headline suppression numbers (e.g. Faster R-CNN +227.1% FN) are not measured on the full multi-object COCO val set, limiting generality. The compatibility axis is purely statistical co-occurrence (NPMI), which the authors themselves flag as a limitation: it ignores visual cues like geometry, lighting, and perspective, so "high compatibility" does not guarantee perceptual realism and the U-shape interpretation conflates statistical association with visual plausibility. The mitigation is a proof-of-concept on a single model (YOLO) with only ~9% augmented data and a 990-image held-out split — it is not shown to transfer to the other four detectors, and no statistical significance testing is reported for the mitigation deltas. Composition artifacts are only partially controlled: while blending ablations (Appendix D.3.5) preserve trends, the LaMa-reconstructed footprint and four-stage blending pipeline still inject unmodeled visual cues that could contribute to suppression independently of "context."

Strengths

  • Genuinely isolates context as an independent variable while preserving object appearance — a cleaner axis than COCO-C/COCO-O which conflate multiple shift factors
  • The prediction-candidate analysis (∆E ≫ ∆S) is a crisp diagnostic distinguishing suppression from confidence drift, reusable for other models
  • Real-image validation on 36,335 unmodified pairs rules out composition-pipeline artifacts as the sole explanation
  • 5-detector cross-architecture replication (two-stage/one-stage/transformer) makes the suppression finding robust

Limitations

  • Compatibility axis is statistical-only (NPMI), ignoring visual realism cues the authors themselves name as future work
  • Natural-background eval limited to single-object, 30-class subset — not full COCO val
  • Mitigation proven only on YOLO with ~9% augmented data; no generalization to the other 4 detectors
  • No statistical significance testing reported on cross-method or mitigation comparisons (though CIs are given for some robustness metrics)
  • Restricted to COCO2017 + Places365; generalization to other domains/modalities untested

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the prediction-suppression failure mode (∆E ≫ ∆S, candidate formation reduced rather than confidence dropped) generalize beyond object detection to dense prediction tasks the authors name as future work (segmentation, tracking, video)?
  • The U-shape is explained via "context deficit" at low NPMI and "unmet specificity" at high NPMI — can these two mechanisms be operationally separated with a controlled ablation, or is the explanation currently post-hoc?
  • The mitigation is proven only on YOLO with ~9% augmented data on a 990-image split — would the same context-aware augmentation transfer to the transformer-based detectors (Deformable DETR, RF-DETR, D-FINE) that also exhibit suppression?
