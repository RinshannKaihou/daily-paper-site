Are Reasoning Vision-Language Models Robust to Semantic Visual Distractions?

["Yizheng Sun", "Mochuan Zhan", "Yanan Ma", "Jia Tong See", "Yifan Wang", "Ziyi Wang", "Hao Li", "Yang Cui", "Wenhao Cai", "Jingyu Sun", "Chenghua Lin", "Riza Batista-Navarro", "Jingyuan Sun"]

Date: 2026-06-08

Tags: ["NLP", "CV", "Robustness", "VLM", "Reasoning"]

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08894

一句话总结： 本文提出 Distract-Bench 基准，通过向图像注入"语义上正确但与问题无关"的干扰项（保留正确答案不变）来评测推理型视觉语言模型（VLM），发现推理增强型 VLM 在感知类腐蚀下几乎完全继承其底座的鲁棒性，却在语义干扰下表现更差——干扰常进入推理链并被视为证据。
TL;DR: The authors introduce Distract-Bench (506 human-verified, answer-preserving semantic-distraction samples built from MathVision/MathVista/MMMU) and evaluate 8 open-source + 2 closed-source VLMs. On Distract-Bench the Qwen2.5-VL-7B base retains 92.1% relative robustness (RR) while its reasoning variants drop to 84.4–90.3% RR; the base-vs-reasoning RR gap is only −1.3 pp under vision corruption but +5.0 pp under semantic distraction, and reasoning models reference injected distractors more often and more harmfully (e.g., Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking wrong-output reference ratio 67.7% vs. base 50.6%).

核心贡献

  • 提出"语义视觉干扰（semantic visual distraction）"这一新的鲁棒性失效模式：干扰内容事实正确、与问题无关、且不改变正确答案，从而把"感知失败"和"推理时的证据选择失败"分离开来。
  • 构建 Distract-Bench：506 条经人工核验的样本，经四阶段流程（原始样本采集→专家种子干扰→GPT-5.5 + GPT-Image2 检索式合成扩展→人工质检）生成，覆盖图表/数学/日常/艺术设计/医疗等视觉域。
  • 在同一组 506 样本上做对照实验，证明语义干扰暴露的失效轴与传统视觉腐蚀完全不同：推理模型在视觉腐蚀下与底座高度对齐，但在语义干扰下显著弱于底座。
  • 通过 DRR（Distraction Reference Rate）和 HFR（Harmful Reference Rate）等新指标，证明干扰项确实进入了推理过程、被当作证据，并最终导向错误答案。

Key Contributions

  • Defines a new robustness failure mode — answer-preserving semantic distraction — distinct from perceptual corruption, since the image stays clear and the ground-truth answer is unchanged.
  • Releases Distract-Bench, a 506-sample human-verified benchmark built from MathVision, MathVista and MMMU via a four-stage pipeline (collection → 100 expert seed distractors → retrieval-guided GPT-5.5/GPT-Image2 synthesis → human verification).
  • Provides paired controlled comparisons: 5 reasoning variants built on the Qwen2.5-VL-7B backbone (R1-OneVision, Vision-R1, MMR1, MM-Eureka, OpenVLThinker) plus the Qwen3-VL-8B / Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking pair, plus closed-source GPT-4o and GPT-5.
  • Introduces distractor-oriented metrics (DRR, HFR, correct/wrong-output reference ratios) that causally tie failures to distractors entering the reasoning chain, not random answer flips.

Methodology

Benchmark construction (Distract-Bench). The pipeline has four stages. (1) Raw sample collection: 800 image–question samples drawn from MathVision, MathVista and MMMU (reasoning-heavy sources, with MMMU for diversity). (2) Expert-designed seed distractors: human annotators produce 100 seeds, each distractor required to (i) carry obvious semantic content, (ii) be factually correct and visually plausible, (iii) be query-irrelevant so the answer is unchanged, and (iv) optionally lead to dead-ends/plausible wrong answers. (3) Scalable synthesis: GPT-5.5 summarizes each seed into a knowledge base of edit patterns; for each remaining sample it describes the image, retrieves 3 relevant seed examples, and emits a detailed edit instruction (content, location, style, layout constraints); GPT-Image2 then synthesizes the edited image preserving all original content except the injected distractor. (4) Human verification: every sample re-checked against the four criteria; samples violating any are removed, yielding the final 506-sample set spanning charts/tables/plots, math, daily scenes, art/design/media and medical images.

Evaluation setup. Eight benchmarks for the corruption study — ChartQA (2500), HallusionBench (951), InfoVQA (2801), MMMU (900), MMStar (1500), TextVQA (5000), MathVision (304 test-mini), MathVista (1000 test-mini) — totalling 14,956 samples. MathVision/MathVista grouped as "reasoning", the other six as "general". Corruption suite is 20 ImageNet-C-style perturbations (noise, blur, weather, appearance, compression, geometric, blank image) at severity 1–5.

Models. Qwen2.5-VL-7B base vs. 5 reasoning variants on the same backbone (R1-OneVision, Vision-R1, MMR1, MM-Eureka, OpenVLThinker); Qwen3-VL-8B vs Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking; plus closed-source GPT-4o and GPT-5 on Distract-Bench.

Metrics. Accuracy on clean/corrupted/distracted inputs; Relative Robustness RR = P_O/P_I; sample-level retention (correct-after-perturbation among clean-correct); error overlap via Jaccard; and the two new Distract-Bench metrics DRR = (1/N)Σr_i and HFR = (1/N)Σ r_i(1−c_i), plus correct-output and wrong-output reference ratios.

Decoding. Deterministic decoding; max output 4096 tokens, raised to 16384 for MathVision/MathVista to allow long reasoning chains; identical open-ended prompt for base and reasoning models on Distract-Bench (no "answer-only" instruction), so reasoning models are not disadvantaged in the distractor-reference analysis.

Results & Highlights

  • Reasoning models track base models under vision corruption but not distraction. Mean Pearson correlation of per-benchmark RR fingerprints is 0.978 for Qwen2.5-VL and 0.996 for Qwen3-VL families; base–reasoning pairs agree on retain/loss for 91.1% of clean-correct samples under corruption (mean Jaccard 0.675, retention gap +1.9 pp). On Distract-Bench the same pairs behave differently.
  • Distract-Bench breaks the inherited-robustness pattern. On the matched 506 samples, the mean base-minus-reasoning RR gap is only −1.3 pp under vision corruption but grows to +5.0 pp under semantic distraction, and the signed retention gap goes from −0.3 pp to +5.9 pp (Table 4) — i.e., reasoning models become less robust specifically to distraction.
  • Reasoning models retain less under distraction. Qwen2.5-VL-7B base keeps 92.1% RR (6.3 pp accuracy drop) on Distract-Bench, while its reasoning variants drop to 84.4–90.3% RR (8.1–12.8 pp drops). In the Qwen3-VL family, Qwen3-VL-8B base reaches 98.6% RR vs 95.3% RR for the Thinking variant (Table 3).
  • Distractors enter wrong answers much more often. Across all models wrong-output reference ratios far exceed correct-output ratios; e.g., Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking references the distractor in 67.7% of wrong vs 32.2% of correct outputs, and base Qwen3-VL-8B in 50.6% wrong vs 35.0% correct. For GPT-5 the figures are 53.4% wrong vs 9.1% correct (Table 5).
  • Harmful reference is concentrated in reasoning models. Reasoning-tuned models have consistently higher HFR than their base models (e.g., Qwen2.5 base HFR 8.9% vs MMR1 10.9% / OpenVLThinker 11.3%; Qwen3-VL-8B base 8.3% vs Thinking 12.8%).
  • Closed-source models are not immune. GPT-4o reaches 90.2% RR (7.5 pp drop) and GPT-5 95.6% RR (3.6 pp drop) on Distract-Bench — GPT-5 is the most robust model tested but still degrades.

Critical Assessment

  • Diagnostic only, no mitigation. The paper itself acknowledges (§Limitations) that it is "primarily diagnostic" and proposes no defense — so a practitioner reading it gets a failure mode and a benchmark but no remediation.
  • Benchmark built from synthetic edits under lab conditions. Distractors are injected via GPT-Image2 editing of clean benchmark images; the authors concede these "may not cover all types of real-world distractions," so the 506-sample set is an underestimate of real messiness rather than a representative sample of natural distraction.
  • Closed-source models are only evaluated on Distract-Bench, not on the full corruption suite. GPT-4o/GPT-5 appear in Table 3 but not in the corruption Tables 1–2, so the headline "reasoning tracks base under corruption" claim is established only for the open-source Qwen pairs — the cross-architecture robustness claim for closed models is weaker.
  • Reasoning-friendly prompting for all models may not reflect deployment. For the distractor-reference analysis all models use the same open-ended prompt; reasoning models that are normally used with chain-of-thought are compared against base models that are not, which could inflate reasoning models' DRR/HFR (more tokens = more chance to mention the distractor) even though the authors argue it makes the comparison fair.

Strengths

  • Clean conceptual separation of perceptual robustness (corruption) from reasoning/evidence-selection robustness (semantic distraction), with an answer-preserving design that controls task difficulty.
  • Strong controlled-pair methodology: reasoning variants share the exact Qwen2.5-VL-7B / Qwen3-VL-8B backbone, removing architecture as a confound.
  • New causal metrics (DRR/HFR + correct/wrong-output split) that go beyond accuracy and actually show the distractor entering the reasoning chain.
  • Human-verified benchmark with a transparent 4-stage construction pipeline and full prompt details (Appendix F).

Limitations

  • Distract-Bench is small (506 samples) and synthetic; generalization to naturally occurring real-world distraction is unproven.
  • No proposed mitigation or robustness-improvement method (the authors flag evidence selection / distraction-aware training / inference-time verification as future work).
  • Coverage limited to math/reasoning-heavy source benchmarks plus MMMU; everyday VQA-style distraction is not directly covered.
  • Closed-source models are not part of the corruption-control analysis.

Relevance

这直接映射到用户的「LLM Inference Evaluation」兴趣区——它本质上是一个评测基准与方法论贡献，重新定义了推理型 VLM（乃至推理型 LLM）的鲁棒性评测维度。其"干扰项进入推理链、被当作证据"的发现也与「LLM Hidden States & Interpretability」相关：它从输出层面揭示了推理过程的证据选择失败模式，可作为后续做 hidden-state / chain-of-thought 可解释性分析的切入点。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • The paper shows distractors are mentioned in wrong outputs (high DRR/HFR), but does not establish whether the distractor caused the error or merely co-occurs with it — could a counterfactual/ablation (same image, distractor removed at the failing sample) be run to measure causal contribution?
  • Since all reasoning variants share the Qwen2.5-VL backbone, which RL/SFT recipe (R1-OneVision, MM-Eureka's rule-based RL, OpenVLThinker's iterative SFT/RL) is most/least distraction-sensitive? The aggregate analysis does not break this down.
  • The paper cites Shi et al. (2023) on LLMs being "easily distracted by irrelevant context" in the text-only setting — is the magnitude of the reasoning-vs-base distraction gap comparable between the text-only and the VLM setting, or does the visual modality amplify it?
  • Could the wrong-output vs correct-output reference ratio gap (e.g., 67.7% vs 32.2% for Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking) be used as an inference-time detector to abstain on likely-distracted inputs?
