> **一句话总结：** 本文系统比较六类分子指纹（ECFP/FCFP/MACCS/PubChem/RDKit/SMARTS）在 MLP 与 MLP+Transformer-encoder（MLP+TL）两种模型上、跨七个药物性质数据集的表现，发现模型在毒性/致突变/副作用等分类任务上 AUC 普遍 >0.9（ToxCast 上 RDKit+MLP 达 0.974），最优指纹依任务/架构而异，且 MLP+TL 借助内在注意力权重（无需 LIME/SHAP）可在 BBB 通透性与致突变性上识别出化学可解释的关键子结构（如 Morphine 比 Heroin 多出被高注意力命中的羟基 MACCS key 139）。

> **TL;DR:** The authors benchmark 6 molecular fingerprints (ECFP, FCFP, MACCS, PubChem, RDKit, SMARTS) on two models (MLP and a Transformer-encoder + MLP, "MLP+TL") across 7 datasets (BACE, BBBP, ClinTox, ToxCast, MUTAG, SIDER, ESOL). Classification AUC is consistently >0.9 — e.g. RDKit+MLP reaches AUC 0.974 ± 0.004 on ToxCast, MACCS+MLP 0.925 ± 0.023 on ClinTox, FCFP+MLP 0.891 ± 0.021 on BBBP — beating other fingerprints; for ESOL regression the best RMSE is 0.986 ± 0.063 (RDKit+MLP). The MLP+TL model's intrinsic attention weights (no LIME/SHAP) recover chemically meaningful BBB determinants (e.g. MACCS key 139 = OH gets max attention 0.01199 in Morphine vs 0.00711 in Heroin).

## 核心贡献

- 系统比较六类分子指纹（路径基 ECFP/FCFP/RDKit、子结构基 MACCS/PubChem、字符串基 SMARTS）在 MLP 与 MLP+TL 两种架构上、跨七个药物性质数据集的预测表现，给出"任务×架构→最优指纹"的实用选择指南。
- 证明加入 Transformer-encoder 层虽在多数任务上略逊于纯 MLP，但带来**内在可解释性**：可直接用注意力权重定位重要指纹位，而无需依赖外部事后解释（LIME/SHAP）。
- 把注意力分析做到化学可解释的颗粒度——在 BBBP 与 MUTAG 上把高频高注意力指纹位归类为脂溶性/极性/氢键/离子化等化学意义群组，并用 Morphine vs Heroin 的对照（同一骨架、羟基是否被乙酰化掩盖）验证注意力确实命中了决定 BBB 通透性的羟基相关子结构。
- 提供完整的对外验证（BBBP→B3DB、ESOL→A+P）以及更新的 ToxCast 数据策展流水线（从 US EPA CompTox 服务经阈值二值化、SMILES 映射构建 assay×分子矩阵，assay 从 1,501 过滤到 555）。

## Key Contributions

- A systematic benchmark of 6 fingerprint types × 2 architectures (MLP, MLP+TL) × 7 datasets, producing a task-specific "best fingerprint" guide (Table 3): e.g. RDKit+MLP best for ToxCast/ESOL/BACE, MACCS+MLP best for ClinTox/MUTAG, FCFP best for BBBP, SMARTS best for SIDER.
- Demonstrates the Transformer-encoder layer is competitive with (slightly behind) plain MLP on accuracy but adds *intrinsic* interpretability via attention weights, avoiding external post-hoc explainers (LIME/SHAP).
- Chemically-grounded interpretability case studies on BBBP and MUTAG: head-averaged attention on the last encoder layer is aggregated per fingerprint bit, the top-20 bits per molecule and top-30 dataset-level bits are grouped into chemically meaningful categories (lipophilicity, polarity, H-bonding, ionization, flexibility).
- A Morphine-vs-Heroin controlled comparison validating that attention recovers the known OH-group determinant of BBB permeability (Morphine's free OH hinders BBB crossing; Heroin's acetylated form crosses more readily).
- External validation on independent datasets (B3DB for BBBP after deduplication = 4,368 molecules; AQUA+PHYS for ESOL = 1,362 molecules) and a refreshed ToxCast curation pipeline.

## Methodology

**Models.** (a) **MLP**: classical feedforward network, neuron zᵢ = Σ wᵢⱼ xⱼ + bᵢ with LeakyReLU activation; RMSE loss for regression, trained by backprop. (b) **MLP+TL**: a Transformer-encoder stack on top of an MLP head. Crucially, the input is a *binary fingerprint vector* x ∈ {0,1}ᴰ (not word embeddings); an identity matrix I_D ∈ ℝᴰˣᴰ is gated by the bits so each active bit becomes its one-hot basis vector (eq. 12–13), giving a (batch, D, D) tensor. Standard multi-head self-attention (scaled dot-product, d_k key dim), residual + LayerNorm, position-wise FFN with ReLU. The encoder output is average-pooled over the sequence dim to a D-vector, then fed to the MLP head. Attention matrix ā (head-averaged, last layer) ∈ ℝᴰˣᴰ is used for interpretability (bit-to-bit heatmaps; column-wise attention sum; top-20 keys per molecule).

**Molecular encodings investigated.** ECFP, FCFP (circular, dynamically generated fragments), RDKit (path-based), MACCS (167 substructure keys), PubChem (881-bit CACTVS fingerprints retrieved via pubchempy), SMARTS (SMILES→SMARTS patterns tokenized by regex on -\/=#~@: delimiters, then TF-IDF bag-of-words embeddings).

**Datasets (7 internal + 2 external).** BACE (1513, β-secretase 1 binding, IC50 ≤ 100 nM = active), BBBP (2050, BBB penetration), ClinTox (1483, clinical toxicity), ToxCast (curated here from US EPA CompTox, 9002→7787 molecules, assays 1501→555 after ≥50 toxic/≥50 non-toxic filter, target assay NVS_ADME_hCYP2C19), MUTAG (188, mutagenicity in S. typhimurium), SIDER (1427, multi-label ADRs, target label "Nervous system disorders"), ESOL (1128, aqueous solubility regression). External: B3DB (BBB, deduplicated to 4368) and AQUA+PHYS (solubility, deduplicated to 1362).

**Preprocessing.** RDKit+MolVS standardization (H removal, metal disconnection, fragment cleanup, charge/radical neutralization, tautomer canonicalization, InChI round-trip), majority-vote label aggregation for duplicates, SMOTEENN oversampling when minority class <0.3, StandardScaler on ESOL targets (fit on train only). 8:2 train-val/test split; 5-fold CV within train-val for hyperparameters; then 8:20 train/val with early stopping. Multi-assay datasets (SIDER/ClinTox/ToxCast) use the Feature Net (FN) imputation framework: XGBoost per-assay imputes missing labels, which are appended as auxiliary inputs.

**Metrics.** AUC-ROC (primary for classification, threshold-independent) and F1/Recall/Precision/Accuracy; RMSE (primary for ESOL regression) and MSE/MAE/R².

## Results & Highlights

- **Classification AUC consistently >0.9 on biologically relevant tasks.** Best per-dataset AUC (5-fold CV): ToxCast RDKit+MLP **0.974 ± 0.004**; ClinTox MACCS+MLP **0.925 ± 0.023**; MUTAG MACCS+MLP **0.913 ± 0.026** (MLP+TL ECFP 0.920 ± 0.046); SIDER SMARTS **0.916 ± 0.021** (MLP) / 0.918 ± 0.013 (MLP+TL); BACE FCFP+MLP **0.882 ± 0.017**; BBBP FCFP+MLP **0.891 ± 0.021** (MLP+TL 0.871 ± 0.015).
- **ESOL regression in the literature range.** Best RMSE = **0.986 ± 0.063** (RDKit+MLP); MLP+TL best 1.132 ± 0.034 (RDKit); the paper notes solubility RMSE is commonly 0.7–1.0, so results are "within a reasonable range."
- **MLP ≳ MLP+TL on accuracy, but MLP+TL adds interpretability.** ESOL and ToxCast show the largest MLP-vs-MLP+TL gaps (significant differences in 100% and 77% of metric comparisons respectively); on BBBP/BACE the gap is negligible (0%/3%).
- **Fingerprint choice is a major determinant.** SMARTS and RDKit show the largest cross-dataset performance variation (frequently first or last); FCFP, ECFP, MACCS are the most robust (most frequently top-3), especially under MLP.
- **External validation reveals generalization limits.** On B3DB (BBBP external), RDKit/FCFP/MACCS AUC stayed ≥0.8 in MLP, but FCFP in MLP+TL dropped to **0.66**. On A+P (ESOL external), RDKit+MLP+TL RMSE rose substantially to **1.45**, dropping its ranking from first to fourth — showing internal CV ranking does not always hold externally.
- **Attention recovers chemically meaningful BBB determinants.** Top MACCS keys for BBBP group into lipophilicity (e.g. C-C-C-C-C chains, which *favor* BBB by increasing hydrophobic surface), polarity/H-bonding (e.g. MACCS key 139 = OH, *unfavorable*), ionization (e.g. key 121 = N-heterocycle), and flexibility — matching prior knowledge that BBB permeability is governed by lipophilicity, polarity, H-bonding, ionization, flexibility.
- **Morphine-vs-Heroin controlled validation.** An OH-related substructure (MACCS key 139) appears in Morphine's top-20 important features but *not* in Heroin's; Morphine's max attention weight on key 139 is **0.01199** (from query 98) vs Heroin's **0.00711** (from query 122) — consistent with the known chemistry that Morphine's free hydroxyls hinder BBB crossing while Heroin's acetylated (masked) form crosses more readily.

## Critical Assessment

- **MLP+TL does not actually beat plain MLP on accuracy** — the paper itself concludes "the MLP model achieved the best performance on most datasets" and the Transformer-encoder's advantage is "not substantial" in MLP+TL's favour; the only real selling point of MLP+TL is interpretability, so the contribution rests entirely on the attention-as-explanation claim rather than a predictive win.
- **Attention-as-explanation is not validated against ground-truth feature importance.** The Morphine/Heroin and BBB-key case studies are *qualitatively* consistent with known chemistry, but the paper never compares the attention-derived important bits against an external ground-truth (e.g. known BBB toxicophores from prior work, or a LIME/SHAP agreement rate), so it is unclear how often attention highlights *incorrect* bits that happen to look plausible in the cherry-picked examples.
- **External validation exposes weak generalization that internal CV hides.** FCFP+MLP+TL AUC drops to 0.66 on B3DB and RDKit+MLP+TL RMSE jumps to 1.45 on A+P — yet the paper still presents internal-CV rankings as the "best fingerprint" guide (Table 3), which the external results partially contradict.
- **Small datasets and single-task framing limit scope.** MUTAG has only 188 molecules; SIDER's 27 labels are collapsed to a single "Nervous system disorders" target; ToxCast's 555 assays are collapsed to NVS_ADME_hCYP2C19 — so the "systematic" claim is really about 7 narrow single-task benchmarks, not multi-task or large-scale property prediction.

## Strengths

- Genuinely systematic design: 6 fingerprints × 2 architectures × 7 datasets with 5-fold CV, plus external validation on 2 independent datasets — more rigorous than typical single-dataset molecular-ML papers.
- Chemically grounded interpretability that connects attention-derived bits to known ADMET determinants (lipophilicity, H-bonding, ionization) rather than leaving them as abstract feature indices.
- The Morphine-vs-Heroin controlled comparison is a clean, convincing validation that attention tracks a real chemical determinant (OH masking by acetylation).
- Refreshed ToxCast curation pipeline (US EPA CompTox, ≥50/50 assay filter, positive-dominant dedup) is a useful community contribution given the original MoleculeNet datasets are stale.

## Limitations

- No predictive advantage for the Transformer-encoder; its value is interpretability only.
- Attention-as-explanation is shown qualitatively but not benchmarked against ground-truth feature importance or against LIME/SHAP agreement.
- External validation contradicts some internal-CV rankings (FCFP on B3DB, RDKit on A+P), so the "best fingerprint" guide should be read as dataset-specific.
- Small/narrow datasets (MUTAG 188; SIDER collapsed to one label) limit the generality of the conclusions.

## Relevance

这篇工作主要映射到用户的「LLM Hidden States & Interpretability」兴趣区——虽然它是分子指纹而非 LLM，但其核心方法论贡献正是"用 Transformer 编码器的内在注意力权重（而非外部事后解释 LIME/SHAP）来做可解释性分析"，与 LLM 中"用注意力/隐藏态做内在可解释性"的思路同源。它也触及「LLM Inference Evaluation」的外延：本质是一份关于"不同输入编码方法如何影响下游模型预测"的系统评测研究。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The Morphine/Heroin case study is compelling but cherry-picked — what is the *dataset-level* agreement rate between MLP+TL's top-attention MACCS/PubChem bits and an independent ground-truth (e.g. known BBB toxicophores from ref. [59, 60], or LIME/SHAP rankings on the same model), across all BBBP molecules rather than two?
- External validation contradicts internal CV for FCFP on B3DB (AUC 0.66) and RDKit on A+P (RMSE 1.45) — does the ranking instability come from chemical-space shift between internal and external sets (Figure S8a-b), and would scaffold-based or time-based splits reduce the internal-vs-external gap?
- The Transformer-encoder's only advantage is interpretability, yet it never beats MLP on accuracy; would a larger fingerprint vocabulary (e.g. 4096-bit circular fingerprints instead of 167-key MACCS) let the attention mechanism's bit-interaction modelling finally outperform MLP, or is the binary-fingerprint input too information-poor for self-attention to add value?
- The paper uses the last encoder layer's head-averaged attention; do different layers/heads highlight different chemical substructure families (e.g. lower layers = local atom patterns, higher layers = functional groups), and could per-layer attention give a richer, multi-granularity chemical interpretation?
