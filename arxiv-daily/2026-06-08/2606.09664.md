> **一句话总结：** 本文针对 tabular foundation model（TabPFN-3）作为 LSBO 代理时与分子隐空间目标分布不匹配的问题，提出 LILBO：用 GuacaMol 分子 VAE 隐空间上的 BO 感知合成任务继续预训练，并用 L2-SP 正则锚定原始权重，在 8 个 held-out GuacaMol 多性质优化任务上取得最佳平均排名（2.64 vs TabPFN-3 的 2.94）。

> **TL;DR:** The paper addresses a train/deploy mismatch: tabular foundation models (TFMs) like TabPFN-3 are pretrained on generic synthetic regression tasks, but latent-space Bayesian optimization (LSBO) needs surrogates that map molecular-VAE latent codes to property scores over value-biased BO histories. The authors propose **LILBO** (Latent In-context Learning for BO): continued-pretrain a TabPFN-3 checkpoint on ~500k synthetic LSBO episodes built from GuacaMol base tasks (logP, QED, similarity, rediscovery) combined linearly/non-linearly, with a Boltzmann value-biased context sampler and an L2-SP regularizer anchoring to the original weights. On 8 held-out GuacaMol multi-property objectives (Osimertinib, Fexofenadine, Ranolazine, Zaleplon, Amlodipine, Perindopril, Median Molecules 1/2), LILBO achieves the best average rank of 2.64±1.56 vs InvBO 2.88±1.62, off-the-shelf TabPFN-3 2.94±1.64, NFBO 2.96±1.69, and GP-LSBO 5.86±1.75.

## 核心贡献

- 识别并形式化 LSBO 中 tabular foundation model 代理的"训练-部署不匹配"：通用合成回归任务的先验与分子 VAE 隐空间→目标值的映射、以及 BO 历史的价值偏置分布差异显著
- 提出 BO 感知的合成任务生成：从 GuacaMol 基础任务（logP/QED/similarity/rediscovery）经线性（Dirichlet 权重）或非线性（随机 MLP）组合产生合成目标，并用 Boltzmann 分布做价值偏置的上下文采样
- 引入 L2-SP 继续预训练目标：在 TabPFN-3 检查点上做轻量适配（~500k episodes vs 原始 >8T tokens），同时锚定原始权重以保留通用回归先验、避免灾难性遗忘
- 提供两类先验诊断（隐空间覆盖 UMAP、目标空间覆盖 UMAP）验证合成分布是否覆盖 LSBO 相关区域
- 在 8 个 held-out GuacaMol 多性质优化任务上取得最佳平均排名，证明 LSBO 特化适配对 in-context 代理的价值

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes the pretrain/deploy mismatch between generic TFM synthetic regression priors and LSBO's latent-code → property-score mapping with value-biased BO histories
- BO-aware synthetic episode generation (Algorithm 1): base tasks from GuacaMol combined linearly (Dirichlet weights) or via random MLPs, with a per-episode Boltzmann temperature T_τ for value-biased context sampling
- L2-SP continued-pretraining objective (Eq. 10) anchoring the adapted TabPFN-3 to its original checkpoint θ_0, trading specialization against catastrophic forgetting (à la Real-TabPFN)
- Two diagnostic suites — latent-space coverage and objective-space coverage via UMAP — that check whether the synthetic prior spans BO-relevant regions
- Empirical demonstration that LILBO achieves the best average rank (2.64) across 8 held-out GuacaMol objectives, beating off-the-shelf TabPFN-3 (2.94) and dedicated LSBO baselines

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** Structured design space X (molecules), expensive black-box f: X → R, learned encoder e_ψ / decoder p_ψ(x|z) over latent space Z ⊂ R^d. LSBO searches over latent codes z; with a stochastic decoder the objective is g(z) = E_{x∼p_ψ(·|z)}[f(x)]. Given history H_t = {(z^i, y^i)}, fit a surrogate q_t(y|z, H_t) and pick the next query by an acquisition rule (here Thompson sampling, Eq. 11). Classically q_t is a Gaussian process; the authors replace it with a pretrained in-context model q_θ(y|z, H_t).

**Synthetic BO-aware task generation (Algorithm 1).** (1) Encode the GuacaMol pool M once via the SELFIES VAE to get latent codes z^m. (2) Build a base-task descriptor vector s(x) = [s_1(x),…,s_K(x)] from molecular base tasks (logP, QED, similarity, rediscovery — the held-out benchmark objectives are *excluded*). (3) For each episode sample a synthetic scalar objective: linear f_τ^lin(x) = w_τ^⊤ s(x) with w_τ ~ Dirichlet(1_K), or nonlinear f_τ^nlin(x) = g_τ(s(x)) with g_τ a random MLP. Additional combiner families (formula trees) in Appendix A. (4) Value-biased context sampling via a Boltzmann distribution p_τ(m) ∝ exp(f_τ(x_m)/T_τ) with per-episode temperature T_τ, biasing contexts toward high-value regions while keeping diversity. (5) Split sampled indices into context (n_ctx) and query (n_qry) sets; emit episode E_τ = (C_τ, Q_τ).

**Continued pretraining (Eq. 10).** Initialize from a pretrained TabPFN-3 checkpoint θ_0. Loss = L_CE(θ) + λ·L2-SP‖θ − θ_0‖₂², where the L2-SP term limits drift and mitigates catastrophic forgetting (Kirkpatrick et al. 2017). Train 8,000 continued-pretraining steps ≈ 500k molecular LSBO episodes (episode length 1024). Full optimization details in Table 3 / Appendix B.1. This is deliberately lightweight relative to TabPFN-3's >8 trillion synthetic pretraining tokens.

**Test-time LSBO loop.** Pipeline unchanged: candidate batch Z_t^cand produced by local search; retrieve up to n_ctx nearby/high-value observations from BO history; score candidates with the pretrained model; select next query by Thompson sampling f̂_t(z) ∼ q_θ(·|z, H_t), z_{t+1} = argmax_z f̂_t(z). Periodic VAE retraining is kept (beneficial per LOLBO), inducing latent drift — a noted limitation.

**Setup values.** SELFIES VAE (Maus et al.) pretrained on 1.27M GuacaMol molecules; 256-dimensional latents; 100–600 context rows. 8 held-out GuacaMol multi-property objectives: Osimertinib, Fexofenadine, Ranolazine, Zaleplon, Amlodipine, Perindopril, Median Molecules 1/2. Each run starts from 100 randomly sampled molecules; best value vs oracle calls over 10 seeds; protocol follows CoBO (Lee et al. 2023). Baselines: GP-LSBO, LOLBO, CoBO, InvBO, NF-BO, random latent search, and off-the-shelf TabPFN-3 plug-in. All baselines use Thompson sampling over a common candidate set; all except LSBO and random do VAE retraining.

## Results & Highlights

- **Average rank across all 8 held-out tasks (Table 1):** LILBO **2.64 ± 1.56** (best), InvBO 2.88 ± 1.62, off-the-shelf TabPFN-3 **2.94 ± 1.64**, NFBO 2.96 ± 1.69, GP-LSBO **5.86 ± 1.75**, LOLBO 5.90 ± 1.37, Random 6.25 ± 1.68, CoBO 6.34 ± 1.19. LILBO beats the off-the-shelf TabPFN-3 plug-in by 0.30 in average rank.
- **TFMs are already competitive LSBO surrogates (Figure 3):** Both off-the-shelf TabPFN-3 and LILBO sit in the leading group across all held-out molecular objectives, with the rest of the LSBO pipeline (candidate generation, Thompson sampling, VAE retraining) held fixed — isolating the surrogate replacement.
- **Scale asymmetry of the adaptation:** TabPFN-3 was pretrained on >8 trillion synthetic tokens; TabICLv2 on ~35M synthetic datasets. LILBO's molecular adaptation uses only ~500k LSBO episodes of length 1024 on 256-dim SELFIES-VAE latents — orders of magnitude smaller, yet still improves average rank, motivating the L2-SP anchor to preserve the broad regression prior.
- **Objective-space coverage (Figure 2):** On a 5,000-molecule probe set, 5,000 synthetic tasks form continuous clusters around the base objectives, and the held-out GuacaMol benchmarks lie close to (not isolated from) the synthetic task mass — supporting that the prior covers relevant molecular-objective landscapes.
- **Latent-space coverage (Figure 1):** UMAP over uniform / Boltzmann / perturbed samples shows the value-biased sampler populates high-value latent regions while the perturbation mechanism stays close to the latent manifold (moderate provenance differences).

## Critical Assessment

- **Workshop paper with a single aggregated metric and no per-task win table in the main text:** The headline result is an average rank (2.64 vs 2.94 for TabPFN-3), and the authors concede LILBO "does not uniformly dominate every task or every specialized LSBO baseline" — the per-task breakdown lives only in Figure 3 (qualitative curves), so the reader cannot see on which of the 8 objectives LILBO actually wins versus ties/loses.
- **Gain over off-the-shelf TabPFN-3 is within ~1 standard deviation:** LILBO's 2.64 ± 1.56 vs TabPFN-3's 2.94 ± 1.64 overlaps substantially in the reported std bands, so the "LSBO-specific adaptation helps" claim is statistically modest rather than decisive, despite being the paper's central message.
- **Latent drift is unaddressed at pretraining time:** The authors flag as a limitation that periodic VAE retraining at test time creates a mismatch with the frozen-latent pretraining, yet the L2-SP anchor and synthetic-prior design do nothing to handle this drift — so the deployed surrogate operates partly out of its training distribution.
- **Synthetic prior never simulates actual BO trajectories:** The Perspectives section itself notes that pretraining episodes sample from the molecular pool rather than simulating short optimization trajectories, so contexts lack high-value molecules beyond those already in GuacaMol — a recognized train/deploy gap that the current method does not close.

## Strengths

- Clean problem framing: explicitly identifies *why* a generic TFM surrogate is mismatched to LSBO (latent-code→property map + value-biased histories), rather than just plugging it in
- The L2-SP continued-pretraining recipe is a principled, lightweight way to specialize without discarding the expensive broad prior — directly inspired by Real-TabPFN
- Honest diagnostics (latent-space and objective-space UMAP coverage) that actually try to validate the synthetic prior rather than just asserting it
- Holds the LSBO pipeline fixed to isolate the surrogate effect — a fair experimental design

## Limitations

- Workshop-length paper; central empirical gain (2.64 vs 2.94 average rank) overlaps within one standard deviation of the off-the-shelf TabPFN-3 baseline
- No per-task quantitative win/loss table in the main text; per-task comparison only via qualitative curves in Figure 3
- Latent drift from periodic VAE retraining is acknowledged but not handled by the method
- Pretraining episodes sample from the pool rather than simulating BO trajectories, leaving a residual train/deploy gap the authors themselves name as future work
- No code release URL found in the body text read; reproducibility depends on the external TabPFN-3 and SELFIES-VAE checkpoints

## Relevance

- 弱相关于 **Self-Evolving Agents & Automated Research**：本文用 in-context tabular foundation model 作为自动化分子设计（贝叶斯优化）的代理，属于自动化研究流程中的一环，但核心是 BO/分子设计而非 self-evolving agent。
- 与其余用户兴趣区（LLM 训练稳定性、推理评估、隐状态可解释性、计算精度、SDC、模型卡、ML 数学基础）相关性较弱——它是 tabular foundation model + Bayesian optimization 的工作，不直接涉及 LLM 内部机制或计算可靠性。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The paper's own Perspectives section proposes simulating short BO trajectories in pretraining episodes (citing Li et al. 2025) rather than sampling only from the GuacaMol pool — would this close the residual train/deploy gap and push LILBO's average-rank lead outside TabPFN-3's std band?
- Since the L2-SP anchor does not handle latent drift from periodic VAE retraining, could the synthetic prior be augmented with evolving latent representations (e.g., pretraining on multiple VAE checkpoints) so the in-context surrogate is drift-robust?
- The 2.64 vs 2.94 average-rank gain overlaps within one standard deviation — does a paired per-seed test across the 8 tasks × 10 seeds show a statistically significant improvement, or is the win driven by 1–2 tasks?
- The framework is demonstrated on small-molecule design; the authors cite protein / antimicrobial-peptide design as extensions — does the same LSBO-aware adaptation transfer when the generative model is a discrete-diffusion protein designer (Gruver et al. 2024) rather than a SELFIES VAE?
- TabPFN-3 is already a competitive plug-in surrogate; how much of LILBO's gain is simply from the value-biased context sampler versus the molecular synthetic objectives — is there an ablation isolating the two?
