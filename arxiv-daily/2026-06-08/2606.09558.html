Integrating gene regulatory priors into Transformer attention with scTransformer for interpretable scRNA-seq analysis

["Mikele Milia", "Louis Fabrice Tshimanga", "Henning Mueller", "Manfredo Atzori", "Barbara Di Camillo"]

arXiv: 2606.09558

一句话总结： scTransformer 把 CollecTRI 转录因子-靶基因调控网络作为硬注意力掩码嵌入 Transformer 多头自注意力，在 MSSM PsychAD 单核 RNA-seq（17 亚型）细胞分类上与无先验基线准确率相当，但在低数据（1%、25%）时更准、跨种子特征选择更稳定，并产生符合已知调控程序的注意力模式。
TL;DR: On the MSSM PsychAD snRNA-seq atlas (17 cell subtypes, 70/20/10 split, 10 seeds), scTransformer with a directed TF→TG prior mask matches the unconstrained baseline at full data (0.911 vs 0.908 accuracy on TF–TG genes; full-gene unconstrained 0.917) and beats it in low-data regimes — at 1% training data 0.486 vs 0.464, at 25% 0.878 vs 0.869 — while dramatically improving cross-run feature-selection reproducibility (Jaccard overlap rises from near-zero to clearly positive, Spearman from negative to positive).

核心贡献

  • 首次将 curated TF–TG 调控网络（CollecTRI）以硬二值掩码形式直接嵌入 Transformer 多头自注意力，约束哪些基因-基因交互被允许
  • 提出连续正弦编码（sinusoidal encoding）保留 scRNA-seq 表达值的完整动态范围，不做离散化/阈值化
  • 基于 CSR 稀疏矩阵的稀疏 batching + 训练流水线，避免稠密张量展开，可扩展到大规模单细胞数据
  • 一套 module 级可解释性指标（focus score φ、module importance）量化 TF 模块的注意力集中度与调控重要性
  • 在 1%–100% 训练数据比例下系统评估先验门控 vs 无先验，证明先验在低数据区提升样本效率与跨种子稳定性

Key Contributions

  • First Transformer that integrates curated TF–TG regulatory networks directly into the attention mechanism via hard binary masks (a directed TF→TG prior), rather than post-hoc regularization or soft penalties
  • A continuous sinusoidal encoding of gene-expression values that preserves quantitative information across the full dynamic range
  • A sparse batching/training pipeline operating directly on CSR matrices, avoiding dense tensor expansion for scalability
  • Formal module-level interpretability metrics (focus score φ, module importance) quantifying attention concentration and regulatory importance per TF module
  • A systematic data-regime study (1%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 100% training data, 10 seeds) isolating the prior's effect on accuracy, reproducibility, and interpretability

Methodology

Architecture. A Pre-LN Transformer encoder: N layers, each = multi-head self-attention (H heads, per-head dim d_k = d/H) + feed-forward network (FFN, GLU variants), both with residual connections. Gene tokens carry identity embeddings plus a continuous sinusoidal encoding of expression values. A pooling layer over non-padding/non-target tokens produces a cell-level representation mapped to class logits by a linear classifier head.

Prior-gated attention (core). Instead of unconstrained self-attention, a hard prior restricts which gene pairs may interact. From the CollecTRI regulatory resource, a directed TF→TG allow-matrix is built: TF source genes s ∈ T may attend to their annotated targets Γ⁺(s), plus self-loops; all other gene–gene interactions are forbidden. TFs with fewer than 15 annotated targets are excluded. The boolean allow-matrix is converted to an additive mask added to scaled dot-product scores before softmax (forbidden entries → −∞ → exactly zero attention weight). The mask is shared across all heads (each head still learns distinct Q/K/V projections under the same constraint); optionally the pooling layer is also restricted to TF tokens.

Evaluation setup. Dataset: MSSM subset of the PsychAD cohort (Mount Sinai, dorsolateral prefrontal cortex, 10x Chromium 3' v3.1 snRNA-seq; QC: UMI 1,500–110,000, genes 1,100–12,500, mito <5%; CellBender ambient correction, Scrublet doublet removal), restricted to 17 well-represented cell subtypes. Split: train/val/test = 70/20/10 stratified; training subset progressively reduced to {1%, 25%, 50%, 75%, 100%} with val/test fixed. Optimizer: AdamW (η=1e-4, λ=1e-5), effective batch size 128, early stopping (patience 5, max 50 epochs). 10 random seeds per config; results as mean ± std. Two factors crossed: feature space (all genes vs TF–TG genes only) and prior (none vs directed TF→TG).

Interpretability metrics. Focus score φ and module importance computed per class k, layer ℓ, head h using the last encoder layer, yielding per-module regulatory importance scores; cross-run stability of top-N selected genes measured by Jaccard overlap and Spearman rank correlation.

Results & Highlights

  • Full-data accuracy (Table 1): TF→TG-prior model on TF–TG genes reaches 0.911 ± 0.013 vs no-prior 0.908 ± 0.008 — statistically comparable, and close to the all-genes unconstrained baseline (0.917 ± 0.012) despite operating on a substantially restricted interaction space.
  • Low-data sample efficiency (Table 1): the prior wins when data are scarce — at 1% training data 0.486 ± 0.066 vs 0.464 ± 0.059 (no prior), and at 25% 0.878 ± 0.015 vs 0.869 ± 0.012; at 50%/75% performance is comparable (e.g., 50%: 0.899 vs 0.897).
  • Cross-run reproducibility (Fig. 3): without priors, top-N selected genes show Jaccard overlap near zero and consistently negative Spearman correlations across runs; the prior-gated model raises both metrics (largest gains for small N), showing distinct unconstrained runs converge on disjoint gene subsets that happen to predict equally well.
  • Module focus & biological coherence: directed TF→TG masking consistently increases focus score φ (TFs attend to smaller, more specific target subsets), most pronounced in intermediate encoder layers, accompanied by higher module importance for biologically coherent TF programs; top recurrent TFs include lineage/plasticity regulators GRHL3, DLX2, NANOG, with subtype-localized activation (e.g., GRHL3/CDX1 in endothelial-like/VLMC, DLX2/NANOG in OPC/adaptive subtypes).

Critical Assessment

  • Headline accuracy gain is marginal / within noise: at 100% data the prior model (0.911 ± 0.013) is within one standard deviation of the no-prior model (0.908 ± 0.008) and below the all-genes unconstrained baseline (0.917) — the paper itself frames the prior's value as interpretability/stability rather than predictive lift, so the abstract's "improves classification accuracy" overstates the full-data result.
  • Single dataset, single task: evaluation is restricted to one snRNA-seq atlas (MSSM PsychAD) and one supervised task (cell-type classification); generalization across tissues, diseases, protocols, and to self-supervised pretraining is explicitly listed as untested future work.
  • No comparison to existing single-cell foundation models: the paper cites scGPT [2], Geneformer [3], scBERT [9], scFormer [10], and the Kedzierska et al. zero-shot evaluation [4] noting their inconsistent benefits, but never benchmarks scTransformer against them — only against its own unconstrained-mask ablation. The strongest external baseline is absent.
  • Hard-mask rigidity: forbidden edges are fully blocked (−∞), so the model cannot override the curated prior even when data strongly support a novel interaction; the authors acknowledge this and propose soft/learnable priors as future work, but the current design can propagate errors or gaps in CollecTRI.
  • Gene-space restriction side effect: applying the prior requires restricting to annotated TF–TG pairs, excluding unannotated genes that may carry signal — a hybrid architecture is proposed but not implemented.

Strengths

  • Architecturally clean idea: regulatory structure embedded directly in attention computation (hard masks), not as a soft post-hoc penalty — a strong, falsifiable inductive bias
  • Rigorous experimental protocol: 10 seeds with mean ± std, stratified splits, fixed test set across data-regime sweep, separate ablation of feature-space restriction vs prior presence
  • Honest framing: the paper explicitly admits accuracy is comparable at full data and locates the prior's value in low-data sample efficiency and cross-run reproducibility rather than inflating a headline metric
  • Biologically grounded interpretability output (focus score, subtype-localized TF modules) that downstream analysts can actually use

Limitations

  • Validated on a single dataset (MSSM PsychAD) and a single task (supervised cell-type classification); no self-supervised pretraining, no transfer/zero-shot evaluation
  • No head-to-head comparison with scGPT/Geneformer/scBERT — only against the authors' own unconstrained ablation
  • Hard binary masks cannot recover from prior errors/gaps; soft priors not implemented
  • Restricting to TF–TG-annotated genes discards unannotated-but-relevant genes
  • Accuracy differences between prior and no-prior are within standard deviations at most data regimes; the gains concentrate at the extreme 1%/25% low-data end

Questions for Further Exploration

  • The prior's accuracy advantage appears only at 1%/25% training data and within standard deviations elsewhere (Table 1) — is the low-data gain robust to the choice of seed and to the exact 1% subset sampled, given the large std (±0.059/0.066)?
  • The paper cites scGPT [2], Geneformer [3], scBERT [9] and the Kedzierska et al. zero-shot benchmark [4] but never reports a head-to-head number — how does prior-gated scTransformer compare to these foundation models on the same 17-subtype MSSM split?
  • Hard masks fully block non-CollecTRI edges (−∞); the authors propose soft/learnable priors as future work — would a finite-penalty variant recover novel TF–TG interactions absent from CollecTRI, and at what cost to reproducibility?
  • Cross-run Jaccard is "near zero" and Spearman "consistently negative" for the no-prior model (Fig. 3) — what does this imply about the reliability of feature-importance conclusions drawn from standard unconstrained single-cell Transformers?
  • Evaluation is restricted to annotated TF–TG genes; unannotated genes are dropped. Would a hybrid (prior-gated for annotated, unconstrained for unannotated) preserve interpretability while recovering the lost signal?
