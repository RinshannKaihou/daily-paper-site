Trajectory Geometry of Transformer Representations Across Layers

["Vishal Pandey", "Gopal Singh"]

arXiv: 2606.09287

Date: 2026-06-08

Tags: ["ML", "Interpretability", "Mechanistic Interpretability", "Representational Geometry", "Hidden States", "Transformer"]

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09287

一句话总结： 本文把 Transformer 前向传播重新建模为高维表征流形上的离散"群体轨迹"，定义五个无探针几何度量（长度、曲率、语义收敛指数、层间余弦相似度、稳定性），在 GPT-2/TinyLlama/Qwen2.5 上发现四类一致现象——语义相关 prompt 在中后层显著收敛（峰值 CI 0.41–0.58，p<0.001）、推理任务曲率远大于词形变化（0.71–0.83 rad vs 0.27–0.31 rad）、歧义词呈现可测量的轨迹分叉（最终层 5.6× 分离）、层间相似度揭示"编码-精炼-输出"三阶段结构——且四类效应在随机层序、随机嵌入、随机标签控制下全部消失。
TL;DR: The authors recast the transformer forward pass as a discrete population trajectory through a high-dimensional representation manifold and define five probe-free metrics (trajectory length, curvature, semantic convergence index CI, layerwise cosine similarity SIM, representational stability STAB) computed directly in the full ambient space R^d. Across three model families (GPT-2 Small 117M/12L, TinyLlama-1.1B/22L, Qwen2.5-1.5B/28L) and five semantically controlled prompt families (N=150 prompts), they report four findings: (1) semantically related prompts converge in middle-to-late layers with peak CI 0.41–0.58 (p<0.001, Mann-Whitney U) vs ≈0.02–0.03 under random-label controls; (2) reasoning/analogy prompts (F4) produce higher mean curvature κ̄ than lexical variations (F2): 0.78/0.83/0.71 rad vs 0.31/0.29/0.27 rad (p<0.001, Cohen's d>1.8), dropping to <0.05 rad under random-embedding controls; (3) ambiguous homograph pairs bifurcate with 5.6× (GPT-2)/4.9×/5.1× final-layer separation vs 1.1× for unambiguous controls; (4) a universal three-phase SIM structure (encoding/elaboration/output-preparation) whose boundaries (⌊L/4⌋, ⌊3L/4⌋) are consistent across architectures. All four effects vanish under shuffled-layer and random-embedding controls.

核心贡献

  • 提出针对 Transformer 表征演化的"轨迹几何"解释框架：把每个 prompt 在各层的均值池化隐状态视作高维流形上的离散轨迹，无需训练探针分类器即可刻画其几何形状。
  • 定义五个直接在全维环境空间 R^d 中计算的度量——轨迹长度 L(τ)、平均曲率 κ̄、语义收敛指数 CI(l)=D_between−D_within、层间余弦相似度 SIM(l)、表征稳定性 STAB(l)——并给出严格的统计验证（Mann-Whitney U + Benjamini-Hochberg FDR、95% bootstrap CI、Cohen's d）。
  • 四类跨架构一致的实证发现：语义相关 prompt 在中后层收敛进入"吸引子盆"（CI 峰值 0.41–0.58）；推理任务曲率显著高于词形变化（0.71–0.83 vs 0.27–0.31 rad）；歧义词呈现渐进式轨迹分叉（最终层 5.6× 分离）；层间相似度揭示普适三阶段计算结构。
  • 四个对照实验（随机标签 C1、随机嵌入 C2、打乱层序 C3、多种降维 C4）证明上述效应均源自学习到的计算而非高维几何或投影伪影。
  • 释放完全开源、模型无关的分析管线（github.com/Vishal-sys-code/latent-trajectories），并将计算神经科学的动力系统工具桥接到深度学习可解释性。

Key Contributions

  • Probe-free trajectory-geometric framework. Five metrics computed directly in the full ambient space R^d (no probing classifiers, no fine-tuning, no pre-specified target feature), making the analysis discovery-oriented rather than confirmatory; dimensionality reduction (PCA/UMAP/t-SNE) is used strictly for visualization and plays no role in any reported number.
  • Four cross-architecture empirical findings, each replicated across GPT-2/TinyLlama/Qwen2.5 and validated against controls C1–C4: semantic convergence into attractor basins (peak CI 0.41–0.58), curvature as a probe-free complexity readout (F4 κ̄ 0.71–0.83 rad vs F2 0.27–0.31 rad), disambiguation as trajectory bifurcation (5.6×/4.9×/5.1× final-layer separation), and a universal three-phase computational structure (encoding/elaboration/output-preparation).
  • A "computational inflection zone" localized at ~20–45% of network depth where curvature peaks and similarity drops sharply, which the authors note corresponds to the layer ranges implicated in induction-head formation (Olsson et al. 2022, ref [2]) and MLP-based knowledge retrieval (Geva et al. 2021, ref [12]).
  • Open-source pipeline (github.com/Vishal-sys-code/latent-trajectories) for trajectory analysis of any causal language model, plus a versioned prompt dataset (data/prompts.jsonl, N=150) and full per-model statistics in the appendix.

Methodology

Trajectory extraction (§3.1). For a transformer f_θ with L layers and hidden dim d, hidden states H^(l) ∈ R^{n×d} at layer l are mean-pooled over non-padding tokens to get a sequence-level representation h^(l) ∈ R^d. Layer 0 is the input embedding prior to any transformer block. The trajectory of a prompt is τ = (h^(0), h^(1), …, h^(L)) ∈ R^d. Mean pooling (vs last-token) is chosen for robustness to positional artifacts. All hidden states extracted via HuggingFace Transformers (output_hidden_states=True, torch.no_grad(), greedy decoding), stored as .pt tensors indexed by prompt ID.

Five geometric metrics (§3.2), all in R^d. (1) Trajectory length L(τ)=Σ_{l=0}^{L−1} ‖h^(l+1)−h^(l)‖₂. (2) Local curvature κ^(l)=arccos(v^(l)·v^(l+1)/(‖v^(l)‖‖v^(l+1)‖)) where v^(l)=h^(l)−h^(l−1); mean curvature κ̄=mean over layers. (3) Semantic Convergence Index CI(l)=D_between(l)−D_within(l) (mean pairwise inter-category distance minus mean intra-category distance), computed on L2-normalized layer representations (so CI is on a ~±2 scale); positive CI = semantic compression / attractor-like convergence. (4) Layerwise cosine similarity SIM(l)=h^(l)·h^(l+1)/(‖h^(l)‖‖h^(l+1)‖). (5) Representational stability STAB(l)=cosine between representations of a prompt and its lexical perturbation (e.g., cat vs a cat).

Four controls (§3.3). C1 random category labels (numpy.random.permutation, seed 42) → CI should ≈0; C2 random embeddings (same architecture re-initialized via model.apply(init_weights), seed 0) → no structured geometry; C3 shuffled layer ordering (seed 1) → length/curvature become uninformative; C4 multiple projection methods (PCA, UMAP, t-SNE each with seeds 0/1/2) → visual consistency regardless of reducer.

Statistical validation (§3.4). Two-sided Mann-Whitney U test (robust to non-normality, small samples); effect sizes as Cohen's d on rank-transformed values; 95% bootstrap CIs with B=10,000 resamples; Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction at α=0.05 across multiple comparisons.

Models (§4.1, Table 1). GPT-2 Small (117M, 12 layers, d=768, 12 heads), TinyLlama-1.1B (1.1B, 22 layers, d=2048, 32 heads), Qwen2.5-1.5B (1.5B, 28 layers, d=1536, 12 heads) — decoder-only, ≤1.5B, runnable on a single RTX 3090; API-only and 70B+ models deliberately excluded for full local control.

Prompt dataset (§4.2, Table 2). N=150 prompts (30/family), 5–15 tokens, versioned JSONL. Five families: F1 Semantic Categories (animals/vehicles/emotions, 10 each, for within- vs between-category convergence); F2 Lexical Variations (cat, a cat, the cat, … for stability); F3 Analogical Reasoning (king:queen::man:?, …); F4 Multi-step Reasoning ("A>B, B>C, therefore A compared to C is…", "All mammals breathe air. Dolphins are mammals. Therefore dolphins…"); F5 Ambiguous Concepts (15 homograph pairs in two disambiguating contexts: "river bank" vs "savings bank", "the bat flew" vs "he swung the bat").

Compute. Full pipeline for all 150 prompts across all 3 models < 4 hours on one RTX 3090 (24GB), <12GB disk (Table 3: GPT-2 <1GB VRAM/45min/0.8GB; TinyLlama <8GB/2.5hr/4.2GB; Qwen2.5 <8GB/3hr/3.9GB).

Results & Highlights

  • Finding 1 — Semantic convergence (§5.1, Table 4/5). Peak CI (trained models): GPT-2 0.41 ± 0.03, TinyLlama 0.58 ± 0.04, Qwen2.5 0.53 ± 0.03; under C1 random-label control these collapse to 0.02 ± 0.01 / 0.03 ± 0.01 / 0.02 ± 0.01 (p<0.001). Per-category peaks (Table 5): e.g., TinyLlama Animals 0.61 at layer 18 [0.57,0.65], Vehicles 0.55 at layer 17, Emotions 0.57 at layer 19; GPT-2 Animals 0.43 at layer 9. CI is near-zero in early layers and rises sharply from the midpoint; C3 shuffled-layer control eliminates the monotonic rise.
  • Finding 2 — Curvature encodes complexity (§5.2, Table 4/6). Mean curvature κ̄ for multi-step reasoning (F4) vs lexical variation (F2): GPT-2 0.78 vs 0.31 (d=2.31), TinyLlama 0.83 vs 0.29 (d=2.48), Qwen2.5 0.71 vs 0.27 (d=2.19), all p<0.001. Analogy (F3) sits intermediate at 0.61/0.65/0.58 rad. Trajectory length follows the same rank order F4 > F3 > F1 > F2. Curvature peaks concentrate at layers 2–5 (GPT-2), 5–10 (TinyLlama), 5–9 (Qwen2.5) ≈ 20–45% of depth ("computational inflection zone"); under C2 random embeddings the family curvature difference shrinks to <0.05 rad (p=0.41).
  • Finding 3 — Disambiguation as bifurcation (§5.3, Table 4). For ambiguous homograph pairs (F5), inter-context Euclidean distance rises from δ_0=0.11 ± 0.02 (GPT-2 normalized space) at layer 0 to δ_L=0.67 ± 0.04 at the final layer — a 5.6× increase (GPT-2), 4.9× (TinyLlama), 5.1× (Qwen2.5); bifurcation onset at ~20–25% of depth, consistent across architectures (Spearman ρ=0.81, p<0.001). Matched unambiguous controls show only 1.1× separation (p<0.001 for the interaction contrast); C3 shuffled layers eliminate the monotonic ordering.
  • Finding 4 — Three-phase structure (§5.4, Table 4). Layerwise cosine SIM(l) reveals: Phase I Encoding (l ≤ ⌊L/4⌋, low similarity 0.35–0.55 in GPT-2), Phase II Elaboration (⌊L/4⌋ < l ≤ ⌊3L/4⌋, stabilized 0.70–0.85), Phase III Output Preparation (l > ⌊3L/4⌋, modest secondary drop 0.60–0.70). Boundaries consistent across all three architectures; under C2 random embeddings SIM stays monotonically >0.92 across all layers (phase structure collapses), confirming the transitions are a consequence of trained computation.
  • All four effects survive controls C1–C4 (p<0.001 unless noted), confirming the geometry is intrinsic to learned computation rather than an artifact of high dimensionality, input statistics, or projection.

Critical Assessment

  • The headline "curvature encodes computational complexity" rests on only N=30 prompts per family and is purely correlational. The authors themselves state in the Limitations and Conclusion that "all reported findings are observational: we demonstrate that geometric structure correlates with semantic and computational properties, but do not establish that any geometric property causes downstream model behavior" and that causal claims would require activation patching/representation surgery (citing Meng et al. 2022, ref [11]) that is "beyond the scope of this work." So the strong-sounding claim that curvature "encodes" complexity is, by their own admission, a correlation, not a mechanism.
  • The prompt dataset (N=150, 5–15 tokens) is linguistically narrow and hand-curated. F4 "multi-step reasoning" is exemplified by very short syllogisms ("A>B, B>C, therefore A compared to C is…"; "All mammals breathe air. Dolphins are mammals. Therefore dolphins…"), and F3 analogy is classic word-analogy pairs (king:queen::man:?); the curvature ranking (F4 > F3 > F1 > F2) could therefore partly reflect prompt length/structure confounds rather than "reasoning complexity" per se, and the authors do not run a length-matched control to separate the two.
  • Only three decoder-only models, all ≤1.5B parameters. The "universal" framing (three-phase structure, bifurcation onset ~20–25%) is established on GPT-2 Small (12L), TinyLlama (22L), Qwen2.5 (28L); the authors explicitly flag that specific layer indices "may shift in 70B+ models, encoder-only architectures (BERT, RoBERTa), or encoder-decoder models (T5)," so the universality claim is untested beyond small decoders — three points is thin for a "universal" structure.
  • Mean-pooling over non-padding tokens discards token-level/positional dynamics. The authors acknowledge this: token-level trajectory analysis "may reveal positional and syntactic dynamics invisible to sequence-level aggregation" but is O(n·L·d) and prohibitive; so the current metrics are sequence-averaged and cannot speak to, e.g., how a specific subject token moves, which is central to much mechanistic-interpretability work (induction heads act on specific positions).
  • CI is computed on L2-normalized layer representations, placing it on a roughly ±2 scale that is not directly comparable to the raw-distance bifurcation metric (δ) or the cosine SIM metric — so "peak CI 0.41–0.58" and "5.6× bifurcation" are on different, non-comparable scales, and the paper does not unify them, which complicates cross-finding comparison.

Strengths

  • Genuinely probe-free and ambient-space: all five metrics are computed in the full R^d with no probing classifiers, no labeled target features, and no fine-tuning, which is a clean methodological contrast to the dominant probing/circuit-analysis paradigms.
  • Strong controls: four controls (random labels, random embeddings, shuffled layers, multiple projections) each directly target a specific confound, and all four findings collapse under them — e.g., curvature difference <0.05 rad under C2, three-phase structure collapses to monotone >0.92 SIM under C2 — which is more rigorous than many interpretability papers that report correlations without null controls.
  • Cross-architecture replication on three models of different families/scales/depths (12/22/28 layers) strengthens the "universal structure" claim within the small-decoder regime.
  • Fully reproducible and cheap: single RTX 3090, <4 hours, open code + versioned prompts, making it easy for others to extend.

Limitations

  • Findings are observational/correlational, not causal (no activation patching); the "curvature encodes complexity" wording overstates what is shown.
  • N=150 short prompts; no length/structure-matched control to isolate "reasoning" from "longer/different prompt."
  • Only three decoder-only models ≤1.5B; universality untested for 70B+, encoder-only, encoder-decoder.
  • Mean-pooling discards token-level/positional dynamics central to induction-head analysis.
  • CI (L2-normalized, ±2 scale), bifurcation ratio (×), and SIM (cosine) metrics are on non-comparable scales, complicating cross-finding synthesis.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • The authors themselves flag that all findings are observational and that causal validation needs activation patching at specific trajectory phases (citing Meng et al. 2022 ROME, ref [11]) — would patching/steering representations near the bifurcation onset (~20–25% depth) before commitment actually redirect the model's interpretation of an ambiguous F5 prompt, which would convert the bifurcation correlation into a causal claim?
  • The curvature ranking F4 > F3 > F1 > F2 is established on N=30 hand-curated short prompts with no length-matched control — is the higher F4 curvature driven by reasoning complexity or by prompt length/structure, since the F4 examples ("A>B, B>C…") are longer and syntactically different from the single-word F2 ("cat, a cat")?
  • The three-phase structure (⌊L/4⌋, ⌊3L/4⌋ boundaries) is shown only on GPT-2 (12L), TinyLlama (22L), Qwen2.5 (28L) — does the same proportional boundary hold for a 70B+ model or for encoder-only (BERT) and encoder-decoder (T5) architectures, given the authors explicitly defer this and it is the basis of the "universal" claim?
  • The paper cites Olsson et al. 2022 (induction heads, ref [2]) and Geva et al. 2021 (MLP key-value retrieval, ref [12]) as the mechanistic correlate of the ~20–45% "computational inflection zone" — does the inflection-zone layer range quantitatively overlap with the empirically localized induction-head formation layers in those works, or is the correspondence only approximate?
  • Mean-pooling over non-padding tokens discards per-token dynamics; the authors note token-level analysis is O(n·L·d) prohibitive — would tracking just the final-content-token trajectory (as in the logit lens, nostalgebraist 2020 ref [17]) recover the same convergence/bifurcation findings at the token level, or are these sequence-averaged effects?
