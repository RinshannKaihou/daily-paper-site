> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示了现成生物医学编码器（BioBERT/PubMedBERT/BioM-ELECTRA）将不相关跨域概念误判为高度相似（余弦 0.76–0.92、跨域判别准确率 0%）的各向异性失效，并提出两阶段对比微调——其中 BODHI 借助生物医学知识图谱的"缺失边"挖掘硬负样本——将域内外分离比从 1.05× 提升到 2.30×、F1 达 93.0%；在 Intel Xeon 6737P（AMX）上经 OpenVINO 将单查询延迟从 1367 ms 降到 10 ms（133×），并发现 FP16 在该硬件上全面优于 INT8。

> **TL;DR:** Off-the-shelf biomedical encoders (BioBERT, PubMedBERT, BioM-ELECTRA) assign 0.76–0.92 cosine similarity to genuinely unrelated cross-domain pairs (e.g., "cortisol 28 µg/dL" vs "stock-market volatility" = 0.83), giving 0% accuracy on a six-part cross-domain discrimination diagnostic. A two-pass contrastive fix is proposed: Pass 1 over 72,034 triplets raises PubMedBERT's BIOSSES ρ from 0.633 to 0.828 and intra/inter-domain separation from 1.05× to 1.63×; the BODHI second pass mines hard negatives from absent edges in the BODHI biomedical knowledge graph, pushing separation to 2.30× and best-link F1 to 93.0% (vs 72.7% pretrained) at the cost of 4.5% BIOSSES. On an Intel Xeon 6737P with AMX, OpenVINO cuts single-query latency from 1,367 ms to 10 ms (133×) at 555 sentences/sec; counter to standard advice, FP16 beats INT8 on this silicon at every batch size up to 256.

## 核心贡献
- 量化揭示现成生物医学编码器的**跨域各向异性失效**：对完全不相关跨域对给出 0.76–0.92 余弦相似度，6 项诊断中跨域判别准确率恰好为 0%。
- 提出**两阶段对比微调**：Pass 1 在 72,034 个三元组上训练，将 PubMedBERT 的 BIOSSES 相关性从 0.633 提到 0.828、分离比从 1.05× 提到 1.63×；Pass 2（BODHI）利用生物医学知识图谱中"无边的概念对"作为硬负样本，将分离比提到 2.30×、判别间隙 +0.392、最优阈值 F1 达 93.0%（预训练模型仅 72.7%）。
- 给出面向部署的**AMX/OpenVINO 加速方案**：单查询延迟从 1,367 ms 降到 10 ms（133×），PubMedBERT Pass 1 吞吐达 617.8 sentences/sec（batch 256）。
- 报告**反直觉的量化结论**：在 AMX 硅片上 FP16 在所有 batch size（≤256）上均快于 INT8，并给出机理解释（INT8 需在标量单元去量化以馈送残差加法，而 BF16 权重从 FP16 在线 cast 是免费的）。

## Key Contributions
- Quantifies a severe cross-domain anisotropy failure in off-the-shelf biomedical encoders: every model tested (BioBERT, PubMedBERT, BioM-ELECTRA) scores 0.76–0.92 cosine on unrelated cross-domain pairs, yielding 0% accuracy on the cross-domain discrimination diagnostic B2.
- A two-pass contrastive fix: Pass 1 (72,034 triplets, Matryoshka-wrapped Multiple Negatives Ranking Loss) raises PubMedBERT BIOSSES ρ from 0.633 to 0.828 and geometry ratio from 1.05× to 1.63×; BODHI (Pass 2) mines hard negatives from absent edges in a biomedical knowledge graph, reaching 2.30× separation, +0.392 discrimination gap, and 93.0% best-link F1 (vs 72.7% for pretrained).
- An AMX/OpenVINO deployment recipe giving a 133× single-query latency cut (1,367 ms → 10 ms) and up to 617.8 sentences/sec, with a 32srv+HT/600c serving configuration delivering ~108k tokens/sec at 80.7 ms p95.
- A counter-intuitive precision finding: FP16 beats INT8 on Granite Rapids AMX at every batch size up to 256, with a silicon-specific mechanistic explanation, plus evidence that PyTorch dynamic INT8 (cosine fidelity 0.68–0.94) and OpenVINO INT4 (0.90) destroy embedding fidelity.

## Methodology
**Problem framing.** The paper targets embeddings for a Large Behavioural Model (LBM) — a foundation model whose subject is a person, not a sentence. The LBM keeps a graph of a user's life (nodes = events such as lab results, journal entries, wearable readings; edges = causal/precedence/recurrence relations) and grows the graph by walking it, treating embedding proximity as evidence for hypothesising new edges. If the embedding space places unrelated things close, the model writes false causal edges that compound; if it places linked things far apart, the graph develops holes. So embedding geometry is correctness, not a tuning knob.

**Models (§3.1).** Three biomedical encoders: BioBERT v1.1 (dmislab, BERT-Base, PubMed+PMC MLM+NSP, 768-d), PubMedBERT (Microsoft, BERT-Base, PubMed from-scratch domain MLM, 768-d), and BioM-ELECTRA-Large (sultan, 1024-d, replaced-token detection, SQuAD2 fine-tune) kept as an informative contrast case.

**Six diagnostics (§3.2, the B1–B6 suite):** B1 within-domain cosine, B2 cross-domain cosine (the one that matters), B3 BIOSSES rank correlation, B4 throughput/latency, B5 hard-negative triplet accuracy, B6 geometry ratio (inter/intra-domain distance).

**Hardware (§3.3).** Primary: Intel Xeon 6737P (Granite Rapids, 4th-gen), 64 physical / 128 logical cores, 288 MiB L3, 1 TB DDR5-6400, native AMX BF16 tiles, NUMA-pinned (numactl, KMP_AFFINITY=compact, OMP_NUM_THREADS=128, INFERENCE_PRECISION_HINT=bf16). Reference: AWS c6i.8xlarge (Ice Lake, 3rd-gen), 16/32 cores, no AMX, AVX-512 VNNI INT8 only; the BF16 hint is silently ignored (runs as FP32). Intel India provided AMX access under NDA.

**Pass 1 (§6.1):** fine-tune on 72,034 (anchor, positive, hard-negative) triplets from eight public datasets (all-nli, BIOSSES, medical_questions_pairs, MedNLI/MIMIC-III, PubMedQA, emotion, go_emotions, mental_health_counseling), plus synthetic cross-domain hard negatives. Loss = MatryoshkaLoss wrapping Multiple Negatives Ranking Loss; batch 128 (127 in-batch negatives); nested dims [768, 512, 256, 128, 64]; LR 2e-5 (BERT) / 5e-6 (ELECTRA); 3 epochs; BF16 autocast on AMX.

**BODHI Pass 2 (§6.2):** from the Pass-1 PubMedBERT checkpoint, train 2 more epochs on ~3,500 ontology triplets derived from the BODHI biomedical knowledge graph: explicit edges (PRESENT_IN, CHILD_OF, IMPACTS) become positives; concept pairs both present in the graph but with no path of length ≤2 become hard negatives. The symmetry is intentional — training on absent edges in one graph so the embedding can hypothesise absent edges in the user's personal graph.

**Deployment (§8).** Export via `optimum-cli export openvino --task feature-extraction --weight-format fp16 --group-size 64 --ratio 1.0`. Recommended serving config: 32 OpenVINO servers pinned to 2 physical + 2 hyperthread cores each (fills 128 logical CPUs), batch 256 with a 10 ms dynamic-batching window.

## Results & Highlights
- **Cross-domain failure is total:** on diagnostic B2, every pretrained model scores 0% accuracy, with unrelated cross-domain cosine averaging 0.756 (BioBERT), 0.910 (PubMedBERT), 0.917 (ELECTRA) against a target below 0.35; the "cortisol vs stock market" pair lands at 0.830–0.932.
- **BIOSSES rank correlation (B3):** PubMedBERT improves from 0.633 (pretrained) to 0.828 (Pass 1), crossing the 0.80 strong-clinician-agreement line; ELECTRA only reaches 0.447 after fine-tuning and "stays unusable."
- **Geometry ratio (B6):** rises from 1.05× (collapsed pretrained) to 1.63× (Pass 1) to 2.30× (BODHI); the Pass-1→BODHI step alone is +41% separation, achieved by suppressing inter-domain cosine from 0.533 to 0.341 while barely moving within-domain numbers.
- **Best-link F1:** pretrained models cap at 72.7% (no threshold works on a collapsed space); Pass 1 reaches 89.7% at τ=0.50; BODHI reaches 93.0% at τ=0.40 — a 20.3 pp gain over pretrained at the deployment threshold.
- **Latency/throughput:** OpenVINO FP16 on AMX cuts single-query latency from 1,367 ms to 10 ms (133× for BioBERT; 122× PubMedBERT; 107× ELECTRA); PubMedBERT throughput tops out at 617.8 sentences/sec (batch 256); cosine fidelity moves by <0.0005 vs PyTorch FP32.
- **AMX gap:** the no-AMX Ice Lake c6i is 13–27× slower at batch 256; AVX-512 VNNI INT8 on c6i is 3–4× faster than FP32 there but keeps only 17–50% of the discrimination gap (BioBERT 17%, PubMedBERT Pass 1 50%, BODHI 36%).
- **Precision fidelity:** FP16 and BF16 are lossless; OpenVINO INT8 holds 0.995; PyTorch dynamic INT8 collapses to 0.68–0.94 and OpenVINO INT4 to 0.90 — both reproduced as failures on both machines.

## Critical Assessment
- **Single biomedical KG underpins BODHI's negatives, but its coverage is undisclosed.** The Pass-2 hard negatives depend entirely on the BODHI knowledge graph's edges, yet the paper never reports the graph's size (nodes, edge types, density) or how many of the ~3,500 triplets come from each relation. A sparse or biased ontology would silently cap the discrimination gains, and the +0.392 gap cannot be interpreted without it.
- **BODHI trades 4.5% BIOSSES for separation, and the win is asserted not stress-tested.** The drop from 0.828 to ~0.783 (4.5%) on BIOSSES is acknowledged, but there is no downstream LBM evaluation showing that 2.30× separation actually yields fewer false causal edges in a real user graph than 1.63× — the central claim is validated only on intrinsic metrics (cosine, F1), not on the LBM task it was built for.
- **ELECTRA is included only to lose, which inflates the "all encoders fail" headline.** ELECTRA is a replaced-token-detection model fine-tuned for SQuAD2 span QA; using it as an embedder is a straw man, and its post-fine-tune gap of +0.066 (an order of magnitude below BERT) is presented as evidence the architecture is wrong rather than as evidence of an unfair setup.
- **The 133× headline is single-query latency only.** The authors flag this honestly ("Other regimes give other multipliers"), but the abstract's "133×" could mislead readers into expecting 133× bulk-throughput gains; the reported throughput multipliers are smaller, and the no-AMX baseline is a single Ice Lake SKU — AMD/ARM/Apple silicon are explicitly unmeasured.

## Strengths
- Pins down a concrete, reproducible failure (0% cross-domain accuracy, 0.76–0.92 cosine floor) with named models and a 10-pair test anyone can rerun.
- The BODHI "absent-edge-as-hard-negative" idea is a genuinely transferable recipe for any domain with a curated ontology (HPO, DSM-5, UMLS, Gene Ontology).
- Honest, mechanism-level explanation of why FP16 beats INT8 on AMX (BF16 cast is free; INT8 dequantisation runs on scalar units), with the crossover at ~batch 512.
- Releases the full stack — benchmark, corpora, generator, training pipeline, OpenVINO scripts, serving template, and weights — under a permissive licence.

## Limitations
- Benchmark is biomedical-heavy; environmental, social, occupational, and wearable-signal domains are explicitly "thin" and still being widened.
- BODHI today covers only biomedical concepts; extending the absent-edge trick to behavioural/environmental ontologies needs graphs that do not all exist yet.
- Cross-platform comparison uses a single no-AMX baseline (c6i.8xlarge); no AMD, ARM, or Apple silicon measured.
- No longitudinal user study validating the LBM end-to-end — the paper explicitly disclaims that the LBM is a validated clinical/behavioural intervention.

## Relevance
本文与"**Computation Reliability & Precision Errors**"高度相关：它系统地比较了 FP16/BF16/INT8/INT4 在不同硬件（AMX vs 无 AMX 的 Ice Lake）上的嵌入保真度与速度，并量化了 INT8 量化对判别信号的破坏（仅保留 17–50% 的判别间隙），这正是数值精度误差如何侵蚀模型有效性的典型案例。同时，关于嵌入各向异性导致语义相似度系统性偏差的诊断（B1–B6 基准）与"**LLM Hidden States & Interpretability**"和"**ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations**"中表征几何的研究直接相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- The BODHI Pass-2 negatives come from a single biomedical KG whose node/edge counts are unreported — how does the +0.392 discrimination gap degrade as the ontology becomes sparser or noisier, and is there a graph-density threshold below which the absent-edge trick stops helping?
- The central claim (2.30× separation → fewer false LBM causal edges) is validated only on intrinsic cosine/F1 metrics; would a downstream LBM-graph evaluation (e.g., false-edge rate on a held-out user timeline) confirm that BODHI beats Pass 1 in practice?
- The FP16>INT8 result holds up to batch ~512 on BERT-base; the paper cites Matryoshka [11] and SimCSE [8] but does not test whether the dequantisation crossover shifts for BERT-large or decoder-only embedders — does the FP16 advice generalise beyond BERT-base on AMX?
- INT8 on c6i keeps only 17–50% of the discrimination gap; the authors note that quantising only feed-forward layers (a "common early mistake") yields no speedup. Is there a mixed-precision scheme (e.g., BF16 attention + INT8 FFN) that recovers VNNI speed without collapsing the gap on Ice Lake?
