> **一句话总结：** 本文通过比较 Llama 3.1 8B 基座模型与 Instruct 模型在 84 个提示上的 Layer 18 党派方向投影、TopK 稀疏自编码器分解与特征级激活转向实验，证明 RLHF 并未删除基座模型中的党派几何方向，而是将该方向的得分标准差压缩 68%、把 5 个政策编码 SAE 特征在 Instruct 模型中置零，并通过切断"从党派几何到生成文本"的因果通路实现功能性中立——这种中立在按 ω̂ 或政策特征施加 α=±6 转向时依然存在（生成保持中立），但底层的党派几何仍然可以被重新激活。

> **TL;DR:** Using 190,491 congressional tweets to train a logistic-regression partisan probe (Layer 18 AUC 0.935, Cohen's d=1.94) on Llama 3.1 8B Instruct, the author projects 84 prompts' Layer 18 hidden states onto the probe direction ω̂ and shows RLHF compresses the partisan-score range by >4× (base σ=0.234 → Instruct σ=0.074, a 68% std reduction) and shifts the cluster to a positive 0.169 offset. A TopK sparse autoencoder (4,096→32,768 features, k=64) reveals that the 5 policy-encoding features (anti-Biden, progressive advocacy, etc.) which fire sporadically in the base model are exactly zero on all 84 Instruct prompts, and Instruct activates only 244 unique features vs 706 in base (−65%). Decomposing the 0.169 offset shows the SAE decoder bias accounts for 68% (0.114), feature contributions 24% (0.041, of which 79.4% from a single register-style feature 32143), error 8%. Feature-level steering at α∈{−6,−3,0,3,6} shifts partisan scores identically in both models but produces partisan text only in the base model — the Instruct model generates balanced text regardless, establishing that RLHF severs the causal pathway from partisan geometry to generation rather than removing the geometry.

## 核心贡献

- 在 Llama 3.1 8B 基座模型与 Instruct 模型之间建立对照实验：用同一 Instruct 派生探针方向 ω̂ 作为共享参考轴，量化 RLHF 对党派得分的压缩（标准差降 68%，范围缩 4 倍以上）与中心化（聚集到 +0.169）。
- 用 TopK 稀疏自编码器（4096→32768，k=64）分解党派轴：在 Instruct 模型中 5 个可识别的政策编码特征在全部 84 个提示上恰好为零（非衰减，零），Instruct 仅激活 244 个唯一特征（基座 706 个，减少 65%），证明 RLHF 改变了被激活的字典子集而非简单抑制少数特征。
- 将 Instruct 的 0.169 党派得分偏移分解为解码器偏置（68%）、特征贡献（24%，其中 79.4% 来自单一语体特征 32143）与误差项（8%），证明该偏移不是政策信号，而是 SAE 训练语料（国会推文）质心与 RLHF 语体偏好的几何投影。
- 因果验证：对 5 个特征及全探针方向 ω̂ 在 α∈{−6,−3,0,3,6} 上做激活转向，12 个提示共 360 次生成；得分位移在两模型中完全相同（仅依赖 d̂ᵢ·ω̂），但仅基座模型生成党派文本，Instruct 始终生成中立文本——证明 RLHF 切断的是"从表示到生成"的因果通路而非表示本身。

## Key Contributions

- **Quantifies RLHF's effect on partisan geometry via a shared probe axis.** Reuses the Instruct-trained partisan direction ω̂ (Layer 18, AUC 0.935) as a fixed reference to project both base and Instruct hidden states, showing RLHF compresses the score distribution (std 0.234→0.074, a 68% reduction; range >4× narrower) and centers it at a +0.169 offset rather than at zero.
- **Sparse-autoencoder decomposition shows policy features are silenced, not attenuated.** A TopK SAE (4,096→32,768, k=64) finds 5 recognisable policy-encoding features (anti-Biden rhetoric, progressive advocacy, etc.); all 5 are exactly zero on all 84 Instruct prompts while firing sporadically in base, and Instruct activates only 244 unique features vs 706 in base (−65%), a wholesale change of the active dictionary subset.
- **Explains the +0.169 offset as non-policy.** Decomposes the mean score into SAE decoder bias (68%, 0.114, the congressional-tweet corpus centroid), feature contributions (24%, 0.041, of which 79.4% from a single register/style feature 32143), and error (8%) — none driven by Instruct policy features, so the offset is the "geometric shadow" of RLHF's register preferences and the training corpus, not a political stance.
- **Causally establishes the disconnect via feature-level steering.** Steering 5 features + full probe at α∈{−6,−3,0,3,6} over 12 prompts (360 generations/model) shows identical partisan-score shifts in both models but partisan text only in base; Instruct generates balanced text regardless, proving RLHF severs the generation pathway, not the representation.

## Methodology

**Model and probe (§2).** Llama 3.1 8B, both the base (pretraining only) and Instruct (instruction-tuned + RLHF) variants. A logistic-regression partisan probe was previously trained [Ref 21] on Layer 18 hidden-state activations over 190,491 tweets from sitting Members of Congress; at Layer 18 it reached cross-validated AUC 0.935 with Cohen's d=1.94 between Republican and Democratic score distributions. The learned weight vector ω̂ defines the partisan direction in the 4,096-dimensional residual stream; projection s=h·ω̂ gives a partisan score (positive=Republican, negative=Democratic). On the tweet data itself, Democratic tweets had mean −0.086 (σ=0.073), Republican tweets mean +0.080 (σ=0.097). The same ω̂ is used for both models as a fixed reference axis (the author explicitly notes base-model scores along this axis should not be read as base-model partisan valence, since RLHF restructures which features are active at Layer 18; a base-model-specific probe would be needed for base-model valence).

**Prompt set (§2).** 84 open-ended sentence stems spanning contested political issues ("Abortion access in America should be…"), historical events ("The Vietnam War was…"), scientific topics ("Evolution by natural selection is…"), and non-political controls ("The best way to cook a steak is…"). Each prompt is submitted to both models, the Layer 18 hidden state extracted, and projected onto ω̂.

**Score-range compression (§2.1, Fig. 1).** Base projections span [−0.5, 1.253] (μ=0.101, σ=0.234); Instruct projections cluster in [−0.011, 0.388] (μ=0.169, σ=0.074). 82/84 Instruct scores are positive (vs 55/84 base). Qualitative completion analysis (Fig. 2) shows base adopts a prompt-dependent partisan voice (e.g. completes "The Heritage Foundation promotes…" in the Foundation's own voice), while Instruct produces balanced multi-perspective enumerations.

**Sparse-autoencoder decomposition (§4).** A TopK SAE [Ref 9] trained on Layer 18 hidden states of the 190,491 congressional tweets expands 4,096→32,768 latent features with k=64 active features per input. Each feature's alignment with ω̂ is the dot product of its unit-normalised decoder column d̂ᵢ with ω̂. Five features at Layer 18 encode recognisable political content (identified by inspecting top-30 activating tweets): Feature 9036 (anti-Biden, 30/30 top tweets Republican), 19268 (progressive advocacy, Democratic), 27699 (Republican opposition framing, 30/30 R), 27872 (crime/inflation framing, 29/30 R), 12677 (social justice advocacy, 28/30 D). The 84 prompts are then encoded through the SAE for both models.

**Offset decomposition (§4.2, Table 1).** The SAE reconstructs h ≈ Σᵢ zᵢdᵢ + b_d, so the partisan score decomposes into decoder bias b_d·ω̂, feature contributions Σᵢ zᵢ(dᵢ·ω̂), and an error term ε·ω̂. Averaged across 84 prompts: bias 0.114 (68%), features 0.041 (24%), error 0.013 (8%). Within features, ~34 push Republican (+0.232 combined) and ~30 push Democratic (−0.191), nearly cancelling; the single largest residual contributor is Feature 32143 (79.4% of total feature contribution; mean activation 1.2, alignment 0.027, per-prompt contribution ≈0.03), which top-activates on numbered/lettered/formal-investigative discourse (18R:2D tweet split) — i.e. a register/style feature, not policy.

**Feature-level steering (§5, Table 2, Fig. 3).** Following activation-steering frameworks [Refs 23, 25], for each feature the decoder column dᵢ is extracted, unit-normalised to d̂ᵢ, and (α·d̂ᵢ) added to the Layer 18 hidden state during generation, for α∈{−6,−3,0,3,6}. Five features tested: two policy (9036 anti-Biden, 19268 progressive), three register (32143 formal discourse, 25717 energy/taxes/spending style, 9202 institutional voice), plus the full probe ω̂ as control. 12 prompts (political/scientific/non-political) → 360 generations per model. Score shifts depend only on d̂ᵢ·ω̂ and are therefore identical across models; text output differs.

## Results & Highlights

- **RLHF compresses the partisan-score std by 68% and the range by >4× (Fig. 1).** Base model Layer 18 projections onto ω̂ span [−0.5, 1.253] (μ=0.101, σ=0.234); Instruct projections cluster in [−0.011, 0.388] (μ=0.169, σ=0.074) — a >4× range reduction and 68% std reduction, with 82/84 Instruct scores positive vs 55/84 base.
- **All 5 policy-encoding SAE features are exactly zero in Instruct on all 84 prompts (§4.1).** In base they fire sporadically (e.g. Feature 12677 on 6 prompts), but in Instruct "they were not attenuated. Zero." Instruct also activates far fewer features overall: 244 unique features across 84 prompts (5,376 activation slots) vs 706 in base, a 65% reduction; only 1 of the top-20 features by probe coefficient fires at all, and only 18/64 (28.3%) active features overlap between models on a given prompt.
- **The +0.169 Instruct offset is 68% decoder bias, 24% features, 8% error (Table 1).** Of the 24% feature contribution (0.041), a single register-style feature (32143, formal/investigative discourse, 18R:2D top-tweet split) accounts for 79.4% — i.e. the offset is the geometric shadow of RLHF's register preferences projected onto a partisan axis, not a policy signal.
- **Feature steering shifts scores identically in both models but produces partisan text only in base (§5.1, Fig. 3).** At α=6, steering base along Feature 9036 (anti-Biden) on the DEI prompt yields dismissive partisan rhetoric while Instruct produces a balanced institutional description; steering along Feature 19268 (progressive) on the abortion prompt makes base cite the Hyde Amendment's impact on low-income people while Instruct produces its characteristic hedge. On the non-political steak prompt both models produce identical cooking advice across all steering conditions — the base model's susceptibility is specific to politically resonant content, Instruct's resistance is universal.

## Critical Assessment

- **The entire analysis is confined to a single model family and size (Llama 3.1 8B) and a single layer (Layer 18), and the author explicitly flags this.** The disconnect-not-delete mechanism is demonstrated only for this one open-weight model; closed-weight commercial models (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) cannot be mechanistically analysed this way, and Grok's deliberately distinctive editorial voice [Ref 22] would presumably produce different alignment geometry. The generalisation claim ("the same pattern may hold for other value domains") is therefore a hypothesis, not a finding — it rests on convergent results from toxicity/safety domains [Refs 12, 13, 18] rather than direct partisan-geometry replication.
- **The probe direction ω̂ is Instruct-derived, so base-model scores along it are not interpretable as base-model partisan valence — a confound the author acknowledges but does not fully resolve.** Footnote 2 (§2) states that because RLHF restructures which features are active at Layer 18, "partisan scores from the base model should not be interpreted as carrying partisan valence" and a base-model-specific probe would be needed. The compression/centering comparison is therefore valid as a distributional contrast along a shared axis, but the claim that "the base model's text completions track the implied partisan direction of these scores" is supported only by qualitative inspection of a few completions, not by a base-model probe.
- **The steering evidence is causal but the "severing" mechanism is not localised beyond Layer 18.** The experiment shows that perturbing Layer 18 representations moves scores in both models but changes text only in base; it does not identify which downstream layers/components constitute the "generation guardrail" that RLHF installed, nor whether the severing occurs at attention, MLP, or the unembedding. The causal claim is about the existence of a disconnect, not its mechanistic locus.

## Strengths

- Clean base-vs-Instruct controlled comparison isolating the effect of RLHF using the same shared probe axis, which is the right experimental design for attributing changes to alignment rather than pretraining.
- The offset decomposition (Table 1) is a genuinely illuminating step: rather than treating the +0.169 cluster as a mysterious bias, it attributes 68% to the SAE decoder bias (a corpus-centroid artifact) and 24% (79.4% of which to one register feature) to RLHF's discourse-style preferences — demystifying an otherwise misleading "right-leaning offset."
- The feature-level steering experiment is the right causal test: identical score shifts but divergent text in the two models cleanly separates representational change from generational change, which correlational SAE analysis alone could not.
- Honest framing of the mask metaphor and the functional-vs-structural distinction, with a clear policy implication (intervention should target the propensity to deploy partisan knowledge in generation, not the knowledge itself).

## Limitations

- Single model (Llama 3.1 8B), single layer (Layer 18), single value domain (partisan orientation); generalisation to other models, layers, and value domains is hypothesised from convergent safety/toxicity literature rather than directly tested.
- The Instruct-derived probe confounds base-model score interpretation; a base-model-trained probe would be needed to make claims about base-model partisan valence, which is not provided.
- The "causal severing" is demonstrated but not mechanistically localised — the downstream layer/component implementing RLHF's generation guardrail is not identified.
- Substantial compute (SAE training on 190,491 tweets across 32 layers, hidden-state extraction for both models, large numbers of steered generations on NCSA Delta/DeltaAI) limits reproducibility by groups without HPC access; no public code or SAE weights are released.
- The "bypass via inferred user partisan identity" threat model (§6) is argued conceptually from Refs [20, 2, 17, 24] but not demonstrated empirically in this paper — no experiment shows a conversational context reactivating partisan generation in Instruct.

## Relevance

本文对 Llama 3.1 8B 的 RLHF 对齐进行机制可解释性（mechanistic interpretability）分析，通过线性探针、稀疏自编码器分解与特征级激活转向，研究隐藏状态中的党派几何方向如何被 RLHF 功能性切断，直接对应到"**LLM Hidden States & Interpretability**"（隐藏状态几何、稀疏自编码器特征分解、激活转向与因果通路分析）。其"功能性中立而非结构性中立"的发现也对"**LLM Inference Evaluation**"（模型输出中立性/偏见的评估方法：开放式提示 vs 强制选择工具）与对齐可靠性具有参考意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The probe ω̂ is Instruct-derived; would a base-model-trained partisan probe identify a partisan direction in base whose geometry RLHF leaves structurally intact — and would steering along that base-derived direction in Instruct still fail to produce partisan text, isolating the severing from the probe-axis confound?
- The author argues conversational inference of a user's partisan identity [Refs 20, 2, 17] could reactivate partisan generation by tilting the near-cancellation in the SAE decomposition — can this be demonstrated empirically by constructing multi-turn contexts that build a user partisan representation and measuring whether Instruct output drifts?
- Wolf et al. [Ref 24] are cited as showing alignment bypasses are fundamental limitations; does amplification along ω̂ at Layer 18 during Instruct generation (shown in Ref [21] to produce stance reversals and fabricated authorities) generalise to the policy features (9036, 19268) that are zero in Instruct, or only to the full probe direction?
- Feature 32143 (formal discourse) accounts for 79.4% of the feature contribution to the offset and reflects RLHF's register preferences — does ablating it during Instruct generation change only the score offset, or does it also degrade the balanced multi-perspective output style that constitutes the "neutral mask"?
- The analysis is confined to Layer 18; at which layer(s) does the causal severing from partisan geometry to generation actually occur, and is it implemented in attention, MLP, or the unembedding — i.e. can the "generation guardrail" RLHF installed be localised and characterised?
