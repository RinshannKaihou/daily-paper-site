From Rigid to Dynamic: Entropy-Guided Adaptive Inference for Long-Context LLMs

["Zhanchao Xu", "Haoyang Li", "Qingfa Xiao", "Fei Teng", "Chen Jason Zhang", "Lei Chen", "Qing Li"]

arXiv: 2606.09508

Date: 2026-06-08

Tags: ["NLP", "AI", "LLM", "Long-Context", "KV-Cache", "Sparse-Attention", "Inference"]

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09508

一句话总结： EntropyInfer 是一个无需训练的长上下文 LLM 推理加速框架，利用在线注意力熵将头分为 Rigid/Dynamic 两类并按头-段粒度自适应分配预算，配合基于输出 token 重排的潜在 KV 缓存压缩，在 100k+ token 下实现最高 2.39× 端到端加速且质量近乎无损。
TL;DR: EntropyInfer, a training-free framework that classifies attention heads online into Rigid (entropy < 1e−5) vs Dynamic via per-head-per-segment entropy to adaptively allocate prefill budgets, plus latent KV-cache compression that defers eviction until N_D output tokens are generated to re-rank cache entries, achieves up to 2.39× end-to-end speedup at >100k tokens on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct with minimal quality loss; on LongBench it averages 48.91 vs full-attention base 48.94 (Llama) and beats SnapKV/AdaKV/CritiPrefill on both LongBench and InfiniteBench.

核心贡献

  • 识别出注意力头的两种熵态——Rigid Heads（行熵恒低于 1e−5）与 Dynamic Heads（熵剧烈波动），并证明同一头在不同输入下归属不同态，因此必须在线判定，无法离线固化
  • 提出 EntropyInfer：用在线熵信号在"头×段"粒度自适应分配稀疏预填充预算——Rigid 头给固定最小预算，Dynamic 头按相邻段熵波动率缩放预算
  • 提出潜在 KV 缓存压缩：将压缩推迟到生成 N_D 个输出 token 之后，把输出 token 纳入观察窗口重排缓存条目，纠正"预填充期注意力模式 ≠ 解码期"的失配
  • 在 Llama-3.1-8B/Qwen-2.5-7B/openPangu-1B/7B 上验证，100k+ token 下端到端最高 2.39× 加速，质量近乎无损，无需微调

Key Contributions

  • Identification of two attention-head regimes (Rigid vs Dynamic) by segment-wise row entropy, with empirical proof that head assignment is context-dependent and therefore cannot be captured by offline profiling (e.g., RazorAttention, Duo-Attention)
  • EntropyInfer: a training-free sparse-prefill scheme that estimates per-head, per-segment entropy from a cheap "observation attention matrix" and allocates Rigid heads a fixed minimal budget while Dynamic heads get a budget scaled by inter-segment entropy fluctuation
  • Latent KV-cache compression: defers eviction past the prefill-decode boundary until N_D output tokens are generated, then includes those output tokens in the observation window to re-rank cache entries — correcting the prefill-to-decoding attention-pattern shift reported by LoopServe/LouisKV
  • Drop-in attention replacement requiring no fine-tuning, validated on 4 model families (Llama, Qwen, openPangu-1B/7B) achieving up to 2.39× end-to-end speedup at >100k tokens

Methodology

Motivation. Prefilling attention cost grows quadratically with context length and dominates first-token latency; KV cache grows linearly during decoding. Existing methods assume homogeneity across positions (MInference fixed templates), heads (FlexPrefill single threshold), or contexts (CritiPrefill uniform per-segment k). Per-head entropy analysis on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct and Qwen-2.5-7B-Instruct shows two regimes: Rigid heads (max segment entropy ≤ 1e−5) and Dynamic heads (entropy fluctuates); the same head switches regime across inputs.

Observation attention matrix. To avoid O(N²) entropy computation, follow CritiPrefill's blocking: segment Q and K into blocks (size L_S), build representative vectors Q_Rep={Q.max, Q.min} per segment (Algorithm 1), then compute a small observation attention A_obs from the 4 softmax products of {Qmax,Qmin}×{Kmax,Kmin} (Algorithm 2). Row entropy H(a_i:)=−Σ_j a_i,j log a_i,j is computed on A_obs.

Entropy-based budget allocation (Algorithm 3). Threshold e_t=1e−5. If max entropy < e_t → Rigid head: every segment gets base budget B_h. Else Dynamic head: B_0=B_h; for each subsequent segment, Δbudget = α·B_h·(|e_i−e_{i−1}|/e_{i−1}) − Δt, with α=0.5, Δt=0.4, clipped to [⅓B_h, 3B_h]. Higher entropy fluctuation → more budget.

Latent KV-cache compression (Algorithm 4). After generating N_D output tokens, build an observation window Q_W from the last N_W positions (input tail + generated tokens), compute importance w = Σ Q_W K^T, keep top-B_d tokens. This contrasts with SnapKV/AdaKV which commit eviction at end-of-prefill using only input-tail attention.

Setup. Backbones: Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Qwen-2.5-7B-Instruct, plus openPangu-Embedded-1B/7B-v1.1. Hardware: single NVIDIA H100 80GB, 192GB CPU. Benchmarks: LongBench (16 tasks), InfiniteBench (>100k tokens), LoCoMo (multi-turn). Baselines: SnapKV (KV budget 1024), AdaKV (1024), CritiPrefill (segment budget 2048), StreamingLLM (4096+4 sink). EntropyInfer: prefill base budget 2048, decode cache budget 1024. Complexity shown near-linear in N (Appendix A).

Results & Highlights

  • LongBench avg (Llama-3.1-8B): EntropyInfer 48.91 vs full-attention base 48.94 (−0.03) vs AdaKV 48.32 vs CritiPrefill 48.55 vs SnapKV 47.66 — EntropyInfer is best among all compressed methods and essentially matches full attention; on Qwen-2.5-7B it reaches 47.43 vs base 47.98, again beating all baselines (SnapKV 47.51, AdaKV 47.34, CritiPrefill 47.43).
  • InfiniteBench avg (Llama-3.1-8B, >100k): EntropyInfer 43.36 vs base 44.06 vs SnapKV 40.59 vs AdaKV 39.62 vs CritiPrefill 26.91 — CritiPrefill collapses badly at extreme length (R.PassKey drops to 85.42, R.Number to 4.41) while EntropyInfer holds 100.00/82.37.
  • End-to-end speedup: On Llama-3.1-8B summarization from 4k→140k tokens, EntropyInfer reaches 2.39× acceleration over full-attention at the longest context (Figure 4); with latent decode disabled (sparse prefill only) it is slightly slower than CritiPrefill due to per-head budget variance, but enabling latent decode closes the gap and makes EntropyInfer the fastest overall.
  • openPangu generalization: On openPangu-Embedded-7B-v1.1, LongBench avg EntropyInfer 29.66 vs base 30.33 (−0.67); LoCoMo overall 20.82 vs base 21.12 (−0.30); efficiency acceleration grows with context length (Figure 7).

Critical Assessment

The headline 2.39× speedup is measured on a single model (Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct) doing a constrained summarization task (generate-100-tokens-and-stop on a modified Needle-in-a-Haystack prompt), not on the LongBench/InfiniteBench workloads where quality is reported — so the speedup and quality numbers come from different setups and are not jointly validated. The Rigid/Dynamic threshold e_t=1e−5 and budget coefficients (α=0.5, Δt=0.4, clip [⅓B_h, 3B_h]) are fixed heuristics with no sensitivity sweep or principled justification; the paper does not show whether the same constants transfer to architectures beyond Llama/Qwen/Pangu (e.g., Mistral, Gemma). CritiPrefill is a weak baseline at extreme context (InfiniteBench avg 26.91, R.Number 4.41) — it is arguably misconfigured rather than fairly beaten, since its segment budget (2048) may be inappropriate >100k. The latent-decode step requires generating N_D tokens before eviction, adding latency before compression kicks in; the paper notes this yields "limited performance boost in short context" but does not quantify the N_D overhead or its memory cost. No statistical significance testing or variance across seeds is reported.

Strengths

  • Principled core insight: head regime is context-dependent, falsifying offline profiling (RazorAttention/Duo-Attention) with a clean per-head entropy visualization
  • Latent-decode idea directly targets a real documented failure (prefill attention ≠ decode attention, per LoopServe/LouisKV) that eviction methods ignore
  • Genuinely training-free and drop-in; validated across 4 model families including an openPangu series not commonly benchmarked
  • Near-lossless on LongBench (48.91 vs 48.94 base on Llama) while beating all compressed baselines

Limitations

  • Speedup (2.39×) and quality (LongBench/InfiniteBench) measured on different setups; no joint latency-quality curve on the benchmark tasks
  • Entropy threshold and budget coefficients are fixed heuristics without sensitivity analysis or cross-architecture tuning
  • CritiPrefill baseline collapses at >100k (possibly misconfigured), inflating the relative win
  • Latent decode adds N_D-token generation latency and the paper does not quantify this overhead or the memory cost of deferring eviction
  • No variance/significance reporting; single H100, no multi-GPU or serving-system evaluation

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Would the fixed entropy threshold e_t=1e−5 and budget coefficients (α=0.5, Δt=0.4) transfer to architectures outside Llama/Qwen/openPangu (e.g., Mistral, Gemma, Mixture-of-Experts), given the paper provides no cross-architecture sensitivity sweep?
  • The latent-decode step defers eviction until N_D output tokens exist — how large is the added latency and peak KV memory before compression, and does it negate gains under tight memory budgets the paper does not test?
  • CritiPrefill collapses on InfiniteBench (R.Number 4.41) — is this a fair comparison at its configured segment budget of 2048, or is the baseline misconfigured for >100k contexts, inflating EntropyInfer's relative win?
  • The paper cites LoopServe/LouisKV as evidence that prefill attention ≠ decode attention: can the latent-decode re-ranking be quantified to show how much the eviction set changes once output tokens are included, isolating that mechanism's contribution?
