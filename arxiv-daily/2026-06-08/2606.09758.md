> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 DARP（Difference-Aware Retrieval Policies），一种半参数检索式模仿学习方法：在推理时为查询状态检索 k 近邻，用预测器处理 (邻居状态, 邻居动作, 查询与邻居的差分向量) 元组并做置换不变聚合；理论上证明该聚合等价于在 k-NN 图上施加固定低通拉普拉斯谱滤波（无需调 λ），在 MuJoCo、Robosuite、RoboCasa 与真实 FurnitureBench 上比标准行为克隆提升 15–46%（如 Robosuite Stack 任务 72% vs BC 47%、HalfCheetah 5515 vs BC 1063）。

> **TL;DR:** DARP is a semi-parametric retrieval-based imitation learning method that, at inference, retrieves k nearest neighbors (k≈500 optimal) for a query state and predicts actions from (neighbor state s*_i, neighbor action a*_i, difference vector Δs_i = s*_i − s_q) tuples aggregated by a permutation-invariant function gψ (simple average or Set Transformer). Theoretically, neighbor aggregation is proven equivalent to applying a fixed low-pass Laplacian spectral filter φ(µ)=1−µ on the k-NN graph (Theorem 2), giving parameter-free smoothing that reduces variance (Theorem 1). On MuJoCo locomotion, DARP reaches HalfCheetah 5515.41±841.33 vs BC 1063.23±371.08 and Ant 4383.28±266.37 vs BC 2376.20±339.43; on Robosuite/RoboCasa manipulation (low-dim state), DARP improves success 15–46% over BC (e.g. Stack 72% vs 47%, Square Peg 62% vs 46%, RoboCasa Drawer 85% vs 54%); on vision tasks (R3M embeddings), DARP reaches Stack 75/Threading 76/Peg 52% vs BC 44/38/17% (~35% avg improvement); on real-world FurnitureBench square-table assembly, DARP 92% vs BC 44% (>2×). Ablations confirm distance vectors and permutation-invariant aggregation are the crucial components.

## 核心贡献

- 提出差分感知的检索式重参数化：不学全局状态到动作映射，而为查询状态检索 k 近邻，用预测器处理 (s*_i, a*_i, Δs_i = s*_i − s_q) 元组，使模型学习"专家动作在局部扰动下如何变化"，再做置换不变聚合。
- 理论上证明该架构隐式实现了拉普拉斯平滑（无需显式正则项或 λ 超参）：Theorem 1 证明流形正则化 BC（MRIL）相对朴素 BC 在方差、Lipschitz 平滑性与闭环稳定性上严格更优；Theorem 2 证明 iMRIL 的邻居聚合等价于在 k-NN 图拉普拉斯算子上施加固定低通谱滤波 φ(µ)=1−µ，保留低频模式、抑制高频方差。
- 保持标准行为克隆的全部假设（仅需专家状态-动作对，无需额外数据收集、在线专家反馈或任务特定知识），将方差抑制嵌入架构而非损失函数。
- 跨低维状态、高维视觉（R3M 嵌入）、多模态动作分布（Push-T 上 GMM 头提升 22%）与真实机器人（FurnitureBench）四类设定验证，并给出系统消融定位关键组件。

## Key Contributions

- **Difference-aware retrieval reparameterization.** Instead of a global state→action map, DARP retrieves k neighbors (k≈500 optimal, Fig. 9) for a query state s_q and predicts each candidate action from the tuple (s*_i, a*_i, Δs_i = s*_i − s_q), so the predictor learns how expert actions vary under local perturbations; candidates are aggregated by a permutation-invariant function gψ (simple average, or a Set Transformer / GMM head for multimodality).
- **Implicit, parameter-free Laplacian smoothing (Theorems 1–2).** Theorem 1 proves manifold-regularized BC (MRIL) strictly improves over vanilla BC in estimator variance, local Lipschitz smoothness, and closed-loop rollout stability (sublinear vs linear error accumulation). Theorem 2 proves the iMRIL neighbor-aggregation step applies the fixed spectral filter φ(µ)=1−µ to the symmetric normalised k-NN graph Laplacian, a built-in low-pass filter with no λ to tune.
- **Pure-BC regime compliance.** DARP needs only the expert state–action dataset; no simulator, interactive expert, sub-optimal data, or task-specific structure — variance reduction is embedded in the architecture, not the loss.
- **Broad empirical validation + ablations.** Low-dim state (MuJoCo, Robosuite, RoboCasa), high-dim vision (R3M), multimodal actions (Push-T GMM head, +22%), and real-world FurnitureBench; ablations isolate distance vectors and permutation-invariant aggregation as the crucial components.

## Methodology

**Problem setting (§2.1).** Finite-horizon MDP M={S, A, P₀}; policy fθ: S→Δ(A); expert dataset D*={(s*_j, a*_j)} of state-action pairs. Standard BC (Pomerleau 1991) minimises the supervised imitation risk L_BC=E[ℓ(f(s*), a*)], which controls bias on the training distribution but leaves variance unchecked in low-density regions encountered during closed-loop rollouts (covariate shift / compounding error).

**Warm-up: explicit neighbor-manifold-regularized IL (MRIL, §2.2).** Adds a smoothness penalty encouraging nearby expert states to map to similar actions: L_MRIL = L_BC + λ·E_{s*∼D*}[Σ_{i∈N_k(s*)} w_i(s*)·‖f(s*)−f(s*_i)‖²], where N_k(s*) is the k-NN set and w_i kernel weights. Theorem 1 (assumes true expert policy f* is C²-smooth on compact S): (i) the Laplacian penalty acts as a data-dependent Tikhonov regularizer → smaller estimator variance than BC; (ii) minimizers satisfy a uniform local Lipschitz bound whereas BC admits arbitrarily large Lipschitz interpolants; (iii) closed-loop deviation recursion Δ_{t+1} ≤ L_s·Δ_t + L_a·‖f(s_t)−f*(s*_t)‖ accumulates linearly for BC but sublinearly for MRIL. Drawback: requires tuning λ.

**Implicit MRIL via architecture (iMRIL, §2.3).** Instead of adding λ·L_S to the loss, build neighborhood aggregation into the policy architecture itself: f̂_iMRIL(s_q) = (1/k)·Σ_{i∈N_k(s_q)} fθ(s*_i). Theorem 2: on the symmetric normalised k-NN graph Laplacian L with eigenpairs {(µ_j, u_j)}, iMRIL applies the fixed spectral filter φ_iMRIL(µ)=1−µ — preserving low-frequency (smooth) modes and suppressing high-frequency (high-variance) modes — with no λ. Both iMRIL and explicit MRIL act as low-pass filters; iMRIL achieves it parameter-free.

**DARP practical instantiation (§2.4).** Two design choices: (1) **Difference-based parameterization** — instead of fθ(s*_i), the predictor takes a'_i = fθ(s*_i, a*_i, Δs_i = s*_i − s_q), i.e. the neighbor state, its action, and the difference vector from the query; this is shown to be crucial for performance in ablations (Fig. 6). (2) **Beyond linear aggregation** — the simple average is a special case of permutation-invariant gψ({a'_i}); gψ can be a Set Transformer (Lee et al. 2019) or DeepSets, and instead of predicting â_q directly DARP can predict parameters α of an action distribution p(a_q; α) (GMM means/covariances/weights, or a diffusion score function) for multimodal maximum-likelihood training (Eq. 6). Training uses only the standard BC objective (Eq. 5); inference retrieves k-NN at test time and aggregates. The aggregation function g in Q1 experiments is a simple average; Set Transformer / GMM heads are used for Q2.

**Distance function (Appendix A.2.1).** Uses Euclidean distance in a pre-trained embedding space, augmented with history: d(s_q, s*_i) = Σ_{n=0}^{ℓ−1} ‖s_{q,−n} − s*_{j,t−n}‖·e^{−γn}, accumulating distances over a lookback window of ℓ past states with exponential decay γ (puts more weight on recent states). Hyperparameter sweep (Fig. 9): k≈500 neighbors is the global optimum (performance poor with few neighbors, plateaus just above BC as k→dataset size); more history slightly improves performance; decay sensitivity exists. Robust to cosine vs Euclidean (Table 7: only 5% difference). Inference ~0.00437s/step at k=500 on Stack (Appendix).

**Tasks and baselines (§3.1).** MuJoCo (D4RL): Hopper, Ant, Walker, HalfCheetah locomotion. Robosuite: Stack, Threading, Square Peg (single-arm manipulation, MimicGen data). RoboCasa: Drawer, Door, Stove (randomized kitchen). Real-world: FurnitureBench square-table assembly (diffusion policy). Baselines: standard BC (MLP); non-parametric R&P and LWR (VINN-style); REGENT (retrieval-augmented causal transformer); MRIL (explicit smoothed DARP); DARP and DARP Set Transformer. Vision: R3M image embeddings (Nair et al. 2022). 100 trials with 95% CI (50 for real-world). NVIDIA L40/L40s GPUs.

## Results & Highlights

- **MuJoCo locomotion: DARP dominates BC across all four tasks (Table 1, 100 trials, 95% CI).** HalfCheetah: DARP 5515.41 ± 841.33 vs BC 1063.23 ± 371.08 (and BC-ensemble-style MRIL only 701.58 ± 195.08); Ant: DARP 4383.28 ± 266.37 vs BC 2376.20 ± 339.43; Walker: DARP 4894.01 ± 75.12 vs BC 2658.40 ± 274.08; Hopper: DARP 3545.57 ± 3.54 vs BC 2313.65 ± 203.75. Purely non-parametric methods (R&P, LWR) perform poorly (e.g. Hopper R&P 711.82, Ant R&P −305.97).
- **Robosuite/RoboCasa manipulation: 15–46% success-rate gains over BC (Table 2).** RoboCasa Drawer: DARP 85% vs BC 54%; Door 45% vs 29%; Stove 43% vs 28%. Robosuite Stack: DARP 72% vs BC 47%; Threading 63% vs 37%; Square Peg 62% vs 46%. Real-world FurnitureBench square-table assembly: DARP 92% vs BC 44% — more than doubling BC's score.
- **High-dimensional vision (R3M embeddings): ~35% average improvement, larger than low-dim (~22%) (Table 3).** Stack: DARP 75% vs BC 44%; Threading: DARP 76% vs BC 38%; Peg: DARP 52% vs BC 17%. The improvement is larger in the visual regime, suggesting DARP adapts better to complex high-dimensional representations than standard BC.
- **Multimodal actions (Push-T): DARP with GMM head +22% over BC (Appendix A.2.2).** DARP employs a Set Transformer head predicting GMM parameters, handling multimodal action distributions effectively.
- **Ablations isolate distance vectors and permutation-invariant aggregation as crucial (Fig. 6, Robosuite Stack, 100 trials).** Standard DARP (s*_i, a*_i, s*_i−s_q): ~0.85 success; without neighbor actions (s*_i, s*_i−s_q): 0.72; BC ensemble (10 agents): 0.67; random neighbors (s*_i, a*_i, ‖s*_i−s_q‖₂): 0.59; BC (query only): 0.58; with query state instead of difference (s*_i, a*_i, s_q): 0.53; permutation-dependent aggregator: 0.42; full query-only ablation 0.19. Random neighbor selection performs poorly (confirming meaningful distance metrics matter); permutation-dependent aggregation significantly degrades performance (confirming permutation invariance matters).
- **Divergence analysis (Fig. 7): DARP's gains come from better OOD prediction via difference reparameterization.** In six rollouts across Robosuite Stack and MuJoCo Hopper where BC fails and DARP succeeds, at the "step of divergence" (SoD) the query state has state likelihood < τ_s (1st-percentile training likelihood, i.e. OOD) but delta likelihood > τ_∆ (in distribution) — supporting the hypothesis that DARP gains come partly from improved prediction on slightly OOD states via difference vectors.

## Critical Assessment

- **The headline 15–46% improvement range conflates very different baselines and metrics.** The locomotion numbers (Table 1) are cumulative reward on MuJoCo (where HalfCheetah DARP 5515 vs BC 1063 is a ~5× raw-score ratio but not a "percentage-point" gain), while the manipulation numbers (Table 2) are success percentages — the abstract's "15–46%" framing blends these incomparable scales, and the largest percentage gains (real-world FurnitureBench 92% vs 44%) come from a single task with only 50 trials and a diffusion policy that is itself a non-trivial architectural change beyond the DARP aggregation.
- **The k≈500 neighbor count is large and the inference-cost claim is under-specified.** Fig. 9 shows the global optimum at ~500 neighbors, and Appendix reports ~0.00437s/step at k=500 on Stack, but the paper does not report how retrieval latency scales with dataset size or state dimension — for the real-world and image-based settings (where the expert dataset and R3M embeddings are large), the k-NN retrieval cost could become a deployment bottleneck that is not analysed, and the 500-neighbor optimum was swept only on Stack.
- **The control-theoretic stability claim (Theorem 1(iii)) is asymptotic and assumes the true expert policy f* is C²-smooth on a compact state space.** Real robotic manipulation tasks with contact-rich dynamics (peg insertion, threading, furniture assembly) have discontinuous dynamics where f* is not C² — the sublinear-error-accumulation guarantee does not directly apply to the exact manipulation tasks where DARP's largest empirical gains occur, so the theory and the strongest empirical results sit in different regimes.

## Strengths

- Clean conceptual contribution: reframing smoothness regularization as an architectural choice (neighbor aggregation) rather than a loss term, with a formal spectral equivalence (Theorem 2: φ(µ)=1−µ low-pass filter) that removes the λ hyperparameter — genuinely parameter-free smoothing.
- The difference-vector parameterization (s*_i, a*_i, s*_i−s_q) is well-motivated (learn how actions vary under local perturbations) and the ablation (Fig. 6) cleanly confirms it is the single most important design choice, dropping from ~0.85 to ~0.53 when replaced by the absolute query state.
- Stays strictly within the pure-BC regime (no simulator, no interactive expert, no extra data) — a fair and practically important constraint that makes the method drop-in for existing BC pipelines.
- Strong, broad evaluation: four MuJoCo tasks, six manipulation tasks across Robosuite/RoboCasa/real-world, vision (R3M), multimodal actions (Push-T GMM), plus a systematic 8-way ablation and a divergence analysis (SoD) that provides a mechanistic explanation for the gains.
- Public code, data, and configuration files with a reproducibility statement (ICLR 2026).

## Limitations

- The 15–46% headline range conflates cumulative-reward (MuJoCo) and success-percentage (manipulation) metrics across incomparable scales; the largest percentage gain (FurnitureBench 92% vs 44%) is a single 50-trial task with a diffusion-policy backbone.
- The k≈500 optimum is large and was swept only on Stack; retrieval-cost scaling with dataset size and state dimension (especially for R3M image embeddings) is not analysed, a potential deployment bottleneck.
- Theorem 1's C²-smoothness assumption on f* does not hold for contact-rich manipulation (peg insertion, threading, furniture assembly) where dynamics are discontinuous — the theory's stability guarantee and the strongest empirical gains are in different regimes.
- The distance function uses a history-augmented Euclidean metric with lookback ℓ and decay γ hyperparameters (Appendix A.2.1) that do require tuning (Fig. 9 shows sensitivity), somewhat weakening the "parameter-free" framing (the λ-free claim applies to the smoothing, not to retrieval).
- No comparison against DAgger or other interactive-expert covariate-shift methods is given (the paper deliberately restricts to the pure-BC regime, but readers should note the comparison set excludes the strongest covariate-shift baseline family).

## Relevance

本文研究模仿学习/行为克隆在分布漂移下的方差抑制与稳定性，通过检索式半参数策略架构隐式实现拉普拉斯流形平滑，对应到"**ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations**"（流形正则化、k-NN 图拉普拉斯谱滤波与方差/稳定性证明）与广义的"**Self-Evolving Agents & Automated Research**"（机器人智能体的检索增强策略学习，无需额外数据/在线专家反馈即可提升部署鲁棒性）。其差分向量重参数化与置换不变聚合为序列决策中的可靠性提升提供了可迁移的架构设计。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Theorem 1 assumes f* is C²-smooth, but DARP's largest gains are on contact-rich manipulation (Threading, Peg, FurnitureBench) with discontinuous dynamics — does the implicit Laplacian smoothing still reduce variance when the true expert policy itself is non-smooth, or do the gains there come purely from the retrieval/averaging rather than the spectral low-pass property?
- The k≈500 optimum (Fig. 9) and the ~0.00437s/step inference cost are reported only on Stack; how does retrieval latency scale with expert-dataset size and R3M embedding dimension, and is k=500 still optimal or affordable on the real-world/image-based settings where datasets are larger?
- The history-augmented distance (lookback ℓ, decay γ) in Appendix A.2.1 requires tuning and Fig. 9 shows sensitivity — does this undercut the "parameter-free" framing, and could the lookback/decay be learned rather than swept?
- DARP is compared against retrieval/in-context methods (REGENT, VINN/LWR) but deliberately excludes DAgger and interactive-expert covariate-shift baselines (§4); in settings where an interactive expert is available, does DARP's architectural smoothing compose with or compete against on-policy correction?
- The divergence analysis (SoD, Fig. 7) shows query states at failure points have low state likelihood but high delta likelihood — could this SoD signal be used as a runtime OOD detector to trigger fallback or re-retrieval, turning the diagnostic into a corrective mechanism?
