> **一句话总结：** Hypnos 是一个多模态睡眠基础模型，用残差向量量化把 EEG/ECG/呼吸等 8 种模态转成离散 token，再训练自回归 RQ-Transformer 做跨模态并行 next-token 预测；在睡眠分期、皮层觉醒、呼吸暂停等任务上显著超过现有睡眠基础模型，线性探针甚至超过强监督基线，并在房颤检测上追平/超过专门的 ECG 基础模型。

> **TL;DR:** Trained on >20,000 overnight PSG recordings from 9 NSRR datasets, Hypnos (Hypnos-Base, 222M params) beats prior sleep foundation models (SleepFM, sleep2vec, OSF) on held-out MrOS — e.g., full-modality sleep-staging AUROC 0.973 vs OSF 0.960, arousal AUROC 0.934 vs 0.922 — and with only a linear probe outperforms supervised U-Sleep/SleepTransformer on SHHS/MrOS; on external ECG benchmarks it matches xECG on CinC 2017 AF (0.984 vs 0.985) and beats it on Apnea-ECG (0.925 vs 0.884) and CPSC 2021 (0.985 vs 0.934).

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统验证 next-token prediction 是多模态生理信号自监督表示学习的有效且可扩展目标，统一了生成式建模与表示学习
- 提出 Hypnos：用残差向量量化（RVQ）把 8 种模态（EEG、ECG、EOG、呼吸等）token 化，自回归 RQ-Transformer 跨模态并行预测下一 token
- 大规模预训练（>20,000 夜间 PSG，9 个 NSRR 公共数据集），并验证 next-token 困惑度与下游探针性能随模型规模持续提升
- 部署友好设计：流式推理、支持任意模态子集与任意长度记录、以 1 Hz 生成嵌入，可用于闭环神经调控等实时场景
- 跨域迁移：超出睡眠域，在白天 ECG 任务（房颤、呼吸暂停检测）上追平/超过专门 ECG 基础模型 xECG

## Key Contributions

- First systematic demonstration that next-token prediction is an effective, scalable SSL objective for multi-modal physiological sensor data, unifying generative modelling and representation learning
- Hypnos: residual vector quantization (RVQ) tokenizes 8 modalities (EEG, ECG, EOG, respiratory) into discrete token streams; an auto-regressive RQ-Transformer jointly predicts the next token across modalities in parallel
- Pre-trained on >20,000 overnight PSG recordings from 9 NSRR public datasets, with next-token perplexity and downstream probing performance both improving with scale
- Deployment-focused: streaming inference, any subset of modalities, arbitrary-length recordings, 1 Hz embedding rate (suitable for closed-loop neuromodulation)
- Cross-domain transfer: matches/beats the dedicated ECG foundation model xECG on external single-lead ECG benchmarks (AF, apnoea detection)

## Methodology

**Pre-training data.** Nine NSRR datasets (SHHS, CCSHS, CFS, CHAT, MESA, MrOS, NCHSDB, WSC) plus Dreem Open Datasets (DOD-H, DOD-O) for external evaluation — collectively >20,000 overnight polysomnography recordings. Splits follow Shuai et al. (OSF); MrOS, DOD-H, DOD-O kept fully external.

**Tokenization.** Each of 8 modalities (Table 1) is tokenized via residual vector quantization (RVQ) with a per-modality codebook; modalities grouped by type (EEG channels share a tokenizer, EOG channels share, respiratory channels share) → 5 tokenizers for 8 modalities. Number of quantizers K chosen per modality to maintain high reconstruction accuracy. Tokenizer trained with a multi-term reconstruction loss + RVQ commitment penalty following BrainTokenizer.

**Architecture (RQ-Transformer).** Two stacked Transformers: a temporal (backbone) Transformer and a depth Transformer, with parameters shared across modalities/timesteps; self-attention scales with T (timesteps) rather than K·T. Outputs embeddings z_t^i at 1 Hz. Three scales evaluated (Table 2): Hypnos-Tiny 37M, Hypnos-Small 81M, Hypnos-Base 222M params (12 layers; hidden D 192/384/768; temporal heads 3/6/12; depth transformer 4 layers). Linear/embedding init from N(0, 0.02²) (GPT-2 style); learning rates 8e-4 (Tiny), 6e-4 (Small), 3e-4 (Base). Hypnos-Base trained to 50k steps in 1.5 days on 8 [GPUs].

**Cross-modal group masking.** During pre-training, cross-modal attention is restricted to randomly sampled sub-groups drawn from a Chinese Restaurant Process with concentration α (Default α=1), improving test-time generalisation to modality subsets. Ablated against No-masking (α→0), Independent (α→∞), and Random (p=0.5).

**Objective.** Next-token prediction: jointly predict the next token across all modalities in parallel. The same architecture is fully generative — tokens can be auto-regressively sampled and decoded back to waveforms.

**Evaluation.** Linear/MLP probes on frozen embeddings. Tasks: sleep stage classification (AASM 5-class: Wake/N1/N2/N3/REM), cortical arousal detection, apnoea detection, desaturation detection; plus external ECG (CinC 2017 AF, Apnea-ECG, CPSC 2021 paroxysmal AF). Metrics: Cohen's κ, macro AUROC, macro AUPRC (per-subject mean). Statistical significance via Wilcoxon signed-rank over recordings with Benjamini-Hochberg FDR (q<0.05).

## Results & Highlights

- **Foundation-model comparison (Table 3, held-out MrOS, full M=8):** Hypnos sleep-staging AUROC **0.973** / AUPRC **0.796** vs OSF 0.960/0.747, sleep2vec 0.960/0.741, SleepFM 0.952/0.718 — significantly better (∗, FDR q<0.05). Same pattern on arousal (0.934 vs 0.922), apnoea (0.795 vs 0.775), desaturation (0.767 vs 0.737).
- **vs supervised baselines (Table 4, linear probe):** Hypnos beats U-Sleep and SleepTransformer on every metric on both SHHS and MrOS — e.g., MrOS Cohen's κ **0.799** (Hypnos) vs 0.753 (SleepTransformer) vs 0.730 (U-Sleep); SHHS κ **0.811** vs 0.791 (OSF) vs 0.806 (SleepTransformer).
- **100× label efficiency (Fig. 6):** on held-out MrOS, Hypnos trained on as little as **1%** of labelled recordings matches U-Sleep trained on the full dataset; Hypnos outperforms all foundation models and supervised baselines across all data fractions (1%→100%).
- **External ECG transfer (Table 5):** matches xECG on CinC 2017 AF (**0.984 vs 0.985** AUROC) and beats it on Apnea-ECG (**0.925 vs 0.884**, +4 pp) and CPSC 2021 paroxysmal AF (**0.985 vs 0.934**, +5 pp) — a sleep model generalising to daytime cardiology.
- **Modality-masking ablation (Table 6):** the Independent variant (no cross-modal attention) lags across every config (e.g., staging κ 0.773 vs Default 0.792 full), confirming cross-modal fusion helps; training-time masking improves restricted-modality robustness while preserving full-modality performance, with Default and Random masking performing comparably.

## Critical Assessment

- **Few-shot "100×" claim is dataset- and baseline-specific:** the 1%-matches-full result is shown on MrOS against U-Sleep specifically; the abstract generalises it to "100× less labelled data," but Figure 6 also shows that on SHHS (in-domain) the gap between Hypnos-at-1% and supervised-at-100% is smaller and metric-dependent, so the multiplier is a best-case framing.
- **AF detection "surpasses a dedicated ECG model" is a tie on the headline benchmark:** on CinC 2017 (the canonical AF task) Hypnos (0.984) is within 0.001 of xECG (0.985) — effectively a tie; the wins are on Apnea-ECG and CPSC 2021, which are apnoea/paroxysmal-AF tasks rather than the AF-detection task the abstract emphasises.
- **Restrictive probe-only evaluation:** all foundation-model comparisons use linear/MLP probes on frozen embeddings; no end-to-end fine-tuning of Hypnos is reported, so it is unclear how much headroom remains and how Hypnos compares when the baselines are also fine-tuned rather than used as frozen embedders.
- **Compute undisclosed at the headline level:** training cost is only given for Hypnos-Base (1.5 days on 8 GPUs), with no total FLOPs, no comparison of compute against the (much smaller — ~1M SleepFM, ≤240M sleep2vec) baselines, so the scaling claim is not compute-matched.
- **Clinical validity not established:** all evaluation is against expert annotations / public benchmarks; the paper itself flags clinical outcomes and biomarker discovery as future work (Section 5 / Section C), so real-world deployment impact is untested.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated central idea: recast physiological SSL as next-token prediction over RVQ tokens, inheriting the LLM/audio recipe (the analogy to Moshi is apt)
- Strong and broad empirical win: significantly beats three prior sleep foundation models across four tasks and four modality configurations, with FDR-corrected significance tests
- Genuine label efficiency and cross-domain transfer (sleep → daytime ECG) demonstrated, not just asserted
- Deployment-focused design (streaming, any modality subset, 1 Hz embeddings) is practically useful for closed-loop applications
- Code/checkpoints promised; pre-training entirely on public NSRR data aids reproducibility

## Limitations

- "100× less data" and "surpasses ECG model" claims are best-case framings (specific baseline/dataset; AF headline is a tie)
- Probe-only evaluation; no end-to-end fine-tuning comparison
- Compute not matched against baselines; scaling claim not compute-normalised
- Clinical outcomes / biomarker validity untested (acknowledged future work)
- Generalisation to arbitrary EEG montages and long-context (circadian, multi-night) regimes not yet demonstrated

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The "100× less labelled data" claim is shown on MrOS vs U-Sleep (Fig. 6) — does it hold on the other seven cohorts and against SleepTransformer, or is it specific to one baseline/dataset?
- All foundation-model comparisons use frozen linear/MLP probes (Tables 3–4); how does Hypnos perform under end-to-end fine-tuning, and does the gap to supervised baselines widen or close?
- On CinC 2017 AF, Hypnos (0.984) is within 0.001 of xECG (0.985) — is the difference statistically significant, and does the abstract's "surpasses a dedicated ECG model" overstate a tie on the canonical AF benchmark?
- Compute is reported only for Hypnos-Base (1.5 days × 8 GPUs); how do total training FLOPs compare to the much smaller baselines (SleepFM ~1M, sleep2vec ≤240M), and is the scaling improvement compute-matched?
- The CRP group-masking (α=1) and Random masking (p=0.5) perform comparably (Table 6) — what does this insensitivity imply about whether the specific masking strategy or simply *any* cross-modal dropout drives restricted-modality robustness?
- The paper cites Moshi [16] and NeuroLM [25] as the closest prior art; given Hypnos extends single-codebook EEG autoregression to multi-modal RVQ, does the RVQ codebook size K per modality materially affect downstream AUROC (the paper says K is chosen for reconstruction accuracy but reports no K ablation)?
