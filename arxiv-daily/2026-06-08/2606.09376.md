> **一句话总结：** 本文用 F1 赛车遥测和 NOAA 天气预报两个"完备真值"领域证明：仅测精度（precision）的忠实性指标会奖励"少说即少错"的回避行为；当补上覆盖率（recall）后，前沿模型的排名会反转——精度最高的 grok-4.3 反而 F1 垫底。

> **TL;DR:** Reference-free faithfulness metrics measure only precision and thereby reward abstention. Using a *complete* oracle (Formula 1 telemetry), the authors build a 7,253-instance multilingual (EN/ES/PT) benchmark across 150 races and show that the most precise frontier model (grok-4.3, precision 0.89) covers only 0.46 of relevant facts and ranks *last* by F1, while a LoRA-fine-tuned Qwen2.5-3B reaches F1 0.982; the precision/recall ranking inversion replicates on a second complete-oracle domain (NOAA weather).

## 核心贡献

- 证明"忠实性=精度"这类无参考评估存在**回避盲区**：模型靠"几乎不说"即可拿近满分，且在完备真值下，一旦要求覆盖率，系统排名会整体反转（在 F1 与天气两个不相关领域都复现）。
- 构建了**完备真值**的多语（EN/ES/PT）基准：7253 个 F1 决策实例、跨 7 赛季 150 场比赛，5 类决策（轮胎策略、undercut、overcut、防守、比赛总结），让 recall（覆盖率）可被精确测量——这是开放域忠实性基准结构性做不到的。
- 提出 **precision+recall 忠实性度量**：经"受控扰动"与"双独立抽取器（正则 vs 跨家族 LLM）一致性"双重验证；并给出一个**校验器引导的生成方法**，无需参考文本即可同时提升精度与覆盖率。

## Key Contributions

- Shows that precision-only faithfulness metrics reward abstention, and that with a complete oracle the model ranking **inverts** once coverage is required — replicated across two unrelated domains (F1 and weather).
- Releases a complete-oracle, multilingual (EN/ES/PT) benchmark of 7,253 grounded F1 decision instances, plus a precision+recall faithfulness metric validated by controlled perturbation and by agreement between a model-free regex extractor and a cross-family LLM extractor (system-level Spearman 1.00).
- Provides a verifier-guided generation method that improves both precision and recall using only the structured verifier as a training-free signal (e.g. raises gpt-5.4-mini precision 0.640 → 0.881).

## Methodology

The core methodological idea is to use a **complete oracle** so that recall (coverage) is exactly measurable, then pair it with precision.

- **Domain choice.** Formula 1 telemetry is extracted via FastF1 (the official live-timing API). Because strategic ground truth (pit laps, compounds, undercuts, defenses, outcomes) is derived deterministically and *exhaustively*, for each decision the full set of checkable facts is enumerable — this enumerable set is the exact recall denominator, which open-domain faithfulness settings lack.
- **Dataset construction.** From 150 races over 7 seasons (2018–2025): 8069 stints, 5088 pit stops, 3159 pit battles → 7253 instances (1500 tyre-strategy, 2034 undercut, 1125 overcut, 2444 defense, 150 race-summary). Split by season to avoid leakage: 6004 train (2018–2024) and 1249 held-out test (2025); model comparisons use a stratified test sample of 207 instances. Five decision types: tyre strategy, undercut, overcut, on-track defense, race summary.
- **Event detection heuristics.** Stints segmented per driver with a within-stint degradation slope fit on green-flag laps only (excluding in/out laps, SC, VSC); undercuts/overcuts detected pairwise only for drivers within a small on-track gap; defenses detected as runs of ≥5 consecutive laps with a faster pursuer held within 1.5s.
- **Metric.** Explanations are decomposed into typed atomic claims (pit lap, compound change, stop count, stint compound, final position, undercut/overcut, outcome, time gained), each labeled *supported / contradicted / unverifiable* against the structured oracle. **Precision** = supported fraction of stated claims (the standard faithfulness quantity); **recall** = fraction of enumerable facts the model correctly stated; combined as their harmonic mean (F1). Two interchangeable extractors: a dependency-free regex extractor (English) and an LLM extractor (any language), both emitting the same typed schema, with identical verification.
- **Validation.** Controlled perturbation (a faithful template scores 1.0; injected errors penalized proportionally) and correlation with an independent LLM judge (gpt-5.5): Pearson 0.55, Spearman 0.54.
- **Verifier-guided generation.** Generate → run verifier → feed back both contradicted claims ("fix these") and uncovered ground-truth facts ("add these") as targeted edit instructions, iterating a few rounds; uses only the structured verifier, no reference text, applicable to any LLM backend.
- **Models evaluated** zero-shot: OpenAI gpt-5.5, gpt-5.4-mini (Azure); xAI grok-4.3; Google gemini-2.5-pro (Vertex AI); DeepSeek-V3.2; plus Qwen2.5-3B zero-shot vs LoRA fine-tuned.

## Results & Highlights

- **Abstention inverts the ranking (central finding).** In Portuguese, the most precise model grok-4.3 (precision 0.89) covers only **0.46** of relevant facts and ranks *last* by F1 (**0.61**), while gpt-5.5 leads by F1 (**0.65**); precision and F1 rankings disagree in *every* language (EN/ES/PT) (Table 2).
- **Verifier-guided generation lifts precision.** Applied to gpt-5.4-mini, it raises precision from **0.640 → 0.881** (English regex verifier), a training-free improvement.
- **Cover-all prompt does not close the gap.** Explicitly asking for completeness makes mean recall *fall* (**0.60 → 0.47**), with only 2 of 5 models improving — confirming low coverage is not an under-prompting artifact (Table 3).
- **Extractor robustness.** A cross-family LLM extractor (DeepSeek-V3.2, no shared lineage with gpt-5.x) agrees near-perfectly at the system level (**Spearman 1.00**, Pearson 0.82, N=1090) across all three languages; the model-free regex extractor agrees at Spearman 0.80, Pearson 0.50 — and the same-family gpt-5.5 is *not* ranked top by its own family's extractor.
- **Small fine-tuned model beats the frontier.** LoRA-fine-tuned Qwen2.5-3B reaches precision **0.995**, recall **0.968**, F1 **0.982** — the highest F1 in the study — by faithfully reproducing deterministic grounded templates (a strength on-distribution, a template-mimicry caveat off-distribution).
- **Replication in a second domain.** On NOAA weather forecasts (150 records/language), the most precise model is again not the most complete; e.g. DeepSeek-V3.2 reaches recall **0.42** at precision only **0.49** — the effect is milder (fewer facts per record) but the precision/F1 disagreement reappears.

## Critical Assessment

- The headline claim — "precision-only faithfulness rewards abstention and reorders rankings" — is established with a deliberately narrow claim ontology; the paper itself concedes (Limitations) that ungrounded entity/causal insertions *outside the schema are never extracted, hence never penalised* (e.g. a model that invented "Verstappen" and a title-fight swing scored a perfect supported fraction). So the abstention finding is real but bounded by what the schema can check.
- The fine-tuned Qwen2.5-3B's near-perfect F1 (0.982) is achieved by mimicking deterministic silver-supervision templates; the authors transparently flag this as a **template-mimicry caveat**, and there is *no out-of-template evaluation* to confirm the gain generalizes — a notable missing ablation.
- The model comparison uses only a **stratified test sample of 207 instances** for cost reasons, and the held-out split is a single season (2025; 2022 omitted due to unavailable timing data), so the ranking-inversion claim rests on a small slice rather than the full 1249-instance test set.
- A pipeline quirk weakens the English multilingual evidence: the hosted AIServices endpoint's content filter rejected ~one-third of English inputs (same instances for both served models), so the cleanest comparison is in ES/PT.

## Strengths

- Novel and important framing: the abstention blind spot of precision-only faithfulness metrics is made *measurable* via the complete-oracle trick, and the ranking-inversion effect is replicated across two unrelated domains (F1 + weather), strengthening the generalization claim.
- Strong metric validation: controlled-perturbation study plus three independent extractors (regex, same-family LLM, cross-family LLM) with reported Spearman/Pearson, including an explicit check that the same-family system (gpt-5.5) is not favored.
- Fully open artifacts (benchmark, code, demo, structured ground truth) and honest, detailed Limitations.

## Limitations

- Recall is defined relative to the deterministic extractor's derived fact set (high-precision but not exhaustive — event detection uses heuristics), not to every conceivable salient detail.
- The claim ontology bounds faithfulness coverage; off-schema hallucinations go unpenalized.
- Fine-tuning supervision is silver (deterministic templates) with no out-of-template evaluation; small sample (207) for the model comparison; held-out split is a single season.
- Human-judge correlation is future work; reasoning models required raised output-token budgets to avoid truncation.

## Relevance

直接对应"**LLM Inference Evaluation（推理评估）**"研究方向：它揭示并量化了主流忠实性评估指标（精度口径）的结构性盲区，提出 precision+recall 双轴度量与校验器引导生成方法，并给出了可复现的多语基准。同时对"**LLM Training Stability & Observability**"也有间接价值——展示了评估管线本身的审计需求（内容过滤、token 预算等）会扭曲测量结果。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would the ranking-inversion effect persist at the full 1249-instance test set (not just the 207-instance stratified sample), and would it survive an out-of-template evaluation of the fine-tuned Qwen2.5-3B?
- How does the precision/recall metric compare against SAFE's F1@K (Wei et al., 2024) and RAGAS faithfulness (Es et al., 2024), which the paper cites as estimating recall against incomplete denominators?
- Can the verifier-guided generation loop's recall gains be replicated when the oracle is *not* complete (e.g. open-domain long-form factuality), where the "uncovered facts" feedback signal is itself uncertain?
