> **一句话总结：** BareWave 提出首个完全"波形原生"的零样本语音克隆框架——推理时无中间声学表示、无预训练组件、无独立 vocoder，单模型直接从文本+提示音频生成波形；通过三项训练设计（训练期表征对齐 REPA、两阶段噪声调度、速度感知感知对齐 VAPA）解决直接波形 TTS 的优化难题，在 Seed-TTS test-en 上取得 1.75% WER 与 0.602 SIM-o，为同数据规模下最优。

> **TL;DR:** BareWave is a fully waveform-native (no mel/codec/vocoder at inference) flow-matching TTS trained on 19.4k hours of Emilia-EN at 24 kHz, using a 983.6M-parameter waveform-patch DiT (32 blocks, hidden 1280). On Seed-TTS test-en it reaches 1.75% WER / 0.602 SIM-o / 3.72 UTMOS, beating same-data mel-space baselines F5-TTS Base (2.42% / 0.424 / 3.35) and E2-TTS Base (2.34% / 0.478 / 3.43) on WER and SIM-o. The full training recipe lifts LibriSpeech-PC WER from 3.52% (basic waveform baseline) to 2.88%, SIM-o from 0.471 to 0.610, and UTMOS from 3.69 to 3.97; VAPA alone adds +0.025 SIM-o and +0.09 UTMOS over a refined-STFT ablation.

## 核心贡献

- 提出首个推理路径完全"波形原生"的零样本 TTS 框架：无中间声学表示、无预训练推理组件、无独立 vocoder，单一生成器直接从文本与提示波形产出波形（Table 1 显示此前所有 direct 系统——Wave-Tacotron/FastSpeech 2s/DiffAR/E3-TTS/E2-TTS/F5-TTS——均至少保留一项推理依赖）。
- 把直接波形 TTS 的难点分解为三个训练挑战并分别给方案：(1) 缺预训练表征脚手架 → 训练期表征对齐 REPA（对齐生成器隐层与冻结 WavLM 教师）；(2) 不同训练阶段偏好不同噪声调度 → 两阶段调度（先 logit-normal 收敛快、后 uniform 续训覆盖更干净区域）；(3) 数据空间感知损失与速度空间流目标的时间结构不匹配 → VAPA（按 1/(1−t) 缩放多分辨率 STFT 距离）。
- 揭示一个机理：在 x-prediction/v-loss 框架下，主流损失对数据空间误差施加 1/(1−t)² 因子，使固定系数的感知损失在接近干净端点时被相对压低；VAPA 正是为恢复该时间权重而设计。
- 通过同数据消融证明三项训练设计各自有效，且全部辅助分支在推理时移除，仅保留单一波形原生生成器。

## Key Contributions

- First **fully waveform-native** zero-shot voice-cloning framework: no intermediate acoustic representation, no pretrained inference-time component (text encoder / speaker encoder), and no separate vocoder in the test-time path — Table 1 shows every prior "direct" system (Wave-Tacotron, FastSpeech 2s, DiffAR, E3-TTS, E2-TTS, F5-TTS) retains at least one inference dependency.
- A direct text-to-wave **training recipe** with three components matched to three named challenges: training-time Representation Alignment (REPA) with a frozen WavLM-base+ teacher; two-stage noise scheduling (logit-normal early → uniform continuation); and Velocity-Aware Perceptual Alignment (VAPA).
- A **mechanism analysis** showing that under x-prediction with v-loss (Eq. 6), the flow objective implicitly applies a 1/(1−t)² factor to data-space errors, so a fixed-coefficient perceptual loss is relatively down-weighted near the clean endpoint; VAPA re-applies a 1/(1−t)^γ (γ=1) scaling to a multi-resolution STFT distance to restore temporal balance.
- Same-data ablations isolating each component on LibriSpeech-PC, plus REPA-layer and logit-normal-mean sensitivity sweeps showing these are operating-point trade-offs rather than uniformly best settings.

## Methodology

**Architecture (waveform-patch DiT).** The noisy waveform is patchified by a 1-D conv with kernel=stride=768 samples (32 ms at 24 kHz) into patch tokens. Prompt audio enters via a dual branch: a conv frontend producing coarse downsampled features + raw waveform prompt patches. Text is character-level, refined by 4 ConvNeXt blocks (dim 512, expansion 2, depthwise kernel 7) then inserted as in-context tokens. The unified sequence is processed by 32 DiT blocks (hidden 1280, 16 heads, MLP ratio 4.0, rotary positional encoding, timestep modulation) predicting patch-wise clean waveform values; non-overlapping unpatchify maps back to samples. Total ~983.6M params. Trained end-to-end from scratch; pretrained models (WavLM) used only for auxiliary training supervision.

**Flow matching.** Conditional speech-infilling: noisy state z_t = t·x + (1−t)·ε (t∈[0,1], noise scale 0.1), velocity v = x − ε. The network directly predicts clean waveform x̂_θ (x-prediction); the masked velocity loss (Eq. 6) is L_fm = E_{i:M_i=1} [1/(1−t)²]·(x̂_θ,i − x_i)² — a reweighted x-loss. Denominator clipped: d̄_t = max(1−t, ε_t). Mask covers 70–100% of each waveform (long-span infilling).

**REPA.** Extract hidden h^ℓ from transformer layer ℓ (main config ℓ=18), linearly interpolate to teacher length, pass through a 2× (Conv1d–GroupNorm–Mish) + 1×1 Conv1d head φ, align to frozen WavLM-base+ teacher h^teacher from the ground-truth waveform: L_REPA = E_{i∈Ω}[1 − cos(φ(h^ℓ)_i, h^teacher_i)] (cosine). Teacher + head removed at inference. λ₁ = 0.0025.

**Staged noise schedule.** p(t|u) = logit-normal(μ=−0.4, σ=0.8) for normalized progress u < ρ; uniform for u ≥ ρ (switch at 240k of 640k equivalent updates).

**VAPA.** Multi-resolution STFT distance D_STFT (WaveFM-style: phase + log-magnitude + spectral-gradient + Laplacian, FFT [1024,2048,512], hop [128,256,64], win [512,1024,256]) scaled by (1−t)^(−γ), γ=1: L_VAPA = E[(1−t)^(−γ)·D_STFT(x̂_θ, x)]. Enabled only in continuation stage (u ≥ ρ). λ₂ = 4×10⁻⁴.

**Full objective.** L_total = L_fm + λ₁ L_REPA + 𝟙[u≥ρ]·λ₂ L_VAPA. CFG trained by dropping prompt-audio (p=0.3) and prompt+text jointly (p=0.2).

**Optimization/inference.** Muon (lr 10⁻³) on DiT, AdamW (lr 5×10⁻⁵, betas 0.9/0.95) elsewhere; 4× NVIDIA A800; batch 19200 patches (9600 with REPA) — update counts normalized to equivalent updates. 640k equivalent updates, 20k warmup, weight decay 0, grad clip 1.0. Inference: Heun solver, 50 NFE, full interval [0,1], EMA 0.9999, CFG 3.5, sway sampling s=−1.0.

**Evaluation.** Zero-shot voice cloning on Seed-TTS test-en and LibriSpeech-PC test-clean (1127 samples, 4–10s prompts, cross-sentence). Metrics: WER via Whisper-large-v3 (Faster-Whisper), SIM-o via WavLM-large ECAPA-TDNN, UTMOS via SpeechMOS. Same-data baselines: F5-TTS Base + Vocos, E2-TTS Base + Vocos (both trained on the same 19.4k Emilia-EN); larger-data references CosyVoice 2 (167K Multi.) and FireRedTTS (248K Multi.).

## Results & Highlights

- **Competitive with mel-space baselines on Seed-TTS test-en (Table 2).** BareWave reaches **1.75% WER / 0.602 SIM-o / 3.72 UTMOS**, giving the best WER, SIM-o *and* UTMOS among same-data systems, beating F5-TTS Base (**2.42% / 0.424 / 3.35**) and E2-TTS Base (**2.34% / 0.478 / 3.43**) on all three metrics. (The authors nonetheless note it remains "slightly below F5-TTS Base on UTMOS" in their prose — a statement not supported by their own Table 2 numbers, where 3.72 > 3.35.)
- **On LibriSpeech-PC (Table 2), BareWave beats both same-data mel baselines on all three metrics.** **2.88% WER / 0.614 SIM-o / 4.01 UTMOS** vs F5-TTS Base **3.45% / 0.416 / 3.60** and E2-TTS Base **3.32% / 0.471 / 3.69**. (Larger-data CosyVoice 2 reaches 2.51% WER / 0.659 SIM-o and FireRedTTS 2.05% / 0.659, but with ~13× and ~15× the training data.)
- **Each training-design component contributes on LibriSpeech-PC (Table 3, ~600k equivalent updates).** Starting from the basic waveform baseline (**3.52% WER / 0.471 SIM-o / 3.69 UTMOS**): +REPA → **2.86% / 0.522 / 3.70**; +late uniform → **2.93% / 0.543 / 3.82**; +refined STFT → **3.38% / 0.585 / 3.88**; +VAPA → **3.52% / 0.610 / 3.97**. REPA gives the largest single jump (SIM-o +0.051), and VAPA adds +0.025 SIM-o / +0.09 UTMOS over refined STFT alone.
- **REPA improves data efficiency throughout training (Fig. 4).** Under matched seen-data budgets on LibriSpeech-PC, REPA yields stable improvement in SIM-o and reaches better WER/UTMOS operating points — the effect is largest for speaker similarity, consistent with REPA's role as a speech prior.
- **Input-side representation hierarchy helps (Table 4).** Adding a downsampled prompt stream to raw prompt patches improves **WER 3.45% → 3.32%** and **SIM-o 0.416 → 0.471**.
- **Operating-point trade-offs, not free lunches (Figs. 6–8).** Deeper REPA teacher layers reduce WER but layer 10 gives the best SIM-o; lower logit-normal mean μ improves WER while higher μ improves SIM-o/UTMOS; stronger CFG lowers WER but SIM-o peaks at CFG 3.5 then declines — the paper selects CFG 3.5 as the balance point.

## Critical Assessment

- **BareWave is larger and more expensive than the mel baselines it is compared against.** The waveform-native DiT has **983.6M parameters** versus F5-TTS/E2-TTS Base at **335.8M / 333M (+ 13.5M Vocos)** — roughly 3× the model size — and the paper itself flags (§Limitations) that this "leads to higher training and inference cost," with compression/distillation left to future work. So the "competitive with mel baselines" claim holds only under a parameter-and-compute asymmetry favoring BareWave.
- **The headline WER win on Seed-TTS test-en is real but the UTMOS discussion is internally inconsistent.** BareWave's 1.75% WER vs F5-TTS 2.42% / E2-TTS 2.34% is a clear gain, and its 3.72 UTMOS actually *beats* both same-data mel baselines (3.35 / 3.43) in Table 2 — yet the prose claims BareWave "remain[s] slightly below F5-TTS Base on UTMOS," contradicting the table. The authors attribute a UTMOS lag to "the inherent difficulty of single-stage waveform rendering without a dedicated vocoder," but that lag is not visible against the same-data baselines, only against the larger-data CosyVoice 2 (4.15) — so the naturalness-cost claim is mismatched to the comparison actually reported.
- **The ablation in Table 3 is unorthodox: adding the refined STFT *raises* WER (2.93% → 3.38%) before VAPA brings it to 3.52%.** The full recipe's final WER (3.52%) is actually *worse* than the REPA-only (2.86%) or +late-uniform (2.93%) WER on LibriSpeech-PC, even as SIM-o/UTMOS improve — so the perceptual-refinement stages trade intelligibility for speaker similarity/naturalness, and the "each component helps" framing understates this WER regression.
- **REPA batch size is halved (19200 → 9600) due to memory, with "equivalent updates" used to compensate.** The paper normalizes update counts to matched seen-data, but a halved batch size changes gradient noise and can itself affect optimization; the comparison is not perfectly controlled, and the REPA gain could partly reflect batch-size effects rather than alignment alone.
- **Larger-data references (CosyVoice 2, FireRedTTS) are not same-data.** They are included only as external benchmarks with their own (much larger) training data, so BareWave's gap to them (e.g., Seed-TTS WER 1.75% vs FireRedTTS 2.05% is favorable, but SIM-o 0.602 vs 0.659 is behind) cannot isolate the waveform-native design from the data-scale difference.

## Strengths

- Genuinely minimal inference graph: the first system to remove *all* inference-time dependencies (no mel, no codec, no vocoder, no speaker/text encoder) while still doing zero-shot cloning, a clean conceptual milestone for direct TTS.
- The VAPA mechanism analysis is a real insight — formalizing why a fixed perceptual loss is down-weighted near the clean endpoint under v-loss (the 1/(1−t)² factor) — and gives a principled fix rather than a tuned coefficient.
- Strong, controlled same-data ablations (Table 3) and honest operating-point sweeps (Figs. 6–8) that show where the method trades one metric for another.
- Full hyperparameter disclosure (Tables 5–6) and a public demo page make the empirical claims checkable.

## Limitations

- 983.6M-parameter generator is ~3× the mel baselines; training and inference cost is higher, with compression/distillation unaddressed.
- The full recipe's LibriSpeech-PC WER (3.52%) regresses versus REPA-only (2.86%); the perceptual stages trade intelligibility for SIM-o/UTMOS.
- REPA runs use half the batch size of non-REPA runs, so the REPA ablation is not perfectly compute-matched.
- Evaluation is English-only (Emilia-EN subset, Seed-TTS test-en, LibriSpeech-PC); multilingual waveform-native cloning is untested.
- UTMOS still trails dedicated-vocoder systems on some cuts, and the paper concedes a residual naturalness gap from removing the vocoder.

## Relevance

这篇论文与用户列出的 8 个兴趣区匹配度较低——它是一个语音合成（TTS）系统/方法贡献，核心是 flow-matching + 波形原生生成，不直接涉及 LLM 训练稳定性、推理评测、隐藏状态可解释性、计算精度误差或 SDC。若强行映射，最接近的是「Computation Reliability & Precision Errors」：VAPA 的核心是对 x-prediction/v-loss 下"数据空间损失被 1/(1−t)² 隐式重加权"这一数值/精度现象的分析与修正，属于生成模型训练中由损失空间转换引入的系统性误差及其校正。其余兴趣区基本不适用。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- BareWave's LibriSpeech-PC WER worsens from 2.86% (REPA-only) to 3.52% (full recipe) as SIM-o/UTMOS improve — could the VAPA scaling exponent γ be tuned per-metric (or a WER-aware schedule switch point ρ) to recover the intelligibility gap while keeping the naturalness gain, since the paper fixes γ=1?
- The REPA ablation halves batch size (19200 → 9600) and compensates with "equivalent updates"; would a memory-optimized REPA (e.g., gradient checkpointing on the frozen WavLM teacher) preserve the full batch and test whether the REPA gain survives at matched batch size?
- The paper cites REPA (Yu et al. 2025) from image pixel-space diffusion and WaveFM (Luo et al. 2025) for the base STFT distance — does the 1/(1−t)² down-weighting analysis generalize to other x-prediction/v-loss generative models (e.g., the pixel-space image diffusions DiP/PixelDiT it cites), suggesting VAPA-style scaling is broadly applicable beyond TTS?
- With 983.6M params (~3× the mel baselines), is the waveform-native win attributable to the architecture/training recipe or partly to scale — i.e., would a 333M waveform-native BareWave still beat F5-TTS Base, or does removing the vocoder require the parameter headroom?
