Automated IEP Generation from Traditional Chinese Parent-Teacher Interviews via Corpus-Grounded Feature Diffusion

["Kuanlin Chen", "Cheng-En Ou"]

arXiv: 2606.09603

Date: 2026-06-08

Tags: ["Traditional-Chinese-NLP", "Low-Resource", "Special-Education", "QLoRA", "Grammar-Constrained-Decoding", "Synthetic-Data", "LLM-as-Judge"]

一句话总结： 本文提出 Corpus-Grounded Feature Diffusion (CGFD) 低资源流程，用 25 个双专家种子访谈扩散出 582 条样本对 Breeze-7B 做 QLoRA 微调，在繁体中文 IEP 生成的 n=10 留出集上 BERTScore F1=0.779，超过 GPT-5.4(0.726) 等云端大模型；且发现 Grammar-Constrained Decoding 在繁体中文 token 预算下适得其反，no-GCD 路径在 55 样本压力集上 100% schema 通过、中位延迟低 34%。
TL;DR: On the n=10 Traditional-Chinese IEP formal hold-out, Breeze-7B+QLoRA (no-GCD) reaches BERTScore F1 = 0.779, beating zero-shot GPT-5.4 (0.726), DeepSeek-V3.2 (0.703), Gemini-3-Flash-Preview (0.703) and Llama-4-Maverick (0.700), while running fully air-gapped; on the n=55 schema stress set the no-GCD path hits 100% schema pass at P50 latency 77.3 s vs GCD's 92.7% pass at 117.2 s — an unexpected finding that GCD is counterproductive under Traditional Chinese token budgets.

核心贡献

  • 提出 CGFD 六阶段低资源微调流程：种子过采 → τ 阈值选择 → 双专家标注 → FeatureProfile 提取 → 多样性扩散 → QLoRA 微调 + 约束推理，从 25 个种子扩散出 567 条有效样本（582 条训练集）
  • 反模式坍缩设计：archetype×speaker×rationalization 三层配额 (8×5×12) + 六层检测（prompt-leak / model-artifact / 重复家长/教师开场 / 过采样 n-gram / 成对 Jaccard ≥0.30）+ 硬/软双重生成，在 80 条种子上达到 Distinct-4=0.787、Self-BLEU=0.618
  • 专家裁决的 AI 预标注工作流：GPT-5.5 生成 IEP 字段草稿，专家从"空白标注"转为"字段级复审裁决"，并报告接受率/纠正率/时间节省
  • 反直觉发现：Grammar-Constrained Decoding 在繁体中文 token 预算下适得其反——no-GCD 路径在 55 样本压力集 100% schema 通过，中位延迟比 GCD 低 34%
  • 交叉供应商评估契约（GPT-5.4 生成 / Kimi-K2.6 预筛 / DeepSeek-V3.2 与 Llama-4 做零样本基线）+ 双专家金标 + centered-DBG 校准，审计 LLM-as-Judge 自偏好偏置

Key Contributions

  • CGFD six-stage low-resource fine-tuning pipeline (seed overproduction → τ-threshold selection → dual-expert annotation → FeatureProfile extraction → diversity diffusion → QLoRA + constrained inference) producing 567 valid samples from 25 seeds (582-sample train set)
  • Anti-mode-collapse design: archetype×speaker×rationalization three-layer quota (8×5×12) plus six-layer detection and hard/soft dual-regeneration, reaching Distinct-4 = 0.787 and Self-BLEU = 0.618 on 80 seeds
  • Expert-adjudicated AI pre-annotation workflow converting expert labor from blank-page labeling to field-level review (acceptance/correction/time-reduction reported)
  • Counter-intuitive finding: Grammar-Constrained Decoding (GCD) is counterproductive under Traditional Chinese token budgets — no-GCD reaches 100% schema pass at 34% lower median latency
  • Cross-vendor evaluation contract (GPT-5.4 generation, Kimi-K2.6 pre-screening, DeepSeek-V3.2/Llama-4 as baselines) with dual-expert gold and centered-DBG to audit LLM-as-Judge self-preference bias

Methodology

Task & target schema. Transform Traditional-Chinese parent-teacher interview transcripts into a hierarchical SMART Goal Ladder compliant with Article 31 of Taiwan's Special Education Act and the 114th Academic Year Preschool IEP Form, output as schema-constrained JSON.

Six-stage CGFD pipeline.

  1. Seed overproduction: GPT-5.4 generates 80 candidate transcripts under an 8×5×12 archetype×speaker×rationalization quota; Kimi-K2.6 pre-screens (80/80 passed: 65 score-5, 15 score-4).
  2. τ-threshold selection: 25 top samples (15 train + 10 hold-out test) selected via τ threshold with flag-aware score caps.
  3. Dual-expert annotation: two experts score; inter-rater reliability via Cohen's weighted κ and Krippendorff's α.
  4. FeatureProfile extraction: sentence length, structure, quantification templates extracted from seeds.
  5. Diversity diffusion: FeatureProfile injected into LLM prompts with Verbalized-Sampling-style diversity control; 15 expert gold seeds as anchors; 585 targeted → 567 valid diffusion samples → 582-sample training set.
  6. QLoRA fine-tuning + constrained inference: Breeze-7B fine-tuned with 4-bit QLoRA (LoRA adapter ≈160 MB) on a single RTX 3060 Ti (12 GB VRAM); inference optionally enforces a Pydantic-derived token mask (Grammar-Constrained Decoding, GCD) vs free-form + post-hoc JSON parsing (no-GCD).

Cross-vendor evaluation. Staggered vendors to mitigate self-preference bias: GPT-5.4 (OpenAI, generation), Kimi-K2.6 (Moonshot, pre-screening), DeepSeek-V3.2 (DeepSeek, baseline A), Llama-4-Maverick (Meta, baseline B), Breeze-7B+QLoRA (MediaTek, system under test), dual expert gold. Centered-DBG (Eqs. 1–2) decouples self-preference from general-strictness bias. Metric: BERTScore F1 with bert-base-chinese, averaged over three generative fields (primary concern raw quote, annual goal text, semester goals best-match).

Hardware. 3-tier local setup; fine-tuning on RTX 3060 Ti 12 GB.

Results & Highlights

  • Main result (Table III, n=10 hold-out): Breeze-7B+QLoRA no-GCD BERTScore F1 = 0.779, exceeding GPT-5.4 zero-shot (0.726), DeepSeek-V3.2 (0.703), Gemini-3-Flash-Preview (0.703), Llama-4-Maverick (0.700); Breeze-7B+QLoRA with GCD scores 0.752 (also beats all API baselines), and zero-shot Breeze-7B scores 0.653 — so QLoRA alone adds +9.9 pp (0.752 vs 0.653).
  • GCD counterproductivity (Table IV, n=55 stress set): no-GCD achieves 100% schema pass at P50 latency 77.3 s / P95 94.3 s, while GCD achieves only 92.7% pass at P50 117.2 s / P95 343.6 s — no-GCD is both more reliable and ~34% faster at the median.
  • Seed diversity (Table II, n=80): archetype entropy 2.957 (max log2 8 = 3.000), speaker entropy 2.293 (max 2.322), rationalization entropy 3.209 (max 3.585); Distinct-4 = 0.787, Self-BLEU = 0.618 — indicating controlled stylistic diversity without collapse.
  • Failure mode localized: GCD's 4/55 stress-set failures are all in the mid-to-long transcript range and share a single EOF-truncation mode; the planned fix is raising max_new_tokens to ≥3072.
  • Per-field (Fig. 4): the fine-tuned local model leads on the two generative fields (annual goal, semester goals); Llama-4-Maverick and Gemini-3-Flash-Preview lead marginally on the extractive primary-concern-quote field.

Critical Assessment

  • Tiny formal hold-out (n=10): all headline BERTScore F1 numbers (0.779 vs 0.726/0.703/0.700) are computed on just 10 samples with no reported confidence intervals or significance test — a 5 pp gap over 10 samples is well within plausible noise, so "exceeds GPT-5.4" is a point-estimate claim, not a statistically established one.
  • GCD conclusion rests on a single fixable bug: the 4/55 GCD failures all share one EOF-truncation mode and the authors state raising max_new_tokens to ≥3072 is the planned fix — meaning the "GCD is counterproductive" headline may reverse once that parameter is set, yet the paper reports no re-run with the fix.
  • Data-side ablations show no observable effect: A1 (exemplars=0 vs 5) and A2 (FeatureProfile on/off) yield Distinct-N/Self-BLEU differences ≤1.8 pp and 100% schema compliance in all configs — so two of the four core CGFD design choices cannot be empirically distinguished from the baseline on the available metrics, weakening the ablation story.
  • Evaluation circularity risk: GPT-5.4 is both the seed/diffusion generator and an evaluated baseline; although the cross-vendor + centered-DBG contract is designed to audit self-preference, the paper notes "full SPB quantification awaits cross-model judge/gold score collection in Phase E" — so the self-preference-bias audit is incomplete in this submission.
  • Self-BLEU 0.618 is moderate, not low: the diversity claim is supported by Distinct-4=0.787, but Self-BLEU 0.618 indicates non-trivial intra-batch repetition; whether this is acceptable for IEP style fidelity is not separately validated by human ratings.

Strengths

  • Genuinely low-resource: only 25 seeds → 582 training samples via a well-specified diffusion pipeline, fine-tuned on a single 12 GB consumer GPU
  • Privacy-preserving air-gapped deployment is a real engineering fit for FERPA/IDEA-regulated special-education data
  • Honest, detailed ablation that openly reports the GCD counter-finding and pinpoints its single failure mode
  • Cross-vendor staggered-judge design is a thoughtful attempt to control LLM-as-Judge self-preference bias

Limitations

  • n=10 formal hold-out with no CIs/significance — headline gaps are statistically unproven
  • GCD "counterproductive" finding may be an artifact of an unraised max_new_tokens; no re-run with the fix
  • Data-side ablations (exemplars, FeatureProfile) show no observable metric effect — their contribution is undemonstrated
  • Self-preference-bias audit incomplete ("awaits Phase E")
  • Domain-specific (Traditional Chinese IEP); generalization to other low-resource domains claimed but not tested
  • No public code/data link in the PDF; reproducibility unverified

Questions for Further Exploration

  • The headline 0.779 vs 0.726 BERTScore gap is over n=10 with no CIs — what is the bootstrap confidence interval, and is the win over GPT-5.4 statistically significant or a point-estimate artifact?
  • The 4/55 GCD failures all share a single EOF-truncation mode with a stated fix (max_new_tokens ≥3072) — does the "GCD is counterproductive" conclusion survive once that fix is applied? The paper reports no re-run.
  • A1/A2 data-side ablations show Distinct-N/Self-BLEU differences ≤1.8 pp and identical 100% schema compliance — on what metric (if any) do in-context exemplars and FeatureProfile injection actually demonstrate a measurable contribution?
  • The self-preference-bias audit using centered-DBG is described but the paper states "full SPB quantification awaits Phase E" — how sensitive are the baseline rankings to the choice of judge model once SPB is fully quantified?
