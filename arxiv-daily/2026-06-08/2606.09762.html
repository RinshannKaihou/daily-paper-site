Preserving Plasticity in Continual Learning via Dynamical Isometry

["Andries Rosseau", "Robert Müller", "Ann Nowé"]

arXiv: 2606.09762

Date: 2026-06-08

Tags: ["cs.LG", "cs.AI", "continual-learning", "dynamical-isometry", "optimization", "plasticity-loss", "neural-tangent-kernel", "AdamO"]

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09762

一句话总结： 本文将持续学习中的可塑性丧失（plasticity loss）与经验神经正切核（NTK）各向异性恶化联系起来，提出以逐层雅可比奇异值趋近 1 的"动态等距"（dynamical isometry）作为保持可塑性的任务无关代理；据此提出仅需 Gram 矩阵（无需 SVD）的等距促进正则项、一种重新激活死亡 ReLU 单元的耦合机制，以及将等距正则与梯度更新解耦的 AdamO 优化器（类比 AdamW 解耦权重衰减），在监督与强化持续学习基准上匹配或超越 NaP、ReDo、L2-Init、SN Reg 等方法。
TL;DR: The authors tie plasticity loss in continual learning to anisotropy of the empirical Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) and argue that approximate dynamical isometry (layer-wise Jacobian singular values ≈ 1) is a principled task-agnostic surrogate for preserving plasticity (Definition 2.1; plasticity gap = L_τ(f_θ) − L*_F,τ). They revisit almost-everywhere isometric networks (GroupSort/MaxMin) that remain universal Lipschitz approximators, propose an efficient isometry-promoting regularizer R_iso = ‖W_ℓᵀW_ℓ − I‖²_F that needs only Gram products (no SVD) and a novel dead-ReLU revival mechanism (coupling inactive weight rows to active ones), and introduce AdamO which decouples the isometry step (−η_iso·λ·r_t) from the Adam moment-based update (analogous to AdamW). On Random-Label Memorization (CIFAR-10), Pixel-Permutation (MNIST/CIFAR-10, 1000 tasks), and Label-Shuffled CIFAR-100 supervised benchmarks (Fig. 1), AdamO (ReLU) and AdamO (MaxMin) maintain higher validation/training accuracy across 40000 epochs than Baseline, ReDo, L2-Init, SN Reg, and NaP; on continual MinAtar (PPO, 4 games × 20 cycles × 15M steps, 1.2B total, 8 seeds, Fig. 2) the PPO baseline loses the ability to learn over cycles while AdamO+ReLU and AdamO+GS maintain consistent performance throughout; on continual Octax (8 games × 3 cycles × 5M steps, Fig. 17) AdamO+GroupSort/ReLU achieves superior cumulative rewards. Prior plasticity methods are reinterpreted as targeting only partial isometry: NaP keeps only the mean squared singular value constant, SN Reg only σ_max→1.

核心贡献

  • 建立可塑性-NTK-动态等距的理论桥梁：将可塑性定义为相对于可达最优损失的"可塑性间隙"（plasticity gap = L_τ(f_θ) − L*_F,τ），证明在任务/输入无关的各向同性先验下，动态等距网络的 NTK 在期望上趋于各向同性，因此保持动态等距是保持可塑性的原则性、任务无关代理。
  • 重新审视几乎处处等距的网络（GroupSort/MaxMin）：证明逐层等距与通用 Lipschitz 函数逼近兼容，打破"严格动态等距只能在坍缩为线性（正交）网络时实现"的限制，使近动态等距可与富有表达力的非线性表征共存。
  • 提出仅需 Gram 矩阵、无需 SVD 的等距促进正则项 R_iso = ‖W_ℓᵀW_ℓ − I‖²_F，并识别其重新激活死亡 ReLU 单元的新机制（正交性惩罚耦合各权重行，当活跃神经元子集移动时会带动死亡单元旋转回正预激活区）。
  • 提出 AdamO 优化器，以 AdamW 解耦权重衰减的方式将等距正则步（−η_iso·λ·r_t）与基于 Adam 矩阵的自适应梯度步解耦，避免任务梯度与正则梯度在自适应预条件器中混合导致的尺度扭曲。
  • 用动态等距视角统一重新解释已有可塑性方法：NaP 仅保持均方奇异值恒定（平均梯度尺度），SN Reg 仅约束 σ_max→1（最大奇异值），二者都只触及等距的"部分度量"。

Key Contributions

  • Plasticity–NTK–dynamical-isometry bridge. Defines plasticity via the plasticity gap ∆_τ(θ)=L_τ(f_θ)−L*_F,τ (loss relative to the optimal achievable loss within the function class, separating plasticity from expressivity), and shows that under a task/input-agnostic isotropic prior, a dynamically isometric network's empirical NTK concentrates near isotropy in expectation — so maintaining dynamical isometry is a principled task-agnostic target for plasticity.
  • Almost-everywhere isometric expressive networks. Revisits GroupSort/MaxMin networks (Anil et al. 2019) that are isometric almost-everywhere yet remain universal Lipschitz function approximators, establishing that near-dynamical isometry is compatible with expressive nonlinear representations (not only linear/orthogonal networks).
  • Efficient isometry-promoting regularizer + dead-ReLU revival. R_iso=‖W_ℓᵀW_ℓ−I‖²_F requires only Gram products (no SVD); identifies a novel mechanism by which the orthogonality penalty couples inactive weight rows to active ones, rotating dead ReLU units back toward positive pre-activations as the active subspace moves (not guaranteed in adversarial collapse, but a mechanism absent from purely task-driven updates).
  • AdamO optimizer. Decouples the isometry step from the Adam adaptive update (Eq. 20): θ_{t+1}=θ_t − η·m̂_t/√(v̂_t+ε) − η_iso·λ·r_t, in direct analogy to AdamW's decoupled weight decay, preventing the dense/layer-coupled regularizer gradient from distorting the adaptive preconditioner.
  • Unified reinterpretation of prior methods. NaP (Lyle et al. 2024a) keeps only the mean squared singular value constant; spectral-norm regularization (Lewandowski et al. 2025) targets only σ_max→1; both are shown to target only a partial measure of isometry, explaining why squaring the spectral-norm deviation improves performance (it approaches the full R_iso sum).

Methodology

Plasticity definition (§2.1). Plasticity gap ∆_τ(θ)=L_τ(f_θ)−L_F,τ where L_F,τ=inf_{f∈F} L_τ(f); fraction of eliminated excess achievable loss after budget t: ρ_{τ,t}(θ)=1−∆_τ(θ^t_τ)/∆_τ(θ). Plasticity PT,t is the expectation of ρ over tasks τ∼T. Focus on first-order gradient-based optimization (t = update steps).

NTK and dynamical isometry (§2.2–2.3). The empirical NTK's anisotropy governs how efficiently gradient descent updates all output directions; a finite network cannot be isotropic for all inputs, but under an isotropic high-dimensional input prior, a dynamically isometric network (input-output Jacobian singular values ≈ 1) has an NTK concentrating near isotropy in expectation. Layer-wise isometry (each J_k(x) isometric) is strictly stronger than end-to-end isometry and is the principled surrogate.

Isometry-promoting regularization (§4). For a layer z_ℓ=W_ℓh_{ℓ−1}+b_ℓ with elementwise nonlinearity, the layer Jacobian factorizes as J_ℓ(x)=D_ℓ(x)W_ℓ (D_ℓ the activation-derivative diagonal). Without architectural control over D_ℓ, the best general-purpose target is W_ℓ (pseudo-)orthogonal (condition number κ(W_ℓ)=1). The training objective adds ℓ_R·R_iso where R_iso=‖W_ℓᵀW_ℓ−I‖²_F = Σ_i(σ_i(W_ℓ)²−1)², computed via Gram products only (no SVD); cost O(d_ℓ·d²_{ℓ−1}) per layer, ≈ a few percent wall-clock + ≈4–5% memory in experiments.

Dead-ReLU revival (§4.3). Even when a weight row w_i has zero task gradient (dead unit), the Gram-deviation penalty generally still produces a nonzero update unless w_i is already unit-norm and orthogonal; as active neurons move under the task loss, the penalty rotates inactive w_i to maintain an approximately orthonormal set, which can move dead units back into positive-pre-activation regions.

AdamO (§4.4). Moments built from task gradient g_t=∇_θ ℓ_t(θ) only (not the regularizer gradient r_t=∇_θ(ℓ_R·R_iso)); update θ_{t+1}=θ_t−η·m̂_t/√(v̂_t+ε)−η_iso·λ·r_t. η_iso defaults to η (the base learning rate) but is tunable. This decoupling prevents the dense, layer-coupled regularizer from rescaling task updates inside the adaptive preconditioner.

Architectural components evaluated (§6). (i) Strictly orthogonal linear operators via differentiable Newton–Schulz (7 iterations/matrix, spectral guardrailing with 1 power iteration, raw weights re-orthogonalized every 10000 steps); (ii) soft-orthogonal operators regularized via AdamO; (iii) GroupSort/MaxMin activations (isometric almost-everywhere); (iv) ReLU activations (assessing the revival mechanism). All weights use orthogonal initialization.

Supervised benchmarks (§6.1). Random-Label Memorization (CIFAR-10, 256 epochs/task, 200 tasks, batch 128, reports training accuracy — pure optimization plasticity); Pixel-Permutation (MNIST/CIFAR-10, 40 epochs/task, 1000 tasks, batch 250, validation accuracy); Label-Shuffled CIFAR-100 (40 epochs/task, 1000 tasks, batch 250). MLPs: depth 4, hidden 512, Adam/AdamO lr 1e-4. CNNs: 3 conv layers × 32 channels (3×3, strides [1,1,2]) + 2 FC hidden 256, lr 1e-3. 8 seeds/method. AdamO regularization strength λ=1e-3; NaP: LayerNorm fixed scale (√2 or 1)/offset 0, project every 1000–10000 steps; ReDo τ=0.1 every 1000 steps batch 64; L2-init λ=1e-3; SN Reg λ=1e-3, 1 power iteration.

RL benchmarks (§6.2). Continual MinAtar (PPO, MLP 64-64-64-64, orthogonal init √2, 4 games Breakout/Asterix/SpaceInvaders/Freeway, 20 cycles, 15M steps/game, 1.2B total, 8 seeds, random channel permutation at each switch; PPO: 2048 parallel envs, rollout 128, 4 epochs, 128 minibatches, lr 2.5e-4 constant, GAE λ=0.95, clip 0.2, entropy 0.01, value coef 0.5). Continual Octax (CNN shared backbone, 8 games brix/submarine/filter/tank/blinky/missile/ufo/wipe-off, 3 cycles, 5M steps/task).

Results & Highlights

  • Supervised continual learning (Fig. 1): AdamO methods maintain higher accuracy across 40000 epochs than all baselines on all three benchmarks. On Pixel-Permutation (MLP, validation accuracy, 1000 tasks): AdamO(ReLU) and AdamO(MaxMin) sustain the highest validation accuracy (~0.50 band) across 40000 epochs while Baseline(ReLU), ReDo, L2-Init, SN Reg, and NaP decay substantially; on Random-Label Memorization (CIFAR-10, training accuracy, 200 tasks, 16000 epochs): AdamO(ReLU), NS(ReLU), NS(MaxMin) stay highest (~0.90+ band) while Baseline(ReLU), NaP, SN Reg, ReDo decline to ~0.60–0.75; on Label-Shuffling (CNN, validation accuracy): margins are narrower due to performance saturation, but AdamO(ReLU)/AdamO(MaxMin) still lead. (The paper reports these as accuracy-vs-epoch curves over 8 seeds rather than tabulated final numbers.)
  • Continual MinAtar (Fig. 2, PPO, 8 seeds, 1.2B steps): the PPO baseline loses the ability to learn over the 20 cycles, while AdamO+ReLU and AdamO+GS(MaxMin) maintain consistent performance throughout. Across Breakout, Asterix, SpaceInvaders, Freeway, the baseline's final return collapses toward 0 in later cycles whereas AdamO variants sustain returns (e.g. Breakout/SpaceInvaders maintained at the high end of the per-cycle return range across all 20 cycles); L2-Init, NaP, WSN also shown for comparison.
  • Continual Octax (Fig. 17, 8 games × 3 cycles): AdamO combined with GroupSort or ReLU consistently preserves plasticity and achieves superior cumulative rewards compared to standard approaches across brix/submarine/filter/tank/blinky/missile/ufo/wipe-off.
  • Dead-ReLU revival (Fig. 7, Appendix B.4): isometry-preserving methods substantially reduce or reverse the buildup of Sokar-style dormant ReLU units relative to baselines, providing direct empirical support for the §4.3 revival mechanism.
  • Diagnostic metrics (Figs. 4–6, Appendix B.3): methods that preserve plasticity also maintain better-conditioned dynamics — tighter weight spectra and higher effective rank (Fig. 4), better-conditioned near-isotropic empirical NTK (Fig. 5), and more stable Jacobian spectra (Fig. 6) throughout training on the CIFAR-10 pixel-permutation benchmark.
  • AdamO is robust to regularization strength (Fig. 3, Appendix B.2): the CIFAR-10 random-label ablation over λ shows a reasonably broad good operating regime rather than a single sharp optimum (final training accuracy near-maximal across a wide λ range, dropping only at extreme under-/over-regularization).

Critical Assessment

  • Quantitative results are presented almost exclusively as accuracy/return-vs-epoch-or-cycle curves (Figs. 1–2, 17) rather than tabulated final numbers with confidence intervals, making precise quantitative comparison difficult. The headline claims ("consistently outperform", "matches or surpasses") are supported visually, and the paper does not report final-cycle mean±std tables that would let a reader quantify the margin over NaP/ReDo at the end of 1.2B MinAtar steps or 40000 supervised epochs — so the strength of the improvement over the strongest baseline (NaP) is hard to gauge precisely from the figures alone.
  • The "novelty" of the isometry regularizer is explicitly disclaimed by the authors. §4 notes R_iso has been successfully employed in single-task settings (Bansal et al. 2018; Li et al. 2019; Huang et al. 2020); the paper's contribution is extending its application to plasticity/continual learning and the AdamO decoupling — so a reader assessing single-task orthogonal regularization will find substantial prior art, and the conceptual leap is the plasticity framing rather than the regularizer itself.
  • The dead-ReLU revival mechanism (§4.3) is heuristic and explicitly non-guaranteed. The authors state the effect "does not guarantee revival in adversarial settings where D_ℓ(x) collapses on all inputs," and the empirical support (Fig. 7 dormant-unit diagnostic) is qualitative (reduced/reversed buildup) rather than a controlled ablation isolating revival from the direct conditioning effect — so it is unclear how much of the plasticity gain comes from revival versus from better-conditioned gradient transport more generally.
  • On Label-Shuffled CIFAR-100 the margins are "narrower due to performance saturation" (§6.1), which the authors acknowledge — meaning the clearest wins are on the Random-Label Memorization (pure optimization plasticity) and Pixel-Permutation benchmarks, while on the generalization-oriented Label-Shuffled setting the practical advantage over NaP is small.

Strengths

  • Principled, unified theory linking plasticity loss to empirical NTK anisotropy and identifying dynamical isometry as a task-agnostic surrogate — this reframes a collection of isolated pathologies (dead units, parameter growth, primacy bias) under one geometric lens.
  • The reinterpretation of prior methods (NaP → mean squared singular value; SN Reg → σ_max only) as partial-isometry targets is genuinely illuminating and explains the empirical finding that squaring the spectral-norm deviation helps (it approaches the full R_iso sum).
  • AdamO's decoupled design (analogous to AdamW) is clean, broadly applicable to linear/convolutional operators without requiring normalization (unlike NaP, which relies on scale-invariance), and adds only a few percent compute and ~4–5% memory.
  • Honest about the regularizer's prior single-task history, the heuristic nature of the ReLU revival, and the narrower margins on Label-Shuffled; includes a broad set of diagnostic metrics (weight spectra, NTK conditioning, Jacobian spectra, dormant units) that corroborate the mechanism rather than just reporting end-to-end accuracy.
  • ICML 2026 publication; both supervised (MLP/CNN) and RL (PPO, MinAtar, Octax) continual-learning benchmarks with 8 seeds and a preliminary transformer study.

Limitations

  • Results are curves (Figs. 1–2, 17) without tabulated final mean±std, so precise quantitative margins over baselines (especially NaP) are not extractable from the paper.
  • The isometry regularizer is acknowledged as not novel (prior single-task work); the contribution is the plasticity framing + AdamO decoupling.
  • The dead-ReLU revival mechanism is heuristic and not guaranteed; empirical support (Fig. 7) is qualitative, not an isolated ablation.
  • Narrower margins on Label-Shuffled CIFAR-100 due to performance saturation; clearest wins are on optimization-plasticity (Random Labels) and Pixel-Permutation.
  • The transformer study (Appendix B.7) is explicitly preliminary/small-scale (4-block, 4-head transformer on continual CIFAR-10 pixel-permutation); the LLM application discussed in §7 is future work, not validated.
  • No public code/repository URL stated, limiting reproduction of the Newton–Schulz and AdamO implementations.

Relevance

本文从优化理论（经验 NTK 各向异性、动态等距、雅可比谱条件数）角度研究持续学习中深度网络的可塑性保持，对应到"LLM Training Stability & Observability"（非平稳训练下的优化稳定性、可塑性丧失诊断与权重谱/Jacobian/NTK 可观测指标）与"ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations"（可塑性的函数论定义、NTK-等距理论桥梁与正交正则化的数学基础）。其 AdamO 优化器与等距促进正则化方法对持续训练与 LLM 微调的稳定性具有可迁移价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • The paper reports curves (Figs. 1–2, 17) rather than tabulated final numbers — what are the final-cycle mean±std returns/accuracies for AdamO vs NaP (the strongest baseline) on MinAtar and the supervised benchmarks, to quantify the practical margin?
  • The dead-ReLU revival mechanism (§4.3) is supported qualitatively by the dormant-unit diagnostic (Fig. 7); can a controlled ablation isolate how much of the plasticity gain comes from revival versus from better-conditioned gradient transport (e.g. by comparing R_iso on a network with artificially fixed dead units)?
  • NaP relies on scale-invariance (layer normalization) for its projection step, while AdamO's isometry regularization applies to linear/conv operators without normalization — how do the two compare in architectures where LayerNorm is harmful or absent (e.g. some RL value heads or convolutional backbones)?
  • The transformer study (Appendix B.7) is small-scale; the §7 discussion notes residuals erode isometry as their contributions grow over time — does AdamO preserve isometry in deep residual/transformer LLM fine-tuning at scale, and does it interact favorably with LayerNorm + weight decay (Lyle et al. 2024b)?
  • Lewandowski et al. (2025) regularize only σ_max→1 and find empirically that squaring improves performance; the paper explains this as approaching the full R_iso sum — is there a regime where constraining only σ_max is preferable (e.g. computational cost or robustness to over-regularization)?
