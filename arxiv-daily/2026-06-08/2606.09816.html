PTL-Diffusion: Manifold-Aware Diffusion with Periodic Terminal Laws

["Danqi Zhuang", "Jisui Huang", "Xiaoyue Xi", "Andrew Kiggins", "Xiaojie Wang", "Ke Chen", "Yue Wu"]

arXiv: 2606.09816

Date: 2026-06-08

一句话总结： PTL-Diffusion 把扩散模型的前向加噪过程改造成收敛到一族非恒定周期性高斯终态律（而非单一不变高斯），在 torus、cylinder 点云和 Olivetti 人脸数据集上以相同骨干网络显著降低了流形级别的分布匹配误差（如人脸像素级 W1 误差从 DDPM 的 0.228 降到 0.110）。
TL;DR: PTL-Diffusion replaces the single time-homogeneous Gaussian terminal law of a standard DDPM with a P-periodic family of Gaussian terminal laws built into the forward noising dynamics, derived as a periodically forced Ornstein–Uhlenbeck process with closed-form marginals and reverse posteriors. On the torus point-cloud benchmark it reduces the phase-conditioned error Ephase from 0.240 (DDPM) to 0.057 and on Olivetti faces it reduces pixel-level Wasserstein-1 (Epixel) from 0.228 (DDPM) / 0.233 (Phase-DDPM) to 0.110, beating both a plain DDPM and a Phase-conditioned DDPM under matched denoising architectures and training budgets.

核心贡献

  • 提出 PTL-Diffusion，其前向加噪过程收敛到非恒定的 P-周期高斯终态律族，而非单一不变高斯分布，把相位结构直接嵌入前向动力学（区别于仅在去噪网络中加入相位嵌入的 Phase-conditioned DDPM）。
  • 为周期性受力的 Ornstein–Uhlenbeck 型前向过程推导出闭式前向边际、极限周期高斯终态族（Theorem 1）和显式高斯反向后验（Lemma 2），从而保持标准噪声预测训练的可行性。
  • 引入"不变平均原理"（Lemma 3）及相应的正则项 L_avg，把各相位反向动力学通过平均参考律耦合，避免相位间彼此无关。
  • 在 torus、cylinder 点云基准和 Olivetti 人脸数据集上以相同骨干与训练预算进行概念验证实验，证明周期终态律在流形级分布匹配上优于 DDPM 与 Phase-DDPM。

Key Contributions

  • Introduces PTL-Diffusion, a diffusion framework whose forward noising dynamics converge to a nonconstant P-periodic family of Gaussian terminal laws rather than a single invariant Gaussian reference law, embedding phase structure into the forward process itself.
  • Derives closed-form forward marginals (Lemma 1), the limiting periodic Gaussian terminal family with phase-wise Wasserstein-1 convergence (Theorem 1), and explicit Gaussian reverse posteriors (Lemma 2) for the periodically forced OU-type forward process, preserving compatibility with standard noise-prediction training.
  • Introduces a phase-conditioned reverse model with circular sinusoidal phase embeddings and an invariant-average regularization (Lemma 3 + the shared-noise phase-average penalty L_avg in Eq. 6) that couples the phase-wise reverse dynamics through the averaged periodic reference law μ̄*.
  • Provides proof-of-concept experiments on manifold-supported data — torus and cylinder point-cloud benchmarks and the Olivetti face dataset — isolating the contribution of the periodic terminal law by comparing against DDPM and Phase-conditioned DDPM baselines under matched denoising architectures and training budgets.

Methodology

The paper is explicitly framed as a proof-of-concept study. The central design change is in the forward process.

Forward dynamics (periodic OU-type). Instead of the standard DDPM forward that contracts toward a single isotropic Gaussian, PTL-Diffusion uses the phase-indexed recursion x_{n+1} = ρ·x_n + b_{r0+n} + σ·ε_{n+1}, where 0<ρ<1, σ>0, ε_{n+1}~N(0,I_d), and (b_n) is a nonconstant P-periodic forcing (b_{n+P}=b_n). The one-step transition q(x_{n+1}|x_n,r0) = N(ρ·x_n + b_{r0+n}, σ²I_d) stays Gaussian. With the normalized choice σ=√(1−ρ²), the marginal covariance is Σ_n=(1−ρ^{2n})I_d (Lemma 1). Theorem 1 proves the law converges phase-wise to a P-periodic Gaussian family {μ_r} with mean m_r=Σ_j ρ^j·b_{r−1−j} and covariance Σ=σ²/(1−ρ²)·I_d, with W1-convergence L(x_n) → μ_{r0+n (mod P)}.

Reverse posterior and phase-conditioned reverse model. Because both one-step transition and marginal are Gaussian, the reverse posterior q(x_n|x_{n+1},x_0,r0) is Gaussian (Lemma 2), enabling the usual noise-prediction parameterization. The learned reverse kernel p_θ(x_{n−1}|x_n,n,r_n) uses a phase-conditioned mean where the phase is encoded circularly as ϕ(r)=(sin(2πr/P), cos(2πr/P)) and the network predicts ε_θ(x_n,n,ϕ(r_n)).

Invariant-average regularization. Lemma 3 defines the invariant-average reference law μ̄=(1/P)Σ μ_r as the state-space marginal of the invariant measure on the lifted phase space {0,…,P−1}×R^d. The training objective is L = L_phase + λ·L_avg, where L_phase is the standard phase-conditioned denoising loss and L_avg (Eq. 6) is a shared-noise phase-average penalty coupling the phase-wise denoisers.

Data-adapted forcing. For manifold-supported data, each sample x_i is assigned a phase r_i ∈ {0,…,P−1}; empirical phase centers c_r are centered (c̃_r = c_r − c̄) and the forcing b_r = c̃_{r+1} − ρ·c̃_r ensures the deterministic part transports centered phase centers cyclically and satisfies Σb_r=0 (Eq. 10). For torus/cylinder the phase is the angular coordinate; for Olivetti it is the angular coordinate of the first two PCA components.

Experimental setup. All four models (DDPM, Phase-DDPM, PTL-Diffusion, PTL-Diffusion w/o reg.) share the same denoising backbone and training budget. Point-cloud benchmarks use 12,000 training and 3,000 evaluation samples, 60 epochs, period P=30. Olivetti faces: 400 grayscale 64×64 images of 40 subjects, 5,000 epochs, P=16; phase from angular coordinate of the first two PCA components. Hardware: Ubuntu 24.04.4 LTS, 376 GiB RAM, two Intel Xeon Silver 4108 CPUs, NVIDIA A100-PCIE-40GB; software: PyTorch 2.6.0 with CUDA 12.4. All reported numbers are mean±std over 10 independent sampling runs.

Results & Highlights

  • Torus point-cloud (Table 1): PTL-Diffusion reduces coordinate-wise Wasserstein-1 EW1 from 0.159 (DDPM) and 0.109 (Phase-DDPM) to 0.043, the phase-conditioned error Ephase from 0.240 (DDPM) and 0.167 (Phase-DDPM) to 0.057, and the torus manifold-constraint error Etorus from 1.319 (DDPM) to 0.496.
  • Cylinder point-cloud (Table 2): PTL-Diffusion reduces the cylinder constraint error Ecyl from 0.715 (DDPM) / 0.341 (Phase-DDPM) to 0.135, while reducing the invariant-average error Eavg from 0.145 (DDPM) / 0.080 (Phase-DDPM) to 0.076.
  • Olivetti faces (Table 3): PTL-Diffusion reduces pixel-level Wasserstein-1 Epixel from 0.228 (DDPM) / 0.233 (Phase-DDPM) to 0.110, eigenface mean error EPCA-mean from 11.338 (DDPM) / 12.120 (Phase-DDPM) to 5.707, eigenface covariance error EPCA-cov from 2.956 (DDPM) to 1.517, nearest-neighbor PCA distance ENN from 9.813 (DDPM) to 7.782, and low-frequency illumination mean error ELF-mean from 10.636 (DDPM) / 11.409 (Phase-DDPM) to 5.292.
  • Phase-DDPM does not consistently help on faces. On Olivetti, Phase-DDPM is close to or slightly worse than plain DDPM on Epixel (0.233 vs 0.228), EPCA-mean (12.120 vs 11.338), ENN (10.183 vs 9.813), and ELF-mean (11.409 vs 10.636) — indicating that merely adding a phase embedding is insufficient; the gain comes from modifying the forward reference law itself.
  • Ablation (Tables 1–3). Removing invariant-average regularization gives nearly identical numbers (e.g., torus EW1 0.043 with and without reg.; Olivetti Epixel 0.110 vs 0.122), implying the dominant gain is from the periodic terminal law, while L_avg mainly stabilizes training.
  • Period sensitivity (Figure 3). Manifold-constraint errors (Etorus, Ecyl) drop sharply as P grows from 5 to ~20, then plateau; EW1 and Ecorr are much less sensitive to P. P=30 is adopted as a practical default for the point-cloud benchmarks.

Critical Assessment

  • The evaluation is restricted to small-scale or synthetic data: two synthetic point-cloud manifolds (torus/cylinder, 15k samples total) and Olivetti faces (400 images), with no large-scale image benchmark. The authors explicitly acknowledge the "need for more expressive phase constructions and larger-scale evaluations," so the headline improvements do not yet extend to datasets like CelebA or CIFAR.
  • Phase-DDPM, the key baseline that isolates the contribution of the periodic forward law, is not a strong baseline in itself: it does not even consistently beat plain DDPM on Olivetti (e.g., Epixel 0.233 vs 0.228), so the comparison overstates how much PTL-Diffusion must overcome — a fairer test would include a stronger conditioning baseline (e.g., classifier-free guidance).
  • The invariant-average regularization, presented as a central theoretical contribution (Lemma 3 + L_avg), shows essentially no measurable benefit in the ablations: PTL-Diffusion w/o reg. is within noise of the full model on every metric (e.g., torus Eavg 0.039 vs 0.038, Olivetti ENN 7.782 vs 7.326). Its empirical value is therefore undemonstrated.
  • The phase descriptor is fixed before training (angular coordinate or low-dimensional PCA embedding) and the period P is a fixed hyperparameter; the construction cannot approximate strongly aperiodic or continuously drifting distributions, and the authors concede this limits expressiveness.

Strengths

  • The periodic terminal law is a clean, well-motivated idea: it gives the reverse model a structured reference geometry for manifold-supported data while keeping forward/reverse distributions analytically tractable (closed-form marginals, Gaussian posteriors).
  • The experimental design carefully isolates the contribution of the periodic forward law by holding the denoising backbone and training budget constant across DDPM, Phase-DDPM, PTL-Diffusion, and the no-regularization ablation.
  • The mathematical development (Theorem 1, Lemmas 1–3) is rigorous, with Wasserstein-1 phase-wise convergence and an explicit invariant-average reference law on the lifted phase space.
  • Improvements are large and consistent across geometry, feature-space, and nearest-neighbor metrics on all three benchmarks.

Limitations

  • Proof-of-concept scale only: no large-scale image generation, no natural images beyond 400 Olivetti faces; FID is explicitly deemed unreliable on Olivetti and not reported.
  • Phase is fixed a priori and the period P is a global constant, restricting applicability to data with a meaningful coarse cyclic coordinate.
  • The invariant-average regularization shows no empirical benefit in the ablations despite being a core theoretical contribution.
  • No comparison to other manifold-aware or Riemannian diffusion methods (e.g., the cited Riemannian score-based generative models [6,17,18]); baselines are limited to DDPM variants.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • The invariant-average regularization L_avg (Lemma 3) shows no measurable benefit in the ablations (Tables 1–3) — under what conditions (e.g., larger P, noisier phase labels, longer training) does it actually reduce Eavg or stabilize training as claimed?
  • The phase descriptor is fixed before training; could a learned or continuously varying phase representation (rather than a fixed angular/PCA bin into P classes) extend the gains to the aperiodic or drifting distributions the authors explicitly exclude?
  • Phase-DDPM is a weak baseline that fails to beat plain DDPM on Olivetti — would PTL-Diffusion still dominate if compared against a stronger conditioning baseline such as the cited classifier-free guidance [16] or against the Riemannian score-based generative models [6,17] the paper references but does not benchmark?
  • The period P is a global constant; the discussion mentions "multiple or hierarchical periods" as future work — does a single P=16 (faces) or P=30 (point clouds) generalize, or is P heavily dataset-tuned, as the sensitivity analysis in Figure 3 hints?
