> **一句话总结：** BSLI 首次将"废水优先流感监测"建模为查询/预测/弃答的选择性决策问题——以废水为强制证据、ED/HOSP/POS/SRC 为可选延迟官方流，用贝叶斯后验维护潜在负担与可识别性，以科学门判别"人源负担陈述是否可采纳"，并用精确的成本校准 Bellman 策略决定何时查询、何时停止或弃答；在 5,933 个预测与 3,102 个源歧义回合的公开数据基准上，BSLI 在等预算下显著提升成本-性能前沿（如 BSLI-med 比 ACO 高 +0.098 macro-F1 / +0.157 EDU 且花费更少），并在源歧义下保持保守弃答。

> **TL;DR:** BSLI is the first method to cast wastewater-first influenza monitoring as a selective query/predict/abstain decision problem under source ambiguity. On a fixed public-data benchmark (5,933 forecasting episodes + 3,102 source-ambiguity episodes from CDC wastewater, ED, hospital, H5, and FluView streams), BSLI-med reaches macro-F1 0.983 / EDU 1.867 at cost 2.179, beating the strongest nongreedy acquisition baseline ACO (F1 0.885, EDU 1.710 at cost 2.455) by +0.098 F1 and +0.157 EDU while spending less; it also beats wastewater-only XGBoost (F1 0.725, EDU 1.538) by +0.199 F1 / +0.242 EDU, and matches the clean all-modality MLP reference (F1 0.936) at +0.047 F1 with ~47% less evidence cost. On the 1,375-episode H5 source-ambiguity subset (51.27% should-abstain), BSLI holds selective risk at 39.09% at 80% coverage.

## 核心贡献

- **首次问题建模**：将废水优先流感监测形式化为"查询/预测/弃答"的选择性决策问题——废水为强制证据，ED、住院、实验室阳性率、源政策为可选延迟官方流，系统须决定废水是否已足够、查询哪条流、何时弃答；核心转变是从"固定输入预测"转为"判断人源负担陈述是否可识别"。
- **BSLI 方法**：维护潜在负担与可识别性的贝叶斯后验；用显式科学门（覆盖率/源兼容性/安全性）认证"可回答性"；用精确成本校准的 Bellman 策略在有限证据格上求解查询/停止控制；并提出成本-能量乘子 λ_B 将统计自由能风险与运营证据成本统一到无量纲尺度。
- **理论保证**：证明了 Gibbs 变分形式（定理 1）、有限格上校准递归的全局最优性（定理 2）、约束 plug-in 可回答性、以及一维成本校准约简等关键性质。
- **实证验证**：在公开数据基准上，BSLI 在等预算成本-性能前沿上优于废水-only 预测器、静态工作流、自适应特征获取基线（EDDI/ACO/GDFS）与通用工具路由代理，并在源歧义下保持保守弃答。

## Key Contributions

- First formulation of wastewater-first influenza monitoring as a selective **query/predict/abstain** decision problem under source ambiguity, where each episode starts with mandatory wastewater evidence and the system must decide whether wastewater is sufficient, which delayed official stream (ED/HOSP/POS/SRC) to query, and when abstention is the scientifically defensible action.
- BSLI, a principled Bayesian method that maintains a posterior over latent burden and identifiability, certifies answerability through explicit scientific gates (coverage × source-compatibility × safety), and optimizes query/stop decisions with an exact cost-calibrated Bellman policy over the finite evidence lattice — plus a learned cost-energy multiplier λ_B that places statistical free-energy risk and operational evidence cost on a common dimensionless scale.
- Formal proofs of the core properties: Gibbs variational form of the posterior surrogate (Theorem 1), global optimality of the calibrated finite-lattice Bellman recursion (Theorem 2), constrained plug-in answerability, and the one-dimensional cost-calibration reduction.
- Empirical demonstration on a fixed public-data benchmark that BSLI improves the matched-budget cost-performance frontier and preserves conservative abstention under source ambiguity.

## Methodology

- **Problem setting.** Each episode t is a state-week summary. Wastewater influenza A is the mandatory input x_t ∈ X_0; the learner may adaptively query four optional modalities M={ED, HOSP, POS, SRC}, where each query reveals a structured record. Supervised outcomes are a four-class phase label (baseline/emerging/surge/declining) and a binary near-future alert label. Operational cost vector: ED=1.0, HOSP=1.5, POS=1.2, SRC=0.4.
- **Posterior surrogate & free energy.** Latent state ν_t=(z_epi_t (human burden), c_id_t (source support)). A frozen E5-Mistral-7B-Instruct LLM encoder embeds textual/categorical summaries; structured features standardized on training split, categoricals one-hot, week-of-year via sine/cosine; unqueried blocks zero-filled with a binary modality mask. A masked probabilistic encoder f_θ (residual MLP, hidden 128, 2 residual blocks, layer norm, dropout 0.10) outputs posterior moments (μ_t,S ∈ R¹⁶, log σ² ∈ R¹⁶); a deterministic NPN decoder g_ψ outputs phase logits + alert logit. Trained with sampling-free free energy F = −log p_ψ(y,b|H) + β_KL·KL(q_θ(ν|H)‖q_θ(ν|H_wastewater-only)), β_KL=0.02; 1000 steps, batch 64, Adam lr 10⁻³; subset sampling (p_∅,p_partial,p_full)=(0.1,0.3,0.6).
- **Answerability certificate.** ι*_t,S = 1{F(t,S)≤τ_id} ∧ G_sci(t,S), where G_sci = G_cov ∧ G_src ∧ G_safe (all binary, fixed before test): coverage checks wastewater quality/coverage thresholds and queried-stream recency windows; source factor maps source-ambiguous signals to answerable human-burden states when compatible source-policy evidence exists; safety factor is a stricter overclaim guard for source-ambiguity episodes. Terminal selective risk R_t(S) = ι*_t,S·F(t,S) + (1−ι*_t,S)·τ_abs.
- **Calibrated Bellman control.** Because R_t(S) (statistical free-energy units) and raw costs c_m (operational units) are in different units, BSLI computes robust scales s_R (of log(1+R)) and s_c (of cost vector) and a budget-specific cost-energy multiplier λ_B, forming calibrated quantities R̃(S)=log(1+R)/(s_R+ε) and c̃_B,m=λ_B·c_m/(s_c+ε). The exact Bellman recursion V_B(S)=min{R̃(S), min_m[c̃_B,m + V_B(S∪{m})]} returns the globally optimal policy (Theorem 2). λ_B selected per budget B by 1-D grid/line search on the dev split (Eq. 11).
- **Policy distillation.** The exact offline oracle (Algorithm 1) labels every (episode, subset, budget) triple; a deployable neural policy π_η (residual MLP, hidden 128, 5-way query/stop head) + answerability head a_ω are distilled by cross-entropy against oracle actions + answerability supervision (Eq. 12), with optional temperature-smoothed soft-Q targets.
- **Benchmark.** 5 official streams: CDC wastewater influenza A, CDC ED trajectories, weekly hospital respiratory data, CDC wastewater H5, FluView clinical-laboratory positivity. 5,933 main-task state-week episodes + 3,102 source-ambiguity safety episodes; train/dev/test splits fixed before model selection. H5 source-ambiguity subset = 1,375 episodes (705 should-abstain, 51.27%).
- **Metrics.** Phase macro-F1, alert AUROC, alert Brier, and EDU = F1_phase + AUC_alert − Brier_alert − ECE (post-hoc accuracy+calibration summary after cost control). Decision quality: AUBF (area under budget frontier), GRisk@80 (selective risk at 80% coverage), sequence alignment to oracle.

## Results & Highlights

- **Matched-budget frontier (Table 1).** BSLI-low (cost 0.933): macro-F1 0.924, AUROC 0.965, Brier 0.068, EDU 1.780 — beating wastewater-only WW-XGB (F1 0.725, EDU 1.538) by +0.199 F1 and +0.242 EDU. BSLI-med (cost 2.179): F1 0.983, AUROC 0.966, Brier 0.068, EDU 1.867 — beating the strongest nongreedy acquisition baseline ACO (F1 0.885, EDU 1.710 at cost 2.455) by +0.098 F1 and +0.157 EDU while spending LESS evidence cost (2.179 vs 2.455).
- **Beats full-evidence reference at lower cost.** Compared with the clean All-Mod-MLP static reference (cost 4.100, F1 0.936, EDU 1.808), BSLI-med spends ~47% less evidence cost and still improves macro-F1 by +0.047 (0.983 vs 0.936).
- **Best decision quality (Table 2).** BSLI-med attains the best budget-frontier utility (AUBF=1.867), the lowest selective risk at 80% coverage (GRisk@80=0.105, vs ACO 0.124, EDDI 0.206, All-Mod-MLP 0.106, ReAct-router 0.356), and the strongest alignment with the calibrated oracle sequence (0.599, vs ACO 0.355, ReAct-router 0.056).
- **Component ablation (Table 3).** Full BSLI: EDU 1.867 at cost 2.179. Removing the probabilistic encoder → cost balloons to 4.100, EDU 1.787. Removing the policy network → cost 0.000, EDU collapses to 1.374. Removing the LLM adapter → EDU 1.840 at cost 2.263. Removing cost calibration → cost 0.000, EDU collapses to 0.560 (illustrating the scale-mismatch failure mode of Appendix B.6).
- **Source-ambiguity safety (Table 6).** On the 1,375-episode H5 source-ambiguity subset (705 should-abstain = 51.27% should-abstain rate), BSLI holds selective risk at 39.09% at 80% coverage — a direct stress test of the answer/query/abstain interface on source-ambiguous H5 episodes.
- **Metric context.** All F1/AUROC/Brier/EDU figures are phase-macro-F1 / alert-AUROC / alert-Brier / EDU on the fixed test split of the public CDC+FluView benchmark, with cost = mean evidence cost (ED=1.0, HOSP=1.5, POS=1.2, SRC=0.4).

## Critical Assessment

- The headline "improves the matched-budget frontier" relies on a **single fixed benchmark with hand-set operational costs** (ED=1.0, HOSP=1.5, POS=1.2, SRC=0.4) and a **4-modality, weekly, finite query lattice** — the authors themselves acknowledge (Discussion §6) this "bounded scope is a feature for evaluation… but it is not the full surveillance problem," so the cost-performance advantage is not shown to survive nonstationary costs, richer source-policy signals, or live recalibration as reporting pipelines change.
- The cost-energy multiplier λ_B is the linchpin that makes the Bellman comparison meaningful, but it is **selected per budget on the development split by 1-D grid/line search** (Eq. 11) — no sensitivity analysis to dev-split size or to misspecified costs is reported, so the frontier's shape could be fragile to the dev split from which λ_B is tuned.
- The scientific admissibility gate G_sci = G_cov ∧ G_src ∧ G_safe uses **development-fixed thresholds (γ_m, L_m)** and binary factors; the safety factor π_human(t,S) and source-compatibility indicator ϕ_src are computed from the SRC block, but no human-expert validation of these gate definitions is reported, so the "conservative abstention under source ambiguity" claim rests on researcher-defined rules rather than independent epidemiological adjudication.
- All baselines use parameter-matched 1-hidden-layer 1024-unit ReLU MLPs as terminal predictors, and BSLI's posterior moments / free-energy values / gate values / oracle labels are **reserved for the proposed method** — so the comparison tests "generic tool routing" vs "calibrated posterior control," not whether a baseline given the same posterior features could match BSLI.

## Strengths

- Genuinely novel problem formulation: making source ambiguity a latent state and abstention part of the optimal policy (not a post-hoc fallback) is a real conceptual advance over generic active-feature-acquisition and tool-use agents.
- Strong theoretical grounding: the Gibbs variational form (Thm 1), finite-lattice Bellman optimality (Thm 2), and one-dimensional cost-calibration reduction are proven, and the cost-calibration ablation (EDU 1.867 → 0.560 without it) empirically confirms the scale-mismatch analysis matters.
- Auditable interface: a weekly reviewer can inspect one short trace (observed evidence, why answerable/not, why a query was/wasn't worth its cost), which is operationally valuable for public-health surveillance.

## Limitations

- Acknowledged (Discussion §6): fixed public-data, finite-modality, weekly decision setting with fixed evidence prices and a finite query lattice; real deployments need nonstationary costs, richer source-policy signals, different abstention thresholds, and online recalibration. Extension to other pathogens, richer action sets, or live operational settings is future work.
- λ_B tuned per budget on dev split; no sensitivity analysis to dev-split size or cost misspecification. Scientific gates use researcher-defined thresholds without independent epidemiological validation. BSLI-specific posterior features reserved from baselines, so the comparison does not isolate the value of the posterior representation itself. Preprint (no peer review); no code/data release stated.

## Relevance

主要映射到"**Self-Evolving Agents & Automated Research（自演化代理与自动化研究）**"——BSLI 是一个 LLM 增强的闭环监测代理，能自主决定查询哪条官方证据流、何时停止或弃答，体现代理式证据获取与决策；其次与"**ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations**"相关，因其给出变分、Bellman 最优性与一维成本校准的严格证明，并把"可识别性/科学可采纳性"作为一等决策约束。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The cost-energy multiplier λ_B is selected per budget on the dev split (Eq. 11) — how sensitive is the matched-budget frontier (e.g., BSLI-med's +0.098 F1 over ACO) to dev-split size and to misspecification of the operational costs (ED=1.0, HOSP=1.5, POS=1.2, SRC=0.4)?
- Baselines are denied BSLI's posterior moments, free-energy values, and gate values — would ACO or the supervised router close the gap (currently EDU 1.710 / 1.604 vs 1.867) if given the same posterior features, isolating the value of the representation from the value of the calibrated Bellman control?
- The scientific gate G_sci = G_cov ∧ G_src ∧ G_safe uses development-fixed thresholds — how would abstention recall and overclaim rate on the 1,375-episode H5 subset (currently 39.09% selective risk at 80% coverage) change if the thresholds (γ_m, L_m) were validated by independent epidemiologists rather than set on the dev split?
- The benchmark fixes 4 optional modalities and weekly cadence — does the exact finite-lattice Bellman recursion (Theorem 2) remain tractable, or need approximation, if the action set is extended to richer source-policy signals or sub-weekly cadence as the authors propose for real deployment?
