Do Video Foundation Models Understand Intuitive Physics? A Layerwise Probing Analysis

["Samuele Punzo", "Niccolò Caselli", "Ippokratis Pantelidis", "Francesco Massafra", "Salvatore Lo Sardo", "Mohammadreza Salehi"]

arXiv: 2606.09646

Date: 2026-06-08

Tags: ["Video", "Foundation-Models", "Probing", "Intuitive-Physics", "Interpretability", "CV"]

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09646

一句话总结： 本文用 frozen-feature probing 在 IntPhys2 与 MVP 两个直觉物理基准上逐层探测 V-JEPA、VideoMAE、LTX-Video 三类视频基础模型，发现 V-JEPA 家族的物理信息最易读出（V-JEPA 2.1 在 IntPhys2 上达 66.67% VOE，base V-JEPA 在 MVP 上达 94.03% pair accuracy），且信息集中在中间偏后层、依赖时间读出机制。
TL;DR: This is a probing study asking whether pretrained video foundation models already encode intuitive-physics structure in their frozen representations. Using linear, MLP, and temporal-attentive probes on the IntPhys2 benchmark (Bordes et al., 2025, with VOE accuracy metric) and the Minimal Video Pairs / MVP benchmark (Krojer et al., 2025, with pair-consistency metric), the authors compare three paradigms: V-JEPA (predictive joint-embedding), VideoMAE (masked reconstruction), and LTX-Video (diffusion). V-JEPA 2.1 leads IntPhys2 at 66.67% VOE vs VideoMAE's 58.82% and LTX-Video's 47.06%; base V-JEPA leads MVP at 94.03% pair accuracy vs VideoMAE's 92.01% and LTX-Video's 84.33%. The strongest temporal-attentive V-JEPA result beats its own linear probe by ~45 points on MVP.

核心贡献

  • 在统一 frozen-feature probing 协议下，首次系统对比三类主要视频预训练范式（预测式 joint-embedding、掩码重建、扩散生成）对直觉物理信息的编码能力
  • 通过逐层 + 多探针（linear / MLP / temporal-attentive）分析，刻画物理信息在网络深度上的可访问性曲线
  • 引入 frame-shuffled 与 single-frame 两种时间控制条件，验证所探测信号是否真正依赖时间动态而非静态外观捷径
  • 在 IntPhys2（VOE 准确率）和 MVP（pair consistency）两个新基准上完成评估，并对 MVP 的 VideoQA 格式做了 binary plausibility 适配以匹配纯视频冻结特征协议

Key Contributions

  • A unified frozen-feature probing protocol comparing V-JEPA, VideoMAE(-v2), and LTX-Video on two intuitive-physics benchmarks (IntPhys2, MVP) under three probe families of increasing expressivity
  • Layerwise depth analysis (probing at ~25%/50%/75%/100% of backbone depth, plus 10 noise levels for LTX) showing physics information concentrates at intermediate-to-late depth
  • Temporal control conditions (frame-shuffled and single-frame) that quantify how much performance survives disruption of temporal order
  • A best-case cross-paradigm ranking: predictive joint-embedding (V-JEPA) > masked reconstruction (VideoMAE) > diffusion (LTX-Video) for accessible physics structure

Methodology

Probing philosophy. Following Alain & Bengio (2018) and the frozen-feature paradigm of El Banani et al. (2024), the authors do not fine-tune backbones. They attach trainable readouts (probes) to frozen features and ask whether physics-relevant structure is already present from pretraining. They probe four relative encoder depths (~25%, 50%, 75%, 100% of backbone layers); e.g., for V-JEPA 2.1's 48-layer ViT-Gigantic they extract from layers 12, 24, 36, 48. For the diffusion LTX-Video they additionally probe 10 noise levels (0.1–1.0) to trace the denoising trajectory.

Models (largest available checkpoints). V-JEPA family: V-JEPA (ViT-H/16), V-JEPA 2 (ViT-G/16), V-JEPA 2.1 (ViT-Gigantic/16), all 16-frame clips at 384×384, patch 16, tubelet 2. VideoMAE family: VideoMAE (ViT-H/16) and VideoMAE-v2 (ViT-G/16), 16 frames at 224×224. LTX-Video: ltxv_13b_0_9_8 distilled, a 13B DiT with 48 transformer blocks, 16 frames at 224×224.

Probes. (1) Linear probe: single layer mapping pooled clip embedding to output. (2) MLP probe: hidden layers with GeLU + LayerNorm, config selected on validation via Optuna search. (3) Temporal attentive probe: operates on the token-embedding sequence (not pooled), one self-attention + one cross-attention layer, 16 heads. Linear/MLP probes use average-pooled spatio-temporal token embeddings (no CLS, for cross-architecture comparability).

Datasets / metrics. IntPhys2 (Bordes et al.): 253 scenes / 1,012 clips, four principles (permanence, immutability, spatio-temporal continuity, solidity); each scene = 2 possible + 2 impossible clips; primary metric is Violation-of-Expectation (VOE) accuracy (a scene correct only if min(score_possible) > max(score_impossible)), stricter than clip accuracy. Split 60/20/20 at scene level. MVP (Krojer et al.): minimal video pairs (visually similar, same question, opposite answers); filtered from 54,828 raw rows to 9,886 balanced samples (4,943 plausible / 4,943 implausible); primary metric is pair consistency. MVP's VideoQA format is adapted to a binary plausibility task because video-only backbones (VideoMAE, V-JEPA) lack a language interface; probe outputs are mapped back to the answer space for pair-consistency scoring.

Temporal controls. Frame-shuffled (randomly permute frame order before feature extraction) and single-frame (repeat one frame for full clip length). Reported as signed relative change vs the main-task score.

Results & Highlights

  • Best-case cross-paradigm on IntPhys2 (Temporal Attentive probe, VOE accuracy): V-JEPA 2.1 = 66.67% (best), V-JEPA = 56.86%, V-JEPA 2 = 58.82%, VideoMAE = 58.82%, VideoMAE-v2 = 15.69% (suspected instability outlier), LTX-Video = 47.06%. The V-JEPA family leads; the diffusion LTX lags by >19 points on IntPhys2 and ~10 points on MVP.
  • Best-case cross-paradigm on MVP (Temporal Attentive probe, pair accuracy): V-JEPA = 94.03% (best), V-JEPA 2 = 93.33%, V-JEPA 2.1 = 93.73%, VideoMAE = 92.01%, VideoMAE-v2 = 91.10%, LTX-Video = 84.33%. V-JEPA edges VideoMAE by ~2 points and LTX by ~10 points.
  • Probe expressivity matters most on MVP (V-JEPA family): pair consistency rises from 48.74% / 47.32% / 43.88% (linear) to 87.26% / 86.75% / 70.78% (MLP) to 94.03% / 93.33% / 93.73% (temporal attentive) for V-JEPA / V-JEPA 2 / V-JEPA 2.1 — roughly a 45-point jump from linear to attentive readout, indicating MVP physics signal is not linearly decodable.
  • Temporal controls on MVP (MLP probe, relative drop): frame-shuffling reduces pair consistency by −39% to −61%, single-frame repetition by −86% to −100%, confirming MVP signal genuinely depends on multi-frame temporal evidence. LTX-Video under the attentive probe collapses worst (−96% shuffle, −97% single-frame).
  • IntPhys2 shuffle anomaly: V-JEPA temporal-attention and V-JEPA 2.1 linear show exactly 0.00% relative drop under frame-shuffling, implying these probes partly exploit order-insensitive plausibility cues / dataset biases rather than causal physics trajectories — a caveat on IntPhys2 reliability.

Critical Assessment

  • Confounded paradigm comparison: The authors explicitly acknowledge they do not isolate pretraining objective from confounds of scale, recency, and training setup — V-JEPA 2.1 is ViT-Gigantic while VideoMAE is ViT-H and LTX is a 13B generation model trained for a different purpose, so the "V-JEPA paradigm wins" claim conflates objective with size/architecture. The paper's own conclusion names this as the key next step.
  • Instability outlier left in the table: VideoMAE-v2's 15.69% IntPhys2 attentive-probe score is flagged as a likely training-instability artifact but still appears in the headline comparison, slightly distorting the cross-paradigm ranking; the authors note a +62.50% anomalous jump for the same config under the single-frame control, undermining its reliability.
  • IntPhys2 VOE reliability undercut by controls: The 0.00% shuffle drop for two V-JEPA configs means part of the headline IntPhys2 VOE performance comes from order-insensitive cues, weakening the "genuine physical reasoning" interpretation the paper leans on — a non-trivial internal tension.
  • MVP adaptation changes the task: Converting MVP from text-conditioned VideoQA to a binary plausibility task (with manual yes/no filtering from 54,828 → 9,886 samples) is necessary for frozen-feature probing but means results are not directly comparable to the original benchmark leaderboard numbers reported by V-JEPA 2 / 2.1 in their text-conditioned setting.

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated probing design with three probe expressivity levels and two complementary physics benchmarks (one shortcut-aware)
  • The frame-shuffled and single-frame controls are exactly the right diagnostic for distinguishing genuine temporal sensitivity from static-appearance shortcuts — and they reveal real differences between MVP (genuinely temporal) and IntPhys2 (partly order-insensitive)
  • Honest reporting of the 0.00% shuffle drop anomaly and the VideoMAE-v2 instability, rather than hiding inconvenient results
  • Layerwise depth analysis plus a denoising-trajectory analysis for LTX adds genuine mechanistic insight

Limitations

  • Comparison is fundamentally correlational across heterogeneous models; cannot attribute the V-JEPA advantage to the predictive objective per se
  • Probing measures information accessibility, not whether the model uses it — frozen-feature probe performance does not establish that the backbone "understands" physics in any functional sense
  • IntPhys2 has only 253 scenes (51 test scenes), so VOE accuracy is computed over a small sample and is sensitive to per-scene errors, as the authors themselves note
  • No language-conditioned evaluation, so results do not transfer directly to video-LLM physical reasoning settings

Relevance

  • 主要关联 LLM Hidden States & Interpretability：虽然是视频模型而非 LLM，但本文的逐层探测方法论（linear / MLP / attentive probe + 层位分析 + 控制条件）与 LLM 表示可解释性研究同源，可为 LLM 内部结构探测提供方法学参考。
  • 其余用户兴趣区（LLM 训练/推理、计算精度、SDC 等）相关性较弱，仅作为跨模态可解释性参考。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • The paper's own conclusion flags the key open question: would the V-JEPA advantage survive if the same probing analysis were repeated on smaller, scale-matched backbones within each family (isolating objective from scale)?
  • Given the 0.00% shuffle-drop anomaly on IntPhys2 for two V-JEPA configs, how much of the VOE signal is genuinely physics versus dataset-bias exploitation — and does the IntPhys2 design (Bordes et al.) need an order-invariance control of its own?
  • The MVP adaptation drops 54,828 rows to 9,886 binary samples; how sensitive are the pair-consistency numbers to the filtering heuristic, and do the excluded (non-binary / non-physics) examples tell a different story?
  • For LTX-Video, the noise-level sweep shows no monotonic improvement — does intuitive-physics information in diffusion features correlate with a specific denoising stage that could be targeted for representation extraction?
