Muon Learns More Robust and Transferable Features than Adam

Date: 2026-06-08

一句话总结： 本文从特征质量角度解释 Muon 优于 Adam/SGD：在 ImageNet-C 与新建的 FineWeb10B-C 损坏基准上，Muon 预训练的 ViT-S/ResNet-18/GPT-2 始终更鲁棒，层探针显示更大 logit margin（ViT-S 末层 3.29 vs Adam 3.13 vs SGD 2.74）和更高 effective rank（GPT-2 均值 3.39 vs Adam 1.86），并在一层多分量分类模型中严格证明 Muon 在等损失下 margin 与 effective rank 严格更大。
TL;DR: The paper explains why the Muon optimizer (Momentum Orthogonalized by Newton-Schulz) beats Adam/SGD — not just in speed but in feature quality. Across ResNet-18 and ViT-S on ImageNet-C and GPT-2 (124M) / GPT-2 Medium (354M) on a newly built FineWeb10B-C text-corruption benchmark, Muon-pretrained models are consistently more robust (best mean accuracy / lowest perplexity in all four panels) and transfer better to downstream tasks (EuroSAT, Food-101, Flowers-102, Stanford Cars; Alpaca/Dolly/WizardLM fine-tuning). On ViT-S the final-layer probe margin is 3.29 for Muon vs 3.13 (Adam) and 2.74 (SGD), and the depth-averaged effective rank is 16.00 vs 6.88 (SGD) and 1.86 (Adam). Theory proves strict margin and effective-rank advantages for Muon over Adam (all losses) and GD (outside an exponentially small terminal window).

Key Contributions

  • Identifies robustness and transferability as the two feature-quality advantages of Muon over Adam/SGD, across CNNs, ViTs, and causal LMs
  • Releases FineWeb10B-C, a character-level typo-corruption benchmark (Add/Drop/Key/Swap) for evaluating LM robustness — the language analogue of ImageNet-C
  • Connects the two advantages to concrete hidden-state diagnostics: layer-wise logit margin (via tuned-lens probes) for robustness, and effective rank / Top-k spectral energy for transferability
  • Theorems 5.2 and 5.3: under a multi-component feature model (Assumption 5.1), at matched loss Muon provably attains strictly larger margin and strictly higher effective rank than Adam (for all ε ∈ (0, log C)) and than GD (for ε = Ω(exp(−c√(n log n))))

Methodology

Optimizers compared. SGD: W_{t+1} = W_t − η_t G_t. Adam/AdamW: element-wise adaptive updates with bias-corrected first/second moments M_t, V_t. Muon: forms momentum M_t = βM_{t-1} + (1−β)G_t, takes its SVD M_t = U_t Σ_t V_t^⊤, and updates W_{t+1} = W_t − η_t U_t V_t^⊤ — i.e., discards singular values Σ_t and keeps only the orthogonal directions (Newton-Schulz iteration approximates this in practice).

Vision setup. ResNet-18 (11M) trained 100 epochs on ImageNet-1K; ViT-S (22M) trained 300 epochs (DeiT recipe). Robustness evaluated on ImageNet-C (15 corruptions × 5 severities, grouped into Noise/Blur/Weather/Digital), reported as Top-1 accuracy averaged over severities. Transferability: freeze backbone, train a linear classifier on penultimate-layer features for EuroSAT, Food-101, Oxford Flowers-102, Stanford Cars.

Language setup. nanoGPT-style causal LMs pretrained on FineWeb for next-token prediction. Main setting: 12-layer GPT-2 (124M); scale check: 24-layer GPT-2 Medium (354M). SGD excluded for LMs (does not train GPT-style models effectively, per Zhao et al. 2024), so only Muon vs Adam. Token-to-parameter ratio ≥ 2× Chinchilla ratio. Robustness evaluated on the new FineWeb10B-C (typo attacks); transferability via full-parameter supervised fine-tuning (same Adam fine-tuner for both) on Stanford Alpaca, Databricks Dolly 15K, WizardLM Evol-Instruct. Performance measured by perplexity.

Hidden-state diagnostics. Logit margin: train a linear probe (tuned-lens framework, Belrose et al. 2023) per layer; margin γ_ℓ(x,y) = T_y^(ℓ)(z^(ℓ)(x)) − max_{j≠y} T_j^(ℓ)(z^(ℓ)(x)). Effective rank: eRank(Z^(ℓ)) = exp(−Σ q_i log q_i) with q_i = σ_i² / Σ σ_j² on the stacked hidden-state matrix. Top-k energy: Σ_{i≤k} σ_i² / Σ σ_j². All results over 3 random seeds.

Fair-comparison protocol. Matched budget: architecture, data pipeline, epochs/tokens, and evaluation fixed across optimizers; only each optimizer's learning rate and scheduler are tuned independently (LR swept over {1,2,5}×{10⁻⁴,…,100} on ResNet-18, jointly with weight decay on ViT-S).

Theory setup (one-layer model). Class set C = [m]×[n], C = mn; each class c=(g,r) has feature x_c = [a·e_g, b·e_{g,r}]^⊤ with 0 < a ≤ b (shared block component + class-specific component, Assumption 5.1). Linear classifier W. Muon's spectral normalization yields a fixed representation imbalance ratio ρ_Muon = s/b with s = √(a²n + b²) > b; Adam's entrywise normalization gives ρ_Adam = 1 + an/b > ρ_Muon; GD has time-varying ρ_GD(t) > ρ_Muon above a terminal loss. Since matched-loss margin and effective rank are both strictly decreasing in ρ for ρ > 1, Muon wins on both.

Results & Highlights

  • Robustness on ImageNet-C (Figure 1): Muon achieves the highest mean accuracy on both ResNet-18 and ViT-S; for ViT-S it is best under every corruption family (Noise/Blur/Weather/Digital). On ResNet-18 Muon is best on Noise and Weather, slightly below SGD on Blur/Digital.
  • Robustness on FineWeb10B-C (Figure 1c,d): For both GPT-2 (124M) and GPT-2 Medium (354M), Muon yields the lowest mean perplexity and the lowest perplexity under every corruption type (Add/Drop/Key/Swap), consistently beating Adam.
  • Layer-wise logit margin (Figure 2, Observation 2): At the ViT-S final layer, Muon's margin is 3.29, vs Adam's 3.13 (+5%) and SGD's 2.74 (+20%); for GPT-2, Muon achieves larger margins than Adam across most layers.
  • Transferability (Figures 3–4, Observation 3): Muon matches or surpasses Adam/SGD on all 8 backbone×task vision combinations (EuroSAT, Flowers-102, Food-101, Stanford Cars × ResNet-18/ViT-S); for language, Muon-pretrained models yield lower perplexity than Adam-pretrained models after fine-tuning on Alpaca, Dolly, and WizardLM across both model sizes.
  • Spectral diversity (Table 1, Observation 4): Depth-averaged effective rank — ViT-S: Muon 16.00 vs SGD 6.88 vs Adam 1.86 (Muon >3× Adam, ~2× SGD); GPT-2: Muon 3.39 vs Adam 1.86. Muon also has the lowest mean Top-10 energy in both, indicating more uniformly distributed representations.
  • Theory (Theorems 5.2 & 5.3): At any matched loss ε ∈ (0, log C), Γ(W^Muon_ε) > Γ(W^Adam_ε) and eRank(Z(W^Muon_ε)) > eRank(Z(W^Adam_ε)); the same strict advantage holds over GD for ε = Ω(exp(−c√(n log n))) — i.e., throughout standard pretraining regimes, excluding only an exponentially small terminal-loss window.

Critical Assessment

  • Theory rests on a stylized one-layer linear model with a strong structural assumption: Assumption 5.1 (each class = shared block one-hot + class-specific one-hot, 0 < a ≤ b) is a deliberate simplification; the margin/effective-rank theorems (5.2, 5.3) are proved only for this single-layer linear classifier, so the strict-advantage guarantee does not directly transfer to deep, nonlinear, multi-layer transformers where the imbalance-ratio reduction (Eq. 5.2) no longer holds.
  • SGD is dropped from the language comparison, breaking the three-way framing: The headline "Muon vs Adam vs SGD" is only honored for vision; for the LLM experiments SGD is excluded because "SGD does not train GPT-style models effectively" (citing Zhao et al. 2024), so the robustness/transferability claims on the language side are really Muon-vs-Adam only — a weaker result than the abstract's framing implies.
  • Small-scale LLMs only: The language results use 124M / 354M nanoGPT models; the paper does not test whether the margin/effective-rank advantage persists at billion-parameter scale where Muon is actually deployed in production (Moonlight, GLM-5, DeepSeek-AI runs cited in the intro), so the practical relevance to frontier LLM training is inferred rather than demonstrated.
  • FineWeb10B-C is author-constructed and not yet independently validated: As a new benchmark, its typo-corruption set and severity calibration have no external baselines, so the absolute robustness numbers are hard to contextualize against prior work.

Strengths

  • Clean matched-budget protocol (fixed architecture/data/epochs, only LR/scheduler tuned per optimizer) genuinely isolates the optimizer's effect on features
  • The two diagnostics (logit margin, effective rank) are well-chosen and connect end-to-end robustness/transferability to interpretable hidden-state quantities across layers
  • Theory and empirics are mutually reinforcing: the imbalance-ratio mechanism (ρ_Muon < ρ_Adam < ρ_GD) explains both the margin and effective-rank ordering in one unified argument
  • Fills a real gap — prior Muon work focused on speed/convergence, not on what features it actually learns

Limitations

  • Theoretical guarantees limited to a one-layer linear multi-component model; deep/nonlinear extension not provided
  • LLM experiments at 124M/354M only; no billion-parameter validation
  • SGD excluded from language experiments, so the three-way comparison is incomplete
  • The paper itself names diffusion models as an unstudied model family where Muon's mechanism may differ
  • No release of code/benchmark URL confirmed in the body text read

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do the margin and effective-rank advantages (Observations 2 & 4) persist at billion-parameter scale where Muon is actually used in production (the Moonlight/GLM-5/DeepSeek runs cited in the intro), or is the 124M/354M result specific to the nanoGPT regime?
  • The theory (Theorems 5.2/5.3) is for a one-layer linear model under Assumption 5.1 — can the representation-imbalance-ratio argument (Eq. 5.2–5.6) be extended to a multi-layer or nonlinear setting, even approximately?
  • Since SGD is excluded from the LM comparison because it "does not train GPT-style models effectively" (Zhao et al. 2024), is there a warmup/curriculum that would let SGD participate, enabling a genuine three-way feature-quality comparison in language?
  • The new FineWeb10B-C benchmark has no external baselines — how do its four corruption types (Add/Drop/Key/Swap) compare in difficulty to established NLP robustness benchmarks, and does Muon's advantage survive on adversarial (not just typo) perturbations?
  • Muon's spectral normalization equalizes singular-value contributions; does this interact with training stability in mixed-precision regimes, where singular-value imbalance is itself a known failure mode?
