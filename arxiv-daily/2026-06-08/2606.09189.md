> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一种"分裂控制 + 端点分歧"的 EEG 基础模型隐私审计框架（AEDS 决策规则），在 BIOT/LaBraM/EEGPT 三个编码器与 EEGMMI/Sleep-EDF/LIMO/CHB-MIT 四个数据集上联合审计四个标准隐私端点，发现单端点审计会放行的发布仍泄漏频谱属性——跨编码器桥接攻击在所有 6 个方向上以 95% CI 下界 ≥ 0.081 跨越迁移泄漏，且 AEDS 在全部 8 个 subject-disjoint 单元为正（p<0.001），而标准防御（高斯噪声、Wiener 自适应攻击、Carlini LiRA、DP-SGD）在保持效用的条件下均无法关闭属性通道。

> **TL;DR:** The authors audit released embeddings of three EEG foundation models (BIOT, LaBraM, EEGPT) on four datasets (PhysioNet EEGMMI, Sleep-EDF, 54-channel LIMO, CHB-MIT pediatric scalp EEG) under four privacy endpoints jointly (raw-copy, membership inference, reference-set identity, DP-SGD on the head). The decisive evidence is a cross-encoder transfer audit: a single ridge attribute decoder learned from one frozen encoder transfers, via a fitted linear bridge, to every other encoder's held-out-subject split, with subject-disjoint matched-control 95% CI lower bound ≥ 0.081 across all six BIOT/LaBraM/EEGPT directions and mean gain ≥ 0.244. The audit-endpoint disagreement score (AEDS) is positive in all eight matched-CI cells (p<0.001), while a head-level Carlini LiRA membership audit reaches AUC only 0.498–0.695. No audited defense (Gaussian release noise σ∈{1,2}, Wiener-style noise-aware adaptive attacker, bottleneck, dropout, DP-SGD at ε∈{4,8}) closes the attribute channel at maintained utility — e.g., at ε≥4 DP-SGD the attribute gain stays 0.399–0.401 (sleep/wake) versus 0.401 non-private.

## 核心贡献

- 提出针对 EEG 基础模型发布嵌入的"联合四端点 + 分裂控制"隐私审计框架：同一批发布嵌入同时接受 raw-copy、membership inference、reference-set identity、DP-SGD head 四种端点审计，且每条强声明都必须通过能证伪它的分裂/控制（随机与置换嵌入控制、temporal-gap、subject-disjoint、reference-set 限定、utility gate）。
- 给出跨编码器桥接可迁移性定理（Theorem 4）：当两个编码器在属性子空间上存在非平凡投影重叠 β 时，链式 ridge 桥接攻击的 centered-gain 下界为 √(β/(1+τ_t²)) − ε_br − ρ₀；在 6 个 BIOT/LaBraM/EEGPT 方向上反解得 β∈[0.008, 0.198]，与定理一致。
- 定义端点分歧评分 AEDS = L^A_π − max{R^M_π, R^I_π}，并证明其正性充分条件（Theorem 3）；用配对种子 bootstrap 校准，8 个 subject-disjoint 单元全部为正、bootstrap 95% CI 下界 ≥ 0.122、p<0.001，把联合审计转化为可部署的"放行/阻断"决策规则。
- 系统性多模型/多数据集/多防御实证：跨 BIOT/LaBraM/EEGPT × EEGMMI/Sleep-EDF/LIMO/CHB-MIT 展示 subject-disjoint 频谱属性泄漏在高容量残差 MLP 下稳健存在；部署级属性分类（LaBraM Sleep-EDF 五类睡眠分期）在 held-out 受试上 balanced accuracy 0.509 ± 0.024（多数类基线 0.200）。
- 负面防御结论：高斯噪声、Wiener 自适应攻击、bottleneck、dropout、Carlini LiRA、RDP 账户的 DP-SGD，在保持效用的 ε∈{4,8} 下均无法使属性通道通过隐私-效用门。

## Key Contributions

- **Joint four-endpoint split-controlled audit framework.** The same released embeddings are audited simultaneously under raw-copy, membership inference (Yeom loss-based + Carlini head-level LiRA), reference-set identity (BPID@1), and DP-SGD on the downstream head; each strong claim is forced to survive the split/control that could falsify it (random/permuted embedding controls, temporal-gap, subject-disjoint, reference-set restriction, utility gate).
- **Cross-encoder bridge transferability theorem (Theorem 4).** Two encoders sharing a nontrivial attribute-projector overlap β admit a chained ridge-bridge attacker with centered-gain lower bound √(β/(1+τ_t²)) − ε_br − ρ₀; back-solved β∈[0.008, 0.198] on all six BIOT/LaBraM/EEGPT directions is consistent with the theorem.
- **Audit-Endpoint Disagreement Score (AEDS) decision rule.** AEDS = L^A_π − max{R^M_π, R^I_π} (attribute slack minus the strongest of membership and identity residuals) turns the joint audit into a per-cell release/block decision; Theorem 3 gives sufficient positivity conditions; paired-seed bootstrap gives AEDS > 0 in all eight matched-CI cells with p<0.001.
- **Multi-model, multi-dataset, multi-defense empirical audit.** Subject-disjoint spectral-attribute leakage is demonstrated across BIOT/LaBraM/EEGPT on EEGMMI plus LaBraM replications on Sleep-EDF, 54-channel LIMO, and CHB-MIT pediatric scalp EEG, with ridge/MLP/kNN/residual-MLP probes, a Wiener-style noise-aware adaptive attacker, head-level Carlini LiRA, and DP-SGD (RDP, ε∈{1,4,8,∞}).
- **Negative defense frontier.** No audited release transformation (Gaussian σ∈{1,2}, 16-d bottleneck, dropout) or training-noise defense (noise-aware adaptive attacker, DP-SGD at utility-preserving ε∈{4,8}) passes the operational privacy-utility gate — identity may drop but the attribute channel stays positive.

## Methodology

**Threat model (§3).** The victim is an EEG foundation-model service that releases embeddings/intermediate features (raw EEG windows are not released). The attacker has a shadow set of EEG windows, can train attribute decoders from released embeddings to standardized PSD attribute vectors, and (for identity linkage only) additionally holds a reference set of candidate-subject centroids. The encoder is frozen throughout.

**Audited encoders.** BIOT (EEG-PREST-16 R03 main; also SHHS+PREST-18 R03 checkpoint, and R07 acquisition), LaBraM-base (EEGMMI R03, plus Sleep-EDF/LIMO/CHB-MIT cross-dataset), EEGPT-MCAE (EEGMMI R03). Main cell: 20 subjects, 16 selected EEG channels, 2000-sample windows, per-window channel mean removal + SD scaling.

**Datasets/cells.** PhysioNet EEGMMI R03/R07 (20 subj); Sleep-EDF (10 and 13 subj, Fpz-Cz/Pz-Oz 2-channel, 2000-sample windows); LIMO (18 subj, 54 mapped 10–20 channels, 200-sample EEG epochs); CHB-MIT pediatric scalp EEG (23 cases, 16 bipolar montage channels, 2000-sample windows); a 60-subject event T1/T2 utility probe (900 trials).

**Three splits.** Window split (same-subject train/test), temporal-gap split (adjacent-window sanity check, random controls only), subject-disjoint split (non-overlapping train/test subjects — supports attribute claims only, identity is not evaluated because candidate identities are absent).

**Attribute attackers.** Ridge (fixed ℓ₂), MLP (defaults), kNN (k=5 with PSD-neighbor averaging), and a strong residual-MLP, all mapping standardized embedding → standardized PSD features; evaluated by centered correlation corrc(A, g(E)) on the test split. Operational replication gate: window MLP gain > 0.2, ≥1 simpler ridge/kNN baseline > 0.05, random/permuted MLP controls |corrc| ≤ 0.15, subject-disjoint MLP gain > 0.1.

**Centered-attribute-leakage definition.** L_attr(g) = corrc(A, g(E)) − max_{C∈C} corrc(A, g(C)), i.e., the attacker correlation minus the largest correlation against negative-control embeddings (random Gaussian or split-local permuted). The negative-control subtraction is what prevents crediting an attacker for matching marginal PSD statistics.

**Reference-set identity.** Nearest-centroid or classifier-based BPID@1 measured only when candidate subjects are in the attacker's reference set (overlap splits); never in subject-disjoint splits.

**Membership inference.** Loss/confidence-based (Yeom) and, for AEDS, a head-level Carlini LiRA (six shadow pairs, Gaussian per-target loss score, encoder frozen) reported per cell as R^M_LiRA = max{0, AUC−1/2}.

**Cross-encoder bridge audit (§6.3).** For each ordered BIOT/LaBraM/EEGPT pair, fit a ridge bridge B_λ from target embedding space → source embedding space on source-training subjects only, chain it with a source-space ridge attribute decoder, evaluate the chained attacker on the target encoder's subject-disjoint test split against random and split-permuted target-embedding controls; invert Theorem 4's lower bound to get an implied projector overlap β = (G+ρ₀)² for a consistency check.

**AEDS calibration.** Per subject-disjoint cell, compute L^A_π (paired-seed mean matched-control attribute gain), R^M_π = max{R^M_paper (conservative 0.085), R^M_LiRA (per-cell)}, R^I_π = 0 (no candidate identities), then AEDS = L^A − max{R^M, R^I}; report a paired-seed bootstrap 95% CI and a one-sided p-value against AEDS ≤ 0.

**Defenses.** Gaussian embedding noise σ∈{0,0.5,1.0,2.0}; Wiener-style noise-aware adaptive attacker pre-whitening each coordinate by s²_j/(s²_j+σ²); 16-d bottleneck; dropout; DP-SGD downstream head with per-sample clipping (C_clip=1), RDP accountant, δ=10⁻⁵, sampling rate 0.1, 200 steps, ε∈{1,4,8,∞}; privacy-utility gate requires U(θ)≥u₀ and M(0)−M(θ)≥ΔM. Five seeded splits per cell; ± values are sample SDs over those five seeds; matched-control contrast computed within each seed before aggregation.

## Results & Highlights

- **Cross-encoder bridge transfer beats matched controls in all six directions (Table 7).** BIOT→LaBraM gain 0.292 [.19,.40]; BIOT→EEGPT 0.366 [.26,.47]; LaBraM→BIOT 0.426 [.28,.57]; LaBraM→EEGPT 0.368 [.30,.43]; EEGPT→BIOT 0.435 [.29,.58]; EEGPT→LaBraM 0.244 [.08,.41] — the weakest 95% CI lower bound across all six directions is 0.081 (EEGPT→LaBraM), mean gain ≥ 0.244. Implied β values all lie in [0.008, 0.198].
- **Seed-matched high-capacity subject-disjoint audit stays positive in all eight cells (Table 4).** Weakest matched-negative-control 95% CI lower bound is 0.174 (LaBraM R03) and weakest target-permutation lower bound is 0.157 (LaBraM R03); LIMO subject-disjoint lower bounds 0.275 (matched) / 0.257 (target-perm); CHB-MIT pediatric scalp EEG 0.374 / 0.346. BIOT R03 subject-disjoint gain 0.399 [0.312, 0.487].
- **AEDS is positive in all eight matched-CI cells with p<0.001 (Table 13).** AEDS values range 0.193 (LaBraM R03) to 0.314 (BIOT R03); bootstrap 95% CI lower bounds range 0.122 (LaBraM R03) to 0.286 (CHB-MIT); endpoint-only verdict is "release" in all eight, AEDS-corrected verdict flips all eight to "block".
- **Head-level Carlini LiRA membership AUC is weak (Table 13).** Per-cell LiRA residuals R^M_LiRA range 0.000 (LIMO) to 0.195 (Sleep-EDF-13), i.e., AUC 0.498–0.695 — far below what would close the attribute gap; a release an MIA-only audit would license is blocked by the joint audit on every cell.
- **Reference-set identity is strong on EEGMMI but does not generalize to Sleep-EDF.** Embedding-centroid BPID@1 = 0.950 ± 0.020 (EEG-PREST-16 R03), 0.927 ± 0.015 (R07), 0.760 ± 0.026 (LaBraM EEGMMI), 0.473 ± 0.042 (EEGPT); but only 0.200 ± 0.014 on Sleep-EDF and 0.136 ± 0.029 (MLP-decoded) — identity is scoped to the reference-set attacker model only.
- **Gaussian noise suppresses identity but not attributes (Table 8, §7.1).** At σ=2.0: BIOT window BPID@1 → 0.260 but attribute gain stays 0.345; LaBraM BPID@1 → 0.174 ± 0.027 with attribute gain 0.313 ± 0.035; EEGPT BPID@1 → 0.166 with attribute gain 0.215.
- **Wiener-style adaptive attacker strengthens, not overturns, the negative-defense conclusion (Table 9).** On LaBraM Sleep-EDF subject-disjoint, at σ=2.0 the adaptive attacker reaches balanced accuracy 0.817 ± 0.019 vs non-adaptive 0.799 ± 0.021 (+0.018) and gain 0.308 ± 0.033 vs 0.289 ± 0.038 — noise does not restore the privacy-utility gate even when the attacker knows σ.
- **DP-SGD does not close the attribute channel at maintained utility (Table 10).** On LaBraM Sleep-EDF, at every ε∈{4,8,∞} balanced accuracy stays within 0.003 of non-private (0.909–0.912) and attribute gain is statistically indistinguishable (0.399–0.401 for sleep/wake; 0.177–0.182 for 5-class stage), MIA AUC 0.514–0.516; only ε=1 reduces the gain (0.119 sleep/wake, 0.026 5-class) after balanced accuracy collapses to 0.642 / 0.228, below the operational utility gate.
- **Deployment-grade attribute classification recovers a meaningful label on held-out subjects (§6.4).** Logistic-regression attacker on LaBraM Sleep-EDF reaches five-class sleep-stage accuracy 0.704 ± 0.025 and balanced accuracy 0.509 ± 0.024 on held-out subjects (majority-class balanced accuracy 0.200); with σ=1.0 Gaussian noise balanced accuracy only drops to 0.492 ± 0.042.
- **Bottleneck/dropout remain leaky (§7.4).** A 16-dimensional bottleneck still gives aggregate BPID@1 = 0.773; the strongest dropout setting gives BPID@1 = 0.673.
- **Raw-copy / near-duplicate checks are negative (§6.5).** Fraction of adjacent raw-window pairs with cosine ≥ 0.99 is 0.000 (no raw-copy support), while adjacent embedding cosine mean is 0.956 — a representation-stability finding, not raw duplication.

## Critical Assessment

- **The theory is stylized and explicitly not a fitted model of the audited encoders.** Theorems 1, 2, 4 are sufficient-condition parameter-existence statements; the authors do not estimate (α, σ, m, d, β) for BIOT/LaBraM/EEGPT and state the theorems "do not certify privacy, prove raw waveform inversion, imply subject-disjoint identity recovery, or provide finite-sample generalization guarantees." The β∈[0.008, 0.198] values are a *consistency check* (inverting the gain bound), not a forward prediction from the embeddings — a tight forward β prediction would require fitting an explicit attribute subspace per encoder and is left to the next round, so the cross-encoder theorem is corroborated but not independently validated.
- **The "deployment-grade" attribute classification and most defense sweeps are run on a single cell (LaBraM Sleep-EDF).** The five-class sleep-stage balanced-accuracy result (0.509 ± 0.024), the Wiener adaptive-attacker sweep (Table 9), and the DP-SGD frontier (Table 10) are all confined to the LaBraM Sleep-EDF cell — so the headline "no defense closes the attribute channel" is strongest for one encoder/dataset combination, and the DP-SGD and adaptive-attacker conclusions are not replicated on BIOT/EEGPT or on EEGMMI.
- **Reference-set identity does not replicate on Sleep-EDF (BPID@1 0.200 ± 0.014, MLP 0.136 ± 0.029),** which the paper honestly reports but which weakens any general "EEG-FM embeddings leak identity" reading — the strong identity numbers (BPID@1 ≈ 0.95) are specific to EEGMMI reference-set settings, and the subject-disjoint claim is therefore restricted to *attributes only*, a narrower finding than a casual reader of the abstract may assume.
- **Five seeded splits per cell is acknowledged as a stability screen, not a population-level guarantee.** The authors flag this themselves; with only n=5 the 95% t-intervals are wide (e.g., BIOT R03 subject-disjoint [0.312, 0.487]), so the precise gain magnitudes are uncertain even though sign and matched-control clearance are robust.
- **The Carlini LiRA is head-level only (encoder frozen), not full-encoder LiRA.** The authors explicitly defer full-encoder LiRA (retraining BIOT/LaBraM/EEGPT) to the next round because head-level LiRA is the strongest membership audit consistent with the frozen-encoder threat model; consequently the AEDS membership residual could be understated relative to a full-encoder membership attack, though they argue it would have to jump from AUC 0.50–0.70 to essentially 1.0 to close the gap.
- **AEDS mixes units across endpoints** (attribute correlation gain vs membership AUC excess vs identity residual), which the authors acknowledge; they treat AEDS as a per-cell decision-margin diagnostic and report L^A, R^M, R^I separately, but it is not a common-unit harm score and the magnitude is not directly comparable across cells with different endpoint mixes.

## Strengths

- Genuinely joint and split-controlled: each strong claim is tied to the split/control that could falsify it (raw-copy, temporal-gap, subject-disjoint, reference-set, utility gate), and Table 12 explicitly shows which wrong conclusion each standard endpoint alone would license — a methodological contribution beyond a single new attack.
- The cross-encoder bridge audit is a novel, concrete finding: a decoder learned from one frozen encoder transfers attribute leakage to every other tested encoder's held-out subjects, which single-endpoint audits cannot detect on the same embeddings.
- Defense evaluation is adversarially thorough: rather than only the same-attacker defense curves, the authors add a Wiener-style noise-aware adaptive attacker that *knows* σ and a principled DP-SGD head with an RDP accountant — and show both strengthen rather than overturn the negative-defense result.
- Honest claim scoping: the paper repeatedly blocks overclaims (no raw waveform inversion, no subject-disjoint identity recovery, no formal DP guarantee, β is a consistency check not an estimate), and reports the Sleep-EDF identity non-replication rather than hiding it.

## Limitations

- Deployment-grade classification, Wiener adaptive attacker, and DP-SGD frontier are each evaluated on only one cell (LaBraM Sleep-EDF); generalization to BIOT/EEGPT and to EEGMMI is not shown for these defenses.
- Theory is stylized and not fitted to the encoders; β values are consistency-check inversions, not forward predictions.
- Only five seeded splits per cell; intervals are stability screens, not population-level guarantees.
- LiRA is head-level only (encoder frozen); full-encoder membership attacks are deferred.
- Evidence covers BIOT-family/LaBraM/EEGPT on EEGMMI plus LaBraM on three other datasets; larger clinical corpora (TUH/TUAB/TUEV) remain future external-validity tests.
- AEDS mixes units across endpoints; it is a decision-margin diagnostic, not a common-unit privacy score.

## Relevance

这篇论文直接映射到用户的「Computation Reliability & Precision Errors」与「ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations」兴趣区——它从数学上（Theorem 1–5 + Proposition 1–3）刻画了"成员推理/身份链接有界 ≠ 属性泄漏有界"这一可靠性盲区，并用严格的分裂控制审计证明多个 EEG 基础模型在标准防御下仍泄漏频谱属性，本质上是对"嵌入发布是否构成可靠隐私边界"的可靠性审计。其 DP-SGD、LiRA 成员推理与隐私-效用前沿的负面结论也直接关系到「LLM Training Stability & Observability」中对训练期隐私机制（如差分隐私训练）有效性的评估方法论。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The Wiener adaptive-attacker sweep (Table 9) and the DP-SGD frontier (Table 10) are confined to LaBraM Sleep-EDF — does the "no defense closes the attribute channel at maintained utility" conclusion survive when DP-SGD (ε∈{4,8}) and the noise-aware adaptive attacker are re-run on BIOT and EEGPT EEGMMI, the two cells with the highest same-subject identity signal (BPID@1 ≈ 0.95)?
- Theorem 4's projector overlap β is only back-solved as a consistency check (β∈[0.008, 0.198]); the paper leaves a forward β prediction (fitting an explicit attribute subspace per encoder) to the next round — would directly estimating β per encoder pair change any of the six cross-encoder directions' sign or magnitude?
- The head-level Carlini LiRA reaches AUC only 0.498–0.695; the authors defer full-encoder LiRA (retraining BIOT/LaBraM/EEGPT) — would a full-encoder membership audit raise R^M enough to shrink AEDS, given the attribute slack L^A is 0.284–0.450 in the same cells?
- The paper cites Cobilean et al. 2025 (ECG-FM MIA) and Tonekaboni et al. 2025 (healthcare-FM memorization) as the closest concurrent biosignal-FM privacy work — does the AEDS endpoint-disagreement framework, when applied to those ECG/EHR settings, also flip a release verdict that a single-endpoint MIA would license?
- The Sleep-EDF identity non-replication (BPID@1 0.200) versus EEGMMI (≈0.95) is unexplained — is the weak Sleep-EDF identity an artifact of the 2-channel Fpz-Cz/Pz-Oz acquisition (vs 16-channel EEGMMI), and would a higher-channel sleep corpus restore identity linkage there?
