> **一句话总结：** 独立训练的同架构 transformer 的残差流基之间仅差一个 SO(d) 上的均匀随机旋转；一次 Procrustes 对齐即可恢复跨种子 SAE 重建、转向向量迁移等功能性迁移，从根本上修正了仅靠解码器余弦相似度衡量特征普适性的方法论缺陷。

> **TL;DR:** Independently trained transformers of the same architecture compute the same function but in residual-stream bases that differ by a uniform random rotation on SO(d_model). A single-batch orthogonal Procrustes fit restores cross-seed SAE reconstruction from negative explained variance to within 0.025 EV of the within-seed ceiling (toy) or 0.85–0.99 (Pythia-70m, 9 seeds). The rotation is statistically indistinguishable from Haar measure (KS p = 1.000), explaining why the standard 98% SAE decoder-cosine metric coexists with catastrophic encoder non-transfer. Steering vectors transfer in three regimes (clean / partial / inverted) determined by alignment with shared output projections, collapsing to universally inverted when no I/O is shared.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示"多态性"现象**：同架构、独立训练的 transformer 实际计算相同函数，但内部残差流坐标系统之间仅差一个均匀随机正交旋转，作者称之为"polymorphism"。
- **提出旋转假设并通过严格实验验证**：在 104k 参数的 Dyck-3 玩具模型和 9 个独立训练的 Pythia-70m 上，跨种子的 Procrustes 旋转 R 的 Frobenius 距离匹配 Haar 分布的理论预测（误差 0.1%），特征值谱与 Haar SO(d) 统计不可区分。
- **指出 SAE 普适性指标的根本缺陷**：标准 98% 解码器余弦相似度只衡量解码器几何，跨种子 SAE 编码器重建方差为负（比预测均值还差）；一次旋转校正即可恢复。
- **建立转向向量迁移的三体制理论**：共享 I/O 时分为 clean/partial/inverted 三种迁移模式，无共享 I/O 时全部退化为 inverted。
- **提出预注册的四栏操作框架**（Bar B/P/C/Pr），为机制可解释性提供可量化的验收标准。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies **polymorphism**: independently trained same-architecture transformers compute identical functions in residual-stream bases related by a uniform random rotation on SO(d_model).
- Validates the **rotation hypothesis** at two scales: a 104k-parameter Dyck-3 model and 9 independently-trained Pythia-70m seeds, showing the cross-seed rotation R is Haar-distributed (∥R − I∥_F matches √(2d_model) to 0.1%; eigenvalue spectrum KS p = 1.000 vs Haar).
- Demonstrates that the standard **SAE universality metric (98% decoder cosine similarity) is misleading**: it measures decoder geometry only, while encoder transfer fails catastrophically (negative explained variance). A single-batch Procrustes rotation restores reconstruction to within 0.025 EV (toy) or 0.85–0.99 EV (Pythia).
- Characterizes **steering vector transfer in three regimes**: clean (direction pinned by shared output weights), partial (mixed alignment), and inverted (direction primarily in rotated subspace). Without shared I/O, all directions are inverted.
- Introduces a **pre-registered four-bar operational framework** (Bar B: behaviour, Bar P: parameters, Bar C: causal patching, Bar Pr: component ablation) as acceptance criteria for mechanistic interpretability.
- Shows the same rotation account applies **across training checkpoints** within a single run (Pythia step 3k → 143k, inter-checkpoint drift covers ~50% of the SO(d) volume of inter-seed polymorphism).

## Methodology

The paper uses a two-scale experimental design:

**Toy scale (Dyck-3):** A 104k-parameter two-layer transformer trained on balanced parentheses (Dyck language) with depth-classification, token-prediction, and validity heads. Five seeds with shared frozen input/output embeddings (Cohort A) and five with fully independent initialization (Cohort B). The author writes a constructive specification of the expected mechanistic algorithm and validates it against the trained model using the four-bar framework.

**Family scale (Pythia-70m):** Nine independently trained Pythia-70m seeds on The Pile (no shared I/O whatsoever). Cross-seed analysis at 7 residual-stream sites using orthogonal Procrustes alignment on single-batch activations.

**Key analytical tools:**
- Orthogonal Procrustes: R = argmin_{O ∈ O(d)} ∥A_N O − A_0∥_F on centred activation matrices.
- SAE transfer test: apply seed-0 SAE to seed-N activations with and without rotation correction.
- Eigenvalue spectrum analysis: compare R's eigenvalue angles against Haar SO(d) using Kolmogorov-Smirnov.
- Diff-of-means steering vectors tested across three functional regimes.
- Integrated gradients for component-level attribution (attribution patching shown to anti-correlate at convergence).

## Results & Highlights

- **SAE decoder cosine matches 98–99%** across seeds at internal layers, but **naive cross-seed SAE reconstruction gives negative explained variance** (worse than predicting the mean).
- **Post-rotation SAE reconstruction**: 0.976–0.990 EV at toy scale (within 0.025 of self-baseline); 0.85–0.99 EV at Pythia scale.
- **Rotation magnitude**: ∥R − I∥_F matches the random-orthogonal prediction √(2d_model) to 0.1% at d_model = 512. KS test of eigenvalue spectrum vs Haar SO(512): p = 1.000.
- **Firing pattern recovery**: raw cross-seed Pearson r = 0.01–0.07 at Pythia; post-rotation r = 0.25–0.44.
- **Steering transfer**: with shared I/O, depth-shift transfers cleanly, sticky-invalid needs ~4× dose, closer-signal inverts. Without shared I/O (Pythia), all three vectors invert universally.
- **Cross-checkpoint rotation**: Pythia step 3k → 143k gives naive EV = −0.86, post-rotation EV = +0.73 at layer 3.
- **Parameter-level Bar P fails**: even after Cayley refinement (50% MSE reduction), max per-tensor MSE remains 280× above threshold. Weight-optimal and activation-optimal rotations sit in disconnected basins.

## Authors & Credibility

Single-author paper by Jordan F. McCann. The work is unusually rigorous for a single-author mechanistic interpretability paper: pre-registered four-bar acceptance criteria, honest reporting of failures (Bar B misses threshold by 1.74×, Bar P fails uniformly), multiple scales (toy + Pythia-70m), 9 independent seeds, and statistical tests against well-defined null hypotheses (Haar measure). The supplementary code archive supports full reproduction (~9 hours on RTX 2060 12GB). The paper candidly acknowledges all limitations (Section 10) and makes concrete falsifiable predictions for frontier-scale replication.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. The core finding—that cross-seed residual-stream bases are Haar-distributed random rotations—is stronger than prior work claiming "Procrustes alignment works well." The three-regime steering analysis and the critique of the SAE decoder-cosine metric are genuinely novel and practically important.

**Rigor:** Very high for the field. Pre-registered thresholds, multiple scales, honest failure reporting, and statistical tests against precise null hypotheses (not just "works better than baseline"). The constructive spec for Dyck-3 is thorough. However, Bar B misses the pre-registered 10⁻⁴ threshold (1.74×), which the author acknowledges. The cross-seed Bars C and Pr collapse to the same measurement by construction—a limitation the author flags.

**Reproducibility:** Good. Full pipeline provided, ~9 hours on consumer GPU. Pythia-70m seeds are publicly available.

**Significance:** Potentially high impact on SAE universality research and representation engineering. The practical prescription—fit a rotation from one batch, apply as correction—is cheap and actionable. The result changes how the community should interpret cross-model SAE comparisons.

**Red flags:**
- Only tested up to 71M parameters (Pythia-70m). The rotation account might weaken, strengthen, or change character at 10B+ scale. The author is transparent about this.
- The cross-checkpoint Pythia result is a single data point (one seed, one layer, one step pair).
- Cohort A's shared frozen I/O is an artificial setup; the author addresses this with Cohort B but acknowledges the parameter-level analysis is less tractable.
- The four-bar framework is specific to models small enough to enumerate all components; scaling to billion-parameter models requires sampling estimators that haven't been validated.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally precise characterization: the rotation isn't just "approximately orthogonal" or "well-approximated by Procrustes"—it's statistically indistinguishable from Haar SO(d).
- Directly identifies a methodological blind spot in the SAE universality literature and provides a cheap fix.
- Three-regime steering analysis gives concrete, actionable guidance for cross-model intervention transfer.
- Honest reporting: pre-registered thresholds, disclosed failures, acknowledged limitations.
- Clean experimental design: toy model for full mechanistic understanding + family-scale model for generality.

## Limitations

- Scale: only validated up to 71M parameters. Frontier-scale (10B+) replication is the critical open question.
- Cross-checkpoint Pythia result is a single data point; a full training-curve analysis would strengthen the claim.
- The constructive-spec approach doesn't scale; the four-bar framework requires component enumeration.
- Firing-pattern recovery post-rotation is substantial but incomplete (r = 0.25–0.44, not 0.95+), suggesting a second-order non-orthogonal component that boundary-pinning partially explains.
- Single author limits the breadth of experimental validation.

## Relevance

本文对理解跨模型 SAE 特征迁移和转向向量迁移提供了关键的几何解释，直接修正了当前机制可解释性研究中仅依赖解码器余弦相似度衡量特征普适性的方法论问题，对后续大规模模型的可解释性研究有重要指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the Haar-uniform rotation account hold at frontier scale (10B+ parameters, multi-billion token training), or does the rotation become structured as model capacity increases?
2. Can the rotation correction be applied iteratively across fine-tuning or RLHF stages to track how representations drift?
3. Does the rotation account extend across architectures (e.g., LLaMA vs Mistral at similar scale), or is it strictly an intra-architecture phenomenon?
4. What is the second-order non-orthogonal component that prevents full firing-pattern recovery post-rotation, and does it grow with model scale?
5. Can the rotation framework be used to define a principled "distance" between training checkpoints that predicts when interpretability tools trained on one checkpoint will fail on another?
