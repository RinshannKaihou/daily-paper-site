Measuring the Depth of LLM Unlearning via Activation Patching

arXiv: 2605.24614

Date: 2026-05-23

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.24614

一句话总结： 本文提出 Unlearning Depth Score (UDS)，通过两阶段激活修补（activation patching）因果地量化 LLM 中目标知识被擦除的机制深度，在 150 个模型、20 个指标的元评估中取得最高忠实度（AUC 0.971）和鲁棒性（HM 0.932），是目前最可靠的遗忘评估指标。
TL;DR: Proposes the Unlearning Depth Score (UDS), a training-free, causal, dataset-invariant metric that measures how deeply target knowledge has been erased from an LLM's internal representations via two-stage activation patching. In a meta-evaluation of 20 metrics across 150 unlearned models (8 methods, Llama 1B/3B/8B), UDS achieves the highest faithfulness (AUC-ROC 0.971) and robustness (HM 0.932). Case studies reveal that observational metrics like Logit Lens can be fooled by representational shifts, and that erasure depth varies significantly across prompt types within a single model.

核心贡献

  • 提出 UDS，首个免训练、因果性、数据集无关的白盒遗忘评估指标，通过两阶段激活修补量化知识擦除的机制深度
  • Stage 1（基线化）：将保留模型 Mret 的隐藏状态修补到完整模型 Mfull，识别编码遗忘集知识的层（KE layers）；Stage 2（量化）：将遗忘模型 Munl 的隐藏状态修补到 Mfull，测量残余知识的可恢复性
  • 定义 Layer Erasure Ratio (LER)，以 Mret 为参照逐层量化擦除程度，加权聚合为 0-1 的模型级分数
  • 在 150 个遗忘模型（8 种方法）、20 个指标的元评估中，UDS 的忠实度和鲁棒性均为最高
  • 揭示了观测性指标（如 Logit Lens）会被表示旋转误导，而因果干预能发现残余知识；同一模型内部不同提示类型的擦除深度差异显著（Yes/No: 0.624 vs. 人名: 0.025）
  • 提出将 UDS 集成到隐私评估轴的具体方案：Privacy = HM(MIAagg, UDS)，可改变方法排名和超参数选择

Key Contributions

  • Introduces UDS, the first training-free, causal, and dataset-invariant white-box metric for quantifying unlearning depth via two-stage activation patching
  • Stage 1 localizes knowledge-encoding (KE) layers by patching Mret hidden states into Mfull; Stage 2 quantifies residual knowledge by patching Munl into Mfull, yielding per-example 0-1 scores
  • Defines Layer Erasure Ratio (LER) that normalizes per-layer erasure against the retain baseline, weighted by knowledge-encoding strength and aggregated across KE layers
  • Largest meta-evaluation of unlearning metrics to date: 20 metrics, 150 models, 8 unlearning methods (GradDiff, NPO, SimNPO, IdkNLL, IdkDPO, AltPO, RMU, UNDIAL)
  • Demonstrates that UDS achieves the highest faithfulness (AUC 0.971) and robustness (HM 0.932), outperforming Truth Ratio (0.947), Logit Lens (0.927), and all output-level metrics
  • Reveals that observational metrics like Logit Lens can be fooled by representational rotations (reporting 0.801 erasure vs. UDS's 0.209 on the same example)
  • Shows per-prompt-type erasure heterogeneity: Yes/No questions reach 0.624 erasure in IdkNLL while person names reach only 0.025
  • Proposes integrating UDS into the privacy evaluation axis, which reshapes method rankings (NPO and SimNPO swap) and steers hyperparameter selection toward deeper erasure

Methodology

The paper proposes UDS based on activation patching from mechanistic interpretability. The approach involves three models: Mfull (trained on all data including forget set), Mret (trained only on retain set, the gold standard), and Munl (the unlearned model). The method operates in two stages:

Stage 1 — Baselining (Knowledge Localization): For each forget set example (input xi, entity span yi), hidden states from Mret are patched into Mfull at each layer l at entity token positions under teacher forcing. The degradation in log-probability ∆S1 = mean(sfull − s_ret_patched) measures how much forget-set-unique knowledge Mfull encodes at that layer. Layers with ∆S1 > τ (τ=0.05) form the Knowledge-Encoding (KE) set.

Stage 2 — Quantification: The same patching procedure is repeated with Munl as the source. If unlearning erased the knowledge, ∆S2 ≈ ∆S1; if intact, ∆S2 ≈ 0. The Layer Erasure Ratio LER = clip(∆S2/∆S1, 0, 1) measures per-layer erasure normalized against the retain baseline.

Score Aggregation: Per-example UDS is the ∆S1-weighted mean of LER across KE layers. Model-level UDS averages over all examples with non-empty KE sets.

Efficient Implementation: Stage 1 depends only on Mret and Mfull, so baselines and KE sets are cached. Evaluating new Munl models requires only Stage 2 forward passes. Teacher forcing enables single-pass computation per layer.

Validation: UDS is validated across Llama 1B, 3B, and 8B, showing monotonic ordering (full < retain99 < retain95 < retain90) proportional to unseen data fraction at all scales.

Meta-evaluation Setup: 150 unlearned models from 8 methods on TOFU forget10 (Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct). Faithfulness measured by AUC-ROC distinguishing models with/without forget set knowledge. Robustness measured as harmonic mean of quantization stability (Q) and relearning stability (R), using a symmetric criterion.

Results & Highlights

  • Faithfulness: UDS achieves AUC-ROC 0.971, highest among all 20 metrics. Truth Ratio is second at 0.947. White-box baselines diverge: CKA (0.648) and Fisher (0.712) achieve poor separation; Logit Lens (0.927) is strong but observational.
  • Robustness: UDS ranks first in aggregate robustness (HM 0.932, Q=0.968, R=0.900). CKA collapses under relearning (R=0.013). Fisher is most vulnerable to quantization (Q=0.583). ROUGE variants degrade sharply under relearning (R=0.06-0.20).
  • Overall: UDS achieves 0.951, highest among all metrics combining faithfulness and robustness.
  • Case study — Observational vs. Causal: On an IdkDPO model, Logit Lens reports 0.801 erasure (complete from layers 7-13), but UDS reveals only 0.209. Representational rotation fools the frozen decoder; causal patching recovers the hidden knowledge.
  • Case study — Erasure Heterogeneity: In IdkNLL, Yes/No questions show 0.624 erasure (the "I don't know" negation modifies deeper layers), while all other prompt types (person names, book titles, biographical, descriptive) fall below 0.049.
  • Integration Impact: Adding UDS to the privacy axis swaps NPO and SimNPO rankings (2↔3), and steers hyperparameter selection toward higher learning rates and longer training for deeper erasure.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The two-stage activation patching formulation is clean and novel. Unlike prior white-box analyses (Lynch et al., Hong et al., Guo et al.) that are diagnostic without a standardized score, UDS provides a generalizable, comparable metric. The retain-model normalization for KE layer identification is methodologically sound.
  • Rigor: Very strong. The meta-evaluation is the most comprehensive in the unlearning evaluation literature. The symmetric robustness criterion (penalizing instability in both directions) improves on the original OpenUnlearning formulation. Validation across three model scales (1B/3B/8B) supports generality. Ablation studies cover component patching locations, KE threshold sensitivity, and entity length bias.
  • Reproducibility: Good. Code and data are public. The method uses only standard model internals (hidden states, log-probs) without auxiliary training. However, it requires access to both Mfull and Mret, which may not always be available.
  • Significance: High. UDS addresses a genuine and important gap — output-level metrics are demonstrably insufficient for auditing unlearning. The finding that Logit Lens can be fooled by representational shifts is itself a valuable contribution. The integration guidelines are practical.
  • Red flags:
  • Evaluated only on TOFU (fictitious author biographies), a narrow domain. Generalization to other benchmarks (MUSE, RWKU, WMDP) is acknowledged but not demonstrated.
  • Entity-span focus under teacher forcing may not capture long-form or reasoning-target unlearning.
  • The clip(·, 0, 1) operation makes over-unlearning indistinguishable from perfect unlearning — the authors acknowledge this but the practical impact is unclear.
  • Computational cost of patching across all layers for all examples is not quantified or compared against simpler metrics.
  • The retain model requirement is a real practical limitation for many deployment scenarios.

Strengths

  • Principled causal methodology that addresses a fundamental limitation of output-level and observational white-box metrics
  • Training-free and dataset-invariant — directly applicable to new forget sets without adaptation
  • Comprehensive meta-evaluation with transparent methodology and reproducible setup
  • Per-layer, per-example granularity enables diagnostic analysis beyond aggregate scores
  • Demonstrates concrete failure modes of existing metrics (Logit Lens fooled by rotation, CKA by geometry shifts, Fisher by gradient landscape distortion)
  • Practical integration guidelines that demonstrably reshape method rankings and hyperparameter selection
  • Clean mathematical formulation with intuitive interpretation (0=intact, 1=erased to retain level)

Limitations

  • Requires white-box access plus both Mfull and Mret models — excludes API-only settings and scenarios where the original training data split is unavailable
  • Evaluated only on TOFU benchmark (fictitious author data, Llama-3.2 family); generalization to other domains, languages, and model architectures is unverified
  • Entity-span focus under teacher forcing; extension to long-form generation, multi-step reasoning, or abstract concept unlearning remains open
  • Over-unlearning (representations deviating beyond Mret) is indistinguishable from perfect unlearning due to clipping
  • Computational cost not quantified; patching all layers × all examples may be expensive for large models or large forget sets
  • KE threshold τ=0.05 is heuristic; while sensitivity analysis shows stability, the choice is not formally justified

Relevance

UDS 与 LLM 安全性、可信赖性和模型编辑研究直接相关。对于关注知识擦除审计、机制可解释性或 AI 安全评估的研究者，这篇论文提供了一个比现有输出级指标更可靠的因果性评估工具，且其实验设计（元评估框架、案例分析方法论）可作为未来遗忘评估研究的参考范式。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does UDS scale computationally to 70B+ parameter models with hundreds of layers? Is layer-wise patching feasible, or can a subset of diagnostic layers suffice?
  • Can UDS be adapted to measure unlearning of abstract concepts, skills, or reasoning patterns (rather than factual entity spans)?
  • How sensitive is UDS to the choice of retain model — does using different retain splits (e.g., retain95 vs. retain90) meaningfully change scores or rankings?
  • Does deeper UDS-measured erasure correlate with greater utility degradation, or can some methods achieve deep erasure while preserving capabilities?
  • How would UDS perform on non-English or multilingual unlearning benchmarks where token-level entity alignment is harder?
  • Could the two-stage patching framework be adapted for other architectures (diffusion models, vision transformers) as the authors suggest?
