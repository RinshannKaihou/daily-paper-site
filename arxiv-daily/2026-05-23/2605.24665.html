An Energy-Efficient Approximate Posit Multiply-Divide Unit

arXiv: 2605.24665

Date: 2026-05-23

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.24665

一句话总结： 本文提出一种基于posit数系统的近似乘除法联合硬件单元，利用2's complement近似的倒数算法和LUT误差校正实现除数求倒，在TSMC 90nm下功耗延迟积比PACoGen精确除法器降低77%，面积减少46%，乘法仍为精确运算。
TL;DR: Proposes a unified posit multiply-divide hardware unit where approximate division is performed via 2's-complement-based reciprocal estimation with a small error-correction LUT, avoiding Newton-Raphson iterations entirely. For ⟨16,2⟩ posits, the design achieves 77% PDP reduction and 46% area savings vs. PACoGen exact divider (TSMC 90nm synthesis), with MRED of only 0.0434% using a 2^8×9 LUT, while multiplication remains exact.

Key Contributions

  • A unified posit hardware architecture performing exact multiplication and approximate division on a shared datapath, with a single div control bit selecting the mode
  • A novel reciprocal approximation algorithm exploiting the mathematical property that 2's complementing a posit (keeping sign bit fixed) closely approximates its reciprocal, requiring only a LUT lookup + one subtraction for error correction
  • An analytically derived optimal error correction (EC) formula minimizing squared relative error, with EC values precomputed and stored in a compact LUT (2^5×9 to 2^8×9 bits)
  • Structural optimizations across all pipeline stages: parallelized Decoder with early partial processing, radix-8 Booth significand multiplier with streamlined 2's complement blocks, truncated partial product reduction (13-bit instead of 24-bit), and parallelized Encoder
  • Synthesis results at TSMC 90nm showing 77% PDP reduction and 46% area reduction vs. PACoGen full divider datapath, with the reciprocal seed being 14× more accurate than PACoGen's LUT seed for the same LUT size

Methodology

The paper targets the ⟨16,2⟩ posit configuration (16-bit word, ES=2, following the ratified posit standard). The core insight is that when a posit number is 2's complemented while keeping its sign bit constant, the resulting bit pattern approximates the reciprocal. For inputs with zero fraction, this approximation is exact; for non-zero fractions, it approximates the hyperbolic curve 1/(1+f) with a line (1−f/2). The authors derive a closed-form optimal error correction term EC = f(1−f)/(2(1+f)), rounded to the nearest integer and stored in a LUT indexed by the MSBs of the fraction.

The hardware architecture features two asymmetric decoders: Decoder A (standard posit decoding) and Decoder B (extends A with a div bit and LUT for reciprocal mode). When div=1, Decoder B applies 2's complement, looks up the EC value from a 2^5×9 or 2^8×9 LUT, and subtracts it from the fraction. The significand multiplier uses radix-8 Booth encoding with a novel 2's complement block in PPG that eliminates the need for dedicated control circuits to gate padded zero bits. The partial product reduction truncates to 13 bits (sufficient for 12-bit max significand + 1 bit for normalization/rounding), avoiding a full 24-bit addition. A hybrid Kogge-Stone/Carry-Lookahead adder serves as the final adder.

The design was synthesized using Cadence RTL Compiler v7.1 with TSMC 90nm (slow-normal) process library. All major components (Decoder A/B, significand unit, encoder) were benchmarked individually against PACoGen, NPFD, POSIT-VAR, PEfPM, and Booth multiplier designs.

Results & Highlights

  • Reciprocal accuracy (⟨16,2⟩): MRED = 0.3834% with no EC, 0.0434% with 2^8×9 LUT — 14× more accurate than PACoGen's LUT seed at the same LUT size (0.61% MRED)
  • Full datapath (division mode): PDP ratio 0.23 vs. PACoGen (77% reduction); area ratio 0.54 (46% reduction); delay 12.828ns vs. 21.739ns
  • Full datapath (multiplication mode): PDP ratio 0.16 (84% reduction); area ratio 0.54; delay 10.221ns; multiplication is exact
  • Decoder A: 31.5% lower PDP than next-best decoder; delay 2.464ns (29.96% improvement)
  • Significand divider: 89% PDP reduction vs. PACoGen significand division; 86% vs. NPFD
  • Encoder: 35% lower delay, 23% lower power, 8% smaller area than POSIT-VAR
  • LUT area overhead: Only 0.036 KB (2^5×9) to 0.288 KB (2^8×9) for ⟨16,2⟩

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate-to-high. The 2's complement reciprocal trick is mathematically clean and posit-specific — it would not work as directly for IEEE float. The analytical derivation of optimal EC values and the unified multiply-divide datapath are genuine contributions. However, LUT-based reciprocal seeding is well-established (used in PACoGen and conventional float dividers); the novelty is in the simpler correction method (single subtraction vs. iterative NR refinement).
  • Rigor: Strong quantitative evaluation with per-component breakdowns and multiple comparison baselines. Error analysis uses MED, MRED, and NMED across all 2048 fraction values for ⟨16,2⟩. However, the paper evaluates only one posit configuration (⟨16,2⟩); results for ⟨32,2⟩ are mentioned in a table but not synthesized. No end-to-end application-level accuracy evaluation is provided (e.g., DNN inference error, DSP benchmarks).
  • Reproducibility: Reasonable — standard tools and process node are used, and the mathematical derivations are clearly presented. No open-source RTL release is mentioned, which limits independent verification.
  • Significance: Practically significant for approximate computing and edge AI hardware where division accuracy can be traded for energy. The claim that the design can be extended to exact division with fewer NR iterations is promising but not demonstrated.
  • Red flags: (1) Only compared against PACoGen for the full datapath — comparison with logarithmic approximate units (PLAUs) at the full-datapath level would be more informative. (2) The "78.8% PDP reduction" in the abstract appears to refer to an earlier version of results; the paper's Table 7 shows 77%. (3) No worst-case error analysis — MRED is an average metric, and the maximum error could be significantly higher for certain fraction values. (4) Application-level impact is unstated — it is unclear whether 0.0434% MRED is acceptable for DNN inference or scientific computing.

Strengths

  • Elegant mathematical insight: the 2's complement reciprocal approximation exploits posit symmetry in a way that is both novel and hardware-friendly
  • Comprehensive per-component evaluation with fair comparisons against multiple baselines
  • The unified architecture adds negligible overhead for multiplication mode (LUT is gated off), making it practical for real designs
  • The significand multiplier innovations (streamlined 2's complement block, truncated reduction) are independently useful
  • Clear writing with well-organized methodology sections and hardware diagrams

Limitations

  • Only ⟨16,2⟩ posits are fully evaluated; extension to ⟨32,2⟩ and ⟨64,2⟩ is mentioned but not synthesized
  • No application-level accuracy evaluation (e.g., DNN inference, signal processing, scientific computing)
  • Worst-case (maximum) error for division is not reported — only average metrics (MED, MRED, NMED)
  • TSMC 90nm is an older process node; results may differ substantially at modern nodes (7nm, 5nm)
  • No comparison with logarithmic approximate posit units (PLAUs) at the full-datapath level
  • The paper does not demonstrate the claimed "easily convertible to exact divider with NR" extension
