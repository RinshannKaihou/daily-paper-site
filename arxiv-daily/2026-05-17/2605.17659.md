> **一句话总结：** 本文严格证明并系统实证了标准损失函数（MSE/交叉熵）与正偏置激活函数（ReLU/GELU/SiLU）交互导致负权重漂移，该漂移在ReLU下引发高达90%激活稀疏性，在79种配置中发现~70%稀疏度处存在精度断崖，进一步揭示ReLU²在Transformer中间层会放大已有的激活尖峰并裁剪后可提升性能（GELU²达到最低验证损失），同时提出基于早期漂移稳定的Accumulation Stop机制消除动态统计计算开销。

> **TL;DR:** The authors formally prove that standard losses (MSE, cross-entropy) combined with positively-biased activations produce a systematic negative weight drift — the gradient w.r.t. positive pre-activations is non-negative in expectation, driving downstream weights negative during early training. This drift is intrinsic to optimization (appears even on random data) and persists across MLP, ResNet, MaxViT, GPT-nano, and MP-SENet. With ReLU, it induces up to 90% activation sparsity. Across 79 configurations, performance remains flat up to ~70% sparsity then collapses sharply (power-law exponent N=16.72). ReLU² excels on GPT-nano but pathologically amplifies activation spikes in intermediate layers (Layer 2 spike: 1055 vs. 42 for GELU). Clipped ReLU²clip50 resolves this and improves over unclipped ReLU² (val loss 3.236 vs. 3.251); GELU² achieves the best overall (3.233). The authors also propose Percentile Centering for controllable sparsity and Accumulation Stop to freeze normalization statistics after warm-up, recovering full training throughput.

## 核心贡献

- **严格证明负权重漂移**：对ReLU网络证明Theorem 1.1（有效权重矩阵行的零均值性和非负交叉相关性）、Theorem 1.2（MSE损失下梯度非负）和Theorem 1.3（交叉熵损失下梯度非负），证明漂移在任意中间层都成立且是局部的（适用于任意连续线性-ReLU堆叠）
- **证明漂移是优化内生的而非数据依赖的**：在完全随机数据上重现相同的漂移模式，跨6种激活函数（ReLU、GELU、SiLU、NoisyReLU、SUGARBSiLU、ReLU²）和5种架构验证
- **系统刻画79种配置的稀疏-精度权衡**：拟合三参数幂律 à(s)=A−B·s^N（A=0.978, B=0.635, N=16.72），发现~70%稀疏度处存在精度断崖，该位置主要由架构决定（跳跃连接提升鲁棒性，MLP在85%稀疏度崩溃而GPT-nano在~91%仍稳定）
- **提出Percentile Centering（PC）**：通过预激活分布的目标百分位数移位直接控制ReLU后激活稀疏度，可集成到现有BN/LN中
- **揭示ReLU²的激活尖峰问题并提出裁剪方案**：ReLU²在GPT-nano第2层产生1055的激活范围（vs. GELU的42.7），裁剪至50恢复为50且验证损失更优（3.236 vs. 3.251）；GELU²达到最低损失3.233
- **ReLUfication实用推荐**：GELU训练模型经1 epoch微调转为ReLU可获得55-74%激活稀疏性且精度损失≤1%
- **Accumulation Stop机制**：利用权重漂移在早期迭代稳定的特点，冻结EMA统计量，在DiT-S/2和MaxViT上恢复至接近基线吞吐量

## Key Contributions

- Formal proof of negative weight drift under MSE (Theorem 1.2) and cross-entropy (Theorem 1.3) for ReLU networks, establishing that the gradient w.r.t. positive pre-activations is non-negative in expectation, with the result holding at any intermediate layer and applying locally to any contiguous stack of linear+ReLU layers
- Demonstration that drift is optimization-intrinsic, not data-dependent: identical drift patterns appear when training on entirely random N(0,1) data across all tested activation functions and architectures
- Large-scale characterization of the sparsity-accuracy tradeoff across 79 (model, sparsity) pairs, fitting a power-law decay à(s)=0.978−0.635·s^16.72 that reveals a sharp accuracy cliff above ~70% sparsity, with architecture (especially skip connections) primarily determining the cliff position
- Introduction of Percentile Centering (PC) as a controllable sparsification mechanism that shifts pre-activations by a target percentile rather than the mean, directly controlling the fraction of post-activation zeros
- Discovery that ReLU² pathologically amplifies existing intermediate-layer activation spikes (Layer 2 range: 1055 vs. 42.7 for GELU in GPT-nano), and that clipping resolves this while preserving representational benefits: ReLU²clip50 (val loss 3.236) outperforms unclipped ReLU² (3.251), while GELU² achieves the best overall (3.233)
- Practical ReLUfication recommendation: converting GELU-trained models to ReLU via one epoch of fine-tuning yields 55-74% activation sparsity with ≤1% accuracy loss
- Accumulation Stop mechanism that freezes EMA normalization statistics after a warm-up phase, restoring training throughput to near-baseline levels on DiT-S/2 and MaxViT

## Methodology

**Theoretical analysis:** The authors analyze the effective weight matrix V_eff formed by composing all layers after an intermediate layer l (Eq. 1). Theorem 1.1 establishes that rows of V_eff have zero mean and non-negative cross-correlation, leveraging the ReLU survival-conditioning argument (conditioning on a⊤x≥0 forces vectors to share a positive component). Theorems 1.2 and 1.3 then show the expected gradient w.r.t. any positive pre-activation is non-negative for both MSE and cross-entropy losses, with strict positivity when the pre-activation is strictly positive. The analysis is local — it applies to any contiguous stack of linear+ReLU layers within a larger architecture.

**Empirical verification:** Weight drift is measured as the average absolute Z-score (|w_t − w_0|/std(w_0)) per layer. Experiments span SGD, SGD+momentum, and Adam across learning rates, confirming: (1) momentum accelerates drift, (2) higher LR → faster/larger drift, (3) drift stabilizes after a characteristic "knee" when gradients diminish. The covariance term Cov_x(∂ℓ/∂p_i, x) is orders of magnitude smaller than the weight mean, validating the theoretical assumptions.

**Sparsity control:** Two mechanisms — Top-K activation sparsity (with GELU) and Percentile Centering (proposed, with ReLU). PC shifts pre-activations by the q-th percentile, causing exactly q% to fall below zero when followed by ReLU. 79 configurations are evaluated across MLP, ResNet-18, MaxViT-Tiny, and GPT-nano.

**Squared activation analysis:** Layer-wise analysis of activation ranges in GPT-nano across 21+ runs. Clipping thresholds (15 and 50) evaluated empirically. GELU² and clipped variants compared against baselines.

**Accumulation Stop:** EMA tracking (γ=0.9999) of centering/percentile statistics during a warm-up phase (T_warm=50,000 for DiT-S/2), then freezing. Evaluated on DiT-S/2 (H200, ImageNet-1K) and MaxViT-Tiny (A100).

## Results & Highlights

- **Weight drift is universal:** Negative drift appears in all tested architectures (MLP, ResNet-18, MaxViT-Tiny, GPT-nano, MP-SENet) and all tested activations (ReLU, GELU, SiLU, NoisyReLU, SUGARBSiLU, ReLU²). The only exception is ResNet with BatchNorm, where mean-centering directly disrupts the drift mechanism
- **Negative pre-activations are the norm:** Table 1 shows 60-89% of pre-activations are negative across most configurations (e.g., GPT-nano with GELU: 89.3%, with ReLU: 89.0%)
- **Sharp accuracy cliff:** The power-law fit (N=16.72) quantifies the cliff: performance is essentially flat below ~70% sparsity and collapses rapidly above ~80%. MLP without skip connections collapses catastrophically at 85% (≈10% accuracy); GPT-nano remains stable up to ~91%
- **Sparsity mechanism is secondary:** R²=0.565 for sparsity alone vs. R²=0.618 when adding mechanism indicator (∆R²=+0.053), confirming sparsity level dominates mechanism choice
- **GELU is the strongest general-purpose baseline:** Highest accuracy on 4/5 classification settings, second-best GPT-nano loss
- **ReLU² excels on GPT-nano but is normalization-sensitive:** Collapses to 10% accuracy on MLP without normalization and ResNet with BN, recovers with RMSNorm/LayerNorm. Best GPT-nano validation loss (3.250)
- **Activation spikes are amplified, not created, by squaring:** Baseline activations already show moderate spikes (Layer 2: ReLU=42.2, GELU=42.7); ReLU² amplifies to 1055. Clipping at 50 reduces to 50 and improves performance
- **GELU² achieves best GPT-nano loss (3.233)** with substantially lower spikes than ReLU², making it a promising starting point for ReLUfication toward ReLU²
- **ReLUfication is practical:** 1 epoch of fine-tuning GELU→ReLU introduces 55-74% sparsity with ≤1% accuracy loss; the fraction of negative pre-activations barely changes, indicating sparsity arises from GELU-trained weights + ReLU thresholding
- **Accumulation Stop preserves quality and recovers throughput:** DiT-S/2: FID actually improves from 49.40 to 48.21 with Percentile Shift. MaxViT: accuracy degrades within 2% relative (70.30% → 69.75%). Throughput recovers to near-baseline after freezing statistics (DiT-S/2: 3.43 vs. 3.42 baseline)

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are affiliated with On-Point R&D. Evgeny Burnaev is a well-established researcher in machine learning and deep learning theory. The paper provides open-source code at https://github.com/On-Point-RND/BugOrFeature. The combination of formal proofs (3 theorems), large-scale empirical evaluation (79 configurations, 5 architectures, 6+ activations), and practical recommendations (ReLUfication, Accumulation Stop) demonstrates significant effort and rigor. The paper is transparent about limitations (Section 7), explicitly listing 7 limitations including restricted theoretical scope (initialization only, ReLU only formally), small-scale language modeling (GPT-nano 124M), and architecture-dependent clipping thresholds.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The formal proof connecting loss structure to weight drift via effective weight matrix analysis is a genuine contribution — extending Li et al. (2022) by proving the result for any intermediate layer (not just the final two) and under weaker assumptions. However, "dead ReLU neurons" and negative weight drift have been informally discussed in prior work (Lu et al., 2019; Li et al., 2022). The activation spike finding partially overlaps with concurrent work by Sun et al. (2026) on SwiGLU transformers.
- **Rigor:** Strong theoretical grounding with explicit assumptions. The 79-configuration sweep and power-law fit provide empirical rigor. The paper honestly acknowledges that the formal proof covers only ReLU and only at initialization — extensions to smooth activations are empirical only.
- **Reproducibility:** Good — open-source code, detailed appendix (Sections A-H with implementation details, extended results), clear experimental protocols. Hardware (H200, A100) and precision (bfloat16) specified.
- **Significance:** The ~70% sparsity cliff is a practical and actionable finding for the sparse inference community. The Accumulation Stop insight (drift stabilizes early, so statistics can be frozen) has immediate practical value. ReLUfication as a practical recommendation is valuable.
- **Red flags:** (1) GPT-nano is only 124M parameters — scaling to billion-parameter LLMs is untested, and the authors acknowledge this. (2) ReLU² clipping thresholds (15, 50) are selected empirically and "appear architecture-dependent" — no principled method for choosing them. (3) The GELU² result (best val loss) lacks investigation of whether this holds beyond GPT-nano. (4) The Accumulation Stop evaluation uses a naive PyTorch implementation against an optimized baseline — the conservative throughput gains may not translate to optimized settings. (5) The squared activation benefits appear modality-specific (work for GPT-nano but not MaxViT), which limits generality. (6) No evaluation on downstream NLP benchmarks (only validation loss) for GPT-nano.

## Strengths

- Clean formal framework (effective weight matrix decomposition) that makes the drift mechanism transparent and the proofs accessible
- Comprehensive empirical sweep (79 configurations) with well-chosen baselines (Top-K, ReLUfication)
- The power-law fit quantifying the sparsity cliff (N=16.72) is a concrete, reusable result
- Honest and detailed limitations section (7 enumerated points)
- Practical recommendations grounded in evidence: ReLUfication for sparsity, clipped ReLU² for GPT-nano, Accumulation Stop for efficiency
- Independent discovery of activation spikes in non-gated MLP blocks, complementing Sun et al. (2026)'s SwiGLU findings

## Limitations

- Theoretical results assume zero-mean i.i.d. weights and hold strictly only at initialization; formal extension to smooth activations (GELU, SiLU) is unsupported
- Language modeling evaluated only on GPT-nano (124M params) with FineWeb data — no scaling evidence to production LLMs
- Clipping thresholds for squared activations are empirical and architecture-dependent with no principled selection method
- Accumulation Stop is evaluated only on fixed-input architectures (DiT, MaxViT); autoregressive models (GPT) with varying sequence lengths may require different treatment
- Squared activation benefits appear limited to autoregressive models — no improvement over plain GELU on MaxViT suggests modality/objective-specific interaction
- No downstream task evaluation for GPT-nano (only validation perplexity), making it unclear whether validation loss improvements translate to practical capabilities
- Throughput gains use naive implementation — real-world gains with optimized kernels are uncertain

## Relevance

本文对理解神经网络训练动态和激活函数设计具有重要价值。权重漂移的严格证明和~70%稀疏度断崖的发现对设计稀疏推理系统有直接指导意义；ReLU²裁剪和GELU²的发现为Transformer激活函数选择提供了新思路；Accumulation Stop机制为高效训练提供了实用方案。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the negative weight drift and the ~70% sparsity cliff hold at billion-parameter scale (e.g., LLaMA-7B, 70B)?
- Can the power-law exponent N=16.72 be predicted from architectural properties (width, depth, skip-connection structure)?
- Is there a principled way to determine optimal clipping thresholds for squared activations beyond empirical search?
- How does Accumulation Stop interact with variable-length sequences in autoregressive models — can positional EMA statistics work?
- Why do squared activations benefit autoregressive language models but not vision transformers — is this a modality effect, an objective function effect, or related to attention patterns?
- Can the weight drift mechanism be deliberately exploited for structured pruning during training rather than post-hoc sparsification?
- Does GELU²'s advantage over GELU persist with larger models, and does it transfer to downstream benchmarks (MMLU, HumanEval, etc.)?
