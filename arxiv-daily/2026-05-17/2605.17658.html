When a Zero-Shooter Cheats

VLMs performing zero-shot age estimation exploit an identity shortcut — recognizing celebrities and recalling their age from memory rather than analyzing visual features, causing systematic errors and deceptively inflated robustness on standard benchmarks.

Erik Imgrund, Pia Hanfeld, Klim Kireev, Konrad Rieck  ·  BIFOLD / TU Berlin

Key Metric ↓ 25.3% MAE Gemma 3 on FG-Net  (8.36 → 6.24)
Why This Matters Celebrity-dominated benchmarks give a false sense of security — memorized predictions persist regardless of input corruption.
Critical Edge α=3 hand-tuned, no sensitivity analysis. Requires open-weight activations. Single-task evaluation only.
Scope 6 VLMs · 2 supervised baselines · 4 datasets · 20 corruption types

Methodology

The paper proceeds in three phases. (1) Characterizing the identity shortcut — the authors formally define the expected impact δk of the shortcut (Eq. 1) and approximate it by comparing VLM predictions against MiVOLO (a supervised model trained without celebrity identity knowledge) as a surrogate estimator on both known-identity (CelebA-K) and unknown-identity subsets (Eq. 2–3). They measure δk + Ef,g on identified celebrities and Ef,g on unknown identities to isolate the shortcut’s effect.

(2) Robustness analysis — they apply 20 common corruption types (noise, blur, weather, digital, adversarial) at 4 severity levels to images from 4 datasets (CelebA, AgeDB, FG-Net, VideoScrape) and measure normalized mean deviation from clean predictions, revealing asymmetric robustness across distributions.

(3) Activation steering — extending the task vector concept of Hendel et al. (2023), they compute task vectors from the first half of the model’s shallow activations at the last token position: tk as the mean task vector over known-identity images and t¬k over unknown-identity images. During inference, they replace the natural task vector with t(x,p) + α(t¬k − tk), where α=3. This pushes the model away from identity-based retrieval and toward visual feature analysis.

Results & Highlights

δk exceeds 1 year for Gemma 3 (1.16) and Gemini 3 Flash (1.45). All VLMs except Qwen 3.5 show positive shortcut impact.
Bimodal error distribution on FG-Net: some unknown individuals are misidentified as celebrities, producing a characteristic dual-peak MAE histogram.
Asymmetric robustness: supervised models show uniform robustness across all datasets; VLMs show significantly higher robustness on CelebA than on other datasets.
Average MAE reduction: 10.8% across all model-dataset combinations. Qwen 3.5 on AgeDB drops from 13.52 to 10.72 (20.7%).
Adversarial fragility: all models reliably attacked with ε < 1/255, but VLMs show a further CelebA-vs-others discrepancy.
Steering eliminates bimodality: after activation steering, the FG-Net error distribution becomes closer to unimodal, confirming identity shortcut as the cause.

Strengths

  • Identifies and formalizes a previously unknown failure mode of VLMs with clear real-world safety implications.
  • The connection between identity memorization and deceptive benchmark robustness is a powerful and generalizable insight.
  • Activation steering is lightweight (negligible inference overhead), requires no retraining, and shows consistent improvements.
  • Thorough experimental design spanning multiple models, datasets, corruption types, and severity levels.
  • VideoScrape dataset addresses an important gap in contamination-free evaluation.

Limitations

  • Only evaluated on age estimation — extension to other attribute inference tasks (gender, height, weight) is claimed but not demonstrated.
  • Requires access to model activations, excluding closed-source VLMs.
  • α=3 is manually tuned with no reported sensitivity analysis or automated selection.
  • VideoScrape contains only 200 images, limiting statistical power for distribution-level claims.
  • No comparison with alternative mitigation strategies such as machine unlearning or fine-tuning on non-celebrity data.

Relevance

Highly relevant to LLM Hidden States & Interpretability and ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations. The work analyzes VLM task vectors in hidden states to reveal and intervene on internal reasoning mechanisms — a representation-level interpretability study. It also exposes systematic failure modes in safety-critical VLM applications, falling within ML reliability analysis.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the identity shortcut generalize to other zero-shot attribute estimation tasks (gender, ethnicity, emotion, BMI) and other domains (medical imaging, satellite imagery)?
  2. Can the task vector approach be extended to identify and suppress other types of memorization-driven shortcuts (e.g., dataset-specific artifacts, prompt leakage)?
  3. What is the sensitivity of the steering coefficient α — is there a universal optimal range, or does it vary significantly across model architectures and tasks?
  4. Could activation steering be combined with adversarial training to simultaneously address both the identity shortcut and adversarial vulnerability?
  5. How does the identity shortcut interact with chain-of-thought prompting or multi-step reasoning in VLMs?
