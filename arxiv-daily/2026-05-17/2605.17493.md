> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 KAN-SAE，用 Kolmogorov-Arnold 网络的可学习 B-spline 激活替代传统 SAE 的 ReLU，在 SONNY 天气预测 Transformer 中将存活特征数提升 72%（975 vs. 566），并发现经因果干预验证的欧洲热浪探测器和西太平洋台风追踪器。

> **TL;DR:** KAN-SAE replaces the standard ReLU encoder in Sparse Autoencoders with per-feature learnable B-spline activations from Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks. Applied to SONNY (a 20.5M-parameter weather transformer at 1.5° resolution), it discovers 975 alive features vs. 566 for a linear baseline (95.2% vs. 55.5% feature utilisation), with 20% lower inter-feature redundancy and 24% lower mean ℓ1 norm at a modest 2.4-point EV cost (84.2% vs. 86.6%). Key findings: (1) a European heatwave feature (F590) with a convex threshold-like activation that ReLU cannot express, spatially concentrated within 2° of the 2019 event centre (vs. 51° displacement for the linear baseline); (2) a Typhoon Mangkhut detector (F831) confirmed by causal steering that modulates EU-mean T2m by up to ±3 K with r² > 0.99 dose-response linearity; and (3) a push-pull circuit between Layer-0 excitatory (L0-F372, r=0.90) and inhibitory (L0-F2, r=−0.897) features driving the heatwave detector at Layer 5.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 KAN-SAE，将编码器的 ReLU 替换为每个潜在维度独立的可学习 B-spline 激活函数（3 阶、9 个控制点），使每个特征可以学习自己的非线性门控曲线（阈值、饱和、非单调响应），适应大气动力学中的体制转换。
- 在 SONNY 天气预测模型 Layer 5 残差流上实验：存活特征 975/1024（95.2%）vs. 线性基线 566/1024（55.5%），特征间冗余度降低 20%（中位 |r| 从 0.092 降至 0.076，强相关对从 11.9% 降至 4.4%）。
- 发现无需气候监督的可解释特征：F590 欧洲热浪探测器（凸激活曲线，距事件中心仅 2°）和 F831 台风"山竹"追踪器（距风暴中心 3°），并通过因果干预实验验证：注入 F590 方向可引起 EU 区域 T2m 变化 +1.42 K（α=2），热点超过 +3 K。
- 揭示 Layer-0 到 Layer-5 的推拉电路机制：L0-F372（r=0.90，兴奋性）和 L0-F2（r=−0.897，抑制性）共同驱动 F590 的激活，与 NLP Transformer 中的推拉电路结构类似。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces KAN-SAE, a nonlinear SAE that replaces ReLU with per-feature learnable cubic B-spline activations (order 3, 9 control points each), allowing each latent dimension to develop its own gating profile including threshold, saturation, and asymmetric responses suited to atmospheric regime changes.
- Achieves 95.2% feature utilisation (975 alive features) vs. 55.5% for a linear SAE baseline on SONNY's Layer-5 residual stream, with 20% lower inter-feature redundancy (median |r|: 0.076 vs. 0.092), 24% lower mean ℓ1 norm, at only 2.4-point EV cost (84.2% vs. 86.6%).
- Discovers F590, an unsupervised European heatwave detector with convex threshold-like activation invisible to ReLU, localised within 2° of the June 2019 event centre (vs. 51° displacement for linear SAE), and F831, a Typhoon Mangkhut tracker localised within 3°.
- Validates features causally: steering F590 injects spatially selective warm anomalies over western Europe (+1.42 K EU-mean at α=2, hotspots >+3 K over France/Iberia) with r² > 0.99 linear dose-response, and parallel Z500/MSLP responses consistent with blocking anticyclone dynamics.
- Traces a push-pull circuit from Layer 0 to Layer 5: excitatory L0-F372 (r=0.90 with F590) and inhibitory L0-F2 (r=−0.897) jointly drive the heatwave feature, mirroring push-pull circuits found in NLP transformers.

## Methodology

**Architecture.** KAN-SAE modifies the standard SAE encoder: instead of z = ReLU(W_enc(x − b_pre) + b_enc), it computes z_j = φ_j([W_enc(x − b_pre) + b_enc]_j) where each φ_j is a cubic B-spline with K=9 control points, uniformly spaced knots over the empirical 1st–99th percentile of pre-activations. The decoder remains linear: x̂ = W_dec z + b_pre. The loss is standard MSE + λ‖z‖₁ with λ annealed from 5×10⁻⁵ to 1×10⁻⁴ over 100 epochs. Decoder columns are unit-ℓ₂ normalised after each step.

**Probed model.** SONNY is a 20.5M-parameter hierarchical weather transformer (ViT-S, 1.5° resolution, 121×240 grid) with a Variable-Aware Embedding splitting inputs into Dynamics (U, V, Z, P) and Thermodynamics (T, Q) groups. Activations are extracted from Layer 5 of the Step-2 (Fast Path) full-width transformer block, yielding x ∈ ℝ³⁸⁴.

**Training data.** ERA5 reanalysis, 500 days from 2016–2017 for training (3.6M tokens), 51 held-out days from 2018–2022 for testing. M=1,024 latent dimensions (expansion ratio ≈2.67×). Single NVIDIA A40 GPU, ~4 hours per model.

**Alive feature criterion.** A feature j is alive if max_k |c_jk| ≥ τ, where τ = 1.4021 separates the bimodal distribution of control-point magnitudes. This is the B-spline analogue of "ever activated."

**Evaluation.** Explained variance, feature utilisation rate, mean ℓ₁ norm, inter-feature redundancy (pairwise |r| of EU-mean activation time series over 500 days, area-weighted 36°–72°N, 10°W–30°E). Case studies use best-m-in-n strategy. Steering injects scaled decoder directions into the residual stream.

## Results & Highlights

- **Feature utilisation:** KAN-SAE achieves 95.2% alive features (975/1,024) vs. 55.5% (566/1,020) for LIN-SAE. Dead features drop from 44.5% to 4.8%. KAN-SAE features sustain activation across a median of 51/53 test days vs. 1/53 for LIN-SAE.
- **Redundancy:** Median inter-feature |r| drops from 0.092 to 0.076; pairs with |r| > 0.3 drop from 11.9% to 4.4%. KAN-SAE's correlation matrix is near-diagonal while LIN-SAE shows prominent off-diagonal redundancy blocks.
- **Reconstruction trade-off:** KAN-SAE trades 2.4 points of explained variance (84.2% vs. 86.6%) for a 72% gain in feature utilisation and 24% reduction in activation norm. Spatially, the MSE ratio is near-unity globally; KAN-SAE is slightly worse only in mid-latitude land regions (high temperature variability).
- **B-spline shape diversity:** 902 of 975 alive features learn convex activations, 73 learn concave; median nonlinearity score 0.43. Most deviate substantially from linear, confirming the need for per-feature nonlinearity.
- **F590 heatwave detector:** Convex accelerating response — near-zero for normal states, sharply rising for blocking-regime pre-activations. KAN-SAE localises within Δc = 2° of the June 2019 event centre; best LIN-SAE match displaced by 51° to the Middle East.
- **F831 typhoon tracker:** Peak activation on 14 Sep 2018 coinciding with Category-5 Typhoon Mangkhut. Both models localise within Δc = 3°, but KAN-SAE shows tighter spatial concentration across all typhoon test days — tropical cyclones' strong coherent vortex signatures are detectable even by linear features.
- **Causal steering of F590:** Injecting α × d₅₉₀ produces EU-mean T2m anomaly of +1.42 K at α=2, with hotspots >+3 K over France and Iberia. Dose-response is near-perfectly linear (r² > 0.99). Z500 rises and MSLP drops in parallel, consistent with blocking anticyclone dynamics (upper-level ridging + surface thermal low).
- **Push-pull circuit:** L0-F372 (excitatory, r=0.90) activates broadly over Eurasia and is stronger on hot days; L0-F2 (inhibitory, r=−0.897) is globally active and suppressed on hot EU days. Together they drive F590 activation — analogous to push-pull circuits in NLP transformers.

## Authors & Credibility

Minjong Cheon, Department of Computer Science and Engineering, Sejong University. Single-authored. The author also created SONNY (the probed weather model, arXiv:2603.21284), which gives confidence that the model internals are well-understood. The paper is well-positioned relative to prior work: MacMillan & Ouellette (arXiv:2512.24440) applied linear SAEs to GraphCast — this work is explicitly positioned as an orthogonal improvement. The paper acknowledges limitations clearly (single model, single layer, two phenomena only).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The combination of KAN B-spline activations with SAEs is architecturally simple but well-motivated. The key insight — that atmospheric regime changes need per-feature nonlinear gating — is compelling and the experimental validation is thorough for the chosen case studies. Not revolutionary, but a solid and meaningful contribution.
- **Rigor:** Strong experimental design with held-out test data (2018–2022), multiple evaluation axes (utilisation, redundancy, reconstruction, spatial localisation, causal steering, circuit analysis). However, the baseline comparison is limited to a vanilla linear SAE — the paper acknowledges TopK, Gated, and JumpReLU SAEs in related work but does not compare against them. This is a significant gap, as these modern variants also address dead-feature and redundancy problems. The alive-feature threshold τ = 1.4021 is hand-tuned from a bimodal distribution; the paper acknowledges the need for a "principled criterion."
- **Reproducibility:** Architecture, hyperparameters, and training procedure are described in sufficient detail (Algorithm 1). Code availability is not mentioned. The single-author factor and SONNY being the author's own model reduce reproducibility concerns for the probed model but raise questions about generalisability.
- **Significance:** The causal steering results are the strongest part — showing ±3 K temperature modulation with physically consistent Z500/MSLP responses is genuinely impressive. The push-pull circuit analysis between layers adds depth beyond single-layer feature discovery. However, demonstrating interpretability on only two hand-picked phenomena leaves open the question of how many of the 975 alive features are genuinely interpretable vs. merely alive.
- **Red flags:** (1) The 2.4-point EV gap is small but non-zero; the paper frames it favourably but doesn't explore whether it degrades downstream forecast skill. (2) The typhoon case study actually shows that linear SAEs work fine for tropical cyclones — the advantage of KAN-SAE is subtle (tighter localisation), not qualitative. The paper is honest about this but it weakens the "nonlinear activations are essential" framing. (3) Only one layer of one model is analysed. (4) B-spline activations add 9 parameters per latent dimension (9×1024 = 9,216 extra parameters) — this is modest but the comparison doesn't account for parameter-matched deeper ReLU baselines.

## Strengths

- Well-motivated theoretical critique of linear superposition for atmospheric dynamics — blocking anticyclones, regime thresholds, and saturation effects genuinely don't fit ReLU.
- Simple, targeted architectural change (replace ReLU with B-spline) that doesn't increase decoder complexity or break SAE interpretability.
- Thorough multi-axis evaluation: utilisation, redundancy, reconstruction, spatial localisation, causal steering, and cross-layer circuit analysis.
- Causal steering with r² > 0.99 dose-response linearity and physically consistent multi-variable responses (T2m, Z500, MSLP) is strong validation.
- Honest about limitations: acknowledges that typhoons are detectable by linear features, and that analysis is restricted to one layer/model.
- Push-pull circuit analysis between Layer 0 and Layer 5 adds mechanistic depth and parallels to NLP interpretability.

## Limitations

- Only compared against a vanilla linear SAE; no comparison with TopK, Gated, or JumpReLU SAEs, which also address dead-feature and redundancy issues through different mechanisms.
- Interpretability demonstrated on only two phenomena (European heatwave, western Pacific typhoon) out of 975 alive features — the interpretability coverage rate is unknown.
- Single model (SONNY, 20.5M params) at single layer (Layer 5); no validation on larger weather models (GraphCast, Pangu-Weather) or other layers.
- The alive-feature threshold τ = 1.4021 is set empirically from a bimodal distribution; no sensitivity analysis or principled derivation.
- 2.4-point EV gap not evaluated for downstream forecast impact.
- B-spline parameter overhead (9 extra params per feature) not compared against parameter-matched deeper linear baselines.

## Relevance

直接相关于 mechanistic interpretability 和稀疏自编码器研究。将 SAE 从 LLM 扩展到天气预测 Transformer，并通过 KAN 非线性激活解决特征发现问题，其中的推拉电路分析和因果干预方法对理解复杂非线性系统的特征表示具有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does KAN-SAE compare against TopK, Gated, and JumpReLU SAEs on the same SONNY activations? These modern variants also reduce dead features and redundancy — is the B-spline advantage additive or redundant?
- Do the discovered features (especially the push-pull circuit) transfer across weather model architectures (GraphCast, Pangu-Weather, STORMER)?
- What fraction of the 975 alive features are genuinely interpretable vs. merely alive? A systematic human or automated interpretability audit would strengthen the claims.
- How does the B-spline order p and control-point count K affect the results? Is p=3, K=9 optimal or arbitrary?
- Does the 2.4-point EV gap translate to degraded downstream forecast skill, or is it absorbed by later layers?
- Can the cross-layer circuit analysis be extended to a full mechanistic account of how SONNY processes atmospheric dynamics end-to-end?
