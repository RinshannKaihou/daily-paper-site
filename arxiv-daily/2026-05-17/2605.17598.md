> **一句话总结：** 本文系统分析了MoE模型在处理低资源语言（希伯来语、日语）时出现的深层路由崩塌现象，发现持续预训练（CPT）比监督微调（SFT）能更有效地纠正路由失衡，且路由熵的改善与下游任务性能提升直接相关。

> **TL;DR:** This paper reveals that MoE LLMs suffer from "deep-layer routing collapse" for low-resource languages (Hebrew, Japanese), where tokens concentrate on a narrow expert subset in final layers. Continual pre-training (CPT) on balanced bilingual data substantially corrects this imbalance, while SFT alone is less effective. Routing entropy improvements correlate with downstream benchmark gains.

## 核心贡献

- **发现深层路由崩塌现象**：两个预训练MoE模型（Qwen3纯Transformer和Nemotron混合Mamba-Transformer）在处理希伯来语时，最终层的专家利用熵急剧下降，token集中在少数专家上，而英语则分布均匀。
- **提出路由分析指标体系**：引入使用熵（Usage Entropy）、基尼系数、语言特异指数（LSI）、余弦相似度等多维度指标，系统量化MoE模型的多语言路由行为。
- **跨语言验证**：将分析扩展到日语（与希伯来语在类型学上完全不相关），观察到定量一致的路由崩塌特征，证明该现象源于预训练数据不足而非语言本身特性。
- **CPT优于SFT**：持续预训练（CPT）在纠正深层路由失衡方面显著优于监督微调（SFT），CPT使专家路由趋向语言无关，而SFT在最终层改善有限。
- **路由改善与性能提升正相关**：路由熵提高、基尼系数降低的模型在下游基准测试中表现更好，确立了路由指标作为多语言能力诊断工具的地位。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies **deep-layer routing collapse** in MoE models for underrepresented languages: in pre-trained Qwen3 and Nemotron, Hebrew tokens concentrate on a narrow expert subset in final layers while English maintains uniform distribution.
- Introduces a comprehensive suite of routing diagnostics (Usage Entropy, Gini Coefficient, Language Specificity Index, Cosine Similarity, Top-K Overlap) for characterizing multilingual routing behavior.
- Demonstrates **cross-linguistic generalization** by replicating the collapse phenomenon in Japanese — a typologically unrelated language — confirming that the bottleneck is driven by pre-training underrepresentation, not language-intrinsic properties.
- Shows **CPT substantially outperforms SFT** for routing correction: CPT drives experts toward near-complete language agnosticism (99.2% shared experts in Nemotron base256), while SFT leaves deep-layer imbalance largely unremediated.
- Establishes a **direct correlation between routing improvements and downstream benchmark gains** across both Hebrew and Japanese, positioning routing metrics as principled diagnostics for multilingual MoE capacity.

## Methodology

The authors analyze two architecturally distinct ~30B MoE models with ~3B active parameters:

1. **Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base**: Pure Transformer MoE (48 layers, 128 experts, top-8 routing, trained on ~36T tokens across 119 languages).
2. **Nemotron-3-Nano-30B-A3B-Base**: Hybrid Mamba-2 + Transformer MoE (52 layers: 23 Mamba-2 + 23 MoE + 6 attention, 128+1 shared experts, top-6 routing, trained on ~25T tokens).

Each model undergoes fine-tuning on a balanced ~60% Hebrew / 40% English corpus via either CPT or SFT. The routing analysis processes ~1300 parallel Hebrew–English text chunks from TED talks and FLORES-200, capturing router logits via PyTorch forward hooks at every MoE layer. Special tokens are excluded to ensure analysis reflects genuine linguistic processing.

For the Japanese cross-linguistic study, three variants sharing the identical Qwen3-30B-A3B architecture are compared: the base model, Qwen3-Swallow (CPT on Japanese), and Shisa-Qwen3 (SFT only). The same evaluation data (translated from the Hebrew setting) ensures controlled comparison.

The load-balancing coefficient is set to α=0.002 based on a three-way ablation (α=0, 0.02, 0.002), as higher values caused training instability and zero values yielded insufficient routing adaptation.

## Results & Highlights

- **Deep-layer entropy collapse**: In both base models, Hebrew usage entropy drops sharply in the final layers while English remains stable (~5.0–5.5 bits in Qwen middle layers). The Gini coefficient mirrors this with a sharp rise for Hebrew in deep layers.
- **CPT correction (Nemotron)**: The Nemotron base256 model virtually eliminates deep-layer collapse, achieves 99.2% shared (language-agnostic) experts, and raises Hebrew benchmark average from 65.61 to 68.00 (+2.39 points). Israeli Trivia improves from 58.14 to 72.10.
- **CPT correction (Qwen)**: CPT Qwen raises entropy across most layers and shifts 68.0% of experts to shared status, but retains residual deep-layer imbalance. Hebrew average improves modestly (61.85 → 62.80).
- **SFT limitations**: The Nemotron instruct64 (SFT) achieves 96.1% shared experts but at a lower entropy baseline. Hebrew SFT shows volatile task performance: GSM8K rises from 60.0 to 72.0 but Hellaswag drops from 70.0 to 52.0.
- **Japanese replication**: Deep-layer collapse is qualitatively identical to Hebrew. Swallow CPT corrects collapse and improves 5/7 benchmarks (JamC-QA +6.3, en→ja WMT +6.3). Shisa SFT shows the same redistribution pattern: ARC +8.52 but GSM8K drops 9.3 points.
- **Cross-lingual cosine similarity**: The lowest similarity consistently occurs in the deepest layers across all models, confirming that final layers are the locus of language-specific divergence.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are researchers from what appears to be an Israeli AI/NLP group (the paper does not list explicit affiliations in the header). The work builds directly on the Hebrew LLM ecosystem (Hebrew LLM Leaderboard, Nemotron Hebrew variants) and collaborates with Japanese LLM projects (Swallow, Shisa). The analysis is methodologically rigorous, spanning two architectures and two typologically unrelated languages with controlled comparisons. The paper is well-positioned relative to concurrent work (Bandarkar et al., 2026) on cross-lingual MoE routing.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. This is the first systematic study comparing CPT vs. SFT for routing correction in MoE models, and the cross-linguistic validation (Hebrew + Japanese) is a strong contribution. The deep-layer routing collapse phenomenon, while hinted at in prior work, is characterized here with unprecedented granularity.
- **Rigor**: Strong. Multiple complementary metrics (entropy, Gini, LSI, cosine similarity), robustness controls (same-language split controls, tokenization statistics, threshold sensitivity analysis), and controlled architectural comparisons (Japanese study fixes architecture across three variants).
- **Reproducibility**: Moderate. The models are publicly available, but the training data composition and training recipes are not fully released. The routing analysis code is not provided, though the methodology is described in sufficient detail to re-implement.
- **Significance**: High. The finding that routing entropy serves as a diagnostic for multilingual capacity has practical implications for anyone adapting MoE models to low-resource languages. The CPT > SFT conclusion provides actionable guidance.
- **Red flags**: The architecture-level conclusions (hybrid > pure Transformer for correctability) are confounded by differences in pre-training data, routing design, and expert configuration — the authors acknowledge this appropriately. The Hebrew evaluation relies on a single domain (TED talks). The Japanese CPT-vs-SFT comparison uses models from different teams with different training recipes, introducing additional confounds.

## Strengths

- Comprehensive, multi-metric routing analysis with clear definitions and intuitive interpretations.
- Cross-linguistic validation across two typologically unrelated languages strengthens the generality claim.
- The controlled Japanese comparison (same architecture, three training variants) is a well-designed experiment.
- Practical implications are clearly articulated: routing metrics as diagnostics, CPT over SFT for adaptation.
- The Language Specificity Index (LSI) is a simple but effective novel metric for quantifying expert language specialization.

## Limitations

- Only two model families analyzed, both at ~30B scale; routing dynamics may differ at other scales or with different expert counts.
- The evaluation domain is limited to TED-style discourse; routing behavior may vary across technical, literary, or conversational text.
- Architecture-level claims are confounded by pre-training data, routing configuration, and expert design differences between Qwen and Nemotron.
- Tokenization differences across Hebrew, Japanese, and English are acknowledged but not explicitly controlled.
- The SFT models (instruct64, Shisa) come from different training recipes than the CPT models, making strict CPT-vs-SFT comparison partially confounded.

## Relevance

本文对MoE模型多语言路由行为的深入分析，为理解和改进低资源语言的LLM适配提供了重要的方法论框架。路由熵等指标可作为诊断工具，直接指导如何优化中文等语言在MoE架构中的表现，同时CPT优于SFT的结论对实际模型训练具有直接的参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does deep-layer routing collapse generalize to other low-resource language families (e.g., Bantu, Austronesian, Native American languages)?
- Can routing entropy be used as a training-time regularizer to prevent collapse from forming during pre-training itself?
- What is the minimum amount of CPT data needed to substantially correct routing collapse for a given language?
- How does routing collapse interact with parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods (LoRA, QLoRA) applied to MoE layers?
- Would a routing-aware auxiliary loss (targeting high entropy specifically in deep layers) be more efficient than general load balancing?
