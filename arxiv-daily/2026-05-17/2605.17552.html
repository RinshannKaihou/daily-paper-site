Q-LocalAdam: Memory-Efficient Client-Side Adaptive Optimization for Edge Federated Learning

Distribution-aware 8-bit quantization cuts optimizer memory by 3.37× while improving accuracy by +5.74pp under extreme non-IID — naive INT8 collapses to random guessing.
Vedant Waykole & Haroon R. Lone · IISER Bhopal · arXiv ↗
Key Metric
3.37× optimizer memory reduction
89.82 MB → 26.67 MB on CIFAR-100
Why This Matters
Drop-in replacement for client-side Adam with zero protocol changes. Directly relevant to deploying FL on resource-constrained edge devices where memory is the bottleneck.
Critical Edge
Only tested on CIFAR-10/100 + ResNet-18 with 10 clients. No transformers, no NLP, no realistic FL scale. Optimizer states reinitialized each round — may not generalize.

Methodology

Q-LocalAdam targets client-side Adam in cross-device FL. The key insight: momentum and variance have fundamentally different distributions — momentum is symmetric & bounded (±0.1, near-Gaussian), while variance spans 7–8 orders of magnitude (log-normal).

Momentum
Block-wise linear 8-bit quantization (min-max scaling per block)
Variance
Log-space 8-bit quantization (log(v+ε)) for constant relative precision
Block size
B=64 → 72 bytes vs. 256 bytes FP32 per block
Flow
Dequantize → Adam update (FP32) → Re-quantize. States reinitialized per round.

Multi-seed validation confirms significance: CIFAR-100 α=0.1 — 61.40±0.82% vs. 54.99±1.28% (p=0.005). Q-LocalAdam consistently shows lower variance than FP32.

Strengths

  • Elegant distribution-aware design — intuitive once the momentum/variance asymmetry is identified
  • Drop-in replacement: no federated protocol changes required
  • Naive INT8 collapse makes a compelling, vivid case for distribution-aware quantization
  • Comprehensive ablations: component, block size (B∈{32,64,128}), learning rate sensitivity
  • Implicit regularization from log-space quantization under extreme heterogeneity is a surprising finding

Limitations

  • Only CIFAR-10/100 with ResNet-18 — no transformers, no NLP, no speech
  • 10 clients / 5 per round — far from realistic cross-device FL (thousands of clients)
  • Optimizer states reinitialized each round; persistent-state behavior unknown
  • No comparison with Dettmers et al.'s 8-bit Adam adapted to FL
  • No theoretical convergence guarantees under quantization noise
  • Implicit regularization claim is post-hoc, not rigorously validated

Questions for Further Exploration

1How does Q-LocalAdam perform with persistent optimizer states across federated rounds?
2Does the log-normal variance structure hold for transformers and NLP tasks?
3What are the theoretical convergence guarantees under quantization noise?
