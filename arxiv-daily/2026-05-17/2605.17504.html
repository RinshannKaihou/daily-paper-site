Key Finding

Visual mechanistic interpretability is a KL-minimization problem — and hard constraints are the wrong answer

Reframing feature explanation as exponential tilting of the natural image distribution reveals systematic bias in Top-K retrieval, regularized optimization, and prompt-guided diffusion. EnergyDPS fixes this with SAE-feature-energy-guided diffusion posterior sampling.

Key Finding
Hard-constraint methods (Top-K, thresholding) provably concentrate mass on distribution boundaries and suffer threshold instability — KL can become +∞ under arbitrarily small perturbations. Soft exponential tilting qβ(x) ∝ p(x)exp(βs(x)) avoids this.
Key Metric
2.92 / 4.0 Human interpretability score for EnergyDPS vs 1.92 (MACO) and 1.95 (DiffExplainer). Also best QualiCLIP 0.5791 and NRQM 6.78.
Why This Matters
The three-axis taxonomy (O, C, S) could do for visual MI what the linear representation hypothesis did for LLM features: replace heuristics with a principled theory that explains why methods fail, not just that they fail.
Critical Edge
Only one model tested (DINOv3 ResNeXt Large). Blinded study has just 4 human raters — underpowered for strong statistical claims. Cost is 96 s/sample on H100; scaling to large SAE dictionaries remains open.

Methodology

  • Distributional framework: Feature interpretation = minimize KL(q∥p) subject to faithfulness Eq[s(X)] ≥ m, where p is the natural image prior and s is SAE feature activation.
  • Three-axis taxonomy (O, C, S): Object of study (SAE features vs raw neurons vs task-injected), Constraint (hard truncation vs KLSC soft), Sampling (retrieval / optimization / diffusion).
  • EnergyDPS: Training-free. Injects ∇s(x̂0) as energy guidance at each DDPM reverse step. No text prompts needed — directly targets the KLSC-induced distribution.
  • SAE backbone: CNN transcoder trained on DINOv3 ResNeXt Large, stage-2 layer-20, ImageNet-1K with Top-K sparsification.

Results & Highlights

  • GMM toy validation: KLSC achieves lower boundary mass, lower TV distance (0.5095 vs 0.6198), and lower instability (0.0187 vs 0.0346) than hard truncation at matched faithfulness.
  • Qualitative: EnergyDPS produces coherent patterns (heart, wing shapes) while MACO shows high-frequency artifacts and DiffExplainer produces less interpretable outputs.
  • AI raters agree: GPT-5.4 scores EnergyDPS 2.90; Gemini-3-flash-lite scores it 3.09 — consistent with human raters.
  • Ablation: Narrower diffusion prior (FFHQ faces-only) degrades quality, confirming broad natural image prior is critical. Object drift experiment validates task-injection distortion theory.

Strengths

  • Principled theory with formal proofs replacing heuristic approaches.
  • Clean taxonomy that explains why prior methods fail, not just that they fail.
  • Training-free and prompt-free — directly uses feature activations as energy.
  • Matched-faithfulness evaluation protocol should become community standard.

Limitations

  • Single model architecture (DINOv3 ResNeXt Large) only.
  • 96 s/sample on H100; expensive for large-scale SAE dictionaries.
  • 4 human raters — limited statistical power.
  • Natural image prior may miss abstract or non-visual model features.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does EnergyDPS scale to models with millions of SAE features (e.g., large ViTs)? Is feature-level amortization possible?
  • Can the distributional framework extend to multimodal MI (CLIP, LLaVA) where features span vision and language?
  • What happens when the diffusion prior pθ poorly approximates the true data distribution? Sensitivity analysis needed.
  • Could EnergyDPS combine with causal circuit analysis to show how features compose in computations?
  • Is KLSC applicable to language model MI where the “natural distribution” is over discrete tokens?
