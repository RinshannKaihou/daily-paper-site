Stop When Reasoning Converges:
Semantic-Preserving Early Exit for Reasoning Models

PUMA — Min, Vaccarino, Chen, Wu, Yona, Cheng · UIC · Google Research · UIUC

arXiv Paper GitHub
Key Finding

41–52% of reasoning tokens are generated after the model has already reached its final answer. PUMA detects this semantic redundancy and exits early—preserving accuracy while cutting 26.2% of tokens.

Key Metric
1.40×
Wall-clock speedup on DS-7B (1.28× on DS-14B). Redundancy detector overhead is only 0.4–1.1% of runtime.
Why This Matters
Plug-and-Play
No model weight or prompt modifications. Works across 5 LRMs, transfers zero-shot to code generation and vision-language reasoning.
Critical Edge
Deployment Cost
Requires a 0.6B detector model alongside the LRM. Similarity threshold (τ=0.35) is low—robustness on out-of-distribution reasoning is unproven.
Bonus
PUMA-RL
GRPO-internalized stopping achieves 34.9% TR + 67.0% accuracy, surpassing Full CoT's 63.0%—without any PUMA module at inference.

M Methodology

01
Redundancy Detector
Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B fine-tuned with InfoNCE + LoRA. Computes max cosine similarity of current step vs. previous k steps. Flags redundancy when sim > τ=0.35.
02
Answer Verification
At flagged candidates, probes trial answers via answer-inducing suffix. Exits only if confidence ≥ 0.98, answers are consistent across L=2 windows, and confidence doesn't drop > ε=0.03.
03
Loop Breaker
Fallback after 50+ steps: if m consecutive redundant steps detected and best trial confidence > 0.8, terminates generation. Handles sustained redundancy that evades verification.

? Open Questions

  • How does the detector handle deliberate recapitulation (proof by induction, Socratic dialogue)?
  • Can the 0.6B detector be distilled or quantized without losing discrimination?
  • Does the redundancy signal conflict with process reward model (PRM) guided search?
  • Can PUMA-RL scale to 70B+ models and non-math domains?
  • Is there a theoretical link between redundancy score and information-theoretic "surprise"?

Strengths

  • Novel signal: reasoning-level semantic redundancy, not just answer confidence
  • Clean separation of where to stop vs. whether to stop vs. fallback
  • No model weight or prompt modification required
  • Comprehensive evaluation: 5 models, 5+ benchmarks, code/VL, ablations, internalization
  • PUMA-RL surpasses Full CoT accuracy, proving the signal is fundamental

Limitations

  • 0.6B detector adds GPU memory and deployment complexity
  • Contrastive training data relies on LLM-generated novelty labels
  • Token reduction varies: 35.6% on DS-7B but only 20.1% on Nemotron-8B
  • Internalization tested on one 7B model, math data only
  • Step segmentation depends on structured output—poorly structured models may not benefit
