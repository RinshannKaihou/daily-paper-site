> **一句话总结：** PUMA 框架通过微调 Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B 作为轻量级冗余检测器，监测推理链中连续步骤的语义相似度，结合答案级验证（置信度+一致性）实现推理级早退出，在 5 个大型推理模型和 5 个基准上平均减少 26.2% token、1.4× 实际加速，同时保持准确率并提升保留推理链的质量。

> **TL;DR:** PUMA is a plug-and-play early-exit framework for Large Reasoning Models that detects reasoning-level semantic redundancy (when consecutive CoT steps stop adding novel progress) via a contrastive-trained embedding model (Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B with LoRA), then verifies candidate exits through answer confidence and consistency checks. Across 5 LRMs (7B–32B) and 5 benchmarks, it achieves 26.2% average token reduction with accuracy preserved, 1.40× wall-clock speedup on DS-7B, and the highest retained CoT quality among all compared methods—unlike answer-only early-exit baselines (DEER, Dynasor) that can trigger premature exits or suffer from expensive frequent probing.

## 核心贡献

- **识别推理级语义冗余作为早退出信号**：不同于现有方法仅依赖答案级信号（置信度/一致性），PUMA 发现当连续推理步不再产生新进展、而是重复已建立结论时，推理轨迹已收敛。分析发现 LRM 中 41–52% 的 token 生成于模型已得出最终答案之后。
- **提出 PUMA 框架（Progress-aware Unified Monitoring for Adaptive early exit）**：即插即用，不修改模型权重或提示。三个核心组件：（1）冗余检测器——微调 Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B 区分有新进展的步骤 vs 重复步骤；（2）答案验证——仅在检测器标记的候选点进行试验答案探测，检查置信度和一致性；（3）Loop Breaker——后阶段连续冗余的兜底退出机制。
- **低开销高收益**：冗余检测器仅占总推理时间的 0.4–1.1%（18ms），答案验证开销 0.2–0.57 秒/题。在 DS-7B 上实现 1.40× 实际加速，DS-14B 上 1.28×。
- **广泛的实验验证**：5 个 LRM（DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B/14B/32B, Llama-Nemotron-Nano-8B, Qwen3-30B-A3B）、5 个推理基准、额外覆盖代码生成（LiveCodeBench）和零样本视觉语言推理（MathVista/MathVision）。
- **可学习的停止策略**：PUMA 选定的退出位置可通过 SFT/DPO/GRPO 内化到模型中，PUMA-RL 在无需推理时 PUMA 模块的情况下实现 34.9% token 减少和 67.0% 平均准确率（超过 Full CoT 的 63.0%）。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies **reasoning-level semantic redundancy** as a complementary early-exit signal—41–52% of LRM tokens are generated after the final answer is already reached, creating massive redundancy.
- Proposes **PUMA**, a three-component plug-and-play framework: (1) Redundancy Detector (Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B fine-tuned with InfoNCE contrastive objective + LoRA) that computes max cosine similarity between current and previous k steps; (2) Answer Verification that probes trial answers only at detector-flagged candidates, checking confidence (geometric mean of token probs ≥ λ=0.98) and consistency across a verification window (L=2); (3) Loop Breaker fallback for sustained late-stage redundancy.
- Achieves **26.2% average token reduction** with accuracy preserved across 5 LRMs and 5 reasoning benchmarks, with **1.40× wall-clock speedup** on DS-7B and **1.28× on DS-14B**. Redundancy Detector overhead is only **0.4–1.1%** of total runtime.
- **Highest retained CoT quality** among all methods (54.3 avg vs 44.1 for Full-CoT), evaluated by GPT-5.4-thinking on completeness, coherence, conciseness, and justification.
- **Transferable signal**: zero-shot application to code generation (18–19% TR) and VL reasoning (23.8–33.6% TR) without retraining.
- **Learnable stopping**: PUMA-RL (GRPO with PUMA exit supervision) achieves 34.9% TR + 67.0% avg accuracy, outperforming Full CoT (63.0%) without any PUMA modules at inference.

## Methodology

PUMA operates online during CoT decoding without modifying model weights or prompts. It consists of three components:

**1. Redundancy Detector.** Initialized from Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B and fine-tuned with LoRA using an InfoNCE contrastive objective to distinguish steps that introduce new logical/semantic progress from those that restate, re-derive, or loop over prior content. Task-specific training is critical because generic semantic similarity fails to capture reasoning-step novelty (two steps may be topically similar yet advance the solution differently). Given detector f(·), redundancy score for step r_t is computed as the max cosine similarity with the previous k steps (default k=1, threshold τ_sim=0.35).

**2. Answer Verification.** When the detector flags a candidate exit, PUMA appends an answer-inducing suffix to the reasoning prefix and probes the model for a trial answer. Confidence is the geometric mean of token probabilities. PUMA collects L=2 consecutive flagged candidates, then exits only if: (a) first candidate confidence > λ=0.98, (b) all trial answers are identical, and (c) confidence does not decline by more than ε=0.03. If any check fails, generation resumes until the next flagged candidate. This decouples *where* to consider stopping from *whether* the exit is reliable.

**3. Loop Breaker.** A fallback activated after 50+ reasoning steps: if m consecutive redundant steps are detected and the best-confidence trial answer exceeds a weak gate (0.8), PUMA terminates. Verified exits take precedence; Loop Breaker handles sustained redundancy that evades verification.

**Training data**: The contrastive detector is trained on reasoning-step pairs labeled for novelty vs. redundancy (details in Appendix B.2). The full pipeline uses conservative hyperparameters calibrated on held-out data.

## Results & Highlights

- **Main results (Table 1)**: Across 5 LRMs and 5 benchmarks, PUMA achieves 26.2% avg TR while preserving accuracy. On DS-7B: 60.2% accuracy with 35.6% TR (Full CoT: 58.0%/0%). On Qwen3-30B: 81.7% accuracy with 23.2% TR (Full CoT: 81.7%/0%). PUMA sometimes *improves* accuracy by preventing late-stage drift after a correct answer.
- **vs. Prompt-based methods**: CCoT, CoD, and Plan&Budget often severely hurt accuracy on strong models (e.g., Qwen3-30B drops to 54.3%, 45.3%, 59.6% from 81.7%).
- **vs. Answer-level early exit**: Answer Convergence is aggressive but causes accuracy collapse (e.g., 26.7% on AIME24 for DS-7B). Dynasor has inconsistent efficiency. DEER is actually *slower* than Full CoT on both DS-7B and DS-14B due to frequent probing overhead.
- **CoT quality (Table 2)**: PUMA achieves 54.3 average quality score (GPT-5.4-thinking judge), ranking #1 in coherence (57.5), conciseness (40.2), and justification (54.8). Full-CoT scores only 44.1 (poor conciseness: 15.7).
- **Wall-clock speedup (Figure 3)**: 1.40× on DS-7B, 1.28× on DS-14B. DEER is slower than Full CoT on both models.
- **Generalization (Table 3)**: LiveCodeBench (code): 18–19% TR with ≤1.5pp pass@1 change. MathVista/MathVision (zero-shot VL): 23.8–33.6% TR with ≤1.5pp accuracy change—no retraining or hyperparameter tuning needed.
- **Internalization (Table 5)**: PUMA-RL (GRPO) achieves 67.0% avg accuracy + 34.9% TR vs. Full CoT's 63.0% + 0%, running pure vLLM without any PUMA modules. PUMA-DPO achieves the highest compression (48.8% TR) with 64.1% accuracy.
- **Ablation (Table 4)**: Removing the Redundancy Detector gate drops accuracy 4.1pp and requires 3.3× more probes. Removing the confidence gate causes a 6.5pp accuracy drop despite higher TR. Each component is complementary.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: The core insight—using *reasoning-level* semantic redundancy rather than answer-level signals alone—is a genuine conceptual advance. Framing early exit through the lens of semantic entropy (adapted from cross-output uncertainty to within-trajectory progress) is elegant. The three-component design (detector + verification + loop breaker) is well-architected.

**Rigor**: Evaluation is thorough: 5 models, 5 math/science benchmarks, additional code and VL tasks, ablation studies, wall-clock measurements, CoT quality evaluation via LLM-as-Judge, and an internalization study. The comparison against 7 baselines is comprehensive. Hyperparameter sensitivity is analyzed in appendices.

**Reproducibility**: Strong—open-source code, clearly specified hyperparameters (τ_sim=0.35, λ=0.98, ε=0.03, L=2, k=1), and results averaged over 3 seeds.

**Significance**: The wall-clock speedups are practical (1.4×), not just token-count reductions. The internalization experiments showing PUMA-RL surpassing Full CoT accuracy suggest reasoning-level redundancy is a fundamental signal, not just an engineering trick. However, the 26.2% average TR is moderate—prompt-based methods achieve much higher compression (though at severe accuracy cost). The value proposition is the accuracy-efficiency Pareto improvement.

**Potential concerns**:
- The redundancy detector is itself a 0.6B model—while small relative to 7B–32B LRMs, it adds deployment complexity and GPU memory.
- The training data for the contrastive detector uses GPT-generated labels (Appendix B.2), introducing a dependency on a specific LLM's judgment of "redundancy."
- The similarity threshold τ_sim=0.35 is quite low for cosine similarity, suggesting the detector operates in a regime where most steps are somewhat similar and the decision boundary is subtle—this raises questions about robustness on out-of-distribution reasoning patterns.
- The internalization experiments are limited to one base model (DS-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B) and math-focused data. Generalization to larger models and other domains is untested.
- The paper's own data shows that on Nemotron-8B, PUMA achieves only 15.7% TR on MATH-500 and 20.1% overall—significantly less than on other models—suggesting the signal strength varies across model families.

## Strengths

- Novel and well-motivated signal: reasoning-level semantic redundancy as a complementary exit criterion, grounded in the semantic entropy framework.
- Elegant three-component design with clear separation of concerns (where to stop vs. whether to stop vs. fallback).
- Plug-and-play: no model weight or prompt modification required.
- Comprehensive evaluation: 5 models, 5+ benchmarks, code/VL generalization, wall-clock measurements, CoT quality assessment, ablation, internalization.
- The internalization experiments are a notable contribution—showing that PUMA's exit positions are semantically informative (PUMA-SFT beats FixedExit-SFT by 12.8 accuracy points) and can teach models to self-regulate reasoning length.
- Open-source code with clear documentation.

## Limitations

- The 0.6B redundancy detector adds deployment complexity and GPU memory overhead, though the runtime overhead is negligible (0.4–1.1%).
- The contrastive training data relies on LLM-generated labels of reasoning-step novelty, which may not perfectly capture the boundary between productive restatement and true redundancy.
- Token reduction varies significantly across model families: 35.6% on DS-7B but only 20.1% on Nemotron-8B, suggesting the redundancy signal is not equally strong for all architectures.
- The internalization study is limited to one 7B model and math data—scalability to larger models and broader domains is untested.
- Step segmentation depends on natural paragraph/step boundaries, so models with poorly structured or very short outputs may not benefit.
- The paper acknowledges that broader domains may require additional detector calibration beyond text-only reasoning traces.

## Relevance

PUMA 直接解决了大型推理模型推理效率的核心痛点——过度思考导致 41–52% 的 token 浪费。其推理级语义冗余信号的设计思路（而非单纯依赖答案级置信度）为理解 CoT 推理过程本身提供了新视角。即插即用、可内化的特性使其对构建高效推理系统和训练更高效的推理模型均具有实际价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the redundancy detector perform on reasoning patterns that deliberately involve recapitulation (e.g., proof by induction, Socratic dialogue, pedagogical explanations)?
- Can the 0.6B detector be further compressed (distilled, quantized) without losing discrimination quality, to reduce the deployment footprint?
- What happens when PUMA is applied to models with explicit process reward model (PRM) training—does the redundancy signal conflict with PRM-guided search?
- How does the similarity threshold (τ_sim=0.35) interact with different embedding spaces—would a different base embedding model require substantial recalibration?
- Can the internalized stopping policy (PUMA-RL) scale to 70B+ models and non-math domains without losing effectiveness?
- Is there a theoretical connection between the redundancy score and the information-theoretic notion of "surprise" or "predictive uncertainty" in the reasoning trajectory?
