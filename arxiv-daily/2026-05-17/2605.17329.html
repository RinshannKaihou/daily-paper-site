LPG: Balancing Efficiency and Policy Reasoning in Latent Policy Guardrails

Compresses two-stage policy reasoning (intent analysis + policy matching) into 10 continuous latent tokens — supervised by teacher distillation and semantic reconstruction — to achieve near-reasoning-model accuracy at near-classifier speed.

Nanxi Li, Zhengyue Zhao, Chaowei Xiao Johns Hopkins University arxiv.org/abs/2605.17329 ↗
Key Finding
Just 10 latent tokens encode policy reasoning well enough to hit 84.5 % safety accuracy — 11× faster than explicit chain-of-thought.
Key Metric
84.5 %
Avg safety accuracy & 77.9 F1 across GuardSet-X & DynaBench at 748 ms latency.
Why This Matters
First
Latent reasoning applied to policy-grounded safety moderation — bridges the accuracy-latency gap for dynamic guardrails with clause-level auditability.
Latency Gain
×11
Faster than Qwen3-4B-Thinking (8 383 ms) and 5.4× faster than GuardReasoner-8B.
Critical Edge

DynaGuard-8B's verdicts swing by 7.4 accuracy points under policy shuffling and its counterfactual flip rate is only 36 % — revealing reliance on positional artifacts rather than genuine policy comprehension. LPG's structured anchoring mitigates this. However, a 10-point gap vs GPT-4o (87.77 vs 77.85 on PolicyGuardBench) remains open.

Methodology

Built on Qwen3-4B + LoRA (rank 128). Given content x and policy document P, produce a verdict and violated clause indices.

Input → 4 latent steps (intent) → 6 latent steps (policy) → Explicit verdict

Three Supervision Signals

  • Teacher hidden-state distillation — aligns student/teacher hidden states at answer position across all layers (removing it drops ~20 points).
  • Summary reconstruction — each latent stage reconstructs a compact teacher summary through the base LM.
  • Reference reasoning loss — cross-entropy regularizer on teacher's full explicit reasoning.

Training

40 k records from DynaBench, GuardSet-X, BeaverTails, Aegis-AI, SaladBench, Toxic-Chat, XSTest v2. Qwen3-32B teacher generates structured reasoning. Policies (K ~ Uniform{4,10}) are reshuffled per example.

Strengths

  • Strong accuracy-latency Pareto frontier: near-reasoning accuracy at near-classifier speed.
  • Counterfactual & shuffling diagnostics expose real baseline weaknesses — new evaluation paradigm.
  • Semantic latent deliberation is principled: each component targets a specific failure mode.
  • Policy anchoring to clause indices provides auditability.
  • Strong OOD generalization (96.44 F1 HarmBench).

Limitations

  • 10-token budget leaves performance on the table — accuracy increases monotonically with more tokens.
  • Evaluated only on English; multilingual policy reasoning is not addressed.
  • Teacher rationales (Qwen3-32B) conditioned on ground-truth labels may propagate bias.
  • No adversarial evaluation targeting the latent pathway.
  • Gap vs GPT-4o on PolicyGuardBench (77.85 vs 87.77).

Relevance

本文与"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"研究方向高度相关：LPG 利用隐空间连续表示来编码推理过程，通过教师隐状态蒸馏和语义重建来监督隐变量的信息内容，为理解隐空间推理的信息编码提供了实证案例。策略洗牌和反事实诊断实验也涉及模型鲁棒性和可靠性分析。

Open Questions
