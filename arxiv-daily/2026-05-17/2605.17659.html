Standard losses + positive-bias activations produce systematic negative weight drift, inducing up to 90% sparsity with a sharp accuracy cliff at ~70%.
Bug or Feature²: Weight Drift, Activation Sparsity, and Spikes — E. Shvetsov, A. Serkov, S. Viacheslav, R. Dmitry, V. Goloshchapov, E. Burnaev
2026-05-17
optimization activation-functions training-dynamics transformers sparsity
Key Metric
90%
activation sparsity induced by negative weight drift under ReLU; accuracy cliff at ~70% sparsity (power-law N = 16.72)
Why This Matters
Proves why sparsity emerges during training and identifies a hard accuracy cliff at ~70% — directly actionable for designing efficient sparse inference systems.
Critical Edge
All language modeling results are on GPT-nano (124M params) with only validation perplexity — no evidence of scaling to production LLMs or downstream task improvement.

Methodology

  • Formal proof: Analyzes the effective weight matrix Veff (composing all layers after layer l). Theorem 1.1 establishes zero-mean rows with non-negative cross-correlation via ReLU survival-conditioning. Theorems 1.2–1.3 prove expected gradient w.r.t. positive pre-activations is non-negative for MSE and cross-entropy. Result holds locally for any contiguous linear+ReLU stack.
  • Drift measurement: Average absolute Z-score |wt − w0|/std(w0) per layer. Tested with SGD, SGD+momentum, Adam across learning rates. Covariance term validated as negligible relative to weight mean.
  • Sparsity control: Percentile Centering (PC) — shifts pre-activations by the q-th percentile so exactly q% fall below zero after ReLU. Evaluated alongside Top-K across 79 configurations.
  • Squared activations: Layer-wise activation range analysis in GPT-nano. Clipping thresholds (15, 50) evaluated empirically.
  • Accumulation Stop: EMA (γ=0.9999) during warm-up, then freeze statistics. Tested on DiT-S/2 (H200) and MaxViT-Tiny (A100).

Strengths

  • Clean formal framework (effective weight matrix decomposition) that makes drift mechanism transparent and proofs accessible
  • Comprehensive 79-configuration sweep with well-chosen baselines (Top-K, ReLUfication)
  • Power-law fit quantifying the sparsity cliff (N=16.72) is a concrete, reusable result
  • Honest, detailed limitations section (7 enumerated points)
  • Practical recommendations grounded in evidence: ReLUfication, clipped ReLU², Accumulation Stop

Limitations

  • Theoretical results hold strictly only at initialization with zero-mean i.i.d. weights; formal extension to smooth activations is unsupported
  • Language modeling evaluated only on GPT-nano (124M) — no scaling evidence to production LLMs
  • Clipping thresholds are empirical and architecture-dependent with no principled selection method
  • Accumulation Stop tested only on fixed-input architectures; autoregressive models may need different treatment
  • Squared activation benefits limited to autoregressive models — no improvement over plain GELU on MaxViT
  • No downstream NLP task evaluation for GPT-nano (only validation perplexity)

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the ~70% sparsity cliff hold at billion-parameter scale (LLaMA-7B, 70B)?
  • Can the power-law exponent N=16.72 be predicted from architectural properties (width, depth, skip-connection structure)?
  • Is there a principled way to determine optimal clipping thresholds for squared activations?
  • How does Accumulation Stop interact with variable-length sequences in autoregressive models?
  • Why do squared activations benefit autoregressive LMs but not vision transformers — modality, objective, or attention pattern effect?
  • Does GELU²'s advantage persist with larger models and transfer to downstream benchmarks (MMLU, HumanEval)?
