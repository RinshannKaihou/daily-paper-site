Key Finding

End-to-end unstructured mask learning becomes tractable for LLMs via per-weight Bernoulli–Gumbell-sigmoid relaxation—improving 6-task average zero-shot accuracy by +2.59 pts over ADMM.

LEAP proves that MaskLLM/PATCH's categorical-over-patterns parameterization hits a combinatorial wall (~101229 valid masks per row). Replacing it with independent Bernoulli logits collapses the parameter space to O(mn), enabling gradient-based mask optimization directly on language-modeling loss.

Methodology

  • Mask parametrization — Logit matrix P ∈ ℝm×n parallel to each weight matrix. Stochastic mask M = σ((αP+g)/τ) via Gumbel-sigmoid relaxation of Bernoulli.
  • Wanda warm-start — Entries selected by Wanda get logit +s, others −s, giving optimization a principled initial point.
  • Annealing — Scale α ramps 25→350 (push sigmoid to {0,1}); temperature τ decays 4.0→0.05 (sharpen distribution). Early iterations explore, later ones commit.
  • Global sparsity regularizer — Lsparse enforces an overall density budget ρ while allowing per-layer density to adapt end-to-end.
  • Magnitude-aware weight regularizer — Lweight biases toward retaining high-magnitude weights, preventing degenerate minima. Ablation: removing this drops accuracy 2.06 pts.
  • Full objective — L = LLM(W̃;X) + Lsparse + Lweight. Only P is trained; W is frozen. 2,000 steps, batch 256, seq-len 4096, SlimPajama calibration.

Results & Highlights

  • +5.40 pts best single gain on LLaMA-3.2 1B @60% sparsity (50.39 vs 44.99).
  • Perplexity — LLaMA-3.1 8B @60%: 8.82 (LEAP) vs 14.10 (ADMM), a 37.5% reduction.
  • Gains widen at higher sparsity — Near-tie at 50% on 8B (+0.21) becomes +3.86 gap at 60%.
  • 4.6× cheaper than MaskLLM — 276 H100 GPU-hrs for 8B vs MaskLLM's 1,280 A100 GPU-hrs for 7B.
  • Ablation — Weight regularizer (λ₂) is most load-bearing; removing it drops acc 2.06 pts and increases PPL by 1.67.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Principled resolution of a fundamental scalability limitation in prior learnable-mask methods.
  • Consistent improvements across all tested settings; especially large gains at 60% sparsity where baselines degrade sharply.
  • Lightweight training recipe: only 2,000 steps, no weight updates. Frozen-weight design preserves pretrained calibration.
  • Masks directly compatible with Flash-LLM, SpInfer, MACKO kernels.

Limitations

  • Compute cost: 276 GPU-hours for 8B vs minutes for Wanda/SparseGPT.
  • Only evaluated up to 8B; behavior at 70B+ is unknown.
  • Logit matrix P doubles trainable state during mask learning.
  • Near-uniform per-block sparsity allocation; non-uniform allocation benefits unexplored.
  • No generation quality, safety benchmarks, or long-tail robustness evaluation.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does LEAP scale to models beyond 8B (e.g., LLaMA-3.1 70B, 405B)? Does the near-uniform sparsity allocation hold?
  2. What are the effects of joint weight-and-mask optimization layered on top of LEAP masks?
  3. Can LEAP masks be combined with quantization (INT4/INT8) for compound compression without compounding accuracy loss?
  4. How do LEAP-pruned models perform on generative tasks (code generation, long-form reasoning) beyond zero-shot classification?
  5. Is the near-uniform per-block allocation an artifact of the global sparsity regularizer, or does it reflect a fundamental property of transformer pruning at 50–60% density?
