Key Finding

MoE models suffer deep-layer routing collapse for low-resource languages — continual pre-training (CPT) corrects it far better than SFT.

In both Qwen3-30B and Nemotron-3-Nano, Hebrew and Japanese tokens concentrate on a narrow expert subset in final layers while English maintains uniform distribution. CPT on balanced bilingual data drives experts toward near-complete language agnosticism.

Ori Bar Joseph, Smadar Arvatz, Noam Kayzer, Dan Revital, Sarel Weinberger

Key Metric
99.2%
Shared language-agnostic experts achieved by Nemotron CPT (base256). Hebrew benchmark avg rose from 65.61 → 68.00 (+2.39 pts).
Why This Matters
Routing as diagnostic
Routing entropy directly correlates with downstream benchmark gains — positioning these metrics as principled diagnostics for multilingual MoE capacity in any language.
Critical Edge
Confounded
Architecture-level conclusions are confounded by pre-training data, routing config, and expert design differences. SFT vs CPT comparison uses models from different teams with different recipes.

⚙ Methodology

Qwen3-30B-A3B Nemotron-3-Nano-30B
  • Two architecturally distinct ~30B MoE models (~3B active params): pure Transformer (48L, 128 experts, top-8) and hybrid Mamba-2 + Transformer MoE (52L, 128+1 experts, top-6).
  • Fine-tuned on balanced ~60% Hebrew / 40% English via CPT or SFT.
  • Routing captured via PyTorch forward hooks on ~1300 parallel Hebrew–English chunks (TED talks, FLORES-200).
  • Japanese cross-linguistic validation: Qwen3 base vs Swallow (CPT) vs Shisa (SFT) — same architecture, controlled comparison.
  • Load-balancing coefficient α=0.002 from three-way ablation.

★ Results & Highlights

  • Entropy collapse: Hebrew usage entropy drops sharply in final layers; English stays ~5.0–5.5 bits through middle layers.
  • Nemotron CPT: Virtually eliminates collapse → 99.2% shared experts. Israeli Trivia jumps 58.14 → 72.10.
  • Qwen CPT: Shifts 68.0% experts to shared; retains residual deep-layer imbalance. Hebrew avg 61.85 → 62.80.
  • SFT volatility: Nemotron SFT: GSM8K 60→72 but Hellaswag 70→52. Shisa SFT: ARC +8.52 but GSM8K −9.3.
  • Japanese replication: Collapse is qualitatively identical. Swallow CPT corrects it (JamC-QA +6.3).
  • Lowest cosine similarity consistently in deepest layers across all models.

✓ Strengths

  • Comprehensive multi-metric routing analysis with clear definitions and intuitive interpretations.
  • Cross-linguistic validation across two typologically unrelated languages strengthens generality.
  • Controlled Japanese experiment (same arch, three training variants) is well-designed.
  • Practical implications clearly articulated: routing metrics as diagnostics, CPT > SFT.
  • Language Specificity Index (LSI) is a simple but effective novel metric.

⚠ Limitations

  • Only two model families at ~30B scale; routing may differ at other scales or expert counts.
  • Evaluation limited to TED-style discourse; behavior may vary across technical or literary text.
  • Architecture claims confounded by pre-training data & routing design differences.
  • Tokenization differences acknowledged but not explicitly controlled.
  • SFT models come from different training recipes, partially confounding CPT-vs-SFT comparison.

? Questions for Further Exploration
