When Efficiency Backfires: Cascading LLMs Trigger Cascade Failure under Adversarial Attack

Zehan Sun (SEU) · Dingfan Chen (MPI-IS) · Songze Li (SEU)

The first systematic study of adversarial vulnerabilities in LLM cascade systems. A joint optimization attack framework simultaneously degrades accuracy by up to 84.6% and inflates inference cost by up to 148.9% with a single adversarial suffix — revealing inherent structural fragility in cascade architectures.

Key Finding
Cascade architectures are structurally more vulnerable than standalone models — even random noise suffixes cause degradation; adversarial suffixes can simultaneously crush accuracy and explode cost.
Key Metric
84.6% accuracy drop  /  148.9% cost increase — with a single adversarial suffix across 9 datasets & 33 cascade configs.
Why This Matters
Cascade systems (Azure AI Foundry, FrugalGPT) are entering production. This proves their efficiency gains carry a hidden security tax that grows under attack.
Critical Edge
First joint-target attack: decomposes adversarial suffix into prediction-targeted & decision-targeted components with priority constraints that prevent self-correction across cascade stages.

Methodology

Targets LLM cascade systems (FrugalGPT paradigm) where lightweight models handle easy queries and stronger models are invoked only when needed, with decision modules controlling early termination vs. escalation.

Threat model: Adversary appends an adversarial suffix to input text within a bounded perturbation neighborhood, without altering model parameters or routing policies. Both white-box and black-box (transfer) settings.

Joint-target attack: Decomposes the adversarial suffix into δ(f) (prediction degradation) and δ(g) (decision escalation), optimized via alternating constrained sequential collaborative optimization under cascade dependencies.

Adaptive pass-rate control: Parameter p ∈ [0,1] controls the fraction of already-compromised samples escalated, balancing accuracy degradation vs. cost inflation.

Analytical proof: Under mild conditions, cascade systems incur strictly higher error than using only the strongest model, and adversarial perturbations amplify this gap via routing shift and conditional gap amplification.

Results & Highlights

  • Up to 84.6% prediction accuracy reduction (p=0)
  • Up to 148.9% increase in normalized token cost and 108.9% increase in execution time (p=1)
  • Up to 81.4% increase in jailbreak success rate vs. attacking standalone strong LLMs
  • Random (unoptimized) noise suffixes already cause noticeable degradation
  • Adversarial suffixes transfer across cascade architectures, including API-based proprietary models
  • Attack remains effective on intermediate stages in 3-layer cascades
  • Evaluated on 9 datasets, 4 task types, 12 LLM architectures, 33 cascade configurations

Strengths

  • First to formalize cascade-specific adversarial vulnerability
  • Elegant, theoretically motivated joint-target framework
  • Comprehensive experimental coverage
  • Analytical proof cascades are inherently more fragile
  • Immediate practical relevance for industry

Limitations

  • Limited to FrugalGPT-style cascades; other routing architectures not tested
  • No defense mechanisms proposed
  • Suffix-based perturbations may be detectable
  • Very deep cascades not explored
  • Attack optimization cost not thoroughly analyzed

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. What architectural modifications could make cascade decision modules more robust against adversarial manipulation?
  2. Can input sanitization be integrated into cascade systems without negating efficiency advantages?
  3. How do these vulnerabilities extend to mixture-of-agents, tool-use pipelines, or RAG chains?
  4. Is there a fundamental trade-off between cascade efficiency gains and adversarial robustness?
  5. How effective are existing defenses (adversarial training, certified robustness, smoothed classifiers) against cascade-specific threats?

Relevance / 相关性

This paper reveals systemic security risks in LLM cascade deployments. Any production system using multi-model collaboration (cascade, routing, MoE) for inference cost reduction should account for adversarial robustness alongside efficiency. The finding that cascades can be more vulnerable than standalone models challenges the assumption that efficiency gains come for free.
