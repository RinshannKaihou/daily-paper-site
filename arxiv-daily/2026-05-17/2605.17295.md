> **TL;DR:** DISA decouples partition-function estimation from policy learning in distribution-matching RL for LLMs by using offline importance sampling to pre-compute and freeze the partition function, eliminating error coupling that plagues online methods like FlowRL, while matching or exceeding accuracy on math/code benchmarks and preserving significantly more solution diversity.

## Key Contributions

- Identifies policyвҖ“partition-function coupling as the core obstacle in distribution-matching RL for LLM post-training: online co-training of Z_П• with ПҖ_Оё on a shared loss injects partition-function error into policy gradients with no separable quality signal.
- Proposes DISA (Decoupled Importance-Sampled Anchoring), a three-stage pipeline: (1) offline IS estimation of per-prompt partition functions, (2) amortization via a lightweight MLP regressor that is frozen, (3) anchored RL using the frozen partition function with stop-gradient through ПҲ.
- Proves formal guarantees: exact frozen Z preserves the reward-tilted optimum; prompt-only bias preserves on-policy stationarity; residual Z error enters through a bounded CauchyвҖ“Schwarz perturbation envelope.
- Empirically validates on 6 math benchmarks (AIME 2024/2025, AMC 2023, MATH-500, Minerva, OlympiadBench) and 3 code benchmarks (LiveCodeBench, CodeForces, HumanEval+) across Qwen2.5-7B and Qwen3-4B-Base, matching or exceeding FlowRL and outperforming GRPO by up to 16.1 avg points.
- Shows DISA retains the largest fraction of backbone strategy diversity (3.72/5.0 on AIME LLM-as-judge vs. GRPO ~3.0), and outperforms SFT distillation by up to 13.8 Mean@8 points on the same offline trajectories.

## Methodology

DISA is built on the key insight that the per-prompt partition function Z(q) in distribution-matching RL depends only on ПҖ_ref, the reward transform, and ОІ вҖ” it is policy-independent and can be estimated offline before RL begins.

**Stage 1 вҖ” Offline IS Estimation:** For each training prompt q, draw N=8 trajectories from a proposal model p_T (Qwen3-235B-A22B-Instruct), compute importance weights w(q,o) = (ПҖ_ref/p_T)В·exp(ОІrМғ), and estimate log Z(q) via the logsumexp aggregator (unbiased on the linear scale, O(1/N) Jensen bias on log scale).

**Stage 2 вҖ” Amortized Denoising:** Fit a small MLP regressor g_ПҲ by least-squares regression to the per-prompt IS labels, producing a generalizable log Z(q) estimator. Validation MSE provides a separable diagnostic of partition-function quality. The regressor is frozen as g_{ПҲ*} after this stage.

**Stage 3 вҖ” Anchored RL:** Substitute the frozen g_{ПҲ*}(q) for log Z_П•(q) in the trajectory-balance loss with stop-gradient on ПҲ. The policy ПҖ_Оё is updated via the residual L_DISA = E[ОЈ Пү_i В· (g_{ПҲ*}(q) + log ПҖ_Оё(o_i|q) - log ПҖ_ref(o_i|q) - ОІrМғ(q,o_i))ВІ]. The partition function acts as a fixed per-prompt scalar вҖ” no gradient flows through it.

**Theoretical framework (Appendix C):** The partition function Z(q) = ОЈ_o ПҖ_ref(o|q)В·exp(ОІrМғ(q,o)) is independent of ПҖ_Оё. DISA preserves the Boltzmann fixed point ПҖ*(o|q) вҲқ ПҖ_ref(o|q)В·exp(ОІrМғ(q,o)) exactly when g_{ПҲ*} is exact, and residual error is bounded by вҲҡ(ПғВІ_g + 2Пғ_gВ·L_TB) via Cauchy-Schwarz.

## Results & Highlights

- **Math reasoning (Mean@8, Qwen3-4B-Base):** DISA 65.5 > FlowRL 64.8 > GSPO 62.7 > GRPO 49.4 > Vanilla 29.7. DISA leads on AIME24 (67.1), AIME25 (59.2), AMC23 (95.3).
- **Math reasoning (Mean@8, Qwen2.5-7B):** DISA 33.5 вүҲ FlowRL 33.2 > GRPO 32.8 > GSPO 29.3 > Vanilla 23.4.
- **Code generation (pass@16, Qwen3-4B-Base avg):** DISA leads at 31.2 > GRPO 30.1 > GSPO 28.6 > FlowRL 28.2. On pass@1 all methods cluster within ~1 point.
- **Strategy diversity (AIME, Qwen2.5-7B):** DISA 3.72/5.0 >> FlowRL 3.40 >> GSPO 2.97 вүҲ GRPO 3.02, with backbone at 3.90. DISA retains 95% of backbone diversity.
- **SFT comparison:** On identical teacher trajectories, DISA outperforms LoRA-SFT by +5.7 avg (Qwen2.5-7B) and +13.8 avg (Qwen3-4B-Base), confirming the soft distribution-matching objective recovers landscape information that hard r=1 SFT discards.
- **Ablation вҖ” proposal strength:** Switching from 235B to 4B proposal drops accuracy from 65.5вҶ’37.1 (bias-variance: weaker proposal inflates IS variance, not bias), with regressor Val-MSE remaining comparably low.
- **Ablation вҖ” temperature ОІ:** ОІ=10 under-tilts (loses pass@16), ОІ=20 over-tilts (variance cliff), ОІ=15 is the stable plateau matching FlowRL's default.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The core insight вҖ” that the partition function in distribution-matching RL is policy-independent and can be estimated offline вҖ” is clean and consequential. The three-stage pipeline (IS вҶ’ regression вҶ’ frozen RL) is well-motivated and the theoretical framework is sound.
- **Rigor: High.** Formal proofs for exact preservation, stationarity under bias, and bounded perturbation. Honest about O(1/N) Jensen bias in log-space IS. Ablations on proposal strength and ОІ follow predicted bias-variance patterns.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate-High.** Uses open-weight Qwen models and standard benchmarks (AIME, MATH-500, LiveCodeBench). The pipeline uses only stdlib Python + standard RL infrastructure. Hyperparameters are provided in appendices. The 235B proposal model is large but publicly available.
- **Significance: High.** Addresses a fundamental design flaw in GFlowNet-based LLM post-training. The decoupling principle is broadly applicable. Results are strong across multiple benchmarks and backbones.
- **Red flags:**
  - The proposal model (235B) is much stronger than the policies being trained (4B/7B) вҖ” DISA's performance depends heavily on proposal quality. The 4B-proposal ablation shows dramatic degradation, raising questions about applicability when a strong proposal is unavailable.
  - The IS stage requires N=8 samples from a 235B model per prompt, which is expensive at scale.
  - The diversity evaluation uses GPT-4o-mini as judge on only 60 AIME problems вҖ” a relatively small and potentially biased evaluation.
  - Code benchmarks show modest differences between methods; the main gains are concentrated in math.

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical motivation: partition function is policy-independent, so estimate it offline.
- Strict separation of partition-function estimation from policy learning in data, gradients, loss, and diagnostics.
- The frozen regressor provides a separable quality diagnostic (Val MSE) that online-coupled methods lack.
- Consistent improvements across two backbones and nine benchmarks, with particularly large gains on contest-level math (AIME, AMC).
- Strong diversity retention: DISA loses only 0.18 points from the backbone's 3.90 diversity score, vs. ~0.9 for GRPO/GSPO.
- SFT comparison on identical trajectories cleanly isolates the value of distribution matching over distillation.

## Limitations

- **Dependence on strong proposal:** The 4B-proposal ablation (37.1 vs. 65.5) shows DISA's quality is tightly coupled to proposal strength. Domains without strong teacher models may see diminished returns.
- **Computational overhead:** Stage 1 requires sampling from a 235B model (N=8 per prompt Г— |D_train| prompts), which is non-trivial even if done once.
- **Code benchmarks less differentiated:** pass@1 differences are within noise margins; pass@16 shows DISA advantage but the gap over FlowRL is small (31.2 vs. 28.2 on Qwen3-4B-Base).
- **Diversity evaluation limited:** 60 problems from AIME only, using GPT-4o-mini as judge with a 1-5 Likert scale вҖ” broader evaluation across domains would strengthen the diversity claims.
- **Finite-batch reward normalization:** The paper acknowledges that group-normalized rewards are plug-in approximations to the fixed transform rМғ assumed in the theory, contributing to partition-function error. The practical impact of this approximation gap is not fully quantified.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can DISA be adapted for domains where no strong proposal model exists (e.g., novel scientific reasoning, low-resource languages) вҖ” perhaps via iterative self-distillation as the authors suggest?
- How does DISA scale to much larger policy models (e.g., 32B, 72B)? Does the IS-label quality degrade as the gap between proposal and policy narrows?
- What is the compute-cost tradeoff between DISA's offline IS stage (235B sampling) versus FlowRL's additional online partition-function training steps?
- Could the frozen regressor g_{ПҲ*} be shared or transferred across different training runs or backbones, amortizing the offline cost further?
- How does DISA interact with process-level (per-step) reward models rather than outcome-level binary rewards?
