Exact Convex Reformulations of Linear Neural Networks via Completely Positive Lifting

Karthik Prakhya & Alp Yurtsever
Umeå University, Sweden · Funded by WASP
Key Finding

Deep linear neural networks under squared loss admit an exact convex reformulation via completely positive lifting — and the lifted dimension is only d₀ + dₙ, independent of network depth or sample size.

F

Nonconvex training of deep linear nets is exactly equivalent to a convex conic program.

  • Step 1: Deep network ŷ = Wₙ…W₁X reduces to bilinear factorization min_{U,V} ||UV'X − Y||²_F with rank constraint r = min dₖ.
  • Step 2: Lift to rank-constrained SDP via W = [U;V][U;V]', then reformulate rank with complementarity: exists W' ≻= 0, tr(W') = r, ⟨W, I−W'⟩ ≤ 0.
  • Step 3: Quadratically constrained QP lifted to a completely positive program over cone CP̄ᵧ where &Kmacr; = R₊ × Sᵤ₊ × Sᵤ₊ × Sᵤ₊. All nonconvexity is encoded in the cone constraint alone.

Both a vectorized formulation (Theorem 1) and a Kronecker-product formulation (Theorem 2) are provided with complete equivalence proofs.

Results & Highlights

  • Exact equivalence: Convex reformulation has the same global optimum as the original nonconvex problem.
  • Dimension independence: Lifted dim = d₀ + dₙ, not depth or sample count.
  • Structural insight: All nonconvexity captured by a single generalized CP cone membership.
  • First-of-its-kind: Exact conic characterization of deep linear factorization nonconvexity.
  • Not algorithmic: Authors explicitly position this as structural/theoretical, not a solver.

Significance
  • Novelty is high — first exact convex reformulation for deep linear nets via CP lifting.
  • Proofs are rigorous, self-contained, building on established copositive optimization results.
  • No code or numerical experiments provided; purely theoretical.
  • No red flags — authors are transparent about computational intractability.

Strengths

  • Clean, systematic three-step pipeline construction.
  • Remarkably compact lifted space (independent of depth & sample size).
  • Precise geometric characterization of linear network nonconvexity.
  • Bridges neural network optimization and copositive programming literatures.
  • Well-written with clear notation and thorough proofs.

Limitations

  • CP cone is computationally intractable — cannot be used as a practical training algorithm.
  • No numerical experiments, even on small synthetic problems.
  • Limited to linear nets with squared loss; extension to ReLU not discussed.
  • No exploration of outer approximations (e.g., SOS hierarchies) for tractable relaxations.
  • Practical implications for real neural network optimization landscapes remain unclear.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can tractable outer approximations of the generalized CP cone (e.g., sum-of-squares hierarchies) preserve useful information about global optima?
  2. Does the lifting strategy extend to networks with ReLU or polynomial activations?
  3. How do gradient-based optimization trajectories on the nonconvex problem relate to the geometry of the lifted convex set?
  4. Could the complementarity condition perspective yield new implicit regularization analyses for gradient descent?
  5. What is the tightest tractable relaxation of the CP cone for neural network training structures?
