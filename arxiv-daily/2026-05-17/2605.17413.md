> **一句话总结：** 本文系统比较了投影、LoRA微调等方法移除LLM安全对齐的效果，发现单向量拒答投影在提升安全任务分数（0.46→0.50）的同时大幅增加越界不安全合规（0.10→0.47），而任务导向LoRA能将安全分数提升至0.87且越界合规仅0.13，证明对齐移除应被视为效用-风险前沿而非简单的"去审查"。

> **TL;DR:** This paper evaluates four families of alignment-removal interventions (prompting, activation projection, representation control, LoRA fine-tuning) on Qwen2.5 and SmolLM2 models using a 60-prompt security benchmark (SECURITY-AR). Task-only LoRA achieves the best utility-risk tradeoff (security 0.87, spillover 0.13), while single-vector refusal projection barely improves security (0.46→0.50) but dramatically increases unsafe out-of-scope compliance (0.10→0.47).

## 核心贡献

- 将对齐移除形式化为**变换-评估问题**，定义了拒答率(R)、尝试率(A)、任务分数(S)、通用能力保留(G)、越界不安全合规(H)五项独立指标，构成效用-风险帕累托前沿
- 提出SECURITY-AR基准，包含30个授权安全任务（漏洞分诊、补丁分析、安全修复）、20个通用任务、10个越界探测，采用红线发布边界——不公开可利用的有效载荷或原始越界输出
- 系统比较四种干预族（提示、激活投影、表示控制、LoRA微调），证明它们在效用-风险前沿上产生根本不同的权衡，不支持仅凭拒答率下降推断能力恢复
- 任务导向LoRA（无拒答抑制数据）实现了最佳权衡：安全分数从0.46提升至0.87，通用分数0.83，越界合规仅0.13

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes alignment removal as a **transformation-evaluation problem** with five independent metrics (refusal, attempt, security score, general retention, unsafe spillover), framing the outcome as a Pareto utility-risk frontier rather than a one-dimensional refusal test
- Introduces **SECURITY-AR**, a 60-prompt benchmark with responsible release design: sanitized prompts, aggregate-only spillover reporting, withheld operational payloads, and executable secure-repair validators
- Provides the first systematic comparison of four intervention families (prompt baselines, activation projection, representation-control projections, LoRA fine-tuning) under a shared scoring harness, showing they occupy distinct positions on the utility-risk frontier
- Demonstrates that **task-only LoRA** (training only on authorized security data without explicit refusal suppression) achieves the best tradeoff: security 0.87, general 0.83, spillover 0.13
- Shows single-vector refusal projection is unreliable as a capability-recovery mechanism: it barely improves security scores while increasing unsafe spillover nearly 5x

## Methodology

The paper proposes ABLATING SAFETY as a controlled evaluation protocol with two tiers:

**Projection pilot** (4 models × 4 conditions × 26 tasks = 416 completions): Tests prompt baselines, random projection controls, and single-vector refusal-direction projection on Qwen2.5-0.5B/1.5B/3B-Instruct and SmolLM2-360M-Instruct. Refusal directions are extracted by computing the mean activation difference between refusal-triggering and matched non-refusal prompts at a selected residual-stream layer. The hook projects out the refusal component: h' = h − α(h^T r̂)r̂.

**Extended LoRA evaluation** (3 Qwen2.5 models × 11 conditions × 60 prompts = 1,980 completions): Adds harmless projection, topic-matched projection, rank-4 subspace projection, and four LoRA variants (retention-only, task-only, refusal-suppression, refusal-suppression-with-retention). LoRA uses rank 16, α=32, AdamW with lr=5e-5, training on 240 authorized-security and 480 benign-retention examples with no overlap with held-out evaluation.

Scoring combines refusal classifiers, attempt classifiers, sandbox validators (for executable secure-repair tasks), rubric-based review, and rule-based scorers. Bootstrap confidence intervals are computed over prompts. Controls include random-direction, harmless-direction, and topic-matched projections to distinguish mechanism-specific effects from generic perturbation.

## Results & Highlights

- **Task-only LoRA is the strongest intervention**: mean security score 0.87 (vs. 0.46 baseline), general score 0.83, unsafe spillover only 0.13. It eliminates security refusals across all three Qwen models.
- **Single-vector refusal projection is counterproductive**: raises mean security score only from 0.46 to 0.50 while exploding unsafe compliance from 0.10 to 0.47. On individual models, it sometimes *lowers* security scores (Qwen2.5-0.5B: 0.51→0.39).
- **Rank-4 refusal-subspace projection** achieves similar security gains (0.51) to single-vector projection but matches the aligned baseline's spillover rate (0.10), showing that subspace rank matters for risk control.
- **Controls are not cosmetic**: topic-matched refusal projection raises unsafe compliance to 0.30 without targeted refusal data, demonstrating that contrast-set artifacts can be mistaken for mechanistic evidence.
- **Model dependence is central**: Qwen2.5-3B maintains zero unsafe compliance under all LoRA variants, while Qwen2.5-0.5B/1.5B show spillover increases. The frontier is model-specific.
- **Robustness sweeps** on α∈{0.0,…,2.0} and k∈{1,2,4,8} confirm that increasing projection strength or rank is not monotonically better—it samples different utility-risk points.

## Authors & Credibility

Isaac David and Arthur Gervais are both at University College London. The paper is carefully scoped to open-weight models and controlled evaluations, with extensive discussion of dual-use concerns and responsible release. The methodology demonstrates strong experimental rigor: separate training/evaluation splits, multiple control conditions, bootstrap confidence intervals, multi-seed robustness checks, and executable validators. The work is positioned at the intersection of mechanistic interpretability and cybersecurity evaluation.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** This is the first paper to systematically frame alignment removal as a utility-risk frontier problem with five independent metrics. Prior work on abliteration and refusal directions focused on whether refusal could be suppressed; this paper asks what else changes and formalizes the answer as a Pareto frontier.

**Rigor (High):** Strong experimental design with multiple control conditions (random, harmless, topic-matched projections), separate training/evaluation data, bootstrap CIs, multi-seed repeats, and evidence-gated interpretation rules (Table 4) that explicitly allow negative findings.

**Reproducibility (Good):** Full code provided, model IDs specified, hyperparameters documented (LoRA rank/α/lr/epochs), hash-verified datasets. The 5,760-row evaluation is comprehensive. Limited by deterministic decoding in main runs (supplemented by 3-seed robustness checks).

**Significance (High):** Challenges the practitioner assumption that removing refusal directions recovers capability. The finding that task-only LoRA (without explicit refusal suppression) outperforms direct refusal interventions has practical implications for security evaluation and red-teaming.

**Red flags:** The 60-prompt suite is relatively small. Only compact models (0.5B–3B) are evaluated—the authors acknowledge the frontier may not generalize to frontier-scale systems. Rule-based scorers may miss nuanced failures. The paper does not evaluate weight-space abliteration, SAE-based steering, or circuit-level interventions.

## Strengths

- Rigorous multi-metric evaluation framework that separates refusal reduction from genuine capability recovery
- Comprehensive control conditions that prevent overclaiming from contrast-set artifacts
- Responsible release design with clear boundaries between public and withheld artifacts
- The key finding—that task adaptation without explicit refusal suppression is the most effective path—has important practical implications
- Well-scoped research questions with clear operationalization

## Limitations

- Small prompt suite (60 prompts) may not capture the full diversity of security tasks
- Only compact open-weight models (0.5B–3B parameters) are evaluated; results may not transfer to frontier-scale models
- Rule-based scoring may miss subtle forms of unsafe compliance or partial capability
- The study does not cover the full space of de-alignment methods (weight orthogonalization, SAE steering, circuit-level edits, preference fine-tuning, model merging, decoding-time controls)
- LoRA training data is synthetic and may not reflect real-world security task distributions

## Relevance

本文将LLM安全对齐移除建模为可控的变换-评估协议，涉及表示工程（激活投影、拒答方向提取）和机械可解释性（残差流方向分析），与LLM隐藏状态与可解释性研究方向直接相关。论文对拒答行为在激活空间中的低维表示及其与通用能力的纠缠/分离分析，为理解对齐机制提供了实证依据。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How do the utility-risk frontiers change when scaling to 7B–70B models? Does the model-dependence finding (3B shows zero spillover while smaller models do not) suggest a scaling benefit for safety retention?
2. Can sparse autoencoder-identified refusal features achieve more targeted ablation with less spillover than the contrast-set directions used here?
3. What happens when the evaluation includes adversarially chosen out-of-scope probes rather than generic non-operational requests?
4. Does the task-only LoRA finding generalize across different PEFT methods (prefix tuning, IA3, prompt tuning) and across non-security domains (e.g., medical, legal)?
5. How stable are the extracted refusal directions across training checkpoints, and could monitoring direction drift serve as a training observability signal?
