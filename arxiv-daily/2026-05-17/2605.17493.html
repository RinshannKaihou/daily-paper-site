Key Finding

Replacing ReLU with learnable B-spline activations in sparse autoencoders boosts alive features by 72% and discovers verified climate event detectors.

Beyond Linear Superposition: Discovering Climate Features in AI Weather Models with KAN-SAE
Minjong Cheon · 2026-05-17 · mechanistic-interpretability sparse-autoencoder weather-prediction kolmogorov-arnold-networks climate-features
Key Metric
95.2% feature utilisation (975/1,024 alive features) vs. 55.5% linear baseline — a 72% gain at only 2.4-point EV cost
Why This Matters
Extends SAE interpretability from LLMs to weather transformers; causal steering methods and push-pull circuit analysis inform feature representation in complex nonlinear systems.
Critical Edge
Interpretability verified on only 2 of 975 alive features; no comparison against TopK, Gated, or JumpReLU SAEs — the advantage over modern variants is unknown.

Methodology

KAN-SAE replaces z = ReLU(…) with per-dimension cubic B-splines (order 3, K=9 control points). Each of the 1,024 latent features learns its own nonlinear gating curve — threshold, saturation, or asymmetric response — suited to atmospheric regime shifts.

Probed Model

SONNY: 20.5M-param weather transformer (ViT-S, 1.5°, 121×240 grid). Activations extracted from Layer 5 residual stream, x ∈ ℝ384.

Training

ERA5 reanalysis, 500 days (2016–2017), 3.6M tokens. Test: 51 held-out days (2018–2022). Single A40 GPU, ~4 h/model. Loss: MSE + λ‖z‖1, annealed 5×10−5 → 1×10−4.

Results & Highlights

  • Dead features drop from 44.5% → 4.8%. Features active on median 51/53 test days vs. 1/53.
  • Redundancy down 20%: median |r| 0.076 vs. 0.092; high-correlation pairs from 11.9% to 4.4%.
  • Reconstruction: 84.2% EV vs. 86.6% baseline — near-unity MSE ratio globally.
  • Shape diversity: 902 convex + 73 concave activations out of 975 alive features.
  • F590 heatwave — Convex threshold activation localises within 2° of June 2019 event centre (vs. 51° displacement for linear baseline). Causal steering at α=2 produces EU-mean +1.42 K anomaly, hotspots >+3 K; dose-response r² > 0.99.
  • F831 typhoon — Peaks on 14 Sep 2018 (Cat-5 Mangkhut). Both models localise within 3°, but KAN-SAE shows tighter concentration.
  • Push-pull circuit circuit — L0-F372 (excitatory, r=0.90) + L0-F2 (inhibitory, r=−0.897) jointly drive F590 at Layer 5.

Strengths

  • Well-motivated critique: atmospheric regime thresholds genuinely do not fit ReLU.
  • Minimal architectural change (swap ReLU → B-spline) that preserves SAE interpretability.
  • Multi-axis evaluation: utilisation, redundancy, reconstruction, spatial localisation, causal steering, cross-layer circuits.
  • Causal steering with r² > 0.99 linearity and physically consistent T2m / Z500 / MSLP responses.
  • Honest about limitations — acknowledges typhoons are detectable by linear features; analysis restricted to one layer/model.

Limitations

  • No comparison with TopK, Gated, or JumpReLU SAEs — these also address dead features via different mechanisms.
  • Interpretability shown on only 2 phenomena out of 975 alive features.
  • Single model (SONNY 20.5M) at single layer — no validation on GraphCast, Pangu-Weather, or other layers.
  • Alive-feature threshold τ = 1.4021 empirically set; no sensitivity analysis.
  • 2.4-point EV gap not evaluated for downstream forecast impact.
  • B-spline overhead (+9 params/feature) not compared to parameter-matched deeper linear baselines.

Questions for Further Exploration

How does KAN-SAE compare to TopK, Gated, and JumpReLU SAEs on the same activations? Is the B-spline advantage additive or redundant?
Do the push-pull circuits transfer across weather model architectures (GraphCast, Pangu-Weather, STORMER)?
What fraction of 975 alive features are genuinely interpretable vs. merely alive? A systematic audit is needed.
How do B-spline order p and control-point count K affect results? Is p=3, K=9 optimal?
Does the 2.4-point EV gap translate to degraded forecast skill, or is it absorbed by later layers?
Can cross-layer circuit analysis extend to a full mechanistic account of how SONNY processes atmospheric dynamics end-to-end?
