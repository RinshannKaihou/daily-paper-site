> **一句话总结：** 本文将神经正切核（NTK）理论从回归损失扩展到分类损失，证明参数空间正则化或非退化目标（如标签平滑）可使宽网络在交叉熵训练中保持惰性训练区间，并以NTK显式刻画训练解，同时将随机初始化诱导的预测器分布与贝叶斯方法建立了联系。

> **TL;DR:** Extends NTK theory from regression to classification by proving wide nets stay in the lazy training regime under cross-entropy when either parameter-space regularization is applied or targets are non-degenerate (e.g. label smoothing), yielding explicit NTK-characterized solutions and a connection to Bayesian inference via the infinite-width ensemble distribution.

## 核心贡献

- 将NTK理论从回归损失推广至分类（交叉熵）损失，填补了该领域的核心理论空白
- 证明参数空间正则化可确保宽网络在交叉熵训练中NTK保持恒定，维持惰性训练区间
- 在无正则化情况下，证明当目标非退化（所有类别概率严格为正，如标签平滑）时仍可恢复惰性训练区间
- 给出训练解基于NTK的显式刻画：通过函数空间逆映射 Φ⁻¹ 实现高效采样
- 分析随机初始化诱导的无限宽集成分布，证明其比Laplace近似贝叶斯后验更保守但比NTK-GP后验更不确定
- 建立了NTK分类解与温度缩放的MAP估计之间的等价关系

## Key Contributions

- Extends NTK theory beyond regression to classification (cross-entropy) losses, a fundamental gap identified by Yu et al. (2025) and Liu et al. (2020)
- Proves that parameter-space regularization ensures a constant NTK during cross-entropy training, preserving the lazy training regime — even a small β = 0.01 suffices empirically
- Shows the lazy regime is recovered without regularization when targets are non-degenerate (full-support distributions), covering label smoothing as a special case
- Provides an explicit characterization of the trained solution via the function-space operator Φ⁻¹, enabling efficient sampling from the infinite-width ensemble
- Proves the approximate ensemble covariance is always less uncertain than the Laplace-approximate NTK-GP posterior covariance, generalizing He et al. (2020)
- Establishes equivalence between the infinite-width neural network solution and the temperature-rescaled MAP estimate in a Gumbel-likelihood GP model (β = τ²)

## Methodology

The authors study wide feedforward neural networks with linear last layers, treating the softmax nonlinearity as part of the function-space loss rather than the architecture. This is the key insight: Liu et al. (2020) showed the NTK diverges when softmax is part of the network, but by keeping the last layer linear and incorporating softmax into the loss, the pre-activation NTK remains constant.

**Theoretical framework:**

1. **Lazy training proof (Theorem 3.1):** For any convex function-space loss C with bounded gradient and Hessian, the wide network is uniformly approximated by its parameter-linearization during training, with error O((log n)^c / √n). Two cases are analyzed:
   - **β > 0 (regularized):** Cross-entropy with arbitrary targets (including one-hot). Regularization prevents logit divergence.
   - **β = 0 (unregularized):** Requires non-degenerate targets and a reference-class softmax formulation. The centered network output (projected onto 1^⊥) is approximated, which suffices since softmax is shift-invariant.

2. **Function-space dynamics (Section 3.2):** The stationary point of the linearized network satisfies a kernel regression problem (Eq. 23), solved via the operator Φ(z) = Θ∇_z C(z) + βz. The trained predictor is g∞(x) = Φ⁻¹(βf_{θ₀}(x)), enabling sampling from the random initialization distribution.

3. **Bayesian connections (Section 4):** The infinite-width ensemble mean equals the Laplace posterior mean. The ensemble covariance is provably less uncertain than the Laplace NTK-GP posterior (Eq. 43: Σ_Lap - Σ_Ens ⪰ 0). The network solution is shown equivalent to MAP inference in a GP with Gumbel observation noise, where β = τ² controls temperature.

**Experiments:** Validated on a 1D 3-class toy dataset and MNIST binary classification (odd/even) with 4-layer MLPs. Key empirical findings: (1) the pre-softmax NTK stays constant while the post-softmax NTK diverges; (2) even small parameter regularization (β = 0.01) keeps the NTK nearly constant on MNIST; (3) label smoothing stabilizes but requires wider networks for full constancy.

## Results & Highlights

- **Lazy regime preserved for classification:** Directly contradicts prior belief that cross-entropy necessarily breaks the constant-NTK approximation
- **Regularization suffices with one-hot labels:** Parameter-space regularization provides a clean condition that works even for standard one-hot targets — β = 0.01 is enough empirically on MNIST
- **Label smoothing recovers the regime:** Without regularization, full-support targets (e.g. label smoothing with ε > 0) prevent logit divergence
- **Explicit solution via Φ⁻¹:** The trained predictor has a closed-form characterization through the function-space inverse map, enabling efficient uncertainty quantification
- **Ensemble vs. Bayesian uncertainty:** The infinite-width ensemble distribution is more conservative than the Laplace GP posterior but less conservative than the NTK-GP posterior (for MSE); for general losses, the ensemble is always less uncertain than Laplace
- **MAP equivalence:** The NTK classification solution equals a temperature-rescaled MAP in a GP-Gumbel model, giving β a principled interpretation as observation noise

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** Extending NTK from regression to classification has been an open problem since Jacot et al. (2018). The key insight — treating softmax as part of the loss rather than the architecture — is simple in retrospect but non-trivial and impactful. The connection to label smoothing as a mechanism to recover the lazy regime is novel and practical.
- **Rigor: Strong.** The main results (Theorem 3.1 and its corollaries) are proven under clearly stated assumptions with explicit convergence rates. The proof strategy uses uniform bounds on the function-space gradient/Hessian and establishes closeness to linearization via Taylor-type arguments.
- **Reproducibility:** Theoretical results are mathematical proofs. Experimental setups (1D toy data, MNIST binary classification) are simple enough to replicate. Code is not mentioned in the paper.
- **Significance: High for theory, moderate for practice.** The results significantly advance the theoretical understanding of classification dynamics in wide networks. The label smoothing connection provides a practical takeaway. However, the theory applies to infinite-width or very wide networks with standard feedforward architectures — the gap to practical ResNets/Transformers remains.
- **Red flags:** (1) The non-degenerate targets assumption excludes one-hot labels without regularization — the most common practical setting. (2) Experiments are limited to MLPs on MNIST; no validation on modern architectures or datasets. (3) The required width n grows inversely with the strong convexity constant μ_C, which may be small for near-degenerate targets. (4) The paper does not address how these results interact with normalization layers (BatchNorm, LayerNorm), skip connections, or attention mechanisms.

## Strengths

- Resolves a fundamental and well-recognized gap in NTK theory with a clean, elegant approach
- Two complementary sufficient conditions (regularization OR non-degenerate targets) cover most practical settings
- The softmax-as-loss insight sidesteps the Liu et al. (2020) impossibility result in a principled way
- Explicit connection to Bayesian methods (MAP + Laplace) bridges frequentist and Bayesian perspectives
- Label smoothing connection provides immediate practical relevance
- Well-structured proofs with clear assumptions and convergence rates

## Limitations

- One-hot labels require explicit regularization; the unregularized case needs full-support targets
- Theory limited to wide/infinit-width feedforward networks; practical finite-width gap is not quantified beyond the O((log n)^c / √n) rate
- No experiments on modern architectures (ConvNets, ResNets, Transformers) or datasets (CIFAR, ImageNet)
- The required width for near-degenerate targets (small smoothing ε) may be impractically large
- Normalization layers and skip connections are not covered by the theory
- Code is not released, limiting empirical reproducibility

## Relevance

本文对理解宽神经网络在分类任务中的训练动力学提供了重要理论突破，将NTK理论扩展到最常用的交叉熵损失场景。其关于标签平滑与惰性训练区间的关系、以及无限宽集成与贝叶斯推断的联系，对NTK理论、核方法、贝叶斯深度学习和不确定性估计的交叉研究具有直接价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the theory extend to practical architectures with BatchNorm, LayerNorm, or multi-head attention?
- Can the finite-width corrections to the NTK (e.g., from Yang & Hu 2021's μP) be combined with these classification results for more practical predictions?
- What is the quantitative relationship between label smoothing strength ε and the required network width n for the lazy regime to hold?
- Can the Φ⁻¹-based sampling procedure be scaled to realistic datasets and used as a practical uncertainty estimation method?
- How does the infinite-width ensemble uncertainty compare to deep ensembles, MC Dropout, and conformal prediction in practice?
- Does the MAP-Gumbel equivalence suggest principled ways to set β (or temperature τ) based on data?
