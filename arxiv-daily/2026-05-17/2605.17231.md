> **一句话总结：** FishBack 通过将 softmax 输出层的 Fisher 信息度量沿后续 Transformer 层的 Jacobian 拉回，推导出逐层闭式的最小失真激活导向方向 δh* = (ρ / q^T G+ q) G+ q，在 GPT-2 上以条件数超 10^7 的极端各向异性证明了欧氏假设失效，并在等概念概率下将非目标 KL 降低 1.3×–2.5×（相对欧氏梯度上升）、1.5×（相对 CAA），胜率 72%–81%。

> **TL;DR:** FishBack shows the Euclidean assumption underlying CAA/ActAdd/ITI activation steering is quantitatively wrong on GPT-2 (the pullback Fisher metric deviates from Euclidean by >97% in relative spectral norm, effective rank 2–17% of ambient d=768, condition number >10^7). It derives a closed-form Fisher-optimal steering direction via Jacobian pullback of the softmax Fisher information and, on three verb-morphology concepts (verb third, verb ing, verb past) over four layers of GPT-2 small, iterative Fisher steering reduces off-target KL by a median 1.32×–2.51× versus Euclidean gradient ascent and 1.42×–1.58× versus CAA at matched concept probability P^W (win rates 72.9%–81.4%, p<10^-4).

## 核心贡献

- 推导出 Transformer 中间层的拉回 Fisher 度量 G^(ℓ) = J^T H J：将 softmax 输出层的 Fisher 信息矩阵 H = ∇²A(λ) = Cov_P(γ) 通过后续各层的复合 Jacobian J 拉回到中间激活空间，并证明其与交叉熵损失的广义 Gauss-Newton (GGN) 矩阵等价，建立起信息几何与自然梯度优化之间的形式桥梁。
- 给出闭式最优导向方程 δh* = (ρ / q^T G+ q) G+ q（Theorem 3.2）：在满足一阶概念变化约束 q^T δh = ρ 的所有扰动中唯一最小化二阶输出 KL，其中 G+ 为伪逆，q = J^T β_W 为概念探针的拉回余向量；该方程可随余向量与 Fisher 矩阵的更新迭代应用，无需流形拟合或数据驱动几何估计。
- 建立逐层递归分解 G^(ℓ) = A_ℓ^T G^(ℓ+1) A_ℓ 及条件数深度上界 κ(G^(ℓ)) ≤ κ(G^(L)) · Π[(1+ρ_k)/(1−ρ_k)]²，首次从形式上解释了导向效果随层变化的现象，并指出 LayerNorm 引入的秩亏损导致条件数随深度增长。
- 提出统一信息几何框架：将 CAA、ActAdd、ITI、Representation Surgery、K-FAC、Park 的对偶导向全部归为"代理度量导向"的特例，用 Fisher–毕达哥拉斯超额代价分解 CG(δ̃) − CG(δ*_G) = ½(δ̃−δ*_G)^T G (δ̃−δ*_G) 及谱分解定量预测各方法的性能差距。
- 在 GPT-2 small 上验证：拉回 Fisher 度量条件数超 10^7、有效秩仅 2.4%–17.1%、欧氏偏差超 97%；迭代 Fisher 导向在三个动词形态概念、四个层上一致优于所有欧氏基线，等概念概率下非目标 KL 中位数降低 1.32×–2.51×，胜率 72.9%–81.4%。

## Key Contributions

- Derives the pullback Fisher metric G^(ℓ) = J^T H J for Transformer intermediate layers by analytically pulling back the output-layer Fisher information H = ∇²A(λ) = Cov_P(γ) through the Jacobian of all subsequent residual blocks, and proves it coincides with the generalized Gauss-Newton (GGN) matrix of cross-entropy loss with respect to intermediate activations.
- Proves a closed-form optimal steering equation (Theorem 3.2): δh* = (ρ / q^T G+ q) G+ q uniquely minimizes second-order output KL subject to the concept constraint q^T δh = ρ, applicable iteratively with refreshed covectors and Fisher matrices without manifold fitting.
- Establishes a layer-wise recursive decomposition G^(ℓ) = A_ℓ^T G^(ℓ+1) A_ℓ with a condition-number depth bound, giving the first formal explanation for the empirically observed layer sensitivity of steering (rooted in LayerNorm-induced rank deficiency).
- Unifies CAA, ActAdd, ITI, Representation Surgery, K-FAC, and Park's dual steering as proxy-metric steering under specific implicit metrics M, and quantitatively predicts each method's excess KL via a Fisher-Pythagorean decomposition and a single spectral cost ratio R_G(M;q) = (q^T M^-1 G M^-1 q)/(q^T M^-1 q)² · (q^T G^-1 q).
- Empirically confirms on GPT-2 small that the pullback Fisher metric has condition numbers >10^7, effective rank 2.4%–17.1% of ambient dimension, and >97% Euclidean deviation; iterative Fisher steering reduces off-target KL by a median 1.32×–2.51× over Euclidean gradient ascent and 1.42×–1.58× over CAA at matched concept probability, with 72.9%–81.4% win rates (p<10^-4).

## Methodology

FishBack's core construction starts from a decoder-only Transformer with L layers and hidden dimension d. The residual stream activation at layer ℓ is h^(ℓ) ∈ R^d, and the subsequent-layer map f^(ℓ): R^d → R^d maps a layer-ℓ activation to the final hidden state λ (after all remaining layers and final LayerNorm), with Jacobian J = Df^(ℓ)(h) = A_{L−1} ··· A_ℓ where A_k = I + DF_k(h^(k)).

The softmax output is treated as an exponential-family categorical distribution P_λ(y) = exp(γ_y^T λ − A(λ)) over the vocabulary Y, with unembedding vectors γ_y as rows of W_U. The log-partition A(λ) is strictly convex; its Hessian H(λ) = ∇²A(λ) = Cov_{P_λ}(γ) is the Fisher information matrix of the output distribution. The KL divergence is a Bregman divergence D_KL(P_λ0 ∥ P_λ1) = A(λ1) − A(λ0) − ∇A(λ0)^T(λ1 − λ0), whose second-order expansion is D_KL ≈ ½ Δλ^T H Δλ.

The pullback Fisher metric (Theorem 3.1) is G^(ℓ)(h0) = J^T H J, so that D_KL(P_λ0 ∥ P_{f(h0+δh)}) = ½ δh^T G δh + O(‖δh‖³). The optimal steering direction (Theorem 3.2) follows from constrained minimization of ½ δh^T G δh subject to the linearized concept constraint q^T δh = ρ (q = J^T β_W is the pullback covector of a linear concept probe β_W), yielding δh* = (ρ / q^T G+ q) G+ q.

Because G is severely ill-conditioned (κ > 10^7), direct pseudoinversion is unstable; the authors use Tikhonov regularization δ_α = (G + αI)^−1 q with adaptive α = c · λ_med(G) (median positive eigenvalue), and prove (Theorem 3.12) this choice is scale-equivariant. A Tikhonov bias bound (Theorem 3.13) shows the regularized direction stays within α/(λ_r + α) of the pseudoinverse direction, and the deliberate sacrifice is in low-eigenvalue directions that have negligible Fisher cost.

Iterative Fisher steering (Algorithm 1): at each step t, compute the concept-probability gradient covector q_t = ∇_h P^W(1)|_{h_t} (one backward pass), refresh the Fisher matrix H_t = ∇²A(λ_t) from current output probabilities (one forward pass), assemble G_t = J_0^T H_t J_0 with the Jacobian J_0 frozen at the initial point, apply α_t = c·λ_med(G_t), step along the normalized (G_t + α_t I)^−1 q_t with sign correction. The Jacobian J_0 is computed once per context (~15 s via 768 backward passes through a hook-based forward function); each subsequent step costs ~1.5 s.

Experimental setup: GPT-2 small (124M parameters, L=12 layers, d=768). Concepts = three English verb-morphology counterfactual-pair sets (verb third: run→runs; verb ing: run→running; verb past: run→ran), contexts drawn from the C4 validation set and filtered by Park et al.'s Top-3 criterion (top-3 predictions all in base or target set, cumulative probability ≥0.7). Four layers ℓ ∈ {3,6,9,11}, ~200 training / 25 test contexts per concept. The Fisher H is computed from the top-5000 tokens by probability mass. Baselines: Euclidean gradient ascent (same covector ∇_h P^W(1), G=I), CAA (mean activation difference µ+ − µ−), ActAdd (single contrastive pair), ITI (top-6 attention heads by probe accuracy). All methods share a per-context calibrated step size (binary-searched on the Euclidean gradient to reach P^W=0.9 in 30 steps) and a 30-step budget, with exact target-crossing bisection to τ ∈ {0.3,0.5,0.7,0.9} at P^W = τ ± 10^-3 for matched-probability comparison.

## Results & Highlights

- On GPT-2 small, the pullback Fisher metric G^(ℓ) has condition numbers exceeding 10^7 at all four tested layers, growing from 4.9×10^7 at layer 11 to 9.5×10^7 at layer 3 (consistent with the exponential depth bound, per-layer factor ≈1.09). (Experiment 2, Table 2)
- Effective rank (Shannon entropy of normalized eigenvalues) ranges from 2.4% to 17.1% of the ambient d=768 dimensions; Euclidean deviation (Proposition 3.6) exceeds 97% at all layers (97.8%–99.6%), meaning no scalar multiple of the identity approximates G within 3% relative error. (Table 2)
- On toy Transformer-like models (d ∈ {4,8,16}), the Fisher-optimal direction achieves median cosine similarity >0.997 with the numerically exact KL-minimizing direction, even when step size exceeds the KL approximation's critical radius r_crit by 80–1000×. The KL ratio R_KL = D_KL^euc / D_KL^Fisher at ρ=0.1 has median 1.82 (Euclidean incurs 82% more output distortion); Spearman correlation between κ(G) and R_KL is r=0.502 (p<10^-56). (Experiment 1, Figure 1)
- At P^W=0.3, median off-target KL is 2.51× lower than Euclidean gradient ascent (win rate 81.4%, n=113, p<10^-4); at P^W=0.9, the advantage is 1.32× with 72.9% win rate (n=96). Against CAA: median 1.42×–1.58× lower off-target KL across all P^W levels, win rates 75.8%–78.0% (p<10^-4). (Experiment 3, Table 3)
- Against the "Strongest per-case" baseline (oracle-selected best of CAA/ActAdd/ITI per case), FishBack achieves median 1.50× lower off-target KL at P^W=0.9 with 72.2% win rate (n=72, p=1×10^-4). (Table 3)
- Per-concept breakdown at P^W=0.9: verb third shows the strongest effect (median 1.82×, win 93.9%, p<10^-4); verb ing (median 1.24×, win 63.4%, p=0.063); verb past (median 1.16×, win 59.1%, p=0.262), with the weaker effect attributed to irregular verb forms' heterogeneous unembedding geometry. (Table 5, Appendix D.1)
- Target-crossing coverage within 30 steps (step size calibrated for Euclidean): Euclidean 100% (by construction), FishBack 48%–57%, CAA 36%–52%, ITI 23%–30%, ActAdd 8%–14% — reflecting a precision-efficiency trade-off intrinsic to geometry-aware methods. (Table 4)

## Critical Assessment

The experimental validation is confined entirely to GPT-2 small (124M parameters), and the authors themselves flag this as the primary limitation: the theoretical framework applies to any decoder-only Transformer with a softmax output, but the magnitude of the Fisher advantage and the behavior of the frozen-Jacobian approximation may differ qualitatively at larger scales (8B+, Llama-3/Gemma-2), where residual-stream geometry and spectral structure could change — yet no scaling experiment is run. The verb past concept fails to reach statistical significance (p=0.262 at P^W=0.9, win rate 59.1%), undermining the claim of "consistent" outperformance across all three concepts; the advantage is heavily concentrated in verb third (regular morphology), suggesting the geometric benefit is concept-dependent rather than universal. The step size is calibrated for the Euclidean direction and inherited by all methods, which artificially depresses FishBack's target-crossing coverage to 48%–57% (vs. Euclidean's 100%) and conditions the main KL comparison on cases where both methods reach the target — a selection that could favor the method being evaluated. The concept vocabulary is narrow (verb morphology only); the framework's claimed relevance to semantic concepts like truthfulness, sentiment, and safety is asserted but not demonstrated. The frozen-Jacobian approximation (J_0 computed once and reused across all 30 steps) introduces unquantified error as the hidden state drifts far from h_0, and no ablation compares frozen vs. refreshed Jacobians.

## Strengths

- Mathematically rigorous: full proofs of the pullback Fisher metric, closed-form steering equation, recursive decomposition, depth bounds, direction stability, and non-target KL optimality, with deferred proofs in appendices.
- The unification of CAA/ActAdd/ITI/Representation Surgery/K-FAC under a single proxy-metric template with a quantitative Fisher-Pythagorean excess-cost decomposition is a genuinely novel theoretical lens that explains when and why Euclidean methods work despite using the wrong geometry.
- The direction-stability theorem (Theorem 3.8) has a striking, practically useful implication: the optimal direction degrades only as O(ρ²) in cosine while the KL value approximation degrades as O(‖δh‖³), so the direction remains valid far beyond the quadratic regime — empirically confirmed (cos > 0.997 at 80–1000× r_crit).
- Honest reporting of negative/non-significant results (verb past p=0.262) and the precision-efficiency trade-off (Table 4), rather than only headline wins.

## Limitations

- Single-model, single-scale validation (GPT-2 small, 124M); no experiments at 1B+ scale, so the central claim that Euclidean steering is "fundamentally wrong" is only directly demonstrated on one small model.
- Narrow concept scope (three verb-morphology concepts); the framework's applicability to the practically important concepts (truthfulness, safety, sentiment) that motivate activation steering is untested.
- Shared Euclidean-calibrated step size disadvantages FishBack's coverage and complicates the fairness of the matched-probability comparison, since only cases where both methods reach the target are compared.
- Per-context computational cost is substantial: ~15 s for the initial Jacobian (768 backward passes) plus ~1.5 s/step × 30 steps, with no amortized or low-rank Jacobian approximation implemented.
- The concept-decomposability assumption (Definition 3.9) required for non-target KL optimality is restrictive; Lemma 3.10 shows it holds iff all counterfactual unembedding difference vectors d_i have equal inner product with λ, a condition not verified to hold in practice on GPT-2.

## Relevance

这篇论文直接映射到「LLM Hidden States & Interpretability」研究领域——它从信息几何角度形式化刻画了 Transformer 中间层激活空间的非欧几何结构（拉回 Fisher 度量），并用闭式最优方向改进激活导向这一可解释性核心技术；同时，其拉回度量与广义 Gauss-Newton 矩阵等价、以及对激活扰动灵敏度的精确谱诊断，也与「Computation Reliability & Precision Errors」和「ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations」相关，为理解激活空间中方向扰动的数值可靠性提供了量化工具。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the >97% Euclidean deviation and >10^7 condition number persist, strengthen, or weaken at larger scales (Llama-3 8B, Gemma-2 9B/27B) where the residual stream is wider and deeper? The authors list this as future work but provide no evidence either way.
- Can the frozen-Jacobian approximation be replaced by a low-rank or amortized Jacobian update without sacrificing directional optimality, and how does the error accumulate over 30 steps as h_t drifts from h_0?
- Does the Fisher advantage extend beyond verb morphology to semantic/safety concepts (truthfulness, refusal, sentiment), where the concept covector q may align very differently with the eigenspectrum of G?
- How does FishBack compare against the concurrent data-driven approaches (Wurgaft's manifold steering, Raval's Curveball/VAE-ensemble steering) that also target non-Euclidean geometry, on the same concepts and layers?
- The concept-decomposability assumption (Definition 3.9) is necessary for the non-target KL optimality theorem — how often does it actually hold on GPT-2 outputs, and what is the degradation when it is violated?
- Independent step-size calibration for the Fisher direction could close the coverage gap (48%–57% vs. Euclidean's 100%) — what is the optimal calibration, and does it change the win-rate picture?
