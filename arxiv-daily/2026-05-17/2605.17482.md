> **一句话总结：** 提出残差语义分解（RSD），通过对词嵌入进行递归二进制加性分解，提取局部语义轴并将残差方向作为未被主轴解释的语义信息的定性诊断工具，在手动配对上下文诊断中实现了完美的锚点分离（AUC 1.000）。

> **TL;DR:** RSD is a neural additive decomposition of word embeddings that recursively extracts semantic axes via binary splits and treats reconstruction residuals as diagnostic directions for relations left unexplained by the extracted axes, achieving perfect anchor separation (AUC 1.000) on six paired-context probes in static GloVe.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了残差语义分解（RSD）模型，将加性模糊关系分解与神经软分配机制和嵌入重建目标相结合，单个目标函数同时优化重建误差和关系结构保持。
- 设计了基于 K=2 局部拟合的递归二进制语义轴提取过程，每一步提取一个局部对比方向（semantic axis），残差方向则暴露未被该轴吸收的信息。
- 引入了配对上下文边界审计（paired-context boundary audit），通过手动指定的歧义词上下文对验证 RSD 能否分离语义锚点，并系统分析残差邻域作为定性诊断工具的有效性。

## Key Contributions

- **RSD model** that extends FADDIS-style additive fuzzy relational decomposition with neural encoder-generated soft assignments (via MLP + softplus normalization) and explicit embedding reconstruction, jointly optimizing reconstruction loss and relational structure preservation in a single objective.
- **Recursive binary semantic-axis extraction**: each local K=2 RSD fit extracts a component contrast d = c₁ - c₀ as a semantic axis; node-root residuals eᵢ = xᵢ - ŝᵢC are retained as lateral diagnostics for information unexplained by that split.
- **Paired-context boundary audit** with six manually specified ambiguous-word probes, demonstrating that RSD separates supplied context anchors at mean AUC 1.000 (vs. 0.498 shuffled), while entropy diagnostics reveal that ambiguous targets are not uniformly high-entropy boundary points in static GloVe.
- **Residual neighborhood analysis** showing that reconstruction residuals are semantically structured (e.g., "may"'s residual retrieves tend, necessarily, seem) rather than arbitrary noise, serving as qualitative diagnostic traces.

## Methodology

RSD operates on a vocabulary embedding matrix X ∈ ℝᴺˣᴰ and its cosine affinity matrix A. The core mechanism uses an encoder MLP fθ that maps each embedding to a K-dimensional soft assignment vector s via softplus normalization. The model jointly minimizes:

- **Embedding reconstruction loss**: Lₑₘb = ‖SC - X‖²F, where C ∈ ℝᴷˣᴰ is the basis matrix.
- **Relational structure loss**: Lᵣₑₗ = ‖SDᵣₑₗS⊤ - A‖²F, where Dᵣₑₗ ∈ ℝᴷˣᴷ is a learnable relational matrix.

The combined objective is L_RSD = Lₑₘb + λLᵣₑₗ. For the local binary decomposition (K=2), the component contrast d = c₁ - c₀ defines the extracted semantic axis, and the node-root residual eᵢ = xᵢ - sᵢC captures unexplained directions.

The recursive extraction procedure fits K=2 RSD at each node, labels component sides by nearest full-vocabulary words, computes normalized assignment entropy H(s)/log K as a split-confidence diagnostic, and deepens the trace only when the split is confident and the node remains underexplained. Residual directions are inspected as lateral probes rather than becoming new tree nodes.

Evaluation uses paired-context diagnostics: each ambiguous word (may, march, fall, spring, bank, seal) is paired with two manually specified sense contexts, and the method tests whether RSD forms a local boundary between context anchors. Baselines include PCA, ICA, and truncated SVD with the same K=2 bottleneck, plus shuffled-label negative controls.

## Results & Highlights

- **Main audit (6 probes)**: RSD achieves mean anchor AUC of 1.000 across all six paired-context tasks, compared to 0.498 for shuffled labels — perfect separation of manually specified context anchors.
- **Random ambiguous audit (12 targets, seed=42)**: Mean anchor AUC of 0.807 vs. 0.500 shuffled, with weak cases including draft (0.438), bark (0.438), and cell (0.562).
- **Seed stability (seeds 0–9)**: Most probes are stable; bank collapses under informativeness threshold in 40% of runs, seal reaches minimum AUC 0.714, while may, march, and spring maintain perfect or near-perfect separation.
- **Entropy is cautionary**: Only "may" has positive target entropy gap (+0.050) in the main audit; random sample mean is -0.219, indicating that ambiguous words are not uniformly high-entropy boundary points in static GloVe.
- **Residual neighborhoods are semantically meaningful**: "may" retrieves tend/necessarily/seem/sometimes/often; "bank" retrieves hsbc/dresdner/ubs/citibank; "seal" retrieves sealing/sealed/stamped — residuals preserve context-specific information after dominant axis extraction.

## Authors & Credibility

Single author: **Seungmin Jin** at HSE University, Faculty of Computer Science, Moscow. The work is positioned as a methodological contribution with controlled diagnostic evaluation rather than a large-scale benchmark claim. The author is transparent about limitations — explicitly stating that RSD residuals are "qualitative diagnostics rather than benchmark sense predictions" and reporting failure cases (bank collapses 40% of runs, weak targets like draft/bark). The paper includes seed stability analysis and shuffled-label controls, demonstrating methodological rigor for a diagnostic tool paper.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate. The core idea of combining additive fuzzy decomposition (FADDIS) with neural assignments and explicit embedding reconstruction is a genuine extension. However, the recursive binary extraction procedure is conceptually straightforward, and the diagnostic framing (rather than predictive) lowers the novelty bar for practical impact.
- **Rigor**: Good for a diagnostic methods paper. Includes shuffled-label controls, seed stability analysis, multiple baselines (PCA, ICA, SVD), and entropy diagnostics. The honest reporting of failure modes (40% collapse for bank, negative entropy gaps for most targets) is commendable. Limited to a single embedding type (GloVe-100) and vocabulary scale.
- **Reproducibility**: High. Fixed seed (42), explicit architecture details, small-scale experiments on publicly available GloVe embeddings. The narrow bottleneck (100→2→2) with occasional collapse is transparently reported.
- **Significance**: Limited. The work operates on static embeddings (GloVe) in an era dominated by contextual models, and the evaluation is confined to 6–18 hand-picked ambiguous words. The diagnostic framing makes it more of a proof-of-concept analytical tool than a broadly applicable method. The jump from 6 curated probes to real-world utility is large.
- **Red flags**: Single author; very small-scale evaluation (6 main probes, 12 random targets); exclusive reliance on static GloVe-100; the method's own entropy analysis undermines the premise that polysemous words are high-entropy boundary points; the paper title suggests a general decomposition method but the evidence supports only a narrow diagnostic claim.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-formulated model that jointly optimizes reconstruction and relational structure in a single objective.
- Honest evaluation with explicit failure reporting, negative controls, and seed stability audits.
- The observation that reconstruction residuals are semantically structured (not noise) is interesting and well-illustrated through case studies.
- Clear positioning as a diagnostic tool rather than overselling as a word-sense disambiguation method.

## Limitations

- Evaluated exclusively on static GloVe-100 embeddings; unclear how RSD transfers to contextual embeddings (BERT, GPT) or other static embeddings (Word2Vec, FastText).
- Extremely small evaluation scale: 6 curated probes + 12 random targets from a hand-declared candidate pool — insufficient for generalizability claims.
- The K=2 bottleneck is unstable: bank collapses in 40% of random seeds, suggesting sensitivity to initialization.
- Entropy diagnostics reveal that the core premise (polysemous words as high-entropy boundary points) does not hold for most targets in static GloVe, undermining the motivation.
- No comparison to established word-sense disambiguation or multi-prototype methods; baselines are limited to PCA/ICA/SVD.

## Relevance

RSD 提出的残差方向作为语义诊断工具的思路，对理解静态嵌入中多义词信息的编码方式有参考价值。虽然方法本身局限于 GloVe 且规模较小，但其加性分解框架和残差分析范式可能启发对大模型表示的可解释性研究。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can RSD's residual diagnostic framework be adapted to contextual embeddings (e.g., analyzing residual structure in BERT's intermediate layers)?
- How does the method scale beyond 100-dimensional GloVe to larger vocabularies and higher-dimensional embeddings?
- What is the theoretical relationship between RSD's entropy diagnostic and the intrinsic dimensionality of polysemous word neighborhoods?
- Could the recursive binary decomposition trace be used as a supervision signal for learning sense-aware representations?
- How do RSD residuals compare to probing classifier residuals for identifying what linguistic information is captured vs. lost at each decomposition level?
