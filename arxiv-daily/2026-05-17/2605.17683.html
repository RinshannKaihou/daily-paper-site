μ-ORCA: Microsecond-Scale DNN Inference on AMD ACAP

First framework to exploit 512-bit/cycle AIE cascade connections for direct inter-layer DNN communication — bypassing FPGA fabric entirely and achieving sub-microsecond end-to-end latency on real physics workloads.

Shixin Ji, Jinming Zhuang, Zhuoping Yang, Xingzhen Chen, Wei Zhang, Peipei Zhou Brown University & collaborators arXiv 2605.17683
Key Finding
Direct 512-bit cascade links inside the AIE array replace 32-bit DMA transfers for inter-layer data, cutting communication latency by ~16× and enabling 0.93 μs end-to-end inference.
Key Metric
0.93μs
6-layer DeepSets, end-to-end — under the LHC 1 μs trigger budget
Why This Matters
Meeting 1 μs budgets unlocks ML-based jet tagging in real-time LHC trigger systems — previously impossible with FPGA-only or GPU approaches.
Critical Edge
Cascade constraints (A=A', C=C'=1) limit parallelism flexibility; only INT8 dense MLP/DeepSets; locked to AMD ACAP VEK280.

Methodology

  1. Cascade-enabled inter-layer communication — Consecutive DNN layers are placed as physical neighbours on the 8×38 AIE-ML array and connected via 512-bit/cycle cascade FIFOs, eliminating DMA round-trips through FPGA fabric. Fallback: direct AIE-to-AIE DMA (not through PL).
  2. Overhead-aware performance model — Analytical model captures VLIW prologue, sync barriers, and Manhattan-distance routing costs that conventional roofline models ignore. Single-AIE estimation error: 1.1 %; end-to-end: 15.6 %.
  3. Joint mapping & placement DSE — Co-optimises parallelism parameters (A, B, C per layer) and physical tile placement. Sometimes reducing per-layer parallelism unlocks a cascade path that saves more cycles than the compute penalty.
  4. Non-MM kernels on AIE — Global aggregation via a VMAC × all-ones-row trick computes sum in one instruction (≥2.8× faster than extract+add). Bias and ReLU fused into MM epilogue.

Headline Numbers

  • 1.83× faster than HLS4ML (avg, 7 real models)
  • 3.75× faster than SSR
  • 18.33× faster than AIE4ML
  • 5.75× global aggregation speedup (64×64, 8 AIEs)
  • 6/7 real workloads meet the 1 μs budget
  • Supports >12 layers of 32³ MM or >4 layers of 64³ MM within 1 μs

Performance Model Accuracy

  • Single-AIE error: 1.1 % (vs. GAMA 25.5 %, SSR 72.3 %)
  • End-to-end error: 15.6 %

Strengths

  • First to exploit AIE cascade for inter-layer DNN communication — 16× bandwidth gain
  • Comprehensive evaluation: 4 baselines, ablations, synthetic & real workloads
  • Overhead-aware model is practical — ignoring prologue/sync is unrealistic at μs scale
  • Global aggregation eliminates a key DeepSets bottleneck
  • Open-source with fully automated DSE

Limitations

  • Only MLP and DeepSets — no CNNs, Transformers, or attention
  • Cascade topology (east/south only) limits very deep/wide models
  • Platform-locked to AMD ACAP AIE-ML; not portable
  • Dense INT8 only; no pruning or mixed-precision support
  • C=1 cascade constraint forces suboptimal parallelism for some shapes
  • First/last layer DMA latency remains unaddressed (~25% for 64³ L8)

Open Questions

Can cascade patterns extend to attention layers or lightweight Transformer blocks for jet tagging?
What is the energy efficiency (pJ/op) vs. FPGA-only and GPU approaches?
How would hardware-software co-design (mixed-precision, structured pruning) interact with the cascade architecture?
Can the overhead-aware model generalise to next-gen AIE with different interconnect topologies?
What is the maximum model complexity that fits within 1 μs on a single VEK280?
