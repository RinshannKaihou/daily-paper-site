> **一句话总结：** 本文从概率分布的视角重新定义视觉机械可解释性，将特征解释建模为KL最小化优化问题，揭示现有方法（Top-K检索、正则化优化、提示引导扩散）的统计偏差，并提出基于SAE特征能量的扩散后验采样方法EnergyDPS，在DINOv3上实现了忠实性与可解释性的最优平衡。

> **TL;DR:** This paper reframes visual mechanistic interpretability (MI) as a distributional KL-minimization problem. It identifies three axes of failure in prior work—boundary bias/threshold instability in hard-constraint retrieval, typical-set mismatch in optimization-based visualization, and prompt information bottleneck in text-guided diffusion—and proposes EnergyDPS, which uses SAE feature activations as energy functions to guide unconditional diffusion posterior sampling. Evaluated on DINOv3 transcoder features with blinded human/AI studies, EnergyDPS achieves the highest interpretability scores (2.92 vs. 1.92–1.95 for baselines) and best QualiCLIP/NRQM metrics, at moderate cost (96 s/sample on H100).

## 核心贡献

- 建立视觉MI的统一分布视角框架，将特征解释形式化为在忠实性约束下最小化KL(q‖p)的优化问题，并沿"研究对象—约束形式—采样方法"三轴对现有范式进行分类。
- 从理论上证明硬约束方法（Top-K检索）存在边界偏差、阈值不稳定性和可解释性差距；正则化优化存在典型集失配和正则化伪影；提示引导扩散存在信息瓶颈。
- 提出KL最小软约束（KLSC）原则：通过指数倾斜分布 q_β(x) ∝ p(x)exp(βs(x)) 平滑地平衡忠实性与可解释性，避免硬截断的突变行为。
- 设计EnergyDPS：将SAE特征激活作为能量函数注入扩散模型反向采样过程，无需文本提示代理，训练无关且忠实于模型内部机制。
- 在DINOv3 ResNeXt Large的transcoder特征上，通过盲审实验（4名人类+2名AI评估者）验证了EnergyDPS的优越性。

## Key Contributions

- Establishes a **unified distributional taxonomy** for visual MI, decomposing all existing paradigms along three orthogonal axes: object of study (O), constraint formulation (C), and sampling methodology (S).
- **Theoretically identifies statistical biases** in prior paradigms: (1) hard-constraint retrieval suffers boundary bias, threshold instability, and an interpretability gap (Theorem D.14); (2) optimization with regularization collapses to MAP-like mode-seeking with typical-set mismatch; (3) prompt-guided diffusion incurs a provable information bottleneck (Theorem F.7) that restricts explanations to verbalizable concepts.
- Proposes the **KLSC (KL-minimal soft-constraint) principle**: instead of hard thresholding, uses exponential tilting q_β(x) ∝ p(x)exp(βs(x)) to smoothly reweight the natural image distribution, provably achieving lower KL deviation under matched faithfulness.
- Introduces **EnergyDPS**: a training-free, energy-guided diffusion posterior sampler that injects ∇s(x̂₀) at each reverse step of an unconditional DDPM, directly targeting the KLSC induced distribution without text prompts.
- Validates on **DINOv3** with a transcoder (CNN-architecture SAE variant), demonstrating superior interpretability in blinded studies with both human and AI (GPT-5.4, Gemini-3-flash-lite) raters.

## Methodology

The paper proceeds in three layers:

**1. Distributional framework (Section 2.1–2.2):** Feature interpretation is reformulated as finding an induced distribution q that minimizes KL(q‖p) subject to a faithfulness constraint E_q[s(X)] ≥ m, where p is the natural image prior and s is an intrinsic score (e.g., SAE feature activation). The framework introduces a three-axis taxonomy (O, C, S):

- **Object of study:** Using SAE features (sparse, monosemantic) vs. raw neurons (polysemantic) vs. task-injected objectives (which distort intrinsic semantics, formalized via task-injection distortion δ(m,r;c)).
- **Constraint formulation:** Hard truncation (sharp cutoff at threshold m) vs. KLSC soft constraint (exponential tilting). Three biases are formally derived for hard constraints: boundary mass concentration (Lemma D.6), threshold instability (KL can become +∞ under arbitrarily small perturbations, Lemma D.8), and interpretability gap (KLSC is at least as interpretable under matched faithfulness, Theorem D.14).
- **Sampling methodology:** Retrieval (winner-takes-most on finite pools, Lemma F.1), optimization with regularization (MAP mode-seeking → typical-set mismatch), and prompt-guided diffusion (confined to Q_prompt ⊂ P(X), creating a representational bottleneck, Theorem F.7).

**2. EnergyDPS (Section 2.3):** Realizes KLSC via diffusion posterior sampling. Uses an unconditional DDPM as the natural image prior p_θ. At each reverse step i, computes the predicted clean image x̂₀ and injects energy guidance ζᵢβ∇_{xᵢ}s(x̂₀), where s is the SAE feature activation treated as an energy function. This is training-free (no fine-tuning of the diffusion model) and avoids text prompts entirely. Algorithm 1 provides the full procedure. Appendix G proves EnergyDPS faithfully targets the KLSC distribution under mild conditions.

**3. Experimental setup (Section 3):** Trained a CNN transcoder on DINOv3 ResNeXt Large stage-2, layer-20 using ImageNet-1K with Top-K sparsification. Compared EnergyDPS against: (i) dataset search (top-4 ImageNet samples), (ii) MACO (activation maximization with magnitude constraints), and (iii) DiffExplainer (prompt-guided diffusion). Fairness ensured by calibrating all methods to comparable activation levels.

## Results & Highlights

- **Toy GMM validation (Table 2):** Under matched faithfulness E[s] ∈ {5, 6, 7}, KLSC consistently achieves lower boundary mass, lower TV(q, p), and lower TV sensitivity TV(q_m, q_{m+ε}) than hard truncation. For E[s] = 7: KLSC TV = 0.5095 vs. hard TV = 0.6198; instability 0.0187 vs. 0.0346.
- **Qualitative (Fig. 4):** For DINOv3 features 81 and 9863, EnergyDPS produces coherent visualizations (heart-shaped and wing-like patterns), while MACO shows high-frequency artifacts and DiffExplainer produces less interpretable outputs.
- **No-reference metrics (Table 3, 100 random features):** EnergyDPS achieves best QualiCLIP (0.5791 vs. 0.4228 MACO, 0.3259 DiffExplainer) and competitive/best NRQM (6.7821 vs. 4.4869, 6.7460).
- **Blinded interpretability study (Table 4):** EnergyDPS: 2.92 (human), 2.90 (GPT-5.4), 3.09 (Gemini-3-flash-lite) vs. MACO: 1.92, 2.09, 2.57 and DiffExplainer: 1.95, 2.14, 1.90. Lowest weighted description variance (0.2505 vs. 0.2917, 0.2643).
- **Efficiency (Table 11):** EnergyDPS: 96 s/sample, 14.6 GB GPU on H100. Faster and less memory than DiffExplainer (217 s, 26.5 GB), modest overhead vs. MACO (48 s, 7.8 GB). Dataset search is 4252 s/sample.
- **Cross-layer generalization (Table 8):** EnergyDPS remains competitive across different DINOv3 layers.
- **Diffusion prior ablation (Table 9):** Using a narrower prior (FFHQ faces-only) degrades explanation quality, confirming the importance of a broad natural image prior.
- **Object drift (Fig. 2, Appendix L.5):** Adding CLIP task alignment to EnergyDPS causes dramatic semantic shifts even when intrinsic activation s(x) is held constant, validating the task-injection distortion theory.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The distributional reframing is genuinely original. The three-axis taxonomy (O, C, S) provides a clean theoretical lens that unifies and explains failure modes of prior work. The formal proofs of bias (boundary bias, threshold instability, interpretability gap) are non-trivial and well-constructed.
- **Rigor: Strong.** The theoretical analysis is thorough with formal lemmas and theorems (Appendices D–G). The toy GMM experiments validate theoretical predictions. The main experiments use matched-faithfulness comparisons (all methods calibrated to same activation level), which is a fairer evaluation protocol than unconstrained comparisons.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate to good.** Algorithm 1 is self-contained. The paper specifies DINOv3 ResNeXt Large, layer-20, ImageNet-1K training, and Top-K transcoder. However, exact hyperparameters (guidance strength β, step sizes ζᵢ) require consulting appendices. The unconditional DDPM prior is standard. The blinded study protocol is described in Appendix K.
- **Significance: High if the framework gains adoption.** The theoretical framework could reshape how the community thinks about visual MI, analogous to how the linear representation hypothesis restructured thinking about LLM features. The practical method (EnergyDPS) demonstrably improves over baselines.
- **Red flags and honest concerns:**
  1. **Single model evaluation.** Only DINOv3 is tested. No results on CNNs (ResNet, ConvNeXT), other ViTs (CLIP, SigLIP), or multi-modal models. Generalization is claimed but not demonstrated.
  2. **Evaluation relies on proxy metrics and subjective studies.** QualiCLIP and NRQM are no-reference proxies—not gold standards. The blinded study has only 4 human raters (plus 2 AI raters), which is a small sample. The interpretability score difference (2.92 vs 1.92–1.95 on a 1–4 scale) is meaningful but the study is underpowered for strong statistical claims.
  3. **Baselines are somewhat limited.** No comparison with more recent feature visualization methods beyond MACO, and no comparison with SAE interpretability approaches that use captioning or multimodal labeling (e.g., automated concept discovery).
  4. **Computational cost.** 96 s/sample on an H100 is not negligible. Scaling to thousands of SAE features (common in practice) would be expensive. The paper acknowledges this but offers only "distillation" as future work.
  5. **The natural image prior as interpretability proxy is debatable.** The paper itself notes (Section 4.1) that realism ≠ interpretability, but the method fundamentally optimizes for closeness to natural images. Features that are genuinely important but correspond to unnatural patterns may be undersampled.

## Strengths

- Principled theoretical framework with rigorous proofs replacing the field's reliance on heuristics.
- Clean three-axis taxonomy that systematically explains *why* prior methods fail, not just *that* they fail.
- EnergyDPS is training-free and avoids the prompt bottleneck—directly uses feature activations as energy.
- Fair evaluation protocol (matched-faithfulness calibration) that should become standard practice.
- Comprehensive toy experiments that validate theoretical predictions before real-model experiments.

## Limitations

- Evaluated on a single vision model architecture (DINOv3 ResNeXt Large).
- Moderate computational cost (96 s/sample); scaling to large SAE dictionaries is expensive.
- The blinded study is small (4 human raters); statistical power is limited.
- The natural image prior may not capture all model-relevant features, particularly those corresponding to abstract or non-visual concepts.
- The transcoder (SAE variant) adds another layer of complexity; the quality of EnergyDPS visualizations depends on the quality of the learned SAE features.

## Relevance

该论文从理论层面统一了视觉机械可解释性的方法论，提出的KLSC原则和EnergyDPS方法为如何忠实且可解释地理解视觉模型内部特征提供了新范式。对关注模型可解释性、稀疏自编码器和视觉模型内部机制的研究者具有高度参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does EnergyDPS scale to models with millions of SAE features (e.g., large ViTs)? Is feature-level amortization possible?
- Can the distributional framework be extended to mechanistic interpretability of multimodal models (e.g., CLIP, LLaVA) where features span vision and language?
- What happens when the diffusion prior p_θ poorly approximates the true data distribution p? How sensitive are results to prior quality?
- Could EnergyDPS be combined with causal circuit analysis to identify not just what features encode, but how they compose in model computations?
- Is the KLSC principle applicable to language model interpretability, where the "natural distribution" is over discrete tokens rather than continuous images?
