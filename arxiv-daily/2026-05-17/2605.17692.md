> **一句话总结：** 本文证明了深层线性神经网络在平方损失下的训练问题可以通过完全正锥提升技术获得等价的精确凸优化重构，提升维度仅取决于输入输出维度，与网络深度和数据量无关。

> **TL;DR:** The training of deep linear neural networks under squared loss admits an exact convex reformulation over a generalized completely positive cone via a three-step lifting procedure; the lifted dimension depends only on input/output dimensions, not on network depth or sample size.

## 核心贡献

- 证明了深层线性网络在平方损失下的非凸训练问题存在精确的凸重构，两者具有相同的最优值。
- 提出三步构造法：多层参数化→秩约束半正定规划→互补条件→完全正提升。
- 提升后的维度仅由输入维度 $d_0$ 和输出维度 $d_N$ 决定，与网络深度 $N$ 和样本数 $n$ 无关。
- 瓶颈宽度 $\min_k d_k$ 仅通过标量约束进入重构，不增加提升空间维度。
- 建立了线性神经网络训练与余正规划（copositive programming）之间的理论联系。

## Key Contributions

- Proves that the nonconvex squared-loss training problem for deep linear neural networks admits an exact convex reformulation with the same global optimal value.
- Constructs the reformulation via a systematic three-step pipeline: rank-constrained SDP → complementarity condition → completely positive lifting.
- Shows the lifted ambient dimension is $d = d_0 + d_N$, independent of network depth and number of data points.
- The bottleneck width $r = \min_k d_k$ enters only through scalar constraints (trace conditions), keeping the lifted representation compact.
- Establishes a novel connection between linear neural network training and copositive/completely positive programming.

## Methodology

The paper develops an exact convex reformulation through three sequential reductions:

1. **Shallow factorization reduction:** The deep linear network $\hat{Y} = W_N \cdots W_1 X$ is reduced to an equivalent bilinear factorization $\min_{U,V} \|UV^\top X - Y\|_F^2$ with rank constraint $r = \min_k d_k$, leveraging the fact that compositions of linear maps remain linear.

2. **Rank-constrained SDP + complementarity:** The bilinear factorization is lifted to a rank-constrained semidefinite program via $W = [U; V][U; V]^\top \succeq 0$ with $\text{rank}(W) \leq r$. The rank constraint is then reformulated using a complementarity condition: there exists $W' \succeq 0$ with $0 \preceq W' \preceq I$, $\text{tr}(W') = r$, and $\langle W, I - W'\rangle \leq 0$.

3. **Completely positive lifting:** The resulting quadratically constrained quadratic program (with the complementarity inequality) is lifted to a completely positive program over the cone $\mathcal{CP}_{\bar{K}}$ where $\bar{K} = \mathbb{R}_+ \times \mathcal{S}^d_+ \times \mathcal{S}^d_+ \times \mathcal{S}^d_+$. This uses a specialization of Bai et al. (2016, Theorem 4). All nonconvexity is encoded purely in the cone constraint; the objective and remaining constraints are linear in the lifted variables.

The paper provides both a vectorized formulation (Theorem 1) and a more interpretable Kronecker-product formulation (Theorem 2), with a complete term-by-term equivalence proof on rank-one atoms extended by convexity.

## Results & Highlights

- **Exact equivalence:** The convex reformulation provably has the same optimal value as the original nonconvex training problem.
- **Dimension independence:** Lifted dimension is $d = d_0 + d_N$, not depending on depth $N$ or sample count $n$.
- **Structural insight:** All nonconvexity from linear factorization is captured by a single generalized completely positive cone membership constraint.
- **Theoretical contribution:** Provides the first exact conic characterization of the nonconvexity induced by deep linear factorization, connecting it to the well-studied copositive programming framework.
- **Not algorithmic:** The authors explicitly state the reformulation is not intended as a practical solver — membership in generalized completely positive cones is NP-hard in general.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. While convex reformulations of two-layer ReLU networks exist (Pilanci, Ergen, Sahiner et al.), this is the first to provide an *exact* convex reformulation for *deep* linear networks via completely positive lifting. The connection to copositive programming is new for neural network training.
- **Rigor:** Excellent. Complete proofs for all claims, with careful verification of the non-strict feasibility condition required by the CP lifting lemma. Both vectorized and Kronecker formulations are proven equivalent.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. The construction is fully specified algebraically. However, no code or numerical experiments are provided.
- **Significance:** Primarily theoretical. The structural insight — that linear network nonconvexity is exactly capturable by a conic constraint — is intellectually important but does not immediately yield practical algorithms. The authors are transparent about this limitation.
- **Red flags:** None. The paper is honest about computational intractability and positions the contribution as structural/theoretical. One could argue the lack of any numerical illustration (even on toy problems) makes the work feel incomplete, but this is consistent with its theoretical focus.

## Strengths

- Clean, systematic construction with a natural three-step pipeline.
- Dimension of the lifted space is remarkably compact (independent of depth and sample size).
- Provides a precise geometric characterization of what makes linear network training nonconvex.
- Connects two previously separate literatures: neural network optimization theory and copositive programming.
- Well-written with clear notation and thorough proofs.

## Limitations

- The completely positive cone is computationally intractable in general, so the reformulation cannot be directly used as a practical training algorithm.
- No numerical experiments or illustrations, even on small synthetic problems.
- Limited to linear networks with squared loss — the extension to nonlinear activations (ReLU, etc.) under the same framework is not discussed.
- The paper does not explore outer approximations of the CP cone (e.g., via sum-of-squares hierarchies) that could lead to tractable relaxations.
- The practical implications for understanding optimization landscapes of real neural networks remain unclear.

## Relevance

本文将线性神经网络的非凸训练问题精确转化为凸优化问题，为理解深度网络优化景观的几何结构提供了新的理论视角。其完全正锥提升技术与半正定规划和余正规划密切相关，对优化理论和深度学习交叉研究有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can tractable outer approximations of the generalized completely positive cone (e.g., via sum-of-squares hierarchies or semidefinite relaxations) preserve useful information about the original training problem's global optima?
- Does the same lifting strategy extend to networks with ReLU or polynomial activations, or does the nonlinearity fundamentally break the construction?
- How do standard gradient-based optimization trajectories on the original nonconvex problem relate to the geometry of the lifted convex feasible set?
- Could the complementarity condition perspective yield new implicit regularization analyses for gradient descent on linear networks?
- What is the tightest tractable relaxation of the CP cone for the specific structure arising from neural network training, and how does its solution compare to gradient descent solutions in practice?
