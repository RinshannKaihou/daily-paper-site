Self-Supervised On-Policy
Distillation for Reasoning LMs

Zhiquan Tan (Tsinghua) · Yinrong Hong (Beihang) · arxiv.org/abs/2605.17497 ↗

TL;DR — SSOPD converts correct-wrong contrast inside a GRPO group into dense process supervision by distilling a teacher (conditioned on the shortest correct completion) into prefixes of the longest wrong completion, improving over GRPO on all 9 model-benchmark settings without external solution traces.

On-policy groups contain enough signal for process-level supervision — no curated reference solutions needed. Self-distillation from intra-group contrast beats GRPO everywhere and matches solution-conditioned baselines at 8B scale.

Why This Matters
Eliminates the dependency on curated reference solutions. The same GRPO group that produces a terminal reward also yields dense process signal — at zero extra annotation cost.
Critical Edge
Gains at smaller scales are modest (+0.3–0.6), no error bars reported. Only evaluated on competition math; generalization to code, science, or partial-credit verification is untested.

Methodology

SSOPD adds a self-supervised distillation term to GRPO. For each prompt, a group of G on-policy completions is sampled. The method has three stages:

STEP 01
Witness-Prefix Selection
Pick y⁺ = shortest correct completion and y⁻ = longest wrong completion from the group. Motivated by a random-deadline stopping-time model.
STEP 02
Self-Distillation
At each prefix of y⁻, a stop-gradient teacher is conditioned on y⁺ as privileged context. Student matches teacher via pointwise-clipped forward KL.
STEP 03
Frontier Weighting
Weight the auxiliary loss by a prompt-level coefficient that peaks for balanced groups (correct ≈ wrong) and vanishes at extremes.
λx = λ0 · 4 p̂x (1 − p̂x)  —  L(x) = LGRPO(x) + λx · LSSOPD(x)

Significance

Code promised but not yet available. Training uses standard components (Qwen3, LoRA, OpenThoughts). Hyperparameters: λ0=0.5, τclip=0.05.

Strengths

  • Elegant stopping-time theory connecting to practical pairing heuristics.
  • Strictly information-controlled comparisons — SSOPD uses the exact same data as GRPO.
  • Consistent improvements across all evaluated settings with zero degradation.
  • Frontier weight has a natural interpretation as branching variance, not a heuristic schedule.
  • Simple drop-in addition to GRPO — minimal implementation overhead.

Limitations

  • Modest gains at smaller scales where self-generated witnesses are less reliable.
  • No error bars or statistical significance tests reported.
  • Only evaluated on math with binary verification; partial credit and non-math domains untested.
  • Requires mixed groups — prompts where the model is uniformly strong or weak get no auxiliary signal.
  • First K positions of the longest wrong completion may miss late-occurring error points.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Would increasing group size G for frontier prompts yield even larger gains with curriculum learning?
  2. Can witness selection be improved via embedding similarity instead of length-based heuristics?
  3. Does the frontier weight λx correlate with training stability metrics (loss spikes, gradient norm)?
  4. How does SSOPD perform on code generation with unit tests or scientific formula verification?
  5. Is there a smooth interpolation between SSOPD and OPSD when mixing self-generated with occasional external traces?
