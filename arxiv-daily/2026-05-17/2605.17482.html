Residual Semantic Decomposition of Word Embeddings

arXiv 2605.17482
Seungmin Jin · HSE University · 2026-05-17
Recursive binary decomposition of word embeddings extracts locally interpretable semantic axes — and the reconstruction residuals are not noise. On six paired-context probes, RSD separates every manually specified sense anchor at AUC 1.000.
Key Finding
RSD residuals carry structured semantics (e.g. "may" → tend, necessarily, seem) — they are qualitative diagnostic traces, not random error.

Why This Matters
Opens a diagnostic pathway: after dominant axes are removed, what remains is still meaningful — a new lens on how polysemy is encoded.
Critical Edge
Only GloVe-100, 6 curated probes. Entropy analysis undermines the core premise: most polysemous words are not high-entropy boundary points.

Methodology

RSD takes a vocabulary embedding matrix X ∈ 𝒩N×D and its cosine affinity matrix A. An encoder MLP maps each embedding to a K-dim soft assignment vector s (softplus normalization). The model jointly minimizes:

L_RSD = L_emb + λ L_rel L_emb = ||SC - X||_F² (reconstruction) L_rel = ||S D_rel S¹ - A||_F² (relational structure)

For K=2 binary splits, the component contrast d = c₁ − c₀ defines the extracted semantic axis. The node-root residual eᵢ = xᵢ − sᵢC captures unexplained directions and is inspected as a lateral probe.

Key Contributions

  • Extends FADDIS-style additive fuzzy decomposition with neural soft assignments (MLP + softplus) and explicit embedding reconstruction.
  • Recursive binary semantic-axis extraction: each K=2 fit produces a contrast direction; residuals are retained as lateral diagnostics.
  • Paired-context boundary audit demonstrates perfect separation across all six probes with shuffled-label negative controls.
  • Residual neighborhoods are semantically structured (e.g., "bank" → hsbc, dresdner, ubs, citibank).

相关性 / Relevance

RSD 提出的残差方向作为语义诊断工具的思路，对理解静态嵌入中多义词信息的编码方式有参考价值。虽然方法本身局限于 GloVe 且规模较小，但其加性分解框架和残差分析范式可能启发对大模型表示的可解释性研究。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can RSD's residual diagnostic framework be adapted to contextual embeddings (e.g., analyzing residual structure in BERT's intermediate layers)?
  2. How does the method scale beyond 100-dimensional GloVe to larger vocabularies and higher-dimensional embeddings?
  3. What is the theoretical relationship between RSD's entropy diagnostic and the intrinsic dimensionality of polysemous word neighborhoods?
  4. Could the recursive binary decomposition trace be used as a supervision signal for learning sense-aware representations?
  5. How do RSD residuals compare to probing classifier residuals for identifying what linguistic information is captured vs. lost?
