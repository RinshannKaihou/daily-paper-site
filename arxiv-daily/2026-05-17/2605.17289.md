> **一句话总结：** LEAP提出了一种基于逐权重Bernoulli-Gumbel-sigmoid松弛的端到端非结构化剪枝方法，通过在冻结预训练权重上学习稀疏掩码，在五个LLM家族（0.5B–8B）的50%和60%稀疏度下，六任务平均零样本准确率比最佳逐层基线ADMM平均提升+2.59个百分点。

> **TL;DR:** LEAP replaces the intractable categorical-over-patterns mask parameterization (used by MaskLLM/PATCH) with a per-weight Bernoulli-via-Gumbel-sigmoid relaxation, making end-to-end unstructured mask learning tractable for LLMs. It improves 6-task average zero-shot accuracy by +2.59 points over ADMM across five model families at 50–60% sparsity.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示了组合障碍：** 证明了MaskLLM/PATCH的"模式类别分布"参数化方式无法扩展到非结构化剪枝——对于宽度为4096的行，50%稀疏度的有效掩码数量约为 $10^{1229}$，无法存储或索引。
- **提出逐权重Bernoulli松弛：** 用独立Bernoulli分布（经Gumbel-sigmoid松弛）替代模式类别分布，参数量与权重矩阵相同（O(mn)），与稀疏度无关。
- **端到端非结构化掩码学习：** 在冻结的预训练权重上，仅训练掩码逻辑矩阵P，直接优化语言建模损失，保持模型校准性。
- **广泛实验验证：** 在Qwen-2.5 0.5B、Gemma-3 1B、LLaMA-3.2 1B/3B、LLaMA-3.1 8B上，50%和60%稀疏度下全面超越Wanda、SparseGPT、Thanos和ADMM。

## Key Contributions

- Identified a combinatorial obstruction preventing MaskLLM/PATCH-style categorical-over-patterns parameterizations from scaling to unstructured sparsity (number of valid masks per row is $\binom{4096}{2048} \approx 10^{1229}$).
- Proposed LEAP: a per-weight Bernoulli-via-Gumbel-sigmoid relaxation that makes end-to-end unstructured mask learning tractable at LLM scale, with O(mn) parameters per weight matrix independent of target sparsity.
- Designed a stable training recipe: Wanda-based initialization, scale/temperature annealing schedules, global sparsity regularizer, and magnitude-aware weight regularizer.
- Demonstrated consistent improvements across five LLM families (0.5B–8B) at 50% and 60% sparsity: +2.59 points average improvement over ADMM, up to +5.40 points on LLaMA-3.2 1B at 60%.

## Methodology

LEAP formulates unstructured pruning as a differentiable mask learning problem over frozen pretrained weights:

1. **Mask parameterization:** For each weight matrix $W \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$, a parallel logit matrix $P \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n}$ is introduced. The stochastic mask is $M = \sigma((\alpha P + g)/\tau)$, where $g$ is Gumbel noise, $\sigma$ is sigmoid, $\alpha$ is a scale factor, and $\tau$ is temperature. This is the Gumbel-sigmoid relaxation of a Bernoulli with logit $\alpha P_{ij}$.

2. **Initialization:** Masks are initialized from a one-shot Wanda pruning result—entries selected by Wanda get logit $+s$, the rest get $-s$, giving the optimization a warm start.

3. **Annealing schedules:** The scale $\alpha$ is ramped (e.g., 25 → 350) to push sigmoid outputs toward {0,1}. The temperature $\tau$ is decayed (e.g., 4.0 → 0.05) to sharpen the distribution. Early iterations explore; later ones commit.

4. **Global sparsity enforcement:** $L_{\text{sparsity}} = \lambda_1 \cdot \frac{1}{N} \sum_i \|M_i\|_1 - \rho$ enforces a global density budget, allowing per-layer density to adapt based on end-to-end importance.

5. **Magnitude-aware regularization:** $L_{\text{weight}} = -\lambda_2 \sum_i \|M_i \odot W_i\|_1$ biases toward retaining higher-magnitude weights, preventing degenerate minima.

6. **Full objective:** $L = L_{\text{LM}}(\tilde{W}; X) + L_{\text{sparsity}} + L_{\text{weight}}$, where only $P$ is trained and $W$ is frozen.

7. **Training setup:** 2,000 steps, batch size 256, sequence length 4096, SlimPajama calibration data. Hyperparameters tuned on Qwen-2.5 0.5B and reused for all larger models.

## Results & Highlights

- **Average improvement over ADMM:** +2.59 points on 6-task zero-shot average accuracy across 10 (model, sparsity) settings.
- **Best single result:** +5.40 points on LLaMA-3.2 1B at 60% sparsity (50.39 vs. 44.99).
- **Perplexity gains:** On LLaMA-3.1 8B at 60% sparsity, LEAP achieves 8.82 PPL vs. ADMM's 14.10 (a 37.5% reduction).
- **Gains widen at higher sparsity:** ADMM is nearly competitive at 50% on LLaMA-3.1 8B (+0.21 points) but the gap reopens to +3.86 points at 60%.
- **Cheaper than MaskLLM:** 276 GPU-hours (H100) for LLaMA-3.1 8B vs. MaskLLM's reported 1,280 A100 GPU-hours for 7B models.
- **Ablation findings:** The weight regularizer ($\lambda_2$) is the most load-bearing component; removing it drops accuracy by 2.06 points and increases PPL by 1.67. Scale/temperature schedules primarily affect mask sharpness, not final accuracy.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** The key insight—replacing categorical-over-patterns with per-weight Bernoulli relaxation—is elegant and principled. The combinatorial obstruction argument is rigorous and clearly articulated. This is the first practical end-to-end unstructured mask learning method for LLMs.
- **Rigor (High):** Comprehensive experiments across 5 model families, 2 sparsity levels, 6 zero-shot tasks, plus WikiText2 perplexity. Ablations isolate each component's contribution. Per-task appendix tables are provided.
- **Reproducibility (Medium-High):** Full hyperparameters listed (Table 4), tuned only on the smallest model and reused. Calibration corpus (SlimPajama) is public. Code is not mentioned as released, but the method is described in sufficient detail for reimplementation.
- **Significance (High):** Unstructured sparsity is increasingly hardware-supported; a method that improves accuracy at 50–60% sparsity while producing kernel-compatible masks has clear deployment value.
- **Red flags:**
  - Results only cover models up to 8B; scalability to 70B+ remains untested.
  - Mask learning takes 22.7–276 GPU-hours (H100), significantly more than one-shot methods (minutes).
  - Frozen weights are a deliberate scope choice, but joint weight-mask optimization could yield further gains.
  - Near-uniform per-block sparsity allocation is observed but not fully explained—it may limit optimality.
  - No evaluation on downstream generation quality beyond zero-shot benchmarks.

## Strengths

- Principled resolution of a fundamental scalability limitation in prior learnable-mask methods.
- Consistent improvements across all tested settings; especially large gains at 60% sparsity where baselines degrade sharply.
- Lightweight training recipe: only 2,000 steps on a small calibration stream, no weight updates needed.
- Frozen-weight design preserves pretrained calibration and simplifies deployment.
- Masks are directly compatible with existing unstructured sparsity kernels (Flash-LLM, SpInfer, MACKO).

## Limitations

- Compute cost: 276 GPU-hours for 8B model vs. minutes for Wanda/SparseGPT, though authors argue it is a one-time offline preprocessing step.
- Only evaluated up to 8B parameters; behavior at larger scales (70B, 405B) is unknown.
- Memory overhead: logit matrix P doubles the trainable state during mask learning, though activations dominate at the sequence lengths used.
- Per-block sparsity allocation is near-uniform; the potential benefits of non-uniform allocation remain unexplored at these sparsity levels.
- No evaluation of generation quality, safety benchmarks, or long-tail input robustness.

## Relevance

这篇论文直接涉及LLM压缩中的数值精度与稀疏性问题，与"LLM训练稳定性与可观测性"和"计算可靠性与精度误差"两个研究方向相关——非结构化剪枝在硬件加速下的精度保持和部署可靠性是实际系统中的关键问题。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does LEAP scale to models beyond 8B (e.g., LLaMA-3.1 70B, 405B)? Does the near-uniform sparsity allocation hold?
- What are the effects of joint weight-and-mask optimization layered on top of LEAP masks?
- Can LEAP masks be combined with quantization (e.g., INT4/INT8) for compound compression without compounding accuracy loss?
- How do LEAP-pruned models perform on generative tasks (code generation, long-form reasoning) beyond zero-shot classification?
- Is the near-uniform per-block allocation an artifact of the global sparsity regularizer, or does it reflect a fundamental property of transformer pruning at 50–60% density?
