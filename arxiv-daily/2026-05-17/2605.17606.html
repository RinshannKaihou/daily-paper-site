The Neural Tangent Kernel for Classification

Jonathan Plenk · Sergio Calvo-Ordonez · Alvaro Cartea · Yarin Gal · Mark van der Wilk · Kamil Ciosek

Extends NTK theory from regression to classification by proving wide networks stay in the lazy training regime under cross-entropy — with either parameter-space regularization or non-degenerate targets (e.g. label smoothing) — yielding explicit NTK-characterized solutions and a Bayesian connection via the infinite-width ensemble.
arxiv.org/abs/2605.17606 ↗
Key Metric

Convergence error O((log n)c / √n); regularization as small as β = 0.01 keeps the NTK constant on MNIST.

Why This Matters

Resolves an open problem since Jacot et al. (2018) — extending NTK to the most common loss function (cross-entropy). Label smoothing link gives immediate practical relevance.

Critical Edge

Theory covers only wide feedforward nets; no validation on ResNets, Transformers, or ImageNet. One-hot labels need explicit regularization.

Bayesian Bridge

Ensemble covariance is provably less uncertain than Laplace NTK-GP posterior. NTK solution ≡ MAP in a GP-Gumbel model (β = τ²).

Methodology

Treats softmax as part of the loss, not the architecture — the pre-activation NTK stays constant even though the post-softmax NTK diverges (resolving Liu et al. 2020's impossibility result).

STEP 1
Lazy Training Proof
Thm 3.1: Wide net approximated by parameter-linearization with error O((log n)c/√n). Two cases: β>0 (regularized, any targets) or β=0 (non-degenerate targets).
STEP 2
Function-Space Dynamics
Stationary point solved via operator Φ(z) = Θ∇zC(z) + βz. Trained predictor: g∞(x) = Φ−1(βfθ₀(x)). Enables efficient sampling.
STEP 3
Bayesian Connections
Ensemble mean = Laplace posterior mean. Ensemble covariance provably less uncertain than Laplace NTK-GP posterior (ΣLap − ΣEns ⪰ 0). MAP-Gumbel equivalence: β = τ².

Results & Highlights

Lazy regime preserved for classification Directly contradicts prior belief that cross-entropy necessarily breaks the constant-NTK approximation.
Regularization suffices with one-hot labels Even small β = 0.01 keeps the NTK nearly constant on MNIST. No label smoothing required.
Label smoothing recovers the regime Without regularization, full-support targets (ε > 0) prevent logit divergence and recover lazy training.
Explicit solution via Φ−1 Closed-form characterization enables efficient uncertainty quantification from the infinite-width ensemble.
Ensemble vs. Bayesian uncertainty Infinite-width ensemble is more conservative than Laplace GP posterior but less conservative than NTK-GP posterior.
MAP equivalence NTK classification solution equals temperature-rescaled MAP in GP-Gumbel model, giving β a principled interpretation.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Resolves fundamental gap in NTK theory with clean, elegant approach
  • Two complementary conditions (regularization OR non-degenerate targets) cover most settings
  • Softmax-as-loss insight sidesteps Liu et al. impossibility result
  • Bridges frequentist and Bayesian perspectives via MAP + Laplace
  • Label smoothing connection provides immediate practical relevance

Limitations

  • One-hot labels require explicit regularization; unregularized case needs full-support targets
  • Theory limited to wide/infinite-width feedforward nets — gap to practical ResNets/Transformers
  • No experiments on modern architectures or datasets beyond MNIST
  • Required width may be impractically large for near-degenerate targets
  • Normalization layers and skip connections not covered

Open Questions

  • How does the theory extend to BatchNorm, LayerNorm, or multi-head attention?
  • Can finite-width corrections (e.g. μP) combine with these classification results?
  • Quantitative relationship between smoothing ε and required width n?
  • Can Φ−1-based sampling scale to realistic datasets for uncertainty estimation?
  • How does infinite-width ensemble uncertainty compare to deep ensembles & MC Dropout?
  • Does the MAP-Gumbel equivalence suggest principled ways to set β (or τ)?
