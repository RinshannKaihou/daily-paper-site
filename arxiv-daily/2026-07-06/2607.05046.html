Methodology — a two-stage control-variate pipeline

STAGE 1

Cross-fold matrix completion

An M×N score matrix S splits into densely-observed anchor models and sparsely-observed target models. IterativeSVD imputes missing entries; K-fold cross-completion keeps predictions Ŷ honest (out-of-sample) so they can act as control variates.

Ŷij ← M(S with fold-k held out), averaged over K folds
STAGE 2

CrossPPI control-variate estimation

θ̂ is the full-N prediction mean plus a rectifier over only the observed set. PPI++-style power tuning (λ*) bounds variance below the classical mean. Theorem 1 gives asymptotically valid (1−α) coverage.

θ̂i = mean(Ŷi·) + rectifier(observed Ji), CI via plug-in Σ̂
Algorithm- & metric-agnostic. Any completion method (IterativeSVD, NN, nuclear-norm, 2PL-IRT) drops in. Statistical validity does not require the matrix to be truly low-rank — low rank only sets the magnitude of variance reduction. Coverage holds even with noisy imputation.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean & theoretically grounded. Unbiased estimates and valid CIs that hold even when the low-rank assumption fails.
  • Near-zero extra cost — skipped prompts need neither inference nor scoring — plus a "never worse than classical" variance guarantee.
  • Broad empirical sweep across five heterogeneous tasks, with thorough ablations (rank, folds, completion algorithm, anchor-set).
  • Unified pointwise + pairwise inference — exactly what practitioners need for A/B and regression testing.

Limitations

  • Asymptotic guarantees only — small-sample behavior is validated empirically, not proven.
  • ≈ MCAR assumption: validity needs the observation mask independent of prompt content; targeted/stratified eval is unaddressed.
  • Gains hinge on anchor predictiveness. With weak/unrelated anchors, CollabEval degenerates to classical estimation (safe, but benefit-free).
  • Raw score matrices not released, limiting exact replication; gains shrink and vanish as p → 1.0.

Open Questions

Q1How does CollabEval behave under non-MCAR observation (difficulty-stratified / active annotation of hard prompts)? Can the bias-correction handle informative missingness?
Q2Can contextual matrix completion with prompt/model covariates push low-p gains past the +13.5% NN baseline?
Q3What is the minimum anchor-set size/composition to realize >20% CI reduction for a new model family absent from the anchors?
Q4Can the control-variate form yield valid CIs for ranking / leaderboard positions, not just means and pairwise differences?
