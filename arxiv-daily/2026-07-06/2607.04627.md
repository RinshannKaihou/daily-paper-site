> **一句话总结：** 本文为"人格训练蒙特卡洛"（PTMC，一种用K个神经策略机器人在限价订单簿中交易来估计市场结果分布的仿真器）建立完整的统计可靠性理论：将估计量方差分解为人格抽样与运行内两部分并给出无偏估计与最优算力分配，证明输入分布失配与策略克隆误差如何传播成可量化的三项误差预算，并核心地证明异质新闻敏感度可从聚合影响曲线严格识别、给出边界校正的同质性检验与两条PTMC优越性分离定理（Jensen偏差下界与Lucas批判的极小化界）。

> **TL;DR:** This is a theory-only paper that makes the word "reliable" precise for Persona-Trained Monte Carlo (PTMC), an agent-based limit-order-book simulator in which K persona-conditioned neural policy bots trade and outcome functionals are averaged over N runs. It decomposes estimator variance into a persona-draw component σ²_P and a within-run component σ²_w with unbiased ANOVA estimators, derives the compute-optimal outer/inner allocation, bounds how misestimated persona distributions and cloned-policy errors propagate (a three-term total-error budget), proves that heterogeneous news sensitivity is identifiable from the aggregate impact curve A(z)=E_Q[g(ηz)] via a strict Jensen gap and odd moments (with a sharp failure when the response nonlinearity g is unknown), gives √n-consistent estimators and a boundary-corrected ½χ²₀+½χ²₁ test, and proves two conditional separation theorems: an irreducible Jensen bias floor against any homogeneous-population simulator and a Lucas-critique minimax impossibility against any observational-data method (BNNs, ensembles, LLM forecasters) on interventional targets. No empirical benchmarks or datasets are evaluated — all results are theorems with full proofs, with the numerical program deferred to a companion experimental study.

## 核心贡献

- **蒙特卡洛收敛性的无条件保证（Theorem 1）：** 证明 PTMC 估计量无偏、以 N⁻¹ᐟ² 速率几乎必然收敛到其估计目标，并给出有效置信区间；通过两层设计把总方差分解为人格间方差 σ²_P 与运行内方差 σ²_w，给出两者的无偏 ANOVA 估计。
- **最优算力分配（Theorem 2）与 Gode–Sunder 异议的可量化化（Corollary 2.1）：** 在固定预算下导出外层人格抽样与内层重复的最优分配 R*（√(c₀σ²_w/(c₁σ²_P))）；当 σ²_P→0 时 R*→∞，即"人格不重要时永远不重抽样"——把行为结构是否有用的老争论变成一个可测的数。
- **输入失配的可预算化（Theorem 3 + Corollary 3.1）：** 用逐步极大耦合证明，人格分布在 Wasserstein-1 距离上偏移 W₁ 与策略在总变差上偏移 ε，会把估计目标推移最多 2MKTL·W₁ 与 2MKTε，构成可分别估计的三项总误差预算。
- **水平无关界（Theorem 4）：** 在 Doeblin 小化条件 (A4) 下，把因子 T 换成 1/α，对终态与时间平均泛函水平无关地稳定（诚实地指出该条件对真实订单簿的验证是开放问题）。
- **异质新闻反应的可识别性（核心，Theorem 5 + Corollary 5.1 + Proposition 1）：** 当响应非线性 g 固定时，聚合影响曲线通过严格 Jensen 间隙检测异质性，并通过原点附近的奇数矩 + Hausdorff 可决性局部识别分布 Q；仅知到尺度时仍识别"活跃子群体"的形状；Proposition 1 给出尖锐边界——若 g 未知，则异质性与同质群体完全观测等价，识别是"模型类内"性质。
- **估计与边界校正检验（Theorem 6–7）：** 非线性最小二乘给出 √n 一致、渐近正态的 (Eη,Varη) 估计；同质性零假设 Var(η)=0 落在参数边界，检验统计量服从 ½χ²₀+½χ²₁，并明确警告朴素自助法在该边界处不一致（Andrews 2000）。
- **两条分离定理（Theorem 8–10）：** 任何同质人口仿真器带不可消除偏差下界 ≥(8/3)z²β(z)Var_Q(η)（Theorem 8）；任意观测数据的泛函方法（含 BNN、深度集成、LLM 预测器）对干预目标的最坏误差恒 ≥Δ，而正确设定的结构 PTMC 估计量一致——即把 Lucas 批判转为可计算常数的极小化界。

## Key Contributions

- **Unconditional Monte-Carlo guarantees (Theorem 1):** PTMC is a Monte Carlo method in the full technical sense — unbiased, N⁻¹ᐟ²-a.s. convergent to its estimand, with valid CIs — because the outer persona loop preserves the i.i.d. structure classical theory needs; variance splits as σ²_P + σ²_w with unbiased ANOVA estimators from a two-stage design.
- **Optimal budget allocation (Theorem 2) reframing the Gode–Sunder objection (Corollary 2.1):** The variance-minimizing inner/outer split is R* = √(c₀σ²_w/(c₁σ²_P)); as σ²_P→0 the optimal design draws one population and never redraws, turning "does behavioral structure do any work?" into a measured quantity per functional.
- **A budgetable, attributed error bound (Theorem 3, Corollary 3.1):** A maximal-coupling argument shows persona-distribution error moves the estimand by ≤ 2MKTL·W₁(P,P̂) and policy-cloning error (TV ≤ ε_BC) by ≤ 2MKTε_BC, assembled into a three-term budget (Monte Carlo + Wasserstein persona error + TV policy error), each separately estimable from data; Lemma 3 verifies the TV-Lipschitz assumption for the actual softmax/Gaussian-head architecture.
- **A uniform-in-horizon bound (Theorem 4):** Under a Doeblin minorization (A4) the horizon factor T is replaced by min(T,1/α) for terminal and time-average functionals, identifying the single constant (mixing rate α) governing horizon reliability.
- **The central contribution — identifiability of heterogeneous news reaction (Theorem 5, Corollary 5.1, Proposition 1):** With response nonlinearity g fixed by the architecture, the aggregate impact curve A(z)=E_Q[g(ηz)] detects heterogeneity via a strict Jensen gap, identifies Q near the origin through odd moments + Hausdorff moment determinacy, and remains shape-identified up to scale; Proposition 1 gives the sharp negative boundary that under unknown g every heterogeneous population is observationally equivalent to a homogeneous one with a deformed g.
- **Operational inference (Theorems 6–7):** A nonlinear-least-squares estimator of (Eη, Varη) is √n-consistent and asymptotically normal, and the homogeneity test — a null on the parameter boundary — has the ½χ²₀+½χ²₁ limit, replacing a naive bootstrap that would be inconsistent exactly at the null of interest.
- **Two conditional separation theorems (Theorems 8–10):** (i) every homogeneous-population simulator carries an irreducible bias floor ≥ (8/3)z²β(z)Var_Q(η) on the impact functional, while the PTMC plug-in converges (Corollary 9.1); (ii) for interventional targets no functional of observational data — BNNs, ensembles, LLM forecasters alike — can beat an explicit worst-case error ≥ Δ at any sample size, while the correctly specified structural estimator does (Corollary 10.1), converting the Lucas critique into a minimax bound with closed-form ∆.

## Methodology

The paper is entirely theoretical: it states assumptions, proves theorems, and classifies guarantees, with **no simulation or numerical experiment** (explicitly deferred to a companion experimental-design document).

Setup (Section 3). PTMC is formalized as a Markov chain: a compact persona space P, a persona distribution P, a Polish market state space S (book + history + inventories), a finite action space A, a trained policy kernel π_φ(·|s,p), i.i.d. exogenous shocks ξ, and a deterministic matching map Ψ. A *run* draws K personas ω∼P^⊗K, then evolves s_{t+1}=Ψ(s_t,a_t,ξ_{t+1}) for T steps with actions a_t^(k)∼π_φ(·|s_t,p^(k)); a bounded functional |F|≤M records the path. The estimand is μ=E_P^⊗K[F(X)], estimated by μ̂_N or a two-stage (D persona populations × R inner replications) design μ̂_{D,R}.

Three core techniques are used:
1. **Variance decomposition / input-uncertainty theory** (Section 4): apply the law of total variance to get σ²_P=Var(μ(ω)) and σ²_w=E[Var(F|ω)] (Theorem 1), then a constrained AM–GM optimization yields the optimal allocation R* (Theorem 2).
2. **Maximal-coupling stability analysis** (Section 5): couple two K-agent simulations agent-by-agent and step-by-step; a single action disagreement (probability ≤ Kε_BC or ≤ L‖p−p̂‖ per agent per step) cascades over T steps, giving linear-in-KT worst-case bounds; under Doeblin minorization, a Dobrushin contraction (1−α) replaces the telescoped sum by a geometric series (Theorem 4).
3. **Random-coefficient identification + boundary econometrics** (Sections 6–8): expand A(z) via the odd Taylor series of g to read off odd moments m_{2j+1}, then solve the (determinate Hausdorff) moment problem to recover Q; a concavity/Jensen argument detects heterogeneity; a Taylor-remainder argument prices the Jensen gap (Theorem 8); a two-point observational-equivalence construction (Proposition 1) gives the Lucas-critique minimax separation (Theorem 10); standard M-estimation (Newey–McFadden / van der Vaart) plus cone-projection asymptotics (Andrews 2001; Self–Liang 1987) deliver √n estimation and the boundary test.

The appendix (Lemma 3) verifies the key TV-Lipschitz assumption (A1) for softmax heads (L=(1−1/|A|)L₀) and composite softmax-plus-Gaussian action heads, including persona-dependent scales via Pinsker's inequality.

## Results & Highlights

No quantitative results reported — this is a theory paper that reports theorems with full proofs and contains no simulations, datasets, or benchmarks. The numerical program that would validate the theory is explicitly deferred to a companion experimental study (Section 9 specifies four checks a correct implementation must pass). The "results" are therefore *theoretical quantitative guarantees with explicit constants*, summarized below as the paper's evidence:

- **Bias floor against homogeneous simulators (Theorem 8):** For any g∈G, the representative-agent impact-curve error satisfies g(zm) − A(z) ≥ **(8/3) z² β(z) Var_Q(η)** for every z>0 whenever Var_Q(η)>0, where β(z)=−min_{y∈[mz/2,η̄z]} g″(y)>0 (closed-form for tanh). No dataset is involved; this is a population-level lower bound that *no data or compute can remove* (Corollary 9.1).
- **Stability budget (Theorem 3, Corollary 3.1):** Persona error ≤ **2MKT L·W₁(P,P̂)** and policy error ≤ **2MKT ε_BC** (M bounds F, L the policy's TV-Lipschitz constant). The total reliability budget is √((σ²_P+σ²_w)/N) + 2MKT ε_BC + 2MKTL·W₁(P,P̂). Under Doeblin (A4) the horizon factor T is replaced by min(T, **1/α**) (Theorem 4).
- **Boundary-corrected homogeneity test (Theorem 7):** The quasi-likelihood-ratio statistic for H₀:Var(η)=0 converges to **½χ²₀ + ½χ²₁** (an atom of ½ at 0 plus a χ²₁ tail), so rejecting when T_n>χ²_{1,1−2α} gives asymptotic size α<½; the naive bootstrap is inconsistent here.
- **Interventional impossibility constant (Theorem 10):** Two observationally identical markets S¹,S² differ in the interventional target by **2∆** (a closed-form integral in g′), so every observational-data estimator has worst-case error ≥ **∆** at *every* sample size, while the correctly specified PTMC plug-in is consistent (Corollary 10.1).

The paper honestly flags (Remark 3) that the worst-case constants are "demanding" with realistic K,T (informative only when KT ε_BC<1 and KTL·W₁<1, i.e. when every bot's every decision is treated as pivotal) and (Remark 5) that the Doeblin constant α "may be astronomically small," in which case min(T,1/α)=T and Theorem 4 "adds nothing beyond Theorem 3."

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — mixed and self-advertised as such.** The paper is unusually honest that Theorems 1–2 (variance decomposition, optimal allocation) are "mathematically standard" transplants of Sun et al. (2011) / Cheng–Holland (1997), with the contribution being "framing of persona draws as input uncertainty, not the mathematics." The genuinely new content is Theorem 5–7 + Proposition 1 (identification of heterogeneous news reaction, not previously stated in the agent-based-market literature per the authors) and the separation theorems of Section 8 — though even these use "elementary" techniques (Taylor remainders, analytic continuation, a two-point argument).
- **Rigor — high within its stated scope, but scope is narrow.** Proofs are complete and use classical, correct machinery (M-estimation, cone-projection asymptotics, maximal coupling, moment determinacy on compact support). However, *every* positive result is conditional on a structural commitment the paper itself proves untestable: Proposition 1 shows the central identification claim collapses completely if the response nonlinearity g is unknown. So "heterogeneous news reaction is identified" is strictly a within-model-class statement, and the paper says so — but it means the headline result cannot be empirically validated as a *property of markets*, only of the PTMC model class.
- **Reproducibility — weak for an empirical claim, fine for a theorem.** There are literally no experiments to reproduce; Section 9 only *specifies* four checks. The single-author, independent-researcher, AI-drafted status, plus dependence on two companion papers (one already posted, one not), means the broader framework cannot be independently assessed from this document alone. The soft-max TV-Lipschitz verification (Lemma 3) is the most concretely checkable artifact.
- **Significance — real but conditional and load-bearing on one unverifiable premise.** The Lucas-critique minimax separation (Theorem 10) is a genuinely nice result: it crisply states that no observational-data method (BNNs, ensembles, LLM forecasters) can answer interventional "stress" questions, with a closed-form ∆. But by Remark 12/13 the advantage holds only when g is correctly specified, which Proposition 1 proves cannot be checked from the same data — making the conditions "the content rather than a caveat," as the authors concede.
- **Practical utility of the bounds — questionable.** The stability constants are linear in KT and the authors admit (Remark 3) they are "informative only when KT ε_BC<1," which is "demanding" for realistic populations and horizons; the Doeblin constant α for a real order book is an open problem and "may be astronomically small," in which case Theorem 4 reduces to Theorem 3. So the worst-case reliability budget may be vacuous in practice, with the realized sensitivity slopes (deferred to the companion study) expected to lie "orders of magnitude below the worst-case constants."
- **Red flags:** (i) single independent author with no institutional backing and AI-assisted drafting; (ii) a key missing ablation — there is no demonstration that σ²_P is ever measurably positive in a realistic setting (the entire framework's value rests on this, yet the empirical evidence is entirely deferred); (iii) the nonparametric estimation of Q is admitted "severely ill-posed" with only logarithmic rates (open problem 4), so the identification theory is currently operational only in a 2-parameter parametric corner; (iv) a data quirk — the "news-ignorers" atom Q({0}) is provably invisible to the impact curve (Remark 7), so a 20% η=0 component (as in the companion Study A) is undetectable from the very curve the theory analyzes.

## Strengths

- Exceptional intellectual honesty: the paper explicitly partitions its claims into "unconditionally proved," "conditionally bounded (worst-case)," and "open," and repeatedly states what its theorems do *not* claim (e.g., Remark 12(iv): no predictive dominance is asserted).
- The Lucas-critique-as-minimax construction (Theorem 10 + Corollary 10.1) is a clean, non-trivial result that crisply delineates when a structural agent-based simulator beats any black-box forecaster, with a closed-form impossibility constant ∆.
- Full, self-contained proofs (not "deferred to a future version"), including the boundary-econometrics detail that naive bootstrapping is invalid at the homogeneity null — a correctness point many applied papers would miss.
- The TV-Lipschitz verification (Lemma 3) actually checks the main assumption for the architecture the companion framework trains, including persona-dependent Gaussian scales via Pinsker.
- Reframes the 30-year-old Gode–Sunder objection ("do zero-intelligence traders already reproduce allocative efficiency?") as a measurable allocation quantity σ²_P and a testable R*, which is a useful conceptual contribution even where the underlying math is standard.

## Limitations

- **Theory-only; zero empirical validation.** No simulations, datasets, or benchmarks; all numerical illustration is deferred to a companion experimental paper not included here.
- **The Doeblin condition (A4) needed for the horizon-uniform bound (Theorem 4) is unverified for any realistic limit-order book**, and the authors concede α may be "astronomically small," making the result vacuous in practice.
- **Worst-case stability bounds are linear in KT and likely loose** — acknowledged to be informative only under demanding small-error regimes; the empirical tightness evidence is absent.
- **Core identification is conditional on a fixed, known response nonlinearity g** (Proposition 1); whether g is correctly specified is itself untestable from impact-curve data, so the headline identifiability claim is not a model-free property of order flow.
- **Only scalar (1-D) news-sensitivity η is identified**; Remark 9 concedes joint identifiability of the multivariate persona vector is not proven and the multivariate moment problem is generally indeterminate.
- **Nonparametric recovery of Q is severely ill-posed** (only logarithmic rates expected — open problem 4), so the paper is operationally limited to a 2-parameter parametric family.
- **Single independent author, AI-assisted writing**, dependent on companion papers (arXiv:2606.29556 and an unposted experiment-design document); the framework cannot be evaluated end-to-end from this file alone.
- The mean-field K→∞ limit is stated as an open problem (open problem 1); all guarantees hold only for fixed K.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the worst-case KT-linear stability bound be tightened using concentration or martingale arguments to something sublinear in T for path functionals (drawdowns), where the Doeblin marginal-coupling argument provably fails?
- How small is the Doeblin constant α for a realistic truncated price-time-priority order book, and is (A4) ever non-vacuous — i.e., does Theorem 4 ever beat Theorem 3 in practice?
- Is the measurement σ²_P > 0 ever achieved in a realistic calibrated PTMC market, or does the Gode–Sunder null (σ²_P = 0) hold empirically, which would make the entire framework's value vanish?
- Can the identification theory be extended to the multivariate persona vector (η, loss-aversion λ, herding h) without imposing independence or a known copula, or is joint identifiability fundamentally blocked by the ill-posedness of high-dimensional moment inversion?
- Does the Lucas-critique minimax separation (Theorem 10) survive when the observational information set is enriched with agent-level cross-sectional order flow, or does the two-point equivalence pair simply migrate to a residual unidentified subspace?
- Given the disclosed AI-assisted drafting and single independent author, what independent verification pathway exists for the multi-page proofs (especially Theorem 7's cone-projection asymptotics) absent a reproducible code artifact?
