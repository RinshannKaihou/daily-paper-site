Wrong Before Right: Late Rescue and Interface Failure in Aligned Language Models

Jiaqi Deng · independent researcher · single-author, 17 models, 64× scale

一句话总结：对齐后的语言模型在中层会短暂"先选错答案"，仅靠后层纠正才输出正确结果——这一 wrong-dip 现象可因果验证，预测结构化压缩失效（非量化），并能用 hinge 惩罚 LoRA 降低 67–70%。

Key finding

Aligned LLMs transiently commit to the wrong answer in mid layers — rescued only by late-layer correction, completely invisible at the output.

A causally verified internal trajectory ("wrong-dip") that audits alignment recipes rather than alignment itself — and silently degrades under ordinary post-training.

A causal audit of internal correctness assembly that works even when outputs look perfect — directly guiding pre-deployment risk assessment for pruning and quantization.

Critical edge

01Methodology

Stimuli. 278 polarity-controlled minimal pairs (200 value-flip, 48 explicit negation, 30 permission) — each pair differs only in a polarity operator with symmetric candidate tokens as a surface control. Plus 200 role-binding pairs, 96/96 natural-language vignettes, and a 256-item relational-complexity ladder.

Trajectory metric (difference-in-differences). For each layer ℓ, margin = logit(t_pos) − logit(t_neg) decoded through final norm + unembedding; DiD(ℓ) = margin_a − margin_b. The construction cancels item-level lexical-frequency effects. Two item statistics: max_wrong_dip (deepest opposite-direction excursion within 25–90% depth) and commit_layer_frac (last disagreeing depth) — both from a single forward pass.

Three validity controls. (i) out-of-domain tuned lens restores the dip that an in-domain lens over-corrects; (ii) causal patchscopes transplant the mid-layer hidden state into a neutral context and causally drive the wrong token (n=278, all 12 ladder models) — every cross-family claim uses this measure; (iii) raw logit lens is shown to overestimate Llama's dip 30× (raw 0.72 vs causal 0.023).

Double dissociation. Genuine weight-level degradations (late/mid MLP SVD, int8/w3/w4 RTN quantization, 12.5% block-drop, 50% structured channel pruning) are applied with the dip always measured on the intact model — baseline-reload agreement 275–277/278.

02Results & highlights

Scale emergence · Table 1

Alignment amplification Δdip is null at small scale, emerges at 3B (+0.140, t=9.70), peaks at 32B (+0.182), yet Llama-3-8B reverses (−0.028) — all invisible at output (accuracy ≥97%).

Double dissociation · Table 2

High-dip items are 3–7× more likely to flip under late-SVD, block-drop, and structured pruning — but quantization deaths are dip-blind (ρ ≈ 0).

Dip-reg SFT vs output-only · Table 4

At 7B / 240-item strict set: mid-SVD retention 0.872 → 0.943, head-to-head 36:6 / 14:3 / 17:7 across 3 seeds — all same direction, clean accuracy 0.996 vs 1.000.

Silent internal corruption · Table 5

Output-only SFT worsens the causal dip 2.8× (0.129→0.360 at 1.5B) while looking perfect at the surface — dip-reg SFT reverses it (→0.043, −67%).

Late-rescue deprivation · §5.5

Scaling last-15% blocks to α=0 barely moves aggregate accuracy (0.98→0.95) but concentrates flips on high-dip items: 11.0% vs 0% flip rate (7B negation).

Family attribution · §7

At RTN w3, Llama survives (0.55) vs Qwen (0.46); forced inversion reveals Qwen-7B carries residual dip 1.13 (late patch) vs Llama-8B shallow commit (mid rewrite).

03Strengths & limitations

Strengths

  • Causally grounded. Patchscopes + late-rescue deprivation, not probe-only correlations; explicit out-of-domain tuned-lens control defeats probe circularity.
  • Clean falsifiable boundary. Structural damage vs quantization, supported by a true double dissociation — rare and valuable.
  • Deployment-facing. Zero-inference-overhead audit (one forward pass per item) plus a training-time monitor that separates recipes after accuracy saturates.
  • Honest limitations naming specific failure modes (cliff-like interface failure, retired ladder tier, single-seed scope).
  • Breadth: 17 models, 3 families, 64× scale, multiple task families and compression operators.

Limitations

  • Multi-seed significance only at 7B — the scale ladder and most compression tables are single-seed, weakening precise deltas.
  • Free-generation remains open; natural-language bridge is significant only at the semantic-candidate readout.
  • Cross-family comparisons are correlational; Qwen3-8B reversal is a non-significant trend.
  • LoRA-scale only; capability tax proxied by corpus NLL, not a broader battery.
  • AWQ/GPTQ kernels could not load — quantization conclusions rest on bitsandbytes-NF4 and group-wise RTN.
  • Code/data repo promised but not public at submission.

04Questions for further exploration

Does the wrong-dip generalize beyond polarity/relation tasks to reasoning, code, or tool-use — where the "correct answer" is not a single token and the late-rescue budget may be larger?

Can the dip audit predict silent compression failures (correct-but-degraded behavior, not just flips) under real kernels like AWQ/GPTQ the authors could not load?

Could monitoring max_wrong_dip during pretraining catch divergence before loss spikes — alongside other silent-corruption signals?

Is the dip-regularized SFT penalty robust under distribution shift and larger-scale full fine-tuning, not just LoRA r16?

Does the late-rescue vs interface-failure decomposition explain evaluation fragility in LLM-as-judge and agentic pipelines, where the interface layer may dominate?

Could the wrong-dip anchor a "system card" field auditing how much correctness a model delegates to its late-layer correction budget?
