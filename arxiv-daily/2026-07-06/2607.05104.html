Grokking is a conditional, fragile phase transition — not a clean law.

On an 11,856-parameter Llama-style toy model, grokking is gated by training coverage, weight decay, and the floating-point environment. A pure CPU thread-count change flips individual seeds while leaving the aggregate rate untouched — exposing single-run phase-transition claims as untrustworthy.

  • Coverage tracks modulus, not structure. Mod-7/9 composites cross over at 5–10% coverage; mod-10 at 20–40%. Raising M from 8→10 drops grok-rate 60–70 pts; swapping composition moves it ≤10 pts.
  • Decomposition = data efficiency. Matched 160-example budget: two-specialist pipeline groks 10/10 vs monolith 0/10; identical-supervision scratchpad monolith still 0/10 → coverage, not supervision density, drives it.
  • Periodicity correlates with generalization. Logit Fourier concentration: r=+0.37 (M=7), r=+0.65 (M=10) — but partly definitional.
  • Mechanistic negative. The dim-16 model does not form the textbook Fourier embedding circle of Nanda et al. (2023).
03

Strengths  ⇄  Limitations

Strengths

  • Treats grokking as a rate over many seeds inside a fixed numerical environment — never a single run — and proves why with three of its own refuted narratives.
  • The weight-decay inverted-U positive control licenses reading the numerical-perturbation null as genuinely null, not as measurement insensitivity.
  • Correct paired statistics (exact McNemar, Newcombe CIs) for same-seed binary outcomes, reported with thresholds not just point estimates.
  • Honest framing of the mechanistic result as a consistency check plus a genuinely independent negative.
  • Full artifact release: checkpoint, code, per-seed records, archived Zenodo DOI, per-run numerics logged.

Limitations

  • One toy architecture (~11.8K params); modular arithmetic with M ≤ 10; only Intel MKL + one GPU tested. Scaling to other BLAS / vendors is open.
  • Threshold-sensitive device flip count (8–23/100 across τ) is noisier than thread-count flips (45–62/300).
  • Cardinality regularity rests partly on 3–5-seed pilots; author flags these as provisional.
  • Periodicity-generalization correlation is partly circular; causal circuit not established.
  • Single author, no peer review — reliance on self-verification and released artifacts.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the reduction-order / device knife-edge persist, damp out, or amplify as width grows from 11.8K toward 10⁶–10⁹ params? averaging could smooth or concentrate sub-ULP effects
  • How does flip rate behave across Intel MKL vs OpenBLAS vs Apple Accelerate and multiple GPU vendors, model + seed held fixed?
  • Can the coverage-cardinality regularity become a quantitative law threshold ≈ f(M, |domain|, width) predicting grokking beyond M=10?
  • Is the flip genuine chaotic sensitivity near a shallow basin, or does a measurable loss-landscape feature predict which seeds flip?
  • Would pinning numerics in large-scale emergent-ability benchmarks change any published "phase transition" claim — and can it be enforced as a checklist?
