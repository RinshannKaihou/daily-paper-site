> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一种基于"神经元行一次性置零"（neuron-row zeroing）的因果审计框架，在语言建模与拒答行为两个难度层级上检验七种重要性选择器（LRP、IG、Consensus-2、Magnitude、Wanda、MeanActivation、Random），证明归因类选择器在 5 个 LLM 上比激活/幅度基线精确 2–4 个数量级，且"排名稳定性"与"因果有效性"相分离（MeanActivation 几乎完美稳定却因果失效）；用对比式 harmful/benign 信号驱动归因可将 LLaMA-3.1-8B 的 hate/crime 拒答率提升到 0.82–0.95 而保持低误拒答，但最关键的反直觉发现是——最稳定的选择器最不可信，拒答子空间高度冗余（LRP 与 IG 的 top-1% 重叠仅 3–6% 却都能安装拒答）。

> **TL;DR:** The authors build a retraining-free causal audit around one-shot neuron-row zeroing to test whether importance "selectors" (LRP, Integrated Gradients, their Borda consensus, plus Magnitude/Wanda/MeanActivation/Random baselines) truly identify causally important rows. Across five LLMs, attribution selectors beat non-attribution baselines by 2–4 orders of magnitude on LeRF perplexity at 30% masking (e.g., on LLaMA-3.1-8B Consensus-2 LeRF PPL = 66 vs MeanActivation 139,874 vs Random 762,286), and a contrastive harmful-vs-benign signal installs refusal to 0.82–0.95 on CAST hate/crime while keeping benign over-refusal ≤ 0.08; the headline lesson is that rank-stability proxies (which MeanActivation passes almost perfectly) systematically fail to detect causally invalid selectors, and the refusal subspace is redundant (LRP↔IG top-1% Jaccard only 3–6%, yet both install refusal).

## 核心贡献

- **一套可复用的因果审计协议（两个难度层级）**：以"神经元行一次性置零"为唯一干预（`W[i,:]=0, b[i]=0`，无需微调、无推理时钩子），在 LM 层用 LeRF/MoRF 扫描检验"是否选对了一般重要神经元"，在行为层用对比式拒答信号检验"是否选对了承载特定行为的神经元"，并定义 Validity Gap = PPL_MoRF − PPL_LeRF 作为单一有效性度量。
- **归因选择器在 LM 层因果有效**：LRP/IG/Consensus-2 在全部 5 个模型上的 LeRF PPL 比激活/幅度/随机基线低 2–4 个数量级，且在 30% 置零率下保持更高下游精度（Consensus-2 在 Gemma-3-12B = 0.503，LLaMA-3.1-8B = 0.392，而非归因基线坍塌到 ~0.28 随机地板）。
- **稳定性 ≠ 有效性的"解离"发现**：MeanActivation 排名稳定性近乎完美（C8→C128 的 Spearman ρ=.994、Jaccard=.934）却 LeRF PPL 高达 139,874；LRP 最不稳定（ρ=.913）却产生最大有效性间隙（8.32e6）。证明低成本的排名稳定性代理会系统性漏掉失效选择器。
- **对比式归因可选择性安装拒答且具行级特异性**：在 LLaMA-3.1-8B 上把 hate/crime 的 CAST-malign 拒答率从基线 0.488 提升到 0.82–0.95，benign 误拒答 ≤ 0.08，PPL 仅 +2–4；层匹配的随机对照在每个域都低 +0.46–+0.77（medical 上对照坍塌到 0.003 vs 真实 0.768），证明效应来自"选对了具体行"而非"选对了层深度"。
- **拒答子空间冗余 + 架构依赖**：LRP 与 IG 的 top-1% 行 Jaccard 仅 3–6%（≈10× LRP-vs-Random 的 0.5%），两者却都能安装拒答；最佳方法跨架构反转（IG 在小 LLaMA 上赢 9/10 格，LRP 在 Qwen3-8B 上领先），说明不存在"通用选择器"，只有"经过验证的选择器"。
- **异议聚合比一致聚合更有信息量**：仅保留两法分歧行的 VETO-LRP 在 LLaMA-3.1-8B 与 Qwen3-8B 上双轴均超过真实 Consensus-2，为"分歧感知聚合"指出方向。

## Key Contributions

- **A reusable causal-audit protocol at two difficulty levels.** A single one-shot intervention — zeroing a neuron row (`W[i,:]=0, b[i]=0`) — requires no fine-tuning and no inference-time hooks, so any behavioral change is attributable purely to the rows the selector ranks, not to surrounding pipeline machinery. The LM level uses LeRF/MoRF (least/most-relevant-first) sweeps with a single Validity Gap metric; the behavior level drives the same selectors with a contrastive harmful-vs-benign signal.
- **Causal faithfulness of attribution selectors at the LM level.** LRP, IG, and Consensus-2 are 2–4 orders of magnitude below non-attribution baselines on LeRF PPL across all five models, and retain substantially higher downstream accuracy at 30% masking (Consensus-2 = 0.503 on Gemma-3-12B, 0.392 on LLaMA-3.1-8B, against a ~0.28 random floor).
- **A sharp rank-stability-vs-validity dissociation.** MeanActivation is almost perfectly rank-stable (Spearman ρ=.994, Jaccard=.934 from C8→C128) yet catastrophically causally invalid (LeRF PPL 139,874), while LRP is the least stable yet has the largest validity gap (8.32e6). Stability is neither necessary nor sufficient.
- **Selective refusal installation with row-level specificity.** Contrastive attribution lifts CAST-malign refusal to 0.82–0.95 on hate/crime with benign over-refusal ≤ 0.08 and PPL within +2–4, generalizes to held-out SorryBench (0.76–0.98), and a layer-matched random control (same per-layer counts, randomized row identities) trails the real mask by +0.46–+0.77 on every domain.
- **A redundant, architecture-dependent refusal subspace.** LRP and IG share only 3–6% of their top-1% behavior rows yet both install refusal; the best method reverses across architectures (IG dominates small LLaMA, LRP leads on Qwen3-8B), and disagreement-aware aggregation (VETO-LRP) outperforms plain consensus.
- **Four control families that rule out trivial explanations:** layer-matched, rank-randomized, LRP∩IG intersection, and veto aggregators — each isolating a distinct confound (depth allocation, the act of averaging, true agreement vs. disagreement signal).

## Methodology

**Intervention — neuron-row masking (§3.1).** Every Linear projection in a transformer block (attention Q/K/V/O, MLP gate/up/down) computes `y = Wx + b`; a "neuron row" is one row of `W`. Masking row `i` sets `W[i,:]=0` and `b[i]=0`, removing a single output dimension. All neuron rows across all blocks are pooled into one global ranking; the selector's lowest-importance k% are zeroed (LeRF) or highest-importance k% (MoRF). This leaves a standard checkpoint that loads and runs unchanged. Embeddings and the LM head are excluded (their rows are vocab-indexed).

**Selectors (§3.2).** Seven scorers under one validity metric: four non-attribution nulls/baselines — **RANDOM** (3-seed uniform), **MAGNITUDE** (data-free mean abs weight), **WANDA** (row-aggregated adaption of Sun et al. 2024), **MEANACTIVATION** (mean abs output activation); and three attribution-based — **LRP** (AttnLRP via the `lxt` library), **IG** (16-step Integrated Gradients), **CONSENSUS-2** (Borda rank aggregation of LRP+IG, motivated by their complementary failure modes). Faithfulness is defined operationally as (i) *sufficiency* — zeroing the top rows produces the intended effect — and (ii) *specificity* — the effect is not reproduced by a layer-matched random control. Necessity/uniqueness is deliberately *not* claimed.

**Validity Gap and the distribution-shift control (§3.2–3.3).** `Gap(S,k) = PPL_MoRF(S,k) − PPL_LeRF(S,k)`; a faithful selector yields a large positive gap, RANDOM ≈ 0. Four design features neutralize the ROAR-style "deletion causes distribution shift" objection: the gap is a differencing measurement; RANDOM is an empirical shift floor; layer-matched controls fix the intervention's shape; and refusal installation is certified on *preserved* benign accuracy/utility/PPL, not on degradation alone.

**Contrastive refusal editing (§5.1, Algorithm 1).** At the behavior level the target changes from next-token loss to a refusal decision margin at the last prompt position, contrasted across matched CAST harmful/benign pairs: `m(x) = mean_{t∈S} z_t − mean_{t∈H} z_t`, where S holds first tokens of refusal-onset phrases ("I must decline", "Unfortunately") and H holds compliance-onset tokens ("Sure", "Absolutely"). Steps: (1) compute per-row signed relevance to m(x); (2) aggregate the compliance-promoting half, per-layer percentile-rank-normalize; (3) form a contrastive score `C_comply = Î_risk − Î_base` and subtract an LM-protect penalty `λ·rℓ(I_lm[i])` that down-weights LM-critical rows. Operating point is `(λ*,k*) = argmax_{V} CAST-malign(λ,k)` over the feasible set `V = {(λ,k): benign ≤ 0.10, PPL ≤ 100}`, relaxed in 0.05 steps if empty.

**Models / data / compute (§3.4).** Five base models across three families: LLaMA-3.2-1B/3B, LLaMA-3.1-8B, Qwen3-8B, Gemma-3-12B. Calibration on 128 WikiText-2 samples; PPL on 256 WikiText-2 test samples; downstream = 8 zero-shot lm-eval-harness tasks. Behavior grid = 4 instruction-tuned models × 5 CAST harm domains × 5 selectors = 70 cells. Refusal judged by ProtectAI/distilroberta-base-rejection-v1 on 500 risk + 500 base prompts; OOD = SorryBench + OR-Bench-Hard; utility = MMLU, GSM8K, IFEval. All on a single A100 80GB; ~6 GPU-days for the behavior grid.

## Results & Highlights

- **LM-level attribution gap (Table 5, LLaMA-3.1-8B at 30% masking):** CONS-2 LeRF PPL = **66**, IG = 98, LRP = 830, versus MEANACTIVATION = 139,874, WANDA = 360,083, and RANDOM = 762,286 — a 2–4 orders-of-magnitude separation that holds across all five models.
- **Downstream accuracy retained at 30% LeRF (Table 5):** CONS-2 reaches **0.503** mean lm-eval-harness accuracy on Gemma-3-12B and **0.392** on LLaMA-3.1-8B, while every non-attribution baseline collapses to the random floor near **0.28–0.30**.
- **Rank-stability dissociation (Table 1, LLaMA-3.1-8B):** MEANACTIVATION has the highest stability (Spearman **.994**, Jaccard **.934**, C8→C128) yet the *worst* attribution validity (LeRF 139,874, Gap 790,875); LRP is least stable (**.913**) yet has the *largest* gap (**8.32e6**) — demonstrating rank stability is neither necessary nor sufficient.
- **Refusal installation (LLaMA-3.1-8B, Fig. 2 / Table 45):** LRP/IG/CONS-2 raise CAST-malign refusal on hate/crime to **0.82–0.95** (baseline hate 0.488, legal 0.024) with benign over-refusal **≤ 0.08** and PPL within **+2–4**. MEANACTIVATION *reduces* refusal (hate 0.488 → 0.124), failing in the opposite direction from RANDOM (0.05–0.38).
- **Specificity / layer-matched control (Table 2):** the real LRP mask beats a layer-count-matched random mask by **+0.46 (adult) to +0.77 (medical)**; on medical the control collapses to a mean of **0.003** vs the real mask's **0.768**, so the effect is attributable to *which rows* were chosen, not depth.
- **OOD transfer vs. over-refusal trade-off:** attribution selectors reach **0.76–0.98** on SorryBench hate/crime (45 unseen categories), but OR-Bench-Hard over-refusal rises from a **0.30** baseline to **0.57–0.88**, and GSM8K loses **0.22–0.34** accuracy at viable operating points.
- **Redundant subspace (Table 35):** LRP↔IG top-1% Jaccard is only **3–6%** at every (model, domain) cell — about 10× the 0.5% LRP-vs-Random overlap — yet both methods install refusal; the refusal subspace admits many disjoint sufficient masks.
- **Disagreement beats agreement (§4.3):** VETO-LRP (only rows LRP ranks important that IG does not) beats real CONS-2 on *both* LeRF and MoRF on LLaMA-3.1-8B (LeRF 28.0 / MoRF 3.32e6) and Qwen3-8B (LeRF 63.1 / MoRF 5.89e7); the strict LRP∩IG intersection gives the lowest LeRF in the whole audit (10.7 on LLaMA-8B) but a catastrophic MoRF cost on Qwen.
- **Architecture-dependent winner (Fig. 3):** IG wins **9 of 10** cells on the two small LLaMA-3.2 models; LRP leads on Qwen3-8B; on LLaMA-3.1-8B and Qwen3-8B the two methods split domain-by-domain — there is no architecture-independent selector.

## Authors & Credibility

The work comes from **Lexsi Labs** (authors Ananth Eswar and Pratinav Seth as co-first authors; Utsav Avaiya; Vinay Kumar Sankarapu). The senior authors have directly relevant prior work on attribution/explainability: the model-agnostic **DLBacktrace** explainer (Sankarapu et al., 2024, arXiv:2411.12643) and an "Interpretability as alignment" position paper (Sengupta, Seth & Sankarapu, 2025), plus row-level LRP pruning heritage they build on. The paper is unusually control-heavy for an interpretability study: it runs four distinct control families (layer-matched, rank-randomized, LRP∩IG intersection, veto aggregators), reports Wilson 95% intervals on refusal rates, and explicitly notes the float-overflow ceiling (~10³⁸) at high masking rates as a representation limit rather than a measurement. The authors commit to releasing all **70 edited checkpoints as standard HuggingFace artifacts** under a research-use agreement with model cards, which materially aids reproducibility. Compute footprint is modest and stated (~6 GPU-days, one A100), and the single-intervention design is inherently simpler to replicate than activation-steering or DPO baselines.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High and well-scoped. The LeRF/MoRF protocol and ROAR retrain-after-removal idea predate this work for *input-feature* attributors on classifiers; the contribution is adapting the causal logic to *internal weight-row* selectors of autoregressive LLMs at two difficulty levels under one intervention. The rank-stability-vs-validity dissociation is the genuinely surprising, memorable result.
- **Rigor:** Strong on controls and honesty. The paper is commendably explicit about its confounds — most notably the **shared surface-form confound**: the contrastive margin and the refusal classifier both key on refusal-onset tokens, so a model that only learned "refusal-shaped openings" could satisfy both. The authors argue SorryBench transfer across 45 unseen categories mitigates this, but they correctly flag a template-diverse judge as the *definitive* missing check. They also disclose that 69 of 70 cells are single evaluations at fixed operating points (only RANDOM is seeded), relying on the grid + Wilson intervals in place of repeated seeds.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Modest compute, single A100, standard models/datasets, promised checkpoint release, and an Algorithm 1 detailed enough to reimplement. The arg-max operating-point selection over a 36-cell (λ,k) grid per (model,selector,domain) is a mild hyperparameter-search surface that could inflate per-cell optima, though the authors show neighboring feasible cells land within ~0.1–0.2 of the reported value.
- **Significance:** Practically important for pruning and safety-editing practitioners: it shows the cheap rank-stability/correlation proxies they often rely on are *systematically blind* to selector failure modes. Conceptually, the "necessity is deliberately not tested" caveat is important — readers tempted to read top-ranked rows as *the* refusal mechanism are warned off.
- **Red flags / caveats:**
  - **The headline medical/legal results are effectively negative.** Refusal installation collapses under the sparsity those domains need (PPL 40–52, GSM8K → 0, MMLU drops ~45 points, IFEval up to 60 points), and the authors are honest that this is a limitation of the signal, not the method — but the abstract's "saturates on hate and crime" phrasing understates that only 2 of 5 domains cleanly work.
  - **Over-refusal cost is large.** OR-Bench-Hard jumps 0.30 → 0.57–0.88, a serious utility penalty that somewhat undercuts "safety improvement" framing.
  - **MEANACTIVATION as the straw-man stability exemplar.** The dissociation is dramatic partly because MEANACTIVATION is a weak baseline; WANDA *inverts* importance on 2 of 5 architectures, which is a more interesting but less centered finding.
  - **Necessity deliberately not tested**, so any "circuit discovery" reading of the top rows overclaims what the audit establishes.
  - **Scope limits:** dense decoder-only, English-only, no MoE/encoder-decoder, no non-gradient attributors (gradient×input, SHAP), and SAE/direction selectors are a distinct intervention class left to future work.

## Strengths

- A single, minimal, retraining-free intervention that serves both the LM-level and behavior-level audits, making the two levels genuinely comparable.
- One of the most control-rich interpretability evaluations in recent memory: layer-matched, rank-randomized, intersection, and veto controls each isolate a distinct confound.
- The rank-stability-vs-validity dissociation is a crisp, actionable warning for anyone using calibration correlation or PPL as a proxy for selector quality.
- Intellectual honesty about what the audit does (sufficiency + specificity) and does not (necessity, uniqueness) establish, and about the negative medical/legal results and the over-refusal cost.
- Architecture-dependence is reported head-on rather than buried, and given a mechanistic gloss (which layers each method routes relevance to vs. where the model computes refusal).

## Limitations

- **Negative results on medical/legal:** refusal installation collapses the model at the sparsity those low-signal-density domains require, so the positive behavior-level story is effectively restricted to hate/crime (and partially adult).
- **Single evaluation per cell** at a fixed operating point; robustness rests on the 70-cell grid and Wilson intervals rather than repeated seeds (only RANDOM is seeded).
- **Surface-form confound** between the contrastive margin and the single refusal classifier (ProtectAI/distilroberta), mitigated but not eliminated by SorryBench transfer; a multi-judge / template-diverse protocol is the recommended follow-up.
- **Narrow scope:** five dense decoder-only models, English only; no MoE, no encoder-decoder, no non-gradient attributors, no SAE/direction selectors.
- **Necessity is out of scope**, so the edits should not be read as recovering a unique refusal "mechanism."
- **Significant utility/over-refusal cost** at the operating points that install refusal (GSM8K −0.22–0.34; OR-Bench-Hard 0.30→0.57–0.88).
- **Hyperparameter surface:** per-cell arg-max over a 36-cell (λ,k) grid, with one rescue relaxation (LLaMA-3.1-8B IG-adult) — small but real.

## Relevance

与你的研究兴趣高度相关：它直接落在 **LLM Hidden States & Interpretability**（对神经元行归因做因果审计）与 **ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations**（证明"排名稳定性"这一常用代理系统性失效，是一种可观测性/可靠性缺陷）的交叉点上，同时为 **LLM Inference Evaluation** 提供了一套以 LeRF/MoRF Validity Gap + 层匹配对照为核心的"选择器有效性"评测范式，可作为评估归因/剪枝/安全编辑方法可靠性的可复用协议参考。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would the rank-stability-vs-validity dissociation survive on MoE or encoder-decoder architectures, or does the high WANDA inversion rate on Qwen3-8B hint that it gets *worse* for non-standard routing?
- Can a **disagreement-aware aggregator** (extending VETO-LRP) systematically beat Consensus-2 across all (model, domain) cells, and does it close the medical/legal gap?
- Does replacing the single refusal classifier with a template-diverse multi-judge protocol change the surface-form-confound conclusion, and by how much do the absolute refusal rates move?
- The {hate, crime} and {medical, legal} row-Jaccard clusters predict that a single union mask could install refusal on both domains in a cluster at once — does that prediction hold, and does it generalize to a third held-out domain?
- Is the redundant refusal subspace (3–6% LRP↔IG overlap) quantitatively predicted by the multi-directional activation-space structure of refusal (Arditi et al. 2024), and can the number of disjoint sufficient masks bound the effective "refusal dimensionality"?
- Can the Validity Gap metric be turned into a per-selector *reliability score* that flags silent selector failure online, analogous to silent-data-corruption (SDC) detectors in numerical kernels?
