> **一句话总结：** 本文通过线性探测将 LLM 隐藏状态中"模型是否真的知道题目不可解"(知识)与"模型是否把这一判断说出来"(语言化)解耦为两个独立方向,发现"编造答案"(fabrication)主要由语言化偏移而非知识缺陷引起,并提出基于知识探针置信度门控的激活引导方法,能在不破坏可解题正确率的前提下显著提升对不可解题的弃答能力。

> **TL;DR:** The paper trains two linear probes on LLM hidden states — one for *knowledge* (does the model internally recognize a math problem as unsolvable?) and one for *verbalization* (does it communicate that judgment?) — and shows on the **ReliableMath** benchmark across 7 LLMs that these are distinct linearly-decodable directions; fabrication stems mainly from K–V misalignment driven by verbalization shifts (e.g., Qwen3-30B-Instruct probe correlation drops from r=0.75 when abstaining to r=0.64 when fabricating), prompting mainly flips verbalization (V-only flips 29.5% vs K-only 8.6%), and gated joint K+V activation steering rescues abstention without the catastrophic specificity collapse (~0.6–0.8 abstention on solvable problems) seen under ungated steering.

## 核心贡献

- **首次在表征层面解耦"知识"与"语言化"**:不同于以往只从行为层面或文本推理轨迹层面研究 verbalization,本文在 LLM 隐藏状态中分别探测"模型是否内部知道题目不可解"(Knowledge, K)与"模型是否把这一判断表达出来"(Verbalization, V),证明两者构成不同的、线性可解码的方向(余弦相似度低至 0.255)。
- **揭示 fabrication 的内部机理**:证明"编造答案"主要源于知识与语言化方向之间的失配(misalignment),且这种失配主要由语言化方向的偏移驱动,而非知识表征本身的变化(所有模型 r_abstain > r_fabric,p<10⁻⁴,Cohen's d∈[0.54, 1.10])。
- **区分提示工程与激活引导的作用机制**:提示中加入不可解性线索主要通过改变语言化方向(V-only flip 远多于 K-only flip)来减少 fabrication,而非改变模型内在知识。
- **揭示推理模型(LRM)中思考轨迹与输出轨迹的不同动力学**:知识信号在思考早期即建立并保持平稳(trace-invariant),而语言化信号随深度单调上升,在输出轨迹中达到峰值——即"最终判断"是在生成过程中逐步固化的。
- **提出门控式联合激活引导方法**:针对无门控引导在可解题上引发灾难性误弃答(abstention 飙升至 0.6–0.8)的问题,设计基于知识探针置信度(p_K>0.5)的 token 级门控机制,使联合 K+V 引导在大幅减少误报的同时可靠地恢复弃答能力。

## Key Contributions

- **First representation-level disentanglement of knowledge vs. verbalization:** Rather than studying verbalization only behaviorally or through textual reasoning traces, the authors probe both quantities as internal linear directions in LLM hidden states, showing they form distinct, linearly decodable representations (cosine similarity as low as 0.255).
- **Mechanistic account of fabrication:** Fabrication is shown to arise from misalignment between the knowledge and verbalization directions, driven primarily by shifts in verbalization rather than in underlying knowledge — this effect is more pronounced in reasoning-oriented model variants.
- **Decomposing the effect of prompting:** Unsolvability cues in prompts reduce fabrication mainly by shifting the verbalization direction (V-only flips dominate K-only flips), not by altering the model's internal knowledge.
- **Distinct dynamics in reasoning vs. output traces of LRMs:** Knowledge is established early in the thinking trace and remains comparatively flat (trace-invariant), whereas verbalization rises monotonically with depth and peaks in the output trace, showing the verbalized verdict consolidates gradually over generation.
- **Gated joint activation steering:** Ungated steering catastrophically damages specificity; a token-level gating policy conditioned on the knowledge probe's confidence (p_K > 0.5) enables joint K+V steering to reliably rescue abstention on unsolvable problems with minimal false alarms on solvable ones.

## Methodology

The pipeline has five stages, applied to the **ReliableMath** benchmark (313 solvable problems drawn from MATH, MinervaMath, AIME24, AMC across high-school to Olympiad difficulty, plus 1,102 unsolvable variants created by removing essential information or introducing contradictions).

1. **CoT generation & hidden-state extraction:** Seven LLMs are prompted under five conditions (standard, standard-plus-solvability, unsolvable-aware, unsolvability-biased, conditioned) and the text CoT plus the corresponding hidden states are stored.

2. **Data preparation for probing:** Each hidden-state sequence is represented by 20 uniformly sampled token vectors; probing is done at the token level. Two labels per trace are needed: (i) the ground-truth solvability label (proxy for the model's *knowledge*), and (ii) the model's *verbalized* verdict. Because unsolvable problems induce complex reasoning trajectories, the verbalized verdict is obtained via an **LLM-as-a-judge** (Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct) classifying whether the model ultimately treated the problem as solved or unsolvable; the judge was human-validated on 100 samples.

3. **Probing:** Logistic-regression linear probes are trained on pooled hidden states to predict two targets — Knowledge (K, ground-truth solvability) and Verbalization (V, the judge-labeled verdict). For each model, the layer achieving the highest probing accuracy is selected and reused for all downstream analyses.

4. **Analysis:** (a) cosine similarity between the learned K and V directions tests whether they are distinct; (b) correlation between probe activations and abstain/fabricate behavior tests the misalignment hypothesis; (c) probe "flip" rates under different prompts isolate which direction (K vs V) is affected by prompting; (d) depth-wise ROC-AUC along thinking vs. output traces characterizes the dynamics in LRMs.

5. **Representation steering:** The residual stream is modified as `h'_t = h_t + m·α·w_unit`, adding a unit-normalized probe direction scaled by the mean residual-stream norm α and a multiplier m. Three directions are studied: pure K, pure V, and their normalized combination KV. A discrete binary **gating** mechanism activates steering only at token positions where the knowledge probe's probability exceeds 0.5, preventing catastrophic false abstentions on solvable problems.

## Results & Highlights

- **Knowledge and verbalization are distinct, linearly decodable directions (Table 1).** Both probes achieve above-chance ROC-AUC across all 7 models, and verbalization is consistently *easier* to decode than knowledge (V-AUC > K-AUC in every case). The cosine similarity between the K and V directions is low — ranging from **0.255 (LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct)** up to 0.647 — confirming they occupy different directions in hidden-state space. On Qwen3-4B-Thinking, K-AUC = **0.872** vs V-AUC = **0.937** with cos(K,V) = **0.504**.

- **Fabrication reflects K–V misalignment driven by verbalization (Table 3).** For every model, probe correlation is higher under abstention than under fabrication (e.g., Qwen3-30B-Instruct: r_abstain = **0.75** vs r_fabric = **0.64**), with the gap statistically significant (p < 10⁻⁴, Cohen's d ∈ [0.54, 1.10]). In the steering sensitivity analysis (Figure 2), the abstain–fabricate gap in probe activations is consistently larger for V than for K, showing fabricated responses differ from abstained ones primarily in how solvability is *verbalized*.

- **Prompting reduces fabrication mainly by flipping verbalization (Table 4).** On unsolvable problems, introducing unsolvability cues causes V-only flips far more often than K-only flips — e.g., Qwen3-4B-Instruct (biased): V-only = **29.5%** vs K-only = **8.6%**; Gemma-4-31B-it (biased): V-only = **55.2%** vs K-only = **8.4%**. The V-only vs K-only difference is significant (two-proportion z-test, p < 10⁻⁵) in every row except Llama-3.1-8B/aware. Notably, K-only flips occur *less* frequently than joint flips, indicating prompts change what the model *says* about solvability, not what it *knows*.

- **Reasoning vs. output traces behave differently (Table 5, Figure 4).** In LRMs, V-only flip rates are substantially lower in thinking traces than in final responses, whereas K-only flips occur more often in thinking traces — perturbations along the knowledge direction surface more in internal reasoning than in final output. Depth-wise (DeepSeek-R1-Llama8B): knowledge is decodable above chance from the earliest bins and stays flat; verbalization starts below knowledge and rises monotonically, overtaking knowledge partway through and peaking in the output trace.

- **Ungated steering is catastrophic for specificity; gating rescues it (Figures 5–6).** Static, ungated steering along V, K, or KV drives abstention on correctly-solvable problems from the 0.01 baseline up to roughly **0.6–0.8** at high intensities. Token-level gating conditioned on p_K > 0.5 drastically reduces these false positives, and **gated KV steering outperforms gated K or V alone** in Qwen3-4B-Instruct (the strongest, most stable rescue at maximum intensity).

- **LLM-as-judge is reliable (Table 6).** Cohen's κ between the judge and human annotators is **0.94**, comparable to inter-human agreement (0.96).

- **Negative result for Gemma and Llama (Appendix H):** although probing confirmed that activation steering shifted internal representations along the targeted K, V, and KV directions, these shifts had *no causal effect* on output — abstention rates stayed statistically indistinguishable from baseline at all multipliers. This suggests the K/V decomposition is representationally present but not causally load-bearing for abstention in those models.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core conceptual move — separating *knowledge of solvability* from *verbalization of solvability* as two internal directions rather than one internal/one behavioral signal — is genuinely novel and well-motivated by Park et al. (2026)'s KAPPA framework generalized from multiple-choice QA. It goes beyond prior work (e.g., Sanyal et al. 2025 found a single solvability-belief direction but did not separate verbalization; Liu et al. 2026 studied verbalization only behaviorally). The depth-wise finding that knowledge is trace-invariant while verbalization consolidates late is a clean, non-obvious result.

- **Rigor:** Strong. Linear probes are standard, the LLM judge is human-validated (κ=0.94), layer selection is principled (best layer per model, reported in Table 2 with full per-layer curves in Appendix D), and statistical tests (two-proportion z-test, Cohen's d with effect sizes) back every claim. The probe training data (~100k train / 20k val for K) is adequate. Class imbalance (81% unsolvable for K) is disclosed.

- **Reproducibility:** Moderate-to-good. Datasets (ReliableMath) and models (all open-weight) are accessible, prompts are fully provided (Tables 7–8), layer indices are reported, and the steering formula is explicit. However, no code release is mentioned, exact gating thresholds beyond p_K>0.5 and the 280-sample steering subset selection procedure could use more detail, and the mean residual-stream norm α is model-specific without tabulated values.

- **Significance:** Solid contribution to mechanistic interpretability of abstention failures. The K/V decomposition and the gated-steering recipe are actionable for inference-time abstention. The distinction between "representationally present but not causally load-bearing" (Gemma/Llama) vs. causally effective (Qwen) is an important caveat that tempers over-generalization.

- **Red flags / weaknesses:**
  - **Causally inconclusive for half the model families.** Steering had no behavioral effect on Gemma-4-31B and Llama-3.1-8B, yet the paper's central framing (and gating mechanism) assumes K/V directions are causally relevant. A reader could over-interpret the Qwen results as universal.
  - **Missing ablations on the gating threshold.** The p_K > 0.5 threshold is a single, unjustified choice; no sensitivity analysis over thresholds is reported, leaving open whether the rescue/specificity trade-off is robust.
  - **Reliance on a single benchmark.** All experiments use ReliableMath (313 solvable / 1,102 unsolvable); the unsolvable items are synthetically constructed (removal/contradiction), which may make the knowledge signal artificially easy to linearly separate compared to naturally ill-posed problems.
  - **Knowledge is only a *proxy*.** The "knowledge" probe predicts the *dataset's* ground-truth label, not the model's actual internal belief — a subtle but important confound the authors acknowledge only implicitly.
  - **Verbalization label depends on an LLM judge**, introducing a circularity risk (a 70B model judging whether a smaller model verbalized abstention), mitigated but not eliminated by κ=0.94 on 100 samples.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated conceptual decomposition (K vs. V) that is directly operationalized as two linear probes, making the analysis falsifiable and manipulable.
- Comprehensive multi-model evaluation (7 LLMs across Qwen3, Llama, DeepSeek-R1, Gemma families, including Instruct vs. Thinking/Reasoning pairs), enabling controlled within-family comparisons.
- Honest reporting of negative results — the null steering effect on Gemma and Llama is disclosed rather than buried, which strengthens credibility.
- The depth-wise analysis of thinking vs. output traces provides a non-trivial mechanistic insight (knowledge is early and flat; verbalization consolidates late).
- Practical, immediately usable gated-steering recipe that addresses the real-world failure mode of ungated interventions harming solvable-problem accuracy.

## Limitations

- The study is restricted to **mathematical solvability**; whether the K/V distinction generalizes to general question answering and other domains is explicitly left as future work.
- The notion of "verbalization" studied is an *internal* verbalization representation, bridging to the more traditional notion of faithfulness (internal representation ↔ generated text) is deferred to future work.
- Steering is causally effective only in Qwen models; the K/V decomposition is representationally present but not causally load-bearing in Gemma and Llama — a significant scope limitation.
- Synthetic construction of unsolvable problems (removal/contradiction edits) may inflate the linear separability of the knowledge signal versus naturally ambiguous or ill-posed problems.
- No public code release is mentioned; the gating threshold and steering-intensity calibration lack sensitivity analysis.

## Relevance

高度契合"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"与"LLM Inference Evaluation"两个研究方向:本文正是通过对隐藏状态的线性探测与激活引导,定量揭示模型"知道但不说"的内部表征机制,并直接服务于推理时(inference-time)的可靠性提升。其关于"编造答案源于语言化而非知识"的结论,以及对计算可靠性与数学基础的讨论,也与"ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations"及"Computation Reliability"方向相关——特别是当模型在数学推理中出现隐藏知识与表层输出失配时,这类静默的可靠性缺陷值得作为 SDC 类问题持续观测。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the K/V decomposition extend beyond mathematics to general QA, code generation, or multi-step agentic reasoning, where "unsolvable" is less crisply defined?
- Why is the K/V direction causally load-bearing for abstention in Qwen but not in Gemma/Llama — is it an architecture/training-difference artifact, or a consequence of how reasoning traces are structured?
- Could the depth-wise finding (knowledge established early, verbalization consolidates late) be exploited as an *early-exit* signal to detect impending fabrication before the model commits to an answer?
- How sensitive is the gated-steering rescue/specificity trade-off to the gating threshold (p_K > 0.5) and to the choice of probe layer — is there a principled way to set these?
- Can the K–V misalignment score itself serve as a training-free abstention calibration signal, and how does it compare to confidence/entropy-based abstention?
- Is the "representationally present but not causally load-bearing" phenomenon (Gemma/Llama) itself a form of silent representation–behavior misalignment worth systematically auditing across model families?
