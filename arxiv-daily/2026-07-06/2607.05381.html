One reverse rate, three coordinate views

Theorem 7 · Proposition 3 · Table 2 — closed-form conversions

Each literature loss optimizes one coordinate of the same object. Use it in the coordinate its loss defines, or convert analytically before sampling — otherwise pay the convert-or-pay penalty.

01

Methodology

A purely theoretical construction in three layers, built on continuous-time Markov chains (CTMCs) and variational inference. Every oracle quantity is available in closed form on the toy model, so results are verified without approximation.

ELBO machinery

§4 · Theorem 1–2

  • Discrete diffusion = a VAE whose latent is the entire noising trajectory.
  • Continuous limit via Lemma 1: one-step KL → Δ·Φ + o(Δ), Φ = Bregman divergence of negative entropy.
  • Second proof via finite-state Girsanov under weaker hypotheses.
  • CTMC ELBO = reconstruction + terminal-prior KL + path integral of Φ.

What the ELBO optimizes

§5 · Thm 3–5

  • Bregman–Pythagoras projection: unique Zₜ-measurable minimizer = posterior mean = true marginal reverse rate (Thm 5).
  • Oracle cost J★ₜ = dH(Z₀|Zₜ)/dt = −dI(Z₀;Zₜ)/dt (Thm 4) — the CTMC analogue of I-MMSE.
  • Oracle Distance: NELBO − H(q₀) = reconstruction mismatch + path KL(P★‖Pθ).

Sequences & instantiations

§6–7 · Thm 6–7

  • Token-factorizing CTMCs split the reverse rate into single-token jumps → the coordinate dictionary.
  • Instantiated on masked, uniform & GIDD; NELBO integrands, samplers, prior-KL boundary terms.
  • Masked: denoiser = cavity (Cor 4); uniform: denoiser coordinate diverges at init (Prop 6).
02

Results & Highlights

Verified on an exactly-solvable homogeneous bigram chain: V = 8 tokens, L = 8, H(q₀) = 10.00 nats (1.25 / token), window [0.02, 0.98], small DiT (8000 steps).

Universal NELBO floor shared by every noising process

1.25 nats/tok

Excess NELBO above the data-entropy floor decays to zero for all parameterizations and all processes (masked, uniform, GIDD). Total info destroyed over the schedule = 1.25/token for every λ.

Convert-or-pay for misread coordinates

+6.03

With correct conversion every coordinate hits 1.25; naively misreading penalizes every off-diagonal — worst under uniform (denoiser→score = 7.28), milder under GIDD (3.92), zero at masked.

Denoiser diverges, cavity stays finite, at init

L·log V

Uninformative denoiser head under uniform diffusion diverges as ((V−1)L/V)·log(1/β); the same head read as a cavity stays finite at exactly L·log V per-token — a calibration identity.

Reverse-rate projection confirmed empirically

match

Learned uniform-diffusion rates, averaged over many (zₜ,y,t), land on the oracle marginal rate — validating Theorem 5's "conditional mean = marginal rate" identification.

Sampling factorization error isolated

oracle

Sampling with the exact oracle denoiser zeroes reverse-rate error, leaving pure token-wise ancestral factorization error — it vanishes only as NFE grows; masked suffers most (no self-correction).

Cross-method ELBO comparability needs boundary terms

Table 5

Dropping reconstruction / terminal-prior boundary terms silently breaks comparability across noising processes; keeping them is the methodological correction the paper insists on.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Rigorous & self-contained: full derivations, explicit regularity/support conditions, two independent proofs of the main theorem, careful boundary conventions.
  • Answers the title crisply, resolving folklore about why some parameterizations "mysteriously misbehave."
  • Strong unifying power: recovers MDLM, D3PM, MD4, UDLM, GIDD, Duo, SEDD, RADD, TCSM from one projection principle.
  • Information-theoretic depth: oracle cost = −dI(Z₀;Zₜ)/dt; ties to Gaussian I-MMSE and the thermodynamic arrow of time.
  • Immediately usable: the log V calibration identity, the keep-boundary-terms rule, and a single practitioner rule.
  • Honest positioning vs concurrent [GJS⁺26] and precursors (Generator Matching, TCSM).

Limitations

  • Toy-model-only validation: all numbers on an exactly-solvable 8-token, length-8 bigram. No LM-scale training, no text benchmarks (LM1B, text8, OpenWebText).
  • Teacher-forced objective only: identity derived at zₜ ~ q_{t|0}, not self-generated rollouts — does not directly bound sampling drift / exposure bias.
  • Convert-or-pay shown at oracle laws, not under learning — proves parameterization correctness, not training benefit.
  • Fixed-support assumption (Asm 3) needed for the information-loss identity; may bind adaptive schedules.
  • No study of variance/conditioning of converted objectives; Prop 6 is an init pathology only.
  • Relies on a concurrent paper for the only scale-level support of the central practical claim.
04

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the cavity's convert-or-pay edge persist at language-model scale with learned heads — on LM1B / OpenWebText perplexity and MAUVE — or merely yield a correctly-specified but equally-performant objective?
  2. Can masking the denoiser head to the reverse support Sᵢ (§6.3) repair the Proposition 6 divergence without an explicit cavity reparameterization?
  3. Can the Oracle Distance be extended — even approximately — to self-generated rollout states, giving a principled bound on sampling drift rather than the "generalization gap" reinterpretation?
  4. Does the information-uniform clock τ(t) = H(Z₀|Zₜ)/H(q₀) cut ELBO estimator variance and beat variance-optimal importance sampling, across noising processes?
  5. Can a corrector / self-correcting sampler minimize the factorization term (Figure 6) while leaving the optimal reverse rate untouched — and how does its NFE–quality trade-off compare to τ-leaping / Tweedie?
  6. Does the denoiser / cavity / score distinction bite for continuous diffusion (flow matching vs denoiser-score matching), predicting an analogous parameterization pathology?
  7. How large is the silent bias in published discrete-diffusion ELBOs that drop boundary terms — and can these formulas retroactively correct the literature?
