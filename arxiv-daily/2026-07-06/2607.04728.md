> **一句话总结：** 本文提出选择性重要性采样（SIS），通过 token 级别的接受-拒绝检验把离线 rollout 中的 off-policy token 直接"转化"为 on-policy（接受则权重置 1），在不牺牲梯度信号的前提下修正策略梯度，仅带来约 1% 的墙上时间开销，在 Qwen3-8B/14B/30B-MoE 与 Llama-3.2 上作为 GRPO/DAPO/GSPO 的即插即用模块带来稳定的数学与 agent 基准增益（最高 +6.37 数学平均分）。

> **TL;DR:** Selective Importance Sampling (SIS) reframes off-policy RL post-training as a rejection-sampling problem: at each token it runs a top-K acceptance test, treating accepted tokens as on-policy (unit weight) while rejected tokens keep the standard IS correction. Plugged into GRPO/DAPO/GSPO on Qwen3-8B/14B/30B-A3B-MoE and Llama-3.2-3B, SIS adds only ~1% wall-clock overhead and lifts math benchmarks by up to +6.37 Avg (Qwen3-30B GRPO: 51.29 → 57.66) and agent benchmarks by up to +2.71 Avg, while surviving stale rollouts (N=16) and MoE train–inference routing mismatch that collapse vanilla DAPO.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 SIS（Selective Importance Sampling）：** 一种受拒绝采样启发的即插即用方法，将行为策略视为当前策略的提议分布，对每个 token 做接受-拒绝检验；被接受的 token 被证明严格服从目标策略分布，因此重要性权重直接置为 1，被拒绝的 token 保留标准 IS 校正——从根本上"转化"off-policy token 而非像裁剪那样压制梯度信号。
- **高效实现（Top-K 包络近似）：** 用 top-K 子集近似逐 token 的最大常数 $M_t$，避免遍历整个词表；复用旧策略生成 logits 与当前策略训练 logits，无需额外前向传播，每步仅增加约 1% 的墙上时间开销，且对 K 几乎不敏感。
- **理论保证：** 证明了 SIS 严格减小序列级与 token 级 off-policy 梯度估计之间的近似误差上界——总对数重要性偏离 $D=\sum|\log w_t|$ 被缩减为 $D_{SIS}=\sum(1-z_t)|\log w_t|\le D$，从而收紧误差界 $E\le|A|(e^{D_{SIS}}-1)\sum\|s_t\|$。
- **算法无关性与可组合性：** SIS 仅修改 token 级校正比 $w_t\to\hat w_t$，可一行替换接入 GRPO/DAPO/GSPO 等；与 MoE 路由修复方法 R3 正交，组合使用比单独任一种更稳定。
- **广泛实验验证：** 跨 dense（Qwen3-8B/14B、Llama-3.2-3B）与 MoE（Qwen3-30B-A3B）架构、三类 RL 算法、数学与 agent 共 10 个基准，并设计三类压力测试（rollout 复用 N=4/8/16、MoE 路由失配、完全去除裁剪）证明鲁棒性。

## Key Contributions

- **SIS (Selective Importance Sampling):** A plug-in method that converts off-policy tokens to on-policy via a token-level acceptance–rejection test. Accepted tokens are provably distributed as the target policy $\pi_\theta$ (Proposition 1) and receive unit weight; rejected tokens retain the standard IS ratio. Unlike clipping, this fixes the off-policy mismatch at its root rather than suppressing gradient signal from highly off-policy tokens.
- **Top-K envelope approximation:** Replaces the vocabulary-wide maximization of $M_t$ with a top-K restriction, exploiting LLM probability concentration. Adds only ~1% wall-clock overhead and is K-invariant in practice (K=10 used throughout), requiring no extra model forward pass.
- **Theoretical justification:** Proves SIS strictly tightens the off-policy approximation-error bound between token-level and sequence-level gradient estimators (Theorem 1 + Proposition 3), reducing the total log-importance deviation from $D$ to $D_{SIS}\le D$.
- **Algorithm-agnostic composability:** Only modifies the importance ratio $w_t\to\hat w_t$; plugs into GRPO, DAPO, GSPO via one-line substitution. The accept/reject split gives it a guaranteed floor (trivial $g(w)=w$ already beats most tricks) and a high ceiling (pairing with DAPO attains the best accuracy). Orthogonal to MoE-routing fix R3.
- **Extensive empirical validation:** Consistent gains across dense and MoE backbones, three RL algorithms, and 10 math/agent benchmarks, plus three stress tests (rollout staleness up to N=16, MoE train–inference routing mismatch, clipping-free training).

## Methodology

SIS is built on three components:

1. **Token-level rejection sampling (§3.1).** For each position $t$, the behavior policy $\pi_{\theta_{old}}$ is treated as a proposal distribution to the current policy $\pi_\theta$. The maximal ratio constant $M_t=\max_{v\in V}\pi_\theta(v|x,y_{<t})/\pi_{\theta_{old}}(v|x,y_{<t})$ defines an acceptance indicator $z_t\sim\text{Bernoulli}(w_t(\theta)/M_t)$. By standard rejection-sampling theory (Proposition 1), the conditional distribution of $y_t$ given $z_t=1$ equals exactly $\pi_\theta(\cdot|x,y_{<t})$—i.e., accepted tokens are genuinely on-policy and need no correction. The modified ratio is $\hat w_t(\theta)=1$ if accepted, else $w_t(\theta)$.

2. **Top-K envelope approximation (§3.2).** Computing $M_t$ over the full vocabulary is prohibitive. SIS restricts the maximization to the top-K set $V_K=\text{TopK}(\pi_{\theta_{old}}, K)$. Tokens outside $V_K$ are never accepted (remain off-policy); accepted tokens inside $V_K$ follow a conditional distribution within total-variation distance $\xi_K$ of $\pi_\theta$, where $\xi_K$ is the target's residual mass outside $V_K$ (Proposition 2). Because LLM probability mass concentrates heavily, $\xi_K$ stays small even for K=5 (Figure 2B). The acceptance test reuses old-policy logits from generation and current-policy logits from training—no extra forward pass.

3. **Why it works (§3.3).** The gap between the true sequence-level gradient $g_{seq}$ and the token-level surrogate $g_{tok}$ is bounded by $E\le|A(x,y)|(e^D-1)\sum_t\|s_t\|$, where $D=\sum_t|\log w_t(\theta)|$ (Theorem 1). SIS replaces each accepted token's $\log w_t$ with 0, yielding $D_{SIS}=\sum_t(1-z_t)|\log w_t|\le D$, which strictly tightens the error bound (Proposition 3). Figure 2C empirically confirms SIS maintains lower log-importance deviation than vanilla GRPO throughout training.

SIS plugs into any policy-gradient objective by substituting $w_{i,t}(\theta)\to\hat w_{i,t}(\theta)$. Concrete instantiations for GRPO, DAPO, and GSPO are given (Eqs. 15, 39, 41); for sequence-level GSPO, accepted tokens contribute zero log-ratio to the sequence ratio.

## Results & Highlights

- **Math benchmarks (Qwen3-30B-A3B-MoE):** GRPO+SIS reaches **57.66 Avg** on MATH500/AMC23/AIME24/AIME25 vs. **51.29** for vanilla GRPO (+6.37 absolute); DAPO+SIS reaches **58.12 Avg** vs. **53.34** for vanilla DAPO (+4.76). On the hardest AIME24, GRPO+SIS lifts accuracy from 25.52 → 35.94 (+10.42 points).
- **Trick-free comparison (Qwen3-8B-Base, Table 2):** SIS(DAPO) achieves **55.14 Avg**, a **+12.4% relative gain over GRPO (49.05)** and beats the strongest clipping-based competitor CISPO (54.44) on math; even trivial SIS with g(w)=w (no clipping trick on rejected tokens) already reaches 51.81 Avg (+5.63%).
- **Wall-clock overhead:** On Qwen3-8B-Base (8×A800), SIS adds **5.2s per step vs. a 465–499s step time (~1.1%)** and is K-invariant (5.20s/5.21s/5.06s for K=10/50/100), since the dominant cost is ratio reconstruction scaling O(B·L) not K.
- **Cross-family generalization (Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct, Table 9):** DAPO+SIS reaches **35.50 Avg** vs. **31.03** for vanilla DAPO (+4.47 absolute, +14.4% relative), with gains larger in relative terms than on Qwen3-8B, attributed to the smaller model's wider policy–rollout gap.
- **MoE stability (Figure 4):** On Qwen3-30B-A3B with DAPO, vanilla DAPO collapses—accuracy peaks then degrades, entropy drops to near-zero, gradient norms spike by two orders of magnitude. SIS alone and R3 alone each mitigate part of the instability; combining SIS+R3 yields the strongest accuracy and most stable optimization, showing the two address complementary off-policy sources.
- **Stale-rollout robustness (Figure 3, Table 8):** Varying mini-batch updates per rollout N∈{4,8,16}, every SIS variant stays consistently above its baseline at every staleness level; the token-level accept rate stays in the 0.80–0.95 range even at N=16.
- **Clipping-free training (Table 7):** With clipping fully disabled, GRPO+SIS still beats clip-based GRPO on both math (51.81 vs. 49.05 Avg) and agent (52.0 vs. 48.8 Bamboogle) tasks, confirming SIS is itself a stabilization mechanism.
- **Top-K sensitivity (Table 3):** Sweeping K∈{10,50,100} on Qwen3-8B, average accuracy varies by at most ~1 point; K=10 used throughout.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High and well-motivated. Reframing off-policy correction as acceptance–rejection sampling that *converts* tokens to on-policy (rather than clipping/suppressing them) is a genuinely fresh angle versus the dominant clipping-recipe family (GRPO/DAPO/CISPO/soft-clip variants). The insight that accepted tokens need no correction because they provably follow $\pi_\theta$ is clean and the IS-lens unification of competitors (Appendix E) is a useful contribution.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Theorem 1 and Proposition 3 give a non-trivial bound showing SIS strictly reduces the token-vs-sequence approximation error; the proofs are provided. The paired experimental protocol (identical hyperparameters, only $w_t\to\hat w_t$ changed) is the right design to isolate SIS's effect.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code stack (FSDP+vLLM), corpora (DAPO-Math-17K, NQ+HotpotQA), hyperparameters, and prompt templates are all specified. The method is a small modification to the importance ratio, lowering the implementation barrier. One caveat: no explicit code/release link is given in the text.
- **Significance:** Practically relevant—off-policy drift from rollout reuse and MoE train–inference routing mismatch is a real, widely-encountered pain point in production RL post-training, and a ~1%-overhead plug-in that improves both accuracy and stability is valuable. The MoE + R3 combination result (Figure 4) directly addresses a known silent-failure mode (DAPO entropy collapse / gradient spikes).
- **Red flags / weaknesses:**
  - **No dedicated "Limitations" section.** The paper omits explicit discussion of failure modes (e.g., how SIS behaves when accept rate collapses, or on tasks with extreme distributional shift beyond agentic search).
  - **Baselines are somewhat narrow in the "trick" comparison (Table 2):** DPPO-TV and Clip-Cov appear weak (49.48 and 49.94 Avg vs. GRPO 49.05), which could reflect suboptimal tuning rather than fundamentally weaker ideas; the paper does not deeply investigate whether these were given their best shot.
  - **Top-K approximation introduces a controlled but nonzero bias ($\xi_K$).** The theory handles it (Proposition 2), but empirical $\xi_K$ values are only shown indirectly via Figure 2B coverage curves, not reported as a headline number, making the bias magnitude harder to gauge precisely.
  - **Single hyperparameter K, but evaluated mainly at K=10:** while robustness is shown, the interaction between K and vocabulary size / language is not explored (all models are Qwen3/Llama with shared tokenizers).
  - **Rewards are purely outcome-based rule rewards;** behavior under learned/noisy reward models—where advantage estimates are themselves biased—is untested, yet that is the realistic RLHF setting.

## Strengths

- Conceptually elegant: "convert off-policy tokens to on-policy" is a cleaner principle than "clip the ratio," and it preserves gradient signal rather than discarding it.
- Theoretically grounded with complete proofs tying the mechanism to a tightened approximation-error bound.
- Genuinely plug-and-play with negligible overhead (~1% wall-clock), and algorithm-agnostic across token-level (GRPO/DAPO) and sequence-level (GSPO) objectives.
- Strong, broad empirical coverage: dense + MoE backbones, two model families (Qwen3, Llama3.2), math + agentic-search domains, 10 benchmarks, three stress tests.
- The MoE routing-mismatch experiment (Figure 4) and clipping-free training result (Table 7) are particularly compelling demonstrations that SIS is a real stabilization mechanism, not just an accuracy booster.

## Limitations

- No explicit limitations section; failure cases (e.g., near-zero accept rate under extreme drift) are not characterized.
- Only rule-based, outcome rewards are used; the noisy-reward / learned-reward-model regime common in real RLHF is untested.
- The top-K approximation bias $\xi_K$ is bounded theoretically but not reported as an explicit empirical number in the main text.
- Competing stabilization tricks (DPPO-TV, Clip-Cov) look weak in Table 2, raising a fair-tuning concern rather than a strictly controlled comparison.
- Accept rate analysis is limited to math and agentic search; behavior on code, long-horizon tool-use, or multilingual tasks is not covered.

## Relevance

这篇论文与"LLM Training Stability & Observability"以及"ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations"高度相关：它从重要性采样的数学根因出发，量化并收紧了 off-policy 梯度估计的近似误差界（$D_{SIS}\le D$），并直接修复 rollout 复用与 MoE 路由失配导致的训练崩溃（熵塌缩、梯度爆炸）这一类静默可靠性问题。对关注 RL 训练可观测性与稳定性的研究路线，它提供了一个低开销、可诊断（接受率可作为 off-policy 程度的探针）的即插即用工具。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does SIS behave under extreme distributional shift where the accept rate approaches zero (e.g., aggressive async RL with very stale rollouts)? Is there a graceful degradation or a cliff?
- Can the per-token accept indicator $z_t$ itself serve as a built-in observability signal for quantifying off-policy-ness / SDC-like drift during training, beyond its use as a correction?
- What happens when the reward model is noisy or biased—does SIS amplify advantage-estimate errors for accepted tokens since they receive full unit weight?
- Could the top-K approximation be replaced by an adaptive K chosen per token based on the residual mass $\xi_K$, to keep bias below a fixed budget without manual tuning?
- Does the accept/reject mechanism interact with precision/quantization errors in low-precision training, where $\pi_\theta$ and $\pi_{\theta_{old}}$ logits already carry rounding noise?
- How does SIS transfer to preference-based objectives (DPO-family) where the "importance ratio" is implicit, and to long-horizon agent tasks with much larger sequence lengths than tested here?
