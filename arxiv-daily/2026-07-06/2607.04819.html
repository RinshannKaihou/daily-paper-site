Critical edge

Softmax is both the dominant error source and the operation whose real FHE cost (comparison circuits) is excluded from the NFE metric — a circular vulnerability where the score understates the very operation that matters most.

01 Methodology

Can layer-parallelism — built for GPU wall-clock — instead cut encrypted nonlinear depth?

Under CKKS-style FHE every nonlinear op (softmax exp, RMSNorm rsqrt, GELU/SiLU) becomes a polynomial, and these approximations compound across L layers, burning the modulus budget and forcing bootstrapping (~600× a plaintext multiply; 50–86% of latency).

SNLP reframes the inter-layer graph. Solve the first S layers as a prefix, then iterate the remaining N suffix layers over K solver steps — all suffix blocks evaluated in parallel, with a structured Newton correction propagating across depth.

NFE = (L − N) + K   vs.   sequential NFE = L
  • IDN (Identity Newton): A = I → purely additive, zero FHE multiplicative depth.
  • HCN (HC Newton): A = Hres_attn · Hres_mlp, a small S×S linear map.
  • Cost model: symbolic CKKS — block depth ≈ 4d+7, 15 usable levels per bootstrap — tracks bootstraps within 0.99–1.02×.

Simulation, not real CKKS. Each nonlinear op is swapped for a degree-d Chebyshev approximation inside the PyTorch forward pass; sequential and SNLP paths share identical approximation code, so any output gap is purely structural (sanity check: max abs diff = 0.0 when bypassed). 8 Nanochat models (L=32) across 4 families, trained on ClimbMix.

03 Strengths & limitations

Two reads of the same work, side by side.

Strengths

  • Clean problem framing: FHE's sequential nonlinear depth is a genuine, under-appreciated bottleneck — distinct from per-operator quality.
  • NFE is intuitive and internally well-validated as a bootstrap proxy (0.99–1.02×).
  • Comprehensive ablations (per-op, per-degree, per-bit, per-arch, per-layer) pin down where the gain comes from.
  • Honestly scoped — explicit limitations section naming the simulation caveat and omitted comparison-circuit cost.
  • Transferable insight: mHC / linear residual mixing is FHE-friendly — independent of SNLP itself.

Limitations

  • Simulation, not deployment: no real CKKS run; absolute numbers are projections. Real comparison circuits & reciprocal approximation are excluded from NFE.
  • Scale: only 0.5B–3B; production-LLM generalization untested.
  • Softmax-bound: gain is capped by softmax approx. quality; may shift with gated/linear attention.
  • Needs SNLP-aware training: the headline n24-K4 result requires IDN-regularized pretraining; vanilla baselines get <1pp gain.
  • No end-to-end latency benchmark vs. THE-X / Iron / BOLT / THOR.

04 Questions for further exploration

Where the simulated advantage must survive contact with reality.

  1. Does the SNLP advantage hold under a real CKKS implementation (OpenFHE / SEAL), where softmax max-subtraction and RMSNorm reciprocal — both excluded from NFE — finally enter the cost?
  2. How does SNLP interact with softmax-replacing architectures (gated, linear/SSM attention) that move the dominant error source?
  3. Does the Pareto frontier survive at production scale (>7B, 64+ layers), or does amplification grow with depth and close the useful NFE window?
  4. Is the "FHE-optimal ≠ wall-clock-optimal" point robust to FHE hardware accelerators (HEAP-style bootstrap units) that change the depth/iteration cost ratio?
  5. Could SNLP-aware training be made post-hoc / adapter-based so existing pretrained models reach the low-NFE regime without full retraining?
