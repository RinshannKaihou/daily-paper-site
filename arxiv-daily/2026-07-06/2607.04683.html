Key Finding

Different pre-decoding features diagnose different VLM failure stages — visual tokens expose perceptual gaps, prompt-boundary hidden states expose factual ones.

A component-wise attribution tree turns scalar uncertainty into a diagnosis of what the model is uncertain about — and a routing signal for targeted repair.

Key Metric
visual-evidence attribution PR-AUC97.0/ near-perfect

VIS feature (final image-token hidden state) reaches 97.0 on image-side attribution and 86.7 on entity recognition — pre-decoding, far above the next-best feature at 67.6.

Why This Matters

Evidence of “failure observability” in VLM internal representations — bridging interpretability and reliability math, with probe-guided self-diagnosis mirroring automated-repair agents.

⚑ Critical Edge

The 97.0 VIS result is measured on synthetically corrupted images — not naturally degraded inputs — so near-perfect attribution likely won’t survive wild deployment.

01

Methodology

Uncertainty as a diagnostic problem — uncertain about what — not a scalar score.

Decomposition & probes

  • Reframes binary error E via the law of total probability into four mutually-exclusive latent causes: P(E=1|x) = Σ P(Z_i=1|x)
  • Z_d insufficient visual evidence · Z_u unknown entity · Z_f unknown fact · Z_r failed recall
  • Each tree node is a local binary probe, trained only on examples passing the preceding checks
  • Labels built at scale: yes/no recognition probes (3 distractors), adaptive image corruption to the recognition-flip point, entity-explicit rewrites (Cohen et al. 2025)
  • Pre-generation features from one forward pass: VIS · EOP · LAST8 · ATTN (+layer offsets −1/−4/−8/−12)
  • Four open-weight VLMs: Gemma-3-12B-it, Llama-3.2-11B-Vision, Qwen2.5-VL-7B, Qwen3-VL-8B; data PopVQA (4,863) + iNaturalist (2,116)

02

Results & Highlights

The central empirical claim holds across four model families.

Diagnosis signal strength

  • VIS → 97.0 visual-evidence attribution & 86.7 entity recognition — the visual stream is the most informative place to probe for perceptual failure
  • LAST8 → 62.7 post-recognition answer success (VIS only 36.0) & 53.5 factual access — competitive with ORACLE-UQ 52.4, but available pre-decoding
  • UNKNOWN FACT dominates the error mix: 50.7–63.8% of iNaturalist, 36.8–37.7% of PopVQA
  • UQ baselines near-random on component tasks — beating random PR-AUC by only ~2–5 pts; scalar uncertainty can’t recover failure-source structure

Structure & downstream gains

  • Tree beats flat: composed probes reach 49.2 macro PR-AUC vs. 41.2 for direct 5-label classification (gap 5.1–11.4 pts) — one bottleneck conflates perceptual & factual distinctions
  • Cross-dataset transfer is decision-dependent: visual-evidence transfers at 84.9, recognition 74.7; answer-success 51.9 & factual-access 50.8 drop more (domain-sensitive)
  • Probe-guided mitigation: +30.8–39.3 accuracy points across all model–dataset pairs — iNaturalist <13% → 39.6–46.6%; PopVQA 22–32% → 55.1–64.3%
  • UNKNOWN FACT is the single largest contributor to gain; non-factual branches still drive ~40–60% of total lift
03

Strengths & Limitations

A clean reframing — with a proof-of-concept router and a synthetic-dependant headline number.

Strengths

  • Reframes uncertainty as a diagnostic, component-wise problem instead of a scalar score
  • Central finding — visual tokens for perception, hidden states for facts — consistent across four model families
  • Comprehensive baselines: full UQ sweep (Table 11) + post-generation alternatives make the pre-generation advantage credible
  • Demonstrates end-to-end utility via mitigation, not just probing accuracy
  • Honest limitations: operational labels characterised as non-causal, mitigation flagged as proof-of-concept

Limitations

  • Visual-evidence failures are synthetically induced — near-perfect attribution may not transfer to occlusion, cropping, or wild artifacts
  • Attribution labels are operational proxies tied to protocol & target model; not causal ground truth, no human validation reported
  • Mitigation relies on GPT-5 — costly, closed, version-unstable; a deployable router needs cheaper specialised tools
  • iNaturalist base accuracies 4.8–12.1% are very low; post-mitigation ceiling ≤46.6% leaves most questions unanswered
  • Mutual-exclusivity is a modeling convenience; the UNKNOWN FACT intervention conflates routing quality with GPT-5 strength
04

Questions for Further Exploration

Q1 · Distribution shiftDo the probes survive naturally occurring visual failures (occlusion, unusual viewpoints, low light) — does VIS’s 97.0 hold off the synthetic distribution?
Q2 · Cross-modelCan probes trained on one VLM transfer to a different target VLM, or must labeling and training repeat per model?
Q3 · Cheaper toolsDoes replacing GPT-5 with specialised tools (retriever, captioner, super-res) preserve the +30-point gains?
Q4 · RAG interactionHow does the tree behave with retrieval-augmented VLMs — does Z_f shrink, and does LAST8 vs. VIS signal strength shift?
Q5 · Selective abstentionCan the pre-decoding signal drive coverage–accuracy abstention that beats scalar UQ beyond binary routing?
Q6 · Mutual exclusivityHow often do real errors involve simultaneous visual & factual deficits — would a multi-label soft attribution improve routing?
Q7 · DepthDo the layer offsets (−1/−4/−8/−12) generalize to much deeper or shallower VLM architectures?
Q8 · Causal labelsCan human validation of the labeling protocol establish the operational labels as closer to causal ground truth?
