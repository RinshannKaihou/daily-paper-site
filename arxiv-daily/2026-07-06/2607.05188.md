> **一句话总结：** 作者通过在 22,714 条真实编码 agent 轨迹（Qwen3.6-35B-A3B 与 Laguna-XS.2，覆盖 SWE-Bench-Verified / Pro 共 1231 个任务）的残差流上训练 logistic 回归探针，发现程序的四类属性（可解析、完全正确、部分正确、是否引入回归）可被线性解码（最高 AUC 0.83），且探针能在编辑实际发生前约 25 步预测其结果，首次揭示了编码 agent 的"潜在编程视野"。

> **TL;DR:** Linear probes trained on the residual stream of coding agents (Qwen3.6-35B-A3B and Laguna-XS.2, driven by mini-swe-agent) decode four program properties — well-formedness, full correctness, partial correctness, and regression — on SWE-Bench-Verified and SWE-Bench-Pro, reaching AUC up to 0.83 (vs. 0.50 shuffled control) for full correctness; the same probes also predict the outcome of a future edit up to ~25 steps before it is materialized, demonstrating a "latent programming horizon," and transfer across benchmarks without retraining.

## 核心贡献

- **首次定义并实证了"潜在程序表示"与"潜在程序空间"**：提出在多步编码 agent 的残差流（residual stream）中，模型对正在编辑的程序（而非仅表面 token）维护了一个线性可解码的内部表示，并随每次 edit/test 动态更新轨迹。
- **解码四类"硬"程序属性**：用 logistic 回归探针在真实代码库（非合成片段、非单步生成）上解码 WELL-FORMEDNESS、FULL CORRECTNESS、PARTIAL CORRECTNESS、REGRESSION，其中 FULL/PARTIAL CORRECTNESS 最高 AUC 达 0.83–0.84。
- **首次报告"潜在编程视野"（latent programming horizon）**：探针在 edit 落地前可预测其结果，预测能力在约前 25 步仍显著高于随机（AUC ~0.55–0.65），是编码 agent 中 learned look-ahead 的首个证据。
- **跨基准迁移性与层定位规律**：探针不经再训练即可在 SWE-Bench-Verified ↔ Pro 之间迁移（FULL/PARTIAL CORRECTNESS 仅下降 0.04–0.09 AUC）；程序信号集中在中间层，呈倒 U 形分布。
- **大规模真实数据与可复现协议**：22,714 条轨迹、22.4M 个隐藏态向量、中位 52 步/2 次编辑，并以 shuffled-label 控制排除探针容量伪影。

## Key Contributions

- **First evidence of latent program representations in coding agents.** Formally defines a "latent program representation" living in the residual stream and a "latent program space" manifold that the hidden state traverses as the agent edits code, distinct from single-step code-correctness probing prior work.
- **Decoding four hard program properties on real codebases.** Logistic-regression probes recover WELL-FORMEDNESS, FULL CORRECTNESS, PARTIAL CORRECTNESS, and REGRESSION from multi-step trajectories (median 52 steps) on real repositories — not synthetic snippets or single-shot generation.
- **Discovery of a long-term "latent programming horizon."** Probes trained to predict a program property k steps ahead remain above chance out to k ≈ 25 steps (AUC ~0.55–0.65), the first look-ahead result for coding agents, analogous to learned look-ahead in chess/Sokoban networks.
- **Cross-benchmark transfer without retraining.** Probes trained on SWE-Bench-Verified transfer to SWE-Bench-Pro (and vice versa) for the semantic properties with only a 0.04–0.09 AUC drop, supporting external validity.
- **Large-scale, controlled, reproducible protocol.** 22,714 trajectories over 1,231 tasks, 22.4M hidden-state vectors, with shuffled-label controls (collapsing to 0.50) and a systematic layer-wise analysis showing an inverted-U concentration in middle layers.

## Methodology

The authors study two open-weight models — **Qwen3.6-35B-A3B** and **Laguna-XS.2** (both d=2048) — running the **mini-swe-agent v2.2.8** harness on **SWE-Bench-Verified** (500 tasks) and **SWE-Bench-Pro** (731 tasks), generating up to n=10 trajectories per task. For each trajectory they record the full program version after every codebase-modifying tool call and dump the residual stream every 5 tokens at layers {1, 11, 21, 31, 40}.

They define four binary **program properties** computed symbolically per edit event: ▲ WELL-FORMEDNESS (parses/compiles), • FULL CORRECTNESS (passes the benchmark test oracle), ■ PARTIAL CORRECTNESS (strictly fewer failing tests than at task start), and ♦ REGRESSION (any initially-passing test now fails). Labels propagate from an edit forward until the next edit.

For probing, they train per-layer **logistic-regression** classifiers (capacity-limited, per Hewitt & Liang 2019 best practice), split by task identifier to prevent leakage, select hyperparameters via 20-trial random search, and include a **shuffled-label control**. The **programming horizon** is operationalized as a separate probe predicting the property label k steps ahead (y^ϕ_{σ(t)+k}) for k swept 0→50, with the final 50 steps excluded to keep the token population constant across horizons. Cross-dataset transfer is evaluated by applying a probe trained on one benchmark directly to the other.

## Results & Highlights

- **Full correctness decodable at AUC up to 0.83.** On SWE-Bench-Pro, the Qwen3.6-35B-A3B FULL CORRECTNESS probe reaches AUC **0.832** (layer 31), versus **0.50** for the shuffled-label control — a +0.332 gap over the random baseline (Table 5 / Figure 3).
- **Partial correctness reaches AUC 0.841.** Qwen3.6-35B-A3B PARTIAL CORRECTNESS on SWE-Bench-Pro hits **0.841** at layer 40; Laguna-XS.2 reaches **0.722**, so the larger model encodes program semantics ~0.10 AUC more strongly (model-comparison, Figure 3).
- **Latent programming horizon extends ~25 steps.** Lookahead probes for FULL CORRECTNESS on Laguna-XS.2 remain above chance out to roughly k=25 (AUC **~0.55** on Verified, **~0.65** on Pro), versus the 0.50 control; even at k=50 the plateau sits weakly above chance (~0.52 Verified, ~0.60 Pro) — first evidence of long-term look-ahead in coding agents (Figure 6).
- **Cross-benchmark transfer holds for semantic properties.** FULL and PARTIAL CORRECTNESS probes transfer between Verified and Pro with AUC retained at **0.63–0.78**, a drop of only **0.04–0.09** versus in-distribution (Table 6).
- **Shuffled controls collapse to 0.50.** Every probe retrained on permuted labels lands at AUC 0.500–0.507, confirming the signal resides in the representation, not probe capacity (Table 5).
- **Known data quirk quantified.** WELL-FORMEDNESS on SWE-Bench-Verified stays near chance (AUC <0.60) because Python trajectories are almost always compilable (positive label rate >0.92), whereas the multi-language SWE-Bench-Pro (positive rate 0.52–0.57) yields WELL-FORMEDNESS AUC up to **0.78**.

## Authors & Credibility

The paper is from **KTH Royal Institute of Technology** (Stockholm) by André Silva, Han Tu, and **Martin Monperrus** — a well-established software-engineering / automated-repair research group. Monperrus has a long track record in program repair and software reliability, and the methodology rigorously follows probing best practices (Hewitt & Liang controls, capacity-limited linear probes, task-level data splits, hyperparameter sweeps). The authors release code and the full 22.4M-vector trajectory dataset. The work is positioned precisely against the closest prior correctness-probing line (Ribeiro et al. 2025; Bui et al. 2025; Vu et al. 2025; Di Cicco 2026; Tahimic & Cheng 2025), correctly noting that all prior work concerns single-step/single-function generation, not multi-step agent trajectories on real repositories.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely novel framing. Prior code-correctness probing assumed a static, fully-in-context program in a single generation step; this is the first to show program representations persist and update across dozens of agentic steps on partially-observed real codebases, and the first to report a programming horizon (look-ahead) in this setting. The analogy to Othello/Sokoban/chess world-model work is apt and well-motivated.
- **Rigor:** Strong controls (shuffled-label baseline collapsing to 0.50, task-level splits, Brier/AUC cross-checks, 20-trial hyperparameter sweeps, layer-wise analysis). The inverted-U layer result replicates across both models and both benchmarks.
- **Reproducibility:** High. Open-weight models, standard harness (mini-swe-agent), public benchmarks, released code, released 22.4M-vector dataset, and fully specified hyperparameters (Tables 2–4).
- **Significance:** Moderate-to-high for mechanistic interpretability of agents and SDC/observability — it suggests one can *monitor* an agent's belief about program correctness from inside the latent stream without re-running tests. But the practical "horizon" magnitude is modest: at k=25 the AUC (~0.55) is barely above 0.50, so the look-ahead is real but weak for any actionable early-warning use.
- **Red flags / weak points:**
  - The **WELL-FORMEDNESS probe is essentially broken on SWE-Bench-Verified** (AUC <0.60, near chance) due to label imbalance (>92% positive) — one of four headline properties is non-informative on half the experiments, and the paper's own Brier score for it is misleadingly low for the same reason.
  - **Decodability is explicitly not causality** (the authors' own caveat): no steering/intervention experiment shows the agent *uses* these representations to decide edits. The "horizon" could partly reflect correlated trajectory regularities rather than genuine planning.
  - **Single agent scaffold (mini-swe-agent) and only two models from a similar family** — generalization to OpenHands/CodeAct or frontier closed models is asserted but not demonstrated.
  - **No explicit comparison to a trivial baseline** for the lookahead (e.g., a constant/last-seen-label predictor), so it is unclear how much of the k>0 signal is just label autocorrelation across consecutive edits in a trajectory with median only 2 edits.
  - Median trajectory has only **2 edits** (mean 3.5), which is short relative to the 50-step horizon claim; the k≤50 analysis filters to trajectories ≥50 *steps*, which is a different (and longer-edit) sub-population.

## Strengths

- First-of-kind result on latent program representations and programming horizon in real multi-step coding agents.
- Methodologically clean: linear (capacity-limited) probes, shuffled controls, task-level splits, layer-wise and transfer analyses.
- Large, realistic dataset (22,714 trajectories, 1,231 real GitHub tasks, 22.4M hidden-state vectors) and full release of code + data.
- Cross-model and cross-benchmark consistency strengthens external validity of the core claims.

## Limitations

- Correlational, not causal — no intervention/steering experiment confirms the agent actually relies on the decoded representation.
- WELL-FORMEDNESS is non-informative on Python-heavy SWE-Bench-Verified due to severe label imbalance.
- Scope is narrow: one harness, two related open-weight models, two benchmarks; frontier models and other scaffolds untested.
- The "25-step horizon" sits only ~0.02–0.15 AUC above chance at long range, so its practical/decision-making value is uncertain.
- No trivial-predictor baseline for lookahead makes it hard to factor out label autocorrelation.

## Relevance

该工作与"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"和"LLM Inference Evaluation"高度相关：它表明编码 agent 的内部隐藏态线性编码了程序正确性甚至未来 edit 的结果，为从 latent stream 做运行时监控与早失败检测提供了机制可解释性基础；对"LLM Training Stability & Observability"和"Self-Evolving Agents"也有间接价值，因为它揭示了 agent 是否在"规划"而非盲目试错，可作为 agent 自我评估与引导的潜在信号源。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does **causal steering** along the FULL CORRECTNESS probe direction actually change the agent's subsequent edits (i.e., make it write more correct code), or is the representation epiphenomenal?
- How much of the lookahead signal survives after subtracting a trivial **last-label / majority-class predictor** — is the "horizon" genuine planning or label autocorrelation?
- Do these latent representations appear under **different scaffolds** (OpenHands, CodeAct, AutoCodeRover) and in **frontier closed models**, or are they specific to mini-swe-agent with these two models?
- Can the probes be used at **inference time as an online observability/SDC signal** to abort or re-prompt trajectories predicted to introduce regressions, and what precision/recall would that achieve?
- Is the inverted-U layer pattern stable across model scale, and does the "best" mid-layer shift predictably with depth?
- How does the horizon length scale with task difficulty, trajectory length, or model size — and can it be *lengthened* via training (e.g., RL with execution feedback)?
