MMethodology

1 · FactorizePolicy πθ(a,z|o) with trace z=(scene, justification, meta-action) along a Markov DAG.
2 · DefineMechanistic faithfulness via do(·) intervention — impractical to optimize.
3 · RelaxProve consistency is necessary (Prop.1); relax to 5 pairwise semantic-consistency edges.
4 · Learn criticFine-tune Qwen3-VL-4B → Pinocchio, 0.87 balanced acc.
5 · GRPO rewardFeed Σ log cϕ(e) as dense reward; +rADE on ~100k US scenes.
  • Trace structure: z1 scene description, z2 move justification, z3 longitudinal/lateral meta-action; action = relative waypoints, quality = ADE.
  • 5 consistency edges C: (o,z1), (z1,z2), (z2,z3), (z3,a), (z2,a) — consistency F(τ) = product of binary R(u,v).
  • Objective (Eq.7): max E[−ADE + Σ λ log cϕ(e)] via policy gradient; λ hand-tuned on validation, dual ascent deferred.
  • Fair baselines (same SFT init): ADE, VLM-Judge (SoTA alignment), ADE-Reason, ADE-Swap.

  • Accepted cost: ADE rises ≈5% (4.324m vs 4.169m) — framed as "modest" faithfulness-vs-functionality trade.
  • Where gains concentrate: E4 (meta-action→waypoint) & E5 (justification→waypoint) — the action-adjacent edges. VLM-Judge beats Ours on E1/E2 grounding.
  • Human validation: Gemini 3.1 Pro vs 4 annotators — 87–95% agreement, Cohen's κ 0.385–0.720 (fair→strong); E2 κ=0.385 even human-vs-human.

＋Strengths

  • Clean conceptual axis: functional vs faithful distinction is immediately usable for the VLA interpretability community.
  • Genuinely tractable pipeline: impossible mechanistic target → necessary surrogate → learned critic → dense reward, with a proven link.
  • Strong fair baselines spanning functionality- and faithfulness-oriented supervision, all from the same SFT checkpoint.
  • Honest about limits: necessity-only surrogate, E2 difficulty, open-loop-only all explicitly stated.
  • Creative OOD probe: generative image-inpainting (Nano Banana Pro) to build a true causality test.

−Limitations

  • Necessary, not sufficient: a policy can emit fully consistent traces while bypassing them — method filters, never certifies.
  • Evaluator circularity: same Gemini labels critic training and grades the OOD benchmark.
  • Gains on action-adjacent edges only (E4/E5); grounding edges E1/E2 don't improve — VLM-Judge wins there.
  • Open-loop only: no closed-loop sim, the regime where driving policies actually fail.
  • Single domain & family: autonomous driving / Alpamayo only; manipulation & general robotics untested.
  • Hand-tuned λ; ~5% ADE regression; absolute OOD scores near-zero.

?Questions for further exploration

  • Could the necessity-only surrogate become sufficient if combined with inference-time interventional probes (do(z3) causal tracing) — a hybrid critic + intervention that certifies, not just filters?
  • Would a closed-loop simulator (CARLA, NuPlan) show the ~5% open-loop ADE regression compounding into large failures — or reveal faithfulness actually helps in closed loop?
  • E4/E5 dominate the gains — is the reward implicitly double-counting ADE-like signal? Would a critic trained only on E1/E2 transfer better to true grounding?
  • If a different frontier VLM or humans graded Dfaith and the OOD set, do the 4pp / 18pp / 1.6× claims survive the circularity?
  • Can λ be replaced by proper dual ascent to satisfy the consistency constraints (Eq.5) instead of a heuristic scalarization?
  • Does faithful reasoning transfer to manipulation / locomotion, or is the Markov-DAG structure too specific to the driving meta-action decomposition?
  • Could low-precision / SDC training silently break the consistency edges the critic rewards — making faithfulness a silent-error probe during training?
