> **一句话总结：** 作者在三个 7–8B 指令模型（Llama-3.1-8B / Olmo-3-7B / Mistral-7B）的冻结残差流上训练最小容量线性探针，发现 LLM 在生成任何 token 之前就已线性编码"剩余输出长度"——在 GSM8K 上 Llama 的 Remaining Count Probe 把逐 token MAE 从 70.15（中位数基线）降到 36.59，且自然语言训练的探针方向可跨数据集（甚至外推到合成集）迁移，而合成集训练的方向则不能反向迁移，提示模型内部存在类"计划"的长度表征。

> **TL;DR:** Training minimal-capacity linear probes on frozen residual streams of Llama-3.1-8B, Olmo-3-7B, and Mistral-7B across seven completion-style datasets (GSM8K, MATH, MMLU-Pro, OpenThoughts-1k, TriviaQA, plus synthetic Count/Countdown), the authors show total response length is linearly decodable from the prompt's last hidden state alone — on Llama/GSM8K the per-token MAE drops from a 70.15 median baseline to 36.59 — that natural-language-trained probe directions transfer OOD (even into synthetic sets) while synthetic→natural transfer fails, and that the probe's estimate shifts upward at retraction tokens ("Wait...") in a way no position-only baseline can reproduce.

## 核心贡献

- **定义了一组探针家族来分层解构残差流的信息贡献**：Remaining Count Probe（在生成过程中每个位置读取 h_t 回归剩余 token 数）、常数中位数统计基线（L1 损失下的最优常数预测器）、以及只在 prompt 末位置训练并用精确递减广播的 Completion Length Probe。三者共用一次 LM 前向传播与同一 dataloader，因此性能差异完全归因于探针目标而非采样方差。
- **证明"总响应长度"在输出第一个 token 之前即可从 prompt 最后一个隐藏状态线性解码**：在所有（模型, 数据集）单元上 Completion Length Probe 都击败常数中位数基线；在合成 Countdown 上 Llama 的 prompt 端 MAE 降至 5.27（基线 150.18），自然语言集上 prompt 端 MAE 约为基线的一半到四分之三。
- **系统性的跨数据集泛化矩阵揭示了非对称迁移**：自然语言集（尤其 OpenThoughts-1k、MMLU-Pro）训练的探针几乎可外推到所有其他目标（包括从未见过的合成 Count/Countdown），而合成集训练的探针无法迁移到自然语言。作者将其解释为正则化故事——自然语言迫使探针落在一个更通用的"长度跟踪方向"上。
- **定性的动态重估现象**：由于 Remaining Count Probe 依据 h_t 而非 t 预测，其预测不必单调；在高 MAE 完成样本中，模型在"Wait/let me try again"等回撤 token 处探针预测向上跳变（如从 71 跳到 277），这是任何位置单调基线都无法产生的方向性行为。
- **方法学上明确区分"近似估计剩余生成长度"与"transformer 精确计数不可能定理"**：作者反复强调其结果是 ~30 token MAE 的低精度信号，与 Yehudai et al. 2026 的精确计数 d≥m 相变等结果正交、互不冲突。

## Key Contributions

- **A probe family that stratifies the residual stream's contribution** into three predictors: a Remaining Count Probe (regresses on h_t at every completion position, non-monotonic by construction), a constant-median statistical baseline (the L1-optimal position-independent predictor), and a Completion Length Probe trained only at the prompt's last position then broadcast as an exact countdown — all sharing one frozen LM forward pass so differences are attributable to target, not sampling noise.
- **Prompt-end decodability of total length.** The Completion Length Probe beats the constant-median baseline in every (model, dataset) cell; on synthetic Countdown, Llama's prompt-end MAE reaches 5.27 tokens vs. a 150.18 baseline, and on natural-language sets it lands at roughly 0.5–0.9× the baseline.
- **A full cross-dataset generalization matrix** for every (model, source, target) triple, closing a gap in the probe-OOD literature for continuous regression targets on residual streams. The matrix is asymmetric: natural-language-trained probes transfer broadly (even back into synthetic sets), synthetic-trained probes do not.
- **A qualitative dynamic re-estimation observation**: on curated high-MAE completions, the probe's per-position estimate jumps upward at retraction tokens ("Wait...", "let's check"), a directional update no monotonic-in-t baseline can reproduce — interpreted (cautiously) as evidence of a plan-like representation that updates.
- **A clean conceptual demarcation** between approximate estimation of remaining generation length (their ~30-token MAE signal) and the exact in-context counting impossibility theorems for transformers (Yehudai et al. 2026, Barbero et al. 2024), which the authors argue are complementary, not conflicting.

## Methodology

The study probes frozen 7–8B instruction-tuned models — Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Olmo-3-7B-Instruct, and Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3 — across seven completion-style datasets: two synthetic controlled-length sets (Count: "Count from 0 to {n}"; Countdown: "Count down from {n} to 0", with n ∈ [0,300], so T is deterministically fixed by the prompt) and five natural-language sets (GSM8K, MATH, MMLU-Pro, OpenThoughts-1k, TriviaQA) spanning reasoning, retrieval, and long-form writing.

For each (prompt, completion) pair, the model generates y under sampling (T=0.7, top-p=0.8, top-k=20, max 1024 tokens); only naturally-EOS-terminated sequences are kept. A single frozen forward pass extracts the residual-stream activation h_t at every token position (and every layer). The headline probes read the concatenation of all layers. The target is r_t = T − t (remaining token count).

Three predictors are compared:
1. **Remaining Count Probe** — a single linear layer (no nonlinearity) regressing h_t → r̂_t with MSE loss at every completion position. Minimal capacity is deliberate so that any recovered signal is already linearly available in h_t, not produced by the probe.
2. **Statistical baseline** — outputs the train-split median of r_t at every position; optimal constant predictor under L1, and the natural MAE floor.
3. **Completion Length Probe** — trained only at the prompt's last hidden state with target T; at eval, its prompt-end estimate T̂_0 is broadcast as max(T̂_0 − t − 1, 0), an exact countdown using no mid-generation hidden-state information.

Training uses AdamW (lr 2e-4, weight decay 0.01, grad clip 10), ReduceLROnPlateau on eval MAE, 4000 steps, batch size 8, three independent seeds {0,1,2}; per-seed variance was <1 token. Headline metric is per-token token-weighted MAE. A complementary K-way classification probe family (K ∈ {2,3,5,7,9}) on normalized progress u_t = t/(T−1) is reported as an ablation via Cohen's κ.

The cross-dataset experiment trains one probe per (model, source) and evaluates on every other dataset, producing a full transfer matrix.

## Results & Highlights

- **Prompt-end length decodability is universal.** On Llama-3.1-8B, the Completion Length Probe's prompt-end MAE drops to **5.27 tokens on Countdown** vs. a **150.18** constant-median baseline, and on **GSM8K to 42.29 vs. 58.66**, on **TriviaQA to 44.20 vs. 57.86** (Table 1). The probe beats the baseline in every one of the 20 reported (model, dataset) cells.
- **Mid-completion residual updates beat prompt-only countdown on reasoning sets.** On Llama/GSM8K the Remaining Count Probe achieves **per-token MAE 36.59** vs. **70.15** (statistical baseline) and **44.04** (exact countdown); on **Llama/TriviaQA 50.05 vs. 61.09 baseline vs. 60.94 countdown**; and most strikingly on **Mistral/Count 69.51 vs. 197.45 baseline and 137.77 countdown** — here the mid-completion probe roughly halves the countdown predictor (Table 2).
- **Asymmetric cross-dataset transfer.** On Llama (Table 3), an OpenThoughts-1k-trained probe beats the eval-side baseline on *every* other dataset in its row, including both synthetic sets (e.g., **Count: 100.37 vs. 147.44 baseline; Countdown: 101.03 vs. 147.45**), whereas a Count-trained probe scores **228.93 on MMLU-Pro against a 176.54 baseline** — a clear failure of synthetic→natural transfer. The diagonal is still the best entry in every row, ruling out pure marginal-memorization.
- **Per-layer sweep localizes the signal late.** MAE starts near the constant-median floor at the embedding layer (layer 0), drops sharply through the middle, and stabilizes in the upper third around MAE ≈ 80; the all-layers-concatenated probe used for headlines beats every single-layer probe, so no single layer is sufficient and information contributes additively (Figure 4).
- **Classification-probe κ degrades smoothly with K.** On Llama, Countdown reaches **κ = 0.983 (K=2) → 0.775 (K=9)** while TriviaQA drops to **0.617 → 0.222**, consistent with TriviaQA's short, variable answers (Table 11).
- **Directional retraction spike (qualitative).** On a curated high-MAE completion (Figure 3), at the ".\n\n"/"Wait" retraction pair the probe's r̂_t shifts upward from **71 to 277** while true r_t keeps decrementing monotonically — an upward jump structurally impossible for any position-only predictor. Four additional retraction examples (Appendix A.9) show the same spike triggered by different phrases at different positions.

## Authors & Credibility

The team spans Mila / McGill University (Mohamed Amine Merzouk, Adam Oberman) and LawZero (Dmitri Carpov, Mirko Bronzi, Damiano Fornasiere, Adam Oberman). Oberman is a senior researcher with a track record in optimization and ML foundations, and LawZero is an AI-safety-oriented lab, lending credibility to the interpretability framing. The methodology is careful: minimal-capacity linear probes (the standard "if it's linearly decodable, the model already had it" argument), a well-justified L1-optimal constant-median baseline, three independent seeds with sub-token variance, and an unusually honest limitations section that repeatedly downgrades its own headline "plan" claim to "decodable, not necessarily used causally." Code is released (anonymized). The related work correctly positions the contribution relative to Lindsey et al. 2025 (circuit tracing of planning over content), Boppana et al. 2026 (confidence probes), and the exact-counting impossibility literature (Yehudai et al. 2026).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. Probing residual streams is well-trodden, and Boppana et al. 2026 already probed per-step answer confidence with the same method shape; the novelty here is the *target variable* (remaining generation length) and the cross-dataset transfer matrix, which is genuinely under-explored for continuous regression targets on residual streams.
- **Rigor:** Strong on within-distribution results (full (model, dataset, predictor) grid, seed variance reported, a properly motivated statistical baseline). Weaker on the dynamic-re-estimation claim, which the authors themselves repeatedly flag as qualitative, drawn from the worst-MAE region of the eval set, and licensed to show only the *sign* of the update — on these completions the probe's absolute predictions read ~4.84 against a true r_t ≈ 814. **The single most important missing experiment is the length-matched non-retraction control**: without it, the retraction-spike could be an artifact of any high-loss position rather than a genuine "plan update," and the authors admit this explicitly (§4.3, Limitations). No activation-patching / causal-ablation study is run, so every "plan" claim is representational, not mechanistic.
- **Reproducibility:** High. Frozen models, fixed hyperparameters (Table 6), per-dataset split sizes (Table 7), exact prompt templates (Table 8), and a released anonymous code repo. The main threat is the **Mistral-7B / TriviaQA cell omitted "due to computational limitations"** and the Llama/Mistral cross-dataset matrices covering only 5 of 7 datasets — small but real holes in the grid.
- **Significance:** A neat, well-scoped interpretability result with plausible downstream hooks (unfaithful-CoT detection via retraction-without-length-shift; prompt-end length estimate as an early-termination signal). The asymmetry of the transfer matrix (natural→synthetic works, synthetic→natural fails) is the most genuinely informative finding, as it argues against a tokenization or marginal-memorization explanation.
- **Red flags / data quirks:** (1) **Selection bias toward naturally-EOS-terminated sequences** — completions hitting the max-length cutoff are excluded by construction, so all claims are conditional on successful termination, a non-trivial censoring the authors acknowledge but do not quantify the effect of. (2) The retraction examples are cherry-picked from the worst-MAE tail, where the probe is by definition most wrong; presenting them as evidence of a "plan-like representation" risks over-reading. (3) Per-layer sweep is on one model only and the all-layers-concatenation choice makes the headline numbers hard to attribute to a clean circuit. (4) TriviaQA on Llama is a near-null result (Remaining Count Probe 0.82× baseline, Exact countdown tied at 1.00×) that slightly undercuts the universality claim.

## Strengths

- Deliberately minimal linear probes make the "linearly available in the representation" interpretation sound; any signal recovered is the model's, not the probe's computation.
- The constant-median baseline is the theoretically correct L1 floor (median = argmin_c E|X−c|), and the exact-countdown predictor cleanly separates prompt-end commitment from mid-completion update — a genuinely informative three-way decomposition.
- The cross-dataset transfer matrix is the real contribution: it is rare to see probes evaluated OOD on continuous regression targets, and the natural→synthetic asymmetry is a non-trivial, replicable finding across all three model families.
- Exceptional intellectual honesty: the paper repeatedly distinguishes decodability from causal use, flags the retraction result as qualitative, and lists the exact control experiment that would upgrade it.
- Clean demarcation from the exact-counting impossibility literature prevents a common misreading of probing results as contradicting expressivity lower bounds.

## Limitations

- **No causal/mechanistic evidence.** Linear probes show what is decodable, not what the model functionally uses; the absence of any activation-patching or steering experiment leaves the "plan" framing as interpretation only.
- **The dynamic re-estimation result is qualitative and selected from the worst-MAE tail.** Absolute predictions there are far off (single digits vs. r_t ≈ 800); only the sign of the update is interpretable, and the crucial length-matched non-retraction control is absent.
- **Model scale and regime.** Only 7–8B instruction-tuned checkpoints are evaluated; behavior at frontier scale or in base (non-instruct) models is unknown, and the per-layer localization is single-model.
- **Grid omissions.** Mistral/TriviaQA is dropped throughout, and Llama/Mistral transfer matrices cover 5/7 datasets (only Olmo covers all 7).
- **Selection bias toward EOS-terminated sequences** means the results speak to length estimation conditional on successful termination, not to whether the model "knows" its generation will overrun.
- **Tokenization confound not fully ruled out**: the authors argue against a tokens-per-character story (it would predict symmetric transfer failure) but defer a shared-BPE replication.

## Relevance

本文直接命中"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"与"LLM Inference Evaluation"两条研究主线：它把残差流中的连续回归信号（剩余生成长度）做成可线性解码的探针，并提供了一个可复现的跨数据集迁移矩阵，可作为后续"LLM Training Stability & Observability"研究（如训练过程中长度表征是否漂移）以及推理期监控（prompt 端长度估计用于提前终止、回撤 token 用于检测不忠实思维链）的基础工具。其方法学上的诚实（明确区分"可解码"与"因果性使用"）也为可观测性研究树立了良好的范式。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would activation-patching or steering along the recovered length-tracking direction *causally* change the realized output length, establishing that the model uses (not merely encodes) this signal?
- Does the natural→synthetic transfer asymmetry survive a shared-BPE / shared-tokenizer control, or is it partly an artifact of the three models' different tokenizers?
- How does the length-tracking direction behave across training checkpoints — does it sharpen, weaken, or relocate during instruction tuning, and is it present at all in base (non-instruct) models?
- Can the retraction-spike result be upgraded from qualitative to aggregate by pairing retraction tokens with length-matched non-retraction controls and reporting the distribution of upward Δr̂_t?
- Does a prompt-end length estimate T̂_0 that exceeds a budget actually serve as a reliable early-termination signal, and can a retraction-without-upward-shift signature detect unfaithful chain-of-thought at scale?
- At frontier scale (70B+), does the residual-stream length signal sharpen toward near-exact countdown, or does it remain a ~30-token MAE low-precision estimate?
