Reliability & Identifiability in Persona-Trained Monte Carlo

Author Salavat Ishbulatov · independent researcher arXiv 2607.04627 Posted 2026-07-06 No experiments · theorems only

Key finding

Makes “reliable” rigorous for an agent-based market simulator: variance decomposition, attributed error budgets, and provable heterogeneous-news identifiability.

PTMC = K persona-conditioned neural bots trading a limit-order book; outcome functionals are averaged over N runs. This paper turns the word “reliable” into unbiased ANOVA estimates, a compute-optimal inner/outer split, a three-term error budget, a Jensen-gap identifiability proof, and a boundary-corrected homogeneity test — all theorems, no benchmarks.

01 Key metric · Theorem 8

≥ (⁸⁄₃) z² β(z) VarQ(η)

The irreducible bias floor any homogeneous-population simulator carries against the impact functional. No data or compute can remove it (Cor. 9.1) — only modeling the heterogeneity does.

02 Why this matters

Strict statistical reliability for a learning-agent simulator: convergence, attributed error budgets, identifiability, boundary tests. Errors either vanish with compute, or attribute to a named, measurable input.

03 Critical edge

Every positive result is conditional on a structural commitment the paper itself proves untestable — identifiability holds only within the model class, not as a property of markets.

§01 Methodology

Three classical techniques, one new framing

Entirely theoretical: states assumptions, proves theorems, classifies guarantees. No simulation or numerical experiment — deferred to a companion study. The machinery is classical; the contribution is how it is wired together around “persona draws as input uncertainty.”

TECHNIQUE 1

Variance decomposition & input-uncertainty

Law of total variance gives persona-variance σ²P and within-run σ²w; a constrained AM–GM optimization yields the compute-optimal inner/outer split.

TECHNIQUE 2

Maximal-coupling stability

Couple two K-agent runs agent-by-agent, step-by-step; a single action disagreement cascades over T steps. Under Doeblin, a Dobrushin contraction replaces the telescoped sum by a geometric series.

TECHNIQUE 3

Random-coefficient identification + boundary econometrics

Expand the impact curve via odd Taylor moments of g; solve the Hausdorff moment problem to recover Q. Cone-projection asymptotics deliver √n estimation and the ½χ²₀+½χ²₁ boundary test.

VERIFICATION

Lipschitz check for the real architecture

Lemma 3 verifies the TV-Lipschitz assumption for softmax and softmax-plus-Gaussian action heads — including persona-dependent scales, via Pinsker. The most concretely checkable artifact.

Formal setup. A run draws K personas ω ∼ P⊗K, evolves the book st+1 = Ψ(st, at, ξ) for T steps under policy kernel πφ(·|s,p), and records a bounded functional |F| ≤ M. Estimand: μ = EP⊗K[F(X)], estimated by a two-stage (D populations × R replications) design.
§02 Results & highlights

Theoretical guarantees with explicit constants

THEOREM 8 · BIAS FLOOR

g(zm) − A(z) ≥ (⁸⁄₃) z² β(z) VarQ(η)

For any g, the representative-agent error on the impact curve is bounded below whenever VarQ(η) > 0. β(z) is closed-form for tanh.

population-level · no data/compute removes it

THEOREM 3 · RELIABILITY BUDGET

√((σ²P+σ²w)/N) + 2MKT εBC + 2MKTL·W₁

Three separately-estimable terms: Monte-Carlo error, policy-cloning error (TV), and persona-distribution error (Wasserstein-1).

under Doeblin (A4): T → min(T, 1/α)

THEOREM 7 · HOMOGENEITY TEST

H₀: Var(η)=0  ⇒  ½χ²₀ + ½χ²₁

Quasi-LR statistic has an atom of ½ at zero plus a χ²₁ tail; reject when Tn > χ²1,1−2α for size α. The naive bootstrap is inconsistent at this null.

Andrews 2000/2001 · Self–Liang 1987

THEOREM 10 · LUCAS MINIMAX

observational worst-case error ≥ ∆,  all sample sizes

Two observationally-identical markets differ by 2∆ on interventional targets. No black-box forecaster (BNN, ensemble, LLM) beats ∆; the correctly-specified PTMC plug-in does.

closed-form ∆ in g′ · Cor. 10.1

Honest caveats Worst-case constants are “demanding” — informative only when KT·εBC<1 and KTL·W₁<1 (Remark 3). The Doeblin constant α for a real order book is an open problem and may be astronomically small, in which case Theorem 4 reduces to Theorem 3 (Remark 5).
§03 Strengths & limitations

✓ Strengths

  • Exceptional honesty. Partitions claims into “unconditionally proved,” “worst-case bounded,” and “open” — repeatedly states what theorems do not claim.
  • Lucas-critique-as-minimax (Thm 10) is clean and non-trivial: a closed-form impossibility constant ∆ separating structural simulators from any black-box forecaster.
  • Full self-contained proofs — not deferred — including the detail that naive bootstrapping is invalid at the homogeneity null.
  • TV-Lipschitz actually checked (Lemma 3) for the trained softmax / Gaussian architecture, incl. persona-dependent scales via Pinsker.
  • Reframes the 30-yr Gode–Sunder objection (“do zero-intelligence traders already work?”) as a measurable σ²P and testable R*.

✕ Limitations

  • Zero empirical validation. No simulations, datasets, or benchmarks — everything deferred to an unposted companion study.
  • Doeblin condition (A4) unverified for any realistic order book; α may be “astronomically small,” making Thm 4 vacuous in practice.
  • Worst-case bounds linear in KT and loose — informative only under demanding small-error regimes; tightness evidence absent.
  • Identification conditional on a fixed, known g (Prop. 1) — whether g is correct is itself untestable from impact-curve data.
  • Only scalar (1-D) η identified. Multivariate persona identifiability not proven; moment problem generally indeterminate.
  • Nonparametric recovery of Q is severely ill-posed (logarithmic rates) — operationally limited to a 2-parameter parametric corner.
  • Single independent author, AI-assisted writing, dependent on companion papers; not evaluable end-to-end from this file.

§04 Questions for further exploration

Can the KT-linear stability bound be tightened to sublinear in T for path functionals (drawdowns) using concentration / martingale arguments, where the Doeblin marginal-coupling argument provably fails?

→ tightness of worst-case constants

How small is the Doeblin constant α for a realistic truncated price-time-priority book — is (A4) ever non-vacuous? Does Theorem 4 ever beat Theorem 3 in practice?

→ horizon-uniform bound

Is a measured σ²P > 0 ever achieved in a calibrated PTMC market, or does the Gode–Sunder null (σ²P = 0) hold — collapsing the framework's value?

→ load-bearing premise

Can identification extend to the multivariate persona vector (η, loss-aversion λ, herding h) without independence or a known copula, or is joint identifiability fundamentally blocked?

→ dimensionality

Does the Lucas minimax separation (Thm 10) survive when the observational set is enriched with agent-level cross-sectional order flow, or does the two-point pair migrate to a residual subspace?

→ robustness of ∆

Given disclosed AI-drafting and a single author, what independent verification pathway exists for the multi-page proofs (esp. Thm 7 cone-projection asymptotics) absent reproducible code?
