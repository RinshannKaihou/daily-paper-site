> **一句话总结：** 本文区分了具身推理中的"功能性"(提升任务表现)与"忠实性"(推理真实反映决策过程),发现SoTA驾驶VLA的CoT与轨迹质量耦合很弱;作者训练了一个学习型评判器 Pinocchio,沿推理DAG的5条边打分作为GRPO的密集奖励,在德国/美国保留驾驶基准上把忠实性提升约4pp(优于VLM-Judge)和18pp(优于ADE基线),并在合成的对抗式长尾危险场景上把"推理-动作因果对齐"提升1.6倍。

> **TL;DR:** The authors separate *functional* reasoning (improves task performance) from *faithful* reasoning (actually reflects the policy's decision process) in Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models, show via human evaluation that a SoTA driving VLA (Alpamayo) has only weak coupling between CoT quality and trajectory gains, then train a learned critic **Pinocchio** (fine-tuned Qwen3-VL-4B) that scores 5 pairwise semantic-consistency edges of a reasoning DAG and feeds them as a dense reward into GRPO; on ~20k withheld German (DE) driving scenarios the post-trained planner reaches 61.4% Overall faithfulness vs 57.5% for the VLM-Judge (SoTA alignment) baseline (+4pp) and vs 43.4% for the trajectory-error (ADE) baseline (+18pp), and on a synthetic counterfactual hazard set it attains 1.6× the causal-alignment score of Alpamayo-1.5-10B (7.6% vs 4.6% Overall).

## 核心贡献

- **概念区分:功能 vs. 忠实。** 首次在具身VLA中明确区分"功能性推理"(CoT是否提升下游任务表现)与"忠实性推理"(CoT是否真正反映策略的内部决策过程),并论证现有SoTA对齐(推理-动作一致性)只是忠实的必要而非充分条件——中间步骤可能未被观测落地、内部矛盾或与动作脱节。
- **人类评估揭示"解耦"。** 对Alpamayo自动驾驶VLA的SFT与RL检查点做200样本人工评估,发现RL后训练带来的轨迹提升与推理质量提升之间的耦合是"微弱且不一致"的:即便推理不变或退化,轨迹仍能改善,说明推理痕迹并非总是"承重"。
- **形式化 + 可处理的代理目标。** 把忠实性形式化为推理DAG `Ggen` 上的一阶马尔可夫链,定义"机制忠实性"(需干预验证,不可直接优化),并推导出其必要条件——成对语义一致性,再松弛为可优化的密集奖励。
- **Pinocchio 评判器。** 用Gemini 3.1 Pro标注5条边(E1场景落地、E2场景-动机、E3动机-元动作、E4元动作-轨迹、E5动机-轨迹),微调Qwen3-VL-4B得到批判器,在250条保留标注上整体平衡准确率0.87。
- **RL后训练 + 长尾增益。** 把Pinocchio接入GRPO作密集奖励,在德国/美国保留集上忠实性显著优于ADE、VLM-Judge、ADE-Reason、ADE-Swap等基线;在Gemini修复式注入危险(火灾/逆行/摔倒骑行者)的66个对抗场景上,因果对齐(Overall)相对Alpamayo-1.5-10B提升1.6×。

## Key Contributions

- **Functional vs. faithful reasoning distinction.** Introduces a clean two-axis framing (Figure 1): *functional* reasoning improves task performance, *faithful* reasoning reflects the actual internal computation producing the action. Argues that SoTA reasoning-action alignment is only a necessary, not sufficient, notion of faithfulness because intermediate steps can be ungrounded, internally inconsistent, or disconnected while still being action-consistent.
- **Human-evaluation motivation.** A 200-sample blind human comparison of SFT vs. RL checkpoints of the Alpamayo driving VLA shows trajectory (ADE) gains frequently occur even when the CoT is unchanged or judged to degrade — i.e., the trace is "inconclusively coupled" to the policy's decision-making, motivating faithfulness as an explicit optimization target.
- **Tractable faithfulness objective.** Formalizes a reasoning policy as a structured latent-variable chain on a DAG `Ggen = (o, z1, z2, z3, a)`, defines mechanistic faithfulness via `do(·)` interventions, proves a necessity proposition (trace consistency F(τ)=1 is necessary but not sufficient for mechanistic faithfulness), and relaxes it into a constrained RL objective (Eq. 5–7) solved by Lagrangian/policy-gradient with a learned critic as dense reward.
- **Pinocchio critic + Dfaith dataset.** Builds a labeled dataset of reasoning traces from four sources (expert demos, SFT rollouts, mechanically perturbed edge-swaps, adversarially corrupted traces); validates Gemini 3.1 Pro as a consistency judge against 4 human annotators (87–95% agreement, Cohen's κ 0.385–0.720); fine-tunes Qwen3-VL-4B-Instruct into Pinocchio, scoring 0.87 overall balanced accuracy.
- **Long-tail robustness result.** On 66 Gemini-inpainted safety-critical hazards (fires, contraflow vehicles, falling cyclists), the faithfulness-trained planner achieves 1.6× the causal-alignment (Overall) score of Alpamayo-1.5-10B, providing directional evidence that faithful reasoning aids OOD robustness.

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** A reasoning policy factorizes as πθ(a,z|o) = πθ(a|z,o)·πθ(z|o) with a structured trace z=(z1,z2,z3): z1 = scene description, z2 = move justification, z3 = a (longitudinal, lateral) meta-action pair. Action a is a sequence of relative waypoints; quality is measured by Average Displacement Error (ADE).

**Faithfulness formalism.** The authors posit that a *faithful* policy factorizes as a first-order Markov chain along a DAG `Ggen = {o, z1, ..., zK, a}` (Eq. 2). A policy is *mechanistically faithful* if the trace variables z1:K are causal mediators of action computation: intervening `do(zk = z'k)` must propagate to the action with no residual channel from the observation (Eq. 3). Because this is interventional and impractical to optimize, they derive a *behavioral surrogate*: a set C of five pairwise semantic-consistency edges — (o,z1), (z1,z2), (z2,z3), (z3,a), (z2,a) — and define trace consistency F(τ) as the product of binary consistency relations R(u,v).

**Proposition 1 (Necessity).** With oracular relations, a mechanistically faithful policy yields E[F(τ)] = 1; the converse does not hold (a policy can produce consistent traces while bypassing them in computation). This makes consistency a filter, not a certificate.

**Tractable surrogate & RL objective.** Each R(u,v) is approximated by a learned critic cϕ(u,v) = Pϕ(⟨CONSISTENT⟩|u,v) — a VLM emitting binary tokens. Substituting and taking logs yields the constrained objective (Eq. 5) `max E[−ADE] s.t. E[R(u,v)] ≥ 1−ϵ`, relaxed to the unconstrained Lagrangian (Eq. 7) `L(θ) = E[−ADE + Σ λu,v log cϕ(u,v)]`, optimized via policy gradient with {λu,v} fixed on validation (dual ascent deferred).

**Pinocchio training.** Dfaith traces are decomposed into 5 independent edge-level examples, each labeled CONSISTENT/INCONSISTENT by Gemini 3.1 Pro (given camera view + BEV trajectory + speed profile); Qwen3-VL-4B-Instruct is fine-tuned with loss only on the verdict token. Validated on 250 held-out annotations.

**GRPO post-training.** The VLM planner is initialized from the Alpamayo SFT checkpoint and post-trained on ~100k US driving scenarios with a composite reward: r_ADE = −ADE, the faithfulness term F̃ϕ(τ) = Σ_e log cϕ(e), and a format reward. Baselines initialized from the same SFT: **ADE** (trajectory only), **VLM-Judge** (causal alignment to expert reasoning via frozen VLM judge, akin to Alpamayo), **ADE-Reason** (reasoning predictive of answer), **ADE-Swap** (counterfactual-intervention scoring from a replay buffer).

## Results & Highlights

- **Overall faithfulness on withheld DE data (~20k scenarios, Table 3):** Pinocchio-trained planner reaches **61.4% Overall consistency vs 43.4% for the ADE trajectory-error baseline (+18 percentage points)** and **vs 57.5% for the VLM-Judge (SoTA alignment) baseline (+3.9pp)**, at an ADE of **4.324 m vs 4.169 m for ADE (≈5% higher trajectory error)** — a clear faithfulness-vs-functionality tension the authors accept as "modest cost."
- **Overall faithfulness on withheld US data (~15k scenarios, Table 4):** **64.8% Overall vs 47.4% for the ADE baseline** and **62.4% for VLM-Judge**; ADE = 3.858 m vs 3.745 m (ADE). The SoTA-alignment gap shrinks to ~2.4pp in-distribution.
- **Pinocchio critic accuracy (Table 2):** **0.87 overall balanced accuracy** on 250 held-out annotations; per-edge: E1 0.81, E2 0.78, E3 0.94, E4 0.88, E5 0.90 — E2 (scene-to-justification grounding) is the weakest.
- **Critic-judge human validation (Table 1):** Gemini 3.1 Pro agrees with the human majority on **87–95% of 100 traces**, Cohen's κ 0.385–0.720 (fair-to-strong); on 4 of 5 edges Gemini–human agreement lies within the human–human range.
- **Counterfactual OOD hazard benchmark (Table 5, 66 inpainted scenes):** the faithfulness planner attains **7.6% Overall causal alignment vs 4.6% for Alpamayo-1.5-10B (1.6×)** and **31.8% waypoint-responsiveness vs 15.4% for Alpamayo**; it also recognizes the hazard in reasoning for 18.2% of scenes vs 9.1% for the LLM-Judge.
- **Where gains concentrate:** the planner's largest improvements over baselines are on **E4 (meta-action→waypoint) and E5 (justification→waypoint)** — the edges closest to the action — while VLM-Judge actually *beats* "Ours" on E1 and E2 (the grounding edges).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High and well-positioned. Translating the LLM faithfulness literature (Lanham 2023, Arcuschin 2025, Turpin 2023) into the *embodied* setting and into a *training* objective (not just a diagnostic) is genuinely new. The DAG/Markov formalization with the necessity-only proposition is clean, though the formal result is modest (consistency ⇒ necessity of faithfulness, a fairly weak statement).
- **Rigor:** Mixed. The human-evaluation motivation (n=200, single annotator) is small and under-powered; the multi-annotator Gemini-validation study (n=100, 4 annotators) is better but reveals **edge E2 has only κ=0.385 (fair agreement) even between humans** — and E2 is exactly the scene-grounding edge where the method shows no gain. The critic's E2 balanced accuracy (0.78) is the weakest, and the authors admit "limitations of the critic transfer directly to the downstream reward signal."
- **Reproducibility:** Medium. Prompts and taxonomy are fully specified in the appendix; the underlying dataset is NVIDIA's PhysicalAI-Autonomous-Vehicles corpus (not public), and Dfaith/weights are promised via a project page (https://mjf-su.github.io/pinocchio/) but not verified. The λ multipliers are fixed by hand-tuning on validation with dual ascent explicitly deferred — so the constrained RL is solved heuristically, not as stated in Eq. 5.
- **Significance:** The central empirical weakness is that **the faithfulness gains are almost entirely on E4/E5 — the edges adjacent to the action the reward already optimizes** — while the harder *grounding* edges (E1, E2) where VLM-Judge is actually superior do not improve. This pattern is consistent with the planner learning "make the action match its stated meta-action," which is somewhat circular and close to action-consistency (the very weak notion the paper critiques). The "1.6×" OOD headline is over absolute scores of 7.6% vs 4.6% — both essentially near-zero, so the multiple is misleading about real-world readiness.
- **Red flags:** (1) The OOD benchmark and its grading are both produced by the same Gemini model used to generate training labels → evaluator-train circularity. (2) Only open-loop evaluation; closed-loop compounding errors (the regime where driving policies actually fail) are explicitly untested. (3) ~5% ADE regression is non-trivial for a safety-critical domain and is repeatedly minimized as "modest." (4) The abstract's "4% and 18%" are *percentage-point* improvements on the DE split only; on the in-distribution US split the SoTA-alignment gap narrows to ~2.4pp, a detail the abstract omits.

## Strengths

- Clean conceptual contribution: the functional/faithful distinction is immediately useful for the VLA interpretability community and is argued carefully.
- Genuinely tractable pipeline: mechanistic-faithfulness impossibility → necessary behavioral surrogate → learned critic → dense RL reward, with a proven necessity result linking the two.
- Strong empirical baselines (ADE, VLM-Judge, ADE-Reason, ADE-Swap) that span functionality- and faithfulness-oriented supervision, all initialized from the same SFT checkpoint for fair comparison.
- Honest about limitations: the necessity-only status of the surrogate, E2 annotation difficulty, and open-loop-only evaluation are all explicitly stated.
- Directional OOD evidence (counterfactual hazard response) is a creative use of generative image-inpainting (Nano Banana Pro) to construct a true causality probe.

## Limitations

- Pairwise consistency is *necessary but not sufficient* for mechanistic faithfulness; a policy can emit fully consistent traces while computing actions via latent bypasses — the method filters unfaithful traces but cannot certify the computation.
- **Evaluator–training circularity:** Gemini 3.1 Pro generates both the critic's labels and the OOD benchmark's grading; any systematic bias in Gemini's notion of "consistency" is amplified rather than checked.
- **Gains concentrated on action-adjacent edges (E4/E5)**; the grounding edges (E1/E2) where Pinocchio is weakest do not improve, and VLM-Judge wins there.
- **Open-loop only** — no closed-loop simulator validation, which is the regime where driving policies actually deploy.
- **Single domain** (autonomous driving) and a single planner family (Alpamayo); generalization to manipulation/general robotics VLAs is untested.
- λ hyperparameters hand-tuned on validation; dual ascent for the constrained objective deferred.
- ~5% ADE regression and low absolute OOD scores (7.6%) show the problem is far from solved.

## Relevance

This paper is **highly relevant** to several of your interests: it is fundamentally about **LLM Hidden States & Interpretability** and **Computation Reliability** — it asks whether a model's verbalized reasoning is causally "load-bearing" (a faithfulness/observability question analogous to silent-data-corruption in that a plausible trace can mask the true computation). It also touches **LLM Training Stability & Observability** via the GRPO post-training-with-a-learned-critic recipe and the human-evaluation finding that RL gains can "route around" the reasoning trace. Its main gap relative to your stack is precision/numerical-error analysis (no low-precision or SDC angle) and it is application-domain-specific rather than a pure foundation-model study.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the necessity-only surrogate ever become *sufficient* if combined with interventional probes at inference time (e.g., `do(z3)` patching à la causal-tracing)? Could a hybrid critic + intervention method certify, not just filter?
- Would a closed-loop simulator (e.g., CARLA, NuPlan) reveal that the ~5% open-loop ADE regression compounds into much larger closed-loop failures — or that faithfulness actually *helps* in closed loop?
- The action-adjacent edges (E4/E5) dominate the gains; is this because the reward is implicitly double-counting ADE-like signal, and would a critic trained *only* on E1/E2 transfer better to true grounding?
- How sensitive are results to the Gemini-as-judge circularity — i.e., if a *different* frontier VLM (or human) grades Dfaith and the OOD set, do the 4pp/18pp/1.6× claims survive?
- Can the λ multipliers be replaced by proper dual ascent to actually satisfy the consistency constraints (Eq. 5) rather than a heuristic scalarization, and does that change the faithfulness/ADE trade-off?
- Does faithful reasoning transfer across domains (manipulation, locomotion), or is the Markov-DAG structure too specific to the discrete-meta-action decomposition used in driving?
- Is there a connection to precision/SDC: could low-precision training silently *break* the consistency edges the critic rewards, making faithfulness a useful silent-error probe during training?
