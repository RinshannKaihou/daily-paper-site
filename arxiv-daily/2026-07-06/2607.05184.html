01Methodology

L_OPD = Σ_t D( π_T(·|x,c,y_<t), π_S(·|x,y_<t) )
  • Student samples on-policy rollout y ∼ π_S(·|x); loss is a token-level divergence to the teacher along that rollout.
  • Teacher-only privileged context c: ∅=vanilla OPD · answer-only=sparse · full gold demo=dense OPSD.
  • Distillation: JSD, 1 epoch, batch 64, rollouts capped at 4,096 tokens — identical across all variants.
  • Data: 15k OpenThoughts math + 15k Countdown (+ 500 held-out).
  • Models: 5 thinking models — Qwen3-1.7B / 4B / 8B, Qwen3-4B-Think-2507, OLMo-7B-Think.
  • Eval: AIME24/25 + HMMT25, avg@16, up to 38,912 tokens; budget sweep 4k→38k.
  • Diagnostics: fork/lock (top-prob <.25 / >.65), per-token log-ratio, deliberation-marker density w/ clustered-bootstrap CIs.

03Strengths

  • Causal-leaning design: privileged-vs-vanilla + thinking-on/off controls isolate the mechanism far better than a single before/after table.
  • The budget-sweep framing is the quiet strength — it explains why prior OPSD work missed this (evaluating where compression looks like a free win).
  • Token-level sign-reversal on fixed trajectories (Fig. 5) is a compelling, intuitive demonstration that's hard to explain away.
  • Honest scoping: complementary positioning vs. concurrent work, explicit non-causal caveat, no overclaimed remedy.

04Limitations

  • No causal proof — fork suppression is only a consistently associated behavioral pattern, not a demonstrated cause.
  • No proposed fix; how to safely use privileged information in thinking-model distillation is left open.
  • Narrow domain: only verifiable math + Countdown; continual-learning and open-ended agent settings untested — yet OPSD is marketed for continual learning.
  • Small benchmarks (30 problems each); some deltas (esp. OLMo) plausibly within noise.
  • Fork/lock heuristic uses fixed 0.25 / 0.65 thresholds; sensitivity to them is unexplored.

05Open questions for further exploration

  1. Would a fork-aware distillation loss — masking/down-weighting at high-entropy fork positions, or preserving positive advantage on self-correction tokens — recover long-budget gains?
  2. Recent OPSD updates disable per-token divergence on deliberation tokens — does that eliminate fork suppression, and at what cost to short-budget compression?
  3. Does the failure generalize beyond verifiable math to continual-learning and long-horizon agents (e.g. Composer 2.5 "targeted textual feedback")?
  1. Can the fork/lock diagnostic become a real-time training monitor that flags suppression before eval reveals the accuracy drop?
  2. Is the instruct-vs-thinking split purely a function of base fork density — would any model above a fork-rate threshold be vulnerable?
  3. How does suppression interact with numerical precision in per-token log-ratios at extreme magnitudes (e.g. the −12.70 on "but")?
