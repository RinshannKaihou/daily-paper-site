⟶ Key Finding

When LLMs fabricate answers to unsolvable math, the failure isn't in what they know — it's in what they say.

Why This Matters

Quantitatively exposes how models "know but don't say" — directly serving inference-time reliability and surfacing silent defects in math reasoning.

01 · PIPELINE

Five stages, one decomposition.

Two linear probes are trained on pooled hidden states of 7 LLMs over the ReliableMath benchmark (313 solvable · 1,102 unsolvable variants) — one reading what the model knows, one reading what it says.

1

CoT + Hidden States

7 LLMs × 5 prompt conditions; text CoT and hidden-state sequences stored.

2

Dual Labels

Ground-truth solvability (K) + LLM-judge verbalized verdict (V), human-validated.

3

Linear Probing

Logistic probes for K & V; best layer selected per model, reused downstream.

4

Analysis

cos(K,V), abstain-vs-fabricate correlation, prompt-flip rates, depth-wise ROC-AUC.

5

Gated Steering

Residual-stream edit along K, V, or KV — gated by pK > 0.5 at the token level.

Steering edit: h′ₜ = hₜ + m · α · ŵ  ·  gated active only where probe pK > 0.5  —  the gate is what stops solvable problems from being falsely abstained.

02 · RESULTS & HIGHLIGHTS

Two directions, not one.

  • Distinct K & V are linearly decodable but occupy different directions — cos(K,V) ranges 0.255 → 0.647. V is consistently easier to decode than K (V-AUC > K-AUC everywhere).
  • Mechanism Fabrication = K–V misalignment driven by V. Probe correlation is higher under abstention than fabrication for every model (r gap, d ∈ [0.54,1.10]).
  • Prompting Unsolvability cues flip verbalization, not knowledge — V-only flips vastly exceed K-only flips.
  • Dynamics In LRMs, knowledge is established early & stays flat; verbalization rises monotonically with depth and peaks in the output trace.
  • Negative Steering shifted Gemma/Llama representations along K, V, KV — but had no causal effect on abstention at any multiplier.

03 · ASSESSMENT

What holds, what slips.

Rigorous probing, multi-family honesty — but causal reach is narrower than the headline.

Strengths

  • Clean K/V decomposition operationalised as two falsifiable probes.
  • 7 LLMs across Qwen3, Llama, DeepSeek-R1, Gemma — within-family controls.
  • Honest null-result reporting for Gemma & Llama (not buried).
  • Thinking-vs-output depth analysis: knowledge early, verbalization late.
  • Practical gated-steering recipe addressing a real failure mode.

Limitations

  • Math solvability only — generalisation to QA/code left as future work.
  • Causally effective only in Qwen; not load-bearing in Gemma/Llama.
  • Synthetic unsolvable items may inflate linear separability.
  • "Knowledge" probes the dataset label, not the model's true belief.
  • No public code; gating threshold & α lack sensitivity analysis.
