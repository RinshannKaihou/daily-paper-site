> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一个统一的"归因树"框架，将知识密集型VQA中的VLM错误分解为视觉证据不足、未知实体、未知事实和召回失败四类互斥原因，并证明在解码前从内部表征（视觉token特征用于图像侧、prompt边界隐状态用于事实侧）即可预测失败来源，从而将不确定样本路由到针对性的修复干预，在iNaturalist和PopVQA上使最终答案准确率提升30.8–39.3个百分点。

> **TL;DR:** The paper introduces a unified "attribution tree" that decomposes knowledge-intensive VQA errors into four mutually exclusive causes—insufficient visual evidence, unknown entity, unknown fact, and failed recall—and shows that pre-decoding internal features (visual-token features for image-side decisions, prompt-boundary hidden states for factual decisions) can predict the failure source, enabling targeted interventions (image repair, entity support, question rewriting, retrieval) that lift final answer accuracy by +30.8 to +39.3 points across PopVQA and iNaturalist over four VLMs.

## 核心贡献

- 提出知识密集型VQA的**归因树分类法**，将错误分解为四个互斥的不确定性分量：视觉证据不足（Z_d）、未知/未识别实体（Z_u）、未知事实（缺失于参数知识，Z_f）和召回失败（实体已识别但事实无法调用，Z_r），并用全概率公式将二元错误检测重新表述为各分量概率之和。
- 构建了**可扩展的模型特定化标注流程**：用带3个干扰项的yes/no识别探针判定"未知实体"，用自适应递进式图像退化（高斯模糊+噪声+JPEG+下采样）找到识别翻转点来标记"视觉证据不足"，用Cohen et al.的实体显式改写测试区分"未知事实"与"召回失败"。
- 实证揭示**不同错误来源由不同内部信号暴露**：视觉token特征（VIS）在实体识别（PR-AUC 86.7）和视觉证据归因（97.0）上最强，而prompt边界隐状态（LAST8）在识别后的答案成功（62.7）和事实访问（53.5）上最强——这一规律跨四个模型家族一致（Qwen3在识别上是例外）。
- 证明**归因树结构优于扁平5标签分类**：树组合达到49.2 macro PR-AUC，而直接5标签分类仅41.2（差距5.1–11.4点），因为扁平表示迫使所有结果穿过同一表征瓶颈。
- 提出**探针引导的缓解流程**：用GPT-5作为工具管理器，按预测的失败类型执行针对性修复，使iNaturalist准确率从<13%升至39.6–46.6%，PopVQA从22–32%升至55.1–64.3%。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces an **attribution-tree taxonomy** for knowledge-intensive VQA that separates failures into four mutually exclusive components—insufficient visual evidence (Z_d), unknown entity (Z_u), unknown fact (Z_f), and failed recall (Z_r)—and reframes binary error detection via the law of total probability over these disjoint components.
- Builds a **scalable, model-specific labeling procedure**: yes/no recognition probes with 3 same-subtype distractors for unknown entities; adaptive progressive corruption (Gaussian blur + noise + JPEG + downsampling) to find the recognition-flip point for visual-evidence failures; and entity-explicit question rewrites (following Cohen et al., 2025) to separate unknown facts from unrecallable facts.
- Empirically shows that **different failure sources are exposed by different internal signals**: visual-token features (VIS) are strongest for entity recognition (86.7 PR-AUC) and visual-evidence attribution (97.0), while prompt-boundary hidden states (LAST8) dominate post-recognition answer success (62.7) and factual access (53.5)—a pattern consistent across four model families (with Qwen3 the exception on recognition).
- Demonstrates that the **hierarchical tree outperforms flat 5-label classification** (49.2 vs 41.2 macro PR-AUC; 5.1–11.4 point gap per model), because forcing all outcomes through one representation conflates perceptual and factual distinctions.
- Proposes **probe-guided mitigation**: routing by predicted failure type to targeted repairs (image repair, entity support, entity-explicit rewrite, external retrieval), using GPT-5 as a tool manager, lifting accuracy from <13% to 39.6–46.6% on iNaturalist and from 22–32% to 55.1–64.3% on PopVQA.

## Methodology

The framework treats VLM uncertainty as a **diagnostic problem** ("uncertain about what") rather than a scalar reliability score. Given a VQA pair `x`, the binary error indicator `E` is decomposed into four mutually exclusive latent variables `Z = (Z_d, Z_u, Z_f, Z_r)` with the constraint that any active state forces an error (`P(E=1|Z_i=1,x)=1`), yielding `P(E=1|x) = P(Z_d=1|x) + P(Z_u=1|x) + P(Z_f=1|x) + P(Z_r=1|x)`.

These define an **attribution tree** with four local binary probe targets:
- `p_d`: among recognition failures, is the image too degraded?
- `p_u`: among recognition failures, is the entity unknown vs. visually degraded?
- `p_s`: given recognition success, is the answer correct?
- `p_f`: among recognized-but-wrong examples, is the fact missing from parametric knowledge? (Z_r is the complement.)

**Data curation.** Seeds from PopVQA (4,863 pairs after filtering) and the iNaturalist subset of Encyclopedic VQA (2,116 pairs). Two filters are applied: exclusion of binary/disjunctive questions, and a visual-grounding test that replaces the image with a white canvas and drops items any model can still answer.

**Pre-generation features** are extracted from a single forward pass before decoding:
- **VIS**: hidden state at the final image-token position.
- **EOP**: hidden state of the end-of-prompt token.
- **LAST8**: concatenation of the final 8 prompt-token hidden states.
- **ATTN**: flattened attention weights from the final 8 prompt tokens to a 4-token lookback window.
- **ATTN+LAST8**: combined.
- All features concatenate mid-to-late layer offsets (−1, −4, −8, −12).

**Probes** are either a linear head or a 2-layer Transformer head, trained per decision only on examples that pass the preceding tree checks. Four VLMs are evaluated: Gemma-3-12B-it, Llama-3.2-11B-Vision-Instruct, Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct, Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct. Baselines include post-generation OUTSEQ (Shelmanov et al., 2025), TOKPROB, and a suite of UQ methods (MSP, MTE, LEXSIM, Semantic Entropy, SAR, Semantic Density, CoCoA), with ORACLE-UQ as the best-per-UQ upper reference.

## Results & Highlights

- **Failure-mode distribution is not monolithic** (Fig. 2): UNKNOWN FACT dominates, accounting for **50.7–63.8% of iNaturalist examples** and **36.8–37.7% of PopVQA examples**; best raw success rates are only **34.9% (Gemma, PopVQA)** and **20.8% (Llama, iNaturalist)**, confirming that many errors persist even after the entity is recognized.
- **Visual-token features nearly solve image-side attribution** (Table 1): VIS reaches **97.0 average PR-AUC on visual-evidence attribution**, far above the next-best pre-generation feature ATTN+LAST8 at 67.6, and achieves **86.7 on entity recognition**, confirming the visual stream is the most informative place to probe for perceptual failures.
- **Prompt-boundary hidden states dominate factual decisions** (Table 1): LAST8 with a linear head reaches **62.7 PR-AUC on post-recognition answer success** vs. VIS at 36.0 and the strongest post-generation baseline OUTSEQ-Tr at 56.2; for factual access, LAST8-Transformer reaches **53.5**, competitive with ORACLE-UQ at 52.4 but available pre-decoding.
- **Tree structure beats flat classification** (Table 2): tree-composed probes reach **49.2 macro PR-AUC** vs. **41.2 for the direct 5-label classifier** (gap 5.1–11.4 points across models), with confusion matrices showing flat classifiers collapsing toward a few outcomes (e.g., Llama over-predicts UNKNOWN FACT and almost never predicts SUCCESS).
- **Cross-dataset transfer is decision-dependent** (Table 3, PopVQA→iNaturalist): visual-evidence attribution transfers best at **84.9 PR-AUC** and recognition at 74.7, while answer success (51.9) and factual access (50.8) drop more, showing image-side signals transfer but factual signals are domain-sensitive.
- **Probe-guided mitigation yields large accuracy gains** (Table 4): across all model–dataset pairs, accuracy improves by **+30.8 to +39.3 points**, with iNaturalist moving from <13% baselines to 39.6–46.6% and PopVQA from 22–32% to 55.1–64.3%; UNKNOWN FACT is the largest single contributor to gain, while the weaker-assisted non-factual branches still account for ~40–60% of the total gain (Fig. 3).
- **UQ baselines are near-random on component-wise tasks** (Table 11): on the visual-evidence, answer-success, and factual-access decisions, the best UQ methods beat the random PR-AUC by only ~2–5 points, indicating that scalar answer-level uncertainty cannot recover the failure-source structure.

## Authors & Credibility

The work is from **MBZUAI** (with Timothy Baldwin also at The University of Melbourne), a group with a strong recent track record in uncertainty quantification and hallucination detection. Co-author **Artem Shelmanov** is first author of "A head to predict and a head to question" (EMNLP 2025) on pre-trained UQ heads for hallucination detection, and **Artem Vazhentsev** co-authored "Unconditional truthfulness" (EMNLP 2025); both are directly cited as methodological baselines (OUTSEQ, ATTN features). The related-work coverage of hallucination benchmarks (POPE, HallusionBench, AMBER, FaithScore, MHALO) and UQ methods is thorough and correctly positions the contribution against the closest prior pre-generation work (Kogilathota et al., 2026, HALP). The use of four open-weight VLMs from different families and a single 40GB GPU setup supports reproducibility.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely novel in framing—not proposing yet another UQ score, but a *component-wise* attribution tree grounded in the law of total probability, and the central empirical claim (different internal features diagnose different error stages) is a clean, actionable finding. However, the building blocks (probing visual vs. hidden states, entity-explicit rewrite tests) are assembled from existing techniques (Cohen et al., 2025; Kogilathota et al., 2026; Shelmanov et al., 2025).
- **Rigor:** Solid. Four model families, stratified train/val/test splits, PR-AUC (appropriate for class imbalance), per-layer ablations (Table 5), validation tables (Tables 9–10), full UQ baseline sweep (Table 11), and confusion matrices (Figs. 4–7) diagnosing *why* the flat classifier fails. The mutual-exclusivity assumption of the four failure states is a simplifying modeling choice that is acknowledged.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Open-weight models, single-GPU setup, explicit corruption schedule (Table 6), hyperparameter grids (Tables 7–8), and full prompts (Appendix H) are provided. The biggest reproducibility gap is the **GPT-5 tool manager in the mitigation experiment**—a closed, cost-inefficient, and version-unstable component that the authors themselves flag as a proof-of-concept rather than a deployable router.
- **Significance:** Practically meaningful: turning scalar uncertainty into *actionable routing* (image repair vs. retrieval vs. recognition support) is the kind of result deployment teams can use, and the +30-point accuracy gains on iNaturalist are striking.
- **Red flags / data quirks:**
  - **Reduced-visual-evidence examples are synthetic**, constructed by injecting Gaussian/JPEG corruption into *originally recognizable* images until recognition flips—so the 97.0 PR-AUC on visual-evidence attribution is measured against an artificially generated, distribution-controlled class, not naturally degraded inputs (occlusion, unusual viewpoints, low resolution). This likely inflates the VIS result relative to wild deployment.
  - **The iNaturalist base accuracies (4.8–12.1%) are extremely low**, so a +34–39 point gain is partly a function of a very low starting point; the post-mitigation ceiling (≤46.6%) still leaves most questions unanswered.
  - **UNKNOWN FACT mitigation gives GPT-5 the entity name without the image** and asks for relevant facts—this is essentially retrieval augmentation with a frontier model, which stacks the deck and conflates "probe routing works" with "GPT-5 is strong," a confound the paper underplays.
  - **Operational labels are model-relative**, not causal ground truth, and entity-explicit rewrites may not cleanly separate missing knowledge from failed recall.
  - No human validation of the labeling procedure's accuracy is reported.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated reframing of uncertainty as a diagnostic, component-wise problem rather than a scalar score.
- Strong and consistent central empirical finding (visual tokens for perception, hidden states for facts) that holds across four model families.
- Comprehensive baselines including a full sweep of modern UQ methods (Table 11) and post-generation alternatives, making the pre-generation advantage credible.
- Demonstrates end-to-end utility via probe-guided mitigation, not just probing accuracy.
- Transparent limitations section that honestly characterizes operational labels as non-causal and the mitigation as a proof of concept.

## Limitations

- Visual-evidence failures are synthetically induced via controlled corruption, so the near-perfect attribution on that branch may not transfer to naturally degraded images (occlusion, cropping, domain artifacts).
- The attribution labels are operational proxies tied to the labeling protocol and target model; they may introduce noise and are not causal ground truth.
- The mitigation pipeline relies on GPT-5 as a costly, closed tool manager, introducing tool errors and cost; the authors note a production router should use cheaper specialized tools.
- Base accuracies on iNaturalist are very low (4.8–12.1%), so absolute post-mitigation accuracy remains modest (≤46.6%) despite large deltas.
- Mutual-exclusivity assumption among the four failure components is a modeling convenience that may not hold for all real errors.
- The UNKNOWN FACT intervention supplies external facts via a frontier model, partially conflating routing quality with the external tool's strength.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the probes perform on **naturally occurring** visual failures (occlusion, unusual viewpoints, low light) rather than synthetic corruption, and does VIS's near-perfect 97.0 PR-AUC survive that distribution shift?
- Can a single set of probes trained on one VLM transfer to a *different* target VLM (cross-model transfer), or must labeling and probe training be repeated per model?
- Would replacing GPT-5 with **cheaper specialized tools** (a dedicated retriever, a captioner, a super-resolution model) preserve the +30-point mitigation gains, or does the result depend on the frontier model's broad competence?
- How does the attribution-tree decomposition interact with **retrieval-augmented VLMs** that already mitigate unknown facts—does the Z_f component shrink, and does the relative signal strength of LAST8 vs. VIS change?
- Can the pre-generation failure signal be used to trigger **selective abstention or confidence calibration** that outperforms scalar UQ on a coverage–accuracy curve, beyond the binary routing shown here?
- Is the mutual-exclusivity assumption empirically justified—i.e., how often do real errors involve simultaneous visual and factual deficits, and would a multi-label soft attribution improve routing?
- Do the mid-to-late layer offsets (−1, −4, −8, −12) that work across these four decoders generalize to much deeper or shallower VLM architectures?
