> **一句话总结：** 本文发现对齐后的语言模型在中层（25–90%深度）会短暂"先选错答案"，仅靠后层（late-layer）纠正才输出正确结果——这一"wrong-dip"现象可在 17 个模型（0.5B–32B）上因果验证，且能预测结构化压缩（剪枝/低秩/去层）而非量化造成的失效，并可用一个中层 hinge 惩罚的 LoRA 微调把因果 dip 降低 67–70% 同时保持完美精度，揭示了"输出正确"可能掩盖脆弱的内部组装过程。

> **TL;DR:** Using layer-wise difference-in-differences (DiD) on 278 polarity-controlled minimal pairs plus 200 role-binding pairs across 17 models (0.5B–32B), the author shows aligned LLMs transiently commit to the wrong (often unsafe) answer in mid layers and are rescued only by late-layer correction—a phenomenon causally verified with patchscopes; the resulting "wrong-dip" metric predicts which items fail under structural compression (late-SVD/block-drop/structured-pruning: 3–7× more likely) but is blind to quantization, and a dip-regularized LoRA SFT matches output-only SFT's perfect accuracy while cutting the causal dip 67–70% and lifting mid-SVD retention from 0.872 to 0.943 at 7B.

## 核心贡献

- **提出并因果验证了"wrong-dip"现象：** 在对齐模型中，中层（25–90% 深度）内部偏好会短暂转向错误（常为不安全）答案，仅由后层纠正。通过 patchscope 式激活移植在 17 个模型（Qwen2.5/Llama-3/Qwen3/Mistral，0.5B–32B）上做因果验证，排除了探针（lens）伪迹。
- **给出两个廉价、可复用的逐项（item-level）指标：** `max_wrong_dip`（25–90% 深度窗口内最深的方向反转偏移）和 `commit_layer_frac`（最后一个与输出符号相反的深度），把抽象的"内部组装"转化为可在前向单次推理上计算的审计量。
- **尺度/配方图谱（scale & recipe map）：** 对齐放大 wrong-dip 在 Qwen2.5 上于 3B 涌现、在 32B 达到峰值（Δdip +0.140→+0.182），但在 Llama-3-8B 上显著反向（−0.028），Mistral-7B 居中——证明 dip 审计的是"对齐配方"而非"对齐"本身，且这一切在输出层完全不可见（精度 ≥97%）。
- **结构化压缩风险的可预测双分离（double dissociation）：** intact 模型上的 dip 能预测晚期低秩压缩（late-SVD）、去层（block-drop）、结构化剪枝下的失效（高风险项 3–7× 更易翻转），但对量化失效完全失效（ρ≈0）——精准匹配"晚期补救"机制。
- **可训练的结构感知后训练干预：** 提出"dip-regularized SFT"（LoRA + 中层 wrong-margin hinge 惩罚），在 7B 240-item strict set 上以多 seed 显著性（McNemar p=2.8×10⁻⁶/0.013/0.064）使 mid-SVD 保留率从 0.872 升到 0.943；并警告 output-only SFT 在完美表面精度下会把因果 dip 恶化 2.8×。
- **把自然语言评测失效拆解为两层：** 在控制 readout 接口后，现象可迁移到叙事输入（semantic-candidate readout 下 dip 分层显著，p=10⁻³–10⁻⁴）；进一步区分出"晚期补救失效"（dip 可审计）与"接口绑定失效"（dip 盲）两个独立失败模式。
- **家族归属（attribution）：** 用匹配尺度家族对决把 dip 分类与外部已知的家族量化鲁棒性/微调塑性差异联系起来——Qwen 学的是"后层打补丁"，Llama 学的是"中层重写"。

## Key Contributions

- **A causally verified account of internal correctness assembly:** Introduces the "wrong-dip"—mid layers transiently prefer the wrong answer, late layers rescue it—verified causally across 17 models (0.5B–32B) via patchscope-style activation transplantation, ruling out lens artifacts (including an out-of-domain tuned-lens control).
- **Two cheap item-level metrics:** `max_wrong_dip` and `commit_layer_frac`, both computable from a single forward pass, turning internal assembly into an auditable quantity.
- **Scale/recipe emergence map:** Alignment amplification of the causal dip is emergent (emerges at 3B in Qwen2.5, peaks at 32B with Δdip up to +0.182), reverses in Llama-3-8B (−0.028), and sits between for Mistral-7B—auditing alignment *recipes*, not alignment per se, all invisible at the output (accuracy ≥97%).
- **Predictive structural-compression audit with a clean boundary:** The dip predicts items that fail under genuine late-layer low-rank compression, block dropping, and structured pruning (3–7× more likely to flip), but is blind to quantization failures (ρ ≈ 0)—a mechanistically specific double dissociation causally confirmed by selectively attenuating late-layer residual contributions.
- **Trainable, structure-aware intervention:** Dip-regularized LoRA SFT (a mid-layer wrong-margin hinge penalty) matches output-only SFT's perfect held-out accuracy while cutting the causal dip 67–70%, with multi-seed significance at 7B improving mid-SVD retention from 0.872 → 0.943; conversely, output-only SFT can worsen the causal dip up to 2.8× while looking perfect at the surface.
- **Decomposition of natural-language evaluation fragility:** Once the readout interface is controlled, dip stratification of structural-damage failures is significant on naturalistic vignettes (p = 10⁻³–10⁻⁴), separating failures into a dip-auditable late-rescue layer and a dip-blind interface layer.
- **Family attribution:** A matched-scale duel links the dip taxonomy to independently reported family differences in low-bit quantization robustness and fine-tuning plasticity (Qwen2.5 = late-layer patch; Llama-3 = mid-layer rewrite).

## Methodology

**Stimuli.** 278 polarity-controlled minimal pairs in three categories (200 value-flip, 48 explicit negation, 30 permission); each pair (ctx_a, ctx_b) differs only in a polarity operator and shares candidate answer tokens (t_pos, t_neg) placed symmetrically across contexts as a surface control. A safety-relevant subset (harm/law/deception/security/trust) is flagged. A second family of 200 agent/patient role-binding pairs is added later, plus 96/96 natural-language DiD vignette pairs and a 256-item relational-complexity ladder.

**Trajectory metric (difference-in-differences).** For each pair and layer ℓ, margin_x(ℓ) = logit_ℓ(t_pos) − logit_ℓ(t_neg) decoded through the final norm + unembedding; DiD(ℓ) = margin_a(ℓ) − margin_b(ℓ). Because the final-layer DiD is the model's own output decision, every internal quantity is referenced to the model's behavior, and the DiD construction cancels item-level lexical-frequency/phrasing effects. Two item statistics are defined: `max_wrong_dip` (deepest excursion opposite to the final DiD sign within the 25–90% relative-depth window) and `commit_layer_frac` (last relative depth whose DiD sign disagrees with the output).

**Measurement validity (three controls).** (i) An in-domain tuned lens shrinks the dip, but an out-of-domain tuned lens restores it (the in-domain lens over-corrects by learning the phenomenon itself). (ii) Causal patchscopes: transplanting the mid-layer hidden state into a neutral decoding context causally drives the wrong token (n = 278, all 12 ladder models) — all cross-family claims use this causal measure. (iii) The authors document that raw logit lens overestimates Llama's dip 30-fold (raw 0.72 vs causal 0.023), a methodological warning for cross-family internal comparisons.

**Scale and recipe analysis.** Matched base↔instruct pairs, causal dip difference (instruct − base) with paired t-test over 278 items, across a 12-model Qwen2.5 ladder (0.5B–32B) plus Llama-3-8B, Qwen3-8B, Mistral-7B-v0.3.

**Compression double dissociation.** Genuine weight-level degradations applied and item flips scored, with the dip always measured on the intact (de-circularized) model; baseline-reload agreement 275–277/278. Operators: late/mid MLP SVD (r = 64/128), whole-depth int8/RTN w3/w4 quantization, block dropping (12.5% depth, identity replacement), structured channel pruning (50% lowest-‖·‖₂-importance MLP intermediate channels).

**Causal confirmation (late-rescue deprivation).** Scale the residual contribution of the last 15% of decoder blocks by α ∈ {1, 0.5, 0.25, 0}, preserving depth and final norm; show flips concentrate on high-dip items while aggregate accuracy barely moves.

**Natural-language bridge (four acts).** Act 1 naive bridge; Act 2 readout ablation (free generation R1 / constrained label-token R2 / semantic-candidate R3 teacher-forced logprob); Act 3 a four-level readout ladder (L1 semantic-candidate → L4 free explanation) under clean/late-deprivation/late-SVD/mid-pruning; Act 4 free-generation tier with a cross-family independent judge and equal-damage-budget titration.

**Intervention (dip-regularized SFT).** Qwen2.5-1.5B (replicated on Llama-3-8B and Qwen2.5-7B), LoRA r16, three arms at identical data/budget: output-only SFT, late-quarter SFT (last 25% layers), and dip-regularized SFT (CE + hinge penalty on wrong-direction logit-lens margins at 30–85% depth). Compositional held-out evaluation (unseen verbs × unseen frames); 240-item strict set at 7B, 3 seeds per arm, per-seed McNemar significance.

## Results & Highlights

- **Scale emergence of alignment amplification (Table 1).** The causal alignment amplification Δdip is null at small scale (Qwen2.5-0.5B +0.007, t=0.53; 1.5B +0.013, t=1.36), **emerges at 3B (+0.140, paired t=9.70)**, persists at 7B (+0.138, t=7.29), and **peaks at 32B (+0.182, t=9.37)**, while **Llama-3-8B reverses significantly (−0.028, t=−2.31)** — yet pair accuracy is matched ≥97% across all 9 models, so the entire effect is invisible at the output.
- **Double dissociation against compression (Table 2).** High-dip items are **3–7× more likely to flip under late-layer SVD** (e.g., Qwen2.5-3B base r64: dying-dip 0.85 vs surviving 0.56, ~7×; Q0.5B base r128 p=0.0016, 3.7×), while **quantization deaths are dip-blind (item-level ρ ≈ 0 in all four models)** under 3-bit RTN. Block-dropping and structured pruning also produce dip-marked deaths (Qwen-7B late block-drop: dying vs surviving dip 1.22 vs 0.43, MW p=0.0003; Llama-8B 1.94 vs 0.62, p=0.003).
- **Dip-regularized SFT beats output-only SFT under structural compression at 7B (Table 4).** On a 240-item strict set (3 seeds), **mid-SVD r64 retention: dip-reg 0.943±0.046 vs output-only SFT 0.872±0.085** (per-seed McNemar p = 2.8×10⁻⁶ / 0.013 / 0.064; head-to-head 36:6 / 14:3 / 17:7 across seeds, 3/3 same direction), with clean accuracy 0.996 vs 1.000.
- **Output-only SFT silently worsens internals while looking perfect (Table 5).** Under the causal patchscopes instrument, **Qwen2.5-1.5B causal dip goes 0.129 (untrained) → 0.360 (output-only SFT, 2.8× worse) → 0.043 (dip-reg SFT, −67%)**, and Qwen2.5-7B 0.230 → 0.378 (worse) → 0.068 (−70%) — all at perfect held-out surface accuracy.
- **Late-rescue deprivation is causal, not correlational (§5.5).** Attenuating the last 15% of decoder blocks (α=0) barely moves aggregate accuracy (7B negation 0.98→0.95) but concentrates flips on high-dip items: **high vs low dip tertile flip rates 11.0% vs 0% (7B negation), 29.3% vs 3.4% (7B role binding, ρ=+0.37)**, ordered exactly as the family commit-depth profiles predict.
- **Natural-language interface vs mechanism separation (§5.6, Act 3).** With semantic-candidate readouts, dip stratification is significant for all three structural operators on Qwen-7B (deprivation rb=−0.99, p=0.018; late-SVD 46% vs 8%, p=0.0017; mid-pruning 54% vs 21%, p=0.0019), while generative readouts collapse to up to 100% flips under identical damage — cleanly separating a dip-auditable internal layer from a dip-blind interface layer.
- **The dip forecasts compression retention during training (§6.5).** After all five recipes saturate output accuracy by step 30, the end-of-training dip still monotonically forecasts final mid-SVD retention (dip 0.66/0.56/0.20/0.08/0.06 → retention 0.887/0.850/0.900/0.975/0.975, a dose-response in λ), replicated at 7B and on Llama-3-8B.
- **Family attribution (§7).** At RTN w3, Llama survives (0.55/0.59 base/instruct) vs Qwen (0.46/0.45); the family-level causal dip difference (0.02 vs 0.30) tracks how many items die, and a forced preference inversion reveals Qwen-7B carries residual dip 1.13 (late-layer patch) while Llama-8B shows shallower commit (0.41 vs base 0.44, mid-layer rewrite).

## Authors & Credibility

Single-author work by **Jiaqi Deng** (independent researcher; contact djq627@163.com). Despite single authorship, the work is methodologically ambitious: it spans 17 models across three families and 64× scale, uses causal patchscope verification rather than relying on probes, applies matched base↔instruct controls with paired t-tests, replicates the key 7B intervention across 3 seeds with per-seed McNemar significance, and provides an explicit reproducibility statement mapping every number to a named JSON artifact and script, with a three-tier reproduction package (no-GPU re-analysis / 24 GB single-GPU / full 96 GB ladder). The author is notably self-critical in the limitations (single-seed results outside the 7B intervention, a retired relational-ladder tier with a design flaw, and an honest admission that AWQ/GPTQ kernels could not be loaded). No institutional affiliation is listed, which modestly affects external credibility, but the empirical rigor and disclosure standard are above typical independent work.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High and specific. The wrong-dip framing and the two item-level metrics (`max_wrong_dip`, `commit_layer_frac`) are original handles; the cleanest novel claim is the *mechanistically specific double dissociation* — the dip predicts structural-compression failures but is provably blind to quantization failures, a boundary that prior layer-pruning/LASER work (refs [8–10]) does not establish at the item level. The finding that output-only SFT can worsen the internal dip 2.8× at perfect accuracy is a genuine and underappreciated warning.
- **Rigor:** Strong on causal inference. Cross-family claims rest on patchscope activation transplantation rather than probes, and the authors explicitly flag that raw logit lens overestimates Llama's dip 30-fold (raw 0.72 vs causal 0.023) — a concrete methodological correction. The double-dissociation logic (structural operators dip-marked; quantization dip-blind; causal confirmation via late-rescue deprivation concentrating flips on high-dip items) is well-designed. However, **multi-seed significance is established only for the 7B intervention (§6.3); results elsewhere (the scale ladder, Table 1, and Tables 2–3) are single-seed**, which weakens the precision of the smaller quantitative deltas.
- **Reproducibility:** Above average. Every number maps to a JSON artifact from a named script; stimuli counts are itemized (n=278, 200, 96/96, 256, 398/142/25, 240 strict); a frozen environment spec and three-tier reproduction package are promised. Caveat: the code/repository link is stated as forthcoming ("the arXiv version will be updated with the link"), so reproducibility is pledged but not yet verifiable at submission.
- **Significance:** Practically actionable and well-aligned with deployment concerns. The division of labor guidance is concrete: run the dip audit before structural compression (pruning/low-rank/distillation), use margin-based confidence before shipping quantized models, monitor the dip after output accuracy saturates during post-training. The framework reinterprets known family robustness differences (Qwen3 PTQ study [16]) mechanistically.
- **Red flags / specific weaknesses:** (1) The **relational-complexity ladder's long-distance tier was retired post-hoc for a design flaw** (it restated the relation near the query, becoming a redundant-cue tier) — an honest disclosure but a blemish on one of the structural claims. (2) **Free-generation mechanism stratification "remains open"** — the damage titration shows the interface fails cliff-like, so the headline "phenomenon survives natural-language I/O" is established only at the semantic-candidate (L1) readout level, not for fully open-ended dialogue, which is the practically important case. (3) **The quantization-robustness transfer shows no per-seed signal**, and AWQ/GPTQ — the most widely deployed quantization kernels — could not be loaded in the authors' environment, so the quantization conclusions rest on bitsandbytes-NF4 and group-wise RTN. (4) Cross-family comparisons are correlational, with causality established only within-family via intervention, and the Qwen3-8B reversal is explicitly a non-significant trend. (5) Single author, no institutional affiliation.

## Strengths

- Causally grounded interpretability (patchscopes + late-rescue deprivation) rather than probe-only correlations, with an explicit out-of-domain tuned-lens control to defeat probe circularity.
- A clean, falsifiable mechanistic boundary (structural damage vs quantization) supported by a double dissociation — rare and valuable.
- Actionable, deployment-facing guidance with a zero-inference-overhead audit (one forward pass per item) and a training-time monitor that separates recipes *after* output accuracy saturates.
- Honest, detailed limitations section that names specific failure modes (cliff-like interface failure, retired ladder tier, single-seed scope).
- Breadth: 17 models, three families, 64× scale, multiple task families (negation, value-flip, role binding, vignettes), multiple compression operators.

## Limitations

- Multi-seed significance is established only for the 7B intervention; the scale-ladder and most compression results are single-seed, limiting confidence in the precise deltas.
- Free-generation (truly open-ended dialogue) mechanism stratification remains open; the natural-language bridge is significant only at the semantic-candidate readout level.
- Cross-family comparisons are correlational; the Qwen3-8B reversal is a non-significant trend.
- Training is LoRA-scale only; capability tax is proxied by corpus NLL rather than a broader capability battery.
- AWQ/GPTQ kernels could not be loaded, so quantization conclusions rest on bitsandbytes-NF4 and group-wise RTN.
- The relational-complexity long-distance tier was retired post-hoc for a design flaw.
- Code/data repository link promised but not yet public at submission.

## Relevance

与你的核心研究方向高度相关：这篇论文本质上是**LLM 隐藏状态可解释性 + 训练可观测性 + 压缩/量化可靠性**的交叉工作——它提供了一种在输出正确时仍能审计内部组装过程（"先错后对"）的因果工具，并揭示了 output-only SFT 可在完美精度下恶化内部轨迹（一种"静默"的训练可靠性缺陷），对模型系统卡中量化/剪枝部署前的风险评估有直接指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the wrong-dip generalize beyond polarity/relation-assembly tasks to reasoning, code generation, or tool-use, where the "correct answer" is not a single token and the late-rescue budget may be larger?
- Can the dip audit predict *silent* compression failures (correct-but-degraded behavior, not just flips) under real deployment kernels like AWQ/GPTQ that the authors could not load?
- How does the wrong-dip interact with other known training-instability/silent-corruption signals — could monitoring `max_wrong_dip` during pretraining catch divergence before loss spikes?
- Is the dip-regularized SFT penalty robust under distribution shift and larger-scale full fine-tuning (not just LoRA r16)?
- Does the late-rescue vs interface-failure decomposition explain known evaluation fragility in LLM-as-judge and agentic pipelines, where the interface layer may dominate?
- Could the wrong-dip serve as a mechanistic foundation for a "system card" field auditing how much correctness a given model delegates to its late-layer correction budget?
