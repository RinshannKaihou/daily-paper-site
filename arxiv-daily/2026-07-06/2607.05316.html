How Much is Left? LLMs Linearly Encode Their Remaining Output Length

Key finding LLMs linearly encode their remaining output length in the residual stream — decodable from the prompt's last hidden state before the first token is even generated.

Mohamed Amine Merzouk · Dmitri Carpov · Mirko Bronzi · Damiano Fornasiere · Adam Oberman

Why this matters

A reproducible length signal in residual streams — usable for early-termination, unfaithful-CoT detection, and future training-observability studies of how length representations drift.

Critical edge

The retraction "plan-update" spike is qualitative, cherry-picked from the worst-MAE tail, and lacks the crucial length-matched control — so every "plan" claim stays representational, not mechanistic.

!
01

Methodology

Minimal-capacity linear probes are fit on the frozen residual stream of three 7–8B instruct models (Llama-3.1-8B, Olmo-3-7B, Mistral-7B) across 7 completion-style datasets — 2 synthetic (Count, Countdown) and 5 natural-language (GSM8K, MATH, MMLU-Pro, OpenThoughts-1k, TriviaQA).

  • Remaining Count Probe — single linear layer ht → r̂t (MSE), non-monotonic by construction.
  • Constant-median baseline — the L1-optimal position-independent predictor; the natural MAE floor.
  • Completion Length Probe — trained only at the prompt's last token, then broadcast as an exact countdown.

AdamW (lr 2e-4), 4000 steps, batch 8, seeds {0,1,2} — per-seed variance < 1 token. All three predictors share one frozen forward pass, so differences are attributable to the target, not sampling noise.

02

Retraction spike. At a "Wait…" token the probe's estimate jumps upward while true rt keeps decrementing — structurally impossible for any position-only baseline. Read as a (qualitative) plan-like update.

Asymmetric transfer: natural-language-trained probes generalize OOD — even into unseen synthetic sets — but synthetic→natural transfer fails (Count→MMLU-Pro: 228.9 vs 176.5 baseline), arguing against a tokenization / marginal-memorization explanation.

03

Strengths & limitations

Strengths
  • Minimal probes, sound interpretation. Any recovered signal is the model's, not the probe's computation.
  • Theoretically correct baseline. The constant-median is the L1 floor; the exact-countdown cleanly separates prompt-end commitment from mid-completion update.
  • The transfer matrix is the real contribution. OOD evaluation on continuous regression targets is rare; the natural→synthetic asymmetry replicates across all three model families.
  • Exceptional honesty. Repeatedly distinguishes decodability from causal use, and names the exact control that would upgrade the retraction claim.
Limitations
  • No causal / mechanistic evidence. No activation-patching or steering — the "plan" framing is interpretation only.
  • Retraction result is qualitative, from the worst-MAE tail (predictions ~single digits vs rt ≈ 800); the length-matched non-retraction control is absent.
  • Scale & regime. Only 7–8B instruct checkpoints; base-model and frontier-scale behavior unknown; per-layer sweep is single-model.
  • Grid holes. Mistral/TriviaQA dropped; Llama/Mistral transfer matrices cover only 5/7 datasets. EOS-censoring selection bias unquantified.
04

Open questions

Would activation-patching or steering along the recovered length direction causally change realized output length — proving the model uses, not merely encodes, this signal?

Does the natural→synthetic asymmetry survive a shared-BPE / shared-tokenizer control across the three models?

How does the direction behave across training checkpoints — does it sharpen, weaken, or relocate during instruction tuning? Is it present in base models at all?

Can the retraction spike be upgraded from qualitative to aggregate via length-matched non-retraction controls and a distribution of upward Δr̂t?

Does a prompt-end estimate T̂0 exceeding budget work as a reliable early-termination signal — and can retraction-without-upward-shift flag unfaithful CoT at scale?

At frontier scale (70B+), does the signal sharpen toward near-exact countdown, or stay a ~30-token-MAE low-precision estimate?
