> **一句话总结：** 作者在 ~11,856 参数的 Llama 式玩具模型 (Glimmer-1-Base) 上系统研究 modular arithmetic 的 grokking，发现它是一个受训练覆盖率、权重衰减和浮点数值环境共同门控的"条件性、脆弱"相变——仅仅改变 CPU 线程数（即浮点求和顺序）就能翻转 16% 的同种子结果而不改变整体 grok-rate（McNemar p=0.39），并以多种子协议推翻了自己数据中三例戏剧性的单种子叙事。

> **TL;DR:** On an ~11,856-parameter Llama-style transformer (Glimmer-1-Base) fine-tuned on modular arithmetic, the author shows grokking is a conditional, fragile phase transition gated by training coverage, weight decay, and the floating-point environment; on `(a+b) mod 10` a pure change of CPU reduction order (1 vs 4 threads) flips 49/300 same-seed outcomes with no aggregate bias (rates 74/300 vs 81/300, exact McNemar p=0.39, Newcombe CI [−6.9, +2.2] pts), and the weight-decay sweep reproduces the Omnigrok inverted-U (grok-rate 20% → 27% → 90% → 0%) that serves as a positive control — establishing that in this regime multi-seed rates under a pinned numerical environment are the only trustworthy unit of evidence.

## 核心贡献

- **完全可解释的研究载体**：在公开发布的 ~11,856 参数 Glimmer-1-Base (hidden 16, 2 层, 4 query head / 1 KV head, vocab 512) 上做 grokking 实验，模型小到可以逐个枚举其权重、attention 和全输入-输出映射，把"可读性"当作实验分辨率而非缺陷。
- **覆盖率-基数规律 (coverage-cardinality regularity)**：grokking 的覆盖率阈值主要由输出基数（模 M）决定，而非任务组合结构；mod-7/9 复合任务在 ~5–10% 覆盖率处跃迁，mod-10 在 ~20–40%，且这一排序在输入域缩小十倍后依然成立。
- **12K 参数下复现 Omnigrok 倒 U**：权重衰减扫描给出 grok-rate 20% → 27% → 90% → 0%，作为对"grok-rate"这一测量方法本身的正向对照。
- **数值刀刃现象 (numerical knife-edge)**：CPU 线程数 (1 vs 4) 翻转 49/300 同种子结果，CPU vs GPU 翻转 19/100，两者都无整体偏移 (McNemar p=0.39 / 1.0)；这与 Prieto et al. (2025) 的 Softmax Collapse 是不同性质的脆弱性轴。
- **机制性负结果**：泛化解的输出 map 更具周期性 (r=+0.37 at M=7, r=+0.65 at M=10)，但 dim-16 模型并未形成 Nanda et al. (2023) 的教科书式 Fourier 嵌入圆——这是一个独立的、可证伪的负结果。
- **分解即数据效率**：在匹配 160 样本预算下，乘法+加法双专家 pipeline 在 decay 0.1 上 grok 10/10 而单模型 0/10；同样预算、同样分解监督的 scratchpad 单模型仍 0/10，证明驱动因素是稠密子域覆盖而非监督密度。
- **方法论贡献**：多种子+固定数值环境协议系统性地推翻了作者自己数据中三例"干净"的单种子叙事（能力墙、线程翻转 grokking、GPU 抑制 grokking），把它们都识别为种子混淆。

## Key Contributions

- **A fully-tractable grokking testbed.** Using the public Glimmer-1-Base (~11,856 params, hidden size 16, 2 layers, grouped-query attention, vocab 512), the regime is small enough to enumerate weights, attention, and the entire input-output map directly; smallness is treated as enabling experimental resolution rather than a tolerated defect.
- **A coverage-cardinality regularity.** The grokking transition is gated by training-set coverage, and the coverage threshold tracks the output cardinality (modulus M) more than composition structure; the ordering holds above the transition and across a ten-fold change in input-domain size.
- **An Omnigrok reproduction at 12K parameters.** A weight-decay sweep yields the predicted inverted-U (grok-rate 20% → 27% → 90% → 0%), serving as a positive control that the rate measurement responds to a known directional intervention.
- **A replicated numerical knife-edge.** Two distinct floating-point perturbations — CPU thread count (pure reduction-order change) and CPU-vs-GPU (device change subsuming it, TF32 disabled) — each flip a minority of same-seed outcomes (49/300 and 19/100) with no detectable aggregate bias, a fragility axis distinct from the directional Softmax Collapse of Prieto et al. (2025).
- **A mechanistic read-out whose informative result is a negative.** Generalizing solutions are more periodic in their output logit map, but the dim-16 model does *not* form the textbook Fourier embedding circle of Nanda et al. (2023) — an independent, falsifiable negative.
- **Decomposition as data efficiency, with coverage isolated as the driver.** A two-specialist pipeline groks where a same-budget monolith cannot (10/10 vs 0/10), and a same-budget scratchpad monolith carrying identical decomposed supervision still fails (0/10), isolating dense sub-domain coverage rather than supervision density.
- **A reproducibility protocol demonstrated end-to-end.** Three dramatic single-seed narratives in the author's own data — a capability "wall," a "thread-count flips grokking" effect, a "GPU suppresses grokking" effect — are all refuted as seed confounds under paired multi-seed control, yielding a concrete protocol for grokking studies.

## Methodology

**Model.** Glimmer-1-Base is a Llama-style decoder: hidden size 16, two layers, four query heads with one key/value head (grouped-query attention), SiLU MLP of width 24, RMSNorm, rotary position embeddings, tied input/output embeddings, vocabulary 512, context 512, float32, totaling 11,856 trainable parameters. The released checkpoint is pretrained on 500K tokens of FineWeb-Edu and scores at chance on standard benchmarks; it is used as a tractable fine-tuning substrate, not a language model.

**Tasks.** Fine-tuning on modular-arithmetic maps written as text (e.g., `7+5= → 2` for `(a+b) mod 10`). The modulus is kept M ≤ 10 so every answer and intermediate is a single digit token, making teacher-forced and free-generation accuracy coincide. Tasks span single binary operations up to four-input composites such as `(a·b+c·d) mod M` and `((a+b)·(c+d)) mod M`.

**Training/evaluation.** AdamW (lr 3×10⁻³, minibatch 16, weight decay 0.01) for up to 1,200–1,500 epochs, on CPU in float32. A run "groks" if best held-out accuracy ≥ τ = 0.70. **Coverage** is defined as n/|domain| (domain is 10² for binary, 10³ for three-input, 10⁴ for four-input composites). Held-out sets are fixed 1,000-tuple samples with a frozen seed.

**The multi-seed, fixed-environment protocol.** The central commitment: no capability claim rests on a single run. Each condition is run over ≥10 seeds and reported as a grok-rate plus mean best accuracy with spread. When comparing two conditions, runs are **paired by seed** (identical data split, batch order, and deterministically loaded initial weights). At ten seeds a Wilson 95% half-width is ~15–30 points, so sub-30-point differences are treated as noise and 30-seed grids plus paired tests (exact McNemar, Newcombe, Fisher-z, Spearman) carry the tightest comparisons.

**Why CPU.** At 11,856 params, kernel-launch overhead dominates and CPU is faster than a high-end GPU (67 s vs 162 s for a representative run); the GPU is used only as a *second numerical environment* for the device control.

**Mechanistic probes.** For 42 add-specialists per modulus, the author measures output periodicity via the 2D discrete Fourier concentration of the true-answer logit across the (a,b) grid, and embedding Fourier concentration / effective rank, correlating each with held-out accuracy.

## Results & Highlights

- **Weight-decay inverted-U (Omnigrok reproduction, positive control).** On `(a+b) mod 10` at 80% coverage, 10 seeds per cell, grok-rate is **20% (decay 0) → 27% (0.01) → 90% (0.1) → 0% (1.0)**, with mean best held-out accuracy tracking it (0.42, 0.40, 0.82, 0.02). The same task thus groks 2–3 in 10 at the default decay and 9 in 10 at decay 0.1 — the grok-rate is not an intrinsic constant.
- **Reduction-order knife-edge.** Pairing 300 seeds across 1 vs 4 CPU threads on `(a+b) mod 10` (decay 0.01), **49/300 seeds flip grok status** (e.g., seed 57: 0.80 → 0.00; seed 132: 0.25 → 1.00), yet aggregate grok-rate barely moves (**74/300 vs 81/300**); exact two-sided McNemar **p = 0.39**, Newcombe paired-difference 95% CI **[−6.9, +2.2] points**. Mean absolute accuracy change is 0.17, max 0.85.
- **Device knife-edge.** CPU vs GPU (TF32 disabled) on `(a+b) mod 10` (decay 0.1), 100 seeds: **19/100 seeds flip**, aggregate grok-rate **86/100 vs 85/100**, exact McNemar **p = 1.0**, Newcombe 95% CI **[−7.8, +9.8] points**.
- **Coverage threshold tracks modulus, not structure.** Mod-7/9 composites cross over near 5–10% coverage; mod-10 composites near 20–40%. On the 10³ domain, at 60% coverage grok-rate is **100% for M ≤ 8, then 50% at M = 9 and 30% at M = 10**. In the 2×2 (n=2000, mid-transition), raising M from 8 to 10 drops grok-rate by 60–70 points while swapping composition structure moves it by ≤10 points (e.g., sum-of-products **10/10 at M=8 vs 4/10 at M=10**).
- **Decomposition as data efficiency.** At a matched 160-example budget on `(a·b+c·d) mod 10`, 10 seeds: monolith groks **0/10** (mean best 0.21) at decay 0.1 while the pipeline groks **10/10** (mean best 0.89) — the pipeline beats the monolith on every one of the ten seeds. The scratchpad monolith carrying identical decomposed supervision still groks **0/10** (mean 0.31), isolating coverage over supervision density.
- **Mechanistic periodicity correlation.** Logit Fourier concentration correlates positively with held-out accuracy: **r = +0.37 at M = 7** (Fisher-z 95% CI [+0.08, +0.61], Spearman ρ = +0.55) and **r = +0.65 at M = 10** (95% CI [+0.43, +0.80], ρ = +0.59), with grokker vs memorizer group means separating cleanly (0.21 vs 0.06 at M=10).
- **Mechanistic negative.** The dim-16 model does *not* form the textbook Fourier embedding circle: per-dimension embedding Fourier concentration is essentially uncorrelated with generalization at both moduli, and embedding effective rank correlates negatively at M = 10 (r = −0.55) but not at M = 7 (r ≈ −0.05).

## Authors & Credibility

- **Yoshiyuki Ootani**, listed as an **independent researcher** (single-author work). The authorship is a credibility caveat worth flagging: there is no institutional affiliation or co-author cross-check, and the paper is a non-anonymous arXiv preprint without evidence of peer review.
- **Reproducibility is taken unusually seriously.** The public checkpoint `Glint-Research/Glimmer-1-Base` is used, and all scripts, per-seed records, and the paper source are released at `github.com/otanl/grokking-microscope` (archived at Zenodo doi:10.5281/zenodo.21221306). Every reported statistic — including exact McNemar, Newcombe, Fisher-z, Spearman — is reproducible, and the full numerical environment is recorded per run (Python 3.12.9, PyTorch 2.12.1+cu126, Transformers 5.12.1, Intel MKL BLAS on AMD Zen 2, NVIDIA RTX 3090 Ti with TF32 disabled).
- The author explicitly discloses use of generative AI tools to implement experiments and draft/edit the manuscript, and takes responsibility for all results — a transparency statement that strengthens rather than weakens credibility.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The combination is novel even though no individual claim is brand-new. The Omnigrok inverted-U, the coverage axis, and the Fourier mechanism are all reproduced/extended from prior work (Liu et al. 2023; Power et al. 2022; Nanda et al. 2023). The genuinely original pieces are (a) the *null* result on reduction-order/device with paired statistics — showing benign numerical noise flips individual seeds without aggregate bias, distinct from the directional Softmax Collapse of Prieto et al. (2025); (b) the *negative* Fourier-circle result at dim 16; and (c) the scratchpad control that isolates coverage from supervision density.
- **Rigor.** Methodologically the strongest part of the paper. Paired-seed design, exact McNemar tests, Newcombe confidence intervals, threshold-robustness re-tabulation at τ ∈ {0.6, 0.7, 0.8}, and honest reporting of within-noise cells (e.g., the ≤10-point structure gap is explicitly flagged as within noise at ten seeds). The author repeatedly pre-registers which comparisons are load-bearing (the coverage-threshold sweeps) versus illustrative (the 2×2 at n=2000).
- **Reproducibility.** Excellent and rare for this subfield. Fully pinned numerical environment, public checkpoint, public code/data, archived DOI. The one gap: only a single GPU and a single CPU BLAS backend (Intel MKL) are tested, so "reduction-order" generalizes across only one vendor's BLAS.
- **Significance.** Bounded but real. The scope is explicitly circumscribed to a 12K toy model on modular arithmetic with M ≤ 10, and the author is forthright that "we do not claim the same sub-ULP sensitivity persists unchanged at scale." The transferable payload is the reproducibility *discipline* (multi-seed rates, pinned+reported numerics, seed-paired comparisons) rather than a scaling law. For phase-transition claims (grokking, emergent abilities, capability walls) this discipline is widely under-applied, so the methodological prescription lands well.
- **Red flags.** (1) Single author, independent researcher, non-peer-reviewed — claims rest entirely on the released artifacts, which mitigate but do not eliminate this. (2) The mechanistic periodicity correlation is partly **definitional** (a model that answers a periodic rule correctly is periodic almost by construction) — the author admits this openly and demotes it to a "consistency check," so the genuinely independent mechanistic claim reduces to the embedding *negative*. (3) The cardinality regularity is built on **3–5-seed pilots** for several cells (mod-9 and product-of-sums) before the denser 10-seed sweeps; the author flags these as "provisional." (4) The class imbalance in the mechanistic study is severe (39 grokkers vs 3 memorizers at M=7; 5 vs 37 at M=10), inflating Pearson r relative to within-cluster trend — again disclosed.

## Strengths

- Treats grokking as a **rate over many seeds inside a fixed numerical environment**, never a single run — and demonstrates concretely, with three of its own near-miss narratives, why this matters.
- The **positive control** (weight-decay inverted-U) is exactly the right move: it licenses interpreting the numerical-perturbation null as genuinely null rather than as measurement insensitivity.
- Paired statistics (McNemar, Newcombe) are the correct tools for same-seed binary-outcome comparisons and are reported with thresholds, not just point estimates.
- Honest framing of the mechanistic result as a partly-definitional consistency check plus a genuinely independent *negative* on the Fourier circle — intellectual honesty is a recurring and welcome theme.
- Full artifact release (checkpoint, code, per-seed records, archived DOI) and a per-run recorded numerical environment.

## Limitations

- **Scale and scope.** A single toy architecture (~11,856 params); tasks restricted to modular arithmetic with M ≤ 10 chosen for single-token answers; the numerics characterized are CPU-specific Intel MKL reductions and a single GPU. Generalization to larger models, other BLAS backends, and other GPU vendors is untested and explicitly left open.
- **Threshold dependence.** Although grok-rates are re-tabulated at τ ∈ {0.6, 0.7, 0.8} and the conclusions are qualitatively unchanged, the device flip count is more threshold-sensitive (8–23/100 across thresholds) than the thread count (45–62/300).
- **Cardinality regularity needs denser testing.** Built partly on 3–5-seed pilots; the author calls for testing at larger M and in input bases other than base-10 before treating it as more than an in-setting regularity.
- **Mechanistic correlation is partly circular.** The periodicity-generalization correlation is acknowledged as definitional; the causal circuit (as recovered by Nanda et al. at larger width) is not established.
- **Single author / no peer review.** Reliance on the author's self-verification and on released artifacts; the broader-impact and reproducibility statements are exemplary but do not substitute for independent replication.

## Relevance

本文与你的研究兴趣高度契合：它把 grokking（一种相变式的训练动力学现象）当作"受浮点数值环境门控的脆弱相变"来研究，直接触及 **Computation Reliability & Precision Errors**、**LLM Training Stability & Observability** 以及 **ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations**——尤其是"仅改变 CPU 线程数（即浮点求和顺序）就翻转 16% 同种子 grok 结果"这一发现，是 silent numerical noise 产生宏观后果的干净案例，可作为 SDC/精度可靠性讨论的论据。其多种子+固定数值环境协议也对"以单次昂贵训练为证据"的涌现能力/能力墙类主张提出了直接的可复现性警示。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the reduction-order / device knife-edge persist, damp out, or *amplify* as model width grows from 11,856 params toward 10⁶–10⁹ params, where averaging over many parameters could either smooth or concentrate sub-ULP effects?
- How does the flip rate behave across different BLAS backends (Intel MKL vs OpenBLAS vs Apple Accelerate) and multiple GPU architectures/Vendor stacks, holding the model and seed fixed?
- Can the coverage-cardinality regularity be expressed as a quantitative law (e.g., threshold coverage ≈ f(M, |domain|, width)), and does it predict grokking thresholds for polynomial composites beyond M = 10?
- Is the reduction-order flip a genuinely chaotic sensitivity of the optimizer trajectory near a shallow generalizing basin, or does a measurable geometric feature of the loss landscape predict which seeds will flip?
- Would pinning and reporting the numerical environment (thread count, device, BLAS) in large-scale grokking / emergent-ability benchmarks change any published "phase transition" claim, and can this protocol be enforced as a reproducibility checklist?
- Does the decomposition-as-coverage result generalize beyond arithmetic to, e.g., compositional reasoning or tool-use pipelines, where sparse composite coverage is the norm?
