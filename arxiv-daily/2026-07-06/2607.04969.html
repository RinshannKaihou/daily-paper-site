Train Smarter, Not Longer: Memorization-Guided Data Reuse for Efficient LLM Training

Key finding A “Memorization Window” — τ* = min(τ_ret, τ_gen) — reveals LLMs can safely reuse training data far past the assumed 4-epoch ceiling.

01Methodology

A framework, not a deployed scheduler — it converts “revisit data when forgetting starts” into measurable quantities.

  • Loss Retention Gap defines sample-level memory:
    Δ_i(τ) = ℓ_i(θ_{t_i}) − ℓ_i(θ_{t_i+τ})large gap = retained; small gap = forgotten
  • Retention window τ_ret = sup{τ : Δ(τ) ≥ ε} — when forgetting begins (rollback loss).ε left unset → τ_ret reported only as a coarse range
  • Generalization window τ_gen = argmax_τ G(θ_τ) — training horizon at which downstream (MATH500) peaks.
  • Effective window τ* = min(τ_ret, τ_gen) — the safe reuse interval, whichever limit bites first.
  • Cheap proxy: rollback loss avoids tracking every sample’s loss throughout training.

02Results & Highlights

Spacing between re-exposures controls when overfitting arrives — and LQ diversity regularizes independently of spacing.

  • τ_ret ∈ 1–5B tokens. Rollback gap peaks at ~0.15 GT then decays.Fig. 2, Table 1
  • Larger N delays the MATH500 peak: 0.85B → ~30 ep; 0.95B → ~125 ep; 1.6B → ~175 ep.Fig. 3a
  • Saturation beyond τ*: N=1.6B & 3.2B both peak at 100–150B HQ tokens → τ_gen ≥ 1.6B.Fig. 3b — further spacing gives no gain
  • 6% FineWeb LQ dramatically beats pure HQ at near-identical epoch size (0.85B vs 0.80B).Fig. 3c — diversity is an independent regularizer
  • Repetition ≈ fresh data at N=4.0 GT: validation loss drops at the same rate as new unique data.Fig. 1b — utility of a token fully restores once forgotten

03Strengths & Limitations

+ Strengths
  • Clean formal framework turning an intuition into measurable τ_ret, τ_gen, τ* with a concrete rule (N ≥ τ*, E ≤ E_max).
  • Rollback-loss is a clever, cheap proxy for per-sample memorization.
  • Unusually honest Limitations & Discussion — open questions are real research directions, not filler.
  • LQ-diversity-as-independent-regularizer is actionable for data mixing.
  • Connects to continual-learning replay, curriculum & grokking literatures.
− Limitations
  • Single scale, single domain, single HQ budget — τ* as a reusable quantity is unverified beyond one point.
  • Optimal-N* is confounded: smaller N ⟹ higher HQ% (spacing vs. mixture entangled).
  • Threshold ε unset → τ_ret is a range, not a point estimate.
  • No full scheduler delivered — “future work” is repeated.
  • Grokking-like secondary peaks acknowledged but unanalyzed, yet they affect where τ_gen sits.
  • No code/checkpoint release; no error bars on high-variance small-model math evals.

04Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does τ* scale with model size (1B, 7B, 70B) and architecture (Transformer vs. Mamba/SSM vs. Falcon-H1 hybrid)? Does τ_ret grow with capacity?
  • Can an online estimator of τ* (streaming rollback loss) drive an adaptive scheduler that adjusts N during training instead of fixing it upfront?
  • Do the secondary grokking-like rises relate to τ_gen — and should the safe regime intentionally push through the first peak?
  • τ* × hyperparameters (LR, batch size, optimizer) — hypothesized to shrink the window, but no controlled experiment.
  • Does the N ⟷ HQ-ratio entanglement survive a controlled run where HQ ratio is fixed and N varies via LQ padding?
  • Transfer to non-math domains (code, prose, science) where entropy & redundancy differ sharply?
  • Per-source τ*(k) estimates enabling heterogeneous scheduling, each source revisited on its own forgetting boundary?
