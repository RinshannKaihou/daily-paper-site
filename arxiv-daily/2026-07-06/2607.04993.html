The Map Behind the Flow

Finite-Step Gradient Descent as a Dynamical System

↗ Thomas Hofmann · ETH Zürich · arXiv:2607.04993

The edge of stability is not failure — it is the first bifurcation of the gradient-descent map, and the learning rate selects representations.

一句话总结：定步长梯度下降被严格视为离散动力系统，"稳定边界"被重构为训练映射的首次分岔——从三次映射的精确二周期、倍周期级联（累积点 a∞≈1.3023），到大深度标度下普适的 Ricker 型极限；学习率由此成为决定吸引子与表征选择的结构性参数。

01 Methodology — a ladder of solvable models

Each rung adds exactly one ingredient of deep learning; every claim is reduced to a statement about an explicit map. Toolkit: Schwarzian derivative, Singer's theorem, critical orbits, Floquet multipliers, Feigenbaum universality.

R1Scalar quartic. ℓ(x)=¼(x²−1)² → cubic map gₐ(x)=(1+a)x−ax³; quotient u=x² kills sign symmetry.
R2Depth limit. ℓₙ under a=c/n → universal Ricker map h_c(u)=u·exp{2cu(1−u)}. + depth
R3Middle ground. Analytic embedding in ε=1/n; thresholds move smoothly (implicit-function theorem).
R4Two-factor. ℓ=½(xy−1)²; imbalance Δ=x²−y²; near-edge averaging → integrable slow system. + factorization geometry
R5Wide linear net. Saxe reduction → per-mode curvature edges aᵢ=2/(Lsᵢ^{2−2/L}); Floquet frame alignment.
R6Activation. Single neuron + linear readout → tanh / ReLU representation selection. + nonlinearity
R7Label noise. Imbalance recursion becomes a random product; Fokker–Planck normal form dv=[4(a−1)v−4v²+4σ²]dt+4σ√v·dB near the edge.

02 Results & highlights

A theory paper — no ML benchmarks. Evidence = exact thresholds, closed-form constants, numerical validation of the derived maps.

µcyc(a) = 9 − 2(1+a)² Post-edge 2-cycle born at a=1, stable for 1<a<√5−1≈1.236; straddles the one-step edge, recovering EoS hovering on an invariant manifold.
σ < 0.5718 ⇒ optimum past first edge At σ=½ contraction ≈0.120/step vs sub-edge best 0.333 — ≈2.8× by parking the leading mode near its superstable cycle.
χ(1,σ) ≈ −0.676·σ Universal noisy-edge crossover (exact −2Γ(3/4)/Γ(1/4)); sims collapse onto one parameter-free curve, ratio 1.35→1.13→1.05→0.99.
χ = −a²σ²/(1−a) + O(σ⁴) Sub-edge balancing recovers small-step a²σ² scaling but with a finite-step 1/(1−a) amplification invisible to continuous time.
C₂(1/n) = C₂(0) − 0.00593/n + O(1/n²) Finite-depth branch joins the cubic endpoint √5−1 (n=1) to the Ricker constant 1.2516 (n=∞).
a* = √27/2 − 1 ≈ 1.598 ReLU inactivation threshold: first onset of "dead ReLU" units, giving the smooth-map well-merging crisis an operational meaning.
µ⊥ = 3 − 2a Transverse multiplier transversely attracting for 1<a<2; parameter-free minimum-selection law G(X∞)=G(X₀).
ccat=0.7218 · c₁=1.2516 · c∞=1.3270 Depth-independent Ricker phase constants; uniform negative-Schwarzian across cubic map, every finite depth, and the Ricker limit.

03 Strengths & limitations

Strengths

  • Exceptionally clean exposition; every result tied to an explicit map, with a glossary and a "Reader's Toolbox" appendix for outsiders.
  • Honest scoping — theorems are repeatedly separated from numerical conjectures; out-of-scope items named explicitly.
  • Pedagogical hierarchy: scalar → depth → middle → two-factor → wide → activation → stochastic, one ingredient per step.
  • Genuine closed-form results: universal Ricker limit, parameter-free selection law, finite-depth negative-Schwarzian, noisy-edge crossover.
  • Unifies scattered EoS phenomenology (hovering, catapult, balancing, alignment) under one dynamical-systems vocabulary.

Limitations

  • All models scalar / rank-one / deep-linear; progressive (data-driven) sharpening — the top-cited EoS driver — is out of scope.
  • Stochastic treatment is label noise only; minibatch noise (vanishing at interpolation) is not modeled — may not transfer to real SGD.
  • Load-bearing post-edge claims (transverse attraction in chaos, convergence to the SDE) rest on numerics, not proof.
  • No experiments on real networks or datasets; LR-schedule predictions for practitioners are untested.
  • Wide-net and activation analyses are minimal/illustrative (two modes, one neuron); anisotropic data beyond scope.
  • Long and dense (~45 main pages + appendices); accessibility is a barrier despite the glossary.
