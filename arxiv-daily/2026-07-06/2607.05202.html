Methodology — Ability Graph, not metadata

Three-stage construction

  1. Multi-Backbone Trace Collection. For 2,605 source tasks across 4 domains, no-skill runs collected from 3 construction backbones (Kimi-K2.5, GLM-5.1, DeepSeek-V3.2) under 2 scaffolds × 2 runs, 1,800s timeout. Cross-backbone aggregation resists any single model's shortcuts. 2,605 tasks · 3 backbones · 2 scaffolds
  2. Trace-Grounded Ability Extraction. An extractor (Claude Sonnet 4.6) jointly reads query, ground-truth, and multi-backbone trace pool, contrasting success vs. failure to surface procedures, failure modes, verification steps. Each card = (trigger γ, procedure π, evidence E, boundary ∂, role ρ). 7,326 raw cards · from 2,516 tasks
  3. Canonicalization + Graph + Split. Embedding cosine (θ=0.82–0.85) used only as recall blocking — never as merge criterion. Three independent LLM judges under strict operational-equivalence rubric; unanimous pairs auto-merge, rest get domain-expert review. Accepted links form a compatibility graph; group-consistency checks promote to canonical units. 170 canonical abilities · 1,108 tasks · 77,197 edges · Louvain partition

The diagnosis

No automatic method sustains positive gain. Every method shows negative transfer in at least one setting — and the regressions are paradigm-driven, not domain-driven.

  • Memento — surface-similarity retrieval collapses: −36.3 pts on Nanobot/Qwen3.5-27B/SWE.
  • GEPA — single broadcast prompt, no retrieval: positive avg but 4 negative cells (e.g. −12.3 on Knowledge-Work).
  • ReasoningBank — distilled reasoning memory: positive avg but 6 negative per-domain cells.

Anchor stays positive on all 12 SWE + Algo cells where Memento catastrophically fails — confirming the Ability content itself transfers; the routing mechanism is what breaks.

Cost–accuracy decoupling

Accuracy tables miss what turn-cost reveals: Anchor reduces overall turn cost in 4 of 6 settings (up to −8.8%); ReasoningBank in 3; GEPA increases cost in 4 of 6. Memento adds +39.4% turns on solved tasks — generic artifacts add overhead without shortcutting exploration.

Strengths vs. Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean evaluation principle — ability-supported + instance-disjoint resolves a real gap left by every surveyed benchmark (Table 1).
  • Diagnostic Anchor decomposes content transferability from delivery — making "content transfers, delivery fails" interpretable, not a black-box ranking.
  • Rare multi-setting design (2×3×4 = 24 cells) exposes brittleness single-setting evals hide; paradigm-level failure diagnoses are insightful.
  • Joint accuracy + turn-cost reporting with solved/unsolved breakdowns surfaces cost–accuracy decoupling.
  • Honest limitations — explicitly flags headroom-sampling, single-extractor, contamination caveats rather than overclaiming.

Limitations

  • Scope — 4 text-based domains only; multimodal/embodied, proprietary families, non-English untested.
  • Modest per-domain test sets (56–86 tasks) limit statistical power for sub-cell claims.
  • Single extractor (Claude Sonnet 4.6); vocabulary reflects its abstractions, sensitivity uncharacterized.
  • Headroom-biased sampling — absolute Δ is split-specific, not a population estimate.
  • No cost model beyond turns — no latency, token, or monetary overhead.
  • Missing pipeline ablations on multi-backbone pooling & expert review (the two most expensive steps).
  • Anchor is non-deployable — oracle upper bound using curator-side test labels.
Open questions
Q1 · routing vs. signal How much of the Anchor–auto gap closes if a method gets the same train-side Ability labels but must learn its own routing policy?
Q2 · split bias Does headroom-biased sampling systematically favor retrieval (Memento/ReasoningBank) over broadcast (GEPA)? A stratified non-headroom re-eval would clarify.
Q3 · extractor risk How sensitive is the Ability vocabulary to the extractor model? A GPT-class / open-weight swap-in study would quantify it.
Q4 · hybrid routing Can paradigm diagnoses seed a hybrid method that retrieves by operational similarity — eliminating the −36.3 catastrophic cell?
Q5 · graph density Relation between Ability Graph density (BrowseComp 0.404 vs. SWE 0.289) and achievable gain — does density mask retrieval-quality differences?
Q6 · beyond text Would multimodal/embodied domains preserve "content transfers, delivery fails" — or do perceptual/motor abilities change the structure?
