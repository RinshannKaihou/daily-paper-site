Key Finding

Attribution selectors pass a one-shot causal audit where rank-stable ones catastrophically fail — the refusal subspace is highly redundant.

Why This Matters

The cheap rank-stability proxies practitioners use to vet attribution, pruning, and safety-edit selectors are systematically blind to causal failure — a reliability defect hiding in plain sight.

A reusable Validity Gap protocol (LeRF/MoRF differencing + layer-matched controls) turns selector quality into something you can measure, not assume.
Critical Edge

Only 2 of 5 domains cleanly install refusal. Medical & legal collapse at the sparsity they need — the abstract's "saturates on hate and crime" quietly restricts the headline to two domains.

hate ✓ crime ✓ adult ~ legal ✗ medical ✗

02Methodology

One intervention, two audit levels, one validity metric — no fine-tuning, no inference hooks.

Intervention

One-shot neuron-row zeroing: W[i,:]=0, b[i]=0. Removes a single output dimension; leaves a standard checkpoint that runs unchanged. Embeddings & LM head excluded.

Seven Selectors

Three attribution (LRP via AttnLRP, 16-step IG, Consensus-2 Borda) + four baselines (Random, Magnitude, Wanda, MeanActivation) — all scored under one metric.

Two Levels

LM-level: LeRF/MoRF perplexity sweep. Behavior-level: contrastive refusal margin m(x) across matched harmful/benign CAST pairs.

Validity Gap

Gap = PPL_MoRF − PPL_LeRF. Differencing + Random floor + layer-matched controls neutralize the ROAR "deletion causes shift" objection.

Contrastive Editing

Signed relevance to refusal-vs-compliance tokens, per-layer normalized, minus an λ LM-protect penalty. Operating point = argmax CAST-malign over a feasible set.

Scale & Compute

5 dense LLMs (LLaMA-3.2-1B/3B, LLaMA-3.1-8B, Qwen3-8B, Gemma-3-12B) · 70-cell behavior grid · single A100 80GB · ~6 GPU-days.

✓Strengths

  • A single minimal intervention serves both audit levels, making them genuinely comparable.
  • One of the most control-rich interpretability evals: layer-matched, rank-randomized, intersection, veto — each isolates a distinct confound.
  • The stability-vs-validity dissociation is a crisp, actionable warning against calibration/PPL proxies.
  • Intellectual honesty: states what it establishes (sufficiency + specificity) and what it does not (necessity, uniqueness).
  • Architecture-dependence reported head-on, with a mechanistic gloss.

!Limitations

  • Negative on medical/legal — the behavior story is effectively restricted to hate/crime (partially adult).
  • Single evaluation per cell at a fixed operating point; robustness rests on the grid + Wilson intervals, not repeated seeds.
  • Surface-form confound between the contrastive margin and the single ProtectAI refusal classifier — mitigated, not eliminated.
  • Narrow scope: 5 dense decoder-only, English-only; no MoE, no encoder-decoder, no non-gradient attributors, no SAE/direction selectors.
  • Necessity out of scope — edits are not a recovered refusal "mechanism."

04·bCredibility & Provenance

How much to trust the bench.

Prior Work

Senior authors built the model-agnostic DLBacktrace explainer (arXiv:2411.12643) and an "Interpretability as alignment" position paper — directly relevant attribution lineage.

Honesty Signals

Reports Wilson 95% intervals on refusal rates, flags the float-overflow ceiling (~10³⁸) as a representation limit, and admits 69/70 cells are single evaluations.

Reproducibility

Modest compute (1× A100, ~6 GPU-days), standard models/datasets, Algorithm 1 detailed enough to reimplement, and 70 checkpoints promised as HuggingFace artifacts.

05Open Questions

What this paper makes worth asking next.

  1. Does the stability-vs-validity dissociation survive on MoE / encoder-decoder, or does Qwen3-8B's high WANDA inversion hint it gets worse for non-standard routing?
  2. Can a disagreement-aware aggregator (extending VETO-LRP) beat Consensus-2 across all cells — and close the medical/legal gap?
  3. Does a template-diverse multi-judge protocol change the surface-form-confound conclusion, and by how much do absolute refusal rates move?
  4. The {hate, crime} and {medical, legal} row-Jaccard clusters predict a single union mask could install refusal on both — does that hold, and generalize to a third held-out domain?
  5. Is the redundant subspace (3–6% overlap) quantitatively predicted by multi-directional refusal structure (Arditi et al. 2024)? Can disjoint-sufficient-mask counts bound "refusal dimensionality"?
  6. Can the Validity Gap become a per-selector reliability score that flags silent selector failure online — analogous to silent-data-corruption detectors in numerical kernels?
