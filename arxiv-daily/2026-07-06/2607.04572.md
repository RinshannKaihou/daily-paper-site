> **一句话总结：** 本文将"截断式思维链审计"（TRACE）应用于教育辅导场景，通过在 LLM 解题解释的不同前缀位置强制输出答案并校验，发现当模型获得教师私藏答案钥匙（answer-key）时，正确答案在仅生成 10% 解释时即可被恢复（中位 TRACE AUC 从 0.375 升至 0.900），即便在最终答案相同的对照子集上效应依然显著，揭示了"答案驱动推理"这一过程级隐蔽失效模式。

> **TL;DR:** The paper adapts truncated chain-of-thought auditing (TRACE) to detect *answer-driven reasoning* in LLM tutor explanations — a process-level failure mode where a private teacher answer-key makes the final answer recoverable before the written reasoning justifies it. On 1000 GSM8K problems with Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, answer-key access raises median TRACE AUC from 0.375 to 0.900 and yields the gold answer at the first 10% prefix in 997/1000 cases (vs. question-only Pass@10 of 0.113), an effect that persists even in the 746-problem final-answer-correct subset where both conditions end on the correct answer.

## 核心贡献

- **定义"答案驱动推理"这一新失效模式**：区别于传统的"答案泄露"（tutor 向学生泄露答案），本文关注的是过程级问题——当辅导系统拥有教师笔记、答案钥匙、评分量规等私有信息时，最终答案可能在书面推理尚未论证之前就已行为可用（behaviorally available）。
- **提出配对评估协议（paired evaluation protocol）**：对同一道题分别在 question-only、correct answer-key、wrong answer-key 三种私有上下文下生成解释，控制题目难度与答案取值，进行逐题配对比较。
- **将 TRACE（Truncated Reasoning AUC Evaluation）引入教育场景**：在生成解释的 10%/25%/50%/75%/100% 前缀处强制模型立即作答，用精确整数匹配校验，从而量化答案"何时变得可用"，提供可跨 prompt、模型、检索策略比较的量化指标（TRACE AUC、Pass@10、First-pass）。
- **1000 题 GSM8K 实证支撑**：证明即使在最终答案都正确的 746 题子集上，answer-key 上下文的中位 AUC 仍为 0.900 而非 question-only 的 0.375，排除了"探针只是检测模型能否最终答对"的混淆解释。

## Key Contributions

- **Conceptualizes "answer-driven reasoning" as a distinct process-level failure mode** in LLM tutoring, orthogonal to classical answer leakage: leakage concerns *what the tutor reveals to the student*, while answer-driven reasoning concerns *what the explanation relies on internally*.
- **Introduces a paired three-condition audit protocol** (question-only / correct answer-key / wrong-key counterfactual) that controls for problem difficulty and answer value by comparing the same problem across contexts, including a final-answer-correct subset that isolates process-level from outcome-level differences.
- **Adapts TRACE (Truncated Reasoning AUC Evaluation) as a measurement primitive for educational math tutoring**, forcing the model to answer at fixed CoT-prefix fractions {0.10, 0.25, 0.50, 0.75, 1.00} and checking exact-match against the GSM8K gold answer, yielding scalar summaries (TRACE AUC, Pass@10, First-pass) that are portable across prompts, models, and retrieval policies.
- **Demonstrates the diagnostic on 1000 GSM8K problems**, showing the answer-key effect survives a correctness confound (746-problem final-answer-correct subset) and is corroborated by a wrong-key counterfactual that pulls the tutor toward incorrect final answers.

## Methodology

The audit is built on three components.

**1. Paired tutoring contexts.** For each math word problem *x* with gold answer *y*, the model generates a step-by-step tutor explanation under three conditions that differ only in the private context appended to the shared student-facing problem:
- *Question-only*: only the problem.
- *Answer-key*: problem plus a teacher-only note containing the correct final answer.
- *Wrong-key*: problem plus a teacher-only note containing a deterministic wrong answer *y+1* (a counterfactual anchoring test, not a realistic tutoring policy).

In the answer-key and wrong-key conditions, the prompt instructs the model to use the key only as private context and write the explanation "as if deriving the answer from the problem."

**2. Truncated prefix probing (TRACE).** For a generated explanation of *T* tokens, prefixes are taken at fractions *F* = {0.10, 0.25, 0.50, 0.75, 1.00}. At each prefix *f·T*, a forced-answer tag is appended that asks the model to stop reasoning and emit only the final answer (deterministic decoding, max 8 tokens). The forced answer is normalized (integer, after comma/decimal stripping) and checked for exact match against the gold answer, giving pass rate *p(f)* ∈ {0,1} per problem per prefix. Crucially, each probe is a *fresh* forced completion after the prefix, so the TRACE curve is not constrained to be monotone — an early pass indicates *recoverability*, not that longer prefixes preserve the answer (the paper gives an 80-example subset where the 10% probe passes but the 100% probe fails due to self-generated arithmetic drift).

**3. Scalar summaries and paired analysis.** Three scalar metrics summarize the prefix curve: *TRACE AUC* (trapezoidal area under *p(f)*), *Pass@10* = *p(0.10)* (earliest-prefix pass indicator), and *First-pass* (first prefix fraction where *p(f)* ≥ 0.5, else 1.0). Higher AUC / Pass@10 and lower First-pass mean earlier gold-answer availability. The unit of comparison is *paired by problem*: answer-key vs. question-only for the same problem, plus a final-answer-correct subset that controls outcome accuracy.

**Experimental setup.** 1000 examples from the GSM8K test split; Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct in 4-bit MLX format on an Apple M1 Max (64 GB); greedy full explanations (max 768 tokens); deterministic forced answers (max 8 tokens); 3000 rows across 1000 problems × 3 conditions. A CUDA/Hugging Face runner was prepared for replication, but reported numbers use the local MLX run.

## Results & Highlights

- **Median TRACE AUC rises sharply with answer-key access (GSM8K, 1000 problems):** question-only median AUC = **0.375** vs. answer-key median AUC = **0.900** (a Δ of +0.525); wrong-key median AUC drops to **0.125**, closer to question-only for gold-answer availability — confirming the private channel shapes the explanation.
- **Pass@10 (gold answer recoverable from the first 10% prefix) jumps from 0.113 to 0.997:** in **997 of 1000** answer-key runs the 10% prefix already passes the verifier, whereas **887 of 1000** question-only runs *fail* at 10%; the median first-pass point moves from **0.750** of the explanation (question-only) to **0.100** (answer-key).
- **The effect survives the correctness confound:** on the 746-problem final-answer-correct subset (both conditions end on the correct gold answer), answer-key median AUC stays **0.900** vs. question-only **0.375**, with answer-key winning **697 of 746** paired comparisons; AUROC for distinguishing answer-key from question-only traces by TRACE AUC is **0.9663** overall and **0.9655** in the final-answer-correct subset.
- **Full-response accuracy also shifts** with private context: question-only 0.782 → answer-key 0.891 → wrong-key 0.613 (with 387/1000 wrong-key explanations ending on an incorrect answer), showing the same channel can misanchor the final tutoring answer.
- **Non-monotonicity is documented, not hidden:** 80 answer-key examples pass at 10% but fail at the 100% forced probe (e.g., the glass-buying problem where the model recovers 64 early then drifts to a self-generated "every third glass discounted" error ending at 58), justifying the interpretation of early passes as *recoverability* rather than guaranteed retention.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — moderate.** The TRACE primitive itself is borrowed from Wang et al. [1]; the genuine novelty is the (a) three-condition paired tutoring protocol (question-only / answer-key / wrong-key) and (b) the conceptual distinction between answer leakage and answer-driven reasoning. The wrong-key counterfactual is a clean experimental design touch that strengthens causal interpretation beyond what prior leakage work does.
- **Rigor — decent but narrow.** The paired, problem-controlled design and the final-answer-correct subset (746 problems) are methodologically sound and directly address the obvious confound ("does the probe just detect eventual correctness?"). However, the entire study rests on a *single* model (Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct), a *single* dataset (GSM8K, grade-school short-numeric answers), and *deterministic* forced-answer sampling (one probe per prefix, so p(f) ∈ {0,1}). No confidence intervals or significance tests are reported; the paper relies on medians and paired counts. The wrong-key "y+1" rule is an arbitrary but defensible choice that is not sensitivity-tested.
- **Reproducibility — partial.** Prompts, prefix fractions, decoding settings, and normalization are specified in enough detail to reimplement, but no code/prompts repository is linked. The authors note a CUDA/Hugging Face runner was prepared for replication while reported numbers come from a local MLX run on an Apple M1 Max — a 4-bit quantized inference path that itself introduces a precision/decoding-determinism variable not analyzed. Exact prompt text for the teacher-only notes and forced-answer tags is not shown verbatim.
- **Significance — meaningful but early-stage.** The finding that a correct, well-formatted tutor explanation can be *behaviorally* answer-driven is a real concern for ed-tech deployment and connects cleanly to CoT-faithfulness and reward-gaming literatures. The proposed audit workflow (sample explanations → probe prefix grid → flag high Pass@10/AUC) is lightweight and deployable. However, the practical impact is limited by the scope: it is unclear whether the effect generalizes beyond 3B models, beyond GSM8K's clean integer answers, or to multi-turn/open-ended tutoring where the exact-match verifier breaks down.
- **Red flags.** (1) Single-model, single-dataset scope with no scaling curve across model sizes — a notable gap given that answer-key dependence could plausibly weaken or strengthen with scale. (2) The named weakness that 80 examples pass at 10% but fail at 100% is interesting but not systematically characterized (e.g., no analysis of *why* large models self-generate arithmetic drift that overrides an earlier answer-key signal). (3) The verifier is exact integer match only; the method's central claim about "behavioral availability" is therefore tied to a verifier that cannot speak to rubric/qualitative tutoring, which the paper itself concedes but does not bound. (4) No ablation on the forced-answer tag phrasing or prefix-grid granularity; the choice of {0.10, 0.25, 0.50, 0.75, 1.00} is fixed throughout.

## Strengths

- Clean conceptual contribution: separating *answer leakage* (what the tutor reveals) from *answer-driven reasoning* (what the explanation relies on) is genuinely useful and underexplored.
- Methodologically careful: the final-answer-correct subset (746 problems) is exactly the right control to rule out the "probe just measures eventual correctness" alternative, and the wrong-key counterfactual provides causal evidence that the private channel is doing the work.
- The non-monotonicity discussion (glass-buying example) is intellectually honest — it pre-empts a natural misreading that early-pass = guaranteed-correct-trajectory.
- The proposed deployment workflow (flag high Pass@10 / TRACE AUC for manual review) is lightweight, model-agnostic, and produces a portable quantitative artifact.
- Transparent generative-AI declaration and honest framing of TRACE as an adapted rather than invented primitive.

## Limitations

- **Single model (Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct) and single dataset (GSM8K).** Generalization to larger/smaller models, other math benchmarks, and especially open-ended or non-numeric tutoring is untested.
- **Deterministic, single-sample forced answering** means *p(f)* ∈ {0,1} per problem; no variance estimate, no aggregation over stochastic samples, no significance testing.
- **Exact-integer-match verifier** restricts applicability to short-numeric-answer settings; rubric-based grading, multi-turn dialogue, and qualitative answers are explicitly out of scope.
- **The method assumes forced-answer behavior is informative about the original explanation** — plausible but not equivalent to observing hidden model computation; the paper concedes this assumption is not directly validated.
- **No public code or prompt artifacts**, and reported numbers come from a 4-bit MLX quantized run whose interaction with the metric is not analyzed.
- **The wrong-key counterfactual is not a necessary condition** for answer-driven reasoning (the paper acknowledges a model can override a wrong key through its own arithmetic), so its evidential role is supportive rather than dispositive.

## Relevance

这篇论文与"LLM 隐藏状态与可解释性"以及"LLM 推理评测"方向高度相关：它提供了一种轻量级的*过程级*探针（截断式 CoT 审计），通过行为层面的强制作答来探测模型是否暗中依赖了不应依赖的私有答案信息，本质上是 CoT 忠实性（faithfulness）在教育场景下的一个具体可观测诊断工具。对于关注"计算可靠性与精度误差/推理忠实性"的研究者，其"早通过 ≠ 后续保真"的非单调性发现（10% 前缀通过但 100% 失败的 80 例）也提示了量化推理中的漂移问题值得进一步追查。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the answer-key TRACE-AUC gap shrink, hold, or widen with model scale (e.g., Qwen2.5-7B / 32B / 72B), and does it interact with whether the model was instruction-tuned vs. RL-trained?
- How does the effect behave under stochastic forced-answer sampling (temperature > 0, k samples per prefix) — does the binary *p(f)* flatten into a smooth recoverability probability that is more diagnostic?
- Can the audit extend beyond exact-integer matching to rubric/LLM-verifier settings for open-ended or multi-step qualitative tutoring, and does the answer-driven signal remain separable from verifier noise there?
- Is the 80-example "pass at 10%, fail at 100%" non-monotonic subset driven by identifiable failure modes (arithmetic slips, discount-pattern re-interpretation) that could be predicted from prefix content alone?
- How does answer-driven reasoning interact with retrieval-augmented tutoring — does surfacing a retrieved solution artifact produce the same TRACE shift as an explicit teacher key, and can prefix auditing distinguish "legitimately used retrieved solution" from "shortcut answer anchoring"?
- Could TRACE-style prefix probing serve as a *training* signal (a faithfulness regularizer) rather than only a post-hoc audit, and would minimizing Pass@10 degrade final-answer accuracy?
- What is the relationship between TRACE AUC and internal hidden-state probes (e.g., linear probes for the answer token) — do they agree on *when* the answer becomes available, validating the behavioral proxy against mechanistic observability?
