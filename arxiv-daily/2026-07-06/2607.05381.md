> **一句话总结：** 本文从连续时间马尔可夫链（CTMC）ELBO 出发严格证明：离散扩散模型负 ELBO 在减去数据熵后，恰好等于"神谕反向过程"到"所学过程"的路径 KL 散度（Oracle Distance 定理），且去噪器/cavity/打分只是同一个最优反向跳率的三种等价坐标——这统一了 MDM/UDM/SEDD/GIDD，并解释了为何均匀扩散下去噪器参数化在初始化时会让 ELBO 发散而桥接器（cavity）不会。

> **TL;DR:** This 66-page theory paper rigorously rederives the continuous-time Markov chain (CTMC) ELBO for discrete diffusion with explicit boundary terms and proves the **Oracle Distance theorem**: the negative ELBO equals the data entropy plus an *exact* path-KL from the oracle reverse process to the learned one (not merely a bound). On a V=8, L=8 bigram toy model it shows every noising process shares the same NELBO floor of H(q₀)/L = 1.25 nats/token, that misreading a denoiser head as a score coordinate inflates the per-token NELBO from 1.25 to 7.28±0.01 (uniform), and that an uninformative denoiser head under uniform diffusion diverges as ((V−1)L/V)·log(1/β_{t₁}) whereas the cavity head stays finite at exactly L·logV — unifying MDM, UDM, SEDD, GIDD, RADD as coordinates of one object.

## 核心贡献

- **通用 CTMC ELBO 推导（含边界项）：** 针对任意前向加噪过程的有限状态跳过程，给出连续时间离散扩散 ELBO 的自洽推导，明确保留重建项与终态先验项这两类边界项，并给出两种互补证明——无穷小 KL 极限与 Girsanov 公式（定理 1、定理 2）。
- **Oracle Distance 定理（定理 3）：** 证明负 ELBO 减去数据熵后，等于"神谕反向过程"到"所学反向过程"的精确路径 KL（含边界项的有限窗口版本），从而把 ELBO 从"似然的松界"重新解释为"反向动力学之间的真实距离"。
- **ELBO 即反向跳率投影：** 最优解是清洁条件反向桥跳率在当前噪声状态信息上的后验平均，由此给出毕达哥拉斯分解（命题 2）——把代价拆为"神谕代价"+"模型-神谕偏离"，二者无交叉项。
- **不可约代价 = 信息损失率：** 证明神谕每时间代价 J⋆_t = dH(Z₀|Zₜ)/dt = −dI(Z₀;Zₜ)/dt（定理 4），即 CTMC 版的 I-MMSE 关系；其全程积分恰为数据熵，因此**所有加噪过程共享同一个最佳可达 NELBO 下界：数据熵 H(p_data)**（推论 3）。
- **一个跳率，三种坐标：** 对乘积型 CTMC，给出最优反向跳率在去噪器（denoiser π⋆）、cavity/桥接器（µ⋆）、具体打分（concrete score s⋆）三种坐标下的精确表达式及闭式互转公式（定理 7、命题 3、表 2）。
- **统一并诊断现有方法：** 从单一投影原理恢复 MDLM/D3PM/MD4（去噪器）、UDLM/GIDD/Duo（cavity）、SEDD/RADD/TCSM（score）；解释了为何掩码扩散下去噪器=cavity、为何均匀/GIDD 扩散下去噪器坐标让 ELBO 在初始化时发散（命题 6）、为何桥接器目标其实是 cavity 律。
- **初始化校准与可比较性工具：** 给出"均匀 cavity 1/V 头 → 每token NELBO = logV"的精确恒等式（命题 4）作为端到端 ELBO 实现调试工具；强调保留边界项才能跨方法比较 ELBO（表 5）。
- **分离两类误差：** 反向跳率误差（ELBO 可测）与采样因子化误差（ELBO 不可见，图 6 用精确神谕去噪器隔离出来）。

## Key Contributions

- **A general CTMC ELBO with explicit boundary terms.** A self-contained rederivation of the continuous-time discrete-diffusion ELBO for finite-state jump processes, keeping the reconstruction and terminal-prior boundary terms that the literature usually discards, with the support/regularity conditions under which it is well defined and two complementary derivations (infinitesimal-KL limit and a Girsanov argument; Theorem 1, Theorem 2).
- **The Oracle Distance theorem (Theorem 3).** After subtracting the data entropy and the two endpoint terms, the negative ELBO is *exactly* the path-law KL from the oracle reverse CTMC to the learned one — an identity, not merely a bound. This reinterprets the ELBO as a genuine distance between reverse dynamics.
- **ELBO as a reverse-rate projection.** The unique Zₜ-measurable population optimizer is the posterior average of the clean-conditioned reverse bridge rate (Theorem 5), giving an exact Pythagorean split (Proposition 2) into irreducible oracle cost + model–oracle mismatch, with no cross term.
- **Irreducible cost = information-loss rate.** The oracle per-time cost is J⋆_t = dH(Z₀|Zₜ)/dt = −dI(Z₀;Zₜ)/dt ≥ 0 (Theorem 4), the CTMC analogue of the Gaussian I-MMSE relation; integrated over the schedule it telescopes to the data entropy, so every noising process shares the universal NELBO floor H(p_data) (Corollary 3).
- **One reverse rate, three coordinates.** For product CTMCs the optimal reverse rate admits three exact factorizations — denoiser π⋆, cavity µ⋆, concrete score s⋆ — with closed-form conversions (Theorem 7, Proposition 3, Table 2). This identifies which law each literature loss actually optimizes.
- **Unification and diagnosis of existing methods.** MDLM/D3PM/MD4, UDLM/GIDD/Duo, and SEDD/RADD/TCSM are recovered as instantiations of one recipe; the framework explains why masked diffusion reduces to ordinary denoising cross-entropy, why the bridge plug-in silently targets a cavity law (not the denoiser) under uniform diffusion, and why a denoiser parameterization makes the uniform/GIDD ELBO diverge at initialization (Proposition 6).
- **Initialization calibration & comparability.** A uniform cavity 1/V head yields per-token NELBO = logV exactly (Proposition 4), a debugging tool; the paper keeps the boundary terms that make reported ELBOs comparable across noising processes (Table 5).
- **Clean separation of two error sources.** Reverse-rate error (measured by the ELBO) versus sampling factorization error (invisible to the ELBO, isolated numerically at the oracle denoiser in Figure 6).

## Methodology

The paper is **theoretical**, built on continuous-time Markov chain (CTMC) theory and variational inference, and proceeds in three layers:

1. **The ELBO machinery (§4).** Discrete diffusion is recast as a VAE whose latent variable is the entire noising trajectory. Starting from the discrete-time ELBO (Proposition 1), the authors pass to continuous time by taking a vanishing-mesh limit, using **Lemma 1** which shows that a one-step KL between jump kernels expands to Δ·Φ(a,b)+o(Δ), where Φ(a,b) = a·log(a/b) − a + b is the Bregman divergence of the negative-entropy potential (the KL rate between two Poisson jump intensities). A second, more general derivation uses the **finite-state Girsanov formula** (Theorem 2), which expresses the path-law KL between two CTMCs as the KL of their starting laws plus an integral of local Φ-divergences. The result (Theorem 1) is the CTMC ELBO on a finite window [t₁,t₂]: reconstruction term + terminal-prior KL + path integral of Φ(Q̃_t(·|z₀), Q̃^θ_t). The role of importance sampling as a *clock reparameterization* of the reverse chain is also made explicit (§4.4).

2. **What the ELBO optimizes (§5).** Two ingredients assemble the Oracle Distance theorem. (a) A **Bregman–Pythagoras identity** (Lemma 2) for Φ yields the projection optimum (Theorem 5): among rates measurable in the noisy state Zₜ only, the unique minimizer is the conditional mean E[Q̃_t(zₜ,y|Z₀)|Zₜ=zₜ], which by a short Bayes computation equals the true marginal reverse rate Q̃_t(zₜ,y). (b) Differentiating the conditional entropy and comparing with the oracle integrand proves J⋆_t = dH(Z₀|Zₜ)/dt (Theorem 4). Combining the two gives the central identity NELBO − H(q₀) = reconstruction mismatch + path KL(P⋆‖P^θ). §5.5 gives a second proof avoiding regularity assumptions via the KL chain rule and the reverse-time factorization of the joint noising law; §5.6 places everything in the Bregman / generator-matching framework and gives the continuous-diffusion analogue (Table 1).

3. **Sequence modeling and instantiations (§6–§7).** For token-factorizing noising processes (product CTMCs, Theorem 6), the reverse rate decomposes into single-token jumps, and the projection yields the denoiser/cavity/score coordinate dictionary (Theorem 7). The framework is instantiated on masked (§7.2), uniform (§7.3), and GIDD (§7.4) processes, recovering their reverse rates, NELBO integrands (Proposition 5 gives a generic cavity ELBO = KL + Itakura–Saito), ancestral samplers, and prior-KL boundary terms (Table 5). The masked special case shows denoiser = cavity (Corollary 4); the uniform case proves the denoiser-coordinate divergence (Proposition 6).

**Numerical verification (§8)** is done on an *exactly solvable* toy model — a homogeneous bigram chain on V=8 tokens of length L=8 (H(q₀)=10.00 nats, 1.25/token) — so every oracle quantity is available in closed form. Training uses a small DiT (hidden 128, 4 heads, 3 blocks; Adam 10⁻³, batch 256, 8000 steps); generative perplexities are exact q₀-NLLs of 2500 ancestral samples over 256 steps.

## Results & Highlights

- **Universal NELBO floor shared by all noising processes.** On the bigram toy model (H(q₀)/L = 1.25 nats/token), the excess NELBO above the floor decays to zero for every parameterization (denoiser, cavity, score) and every noising process (masked, uniform, GIDD family) — Figure 3. The total information destroyed over the schedule equals H(q₀)/L = 1.25 nats/token for masked, uniform, and every GIDD interpolation λ (Figure 2a), confirming the universal floor of Corollary 3 numerically.
- **Convert-or-pay penalty for misreading coordinates (Table 6).** With the correct closed-form conversion (Table 2) every coordinate gives the oracle per-token NELBO of 1.25; but *naively misreading* a head without converting penalizes every off-diagonal. For uniform diffusion, reading a denoiser head as a score coordinate yields **7.28 ± 0.01 nats/token** (vs. the correct 1.25, a **+6.03 nats/token** penalty); reading it as a cavity yields **2.03 ± 0.01**. For GIDD the denoiser→score penalty is **3.92 ± 0.01**. The penalty grows continuously from zero at masked diffusion, where denoiser and cavity coincide.
- **Denoiser divergence vs. cavity calibration at initialization (Propositions 4 & 6, Figure 7).** Under uniform diffusion, an uninformative denoiser head π^θ_i ≡ 1/V makes the NELBO diverge as **((V−1)L/V)·log(1/β_{t₁}) + O(1)** as t₁↓0 (non-integrable), with slope (V−1)/V in log(1/β_{t₁}); the *same* head read as a cavity stays finite at exactly **L·log V** per-token (Proposition 4), independent of the diffusion process and window endpoints once boundary terms are kept. This is offered as both a theoretical account of why denoisers are hard to train near the clean endpoint and a practical end-to-end ELBO-implementation sanity check.
- **Reverse-rate projection confirmed empirically (Figure 1).** Model rates Q̃^θ_t(zₜ,y) from a trained uniform-diffusion denoiser network, averaged across many (zₜ,y,t), land on the oracle marginal rate Q̃_t(zₜ,y) = E_{q(z₀|zₜ)}[Q̃_t(zₜ,y|z₀)], validating Theorem 5's "conditional mean = marginal rate" identification.
- **Sampling factorization error isolated (Figure 6).** Sampling with the *exact oracle denoiser* zeroes the reverse-rate error, so the residual excess generative perplexity over the true PPL is pure factorization error of the token-wise ancestral sampler — it vanishes only as NFE grows, with masked diffusion penalized more due to its inability to self-correct a committed token.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The paper's main unifying object — the Oracle Distance identity and the denoiser/cavity/score coordinate dictionary — is presented as new at the *general product-CTMC* level with explicit boundary terms, and the authors are careful to position it against two close precursors: Generator Matching (Holderrieth et al. 2025), which already contains the marginal-generator-as-posterior-average and Bregman-gradient ideas at the matching-objective level but *without* the likelihood/ELBO side; and the *concurrent and independent* work of Gourevitch et al. [GJS⁺26], which derived the denoiser/leave-one-out/score conversions *specifically for uniform diffusion* (UDM) and reports empirical text-modeling gains. The honest framing — "our work obtains this result by a different route that places it within a general product-CTMC treatment" — is appropriate; the genuine novelty is the generality (any token-factorizable process), the exact-ELBO-with-boundary-terms statement, the information-loss interpretation J⋆_t = −dI(Z₀;Zₜ)/dt, and the explicit divergence rate for UDM denoisers (Proposition 6). Risk: reviewers may view the contribution as a re-derivational synthesis whose sharpest empirical claim (cavity > denoiser for uniform) overlaps a concurrent paper.
- **Rigor.** High. Theorems state explicit support/regularity conditions (Assumptions 1–3); Theorem 1 has two proofs (infinitesimal-KL under Lemma 1's stronger boundedness conditions, and a Girsanov proof under weaker hypotheses); Theorem 3 has two proofs (assembly via Proposition 2 + Theorem 4, and a direct path-law proof avoiding the fixed-support assumption). Boundary conventions (0/0 := 0, Φ at the boundary) are handled. The fixed-support assumption (Assumption 3) is flagged as needed only for the information-loss identity, not for the Oracle Distance itself.
- **Reproducibility.** Mixed. The numerical section (§8) is fully specified — bigram seed, transition-matrix construction (row-softmax of N(0,1) at temperature 0.3), V=8, L=8, schedule α_t=1−t, window [0.02,0.98], DiT architecture (hidden 128, 4 heads, 3 blocks), Adam 10⁻³, batch 256, 8000 steps, 2500 ancestral samples over 256 steps — and the model is small enough to reproduce cheaply. However, **no code release is mentioned**, and every result is on a single toy model with H(q₀)=1.25 nats/token; the headline convert-or-pay table (Table 6) and the divergence claim (Proposition 6) are *not* validated at language-model scale by the authors themselves.
- **Significance.** Conceptually high for the discrete-diffusion subfield: it gives practitioners a crisp operational rule ("learn a head in the coordinate its loss optimizes, or convert analytically before sampling") and a calibration tool (the log V identity) that are immediately actionable. The cross-method ELBO comparability argument (keep boundary terms) is a genuinely useful methodological correction. Significance beyond the subfield is moderate — it is a foundational/interpretability contribution, not a capability advance.
- **Red flags.** (1) **No large-scale experiments.** The framework's central practical promise — that correct coordinate conversion improves uniform/GIDD training — is *asserted* but only demonstrated on an 8-token, length-8 toy bigram; the authors lean on the concurrent [GJS⁺26] for scale-level evidence, which is an unusual dependency for a paper's own central claim. (2) The "every identity verified numerically, without approximation" framing is slightly oversold: verification is on an exactly-solvable model where the oracle is in closed form, so the experiments confirm consistency rather than demonstrate empirical benefit. (3) A **missing ablation**: the convert-or-pay comparison (Table 6, Figure 5) is done at the *oracle* laws, not with trained networks of matched capacity, so it does not isolate whether conversion helps *learning* as opposed to merely *parameterization correctness*. (4) The teacher-forced caveat (§9) means the clean Oracle Distance identity does *not* directly describe sampling drift / exposure-bias behavior, which the authors reframe as a "finite-capacity generalization gap" — a reasonable but unproven reinterpretation.

## Strengths

- Mathematically rigorous and self-contained: full derivations, explicit regularity/support conditions, two independent proofs of the main theorem, careful handling of boundary conventions.
- A genuinely clarifying conceptual contribution: the title's question is answered crisply ("a discrete diffusion model learns the marginal reverse jump rate, and denoiser/cavity/score are three coordinates of this one object"), resolving long-standing folklore about why some parameterizations "mysteriously misbehave."
- Strong unifying power: recovers MDLM, D3PM, MD4, UDLM, GIDD, Duo, SEDD, RADD, TCSM from a single projection principle, with a clean dictionary (Tables 2–5).
- The information-theoretic interpretation (oracle cost = −dI(Z₀;Zₜ)/dt, universal floor = data entropy) and the connection to the Gaussian I-MMSE relation and the thermodynamic arrow of time (Remark 1) give the results depth beyond pure optimization theory.
- Practical, immediately usable outputs: the log V cavity calibration identity (Proposition 4), the keep-the-boundary-terms comparability rule, and the single operational practitioner rule at the end of §9.
- Honest, non-defensive positioning against concurrent work (GJS⁺26) and close precursors (Generator Matching, Target Concrete Score Matching).

## Limitations

- **Toy-model-only validation.** All quantitative results are on an exactly-solvable 8-token, length-8 bigram model. No language-model-scale training, no standard text benchmarks (e.g., LM1B, text8, OpenWebText), no comparison of generated-sample quality (BLEU, perplexity on held-out data) across coordinate choices. The authors explicitly flag this as a limitation and defer scale-level confirmation to future work / the concurrent [GJS⁺26].
- **Teacher-forced objective only.** The Oracle Distance identity and the entropy floor presuppose that reverse rates are regressed at noisy states zₜ ~ q_{t|0} drawn from the *true* forward process, not at the model's own self-generated (rollout) states. The framework therefore does not directly characterize sampling drift / exposure bias; the authors' reframing of drift as a "finite-capacity generalization gap" is plausible but not proven.
- **Convert-or-pay is shown at the oracle, not under learning.** Table 6 and Figure 5 use the exact oracle laws, so they demonstrate parameterization *correctness*, not that conversion improves *training efficiency or final sample quality* with finite-capacity networks — the practically important question.
- **Rate-support and fixed-support assumptions.** The information-loss identity (Theorem 4) requires Assumption 3 (piecewise-continuous bounded rates with fixed support), satisfied by masked/uniform/GIDD but potentially restrictive for adaptive or data-dependent noising schedules.
- **No study of the variance/conditioning of the converted objectives** in actual gradient-based training; the divergence result (Proposition 6) is an *initialization* pathology, and whether well-initialized denoiser heads remain competitively trainable is not addressed.
- **Dependence on a concurrent paper** [GJS⁺26] for the only scale-level empirical support of the central practical claim, which weakens the paper's self-contained empirical case.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the convert-or-pay advantage of the cavity coordinate (Table 6) persist at language-model scale when heads are *learned* rather than set to the oracle — i.e., does correct parameterization translate into better sample quality (perplexity, MAUVE) on benchmarks like LM1B/OpenWebText, or merely into a correctly-specified but equally-performant objective?
- Can the denoiser-coordinate divergence of Proposition 6 be *repaired* by masking the denoiser head to the reverse support S_i (as hinted in §6.3), and does that recover cavity-level stability without an explicit cavity reparameterization?
- The Oracle Distance identity is derived under teacher-forced states zₜ ~ q_{t|0}. Can it be extended — even approximately — to characterize the path KL at *self-generated* rollout states, giving a principled bound on sampling drift / exposure bias rather than the current "generalization gap" reinterpretation?
- The information-uniform clock τ(t) = H(Z₀|Zₜ)/H(q₀) (§5.4, end) straightens the information-loss curve; does training on this clock empirically reduce ELBO estimator variance and improve final likelihood compared to variance-optimal IS, across noising processes?
- The framework cleanly separates reverse-rate error from sampling factorization error (Figure 6). Can a corrector / self-correcting sampler be designed that directly minimizes the *factorization* term while leaving the (already-optimal) reverse rate untouched, and how does its NFE–quality trade-off compare to τ-leaping / Tweedie schemes?
- Remark 2 shows the Oracle Distance is not specific to finite state spaces and gives the continuous-diffusion analogue. Does the analogous denoiser/cavity/score distinction bite for continuous diffusion (e.g., flow matching vs. denoiser-score matching), and does it predict a corresponding parameterization pathology?
- Boundary-term comparability (§7, Table 5) is needed to compare ELBOs across noising processes. How large is the silent bias in published discrete-diffusion ELBO numbers that drop these terms, and can the paper's explicit formulas be used to *retroactively correct* reported numbers in the literature?
