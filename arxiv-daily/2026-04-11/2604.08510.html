What do Language Models Learn and When? The Implicit Curriculum Hypothesis

一句话总结： 提出隐式课程假设（Implicit Curriculum Hypothesis），发现预训练中技能涌现遵循组合性且可预测的顺序（跨模型对ρ=0.81），且可通过模型内部表示空间预测未见过任务的训练轨迹（R²=0.68-0.84）。
TL;DR: Proposes the Implicit Curriculum Hypothesis showing that skill emergence during pretraining follows a compositional, predictable order (ρ=0.81 across 45 model pairs), and that model representations can predict training trajectories of unseen tasks (R²=0.68-0.84).

核心贡献

  • 提出隐式课程假设：预训练遵循组合性和可预测的课程，跨模型和数据混合保持一致
  • 设计了涵盖检索、形态变换、共指、逻辑推理和数学的可组合任务套件
  • 发现技能涌现顺序在4个模型家族（410M-13B参数）间高度一致（ρ=0.81）
  • 利用表示空间中的函数向量，可预测未见过组合任务的训练轨迹（R²=0.68-0.84）

Key Contributions

  • Proposes and validates the Implicit Curriculum Hypothesis for pretraining skill emergence
  • Designs a suite of composable tasks spanning retrieval, morphology, coreference, logic, and math
  • Shows emergence orderings are strikingly consistent (ρ=0.81 across 45 model pairs, 410M-13B params)
  • Predicts held-out compositional task trajectories from representations (R²=0.68-0.84)

Methodology

The authors design composable probe tasks, track emergence points across 4 model families (410M-13B), measure consistency of emergence ordering via Spearman correlation, and use function vector representations to predict training trajectories of held-out tasks without evaluating them.

Results & Highlights

  • Emergence ordering consistency: ρ=0.81 across 45 model pairs
  • Composite tasks emerge after their component tasks
  • Tasks with similar function vector representations follow similar training trajectories
  • R²=0.68-0.84 for predicting held-out task trajectories from representations

Authors & Credibility

Neubig (CMU) is a highly cited NLP researcher with extensive publications. The team includes researchers from established NLP labs. High credibility.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: High — the Implicit Curriculum Hypothesis is a significant conceptual contribution with strong empirical support. Rigor: Excellent — tested across 4 model families with 45 model pairs, compositional task design. The R² values for prediction are convincing. Significance: Could fundamentally change how we understand pretraining dynamics and plan training curricula. Red flags: Limited to relatively small models (up to 13B) — unclear if findings scale to frontier models. The task suite may not capture all aspects of real-world capabilities.

Strengths

  • Strong conceptual contribution with excellent empirical validation
  • Composable task design enables systematic analysis
  • Practical implications for predicting and controlling training dynamics

Limitations

  • Tested up to 13B parameters only; scaling to frontier models uncertain
  • Task suite may not represent all real-world capabilities
  • The "curriculum" may be an artifact of the specific task design

Relevance

直接研究训练动态中技能涌现的规律和可预测性，与用户关注的LLM训练稳定性和可观测性方向高度相关，为理解预训练过程提供了新的理论框架。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the Implicit Curriculum Hypothesis hold for 70B+ parameter models?
  • Can this framework be used to diagnose training issues (e.g., stalled learning)?
