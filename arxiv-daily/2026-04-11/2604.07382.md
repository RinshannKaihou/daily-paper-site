# Latent Structure of Affective Representations in Large Language Models

> **一句话总结：** 以情感处理为测试床，发现 Gemma-2-9B、Mistral-7B、LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct 学到的情感表示与心理学效价-唤醒模型（ANEW）显著对齐（Procrustes R² 排列检验 p<0.05），呈抛物线（"V"形）几何但可被线性良好近似，且可用超平面距离实现校准的不确定性量化（Gemma ECE=0.011，Mistral ECE=0.007）。

> **TL;DR:** Probing mean-pooled hidden states from Gemma-2-9B (42 layers), Mistral-7B (32 layers), and LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct on the GoEmotions dataset (58,009 Reddit comments, 27 emotion categories), the authors show via Procrustes-aligned classical MDS that LLM affective representations significantly match the ANEW valence-arousal benchmark (36/42 Gemma layers, 17/32 Mistral layers, 34/80 LLaMA layers at p<0.05 vs. 2000-permutation null), exhibit only modest nonlinearity (geodesic/Euclidean distance ratios 1.00–1.80 in Gemma), and yield calibrated uncertainty models (Gemma: 77.6% acc / 0.813 AUC vs. 69.4% majority baseline, ECE=0.011; Mistral: 85.7% acc / 0.871 AUC vs. 82.2% baseline, ECE=0.007).

## 核心贡献

- 证明 LLM（Gemma-2-9B、Mistral-7B、LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct）在零样本情感分类中形成与心理学效价-唤醒模型（ANEW 基准）显著对齐的潜在表示，排列检验 p<0.05
- 发现这些表示呈抛物线（"V"形）非线性几何但可被线性良好近似：Isomap 相对经典 MDS 仅在 rank-1 上提升可信度（Gemma +0.155，Mistral +0.099），高秩下差异 <0.03，为线性表示假设提供有条件实证支持
- 利用超平面距离实现校准的预测不确定性量化：误分类激活更靠近分离超平面，校准模型 ECE 低至 0.007–0.011
- 附录提供两条补充证据：人类 EEG 数据（FACED，123 受试者）再现抛物线情感布局；探针方向可用于因果情感转向

## Key Contributions

- Shows Gemma-2-9B, Mistral-7B, and LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct develop coherent internal affective representations that significantly align with the ANEW valence-arousal benchmark (Procrustes R² permutation test, p<0.05: 36/42 Gemma layers, 17/32 Mistral layers, 34/80 LLaMA layers)
- Finds the affective geometry exhibits modest nonlinearity consistent with the parabolic ("V"-shaped) valence-arousal layout, but is well-approximated linearly — conditional empirical support for the linear representation hypothesis (Isomap improves over MDS only at rank-1: +0.155 Gemma, +0.099 Mistral; differences <0.03 at higher ranks)
- Demonstrates a geometry-based uncertainty quantification method using activation-to-hyperplane distances that achieves strong calibration (ECE 0.011 Gemma, 0.007 Mistral) and beats majority-class baselines
- Provides corroborating evidence from human EEG data (FACED, 123 subjects) showing similar parabolic affective layout, plus causal steering experiments (Appendix D)

## Methodology

**Models:** Gemma-2-9B (9B params, 42 layers, Google DeepMind), Mistral-7B (7B params, 32 layers, Mistral AI), LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct (70B, instruction-tuned). Run at float16 with AutoModelForCausalLM. Qwen2.5-7B and LLaMA-2-7B were tried but excluded for insufficient correct classifications (>100 across >3 categories). Compute: NVIDIA H200 GPUs, PyTorch 2.5.1, CUDA 12.1.

**Dataset:** GoEmotions — 58,009 English Reddit comments, 27 fine-grained emotion categories + Neutral (12 positive, 11 negative, 4 ambiguous), annotated by 3–5 raters (82 total). Restricted to single-label examples with multi-rater agreement (~83%). Per-class correct sample counts range 101–2,212 (Gemma) and 103–1,293 (Mistral); analyses restricted to emotions with ≥100 correct classifications → 20 emotions for Gemma, 16 for Mistral.

**Probing pipeline:** Zero-shot (no fine-tuning) emotion classification with shuffled GoEmotions choice list to reduce positional bias. Mean-pooled hidden states extracted from every transformer layer via forward hooks (one vector per layer per sample). Pairwise balanced logistic regression (L2-regularized, 80:20 train-test split) between emotion pairs; test accuracy used as dissimilarity Dij = accij (robustness checked with cosine distance, hyperplane distance, alternative transforms).

**Geometric analysis:** Classical MDS (linear Euclidean) and Isomap (nonlinear, kNN graph + geodesic distances) on the dissimilarity matrix. Statistical alignment with ANEW valence-arousal scores via scaled orthogonal Procrustes; significance via 2000-permutation label-shuffle null. Eigenspectrum analysis with permutation-based eigengap test (φ<0.05).

**Uncertainty quantification:** Misclassified activations (linked to two emotions: output and ground-truth) shown to lie near the separating hyperplane. Second-round logistic regression on per-layer activation-to-hyperplane distances, calibrated on validation data (60:20:20 split). 180 viable binary problems for Gemma (3,858 correct vs. 8,748 misclassified), 122 for Mistral (5,254 vs. 15,216).

## Results & Highlights

- **ANEW alignment (§4.3):** 36/42 Gemma-2-9B layers and 17/32 Mistral-7B layers (including all final 14 Mistral layers) exhibit statistically significant alignment with ANEW valence-arousal scores (Procrustes R², p<0.05 vs. 2000-permutation null). LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct replicates: 34/80 layers significant at p2D<0.05, concentrated in later layers.
- **Separability (§4.2):** Gemma-2-9B mean pairwise test accuracies >0.9 across all layers vs. ~0.55–0.89 for Mistral-7B; zero-shot emotion classification accuracy 19.4% (Gemma), 13.7% (Mistral), 21.3% (LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct). Separability peaks at middle layers, slight drop at final layers.
- **Nonlinearity is modest (§5):** Geodesic/Euclidean distance ratios span 1.00–1.80 (10th–90th pct, Gemma) and 0.80–1.42 (Mistral). Isomap vs. MDS trustworthiness differences <0.03 at ranks 2–8 except rank-1 (Gemma +0.155, Mistral +0.099; Table 1 also reports +0.161/+0.127). Participation ratio is high and diffuse (~17 Gemma, ~9–14 Mistral), not low-dimensional. 16/42 Gemma layers show a significant first eigengap (k=1, φ<0.05), consistent with a dominant valence-like axis.
- **Uncertainty quantification (§6):** Gemma-2-9B: 77.6% accuracy, 0.813 AUC-ROC, ECE=0.011 vs. 69.4% majority baseline. Mistral-7B: 85.7% accuracy, 0.871 AUC-ROC, ECE=0.007 vs. 82.2% baseline. LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct: 80.1% accuracy, 0.822 AUC-ROC, ECE=0.011 vs. 75.6% baseline. Misclassified activations lie closer to the separating hyperplane than correct ones (not tautological — evaluated on held-out data).
- **EEG corroboration (Appendix B):** FACED dataset (123 subjects, 32 EEG channels, 28 clips, 9 emotions) reproduces the parabolic "V"-shaped affective layout via the same pairwise-logistic-regression + MDS pipeline.
- **Robustness (Appendix A.2):** Cosine-distance dissimilarity replicates the Procrustes R² ANEW alignment across all layers; UMAP (Appendix A.1) recovers the same semantic clustering (41 significant Gemma layers, 10 Mistral, 34 LLaMA at p<0.05).

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Moderate-to-high — using psychology's well-validated valence-arousal model (ANEW) as ground truth for probing LLM representational geometry is a clever validation strategy, and the uncertainty-quantification-by-hyperplane-distance application is a nice practical twist. The linear-vs-nonlinear finding is nuanced (modest nonlinearity, linear approximability holds) rather than a clean win for either hypothesis.

**Paper-specific weaknesses:**
1. The "alignment with ANEW" result is statistically significant but the paper does not report the actual Procrustes R² values in the main text (only p-values and layer counts); Figure 9 in the appendix shows R² values but the main text relies on layer-count significance, making effect size hard to assess. A model could align significantly yet explain little variance.
2. The dissimilarity metric Dij = accij couples the inferred geometry to the supervised probing pipeline, and the authors acknowledge only 1–2 negative eigenvalues per layer (<1% variance) — but the participation ratio is high and diffuse (~17 Gemma, ~9–14 Mistral), meaning the "structure" is a weak signal atop a high-rank bulk, not a clean low-dimensional manifold. The eigengap test finds a significant k=1 gap in only 16/42 Gemma layers and does not establish a fixed intrinsic dimension.
3. Generalization is limited to three models that passed an emotion-recognition accuracy bar; Qwen2.5-7B and LLaMA-2-7B were excluded for insufficient correct classifications, raising selection bias concerns — the affective geometry may be an artifact of models that happen to classify emotions well.
4. The "practical implications for model transparency and AI safety" are asserted but the safety relevance is thin: the steering experiments are in an appendix and the uncertainty method is demonstrated only on emotion classification, not on safety-critical tasks.
5. The EEG parallel (Appendix B) is based on a single dataset with acknowledged limitations (no shared-cognitive-encoding evidence, cultural biases unaddressed).

## Strengths

- Rigorous statistical framework: permutation-based Procrustes alignment test (2000 shuffles), eigengap permutation null, multiple dissimilarity metrics for robustness
- Cross-model, cross-scale, cross-training-regime replication (7B–70B, base and instruction-tuned)
- Interdisciplinary validation: ANEW ground truth + human EEG corroboration + causal steering
- Honest nuance about nonlinearity — does not overclaim a clean low-dimensional manifold
- Well-calibrated uncertainty quantification with low ECE (0.007–0.011) and held-out evaluation

## Limitations

- Effect sizes (Procrustes R²) not reported in main text; significance-by-layer-count may overstate alignment strength
- High, diffuse participation ratio means structure is a weak signal over a high-rank bulk
- Model selection bias: only models passing an emotion-accuracy bar were analyzed
- Safety implications asserted but not demonstrated on safety-critical tasks
- Single EEG dataset for the human-cognition parallel; cultural/linguistic biases unaddressed
- GoEmotions is English Reddit text — generalization to other languages/modalities untested

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the parabolic valence-arousal geometry generalize beyond emotion to other psychologically-grounded semantic dimensions (e.g., moral dimensions, power/distance in social relations)?
- Can the hyperplane-distance uncertainty method extend to safety-critical classification (e.g., harmful-content detection) where calibration matters most?
- Why do Qwen2.5-7B and LLaMA-2-7B fail the emotion-accuracy bar — is the affective geometry absent, or merely encoded in a different layer/probe setup?
- Does instruction tuning (LLaMA-3-70B-Instruct) reshape the affective geometry relative to base models, and does this affect steering reliability?
