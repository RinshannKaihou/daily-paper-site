SAGE: Sign-Adaptive Gradient for Memory-Efficient LLM Optimization

Wooin Lee, Hyuntae Kim

arXiv: 2604.07663

Date: 2026-04-11

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07663

SAGE: Sign-Adaptive Gradient for Memory-Efficient LLM Optimization

一句话总结： SAGE针对嵌入层的高方差稀疏梯度（即"embedding layer dilemma"），用一个O(d)的有界自适应阻尼尺度Ht（||Ht||_∞ ≤ 1.0）替代Lion静态1.0尺度，在LLaMA架构（270M/0.6B/1.3B）上以更低显存达到更低测试困惑度（1.3B: 24.33 PPL vs AdamW 27.81）。
TL;DR: SAGE is a Lion-style sign-based optimizer that replaces Lion's static 1.0 update scale with a memory-efficient O(d) adaptive damper Ht, provably bounded by 1.0, which tames sparse high-variance embedding gradients that defeat stateless optimizers like SinkGD-Pure (which blows up to 98.84 PPL on 1.3B). In a SinkGD hybrid, SAGE replaces AdamW on embeddings; on LLaMA 1.3B pretrained on a 6.6B-token Pile subset it reaches test PPL 24.33 vs Lion-Hybrid 28.40, SinkGD-Hybrid 28.71, and AdamW 27.81, while cutting optimizer-state memory from AdamW's 9.83 GB to 0.98 GB at 1.3B.

核心贡献

  • 识别并形式化"嵌入层困境"（embedding layer dilemma）：无状态/轻状态优化器（如SinkGD-Pure）在嵌入层稀疏高方差梯度（Zipf分布）下学习效率严重退化，迫使现有混合方法退回AdamW，部分抵消显存收益。
  • 提出SAGE：Lion式符号更新方向 + 新的O(d)维度级自适应尺度Ht，作为"安全阻尼器"，可证明被1.0上界约束（Lemma 3.2），只在"高方差"维度施加阻尼，安静维度默认Lion行为。
  • 设计混合优化器配置：嵌入层与1D参数用SAGE，2D稠密权重用SinkGD（去除sqrt(nm)缩放改为unit-norm），相比AdamW混合大幅省显存。
  • 提供收敛性证明（Theorem 3.3）：在L-平滑、有界方差、有界梯度标准假设下SAGE收敛到驻点，且其更新幅度不超过Lion（更安全）。

Key Contributions

  • Formalizes the "embedding layer dilemma": stateless light-state optimizers (SinkGD-Pure) catastrophically fail on embeddings (e.g., 192.7 PPL at 270M, 98.84 at 1.3B) due to sparse Zipf-distributed gradients, forcing prior hybrids to fall back to memory-heavy AdamW for embeddings.
  • Proposes SAGE, a Lion generalization: keeps Lion's sign direction Ct but replaces its static 1.0 scale with an O(d) adaptive scale Ht derived from an EMA of mean-absolute gradients (L1 norm) relative to the layer RMS, provably bounded ||Ht||_∞ ≤ 1.0.
  • Proves Ht is non-amplifying (Lemma 3.2) and that SAGE converges to a stationary point under standard assumptions (Theorem 3.3), with updates provably no larger than Lion's.
  • Designs a hybrid: SAGE for embeddings + 1D params, unit-norm SinkGD for 2D dense weights, eliminating the AdamW dependency and cutting dominant embedding-state memory by ~50%.

Methodology

SAGE algorithm (Algorithm 1): decoupled weight decay theta <- theta·(1-eta·w); compute gradient gt; for 2D embedding params compute per-dimension L1 mean st_j = (1/V) Σ |gt,ij| across vocabulary, for 1D params st = |gt|; update EMA St = beta2·St-1 + (1-beta2)·st with bias correction to get St_hat; compute layer RMS references sigma_rms (over St_hat) and gamma_rms (over st); per-dimension dampers D_ema_j = sigma_rms/(St_hat_j + eps), D_inst_j = gamma_rms/(st_j + eps); adaptive scale Ht_j = min(D_ema_j, D_inst_j, 1) — instantaneous constraint acts as a circuit breaker on spikes; direction Ct = sign(beta1·Mt-1 + (1-beta1)·gt); update theta_t = theta_t-1 - eta · (Ct ⊙ Ht); momentum Mt = beta2·Mt-1 + (1-beta2)·gt. Defaults: beta1=0.9, beta2=0.99, wd=0.01. Hybrid config: SAGE on embeddings+biases/norms, unit-norm SinkGD (sqrt(nm) scaling removed) on 2D dense weights, controlled by scalar alpha≈10. Models: LLaMA-architecture 270M (h=1024, 13 layers, vocab 128256, emb=48% params), 0.6B (emb=33%), 1.3B (h=2048, 24 layers, emb=20%). Trained from scratch on a 6.6B-token importance-resampled subset of The Pile, bfloat16, single NVIDIA H200, global batch 130k tokens (seq 512), cosine LR with 10% warmup. Baselines: AdamW, Lion, Lion-Hybrid, SinkGD-Hybrid, SinkGD-Pure, SAGE-Pure, APOLLO. SAGE LR grid [1e-4, 2e-4, 5e-4, 1e-3, 2e-3]; Lion-Hybrid given same LRs; 3 seeds averaged.

Results & Highlights

  • Test PPL at 1.3B (Table 2): SAGE-Hybrid 24.33 vs Lion-Hybrid 28.40 (-4.07), SinkGD-Hybrid 28.71 (-4.38), AdamW 27.81 (-3.48), Lion 28.37; at 0.6B SAGE 26.71 vs AdamW 31.74; at 270M SAGE 29.95 vs AdamW 37.35.
  • Embedding-dilemma evidence (Table 2): SinkGD-Pure collapses to 192.7 PPL (270M), 117.8 (0.6B), 98.84 (1.3B); SAGE-Pure (all params) also fails (116.0 / 179.0 / 216.0), justifying the hybrid design.
  • Memory (1.3B, Table 4): SAGE-Hybrid optimizer state 0.979 GB vs AdamW 9.833 GB (-90%), vs SinkGD-Hybrid 1.958 GB (-50%); total VRAM 10.81 GB vs AdamW 19.67 GB (-45%), matching Lion-Hybrid 10.81 GB and SinkGD-Pure 9.83 GB.
  • LR stability (Sec 4.4): Lion-Hybrid peaks at LR 1e-4 and collapses beyond; SAGE stays stable and peaks at 1e-3 (10x higher), demonstrating the safe-damper unlocks more aggressive LRs.
  • SAGE remains stable at effective batch size <10 without LR retuning, whereas SinkGD requires re-tuning (Appendix A.4).
  • Internal-state visualization (Fig 3): Ht is a sparse damper — most dimensions sit at 1.0 (Lion default), with colored stripes marking the few persistently high-variance dimensions damped below 1.0; early-training damping is dense, later becomes sparse/surgical; PCA of Ht shows a two-phase "excursion and return" trajectory (Fig 4).

Critical Assessment

The embedding-layer dilemma is a real, well-motivated observation and the SinkGD-Pure collapse numbers (192.7 PPL) make the case convincingly. The SAGE design is clean and the boundedness proof (Lemma 3.2, Theorem 3.3) is correct under its assumptions. A significant weakness is scale: the largest model is 1.3B and training is only 6.6B tokens on a single GPU — the authors themselves flag that "further validation on larger scales (e.g., 7B+) is necessary." The perplexity gains are reported on a single dataset (The Pile subset) with no downstream-task or zero-shot evaluation, so it is unclear whether the PPL advantage transfers to capability. The strongest omitted baseline is Muon (Jordan et al. 2024), which the authors acknowledge but exclude citing tuning cost — a non-trivial gap given Muon's current prominence. The "effective throughput" metric is defined relative to Lion-Hybrid's final PPL, so the reported efficiency ratios are explicitly tied to this specific horizon and the authors admit they "may represent conservative estimates." The Theorem 3.3 convergence proof leans on sign-based-optimizer frameworks and its assumptions (bounded gradients, L-smoothness) are standard but the bound is asymptotic with no concrete rate. Single-author-pair provenance with no affiliation warrants extra scrutiny of reproducibility despite the public code.

Strengths

  • Cleanly identifies and demonstrates the embedding-layer dilemma with concrete failure numbers.
  • SAGE is a principled, minimal generalization of Lion with a provably bounded adaptive scale.
  • Convergence and boundedness proofs provided.
  • Large memory savings at 1.3B (optimizer state -90% vs AdamW, total -45%).
  • Allows 10x higher LR than Lion-Hybrid and is robust to small batch sizes.
  • Public code, honest limitations section, sparse-damping visualization and PCA analysis of Ht dynamics.

Limitations

  • Scale capped at 1.3B; no 7B+ validation (authors acknowledge).
  • Single dataset (Pile subset), 6.6B tokens only; no downstream/zeroshot eval.
  • Muon baseline omitted despite acknowledged relevance.
  • Two unaffiliated authors; reproducibility depends on the public repo.
  • Effective-throughput metric is relative to a chosen baseline/horizon.
  • Hyperparameters (alpha, beta1/beta2, eps, the instantaneous damper) add tuning surface beyond Lion.
