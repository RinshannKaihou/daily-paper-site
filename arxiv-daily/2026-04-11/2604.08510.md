# What Do Language Models Learn and When? The Implicit Curriculum Hypothesis

> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"隐式课程假说"——预训练中技能以跨模型一致、且尊重组合依赖的顺序涌现；用 91 个可组合任务在 9 个模型（410M–13B，4 个家族）上验证：绝对阈值下的涌现顺序 Spearman ρ = .81（45 对模型，p < 10⁻⁷），组合任务多在其组件之后涌现；并用 function vector 表示空间留一预测 held-out 组合任务的训练轨迹，R² = .68–.84。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes the Implicit Curriculum Hypothesis: during pretraining, skills emerge in a stable, compositional order consistent across models. Tests it with 91 composable tasks (53 elemental + 38 composite) spanning string ops, morphology, translation, retrieval, coreference, logic, and math, across 9 models from 4 families (410M–13B: OLMo-2 1B/7B/13B, OLMo-3 7B, LLM360 Amber/Crystal 7B, Pythia 410M/1.4B/2.8B/12B), ~20 checkpoints each over the first 1T tokens. Emergence orderings (absolute 80%-accuracy threshold) correlate at Spearman ρ = .81 across all 45 model pairs (range .64–.93, all p < 10⁻⁷); 54/76 composite tasks emerge no earlier than their parents. Function-vector (Todd et al. 2024) similarity predicts held-out composite trajectories via kernel ridge regression with R² = .68–.84 across models (per-task R² up to .99); restricting the basis to elementals only degrades MAE by +0.135 on average, indicating a composition bottleneck.

## 核心贡献

- 提出"隐式课程假说"（Implicit Curriculum Hypothesis）：预训练遵循跨模型一致、且反映组合依赖的可预测课程，比"quanta 假说"更强（不仅简单先于复杂，且顺序可复现、反映组合依赖）
- 设计 91 个可组合任务套件（53 个元素任务 + 38 个组合任务），覆盖字符串操作、形态变换、翻译、知识检索、共指、逻辑、数学，组合任务的依赖关系由构造精确已知
- 在 9 个模型（410M–13B，4 个家族，含不同数据混合）上验证：绝对阈值下涌现顺序 Spearman ρ = .81（45 对，p < 10⁻⁷），组合任务多在组件之后涌现
- 用 function vector 表示空间留一预测 held-out 组合任务训练轨迹，R² = .68–.84，无需在训练中评估该任务

## Key Contributions

- Formulates the Implicit Curriculum Hypothesis (H1 compositional ordering, H2 cross-model stability, H3 representational alignment), a stronger claim than the quanta hypothesis: pretraining follows a reproducible, compositional skill order across models and data mixtures
- Designs a suite of 91 composable tasks (53 elemental + 38 composite) with mechanically guaranteed prerequisite structure, spanning retrieval, morphology, translation, coreference, logic, and math; exact-match accuracy evaluation
- Empirically shows emergence orderings (absolute threshold) are strikingly consistent: Spearman ρ = .81 mean across 45 model pairs (range .64–.93, all p < 10⁻⁷), including cross-family and different-data-mixture comparisons; 54/76 composites emerge no earlier than their parents
- Demonstrates function-vector representational geometry predicts held-out composite task trajectories throughout pretraining via leave-one-out kernel ridge regression (R² = .68–.84 across models, per-task up to .99), without evaluating the held-out task during training

## Methodology

**Models & checkpoints (§3.1).** 9 models from 4 families, all with publicly released intermediate checkpoints: OLMo-2 (1B, 7B, 13B), OLMo-3 (7B), LLM360 Crystal (7B, code-oriented) and Amber (7B, natural-language-oriented), Pythia (410M, 1.4B, 2.8B, 12B). ~20 checkpoints sampled over the first 1T training tokens (~every 20B tokens). Models below 410M excluded for poor performance.

**Task design (§3.2).** 53 elemental tasks: string ops (copy, uppercase, lowercase, first/last letter, reversal), morphology (present→gerund, singular→plural), translation (en-fr, fr-en, en-sp, sp-en), world knowledge (country→capital, country→currency), arithmetic (basic, multi-step 2/3, TextFRCT RG1-3), logic (negation, conjunction, conditional, TextFRCT RL1/3/4), reading comprehension (fact extraction, coreference simple/hard, ignoring context, IOI, part of speech), and psychometric TextFRCT tasks (verbal closure, induction, associative memory, verbal comprehension). 38 composite tasks built by chaining elementals (e.g., gerund upper = present→gerund then uppercase; translate eng fr upper reverse). Exact-match accuracy; full list in App. D.

**Emergence definition (§3.3).** Absolute threshold: first checkpoint exceeding fixed θ_abs (0.5 and 0.8 reported). Relative threshold: first checkpoint reaching α fraction of the model's best performance. Tasks never emerging are binned at 1001B tokens.

**Function vectors (§3.4, Todd et al. 2024).** Two extraction methods, best one used per model: (1) head-based via causal indirect effect (CIE) to select a sparse same-block set of attention heads, averaged over correctly-answered ICL prompts; (2) hidden-state extraction, averaging post-MLP block-output hidden state at last non-pad token position t_last over correct prompts. Task similarity = cosine similarity between FVs.

**LOO trajectory prediction (§3.5, H3).** Leave-one-out over 26 composite tasks. Unit-normalized FVs, RBF kernel. Kernel ridge regression: α_t = (K_S + λI)⁻¹ y_t, predict â_c(t) = k_c^T α_t. Trajectories interpolated onto held-out task's token grid, Gaussian smoothing σ=1.0, near-zero-variance tasks discarded. Two basis conditions: "All tasks" (elementals + composites minus held-out) vs "Simple only" (elementals only). Quality: per-task Pearson r² and MAE vs smoothed ground truth.

**Emergence order tests.** H1 violation rate: fraction of (composite, prerequisite, model) triples where t*_prereq > t*_composite. H2: Spearman ρ between emergence orderings σ_m1, σ_m2 over all 45 model pairs.

## Results & Highlights

- **Cross-model emergence consistency (Tbl. 2, absolute threshold 0.8):** Spearman ρ across all 45 model pairs ranges .64–.93, mean .81, all p < 10⁻⁷. Within OLMo-2 family .72–.93; cross-family e.g. Amber vs OLMo-2 models .82–.88, OLMo-2 vs Pythia-410M .64–.84. Order: copying and simple coreference first, then simple ICL (upper/lowercase), morphology, knowledge-dependent translation, then multistep arithmetic and compositional tasks.
- **Compositional ordering (§4, H1):** 54/76 compositional tasks emerged no earlier than their parent tasks; 19 weak inversions (composite before one parent) and 3 strong inversions (before both), all three involving the first-letter component task.
- **Absolute vs. relative threshold (Tbl. 6):** absolute θ=0.5 gives mean ρ = 0.860 (min .597, max .955); absolute θ=0.8 stable-3 gives mean 0.790. Relative thresholds collapse: α=0.5 mean ρ = 0.528 (min .085), α=0.8 mean 0.500 (min .077). The authors argue absolute thresholds (above chance) capture when task-relevant circuits form, while relative thresholds conflate with a model's maximum performance.
- **LOO trajectory prediction (Tbl. 3, "All tasks" basis):** R² ranges .67 (Crystal) to .838 (OLMo2-13B); MAE .068–.195 on 0–1 accuracy. Per-task examples (OLMo2-7B, Fig. 3): fr eng upper R² = .99 / MAE .017; plural lower R² = .89 / MAE .028; eng fr upper R² = .51 / MAE .068 (weaker).
- **Composition bottleneck (Tbl. 3, "Sim. only" basis):** restricting the basis to elemental tasks only degrades MAE for every model, mean ΔMAE = +0.135 (e.g., Crystal MAE .133→.315, OLMo3-7B .079→.215), indicating composite trajectories share structure with each other not captured by their elemental components alone.

## Critical Assessment

- **Consistency hinges on the absolute-threshold definition.** The headline ρ = .81 holds only under absolute accuracy thresholds; under relative thresholds it collapses to ~0.50 (Tbl. 6, min .077). The authors argue absolute thresholds capture circuit formation, but this is a post-hoc rationalization — the result is fragile to the emergence definition, and the choice of θ (0.5 vs 0.8) changes the numbers (0.860 vs 0.790).
- **The "compositional ordering" claim is violated in 22/76 cases.** 19 weak + 3 strong inversions (including all 3 strong inversions involving first-letter) mean ~29% of composite-prerequisite triples violate the predicted ordering, which is a substantial exception that the paper underplays.
- **Task suite is artificial and may not reflect real pretraining skills.** The 91 tasks are hand-designed probes (string ops, synthetic translation chains, psychometric FRCT items), not the actual skills web pretraining rewards. The paper itself notes (§3.2) it does not claim these "are the true minimal units of model computation," so the external validity of the "curriculum" for realistic capabilities (GSM8k-style reasoning, code generation) is unproven.
- **Small-model bias.** All models are ≤13B and the smallest (Pythia-410M) drags down several cross-family correlations; the emergence order on frontier-scale models (70B+) is untested, and several tasks never emerge for the smallest models, which the binning-at-1001B convention handles but coarsely.
- **LOO prediction R² varies a lot per task.** Per-task R² ranges from .51 (eng fr upper) to .99 (fr eng upper) on OLMo2-7B, so the .68–.84 mean masks substantial task-level unreliability; prediction works best for rule-based composites and worse for knowledge-dependent ones.

## Strengths

- Clean, falsifiable hypothesis (H1/H2/H3) with explicit violation-rate and rank-correlation tests, not just qualitative emergence curves
- Broad model coverage (4 families, 410M–13B, different data mixtures including code vs natural-language) with ~20 checkpoints each over 1T tokens — a real data-engineering effort
- The function-vector → trajectory prediction (H3) is a genuinely novel link between representational geometry and training dynamics, with a held-out-task protocol and a composition-bottleneck control
- Code and task suite released; psychometric TextFRCT tasks add a principled skill source

## Limitations

- Cross-model consistency holds only under absolute thresholds; relative thresholds collapse to ~0.50
- 22/76 composite-prerequisite triples violate the compositional ordering, including all 3 strong inversions involving first-letter
- Artificial probe tasks may not reflect realistic pretraining skills; external validity to GSM8k/code is unproven
- Only ≤13B models; frontier-scale untested; smallest models never solve many tasks and are binned
- Per-task LOO R² is highly variable (.51–.99); prediction is least reliable for knowledge-dependent composites
- ~20 checkpoints over 1T tokens gives ~20B-token granularity, which may miss sharp transitions the paper itself cites (Kangaslahti et al. 2025)

## Questions for Further Exploration

- 绝对阈值下 ρ=.81、相对阈值下 ρ≈.50 的巨大差异，是否意味着"涌现顺序一致性"本质上依赖于"何为涌现"的定义？能否用一个不依赖阈值的参数化学习曲线拟合来仲裁？
- 22/76 个组合-前置违反（含 3 个强反演）背后是否有共同机制？为何 first-letter 反复成为强反演源？
- 人工 probe 任务上的课程顺序，能否推广到 GSM8k、HumanEval 这类真实下游基准？是否可以用同一 function-vector 框架预测它们的涌现？
- function-vector 预测轨迹在知识依赖型组合任务上较差（R²=.51），是否因为知识本身在预训练数据中的暴露时序与规则无关？能否加入数据混合特征改进预测？
- 该框架能否用于预训练监控——当某模型的某技能"落后于课程"时预警，甚至指导数据混合调整以补齐？
