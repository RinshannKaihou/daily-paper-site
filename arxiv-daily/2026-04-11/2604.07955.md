# Rethinking Residual Errors in Compensation-based LLM Quantization

> **一句话总结：** 本文指出 GPTQ/GPTAQ 在逐列量化时把"补偿后的权重输出"当作校准目标，偏离了原始全精度输出；由此重新定义残差为"层间传播误差 + 层内补偿感知误差（compensation-aware error）"，并用神经元分解高效地融入权重更新，在 Llama2-7B 3-bit W2A16 上将 GPTQ 的 C4 PPL 从 13.60 降到 8.34。

> **TL;DR:** This ICLR 2026 paper reformulates the residual error in compensation-based PTQ (GPTQ/GPTAQ) by aligning the quantized output strictly to the original full-precision output rather than to the compensated-weight output, and decomposes the new residual into the inter-layer propagated error r1 plus a novel intra-layer "compensation-aware error" r2. Integrated into GPTAQ via neuron decomposition, on Llama2-7B 3-bit weight-only it reduces C4 PPL from 13.60 (GPTQ) to 8.34 and improves average zero-shot accuracy from 64.9% to 66.5%; on Llama3.1-8B-Instruct W2A16 it raises average accuracy from 70.3% (GPTAQ) to 72.3%.

## 核心贡献

- 重新审视 GPTQ/GPTAQ 的逐列校准目标：指出其在 q≥1 步用"补偿后权重 w^(q) 的输出"而非"原始全精度权重 w^(0) 的输出"作为对齐目标，导致残差定义不精确。
- 推导出新的残差 r′ = r1 + r2：r1 是 GPTAQ 已有的层间输入误差，r2 是本文新定义的"补偿感知误差"——同一层内补偿权重与原始权重差异所引入的误差。
- 利用 GPTAQ 的神经元分解技术将 r2 高效融入权重更新，仅需预计算矩阵 P2 = (XX̃ᵀL) ⊙ MU Lᵀ，与现有 GPTQ/GPTAQ 流水线无缝集成，量化时间开销仅约 5%。
- 在 Llama2/3（1B–70B）、Qwen3、DeiT 视觉 Transformer 上广泛验证：3-bit/2-bit 权重量化及 W2A4KV4 权重激活量化均带来一致提升。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies a sub-optimal calibration objective in GPTQ/GPTAQ: during intra-layer iteration the column-level objective aligns to the output of compensated weights w^(q)X̃ rather than the true full-precision output w^(0)X̃, so the residual becomes inaccurate for q ≥ 1.
- Reformulates the residual as r′ = r1 + r2, where r1 = w^(q)(X̃ − X) is the inter-layer propagated input error (already in GPTAQ) and r2 = (w^(0) − w^(q))X̃ is a new "compensation-aware error" capturing the intra-layer discrepancy between original and compensated weights.
- Develops an efficient closed-form update using neuron decomposition (inherited from GPTAQ) that folds r2 into a single precomputed matrix P2 = (XX̃ᵀL) ⊙ MU Lᵀ, adding only ~5% quantization-time overhead and zero inference-time overhead.
- Demonstrates consistent gains by plugging the term into both GPTQ and GPTAQ across Llama2/3 (1B–70B), Qwen3 (4B/8B/14B), and DeiT vision transformers, under 3-bit and 2-bit weight-only as well as W2A4KV4 weight-activation settings.

## Methodology

The paper works within the compensation-based PTQ framework where a linear layer y = wX is quantized column by column, updating the remaining full-precision weights to compensate for each column's quantization error.

**Baseline recap.** GPTQ minimizes ‖ŵX − wX‖²_F using the Quant-flow input X throughout (Eqn. 2–4), with the inverse Hessian H⁻¹ = (XXᵀ)⁻¹ solved via Lagrange multipliers and made efficient by Cholesky reformulation and lazy batch updates. GPTAQ observes that using only the Quant-flow X causes inter-layer error accumulation, so it introduces an asymmetric calibration aligning the quantized output to the FP-flow output wX̃ (Eqn. 5–7), defining a residual r = w^(q):X̃_q: − w^(q)X_q: that adds a correction term rXᵀH⁻¹ to the standard update (Eqn. 8).

**The rethink.** The authors note GPTAQ's column-level objective (Eqn. 9) uses w^(q)X̃ as the target, which only equals the true full-precision output w^(0)X̃ at q = 0. For q ≥ 1 the compensated weights have drifted, so the proper objective is Eqn. 10: minimize ‖(w^(q) + Δw)X − w^(0)X̃‖²_F. Expanding yields the new residual r′ = (w^(0)X̃ − w^(q)X) = w^(q)(X̃ − X) + (w^(0) − w^(q))X̃ = r1 + r2. r2 is the compensation-aware error.

**Efficient solution.** Directly recomputing R1 = W^(q)(X̃ − X) and R2 = (W^(0) − W^(q))X̃ each step is costly. Following GPTAQ's neuron decomposition of R1 (R1 = Σ_q W_:,q ΔX_q:), they decompose R2 = Σ_q (W_:,q^(0) − W_:,q^(q)) X̃_q:. This lets the per-column update (Eqn. 21) reuse two precomputed matrices: P1 = (ΔXXᵀL) ⊙ MU Lᵀ (from GPTAQ) and a new P2 = (XX̃ᵀL) ⊙ MU Lᵀ, noting XX̃ᵀ = XXᵀ + ΔXXᵀ so no extra X̃ storage is needed. Baselines: AWQ, GPTQ, GPTAQ, plus QuaRot and SpinQuant rotations for W2A4KV4. ## Results & Highlights

- On Llama2-7B 3-bit weight-only, GPTQ+Ours reduces **C4 PPL from 13.60 (GPTQ) to 8.34** and raises average zero-shot accuracy from 64.9% to 66.5%; GPTAQ+Ours further reaches WikiText2 PPL 6.25 vs GPTAQ's 6.53.
- On Llama3.1-8B-Instruct 3-bit weight-only, GPTAQ+Ours raises **average zero-shot accuracy from 70.3% to 72.3%** and lowers WikiText2 PPL from 8.90 to 8.67; C4 from 13.85 to 13.79.
- On Llama2-13B 2-bit weight-only + QuaRot (Table 2), GPTAQ+Ours reduces **WikiText2 PPL from 7.50 to 7.32** and improves average accuracy from 55.8% to 58.2% over QuaRot+GPTAQ.
- On Llama2-13B W2A4KV4 + SpinQuant (Table 3), GPTAQ+Ours reduces **WikiText2 PPL from 9.55 to 8.60** and improves average accuracy from 50.2% to 52.2% over SpinQuant+GPTAQ; on Llama2-7B it reduces PPL from 11.6 to 11.1 and raises accuracy from 48.1% to 49.2%.
- On Qwen3-4B 2-bit weight-only (Table 9), GPTAQ+Ours cuts **C4 PPL from 57.1 to 39.9** and raises average accuracy from 45.6% to 52.2% — a large gain showing generalization beyond Llama.
- On DeiT-Base W2A4 (Table 7), Ours reaches **62.1% ImageNet accuracy** vs GPTAQ's 61.3% and GPTQ's 51.8%.
- Ablation (Table 4) isolates the new P2 term: adding it to GPTQ on Llama2-7B improves average accuracy from 44.9% to 47.5% and WikiText2 PPL from 19.0 to 17.9; adding it to GPTAQ improves Llama2-13B WikiText2 PPL from 7.5 to 7.3.

## Critical Assessment

The paper's core theoretical contribution is a clean, correct algebraic refinement: Eqn. 13 shows the GPTAQ residual r1 is provably incomplete by exactly the r2 = (w^(0) − w^(q))X̃ term, and the neuron-decomposition reuse makes integration essentially free. However, a key weakness is that the gains are uneven across settings — on Llama2-7B W2A4KV4 (Table 3) the QuaRot+GPTAQ+Ours WikiText2 PPL (11.5) is only marginally better than QuaRot+GPTAQ (11.7) and average accuracy (48.9 vs 48.5) barely moves, suggesting the compensation-aware term matters most where weight-only error dominates and adds little when activation-quantization error dominates. Second, the method increases peak calibration memory materially: on Llama3-70B down_proj it goes from 4.48GB (GPTAQ) to 6.95GB, and total peak from 63.7GB to 69.5GB (Table 5), which on a 96GB H20 is fine but tightens memory headroom for the largest models — the CPU-offload variant (41.5GB peak) trades memory for added complexity. Third, the paper documents a catastrophic failure it does not fully resolve: SpinQuant+GPTAQ on Llama3-70B yields PPL > 1e5 because the pretrained rotation matrix was optimized for W16A4KV4 and cannot adapt to 2-bit weights; the authors only hypothesize the cause and do not fix it. Finally, the comparison is purely against GPTQ/GPTAQ/AWQ + rotation baselines — it does not include newer QAT or stronger PTQ methods like QuIP#, so the head-to-head against the state of the art at 2-bit is incomplete.

## Strengths

- Mathematically clean and correct reformulation; the new r2 term is a provably missing component of the residual, not a heuristic.
- Drop-in integration with both GPTQ and GPTAQ via a single precomputed P2 matrix, with ~5% quantization-time overhead and zero inference overhead.
- Broad, honest evaluation: 1B–70B Llama2/3, Qwen3 4B/8B/14B, DeiT vision transformers, 3-bit/2-bit weight-only and W2A4KV4 weight-activation.
- Detailed memory and timing tables (Tables 5, 6, 10) make the engineering cost transparent.
- Public code (github.com/list0830/ResComp) and ICLR 2026 acceptance lend reproducibility.
- Calibration-sensitivity study (Tables 11, 12) shows gains are stable across 64–192 samples and four calibration datasets.

## Limitations

- Gains are small or negligible when activation-quantization error dominates (e.g., QuaRot+GPTAQ+Ours on Llama2-7B W2A4KV4: +0.4 avg accuracy).
- Peak calibration memory grows materially (Llama3-70B: 63.7GB → 69.5GB), constraining the largest models without CPU offload.
- SpinQuant on Llama3-70B collapses (PPL > 1e5) and is only hypothesized, not fixed.
- No comparison against QuIP# or other state-of-the-art 2-bit PTQ methods at the W2A16 extreme.
- The method is orthogonal to rotation/activation-smoothing but does not itself address activation outliers, so it must always be combined with QuaRot/SpinQuant for W2A4KV4.
- Vision-transformer gains (Table 7) are present but small at W4A4 (DeiT-Small 74.0 vs GPTAQ 73.8).

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the compensation-aware error r2 be extended to non-uniform (e.g., codebook/lattice) quantizers where the neuron decomposition no longer factorizes column-wise?
- Why does SpinQuant's pretrained rotation fail catastrophically under 2-bit weights on Llama3-70B but QuaRot's random Hadamard rotation stays stable — is it the rotation's conditioning or its activation-outlier distribution?
- Is the r2 term's marginal benefit on W2A4KV4 (Table 3) a fundamental limit of weight-compensation when activation error dominates, or could an analogous "activation-compensation-aware error" be derived?
- Does the P2 precomputation remain tractable for ultra-long-context models where X̃ is much larger, or does the memory overhead become prohibitive?
- Could the r1 + r2 decomposition be applied to GPTQ-style methods for MoE expert matrices where per-expert Hessian conditioning differs?
