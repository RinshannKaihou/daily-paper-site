An Illusion of Unlearning? Assessing Machine Unlearning Through Internal Representations

Yichen Gao, Altay Unal, Akshay Rangamani, Zhihui Zhu

arXiv: 2604.08271

Date: 2026-04-11

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08271

An Illusion of Unlearning? Assessing Machine Unlearning Through Internal Representations

一句话总结： 本文揭示主流机器遗忘方法（如NegGrad+、SalUn、SCRUB、UNSIR）虽然能将遗忘类输出精度降到0%，但通过线性探针（linear probe）可恢复接近原始精度；作者提出CMF（class-mean features）分类器，强制特征与分类器对齐，使遗忘信息真正从隐藏表示中移除，特征级遗忘精度显著低于Retrain基线。
TL;DR: Many state-of-the-art machine unlearning (MU) methods on CIFAR-10/100 and Tiny-ImageNet drive forget-class output accuracy to 0%, yet a linear probe on frozen features recovers up to ~92% forget accuracy (Random-label on CIFAR-10). The authors diagnose this as feature-classifier misalignment under Neural Collapse, and propose CMF (class-mean features) classifier unlearning, which cuts feature-level forget accuracy well below the Retrain-only baseline (e.g., UNSIR+CMF linear-probe forget 9.29% vs Retrain-only 77.35% on CIFAR-10) while preserving retain accuracy.

核心贡献

  • 揭示当前主流机器遗忘方法（Random-label、SalUn、NegGrad+、SCRUB、UNSIR、SVD）仅通过分类器权重错位实现"浅遗忘"（shallow unlearning），隐藏特征仍保持高度可分性。
  • 在神经塌缩（Neural Collapse）假设下，理论证明NegGrad遗忘的最优配置是将遗忘类分类器向量翻转至与类均值最大错位（Proposition 1 / Corollary 1）。
  • 提出CMF（class-mean features）分类器遗忘框架，显式强制分类器权重等于类均值特征，迫使遗忘真正发生在特征空间。
  • 实验表明CMF-based方法在所有数据集上特征级遗忘精度持续低于Retrain-only Retrain基线。

Key Contributions

  • Reveals that six SOTA MU methods (Random-label, SalUn, NegGrad+, SCRUB, UNSIR, SVD) achieve ~0% output-level forget accuracy but leave hidden features highly discriminative, recoverable by a simple linear probe or NCC.
  • Provides a Neural-Collapse-based theoretical explanation: feature-classifier misalignment (NC3 degrades for forget class, preserved for retain classes), with Proposition 1 showing NegGrad flips the forget-class classifier weight to maximally anti-aligned with the class mean.
  • Demonstrates classifier-only fine-tuning matches full-model unlearning at the output level, proving current methods suppress outputs rather than erase representations.
  • Proposes CMF-based unlearning that ties classifier weights to class-mean features, reducing feature-level forget accuracy consistently below the Retrain-only gold standard.

Methodology

The authors evaluate class-unlearning on ResNet-18 (CIFAR-10/100) and ResNet-50 (Tiny-ImageNet), plus ViT-S/16 (ImageNet-pretrained, fine-tuned). Original ResNet models trained with SGD (momentum 0.9, weight decay 5e-4), batch size 128, up to 300 epochs, initial LR 5e-2 with 5-epoch warmup and cosine decay to 1e-5. ViT-S/16 fine-tuned 10 epochs at LR 3e-4, batch size 128. Forget scenarios: CIFAR-10 {1,3} classes, CIFAR-100 {1,10} classes, Tiny-ImageNet {1,20} classes. Hardware: 8x NVIDIA RTX A5000 (24GB), Ubuntu 20.04, PyTorch 2.5.1, CUDA 12.1.

The CMF classifier is constructed as W^CMF = [µ1 ··· µK]^T where µc is the class-mean feature (Algorithm 1), centered by global mean and normalized, then frozen while the encoder updates against the chosen unlearning objective (Algorithm 2). This is integrated into Random-label, SalUn, NegGrad+, SCRUB, and UNSIR by replacing W with W^CMF. Evaluation adds feature-level metrics: linear probe (train new linear classifier on frozen features over full dataset) and NCC accuracy, plus NC3 alignment. Each scenario is averaged over 5-10 retain/forget class combinations with standard deviations reported.

Results & Highlights

  • Random-label unlearning on CIFAR-10 (forget 1 class): output forget accuracy 0.00%, but linear-probe forget accuracy 92.49% and NCC forget accuracy 80.25% — i.e., forgotten-class features remain almost fully recoverable.
  • Retrain-only Retrain (gold standard) on CIFAR-10 (forget 1 class): output forget 0.00%, yet linear-probe forget accuracy 77.35% and NCC 47.06%, showing even the "true" baseline retains transferable forget-class features.
  • CMF improvement (CIFAR-10, forget 1 class, UNSIR+CMF vs Retrain-only): linear-probe forget accuracy drops to 9.29% (NCC 31.16%) vs Retrain-only's 77.35% (NCC 47.06%), while retain accuracy stays at 91.79% (output) — a ~68 percentage-point reduction in feature-level forget leakage.
  • Classifier-only unlearning matches full-model unlearning: e.g., Retain-only FT on CIFAR-10 (forget 1) gives output forget 0.00% with classifier-only (vs 47.67% full-model), proving output suppression can be achieved by last-layer manipulation alone.
  • On Tiny-ImageNet (forget 20 classes), UNSIR+CMF linear-probe forget accuracy is 23.02% vs Retrain-only 30.36%, again below the gold standard.

Critical Assessment

The paper delivers a rigorous and well-motivated diagnostic: the linear-probe recovery result (e.g., 92.49% forget accuracy after "successful" Random-label unlearning) is a strong, concrete demonstration that output-level metrics are misleading. The Neural Collapse analysis (Proposition 1) gives a clean theoretical account of feature-classifier misalignment. A notable weakness: CMF methods no longer achieve near-zero output-level forget accuracy (e.g., NegGrad+ with CMF has output forget 54.50% on CIFAR-10 forget-1), meaning the method trades output-level unlearning success for feature-level erasure — this is a genuine concern for deployment scenarios where output behavior itself is the compliance target. The scope is also limited to image classification (CIFAR/Tiny-ImageNet) and class-unlearning; extension to generative models and LLM concept erasure is left as future work, so applicability to large-scale foundation models is unproven.

Strengths

  • Strong, quantitative diagnostic (linear probe / NCC / NC3) that exposes a real flaw in standard MU evaluation.
  • Clean theory tying the "illusion" to Neural Collapse feature-classifier misalignment (Proposition 1 / Corollary 1).
  • CMF classifier is a simple, modular drop-in compatible with five existing MU methods.
  • Feature-level forget accuracy consistently beats the Retrain-only gold standard across all three datasets.
  • Public code and detailed experimental appendix with standard deviations.

Limitations

  • Image classification only; LLM/diffusion concept erasure is future work, not validated.
  • CMF sacrifices output-level forget accuracy (no longer ~0%), which may be unacceptable for strict output-level compliance requirements.
  • CMF assumes a class-mean structure most natural to classification with labels; less clear how it transfers to unstructured generative unlearning.
  • Relies on Neural Collapse assumptions for the theory, which hold best in the terminal phase of training.

Relevance

直接对应"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"——通过线性探针和神经塌缩分析揭示隐藏表示中残留的遗忘信息，正是隐藏状态层面的可解释性诊断；同时触及"ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations"，提供了机器遗忘评估与神经塌缩理论结合的数学化分析。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the feature-classifier misalignment illusion persist in LLM concept erasure (e.g., RLHF-based or activation-engineering unlearning of facts/concepts)?
  • Can CMF-style class-mean alignment be adapted to non-classification settings (generative/diffusion models) where there is no label-defined class mean?
  • What is the right operating point between output-level forget accuracy and feature-level leakage when both are compliance-relevant?
  • Do intermediate-layer Neural Collapse extensions (per Rangamani et al. 2023) further improve CMF unlearning at deeper layers?
