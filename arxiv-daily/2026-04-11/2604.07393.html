Yeran Zhang, Pengwei Yang, Guoqing Wang, Tianyu Li

Date: 2026-04-11

DSPR: Dual-Stream Physics-Residual Networks for Trustworthy Industrial Time Series Forecasting

一句话总结： 提出 DSPR 双流物理残差网络，将工业时序分解为统计趋势流与物理感知残差流（自适应窗口学习流相关传输延迟 + 物理先验动态图抑制伪相关），在四个工业基准上同时实现 SOTA 精度与近理想物理保真度（MCA>99%、TVR 达 97.2%）。
TL;DR: On four industrial benchmarks (SCR chemical kinetics, Rotary Kiln thermodynamics, Tennessee Eastman Process, SDWPF wind power) versus 8 baselines (L-MPC, Informer, PatchTST, iTransformer, TimeMixer, TimesNet, MSGNet, TimeFilter, PG-NN), DSPR achieves SOTA accuracy with near-ideal physical fidelity — e.g., on SCR MAE 0.265 vs TimeMixer 0.286 (−7.3%) with MCA 99.8% and TVR 97.2% (vs TimeMixer 99.1%/88.5%); on SDWPF MAE 0.335 vs TimeMixer 0.338 with TDA 82.2% vs 74.7%; on Kiln MAE 0.291 vs TimeMixer 0.308; ablations show removing the prior mask raises MAE by 14.09% and the loss-constrained PG-NN baseline actually degrades MAE (0.312 vs 0.308).

核心贡献

  • 提出 DSPR 双流分解框架：预测 Y = T(X,M) + σ(λ)·R(X,M,A_prior)，统计趋势流（基于 TimeMixer）吸收高能量惯性模式，物理感知残流专注机制依赖的瞬态；门控 σ(λ) 初始化为 0，先收敛稳定趋势再逐步激活残流，逐变量自适应加权。
  • 自适应窗口模块（Adaptive Window）：学习通道相关感受野 δ_i = 1 + (δ_max−1)·σ(h_i·W_δ)，构建时序掩码 M 对齐异步信号，显式建模流相关传输延迟（如 SCR 中 45–185s 随烟气速度波动的滞后）。
  • 物理先验动态图：静态分支用门控融合 A^(s) = α·A_prior + (1−α)·A_learned 将物理先验掩码与可学习嵌入结合；动态分支用 A^(d)=Softmax(H·H^T/√d + M_diag) 捕获瞬态耦合；总损失含稀疏正则 L_sparse·‖A^(s)−A_prior‖·M_phys² 在确认依赖上惩罚矛盾、在未掩码区域允许数据驱动发现。
  • 用统一保真度评估（MCA/TVR/TDA）揭示纯数据驱动基线的保真度崩塌，并在 5000 t/d 水泥生产线实现 3+ 个月连续安全工业部署。

Key Contributions

  • DSPR dual-stream decomposition: Y = T(X,M) + σ(λ)·R(X,M,A_prior), where the Statistical Trend Stream (TimeMixer-based) absorbs high-energy inertial patterns and the Physics-Aware Residual Stream focuses on regime-dependent transients; the gating σ(λ) is initialized to 0 so the model converges on the stable trend before gradually activating the residual branch, with per-variable adaptive weighting.
  • An Adaptive Window module that learns channel-specific receptive fields δ_i = 1 + (δ_max−1)·σ(h_i·W_δ) to build a temporal mask M aligning asynchronous signals, explicitly modeling flow-dependent transport delays (e.g., SCR's 45–185s lags fluctuating with flue gas velocity).
  • A Physics-Guided Dynamic Graph: the Static Branch fuses the prior mask with learned embeddings via A^(s) = α·A_prior + (1−α)·A_learned; the Dynamic Branch uses A^(d)=Softmax(H·H^T/√d + M_diag) for transient couplings; the total loss L_total = L_MSE + λ_phys·‖A^(s)−A_prior‖·M_phys² penalizes contradictions with confirmed dependencies while permitting data-driven discovery in unmasked regions.
  • A unified fidelity evaluation (MCA/TVR/TDA) exposing the fidelity collapse of purely data-driven baselines, plus a 3+ month continuous safe deployment on a 5,000 t/d cement line bridging advanced forecasting to trustworthy autonomous control.

Methodology

Problem formulation: given historical observations X={X_{t-L+1:t}} ∈ R^{L×N} over lookback L, auxiliary time features M, and a physics-consistent prior mask A_prior ∈ R^{N×N} restricting the hypothesis space of plausible interactions, predict Y = F(X_{t+1:t+H}, M, A_prior). DSPR decomposes the forecast into an additive composition Y = T(X,M) + σ(λ)·R(X,M,A_prior) (Equation 2), where σ(λ)∈R^N is a learnable per-variable gating vector initialized to 0. Stream 1 (Statistical Trend) uses TimeMixer (a SOTA MLP-based multi-scale decomposition model) as T to generate a stable base forecast Y^(t)=T(X,M). Stream 2 (Physics-Aware Residual) has two parallel branches. Static Branch: A_learned = Softmax(ReLU(EE^T)); A^(s) = α·A_prior + (1−α)·A_learned (α∈[0,1] learnable); spatial aggregation S = A^(s)X followed by linear projection Z^(s) = (S)W^(s)+b^(s) with W^(s)∈R^{N×(N/2)} for dimensionality reduction. Dynamic Branch: time-varying adjacency A^(d) = Softmax(H·H^T/√d + M_diag) (M_diag prohibits self-loops); adaptive temporal mask M built from channel-specific receptive fields δ_{i,t} = 1 + (δ_max−1)·σ(h_i·W_δ); spatiotemporal aggregation H_sp = ReLU(A^(d)H W+b) (dynamic graph convolution) and H_tmp = MHSA(Q=H, K=H_{1:t}, V=H_{1:t}; M) (graph-temporal attention with mask), fused via gated mechanism g=σ(H_sp·W_g), Z^(d) = g⊙H_sp + (1−g)⊙H_tmp. Static-Dynamic Fusion: Δ = [Z^(s)⊕Z^(d)]W_fuse + b_fuse, final update Y = Y^(t) + σ(λ)·Δ. Optimization: L_total = L_MSE(Y,Ŷ) + λ_phys·‖A^(s)−A_prior‖·M_phys² (M_phys binary mask of confirmed dependencies).

Datasets: SCR (proprietary, chemical, 259,200 steps, 9 vars, 10s sampling, target Outlet NO, transport delays 45–185s); Rotary Kiln (proprietary, thermal, 298,790 steps, 7 vars, 10s, target CO); TEP (public benchmark, 250,000 steps, 10 vars, 3min, target Reactor Pressure xmeas_7); SDWPF (public KDD Cup 2022, single turbine #1 245-day trajectory, 7 kinematic vars, 10min, target Active Power). Baselines: 8 models — Linear MPC (ARX), Informer, PatchTST, iTransformer, TimeMixer (also the Trend Stream base), TimesNet, MSGNet, TimeFilter, and PG-NN (TimeMixer + soft conservation loss L=L_MSE+λ_phy·‖cons(x)‖², isolating loss-level vs architecture-level physics integration). Configuration: Physics-Residual Stream uses emb=64, adaptive window δ∈[0,20], gating init λ_init=0; loss weights λ_phys=10², λ_sparse=10⁻⁴. Protocols: Chronological Split (6:2:2) and Regime-based Split (High/Medium/Low volatility tertiles); metrics MAE, RMSE plus MCA (mass conservation, Eq 16), TVR (total variation ratio, Eq 17, ideal 100%), TDA (trend directional accuracy on significant shifts |Δ|>θ, Eq 18). Ablation on Kiln averaged over horizons H∈{96,192,336,720}; robustness over 3 random seeds (Table 6).

Results & Highlights

  • SCR (Table 1): DSPR MAE 0.265 / RMSE 0.415 / MCA 99.8% / TVR 97.2% / TDA 83.5% vs TimeMixer 0.286 / 0.435 / 99.1% / 88.5% / 74.9% — a 7.3% MAE reduction with TVR +8.7pp and TDA +8.6pp. The loss-constrained PG-NN actually worsens MAE (0.292 vs 0.286) and drops TVR to 82.0%, confirming soft penalties force over-smoothed minima.
  • Kiln (Table 1): DSPR MAE 0.291 / RMSE 0.436 / MCA 99.5% / TVR 96.8% / TDA 81.0% vs TimeMixer 0.308 / 0.465 / 98.8% / 84.2% / 72.5%; PG-NN MAE 0.312 (worse than unconstrained TimeMixer 0.308). Ablation (Table 2): No-prior raises MAE to 0.332 (+14.09%), Shuffled-prior 0.328 (+12.71%), w/o Adaptive Window 0.306 (+5.15%), Statistical-Trend-only 0.308 (+5.84%).
  • TEP (Table 1): DSPR MAE 0.437 / RMSE 0.564 / MCA 99.8% / TVR 91.7% / TDA 85.2% vs TimeMixer 0.456 / 0.592 / 98.8% / 84.4% / 81.0% and TimesNet 0.459 / 0.595 / 98.0% / 83.8% / 78.3%.
  • SDWPF (Table 1): DSPR MAE 0.335 / RMSE 0.522 / MCA 99.2% / TVR 83.2% / TDA 82.2% vs TimeMixer 0.338 / 0.537 / 98.2% / 76.5% / 74.7% and Informer 0.602 / 0.837 / 94.0% / 45.6% / 61.5% — DSPR's TVR 83.2% vs Informer's 45.6% shows it preserves physically meaningful transients rather than over-smoothing.
  • Regime adaptation (Table 4, SCR, H=24, L=24): DSPR MAE 0.265 (High) / 0.225 (Med) / 0.195 (Low) vs TimeMixer 0.315 / 0.240 / 0.210 and PG-NN 0.385 / 0.265 / 0.205 — 16–19% MAE reduction in High/Medium regimes where transport delays vary most; DSPR matches PatchTST at Low-Load (0.195) where physics acts as a noise filter.
  • Generality (Table 3, Kiln): the Physics-Residual stream yields gains across base architectures — TimesNet 0.340→0.320 (+5.9%), iTransformer 0.327→0.310 (+5.2%), PatchTST 0.315→0.302 (+4.1%), TimeMixer 0.308→0.291 (+5.5%).
  • Robustness (Table 6, 3 seeds): DSPR shows lower variance than TimeMixer on all datasets — e.g., SDWPF RMSE std 0.003 vs TimeMixer 0.006; SCR MAE 0.265±0.001 vs 0.286±0.003.
  • Mechanism recovery I (SCR, Figure 5): DSPR resolves distinct physics-consistent transport-lag shifts (105s/115s across High/Med/Low regimes) matching the expected 10s variation under flue gas velocity 8–12 m/s over a 15m reactor; PG-NN fails to distinguish regime-dependent delays (confounded distributions).
  • Mechanism recovery II (SDWPF, Figure 6): the learned dynamic graph recovers the yaw-control loop Ndir→Wdir (edge weight 1.0), the aerodynamic Wspd→Patv scaling (0.63, consistent with Betz's Law) and inverse Patv→Wspd (0.82, generator as low-pass filter), and the Itmp→Etmp thermal coupling (0.65); physically irrelevant edges like Pab1→Etmp are suppressed.

Critical Assessment

The evaluation rests on two proprietary datasets (SCR, Rotary Kiln) that cannot be independently inspected due to corporate IP protections — only "key physical features" are described — so the central claims about physical-plausibility gains (MCA>99%, TVR up to 97.2%) and the 3+ month safe deployment on a 5,000 t/d cement line (Appendix E, not in the extracted text) cannot be reproduced or audited by third parties, and the deployment's "measurable resource savings" are asserted but not quantified in the main text. The physics-prior mask A_prior is acknowledged in the Limitations section as relying on "known interaction pathways," meaning unmodeled secondary coupling or evolving degradation falls outside the hypothesis space — so the method's fidelity guarantees hold only within the (human-authored) prior, and the "mechanism discovery" claims (e.g., recovering Betz's Law scaling) are partly circular: the prior already encodes the plausible edges, and the model only re-weights within that space (the Remark on page 3 concedes A_prior is "not a ground-truth causal graph" and DSPR "refines" rather than discovers de novo). The PG-NN ablation, while a useful contrast, uses a single soft-loss formulation (L_MSE + λ_phy·‖cons(x)‖²) chosen by the authors, so the conclusion that "architectural inductive biases surpass soft optimization constraints" is under-tested against stronger PINN variants (conservative PINNs, neural operators) that the paper itself cites but does not benchmark. The MCA/TVR/TDA metrics are borrowed from prior work ([1,15,20]) but their sensitivity to hyperparameters (horizon H, shift threshold θ) and their correlation with downstream control-safety outcomes are not analyzed, so the link between near-100% MCA and "trustworthy autonomous control" is asserted rather than demonstrated. Finally, the regime-split results (Table 4) use a very short lookback L=24 (4 minutes) chosen to "force models to capture immediate physical dynamics," which advantages DSPR's adaptive-window mechanism but may not reflect typical deployment lookbacks.

Strengths

  • Clean architectural thesis: embedding physics as structural inductive biases (adaptive windows for transport delays, prior-guided dynamic graphs) rather than soft loss penalties is a well-motivated, falsifiable design choice, and the PG-NN ablation directly tests the contrast (loss-level vs architecture-level integration).
  • The dual-stream decomposition with σ(λ) initialized to 0 is a principled training trick: it lets the stable trend converge first before the residual branch activates, avoiding early-training instability from the physics terms.
  • Strong and broad empirical results: 4 datasets across chemical/thermal/control/wind regimes, 8+ baselines spanning classical (L-MPC), Transformer (Informer/PatchTST/iTransformer/TimeMixer), CNN (TimesNet), spectral-graph (MSGNet/TimeFilter), and physics-informed (PG-NN) paradigms, with DSPR winning or tying on all four.
  • The unified fidelity evaluation (MCA/TVR/TDA) is a genuine contribution: it exposes that SOTA forecasters achieve low MAE/RMSE while violating mass conservation and causal monotonicity (Figure 1's fidelity-collapse taxonomy), a blind spot in standard time-series benchmarks.
  • Mechanism interpretability (Figures 5–6) goes beyond predictive accuracy: the recovered transport-lag shifts (105s/115s matching 8–12 m/s flue gas over 15m) and the wind-to-power scaling edges (Ndir→Wdir=1.0, Wspd→Patv=0.63) are quantitatively consistent with domain physics, supporting the "scientific mechanism discovery" claim within the prior's scope.
  • Real deployment evidence (3+ months on a 5,000 t/d cement line) is rare in this literature and strengthens the trustworthiness argument, even if details are in an appendix.

Limitations

  • Two of four datasets (SCR, Kiln) are proprietary and cannot be independently inspected or reproduced; the central fidelity claims and the deployment's "measurable resource savings" rest on unauditable data.
  • The physics-prior mask A_prior encodes known interaction pathways, so unmodeled coupling/degradation falls outside the hypothesis space; the "mechanism discovery" is refinement within the prior, not de novo causal discovery (conceded in the Remark).
  • The PG-NN contrast uses a single soft-loss formulation; stronger PINN variants (conservative PINNs, neural operators) are cited but not benchmarked, so "architecture > loss" is under-tested.
  • MCA/TVR/TDA metric sensitivity (to H, θ) and their correlation with control-safety outcomes are not analyzed; the link between near-100% MCA and trustworthy autonomous control is asserted, not demonstrated.
  • Regime-split results use an artificially short lookback L=24 that favors DSPR's adaptive-window mechanism; performance under typical deployment lookbacks is not reported in the main text.
  • The deployment analysis and economic impact are deferred to Appendix E (not in the extracted text), so the headline deployment claim is unverifiable from the main paper.
  • Code release gated on acceptance (no URL at time of writing).

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How sensitive are the MCA/TVR/TDA fidelity metrics to the horizon H and shift threshold θ, and what is their quantitative correlation with downstream control-safety outcomes (e.g., constraint violations, trips) in the cement-line deployment?
  • Since A_prior encodes only known interaction pathways, how does DSPR behave under unprecedented failure modes or evolving degradation outside the prior — does fidelity collapse like the data-driven baselines, or does the residual stream provide graceful degradation?
  • Would benchmarking against stronger PINN variants (conservative PINNs, neural operators, sparse identification) change the "architecture > loss" conclusion, or is the contrast specific to the single PG-NN formulation tested?
  • Can the adaptive-window transport-delay recovery (Figure 5) be validated against ground-truth flow-velocity measurements on SCR, rather than only against the expected 8–12 m/s / 15m arithmetic?
  • Does the dual-stream decomposition generalize to non-industrial physics (e.g., climate, fluid dynamics PDEs) where the prior mask is harder to construct, and how is A_prior authoring cost amortized across facilities?
  • What are the quantitative "measurable resource savings" and safety-event statistics from the 3+ month cement-line deployment (Appendix E), and how do they compare to the existing Linear MPC controller?
