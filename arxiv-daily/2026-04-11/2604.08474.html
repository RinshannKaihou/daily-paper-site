Quantization Impact on the Accuracy and Communication Efficiency Trade-off in Federated Learning for Aerospace Predictive Maintenance

一句话总结： 本文在 NASA C-MAPSS 涡扇 RUL 基准上用 10 颗种子系统评估对称均匀梯度量化（b∈{32,8,4,2}），证明 INT4 在正确的 Non-IID 客户端划分下与 FP32 统计无差异（FD002 MAE p=0.264、NASA score p=0.534）且 8× 降低通信，而 INT2 虽在 FD002 上 MAE 更低却是过正则化假象并伴随灾难性 NASA 分数不稳定（CV=45.8% vs 22.3%），无法用于安全攸关场景。
TL;DR: The author studies symmetric uniform gradient quantization (b∈{32,8,4,2} bits) in a 10-client FedAvg setup training AeroConv1D (9,697 params, purely feed-forward 1-D CNN) for turbofan remaining-useful-life (RUL) regression on NASA C-MAPSS FD001/FD002 under a Non-IID engine-based client partition, evaluated over N=10 seeds with paired t-tests. INT4 achieves statistical parity with FP32 on both subsets (FD001 MAE 17.48 vs 17.52 cycles, p=0.341; FD002 MAE 27.20 vs 26.99, p=0.264; FD002 NASA score p=0.534) while cutting gradient communication 8× (37.88 KiB → 4.73 KiB/round). A direct IID-vs-Non-IID comparison shows naive IID partitioning suppresses NASA-score std (41k vs 123k on FD001) and inflates the apparent benefit of quantization. INT2 is ruled out: its lower FD002 MAE (21.53 vs 26.99, p=0.001) is an over-regularization artefact of the 3-level grid {−α,0,+α}, accompanied by catastrophic NASA-score instability (CV=45.8% vs 22.3% for FP32). Analytical hls4ml projections show INT4 fits the Xilinx ZCU102 at 85.5% DSP utilization. Code: github.com/therealdeadbeef/aerospace-fl-quantization.

核心贡献

  • 设计 AeroConv1D：一个 9,697 参数的纯前馈 1-D CNN，专为 FPGA 推理优化（避免 LSTM 的递归状态累积放大量化噪声），用于 C-MAPSS RUL 回归。
  • 多种子（N=10）、Non-IID 评估协议：用配对 t 检验（α=0.05, df=9）系统比较四种量化级别 b∈{32,8,4,2}，量化施加在每轮传输的权重 delta 上而非本地训练的权重上。
  • 方法论贡献——IID 划分偏差：通过 FD001 上 IID vs Non-IID 的直接对照，证明朴素 IID 客户端划分人为压低方差（NASA score std 41k vs 123k），从而虚增量化带来的"精度优势"，掩盖了真实的统计等价性。
  • INT2 不稳定性刻画：INT2 在 FD002 上的低 MAE 是 3 级网格 {−α,0,+α} 过正则化的假象，伴随 NASA 分数变异系数 CV=45.8%（FP32 为 22.3%），在安全攸关场景不可部署。
  • FPGA 资源投影：按 hls4ml 缩放模型给出 Xilinx ZCU102 的解析资源估计，INT4 占用 85.5% DSP（2 µs @500 MHz），可整片 SoC 完成 FL 训练-量化-推理流水线，并留 366 个 DSP 给潜在的 NTT 同态加密协处理器。

Key Contributions

  • AeroConv1D: a 9,697-parameter purely feed-forward 1-D CNN designed for FPGA inference (recurrent architectures avoided because weight-state accumulation amplifies quantization noise and blocks deep hardware pipelining), for C-MAPSS RUL regression.
  • A multi-seed (N=10) Non-IID evaluation protocol using paired t-tests (α=0.05, df=9) to compare four quantization levels b∈{32,8,4,2}; quantization is applied to the per-client weight delta before transmission, not to local-training weights, preserving full-precision gradient accumulation.
  • Methodological contribution on IID bias: a direct IID-vs-Non-IID comparison on FD001 showing naive IID partitioning artificially suppresses NASA-score variance (std 41k vs 123k) and inflates the apparent accuracy benefit of quantization, masking true statistical parity.
  • Characterization of INT2 instability: the lower FD002 MAE of INT2 is identified as an over-regularization artefact of the 3-level grid {−α,0,+α}, accompanied by catastrophic NASA-score coefficient of variation (CV=45.8% vs 22.3% for FP32), rendering it operationally unusable for safety-critical RUL regression.
  • Analytical FPGA resource projections (hls4ml scaling model) for the Xilinx ZCU102 showing INT4 fits at 85.5% DSP utilization (2 µs latency at 500 MHz), enabling a full FL training-quantization-inference pipeline on a single SoC with 366 spare DSPs for a potential NTT-based homomorphic-encryption co-processor.

Methodology

FL framework (Section 3.1). Synchronous FedAvg with N=10 clients and a central aggregator. Each client trains a local copy for E=2 local epochs per round; aggregator update w_{r+1} = w_r + (1/N)·Σ_k Q_b(w_{r,(k)} − w_r), where Q_b(·) is symmetric uniform quantization applied to the per-client weight delta Δw^(k) before transmission (Eq. 1). Local training keeps full-precision gradient accumulation.

AeroConv1D (Table 1, 9,697 params). Input: windows of 50 time steps × 14 variance-filtered sensor channels. Layers: Conv1D(k=3,s=1,p=1)+ReLU → 32 ch; MaxPool1D(k=2,s=2); Conv1D(k=3,s=1,p=1)+ReLU → 64 ch; AdaptiveAvgPool1D; Flatten; Linear+ReLU → 32; Linear → 1. Param count verified by a runtime assertion. Channel doubling 14→32→64 builds hierarchical features while bounding params below 10k.

Symmetric uniform quantization (Eq. 2). Q_b(Δw^(l)) = α^(l)·clip(round((2^{b−1}−1)·Δw^(l)/α^(l))/(2^{b−1}−1)), where α^(l)=max|Δw^(l)| is the per-tensor scale per layer. For INT2, 2^{b−1}−1=1 gives the 3-level grid {−α^(l), 0, +α^(l)} (effectively a per-layer ternary update with dynamic scale, distinct from fixed {−1,0,+1} ternary nets).

Dataset & Non-IID partition (Section 3.4). NASA C-MAPSS: FD001 (100 train engines, 1 operating condition) and FD002 (260 train engines, 6 operating conditions). RUL targets capped at 125 cycles (piece-wise linear). 14 sensors retained (s2,s3,s4,s7,s8,s9,s11,s12,s13,s14,s15,s17,s20,s21), z-score standardized on training stats. Non-IID partition assigns engines per client sampled without replacement and without RUL sorting; per-client EMD from global RUL histogram averaged 3.9 cycles (FD001) and 2.8 cycles (FD002), confirming heterogeneity.

Metrics. MAE (cycles) and NASA asymmetric score S=Σ_i s(d_i) with s(d)=e^{−d/13}−1 for d<0 (under-prediction), e^{d/10}−1 for d≥0 (over-prediction, penalized more steeply); reported as sum over ~8,700 (FD001) / ~22,000 (FD002) test windows. Gradient-distortion proxy L_priv=(1/2|θ|)·‖Δw−Q_b(Δw)‖² (exploratory only, not a formal DP bound).

Protocol (Section 4). 20 FL rounds, local batch 32, LR 1e-3, Adam. N=10 seeds {42,123,256,789,1024,2024,3141,4242,5555,9999} controlling partition/shuffle/init; per-round per-client RNG seed = s·10⁴+r·10²+k. Results mean±std (df=9); significance by two-tailed paired t-test at α=0.05; Cohen's d reported as directional only (95% CI ≈ d̂±0.95 at N=10). An IID baseline was additionally simulated on FD001 to expose partitioning bias. FPGA projections target Xilinx ZCU102 (274,080 LUT / 2,520 DSP / 912 BRAM36) via hls4ml scaling: LUT=|θ|·b/6, DSP=|θ|·b/18, Latency=b/2 µs @500 MHz.

Results & Highlights

  • INT4 statistical parity with FP32 (Table 3): on FD001, INT4 MAE 17.48±0.51 vs FP32 17.52±0.47 cycles (p=0.341), NASA score 452k±115k vs 449k±123k (p=0.802); on FD002, INT4 MAE 27.20±1.84 vs 26.99±1.69 (p=0.264) and NASA score 938k±233k vs 923k±206k (p=0.534). INT4 vs FP32 is non-significant on every metric×subset combination, while cutting gradient communication 8× (37.88 KiB → 4.73 KiB per round).
  • INT8 also matches FP32 (Table 3): FD001 MAE p=0.520, NASA score p=0.746; FD002 MAE p=0.265, NASA score p=0.364 — confirming 8-bit preserves quality, with a 4× communication reduction (INT8 fits 171.0% of ZCU102 DSP, so it does not fit the FPGA).
  • IID partitioning bias (Table 4, FD001): under IID, FP32 NASA score std=41k vs 123k under Non-IID; INT4 NASA score std=31k (IID) vs 115k (Non-IID). The IID protocol artificially suppresses variance and makes INT4 appear to outperform FP32, whereas Non-IID correctly establishes statistical parity.
  • INT2 unsuitability (Table 3, Section 5.4): on FD001 INT2 MAE degrades to 19.03±1.62 vs 17.52±0.47 (+8.6%, p=0.018) with NASA-score CV=72.0% vs 27.3% for FP32. On FD002, INT2 shows an apparent MAE improvement 21.53±2.31 vs 26.99±1.69 (−20.2%, p=0.001), but this is an over-regularization artefact of the 3-level grid; the NASA score CV is 45.8% vs 22.3% for FP32, i.e. ~2× higher variance, making INT2 non-reproducible and operationally unusable regardless of average MAE.
  • LoRaWAN feasibility (Section 5.2): the 4.73 KiB INT4 payload at 5 kbps requires ≈7.5 s per round; under a 1% EU ISM-band duty-cycle limit the minimum inter-round interval is ≈12.5 min, consistent with predictive-maintenance FL schedules spaced minutes-to-hours apart.
  • FPGA feasibility (Table 5): FP32 requires 684.1% of ZCU102 DSP (does not fit), INT8 requires 171.0% (does not fit), INT4 fits at 85.5% DSP (2,154 of 2,520 DSP, 2 µs latency @500 MHz, leaving 366 spare DSPs), INT2 fits at 42.7% DSP. Only INT4 simultaneously fits the hardware and maintains accuracy parity.

Critical Assessment

The paper's most significant limitation, explicitly conceded by the author, is that the FPGA resource numbers are analytical projections from the hls4ml scaling model and have not been validated on physical ZCU102 silicon — silicon validation is deferred to "ongoing independent research." This means the headline hardware-feasibility claim (INT4 fits at 85.5% DSP, enabling on-chip FL with an HE co-processor) rests entirely on a first-order linear model (LUT=|θ|·b/6, DSP=|θ|·b/18) that ignores routing, control logic, memory bandwidth, and quantization/dequantization overhead, so the 366-spare-DSP figure for a potential NTT-based homomorphic-encryption co-processor is speculative. Second, the privacy claim is deliberately downgraded: the gradient-distortion proxy L_priv is explicitly stated to be "not a formal (ε,δ)-DP bound," only a heuristic indicator of gradient-inversion attack surface, so any privacy interpretation is illustrative rather than proven. Third, the empirical scope is narrow: only two of the four C-MAPSS subsets (FD001, FD002) are evaluated — FD003 and FD004 are left to future work — and only one architecture (the author's own AeroConv1D) is tested, so it is unclear whether the INT4-parity / INT2-instability conclusions generalize to LSTM or CNN-LSTM baselines that dominate the RUL literature. Finally, the model is tiny (9,697 params) and trained for only 20 FL rounds, so the statistical-parity claims may not hold for the larger models and longer training typical of real aerospace deployments; the author acknowledges the broader engine-partitioning parameter sweep is future work.

Strengths

  • Rigorous statistical reporting: N=10 seeds, paired t-tests, explicit Cohen's d confidence-interval caveat, and reporting of coefficient of variation alongside means — well above typical FL-quantization eval hygiene.
  • The IID-vs-Non-IID direct comparison (Table 4) is a genuinely useful methodological warning that IID partitioning suppresses variance and inflates apparent quantization benefits — a real contribution for practitioners.
  • Honest framing of INT2's "improvement" as an over-regularization artefact rather than cherry-picking the lower MAE; the CV analysis makes the non-reproducibility argument concrete.
  • Quantitative LoRaWAN duty-cycle feasibility calc grounds the 8× communication reduction in a realistic deployment constraint.
  • Full reproducibility: code, raw 10-seed logs, and FPGA estimation scripts released.

Limitations

  • FPGA projections are analytical (hls4ml scaling) and not validated on physical ZCU102 silicon; the spare-DSP-for-HE claim is speculative.
  • Privacy analysis is only a gradient-distortion proxy, explicitly not a formal (ε,δ)-DP bound.
  • Only 2 of 4 C-MAPSS subsets (FD001, FD002) evaluated; FD003/FD004 deferred.
  • Only one architecture (AeroConv1D) tested; generalization to LSTM/CNN-LSTM RUL baselines unknown.
  • Small model (9,697 params) and short training (20 rounds); parity claims may not scale to larger models/longer schedules.
  • Symmetric uniform per-tensor quantization only; QSGD/SignSGD/hybrid schemes deliberately excluded to avoid conflating the methodological contribution with a compression benchmark, leaving the compression-scheme comparison open.
  • Single-author work; independent replication of the parity and instability claims is not yet available.
