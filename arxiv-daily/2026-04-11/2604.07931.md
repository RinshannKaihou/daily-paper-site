# Robust Length Prediction: A Perspective from Heavy-Tailed Prompt-Conditioned Distributions

> **一句话总结：** 提出 ProD 方法，通过同一 prompt 的多次独立采样构建鲁棒训练标签（ProD-M 用中位数、ProD-D 用分箱分布），在 Qwen-2.5-7B 与 Llama-3-8B 上将输出长度预测平均 MAE 较 SOTA 降低最多 25%，并证明增加采样预算使估计失败概率指数下降。

> **TL;DR:** The paper reframes LLM output-length prediction as robust estimation of a heavy-tailed prompt-conditioned distribution (not a deterministic scalar), and proposes ProD-M (median target) and ProD-D (binned-histogram target) built from r=16 repeated generations per prompt, both reusing the served LLM's last-layer hidden states. On Qwen-2.5-7B and Llama-3-8B across GSM8K/MBPP/LongBench/LMSYS-Chat-1M (MAE metric), ProD-D reaches avg MAE 56.78 (Qwen) / 44.31 (Llama) vs the strongest baseline TRAIL-Last at 64.23 / 49.08 — a 11.6% / 9.7% reduction — and up to 25% over previous SOTA methods; on Qwen-Math, ProD-D MAE 30.35 beats even the Noise Radius 32.94.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示一个被忽视的事实：在固定模型与解码配置下，同一 prompt 的输出长度是 prompt 条件分布而非确定性标量，且该分布呈现重尾特征（如 Llama 上 max/median 比高达 4.03×）。
- 在 MAE 准则下证明 Bayes 最优点预测是条件中位数，并据此提出 ProD-M（多次采样取中位数作为点标签）与 ProD-D（将采样长度投影到分箱直方图作为软标签）两种监督策略。
- 通过简化线性代理模型给出估计误差界（Theorem 1），证明增加重复采样预算 r 使失败概率项 4N·e^(−r/8) 指数衰减；当 r ≥ 8 log(4N/δ) 时界以至少 1−2δ 概率成立。
- 在固定总推理预算下证明重复采样仍优于全覆盖单样本训练，即"少 prompt 多采样"可作为有效的预算再分配机制。

## Key Contributions

- Empirically establishes that prompt-conditioned output length is a distribution (not a scalar) with heavy-tailed tendency: max-to-median ratios of representative prompts reach 2.91×–4.03×, and prompt-level Median-MAE noise radius is tens of tokens in every setting.
- Shows the Bayes-optimal point predictor under MAE is the conditional median, motivating two robust supervision variants ProD-M and ProD-D built from r independent generations per prompt, both reusing the served LLM's last-layer hidden states (no auxiliary model, single-shot inference).
- Provides Theorem 1: under a linear surrogate with heavy-tailed noise (Assumption 1, only (1+ε)-th moment bounded), the failure-probability term 4N·e^(−r/8) decays exponentially in the repeated-sampling budget r.
- Demonstrates consistent gains on Qwen-2.5-7B and Llama-3-8B across four scenarios, and shows the advantage persists under a fixed total inference budget (budget-fairness ablation).

## Methodology

- **Problem setup**: Prompt-only length prediction for autoregressive LLMs under a fixed served model and decoding config. For request i with prompt x_i, after t decoded tokens the state is z_it = {x_i, generated tokens}; remaining length L_i^t is random. Assume a representation ϕ(z_it) that is a sufficient statistic, so length depends on ϕ(z_it) only; prompt-only is the special case t=0. Objective is conditional MAE R_pt(L̂_i | ϕ(x_i)) = E[|L_i − L̂_i| | ϕ(x_i)].
- **Bayes target**: Discrete-difference derivation shows R_pt(a+1|ϕ) − R_pt(a|ϕ) = 2·P[L_i ≤ a | ϕ] − 1, so the MAE-optimal point target is the conditional median of L_i given ϕ(x_i).
- **Repeated sampling**: Draw r independent generations per prompt, get lengths {L_{i,1},…,L_{i,r}}. ProD-M uses sample median L̄_i = median(L_{i,1},…,L_{i,r}) as the label (discretized to one-hot on a K-bin grid, cross-entropy loss). ProD-D projects the r lengths onto the same K bins to form empirical distribution p_i^dist(k) = (1/r)Σ_j 1[b(L_{i,j})=k], trained with soft cross-entropy. At inference, both extract the median of the predicted bin distribution (cumulative-mass-0.5 bin, linearly interpolated) as the point estimate.
- **Predictor**: ϕ(x_i) = last-layer hidden state of the last prompt token (TRAIL-style probing); a shared 2-layer MLP maps ϕ ∈ R^d → 512-dim ReLU → K-bin logits, q_θ(·|x_i) = softmax(g_θ(ϕ(x_i))).
- **Theoretical surrogate**: Linear model L_i = ϕ(x_i)^⊤ θ* + η_i, with ‖θ*‖₂ ≤ S, ‖ϕ(x_i)‖₂ ≤ 1, and noise satisfying Assumption 1 (symmetric, E[|η|^{1+ε}] ≤ v). Ridge estimator θ̂_N fit on median labels; Theorem 1 bounds |ϕ(x)^⊤θ* − ϕ(x)^⊤θ̂_N| ≤ β_N · ‖ϕ(x)‖_{V_N^{-1}} with failure term 4N·e^(−r/8).
- **Models/datasets/splits**: Qwen-2.5-7B and Llama-3-8B. Scenarios: Math=GSM8K (7473/1319), Coding=MBPP (374/500), LongSequence=LongBench (3789/961 Qwen, 3780/970 Llama), Chat=LMSYS-Chat-1M (4070/930 both). R=16 generations per prompt at temperature 0.8, zero-shot, official chat template + EGTP system prompt. Served on vLLM 0.15.1, two A100 80GB GPUs, two Xeon Platinum 8358 CPUs, 1024 GiB RAM.
- **Baselines**: S³ (DistilBERT bucket classifier), TRAIL-Mean (avg hidden states), TRAIL-Last (last-token hidden state), EGTP (entropy-weighted pooling); reference lines Noise Radius and Constant Median.

## Results & Highlights

- **Main MAE (Table 1, vs 16-sample median target)**: ProD-D avg MAE 56.78 (Qwen) / 44.31 (Llama) vs strongest baseline TRAIL-Last 64.23 / 49.08 — improvements of 11.6% / 9.7%; ProD-M is second-best (60.74 / 45.75). On Qwen-Math ProD-D reaches 30.35 vs Noise Radius 32.94, and on Llama-LongSequence 37.68 vs Noise Radius 56.38 — beating even the finite-sample deviation floor.
- **Headline reduction**: Up to 25% reduction in average prediction error over previous SOTA methods (stated in intro and conclusion).
- **Single-sample ablation (Table 2, single-label eval, 8 trials mean±std)**: Removing repeated-sampling targets degrades ProD-M from 60.74 → 80.93 avg MAE on Qwen and 45.75 → 63.28 on Llama (Table 2), confirming the gain comes from target construction, not architecture.
- **Median-target eval under single-sample training (Table 3)**: ProD-M still best (e.g., Qwen-Math 33.56 ± 0.4 vs TRAIL-Last 42.46 ± 9.6; Llama-Chat 98.95 ± 1.3 vs 102.48 ± 2.6), isolating training-side target quality.
- **Budget-fairness (Figure 2)**: Under fixed total inference budget, ProD-D with 3 samples on Qwen-LongSequence (~1263 unique prompts) achieves 57.83 MAE vs full-coverage TRAIL-Last 67.91; on Llama-LongSequence ProD-D with 5 samples reaches 40.80 vs TRAIL-Last 48.11; on Qwen-Chat ProD-D with 3 samples 128.31 vs 138.46; on Llama-Chat ProD-D with 2 samples 97.15 vs 102.48.
- **Heavy-tail evidence (Figure 1)**: Prompt-level Median-MAE noise radius per setting (tokens): Math 27.8 (Qwen)/16.1 (Llama), Coding 21.7/23.0, LongSequence 42.9/38.0, Chat 35.3/33.4; median noise ratio 11.5% (Qwen/Math) to 18.2% (Llama/LongSequence). Max-to-median ratios of representative prompts: 2.91×, 2.42×, 1.98× (Qwen) and 2.99×, 3.27×, 4.03× (Llama).
- **System-prompt effect (Appendix A.3, Figure 3)**: Adding the EGTP system prompt on 500 MBPP prompts / Qwen shortens mean length, reduces variance (P(with<without)=0.994 for log-variance), and shifts Mean-MAE / Median-MAE distributions left and more concentrated (P(with<without)=0.776 for mean length), lowering the per-prompt MAE baseline.

## Critical Assessment

- The theoretical guarantee (Theorem 1) is proved only for a linear surrogate L_i = ϕ(x_i)^⊤θ* + η_i, which the authors themselves acknowledge "is not intended to exactly match the deployed nonlinear predictor"; the connection between the surrogate bound and the actual 2-layer-MLP ProD predictors is informal, so the exponential-in-r failure claim is suggestive rather than a tight guarantee for the deployed system.
- All experiments use temperature 0.8 and zero-shot prompting with a single fixed system prompt; the paper does not study how the heavy-tail characterization or ProD's advantage changes with greedy/low-temperature decoding, where the noise radius (and hence the motivation for repeated sampling) may shrink substantially — the system-prompt ablation already shows variance is highly sensitive to prompting choices.
- ProD-D's advantage over ProD-M is inconsistent across settings (e.g., on Llama-LongSequence ProD-M 43.45 beats ProD-D 37.68, but on Llama-Chat ProD-M 93.39 vs ProD-D 93.39 are essentially tied at the avg level), and the paper offers no principled rule for when the distributional target pays off vs the simpler median target.
- The prompt-only setting is a subset of the formulation (Section 2.2 allows iterative refinement), but the actual iterative/online remaining-length regime — the more practically impactful case for serving schedulers — is left entirely to future work, so the scheduling-impact claims are not demonstrated end-to-end.

## Strengths

- Identifies and rigorously frames a genuine, previously under-examined mismatch: one-shot length labels are statistically misaligned with the true prompt-conditioned distribution.
- Clean Bayes-optimality argument (conditional median under MAE) directly motivates the median-target design; both variants are minimal changes to the supervision signal, reusing existing TRAIL-style probing with no extra inference cost.
- Strong, consistent empirical gains across two model families and four diverse scenarios, including beating the Noise Radius floor on some settings.
- Budget-fairness ablation directly addresses the obvious objection ("repeated sampling costs more inference") and shows the gain survives fixed-budget reallocation.
- Heavy-tail diagnostics (Appendix A.4) and system-prompt sensitivity analysis add credibility and reproducibility detail.

## Limitations

- Theory is for a linear surrogate only; the exponential-in-r claim is not formally tied to the deployed nonlinear predictor.
- No end-to-end serving/scheduling evaluation (throughput, latency, KV-cache utilization) — the ultimate motivation — is reported; gains are purely in prediction MAE.
- Restricted to temperature 0.8, zero-shot, single system prompt; behavior under greedy or low-temperature decoding (where many production systems run) is untested.
- ProD-D requires choosing a bin grid K and adds distributional target complexity, but the paper does not give clear guidance on when ProD-D is preferred over the simpler ProD-M.
- Only two 7B–8B models; no test of whether heavy-tail characterization scales to much larger or much smaller models, or to reasoning models with long chain-of-thought.

## Relevance

该论文研究 LLM 推理服务中的输出长度预测，将 prompt 条件长度建模为重尾分布并提出鲁棒估计方法，直接映射到用户关注的「LLM Inference Evaluation」（推理输出长度预测的评估与噪声刻画）方向，并通过重尾噪声与估计误差界的理论分析涉及「ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations」。其揭示的"单样本标签与真实分布统计失配"问题对推理可靠性与调度稳定性具有实际意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- 在贪婪/低温度解码（noise radius 大幅缩小）下，ProD 相对单样本基线的优势是否仍然显著？重尾特征是否消失？
- ProD 的预测若接入真实 vLLM 调度器（SRTF/KV-cache 预留），对端到端吞吐与尾延迟的实际增益是多少？预测误差的 MAE 改善是否线性转化为调度收益？
- 重尾长度分布是否在更大规模模型（70B+）或推理类模型（长 CoT）上更显著？r=16 是否仍为合适预算？
- ProD-D 的分箱直方图与 ProD-M 的中位数点标签之间，能否用一个自适应准则（如分布偏度/重尾强度）来选择，而非经验性地两者都报告？
