Conservation Law Breaking at the Edge of Stability: A Spectral Theory of Non-Convex Neural Network Optimization

一句话总结： 推导出梯度下降在非凸神经网络优化中的守恒律破缺精确公式，发现交叉熵损失通过softmax概率浓度驱动指数级Hessian谱压缩，自正则化漂移指数接近α≈1.0，揭示了稳定性边缘的精确数学机制。
TL;DR: Derives exact formulas for conservation law breaking under gradient descent in neural networks, showing cross-entropy loss drives exponential Hessian spectral compression via softmax probability concentration, self-regularizing the drift exponent near α≈1.0, with a spectral crossover formula validated across 23 experiments.

核心贡献

  • 证明L层ReLU网络的梯度流保持L-1守恒律（层间权重范数差异），在离散梯度下降下以η^α（α≈1.1-1.6）速率破缺
  • 推导出精确的谱交叉公式，模系数c_k ∝ e_k(0)²·λ²_{x,k}，在线性网络（R=0.85）和ReLU网络（R>0.80）中得到验证
  • 发现交叉熵损失通过softmax概率浓度驱动指数级Hessian谱压缩，时间尺度τ=Θ(1/η)，自正则化漂移指数至α≈1.0

Key Contributions

  • Shows gradient flow on L-layer ReLU networks preserves L-1 conservation laws, breaking under discrete GD at rate η^α (α≈1.1-1.6)
  • Derives closed-form spectral crossover formula with mode coefficients validated for both linear (R=0.85) and ReLU (R>0.80) networks
  • Identifies cross-entropy loss self-regularizes drift exponent near α≈1.0 via exponential Hessian spectral compression (τ=Θ(1/η))

Methodology

The author derives conservation laws from gradient flow equations on L-layer ReLU networks, decomposes the discrete drift as η²·S(η) with a closed-form spectral crossover formula, and validates all predictions across 23 experiments varying architecture, loss function, and width.

Results & Highlights

  • Conservation law drift: η^α where α≈1.1-1.6 depending on architecture
  • Spectral crossover formula: R=0.85 (linear), R>0.80 (ReLU)
  • Cross-entropy self-regularization: τ=Θ(1/η), independent of training set size
  • Two dynamical regimes: perturbative sub-EoS and non-perturbative with extensive mode coupling

Critical Assessment

Novelty: Very high — the conservation law framework and spectral crossover formula are genuinely new theoretical contributions to understanding training dynamics. Rigor: 23 experiments validate predictions, and the mathematical derivation appears thorough. Red flags: Single author raises questions about verification of the proofs. The theory is developed for relatively simple architectures (fully connected ReLU) — applicability to transformers is implied but not directly demonstrated. The "NP-hard worst case" motivation is somewhat of a strawman.

Strengths

  • Beautiful theoretical contribution with exact closed-form formulas
  • Comprehensive experimental validation (23 experiments)
  • Explains why cross-entropy is particularly well-behaved during training

Limitations

  • Theory developed for fully connected networks; extension to transformers not demonstrated
  • Single author — no co-author verification of mathematical derivations
  • Practical implications for large-scale LLM training unclear
