Latent Structure of Affective Representations in Large Language Models

一句话总结： 利用情感处理作为测试平台，发现LLM学到的情感表示与心理学中的效价-唤醒模型（valence-arousal）高度对齐，且这些表示虽呈非线性几何结构但可被线性近似，为线性表示假设提供了实证支持。
TL;DR: Shows LLMs learn coherent affective representations aligning with psychology's valence-arousal models, exhibit nonlinear geometric structure well-approximated linearly (supporting the linear representation hypothesis), and can quantify uncertainty in emotion processing.

核心贡献

  • 证明LLM学到的情感表示与心理学中广泛使用的效价-唤醒（valence-arousal）模型高度对齐
  • 发现这些表示呈非线性几何结构但可被线性良好近似，为线性表示假设提供实证支持
  • 展示学到的潜在表示空间可用于量化情感处理任务中的不确定性

Key Contributions

  • Shows LLM affective representations align with established valence-arousal models from psychology
  • Finds nonlinear geometric structure that can be well-approximated linearly — empirical support for linear representation hypothesis
  • Demonstrates learned latent space can quantify uncertainty in emotion processing tasks

Methodology

The authors use geometric data analysis tools to probe the latent structure of affective representations in LLMs, leveraging the well-established valence-arousal framework from psychology as ground truth for validation.

Results & Highlights

  • LLM affective representations align with valence-arousal models
  • Nonlinear structure but good linear approximation
  • Latent space enables uncertainty quantification for emotion tasks
  • Geometric structure parallels human emotion models

Authors & Credibility

Authors appear to be from an established research group. The interdisciplinary approach (psychology + ML) is well-executed.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: Moderate — the linear representation hypothesis has been studied before, but the emotion/affect domain provides a uniquely suitable testbed with established ground truth. Rigor: Strong — using psychology's well-validated valence-arousal model as ground truth is a clever validation strategy. Red flags: The practical implications for "model transparency and safety" may be overstated. Results are specific to emotion representations.

Strengths

  • Clever use of emotion processing as a testbed with established ground truth
  • Provides empirical support for the linear representation hypothesis
  • Interdisciplinary approach bridges psychology and ML interpretability

Limitations

  • Results specific to emotion/affect domain
  • Practical implications for safety may be overstated
  • Unclear if findings extend to other semantic domains

Relevance

研究LLM隐藏状态中的情感表示几何结构，为线性表示假设提供实证支持，与用户关注的表示几何和潜在结构分析方向直接相关。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do similar linear approximability findings hold for other semantic domains?
  • Can the uncertainty quantification in affective representations generalize to other reasoning tasks?
