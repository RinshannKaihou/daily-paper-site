Rethinking Data Mixing from the Perspective of Large Language Models

一句话总结： DoGraph从模型视角重新定义"域"为梯度几何分布，定理证明域差异=梯度的MMD距离，每轮用随机投影+K-means聚类得到模型感知的域并求解图约束权重；在GPT-2 Small/Medium与LLaMA-1.1B/3.2-3B上SlimPajama验证困惑度降到3.09（vs Uniform 4.13）并在9个基准上多数领先。
TL;DR: DoGraph redefines domains via gradient geometry (Theorem 2.2: domain difference = MMD under a gradient-induced kernel, proved under a linearized Transformer), then at each epoch projects per-sample gradients via Johnson-Lindenstrauss random projection (k=5000), K-means-clusters them into m=11 model-centric domains, and solves a simplex-constrained objective for domain weights. On SlimPajama with GPT-2 Medium it reaches validation PPL 3.09 vs Dynamic Loss-Based 3.10, DoReMi 3.30, RegMix 4.51, Data Mixing Law 4.50, Uniform 4.13; on 9 downstream benchmarks (HellaSwag/PiQA/OBQA/COPA/LogiQA/WinoGrande/SciQ/ARC-E/Lambada) it averages 37.9% vs next-best 37.2%, with gains concentrated on reasoning tasks (LogiQA 14.8 vs 13.8).

核心贡献

  • 从学习理论视角重新定义"域"（Definition 2.1）：域是模型感知的输入概率分布，而非人类直觉标签。
  • 在线性化Transformer假设下证明定理2.2：两个域期望梯度之差的范数等于其梯度诱导核下的MMD距离，即域差异可由梯度几何刻画，无需先验人类标签。
  • 提出DoGraph：将数据调度形式化为图约束优化——每轮用随机投影降维、K-means聚类得到模型感知域，再在概率单纯形上求解辅助目标得到域权重，加权聚合梯度更新参数。
  • 实证验证域表示在训练中动态演变（人类定义域逐渐融合、模型感知域仍可分离），且DoGraph在多模型规模与多基准上稳定优于Uniform/DoReMi/DOGE/RegMix/Data Mixing Law/Dynamic Loss-Based。

Key Contributions

  • Redefines domain from a learning-theoretic standpoint (Definition 2.1) as the model-perceived input probability measure P_X over token-sequence space, not a human label.
  • Proves Theorem 2.2: under a linearized Transformer, the difference of expected gradients between two domains equals the integral of per-sample gradients against (P1-P2), and its squared norm is exactly MMD^2 under a gradient-induced kernel k_b(s,s') = ; thus domains are identifiable from gradient geometry.
  • Proposes DoGraph (Algorithm 1): per-epoch random Gaussian projection (JL lemma, k=5000) of per-sample gradients, K-means clustering into m=11 model-centric domains, and solving a simplex-constrained auxiliary objective (variance / robust-softmax / alignment / uncertainty variants) for domain weights, then weighted-gradient update.
  • Empirically shows human-defined domains homogenize during training (Fig 1 PCA) while model-centric domains remain separable (Fig 3), and that DoGraph beats six reweighting baselines on SlimPajama and The Pile across four model scales.

Methodology

Domain definition: a domain is (X, F, P_X); two domains differ iff P1 != P2. Theorem 2.2 (proved in Appendix A.9 under four assumptions: linearized attention A.1, linear output transforms A.2, upstream-gradient/mismatch-tensor A.3, regularity A.4) gives g_bar_b(P1)-g_bar_b(P2) = integral of g_b(s) (P1-P2)(ds) and ||g_bar_b(P1)-g_bar_b(P2)||^2 = MMD^2_{k_b}(P1,P2). DoGraph pipeline (Algorithm 1): each epoch t sample R^(t) in R^{d×k}, R_pq ~ N(0,1/k), k=5000; compute per-sample gradients g_i^(t); project g_tilde_i = R^(t)^T g_i (JL preserves pairwise distances within factor (1±eps)); K-means cluster into m=11 domains {D_j}; compute mean gradients g_bar_j; solve w^(t) = argmin_{w in simplex} L_opt(Σ w_j g_bar_j); update theta^(t) = theta^(t-1) - eta Σ w_j^(t) g_bar_j^(t). Four Lopt variants (Appendix A.6): gradient-variance minimization (closed-form softmax), gradient-alignment regularization (convex QP), robust min-max (fixed-point on cosine sim), domain uncertainty weighting (inverse intra-domain variance); uncertainty variant gives lowest PPL (3.09). Models: GPT-2 Small (210M, 24 layers), GPT-2 Medium (300M, 36 layers), TinyLLaMA-1.1B (22 layers), LLaMA-3.2-3B (28 layers, seq 131072). Data: SlimPajama-6B (7 domains: CC 54.1%, C4 28.7%, GitHub 4.2%, Books 3.7%, ArXiv 3.4%, Wikipedia 3.1%, StackExchange 2.8%); Pile 17 subsets for LLaMA. Training: linear warmup cosine LR, LR 5e-4 (start) to 1e-3 (end scale), warmup 500 steps, weight decay 0.01, grad clip 1.0, minibatch 48-64, 20k-25k steps. Eval via lm-eval-harness on 9 benchmarks. Hardware: 2x NVIDIA H200. Baselines: Uniform, Dynamic Loss-Based, DoReMi, DOGE, RegMix, Data Mixing Law.

Results & Highlights

  • SlimPajama validation PPL (GPT-2 Medium, Table 2): DoGraph 3.09 vs Dynamic Loss-Based 3.10 (-0.01), DoReMi 3.30 (-0.21), DOGE 3.31 (-0.22), Uniform 4.13 (-1.04), RegMix 4.51 (-1.42), Data Mixing Law 4.50 (-1.41).
  • SlimPajama 9-benchmark avg (GPT-2 Medium, Table 1): DoGraph 37.9% vs Uniform 37.2%, Dynamic Loss-Based 36.5%, DoReMi 36.5%, DOGE 35.7%, RegMix 35.4%, Data Mixing Law 35.4%; largest per-task gains on LogiQA 14.8% vs Dynamic Loss-Based 13.8% (+1.0) and SciQ 53.5% vs Uniform 49.0% (+4.5).
  • The Pile 9-benchmark avg (LLaMA-1.1B, Table 3): DoGraph 42.5% vs RegMix 42.1%, DoReMi 41.7%, Uniform 40.9%; on SciQ 66.1% vs Uniform 60.3% (+5.8), Lambada 15.9% vs RegMix 14.2% (+1.7).
  • The Pile 9-benchmark avg (LLaMA-3.2-3B, Table 4): DoGraph 43.1% vs RegMix 42.2%, Data Mixing Law 41.6%; lowest PPL across all scales (Fig 2), with gap widening as model size grows.
  • Lopt ablation (Table 10): uncertainty-weighting PPL 3.09 beats alignment 3.15, variance 3.24, robust-softmax 3.31.
  • Cluster granularity U-curve (Fig 4 / Sec A.7): PPL minimized at m=11; degradation both below (m=7) and above (m>11) due to over-partitioning.
  • Computational overhead (Fig 5, Appendix A.8): 20.37 GPU-hours on 2x H200, only 4.51% over RegMix baseline.

Critical Assessment

The conceptual reframing — domains defined by gradient geometry, with a clean MMD theorem (Theorem 2.2) — is the paper's main intellectual contribution and is well-motivated by the PCA evidence (Fig 1) that human-defined domains merge as training proceeds. A real weakness is that Theorem 2.2 rests on a heavily simplified linearized-Transformer setting (Assumptions A.1-A.4: linearized softmax attention, linear output maps); it is not established that the MMD correspondence holds for real nonlinear Transformers, so the theorem is suggestive rather than directly prescriptive. The empirical gains are modest and partly inconsistent: on SlimPajama the headline PPL win over Dynamic Loss-Based is only 3.09 vs 3.10 (within plausible noise given no error bars/seed counts are reported), and on The Pile (LLaMA-3.2-3B, Table 4) DoGraph is actually worse than baselines on HellaSwag (29.6 vs 29.7 tied), OBQA (29.0 vs 29.8 DoReMi), LogiQA (29.7 vs 30.2 Data Mixing Law), WinoGrande (51.4 vs 53.6 RegMix) and ARC-E (40.2 vs 41.2 RegMix) — only SciQ and Lambada carry the average. No standard deviations or seed counts are reported for any table, so statistical significance is unverifiable. Scale is capped at 3B / 6B tokens, far below production LLM pretraining; the "scale-free" claim is asserted, not demonstrated at 70B+. The Lopt choice (uncertainty) is selected post-hoc via the ablation, raising selection concerns. The m=11 default is tuned on SlimPajama and may not transfer.

Strengths

  • Principled gradient-based domain definition with a formal MMD theorem (Theorem 2.2) and full proof.
  • DoGraph is online, proxy-model-free, and architecture-agnostic (drop-in per-epoch reweighting).
  • Modest overhead (4.51% over RegMix) and a clear U-shape analysis for cluster granularity m.
  • Evaluated on four model scales (210M-3B) and two corpora against six baselines including recent RegMix and Data Mixing Law.
  • Public (anonymous) code and detailed appendix with proofs and hyperparameter tables.

Limitations

  • Theorem 2.2 holds only under a linearized Transformer; validity for real nonlinear Transformers is unproven.
  • No error bars or seed counts reported; the SlimPajama headline win (3.09 vs 3.10) is within noise.
  • On The Pile / LLaMA-3.2-3B DoGraph loses to baselines on 5 of 9 benchmarks (HellaSwag, OBQA, LogiQA, WinoGrande, ARC-E).
  • Scale capped at 3B parameters / 6B tokens; no 70B+ or multi-trillion-token validation.
  • Lopt variant and m=11 chosen via ablation on the same data, risking overfitting of design choices.
  • Anonymous code link; final public release unconfirmed.

Relevance

直接对应"LLM Training Stability & Observability"：DoGraph用每步梯度几何（MMD、梯度诱导核）作为训练过程中域演变的可观测量，并据此动态调整数据混合以稳定收敛；同时触及"ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations"——给出了线性化Transformer下域-梯度-MMD的形式化定理与证明。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the Theorem 2.2 MMD correspondence extend beyond the linearized-attention regime to nonlinear Transformers, and can it be approximated empirically?
  • How sensitive are the gains to random seeds — is the 3.09 vs 3.10 SlimPajama PPL win statistically significant?
  • Does DoGraph's advantage persist at 70B+ scale and multi-trillion-token budgets, or does the gradient-clustering overhead dominate?
  • Can the per-epoch gradient projection/clustering be replaced by a cheaper online sketch to cut the 4.51% overhead further?
