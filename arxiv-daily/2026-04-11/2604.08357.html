Bias-Constrained Diffusion Schedules for PDE Emulations: Reconstruction Error Minimization and Efficient Unrolled Training

Constantin Le Cleï, Nils Thuerey, Xiaoxiang Zhu

arXiv: 2604.08357

Date: 2026-04-11

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08357

Bias-Constrained Diffusion Schedules for PDE Emulations: Reconstruction Error Minimization and Efficient Unrolled Training

一句话总结： 提出自适应噪声调度（Adaptive Noise Schedule）通过约束"重构暴露偏差"最小化推理重构误差，并据此提出快速 Proxy Unrolled Training 稳定长程 rollout，在 Kolmogorov 流上 Fréchet Spectral Distance 较基线改善数个数量级。
TL;DR: The paper tackles two limits of autoregressive PDE diffusion models — sub-optimal single-step accuracy and the prohibitive cost of unrolled training — by (1) defining the Reconstruction Exposure-Bias (REB) and proving it is dominated by a local two-step bias controlled by the noise schedule, then (2) an Adaptive Schedule that greedily picks the largest feasible noise-level jump subject to B^(2S) ≤ τ, and (3) a Proxy Unrolled Training that uses only the final n denoising steps as a cheap in-distribution proxy. On forced Navier-Stokes (Kolmo), Kuramoto-Sivashinsky (KS), and Transonic Flow (Tra), Adaptive+Proxy UT reaches 1-step MSE 8.07e-7 on Kolmo (vs Linear-TF 1.30e-6) and 8.83e-8 on KS (vs Cosine-TF 2.79e-7), and improves last-step FSD on Kolmo from 2.1e5 (Linear-TF) to 3.8e-1 (multiple orders of magnitude).

核心贡献

  • 定义"重构暴露偏差"（Reconstruction Exposure-Bias, REB）作为推理输入误差与干净输入误差之比，并将其递归地界定为局部"两步偏差"（Two-Steps Bias）驱动，证明 REB(t) ≲ B^(2S)(t) + λ_t·REB(t+1)（Proposition 3.3）。
  • 证明两步偏差分解为"自预测偏差" B^(own)（模型吃自己预测的放大）与"传播残差" δ；并在 Wiener 去噪器结构与谱偏置假设下给出稳定性阈值 γ(σ,τ)（Proposition 3.4）：若干净输入误差低于 γ(σ,τ) 则自预测偏差有界。
  • 提出 Slow Error Decrease Principle（Proposition 3.5）：最优稳定调度不含任何冗余噪声级，贪心地取最大可行跳跃即最优；据此给出两阶段自适应调度算法（探索稳定阈值 + 贪心构造）。
  • 利用低暴露偏差模型的"近分布代理"性质，提出 Proxy Unrolled Training：仅用最后 n 步去噪作为完整扩散链的廉价估计，使多步展开训练在可接受成本下可行，n=1 即可显著抑制伪影。

Key Contributions

  • Formalizes Reconstruction Exposure-Bias (REB) and proves (Proposition 3.3) it is recursively dominated by a local Two-Steps Bias B^(2S)(t), attenuated/amplified across steps by λ_t = ‖J_t‖·√ᾱ_t·(ratio of clean-input errors).
  • Decomposes B^(2S) into Own-Prediction Bias B^(own) and Propagation Residual δ; proves (Proposition 3.4, under Wiener-denoiser + spectral-bias assumptions) a Stability Threshold γ(σ,τ) such that E_clean(σ) ≤ γ(σ,τ) ⇒ B^(own) ≤ τ, with γ increasing in σ and decreasing in τ.
  • Proves the Slow Error Decrease Principle (Proposition 3.5): under finite-capacity trade-off (A1) and monotone error decay (A2), the optimal stable schedule has no redundant noise levels, so greedy largest-feasible-jump construction is optimal — yielding minimal diffusion steps and no wasted capacity.
  • Introduces Proxy Unrolled Training: because a low-REB model's sampling distribution matches the ground-truth forward distribution, the final n denoising steps (n=1 used) serve as a faithful, cheap proxy for full Markov-chain sampling, enabling tractable unrolled training that stabilizes long rollouts.

Methodology

  • Setup: Autoregressive conditional DDPM emulating fluid time evolution — x̂_{k+1} = M_θ(x̂_k), where M_θ is the diffusion sampler reversing a noise-addition process conditioned on the previous state. ε-prediction formulation; one-step transition approximation ŷ_{t−1} = √ᾱ_{t−1}·y_est(ŷ_t,x,σ_t) + σ_{t−1}·ε (Eq. 8). Model trained with standard diffusion loss L_diff = E‖ε_θ(ỹ_t,x,σ_t) − ε‖² (Eq. 6).
  • REB decomposition: Clean-Input Error E_clean(t)=E‖y_est(ỹ_t)−y‖ vs Inference-Input Error E_inf(t)=E‖y_est(ŷ_t)−y‖; REB(t)=E_inf/E_clean. Two-Steps Bias B^(2S)(t) isolates single-transition degradation; B^(2S)=B^(own)+δ(t). Proposition 3.3 bounds REB(t) ≲ B^(2S)(t)+λ_t·REB(t+1), λ_t=‖J_t‖·√ᾱ_t·‖y_est(ỹ_{t+1})−y‖/‖y_est(ỹ_t)−y‖.
  • Stability threshold (Prop 3.4): Under (A1) Wiener denoiser structure (Jacobian diagonal in Fourier basis, eigenvalue λ_k=(√ᾱ/σ²)E_θ(k)) and (A2) spectral bias (networks learn low frequencies first), B^(own) is an increasing function of E_clean; for each σ_t and τ≥1 exists γ(σ_t,τ) (increasing in σ, decreasing in τ) s.t. E_clean(σ_t)≤γ ⇒ B^(own)(t)≤τ.
  • Optimal stable schedule (Eq. 15–16): S* = argmin E_clean(0) s.t. B^(2S)(t)≤τ ∀t. Reformulated as min σ_0 s.t. bias constraint. Slow Error Decrease Principle (Prop 3.5): under (A1) finite-capacity trade-off and (A2) monotone error decay, no intermediate σ_k is redundant; greedy largest-feasible-jump is optimal.
  • Adaptive Scheduling Algorithm (2 phases): (1) Exploration — train on dense log-uniform schedule, periodically evaluate B^(own), mark solved levels (B^(own)≤τ) and remove from active set; terminates when no new level solved in a pass (total epochs ≤ E). (2) Construction — greedily set σ_{t+1}=max{σ'∈σ_solved : B^(2S)(σ_t,σ')≤τ} (Eq. 17), using checkpoint θ(σ') for first denoise and θ(σ_t) for second; phase needs only forward passes. Model retrained from scratch on constructed schedule.
  • Proxy Unrolled Training: Proxy Estimate P_θ^(n)(x_k,x_{k+1}) = Denoise_{n…0}(ỹ_{k+1}^n, cond=x_k) (Eq. 19), ỹ_{k+1}^n=√ᾱ_n·x_{k+1}+√(1−ᾱ_n)·ε. Faithfulness: if model is exposure-bias-free up to step n, sampling distribution matches ground-truth forward distribution. 2-steps proxy loss L_P-UT = E_k L_diff(x_k,x_{k+1}) + E_k L_diff(P_θ(x_k,x_{k+1}),x_{k+2}). Used only as fine-tuning (n=1) after adaptive pre-training.
  • Datasets: 1D Kuramoto-Sivashinsky (256 spatial points, Δτ=0.2, 512 train traj length 140Δτ, 64 val/test length 640Δτ, step-size 4Δτ); 2D Transonic Flow (128×64, fields u,p,ρ, R=60 timesteps); 2D Kolmogorov Flow (64×64, incompressible Navier-Stokes, 800 train traj length 64, 100 val/test, Δτ=0.2).
  • Baselines: diffusion schedules Linear/Cosine/Sigmoid with Teacher-Forcing; deterministic U-Net with TF and Unrolled-Training (U=2, U=8); PDE-Refiner (low-noise-focus diffusion). Same U-Net+attention architecture throughout; continuous SNR-parameterized time embeddings.
  • Metrics: 1-step MSE, 10-steps MSE, High-Correlation Time (with worst/best-10 in parentheses) or Final MSE, last-step Fréchet Spectral Distance (FSD). Training budget E per run; adaptive method costs 2E (exploration + final). τ slightly above 1 used.

Results & Highlights

  • Kolmogorov Flow (Table 1): Adaptive+Proxy UT 1-step MSE 8.07e-7 vs Linear-TF 1.30e-6 (best baseline 9.53e-7 U-Net UT U=2); 10-steps MSE 1.72e-5 vs Linear-TF 3.16e-4; High-Correlation Time 11.0s (9.0, 12.6) vs Linear-TF 9.7s (6.0, 12.3); last-step FSD 3.8e-1 vs Linear-TF 2.1e5 — multiple orders of magnitude improvement, the headline result.
  • Kuramoto-Sivashinsky (Table 1): Adaptive 1-step MSE 9.55e-8 vs Cosine-TF 2.79e-7 (PDE-Refiner 8.99e-8 competitive); 10-steps MSE 9.74e-6 (Adaptive) vs 1.19e-5 (PDE-Refiner best baseline); High-Correlation Time 85.8s (55.7, 117.4) vs PDE-Refiner 88.0s (52.5, 123.6) — best baseline; Adaptive+Proxy UT 86.1s.
  • Transonic Flow (Table 1): Adaptive 1-step MSE 3.87e-5 vs Cosine-TF 4.90e-5 (U-Net UT U=1 best baseline 5.63e-5); 10-steps MSE 8.11e-4 (Adaptive) vs U-Net UT U=8 2.54e-3 and Cosine-TF 8.79e-4; Final MSE 1.33e-1 (Adaptive) vs U-Net UT U=8 1.08e-1 (best baseline); Adaptive+Proxy UT Final MSE 1.25e-1, last-step FSD 3.8e1 vs Linear/Cosine ~2.3e1–3.54e1.
  • Adaptive schedule is the dominant factor: "the proposed schedule optimization framework is the dominant factor driving performance gains"; consistently outperforms standard diffusion baselines on one-step MSE by a significant margin.
  • Proxy UT impact: on Kolmo, one-step error similar to Teacher-Forcing but proxy method improves long-term correlation and FSD — "prevention of artifact formation and maintains bounded physical consistency for long rollouts." Validates that the n=1 proxy is sufficient to sensitize the model to its own distribution shifts.
  • Exposure-bias-free schedules (Figure 4): inference-input error matches clean-input error on all three datasets after adaptive training; obtained σ_0 differs vastly per dataset, with smaller σ_0 correlated with higher high-frequency content (consistent with Lippe et al. 2023).
  • PDE-Refiner comparison: PDE-Refiner performs competitively on KS (best 1-step MSE 8.99e-8) due to its low-noise focus aligning with the Slow Error Decrease Principle, but its fixed low-noise focus is less effective on Kolmo/Tra where Adaptive generalizes.

Critical Assessment

  • The theoretical results (Propositions 3.3–3.5) rely on assumptions that the authors themselves acknowledge do not hold exactly in practice: (A1) Wiener denoiser structure (Jacobian diagonal in Fourier basis) "does not hold exactly in practice," and (A2) spectral bias "may not be true for all classes of neural networks" — the empirical correlation in Figure 1 is suggestive but not a validation of the assumptions for arbitrary architectures.
  • The adaptive method doubles training cost (2E: exploration + final retrain from scratch), which the authors defend only verbally as "smart hyperparameter exploration" without a wall-clock/GPU-hour comparison against simply grid-searching a few standard schedules at the same total budget — so the practical efficiency claim is under-substantiated.
  • On Transonic Flow the Adaptive method does not dominate: U-Net UT U=8 achieves a better Final MSE (1.08e-1) than Adaptive (1.33e-1), and Adaptive's 10-steps MSE (8.11e-4) is only marginally better than Cosine-TF (8.79e-4) — the gains are dataset-dependent and the method is not uniformly best across all metrics.
  • The proxy unrolled-training faithfulness argument is conditional on the model already being low-exposure-bias (i.e., on having run the adaptive schedule first); the paper does not characterize how quickly proxy fidelity degrades if n is increased or if the schedule is sub-optimal, leaving the robustness of the n=1 choice unexamined.

Strengths

  • Principled, formal framework linking noise schedule → reconstruction error → exposure bias via provable (under stated assumptions) recursive bounds and a stability threshold.
  • The Slow Error Decrease Principle gives a clean greedy construction with a clear optimality guarantee, and the resulting schedule naturally adapts per-dataset (coarse at high noise, fine near σ_0).
  • Proxy Unrolled Training is an elegant, cheap workaround for the prohibitive cost of full-chain unrolled training in diffusion PDE models, and is shown to prevent long-rollout artifacts.
  • Multi-benchmark evaluation across three distinct PDE regimes (chaotic KS, incompressible Kolmo, transonic Tra) with multiple baselines (deterministic U-Net TF/UT, Linear/Cosine/Sigmoid diffusion, PDE-Refiner).

Limitations

  • Theory rests on Wiener-denoiser and spectral-bias assumptions that are acknowledged to be inexact in practice; empirical validation is correlational only.
  • Doubled training cost (2E) without a matched-budget comparison against schedule grid search.
  • Gains are dataset-dependent; on Transonic Flow the method is not uniformly best (U-Net UT U=8 beats it on Final MSE).
  • Proxy faithfulness only established for n=1 and only conditionally on an already-low-REB model; robustness to larger n or sub-optimal schedules uncharacterized.
  • Restricted to ε-prediction; the paper itself flags that x_0-prediction and v-prediction may alter the error dynamics and this is left to future work.
  • No code release or venue acceptance stated.

Relevance

该论文研究自回归扩散 PDE 仿真中的噪声调度优化与暴露偏差抑制，核心是从误差传播动力学（重构暴露偏差的递归界、稳定性阈值、Jacobian 谱结构）角度提升长程 rollout 的数值稳定性。这映射到用户关注的「Computation Reliability & Precision Errors」（高精度数值仿真中的重构误差与精度控制）与「ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations」（误差传播界的数学基础、Wiener 去噪器与谱偏置假设下的稳定性分析）两个方向，其"以偏差约束驱动调度设计"的思路对自回归生成的误差累积分析具有方法论借鉴价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • 稳定性阈值 γ(σ,τ) 是否在不同 PDE 任务/数据集间是共享量？能否用一个与能谱相关的解析形式预测 γ，从而免除探索阶段的训练成本？
  • Proxy Unrolled Training 的保真度如何随 n（代理步数）和调度质量变化？是否存在一个 n 的下界保证梯度方向与全链一致？
  • 该偏差约束调度框架能否迁移到视频/语言自回归扩散生成，以抑制长序列生成的暴露偏差与误差累积？
  • Wiener 去噪器与谱偏置假设在 Transformer-based 去噪器（而非 U-Net）上是否近似成立？是否可用更弱的假设得到同构的稳定性阈值？
