# Hidden Biases in Conditioning Autoregressive Models

> **一句话总结：** 本文形式化定义了自回归模型在约束生成（押韵、固定音节、inpainting 等）中的"隐式推断偏差"，并证明精确的句子级 MAP 解码是 NP-hard（即使在单约束和格律约束下），精确条件归一化是 #P-hard（即使对固定长度终止事件这一正则约束），从而解释了局部自回归采样容易而精确约束解码不可行的根本原因。

> **TL;DR:** The paper formalizes a "hidden inferential bias" in constrained generation with autoregressive models: practical systems (beam search, reranking, rejection sampling, infilling architectures) do not exact-condition the fixed pretrained model, distorting the true conditional distribution with no guarantee of complete coverage or correct conditional probabilities. It proves that for succinctly represented autoregressive models with polynomial-time next-token evaluation, exact sentence-level MAP decoding is NP-hard (Theorem 1, via reduction from SAT), remains NP-hard under unary and metrical constraints (Corollaries 1, 3), and that exact conditioned normalization Z_L(θ) is #P-hard even for the regular constraint "fixed length L ending in eos" (Theorem 2, via reduction from #SAT). Unlike finite-state Markov models, general autoregressive models admit no bounded-state dynamic program for these tasks.

## 核心贡献

- 形式化定义"隐式推断偏差"：与训练集偏差不同，这是在预训练模型固定的前提下，近似约束生成过程对真实条件分布的计算性扭曲。
- 证明精确句子级 MAP 解码在简洁表示的自回归模型上是 NP-hard（从 SAT 归约），且在单约束与格律约束下仍为 NP-hard。
- 证明精确条件归一化在固定长度终止事件这一正则约束下已是 #P-hard（从 #SAT 归约）。
- 划定可处理与不可处理约束的边界：可由有界状态递归处理的约束（如前缀条件、有限状态 Markov 模型下的正则约束）可处理；需全局延续质量求和/优化的约束不可处理。

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes a "hidden inferential bias" in constrained generation: distinct from training-set bias, it is a computational distortion of the exact conditional distribution induced by the requested constraint when approximate procedures (heuristic search, reranking, local reweighting, rejection sampling, dedicated infilling architectures) are applied to a fixed pretrained autoregressive model.
- Proves that exact sentence-level MAP decoding is NP-hard for succinctly represented autoregressive models with polynomial-time next-token evaluation (Theorem 1, reduction from SAT), and that this hardness persists under unary position-wise constraints (Corollary 1) and metrical constraints (Corollary 3).
- Proves that exact conditioned normalization Z_L(θ) is #P-hard even for the regular constraint of fixed-length sequences ending in eos (Theorem 2, reduction from #SAT), and consequently exact sampling from the fixed-length conditional distribution requires #P-hard continuation masses (Corollary 2).
- Clarifies the tractability boundary: constraints enforceable from a bounded state (prefix conditioning; regular constraints under finite-state Markov models) are tractable, while constraints requiring global continuation masses (exact terminal position, syllable count, inpainting) are intractable in the general autoregressive setting.

## Methodology

The paper is a complexity-theoretic note. It considers a class M of succinctly represented autoregressive models P_θ such that: (i) each model has a finite description polynomial in instance size; (ii) for every prefix x_{1:i-1} and token a, the conditional probability P_θ(a|x_{1:i-1}) is a rational number computable in polynomial time. This captures the natural setting where local next-token evaluation is efficient but global optimization/exact conditioning may be hard.

An autoregressive model factorizes as P_θ(x_{1:n}) = Π_{i=1}^n P_θ(x_i | x_{1:i-1}), with C the set of complete sequences terminating in eos.

**Theorem 1 (NP-hardness of MAP).** Reduction from SAT. Given a CNF formula φ over m variables, construct a model P_φ over Σ={0,1,b0,b1,eos}. For the first m positions, bits are chosen uniformly (prob 2^{-m} per assignment prefix). At step m+1: if the assignment satisfies φ, emit eos with probability 1; otherwise emit b0/b1 with probability 1/2 each, then eos at m+2. Thus P_φ(a eos)=2^{-m} if a⊨φ, and 2^{-m-1} otherwise. A MAP decoder decides SAT by checking the (m+1)-th token.

**Corollary 1 (unary constraints).** Impose x_i∈{0,1} for i≤m and x_{m+1}=eos; the optimum is positive iff φ is satisfiable.

**Theorem 2 (#P-hardness of normalization).** Reduction from #SAT with L=m+1. Z_L(P_φ) = Σ P_φ(a,eos) = #SAT(φ)/2^m, so exact Z_L computation yields the count of satisfying assignments.

**Metrical constraints.** Assign each token a nonnegative integer weight w(a); metrical sentences of total weight K form S_K. Unit weight w(a)=1 specializes to fixed-length, transferring NP-hardness (Corollary 3) and #P-hardness (Corollary 4).

**MAP-THRESHOLD (Definition 2).** The decision variant "does a complete sequence of length n with P_θ≥τ exist?" is NP-complete (Corollary 5).

**Inpainting (Section 9).** Exact inpainting P_θ(y | u y v ∈ C) contains fixed-length terminal conditioning as a special case (u=ε, v=eos, length L), so any generic exact inpainting via causal left-to-right renormalization must compute #P-hard continuation masses (Corollary 6).

## Results & Highlights

- Exact sentence-level MAP decoding for succinct autoregressive models is NP-hard (Theorem 1), via a polynomial-time reduction from SAT in which a satisfying assignment yields a complete sequence of probability 2^{-m} versus 2^{-m-1} for non-satisfying assignments.
- Exact MAP decoding remains NP-hard under unary position-wise constraints (Corollary 1) and under fixed metrical constraints (Corollary 3), even in the degenerate unit-syllable case.
- Exact computation of the conditioned normalization constant Z_L(θ) is #P-hard (Theorem 2), via reduction from #SAT where Z_L(P_φ) = #SAT(φ)/2^m; this holds even though the constraint "fixed length L ending in eos" is a regular language (Remark 1).
- Exact sampling from the fixed-length conditional distribution does not reduce to ordinary ancestral sampling and requires #P-hard global conditioned continuation masses (Corollary 2).
- Exact computation of the total probability mass of metrical sentences of syllable count K is #P-hard (Corollary 4).
- The MAP-THRESHOLD decision problem is NP-complete (Corollary 5): membership in NP by guessing a sequence and multiplying n conditionals; NP-hardness by τ=2^{-m}.
- Any generic exact inpainting procedure sampling from P_θ(y | u y v ∈ C) by causal local renormalization must compute #P-hard conditioned normalization terms (Corollary 6).
- No quantitative results reported — the paper is a pure complexity-theoretic note with theorems and proofs rather than empirical experiments; the evidence consists of formal reductions establishing worst-case hardness.

## Critical Assessment

- The hardness results are worst-case and established via contrived model constructions (the P_φ model is hand-built for the reduction, encoding SAT into next-token conditionals); they do not measure the typical-case difficulty of conditioning real, large pretrained LLMs, whose conditional distributions are not adversarially structured, so the practical gap between worst-case intractability and empirical near-satisfaction of constraints remains unquantified.
- The class M of "succinctly represented autoregressive models with polynomial-time next-token evaluation" is natural but broad; the reductions rely on models that perform explicit SAT evaluation at step m+1, which is a degenerate member of the class, and the paper does not characterize which structurally restricted subclasses (beyond first-order Markov) might recover tractability.
- The note is purely theoretical with no experiments, approximations, or algorithms; it proves impossibility but offers no constructive guidance on how close practical constrained-decoding systems (beam search, FST controllers, reranking) come to the exact conditional distribution, nor any bound on the bias magnitude.
- The "hidden inferential bias" framing is conceptually valuable but operationally underspecified: the paper asserts samples are "distorted relative to the true constrained distribution" but does not define a metric of distortion or provide any bound on how far a given approximate procedure's output distribution diverges from the exact conditional.

## Strengths

- Clean, conceptually clarifying formalization that distinguishes computational/inferential bias from training-set bias — a distinction rarely made explicit in the constrained-generation literature.
- Rigorous complexity-theoretic proofs via standard reductions (SAT, #SAT) that make precise the folklore claim that "local autoregressive sampling is easy, exact decoding/conditioning is hard."
- The tractability boundary (bounded-state recursion vs. global continuation masses) is a useful organizing principle that explains why finite-state Markov models admit exact constrained sampling while general autoregressive models do not.
- The inpainting analysis (Section 9) neatly subsumes fixed-length terminal conditioning as a special case, unifying text-infilling and music-inpainting failures under one #P-hardness umbrella.

## Limitations

- Worst-case-only hardness via contrived model constructions; no typical-case analysis for realistic LLMs.
- No experiments, approximation algorithms, or bias-magnitude bounds; purely impossibility results.
- The model class is broad and the reductions use degenerate members; tractable subclasses beyond first-order Markov are not characterized.
- "Hidden inferential bias" is framed qualitatively without a formal divergence metric or bound between approximate and exact conditional distributions.
