# Linear Representations of Hierarchical Concepts in Language Models

> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Linear Hierarchical Encoding (LHE)，为每个层级深度与语义域学习一个线性变换，把子概念在中间层的隐状态映射到父概念表示；在 Llama 3.2 3B / Llama 3.1 8B / Qwen3 8B / Qwen3 14B 四个模型、五个领域上验证层级关系可被线性恢复且因果有效，并发现层级信息编码在约 150–250 维的领域特异子空间中，但跨域的层级表示结构高度相似。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes Linear Hierarchical Encoding (LHE), which learns depth- and domain-specific linear transformations (via Linear Relational Concepts / Linear Relation Embeddings) mapping a child concept's intermediate-layer hidden state to its parent's representation. Across 4 LMs (Llama 3.2 3B, Llama 3.1 8B, Qwen3 8B, Qwen3 14B) and 5 domains (Location, Person, Organization, Organism, Research Topic), parent-child relations are linearly recoverable and causally steerable. On Llama 3.1 8B, LHE beats the Input-Averaging and SVM baselines on Accuracy in all 5 domains (e.g., Organism 0.93 vs. SVM 0.72 vs. Input-Avg 0.55) and on Causality in all 5 domains (e.g., Organism 0.57 vs. SVM 0.28 vs. Input-Avg 0.35). Hierarchy is encoded in a low-dim (~150–250d) domain-specific subspace, but hierarchical representational structure is similar across domains (Wasserstein distances ~0.3–2.5 between hierarchical-domain pairs vs. ~4.3 for hierarchical-vs-non-hierarchical baselines).

## 核心贡献

- 提出 LHE 框架：基于 Linear Relational Concepts (Chanin et al., 2024)，为每个层级深度与领域分别学习线性变换 R_r(s) = W_r s + b_r，把子节点中间层表示映射到父节点表示，并构造概念方向向量 v_{r,c}
- 把层级表示分析从 Park et al. (2025) 仅在 unembedding 层、单 token 词的设定，扩展到中间层与多 token 实体（如 "Babe Ruth"、"The New York Times Company"）
- 用 Accuracy（线性可解码性）与 Causality（干预有效性：加减概念向量是否翻转预测）两个指标系统评估，并对比 Input Averaging、SVM 两个基线
- 发现层级信息编码在约 150–250 维的领域特异低维子空间，但跨域层级结构高度相似（用拓扑数据分析 TDA 量化）

## Key Contributions

- Introduces Linear Hierarchical Encoding (LHE), building on Linear Relational Concepts (Chanin et al. 2024) and Linear Relation Embeddings (Hernandez et al. 2024), learning depth- and domain-specific linear maps R_r(s)=W_r s + b_r that send a child concept's intermediate-layer hidden state to its parent's representation; constructs per-parent concept direction vectors v_{r,c} via pseudo-inverse
- Extends hierarchical-representation analysis beyond Park et al. (2025): covers intermediate layers (not only the unembedding layer) and multi-token entities (e.g., "Babe Ruth", "The New York Times Company"), which prior single-token methods could not handle
- Evaluates with two metrics — Accuracy (whether the argmax concept vector recovers the gold parent) and Causality (whether adding/subtracting v_{r,c} flips the LM's next-token prediction to the target parent) — against Input-Averaging and SVM baselines
- Empirically shows hierarchy is encoded in a low-dimensional (~150–250d) domain-specific subspace, yet the hierarchical representational structure is similar across domains, quantified via SVD subspace overlap and Topological Data Analysis (Wasserstein distance on persistence diagrams)

## Methodology

**Framework (§3).** Each hierarchy instance is a triple (s, r, o): child subject s, relation r labeled by depth (e.g., PART-OF_{2→3}), parent object o. Subject representation s_n = h_{i_s}^{(ℓ_s)} is the hidden state at layer ℓ_s at the last token of s; parent representation o_n is the mean of hidden states at layer ℓ_o over all tokens of o. For each relation r, learn a Linear Relation Embedding R_r(s) = W_r s + b_r, where W_r is the average Jacobian ∂F/∂s over training samples and b_r the average bias. Inverse map R_r†(o) = W_r†(o − b_r) (low-rank pseudo-inverse) yields per-parent concept vectors v_{r,c} = normalize(mean over children of W_r†(o_n − b_r)).

**Evaluation.** Accuracy: given test child representation a, predict parent via ŷ = argmax_c v_{r,c}·a. Causality: intervene on the final-token child representation at every layer as h_i^{(ℓ)} ← h_i^{(ℓ)} + β‖h_i^{(ℓ)}‖_2 (v_{r,c′} − v_{r,c}); success if predicted probability of target parent c′ exceeds original parent c (min-token probability for multi-token parents).

**Data (§4, Tbl. 1).** Five domain-specific hierarchical datasets: Locations (ISO 3166-1 + Gurnee & Tegmark 2024 geographic data), Persons & Organizations (GPT-5-generated candidates filtered against Wikidata), Organisms (Park et al. 2025 Linnaean taxonomy), Research Topics (OpenAlex, Barrett 2024). Each node has a single parent. Dataset is filtered to instances the LM answers correctly in 5-shot multiple-choice QA; train/test split partitions each tree at the root (e.g., train on Europe/Americas/Oceania, test on Asia/Africa for Location).

**Models (§4, Tbl. 7).** Llama 3.2 3B (hidden 3072, 28 layers, 24 heads), Llama 3.1 8B (4096, 32, 32), Qwen3 8B (4096, 36, 32), Qwen3 14B (5120, 40, 40). Per-model best hyperparameters reported in App. C. Training uses 8 samples per linear transformation (following LRC), 5 random seeds, 5-shot prompts.

**Baselines.** (i) Linear SVM (favorable setting: includes some test data in training since SVMs can't predict unseen labels); (ii) Input Averaging (mean child-node vector per parent as the concept vector). Park et al. (2025) not compared because inapplicable to intermediate layers.

## Results & Highlights

- **LHE vs. baselines on Llama 3.1 8B (Tbl. 2, averaged across depths):** LHE Accuracy beats both baselines in all 5 domains — Location 0.68 vs. SVM 0.55 vs. Input-Avg 0.42; Research Topic 0.52 vs. 0.36 vs. 0.29; Person 0.89 vs. 0.63 vs. 0.35; Organization 0.93 vs. 0.72 vs. 0.44; Organism 0.72 vs. 0.55 vs. 0.47. LHE Causality also beats both baselines in all 5 domains — e.g., Organism 0.57 vs. SVM 0.28 vs. Input-Avg 0.35; Organization 0.65 vs. 0.32 vs. 0.07.
- **Cross-model LHE (Tbl. 3, shared filtered test set):** Accuracy ranges 0.5–0.96 across models/domains (e.g., Organization: Llama 3.2 3B 0.96, Llama 3.1 8B 0.96, Qwen3 8B 0.98, Qwen3 14B 0.90). Causality 0.12–0.75; Qwen3 14B shows notably low Causality (e.g., Organism 0.12, Organization 0.25, Person 0.25), suggesting its hierarchy-relevant features may not be linearly encoded.
- **Low-dimensional subspace (Fig. 3, Llama 3.1 8B):** sweeping pseudo-inverse rank k, Accuracy and Causality peak at ≈150–250 dimensions out of hidden-state sizes 3000–5000, indicating hierarchy is concentrated in a low-dim subspace; the same peak-rank trend holds across all 4 models (App. E).
- **Domain-specific subspaces (Fig. 4, cross-domain transfer):** Accuracy stays relatively high under domain shift, but Causality is highest on the diagonal (same-domain train/test) and "drops substantially" off-diagonal — for some domain pairs interventions "almost never succeed." SVD subspace overlap (Fig. 5) consistently decreases across domains.
- **Cross-domain structural similarity (Fig. 7, TDA Wasserstein on persistence diagrams, Llama 3.1 8B):** distances between hierarchical-domain pairs are typically 0.3–2.5, whereas distances between a hierarchical domain and a non-hierarchical baseline (Programming Q&A, Business Reviews, Movie Reviews, Brown Corpus) are around 4.3 — supporting shared hierarchical representational structure across domains.

## Critical Assessment

- **Filtering induces selection bias.** The dataset is restricted to instances each LM answers correctly in 5-shot MCQA, so all conclusions about "hierarchical encoding" apply only to relations the model already knows — LHE is not shown to recover hierarchy the model fails to use, and the filtered test sets differ per model (Tbl. 6), complicating cross-model comparison.
- **Tiny training sets and high variance.** Each linear transformation is trained on only 8 samples (App. B), with 5-seed averaging. With 8 samples per depth-domain relation, the learned W_r is likely noisy, and the reported Accuracy/Causality should be interpreted with that variance in mind.
- **Causality is weak for several model-domain combinations.** Qwen3 14B Causality is 0.12 (Organism), 0.25 (Organization), 0.25 (Person) — close to or below what one would want for a "causally relevant" claim. The paper itself notes this suggests "the representations that influence word prediction may not be linearly encoded" in Qwen3 14B, which undercuts the headline "all models encode concept hierarchies in highly interpretable linear representations."
- **Cross-domain "similarity" rests on TDA without a strong null.** The Wasserstein-distance comparison (Fig. 7) uses random representations and 4 non-hierarchical text datasets as baselines, but there is no statistical test or confidence interval; the 0.3–2.5 vs. ~4.3 contrast is suggestive but not formally validated.
- **Single-parent trees only.** The datasets enforce one parent per node, so the method is not tested on polyhierarchical or graph-structured knowledge (e.g., a city that is both in a country and in a trade bloc).

## Strengths

- Genuinely extends prior work (Park et al. 2025) to intermediate layers and multi-token entities — a real methodological advance
- Two-metric evaluation (Accuracy + Causality) is more convincing than decodability alone; the Accuracy/Causality mismatch under domain shift is a nuanced, honest finding
- Four models and five domains give reasonable breadth; per-depth breakdowns (Fig. 8) and full layer profiles are provided
- Code and data released; funded by recognizable grants (JSPS, JST)

## Limitations

- Selection bias from correctness filtering limits claims to already-known relations
- Only 8 training samples per linear transformation; per-model filtered test sets differ
- Several Qwen3 14B Causality scores are near-chance, weakening the universality claim
- Single-parent hierarchies only; polyhierarchical knowledge untested
- TDA cross-domain similarity lacks statistical significance testing

## Relevance

直接对应配置中的「LLM Hidden States & Interpretability」方向：LHE 用线性变换从中间层隐状态恢复层级概念结构，并用因果干预验证其有效性，是线性表示假设在层级知识上的延伸。其"层级信息集中在低维领域特异子空间、但跨域结构相似"的发现，也为理解 LLM 内部表示几何提供了机制性证据。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- 若不过滤掉模型答错的实例，LHE 能否恢复模型"未正确使用"的层级关系？这能否作为探测知识缺口的方法？
- 每个线性变换仅用 8 个样本训练，增大训练集能否提升 Causality，尤其是 Qwen3 14B 在 Organism/Organization 上的低分？
- 单父树假设下成立的方法，能否推广到多父图结构（如 Wikidata 的多继承分类）？
- 跨域结构相似性（TDA Wasserstein 距离）是否有统计显著性？能否用 RSA/CKA 等有对应关系的方法做交叉验证？
- 层级表示在训练过程中如何涌现？in-context learning 是否会调制这些子空间？
