# Sinkhorn Doubly Stochastic Attention Rank Decay Analysis

> **一句话总结：** 本文证明用 Sinkhorn 算法得到的双随机（doubly stochastic）注意力归一化在纯自注意力网络中也会按双重指数速度坍缩到秩一（与 Softmax 相同），但在训练好的 Transformer 上经验性度量显示双随机归一化比标准行随机 Softmax 更好地保留秩，且 skip connection 存在时这一优势被放大。

> **TL;DR:** The authors give the first theoretical characterization of rank collapse under doubly stochastic (Sinkhorn-normalized) attention. They prove that, as for Softmax in Dong et al. (2021), pure self-attention with Sinkhorn converges to a rank-one matrix doubly exponentially with depth (Theorem 2: ‖res(SAN(X))‖₂ ≤ (λβH/√(n³dqk)) raised to ((3^L−1)/2) · ‖res(X)‖₂³). Empirically, on AG's News (encoder Transformer), MNIST and Cats and Dogs (ViT) trained from scratch with the Sinkformers codebase (50 Sinkhorn iterations, Adam), they measure the normalized spectral norm of the residual of chained attention products (Eq. 10) across path depth: Sinkhorn doubly stochastic attention consistently preserves more rank than row-stochastic Softmax, especially when skip connections are enabled, and the advantage disappears for products of independent random stochastic matrices — suggesting it stems from inter-layer attention correlations.

## 核心贡献

- 首次对双随机（Sinkhorn 归一化）自注意力在深度方向的秩坍缩给出严格理论刻画，填补了此前只对 Softmax 行随机情形（Dong et al. 2021）分析的空白。
- 证明纯自注意力网络（无 skip、无 FFN）在 Sinkhorn 双随机归一化下，残差范数 ‖res(SAN(X))‖₂ 随深度 L 以双重指数速度收敛到 0（即输出坍缩到秩一矩阵 (1/n)11⊤）。
- 用谱范数 ℓ₂（真正的范数）替换 Dong et al. 使用的 ℓ₁,∞ 组合范数（不满足三角不等式），并通过引理给出 ℓ₂ 与 ℓ₁,∞ 的关系。
- 给出 Sinkhorn 算子的一阶投影逼近 Q（Lemma 1 / Proposition 4）：Sinkhorn(tY) = (1/n)11⊤ + (t/n²)Q(Y) + o(t)，Q 是到双随机矩阵空间切空间的正交投影。
- 经验性比较训练好的 Transformer 在 Softmax vs Sinkhorn 下的注意力路径秩衰减，并给出对照实验说明该优势依赖于跨层注意力的相关性而非随机矩阵的普遍性质。

## Key Contributions

- First theoretical analysis of rank collapse for doubly stochastic attention normalized via the Sinkhorn algorithm; prior work (Dong et al. 2021) only covered row-stochastic Softmax.
- A norm bound (Theorem 2) showing pure self-attention with Sinkhorn converges to a rank-one matrix doubly exponentially with depth, with a cubic convergence rate arising from the input-dependent nature of the attention matrix.
- Replacement of the non-triangle-inequality-satisfying ℓ₁,∞ norm used in Dong et al. with a proper spectral ℓ₂ norm, plus an explicit relationship between the two (Appendix E).
- A first-order projection-operator approximation of the Sinkhorn operator at zero (Lemma 1, Proposition 4), using the orthogonal projection Q onto the tangent space TDS of the doubly stochastic manifold.
- Empirical evidence on AG's News / MNIST / Cats and Dogs that doubly stochastic normalization preserves rank better than Softmax across depth, amplified by skip connections, plus a control showing the advantage vanishes for independent random stochastic matrices — implicating inter-layer attention correlations.

## Methodology

**Setup.** Standard multi-head self-attention head: SA_h(X) = P·X·W_{V,h} + (1/d_v)1·b_{V,h}^⊤, where the attention matrix P (Eq. 1) is either row-stochastic Softmax or doubly stochastic Sinkhorn applied to (XW_Q+h·1b_Q^⊤)(XW_K+h·1b_K^⊤)^⊤ / √dqk. The Sinkhorn operator maps R^{n×n} → DS^{n×n} = {S : S1=1, 1^⊤S=1^⊤}, implemented in the log domain for numerical stability (50 iterations, empirically sufficient for effective double-stochasticity).

**Path decomposition (after Dong et al. 2021).** A multi-head L-layer network SAN(X) is decomposed into a sum over paths, each path selecting one head per layer: SAN(X) = Σ_{h_1,…,h_L} (P_{h_L}^L···P_{h_1}^1)(X·W_{h_1}^1···W_{h_L}^L) (Eq. 3). Rank decay is studied via how the product of attention matrices P_{h_L}^L···P_{h_1}^1 reduces rank, using the residual res(X) = X − 1·x^⊤ (Eq. 4) where x is the row-mean and (1/n)11⊤ is the rank-one limit of products of doubly stochastic matrices.

**Proof technique.** Define the orthogonal projection Q onto TDS^{n×n} (Eq. 14). Lemma 1 / Proposition 4: Sinkhorn(tY) = (1/n)11⊤ + (t/n²)Q(Y) + o(t). Proposition 1/3: ‖Q(Y)‖₂ ≤ ‖Q(Y)‖_F ≤ λ√rank(Y)·‖Y‖₂ for some 0<λ≤1. The single-head bound (Theorem 1): ‖res(SA_h(X))‖₂ ≤ (λβ/√(n³·dqk))·‖res(X)‖₂³, where ‖W_QW_K^⊤‖₂‖W_V‖₂ ≤ β. Stacking across heads and layers gives Theorem 2's doubly-exponential bound. Proofs in Appendix D recurse backward through layers.

**Empirical protocol.** Models trained from scratch (random init) with the Sinkformers codebase (github.com/michaelsdr/sinkformers), Adam optimizer, cross-entropy loss. Four inference-time configurations on the same trained model: pure SAN, SAN+skip, SAN+FFN, full Transformer. Rank measured two ways: (1) along attention paths P_t = Π_i P_{h_{ℓ_i},b}^{ℓ_i} (Eq. 10) via normalized spectral norm ‖res(P_t)‖₂/‖P_t‖₂ using truncated SVD best-rank-1 approximation, 100 sampled paths per depth; (2) along layer outputs SAN(X)_{ℓ,b} via ‖res(SAN(X)_{ℓ,b})‖₂/‖SAN(X)_{ℓ,b}‖₂, batch mean ± std. Control: products of independently random stochastic matrices (Appendix G).

**Hyperparameters.** AG's News (Table 1): seq len 128, d_model 128, FFN 512, H=4 heads, 24 layers, batch 32, LR 5e-5, dropout 0.3, 20 epochs. MNIST ViT (Table 2): 28×28, patch 4, d_model 128, FFN 512, H=4, 24 layers, batch 100, LR 2e-2, dropout 0, 50 epochs. Cats and Dogs ViT: 224×224, patch 16, d_model 128, FFN 512, H=4, 24 layers, batch 32, LR 3e-5, dropout 0, 50 epochs.

## Results & Highlights

- **Theoretical rank decay bound (Theorem 2, multi-head multi-layer):** ‖res(SAN(X))‖₂ ≤ (λβH/√(n³·dqk))^((3^L−1)/2) · ‖res(X)‖₂³, where 0<λ≤1, n = sequence length, dqk = query/key dim, L = depth, H = heads, and ‖W_QW_K^⊤‖₂‖W_h‖₂ ≤ β. As L grows this goes to zero doubly exponentially — same qualitative behavior as Softmax (Dong et al. 2021), but derived with a proper spectral norm.
- **Single-head bound (Theorem 1):** ‖res(SA_h(X))‖₂ ≤ (λβ/√(n³·dqk))·‖res(X)‖₂³ — the cubic-power dependence on ‖res(X)‖₂ is what produces the doubly-exponential (rather than the classical singly-exponential) depth convergence, because the attention matrix P depends on the input X it is then applied to (Section 3, Appendix G).
- **Empirical rank preservation along attention paths (Figures 3a–c):** across AG's News (Transformer), MNIST (ViT), and Cats and Dogs (ViT), Sinkhorn doubly stochastic normalization yields higher normalized spectral norm of res(P_t) at every path depth than Softmax, i.e., rank is better preserved; the gap widens when skip connections are present (skip connections "shorten" the product by bypassing layers).
- **Output-level rank decay (Figures 4a–c):** a clear Sinkhorn-over-Softmax advantage appears only on AG's News (Figure 4a); on MNIST and Cats and Dogs (Figures 4b–c) the two normalizations look nearly identical at the SAN(X) output level despite the attention-product advantage in Figures 3b–c, because the output is also shaped by multiplication with X and the W_h products.
- **Random-matrix control (Figure 5, Appendix G):** for products of independently sampled random stochastic matrices, Softmax and Sinkhorn exhibit essentially identical rank decay, and skipping half the factors (replacing with identity to mimic skip connections) does not separate them — implying the in-Transformer Sinkhorn advantage is not a generic property of doubly stochastic matrices but depends on inter-layer attention correlations.

## Critical Assessment

The most substantive weakness is the mismatch between the theory and the experiments. The norm bound (Theorem 2) is derived only for pure self-attention (no skip connections, no FFN, no LayerNorm) and shows the same doubly-exponential collapse already established for Softmax by Dong et al. (2021) — so the theory does not by itself predict the empirical Sinkhorn advantage, which is observed only in the full Transformer with skip connections where the bound no longer applies. The authors concede this in Section 4.2: the output-level rank advantage of Sinkhorn is clear only on AG's News (Figure 4a) and essentially disappears on the two ViT tasks (Figures 4b–c), even though the attention-path advantage (Figures 3b–c) is consistent — i.e., the central empirical claim is fragile at the level (output rank) that actually matters for representation quality. The random-matrix control (Appendix G) is honest but undermining: it shows the Sinkhorn advantage vanishes for independent stochastic matrices, meaning the paper cannot explain mechanistically why doubly stochastic attention helps; the authors explicitly leave "how Sinkhorn doubly stochastic attention matrices interact, in comparison to Softmax" as future work. Finally, the analysis carries no task-performance numbers — there is no accuracy/F1 comparison between Softmax and Sinkhorn training, so the reader cannot tell whether better rank preservation translates into better downstream results.

## Strengths

- Genuinely fills a theoretical gap: the doubly-stochastic case had not been analyzed, and the proof machinery (first-order Sinkhorn approximation via the Q projection, Lemma 1/Proposition 4) is clean and reusable.
- Uses a proper spectral ℓ₂ norm instead of the triangle-inequality-violating ℓ₁,∞ of Dong et al., and gives the explicit norm relationship (Appendix E) — a technical improvement.
- Honest empirical reporting, including the negative output-level result on ViT (Figures 4b–c) and the random-matrix control that complicates the story.
- Four inference-time ablations (pure SAN, +skip, +FFN, full Transformer) on the same trained model isolate the contribution of each architectural component cleanly.

## Limitations

- The theoretical bound applies only to pure self-attention and reproduces the known doubly-exponential behavior; it does not predict the empirical Sinkhorn advantage observed in full Transformers.
- The output-level rank advantage is inconsistent across tasks (clear on AG's News, absent on MNIST/Cats and Dogs ViTs).
- No downstream task-accuracy comparison between Softmax and Sinkhorn — rank preservation is not shown to correlate with better classification.
- The mechanism behind the in-Transformer advantage is unidentified; the random-matrix control shows it depends on inter-layer correlations that are not characterized.
- Small models (d_model 128, 24 layers) and small datasets (AG's News, MNIST, Cats and Dogs); no LLM-scale or large-vision-scale validation.
- Sinkhorn iteration count (50) and other hyperparameters chosen empirically; the paper does not study sensitivity to them.

## Relevance

直接映射到 **LLM Hidden States & Interpretability** 与 **ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations**：本文从数学上刻画 Transformer 自注意力在深度方向的"表示坍缩"（rank collapse / 信号退化），并比较两种归一化（Softmax 行随机 vs Sinkhorn 双随机）对隐藏状态秩保持的影响——既属于注意力机制的隐藏状态结构分析，也属于神经网络训练动力学/信号传播的数学基础研究。它对理解深层 Transformer（含 LLM）为何会损失信息、skip connection 与归一化选择如何缓解该问题提供了理论工具。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- 在 LLM 规模（数十层、大词表、因果掩码 decoder）上，Sinkhorn 双随机归一化是否仍比 Softmax 更好地保留秩？因果掩码如何改变双随机性？
- 能否把 skip connection 与 FFN 显式纳入秩衰减界，从而让理论能预测经验优势，而不是只在纯自注意力上成立？
- 输出级秩优势在 ViT 任务上消失、却在文本任务上显著——这是任务性质（因果 vs 全局）、归一化交互，还是 LayerNorm/CLS token 的副作用？
- Sinkhorn 双随机注意力与 Softmax 在下游准确率/收敛稳定性上是否有可测差异？仅看秩保持是否足以预测实用性？
- 注意力矩阵间的跨层相关性是 Sinkhorn 优势的来源——能否量化这种相关性并据此预测何时双随机归一化会失效？
