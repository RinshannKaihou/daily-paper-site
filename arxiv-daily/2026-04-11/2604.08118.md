# Initialisation Determines the Basin: Efficient Codebook Optimisation for Extreme LLM Quantization

> **一句话总结：** 提出 OA-EM（输出感知 EM 初始化）方法，用 Hessian 加权 Mahalanobis 距离替代贪心残差 k-means 初始化 AQLM 加性量化，证明初始化决定优化盆地：在 Llama 3.2 3B 2-bit 下将 PV-tuning 后 WikiText-2 困惑度从 11.76 降至 11.53，且在 b=4 时比贪心 b=16 快 2.8 倍还更好。

> **TL;DR:** On Llama 3.2 3B, Llama 3.1 8B, and Qwen 2.5 3B quantized with AQLM (additive quantization, K=256, group size g=8) at 2 bpp and 3 bpp, the authors introduce the representational ratio ρ=N/K^M and show greedy sequential initialization traps the model in a poor basin: at 2 bpp on Llama 3.2 3B (ρ≈18), greedy gives WikiText-2 perplexity 60.61 (b=8) vs. OA-EM's 17.39 pre-PV-tuning, and 11.76 vs. 11.53 post-PV-tuning; OA-EM at beam 4 (6.1h) beats greedy at beam 16 (16.9h) — a 2.8× speedup with better quality — and the basin persists across PV-tuning, beam widths, and architectures.

## 核心贡献

- 提出表示比 ρ = N/K^M，刻画权重组数与码本容量关系：ρ<1（过完备）时初始化误差可被吸收，ρ≫1（欠完备）时初始化决定优化盆地；Llama 3.2 3B 在 3 bpp 时 ρ≈0.07，2 bpp 时 ρ≈18
- 提出 OA-EM（输出感知期望最大化）初始化：用 Hessian 加权 Mahalanobis 距离替代欧氏距离的 k-means，直接最小化输出重建误差而非权重空间距离，作为 beam search 前的 drop-in 替换
- 证明初始化盆地具有持久性：PV-tuning 将 43 点困惑度差距压缩至 0.23，但 OA-EM 在每个配置下仍更优；beam 宽度对两种初始化有相反效果（贪心 b=8→16 恶化 11.76→12.01，OA-EM 改善 11.53→11.49）
- 展示 OA-EM 在质量-计算前沿 Pareto 占优：最便宜的 OA-EM 运行比最贵的贪心运行产出更好的最终模型

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the representational ratio ρ = N/K^M (weight groups / codebook capacity) that predicts when additive quantization becomes sensitive to initialization: overcomplete (ρ<1) vs. undercomplete (ρ≫1) regimes — for Llama 3.2 3B, ρ≈0.07 at 3 bpp (M=3) vs. ρ≈18 at 2 bpp (M=2), a 256× capacity reduction
- Proposes OA-EM (Output-Aware Expectation-Maximization): replaces greedy residual k-means with Hessian-weighted Mahalanobis distance initialization that directly minimizes output reconstruction error, as a drop-in replacement before beam search
- Proves basin persistence: PV-tuning compresses a 43-point pre-PV perplexity gap to 0.23, yet OA-EM remains better in every configuration; beam width has opposite effects on the two initializations (greedy worsens b=8→16: 11.76→12.01; OA-EM improves: 11.53→11.49)
- Demonstrates Pareto dominance: OA-EM at beam 4 (6.1h) outperforms greedy at beam 16 (16.9h) — 2.8× speedup with better final quality — and the cheapest OA-EM run beats the most expensive greedy run

## Methodology

**Background (AQLM):** Each weight group w∈R^g is encoded as a sum of M codewords ŵ = Σm c_{m,b_m}, from M codebooks of K=256 entries each. At 2 bpp with g=8, M=2 (each group of 8 weights = two 8-bit indices). The layer-wise objective minimizes output reconstruction error L = ||XW − XŴ||²_F (Eq. 1) on calibration data. Codebooks are initialized via residual k-means, then assignments refined by beam search (width b, maintaining b candidates per stage, O(MbK) per group).

**Representational ratio:** ρ = N/K^M where N = weight groups per layer, M = codebooks, K = 256. When ρ<1, K^M representable points exceed N groups, absorbing initialization errors. When ρ≫1, groups compete for limited capacity and greedy placement becomes critical. For Llama 3.2 3B: 3 bpp (M=3, KM=16.8M) gives ρ≈0.07; 2 bpp (M=2, KM=65K) gives ρ≈18.

**Greedy suboptimality (Proposition 1):** Decomposes greedy error into direct cost ||δ||² (δ = first-codebook displacement from optimal), coupling term 2⟨δ, c_{2,j_g} − r*⟩, and non-negative residual mismatch. At 2 bpp only K=256 residual configurations exist (vs. K²=65,536 at 3 bpp), a 256× reduction in error-correction capacity.

**OA-EM algorithm:** Starting from k-means-initialized centroids, alternates for R=3 rounds:
- M-step: Fix assignments, optimize centroids via S=100 Adam steps (cosine LR from η=10⁻⁴ to 0.1η) minimizing L_EM = (1/N)Σ e_i⊤ H_i e_i, where e_i = w_i − c_{b_i} and H_i = X_i⊤X_i + λI is the damped block-diagonal Hessian (λ = 0.01·diag(H)).
- E-step: Fix centroids, reassign each group to nearest centroid under Mahalanobis distance b_i ← argmin_k (w_i−c_k)⊤ H_i (w_i−c_k).

**Models:** Llama 3.2 3B, Llama 3.1 8B, Qwen 2.5 3B. Quantized with AQLM at 2 bpp (M=2, g=8); Llama 3.2 3B also at 3 bpp (M=3, g=8).

**Calibration:** 128 sequences from C4, length 4096. Beam search: b∈{4,8,16}, epochs e∈{5,100} with early stopping at 0.01 relative MSE. PV-tuning: Adam, η_ft=3×10⁻⁴, batch 32, 10K samples, 5 epochs; best WikiText-2 checkpoint selected. Hardware: single NVIDIA A100 80GB (B200 192GB for Llama 8B PV-tuning). Single run, seed 42.

**Evaluation:** WikiText-2 and C4 perplexity (4096 context); zero-shot on ARC-Easy, ARC-Challenge, HellaSwag, PIQA, WinoGrande, LAMBADA via LM Evaluation Harness.

## Results & Highlights

- **Catastrophic greedy failure at 2 bpp (Table 1, Llama 3.2 3B):** Greedy pre-PV-tuning WikiText-2 perplexity: 352.39 (b=4), 60.61 (b=8), 46.01 (b=16) vs. FP16 7.28 — even quadrupling beam width (4→16) only reduces from 352.39 to 46.01. OA-EM stays stable: 16.82 (b=4), 17.39 (b=8), 16.53 (b=16). At b=8, OA-EM achieves 17.39/18.00 (WikiText-2/C4) in 9.2h vs. greedy's 60.61/18.64 in 9.9h.
- **Basin persistence post-PV-tuning (Table 2, Llama 3.2 3B, 2 bpp):** PV-tuning compresses the gap dramatically but OA-EM wins every config: b=4 greedy 12.66 vs. OA-EM 11.53 (−1.13); b=8 greedy 11.76 vs. OA-EM 11.53 (−0.23); b=16 greedy 12.01 vs. OA-EM 11.49 (−0.52); b=8 e=5 greedy 12.69 vs. OA-EM 11.76 (−0.93). The 43-point pre-PV gap (b=4: 352.39 vs. 16.82) compresses 188× to 0.23 (b=8) yet OA-EM remains better.
- **Asymmetric beam scaling (§5.2):** Greedy is non-monotonic post-PV: b=4 (12.66) → b=8 (11.76, best) → b=16 (12.01, worsens). OA-EM is stable and slightly improves: b=4 (11.53) → b=8 (11.53) → b=16 (11.49). Wider search helps only when initialization is already good; for greedy, b=8→16 worsens C4 perplexity (18.64→19.00), suggesting overfitting to calibration.
- **Pareto dominance (Table 16):** OA-EM b=4 (6.1h, WikiText-2 11.53, avg acc 0.589) beats greedy b=8 (9.9h, 11.76, 0.585) — 38% less time, better quality. OA-EM b=8 e=5 (7.3h) matches greedy b=8 e=100 (9.9h) in 26% less time. The cheapest OA-EM run beats the most expensive greedy run.
- **Cross-architecture generality (Table 4, 2 bpp, b=8):** Llama 3.2 3B: greedy 60.61→11.76 vs. OA-EM 17.39→11.53 (post-PV). Llama 3.1 8B: greedy 18.86→9.39 vs. OA-EM 16.38→9.25. Qwen 2.5 3B: greedy 12.50→10.93 vs. OA-EM 12.30→10.73. OA-EM wins on both WikiText-2 and C4 after PV-tuning across all three models.
- **Overcomplete regime (3 bpp, ρ≈0.07, Table 6/7):** Pre-PV gap 0.65 (greedy 9.52 vs. OA-EM 8.87 WikiText-2); post-PV gap compresses 5.4× to 0.12 (8.66 vs. 8.54). OA-EM wins 4/6 downstream tasks, ARC-Easy +3.5pp (.658→.693), LAMBADA acc +1.5pp (.670→.685), avg acc +0.7pp (.647→.654). Gap attenuates but does not vanish.
- **Domain-dependent degradation (Table 5, 2 bpp pre-PV):** Greedy baseline/OA-EM ratio scales with domain distance from C4 calibration: in-domain C4 1.04× (18.64/18.00), near-OOD LAMBADA 1.39× (12.28/8.85), far-OOD WikiText-2 3.49× (60.61/17.39).
- **Downstream accuracy (Table 3, 2 bpp post-PV):** Llama 3.2 3B: OA-EM wins avg acc at all beam configs, largest at b=4 (+1.6pp, .573→.589). Llama 3.1 8B: OA-EM +0.7pp (.649→.656). Qwen 2.5 3B: greedy slightly leads avg (.606 vs. .603) despite OA-EM winning perplexity — attributed to mild bottleneck and high zero-shot variance at 3B scale.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High — the representational ratio ρ as a predictor of initialization sensitivity, the formal greedy suboptimality decomposition (Proposition 1), and the OA-EM algorithm are genuinely new contributions to the additive-quantization-for-LLMs literature. The "initialization determines the basin" finding, with persistence through PV-tuning, is a substantive empirical observation.

**Paper-specific weaknesses:**
1. The downstream accuracy signal is clear only on Llama 3.2 3B where the bottleneck is most severe; on Qwen 2.5 3B the greedy baseline actually leads on average accuracy (0.606 vs. 0.603) despite OA-EM winning perplexity — the authors attribute this to "high variance at 3B scale" but this undermines the claim that OA-EM uniformly improves downstream quality. Only 3 models from 2 families are tested.
2. The paper does not compare absolute perplexity against structured methods (GLVQ, QTIP, QuIP#), explicitly stating the contribution is "orthogonal." This is defensible but leaves open whether free-form + OA-EM is competitive with lattice-based methods at 2 bpp — the central practical question for edge deployment.
3. Single run, fixed seed (42) — no error bars or variance reported. The authors themselves note "zero-shot evaluations are inherently high-variance" yet rely on single-run accuracy numbers for downstream claims.
4. OA-EM introduces hyperparameters (R=3 rounds, S=100 Adam steps, η=10⁻⁴, λ=0.01·diag(H)) that are not tuned per-model; sensitivity to these choices is not analyzed. The Hessian approximation H_i = X_i⊤X_i + λI is block-diagonal and may be crude for layers with strong cross-group coupling.
5. The "basin persistence" claim rests on PV-tuning not erasing the gap, but PV-tuning uses a fixed configuration (5 epochs, η=3×10⁻⁴) — it is possible that more aggressive fine-tuning would erase the difference, which is not tested.

## Strengths

- Clear theoretical framing: ρ predicts initialization sensitivity, Proposition 1 decomposes greedy error into interpretable terms
- OA-EM is a practical drop-in replacement preserving the LUT inference pathway (zero MACs, critical for edge/ARM)
- Comprehensive sweep: 4 beam configs × 2 compression rates × 3 architectures, with pre-PV and post-PV separation isolating initialization effects
- Strong Pareto result: 2.8× speedup with better quality is a concrete practical win
- Honest about limitations (Qwen downstream, single-run, no comparison to structured methods)

## Limitations

- Downstream accuracy advantage not universal (Qwen 2.5 3B: greedy leads on avg acc)
- No comparison to structured methods (GLVQ, QTIP, QuIP#) at 2 bpp
- Single run, seed 42; no variance reported despite acknowledging high-variance evaluation
- OA-EM hyperparameters not sensitivity-analyzed
- Only 3 models, 2 architecture families, English-only benchmarks
- Applies only to free-form additive quantization; does not transfer to lattice/trellis methods

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does OA-EM's basin-persistence finding hold under more aggressive fine-tuning (more epochs, higher LR), or can sufficiently strong PV-tuning erase the initialization gap?
- How does free-form AQLM + OA-EM compare in absolute perplexity to structured methods (GLVQ, QTIP) at 2 bpp on the same models — is the LUT inference advantage worth the perplexity cost?
- Can the representational ratio ρ predict initialization sensitivity for other learned-codebook VQ methods (e.g., GPTVQ, future hybrid structured+free-form)?
- Does the domain-dependent degradation (3.49× far-OOD) imply that OA-EM-quantized models are more robust for multi-domain deployment than greedy-quantized ones?
