# Inside-Out: Measuring Generalization in Vision Transformers Through Inner Workings

> **一句话总结：** 本文提出基于电路发现（circuit discovery）的两个内部机制度量——Dependency Depth Bias (DDB) 用于部署前模型选择、Circuit Shift Score (CSS) 用于部署后性能监控，分别将 OOD 泛化相关性提升 13.4% 和 34.1%，并在 Camelyon17 上使静默失效检测 F1 提升约 45%。

> **TL;DR:** The paper introduces two circuit-based, label-free proxy metrics derived from a Vision Transformer's inner workings: Dependency Depth Bias (DDB) for pre-deployment model selection and Circuit Shift Score (CSS) for post-deployment performance monitoring. On PACS, Camelyon17, and Terra Incognita, DDB's best variant (DDBout) achieves an average correlation of 0.766 vs. 0.699 for the strongest baseline (ID Accuracy), improving correlation by 13.4%; CSS's best variant (CSS(v,SRCC)) reaches 0.811 average correlation across PACS, Camelyon17, FMoW, and ImageNet, beating the strongest baseline (ATC, 0.470) by 0.341 (34.1%), and raises silent-failure alarm F1 by ~45% on Camelyon17.

## 核心贡献

- 提出用模型内部机制（电路）作为预测泛化性能的标签无关代理度量，开辟"机制可解释性用于评估"的新范式。
- 设计 Dependency Depth Bias (DDB)：度量模型对深层 vs 浅层特征的相对依赖，用于部署前在无标签目标数据上选择最佳模型。
- 设计 Circuit Shift Score (CSS)：度量测试分布下电路相对 ID 基线的偏移，用于部署后持续监控性能退化与静默失效。
- 在 PACS、Camelyon17、Terra Incognita、FMoW、ImageNet 等多领域基准上验证两度量的预测能力，显著优于 ID Accuracy、Average Confidence、ATC、RANKME、α-ReQ 等现有代理度量。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a new perspective that uses a model's inner workings (circuits from mechanistic interpretability) as label-free predictive metrics of generalization, rather than only assessing model outputs.
- Proposes Dependency Depth Bias (DDB), a circuit metric quantifying relative reliance on deep vs. shallow source layers, tailored to pre-deployment model selection on unlabeled target data.
- Proposes Circuit Shift Score (CSS), a circuit metric measuring deviation of a test-distribution circuit from an in-distribution baseline circuit, tailored to post-deployment performance monitoring and silent-failure detection.
- Empirically demonstrates superior predictive power across PACS, Camelyon17, Terra Incognita, FMoW, and ImageNet, with DDB improving correlation by 13.4% and CSS by 34.1% on average over the strongest existing proxies.

## Methodology

The method reverse-engineers a Vision Transformer's computational graph as a circuit and derives two metrics from it.

**Circuit definition.** A ViT's computation is represented as a directed graph G=(V,E) at sub-layer granularity (each MLP layer is one node; each attention layer is split into heads). Rather than a binary circuit, the authors adopt a continuous relaxation: for each edge e, the circuit weight c(e) is the expected KL divergence between the full model M(x) and the model M\{e}(x) with edge e mean-ablated, averaged over the (unlabeled) data distribution. Edge weights are discovered with EAP-IG (chosen after benchmarking Causal Tracing, EAP, and EAP-IG in Appendix G for faithfulness/efficiency balance).

**Inter-layer Dependency Matrix (IDM).** Edge weights are aggregated into an inter-layer matrix Λij summing c(e) over edges from source layer i to target layer j. Canonical Correlation Analysis (CCA) is then applied to find a canonical direction v_T maximizing correlation between flattened IDM entries and ground-truth performance, yielding a "Generalization Motif." Averaging across tasks gives a Universal Generalization Motif showing deep-layer edges (rows ≥6) positively correlated with generalization and shallow-layer edges (rows 1-4) negatively correlated.

**Dependency Depth Bias (DDB).** For a ratio parameter τ∈(0,0.5] defining shallow set L_low={1,...,k(τ)} and deep set L_high={L−k(τ)+1,...,L,O} with k(τ)=max{1,⌊τL⌋}, DDB(J)=log(Σ_{i∈L_high,j∈J} Λij / Σ_{i∈L_low,j∈J} Λij). Three variants: DDB_global (all target layers), DDB_deep (deep target layers), DDB_out (output node only). Ablation finds τ=0.3 optimal.

**Circuit Shift Score (CSS).** For a test distribution D_i, CSS(R,d)(M,D_i)=d(R(c^{M}_{D_ID}), R(c^{D_i})) measures distance between the ID-baseline circuit and the test circuit. Two families: vector-based CSS(v,·) with R mapping to an edge-weight vector and d as ℓ2/cosine/SRCC; and graph-based CSS(g,·) with R mapping to a weighted graph and d as Laplacian spectral/NetLSD/Jaccard distance. CSS(v,SRCC) performs best.

**Threshold calibration.** A surrogate-data strategy builds 39 corrupted ID validation domains (CIFAR10-C corruptions + stylization transforms); the CSS value of the corrupted domain whose performance is closest to the desired threshold δ is adopted as the alarm threshold δ′.

**Setup.** Pre-deployment: model zoos of 72-144 ViTs per dataset, trained/fine-tuned from 5 pretrained checkpoints (random-init, OpenAI CLIP, LAION2b CLIP, ImageNet-21k, ImageNet-1k, MAE) via Timm, with learning rates {10⁻¹,3×10⁻²,10⁻²,3×10⁻³,10⁻³}, batch sizes {128,256}, weight decays {0,10⁻¹,10⁻²,10⁻³}. Post-deployment ImageNet uses the ImageNet-1k pretrained model from Timm directly. Correlations are measured by R², SRCC, KRCC.

## Results & Highlights

- DDBout achieves the best overall pre-deployment correlation score of 0.766 (SEM 0.029), outperforming the strongest baseline ID Accuracy (0.632±0.047) and ATC (0.699±0.054) — an average improvement of 13.4% over existing proxies.
- On PACS (style shift), DDBglobal reaches R²=0.913, SRCC=0.921 vs. ID Accuracy R²=0.765, SRCC=0.878 and ATC R²=0.555, SRCC=0.720.
- On Terra Incognita (geographic shift), DDBout reaches R²=0.802, SRCC=0.860, KRCC=0.646 vs. ATC R²=0.199, SRCC=0.358, KRCC=0.257.
- CSS(v,SRCC) achieves the best post-deployment average correlation of 0.811, exceeding the strongest baseline ATC (0.470±0.095) by 0.341 (a 34.1% gain).
- On ImageNet (adversarial + style), CSS(v,SRCC) reaches R²=0.953, SRCC=0.961, KRCC=0.855 vs. ATC R²=0.055, SRCC=0.145, KRCC=0.087.
- For alarm raising on Camelyon17, CSS(v,SRCC) consistently outperforms the best baseline metrics by ~45% in F1 score across the clinically acceptable performance threshold range 0.8-0.9.
- Training-dynamics alignment on PACS: DDBout rises from 2.6 to 4.1 in tandem with OOD accuracy rising from 0.19 to 0.83 for a generalizing model, while a non-generalizing model's DDBout stagnates around -0.9 with OOD accuracy stuck near 0.19.
- Ablation on τ shows τ=0.3 yields the best DDBout correlation (R²=0.801, SRCC=0.849, KRCC=0.671 averaged across datasets).

## Critical Assessment

- The main stated limitation is the computational cost of circuit discovery: while acceptable for one-time pre-deployment selection, it hinders real-time post-deployment monitoring, which is precisely the scenario CSS targets — a tension the paper acknowledges but does not fully resolve.
- The approach is evaluated only on Vision Transformers (ViT-base patch16 224); the paper does not demonstrate that the circuit-motif findings (e.g., the deep-vs-shallow "Generalization Motif") transfer to language models or other architectures, despite drawing heavily on MI techniques developed for LMs.
- Post-deployment, the Generalization Motif (inter-layer topology) becomes unreliable, showing contradictory patterns across PACS (mostly positive) and FMoW (mostly negative); CSS works around this by switching to relative rewiring, but this means the two metrics rely on different signals and the unifying "inner workings" story is partly an a-posteriori adaptation.
- Threshold calibration depends on constructing surrogate corrupted domains (CIFAR10-C corruptions + stylization), whose distribution may not resemble the real shifts a deployed model encounters, leaving the alarm threshold's portability to genuinely novel shifts unvalidated.

## Strengths

- Novel and well-motivated paradigm: turns mechanistic-interpretability circuit discovery from a post-hoc explanatory tool into a predictive evaluation metric.
- Strong and consistent empirical gains over a broad set of competitive baselines (ID Accuracy, Sharpness, AC, ANE, MDE, RANKME, α-ReQ, ATC) across multiple shift types (style, institution, geographic, temporal, adversarial).
- Practically useful framing addressing two real deployment pain points: pre-deployment model selection and post-deployment silent-failure monitoring, both label-free.
- Honest about a key failure mode (post-deployment motif inconsistency) and proposes a concrete workaround (relative rewiring via CSS).

## Limitations

- Computational cost of circuit discovery limits real-time applicability for post-deployment monitoring.
- ViT-only evaluation; transferability to LLMs and other architectures is unverified.
- CSS threshold calibration relies on surrogate corruptions whose realism relative to unseen deployment shifts is uncertain.
- Circuit discovery method choice (EAP-IG) and pruning (top-100 edges) introduce hyperparameters whose sensitivity is only partially studied.

## Relevance

该论文将机制可解释性的电路发现方法转化为预测泛化与静默失效的度量，直接对应"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"（内部机制/电路分析）方向，同时其无标签泛化评估与静默失效检测也服务于"ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations"和"Silent Data Corruption (SDC)"关注点。虽以视觉 Transformer 为载体，但其"用内部机制而非输出预测可靠性"的思路对 LLM 可观测性与可靠性评估具有方法学迁移价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- DDB/CSS 的"深层依赖越强则泛化越好"这一 Generalization Motif 是否在 LLM 上同样成立？能否在 decoder-only transformer 上复现？
- CSS 的相对重连信号能否用于检测 LLM 推理过程中的静默失效（如数值精度退化或激活异常），而非仅分布漂移？
- 电路发现的高计算成本能否通过稀化、缓存或近似 EAP-IG 缓解，使其适合实时部署监控？
- 用电路度量作为训练目标（直接优化 DDB）是否能诱导更可泛化的机制，正如作者 future work 所设想？
- 在 LLM 中，CSS 阈值标定能否借助已有的扰动/损坏基准（而非视觉的 CIFAR10-C 类代理）实现？
