# The Impact of Dimensionality on the Stability of Node Embeddings

> **一句话总结：** 本文系统研究了嵌入维度对五种节点嵌入方法（ASNE、DGI、GraphSAGE、node2vec、VERSE）稳定性与下游性能的影响，发现稳定性随维度变化呈现方法依赖的不同模式，且最高稳定性未必出现在最优性能维度，揭示稳定性与性能之间的权衡。

> **TL;DR:** The paper investigates how embedding dimensionality affects the stability and downstream performance of node embeddings across five methods (ASNE, DGI, GraphSAGE, node2vec, VERSE) on five datasets (Cora, PubMed, Facebook, Wikipedia, BlogCatalog). With 30 runs per dimension across D∈{4,8,16,32,64,128,256,512,1024,2048,4096}, it finds that node2vec and ASNE become more stable at higher dimensions, while GraphSAGE and VERSE peak at medium dimensions then degrade. Crucially, maximum stability does not align with optimal task performance (e.g., VERSE is most functionally stable around D=128-256, not at its accuracy peak), exposing a stability-performance trade-off in graph representation learning.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统研究嵌入维度对节点嵌入稳定性（表征视角与功能视角）的影响，填补了该领域仅被边缘性分析的空白。
- 在五种主流方法（ASNE、DGI、GraphSAGE、node2vec、VERSE）与五个数据集上，跨越 D∈{4…4096}、每组 30 次重复运行的大规模实验，刻画稳定性随维度的变化模式。
- 揭示稳定性与下游性能的不一致：最高稳定性维度与最优性能维度常常不重合，突出维度选择的权衡。
- 区分局部稳定性（k-NN Jaccard、二阶余弦）与全局稳定性（对齐余弦、距离相关），发现二者呈现不同趋势，为稳定性度量选择提供依据。

## Key Contributions

- Conducts the first systematic study of how embedding dimensionality influences node embedding stability, from both representational and functional perspectives, across five widely used methods (ASNE, DGI, GraphSAGE, node2vec, VERSE).
- Empirically characterizes divergent stability-dimensionality patterns: node2vec and ASNE generally become more stable with higher dimensionality, while DGI, GraphSAGE, and VERSE often peak at medium dimensions and degrade thereafter.
- Demonstrates that maximum stability does not necessarily coincide with optimal downstream performance, revealing a stability-performance trade-off relevant to dimension selection.
- Distinguishes local representational stability (k-NN Jaccard, second-order cosine) from global representational stability (aligned cosine, distance correlation), showing they can exhibit qualitatively different trends across dimensions.

## Methodology

The study trains node embeddings with five algorithms — node2vec (via grape), GraphSAGE and DGI (via PyTorch Geometric), VERSE (reference implementation by Tsitsulin et al.), and ASNE (via karateclub) — on five empirical datasets: Cora (2,708 nodes, 7 classes), PubMed (19,717 nodes, 3 classes), Facebook (22,407 nodes, 4 classes), Wikipedia (2,405 nodes, 17 classes), and BlogCatalog (5,196 nodes, 6 classes). Densities range from 0.0002 (PubMed) to 0.0254 (BlogCatalog).

**Dimension sweep.** Dimensionality is varied exponentially over D∈{2^i, i∈{2,3,...,12}} i.e., {4,8,16,32,64,128,256,512,1024,2048,4096}, with 30 embeddings computed per (algorithm, dataset, dimension) combination, yielding 435 pairwise comparisons per cell. Parameter tuning is performed only at D=128 (since dimension does not affect the loss functions), with downstream node classification via a logistic regressor (MLP results in Appendix B). For Cora/PubMed, existing benchmark splits are used; for BlogCatalog/Facebook/Wikipedia, fixed 70%/10%/20% train/val/test splits are sampled.

**Representational stability measures.** Four measures are used: (1) Aligned cosine similarity — solves the orthogonal Procrustes problem to find best orthogonal mapping Q*, then averages cosine similarities after alignment (global); (2) Distance correlation — operates on representational similarity matrices (RSMs) via distance covariance (global, no alignment needed); (3) k-NN Jaccard similarity — compares nearest-neighbor sets (local); (4) Second-order cosine similarity — compares cosine similarities to the union of nearest neighbors (local).

**Functional stability measures.** Four measures capture variability in downstream predictions: (1) Disagreement — rate of conflicting hard predictions between two output matrices; (2) Min-max-normalized disagreement — relates observed disagreement to the minimum/maximum possible given error rates; (3) Stable core — fraction of instances where predictions agree across all 30 embeddings; (4) Jensen-Shannon Divergence (JSD) — average divergence of class-probability outputs.

## Results & Highlights

- Downstream accuracy generally improves with dimension up to a plateau: node2vec and ASNE reach plateaus around D=256-512, while DGI keeps improving up to D=4096 on most datasets (Wikipedia is the exception, where performance decreases after D=1024).
- node2vec and ASNE show increasing local (k-NN Jaccard) stability with higher dimension, but these stability plateaus are reached at much higher dimensions than the downstream-performance plateaus — i.e., performance saturates far before stability does.
- For GraphSAGE and VERSE, k-NN Jaccard stability increases up to a peak dimension then decreases; for GraphSAGE the peak-stability dimension roughly aligns with peak performance, but for VERSE it does not.
- Global (aligned cosine) stability behaves differently from local measures: ASNE and node2vec show high stability at low dimension, decrease to a minimum, then rise again; for VERSE, stability at D=4096 remains much lower than at D=4.
- Functional stability (stable core): for most methods, higher dimension yields more stable hard predictions, correlating with higher accuracy — except GraphSAGE and VERSE, where VERSE is most stable at medium dimensions around D=128-256, which does not always coincide with high accuracy.
- Soft-prediction stability (JSD): ASNE and node2vec achieve highest stability at higher-dimension regimes matching their performance peaks, with divergence values consistently below 0.1; VERSE becomes less stable at higher dimensions after an optimum is reached.
- Across all methods, there is no overlap between optimal dimensions for performance and dimensions of maximum (or minimum) global representational stability, reinforcing the stability-performance divergence.

## Critical Assessment

- The study is limited to small and medium-sized graphs with fewer than 25,000 nodes; the authors explicitly note that at higher dimensions (D>1000) computational cost became prohibitive, so potential linear relationships between dimension and stability at that range may be missed, and stark differences were sometimes observed between D=2048 and D=4096 without intermediate sampling.
- Stability results may be confounded by implementation differences: VERSE uses the original reference implementation while the other four methods come from different frameworks (PyTorch Geometric, grape, karateclub), so some observed instabilities could be implementation-specific rather than algorithmic — the authors acknowledge they cannot rule this out.
- The paper reports trends qualitatively via figures but provides limited quantitative aggregation (e.g., no correlation coefficients between stability and performance dimensions are reported across the full grid), making it hard to assess effect sizes or statistical significance of the claimed stability-performance divergence.
- The choice of similarity measures is acknowledged as a potential confound: representational stability depends on measure selection, and the chosen measures may not perfectly represent the geometry of embeddings from every method.

## Strengths

- First systematic, large-scale (5 methods × 5 datasets × 11 dimensions × 30 runs) study linking dimensionality to both representational and functional node-embedding stability.
- Thoughtful separation of local vs. global and hard-prediction vs. soft-prediction stability, revealing that these perspectives can diverge.
- Practically actionable finding that stability and performance optima differ, informing dimension-selection practice in graph ML.
- Strong reproducibility commitment: all code and experiments are publicly released.

## Limitations

- Restricted to graphs under 25,000 nodes; scalability of findings to large real-world networks unverified.
- Implementation heterogeneity across methods complicates pure algorithmic comparison.
- Largely qualitative results presented through figures; limited quantitative/statistical aggregation of effect sizes.
- Stability measure choice can influence representational-stability conclusions; no single canonical measure.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- 稳定性与性能维度不一致的根因是什么？是过参数化引入的冗余自由度，还是优化景观的多解性？
- 这些表征/功能稳定性度量（对齐余弦、距离相关、稳定核）能否直接迁移到 LLM token/层嵌入的稳定性评估？
- 在更大规模图（>10^5 节点）上，维度-稳定性曲线是否仍呈方法依赖的分异模式？
- 能否设计一种同时优化下游性能与稳定性的维度选择准则（如基于 PIP loss 或 MinGE 的稳定性正则化）？
- 不同实现框架对稳定性的贡献能否通过统一参考实现加以剥离，以验证算法本身vs.实现的归因？
