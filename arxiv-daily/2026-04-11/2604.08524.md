# What Drives Representation Steering? A Mechanistic Case Study on Steering Refusal

> **一句话总结：** 本文提出多 token 激活补丁框架，对 Llama 3.2 3B 与 Gemma 2 2B 的拒绝引导（refusal steering）做机制分析，发现不同方法（DIM/NTP/PO）得到的引导向量使用功能可互换的电路（重叠 ≳90%），且引导主要经 OV 电路传播——冻结全部 QK 注意力分数仅使 ASR 下降 8.75%；据此提出 steering value vector (svv) 分解并对引导向量稀疏化 90–99% 仍保留大部分性能。

> **TL;DR:** Extends activation patching (EAP-IG) to multi-token steered generation to ask which LLM components propagate refusal steering. On Gemma 2 2B Instruct and Llama 3.2 3B Instruct, three steering methodologies (Difference-in-Means DIM, Next-Token-Prediction NTP, Preference-Optimized PO) yield circuits with ≳90% overlap and are functionally interchangeable (faithfulness recovered across vector-circuit permutations; random circuits at 2× size give <10% faithfulness). Steering propagates mainly through the attention OV circuit: freezing all QK attention scores drops ASR by only 8.75% on average across Alpaca and JailbreakBench, whereas freezing OV drops it 71.75%. A mathematical "steering value vector" (svv) decomposition yields semantically interpretable tokens even when the raw steering vector is not interpretable (Gemma NTP). Gradient-based sparsification retains ASR up to ~90% sparsity (Gemma 2 2B) and ~95% (Llama 3.2 3B); IoU of surviving dimensions across DIM/NTP/PO is statistically significant (p ≲ 1e−10 at 20–95% sparsity), indicating a shared subspace.

## 核心贡献

- 提出可推广到多 token 引导生成的激活补丁框架（基于 EAP-IG，T=10 步），把电路发现从单 token 推广到 steered 多 token 解码
- 发现三种引导方法（DIM/NTP/PO）在同一层施加时使用功能可互换的电路（重叠 ≳90%，且交叉置换仍保持 faithfulness），尽管向量间余弦相似度仅 0.10–0.42
- 证明拒绝引导主要经 OV 电路传播：冻结全部 QK 注意力分数仅使 ASR 下降 8.75%，而冻结 OV 下降 71.75%；并提出 steering value vector (svv) 分解，给出输入无关的因果通路
- 用 svv 的 logit lens 揭示语义可解释概念（即使原始引导向量不可解释，如 Gemma NTP），并据此把引导向量稀疏化 90–99% 仍保留大部分 ASR，且不同方法收敛到共享低维子空间

## Key Contributions

- Generalizes activation patching to multi-token steered generation using EAP-IG (T=10 integrated-gradient steps), with a logit-difference importance metric summed across response token positions; enables circuit discovery on steered (not just standard) inference
- Shows three steering methodologies (DIM, NTP, PO) applied at the same layer leverage functionally interchangeable circuits: ≳90% edge overlap, and each vector's minimum-faithful circuit (≥0.85 faithfulness) recovers faithfulness for the other vectors, despite pairwise cosine similarities of only 0.10–0.42
- Demonstrates refusal steering propagates primarily through the attention OV circuit: freezing all QK attention scores during steering drops ASR by only 8.75% on average, vs. 71.75% for freezing OV, 53.75% for ablating svvs, and 44.50% for ablating the direct MLP effect
- Introduces the steering value vector (svv) decomposition svv_h(s) = (s⊙γ) W_OV^h, an input-invariant direct contribution of s through head h; logit-lens on svvs surfaces semantically interpretable refusal/harmfulness tokens even when the raw steering vector is uninterpretable (e.g., Gemma 2 2B's NTP vector)
- Sparsifies steering vectors 90–99% via gradient-based attribution while retaining most ASR; IoU of surviving dimensions across DIM/NTP/PO is statistically significant (hypergeometric p ≲ 1e−10 at 20–95% sparsity), indicating a shared important subspace

## Methodology

**Models & data (§3.1).** Gemma 2 2B Instruct and Llama 3.2 3B Instruct. Steering vectors learned from D_safe (harmless, Alpaca) and D_harm (harmful, from AdvBench, MaliciousInstruct, TDC2023, HarmBench); 128 train / 32 val each. Test: 100 harmful JailbreakBench prompts, 100 harmless Alpaca prompts. Best DIM vector: Gemma layer 15 position -1; Llama layer 12 position -4. Steering coefficient α = −1 (jailbreak) / +1 (induce refusal), applied at every decoded token (multi-token steering).

**Steering methods (§3.2, App. B).** DIM (difference-in-means of mean activations on D_harm vs. D_safe). NTP (Equation 7: minimize −Σ log p(y_i | x, h←h+αv) on prompt-response pairs expressing the concept). PO (uni-directional RePS, Equations 8–9, contrastive desired/undesired responses). NTP/PO trained on 256 harmful + 256 harmless prompts (DIM's 128 + 128 augmented), manually filtered.

**Multi-token activation patching (§4.1).** Residual stream = sum of per-layer MLP + MHA outputs; model as DAG. Clean = steered representation H_steer = H_base + α·S; corrupt = H_base. EAP-IG (Hanna et al. 2024) approximates indirect effect IE(u,v) = (1/T) Σ (u−u*)^T · ∂m/∂v with T=10 steps (Equation 4); gradients taken of the steered model with linearly increasing α·T_i. Importance metric m = logit difference logit(y|x') − logit(y*|x'). Each response token treated as a separate patching sample via teacher forcing; positions where steered and base agree on greedy prediction are masked (zero signal). Edges only from layers ≥ steering layer.

**Circuit extraction & faithfulness (§4.2).** Greedy graph construction (Mueller et al. 2025). |M| = 8790 (Gemma) / 124441 (Llama) edges. Faithfulness = (m(C) − m(∅)) / (m(M) − m(∅)); threshold 0.85. Complement {e∈M : e∉C} tested for near-0 faithfulness. Overlap metric |C1∩C2| / min(|C1|,|C2|).

**OV/QK ablation (§6.3).** To freeze QK: run unsteered forward pass, cache attention weights, then steered forward pass patching cached attention scores at every layer, greedy decode. Same for OV. To ablate svvs: subtract layer-normalized s from value-projection input at each layer during steered generation (removes D_ch svv_h(S) term). To ablate direct MLP effect: subtract s from MLP inputs while steering.

**svv decomposition (§6.2, App. A).** Attention(H^ℓ + α·S) = Σ_h A_h D_c H̃^ℓ W_OV^h + D_ch svv_h(S), where svv_h(s) = (s⊙γ) W_OV^h. Handles RMSNorm (Sinii et al. 2025 ignored it). Logit lens projects svvs to vocabulary (independent of D_ch, hence input-invariant).

**Sparsification (§7).** Gradient-based: r = IE/s (element-wise ratio = average gradient), zero dimensions where r_i < τ for τ ∈ {0, 0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 1, 1.5, 2, 2.5}. IE-based: drop bottom-k by |IE|. Baselines: bottom-k (by |s|), random dropout. Eval on Alpaca (200 samples), JailbreakBench, StrongReject (unseen). IoU of non-zero dimensions across DIM/NTP/PO; hypergeometric significance test.

## Results & Highlights

- **Circuit faithfulness (Fig. 2):** ~10% of edges (900/8790 for Gemma 2 2B; ~13500/124441 ≈ 11% for Llama 3.2 3B) recover 0.85 average faithfulness on multi-token steered refusal; the circuit complement has near-0 faithfulness at all sizes, validating completeness.
- **Cross-method interchangeability (Fig. 3 right, Fig. 4):** DIM/NTP/PO circuits have nearly 100% overlap even between smaller and larger circuits; each vector's minimum-faithful circuit recovers faithfulness for the other two vectors (e.g., Gemma 2 2B DIM circuit faithfulness = 0.97 with DIM, 0.98 with NTP, 0.76 with PO at the smallest size; random circuits at 2× size give <10% faithfulness). Pairwise vector cosine similarities only 0.10–0.42.
- **QK vs. OV ablation (Tbl. 1, ASR on Alpaca A↓ / JailbreakBench JBB↑):** Freezing QK drops performance only 8.75% on average (Gemma: A 0.00→0.03, JBB 0.80→0.74; Llama: A 0.03→0.14, JBB 0.85→0.70). Freezing OV drops it 71.75% (Gemma: A 0.00→0.57, JBB 0.80→0.06; Llama: A 0.03→0.99, JBB 0.85→0.25). Ablating svvs: 53.75%; ablating direct MLP effect: 44.50%. The svv ablation accounts for 74.9% of the OV-circuit performance drop.
- **svv interpretability (Fig. 5):** logit lens on Gemma 2 2B svvs surfaces refusal/harmfulness tokens (e.g., L16H1 → "forbid/forbidding/prohibition" for DIM, PO, NTP; L25H6 → "violence/vandalism/violation" for DIM and PO; SUM → "unethical/illegal/dangerous") even though the raw NTP steering vector is not logit-lens interpretable. L17H6 has a high negative IE and incoherent top tokens, but sign-flipping its svv reveals harmful tokens — suggesting it removes harmful concepts during steering (superposition).
- **Sparsification (Fig. 6):** gradient-based and IE-based sparsification retain ASR up to ~90% sparsity on Gemma 2 2B and >95% on Llama 3.2 3B; on Llama, DIM ASR on StrongReject stays nearly constant with only 9/3072 non-zero dimensions (τ=2.5). Random dropout retains ASR up to ~40% sparsity; bottom-k up to ~80%. IoU across DIM/NTP/PO surviving dimensions is statistically significant (hypergeometric p < 0.05 at every τ > 0, p ≲ 1e−10 at 20–95% sparsity), indicating a shared subspace.
- **Steering ASR (Tbl. 2, LlamaGuard2 judge):** Gemma 2 2B unsteered Alpaca ASR 0.990 / JBB 0.000 / SR 0.013; with DIM 0.000 / 0.800 / 0.850; with NTP 0.030 / 0.840 / 0.831; with PO 0.000 / 0.800 / 0.824. Llama 3.2 3B unsteered 1.000 / 0.020 / 0.035; with DIM 0.030 / 0.850 / 0.872; with NTP 0.000 / 0.830 / 0.882; with PO 0.000 / 0.810 / 0.866.

Authors are Stephen Cheng, Sarah Wiegreffe, and Dinesh Manocha, all at University of Maryland, College Park (per the PDF header). Equal contribution noted for the latter two. The work is an arXiv preprint (v1, 9 Apr 2026); the PDF states "Code will be released upon publication." The paper builds on Arditi et al. (2024, refusal as a single direction), Wu et al. (2025a/b, NTP/PO steering), Hanna et al. (2024, EAP-IG), and Mueller et al. (2025, MIB circuit discovery).

## Critical Assessment

- **Only two small models, one concept.** All experiments are on Gemma 2 2B and Llama 3.2 3B (both ≤3B params) and only the refusal concept. The authors explicitly acknowledge (§Limitations) that findings may be "unique to the refusal concept" and encourage validation on other concepts; generalization to larger models and to non-refusal steering is unproven.
- **MLP role under-analyzed.** The paper repeatedly notes MLPs "make a fairly strong appearance in the circuits" (top edges connect to MLPs and LM head) but does not deeply inspect them (§Limitations), so the mechanism by which steering propagates through MLPs — a stated component of the circuit — remains open.
- **Single steering layer.** Only the best DIM steering layer is analyzed (Gemma L15, Llama L12); NTP/PO are also learned at that layer. The authors concede (§Limitations) they "do not evaluate steering vectors at different layers," so the claim that different methods converge to a shared circuit may not hold at other layers.
- **Faithfulness threshold is somewhat arbitrary.** The 0.85 faithfulness threshold and the ~10% edge budget are chosen by the authors; the complement having near-0 faithfulness is reassuring but the specific "minimum-faithful circuit" size used for interchangeability and sparsification depends on this cutoff.
- **QK-freeze "8.75%" averages two very different models.** The 8.75% figure averages Gemma (3% on Alpaca, 6% on JBB) and Llama (11% on Alpaca, 15% on JBB) — Llama's 15% QK drop on JBB is not negligible, slightly weakening the "QK is irrelevant" framing.

## Strengths

- Genuinely novel multi-token activation patching extension; prior circuit-discovery work was single-token and the closest related work (Sinii et al. 2025) only examined the last two layers
- The OV-vs-QK ablation with the svv decomposition is a clean, mechanistically interpretable result; the svv handling of RMSNorm (which Sinii et al. ignored) is a real technical improvement
- Cross-method interchangeability finding (different steering vectors → same circuit) is counterintuitive given low cosine similarity and is well-supported by the permutation experiment with a random-circuit control
- The sparsification result (90–99% with retained ASR, statistically significant shared subspace) has practical value for efficient steering

## Limitations

- Only two ≤3B models; only the refusal concept
- MLP role in the circuit is acknowledged but not mechanistically analyzed
- Single steering layer analyzed; other layers untested
- Faithfulness threshold and circuit-size choices are author-set
- The 8.75% QK figure averages across models/datasets where one cell is 15%
- Code "will be released upon publication" — not yet available at time of writing

## Questions for Further Exploration

- OV-dominant、QK-near-irrelevant 的结论在更大模型（如 8B/70B）和其他概念（persona、推理风格）上是否成立？
- 电路中占据重要位置的 MLP 子模块究竟如何传播引导信号？是否也存在类似 svv 的可分解结构？
- 若在不同层施加引导，三种方法是否仍收敛到同一电路？层选择本身是否是比方法选择更重要的变量？
- 90–99% 稀疏化后保留的共享子空间，与 Arditi et al. (2024) 的"单一拒绝方向"在几何上是什么关系？是同一方向还是其低维支撑？
- svv 分解能否用于诊断引导失败案例（Braun et al. 2025 的不可靠性），例如当 svv 在某些头语义不一致时预测性能下降？
