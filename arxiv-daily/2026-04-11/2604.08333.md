# Lost in the Hype: Revealing and Dissecting the Performance Degradation of Medical Multimodal Large Language Models in Image Classification

> **一句话总结：** 本文通过逐模块、逐层的特征探针（feature probing）系统诊断14个医疗多模态大模型在图像分类任务上性能退化的根源，揭示四种失效模式（视觉表征质量受限、连接器投影保真度损失、LLM推理理解缺失、语义映射错位），并提出特征健康分数（FHS）量化表征演化健康度。

> **TL;DR:** The paper diagnoses why 14 open-source medical MLLMs (MedVLM-R1, HuatuoGPT-Vision-7B/34B, Lingshu-7B/32B, ShizhenGPT-7B/32B-VL, Hulu-Med-4B/7B/14B/32B, MedGemma-4B/27B, MedGemma-1.5-4B) consistently underperform traditional DL models (CNNs/MLPs/ViTs/hybrids) on three medical image classification datasets (BUSI, COVID19-CT, Chest-Xray). Using module-by-module, layer-by-layer feature probing across the vision tower→connector→LLM pipeline, it identifies four failure modes and introduces a Feature Health Score (FHS). It shows medical adaptation yields only ~0.59-1.37% average accuracy gains over general-domain base models, and that vision-tower SFT with cross-entropy dramatically outperforms MLLM SFT (e.g., ShizhenGPT-7B-VL: 89.33 vs 86.00 on BUSI), exposing the suboptimal state of the vision tower under autoregressive objectives.

## 核心贡献

- 首次对医疗 MLLM 分类性能退化进行逐层、逐模块的系统性解剖，覆盖视觉塔、连接器、LLM 及最终语义输出全流程。
- 提炼出四种关键失效模式：视觉表征质量受限、连接器投影保真度损失、LLM 推理理解缺失、语义映射错位，为诊断提供统一词汇。
- 提出受 Sharpe Ratio 启发的特征健康分数（FHS），含增长因子与波动惩罚，实现跨模型、跨数据集的可比量化评估。
- 揭示三个反直觉障碍：医疗适配收益有限、视觉塔在自回归目标下处于次优状态、LLM 推理中理解与利用的耦合纠缠。

## Key Contributions

- Conducts the first layer-wise, module-level dissection of classification performance degradation in medical MLLMs, tracing how visual information is distorted, diluted, or overridden across the vision tower → connector → LLM pipeline.
- Distills recurring degradation patterns into a taxonomy of four failure modes: quality limitation in visual representation, fidelity loss in connector projection, comprehension deficit in LLM reasoning, and misalignment of semantic mapping.
- Introduces a Feature Health Score (FHS) inspired by the financial Sharpe Ratio, combining a growth factor (net improvement from early/worst to final layer) and a volatility penalty (stability of the feature-evolution path), enabling apples-to-apples cross-model comparison via a four-score profile (FHS_V, FHS_C, FHS_L, P_final).
- Reveals three counter-intuitive barriers: medical adaptation offers only marginal gains, vision towers trained under autoregressive objectives yield suboptimal classification features, and comprehension-utilization entanglement in LLM reasoning fundamentally limits utilization ceiling.

## Methodology

**Datasets.** Three medical image classification datasets: BUSI (breast ultrasound, 3 classes: normal/benign/malignant, 780 images), COVID19-CT (CT COVID-19 diagnosis, 2 classes, 349 positive + 397 negative), Chest-Xray (pediatric pneumonia screening, 2 classes, 5,856 images). Train/test splits: BUSI 598/148, COVID19-CT 630/150, Chest-Xray 5216/640.

**Models.** 14 open-source medical MLLMs spanning six series: MedVLM-R1-2B (2.21B), HuatuoGPT-Vision-7B/34B (8.29B/34.75B), Lingshu-7B/32B (8.29B/33.45B), ShizhenGPT-7B/32B-VL (8.29B/33.45B), Hulu-Med-4B/7B/14B/32B (4.83B–33.21B), MedGemma-4B/27B (4.30B/27.43B), MedGemma-1.5-4B (4.30B). Vision towers include redesigned ViT (trained from scratch), CLIP ViT-L/14@336, SigLIP with 2D RoPE, MedSigLIP; connectors are MLP; LLMs include Qwen2/Qwen2.5 (2B–34B), Yi-1.5-34B, Gemma-3 (4B/27B). Compared against 23 traditional DL models: 8 CNN-based (ResNet50, MobileNetV2, FasterNet, RegVGG, ConvNeXt-S, ConvMixer, InceptionNeXt-S, EfficientNet-B0), 4 MLP-based (MLP-Mixer-B, S2-MLP, HireMLP-B, CycleMLP-B4), 4 ViT-based (CrossViT-18, BiFormer-B, FasterViT-2, GroupMixFormer-B), 7 hybrid-based (SCNet, MoCoViT1.0, HiFuse-S, MedVMamba, ConvMLP-S, TransXNet-B, Conv-SdMLPMixer).

**Three complementary experiments.** (1) Dataset-specific SFT of each medical MLLM via LoRA (rank 8, alpha 32) on all linear layers. (2) Feature probing on original (frozen) MLLMs: a two-layer MLP probe (ReLU, dropout 0.1) is attached at each layer of the vision tower, connector, and LLM; probe parameters optimized while backbone frozen, so probe accuracy reflects task-relevant information at that point. (3) Feature probing repeated on fine-tuned MLLMs. Training: AdamW, lr 1e-4, cosine scheduler with 0.05 warmup ratio, 20 epochs, batch size 4, on 8 NVIDIA A800 GPUs and 8 Ascend 910B NPUs.

**Feature Health Score (FHS).** For module M∈{V,C,L} with n layers: Growth Factor GF_M = ln(P(M,end)/P(M,start)) · sqrt(P(M,min)); Volatility Penalty VP_M = exp(-λ · Σ_{i=1}^{n-1} |P(M,i+1)-P(M,i)|/P(M,end)) with λ=0.2; FHS_M = P(M,end)·(1+GF_M)·VP_M. Yields a four-score profile (FHS_V, FHS_C, FHS_L, P_final).

## Results & Highlights

- Medical MLLMs consistently underperform traditional DL methods across all three datasets (Figure 1): on BUSI traditional methods reach ~95% accuracy vs. MLLMs lagging; on COVID19-CT ~94% vs. lower; on Chest-Xray ~93% vs. lower, with the gap persisting across imaging modalities indicating a systematic architectural limitation.
- Medical adaptation yields only marginal gains over general-domain base models: HuatuoGPT-Vision-7B +1.37%, Lingshu-7B +1.27%, ShizhenGPT-7B-VL +0.59% average accuracy over Qwen2.5-VL-7B; MedGemma-4B actually underperforms Gemma-3-4B by 1.66%, while MedGemma-1.5-4B yields only +1.17%.
- Vision tower SFT with cross-entropy dramatically outperforms MLLM SFT: ShizhenGPT-7B-VL achieves 89.33 (BUSI), 70.03 (COVID19-CT), 85.22 (Chest-Xray) under vision-tower SFT vs. 86.00, 64.13, 78.66 under MLLM SFT — exposing that autoregressive objectives yield far less discriminative vision-tower features than simple cross-entropy.
- Unfreezing the vision tower during LoRA fine-tuning does not reliably help: HuatuoGPT-Vision-7B shows -0.38% average gap (frozen vs. fine-tuned), MedGemma-4B only +0.27%.
- The connector score FHS_C shows the least variation across models (best and worst values are closest), indicating the connector's impact is secondary; FHS_V and FHS_L range widely, identifying the vision tower and LLM as the dominant bottlenecks.
- Dataset hierarchy: averaged over all models, BUSI achieves the highest FHS scores, followed by COVID19-CT, with Chest-Xray lagging substantially behind — mirroring the performance gap between MLLMs and traditional DL.
- Comprehension-utilization entanglement: utilization curves (probing final input token) typically originate below comprehension curves (probing averaged image token embeddings) in early layers but catch up in mid-to-deep layers; the utilization ceiling is fundamentally constrained by the comprehension ceiling, which on Chest-Xray is severely limited.

## Critical Assessment

- The study is confined to three relatively small, binary/3-class classification datasets (BUSI 780 images, COVID19-CT 746 images, Chest-Xray 5856 images); the failure modes and FHS framework are not validated on larger, multi-class, or more complex diagnostic tasks (e.g., lesion detection, grading), so generalization of the taxonomy beyond simple classification is unverified.
- The FHS metric, while motivated by the Sharpe Ratio, is a heuristic composite whose weighting (λ=0.2, the multiplicative form) is not justified by any theoretical or clinical optimality criterion; the paper does not provide sensitivity analysis on λ or on the functional form, so the cross-model rankings could be fragile to metric-design choices.
- The "comprehension deficit" failure mode is attributed to causal attention preventing image-token representations from being contextualized by task-relevant language — but this is a known architectural property of all autoregressive MLLMs, and the paper does not test whether remedies (e.g., bidirectional attention over the visual prefix, or prepending the question) actually close the gap, leaving the diagnosis causal-but-unmitigated.
- The vision-tower SFT vs. MLLM SFT comparison (Table 3) uses only two models (ShizhenGPT-7B-VL, MedGemma-1.5-4B) and does not control for data or compute, so the strong claim that autoregressive objectives inherently cap vision-tower discriminability rests on a narrow evidence base.

## Strengths

- Genuinely systematic, pipeline-wide probing design (84 curves across 14 models × 3 datasets) that moves beyond phenomenological benchmarking to locate where information is lost.
- The four-failure-mode taxonomy is a useful, reusable vocabulary for diagnosing MLLM classification failures.
- The FHS provides a principled, comparable quantitative lens where prior work relied on isolated, study-specific probing.
- Honest, counter-intuitive findings (medical adaptation barely helps; vision-tower SFT dominates MLLM SFT) challenge scale-centric assumptions in the field.

## Limitations

- Only three small, simple classification datasets; taxonomy not validated on complex multi-class or detection tasks.
- FHS is a heuristic metric without theoretical justification or sensitivity analysis.
- Comprehension-deficit cause (causal attention) is diagnosed but no mitigation is tested.
- Vision-tower-SFT-dominance claim rests on only two models without compute/data controls.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- 四种失效模式（视觉表征、连接器、LLM 理解、语义映射）是否在通用（非医疗）多模态大模型分类任务上同样成立？能否用 FHS 框架跨域验证？
- 因果注意力导致的"理解缺失"，能否通过视觉前缀双向注意力或问题前置等简单改造缓解，从而提升 LLM 对图像 token 的语境化？
- FHS 的 λ 参数与乘法形式对跨模型排序的敏感性如何？是否存在更鲁棒或理论支撑的健康度度量？
- 视觉塔在自回归目标下次优的发现，是否意味着医疗 MLLM 应采用判别式视觉塔 + 生成式 LLM 的解耦训练？这对 LLM 隐藏状态可解释性有何启示？
- 探针准确率与最终语义输出之间的"语义映射错位"差距，能否作为检测 LLM 推理可靠性的实用信号？
