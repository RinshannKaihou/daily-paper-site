# The Lifecycle of the Spectral Edge: From Gradient Learning to Weight-Decay Compression

> **一句话总结：** 在 Dyck-1 与 SCAN 的 grokking 过程中将谱边缘分解为梯度与权重衰减分量，发现 grokking 时两者沿边缘对齐、边缘从梯度驱动（88–98% 梯度能）翻转为权重衰减驱动的压缩轴（0.2–5% 梯度），该压缩轴对扰动平坦（max KL≈0.00005）但消融灾难性（∆acc=−0.58，比随机方向敏感 >4000 倍），且信息被非线性重编码（MLP R²=0.99 vs 线性 R²=0.86）而非丢失。

> **TL;DR:** On a 2-layer Transformer (Dyck-1, ~150K params) and a 6-layer encoder-decoder Transformer (SCAN, ~1.5M params) trained with AdamW, the author decomposes the spectral edge (dominant Gram-matrix singular vector of parameter updates) into gradient vs. weight-decay components and shows it flips from gradient-driven (97.6% gradient energy pre-grok on Dyck) to WD-driven (99.8% WD at grok on SCAN); the post-grok edge is perturbation-flat (max KL≈0.00005, Hessian curvature 0.078) yet ablation-critical (∆acc=−0.58±0.09 vs. <10⁻⁴ for 20 random-direction controls, >4000× ratio), and removing WD post-grok reverses compression (linear R²: 0.85→0.99) while preserving accuracy at 0.97.

## 核心贡献

- 证明谱边缘在 grokking 中经历两阶段生命周期：grokking 前梯度驱动（88–98% 梯度能）且功能活跃；grokking 时梯度与权重衰减沿边缘对齐（同向推动），随后翻转为权重衰减驱动的压缩轴（0.2–5% 梯度，95–99.8% 权重衰减）
- 揭示消融悖论：压缩轴对扰动平坦（max KL≈0.00005，Hessian 曲率 0.078）但对消融灾难性（∆acc=−0.58±0.09，比随机 2 维子空间敏感 >4000 倍，20 次试验全部 |∆acc|<10⁻⁴）
- 证明信息在压缩中被非线性重编码而非丢失：线性探针 R² 从 0.98 降至 0.86±0.01，但 MLP 探针（64 隐单元）保持 R²=0.990±0.003；二次探针失败（R²=0.59），说明重编码是深层非线性
- 识别三种普适类（功能型/混合型/压缩型），由间隙流方程中梯度驱动（Term 3）与权重衰减阻尼（Term 2）的平衡预测

## Key Contributions

- Establishes a two-phase lifecycle of the spectral edge: gradient-driven (88–98% gradient energy) and functionally active pre-grokking, then flipping at grokking to a weight-decay-driven compression axis (0.2–5% gradient, 95–99.8% WD) when gradient and WD align along the same direction
- Documents the ablation paradox: the post-grok edge is perturbation-flat (max KL≈0.00005, Hessian curvature 0.078 on Dyck) yet ablation-critical (∆acc=−0.58±0.09 vs. |∆acc|<10⁻⁴ for 20 random-direction controls, >4000× ratio)
- Shows information is nonlinearly re-encoded, not lost: linear probe R² drops 0.98→0.86±0.01 while an MLP probe holds at R²=0.990±0.003; quadratic probes fail (R²=0.59), confirming genuinely deep nonlinear re-encoding
- Causally establishes WD as the engine of compression (not generalization) via a post-grok WD intervention: removing WD recovers linear R² from 0.85 to 0.99 while accuracy stays at 0.97
- Identifies three universality classes (functional/mixed/compression edges) predicted by the Term 2/Term 3 balance in the gap flow equation, with a concrete WD-scheduling prediction

## Methodology

**Tasks and models:**
- Dyck-1 balanced-parentheses depth prediction: 2-layer causal Transformer (d_model=128, 4 heads, ~150K params), 50 training sequences, 5000 test, predicts stack depth 0–12.
- SCAN compositional generalization: 6-layer encoder-decoder Transformer (d_model=256, 4 heads, ~1.5M params), 2048 training pairs, 500 test.
- Both trained with AdamW (β2=0.98), weight decay ω∈{0,1}, 3 seeds each (42, 137, 2024). With ω=1: Dyck groks at steps 600–1400, SCAN at 2500–4000. With ω=0: no grokking in any run (0/6 per task; combined with thesis runs, 24/24 grok with WD, 0/24 without).

**Spectral edge construction:** Trajectory matrix X(t) from W=5 consecutive parameter-update deltas (restricted to attention weights); extract singular values σ1≥...≥σW and right singular vectors. Spectral gap g23=σ2²−σ3² compresses 33× (Dyck) and 43× (SCAN) during grokking, k*=1 universally.

**Decomposition:** AdamW's update splits exactly as Δθ = Δθ_grad (Adam-processed gradient) + Δθ_wd (=−ηωθ). Each component is projected onto the Gram singular vectors vk to measure the fraction of update energy from each source.

**Six causal experiments:** grad-WD decomposition, ablation with random controls (20 trials/phase), ε-sweep perturbation curves, Hessian curvature (v⊤Hv), nonlinear probes (linear/quadratic/MLP-64), and a four-condition WD intervention (ω=0, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0) continued from a post-grok checkpoint.

## Results & Highlights

- **The flip (§3.2):** Pre-grok, v1 is gradient-driven at 97.6% (Dyck) and 88.7% (SCAN) of update energy; at grok this drops to 5.3% (Dyck) and 0.2% (SCAN), with weight decay providing the remainder. On bulk directions (v3, v4) gradient and WD oppose, as in normal regularization; on v1 they align (both push the same direction) — the microscopic signature of the phase transition.
- **Ablation paradox (§4):** Projecting out v1+v2 degrades accuracy by 0.26–0.62 across phases; 20 random 2-D controls per phase all give |∆acc|<10⁻⁴ (>4000× ratio). SCAN edge ablation: ∆acc=−0.49 (grok) vs. −0.03 (memorized, where the edge has collapsed). Yet perturbing along v1 barely changes output: max KL≈0.00005, Hessian curvature v1⊤Hv1=0.078 (grok edge) vs. 1.84 (memorized bulk).
- **Nonlinear re-encoding (§5):** Linear probe for depth on layer-1 reps drops 0.98→0.86±0.01 (as low as 0.67 for individual seeds); MLP probe (64 units) holds R²=0.990±0.003; quadratic probe fails (R²=0.59). Depth information is present but nonlinearly encoded.
- **WD intervention causal test (§6, Table 1):** From a post-grok Dyck checkpoint, ω=0 gives acc=0.973, linear R²=0.987, entropy=2.15, ||θ||=40.4; ω=1.0 gives acc=0.982, linear R²=0.851, entropy=2.27, ||θ||=15.3; ω=2.0 gives acc=0.985, linear R²=0.709, entropy=2.28, ||θ||=14.3. WD drives compression (lower R², higher entropy, lower ||θ||) but not generalization (accuracy survives removal).
- **Three universality classes (§7, Table 2):** Functional edge (modular arithmetic: high grad%, edge carries distinct Fourier modes ω=25/29); Mixed edge (Dyck: 87% grad late, R²=0.80, 18° rotation); Compression edge (SCAN: 2% grad late, R²=0.04, frozen at 8° rotation, 99.8% WD at grok).
- **Multi-seed replication (§9):** Across seeds 42/137/2024: ablation ∆acc=−0.58±0.09; linear R²=0.86±0.01; MLP R²=0.990±0.003; depth Fourier elevation=4.1±0.7×. SCAN full suite: edge ablation ∆acc=−0.49 (grok) vs. −0.03 (memo); Hessian curvature 0.001 (grok edge) vs. 1.83 (memo bulk); random ablation zero effect (>10⁷× edge/random ratio).

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High — the grad-WD decomposition of the spectral edge and the two-phase lifecycle (learning→compression) is a genuinely new lens on grokking, and the ablation paradox (flat-but-essential) plus the causal WD intervention are well-designed.

**Paper-specific weaknesses:**
1. Both tasks are small-scale (150K–1.5M params, 50–2048 training samples). The author explicitly acknowledges (§10.4) that the grad-WD decomposition "has not been tested at that scale" — the thesis confirms k*≤3 and gap-loss correlation up to 124M params, but the central decomposition result of *this* paper is unvalidated beyond 1.5M params.
2. The causal claim is overstated relative to evidence. The paper says alignment appears "if and only if grokking occurs" (§3.4) and calls this causal, but immediately admits "this is correlational, not causal—alignment could be a consequence rather than a cause of generalization." The WD intervention (§6) establishes WD as necessary for *compression*, not for *grokking* — the algorithm survives WD removal (acc 0.973), so WD's causal role in the phase transition itself remains unproven.
3. Heavy self-citation: 8 of ~17 references are the author's own arXiv preprints, several of which appear to overlap substantively (the "spectral edge thesis," "functional modes," "commutator analysis"). This makes the novelty boundary of this paper vs. the companion papers hard to assess without reading all eight.
4. The depth basis for Dyck is "correct by construction" (§10.4); for tasks where the natural functional coordinate is unknown, the Fourier-analysis framework requires discovering the basis — an open problem that limits generality.

## Strengths

- Elegant, multi-projection experimental design (weight space, function space, representation space, attention space, Fourier) all pointing at the same mechanism
- Striking and well-controlled ablation result (>4000× edge/random ratio with 20 random controls per phase)
- The causal WD intervention (4 conditions) cleanly separates compression from generalization
- Concrete, testable WD-scheduling prediction (high WD to grok, then reduce WD for linearly-accessible representations)
- Full multi-seed replication with standard errors

## Limitations

- Only two small-scale sequence tasks; grad-WD decomposition untested at LLM scale (acknowledged)
- Correlational alignment↔grokking evidence does not prove alignment causes grokking
- The natural functional coordinate (depth basis) is known for Dyck by construction; generalization to tasks with unknown coordinates is open
- Single author and heavy reliance on self-cited companion preprints
- No code-of-conduct/peer-review signal beyond arXiv preprint status

## Relevance

直接映射到「LLM 训练稳定性与可观测性」与「LLM 隐藏状态与可解释性」两个关注领域：论文通过谱边缘分解揭示 grokking 阶段的训练动力学相变机制（梯度→权重衰减驱动的压缩），并提供非线性重编码的探针证据，为训练可观测性和表示可解释性提供数学工具。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the grad-WD alignment flip occur during LLM pretraining (e.g., at capability-emergence transitions), and can it serve as an early-warning signal?
- Can the WD-scheduling prediction (high WD to grok, then reduce) improve linear probe-ability of large-scale model representations without sacrificing capability?
- Does the compression-edge universality class dominate in overparameterized LLMs as the author predicts, and what would that imply for parameter-efficient compression along the spectral edge?
- Is the grad-WD alignment a cause or consequence of grokking — can inducing alignment without WD trigger generalization?
