Searching for GEMS: Discovery of the Nearby Post-Common-Envelope Binary System TIC-460388167

一句话总结： 本文发现近邻（57 pc）食双星系统TIC-460388167（白矮星+M型矮星，PCEB），周期0.63596258天，白矮星温度7607 K（属于此类系统中最冷之一），并首次给出PCEB中主序伴星的速率分辨自转轮廓，确认其与轨道周期同步自转（Vrot≈26 km/s）。
TL;DR: Discovery via TESS Sector 51 plus ground-based multi-band photometry (APO g', RBO R, TMMT I) and HPF/HET radial velocities of the nearby (57 pc) detached eclipsing post-common-envelope binary TIC-460388167, with an orbital period P=0.63596258±0.00000012 d, a 7607±127 K / 0.0131±0.0003 R_sun white dwarf (one of the coolest WDs in a PCEB, 97th percentile) and a 3151±59 K / 0.327±0.006 R_sun M dwarf, plus the first velocity-resolved rotational profile of a PCEB MS companion confirming synchronous rotation (Vrot≈26 km/s vs predicted V sin i≈26 km/s).

核心贡献

  • 发现并精确刻画了近邻（57 pc）食后公共包络双星系统TIC-460388167（白矮星+M矮星）。
  • 通过TESS S51光变曲线、APO g'/RBO R/TMMT I多波段测光及HPF/HET视向速度，联合MCMC+PHOEBE建模同时拟合光变与视向速度曲线。
  • 该系统白矮星（T=7607 K）为已知PCEB中最冷之一（第97百分位），轨道周期（0.636天）为已知食PCEB中最长之一（第92百分位）。
  • 首次给出PCEB主序伴星的速率分辨自转轮廓，直接证实其潮汐锁定、与轨道周期同步自转。

Key Contributions

  • Discovery and full characterization of the nearby (57 pc) detached eclipsing post-common-envelope binary (PCEB) TIC-460388167, originally flagged as a planet candidate in the GEMS survey.
  • Joint photometric+spectroscopic modeling (PHOEBEv2.4 + emcee MCMC) of multi-band light curves and HPF/HET radial velocities yielding precise stellar and orbital parameters.
  • Hosts one of the coolest known white dwarfs (T1=7607±127 K) in a PCEB (97th percentile for coolness) and one of the longest-period eclipsing PCEBs known (P=0.636 d, 92nd percentile).
  • First velocity-resolved rotational broadening profile of the main-sequence companion in a PCEB, directly confirming synchronous rotation (V sin i≈Vrot≈26 km/s matches the synchronous prediction).

Methodology

Discovery data: TESS Sector 51 (600s cadence, ~24-day baseline from 2022 Apr 23 to May 18) reduced with tglc; ground-based multi-band photometry — APO 3.5m ARCTIC SDSS g' (45s exposures, 0.2% RMS, one full 3.6 hr eclipse on 2025 Apr 18), Red Buttes Observatory 0.6m Bessel R (45s, ~0.5% RMS), TMMT 0.3m Bessel I (120s, ~1.5% RMS, three nights); spectroscopy — LAMOST archival (R~1800, 3700-9000 Å) for activity, and HPF on the 10m Hobby-Eberly Telescope (R~55,000, 8080-12780 Å, 28 orders) for 12 RV points over six nights (two back-to-back 969s exposures per night, RV uncertainty ~0.2 km/s). HPF-SpecMatch (orders 5 and 17) gave M-dwarf parameters; broadening functions computed with a PHOENIX template. Light curve and RV jointly fit with PHOEBEv2.4 + emcee MCMC; free parameters T1, R1, R2, inclination, P, t0 (light curve) and M1, e, gamma (RV); M2=0.34 M_sun fixed from EXOFASTv2 SED+MIST fit. SED fit used Gaia parallax + 8 broadband fluxes (Pan-STARRS through WISE2). Out-of-eclipse modulations modeled with ellipsoidal variations plus a single 55-deg-radius cool starspot at 70 deg longitude (temp 0.995×stellar).

Results & Highlights

  • Orbital period P = 0.63596258 ± 0.00000012 d, inclination i = 89.0 ± 0.4 deg, eccentricity e = 0.001 ± 0.001 (consistent with expected circularization for P < 2 d).
  • White dwarf: T1 = 7607 ± 127 K, R1 = 0.0131 ± 0.0004 R_sun (~1.43 R_earth), M1 = 0.61 ± 0.04 M_sun (near peak of WD mass function), log g = 7.991 ± 0.033 — consistent with a ~1.2 Gyr WD cooling age.
  • M dwarf: T2 = 3151 ± 59 K, R2 = 0.327 ± 0.006 R_sun, M2 = 0.34 ± 0.01 M_sun, log g = 4.94 ± 0.02, [Fe/H] = -0.04 ± 0.16; note R2 is larger than the 0.22 R_sun predicted by standard T-R relations, an unexplained discrepancy.
  • RV semi-amplitude K2 = 156.2 ± 3.1 km/s (the ~150 km/s signal was the first conclusive evidence the companion is stellar, not planetary); systemic velocity gamma = -12.2 ± 0.2 km/s.
  • First velocity-resolved rotational profile of a PCEB MS companion: measured broadening matches the PHOEBE synchronous-rotation model predicting V sin i ≈ Vrot ≈ 26 km/s, confirming tidal locking (synchronization timescale ~3000 yr for P=0.636 d).

Critical Assessment

The paper is a careful, self-consistent discovery and characterization with internal cross-checks (light-curve R2 = 0.327 R_sun agrees with SED R2 = 0.34 R_sun; HPF temperature 3151 K agrees with SED 3182 K). A concrete weakness is the M-dwarf radius anomaly: R2 = 0.327 R_sun is larger than the 0.22 ± 0.03 R_sun predicted by standard temperature-radius relations, and the paper states "The resolution to this discrepancy is not obvious" — no physical explanation is given beyond noting the CE phase should not affect secondary mass/radius. The single-spot model for out-of-eclipse modulation is acknowledged as "the simplest possible model for illustrative purposes"; real spot distributions are unconstrained. The WD mass depends on a fixed M2 = 0.34 M_sun from SED/MIST, and the paper admits adopting the alternative M2 = 0.22 M_sun would shift component radii by up to ~10%. SED model discrepancies in the 5500-6100 Å and >8800 Å ranges are unresolved (attributed to general Phoenix model failures for ~3150 K stars, validated against five comparison stars).

Strengths

  • Joint, self-consistent fit of multi-band light curves and radial velocities with full MCMC posteriors and reduced chi-squared reported per dataset.
  • Multiple independent parameter estimates (HPF spectra, SED, light curve) that broadly agree, strengthening credibility.
  • First velocity-resolved rotational broadening profile for a PCEB main-sequence companion — a genuine observational first.
  • Long >2.99-year TESS+APO baseline yields period constrained to a fraction of a second.
  • Adds a rare cool-WD / long-period eclipsing PCEB to a sample of only a few dozen eclipsing systems.

Limitations

  • Unexplained M-dwarf radius inflation relative to standard models.
  • Single-spot model is a simplification; spot properties (size, longitude, temperature contrast) are illustrative.
  • WD mass relies on a fixed secondary mass; adopting a different M2 shifts results by up to ~10%.
  • No determination of the flare's physical location on the system.
  • Only one TESS sector (~24 days), so long-term activity/starspot evolution is unprobed.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • What physical mechanism inflates the M-dwarf radius to 0.327 R_sun versus the 0.22 R_sun predicted by standard models?
  • How does the WD cooling age (~1.2 Gyr) constrain the common-envelope ejection physics and the prior evolution of the system?
  • Could multi-spot or Doppler imaging of the M dwarf refine the starspot geometry and out-of-eclipse modulation model?
  • Does the shallow secondary eclipse (~0.1% in TESS T band at +7.63 h) provide additional independent constraints if observed at higher precision?
