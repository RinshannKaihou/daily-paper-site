Accelerating Training of Autoregressive Video Generation Models via Local Optimization with Representation Continuity

一句话总结： 提出 Local Optimization 与 Representation Continuity (ReCo) 两种训练策略，通过窗口内优化和 Lipschitz 连续性约束抑制自回归视频生成的误差累积，在 FFS/SKY/UCF/Taichi 四个数据集上以约 2 倍训练加速超越 Baseline，ReCo⋆ 在 FFS 上取得 FVD 42.5。
TL;DR: The paper accelerates training of autoregressive (AR) video generation by (1) analyzing that the Fewer-Frames model reduces training time but causes cascading error accumulation, and (2) proposing Local Optimization (optimize tokens within overlapping windows conditioned on frozen ground-truth context) plus ReCo, a Lipschitz-inspired representation continuity loss. On FFS/SKY/UCF101/Taichi-HD (FVD metric), ReCo⋆ (343M) reaches 42.5/58.8/251.4/98.3 FVD vs the Baseline⋆ at 46.1/62.7/254.5/105.5, achieving ~2× training speedup over the Baseline (Train Speed 190.46 vs 73.65 on FFS) without sacrificing quality.

核心贡献

  • 通过经验分析揭示 Fewer-Frames 训练虽能减少训练时间，却因迭代推理中上下文受限导致级联误差累积与时间不一致（并给出 Proposition 1 的理论证明）。
  • 提出 Local Optimization (Local-Opt.) 训练方法：在重叠窗口内优化 token，前序真实序列作为冻结上下文，既保留 Fewer-Frames 的训练加速，又维持 Baseline 级别的推理流程，理论证明其窗口内误差低于 Fewer-Frames (Proposition 2)。
  • 提出 Representation Continuity (ReCo) 正则化，受 Lipschitz 连续性启发，以连续性损失约束相邻隐状态变化，进一步降低误差传播（Proposition 3），使方法在半训练成本下达到/超过 Baseline 质量。
  • 在 FFS、SKY、UCF101、Taichi-HD 及 MSR-VTT（零样本文生视频）上系统验证，并证明与 LARP tokenizer 等架构改进正交可叠加。

Key Contributions

  • Empirical analysis showing Fewer-Frames AR training trades inference consistency for training speed, formalized via Proposition 1 (expected deviation of Fewer-Frames ≥ Baseline).
  • Local Optimization (Local-Opt.) method: optimize tokens inside randomly placed, overlapping windows while freezing preceding ground-truth context (stop-gradient), keeping Baseline inference unchanged; theoretically lower window error than Fewer-Frames (Proposition 2).
  • Representation Continuity (ReCo): a Lipschitz-continuity-inspired loss penalizing large adjacent hidden-state variation, bounding error propagation during inference (Proposition 3) and improving temporal consistency.
  • Demonstrated ~2× training speedup over Baseline while matching/beating FVD across FFS, SKY, UCF101, Taichi-HD, and zero-shot MSR-VTT text-to-video; complementary to LARP tokenizer.

Methodology

  • Task/setup: Autoregressive video generation where videos are tokenized via VQ-VAE (OmniTokenizer) into discrete codes, then predicted token-by-token by a GPT-style LM and decoded back to video. Videos are 17-frame, 256×256 resolution; full 17-frame video encodes to ~5120 tokens; the Fewer-Frames model is trained on 2-frame blocks (~2048 tokens) and iteratively unrolled at inference.
  • Fewer-Frames model: trained on isolated short (2-frame) blocks; at inference each generated block is re-fed to predict the next block, so block T_k is conditioned only on T̂_{k-1} (Eq. 4) rather than the full history T̂_{
  • Local Optimization (Local-Opt.): sample a random window start s of length W, optimize negative log-likelihood of tokens in E_W=(e_s,…,e_{s+W-1}) conditioned on the preceding ground-truth context E_{
  • First-frame imbalance fix: loss on generated samples is higher/uneven vs training; rebalanced variant samples the first-frame window with proportion 0.5 ("Local-Opt. w/ balanced").
  • Representation Continuity (ReCo): views the AR model as a discrete-time dynamical system h_t = g(h_{t-1}, e_t; θ). Defines L_ReCo as mean squared distance between adjacent hidden states in a window (Eq. 10); total loss L_Total = L_CE + λ·L_ReCo (Eq. 11). A small implied Lipschitz constant L bounds error growth ‖ε_{t+1}‖ ≤ L·‖ε_t‖ + δ_t (Eq. 13).
  • Models/sizes: Baseline and ReCo at 110M; Baseline⋆ and ReCo⋆ at 343M (denoted ⋆); ReCo♠ uses the LARP tokenizer; text-to-video uses the Loong architecture at 7B parameters. Training: 300 epochs, learning rate 1×10⁻⁴, γ=0.01, λ=0.1, batch sizes 96 (110M) and 40 (343M), on a cluster of four NVIDIA A100 GPUs. Text-to-video trained on Vimeo (~300K video-text pairs), zero-shot evaluated on MSR-VTT.

Results & Highlights

  • FVD vs Baseline (class-to-video, Table 4): ReCo⋆ (343M) reaches 42.5 FVD on FFS vs Baseline⋆ 46.1; 58.8 on SKY vs 62.7; 251.4 on UCF vs 254.5; 98.3 on Taichi vs 105.5 — ReCo beats its same-size baseline on all four datasets.
  • Training speedup (Table 2, FFS/SKY): Local-Opt. Train Speed 190.46 (FFS) / 256.94 (SKY) vs Baseline 73.65 / 89.09, a 1.7× speedup; Fewer-Frames is 2.5× faster (229.32/292.41) but lower quality. ReCo keeps ~2× speedup over Baseline (~2 times faster, per the results discussion).
  • Fewer-Frames quality degradation (Table 1): Fewer-Frames FVD on FFS 1.75 (×2.5 speed) but worse FVD than Baseline; PSNR drops and Optical Flow rises with source-target frame interval (Figure 5), evidencing error accumulation.
  • Local-Opt. w/ balanced (Table 3): 127.11 (FFS) / 179.84 (SKY) FVD at 1.68× (×2.0) speedup, outperforming both Baseline and original Local-Opt. (134.73/186.63).
  • With LARP tokenizer (Table 4, ReCo♠): 46.2 FFS / 61.7 SKY / 56.1 UCF / 104.6 Taichi, surpassing LARP (62.6/70.4/57.0/119.5) on all four — showing the strategy stacks with architectural advances.
  • Text-to-video zero-shot MSR-VTT (Table 5): ReCo* (7B) matches Loong Baseline CLIP Score (0.2911 vs 0.2903) at 50% training cost while improving FVD (4000 vs 5000).
  • Motion-robustness (Table 6, UCF101): ReCo improves FVD +5.0% (Low Motion, 204.6 vs 215.4), +28.1% (Medium Motion, 390.2 vs 542.7), +17.2% (High Motion, 815.7 vs 985.2) over Baseline.
  • Long video (Table 9, SkyTimelapse): ReCo 78.8 (32f) / 71.1 (64f) vs Baseline 83.5 / 79.2 — gap widens with length, confirming long-horizon error suppression.
  • λ sensitivity (Table 7): FVD stable across λ=0.01–0.5 (FFS 91.42→76.93; SKY 105.30→94.15); degrades only at λ=1.0 (88.24/112.67, over-regularized).

Critical Assessment

  • The theoretical propositions (1–3) are largely intuitive restatements: Proposition 1 essentially argues that a model with strictly more conditioning context has lower expected error, and the "proof" of Proposition 2 reduces to noting that θ_LO is the minimizer of L_LO while θ_FF is not optimized for that objective — which is somewhat tautological rather than a tight quantitative bound.
  • Experiments are confined to small academic scales (110M/343M) on short 17-frame 256×256 videos; the authors themselves acknowledge (Limitations) that the study "mainly emphasizes algorithmic innovations rather than experiments on commercial-scale large language models," leaving scalability to 7B+ genuinely unproven except for one MSR-VTT zero-shot run.
  • The strongest baseline for the headline speedup claim (Fewer-Frames 2.5× but worse quality; Local-Opt. 1.7×) is not a directly apples-to-apples efficiency comparison: training-cost savings come partly from optimizing fewer tokens per step, so wall-clock and token-throughput normalization are not reported.
  • ReCo's improvement margins on UCF101 and Taichi over the same-size Baseline⋆ are modest (251.4 vs 254.5; 98.3 vs 105.5), and no statistical significance / variance over seeds is reported.

Strengths

  • Cleanly isolates the mechanism (cascading context loss) behind Fewer-Frames inconsistency and backs it with both PSNR/Optical-Flow diagnostics and a formal proposition.
  • Local-Opt. is a training-only change that leaves Baseline inference untouched — a practical, drop-in property for deployment.
  • ReCo has a principled (Lipschitz) motivation and is shown robust to λ over a wide range and to motion regime, and it composes with LARP.
  • Provides ablations on first-frame rebalancing, window overlap, λ sensitivity, and long-video generation.

Limitations

  • No experiments at commercial scale; largest genuine class-to-video model is 343M (the 7B run is a single MSR-VTT zero-shot config).
  • Theoretical bounds are qualitative (inequality of expectations) rather than quantitative constants; the Lipschitz proxy L_ReCo is not measured or bounded numerically.
  • No variance/seed reporting; some gains over the same-size baseline are small.
  • Only FVD/PSNR/Optical-Flow/CLIP-Score are reported; no human evaluation of temporal coherence.

Relevance

该论文研究自回归视频生成的训练加速与误差累积抑制，核心是从训练动力学稳定性角度改善长序列自回归生成的鲁棒性。这映射到用户关注的「LLM Training Stability & Observability」（训练稳定性与误差累积）以及「ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations」（Lipschitz 连续性、误差传播界的数学基础）两个方向，提供了一种以连续性正则抑制自回归误差爆炸的可借鉴思路。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • ReCo 的连续性损失是否可迁移到文本 LLM 的长上下文自回归生成，以抑制长序列生成中的 representation drift？
  • 所提 Lipschitz 代理损失 L_ReCo 与显式谱归一化/Lipschitz margin 方法在误差界紧致度上有何差异？能否给出 L 的数值估计？
  • 在 7B+ 商业规模且长视频（>64 帧）设置下，ReCo 的加速比与一致性增益是否会因 batch/序列长度变化而衰减？
  • Local-Opt. 的 stop-gradient 上下文与 teacher-forcing 的 exposure bias 之间的关系如何？能否与 scheduled sampling 等暴露偏差缓解方法结合？
