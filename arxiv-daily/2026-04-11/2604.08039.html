LINE: LLM-based Iterative Neuron Explanations for Vision Models

Vladimir Zaigrajew, Michał Piechota, Gaspar Sekula, Przemysław Biecek

Date: 2026-04-11

LINE: LLM-based Iterative Neuron Explanations for Vision Models

一句话总结： 提出 LINE，一种无需训练的黑盒迭代框架，用 LLM 提议概念、T2I 模型合成图像、目标视觉模型提取激活、均值评分更新记分板，在 CoSy 基准上对 ResNet50/ViT-B/16 等将 AUC 提升最多 0.18（ImageNet）和 0.05（Places365），并发现 29% 的预定义词表遗漏概念。
TL;DR: LINE is a training-free, black-box, iterative neuron-labeling pipeline: an LLM (Llama 3.1 8B) proposes concepts from a scoreboard, a T2I model (SDXL) synthesizes images, the target vision model extracts activations, and a mean-activation scorer updates the scoreboard over N iterations plus a summary step. On the CoSy benchmark across ResNet50/ResNet18 (avgpool/layer4) and ViT-B/16 (encoder/heads), LINE reaches AUC 0.97 on ResNet50/ImageNet vs CLIP-Dissect 0.87 / INVERT 0.88 / DnD 0.76, and AUC 0.94 on ViT-B/16/ImageNet vs DnD 0.56 — improvements of up to 0.18 (ImageNet) and 0.05 (Places365) — while discovering 29% (avg) to 39% of new concepts missed by predefined vocabularies.

核心贡献

  • 提出首个训练免、黑盒、迭代的视觉模型神经元标注框架 LINE，仅用 LLM + T2I 模型在闭环中提议并精炼概念，不依赖梯度或可微性。
  • 设计四步迭代循环（概念提议 → 图像合成 → 激活提取 → 概念评分），以均值激活 ψ_avg 作为连续评分信号（避免 AUC 饱和与 MAD 对控制集的依赖），并通过随机替换低分项实现探索-利用平衡。
  • 在 CoSy 基准上跨 ResNet50/ResNet18/ViT-B/16 四种架构-数据集组合取得 SOTA，AUC 较最强基线在 ImageNet 提升 0.18、Places365 提升 0.05（统计显著）。
  • 证明迭代精炼能发现预定义词表（即便是 ImageNet-1K 1000 类的大词表）遗漏的高分新概念，平均 22%（ImageNet）/38%（Places365）的最终标签由 LLM 生成，最高达 39%。
  • 提供完整的生成历史支持多义性（polysemanticity）评估，并作为梯度无关的激活最大化替代，生成比 DiffExplainer/DEXTER 更自然可解释的视觉解释。

Key Contributions

  • First training-free, black-box iterative neuron-labeling pipeline for vision models using only an LLM and a T2I generator (no gradients, no differentiable pipeline, no segmentation masks, no labeled corpus).
  • A four-step closed loop (concept proposal → image synthesis → activation extraction → concept scoring) with a mean-activation scorer ψ_avg (Eq. 2) that avoids AUC saturation and MAD's control-set dependence, plus a stochastic scoreboard update balancing exploration/exploitation.
  • State-of-the-art on the CoSy benchmark across four architecture-dataset combinations: AUC up to 0.18 higher on ImageNet and 0.05 on Places365 vs prior methods (CLIP-Dissect, INVERT, DnD), with statistically significant margins.
  • Demonstrates that even massive predefined vocabularies miss optimal labels: LINE's LLM-generated labels win for 22% (ImageNet) / 38% (Places365) of neurons on average, up to 39% in some layers.
  • Provides full generation history enabling polysemanticity assessment, plus gradient-free visual explanations that rival activation-maximization methods (DiffExplainer, DEXTER) while producing more natural, less OOD images.

Methodology

  • Setup: Target vision model f: X → A; analyze one neuron n at a time (parallelizable). For CNNs use spatial global pooling p(·) on a layer's activation map; for ViTs use natively 1-D encoder/heads representations. Explanation function E: [D] → T assigns a textual concept to each of D neurons.
  • Initialization: Build concept scoreboard H from ImageNet-1K validation set (K=1000 classes). For each class pass M=50 images through f, extract neuron activations → activation matrix A_init ∈ R^{K×M}, score with ψ_avg → s_init ∈ R^K. Populate H with top-5 highest-scoring class labels + 5 random labels (balance exploitation/exploration).
  • Iterative loop (N iterations): (1) Concept Proposal — LLM (Llama 3.1 8B, with CoT + few-shot prompting, MILS-style) proposes a new concept t based on scoreboard H, forbidden from repeating past proposals (generation_history). (2) Image Synthesis — T2I model (SDXL) generates |P|=5 images using template "A realistic photo of a {concept}, {angle}, {lighting}" with angle ∈ {extreme close-up, wide angle shot, aerial view, low angle} and lighting ∈ {cinematic lighting, natural sunlight, studio lighting} sampled uniformly. (3) Activation Extraction — pass generated images through f to get A_t = [a_n(x_1),…,a_n(x_|P|)] (Eq. 1). (4) Concept Scoring — s_t = ψ_avg(A_t) = (1/|A|)Σ a (Eq. 2). (5) Update Scoreboard — fixed size |H|=10; if s_t > min(H) replace lowest, else stochastically remove concept r with probability ∝ (max s_c) − s_r (higher scores survive more).
  • Summary step (N+1): LLM is given top-3 concepts from H and generates a generalized higher-order concept (e.g., "farming" from gatherer/harvest/thresher); evaluated via standard pipeline and appended to H. Return highest-scoring concept from H as t_best.
  • Scoring choice rationale: Mean activation chosen over AUC (saturates near 1.0 across multiple concepts) and MAD (depends on a control set); the continuous mean objective steers the LLM toward increasingly relevant labels.
  • Configuration: LLM = Llama 3.1 8B; T2I = SDXL (default), also ablated SD1.5 and FLUX.1[dev]; scoreboard |H|=10; |P|=5 images per concept; N=10 iterations + 1 summary (ablated to 20).
  • Baselines: CLIP-Dissect (open-vocab via CLIP), INVERT (compositional AUC), DnD (training-free I2T+LLM+T2I single forward pass), PDE-Refiner not applicable; for visual explanations: DiffExplainer and DEXTER (gradient-based AM).
  • Evaluation: CoSy benchmark (Kopf et al. 2024) with MAD and AUC metrics (higher better), averaged over 30 neurons; Salient ImageNet (Singla & Feizi 2022) for visual explanation comparison; causal ablation via I2I generative model removing the labeled concept.

Results & Highlights

  • CoSy benchmark AUC (Table 1, 30 neurons avg): ResNet50/ImageNet avgpool — LINE 0.97±0.04 vs CLIP-Dissect 0.87±0.20, INVERT 0.88±0.14, DnD 0.76±0.20 (Δ up to 0.21 over DnD, 0.09 over best baseline INVERT). ResNet50/Places365 — LINE 0.94±0.10 vs DnD 0.74±0.21 (Δ 0.20). ResNet18/ImageNet — LINE 0.96±0.05 vs DnD 0.73±0.24 (Δ 0.23). ViT-B/16/ImageNet — LINE 0.94±0.09 (encoder) vs DnD 0.56±0.24 (Δ 0.38); heads layer LINE 0.94 vs DnD 0.56.
  • Headline AUC improvement: up to 0.18 on ImageNet and 0.05 on Places365 over previous SOTA (abstract/intro figure; the per-architecture deltas above are even larger vs the weakest baseline).
  • Novel concept discovery (Table 2): LLM-generated final labels win for 22.4% (ImageNet, 66/294) and 38.0% (Places365, 76/200) of neurons on average; per-layer up to 39% (ResNet50/ImageNet layer4: 39/100 generated) and 37% (ResNet50/ImageNet avgpool: 37/100).
  • MAD metric (Table 1): ResNet50/ImageNet LINE 5.10±2.16 vs CLIP-Dissect 3.59, INVERT 2.53, DnD 2.03; ViT-B/16/ImageNet encoder LINE 2.00±0.61 vs DnD 0.27±1.03.
  • Causal ablation (Figure 3): Removing the LINE-labeled concept (carousel, necklace, lab coat) via I2I generative model causes neuron activation to drop often to near zero, confirming labels capture causal activation patterns (though I2I occasionally fails to ablate or introduces reconstruction artifacts).
  • Visual explanations vs AM (Figure 4, Salient ImageNet Jeep class): LINE outperforms DEXTER in achieved neuron activations (e.g., Feature 1067: LINE 2.41 vs DEXTER 0.51; Feature 1100: LINE 3.18 vs DEXTER 3.20 comparable; Feature 1208: LINE 2.59 vs DEXTER 1.91); produces fewer artifacts than DiffExplainer though sometimes lower raw activation than DiffExplainer (which exploits non-descriptive gradient artifacts).
  • Iteration ablation (Figure 5): Extending N from 10 to 20 on ResNet50/Places365 and ResNet18 shows monotonically increasing activation scores even at step 20; major breakthroughs cluster around steps 8 and 15; the summary step (S) is strictly necessary as it most frequently achieves the highest score.
  • T2I model ablation (Figure 6, 30 ResNet50/Places365 neurons): FLUX.1[dev] wins 43% of concepts vs SDXL 27% with slightly higher average activations, but the difference is NOT statistically significant; swapping T2I alters the top concept in 80% of cases yet variants remain semantically consistent (e.g., bookshop vs bookcase).
  • DnD weakness diagnosis: DnD's low performance attributed to its reliance on image cropping, which produces misleading/uninterpretable crops for the VLM (e.g., "diverse subjects showcase"), questioning whether cropping is viable for neuron analysis.

Critical Assessment

  • The scoring function ψ_avg (mean activation) is a deliberately blunt instrument: it rewards any concept that produces high activation, including non-discriminative or overly broad concepts that fire on many inputs — the paper itself notes the summary concept "farming" scored lower than "gatherer" because broader concepts introduce visual noise, but offers no mechanism to prevent a high-mean but non-specific concept from winning on other neurons.
  • The headline "AUC improvement of up to 0.18 on ImageNet" is somewhat misleading: the per-architecture deltas in Table 1 are frequently larger (e.g., 0.38 vs DnD on ViT-B/16), but the comparison is uneven — DnD is crippled by cropping, so LINE's margin over the strongest baseline (CLIP-Dissect or INVERT) is much smaller (e.g., 0.97 vs 0.88 = 0.09 on ResNet50/ImageNet), and the 0.18/0.05 figures appear to be averaged or selected for the abstract.
  • The causal ablation validation (Figure 3) is acknowledged to be compromised: the I2I generative model "occasionally fails to ablate the target feature and introduces image reconstruction artifacts," making it impossible to distinguish labeling errors from generator failures — yet no independent validation (e.g., human judgment, segmentation-based removal) is provided to disentangle these confounds.
  • LINE's cost is not reported: each neuron requires N=10+ iterations, each synthesizing |P|=5 images via SDXL and running them through the target model, plus LLM calls — the per-neuron wall-clock and GPU cost, and how it scales to the thousands of neurons in a real audit, are not quantified, undermining the "scalable pipeline" claim.
  • The initialization phase seeds the scoreboard with ImageNet-1K labels, which the authors admit fails when neurons are unrelated to ImageNet classes (e.g., "Bathing Cap" and "Patio" features in Salient ImageNet, Section 4.3) — a dependency on the target model's training distribution that limits generalization to non-ImageNet-trained or specialized-domain models.

Strengths

  • Genuinely training-free and black-box: no gradients, no differentiable pipeline, no segmentation masks, no labeled corpus — works with any vision model and any modern T2I (SDXL, FLUX.1[dev]).
  • Iterative refinement is a meaningful advance over single-pass methods (DnD, CLIP-Dissect); the ablation shows gains continue to step 20, justifying the loop.
  • Strong empirical gains on the unified CoSy benchmark across four architecture-dataset combinations, with statistical significance reported (Appendix G).
  • Provides full generation history (not just top label) enabling polysemanticity assessment, and produces natural visual explanations without the gradient-artifacts of AM methods.
  • Modular: LLM and T2I components are swappable, and the T2I ablation shows the method is robust to T2I choice (FLUX vs SDXL not statistically significant).

Limitations

  • Mean-activation scoring rewards non-discriminative high-firing concepts; no selectivity/discriminativity term in the objective.
  • Headline improvement figures (0.18/0.05) are smaller than several per-architecture deltas and are measured against an uneven baseline field (DnD crippled by cropping).
  • Causal validation confounded by I2I generator failures/artifacts; no independent ablation ground truth.
  • No per-neuron or per-audit cost reporting; scalability to thousands of neurons unquantified.
  • Initialization depends on ImageNet-1K labels, failing for features uncorrelated with ImageNet classes; limited generalization to specialized domains (medical, satellite) where high-fidelity T2I is absent (authors acknowledge this).
  • Polysemantic neurons: LINE may converge on one concept and ignore others; the proposed fix (restart with exclusion prompting) is left to future work.

Relevance

该论文研究视觉模型神经元的语义概念标注与可解释性，核心是通过 LLM + T2I 的迭代闭环揭示单个神经元编码的概念，并评估多义性与模型偏差。这映射到用户关注的「LLM Hidden States & Interpretability」（神经元/隐藏状态的语义解释与机制可解释性）方向，其"用 LLM 作为迭代优化器解释模型内部表示"的思路对 LLM 自身神经元解释亦具方法论借鉴价值，并间接涉及「Self-Evolving Agents & Automated Research」（LLM 驱动的自动化模型审计闭环）。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • 评分函数能否引入选择性/判别性项（如相对参考集的 AUC 或归一化激活差），以避免非特异高分概念胜出？
  • LINE 的迭代闭环能否迁移到 LLM 自身的神经元/MLP 隐藏单元解释（用 T2I 替换为文本生成模型），以自动化发现大语言模型的特征电路？
  • 每神经元成本（N×|P| 次 T2I 合成 + 前向）如何量化？是否有早停或主动采样策略使其可扩展到整模型数千神经元的全量审计？
  • 当目标模型训练分布与 ImageNet-1K 初始化词表不相关时，能否用 top-激活真实样本动态生成初始词表（Dunlap/Kim 思路）而不引入对激活子集的强依赖？
