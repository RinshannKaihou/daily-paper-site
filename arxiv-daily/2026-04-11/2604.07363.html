Hongjian Zou, Yidan Wang, Qi Ding, Yixuan Liao, Xiaoxin Chen

Benchmark Shadows: Data Alignment, Parameter Footprints, and Generalization in Large Language Models

一句话总结： 本文提出"基准阴影（Benchmark Shadows）"的 regime-centric 框架，通过受控数据干预证明"频率集中型"（缩小语义支撑）比"重复集中型"数据更难恢复，并在 Qwen3-VL / InternVL3.5 / AndesVL 等 4B 多模态模型上发现可复现的参数空间谱签名（尤其集中在 attention 的 v_proj），且这些签名与跨基准的非对称能力分布一致。
TL;DR: The authors introduce a "benchmark shadow" framework arguing that benchmark-aligned data regimes induce distinct, partly persistent parameter-space signatures rather than uniform capability growth. Using a controlled 0.6B decoder trained for 1.2T tokens under four conditions (coverage-expanding baseline A, optimization-control B, repetition-concentrated C, frequency-concentrated D), they show frequency-concentrated data (Condition D) leaves a persistent spectral gap (α∈[2,6] proportion 77.2% vs baseline 78.6% at 72k steps) that repetition-concentrated data (Condition C, 81.3%) recovers from. Across Qwen3-VL-4B / InternVL3.5-4B / AndesVL-4B families, the same signatures recur in attention v_proj and align with asymmetric benchmark profiles (e.g., AndesVL-4B scores 21.2 on DynaMath vs Qwen3-VL-4B's 65.3, despite similar DocVQA of 96 vs 95.3).

核心贡献

  • 提出 regime-centric 框架，将训练数据分布（覆盖扩展 vs 集中）作为连接学习动力学、参数空间结构与泛化行为的组织因素。
  • 设计受控数据干预实验，在固定架构/优化器/训练预算下分离"重复集中"（Condition C）与"频率/支撑集中"（Condition D）两种机制。
  • 提出基于谱分析（heavy-tailed exponent α）与秩分析（effective rank、delta effective rank、change variance）的参数空间诊断，能区分数据驱动 vs 优化器驱动的结构性变化。
  • 在 Qwen3-4B-Base 及其衍生多模态模型（Qwen3-VL-4B、InternVL3.5-4B、AndesVL-4B）上做外部验证，发现受控实验的谱签名在真实模型中以"更窄但可识别"的形式重现，并与非对称基准能力分布共现。
  • 通过 prompt 重复去重的案例研究（Section 6）澄清：仅引入冗余而不缩小语义支撑的数据扰动不会触发 regime 级结构变化，但去重仍能在多模态/指令跟随基准上带来收益。

Key Contributions

  • A regime-centric framework that treats data distribution (coverage-expanding vs concentration) as the organizing factor linking training distribution, parameter-space structure, and downstream generalization.
  • Controlled data interventions isolating two benchmark-aligned mechanisms (repetition-concentration via 10× duplication of 10% of D1; frequency-concentration via Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct-2507 teacher rewriting toward canonical forms) under a fixed 0.6B decoder, 1.2T-token budget.
  • Parameter-space diagnostics (heavy-tailed α, effective rank, delta effective rank, change variance) that disentangle data-induced regime shifts from optimizer-induced artifacts: change variance tracks optimization, while delta effective rank tracks data regime.
  • External validation across independently trained 4B-scale multimodal families (Qwen3-VL-4B, InternVL3.5-4B, AndesVL-4B) showing the controlled signatures recur, most clearly in attention v_proj, and co-occur with asymmetric benchmark capability profiles.
  • A prompt-duplication de-duplication case study establishing a boundary: redundancy without semantic-support narrowing improves multimodal/instruction benchmarks (+0.1084 on Business-LLM) without inducing regime-level spectral restructuring.

Methodology

Controlled model. A from-scratch decoder-only Transformer, ~0.6B parameters, 32 layers, hidden size 1024, FFN dim 4096, 8 attention heads with grouped-query attention (4 query groups), RMSNorm, RoPE, SwiGLU, max sequence length 4096, dropout disabled, Xavier-uniform init (std 0.02), bias-free linear layers. Trained with Adam (β1=0.9, β2=0.95, ε=1e-8), peak LR 3e-4 cosine-decaying to 3e-5, global batch 4096 (micro-batch 4), gradient clip 1.0, weight decay 0.1, bf16, total 300M samples (~1.2T tokens), linear warmup over first 1.64M samples.

Four conditions (Section 4.1). A = coverage-expanding baseline (deduplicated, broad diversity). B = optimization-control baseline (same data as A but no-fast-decay high-LR schedule) to isolate optimizer effects. C = repetition-concentrated: random 10% of first half D1 repeated 10×, discarding the other 90%, preserving sample count. D = frequency-concentrated: each D1 sample rewritten by Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct-2507 toward canonical/high-frequency forms (code normalized to JavaScript, NL simplified). C and D use a phase-shift curriculum: first half (steps 0–36k) concentrated, second half (36k–72k) reverts to baseline, to test recoverability. Checkpoints analyzed at 2k/20k/40k/60k/72k.

Diagnostics. Heavy-tailed exponent α (well-conditioned range [2,6]); effective rank / effective feature number (representational spread); change variance (update-magnitude concentration across depth); delta effective rank (representational restructuring). Layer-wise and per-module (v_proj, mlp.up_proj) analysis. Uses WeightWatcher-style spectral statistics (Martin & Mahoney 2020, 2021).

External validation (Section 5). 4B-scale open models: Qwen3-4B-Base as shared reference, plus Qwen3-VL-4B-Instruct, InternVL3.5-4B-Instruct, AndesVL-4B-Instruct (and thinking variants). Compares layer-wise α, λ_min, effective feature number, relative parameter change, and weight correlation against the base. Benchmarks from Table 2 across 34 multimodal tasks (MMMU, MMMU-Pro, MathVista, MathVision, DynaMath, DocVQA, OCRBench, etc.).

Prompt de-duplication case study (Section 6). VL dataset with ~75% prompt duplication; de-duplicated variant removes redundant prompt segments while preserving image/task/semantic content and overall size. Evaluated across 5 categories (General-MLLM, General-LLM, Business-MLLM, Business-LLM, Instruct-Following) with per-benchmark tables in Appendix.

Results & Highlights

  • Recoverability asymmetry (Table 1, α∈[2,6] % at 72k steps): Condition A baseline 78.6% (stable), Condition B optimization-control 61.2% (stuck), Condition C repetition-concentrated 81.3% (recovers), Condition D frequency-concentrated 77.2% (persistent ~1.4-point gap). Repetition-concentrated training is largely reversible; frequency-concentrated / support-collapse leaves a persistent structural footprint.
  • Cross-model structural divergence localizes in attention v_proj (Figure 6): AndesVL-4B-Instruct shows systematic early-layer inflation (α≈7.5–9 at layers 0–5 vs 6.5–7.5 for others) and an isolated Qwen3-VL spike at layer 23; MLP α and effective feature number are nearly invariant across families (Figure 7, Figure 21). Aggregate α∈[2,6] proportions are similar across families (69.0%–73.2%) and hide these layer-wise differences.
  • Adaptation-depth regimes (Figures 9–10): InternVL3.5/AndesVL maintain r≈0.97–0.98 weight correlation with Qwen3-4B-Base and ~20% relative change (conservative fine-tuning), while Qwen3-VL-4B-Instruct shows ~140–150% relative change and near-zero correlation at layers 2–20 (deep reparametrization), with a sharp transition at layer 21. This persists into the thinking variant across all 36 layers.
  • Benchmark asymmetry aligns with structure (Table 2): Qwen3-VL-4B-Instruct (broadest/deepest restructuring) leads on reasoning-heavy tasks — DynaMath 65.3 vs AndesVL 21.2, MathVision 51.6 vs AndesVL 27.1, MMMU-Pro 53.2 vs AndesVL 37.6 — while AndesVL-4B-Instruct (conservative adaptation) is competitive on perception/document tasks (DocVQA 96.0 vs 95.3, ChartQA 87.8 vs 84.6, OCRBench 861 vs 881) but lags sharply on reasoning.
  • Prompt de-duplication improves multimodal/instruction benchmarks (Table 3) without regime shift: Business-LLM +0.1084 (avg 0.4799 → 0.5883, driven by Keywords F1 +0.4110), Business-MLLM +0.0266, Instruct-Following +0.0208, General-MLLM +0.0198, while General-LLM slightly degrades −0.0134. Model-level α∈[2,6] is 61.61% (duplicated) vs 58.93% (de-duplicated) — de-duplication slightly worsens the aggregate metric yet improves downstream scores, showing aggregate metrics can move opposite to task-relevant structural quality.

Critical Assessment

The paper's central correlational link is its most contestable point. The external validation (Section 5) is explicitly correlational: the authors cannot attribute the AndesVL vs Qwen3-VL structural/benchmark differences to data regime specifically, because the third-party models differ simultaneously in data mixture, optimization, training duration, and multimodal design — yet the abstract and conclusion lean on this external evidence as if it corroborates the causal claim. Relatedly, the diagnostics rest on spectral/rank proxies (α, effective rank, λ_min) rather than direct gradient-flow measurement; the authors themselves note (Section 7.4) that "further work is needed to establish tighter theoretical connections between these metrics and underlying learning dynamics," so the interpretation that low delta effective rank equals "structurally limited adaptation" (spectral inertness) is an inference, not a measured mechanism. The controlled experiments are also limited to a single 0.6B architecture, leaving scale dependence untested, and the prompt-de-duplication case study tests only one de-duplication threshold (75%), so the boundary between "redundancy" and "support collapse" is underexplored.

Strengths

  • Clean controlled design that holds architecture, tokenizer, optimizer, and budget fixed and separately varies data regime (C, D) vs optimizer (B), enabling a real data-vs-optimization separation.
  • Practically useful disentanglement: change variance = optimizer diagnostic, delta effective rank = data-regime diagnostic — a concrete, usable monitoring recipe.
  • Cross-family external validation on four real 4B models (including the authors' own AndesVL) gives the regime signatures face validity beyond the toy setting.
  • The prompt-de-duplication case study is a genuine boundary test that distinguishes "redundancy" from "support collapse" rather than lumping all data artifacts together.

Limitations

  • Single controlled scale (0.6B) and single architecture; generalization to larger models and non-decoder architectures is unverified.
  • External validation is correlational; multiple confounds (scale, optimization, multimodal design) are not isolated.
  • Diagnostics are spectral/rank proxies, not direct measurements of learning dynamics; theoretical grounding is acknowledged as incomplete.
  • Only one de-duplication threshold (75%) tested; the redundancy-vs-support-collapse boundary is not mapped out.
  • AndesVL is the authors' own model, which introduces a potential bias in model selection for the asymmetry narrative.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • 这些谱/秩诊断是否能在线运行、在支持坍塌（support collapse）尚未反映到基准分数之前就发出预警？延迟与灵敏度如何权衡？
  • 当模型规模从 0.6B 扩展到 7B/70B 时，attention-MLP 非对称性与"谱惰性"签名是否仍然成立？
  • 在 RL / reasoning 后训练（thinking 变体）下，regime 压力是否会被强化学习信号放大而非被微调抵消？
  • delta effective rank 与梯度分布/表示几何之间能否建立可证伪的理论联系，而非仅靠经验共现？
  • 能否设计一个"coverage-sensitive validation"基准套件，直接量化支持坍塌而不是依赖现有多模态基准？
