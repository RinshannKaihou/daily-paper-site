Linear Representations of Hierarchical Concepts in Language Models

Sakata, M, Heinzerling, B, Ito, T, Yokoi, S, Inui, K

arXiv: 2604.07886

Date: 2026-04-11

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07886

Linear Representations of Hierarchical Concepts in Language Models

一句话总结： 发现语言模型中层级关系（如日本⊂东亚⊂亚洲）以高度可解释的线性表示编码，且这些层级信息被编码在相对低维的子空间中，不同语义领域的子空间结构高度相似。
TL;DR: Shows that hierarchical relations (e.g., Japan ⊂ Eastern Asia ⊂ Asia) are encoded as highly interpretable linear representations in LMs, with hierarchy information in low-dimensional domain-specific subspaces that share similar structure across domains.

核心贡献

  • 证明了语言模型中层级关系可以通过线性变换从模型表示中恢复，且在未见数据上具有泛化能力
  • 发现层级信息被编码在相对低维的子空间中，该子空间倾向于领域特定
  • 主要发现：不同领域特定子空间中的层级表示高度相似，具有跨领域一致性

Key Contributions

  • Demonstrates linear recovery of hierarchical relations from model representations with in-domain generalization
  • Shows hierarchy information resides in relatively low-dimensional, domain-specific subspaces
  • Main finding: hierarchy representation is highly similar across domain-specific subspaces, suggesting a universal encoding scheme

Methodology

Building on Linear Relational Concepts, the authors train linear transformations specific to each hierarchical depth and semantic domain, then compare these transformations to characterize representational differences. The analysis extends beyond single-token entities to multi-token entities and cross-layer representations.

Results & Highlights

  • Linear transformations successfully recover hierarchical relations within domains
  • Cross-domain transfer analysis reveals shared subspace structure for hierarchy encoding
  • All tested models encode concept hierarchies as interpretable linear representations

Authors & Credibility

Authors are from Japanese NLP groups (NAIST/RIKEN). Heinzerling and Inui have published at top NLP venues. The work builds on established linear representation literature.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: Moderate — extends prior work on Linear Relational Concepts to hierarchical relations. The cross-domain subspace similarity finding is the most novel contribution. Rigor: Systematic evaluation across multiple domains and models. Red flags: Limited to taxonomic hierarchies — unclear if results generalize to more complex relational structures.

Strengths

  • Clean, interpretable results showing linear encoding of hierarchical structure
  • Cross-domain analysis reveals universal patterns in hierarchy encoding
  • Extends to multi-token entities, addressing a limitation of prior work

Limitations

  • Focused on taxonomic hierarchies; other hierarchy types not explored
  • Unclear how results scale to very large models or more complex knowledge graphs
  • Linear representation assumption may not capture all hierarchical structure

Relevance

直接研究LLM隐藏状态中层级关系的编码方式，与用户关注的表示几何和潜在结构分析高度相关，为理解模型内部知识表示提供了实证依据。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do these findings hold for more complex hierarchical structures (e.g., part-whole, temporal)?
  • Can hierarchy subspaces be used for targeted knowledge editing?
