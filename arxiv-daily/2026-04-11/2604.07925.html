TL;DR: The authors give the first theoretical characterization of rank collapse under doubly stochastic (Sinkhorn-normalized) attention. They prove that, as for Softmax in Dong et al. (2021), pure self-attention with Sinkhorn converges to a rank-one matrix doubly exponentially with depth (Theorem 2: вҖ–res(SAN(X))вҖ–вӮӮ вүӨ (О»ОІH/вҲҡ(nВіdqk)) raised to ((3^LвҲ’1)/2) В· вҖ–res(X)вҖ–вӮӮВі). Empirically, on AG's News (encoder Transformer), MNIST and Cats and Dogs (ViT) trained from scratch with the Sinkformers codebase (50 Sinkhorn iterations, Adam), they measure the normalized spectral norm of the residual of chained attention products (Eq. 10) across path depth: Sinkhorn doubly stochastic attention consistently preserves more rank than row-stochastic Softmax, especially when skip connections are enabled, and the advantage disappears for products of independent random stochastic matrices вҖ” suggesting it stems from inter-layer attention correlations.

Key Contributions

  • First theoretical analysis of rank collapse for doubly stochastic attention normalized via the Sinkhorn algorithm; prior work (Dong et al. 2021) only covered row-stochastic Softmax.
  • A norm bound (Theorem 2) showing pure self-attention with Sinkhorn converges to a rank-one matrix doubly exponentially with depth, with a cubic convergence rate arising from the input-dependent nature of the attention matrix.
  • Replacement of the non-triangle-inequality-satisfying в„“вӮҒ,вҲһ norm used in Dong et al. with a proper spectral в„“вӮӮ norm, plus an explicit relationship between the two (Appendix E).
  • A first-order projection-operator approximation of the Sinkhorn operator at zero (Lemma 1, Proposition 4), using the orthogonal projection Q onto the tangent space TDS of the doubly stochastic manifold.
  • Empirical evidence on AG's News / MNIST / Cats and Dogs that doubly stochastic normalization preserves rank better than Softmax across depth, amplified by skip connections, plus a control showing the advantage vanishes for independent random stochastic matrices вҖ” implicating inter-layer attention correlations.

Methodology

Setup. Standard multi-head self-attention head: SA_h(X) = PВ·XВ·W_{V,h} + (1/d_v)1В·b_{V,h}^вҠӨ, where the attention matrix P (Eq. 1) is either row-stochastic Softmax or doubly stochastic Sinkhorn applied to (XW_Q+hВ·1b_Q^вҠӨ)(XW_K+hВ·1b_K^вҠӨ)^вҠӨ / вҲҡdqk. The Sinkhorn operator maps R^{nГ—n} вҶ’ DS^{nГ—n} = {S : S1=1, 1^вҠӨS=1^вҠӨ}, implemented in the log domain for numerical stability (50 iterations, empirically sufficient for effective double-stochasticity).

Path decomposition (after Dong et al. 2021). A multi-head L-layer network SAN(X) is decomposed into a sum over paths, each path selecting one head per layer: SAN(X) = ОЈ_{h_1,вҖҰ,h_L} (P_{h_L}^LВ·В·В·P_{h_1}^1)(XВ·W_{h_1}^1В·В·В·W_{h_L}^L) (Eq. 3). Rank decay is studied via how the product of attention matrices P_{h_L}^LВ·В·В·P_{h_1}^1 reduces rank, using the residual res(X) = X вҲ’ 1В·x^вҠӨ (Eq. 4) where x is the row-mean and (1/n)11вҠӨ is the rank-one limit of products of doubly stochastic matrices.

Proof technique. Define the orthogonal projection Q onto TDS^{nГ—n} (Eq. 14). Lemma 1 / Proposition 4: Sinkhorn(tY) = (1/n)11вҠӨ + (t/nВІ)Q(Y) + o(t). Proposition 1/3: вҖ–Q(Y)вҖ–вӮӮ вүӨ вҖ–Q(Y)вҖ–_F вүӨ О»вҲҡrank(Y)В·вҖ–YвҖ–вӮӮ for some 0<О»вүӨ1. The single-head bound (Theorem 1): вҖ–res(SA_h(X))вҖ–вӮӮ вүӨ (О»ОІ/вҲҡ(nВіВ·dqk))В·вҖ–res(X)вҖ–вӮӮВі, where вҖ–W_QW_K^вҠӨвҖ–вӮӮвҖ–W_VвҖ–вӮӮ вүӨ ОІ. Stacking across heads and layers gives Theorem 2's doubly-exponential bound. Proofs in Appendix D recurse backward through layers.

Empirical protocol. Models trained from scratch (random init) with the Sinkformers codebase (github.com/michaelsdr/sinkformers), Adam optimizer, cross-entropy loss. Four inference-time configurations on the same trained model: pure SAN, SAN+skip, SAN+FFN, full Transformer. Rank measured two ways: (1) along attention paths P_t = О _i P_{h_{в„“_i},b}^{в„“_i} (Eq. 10) via normalized spectral norm вҖ–res(P_t)вҖ–вӮӮ/вҖ–P_tвҖ–вӮӮ using truncated SVD best-rank-1 approximation, 100 sampled paths per depth; (2) along layer outputs SAN(X)_{в„“,b} via вҖ–res(SAN(X)_{в„“,b})вҖ–вӮӮ/вҖ–SAN(X)_{в„“,b}вҖ–вӮӮ, batch mean Вұ std. Control: products of independently random stochastic matrices (Appendix G).

Hyperparameters. AG's News (Table 1): seq len 128, d_model 128, FFN 512, H=4 heads, 24 layers, batch 32, LR 5e-5, dropout 0.3, 20 epochs. MNIST ViT (Table 2): 28Г—28, patch 4, d_model 128, FFN 512, H=4, 24 layers, batch 100, LR 2e-2, dropout 0, 50 epochs. Cats and Dogs ViT: 224Г—224, patch 16, d_model 128, FFN 512, H=4, 24 layers, batch 32, LR 3e-5, dropout 0, 50 epochs.

Results & Highlights

  • Theoretical rank decay bound (Theorem 2, multi-head multi-layer): вҖ–res(SAN(X))вҖ–вӮӮ вүӨ (О»ОІH/вҲҡ(nВіВ·dqk))^((3^LвҲ’1)/2) В· вҖ–res(X)вҖ–вӮӮВі, where 0<О»вүӨ1, n = sequence length, dqk = query/key dim, L = depth, H = heads, and вҖ–W_QW_K^вҠӨвҖ–вӮӮвҖ–W_hвҖ–вӮӮ вүӨ ОІ. As L grows this goes to zero doubly exponentially вҖ” same qualitative behavior as Softmax (Dong et al. 2021), but derived with a proper spectral norm.
  • Single-head bound (Theorem 1): вҖ–res(SA_h(X))вҖ–вӮӮ вүӨ (О»ОІ/вҲҡ(nВіВ·dqk))В·вҖ–res(X)вҖ–вӮӮВі вҖ” the cubic-power dependence on вҖ–res(X)вҖ–вӮӮ is what produces the doubly-exponential (rather than the classical singly-exponential) depth convergence, because the attention matrix P depends on the input X it is then applied to (Section 3, Appendix G).
  • Empirical rank preservation along attention paths (Figures 3aвҖ“c): across AG's News (Transformer), MNIST (ViT), and Cats and Dogs (ViT), Sinkhorn doubly stochastic normalization yields higher normalized spectral norm of res(P_t) at every path depth than Softmax, i.e., rank is better preserved; the gap widens when skip connections are present (skip connections "shorten" the product by bypassing layers).
  • Output-level rank decay (Figures 4aвҖ“c): a clear Sinkhorn-over-Softmax advantage appears only on AG's News (Figure 4a); on MNIST and Cats and Dogs (Figures 4bвҖ“c) the two normalizations look nearly identical at the SAN(X) output level despite the attention-product advantage in Figures 3bвҖ“c, because the output is also shaped by multiplication with X and the W_h products.
  • Random-matrix control (Figure 5, Appendix G): for products of independently sampled random stochastic matrices, Softmax and Sinkhorn exhibit essentially identical rank decay, and skipping half the factors (replacing with identity to mimic skip connections) does not separate them вҖ” implying the in-Transformer Sinkhorn advantage is not a generic property of doubly stochastic matrices but depends on inter-layer attention correlations.

Critical Assessment

The most substantive weakness is the mismatch between the theory and the experiments. The norm bound (Theorem 2) is derived only for pure self-attention (no skip connections, no FFN, no LayerNorm) and shows the same doubly-exponential collapse already established for Softmax by Dong et al. (2021) вҖ” so the theory does not by itself predict the empirical Sinkhorn advantage, which is observed only in the full Transformer with skip connections where the bound no longer applies. The authors concede this in Section 4.2: the output-level rank advantage of Sinkhorn is clear only on AG's News (Figure 4a) and essentially disappears on the two ViT tasks (Figures 4bвҖ“c), even though the attention-path advantage (Figures 3bвҖ“c) is consistent вҖ” i.e., the central empirical claim is fragile at the level (output rank) that actually matters for representation quality. The random-matrix control (Appendix G) is honest but undermining: it shows the Sinkhorn advantage vanishes for independent stochastic matrices, meaning the paper cannot explain mechanistically why doubly stochastic attention helps; the authors explicitly leave "how Sinkhorn doubly stochastic attention matrices interact, in comparison to Softmax" as future work. Finally, the analysis carries no task-performance numbers вҖ” there is no accuracy/F1 comparison between Softmax and Sinkhorn training, so the reader cannot tell whether better rank preservation translates into better downstream results.

Strengths

  • Genuinely fills a theoretical gap: the doubly-stochastic case had not been analyzed, and the proof machinery (first-order Sinkhorn approximation via the Q projection, Lemma 1/Proposition 4) is clean and reusable.
  • Uses a proper spectral в„“вӮӮ norm instead of the triangle-inequality-violating в„“вӮҒ,вҲһ of Dong et al., and gives the explicit norm relationship (Appendix E) вҖ” a technical improvement.
  • Honest empirical reporting, including the negative output-level result on ViT (Figures 4bвҖ“c) and the random-matrix control that complicates the story.
  • Four inference-time ablations (pure SAN, +skip, +FFN, full Transformer) on the same trained model isolate the contribution of each architectural component cleanly.

Limitations

  • The theoretical bound applies only to pure self-attention and reproduces the known doubly-exponential behavior; it does not predict the empirical Sinkhorn advantage observed in full Transformers.
  • The output-level rank advantage is inconsistent across tasks (clear on AG's News, absent on MNIST/Cats and Dogs ViTs).
  • No downstream task-accuracy comparison between Softmax and Sinkhorn вҖ” rank preservation is not shown to correlate with better classification.
  • The mechanism behind the in-Transformer advantage is unidentified; the random-matrix control shows it depends on inter-layer correlations that are not characterized.
  • Small models (d_model 128, 24 layers) and small datasets (AG's News, MNIST, Cats and Dogs); no LLM-scale or large-vision-scale validation.
  • Sinkhorn iteration count (50) and other hyperparameters chosen empirically; the paper does not study sensitivity to them.
