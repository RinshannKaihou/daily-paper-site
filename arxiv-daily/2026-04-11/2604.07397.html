Data Warmup: Complexity-Aware Curricula for Efficient Diffusion Training

Jinhong Lin, Pan Wang, Zitong Zhan, Lin Zhang, Pedro Morgado

arXiv: 2604.07397

Date: 2026-04-11

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07397

Data Warmup: Complexity-Aware Curricula for Efficient Diffusion Training

一句话总结： 提出 Data Warmup 课程学习策略，用 DINO-v2 离线计算每张图像的"前景显著性×典型性"复杂度分数，并通过温度退火采样器由简到难调度，在 ImageNet-256 的 SiT 系列（S/2 到 XL/2）上将 IS 提升最多 6.11、FID 降低最多 3.41，且反向课程会显著损害性能。
TL;DR: Data Warmup is a data-centric curriculum for diffusion training that scores each image offline (DINO-v2 features → foreground dominance Ωdom × foreground typicality Ωprot) and samples via a temperature-controlled softmax that anneals from simple to uniform over a warmup phase. On ImageNet-1K 256×256 with SiT backbones (S/2 to XL/2), it improves IS by up to +6.11 and FID by up to −3.41 vs the uniform-sampling SiT-B/2 baseline (IS 44.79→50.90, FID 35.32→31.95 on IN-500); reversing the curriculum (hard-first) degrades performance below baseline (IS −4.80, FID +4.89), and stacking on REPA gives further gains (IS +2.72, FID −1.70).

核心贡献

  • 将扩散模型早期训练低效的根因归结为"数据复杂度与模型准备度失配"：随机初始化网络被迫去噪其尚无能力处理的复杂图像，梯度噪声大且信息量低。
  • 提出语义感知的图像复杂度度量，由两个因子组成：前景显著性（Ωdom，显著物体占画面比例，经 sigmoid 修正）与前景典型性（Ωprot，前景相对 k-means 原型的距离），二者乘积作为总复杂度。
  • 设计温度退火采样器：以有效数据集大小 |Dτ(t)| 沿 power-2 曲线从 |D0| 增长到 |D|×(1−1/e)，对应温度通过二分搜索恢复，warmup 阶段后切换为均匀采样。
  • 通过"反向课程"对照实验严格证明：增益来自简单→复杂的"方向"本身，而非单纯非均匀采样——反向课程使性能低于均匀基线。

Key Contributions

  • Identifies the data-complexity/model-readiness mismatch as a concrete source of early-stage inefficiency in diffusion training and resolves it with a simple-to-complex curriculum that leaves model and loss untouched.
  • Introduces a semantic-aware image complexity metric combining foreground dominance (Ωdom, sigmoid-corrected background ratio) and foreground typicality (Ωprot, distance to nearest k-means prototype), computed in a single offline DINO-v2 pass with zero per-iteration overhead.
  • A temperature-controlled softmax sampler that anneals from concentrated (simple) to uniform via a power-2 schedule on effective dataset size, with within-cluster score normalization to avoid biasing toward low-raw-score visual concepts.
  • A direction-matters ablation showing the reverse curriculum actively hurts (below baseline), isolating ordering — not non-uniformity — as the mechanism; plus additivity with the model-centric accelerator REPA.

Methodology

  • Offline complexity scoring (Stage 1, ~10 min on one H100): Extract DINO-v2 spatial tokens {z_{i,j}} for each image; compute the first principal component u1 across the dataset; per-token saliency s_{i,j} = u1^⊤ z_{i,j}; foreground tokens are those with s_{i,j} > θ, θ=0.05 (following DINO-v2).
  • Foreground dominance Ωdom: background ratio r_bg = (L − L_fg)/L mapped through a sigmoid correction Ωdom = 1 / (1 + e^{−(κ r_bg + α(v_min))}) with κ=12.0, v_min=0.002, so that Ωdom = v_min when r_bg=0. The sigmoid captures that the complexity jump from 40%→20% foreground matters more than 80%→60%.
  • Foreground typicality Ωprot: average foreground tokens into z̄_i^fg (or all tokens if none), run k-means (K=1000 on ImageNet) to get prototypes {μ_k}, assign each image to nearest centroid k(i), Ωprot = ‖z̄_i^fg − μ_{k(i)}‖₂. Low = prototypical, high = outlier.
  • Overall score Ω = Ωdom × Ωprot (multiplicative: simple only if both dominant and prototypical). Normalize within each cluster: Ω̃_i = (Ω_i − Ω_min^{k(i)}) / (Ω_max^{k(i)} − Ω_min^{k(i)}).
  • Warmup sampling (Stage 2): At iteration t, sample image i with P(i|t) ∝ exp(−Ω̃_i / τ(t)) (Eq. 7). Temperature is parameterized via effective dataset size |D_τ(t)| = Σ_i [1 − (1 − P(i|t))^{|D|}] (expected unique images per epoch), recovered by binary search. Schedule |D_τ(t)| on a power-2 curve from |D0| to |D_max| = |D|×(1−1/e) ≈ 0.632|D| over T_w warmup iterations, then uniform (τ→∞) for t > T_w.
  • Training setup: ImageNet 256×256, SiT framework (S/2, B/2, L/2, XL/2 with 2×2 non-overlapping patches), ADM preprocessing, Stable Diffusion VAE latents z ∈ R^{32×32×4}, batch size 256. Warmup runs use 200k curriculum + 200k uniform iterations; baselines train 400k uniform. A100 GPUs for training; H100 for preprocessing timing. Metrics: FID, sFID, IS, precision/recall. Qualitative model: SiT-XL/2 + REPA + Data Warmup, 2M iterations, CFG w=4.0.
  • Baselines/controls: uniform-sampling SiT (baseline); inverse Data Warmup (same mechanism, hard-first); REPA (representation-alignment accelerator) alone and stacked; ablations on Ωdom-only, Ωprot-only, and sigmoid hyperparameters (κ, v_min).

Results & Highlights

  • Main result (Table 1, SiT-B/2 on IN-1K 256×256): Data Warmup IS 45.70 (+4.30) vs baseline 41.40; FID 32.75 (−3.41) vs 36.16; sFID 6.56 (−0.24); precision 0.54 (+0.02). Inverse Data Warmup degrades to IS 36.60 (−4.80), FID 41.05 (+4.89) — below baseline — proving direction is the mechanism.
  • Direction asymmetry: gap between forward and reverse curricula ≈ ΔIS 9, ΔFID 8, far larger than either's gap to baseline.
  • Dataset-size threshold (Table 3, SiT-B/2): IN-100 — warmup hurts (IS 43.05→28.75, −14.30; FID 79.38→100.97, +21.59); IN-500 — largest gains (IS 44.79→50.90, +6.11; FID 35.32→31.95, −3.37); IN-1K — IS +4.30, FID −3.41. Establishes a diversity threshold.
  • Model-capacity scaling (Table 4, IN-1K): gains grow with scale — SiT-S/2 IS +1.16 (FID −2.47), SiT-B/2 +4.30 (−3.41), SiT-L/2 +5.96 (−1.62), SiT-XL/2 +4.67 (−1.31).
  • REPA stacking (Table 2): Data Warmup on top of REPA improves IS 55.36→58.08 (+2.72) and FID 27.54→25.84 (−1.70), confirming orthogonality with model-centric accelerators.
  • Component ablation (Table 5, SiT-B/2 IN-1K): Ωdom alone IS +3.02 / FID −2.22; Ωprot alone IS +1.51 / FID −1.15; combined IS +4.30 / FID −3.41 — super-additive, indicating synergy.
  • Hyperparameter robustness (Table 6): κ ∈ [10,16] and v_min ∈ [0.002, 0.02] all beat baseline; best at κ=12, v_min=0.002 (IS 45.69, FID 32.75).
  • Convergence speed (Figure 6, IN-500): warmed-up model pulls ahead from earliest iterations, gap widens through the 40k–160k curriculum phase, and persists at 400k with ~6 IS point lead — "not merely front-loading gains the baseline eventually recovers."

Critical Assessment

  • The complexity metric is computed once offline from a frozen DINO-v2 backbone and is static throughout training — the authors themselves flag this as a limitation, noting that an adaptive, loss-aware re-scoring could yield tighter curricula in later phases; the current schedule defaults to uniform after T_w, so any late-training benefit from complexity-aware sampling is forfeited.
  • The method's headline failure mode is severe and under-explored: on IN-100 it actively hurts (FID +21.59), and the paper's explanation ("too few unique images per epoch → overfit to a narrow manifold") is plausible but not formally characterized — there is no quantitative rule for predicting the diversity threshold on a new dataset, which limits safe deployment on smaller or uncurated corpora.
  • The "direction matters" ablation, while a clean control, does not rule out that the specific complexity metric is doing the work rather than the ordering: a random ordering with the same non-uniform concentration, or an orthogonal difficulty metric (e.g., loss-based), would be a stronger comparison.
  • All quantitative results are on class-conditional ImageNet 256×256 with SiT; text-conditioned generation, uncurated/web-scale data, and non-SiT architectures are entirely untested, so generalization claims are unsupported by evidence.

Strengths

  • Clean, minimal, and orthogonal intervention: changes only the data sampling distribution, leaving model/loss untouched, which is why it composes with REPA.
  • Strong direction ablation that isolates ordering as the causal factor — a methodologically rigorous control rarely seen in curriculum-learning papers.
  • Comprehensive ablations: dataset-size threshold, model-capacity scaling, both complexity factors, sigmoid hyperparameters, and REPA stacking.
  • Practically cheap: ~10 minutes one-time offline preprocessing on a single H100, zero per-iteration overhead.

Limitations

  • Static, frozen DINO-v2 complexity scores; no adaptive re-scoring as the model improves.
  • Catastrophic failure on small datasets (IN-100) with no quantitative threshold rule for when it is safe to apply.
  • Restricted to class-conditional ImageNet 256×256 / SiT; text-conditioned and uncurated regimes untested.
  • The "direction matters" control does not disentangle the specific semantic metric from the ordering effect itself.
  • No theoretical/empirical connection to loss-landscape geometry beyond a brief hand-wavy hypothesis in the Discussion.

Relevance

该论文研究扩散模型训练的加速与数据课程调度，核心是从训练动力学（早期梯度信号质量、模型准备度与数据复杂度失配）角度提升训练稳定性与效率。这映射到用户关注的「LLM Training Stability & Observability」（训练阶段的数据/梯度信号稳定性与课程调度）与「ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations」（数据复杂度度量、采样分布退火的数学构造）两个方向，其"先简后难"的样本调度思想对自回归/大模型训练稳定性亦有借鉴价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • 复杂度度量能否改为随训练自适应更新（如用模型自身 loss 或梯度范数在线重打分），以在 warmup 后期仍保留复杂度感知？
  • 该课程思想是否可迁移到 LLM 预训练/指令微调，按 prompt/样本复杂度（如 reasoning 链长度、token 熵）做简单→困难调度以稳定早期训练？
  • IN-100 的失败是否可由"有效数据集大小下限"给出可预测的阈值规则（如 |D0|/类别数 ≥ 某常数），从而安全部署到小数据集？
  • 反向课程损害性能的根因（loss landscape 几何、梯度冲突平均化）能否用 loss-landscape 可视化或梯度余弦相似度定量验证，而非仅作假设？
