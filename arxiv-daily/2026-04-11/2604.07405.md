# Conservation Law Breaking at the Edge of Stability: A Spectral Theory of Non-Convex Neural Network Optimization

> **一句话总结：** 推导出梯度下降在非凸 ReLU 网络优化中的守恒律破缺精确公式，证明离散 GD 下守恒量漂移以 η^α（α≈1.1–1.6）破缺，给出谱交叉闭式公式并揭示交叉熵损失通过 softmax 概率浓度驱动 24× Hessian 谱压缩、将漂移指数自正则化至 α≈1.0。

> **TL;DR:** On 2-layer ReLU/linear networks trained full-batch on Gaussian-mixture data (n=200, d=20, K=5), the author proves L−1 conservation laws (Cl = ||Wl+1||²F − ||Wl||²F) of gradient flow break under discrete GD as η^α (α=1.16, R²>0.99) and derives a closed-form spectral crossover formula predicting the gradient imbalance sum to within 14–27% of measured values for ReLU (vs. the prior balancedness/EoS result of Ghosh et al. for linear nets), and shows cross-entropy loss compresses λmax(H) 24× (from ~7.2 to ~0.3 over 2000 steps) — clamping α near 1.0 while MSE lets α grow to 1.6+.

## 核心贡献

- 证明 L 层无偏置 ReLU 网络在梯度流下保持 L−1 个守恒律 Cl = ||Wl+1||²F − ||Wl||²F，将轨迹约束到低维流形；离散梯度下降以 η^α（α≈1.1–1.6）速率破缺这些守恒律
- 推导出守恒漂移的精确分解 ΔCl = η²·S(η)，并给出梯度不平衡和 S(η) 的闭式谱交叉公式 S(η) = Σk ck·[1−(1−ηλk)²T]/[ηλk(2−ηλk)]，从第一性原理导出模系数 ck ∝ ek(0)²·λ²x,k
- 证明交叉熵损失通过 softmax 概率浓度 q_i(1−q_i)→0 驱动指数级 Hessian 谱压缩（24×，τ=Θ(1/η) 且与训练集大小 n 无关），将漂移指数自正则化至 α≈1.0
- 识别由宽度控制的两个动力学区域：扰动亚稳定性边缘（sub-EoS，谱公式适用）与非扰动（EoS，模耦合为 O(m)）

## Key Contributions

- Proves L−1 conservation laws Cl = ||Wl+1||²F − ||Wl||²F are exactly preserved by gradient flow on L-layer ReLU networks without bias, confining trajectories to a codimension-(L−1) manifold; discrete GD breaks them as η^α with α≈1.1–1.6
- Derives an exact drift decomposition ΔCl = η²·S(η) and a closed-form spectral crossover formula for S(η), with first-principles mode coefficients ck ∝ ek(0)²·λ²x,k validated for linear (R=0.847) and ReLU (R>0.80) networks
- Proves cross-entropy loss induces exponential Hessian spectral compression (λmax drops 24×) with timescale τ=Θ(1/η) independent of dataset size n, explaining CE self-regularization of α near 1.0 while MSE permits α to grow to 1.6+
- Identifies two dynamical regimes (perturbative sub-EoS vs. non-perturbative EoS) separated by a width-dependent transition governed by absolute overparameterization rather than the m/d ratio

## Methodology

The author develops a unified spectral theory analytically and validates it empirically across 23 experiments.

**Setup:** 2-layer fully connected networks (linear and ReLU, no bias), trained with full-batch gradient descent on Gaussian-mixture data (n=200 samples, d=20 input dim, K=5 classes, separation 2.0). Layer widths m vary from 16 to 192. Seeds {42, 137, 256, 512, 1024}. Loss functions include MSE and cross-entropy (CE).

**Analytical approach:**
1. Derives conservation laws from the 1-homogeneity of ReLU (rescaling symmetry Wl→αWl, Wl+1→α⁻¹Wl+1).
2. Expands the discrete GD update to show the per-step drift is exactly η²·(||∂L/∂Wl+1||²F − ||∂L/∂Wl||²F) — the O(η) cross-term cancels between layers by the same symmetry.
3. Decomposes the gradient imbalance sum S(η) mode-by-mode in the SVD basis of the data covariance, yielding the spectral crossover formula where each mode k transitions between an "unconverged" regime (local α=2) and a "converged" regime (local α=1) at crossover η*k = 1/(λk T).
4. For CE, uses the Gauss-Newton Hessian factorization HCE = (1/n)J⊤S(p)J and the variational characterization of λmax to bound compression by qi(1−qi), connecting to NTK theory for the n-independent timescale.

**Hardware/software:** Intel Core i5-1038NG7 (4 cores, 2.0 GHz), 16 GB RAM, CPU only (no GPU). Python 3.12.7, PyTorch 2.2.2, NumPy 1.26.4. Full suite runs in ~4 hours of CPU time; individual experiments 30s–15min.

## Results & Highlights

- **Drift exponent (E5):** Total conservation-law drift scales as η^α with α=1.16 and R²>0.99 across four decades of learning rate; the ReLU exponent α=1.08 is essentially identical to the linear-network exponent α=1.10±0.01 (R²=0.99), proving the non-integer exponent is a spectral (not nonlinearity) phenomenon.
- **Spectral crossover formula accuracy (E8):** The closed-form S(η) predicts the measured gradient imbalance sum with 14–18% relative error for linear networks and 14–27% for ReLU networks across three decades of learning rate (including at EoS); mode-coefficient predictions achieve R=0.847 for linear (E20) and R>0.80 for ReLU (E21).
- **Cross-entropy self-regularization (E18):** CE Hessian λmax drops 24× from ~7.2 to ~0.3 over 2000 training steps, with compression timescale τ=1.33/η+29 (R²=0.988, E23) independent of dataset size n; CE holds α≈1.0–1.1 at all widths while MSE permits α−1 ~ m^1.18 (E19) growing beyond 1.6.
- **EoS dichotomy (E4, E16):** At the Edge of Stability, conservation-law drift increases 5500× relative to gradient flow; the per-neuron activation switch rate becomes width-independent (total mode coupling O(m)) vs. sub-EoS where it scales as m^−0.5 (coupling O(√m)).
- **Width-dimension transition (E22):** The regime transition width m* satisfies m*/d = 6.0, 3.0, 1.0 for input dimensions d∈{10, 20, 40} — the transition depends on absolute overparameterization, not a fixed m/d ratio.
- **Depth and optimizer dependence (E6, E7):** α grows from 1.07 (2-layer) to 1.72 (8-layer) with depth; Adam yields α=0.56, qualitatively different from GD.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Very high — the conservation-law framework, the exact drift decomposition, and the closed-form spectral crossover formula are genuinely new theoretical contributions extending Ghosh et al.'s linear-network EoS result to nonlinear ReLU networks with a complete spectral theory.

**Paper-specific weaknesses:**
1. The theory is developed only for 2-layer networks in the spectral analysis (Theorems 4–8); the conservation laws (Theorem 1) hold for L layers but the spectral formula's multi-layer generalization is left as an open problem (Section 6, item ii). The mean-field quasi-convexity result (Appendix A.2) covers 2-layer only and has an explicit gap: finite-width convergence at rate O(1/√m) is asserted via "standard propagation-of-chaos results" but not proven.
2. The ReLU correction to the mode coefficients is dismissed as "O(10⁻⁴) at width 64, where the activation switch rate is below 0.1%" — but at EoS the per-neuron switch rate is width-independent and the independent-mode decomposition breaks down, so the 14–27% ReLU prediction error may worsen precisely where the theory is most interesting.
3. The "NP-hard worst case" motivation (citing Choromanska et al. 2015) is somewhat of a strawman: the cited result is about the spin-glass analogy, not a hardness result for the optimization problems actually solved, and the paper does not engage with the subtlety.
4. Applicability to transformers/attention is implied in the Discussion ("learning rate schedules should respect the EoS boundary") but never demonstrated — the conservation laws rely on 1-homogeneity of ReLU and no-bias, conditions that do not cleanly transfer to attention/LayerNorm architectures.

## Strengths

- Exact closed-form formulas (not asymptotic bounds) with first-principles, parameter-free mode-coefficient predictions
- Comprehensive experimental validation: 23 experiments varying architecture, loss, width, depth, optimizer, with seeds and full code release
- The CE self-regularization result (τ=Θ(1/η), n-independent) elegantly connects conservation-law breaking to NTK theory and explains a striking empirical dichotomy (CE vs. MSE width scaling)
- Accessible on consumer CPU hardware, lowering the barrier to verification

## Limitations

- Spectral theory is for 2-layer networks; L-layer generalization is open
- Conservation laws require no-bias ReLU homogeneity — extension to transformers/LayerNorm/bias not addressed
- Non-perturbative EoS regime lacks a closed-form theory (acknowledged open problem)
- Single author; mathematical derivations would benefit from independent verification
- Practical implications for large-scale LLM training are conjectural, not demonstrated
