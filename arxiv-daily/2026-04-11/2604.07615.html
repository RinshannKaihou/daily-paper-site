arXiv: 2604.07615

Date: 2026-04-11

ADAG: Automatically Describing Attribution Graphs

一句话总结： ADAG 是首个端到端全自动的归因图描述管道，通过归因特征谱（attribution profiles）量化 MLP 神经元的功能角色、多视图谱聚类把它们分组成 supernode，再用 LLM explainer–simulator 生成自然语言描述；在 Llama 3.1 8B Instruct 上无人工介入地复现了多跳首府推理电路，并定位到可将有害医疗建议越狱 ASR 从 28% 拉到 90% 的可引导特征簇。
TL;DR: ADAG is the first fully automated end-to-end pipeline for describing LLM attribution graphs. It replaces ad-hoc human interpretation with attribution profiles (input-gradient + output-logit-contribution vectors), multi-view spectral clustering into supernodes, and an LLM explainer–simulator that generates and scores natural-language descriptions. On Llama 3.1 8B Instruct it recovers the human-analyzed capitals (Texas) multi-hop circuit with no human in the loop, and on the pills harmful-medical-advice jailbreak dataset (Chowdhury et al. 2025, 150 variants, 0%–88.5% ASR) it finds a cluster (C9, "ridiculous-to-introductory") that raises ASR from 28±6% (unsteered) to 90±5% when steered at 2×, and a safety cluster (C3) that drops ASR to 5±3%.

核心贡献

  • 提出归因特征谱（attribution profiles）：用输入归因向量与输出贡献向量量化每个特征的功能角色，克服了最大激活样本与 logit lens 的"局部性偏差"（locality bias）
  • 设计多视图谱聚类算法把功能相似的特征自动聚成 supernode，并用 silhouette / cluster-size CV / 对立符号对比例三个指标系统消融
  • 扩展 Bills et al. (2023) 的 explainer–simulator 框架，对 supernode 的输入归因与输出贡献分别生成并评分自然语言描述，用 Pearson r 量化
  • 在 Llama 3.1 8B Instruct 上无人工介入地复现了 capitals 多跳电路，并发现导致 pills 越狱的可引导特征簇

Key Contributions

  • Introduces attribution profiles (input attribution + output contribution vectors per feature) that quantify functional role via gradient effects, overcoming the locality bias of max-activating exemplars and logit-lens top logits
  • Proposes a multi-view spectral clustering algorithm with harmonic-mean aggregation that produces balanced, cohesive supernodes while minimizing mixing of opposing-sign contribution features
  • Extends the explainer–simulator framework (Bills et al. 2023) to describe both input-attribution and output-contribution profiles, scoring candidates by global Pearson correlation between true and simulator-predicted scores
  • Validates the pipeline end-to-end by replicating the human-analyzed capitals multi-hop circuit and by discovering steerable jailbreak clusters in Llama 3.1 8B Instruct with no human in the loop

Methodology

ADAG is a four-stage pipeline (Figure 1), run on Llama 3.1 8B Instruct by default (Qwen3 32B in appendix §D.4).

  1. Circuit tracing backbone. Uses the MLP-neuron circuit tracing of Arora et al. (2026): a locally linear replacement model built via RelP (Jafari et al. 2025) that freezes nonlinearities (SiLU, softmax), stops gradients through QK paths, and halves elementwise multiplications. Attribution score for each MLP neuron is α = m · ∂target/∂m where target = sum of top-k logits, k=5. Neurons below threshold τ = 0.005·target are pruned; edge weights computed similarly with intermediate-layer gradients frozen.
  1. Attribution profiles (§3.2). For each feature in each context, two vectors: input attribution Attr(m,x) = x · ∂m/∂x (per-input-token share of the feature's activation) and output contribution Contrib(m,x) = m · ∂M/∂m (per-top-k-logit share contributed by the feature). These overcome locality bias — the assumption that the token where a feature fires is enough to describe it. BOS attribution is excluded in all experiments.
  1. Clustering (§3.3). Per-context cosine similarity matrices for attribution and contribution are combined via the harmonic mean of clamped non-negative parts (a form of multiple kernel learning), then uniformly averaged over co-occurring contexts. Spectral clustering on the normalized Laplacian L_norm = I − D^(−1/2) A D^(−1/2) uses the k smallest eigenvectors as an embedding for k-means. Cluster count k is set manually (k=64 for capitals, k=20 for pills). Quality is measured by silhouette score, cluster-size CV, and fraction of intra-cluster pairs with all-opposing contribution signs.
  1. Description (§3.4). For each supernode, attribution/contribution profiles are averaged. Input-attribution descriptions use finetuned models Transluce/llama_8b_explainer and Transluce/llama_8b_simulator (Choi et al. 2024); output-contribution descriptions use claude-haiku-4-5-20251001 via the Anthropic API, with scores normalized to integers in [−10,10]. Each candidate is scored by Pearson r between true and predicted scores over all contexts, best-of-20 candidates. Final summaries use claude-opus-4-6 with adaptive thinking. Datasets: capitals (multi-hop state-capital queries, Arora et al. 2026 / Ameisen et al. 2025) and pills (harmful medical-advice sensitivity analysis, Chowdhury et al. 2025, 150 variants). Non-locality analysis samples 128 MLP neurons/layer, 1,000 FineWeb docs truncated to 128 tokens. All experiments on H100 80GB GPUs; the 10,000-example math dataset traced in ≈6 hours over 4 GPUs at batch size 4.

Results & Highlights

  • Jailbreak steering (pills, Table 1, 50 generations, temperature 0.7): unsteered base prompt ASR = 28±6%. Steering C9 ("ridiculous-to-introductory", r=+0.71 vs. ASR) at multiplier 2× raises ASR to 90±5% (from 0±0% at 0×); steering C3 ("pills safety redirect", r=−0.70) at 0× raises ASR to 88±5% and at 2× drops it to 5±3%. The original work (Chowdhury et al. 2025) reported 5.5% base ASR and 0%–88.5% across 150 variants.
  • Capitals circuit steering (Llama 3.1 8B Instruct, §4.2): default top output _Austin = 96.5%. Ablating C2 ("capital city first token") flips the top output to _Texas = 93.4%; ablating C44 ("Dallas Texas") drops _Austin to 52.7% and raises _Oklahoma to 22.1%; ablating the inhibitory C59 ("not[southern capitals]") raises _Austin to 98.4% — matching Arora et al. (2026) but with no human in the loop.
  • LLM vs. human descriptions (Figure 3b, gold supernodes, best-of-20): mean input-attribution score LLM = 0.774 vs. human expert = 0.779 (roughly tied); mean output-contribution score LLM = 0.812 vs. human = 0.648 (LLM substantially better).
  • Clustering ablation (Figure 3a, capitals): the chosen multi-view spectral + harmonic-mean + normalized-Laplacian variant yields the lowest all-opposing-sign intra-cluster rate (0.1%–1.3% across k∈{16,64}) while keeping silhouette ≈0.19–0.39 and cluster-size CV ≈0.07–0.34; concatenated-embedding baselines (K-Means, Ward, Spectral-RBF) give negative silhouette (−0.06 to −0.17) and much higher CV (2.97–7.12).
  • MLP non-locality (Figure 2): only the first 3 layers are well-explained by the preceding 8 tokens; in later layers local context is increasingly insufficient, and >50% of the time the layer-0 top-contributing neuron sits on an earlier token than the target.

Authors & Credibility

Authors are affiliated with Stanford University (Aryaman Arora, Zhengxuan Wu) and Transluce (all four authors), per the PDF header. The work is a preprint "Under review." Code is released at github.com/TransluceAI/circuits. The paper builds directly on Arora et al. (2026), Ameisen et al. (2025), and Choi et al. (2024), and uses the Llama 3.1 8B Instruct and Qwen3 32B models plus Anthropic API models as components.

Critical Assessment

  • Limited and partly qualitative evaluation. The end-to-end validation rests on two small, well-studied tasks (capitals, pills). Clustering quality is reported only via silhouette/CV/Opp% rather than a downstream task metric, and the "gold supernodes" used to score descriptions come from a single prior manual analysis — there is no large-scale benchmark of what a correct automated interpretation should look like.
  • Number of clusters k and threshold τ are set manually. The paper states "We manually select the number of clusters k," and τ = 0.005·target is fixed; sensitivity of the recovered circuits to these choices is only partially explored (§D.1 sweeps the highlight threshold, not k itself). This weakens the "fully automated" claim.
  • Steering side effects. Several clusters produce large coherence drops when steered (e.g., C9 at 0× gives 52±6% incoherent generations), so the ASR deltas partly reflect degraded generations rather than clean causal redirection.
  • Reliance on closed-source explainer/simulator LLMs. Output-contribution descriptions and the final summarizer depend on the Anthropic API (claude-haiku-4-5, claude-opus-4-6), making the pipeline's interpretability outputs depend on an opaque model whose own internals are not characterized.

Strengths

  • Genuinely end-to-end: tracing, profiling, clustering, and natural-language description with no manual supernode assignment — a real reduction in human labor over Ameisen et al. (2025) / Shu et al. (2026)
  • The attribution-profile concept and the non-locality analysis are a substantive contribution beyond engineering; they show why max-activating exemplars are insufficient
  • The clustering ablation is unusually thorough, with three explicit quality metrics and four algorithm variants
  • Code is released; experiments reproduce a known human circuit and add a novel jailbreak application

Limitations

  • Only MLP neurons are studied; attention/QK contributions are explicitly deferred (Discussion, citing Kamath et al. 2025)
  • Evaluation datasets are tiny by ML standards (capitals is a handful of prompts; pills has 150 variants), so generalization to diverse behaviors is unproven
  • The "no human in the loop" claim holds for the pipeline, but k and τ are still human-set and the judge model differs from Chowdhury et al. (2025), complicating direct ASR comparison
  • Closed-source API dependence for the explainer/summarizer stages

Questions for Further Exploration

  • 能否把 k 和 τ 的选择也自动化（如基于 silhouette 或下游 steering 效果的自动选参），从而真正消除人工超参？
  • attribution profiles 框架能否扩展到注意力/QK 电路与 SAE 特征，而不仅限于 MLP 神经元？
  • steering 产生的"不一致"输出（如 C9 在 0× 时 52% incoherent）如何在 ASR 评估中被解耦，以区分"干净的因果 redirection"与"退化生成"？
  • 在更大、更多样的行为数据集（而非 capitals/pills 两个小任务）上，自动描述的 Pearson r 与人类判断的一致性如何变化？
