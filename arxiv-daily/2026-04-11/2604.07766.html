Sensitivity-Positional Co-Localization in GQA Transformers

一句话总结： 在Llama 3.1 8B的GQA架构中发现任务敏感层和位置编码影响层呈现强反局部化现象（Spearman r=-0.735），即敏感层集中在网络后部而位置编码影响层集中在网络前部，挑战了共局部化假设。
TL;DR: Discovers strong anti-localization between task-sensitive layers (late network) and RoPE-influential layers (early network) in Llama 3.1 8B, with Spearman r=-0.735, challenging the co-localization hypothesis while achieving near-Claude 3.5 Haiku performance on HumanEval+ at $100 compute cost.

核心贡献

  • 提出LSLORA——基于正确性差异隐藏状态度量（correctness-differential hidden-state metric）的LoRA适应方法
  • 设计GARFA（GQA-Aware RoPE Frequency Adaptation），为每个目标层附加8个可学习的KV头标量乘数
  • 发现任务敏感层与位置编码影响层之间存在强反局部化现象（Spearman r=-0.735），打破共局部化假设

Key Contributions

  • Introduces LSLORA that restricts LoRA to layers identified via a novel correctness-differential hidden-state metric
  • Proposes GARFA with 8 learnable per-KV-head scalar multipliers per targeted layer for RoPE frequency adaptation
  • Discovers strong anti-localization (r=-0.735): task-sensitive layers concentrate in late network while RoPE-influential layers dominate early network
  • Achieves near-Claude 3.5 Haiku on HumanEval+ (67.1% vs 68.3%) at $100 total compute cost

Methodology

The author tests the co-localization hypothesis on Llama 3.1 8B (32-layer GQA model) by developing two interventions: LSLORA identifies task-sensitive layers via a correctness-differential hidden-state metric and restricts LoRA adaptation to those layers; GARFA attaches learnable scalar multipliers to RoPE frequencies at each KV head. A 4-way cross-layer ablation tests all combinations.

Results & Highlights

  • Task-sensitive layers: l=23-31; RoPE-influential layers: l=0-9; Spearman r=-0.735 (p=1.66e-6)
  • Applying both interventions to sensitivity-identified layers outperforms alternatives by 4-16pp across 6 benchmarks
  • Approaches Claude 3.5 Haiku on HumanEval+ (67.1% vs 68.3%)

Critical Assessment

Novelty: Good — the anti-localization finding is genuinely surprising and the LSLORA/GARFA methods are creative. Rigor: Tests across 6 diverse benchmarks with clear ablation design. The Spearman correlation provides strong statistical evidence. Red flags: Single-author paper raises questions about the scope of review and validation. The $100 compute cost comparison to Claude 3.5 Haiku is eye-catching but potentially misleading — different training paradigms. The anti-localization finding may be specific to Llama 3.1 8B architecture.

Strengths

  • Thorough cross-layer ablation design across 6 diverse benchmarks
  • Low compute cost demonstrated

Limitations
