ExplAIner: A Declarative Query Language for Explaining Classification Models

Marcelo Arenas · Pablo Barceló · Diego Bustamante · José Caraball · María Alejandra Schild · Bernardo Subercaseaux | 2026-07-07

A layered declarative query language tames model-explanation complexity — from unbounded polynomial hierarchy down to a fixed number of SAT calls.

A pure theory paper reframing formal XAI as a database-style data-complexity problem. It diagnoses two flaws in the prior logic FOIL, then defines ExplAIner and its computable fragment Opt-FOIL with tight complexity characterizations.

Key metric

ΣkP-hard → BHk

FOIL evaluation over decision trees is hard for every level of the polynomial hierarchy; every fixed ExplAIner formula lives in BHk for a constant k — a fixed number of NP-oracle (SAT) calls. Opt-FOIL computes in FPNP.

Why this matters

A principled ceiling for SAT-based explainers

Gives formal XAI a precise complexity bound (BH / FPNP) that justifies SAT-based explainers and unifies ten explanation notions under one declarative language.

Critical edge

Boolean-only, empirically unvalidated

No experiments show the constant number of SAT calls is actually small, and the whole framework covers only Boolean classifiers — a large gap to modern ML practice.

01

Methodology

A purely theoretical construction fusing finite model theory, computational complexity, and knowledge compilation. Boolean models are abstracted as functions M:{0,1}n→{0,1}; complexity is measured as data complexity — the query formula is fixed, the model representation plus instance is the input — the yardstick the authors argue is correct because representations are moderate-sized and SAT-solver-friendly.

Stage 1 · Framework

Isolate two relations

Subsumption ⊆ and a cardinality comparison ⪯ over partial instances; study NNF, d-DNNF, BDDs, and decision trees.

Stage 2 · Negative results

Break FOIL twice

Inexpressibility of mAXp via an Ehrenfeucht–Fraïssé game; PH-hardness by reducing QBF over decision trees through guarded-quantifier formulas φk.

Stage 3 · Build ExplAIner

Layered vocabulary

Atomic / quantified / full layers. Atomic decidability and P-membership via a pattern-count encoding into Presburger arithmetic; BH containment + BH-hardness via a "cascade" decision tree.

Stage 4 · Opt-FOIL

Computable fragment

A minimization operator over first-order-definable strict partial orders. Iterated oracle-driven descent on a polynomially-bounded ρ-path yields FPNP.

No quantitative experiments; the "numbers" are exact complexity-class membership and matching hardness over decision trees — the closest analog to a benchmark. The load-bearing delta: FOIL is unbounded in PH, ExplAIner collapses it to a fixed Boolean combination of NP predicates.

03

Strengths & limitations

Strengths

  • Clean reframing. Formal XAI as a data-complexity problem, separating query specification from representation-dependent evaluation.
  • Tight characterizations. Atomic = P, quantified = NP, full = BH, all with matching hardness over decision trees.
  • Constructive algorithm. Opt-FOIL's FPNP descent is concrete and directly usable in a SAT-based explainability toolbox.
  • Broad, uniform coverage. Ten named explanation notions under one language, with closed-form formulas.
  • Knowledge-compilation link. Applies to d-DNNF, 2CNF, HORN, not just trees.

Limitations

  • Boolean models only. No continuous features, multi-class outputs, or structured domains.
  • No experiments. All claims are asymptotic; constants (SAT-call counts, formula widths in practice) are unstudied.
  • Impractical syntax check. Verifying a strict-partial-order formula is double-exponential space (Prop. 5.2).
  • Unproven separations. Opt-FOIL ⊊ ExplAIner ⊊ FOIL relies on PH not collapsing — standard but unproven.
  • Self-comparison bias. The authors created FOIL; a restricted-FOIL middle ground is not explored.
04

Open questions

Q1

Does the BH level k (hence SAT-call count) stay small (say ≤ 3) for wAXp/AXp/mAXp/MCR/MCA, or grow with formula size?

Q2

Is there a tractably-checkable syntactic fragment replacing the 22poly decision procedure of Prop. 5.2?

Q3

Does the framework extend to multi-class via a family of AllClassi predicates, with the BH / FPNP bound surviving?

Q4

How does Opt-FOIL relate to existing SAT-based mAXp / MCR algorithms (Ignatiev–Marques-Silva 2021; Yu et al. 2020)?

Q5

Can database-style query optimization (CSE, memoization of AllPos/AllNeg) speed up batches of ExplAIner queries?

Q6

Is the layered structure necessary, or does a syntactic FOIL fragment already land in BH while keeping most of its expressiveness?
