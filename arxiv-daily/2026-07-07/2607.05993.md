> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示 Bit2Watt：恶意云租户仅用合法用户态 GPU 工作负载即可产生高达 6,000 Hz 的高频功率调制，利用恒功率负载的负阻抗特性与逆变器型 DER 的频率整形阻抗，使 1 MW、90% DER 渗透的本地电网在 1,000 块 GPU 操控下电流 THD 升至 46.8%、阻尼比变为 -0.27，并可反向触发 Watt2Bit 的服务中断与 EMI 隐蔽外泄。

> **TL;DR:** Bit2Watt is a new cyber-physical attack where a legitimate cloud tenant manipulates GPU workloads (SWMA via custom CUDA kernels up to 6,000 Hz, LTMA embedded inside LLM training pipelines) to inject high-frequency power modulations that destabilize inverter-dominated DER grids; in a 1-MW / 90%-DER Simulink system, 1,000 RTX-3090-equivalent GPUs drive current THD to 46.8% (vs. the 13% IEC 61000-3-12 limit and ~19.6% at 10% DER) and the damping ratio to -0.27 (from +0.46 at 200 GPUs), and on a modified 9,241-bus European transmission network a 2%-of-load disturbance cascades to ~81% load loss and 1,238 tripped lines, while routine cloud/facility telemetry (NVML, RAPL, BMC, PDU) gives only ~1-9% TPR @ 1% FPR.

## 核心贡献

- **首次形式化 Bit2Watt 这类"工作负载驱动"的网络物理威胁**：将计算过程建模为可控的"激励源"，与电网动态耦合，建立了连接计算工作负载编排与电网级动态响应的全新攻击面。
- **提出两种纯用户态 GPU 功率调制技术**：合成负载调制攻击 SWMA（自定义 CUDA kernel，最高 6,000 Hz）与 LLM 训练调制攻击 LTMA（把调制逻辑嵌入 GPT-2 训练流水线，最高 3,000 Hz），均不需要 root 或固件级权限。
- **基于阻抗的系统化分析框架**：用 Thévenin/Norton 等效与 Middlebrook 判据量化 GPU 集群的负增量阻抗（NIR）与 DER 输出阻抗的频率整形特性如何共同侵蚀稳定裕度（相位裕度从 ~60° 降到 ~10°）。
- **揭示 Watt2Bit 反馈环路**：攻击诱发的电应力会以 DoS（IEC 60255 IDMT 继电器在电流翻倍时约 2 秒跳闸）和 EMI 隐蔽信道（FSK 2 kHz/200 Hz，USRP B210 解码 50-bit 序列零错误、准确率 >99%）形式反噬计算基础设施。
- **跨尺度实验验证**：从单 GPU/6-GPU 硬件测试台、1-MW 本地系统（Simulink）、IEEE 118-bus，到 9,241-bus 欧洲输电网级联仿真，覆盖 cyber 与 physical 两层。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalizes the "Bit2Watt" workload-driven cyber-physical threat class**, modeling computational processes as controllable excitation sources coupled to grid dynamics, providing a principled bridge between workload orchestration and power-system stability analysis.
- **Two user-space modulation techniques**: SWMA (custom CUDA kernel with active/passive tensor-core + global-memory modes, up to 6,000 Hz on an RTX 4090) and LTMA (modulation embedded in legitimate LLM training primitives via GPT-2, up to 3,000 Hz), both requiring no privileged access.
- **Impedance-based vulnerability analysis** capturing GPU-cluster negative incremental resistance (NIR), constant-power-load (CPL) behavior, and the resonant, frequency-shaped output impedance of inverter-based DERs, with the Middlebrook ratio degrading the phase margin from ~60° to ~10° as DER penetration rises.
- **Extends the impact to the Watt2Bit feedback path**: harmonic-induced heating (+17.8% at the 50th harmonic order), protection trips (IEC 60255 IDMT Standard Inverse trips in ~2 s on a 2x current), and a covert EMI exfiltration channel (FSK at 2 kHz / 200 Hz decoded with >99% accuracy and zero bit errors on a 50-bit sequence).
- **Multi-scale validation** spanning single-GPU DAQ measurements, a 1-MW Simulink local system, the IEEE 118-bus network, and the 9,241-bus European transmission system for cascading-failure analysis.

## Methodology

**Threat model.** A legitimate but malicious cloud tenant (e.g., on AWS / GCP / Alibaba / Huawei / Azure) controls GPU workloads through standard SDKs and scheduling. By coordinating start/stop timing and compute intensity, the tenant induces fast, repetitive, facility-level power variations. Topology-aware schedulers (NVLink/InfiniBand placement) inadvertently co-locate attacker GPUs in the same rack, enabling physical aggregation; PTP or provider-managed clocks provide temporal synchronization. Rental cost is only $0.39-$2.99/hour/GPU and a synchronized burst needs only minutes.

**Two cyber-layer modulation methods (no root).**
- *SWMA (Synthetic Workload Modulation Attack)*: a persistent CUDA kernel with two internal modes — an active mode saturating SMs with tensor-core/FP32 FMA loops and large global-memory accesses, and a passive mode that idles. A user-space CPU controller flips a `cudaMallocManaged` flag to switch modes; fine-grained host-side scheduling yields precise frequency/duty-cycle control.
- *LTMA (LLM Training Modulation Attack)*: modulation logic embedded directly inside a GPT-2 training script (legitimate tensor-core GEMMs, fused attention, backprop primitives), so malicious power signatures masquerade as normal training noise — no synthetic kernel is visible.

**Physical-layer modeling.** The local grid is represented at the PCC by three Norton/Thévenin equivalents: the main grid (Vg, Zg), the inverter-based DER (controlled current source with frequency-shaped Zder from PLL + outer voltage + inner current loops), and the GPU cluster modeled as a near-constant-power load (CPL) with NIR Zl ≈ -Vl²/Pl in parallel with a workload-controlled current sink -Ils. Stability is judged by the Middlebrook ratio |Tm| = |Zg/Zdl| < 1; Hopf-bifurcation conditions on the Jacobian eigenvalues λ₁,₂ = α ± jβ explain the damping collapse.

**Validation ladder.** (1) Real GPU DAQ on RTX 2080/3080/3090/4090, Tesla V100, A100 with an NI USB-6421 sensing the +12 V aux rail; (2) a ~100-GPU cluster testbed fed by a 3-phase AC UPS to verify CPL/NIR via 225 V↔195 V step perturbations; (3) a TI C2000 inverter + PV-simulator impedance scan (1 Hz-10 kHz) confirming resonant peaks; (4) a 1-MW Simulink local system with detailed PV/UPS/SPS PI controllers (Tables 2-3); (5) IEEE 118-bus and 9,241-bus European transmission simulations for wide-area behavior; (6) an 8 kW hardware testbed (~40% PV, 6 GPUs across two ThinkStation PX + Dell T5820, SHANKE C3KS UPS) measuring SPS→PDU→UPS current waveforms and spectra; (7) EMI exfiltration via a USRP B210 + near-field antenna.

## Results & Highlights

- **GPU modulation bandwidth (Table 1, measured).** SWMA reaches 1,500 Hz (RTX 2080, 103 W amplitude), 2,000 Hz (RTX 3080, 157 W), 5,000 Hz (RTX 3090, 204 W), **6,000 Hz (RTX 4090, 248 W)**, 3,000 Hz (Tesla V100, 124 W), 5,000 Hz (A100, 135 W); LTMA trails at 1.2-3 kHz with larger amplitudes — versus only "a few hertz" for air-conditioner loads.
- **THD in the 1-MW / 90%-DER system.** With 1,000 synchronized RTX-3090-equivalent GPUs, **current THD = 46.80%**, far above the IEC 61000-3-12 13% guideline; even at 10% DER the THD stays at **19.60%**. At 50% DER, only 500/400/900 GPUs are needed to breach voltage / harmonic / stability thresholds respectively.
- **Damping collapse.** At 90% DER the damping ratio falls from **+0.46 at 200 GPUs to -0.27 at 1,000 GPUs**, crossing the Hopf threshold; the negative-damping transition occurs around **~800 GPUs at 80% DER**.
- **Wide-area cascade (9,241-bus European network).** A disturbance equal to only 2% of total load, after 13 cascading stages, **disconnects 1,238 transmission lines and sheds ~81% of load**, with sharp extrema in both loss and propagation abruptness near 300 tampered GPUs/MW (chaotic sensitivity).
- **IEEE 118-bus bifurcation.** As damping crosses zero the system splits into two coherent frequency clusters — a "pseudo-stable" bounded oscillation indicating transient grid partitioning.
- **UPS DC-bus ripple.** In hardware, attack drives ripple from **0.45% to 2.82%** (above the ABB guideline), forcing the UPS out of constant-power into current-limiting mode; UPS-stage THD rises from 5.55% to **50.76%**, SPS-stage from 5.08% to 8.91%, PDU-stage from 5.55% to 8.17%.
- **Watt2Bit DoS.** Harmonic-induced cumulative heating rises **+17.8% at the 50th harmonic order**; under the IEC 60255 Standard Inverse curve, a current doubling trips protection in ~2 seconds.
- **EMI exfiltration.** FSK encoding (2 kHz = "1", 200 Hz = "0", 10 ms/bit) decoded via the band-power ratio M = E(1080-1180 kHz)/E(600-800 kHz) recovers a **50-bit sequence with zero bit errors and >99% accuracy**.
- **Stealth against routine telemetry (logistic-regression detector, TPR @ 1% FPR).** Power-only: SWMA 1.76% / LTMA 1.12%; Power+NVML: 9.01% / 3.62%; Power+NVML+CUPTI: 25.08% / 18.05%; **EMI side-channel: 69.33% / 40.93%** — i.e., coarse cloud/facility telemetry is largely blind and LTMA is consistently harder than SWMA.
- **Timing-jitter robustness.** For 2 kHz modulation with σ_τ = 100 µs the amplitude drops only ~20%, and the loss can be compensated by adding more victim GPUs.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high).** The cross-domain framing — unprivileged workload-level GPU control weaponized against inverter-dominated DER grids via the CPL/NIR + frequency-shaped Zder interaction — is genuinely fresh and not reducible to the prior MaDIoT/BlackIoT IoT-botnet load-switching line or to GPU power-side-channel work. The Watt2Bit feedback closure (DoS via IDMT trips + EMI exfiltration) is a thoughtful and non-obvious extension.
- **Rigor (mixed-strong).** The impedance-based analysis is principled and the multi-scale validation ladder is impressive. **However, the headline grid-impact numbers (THD 46.8%, damping -0.27, 81% cascade) all rest on an explicit, worst-case "synchronized assumption": identical frequency and phase across all GPUs, with "device-level heterogeneity, phase dispersion, and communication delays ... not explicitly modeled," which the authors themselves describe as "a conservative bound on realistic system behavior" (i.e., a pessimistic/worst-case bound, not a realistic expectation). The paper is honest about this, but the abstract leads with the 46.8%/-0.27 figures without that caveat.
- **Baseline/detector weakness.** Stealth is evaluated only against a "lightweight logistic-regression baseline" because "no established detector specifically targets this attack class." That is fair for a first paper, but it inflates the stealth claim — a frequency-domain anomaly detector on existing 450 Hz NVML or 1 kHz BMC streams, or a trained DNN, could plausibly do much better and is not tried.
- **Missing ablations.** There is no systematic sensitivity study over attacker-controlled GPU fraction at fixed DER penetration (only scattered points), no study of how many GPUs a single realistic tenant can actually co-schedule under real cloud quotas, and no measurement of the modulation that survives a real end-to-end SPS→PDU→UPS→PCC chain at data-center scale (the chain results are only at the 8 kW / 6-GPU testbed).
- **Reproducibility (moderate).** Simulink parameters are fully tabulated (Tables 2-3) and the hardware BOM is detailed; but the modified 9,241-bus case file, the LTMA training-script instrumentation, and the EMI decoder are not released, and the "1,000 GPUs in 1 MW" extrapolation rests on idealized aggregation that others cannot easily re-verify.
- **Significance.** If even partially realistic, the coupling identified is policy-relevant for data-center-rich regions (Virginia's 25.6% state-electricity share is cited) and for the rapidly growing DER-penetration trend; it credibly motivates cross-layer defenses.
- **Red flags.** (a) Worst-case synchronized aggregation is doing a lot of work in the headline numbers and timing-jitter results suggest real-world amplitude could be materially lower; (b) the "blackouts exceeding 80%" phrasing is a simulated cascade on an idealized network, not a demonstrated outcome; (c) some cost and scale figures (e.g., "1,000 GPUs" being "approximately one quarter" of a 1-MW system) implicitly assume very low-power GPUs relative to the stated RTX-3090 reference.

## Strengths

- Genuinely novel threat model that does not require compromising any device, firmware, or communication channel — only legitimate tenant privileges.
- Two complementary attack instantiations (precise/high-frequency SWMA vs. stealthy LTMA) that bracket the precision-concealment trade-off.
- Strong analytical backbone (CPL, NIR, Middlebrook, Hopf bifurcation) tying cyber-level workload to physical-level instability.
- Multi-scale empirical evidence from single-GPU DAQ, a 100-GPU cluster CPL/NIR test, inverter impedance scans, an 8 kW hardware grid testbed, and IEEE 118-bus / 9,241-bus European transmission simulations.
- Honest treatment of asynchronization, chaotic sensitivity, damping-vs-inertia ranking, and the attenuation-and-reshaping behavior across SPS/PDU/UPS stages.
- Concrete Watt2Bit demonstrations (IDMT trip times, EMI FSK decoding) rather than speculation.
- Actionable, layered countermeasure discussion (BESS/supercaps, runtime CUPTI-style profiling, pipeline integrity checks) and explicit ethical-considerations section.

## Limitations

- All quantitative grid-impact results assume **perfect synchronization** of all attacker GPUs; the only de-synchronization study (σ_τ up to 100 µs) is for a single 2 kHz case and only measures amplitude loss, not the resulting THD/damping.
- **Detector baseline is intentionally weak** (logistic regression); no frequency-domain, ML, or operator-style detectors are evaluated, so the "stealth" claim is an upper bound.
- Hardware validation is at the **8 kW / 6-GPU / 40%-PV scale**; data-center-scale (>MW, hundreds of GPUs) effects are only simulated, not measured, and the 9241-bus cascade uses a heavily idealized protection model.
- No study of how many GPUs a **single real tenant account** can practically co-schedule across availability zones, or how cloud-provider placement policies would dilute aggregation in practice.
- **No code or datasets released** (Simulink model, modified 9241-bus case, LTMA scripts, EMI decoder) — limited independent reproducibility.
- Countermeasures are discussed qualitatively; none are implemented or benchmarked, so defensive cost/effectiveness is open.
- The EMI exfiltration demo assumes the attacker already controls the modulating GPUs (so it is exfiltration from the attacker's own job, raising the question of what threat scenario it serves beyond a proof-of-concept covert channel).

## Relevance

高度相关于"安全 × 系统"与"AI 基础设施可靠性"两条研究主线：它首次指出大模型训练/推理的合法 GPU 工作负载本身就可被武器化为对新型高 DER 渗透电网的攻击面，对 AI 数据中心选址、云租户权限模型、以及数据中心—电网协同防御都具有直接的政策与工程启示，是任何关注 LLM 训练基础设施安全与电网耦合风险的研究者都应追踪的工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Under realistic cloud placement and scheduling jitter (σ_τ > 100 µs, multi-rack dispersion), what is the *expected* THD and damping impact rather than the worst-case synchronized bound?
- How much does detection improve if a defender applies a simple FFT/STFT anomaly detector on existing 450 Hz NVML or 1 kHz BMC streams, or a lightweight trained classifier on CUPTI counters — can the ~25% TPR ceiling be materially broken?
- What is the minimum DER penetration and minimum attacker GPU fraction at which Bit2Watt becomes physically consequential on a *measured* (not simulated) >100 kW deployment?
- Can the attack be neutralized at the cyber layer by capping user-controlled modulation bandwidth (e.g., scheduler-enforced power-ramp limits, GPU power-cap smoothing as in Microsoft/OpenAI/NVIDIA's 2025 power-stabilization work) without harming legitimate training throughput?
- Does the Watt2Bit EMI exfiltration channel survive in a noisy multi-tenant data center, and what is its achievable channel capacity under realistic EM environments?
- How do the cascade results change under more realistic protection-relay coordination, BESS buffering, and cloud-provider load-shedding policies?
