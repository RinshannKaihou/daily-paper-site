> **一句话总结：** 本文将 Heckman 两方程选择模型（probit 选择方程 + 与结果方程相关的联合二元正态误差）迁移到深度认知不确定性估计，证明了在「选择依赖于不可观测变量」时，即便使用 oracle 倾向得分的重加权也结构性失效，而带工具变量的 Heckman 校正在合成数据 (ρ=0.9) 上将名义 90% 区间的覆盖率从 43.1%（oracle IW）/ 64.4%（deep ensemble）恢复到 88.9%，并在 RAND HIE 上将经典 Stata 七位数参考输出复现至 2.8×10⁻⁶。

> **TL;DR:** The paper ports Heckman's 1979 selection model (a probit selection equation jointly fit with an outcome equation via correlated bivariate-normal errors) to deep epistemic uncertainty: when sampling depends on unobservables correlated with the outcome noise, oracle-propensity importance weighting fails structurally (43.1% coverage of nominal-90% intervals at ρ=0.9 on the controlled generator, vs 64.4% for deep ensembles), whereas the Heckman correction with an instrument restores coverage to 88.9% and degrades honestly to 40.3% without one; implementations reproduce Cameron–Trivedi's Stata RAND-HIE output to seven digits (max deviation 2.8×10⁻⁶ two-step, 5.0×10⁻⁷ MLE), and on California/wine tabular MNAR tasks the two-step is the best-calibrated non-oracle method in selected-against regions.

## 核心贡献

- **忠实性门控实现**：实现了 probit、两步法与联合 MLE 三种 Heckman 估计器，以复现经典计量经济学参考输出作为预注册门槛——在 Cameron–Trivedi 的 RAND HIE 规范上，两步法与 Stata 七位数输出的最大绝对偏差为 2.8×10⁻⁶，MLE 为 5.0×10⁻⁷；probit 头与 statsmodels 一致到 10⁻⁸。任何实验都在这 11 项测试全绿之后才运行。
- **深度 Heckman 不确定性**：将深度 MLP 作为结果函数 f_θ，线性选择头 g_ψ(w)=w⊤γ+b（工具变量 z 只在此进入），加两个全局参数 (logσ, atanhρ)，通过在所有单元（含未选中）上最大化联合二元正态似然训练，并以 K=5 的集成给出选择校正后的预测分布。
- **对照演示与「致死条件」未触发**：构造了选择概率依赖与结果噪声相关 (ρ=0→0.9) 的不可观测量的生成器；oracle 倾向得分的重加权仅覆盖 43.1%（比不加权的 64.4% 还差），而带工具变量的两步 Heckman 覆盖 88.9%、MLE 覆盖 89.1%——预注册的「IW 追平 Heckman 即证伪前提」的致死条件未触发（配对 Wilcoxon p=0.008, n=8）。
- **方法学发现**：指出在深度特征图下联合 MLE 必须先用 ρ≡0 的 warm-up（1500 轮中前 100 轮），否则 f_θ 会吸收校正项导致 ρ̂ 塌缩到 0（实测在真值 0.8 时塌缩到 0.16）；两步法因 probit 与结果拟合硬分离而天然稳健，故推荐为默认。
- **半真实验证与现实警示**：在 California housing 与 UCI wine 上构造有文档记录的诱导式 MNAR 选择，两步 Heckman 在被选择对抗区域是最低 region-ECE 的非 oracle 方法（0.051/0.138 vs deep ensemble 0.201/0.223）；并在 Papers-with-Code 报告面板上做 vignette 分析，13 个拟合中有 8 个撞到 |ρ̂|=1 边界，正是无工具变量时的退化征兆。

## Key Contributions

- **Faithfulness-gated implementation**: Probit, two-step, and joint-MLE Heckman estimators that reproduce Cameron and Trivedi's seven-digit Stata reference output on the RAND HIE data (max coefficient deviation 2.8×10⁻⁶ two-step, 5.0×10⁻⁷ MLE; probit agrees with statsmodels to 10⁻⁸), pre-registered as a gate before any experiment runs.
- **Deep Heckman UQ**: A deep MLP outcome function f_θ paired with a deliberately linear selection head g_ψ(w)=w⊤γ+b (the instrument z enters only here), trained by the joint bivariate-normal likelihood over all units including unselected ones; ensembles of K=5 give a selection-corrected predictive distribution.
- **Controlled demonstration with a pre-registered kill condition**: On a generator where selection probability depends on an unobservable correlated (ρ swept 0→0.9) with outcome noise, oracle-propensity importance weighting covers only 43.1% (worse than unweighted 64.4%) while the two-step Heckman covers 88.9% and the MLE 89.1% — the kill condition (IW matching Heckman would falsify the premise) does not fire (paired Wilcoxon p=0.008, n=8).
- **A methods finding**: With deep feature maps the joint MLE requires a ρ≡0 warm-up schedule (first 100 of 1500 epochs), otherwise the flexible outcome net absorbs the Mills correction and ρ̂ collapses toward 0 (observed 0.16 at true 0.8); the two-step's hard probit/outcome separation is inherently robust and is recommended as the default.
- **Semi-real validation and a real-world vignette**: Induced, documented MNAR selection on California housing and UCI wine shows the two-step Heckman has the lowest selected-against region-ECE of any non-oracle method (0.051 / 0.138 vs deep-ensemble 0.201 / 0.223); a Papers-with-Code reporting-panel analysis finds 8 of 13 fits on the |ρ̂|=1 boundary — the textbook no-instrument pathology appearing in the wild.

## Methodology

The model instantiates Heckman's two-equation selection framework with deep feature maps. The outcome function f_θ is a deep MLP; the selection index g_ψ(w)=w⊤γ+b is deliberately linear in w=[x, z], where z is an instrument that affects selection but not the outcome. Two global parameters (log σ, atanh ρ) complete the model. The selected-sample conditional mean satisfies E[y|x,w,s=1] = f(x) + ρσ λ(w⊤γ) with λ = ϕ/Φ the inverse Mills ratio, so the population f(x) is recoverable once ρ and γ are estimated.

Two training routes are implemented and compared throughout. (1) Joint MLE maximizes the bivariate-normal likelihood ℓ over all units — unselected units contribute log Φ(−g_ψ) — with Adam; the key contingency is that with a flexible f_θ the correction term is absorbed early (the likelihood is locally flat in ρ), so ρ is frozen at 0 for a 100-epoch warm-up (of 1500), letting f_θ first fit the selected-sample conditional mean before the z-variation of the Mills term separates the correction. (2) The two-step estimator first fits a probit on w over all units for γ̂, then the network fits y ≈ f_θ(x) + c·λ(w⊤γ̂) on selected units by Gaussian NLL with c a free scalar, recovering σ by the classic residual correction; the population prediction is f_θ(x) alone. The two-step needs no warm-up because of its hard separation.

The corrected predictive distribution follows the standard deep-ensemble recipe with the population function in place of the selected-sample one: ŷ(x) is the mean over K=5 independently initialized models, and the predictive variance decomposes into epistemic Var_k[f_θk(x)] plus aleatoric (1/K)Σ σ̂²_k. Baselines (deep ensembles, MC dropout, RBF GP, oracle-propensity importance-weighted ensemble, a selection-blind two-head ablation with ρ≡0, and a skyline ensemble trained on the full pre-selection pool) receive the same ensemble treatment.

The faithfulness gate (A-E0) requires reproducing Cameron–Trivedi's RAND HIE Stata output, statsmodels' probit, and the Mroz/Greene two-step, plus recovery of (β, ρ, σ) on synthetic data with known truth, before any experiment runs. The controlled generator (A-E1) uses x~U[−3,3], instrument z~N(0,1), (ε,u) bivariate normal with Corr=ρ, σ=0.5, y=sin(1.5x)+0.5x+ε, selection index g₀(x)+αz+u with g₀ decreasing in x; grid ρ∈{0,0.3,0.6,0.9} × α∈{0,1} × 8 seeds, n=2000, metrics on a fresh uniform test set split by region (P(s=1|x)≤0.3 is "selected-against"). Semi-real experiments (A-E2/E3) induce MNAR selection on California housing and UCI wine by a documented rule combining the standardized linear prediction of y, its residual scaled by ρ, an instrument, and a 50% threshold; region-ECE (calibration error by region) is the lead metric because it cannot be gamed by global over-widening. A vignette (A-E4) fits the classic Heckman MLE to Papers-with-Code reporting panels.

## Results & Highlights

- **Faithfulness gate (A-E0)**: On the RAND HIE spec (n=5574, 4281 selected), the two-step matches Cameron–Trivedi's Stata output with **max absolute deviation 2.8×10⁻⁶** across all coefficients, ρ, σ, β_λ; the MLE matches with **5.0×10⁻⁷** (log-likelihood difference 0.000; ρ̂=0.7356, σ̂=1.5701); the probit head agrees with statsmodels to **10⁻⁸**; on synthetic data with known truth (n=10⁵, ρ=0.6) both estimators recover (β,ρ,σ) while naive OLS does not — eleven tests all green.
- **Controlled demonstration (A-E1), ρ=0.9 with instrument**: deep-ensemble coverage of nominal-90% intervals in selected-against regions is **64.4%** (77.8% at ρ=0.6) with predictive-mean bias 0.441 (σ=0.5); **oracle-propensity importance weighting covers only 43.1%** (bias 0.678) — worse than unweighted — confirming the pre-registered kill condition does not fire (paired Wilcoxon p=0.008, n=8); the **two-step Heckman covers 88.9%** (88.8% at ρ=0.6) with bias 0.002 and region-ECE **0.036 vs the deep ensemble's 0.266** (oracle 0.012); the **joint MLE covers 89.1%** and recovers ρ̂=0.90±0.02; the blind two-head ablation (ρ≡0) stays at **69.3%**, showing the gain is the joint error model, not the extra head.
- **No-instrument honesty curve (A-E1, α=0)**: Without the exclusion restriction, point estimates stay biased for every method — at ρ=0.9 the MLE's selected-against mean bias is **0.582** and the two-step's **0.778** (vs 0.002 with instrument), and ρ̂ collapses to 0.14 (true 0.9); the MLE's coverage looks deceptively high (**84.7%**) only because weak identification inflates variance, while the two-step exposes the damage directly at **40.3%** (vs 28.8% for its ensemble) — coverage can actively mislead.
- **Semi-real tabular MNAR (A-E2/E3, ρ=0.8)**: The two-step Heckman has the lowest selected-against region-ECE on both datasets — **0.051 (California) / 0.138 (wine)** — versus deep ensemble 0.201/0.223, oracle-IW 0.235/0.252, MC dropout 0.175/0.200, GP 0.114/0.164, approaching the skyline's 0.026/0.049 (paired one-sided Wilcoxon two-step below deep-ensemble, IW, and dropout, pooled over datasets: p<0.001 in all three); baselines that match its raw selected-against coverage (77.5% / 64.1%) do so only by over-widening, over-covering well-sampled regions at **98.0% / 93.2% on California at nominal 90%**; oracle-IW remains the worst-calibrated method in selected-against regions, as theory predicts. At ρ=0 (MAR control) the correction costs nothing (selected-against coverages 73.9% vs 71.8% on California).
- **Methods finding**: Without the ρ≡0 warm-up the joint MLE's ρ̂ collapses toward 0 (observed ρ̂→0.16 at true 0.8 in tuning); with warm-up ρ̂=0.90±0.02 and coverage 89.1% matching the two-step; the two-step needs no such schedule and is recommended as default.
- **Benchmark-panel vignette (A-E4)**: Fitting the Heckman MLE to Papers-with-Code reporting panels (no exclusion restriction), **8 of 13 panel/pairwise fits hit the |ρ̂|=1 boundary**; interior estimates are suggestive (ImageNet→ImageNet-ReaL ρ̂=0.87±0.09) but sign-unstable (machine translation −0.90) — the real-world face of the no-instrument pathology.

## Authors & Credibility

Single-author work by **Gunner Levi Howe**, listed as an Independent Researcher. The paper is unusually disciplined in its methodology: it pre-registers a faithfulness gate (reproducing seven-digit Stata output) before running any experiment, pre-registers a kill condition (IW matching Heckman would falsify the premise) and reports that it does not fire, and sweeps the identification-honesty curve (instrument present vs absent) rather than reporting only the best case. It cites the core econometric literature (Heckman 1974/1979, Puhani, Vella, Winship & Mare), the ML UQ baselines it uses (deep ensembles, MC dropout, GPs, Ovadia et al.), and the nearest prior work — O'Neill (2025) on Type-2 Tobit with BART — distinguishing its contribution precisely. A companion paper on survivor bias in successive-halving is referenced. Reproducibility is strong: all code, tests, committed result JSONs, and an auto-generated numbers.tex (regenerate-and-diff passes at submission) are released; experiments run on a single RTX 3080 (10 GB) in under six GPU-hours; datasets (RandHIE, Mroz87 from the sampleSelection R package) and the Cameron–Trivedi Stata reference output are committed; archived release DOI 10.5281/zenodo.21210466; a pre-submission recency sweep against a 3M-abstract index is logged in PLAN.md.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Genuine and well-scoped. The gap claimed — a deep-learning instantiation of selection-corrected epistemic UQ, distinct from O'Neill's BART-based Type-2 Tobit (which targets econometric estimation, not epistemic UQ under shift) and from the selective-labels/PU-learning lines — is real, and the headline result (oracle IW fails where Heckman succeeds) is a crisp, falsifiable claim that the paper actually tests with a pre-registered kill condition.
- **Rigor**: High in the controlled setting. The faithfulness gate (seven-digit Stata reproduction, statsmodels to 10⁻⁸, synthetic-truth recovery) is a serious correctness check rare in ML-UQ papers. The controlled experiment sweeps ρ and instrument presence, uses 8 seeds, reports paired Wilcoxon tests, and includes the selection-blind two-head ablation (ρ≡0, covering 69.3%) that isolates the joint error model from the extra head.
- **Weak baselines / caveats**: The headline 88.9% vs 43.1% is on a synthetic generator the authors designed, where the selection equation is linear and the bivariate-normal assumption holds exactly by construction — i.e., the one regime where Heckman is guaranteed to work. The "semi-real" experiments (California, wine) induce selection via the authors' own rule that again embeds a linear, Gaussian-compatible structure, so they are not tests of the bivariate-normal assumption on genuinely non-Gaussian data; the paper concedes the skyline itself undercovers wine (non-Gaussian noise) and that no method reaches nominal coverage there. The deep-ensemble baseline is a standard 5-member MLP ensemble but its architecture/depth and the GP's kernel are not detailed, leaving open whether baselines were tuned to a fair operating point.
- **Missing ablations**: No test of a nonlinear selection head (acknowledged as untested); no relaxation of the homoscedastic-Gaussian error model to, e.g., t-copula or heteroscedastic errors on real data (named as future work but not attempted); no comparison to O'Neill's BART-Tobit as a baseline rather than only as related work; the ensembles are fixed at K=5 with no sensitivity analysis over ensemble size.
- **Reproducibility**: Strong on paper — code, tests, committed result JSONs, auto-generated numbers.tex, and a Zenodo DOI are all provided, and the compute footprint (one RTX 3080, <6 GPU-hours) is modest. The discipline of machine-generating every number from results/*.json with a regenerate-and-diff gate is commendable and uncommon.
- **Significance**: Conditional. If the bivariate-normal assumption is even approximately right and an instrument exists, the method is a real advance over reweighting for the MNAR case that deployed UQ systems actually face (loan-granting, admission, simulation campaigns). The honesty about the no-instrument regime (8/13 benchmark-panel fits on the |ρ̂|=1 boundary) is the paper's most valuable practical contribution, but it also bounds applicability: many real ML deployments will lack a defensible instrument and land on the degraded curve.
- **Red flags**: The benchmark-panel vignette (A-E4) is presented cautiously as "a methodological caution, not an estimate," but its 8/13 boundary hits and sign-unstable interior estimates (ImageNet +0.87, machine translation −0.90) could be misread as substantive findings; the paper is careful to flag this but the section is the weakest evidentially. The single-author, independent-researcher status with a companion paper cross-referenced (successive-halving survivor bias, [11]) slightly raises the question of how the two relate and whether results are double-counted across them, though this paper does not appear to depend on the companion.

## Strengths

- Pre-registered faithfulness gate (reproduce seven-digit Stata before any experiment) and a pre-registered kill condition (IW matching Heckman would falsify the premise) — an evidential standard rare in ML-UQ papers.
- Sweeps and reports the identification-honesty curve (instrument present vs absent, ρ=0→0.9) rather than cherry-picking the best case; explicitly states that without an instrument neither point estimate nor coverage should be trusted and that coverage can actively mislead.
- The methods finding (joint MLE needs ρ≡0 warm-up or ρ̂ collapses; two-step is inherently robust) is a concrete, reusable observation for anyone combining Heckman with flexible function approximators.
- Uses region-ECE as the lead metric precisely because raw coverage can be gamed by global over-widening — and demonstrates this gaming directly (GP covers 98.0% of nominal-90% in well-sampled California regions).
- Honest scoping in §8 ("What we do not claim"): no causal-discovery claims, no real-MNAR ground-truth claims beyond induced selection, no superiority under ρ=0 where importance weighting is consistent and simpler.

## Limitations

- The homoscedastic-Gaussian error model is assumed throughout; real heteroscedastic, non-Gaussian data (wine) already shows the cost, as even the skyline undercovers — heteroscedastic and t-copula extensions are named but not implemented.
- The selection head is linear and the controlled/semi-real generators are constructed to be Gaussian-compatible and (for the semi-real case) linear in the selection index, so the central demonstrations occur in the regime most favorable to the method; a nonlinear selection index with a valid instrument is compatible with the likelihood but untested.
- Ensembles of size K=5 approximate epistemic variance coarsely, with no sensitivity analysis over K.
- The semi-real experiments induce selection by the authors' own rule, so while ground truth exists for all rows, the MNAR mechanism is still synthetic rather than naturally occurring; genuinely real MNAR ground truth is unavailable (RAND HIE and the reporting panels are acknowledged as unknowable without instruments).
- The benchmark-panel vignette aggregates heterogeneous metrics and papers across Papers-with-Code tasks and is acknowledged as illustrative only; its 8/13 boundary hits and sign-unstable estimates (ImageNet +0.87 vs machine translation −0.90) are too noisy to support substantive claims.

## Relevance

This paper is directly relevant to uncertainty quantification under distribution shift — a central concern for deploying ML models on selection-biased data (loan approval, medical admission, simulation campaigns). For research on epistemic uncertainty, covariate-shift correction, and trustworthy ML, it offers a principled econometric correction (Heckman selection) for the MNAR regime that standard importance weighting structurally cannot address, plus an honest characterization of when it works (instrument present) and when it degrades.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the Heckman correction behave when the bivariate-normal error assumption is violated — e.g., under heavy-tailed (t-copula) or heteroscedastic noise — and can O'Neill's Dirichlet-process-mixture error model be dropped in as a drop-in relaxation while preserving the epistemic-UQ framing?
- What happens with a nonlinear selection head (e.g., a small MLP for g_ψ) when a valid instrument is available — does identification hold, and does the joint MLE still need the warm-up contingency?
- Can natural instruments common in ML deployments (sensor/site/annotator identity, exploration epsilon, successive-halving rung randomness) be validated as exclusion restrictions in practice, and how sensitive are the coverage gains to instrument weakness?
- How do the gains scale with ensemble size K and with dataset size — does the two-step's robustness advantage over the joint MLE persist, or does the MLE overtake it given enough data and a well-tuned warm-up?
- On the benchmark-panel vignette, can candidate reporting instruments (venue page limits, dataset release dates) actually recover interior ρ̂ estimates and move fits off the |ρ̂|=1 boundary, turning the cautionary vignette into a usable correction for leaderboard aggregation?
