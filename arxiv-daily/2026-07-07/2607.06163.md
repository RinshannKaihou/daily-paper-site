> **一句话总结：** 本文提出首个面向电子病历基础模型（FEMR）的 token 级可解释方法 X-FEMR，用一个事件级 Transformer 替代模型逼近 CLMBR-T-Base 的输入输出行为，再通过 SHAP 定位关键 token，并提出"临床验证事件比例"指标；在 EHRSHOT 数据集的住院时长（LOS）与 ICU 转移任务上，替代模型在 hard-label 下超越 FEMR（LOS AUPRC 0.4434 vs. 0.4173），且被识别的关键 token 与 NEWS2 等临床知识高度一致（临床验证事件比例约 0.27–0.31）。

> **TL;DR:** X-FEMR is the first token-level explainability framework for Electronic Health Records Foundation Models (FEMRs): it trains an event-level Transformer surrogate to imitate CLMBR-T-Base's input-output behavior on the EHRSHOT dataset, applies SHAP to attribute importance to event tokens, and introduces a "Clinical Validated Events Ratio" to score explanation quality. On the LOS (length-of-stay ≥7 days) and ICU-transfer tasks the hard-label surrogate matches or exceeds the 141M-param FEMR (LOS AUPRC 0.4434 vs. 0.4173; ICU-transfer AUPRC 0.2701 vs. 0.1592), and the top attributed tokens (heart rate, blood pressure, respiratory rate, oxygen saturation, temperature) align with NEWS2 clinical predictors, yielding a clinical-alignment ratio of 0.31 (LOS) and 0.27 (ICU).

## 核心贡献

- **首个面向 FEMR 的 token 级可解释框架 X-FEMR**：区别于以往面向特征/面向医学影像的可解释方法，针对结构化纵向电子病历提出事件级 token 级解释，明确指出"哪些 token、在何时"驱动了 FEMR 的预测。
- **事件级 Transformer 替代模型**：每个临床事件由 code + 数值 + 文本 + 到预测时间的时间差 ∆t 四通道构成，使用门控数值注入、时间投影与可学习位置嵌入；同时支持 hard-label（二值决策）与 soft-label（logit 变换后的概率）两种监督，用于区分"驱动决策的因素"与"影响内部不确定性的因素"。
- **临床验证事件比例（Clinical Validated Events Ratio, R）**：提出新的可解释性评估指标 R = Σ_{fj∈Fc} c(fj) / Σ_{fj∈F} c(fj)，量化 SHAP 关键 token 与经临床验证特征（NEWS2 六项生理参数、ICD-10 等组成的 Fc）的对齐度，将抽象的 SHAP 值转化为可解释、可量化的临床对齐度量。
- **实证证据**：在 EHRSHOT 的 LOS 与 ICU 转移两项任务上，替代模型在 hard-label 下达到或超过 141M 参数 CLMBR-T-Base 的性能；top-5 高频 token（心率、收缩压、体温、舒张压、呼吸频率/血氧饱和度）与临床指南一致，为 FEMR 的临床可信度提供了 token 级证据。

## Key Contributions

- **First token-level explainability method for FEMRs.** X-FEMR moves beyond feature-level or image-focused XAI by attributing predictions to individual EHR event tokens, answering both *which* variable and *when* it influenced the FEMR's decision.
- **Event-level Transformer surrogate with time-aware encoding.** Each clinical event token combines a learned code embedding, text embedding, a content-gated numeric injection, and a log-transformed time-to-prediction feature ∆t = (T − tevent)/3600; the surrogate is trained under both hard-label and soft-label (logit-transformed probability) supervision, enabling the authors to separate decision-driving factors from uncertainty-driving factors.
- **A novel clinical-alignment metric.** The Clinical Validated Events Ratio R measures the fraction of SHAP-attributed token occurrences that fall inside a clinically validated feature set Fc (NEWS2 vitals, ICD-10 codes for frailty/comorbidity, etc.), converting SHAP values into a quantitative, clinician-meaningful score.
- **Empirical demonstration on two clinical tasks.** On EHRSHOT, the hard-label surrogate matches or exceeds CLMBR-T-Base, and the top-attributed tokens (heart rate, SBP/DBP, temperature, respiratory rate, oxygen saturation) coincide with NEWS2 clinical predictors, providing token-level evidence that FEMR predictions are clinically grounded.

## Methodology

**Setting & target model.** The FEMR under study is CLMBR-T-Base, a 141M-parameter autoregressive model pretrained on 2.57M de-identified longitudinal EHRs from Stanford Medicine's EHRSHOT dataset (the only FEMR with public weights). Patient trajectories are constructed under the OMOP-CDM schema as ordered sequences of events, each event comprising four channels: a medical code (SNOMED CT / LOINC / RxNorm), a numeric value (e.g., lab result), a text field, and a timestamp.

**Explainability pipeline (3 stages).**

1. **Collect input-output pairs.** For each prediction task a prediction time Tᵢ is fixed; the FEMR ingests the patient history before Tᵢ and emits both a binary hard label (0.5 threshold) and a continuous soft label. Both (input, output) pairs are recorded.
2. **Train a Transformer surrogate.** Each event token is encoded as eₜ = E_code(cₜ) + E_text(uₜ) + g(·)⊙(W_num·nₜ) + W_∆t·∆tₜ + E_pos(t), where ∆t is log-transformed/normalized time-to-prediction in hours. A 3-layer encoder (hidden 128, 4 heads, FFN 256, GELU, dropout 0.2, pre-norm, max length 2048, Adam lr 1e-4, batch 16, ≤100 epochs, early stopping on validation AUPRC) with a prepended [CLS] token produces a sequence-level prediction under either hard- or soft-label supervision. Soft labels are passed through a logit transform to expand compressed probability mass near 0/1.
3. **Token-level attribution + clinical alignment.** SHAP assigns per-token contribution scores to the surrogate's predictions. The Clinical Validated Events Ratio is then R = Σ_{fⱼ∈Fc} c(fⱼ;D) / Σ_{fⱼ∈F} c(fⱼ;D), where c counts token occurrences and Fc ⊆ F is the clinically validated subset (NEWS2 physiological parameters for ICU transfer; ICD-10 comorbidity/frailty codes + vitals for LOS).

**Tasks.** (1) Long length-of-stay (LOS): predict whether total LOS during a visit will be ≥7 days, predicted at 11:59pm on admission day. (2) ICU transfer: predict whether a patient will be transferred to the ICU, predicted at 11:59pm on admission day; same-day ICU transfers excluded.

## Results & Highlights

- **FEMR (CLMBR-T-Base) baseline performance.** On LOS (1,274 positives / 5,257 records, 24.2% prevalence) it achieves AUROC 0.7046, AUPRC 0.4173, precision 0.4556, recall 0.3140, F1 0.3717 — an AUPRC ~1.7× the prevalence, competitive with literature benchmarks of AUROC 0.72–0.77 / AUPRC 0.34–0.36. On the highly imbalanced ICU-transfer task (213 positives / 4,868 records, 4.4% prevalence) it achieves AUROC 0.7282, AUPRC 0.1592 (more than 3× the 4.4% prevalence), but only recall 0.0704 and F1 0.1149.
- **Surrogate matches or exceeds the FEMR under hard-label supervision (Table 5).** For LOS the hard-label surrogate reaches AUPRC 0.4434 (vs. 0.4173 for CLMBR-T-Base) and F1 0.4889 (vs. 0.3717). For ICU transfer the hard-label surrogate reaches AUPRC 0.2701 (vs. 0.1592) and F1 0.4000 (vs. 0.1149), a large jump driven by precision 1.0000 / recall 0.2500.
- **Soft-label supervision partially degenerates on the imbalanced task.** The ICU-transfer soft-label surrogate scores AUROC 0.9351 but precision 0.0000, recall 0.0000, F1 0.0000 — it predicts no positives at all — illustrating that soft-label imitation collapses under severe class imbalance. The authors therefore adopt the hard-label surrogate for downstream explainability.
- **Clinical Validated Events Ratio (Table 7).** R = 0.3093 for LOS (2,257 clinically validated events out of 7,296 total attributed) and R = 0.2717 for ICU transfer (1,985 / 7,305). The top-5 most frequent attributed codes account for 29% (LOS) and 24% (ICU) of all attributed events, meaning clinically validated features are concentrated among the highest-attribution tokens.
- **Top attributed tokens match NEWS2 vitals (Table 6).** For both tasks the top-5 tokens are heart rate (LOINC/8867-4: 806 occurrences LOS / 581 ICU), systolic blood pressure (LOINC/8480-6: 412 / 314), body temperature (LOINC/8310-5: 325 / 405), diastolic blood pressure (LOINC/8462-4: 308 / 272), and respiratory rate (LOINC/9279-1: 258) / oxygen saturation (LP21258-6: 214) — exactly the physiological parameters NEWS2 aggregates.
- **Feature-type attribution.** Across the four channels of each event, numeric values and the time-delta ∆t contributed most; text and code embeddings contributed least, because numeric values carry the patient's actual clinical state and ∆t encodes the temporal history.

## Authors & Credibility

The work comes from the School of Computing and Information Systems at the University of Melbourne (Jie Huang, Pengfei Yin as co-first authors, Zihan Xu, Mike Conway, corresponding author Ting Dang) with Daniel Capurro jointly affiliated with the Department of General Medicine at Royal Melbourne Hospital. The clinical-validated feature lists (Tables 2–3) are drawn from peer-reviewed medical-informatics literature (BMJ NEWS2, Lancet Healthy Longevity frailty scores, JBI/JAMIA LOS-prediction reviews), giving the clinical-alignment metric external grounding rather than ad hoc selection. They use the only publicly available FEMR (CLMBR-T-Base) on the public EHRSHOT benchmark, which materially aids reproducibility. The framing honestly positions the work as the *first* token-level XAI for FEMRs rather than as a new SOTA FEMR.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty is genuine but narrow.** Token-level surrogate + SHAP is well-trodden ground in NLP/LLM XAI; the novelty is transporting it to structured longitudinal EHRs and proposing the Clinical Validated Events Ratio. The "first" claim is defensible because FEMR XAI is a near-empty niche.
- **A surrogate that beats the model it explains is a red flag for fidelity.** On the LOS hard-label setting the surrogate *outperforms* CLMBR-T-Base (AUPRC 0.4434 vs. 0.4173; F1 0.4889 vs. 0.3717), and on ICU transfer it more than doubles AUPRC (0.2701 vs. 0.1592). If the surrogate is strictly better, it is not a faithful imitation of the FEMR — it is a different (better) model — so the SHAP tokens explain the *surrogate*, not necessarily the FEMR. The paper does not report surrogate→FEMR agreement metrics (e.g., prediction agreement rate, KL/fidelity curves, AUC of the surrogate-vs-FEMR decision match) that the XAI literature normally requires; this is a missing ablation.
- **Degenerate soft-label result on ICU transfer.** Precision 0 / recall 0 / F1 0 (AUROC 0.9351) for the soft-label ICU surrogate means it predicts the majority class only; reporting AUROC 0.9351 alongside this is misleading and the paper does not reconcile the gap beyond a one-sentence acknowledgment.
- **The alignment metric is partly circular.** R is computed against a hand-curated Fc chosen to be the clinically known predictors of the *same* tasks being explained; a model that merely learns "vitals matter" will score high by construction. There is no comparison against a random-token null or an Fc of *irrelevant* clinical codes to show R discriminates meaningful from spurious attribution.
- **No head-to-head XAI baselines.** The paper motivates against LIME, SHAP-on-FEMR-directly, Integrated Gradients, attention-rolling, and logistic-regression/decision-tree surrogates but never actually runs them; the claim that this approach is *better* than IG or a linear surrogate is asserted, not demonstrated.
- **Scale and generalization are thin.** One FEMR (forced, since only CLMBR-T-Base is public), one dataset (single-institution Stanford EHRSHOT), two binary tasks. The abstract claims a generic FEMR-XAI framework, but it is validated on exactly one model.
- **Internal data inconsistencies undermine confidence.** Section 4.1 states the cohorts are "4,868 and 5,275 longitudinal records for LOS and ICU transfer," but Section 5.1 reports LOS as 1,274 positives out of 5,257 and ICU as 213 positives out of 4,868 — the totals and the task↔size mapping are swapped/contradictory. Table 1's cohort-length distribution is also hard to reconcile with these counts.
- **Tokenization granularity is coarse.** Aggregating code/numeric/text/time into one event token is acknowledged as a limitation; numeric and time dominate attribution partly because they subsume the others, so the "text/code contributed least" finding may be an artifact of the token design rather than a property of the FEMR.

## Strengths

- First principled, end-to-end token-level XAI pipeline purpose-built for longitudinal structured EHR foundation models, filling a genuine literature gap.
- Time-aware event encoding (log-transformed time-to-prediction) and the gated numeric injection are sensible, lightweight, and well-justified for irregular EHR sequences.
- The Clinical Validated Events Ratio is a useful, clinician-legible scalar that converts SHAP into an auditable number — a practical contribution for clinical AI governance.
- Honest reporting of the degenerate soft-label ICU result and of the FEMR's weak ICU recall (0.0704), instead of hiding it behind AUROC.
- Use of the only public FEMR and the public EHRSHOT benchmark, with explicit model/training hyperparameters (3 layers, hidden 128, lr 1e-4, batch 16, max len 2048, early stopping on AUPRC), materially aids reproducibility.
- Clinically grounded: the Fc feature sets are sourced from validated instruments (NEWS2, Hospital Frailty Risk Score), so the alignment story has external medical credibility.

## Limitations

- **Surrogate–FEMR fidelity is not directly measured.** No fidelity/agreement curves; the surrogate actually exceeds the FEMR, weakening the "we explain the FEMR" claim.
- **Single FEMR, single institution, two binary tasks.** Generalization across FEMRs (ETHOS, Med-BERT), datasets, and continuous/multiclass outcomes is unverified.
- **Severe-class-imbalance failure.** Soft-label ICU surrogate collapses to all-negative predictions; the framework needs rebalancing or calibrated soft targets.
- **Circularity in the alignment metric.** R is evaluated against the same clinical features used to define the tasks; no negative/null Fc control.
- **No comparison to alternative XAI methods** (IG, LIME, attention-based, direct SHAP on the FEMR, linear/decision-tree surrogates) — the superiority claim is unsupported.
- **Coarse event tokenization** conflates feature channels and biases attribution toward numeric/time fields.
- **Cohort-size reporting is internally inconsistent** between Sections 4.1 and 5.1.
- **Sequence truncation.** Only the top-75% longest trajectories and a 2048 max length are used; behavior on the longest, most complex patients (up to 199,913 events) is unexamined.

## Relevance

该论文直接落在「医疗 AI 基础模型 + 可解释性」交叉点上，与你关注的纵向电子病历（longitudinal EHR）建模、临床预测（LOS / ICU 转移）以及可信医疗 AI 强相关：它给出了首个针对 FEMR 的 token 级解释框架与可量化的临床对齐指标，对评估和部署 FEMR 在真实临床中的可信度具有参考价值。其替代模型 + SHAP + 临床验证事件比例的思路，也可迁移到你关心的其它医疗时序基础模型的解释与偏差审计工作中。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What is the direct surrogate→FEMR fidelity (prediction agreement rate, fidelity-AUC, KL divergence between surrogate and FEMR output distributions), and does the hard-label surrogate that *exceeds* CLMBR-T-Base actually explain the FEMR or a better-but-different classifier?
- How does X-FEMR compare against attributions computed directly on the FEMR (e.g., IG, gradient SHAP, attention rollout) and against simpler transparent surrogates (logistic regression, decision tree, additive models) on the same R metric?
- Does the Clinical Validated Events Ratio R still discriminate when evaluated against an Fc of *clinically irrelevant* codes, or against randomly sampled token sets — i.e., is it a meaningful null-beating signal?
- Can the framework handle continuous/multiclass/regression outcomes and longer-than-2048 trajectories (EHRSHOT's max is 199,913 events) without truncation, e.g., via hierarchical or trajectory-tokenized encoders?
- How do explanations and R transfer across FEMRs (ETHOS, Med-BERT) and across institutions — are the same tokens (NEWS2 vitals) universally dominant, or do institution-specific practice patterns shift attribution?
- Why does the soft-label ICU surrogate collapse to majority-class predictions (precision/recall 0) while hard-label succeeds, and would focal/Tversky losses, calibrated probabilities, or temperature-scaled logits recover soft-label fidelity?
