Methodology

§ 1 Three retrieval modes over held-constant chunking

Three modes run over a proprietary structure-aware chunking that splits documents into clause-aligned nodes (not fixed-size spans). The corpus is 15 transactional legal agreements across credit facilities, LP agreements, and share/asset purchases (SEC EDGAR / Kentucky pension FOIA / UK filings). The scored benchmark uses 6 documents and 20 questions with verified reference answers — 18 document-bound plus 2 out-of-scope declination controls. Phases I–II tuned on a disjoint 15-question pool.

01 · inject
Full corpus placed in context every query, cache-discounted prefix. The expensive baseline.
02 · navembed
Pre-embed nodes, top-k cosine retrieval, optional cross-encoder rerank; only fetched nodes enter the answering context.
03 · navindex
Three-step LLM navigation: S1 structured scan of a compact index (boolean pre-filters → ≤10 node IDs), S2 deterministic verbatim fetch following cross-reference edges, S3 grounded answer.

Judging. Each question answered twice in parallel; a reference-anchored pairwise judge runs forward and reverse, awarding a win only when both directions agree (hedged = tie). Clopper–Pearson 95% CIs on every headline rate. Cost model. Token footprint (all attended input, governing long-context degradation) tracked separately from dollar cost (cache reads $0.30/M, writes $3.75/M, base input $3.00/M, output $15/M).

Critical assessment

§ 3 Strengths & limitations, side by side

+ STRENGTHS
What the paper does right
  • Two independent cost axes. Token footprint (structural, governs degradation) separated from dollar cost (cache-reshaped), yielding a concrete crossover threshold.
  • Rigorous judging. Forward/reverse with agreement-required wins, hedged-as-tie, reference-anchored criteria, forced-decision probe — directly tackling LLM-judge position bias.
  • Honest threats section. Names the missing ablations (descriptor-ladder rungs, forced-decision on Config 6), the Phase II phantom-tie artefact, and the narrow scale.
  • Failure-mode transparency. BM25 length bias, titles-only over-selection, uncapped nodes — all documented as would-not-ship, with dollar/token consequences.
  • Released artifacts. Question set, verified reference answers, full prompts, per-question ledgers enable partial external scrutiny.
! LIMITATIONS
Where the claim weakens
  • Single run, n=18–20. Wide CIs (RUN-012 ties [0.65, 0.99]); no variance estimate across runs.
  • Config 6 confound. Serialisation + question set + harness changed together — complicates attribution of the 18/18 headline.
  • Proprietary pipeline unreleased. Structure-aware chunking and Syntheia enrichment not open; navindex not fully reproducible.
  • No ground-truth node labels. Retrieval recall/precision unmeasurable; RUN-012's two losses diagnosed conjecturally.
  • Same provider answers & judges (Anthropic); cross-provider judge deferred — residual tie-inflation risk unmeasured.
  • Answer-aware phrasing may flatter retrieval vs. real issue-driven practitioner queries; says little about legal reasoning.
Open questions

§ 4 For further exploration

Q1
Does the navindex 18/18 tie survive a forced-decision run under the final reference-anchored protocol — genuine interchangeability or sub-judge-resolution differences?
Q2
How do the two navigate modes compare head-to-head (judged against each other, not each vs. inject), and on which question types does structural navigation beat embedding retrieval?
Q3
With ground-truth node labels, what is retrieval recall/precision — and do RUN-012's two inject wins stem from retrieval failure (ranked outside top-10) or reasoning failure?
Q4
Does the C < 10·R crossover hold across OpenAI / Google cache pricing, and how does the 5-minute TTL interact with asynchronous legal-review workflows days apart?
Q5
Climbing the descriptor ladder D1→D5 one rung at a time: is the boolean-flag pre-filter or the cross-reference graph the binding contributor to navindex's tie rate?
Q6
Do results transfer beyond Claude — to GPT-class or open answerers — and to corpora that exceed the context window, where inject ceases to be runnable at all?
