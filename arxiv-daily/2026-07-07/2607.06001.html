§ 01

Methodology — one study, frozen before first call

A single pre-registered study on Claude Opus 4.8, run under unusually strict discipline. Every prediction, acceptance band, and decision rule was committed to a public git chain before the runs it governs (a159ca9 → a7e639b); three amendments were each committed blind, before their runs; every API call is cached in SQLite keyed by full request, so the analysis is deterministic and re-runnable offline at zero cost.

Experiment A — coupled capacity region. World state X is three fair bits (8 states, H(X)=3 ln 2). Each agent observes a signal, reports a posterior, and bets wealth parimutuel-style; its claimed information Î̂a is the expected KL of reports from the prior, and per-round log growth is Ĝa=E[ln(ba(X)/B(X))]. Five structures tested: disjoint bits, overlap, clones, 10%-noisy sensors, and an XOR synergy control (two individually worthless signals that jointly fix the world). A live 40-round parimutuel market tests wealth selection across 5 seeds.

Experiment B — residual-scaling grid. N=20 agents occupy niches on a tent landscape with reward gradient g, control bonus γ, and a coordination dividend. The mean-field law under test predicts residual ∝ V·g/γ in a noise-maintained-dispersion regime. A 3×3 grid × 8 seeds (72 runs) probes the response surface, with niche labels shown under each agent's own random permutation so token-level label bias cancels. A cheap responsiveness capability gate ($8.76, passed 3/3) licenses the expensive grid — after two earlier small-model arms had already failed the same prediction.

§ 03

Strengths & limitations, side by side

✓ Strengths

  • Pre-registration with frozen, mechanically applied acceptance bands — rare in ML.
  • Full reproducibility at ~$0: cached SQLite, committed raw outputs, deterministic offline analyzer, public commit chain.
  • Equal billing for the positive (capacity-region) and negative (attractor) results.
  • Mechanistic explanations for every deviation rather than hand-waving.
  • Transferable capability-gate pattern: a cheap gate licenses the expensive grid.
  • XOR control — agents reasoned parity from prose, hitting 90% of an ideal information bit.

✗ Limitations

  • Single vendor / model family / tier (Claude Opus 4.8 only); cross-vendor replication is the open arm.
  • Toy scale: N=12–20, T=8–18, 8-state worlds; no take, strategic sharding, or entry/exit.
  • Unincentivized elicitation — agents not paid for calibration; a self-consistency gap remains.
  • The negative is a Cap (domain not found), not a falsification; a modified design could revive the question.
  • P4b clone equality genuinely fails; P6 is formally INCONCLUSIVE via a pre-registration drafting gap.
  • Niche-economy prompt-γ calibration absorbed into the fit constant; J=1 dividend adds a ±1–3 niche systematic.
§ 04

Open questions for further work

Q1

Does the capacity-region confirmation replicate on a different frontier family (GPT- / Gemini-class) and tier, under the unchanged protocol and analyzer?

Q2

Would fractional Kelly or wealth floors in the live market restore clone equality (P4b) without introducing a new artifact — and does the gap law survive?

Q3

Can a deliberately modified niche design (explicit exploration incentives, heterogeneous objectives, stochastic payouts) sustain V ≫ 0 and enter the mean-field regime?

Q4

Is the bistability robust at deployment scale (N ⋙ 20, longer horizons, non-uniform resources), or does it dissolve into a smoother surface?

Q5

How does the fact that joint posteriors deviate from singleton products (max 0.466 nats) propagate into independence-assuming belief-aggregation schemes?

Q6

Can “an intervention either crosses an attractor boundary or does nothing measurable” be operationalized into a basin-based protocol for evaluating multi-agent-safety interventions?
