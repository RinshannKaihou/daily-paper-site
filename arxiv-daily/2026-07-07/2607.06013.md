> **一句话总结：** 本文证明了对可分数据上的加权指数损失，记忆型稳定退火热光滑符号下降法（ε_t = ε_0 exp(-κ S_t)）的归一化迭代点收敛到 Burg 型凸障碍在间隔切片上的极小点 u_κ，其中退火速率 κ 恰好充当间隔约束，使该路径在 κ↓0 时退化为 ℓ2 最大间隔解、κ↑γ∞ 时趋向 ℓ∞ 最大间隔解，并通过精确的 entropic mirror ascent 与 KL 递归给出显式 S_t^{-1/2} 包络。

> **TL;DR:** For full-batch linear classification on separable data with weighted exponential loss, the paper proves that memoryless stability-annealed smoothed-sign descent (ε_t = ε_0 exp(-κ S_t)) converges, after normalization by cumulative stepsize S_t, to the minimizer u_κ of a convex Burg-type barrier over the margin slice {u : z_i^T u ≥ κ}; the rate κ interpolates the ℓ2 hard-margin separator (κ→0) and the ℓ∞ max-margin separator (κ→γ∞), and on the synthetic theorem-compatible suite the exact dual algebra residuals reach floating-point scale (max r_q = 3.5e-13) while Adam/RMSProp transfer is empirically rejected (mean path error 0.8443 for Adam vs. 0.0886 for smoothed-sign descent).

## 核心贡献

- **速率索引的隐式偏差定理**：首次为稳定退火的 smoothed-sign 动力学给出精确的速率索引隐式偏差结论。退火速率 0 < κ < γ∞ 不再是超参数，而是作为间隔约束直接进入所选中分隔器的定义，给出介于 ℓ2 与 ℓ∞ 端点之间的"中间情形"答案。
- **Burg 型障碍几何的完整刻画**：识别极限分隔器为障碍 B(u) = Σ_j [-|u_j| - log(1-|u_j|)] 在间隔切片 {Zu ≥ κ1} 上的唯一极小点 u_κ；证明良定性、KKT 条件，并刻画两端点极限（κ↓0 时 u_κ/κ → v_2，κ↑γ∞ 时 u_κ → U_∞）。
- **无启发式假设的精确证明**：通过将动力学精确改写为凹对偶目标上的 entropic mirror ascent（λ_{t+1} = λ_t ⊙ exp(η_t ∇D(λ_t))），并用 KL 递归控制对偶间隙，得到显式 S_t^{-1/2} 归一化迭代包络。证明不假定活跃集稳定性、归一化迭代有界、严格互补、对偶乘子唯一或 generic-position 数据。
- **诚实的范围界定与边界诊断**：数值实验在浮点误差尺度验证精确对偶恒等式，并明确区分已证定理与经验诊断——logistic 尾、固定 ε 交叉、Adam/RMSProp 转移均作为边界诊断而非定理结论，且如实报告 Adam 转移残差不消失。

## Key Contributions

- **Rate-indexed implicit-bias theorem**: Gives the first exact rate-indexed implicit-bias result for stability-annealed smoothed-sign descent. The annealing rate 0 < κ < γ∞ acts as a margin constraint in the selected separator, providing a "middle case" between the sign-like and gradient-descent-like endpoints.
- **Full characterization of Burg-type barrier geometry**: Identifies the limiting separator as the unique minimizer u_κ of B(u) = Σ_j [-|u_j| - log(1-|u_j|)] over the margin slice {u : z_i^T u ≥ κ}; proves well-posedness, KKT conditions, and both endpoint limits (u_κ/κ → v_2 as κ↓0; u_κ → U_∞ as κ↑γ∞).
- **Heuristic-free exact proof via entropic mirror ascent**: Rewrites the dynamics exactly as entropic mirror ascent on a concave dual objective D(λ) = κ1^T λ - Φ*(Z^T λ), controls the dual gap by a KL recursion, and obtains an explicit S_t^{-1/2} normalized-iterate envelope — without assuming active-set stability, bounded normalized iterates, strict complementarity, dual uniqueness, or generic-position data.
- **Honest scope delineation with boundary diagnostics**: Numerical experiments validate exact dual identities to floating-point error, and clearly separate the proved theorem from empirical diagnostics — logistic tails, fixed-ε crossover, and Adam/RMSProp transfer are reported as boundary diagnostics, with the Adam transfer residual honestly reported as non-vanishing.

## Methodology

The setting is full-batch linear classification on separable data with signed examples z_i = y_i x_i, separability γ∞ = max_{‖u‖∞≤1} min_i z_i^T u > 0. The central object is the memoryless stability-annealed smoothed-sign update

  w_{t+1} = w_t + η_t · (-∇L(w_t)) / (|∇L(w_t)| + ε_t),   ε_t = ε_0 exp(-κ S_t),   S_t = Σ_{s<t} η_s,

with weighted exponential loss L(w) = Σ_i a_i e^{-z_i^T w}, stepsize conditions η_t > 0, S_t → ∞, Σ_t η_t^2 < ∞, and η_t G ≤ 1 where G = max_i (κ + ‖z_i‖_1).

**Static barrier program.** The candidate separator at rate κ is u_κ = argmin_{u∈(-1,1)^d} B(u) s.t. z_i^T u ≥ κ ∀i, where B(u) = Σ_j [-|u_j| - log(1-|u_j|)] is a Burg-type barrier that is 1-strongly convex on the open cube. Proposition 1 establishes uniqueness, that γ(u_κ) = κ (at least one margin constraint active), and KKT conditions ∇B(u_κ) = Z^T λ. Proposition 2 gives the endpoint geometry: u_κ/κ → v_2 (the ℓ2 hard-margin separator) as κ ↓ 0 via the expansion B(κv) = (κ²/2)‖v‖² + O(κ³); and dist(u_κ, U_∞) → 0 as κ ↑ γ∞.

**Dynamic proof via exact mirror ascent.** The key change of variables λ_{i,t} = a_i exp(κ S_t - z_i^T w_t) yields the exact identities e^{κ S_t}(-∇L(w_t)) = Z^T λ_t =: q_t and d_t = ∇Φ*(q_t) (with Φ = ε_0 B), and the update becomes λ_{t+1} = λ_t ⊙ exp(η_t ∇D(λ_t)) — entropic mirror ascent on the nonnegative orthant. A one-step KL identity V_{t+1} - V_t = η_t⟨λ_t - λ*, ∇D(λ_t)⟩ + Σ_i λ_{i,t}[e^{η_t g_{i,t}} - 1 - η_t g_{i,t}] combined with concavity (⟨λ_t - λ*, g_t⟩ ≤ -Δ_t), the bound ‖∇D(λ)‖∞ ≤ G, and the inequality e^x - 1 - x ≤ (e/2)x² give V_{t+1} - V_t ≤ -η_t Δ_t + (e/2)η_t² G² ‖λ_t‖_1. Noncompact dual-mass control ‖λ‖_1 ≤ 2 D_h(λ*, λ) + 2 log 2 · ‖λ*‖_1 and discrete Gronwall yield sup_t V_t < ∞ and Σ_{s<t} η_s Δ_s ≤ C_D. KKT/Fenchel equality then gives Δ_t = D_{Φ*}(q_t, q_κ) + λ_t^T(Z u_κ - κ1), which controls ‖d_t - u_κ‖² ≤ C_Φ Δ_t. Jensen yields the final envelope ‖w_t/S_t - u_κ‖ ≤ ‖w_0‖_2/(2 S_t) + sqrt(C_Φ C_D)/S_t. A spike-exclusion lemma upgrades averaged control to last-iterate convergence Δ_t → 0.

**Boundary diagnostics.** A perturbation lemma shows Adam/RMSProp would inherit the limit only if the averaged direction residual (1/S_t)Σ_{s<t} η_s ‖e_s‖ → 0; the prespecified E3 comparison measured this and the path error to the same u_κ. Fixed-ε crossover τ_ε(c) = min{t : ‖-∇L(w_t)‖∞ ≤ c ε} is analyzed via a conditional two-sided tail bound argument, yielding S_{τ_ε}(c) = (1/γ∞) log(1/ε) + O(1) conditionally (the full two-sided proof remains open).

## Results & Highlights

- **Theorem validation breadth**: The primary run completed all 3200 dynamic trajectories and obtained 2716 successful barrier targets (484 records retained as numerical barrier-boundary cases and excluded from theorem-evidence aggregation). On the synthetic theorem-compatible suite (d ∈ {10, 25, 50, 100}, n = 4d, κ/γ∞ ∈ {0.10, 0.25, 0.50, 0.75, 0.90}), the exact dual-transformation residuals sit at floating-point scale: max r_q = 3.5e-13, max relative r_q = 6.7e-16, max r_λ = 4.7e-15 — directly validating the mirror-ascent identity (22).
- **Long-horizon showcase**: A separate run attempted 90 theorem-compatible configurations, retained one failed barrier solve, and used 89 successful targets. The saved horizon extends the maximum cumulative time from S_t = 1.09 in the primary grid to S_t = 4.38, with final/first diagnostic factors continuing to point in the theorem-predicted direction (median improvement factors 0.43, 0.19, 0.07 for E_pc, E_d, E_q respectively, all below 1 indicating finite-window progress).
- **Fixed-ε crossover scaling**: Thresholds were reached in 99.23% of saved transition records. Across all ε values and c = 1, mean slope ratios ρ = b_1 γ∞ were 0.994, 0.987, and 0.985 for learning-rate powers a = 0.25, 0.50, 0.75 (with subgroup confidence intervals centered near one), empirically supporting the law S_{τ_ε} = (1/γ∞) log(1/ε) + O(1). Changing c primarily shifted the intercept in the direction predicted by -log c.
- **Adam/RMSProp transfer rejected**: Mean final path error to u_κ was 0.0886 for smoothed-sign descent versus 0.8432 for RMSProp and 0.8443 for Adam; mean weighted transfer residuals were 3.8404 (RMSProp) and 3.8668 (Adam). Even the least-bad tested variants had path errors 0.4516 (RMSProp, β2 = 0.9) and 0.4226 (Adam, β1 = 0.9, β2 = 0.9, bias-corrected) — roughly 5-10x worse than the theorem method, so the perturbation-lemma transfer condition (38) was not satisfied and no Adam theorem is claimed.
- **Robustness classification (Table 1)**: Across finite-horizon sweeps, 12/12 loss runs, 9/9 schedule runs, 18/18 ε_0 runs, 12/12 support-vector runs, and 2/2 high-dimension runs were classified "within assumptions"; all 16 boundary-pathology runs fell "outside" (mean error 0.106). No numerical failure or genuine counterexample was observed in the prespecified grid.

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are from the University of Hong Kong (Xiangwu Wang, Ran Bi, Peilin Yu), UC San Diego (Chengwei Cao), and Beijing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics (Yicheng Song). The work engages directly with the canonical implicit-bias literature (Soudry et al. 2018; Nacson, Srebro, and Soudry 2019; Lyu and Li 2020; Gunasekar et al. 2018; Ji and Telgarsky 2019) and the closest prior work Wang and Klabjan (UAI 2025), explicitly distinguishing the rate-indexed separable-classification theorem from the fixed-ε regression mirror-descent view. The proof uses standard convex-duality and Bregman tools (Rockafellar 1970; Bregman 1967; Beck and Teboulle 2003). The experimental protocol is unusually disciplined: float64 arithmetic, deterministic seeds, configuration hashes, solver-status logging, KKT residual reporting, and explicit retention (not silent filtering) of solver failures.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (genuine)**: The rate-indexed selector is a real conceptual contribution. Treating the exponential decay rate κ as a margin constraint that interpolates the ℓ2 endpoint (u_κ/κ → v_2) and the ℓ∞ endpoint (u_κ → U_∞) is new, and is cleanly distinguished from Wang and Klabjan (2025), which handles fixed-ε regression rather than annealed-κ separable classification. The Burg-barrier identification is a non-obvious consequence of the coordinatewise smoothing map.
- **Rigor (strong)**: The proof is exact rather than asymptotic — the dynamics is rewritten with no approximation as entropic mirror ascent, and the KL recursion yields a concrete S_t^{-1/2} envelope with explicit constants C_Φ = 2ϵ_0/(ϵ_0 + Q)² and C_D. Crucially, the proof avoids the usual heuristic assumptions (active-set stability, bounded normalized iterates, strict complementarity, dual uniqueness, generic position); the spike-exclusion lemma handles the last-iterate upgrade honestly.
- **Reproducibility (excellent)**: float64, deterministic seeds, configuration hashes, solver-status logging, KKT residuals, and documented conventions for all public numerical routines. The 484/3200 barrier-solve failures are retained and reported rather than hidden. However, no code release is explicitly mentioned in the main text.
- **Significance (moderate, theory-focused)**: A clean theoretical result for the implicit-bias literature, but the scope is narrow — only weighted exponential loss, only the memoryless proxy, only synthetic separable linear data. The connection to practical Adam is explicitly negative (transfer fails), so the title's resonance with practitioners is limited.
- **Red flags / honest weaknesses**:
  - The critical case κ = γ∞ is excluded from the open-domain path, and the endpoint regimes κ = 0 and κ > γ∞ are not in the theorem — the "interpolation" is therefore proved only on the open interval.
  - The fixed-ε crossover is only a conditional implication; the required two-sided uniform tail bound (83) is open, and the stronger O(log log(1/ε)) secondary target is not proved. The 0.994/0.987/0.985 slope ratios are empirical, not theorem-backed.
  - The Adam/RMSProp boundary diagnostics are the weakest part relative to the title's motivation: the broad transfer target (38) is not supported, and the paper is commendably explicit that "Adam remains a documented empirical boundary case, not part of the main claim or title." A reader expecting an Adam theorem will be disappointed.
  - Experiments are purely synthetic (n = 4d, d up to 100); the two-dimensional dataset in Figure 1 is explicitly "illustrative only and excluded from aggregate statistics." No real datasets, no neural networks — the authors state "they are not large-scale benchmarks."
  - The 484 failed barrier solves (15% of primary records) are honestly reported but indicate the numerical barrier program is non-trivial to solve at the boundary; the open-domain proxy |u_j| ≤ 1 - δ with δ = 10⁻⁷ is a numerical workaround, not the theoretical object.

## Strengths

- Mathematically exact: the dual mirror-ascent identity is verified to floating-point error (max r_q = 3.5e-13), which is rare and convincing.
- Conceptually clean: κ-as-margin-constraint gives a principled interpolation between two well-known margin geometries, with both endpoint limits proved.
- Proof discipline: no reliance on generic-position, strict-complementarity, or active-set-stability assumptions that often quietly underpin implicit-bias proofs.
- Intellectual honesty: the paper repeatedly separates proved claims (weighted exponential loss, memoryless proxy, 0 < κ < γ∞) from empirical diagnostics (logistic tails, fixed-ε crossover, Adam transfer), and explicitly states where proofs are missing.
- Comprehensive ablations: schedule powers, ε_0 values, data families, dimensions, support-vector counts, rescaling, and boundary pathologies are all swept, with failures classified rather than discarded.

## Limitations

- Theorem covers only weighted exponential loss; logistic loss is an empirical robustness check with no perturbation theorem.
- Only the memoryless smoothed-sign proxy is proved; Adam/RMSProp transfer is empirically rejected under the tested grid (though no impossibility theorem is claimed for all adaptive variants).
- Only synthetic separable linear-classification instances; no real data, no neural networks, no large-scale benchmarks.
- The fixed-ε crossover law S_{τ_ε} = (1/γ∞) log(1/ε) + O(1) is only conditionally proved — the two-sided tail bound and leading-coordinate-change control remain open.
- The critical case κ = γ∞ and the endpoint regimes κ = 0, κ > γ∞ are excluded from the theorem.
- The S_t^{-1/2} envelope is not claimed to be sharp; the authors explicitly state "We do not claim that the empirical rate is S_t^{-1/2} or that the proof constants are sharp."
- No explicit code release is mentioned in the main text, though the artifact package (saved trajectories, metadata sidecars) is described.

## Relevance

该论文属于深度学习优化理论/隐式偏差方向，精确刻画了符号型与自适应方法在可分数据上的间隔几何选择机制，并对 smoothed-sign 与 Adam 类方法的差异给出严格区分。对于关注优化算法隐式偏差、margin 最大化理论及自适应方法理论分析的研究者具有直接参考价值，但其结论局限于合成可分线性分类与加权指数损失，与实际深度网络训练的联系仍待后续工作建立。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the theorem be extended to logistic loss via a perturbation argument, and would the same Burg-barrier selector survive under a logistic tail with a modified rate index?
- Is the critical case κ = γ∞ genuinely singular, or is there a well-defined limit separator (and a distinct barrier geometry) that closes the open interval?
- Can the open two-sided tail bound (83) for fixed-ε crossover be proved, and does the stronger O(log log(1/ε)) secondary target hold?
- Under what narrower conditions on β_1, β_2, or the schedule could Adam/RMSProp satisfy the averaged transfer residual (38) and inherit the smoothed-sign limit?
- Does the rate-indexed Burg-barrier selector extend beyond linear classification to homogeneous or wide neural networks, where margin-geometry implicit-bias results are known to depend on parameterization?
- Are the proof constants C_Φ, C_D sharp, and can the S_t^{-1/2} envelope be tightened to match the empirically observed convergence rate?
