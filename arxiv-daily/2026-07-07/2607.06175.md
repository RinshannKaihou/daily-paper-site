> **一句话总结：** 本文系统研究了基于强化学习（GSPO）的 BPMN 流程模型生成中奖励函数设计的影响，在 Llama 3.1 8B 和 Qwen 2.5 14B 上跑了 48 组配置，发现等权奖励始终优于针对单一维度加权（加权反而会触发"策略坍塌"），且 RL 相比纯 SFT 在 pragmatic 质量上提升超 0.11 分并将输出方差缩小约 6.5 倍。

> **TL;DR:** A systematic study of reward-function design for RL-based BPMN generation that trains Llama 3.1 8B and Qwen 2.5 14B under 48 GSPO configurations with rewards from the 38-metric BEF4LLM framework; on a 105-sample held-out set, equal-weight rewards (R1) beat the SFT-only baseline on pragmatic quality for both models (Llama +0.139, Qwen +0.116, p<10⁻⁴) while targeted 3:1:1 weighting collapses Llama's syntactic quality by 0.264 points, and the invalidity penalty/SFT-initialization effects turn out to be strongly architecture-dependent.

## 核心贡献

- 提出一套基于 GSPO 的训练框架，将 BEF4LLM 质量评估框架的 38 个指标（横跨语法/语用/语义三个维度）组合为可验证的奖励信号，可应用于 SFT 初始化后或直接施加于未适配 BPM 的指令微调基座模型。
- 首次系统性地对结构化生成任务中的多维奖励函数设计进行实证研究：覆盖维度加权（等权 vs. 3:1:1 目标加权）、无效输出惩罚（−1 vs. 0）、两种数学等价实现路径的对比，共 48 组配置，在 105 个流程描述上评测。
- 揭示了奖励设计、模型架构与初始化策略之间存在非平凡交互：无效惩罚对 Qwen 必不可少但对 Llama 形同虚设；SFT 初始化对 Llama 不可或缺，对 Qwen 反而压制了语义质量。
- 给出实用工程指南：等权是稳健默认值、惩罚强度应与基座模型的有效性表现相匹配、是否需要 SFT 应由基座先验能力决定，并指出"奖励设计的影响可与是否使用 RL 本身一样大"。

## Key Contributions

- A GSPO-based training framework that turns BEF4LLM's 38 automated metrics (syntactic/pragmatic/semantic) into verifiable, multi-dimensional reward signals usable either after SFT or directly on BPM-unadapted instruction-tuned base models.
- The first systematic empirical investigation of multi-dimensional reward-function design for structured generation, sweeping 48 configurations (6 reward functions × 2 initialization strategies × 2 grammar settings × 2 LLM families) evaluated on 105 held-out process descriptions with paired permutation tests and Bonferroni correction.
- An interaction analysis showing reward design, model architecture, and initialization are not independently tunable: the invalidity penalty is essential for Qwen but inert for Llama; SFT initialization is indispensable for Llama but counterproductive for Qwen's semantic quality.
- Practical guidelines: equal weighting is the most robust default, penalty regime should match the base model's validity behavior, and SFT initialization should be informed by the base model's task-relevant prior capabilities.

## Methodology

The task is text-to-model generation: an LLM policy π_θ maps a prompt c=(s,x) (system instruction + natural-language process description) to a raw output ỹ, which is parsed into a compact JSON-based intermediate model y and translated by a deterministic function φ into BPMN XML. Outputs that fail parsing, translation, schema conformance, or that have fewer than two flow objects are deemed invalid (v(ỹ)=0).

**Training pipeline.** Two stages, both using LoRA in bfloat16. Stage 1 is SFT (LLaMA-Factory, LoRA rank 8, lr 1e-4, 3 epochs) on 1,552 bilingual (German/English) samples. Stage 2 is RL with Group Sequence Policy Optimization (GSPO) via Unsloth (LoRA rank 16, lr 5e-5, 1 epoch, K=4 candidates per prompt, temperature 0.7, top-p 0.95). GSPO is chosen over PPO/GRPO because its sequence-level, group-relative ranking aligns with holistic model-level quality metrics and avoids token-level credit assignment problems in long structured outputs. Crucially, SFT is treated as an experimental variable: GSPO is applied either to the SFT-initialized model or directly to the BPM-unadapted instruction-tuned base.

**Reward design.** Reward signals come from the BEF4LLM framework: syntactic quality r_syn (16 rule-based metrics, e.g., matching split/join gateways, exactly one start/end event), pragmatic quality r_pra (15 complexity metrics with literature-validated thresholds), and semantic quality r_sem (7 similarity metrics vs. a ground-truth reference — label, graph-structure, and behavioral similarity). All three are bounded in [0,1]. The unified reward is R(w,p;ỹ) = w_syn·r_syn + w_pra·r_pra + w_sem·r_sem for valid outputs and p∈{−1,0} for invalid outputs. Six reward functions are defined: R_avg and R_1 (both equal 1/3:1/3:1/3, p=−1, but separate implementation pipelines), R_2 (equal, p=0), R_3 (3:1:1 syntax-emphasized), R_4 (1:3:1 pragmatic-emphasized), R_5 (1:1:3 semantic-emphasized).

**Additional factor.** Grammar-constrained decoding (EBNF) at inference time is studied as an orthogonal factor. Evaluation is on 105 held-out manually-verified text/process-model pairs, one generation per sample, with paired permutation tests (99,999 resamples, Bonferroni-corrected) on the subset where both compared configs produce valid output.

## Results & Highlights

- **RQ1 — GSPO beats SFT-only (Llama 3.1 8B):** Syntactic quality rises from 0.824 (SFT-only, n=36) to 0.926 (R1, n=102), Δ=+0.092, p_adj<10⁻⁴; pragmatic quality from 0.794 to 0.934, Δ=+0.139, p_adj<10⁻⁴; semantic from 0.561 to 0.594, Δ=+0.030, p_adj=0.018. Validity jumps from 36 to 102 valid outputs out of 105.
- **RQ1 — Output variability collapses:** Pragmatic-quality standard deviation drops from 0.085 (SFT-only) to 0.013 (R1), a ~6.5× reduction — RL-trained models produce dramatically more consistent outputs.
- **RQ1 — Qwen 2.5 14B:** Pragmatic quality improves 0.807→0.923 (Δ=+0.116, p_adj<10⁻⁴); syntactic +0.032 (p_adj=0.034); semantic essentially unchanged (−0.018, n.s.), confirming structural/comprehensibility gains without sacrificing fidelity.
- **RQ2a — Targeted weighting backfires (Llama):** R3 (syntax-emphasized, 3:1:1) achieves syntactic quality of only 0.662 — a drop of 0.264 points vs. R1's 0.926 (p_adj<10⁻³) — despite syntax receiving 60% of the reward weight. The model undergoes "policy collapse," producing mostly rule-violating models. R5 (semantic-emphasized) also *lowers* semantic quality.
- **RQ2b — Invalidity penalty is architecture-dependent:** For Llama, R1 (p=−1) vs. R2 (p=0) is statistically indistinguishable on all dimensions. For Qwen, removing the penalty collapses syntactic quality to 0.750 (std=0.002, essentially a single template) while inflating pragmatic to 0.964 — the penalty acts as an implicit diversity regularizer preventing template convergence.
- **RQ3 — SFT initialization is architecture-dependent:** For Llama, SFT base beats BPM-unadapted base by Δ=+0.262 syntactic (p_adj<10⁻⁴), one of the two largest effects in the study. For Qwen, SFT gives no significant syntactic/pragmatic advantage and the BPM-unadapted base actually achieves *higher* semantic quality (0.601 vs. 0.567, Δ=−0.032 favoring the untrained base, p_adj<10⁻⁴).
- **Curiosity:** The BPM-unadapted Qwen base under R5 (semantic-weighted) achieves the highest syntactic quality in the entire study (0.928), exceeding all SFT-based configurations.

The paper is submitted as a preprint to Elsevier and follows a rigorous statistical methodology (paired permutation tests with 99,999 resamples and Bonferroni correction), which exceeds the rigor typical of RL-for-LLM empirical studies.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core novelty is not "RL improves BPMN generation" (Berti et al. [3] already showed that with GRPO+POWL) but the *systematic dissection of reward composition* — weighting, penalty, and initialization interactions. The counter-intuitive finding that equal weighting strictly dominates targeted weighting, and the discovery of penalty-driven template collapse, are genuinely new contributions to multi-objective RLVR.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Pre-registered-style research questions, 48 configurations, non-parametric paired permutation tests with Bonferroni correction, explicit reporting of validity counts alongside conditional quality scores, and honest treatment of the mathematically-equivalent R_avg vs. R_1 discrepancy (they find a Δ=+0.059 syntactic difference for Llama and explicitly flag it as preliminary, possibly an implementation artifact).
- **Reproducibility:** Good in principle — code released, hyperparameters fully tabulated (LoRA rank, lr, K, temperature, epochs), datasets described. However, the training set is "assembled from multiple publicly available BPMN datasets, supplemented with synthetically generated samples" without a precise manifest, and the bilingual mix is not quantified, which would hinder exact replication.
- **Significance:** The "equal weighting beats targeted weighting" and "penalty as implicit diversity regularizer" findings generalize beyond BPMN to any structured-generation RLVR task with multi-dimensional rewards (UML, DB schemas, code). The architecture-dependence of SFT initialization is an actionable insight for practitioners.
- **Red flags / weaknesses:**
  - **Evaluation scale is small:** only 105 held-out samples with a single generation per sample. This admits no within-configuration variance estimate and limits sensitivity to small effects — the authors acknowledge this. Several headline comparisons (e.g., R5 for Llama with only n=38 valid) rest on thin data.
  - **Conditional-on-validity scoring is a confound:** because quality scores are means over *valid* outputs only, a config that produces fewer-but-better valid outputs (e.g., SFT-only Llama with n=36) is not directly comparable to one with n=102. The authors report validity counts and flag this, but the framing of "GSPO improves quality" partly conflates quality with validity.
  - **Conflated factors:** the two LLMs differ in both architecture *and* scale (8B vs. 14B), so architecture-vs-scale attributions (e.g., "Qwen's richer landscape") cannot be disentangled — the authors admit this.
  - **Fixed 3:1:1 weighting:** the total failure of R3–R5 for Llama does not rule out gentler imbalances (e.g., 2:1:1); the conclusion "equal weighting is best" is stronger than the evidence supports.
  - **Semantic quality is reference-based:** it penalizes structurally novel-but-valid alternatives, which could systematically disadvantage more creative policies (notably the BPM-unadapted Qwen base).
  - **Single-epoch GSPO with LoRA** mitigates but does not test for reward overfitting or catastrophic forgetting.

## Strengths

- Genuinely systematic experimental design: 48 configs, two model families, full cross-product of weighting × penalty × initialization × grammar, with proper multiple-comparison correction.
- Surprising, well-evidenced findings (equal-weight dominance, penalty-as-diversity-regularizer, SFT-hurts-Qwen-semantics) that contradict naive practitioner intuitions and are backed by distributional violin/radar analyses.
- Intellectual honesty: reports the R_avg vs. R_1 anomaly, flags low-validity comparisons, lists concrete limitations, and avoids overclaiming semantic-quality gains.
- Strong methodological fit rationale: GSPO's sequence-level, group-relative ranking is well-motivated for holistic model-quality rewards where token-level credit assignment is hopeless.
- Public code release and full hyperparameter tables support reproducibility.

## Limitations

- Small evaluation set (105 samples, single generation) limits statistical power and prevents variance estimation.
- Architecture and scale are confounded (Llama 8B vs. Qwen 14B), so "architecture-dependent" claims cannot separate model-family from size effects.
- Only one weighting ratio (3:1:1) tested; the generality of "equal weighting wins" is under-explored.
- Conditional-on-validity scoring entangles quality with validity rate.
- Reference-based semantic quality may undervalue structurally novel valid models.
- Single-epoch GSPO with LoRA does not explicitly test for reward overfitting or catastrophic forgetting.
- Training set composition (bilingual mix, synthetic supplementation) is not fully specified.
- Generalization beyond BPMN, beyond these two model families, and to larger models is claimed but untested.

## Relevance

对于关注大模型强化学习微调（RLVR / GRPO / GSPO）、奖励函数工程以及结构化输出生成的研究方向，本文提供了一个高质量的多维奖励设计实证范例：它直接回答了"多维奖励该如何加权""SFT 初始化是否必要"等工程核心问题，其关于等权优于目标加权、惩罚作为隐式多样性正则的结论可迁移到代码生成、知识图谱、数据库 schema 等任意结构化生成任务，对推进 RLHF/RLVR 的奖励设计方法论具有较高参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would gentler weight imbalances (e.g., 2:1:1 or adaptive/curriculum-based weighting that adjusts per-dimension weights over training steps) avoid the policy collapse seen with 3:1:1, or is equal weighting genuinely optimal?
- Is the SFT-hurts-Qwen-semantic-quality effect stable across seeds and larger Qwen models, or is it an artifact of the single-generation, single-epoch protocol?
- Can embedding-based or LLM-as-judge semantic rewards provide the "optimization accessibility" that metric-based semantic rewards lack, finally moving the semantic-quality needle?
- Does the penalty-as-diversity-regularizer finding generalize to other structured tasks (UML, DB schemas, code), and can an explicit diversity term replace the indirect penalty mechanism?
- How much of the architecture-dependent behavior is actually scale rather than architecture, if Llama and Qwen are compared at matched parameter counts?
- What happens under multi-epoch GSPO with KL regularization — do the gains compound or does reward hacking/template collapse eventually emerge even for equal-weight rewards?
