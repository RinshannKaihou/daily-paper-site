> **一句话总结：** 作者发现 LLM agent 的"注定失败"在其第 1–2 轮交互时即可由隐藏状态上的轻量线性探针提前预测（远早于行为打分器在第 3–4 轮才变得有效），并据此构建一个分布无关、逐轮校准的中止级联，在保证全局成功召回率的前提下，于 TextCraft 上为 Qwen-2.5-7B / Llama-3.2-3B 节省 47.1% / 37.2% 的推理计算量，约为最佳单门策略的 1.6–1.7 倍。

> **TL;DR:** The authors show that eventual failure of LLM agent episodes is linearly decodable from the agent's hidden activations as early as round 1 (where behavior-only scorers are near chance), and turn this into a distribution-free, recall-controlled abort cascade of Clopper–Pearson calibrated gates with jointly searched per-round recall budgets; on TextCraft with Qwen-2.5-7B and Llama-3.2-3B, the cascade saves up to 47.1% of inference compute at a 90% global success-recall target (1.6–1.7× the best single-gate baseline), and they characterize the sample complexity of certifying stricter recall targets (their data caps certified targets near 0.974).

## 核心贡献

- **失败信号在内部状态中提前出现**：在两个 agent 模型（Llama-3.2-3B、Qwen-2.5-7B）上，残差流隐藏状态上的轻量线性探针在第 1 轮即可达到甚至超过行为打分器在第 3–4 轮才达到的峰值 AUC；在第 2 轮达到自身峰值，对行为打分器的绝对优势达 0.12–0.21 AUC——恰好在大部分计算量尚未花费之时。
- **首个跨多轮序贯决策点的中止策略，控制 episode 级成功召回率**：每个 gate 使用 Clopper–Pearson 精确二项下置信界给出分布无关的逐轮召回保证；逐轮预算向量 (t₁,…,t_R) 在验证集上联合搜索以最大化节省、约束全局召回 ≥ 用户指定目标 ρ*；可选的全局证书给出部署前可验证的先验保证。
- **强算力节省与风险控制的实证**：在 TextCraft 上，90% 全局召回目标下级联节省 47.1%±10.3%（Qwen-2.5-7B）和 37.2%±8.8%（Llama-3.2-3B）推理计算，是最佳单门策略的 1.6–1.7 倍；在所有 8 个 agent–目标配置中，实测召回与目标的偏差 ≤ 0.027 且 7/8 配置高于目标。
- **内部访问的必要性得到量化**：在 0.95 目标下，把 probe 换成行为打分器在 Qwen 上节省减半（17.0% vs 32.0%），把行为特征叠加到 probe 上无额外收益——隐藏状态已经编码了行为揭示的信息。
- **诚实地刻画了证书的数据成本**：给出"三的法则"型样本复杂度界 n ≥ lnα_m/lnρ*；在 n_pos=113/115 的规模下可证书化的目标上限约 0.974，目标 0.98 / 0.99 将分别需要约 1.3× / 2.6× 成功 episode；超过上限时策略主动弃权（不中止），将"数据能背多严的承诺"提前告知实践者。

## Key Contributions

- **Failure is decodable from internal activations long before it surfaces in behavior.** On two agent LLMs, logistic probes on the residual-stream hidden state of the final generated action token (layer 14 for Llama-3.2-3B, layer 20 for Qwen-2.5-7B) at round 1 already match or exceed the peak AUC that a behavior-only surface scorer reaches only at rounds 3–4, and peak themselves at round 2 with a 0.12–0.21 AUC absolute advantage—precisely in the rounds where most of the episode's compute is still unspent.
- **First abort policy for LLM agents that controls episode-level success recall across multiple sequential gates.** Each gate is calibrated via a Clopper–Pearson exact-binomial lower confidence bound on its per-round survival of successful calibration episodes, giving a distribution-free, finite-sample per-round guarantee; per-round recall budgets (t₁,…,t_R_g) are jointly searched on a disjoint validation split to maximize compute savings subject to global recall ≥ user-chosen ρ*. An optional global certificate replaces the empirical margin with a distribution-free, a priori verifiable bound on the global recall.
- **Strong, honestly risk-controlled savings.** On TextCraft, at a 90% global recall target the cascade saves 47.1%±10.3% (Qwen-2.5-7B) and 37.2%±8.8% (Llama-3.2-3B) of inference compute—1.6–1.7× the best single-gate policy at the same target. Achieved global recall matches its target within one standard deviation in every one of the eight agent–target configurations, erring above the target in seven of eight.
- **Internal access is shown to be the load-bearing signal.** At the 0.95 target, swapping the probe for a behavior-only scorer inside the identical cascade roughly halves savings on Qwen (17.0% vs 32.0%); stacking surface features onto the probe adds nothing—whatever behavior reveals, the hidden states already encode (the converse is false).
- **Transparent sample complexity for certification.** The authors derive a "rule of three"-style bound n ≥ lnα_m/lnρ*; with n_pos = 113/115 successful validation episodes, certifiable targets cap near 0.974, and the certified variant abstains (zero savings by construction) at 0.98 and 0.99, which would require ≈1.3× and ≈2.6× more successful episodes respectively.

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** An agent episode is a sequence of interaction rounds (s₁,a₁,s₂,a₂,…) terminating with binary outcome y∈{0,1}; episodes run up to R_full = 20 rounds; gates are placed at rounds 1–6 (R_g = 6). The agent aborts an episode at gate r if f_r(x) > τ_r, losing any potential success but saving the remaining cost C − c_r. The design objective is to maximize expected cost savings subject to a user-specified global success-recall floor ρ* (fraction of y=1 episodes that survive every gate). The authors stress that per-gate guarantees do not compose into an episode-level guarantee because false-abort probability accumulates across gates and late gates see fewer successes.

**Per-round failure scorers.** A feature vector is extracted via a teacher-forced forward pass: the residual-stream hidden state at the final token of the agent's generated action in round r, at a single layer per model (layer 14 of 28 for Llama-3.2-3B, layer 20 of 28 for Qwen-2.5-7B; layers fixed once per model via independent pilot per-layer AUC sweeps). A per-round logistic regression (standardized features, L2 regularization, C=1) is trained to predict 1−y from episodes alive at round r. Two ablated scorers share the entire downstream pipeline: a *surface scorer* (logistic model over serving-API-observable features: mean action-token log-probability of the current and preceding rounds, generated-token count, prefix length, count of preceding rounds whose environment feedback contains error keywords) and a *stacking scorer* that concatenates surface features onto the activation features.

**Per-round recall-calibrated gates.** Given per-round recall budget t_r, with S_r the scores of the n_r successful calibration episodes alive at round r and k(τ) the survivor count under threshold τ, the gate sets τ_r to the smallest calibration score whose Clopper–Pearson exact-binomial lower confidence bound p(τ) = Beta⁻¹(α; k(τ), n_r − k(τ)+1) ≥ t_r at per-gate confidence 1−α (α=0.05). A budget of t_r = 1 disables the gate. This is deliberately stronger than a marginal conformal quantile; a conformal-quantile variant is compared in the appendix (it saves more but violates the target on Llama at small per-round sets).

**Recall budget search under a global constraint.** Probes are produced via task-grouped cross-fitting (stratified group k-fold) so every episode is scored by a probe that never saw its task; tasks are partitioned 20% calibration / 20% validation / 60% held-out test, with task-level grouping preventing leakage across splits. The budget vector is treated as a hyperparameter and selected on the validation split by enumerating 6^6 = 46,656 candidates on the grid {0.85,0.90,0.95,0.98,0.99,1.0}^6, simulating the full cascade per candidate, and recording global validation recall ρ̂_val and savings. Two feasibility rules are studied: a *margin* rule (default, ρ̂_val ≥ ρ* + δ, δ = 0.02, heuristic) and a *certificate* rule (Clopper–Pearson lower bound at level 1−α_m, α_m = 0.05, on the global recall computed from n_pos successful validation episodes, distribution-free). If no candidate is feasible, the policy abstains and aborts nothing. Two structural baselines share the same machinery: *single-gate* (spend the entire recall budget at one round, both round and budget searched) and *uniform* (same t_r at every gate).

**Environment and protocol.** TextCraft (AgentGym suite), 800 episodes over 100 tasks per agent at temperature 1.0 (8 episodes/task), for Llama-3.2-3B (63.0% task success) and Qwen-2.5-7B (74.8%); the full pipeline (splitting, cross-fitting, calibration, budget search, evaluation) is repeated over 20 random seeds, reporting test-split mean ± standard deviation. Compute savings are reported as the fraction of total generated tokens saved by aborts.

## Results & Highlights

- **Timing of signal (Figure 3):** Per-round cross-fitted AUC of the probe peaks at round 2; the behavior-only surface scorer is barely better than chance at the first gate and only becomes informative at rounds 3–4, by which point over a third of episodes have already finished. The probe's absolute AUC advantage over the surface scorer is 0.12–0.21 in the rounds where most compute is still unspent.
- **Main cascade savings (Table 1), probe-based cascade with margin-based budget search (δ=0.02, 20 seeds, test split):**
  - **Qwen-2.5-7B at ρ\*=0.90: saves 47.1%±10.3% of inference compute** vs 27.6%±5.6% for the best single-gate policy (1.7×) and 6.9%±5.7% for the uniform allocation.
  - **Llama-3.2-3B at ρ\*=0.90: saves 37.2%±8.8%** vs 23.6%±5.3% single-gate (≈1.6×) and 3.1%±2.5% uniform.
  - At ρ\*=0.95: Qwen 32.0%±12.2% (cascade) vs 17.4%±5.5% (single-gate); Llama 19.9%±9.2% vs 10.4%±3.9%. At ρ\*=0.97: Qwen 18.2%±12.5% vs 10.8%±4.3%; Llama 9.3%±7.7% vs 4.1%±3.6%.
- **Recall control is honest (Figure 4):** Across all eight agent–target pairs the cascade's mean test recall deviates from its target by at most 0.027, always within one standard deviation, and errs above the target in seven of eight cases.
- **Where the budget is spent, not just how much:** The modal allocation at Llama's 0.90 target is (0.95, 0.85, 0.85, 1.0, 1.0, 1.0)—spending recall aggressively at rounds 1–3 and disabling late gates entirely. The uniform allocation collapses to ≈0% savings at strict targets (0.0±0.0 at 0.95/0.97 for both agents).
- **Internal access is the load-bearing signal (Figure 6, target 0.95, achieved recall 0.954–0.970):** On Qwen, the probe cascade saves 32.0% vs 17.0% for the surface cascade (≈half); on Llama the gap is narrower. Stacking behavioral features onto the probe lands within noise of probe-alone, indicating hidden states already encode what behavior reveals. Cascade structure amplifies weak scorers even more than strong ones (surface savings multiply ≈3.6× moving from single-gate to cascade, vs ≈1.9× for the probe), because the cascade can defer part of the budget to rounds 3–4 where behavioral evidence finally matures.
- **Exposure decay motivates the cascade (Figure 2):** 35% (Llama) / 39% (Qwen) of episodes finish within two rounds; 53% / 69% finish before the last gate, quantifying how sharply late gates' exposure—and remaining-compute benefit—decays.
- **Sample complexity of certification (Figure 7):** With n_pos = 113 (Llama) / 115 (Qwen), certifiable targets cap near 0.974; targets 0.98 and 0.99 cannot be certified even for the no-op policy and the certified variant abstains with zero savings. Stricter targets require substantially more data independent of scorer quality.
- **Margin rule absorbs selection optimism almost exactly (Figure 8):** Across the eight main configurations, validation recall exceeds test recall by 0.008–0.030, precisely the gap the δ = 0.02 margin is sized to absorb; worst case (Llama at 0.92) mean test recall lands 0.001 under the nominal target (well within seed noise). Removing the margin (δ=0) at the 0.95 target lands at test recall 0.943±0.032 (Qwen) and 0.942±0.024 (Llama), both below target, while saving more (41.0% / 26.3%).
- **Appendix gate comparison (Table 2, single gate at round 1, per-round target 0.95, post-gen probe):** The conformal-quantile gate saves more but violates the target on Llama (0.933±0.029 recall vs 20.7%±5.6% saved); Clopper–Pearson is conservative (0.972–0.977 recall) but never violates.

## Authors & Credibility

The work is led by Kai Ruan and corresponding author Hao Sun at the Gaoling School of Artificial Intelligence, Renmin University of China, with co-authors from the Institute of Computing Technology (CAS), Duke University, the Institute of Automation (CAS), and Zhejiang University. The methodology reflects genuine statistical care: distribution-free Clopper–Pearson calibration rather than ad-hoc thresholds, task-grouped cross-fitting to prevent leakage, a 20-seed evaluation with mean ± standard deviation reporting, an a priori-fixed δ = 0.02 margin that the authors verify is correctly sized (≈ the binomial standard error √(ρ*(1−ρ*)/n_pos) ≈ 0.02 at their scale), and an explicit, non-hand-wavy account of what their data can and cannot certify. The framing is unusually honest about the limits of the approach rather than overselling it.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The combination is genuinely new even though the ingredients are not. Probing internal states for correctness is well established (Kadavath 2022, Burns 2023, Orgad 2025, Padhi 2026), conformal prediction for LLMs is mature, and early-exit / cascades (CALM, FrugalGPT, Atropos) exist; but nobody had previously (a) characterized *when in an episode* the outcome signal becomes actionable, (b) turned it into a multi-gate stopping rule with a controllable, composable episode-level recall guarantee, or (c) given a finite-sample certificate on the global recall. The recall-budget-as-searchable-hyperparameter under a global constraint is the key methodological move.
- **Rigor:** High. Clopper–Pearson per-gate calibration, task-grouped cross-fitting, disjoint calibration/validation/test splits with task-level grouping, 20-seed reporting, an empirical check that the margin actually absorbs selection optimism (Figure 8), and an explicit certificate that provably abstains when the data cannot support the target. Achieved recall matches the target in every configuration rather than being post-hoc-tuned. The conformal-quantile ablation in Table 2 honestly documents the conservative cost of Clopper–Pearson at small per-round sets.
- **Reproducibility:** Good but contingent. Algorithm 1 is fully specified; probe layer, regularization (C=1), grid (6^6 candidates), budget (R_g=6), episode count (800), seed count (20), and the TextCraft + AgentGym environment are all named. Code is promised ("will be released soon") but not yet available at submission. A real reproducibility wrinkle is the *deployment gap*: activations are extracted via an offline teacher-forced re-run, and the authors candidly note that exposing intermediate activations inside optimized serving stacks (fused attention kernels, paged KV caches) is "a real engineering task in its own right"—a deployed cascade does not yet exist.
- **Significance:** Practically meaningful for inference-cost reduction in agentic LLM systems, and conceptually clean: it relocates the failure-prediction signal from externally trained auditors (e.g., AgentForesight's separate 7B RL auditor) to the agent's own activations at negligible inference cost, and adds an episode-level guarantee that no prior monitor provides. The clear characterization of which recall targets a given dataset can back is immediately useful to practitioners.
- **Red flags / weaknesses:**
  - **Single environment.** All evidence comes from TextCraft only, with two agents and 800 episodes each. The authors flag this and note the exchangeability assumption can break under distribution shift (new task types at deployment), but generalization to web navigation, tool use, or embodied simulation is unverified.
  - **Small per-round calibration sets.** n_pos = 113/115 caps certifiable targets near 0.974 and forces the certified variant to abstain at 0.98/0.99; the authors are transparent that this is a data-cost statement, not a method failure, but it means the formal guarantee is unavailable at the strictest targets practitioners may want.
  - **Wall-clock vs token savings.** Savings are measured in generated tokens; realized dollar/wall-clock savings depend on batching, prefill–decode asymmetry, and whether freed capacity is actually reused—unmeasured here.
  - **Behavior-only baseline is relatively weak by construction.** The surface scorer uses only a handful of hand-crafted trajectory features (mean log-prob, token counts, prefix length, error-keyword counts); a stronger learned behavior encoder (e.g., a fine-tuned small LM auditor like AgentForesight) is not compared head-to-head inside the cascade, so the claim that "internal access is load-bearing" is demonstrated against a deliberately weak behavior baseline.
  - **Margin is a heuristic.** δ = 0.02 happens to match the binomial standard error at the paper's scale and is validated empirically, but the authors acknowledge formal episode-level guarantees over sequential gates with searched budgets still require sharper multiple-testing machinery (fixed-sequence or e-value methods) than the one-shot bound they certify with.

## Strengths

- Converts a probing observation into a deployable, risk-controlled policy with a clear, controllable episode-level recall guarantee—exactly the quantity that matters for deployment.
- Honest, non-oversold presentation: explicit abstention at targets the data cannot back, explicit gap between margin (heuristic) and certificate (formal), and explicit acknowledgment of the serving-stack engineering work still required.
- Statistically careful: Clopper–Pearson calibration, task-grouped cross-fitting, disjoint task-level splits, 20-seed evaluation, and direct empirical verification that the safety margin is correctly sized.
- The sample-complexity result (rule-of-three bound, Figure 7) is a genuinely useful practitioner-facing artifact—it tells you up front how much labeled data a given recall promise costs.
- Cascade structure is shown to amplify weak scorers more than strong ones (≈3.6× for surface vs ≈1.9× for probe), making the framework valuable even for environments where internal access is unavailable.

## Limitations

- Single-environment evaluation (TextCraft only); generalization to other agent benchmarks (web navigation, tool use, embodied) is unverified.
- Formal certificate caps at ρ* ≈ 0.974 at the paper's data scale; 0.98/0.99 require ≈1.3×/2.6× more successful episodes and trigger abstention.
- Activations are extracted via offline teacher-forced re-run; a deployed cascade reading live activations from optimized serving stacks is not implemented.
- Savings reported in generated tokens, not wall-clock or dollars; batching and prefill–decode asymmetry effects are unmodeled.
- The behavior-only baseline is a hand-crafted feature scorer; stronger learned behavior auditors are not compared inside the identical cascade.
- The safety margin is heuristic; tightening it into a fully formal sequential multiple-testing guarantee is left as the main methodological open problem.
- Gates beyond round 6 are not used; the authors argue headroom is small there, but this is only established for TextCraft.

## Relevance

与"LLM agent 推理效率/成本优化"和"内部状态可解释性"两个研究方向高度契合：它把探针（probing）发现从"模型知道什么"扩展到"何时可在 episode 中做出可控中止决策"，并提供分布无关、episode 级的召回保证，对降低 agentic LLM 部署成本与构建可信在线监控有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the early-decodable failure signal generalize beyond TextCraft to web-navigation (WebArena), tool-use (ToolBench), and embodied (ScienceWorld, AlfWorld) agents, and at which round does the probe-vs-surface timing gap close in those domains?
- Can the heuristic δ-margin be replaced by a fully formal sequential multiple-testing procedure (fixed-sequence tests, e-values, or confidence-sequence methods) so the episode-level guarantee holds by theorem rather than empirical validation?
- How do savings change when measured in wall-clock / dollars under realistic serving conditions (continuous batching, paged KV caches, prefill–decode asymmetry), and is the freed capacity actually reusable for retries?
- Would a stronger learned behavior encoder (e.g., a fine-tuned small-LM auditor) inside the cascade close the probe-vs-surface gap, or do hidden states remain strictly more informative?
- Can the cascade be extended to mid-trajectory *recovery* (e.g., hot-swap to a stronger model, as in Atropos) rather than pure abort, while preserving the episode-level recall guarantee?
- How does the framework behave under distribution shift (new task types at deployment), and what is the minimum labeled recalibration sample that restores the guarantee?
