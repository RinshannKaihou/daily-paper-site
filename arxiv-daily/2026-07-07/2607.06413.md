> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一种基于全因子实验设计与多元分析（含效用对齐典型分解 UACD）的框架，将 LLM 编码代理（Codex、Claude Code）视为随机模型发现算子，在联网拼字游戏数据上系统评估推理努力等控制因素对性能、成本与过程复杂度的联合影响，发现提高推理努力主要增加成本与复杂度而非提升模型质量。

> **TL;DR:** The paper proposes an experimental-design and multivariate-analysis framework that treats LLM coding agents (Codex GPT-5.5, Claude Code Opus 4.7) as stochastic model-discovery operators on networked anagram-game data, running a 144-run full factorial over agent, task, primary metric, reasoning effort, fold, and discovery regime; on the networked anagram testbed, higher reasoning effort raises cost and process complexity (median fresh tokens for claude-code climb from 29.4k to 91.6k, codex median dollar cost $0.60 vs claude-code's $4.19) but yields a negative utility slope in all 8 strata (significant in 7/8 after Holm correction), so effort functions as a resource-control parameter rather than a reliable path to better models.

## 核心贡献

- 提出"模型发现算子"概念：将 LLM 编码代理形式化为一个把任务数据与优化目标映射为拟合模型的随机算子，使其本身成为被设计的实验对象，而非仅评估其产出模型。
- 构建六因子全因子实验设计（agent × task × metric × reasoning effort × fold × regime，共 144 次运行，140 次有效），以 agent/task/metric 为分层因子、effort/fold/regime 为设计因子，专门刻画推理努力的效应。
- 提出 Utility-Aligned Canonical Decomposition (UACD)：用 Pillai 多元迹检验努力是否移动响应向量，用预先指定的效用方向 u 的有向检验判定努力是否沿效用方向移动，并以集中度 π 与对齐度 η^(1) 描述努力效应的主方向是否与效用一致。
- 将性能、成本（美元/墙钟时间）与过程复杂度（候选模型数、特征数、决策数、脚本长度）作为 8 维联合响应 y∈R^8 进行多元回归与推断，避免仅看单一指标而遗漏成本/过程的权衡。
- 在联网拼字游戏科学发现测试床上实证发现：推理努力在所有 8 个分层中对效用方向呈负斜率（7/8 在 Holm 校正后仍显著），即更多努力换来的是更长、更贵、更复杂的运行，而非更好的模型；codex 与 claude-code 质量相当但美元成本相差约 7 倍。
- 公开数据与可复现评估流水线（GitHub 仓库），并披露了 4 次因模拟有效性不足被剔除的运行作为失败案例分析。

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes LLM coding agents as stochastic "model-discovery operators" that map task data and an optimization target to a fitted model, making the agent itself (not just its output model) the object of a designed experiment.
- Constructs a six-factor full factorial design (agent × task × metric × reasoning effort × fold × regime = 144 runs, 140 valid) with A/T/M as stratification factors and E/F/R as design factors, isolating the within-stratum effect of reasoning effort.
- Introduces Utility-Aligned Canonical Decomposition (UACD): pairs Pillai's trace omnibus test with a pre-specified utility contrast u for a directed test, and uses concentration π ∈ [0.84, 0.99] and alignment η^(1) to characterize whether the dominant effort direction aligns with a performance-cost utility direction.
- Treats performance, cost (USD, wall-clock), and process complexity (registered models/features/decisions, script length) as an 8-dimensional joint response y ∈ R^8 analyzed via multivariate regression, avoiding the blind spot of single-metric evaluation.
- Demonstrates on a networked-anagram scientific-discovery testbed that reasoning effort yields a negative utility slope in all 8 strata (significant in 7/8 after Holm correction), establishing effort as a resource-control parameter rather than a reliable quality lever.
- Releases the documented dataset and reproducible evaluation harness on GitHub, and transparently reports the 4 dropped simulation-invalid runs as a case study of agentic-discovery failure.

## Methodology

The framework probes two commercial coding agents — codex (OpenAI Codex CLI v0.125, GPT-5.5) and claude-code (Claude Code, Opus 4.7) — each with a native three-level reasoning-effort ladder (relabeled low/default/max). Each agent works in an isolated workspace with a no-leakage rule for held-out data, explores the data, registers candidate models/features, logs modeling decisions, and submits a single runnable script that a fixed evaluation harness scores on held-out sessions.

**Data and tasks.** The testbed is a fixed networked group-anagram dataset (28 sessions, 209 players, 62,700 player-time records; 24 closed-ring + 4 partial-path sessions), split into three session-level CV folds (9/9/10). Two discovery tasks are defined: Task 1 (timestep) is four-class next-action prediction, scored by weighted AUC (wAUC) and a rare-observation relative-improvement metric (MRI-RO); Task 2 (ABM) is generative, where the agent builds an agent-based simulator run forward on held-out sessions and scored by a 6-component player-summary KL divergence (KL6) and a Levenshtein-distance-distribution Wasserstein-1 distance (DLD).

**Factorial design.** Six crossed factors give 2×2×2×3×3×2 = 144 runs with no replicates: stratification factors A (agent), T (task), M (primary metric) define 8 strata; design factors E (reasoning effort, ordered), F (discovery fold), R (discovery regime full vs partial) vary within each stratum (18 runs per stratum, 16 for a-DLD after dropping 4 invalid runs).

**Responses.** Each run produces an 8-vector y ∈ R^8: primary-metric score and non-primary-metric score (direction-normalized so higher is better), log10 monetary cost and wall-clock time, and log10(1+·) counts of registered models, features, logged decisions, and submitted-script length. Coordinates are standardized within stratum.

**Analysis.** A per-stratum multivariate linear model Y = XB + E with orthogonal-polynomial effort contrasts (linear x_L = −1,0,+1; quadratic x_Q = +1,−2,+1) and fold/regime blocking terms is fit by OLS. Three inferential tools are used: (1) a directed utility test on u^T β̂_l with u = (½, ½, −½, −½, 0, 0, 0, 0) rewarding performance and penalizing cost, with Holm correction across the 8 strata; (2) Pillai's trace V as an omnibus MANOVA test of whether effort moves the response in any direction; (3) UACD, which extracts the leading canonical direction a_1 of S_H a = λ S_E a, computes concentration π = λ_1/(λ_1+λ_2) and alignment η^(1) = a_1^T u, and assesses sign consistency across strata via a sign test (treating the result as descriptive because strata share agents/tasks/folds).

## Results & Highlights

- Across the 8 strata, the directed utility slope u^T β̂ is negative in all 8 (range −0.46 to −1.08); it is significant without correction in 7/8 and remains significant in 7/8 after Holm correction (e.g., codex/t-wAUC slope −0.97, 95% CI [−1.23, −0.70], p_Holm < 0.001; claude-code/a-KL slope −0.58, p_Holm = 0.044; the only non-significant stratum is codex/a-DLD, slope −0.46, p = 0.105).
- On the primary metric alone, effort is weak: the univariate joint effort test reaches uncorrected significance only in a-DLD (p = 0.029, not significant at Bonferroni α/4 = 0.0125); the strongest univariate effect is the agent indicator in t-MRI (p_A < 0.001), not effort.
- Median fresh-token use climbs from 29.4k to 91.6k for claude-code and from 69.6k to 170.5k for codex between lowest and highest effort; the number of candidate models considered rises from median 3 (low) to 4 (default) to 6 (max), with means 3.2/5.3/6.5.
- Codex stays cheap (median $0.60) versus claude-code (median $4.19) — roughly 7× higher cost — while reaching comparable primary-metric quality; codex uses more fresh tokens at every level yet costs less because most of claude-code's cost is cache reads omitted from the fresh-token count. (Note: codex USD cost is imputed at a blended $6.50/10^6 tokens, not metered.)
- The token-performance relationship is weakly positive for claude-code (Spearman ρ = 0.28) and essentially flat for codex (ρ = 0.01), anticipating the negative effort-to-utility slope.
- The omnibus Pillai trace V ∈ [1.31, 1.66] is large but underpowered per stratum: 4/8 strata reach uncorrected p < 0.05 and none survive α/8 = 0.00625; the directed test is more powerful, reaching significance in 7 strata before and after Holm correction.
- UACD: the leading canonical direction carries π ∈ [0.84, 0.99] of the canonical effect (effect is essentially one-dimensional) and is utility-misaligned with η^(1) < 0 in all 8 strata; a sign test gives p = 0.008 (treated as descriptive due to shared strata).
- Self-assessment is reliable on Task 1 (only 1 of 72 runs overconfident) but unreliable on Task 2: 83% of claude-code's a-KL runs and about half of codex's are overconfident, and higher effort did not make self-reports more cautious; the most extreme discarded run (max-effort claude-code, $15.37) was valid in only 1/900 simulations yet reported a strong internal score.
- Task-1 submissions are almost all gradient-boosted trees (93%, 67/72); on Task 2 the edit-distance-targeted simulator family attains the lowest median DLD (~0.16 vs 0.63 for template/replay).

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are Hao He and Xinwei Deng (Department of Statistics, Virginia Tech), Xueying Liu (Department of Statistical Science, Baylor University), and Chris J. Kuhlman (Advanced Research Computing, Virginia Tech). The group has a sustained line of prior work on the networked anagram-game data and its modeling (Cedeño-Mieles et al. 2019; Ren et al. 2018; He et al. 2024, 2025), so the testbed and evaluation metrics are well grounded in their own validated literature. The methodology draws on classical design-of-experiments and multivariate statistics (Montgomery; Wu & Hamada; Anderson; Pillai 1955; Rencher & Christensen), and the framework is positioned honestly as a demonstrated technique on two agents rather than a survey. A documented dataset and reproducible evaluation pipeline are released on GitHub, with agent/model/effort flags pinned per run.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The framing of an LLM coding agent as a stochastic "model-discovery operator" that is itself the object of a factorial experiment, with cost and process treated as joint response coordinates rather than diagnostics, is a genuinely fresh contribution that, to the authors' knowledge and plausibly, no prior work combines for agentic model discovery.
- **Rigor:** The multivariate machinery is sound and appropriately hedged: Pillai's trace, a pre-specified directed utility contrast with Holm correction, and UACD with sign-test interpretation are correctly applied; the authors are explicit that the omnibus test is underpowered per stratum (n ∈ {16,18} against d = 8) and that UACD's leading direction is "weakly identified within a single stratum," so η^(1) is read by sign only. The negative-utility conclusion rests on the directed contrast (7/8 significant after Holm), not on the descriptive sign test, which is the correct evidentiary ordering.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong on software hygiene — agent, model, CLI version, and effort flags are pinned per run and stored with configuration; held-out data never leaks to the agent; the harness and data are public. Weak on replicates: the design has no replicates (each configuration run once), so per-stratum uncertainty rests entirely on the factorial structure, and the authors flag this as limiting stability of inference.
- **Significance:** The practical takeaway — that reasoning effort is a resource-control knob, not a quality lever, on this testbed — is actionable for deployment economics and is supported across two agents and four conditions. However, generalization is limited: only two agents, one task family, one dataset, and one family of metrics are studied.
- **Red flags / specific concerns:**
  - **Confounded codex Task-1 prompt wording:** The 18 codex Task-1 full runs used an earlier prompt revision while all partial runs used the current one, lining up exactly with the full-vs-partial split; the authors honestly decline to read codex Task-1 full-vs-partial causally, but this means the full-information advantage claim is restricted to a-KL.
  - **Imputed codex cost:** Codex USD cost is imputed from token counts at list prices (blended $6.50/10^6 tokens, 94:6 input/output split), not metered, so the headline 7× cost difference is a deployment-economics description, not a controlled contrast — and the negative utility slope partly depends on this imputed coordinate (though the authors note η^(1) < 0 persists when cost is redefined as fresh-token count).
  - **Dropped runs concentrated in one condition:** All 4 dropped runs are in a-DLD (the generative distance condition), leaving n = 16 there vs 18 elsewhere, which weakens precisely the stratum where effort shows the only univariate signal.
  - **Single utility direction:** The conclusion is conditional on u = (½, ½, −½, −½, 0, 0, 0, 0); a systematic sensitivity analysis over alternative utility weights is deferred to future work, so the robustness of "effort lowers utility" to the choice of weights is untested.
  - **Weak within-stratum identification of canonical direction:** Bootstrap indicates a_1 is weakly identified per stratum, so the UACD alignment numbers should not be over-interpreted pointwise — the authors handle this correctly by reading sign only, but a reader of the η^(1) table should be cautious.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clean reframing of agent evaluation: the agent is the experimental unit, and performance/cost/process are joint responses to its controls — this directly addresses the field's over-reliance on single-run benchmarks.
- Methodologically principled: combines classical DOE (orthogonal-polynomial contrasts, blocking, factorial structure) with multivariate inference (Pillai's trace, directed contrast, canonical decomposition) and is transparent about power limits and identification weakness.
- Honest, paper-specific reporting of failures: the 4 dropped runs are dissected as a case study (e.g., the max-effort claude-code run valid in 1/900 simulations, treating inventory as a set rather than a multiset), and the prompt-wording confound is disclosed rather than buried.
- Practically useful findings: effort-as-resource-control, agent metric specialization, Task-1-vs-Task-2 self-assessment reliability gap, and the ~7× cost gap at comparable quality are all directly deployable insights.
- Public, pinned, reproducible harness and data.

## Limitations

- No replicates: each of the 140 configurations is run once, so per-stratum inference relies on the factorial structure and uncertainty estimates may be unstable; replicated studies are deferred as costly (single runs exceeded 70 minutes and $33).
- Narrow scope: only two agents (Codex GPT-5.5, Claude Opus 4.7), one task family (anagram-game modeling), one dataset, and four metrics; generalization to other agents, domains, and metrics is untested.
- Imputed rather than metered cost for codex, so cross-agent cost comparisons are descriptive, not controlled.
- The leading canonical direction is weakly identified within a single stratum, so UACD alignment is interpreted by sign only, supplemented by a sign test whose independence assumption is only approximate (strata share agents/tasks/folds).
- The utility conclusion is tied to a single pre-specified utility direction u; sensitivity to alternative weightings is left to future work.
- The four dropped runs all concentrate in the a-DLD condition, leaving that stratum with n = 16 and the only condition where univariate effort significance was found.

## Relevance

本文与"代理式 AI / LLM 编码代理的系统化评估"高度相关：它把推理努力、成本与过程复杂度作为联合响应进行因子实验与多元推断，直接回应了"如何科学评估随机自适应代理"这一开放问题，对关注 agentic AI 评估方法学、成本-质量权衡与可复现基准的研究者具有方法学参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the negative effort-to-utility slope persist under alternative utility directions (e.g., weighting process complexity negatively, or asymmetric performance/cost weights), and is there a utility direction under which higher effort becomes net positive?
- How do the findings generalize beyond two agents and the anagram-game domain — e.g., to open-source coding agents, to SWE-bench-style software tasks, or to scientific-discovery tasks with different signal-to-noise?
- With replicated runs (the deferred larger-scale study), do the per-stratum Pillai omnibus tests gain enough power to survive α/8 correction, and does the UACD leading direction become point-identifiable rather than sign-only?
- If cost were metered directly for codex rather than imputed at list prices, would the 7× cost gap and the negative utility slope quantitatively change?
- Where exactly does the extra reasoning effort go in the run traces, and can trace-level analysis explain why more tokens and more candidate models do not translate into better held-out performance?
- Can the self-assessment gap on generative Task 2 be closed by feeding the agent its own simulation-validity check or an external calibration signal, so that self-report becomes a reliable proxy for evaluated quality?
