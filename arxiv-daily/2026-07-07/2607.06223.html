Methodology

IGRPO inherits the GRPO tree perspective: every partial interaction history is a node, every turn an edge, a full rollout a root-to-leaf path. Two parts are coupled.

Node-level information gain. The answer score is the normalized ground-truth likelihood under the current policy sθ(h;a) = exp((1/L)·Σ log πθ(aⱼ|h,a<ⱼ)). Information gain is its marginal lift over the parent:

IG(h) = sθ(h;a) − sθ(parent(h);a)    // IG(h₀)=sθ(h₀;a)

Budget-aware tree rollout. At each stage it samples Bᵢ active nodes with a soft, temperature-controlled rule rather than uniform or hard-threshold branching:

pᵢ(h) ∝ exp(γ·val(h)),    val(h) = (sθ(h;a) + IG(h)) / 2

Theoretical target. As per-layer budgets → ∞ the empirical trajectory distribution converges (Thm 1) to μθ(o) ∝ πθ(o)·exp(γV(o)), which (Thm 2) uniquely optimizes the variational objective max_μ E[V(o)] − (1/γ)·KL(μ‖πθ). IGRPO then minimizes a GRPO-style clipped surrogate JIGRPO with rollouts drawn from μθ_old and a KL anchor to πref; only decision tokens ([THINK],[SEARCH]) carry gradient.

Strengths & Limitations

PRO Strengths

  • Theoretically grounded. The limiting-distribution + variational theorems give the rollout a real optimization target, not a heuristic.
  • Elegant soft allocation. γ→0 subsumes uniform tree expansion; γ→∞ subsumes greedy exploitation — and the ablation confirms a sweet spot.
  • Honest scaling story. Clean explanation of why 3B benefits more than 7B, plus a Fig. 4 entropy analysis separating reallocation from blind expansion.
  • Consistent multi-hop wins. HotpotQA, 2Wiki, Bamboogle — exactly the regime where intermediate-state utility matters.
  • Clean protocol. Identical retriever, turn limit, iterations and rollout budget across all baselines. Code public ↗.

CON Limitations

  • Needs ground-truth answers to compute sθ(h;a) — directly applicable only to verifiable-answer tasks; open-ended work needs a new node utility.
  • R(o)–V(o) correlation — the linchpin tying theory to reward — is asserted, not measured for the instantiated val(·).
  • 7B gains are modest (+0.9% avg, ties on TriviaQA); scaling to 14B/32B is open.
  • Missing tree baselines: no head-to-head with the cited entropy-guided AEPO / ARPO; StepSearch reports "–" on single-hop.
  • Max 4 turns — long-horizon behavior unverified; no variance across seeds (point estimates only).

Open Questions for Follow-up

Q1Does the variational teacher story hold when ground-truth is unavailable — e.g. with a learned reward model or self-consistency score in sθ(h;a)?
Q2What is the measured Pearson/Spearman correlation between R(o) and V(o) for val(h)=(sθ(h;a)+IG(h))/2, across datasets and training steps?
Q3How does IGRPO compare directly to entropy-guided tree baselines AEPO and ARPO under matched budgets?
Q4Do the gains persist (or shrink further) at 14B/32B, and on genuinely long-horizon (>4 turn) deep-research tasks?
Q5Does mixing a continuous pseudo-reward (cumulative IG on truncated paths) with the 0/1 outcome reward harm group-normalization?
Q6Can val(·) absorb a UCB-style uncertainty bonus so high-IG-but-low-confidence nodes aren't over-expanded? Is γ=1 still optimal at 7B+?
