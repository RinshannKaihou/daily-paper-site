01

Methodology

Each autoregressive decode step is modeled as a five-dimensional resource vector r = (HBM bytes, FLOPs, network bytes, network messages, KV capacity). The first four convert to time via calibrated hardware rates; the fifth bounds feasible batch size and enters the goodput judgment. Same-resource terms add (weight reads + KV reads share HBM); independent engines — HBM, SMs, NIC — take the max under perfect overlap, yielding an optimistic floor t_opt = max(t_HBM, t_compute, t_network), while the plain sum gives a pessimistic floor t_sum. The interval [t_opt, t_sum] is retained, not collapsed: a measurement near t_opt implies good overlap; near t_sum implies serialized engines; above t_sum implies time leaking outside the model.

KV capacity is treated as a first-class fifth resource, not a post-hoc OOM check. A residual above 1.3× the floor escalates to Nsight profiling; below it the workflow stops — profiling as exception handling, not a pipeline stage.

Two-sided floor
[max, sum] keeps overlap observable instead of fixing it as an assumption up front.
Wall ordering
Compare deployments by which wall binds first — verdicts on the first wall survive errors in walls never reached.
MoE union correction
Weight bytes follow the union of experts activated across the batch; uniform routing tightens terms by ~13% at B=64.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Two-sided floor turns overlap from an assumption into an observable, with a concrete reading of interval position.
  • Wall ordering reframes deployment comparison from point benchmarks to "which resource binds first" — robust to errors in later-wall accounts.
  • H20 ridge-point portrait (74 vs 590 FLOP/byte) reframes the export part's decode-friendliness from geometry, not folklore.
  • Honest calibration: datasheet vs calibrated floors separated; ceiling-vs-implementation question made explicit for communication.
  • Compositional module-declaration design — DSA, state-space layers, MLA-sharding redesigns enter by changing one module.
  • Ships as both a human checklist and an enforceable agent skill, countering agents that reflexively benchmark and profile.

Limitations

  • Headline empirical validation not yet performed: residual sweep, triage traces, cross-platform, judgment validation all planned.
  • Triage thresholds (1.3×, 70%/40% MBU) are systematized practitioner experience — not derived, calibration protocol unfinished.
  • Uniform-routing assumption breaks under expert imbalance (common in production); error not bounded.
  • Continuous batching not modeled — B varies within a window, so floors should bracket a distribution, not assume a point.
  • 2.4× single-stream TP advantage is a physical bound, not an expectation: batch-1 decode attains only ~27% of its analytic floor.
  • Artifact hard-codes two layouts; per-module plan interface that generalizes it is "staged as the next revision."

Questions for further exploration

Q1
When the residual sweep runs across {TP16, TP16+DSA, EP16+DPA} × B × S, does the 1.3× threshold hold, or must it become layout- and batch-dependent?
Q2
If CUDA-graphed production paths close the 33 µs launch-overhead gap toward ~10 µs, how much of the single-stream TP advantage survives?
Q3
Under hot-expert routing, how much does the union correction underestimate weight traffic — and does EPLB restore the floor, or do stragglers invalidate the sum floor first?
Q4
Can wall-ordering robustness be quantified as a sensitivity bound — how wrong can a later-wall constant be before it promotes and changes the verdict?
Q5
Do TP-enabling latent attention variants (GQLA, TPLA) shift the capacity wall and the TP-vs-DP crossover, or merely move the operating point?
Q6
Does the floor-first agent skill measurably cut wasted benchmark/profiler invocations vs a baseline agent without the floor gate?
