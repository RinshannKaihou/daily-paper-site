> **一句话总结：** 作者在 Claude Opus 4.8 上以预注册（pre-registered）方式、仅花费 138.76 美元，首次实验确认了市场耦合下 LLM 智能体经济的"信息→财富"容量区域定律（相对增长率等于相对信息量，误差仅 46 毫纳特），同时结构性证伪了"LLM 群体对激励/控制杠杆呈平滑响应"的均场假设——在全部 72 次群体运行中目标弥散度坍缩为零，群体响应表现为跨越支配边界的阶跃函数与种子选择的双稳态。

> **TL;DR:** A pre-registered, $138.76 experiment on Claude Opus 4.8 confirms that in parimutuel-coupled LLM-agent economies, relative wealth growth equals relative claimed information (worst-case 0.0457 nats vs a 0.05 frozen band), coalition value bends exactly where conditional independence predicts, an XOR synergy control flips it supermodular by 0.621 nats (90% of the ideal ln 2 bit), and the best-informed agent absorbs ≥99.9% of the pool in 5/5 market seeds — while the companion mean-field residual-scaling law finds no in-regime domain at all: in all 72 population runs on the (g, γ) grid, goal dispersion collapsed (max V = 4.85 vs a 5.31 floor), making the population a discrete attractor system with step-function and bistable responses rather than a smooth mean-field one.

## 核心贡献

- **首次在前沿 LLM 智能体上预注册并确认了"耦合（市场）容量区域"结构**：在四种感知结构上，parimutuel 差距定律 Ĝa − Ĝb = Îa − Îb 最坏偏差仅 0.0457 纳特（预注册带宽 0.05）；联盟价值在信道条件独立处严格次模、在 XOR 协同控制下翻转为超模（0.621 纳特，达理想 ln 2 的 90%）；联合增长上限 ĜS ≤ H(X) 精确成立；市场选择动态使最优信息智能体吸收几乎全部财富池（5/5 种子中份额 ≥ 99.9%）。
- **对"LLM 群体平滑响应"均场模型的结构性负面结果**：在 3×3 的 (g, γ) 网格 × 8 种子共 72 次运行中，目标弥散度 V 全部坍缩（最大 4.85，中位数 0.00，低于预注册下限 5.31），群体对两个杠杆的响应是跨越支配边界 γ ≷ 12g 的阶跃函数，边界处呈现种子选择的双稳态；与两次小模型实验臂（梯度饱和、完全不响应）共同构成跨能力层级的"三仪器夹击"。
- **一套完整、廉价、可审计的前沿模型预注册实验方法学**：所有预测、接受带宽、决策规则在公开 git 链上先冻结再运行；三次修正均在受影响运行前盲提交；每次 API 调用缓存于 SQLite，分析离线确定性可复现、零成本重跑；全程花费 138.76 美元，带硬性预算上限与逐调用计费。
- **诚实的负面结果报告与机制披露**：克隆等价性（P4b）在吸收式 all-in 押注下失败并被如实披露；P6 因预注册文案漏洞（噪声底定义未预料到空域）而被判形式上 inconclusive，作者明确点名而非用事后阈值替代。

## Key Contributions

- First pre-registered confirmation of the coupled (market) capacity-region structure on frontier LLM agents: the gap law Ĝa − Ĝb = Îa − Îb holds to a worst-case 0.0457 nats (frozen band 0.05), coalition value is submodular exactly where channels are conditionally independent, a designed XOR synergy control flips it supermodular by 0.621 nats (90% of the ideal ln 2 bit), the joint ceiling ĜS ≤ H(X) binds to numerical precision, and Kelly/market selection concentrates ≥99.9% of wealth on the best-informed agent in 5/5 market seeds.
- A structural negative result for smooth mean-field models of LLM populations: goal dispersion collapsed in all 72 grid runs (max V = 4.85, median 0.00 vs a frozen floor of 5.31 and a uniform-scatter reference of 21.25), the response to the incentive lever g and control lever γ is a step function across the dominance boundary γ ≷ 12g, and boundary cells are bistable with seed-selected outcomes — completing a three-instrument bracket (saturation, non-response, concentration) over two capability tiers.
- A complete, cheap, auditable methodology for pre-registered experiments on frontier-model populations: frozen bands applied mechanically by a committed analyzer, three blind amendments each committed before the runs they govern, per-call SQLite caching giving deterministic offline re-runs, per-call spend metering with a hard $160 cap, and public provenance via the commit chain.
- Honest, equal-billing reporting of negatives: P4b (clone equality) fails under absorbing all-in betting dynamics and is disclosed with its mechanism; P6 is formally INCONCLUSIVE due to a pre-registration drafting gap (the noise-floor definition never anticipated an empty regime), and this is named rather than papered over with a post-hoc floor.

## Methodology

The experiment is a single pre-registered study on **Claude Opus 4.8** run under unusually strict discipline: every prediction, acceptance band, protocol gate, and decision rule was committed to a public git repository before the runs it governs (chain a159ca9 → a7e639b in https://github.com/macrokit/value); three amendments were each committed blind to outcomes before the runs they modified; every API call is cached in a SQLite store keyed by the full request, so the analysis is deterministic and re-runnable offline at zero cost; total metered spend was $138.76 against a $160 hard cap (43,074 frontier calls, 16.67M input / 2.13M output tokens).

**Experiment A — the coupled capacity region.** A world state X is three i.i.d. fair bits (8 states, H(X) = 3 ln 2 ≈ 2.0794 nats). Each agent a observes a signal through a perception channel and reports a posterior; its claimed information Îa is the expected KL divergence of reports from the prior. Agents bet wealth across states in proportion to their posteriors in a parimutuel pool, and per-round log growth is Ĝa = E[ln(ba(X)/B(X))]. Five structures are tested: disjoint bits (baseline), overlapping bit-pairs (redundancy), clones (identical signals), 10%-flip noisy sensors, and an XOR synergy control (two individually worthless signals that jointly determine the world). Twelve repeats per elicitation cell are averaged; coalition throughput is defined by coalition-level elicitation (an instance shown all of S's signals) because naive-Bayes product fusion is mathematically incapable of expressing XOR synergy. A live 40-round parimutuel market (ladder of clean / 10%-noisy / 25%-noisy sensors, plus a clones structure) tests wealth selection across 5 seeds.

**Experiment B — the residual-scaling grid.** N = 20 agents occupy niches k ∈ {0,…,15} on a tent landscape with a reward gradient g (peak 12g at kg = 12), a control bonus γ at target niche k* = 0, and a coordination dividend J = 1. The mean-field law under test predicts residual ∝ Vg/γ (log-log exponents (p, q) = (+1, −1)) in a linear-response regime that requires noise-maintained dispersion V ≫ 0. A 3×3 grid g ∈ {0.25, 0.7, 2.0} × γ ∈ {2, 6, 18} × 8 seeds (72 runs) plus two P6 direction points × two arms × 8 seeds (32 runs) is run, with niche labels shown under each agent's own random permutation so token-level label bias cancels. A pre-registered responsiveness capability gate (passed 3/3 for $8.76) licenses the grid, having been designed after two earlier small-model arms (1.5B-parameter) failed the same prediction via saturation and non-response.

## Results & Highlights

- **Gap law (P1, PASSED):** Across all agent pairs in the four fittable structures, the worst deviation between measured growth gap and information gap was **0.0457 nats** (46 millinats), within the **frozen 0.05-nat band**, on gaps whose underlying quantities range up to 2.08 nats — a calibration-free equality between independently measured "claimed information" and "earned growth."
- **XOR synergy control (P2e, PASSED):** Singleton coalition posteriors are exactly uniform (Ĝ{1} = Ĝ{2} = 0.000), while the coalition-level instance reports posteriors worth **Ĝ{1,2} = 0.621 nats**, a supermodular gap of **0.621 ≥ ln 2/2 = 0.347** (90% of the ideal ln 2 = 0.693) — the model reasoned out the parity structure from two individually worthless prose-described observations, not by fusing numbers.
- **Joint ceiling (P3, PASSED):** No coalition exceeded H(X); worst overage was **−7 × 10⁻¹² nats**, with several saturating pairs sitting exactly on the ceiling (ĜS = 2.0794 = 3 ln 2).
- **Live-market wealth selection (P4a, PASSED):** In the 40-round ladder market, the clean-sensor agent's terminal share was **≥ 0.999 in all 5 seeds**, with full wealth ordering by information in 4/5 seeds; the single ordering miss is a tie at zero between the two ruined noisy agents.
- **Dispersion collapse (Experiment B grid):** Across all 72 grid runs the maximum dispersion was **V = 4.85** (median **0.00**) against the frozen regime floor of **5.31** and a uniform-scatter reference of **21.25**; the pre-registered verdict is **P5 = CAP** ("insufficient in-regime span: 0 of 9 conditions").
- **Step-function response:** Control-dominated cells (γ > 12g) sit at exactly **0.000 ± 0.000 residual across all 8 seeds** in three cells (all 20 agents on the target every round); at the boundary (γ, g) = (6, 0.7) the eight per-seed residuals span [10.0, 13.0, 13.0, 12.0, 14.6, 0.3, 10.0, 12.0] — seven seeds at the reward corner, one in near-perfect alignment under an identical condition (bistability).
- **Three-instrument bracket:** The 1.5B/K=8 arm saturated (signal-to-noise 2.63 vs a 3 bar), the 1.5B/K=16 arm showed non-response (free exponents (+0.05, −0.05) vs predicted (+1, −1)), and the frontier gate-proven arm found concentration (V → 0) — no tested LLM population at any capability level holds the noise-maintained dispersion the mean-field law assumes.
- **Disclosed miss (P4b, FAILED):** Clone relative gaps across seeds were [1.0, 0.5, 0.0, 0.0, nan] — 2/4 scoreable seeds pass, below the 4/5 bar; mechanism is absorbing all-in betting composed with provider-default sampling noise.
- **Cost:** Total metered spend **$138.76** (claude-opus-4-8 $136.53 + claude-sonnet-5 debug $2.23) of a $160 cap; capability gate alone $8.76 of a $30 cap.

## Authors & Credibility

Single author, Cheng Qian, listed as an Independent Researcher, self-funded ($138.76 in metered API spend with no external compute, grants, or institutional resources). The two predictions under test are drawn from the author's own companion synthesis paper (Qian, 2026, arXiv:2606.12502), so the work is partly self-validating the author's theory — a potential conflict of interest that the pre-registration discipline is explicitly designed to counter. The methodology is unusually rigorous for an ML paper: a frozen public git chain, blind amendments, cached determinism, a mechanical analyzer that re-derives every number offline, and full disclosure of gate firings and the one failed supporting prediction. The referenced baseline literature (Kelly 1956, Breiman 1961, Barron & Cover 1988, Blume & Easley 1992, Sandroni 2000) is the canonical information-theoretic / market-selection corpus, appropriately cited.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely novel in two respects — (a) it is the first pre-registered, quantitative (in nats) test of coupled information-growth laws on frontier LLM agents, and (b) it reports a structural "domain not found" result for mean-field population models, which has direct governance implications. The novelty is in the empirical test and its discipline, not the underlying laws themselves (which come from the author's companion theory).
- **Rigor:** High. Pre-registration with frozen acceptance bands is rare in ML; the three blind amendments, the mechanical analyzer committed before runs, and the SQLite per-call cache (19.9MB, keyed by full request) make every number auditable and every model response individually inspectable. The gate-proven-instrument design (a cheap responsiveness gate that licenses the expensive grid) is methodologically sound and explicitly designed to avoid repeating the two earlier instrument failures.
- **Reproducibility:** Excellent and arguably the paper's strongest dimension. `python3 sim/stage2/analyze_stage2.py` reproduces every number offline deterministically at zero cost; a mock backend allows full dry runs without an API key; the live re-run cost is documented at ≈$139. This is a model for how cheap, reproducible frontier-model science can be done.
- **Significance:** Moderate-to-high for the multi-agent-safety and LLM-economics communities. The capacity-region confirmation ties a measurable epistemic quantity to a measurable economic one at millinat precision; the attractor-dynamics negative directly challenges the "a bit more oversight buys a bit more alignment" intuition that underlies much multi-agent-safety reasoning (Critch & Krueger 2020; Hammond et al. 2025).
- **Red flags / weaknesses worth naming:**
  - **Single vendor, single model, single tier.** Every verdict token came from claude-opus-4-8; both results are statements about this instrument class until cross-vendor replicated. The paper is upfront about this as the "open arm" of the program.
  - **Toy scale.** N = 12–20 agents, T = 8–18 rounds, 8-state worlds, no take, no strategic bet-shading, no entry/exit. The author concedes these are "the smallest systems in which the predictions are well-posed — not markets, not deployments." Extrapolating the attractor-dynamics conclusion to real deployments is not licensed by this data.
  - **Unincentivized elicitation.** Part A asks for stated posteriors without paying agents for calibration; the gap law held with Î measured from statements and Ĝ from the same statements' market consequences, but a fully incentive-compatible elicitation is left to future work — a self-consistency loop that could mask systematic miscalibration.
  - **The "negative" is technically a CAP, not a falsification.** The author is admirably precise about this ("retired to its mathematical scope — domain not found, not disproved"), but readers should note the mean-field law is neither confirmed nor refuted; the experiment shows the regime is empty for these populations, which a modified design (explicit exploration incentives, heterogeneous objectives) could in principle revive.
  - **P6 drafting gap.** The pre-registered noise-floor definition σ_in√2/8 never anticipated an empty regime, so P6 is INCONCLUSIVE by the rule's letter. Disclosed, but it is a flaw in the pre-registration itself.
  - **P4b genuine failure.** Clone equality fails under absorbing all-in dynamics; the disclosed mechanism (sampling noise composed with absorbing wealth dynamics) is plausible and likely generic, but it means one of the two live-market predictions did not hold.
  - **Self-citation dependency.** The experiment validates predictions from the author's own companion theory; while the paper is self-contained and the laws tested predate the runs via the frozen chain, the framing privileges the author's framework.

## Strengths

- Pre-registration discipline with frozen, mechanically applied acceptance bands is rare and commendable in ML/AI research.
- Full reproducibility at near-zero cost: cached per-call SQLite store, committed raw outputs, deterministic offline analyzer, public commit chain proving every prediction precedes its data.
- Equal billing for the positive (capacity-region confirmation) and negative (attractor-dynamics) results; the paper explicitly argues the confirmation is only credible because the program publishes its negatives.
- Mechanistic explanations for every measured deviation (the binding worst-case gap-law cell, the XOR reasoning, the P4b absorbing-pathology, the J-granularity systematic), rather than hand-waving.
- The capability-gate design (cheap responsiveness check that licenses the expensive grid, after two prior instruments failed) is a transferable methodological pattern.
- The XOR synergy control is the sharpest single result: agents reasoned out parity from prose, achieving 90% of the ideal information bit through coalition-level elicitation that naive-Bayes product fusion provably cannot express.

## Limitations

- Single vendor / single model family / one frontier tier (Claude Opus 4.8 only); cross-vendor replication is the explicit open arm.
- Toy scale (N = 12–20, T = 8–18, 8-state worlds, no take/strategic-shading/entry-exit) — not markets or deployments.
- Unincentivized elicitation: agents are not paid for calibration, leaving a self-consistency gap between stated and acted beliefs.
- Population-dynamics caveats: niche-economy prompt-γ calibration is absorbed into the fit constant; the J = 1 coordination dividend produces a pre-stated ±1–3 niche coarseness systematic; ~3 runs double-sampled across a machine migration (disclosed, cache-replayed).
- The negative result is a "domain not found" CAP, not a falsification — a modified design creating sustained dispersion could revive the question, but would test a modified theory.
- P4b (clone equality) genuinely fails; P6 is formally INCONCLUSIVE due to a pre-registration drafting gap.

## Relevance

对于关注 LLM 多智能体系统、智能体经济模拟与多智能体安全（multi-agent safety）的研究者，本文提供了前沿模型上少见的、可量化、可复现的实证规律：它既把"智能体知道什么 ↔ 智能体赚到什么"用纳特级精度钉死，又直接挑战了"群体对激励/监管杠杆呈平滑响应"这一被多智能体安全论证广泛默认的假设，对评估与治理多智能体部署有方法论与概念上的双重启发。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the coupled capacity-region confirmation replicate on a different frontier model family (e.g., GPT-class, Gemini-class) and at a different capability tier, using the unchanged protocol and analyzer against an OpenAI-compatible endpoint?
- Would fractional Kelly betting or wealth floors in the live market restore clone equality (P4b) without introducing a different artifact, and does the gap law still hold under such modified betting rules?
- Can a deliberately modified niche-economy design (explicit exploration incentives, heterogeneous agent objectives, or stochastic payout perturbations) sustain the noise-maintained dispersion V ≫ 0 required to actually enter the mean-field linear-response regime — and if so, do the free log-log exponents then match (p, q) = (+1, −1)?
- Is the attractor-switching / bistability finding robust to scale (N ≫ 20, longer horizons, non-uniform resource weights), or does it dissolve into a smoother response surface at deployment-relevant scale?
- How does the measured fact that elicited joint posteriors are not the product of elicited singletons (max deviation 0.466 nats even under conditional independence) propagate into multi-agent belief-aggregation schemes that assume independence?
- Can the governance corollary — "an intervention either crosses an attractor boundary or does nothing measurable" — be operationalized into a concrete protocol for evaluating multi-agent-safety interventions by basin rather than by marginal strength?
