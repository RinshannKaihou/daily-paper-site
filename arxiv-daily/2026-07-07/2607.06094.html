Modeling Normal Is All You Need: Joint Latent Clustering for Anomaly Detection in Multimodal Cyber-Physical Systems

Alexander Apartsin  ·  Yehudit Aperstein
anomaly detection cyber-physical systems time series unsupervised multimodal

Drop the reconstruction term,
score only in the latent mixture.

LatAD models CPS normal behavior as a jointly-learned latent + explicit GMM (VaDE), then detects anomalies purely in the latent — converting VaDE's reconstruction liability into a win on the hardest correlation/dynamics faults.

Key metric
0.831 vs 0.467+0.364
HAI difficult-subset AUROC — LatAD over TranAD, the paper's largest margin. All three deep SOTA baselines collapse on the difficult subset once point adjustment is removed.
Why this matters
Multimodal normal modeling, reconstruction-dropping, and point-adjustment-free evaluation transfer directly to building robust anomaly-detection benchmarks and fair evaluation pipelines.
Critical edge
The SKAB win is thin (0.610 vs 0.591, single seed) on a contaminated dataset, and the difficulty split hinges on one arbitrary 99th-percentile cut with no sensitivity analysis.
01

Methodology

VaDE · latent-only scoring · auto-gated heads

Representation (VaDE). An encoder maps each window feature vector x to a latent Gaussian; the prior is a Gaussian mixture (K=80) trained by minimising the negative ELBO. Three anti-collapse measures — KL/cluster warm-up annealing β, a DAGMM-style variance floor plus Σσ_c⁻² penalty, and a slower LR on mixture parameters — keep the modes alive.

Why reconstruction is dropped. Table 2 shows the whitened residual ½r⊤Σ⁻¹r sits at/below chance on correlation-break faults (WADI 0.490, SKAB 0.457) because a flexible decoder faithfully rebuilds in-range broken correlations. The latent NLL carries the signal — so reconstruction is explicitly discarded and scoring lives entirely in the latent z=μ(x).

Scoring stack. A high-K diagonal mixture-density head (NLL) captures thin non-Gaussian fringes; a nearest-component NLL (not π-weighted) protects rare-but-valid modes from being penalised for rarity. Two optional heads — a responsibility-weighted whitened residual (Ledoit–Wolf) and a basin-agreement rescue — are auto-gated from train-normal signals alone, so the single config VaDE-hard+resid(auto) adapts per dataset (residual on for HAI, basin on for SKAB, both off for WADI) with no test-set tuning.

Robustness note

Re-scoring full-resolution WADI at all ten phase offsets (560 anomaly windows, 10× larger, no retraining) yields essentially unchanged Difficult AUROC 0.722 (vs 0.726 downsampled) — ruling out a thin-sample fluke. The trivial max|z| baseline is near-perfect on Easy (SKAB 1.000, HAI 0.966) but near chance on Difficult (0.31–0.39), validating the split.

Strengths & Limitations

side-by-side

Strengths

  • Mechanistic, assumption-driven design — every choice traces to a named MIIM assumption (A1–A10).
  • Table 2 isolates reconstruction's harm on difficult faults: a crisp, citable negative result with a clear mechanism.
  • Auto-gating from train-normal alone yields one config that wins three structurally different datasets.
  • Evaluation hygiene is exemplary: no point adjustment, difficulty stratification, prevalence matching, honest SOTA re-scoring.
  • Advantage tracks multimodality (HAI/WADI large, SKAB slim) — exactly the thesis's predicted signature.

Limitations

  • Three datasets only, one model each, several single-seed comparisons; multi-seed CIs are future work.
  • F1/FPR use a disclosed oracle best-F1 threshold; only AUROC is threshold-free.
  • SKAB is contaminated and its margin (0.610 vs 0.591) is fragile; vibration faults need a raw-waveform model.
  • Difficulty split hinges on one parameter (99th-pct cut); no sensitivity analysis across percentiles.
  • No public code/data release; latent dim (via A5), LRs, R, λ₀, δ under-specified.
  • Trajectory assumptions A9–A10 untested — benchmarks lack path-dependent faults; GDN run on WADI only.

Open Questions

for further exploration
  1. Does "drop reconstruction, score only in the latent" generalise beyond VaDE — to DAGMM or normalising-flow detectors — or is it specific to a flexible VAE decoder? generalise
  2. How sensitive are the margins to the difficulty-split percentile? A sweep over 95/97/99/99.5th would test whether the Easy/Difficult gap is knife-edge. sensitivity
  3. Can a synthetic or newly-collected CPS dataset with genuine path-dependent faults exercise the untested trajectory assumptions A9–A10? data
  4. Given the slim SKAB margin (0.610 vs 0.591), does the win survive averaging over ≥5 seeds? rigor
  5. Would an adversarial-perturbation or mode-flip-count formulation beat Gaussian perturbations for the basin-agreement rescue? method
  6. Could the train-normal auto-gating principle transfer to TranAD-style detectors to repair their difficult-subset collapse? transfer
