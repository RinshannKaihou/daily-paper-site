> **一句话总结：** 本文将 Yang 的张量程序（Netsor）的定性无穷宽高斯过程极限推广为定量的版本，给出有限宽网络执行与其无穷宽高斯过程极限之间阶为 O(Σ 1/√m)（m 取遍隐藏宽度）的显式 Wasserstein 误差界，且该框架与架构无关，覆盖 MLP、含权重共享的 RNN 以及扩展到 NetsorK 后的注意力层。

> **TL;DR:** The paper proves a quantitative central-limit theorem for tensor (Netsor) programs: the finite-width execution of a random neural network converges to its infinite-width Gaussian-process limit in Wasserstein distance at rate O(Σ_{m∈n_hidd} 1/√m), an architecture-agnostic bound covering MLPs, weight-sharing RNNs, and (via the NetsorK extension with kernel/scalar variables) attention layers; numerically, sliced-Wasserstein-1 (SW1) output-law convergence slopes of −0.721 (shallow MLP), −0.916 (deep MLP L=4), −0.797 (residual net), and −0.579 (unrolled RNN) all lie below the N=5000 sampling floor of −1/2, and the MLP specialization recovers the Basteri–Trevisan O((Σ 1/√n_i)) baseline bound.

## 核心贡献

- **定量的张量程序 CLT（Theorem 1.2）：** 给出有限宽 Netsor 程序执行与其无穷宽高斯过程执行之间的显式 Wasserstein 误差界，阶为 `c·Σ_{m∈n_hidd} 1/√m`，常数 `c` 仅依赖结构参数（输入维度、激活、阶数 p），与隐藏宽度无关。这是对 Yang 主定理（仅给出定性 LLN）的定量补充。
- **核大数定律（Corollary 1.3）：** 证明由 H-vars 构成的经验核 `hᵀh'/n` 以同阶 `O(Σ1/√m)` 收敛于无穷宽核 `h̄*h̄'`，且它既是定理的推论又是归纳证明的关键输入（通过截断程序避免循环论证）。
- **架构无关性与权重共享覆盖：** 框架天然处理同一权重矩阵在多层或多个输入分支中复用的情况，从而覆盖全连接 MLP、时间展开 RNN（Algorithm 3，复用 Wₓ、Wₕ）、残差网络（Algorithm 6）等，而逐层独立 CLT 无法处理这类依赖。
- **NetsorK 扩展覆盖注意力（Theorem 2.4）：** 引入带标量核变量（Kernel）、标量参数化非线性（NonLin+）和标量映射（Scal）的 NetsorK 语言，在 G-vars 非退化假设下将主定理推广到含 LayerNorm 与 Softmax 的单头注意力层（Algorithm 5）。
- **数值验证：** 用 N=5000 独立网络的集成采样、Gauss–Hermite 计算极限矩、Sliced-Wasserstein-1 距离，在四种架构上验证收敛并给出 log-log 拟合斜率（Table 1）。

## Key Contributions

- **A quantitative CLT for tensor programs (Theorem 1.2).** For any Netsor program with Lipschitz nonlinearities, the joint law of the scaled H-vars `(h^(ℓ)/√n_ℓ)` is within `c·Σ_{m∈n_hidd} 1/√m` in p-Wasserstein distance of the infinite-width Gaussian-process execution. This is the first explicit finite-width error bound stated at the level of generality of tensor programs, upgrading Yang's qualitative master theorem to a quantitative Wasserstein estimate.
- **A companion kernel law of large numbers (Corollary 1.3).** Empirical kernels `n⁻¹ hᵀh'` built from program variables converge to their deterministic infinite-width limits at the same `O(Σ1/√m)` rate over `n_hidd ∪ n_out`. The corollary is both a consequence of and an induction ingredient for the main theorem (circularity avoided by always invoking it on the truncated program).
- **Architecture-agnostic treatment of weight sharing.** Because the proof tracks the conditional Gaussian structure per A-var across all its MatMul uses, the bound covers architectures with shared weights (tied-weight RNNs, joint evaluation on multiple inputs, residual nets) where a layer-by-layer independent CLT breaks down.
- **NetsorK extension to attention (Theorem 2.4).** A restricted extension adding scalar kernel variables and scalar-parametric nonlinearities (locally Lipschitz with linear growth, Eq. 2.5) lets the same inductive coupling argument reach LayerNorm + softmax single-head attention, while keeping analytic assumptions transparent and checkable.
- **Proof technique: line-by-line induction with explicit Gaussian coupling.** The argument combines structure lemmas (3.5/3.6) that express conditional means/covariances as finite-dimensional functions of Gram matrices, a non-degeneracy reduction (Lemma 3.9) to make those functions differentiable at the infinite-width Gram point, Rosenthal's inequality for kernel concentration, and coupling finite/infinite-width conditionally Gaussian laws via shared Gaussian noise.

## Methodology

The paper is a rigorous probability-theory work; the "method" is the proof strategy for Theorems 1.2 and 2.4.

1. **Program model.** A Netsor program is a sequence of lines built from inputs (fixed H-vars `x_k` and random A-var matrices `W ~ N(0, 1/m)`), combined via `MatMul` (g = Wh producing G-vars that behave Gaussian) and `NonLin` (h = ϕ(h₁,…,h_k)). Two executions are defined: a *finite-width* one (standard forward pass) and an *infinite-width* one where each MatMul is replaced by a Gaussian whose cross-covariance is given by the `*` product `h̄*h̄' = m⁻¹E[h̄ᵀh̄']` (Eq. 1.3–1.4).
2. **Main bound (Theorem 1.2).** Under Lipschitz nonlinearities, for every p ≥ 1 there is a structural constant c with
   `W_p( (h^(ℓ)/√n_ℓ)_ℓ , (h̄^(ℓ)/√n_ℓ)_ℓ ) ≤ c · Σ_{m∈n_hidd} 1/√m`.
   The hidden widths `n_hidd` are exactly those dimensions that appear in a chain of matrix contractions.
3. **Induction on program lines.** The proof proceeds line by line. NonLin lines are handled by Lipschitz stability; MatMul lines use the fact that, conditioned on previous H-vars, both executions are Gaussian with means/covariances that the structure lemmas (3.5/3.6) show are finite-dimensional functions of the parent H-var Gram matrices. The two conditional Gaussians are coupled using the *same* standard Gaussian noise, leaving an error controlled by the difference of conditional means and covariance square-roots.
4. **Kernel LLN as the engine (Corollary 1.3).** Before the MatMul step at line r one invokes the theorem only on the truncated program up to r−1, which — combined with Rosenthal's inequality for the i.i.d. coordinates of the infinite-width execution — yields the kernel LLN needed to control the Gram-matrix differences driving the conditional-parameter error. This simultaneous-but-non-circular use closes the induction.
5. **Non-degeneracy reduction (Lemma 3.9 / Prop. 3.11).** Degenerate MatMul lines (where the infinite-width conditional covariance is singular) are replaced by equivalent NonLin lines, so that, after reduction, the Gram-matrix functions are differentiable at the infinite-width Gram point and Lemma 3.1 (a differentiability-based concentration bound) applies.
6. **NetsorK extension (Section 2.2 / 4.3).** Scalar C-variables are random at finite width but deterministic constants at infinite width; they are controlled directly by the kernel LLN, and downstream scalar-parametric nonlinearities must satisfy a local Lipschitz bound with linear growth (Eq. 2.5) — satisfied by softmax attention output and LayerNorm (with constant depending on ε). The extension requires an *explicit non-degeneracy assumption* on the G-vars, because the pointwise lifting of infinite-width linear identities used in Prop. 3.11 fails when identities depend on empirical-kernel fluctuations.
7. **Numerics (Section 5).** Infinite-width moments computed by deterministic Gauss–Hermite quadrature. Convergence of the output law measured by Sliced-Wasserstein-1 (SW1 ≤ W1, avoids curse of dimensionality) between N=5000 independent-network readouts and the analytic limiting Gaussian distribution. Empirical exponents estimated by least-squares fits of `log d_n = α + β log n`. A sampling-noise analysis (Eq. 5.2) gives `SW1(μ̂_{n,N}, μ∞) ≤ c₂/n + c₁/√N`, predicting a visible architectural signal only for widths n ≲ √N ≈ 70 and a flattening toward −1/2 beyond.

## Results & Highlights

- **Main rate (Theorem 1.2):** finite-width vs. infinite-width executions of any Netsor program with Lipschitz activations are within `O(Σ_{m∈n_hidd} 1/√m)` in p-Wasserstein distance, uniformly in the hidden widths; the constant depends only on structural parameters (Eq. 1.5).
- **Kernel LLN rate (Corollary 1.3):** `|n⁻¹ hᵀh' − h̄*h̄'|` in Lp is bounded by `c·Σ_{m∈n_hidd ∪ n_out} 1/√m`.
- **Recovery of prior bound:** specializing Theorem 1.2 to the G-vars of a deep fully connected network recovers the Basteri–Trevisan [BT22] bound `W_p(g^(ℓ)/√n_ℓ , G) ≤ c·Σ_{i=1}^{M−1} 1/√n_i` (Eq. 2.2); the authors note Trevisan [Tre23] later improved the fully-connected rate to `O((min_i n_i)^{−1/2})`.
- **Attention coverage (Theorem 2.4):** under the non-degeneracy assumption, the same `O(Σ 1/√m)` Wasserstein bound holds for NetsorK programs, which formalize a single-head attention layer (Algorithm 5) with LayerNorm and softmax.
- **Table 1 — empirical SW1 log-log slopes (N=5000, predicted sampling floor −1/2):** Shallow MLP **−0.721**, Deep MLP L=4 **−0.916**, Residual network **−0.797**, Time-unrolled RNN **−0.579**. All four slopes lie strictly below −1/2, which the paper cites as confirmation that the architectural convergence signal is visible above the empirical sampling floor; all lie in the (−1, −1/2) interval expected from the `O(n⁻¹) + O(N⁻¹/²)` mixture in Eq. 5.2.
- **Sampling-floor prediction:** with N=5000 ensemble samples, the `N⁻¹/²` estimation-noise term is predicted to dominate the architectural signal for widths n > √N ≈ 70, consistent with the flattening observed in Figure 7.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are established researchers in the mathematical theory of neural networks and scaling limits. **Andrea Agazzi** (Institute for Mathematical Statistics and Actuarial Sciences, University of Bern) and **Dario Trevisan** (Department of Mathematics, University of Pisa) are co-authors of related quantitative-CLT and stochastic-scaling-limit works, including the directly relevant [BT22] (Basteri–Trevisan, the bound this paper recovers/generalizes) and [MAT26] (quantitative convergence of *trained* single-layer networks to GPs). **Eloy Mosig García** (PhD student, University of Pisa) is a co-author of [MAT26]. The group is therefore one of the most natural and credible originators of this exact generalization, and [BT22]/[Tre23] (the strongest prior quantitative results, which this work strictly extends to a richer architecture class) are their own. The work cites and correctly positions itself relative to the canonical references: Neal [Nea96], Lee et al. [Lee+20], Jacot et al. [JGH20], and Yang's Tensor Programs series [Yan20a; Yan20b; Yan21; YH20; YH21].

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely new. Prior quantitative CLTs [BT22; Tre23; Fav+25; BFF25; EMS21] were restricted to fully connected feed-forward networks where a layer-by-layer independent CLT suffices; the key obstacle this paper removes is the *conditional* Gaussian structure required when a single A-var is reused across lines (as in RNNs, multi-input evaluation, residual nets). Relative to Yang's master theorem [Yan21, Thm 5.4], the trade is explicitness for scope: Yang covers a much broader program class qualitatively, while this work gives a quantitative Wasserstein estimate under stronger (Lipschitz) regularity. The honest framing of this trade-off is a strength.
- **Rigor:** High. Full proofs are given for all stated results (Section 4 + Appendices), the inductive argument is carefully de-circularized, and the non-degeneracy reduction (Lemma 3.9/Prop. 3.11) and Greville-pseudoinverse machinery are handled correctly. The conditional Gaussian formulas (Lemmas 3.5/3.6) are the load-bearing technical novelty and are spelled out.
- **Sharpness of the rate — a real caveat.** The proven rate `O(Σ_{m∈n_hidd} 1/√m)` is *weaker* than the `O((min n)^{−1/2})` rate known for fully connected nets [Tre23; Fav+25], which the authors themselves acknowledge (§2, Eq. 2.2 discussion). Moreover the structural constant `c` is existential ("there exists c < ∞"), not made explicit in terms of the program's Lipschitz constants or depth — so the bound is qualitative-quantitative (right rate in n, unspecified prefactor). This limits its direct use as a finite-size approximation guarantee.
- **Restrictiveness vs. Yang:** The Lipschitz assumption on NonLin activations excludes ReLU's exact treatment (ReLU is Lipschitz, so it is technically covered, but e.g. unbounded-growth or discontinuous nonlinearities used in some architectures are not), and the local-Lipschitz-with-linear-growth condition (2.5) for NetsorK is a genuine restriction — softmax attention output satisfies it only with constant C=1, and LayerNorm only with a constant depending on the ε floor. The non-degeneracy assumption for Theorem 2.4 is stated explicitly because the reduction proof breaks down for scalar-kernel-dependent identities; this is honestly flagged but means the attention result is conditional on a property the paper does not verify holds for realistic attention programs.
- **Numerical evidence — supportive but not theorem-strength.** The experiments measure *output-law* convergence only (not the full joint Wasserstein bound of Theorem 1.2), and the authors are explicit that for non-fully-connected architectures the experiments "should be interpreted as evidence of the finite-width convergence predicted by the present theorem, rather than as a theorem-level claim of an O(n⁻¹) rate." The four SW1 slopes all fall in (−1, −1/2), which is *consistent* with the theoretical `O(n⁻¹) + O(N⁻¹/²)` mixture (Eq. 5.2) but, because they are blends of signal and sampling floor, they neither confirm the proven `O(Σ1/√m)` rate sharply nor reach the known `O(n⁻¹)` ceiling. There is **no ablation** isolating width from sampling noise (e.g., no scaling of N with n to suppress the floor), and **no comparison** to the existing [BT22]/[Tre23] rates on the architectures where those apply.
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate-to-good. Algorithms 1–6 specify the architectures concretely; infinite-width moments use deterministic Gauss–Hermite quadrature (reproducible); SW1 over N=5000 ensembles is a standard computation. However, hyperparameters for the architectures (input dimension d, input distributions, exact width grid used for the fits) and code are not provided in the paper.
- **Red flags:** None severe. No cherry-picked benchmarks (there are no SOTA comparisons to game). The main risk is *overclaiming by proximity*: the title and abstract emphasize the transformer-relevant scope, but the attention result (Theorem 2.4) carries an unverified non-degeneracy hypothesis, and the basic Netsor language does *not* by itself cover softmax attention without the NetsorK extension. A casual reader could over-estimate how decisively transformers are covered.

## Strengths

- First quantitative (finite-width, explicit-in-n) Gaussian-process convergence result stated at the generality of tensor programs, genuinely extending the quantitative-CLT literature beyond fully connected nets.
- Clean, reusable proof template: line-by-line induction + conditional-Gaussian coupling + structure lemmas + non-degeneracy reduction + Rosenthal. The kernel LLN is elegantly made both consequence and ingredient without circularity.
- Honest, precise positioning against Yang's master theorem and the [BT22]/[Tre23]/[Fav+25] quantitative line, including an open acknowledgment that the proven rate is weaker than the FC-only state of the art.
- Architecture-agnostic bound naturally inherits to MLPs, RNNs (weight sharing), residual nets, and (with NetsorK) attention — all from one theorem.
- Numerical section is upfront that non-FC results are corroborating evidence, not rate proofs, and provides a principled sampling-noise analysis (Eq. 5.2) explaining why slopes lie in (−1, −1/2).

## Limitations

- **Rate is not sharp:** `O(Σ 1/√m)` is worse than the `O((min n)^{−1/2})` known for fully connected networks [Tre23; Fav+25]; closing this gap for the tensor-program setting is left open.
- **Non-explicit constants:** the prefactor `c` depends only on structural parameters but is not given in closed form (no dependence on Lipschitz constants, depth, or program length is quantified), limiting use as a concrete finite-size approximation certificate.
- **Stronger regularity than Yang:** requires Lipschitz NonLin and local-Lipschitz-with-linear-growth (2.5) for NetsorK; excludes some nonlinearities admissible under the qualitative master theorem, and the constant for LayerNorm depends on the ε floor.
- **Conditional attention result:** Theorem 2.4 assumes G-var non-degeneracy in the infinite-width limit, a property the paper does not verify for actual attention programs; the non-degeneracy reduction (Prop. 3.11) is explicitly noted to fail when identities depend on empirical scalar-kernel fluctuations.
- **NTK regime only:** the framework addresses the forward pass / GP (NTK) regime and explicitly excludes the feature-learning regime [YH21]; backward pass and training dynamics beyond initialization are out of scope (only [MAT26]-style shallow trained cases are referenced).
- **Experiments are output-law only:** no validation of the full joint Wasserstein bound, no width-vs-sampling-noise ablation (N is fixed at 5000), and no head-to-head comparison with [BT22]/[Tre23] on FC nets where sharper rates are available.
- No released code or detailed hyperparameter table in the paper.

## Relevance

对研究"大模型/无限宽网络理论与高斯过程极限"方向的读者高度相关：它把 Yang 张量程序框架下仅有的定性无穷宽结论升级为定量 Wasserstein 误差界，是理解任意架构（含 RNN、注意力）有限宽逼近 GP 极限速率的核心理论进展，可作为后续研究更精细速率、特征学习机制或可验证有限宽近似的理论基础。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the `O(Σ 1/√m)` rate be sharpened to `O((min n_hidd)^{−1/2})` — matching [Tre23] for FC — at the tensor-program level, and what structural property of the program governs which rate is attainable?
- Under what concrete, checkable conditions does the G-var non-degeneracy assumption of Theorem 2.4 actually hold for a realistic multi-head, multi-layer transformer (and does it persist across blocks with tied weights)?
- Can the existential structural constant `c` be made explicit as a function of Lipschitz constants, program length L, and depth, yielding a usable finite-width approximation certificate?
- Does the inductive coupling argument extend to the fluctuation (`1/√n`) kernel scaling mentioned in Remark 2.3, giving a quantitative CLT (rather than LLN) for the scalar kernel variables used in softmax attention?
- How do these bounds behave for the *backward* pass / NTK and, ultimately, for the trained network along positive training time (toward a tensor-program analogue of [MAT26])?
- Would a width-dependent ensemble size N(n) (rather than the fixed N=5000) let the experiments separate the architectural signal from the sampling floor and resolve whether the empirical rate reaches the `n⁻¹` ceiling?
