> **一句话总结：** 本文通过"同一步骤拆批对照实验"证明 GaLore 类低秩优化器所投影的 top-r 梯度子空间在约 39/128 个方向之外是统计上不可识别的——每次刷新近乎正交的"旋转"其实是估计噪声而非真实漂移；并据此给出可操作的工程处方：用旋转矩阵搬运优化器状态（一阶矩精确、二阶矩经平方项映射）并把 β2 从 0.999 降到 0.99，在 Pythia-1B 上以默认 β2 达到 18.73 困惑度，优于未搬运 GaLore 最佳 β2 修正后的 19.28。

> **TL;DR:** Using a same-step split-batch control on Pythia-160M, the authors show GaLore's recomputed top-r gradient subspace is statistically non-identifiable beyond ~39 of 128 directions: the per-refresh rotation saturates 96.7–99.6% of the geometric maximum √(2r) because it is estimator noise (two simultaneous estimates disagree 0.725√(2r) vs 0.742√(2r) across a full refresh), the gradient spectrum has no gap at r (adjacent ratio ≈1.005), and N-fold averaging shrinks the tail only as N^{-1/4}; consequently basis averaging cannot help (0.36 PPL span over four decades), while transporting optimizer state through the rotation (LDAdam) at default β2=0.999 reaches 18.73 vs untransported GaLore's best β2 fix of 19.28 perplexity at Pythia-1B, and lowering β2 to 0.99 helps every refreshing optimizer but reverses in a full-rank control.

## 核心贡献

- **测量了 GaLore 家族未经验证的核心前提**：通过"同一步骤拆批对照"（在同一训练步把一个 batch 拆成两半分别做 SVD）证明，GaLore 每 T 步重算的 top-r 子空间在 r=128 时只有约 k*≈39 个方向是可复现的，其余方向随样本重抽；所谓的"每刷新旋转一次"在量级上（0.725√(2r) vs 0.742√(2r)）等于两个同时估计之间的分歧，即"旋转"主要是估计噪声而非稳定对象的运动。
- **从谱结构给出根因，并证明平均化无法修复**：梯度谱在 r 处无间隙（相邻奇异值比 ≈1.005），深层尾部是幂律信号（指数 α=1.03–1.35），在 N 倍平均下仅以 N^{-1/4}（而非纯噪声的 N^{-1/2}）收缩；因此任何有限平均预算都无法在 r 处打开间隙，使子空间变得良定义。
- **跨架构/跨规模/跨模态验证非可识别性**：在 7 个 Pythia 规模（70M–6.9B）、GPT-2-large、Qwen2.5-3B、Llama-3-8B 及 ViT-B（CIFAR-10）上重复同一探针，同一步分歧稳定在 0.53–0.83 倍 √(2r)，且随规模增强（k* 从 70M 的 50 降到 6.9B 的 21）；由于这些模型都是全秩 Adam 训练的，证明非可识别性是梯度本身的属性，而非 GaLore 投影的产物。
- **解释 GaLore 为何仍然有效**：重新定位 GaLore 为"旋转的能量捕获投影器"而非"子空间跟踪器"——在无间隙的近各向同性尾部中，任何包含主导尖峰并采样尾部的 r 个方向捕获的能量分数近乎相同（1B 训练中 γg≈0.67–0.73），所以下降方向能在重抽中存活。
- **给出有理论支撑的工程处方并做受控验证**：一阶矩经旋转 R=U_new^⊤ U_old 精确搬运，二阶矩经 R 的平方项搬运，无视 R 的盲搬运在 Haar 模型下可证明差 (r−k*)/2 倍；在 Pythia-1B/40K 的三种子 2×2 受控实验中，搬运+默认 β2 达 18.73，优于未搬运最佳 β2 修正的 19.28；同时把 β2 降到 0.99 对所有刷新型优化器有效，但全秩对照中偏好反转，从而把效果隔离到周期性重抽这一原因上。
- **提出可移植的诊断探针 k\***：在信任任何 top-r 梯度子空间之前，拆批两次算子空间、数一致方向；若 r > k*，多余方向是每刷新都被重抽的噪声，正确做法是搬运状态而非寻找更好的跟踪器。

## Key Contributions

- **Measures the unverified premise of the GaLore family.** A same-step split-batch control (split one batch into two disjoint halves and SVD each at the identical step) shows that only k*≈39 of r=128 directions are reproducible at Pythia-160M; the rest reshuffle with the sample. The per-refresh "rotation" (0.742√(2r)) is statistically indistinguishable from the disagreement between two simultaneous estimates (0.725√(2r)), so the rotation is estimator noise, not the motion of a stable object.
- **Traces the cause to a gapless spectrum and proves averaging cannot fix it.** The per-matrix gradient spectrum has no gap at r (adjacent ratio ≈1.005), the deep tail is power-law signal (α=1.03–1.35) that shrinks only as N^{-1/4} under N-fold averaging rather than the N^{-1/2} of pure noise, so no finite averaging budget opens a gap. Basis averaging over 10,000 steps at 1B still leaves the refresh-to-refresh basis change at 84% of maximal and perplexity flat within 0.36 over four decades.
- **Demonstrates generality across architectures, scales, and domains.** The same probe on seven Pythia scales (70M–6.9B), GPT-2-large, Qwen2.5-3B, Llama-3-8B, and a ViT-B on CIFAR-10 gives same-step disagreement of 0.53–0.83 of √(2r) with no spectral gap at r; the effect strengthens with scale (k* falls from 50 at 70M to 21 at 6.9B). All models are full-rank-Adam-pretrained, so non-identifiability is a property of the gradient, not of GaLore.
- **Reconciles why GaLore still works.** GaLore is reframed as a rotating energy-capture projector: because the tail past the small dominant spike is smooth and near-isotropic, any r directions that include the spike and sample the tail capture a comparable energy fraction (γg≈0.67–0.73 during 1B training), so the descent direction survives the redraw even though the frame turns over.
- **Delivers a theory-backed prescription with a controlled test.** The first moment transports exactly through R=U_new^⊤ U_old (the MMSE linear map under isotropic gradients); the second moment transports through the squared entries of R; blind carry is provably (r−k*)/2 suboptimal under a Haar rotation model (Weingarten bound). A three-seed 2×2 at Pythia-1B/40K shows transport at default β2 reaches 18.73 PPL, beating untransported GaLore after its best β2 fix (19.28), and the two remedies are partially independent.
- **Isolates the redraw as the cause of the β2 preference.** Lowering β2 from 0.999 to 0.99 helps every refreshing optimizer tested (LDAdam 57.69→31.27 at 10K; canonical GaLore 21.27→19.04 at 40K), while a full-rank AdamW control with no refresh reverses the preference back to 0.999 (97.9 vs 118.0), tying the effect to the periodic redraw rather than to training in general.
- **Proposes a portable diagnostic, k\*.** Before trusting a top-r gradient subspace, split a batch, compute the subspace twice, and count agreeing directions; if r > k*, the excess is noise that will be redrawn at every refresh, and the fix is transported state, not a better tracker.

## Methodology

The paper studies optimizers that run Adam inside a periodically recomputed top-r left-singular subspace of the gradient (GaLore and its family — LDAdam, SubTrack++, FRUGAL, GoLore, APOLLO, Q-GaLore), explicitly excluding LoRA-style weight parameterizations. The central methodological innovation is a set of measurement protocols that isolate estimator noise from genuine drift:

- **Subspace rotation metric.** To avoid the sign artifact of raw Frobenius distance between SVD bases, the authors use the sign-invariant principal-angle (chordal) distance ∥∆U∥_F = √(2r − 2Σcos θᵢ), bounded by √(2r) (orthogonal frames) and 0 (identical). They also track gradient energy coverage γ_g = ∥U^⊤G∥²_F/∥G∥²_F.
- **Same-step split-batch control (the decisive experiment).** At a single step on a pretrained checkpoint, the batch is split into two disjoint halves, the top-r subspace of each half's gradient is computed, and their chordal distance is measured. Because nothing changes between the two estimates except the sample, any disagreement is pure estimator noise. The result (0.725√(2r) at Pythia-160M, r=128) is essentially equal to the across-time rotation over a full refresh interval (0.742√(2r)), proving the rotation is dominated by non-identifiability.
- **Reproducible rank k\*.** A direct count of how many of the r directions agree across the split, averaged over repetitions and layers, giving k*≈39 of 128 at Pythia-160M (48 on QKV, down to 31 on attention output). A second, independent estimator (inverting the measured spectrum through a fixed detectability threshold) recovers the same per-layer ordering.
- **Spectral analysis.** Per-matrix gradient singular spectra show a small dominant spike (a few to ~20 directions holding half the squared mass), then a smooth gapless tail; the adjacent ratio at r=128 is ≈1.005 and effective rank (89–358) straddles the cutoff, so r sits inside the flat tail.
- **N-fold averaging budget test.** The deep-tail singular value, scaled by √N, grows 2.0–3.2× from N=1 to N=64 (i.e., shrinks as N^{-1/4}), confirming the tail is power-law signal, not noise being averaged away. This is confirmed at 410M, 1B, and 2.8B and reproduced across NVIDIA L40S and AMD MI210 backends.
- **EMA-basis variant.** To test whether stabilizing the basis by averaging GG^⊤ helps, the authors vary a decay β3 from 0 (canonical GaLore) to 0.9999 and measure both realized rotation and final perplexity.
- **Controlled 2×2 remedy comparison.** At Pythia-1B/40K with three seeds per cell, they cross {carry, transport} against {β2=0.999, β2=0.99}, isolating the joint effect of state transport and memory shortening.
- **Full-rank AdamW causal control.** The same model, data, and schedule without any subspace refresh checks whether the β2 preference is specific to refreshing.
- **Theory.** A Weingarten-calculus lower bound (Theorem 1) shows blind second-moment carry is (r−k*)/2 suboptimal; a forgetting-factor bias–variance argument (Appendix I) predicts the interior β2*<0.999 minimizer when the target is reset every T≪τ_v steps.

## Results & Highlights

- **Per-refresh rotation saturates the geometric maximum.** At Pythia-1B (T=50), ∥∆U∥_F reaches 96.7–99.6% of √(2r) at every rank r∈{32,64,128,256} (e.g., 7.93 vs 8.00 at r=32; 22.10 vs 22.63 at r=256), implying mean principal angles of 86–90°; each refresh adopts a nearly orthogonal frame.
- **The rotation equals the same-step noise floor.** At Pythia-160M (r=128), the same-step split-batch disagreement is 0.725√(2r), essentially identical to the across-time rotation of 0.742√(2r); repeated at five training checkpoints (1K–143K) the floor stays in 0.64–0.71√(2r), so the rotation carries little information about elapsed time.
- **Only k*≈39 of 128 directions are reproducible**, ranging from 48 on QKV down to 31 on attention output (standard error below 1 over repetitions).
- **No spectral gap at r.** The adjacent singular-value ratio at r=128 is ≈1.005; effective rank straddles the cutoff (89–358 across layers); the top-r block captures only 46–68% of the gradient's nuclear mass.
- **Averaging cannot manufacture a gap.** Under N-fold averaging the deep-tail singular value grows 2.0–3.2× from N=1 to 64 (shrinking as N^{-1/4} not N^{-1/2}); the de-censored power-law exponent is α=1.03–1.35. Across 160M–2.8B, averaging to N=64 removes only 24–29% of the single-batch disagreement.
- **Basis averaging does not help at 1B.** A basis averaged over 10,000 steps still moves by 84% of maximal per refresh (vs 97% with no averaging); final perplexity spans only 0.36 across four decades of β3 (21.025 to 21.385 at default β2).
- **Transport beats the β2 fix alone (Table 1, three seeds, Pythia-1B/40K).** Transport (LDAdam rule) at default β2=0.999 reaches 18.73±0.13 vs untransported GaLore at its best β2=0.99 fix of 19.28±0.18; the carry-β2 gap is 2.79 PPL (22.07 vs 19.28), about 20× the standard error of the difference, and persists under transport (1.81).
- **Lowering β2 helps every refreshing optimizer (Table 3).** LDAdam: 57.69±0.11 → 31.27±0.31 (10K, gap 26.4); canonical GaLore: 21.27±0.17 → 19.04±0.13 (40K, gap 2.23); APOLLO-Mini: ~99.8 → ~97.0 (two seeds). The full-rank AdamW control reverses the preference to 97.9 vs 118.0 (−20.1), isolating the redraw as the cause.
- **Generality across scales and domains (Table 5).** Same-step disagreement is 0.67–0.83 of √(2r) across Pythia 70M–6.9B (k* falls from 50 to 21), with GPT-2-large (0.71), Qwen2.5-3B (0.78), Llama-3-8B (0.81) in the same band, and ViT-B on CIFAR-10 weaker at 0.53.
- **Rank should not be truncated toward k\*.** A single-seed sweep at Pythia-1B shows canonical GaLore reaches 27.9 PPL at r=128 vs 29.8 at r=64 and worse below, because the non-identifiable directions beyond k* still capture useful gradient energy; the fix is transport, not truncation.

## Authors & Credibility

The paper is single-authored by Noel Thomas at the Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence (MBZUAI), and is published as a conference paper at ICOMP 2026. The empirical rigor is high for a measurement-driven study: claims are cross-validated by two independent estimators (direct count vs spectral inversion), reproduced across two GPU clusters (NVIDIA L40S and AMD MI210) and within-cluster comparisons only, and scoped with explicit recipes. Theoretical claims (the Weingarten blind-carry bound, the MMSE first-moment transport, the forgetting-factor bias–variance argument) are placed in appendices with full proofs. The author is candid about the boundaries of evidence (a dedicated Limitations section lists five), distinguishes direction from magnitude across recipes, and never compares perplexities across recipes or clusters. The connection to classical results (Gur-Ari et al. 2018 on the tiny gradient subspace; Davis–Kahan/Wedin perturbation theory; Weingarten calculus; forgetting-factor RLS) grounds the findings in established theory rather than ad hoc claims.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High and genuine. The same-step split-batch control is a simple but original experimental design that cleanly separates estimator noise from genuine drift — a confound that prior "subspace stability" reports (Zmushko et al. 2024 measuring principal-angle distributions, Rajabi et al. 2025b measuring incremental-tracker self-consistency) had conflated with the sign-corrected chordal distance ∥∆U∥_F that actually governs carried state. The N^{-1/4} averaging-scaling result and the Weingarten (r−k*)/2 bound are new.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Quantitative claims carry standard errors, three-seed replicates where it matters (the 2×2), cross-backend reproduction (0.3% agreement), and a pre-registered pass threshold for the spiked-covariance prediction that is honestly reported as *failed* (MAE 0.073 vs threshold 0.03), leading the author to report only the measured scaling rather than overclaim a closed-form law. The full-rank AdamW causal control that reverses the β2 preference is exactly the right ablation to attribute the effect to the redraw.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Models are public (Pythia checkpoints, GPT-2-large, Qwen2.5-3B, Llama-3-8B, ViT-B); corpora are public (WikiText-103, C4, CIFAR-10); recipes are stated per table with rank, refresh interval, learning rate, warmup, step count, and seed counts. The probe protocol (Appendix F) is described in enough detail to reimplement without the code, including pool size K=128, batch B=8, sequence length 512, four layer types, and the sign-correction procedure. A code release would further help, but is not promised.
- **Significance:** Practically important for the GaLore family. The prescription (transport state + lower β2, measure k* before trusting a rank) is actionable and the k* probe is portable. The conceptual reframing — GaLore is an energy-capture projector, not a subspace tracker — explains why random-projection variants (GoLore, the Grassmann variant) and SVD-based GaLore perform comparably.
- **Red flags and caveats:**
  - **Transport vs error feedback are not separated.** The transport arm is the *full* LDAdam update (transport of both moments *plus* error feedback), so the 2×2 comparison bounds their joint effect; the author states this caveat explicitly but it weakens the clean attribution of the 18.73-vs-19.28 gain to transport alone.
  - **The transport rule itself is LDAdam's**, not the author's; the author's contribution is the optimality statement (Proposition 3, Theorem 1) and the controlled comparison. This is honestly scoped but means the paper's "what to do" partly endorses an existing method.
  - **LDAdam already defaults to β2=0.99**, so the prescription "lower β2 to 0.99 for LDAdam" is partly tautological; the novel content is the *explanation* (staleness under self-imposed redraws), which is then validated as recipe-dependent for canonical GaLore.
  - **The canonical-GaLore β2 margin (2.23 PPL) is training-length dependent** — present at 40K, absent or slightly reversed at 10–20K — and hardware-sensitive (reproduced on one cluster, not on a different backend), which the author flags but which limits the robustness of the headline prescription for canonical GaLore specifically.
  - **The basis-averaging sweep is one seed per cell**, read as a direction rather than a headline number; the N^{-1/4} closed-form fit is reported only at 160M (the out-of-sample fit fails elsewhere).
  - **APOLLO-Mini enters as only a "directional consistency check"** (two seeds, small margins, high absolute perplexity ~97, and it uses a random Gaussian projection rather than an SVD top-r subspace), so the "every refreshing optimizer" claim leans on two strong legs (LDAdam, canonical GaLore) plus one weak one.

## Strengths

- The same-step split-batch control is an elegant, decisive experimental design that resolves a long-standing ambiguity in the GaLore literature.
- Claims are cross-validated by independent estimators and reproduced across scales (70M–6.9B), architectures (GPT-NeoX, GPT-2, Llama-class), domains (language and vision), and GPU backends (CUDA and ROCm).
- Intellectual honesty is exemplary: a pre-registered prediction that fails is reported as failed, and a dedicated Limitations section lists five specific boundaries.
- The reframing of GaLore as an energy-capture projector (§4) explains both why it works and why random-projection variants match it, reconciling a tension in recent work (He et al. 2024; Rajabi et al. 2025a; Su et al. 2025).
- The k* probe is a portable, cheap diagnostic that practitioners can run before trusting any low-rank gradient subspace assumption.

## Limitations

- The transport experiment does not separate state transport from error feedback (both come bundled in the LDAdam update).
- The canonical-GaLore β2 benefit is training-length dependent (visible at 40K, absent at 10–20K) and hardware-sensitive.
- The basis-averaging sweep uses one seed per cell; the closed-form N-scaling fit is reported only at 160M.
- Perplexity results are single-recipe per table, on WikiText and Pythia only; magnitudes shift between recipes (directions do not).
- Claims are scoped to optimizers that explicitly compute and refresh a gradient subspace; LoRA and other low-rank weight parameterizations are out of scope.

## Relevance

对关注大模型高效训练与优化器设计的研究者高度相关：它直接审视 GaLore 类低秩优化器的几何前提，给出可落地的诊断探针 k\* 与状态搬运+降 β2 的工程处方，并解释了为何随机投影变体能与 SVD 投影打平——为内存受限下的 LLM 预训练/微调方法选择提供了理论依据与可复现的实验范式。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would separating state transport from error feedback (an ablation the authors did not run) preserve the 18.73-vs-19.28 advantage, or does error feedback carry most of the gain?
- Does the k* probe predict the *optimal* rank for a given layer, and can per-layer adaptive rank (à la Q-GaLore) be grounded in measured k* rather than heuristics?
- The N^{-1/4} tail scaling is measured but the spiked-covariance prediction fails out of sample — what generative model (e.g., a specific power-law+spike covariance) reproduces both the spectrum and the overlap-vs-N curve?
- Does the non-identifiability finding extend to fine-tuning regimes (LoRA aside) where the gradient may concentrate more, and to tasks beyond language modeling where the gradient subspace is known to be tiny (e.g., classification, per Gur-Ari et al.)?
- Can the Haar-redraw model be relaxed to a partial-rotation model (mixing a stable k*-dim core with a Haar block) to tighten the (r−k*)/2 bound into a quantitative predictor of the carry-vs-transport perplexity gap?
