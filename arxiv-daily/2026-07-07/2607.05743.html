01

Methodology

four-stage SoK · no new attack or defense

A Systematization of Knowledge, not a measurement study. Credibility rests entirely on the rigor of its verification protocol — fetching each paper at its own arXiv abstract page rather than trusting secondary summaries.

The 39 papers are organized first by primary execution-security mechanism into 17 categories, then re-read along two independent axes: root cause (what design defect a technique responds to) and pipeline stage (pre / at / post-action / post-hoc). A pairwise cosine-similarity heatmap of root-cause weights surfaces the same cross-cutting gaps that close reading identifies in §6 — an internal consistency check on the synthesis.

  1. Search & inclusion. Iterated arXiv/web queries; include only execution-layer work, 2023→present. Excludes model-level alignment and text-only prompt-injection defenses.
  2. Verification. Fetch each abstract page; record exact title, authors, venue, one-sentence contribution; admit only on agreement. Two misattributions caught & corrected. Four CVEs confirmed against NIST NVD.
  3. Categorization. One of 17 categories by primary mechanism. Full corpus released as plain CSV — classification is falsifiable, not asserted.
  4. Reproducibility. Every count mechanically re-derived from one data file by an independent checker; reports a single pass/fail. Catches corpus/body drift before build.

Real-world validation · Pwn2Own Berlin 2026 — Coding Agents category

The ZeroDay Initiative's dedicated category (targets: Claude Code, OpenAI Codex, Cursor) paid out $1,298,250 across 47 unique zero-days. OpenAI Codex fell to a single input-neutralization bug (CWE-150) for a $40,000 award — direct evidence the catalogued attack surface is neither hypothetical nor niche.

03

Five cross-cutting gaps

found by reading across categories, not within one
01
Isolation vs. access-control evaluation gap
No isolation architecture (e.g. IsolateGPT) has ever been benchmarked head-to-head against any access-control system (e.g. PORTICO, SEAgent) on a shared attacker corpus.
02
Enforcement-fragility vs. defense-evaluation gap
Defenses report strong rates against author-built attackers but are never re-tested against ShellSieve's released bypass set — "the most consequential finding in the whole literature."
03
TOCTOU–MCP vocabulary gap
Filesystem TOCTOU races and MCP tool-poisoning are the same validate-then-act defect at different layers, yet studied as two mutually non-citing literatures.
04
Honest-policy-author assumption
Every mechanism assumes a correct policy author. Policy-authoring error is "plausibly at least as large" a risk as enforcement failure — never measured.
05
Scope-creep failure mode
The 17.1% overeager-action rate is not addressed by any access-control or capability paper — a wrong-question failure no "is this allowed" mechanism catches.
04

Four root causes (RC1–RC4)

17 categories collapse onto these
RC1
No structured separation
Untrusted content is not structurally separated from control input.
RC2
Authorize-once, trust-forever
A single authorization check grants permanent trust with no revocation.
RC3
Wrong question
Asks "is this action type allowed?" not "does this call match intent right now?"
RC4
Self-validated defense
Defenses are checked against author-built attackers, not observed real failures.

No single existing mechanism addresses both a missing-state failure (RC1/RC2) and a wrong-question failure (RC3/RC4); partial defenses compose in the taxonomy but do not compose along the causal chain.

05

Critical assessment

strengths & limitations, side by side

▲ Strengths

  • First dedicated systematization of execution security for AI coding agents as a subject with internal structure — a gap four broader surveys left open.
  • Verification rigor unusual for a survey: direct arXiv fetch, two misattributions caught & corrected, four CVEs confirmed against NVD (not secondary descriptions).
  • Released machine-readable corpus + generator + verifier make every count mechanically re-derivable and falsifiable.
  • The RC1–RC4 abstraction and pipeline-stage re-reading are genuine cross-cutting synthesis surfacing non-obvious structural similarities.
  • Grounded in real incidents (Pwn2Own 2026, four patched CVEs incl. two in Claude Code) — externally verifiable, not speculative.
  • Honest about limits: coverage not measurement; iterative not exhaustive search; single-category assignment understates overlap.

▼ Limitations

  • SoK, not a measurement study — no new attacks or defenses; central limit is coverage, not measurement error.
  • 8 of 17 categories contain exactly one paper — ambiguous between a nascent direction and an incomplete search.
  • Verification confirms bibliographic identity, not independent replication of empirical claims; CVE exploits were not reproduced.
  • The five gaps are argued from absence of a citation — "a real but indirect form of evidence"; cross-category work may exist outside the corpus.
  • The pairwise-similarity heatmap depends on interpretive RC-weight assignments (X=1.0, (x)=0.5) — a synthesis claim, not a measurement.
  • Single author: 39-paper attribution is a large interpretive load for one reviewer; a second reviewer might dispute classifications.
06

Open questions for further work

agenda aimed at closing the five gaps
  1. If an isolation architecture (IsolateGPT) and an access-control system (PORTICO/SEAgent) ran against a shared corpus (SandboxEscapeBench, RedCode) — substitutes or complements? Does layering both beat either alone? Closes Gap 1.
  2. How do the §4 access-control mechanisms perform when re-tested against ShellSieve's released bypass corpus (1,709 denylists, 69–98.6% fragility) instead of their own author-built attackers? Closes Gap 2 / RC4 — the "most consequential" experiment.
  3. Can one formal model of validate-then-act races cover filesystem TOCTOU, browser-DOM TOCTOU and MCP tool-metadata poisoning — so a mitigation for one transfers to the others without translation? Closes Gap 3.
  4. What is the empirical rate of policy-authoring error in real deployments, and does it dominate enforcement failure as a source of risk? (The ShellSieve-style study aimed at policies, not denylists.) Closes Gap 4 — most consequential and least addressed.
  5. When OverEager-Bench's overeager trajectories are replayed against a capability system (PORTICO) or information-flow framework (SEAgent), what fraction does an authorization mechanism built for a different threat model actually catch? Closes Gap 5 — tests the "allowed ≠ invoked-correctly" premise.
