Key metric · anchor fact

3‑way equivalence

Infinite-width ↔ polynomial-width ↔ polynomial s∞ — all three collapse to the same polynomial-sample learnable class.

No empirical number; this is a theorem statement. Theory paper.

Why this matters

Answers a sharp question: does the mean-field limit invent width-dependent computing power? No. Reduced entropy s∞ is the width-independent learnability criterion.

Critical edge

Bayesian/Gibbs object only. No claim that SGD — or any finite optimizer — reaches the Gibbs posterior. “The constants are not practical.”

01

Methodology

A pure theory paper — no experiments. Fixed depth L, width N, fully-connected nets on the Boolean cube B_d = {±1}^d, mean-field scaling γ=1, Gaussian priors, population-MSE objective. The headline object is the reduced entropy — the intensive prior log-cost per neuron.

Reduced entropy (intensive complexity measure) s∞(y,ε) = limsupN −(1/N) log π0N(L ≤ ε) — prior “nats per neuron” to represent y to MSE ε; width-independent, finite for every y (memorization: s∞ = O(2d)).

The learner studied is the tempered empirical Gibbs posterior π ∝ exp(−Nβ̄ L̂D) π0N at intensive inverse temperature; the hard MSE ball only supplies the entropy budget. The proof runs on two inverse engines:

▸ Compression — forward (§5)

Low-entropy infinite-width solution → split each layer kernel at cutoff λ0 into a low-dim active block (reconstructed against fixed top eigenfunctions) and a lazy bulk (resampled from the prior). The lazy-swap lemma (Stein / OU generator) bounds replacement error uniformly in the input, lifting MSE-accuracy to pointwise agreement.

▸ Cloning — converse (§6)

A width-W polynomial-weight teacher is embedded by cloning each neuron into many near-copies inside a “microscopic cylinder.” Only d+W+1 coordinates per neuron cost relative entropy; orthogonal directions are free. Data-processing yields s∞ ≤ poly(W,d).

03

Strengths & limitations

▸ Strengths

  • Clean headline: a precise three-way equivalence in an interpretable complexity measure (prior nats per neuron).
  • Pointwise (sup-norm) compression witness — stronger than risk-matching, and exactly what the union bound consumes.
  • Active/lazy split + Stein-method lazy-swap give a transparent mechanism for why γ=1 is the delicate case.
  • Honest scope: repeatedly separates proved from cited; explicitly disclaims algorithmic/practical claims.
  • Transparent LLM-use disclosure; clean body/appendix separation.

▸ Limitations

  • Boolean-cube inputs only; the 2d-input union bound & O(d) Gaussian-max are load-bearing.
  • Fixed depth L; exponents depend on L, depth is not preserved → circuit-class reading is coarse.
  • Bayesian/Gibbs object only; no finite optimizer (SGD) shown to attain it; “constants not practical.”
  • General finite-width & γ>1 regimes absent (deferred to companion, contingent on unverified concentration hypothesis).
  • No experiments; the predicted kernel-vs-mean-field separation is not demonstrated empirically.
  • Polynomial slack everywhere; Δ absorbed into constants, no sharp rates.
04

Open questions

Q1 · Concrete targets

Can s∞ be bounded explicitly for parities, juntas, low-degree Fourier polys, CNFs — to confirm the kernel-vs-mean-field separation quantitatively?

Q2 · Continuous inputs

Does the equivalence survive replacing the Boolean cube with sphere / Gaussian measure, where the 2d union bound breaks?

Q3 · Algorithmic capture

The result characterizes the Gibbs posterior, not an optimizer. Can mean-field Langevin / noisy µP-SGD reach it in poly time?

Q4 · Depth & circuit classes

How does the exponent's dependence on L grow? Can the coarse P/poly statement sharpen into depth-sensitive TC⁰ vs NC¹?

Q5 · Companion's hypothesis

What does the super-polynomial finite-width “concentration hypothesis on the single-site law” amount to concretely — and is it standard?

Q6 · Practical compression

Can the pointwise compression construction become a real distillation recipe (active-direction projection + lazy resampling)?
