Why This Matters

Turn-level budgeting — adaptive rollout depth plus progressive loss rebalancing — transfers directly to tool-use, deep-research, and multi-turn retrieval agent pipelines, lowering compute costs.

Critical Edge

Every experiment runs for only 100 optimizer steps — no scaling curve shows whether the advantage survives at 500–1000 steps or shrinks as vanilla OPD catches up.

01

Methodology

TurnOPD is built on a diagnosis that vanilla on-policy distillation (OPD) suffers two budget mismatches in long-horizon agents. External: fixed full-horizon rollouts waste compute on low-signal tail turns. Internal: trajectory-level KL loss concentrates on shallow turns, starving deep decision points of supervision.

The contamination-compression model (Proposition 1) decomposes each next-token distribution into a context-forced and a free component, proving observable KL ≤ (1−λ)·free-KL — a structural reason raw KL decays with depth even when the student has not learned the teacher, and why the success/fail KL gap can invert.

Two controllers fix the mismatches. Adaptive rollout depth H_ctrl = max(H_eff, H_cov) blends a survivor-weighted KL centroid (efficiency arm) with a success-conditioned 80%-quantile coverage floor, refreshed by periodic full-depth probes with EMA smoothing — collecting only the interaction that matters. A progressive loss controller linearly blends trajectory-level and turn-uniform KL weights as α ramps 0→1, rebalancing loss mass toward deep turns late in training. The two are complementary: depth is the efficiency lever, normalization the optimization lever.

Loss-budget diagnosis: Under vanilla OPD, the deepest third of reliable turns receives only 3.6–4.5% of KL loss on ALFWorld (11–13% on Multi-Hop Search). TurnOPD's linear blend ramps the deep-turn share from 12.8% → 27.7% as training progresses.

▲ Strengths

  • Diagnosis-first methodology: per-turn analysis plus a proven proposition give a mechanistic rationale, not a trick that happens to work.
  • Clean two-controller decomposition with complementary, well-isolated roles (efficiency vs optimization), confirmed by ablation.
  • Broad evaluation across three structurally different agent settings (embodied, web, multi-hop QA) and three student scales — no cherry-picked benchmark.
  • Practical and deployable: lightweight probes plus reweighting atop standard reverse-KL OPD, with no architecture changes.
  • Honest reporting of cases where the method does not win (Multi-Hop Search Same-Step) and of the tiny margin of progressive over a hard switch.

▼ Limitations

  • All experiments run only 100 optimizer steps; long-horizon dynamics and whether vanilla OPD closes the gap with more compute are untested.
  • Coverage arm H_cov depends on task-success labels; the reward-free full-population variant is only swept, not used in the main table.
  • The advantage of progressive blending over a hard turn-level switch is within noise (85.1 vs 85.0), so the "progressive" component's value is modest.
  • The contamination-compression decomposition is explanatory; λ(c) and free-component disagreement are never measured directly.
  • TCOD-F2B underperforms vanilla OPD on the 4B student, weakening the "vs strong baseline" framing; SOD and step-aware distillers are cited but not benchmarked.
  • Training/teacher corpora and GRPO teacher checkpoints are not released, capping third-party reproducibility.
03

Questions for Further Exploration

Does TurnOPD's accuracy–time advantage persist at 500–1000 optimizer steps, or does vanilla OPD eventually close the gap once shallow-turn convergence completes?
Can the contamination-compression decomposition be made measurable — estimating λ(c) per turn from teacher/student entropy on held-out forced spans — turning Proposition 1 into a monitored training signal?
How does TurnOPD interact with reward-based RL (GRPO/PPO) stacked on top of OPD, where success signals are already available but KL is not the only objective?
The reward-free full-population CDF variant works on ALFWorld-1.7B — does it hold on WebShop and Multi-Hop Search, where failure modes (stalling, loops) differ?
Would combining TurnOPD's turn-budgeting with SOD's step-level reweighting or entropy-aware OPD yield further gains, or are these mechanisms redundant?
Is the progressive linear blend meaningfully better than a hard turn-level switch on harder or larger students, given the near-tie (85.1 vs 85.0) on ALFWorld-1.7B?
