> **一句话总结：** 本文系统刻画了在 NVIDIA Jetson AGX Orin 上对 BERT 与 Pythia 系列 SLM 微调时的 GPU 频率—延迟—功耗—能耗权衡（呈 U 形），并提出基于可解释决策树的 GPU governor 策略，在 GLUE 未见任务上相对无功率限制的 MAXN Mode 0 平均节能 13.11%（最高 26.73%），且不损失验证精度。

> **TL;DR:** The paper characterizes how GPU DVFS affects latency, power, and energy during fine-tuning of BERT and Pythia SLM variants on NVIDIA Jetson AGX Orin, then proposes an interpretable Decision-Tree-based governor (MLG) plus a majority-rule fixed-frequency governor (FFG). Evaluated on six unseen GLUE tasks (QQP, RTE, CoLA, STS-B, MNLI, WNLI) versus the unconstrained MAXN Mode 0 baseline, MLG achieves average energy savings of 13.11% (up to 26.73% on BERT-base/MNLI), with larger models (>70M params) consistently above 22%, while preserving identical validation accuracy.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统刻画了在资源受限的嵌入式平台 Jetson AGX Orin（64GB, MAXN Mode 0）上，对 encoder-only（BERT/DeBERTa-v2-xlarge）与 decoder-only（Pythia）SLM 微调时的 GPU 频率—训练时间—总功耗—总能耗关系：能耗曲线呈明显的 U 形，最优频率点集中在 510–816 MHz，偏离 MAXN Mode 0 导致最多约 25% 的可避免能耗。
- 提出两种轻量级 GPU 频率选择策略：(1) 基于多数规则的固定频率 governor（FFG，固定 714 MHz）；(2) 基于可解释决策树的 ML governor（MLG，Algorithm 1），仅依赖模型层数、平均序列长度、GPU 利用率等少量静态/运行时特征，即可离线给出确定性的频率选择规则。
- 在 6 个未见 GLUE 任务（QQP、RTE、CoLA、STS-B、MNLI、WNLI）上验证：MLG 相对 MAXN Mode 0 平均节能 13.11%（FFG 为 12.21%），最高达 26.73%（BERT-base/MNLI），且所有配置验证精度一致。
- 提供可复现的开源式方法论（11 档 GPU 频率扫描 306→1300 MHz、tegrastats 10ms 采样、LOOCV 网格搜索 DT 超参），并指出参数量 > 70M 的模型 U 形曲线更清晰、节能更稳定，小模型（≤ 70M）则波动大。

## Key Contributions

- First comprehensive per-frequency characterization of SLM fine-tuning energy on Jetson AGX Orin under the unconstrained MAXN Mode 0, covering encoder-only (BERT-small/medium/base/large, DeBERTa-v2-xlarge) and decoder-only (Pythia-31M/70M/410M/1B) models. Energy-vs-frequency curves exhibit a well-defined U-shape with optima in 510–816 MHz, exposing up to ~25% avoidable energy in default power modes.
- Two lightweight, interpretable GPU frequency governors: a Fixed-Frequency Governor (FFG) using the most frequent optimal frequency (714 MHz), and an ML-based Governor (MLG) built from a Decision Tree over only 5 features (model type, GPU utilization, layers, hidden size, average sequence length), producing transparent deterministic rules in Algorithm 1.
- Demonstration that workload-aware frequency selection achieves average 13.11% (up to 26.73%) energy savings over the uncapped MAXN Mode 0 baseline across six unseen GLUE test tasks, with identical validation accuracy across all configurations.
- Empirical finding that result stability scales with parameter count: models > 70M parameters give consistently > 22% savings, while small models (Pythia-31M, BERT-small/medium, Pythia-70M) suffer erratic, sometimes negative savings due to unclear/spiky U-shapes.

## Methodology

The pipeline (Figure 3) has five phases: data collection, SLM workload characterization, ML model selection, governor policy design, and policy evaluation.

**Platform & Workloads.** NVIDIA Jetson AGX Orin Developer Kit (64 GB LPDDR5, Ampere GPU with 2048 CUDA cores / 64 Tensor Cores, 12-core ARM Cortex-A78AE), supporting 11 discrete GPU frequencies (306–1300 MHz) under user-space governor control. SLMs: encoder-only BERT variants (BERT-small 29M/4L, BERT-medium 41M/8L, BERT-base 110M/12L, BERT-large 340M/24L, DeBERTa-v2-xlarge 900M/24L) and decoder-only Pythia variants (31M/6L, 70M/6L, 410M/24L, 1B/16L). GLUE benchmark split: SST-2/QNLI/MRPC for characterization and model selection; QQP/RTE/CoLA/STS-B/MNLI/WNLI as unseen test tasks.

**Data Collection.** GPU frequency swept over the 11 levels while CPU runs the default schedutil governor (29 CPU frequencies, 120–2200 MHz). Power/energy captured with NVIDIA's `tegrastats` at 10 ms sampling plus a custom Python parser, recording training time, CPU/GPU power, EMC/GPU/CPU frequencies and utilizations. Each config profiled with two methods (fixed 5-minute run and fixed-step run); the 5-minute method is used for final evaluation because it gives more stable numbers across experiments (full fine-tuning results used when training completes under 5 min). Fixed hyperparameters: 1 epoch, AdamW, LR 2×10⁻⁵ (DeBERTa-xlarge uses 1×10⁻⁵), FP16 mixed precision (DeBERTa-xlarge full FP32), fixed seed.

**Characterization.** Frequency-vs-latency decreases near-exponentially; frequency-vs-total-power follows P ∝ C·V²·f (monotonic increase, highest frequencies often exceed Mode 0 power); frequency-vs-energy is U-shaped — energy rises at low f due to long training time and at high f due to high power, optima mostly in 510–816 MHz.

**ML Model Selection.** The optimal-frequency prediction is framed as a 6-class classification over labels {510, 612, 714, 816, 1020, 1122} MHz. A Decision Tree is chosen for interpretability; trained on 27 model-task pairs using grid search with Leave-One-Out CV (LOOCV), reaching validation accuracy 0.62. The resulting tree is pruned to a compact rule set.

**Governor Policy.** Algorithm 1 (MLG): primarily splits on depth (Layers ≤ 7 vs > 7), then on average sequence length (thresholds 32.5 / 53.0), with a GPU-utilization override (UGPU ≤ 22.0 → 1020 MHz). FFG simply fixes 714 MHz. Policies compared against MAXN Mode 0 (uncapped DVFS up to 1300 MHz).

## Results & Highlights

- **Average 13.11% energy savings (MLG) and 12.21% (FFG) over MAXN Mode 0** averaged across six unseen GLUE test tasks (QQP, RTE, CoLA, STS-B, MNLI, WNLI) and nine SLM variants — positive ΔE indicates savings, computed as (E_Mode0 − E_policy)/E_Mode0 × 100%.
- **Up to 26.73% peak saving**: BERT-base on MNLI with MLG (ΔE_MLG = 26.73%); BERT-base also saves 25.80% on WNLI and 24.37% on QQP, all versus uncapped MAXN Mode 0.
- **Large models (>70M params) consistently >22%**: per-model averages — BERT-large 22.74%, DeBERTa-xlarge 23.35%, Pythia-410M 22.32%, Pythia-1B 23.24%, BERT-base 20.93% — confirming stable U-shaped curves for higher-parameter-count workloads.
- **MLG beats FFG by +0.9% on average**, with the largest gains on the smallest models (BERT-small +2.29%, Pythia-31M +2.53%); RTE task improves 3.54% under MLG, indicating the sequence-length branch better captures longer-sequence workloads.
- **Small models are erratic**: BERT-medium shows 18.38% saving on QQP but −2.31% on CoLA; Pythia-70M shows +9.38% on QQP but −7.23% on WNLI; Pythia-31M swings from −4.15% (FFG) to +8.71% (MLG) on RTE while dropping from +9.82% to −0.35% on CoLA — attributed to unclear/spiky U-shapes at small parameter counts.
- **Accuracy unchanged**: all tested frequency configurations retain identical validation accuracy on GLUE, so energy gains come at no quality cost.
- **DT model validation accuracy = 0.62 (LOOCV)** — moderate but sufficient to produce transparent, deterministic frequency-selection rules.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine but incremental. The DVFS-for-LLM-inference space (refs [3]–[5], [11], [12]) and datacenter training power-capping (Zeus [6], Perseus [7], EnvPipe [13]) are crowded; the novelty here is the specific niche of *on-device SLM fine-tuning* on Jetson MAXN mode with per-frequency energy curves, which prior federated-Jetson work [15] did not do (it ran only the default power mode). The ML contribution (an interpretable DT with LOOCV accuracy 0.62) is modest — essentially a small rule tree.
- **Rigor:** Reasonable for a short paper. Strengths: LOOCV for the small (27 sample) dataset, six *unseen* test tasks for policy evaluation (no train/test leakage on tasks), consistent fixed hyperparameters, 10 ms `tegrastats` sampling, two profiling methods cross-checked. Weaknesses: only a single device (one Jetson AGX Orin SKU, 64 GB), no variance/error bars reported across runs, and the DT's 0.62 LOOCV accuracy is low enough that the rule's generalization is questionable.
- **Reproducibility:** High in principle — fixed seed, named hyperparameters, public models (HuggingFace), public benchmark (GLUE), commodity Jetson hardware, standard `tegrastats` tool. But no mention of code release, and the dataset of 27 model-task configurations is not published, so reproducing the DT exactly would require re-collecting data.
- **Significance:** Practical for the on-device/edge SLM community. The headline 13.11% average / 26.73% peak saving on an already-uncapped MAXN baseline is meaningful for battery-operated devices, but it is constrained to Jetson AGX Orin and to the GLUE fine-tuning regime (single-epoch, short tasks).
- **Red flags / specific concerns:**
  - The "up to 26.73%" headline is cherry-picked from one model-task cell (BERT-base/MNLI); the average is 13.11%, and several small-model cells are *negative* (energy regression up to −9.44% for BERT-small/CoLA under FFG), which the paper honestly discloses but which the abstract/TL;DR framing under-emphasizes.
  - Baseline is only MAXN Mode 0 — there is **no comparison against the 15W/30W/50W Jetson power modes** (which real deployments actually use), nor against an obvious SOTA baseline like Zeus's [6] greedy DVFS for training or any inference-DVFS method adapted to fine-tuning. The motivating example claims the U-shape beats Mode 2 (30W) too, but the results table never quantifies it.
  - **Missing ablations:** no analysis of CPU governor co-optimization (CPU is just left on schedutil), no EMC frequency effect, no breakdown of energy split into compute vs. memory vs. idle, no test of whether the GPU-utilization override branch in Algorithm 1 actually helps on average (CoLA degrades because of it).
  - 27 training samples for a 6-class DT is dangerously small; the 0.62 accuracy is barely above the 16.7% random baseline for the imbalanced majority class but the imbalance (714 MHz dominates) is not really addressed.
  - No statistical significance testing on the savings numbers.
  - Generalization beyond BERT/Pythia and beyond GLUE is left to future work; the authors themselves flag this.

## Strengths

- Clear, well-motivated problem: on-device SLM fine-tuning energy is genuinely under-studied versus inference-DVFS and datacenter-training-DVFS.
- The U-shaped energy-vs-frequency characterization across both encoder- and decoder-only families is a useful, reusable empirical finding.
- Interpretable policy (Algorithm 1) is deployable: it requires only static features (layers, sequence length) plus a cheap runtime feature (GPU utilization), no online optimizer or model.
- Honest reporting: negative per-cell results and the small-model instability are disclosed rather than hidden.
- Evaluation on *unseen* tasks gives credible out-of-distribution evidence, not just in-distribution fit.

## Limitations

- Single hardware platform (Jetson AGX Orin 64GB only); no Orin NX, Nano, or non-Jetson embedded GPU, so generality across devices is unproven.
- Only GLUE fine-tuning tasks (single epoch, short); no instruction tuning, no longer-context tasks, no generation/decoder fine-tuning beyond GLUE-style classification.
- DT classifier has low accuracy (0.62) and the authors admit more sophisticated ML models are future work.
- Only the uncapped MAXN Mode 0 baseline is reported in the results table; the 15W/30W/50W modes that the motivation section discusses are not quantitatively compared.
- No comparison to established training-DVFS baselines (Zeus, Perseus, EnvPipe) even as adapted points.
- No code or dataset release mentioned; reproducibility depends on the reader re-collecting all sweeps.
- No error bars or significance tests; energy measurements at 10 ms are noisy and the 5-minute window is arbitrary for fast tasks.

## Relevance

与用户关注的小模型/边缘端高效推理与微调方向高度相关：本文给出在嵌入式 GPU（Jetson AGX Orin）上对 BERT/Pythia 类 SLM 进行本地微调时的能耗优化方法，可作为端侧 SLM 部署、绿色 AI 与 DVFS 调度研究的参考基线，尤其是其"参数量越大、U 形越稳"的实证结论对 SLM 选型有直接指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do FFG/MLG perform against the 15W/30W/50W Jetson power modes (not just MAXN Mode 0), and against adapted training-DVFS baselines such as Zeus or Perseus?
- Does jointly scaling CPU frequency (EMC, RAM) alongside GPU frequency yield additional energy savings beyond the GPU-only policy studied here?
- Why do small models (≤70M) show spiky, non-monotonic energy curves — is it kernel-launch overhead, batch padding, or memory-bandwidth domination at small parameter counts?
- Can a more expressive model (gradient-boosted trees, small MLP) or an online Bayesian-optimization governor substantially beat the 0.62-accuracy DT while remaining interpretable?
- Do the savings transfer to instruction-tuning / longer-context / decoder-generation workloads, or to other Jetson SKUs (Orin NX, Nano) and non-NVIDIA embedded GPUs?
- What is the energy-quality trade-off if validation accuracy is allowed to vary slightly (e.g., lower precision, quantized fine-tuning, LoRA)?
