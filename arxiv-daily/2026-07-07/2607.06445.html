Analysis-by-Proxy: Localization Signals in VLMs Operating as Condition Encoders
Yoav Baron · Sara Dorfman · Roni Paiss (Google DeepMind) · Daniel Cohen-Or · Or Patashnik  — Tel Aviv University
arXiv:2607.06445  ·  published 2026-07-07  ·  venue ICML 2026
Key Finding

VLM localization signals live sparingly in input-dependent intermediate layers — not the final layer that editing pipelines actually use.

Mean Gemini-2.5-Pro VQA rises 3.17 → 3.42 (and background LPIPS drops 0.1191 → 0.0794) — best of six systems including FLUX.2 and FLUX-Kontext.

Why This Matters

First mechanistic account of why VLM-conditioned editors mislocalize — a reusable Q-Former probe plus a minimal LoRA fix that directly informs next-gen conditioning design.

Critical Edge

Ground-truth boxes come from the same VLM being probed (mild circularity), and no box-only vs. LoRA-only ablation isolates which component actually drives the gains.

01

Methodology

The work pairs an analysis framework with a downstream fix, both centered on Qwen-Image-Edit, which runs Qwen2.5-VL-7B (28 layers) as a non-generative condition encoder: a single forward pass over (image, instruction), after which a fixed subset of hidden states feeds the DiT.

Analysis-by-Proxy. Because the VLM is never sampled, logit-lens interpretability does not apply. The authors train a lightweight BLIP-2-style Q-Former whose learned queries cross-attend to VLM hidden states {H(l)} (queries→Q only; states→K/V only), with a coordinate head regressing (x1,y1,x2,y2). Trained on a 128k subset of OmniEdit-Filtered-1.2M; loss is L1 for 100 warmup steps, then (1−λ)L1 + λ·LGIoU, λ=0.4.

Finding where the signal lives. Per-layer proxies, then a "mid-layer text-token only" proxy over L={15..24}. A decisive-head analysis (attention-mass drop of 0.6 between top- and second-ranked layer) plus majority vote recovers a sample-specific optimal layer l*, and token-level magnitude ranking exposes sparse dominant instruction tokens.

Downstream fix. The proxy's predicted box is rendered onto the source RGB; a rank-16 LoRA (α=16, q/k/v/out) teaches the DiT to treat the overlay as a strict localization cue and erase it from the output. Cost: ~60 GB VRAM, 2 h Q-Former + 5 h LoRA on one A100 80 GB.

03

Strengths

  • First mechanistic account of why VLM-conditioned editors mislocalize — a concrete, testable single-pass-vs-decoding hypothesis, not a vague quality complaint.
  • Reusable interpretability tool. The Q-Former proxy is lightweight (1×A100, 2 h), architecture-agnostic in principle, and yields human-readable routing (decisive heads, dominant tokens).
  • Minimal additive fix — a LoRA + box overlay — yet lifts all three VQA sub-scores and lowers background LPIPS, including over the strong describe-then-edit baseline.
  • Refreshingly honest about scope: authors state the fix is "intentionally straightforward," limited to one pipeline, and flag deepfake dual-use.
04

Limitations

  • Small, synthetic eval (n=200) biased toward ordinal-disambiguation scenes; real editing distributions may show a smaller gap.
  • Single pipeline studied end-to-end — only Qwen-Image-Edit jointly conditions on image+text; FLUX.2/FIBO/text-only generalization is argued, not shown.
  • Circular data construction: ground-truth boxes (proxy + LoRA targets) come from the same Qwen2.5-VL whose representations are probed.
  • Missing control: no LoRA-only or box-only ablation to attribute the gains cleanly.
  • Closed commercial baselines (FLUX.2, FLUX-Kontext) are not re-tuned, so cross-system numbers aren't strictly apples-to-apples.
05

Open Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the input-dependent optimal layer l* generalize across VLM families (InternVL3.5, Qwen3-VL, Pixtral), or is decisive-head routing an artifact of Qwen2.5-VL's training?
  2. Can the proxy be trained end-to-end with the DiT instead of as a frozen two-stage predictor — and would that close the remaining gap to the autoregressive "Full Description" baseline?
  3. How much of the 31.5-point gap survives on natural, non-ordinal instructions (edit-by-appearance, edit-by-region) versus the synthetic N−1 stress test?
  4. Would a latent-level spatial mask (no RGB artifact to erase) simplify training and remove the LoRA's dependence on the rendering style?
  5. Majority vote routes to one layer — is the runner-up usually adjacent, and would top-k ensembling cut variance on hard inputs?
  6. Given the circularity, how decodable are intermediate layers when boxes come from an independent detector (e.g., Grounding-DINO)?
