Association Restoration Test: Revealing Restorable Shortcuts after Unlearning

Amy Lu · Changxiu Ji · Stanford University · arXiv 2607.05726 (v1)

machine-unlearning spurious-correlations model-auditing shortcut-learning robustness-evaluation

▍ Key Finding

Many "unlearned" label–attribute shortcuts remain functionally restorable by the original classifier head — so robustness and readability don't prove removal.

02 Key Metric

28.2pt ΔWGA

ART restores the shortcut on A-NegGrad+ / Waterbirds at β=2 (ΔCSR 37.0). Peak restorability: ΔWGA 47.8 (A-SSD, CelebA).

vs. Balanced Retrain: 3.0 / −7.0  ⟵  near-zero reference

03 Why This Matters

WGA and feature-probe scores don't prove shortcuts are gone — ART is a plug-in audit you can run on any debiasing or unlearning pipeline you already trust.

04 Critical Edge

The "unlearning fails" conclusion rests largely on self-built A-* baselines the authors designed — no standardized association-unlearning baselines exist.

— Critical Assessment · Rigor

Methodology

Find → Gate → Restore, on frozen penultimate features

ART is a post-hoc, feature-space diagnostic run on the frozen penultimate features hᵢ = φ(xᵢ) ∈ ℝ²⁰⁴⁸ of a trained ResNet-50 classifier. It tests whether controlled amplification of pre-existing shortcut structure brings back shortcut behavior under the original classifier head ψ — not a retrained one.

① Find
Estimate the class-conditional association direction u_c separating the two attribute groups within each class, after a partial label-null correction (ρ=0.5) removes trivial between-class leakage.
② Gate
Drop noisy/poorly-supported directions: keep u_c only if separation T_c > 0.5 and each subgroup has ≥ 20 samples.
③ Restore
Amplify the gated residual component at β=2 and re-apply the original head: h′ = h + β·P_U(h−μ). Measure how much shortcut behavior returns.
ΔWGA = WGA(β=0) − WGA(β)  ·  ΔCSR = CSR(β) − CSR(β=0)  →  larger = stronger restoration

Practitioner Tool

Probe–ART taxonomy: where does your method sit?

A 2D map — probe accuracy (is the shortcut readable?) vs. ART vulnerability (does the head still use it?) — partitioning method–dataset pairs. Thresholds are heuristic (probe > 70%, ΔWGA > 15 pt) and unvalidated.

probe high · ART high

Restorable shortcut

A-NegGrad+, A-SCRUB, A-SSD — readable AND reactivatable. The danger zone.

probe high · ART low

Functional decoupling

Shortcut is readable but the head no longer uses it. ART-head lands here.

probe low · ART high

Probe miss

Linear probes miss it; the original head still exploits it. Probes alone are unsafe.

probe low · ART low

Deletion-like

Balanced Retrain. Note: low ART ≠ true deletion (nonlinear recovery may still work).

Strengths

What lands

  • Clean three-stage formalization with a label-null correction (ρ) that separates within-class attribute structure from between-class label variation.
  • Strong negative controls (shuffled-a, random, matched-subspace) genuinely substantiate association-specificity — rare in auditing work.
  • Tri-axis evaluation — probes (readability) + ART (restorability) + output metrics — enables the four-quadrant taxonomy.
  • Brutally honest: states low ART ≠ deletion, admits no standardized baselines, flags β as a stress test, discloses ART-head clean-WGA cost.
  • Multiclass ISIC extension (date-stamp artifact) shows the method isn't wedded to binary settings — a realistic medical-imaging failure mode.

Limitations

Where it frays

  • Linear, penultimate-space only. Cannot see nonlinear/cross-layer structure; may produce false negatives (acknowledged) — a real risk for an auditing tool.
  • Needs a labeled audit split with both target and attribute labels — limits real-world multi-attribute or unlabeled use.
  • Single backbone (ImageNet-pretrained ResNet-50). No ViT/ConvNeXt; no cross-layer study.
  • Self-built A-* baselines — the "unlearning fails" story is partly self-fulfilling.
  • β=2 inflates numbers (CelebA ΔWGA 4.2 → 32.2 across β=0.25→2); choose β judiciously.
  • ART-head sacrifices clean WGA (CelebA 81.8 → 50.6) — a decoupling probe, not a deployable fix.

Open Questions

Where this could go next

  1. How does ART behave on transformer backbones (ViT, ConvNeXt) and across earlier layers — are mid-network shortcuts more or less restorable?
  2. Can ART extend to nonlinear association directions (kernelized / small-MLP) to cut the acknowledged false-negative risk?
  3. What happens when attribute labels are unknown at audit time — can unsupervised estimation recover similar signals?
  4. Is there a principled (non-heuristic) way to set the taxonomy thresholds, calibrated against known-deleted ground truth?
  5. Does ART-head generalise beyond A-NegGrad+, and can the CelebA clean-WGA collapse (81.8→50.6) be softened?
  6. Could ART port to vision-language / generative models, where associations span cross-attention layers rather than one penultimate feature?
