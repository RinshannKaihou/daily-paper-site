Why this matters

On-device SLM fine-tuning energy is under-studied versus inference- and datacenter-DVFS. This gives a deployable, interpretable baseline for edge AI — and shows larger models save energy more reliably.

Critical edge

The 26.73% headline is cherry-picked from one model-task cell; several small-model cells are negative (down to −9.44%), and the decision tree scores only 0.62 LOOCV accuracy on just 27 samples.

Methodology

5-phase pipeline · Jetson AGX Orin

Energy-vs-frequency is U-shaped: optima cluster in 510–816 MHz, and the default uncapped MAXN Mode 0 leaves roughly a quarter of fine-tuning energy on the table.

Workloads span encoder-only BERT variants (small 29M → large 340M, plus DeBERTa-v2-xlarge 900M) and decoder-only Pythia variants (31M / 70M / 410M / 1B), fine-tuned one epoch with AdamW (LR 2×10⁻⁵) and FP16 on the GLUE benchmark.

GPU frequency is swept across 11 discrete levels (306–1300 MHz) under user-space governor control, while power is captured via NVIDIA tegrastats at 10 ms sampling. SST-2 / QNLI / MRPC train the policy; QQP / RTE / CoLA / STS-B / MNLI / WNLI are held out as unseen evaluation tasks.

The optimal-frequency predictor is a Decision Tree over just 5 features — model type, GPU utilization, layers, hidden size, average sequence length — trained on 27 model-task pairs with grid-search + LOOCV, then pruned to the compact Algorithm 1. MLG splits primarily on depth (≤7 vs >7 layers), then sequence length, with a GPU-utilization override (UGPU≤22 → 1020 MHz).

MLG · Algorithm 1 (distilled)

  1. if Layers ≤ 7 → small-model branch
  2. elif AvgSeqLen > 53.0 → long-seq rule
  3. elif AvgSeqLen > 32.5 → mid rule
  4. if UGPU ≤ 22.0 → 1020 MHz
  5. else → depth/seq-driven pick from {510,612,714,816,1122}

Strengths & Limitations

side-by-side

+ Strengths

  • Well-motivated niche: on-device SLM fine-tuning energy is genuinely under-studied vs. inference- and datacenter-DVFS.
  • Reusable empirical finding: the U-shaped energy-vs-frequency curve holds across both encoder- and decoder-only families.
  • Policy is interpretable and deployable — needs only static features plus cheap GPU-utilization, no online optimizer.
  • Honest reporting: negative per-cell results and small-model instability disclosed, not hidden.
  • Evaluated on unseen tasks (QQP/RTE/CoLA/STS-B/MNLI/WNLI) — credible out-of-distribution evidence.

− Limitations

  • Single device only (Jetson AGX Orin 64 GB); no Orin NX / Nano / non-Jetson GPU — generality unproven.
  • Only GLUE fine-tuning (single epoch, short); no instruction tuning, long context, or decoder generation.
  • DT classifier accuracy 0.62 on 27 samples — authors flag richer ML models as future work.
  • No comparison to 15W/30W/50W Jetson modes or training-DVFS baselines (Zeus, Perseus, EnvPipe).
  • No code or dataset release; reproducing the DT requires re-collecting all 11-level sweeps.
  • No error bars or significance tests; 10 ms measurements are noisy and the 5-min window is arbitrary.

Open Questions

for further exploration
Q1How do FFG/MLG compare to the 15W/30W/50W Jetson power modes — and to adapted baselines like Zeus or Perseus, not just MAXN Mode 0?
Q2Does jointly scaling CPU / EMC / RAM frequency alongside GPU yield savings beyond the GPU-only policy?
Q3Why do small models (≤70M) show spiky, non-monotonic curves — kernel-launch overhead, batch padding, or memory-bandwidth domination?
Q4Can a more expressive model (GBT, small MLP) or an online Bayesian-optimization governor beat the 0.62-accuracy DT while staying interpretable?
Q5Do savings transfer to instruction-tuning, longer-context, or decoder-generation workloads — and to other Jetson SKUs or non-NVIDIA GPUs?
Q6What is the energy–quality trade-off if accuracy is allowed to drift (quantized fine-tuning, LoRA, lower precision)?
