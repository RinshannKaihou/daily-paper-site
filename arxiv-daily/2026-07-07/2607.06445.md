> **一句话总结：** 作者提出 Analysis-by-Proxy 框架，用轻量级 Q-Former 代理模型探查 Qwen2.5-VL 在"单次前向作为条件编码器"模式下的中间隐藏状态，发现最终层几乎不携带定位信号、真正可解码的空间信息稀疏地集中在若干中间层与若干语义显著的 token 上，并据此用一个简单的边界框叠加 + LoRA 方案，将 Qwen-Image-Edit 在多实体场景下的定位成功率从 57.5% 显著提升，同时取得最高的 VQA 编辑评分与最低的背景 LPIPS。

> **TL;DR:** The paper exposes a 31.5-point localization gap in Qwen-Image-Edit: the Qwen2.5-VL backbone correctly predicts the target bounding box in 89.0% of 200 multi-entity cases when run autoregressively, but the full editing pipeline localizes correctly in only 57.5%. To explain this, the authors train a lightweight Q-Former proxy on a bounding-box regression task over VLM hidden states (OmniEdit-Filtered-1.2M subset) and show that localization signals are sparsely concentrated in input-dependent intermediate layers (15-24) and a few semantically meaningful tokens, not in the final layer that pipelines actually use. Overlaying the proxy's predicted box and fine-tuning a DiT LoRA, they beat Qwen-Image-Edit, FLUX.2, FLUX-Kontext, FIBO-Edit, and the "Full Description" baseline on a Gemini-2.5-Pro VQA score (mean 3.42 vs. baseline 3.17) while achieving the lowest background LPIPS (0.0794 vs. baseline 0.1191).

## 核心贡献

- 揭示"条件编码器"模式下的定位鸿沟：在 200 个多实体场景上，Qwen2.5-VL 自回归生成边界框的准确率高达 89.0%，但完整 Qwen-Image-Edit 流水线的端到端正确定位率仅 57.5%，存在 31.5 个百分点的差距，说明问题不在 VLM 本身的知识，而在"单次前向"提取条件信号的方式。
- 提出 Analysis-by-Proxy 分析框架：用一个轻量、可解释的 Q-Former 代理（仅将 VLM 隐藏状态投影为 K/V，learned queries 投影为 Q），在显式的边界框回归任务上训练，从而充当下游 DiT 的"分析替身"，可在非生成模式下定位 VLM 内部的空间信号。
- 给出两条关键发现：(1) 空间信号沿层极不均匀——最终层 H(27) 表现最差，中间层（15-24）最强；(2) 在 token 层面，信号稀疏地集中在与编辑语义最相关的名词/形容词 token 上。两条共同说明"用固定层 / 最终层 / 跨层池化做 conditioning"的现有做法是结构性次优的。
- 提出输入相关的"决策型注意力头"分析：识别 Q-Former 中那些把注意力几乎全部投到单一 VLM 层的 head，并通过多数投票得到每个样本的最佳层 l*，进一步在该层内找出主导 token，与人类语义判断高度吻合。
- 提出最小化落地方案：将代理预测的边界框以 RGB 形式叠加到原图上，再用一个 rank-16 的 LoRA 教会 Qwen-Image-Edit 的 DiT 把叠加框当作显式定位线索并最终移除该可视化痕迹，在 VQA 评分、背景保持 LPIPS/L2 上同时取得最佳。

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies a striking **localization gap**: on a curated 200-sample multi-entity benchmark, the Qwen2.5-VL-7B backbone autoregressively predicts the correct target bounding box 89.0% of the time, while the end-to-end Qwen-Image-Edit pipeline localizes correctly only 57.5% of the time — a 31.5-point gap that pins the failure on the single-pass conditioning regime rather than on missing VLM knowledge.
- Introduces **Analysis-by-Proxy**, a general probing framework: a lightweight Q-Former (BLIP-2 style) is trained with an explicit GIoU + L1 bounding-box regression objective over VLM hidden states, serving as a tractable, interpretable stand-in for the downstream DiT in a non-generative setting where logit-lens / sampling-based interpretability methods do not apply.
- Produces two structural findings about VLM condition encoders: (i) localization decodability is sharply layer-dependent and **worst at the final layer**, peaking at intermediate layers 15-24; (ii) within a layer the signal is **sparse** and concentrated on a handful of semantically meaningful instruction tokens (the nouns/adjectives that describe the target).
- Shows the informative layer is **input-dependent**: a small set of "decisive" Q-Former attention heads route each sample to its own optimal layer l* via majority vote, and the dominant tokens within that layer correspond precisely to the edit-relevant noun phrases, demonstrating the VLM essentially encodes a dynamic routing structure that current pipelines ignore.
- Delivers a **minimal, effective fix**: overlay the proxy's predicted box onto the source image and LoRA-fine-tune the DiT (rank 16, attention projections only) to consume the overlay as an explicit spatial cue while erasing it from the final output, yielding the best mean VQA score and the lowest background LPIPS among all compared systems.

## Methodology

The work has two parts: an **analysis framework** and a **downstream application**.

**Analysis-by-Proxy.** The object of study is the Qwen-Image-Edit pipeline, which uses Qwen2.5-VL-7B (28 transformer layers) as a non-generative condition encoder: a single forward pass over (image, instruction), after which a fixed subset of hidden states is fed to the DiT. Because the VLM is not sampled, classical autoregressive interpretability (logit lens, attention-to-output tracing) does not apply. The authors therefore train a small **Q-Former** proxy whose learned queries cross-attend to VLM hidden states {H^(l)}_{l∈L} (queries projected only to Q, VLM states only to K/V) and a coordinate head regresses (x1, y1, x2, y2). The proxy is trained on a 128k-sample subset of OmniEdit-Filtered-1.2M, with ground-truth boxes obtained by autoregressively prompting Qwen2.5-VL-Instruct and human-verifying. Loss is L1 for a 100-step warmup, then (1-λ)L1 + λ·L_GIoU with λ = 0.4.

**Probing where the signal lives.** The authors train per-layer proxies, then a "mid-layer text-token only" proxy over L = {15..24}. They define per-(head, query, layer) attention mass A^{(l)}_{h,q}, identify **decisive heads** whose attention mass drops by a mean of 0.6 between the top-ranked and second-ranked layer, and use majority vote to recover a sample-specific optimal layer l*. The same magnitude-ranking is applied at token level within l*, revealing sparse dominant tokens. Finally, they examine VLM-internal attention α_{i,j} from those dominant instruction tokens to image tokens, showing they attend to the correct visual subject (replicating the "grounding" effect of Kaduri et al. but without autoregressive generation).

**Downstream application.** At inference the proxy produces a bounding box that is rendered onto the source RGB image; the VAE encodes the overlaid image; a LoRA (rank 16, α = 16, on q/k/v/out projections of the DiT) trained on a curated synthetic multi-entity dataset teaches Qwen-Image-Edit to use the overlay as a strict localization cue and to remove the box artifact from the output. The curated dataset is built by (1) Gemini 2.5 Pro generating scene+edit prompt pairs containing N-1 identical objects and one anomaly, (2) FIBO generating source images, Qwen-Image-Edit producing harmonized targets, (3) the relationship inverted so the uniform image is the input, and (4) Mistral-Small-3.2-24B re-captioning with spatial-only language ("leftmost", "second from right"). All boxes are human-verified; the set is split 50/50 train/eval (200-sample evaluation set).

## Results & Highlights

- **Localization gap (Section 4.1, n=200 multi-entity scenes):** Qwen2.5-VL-7B autoregressively predicts the correct target bounding box in **89.0%** of samples, while the full Qwen-Image-Edit pipeline localizes the edit correctly in only **57.5%** — a **31.5-point** gap that motivates the entire analysis.
- **Layer decodability (Figure 4):** The proxy trained on the **final layer H^(27)** converges slowly and to a poor bounding-box center-distance error (~0.14). Proxies trained on a **single intermediate layer l ∈ {15..24}** converge much faster and to lower error, and restricting to **user-prompt text tokens only** from layers 15-24 gives the fastest convergence (~0.08). Early/very-late layers (0-13, 25-26) fail to converge.
- **Attention sparsity (Figure 6):** Across decisive Q-Former heads, the mean drop in layer-wise attention mass between the top-ranked and second-ranked VLM layer is **0.6**, indicating the informative layer is sharply and consistently selected on a per-input basis. The same steep drop-off is observed at token level within l*.
- **Main editing benchmark (Table 1, n=200, mean ± SEM):** The proposed method achieves **mean VQA score = 3.42** (=(3.2550 + 3.3300 + 3.5500)/3 via Gemini 2.5 Pro) and **background LPIPS = 0.0794**, versus **Qwen-Image-Edit baseline = 3.17 / 0.1191**, **FLUX.2 = 2.55 / 0.1377**, **FLUX-Kontext = 2.26 / 0.1847**, **FIBO-Edit = 2.36 / 0.2560**, and the strongest conditioning baseline **"Full Description" = 3.27 / 0.0818**. The method wins on all three VQA sub-scores (edit 3.2550, accuracy 3.3300, leakage-resistance 3.5500) while matching the best background preservation; notably the autoregressive "Full Description" variant is the strongest baseline, directly corroborating the authors' hypothesis that sequential decoding surfaces spatial cues a single pass leaves dormant.
- **Compute cost:** Q-Former training ≈ 60 GB VRAM, ~2 hours on a single A100 80GB; LoRA fine-tuning of the DiT ≈ 5 hours on the same node — minimal relative to retraining a conditioning architecture.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high).** The framing is genuinely fresh: most VLM-interpretability work (Kaduri et al., Cohen et al., Nikankin et al.) requires autoregressive sampling and analyzes the *generated* output; this paper is the first to systematically probe VLM hidden states **in the non-generative condition-encoder regime** that real editing pipelines use. The "proxy as a stand-in for the DiT" trick is a clean methodological contribution that other groups can reuse.
- **Rigor (mostly strong, with caveats).** The analysis is carefully designed — per-layer vs. text-token-only proxies, decisive-head identification, majority-vote layer routing — and the headline 31.5-point gap is measured on a human-judged set. However, the **evaluation set is small (n=200) and synthetic**: scenes are auto-generated to contain N-1 identical objects plus one anomaly, which is a much cleaner distribution than real-world editing requests (real instructions rarely demand disambiguating eight near-identical objects by ordinal position). This risks **overstating the gap** for natural usage even if it is real for the stress-test setting.
- **Baseline honesty (good but asymmetric).** The authors include FLUX.2, FLUX-Kontext, FIBO-Edit, and two conditioning variants (Full Description, Norm-AVG), which is reasonable. Yet several baselines (FLUX.2, FLUX-Kontext) are **closed commercial systems** compared at a single qualitative figure and the metric table without matched compute or hyperparameter tuning, so their weaker numbers partly reflect system-level disadvantage rather than a pure conditioning-architecture comparison. The "Full Description" baseline being within 0.15 of the proposed method on mean VQA while using no extra trained module is a **closer contest than the narrative implies**.
- **Reproducibility (strong).** Open-weights backbone, MIT/CC-BY-4.0 release promised, modest compute (single A100), and detailed appendices (dataset construction prompts, LoRA config r=16/α=16, loss schedule, 50/50 split) make the work reproducible.
- **Significance (medium-high, scope-limited).** The diagnostic value is clear and likely to influence future conditioning-architecture design (input-dependent layer selection, token pruning). The **practical fix is intentionally minimal** — the authors admit this — and is demonstrated only on a single pipeline (Qwen-Image-Edit) precisely because it is the only one whose VLM jointly consumes text and image; generalization to FLUX.2-style text-only conditioners is asserted but not shown.
- **Red flags.** (1) The 200-sample test set is small enough that the SEM-overlapped confidence intervals of "Ours" and "Full Description" partially overlap on VQA-edit and VQA-leakage, so some wins are not statistically decisive at n=200. (2) The autoregressive ground-truth boxes used to train both the proxy and the LoRA are produced by the same Qwen2.5-VL model whose hidden states are being analyzed, creating a mild **circularity**: the proxy is partly learning to invert the VLM's own generation, which inflates the apparent decodability of intermediate layers. (3) No ablation isolates the effect of the **box-overlay mechanism** versus the **LoRA fine-tuning** itself — a LoRA trained on the same data without boxes would be a necessary control that is missing.

## Strengths

- Provides the first mechanistic account of *why* VLM-conditioned editors mislocalize, framed as a concrete, testable hypothesis (single-pass vs. autoregressive decoding) rather than a vague quality complaint.
- The Q-Former proxy is a genuinely reusable interpretability tool: lightweight (single A100, 2h), architecture-agnostic in principle, and produces human-readable routing decisions (decisive heads, dominant tokens) that match semantic intuition.
- The fix is minimal and additive — a LoRA + a box overlay — yet moves all three VQA sub-scores up and the background LPIPS down, including over the strong "describe-then-edit" baseline.
- Honest about limitations: the authors explicitly state the fix is "intentionally straightforward" and limited to one pipeline, and acknowledge dual-use/deepfake risks in the Impact Statement.

## Limitations

- Evaluation set is small (n=200), synthetic, and biased toward ordinal-disambiguation scenes; real-world editing distributions may show a smaller gap.
- Only Qwen-Image-Edit is studied end-to-end because it is currently the only open pipeline whose VLM jointly conditions on image and text; generalization to FLUX.2 / FIBO / text-only conditioners is argued but not demonstrated.
- Circular data construction: ground-truth boxes (proxy training and LoRA targets) come from the same Qwen2.5-VL whose internal representations are probed.
- Missing control: no LoRA-only (without box overlay) or box-only (without LoRA) ablation to attribute gains cleanly.
- Several baselines are closed commercial systems not re-tuned for this benchmark, making cross-system numbers not strictly apples-to-apples.
- Some headline VQA wins have overlapping standard errors at n=200, so statistical significance is not established for every sub-metric.

## Relevance

与用户对图像编辑、扩散模型与多模态大模型可解释性的研究方向高度相关：本文正落在"VLM 作为条件编码器 + DiT 编辑"这一主流范式下，提供了对 conditioning 信号失配的机理分析与可操作的修复方案，对设计下一代图像编辑架构、改进 token/layer 级 conditioning 以及 VLM 内部机制探查都有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the **input-dependent optimal layer l\*** generalize across VLM families (InternVL3.5, Qwen3-VL, Pixtral), or is the decisive-head routing an artifact of Qwen2.5-VL's training?
- Can the proxy be trained **end-to-end with the DiT** (jointly optimizing localization and editing) instead of as a frozen two-stage predictor, and would that close the remaining VQA gap to the autoregressive "Full Description" baseline?
- How much of the 31.5-point localization gap survives on **natural, non-ordinal** editing instructions (e.g., edit-by-appearance, edit-by-region) versus the synthetic N-1-identical-objects stress test?
- Would replacing the box overlay with a **latent-level spatial mask** (no RGB artifact to erase) simplify training and remove the LoRA's dependency on the rendering style?
- The decisive heads route to a single layer by majority vote — is the runner-up layer usually adjacent, and does an **ensembling over top-k layers** reduce variance on hard inputs?
- Since the ground-truth boxes come from the same VLM being probed, how decodable are intermediate layers when boxes are sourced from an **independent detector** (e.g., Grounding-DINO)?
