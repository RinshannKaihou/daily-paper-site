> **一句话总结：** 本文诊断出长程智能体 OPD 训练中"外部 rollout 深度固定浪费算力"与"内部 token 级 KL 损失过度集中于浅层轮次"两类预算错配，提出 TurnOPD 通过自适应 rollout 深度控制器（H_eff/H_cov）与渐进式轮次归一化损失控制器，在 ALFWorld/WebShop/Multi-Hop Search 三大基准上以最高 2.29× 的训练加速取得更高准确率。

> **TL;DR:** TurnOPD reframes on-policy distillation (OPD) for long-horizon agents as a turn-level budgeting problem, fixing two inefficiencies — fixed full-horizon rollouts that waste compute on low-signal tail turns, and trajectory-level KL that starves deep decision turns of loss mass. With an adaptive rollout-depth controller (Hctrl = max(Heff, Hcov)) plus a progressive trajectory→turn KL blend, TurnOPD lifts ALFWorld-1.7B Same-Step Avg@4 from 83.00 (vanilla OPD) to 86.29 while cutting 100-step wall time from 4.42h to 1.93h (2.29× speedup), and improves the accuracy–time frontier across ALFWorld, WebShop, and Multi-Hop Search.

## 核心贡献

- **轮次级 OPD 诊断框架**：首次系统刻画长程智能体 OPD 中监督信号沿交互轮次的分布，发现反向 KL 信号前重后轻、非平稳，且深层轮次的"结果区分力"（K_fail − K_succ）随深度衰减甚至反转。
- **污染-压缩机制（Proposition 1）**：将 token 分布分解为"上下文强制分量"与"自由分量"，证明可观测 KL ≤ (1−λ)·自由分量 KL，从理论上解释了为何深层 KL 衰减并不等于学生已学会教师。
- **TurnOPD 双控制器**：自适应 rollout 深度控制器（效率臂 H_eff 的幸存者加权 KL 质心 + 覆盖臂 H_cov 的成功完成 80% 分位数）解决外部错配；渐进式轮次归一化损失控制器（α 随训练进度从 0 线性升至 1）解决内部错配。
- **统一的效率-准确率前沿**：在三大基准上证明 TurnOPD 在等算力预算下准确率更高，在等步数下训练更快（最高 2.29×），消融显示两控制器互补——深度控制负责"效率杠杆"，损失重分配负责"优化杠杆"。

## Key Contributions

- **A turn-resolved diagnosis of agent OPD**: The paper measures per-turn reverse-KL, the success/fail KL gap G_t = K_fail − K_succ, and the realized KL loss-share, showing that on ALFWorld-4B the deepest third of reliable turns receives only 3.6–4.5% of the KL loss budget (11–13% on Multi-Hop Search), while shallow turns dominate.
- **A formal contamination-compression model (Proposition 1)**: Decomposing each next-token distribution into a context-forced component p_F and a free component, the authors prove D_KL(π_S‖π_T) ≤ (1−λ)·D_KL(p_S^free‖p_T^free), giving a structural reason why raw KL decays with depth and why the success/fail gap can invert on ALFWorld.
- **TurnOPD, a two-controller budgeting method**: (1) an adaptive rollout-depth controller H_ctrl = max(H_eff, H_cov) blending a survivor-weighted KL centroid with a success-conditioned 80%-quantile coverage floor, refreshed by periodic full-depth probes with EMA smoothing; (2) a progressive loss controller that linearly blends trajectory-level (token-mass) and turn-level (uniform) KL weights as α ramps 0→1.
- **Empirical frontier advance**: Across ALFWorld, WebShop, and Multi-Hop Search with three student-teacher pairs, TurnOPD achieves the best Least-Time Avg@4 and up to 2.29× wall-clock speedup over vanilla OPD, with ablations cleanly attributing efficiency gains to adaptive depth and accuracy gains to turn-balanced loss.

## Methodology

TurnOPD is built directly on the diagnosis in Section 4, which first shows that vanilla OPD suffers two budget mismatches.

**Diagnosis.** The authors instrument 100 steps of vanilla OPD on ALFWorld-4B (Qwen3-4B ← Qwen3-8B-GRPO) and Multi-Hop Search-2B (Qwen3.5-2B ← Qwen3.5-9B-GRPO). Three measurements are introduced: (i) raw per-turn reverse-KL, which is front-loaded and non-stationary; (ii) the outcome-separation gap G_t = K_t^fail − K_t^succ, which weakens and on ALFWorld even inverts with depth (successful trajectories show *higher* KL because failed ones fall into low-entropy loops); and (iii) the realized batch KL loss-share per turn. The contamination-compression model (Eq. 4–8, Proposition 1) then proves that a high context-forced mass λ(c) compresses observable KL even when free-component disagreement persists, explaining the depth decay.

**External-mismatch controller — adaptive rollout depth.** The latent optimum H* is bounded by an efficiency–coverage sandwich (Appendix C). TurnOPD estimates it online as H_ctrl = max(H_eff, H_cov). The efficiency arm H_eff is the survivor-weighted KL centroid: m_t = [K_t]_+ · (n_t/n_0), q_t = m_t / Σm_j, H_eff = round(Σ t·q_t). The coverage arm H_cov = Q̂_p(L_succ) is the p=0.80 quantile of success-conditioned completion length. Both are refreshed only from periodic full-depth *probe* rollouts (every 8 steps, plus a warmup) — truncated routine rollouts are excluded from statistics to avoid censoring bias — and smoothed with an EMA (α_ema=0.30) before being clipped to [H_min, H_max].

**Internal-mismatch controller — progressive turn-normalized loss.** The final per-turn weight is q_t^blend = (1−α)·q_t^traj + α·q_t^turn, where q_t^traj = n_t/Σn_j (token-mass) and q_t^turn = 1/T (uniform). The blend coefficient α = clip((progress − s)/(e − s), 0, 1) ramps linearly from 0 (pure trajectory-level, stable early) to 1 (turn-balanced, refining deep decisions late). The two controllers regulate complementary resources — depth controls how much interaction is collected, normalization controls where the collected loss mass goes.

**Training stack.** Pure top-50 reverse-KL distillation (kl_coef=0, kl_loss_coef=0), AdamW lr=1e-6, batch=64, 100 optimizer steps, importance-ratio clipping 0.9/9, validation mean@4 at temperature 0.85, on 32× NVIDIA H20-80GB GPUs.

## Results & Highlights

- **ALFWorld-1.7B (Same-Step Avg@4):** TurnOPD reaches **86.29** vs **83.00** for vanilla OPD and **80.06** for TCOD-F2B — a +3.29 / +6.23 absolute gain — while cutting 100-step wall time from **4.42h to 1.93h**, a **2.29×** speedup (Table 2). Under the Least-Time protocol TurnOPD scores 85.60 vs vanilla OPD's 73.52.
- **ALFWorld-4B:** TurnOPD achieves 92.21 Same-Step Avg@4 (vs 91.81 vanilla OPD, 86.50 TCOD-F2B) in 2.16h vs 2.86h (1.33×), and on the Least-Time metric (91.73) even exceeds the GRPO teacher reference (90.75) — though the teacher is a context point, not a baseline.
- **WebShop-1.7B:** TurnOPD improves both accuracy (**82.80** vs vanilla OPD 81.65, TCOD-F2B 81.66) and wall time (**1.24h vs 1.57h**, 1.26×); Least-Time 82.80 vs 76.98 for vanilla OPD.
- **Multi-Hop Search-2B:** Under Least-Time TurnOPD scores **47.24** vs vanilla OPD **45.77** and TCOD-F2B **45.64**, with wall time **2.94h vs 4.45h** (1.51×). Per-source, TurnOPD lifts PopQA to 49.38 (vs 48.00) and 2Wiki to 56.00 (vs 50.81).
- **Ablation (Table 5, ALFWorld-1.7B):** Adaptive depth alone cuts wall time 4.42h→1.96h but slightly *drops* accuracy (82.8 vs 83.0); linear-blend norm alone raises accuracy to 85.1 at 2.59h; full TurnOPD gets the best of both (86.3 at 1.93h), confirming the two controllers are complementary.
- **KL-normalization study (Table 6):** trajectory-level KL assigns the deepest third only 3.2/0.7/1.2% of budget in early/mid/late training; hard turn-level KL swings this to ~30% throughout; the linear blend ramps deep-turn budget from 12.8%→27.7% as α rises 0.17→0.83, yielding the best Same-Step Avg@4 (85.1 vs 85.0 hard, 83.0 trajectory).
- **Loss-budget diagnosis (Table 1):** the deepest reliable third of turns carries only **3.6–4.5%** of total KL loss on ALFWorld and **11–13%** on Multi-Hop Search under vanilla OPD, despite holding 31–92% of the shallow prefix's raw KL.

## Authors & Credibility

The work is a collaboration between **Fudan University** (Yuhang Zhou, Dengyun Peng, Jingjing Chen) and **Tencent Hunyuan** (Kai Zheng as project lead, Haoling Li, Can Xu). The authors have a strong recent footprint in agentic LLM training: Zhou et al. authored OffSeeker (deep-research agents) and co-authored the Owl multi-agent system (NeurIPS 2026), and the Tencent side has shipped distillation/agent work at scale (Qwen-series distillation, AgentMath). The methodology is unusually rigorous for a training-efficiency paper: it leads with a formal diagnosis (Proposition 1, proven via joint convexity of KL in Appendix B; an existence proof for H* in Appendix C), instruments concrete per-turn metrics, and only then proposes the controllers — a theory-then-method structure that lends the empirical claims mechanistic credibility. Full hyperparameters (Table 7), the complete training algorithm (Algorithm 1), and dataset split construction (Table 8) are disclosed, supporting reproducibility.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely fresh angle. Most OPD work (MiniLLM, GKD, Thinking Machines' OPD) treats each example as a flat sequence; TCOD adds a trajectory-length curriculum and SOD does step-level reweighting for tool reasoning, but neither *budgets both rollout depth and loss allocation at the turn level with probe-based online statistics*. The contamination-compression proposition is a real (if idealized) theoretical contribution, not hand-waving.
- **Rigor of baselines:** Mixed. TCOD-F2B is the obvious closest competitor and is included, but on the larger ALFWorld-4B student TCOD-F2B (86.50) actually *underperforms* vanilla OPD (90.79) — i.e., the curriculum baseline is mis-tuned or ill-suited once the student is already competent, which weakens TCOD as a "strong baseline" and makes TurnOPD's win there partly a comparison against a stragger. SOD (step-wise distillation) is discussed as related work but never run. No comparison against entropy-aware or relaxed OPD variants ([8], [11], [12]) is provided.
- **Honesty of claims:** Mostly honest. The authors openly report that vanilla OPD *retains a Same-Step advantage on Multi-Hop Search* (47.82 vs 47.24), and that adaptive depth alone does *not* fix loss allocation (82.8 vs 83.0). The "exceeds the teacher" highlight on ALFWorld-4B is technically an apples-to-oranges comparison (OPD-distilled student avg@4 vs GRPO teacher avg@4 with reward) and should be read as context, not superiority.
- **Missing ablations / weak spots:** (1) Only **100 optimizer steps** for every experiment — no scaling curve shows whether the frontier advantage holds at 500 or 1000 steps or simply shrinks as vanilla OPD catches up with more compute. (2) The marginal benefit of *progressive* blending over a *hard* turn-level switch is tiny (85.1 vs 85.0 in Table 6), so the "progressive" component is close to ablated away by a simpler design — the paper does not stress-test this. (3) The main controller's H_cov uses a **success-conditioned CDF**, which requires task-success labels; the reward-free full-population variant is offered only in a sensitivity sweep (Section 7.3), so reward-free applicability is under-evidenced.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong on paper — Algorithm 1, Table 7 hyperparameters, and Appendix A environment/tool specs are detailed, and all three benchmarks are public. But the training corpus is "externally prepared" (≈30k Search-R1-format questions, ≈5k ALFWorld instances) and not released, and the GRPO teachers (which materially set the achievable ceiling) are not checkpointed publicly, so an exact reproduction would require re-GRPO'ing teachers.
- **Significance:** Solid contribution to a live problem (efficient agent distillation); the diagnosis and the contamination-compression framing are likely to be reused even if the specific controllers are superseded.
- **Red flags:** None severe. The speedups are partly mechanical (truncating rollouts trivially reduces wall time — the real question is accuracy retention, which holds), and the "2.29× faster" headline is the best case on the smallest student; on the 4B student the speedup is 1.33×.

## Strengths

- Diagnosis-first methodology: empirical per-turn analysis plus a proven proposition give the method a clear mechanistic rationale rather than a trick that happens to work.
- Clean two-controller decomposition with complementary, well-isolated roles (efficiency vs optimization), confirmed by ablation.
- Broad evaluation across three structurally different agent settings (embodied planning, web navigation, multi-hop QA) and three student scales/families, not a single cherry-picked benchmark.
- Practical and immediately deployable: the controllers are lightweight probes plus a reweighting scheme atop a standard reverse-KL OPD stack, with no architecture changes.
- Honest reporting of cases where the method does not win (Multi-Hop Search Same-Step) and of the small margin of progressive blending over a hard switch.

## Limitations

- All experiments run for only 100 optimizer steps; long-horizon training dynamics and whether vanilla OPD closes the gap given more compute are untested.
- The coverage arm H_cov in the main results depends on task-success labels, limiting direct applicability to genuinely reward-free agentic settings (the full-population CDF variant mitigates this but is only swept, not used in the main table).
- The advantage of *progressive* (linear-blend) KL normalization over a *hard* turn-level switch is within noise (85.1 vs 85.0), so the progressive component's value is modest.
- The contamination-compression decomposition (forced vs free component) is an explanatory model; λ(c) and the free-component disagreement are never measured directly, only inferred from the KL/gap behavior they are invoked to explain.
- TCOD-F2B, the principal non-vanilla baseline, underperforms vanilla OPD on the 4B student, so the "vs strong baseline" framing is weakest exactly where TurnOPD's margins are smallest; SOD and other step-aware distillers are cited but not benchmarked.
- Training/teacher corpora and GRPO teacher checkpoints are not released, which caps third-party reproducibility.

## Relevance

与长程智能体训练、on-policy 蒸馏及智能体效率优化方向高度相关：TurnOPD 提供的"轮次级预算"视角（自适应 rollout 深度 + 渐进式损失重分配）可直接迁移到 tool-use、deep-research、多轮检索等需要长交互轨迹的智能体 RL/蒸馏流程中，对降低训练算力门槛有实际价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does TurnOPD's accuracy–time advantage persist at 500–1000 optimizer steps, or does vanilla OPD eventually close the gap once shallow-turn convergence is complete?
- Can the contamination-compression decomposition be made *measurable* — e.g., estimating λ(c) per turn from teacher/student entropy on held-out forced spans — to turn Proposition 1 from an explanation into a monitored training signal?
- How does TurnOPD interact with reward-based RL (GRPO/PPO) stacked on top of OPD, where success signals are already available but KL supervision is not the only objective?
- The reward-free full-population CDF variant works on ALFWorld-1.7B (Section 7.3); does it hold on WebShop and Multi-Hop Search, where failure modes (stalling, loops) differ?
- Would combining TurnOPD's turn-budgeting with SOD's step-level reweighting or entropy-aware OPD yield further gains, or are these mechanisms redundant?
- Is the progressive linear blend meaningfully better than a hard turn-level switch on harder/larger students, given the near-tie (85.1 vs 85.0) on ALFWorld-1.7B?
