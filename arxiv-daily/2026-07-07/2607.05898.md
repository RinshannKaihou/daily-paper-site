> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一种基于成员推断攻击（MIA）的假设检验审计器，通过数据相关的下界 ε_LB 来经验性证伪机器遗忘算法的（ε, δ）保证；在 CIFAR-100 和 Shakespeare 上验证发现，模型裁剪（model clipping）与回滚删除（rewind-to-delete）等有证明保证的方法 ε_LB ≈ 0（未被证伪），而 Hessian 遗忘、交替升降（IDA）、遗忘集梯度上升、保留集微调等启发式方法 ε_LB 经常超过 50–142，显示遗忘失效。

> **TL;DR:** The authors build a membership-inference-based auditor that, for δ=0, returns a data-dependent lower bound ε_LB on the true unlearning parameter ε of any certified (ε, δ) algorithm by planting many canary forget-batches and predicting which were included; on CIFAR-100 and Shakespeare it cleanly separates certified methods (model clipping, rewind-to-delete → ε_LB ≈ 0, not falsified even at target ε up to 10⁵) from heuristic ones (Hessian-based unlearning reaches ε_LB = 142.5, IDA up to 100.5, pure retain fine-tuning 81.5, forget ascent 79.5), and flags Hessian-based unlearning whose convex-loss certificate silently fails in the nonconvex regime.

## 核心贡献

- **实用的审计框架**：提出一种基于成员推断（MIA）与假设检验的审计器，对任意 certified (ε, δ) 遗忘算法输出数据相关的下界 ε_LB，以可控 Type I 错误率（默认 ζ = 0.05）拒绝原假设 ε < ε_LB；高 ε_LB 即遗忘保证偏弱的直接证据。
- **更强的威胁模型**：设定一个黑盒但掌握学习算法、遗忘算法、数据加载 shuffler 与初始模型权重 x_o 的强对手；并证明 model clipping（Koloskova et al. 2025）与 R2D（Mu & Klabjan 2025）在该更强模型下仍保持 (ε, δ) 保证。
- **从局部差分隐私（LDP）归约出新界**：通过三角不等式将子集级遗忘保证归约为 (2ε, 0)-LDP，再扩展 Steinke et al. (2023) 的单次运行审计范式，给出在重叠得分（overlap score）下同时支持均值（Chernoff）与中位数聚合的 ε 下界定理（Theorem 4.2）。
- **两个具体实例化**：批内包含/排除预测（Instantiation I，LiRA 风格逐批高斯似然比，适合大 m）与联合符号向量预测（Instantiation II，枚举 Sm 利用跨批相关性）。
- **清晰的方法分层**：在 CIFAR-100（TinyNet，~55% val acc）与 Shakespeare（2 层字符 LSTM，~0.53 acc）上系统审计 6 类方法，揭示 certified 与 uncertified 之间的尖锐分离，并量化 uniform vs. adversarial split 的差异。
- **诊断价值验证**：Hessian-based 遗忘（Zhang et al. 2025）声称在凸损失下 certified，但本文在非凸 TinyNet 上测得 ε_LB = 142.5（uniform split），证明其证书前提在部署时静默失效——这是审计器最具说服力的诊断案例。

## Key Contributions

- **A practical falsification auditor**: a hypothesis-testing framework, built on membership-inference-style canary attacks, that returns a data-dependent lower bound ε_LB on the true unlearning parameter ε for any certified (ε, δ) algorithm, with Type I error controlled at ζ = 0.05.
- **A strengthened, deployment-relevant threat model**: a black-box adversary who additionally observes the initial weights x_o and the per-epoch shuffling order π; the paper argues the two audited certified methods (model clipping, R2D) remain (ε, δ)-certified under this stronger adversary.
- **New ε lower bounds via an LDP reduction**: extends the single-run many-canary paradigm of Steinke et al. (2023) to subset-level unlearning guarantees, providing valid bounds for both mean (Chernoff-style) and median aggregation of the overlap score (Theorem 4.2), with a clean monotonicity corollary (B.2).
- **Two auditor instantiations plus a pairwise baseline**: batchwise inclusion/exclusion scoring (LiRA-style per-batch Gaussian likelihood ratios, scalable to large m) and joint sign-vector prediction (exploits cross-batch correlations when |Sm| is enumerable), benchmarked against an adapted Nasr et al. (2021) pairwise auditor.
- **Empirical separation of certified vs. heuristic unlearning**: a sharp dichotomy on CIFAR-100 and Shakespeare — certified methods (model clipping ε_LB ≈ 0 up to target ε = 10⁵; R2D tight but small) are not falsified, while heuristic methods produce large bounds (Hessian 142.5, IDA up to 100.5, pure retain FT 81.5, forget ascent 79.5).
- **A high-value diagnostic case**: Hessian-based unlearning (Zhang et al. 2025) is provably certified only under convexity; the auditor flags it with ε_LB = 142.5 in the nonconvex TinyNet regime, demonstrating silent certification failure at deployment.

## Methodology

The auditor is given black-box query access to the training algorithm A and the unlearning algorithm U, plus (under the threat model) the initial weights x_o and the shuffling order π. The dataset is partitioned into n batches; the candidate forget pool D_f contains m batches and the retain set D_r the remaining n − m. Across L independent runs the auditor (1) samples a balanced sign vector S^(ℓ) ∈ Sm deciding which candidate batches enter the forget set, (2) runs training followed by unlearning to obtain f_u, (3) emits a guess Ŝ^(ℓ) with exactly r/2 entries +1 and r/2 entries −1 (the remaining m − r entries are abstentions), and (4) records the overlap score V^(ℓ) counting correct non-zero guesses.

The induced mechanism M : Sm → {−1,0,+1}^m is post-processing of f_u, so certified guarantees pass through. Lemma 4.1 reduces the problem to (2ε, 0)-local differential privacy via the triangle inequality f_u(S₁) ≈_{2ε,0} f_u(S₂). Theorem 4.2 then converts the empirical mean or median of {V^(ℓ)} across runs into a valid ε lower bound using a pointwise posterior bound p_ε(u), a Chernoff (mean) bound, and a binomial (median) bound; setting the chosen tail probability to ζ and solving for ε yields ε_LB (divided by 2 to undo the LDP reduction).

Two instantiations of M realize the prediction step. Instantiation I (batchwise) fits, per forget example, an "in-distribution" and an "out-of-distribution" Gaussian to logit-scaled confidences collected over Γ calibration runs (LiRA-style), sums per-point log-likelihood ratios into a batch score Λ_j, and predicts +1/−1 for the top/bottom r/2 batches — scalable to large m. Instantiation II (joint) treats each candidate sign vector as a hypothesis, fits one Gaussian per (candidate, example) pair, and returns the argmax-likelihood vector (r = m), capturing cross-batch correlations when |Sm| is enumerable. A pairwise auditor adapted from Nasr et al. (2021) with Clopper–Pearson confidence intervals serves as a baseline. The whole procedure is restricted to δ = 0; an extension to δ > 0 is sketched in Appendix C but not empirically validated.

## Results & Highlights

- **Certified methods are not falsified.** Auditing model clipping (Koloskova et al. 2025) on CIFAR-100 with B = 750, m = 6, Γ = 50, L = 100 yields ε_LB ≈ 0 across a grid sweeping target ε over five orders of magnitude (up to ε = 10⁵), and the per-iterate ε_LB(t) stays near zero throughout the noisy phase — the audit gains no traction even between certification checkpoints.
- **Rewind-to-delete attains tighter-but-small bounds.** R2D produces tighter lower bounds than model clipping across the sweep (single-shot full-batch noise calibration vs. per-step noise budget), and both methods remain well below their claimed ε.
- **Hessian-based unlearning fails in the nonconvex regime.** On CIFAR-100 under uniform splitting the auditor reports ε_LB = 142.5 for Hessian-based unlearning (Zhang et al. 2025) — larger than any heuristic method tested — vs. ε_LB = 10 under adversarial splitting; this directly exposes the silent failure of its convexity precondition on the nonconvex TinyNet model.
- **Heuristic methods show large, often >50, bounds.** On CIFAR-100 (uniform/adversarial): pure retain fine-tuning ε_LB = 81.5 / 84; forget ascent 79.5 / 86; IDA reaches 100.5 / 0.0 (q = 1) up to 95.5 / 66 (q = 4), i.e. unlearning quality degrades monotonically with q on the adversarial split (0 → 18 → 66 for q ∈ {1, 2, 4}).
- **Shakespeare largely confirms the pattern.** On the LSTM character model, IDA yields ε_LB between 9.97 and 14.42 depending on (λ, E_f); forget ascent is nearly undetectable (ε_LB = 0.17) while retain fine-tuning reaches 16.06; Hessian-based unlearning gives ε_LB = 4.18 — all methods retain forget/test accuracy ≈ 0.56 / 0.53, so the audit is detecting privacy leakage rather than utility collapse.
- **Convex-loss audit is tight.** On a bounded cubic loss (d = 1) and a linear-regression output-perturbation mechanism, ε_LB saturates near 10.19 at 2L = 200,000 test points and tracks the certified ε far more closely than in the nonconvex case (Table 2), confirming that the large nonconvex gaps reflect genuine weak unlearning rather than pure auditor slack.
- **Pairwise baseline saturates quickly.** With L = 10, the Nasr et al.-style pairwise auditor yields a zero lower bound even at FPR = FNR = 0 due to loose Clopper–Pearson intervals — it is competitive only when bounds are small.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are Sahasrajit Sarmasarkar (Stanford University), Anastasia Koloskova (University of Zurich) and Sanmi Koyejo (Stanford University). Koloskova is an author of the certified model-clipping algorithm (Koloskova et al., 2025) that is one of the two certified methods audited, which gives the team first-hand command of the certified-unlearning machinery they are stress-testing; Koyejo is a senior researcher co-authoring multiple of the cited certified-unlearning works (Allouah et al. 2025; Koloskova et al. 2025). Code is publicly released at the GitHub URL footnoted in the paper, and the theoretical claims (Lemmas 4.1, 4.2; Theorem 4.2; Corollary B.2) are fully proved in the main text and Appendix B. The work is positioned carefully within the DP-auditing lineage (Jagielski et al. 2020; Nasr et al. 2021/2023; Steinke et al. 2023; Mahloujifar et al. 2025) and clearly distinguished from per-sample unlearning-completeness scores (Wang et al. 2025a; Triantafillou et al. 2024) and reconstruction/backdoor audits.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Genuine. The reduction of subset-level certified unlearning to (2ε, 0)-LDP and the resulting Theorem 4.2 (pointwise + Chernoff + median bounds with a monotonicity corollary) are new technical contributions, not a straightforward port of Steinke et al. (2023); the authors are explicit that the single-point DP-audit machinery yields loose bounds here and that new lemmas are needed for the subset neighbouring relation.
- **Rigor**: Strong on theory (full proofs), weaker on cost reporting. The audit's validity rests on Γ calibration runs being independent of L evaluation runs — a requirement the authors honor but do not quantify in compute terms; nowhere in the main text do they report wall-clock, GPU-hours, or total number of training/unlearning invocations, even though certified-method settings use L = 100 and uncertified use Γ = 50 with up to L = 500 (for the uncertified model-clipping variant). This makes practical reproducibility of the runtime hard to assess.
- **Reproducibility**: Reasonable. Code is released; hyperparameters are largely deferred to Appendices D and G but the key configurations (B = 750 certified / B = 1 uncertified on CIFAR-100; B = 400 on Shakespeare; r = 3000 / m = 4500 CIFAR; r = 100 / m = 400 Shakespeare; ζ = 0.05) are stated. However, the ε_LB values are reported only as point estimates with no confidence intervals around ε_LB itself, despite the bound being a random variable across the L runs.
- **Significance**: High if it holds up. The headline finding — that a single hypothesis test cleanly separates certified from heuristic unlearning and catches Hessian-based unlearning failing its convexity precondition (ε_LB = 142.5 vs. certified methods ≈ 0) — is exactly the kind of falsification tool the unlearning field has been missing.
- **Red flags / overclaiming**: A few. (1) The main results are restricted to δ = 0; the δ > 0 extension (Appendix C) is only sketched, yet many practically deployed (ε, δ) certificates use non-zero δ — so the audit currently cannot speak to a large slice of real guarantees. (2) The pairwise baseline is set with only L = 10 runs, which the authors themselves note forces a zero bound via Clopper–Pearson — this makes the proposed auditor look good by comparison but is a weak baseline setting. (3) The "uniform vs. adversarial split" effect (uniform bounds typically larger) is explained post hoc via memorization/semantic-adjacency arguments but never ablated against, e.g., forget-set size or label overlap fraction, leaving the mechanism under-tested. (4) The audit returns a single scalar ε_LB that cannot localize which samples or layers leak, limiting its diagnostic granularity relative to per-sample completeness scores it claims to complement.

## Strengths

- Clean, falsifiable hypothesis-testing formulation that returns a quantity (ε_LB) directly comparable to a claimed (ε, δ) certificate — a real advance over per-example completeness scores.
- The LDP reduction plus Theorem 4.2 gives a principled, provably valid (Type-I-controlled) lower bound rather than an ad-hoc MIA accuracy.
- Two complementary instantiations (scalable batchwise vs. correlation-aware joint) cover both ends of the m regime.
- The Hessian-based-unlearning case study (ε_LB = 142.5 in the nonconvex regime where its certificate silently fails) is a compelling, concrete demonstration of diagnostic value.
- Honest about scope: the paper explicitly restricts to δ = 0, states that the δ > 0 reduction does not extend cleanly, and lists tightening via stronger MIAs as future work.
- Public code release and full proofs in the appendix.

## Limitations

- **δ = 0 only.** The empirically validated audit covers pure (ε, 0) unlearning; the δ > 0 version (Appendix C, Lemma C.1) is stated but not run, so the many practically relevant (ε, δ) certificates with δ > 0 cannot yet be audited.
- **Computational cost is undisclosed.** Each audit requires Γ calibration + L evaluation training-and-unlearning cycles (up to Γ = 50, L = 500 for the uncertified model-clipping variant), but no wall-clock or GPU-hour figures are given; the procedure is clearly expensive and may be infeasible for large production models.
- **Small-scale benchmarks only.** CIFAR-100 with a TinyNet (~55% val acc) and Shakespeare with a 2-layer LSTM (~0.53 acc); no ImageNet-scale or modern-LLM evaluation, so generalization to large models is untested.
- **No confidence intervals on ε_LB.** Bounds are reported as point estimates; variance across the L runs is used only internally via mean/median aggregation.
- **Weak baseline configuration.** The pairwise auditor is run with L = 10, which the authors concede forces a zero bound under Clopper–Pearson — an unfairly weak comparison.
- **Uniform-vs-adversarial split effect under-ablated.** The large gap (e.g., Hessian 142.5 uniform vs. 10 adversarial) is explained qualitatively but not isolated against confounds like forget-set size or label overlap.
- **Single-scalar output.** ε_LB cannot localize leakage to specific samples, batches, or layers, limiting actionable diagnostics for algorithm developers.
- **R2D restricted to full-batch training** (the authors note R2D is only formally analysed in the full-batch setting), narrowing the certified-method coverage.

## Relevance

对于关注机器遗忘（machine unlearning）隐私保证与差分隐私审计的研究者，本文提供了一种可直接复用的、面向 certified (ε, δ) 遗忘算法的证伪工具，并能定量区分"有证书"与"启发式"遗忘方法的真实安全性；其基于 MIA/LiRA 的审计范式对评估 SCRUB、Hessian scrubbing 等方法在非凸深度网络中的实际遗忘强度具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does ε_LB behave for δ > 0 certificates, and can the Appendix C reduction be tightened enough to yield useful bounds on real (ε, δ) deployments?
- What is the actual compute cost (GPU-hours, number of training/unlearning invocations) of the audit at the reported configurations, and can calibration costs be amortized across algorithms or reduced via simulation-based inference?
- Does the sharp certified-vs-heuristic separation hold at ImageNet/LLM scale, or does the per-batch leakage signal become undetectable as model capacity grows?
- Can the audit be extended to localize leakage (per-layer, per-sample) rather than returning a single scalar ε_LB?
- Is the uniform-vs-adversarial split gap (e.g., Hessian 142.5 vs. 10) driven primarily by label overlap, feature support sharing, or memorization — and how does it scale with forget-set size?
- Would stronger MIA scoring (e.g., RMIA-style) tighten the lower bounds, particularly for certified methods where ε_LB ≈ 0 currently leaves a wide undetermined range?
- Can the pairwise baseline be made competitive with larger L and a tighter conversion than Clopper–Pearson, to give a fairer head-to-head comparison?
