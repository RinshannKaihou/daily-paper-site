Improving LLM-Generated Process Model Quality Through Reinforcement Learning: The Role of Reward Function Design

Alexander Rombach · Chantale Lauer · Nijat Mehdiyev  —  DFKI & Saarland University

Reinforcement Learning GSPO BPMN Reward Shaping LLM Fine-tuning

Key finding

Equal-weight rewards beat targeted weighting in RL-based BPMN generation — reward design matters as much as using RL itself.

01 Methodology

Text-to-model generation: an LLM policy π_θ maps a process description to a JSON intermediate model, deterministically translated into BPMN XML. Outputs failing parse, schema, or with <2 flow objects are invalid.

Two-stage training, both LoRA in bfloat16. Stage 1 SFT (LLaMA-Factory, rank 8, lr 1e-4, 3 epochs, 1,552 bilingual samples). Stage 2 RL via GSPO through Unsloth — chosen over PPO/GRPO because its sequence-level, group-relative ranking fits holistic model-quality rewards and sidesteps hopeless token-level credit assignment on long structured outputs.

Rewards come from BEF4LLM: syntactic r_syn (16 rules), pragmatic r_pra (15 complexity metrics), semantic r_sem (7 similarity metrics) — each bounded [0,1]. Unified reward R = w·r_syn + w·r_pra + w·r_sem, with penalty p ∈ {−1, 0} for invalid outputs. SFT initialization is itself an experimental variable.

03 Assessment

Strengths

  • Genuinely systematic design: 48 configs across two model families, full cross-product with Bonferroni correction.
  • Surprising, well-evidenced findings — equal-weight dominance, penalty as diversity regularizer, SFT-hurts-Qwen-semantics — backed by violin/radar analyses.
  • Intellectual honesty: reports the R_avg vs R1 anomaly, flags thin-data comparisons, lists concrete limitations.
  • Strong methodological fit: GSPO's sequence-level ranking is well-motivated for holistic quality rewards.
  • Public code + full hyperparameter tables; rigorous paired permutation tests (exceeds typical RL-for-LLM rigor).

Limitations

  • Small eval set (105 samples, single generation) — no within-config variance estimate, low sensitivity.
  • Conditional-on-validity scoring entangles quality with validity rate (n=36 vs n=102 not directly comparable).
  • Architecture confounded with scale (8B vs 14B) — "architecture-dependent" claims cannot separate family from size.
  • Only 3:1:1 weighting tested; "equal wins" is stronger than the evidence supports (2:1:1 untried).
  • Reference-based semantic reward may undervalue structurally novel-but-valid alternatives.
  • Single-epoch GSPO with LoRA doesn't test reward overfitting or catastrophic forgetting; training-set manifest not fully specified.

04 Open questions

Would gentler imbalances (2:1:1, or adaptive/curriculum weighting) avoid the 3:1:1 policy collapse, or is equal weighting genuinely optimal?
Is the SFT-hurts-Qwen-semantics effect stable across seeds and larger Qwen models, or an artifact of the single-generation, single-epoch protocol?
Can embedding-based or LLM-as-judge semantic rewards finally move the semantic-quality needle that metric-based rewards cannot?
Does the penalty-as-diversity-regularizer finding generalize to UML, DB schemas, code — and can an explicit diversity term replace it?
How much of the "architecture-dependent" behavior is actually scale, if Llama and Qwen are matched at equal parameter counts?
Under multi-epoch GSPO with KL regularization, do gains compound — or does reward hacking / template collapse emerge even for equal weights?
