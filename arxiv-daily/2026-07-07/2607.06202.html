01

Methodology — killing the synchronization tax

UBEP targets the dispatch stage of MoE All-to-All on a two-tier UB fabric (CM384, 392 GB/s). It is driven by three measurements showing that classic BSP-style EPCLs break on sub-microsecond links: bandwidth fills the pipe with only half the AIVs, so the remaining cores idle at global barriers. Rather than accept the barrier, UBEP co-designs three synergistic mechanisms.

Kernel Decomposition partitions AIVs into groups running independent tasks (Token-Sending vs. TokenCnt / CalCumSum) in parallel, replacing global barriers with point-to-point polling so prefix-sum overlaps dispatch. Hierarchical Token-level Scheduling formally models token-to-core assignment as an NP-hard min-makespan problem and solves it heuristically via latency homogenization plus a SIMD Quaternary Search that converges in 4–5 steps under 1 µs. Data-as-Flag piggybacks synchronization onto the data payload using the UB fabric's 512B atomic writes — three variants (Token-Flag Fusion, Data-for-Checksum, Sentinel Polling) trade bandwidth for granularity, with a formal happens-before argument and a 2−256 sentinel-collision bound.

Receive address space is pre-partitioned so senders write tokens directly into destination HBM; SIMD primitives (VectorCount, CompareScalar, FindFirstValue) replace scalar token loops. End-to-end runs use vLLM-Ascend v0.14.0.

03

Strengths & Limitations

● Strengths

  • Production-scale validation on a real 256-die CM384 allocation across four SOTA MoE models (30B–685B), not simulation.
  • Synergistic triad: decomposition exposes fine-grained work; the scheduler balances it; Data-as-Flag removes the barrier that would re-serialize it.
  • Formal treatment — NP-hardness proof (App. B), cost model (App. A), happens-before safety, and a portability checklist (Table 5).
  • Honest reporting of the diverted-cores trade-off and why end-to-end gains trail operator gains.

▲ Limitations

  • Baseline breadth: only CANN EP runs on CM384 — DeepEP / Hybrid-EP / UCCL-EP / FUSCO are characterized but never benchmarked.
  • Hardware lock-in: needs 512B atomic writes + Ascend AIV concurrency; numbers are CM384-specific and not reproducible on NVIDIA/AMD.
  • Combine stage under-documented — reader must trust symmetric gains ("trivial yet intricate details" deferred).
  • Sentinel collision bound assumes uniform distributions; BF16/FP16 activations are highly non-uniform and not stress-tested.
  • End-to-end ceiling: ~11% TPOT despite ~52% operator gain — large residual framework stalls remain.
04

Open Questions

How does UBEP fare when DeepEP / Hybrid-EP / UCCL-EP are actually ported to CM384, isolating algorithmic wins from hardware advantage? Is CANN EP a tuned baseline or a strawman?
Can the Sentinel Polling deadlock risk be characterized empirically under adversarial / quantization-induced activation distributions (W4A8, heavy zero-padding) instead of the uniform bound?
Does the combine stage see symmetric gains, and how does UBEP interact with Attention-FFN Disaggregation's bipartite M2N traffic in practice?
How much of the residual end-to-end stall (operator 52% vs TPOT 11%) can be recovered by extending dependency-driven machinery to FFN, attention, and scheduling?
How do the three mechanisms transfer to non-Ascend superpods — NVL576 with UALink, or SUE fabrics — where 512B atomicity and AIV concurrency differ?
