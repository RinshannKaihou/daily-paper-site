Heckman-Corrected Epistemic Uncertainty: Selection on Unobservables Defeats Importance Weighting

Gunner Levi Howe
Uncertainty Quantification Selection Bias Heckman Selection Epistemic Uncertainty Distribution Shift
Key Finding

When selection depends on unobservables correlated with outcome noise, Heckman correction restores epistemic uncertainty where oracle importance weighting structurally fails.

Key Metric
88.9% vs 43.1% oracle importance weighting
Nominal-90% interval coverage in selected-against regions at ρ=0.9 (controlled generator). Pre-registered kill condition (IW matching Heckman) did not fire — paired Wilcoxon p=0.008, n=8.
Why This Matters
Gives deployed ML on selection-biased data — loan approval, medical admission, simulation campaigns — a principled econometric correction for the MNAR regime that standard importance weighting structurally cannot address.
Critical Edge
The headline 88.9% vs 43.1% sits on a synthetic generator the authors designed where Heckman is guaranteed to work; on real non-Gaussian data (wine) the bivariate-normal assumption already breaks, and even the skyline undercovers.
01 Methodology

The model instantiates Heckman's two-equation selection framework with deep feature maps. The outcome function f_θ is a deep MLP; the selection index g_ψ(w)=w⊤γ+b is deliberately linear in w=[x,z], where z is an instrument affecting selection but not the outcome. Two global parameters (log σ, atanh ρ) complete the model. Because E[y|x,w,s=1] = f(x) + ρσ·λ(w⊤γ) with λ the inverse Mills ratio, the population f(x) is recoverable once ρ and γ are estimated.

Two training routes are compared. Joint MLE maximizes the bivariate-normal likelihood over all units (unselected contribute log Φ(−g_ψ)); the key contingency is a ρ≡0 warm-up (first 100 of 1500 epochs), else the flexible net absorbs the Mills term and ρ̂ collapses to 0.16 (true 0.8). The two-step estimator first fits probit for γ̂, then regresses y ≈ f_θ(x) + c·λ by Gaussian NLL — its hard probit/outcome separation needs no warm-up and is recommended as default. The corrected predictive distribution uses K=5 deep ensembles with population f_θ in place of the selected-sample one.

A pre-registered faithfulness gate (A-E0) — reproducing Cameron–Trivedi's RAND-HIE Stata output, statsmodels probit, and Mroz/Greene two-step, plus synthetic-truth recovery — must pass before any experiment runs.

03 Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Pre-registered faithfulness gate — reproduce seven-digit Stata output before any experiment — plus a pre-registered kill condition (IW matching Heckman falsifies the premise). Rare evidential standard in ML-UQ.
  • Identification-honesty curve swept (instrument present vs absent, ρ=0→0.9) rather than cherry-picking the best case; explicitly warns coverage can actively mislead without an instrument.
  • Reusable methods finding: joint MLE needs ρ≡0 warm-up or ρ̂ collapses; two-step is inherently robust — concrete guidance for Heckman + flexible approximators.
  • region-ECE as lead metric precisely because raw coverage is gameable by over-widening — and demonstrates the gaming (GP covers 98.0% in well-sampled California regions).
  • Honest scoping in §8: no causal-discovery claims, no real-MNAR ground-truth claims, no superiority under ρ=0 where IW is consistent and simpler.

Limitations

  • Homoscedastic-Gaussian errors assumed throughout; real heteroscedastic non-Gaussian data (wine) already shows the cost — even the skyline undercovers. t-copula / heteroscedastic extensions named but not implemented.
  • Linear selection head and Gaussian-compatible generators mean demonstrations occur in the regime most favorable to the method; nonlinear selection with a valid instrument is compatible with the likelihood but untested.
  • Ensembles fixed at K=5 approximate epistemic variance coarsely, with no sensitivity analysis over K.
  • Semi-real MNAR is still author-induced — ground truth exists for all rows but the mechanism is synthetic, not naturally occurring; genuinely real MNAR ground truth is unavailable.
  • Benchmark-panel vignette aggregates heterogeneous metrics; 8/13 boundary hits and sign-unstable estimates (ImageNet +0.87 vs MT −0.90) are too noisy to support substantive claims.
04 Open Questions
  1. How does the correction behave when the bivariate-normal assumption is violated — heavy-tailed (t-copula) or heteroscedastic noise — and can O'Neill's Dirichlet-process-mixture error model drop in while preserving the epistemic-UQ framing?
  2. What happens with a nonlinear selection head (small MLP for g_ψ) given a valid instrument — does identification hold, and does joint MLE still need the warm-up contingency?
  3. Can natural ML instruments (sensor/site/annotator identity, exploration epsilon, successive-halving rung randomness) be validated as exclusion restrictions in practice, and how sensitive are coverage gains to instrument weakness?
  4. How do gains scale with ensemble size K and dataset size — does the two-step's robustness edge over joint MLE persist, or does MLE overtake it with enough data and a tuned warm-up?
  5. On the benchmark-panel vignette, can candidate reporting instruments (venue page limits, dataset release dates) recover interior ρ̂ and move fits off the |ρ̂|=1 boundary, turning the caution into a usable leaderboard correction?
