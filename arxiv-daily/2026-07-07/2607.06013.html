Methodology

heuristic-free · exact mirror ascent

The setting is full-batch linear classification on separable data with signed examples z_i = y_i x_i and separability γ_∞ > 0. The update is memoryless stability-annealed smoothed-sign descent under weighted exponential loss L(w) = Σ a_i e^{−z_i^T w}:

wt+1 = wt + ηt · (−∇L(wt)) / (|∇L(wt)| + εt),   εt = ε0 exp(−κ St),   St = Σs<t ηs

The candidate separator at rate κ is u_κ = argmin B(u) subject to z_i^T u ≥ κ, where B is the Burg-type barrier above. The proof rewrites the dynamics exactly (no approximation) as entropic mirror ascent on the concave dual D(λ) = κ1^T λ − Φ*(Z^T λ):

λt+1 = λt ⊙ exp(ηt ∇D(λt)),   with Vt+1 − Vt ≤ −ηt Δt + (e/2) ηt² G² ‖λt‖1

A one-step KL identity + discrete Gronwall yield supt Vt < ∞ and a KKT/Fenchel equality controlling ‖dt − uκ‖². Jensen gives the normalized-iterate envelope, and a spike-exclusion lemma upgrades averaged control to last-iterate convergence Δt → 0.

‖wt/St − uκ‖ ≤ ‖w0‖2/(2 St) + √(CΦ CD) / St

What the proof avoids

  • active-set stability
  • bounded normalized iterates
  • strict complementarity
  • dual multiplier uniqueness
  • generic-position data

Constants are explicit: CΦ = 2ε0/(ε0+Q)². No asymptotic hand-waving — the dual identity is verified to floating-point error.

Strengths & Limitations

honest scope delineation

+ Strengths

  • Mathematically exact. The dual mirror-ascent identity is verified to floating-point error (max rq = 3.5e−13) — rare and convincing.
  • Conceptually clean. κ-as-margin-constraint gives a principled interpolation between ℓ2 and ℓ∞ margin geometries, with both endpoint limits proved.
  • Proof discipline. No reliance on generic-position, strict-complementarity, or active-set-stability assumptions that quietly underpin most implicit-bias proofs.
  • Intellectual honesty. Proved claims (weighted exp loss, memoryless proxy, 0<κ<γ∞) are repeatedly separated from empirical diagnostics (logistic tails, fixed-ε crossover, Adam transfer).
  • Comprehensive ablations. Schedule powers, ε0, data families, dimensions, support-vector counts, rescaling, and boundary pathologies all swept — failures classified, not discarded.

! Limitations

  • Narrow loss scope. Only weighted exponential loss; logistic loss is an empirical check with no perturbation theorem.
  • Memoryless proxy only. Adam/RMSProp transfer empirically rejected under the tested grid (no impossibility theorem for all adaptive variants).
  • Synthetic only. Separable linear instances (n=4d, d≤100); no real data, no neural networks, no large-scale benchmarks.
  • Open crossover proof. Sτ = (1/γ∞) log(1/ε) + O(1) is only conditionally proved — the two-sided tail bound (83) and leading-coordinate-change control remain open.
  • Excluded endpoints. Critical case κ = γ∞ and regimes κ = 0, κ > γ∞ are outside the theorem; the |uj| ≤ 1−δ proxy (δ=10⁻⁷) is a numerical workaround.
  • Envelope not sharp. Authors explicitly state they do not claim the empirical rate is St−1/2 or that constants CΦ, CD are sharp. No explicit code release mentioned.

Questions for Further Exploration

open problems
  1. Can the theorem extend to logistic loss via a perturbation argument, and would the same Burg-barrier selector survive under a logistic tail with a modified rate index?
  2. Is the critical case κ = γ∞ genuinely singular, or is there a well-defined limit separator (and a distinct barrier geometry) closing the open interval?
  3. Can the open two-sided tail bound (83) for fixed-ε crossover be proved, and does the stronger O(log log(1/ε)) secondary target hold?
  4. Under what narrower conditions on β1, β2, or the schedule could Adam/RMSProp satisfy the averaged transfer residual (38) and inherit the smoothed-sign limit?
  5. Does the rate-indexed Burg-barrier selector extend beyond linear classification to homogeneous or wide neural networks, where margin-geometry results depend on parameterization?
  6. Are the proof constants CΦ, CD sharp, and can the St−1/2 envelope be tightened to match the empirically observed convergence rate?
