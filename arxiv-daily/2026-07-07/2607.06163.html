01

Methodology

X-FEMR targets CLMBR-T-Base, a 141M-parameter autoregressive FEMR pretrained on 2.57M Stanford EHRSHOT records under OMOP-CDM. Each clinical event carries four channels — code, numeric value, text, and timestamp.

The explainability pipeline fixes a prediction time T, records the FEMR's hard (0.5-threshold) and soft (probability) outputs, then trains a lightweight 3-layer Transformer surrogate (hidden 128, 4 heads, FFN 256, max length 2048, Adam 1e-4, batch 16) to imitate those input–output pairs.

Each event token is encoded as e = E_code + E_text + g(·)⊙(W·n) + W·∆t + E_pos, where ∆t is log-transformed time-to-prediction in hours and the numeric channel is content-gated. SHAP then attributes per-token importance, and the novel Clinical Validated Events Ratio scores how often the top tokens fall inside validated feature sets (NEWS2 vitals, ICD-10 frailty/comorbidity).

03

Critical Assessment

+Strengths

  • First end-to-end token-level XAI purpose-built for longitudinal structured EHR foundation models — a genuine literature gap.
  • Time-aware event encoding (log-∆t, gated numeric injection) is lightweight and well-justified for irregular EHR sequences.
  • Clinical Validated Events Ratio turns SHAP into a clinician-legible, auditable scalar — practical for clinical AI governance.
  • Honest reporting of the degenerate soft-label ICU result and the FEMR's weak recall, rather than hiding behind AUROC.
  • Reproducible: only public FEMR + public EHRSHOT, with explicit hyperparameters and externally grounded feature lists (NEWS2, Hospital Frailty Risk Score).

–Limitations

  • Surrogate–FEMR fidelity never measured. The surrogate exceeds the FEMR, weakening the "we explain the FEMR" claim; no agreement/fidelity curves.
  • Single FEMR, single institution, two binary tasks — generalization to ETHOS/Med-BERT, other sites, or continuous outcomes is unverified.
  • Severe-imbalance failure: soft-label ICU surrogate collapses to all-negative predictions.
  • Circularity in R: validated features are the same ones used to define the tasks; no negative/null Fc control.
  • No XAI baselines (IG, LIME, attention rollout, direct SHAP, linear surrogates) — the superiority claim is asserted, not demonstrated.
  • Coarse tokenization conflates channels; numeric/time dominate, possibly an artifact. Cohort-size reporting is internally inconsistent across §4.1 and §5.1.
04

Questions for Further Exploration

What is the direct surrogate→FEMR fidelity (prediction-agreement rate, fidelity-AUC, KL divergence), and does the hard-label surrogate that exceeds CLMBR-T-Base actually explain the FEMR — or a better-but-different classifier?

Fidelity · Core validity

How does X-FEMR compare to attributions computed directly on the FEMR (gradient SHAP, Integrated Gradients, attention rollout) and to simpler transparent surrogates on the same R metric?

XAI baselines

Does R still discriminate when evaluated against an Fc of clinically irrelevant codes or random token sets — i.e., is it a meaningful null-beating signal?

Metric circularity

Can the framework handle continuous / multiclass outcomes and trajectories longer than 2048 events (EHRSHOT's max is 199,913) without truncation — e.g., via hierarchical encoders?

Scale & scope

Do explanations and R transfer across FEMRs (ETHOS, Med-BERT) and institutions — are NEWS2 vitals universally dominant, or do practice patterns shift attribution?

Generalization

Why does the soft-label ICU surrogate collapse to majority-class predictions while hard-label succeeds — would focal/Tversky losses or calibrated/temperature-scaled logits recover soft-label fidelity?
