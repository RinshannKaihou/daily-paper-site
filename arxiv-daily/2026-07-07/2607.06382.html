A Function-Space Dichotomy for Compositional Learning: Exponential Sub-Optimality of the Neural Tangent Kernel

Arkaprabha Ganguli & Emil Constantinescu / Argonne National Laboratory / 2026-07-07

neural-tangent-kernel deep-learning-theory compositional-learning minimax-rates sample-complexity
Key Metric
4–6 orders

test L² gap on sparse parity (d=30, k=4): ReLU ERM ≈ 3×10⁻⁷ vs. NTK-KRR ≈ 1.0. Analytically: NTK needs Ω(4L) samples vs. a polynomial minimax floor.

Why This Matters

A target-specific criterion for when kernel surrogates suffice vs. when deep networks are necessary for compositional structure — turning “kernels dislike oscillation” into a checkable condition.

Critical Edge

The cleanest analytical witness (sawtooth) isn’t SGD-learnable — so theory and the end-to-end experiment never meet on a single target. Both witnesses are stylized/toy.

01

Methodology — five lenses on the unit circle

Set on the unit circle S¹ (where the ReLU NTK has a clean Fourier form), the argument combines five pieces. The gap reduces to a one-line accounting: divide a kernel-regression lower bound by the minimax upper bound, so it stays valid even with the unresolved factor-of-L slack.

01 · LAZY LENS
NTK spectrum

Depth-D NTK eigenvalues satisfy μk ≳ D²/k² (k⁻² from the arc-cosine kink). The NTK-RKHS equals first-order Sobolev H¹ — frequency k⋆ costs ≳(k⋆)²/D².

02 · RICH LENS
Architectural cost

The deep variation space RBV²deep charges only layerwise variation. The Telgarsky sawtooth gL has linear cost (∥gL∥≤6L, width 2) but frequency k⋆=2L.

03 · FLOOR
Minimax rate

Thm 4: VC→Rademacher→Talagrand gives Õ(L²w²R²/n); Varshamov–Gilbert packing + Fano gives the matching lower bound, tight up to one factor of L.

04 · THE GAP
Thm 6 ratio

KRR lower bound ÷ minimax upper bound ⇒ nKRR/nmin ≳ δ(k⋆)²/(D²L²w²R²) — exponential whenever k⋆ is exponential in L.

05 · COMPANION
Hypercube parity

Prop 10: sparse parity χ[k] has NTK-RKHS norm Θ(dk) yet is realized by a depth-⌊log²k⌋ net — and is SGD-learnable, enabling the end-to-end test.

✓ Control — No gap (E2)

On bandlimited targets cos(kπz), NTK-KRR and width-2048 ERM both converge polynomially with no gap growing in k — confirming the dichotomy: where Fourier and architectural complexity agree, kernels are near-minimax-optimal.

⚠ Sawtooth optimization failure (E3a)

Every config (MLP/ResMLP to depth 2L, width 256, exact Telgarsky init, LayerNorm, GELU, Adam warmup-cosine, full-batch L-BFGS) plateaued at Var=1/12 for L≥5 — an algorithmic, not statistical, failure that forced substituting the SGD-learnable parity.

03

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean dichotomy. Fourier-vs-architectural complexity makes “kernels dislike oscillation” precise and target-specific.
  • First architectural minimax rate. Indexed by (L,w,R) for the deep variation space — a genuine literature gap, tight to one factor of L.
  • Honest stat/algorithm split. Separates the statistical gap (vs. minimax floor) from SGD-realizability (E3a fail vs. E3 success).
  • Controlled experiments. Closed-form NTK, validation-tuned λ, wide ERM with L-BFGS finetune, plus a no-gap control (E2).
  • Complete, credited proofs. Supplement proves all five results; borrowed vs. new contributions explicitly flagged; fixed seeds + code.

Limitations

  • Univariate only. All function-space results are on S¹; the Sd−1 extension is future work.
  • Theory & experiments diverge. Sawtooth isn’t SGD-learnable, so the cleanest witness and the end-to-end test use different targets/domains.
  • Stylized witnesses. Both sawtooth and sparse parity are toy; no realistic hierarchical benchmark (e.g. Cagnetta et al. 2024).
  • Factor-L slack. Closing the minimax rate needs a depth-linear RBV²deep Rademacher bound unavailable now.
  • Statistical, not algorithmic. No characterization of which compositional targets are SGD-learnable.
  • Single kernel depth. NTK fixed at D=4 in experiments; sensitivity to D and other kernels untested.

Open Questions — for further exploration

Q1

Does the dichotomy extend to multivariate Sd−1 with a concrete witness, and do depth- and dimension-axis gaps compound multiplicatively?

Q2

Is there one target where both the analytical (sawtooth-scale) gap and an end-to-end SGD-realized gap coexist?

Q3

Can the factor-L minimax slack be closed — does a depth-linear Rademacher bound for RBV²deep exist under sum-of-layer-norms?

Q4

How does the gap manifest on realistic hierarchical data (random hierarchy model, natural-image composition) beyond toy targets?

Q5

What separates SGD-learnable compositional targets (parity) from SGD-hard ones (sawtooth) — when does weight-decay SGD realize the variational floor?

Q6

Does the predicted NTK / Laplace / Gaussian kernel equivalence hold empirically, and does kernel depth D materially change the gap?
