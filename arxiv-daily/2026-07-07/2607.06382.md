> **一句话总结：** 本文在单位圆上通过两种目标复杂度（Fourier 复杂度 vs. 架构复杂度）的对立，证明神经正切核（NTK）核回归在组合结构目标上的样本复杂度比极小化极大下界高出指数倍（深度-L 锯齿波上需 Ω(4^L) 样本而下界仅为 L 的多项式），实验上在超立方体稀疏奇偶任务上用标准两层 ReLU 网络击败 NTK 达四到六个数量级。

> **TL;DR:** On the unit circle, the authors characterize the minimax rate of depth-L width-w ReLU networks (between Ω(Lw²R²/n) and Õ(L²w²R²/n)) and prove the NTK kernel-regression estimator is exponentially sub-optimal against this floor whenever a target's Fourier complexity decouples from its architectural complexity — for the depth-L sawtooth needing Ω(4^L) samples vs. a polynomial floor; on the hypercube sparse-parity benchmark (d=30, k=4) a standard two-layer ReLU net beats NTK-KRR by four-to-six orders of magnitude in test L2 error (≈3×10⁻⁷ vs. ≈1.0), while on bandlimited smooth targets there is no gap.

## 核心贡献

- **架构类的极小化极速率刻画**：首次为以架构参数 (L, w, R) 索引的深度 ReLU 网络类 C_{L,w,R}（变分范数 ≤ R）给出极小化极 L² 速率，紧到仅差一个 L 因子：下界 Ω(Lw²R²/n)、上界 Õ(L²w²R²/n)。这是首个与深度变分空间 RBV²_deep 配对的样本复杂度估计。
- **NTK 的指数次优性定理**：当目标的 Fourier 复杂度（主频 k⋆）与架构复杂度 L²w²R² 解耦时，NTK-KRR 样本数与极小化极下界之比 ≳ δ(k⋆)²/(D²L²w²R²)，对深度-L 锯齿波即为 Ω(4^L) NTK 样本对多项式下界。
- **二元对立法（dichotomy）机制**：将 gap 归结为同一目标的两种复杂度量度之争——NTK 由 Sobolev/H¹ 范数（惩罚高频）主导，富特征学习由深度变分范数（奖励组合稀疏）主导；二者解耦即出现指数 gap，二者一致（如带限目标）则无 gap。
- **三维实验验证**：(E1) NTK 谱在 D∈{2,3,5,10} 上对数斜率 ≈ −1.988 至 −1.980，1% 内吻合预测 k⁻²；(E2) 在带限光滑目标 cos(kπz) 上 NTK 与宽 ERM 均多项式收敛、无随 k 增长的 gap；(E3) 在超立方体稀疏奇偶上 NTK 停在平凡基线（L²≈1）而 ERM 相变到 ≈3×10⁻⁷，gap 达四到六个数量级。
- **诚实区分统计与算法**：明确指出锯齿波虽属于架构类却无法被 SGD 恢复（L≳5 时平台于 Var=1/12），故用 SGD 可学的稀疏奇偶作为端到端实证分离的见证，统计 gap 与优化可分性清晰区分。

## Key Contributions

- **Minimax characterization of the architecture class.** The authors pin the minimax L² regression rate over the depth-L, width-w, variation-norm-≤R class C_{L,w,R} to within a single factor of L: between Ω(Lw²R²/n) and Õ(L²w²R²/n) (Theorem 4). This is presented as the first minimax rate indexed directly by architectural parameters (L,w,R) rather than a target smoothness exponent, and the first lower bound paired with the deep variation space RBV²_deep of Parhi & Nowak (2022).
- **Exponential NTK sub-optimality theorem.** Theorem 6 gives a closed-form sample-complexity ratio n_KRR/n_minimax ≳ δ(k⋆)²/(D²L²w²R² log·), exponential exactly when the target's Fourier frequency k⋆ is exponential in its architectural size. For the depth-L Telgarsky sawtooth (Corollary 8) this becomes Ω(4^L) NTK samples vs. an Õ(L⁴) minimax floor.
- **A function-space dichotomy.** The gap is reframed as the tension between two complexity measures of the same target: Fourier complexity (governing the NTK via the Sobolev/H¹ equivalence, Proposition 3) vs. architectural complexity (governing the minimax upper bound). The two are independent, and the gap is a property of their mismatch, not of "kernels vs. networks" in general (Corollary 9 shows no gap for bandlimited targets).
- **Depth-axis separation at fixed input dimension.** Unlike prior kernel-vs-network gaps that are exponential in input dimension d (Bach 2017; Ghorbani et al. 2020; Daniely & Malach 2020), this separation is exponential in depth L at fixed dimension, which is what allows comparison against the minimax floor rather than a dimension-dependent competitor.
- **End-to-end empirical realization on a learnable target.** Because the sawtooth is an SGD-hard optimization target (E3a: every configuration plateaus at Var=1/12 for L≳5), the authors use the SGD-learnable hypercube sparse parity (Proposition 10) to realize the predicted separation end-to-end, cleanly separating the statistical gap from optimization difficulty.

## Methodology

The analysis is set on the unit circle S¹ (identified with [0,2π)), chosen because the ReLU NTK admits a clean Fourier description there. Three building blocks are combined:

**1. The lazy lens — NTK spectrum.** Using the bias-augmented depth-D NTK assembled from arc-cosine kernels (Cho & Saul 2009) and the Bietti–Bach "deep equals shallow" theorem, the eigenvalue bracket is established (Proposition 3): c·D²/k² ≤ μ_k(D) ≤ C·D²/k² for all D≥2, k≠0. The k⁻² exponent originates from the √(1−u) kink of the arc-cosine profile at the diagonal (a |θ| kink ⇒ Fourier coefficients decay as k⁻²), and depth enters only as a D² prefactor. Substituting into the RKHS norm gives ∥f∥²_{H_Θ(D)} ≍ (1/D²)·Σ_k|f̂(k)|²k², i.e. the NTK-RKHS equals the first-order Sobolev space H¹(S¹) up to a depth rescaling. Thus a target with Fourier mass at frequency k⋆ costs ≈(k⋆)²/D² in NTK-RKHS norm — its "Fourier complexity."

**2. The rich lens — architectural complexity.** The deep variation space RBV²_deep(L) (Parhi & Nowak 2022) consists of L-fold compositions with norm = inf over decompositions of layerwise variation norms. The architecture class C_{L,w,R} restricts to width-≤w single-hidden-layer blocks with total norm ≤R and ∥f∥∞≤R; each member is piecewise-linear with W=O(Lw²) parameters. The Telgarsky sawtooth g_L = τ∘…∘τ (τ a tent map, ∥τ∥_RBV²=6) is the running witness: width-2, depth-L, ∥g_L∥_RBV²_deep ≤ 6L (linear architectural cost) but with spectrum concentrated at k⋆=2^(L−1) (exponential Fourier complexity, Lemma 2).

**3. The minimax floor (Theorem 4).** The upper bound uses VC-dimension O(L²w² log(Lw)) → Rademacher complexity Õ(R√(L²w²/n)) → Talagrand contraction → excess risk Õ(σ²L²w²/n), with σ²≍R². The lower bound uses a Varshamov–Gilbert packing of 2^(Ω(Lw²)) parameter perturbations of a reference network, with local bi-Lipschitzness giving pairwise L² separation ≍ε, then Fano's inequality forces R⋆_n ≥ Ω(σ²Lw²/n) = Ω(Lw²R²/n). The residual factor of L is explicitly attributed (Remark 5) to the mismatch between VC's depth factor and the W free parameters, and is left open.

**4. The gap (Theorem 6).** A one-line accounting: (i) the Sobolev lens charges the NTK (k⋆)² for the dominant frequency; (ii) a two-point Fano/Caponnetto–De Vito KRR lower bound converts a large RKHS norm into n_KRR ≥ Ω(δ(k⋆)²∥f⋆∥²/(D²ε²)) samples; (iii) dividing by the minimax upper bound Õ(L²w²R²/ε²) yields the ratio. Because the numerator divides by the minimax *upper* bound, the gap is a valid lower bound insensitive to the factor-L ambiguity. For the sawtooth (k⋆=2^(L−1), δ=12/π⁴, L²w²R²=144L⁴) this gives n_KRR/n_minimax = Ω(4^L/(D²L⁴ log L)).

**5. Hypercube companion (Proposition 10).** On {−1,+1}^d, Walsh characters χ_S are NTK eigenfunctions with eigenvalue Θ(d^(−|S|)); the sparse parity χ_[k] has RKHS norm Θ(d^k), giving n_KRR ≥ Ω(d^k/ε²) while being realized by a depth-⌈log₂ k⌉, O(k)-neuron network — and crucially SGD learns it (Glasgow 2023), enabling the end-to-end empirical test.

**Experiments.** Three controlled studies on a single H200 GPU with fixed seeds and CSV-regenerated figures: (E1) diagonalize the 5000×5000 NTK Gram matrix for D∈{2,3,5,10} and fit log–log slopes over the clean window k∈[125,625]; (E2) bandlimited targets cos(kπz), k∈{1,…,5}, comparing depth-4 NTK-KRR (validation-tuned λ) against width-2048 two-layer ReLU ERM (Adam + L-BFGS finetune) over n∈{50,…,5000}; (E3) sparse parity on {−1,+1}^d (d∈{30,50}, k∈{3,4}) comparing depth-4 NTK-KRR against width-512 two-layer ReLU ERM, with an additional (E3a) sawtooth optimization-failure probe.

## Results & Highlights

- **Minimax rate (Theorem 4):** For n ≥ L²w² log(Lw) and σ²≍R², the minimax L² risk over C_{L,w,R} satisfies c₅·Lw²R²/n ≤ R⋆_n ≤ c₆·L²w²R² log(Lwn)/n — tight up to a single factor of L, the first architectural-parameter-indexed rate for deep ReLU networks.
- **NTK spectrum verification (E1):** Fitted log–log slopes of μ_k(D) on S¹ for D∈{2,3,5,10} are −1.980, −1.989, −1.989, −1.988, all within 1% of the predicted −2, confirming the k⁻² decay (and hence the kernel side of the gap) that drives the Ω(4^L) NTK cost.
- **Sawtooth gap (Corollary 8 / Table 1):** n_KRR(g_L) ≥ c·4^L/(D²ε²) vs. n_minimax(g_L) ≤ Õ(L⁴/ε²); at L=12 the NTK needs ≈1.7×10⁷ times more samples than the floor (ratio 4^L/L⁴ ≈ 810), comparable for L≤4 and explosively separated for L≥5.
- **No gap on bandlimited targets (E2, Corollary 9):** On cos(kπz), k∈{1,…,5}, NTK-KRR and width-2048 ERM both converge polynomially with no gap growing in k; at k=1, n=50 ERM even beats NTK (2.5×10⁻⁶ vs. 3.4×10⁻⁵), and at n=5000 NTK leads only ~10⁻¹¹ vs. ~10⁻⁷ — a constant-to-polynomial factor, consistent with near-minimax optimality on H¹-smooth targets.
- **End-to-end parity gap (E3, Figure 4, Table 2):** On sparse parity χ_[4] over {−1,+1}³⁰, NTK-KRR stays at the trivial baseline (test L² ≈ 1.0 = Var) for all tested n up to 10⁴, while two-layer ReLU ERM phase-transitions to ≈3×10⁻⁷ at n=10⁴ — a head-to-head gap of four-to-six orders of magnitude. The phase transition shifts with d and k exactly as the Ω(d^k) barrier predicts (e.g. d=50,k=4: NTK 1.09 vs. ERM 6.8×10⁻² at n=5000).
- **Sawtooth optimization failure (E3a):** Every attempted configuration (MLP/ResMLP up to depth 2L and width 256, exact Telgarsky init, LayerNorm, GELU, Adam warmup-cosine, full-batch L-BFGS) plateaued at test loss ≈ Var(g_L)=1/12 (the constant-mean predictor) for L≥5 — an algorithmic, not statistical, failure that motivated substituting the SGD-learnable parity for the empirical witness.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** The genuinely new piece is the *combination*: pairing the deep-variation-space minimax rate (Theorem 4, claimed as the first for the architectural class C_{L,w,R}) with the NTK-Sobolev equivalence to obtain a gap measured against the *minimax floor* rather than a hand-picked competitor network. The two ingredients individually are known (NTK-RKHS = Sobolev via Bietti & Bach 2021; sawtooth as high-frequency via Telgarsky 2016), and the authors are commendably explicit about this. The framing as a "function-space dichotomy" between Fourier and architectural complexity is a clean conceptual contribution even if each side is classical.

**Rigor.** Proofs are complete in the supplement and follow standard minimax machinery (VC → Rademacher → Talagrand for the upper bound; Varshamov–Gilbert packing + Fano for the lower bound; Caponnetto–De Vito for the KRR lower bound). The honest treatment of the factor-L gap (Remark 5) — explicitly open, with the conjecture stated and the obstacle (no depth-linear RBV²_deep Rademacher bound under sum-of-layer-norms) named — is a mark of integrity. The division-by-upper-bound trick that makes the gap robust to the factor-L ambiguity is a nice technical observation.

**Reproducibility.** Strong on paper: fixed seeds, CSV-regenerated figures, anonymous code repo, single H200 GPU, full hyperparameter specification in Section 6. The NTK is computed in closed form from the arc-cosine recursion (no approximation), and test sets of 5000 fresh draws are used. However, the sawtooth optimization-failure result (E3a) is reported qualitatively ("every configuration we tried") without a table of the attempted configurations, making that specific negative result harder to reproduce.

**Significance.** The depth-axis separation at fixed dimension (exponential in L, not d) complements the dimension-axis separations of Bach (2017) and Ghorbani et al. (2020), and the practitioner-facing takeaway — "a kernel surrogate suffices iff the target's Fourier complexity does not decouple from its architectural complexity" — is a sharper, more actionable criterion than "kernels dislike oscillation." Whether this reframing changes practice depends on whether the dichotomy extends beyond the stylized witnesses to realistic hierarchical data.

**Red flags / specific concerns.**
- *Stylized witnesses.* Both the sawtooth (analytical) and the sparse parity (empirical) are textbook hard targets; the gap is demonstrated on exactly the targets designed to exhibit it. The authors acknowledge this ("The witness is stylized but the mechanism is not") but provide no experiment on a non-toy compositional benchmark (e.g. a real hierarchical dataset or the random hierarchy model of Cagnetta et al. 2024, which they cite as closest in spirit but do not run).
- *Sawtooth is not SGD-learnable.* The cleanest analytical witness (g_L) cannot be fitted by gradient descent for L≳5 — the statistical gap is realized there only against the *minimax floor*, not against an actual trained network. The end-to-end empirical gap relies on the parity, a different domain and mechanism. The two halves of the argument do not meet on a single target.
- *Univariate-only theory.* All function-space results are on S¹; the multivariate S^(d−1) extension is explicitly listed as future work requiring a separate Fourier-coefficient calculation. The hypercube parity (Proposition 10) is a multivariate companion but is presented without full proof of the realizability/SGD-learnability gap being the *same* mechanism.
- *Single NTK depth and a single baseline kernel family.* Experiments fix the NTK at depth D=4; the theoretical claim holds for every D≥2 but the empirical sensitivity to D is not probed. The claim that Gaussian/Laplace kernels "of comparable decay" pay the same Fourier price is asserted, not tested.
- *The minimax lower bound uses bit-extraction networks whose weights grow with depth and fall outside the norm ball* (Remark 5) — a known looseness that the authors flag but that means the floor's exact location in [Lw²R², L²w²R²] is unresolved.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clean dichotomy (Fourier vs. architectural complexity) that makes the "kernels dislike oscillation" intuition precise and target-specific rather than generic.
- First minimax rate indexed by architectural parameters (L,w,R) for the deep variation space, a genuine gap in the literature.
- Honest separation of statistical gap (Theorem 6, against the minimax floor) from algorithmic realizability (E3a sawtooth failure vs. E3 parity success), avoiding the common conflation.
- Strong, controlled experiments with closed-form NTK computation, validation-tuned regularization, wide ERM with L-BFGS finetune to isolate statistical from approximation error, and a clean no-gap control (E2) that rules out the "networks just beat kernels everywhere" alternative.
- Complete proofs in the supplement; explicit acknowledgment of borrowed results vs. new contributions; reproducible code and fixed seeds.

## Limitations

- Theory is restricted to the unit circle S¹; multivariate extension to S^(d−1) is structurally available but not executed.
- The analytical witness (sawtooth) is not SGD-learnable, so the theory's cleanest target and the experiments' end-to-end realization use different targets on different domains.
- Both witnesses (sawtooth, sparse parity) are stylized/toy; no demonstration on realistic hierarchical or compositional benchmarks.
- The minimax rate is tight only up to a factor of L; closing it requires a depth-linear RBV²_deep Rademacher bound unavailable in current norm-based analyses.
- Guarantees are statistical, not algorithmic: the paper does not characterize which compositional targets are SGD-learnable, leaving the algorithmic counterpart of Theorem 6 as future work.
- NTK depth is fixed at D=4 in experiments; empirical sensitivity to D is not explored, and the assertion that other smoothness-biased kernels (Gaussian, Laplace) pay the same price is untested.

## Relevance

与深度学习理论研究兴趣高度相关：本文从极小化极速率与函数空间对偶的角度，定量刻画了 NTK（lazy regime）在组合结构目标上相对富特征学习的指数级样本复杂度劣势，并给出了以架构参数 (L,w,R) 索引的极小化极速率这一可直接用于估计组合问题样本复杂度的工具，对理解"何时该用核方法、何时必须用深度网络"提供了具体判据。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the dichotomy be extended to multivariate S^(d−1) with a concrete witness (e.g. via Telgarsky's affine-projection construction), and does the depth-axis and dimension-axis gap compound multiplicatively?
- Is there a single target on which both the analytical (sawtooth-scale) gap and an end-to-end SGD-realized gap coexist, closing the present split between the theory witness and the empirical witness?
- Can the minimax factor-L gap be closed — specifically, does a depth-linear Rademacher bound for RBV²_deep(L) under the sum-of-layer-norms constraint exist, confirming the conjectured Θ(Lw²R²/n) floor?
- How does the gap manifest on realistic hierarchical datasets (e.g. the random hierarchy model of Cagnetta et al. 2024, or natural image compositional structure), beyond the stylized parity/sawtooth?
- What characterizes the boundary between SGD-learnable compositional targets (parity) and SGD-hard ones (sawtooth) — i.e., when does weight-decay SGD implicitly realize the variational objective that defines the statistical floor?
- Does the predicted equivalence of NTK, Laplace, and Gaussian kernels' Fourier-complexity penalty hold empirically, and does kernel depth D materially change the gap on non-toy targets?
