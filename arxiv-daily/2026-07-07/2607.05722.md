> **一句话总结：** NVIDIA 提出 Nemotron-Labs-Diffusion，通过联合 AR-扩散训练目标在单一模型中统一自回归、扩散与自推测三种解码模式，8B 模型在 SPEED-Bench 上以 SGLang/GB200 取得相对 Qwen3-8B 4 倍吞吐，且 AR 精度不降反升。

> **TL;DR:** NVIDIA's Nemotron-Labs-Diffusion jointly trains a single LM on AR + block-wise diffusion losses (α=0.3, two-stage) to yield a tri-mode model (AR / diffusion / self-speculation) at 3B/8B/14B scales. The 8B instruct model in AR mode reaches 63.61% avg accuracy (vs 62.75% for Qwen3-8B, +0.86 pts) while linear self-speculation achieves 5.99× tokens-per-forward (TPF) and quadratic SS 6.38× TPF; on SPEED-Bench with SGLang on a GB200 GPU this translates to 4× higher throughput than Qwen3-8B, and a speed-of-light analysis shows diffusion decoding could still deliver 76.5% more TPF than current self-speculation under an optimal sampler.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **三模式语言模型（Tri-Mode LM）**：在单一架构内同时支持 AR 解码、块状扩散解码、以及自推测（self-speculation，扩散起草 + AR 验证）三种推理模式，无需任何模式专属的结构改动，可作为现有 AR 模型的 drop-in 替换。
- **联合 AR-扩散训练框架**：以加权损失 ℒ = ℒ_AR + α·ℒ_diff（α=0.3）联合优化，配合全局损失平均（Global Loss Averaging）降方差、两阶段训练（先纯 AR 强化左到右先验，再开扩散），实证 AR 与扩散目标互补而非零和——两者在同一 α=0.3 处同时达到峰值。
- **自推测解码优于 MTP**：用扩散模式生成多 token 草稿、用同模型 AR 验证，省去辅助预测头；在 SPEED-Bench 上平均接受长度 6.82（LoRA）vs Eagle3 的 2.75 与 MTP 的 4.24，低并发下对 Qwen3-8B-Eagle3 取得 2.4×/2.3×/1.8× 加速（GB200/RTX Pro 6000/DGX Spark）。
- **Speed-of-Light（SOL）分析**：通过递归动态压缩（recursive dynamic compaction）构造扩散解码的上界，显示 BL=32 时平均 7.60× 接受率、多语言与编码超 10×；相比线性自推测的真实 TPF 高出 76.5%，揭示当前置信度采样器远未触顶。
- **完整模型家族**：交付 3B/8B/14B 的 base/instruct 及 8B 视觉语言模型（VLM），VLM 在 AR 模式比最强基线 LLaDA-V-8B 高 1.3%，线性自推测达 3.63×–7.45× TPF 且精度仅降 0.1%。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a **tri-mode language model** that supports AR, block-wise diffusion, and self-speculation decoding (diffusion drafts + AR verifies) within one set of weights, requiring no mode-specific architectural changes and acting as a drop-in AR replacement.
- A **joint AR-diffusion training recipe** (ℒ = ℒ_AR + αℒ_diff, α=0.3) augmented with global loss averaging (to reduce gradient variance from variable masking) and two-stage training (pure AR first, then joint), with ablations showing the two objectives are complementary and peak at the same α rather than trading off.
- **Self-speculation beats MTP methods**: diffusion-mode drafting verified by the same model's AR head eliminates auxiliary draft heads, yielding average acceptance length 6.82 (with LoRA) vs 2.75 for Eagle3 and 4.24 for MTP on SPEED-Bench, and 2.4×/2.3×/1.8× speedup over Qwen3-8B-Eagle3 at batch size 1 on GB200/RTX Pro 6000/DGX Spark.
- A **speed-of-light (SOL) analysis** using recursive dynamic compaction to bound diffusion-mode parallelism: 7.60× average acceptance rate at block length 32 (>10× on multilingual/coding), and 76.5% more real TPF than linear self-speculation — quantifying the headroom left by current confidence-based samplers.
- A full **model family at 3B/8B/14B** (base, instruct, VLM), with the VLM achieving +1.3% over LLaDA-V-8B in AR mode and 3.63×–7.45× TPF via linear self-speculation at only −0.1% accuracy.

## Methodology

**Training objectives.** The model is trained on a weighted sum of a standard AR next-token loss and a block-wise diffusion denoising loss. The diffusion loss partitions the sequence into B contiguous blocks and denoises one block at a time conditioned on its clean prefix; within a block tokens attend bidirectionally (enabling parallel intra-block prediction), across blocks attention is causal (so KV cache can be reused). The joint loss is ℒ(θ) = ℒ_AR(θ) + α·ℒ_diff(θ) with α=0.3, chosen so the two loss magnitudes align rather than letting diffusion (which is intrinsically larger) overwrite AR priors.

**Global loss averaging.** Because random masking yields a variable number of loss-contributing tokens per sample, sequence-wise averaging amplifies the influence of low-noise samples (whose per-token losses carry large 1/t weights). The authors instead average over all N·L tokens globally (Eq. 5), which empirically reduces batch-to-batch fluctuation and improves average accuracy by +2.12% in ablation.

**Two-stage training.** Stage 1 sets α=0 (pure AR, ~1T tokens) to anchor left-to-right priors; Stage 2 turns on diffusion supervision with α=0.3. This single change adds +5.74% average accuracy in ablation, indicating strong AR initialization eases AR-to-diffusion conversion.

**Attention pattern (dual-stream).** Inputs are a concatenated noisy view + clean view of the same sequence. Noisy→Noisy and Noisy→Clean follow standard block-diffusion design; the key novelty is a **strictly causal mask within the clean stream**, so the AR objective on the clean context can be computed in the same forward-backward pass as the diffusion objective without label leakage.

**Tri-mode inference.**
1. *AR mode*: standard causal sampling, preferred for high-concurrency compute-bound serving.
2. *Diffusion mode*: block-wise denoising with a confidence threshold; optionally a lightweight trained sampler predicts whether each masked position's top-1 is correct, shifting the accuracy-TPF Pareto frontier up (+10.6% accuracy or 1.3× TPF).
3. *Self-speculation*: diffusion drafts k tokens in one forward pass, AR verifies in a second pass reusing prefix KV cache, accepting the longest matching prefix. A LoRA adapter (rank 128, ~0.4% of backbone params) on the o_proj of the drafter is trained with an LK-hybrid distribution-matching loss + token-level cross-entropy on the "accepted + first rejected" positions, boosting TPF by 14.4%/32.5%/27.6% at 3B/8B/14B. A quadratic variant performs drafting and verification in a single forward via a structured mask (predicts the next block at all acceptance positions).

**Speed-of-light construction.** A serial denoising pass (B forward passes, committing the highest-confidence position each step) defines the oracle target t for each block. Greedy parallel acceptance commits all matching positions but can drift; recursive dynamic compaction instead searches for the largest safe prefix of matches (safety re-checked via shallower greedy simulation under a 5000-forward-pass budget), recovering t exactly and giving the SOL ceiling.

## Results & Highlights

- **8B instruct, AR mode reaches 63.61% avg accuracy vs 62.75% for Qwen3-8B (+0.86 pts)** across GPQA/IFEval/MMLU/HumanEval/MBPP/LCB-CPP/Math500/GSM8K/AIME24/AIME25 (Table 5), while the diffusion mode delivers 63.18% at 2.57× TPF and quadratic self-speculation reaches 64.04% at 6.38× TPF — the highest accuracy and TPF simultaneously.
- The diffusion mode of NLD-8B outperforms **SDAR-8B Chat by +9.09%** in average accuracy (63.18 vs 50.57, Table 5) and beats LLaDA-8B Instruct (+25.94 pts, 63.18 vs 37.24) and Dream-7B Instruct (+22.64 pts), demonstrating that joint AR-diffusion training closes the historical accuracy gap of diffusion LMs.
- **On SPEED-Bench with SGLang on a GB200 GPU, NLD-8B decodes 6× more tokens per forward than Qwen3-8B** (TPF 5.99× for linear SS vs 1.00 for Qwen3-8B AR), translating to **4× higher throughput**; linear self-speculation delivers 2.4×/2.3×/1.8× speedup over Qwen3-8B-Eagle3 at batch size 1 on GB200/RTX Pro 6000/DGX Spark (Fig. 9).
- **Acceptance length on SPEED-Bench (Table 10): 6.82 (LoRA) vs 2.75 (Eagle3) vs 4.24 (MTP)** on average, widening to 8.69 vs 2.81 vs 4.73 on the four diffusion-friendly categories (coding/math/reasoning/multilingual) — diffusion drafting yields more reliable multi-token proposals on structured tasks.
- **Speed-of-light ceiling (Table 4, Fig. 7): 7.60× average acceptance rate at BL=32** (from 2.89× at BL=4), exceeding 10× on multilingual (11.26×) and coding (10.24×); the real TPF gap to linear self-speculation is 6.02× vs 3.41× = **76.5% more TPF under an optimal sampler**, indicating current confidence-based samplers (~3× TPF) leave large headroom.
- **LoRA ablation (Table 6):** adding the drafter LoRA improves linear-SS TPF by 14.4%/32.5%/27.6% at 3B/8B/14B (e.g., 8B from 4.52× to 5.99×) with essentially unchanged accuracy (62.88 → 62.81).
- **Scaling (Table 7):** NLD-3B linear SS beats Qwen3-4B by +1.77% accuracy (55.00 vs 53.23) at 4.36× TPF; NLD-14B linear SS beats Qwen3-14B by +1.19% (66.36 vs 65.17) at 5.96× TPF — improvements hold across scales and TPF grows with size (4.36× → 5.96×).
- **Base model (Table 8):** NLD-8B AR mode +5.14%/+0.31% over Ministral3-8B/Qwen3-8B; diffusion mode +17.21%/+6.83% over LLaDA-8B/Dream-7B; self-speculation achieves 4.67× (linear) and 7.04× (quadratic) TPF with >0.5% higher accuracy than Qwen3-8B.
- **VLM (Table 9):** NLD-Diffusion-VLM-8B AR mode 59.5% avg vs LLaDA-V-8B 58.2% (+1.3%); linear self-speculation preserves near-AR accuracy (−0.1%) at 3.63×–7.45× TPF for responses >200 tokens, most pronounced on long-reasoning tasks.
- **Ablation (Table 1):** the full training pipeline improves the block-wise-attention baseline from 54.23% to 70.28% (+16.05 pts); the AR loss contributes the single largest gain (+7.48 pts) and two-stage training adds +5.74 pts, validating that preserving the AR objective is critical for strong diffusion-LM accuracy.
- **Mutual impact (Table 3):** adding the diffusion loss (α=0.3) preserves or slightly improves AR-mode average accuracy (+0.14% base, +0.43% instruct), with the instruct model gaining +4.24% on LCB-CPP and +1.59% on MBPP, though IFEval drops 3.01% and HumanEval 2.44%.

## Authors & Credibility

Authored by a 26-person NVIDIA team including Pavlo Molchanov, Jan Kautz (VP of Applied Learning Research), Song Han (MIT, NVIDIA), and Enze Xie (HKU), with interns from Georgia Tech, HKU, U Chicago, and MIT. The work builds directly on the team's prior Block-Diffusion / Fast-dLLM / Efficient-dlm line (citations [9][10][13]) and NVIDIA's Ministral3 base ([21]), giving it strong infrastructure and methodological continuity. The paper is unusually thorough in self-analysis: it includes a controlled α-sweep, a mutual-impact study, a speed-of-light oracle analysis, per-category acceptance-length breakdowns, and multi-device (GB200/RTX Pro 6000/DGX Spark) FP8/INT4 profiling. Models are released on Hugging Face.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty** is genuine but incremental relative to [14] (Set Block Decoding), which already performed joint AR-diffusion training; the authors are explicit that their attention pattern follows [14], and their claimed deltas are (1) tri-mode inference / self-speculation, (2) the full training pipeline, and (3) the SOL analysis — not a new training paradigm. The self-speculation idea (diffusion drafts, AR verifies) is the cleanest conceptual contribution.
- **Rigor** is high on training ablations (Table 1, 2, 3) but the headline **+9.09% over SDAR-8B Chat** is partly a weak-baseline effect: SDAR-8B Chat scores only 50.57% avg, well below modern AR 8B models, so beating it does not by itself prove diffusion LMs have closed the AR gap — the more meaningful comparison is the +0.86 pts over Qwen3-8B in AR mode, which is within plausible noise for a 10-task average.
- **The SOL analysis has a subtle circularity**: the oracle target *t* is itself the diffusion model's own serial-denoising output, so SOL measures "how close parallel sampling gets to the model's own converged answer," not proximity to a ground-truth correct token. This is a reasonable efficiency ceiling but should not be read as an accuracy ceiling; the authors do not emphasize this distinction.
- **Quadratic self-speculation's 6.38× TPF is misleading for deployment**: the paper concedes its real-device efficiency "falls behind the linear one" because FlexAttention kernels are "less optimized," so the highest-TPF headline number is not the one users will actually see.
- **Missing ablations**: there is no comparison against a pure-diffusion model trained from scratch on the same data budget (the "α=1, no AR" row in Fig. 4 exists but is only a loss curve, not a benchmark table), so the claim that AR anchoring is *necessary* rather than merely *helpful* is under-supported. There is also no ablation isolating the LoRA loss components (LK-hybrid vs cross-entropy).
- **Reproducibility** is mixed: training hyperparameters are detailed (α=0.3, 25B continuous-pretrain tokens, 45B SFT tokens, 256 H100s, LoRA rank 128), but the trained sampler's architecture/feature engineering is deferred to "Appendix A" and the SPEED-Bench throughput numbers depend on specific SGLang/GB200 infrastructure that most readers cannot replicate; no training code is mentioned as released (only model weights on HF).
- **Data quirk**: the instruct model's IFEval drops 3.01% and HumanEval 2.44% when diffusion loss is added (Table 3), which the authors attribute to "strict instruction-following compliance is slightly affected" — this is a real regression that the headline "AR accuracy preserved or improved" framing somewhat obscures.

## Strengths

- Clean unification: tri-mode inference from a single joint objective with no architectural changes, and the ablations genuinely support the "complementary not competing" thesis (both modes peak at α=0.3, neither improves at the other's expense across [0.1, 0.5]).
- Practical deployment focus: real-device throughput on three GPU tiers with FP8/INT4, a head-to-head with Eagle3 (the strongest MTP baseline), and an honest acknowledgment that quadratic SS underperforms linear SS on current kernels.
- The SOL analysis is a valuable methodological contribution for the field — it gives diffusion-LM researchers a concrete upper bound to target and quantifies how far confidence samplers are from it (≈3× realized vs 7.60× ceiling).
- VLM extension is non-trivial: the asymmetric dual-stream (stripping vision tokens from the noisy half) is a sensible FLOP-saving design that keeps accuracy within 0.1% of AR while reaching 7.45× TPF.
- Strong scaling story: improvements and TPF both grow from 3B to 14B, and the model is a true drop-in for AR workloads.

## Limitations

- The strongest AR comparison (+0.86 pts over Qwen3-8B on 10 tasks) is a modest margin that could be within evaluation variance; no error bars or repeated-run variance are reported.
- Diffusion-mode accuracy degrades at larger block lengths (Table 4: benchmark accuracy 65.43 at BL=8 but 61.81 at BL=32), so the high-TPF regime trades accuracy, and "improving diffusion performance under larger block lengths" is left as future work.
- Self-speculation's real TPF (3.41×) is far below its acceptance rate (6.82×) because of the doubled forward-pass cost and prefix-only acceptance — the gap to SOL (6.02×) is large and the paper's own conclusion is that closing it requires samplers that do not yet exist.
- The paper does not release training code, and several key components (sampler architecture, trajectory data collection, the AR-diffusion ensemble verifier for quadratic SS) are only sketched or pushed to appendices.
- IFEval and HumanEval regress for the instruct model when diffusion loss is added, a real capability cost for instruction-following deployments.
- Evaluation is largely on coding/math/QA; open-ended generation quality, hallucination rates, and long-context behavior are not assessed.

## Relevance

对关注推理加速与扩散语言模型的研究者高度相关：它系统性回答了"AR 与扩散能否共存""扩散能否超越 MTP""扩散解码的天花板在哪"三个核心问题，并提供 3B–14B 可复用模型与 SOL 分析框架，直接服务于高效 LLM 部署与下一代并行解码算法设计。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the +0.86-pt AR advantage over Qwen3-8B hold up under repeated runs or alternative evaluation harnesses (e.g., lm-eval-harness vs NeMo-Skills)? Is the margin statistically significant?
- Can the SOL gap (3.41× realized vs 6.02× ceiling) be closed by a learned sampler that commits non-contiguous positions, and would a diffusion-mode verifier (rather than prefix-only AR verification) actually preserve correctness?
- What is the pure-diffusion (α=1, no AR) model's benchmark accuracy on the same 25B-token budget? The paper shows its loss curve but not its task scores, leaving the necessity of AR anchoring under-tested.
- How do the three modes behave on open-ended generation, long-context tasks, and hallucination-prone QA, where token entropy is higher than the structured coding/math benchmarks that dominate the evaluation?
- Does the IFEval/HumanEval regression for the instruct model compound with scale, or is it a transient effect of the 45B-token SFT mix that better data could fix?
