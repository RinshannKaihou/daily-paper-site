§ 01 Methodology

DP-NGD attacks the three obstacles to fusing natural-gradient descent with differential privacy through three mutually reinforcing modules. The whole pipeline keeps the (ε, δ)-DP guarantee of DP-SGD, trackable by any standard accountant (Rényi DP, PRV) without modification.

  1. Privacy-free coarse curvature. The K-FAC Fisher matrix F is estimated on a 500-image (~1%) OOD public set — outside the DP adjacency relation, so it costs zero privacy budget. DP noise corrupts fine-grained components anyway, so only dominant coarse eigenspaces need fidelity. Recomputed every T_cur=8 steps.
  2. Whitened-space update (Alg. 1). All DP ops move into the F−1/2 whitened space: project → clip ‖ĝ‖≤C → isotropic Gaussian noise → inverse-project. Theorem 3.1: the expected update is the adaptively-clipped natural gradient and noise covariance becomes (η²σ²C²/B²)F−1. By DP post-processing, projections add no privacy cost — the KL-DP duality: C is both DP sensitivity and NGD trust-region radius.
  3. Dynamic curvature clamping. Inverse projection is unbounded in flat directions (λ→0 ⇒ 1/√λ→∞). A safety floor λ_safe=(η_ngd C_ngd / η_sgd C_sgd)² is derived from DP-SGD's expected step; a three-phase schedule (conservative warmup → high-SNR plateau → restored safety) lifts only dangerous directions. Removing the floor diverges to NaN.

§ 03 Strengths vs Limitations

Strengths

  • Provable reconciliation of isotropic DP with anisotropic second-order optimization (Thm 3.1 + KL-DP duality) — not a heuristic.
  • Zero added privacy cost: the full budget stays on gradients; Table 3 shows this is essential, not incidental.
  • Frugal auxiliary data — stable within 1% from 50 to 5,000 public samples.
  • Biggest gains exactly where they matter: under the strictest ε.
  • Net 3.4× wall-clock speedup — practical, not just theoretical.

Limitations

  • Requires a suitable public auxiliary set; OOD-transfer untested on severely mismatched / non-image domains.
  • Only small image tasks & modest architectures; LLM/ViT scale left as future work.
  • Code is a placeholder repo at submission — not yet reproducible.
  • ~2.7× per-step compute; benefit only materializes when step reduction is large.
  • Asymmetric clip (C=10 vs C=1) is justified but a matched-C ablation is missing.
  • Gains attenuate at high ε (sub-1% on CIFAR-10 at ε=8.0).

§ 04 Open Questions

  1. Does coarse-grained curvature transfer survive text, tabular, or speech, where low-level statistics are less universal?
  2. Can the public-data requirement be dropped via fully private sketched curvature without the collapse seen in Table 3?
  3. Behavior at foundation-model scale — can block-diagonal K-FAC keep per-step cost tractable for LLMs/ViTs?
  4. Is there a quantitative domain-mismatch threshold beyond which dominant eigenspaces diverge and acceleration degrades?
  5. Does stacking DP-NGD with DP-Mix / auto-clipping push the frontier, or do gains saturate?
  6. A matched-C ablation (C=10 for all, or C=1 for DP-NGD) would cleanly isolate geometry from trust-region dynamics.
