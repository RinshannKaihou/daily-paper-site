> **一句话总结：** SpanUQ 提出在"语义连贯的文本片段（span）"层级进行 LLM 不确定性估计的新任务，用一个约 25M 参数的轻量探针（DETR 风格 span 解码器 + Mixture of Beta 不确定性头 + 迭代精炼）从冻结 LLM 隐藏状态的单次前向传播中蒸馏出多采样不确定性，在自建的 SpanUQ-Bench 上五个 LLM 取得 AUROC 0.908–0.944，比最强探针基线和采样方法都快 10–20 倍且更准。

> **TL;DR:** SpanUQ formalizes span-level uncertainty estimation (SLUE) for LLMs and solves it with a ~25M-parameter probe that distills expensive multi-sample uncertainty into a single forward pass over frozen hidden states (DETR span decoder + Mixture of Beta head + uncertainty-conditioned refinement); on the new SpanUQ-Bench (20K prompts, ~293K spans, five domains), it reaches AUROC 0.908–0.944 and MAE 0.110–0.129 across five LLMs, beating the MLP Probe baseline (AUROC 0.881 on Qwen3-14B) by +5.8 points and sampling-based SelfCheckGPT-NLI (0.808) by +13.1 points while being 10–20× faster, with its DETR detector hitting 0.910 F1 vs the best heuristic's 0.653 (+39.4%).

## 核心贡献

- **新任务形式化：** 提出并形式化"Span 级不确定性估计（SLUE）"任务，填补 token 级（语义不完整）与序列级（无法定位错误）两种粒度之间的空白，把"单一可验证语义单元"作为不确定性估计的天然粒度。
- **轻量探针框架 SpanUQ：** 设计约 25M 参数（<0.2% LLM 参数）的探针，集成 DETR 风格 span 解码器（可处理重叠 span）、Mixture of Beta 不确定性头（捕捉双峰分布）、以及 Uncertainty-Conditioned Iterative Refinement（UCIR，第二轮"再看一眼"），将 20 次采样的不确定性知识蒸馏进单次前向传播。
- **首个 span 级基准 SpanUQ-Bench：** 构建含 20K 提示、约 293K 标注 span、覆盖五个领域（Natural Questions、TriviaQA、ELI5、Biography/FActScore、FELM 聚合）的基准，并配套多采样蒸馏管线自动生成连续软标签。
- **跨模型实证：** 在五个 LLM（Qwen3-14B/8B/4B/30B-A3B、Mistral-7B，覆盖 4B–30B、dense 与 MoE）上一致取得最优 span 级不确定性质量，并发现序列级不确定性可部分分解为 span 级分量（ρseq = 0.839），暗示 span 级是序列级的更一般情形。

## Key Contributions

- **New task formalization:** Formalizes Span-Level Uncertainty Estimation (SLUE), bridging token-level (semantically incomplete) and sequence-level (cannot localize errors) granularities, positioning the single verifiable semantic unit as the natural granularity for uncertainty.
- **Lightweight SpanUQ probe:** A ~25M-parameter (<0.2% of the LLM) module combining a DETR-style span decoder (handles overlapping spans), a Mixture of Beta (MoB) uncertainty head (captures bimodal uncertainty), and Uncertainty-Conditioned Iterative Refinement (UCIR) — distilling 20-sample uncertainty into a single deterministic forward pass over frozen hidden states.
- **First span-level benchmark:** Constructs SpanUQ-Bench with 20K prompts, ~293K annotated spans across five domains, featuring continuous soft labels derived from multi-sample claim verification against Wikipedia.
- **Cross-model empirical validation:** Achieves the best span-level uncertainty quality across five LLMs spanning two families, three scales, and both dense/MoE architectures, and provides evidence that sequence-level uncertainty is partially decomposable into span-level components (ρseq up to 0.839).

## Methodology

SpanUQ is a lightweight (~25M-parameter) probe attached to a frozen LLM backbone, operating on hidden states from a single forward pass. It has five components:

1. **Multi-Layer Fusion.** Hidden states from three layers in the peak uncertainty zone (layers 22/24/26 for Qwen3-14B, selected via a single-layer probing sweep following an inverted-U curve) are mean-fused into H̄.

2. **Token Encoder.** H̄ is projected to a low dimension d_p=512 (10× compression) and contextualized via a 2-layer Transformer encoder with sinusoidal positions, yielding the shared feature pool Z used by both the decoder and the enrichment module.

3. **DETR-Style Span Decoder.** Span detection is formulated as set prediction: N=32 learnable content-agnostic span queries attend to Z through a 3-layer Transformer decoder (self-attention for inter-span coordination, cross-attention for localization). Hungarian matching assigns predictions to ground-truth spans. The set-prediction formulation naturally handles overlapping spans (unlike BIO tagging). Regression (L1 + Generalized IoU) is used for continuous, differentiable boundary prediction rather than pointer/argmax.

4. **Span Feature Enrichment.** A differentiable soft boundary mask extracts span content from Z via mask-weighted attention pooling, fused with the query through a gated residual connection — giving the scorer direct access to "what" the span says, not just "where" it is.

5. **Prediction Heads.** Three heads: boundary regression, validity classification (to suppress unused query slots), and a Mixture of Beta (MoB, J=3 components, α_min=0.5) uncertainty estimator. Beta (not Gaussian) is chosen because uncertainty is bounded in [0,1] and Beta can express U-shaped/bimodal distributions; the mixture captures the empirically bimodal uncertainty (spans cluster near 0 or 1). Span scores aggregate to sequence level via importance-weighted pooling.

**Training** is two-phase: a warmup training only span detection, then a joint phase activating MoB NLL loss, a contrastive ranking loss (preserving ordinal structure among spans), a sequence-consistency loss, and DETR-style auxiliary losses on intermediate layers.

**Inference** uses a single LLM forward pass plus the probe; UCIR feeds Round-1 estimates back through the shared decoder (with a lightweight MLP adapter) for a second pass, combined via α=0.7, adding <15% overhead. The whole module adds <5% latency to the LLM forward pass. No sampling, multiple passes, or external retrieval is needed at inference.

**Labels** come from multi-sample distillation: for each prompt, one greedy response provides probe inputs, while S=20 sampled responses (T=1.0) are decomposed into atomic claims and verified against Wikipedia; the soft label u*_k is the fraction of samples in which span k is unsupported.

## Results & Highlights

- **Span-level discrimination (Qwen3-14B, Tab. 1):** SpanUQ achieves AUROC 0.939, outperforming the strongest probe baseline MLP Probe (0.881, which even uses oracle ground-truth span boundaries) by +5.8 absolute points, and the best multi-sample method SelfCheckGPT-NLI (0.808, requiring 10× generation cost) by +13.1 points.
- **Span-level regression & ranking:** SpanUQ reaches the lowest MAE 0.110 (vs MLP Probe 0.139) and Spearman ρ_s 0.685, a 19.1% relative improvement over MLP Probe (0.575) — preserving fine-grained uncertainty ordering that sequence-level methods cannot (SelfCheckGPT-NLI ρ_s only 0.471, FActScore only 0.181).
- **Cross-model generalization (Tab. 1):** AUROC 0.908–0.944 and MAE 0.110–0.129 consistently across five backbones (Qwen3-14B/8B/4B/30B-A3B and Mistral-7B), including dense and MoE architectures spanning 4B–30B parameters.
- **Speed:** 10–20× faster than sampling-based methods while being more accurate, since it requires only a single forward pass.
- **End-to-end span detection (Tab. 2):** The DETR detector attains F1 0.910 (precision 0.857, recall 0.970), surpassing the best heuristic (Sliding Window 20, F1=0.653) by 39.4%; downstream AUROC reaches 0.927 vs the best heuristic's 0.861 (+7.7%), and ρ_span 0.736 vs 0.556 (+32.4%).
- **BIO tagger failure:** The standard BIO sequence tagger performs worst (F1=0.079, AUROC=0.763) because it cannot represent overlapping spans — it detects 165K candidate spans but only 7,663 match ground truth (4.6% precision), motivating the DETR set-prediction design.
- **Sequence-level aggregation (Tab. A3):** SpanUQ's importance-weighted span composition reaches AUROC@0.3=0.948 and Spearman ρ_seq=0.851 on Qwen3-14B, surpassing dedicated sequence-level methods SelfCheckGPT-NLI (ρ=0.805), Semantic Entropy (ρ=0.606), and Verbalized Confidence (ρ=0.322); decomposability holds across all five backbones (ρ_seq 0.775–0.857).
- **Ablation (Tab. 3):** The Mixture of Beta head yields the largest single gain (+1.3% AUROC, −62% ECE to 0.020, −19% MAE); UCIR adds +0.6% AUROC and +3.0% ρ_span; the enrichment gate adds +1.3% AUROC.
- **Weak baselines exposed:** P(True) performs near chance (AUROC=0.526) because the LLM predicts "True" 93.8% of the time; Token Entropy is near chance on ranking (ρ_s=0.097); Verbalized Confidence on Mistral-7B has negative Spearman (−0.026).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (genuine):** The SLUE task formalization is a real conceptual contribution — the argument that spans (not tokens or whole sequences) are the natural uncertainty granularity is well-motivated, and the observation that sequence-level uncertainty is partially decomposable (ρ_seq=0.839) is a non-trivial empirical finding. The DETR-for-uncertainty adaptation with overlapping-span support and a Mixture of Beta head is a clean synthesis, though it builds directly on CLUE and HaluNet (DETR-style span architectures) and Semantic Entropy Probes (hidden-state distillation of multi-sample signals).
- **Rigor (mixed):** The evaluation is extensive — five LLMs, six baselines across four granularity levels, ablations on every component, layer-selection sweeps, entity-popularity strata, and qualitative failure cases. However, the headline span-level comparison (Tab. 1) is partly favorable by construction: sequence-/claim-level baselines (SelfCheckGPT-NLI, FActScore, Semantic Entropy) are *broadcast* to all spans in a sequence, which structurally caps their span-level Spearman — so the +13.1 AUROC gap over SelfCheckGPT-NLI partly reflects the evaluation regime rather than pure method quality at sequence level, where SpanUQ's ρ_seq=0.851 only marginally beats SelfCheckGPT-NLI's 0.805. The paper is commendably explicit about this but readers may still over-read the span-level deltas.
- **Label quality caveat:** Ground-truth labels are *automatically* derived from Wikipedia-verified multi-sample distillation, validated only on a 10% random sample. This means the probe is arguably learning to mimic an automatic verifier (and its systematic biases) rather than ground-truth human factuality — a circularity the paper acknowledges only lightly. The bimodal label distribution (most spans near 0 or 1) also means AUROC is somewhat forgiving; the MAE and the ~5% failure rate on nuanced claims are more telling.
- **Reproducibility (good):** Architecture, losses, hyperparameters, and layer-selection procedure are fully specified; training is on a single H100 with 17,494 samples over 40 epochs. However, the full data construction pipeline (20-sample generation + LLM-judge claim decomposition + Wikipedia verification) is compute-intensive and dependent on a specific external LLM judge (Claude Opus via a particular endpoint), which may drift over time and is not trivially reproducible.
- **Significance (solid):** The 10–20× speedup with a <0.2%-parameter probe is the practically compelling result for deployment, and the end-to-end detection (F1 0.910 vs heuristic 0.653) shows the DETR design is necessary, not decorative. The BIO-tagger failure (F1=0.079) is a useful negative result for the community.
- **Red flags:** (1) SpanUQ *cannot* capture generation-process (aleatoric) uncertainty — only hidden-state epistemic patterns — so it is conceptually narrower than sampling-based methods despite outperforming them on this benchmark's labels. (2) Requires white-box hidden-state access, ruling out API-only LLMs. (3) Evaluation is English factual QA only; the paper explicitly flags reasoning and multilingual extension as future work, so generalization to math/code/reasoning hallucinations is untested. (4) The Qwen3-4B probe shows a systematic +0.2 MAE_seq bias (overestimating sequence uncertainty), a known weak point.

## Strengths

- Principled task formulation: SLUE cleanly fills the granularity gap with a clear definition of "span" and a concrete motivating example (Marie Curie / Nobel Prize year).
- Strong engineering: a genuinely lightweight probe (<0.2% of LLM params, <5% latency) that is 10–20× faster than sampling while more accurate on span-level metrics.
- Architectural ablations are informative and each component earns its place (MoB gives the biggest single jump; UCIR improves ranking; enrichment gate helps short-span content access).
- Comprehensive cross-model evaluation across two families, three scales, and dense + MoE architectures, with decomposability analysis showing span-level subsumes sequence-level.
- Honest reporting of failure modes (compositional hallucinations, nuanced/temporal claims, the 4B aggregation bias) rather than only cherry-picked successes.
- Useful negative result: the standard BIO tagger catastrophically fails (F1=0.079) on overlapping uncertainty spans, motivating set-prediction.

## Limitations

- **Epistemic-only, not aleatoric:** SpanUQ reads hidden-state patterns and cannot capture uncertainty arising from the stochastic generation process itself; it is conceptually narrower than sampling-based semantic entropy.
- **White-box requirement:** Needs access to internal hidden states, so it is inapplicable to closed API-only LLMs.
- **Compute-intensive label construction:** Building the benchmark requires 20 samples/prompt plus LLM-judge decomposition plus Wikipedia verification — a one-time but heavy cost, and dependent on a specific external judge model that may drift.
- **English factual QA only:** Evaluated solely on English factual uncertainty; reasoning, math, code, and multilingual settings are untested and explicitly deferred.
- **Label circularity risk:** Soft labels come from an automatic verifier, so the probe learns to reproduce that verifier's (possibly biased) judgments rather than human ground truth; only 10% of the test set was human-validated.
- **Compositional and nuanced failures:** Struggles with compositional hallucinations (correct entities, wrong relations) and underestimates uncertainty for ~5% of historically nuanced/temporally sensitive claims.
- **Scale-dependent bias:** The Qwen3-4B probe exhibits a +0.2 systematic overestimate in sequence-level MAE.
- **Modest sequence-level margin over dedicated methods:** At sequence level, SpanUQ (ρ_seq=0.851) only narrowly beats SelfCheckGPT-NLI (0.805) — its dominant advantage is at the span granularity, which is also the granularity where competitors are structurally disadvantaged by the broadcast evaluation.

## Relevance

SpanUQ 直接命中"LLM 不确定性量化与幻觉定位"这一研究兴趣，其 span 级粒度与单次前向推理的轻量探针设计，对可信 LLM 部署、自精炼（self-refinement）以及检索增强场景下的错误定位都具有较强的方法论参考价值；同时其自建 benchmark 与多采样蒸馏标注管线也可作为后续不确定性研究的基准设施。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does SpanUQ transfer to non-factual uncertainty — multi-step reasoning, math/code generation, or instruction-following — where "verifiable atomic claim" decomposition is far less clean?
- Could the probe be trained on cheaper pseudo-labels (e.g., self-consistency without external Wikipedia verification) without losing AUROC, reducing the circularity and compute burden of label construction?
- Does the learned span-to-sequence decomposability (ρ_seq=0.839) hold under distribution shift (e.g., a probe trained on Qwen3-14B applied to a different domain or a post-finetune checkpoint)?
- Can SpanUQ be adapted to black-box API LLMs by distilling hidden-state probes from a small open surrogate model trained to mimic the API's outputs?
- How does the Mixture of Beta head interact with the bimodal label distribution — would a more continuous (less polarized) labeling scheme reveal calibration weaknesses hidden by the current 0/1-clustered labels?
- Does feeding SpanUQ's span-level scores back into self-refinement actually improve downstream task accuracy, and by how much, compared to sequence-level confidence gating?
