LAQuant: Simple Overhead-free Large Reasoning Model Quantization via Layer-wise Lookahead Loss

Euntae Choi, Sumin Song, Sungjoo Yoo — Seoul National University

A layer-wise weight-only quantization-aware training method that uses a one-layer lookahead loss to preserve KV-cache fidelity during quantization. Through systematic gradient-direction analysis, the authors identify two root causes of reasoning accuracy degradation in end-to-end QAT and address both without online transform overhead — producing standard integer weights deployable on any existing kernel.

Key Finding
End-to-end QAT degrades long-chain reasoning because the softmax Fisher metric attenuates gradient signals along KV-cache-preserving residual directions, and mismatched calibration data misaligns the activation Hessian subspace. A one-layer lookahead loss (Mla = Jl+1T Jl+1) eliminates both problems by inducing cross-layer co-adaptation without softmax Fisher dilution — no online transforms required.

Why This Matters
Quantized reasoning models that actually preserve long-chain accuracy — no online transforms, standard integer weights deployable on any existing kernel, single-GPU training. The calibration data finding alone (reasoning traces yield up to 38.45pp improvement) is a standalone practical insight.
Critical Edge
The +15.11pp headline conflates calibration data choice with the lookahead loss; the controlled comparison yields only +1.93pp. Untested beyond 8B parameters where quantization matters most. General-task performance is matched, not improved — this method is reasoning-specialized.

Method & Analysis

  1. Proposition 1 — E2E QAT induces metric ME2E = JT HKL J where HKL (softmax Fisher) is anisotropic, bounded by ½, and vanishes as teacher max-probability approaches 1. This dilutes gradients along KV-cache directions.
  2. Calibration alignment — Activation Hessian eigenspace must align with deployment distribution. Switching from WikiText2/C4 to DeepSeek-R1 reasoning traces on OpenR1-math closes this gap, yielding up to 38.45pp improvement on AIME25.
  3. Lookahead loss — LLA = SmoothL1(Fl+1(Q(hl)), Fl+1(Fl(hlt))) passes quantized output through the next teacher layer, inducing Mla = Jl+1TJl+1 — cross-layer co-adaptation without softmax Fisher attenuation.
  4. Overhead-free deployment — Produces standard integer quantized weights with per-group scales/zeros. Compatible with AWQ GEMV kernels. Eliminating online rotation adds 6–14% throughput over ParoQuant.
  5. Training — Built on EfficientQAT. Per-layer 20 epochs, frozen embeddings/LM head, AdamW. Qwen3-8B trains in 14.91h on single H200 (24.8% faster than ReasoningQAT).
