> **一句话总结：** ReST-KV将KV缓存驱逐建模为逐层输出重建优化问题，通过推导出闭式指标I_t[n] = A_t[n]/(1-A_t[n]) * ||MHA(·) - v_n W_O||_2 自然捕获注意力重分配效应，结合EMA时间平滑和自适应窗口空间平滑，在LongBench +2.58%、RULER +15.2%上超越SOTA，128k上下文下解码延迟降低10.61倍，已获ICLR 2026接收。

> **TL;DR:** ReST-KV reformulates KV cache eviction as a layer-wise output reconstruction problem, deriving a closed-form indicator that captures attention redistribution effects beyond raw attention weights. Combined with EMA temporal smoothing and adaptive window-based spatial smoothing, it achieves 2.58% improvement on LongBench, 15.2% on RULER, and 10.61x decoding latency reduction at 128k context. Accepted at ICLR 2026.

## 核心贡献

- 将KV缓存驱逐重新建模为逐层注意力输出重建的优化问题，推导出闭式重要性指标：I_t[n] = A_t[n]/(1-A_t[n]) * ||MHA(x_t, <K_T,V_T>) - v_n W_O||_2，该指标分解为非线性注意力重加权项和重分配敏感项两部分
- 设计指数移动平均（EMA）时间平滑处理KV重要性的时间波动，以及自适应窗口空间平滑捕获KV重要性在位置上的漂移模式（如vertical-slash结构）
- 在5个开源LLM（Llama2-7B/13B, Llama3-70B, Mistral-7B, Qwen2.5-7B, Gemma-7B）上验证，LongBench +2.58%，RULER +15.2%，Needle-in-a-Haystack保留98%性能
- 128k上下文下解码延迟降低10.61倍，与FlashAttention-2兼容，与PyramidKV/AdaKV等预算分配策略正交可组合，与稀疏预填充方法（MInference/FlexPrefill）结合实现TTFT 2.37倍加速
- Prefill额外开销仅约2%（相对于SnapKV），代码开源：https://github.com/an-yongqi/rest-kv

## Key Contributions

- Reformulates KV cache eviction as a layer-wise output reconstruction optimization with a closed-form indicator: I_t[n] = A_t[n]/(1-A_t[n]) * ||MHA(·) - v_n W_O||_2, decomposing into a nonlinear attention reweighting term and a redistribution sensitivity term
- Introduces EMA temporal smoothing for handling temporal fluctuations in KV importance, and adaptive window-based spatial smoothing to capture positional drift patterns (e.g., vertical-slash attention structures)
- Evaluated on 5 open-source LLMs (Llama2-7B/13B, Llama3-70B, Mistral-7B, Qwen2.5-7B, Gemma-7B), achieving LongBench +2.58%, RULER +15.2%, and 98% Needle-in-a-Haystack performance retention
- 10.61x decoding latency reduction at 128k context with FlashAttention-2; orthogonal to and composable with PyramidKV/AdaKV budget strategies and MInference/FlexPrefill sparse prefill methods (2.37x TTFT speedup combined)
- Negligible prefill overhead of approximately 2% over SnapKV; publicly available code at https://github.com/an-yongqi/rest-kv

## Methodology

ReST-KV consists of two core components:

**1. Layer-wise Output Reconstruction (LOR) Indicator.** Rather than ranking KV pairs by raw attention weights, ReST-KV measures the reconstruction error of the MHA output when each KV pair is removed. Starting from the objective of minimizing ||MHA(x_t, <K_T,V_T>) - MHA(x_t, <K_T\setminus_n, V_T\setminus_n>)||_2, the authors derive a closed-form expression (Eq. 7) that decomposes into two factors:

- **Nonlinear Attention Reweighting:** A_t[n]/(1-A_t[n]) acts as a monotonic amplifier that better discriminates between high-competition KV pairs compared to linear attention weight scaling.
- **Redistribution Sensitivity:** ||MHA(·) - v_n W_O||_2 captures how well the remaining KV pairs can compensate for the removed value, i.e., the attention redistribution effect. If other values can collectively approximate the removed one, the KV pair is less important.

This indicator only requires computing attention outputs over a small observation window (S_w=32), sharing the same O(wND) complexity as SnapKV.

**2. Spatial-Temporal Smoothing.** Empirical analysis (Figure 3) reveals that KV importance exhibits both temporal fluctuations and spatial shifts:

- **EMA Temporal Smoothing (Eq. 8-9):** Uses exponential moving average over recent query steps within the observation window (alpha=0.3) to dampen short-term fluctuations while emphasizing recent importance patterns. Recent S_w tokens are always preserved (assigned value Omega).
- **Adaptive Window-Based Spatial Smoothing (Eq. 10-13):** Splits the observation window into front/rear halves, computes the average position of top-B important KV pairs in each half, and uses the difference (Delta D) to adaptively adjust the spatial smoothing window size W_s and shift gamma_shift (controlled by scaling factor beta=2000). The final indicator is the average of EMA-smoothed indicators over this adaptive window.

The method performs eviction only once during the prefill stage, selecting the top-B KV pairs per head/layer based on the smoothed indicator.

## Results & Highlights

**LongBench (16 tasks, 6 categories):**
- At tight budget B=64L: ReST-KV achieves 33.54 on Llama3.1-8B vs. 31.48 (SnapKV), a 2.06 point gain; on Mistral-7B: 40.05 vs. 37.53 (SnapKV), 2.52 points
- At B=512L: ReST-KV achieves 38.33 on Llama3.1-8B vs. 37.47 (SnapKV); on Mistral-7B: 46.47 vs. 45.66
- Dramatically outperforms LaCache on Llama2-7B at B=64L: 25.54 vs. 13.51 (nearly 2x)
- Consistent gains across Qwen2.5-7B, Gemma-7B, Llama2-13B, Llama3-70B

**RULER (4k-128k context lengths, 13 tasks):**
- 15.2% average accuracy improvement over prior methods at B=1024L on Llama3.1-8B
- At 128k context where less than 1% of original cache is retained, ReST-KV maintains effective retrieval
- Particularly strong on retrieval tasks (S-NIAH, MQ-NIAH, MV-NIAH) and variable tracking (VT)

**Needle-in-a-Haystack:**
- At B=1024L on Mistral-7B: retains 98% of full KV cache performance
- At B=128L on Llama3.1-8B: achieves 74% accuracy, surpassing SnapKV by 6%
- At B=1024L on Llama3.1-8B: achieves 100% accuracy (matching full cache with 1/32 of tokens)

**InfiniteBench (tasks up to 2M+ tokens):**
- 38.8% vs. 36.8% (SnapKV) overall average on Llama3.1-8B
- Confirms no cumulative degradation at extreme context lengths (En.QA: avg 192.6k tokens; Zh.QA: avg 2,068.6k tokens)

**Efficiency:**
- 10.61x decoding latency reduction at 128k context with FlashAttention-2
- 36.0% peak memory reduction at 128k context
- Prefill overhead: only approximately 2% additional TTFT over SnapKV at 128k
- Compatible with FlexPrefill: combined 2.37x TTFT speedup + 10.54x decoding speedup
- Compatible with KV cache quantization (KIVI, KVQuant): ReST-KV + 4-bit quantization outperforms aggressive 2-bit quantization on full cache

**Ablation Studies:**
- LOR indicator alone: +1.91% over attention-weight baseline
- EMA temporal smoothing: +1.84% contribution
- Adaptive window spatial smoothing: +2.36% contribution
- Output reconstruction (35.86) substantially outperforms attention weights (33.95), attention+value norm (33.88), and random (6.83)
- Hyperparameters alpha and beta are stable across wide ranges; fixed values (alpha=0.3, beta=2000) used for all experiments

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** The reformulation of KV eviction as output reconstruction is a genuine conceptual advance. The closed-form derivation (Eq. 7) is elegant and provides clear insight into why output-aware eviction works better than attention-weight-only methods. The decomposition into reweighting and redistribution terms is theoretically motivated and empirically validated.
- **Rigor (Strong):** Evaluated on 4 benchmarks (LongBench, RULER, NIAH, InfiniteBench) across 7 models spanning MHA and GQA architectures, with sizes from 7B to 70B. Comprehensive ablations isolate each component's contribution. Compatibility tested with PyramidKV, AdaKV, FlashAttention-2, MInference, FlexPrefill, KIVI, and KVQuant. However, the main comparison is against 5 baselines (StreamingLLM, H2O, TOVA, SnapKV, LaCache) -- some recent methods like DMC or LeanKV are not discussed.
- **Reproducibility (Strong):** Public code, fixed hyperparameters across all experiments (alpha=0.3, beta=2000), detailed implementation details including observation window size (S_w=32) and kernel sizes.
- **Significance (High):** KV cache eviction is a critical practical problem. The 15.2% gain on RULER at aggressive compression is substantial. The 10.61x latency reduction with minimal quality loss has direct deployment implications. ICLR 2026 acceptance validates community interest.
- **Potential concerns:**
  - The eviction happens only during prefill; the paper mentions that decoding-time eviction is straightforward but does not evaluate it, which is a gap for streaming/growing-context scenarios.
  - The comparison with LaCache shows dramatic differences at tight budgets (25.54 vs. 13.51 on Llama2-7B), but the gap narrows at higher budgets and larger models, suggesting the method's advantage is most pronounced under severe constraints.
  - The spatial-temporal smoothing has several hyperparameters (alpha, beta, S_w, kernel size) though the paper shows they are robust. The kernel size is changed from 5 to 21 for RULER/InfiniteBench without much justification.
  - No comparison with training-based compression methods (e.g., GQA, token merging during training), though this is orthogonal to the paper's focus on inference-only methods.

## Strengths

- Principled formulation with elegant closed-form derivation that provides clear interpretability
- Directly addresses the overlooked problem of attention redistribution after KV eviction
- Comprehensive evaluation across models, architectures, sizes, and benchmarks
- Demonstrated compatibility with a wide range of orthogonal optimization techniques
- Negligible prefill overhead (approximately 2% over SnapKV)
- Strong practical impact: 10.61x decoding speedup at 128k with minimal quality loss
- ICLR 2026 acceptance indicates strong peer validation

## Limitations

- Only performs eviction during prefill; no evaluation of dynamic/online eviction during decoding, which is important for multi-turn dialogue and streaming applications
- Does not compare with some recent eviction methods beyond the 5 selected baselines
- The advantage over SnapKV narrows at higher cache budgets and larger model sizes, suggesting diminishing returns when cache is less constrained
- Kernel size is increased from 5 to 21 for RULER/InfiniteBench experiments without thorough justification or systematic tuning analysis
- No evaluation on multimodal models or structured/quantized KV caches beyond the basic integration tests
- The paper claims model-agnostic design but does not test on non-transformer architectures

## 相关性

该论文与LLM高效推理和长上下文处理直接相关。KV缓存优化是部署大规模语言模型的核心瓶颈之一，ReST-KV通过输出重建视角提供了比纯注意力权重更准确的KV重要性评估方法。其在极低缓存预算下的显著性能优势和与多种优化技术的正交兼容性，对实际LLM部署系统有直接参考价值，尤其是在128k及以上上下文长度场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How would ReST-KV perform with dynamic/online eviction during decoding, where KV importance must be continuously reassessed? The paper mentions this extension but does not evaluate it.
- Can the LOR indicator be used to inform adaptive per-layer or per-head budget allocation more directly, rather than being combined with existing strategies as a post-hoc addition?
- How does the method interact with speculative decoding, where multiple draft tokens require simultaneous KV cache access?
- Is the spatial smoothing mechanism capturing genuinely useful structural information (e.g., vertical-slash patterns), or is it primarily acting as a denoising regularizer?
- Could the reconstruction error indicator be extended to cross-attention layers in encoder-decoder models or multimodal architectures?
- What is the performance at extremely aggressive eviction budgets (e.g., B=16L or B=32L) where less than 0.1% of tokens are retained?
