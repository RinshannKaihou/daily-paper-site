Muon-OGD: Muon-based Spectral Orthogonal Gradient Projection for LLM Continual Learning

Binghang Lu, Zheyuan Deng, Runyu Zhang, Bing Hu, Yunhan Zhao, Yuan Tian, Changhong Mou, Guang Lin, Xiaomin Li
Purdue · Brown · MIT · UC Irvine · Utah State · Harvard
Key Finding

Frobenius-norm projection in continual learning is geometrically incompatible with spectral-norm Muon optimizer updates. Muon-OGD unifies both via a spectral-norm-constrained formulation solved through Lagrangian duality.

Key Metric
78.9%
Avg Accuracy on T5-Large 5-task benchmark (vs O-LoRA 75.8%). TRACE: 49.4% AA, -4.7% BT — best across all methods.
Why This Matters

Opens a new direction for LLM continual learning: norm choice fundamentally affects forgetting geometry. Spectral-norm-aware protection consistently wins across 7 architectures and 4 benchmark types.

Critical Edge

Dual correction strength has a strict sweet spot — too weak fails to protect, too strong kills plasticity. No convergence theory. Gains over strongest baselines sometimes modest (0.7% on 15-task). Five sensitive hyperparameters.

Method: Spectral-Norm-Constrained Orthogonal Projection

min ⟨G, Δ⟩   subject to   ||Δ||2 ≤ η,  ⟨Ci, Δ⟩ = 0 for all i

Dual:  minλ ||G + Σ λi Ci||*  (nuclear norm)
Solved via Newton-Schulz matrix-sign approximation — only 1–2 inner dual iterations suffice.
  • Replaces Frobenius-norm regularization (classical OGD) with spectral-norm ball constraint aligned to Muon's operator-norm geometry
  • Low-rank instantiation: constraints from top-k singular vectors of pretrained weights; dual variables form compact k×k matrix Λ
  • Correction term = U · Λ · VT — efficient low-rank product applied to q/k/v/o projection layers

Strengths

  • Principled formulation bridging Muon optimization and projection-based CL with Lagrangian duality
  • Practical: 1–2 inner dual iterations; warm-starting makes it nearly single-loop
  • Consistent improvements across classification, instruction-following, and domain-specific reasoning
  • Comprehensive ablations covering learning rate, dual correction strength, protected rank, orthogonalization precision

Limitations

  • 5 sensitive hyperparameters (muon_k, muon_T, muon_eta, muon_eta_dual, lr) with sharp sensitivity profiles
  • No convergence guarantees for the dual iteration scheme
  • Only applied to projection layers (q, k, v, o); LoRA extension is future work
  • Memory overhead for protected subspaces across many tasks not quantified
  • No wall-clock training time comparison reported

Open Questions

  • Can Muon-OGD extend to LoRA-based fine-tuning where spectral-norm constraints need reinterpretation under low-rank parameterization?
  • Does the warm-starting strategy introduce bias in the dual subgradient descent?
  • How does memory cost scale with task count — can older subspaces be compressed or merged?
  • Would combining with replay-based methods yield further gains for long task sequences?
  • How does it perform at 70B+ scale where O(N3) Newton-Schulz overhead becomes significant?
