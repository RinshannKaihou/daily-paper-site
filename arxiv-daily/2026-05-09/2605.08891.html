Key Finding

Bilinear Autoencoders Find Interpretable Manifolds

Lifting autoencoding from x to x xT turns nonlinear manifold discovery into linear dictionary learning — quadratic latents compose linearly in weight space yet their receptive fields are ellipsoids, hyperboloids, and other curved surfaces the standard SAE can never represent.

Thomas Dooms, Ward Gauderis, Geraint Wiggins, Jose Oramas

arXiv 2605.08891 · 9 May 2026 · github.com/tdooms/bae · visualizer
Key Metric — Receptive Field Shrinkage
~10−56

Exponential ratio of quadratic vs. linear atom receptive-field volume at d = 1024, τ = 0.5 (Theorem 1).

Why This Matters

Standard SAEs assume every feature is a single linear direction. In practice, many salient LLM features — clusters, cones, smooth interpolations — live on multi-dimensional manifolds that no linear probe can isolate. Bilinear autoencoders capture that geometry with negligible extra cost via the kernel trick.

Critical Edge

No causal evaluation yet: the method discovers geometric structure but cannot steer or ablate. Antipodal symmetry means +w and −w are indistinguishable, losing signed information. Individual latents match only 15–50 % across runs — subspaces are stable, specific decompositions are not.

Methodology
  • Encoder computes zi = xT LT diag(ci) R x — each latent is a Frobenius inner product in the lifted space X = x xT.
  • Decoder reconstructs the product space; a closed-form correction (Eq. 9) converts product-space NMSE to input-space NMSE for fair comparison.
  • Kernel trick K = CT(L LT ˆ R RT)C avoids materializing the d2-dimensional lifted space, keeping memory identical to a standard SAE.
  • Three nested priors trade expressivity for interpretability: Atomic (rank-1, symmetric slabs) → Composite (sparse rank-1 mixtures, ellipsoids/hyperboloids) → Quadratic (unconstrained, arbitrary quadric surfaces).
  • Scale-invariant Hoyer density with per-latent learnable offset replaces L0/L1, avoiding trivial shrinkage.
  • Trained on Qwen 3.5 (0.8B), FineWeb data, 8192 latents, ~8 M tokens, ~15 min on RTX 4080. Validated on Gemma-3-1B and Llama-3.2-1B.
Results & Highlights
  • Reconstruction error consistently orders quadratic < composite < atomic across all even layers of Qwen 3.5, confirming that composite latents capture structure atomic ones miss.
  • Four geometric patterns qualitatively identified in activations: cone (years), cluster (U.S. references), interpolation (drug ↔ addictions), subset (for-contexts).
  • Different autoencoders with varying sparsity penalties recover > 90 % Frobenius-similar subspaces but only 15–50 % per-latent overlap — subspace is robust, specific decomposition is not.
  • After product-space correction, bilinear autoencoders beat the TopK (K = 32) baseline across most layers.
  • Spectral analysis on layer 18: most composite latents need ~5 dimensions, with a long tail of higher-rank structures that only emerge under the quadratic prior.
Strengths
  • Elegant Veronese lifting turns nonlinear dictionary learning into linear dictionary learning; kernel trick keeps it tractable.
  • Exponential receptive-field gap (Theorem 1) gives a clean theoretical basis for intrinsic activation sparsity.
  • Composite regime discovers manifold subspaces during training instead of requiring post-hoc co-activation analysis.
  • Weight-based Frobenius and Hungarian similarity metrics enable exact autoencoder comparison without forward passes.
  • Strong reproducibility: public code, 15-min training, interactive visualizer with 8192 latents.
Limitations
  • Antipodal blindness: cannot distinguish +w from −w — fundamental information loss for asymmetric concepts.
  • Compute scales as O(k2): the zTKz term is already heavy at k = 8192; Llama 3.2 results degrade when d grows without scaling k and h.
  • No downstream evaluation: discovers geometry but does not show steering, ablation, or circuit-discovery utility.
  • Limited model scale: primary results at 0.8–1 B parameters; transfer to 70 B+ models unknown.
  • Narrow baseline: only TopK SAE; no comparison with Gated, JumpReLU, or recent manifold-focused methods.
Open Questions
  • Can quadratic latents support signed causal interventions (steering, ablation) despite antipodal symmetry?
  • Can block-diagonal approximations of K tame the O(k2) compute cost at scale?
  • Does subspace stability hold when the same autoencoder is trained on different data distributions?
  • Could a hybrid of bilinear + linear latents recover signed information?
  • Do multi-dimensional geometries become more or less prevalent in 7 B / 70 B models?
  • Is there a principled way to select among the three structural priors for a given layer?
  • The Born-rule analogy ( zi as expectation of projector on pure state ) — cosmetic or a route to quantum-inspired methods?
