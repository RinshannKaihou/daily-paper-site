> **一句话总结：** 本文提出因果维度 $\kappa(L,M,\mathcal{T})$ 作为 Transformer 残差流中有效因果方向数的度量，定义为期望 Jacobian 外积的有效秩，通过 SAE 宽度扫描与归因修补估计。在 Gemma-2-2B 上发现"表征-因果楔形"（64x 宽度增长下表征容量增长 15.6x，因果容量仅增长 4.35x），$\hat{\kappa} \approx 1{,}990$（$\hat{\kappa}/d \in [0.77, 0.86]$），且 $\kappa$ 在模型缩放下形状不变、在深度上恒定但归因阈值下降 20x。编码器/解码器消融实验揭示训练编码器是"稀疏过滤器"而非特征选择器，颠覆了对 SAE 编码器功能的传统理解。

> **TL;DR:** Introduces causal dimensionality $\kappa$ — the effective rank (exponential Shannon entropy of the normalized eigenspectrum) of the expected Jacobian outer product $\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_L = \mathbb{E}[\mathbf{J}_L^\top \mathbf{J}_L]$ — to measure how many directions in a transformer's residual stream causally influence output. The "representational-causal wedge" shows that SAE width increases expand representational capacity 15.6x but causal capacity only 4.35x (bootstrap 95% CI [3.86, 4.91]). A saturating fit gives $\hat{\kappa} \approx 1{,}990$ with $\hat{\kappa}/d \in [0.77, 0.86]$ robust across two fit families. The AtP score distribution is shape-invariant under 3.46x model scaling (Gemma-2-2B vs 9B), and attribution thresholds drop 20x from layer 1 to 23 while $N_{causal}$ stays constant. A four-cell encoder/decoder ablation reveals the trained encoder acts as a sparsity filter (random encoder inflates $N_{causal}$ by 9.27x), inverting the standard understanding of SAE encoder function. Cross-family replication on LLaMA-3.1-8B and Pythia-410m confirms the wedge is not a Gemma artefact. Companion short paper under review at ICML 2026 Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop.

## 核心贡献

- 定义因果维度 $\kappa(L,M,\mathcal{T}) = \mathrm{eff\text{-}rank}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_L)$，其中 $\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_L = \mathbb{E}_{x \sim \mathcal{T}}[\mathbf{J}_L(x)^\top \mathbf{J}_L(x)]$，并证明 $\kappa \leq \min(d, |\mathcal{V}|)$（命题 3.3）
- 证明 SAE 宽度扫描是 $\kappa$ 的一致估计量（命题 3.7），在三个假设下（Spanning、Faithfulness、No dead features）$N_{causal}(m) \to r_{\varepsilon'}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_L)$
- 发现"表征-因果楔形"：在 Gemma-2-2B 层 12 上，64x 的 SAE 宽度增长使 $N_{repr}$ 增长 15.6x 而 $N_{causal}$ 仅增长 4.35x（bootstrap 95% CI [3.86, 4.91]，排除了楔形作为采样伪影的可能）
- 估计 $\hat{\kappa} \approx 1{,}990$（$\hat{\kappa}/d \in [0.77, 0.86]$ 在两个拟合族中鲁棒），参与比下界 $\kappa_{PR} \approx 280$
- 发现 AtP 分数分布在 3.46x 模型缩放下的形状不变性（Gemma-2-2B 层 12 vs Gemma-2-9B 层 20，三重验证：跨模型阈值交换 $\Delta=2.4\%$、灵敏度扫描比例 0.95x-1.00x、惰性分数差异 0.02pp）
- 发现 $\kappa$ 在网络深度上恒定，但绝对归因阈值 $\varepsilon_{abs}$ 从层 1 到层 23 下降 20x（$2.27 \times 10^{-4}$ 到 $1.13 \times 10^{-5}$）
- **最令人惊讶的机制发现**：四单元编码器/解码器消融实验显示，随机编码器使 $N_{causal}$ 膨胀至 9.27x，揭示训练编码器是"稀疏过滤器"而非特征选择器，暗示可解释性研究应关注解码器结构而非编码器特征标签
- 跨族复制在 LLaMA-3.1-8B（惰性分数 97.86%，与 Gemma 差异 -0.09pp）和 Pythia-410m 上确认楔形不是 Gemma 特有的伪影

## Key Contributions

- Defines causal dimensionality $\kappa(L,M,\mathcal{T}) = \mathrm{eff\text{-}rank}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_L)$ where $\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_L = \mathbb{E}[\mathbf{J}_L^\top \mathbf{J}_L]$, using the effective rank measure of Roy & Vetterli (2007), and proves the upper bound $\kappa \leq \min(d, |\mathcal{V}|)$ (Proposition 3.3)
- Proves the SAE width sweep is a consistent estimator of the spectral count $r_{\varepsilon'}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_L)$ under three stated assumptions (Proposition 3.7), providing theoretical grounding for the empirical methodology
- Identifies the "representational-causal wedge": $N_{repr}$ grows 15.6x vs $N_{causal}$ only 4.35x across seven matched-L0 SAE widths (16K to 1M) on Gemma-2-2B layer 12, with bootstrap 95% CI [3.86, 4.91] on the wedge ratio
- Estimates $\hat{\kappa} \approx 1{,}990$ with $\hat{\kappa}/d_{model} \in [0.77, 0.86]$ across two fit families (saturating exponential and exponential-with-dip), and participation ratio lower bound $\kappa_{PR} \approx 280$
- Demonstrates shape invariance of the AtP score distribution under 3.46x model scaling (Gemma-2-2B vs 9B), validated by three concordant tests: cross-model threshold exchange ($\Delta=2.4\%$), sensitivity sweep ($N_{causal}$ ratio 0.95x-1.00x across $\varepsilon \in [0.1\times, 10\times]$), and inert fraction match (97.95% vs 97.93%)
- Shows $\kappa$ is constant across eight network depths while $\varepsilon_{abs}$ drops 20x from layer 1 ($2.27 \times 10^{-4}$) to layer 23 ($1.13 \times 10^{-5}$), with practical implication that fixed absolute thresholds miscount causal features across layers
- **Most surprising mechanistic finding**: four-cell encoder/decoder ablation shows RANDOM_ENC inflates $N_{causal}$ to 3,040 (9.27x the baseline of 328), while RANDOM_DEC loses 31% and SHUFFLED_DEC loses an additional 17% — the trained encoder acts as a sparsity filter, not a feature selector
- Cross-family replication on LLaMA-3.1-8B (inert fraction 97.86%, -0.09pp from Gemma) and Pythia-410m confirms the wedge is not a Gemma artefact; Pythia gap explained as SAE-utilisation artefact (58% dead features)

## Methodology

The paper introduces a rigorous framework for measuring causal dimensionality:

**Formal Definition (Section 3):**
- **Layer Causal Jacobian**: $\mathbf{J}_L(x) = \partial \mathbf{y}(x) / \partial \mathbf{h}_L(x) \in \mathbb{R}^{|\mathcal{V}| \times d}$ maps residual stream perturbations to logit changes.
- **Causal Dimensionality**: $\kappa(L,M,\mathcal{T}) = \mathrm{eff\text{-}rank}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_L)$ where $\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_L = \mathbb{E}_{x \sim \mathcal{T}}[\mathbf{J}_L(x)^\top \mathbf{J}_L(x)]$ and effective rank is the exponential Shannon entropy of the normalized eigenspectrum.
- **Upper Bound**: $\kappa \leq \min(d, |\mathcal{V}|)$. For Gemma-2-2B ($d=2304$, $|\mathcal{V}|=256{,}000$), the binding bound is $d=2304$.

**Estimation via Attribution Patching (Section 3.3):**
- Computing $\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_L$ exactly requires $d$ backward passes per input (~2,300 for Gemma-2-2B), which is intractable.
- Attribution patching (AtP): $\text{AtP}(L) = (\mathbf{h}_L^c - \mathbf{h}_L^r) \odot \nabla_{\mathbf{h}_L^r} \mathcal{L}$, costing only 2 forward + 1 backward pass.
- Validated: $\rho = 0.838$ globally, $\rho > 0.95$ in the top-5% regime that determines $N_{causal}$.

**Causal Feature Count (Section 3.4):**
- $N_{causal}(m) = |\{i \in [m] : \text{AtP}_i \geq \varepsilon\}|$ where $\varepsilon$ is calibrated once at reference width $m_{ref} = 16{,}384$ as the 98th percentile ($\varepsilon_{abs} = 3.563 \times 10^{-6}$), then held fixed across all wider SAEs.
- Consistency (Proposition 3.7): Under spanning, faithfulness, and no-dead-features assumptions, $N_{causal}(m) \to r_{\varepsilon'}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_L)$ as $m \to \infty$.

**Saturating Curve Fit (Section 3.5):**
- $N_{causal}(m) = \kappa(1 - e^{-m/\tau})$ where $\tau \approx 881{,}000$ is the characteristic width for 63% recovery.
- $\hat{\kappa} = 1{,}990$ (Wald CI$_{95} = [0, 4225]$, bootstrap CI$_{95} = [545, 5130]$, median 1,705).
- Participation ratio $\kappa_{PR} \approx 280$ as threshold-free robustness check.

**Experimental Setup (Section 4):**
- **Models**: Gemma-2-2B ($d=2304$, 26 layers), Gemma-2-9B ($d=3584$, 42 layers), Pythia-70m (12 layers), LLaMA-3.1-8B ($d=4096$, 32 layers).
- **SAEs**: GemmaScope canonical and matched-L0 at seven widths (16K to 1M, 2x steps), plus cross-family SAEs.
- **Corpus**: 200 prompts from Pile-10k, capped at 512 tokens.
- **Compute**: Single A100 PCIe 80GB, ~120 A100-hours total.

## Results & Highlights

- **Representational-causal wedge (Section 5.1)**: $N_{repr}$ grows from 15,973 to 249,566 (15.6x); $N_{causal}$ grows from 328 to 1,427 (4.35x). Bootstrap 95% CI [3.86, 4.91] excludes the $N_{repr}$ ratio by an order of magnitude. The non-monotonic dip at $m=65{,}536$ is attributed to feature splitting (a known SAE pathology documented by Chanin et al. 2025).

- **Scale invariance (Section 5.2)**: Gemma-2-2B layer 12 and Gemma-2-9B layer 20 both yield $N_{causal} = 328$ at $m=16{,}384$. Three concordant tests support shape invariance: (i) cross-model threshold exchange ($\varepsilon_{9B}$ applied to 2B gives $N_{causal} = 336$, $\Delta = 2.4\%$), (ii) sensitivity sweep holds cross-model ratio at 0.95x-1.00x, (iii) inert fractions match at 97.95% vs 97.93%. Caveat: only a single 9B width point; full 9B width sweep deferred.

- **Layer profile (Section 5.3)**: $N_{causal} = 328$ constant across eight layers (by construction), but $\varepsilon_{abs}$ drops 20x from layer 1 ($2.27 \times 10^{-4}$) to layer 23 ($1.13 \times 10^{-5}$). Practical implication: any cross-layer study with a fixed absolute threshold will over-count causal features in late layers.

- **Architecture invariance (Section 5.4)**: Sub-linear $N_{causal}$ growth holds across TopK (1.32x), JumpReLU (1.43x), and standard ReLU (1.56x) over a 16x width range. The wedge is a property of the attribution-patching measurement, not any specific SAE architecture.

- **Encoder/decoder ablation (Section 6.3)**: REAL=328, RANDOM_DEC=225 (-31%), SHUFFLED_DEC=170 (-17% additional), RANDOM_ENC=3,040 (9.27x inflation). The random encoder activates nearly all features ($N_{repr} = 16{,}367$, ~100% utilisation), routing through meaningful decoder directions.

- **Threshold robustness (Section 6.1)**: Pre-registered criterion (ratio < 10x at 4/5 thresholds) FAILS (only 3/5 pass). However, at calibrated and stricter regimes ($1.0\times$ to $10\times$), the ratio remains 3.1-4.4x. Failure at loose thresholds ($0.1\times$, $0.3\times$) due to signal/noise inseparability. Authors transparently report the FAIL verdict.

- **Synthetic ground-truth recovery (Section 6.4)**: Zero recall across all 24 runs at cosine thresholds {0.3, 0.5, 0.7}. This pins down that AtP measures encoder activation patterns times decoder routing, not recovery of a causal basis.

- **SVA circuit validation (Section 6.5)**: Against Marks et al.'s 56-feature SVA ground-truth: p99 recovers 24/56 (42.9%), p98 recovers 43/56 (76.8%), p95 recovers 55/56 (98.2%). On wrong distribution (Pile-10k), recall collapses to 0.04.

- **Cross-family replication (Appendix J)**: LLaMA-3.1-8B inert fraction 97.86% (-0.09pp from Gemma). Pythia-410m gap (-2.72pp) explained as SAE-utilisation artefact (58% dead features in the public sae-pythia-410m-65k SAE).

- **Implications for SAE training (Section 7)**: At the smallest GemmaScope width, empirical capture is 328/1,990 ~ 16%. At $m=1{,}048{,}576$, capture is ~72%. Reaching 95% requires $m \approx 2.6 \times 10^6$, beyond any publicly released Gemma-2 SAE. Current SAE widths are substantially under-sampled for complete causal enumeration.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The formal definition of causal dimensionality and the consistency proof (Proposition 3.7) are clean theoretical contributions. The "representational-causal wedge" and the encoder-as-sparsity-filter finding are genuinely surprising empirical results that challenge prevailing assumptions in SAE-based interpretability.

- **Rigor**: Strong overall. The paper states three propositions with proofs/proof sketches, carefully separates what is proved from what is empirically supported (Proposition 3.8 is explicitly backed by empirical evidence rather than formal proof), and the consistency result clearly states its three assumptions. The calibration at the 98th percentile (forcing $N_{causal} = 0.02 \cdot m$ at reference width) is honestly discussed as tautological at the reference point; the empirical content is the trajectory at other widths. The transparent reporting of pre-registration failures is a significant methodological strength.

- **Reproducibility**: Good. All models and SAEs are public (GemmaScope, Pile-10k, Pythia, LLaMA SAEs). Code submitted as supplementary. The detailed Section 4 and appendices provide sufficient experimental detail. However, code not yet public at time of review.

- **Significance**: Potentially high for the mechanistic interpretability field. The finding that ~98% of SAE features are causally inert on generic text has direct implications for how SAE-based safety analyses should be conducted. The encoder-as-sparsity-filter result suggests the community's focus on encoder feature labels may be misplaced.

- **Red flags and honest concerns**:
  1. The $\hat{\kappa}$ estimate has a very wide CI ([0, 4225] Wald, [545, 5130] bootstrap) with only 4 post-minimum data points, making the absolute value unreliable. The authors' reframing around $\hat{\kappa}/d \in [0.77, 0.86]$ is more robust but still based on limited data.
  2. The scale invariance claim is based on a single 9B width point, not a full width sweep. The authors acknowledge this but the title ("Scaling") somewhat overstates the evidence.
  3. The U-shape at $m=65{,}536$ and the authors' proxy analysis for feature splitting (using AtP-score proximity rather than decoder-cosine similarity because $W_{dec}$ tensors were not retained) is a methodological gap they themselves flag.
  4. The gap between $\hat{\kappa} \approx 1{,}990$ and $\kappa_{PR} \approx 280$ is large and not fully resolved — these measure different things, but the paper could be clearer about which is the "right" answer in practice.
  5. Cross-family replication uses different hook positions (residual stream for Gemma, MLP output for LLaMA/Pythia) due to SAE availability constraints, which limits the strength of the cross-architecture comparison.
  6. The study is limited to generic next-token prediction on Pile-10k (200 prompts, seq=512); task-specific $\kappa$ behavior remains unexplored.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally honest reporting: transparent about pre-registration failures, acknowledges limitations upfront, reports both Wald and bootstrap CIs
- Clean theoretical framework with formal propositions connecting the empirical estimator to the theoretical quantity
- The "representational-causal wedge" is a powerful conceptual tool for understanding SAE limitations
- The encoder-as-sparsity-filter finding is genuinely surprising and inverts a standard assumption in the field
- Comprehensive control suite (five controls plus cross-family replication) that carefully delineates what $\kappa$ does and does not measure
- Well-structured experimental design spanning width, depth, scale, architecture, and model family axes
- AtP validated against exact patching ($\rho = 0.838$ global, $> 0.95$ in the regime that matters)
- Practical implications clearly stated: current SAE widths capture only ~72% of causal directions at 1M features, needing ~2.6M for 95%

## Limitations

- $\hat{\kappa}$ has very wide confidence intervals; the robust claim is only the ratio $\hat{\kappa}/d$, not the absolute value
- Scale invariance based on a single 9B width point; full width sweep deferred due to compute cost (~63GB, 24-48 A100h)
- Cross-family replication uses different hook positions (residual stream vs MLP output) due to public SAE availability
- Single task distribution (generic Pile-10k text); task-specific $\kappa$ behavior unexplored despite $\kappa$ being defined as task-dependent
- The gap between $\hat{\kappa} \approx 1{,}990$ and $\kappa_{PR} \approx 280$ is substantial and reflects fundamental ambiguity in what "causal dimensionality" means for heavy-tailed spectra
- Feature splitting U-shape analysis uses a proxy (AtP-score proximity) rather than the preferred decoder-cosine similarity
- Pre-registered tests for threshold robustness, sparsity dependence, and architecture invariance all formally FAIL, though substantive claims hold
- No public code at time of writing

## Relevance

本文与 mechanistic interpretability 研究高度相关，特别是 SAE 特征的因果有效性评估。如果你关注 Transformer 内部表示的结构性质、SAE 解释性方法的局限性（~98% 的 SAE 特征在通用文本上因果惰性）、或模型缩放对内部表征的影响（因果维度不随参数量增长），这篇论文提供了重要的定量框架和反直觉的实验发现，对 SAE 安全分析的可信度有直接的实际意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does $\kappa$ change under task-specific distributions (e.g., code, math, multilingual text)? The paper shows recall collapses to 0.04 on the wrong distribution — does $\kappa$ itself change, or only the mapping between SAE features and causal directions?
2. The encoder-as-sparsity-filter finding suggests interpretability should focus on decoder structure — what does a decoder-centric analysis workflow look like in practice?
3. Can the wedge ratio be used as a quality metric for SAE training? If $\kappa$ saturates at $\hat{\kappa}/d \approx 0.86$, does an SAE with higher $N_{causal}/N_{repr}$ ratio indicate better training?
4. Does the scale invariance hold across larger jumps (2B to 70B) and across fundamentally different architectures (e.g., mixture-of-experts models)?
5. The synthetic ground-truth experiment shows zero recall — what does this imply for the validity of SAE-based causal circuit discovery? Are current circuit findings capturing true causal structure or merely activation pattern correlations?
6. Reaching 95% causal capture requires $m \approx 2.6 \times 10^6$ — is this feasible, and are there more efficient SAE architectures that could achieve higher capture at lower width?
7. The 20x attribution threshold attenuation across depth suggests late-layer features have weaker individual causal influence — does this mean interpretability is inherently harder for later layers, or just that the measurement tool needs recalibration?
