> **一句话总结：** 本文提出PriUS框架，通过三个感知对齐原则（对比度、损坏严重度、几何复杂度）将不确定性监督转化为基于图像梯度的排序约束、噪声单调性约束和边界距离衰减约束，在证据学习框架中显式正则化不确定性的空间分布，在ACDC/ISIC/WHS数据集上实现更可解释的不确定性估计和最佳HD95分割精度。

> **TL;DR:** PriUS formalizes uncertainty interpretability through three perception-aligned principles and implements them as gated supervision losses (contrast ranking, corruption monotonicity, geometry decay) within evidential deep learning. On ACDC, ISIC, and WHS, it is the only method among six baselines (DEviS, PU, UDrop, TTA, EU) to maintain correct uncertainty-ambiguity correlation signs across all three principles, while achieving the best HD95 on ACDC (7.45 mm) and WHS (5.49 mm) and competitive DSC.

## 核心贡献

- 首次将"不确定性可解释性"形式化定义为：估计的不确定性的空间分布是否以人类可理解的方式反映模糊性来源
- 提出三个感知对齐原则：不确定性应随边界对比度降低而升高、随图像损坏加剧而单调递增、随距语义边界距离增加而衰减
- 设计原则引导的不确定性监督框架PriUS，每个原则通过可测量的图像代理（梯度幅值、噪声水平、边界距离）转化为带门控的排序/单调性损失函数
- 引入两种可解释性评估指标：UCC（不确定性-模糊性相关系数）和UR（不确定性正确排序比例），填补了该领域缺乏定量评估的空白
- 在ACDC、ISIC和WHS三个数据集上，PriUS是唯一在所有三个原则维度上均保持正确相关性符号的方法，同时在ACDC和WHS上取得最佳HD95

## Key Contributions

- Formalize uncertainty interpretability as whether uncertainty spatial patterns coherently reflect identifiable sources of image ambiguity
- Propose three perception-aligned principles linking uncertainty to boundary contrast, corruption severity, and anatomical geometry
- Develop PriUS, which translates each principle into a gated supervision loss using image-derived proxies (gradient magnitude, noise level, boundary distance map) within evidential deep learning
- Introduce two quantitative interpretability metrics: Uncertainty-Correlation Coefficient (UCC) and Uncertainty Ranking (UR), measuring alignment between uncertainty and ambiguity sources
- Demonstrate that PriUS is the only method among six baselines to preserve correct uncertainty-ambiguity relationships across all three principles on all datasets, while achieving best HD95 on ACDC and WHS

## Methodology

PriUS is built on evidential deep learning (EDL) and adds principle-guided uncertainty supervision as auxiliary training objectives. The overall loss is:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{total}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{seg}} + \mathcal{L}_u$$

where $\mathcal{L}_{\text{seg}}$ combines cross-entropy, Dice, and KL divergence losses (with annealed KL and Dice weights), and $\mathcal{L}_u$ is the uncertainty supervision.

**Evidential formulation:** The network outputs evidence vectors per pixel, parameterizing a Dirichlet distribution over class probabilities. A key design choice is using a non-uniform base-rate prior estimated from training set class frequencies rather than the standard uniform prior, which incorporates dataset-level class imbalance. Pixel-wise uncertainty is computed as $u_i = C / \sum_j \alpha_{i,j}$.

**Uncertainty supervision** follows a unified gated formulation:

$$\mathcal{L}_u = \mathbb{E}_{s \sim \mathcal{S}} \left[ \sum_{k \in \{g, \sigma, d\}} \phi_s(s; \mathcal{L}_k(u_s; z_s^{(k)})) \right]$$

Each principle contributes a base supervision loss $\mathcal{L}_k$ (encoding the desired uncertainty ordering/monotonicity) and a gating function $\phi_s$ (restricting supervision to spatially relevant regions):

1. **Contrast supervision ($\mathcal{L}_g$):** For boundary-adjacent pixel pairs $(i,j)$ of the same class, the loss penalizes violations of inverse ordering between image gradient and uncertainty. Image gradient is aggregated along boundary normals. Gating uses a sigmoid-based soft mask on boundary distance to restrict supervision to near-boundary regions.

2. **Corruption supervision ($\mathcal{L}_\sigma$):** Three noise levels of Gaussian noise are added to the input. The loss penalizes violations of monotonic uncertainty growth as noise increases, enforced via a hinge-style ranking loss on aggregated local uncertainty. Gating emphasizes near-boundary regions while suppressing homogeneous interiors.

3. **Geometry supervision ($\mathcal{L}_d$):** Uses Euclidean distance maps to semantic boundaries. Implements a two-regime loss: (a) near-boundary, uncertainty should decrease with distance from boundary (ranking constraint); (b) in homogeneous interior regions, uncertainty should be jointly suppressed toward zero. A soft regime indicator $t_{ij}$ smoothly transitions between the two behaviors. An adaptive modulation term $\Omega_d(i,j)$ handles gating implicitly.

**Implementation:** U-Net backbone, Adam optimizer (lr=$10^{-3}$), dataset-specific supervision coefficients. ACDC uses 5-fold cross-validation with $128\times128$ crops. ISIC uses $256\times256$ resized images. WHS uses 3D patches ($64^3$) with Gaussian-weighted sliding window inference.

## Results & Highlights

**Uncertainty interpretability (UCC and UR metrics):**
- On ACDC, PriUS achieved the strongest UCC magnitudes and highest UR on all three dimensions. It was the only method to preserve all three expected correlation signs simultaneously.
- On ISIC, several baselines recovered correct signs for contrast and geometry (dermatoscopic images may be easier in this regard), but PriUS remained the most balanced.
- On WHS (challenging 7-class multi-structure task), some baselines achieved individual geometry scores, but PriUS was the most consistent across all principles.

**Per-principle quantitative analysis:**
- Contrast: PriUS showed a clearly negative regression slope between image gradient and uncertainty ($-9.0 \times 10^{-3}$), while all competing methods showed positive trends. Under progressive Gaussian blur, PriUS had the smallest proportion of negative $\Delta u$ values, indicating stable monotonic uncertainty increase as boundary contrast degraded.
- Corruption: Under increasing Gaussian noise, PriUS showed a dominant positive shift in $\Delta u$ with the smallest proportion of negative changes. Competing methods showed symmetric or corruption-insensitive distributions.
- Geometry: PriUS exhibited a clear negative trend between uncertainty and boundary distance (slope $\approx -2.6 \times 10^{-2}$), while competing methods showed slopes near zero.

**Segmentation accuracy (Table II):**
| Method | ACDC DSC (%) | ACDC HD95 (mm) | ISIC DSC (%) | ISIC HD95 (px) | WHS DSC (%) | WHS HD95 (mm) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PriUS** | **91.05** | **7.45** | 84.33 | 34.31 | 88.72 | **5.49** |
| DEviS | 88.74 | 9.12 | **85.68** | 25.85 | **89.80** | 5.51 |
| PU | 86.78 | 9.89 | 84.49 | 33.56 | 81.77 | 8.86 |
| UDrop | 91.36 | 8.10 | 86.20 | **24.16** | 89.14 | 6.06 |
| TTA | 70.14 | 29.02 | 83.30 | 55.66 | 87.89 | 7.18 |
| EU | 86.79 | 10.25 | 85.40 | 22.35 | 81.92 | 10.04 |

PriUS achieved the best HD95 on ACDC and WHS, attributed to the boundary-sensitive uncertainty supervision reducing severe boundary outliers. DSC was competitive but not always the best.

**Ablation study (ACDC):**
- Full model: DSC 91.05%, HD95 7.45 mm, correct UCC signs on all three principles.
- Removing $\mathcal{L}_g$: contrast-related UCC flipped sign, UR dropped.
- Removing $\mathcal{L}_\sigma$: corruption-related behavior broke down.
- Removing $\mathcal{L}_d$ (geometry): most severe segmentation degradation (DSC 54.67%, HD95 31.79 mm without corruption supervision; 90.65% DSC without geometry), indicating geometry-consistent uncertainty is critical for structural prediction stability.
- No individual or partial combination matched the full model.

**Parameter sensitivity:** Boundary threshold $d_0=8$ provided the best trade-off. $d_0=16$ caused training failure (DSC 25.75%).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The conceptual contribution is strong. The framing of uncertainty interpretability through perception-aligned principles and the formalization via gated ranking/monotonicity losses is genuinely novel. The two-regime geometry supervision (boundary ranking + interior suppression) is a particularly elegant design. The idea that uncertainty should be semantically constrained during training, not just estimated post hoc, is a meaningful paradigm shift.

- **Rigor:** The paper introduces two new metrics (UCC, UR) and evaluates them thoroughly with both quantitative and qualitative analysis. The ablation study is comprehensive, showing each component's contribution. The parameter sensitivity analysis (with failure mode at $d_0=16$) is honest and informative. However, the new metrics are self-proposed and self-evaluated -- there is no external validation (e.g., against human expert annotations of where uncertainty should be high) to confirm that the metrics themselves capture clinically meaningful interpretability.

- **Reproducibility:** Public datasets, U-Net backbone, and detailed hyperparameter settings (including dataset-specific coefficients) are provided. Code availability is not mentioned in the paper. The dataset-dependent tuning of supervision coefficients ($\lambda_g$, $\lambda_\sigma$, $\lambda_f$) raises some concern about generalizability.

- **Significance:** If the community adopts the principle-based perspective and the UCC/UR metrics, this could shift how uncertainty evaluation is done in medical image segmentation. The finding that existing methods (EDL, MC Dropout, ensembles, TTA) all produce poorly interpretable uncertainty is itself an important negative result.

- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - The baselines are somewhat limited: DEviS (prior work from the same group), Probabilistic U-Net, Uncertainty Dropout, TTA, and Ensemble U-Net. Notable absences include PhiSeg, Post-Net, and more recent calibrated EDL methods.
  - The segmentation accuracy is competitive but not state-of-the-art. On ISIC, PriUS achieves 84.33% DSC vs. UDrop's 86.20%. The paper argues HD95 is the more relevant metric, but this deserves scrutiny.
  - The corruption principle uses synthetic Gaussian noise, which may not reflect realistic clinical image degradation (motion artifacts, bias field, low SNR in specific sequences).
  - TTA's anomalously poor performance on ACDC (70.14% DSC) is not discussed -- this may indicate implementation issues or unfavorable settings for that baseline.

## Strengths

- Elegant formalization: three intuitive principles translated into well-defined mathematical objectives with principled gating
- The two-regime geometry loss (boundary ranking + interior suppression) is a particularly clean formulation
- Only method to preserve all three expected uncertainty-ambiguity relationships across all datasets
- Best HD95 on the two cardiac datasets (ACDC, WHS), suggesting genuine boundary quality improvement
- Comprehensive ablation study demonstrating complementarity of all three supervision terms
- Honest reporting of training failure at $d_0=16$, adding credibility
- The qualitative visualizations (Figures 6, 7) with per-region uncertainty values provide convincing evidence

## Limitations

- Dataset-dependent hyperparameter tuning ($\lambda_g$, $\lambda_\sigma$, $\lambda_f$ vary by 10x across datasets) raises concerns about ease of deployment to new datasets
- Corruption supervision uses synthetic Gaussian noise rather than clinically realistic degradations
- The baselines are somewhat narrow; modern alternatives like calibrated EDL methods and PhiSeg are missing
- TTA's anomalously poor ACDC performance (70.14% DSC) is unexplained
- No evaluation of whether improved uncertainty interpretability translates to better downstream clinical decisions
- Limited to 2D (ACDC, ISIC) and patch-based 3D (WHS); applicability to full 3D volumetric segmentation at scale is unclear
- The principles assume clean segmentation masks for boundary distance computation during training, which may be problematic with noisy annotations

## Relevance

本文与医学图像分割中的不确定性量化研究直接相关。PriUS提出的不确定性可解释性原则和UCC/UR评估指标为构建可信赖的医学AI系统提供了新的视角和方法论，特别是将不确定性的空间分布与临床可理解的图像特征对齐的思路，对不确定性估计领域具有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How well do the proposed UCC/UR metrics correlate with clinician-assessed reliability or human uncertainty annotations?
- Can the corruption principle be extended beyond Gaussian noise to clinically realistic artifacts (motion, bias field, partial volume effects)?
- How does PriUS perform under distribution shift (e.g., cross-site, cross-scanner, cross-modality transfer)?
- What is the additional computational cost of the three supervision terms (especially the corruption term requiring three forward passes)?
- Would the principles transfer to other dense prediction tasks (e.g., depth estimation, optical flow) beyond medical segmentation?
- Can data-driven principle discovery (mentioned as future work) uncover additional useful constraints beyond the three hand-crafted ones?
- How does the non-uniform base-rate prior contribute to results compared to the standard uniform prior in EDL?
