DocScope: Benchmarking Verifiable Reasoning for Trustworthy Long-Document Understanding

A 273-document, 1,124-question benchmark that decomposes long-document QA into a four-stage reasoning trajectory — revealing that even the best models produce fully verifiable evidence chains only 29% of the time when their answers are correct.

Feng et al. 2026 Wuhan University · Alibaba · MBZUAI 18+ models evaluated
Key Finding
Answer correctness is a poor proxy for trustworthy reasoning: the best complete evidence-chain rate among correct answers is only 29% (GPT-5.4). Models frequently give the right answer via incomplete or unreliable reasoning paths.
Key Metric
29%
Complete evidence chain among correct answers (GPT-5.4, the best). Gemini 3.1 Pro leads accuracy at 78.9% but chains at only 14%.
Why This Matters
Deploying models that give right answers for wrong reasons is dangerous. Fact extraction — not region grounding — is the primary bottleneck, a finding that redirects where the field should invest effort.
Critical Edge
Questions are LLM-synthesized (Claude Opus 4.6), region grounding relies on GPT-5.5 as judge, and documents exclude formula-heavy domains. The 29% headline uses the strongest region-grounding model — other models fare far worse.

Key Takeaways

  • No single model dominates all four stages
  • Region grounding is the weakest stage across the board (>40 pt drop from Page F1)
  • Dense models consistently outperform MoE counterparts within the same family
  • Evidence spatial dispersion, not position, is the key difficulty factor
  • Domain-specific systems (SimpleDoc 50.5%) trail general MLLMs substantially
  • All judge metrics calibrated against human annotations (kappa up to 0.946)

Benchmark Construction

  • 273 documents (35–100 pages) from FinePDF corpus, English, >80 tokens/page
  • 1,124 questions: 1,046 answerable + 78 unanswerable, 7 question types
  • 13 annotators, 260 person-hours; only 48 answers revised at adjudication (3.7%)
  • Questions LLM-synthesized (Claude Opus 4.6) then filtered by multi-model blind testing

Strengths & Limitations

  • Exemplary judge validation: kappa, MCC, DeLong tests, geometric baseline comparisons
  • Clean oracle study design isolates the fact-extraction bottleneck
  • LLM-synthesized questions may not match real user query distributions
  • Region grounding depends on frontier LLM judges (GPT-5.5), adding cost
  • English-only; excludes formula-heavy and highly visual documents
