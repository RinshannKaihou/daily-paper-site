Practical Scaling Laws: Converting Compute into Performance in a Data-Constrained World

Christopher M. Bryant & Hao Liu 2026-05-09 cs.LG
L(N,D,T) = E + (L₀ − E) · h / (1+h)   h = a/Nα + b/Tβ + cNγ/Dδ
Key Finding
First closed-form scaling law with an explicit overfitting term cNγ/Dδ. The saturation wrapper h/(1+h) bounds loss between irreducible E and uninformed baseline L₀, satisfying 6 limit behaviors that Chinchilla violates 5 of.
Key Metric
16/18 cells won
RMSE 0.007 on Chinchilla grid vs 0.024–0.030 for closest competitors. SOTA on every published LLM grid.
Why This Matters
When data is expensive, you need a law that knows when to stop buying data and start training longer. This is that law — with a cost-aware allocator to prove it.
Critical Edge
Eight free parameters demand full recalibration per training recipe. The overfitting cross-term over-extrapolates from small-D to large-D regimes, losing on MNIST high-D by ~12%.

Loss Decomposition

a / Nα Undercapacity
b / Tβ Undertraining
cNγ/Dδ Overfitting

L₀ is not a free parameter — it is fixed by the loss type (e.g. ln(vocab size) for cross-entropy). The wrapper h/(1+h) guarantees L ∈ [E, L₀]. Reduces to Chinchilla in the data-rich, single-epoch limit.

Validation Scope

  • 4 self-run experiments: MLP/MNIST, ResNet/CIFAR-100, FNO/Darcy PDE, Transformer/TinyStories
  • 5 published LLM grids: Chinchilla, Muennighoff, Gadre, Porian, Farseeer
  • 5 baselines + 4 ablations of own form
  • Total compute: ~206 GPU-hours on A10G

Strengths

  • Each term has a clear physical meaning — no black-box fitting
  • L₀ analytically determined, not a free parameter
  • Backward-compatible: reduces to Chinchilla when appropriate
  • Honest reporting of MNIST high-D weakness

Limitations

  • 8 free params — fits are recipe-dependent, no transfer
  • Cannot capture double descent at N ≈ D
  • Predicts validation loss only; downstream task perf out of scope
  • Untested at frontier scale (100B+ params)
