Emotion amplifies knowledge but does not replace it — faithfully replicating Damasio’s somatic marker hypothesis in an LLM.

The Echo Amplifies the Knowledge: Somatic Marker Analogues in Language Models via Emotion Vector Re-Injection Jared Glover · 2026-05-09
mechanistic-interpretability sparse-autoencoders emotional-memory somatic-markers activation-steering
Key Metric
80vs52%

Decision accuracy: emotion echo + semantic knowledge (BC) vs. knowledge alone (B), p < 0.01. The echo alone achieves only 22% — inert without knowledge.

Why This Matters

Emotion echoes resist adversarial prompting more than semantic labels, and emotional memory offers a new technical path for safer AI agent decision-making.

Critical Edge

~50% of generations at the decision-critical alpha=0.20 are tangential or disrupted — a very high failure rate that the paper acknowledges but does not fully resolve.

Methodology

8 emotion probes → 310 SAE features (L22) → Distinctive-feature echo → Re-inject at L22

Uses Gemma 3 1B-IT with Gemma Scope 2 SAEs (JumpReLU, 16K features/layer) on consumer GPUs (RTX 2060/2070 SUPER, 4.18 GB VRAM total).

Emotion Feature Discovery

Eight emotional texts and eight neutral texts serve as probes. At layer 22, 310 features activate strongly on emotional input (>5) but minimally on neutral (<1). Mean inter-emotion cosine similarity is 0.88 at L22 vs. 0.94 at L13 — layer 22 distinguishes specific emotions. The geometry is psychologically valid: love/betrayal most dissimilar (0.82); hope/joy/awe tightly clustered (0.92–0.93).

Distinctive-Feature Echo Construction

For N conditioning experiences, compute mean feature vector. Select top-K=50 features with largest absolute deviation from mean. Reconstruct echo in residual-stream space using only these distinctive features and the SAE decoder. Inject at every token at L22 with scaling: alpha=0.05 (orientation), alpha=0.20 (decisions).

Two-Vector Architecture

Layer 7 — context matching (when to recall) via BDN-mean-sub (binary-dot-normalized mean subtraction). Achieves 19/20 accuracy. Layer 22 — emotion echo injection (what to recall).

Four-Condition Design

Parallels Damasio's clinical framework: A (no memory / amnesia), B (semantic label only / VMPFC patient), C (echo only / artificial marker), BC (semantic + echo / healthy). Blind evaluation by Gemini 2.5 Pro rating threat (1–10) and warmth (1–10).

Results

Behavioral Differentiation

Different emotion echoes (fear, hope, love, joy) produce distinctly different continuations at temperature 0.01. Fear → hypervigilance; hope → light and peace; love → reaching and touch. The echo changes how the model relates, not what it describes.

Alpha Threshold

Sharp transition at alpha=0.20 for decisions: below 0.15, no effect; at 0.20, good choices jump 15% → 67%. The fourfold amplitude difference between orientation (0.05) and decisions (0.20) is itself a finding: a faint unease colors description, but overriding behavior requires much stronger signal.

The Damasio Dissociation

D(K,E) > D(K) + D(E), where D(E) ≈ 0 for decisions. The echo is primarily a multiplier on knowledge, not an independent channel.

Strengths

  • Elegant four-condition design directly mapping to Damasio's clinical observations enables precise dissociation of emotional vs. semantic contributions.
  • Blind evaluation by Gemini 2.5 Pro (different model, different company) eliminates the most common form of experimenter bias.
  • Psychologically valid geometry — emotion features cluster as human psychology predicts (love/betrayal opposition, positive clustering).
  • The Damasio dissociation (echo changes orientation independently but decisions only with knowledge) is genuinely novel and cannot be explained by prompt contamination.
  • Consumer hardware — 1B model on RTX 2060/2070 SUPER makes the work widely reproducible.
  • Thoughtful ethics — Section 5.5 presents both cases with intellectual honesty, including concrete risks and a principled framework.

Limitations

  • 1B-parameter model only. Whether the dissociation, feature geometry, and alpha thresholds replicate at 7B+ is unknown.
  • ~50% tangential generations at alpha=0.20 — a serious practical deployment limitation.
  • Simplified tasks: 6 memories, 7 scenarios for fear conditioning; binary single-trial IGT (vs. multi-deck, multi-trial original).
  • No code release. Distinctive-feature method, BDN-mean-sub, and injection pipeline cannot be independently verified.
  • Single emotion per echo. Human emotional memory involves complex blends; competing echo interaction is an open question.
  • AI co-authorship. Claude Opus contributed to experimental design, code, analysis, and writing — raising questions about human intellectual contribution.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the Damasio dissociation replicate at 7B, 70B? Does required alpha decrease with model size?
  2. Can the distinctive-feature method be made adaptive (automatic K selection) rather than fixed top-50?
  3. How do competing emotion echoes interact — compose into meaningful blends, or interfere into noise?
  4. Does activation-level bias robustness hold under systematic adversarial evaluation? What attacks could defeat the echo?
  5. Could emotional persistence during RLHF training create functional "trauma" in the model, and how would this be detected?
