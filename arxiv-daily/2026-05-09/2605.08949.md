> **一句话总结：** Muon-OGD将Muon优化器的谱范数(spectral norm)几何与正交梯度投影结合，通过在对偶空间求解谱范数约束优化来保护历史任务方向，在T5-Large (78.9% vs O-LoRA 75.8%)、TRACE (49.4% AA, -4.7% BT)及Coding-Math-Medical跨领域课程上均显著优于现有正交梯度基线。

> **TL;DR:** Muon-OGD identifies a fundamental geometry mismatch between Frobenius-norm-based projection in continual learning and spectral-norm-based Muon optimizer updates. It reformulates the continual learning update as a spectral-norm-constrained optimization with orthogonality constraints to past-task directions (Eq. 6), solved via dual subgradient descent on the nuclear norm objective with Newton-Schulz matrix-sign approximations. Only 1-2 inner dual iterations suffice. On T5-Large standard CL benchmark, it achieves 78.9% AA vs O-LoRA's 75.8%; on TRACE with LLaMA-2-7B-Chat, 49.4% AA with -4.7% BT (best among all methods); on Qwen2.5-3B Coding-Math-Medical curriculum, 43.8% final AA. Evaluated across 7 model architectures (T5-Large, LLaMA2-7B, Qwen2.5-1.5B/3B/7B, LLaMA3.2-1B/3B). Extensive ablations reveal hyperparameter sensitivity, especially dual correction strength having a strict sweet spot.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示了持续学习中基于Frobenius范数的正交投影与现代Muon优化器基于谱范数的更新之间存在根本性几何失配，并提出统一框架。
- 提出谱范数约束优化问题(Eq. 6)：在谱范数球约束下寻找最优下降方向，同时满足与历史任务方向的正交约束，通过对偶问题转化为核范数(nuclear norm)最小化，用Newton-Schulz迭代近似求解。
- 提出低秩约束实例化(Algorithm 2)：利用预训练模型的top-k奇异向量构造约束子空间 U^T * Delta * V = 0，将对偶变量组织为 k x k 矩阵 Lambda，所有操作可通过低秩矩阵乘积高效实现。
- 在7种模型架构、4类基准（标准5任务、15任务长序列、TRACE、Coding-Math-Medical）上进行全面评估，并给出详尽的消融实验（学习率、对偶校正强度、保护秩、正交化精度）。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies and formalizes the geometry mismatch between Frobenius-norm projection in continual learning (Eq. 1/3) and spectral-norm steepest descent in the Muon optimizer (Eq. 2/5), proposing a unified spectral-norm-constrained formulation (Eq. 6).
- Derives a practical algorithm via Lagrangian duality: the primal solution is a matrix-sign update of the corrected gradient, and the dual problem minimizes the nuclear norm of G + sum(lambda_i * C_i). Solved with 1-2 subgradient descent steps with warm-starting from previous iterations.
- Introduces a low-rank constraint instantiation (Algorithm 2) using top-k singular vectors from pretrained models, structuring dual variables as a k x k matrix Lambda with efficient low-rank matrix products (correction term = U * Lambda * V^T).
- Comprehensive evaluation across 7 architectures (T5-Large, LLaMA2-7B, Qwen2.5-1.5B/3B/7B, LLaMA3.2-1B/3B) on 4 benchmark types with extensive ablations covering all key hyperparameters.

## Methodology

**Core Formulation (Eq. 6):** Given momentum matrix G and protected directions {C_i}, the update Delta solves:
```
min <G, Delta>  subject to  ||Delta||_2 <= eta,  <C_i, Delta> = 0 for all i
```
This replaces the Frobenius-norm regularization in classical OGD (Eq. 1) with a spectral-norm ball constraint, aligning with Muon's operator-norm geometry.

**Dual Problem (Eq. 7):** Since the primal has no closed-form solution, the authors derive the dual:
```
min_lambda  ||G + sum(lambda_i * C_i)||_*  (nuclear norm)
```
with subgradients computed via matrix sign: d/d(lambda_i) f = <C_i, msgn(G + sum lambda_i C_i)>.

**Algorithm 1 (General):**
1. Compute momentum G_t from current mini-batch.
2. Inner loop (T_in = 1 or 2 steps): Form corrected matrix H = G + sum(lambda_i * C_i), compute Newton-Schulz approximation S = NS5(H), update dual variables lambda_i via subgradient descent with warm-start from previous step.
3. Primal update: Delta = -eta * NS5(H_t), applied as a Muon-style step.

**Algorithm 2 (Low-Rank Constraints):** A practical instantiation where constraints come from top-k singular vectors (U, V) of pretrained model statistics. The constraint U^T * Delta * V = 0 is equivalent to k^2 individual constraints C_ab = u_a * v_b^T. Dual variables form matrix Lambda in R^{k x k}, and the correction term becomes the structured low-rank product U * Lambda * V^T. Applied to projection layers (q_proj, k_proj, v_proj, o_proj).

**Newton-Schulz Iteration:** The matrix sign function msgn(G) = U * V^T (from SVD G = U * Sigma * V^T) is approximated by 5 composed applications of f(X) = 3/2*X - 1/2*X*X^T*X. In practice, only T=1 iteration often suffices for good performance.

## Results & Highlights

**T5-Large Standard CL Benchmark (5 tasks):**
- Muon-OGD: 78.9% AA (best among CL methods)
- O-LoRA: 75.8%, OSFT: 75.9%, SeqFT: 68.3%

**T5-Large 15-Task Benchmark:**
- Muon-OGD: 72.0% AA
- O-LoRA: 69.6%, OSFT: 71.3%

**TRACE Benchmark (LLaMA-2-7B-Chat):**
- Muon-OGD: 49.4% AA, -4.7% BT (best overall)
- Significantly outperforms all baselines on both retention and forgetting metrics.

**Coding-Math-Medical Domain Curriculum:**
- Qwen2.5-1.5B: Muon-OGD 29.8% final AA (best at every stage)
- Qwen2.5-3B: Muon-OGD 43.8% final AA (best at every stage)
- LLaMA3.2-3B: Muon-OGD retains Math accuracy of 55.1% after Medical stage (peak was 56.0%), showing minimal forgetting vs baselines.
- Qwen2.5-7B: Muon-OGD 49.6% final AA, achieving highest per-domain accuracy across all three domains (Coding 38.1%, Math 75.2%, Medical 35.6%).

**Key Ablation Findings:**
- Dual correction strength has a strict sweet spot: too weak fails to protect old tasks, too strong over-constrains plasticity.
- Protected rank k is sensitive but non-monotonic; for Qwen2.5, k=3 is optimal (prior knowledge concentrated in compact singular subspace).
- Newton-Schulz iterations T=1 is often sufficient; more iterations add O(N^3) overhead with diminishing returns.
- Muon step size and AdamW learning rate must both be sufficiently large; reducing AdamW LR cannot substitute for subspace protection.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The core observation -- that Frobenius-norm projection in CL is geometrically incompatible with spectral-norm Muon updates -- is clean and well-motivated. The dual formulation reducing to nuclear norm minimization is a technically sound contribution. The low-rank instantiation with structured Lambda is practical and elegant.

- **Rigor:** The mathematical derivation from primal (Eq. 6) through Lagrangian to dual (Eq. 7) is rigorous. However, the paper lacks convergence guarantees for the dual iteration scheme. The claim that "1-2 inner iterations suffice" is empirical only, without theoretical backing on approximation quality. No formal forgetting bounds are provided.

- **Reproducibility:** Good. Algorithm boxes are detailed, hyperparameters for each experiment are provided (Appendix C, D), and the final configurations are tabulated. The method has many hyperparameters (muon_k, muon_T, muon_eta, muon_eta_dual, lr), but ablations characterize their sensitivity. Code availability is not mentioned.

- **Significance:** Potentially influential. The paper opens a new direction (spectral-norm-aware CL) and shows consistent gains. The insight that norm choice matters for CL geometry could generalize beyond Muon. However, the improvements over the strongest baseline (OSFT at 71.3% vs Muon-OGD at 72.0% on 15-task) are sometimes modest in absolute terms.

- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - The comparison baselines are strong (O-LoRA, Sculpting Subspaces, OSFT) but the paper does not compare against recent replay-based methods with episodic memory for LLMs, or against methods like EASE or L2P in all settings.
  - The TRACE benchmark uses only one task order (not averaged over permutations), making robustness harder to assess.
  - On Qwen2.5-7B, O-LoRA actually beats Muon-OGD at Stage B by 0.8%, suggesting the advantage is not uniform across all stages.
  - The method operates only on projection layers (q, k, v, o), not all parameters. The justification is reasonable but limits scope.
  - Memory cost of storing U, V matrices per task for long sequences is not analyzed.
  - The paper does not report wall-clock training time comparisons, only claiming "computational scalability" without quantification.

## Strengths

- Clean, principled formulation bridging two active research areas (Muon optimization and projection-based CL).
- Strong mathematical foundation with Lagrangian duality derivation.
- Practical: only 1-2 inner dual iterations needed; warm-starting makes it nearly single-loop.
- Comprehensive evaluation across 7 architectures and 4 benchmark types with detailed ablations.
- Consistent improvements across diverse settings (classification, instruction-following, domain-specific reasoning).
- The low-rank constraint instantiation is efficient and well-motivated.

## Limitations

- Many hyperparameters require tuning (muon_k, muon_T, muon_eta, muon_eta_dual, lr); the ablations show sharp sensitivity to dual correction strength and protected rank.
- No convergence theory for the dual iteration scheme.
- Only applied to projection layers; extension to other parameter types and PEFT (LoRA) is left as future work.
- Memory overhead for storing protected subspaces across many tasks is not quantified.
- Wall-clock time comparison vs baselines is not reported.
- TRACE evaluation uses only one fixed task order.
- The absolute improvement over the strongest baselines is sometimes modest (e.g., 0.7% on 15-task benchmark).

## Relevance

本文将Muon优化器的谱范数几何与持续学习中的正交梯度投影结合，直接解决LLM持续微调中的灾难性遗忘问题。该方法在多种架构和基准上展示了稳定的改进，对关注LLM高效适应、持续学习和优化器设计的研究者具有较高参考价值。谱范数几何的视角也可能启发其他参数高效微调方法的设计。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can Muon-OGD be extended to LoRA-based fine-tuning, where the spectral-norm constraint would need reinterpretation under low-rank parameterization?
- What are the convergence guarantees for the dual subgradient descent scheme, and does the warm-starting strategy introduce any bias?
- How does the memory cost scale with the number of tasks, and is there a natural way to compress or merge protected subspaces from older tasks?
- Would combining Muon-OGD with replay-based methods yield further improvements, especially for long task sequences?
- Is the spectral-norm advantage specific to the Muon optimizer's inductive bias, or would any norm-aware projection show similar benefits?
- How does the method perform on truly large-scale models (70B+) where the O(N^3) Newton-Schulz overhead becomes more significant?
