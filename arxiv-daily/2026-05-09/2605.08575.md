> **一句话总结：** 本文发现现有预训练MoE模型中每个expert内部存在高达90%的激活稀疏性，无需修改模型参数即可利用，通过在vLLM中实现跳过非活跃神经元计算，在MoE层获得最高2.5倍加速，端到端推理加速1.2倍。

> **TL;DR:** The authors discover that existing pretrained MoE models (1B-400B params) contain up to 90% intra-expert activation sparsity that can be exploited without any model modification. By extending vLLM's MoE pipeline to skip inactive neuron computations via custom Triton kernels, they achieve up to 2.5x speedup in MoE layer execution and 1.2x end-to-end inference speedup. Larger models exhibit even higher sparsity, making this optimization increasingly relevant for frontier-scale models.

## 核心贡献

- **发现现成MoE模型中存在大量内专家激活稀疏性**：在8个模型（1B到400B参数）上验证，无需修改模型或激活函数即可获得高达90%的稀疏性，且保持95%的原始准确率。
- **揭示了共享专家和路由专家神经元的重要性差异**：共享专家的神经元对准确率影响远大于路由专家；在大型模型中（如Qwen3.5-122B-A10B），当共享专家全部激活时，仅需2%的路由专家参数即可维持模型能力。
- **在vLLM生产级推理系统中实现了内专家稀疏执行管线**：设计了基于Triton的自定义稀疏激活核函数和融合稀疏上下投影核函数，保持了对CUDA Graph、HIP Graph、kernel fusion和专家并行的兼容。
- **提出了基于批次大小的动态稀疏/稠密切换策略**：在小批量时使用稀疏路径获得低延迟，在大批量时自动切换到高吞吐稠密路径。

## Key Contributions

- **Comprehensive sparsity study across 8 MoE models (1B-400B params)**: Demonstrates that substantial intra-expert activation sparsity is readily available in pretrained MoE models without any retraining or parameter modification, with sparsity cutoffs ranging from 26.5% (Granite-1B-A400M) to 90.8% (Llama-4-Maverick).
- **Production-grade vLLM integration**: Extends vLLM's fused MoE execution pipeline with three new components -- standalone gate projection, custom Triton-based sparse activation kernel with O(n) threshold-based masking, and a fused sparse up-down projection kernel with gather-based execution.
- **Hardware-aware adaptive execution**: Implements dynamic switching between sparse and dense execution paths based on batch size profiling, ensuring speedup across all configurations.
- **Full source code disclosure**: Shares the complete implementation (Appendix B) to enable reproducibility and future research.

## Methodology

The paper follows a two-phase approach:

**Phase 1 -- Sparsity Analysis (Section 3):**
- Select 8 pretrained MoE models spanning 1B to 400B parameters: Granite-1B-A400M, OLMoE-1B-7B, DeepSeek-V2-Lite, GPT-OSS-20B, Qwen3.5-35B-A3B, Qwen3.5-122B-A10B, Qwen3.5-397B-A17B, and Llama-4-Maverick.
- For each model, sort neurons by post-SwiGLU activation magnitude and zero out the lowest-scoring neurons at varying sparsity levels.
- Evaluate on 5 benchmarks (ARC-Challenge, ARC-Easy, HellaSwag, Winogrande, TruthfulQA-mc2) using lm-eval-harness with vLLM backend.
- Define "sparsity cutoff" as the maximum sparsity retaining 95% of baseline average benchmark score.
- Analyze neuron importance across three dimensions: shared expert neurons, neurons across routed experts, and neurons within a single expert.

**Phase 2 -- System Integration (Section 4):**
- Extend vLLM's MoE pipeline by modifying only the routed per-expert execution code.
- Design a custom Triton kernel for O(n) threshold-based sparse masking (vs. O(n log n) top-k).
- Implement a fused sparse up-down projection kernel that gathers active neuron weights into dense shared memory blocks for efficient computation.
- Maintain graph capture compatibility by padding active neuron indices to full size and using fixed 64-neutron tile granularity, allowing Triton programs to skip inactive tiles.
- Profile a tipping batch size during engine startup to dynamically switch between sparse (low batch) and dense (high batch) execution paths.

## Results & Highlights

- **Sparsity scales with model size**: Larger models exhibit higher sparsity cutoffs -- Granite-1B-A400M at 26.5%, OLMoE-1B-7B at 47.3%, DeepSeek-V2-Lite at 49.0%, GPT-OSS-20B at 56.1%, Qwen3.5-35B-A3B at 84.5%, Qwen3.5-122B-A10B at 87.4%, Qwen3.5-397B-A17B at 89.6%, and Llama-4-Maverick at 90.8%.
- **MoE layer speedup**: Up to 2.5x speedup at 85-87% sparsity on RTX4090 (most resource-constrained GPU), 1.5-2.0x on H200 and MI355X. Speedup forms a convex curve across batch sizes, peaking at batch sizes 16-128.
- **End-to-end speedup**: 1.1-1.2x over dense vLLM baseline on Qwen3.5-35B-A3B at 85% target sparsity, limited by Amdahl's law since MoE layers account for ~45% of total execution time.
- **Shared vs. routed expert neurons**: Shared expert neurons have significantly higher per-neuron impact on accuracy. When shared experts are fully activated, only 2% of routed expert neurons suffice for large models (Qwen3.5-122B-A10B and larger).
- **Neuron budgeting across experts shows minimal benefit**: Allocating more neurons to higher-priority experts yields only ~2 percentage points improvement, suggesting uniform allocation is sufficient.
- **Threshold-based masking accuracy**: The O(n) threshold approach achieves target sparsity within +/-3 percentage points and closely reproduces the accuracy observed in the analysis phase.
- **Activation distribution**: Expert FFN activations follow a long-tailed distribution with 54.85% of activations being zero or near-zero, confirming extreme parameter inefficiency.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate-High)**: The key insight -- that intra-expert activation sparsity is "free" in pretrained MoE models -- is genuinely surprising and well-demonstrated. The sparsity analysis across 8 models including frontier-scale models (400B) extends prior work that only covered up to 30B parameters. The vLLM integration is a practical engineering contribution, though the sparse kernel design follows established gather-based patterns.
- **Rigor (High)**: The evaluation is thorough across multiple models, benchmarks, GPUs (MI355X, H200, RTX4090), and batch sizes. The paper honestly discusses the limitation of Amdahl's law reducing end-to-end speedup, and the convex speedup curve is well-explained. The threshold-based masking vs. top-k comparison could have been more detailed.
- **Reproducibility (High)**: Full source code is provided in Appendix B. The evaluation uses publicly available models and standard benchmarks (lm-eval-harness). Hardware configurations are clearly specified.
- **Significance (Moderate-High)**: The 1.2x end-to-end speedup is modest but real, and the finding that sparsity increases with model scale makes this increasingly relevant for frontier models. The approach requires zero model modification, which is a major practical advantage over methods requiring retraining.
- **Red flags / Concerns**:
  - The end-to-end speedup of 1.2x is relatively modest given the 2.5x MoE layer speedup, limited by Amdahl's law. The paper is transparent about this.
  - The gate projection must still be computed densely, limiting theoretical maximum speedup to 3x and reducing realized gains. This is a fundamental architectural constraint.
  - The benchmarks used (ARC, HellaSwag, Winogrande, TruthfulQA) are relatively simple multiple-choice tasks. It is unclear how well the sparsity findings transfer to more complex generation tasks or code/math benchmarks.
  - The paper does not evaluate the impact on output quality for generative tasks -- only benchmark accuracy is reported.
  - The batch-size-dependent switching strategy adds complexity and the tipping point may vary across workloads.

## Strengths

- Zero modification to pretrained models -- directly applicable to existing MoE deployments.
- Comprehensive evaluation across 8 models spanning 3 orders of magnitude in parameter count.
- Production-grade integration in vLLM with full source code, not just a research prototype.
- Honest discussion of limitations (Amdahl's law, dense gate projection overhead, batch size constraints).
- The finding that larger models have higher sparsity is practically important and counteracts the narrative that efficiency gains diminish at scale.
- Clean separation of shared vs. routed expert analysis provides actionable guidance for future MoE architecture design.

## Limitations

- **Dense gate projection bottleneck**: Only up and down projections benefit from sparsity (~2/3 of expert computation), capping theoretical speedup at 3x. Gate projection must remain dense since its output is needed to compute the sparsity mask.
- **Diminishing returns at large batch sizes**: The gather-based sparse execution has lower peak throughput than dense BMM in compute-bound regimes, limiting applicability in high-throughput serving scenarios.
- **Benchmark coverage**: Evaluation uses only multiple-choice benchmarks. No assessment of generation quality, long-context performance, or specialized domains (code, math).
- **Single-model end-to-end evaluation**: End-to-end results are reported only for Qwen3.5-35B-A3B. It would be valuable to see results on the larger models where sparsity is higher.
- **Static threshold**: The sparsity threshold is determined at engine startup and applied uniformly. Per-layer or per-token adaptive thresholds could improve the accuracy-speedup tradeoff but are not explored.
- **Hardware-specific tuning**: The tipping batch size between sparse and dense paths requires profiling, which adds deployment complexity.

## Relevance

本文对MoE模型推理效率的研究具有直接参考价值。激活稀疏性作为一种无需重新训练即可利用的优化手段，可直接应用于现有MoE模型的部署。特别是稀疏性随模型规模增大而增强的发现，对大规模MoE模型的实际部署有重要指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can a lightweight gate-activation prediction mechanism approximate the sparsity mask before computing the full gate projection, enabling sparse gate projection and pushing speedup beyond 3x?
2. How does intra-expert activation sparsity interact with quantization techniques (e.g., FP8, INT4) that are commonly applied in production MoE serving?
3. Does the sparsity-accuracy tradeoff hold for complex generative tasks (code generation, mathematical reasoning, long-form text) or only for the simple multiple-choice benchmarks evaluated here?
4. Could grouped intra-expert activation sparsity (block-level rather than individual neuron-level) improve GPU hardware utilization and narrow the gap between theoretical sparsity and realized speedup?
5. Is there a way to reuse sparsity patterns across tokens in the same sequence or across similar sequences, similar to sparse KV cache techniques, to further reduce the gate projection overhead?
