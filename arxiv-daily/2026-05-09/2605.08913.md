> **一句话总结：** 本文系统刻画了 Apple MPS 后端在自回归 Transformer 解码中的非单调延迟异常——在特定 decoding budget 区间内延迟尖峰高达 21×（如 GPT-2 Large 在 384 tokens 处达 103.4s，而 256 与 512 tokens 分别仅 4.9s 与 9.3s），异常源于 decode 阶段而非 prefill，KV 缓存的加速比在病态配置处从 4.9–20.3× 崩塌至 1.9×，且 CPU/CUDA 后端在相同条件下保持严格单调。

> **TL;DR:** The author identifies non-monotonic latency behavior in Apple's MPS backend during autoregressive transformer decoding: latency spikes of up to 21× occur within narrow decoding-budget intervals (e.g., GPT-2 Large on MPS: 4.91s at 256 tokens → 103.41s at 384 tokens → 9.27s at 512 tokens), followed by recovery at neighboring configurations. Across GPT-2, BLOOM-560M, and OPT-350M, the anomaly originates in the decode phase (prefill is 0.1% of total latency at the 512-token spike), is absent on CPU and NVIDIA CUDA T4 baselines under identical conditions, and is not explained by memory pressure alone. KV-cache speedup over cache-disabled decoding collapses from 4.9–20.3× at normal configurations to 1.9× at the anomalous 512-token configuration on GPT-2 Medium/MPS, while cache-disabled decoding still exhibits residual non-monotonicity.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统识别 Apple MPS 后端自回归解码中的非单调延迟行为：在特定 decoding budget 区间出现高达 21× 的延迟尖峰，相邻配置处即恢复
- 通过跨后端受控实验证明异常主要出现在 decode 阶段而非 prefill（512 tokens 处 prefill 仅占 0.1%），且在 CPU 与 NVIDIA CUDA T4 后端相同条件下不出现
- 揭示 KV 缓存与病态执行区间的强交互：KV 缓存的加速比在病态配置处从 4.9–20.3× 崩塌至 1.9×，而禁用缓存时仍残留非单调性
- 排除内存压力作为唯一解释：GPU 分配内存近似线性增长，系统统一内存在延迟恢复后仍维持高位
- 跨模型家族（GPT-2、BLOOM-560M、OPT-350M）与跨设备（M3 Max、M3 Pro）验证，证明为后端级现象而非模型/设备特异

## Key Contributions

- First systematic identification of non-monotonic latency behavior in Apple's MPS backend during autoregressive transformer decoding, with spikes up to 21× at model-dependent decoding budgets, while CPU and CUDA T4 baselines remain strictly monotonic under identical conditions
- Controlled experiments across GPT-2, BLOOM-560M, and OPT-350M showing anomalies originate primarily in the decode phase rather than prefill (prefill is 0.1% of total latency at the 512-token spike)
- Discovery of strong interaction between KV cache and pathological execution regimes: KV-cache speedup over cache-disabled decoding collapses from 4.9–20.3× at normal configurations to 1.9× at the anomalous 512-token configuration, while cache-disabled decoding still exhibits residual non-monotonic behavior
- Elimination of memory pressure as the sole explanation: peak MPS GPU memory scales approximately linearly (1426 MB at 128 tokens → 1550 MB at 768 tokens) with no sharp transition at the anomalous boundary, and elevated system memory persists after latency recovery
- Cross-device (M3 Max, M3 Pro) and cross-PyTorch-version (2.7.0, 2.8.0, 2.11.0) reproduction, supporting a backend-level rather than model- or release-specific origin

## Methodology

**Hardware and software (Sec. 3.1).** Primary device: MacBook Pro with Apple M3 Max (14-core CPU, 30-core GPU, 36 GB unified memory), macOS 14.8.1 (Sonoma). Stack: Python 3.9.6, PyTorch 2.8.0, HuggingFace Transformers 4.57.6, LitServe 0.2.16 (production-grade serving framework). Cross-device reproduction on Apple M3 Pro (11-core CPU, 8-core GPU, 18 GB unified memory). Cross-version check across PyTorch 2.7.0, 2.8.0, and 2.11.0. Three backends compared under identical configs: CPU, Apple MPS (Metal Performance Shaders), and NVIDIA CUDA (T4).

**Models (Sec. 3.1).** GPT-2 family (Radford et al., 2019): DistilGPT-2, GPT-2, GPT-2 Medium, GPT-2 Large. Cross-model generalization: BLOOM-560M (Scao et al., 2022) and OPT-350M (Zhang et al., 2022).

**Benchmark design (Sec. 3.2).** All experiments served through LitServe so request-handling, worker-scheduling, and serialization overheads are present. Each experiment: isolated server process, 3 warmup runs (discarded) + 5 measured runs (averaged). Deterministic decoding with fixed seed. Decoding budgets (max_tokens) range 128–768. EOS-based early stopping disabled — realized output length always equals configured decoding budget, isolating backend execution dynamics from model-dependent stopping. Token-level timing for selected experiments (per-step instrumentation inserts a synchronization barrier after each decode step; absolute values from instrumented and uninstrumented runs are not directly comparable, but KV-cache on/off comparisons use identical instrumentation).

**Ablations.** (i) Instability probe (Sec. 4.3): focused sweep over 480–656 tokens at 16-token spacing for GPT-2 Medium/MPS. (ii) KV cache ablation (Sec. 4.4): GPT-2 Medium/MPS, cache on vs. off, 128–768 tokens. (iii) Memory pressure analysis (Sec. 4.5): peak MPS GPU memory and peak system unified memory across decoding budgets. (iv) Cross-model (Sec. 4.6): BLOOM-560M and OPT-350M under MPS. (v) Prompt-length ablation (Sec. 4.7): short prompt (3 tokens) vs. long prompt (~65 tokens) at fixed max_tokens, to separate decoding-budget from total-sequence-length effects.

## Results & Highlights

1. **Latency collapse on MPS at specific decoding budgets (Table 1, GPT-2 Medium/MPS).** Strictly non-monotonic: 384 tokens → 5.12±0.06 s (75.27 tok/s); 512 tokens → 84.54±12.18 s (6.20 tok/s); 640 tokens → 8.67±0.05 s (73.88 tok/s). For GPT-2 Large/MPS: 256 tokens → 4.91±0.02 s; 384 tokens → 103.41±4.99 s (the anomalous peak, 3.70 tok/s); 512 tokens → 9.27±0.05 s. The author states the slowdown reaches up to 21× relative to adjacent non-anomalous configurations.

2. **Average latency across backends (Table 2, avg over all tested decoding budgets).** DistilGPT-2: CPU 3.018 s, CUDA 2.374 s, MPS 2.890 s. GPT-2: CPU 5.208 s, CUDA 4.205 s, MPS 3.914 s. GPT-2 Medium: CPU 14.899 s, CUDA 7.862 s, MPS 18.713 s (MPS slower than CPU). GPT-2 Large: CPU 26.966 s, CUDA 11.555 s, MPS 25.001 s. CUDA consistently outperforms CPU; MPS is competitive for smaller models but becomes comparable to or slower than CPU for GPT-2 Medium/Large — averaging obscures the discrete pathological regimes.

3. **Instability probe (Sec. 4.3, GPT-2 Medium/MPS, 16-token spacing).** Latency stays normal at 480–496 tokens (9.2–9.5 s), jumps abruptly at 512 tokens (89.2 s), persists through 624 tokens (83.2–110.1 s), then collapses back to normal at 640–656 tokens (8.8 s and 8.2 s). Standard deviation within the degraded regime is 8–14 s at 512 tokens (stable but pathological, not transient noise). Transition occurs within a single 16-token step on both sides. Per-step timing: at 512 tokens prefill is 0.11 s (0.1% of total) vs. 0.08 s (0.8%) at 496 tokens; the 496→512 latency delta (9.5 s → 89.2 s) is almost entirely in the decode phase (9.4 s → 89.1 s).

4. **KV cache ablation (Table 3, GPT-2 Medium/MPS).** Cache ON vs. OFF and ratio (cache-off/cache-on): 128 tokens → 1.38 s vs. 6.76 s (4.9×); 256 → 2.90 vs. 20.63 (7.1×); 384 → 5.11 vs. 42.67 (8.4×); **512 → 83.21 vs. 161.60 (1.9× — speedup collapse)**; 640 → 8.79 vs. 139.00 (15.8×); 768 → 9.69 vs. 196.64 (20.3×). Disabling KV cache does not eliminate the anomalous regime (161.6 s at 512 tokens); the practical speedup advantage of KV caching is substantially reduced within the anomalous regime.

5. **Memory pressure (Table 4, GPT-2 Medium/M3 Max).** Peak MPS GPU memory scales approximately linearly: 1426 MB (128 tokens) → 1451 → 1476 → 1500 (512) → 1525 → 1550 MB (768) — no sharp transition at the anomalous 512-token boundary. Peak system unified memory: 13,166 MB (128) → 16,809 → 22,111 → 32,881 (512) → 33,261 (640) → 31,227 MB (768) — rises substantially near the anomalous regime but remains elevated at 640 tokens where latency has already returned to normal, so memory growth alone does not explain the abrupt onset and recovery.

6. **Cross-model generalization (Table 5, MPS).** BLOOM-560M: 128→2.90 s, 256→7.53, 384→15.17, **512→80.76±5.04**, 640→134.58±6.13, 768→40.80 — same non-monotonic structure. OPT-350M: 128→1.71, 256→3.74, 384→7.40, **512→95.13±2.03**, 640→15.71, 768→19.35 — more than an order-of-magnitude latency increase between 384 and 512 tokens. The regime structure is consistent across architectures, supporting a backend-level origin.

7. **Prompt-length ablation (Table 6, GPT-2 Medium/MPS).** At max_tokens=512, the long prompt (~65 tokens, total ≈577) enters the anomalous regime (83.9 s) while the short prompt (3 tokens, total ≈515) remains normal (7.3 s) — consistent with an influence of total sequence length on onset. However, return to lower latency occurs at the same max_tokens configuration (640) for both prompt conditions despite differing total lengths, indicating entry and recovery do not follow the same dependency structure.

## Critical Assessment

- The paper is a characterization/profiling study that identifies the phenomenon but does not diagnose the root cause at the MPS kernel or hardware level. The author explicitly states (Sec. 5.2) that "the MPS backend does not expose kernel-level instrumentation through the PyTorch API, limiting visibility into low-level execution behavior from user space" and leaves "deeper investigation of the underlying mechanism and potential mitigation strategies to future work." The "discrete execution regimes" hypothesis (scheduling or kernel selection within the MPS backend) is plausible but not directly verified — it is inferred from user-level observations only. This is a paper-specific weakness: the central explanatory claim is unsupported by internal profiling.
- No mitigations or workarounds are proposed for practitioners encountering these latency spikes; the implications section (Sec. 5.1) only warns that coarse benchmarking may miss the effects and that KV cache benefit degrades in the anomalous regime.
- The generalizability claim is qualified but not exhaustively tested: the author notes reproduction on M1/M2 and broader decoding configurations would "further strengthen generalizability, which we leave to future work" (Sec. 5.2). Only M3 Max and M3 Pro are tested.
- The instability probe (Sec. 4.3) uses 16-token spacing, which may miss finer-grained structure; the author does not test whether the regime boundary is stable at finer resolution or across reruns with different seeds (deterministic decoding is used, so seed sensitivity is not assessed).

## Strengths

- Surprising and practically important empirical finding with concrete, reproducible numbers (specific decoding budgets, specific latency values, specific speedup ratios).
- Rigorous cross-backend comparison (CPU, MPS, CUDA T4 under identical configs) that rules out model-architecture and configuration confounds.
- Multiple model families (GPT-2, BLOOM, OPT) and multiple Apple Silicon devices (M3 Max, M3 Pro) strengthen the backend-level-origin claim.
- Clean isolation of decode vs. prefill contribution via per-step instrumentation, quantitatively showing prefill is 0.1% of total latency at the spike.
- The memory-pressure analysis (Table 4) is a well-designed control: GPU memory scales linearly with no transition, and the system-memory/latency mismatch directly argues against simple capacity exhaustion.
- The prompt-length ablation (Table 6) cleverly separates decoding-budget from total-sequence-length effects, revealing an asymmetry between entry and recovery that a single-factor analysis would miss.
- Honest about limitations (single-device primary characterization, no kernel-level visibility, no mitigations).

## Limitations

- Root cause at the MPS implementation or hardware level is not diagnosed — the paper identifies and characterizes the problem but does not explain it mechanistically.
- No proposed mitigations or workarounds for practitioners; no reporting to Apple or vendor-side follow-up is mentioned.
- Single-author work; the depth of low-level analysis is constrained by what the PyTorch MPS API exposes.
- Only M3 Max and M3 Pro tested; M1/M2 and future Apple Silicon generations untested.
- The "discrete execution regimes" hypothesis is inferred from user-level latency and memory observations, not validated with internal MPS profiling tools (which are not exposed).
- Deterministic decoding with a fixed seed means seed/numerical sensitivity of the regime boundary is not assessed.
- The 16-token spacing in the instability probe may be too coarse to characterize the transition precisely.

## Relevance

对应配置兴趣区 **"Computation Reliability & Precision Errors"**（核心：揭示特定硬件后端在自回归解码中出现非单调、高达 21× 的延迟异常，属于计算执行层面的可靠性缺陷，影响推理时延的可预测性），同时与 **"LLM Inference Evaluation"** 相关（指出粗粒度基准测试会掩盖病态执行区间，强调硬件感知的细粒度推理评估方法论），并间接关联 **"Silent Data Corruption (SDC)"**（输出在 cache on/off 下保持一致，但执行行为异常——属于性能层面的非显性故障模式）。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What specific MPS kernel-selection, memory-allocation, or scheduling decisions trigger the transitions into and out of the high-latency regime? Can Metal Performance Shaders profiling tools (outside PyTorch) reveal the internal state change?
- Do these anomalies persist across Apple Silicon generations (M1, M2, M4), and does the anomaly boundary shift predictably with unified-memory capacity or GPU core count?
- Is there a practical decoding-budget threshold or padding strategy practitioners can use to avoid anomalous configurations on MPS?
- Does the phenomenon appear in other Metal-based inference frameworks (MLX, llama.cpp Metal backend, MLC-LLM) or is it specific to PyTorch MPS?
- Could adaptive scheduling that detects regime onset (e.g., from per-step timing) dynamically switch to a cache-disabled or padded-decoding fallback to avoid the worst spikes?
- The prompt-length ablation shows entry depends on total sequence length but recovery depends on max_tokens — what underlying mechanism (e.g., a buffer-size threshold that triggers a different kernel path) would produce this asymmetry?
- Is the anomaly numerically benign (identical outputs under deterministic decoding, as observed) or could it interact with non-deterministic kernels under stochastic decoding to produce output divergence?
