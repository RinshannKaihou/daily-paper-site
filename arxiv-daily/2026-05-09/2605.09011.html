Key Finding

A Geometric Perspective on Next-Token Prediction in Large Language Models: Three Emerging Phases

Decoder-only LLMs exhibit a universal three-phase geometric structure in their forward pass -- Seeding Multiplexing, Hoisting Overriding, and Focal Convergence -- consistent across 8 models, 2 architectures, and 1B–32B scales.

Gianfranco Lombardo, Giuseppe Trimigno, Stefano Cagnoni University of Parma arXiv 2605.09011
Key Metric
R² = 0.993cross-family
Phase 2 (disambiguation) width scales linearly with model depth. Phases 1 and 3 barely grow.
Why This Matters
Deeper models spend their extra capacity on candidate disambiguation, not seeding or convergence -- explaining what depth actually buys in LLMs.
Critical Edge
Evidence is correlational only. No causal interventions verify that geometric phases map to mechanistically distinct circuits. Just two model families tested.

Methodology

  1. Geometric probing via representation lenses. Train TunedLens-style affine correction maps per layer, decompose the correction matrix via SVD, extract top-k right singular vectors as the Predictive Readout Subspace on the Grassmann manifold Gr(k,d).
  2. Grassmann trajectory analysis. Compute Readout Subspace Similarity (RSS) between consecutive layers as mean cosine of principal angles. Validate the profile as unimodal (R² ≥ 0.9 against Beta, Kumaraswamy, and Generalized Normal families).
  3. Phase detection. Two-stage Kneedle breakpoint detection on Gaussian-smoothed RSS, with consensus across k ∈ {5%, 10%, 15%}. Functional characterization via effective rank, update orthogonality, Hit@k, and FFN/MHA norms on held-out data.
Models: Qwen2.5 {1.5B, 7B, 14B, 32B} and OLMo2 {1B, 7B, 13B, 32B}.
Training: SlimPajama (226M tokens); validation on 16M held-out tokens.
Resolution: k from 1%–50% of hidden dim; 5%–15% used for consensus (Pareto-optimized).

Results

  • Phase proportions are consistent: P1 19.8% ± 6.9%, P2 59.0% ± 12.4%, P3 21.2% ± 8.6% of layers.
  • Representation lens vs. LogitLens: Lens recovers the final token from layer 1 (Hit@1 ~0.28–0.33); LogitLens is nearly blind (Hit@1 ~0.01). Predictive info is present but hidden in superposition.
  • Phase boundaries scale linearly: P3 onset R² = 1.00 (Qwen), 0.99 (OLMo). P2 width R² = 0.99+ for both families.
  • Effective rank expands in P1, stabilizes in P2, concentrates in P3. Update norms grow up to 10x in P3.
  • Updates are approximately orthogonal to the residual stream throughout; P2 is most orthogonal, P3 least.
  • FFN/MHA ratio: Qwen2.5 is FFN-dominated in P1 (2.4–3.2x); both families converge by P3.

Strengths

  • Creative repurposing of representation lenses from decoding tools to geometric probes
  • Rigorous validation: unimodality tested against flexible alternatives, breakpoints robust across k values and models, Pareto analysis of resolution parameter
  • Clean, consistent results across two architecturally distinct families and four model scales
  • Scaling analysis of phase boundaries is compelling (linear R² = 0.99+ for Phase 2 width)
  • Clear functional interpretation of each phase with multi-metric support
  • Well-organized paper with excellent figures and appendices

Limitations

  • Only two model families and dense attention architectures; no MoE, state-space, or non-residual models
  • Correlational evidence only; no causal interventions to verify functional interpretations
  • Single training corpus (SlimPajama); no validation on domain-specific distributions
  • Analysis depends on learned affine maps, which are imperfect proxies for predictive geometry
  • Phase boundaries may vary by 1–3 layers across k values
  • No exploration of practical applications (model editing, pruning, efficiency)

Open Questions

  • Would causal interventions (activation patching, causal mediation) confirm that geometric phases correspond to mechanistically distinct circuits?
  • How do the three phases manifest in MoE models, where different experts may serve different roles across depth?
  • Can phase boundaries inform layer-wise pruning -- are Phase 2 layers more compressible than Phase 3?
  • Does the structure hold for instruction-tuned or RLHF-aligned models, or does alignment shift the boundaries?
  • Could the RSS profile serve as a diagnostic for anomalous layer behavior or training instabilities?
  • How does the phase structure change during training -- do phases emerge gradually or appear at a specific stage?
