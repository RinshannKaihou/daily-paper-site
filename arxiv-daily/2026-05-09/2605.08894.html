Fitting Is Not Enough: Smoothness in Extremely Quantized LLMs

Extreme quantization causes a systematic smoothness degradation in LLMs that is orthogonal to numerical precision loss — fitting weights correctly is necessary but insufficient for preserving generation quality.

Key Metric

Sparser
Decoding Tree

Quantized models' effective token candidates shrink rapidly in the prediction neighborhood. The model over-commits to a small set of high-probability tokens, losing nuanced probability calibration.

Why This Matters

Smoothness preservation yields gains on top of numerical accuracy optimization in both PTQ and QAT pipelines. This opens a new optimization dimension for deploying LLMs under 1–2 bit extreme compression — where naïve fitting alone leaves measurable quality on the table.

Critical Edge

  • Absolute gains from smoothness preservation are modest in some configurations — the paper could be clearer about improvement magnitude relative to baselines.
  • Evaluation is limited to LLaMA-family models; generalization to MoE, RWKV, and other architectures is unexplored.
  • The “Fitting Is Not Enough” framing slightly overclaims — smoothness is an additional factor, but the gap is not always dramatic.

Methodology

Smoothness Diagnosis

A “smoothness proxy” measures distributional smoothness of output logits. As bit-width decreases (FP16 → 4b → 2b → 1b), smoothness degrades systematically even when numerical accuracy is controlled for.

Neighborhood Modeling

Analysis of token-prediction neighborhoods shows quantized models consider fewer viable candidates per step. This compresses the prediction space into a sparser, more dangerously deterministic decoding tree.

Preservation Principle

A smoothness-aware calibration loss (PTQ) or auxiliary training objective (QAT) penalizes excessive distributional sharpening — complementary to, not replacing, existing numerical optimization.

Results & Evidence

Perplexity Reduction

Adding smoothness preservation on top of PTQ methods (GPTQ, AWQ-style) yields additional perplexity reductions and downstream task improvements — gains orthogonal to numerical fitting.

QAT Improvements

In BitNet-style QAT settings, smoothness preservation improves generation quality metrics beyond what weight-fitting alone achieves.

Scale of Effect

Smoothness degradation is most pronounced at 1-bit and 2-bit quantization and empirically demonstrated across multiple LLaMA model sizes.

Mechanistic Explanation

Quantized models “over-commit” to a small set of high-probability tokens, losing the nuanced probability calibration that enables high-quality generation.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Identifies a real, previously underappreciated problem with a clear mechanistic explanation
  • Sequence neighborhood modeling framework is intuitive and actionable for practitioners
  • Applicable to both PTQ and QAT — covers the two dominant quantization paradigms
  • Open-source code; clear, accessible writing

Limitations

  • Smoothness proxy lacks formal theoretical grounding — primarily empirical
  • No comparison against all recent extreme quantization baselines (SqueezeLLM, QuIP#)
  • Hyperparameter sensitivity of the smoothness-preserving method not extensively analyzed
  • Absolute improvement margins are relatively small in some experiments

Open Questions

Compression Interactions

How does smoothness degradation interact with pruning, knowledge distillation, and speculative decoding?

Mixed-Precision

Can smoothness preservation be incorporated into mixed-precision schemes where layers have different bit-widths?

Theoretical Bound

Is there a theoretical lower bound on smoothness degradation that any quantization method must incur at a given bit-width?

MoE Models

How does smoothness degradation manifest in Mixture-of-Experts models, where routing already introduces discrete selection?
