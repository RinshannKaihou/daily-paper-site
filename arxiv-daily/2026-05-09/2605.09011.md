> **一句话总结：** 本文将表示透镜（representation lens）的校正矩阵通过SVD分解，在Grassmann流形上追踪预测性子空间随深度的轨迹，在Qwen2.5和OLMo2（1B-32B）八个模型中发现了一致的三阶段几何结构：候选种子叠加（Seeding Multiplexing）、提升覆盖（Hoisting Overriding）和焦点收敛（Focal Convergence），并发现模型越深，额外容量主要被用于候选消歧而非初始播种或最终收敛。

> **TL;DR:** This paper repurposes representation lenses as geometric diagnostic tools: by decomposing the per-layer affine correction matrix via SVD and tracking the resulting k-dimensional predictive readout subspace on the Grassmann manifold across layers, the authors identify a universal three-phase structure in the forward pass of decoder-only LLMs (Qwen2.5 and OLMo2, 1B-32B). Phase 1 (Seeding Multiplexing, ~20% of layers) seeds multiple candidates in superposition with rising effective rank. Phase 2 (Hoisting Overriding, ~59% of layers) concentrates the distribution without expanding rank -- the dominant phase that scales linearly with model depth. Phase 3 (Focal Convergence, ~21% of layers) writes the winning token via high-energy, low-rank updates aligned with the unembedding direction. The key finding is that deeper models allocate their additional capacity primarily to candidate disambiguation (Phase 2), not to initial seeding or final convergence. Updates are approximately orthogonal to the residual stream throughout; phases differ in how they affect effective rank (expand, stabilize, concentrate).

## 核心贡献

- **预测读出子空间（Predictive Readout Subspace）框架：** 对每层的表示透镜校正矩阵进行SVD分解，取前k个右奇异向量张成的子空间作为该层预测信息的几何载体，在Grassmann流形 Gr(k,d) 上定义逐层轨迹。
- **三阶段几何结构的一致性发现：** 在8个模型、2个架构家族（Qwen2.5和OLMo2）、1B到32B参数规模中均观察到一致的上升-平台-下降RSS轮廓，通过单峰分布拟合和Kneedle断点检测划分为三个阶段。
- **关键洞察——深层模型容量的用途：** Phase 2（候选消歧）的宽度随模型深度线性增长（R^2 > 0.99），而Phase 1和3几乎不变，表明深层模型的额外容量主要用于消歧而非播种或收敛。
- **分辨率参数k的Pareto分析：** 引入Visibility@k与能量保留E(k)的Pareto前沿，表明5%-15%的截断比例在几何可分辨性和信息保真度之间取得最佳平衡。
- **与平均场理论的对应：** 发现本文的三阶段结构与Bruno et al. [2026]在encoder-only模型中发现的三阶段动力学结构存在对应关系，暗示这是Transformer架构的通用结构性质。

## Key Contributions

- **Predictive Readout Subspace framework:** Defines, at each layer, the dominant k-dimensional singular subspace of the representation lens correction matrix as the geometric locus of predictive information, and tracks its trajectory on the Grassmann manifold Gr(k,d) via a layer-wise Readout Subspace Similarity (RSS) profile.
- **Universal three-phase decomposition:** Across 8 models from 2 families (Qwen2.5, OLMo2) at scales 1B-32B, identifies three consistent geometric phases: Seeding Multiplexing (~20% of layers), Hoisting Overriding (~59%), and Focal Convergence (~21%), with phase boundaries robust across subspace resolution k.
- **Scaling insight -- capacity goes to disambiguation:** Phase 2 width scales linearly with model depth (cross-family R^2 = 0.993), while Phases 1 and 3 grow slowly or not at all, demonstrating that additional depth is primarily used for candidate disambiguation, not initial seeding or final convergence.
- **Pareto analysis of resolution parameter k:** Introduces Visibility@k vs. energy retention E(k) trade-off, showing k in {5%, 10%, 15%} balances geometric discriminability and spectral fidelity, with unimodal structure emerging at all scales.
- **Functional characterization per phase:** Each phase has distinct geometric signatures -- Phase 1: effective rank expands, candidates in superposition; Phase 2: rank stabilizes, maximally orthogonal updates, progressive concentration; Phase 3: rank concentrates, update norms grow up to 10x, final token becomes LogitLens-readable.

## Methodology

The paper's methodology proceeds in three stages:

1. **Geometric probing via representation lenses.** The authors train TunedLens-style affine correction maps (A_l, b_l) on SlimPajama (226M tokens) for each of 8 models. Rather than using these maps to decode predictions (the standard use), they decompose the correction matrix A_l via SVD and extract the top-k right singular vectors as the Predictive Readout Subspace S_l^(k). This subspace is treated as a point on the Grassmann manifold Gr(k,d).

2. **Grassmann trajectory analysis.** The Readout Subspace Similarity (RSS) between consecutive layers is computed as the mean cosine of principal angles between their subspaces. This yields a layer-wise similarity profile {RSS_l} that reveals how the geometry of predictive information evolves through depth. The authors validate that this profile is well-described by unimodal distributions (fitted with Beta, Generalized Normal, Asymmetric Generalized Normal, and Kumaraswamy families, achieving R^2 >= 0.9 in most cases).

3. **Phase detection and characterization.** Two breakpoints are identified via a two-stage Kneedle criterion applied to each half of the Gaussian-smoothed (sigma=2) RSS profile. Consensus breakpoints are obtained by aggregating over k in {5%, 10%, 15%}. Functional characterization uses four metrics on held-out data (16M tokens): effective rank of hidden states, mean |cos(delta_l, h_{l-1})|, lens Hit@k, and FFN/MHA norm ratio.

Key technical choices:
- Resolution parameter k ranges from 1% to 50% of hidden dimensionality d, with intermediate values (5%-15%) used for consensus.
- Validation on held-out SlimPajama (6 domains) to avoid artifacts from lens training data.
- Eight models: Qwen2.5 {1.5B, 7B, 14B, 32B} and OLMo2 {1B, 7B, 13B, 32B}.

## Results & Highlights

- **Phase proportions are consistent across architectures:** Phase 1: 19.8% +/- 6.9%, Phase 2: 59.0% +/- 12.4%, Phase 3: 21.2% +/- 8.6% of layers.
- **Representation lens vs. LogitLens gap:** The representation lens recovers the final token from layer 1 (Hit@1 ~ 0.28-0.33 over ~100k vocabulary), while the LogitLens remains nearly blind (Hit@1 ~ 0.01), confirming predictive information is present but hidden in superposition.
- **Scaling of phase boundaries:** Phase 3 onset (b_2) scales linearly with L (R^2 = 1.00 for Qwen, 0.99 for OLMo). Phase 2 width also scales linearly (R^2 = 1.00 for Qwen, 0.99 for OLMo). Phase 1 boundary (b_1) grows slowly (slope 0.11 for Qwen, 0.26 for OLMo).
- **Updates are approximately orthogonal throughout:** |cos(delta_l, h_{l-1})| is low across all phases but lowest in Phase 2 (7/8 models) and highest in Phase 3 (8/8 models), anti-correlated with RSS.
- **Effective rank dynamics:** Increases through Phase 1, peaks near b_1, stabilizes in Phase 2. Update rank peaks in Phase 2 and declines in Phase 3 while update norms grow sharply (up to 10x).
- **FFN/MHA ratio:** Qwen2.5 is FFN-dominated in Phase 1 (ratio 2.4-3.2) while OLMo2 is more balanced (parallel architecture); both converge at b_1. FFN dominates in Phase 3 for both families.
- **Hit@5/Hit@1 ratio:** Decreases smoothly from ~2.0 at layer 1 to ~1.2-1.5 in Phase 3, reflecting continuous disambiguation across all phases.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The core idea of repurposing representation lenses as geometric probes (rather than decoding tools) is genuinely novel. The Grassmann manifold trajectory analysis and the three-phase decomposition are creative contributions that go beyond existing mechanistic interpretability work. The connection to mean-field theory (Bruno et al. 2025) is an interesting cross-pollination.

**Rigor:** The methodology is thorough. The unimodality assumption is explicitly validated against flexible alternatives (Beta, Kumaraswamy) and confirmed. Breakpoint robustness is tested across 8 resolution levels and 8 models. The Pareto analysis of the resolution parameter k is well-motivated. The use of held-out validation data to avoid artifacts is commendable.

**Weaknesses and concerns:**
- **Correlational, not causal:** The authors themselves acknowledge that phase interpretations are based on correlational evidence without causal interventions (activation patching, causal mediation). The three phases are defined by geometric similarity of the *lens* correction matrices, not directly by model computation. Whether these lens-identified phases correspond to mechanistically distinct circuit behaviors remains unverified.
- **Two model families only:** Only Qwen2.5 and OLMo2 are studied. The claim of universality would be much stronger with validation on Llama, Mistral, Gemma, or other architectures (especially MoE models). The authors acknowledge this limitation.
- **Single training corpus:** All experiments use SlimPajama. Domain-specific or task-specific behavior might differ.
- **The lens as a proxy:** The entire framework hinges on the quality of the learned affine maps. While the authors show training/validation gap is small, the lens itself is an imperfect proxy for "predictive information." Low-rank sufficiency for lens performance does not necessarily mean predictive information itself is low-rank in the residual stream -- it means the *lens correction* is low-rank.
- **Missing ablations:** No ablation on the choice of unimodal fitting, Gaussian smoothing parameter (sigma=2), or the Kneedle detection method. How sensitive are phase boundaries to these choices?
- **No downstream implications:** The paper identifies phases but does not explore whether this knowledge can be leveraged for model editing, pruning, or improved interpretability tools.

**Significance:** Despite the limitations, this is a strong mechanistic interpretability paper that provides a new geometric lens (pun intended) on how LLMs process information. The finding that additional depth primarily serves candidate disambiguation is intuitively satisfying and practically relevant for understanding scaling behavior. The framework is generalizable and could inspire causal follow-up work.

**Red flags:** None major. The paper is transparent about limitations and does not overclaim causal mechanisms.

## Strengths

- Creative repurposing of representation lenses from decoding tools to geometric probes.
- Rigorous validation: unimodality tested against flexible alternatives, breakpoint robustness across k values and models, Pareto analysis of resolution parameter.
- Clean and consistent results across two architecturally distinct families and four model scales.
- The scaling analysis of phase boundaries is particularly compelling (linear R^2 = 0.99+ for Phase 2 width).
- Clear functional interpretation of each phase with supporting evidence from multiple metrics.
- Well-organized paper with excellent figures and appendices.

## Limitations

- Only two model families (Qwen2.5, OLMo2) and dense attention architectures; no MoE, state-space, or non-residual models.
- Correlational evidence only; no causal interventions to verify functional interpretations.
- Single training corpus (SlimPajama); no validation on domain-specific or task-specific distributions.
- The analysis depends on the quality of the learned affine maps, which are imperfect proxies for predictive geometry.
- Phase boundaries may vary by 1-3 layers across k values, introducing some ambiguity.
- No exploration of practical applications (model editing, pruning, efficiency).

## Relevance

这篇论文从几何视角深入分析了LLM内部预测信息的演化过程，提出的"三阶段"框架为理解Transformer前向传播的信息处理机制提供了新的结构化视角。对于研究模型缩放规律、机制可解释性、以及残差流几何性质的读者具有直接参考价值。其中"深层模型额外容量主要用于候选消歧"的发现对理解模型深度与性能的关系有重要启示。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would causal interventions (activation patching, causal mediation analysis) confirm that the geometrically identified phases correspond to mechanistically distinct circuit behaviors?
- How do the three phases manifest in MoE models, where different experts may serve different roles across depth?
- Can the phase boundaries inform layer-wise pruning strategies -- e.g., are Phase 2 layers more amenable to compression than Phase 3 layers?
- Does the three-phase structure hold for instruction-tuned or RLHF-aligned models, or does alignment shift the phase boundaries?
- Could the RSS profile be used as a diagnostic for detecting anomalous layer behavior or training instabilities?
- How does the phase structure change during training itself -- do the phases emerge gradually or appear at a specific training stage?
