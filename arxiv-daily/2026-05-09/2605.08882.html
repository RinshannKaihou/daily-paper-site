Discrete Flow Matching: Convergence Guarantees Under Minimal Assumptions

Pham, Conforti, Ren, Durmus 2026-05-09 Ecole Polytechnique / Padova / Evry
flow-matching discrete-generative-models convergence-analysis CTMC
Key Finding
First non-asymptotic convergence guarantees for Discrete Flow Matching on ℤmd under only an approximation-error assumption — no score learnability or boundedness conditions required.
Key Metric
Õ(d²/ε²) URW iteration complexity — independent of vocabulary size m
Why This Matters
Removes the theoretical bottleneck: DFM with uniform random walk provably scales to large vocabularies without m-dependent cost.
Critical Edge
No experimental validation. NNRW early-stopping error is O(dmη), potentially dominant for large m and d.

Main Result (Theorem 2)

KL divergence bound decomposing total error into approximation and discretization components:

KL(μ1-η | Law(X1-ηθ*)) ≤ m²η-2ε̃(√|M| + ε̃) + λ(m)² d²m² h log(η-1) log(mη-1)

where λ(m) = 1/2 for NNRW and 1/m for URW. The adaptive grid tk = 1 − (1+h)−k concentrates steps near t = 1 where the score changes fastest.

Proof Architecture

  • Girsanov decomposition splits KL into approximation error (Eapprox) and discretization error (Edisc)
  • Lemma 1 (Score Stability): uniform bound on score for t ∈ [0, 1−η]: (1−t)/m ≤ utM ≤ m/(1−t)
  • Lemma 2 (Score ODE): Kolmogorov-derived ODE governing score evolution, with adaptive grid countering 1/(1−t) blow-up
  • Lemma 3 (Entropy dynamics): Ito's formula controls entropy along the discretized path
  • Amos inequality for Bessel function ratios (NNRW); closed-form Hamming distance formulas (URW)

Strengths & Limitations

  • First comprehensive analysis of DFM with both KL and TV bounds under minimal assumptions
  • Score stability lemma is a standalone technical contribution
  • Adaptive geometric grid is a clean design choice
  • Restricted to ℤmd with product structure; generalization to trees, graphs, variable-length sequences not addressed
  • Approximation error assumption M1 combines score entropy + L2 loss, stronger than standard training
  • Quadratic dependence on d (d²) may be suboptimal
  • Combined loss may not align with practical training procedures

Open Questions

  1. Can assumption M1 be verified for transformers or MLPs used in practical DFM? What sample complexity would be needed?
  2. How do these bounds compare to empirical convergence rates when m = 32,000 (BPE vocabularies)?
  3. Can hybrid dynamics interpolate between URW's m-independence and NNRW's m² cost for structured spaces?
  4. Does the lack of transitive identity in DFM scores (vs. discrete diffusion) affect generation quality beyond convergence rates?
  5. Can the analysis extend to variable-length sequences or autoregressive generation where the state space is not a simple product?
