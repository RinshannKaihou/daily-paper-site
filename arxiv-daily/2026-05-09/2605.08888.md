> **一句话总结：** DocScope提出了一个长文档问答的结构化推理轨迹基准（273篇文档、1124个问题），通过四级解耦评估协议（页面定位、区域定位、事实提取、答案验证）揭示即使模型最终答案正确，完整证据链率最高也仅29%（GPT-5.4），Oracle研究表明事实提取（而非区域定位）是主要能力瓶颈，且同家族中稠密模型一致优于参数量数倍于其的MoE模型。

> **TL;DR:** DocScope is a 50-page benchmark paper that evaluates whether multimodal LLMs can produce trustworthy, verifiable reasoning over long, visually rich documents. It decomposes document QA into a four-stage reasoning trajectory (page localization, region grounding, fact extraction, answer verification) evaluated independently via inter-stage decoupling with LLM judges calibrated against human annotations. Across 18+ models (Gemini 3.1 Pro, Claude Opus 4.7, GPT-5.4, etc.), the highest complete evidence chain rate among correct answers is only 29% (GPT-5.4). Oracle studies reveal fact extraction -- not region grounding -- as the primary bottleneck. Dense models consistently outperform MoE counterparts within the same family despite having far fewer total parameters.

## 核心贡献

- 提出DocScope基准，将长文档问答建模为结构化推理轨迹预测问题：模型需输出证据页面、支撑区域、事实陈述和最终答案
- 设计四级解耦评估协议（页面定位F1、区域定位Strict/Lenient F1、事实一致性、答案准确率），每级独立审计，所有评判器经人类对齐校准（Kappa最高0.946）
- 包含273篇文档（35-100页）、1,124个问题（1,046可答+78不可答），覆盖7种问题类型，全部层级证据标注由13名标注员完成（260人时）
- 评估了6个商业模型、12个开源模型、2个Agentic RAG框架和3个领域专用系统，提供了全面的跨模型对比
- 核心发现：答案正确时完整证据链最高仅29%；Oracle研究揭示事实提取是主要瓶颈；稠密模型一致优于同家族MoE模型；证据空间分散度是关键难度因子

## Key Contributions

- Introduce DocScope, a benchmark reformulating long-document QA as a structured reasoning trajectory prediction task requiring evidence pages, bounding-box regions, factual statements, and a final answer
- Design a four-stage evaluation protocol with inter-stage decoupling, using LLM judges (GPT-5.5 for region grounding, Qwen3.6-Plus for fact/answer) calibrated against human annotations across multiple alignment studies
- Curate 1,124 questions (1,046 answerable + 78 unanswerable) from 273 documents (35-100 pages), with 7 question types covering visual counting, structure, numerical, technical, entity, semantic, and temporal reasoning
- Evaluate 18+ models including Gemini 3.1 Pro (78.9% ACC), Claude Opus 4.7 (76.0%), GPT-5.4 (62.7%), Qwen3.5-27B (66.4%), and domain-specific systems (SimpleDoc, VidoRAG, URaG, Docopilot)
- Key findings: complete evidence chains among correct answers max out at 29% (GPT-5.4); oracle studies identify fact extraction as the dominant bottleneck; dense models consistently outperform MoE counterparts; evidence spatial dispersion is the key difficulty factor

## Methodology

- **Data Construction:** Documents sourced from FinePDF corpus, filtered for 35-100 pages, English, text density >80 tokens/page, crawl dates >2025. Final 273 documents selected after layout analysis (PP-DocLayoutV3) and manual inspection.
- **Question Synthesis:** Claude Opus 4.6 generates 8 question types from page images. Multi-model blind testing (Claude Opus 4.5/4.6, GPT-5.1/5.2/5.4, Gemini 2.5 Pro) filters questions answerable without document access. 1,300 synthesized, 176 removed during annotation/adjudication/PII control, yielding 1,124.
- **Human Annotation:** 13 annotators from 2 channels, 260 person-hours. Each annotator selects pages, draws bounding boxes, annotates fact-bearing statements, and provides answers. Two senior adjudicators perform final review. Only 48 answers revised (3.7%), indicating high annotation quality.
- **Four-Stage Evaluation Protocol:**
  1. **Page Localization (P/R/F1):** Whether the model identifies the correct evidence pages
  2. **Region Grounding (Strict/Lenient F1):** A multimodal judge (GPT-5.5, kappa=0.821 vs human) labels each gold region as covered, imprecise, or not_covered against predicted bounding boxes rendered on the page image
  3. **Fact Extraction (Consistency):** A text-only judge (Qwen3.6-Plus, kappa=0.880 vs human) evaluates whether extracted facts are consistent with gold evidence
  4. **Answer Verification (Accuracy):** A text-only judge (Qwen3.6-Plus, kappa=0.946 vs human) evaluates semantic correctness
- **Inter-Stage Decoupling:** Each stage evaluated independently on correctly retrieved items from prior stages, isolating per-stage capability
- **Oracle Evidence Access Study:** Four models (Claude Sonnet 4.6, GPT-5.4, Qwen3-VL-235B-A22B, Ministral3-8B) tested under 3 cumulative oracle settings: oracle pages, oracle regions, oracle facts
- **Judge Validation:** Comprehensive human alignment studies comparing 4-6 judge candidates per stage, plus comparison against geometric baselines (IoU, IoM, GT-recall) for region grounding, showing frontier LLM judges outperform rule-based metrics

## Results & Highlights

- **Top performers by accuracy:** Gemini 3.1 Pro (78.9%), Claude Opus 4.7 (76.0%), Claude Sonnet 4.6 (70.5%). Only these three exceed 70% accuracy.
- **No model dominates all stages:** GPT-5.4 leads region grounding (Strict F1 57.4) but has lowest Fact Consistency (59.4%) among proprietary models. Claude Opus 4.7 has highest Fact Consistency (77.4%) with balanced scores. Gemini 3.1 Pro leads page localization (87.6 F1) and accuracy but trails in region grounding (39.7 Strict F1).
- **Region grounding is the weakest stage:** Page F1 to Strict Region F1 drops exceed 40 percentage points for most models.
- **Evidence-chain completeness among correct answers (GPT-5.4 example):** 29.06% complete, 51.92% partially reliable, 19.02% completely unreliable.
- **Oracle study:** Providing oracle facts yields the largest accuracy gain across all models, identifying fact extraction (not region grounding) as the primary bottleneck. Even with full oracle access, gaps persist (Claude Sonnet 4.6: 92.0% vs. Ministral3-8B: 78.6%), indicating intrinsic reasoning capacity bounds performance.
- **Conservative-to-aggressive strategy shift:** Oracle pages can decrease Region F1 as models shift from large conservative boxes to smaller but mislocalized predictions.
- **Dense vs. MoE:** Within the same family (Qwen3.5, Qwen3-VL, Gemma4), dense models consistently outperform MoE counterparts despite having far fewer total parameters. E.g., Qwen3.5-27B (66.4% ACC) outperforms Qwen3.5-397B-A17B (63.8%) and Qwen3.5-122B-A10B (63.6%).
- **Evidence dispersion is the key difficulty factor:** Accuracy drops when evidence is split across more disconnected clusters or separated by larger page gaps. Evidence position within the document is less important than dispersion.
- **Domain-specific systems struggle:** Agentic RAG frameworks (SimpleDoc: 50.5%) outperform end-to-end models (Docopilot 8B: 7.4%), but both trail general MLLMs substantially.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The four-stage decomposition with inter-stage decoupling is a genuine methodological advance. The observation that answer accuracy is a poor proxy for verifiable reasoning quality (29% complete chains) is important and well-supported. The conservative-to-aggressive strategy shift under oracle conditions is a nuanced and novel finding.
- **Rigor:** Extremely thorough. Judge-human alignment studies with Cohen's kappa, Krippendorff's alpha for annotator agreement, DeLong tests for AUROC comparisons, paired bootstrap for geometric baseline comparisons. Evidence completeness validated (only 2.67% required missing). Distributional analysis vs. MMLongBench-Doc confirms synthetic questions are semantically similar to human-authored ones (centroid cosine similarity 0.935).
- **Reproducibility:** Detailed appendices with inference prompts, metric definitions, experiment environment, and a complete datasheet. Code and data promised for public release. All judge prompts reproduced verbatim.
- **Significance:** High. The benchmark addresses a real gap -- existing long-document benchmarks only measure answer correctness or coarse evidence use. The findings about activated parameters vs. total scale and the bottleneck analysis have implications beyond document understanding.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - Questions are LLM-synthesized (Claude Opus 4.6), which may introduce subtle distributional biases not captured by embedding-space similarity metrics. The distributional analysis against MMLongBench-Doc is reassuring but limited to one comparison benchmark.
  - The 29% complete chain rate uses GPT-5.4 as the exemplar -- this is the model with the strongest region grounding. Other models have even lower rates (e.g., Gemini 3.1 Pro: 14.04%). The paper could be clearer about whether the 29% headline figure generalizes.
  - Region grounding evaluation relies on an LLM judge rather than geometric metrics. While the paper shows GPT-5.5 and Qwen3.6-Plus outperform geometric baselines, GPT-5.4 and Claude Opus 4.7 do not. This means the evaluation quality depends on using the right judge model, adding cost and complexity.
  - The document pool excludes math/chemistry papers and requires >80 tokens/page density. This may bias toward certain document types (reports, manuals) and away from formula-heavy or highly visual documents.
  - Domain-specific systems (Docopilot, URaG) are evaluated with modified input protocols (image merging) that may disadvantage them unfairly. The paper acknowledges this but does not ablate the effect.

## Strengths

- The four-stage evaluation protocol is well-designed and enables precise diagnosis of where models fail in the evidence chain
- Comprehensive judge validation with human alignment studies is exemplary -- includes kappa, MCC, cost analysis, and comparison against prior methods
- Oracle study design is clean and reveals the counter-intuitive finding that fact extraction (not region grounding) is the primary bottleneck
- Error analysis of ~200 samples with 7 fine-grained error types across 4 stages provides actionable insights
- Dense vs. MoE comparison within model families is a valuable finding with clear practical implications
- Evidence dispersion analysis (6 factors) systematically decomposes what makes questions hard
- The paper includes a full datasheet and broader impacts statement, demonstrating responsible benchmark creation

## Limitations

- LLM-synthesized questions may not fully capture the distribution of real user queries over long documents, despite embedding-space similarity analysis
- Region grounding evaluation depends on frontier-quality LLM judges (GPT-5.5), which adds cost and potential bias; geometric baselines are competitive (tau_b 0.83-0.86) for mid-tier judges
- Document selection excludes formula-heavy domains and requires high text density, potentially limiting generalizability
- The benchmark is English-only; the authors acknowledge multilingual extension as future work
- Annotation completeness is validated on 150 samples (2.67% required missing), which provides confidence but cannot guarantee zero omissions across all 1,124 instances
- The conservative-to-aggressive strategy shift under oracle conditions suggests that region grounding metrics may be noisy -- models can score well with lucky large boxes
- Domain-specific systems are evaluated with modified protocols (image merging) that may not reflect their intended use cases

## Relevance

该论文对多模态长文档理解的可验证推理评估提出了系统化方案，其四级解耦评估方法和Oracle瓶颈分析对需要评估模型推理可信度的研究具有重要参考价值。特别是其发现——事实提取（而非区域定位）是主要瓶颈，以及稠密模型优于MoE模型的结论——对模型架构设计和训练策略有直接启示。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How would the bottleneck analysis change if models were specifically fine-tuned on structured evidence-chain outputs before evaluation?
- Can the conservative-to-aggressive strategy shift be mitigated through calibration or box-scoring mechanisms in the evaluation protocol?
- How does performance scale with document length beyond 100 pages, and is there a critical context length where even frontier models collapse?
- What training interventions (e.g., bounding-box prediction heads, fact-extraction pretraining) would most effectively address the identified bottlenecks?
- Does the dense-vs-MoE finding generalize to document generation tasks, or is it specific to evidence-retrieval and grounding?
- How would the benchmark need to be adapted for multilingual or code-mixed documents common in global enterprise settings?
