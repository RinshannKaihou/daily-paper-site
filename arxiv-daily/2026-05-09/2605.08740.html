Causal Dimensionality of Transformer Representations: Measurement, Scaling, and Layer Structure

Nilesh Sarkar, Dawar Jyoti Deka 2026-05-09 arXiv 2605.08740
Key Metric — The Wedge
15.6× vs 4.35×

Representational capacity grows 15.6× while causal capacity only 4.35× across 64× SAE width expansion on Gemma-2-2B.

Why This Matters

Current SAE widths capture only ~72% of causal directions at 1M features, needing ~2.6M for 95%. SAE-based safety analyses are substantially under-powered — and the community’s focus on encoder feature labels is misplaced.

Critical Edge

The κ̂ estimate has very wide confidence intervals (Wald CI [0, 4225], bootstrap CI [545, 5130]) from only 4 post-minimum data points, making the absolute value unreliable. Scale invariance rests on a single 9B width point.

Methodology

Core Definition

Causal dimensionality κ(L,M,T) = eff-rank(ΣL) where ΣL = E[JL⊤ JL] — the effective rank (exponential Shannon entropy of the normalized eigenspectrum) of the expected Jacobian outer product. Upper bound: κ ≤ min(d, |V|).

Estimation

Attribution patching (AtP) approximates ΣL in 2 forward + 1 backward pass (vs ~2,300 backward passes for exact). Validated: ρ = 0.838 global, ρ > 0.95 in the top-5% regime that determines Ncausal.

Experimental Setup

Models: Gemma-2-2B, Gemma-2-9B, Pythia-70m, LLaMA-3.1-8B. SAEs: GemmaScope at seven widths (16K–1M). Corpus: 200 Pile-10k prompts, 512 tokens. Compute: ~120 A100-hours on a single A100 80GB.

Results & Highlights
  • Wedge: Nrepr grows 15.6× vs Ncausal 4.35× across seven matched-L0 SAE widths. Bootstrap 95% CI [3.86, 4.91].
  • Scale invariance: AtP score distribution is shape-invariant under 3.46× model scaling (Gemma-2-2B vs 9B), confirmed by three concordant tests.
  • Depth profile: κ is constant across 8 layers, but absolute attribution threshold drops 20× from layer 1 to layer 23.
  • Encoder ablation: Random encoder inflates Ncausal by 9.27×. Trained encoder filters sparsity, it does not select features.
  • Architecture invariance: Sub-linear Ncausal growth holds across TopK, JumpReLU, and standard ReLU SAEs.
  • Ground-truth validation: Against 56-feature SVA circuit: p95 recovers 55/56 (98.2%). On wrong distribution, recall collapses to 0.04.
  • Cross-family: Wedge replicated on LLaMA-3.1-8B and Pythia-410m.
Strengths
  • Exceptionally honest reporting of pre-registration failures and limitations
  • Clean theoretical framework with formal propositions and consistency proof
  • The wedge and encoder-as-sparsity-filter findings are genuinely surprising and field-shaping
  • Five control experiments plus cross-family replication
  • AtP validated against exact patching (ρ > 0.95 in the regime that matters)
  • Practical implications clearly quantified: need ~2.6M features for 95% capture
Limitations
  • κ̂ wide CIs; robust claim is only the ratio κ̂/d, not the absolute value
  • Scale invariance from a single 9B width point; full sweep deferred
  • Cross-family replication uses different hook positions (residual stream vs MLP output)
  • Single task distribution (generic Pile-10k); κ is task-defined but task-specific behavior unexplored
  • Gap between κ̂ ≈ 1,990 and κPR ≈ 280 is substantial and unresolved
  • Pre-registered tests for threshold robustness and sparsity dependence formally FAIL
  • No public code at time of writing
Open Questions
  1. How does κ change under task-specific distributions (code, math, multilingual)? Recall collapses to 0.04 on the wrong distribution — does κ itself shift, or only the feature-to-direction mapping?
  2. The encoder-as-sparsity-filter finding suggests focusing on decoder structure — what does a decoder-centric analysis workflow look like in practice?
  3. Can the wedge ratio serve as a quality metric for SAE training?
  4. Does scale invariance hold across larger jumps (2B → 70B) and different architectures (e.g., mixture-of-experts)?
  5. Synthetic ground-truth shows zero recall across all 24 runs — are current circuit findings capturing true causal structure or merely activation correlations?
  6. Is m ≈ 2.6 × 106 feasible for 95% capture, or do we need fundamentally more efficient SAE architectures?
  7. Does the 20× threshold attenuation across depth mean interpretability is inherently harder for later layers?
