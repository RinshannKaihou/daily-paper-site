> **一句话总结：** 提出Relative Kinetic Utility (RKU)框架，通过连续动力学积分和Fisher trace归一化识别高曲率逻辑通路，在40%稀疏度下GSM8K准确率翻倍于幅度剪枝基线，但绝对性能仍然很低（13.34%）。

> **TL;DR:** Magnitude-based structural pruning destroys chain-of-thought reasoning at high sparsities (~40%) because it prioritizes high-frequency syntactic tokens over logical pathways (the "magnitude trap"). This paper proposes Relative Kinetic Utility (RKU), which reformulates pruning as a continuous kinetic integral over the model's depth manifold via Alternating Gradient Flow, then applies Fisher trace normalization to isolate "kinetic spikes" — neurons critical for high-curvature logical routing. On Qwen-2.5-7B, RKU achieves 13.34% on GSM8K at 40% sparsity (vs. Wanda's 4.85%), and shows superior OOD transfer after LoRA fine-tuning (63% vs. 59.33% on MathQA). However, absolute performance at 40% sparsity remains very low, and RKU trades off static knowledge retrieval accuracy (2-4% drop on PIQA/WinoGrande) for reasoning preservation.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **Relative Kinetic Utility (RKU)** 理论框架，将离散剪枝提升为基于交替梯度流(AGF)的连续动力学积分问题，用连续L2范数能量函数替代离散交叉熵损失
- 通过 **Fisher trace 归一化** 充当零成本Hessian预条件器，识别"kinetic spikes"——负责高曲率逻辑路由的关键结构通路
- 系统性揭示传统幅度剪枝的"幅度陷阱"（Wanda's Trap）：微观分析显示高激活、低梯度的神经元被优先保留，而低激活、高梯度的逻辑路由神经元被错误剪除
- 在 Qwen-2.5-7B 上40%稀疏度时GSM8K达13.34%（Wanda仅4.85%），LoRA微调后OOD迁移在MathQA上达63%
- 揭示结构可塑性中的**shortcut learning陷阱**：幅度方法在域内恢复率高但OOD迁移差，RKU则相反

## Key Contributions

- Introduces **Relative Kinetic Utility (RKU)**, a unified framework that replaces discrete cross-entropy pruning objectives with a continuous kinetic integral over the depth manifold via Alternating Gradient Flow (AGF)
- Proposes **Fisher trace normalization** as a zero-cost Riemannian pre-conditioner to isolate "kinetic spikes" — structural pathways with high kinetic gradients but potentially low activation magnitudes, critical for multi-hop logical routing
- Empirically identifies the **"magnitude trap"** (Wanda's Trap) at the microscopic level: neurons with massive activations but near-zero kinetic gradients dominate magnitude-based pruning, systematically destroying reasoning pathways
- Demonstrates via **IoU topological analysis** that a phase transition occurs at ~40% sparsity: pruning masks decouple drastically, with magnitude methods diverging faster in deep routing layers (e.g., Layer 26 IoU drops to 61.9%)
- Shows RKU avoids **shortcut learning** during LoRA recovery: magnitude methods achieve high in-domain scores but collapse on OOD transfer, while RKU shows the opposite pattern

## Methodology

RKU reformulates structural pruning in three steps:

**Step 1: The Discrete Objective Gap.** Traditional methods (Wanda, FLAP) use activation magnitude weighted by input norms, optimizing the discrete cross-entropy loss. This biases toward high-frequency syntactic tokens. RKU replaces the discrete LCE with a continuous energy functional: L_continuous = ||H^(L)||_2^2 (squared L2 norm of final hidden states).

**Step 2: Continuous Kinetic Integral via AGF.** The kinetic utility of a structural component c is defined as:

  U_AGF(c) = integral from 0 to L of [ E_x~D [ Y_c^(l) (x) * nabla_{Y_c} L_continuous ] ] dl

This integrates gradient-weighted activations across all layers as a continuous flow. The key insight: nabla_{Y_c} L_continuous acts as a "physical score function" that highlights kinetic spikes (high-gradient, reasoning-critical neurons) rather than high-activation syntactic neurons.

**Step 3: Fisher Trace Normalization (RKU).** Raw AGF suffers from localized kinetic noise under extreme compression. RKU normalizes the kinetic utility within the local Riemannian subspace:

  U_tilde_AGF(c) = U_AGF(c) / (sum_j U_AGF(j) + epsilon)

  S_RKU = ||W_*,c||_2 * U_tilde_AGF(c)

Theoretical connection: since E[(nabla_Y)^2] approximates the diagonal of the Fisher Information Matrix (which approximates the Hessian H), this normalization performs Fisher trace normalization: U_tilde_AGF(c) ~ |Y_c| * sqrt(H_cc) / sqrt(Trace(H)).

**Pruning procedure:** FFN intermediate dimensions are ranked by S_RKU and removed to the target sparsity. All experiments run on 8x NVIDIA RTX 4090 GPUs.

## Results & Highlights

**Main Results (Qwen-2.5-7B, zero-shot/few-shot, no fine-tuning):**

| Sparsity | Method | GSM8K | AQuA |
|----------|--------|-------|------|
| 30% | Wanda-Struct | 13.72% | 25.98% |
| 30% | Taylor-FO | 24.18% | 26.77% |
| 30% | RKU | **34.34%** | **28.35%** |
| 40% | Wanda-Struct | 4.85% | 24.41% |
| 40% | Taylor-FO | 6.90% | 24.02% |
| 40% | RKU | **13.34%** | **27.56%** |
| 50% | Wanda-Struct | 1.90% | 22.44% |
| 50% | RKU | **3.64%** | 24.02% |

- RKU roughly doubles GSM8K survival rate at 40% sparsity vs. best baseline
- On AQuA, RKU maintains 27.56% at 40% sparsity while Wanda drops to 24.41%

**Knowledge-Logic Trade-off (LLaMA-3-8B):**
- RKU yields 2-4% lower accuracy on static retrieval tasks (ARC-C, WinoGrande, PIQA) — this is a deliberate trade-off for preserving reasoning pathways
- Example: PIQA at 40% sparsity — Wanda 66.49%, RKU 65.18%

**OOD Generalization via LoRA (Qwen-2.5-7B, 150-step fine-tuning):**
- GSM8K-tuned, tested on MathQA at 40% sparsity: RKU 63.00%, Wanda 59.33%, Taylor-FO 54.33%
- AQuA-tuned, tested on MathQA at 40% sparsity: RKU 37.45%, Wanda 35.71%, Taylor-FO 35.38%
- Magnitude methods show high ID recovery but poor OOD transfer = shortcut learning

**Ablation — Fisher Trace is critical:** Raw AGF without normalization collapses to 0.00% on AQuA at 40% sparsity with severe generation degradation (repetitive hallucinations).

**Perplexity:** RKU has slightly higher perplexity than Wanda at all sparsity levels (e.g., 10.40 vs 10.68 on WikiText2 at 30%), consistent with the knowledge-logic trade-off.

**Hardware speedup:** 50% RKU-pruned Qwen-2.5-7B achieves 1.42x wall-clock prefill speedup (0.1519s to 0.1066s on RTX 4090).

**SVD Analysis:** At Layer 26 under 50% sparsity, Wanda shows exponential singular value decay (rank collapse), while RKU maintains a heavy-tailed spectrum (higher-dimensional feature space).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The reformulation of pruning as a continuous kinetic integral with Fisher trace normalization is genuinely novel. The microscopic analysis of Wanda's Trap (high activation / low gradient neurons vs. kinetic spikes) provides a clean mechanistic explanation for reasoning collapse.

- **Rigor: Moderate-High.** The paper provides substantial empirical evidence across multiple dimensions: main accuracy, perplexity, OOD generalization, IoU topological analysis, SVD rank analysis, and qualitative case studies. The ablation showing AGF without Fisher normalization collapses to 0% on AQuA is convincing. However:
  - The theoretical connection between the integral formulation and Fisher information is sketched rather than formally proven (uses "approximates," "corresponds to" language).
  - The claim of "zero-cost Hessian pre-conditioner" is not benchmarked against actual second-order methods.
  - Results at 30% sparsity show RKU at 34.34% on GSM8K while Wanda is at 13.72% — this is a dramatic gap that warrants deeper investigation into why Wanda fails even at 30%.

- **Reproducibility: Moderate.** The core equations are provided but implementation details (calibration data, number of samples for gradient computation, epsilon value) are not fully specified. No code released.

- **Significance: Moderate.** The magnitude trap insight is the strongest contribution and will likely influence future pruning work. However, the practical utility is questionable: even RKU's 13.34% on GSM8K at 40% sparsity represents catastrophic degradation (unpruned Qwen-2.5-7B presumably scores 70%+). The method mainly shows that reasoning collapses slightly less catastrophically.

- **Red flags:**
  (1) Absolute performance at the target sparsity (40%) is extremely low across all methods — the paper frames this as "doubling survival rate" but the absolute numbers are still dismal.
  (2) RKU consistently trades off perplexity and static knowledge for reasoning preservation, but the paper does not quantify whether this trade-off is worth it in practice.
  (3) The paper tests only 7B-8B models — no evidence the approach scales to the 70B+ models where structural pruning is most urgently needed.
  (4) Single author with dense theoretical framing creates risk of overselling mathematical sophistication for what may be a relatively simple gradient-normalization heuristic.
  (5) The PEFT experiments use only 150 LoRA steps — an unusually small number that may not represent realistic fine-tuning scenarios.

## Strengths

- The "magnitude trap" analysis is a genuine and valuable insight — the microscopic plot of activation norm vs. gradient norm clearly shows two distinct clusters (syntactic neurons vs. kinetic spikes)
- The IoU analysis across sparsity levels provides clean evidence of a topological phase transition at ~40%
- The OOD generalization experiments (LoRA on GSM8K/AQuA, test on MathQA) reveal the shortcut learning phenomenon convincingly
- The knowledge-logic trade-off is honestly reported — RKU does worse on PIQA/WinoGrande
- SVD rank analysis provides a nice mechanistic explanation for representational collapse
- The ablation showing Fisher normalization is critical (0% on AQuA without it) is a strong result
- Extensive appendix with full sparsity sweeps (10-90%) and perplexity tables

## Limitations

- Absolute reasoning performance at 40% sparsity remains catastrophically low (13.34% GSM8K) — the method delays collapse but does not prevent it
- Only tested on 7B-8B models; scalability to larger models is unknown
- RKU consistently loses on perplexity and static knowledge benchmarks — the trade-off may be unacceptable in practice
- No code released; implementation details (calibration dataset, gradient computation specifics) are underspecified
- The theoretical framework uses heavy jargon ("Riemannian manifold pre-conditioning," "topological phase transition," "depth manifold") that may obscure relatively straightforward gradient-normalization intuition
- The 150-step LoRA is an unrealistically short fine-tuning regime
- The paper does not compare against other reasoning-aware compression methods (e.g., task-specific pruning, distillation-based approaches)
- All experiments on consumer GPUs — no validation on deployment-relevant hardware or serving frameworks

## Relevance

本文研究LLM结构剪枝如何保持CoT推理能力，通过曲率感知剪枝在高稀疏度下减缓推理崩溃。对关注模型压缩、推理效率和CoT机制的研究者有直接参考价值，尤其是"幅度陷阱"的分析为理解剪枝对推理的影响提供了新视角。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does RKU perform on larger models (70B+) where structural pruning is most impactful and gradient computation becomes expensive?
- Can the kinetic spike detection insight be combined with quantization (e.g., mixed-precision assignment based on kinetic utility)?
- What happens with longer fine-tuning (1000+ LoRA steps) — does RKU's OOD advantage persist?
- Is the knowledge-logic trade-off fundamental, or can a hybrid criterion (e.g., RKU + magnitude ensemble) get the best of both worlds?
- How does RKU interact with attention head pruning vs. FFN pruning — does the kinetic integral behave differently for attention components?
- Could the continuous kinetic integral framework be applied to dynamic pruning during inference (token-level sparsity) rather than static structural pruning?
