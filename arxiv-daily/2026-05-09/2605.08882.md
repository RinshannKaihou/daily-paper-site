> **一句话总结：** 本文在$\mathbb{Z}_m^d$上研究了两种离散Flow Matching模型（最近邻随机游走和均匀随机游走），仅依赖近似误差假设，采用DMPM离散化方案，建立了KL散度（对早停目标分布）和全变差距离（对真实目标分布）的非渐近收敛界，其中均匀随机游走的迭代复杂度为$\tilde{O}(d^2/\varepsilon^2)$，改善了对词表大小$m$的依赖。

> **TL;DR:** This paper establishes non-asymptotic convergence guarantees for Discrete Flow Matching on $\mathbb{Z}_m^d$ using two base dynamics (nearest-neighbor and uniform random walks). Under only an approximation error assumption (no score learnability conditions), with DMPM discretization, the authors derive KL divergence bounds for early-stopped targets and TV distance bounds for true targets. For URW, the iteration complexity achieves $\tilde{O}(d^2/\varepsilon^2)$ — improving over prior work's $\tilde{O}(d^2 m/\varepsilon^2)$ dependence on vocabulary size $m$.

## 核心贡献

- **建立了两种离散Flow Matching模型的非渐近收敛界**：针对最近邻随机游走（NNRW）和均匀随机游走（URW）两种参考动态，在$\mathbb{Z}_m^d$上推导了严格的KL散度和全变差距离误差界。
- **放宽了标准假设**：仅需近似误差假设（Assumption M1: $\mathcal{L}(\theta^*) \leq \tilde{\varepsilon}^2$），不依赖先前工作中对score函数的有界性或可学习性假设。
- **URW的迭代复杂度为$\tilde{O}(d^2/\varepsilon^2)$**：消除了对词表大小$m$的依赖，相比Wan et al. (2026)的$\tilde{O}(d^2 m/\varepsilon^2)$有显著改善。
- **首次给出真实目标分布的TV距离界**：填补了现有DFM理论文献中缺少对真实目标分布误差界的空白。
- **关键技术贡献：score的一致稳定性界**：证明了对NNRW和URW，score函数在$[0, 1-\eta]$上一致有界（$\frac{1-t}{m} \lesssim u_t^M \lesssim \frac{m}{1-t}$），这是收敛分析的核心。

## Key Contributions

- **Theorem 2 (Main Result)**: Non-asymptotic KL divergence bound decomposing error into approximation and discretization components: $\mathrm{KL}(\mu_{1-\eta}|\mathrm{Law}(X_{1-\eta}^{\theta^*})) \lesssim m^2\eta^{-2}\tilde{\varepsilon}(\sqrt{|\mathcal{M}|}+\tilde{\varepsilon}) + (\lambda(m))^2 d^2 m^2 h \log(\eta^{-1})\log(m\eta^{-1})$.
- **Corollary 1 (TV Distance)**: First explicit TV distance bound against the true target $\mu_1$: $\|\mathrm{Law}(X_{1-\eta}^{\theta^*})-\mu_1\|_{\mathrm{TV}} \lesssim \mathrm{C}(m)d\eta + m\eta^{-1}\sqrt{\sqrt{|\mathcal{M}|}\tilde{\varepsilon}+\tilde{\varepsilon}^2} + \lambda(m)dm\sqrt{h\log(m\eta^{-1})\log(\eta^{-1})}$, where C(m)=1 for URW and C(m)=m for NNRW.
- **Lemma 1 (Score Stability)**: Uniform boundedness of the score on $[0,1-\eta]$: $\frac{1-t}{m} \lesssim u_t^M(x,\sigma(x)) \lesssim \frac{m}{1-t}$, proved using Amos inequality for Bessel function ratios (NNRW) and explicit transition density formulas (URW).
- **Improved iteration complexity**: $\tilde{O}(d^2/\varepsilon^2)$ for URW (independent of $m$) and $\tilde{O}(d^2 m^2/\varepsilon^2)$ for NNRW, eliminating restrictive boundedness conditions on the approximate score from prior work.

## Methodology

The paper studies DFM through the stochastic interpolant perspective on $\mathbb{Z}_m^d = \{0,\ldots,m-1\}^d$:

1. **Interpolant Construction**: Given a coupling $\pi \in \Pi(\mu_0, \mu_1)$ and a homogeneous CTMC with generator $q$, define $(X_t^I)_{t \in [0,1]}$ via the jump Markov bridge: $(X_0^I, X_1^I) \sim \pi$ and $(X_t^I)_{t \in [0,1]} | (X_0^I, X_1^I) \sim \mathbb{B}^q(\cdot|X_0^I, X_1^I)$.

2. **Two Reference Dynamics**:
   - **NNRW** (Nearest-Neighbor Random Walk): Generator $q(x,y) = 1/2$ if $y = \sigma(x)$ for $\sigma \in \mathcal{M}^N$ (nearest-neighbor moves). Transition density involves Skellam distributions and modified Bessel functions.
   - **URW** (Uniform Random Walk): Generator $q(x,y) = 1/m$ if $d_H(x,y)=1$. Transition density has closed form via Hamming distance: $p_{s+t|s}(y|x) = \left(\frac{1+(m-1)e^{-t}}{m}\right)^d \alpha_t^{d_H(y,x)}$.

3. **Markovian Projection (Theorem 1)**: The interpolant $(X_t^I)$ is non-Markovian. Its Markov projection $(X_t^M)$ is a CTMC with generator $q_t^M(x,y) = q(x,y) u_t^M(x,y)$, where the score $u_t^M(x,y) = \mathbb{E}[u_t^{(X_1^I)}(x,y)|X_t^I=x]$ with bridge score $u_t^{(x_1)}(x,y) = p_{1|t}(x_1|y)/p_{1|t}(x_1|x)$.

4. **Training**: Minimize combined loss $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_e + \mathcal{L}_2$ (score entropy loss + concrete score matching loss) over discretization grid $\{t_k\}_{k=0}^K$ of $[0, 1-\eta]$.

5. **DMPM Sampling**: Piecewise-constant generator on each interval $[t_k, t_{k+1})$, with exponential holding times. Simulation stops at $t_K = 1-\eta$ to avoid score singularity at $t=1$.

6. **Proof Strategy**:
   - Apply Girsanov's theorem for jump processes to decompose KL divergence into approximation error ($E_{\text{approx}}$) and discretization error ($E_{\text{disc}}$).
   - For $E_{\text{approx}}$: Use score boundedness (Lemma 1) + Cauchy-Schwarz/Jensen inequalities + Assumption M1.
   - For $E_{\text{disc}}$: Derive ODEs for score evolution (Lemma 2) and entropy dynamics (Lemma 3) via Kolmogorov equations and Ito's formula. Choose adaptive time grid $t_k = 1-(1+h)^{-k}$ to counteract $1/(1-t)$ growth.
   - For TV bounds: Pinsker's inequality + triangle inequality, with early-stopping error bounded via explicit transition density analysis (NNRW: $\lesssim dm\eta$; URW: $\lesssim d\eta$).

## Results & Highlights

- **Theorem 2 (KL bound)**: Under Assumption M1 with adaptive grid $t_k = 1-(1+h)^{-k}$:
  $$\mathrm{KL}(\mu_{1-\eta}|\mathrm{Law}(X_{1-\eta}^{\theta^*})) \lesssim m^2\eta^{-2}\tilde{\varepsilon}(\sqrt{|\mathcal{M}|}+\tilde{\varepsilon}) + (\lambda(m))^2 d^2 m^2 h \log(\eta^{-1})\log(m\eta^{-1})$$
  where $\lambda(m) = 1/2$ for NNRW, $1/m$ for URW.

- **Iteration complexity** (for $O(\varepsilon^2)$ KL error):
  - URW: $\tilde{O}(d^2/\varepsilon^2)$ — **independent of vocabulary size $m$**
  - NNRW: $\tilde{O}(d^2 m^2/\varepsilon^2)$

- **Corollary 1 (TV bound against true target)**: First result providing TV distance to the actual $\mu_1$ (not just early-stopped $\mu_{1-\eta}$), with early-stopping error $\mathrm{C}(m)d\eta$ where $\mathrm{C}(m)=1$ for URW and $\mathrm{C}(m)=m$ for NNRW.

- **Comparison Table (Table 1)**: vs. Wan et al. (2026), this work achieves better $m$-dependence for URW and provides TV bounds where prior work only had KL bounds or required different approximation assumptions.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The key technical innovations are: (1) proving score stability for DFM (Lemma 1) without the transitive identity available in discrete diffusion, (2) deriving the score ODE (Lemma 2) via Kolmogorov equations, and (3) the adaptive time grid that counteracts near-singularity at $t \to 1$.
- **Rigor**: Excellent. All bounds are explicit with clearly stated assumptions. The 27-page paper includes complete proofs in appendices, with careful handling of Bessel function ratios (via Amos inequality) and rigorous application of Girsanov's theorem for jump processes.
- **Honest limitations**: The authors explicitly note in Section 5 that their results do not include statistical analysis for specific neural architectures, and that extending to non-$\mathbb{Z}_m^d$ spaces remains open.
- **Significance**: Substantial impact on the theoretical foundations of DFM. The URW complexity $\tilde{O}(d^2/\varepsilon^2)$ being independent of $m$ is a notable result — it suggests DFM with URW scales well to large vocabulary settings.
- **Potential concerns**:
  - The assumption M1 combines score entropy loss and L2 loss ($\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_e + \mathcal{L}_2$), which is stronger than just score entropy minimization used in some practical implementations.
  - The score boundedness $\frac{1-t}{m} \lesssim u_t^M \lesssim \frac{m}{1-t}$ grows as $1/(1-t)$ near the terminal time, requiring early stopping. The gap between $\mu_{1-\eta}$ and $\mu_1$ for NNRW scales as $O(dm\eta)$, which can be significant for large $m$.
  - No experimental validation — purely theoretical. The authors acknowledge this gap.
  - The DFM framework lacks the transitive identity of discrete diffusion scores (Remark 2), making analysis inherently more complex. This is handled well but means the bounds may be looser than what is achievable in the transitive case.

## Strengths

- First comprehensive non-asymptotic analysis of DFM with both KL and TV bounds under minimal assumptions.
- The score stability lemma (Lemma 1) is a standalone technical contribution, using Amos inequality for Bessel functions (NNRW) and Hamming distance analysis (URW).
- The adaptive geometric time grid $t_k = 1-(1+h)^{-k}$ is a clever design choice that naturally concentrates steps near $t=1$ where score changes rapidly.
- Clear comparison with prior work (Table 1) showing concrete improvements in iteration complexity.
- The URW result ($\tilde{O}(d^2/\varepsilon^2)$, independent of $m$) has practical implications for scaling to large vocabularies.

## Limitations

- No experimental validation — the bounds may be loose in practice due to hidden constants and logarithmic factors.
- Restricted to $\mathbb{Z}_m^d$ with product structure; generalization to structured discrete spaces (trees, graphs, variable-length sequences) is not addressed.
- The approximation error assumption M1 is imposed but not verified for specific neural architectures.
- The combined loss $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_e + \mathcal{L}_2$ requires both score entropy and L2 control, which may not align with standard training procedures.
- Early stopping is essential: the NNRW early-stopping error scales as $O(dm\eta)$, which could dominate in high-dimensional/large-vocabulary settings.
- The quadratic dependence on $d$ ($d^2$ in iteration complexity) may be suboptimal — the authors note this is consistent with prior work but it remains an open question.

## Relevance

该论文为离散空间上的Flow Matching生成模型提供了严格的理论收敛保证，在$\mathbb{Z}_m^d$上给出了具体的非渐近误差界和迭代复杂度。对于关注离散扩散/Flow Matching的理论分析、CTMC-based生成模型的采样复杂度、或离散生成模型scaling law的研究者，该论文提供了重要的理论基础，特别是URW动态下与词表大小$m$无关的$\tilde{O}(d^2/\varepsilon^2)$复杂度结果。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the approximation error assumption M1 be verified for standard architectures (transformers, MLPs) used in practical DFM implementations, and what sample complexity would be needed?
2. How do these bounds compare empirically with actual convergence rates in language modeling tasks where $m$ is large (e.g., $m = 32000$ for BPE vocabularies)?
3. The URW achieves $m$-independent complexity while NNRW has $m^2$ dependence — can hybrid dynamics interpolate between these extremes for structured state spaces?
4. Can the analysis be extended to variable-length sequences or autoregressive-style generation, where the state space is not a simple product $\mathbb{Z}_m^d$?
5. The score ODE (Lemma 2) reveals that DFM lacks the transitive property of discrete diffusion — does this fundamental difference have implications for the quality of learned generative models beyond convergence rates?
