Cosine-Gated Adam-Decay: Drop-In Staleness-Aware Outer Optimization for Decoupled DiLoCo

Vatsal Shah · Jiahao Sun — FLock.io

A one-line drop-in replacement for the Nesterov outer optimizer in asynchronous DiLoCo. Scales each incoming pseudo-gradient by σ(τ) = γ(τ) · e−ατ (cosine gate × exponential decay) before Adam's moment buffers, making the staleness penalty depend on α instead of τmax.
Key Finding
CGAD turns asynchronous DiLoCo from unstable to production-viable. Where Nesterov diverges to loss 319 at 1B parameters, CGAD reaches 7.44 — a 43× improvement — using the same training budget and a two-hyperparameter drop-in modification.

Critical Edge
The evidence is suggestive but not yet conclusive at production scale.
  • Inner steps H=8 vs. production H=100–500; H=30 ablation only partial
  • Token budgets are 30M (C4), far below Chinchilla-scale
  • 7B runs have only 15 outer rounds, n=2–4 seeds, explicitly labeled preliminary
  • Single-accelerator sequential setup, not true multi-node
Mechanism
01
Cosine Gate

Compute γ(τ) = ½(1 + cos(πτ/τcut)) for τ ≤ τcut, else 0. Smoothly zeroes out extremely stale gradients before they touch Adam's state.

02
Exponential Decay

Multiply by e−ατ so older gradients contribute proportionally less. Default α=0.2 transfers across scales; the staleness bias in the convergence bound becomes O(1/(e·α)).

03
Gated Adam Update

Scale the pseudo-gradient by σ = γ · e−ατ before updating Adam's m and v buffers. This keeps v clean: stale noise doesn't get squared into the second moment.

04
Scale Insurance

Without the cosine cutoff, residual e−ατ > 0 terms near int8 precision get squared into v and compound across rounds. The hard zero from the cosine gate prevents this — invisible at 25M, critical at 7B.
