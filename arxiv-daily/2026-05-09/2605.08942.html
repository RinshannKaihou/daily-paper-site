Decomposing and Steering Functional Metacognition in Large Language Models

Li, Bai, Liu, Zhang, Zeng Shopee cs.CL 5 models: 0.6B – 235B
Key Finding

LLMs maintain six near-orthogonal, linearly decodable functional metacognitive dimensions in their residual stream—and these internal states can be causally steered to modulate reasoning behavior without retraining.

Key Metric
−28%
output length · +7pp accuracy

Computational effort steering at Qwen3-30B cuts verbosity 28% while boosting accuracy from 81% to 88%. Self-assessed capability steering at 14B raises accuracy from 25% to 44%.

Why This Matters
Benchmark scores don’t just reflect raw capability—they reflect internal metacognitive configuration. Evaluation methodology must account for this hidden state, or results conflate knowledge with self-awareness about knowledge.
Critical Edge
Audience expertise is near-perfectly decodable yet causally inert—steering fails (|Δ| < 0.05). Decodability ≠ steerability. Only 2 model families tested. Steering n = 16 for most conditions. All analysis is strictly linear.
Structural Evidence
<0.25
max |cos θ| across all dimension pairs

Six probe directions are near-orthogonal (mean |cos| < 0.06), cross-task probes transfer at 81% mean accuracy, and joint 6D steering shifts 5/6 dimensions simultaneously at 30B.

Methodology

  • Stage 1 – Probing: 200 minimal-contrast prompt pairs per dimension. Per-layer logistic regression on last-token residual stream hidden states. Strictly linear probes.
  • Stage 2 – Steering: Probe-derived weight vectors injected into residual stream: h′ = h + α·ŵ. Evaluated on held-out GSM8K with chain-of-thought.
  • Stage 3 – Generalization: Cross-task probes (math/knowledge QA → SimpleQA, 4320 questions) and joint 6D superposed steering to rule out task-specific shortcuts.

Scale-Gated Phase Transition

  • <10B: Dimensions are “scale-gated”—some at chance, others partially decodable. Representations diffuse and unlocalized.
  • 14B: All six dimensions reach ≥0.80. Early-layer concentration emerges.
  • 30B+: Near-perfect linear separability across all dimensions. Immediate encoding at layer 0 for some dimensions.
  • Llama-4-Scout: Only 0.74 average; audience expertise below chance (0.43). Training recipe matters, not just parameter count.

Strengths

  • Three-stage converging-evidence pipeline (probing → steering → generalization)
  • Full cosine similarity matrices across all models, not just best-performing
  • Honest null results: audience unsteerable, Llama-4 weak, inverted effects reported
  • Adapter-vs-metacognition hypothesis comparison (Table 4) as a model for ruling out alternatives
  • Code and data public: github.com/xlands/meta-cognition

Limitations & Open Questions

  • Only two model families; Llama-4 qualitatively different and weaker
  • Composite scoring metrics use hand-tuned weights, not cross-validated
  • LLM-based evaluator introduces second-order confound
  • All analysis strictly linear; nonlinear manifolds and feature superposition unexplored
  • Steering n = 16 for most conditions (0.6B: n = 200)
  • Six dimensions are theory-driven; unsupervised discovery might reveal different axes
