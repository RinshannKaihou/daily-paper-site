SURGE: Surrogate Gradient Adaptation in Binary Neural Networks

Huang, Liu, Yang, Li, Yang, Liu, Chen, Fu, Zhang Beihang Univ. / Huawei Noah's Ark / KAUST arXiv 2605.10989
Key Finding

A Dual-Path Gradient Compensator (DPGC) pairs every binarized layer with a parallel full-precision auxiliary branch. Forward output stays identical to a standard BNN; backprop routes through both paths to recover the gradient information that STE discards. Auxiliary branch is discarded at inference -- zero deployment overhead.

Key Metric
62.0%

ImageNet top-1, ResNet-18, 1-stage, 1/1 bit. +0.6% over RBONN, +5.6% over IR-Net. Two-stage reaches 66.7%, closing the gap to full-precision (69.6%) to just 7.6 pp.

Why This Matters

SURGE works across both CNNs and Transformers (ResNet, VGG, BERT), cuts compute by 5-7x, and even surpasses the real-valued Faster-RCNN baseline on PASCAL VOC (77.0 vs 76.4 mAP) -- making binary networks viable for edge deployment without accuracy sacrifice.

Critical Edge

The paper never reports training time or GPU memory. Maintaining a parallel full-precision branch roughly doubles per-layer training compute. On ImageNet two-stage, the gain over RBONN is only +0.2%, within typical run-to-run variance. GLUE 72.0% remains 11.9 pp below full-precision BERT.

Methodology

  • DPGC: For each binarized layer f_b(x), a parallel full-precision branch f_a(x;W_a) is trained. Output decomposition lets gradients flow through both the STE path and the less-biased auxiliary path.
  • AGS: Adaptive Gradient Scaler uses lambda = eta * ||delta_b|| / (||g_a|| + eps) to balance branches, derived from Theorem 5.3 minimizing expected gradient error.
  • Training: Total gradient = STE gradient + scaled auxiliary gradient. AGS rebalances at every step.
  • Inference: Auxiliary branch removed; model behaves as a standard BNN.

Cross-Benchmark Highlights

  • CIFAR-10 ResNet-18: 93.1% (+0.3% vs ReCU)
  • PASCAL VOC Faster-RCNN: 77.0% mAP (+0.5% vs IDa-Det), surpasses real-valued baseline (76.4%) with 5.2x compute savings and 6.8x memory savings
  • GLUE BERT-base: 72.0% avg (+1.4% vs BiT, +8.8% vs BiBERT) -- notable on QQP 87.1%, STS-B 71.7%, RTE 60.6%

Strengths

  • Principled theoretical framework (Theorem 5.3, Corollary 5.4), not purely empirical
  • Architecture-agnostic: validated on ResNet, VGG (CNNs) and BERT (Transformer)
  • Well-designed ablations: DPGC alone +0.4%, DPGC+AGS +0.6%
  • Visualizations clearly show gradient recovery in clipped regions
  • Fixed scaling collapses at scale >0.05 (up to -17.7%); adaptive scaling is essential

Limitations & Open Questions

  • Training overhead unreported: auxiliary branch roughly doubles per-layer compute during training
  • Modest margins: +0.6% (1-stage) and +0.2% (2-stage) over RBONN on ImageNet
  • Single architecture: ImageNet only tested on ResNet-18; no deeper networks or modern architectures
  • Hyperparameter sensitivity: eta=0.01 ablated only on CIFAR-10, not ImageNet or GLUE
  • GLUE gap: 72.0% vs full-precision 83.9% -- practical utility of binary BERT remains unclear
  • Auxiliary branch initialization of W_a is not discussed
