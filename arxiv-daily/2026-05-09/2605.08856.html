Controlling Transient Amplification Improves Long-horizon Rollouts

Adeel Pervez & Francesco Locatello — ISTA
arXiv
2605.08856
Date
2026-05-09
Key Finding
Single-step MSE and rollout error rank models in opposite orders. Non-normal, non-commuting latent Jacobians cause transient amplification that is invisible to eigenvalue analysis—and the proposed commutativity regularizer trades small one-step accuracy for orders-of-magnitude long-horizon stability.
Key Metric
41%RMSE ↓
t2m improvement at day 10 on FourCastNet / ERA5 fine-tuning (74.7M params)
vs. 3× degradation from plain fine-tuning on same data
Why This Matters
A purely training-time modification with zero inference cost converts divergent neural simulator rollouts into bounded ones across KdV, BVE, ERA5 weather, and SST forecasting. Architecture-agnostic—demonstrated on UNet, FNO, U-FNO, and AFNO backbones.
Critical Edge
Improvements concentrate on t2m; upper-air variables (z500, t850, u850) merely match the frozen baseline. The latent-space regularization point is not rigorously justified, and the single-probe Hutchinson estimator is a coarse approximation. Code is not yet released.

Results by Domain

KdV (1D, integrable)
5–80× nMSE improvement at 25× training horizon. Converts divergent FNO rollout into bounded for 2000+ steps. Outperforms PDE-Refiner (5× inference cost) past ∼2000 steps.
BVE (2D, chaotic)
9× RMSE and 82× nMSE improvement. Preserves coherent vortex structures throughout 10s horizon where baseline destabilizes within the training window.
ERA5 / FourCastNet
Plain fine-tuning on 2013–2015 degrades t2m by 3× at day 10. Regularized fine-tuning on the same data yields 41% improvement. Held-out 2019 shows same pattern.
OISST (7.2-year rollout)
Models tied at 1-week lead; regularized model 3.0× more accurate over full 376-week window. Tracks seasonal cycle while baseline drifts in phase.

Method

  • Define latent advance map Gθ: zt → zt+1 at bottleneck; compute Jacobian Jt
  • Commutator penalty Lcomm: estimates ||[Jt+1, Jt]||F2 via 4 JVP calls with a single random probe
  • Normality penalty Lnorm: estimates ||JtTJt − JtJtT||F2 via 2 JVP + 1 VJP
  • Total loss: Lpred + λcLcomm + λnLnorm. Evaluated every K=5–10 minibatches.
  • Training overhead: ∼20–30%. Inference overhead: zero.

Theoretical Insight

  • Transient amplification from non-normal Jacobians causes rollout drift even when all eigenvalues have modulus < 1 (asymptotic stability holds)
  • Theorem 2.3: Under approximate normality and commutativity, propagator bound improves from spectral-norm rate αT to spectral-radius rate ρT—a strict improvement for non-normal Jacobians
  • Connection to Koopman operator theory: commutativity regularization is a relaxation, requiring only that time-varying Jacobians share an orthogonal eigenbasis

Strengths

  • Principled causal chain: non-normal Jacobians → transient amplification → rollout drift
  • Zero inference overhead; purely a training modification
  • Architecture-agnostic across 4 diverse settings
  • Honest discussion of limitations; fair comparison to related methods

Caveats & Open Questions

  • Latent-space regularization point is not rigorously justified; choosing where to regularize may require per-architecture tuning
  • Single-probe Hutchinson estimator is coarse; no sensitivity analysis on probe count
  • Adjacent-step commutativity is a tractable surrogate; tightest bound requires all-pairs
  • No comparison against combining with pushforward trick or noise injection
  • ERA5 gain comes from preventing fine-tuning regression, not fundamentally improving the model
