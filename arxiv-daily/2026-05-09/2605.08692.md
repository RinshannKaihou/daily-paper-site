> **一句话总结：** AAAC提出一种轻量级的4-bit LLM权重量化方法，用两个小的可学习标量码本（每层64字节）替代固定量化网格，每组权重通过激活加权重构误差自适应选择最优码本，码本选择信息编码在缩放因子的未使用符号位中实现零存储开销，量化仅需3-30分钟单GPU，在Llama和Qwen模型上超越AWQ/GPTQ/OmniQuant/QuIP#等7种基线方法，与AWQ组合后可恢复70.5%的量化精度损失。

> **TL;DR:** AAAC replaces the fixed 4-bit quantization grid with two learned scalar codebooks (64 bytes) per layer, optimized via activation-weighted k-means (no gradients, no Hessian). Each weight group selects the codebook minimizing activation-weighted reconstruction error, with the choice stored in the unused sign bit of the group's positive scale at zero storage overhead. The method completes in 3-30 minutes on a single GPU, outperforms all gradient-free baselines (AWQ, GPTQ, IF4) and matches or exceeds gradient-assisted methods (OmniQuant, SqueezeLLM, QuIP#) that require hours and higher memory. Combined with AWQ, AAAC recovers 70.5% of the quantization gap on average.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 AAAC（Activation-Aware Adaptive Codebooks），一种轻量级的4-bit LLM权重量化方法，将固定标量码本替换为每层两个可学习的自适应码本
- 码本通过激活加权 k-means 交替优化学习，无需梯度、Hessian或反向传播
- 每组权重通过最小化激活加权重构误差选择码本，选择位编码在正缩放因子的未使用符号位中，实现零额外存储开销
- 量化仅需3-30分钟（单GPU，H100），峰值内存不超过模型本身大小
- 在 Llama (1B-8B) 和 Qwen (0.5B-32B) 模型上，g=16 NVFP4设置下所有模型均取得最低困惑度
- 与 AWQ 正交可组合：AWQ+AAAC 平均恢复 70.5% 的量化精度损失（AAAC单独53.3%）
- 8个下游任务（BBH, MMLU, GPQA, HellaSwag, ARC-C, PIQA, MuSR, MATH）上平均准确率 64.21%，接近 BF16 基线的 64.59%

## Key Contributions

- Introduces AAAC, a gradient-free post-training quantization method that replaces the fixed 4-bit scalar codebook with two learned scalar codebooks (64 bytes total) per layer
- Codebooks are learned via alternating optimization: activation-weighted k-means for table refinement + per-group table assignment, requiring only forward-pass calibration data
- Per-group codebook selection bit is stored in the unused sign bit of the positive scale factor (when S=g), achieving zero additional storage overhead
- Completes in 3-30 minutes on a single H100 GPU with peak memory no larger than the model itself, enabling in-memory calibration of 27B models
- Achieves lowest perplexity on all tested models at g=16 (NVFP4 layout) across Llama and Qwen families; outperforms AWQ and GPTQ at g=128 (INT4 layout)
- Matches or exceeds gradient-assisted methods (OmniQuant, SqueezeLLM, GPTVQ, QuIP#) at 60.0% average gap recovery vs. their 25-55%
- Orthogonal to AWQ: combining AWQ channel scaling + AAAC codebooks recovers 70.5% of the quantization gap

## Methodology

AAAC targets a different degree of freedom from prior PTQ methods: instead of improving how weights map onto a fixed grid (like AWQ/GPTQ), it improves the grid itself.

**Background:** In group-wise 4-bit quantization, each group of g weights shares a scale s, and normalized weights are rounded to entries in a format-defined table T. Standard methods treat T as fixed (INT4 grid or NVFP4 E2M1 values). AAAC argues this fixed-grid assumption leaves quality on the table.

**Core mechanism:** AAAC replaces the single fixed table T with two learned scalar codebooks T_0 and T_1 per layer, each with the same number of entries as the base format (15 for NVFP4, 16 for INT4). Each selection group of S weights chooses which codebook to use.

**Activation importance:** For a linear layer y = xW^T, the per-column activation importance is I_k = sum(X_{t,k}^2) over calibration samples, providing a diagonal proxy for output error sensitivity.

**Table selection:** Each selection group j is assigned to the codebook minimizing activation-weighted reconstruction error: sigma_j = argmin_r sum_k I_{j,k} * (w~_{j,k} - recon_{T_r}(w~_{j,k}))^2.

**Learning algorithm (Algorithm 1):**
1. Initialize T_0 from full-range quantiles, T_1 from half-shifted quantiles of normalized weights
2. Alternating optimization for n_outer=3 iterations:
   - Assignment step: assign each selection group to its better-fitting table
   - Table update step: n_inner=10 iterations of activation-weighted scalar k-means (update each table entry to activation-weighted centroid of its Voronoi cell)
3. A single outer iteration achieves 82% of total improvement

**Granularity:** Scale group size g is format-determined (g=16 for NVFP4, g=128 for INT4). Selection group size S determines codebook adaptation granularity. When S=g, selection bits fit in unused sign bits of positive scales at zero overhead. For INT4, S=16 adds only 0.0625 bpw.

**Storage overhead:** Two BF16 tables per layer = 60-64 bytes. For a 7B model with ~224 linear layers, this is ~14 KB total.

## Results & Highlights

**vs. Gradient-free methods (g=16, NVFP4, Table 1):** AAAC achieves lowest WikiText-2 perplexity on all 7 Llama and Qwen3.5 models. IF4 shows inconsistent behavior (e.g., worse than RTN on Qwen3.5-9B).

**vs. AWQ (g=128, INT4, Table 2):** At S=128 (zero overhead), AAAC outperforms AWQ on majority of models. At S=16 (+0.0625 bpw), AAAC consistently outperforms AWQ on all models.

**vs. GPTQ (g=128, Table 3):** AAAC at S=16 outperforms GPTQ on all four LLaMA-1/2 models. Even at S=128 (zero overhead), matches or outperforms GPTQ.

**vs. Gradient-assisted methods (Table 4):** AAAC achieves 60.0% average gap recovery, matching QuIP#-FT and exceeding OmniQuant (55.0%), SqueezeLLM (55.0%), GPTVQ (25.0%), and QuIP# without fine-tuning (30.0%).

**AWQ+AAAC combination (Table 5):** Recovers 70.5% of quantization gap on average vs. 53.3% for AAAC alone, demonstrating orthogonality of the two methods.

**Downstream tasks (Table 6):** On 8 tasks (BBH, MuSR, MMLU, GPQA, HellaSwag, ARC-C, PIQA, MATH) with Qwen3.5-9B/27B, AAAC achieves 64.21% average accuracy vs. BF16 baseline 64.59%.

**W4A8 inference (Appendix C):** Same quantized models evaluated with FP8 activations; AAAC improvements carry over fully (49.1% gap recovery at S=16).

**Hyperparameter sensitivity:** Largely insensitive to iteration budget (n_outer=3 vs. 10 differs by ~0.01 perplexity) and calibration data size (1 sequence vs. 4 yields within 0.01 perplexity).

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The core insight -- that the dequantization table itself is a learnable degree of freedom -- is simple but genuinely useful. The sign-bit encoding trick is elegant. However, the approach is essentially a two-codebook variant of weighted k-means applied at the scalar quantization level. The connection to IF4 (which selects between two fixed codebooks) is acknowledged but the step from fixed to learned codebooks is conceptually straightforward.

**Rigor:** The evaluation is thorough: 7 baselines, multiple model families (Llama 1/2/3, Qwen 2.5/3.5), two quantization formats (NVFP4 and INT4), both perplexity (WikiText-2, C4) and downstream tasks. The gap recovery metric provides a normalized comparison. However, the comparison with gradient-assisted methods is limited to four LLaMA models (7B/13B) where prior work reports numbers, which is a small and somewhat dated set. The authors acknowledge this limitation.

**Reproducibility:** The algorithm is fully specified with pseudocode, all hyperparameters disclosed (n_outer=3, n_inner=10, 4 calibration sequences), and the method runs on standard hardware (H100). However, no code repository is linked. The CC BY 4.0 license is encouraging but code release is not mentioned.

**Significance:** This is a strong practical contribution. The result that a simple gradient-free method can match or exceed gradient-assisted methods is surprising and important for the field. The AWQ+AAAC combination achieving 70.5% gap recovery is compelling. The method's simplicity, speed, and minimal overhead make it highly deployable.

**Red flags:** (1) No evaluation on 70B+ models, acknowledged by the authors. (2) The "orders-of-magnitude less quantization time" comparison against gradient-assisted methods is somewhat unfair -- those methods optimize many more parameters. (3) The two-codebook design choice is not justified by analysis or ablation beyond "it works." (4) GPTVQ performs poorly (25% gap recovery) which seems inconsistent with its published results, suggesting possible evaluation protocol differences.

## Strengths

- Extremely lightweight: 64 bytes per layer, zero storage overhead when S=g, 3-30 min quantization time
- Elegant sign-bit encoding trick for codebook selection at zero cost
- Comprehensive evaluation against 7 baselines across 13+ models and 2 quantization formats
- Gradient-free yet matches gradient-assisted methods -- challenges the prevailing assumption in the field
- Orthogonal to AWQ: combination yields 70.5% gap recovery
- Robust across architectures (Llama, Qwen) and model sizes (0.5B-32B)
- Minimal hyperparameter sensitivity and calibration data requirements
- Handles both W4A16 and W4A8 inference without re-calibration

## Limitations

- Only evaluated up to 32B parameters; 70B+ models untested where quantization dynamics may differ
- Comparison with gradient-assisted methods limited to four LLaMA models (7B/13B) -- a dated benchmark
- Only targets weight-only quantization (W4A16/W4A8); does not address joint weight-activation quantization (W4A4)
- The choice of exactly two codebooks is not justified through ablation or theoretical analysis
- No code repository linked at time of publication
- IF4's poor performance on some models (worse than RTN) is noted but not deeply analyzed
- The method's benefit at g=128 with S=128 (zero overhead) is modest; meaningful gains require S=16 which adds 0.0625 bpw

## Relevance

该论文提出了一种极其轻量且高效的LLM 4-bit权重量化方法，通过学习自适应码本替代固定量化网格，在无梯度优化的情况下匹敌甚至超越需要数小时的梯度辅助方法。对于关注大模型高效部署、推理加速和量化技术的研究具有直接且重要的参考价值。AAAC与AWQ的正交组合设计思路也可启发其他量化方法的改进。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would using more than two codebooks (e.g., 4 or 8) yield further improvements, and what is the storage-accuracy Pareto frontier?
- How does AAAC perform on 70B+ models (Llama 3.1 70B, Qwen 2.5 72B) where the weight distribution may have different characteristics?
- Can the adaptive codebook approach be extended to joint weight-activation quantization (W4A4) or to sub-4-bit regimes (W2)?
- What is the theoretical relationship between AAAC's activation-weighted k-means and the Fisher-weighted approach of SqueezeLLM -- are they solving similar optimization problems at different scales?
- How does AAAC interact with sparse quantization methods (e.g., SpQR) that combine pruning with quantization?
- Could the alternating optimization procedure be warm-started with format-specific priors (e.g., initializing near the FP4 grid) for faster convergence?
