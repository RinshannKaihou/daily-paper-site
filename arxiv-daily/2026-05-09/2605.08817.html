How You Begin is How You Reason: Driving Exploration in RLVR via Prefix-Tuned Priors

IMAX trains a pool of soft prefixes on a frozen LLM to reshape its prior over reasoning trajectories. A variational InfoMax reward prevents different prefixes from collapsing into redundant controls—yielding consistent Pass@4 improvements on math benchmarks at a fraction of the training cost.

Xu, Chen & Chen HKBU & UMD 2026-05-09 Qwen 1.5B–8B
Key Finding

Soft prefixes optimized under an InfoMax reward produce semantically distinct reasoning strategies (algebraic vs. case-based) from a frozen backbone, outperforming standard RLVR exploration without touching model weights.

Key Metric

+11.60%Pass@4   +10.57%Avg@4
at 3–6× lower GPU cost vs. full-parameter GRPO

Why This Matters

A lightweight, algorithm-agnostic exploration layer for RLVR that matches full-parameter training on math reasoning while costing a fraction of the compute—runnable on a single RTX PRO 6000.

Critical Edge

Only C = 2 prefixes tested, all on math reasoning with Qwen models. Scaling to larger prefix pools or generalizing to code, science, and multi-step planning remains unproven.

Method Pipeline
  1. Initialize a pool of C soft prefixes from GPT-generated strategy prompts (e.g., symbolic manipulation vs. case-based analysis), mapped through the frozen embedding layer.
  2. For each training batch, fix prefixes and update the variational posterior classifier q_phi that predicts which prefix generated a given response.
  3. Fix q_phi and update the prefix pool using GRPO with the augmented reward: verifiable reward + beta * log q_phi(z|x,y).
  4. The InfoMax reward (variational lower bound on I(Z;Y|X)) prevents prefix collapse by maximizing mutual information between prefix identity and generated response.
Why Reverse MI

The reverse form H(Z|X) − H(Z|X,Y) avoids intractable marginalization over latent prefixes required by the forward form. A single-forward-pass approximation of the forward form creates a biased contrastive target that encourages exploiting one prefix rather than capturing true dependence between Z and Y.

Evidence of Distinct Strategies
  • t-SNE visualization shows IMAX yields more separated response-embedding clusters across prefixes vs. baselines
  • Reasoning component heatmaps: Prefix 1 induces more equation/algebra operations; Prefix 2 induces more subgoals, case analysis, and verification
  • Oracle (combined) pass rate of two IMAX prefixes beats the single best-performing prefix, confirming complementary coverage
  • IMAX surpasses full-parameter GRPO when K > 4, indicating improved coverage at higher rollout budgets

Strengths
  • Principled InfoMax reward with explicit variational derivation
  • Only soft prefixes + lightweight classifier head trained; backbone stays frozen
  • Algorithm-agnostic: pluggable into GRPO, DAPO, or any RLVR method
  • Thorough analysis: t-SNE, reasoning heatmaps, complementary correctness, cost comparison
  • Single-GPU reproducibility (RTX PRO 6000-96GB)
Open Questions
  • Does InfoMax reward remain effective at C = 8, 16, 32 prefixes?
  • Can learned prefixes transfer across domains (math → code, science)?
  • How does IMAX compare with LoRA-based RLVR in performance and cost?
  • Is the approach compatible with online RLVR where the backbone is also updated?
  • Sensitivity to text-prompt initialization quality not systematically ablated
