Non-Monotonic Latency in Apple MPS Decoding: KV Cache Interactions and Execution Regimes

Willy Fitra Hendria — 2026-05-09
Key Finding
Apple's MPS backend shows non-monotonic latency spikes up to 21× during autoregressive decode — spikes absent on both CPU and CUDA under identical conditions. The anomalies reproducibly appear across GPT-2, BLOOM, and OPT.
Key Metric
21×
latency spike in decode phase
Latency spikes occur within specific decoding-budget intervals, then recover at neighboring configurations.
Why This Matters
KV cache speedup collapses within anomalous configurations. Deploying LLMs on Apple Silicon can hit invisible performance cliffs that standard benchmarking will not reveal.
Critical Edge
Root cause is undiagnosed — no mitigations or workarounds proposed. The "discrete execution regimes" hypothesis is plausible but lacks internal MPS profiling. Unknown whether issue spans M1–M4 generations.
Methodology

Controlled experiments across three model families (GPT-2, BLOOM, OPT) on Apple's MPS backend. Decoding latency measured across varying sequence lengths and budgets, compared against CPU and CUDA under identical configurations. Experimental design isolates prefill vs. decode contributions, tests with/without KV cache, and systematically sweeps decoding-budget intervals to characterize anomalous spikes.

Results & Highlights
  • 21× latency spikes within specific decoding-budget intervals on MPS, with recovery at neighboring configurations
  • Reproducible across GPT-2, BLOOM, and OPT families
  • Non-monotonic behavior absent on both CPU and CUDA
  • KV caching remains beneficial overall but speedup collapses sharply within anomalous configurations
  • Cache-disabled decoding still shows residual non-monotonicity, suggesting the issue is deeper than KV cache management
Strengths
  • Surprising and practically important empirical finding
  • Rigorous cross-backend comparison eliminates many alternative explanations
  • Multiple model families strengthen generality
  • Clear isolation of decode phase vs. prefill contribution
  • Timely given growing interest in on-device inference on Apple Silicon
Limitations
  • Root cause at MPS implementation or hardware level not fully diagnosed
  • Single-author work limits depth of analysis
  • No proposed mitigations or workarounds for practitioners
  • Unclear whether issue is generation-specific (M1 vs M2 vs M3 vs M4) or universal
  • "Discrete execution regimes" hypothesis plausible but not rigorously validated
Critical Assessment
  • Novelty: High — non-monotonic latency on MPS is, to our knowledge, not previously documented at this level of detail
  • Rigor: Reasonable empirical methodology with cross-backend comparison; however, identifies phenomenon without fully diagnosing root cause
  • Reproducibility: Should be highly reproducible given Apple Silicon hardware and standard PyTorch MPS backend
  • Significance: Practically significant for anyone deploying autoregressive models on Apple Silicon
Open Questions
  • What specific MPS kernel compilation or memory allocation decisions trigger transitions between execution regimes?
  • Do these anomalies persist across different Apple Silicon generations (M1 vs M2 vs M3 vs M4)?
  • Are there practical decoding-budget thresholds practitioners can use to avoid anomalous configurations?
  • Does the phenomenon appear in other Metal-based inference frameworks beyond PyTorch MPS?
  • Could adaptive scheduling or padding of decoding budgets serve as a workaround?
