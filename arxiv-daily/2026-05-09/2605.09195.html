The Geometry of Forgetting: Temporal Knowledge Drift as an Independent Axis in LLM Representations

Rania Elbadry · Ahmed Heakl · Fan Zhang · Dani Bouch · Yuxia Wang · Preslav Nakov · Zhuohan Xie — MBZUAI, INSAIT

Any method relying on output confidence or uncertainty is structurally blind to outdated knowledge—by geometric construction. Drift lives on its own axis.

Key Finding
Temporal knowledge drift is encoded as a direction in the LLM residual stream that is geometrically orthogonal to both correctness and uncertainty. Five complementary orthogonality tests—weight cosine, score correlation, null-space projection, INLP, and DoM dissociation—all converge on the same conclusion.
Key Metric
0.83–0.95
Controlled AUROC (mean 0.89) across six instruction-tuned models. All existing baselines: 0.49–0.57.
Why This Matters
The MLP retrieval circuit produces near-identical activation trajectories for stale recall and confabulation (r > 0.81). Output confidence cannot tell them apart. This gives a structural explanation for why every confidence-based hallucination detector fails on temporal drift.
Critical Edge
The drift direction is latent-but-activatable: ablation has zero effect on outputs, meaning the model never naturally uses this signal during inference. Immediate deployment requires an external intervention. Only 2–9B models were tested; frontier-scale behavior is unknown.

Method

  • L1-regularized linear probes on residual-stream activations at first content-token position across all layers
  • 3,511 queries over 1,342 Wikidata-verified facts with day-level tenure precision; five-cell temporal taxonomy
  • Entity-disjoint GroupShuffleSplit; controlled evaluation restricts to query_year ≥ τ+1 to remove calendar-token confound
  • Six models: Llama-2-7B, Mistral-7B, Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen-2.5-7B, Gemma-2-9B, Gemma-2-2B

Orthogonality Evidence

  • Weight cosines |cos| ≤ 0.136, median 0.033
  • Score correlations |r| ≤ 0.20
  • Null-space projection |Δ| ≤ 0.008, median 0.0006
  • INLP (k=10) removes at most 0.013 AUROC
  • DoM dissociation: trained probes suppress confounded features dominating untrained mean shifts

Cross-Cutoff Validation

  • Byte-identical inputs fed to twelve model pairs with different training cutoffs
  • Probe fires on the model whose training predates the fact's transition; stays silent otherwise
  • P(A>B) = 0.975–0.998; falsifier rate 0.0–0.2%
  • Ruled out all input-level confounds: only model-internal state differs

Mechanistic Account

  • MLP retrieval circuit: stale-recall vs. confabulation trajectory correlation r > 0.81 (mean 0.94)
  • Both failure modes peak at the same layer in all six models
  • Stale-Recall vs. Confabulation linearly separable at AUROC 0.89–0.99 despite being output-indistinguishable
  • Causal steering: amplifying drift direction increases current-holder logit, decreases stale output
  • Anachronism falsifier: probe assigns strongly negative scores to non-drifted past-holder outputs (gap 4.20–9.75 logits)

Critical Edge — Limitations

  • Four Wikidata relations with discrete factual transitions only; gradual or subjective knowledge shifts untested
  • 2–9B parameter range; orthogonality at frontier scale (70B+) explicitly left as future work
  • Supervised probe requires drift-labeled data; unsupervised variants mentioned but not demonstrated
  • Drift direction is latent-but-activatable: the model does not exploit it during normal inference
  • Models with later cutoffs (e.g. Qwen-2.5) have narrower post-cutoff windows and wider CIs
  • Five-cell taxonomy specific to "holder" facts; unclear extension to other temporal knowledge types
