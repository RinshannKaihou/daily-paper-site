Relative Kinetic Utility for Reasoning-Aware Structural Pruning in LLMs

Tianhao Qian — Magnitude pruning destroys chain-of-thought reasoning. RKU traces kinetic spikes through the depth manifold to protect the neurons that matter for logic.

Key Finding
Magnitude-based pruning falls into a “magnitude trap”: it preserves neurons with massive activations but near-zero kinetic gradients (syntactic tokens), while destroying low-activation, high-gradient neurons that route multi-hop logical reasoning. RKU flips this by integrating gradient flow across the entire depth manifold.
Key Metric
13.34% GSM8K
40% sparsity · Wanda: 4.85% · Taylor-FO: 6.90%
Why This Matters
The magnitude-trap diagnosis explains why structurally pruned LLMs lose reasoning ability wholesale. This insight will shape how future compression methods identify and protect logical pathways — a prerequisite for deploying pruned models in any reasoning-heavy task.
Critical Edge
13.34% on GSM8K at 40% sparsity is still catastrophic degradation (unpruned Qwen-2.5-7B scores 70%+). RKU delays collapse, it does not prevent it. The method also trades 2–4% static knowledge accuracy (PIQA, WinoGrande) for reasoning preservation, and remains untested beyond 7B–8B scale.

Method Pipeline

1
Replace the discrete loss. Swap cross-entropy for a continuous energy functional: L = ||H^(L)||_2^2 over final hidden states. This eliminates bias toward high-frequency syntactic tokens.
2
Integrate kinetic utility via AGF. Compute a depth-continuous integral of gradient-weighted activations. This highlights “kinetic spikes” — neurons with high gradient flow that route logical inference.
3
Fisher trace normalization. Normalize within the local Riemannian subspace to suppress kinetic noise under extreme compression. Ablation: removing this step collapses AQuA to 0.00%.
4
Rank and prune. Score each FFN intermediate dimension by S_RKU = ||W||_2 * U_normalized, then remove lowest-scoring structures to target sparsity.

Knowledge vs. Logic Trade-off

RKU deliberately sacrifices static knowledge retrieval accuracy (2–4% on PIQA, WinoGrande, ARC-C) to preserve reasoning pathways. The perplexity penalty is consistent across all sparsity levels:

Magnitude methods show high in-domain recovery but poor OOD transfer — evidence of shortcut learning. RKU shows the opposite pattern.

Strengths

  • Magnitude-trap analysis provides a clean mechanistic explanation: two distinct neuron clusters (syntactic vs. kinetic spike) visible in activation-vs-gradient plots
  • IoU topological analysis shows a clear phase transition at ~40% sparsity; Wanda diverges faster in deep routing layers (Layer 26 IoU drops to 61.9%)
  • SVD rank analysis confirms Wanda causes exponential singular value decay (rank collapse) while RKU maintains a heavy-tailed spectrum
  • Ablation removing Fisher normalization collapses to 0% on AQuA — strong evidence it is not decorative
  • Honest reporting of knowledge-logic trade-off; RKU does worse on PIQA/WinoGrande and the paper acknowledges this

Red Flags & Limitations

  • Absolute performance at 40% sparsity is dismal (13.34% GSM8K vs. ~70%+ unpruned); framing as “2.75x survival” masks this
  • Only tested on 7B–8B models; structural pruning matters most at 70B+ where gradient computation becomes expensive
  • 150-step LoRA is unrealistically short; unclear if OOD advantage persists at 1000+ steps
  • No code released; calibration data and epsilon values underspecified
  • Dense theoretical framing (Riemannian manifolds, kinetic integrals) may obscure what is essentially a gradient-normalization heuristic

Hardware & Speedup

All experiments on 8x NVIDIA RTX 4090 (consumer-grade). At 50% sparsity, RKU-pruned Qwen-2.5-7B achieves a 1.42x wall-clock prefill speedup (0.1519s → 0.1066s). No deployment-framework validation (vLLM, TensorRT-LLM, etc.) provided.
