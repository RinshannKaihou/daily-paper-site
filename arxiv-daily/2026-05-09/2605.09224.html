SMIXAE: Towards Unsupervised Manifold Discovery in Language Models

Key Finding: By extending sparse autoencoders into a 4-layer mixture-of-experts architecture with 3D bottlenecks, SMIXAE directly discovers multidimensional manifold features in LLM activations -- rings, helices, and linear structures -- that standard SAEs fundamentally cannot capture. On Gemma 2 (2B & 9B), it recovers known manifold structures and reveals previously unidentified representations.

Why This Matters

Standard SAEs decompose activations into independent 1D features, making them structurally incapable of representing the multidimensional manifolds that increasingly appear in large language models. SMIXAE fills this gap with a practical architecture that can unsupervisedly recover ring, helix, and linear structures -- providing a new lens into how LLMs organize periodic, ordinal, and cyclic concepts internally.

Each expert: independent 2-layer encoder + 3D bottleneck. BatchTopK selects active experts by L2 norm.
Design rationale
The 3D bottleneck forces extra parameter capacity to model nonlinear structures rather than over-optimizing reconstruction. BatchTopK over expert L2 norms ensures whole experts activate sparsely. A novel auxiliary loss (styled after JumpReLU pre-activation loss) prevents dead experts without inducing feature splitting -- a known problem with standard TopK auxiliary losses.
Critical Edge
  • No causal verification -- all results are correlational; unknown whether discovered structures are functionally relevant to model behavior
  • Fails on toy models -- SMIXAE does not recover multidimensional features in synthetic setups, raising questions about generality
  • Fixed 3D bottleneck -- cannot capture features requiring >3 dimensions; the choice of d=3 is motivated by visualization convenience, not principled analysis
  • Expert sharing problem -- multiple independent features may occupy a single expert, complicating interpretability
  • Small evaluation sample -- unsupervised assessment uses only 10 random experts per model

Novel Discoveries

  • Temperature pinching: Expert 485 (Gemma 2 9B, layer 11) shows a "pinched" representation where moderate temperatures cluster together spatially while extreme temperatures (hot/cold) group separately -- a non-linear organization not previously documented.
  • Model/layer variation: Gemma 2 9B layer 11 has the most structurally aligned experts (6/7 tasks), followed by layer 20 (4/7) and Gemma 2 2B layer 12 (3/7), suggesting manifold density varies substantially across layers.
  • Interpretable random experts: 7-10 out of 10 randomly sampled experts are interpretable per model, with a sizable fraction exhibiting approximately linear structure in the 9B model.
  • Reconstruction discipline: Despite vastly more parameters than GemmaScope SAEs, SMIXAE matches (not exceeds) reconstruction performance, validating that the bottleneck prevents over-optimization.

Strengths

  • Clean solution to a real problem: directly addresses the well-known inability of SAEs to model multidimensional features
  • Strong visual evidence: bottleneck activations clearly show ring, helix, and linear structures aligned with semantic concepts
  • Positive control validation: recovers known manifolds from prior work (Sinii & Balagansky 2026 newline helices)
  • Genuine novelty: discovers previously undocumented representation structures (temperature pinching)
  • Practical auxiliary loss: prevents dead experts without inducing feature splitting -- useful beyond the main architecture
  • Honest limitations: thorough discussion of failures, missing work, and complications

Context & Credibility

  • Author: Collin Francel (single author, anonymous submission)
  • Venue: Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop, ICML 2026
  • Models tested: Gemma 2 2B (layer 12), Gemma 2 9B (layers 11, 20)
  • Hardware: single NVIDIA A100; same hyperparameters across all model/layer combinations
  • Framework: SAELens; code, models, and visualizations publicly available
  • Theoretical grounding: connects SMIXAE to the multidimensional Linear Representation Hypothesis (mLRH), showing SMIXAE's hypothesis space contains approximations to the mLRH decomposition
  • Note: ambitious scope for a single author; independent verification of some findings would strengthen confidence
