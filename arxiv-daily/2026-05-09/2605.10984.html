Principle-Guided Supervision for Interpretable Uncertainty in Medical Image Segmentation

An Sui, Yuzhu Li, Gunter Schumann, Fuping Wu, Xiahai Zhuang venueIEEE TPAMI (under review) affilFudan University / Imperial College London
Key Finding
PriUS is the only method among six baselines (DEviS, PU, UDrop, TTA, EU) to preserve correct uncertainty–ambiguity correlation signs across all three perception-aligned principles — contrast, corruption, geometry — on ACDC, ISIC, and WHS. All other methods violate at least one expected relationship.
Key Metric
7.45 mm
Best HD95 on ACDC (cardiac) | also best HD95 on WHS: 5.49 mm

Why This Matters
Existing uncertainty methods produce spatially incoherent maps that clinicians cannot interpret. PriUS constrains uncertainty to reflect human-understandable ambiguity sources — low boundary contrast, image corruption, geometric complexity — making the model's doubt structurally legible rather than opaque.
Critical Edge
Supervision coefficients (λg, λσ, λf) vary ~10× across datasets, raising deployment concerns. Corruption supervision uses synthetic Gaussian noise, not clinically realistic artifacts. No evidence yet that improved interpretability translates to better downstream clinical decisions. TTA's anomalously poor ACDC performance (70.14% DSC) is unexplained.
Method — Three Principles, Gated Losses

Contrast (Lg)

For boundary-adjacent same-class pixel pairs, penalize violations of inverse ordering between image gradient magnitude and uncertainty. Sigmoid-gated to near-boundary regions.

Corruption (Lσ)

Three levels of Gaussian noise added to input. Hinge-style ranking loss enforces monotonic uncertainty growth as noise increases. Gating suppresses homogeneous interiors.

Geometry (Ld)

Two-regime loss using boundary distance maps: near-boundary, uncertainty should decrease with distance; in homogeneous interiors, jointly suppress toward zero. Smooth regime transition.

Per-Principle Evidence & Ablation

Contrast

PriUS shows a regression slope of −9.0 × 10³ between image gradient and uncertainty. All other methods show positive slopes — meaning their uncertainty increases where boundaries are sharp, the opposite of expected behavior.

Corruption

Under increasing Gaussian noise, PriUS shows dominant positive Δu shift with the smallest proportion of negative changes. Competing methods produce symmetric or noise-insensitive uncertainty distributions.

Geometry

Uncertainty–boundary-distance slope: −2.6 × 10² (PriUS) vs. ~0 (all others). Removing geometry loss causes the most severe degradation: DSC drops from 91.05% to 90.65%, HD95 rises to 31.79 mm when combined with corruption removal.

Ablation Summary

  • Full model: 91.05% DSC, 7.45 mm HD95, correct UCC on all principles
  • Remove Lg: contrast UCC flips sign
  • Remove Lσ: corruption behavior breaks down
  • Remove Ld: most severe segmentation degradation
  • d0=16 causes training failure (DSC 25.75%)
Assessment

Strengths

  • Elegant three-principle formalization with principled gating and mathematically well-defined losses
  • Only method preserving all three uncertainty–ambiguity relationships across all datasets
  • Best HD95 on cardiac datasets, suggesting genuine boundary quality improvement
  • Comprehensive ablation demonstrating complementarity of all supervision terms
  • Honest reporting of failure mode at d0=16
  • New metrics (UCC, UR) fill a gap in quantitative interpretability evaluation

Limitations

  • Dataset-specific hyperparameters vary ~10×, raising deployment concerns
  • Synthetic Gaussian noise, not clinically realistic artifacts
  • Baselines exclude PhiSeg, Post-Net, and modern calibrated EDL methods
  • TTA's anomalously poor ACDC performance (70.14%) unexplained
  • No validation that improved interpretability aids clinical decisions
  • UCC/UR metrics are self-proposed without external validation against expert annotations
