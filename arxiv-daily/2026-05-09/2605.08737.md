> **一句话总结：** 本文发现在策略蒸馏中，奖励外推系数超过某个闭式阈值后会导致结构化输出格式崩溃，推导了该阈值 lambda*(p,b,c) 的解析公式，并通过三组预注册实验在Amazon Fashion数据集上精确验证，使1.7B Qwen3学生模型达到8B-SFT基线的域内性能。

> **TL;DR:** On-policy distillation (OPD) with reward extrapolation (lambda > 1) can help a student surpass the teacher, but beyond a closed-form threshold lambda*(p,b,c) -- determined by teacher modal probability, warm-start mass, and IS clip strength -- structured outputs collapse (invalid JSON). On Amazon Fashion K=8 listwise JSON ranking, the predicted cliff at lambda* approximately 1.22 matches observed parse-rate collapse within one grid step across three pre-registered tests. NDCG@1 on parsed outputs remains flat across lambda, confirming the gain is entirely from format adherence. A 1.7B Qwen3 student matches a pre-registered 3-seed 8B-SFT baseline at one-fifth the parameters.

## 核心贡献

- 推导了在策略蒸馏中奖励外推系数的闭式安全阈值 lambda*(p,b,c)（Theorem 4.1），由教师模态概率 p、热启动质量 b 和重要性采样裁剪强度 c 三个可测量量决定
- 揭示了超过阈值后训练从"格式保持"到"格式崩溃"的相变现象，该现象由IS裁剪非对称性驱动，是累积漂移而非单步裁剪事件
- 将理论从单位置Bernoulli约简（Lem. C.4 的off-modal-ratio不变性条件）推广到多token序列级别，给出可证安全的上界（Prop. 4.3(A)）和校准的操作规则（Prop. 4.3(B)）
- 在Amazon Fashion K=8列表式JSON排序上进行了三组预注册实验验证：细网格悬崖区间、预算扩展测试、c=1.5跨裁剪预测，全部落在锁定的预测窗口内
- c=1.5 预注册实验在 N=200 预算下观测到的悬崖中点 1.069 与闭式预测 1.070 的误差仅为 0.0005
- 使1.7B Qwen3学生模型以五分之一的参数量达到8B-SFT基线的域内性能（useful: 0.857 vs 0.833），且种子方差降低约5倍
- 在JSONSchemaBench、MBPP、BFCL、GSM8K、MS MARCO等多个任务上进行了scope check，明确界定了理论的适用与不适用范围

## Key Contributions

- Derives a closed-form clip-safety threshold lambda*(p,b,c) for on-policy distillation extrapolation (Theorem 4.1), determined by three measurable quantities: teacher modal probability p, warm-start mass b, and importance-sampling clip strength c
- Identifies a sharp phase transition from format-preserving to format-collapsing training above the threshold, driven by IS-clip asymmetry through cumulative drift (not discrete clip events)
- Extends the Bernoulli reduction to multi-token sequences via the off-modal-ratio invariance condition (Lemma C.4), providing a provable safety bound (Prop. 4.3(A)) and a calibrated operating rule (Prop. 4.3(B))
- Three pre-registered tests on Amazon Fashion all land within locked prediction windows: 5-seed fine-grid cliff onset CI [1.204, 1.228] containing predicted lambda*=1.22; N=200 budget extension at midpoint 1.061 inside locked [1.00, 1.10]; c=1.5 cross-clip midpoint 1.069 matching closed-form prediction 1.070 (error 0.0005, below grid resolution)
- Demonstrates 1.7B Qwen3 student reaches in-domain parity with 3-seed 8B-SFT baseline at 1/5 the parameters (useful: 0.857 +/- 0.016 vs 0.833 +/- 0.082), with ~5x lower seed variance
- Thorough scope checks across JSONSchemaBench, MBPP, BFCL, GSM8K, MS MARCO/TREC-DL, and Llama cross-architecture explicitly delineate where the predicate applies, abstains, or fails
- ASPO head-to-head confirms the cliff is a property of IS-clipped reverse-KL extrapolation, not method-specific: ASPO collapses at lambda=1.5 under the same protocol

## Methodology

The paper introduces ListOPD (listwise on-policy distillation), an instantiation of OPD for structured JSON listwise ranking tasks. The core methodology:

**1. Listwise PL Rollout:** For each product with K=8 reviews, the model autoregressively generates a JSON list of K objects with review_id and score fields. Under the Plackett-Luce model, the joint likelihood factors into position-conditional softmaxes that arise naturally from token-level autoregression.

**2. On-Policy Reverse-KL with Extrapolation:** The per-token ListOPD advantage is a base-relative teacher-student log-ratio (Eq. 2): A(s,a;lambda) = lambda * (log pi_T - log pi_B) - (log pi_S - log pi_B), where lambda >= 1 is the extrapolation coefficient. Setting lambda=1 recovers vanilla reverse-KL; lambda>1 targets a sharpened teacher distribution proportional to pi_B * (pi_T/pi_B)^lambda. The student is updated via GRPO with token-level IS correction clipped at c=5.0.

**3. Theoretical Framework:**
- **Bernoulli Reduction (Thm. 4.1):** At a structural position with teacher modal probability p, warmstart b, and clip c, the clip-safe threshold is lambda*(p,b,c) = [log((1-p)/(c-1+p)) - log((1-b)/b)] / [log((1-p)/p) - log((1-b)/b)]. Above this threshold, the extrapolated fixed point exits the clip-safe region.
- **Sequence-level Lift (Prop. 4.3):** The binding (most-concentrated) structural position pins the cliff via monotonicity. Two predictions: (A) provable safety using p_safe >= max p_t, and (B) empirical operating scale using p_typ (mean over tau-filtered positions).
- **Finite-budget Drift (Cor. 4.2):** The characteristic first-passage time to the clip boundary scales as O(1/[eta * lambda * p * (1-p)]), so the cliff shifts leftward as training lengthens.

**4. Calibration on Fashion:** With p_typ=0.9993, b=0.81, c=5, the predicted bracket [lambda_safe*=1.18, lambda_typ*=1.28] contains the observed onset window [1.15, 1.25] within one grid step.

**5. Experimental Setup:** Qwen3 models (0.6B, 1.7B, 4B, 8B), SFT-warmstarted on Fashion PL-K8 for 5 epochs. OPD trained with AdamW, lr=1e-6, batch=128, on 8 B200 GPUs. Evaluation uses strict review_id-aligned JSON parsing with zero credit on parse failure.

## Results & Highlights

- **Cliff Localization:** Parse rate collapses sharply between lambda=1.20 (0.868) and lambda=1.25 (0.651), within one grid step of predicted lambda*=1.22. NDCG@1 on parsed outputs is statistically flat across the entire sweep (p=0.61).
- **Pre-registered 5-seed CI:** Parse >= 0.80 onset at 95% bootstrap CI [1.204, 1.228], containing the closed-form prediction lambda*=1.22.
- **Budget Extension (N=200):** Pre-registered locked bracket [1.00, 1.10]; observed cliff midpoint at 1.061 (single-seed), 1.068 (3-seed mean) -- both inside.
- **Cross-clip Prediction (c=1.5, N=200):** Closed-form predicts lambda_typ*=1.070; observed midpoint between lambda=1.05 (parse 0.939) and lambda=1.075 (parse 0.632) is 1.0695, matching the formula within 0.0005.
- **Parameter Efficiency:** 1.7B-ListOPD (useful=0.857 +/- 0.016) matches 3-seed 8B-SFT (0.833 +/- 0.082) within seed noise. The gain comes from format adherence (parse 0.33 -> 0.92), not ranking quality.
- **Constrained Decoding Comparison:** Five strict-K constrained systems raise 1.7B-SFT parse to 0.783; unconstrained ListOPD reaches 0.943. Post-hoc permutation repair closes 82% of the gap but leaves a 0.051 residual.
- **ASPO Head-to-head:** ASPO at lambda=1.0 is comparable to ListOPD lambda=1.15 within seed noise, but collapses at lambda=1.5, confirming the cliff is mechanism-driven, not GRPO-specific.
- **Scope Checks:** MBPP shows analogous peak near Fashion marker; JSONSchemaBench K=4 list hits predicted bracket but with attenuated cliff sharpness; GSM8K/BFCL/Llama-1B are correctly identified as out-of-scope (diffuse scaffolding, parse saturation, insufficient budget).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** Deriving a closed-form safety threshold for reward extrapolation in structured-output distillation is genuinely novel. The phase-transition framing is clean and the Bernoulli reduction is elegant. The observation that NDCG@1 is invariant while parse rate collapses is an important empirical finding.
- **Rigor: Very strong.** Pre-registered experiments with locked prediction windows are rare in ML. The proofs are complete for the single-position case (Thm. 4.1), with honest caveats about the multi-token lift being approximate and the super-critical dynamics being empirical (not almost-sure). The c=1.5 match at 0.0005 error is striking.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** The threshold depends on three measurable quantities with clear measurement protocols. Compute is modest (~12 GPU-hours total, 10 min per 3-epoch run on 8 B200). Public project page with verification artifacts. However, full training launchers are omitted from the initial release.
- **Significance: High for practitioners doing LLM distillation for structured output tasks.** The finding that gains come from format adherence rather than ranking quality is practically important. The lambda* formula gives a concrete operating rule.
- **Red flags / Concerns:**
  - The parity claim depends on a Gemini-graded rubric with acknowledged contamination exposure. The authors are transparent about this but it limits the strength of the parameter-efficiency claim.
  - Scope is narrow: near-deterministic structured outputs with a single dominant binding equivalence class. The paper honestly shows this -- GSM8K, BFCL, and JSONSchemaBench single-instance are all out of scope -- but this means the theory does not yet generalize to free-form generation.
  - The no-base ablation (S2b) shows no cliff at N=42, meaning the finite-budget cliff depends on implementation details, not just the asymptotic fixed point. This limits the formula's universality.
  - The Llama cross-architecture test fails to reproduce the cliff even at N=200, suggesting architecture-specific reachability is a confound.
  - The c-axis N=42 sweep (Tab. 16) shows non-monotone behavior (lowest parse at c=2, not c=1.5), contradicting the qualitative prediction. The paper honestly demotes this to qualitative.
  - The paper is dense and notation-heavy (40 pages with appendices), which may limit practical adoption.

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical derivation with a closed-form, interpretable threshold requiring only three measurable quantities
- Pre-registered experiments with locked prediction windows and explicit decision rules -- a gold standard for empirical ML research
- Honest and thorough scope delineation: the paper explicitly reports where the predicate applies, abstains, and fails
- Rubric-independent cliff diagnostic cleanly separated from rubric-dependent parity claim
- The c=1.5 cross-clip prediction matching at 0.0005 error is a remarkably precise empirical validation
- ASPO head-to-head confirms mechanism generality rather than claiming method superiority
- Six pre-registered ablations systematically rule out alternative explanations
- Practical impact demonstrated with real models across multiple Qwen3 sizes and tasks

## Limitations

- Scope limited to near-deterministic structured outputs with a single dominant binding equivalence class; does not apply to free-form text, math CoT (GSM8K), or already-saturated formats (BFCL)
- The multi-token lift (Prop. 4.3(B)) is a calibrated operating rule, not a theorem; it relies on the off-modal-ratio invariance condition (Lem. C.4)
- The no-base implementation shows no cliff at N=42, demonstrating finite-budget reachability depends on the specific clipped estimator, not just the algebraic fixed point
- Llama cross-architecture test fails to reproduce the cliff at N=200, indicating architecture-specific reachability issues
- The c-axis N=42 sweep (Tab. 16) shows non-monotone behavior contradicting the qualitative prediction
- Parity claim relies on Gemini as evaluator, inheriting any biases from its pretraining data; cross-domain parity does not hold (8B-SFT retains edge on Baby/Software)
- The paper's density (40 pages, heavy notation) may limit practical adoption
- The entropy bonus regularizer causes parse collapse at gamma=0.001 despite the theorem predicting no lambda* shift -- an orthogonal failure mode the theory does not cover
- Compute hardware (8x B200) may be inaccessible for many researchers, though actual GPU-hours are modest

## Relevance

本文对LLM知识蒸馏中奖励外推的理论分析具有重要参考价值，尤其对于需要结构化输出的蒸馏场景（如JSON生成任务）具有直接的实践指导意义。闭式安全阈值为实际部署提供了可计算的操作规则。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the lambda* threshold be extended to free-form text generation or non-structured tasks by identifying analogous "structural positions" in reasoning chains?
- What is the interplay between the IS-clip cliff and other OPD failure modes documented in concurrent work (Fu et al. 2026)?
- How does the threshold behave under mixed-objective training (e.g., EOPD's entropy-conditional forward-KL), where the analytical prediction is that lambda* is unchanged but post-cliff dynamics may differ?
- Can the cumulative-drift mechanism be leveraged for early stopping or adaptive lambda scheduling during training?
- Is the single-binding-class precondition a fundamental limitation, or can the theory be extended to heterogeneous schemas via mixture-of-equivalence-class analysis?
- What role does the base policy choice play in non-listwise tasks, and can the weak base-dependence observed on Fashion be proven more generally?
- How does the cliff interact with constrained decoding at inference time -- does combining sub-threshold OPD with light grammar constraints give strictly additive improvements?
