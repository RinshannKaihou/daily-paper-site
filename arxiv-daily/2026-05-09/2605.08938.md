> **一句话总结：** 本文证明固定权重与网格后 FNO 的谱卷积是线性映射，从而将整个前向传播编译为 Z3 的分段线性算术，在 10 个一维平流-扩散-反应 PDE 代理模型上获得了首个神经网络 PDE 算子的形式化正性证明，并揭示了精确编码与冻结编码之间的 soundness-scalability 权衡。

> **TL;DR:** The authors prove that a trained FNO with fixed weights has a piecewise-linear forward pass (spectral convolution is a linear map), enabling exact compilation into Z3's linear real arithmetic. On 10 small 1D advection-diffusion-reaction FNO surrogates (85-117 params, grids 8-32), they achieve the first sound formal positivity proofs for neural PDE operators (on linear models at grids up to 16), find mass-violation counterexamples worse than gradient-based and Monte Carlo methods on 7/10 models, and characterize the boundary where exact verification times out (ReLU models) versus where the approximate frozen encoding scales (grid 64) at the cost of soundness.

## 核心贡献

- 证明固定网格和权重后，FNO 的谱卷积（DFT -> 模态缩放 -> IDFT）是线性映射，可构造显式谱矩阵 W^spec，使整个前向传播为分段线性函数（命题 1）。
- 提出两种 Z3 编码：**精确编码**将谱矩阵编译为稠密矩阵乘法（O(N^2 H^2 L) 项，保证正确性）；**冻结编码**用参考输入处的常量替代谱路径（O(N H^2 L) 项，更快但仅验证近似模型）。
- 获得**首个神经网络 PDE 算子的形式化证明**：在两个线性 FNO（L1-N8, L1-N16）上给出正性证书，证明对所有满足约束的光滑正输入，输出非负。
- 在质量非递增验证中，Z3 在 10/10 模型上找到违反反例，且在 7/10 模型上反例比梯度优化和 Monte Carlo 更严重（1.1-1.6x 和 1.2-2.5x）。
- 明确刻画了 soundness-scalability 边界：精确编码在 N<=16 线性模型可证明，N=32 ReLU 模型超时；冻结编码可达 N=64 但丧失对原始 FNO 的形式化保证。

## Key Contributions

- Prove that once the grid and weights are fixed, the FNO spectral convolution is a composition of linear maps (DFT, learned mode-wise scaling, IDFT), yielding an explicit spectral matrix W^spec with validation error below 10^{-15} (Proposition 1).
- Introduce two Z3 encodings: **exact encoding** compiles the spectral matrix into dense matrix multiplication with O(N^2 H^2 L) terms (sound for proofs and counterexamples); **frozen encoding** replaces the spectral path with a constant evaluated at a reference input with O(N H^2 L) terms (faster but only certifies the frozen surrogate, not the original FNO).
- Obtain the **first sound formal proofs for a neural PDE operator**: positivity certificates for two linear (ReLU-free) FNOs at grids N=8 and N=16, certifying non-negative output for all smooth positive inputs.
- For mass non-increase verification, Z3 finds sound counterexamples on all 10 models; on 7/10 models, Z3's counterexamples are 1.1-1.6x more severe than gradient falsification and 1.2-2.5x more severe than Monte Carlo (5K samples).
- Characterize the soundness-scalability boundary: exact proofs work for small linear models (N<=16, <3s); exact counterexamples extend to moderate ReLU models (N<=32); ReLU positivity proofs time out due to 2^{NH} activation patterns; frozen encoding reaches N=64 with sub-second checks but sacrifices formal soundness.

## Methodology

The paper targets Fourier Neural Operators (FNOs) for 1D advection-diffusion-reaction equations with periodic boundary conditions. The core technical insight is:

1. **Spectral matrix construction:** For a fixed grid of size N and hidden width H, the spectral convolution K(h) = IFFT(R * FFT(h)) is linear in h. By applying this operation to each standard basis vector and stacking outputs, one obtains an explicit real matrix W^spec of dimension NH x NH. Validation against the FFT code path shows discrepancies below 10^{-15}.

2. **Piecewise-linear representation:** Combined with affine bypass maps and ReLU activations (which are piecewise-linear), the entire FNO is a piecewise-linear map (Proposition 1). This means it can be exactly represented in Z3's linear real arithmetic without any approximation of the forward pass.

3. **Two encodings:**
   - **Exact:** Merges spectral matrix and bypass into one affine expression per layer. Each ReLU is encoded as an if-then-else term in Z3. UNSAT proves no admissible input violates the property; SAT returns a valid real counterexample. Cost: O(N^2 H^2 L) terms.
   - **Frozen:** Evaluates the spectral path at a reference input and substitutes as a constant. This is a zeroth-order substitution (not a linearization), reducing terms to O(N H^2 L). Faster but the SMT problem now describes the frozen surrogate, not the original FNO. Frozen results must be verified on the original model.

4. **Verification properties:**
   - **Mass non-increase:** M(f(u)) <= M(u) + epsilon (epsilon=0.05), reflecting physical mass conservation under nonnegative reaction.
   - **Positivity:** min_i f(u)_i >= 0 for all smooth inputs with u_i >= 0.1.
   - Input constraints define a continuous family: 0 <= u_i <= 5, |u_{i+1} - u_i| <= 15/N (smoothness), plus periodic boundary condition.

5. **Experimental setup:** 10 FNO surrogates trained on 1D ADR with random-feature regression (frozen hidden layers, projection layer fit by least squares on 500 samples). Grid sizes N in {8,16,32}, hidden width H=2, depths L=1 (6 linear models) and L=2 (4 ReLU models), yielding 85-117 parameters. Baselines: Monte Carlo (5K samples) and gradient-based falsification (finite-difference projected ascent, 10 restarts x 100 steps).

## Results & Highlights

- **Mass non-increase (exact encoding):** Z3 finds sound counterexamples on all 10 models with violation severity 0.12-0.64. Z3 produces the most severe counterexample on 7/10 models. Solve times: <0.1s at N=8 to 1.8s at N=16 L=2.
- **Positivity (exact encoding):** 2 sound proofs on linear models (L1-N8 in 0.1s, L1-N16 in 2.3s); 4 sound counterexamples on remaining linear models; 1 sound counterexample on a ReLU model (L2-N8, min output -0.063); 3 ReLU positivity queries time out due to 2^{16} activation pattern case splits.
- **Gradient falsification as strong baseline:** On ReLU models, gradient search finds violations in all 4 cases (min output -0.088 to -0.577), sometimes more severe than Z3. The authors acknowledge their finite-difference implementation is not the strongest possible baseline.
- **Frozen encoding:** Scales to N=32-64 with H in {2,4,8} (117-3249 parameters). Positivity certified on all 8 frozen surrogates in 0.1-2.2s. Mass counterexamples on 4/8 models in 0.03-7.5s; 3/4 confirmed on original model, 1 not confirmed (severity 0.02 < epsilon). Positivity proofs are approximate, not formal guarantees for original FNOs.
- **Soundness-scalability boundary clearly mapped:** Linear model proofs up to N=16; counterexamples up to N=32; ReLU proofs time out; frozen encoding reaches N=64 without soundness.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. The spectral matrix construction is simple once stated, but compiling FNOs into exact SMT form and obtaining the first formal proofs for neural PDE operators is a genuine contribution. The piecewise-linear insight is specific to FNOs and not trivially transferable to other neural operators (e.g., DeepONets).
- **Rigor:** High. The paper is remarkably honest about limitations. The exact encoding is validated against the FFT code path (error < 10^{-15}). Counterexamples are re-verified on the original model. The discussion section explicitly lists what is and is not demonstrated.
- **Reproducibility:** Likely high. The FNO construction, spectral matrix procedure, and Z3 encoding are described in sufficient detail. The authors promise code and data upon publication.
- **Significance:** Currently limited by scale (85-117 parameters, grids 8-32), but the conceptual contribution is important: it establishes that FNOs are in principle amenable to formal verification and maps the practical boundary. The path-to-scale discussion (integration with alpha-beta-CROWN, Marabou) is well-reasoned.
- **Red flags:**
  - Models use random-feature regression (frozen hidden layers, least-squares projection), not standard end-to-end training. This limits the practical relevance of the trained models, though the compilation method is training-agnostic.
  - The gradient falsification baseline uses finite differences rather than autograd; a PyTorch implementation with Adam would likely be stronger, potentially closing the gap with Z3 on counterexample severity.
  - Only 1D PDEs tested; extension to 2D/3D would dramatically increase the spectral matrix size (e.g., N=128, H=32 yields 4096x4096 matrices).
  - The frozen encoding, while practically useful, verifies a different model than the original FNO. Its value as an "approximate certificate" is not rigorously bounded (no Lipschitz-based error analysis).

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical contribution: the spectral matrix construction and piecewise-linearity proof are elegant and correct.
- First formal proofs for neural PDE operators, establishing a new research direction at the intersection of formal methods and scientific ML.
- Honest, thorough characterization of the soundness-scalability boundary with clear tables and figures.
- Useful comparison showing Z3 finds more severe counterexamples than sampling-based methods on linear models.
- Well-written discussion that charts a clear path to scale through integration with specialized neural-network verifiers.

## Limitations

- Scalability is severely limited: exact encoding handles only 85-117 parameter models on grids 8-32. Production FNOs (N>=128, H>=32) would produce spectral matrices of dimension 4096x4096 or larger.
- ReLU positivity proofs time out even at grid size 8-16 due to exponential case splits; this is a fundamental limitation of the piecewise-linear encoding approach with general-purpose SMT solvers.
- Models are trained with random-feature regression, not standard end-to-end gradient descent, raising questions about whether the verified models are representative of practically deployed FNOs.
- Only tested on 1D advection-diffusion-reaction; no evaluation on 2D problems or more complex PDE systems.
- The frozen encoding does not provide bounded approximation guarantees for the original FNO; the gap between frozen and original model behavior is not characterized.
- No comparison with specialized neural-network verification tools (Marabou, alpha-beta-CROWN, ERAN) that might handle the piecewise-linear structure more efficiently than Z3.

## Relevance

本文将形式化验证方法应用于神经 PDE 算子，与科学机器学习中模型可信性研究相关。虽然当前仅验证了小规模模型，但其提出的 FNO 分段线性编译方法和 soundness-scalability 分析为后续可扩展验证工具的开发提供了理论基础。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can specialized neural-network verifiers (alpha-beta-CROWN, Marabou) handle the compiled piecewise-linear FNO representation more efficiently than Z3, particularly for ReLU models where Z3 times out?
- For the frozen encoding, can Lipschitz-based error bounds be derived to provide guaranteed approximation quality relative to the original FNO?
- How does the spectral matrix size scale for 2D problems (e.g., N=64x64 grid), and can spatial decomposition or layer-wise verification reduce the constraint complexity?
- Would end-to-end trained FNOs (rather than random-feature regression) exhibit different verification properties, e.g., stronger physical constraint satisfaction that makes verification easier?
- Can the compilation approach be extended to other neural operators with spectral components (e.g., U-FNO, SFNO) or to architectures with non-piecewise-linear activations (e.g., GELU, Swish)?
