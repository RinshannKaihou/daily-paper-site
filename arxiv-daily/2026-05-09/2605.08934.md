> **一句话总结：** 本文提出基于范畴论的"组合可解释性"框架，将神经网络解释定义为必须交换的语法-语义映射对，通过MDL将解释质量分解为忠实度与复杂度，引入压缩精炼机制重构模型结构，并在解释乐观主义和组合稀疏性原则下证明语法压缩可保证产生更简洁的人类对齐解释。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces "compositional interpretability," a category-theoretic framework that formalizes mechanistic interpretability of neural networks. It defines interpretations as commuting syntax-semantics pairs in a four-category setup (D, S, C, H), decomposes explanation quality into faithfulness and compositional description length (representation + interpretation complexity), and introduces compressive refinement -- a syntactic restructuring that preserves semantics while reducing representation cost. The authors prove a parsimony criterion guaranteeing that compressive refinement yields more concise explanations when the coding distribution is comonotonic with interpretation complexity, and show that existing methods (sparse autoencoders, transcoders, crosscoders) are all special cases of refinement.

## 核心贡献

- **提出"组合可解释性"（Compositional Interpretability）范畴论框架**：构建四元组(D, S, C, [[-]])模型，其中D为字符串图（接线结构）、S为语法范畴、C为语义范畴、[[-]]为语义grounding函子；解释I_S: S -> H必须满足交换条件确保语法分解与观测行为一致
- **区分行为忠实度与解释忠实度**：行为忠实度仅要求输入输出匹配，而解释忠实度要求解释的中间步骤与模型内部机制对齐；进一步提出"组合忠实度"（Definition 3.1），要求能从解释重建每个组件的语义
- **定义组合描述长度（Definition 3.2）**：L(M, I_S) = L^rep(M) + L^int(M, I_C)，将解释复杂度分解为客观的结构代价（表示复杂度）和主观的对齐代价（解释复杂度），并证明其上界于最小描述长度
- **引入压缩精炼（Definition 4.1）**：作为函子R: S -> S'在保持语义不变的条件下重构语法，实现将黑盒组件拆解为更简单子组件的信息重分配
- **证明精炼的简洁性准则（Theorem 4.2）**：压缩精炼改善解释当且仅当表示复杂度的减少量超过解释复杂度的增加量
- **提出共单调编码推论（Corollary 4.4）**：若编码分布P^rep与解释分布共单调，则任何压缩精炼满足简洁性准则
- **统一现有方法**：将神经元级分析（identity refinement）、稀疏自编码器（piecewise substitution）、crosscoder（architectural invariants）等方法统一为精炼的子类

## Key Contributions

- Establishes a **four-category formal model** (D, S, C, [[-]]) where D is a string diagram (wiring), S is the syntactic category, C is the semantic category, and [[-]] is the semantic grounding functor; interpretations I_S: S -> H must satisfy commutativity conditions
- Distinguishes **behavioural vs. explanatory faithfulness** and introduces **compositional faithfulness** (Def 3.1): requiring component-level semantic reconstruction via a functor I_C: H -> C, with tolerance epsilon on a metric d
- Defines **compositional description length** (Def 3.2): L(M, I_S) = L^rep(M) + L^int(M, I_C), decomposing explanation cost into objective representation complexity (wiring + component semantics) and subjective interpretation complexity (human-alignment cost), proved to upper-bound minimal description length (Prop 3.3)
- Introduces **compressive refinement** (Def 4.1): a functor R: S -> S' that restructures syntax while preserving semantics [[-]] = [[-]]' o R, redistributing information from semantically complex atoms to syntactically explicit structure
- Proves the **parsimony criterion** (Theorem 4.2): a compressive refinement improves explanation quality iff the reduction in representation complexity outweighs any increase in interpretation complexity
- Derives **comonotonic coding corollary** (Corollary 4.4): any compressive refinement satisfies the parsimony criterion if the coding distribution P^rep is comonotonic with interpretation distribution
- Invokes two foundational principles: **Weak Principle of Explanatory Optimism** (Conjecture 4.3, from Ayonrinde & Jabri 2025) and **Compositional Sparsity** (Theorem 4.5, from Danhofer et al. 2025) to justify why representation compression aligns with human interpretability
- **Unifies existing methods** as subclasses: identity refinement (neuron-level analysis), piecewise substitution (SAEs), architectural invariant-based methods (crosscoders), and syntax-free methods

## Methodology

The paper develops its framework through several interconnected formal constructions:

**1. Compositional Model (Section 2):** A model M = (D, S, C, [[-]]) consists of a string diagram D in a monoidal category S (syntax), a semantic category C, and a strict monoidal functor [[-]]: S -> C providing semantic grounding. The key insight is separating the wiring structure (how components connect) from the semantics (what components compute). A compositional interpretation requires both I_S: S -> H (mapping syntax to human concepts) and I_C: H -> C (reconstructing semantics from concepts) such that the diagram commutes.

**2. Faithfulness and Complexity (Section 3):** Explanation quality is formalized through MDL as a rate-distortion problem: minimize complexity L(I_S(D)) subject to a faithfulness constraint. Rather than treating this as a soft tradeoff, the authors separate the terms. Faithfulness is operationalized as compositional faithfulness -- requiring d([f], I_C(I_S(f))) <= epsilon for all morphisms f in S. Complexity is bounded by the compositional description length L^rep(M) + L^int(M, I_C), where L^rep(M) = L(D) + sum L([[f]]) captures diagram size plus component semantic cost, and L^int captures the human-alignment cost.

**3. Compressive Refinement (Section 4):** The central operation is a syntactic refinement R: S -> S' that preserves semantics ([[D]] = [[D]]'). When L^rep(M') <= L^rep(M), it is compressive. The parsimony criterion (Theorem 4.2) gives the precise condition under which compressive refinement improves the full explanation. The Weak Principle of Explanatory Optimism is invoked to argue that the regularities isolated by lossy compression correspond to human-understandable concepts. Compositional Sparsity (Theorem 4.5) ensures the diagram grows only polynomially.

**4. Relationship to Existing Work (Section 5):** The framework is positioned relative to causal abstraction (Geiger et al. 2025), symmetry-formalized interpretability (Barbiero et al. 2026), and measurable interpretability (Madsen et al. 2024). The authors argue their framework addresses the antecedent decomposition step that causal abstraction assumes given.

## Results & Highlights

- The paper is **entirely theoretical** -- no experiments on real neural networks. The contribution is a formal mathematical framework with proven theorems.
- **Proposition 3.3:** Compositional description length upper-bounds minimal description length, providing a tractable optimization surrogate.
- **Theorem 4.2 (Parsimony Criterion):** A compressive refinement improves explanation quality iff L^rep(M) - L^rep(M') >= L^int(M', I_C) - L^int(M, I_C), making explicit the tradeoff between representation simplicity and interpretation difficulty.
- **Corollary 4.4 (Comonotonic Coding):** Provides a sufficient condition -- if the coding distribution aligns with interpretability, compressive refinement is guaranteed to help. This can be operationalized via Bayesian priors encoding sparsity, low-dimensionality, or hierarchy.
- **Theorem 4.5 (Compositional Sparsity):** Ensures that deep networks of polynomial size efficiently approximate compositionally sparse functions, bounding diagram growth.
- The framework explicitly identifies **two key tensions**: (1) representation vs. interpretation complexity, and (2) wiring vs. component complexity, and resolves each via explanatory optimism and compositional sparsity respectively.
- **Practical implications**: The authors suggest tensor networks as a potential tool for implementing automated compressive refinement, and note that integrating with causal inference (Joshi et al. 2026) is a natural next step.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** This is one of the most rigorous attempts to formalize mechanistic interpretability. The four-category model (D, S, C, [[-]]) with commuting interpretation diagrams is genuinely new. The connection between compressive refinement and existing methods (SAEs, transcoders, crosscoders) provides unifying insight. However, the individual components (category theory for computation, MDL for model selection, compositionality from linguistics) are individually well-established.
- **Rigor: High.** The paper proves formal theorems (parsimony criterion, description length bound, comonotonic coding) with proper mathematical definitions. The framework is self-consistent and well-grounded. The key conjecture (explanatory optimism) is honestly labeled as such rather than asserted as fact.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate.** The mathematical framework is fully specified, but the paper provides no code, no algorithms, and no empirical benchmarks. Reproducing the theoretical results requires category theory expertise.
- **Significance: Potentially very high, but contingent.** If the framework can be operationalized (which the authors acknowledge as future work), it could provide the formal foundation that mechanistic interpretability urgently needs. However, purely theoretical papers in ML sometimes fail to bridge the gap to practice.
- **Red flags:** (1) The paper is entirely theoretical with zero empirical validation. While the authors are upfront about this and identify it as future work, the distance from string diagrams to practical tools for interpreting LLMs is substantial. (2) The unification of existing methods as "subclasses of refinement" is elegant at the abstract level but may paper over important practical differences -- an SAE and a crosscoder solve very different engineering problems. (3) The Weak Principle of Explanatory Optimism is a conjecture, and the entire practical case for compressive refinement improving interpretability rests on it. If neural network regularities do not correspond to human concepts, the framework's guarantee collapses. (4) The paper acknowledges but does not resolve the tension that representation compression could produce components that are mathematically simple but semantically opaque to humans. (5) The MDL framework requires choosing a coding distribution P^rep, which introduces subjectivity that the framework aims to eliminate.

## Strengths

- **Exceptional conceptual clarity.** The four-category model (D, S, C, H) cleanly separates wiring, syntax, semantics, and human concepts -- a decomposition that brings genuine insight.
- **Honest about assumptions.** The authors clearly label the Weak Principle of Explanatory Optimism as a conjecture, note the two key tensions, and acknowledge the subjectivity in coding distributions.
- **Unification is genuinely illuminating.** Showing that neuron-level analysis, SAEs, transcoders, and crosscoders are all points on a spectrum of syntactic refinement (from identity refinement to syntax-free methods) is a real conceptual contribution.
- **Principled framing as optimization.** Moving interpretability from "find sparse features" to "minimize compositional description length subject to faithfulness constraints" is a significant upgrade in rigor.
- **Constructive rather than dismissive.** The paper builds on rather than attacks existing work, positioning its contributions as foundational infrastructure for the field.
- **Well-organized structure.** The paper follows a clear logical progression: define the model (Sec 2), characterize quality (Sec 3), improve via refinement (Sec 4), situate relative to alternatives (Sec 5).

## Limitations

- **No empirical validation whatsoever.** The paper is a 10-page theoretical contribution with no experiments, no case studies, and no worked examples on real neural networks.
- **The gap to practice is large.** Computing compositional interpretations for models with billions of parameters requires scalable algorithms that are not specified. The suggested direction (tensor networks) is promising but undeveloped.
- **The Explanatory Optimism conjecture is untested.** The entire practical argument rests on the assumption that model regularities are human-understandable, which is an empirical claim that could fail in practice (e.g., polysemantic neurons that resist clean decomposition).
- **Coding distribution choice is underexplored.** The comonotonic coding condition is elegant but provides little guidance on how to choose P^rep in practice.
- **The framework may be too abstract for some practitioners.** The heavy use of category theory (monoidal categories, functors, string diagrams, adjunctions) creates a significant barrier to entry for the interpretability community.
- **No discussion of computational complexity.** Even if the framework is theoretically sound, computing the optimal refinement could be intractable for realistic models.

## Relevance

本文对机械化可解释性研究具有基础性理论意义。其范畴论框架为"什么是好的解释"提供了形式化回答，将SAE、transcoder等主流方法的统一视角对可解释性研究者具有重要参考价值，特别适合关注AI安全、模型透明性及形式化方法的研究者。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What concrete algorithms can implement compressive refinement at scale? The authors suggest tensor networks -- what would a tensor-network-based SAE look like?
- Can the Weak Principle of Explanatory Optimism be empirically tested? What would a falsification look like?
- How does the framework handle attention mechanisms, residual streams, and other architectural features specific to transformers?
- What is the computational complexity of finding the optimal compressive refinement? Is it NP-hard in general?
- Can the framework be extended to handle distributional/approximate notions of compositionality, rather than requiring exact commutativity?
- How does compositional faithfulness with a semantic metric d relate to causal scrubbing and other validation methods?
- What coding distributions P^rep best encode human priors about interpretability (sparsity, hierarchy, etc.)?
- Can the framework accommodate multi-level explanations (e.g., features at different levels of abstraction)?
