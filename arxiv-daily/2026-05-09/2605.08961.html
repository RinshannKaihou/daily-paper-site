Dolphin-CN-Dialect: Where Chinese Dialects Matter

Yangyang Meng, Huihang Zhong, Guodong Lin, Guanbo Wang, Hu Du, Zhiming Shao, Yukai Huang, Ke Li, Wei-Qiang Zhang
Dataocean AI & Tsinghua University · Accepted to Interspeech · 0.4B parameters · 22 dialects

ASR Chinese Dialects Streaming Tokenizer Design Low-Resource

A 0.4B-parameter streaming ASR model closes the gap with billion-scale systems across 22 Chinese dialects—achieving 38% dialect accuracy improvement and 16% CER reduction over its baseline—while sacrificing only ≈0.2% on standard Mandarin. Three techniques make this possible: temperature-based data sampling, a redesigned 18K-token hybrid tokenizer, and a dual hotword biasing framework.

Core Techniques

  • Temperature-based sampling — pi = niα / Σnjα, with α ∈ (0,1) to interpolate between natural and uniform sampling, upweighting low-resource dialects
  • Hybrid tokenizer — Vocabulary reduced from 40K to 18,173 (character-level for Chinese, BPE for English), with 80 reserved extensible dialect token slots
  • Dual hotword biasing — Encoder-level contextual biasing (CPPN-based MHA fusion, only biasing module fine-tuned) + prompt-based decoder conditioning (dynamic prefix + two-stage filtering)
  • Engineering fixes — CTC streaming degradation (deletion errors 9.17→3.66 via short audio augmentation), GPU OOM filtering, data pipeline 50→800 MB/s

Strengths

  • Comprehensive evaluation across 22 dialects with per-dialect CER breakdowns
  • Dramatic improvement: Wu dialect 9.49% vs. Paraformer_zh 42.43%
  • Dual hotword framework well-designed with encoder + prompt modes
  • Practical engineering: CTC degradation, OOM, data pipeline fixes documented
  • Supports both streaming and non-streaming inference
  • Interspeech acceptance signals peer-review validation

Limitations

  • No systematic ablation on α values or tokenizer vocabulary sizes
  • Evaluation limited to Chinese dialects; multilingual generalization not tested
  • Streaming vs. non-streaming accuracy gap not quantified
  • Prompt-based hotword biasing is non-streaming only
  • Code-switching (Mandarin-dialect mixing) scenarios untested
  • α requires manual tuning; no automated or adaptive strategy

Open Questions

  • Can the temperature-based sampling generalize to other multilingual ASR families (Indo-European, African)?
  • How does the 80-slot dialect token reservation affect embedding quality as more dialects are added? Is there a saturation point?
  • What is the streaming vs. non-streaming accuracy gap across individual dialects?
  • Can prompt-based hotword biasing be extended to streaming mode?
  • Is there a principled way to set α based on dataset entropy or dialect count, rather than manual search?
