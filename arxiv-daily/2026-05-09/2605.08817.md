> **一句话总结：** 本文提出IMAX框架，通过训练一组软前缀（soft prefixes）来重塑冻结LLM的推理轨迹先验分布，利用信息最大化（InfoMax）奖励防止前缀坍缩为冗余控制，在数学推理基准上相比标准RLVR在Pass@4上最高提升11.60%。

> **TL;DR:** IMAX trains a pool of soft prefixes on a frozen LLM to reshape its prior over reasoning trajectories for RLVR. An InfoMax reward (variational lower bound on mutual information between prefix identity and generated response) prevents prefix collapse. Across three model scales (1.5B--8B), IMAX consistently outperforms standard prefix-tuned RLVR baselines (GRPO, DAPO) on math reasoning benchmarks, achieving up to 11.60% Pass@4 and 10.57% Avg@4 gains, at 3--6x lower GPU cost than full-parameter GRPO.

## 核心贡献

- 提出IMAX（Information-Maximizing Augmented eXploration）框架，通过优化冻结LLM上的软前缀池（soft prefix pool）来重塑推理轨迹先验分布，而非更新模型参数本身
- 推导了一个基于变分下界的InfoMax奖励，最大化前缀身份与生成响应之间的互信息，防止不同前缀坍缩为冗余控制
- 提出交替优化策略：先更新后验网络q_phi（预测哪个前缀生成了当前响应），再固定q_phi更新前缀池，遵循坐标上升变分推断（CAVI）思想
- 证明软前缀RLVR在多个数学推理基准上与全参数GRPO具有竞争力，且GPU训练成本低3-6倍
- 通过t-SNE可视化和推理成分分析，验证IMAX诱导的前缀确实产生了语义上有区分度的推理模式（如代数方法vs.分情况讨论）

## Key Contributions

- Introduces IMAX, a lightweight RLVR framework that optimizes soft prefixes on a frozen LLM to reshape reasoning trajectory priors without updating backbone parameters
- Derives a variational InfoMax reward that maximizes mutual information between prefix identity and generated response, preventing learned prefixes from collapsing into redundant controls
- Proposes alternating optimization between a posterior classifier q_phi and the prefix pool, following the spirit of coordinate ascent variational inference (CAVI)
- Demonstrates that prefix-tuned RLVR is competitive with full-parameter GRPO on math reasoning benchmarks at 3-6x lower GPU cost
- Provides qualitative analysis via t-SNE visualization and reasoning-component heatmaps showing IMAX induces semantically distinct reasoning patterns per prefix

## Methodology

**Core idea:** Rather than modifying the RL training process itself (as prior exploration methods do), IMAX shifts the optimization target to the model's prior distribution by learning a pool of soft prefixes. Each soft prefix, when prepended to the frozen LLM input, induces a distinct rollout distribution over reasoning trajectories.

**Prefix initialization:** A pool of C=2 text prompts (generated by GPT-5.4) encoding different reasoning strategies (e.g., symbolic manipulation vs. case-based analysis) are tokenized and mapped through the frozen embedding layer to initialize the soft prefixes. These are then optimized as continuous parameters.

**RLVR formulation under prefix tuning:** The objective maximizes expected verifiable reward over prefix sampling and response generation, where the policy is the frozen LLM conditioned on the selected prefix. The authors use GRPO as the base RLVR algorithm, with group-wise relative comparison within each prompt-prefix pair.

**InfoMax reward:** To prevent prefix collapse, the authors derive a variational lower bound on I(Z;Y|X) using the reverse decomposition (H(Z|X) - H(Z|X,Y)). The intractable posterior p(z|x,y) is approximated by a C-way classifier q_phi that predicts which prefix generated a given response. This classifier is implemented as a lightweight head on top of the frozen backbone's last-token hidden state. The InfoMax reward is log q_phi(z|x,y), added to the verifiable reward with weight beta=0.01.

**Alternating optimization:** For each training batch: (1) fix the prefix pool, update q_phi on prompt-response pairs (forward pass without soft prefix to prevent information leakage); (2) fix q_phi, update the prefix pool using the base RLVR algorithm with augmented rewards.

**Why reverse MI (not forward):** The authors explicitly justify choosing the reverse form over the forward form. The forward form requires marginalizing over latent prefixes, which is intractable and expensive. Using a single forward pass as an approximation creates a biased contrastive target that encourages exploiting a particular z rather than capturing true dependence between Z and Y.

## Results & Highlights

- **Three model scales tested:** Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct, Qwen3-4B-Base, Qwen3-8B-Base
- **Benchmarks:** MATH-500, GSM8K, Minerva Math, AMC (reasoning); IFEval (instruction following)
- **IMAX consistently improves over standard prefix-tuned RLVR:** On Qwen3-4B-Base, GRPO+IMAX achieves strongest Pass@4 on GSM8K, MATH-500, and AMC among prefix-tuning methods
- **Best gains:** Up to 11.60% in Pass@4 and 10.57% in Avg@4 over standard RLVR baselines
- **No degradation on general tasks:** IFEval performance is maintained, indicating prefixes do not harm general instruction following
- **Prefix-tuned RLVR is competitive with full-parameter GRPO** on several benchmarks in Pass@4, at substantially lower training cost
- **Cost efficiency:** IMAX requires 3-6x less GPU-hours than full-parameter GRPO across model scales
- **Qualitative evidence:** t-SNE shows IMAX yields more separated response-embedding clusters across prefixes; reasoning component analysis shows Prefix 1 induces more equation/algebra operations while Prefix 2 induces more subgoals/case analysis/verification
- **Effective exploration:** Oracle (combined) pass rate of two IMAX prefixes beats single best-performing prefix, showing complementary coverage of successful reasoning regions
- **Pass@K scaling:** IMAX surpasses full-parameter GRPO when K>4, indicating improved coverage of successful regions at higher rollout budgets

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty: Moderate-to-high.** The idea of optimizing soft prefixes rather than full model parameters for RLVR exploration is genuinely novel. The InfoMax reward derivation is principled (variational lower bound on MI, with explicit justification for the reverse form). However, the overall approach is a relatively natural combination of existing ideas: soft prompt tuning + RLVR + InfoGAN-style mutual information maximization.

**Rigor: Good.** The paper is unusually thorough in justifying design choices (Appendix B on why not forward MI, ablations on prefix count, training algorithm vs. SFT). The alternating optimization procedure is well-motivated by CAVI. The evaluation uses both Pass@K and Avg@K metrics with standard errors.

**Reproducibility: Good.** All hyperparameters are listed (Table 8), the algorithm is specified in pseudocode (Algorithm 1), and the framework is built on public TRL. The single-GPU setup is accessible. However, no code repository link is provided.

**Significance: Moderate.** The gains are meaningful and consistent, but the experimental scope is limited to math reasoning on Qwen models. The prefix pool size is very small (C=2 by default), raising questions about scalability. The approach addresses a real problem (entropy collapse in RLVR), but the restriction to frozen backbones means it cannot unlock capabilities absent from the base model.

**Potential concerns:**
- Only C=2 prefixes used in main experiments; the scaling ablation is limited and does not explore larger pools where the method should theoretically shine
- All experiments are on mathematical reasoning; generalization to code, science, or multi-step planning is not tested
- Prefix initialization depends on GPT-5.4 generated prompts with manual design; sensitivity to initialization quality is acknowledged as a limitation but not thoroughly ablated
- The variational posterior q_phi is a simple classifier head; how well it scales to larger prefix pools remains unclear
- The paper mentions MERCI [50] and DIVER [16] as baselines but notes they are adapted to the prefix-tuning setting using their default reward weights, which may not be optimal for this setting

## Strengths

- Clean, principled formulation: the InfoMax reward has a solid information-theoretic foundation with explicit variational derivation
- Lightweight and practical: only soft prefixes and a small classifier head are trained; backbone stays frozen
- Algorithm-agnostic: can be plugged into any RLVR algorithm (GRPO, DAPO, etc.)
- Thorough analysis: t-SNE visualization, reasoning-component heatmaps, complementary correctness analysis, runtime comparison
- Cost-efficient: 3-6x cheaper than full-parameter GRPO with competitive performance
- Good empirical gains that are consistent across three model scales and multiple benchmarks

## Limitations

- Only evaluated on math reasoning benchmarks with Qwen models; generalization to other domains is untested
- Prefix pool size is very small (C=2 default); it is unclear whether the method scales to larger, more diverse prefix pools
- Initialization depends on manually designed, GPT-generated text prompts; sensitivity to initialization quality is acknowledged but not systematically studied
- The learned soft prefixes must be prepended during inference, adding a persistent (though small) overhead
- Limited gains on general instruction-following tasks (IFEval), suggesting the prefixes are domain-specific
- No comparison with other parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods (LoRA, adapters) for RLVR
- The paper does not discuss how to select the number of prefixes C or the InfoMax weight beta for new tasks/domains

## Relevance

本文与RLVR训练中的探索问题和LLM推理能力提升直接相关。软前缀作为轻量级控制变量来引导推理分布的思路，为解决RLVR中的熵坍缩问题提供了一种低成本且理论上更有根据的替代方案，值得在实际训练pipeline中参考。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does IMAX scale with larger prefix pools (C=8, 16, 32)? Does the InfoMax reward remain effective at preventing collapse, or does the variational approximation degrade?
- Can the learned prefixes transfer across domains (e.g., from math reasoning to code generation or scientific QA)?
- How does IMAX compare with parameter-efficient methods like LoRA-based RLVR in terms of both performance and training cost?
- Is the approach compatible with online RLVR training where the backbone is also being updated, or does it strictly require a frozen backbone?
- What is the sensitivity of IMAX to the text-prompt initialization? Would random initialization or learned initialization (without GPT-generated prompts) work comparably?
