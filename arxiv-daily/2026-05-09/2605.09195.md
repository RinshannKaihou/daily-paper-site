> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示了LLM中时间知识漂移（事实是否过时）在残差流中沿一个与正确性和不确定性几何正交的独立方向编码，线性探针可高效检测漂移（受控AUROC 0.83-0.95），而所有基于熵或不确定性的方法均接近随机水平（0.49-0.57），根本原因是MLP检索电路对过时回忆和虚构产生近乎相同的激活轨迹。

> **TL;DR:** Temporal knowledge drift in LLMs is encoded as a geometrically orthogonal direction to both correctness and uncertainty in the residual stream. A linear probe on drift labels achieves controlled AUROC 0.83-0.95 across six instruction-tuned models (2-9B, four architecture families), while all existing methods (token entropy, semantic entropy, CCS, SAPLMA, SEP) remain near chance (0.49-0.57). Five complementary orthogonality tests confirm this is not an artifact. A cross-cutoff experiment with byte-identical inputs shows the probe reads model-internal state (P(A>B)=0.975-0.998). Mechanistically, the MLP retrieval circuit produces near-identical trajectories for stale recall and confabulation (r>0.81), explaining why output confidence is structurally blind to drift. The drift direction is latent-but-activatable: ablation has no effect, but amplification restructures holder logits.

## 核心贡献

- **发现时间漂移的几何正交性**：证明LLM残差流中"知识是否过时"的表示方向与"是否正确"和"是否不确定"几何正交（权重余弦|cos|<=0.14，分数相关|r|<=0.20，零空间投影|Delta|<=0.008），从结构上解释了为什么所有基于置信度的幻觉检测方法无法检测过时知识
- **构建了3,511查询/1,342事实的数据集**：基于Wikidata构建，涵盖4种关系（head_of_government, head_coach, chair_of, owned_by），具有日级别任期精度，并定义了五类时间知识状态（Stable-Correct, Stale-Recall, Confabulation, Anachronism, Obsolete-Current）
- **高效漂移检测探针**：L1正则化线性探针在六个指令微调模型（Llama-2-7B, Mistral-7B, Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen-2.5-7B, Gemma-2-9B, Gemma-2-2B）上受控AUROC达0.83-0.95，平均0.89，比最佳非漂移方法高0.32
- **跨截止日期因果实验**：固定输入仅换模型，探针在12个模型对上准确判断哪个模型的知识已过时（P(A>B)=0.975-0.998），排除了所有输入层面的混淆因素
- **机制性发现**：(1) MLP检索电路对Stale-Recall和Confabulation产生近乎相同的轨迹（r>0.81，平均0.94）；(2) 漂移方向呈"latent-but-activatable"特性——消融无效果但放大可重组holder logits；(3) Stale-Recall与Confabulation在表示空间中线性可分（AUROC 0.89-0.99）

## Key Contributions

- **Discovery of geometric orthogonality of temporal drift**: Demonstrates through five complementary tests that whether a stored fact has changed since training is encoded as a direction in the residual stream geometrically orthogonal to both correctness and uncertainty (weight cosines |cos|<=0.14, score correlations |r|<=0.20, null-space projection |Delta|<=0.008, INLP with k=10, and DoM dissociation).
- **Dataset of 3,511 queries over 1,342 Wikidata-verified facts**: Four relations (head_of_government, head_coach, chair_of, owned_by) with day-level tenure precision, partitioned into a five-cell taxonomy distinguishing Stale-Recall from Confabulation.
- **Highly effective drift detection probe**: L1-regularized linear probe achieves controlled AUROC 0.83-0.95 across six instruction-tuned models (mean 0.89), a 0.32-point gap over the best non-drift method, with entity-disjoint splits ensuring generalization to unseen facts.
- **Cross-cutoff causal experiment**: On byte-identical inputs, the probe fires on the model whose training predates the fact's transition and stays silent otherwise (P(A>B)=0.975-0.998 across twelve model pairs, falsifier rate 0.0-0.2%), ruling out all input-level confounds.
- **Mechanistic account**: (1) MLP retrieval circuit produces near-identical trajectories for stale recall and confabulation (r>0.81, mean 0.94 across six models), explaining why output confidence cannot separate them; (2) the drift direction is latent-but-activatable -- ablation produces no output changes, but amplification restructures holder logits selectively; (3) Stale-Recall vs. Confabulation is linearly separable at AUROC 0.89-0.99 despite being output-indistinguishable.

## Methodology

The authors study temporal knowledge drift through a geometric and mechanistic lens across six instruction-tuned models spanning four architecture families (Llama-2-7B-Chat, Mistral-7B-Instruct, Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Qwen-2.5-7B-Instruct, Gemma-2-9B-IT, Gemma-2-2B-IT) with training cutoffs from Sep 2022 to Jun 2024.

**Dataset construction**: 3,511 queries over 1,342 unique facts across four relations (head_of_government, head_coach, chair_of, owned_by) selected for frequent holder transitions and day-level Wikidata tenure precision. A frozen Wikidata snapshot (2026-04-27) provides ground truth. Each (sample, model) pair is classified into one of five temporal knowledge states: Stable-Correct, Stale-Recall (past holder retrieved post-cutoff), Confabulation (no timeline match), Anachronism (pre-training holder, not drifted), Obsolete-Current. Cell assignment is deterministic via five-tier cascaded string matching with 73 regression tests.

**Probing setup**: Residual-stream activations extracted at the first content-token position of the generated answer across all layers. L1-regularized (ISTA) linear probes trained with BCE loss, lambda selected by 3-fold stratified CV. Entity-disjoint train/test splits via GroupShuffleSplit. A critical "controlled evaluation" protocol restricts to query_year >= tau+1 to prevent calendar-token confound (unconstrained probes achieve >= 0.94 AUROC partly by detecting which year appears in the prompt).

**Orthogonality verification**: Five complementary tests: (1) Dense L2 probe weight cosines (all |cos|<=0.136); (2) Score-space Pearson correlations (|r|<=0.20); (3) Bidirectional null-space projection removing joint nuisance subspace (|Delta AUROC|<=0.008); (4) Iterative null-space projection (INLP) with k=10 removing multi-directional nuisance subspaces (|Delta|<=0.013); (5) Difference-of-means dissociation showing trained probes suppress confounded features that dominate untrained mean shifts.

**Mechanistic analysis**: Three convergent interventions: (1) Activation patching to localize temporal processing, revealing two routing regimes (entity-position vs. prediction-position models); (2) Direct Logit Attribution (DLA) showing identical MLP trajectories for stale and confabulated outputs; (3) Causal steering (difference-of-means amplification/suppression) revealing the drift direction encodes holder competition and is latent-but-activatable.

**Baselines**: Token entropy, token probability, sequence log-prob, sequence entropy, top-k margin, generation length, semantic entropy (Farquhar et al., 2024), scalar ensemble, SEP (Kossen et al., 2024), SAPLMA (Azaria and Mitchell, 2023), CCS (Burns et al., 2022). All operate on the controlled post-cutoff split.

## Results & Highlights

- **Drift probe (controlled)**: AUROC 0.83-0.95 across six models (mean 0.89), with bootstrap 95% CIs. MLP probe control achieves 0.82 mean, confirming signal accessibility.
- **All baselines near chance**: Token entropy mean 0.53, semantic entropy 0.54, CCS 0.54, SAPLMA 0.53, SEP 0.57. No existing method exceeds 0.57 mean AUROC.
- **Calendar-token confound is real**: Unconstrained probes inflate to >=0.94 and select wrong layers on 5/6 models. Controlled evaluation drops AUROC by mean 0.07 and shifts peak layers (Gemma-2-9B shifts 23 layers).
- **Orthogonality across five tests**: Weight cosines all <=0.136 (median 0.033); null-space projection |Delta|<=0.008 (median 0.0006); INLP with k=10 removes at most 0.013 AUROC.
- **Cross-cutoff validation**: P(A>B)=0.975-0.998 across twelve model pairs with byte-identical inputs. Falsifier rate 0.0-0.2%. Seven Llama-2-free pairs all satisfy P(A>B)>=0.975.
- **MLP circuit dynamics**: Stale-Recall vs. Confabulation trajectory correlation 0.815-0.997 (mean 0.94). Both failure modes peak at the same layer in all models.
- **Stale-Recall vs. Confabulation linearly separable**: AUROC 0.89-0.99 (mean 0.96) despite being output-indistinguishable.
- **Stale-Recall is the hard case**: Mean entropy 1.12 (Stable-Correct), 1.99 (Stale-Recall), 2.93 (Confabulation). At 80th percentile threshold, 55% of stale recalls slip through entropy screening; 31% are more confident than the median correct answer.
- **Causal steering**: Amplifying the drift direction increases the current holder's logit and decreases the stale output's logit on Stale-Recall. Ablation produces no output changes, confirming the direction is latent-but-activatable.
- **Anachronism falsifier**: The drift probe assigns strongly negative scores to Anachronism (no drift) and strongly positive to Stale-Recall (drift), despite both producing past-holder outputs. Gap ranges 4.20-9.75 logits.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The framing of temporal drift as a geometrically independent axis in representation space is genuinely new. The five-fold orthogonality verification, cross-cutoff experiment, and convergent mechanistic interventions go well beyond typical probing papers. The "latent-but-activatable" characterization of the drift direction is a particularly insightful finding.
- **Rigor**: Very strong. The paper anticipates and addresses multiple confounds: calendar-token confound (controlled evaluation), layer-selection bias (plateau analysis), input-level confounds (cross-cutoff experiment), probe-artifact concerns (dense L2 probes + untrained DoM), sparsity artifacts, and INLP multi-directional removal. The near-chance performance of strong baselines (semantic entropy published in Nature, CCS) is a convincing negative result.
- **Reproducibility**: Excellent. All models are public HuggingFace checkpoints; decoding settings, probe architecture, lambda selection, split strategy, and cell assignment procedure (73 regression tests) are fully specified. Code and data released with submission.
- **Significance**: Potentially high impact. The structural argument -- that any method operating on correctness or uncertainty signals is blind to drift by construction -- has fundamental implications for LLM reliability, selective retrieval, and knowledge editing. The paper changes how we should think about the limitations of confidence-based detection.
- **Red flags**: (1) Limited to discrete factual transitions with unambiguous ground truth (four Wikidata relations). Generalization to gradual knowledge shifts, scientific consensus evolution, or contested facts is unknown. (2) Only 2-9B models tested; whether orthogonality holds at frontier scale (70B+) is explicitly left as future work. (3) The probe is supervised, requiring drift-labeled training data. Unsupervised variants are mentioned but not demonstrated. (4) The controlled evaluation protocol restricts to post-cutoff years, which narrows the test set considerably for models with late cutoffs (e.g., Qwen-2.5 has wider CIs). (5) The five temporal knowledge states are specific to "holder" facts with discrete transitions; the taxonomy may not apply to other types of knowledge. (6) The latent-but-activatable finding means the model does not naturally use the drift signal during inference, limiting immediate practical deployment without intervention.

## Strengths

- Compelling geometric framing supported by five complementary independence tests that converge on the same conclusion
- Elegant cross-cutoff experiment that isolates model-internal state from all input-level confounds with byte-identical inputs
- Clear mechanistic explanation: MLP retrieval circuit commits identically to stale and fabricated answers (r>0.81), explaining why confidence-based detection is structurally blind
- Careful attention to confounds: calendar-token contamination, layer-selection bias, sparsity artifacts, DoM confound mitigation
- Strong practical implications: the probe could enable selective retrieval triggered on internal drift signal rather than output uncertainty
- The Stale-Recall vs. Confabulation distinction is a useful conceptual contribution for the hallucination detection literature
- Three convergent mechanistic interventions (patching, DLA, steering) on a single temporal concept is novel in the mechanistic interpretability literature

## Limitations

- Restricted to four Wikidata relations with discrete factual transitions (head_of_government, head_coach, chair_of, owned_by); generalization to gradual or subjective knowledge shifts is untested
- Only 2-9B parameter models evaluated; scaling to 70B+ or frontier models is an open question
- Supervised probe requires drift-labeled training data; unsupervised or zero-shot variants are not demonstrated
- The drift direction is latent-but-activatable (ablation has no effect), meaning the model does not naturally exploit this information during inference
- Models with later cutoffs have narrower post-cutoff windows, leading to wider confidence intervals (Qwen-2.5: 0.85 +/- 0.06)
- The practical pipeline for runtime drift detection at inference time is not addressed
- The five-cell taxonomy is specific to "holder" facts; it is unclear how it extends to other temporal knowledge types (e.g., "What is the capital of X?" for countries that changed capitals)

## Relevance

本文对理解LLM内部知识表示和时间漂移有重要意义，与LLM可靠性、幻觉检测和知识更新研究密切相关。其核心发现——时间漂移在表示空间中是一个独立于正确性和不确定性的几何正交维度——为幻觉检测领域提供了一个结构性的解释和新的研究方向，对选择性检索、知识编辑和模型可靠性评估均有实际价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the drift probe generalize to continuous or ambiguous knowledge transitions (e.g., evolving scientific consensus) rather than clean binary holder changes?
- Can the drift direction be used not just for detection but for targeted editing or unlearning of outdated facts, combined with methods like ROME or MEMIT?
- Does the orthogonality result hold for larger models (70B+) and multilingual settings? The authors explicitly flag this as an open question.
- Can unsupervised variants of the drift probe be developed, perhaps via contrastive objectives over model pairs with known cutoff gaps?
- What is the relationship between the drift direction and the model's self-reported uncertainty when explicitly asked "Are you confident this answer is current?"
- Could the latent-but-activatable drift direction be exploited during inference to dynamically trigger retrieval or flag stale outputs in real-time deployments?
- How does the probe perform on facts where the holder transition occurred very recently relative to the model's cutoff (temporal boundary cases)?
- Can the five-cell temporal knowledge state taxonomy be extended to non-holder knowledge types?
