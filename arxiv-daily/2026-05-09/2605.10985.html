Structural Interpretations of Protein Language Model Representations via Differentiable Graph Partitioning

Siddhant Dutta, Edward Tan Beng Wai, Soumick Sarker, Pasan Gunawardane, Jagath C. Rajapakse NTU Singapore

SoftBlobGIN is a lightweight 1.1M-param GNN that projects frozen ESM-2 embeddings onto protein contact graphs and learns interpretable functional substructures ("blobs") via differentiable pooling — revealing precisely when and where graph structure helps protein language models.

Core Insights
Key Finding

Graph structure adds almost nothing for graph-level enzyme classification (0.910 vs 0.912 accuracy) when PLM embeddings are available — but is essential for residue-level binding-site detection, closing a 9.8-point AUROC gap that ESM-2 alone cannot recover.

Why This Matters

Knowing when structure helps PLMs directly guides resource allocation. For graph-level tasks like enzyme classification, expensive structural inference is unnecessary — sequence embeddings suffice. But for binding-site prediction, the 3D contact graph is indispensable. This empirical boundary prevents wasted compute and focuses structural modeling where it actually matters.

no LM retraining frozen ESM-2 backbone 1.1M params interpretable blobs
Critical Edge
Performance cost: Single SoftBlobGIN underperforms plain GIN on accuracy (0.912 vs 0.925); blob interpretability is not free.
Decoupled results: Binding-site gains come from the GIN backbone, not the blob pooling module.
Unfair baselines: GearNet/ProNet lack ESM-2 features; the meaningful comparison is SoftBlobGIN vs plain GIN.
No ESM-3 comparison: Structure-aware transformers (ESM-3, SaProt) are absent from baselines.
No code released.

Training & Data
Dataset: ProteinShake, 15,603 PDB structures
Split: 14,042 / 780 / 781 (seq-sim θ=0.7)
Loss: Focal loss, γ=1, label smoothing 0.05
Optimizer: AdamW, lr=1e-3, cosine anneal
Regularization: Edge dropout 5%, feature mask 5%
Ensemble: 5 seeds, softmax averaging (+0.040 F1)
Hardware: 1x RTX 6000 Ada (48 GB)
