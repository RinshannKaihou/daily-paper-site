Bridging Spectral Operator Learning and U-Net Hierarchies: SpectraNet for Stable Autoregressive PDE Surrogates

Authors Hernández Noguera, Ferdaus, Ioup, Abdelguerfi, Simeonov arXiv 2605.09096 Date 2026-05-09
Key Finding
Residual-target parametrization inside a spectral U-Net converts autoregressive error from exponential blow-up (LT) to linear drift (T·δ), yielding bounded T = 100 rollout where FNO diverges on every test trajectory.
Key Metric
0.0822
Test relative L2 at 2.04M params — ~20% lower than FNO (0.1024 at 4.75M). Bounded T = 100 free rollout vs. FNO divergence on all 200 trajectories.
Why This Matters
Long-horizon stability is the deployment bottleneck for neural PDE surrogates. SpectraNet's bounded T = 100 rollout with <200 ms CPU latency is a genuinely practical result for real-time simulation.
GPU: 32.8 ms (H100) · CPU: <200 ms (i5-1155G7) · ~60x faster than full-attention Transformer on CPU
Critical Edge
Primary comparison is against FNO (2021); missing SFNO, LNO, and other recent spectral-hybrid architectures. Resolution-transfer gap (2.2x at 1282) contradicts resolution-invariance narrative. Only 2 seeds per configuration.

Truncated Spectral Conv

rfft2 → extract low-freq M×M blocks → learned complex weights via einsum → irfft2. 2·Cin·Cout·M2 params per layer, resolution-invariant by Proposition 1. Modes halved at each U-Net level.

Residual-Target (RTSB)

Network predicts Δt = ωt+1 − ωt. Training loss is relative L2 on the residual, not the integrated state. Avoids identity-term compounding; converts LT to T·δ (Lemma 7.3).

Semigroup-Consistency

λ-weighted penalty enforcing fθ ⋅ fθ ≈ two-step ground truth. Zero parameter cost. Contributes −0.0015 L2 independently (Theorem 1).
