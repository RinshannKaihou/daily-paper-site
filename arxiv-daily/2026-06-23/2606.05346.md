> **一句话总结：** 单作者论文提出"轨迹外推误差"（trajectory extrapolation error）——在每个词位上对前 k 个隐藏状态做线性拟合并度量下一位实际状态偏离外推位置的程度，证明该指标与 surprisal 近乎正交（r ≈ 0.044），却能在 Natural Stories 和花园路径句上独立预测自定步速阅读时间，且效应在 GPT-2 Small/Medium/Large 以及使用 RoPE 的 Pythia 模型上均得到复现。

> **TL;DR:** Elan Barenholtz (Florida Atlantic University) introduces **trajectory extrapolation error** as a new, directional counterpart to surprisal for predicting human incremental reading costs. At each word, the author fits an OLS line to the previous *k* hidden states (k = 3, layer 6) of a transformer LM, extrapolates one step ahead, and measures the Euclidean deviation from the actual hidden state. On Natural Stories (181 participants, 845k observations) this measure correlates only r = .044 with surprisal, contributes independently to reading times in a mixed-effects model, and survives joint regression with one-step embedding displacement — in GPT-2 the two even predict in **opposite signs** (displacement facilitative, extrapolation error costly). The same dissociation replicates across GPT-2 Small/Medium/Large and across Pythia-160M/410M (RoPE), and the effect is strongest at garden-path disambiguation points. A direction-preservation analysis shows the relevant structure is strictly local — at layer 6 the directional signal dies within a single word — supporting a dynamical-systems view of comprehension in which **two dissociable components** of processing cost coexist: word-level prediction error and sensitivity to local continuity in the evolving representation.

## 核心贡献

- 提出**轨迹外推误差**（trajectory extrapolation error）这一可计算指标：在每个词位用前 k 个隐藏状态做线性拟合，外推一步后用欧氏距离度量实际状态偏离外推位置的程度，从而捕获隐藏表征的**方向性局部连续性**。
- 在 **Natural Stories**（181 名被试、845,479 条观测）与 **SAP Benchmark Classic Garden Path**（24 项花园路径句、2,000+ 被试）两套数据上证明：轨迹外推误差与 surprisal **几乎正交**（r ≈ 0.044），并能在线性混合效应模型中**独立、累加地**预测对数阅读时间。
- 通过 **displacement 对照分析**（joint regression with ∥hₜ − hₜ₋₁∥）排除"测量的是表征变化幅度"这一最简解释：在 GPT-2 中，位移系数为 −0.008（加快），外推误差系数为 +0.001（拖慢），**符号相反**。
- 系统进行**跨模型稳健性测试**：GPT-2 Small/Medium/Large（117M/345M/774M）全部复制效应；GPT-2 之外用 RoPE 架构的 **Pythia-160M/410M** 同样得到独立贡献（∆AIC = +67.0 / +13.3），且 surprisal 与外推误差的相关性保持 r ≈ .046–.047。
- 用 **direction preservation** 分析刻画底层结构：在第 6 层，方向保持度从当前词的 0.44 在下一词即跌至 0.10（接近 768 维随机基线 0.029），表明被捕获的是**严格短程（3 词窗口）的局部连续性**而非长程方向性动量。
- 为加工成本提出**二维框架**：surprisal 度量词级预测误差，trajectory extrapolation error 度量表征方向重新校准代价；二者对应不同的心理语言学构造（前者更敏感于词汇可识别性，后者更敏感于结构/句法/主题连贯性）。
- 与 surprisal scaling paradox（Oh & Schuler 2023b）和 lossy-context surprisal（Futrell et al. 2020）建立显式理论联系：随着模型规模增大、full-context 预测变准，surprisal 反而拟合变差，可能正是因为人类加工是**短时近因主导、方向敏感**的。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces **trajectory extrapolation error** — a simple, interpretable geometry-based measure of how far an evolving transformer hidden state deviates from where its preceding trajectory was heading — as a directional counterpart to scalar surprisal.
- Empirically demonstrates that this measure is **nearly orthogonal to surprisal** (r = .044 on Natural Stories; .046–.047 on Pythia), and contributes **independently and additively** to log reading times in linear mixed-effects models beyond log word frequency, word length, word position, and previous log RT.
- Provides a **displacement control** that rules out the simplest alternative explanation: in GPT-2, one-step embedding displacement and trajectory extrapolation error predict in **opposite directions**, ruling out the "just measures change magnitude" interpretation.
- Conducts a **multi-model robustness sweep** across GPT-2 Small / Medium / Large and a **cross-architecture replication** using Pythia-160M / Pythia-410M (Rotary Position Embeddings), demonstrating that the effect is not an artifact of GPT-2's absolute positional embeddings or any single architecture.
- Performs a **direction-preservation analysis** showing that at the predictive layer (layer 6 in GPT-2 Small, mid-layer in Pythia), directional structure decays to near-random levels within a single word — confirming the measure captures **strictly local** positional continuity over a 3-word window, consistent with the planning horizon of human language production.
- Identifies a **two-component theory of incremental processing cost**: (1) word-level prediction error (surprisal) and (2) sensitivity to local representational continuity (trajectory extrapolation error), with dissociation evidence from a 3 × 3 tercile matrix and qualitative inspection of off-diagonal cell vocabulary (coordinators/complementizers vs. discourse pivots).
- Connects the trajectory account to the **surprisal scaling paradox** and **lossy-context surprisal**, framing trajectory extrapolation as an extreme, geometric form of lossy context summarization — a "lossiest summary imaginable" of the past three hidden states.

## Methodology

**Materials.** Two complementary datasets: (1) **SAP Benchmark Classic Garden Path** subset (Huang et al. 2024) — 24 items covering MVRR (e.g., "The horse raced past the barn fell"), NP/S, and NP/Z ambiguity types, presented in ambiguous vs. unambiguous conditions with overt syntactic markers; self-paced reading RTs from 2,000+ participants filtered to 100–5,000 ms. (2) **Natural Stories corpus** (Futrell et al. 2018) — 10 naturalistic narratives, ~10,000 words, 845,479 observations from 181 participants filtered to 100–3,000 ms; processed in overlapping chunks to fit GPT-2's 1,024-token context window.

**Trajectory extrapolation error — definition.** At each word position *t*, fit an ordinary-least-squares line through hidden states hₜ₋ₖ … hₜ₋₁ (default k = 3) at a chosen layer; extrapolate to time *k*; measure Euclidean distance ‖ĥₜ − hₜ‖ between predicted and actual hidden state. For subword-tokenized words, use the last subword's hidden state.

**Layer and window selection.** Sweep over layer 6 (intermediate; known to best capture syntax per Hewitt & Manning 2019, Jawahar et al. 2019) and the final layer (12); window sizes k ∈ {3, 5, 7}; linear vs. quadratic polynomial fits. **k = 3 linear at layer 6** consistently won out, and the direction-preservation analysis confirmed that longer windows did not capture any longer-horizon structure.

**Direction preservation analysis.** At each word, compute absolute cosine similarity between the fitted trajectory direction and the actual displacement vector at the current position and at +1/+2/+3 steps ahead. Random baseline in 768-d is ~0.029. This characterizes the temporal scale over which directional information actually persists in the hidden states.

**Displacement control.** Compare ‖ĥₜ − hₜ‖ (trajectory extrapolation error) with one-step displacement dₜ = ‖hₜ − hₜ₋₁‖ in a joint regression; the two are only weakly correlated (r = .16 in GPT-2), allowing separable contributions.

**Statistical models.** Linear mixed-effects models on log RTs with random intercepts for participants, all continuous predictors z-scored. Baseline (M0): word length + word position + previous log RT + log word frequency (Zipf). M1 adds surprisal; M2 adds trajectory extrapolation error; M3–M5 test robustness across layer/window. Comparisons via AIC, BIC, and likelihood ratio χ²(1) tests. Garden-path analyses restrict to disambiguation + 2 spillover positions. Natural Stories analyses use tercile-based 3 × 3 dissociation matrices and a tercile-orthogonality check. Models across GPT-2 Small / Medium / Large (mid-layer = 50% depth: 6, 12, 18) and across Pythia-160M / 410M replicate the core finding.

**Validation check.** Ambiguous garden-path sentences show substantially higher extrapolation error at the disambiguation point than their unambiguous counterparts across all layer/window configurations — confirming the measure is sensitive to the garden-path manipulation by construction.

## Results & Highlights

**Garden-path sentences.** Adding surprisal to a frequency-controlled baseline does not improve fit (M1: ∆AIC = −1.9, p = .71). Adding trajectory extrapolation error at layer 6, k = 3 does (M2: ∆AIC = +10.7, χ²(1) = 12.7, p = 3.7 × 10⁻⁴; β = +0.005). The improvement is robust across configurations: at layer 12, k = 5 the ∆AIC reaches +56.4 (p < 10⁻¹⁰).

**Natural Stories — independence of measures.** Word-level correlation between surprisal and trajectory extrapolation error is r = .044, confirming near-orthogonality. The 3 × 3 tercile matrix reveals significant off-diagonal effects: high-surprisal / low-extrapolation words add +0.039 to log RT (t = 21.24, p < 10⁻¹⁰⁰); low-surprisal / high-extrapolation words add +0.008 (t = 4.67, p = 3.0 × 10⁻⁶). The additive model is preferred over the interaction model (∆AIC = −2.0), showing the two measures combine without amplification or dampening.

**Displacement control.** Joint regression with one-step displacement ‖hₜ − hₜ₋₁‖: displacement predicts faster reading (β = −0.008, p = .004); extrapolation error predicts slower reading (β = +0.001, p = .012). **Opposite signs**, ruling out "extrapolation error = representational change magnitude." Pythia does not replicate the opposite-sign pattern (both same direction), which the author attributes to representational-geometry differences across positional-encoding schemes — but the core finding (extrapolation error contributes beyond surprisal) holds.

**Direction preservation.** Layer 6: 0.44 (current) → 0.10 (one step ahead) → 0.08 (+2, +3). Final layer (12): 0.61 → 0.54 → 0.54 → 0.54 (persistent). Random 768-d baseline = 0.029. The strictly local decay at the predictive layer means the 3-word linear fit captures **positional continuity** rather than persistent directional momentum. Higher-surprisal words show lower direction preservation (r = −0.10), confirming that surprising words disrupt the trajectory at both positional and directional levels.

**Robustness across model scale.** ∆AIC for trajectory extrapolation error over surprisal: GPT-2 Small +10.7 (p = 3.7 × 10⁻⁴); Medium +1.6 (marginal, p = .058); Large +13.1 (p = 1.0 × 10⁻⁴). Effect present at all three sizes, not an artifact of any one.

**Cross-architecture replication (Pythia with RoPE).** ∆AIC over surprisal: Pythia-160M **+67.0** (p < 10⁻¹⁶), Pythia-410M +13.3 (p < 10⁻⁴). r(surprisal, extrapolation error): Pythia-160M .046, Pythia-410M .047 — essentially identical to GPT-2's .044. Direction preservation at mid-layer shows the same rapid within-one-word decay (0.42 → 0.13/0.11), confirming that local trajectory structure is not a GPT-2 coordinate-system artifact.

**Qualitative off-diagonal vocabulary.** Low-surprisal / high-extrapolation cell is enriched for coordinators and complementizers ("and", "as", "that", "had") — lexically routine words that open a new syntactic constituent whose trajectory will diverge from the preceding one. High-surprisal / low-extrapolation cell contains both rarer thematic-content words ("ocean", "manor", "tics") and discourse pivots ("then", "however", "now", "first") that are lexically unexpected but whose structural role the trajectory has already begun to accommodate — supporting the interpretation that **trajectory indexes structural continuity while surprisal indexes lexical identifiability**.

## Authors & Credibility

**Elan Barenholtz** is sole author, affiliated with the **Machine Perception & Cognitive Robotics Laboratory, Department of Psychology / Center for Complex Systems, Florida Atlantic University**. The work is squarely within his established research program on **dynamical-systems approaches to cognition and perception** — the paper's framing, prior citations to Tabor & Tanenhaus 1999, Spivey 2007, Cho et al. 2017, and Levelt 1989, are all consistent with a long-standing line of work arguing that mental representations evolve through continuous state spaces rather than discrete symbolic transitions. Strengths: deep domain expertise, careful theoretical positioning, and a clean methodological contribution (a single, simple, easily-replicated measure). Limitations: **single-author paper** with no statistical or NLP co-author; no reported inter-rater reliability for any human-coded element (though the reading-time data come from large-N published corpora); no preregistration mentioned; no public code/data link stated in the extracted text. The work draws on established corpora and well-known models, so replication is feasible even without a public repo.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty: high.** The measure itself — Euclidean deviation from a linearly-extrapolated hidden state — is simple, but its identification as a *psychologically real*, *surprisal-independent* cost dimension is new. The opposite-sign displacement dissociation is a particularly clean empirical contribution. The connection to surprisal scaling paradox and lossy-context surprisal is original synthesis.

**Rigor: mixed-to-strong.**
- ✅ Large N on Natural Stories (845k observations, 181 participants) provides strong statistical power for the dissociation claim.
- ✅ Cross-architecture replication (GPT-2 vs. Pythia/RoPE) directly addresses the obvious "this is a GPT-2 coordinate artifact" objection.
- ✅ Displacement control is the right comparison and is done honestly (the Pythia opposite-sign failure is acknowledged, not buried).
- ✅ Direction-preservation analysis pinpoints *why* k = 3 wins and clarifies what the measure actually captures.
- ⚠️ **Garden-path analysis uses only 24 items**, and the author notes that by-item random intercepts absorb much of the trajectory-extrapolation variance — the very variance the theory predicts should be captured. This is mitigated by the Natural Stories replication but means the garden-path evidence alone is fragile.
- ⚠️ **Only self-paced reading**; no eye-tracking (which would distinguish first-pass from reanalysis effects) and no neural data (EEG/MEG) to triangulate to mechanism.
- ⚠️ **Surprisal itself does not improve over frequency controls on the garden-path data** (∆AIC = −1.9). This is consistent with Huang et al. 2024's null result for surprisal on the SAP Benchmark, but it weakens the rhetorical "surprisal plus trajectory" framing — within this dataset at least, trajectory extrapolation error is doing more of the predictive work than surprisal.
- ⚠️ Pythia results come from a **100-participant subsample** of Natural Stories, smaller than the GPT-2 full-sample analysis; the strikingly large ∆AIC = +67.0 for Pythia-160M should be read with this in mind.

**Reproducibility: moderate.** The measure is definitionally simple (linear fit + Euclidean distance) and uses public corpora + public models, so independent replication is feasible. However, no GitHub URL or supplementary code is cited in the extracted text — anyone wishing to reproduce must re-implement the pipeline.

**Significance: high.** The paper opens a measurable, dissociable axis of processing cost that bridges surprisal theory and dynamical-systems cognition. Theoretically diagnostic because it is consistent, independent, and directionally specific in ways the simplest alternative explanations do not predict. The framing of "two dimensions" rather than "surprisal-only" is a substantive shift in how computational psycholinguistics models incremental reading costs.

**Red flags.** None major. The work is intellectually honest about its limitations (explicitly notes the 24-item garden-path fragility, the Pythia opposite-sign non-replication, and the subsample size). The single-author structure is unusual but not a red flag — it reflects the lab's style of focused methodological contributions.

## Strengths

- **Clean, simple, replicable measure**: a single line of math (linear fit + Euclidean distance) is the entire methodological contribution, making independent verification trivial.
- **Near-orthogonality of surprisal and extrapolation error** (r ≈ .044) is a striking empirical fact that should drive follow-up work in computational psycholinguistics.
- **Opposite-sign displacement control** in GPT-2 is a particularly elegant way to rule out "this is just change magnitude."
- **Cross-architecture replication (RoPE vs. absolute embeddings)** is unusually thorough for a psycholinguistics paper and addresses a real threat to inference.
- **Direction-preservation analysis** clarifies *what timescale* the measure operates at, avoiding over-interpretation as long-range momentum.
- **Theoretical integration**: ties together surprisal theory, the surprisal scaling paradox, lossy-context surprisal, and production-planning horizon into a single coherent account.
- **Qualitative off-diagonal vocabulary analysis** (coordinators vs. discourse pivots) is a thoughtful interpretive addition that maps the statistical dissociation onto psycholinguistic intuition.
- **Effect is detected in naturalistic text** (Natural Stories), not only in highly artificial garden-path stimuli — supporting the claim that trajectory sensitivity is a general feature of processing.

## Limitations

- **Self-paced reading only.** No eye-tracking to separate first-pass from reanalysis; no EEG/MEG to localize the neural substrate of trajectory sensitivity.
- **Garden-path sample is small** (24 items) and the trajectory effect partially absorbs into by-item intercepts; the SAP Benchmark null result for surprisal itself (∆AIC = −1.9) means the contrast is really "trajectory vs. nothing" rather than "trajectory beyond surprisal" within this subset.
- **Pythia results are on a 100-participant subsample**, weakening the cross-architecture replication relative to the headline GPT-2 number.
- **Displacement-vs-extrapolation opposite-sign pattern does not replicate in Pythia**, attributed to representational-geometry differences — but this leaves the "what specifically does extrapolation error capture?" question less settled than it appears.
- **No public code or data release** mentioned in the extracted text, hindering direct reproducibility.
- **Single-author paper** with no co-author for statistical or NLP oversight; no preregistration; no reported inter-rater reliability for any human-coded judgments.
- **Linear-only fits dominate.** Quadratic and longer windows are tested and lose; while the direction-preservation result explains *why*, it also means the measure is essentially a smoothness probe rather than a full dynamical-systems descriptor.
- **No cognitive model of the trajectory cost mechanism**: the paper shows the measure *predicts* reading times but does not specify *why* trajectory deviation is computationally expensive for a human comprehender (vs. merely informative).
- **Effect size is small in absolute terms on naturalistic text**; the paper is candid about this, but the practical magnitude is modest.

## Relevance

该论文对**计算心理语言学、Transformer 可解释性、以及阅读/认知建模**三条主线有直接意义：它把隐藏状态的**几何结构**（短程方向连续性）从"附带的统计副产品"提升为一个**有独立预测力的加工成本维度**，从而把 surprisal 理论与动态系统视角统一到一个二维框架下。对于研究 LLM 表征如何映射到人类认知、关注阅读困难建模、或探索 surprisal scaling paradox 出处的工作者来说，这是一份方法简洁、证据扎实、且坦诚承认局限的参考性贡献。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does **trajectory extrapolation error computed from larger, more recent models** (e.g., Llama 3, Mistral, Qwen 2.5) compare to GPT-2/Pythia — does the effect size or the surprisal/extrapolation correlation change with scale and training compute?
- Can the effect be detected in **eye-tracking data** (e.g., Dundee, GECO, ZuCo corpora), distinguishing first-pass from regression-path effects, and is the opposite-sign displacement dissociation preserved?
- Do **neural measures (EEG/MEG, ECoG)** show trajectory-extrapolation-error-correlated responses (e.g., in N400, P600, or frontal theta) independent of surprisal, moving the evidence from behavior to mechanism?
- What does **trajectory extrapolation error** computed at **different layers** of the same model (e.g., layer-by-layer sweep) reveal about where directional structure emerges and disappears?
- Can **stimuli matched on surprisal but varying in trajectory disruption** (and vice versa) be constructed experimentally to deliver a cleaner, stimulus-level dissociation than naturalistic corpora afford?
- Does the effect **generalize to other languages** with different morphosyntactic properties (e.g., head-final languages, highly agglutinative languages, logographic scripts)? The Natural Stories corpus is English-only.
- How does trajectory extrapolation error behave under **controlled context manipulations** (e.g., lossy-context windows, surprisal-with-truncation following Futrell et al. 2020) — does the geometric measure track the lossy-context hypothesis quantitatively?
- Is there a **decoding-time intervention** (e.g., a beam-search penalty on trajectory extrapolation error) that produces text measurably easier for humans to read, closing the loop on the paper's speculation about generation?
- Can **categorical-by-item random slopes** in a crossed mixed-effects model recover the absorbed garden-path trajectory variance if a larger item pool (≫ 24) is collected?
- Does the **low-surprisal / high-extrapolation cell** behave identically across languages, or does its lexical composition (coordinators vs. discourse markers vs. complementizers) shift with typology?
