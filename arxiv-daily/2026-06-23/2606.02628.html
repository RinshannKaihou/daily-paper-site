Hallucination Is Linearly Decodable from Mid-Layer Hidden States in Quantized LLMs

Aizierjiang Aiersilan · 2026-06-23

A single linear probe on a mid-network layer reaches 0.904–1.000 AUROC for hallucination detection in 4-bit quantized 7B–8B LLMs — sampling detectors stall at 0.541.

METHODOLOGY

§How the signal is read

  • Models: three 7B–8B instruction-tuned LLMs loaded in 4-bit NF4 (BitsAndBytes-style) — Llama-3.1-8B, Mistral-7B, Qwen2.5-7B — matching realistic deployment.
  • Datasets: four hallucination benchmarks — TruthfulQA, HaluEval-QA, FEVER, and a controlled synthetic set — covering knowledge-grounded and adversarial truthfulness.
  • Hidden-state extraction: per-layer hidden states pulled for every (question, truthful, hallucinated) triple → paired binary classification.
  • Detectors compared: linear probe (logistic regression), MLP probe (nonlinearity stress test), INSIDE EigenScore, self-consistency, per-block attention entropy.
  • Evaluation: AUROC on held-out splits, paired-label protocol — applied uniformly so detectors are apples-to-apples.
  • Layer-sweep: AUROC recorded per transformer block to localize the peak-probing depth.

STRENGTHS

What this paper gets right

  • Clean apples-to-apples comparison across four detector families on three models.
  • Identifies a cross-family mid-band with practical deployment value.
  • Quantized-LLM setting is realistic and underexplored in probing literature.
  • Linear probe + attention entropy = a cheap, no-sampling detector on modest hardware.
  • Public code, data, and a reproducible single-8 GB-GPU footprint.
LIMITATIONS

Where to push back

  • Paired-label protocol may favor hidden-state probes and unfairly penalize sampling detectors; a free-form generation + external fact-check evaluation is missing.
  • Synthetic dataset inclusion can inflate headline numbers — per-benchmark ablations are needed to know which gains are real.
  • No cross-task transfer test: the paper doesn't show whether a probe trained on one benchmark transfers to another.
  • Sole-author + no affiliation — venue / peer-review expectations are unclear.
  • First-block attention entropy is labeled "complementary" but no clear fusion experiment (probe + entropy combined) is described.
  • Layer selection is post-hoc per-model/per-benchmark; no single "rule of thumb" (e.g., 50% depth) is established.
QUESTIONS FOR FURTHER EXPLORATION

06Six probes worth running next

  1. Cross-benchmark transfer: train on TruthfulQA, test on HaluEval-QA — does the "truthfulness" signal decompose into benchmark-specific subspaces?
  2. Quantization sweep: how does the sweet-spot layer shift from 4-bit NF4 → 8-bit → full precision? Is the mid-band a quantization artifact?
  3. Joint head: can a linear probe + first-block attention entropy + a lightweight MLP head close the gap to 1.0 AUROC while staying parameter-free at inference?
  4. Sample-protocol test: re-evaluate self-consistency under a generation-time protocol with multiple stochastic samples — is the "structural mismatch" explanation empirically real?
  5. Long-form generation: does the mid-layer signal persist for factual errors in free-form generation, not just multiple-choice QA?
  6. Adversarial robustness: paraphrase and distractor injection — is this a genuine truthfulness feature or a benchmark fingerprint?
