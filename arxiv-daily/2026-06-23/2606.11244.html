SPEAR: A System for Post-Quantization Error-Adaptive Recovery Enabling Efficient Low-Bit LLM Serving

Hongyuan Liu · Yawei Li · Zhiqiang Que

2026 · 06 · 23

Key metric
56–75%
W4→FP16 perplexity gap closed, <1% extra memory
Why this matters

First deployment-grade stack to co-design quantization-error compensation with kernel fusion, tensor-parallel communication, and SLO scheduling — drop-in on vLLM and Sarathi-Serve.

Critical edge

P2P dual-write assumes NVLink topology; portability to W4A16/ATOM, LUT-based T-MAC, or PCIe-only clusters is not evaluated.

01 — METHODOLOGY

Two-axis attack on a structural inefficiency 算法 × 系统协同设计

A1Input-Adaptive Error Compensator

A low-rank branch y = Wcx + α·B(γ(Ax) ⊙ Ax) where γ is a bottleneck-MLP gate in the rank-r latent space: γ(z) = 1 + tanh(W₂ ReLU(W₁z + b₁) + b₂). The 1+ residual form lets EC initialize as a static low-rank adapter — calibration is data-free (self-sampled KL divergence, two stages).

Δparams = 8r² + 6r

A2CKA-Guided Module Selection

Training-free per-module damage via Centered Kernel Alignment. Entropy-aware top-K% (τeff ∈ [0.15, 0.6]) concentrates budget on damaged layers; hybrid score scorei = δ̃i − λ·tdepi protects the worst-hit modules from cost-driven eviction.

S1Phase-Aware Kernel Fusion

Decode (M=1): full EC chain folded into the MARLIN 4-bit GEMM epilogue — single kernel, no launch gap. Prefill (M>1): semi-fused path on a side CUDA stream, captured in a replayable CUDA-Graph DAG. Decision is made at runtime per iteration.

S2Epilogue P2P Dual-Write

Gate is nonlinear, so TP partials cannot be gated independently. Instead of an explicit NCCL AllReduce hop (60–116 µs sync/query), the MARLIN epilogue writes Ybase locally and the Ax partial to peer-GPU staging memory over NVLink in a single fused kernel.

S3SLO-Constrained EC-Aware Chunk Scheduler

Offline microbenchmarks produce three lookup tables — ℓEC(M), ℓW4(M), ℓattn(M). Online, given the current CKA selection S, binary search picks the largest prefill chunk c satisfying TS(d) + TS(c) ≤ TSLO, automatically rebalancing TTFT ↔ P99 ITL as EC density changes.

Quality–memory Pareto (vs. static)

  • 31–66% of the compensation memory of LoftQ / LQER / QERA / EoRA / ASER at matched-or-better PPL.
  • 70B: 148 MB vs. QERA 301 MB (PPL 5.84 vs. 5.88).
  • Decode latency: naive EC ~5× → SPEAR ~25% of W4 MARLIN overhead (1B: 2.5 vs. 2.0 ms).
  • TP=2/3/4 on GH200: 13B at 6.0–6.3 ms/token (matches W4); 70B within +6%–14% of W4.
  • SLO scheduling: up to 2.7× lower TTFT than SLO-compliant static chunked-prefill.
03 / 04 — ASSESSMENT

Where it lands — and where it doesn't 优势 / 局限

Strengths

  • Algorithmic clarity. Two-axis decomposition (input-adaptive × module-selective) is crisp and empirically motivated.
  • Strong quality–memory Pareto. 31–66% of static-comp methods' memory at matched-or-better PPL across 1B–70B.
  • Deployment-grade engineering. Phase-aware fusion + epilogue P2P dual-write + SLO-aware scheduler cut naive EC overhead from 5× to ~25%.
  • Plug-and-play. Stacks on RTN / GPTQ / AWQ / OmniQuant without modification.
  • SLO compliance under continuous batching. Up to 2.7× lower TTFT while keeping ITL targets across all EC densities.

Limitations

  • Small-model single-GPU gap. 1B is +25% over W4 MARLIN — EC overhead is harder to hide when the bandwidth bottleneck is weak.
  • Narrow 70B coverage. Quality numbers reported mainly under per-channel RTN.
  • Kernel-portability. Tuned for MARLIN-style W4 + NVLink; W4A16/ATOM, LUT-based T-MAC, PCIe-only or cross-node are unevaluated.
  • Calibration sensitivity. Sampling temperature, sample count, sequence length for self-sampled calibration not specified.
  • No MoE / multimodal. Authors explicitly defer expert-routing + cross-modal extensions.
  • Reproducibility. No public code link in the excerpt; τeff clip range and λ need supplementary.
05 — QUESTIONS

Open questions for further exploration 延伸问题

Q1What is the equivalent of epilogue P2P dual-write under PCIe-only or cross-node InfiniBand? Can NVSHMEM or RDMA-write provide an implicit remote-reduce?
Q2Does CKA-based module selection still work for MoE — and does the EC gate break expert load balancing when stacked on top of routing?
Q3Is there a more principled substitute for the empirical τeff ∈ [0.15, 0.6] — say, Fisher information or Hessian trace?
Q4Can the EC gate share parameters with a speculative-decoding draft model? Draft is itself input-dependent — might amortize per-token cost further.
Q5Under W4A16/ATOM or LUT-based T-MAC, where does the optimal fusion split move? Does activation quantization need its own epilogue scheme?
Q6How robust is CKA damage estimation to distribution shift between self-sampled calibration and real deployment prompts — and do we need online CKA re-estimation?
