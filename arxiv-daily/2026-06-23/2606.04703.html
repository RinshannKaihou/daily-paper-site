Rethinking Continual Experience Internalization for Self-Evolving LLM Agents

Jingwen Chen, Wenkai Yang, Shengda Fan, Wenbo Nie, Chenxing Sun, Shaodong Zheng, Yangen Hu, Lu Pan, Ke Zeng, Yankai Lin (corresponding)
self-evolving-agents experience-internalization context-distillation continual-learning web-agents
Key Finding · 一句话总结
On-policy context-distillation collapses across self-evolution iterations; a three-axis recipe — principle-level experience + step-wise injection + off-policy distillation — keeps Qwen3-4B/8B web agents improving on WebWalkerQA, GAIA, and BrowseComp-ZH.
Key Metric
63.82% → 0%
Premature-answer failure rate, global vs step-wise injection (iter. 3). Step-wise state-conditioned selection eliminates the model giving up before invoking search.
Why This Matters
Without fixing the on-policy collapse, self-evolving agents can't keep learning. The paper turns three murky design choices into a clean, actionable recipe for sustained multi-iteration gains.
Critical Edge
WebWalkerQA-silver sits inside the training corpus, so part of the headline gain is just training-set fitting; out-of-domain numbers stay modest (22.7% GAIA, 5.2% BrowseComp-ZH).

Methodology
01

The three design axes studied

  • Experience Granularity. Instance-level (URLs, entities) vs principle-level (reusable strategies). 84% of principle items encode reusable strategies vs 3.7% of instance items.
  • Injection Pattern. Global trajectory-level context [x; E] vs step-wise state-conditioned selection via learned selector R_φ.
  • Internalization Regime. On-policy distillation on student trajectories vs off-policy distillation on teacher trajectories with rejection sampling.

Setup

ReAct-style web agent with five tools (Search, Visit, Python, Scholar, File Parser). Experience summarized by DeepSeek-V4 into pool E; student π_θ trained to match experience-aware teacher π_T. 15K examples from WebWalkerQA-silver, DeepDive, WebShaper, WebDancer, SailorFog-QA. Trained with verl on 8× A800, lr 1e-5, bs 128, 5 epochs.

Results & Highlights
02
  • The collapse (Fig. 1). Iterative on-policy context-distillation drives WebWalkerQA from ~30% → ~10% by iter. 3.
  • Granularity (Fig. 2). Instance improves iter. 1, then falls below base on GAIA; principle holds up across iterations.
  • Premature-answer failure (Tab. 2). 63.82% of global-injection trajectories emit terminal <answer> with no preceding tool call by iter. 3; step-wise: 0%.
  • Trajectory inflation (Tab. 3). One on-policy update stretches the student to 21.9 turns vs 2.5 base / 4.5 teacher; off-policy stays concise (2.5–4.5).
  • Final recipe (Fig. 6). principle + step-wise + off-policy sustains gains across three iterations on all three benchmarks for both 4B and 8B.
Strengths
03
  • Sharp, memorable collapse demonstration in Figure 1.
  • Methodologically disciplined: each axis isolated with both quantitative and qualitative evidence.
  • Practical and immediately actionable — recommendations are orthogonal to model architecture.
  • Honest about limitations (web-only, three iterations, DeepSeek dependency).
  • Open release of code and data; both 4B and 8B results.
Limitations
04
  • WebWalkerQA is in-domain (training includes WebWalkerQA-silver) — apparent gains inflated.
  • Three iterations is not "continual" in the strong sense; no evidence past iter. 3.
  • Heavy reliance on closed-source DeepSeek-V4 (version unspecified) — reproducibility bottleneck.
  • No comparison to memory/RAG baselines or other self-evolution methods (R-Zero, Absolute Zero, WebDancer, A-mem).
  • Two model sizes, one task domain, one language regime — generalization unverified.
  • Three-axis grid is a slice of a much larger design space (pool size, selector quality, mixing strategies all unexamined).
Questions for Further Exploration
05
  • Does the principle + step-wise + off-policy recipe still hold when the experience pool is generated by a weaker model than the student?
  • How does the recipe interact with experience-pool size and refresh rate? At what size does principle-level experience itself start to drift or fragment?
  • Can on-policy and off-policy distillation be combined — e.g., warm-start off-policy, then on-policy refinement — to get the best of both?
  • Does the collapse generalize to non-web domains (code generation, software-engineering tool use, multi-turn dialogue, embodied agents)?
  • How does the in-context vs internalized gap (the "experience-use ability") evolve past three iterations?
  • How does the recipe compare to simply maintaining the experience pool in context at inference time, with no distillation at all?
