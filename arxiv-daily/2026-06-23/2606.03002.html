Why This Matters

FP16 interpretability findings don't automatically transfer to quantized deployments — behavioral metrics can mask feature-level damage in compressed LLMs.

Critical Edge

Only round-to-nearest quantization is tested — no GPTQ, AWQ, or SmoothQuant — and conclusions ride on a single SAE dictionary's biases.

MethodologyQDM · FROZEN-SAE AUDIT

Hold the SAE fixed. Sweep the weight precision. Measure every feature.

  • Fixed measurement basis. Same frozen SAE encoder $E$, same token set, same read-out layer; only the model weights $\theta$ change between FP16 and compressed runs.
  • Per-feature Pearson $c_j$. For every active SAE feature ($f_j > 0.001$ on FP16), compute correlation between FP16 and compressed activations on shared tokens.
  • Three-tier survival. $c_j > 0.9$ survived; $0.5 \le c_j \le 0.9$ degraded; $c_j < 0.5$ damaged — with thresholds swept in Appendix B.
  • Compression operators. Per-output-channel RTN quantization $b \in \{8,7,6,5,4\}$ on every transformer linear; magnitude pruning only as a matched-PPL baseline.
  • Models & SAEs. Pythia-70M-deduped (blocks.4 hook_resid_post SAE) + Gemma-2-2B with Gemma Scope layer-12 residual-stream SAE ($d_{sae}=16{,}384$).
  • Behavior ↔ feature decoupling. WikiText-2-raw perplexity measured under both chunked and sliding-window protocols; the $c_j$ audit never shares its protocol.
  • Fragility predictor. L2 logistic regression on four FP16 feature statistics (rarity, log mean, log peak, log concentration) → 5-fold stratified CV-AUC.

Gemma-2-2B INT7: ∆PPL = −5.65% (improved) yet 18.7% of active features degraded. INT6 flips sign under sliding-window eval but feature survival stays at 51.3%.
  • RTN bit-width sweep (Table 1): Pythia 98.3% → 86.3% → 62.4% → 38.0% → 14.5%; Gemma 99.1% → 81.3% → 51.3% → 27.7% → 12.4%. Mean correlation decays 0.98 → 0.58 (Pythia), 0.97 → 0.49 (Gemma).
  • Prediction (Table 2): 5-fold CV-AUC 0.924 ± 0.007 (Pythia, 5,688 features), 0.971 ± 0.002 (Gemma, 7,144). Survival rises from 13%/5% (lowest-peak quartile) to 96%/92% (highest).
  • Quantization ↔ pruning (Table 4): Matched-PPL magnitude pruning overlaps non-survivors with Jaccard 0.86/0.79, per-feature damage Spearman 0.976/0.978 — fragility is shared.
  • Robustness (Table 5 + Appx B): Pythia INT6 stable at 60.7%–62.8% across 50k–200k tokens; 3 random 100k subsets differ by only 0.71pp; FP16 null gives mean corr 1.000000 / 0.0% damaged.
StrengthsWHAT HOLDS UP
  • Clean control variables. Fixed SAE, token set, read-out layer — the $c_j$ measurement is almost exclusively a function of compression.
  • Two-order-of-magnitude span. 70M and 2B give qualitatively identical decay curves, so the conclusion is not scale-bound.
  • Protocol decoupling. Perplexity is measured under chunked and sliding-window regimes independently of the feature audit; the sliding-window sign-flip on Gemma INT6 confirms the dissociation.
  • Structured fragility. AUC 0.92–0.97 + cross-method Spearman 0.98 is strong evidence that vulnerability is predictable, not noise.
LimitationsREAD WITH CARE
  • Only RTN tested — no GPTQ, AWQ, SmoothQuant, mixed-precision, or QAT.
  • Only Pythia-70M + Gemma-2-2B, with one residual-stream SAE per model.
  • Behavior = perplexity only; no downstream tasks, instruction following, safety, or refusal probes.
  • Thresholds $t_s=0.9$, $t_d=0.5$ are conventional — at $t_s=0.95$, Pythia INT6 survival falls to 44.4%.
  • No causal validation: the authors explicitly defer the question of whether $c_j$ decline actually breaks steering / auditing downstream.
Questions for Further ExplorationOPEN GROUND

If a frozen SAE can already tell us which features will die — what do we owe them before deployment?

  • Does the same perplexity-improves-while-features-break dissociation hold under GPTQ, AWQ, SmoothQuant, or QLoRA?
  • Across all Gemma Scope layers, do features collapse first in early or late layers — and does the order match standard intuitions?
  • Can SAE feature steering on a quantized model still reproduce FP16 effects — and do $c_j$ drops predict the failures?
  • Do FP16 statistics still predict feature survival after a domain shift or LoRA fine-tune on the quantized model?
  • How sensitive is the survival threshold to SAE dictionary choice — different sparsity, different seeds, different recipes?
  • If we add SAE feature survival as a constraint inside QAT, can we hit tighter compression without losing interpretability?
