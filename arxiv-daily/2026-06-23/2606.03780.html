WHY THIS MATTERS

Reframes the right unit of analysis for sparse LLMs — from "which MLP layer?" to "which routed expert?" — critical granularity for expert pruning, merging, and editing. But the localization only works for some architectures. Singleton-expert tracing is a model- and protocol-dependent finding, not a universal property of MoE.

CRITICAL EDGE

red flag n = 2 models, six confounded dimensions. Routing arity (top-8 vs top-2), layer count, expert count, training data, tokenizer — the headline Qwen/Mixtral asymmetry cannot be cleanly attributed to architecture, and the "coalition" fix is operationally under-defined. A first controlled study, not a settled property of MoE.

Methodology · Two-stage causal-tracing protocol

From MoE-block patching to per-expert ablation-difference patching

Extends Meng et al. (2022) ROME-style causal tracing to sparse MoE. CounterFact records, single-token true/foil, clean margin ≥ 1.0, subject-noise drop ≥ 0.5, σ = 3 × embedding std. Object token never in input.

  • stage 1 MoE-block rescue · layer sweep — cache MoE-block output at layer ℓ from a clean run, inject into the noised run. Rescueℓ = Δpatched,ℓ − Δnoised. Selected on 128 discovery cases, evaluated on 128 held-out validation cases.
  • stage 2 Expert-level rescue · ablation-difference — for clean-active expert e at the selected layer, ce(x) = MoEOut(x) − MoEOut∖e(x) under the original top-k routing (no rerouting, no renormalization). Patch δe = ce(xclean) − ce(xnoised) added to noised MoE-block output.
  • selection Recurrence-first + specificity. Candidate must be clean-active in ≥ 64 / 128 discovery cases. Spec = Rescue(e*) − mean(Rescue(r)) over same-source, same-layer clean-active controls. Active-random controls, not null-vector controls.
  • models Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base (48 MoE layers · 128 experts · top-8) and Mixtral-8x7B-v0.1 (32 MoE layers · 8 experts · top-2). 5,000 bootstrap resamples; 10,000 sign-flip tests.

Strengths
  1. Picks the expert as the unit of analysis, not the MoE block — the right granularity.
  2. Recurrence-first selection rule: prevents single-case "spurious expert" findings, a common MoE-interpretability failure mode.
  3. Strong controls: active-random, gate-weight-matched, equal-norm; plus 5×5 split/threshold grid, 5 relation-held-out folds, 512-case scale-up.
  4. Cross-model contrast surfaces a genuine empirical asymmetry between low-arity and high-arity routing.
  5. Scripts, row-level outputs, hook points, and exact CounterFact case IDs released — non-trivial reproducibility for sparse-MoE checkpoints.
  6. Concrete ethics statement: notes dual-use risk of fine-grained factual localization (targeted manipulation / suppression / editing).
Limitations
  1. n = 2 models, six confounded dimensions. The Qwen/Mixtral asymmetry cannot be cleanly attributed to routing arity.
  2. 256 cases per model is small; relations with single-token, high-margin, noise-sensitive objects are over-represented.
  3. Equal-norm control weakens the Qwen3 result. Spec drops from +0.459 → +0.188 — part of L44E069's specialness is patch-vector magnitude, not just direction.
  4. The Mixtral "coalition" is operationally under-defined — no principled size or composition rule; succeeds for any subset of size k.
  5. No multi-token objects, no subject-position sweep, no complete subject-to-answer circuit.
  6. No causal editing demonstration — diagnostic is not yet closed with a downstream intervention (ablate L44E069 → lose the fact).
  7. "Coalition" terminology risks overinterpretation — could just mean "the layer-level signal is distributed across whichever experts happen to route."
Questions for further exploration

What this paper opens, not what it closes

Q01Does the Qwen3 / Mixtral asymmetry replicate on a third MoE (DeepSeek-V2/V3 fine-grained, DBRX) to disentangle routing arity from architecture?
Q02Can a coalition be defined operationally (size, weight threshold, intersection across prompts) rather than post hoc?
Q03Does L44E069 survive SAE decomposition — is the functional unit an expert, or a feature within an expert?
Q04Can the identified expert be edited (ablated or perturbed) to causally flip the fact, closing the loop between tracing and editing?
Q05How does the result change under subject-position / relation-position sweeps, multi-token objects, non-final-position interventions?
Q06Is there a learned or heuristic rule predicting which MoE layers admit singleton-expert localization (routing sparsity, # active experts, training stage)?
Q07Does the result hold under different noise distributions and corruption strengths beyond the σ-sensitivity check?
Q08Can the protocol be extended to multilingual factual recall — given LMU's multilingual MoE routing work (Bandarkar et al. 2026)?
Q09How does the identified expert interact with attention-head-level circuits (Wang et al. 2023-style) — part of a larger circuit or sufficient node?
Q10Does pruning or merging L44E069 degrade factual performance in proportion to the rescue score — an OOD test of the localization claim?
