Medical LLM agents can run a pipeline end-to-end — but they cannot verify it.

AutoMedBench: Towards Medical AutoResearch with Agentic AI Models ↗

Key Metric · Error-code cliff

Why this matters

Workflow quality becomes a first-class citizen for medical-agent evaluation, and S3 Validate emerges as the real bottleneck — not raw capability.

Critical edge

All tasks are inference-time — no fine-tuning, no hypothesis generation. The benchmark tests “run a known pipeline on a known dataset,” not “pose and answer a new medical-AI question.”

Methodology · S1–S5 Workflow

  1. Plan25%Write plan.md — method, dependencies, validation strategy. LLM-judged.
  2. Setup15%Install deps, load models, verify paths/outputs. LLM-judged.
  3. Validate35%Pilot case + intermediate inspection + self-correction. Highest weight — gates inference.
  4. Inference15%Run full evaluation inputs; OOM/timeout checks. Deterministic.
  5. Submit10%Schema-validate artifacts. Deterministic.

OVERALL = 0.5·AGENTIC + 0.5·TASK · Two-container isolation (agent GPU / offline evaluator) prevents ground-truth leakage. 6 agents × 24 tasks × 2 tiers = 288 cells, N = 10 runs (20 for segmentation).

Setup is the strongest stage, Validate the weakest. AGENTIC > TASK for every model. Higher spend ≠ better — Opus 4.6 tops the table at the highest cost; GLM-5 is the cheaper runner-up.

Strengths

  • Stage-level + error-code analysis surfaces failure modes invisible to accuracy-only benchmarks.
  • Broadest medical-agent benchmark to date: 24 tasks × 5 tracks × 7+ modalities.
  • Two-container isolation, fixed cost snapshot, N=10/20 replication, atomic ledger.
  • Recovery-after-≥2-errors reframes reliability from error-counting to error-tolerance.
  • Open harness + public leaderboard lower the barrier to third-party verification.

Limitations

  • LLM-judge bias; no human calibration for S1/S2/S3.
  • Only 6 frontier agents — no open-source or specialized medical stacks.
  • No training-time tasks; "auto-research" is inference-only.
  • No human baseline to anchor absolute score calibration.
  • MIMIC-CXR / fastMRI require credentialed access → partial external reproducibility.
  • GPT-5.4 −16.3% under LITE reported without ablation.
  • Cost snapshot ignores prompt-cache and enterprise discounts.
  • No confidence intervals on leaderboard ordering.

Questions for further exploration

  1. Can S3 Validate be upgraded from LLM-judge to executable unit-test gates?
  2. Why does GPT-5.4 lose 16.3% under LITE — prompt format, hint specificity, or training bias?
  3. Should recovery-after-≥2-errors enter OVERALL as a reliability term?
  4. What does a medical-student baseline look like on even 3–5 of these tasks?
  5. Is a legitimate fine-tune / LoRA path possible under the current protocol?
  6. How large is LLM-judge self-bias when judge and agent share model family?
  7. Could E3 errors trigger an automatic harness-injected re-check step?
  8. Do ultrasound, endoscopy, dermoscopy, fundus need stage-protocol adjustments?
