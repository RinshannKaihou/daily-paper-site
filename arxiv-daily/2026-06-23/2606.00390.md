> **一句话总结：** Zyphra 发布 Zamba2-VL——首个基于混合 Mamba2+Transformer 的开源视觉语言模型家族（1.2B/2.7B/7B），在图像理解、推理、OCR、grounding 与计数基准上与同等规模的 Transformer VLM 持平，并在 32k token prefill 下 TTFT 降低近一个数量级。

> **TL;DR:** Zamba2-VL is the first family of open-weight vision-language models built on a hybrid SSM–Transformer LLM (the Zamba2 backbone, which interleaves Mamba2 layers with a small number of shared attention blocks augmented by LoRA projectors). Across image understanding, OCR, grounding, counting, and reasoning benchmarks, Zamba2-VL at 1.2B/2.7B/7B is competitive with leading open-weight Transformer VLMs of comparable size — Molmo2, Qwen3-VL, InternVL3.5 — while inheriting Zamba2's near-linear prefill compute and small constant-size recurrent state. On a 32k-token prefill the time-to-first-token (TTFT) is roughly an order of magnitude lower than the closest Transformer baseline, with the gap most pronounced at the 1.2B and 2.7B scales relevant to on-device and edge deployment. The model uses the Qwen2.5-VL ViT as the vision encoder, a two-layer MLP adapter with 2×2 pooling for 4× vision-token reduction, and a three-stage training curriculum (alignment → 30B pretraining → 20B SFT) over open-source data.

## 核心贡献

- **首个开放权重的混合 SSM–Transformer VLM 家族**：在 1.2B / 2.7B / 7B 三档规模上构建于 Zamba2 混合架构（Mamba2 主干 + 少量共享 Transformer block + 每插入点 LoRA 投影器），证明 hybrid state-space LLM 不只是"可行"，而是 VLM 的强力基底。
- **完整的架构与训练流水线设计**：明确给出 vision encoder（Qwen2.5-VL ViT）、LLaVA 风格两层 MLP adapter（含 2×2 pooling 把 vision token 数量除以 4）、位置编码策略保持原 Zamba2 不变，以及三阶段课程（alignment → 30B 预训练 → 20B SFT），并对关键设计选择做消融。
- **效率-精度双重优势**：在 13 项 VLM 基准（AI2D、ChartQA、DocVQA、InfoVQA、TextVQA、OCRBench、VQAv2、MathVista、MMMU、SEED、Blink、RealWorldQA、CountBenchQA、PixMoCount）上与最佳 Transformer VLM 持平的同时，32k prefill 的 TTFT 降低近一个数量级；在 1.2B / 2.7B 小尺度上效率优势最显著。
- **公开三个尺度的 checkpoint 与推理代码**：1.2B / 2.7B / 7B 全系列在 HuggingFace 与 GitHub 开源，便于研究与部署复现；总训练预算约 50B 多模态 token，明显少于多数领先 VLM 家族的数据规模。
- **针对纯 SSM VLM 缺陷的系统补救**：刻意在预训练中上采样文档/OCR 数据、采用混合架构保留 attention 的 in-context 检索能力，从而在 counting / grounding 等"需要精确回看"的子任务上追平甚至超越同尺度 Transformer 基线。

## Key Contributions

- **First open-weight hybrid SSM–Transformer VLM family.** Zamba2-VL instantiates the LLaVA-style template (vision encoder → MLP connector → LLM) on top of the Zamba2 hybrid backbone, released at 1.2B / 2.7B / 7B. The Mamba2 layers carry bulk computation in linear time with a fixed-size recurrent state; the shared attention blocks supply the in-context retrieval that pure-SSM VLMs lack. This is the strongest open hybrid SSM–Transformer VLM released to date, and the first to demonstrate that the inference-efficiency advantages of hybrid state-space LLMs carry cleanly into the multimodal setting.
- **Concrete architectural and training recipe.** Vision encoder = Qwen2.5-VL ViT (chosen via ablation for its 2D RoPE and native dynamic-resolution processing). Connector = two-layer MLP that pools each 2×2 window of patch embeddings, reducing vision-token count by 4× and projecting to the LLM embedding space. Position embedding scheme is kept identical to each Zamba2 variant (1D RoPE for 1.2B / 7B, none for 2.7B) — multimodal RoPE modifications are deferred. Two new tokens (`<|vision_start|>`, `<|vision_end|>`) delimit image boundaries; turn boundaries reuse the tokenizer's native BOS/EOS.
- **Three-stage training curriculum with loss-masking design.** Stage 1 (alignment, 230M tokens, ≤0.3 MP images, 800-token cap) trains only the MLP adapter while the ViT and LLM are frozen. Stage 2 (pretraining, ~30B tokens, 4k context, 2.7 MP cap) unfreezes the whole model on a heterogeneous mixture with deliberate document/OCR upsampling. Stage 3 (SFT, ~20B tokens) emphasizes grounding, multi-turn dialogue, and reasoning. From Stage 2 onward cross-entropy is computed only over answer tokens, supervised via AdamW, with streaming data delivery via Mosaic Streaming.
- **Grounding supervision format.** Pointing follows PixMo's XML style — `<point x="_" y="_" alt="label">label</point>` for single points and `<points x1="_" y1="_" ...>` for multi-point groups; bounding boxes use `[x1, y1, x2, y2]` normalized to [0,1]. Coordinates are emitted as relative image percentages / normalized coordinates so targets stay resolution-independent.
- **Document/OCR upsampling insight.** Under matched training, the Zamba2 backbone matches a Llama-3.x Transformer on general image understanding but lags on text-dense DocVQA / InfographicVQA. Deliberately upweighting document and OCR data closes this gap — a finding that informs both the reported results and the broader design principle for hybrid VLMs.
- **Comprehensive open release.** All three checkpoints, inference code, and the full recipe are released; total multimodal training budget is roughly 50B tokens, materially less than what leading Transformer VLM families consume.

## Methodology

**Architecture.** A LLaVA-style stack: Qwen2.5-VL ViT → 2-layer MLP adapter → Zamba2 decoder. The ViT uses 2D RoPE applied directly to patches and native dynamic resolution, both of which preserve fine-grained spatial structure on text-dense and document-style images. Each input image is resized so that both H and W are multiples of 28 (patch size 14×14, 2×2 pooling). The MLP adapter halves each spatial dimension, achieving a 4× reduction in vision tokens. The resulting sequence is interleaved with text tokens and consumed by the Zamba2 backbone in the standard autoregressive manner; cross-attention is never added — only the standard self-attention inside the shared Transformer blocks.

**Zamba2 backbone.** A Mamba2 state-space backbone interleaved with a small number of shared Transformer blocks; each shared block carries per-insertion-point LoRA projectors that specialize it to its position. Mamba2 layers deliver linear-time prefill over long visual contexts and a fixed-size recurrent state during decoding (replacing the growing Transformer KV cache); the shared attention blocks supply the content-addressable in-context retrieval that pure SSMs give up. Three scales (1.2B / 2.7B / 7B) are released, each matching the corresponding Zamba2 LLM as released by Zyphra in 2024.

**Chat template.** Lightweight: each image is bracketed by `<|vision_start|><image><|vision_end|>` and each turn uses the tokenizer's native `<s>` / `</s>`. Beyond the two new boundary tokens, no role delimiters are added, keeping embedding-table changes minimal.

**Training stages.**
- *Alignment (Stage 1):* MLP adapter only, 230M tokens, ≤0.3 MP images, 800-token cap, loss over all text tokens. Goal: initialize the vision-language interface in isolation.
- *Pretraining (Stage 2):* full model unfrozen, ~30B tokens, 4k context, 2.7 MP image cap. Heterogeneous mixture with deliberate document/OCR upsampling. Loss computed only over answer tokens.
- *Supervised fine-tuning (Stage 3):* ~20B tokens. Heavier weight on multi-turn conversation, instruction following, and grounding (both pointing and bounding-box formats). Loss-masking scheme unchanged from Stage 2.

**Data mixture.** Built from open datasets: image captioning / VQA (FineVision 24M-sample corpus, PixMo-Cap, MAmmoTH-VL, M4-Instruct, The Cauldron, VQAv2, OK-VQA, TextVQA, AI2D, ChartQA, DocVQA, InfographicVQA, A-OKVQA, ScienceQA, TabMWP, TallyQA, DVQA, FigureQA, PlotQA); document/OCR (PDFA, UCSF Industry Documents Library, DocMatix, PixMo-Docs, UniChart, ECD-10K); grounding (PixMo-Point/Count, MultiUI, OS-Atlas, UGround, AutoGUI, Aria-UI, OpenImages, Objects365); multimodal reasoning (M3 CoT, Geometry3K, Geo170K, ViRL39K); text (a small GSM8K fraction). All data is streamed online via Mosaic Streaming.

**Optimization.** AdamW (consistent with the base Zamba2 pretraining). Per-token cross-entropy summed within each minibatch, normalized by the number of answer tokens. The nominal batch size is set larger than typical LLM pretraining to compensate for the smaller effective gradient signal from answer-only loss masking.

**Evaluation.** Reproduced through a single pipeline (VLMEvalKit) to eliminate prompt-template discrepancies, except DocVQA / InfoVQA which require conference-server submission. Benchmarks: AI2D, ChartQA, DocVQA, InfoVQA, TextVQA, OCRBench, VQAv2, MathVista, MMMU, SEED, Blink, RealWorldQA, CountBenchQA, PixMoCount. TTFT measured on a 32k-token prefill of the LLM backbone.

## Results & Highlights

- **At every parameter scale Zamba2-VL matches Transformer VLM accuracy at roughly an order-of-magnitude lower TTFT.** Figure 2 plots average benchmark score vs. 32k-token TTFT; Zamba2-VL occupies the upper-left region across the 1B / 2–4B / 7–8B bands, with no Transformer-based VLM in the comparison set matching the score of any Zamba2-VL model at comparable latency.
- **Standout on visual counting.** On PixMoCount, Zamba2-VL-1.2B reaches **62.5** — nearly double InternVL3.5-1B's **32.8** and more than 3× PerceptionLM-1B's **17.7**. Zamba2-VL-2.7B posts **82.5**, Zamba2-VL-7B posts **85.3**, both at or near the top of their bands. CountBenchQA scores of **87.5** (2.7B) and **90.6** (7B) are competitive with Transformer baselines.
- **Competitive on document/chart/OCR benchmarks in every band.** Zamba2-VL-7B reaches DocVQA **92.9**, ChartQA **85.3**, TextVQA **81.0**, OCRBench **81.6** — comparable to Molmo2-8B, Qwen3-VL-8B, InternVL3.5-8B, PerceptionLM-8B despite the smaller parameter budget.
- **Position improves with scale.** 1.2B is competitive but trails on ~half the benchmarks; 2.7B is mid-pack in a band where Transformer baselines have 40–50% more parameters; 7B is consistently competitive with the strongest 7–8B open-weight Transformer VLMs across nearly every category. The narrowing gap mirrors the controlled ablation: Llama-3.2-1B leads Zamba2-1.2B by 5.5 points on average, Llama-3.2-3B leads Zamba2-2.7B by 11 points, and Zamba2-7B is nearly indistinguishable from Llama-3.1-8B (72.64 vs. 73.08).
- **Largest gap on multi-step reasoning.** Zamba2-VL trails on MathVista and MMMU (e.g., MMMU 43.8 at 7B vs. 54.3 for Qwen3-VL-8B; MathVista 61.2 at 7B vs. 73.9 for InternVL3.5-8B). The authors attribute this to a training pipeline that emphasizes capability acquisition over RL-style reasoning specialization.
- **Vision-encoder ablation (Zamba2-2.7B, LLaVA-NeXT 790K):** Qwen2.5-VL ViT > SigLIP-2 > CLIP on the six-benchmark average (Qwen 73.14, SigLIP-2 66.18, CLIP 61.87). Gap attributed to 2D RoPE and native dynamic resolution preserving spatial structure on text-dense images.
- **Language-backbone ablation (Qwen2.5-VL ViT fixed, LLaVA-NeXT 790K):** Llama-3.2-1B 61.92 vs. Zamba2-1.2B 56.44; Llama-3.2-3B 65.94 vs. Zamba2-2.7B 54.82; Llama-3.1-8B 73.08 vs. Zamba2-7B 72.64. Zamba2 matches or exceeds Llama on AI2D, ChartQA, TextVQA, and RealWorldQA across all scales; the gap concentrates on text-dense DocVQA / InfographicVQA, motivating the document/OCR upsampling used in full training.
- **Reproducibility note.** DocVQA / InfoVQA scores are taken from original papers (server-only). For PixMoCount, the 531-image retrievable subset is used consistently across all models. InfoVQA evaluation is downscaled to Zamba2-VL's 3.4k vision-token budget, which likely understates accuracy on text-dense infographics relative to Transformer baselines.

## Authors & Credibility

**Authors:** Hassan Shapourian, Kasra Hejazi, Olabode M. Sule, Beren Millidge — all from **Zyphra** (San Francisco). Zyphra is an AI research company with a track record in state-space and hybrid SSM language modeling (Zamba, Zamba2). Beren Millidge is a known contributor to the SSM research community and a co-author of the Zamba2 technical report.

**Affiliation credibility:** Zyphra has shipped multiple open-weight releases (Zamba, Zamba2) with associated technical reports; this report continues the same engineering-style documentation tradition. All three scales are released with checkpoints and inference code — uncommon transparency for a commercial lab.

**Limitations of credibility:** This is a *technical report* rather than a peer-reviewed paper, so claims are not externally refereed. The benchmark comparisons are framed favorably (the authors group baselines into bands and compare Zamba2-VL against the strongest Transformer in each band rather than head-to-head at matched params). Some baselines at the 2–4B band have 40–50% more parameters than Zamba2-VL-2.7B, which the authors acknowledge but do not normalize.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** Moderate-to-high. The hybrid SSM–Transformer recipe itself is established (Zamba2, Jamba, Kimi-Linear, Gemma-3, MaTVLM); the contribution is the *first* demonstration that this recipe is competitive with dense Transformers at scale in the VLM regime, paired with a complete from-scratch training pipeline (not a distillation from a Transformer VLM, unlike mmMamba). The deliberate document/OCR upsampling insight and the resulting closing of the text-dense benchmark gap is genuinely useful.

**Rigor.** Reasonably high. The report includes controlled ablations on both the vision encoder and the language backbone, uses VLMEvalKit to control prompting variance, and reports a reproducible evaluation pipeline. However, RL post-training is acknowledged as out of scope — a significant omission given that the leading open VLMs in the 7–8B band (Molmo2, InternVL3.5) lean on RL-style reasoning specialization to dominate MathVista/MMMU. The 2.7B variant underperforms the 1.2B on some benchmarks, an anomaly the authors attribute to the absence of rotary position embeddings in its shared attention blocks — a useful diagnostic but also a sign that the architecture search left an unresolved trade-off.

**Reproducibility.** Strong in principle (all checkpoints + inference code released; single-pipeline evaluation), but the absence of released *training code* and *training data* means the recipe is not directly reproducible end-to-end. FineVision, the largest single data source, is itself a curated derivative corpus.

**Significance.** High for practitioners targeting on-device and edge VLM deployment, where the ~10× TTFT advantage at small scale is decisive. The release is a serious contribution to the open VLM ecosystem and a strong data point for the "hybrid sub-quadratic + attention" hypothesis. Lower immediate significance for reasoning-heavy applications, where the gap to RL-tuned Transformer VLMs remains meaningful.

**Red flags.** (1) The ~10× TTFT claim is measured on 32k prefill — extreme long-context regime where attention hurts most; real-world gains at typical 1k–4k contexts are smaller (the paper does not report this regime in detail). (2) The 4k context cap is inherited from the Zamba2 base models; this is a hard ceiling that limits high-resolution multi-image workloads. (3) The InfoVQA downscaling caveat likely understates Transformer-baseline performance on text-dense infographics, narrowing the apparent gap. (4) Total training is 50B tokens, a fraction of frontier VLM budgets — the favorable efficiency story partly reflects this, not just architectural merit. (5) No RL post-training means the comparison is structurally biased against reasoning-specialized competitors.

## Strengths

- **First open-weight hybrid SSM–Transformer VLM family** — a meaningful category-of-one result that gives the open-source community a deployable alternative to dense Transformer VLMs.
- **~10× TTFT advantage at 32k prefill** is the headline result, and at small scales (1.2B / 2.7B) it is most pronounced — directly relevant to on-device and edge deployment.
- **Strong counting and grounding performance** suggests the hybrid recipe genuinely preserves the in-context retrieval capabilities that pure SSM VLMs lose.
- **Clean ablations** isolate the contribution of vision-encoder choice (Qwen2.5-VL ViT wins clearly) and language-backbone choice (gap to Llama reference closes with scale).
- **Document/OCR upsampling is a transferable insight** for any hybrid-SSM VLM training run, not just Zamba2-VL.
- **Full open release of all three scales and inference code** — uncommon transparency that lowers the barrier for follow-on research.

## Limitations

- **No RL post-training.** Zamba2-VL trails on MathVista, MMMU, and other multi-step reasoning benchmarks because the authors defer RLHF / RLAIF / RLVR-style reasoning specialization to future work. This is a real capability gap versus Molmo2 / InternVL3.5 / Qwen3-VL.
- **4k context cap.** Inherited from the Zamba2 base models. Limits high-resolution multi-image workflows; a context-length extension stage is needed before scaling up.
- **2.7B variant is the weakest performer in absolute terms** within the family on several benchmarks, attributed to the absence of rotary position embeddings in its shared attention blocks — an artifact of the original Zamba2 architecture search that the report does not fix.
- **InfoVQA evaluation asymmetry.** Zamba2-VL's 3.4k vision-token budget causes downscaling of text-dense infographics, which likely understates the real gap on that benchmark.
- **No released training code or full data manifest.** Checkpoints and inference code are open; training pipeline and data are not reproducible end-to-end by third parties.
- **Document/OCR upsampling trades off general capability.** Squeezing more text-dense data into the mixture is the right move for DocVQA-style benchmarks but may partially explain the relative weakness on reasoning tasks.
- **Hallucination, robustness, and 3D/multi-view understanding are explicitly out of scope.** Open VLM challenges that the report does not address.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does Zamba2-VL behave after a context-length extension stage on the Zamba2 backbone — does the ~10× TTFT advantage scale gracefully to 64k / 128k contexts, and does it hold at shorter (1k–4k) contexts where attention overhead is smaller?
- Does integrating a multimodal RoPE variant into the shared attention blocks (deferred in this report) close the residual gap on MathVista / MMMU, or does it require commensurate compute/data increases?
- What is the impact of RL post-training (RLHF / RLAIF / RLVR-style reasoning specialization) on Zamba2-VL-7B's reasoning benchmarks, and does the linear-time prefill advantage persist through RLHF rollout generation?
- Can the 2.7B variant's anomaly (no RoPE in shared attention blocks) be fixed by retrofitting rotary embeddings without destabilizing the pretrained weights, and does that close its intra-family deficit?
- Does the counting / pointing format (PixMo-style XML tags) transfer cleanly to a video extension, given that counting across frames is the natural next step beyond Zamba2-VL's image-only regime?
- How does Zamba2-VL's effective token efficiency (accuracy per training token) compare to Qwen3-VL and Molmo2 under matched data budgets — the 50B-token training budget is materially smaller than frontier families, so the question is whether the hybrid recipe genuinely requires less data or simply hasn't yet been pushed to the same scale?
- Does the hybrid recipe remain competitive in a distilled-from-Transformer regime (à la mmMamba), or does the from-scratch advantage documented here collapse under distillation?
- What is the carbon / cost-per-token serving profile of Zamba2-VL-7B vs. Qwen3-VL-8B and InternVL3.5-8B at production traffic mix (not just 32k prefill), and does the linear-time advantage survive short-context batched decoding where batching amortization matters?
