> **一句话总结：** SCORE 把"评测器"和"求解器"做成同一个 LLM 的两个角色，在共享参数上做 GRPO+REINFORCE 交替训练，并用一个由 GPT-5.2 驱动的 meta-harness 动态调整评测环境（维度池、调度频率、约束），从而在深度研究这类没有 ground-truth 的开放式报告生成上拿到比 GRPO/DPO 更稳定、比静态 LLM-as-a-judge 更不饱和的奖励信号。

> **TL;DR:** SCORE is a self-evolving co-evolutionary training framework for deep-research agents that fuses an evaluator and a solver into one shared-parameter LLM. The solver is updated every step with GRPO on evaluator-derived multi-dimensional report rewards, while the evaluator is updated with REINFORCE on a rubric-level consistency signal (inter-report agreement under the same rubric). A **Meta-Harness** (implemented by GPT-5.2 and invoked every 5 training steps) dynamically shapes the evaluation environment — dimension pool, dimension scheduler, global constraints — to prevent reward saturation and evaluator–solver collusion. Trained on a remarkably small Reddit-derived query set, SCORE consistently improves overall / comprehensiveness / insight / instruction-following / citation-validity on DeepResearchBench and DeepResearchEval across both ReAct and Plan-and-Execute agents, while naive GRPO/DPO can collapse to zero citation-validity. The paper also provides a first-order theoretical analysis showing the alternating updates are, to first order, a KL-regularized surrogate descent.

## 核心贡献

- **SCORE 框架**：把深度研究 agent 训练中的求解器和评测器建模为同一 LLM 的两个角色，共享参数 $\theta$，消除"评测模块独立训练再冻结"的瓶颈。
- **双通道奖励 + 交替更新**：求解器侧用评测器产出的多维加权和奖励做 GRPO；评测器侧用 rubric-induced 报告间一致性（pairwise agreement）做 REINFORCE，由 EMA baseline 中心化降方差。
- **Meta-Harness（环境动态控制）**：由 GPT-5.2 实例化，每 5 个训练步调用一次，根据求解器当下表现调整（a）维度池、（b）维度调度器、（c）全局约束（互斥组、流程约束、有效性校验等），防止评测标准僵化与共谋。
- **理论分析**：在 L-smooth + 无偏有界方差梯度的标准假设下，给出 Proposition 1（一致性是 rubric-induced 可复现性的无偏估计）和 Proposition 2（交替更新的一阶方向等价于 KL 正则化的 surrogate 下降，余项为 $O(\eta_s^2)$）。
- **经验贡献**：在 DeepResearchBench / DeepResearchEval 上、两个 agent 框架（open-deep-research 的 ReAct 与 gpt-researcher 的 Plan-and-Execute）、两个底模（Qwen2.5-7B-Inst、Llama-3.1-8B-Inst）上均稳定提升；并指出 GRPO/DPO 在 tool-integrated RL 中遭遇的 *Lazy Likelihood Displacement (LLD)* 崩塌现象，SCORE 能避免。

## Key Contributions

- **Self-play co-evolution of evaluator and solver** in a single shared-parameter LLM, motivated by an empirical observation (Figure 1) that an LLM's generation quality and evaluation agreement are positively correlated across Qwen2.5-7B/72B, Qwen3-8B, GLM-4.7-Flash, gemini-2.5-pro, and Deepseek-V3.2.
- **Meta-Harness as an environment shaper**, not a third reward channel: a GPT-5.2 controller that periodically re-specifies the dimension pool, dimension scheduler (frequency / preferred / new-pool signals), and global constraints (exclusive groups, process constraints), then a **reward verifier** filters invalid rewards before they reach the solver.
- **Two-channel alternating optimization**: solver = GRPO on $R_s(r; q, D_q^e, H_q, w_q)$ updated every step; evaluator = REINFORCE on EMA-centered $A_e(\Gamma_q) = C(\Gamma_q) - b_{t-1}$ updated every step. Both regularized by KL against $\pi_{\text{ref}}$.
- **Theoretical guarantees**:
  - *Prop. 1:* $\mathbb{E}[R_e(\Gamma_q)] = \mathbb{E}[\mu_C(\Gamma_q)]$ — the consistency signal is an unbiased estimator of the rubric-induced reproducibility of solver rollouts.
  - *Prop. 2:* $\mathbb{E}[\theta_{t+1} - \theta_t \mid \theta_t] = -\eta_s \nabla \tilde{L}(\theta_t) + \rho_t$ with $\|\rho_t\| = O(\eta_s^2)$, where $\tilde{L} = L_s + \alpha L_e + \lambda \text{KL}(\pi_\theta \Vert \pi_{\text{ref}})$. To first order, alternating updates are a regularized joint descent.
- **Empirical evidence on LLD collapse**: SCORE avoids the failure mode (catastrophic drop in at least one dimension, sometimes to exactly 0 in Plan-and-Execute + Llama-3.1-8B) that vanilla GRPO/DPO exhibit when integrated into long-horizon tool-use agents.
- **Small-data regime**: SCORE works with a small Reddit-derived query set, suggesting the framework is data-efficient.

## Methodology

### Problem setting

Open-ended deep research report generation: given a query $q$, the agent interacts with a search/retrieval environment $E_q$ and must produce a long-form, well-cited, multi-dimensional report. Unlike QA, there is no gold answer, so standard RLVR (verifiable-reward RL) does not apply.

### Framework (Figure 2)

1. **Evaluator.** Samples a query-conditioned rubric $(H_q, w_q) \sim \pi_e(\cdot \mid q, E_q)$ from the current dimension pool. Then performs constrained supplementary retrieval/verification to build $D_q^e$ and produces structured judgments: dimension-wise scoring, evidence-support checking, contradiction analysis, citation verification. A **reward verifier** checks format / claim / constraint validity and a **gap analyzer** parses gaps.
2. **Solver.** Conditioned on $q$, $D_q^s$ (its own evidence), and the evaluator's rubric $(H_q, w_q)$, generates $K$ candidate reports $r_1, \dots, r_K \sim \pi_s(\cdot \mid q, D_q^s, H_q, w_q)$.
3. **Shared actor.** Both roles are implemented by a single LLM with parameters $\theta$. Generation and evaluation are seen as two role-specific mappings from a shared representation, so evaluator adaptation can shape the same internal features used by the solver.

### Two-channel rewards

- **Solver-side report reward:**
  $$R_s(r) = a(r) \cdot \left(1 + \sum_{j=1}^m w_j s_j(r; q, D_q^e)\right)$$
  where $a(r)$ is a report-level validity/accuracy score and $s_j$ is the evaluator's per-dimension score.
- **Evaluator-side auxiliary reward:**
  $$R_e(\Gamma_q) = C(\Gamma_q) = \text{batch mean of pairwise agreement over extracted report conclusions}$$
  $$A_e(\Gamma_q) = R_e(\Gamma_q) - b_{t-1}, \quad b_t = \beta b_{t-1} + (1-\beta) R_e(\Gamma_q)$$
  Consistency acts as a *stability-oriented auxiliary* signal for rubric adaptation, not a direct correctness criterion.

### Meta-Harness

A controller (instantiated by GPT-5.2) invoked every 5 training steps. Inputs: per-dimension solver performance, frequency of each dimension, preferred dimensions, the new dimension pool. Outputs: updates to the dimension pool, dimension scheduler, and global constraints (exclusive groups, process constraints, format validation, constraint checking, claim verification, weight checking, gap-analysis parsing). The reward verifier downstream then filters out invalid rewards before they update the solver.

### Training

- Framework: **VeRL**; **GRPO** for solver (every step), **REINFORCE** for evaluator (every 1 step — note the "1" in the paper is likely a typo for the same cadence, or alternatively denotes a small batch stride).
- Rollout number $K$: 1 for the evaluator, **8** for the solver.
- Max tool calls: 10; batch size 64; max response 4,096 tokens; max input 8,192 tokens; EMA decay $\beta = 0.7$.
- Search engine: local WikiData-backed for training; web search for evaluation.
- Training data: user queries collected and preprocessed from Reddit (notably small).
- KL regularization at the actor level, not as a reward term.

## Results & Highlights

### Main results (Table 1, DeepResearchBench)

- **open-deep-research (ReAct) + Llama-3.1-8B**: SCORE pushes Overall from 27.00 → 30.07 (+3.07), Comprehensiveness 24.05 → 30.31 (+6.26), Citation Validity 22.58 → 30.55 (+7.97). GRPO actually *regresses* (Overall −3.59, readability −2.39).
- **open-deep-research (ReAct) + Qwen2.5-7B**: SCORE Overall 31.92 → 34.43 (+2.51), Comprehensiveness 28.93 → 34.86 (+5.93), Citation Validity 11.76 → 19.35 (+7.59).
- **gpt-researcher (Plan-and-Execute) + Llama-3.1-8B**: SCORE Overall 28.92 → 33.89 (+4.97), Citation Validity **38.23 → 52.38 (+14.15)**. GRPO **catastrophically collapses** to VC = 0.00 and Overall 16.97.
- **gpt-researcher (Plan-and-Execute) + Qwen2.5-7B**: SCORE Overall 32.00 → 35.91 (+3.91), Citation Validity **36.11 → 66.15 (+30.04)** — a near-doubling.

The pattern: **naive GRPO/DPO are unstable in long-horizon tool-integrated RL** (LLD collapse, sometimes to zero in at least one dimension), while **SCORE maintains stable, broad improvement**.

### Module ablation (Figure 3)

- Removing any one module (Meta-Harness / Evaluator / Solver) significantly degrades comprehensiveness.
- **Freezing the solver** → massive drop in citation validity (since the evaluator no longer co-evolves).
- **Internal consistency reward alone (no Meta-Harness)** → reward hacking: the evaluator learns *superficial* rubrics that elicit highly consistent (but low-rigor) text from the static solver, actively avoiding high-variance behaviors like rigorous external citation.
- Full SCORE sacrifices a small amount of readability for substantial gains in academic rigor (the authors frame this as a "diversity ↔ factuality"-style trade-off).

### Rollout-number study (Figure 4)

- $K = 3$: insufficient ranking samples → noisy REINFORCE gradients, no meaningful reward improvement.
- $K = 8$ (chosen): stable.
- $K = 10$: highly precise advantages → mutual acceleration of solver and evaluator updates → **policy drift compounds → model collapse** (visible as a sudden entropy drop).

### Case study (Appendix G.2)

Different query types demand different rubrics: legal queries require precise statutory citation; open-ended questions demand aggregation of diverse perspectives. A static rubric misses these nuances; SCORE's adaptive rubric selection penalizes/rewards reports according to the rigour demanded by the specific task.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** Each component (self-play, LLM-as-a-judge, query-adaptive rubric, KL-regularized shared actor) has prior art. The novelty is in the *combination*: explicitly co-evolving evaluator and solver under a shared actor with a consistency-based auxiliary signal and an external Meta-Harness imposing environmental constraints. The "consistency-as-reproducibility-not-correctness" framing (Prop. 1) is a clean and useful conceptual move that future work should borrow.
- **Rigor.**
  - **Positives:** the paper explicitly diagnoses a real failure mode (LLD collapse in tool-integrated GRPO/DPO) and shows it empirically. The rollout-number ablation is honest about the failure region. The shared-vs-separate parameter ablations are reported (Section 5.2 reference).
  - **Gaps:**
    - **Self-evaluation bias is not fully neutralized.** Both the Meta-Harness (GPT-5.2) and the final benchmark judge (GPT-5.2) are from the same model family. A more convincing setup would (a) use an external judge family (Claude / Gemini / Qwen3-Max) and (b) report human evaluation on a non-trivial sample (50–100 reports).
    - **No theory experiments on the theory.** Prop. 1 and 2 are stated but the empirical evidence for the $O(\eta_s^2)$ residual and the EMA-centering effect is not directly visualized in the extracted sections.
    - **The internal-consistency ablation is concerning:** the authors show that without Meta-Harness, the evaluator learns superficial rubrics. This means **the Meta-Harness is doing much of the work**, but it is itself a learned (or prompted) external LLM. The relative contribution of (i) shared actor, (ii) consistency reward, (iii) Meta-Harness is not cleanly decomposed.
    - **Readability regression** is hand-waved as an "inherent trade-off" without user studies; this is a real product-level concern for agent deployment.
- **Reproducibility.** Concrete hyperparameters are reported (VeRL, GRPO + REINFORCE, $K=8$, batch 64, 4096/8192 tokens, $\beta=0.7$, Meta-Harness every 5 steps). However: the Meta-Harness prompt, the rubric-sampling policy, the dimension-pool initialization, and the reward verifier's rules are not in the extracted sections. Code release status is not stated. Re-implementation will be non-trivial.
- **Significance.** If the LLD-collapse diagnosis generalizes (it appears to), this is a meaningful contribution to **tool-integrated RL for open-ended generation**, a known weak spot where most published recipes silently break. The framework is also a clean recipe for any open-ended long-form task (summarization, code review, scientific writing) where ground truth is unavailable.
- **Red flags to watch.**
  - Heavy reliance on **GPT-5.2** for both harness and evaluation; an external-judge replication is the obvious next step.
  - The "remarkably small" training data point is a strength *and* a concern: small data can mask variance and overfit the metric.
  - GRPO/DPO baselines are run only on the same base models, not against **stronger non-RL baselines** (e.g., prompted GPT-5.2 zero-shot, a fixed-judge RL baseline like WebThinker or Step-DeepResearch).
  - The "consistency = reward" choice is principled but has known failure modes (e.g., a solver can produce boring, generic reports that consistently agree with each other for the wrong reasons).
  - No report on **inference-time cost** or **tool-call budget** beyond the 10-call cap, despite agent efficiency being central to deep-research deployment.

## Strengths

- Diagnoses a specific, underappreciated failure mode (LLD collapse) and shows it convincingly.
- The shared-actor + Meta-Harness design is principled, with both theoretical (Prop. 1, 2) and empirical support.
- Multi-dimensional improvements (overall, comprehensiveness, insight, IF, citation validity) across **two agent paradigms and two base models** — not a single cherry-picked benchmark.
- The Meta-Harness is a *constraint* rather than a reward, which is a clean architectural choice that decouples environment shaping from reward shaping.
- Data-efficient: works with a small Reddit query set.
- Concrete ablation on rollout number — a rare and useful honesty about hyperparameter brittleness.

## Limitations

- **Judge–harness–system co-adaptation**: Meta-Harness and final judge are both GPT-5.2; external-judge / human-rater cross-validation is missing.
- **Self-rewarding failure mode acknowledged but not eliminated**: the paper itself shows the evaluator *will* hack on consistency rewards without the Meta-Harness, so the framework's robustness hinges on the harness's quality.
- **Readability regression** is admitted but not user-validated.
- **No public code/weights mentioned** in the extracted portions; reproducibility will require careful re-implementation.
- The Meta-Harness is an *LLM* (GPT-5.2), not a symbolic controller — meaning the "constraint" is itself a learned system with its own biases and failure modes.
- Consistency is a *stability* proxy, not a *quality* proxy; a consistent bad rubric yields a consistent bad solver.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does SCORE perform when the **Meta-Harness and the final evaluator are different model families** (e.g., Claude / Gemini / Qwen3-Max), and how does the gain change under **human evaluation** of report quality?
- Can the *Lazy Likelihood Displacement* diagnosis be **replicated outside the Llama-3.1 / Qwen2.5 family** (e.g., on larger reasoning models such as DeepSeek-R1 or Qwen3-235B), and does SCORE's stability hold at scale?
- What is the **clean contribution of each component** — shared actor vs. consistency reward vs. Meta-Harness — under a fully crossed ablation, ideally with the Meta-Harness ablated to a *symbolic* controller?
- Can the consistency-based evaluator signal be **augmented with cheap external verifiers** (e.g., citation-presence check, claim–source matching) to reduce the temptation toward superficial rubric learning?
- How does **inference-time cost** (number of search calls, total tokens, wall-clock latency) of a SCORE-trained agent compare with a WebThinker / Step-DeepResearch / AgentCPM-Report agent at matched quality?
- Is the consistency signal robust to **adversarial rubric design**? If an evaluator intentionally produces a degenerate rubric, can the system detect and recover?
- Could the Meta-Harness be made **periodic and self-supervised** (e.g., a small frozen judge) to remove the dependence on a frontier external model at training time?
- How does the framework behave on **non-English, multi-source deep research** where the dimension pool would have to encode language- and culture-specific rigour criteria?
