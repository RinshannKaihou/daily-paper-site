> **一句话总结：** BitsMoE 把每个 MoE 层用 SVD 拆成"共享基（FP16 不量化）+ 专家专属谱向量"，再用激活感知的 ILP 在固定比特预算下给每个谱分量分配不同位宽，在 2-bit 极限量化下相比 GPTQ 准确率提升 27.83 个百分点、量化速度提升 12.3×、解码速度提升 1.76×。

> **TL;DR:** BitsMoE is a spectrum-wise mixed-precision post-training quantization (PTQ) framework for Mixture-of-Experts LLMs. Each MoE layer is decomposed via SVD into a **shared spectral basis** (kept in FP16 to preserve cross-expert structure) and **expert-specific normalized spectral vectors** that act as fine-grained quantization units. Bit-widths are allocated to these units by an **activation-aware integer linear program (ILP)** that minimizes a routing-weighted reconstruction surrogate under a fixed bit budget, with a piecewise distortion model spanning {16, 8, 6, 4, 3, 2, 1, 0} bits. Across DeepSeek-V2-Lite, Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base, Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Instruct, Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B, and Mixtral-8×7B-v0.1, BitsMoE delivers state-of-the-art accuracy in the ultra-low-bit regime (e.g., 2-bit average accuracy 61.91% vs. GPTQ 34.08% on Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base, +27.83 pp; 1.4-bit still preserves 47.46% average accuracy), while running 12.3× faster than GPTQ during quantization and 1.76–3.04× faster during decoding, with 5.4–6.6× MoE-memory savings.

## 核心贡献

- **共享谱分解（Shared Spectral Decomposition）**：对每个 MoE 层按投影类型（gate/up/down_proj）做 SVD，将专家权重分解为共享正交基 Φ（FP16 保留，不量化）与专家专属归一化谱向量 $\tilde{P}_e$（作为最细粒度的量化单元），从而保留跨专家共有结构、避免传统 SVD 低秩方法直接截断谱分量的能力损失。
- **激活感知 + 分段重建误差的 ILP 比特分配**：推导路由加权的谱分量重建损失 $L(\tilde W) \approx \sum_k \alpha_k^2 \beta_k \gamma \mathbb{E}\|\varepsilon_k\|^2$（其中 $\alpha_k$ 为谱能量、$\beta_k = p_k^\top H p_k$ 为激活侧重要性、$\gamma\in[0,1]$ 为平滑系数），并为 8 档位宽（{16,8,6,4,3,2,1,0}）建立分段误差代理（高位指数衰减、中低位幂律、一位用 $\sin^2\theta$、零位为 1），最终将比特分配建模为标准 ILP。
- **极致低比特下的端到端部署收益**：在 Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base 2-bit 上比 GPTQ 量化快 12.3×、平均准确率高 27.83 pp、解码快 1.76×；MoE 层显存降至 FP16 的 1/5.4–1/6.6；并在 1.4-bit 仍保留 52.62% GSM8K / 47.46% 平均准确率。
- **对 SVD-秩缩减与 coarse-grained ILP 的明确定位**：与 MoE-I2 / MoE-SVD / D²-MoE 等"靠 SVD 减秩"的路线区分（BitsMoE 不丢谱分量，只给它们分配不同位宽），也与 MxMoE / Mixture-Compressor 等"layer/expert/block 粒度"的 ILP 区分（BitsMoE 粒度细到单个谱分量 $k$）。
- **公开代码与模型**：https://github.com/zjiayu064/BitsMoE。

## Key Contributions

- **Capacity-preserving spectral quantization.** A shared spectral parameterization for MoE layers preserves cross-expert structure and treats expert-specific spectral components as fine-grained quantization units. Unlike prior SVD-based MoE compressors (MoE-I2, MoE-SVD, D²-MoE) that reduce rank and discard components, BitsMoE retains every component and varies its bit-width.
- **Importance-aligned bit allocation under a fixed budget.** Spectrum-wise bit allocation is formulated as an integer linear program driven by an **activation-aware reconstruction surrogate** combining spectral energy $\alpha_k$, routing-weighted activation importance $\beta_k$, and bit-dependent distortion $E_k(b)$. This departs from layer/expert/block-level ILP methods (MxMoE, Mixture Compressor) by going one level finer.
- **Piecewise distortion surrogate covering all bit regimes.** Closed-form/exponential distortion $\eta e^{-\lambda b}$ for {6, 8, 16}; empirical power-law $\kappa_b$ for {2, 3, 4}; angular $\sin^2\theta_k$ for 1-bit sign; and unit distortion for 0-bit eviction — making a single unified ILP tractable across the full ultra-low-bit range.
- **Accurate and efficient end-to-end deployment.** 12.3× faster offline quantization than GPTQ (A100-PCIe-80GB), 1.76–3.04× faster decoding (A6000), 5.4–6.6× MoE-memory savings. Numerically: 2-bit Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base average accuracy 61.91% vs. GPTQ 34.08% (+27.83 pp), and even at 1.4-bit the model retains 47.46% average accuracy with 52.62% on GSM8K.
- **A clean ablation taxonomy** (NS/QS/FS × UniBit/AdaBit) that isolates the contribution of basis sharing, FP16 basis retention, and adaptive bit allocation.

## Methodology

### Stage 1 — Shared-basis spectral decomposition

For each MoE projection type $h\in H=\{\text{gate\_proj}, \text{up\_proj}, \text{down\_proj}\}$:

- For $h\in H^{in}$ (gate/up), concatenate expert weights $W_e^{(h)}\in\mathbb{R}^{d_{\text{out}}\times d_{\text{in}}}$ along the output channel and apply SVD:
  $$W_{\text{cat}}^{(h)} = U_{\text{cat}}^{(h)}\Sigma(\Phi^{(h)})^\top.$$
  Define the shared basis $\Phi^{(h)}$, the spectral-energy matrix $A^{(h)}=\text{diag}(\alpha_1,\dots,\alpha_{n_h})$, and the expert-specific normalized spectral matrix
  $$\tilde P_e^{(h)} = P_e^{(h)}(A^{(h)})^{-1} = [\,\tilde p_{e,1}^{(h)},\dots,\tilde p_{e,n_h}^{(h)}\,],\qquad \tilde p_{e,k}^{(h)} := p_{e,k}^{(h)}/\alpha_{e,k}^{(h)}.$$
  Each expert weight is reconstructed as $W_e^{(h)} = \tilde P_e^{(h)} A^{(h)} (\Phi^{(h)})^\top$.
- For $h=h^{dn}$ (down_proj), experts are concatenated along the input channel and the formula becomes $W_e^{(h)} = \Phi^{(h)} A^{(h)} (\tilde P_e^{(h)})^\top$.
- $\Phi^{(h)}$ is **kept in FP16**; only the columns of $\tilde P_e^{(h)}$ are quantized, with mixed bit-widths.

### Stage 2 — Activation-aware reconstruction surrogate

For a single expert and projection type, the routing-weighted reconstruction loss is (Lemma 3.1):

$$L(\tilde W) := \mathbb{E}\,\|(W-\tilde W)X_g\|_F^2 = \sum_{k,l} \alpha_k \alpha_l\, \phi_k^\top H \phi_l\, \mathbb{E}[\varepsilon_k^\top \varepsilon_l], \quad X_g = X\,\text{Diag}(g)^{1/2}, \quad H = X_g X_g^\top.$$

Under the **diagonal-error approximation** (independent zero-mean quantization errors across spectral components, Eq. 13), this reduces to (Corollary 3.1, Eq. 19):

$$L(\tilde W) \approx \sum_k \alpha_k^2\, \beta_k\, \gamma\, \mathbb{E}\|\varepsilon_k\|^2,\qquad \beta_k := \phi_k^\top H \phi_k,$$

where $\gamma\in[0,1]$ smooths activation importance. For down_proj the analogous surrogate uses $H$ on the expert-side and the unquantized direction as the calibration vector (Eq. 17–18).

### Piecewise distortion surrogate (Lemmas 3.2–3.5, Eq. 25)

For each spectral component $(e,h,k)$ and candidate bit-width $b\in\{16,8,6,4,3,2,1,0\}$:

| Bit regime | Distortion $E_{e,h,k}(b)$ |
|---|---|
| $b\in\{6,8,16\}$ | $\eta_{e,h,k}\exp(-\lambda b),\ \lambda=2\ln 2,\ \eta=d\rho_k^2/3$ (high-bit exponential) |
| $b\in\{2,3,4\}$ | $\kappa_b$ (offline-estimated low-bit coefficient) |
| $b=1$ | $\sin^2\theta_{e,h,k}$ (one-bit sign, $\theta$ measured against sign vector) |
| $b=0$ | $1$ (eviction) |

This is the per-component cost term $C_{e,h,k,b} = \alpha_{e,h,k}^2\, \beta_{e,h,k}^\gamma\, E_{e,h,k}(b)$ used in the ILP.

### Stage 3 — Component-wise ILP

For each projection type $h$, with one-hot indicators $y_{e,k,b}\in\{0,1\}$:

$$\min_{Y^{(h)}} \langle Y^{(h)}, C^{(h)}\rangle \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \langle Y^{(h)}, \Omega^{(h)}\rangle \le B_h,\quad \sum_b y_{e,k,b}=1,\ \forall e,k,$$

where $\Omega_{e,k,b}=b$ is the normalized bit cost and $B_h$ is the per-projection normalized bit budget. The bit budget is **uniformly partitioned across MoE layers and projection types**. ILPs are solved independently per projection type using **Gurobi**.

### Stage 4 — Inference

- For each MoE layer, the input is projected onto the shared basis **once**; the projection is reused across all routed experts.
- Packed expert-specific spectral factors are unpacked and dequantized **inside GEMM kernels** — full expert weights are never reconstructed in memory.
- Experts are executed in parallel within each MoE layer.
- The result: quantization (offline) is dominated by calibration-statistics collection (≈51–71 min of ≈80 min on the heaviest model); SVD + ILP contribute only a few minutes.

## Results & Highlights

### Main accuracy (Table 1, 2-bit and 3-bit, three MoE backbones)

- **DeepSeek-V2-Lite @ 2-bit**: BitsMoE avg = **41.04%** vs. MoEQuant 40.40%, HQQ 38.06%, MiLo 37.82%, GPTQ 30.43%. BitsMoE leads on MathQA (33.37), MMLU (46.41), HumanEval (14.02).
- **DeepSeek-V2-Lite @ 3-bit**: BitsMoE 48.38% vs. MoEQuant 48.80% (essentially tied).
- **Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base @ 2-bit**: BitsMoE avg = **61.91%** vs. MoEQuant 52.21%, MiLo 37.44%, HQQ 39.51%, GPTQ 34.08% → **+27.83 pp over GPTQ**. GSM8K 75.51% vs. MoEQuant 49.36%, GPTQ 4.32%; HumanEval 43.90% vs. GPTQ 0.00%.
- **Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base @ 3-bit**: BitsMoE 67.34% vs. MoEQuant 66.59%, GPTQ 63.32%, MiLo 66.36%.
- **Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Instruct @ 2-bit**: BitsMoE avg = **72.14%** vs. MiLo 69.53%, HQQ 68.79%, GPTQ 39.44%. MMLU 81.47%, GSM8K 71.49%.
- **Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Instruct @ 3-bit**: BitsMoE 74.19% vs. HQQ 74.14%, MiLo 73.88%.

### Sub-2-bit (Table 2, Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base)

| Avg bits | GSM8K | Avg accuracy |
|---|---|---|
| 2.0 | 75.51 | **61.91** |
| 1.8 | 69.14 | 56.23 |
| 1.6 | 63.53 | 53.45 |
| 1.4 | 52.62 | 47.46 |

The framework remains effective at near-1.4-bit — GSM8K at 1.4-bit (52.62%) actually beats MoEQuant at 2-bit (49.36%).

### Ablation (Table 3, 2-bit, average accuracy %)

| Setting | DSV2-16B | QW3-30B | QW3-80B-I |
|---|---|---|---|
| NS/UniBit (no shared basis, uniform 2-bit) | 29.72 | 36.83 | 20.82 |
| QS/UniBit (quantized shared basis, uniform 2-bit) | 21.22 | 21.46 | 21.31 |
| FS/UniBit (FP16 shared basis, uniform 2-bit) | 30.56 | 43.92 | 67.69 |
| **FS/AdaBit (full BitsMoE)** | **41.04** | **61.91** | **72.14** |

Key reads: (i) quantizing the shared basis (QS) actively hurts — it encodes common cross-expert information; (ii) FP16 basis retention alone already buys large gains over NS; (iii) the ILP adds another +10–20 pp on top.

### Efficiency (Figure 3, Table 4)

- **Offline quantization time** (A100-PCIe-80GB, 2-bit): BitsMoE ≈ 80 min vs. GPTQ ≈ 1279 min on Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B → ~16× speedup; ≈ 67 min vs. 67.2 min for Qwen3-30B in a closer comparison; structurally most BitsMoE time is **calibration**, not SVD/ILP.
- **Online inference (A6000, batch=1, prefill=256, gen=128)**:

| Model | Decode speed (FP16 → GPTQ → BitsMoE) | TTFT | MoE memory saving |
|---|---|---|---|
| DSV2-16B | 10.39 → 7.43 (0.71×) → **12.46 (1.20×)** | 0.47 → 1.27 → 0.64 | **5.44×** |
| QW3-30B | 3.07 → 3.25 (1.06×) → **5.71 (1.86×)** | 2.35 → 2.94 → 1.51 | **6.29×** |
| QW3-80B-I | 1.65 → 2.59 (1.57×) → **5.01 (3.04×)** | 8.35 → 7.32 → **1.06** | **6.56×** |

BitsMoE **beats FP16** in tokens/sec on DSV2-16B and QW3-80B-I because of the MoE-memory relief (better cache locality, larger effective batch on memory bandwidth).

### Qualitative observations

- The biggest accuracy lifts at 2-bit are on **reasoning (GSM8K) and code (HumanEval)** tasks — exactly where uniform quantization destroys structure that spectrum-wise allocation can rescue.
- Perplexity is **not always the lowest** (BitsMoE 12.20 vs. MoEQuant 11.83 on DSV2-Lite 2-bit), but downstream accuracy is consistently higher, indicating that spectrum-wise allocation preserves task-relevant directions better than it preserves generic log-likelihood.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The shared-basis + spectrum-wise ILP combination is genuinely new for MoE PTQ. Two older threads (SVD-based MoE compression; mixed-precision MoE PTQ) have existed, but prior work either **discards** spectral components (MoE-I2 / MoE-SVD / D²-MoE) or **allocates at a coarser grain** (MxMoE / Mixture Compressor at layer/expert/block). Allocating per spectral vector while keeping the shared basis in FP16 is a clean, original move.
- **Rigor — positives.**
  - Lemmas 3.1, 3.2–3.5 are stated with proofs (full derivations in Appendix B, not extracted here) and yield the closed-form surrogate used in the ILP — this is above-average for a systems/ML paper.
  - The ablation table cleanly separates the contribution of basis sharing (NS vs. QS), FP16 retention (QS vs. FS), and adaptive bit allocation (FS/UniBit vs. FS/AdaBit).
  - Five MoE backbones, including an instruction-tuned model (Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Instruct) and an architecture with linear attention — solid breadth.
  - Both **average-bit** (Table 1) and **exact-bit** (Table 2) sweeps, plus ablations and inference-efficiency numbers, are reported.
- **Rigor — gaps.**
  - **Diagonal-error approximation (Eq. 13)** is asserted but not empirically validated: how much of the gap to the "true" coupled reconstruction loss is left on the table? A sensitivity study with a small off-diagonal penalty would be convincing.
  - **The $\kappa_b$ coefficients for {2, 3, 4} bits are "estimated offline"** without specifying the procedure — a reader cannot reproduce the ILP without this. Is it averaged across components? Per-component? Calibrated?
  - **Bit budget is uniformly split across layers and projections** — the paper admits this in §5 but does not show whether a layer-aware budget allocation would help. This is itself the most natural next ablation.
  - **PPL is not always best**, even at 3-bit (DSV2-Lite BitsMoE 9.38 vs. MiLo 9.18). The paper attributes downstream wins to "fine-grained direction protection," but no per-direction sensitivity analysis is shown.
  - **MoEQuant is excluded** for Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Instruct because of "linear-attention incompatibility." A fair 2-bit comparison on the most memory-stressed model is therefore missing.
- **Reproducibility.** Hyperparameters are mostly stated (group size 128, 1024 C4 calibration samples for GPTQ/BitsMoE, Gurobi solver, A100/A6000 GPUs). The URL https://github.com/zjiayu064/BitsMoE is provided. Missing details: exact $\kappa_b$ estimation protocol, dimension of the shared basis (top-$n_h$ choice), and the down_proj heuristic derivation.
- **Significance.** A 27.83-pp gap on Qwen3-30B-A3B at 2-bit is large enough that this is the new SOTA reference for ultra-low-bit MoE PTQ; the 12.3× quantization speedup and 5–6× memory reduction make it deployable, not just an academic result.
- **Red flags.**
  - The "shared basis in FP16" design **does** add memory overhead (it is uncompressed). The paper reports only the compressed *MoE-layer* footprint — a fair comparison should subtract the shared-basis cost from the savings.
  - The framework leaves attention, activations, and KV cache unquantized. For memory-bound 80B-class inference, KV cache is often the dominant cost — this is acknowledged but limits the practical ceiling.
  - Per-layer/per-projection **budget allocation** is left as future work, but is arguably the easiest 1–2 pp to win next.
  - No robustness study on calibration-set size; 1024 C4 samples may be tight for 80B-class models.

## Strengths

- **New SOTA** in the ultra-low-bit MoE-PTQ regime on five diverse backbones; gains are largest where they matter most (reasoning and code).
- **Conceptually clean**: shared basis (FP16) + expert-specific spectrum (mixed-precision) is a decomposition that matches MoE's own structure (shared input/output feature space, divergent experts).
- **Spectrum-wise granularity** is finer than any prior mixed-precision MoE quantizer, and the ILP formulation is provably tractable (per-projection, with Gurobi).
- **Strong ablation** isolating basis-sharing, basis-quantization, and adaptive allocation.
- **Practical deployment wins**: 12.3× offline quantization speedup, 1.76–3.04× decoding speedup, 5.4–6.6× MoE-memory savings; beats FP16 decoding on memory-bound models.
- **Piecewise distortion surrogate** that uniformly covers {0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 16} bits in a single ILP is elegant.
- **Public code and model release**.

## Limitations

- **Diagonal-error approximation** discards cross-component interaction in the reconstruction loss; the additive surrogate is what makes the ILP linear.
- **Uniform bit-budget allocation** across layers/projections ignores the well-known heterogeneity of layer sensitivity (the same observation that motivates the framework at the spectral level applies, in principle, at the layer level).
- **MoE-only compression**: attention layers, activations, and KV cache remain FP16 — the framework is complementary to, not a replacement for, KV-cache compression and activation quantization.
- **High-bit overhead of the shared basis**: storing $\Phi^{(h)}$ in FP16 is essentially free compared to total MoE weight memory, but should be reported explicitly in a deployment table.
- **MoEQuant incompatibility on linear-attention models** means the strongest 2-bit baseline is missing on Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Instruct.
- **Calibration size**: 1024 C4 samples; no sweep over calibration-set size.
- **The $\kappa_b$ estimation procedure is undocumented** in the main text.
- **No comparison with newer mixed-precision MoE methods** released after HQQ / GPTQ / MiLo / MoEQuant baselines — the field is moving fast.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How much of the remaining reconstruction-error gap is attributable to the **diagonal-error approximation**? Could a low-rank off-diagonal correction (e.g., retaining the top-K cross-component terms) be added without re-quadratizing the ILP?
- Can **layer- and projection-aware bit budgets** (replacing the uniform split) close the remaining 1–2 pp gap to FP16 — especially at 3-bit, where BitsMoE is essentially tied with MoEQuant?
- What is the impact of **calibration-set size and diversity** on 2-bit accuracy? A study with {128, 512, 1024, 4096, 16384} C4 / WikiText / EBSS samples would inform production deployments.
- How does BitsMoE compose with **KV-cache quantization, activation quantization, and attention-layer PTQ**? A full-memory-budget breakdown (attention + KV + MoE) would make the deployment case complete.
- Can the **shared basis itself be compressed** (e.g., to 4 or 6 bits) with a small additional surrogate, to recover further memory without dropping accuracy below MoEQuant?
- How does the framework behave on **newer MoE architectures** such as DeepSeek-V3, Qwen3-235B-A22B, or fine-grained-expert MoEs with hundreds of small experts per layer?
- Is there a **closed-form or learned alternative to Gurobi** for the per-projection ILP, to remove the proprietary-solver dependency in open-source deployments?
- How does BitsMoE interact with **speculative decoding, MoE offloading, or expert-parallel serving** — does the spectrum-wise layout remain cache-friendly when an MoE layer spans multiple devices?
