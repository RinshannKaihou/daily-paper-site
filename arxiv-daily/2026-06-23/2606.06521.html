Why this matters
Reveals the FP8 attention P-matrix cast collapse via closed-form math, and gives kernel authors a bit-exact S=256 fix — already adopted in hpc-ops, with concrete 10–15% MSE gains predicted (and confirmed) for FlashAttention, FlashInfer, TRT-LLM XQA and SageAttention2.
Critical edge
No perplexity or task-accuracy numbers — the practical significance of the 3–10× P-cast MSE improvement remains unverified at the application level, and QKV quantization noise may compress the gain in real workloads.
Methodology §3 · §4 · §5

Builds on standard online-softmax attention (Algorithm 1). Isolates the P-cast effect by holding Q, K, V in FP32 and casting only the post-softmax matrix P to E4M3 with a static scale S. E4M3 has only 3 mantissa bits (8 values per normal binade); subnormals uniformly span 7 values down to 2⁻⁹, below which the cast rounds to zero.

§3 P-Collapse quantification closed form

Under forward iteration, the sink block sets the running maximum m_global = ∆ + δₖ (δ₄ ≈ 1.03, not the asymptotic √(2 ln k_sink) ≈ 1.67). The E4M3 underflow condition sᵢ < ∆ + δₖ − 6.93 − ln S yields the closed-form collapse fraction (Corollary 2):

F(∆, S) = Φ(∆ + δₖ − 6.93 − ln S)   with MSE bound (Prop. 3)   MSE_collapse = (σ²_V / ℓ²) Σⱼ∈Z Pⱼ²

§4 Reverse iteration sufficiency zero-underflow

Reversing the block order defers the sink to the last iteration. The pre-sink running maximum is bounded by √(2 ln(N − k_sink)); for N = 8192 this gives m ≤ 4.25 and P_min ≈ 7×10⁻⁴, comfortably above the S=256 round-to-zero boundary 2⁻¹⁸. Theorem 4 formalizes: Φ(m − 12.48) < 10⁻¹² for N ≤ 10⁶.

§5 dp(S) sawtooth & S=256 3 conditions

The most original mathematical contribution. The "normalized worst-case quantization step" dp(S) ≜ max LSB_{E4M3}(x) / S is a sawtooth: every S = 2ᵏ for k ∈ {0,…,8} hits dp = 2⁻⁴ exactly; non-power-of-two scales lie strictly above in (2⁻⁴, 2⁻³). Theorem 7 composes:

C1 bit-exact IEEE-754 scaling  ·  C2 dp-minimality  ·  C3 max normal-range coverage among 2ᵏ
→ uniquely picks S = 256 = 2⁸

S=256 vs S=448 trade-off

S=448 has better deep-tail coverage (threshold 3.5e-5 vs 6.1e-5) but loses 14% on the dp sawtooth (dp(448)=0.0714 vs dp(256)=0.0625) and is not bit-exact. Experiments confirm 10–15% MSE gap favoring 256.

Saturation paired t, 100 inst.

Forward+S=256 and Reverse+S=256 are statistically indistinguishable for ∆ ≤ 9; residual ~10⁻⁸ gap at ∆ ≥ 10 sits three orders below the MSE floor — both fixes saturate to the same precision bound.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Tight mathematical focus — dp(S) sawtooth characterization and three-condition S=256 theorem are genuinely elegant; closed-form F(∆, S) is a useful diagnostic.
  • Strong production grounding — analysis tied to FA3/4, FlashInfer, TRT-LLM XQA, SageAttention2; the hpc-ops update (S=1 → S=256) is a concrete outcome.
  • Honest limitation discussion — end-to-end metrics, worst-case vs average-case, QKV interaction all flagged explicitly.
  • Actionable diagnostic — ∆_c = 6.93 + ln S − δₖ predicts failure regimes without running kernel experiments.
  • Careful handling of dp(S) above 448 (Remark 3) — a subtle point many FP8 treatments miss.

Limitations

  • No end-to-end evaluation — all numbers are isolated output MSE vs FP32 reference; no perplexity or task accuracy.
  • QKV quantization decoupled — FP32 Q,K,V isolates the P-cast but misses the full FP8 noise floor; relative gain may compress at high ∆.
  • Worst-case vs average-case — dp(S) is minimax-optimal; real post-softmax P is highly non-uniform, dynamic per-block could help.
  • Restricted to E4M3 P-cast — does not transfer to MXFP4, FP4, or per-channel quantization.
  • Single-sink assumption — distributed sink patterns not analyzed (§7.4 mentions as future work).
  • Single author, single affiliation — broader adoption benefits from independent reproduction.
