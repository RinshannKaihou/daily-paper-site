> **一句话总结：** 在三个 4-bit NF4 量化的 7B–8B 指令微调 LLM（Llama-3.1-8B、Mistral-7B、Qwen2.5-7B）上，单层线性探针从中间层隐藏状态即可线性解码"幻觉 vs. 真实"信号，在 TruthfulQA、HaluEval-QA、FEVER 与一个合成基准上取得 0.904–1.000 AUROC，远超 INSIDE EigenScore 与自一致性等采样式检测器（≤0.541 AUROC）。

> **TL;DR:** Across three 7B–8B instruction-tuned LLMs loaded in 4-bit NF4 quantization (Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct), a single SAPLMA-style linear probe on a mid-network hidden state achieves **0.904–1.000 AUROC** for hallucination detection on TruthfulQA, HaluEval-QA, FEVER, and a controlled synthetic benchmark, while sampling-based detectors (INSIDE EigenScore, exact-match and embedding self-consistency) never exceed **0.541 AUROC** under the same paired-label protocol. On HaluEval-QA the linear probe reaches **0.997–0.998 AUROC** versus INSIDE's 0.439–0.529; MLP probes beat linear probes by at most **+0.010 AUROC** (≤0.005 in 10 of 12 cells), evidencing near-linear separability. Peak probing layers fall in a consistent mid-band — blocks 14–18/32 for Llama/Mistral, blocks 19–25/28 for Qwen — and first-block attention entropy adds a free complementary signal (0.866–0.941 AUROC) on knowledge-grounded HaluEval-QA.

## 核心贡献

- **量化 LLM 中真实性的线性可解码性**：在 4-bit NF4 量化的三个 7B–8B 指令微调模型上，单层线性探针在四个幻觉基准上达到 0.904–1.000 AUROC，证明真实性信号在量化后仍近似线性可分。
- **跨模型一致的"探测甜区"**：定位到一个跨模型家族的中段层带——Llama/Mistral 第 14–18/32 块、Qwen 第 19–25/28 块；用单一固定块（如 Llama≈15、Mistral≈16、Qwen≈20）相对每数据集最优仅损失 ≤0.02 AUROC。
- **线性 ≈ 非线性**：MLP 探针相对线性探针的最大增益仅 +0.010 AUROC（12 个单元中 10 个 ≤0.005），支持"信号近似线性"的论断。
- **采样式检测器失效的诊断**：INSIDE EigenScore 与自一致性在配对标签协议下 AUROC ≤ 0.541，作者将此归因为"配对标签评估"与采样方法所访问信息之间的结构性不匹配（探针看候选答案的内部表示，采样方法只看提示的生成多样性），而非方法本身的固有缺陷。
- **零成本互补信号**：第一块注意力熵在 HaluEval-QA 等知识锚定场景提供 0.866–0.941 AUROC 的补充信号，无需额外前向/采样开销。
- **可复现性**：代码、数据与训练好的探针权重公开，整条流水线在单卡 8 GB GPU（RTX 5060）上约 4–8 小时跑完。

## Key Contributions

- **Linear decodability of truthfulness under 4-bit quantization**: A single SAPLMA-style linear probe on one mid-network hidden state yields 0.904–1.000 AUROC across four benchmarks and three 7B–8B chat models loaded in 4-bit NF4 quantization, showing hidden-state probing survives aggressive weight quantization.
- **Cross-family mid-band localization**: A consistent "probing sweet spot" is identified — peak blocks 14–18/32 for Llama-3.1-8B and Mistral-7B, blocks 19–25/28 for Qwen2.5-7B on natural-language benchmarks; a single fixed block costs ≤0.02 AUROC versus the per-dataset optimum.
- **Evidence of approximate linearity**: MLP probes exceed linear probes by at most +0.010 AUROC (≤0.005 in 10/12 cells), indicating the truthfulness signal is approximately linear in hidden-state space.
- **Diagnosis of sampling-detector failure**: INSIDE EigenScore and self-consistency stay ≤0.541 AUROC; the paper traces this to a structural mismatch between the paired-label protocol (probe sees the candidate answer's representation) and the information sampling methods access (prompt-only generative diversity), rather than an inherent detector weakness.
- **Free complementary cue**: First-block attention entropy yields 0.866–0.941 AUROC on HaluEval-QA at no extra inference cost in knowledge-grounded settings.
- **Reproducibility**: Code, data, and trained probe weights released; the full 12 model–dataset experiment runs end-to-end on a single RTX 5060 (8 GB) in ~4–8 hours.

## Methodology

- **Models**: Three 7B–8B instruction-tuned chat models, all loaded in **4-bit NF4 quantization** (QLoRA-style, with double quantization and bfloat16 compute dtype): Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (32 blocks, dim 4096, ~5.5 GB), Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3 (32 blocks, dim 4096, ~4.8 GB), Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct (28 blocks, dim 3584, ~4.5 GB).
- **Datasets** (each capped at N=400 items, paired truthful label=1 / hallucinated label=0 per prompt, ~50:50 balance):
  - **TruthfulQA** — multiple-choice config; each question pairs the canonical correct choice with one randomly sampled incorrect choice (the hardest benchmark).
  - **HaluEval-QA** — knowledge-conditioned QA split with supplied knowledge prepended (gives an explicit evidence channel).
  - **FEVER** — Wikipedia-supported (1) vs. refuted (0) claims; insufficient-evidence claims excluded.
  - **Synthetic** — paired true/false statements about world capitals, chemical symbols, literary authorship, planetary orbits, differing by a single substantive token.
- **Hidden-state extraction**: For each ⟨prompt, answer⟩ pair a single forward pass extracts all L+1 hidden-state tensors (embedding output + L block outputs); each is reduced to one vector via **last-token pooling over the answer span** (SAPLMA convention), giving shape [N, L+1, D]. Attention weight matrices are captured at blocks {0, ⌊L/2⌋, L−1}.
- **Detection methods**:
  - **Linear probe** — single affine logistic head per block index ℓ∈{0,…,L}.
  - **MLP probe** — two hidden layers (256→64, GELU, 0.2 dropout) + softmax.
  - **INSIDE EigenScore** — sample K=10 stochastic continuations (temp 0.7, top-p 0.95, 64 tokens), capture last-token final-block hidden state, compute log-det of regularized covariance (α=10⁻³) in K×K Gram space; higher = more hallucinated.
  - **Self-consistency** — K=5 stochastic completions scored by (a) exact-match plurality and (b) mean pairwise cosine of sentence embeddings (Reimers & Gurevych); hallucination score = 1 − consistency.
  - **Attention entropy** — head-averaged Shannon entropy of the last-answer-token attention distribution at the kept blocks; high entropy ⇒ diffuse attention ⇒ unclear evidence.
- **Probe training**: AdamW (lr=10⁻³, weight decay 10⁻⁴), 30 epochs, batch size 128, stratified 70:10:20 train/val/test split, 3 seeds; reports mean±std of AUROC/AUPRC/accuracy/F1. Sampling detectors use Youden-J optimal threshold.
- **Class-geometry analysis**: Classifier-free per-block statistics — centroid distance ‖μ₁−μ₀‖, within-class spread, and their ratio (separation ratio) — track whether separability is a geometric property of the representations.

## Results & Highlights

- **Best-block linear-probe AUROC** (Table 2, 3 seeds): On **TruthfulQA** 0.904–0.915 (Llama .914@13, Mistral .904@16, Qwen .915@19); on **HaluEval-QA** 0.997–0.998 (Llama .998@15, Mistral .998@18, Qwen .997@19); on **FEVER** 0.921–0.960 (Llama .955@17, Mistral .960@14, Qwen .921@20); on the **synthetic** benchmark 0.998–1.000.
- **Probe vs. sampling gap**: The best probe AUROC exceeds the best sampling-based AUROC by at least **0.367 in every one of the 12 cells** (median gap 0.453). On HaluEval-QA, linear probes reach ≥0.997 while INSIDE EigenScore is only 0.439–0.529.
- **MLP ≈ Linear** (Finding F2): The absolute MLP−Linear difference is ≤0.005 in 10/12 cells and ≤0.01 in all 12; the largest gap is +0.010 (TruthfulQA, Qwen2.5). Probe std across seeds is ≤0.025.
- **Sampling detectors at chance** (Finding F3): INSIDE EigenScore lies in [0.433, 0.529] across all 12 cells; exact-match self-consistency in [0.467, 0.505] (effectively 0.50 on 11/12 cells, since stochastic completions rarely produce identical strings); embedding self-consistency in [0.425, 0.541]. No sampling method exceeds 0.55 AUROC on any cell.
- **First-block attention entropy on HaluEval-QA**: 0.866–0.941 AUROC (Llama .941@0, Mistral .902@0, Qwen .866@0) — a complementary signal at no extra forward pass; the effect does **not** generalize to datasets lacking an explicit evidence passage (TruthfulQA/FEVER/synthetic, Appendix N).
- **Accuracy at best block** (Table 3): Probes achieve ≥0.800 accuracy on every natural-language benchmark (e.g., HaluEval-QA 0.967–0.979; FEVER 0.821–0.900); sampling methods stay near chance; attention entropy is again the exception on HaluEval-QA (0.803–0.863 accuracy).
- **Peak-band consistency**: MLP-probe peak blocks {14,16,19} on TruthfulQA, {15,18,25} on HaluEval-QA, {17,15,21} on FEVER for Llama/Mistral/Qwen — consistently in the second half of the network (40%–90% of depth). A single fixed block (≈15 Llama, ≈16 Mistral, ≈20 Qwen) costs ≤0.02 AUROC vs. the per-dataset optimum.

## Critical Assessment

- **The paired-label protocol structurally advantages probes and structurally disadvantages sampling detectors.** This is the central caveat the paper itself raises (§3.1, §4.5, §5): probes observe the candidate answer's internal representation, whereas INSIDE/self-consistency only sample free continuations from the prompt and measure generative diversity. The headline "sampling detectors fail" result is therefore protocol-dependent — the paper explicitly states sampling approaches would be expected to recover effectiveness in generate-then-judge settings, but does **not** run that complementary experiment, so the comparison is not fully symmetric.
- **No direct full-precision baseline.** The paper notes 4-bit AUROC (0.998 on HaluEval-QA, 1.000 synthetic) is "comparable to full-precision results reported on similar models [Azaria & Mitchell]" but does not run its own full-precision control, so the quantization effect on the signal is asserted via cross-paper comparison rather than measured.
- **Small, English-only, candidate-answer evaluation.** All four benchmarks are capped at N=400 items and supply benchmark-provided candidate answers; free-form generation without a supplied answer (the realistic deployment setting) is not evaluated, and the authors flag this in §6.
- **No cross-dataset transfer test.** Probes are trained and tested on the same dataset (acknowledged in §6 as an important next step), so the headline AUROC does not demonstrate out-of-distribution generalization.
- **Synthetic benchmark inflates the upper bound.** The synthetic set saturates at 1.000 AUROC over wide late-block plateaus, making its argmax arbitrary; its inclusion in the "0.904–1.000" headline range gives an optimistic anchor, though the natural-language benchmarks (0.904–0.960) are the meaningful ones.
- **Fixed sampling budget may understate sampling methods.** INSIDE uses K=10 with a fixed temperature 0.7/top-p 0.95; the authors note (§6) a budget sweep, intermediate-layer EigenScores, or semantic-entropy clustering (Kuhn et al.; Farquhar et al., both cited) might recover discriminative power — but these are not tried.

## Strengths

- Clean head-to-head, layer-resolved comparison of four detector families on three model families × four datasets under matched compute — a genuinely apples-to-apples design that prior work largely lacked.
- Identifies a deployable, cross-family mid-band layer rule with quantified cost (≤0.02 AUROC loss for a single fixed block).
- Quantized-LLM setting (4-bit NF4) is realistic for consumer-hardware deployment and underexplored in the probing literature.
- The MLP≈Linear result, plus classifier-free geometric confirmation (separation ratio, t-SNE clusters), strengthens the "approximately linear" claim beyond a single probe accuracy.
- Strong reproducibility: public code/data/weights, fixed seeds, single 8 GB GPU footprint, 18 appendices.

## Limitations

- **Protocol asymmetry** (paired-label) favors probes and penalizes sampling detectors; the generate-then-judge regime that would fairly test sampling methods is not run.
- **No full-precision control**, so the quantization effect on probe AUROC is inferred cross-paper rather than measured.
- **Candidate-answer, English-only, N=400** evaluation; free-form generation, larger/base models, and non-English settings are untested (§6).
- **No cross-dataset transfer** — probes are trained and evaluated within the same benchmark.
- **Synthetic benchmark** saturates near 1.000 AUROC and inflates the headline range.
- **Attention entropy** is described as "complementary" but a clear joint fusion experiment (linear probe + entropy combined) is not reported in the main text; the entropy signal also fails outside knowledge-grounded datasets.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does a probe trained on one benchmark transfer to another (train on TruthfulQA, test on HaluEval-QA), or does the "truthfulness" direction decompose into dataset-specific subspaces? (The paper explicitly leaves cross-dataset transfer to future work.)
- Would INSIDE EigenScore and self-consistency recover their reported effectiveness under a generate-then-judge protocol with multiple stochastic samples, as the paper hypothesizes in §5? (Cited sampling methods: INSIDE [Chen et al.], semantic entropy [Kuhn et al.; Farquhar et al.].)
- How does the mid-band sweet spot shift when moving from 4-bit NF4 to 8-bit or full-precision weights — is the peak-band a quantization artifact or an architectural invariant?
- Does the SAPLMA-style probe remain linearly decodable for **base** (non-instruction-tuned) models, which the paper speculates may have less linearly decodable directions (§5)?
- Can a joint model (linear probe + first-block attention entropy + lightweight head) close the gap to 1.0 AUROC while remaining parameter-free at inference?
- Is the first-block attention-entropy signal specific to HaluEval-QA's explicit knowledge channel, or does it generalize to other retrieval-augmented settings the paper did not test?
