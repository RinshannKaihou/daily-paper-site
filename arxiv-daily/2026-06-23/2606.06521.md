# P-Cast Precision in FP8 Attention: Sink-Induced Collapse and the Optimality of S=2^8

> **一句话总结：** 本文对 FP8 (E4M3) Attention 中 softmax 概率矩阵 P 的 cast 精度进行系统化分析,推导出"注意力沉陷 (Attention Sink) 触发的 P 塌缩"闭式公式 F(∆, S) = Φ(∆ + δₖ − 6.93 − ln S),并从三个独立条件严格证明 S=256=2⁸ 是**位精确 IEEE 754 缩放**意义下的最优静态缩放因子——kernel-faithful 实验显示在 sink 强度过渡区(∆=5–9)可获得 3–10× MSE 改善。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides a quantitative account of two "implementation details" in FP8 (E4M3) attention kernels that turn out to be first-order precision determinants: (1) KV block iteration order (forward 0→N vs. reverse N→0) and (2) the static scale factor S applied to the softmax probability matrix P before casting it to E4M3. The author shows that forward iteration under the Attention Sink phenomenon causes a precise P-collapse—roughly a fraction Φ(∆ + δₖ − 6.93 − ln S) of non-sink P values underflow to zero, where δₖ is the within-sink extreme-value shift—and proves that reverse iteration combined with S=256 gives a zero-underflow guarantee. The paper then introduces a *normalized maximum quantization step* function dp(S) = max LSB(S)/S over the E4M3 number line, characterizes it as a sawtooth whose lower envelope is hit by every power-of-two S = 2ᵏ at dp = 2⁻⁴, and proves S = 256 = 2⁸ is the unique scale jointly satisfying (C1) bit-exact IEEE 754 scaling, (C2) the lower-envelope quantization step, and (C3) maximum normal-range coverage among 2ᵏ scales. Kernel-faithful simulations (Q,K,V held in FP32 to isolate the P-cast effect) measure 3.4× to 10.5× MSE improvement over the S=1 forward baseline at the critical transition region, with paired t-tests confirming that reverse + S=256 and forward + S=256 saturate to the same precision floor. Practical recommendations: switch any kernel currently using S=448 (FlashInfer, TRT-LLM XQA, SageAttention2) to S=256 for a 10–15% MSE reduction at near-zero cost.

## 核心贡献

- **P 塌缩的闭式量化**: 在 Attention Sink 场景下推导出"非 sink P 值在 E4M3 cast 中塌缩到 0 的比例"的显式表达式 F(∆, S) = Φ(∆ + δₖ − 6.93 − ln S),其中 6.93 = 10 ln 2, δₖ ≈ 1.03 是 sink 块内 N(0,1) 分数最大值的期望(精确值,非渐近 √(2 ln k_sink) ≈ 1.67),并配以输出 MSE 上界 MSE_collapse = (σ²_V / ℓ²) Σⱼ∈Z Pⱼ²。
- **反向迭代的形式充分性**: 严格证明反向 KV 迭代 + S=256 组合下,P 值塌缩概率 Pr[underflow] < Φ(−7.18) < 10⁻¹²,实际上对所有实用序列长度(≤ 10⁶)给出"零塌缩保证";并展示最终 α 校正项 exp(√(2 ln N) − ∆) 在 FP32 累加器中可忽略精度损失。
- **dp(S) 锯齿函数与 S=256 的构造性刻画**: 引入"归一化最差量化步长" dp(S) ≜ max_{x∈[0,S]} LSB_{E4M3}(x) / S,证明其形状为锯齿波——所有 S=2ᵏ 都坐落在下包络 dp=2⁻⁴ 上,非 2ᵏ 标度均严格高于 2⁻⁴(2⁻⁴, 2⁻³) 区间。Theorem 7 在约束 S ≥ 1 下严格证明 256 = 2⁸ 是唯一同时满足三个独立条件 (C1) 位精确、(C2) 最小量化步长、(C3) 2ᵏ 中最大 normal 覆盖的标度。
- **核忠实 (kernel-faithful) 实验验证**: 实现与 hpc-ops / FlashAttention-3/4 生产代码语义完全一致的模拟(Q, K, V 保持 FP32 以隔离 P-cast 效应,ℓ 来自 pre-cast P 的 FP32 求和,O 来自 cast 后 P_fp8 × V 的 FP32 累加),扫描 ∆ ∈ [4, 13] 与 N ∈ [512, 16384],共五组配置对比——前向 S=1(基线)、前向 S=256、反向 S=256、前向 S=448、反向 S=1,观察到 3.4–10.5× MSE 改善。
- **饱和性证明**: 配对 t 检验(100 实例)在 P-collapse 主导区域(∆ ≤ 9)证明前向 S=256 与反向 S=256 统计上不可区分;∃ ≥ 10 时反向略优但差距仅 ∼ 10⁻⁸,远低于 MSE 量级 10⁻⁵–10⁻⁶,印证两种优化殊途同归。
- **可操作的生产建议**: 将 hpc-ops 内核的 S 从 1 切换到 256(已落地);为 FlashInfer / TRT-LLM XQA / SageAttention2(均用 S=448)提供改用 S=256 的具体收益预期与实现细节——P·256 与除法都是位精确,只需两个 FMA。

## Key Contributions

- **A closed-form P-collapse fraction F(∆, S) = Φ(∆ + δₖ − 6.93 − ln S)** with an accompanying MSE upper bound, derived rigorously from the underflow condition p·S < 2⁻¹⁰ and the standard-normal distribution of non-sink scores. Validated against kernel-faithful simulation within a few percentage points (Table 1).
- **A zero-underflow guarantee (Theorem 4)** for reverse iteration combined with S=256: Pr[underflow] < 10⁻¹² for all practical sequence lengths, derived by combining extreme-value bounds on the pre-sink running maximum m ≤ √(2 ln N) + O(1) with the E4M3 round-to-zero threshold 2⁻¹⁰/S.
- **A constructive characterization of S = 256 via the dp(S) sawtooth** — the normalized worst-case quantization step. Theorem 6 proves that all S = 2ᵏ for k ∈ {0,…,8} hit the lower envelope dp = 2⁻⁴, that every non-power-of-two scale sits strictly above the envelope, and that scales above 448 (overflow) also exceed 2⁻⁴. Theorem 7 then shows S = 256 is the unique bit-exact scale maximizing normal-range coverage.
- **A three-way comparison (256 vs 448 vs 1) of the dp ratio** explaining why S=448 attains slightly better deep-tail coverage (normal threshold 2⁻⁶/448 ≈ 3.5×10⁻⁵ vs 2⁻⁶/256 ≈ 6.1×10⁻⁵) but loses 14% in worst-case step (dp(448) ≈ 0.0714 vs dp(256) = 0.0625) and is not bit-exact — predicting a 10–15% MSE gap that the experiments confirm.
- **A complete design-space audit of production FP8 attention kernels** (Table 3), positioning FlashAttention-3/4 and the updated hpc-ops as "Optimal", FlashInfer as "Near-optimal" (correct S=448 reverse iteration), and TRT-LLM XQA / SageAttention2 as "Suboptimal scale" (forward iteration + S=448).
- **Kernel-faithful empirical validation** sweeping ∆ ∈ [4, 13] at N=4096 and N ∈ [512, 16384] at ∆=7, showing 3.4× to 10.5× MSE improvement for the optimized configurations over the S=1 forward baseline, with the gap widening as the non-sink probability mass grows.

## Methodology

The paper follows a single-author quantitative-analysis style. It builds on standard online-softmax attention (Algorithm 1) and isolates the P-cast effect by holding Q, K, V in FP32 while casting the post-softmax probability matrix P to E4M3 with a static scale S. The key analytical move is to recognize that E4M3 has only 3 mantissa bits — 8 representable values per normal binade — so the per-element relative precision is at most 2⁻³ ≈ 12.5%, and that the subnormal region has only 7 uniformly-spaced values down to 2⁻⁹ (below 2⁻⁹, the cast rounds to zero).

**Section 3 — P-Collapse quantification.** Under forward iteration the sink block (positions 0..k_sink−1) sets the running maximum m_global = ∆ + δₖ, where δₖ is the expected maximum of k_sink standard Gaussians (δ₄ ≈ 1.03, not the asymptotic √(2 ln k_sink) ≈ 1.67). All subsequent P values are then exp(sᵢ − ∆ − δₖ), and the E4M3 cast underflows whenever P·S < 2⁻¹⁰. The underflow condition sᵢ < ∆ + δₖ − 6.93 − ln S (Proposition 1) yields the closed-form collapse fraction F(∆, S) = Φ(∆ + δₖ − 6.93 − ln S) (Corollary 2), treating the per-row m_global as fixed at its mean. The output MSE bound (Proposition 3) follows from the missing-mass argument ε = (1/ℓ) Σⱼ∈Z Pⱼ Vⱼ, with σ²_V being the per-dimension value variance. Numerical evaluation at N=4096, k_sink=4, B=64 (Table 1) shows the *effective information loss* (non-sink mass × fraction zeroed) peaks at ∆ ≈ 6–7, where the majority of non-sink mass is silently discarded.

**Section 4 — Reverse iteration sufficiency.** The key observation is that reversing the block order defers the sink block to the last iteration. During all preceding iterations, the running maximum is bounded by the non-sink extreme value m_pre-sink ≤ √(2 ln(N − k_sink)). For N = 8192 this gives m ≤ 4.25, and P_min ≈ exp(−7.25) ≈ 7×10⁻⁴, comfortably above the S=256 round-to-zero boundary 2⁻¹⁰/256 = 2⁻¹⁸ ≈ 3.8×10⁻⁶. Theorem 4 formalizes this with Φ(m − 12.48) < 10⁻¹² for N ≤ 10⁶, and Remark 2 notes the final α-correction exp(√(2 ln N) − ∆) is negligible against the 23-bit FP32 mantissa.

**Section 5 — The dp(S) sawtooth and S=256 characterization.** This is the paper's most original mathematical contribution. Definition 1 defines the *normalized maximum quantization step* dp(S) = max_{x∈[0,S]} LSB_{E4M3}(x) / S. Theorem 6 proves three properties: (i) for S = 2ᵏ with k ∈ {0,…,8}, dp(2ᵏ) = 2⁻⁴ exactly (because LSB in the highest overlapping binade [2ᵏ⁻¹, 2ᵏ) is 2ᵏ⁻⁴, and the denominator is also 2ᵏ); (ii) for non-power-of-two S, dp(S) > 2⁻⁴, lying in (2⁻⁴, 2⁻³); (iii) for S > 448, both terms in the extended definition (Remark 3) exceed 2⁻⁴. Proposition 5 separately shows power-of-two S gives bit-exact scaling in IEEE 754 (multiplication by 2ᵏ is exponent-only, no mantissa rounding). Theorem 7 then composes these results: under the bit-exactness constraint (C1), the dp-minimality condition (C2) admits exactly nine candidates {1, 2, 4, …, 256}, and (C3) — maximum normal-range coverage among 2ᵏ — uniquely picks S = 256.

**Section 6 — Kernel-faithful validation.** The author implements an exact simulation of the production FP8 attention kernel: ℓ is summed in FP32 from pre-cast P (line 8 of Algorithm 1), O accumulates in FP32 from cast P_fp8 × V (line 10), and the epilogue divides by S·ℓ. Q, K, V are held in FP32 so the only quantization error is from the P-cast. Two experiments are run: (1) sweep ∆ ∈ [4, 13] at N=4096, d=128, qlen=32, B=64, k_sink=4, 20 seeds; (2) sweep N ∈ [512, 16384] at ∆=7. Five configurations are compared: Forward S=1 (hpc-ops prior baseline), Forward S=256, Forward S=448, Reverse S=1, Reverse S=256. Paired t-tests (Appendix B, 100 instances) confirm Forward+S=256 and Reverse+S=256 are statistically indistinguishable for ∆ ≤ 9; the residual ∼10⁻⁸ gap at ∆ ≥ 10 is three orders of magnitude below the MSE floor of 10⁻⁵–10⁻⁶.

## Results & Highlights

**P-collapse quantification (Table 1, N=4096, k_sink=4, B=64).** At S=1 forward iteration, the fraction of non-sink P values that underflow is 22.3% at ∆=5, rises to 51.6% at ∆=6, 82.0% at ∆=7, 94.8% at ∆=8, and saturates to ≈100% by ∆=10. The corresponding "effective information loss" (non-sink mass × fraction zeroed) peaks at 42.4% at ∆=7. Crucially, S=256 forward iteration shows 0% underflow at ∆ ∈ {5, 6, 7, 8}, only 0.3% at ∆=9, 2.3% at ∆=10, 11.7% at ∆=12 — confirming that S=256 alone (without reverse) is sufficient for ∆ ≤ 9. The closed-form formula Φ(∆ + δₖ − 6.93 − ln S) reproduces the simulated values within a few percentage points.

**Sink strength sweep (Figure 2 top, N=4096).** Forward S=1 exhibits a sharp MSE peak at ∆=7–8 in the 10⁻⁵ range. All other configurations (Reverse S=1, Forward S=256, Forward S=448, Reverse S=256) remain at the quantization-noise floor (10⁻⁶ to 10⁻⁷) across the same range. The diagnostic (Figure 2 bottom) confirms the correspondence between P-zeroing fraction and MSE: the non-sink probability mass (blue) and the S=1 P-zeroed fraction (red) overlap maximally at ∆=7, while the S=256 P-zeroed fraction (green) is suppressed to near-zero across ∆ ∈ [4, 8].

**Sequence length sweep (Figure 3, ∆=7).** The improvement of Forward+S=256 over Forward+S=1 grows monotonically with N: 1.3× at N=512, 3.4× at N=4096, 5.5× at N=8192, and 10.5× at N=16384. This monotonicity is exactly what the theory predicts — as N grows, the non-sink mass increases linearly while the sink mass remains fixed at k_sink, so the *fraction* of total attention output affected by P-collapse grows.

**MSE table (Table 2, ∆=7).** At N=4096: Forward S=1 = 5.65×10⁻⁵ vs. Forward S=256 = 1.64×10⁻⁵ (3.4× ratio). At N=8192: 4.40×10⁻⁵ vs. 0.80×10⁻⁵ (5.5×). At N=16384: 2.94×10⁻⁵ vs. 0.28×10⁻⁵ (10.5×). Reverse S=256 is statistically indistinguishable from Forward S=256 in this range. Forward S=448 sits slightly above Forward S=256 (1.81×10⁻⁵ vs. 1.64×10⁻⁵ at N=4096), consistent with the dp ratio prediction of 1.14 → (1.14)² ≈ 1.30 worst-case, averaged to ≈10% in practice.

**S=256 vs S=448 trade-off.** S=448 has strictly better normal-range coverage (threshold 2⁻⁶/448 ≈ 3.5×10⁻⁵ vs. 2⁻⁶/256 ≈ 6.1×10⁻⁵) but loses on (a) bit-exactness (1/448 is not exactly representable in IEEE 754) and (b) the dp sawtooth (dp(448) = 0.0714 vs. dp(256) = 0.0625, a 14% gap). The experiments confirm a 10–15% MSE difference favoring 256.

**Saturation proof (Appendix B).** Paired t-tests with 100 instances at (∆=7, N=4096), (∆=7, N=16384), (∆=10, N=16384), (∆=12, N=16384) yield mean differences −7×10⁻⁹, −3×10⁻⁹, −2×10⁻⁸, −1×10⁻⁸ respectively, with statistical significance only at ∆=10 and 12 (but at magnitudes three orders below the MSE floor). The conclusion: both fixes saturate to the same precision floor when combined, validating the practical recommendation that "either optimization alone suffices; the choice is driven by engineering constraints."

## Authors & Credibility

**Single-author work**: Reed Lau (Tencent). The paper positions itself as a quantitative explanation of design choices already deployed in FlashAttention-3/4 and hpc-ops, framed as a "kernel-level diagnostic" rather than a new technique. The author has direct access to the hpc-ops codebase (the hpc-ops kernel update from S=1 to S=256 is described as a concrete outcome of this work) — this is the strongest credibility signal, as the analysis is grounded in production code and has produced a measurable production change.

The bibliography (10 references) is tight and on-topic: FlashAttention [Dao 2022, Dao 2024, Shah 2024], FP8 formats [Micikevicius 2022], Attention Sink [Xiao 2024, Sun 2024, Gu 2024, Barbero 2025], and SageAttention2/2++ [Zhang 2024, 2025]. The author also provides an `experiments/proof_mathematical.py` script enumerating all 126 positive E4M3 values to verify the dp(S) claims — a small but valuable reproducibility artifact.

Credibility caveats: the paper is single-author and uses simulated (kernel-faithful) experiments rather than end-to-end model evaluations. There is no perplexity or task-accuracy number to anchor the P-cast MSE in downstream quality. The author is transparent about this (§7.4) and frames the S=256 recommendation as "zero-cost and strictly-no-worse" rather than a guaranteed quality win.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** The technical insight is more analytical than algorithmic: both optimizations (reverse iteration, S=256 scaling) were already deployed in FlashAttention-3/4 on engineering grounds (register savings, range utilization). The paper's novelty is the *quantitative explanation* — the closed-form F(∆, S), the dp(S) sawtooth characterization, and the three-condition theorem identifying S=256 as jointly optimal. This is a useful contribution to the FP8 systems literature, but not a new technique. Readers looking for novel algorithmic ideas will be disappointed; readers building FP8 kernels or debugging their precision behavior will find the diagnostic formula ∆_c = 6.93 + ln S − δₖ immediately actionable.

**Rigor.** The mathematical analysis is clean and complete. Propositions 1, 3, 4, 5 and Theorems 6, 7 are all proved in-line, and the proofs of Theorem 6(i) and 6(ii) are tight. The δₖ vs. asymptotic √(2 ln k_sink) correction is an important detail that the author handles correctly (using the exact value 1.03 rather than the asymptotic 1.67). The MSE bound uses a standard pairwise-uncorrelated V assumption (Remark 1 acknowledges this idealization and notes the bound captures O(σ²_V) scaling and the ΣPⱼ² dependence, with absolute constant calibrated empirically). The dp(S) sawtooth is verified by exhaustive enumeration (Appendix A). Where the theory is approximate (per-row m_global spread, average-case vs. worst-case), the author flags it explicitly.

**Reproducibility.** High. The simulation is described in enough detail to reproduce (Q,K,V in FP32, ℓ from pre-cast P, O from cast P·V, epilogue divide by S·ℓ), the five configurations are tabulated with their production-kernel mappings, and the `experiments/proof_mathematical.py` script is referenced. The configurations table maps each variant to a real-world implementation (FA3/4, FlashInfer, TRT-LLM XQA, SageAttention2, hpc-ops), making the "where to apply this" decision straightforward.

**Significance.** Moderate to high. The analysis is genuinely useful for FP8 kernel authors and the closed-form threshold ∆_c lets practitioners predict P-cast failure regimes analytically. The hpc-ops production update (S=1 → S=256) is concrete evidence of impact. However, the paper does not show that the P-cast MSE reduction translates to perplexity or task-accuracy improvement — and the author is honest that the QKV FP8 quantization noise floor may dominate end-to-end metrics in many regimes.

**Red flags.** None significant. The framing is conservative ("not a proposal of new techniques, but a quantitative explanation"), the limitations are stated clearly (§7.4 covers worst-case vs. average-case, QKV interaction, no end-to-end metric, scope of dp analysis), and the recommendation is appropriately caveated as "zero-cost, strictly-no-worse, to be confirmed per model." The single-author and single-affiliation scope is the only structural concern, but the analysis is self-contained and the proofs are verifiable.

## Strengths

- **Tight mathematical focus** — the dp(S) sawtooth characterization and the three-condition theorem identifying S=256 are genuinely elegant, and the closed-form F(∆, S) is a useful diagnostic formula.
- **Strong production grounding** — the analysis is tied to specific production kernels (FA3/4, FlashInfer, TRT-LLM XQA, SageAttention2, hpc-ops), and the hpc-ops update from S=1 to S=256 is presented as a concrete outcome.
- **Honest limitation discussion** — the author explicitly flags the absence of end-to-end metrics, the worst-case vs. average-case gap, the QKV quantization interaction, and the scope limits of the dp(S) analysis.
- **Actionable diagnostic** — the threshold formula ∆_c = 6.93 + ln S − δₖ lets practitioners predict P-cast failure regimes without running kernel experiments.
- **Careful attention to the dp(S) extension above 448** (Remark 3) — a subtle point that many treatments of FP8 quantization miss.

## Limitations

- **No end-to-end evaluation**: All numbers are isolated output MSE against an FP32 reference. There is no perplexity, downstream task accuracy, or model-quality measurement. The author is transparent about this, but the practical significance of the 3–10× P-cast MSE improvement remains unverified at the application level.
- **QKV quantization interaction is decoupled**: Holding Q,K,V in FP32 isolates the P-cast effect, but it also means the experiments do not capture the full FP8 noise floor. The relative improvement ratio would compress at high ∆ where QKV noise dominates, but the paper does not quantify this compression.
- **Worst-case vs. average-case gap**: The dp(S) analysis is minimax-optimal for P uniformly spanning [0, 1], but real post-softmax P is highly non-uniform (a few large values, many small). A dynamic per-block scale could improve average-case precision; the paper acknowledges this in §7.4 but does not pursue it.
- **Scope restricted to E4M3 P-cast**: The analysis does not transfer to MXFP4, FP4, or per-channel quantization, where the preconditions for the dp(S) construction break down (e.g., MXFP4's E8M0 block exponent already forces 2ᵏ scales).
- **Single-sink assumption**: The P-collapse analysis assumes a single sink cluster at position 0. Distributed sink patterns are mentioned in §7.4 but not analyzed.
- **Single affiliation, single author**: The work is from one Tencent researcher. While the analysis is verifiable, broader community adoption would benefit from independent reproduction by other FP8 kernel authors.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- **End-to-end impact**: Does switching the S factor in a production FP8 attention kernel (e.g., FlashInfer S=448 → S=256) actually move downstream perplexity or task accuracy on realistic sequence lengths (8K–128K)? The paper predicts "zero-cost, strictly-no-worse" but the QKV quantization floor may compress the improvement in practice.
- **Dynamic per-block scaling**: Given that post-softmax P is highly non-uniform, can a per-block scale S_block ∈ {2ᵏ} (constrained for bit-exactness) improve average-case precision over the static S=256? The dp(S) sawtooth analysis still applies per-block; the question is whether the overhead of computing per-block maxima justifies the gain.
- **Multi-sink patterns**: Distributed sink patterns (mentioned in §7.4 as future work) likely have different optimal S and iteration-order strategies. Can the F(∆, S) formula be generalized by replacing ∆ with a per-block maximum gap, and does reverse iteration still suffice for distributed sinks?
- **MXFP4 / FP4 P-cast analog**: The E8M0 block exponent in MXFP4 already enforces 2ᵏ scales, removing the freedom dp(S) exploits. Is there an equivalent optimality statement for MXFP4 P-cast, or does the block-level structure make the problem trivially resolved?
- **Connection to attention-output accuracy bounds**: The MSE bound (Proposition 3) is in the P-cast isolation setting. What is the relationship between the P-cast MSE and the output attention distribution's KL divergence from the FP32 reference? This would connect the precision analysis to model-quality guarantees.
- **Empirical ∆ distributions**: The paper assumes ∆ ∈ [4, 13] based on existing literature [Gu 2024, Xiao 2024]. A systematic measurement of per-model ∆ distributions across model families and context lengths would let practitioners compute the *expected* P-cast error via the closed form, without re-running kernel benchmarks.
- **Reverse iteration + causal masks**: The author notes (§7.1) that "the choice between [fixes] should be driven by engineering constraints (reverse may simplify causal mask handling; forward may offer better memory access patterns)." A careful analysis of when reverse iteration interacts poorly with causal masking or KV-cache prefill semantics would be practically valuable.
