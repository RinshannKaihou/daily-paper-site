Finding · 核心结论

Compression can decouple accuracy from uncertainty — and it often breaks in threshold-like jumps, not gradual drift.

Does Compression Preserve Uncertainty? A Unified Benchmark for Quantized and Sparse LLMs via Conformal Prediction

Yujia Tong, Yuxi Wang, Yunyang Wan, Tian Zhang, Junhao Dong, Jingling Yuan · Wuhan University of Technology · Nanyang Technological University · arXiv:2606.01850 · 2026-06-23

LLM Compression Uncertainty Quantification Conformal Prediction Quantization Pruning

Key Metric

6.9×

Reduction in compression-induced uncertainty inflation from Llama-2-7B to Llama-2-70B under W4A4 QuaRot (avg ΔSS across tasks).

Why This Matters

Accuracy-only evaluation is insufficient — compressed LLMs can preserve task accuracy while silently inflating prediction-set size, a hidden deployment risk.

Critical Edge

RED FLAG

No open-source code link accompanies the benchmark, sharply limiting reproducibility and downstream adoption for a paper whose value is precisely its protocol.

Methodology

方法核心：采用分治式共形预测（split conformal prediction），在 α = 0.1 目标覆盖率下，从校准集分布的 (1−α)(n+1)/n 分位数估计非一致性阈值 q̂，并以 LAC 与 APS 两种得分函数取平均。

Conformal scoring

Two distribution-free score functions averaged for stability:

  • LAC — s = 1 − π̂(Y|X). Tighter sets, occasionally under-covered.
  • APS — accumulates class probabilities in descending order; more adaptive coverage, larger sets.

Compression matrix

Quantization. W4A16 via RTN / AWQ / GPTQ. W4A4 via rotation-based QuaRot / SpinQuant / FlatQuant.

Pruning. 50% unstructured via Magnitude / SparseGPT / Wanda. 20% structured via LLM-Pruner / SliceGPT.

Models & tasks

12 LLMs, 1B–70B: Llama-2 (7/13/70B), Llama-3 (1/8/70B), Qwen-3 (8/14/32B/30B-A3B), DeepSeek-7B, Falcon-7B. Four families total.

5 multiple-choice NLP tasks standardized to 6 options (MMLU, CosmosQA, HellaSwag, HaluDial, HaluSum). 2,000 instances / task, 50/50 calibration-test split. α = 0.1. 8× H100.

Metrics

  • Acc — prediction accuracy.
  • CR — coverage rate (sanity check: CR ≥ 1 − α).
  • SS — average prediction-set size (primary uncertainty metric; smaller = more confident).

Results

W4A16 ≈ FP16 · W4A4 disruptive

Llama-2-70B under AWQ: 73.20 Acc / 2.18 SS vs. FP16 72.48 / 2.17.

Llama-2-7B under QuaRot: Acc collapses 47.09 → 22.04; SS inflates 3.09 → 5.65.

Three findings

  1. Accuracy ≠ Uncertainty. ΔAcc vs. ΔSS scatter is dispersed. Llama-3.1-8B under 50% Wanda drops QA Acc 62.93 → 45.97 while SS barely moves (2.98 → 3.09).
  2. Scale absorbs compression. Avg ΔSS under W4A4 QuaRot: +2.56 (7B) → +0.72 (13B) → +0.37 (70B).
  3. Threshold-like inflation. Wanda pruning sweep: Llama-3.1-8B RC SS stays 1.89–2.06 from 0–40% sparsity, then jumps to 2.53 at 50%.

MoE · Qwen3-30B-A3B

W4A16 RTN stable (Acc 46.52 → 45.64, SS 3.32 → 3.09); SliceGPT 20% collapses (Acc 16.05, SS 5.57). Highly method-dependent.

Strengths

  • Comprehensive scope. First benchmark to systematically unify quantization (weight-only & weight-activation) and pruning (structured & unstructured) under one conformal protocol.
  • Statistical rigor. Conformal prediction gives distribution-free, finite-sample coverage — far stronger than verbalized-confidence heuristics.
  • Actionable findings. Three rules translate directly into deployment: measure SS, prefer larger base models, sweep the ratio.
  • Multi-prompt averaging. 3 prompting strategies × 2 score functions reduces prompt-sensitivity noise.
  • Honest framing. Authors explicitly flag the multiple-choice limitation rather than overclaim transferability to chat or code generation.

Limitations

  • SCOPEMultiple-choice only. Sidesteps free-form generation uncertainty (semantic equivalence classes, sequence entropy). No chat / code-generation transfer.
  • SAMPLEModest N. 2,000 instances/task, 50% calibration — finer-grained SS comparisons may be under-powered.
  • FIXEDProtocol held constant. α = 0.1 and 50/50 split only; low-α high-coverage regimes unexamined.
  • CODENo public repository. A benchmark paper without open code sharply limits reproducibility.
  • COVERAGEMethods omitted. No QLoRA, KV-cache compression, or low-rank approximation.
  • MoESingle MoE probe. Findings 1–3 derive mostly from dense Llama results; MoE generalization untested.
  • F3Threshold finding is single-method. Only Wanda sparsity sweep; QuaRot & structured pruning tipping points unknown.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Open-ended generation under compression. Can conformal language modeling be combined with quantization / pruning to provide distribution-free guarantees on free-form outputs? Does the threshold-like inflation pattern transfer?
  • Phase-transition analysis. Can Finding 3 be formalized — fitting a step function or changepoint model to the sparsity-vs-SS curve — to predict tipping sparsity a priori?
  • QLoRA & PEFT under conformal prediction. Does parameter-efficient fine-tuning on a quantized backbone restore or further degrade uncertainty properties?
  • MoE generalization. Does the threshold-like inflation hold across more MoE architectures, and how does expert routing interact with quantization noise?
  • Calibration–uncertainty decoupling. Beyond SS, do ECE and conformal SS diverge under the same compression — i.e., is a model "calibrated but uncertain" a coherent regime?
  • Cost-aware deployment metric. Could a unified utility function over Acc, SS, and inference cost (FLOPs, memory) guide the Pareto-optimal compression choice per task?
  • Pre-deployment auditing tool. Can the W4A16 → W4A4 → pruning sweep be packaged as an automated "uncertainty safety audit" for compressed LLM releases?
