> **一句话总结：** 将 ROME 风格的因果追踪下沉到稀疏 MoE 路由粒度：在 Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base 上单层 L44 信号可进一步定位到单一专家 L44E069（spec ≈ +0.40），而在 Mixtral-8x7B-v0.1 上 L19 信号在选定单专家 L19E006 上不特异（spec ≈ −0.18），需 routed top-2 / routing-union 多专家协同才能恢复层级别信号——表明 MoE 事实回路的专家级定位并非通用，而是模型/协议相关的。

> **TL;DR:** This paper extends ROME-style causal tracing to sparse Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) language models, asking whether the routed expert (not just the MoE block) carries the factual-recall signal. Using CounterFact with subject-embedding noise, the authors run a two-stage protocol: (1) MoE-block-output patching + layer sweep, then (2) expert-level ablation-difference patching at the selected layer. On Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base (48 MoE layers, 128 experts, top-8), layer L44 is selected (rescue = +0.901) and expert L44E069 is recurrent on discovery cases, validates on held-out cases, and outperforms same-layer active-random controls (rescue = +0.463, specificity = +0.400). On Mixtral-8x7B-v0.1 (32 MoE layers, 8 experts, top-2), L19 is selected with a broader mid-layer band (rescue = +0.457, gap to next layer only 0.038), but the singleton L19E006 is non-specific (rescue = +0.099, specificity = −0.175); clean-routed top-2 and clean/noised routing-union coalitions recover the layer-level signal. The conclusion: expert-level localization in MoE factual tracing is feasible but model- and protocol-dependent, not a universal property.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **expert-aware causal tracing**，将 ROME 风格的事实可追溯性方法从稠密 Transformer 的 MLP 粒度下沉到稀疏 MoE 的 **routed expert 粒度**，区分 MoE-block 级别 patching 与 per-expert contribution patching。
- 设计 **两阶段协议**：阶段一用 clean MoE-block output 替换 noised run 并在 discovery cases 上做层扫描；阶段二在选定层用 **ablation-difference** 估计每个 clean-active 专家的贡献 δe，把 clean-minus-noised 的专家 patch 注入 noised run。
- 引入 **recurrence-first + specificity** 的专家选择规则：先要求候选专家在 ≥64/128 discovery cases 中 clean-active（避免单次偶发），再用 **active-random 对照集**（同源、同层、clean-active 但非选定专家）做特异性检验，避免选择偏差。
- 在 Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base 上定位出 **单一专家 L44E069**（gate-weight-matched 与 equal-norm 对照均通过），在 Mixtral-8x7B-v0.1 上证明 **coalition 现象**：层信号由多专家协同承载，单专家无法特异恢复。
- 给出完整可复现协议：单 token true/foil、clean margin ≥ 1.0、subject-noise drop ≥ 0.5、σ=3·embedding std、5000 bootstrap、10000 sign-flip；并附带 relaxed-filter 512-case scale-up 与 L44E069 在 25 种 split/threshold 组合下的稳定性验证。

## Key Contributions

- **Methodology.** A two-stage MoE causal-tracing protocol: (i) MoE-block-output rescue with layer sweep on discovery cases; (ii) expert-level ablation-difference patch δe = ce(xclean) − ce(xnoised) where ce(x) = MoEOut(x) − MoEOut∖e(x) is the routed update of expert e under the original top-k routing (no rerouting, no renormalization).
- **Expert-selection rule.** Recurrence-first: candidate expert must be clean-active in ≥64/128 discovery cases. Specificity: Spec = Rescue(e*) − mean(Rescue(r)) over same-source same-layer clean-active controls.
- **Empirical finding (Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base).** Layer sweep selects L44 with sharp localization (next-best L42 = +0.592, gap = +0.309). Expert-level tracing selects L44E069 (clean-active on 112/128 discovery and 116/128 validation cases): validation rescue = +0.463, specificity = +0.400, expert/layer ratio = +0.515, spec/layer = +0.444.
- **Empirical finding (Mixtral-8x7B-v0.1).** Layer L19 validates (rescue = +0.457) but with a broad mid-layer band (next-best L21 = +0.496, gap = +0.038). The selected singleton L19E006 has rescue = +0.099 and **negative** specificity = −0.175. Clean-routed top-2 and clean/noised routing-union coalitions recover rescue close to the L19 MoE-block effect.
- **Robustness.** Qwen3 L44E069 is selected in all 5 split seeds × 5 recurrence thresholds (25 settings); held-out across five relation folds; gate-weight-matched and equal-norm controls both keep specificity positive (+0.459 raw / +0.188 equal-norm). Relaxed-filter 512-case scale-up preserves the Qwen-positive / Mixtral-boundary pattern.
- **Methodological lesson.** MoE-block rescue is necessary but not sufficient evidence of expert-level localization. Causal evidence requires patching the estimated expert update and comparing it to active controls; router selection alone is not a causal claim.

## Methodology

The protocol extends Meng et al. (2022) ROME-style causal tracing to sparse MoE.

- **Factual metric.** For each CounterFact record, define the true-vs-foil next-token logit difference Δ(x; at, af) = ℓx(at) − ℓx(af). The object token is never included in the input.
- **Corruption.** Add Gaussian noise to subject-token embeddings: hnoised = hs + ε, ε ∼ N(0, σ²I), with σ = 3.0 × embedding-matrix std. The "subject-noise drop" Δclean − Δnoised measures corruption strength.
- **Stage 1 — MoE-block rescue.** Cache the final-position output of MoE block at layer ℓ from a clean run; in the noised run, replace the corresponding MoE-block output with its clean value. Rescueℓ = Δpatched,ℓ − Δnoised. Layers selected on 128 discovery cases, then fixed and evaluated on 128 held-out validation cases.
- **Stage 2 — expert-level rescue.** For clean-active expert e at the selected layer, estimate its routed update ce(x) = MoEOut(x) − MoEOut∖e(x) by suppressing e's contribution under the **original top-k routing** (no rerouting, no weight renormalization). Form the clean-minus-noised expert patch δe = ce(xclean) − ce(xnoised) and add it to the noised MoE-block output at the final position.
- **Expert selection.** Recurrence-first: candidate must be clean-active in ≥ 64/128 discovery cases. Specificity: Spec = Rescue(e*) − mean over same-source same-layer clean-active controls. Qwen3 uses 3 active-random controls per case; Mixtral uses 1 (since top-2 routing makes the unique other clean-active expert the natural control).
- **Models.** Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base (48 MoE layers, 128 experts/layer, top-8) and Mixtral-8x7B-v0.1 (32 MoE layers, 8 experts/layer, top-2).
- **Filtering.** Single-token true/foil, clean margin ≥ 1.0, subject-noise drop ≥ 0.5. 256 usable cases per model; 128 discovery + 128 validation.
- **Statistics.** 5,000 bootstrap resamples; 10,000 two-sided sign-flip tests. Qwen3 anchor-active controls (gate-weight-matched, equal-norm) and a relaxed-filter 512-case scale-up are reported as robustness checks.

| Qwen3  | L44   | +0.901 [+0.752, +1.053]       | L44E069  | +0.463 [+0.344, +0.590]      | +0.400 [+0.276, +0.533]    |
| Mixtral| L19   | +0.457 [+0.331, +0.579]       | L19E006  | +0.099 [+0.018, +0.175]      | **−0.175** [−0.284, −0.072] |

- **Qwen3 layer sweep is sharp** (L44 vs L42: gap = +0.309). Mixtral is broad (L21 vs L19: gap = +0.038) — interpretable as mid-layer transfer, not sharp single-layer localization.
- **Expert-to-layer ratio:** Qwen3 expert/layer = +0.515, spec/layer = +0.444 (single expert captures ~51% of the block-level rescue). Mixtral expert/layer = +0.216, spec/layer = **−0.383** (single expert underperforms controls).
- **Relation-wise breakdown (Qwen3, selected relations):** P937 work location Spec = +1.350 (n=5), P131 admin. location = +1.160 (n=6), P17 country = +0.910 (n=6), P159 HQ = +0.988 (n=5), P27 citizenship = +0.780 (n=9), P103 native language = +0.406 (n=12). All positive.
- **Relation-wise breakdown (Mixtral):** most retained relations have negative specificity for L19E006 (e.g., P136 genre = −0.562, P131 admin. location = −0.557, P178 developer = −0.368); only P103 native language and P106 occupation are weakly positive.
- **Coalition check (Mixtral L19):** clean-routed top-2 and clean/noised routing-union coalition patches recover rescue close to the L19 MoE-block effect.
- **Anchor-active controls (Qwen3, n=116):** gate-weight-matched control raw rescue = +0.054 vs selected +0.513 → Spec = +0.459; equal-norm variant Spec = +0.188 — both positive.
- **Rank comparison (Qwen3, n=115):** L44E069 is top-1 in 53/115 and top-2 in 74/115 among all clean-active layer-44 experts; mean rank 2.90, mean percentile 0.73.
- **Stability:** L44E069 is selected in all 25 split-seed × threshold combinations (mean validation rescue +0.398, specificity +0.344) and in all 5 relation-held-out folds.
- **Noise-scale sensitivity:** L44E069 intervention stays positive across σ variations (fixed-hypothesis check).
- **Relaxed-filter 512-case scale-up:** main Qwen-positive / Mixtral-boundary pattern preserved.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The conceptual move from MoE-block patching to per-expert ablation-difference patching is a natural but non-trivial extension of ROME to MoE, and the recurrence-first + specificity selection rule is a real methodological improvement over "pick the most-active expert." This is genuinely new territory; prior MoE interpretability work (Fayyaz et al. 2026; Li et al. 2025; Gu et al. 2026) has mostly focused on steering / editing, not on causal localization of factual recall.
- **Rigor.** Strong. Single-token objects, explicit margin/noise-drop filters, fixed-hypothesis held-out validation, bootstrap and sign-flip CIs, gate-weight-matched and equal-norm controls, relation-held-out folds, 5 × 5 split/threshold stability grid, and a relaxed-filter 512-case scale-up. The protocol is much more thorough than typical MoE interpretability work.
- **Reproducibility.** The authors provide scripts, row-level outputs, hook points, and exact CounterFact case IDs (Table 8). Compute is "nontrivial" (large sparse MoE checkpoints), so exact-bit reproducibility will require substantial GPU. The discovery/validation case ID list, however, removes a major source of variance.
- **Significance.** A genuinely useful diagnostic. It reframes the right unit of analysis: in MoE models, "which MLP layer?" is the wrong question — the right question is "which routed expert within which layer?" For practitioners doing expert pruning, expert merging, or expert-level knowledge editing, this protocol is the right level of granularity.
- **Red flags / honest concerns.**
  - **Two models is thin for an architecture-level claim.** Qwen3 (top-8 of 128) and Mixtral (top-2 of 8) differ in *six* dimensions: layer count, expert count, routing arity, training data, tokenizer, and the *amount of routing-decision information per token*. The authors are honest about this ("two controlled case studies, not a broad survey") but the headline asymmetry (single expert vs. coalition) is partly confounded with routing arity. A top-8 model can afford sparse, specialized experts; a top-2 model must reuse capacity. Without a third model (e.g., DeepSeek-MoE with fine-grained experts) the claim that the asymmetry is *architecture-driven* is under-supported.
  - **The Mixtral coalition is operationally under-defined.** "Clean-routed top-2" and "clean/noised routing-union" are two different coalitions with different semantic interpretations. There is no principled criterion for *which* coalition to choose; both happen to recover the signal, but a coalition check that succeeds for any subset of size k is itself a weak result.
  - **Filtering is strict and possibly bias-inducing.** 256 cases is small; relations with single-token, high-margin, noise-sensitive objects are over-represented. The relaxed-filter 512-case check helps, but only 15/256 cases overlap with the strict set, which means the relaxed validation is a partly different distribution.
  - **No multi-token objects, no subject-position sweep, no complete subject-to-answer circuit.** Acknowledged as limitations.
  - **Equal-norm control weakens the Qwen3 result.** Raw Spec = +0.459; equal-norm Spec = +0.188. The drop suggests that part of L44E069's "specialness" is patch-vector magnitude, not just direction. The authors still keep the result positive and significant, but a critical reader should weight the +0.188 number, not the +0.459 number, when judging how unique the expert is.
  - **No counterfactual / editing demonstration.** A causal tracing result is most convincing when combined with a causal editing result ("if we ablate L44E069, the model loses the fact"). The paper stops at the diagnostic step.
- **Bottom line.** A well-executed, methodologically careful, and intellectually honest diagnostic study. The Qwen3 L44E069 result is the strongest single-expert localization evidence in MoE factual tracing to date, and the Qwen/Mixtral asymmetry is a real and interesting finding. The architectural interpretation, however, rests on n=2 models and a partly confounded comparison; it should be cited as a "first controlled study" rather than as a settled property of MoE.

## Strengths

- Right level of granularity: the paper picks the *expert* as the unit of analysis, not the MoE block.
- Strong, well-documented protocol: CounterFact with strict filters, held-out discovery/validation split, two active-random controls, gate-weight-matched and equal-norm controls, noise-scale sensitivity, 5×5 split/threshold grid, 5 relation-held-out folds, and a 512-case scale-up.
- Recurrence-first expert selection is a meaningful methodological contribution: it prevents single-case "spurious expert" findings, a common failure mode in MoE interpretability.
- Cross-model contrast: highlights a genuine empirical asymmetry between low-arity (Mixtral) and high-arity (Qwen3) routing.
- Reproducibility: scripts, row-level outputs, hook points, and exact CounterFact case IDs are released.
- Ethics statement is concrete: notes dual-use risk of fine-grained factual localization (targeted manipulation / suppression / editing).

## Limitations

- Only two MoE models, with different routing arities, layer counts, expert counts, tokenizers, and training data. The headline asymmetry is partly confounded.
- 256 cases per model (128 discovery + 128 validation) is small; relation coverage is biased toward relations with single-token, high-margin objects.
- Multi-token objects, multi-position interventions, and full subject-to-answer circuits are out of scope.
- The Mixtral coalition is operationally under-defined — no principled size or composition rule.
- The Qwen3 equal-norm specificity drops from +0.459 to +0.188, weakening the "this expert is uniquely special" claim.
- No demonstration that the identified expert can be edited or ablated to causally change a fact (i.e., the diagnostic is not yet connected to a downstream causal intervention).
- "Coalition" terminology risks overinterpretation — it could just mean "the layer-level signal is distributed across whichever experts happen to route, with no fixed structure."

## Relevance

与研究兴趣高度相关。本文属于 MoE 语言模型机制可解释性方向，把 ROME 风格的因果追踪下沉到 routed expert 粒度，区分 MoE-block-level rescue 与 per-expert rescue，并提出 recurrence-first + specificity 的专家选择规则；对理解"专家是否对应可解释的功能单元"以及后续做专家级编辑/剪枝/合并具有直接借鉴意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the Qwen3/Mixtral asymmetry replicate on a third MoE architecture (e.g., DeepSeek-V2/V3 with fine-grained experts, or DBRX) to disentangle routing arity from architecture?
- Can a coalition be defined operationally (size, weight threshold, intersection across prompts) rather than post hoc, so the Mixtral finding is testable rather than descriptive?
- Does L44E069 survive under SAE decomposition — i.e., is the functional unit an expert, or a feature within an expert?
- Can the identified expert be *edited* (e.g., ablated or perturbed) to causally flip the fact, closing the loop between causal tracing and causal editing?
- How does the result change with subject-position / relation-position sweeps, multi-token objects, and non-final-position interventions?
- Is there a learned or heuristic rule predicting whether a given MoE layer will admit singleton-expert localization (e.g., sparsity of routing, number of active experts, training-stage)?
- Does the result hold under different noise distributions and corruption strengths beyond the σ-sensitivity check?
- Can the protocol be extended to multilingual factual recall, given the LMU group's expertise in multilingual MoE routing (Bandarkar et al. 2026)?
- How does the identified expert interact with attention-head-level evidence (Wang et al. 2023-style circuits) — is it part of a larger circuit or is the MoE expert a sufficient node?
- Does pruning the identified expert (or merging it with another) degrade factual performance in proportion to the rescue score, providing an out-of-distribution test of the localization claim?
