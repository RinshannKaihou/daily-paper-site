An agentic explore–plan–execute loop solves two Simons Workshop open problems — a CG-vs-RCD phase diagram and a QRCP bounded-coherence counterexample family.

Iteris: Agentic Research Loops for Computational Mathematics ↗
Leheng Chen, Zihao Liu, Wanyi He, Bin Dong (corresp.) Peking University · Zhongguancun Academy 2026-06-02
agentic-ai computational-math linear-systems qr-factorization theorem-proving

Key Metric

100+ iters / problem

with foundation, experiment, proof & review agents in every cycle

Why This Matters

Resolves two real Simons Workshop open problems — quantitative rate bounds for CG vs RCD on power-law spectra, plus a strong negative result ruling out a QRCP pivot-quality guarantee even under bounded coherence.

Critical Edge

Two case studies don't prove Iteris handles arbitrary open problems — chosen problems may decompose too cleanly into numerical-plus-proof phases, and no negative-case analysis is reported.

01 · Methodology

Three-phase per-iteration protocol over a file-mediated shared memory:

  • Explore — repository-level probes + advisory on task shape, local-minimum checks, and risks.
  • Plan — consumes the advisory plus global project files, writes TASK_POOL.json with category, goal, inputs, scope, verification, and result contract.
  • Execute — four specialized agents (Foundation, Experiment, Proof, Review) each take one well-scoped action and write a structured result back to memory.

Foundation freezes definitions and conjecture cards; Experiment runs numerical work and counterexample searches (evidence ≠ proof); Proof constructs proof artifacts for stable statements; Review evaluates evidence, gaps, and route value. Implemented with the OpenAI Codex agent on GPT-5.5.

02 · Results

Theorem 1 — fixed-parameter phase diagram for the asymptotic CG-vs-RCD ratio ρn(ε, p) on power-law spectra, split by the active degree K(p) and CG floor F(p).

Theorem 2 — answers Simons Problem 4.3 in the negative: for every (H*, B, e, K) there exists an orthonormal-row Q with normalized coherence µ(Q) < H* such that QRCP selects a badly-conditioned M(Q) with

γ(Q, Ipiv) / √(k(n−k+1)) > B ke

The QRCP proof is fully verified in Lean 4 via the autoformalization agent Archon; the CG proof is verified in Lean by Zhangsong Li.

+ Strengths

  • Two genuine open problems from a Simons Workshop collection — not toy benchmarks.
  • Unusually transparent role assignment: what Iteris contributed vs. what humans had to fix.
  • Direct GPT-Pro baseline makes the value of multi-turn exploration explicit.
  • Lean 4 verification of the QRCP proof via Archon adds independent audit.
  • Play-by-play trajectory analyses (Sections 4.2.2 & 4.3.2) show which proof modules were blocked, repaired, or restructured.

− Limitations

  • Architectural novelty is modest — explore-plan-execute composes established LLM-agent primitives.
  • No ablation, no compute report (tokens, wall-clock, per-iteration intervention counts).
  • Iteris codebase is not released; only the QRCP Lean repo and Archon are public.
  • Theorem 1's rate bounds (OP(1/bn)) require bn to grow super-slowly — explicitly conservative envelopes, not asymptotic orders.
  • GPT-Pro comparison is qualitative; no controlled benchmark.
  • Lean 4 ≠ correctness — semantic alignment between formal statement and the informal theorem required human curation.

03 · Questions for Further Exploration

  • Sharp rates — what is the true asymptotic order of ρn(ε, p) in each regime, beyond the conservative envelopes?
  • Lower bounds — is the bounded-away η(p, ε) tight? Does ρn concentrate or carry a nontrivial limit distribution?
  • Critical boundary — does the K ≥ 1 obstruction appear in the active interior, or only at the floor ε = F(p)?
  • Ablations — what happens with Foundation or Review removed, or with file-memory swapped for conversation-memory?
  • Model dependence — does Iteris's success transfer from GPT-5.5 to GPT-4-class or open-source reasoning models (DeepSeek-R1, Qwen3)?
  • Beyond Simons — can the pattern extend to pure mathematics (proof-only feedback) or natural-science research (experimental-only feedback)?
  • Pivot variants — do similar obstructions hold for partial pivoting, Rook pivoting, or randomized pivoting rules?
