01 · METHODOLOGY
Four components, four evolution stages

Behaviorally-grounded factorization

The generator factorizes trip-chain generation into four interpretable decisions — each with its own conditional probability and a clear behavioral role:

P1
Participation
p(zi | xi)
out-of-home?
P2
Retrieval

pick a routine
P3
Adaptation
p(ỹi | xi, zi, ri)
personalize
P4
Refinement
p(ŷi | ỹi, xi)
finalize chain

Self-evolution loop (one round)

  • Rollout — apply current generator G(k) on evolution population Xtar to produce synthetic trip chains Ŷ(k).
  • Attribution — compare Ŷ(k) with Dtar via evaluator Emob; localize failures by time slot, persona, and representative chains.
  • Heuristic Evolution — Codex + GPT-5.5 proposes candidate G̃(k+1); promote only after empirical verification.
  • Memory Evolution — append trial τ(k) (failure mode, hypothesis, intervention, outcome) to M(k+1) so later rounds accumulate.
02 · RESULTS
SOTA on both cities, all three dimensions

Singapore test (lower is better)

Overall 0.5176 — Individual 0.3839 · Distribution 0.0578 · Behavior 0.0759. Beats best baseline AIRL BC 0.7854 by −34.1%; Behavior is 3.8× better (0.0759 vs. 0.2880).

Montreal test

Overall 1.6762 vs. AIRL BC 1.8790 — −10.8%; Behavior 2.2× better (0.1172 vs. 0.2556). LLMob zero-shot stays weak on Distribution (4.7400).

Inference speed (Singapore)

7.65 ms/sample — same order as MNL (3.05 ms); ~300× faster than LLMob few-shot (3200 ms) and 420× faster than zero-shot (2200 ms).

Evolution trajectory

Validation score 0.8621 → 0.5118 over hundreds of rounds (−40.6%). Improvements are not smooth — they are discrete jumps tied to concrete behavioral fixes (e.g. "keep senior workers at Work later on weekends", "preserve reliable retrieved routines").

03 · STRENGTHS
What lands
  • Unifies all four desired properties — interpretability, plausibility, alignment, efficiency — in one system. A genuine first.
  • −34.1% / −10.8% overall error vs. strongest baselines on Singapore / Montreal; consistent across all three metric families.
  • Audit trail per evolution round — each performance jump links to a behavioral failure and a targeted heuristic update.
  • 7.65 ms/sample — orders of magnitude faster than LLM-based methods (2200–3200 ms); competitive with the lightest baselines.
  • Comprehensive baselines across 3 paradigms; rigorous ablation isolating the structural prior; multi-seed robustness check.
  • Detailed metric definitions in appendix (Singapore: 17 behavior JSDs; Montreal: 10 behavior JSDs) make the evaluator reproducible.
04 · LIMITATIONS
Where to be careful
  • Overfitting to the evaluator — repeated rounds can specialize to the frozen evaluator; acknowledged but not quantified.
  • Partial interpretability — the four-component structure is conceptual; actual hs/ is procedural Python whose readability is never evaluated.
  • Closed-source agent stack — Codex + GPT-5.5 dependence makes exact replication hard; only "notes" and "diffs" released, not full prompts or chains.
  • Only two cities, only typical-day trip chains over 24 hourly slots — generalization is asserted, not demonstrated.
  • Single trip-maker-per-record — within-person variability and day-to-day dynamics are not modeled.
  • No privacy / segment-level fairness audit, despite raising the concern in ethical considerations.
05 · QUESTIONS FOR FURTHER EXPLORATION
Open threads worth chasing
  1. How does MobEvolve transfer to cities with substantially different mobility cultures — car-dominated suburbs, transit-rich megacities, or low-income settings with sparse data?
  2. Can the four-component factorization be extended with destination-choice, mode-choice, or multi-day dependency modules and still benefit from agentic self-evolution?
  3. What is the right way to bound overfitting to the frozen evaluator — a held-out evolution set, or a cross-city transfer evaluation?
  4. How sensitive is performance to the choice of LLM agent (GPT-5.5 vs. Claude Opus 4 vs. open-source DeepSeek-Coder / Qwen-Coder)?
  5. Can the evolution memory become a retrieval-augmented vector store so thousands of rounds don't blow context?
  6. Does the agent surface bias when the reference data under-represents low-income or minority groups — and can it be steered to repair it?
