Why This Matters Brings self-evolving agents from games and QA into high-stakes clinical prediction — catching the hidden high-risk never-smokers that current models overlook.

Methodology

方法

Two complementary evolving paths, one chain

Built on the Traj-CoA worker–LTM–manager chain over XML-serialized, time-sliced EHRs (up to 5 years, multimodal). Self-evolution happens via two parallel mechanisms:

  • ExPool (non-parametric memory). Rejection-sample rollouts, keep the highest-risk trace for cases / lowest for controls, embed the Worker summary, store reasoning + score + label in a vector DB. At inference, retrieve top-k "patients-like-me" as Manager in-context examples.
  • MARL (parametric memory). Extend RAFT to the CoA chain: hard-reward filter rollouts (case s≥6, control s≤4), decouple training into a Worker set (temporal reasoning) and a Manager set (risk aggregation).
  • Leave-one-out cross-retrieval. During MARL rollouts, build E\{i} per training patient so retrieval-augmented inputs match the inference distribution — no leakage.
  • Stack. GPT-OSS-20B base · nomic-embed-text-v1.5 embeddings · m=4 rollouts, τ=1.5 · QLoRA (rank 32) on 4 GPUs · top-k ∈ {5, 10, 15}.

Strengths · Limitations

优势与局限

Strengths

  • First self-evolving agents on real clinical EHR. Moves the paradigm from games/QA into high-risk medicine.
  • Two mechanisms, unified. ExPool + MARL are complementary, not competing — visible in the density scatter.
  • Hard subgroup win. AUROC +0.06 and AUPRC +0.05 in never-smokers — exactly where traditional risk models collapse.
  • Distribution-aligned training. Leave-one-out retrieval cleanly solves the train/inference gap.
  • Reproducible. System + user prompts disclosed (Tables 4, 5); QLoRA + 4-GPU footprint is reasonable; HIPAA-compliant.

Limitations

  • Single-center, retrospective. External validity unverified; test set n=90 / 27 cases — statistical power limited.
  • Right answer ≠ right reasoning. Final score matching y says nothing about intermediate steps (paper acknowledges this).
  • Label noise. True cancer labels arrive months-to-years later — verification noise and loss-to-follow-up bias possible.
  • No comparison to medical LLMs. Misses MedGemma, Med-PaLM, EHR-MLM families.
  • 1:10 case–control mismatch. Far from real deployment prevalence; calibration untested.
  • No clinician evaluation. LLM-as-judge is a proxy — no blinded physician review or outcome follow-up.

Questions for Further Exploration

延伸问题
  • Replace the Worker-summary embedding with task-aware contrastive embeddings (or fused structured LTM features) — does retrieval purity stabilize on never-smokers?
  • Swap the thresholded hard reward for dense / process-level rewards (clinical consistency per step, retrieval usage rate) — does Worker loss converge faster?
  • Transfer ExPool + MARL to other diseases and EHR lengths (ICU time-series, multi-disease co-prediction).
  • At real-deployment prevalence, does the score remain well-calibrated? Pair with conformal prediction for coverage-guaranteed risk intervals.
  • Generalize leave-one-out to multi-center streaming: deduplication, privacy, version control of the experience pool.
  • Counterfactual retrieval: inject mislabeled "bad experiences" into ExPool — does the system wrongly evolve, motivating human-in-the-loop governance?
  • Validate GPT-OSS-120B-as-judge with blinded clinician review and longitudinal patient outcomes.
