Methodology

The training stack is decomposed into nested transformer blocks; inside each block, sibling operators (attention heads, MLP projections, MoE experts) are monitored via their current gradient norm nb,t = ‖gb,t‖₂. Two dimensionless scores quantify local risk:

GNMRt(θ[u,k]) = ‖gt[u,k]‖ / meanτ<t ‖gτ[u,k]‖  |  ∆-GNMRt = GNMRt / mean of last δ GNMR values

At each step, the controller computes Rb,t = max{GNMR/αt, ∆-GNMR/βt}, forms a candidate pool Ct = {b : R > 1} ∪ {locked units}, and routes the top-maxO ranked units to a higher-fidelity path — capping instantaneous memory and casting overhead.

ΔMt = Σb ∈ At cb ≤ maxO · cmax  |  Lock counters reset to Tlock on selection, decay otherwise (anti-thrashing).

Granularity: projection operators in LLaMA attention + SwiGLU MLP (activations 4-bit, weights/optimizer BF16); whole-block recovery for DeepSeek-style recipes (24 layers, maxO=6 ⇒ at most 25% layers per step).

Results & Highlights

4-bit activation stress (LLaMA-2 / C4-en)

Naïve token-wise 4-bit fails to converge at 1.3B (✗). GNMR-controlled 4/8-bit reaches PPL 30.59 / 24.66 / 18.84 / 15.71 across 60M→1.3B, matching the fixed 8-bit baseline (30.88 / 25.13 / 18.59 / 15.68) and BF16 references (30.79 / 25.06 / 18.80 / 15.56).

DeepSeek-V3-style hierarchy

Dynamic 6/8-bit at 13.1B tokens: 15.20 PPL vs 15.18 (fixed 8-bit) and 15.06 (fixed 16-bit). Dynamic 8/16-bit: 15.10 PPL vs 15.06. Two-stage threshold (1.5 init / 1.1 main) is robust throughout training.

Throughput preserved

LLaMA-2 1.3B with dynamic 8/16-bit runs at 1.264 s/step · 103,696 tok/s — marginally faster than fixed 8-bit (1.266 s) and within 2.4% of fixed 16-bit. Recovery overhead is in the noise.

LLaMA-2 13B LoRA (maxO=10)

INT8/BF16 with GNMR+∆: GSM8K 15.01 (BF16: 15.09, fixed INT8: 14.71), MMLU 29.92 (matches BF16), HellaSwag 64.67, WikiText-2 PPL 5.4511. Beats fixed INT8 on every metric.

Strengths

  • Clean abstraction. Reframes low-precision instability as budgeted runtime control, decoupling controller quality from kernels.
  • Cheap, online signals. Only the current gradient norm and a running mean — no extra backward, no Jacobian estimation.
  • Strong quality preservation. Near-BF16 PPL at 4-bit activations, fixed-16-bit PPL at 8/16-bit dynamic, with <0.2% trigger rate.
  • Multi-level evidence. Pre-training (60M–3B), recipe integration (DeepSeek-V3), and 13B LoRA fine-tuning separated cleanly.
  • Theoretical motivation. Theorem 1 + Corollary 1 + Theorem 2 give a coherent narrative even without end-to-end convergence.
  • Implementation clarity. Operator vs. block granularity and dual-path interface spelled out per setting.

Limitations

  • Backend coupling. Requires the stack to expose both a low-cost and a higher-fidelity path for some units.
  • Engineering overhead unquantified at production scale; the 1.3B throughput number may not extrapolate to larger clusters.
  • No automated calibration of αt, βt, Tlock, maxO; the two-stage schedule is heuristic.
  • Limited architecture coverage. All pre-training is LLaMA-2 / C4-en; MoE / PanGu are appendix-only.
  • No end-to-end convergence theorem. Theory is bound-level and diagnostic, not finite-sample.
  • Fine-tuning is LoRA-only at 13B. Not a full-scale pre-training of a 13B+ model.
  • Partial comparisons. SPAM, GSNR, GSS, spike-aware Adam are not head-to-head in the main text.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can TopmaxO be replaced with a learnable bandit / small RL controller using GNMR / ∆-GNMR as state features, adapting α, β, Tlock online?
  • How does GNMR behave when the recovery path is itself approximate (e.g. FP4 → FP8 instead of FP8 → BF16), and at what cmax does maxO bind?
  • Is there a principled calibration for α, β based on training-trace statistics (e.g. a high quantile of historical GNMR) that removes the two-stage schedule?
  • Can operator-level GNMR be aggregated into a layer- or block-level risk score with approximation guarantees, cutting monitoring overhead at very large scales?
  • How does GNMR interact with optimizer-level stabilizers (SPAM, spike-aware Adam) — complementary, redundant, or antagonistic?
  • What is the cost of providing the dual-path interface (low-cost + recovery) at production scale in Megatron-LM, FSDP, or DeepSpeed?
  • Can GNMR be extended to MoE training, where expert-level gradient statistics have very different typical scales and recovery at expert granularity may be more natural?
  • Does the same controller transfer to non-Transformer architectures (state-space, hybrid SSM-Attention) and to other modalities (vision, multimodal)?
  • How sensitive is the LLaMA-2 13B downstream preservation to maxO = 10 — is there a sweet spot in the recovery-overhead trade-off at large scale?
  • Can historical GNMR statistics serve as a curriculum signal (layer-wise "stability maturation") to inform LR schedules or precision warm-up?
