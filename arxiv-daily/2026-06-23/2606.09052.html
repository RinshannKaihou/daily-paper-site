INFUSER: Influence-Guided Self-Evolution Improves Reasoning  ↗

Siyu Chen, Miao Lu, Beining Wu, Heejune Sheen, Fengzhuo Zhang, Shuangning Li, Zhiyuan Li, Jose Blanchet, Tianhao Wang, Zhuoran Yang  ·  Tsinghua & Stanford
arXiv:2606.09052 2026-06-23 cs.LG · cs.CL · cs.AI self-evolution reinforcement-learning reasoning co-training curriculum-learning
Key Metric
>20%
relative gain on Olympiad & SuperGPQA over strong self-evolution baselines — measured on Qwen3-8B-Base, no human-curated data.
Bonus: an 8B co-evolving generator beats a frozen 32B thinking generator on math & coding — "purpose-trained small > generic large".
Why This Matters
A portable, influence-driven curation idea: reward the data generator by its usefulness to the current solver, not its difficulty. Borrowable for any LLM data-filtering, RLVR, or curriculum-learning pipeline — not just reasoning.
Critical Edge
The 8B-beats-32B headline is striking but not compute-matched — readers shouldn't generalize it to "8B suffices" in general. The faithfulness of the influence-function approximation — and its cost per generator step — remains the key open question.
Methodology

Two roles, one LLM, two reward functions.

A Generator drafts (question, reference answer) pairs from an unstructured document pool. A Solver trains on them with standard correctness rewards.

The generator is rewarded by an optimizer-aware influence score — a one-step, gradient-based estimate of how much each candidate would change the solver's loss on the target distribution. Because this continuous, high-variance signal breaks vanilla GRPO, the authors introduce DuGRPO, a dual-normalized GRPO variant. The full loop turns the document pool into a dynamic curriculum that co-adapts with the solver.

Results & Highlights

Qwen3-8B-Base, no humans in the loop.

  • >20% relative gain on Olympiad and SuperGPQA over strong self-evolution baselines.
  • 8B co-evolving generator outperforms a frozen 32B thinking generator on math & coding.
  • Each design choice (influence reward, DuGRPO, co-evolution) is necessary — removing any one degrades results.
  • Extensions: applies to instruction-tuned anchors and composes cleanly with rule-verifiable RLVR data.
  • Code released — the main positive reproducibility signal.
Critical Reading

Strengths

  • Tackles a real bottleneck: data curation cost for self-improving reasoners.
  • Clean conceptual move — usefulness > difficulty as the generator's objective.
  • Headline numbers (20% gain; 8B > 32B generator) are genuinely interesting and well-chosen.
  • Novel algorithmic contribution (DuGRPO), not a prompt-engineering tweak.
  • Code released; recipe composes with instruction tuning and RLVR.

Limitations

  • Influence-function approximation cost & faithfulness are the riskiest detail — which proxy (LiSSA? TracIn? exact)?
  • DuGRPO design space is large; ablations on alternative normalizations are missing.
  • Sensitivity to the document pool (size, domain, language) not quantified.
  • Evaluation breadth is narrow — Olympiad, SuperGPQA, math/code; no ARC-AGI, MMLU-Pro, GPQA Diamond.
  • Compute parity absent; single base-model family (Qwen3); long-horizon (10+ rounds) stability unreported.
Questions for Further Exploration
  • How faithful is the "optimizer-aware" influence approximation to true influence functions, and what is its wall-clock overhead per generator step?
  • Does the influence score stay well-calibrated across many self-evolution rounds, or does it collapse into a difficulty proxy?
  • How sensitive is INFUSER to the size, domain, and quality of the initial document pool?
  • Can the influence-guided generator combine with process-reward models (PRMs) or verifier-based filtering for additional gains?
  • Does DuGRPO generalize beyond influence-style rewards — e.g. to any continuous, high-variance, dense-but-noisy signal?
  • How does INFUSER behave under different base-model families (LLaMA, Mistral) and sizes (1.5B → 70B)?
  • Can the framework run offline — curating a static dataset for an independent solver — to decouple generator and solver training?
  • Is there a theoretical justification (or a regret/optimality bound) for influence-driven curricula, beyond the empirical evidence?
