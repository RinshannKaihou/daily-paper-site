Hero · core claim

Find which heads + neurons actually cause model behavior — 229× faster than greedy.

PGB-CT turns multi-component causal tracing from a brute-force combinatorial search into a single gradient-based continuous optimization with soft masks, a transformed reward, and a scheduled binarity penalty — and the paper shows the joint effect of two heads or two MLP layers is not the sum of their individual effects, contradicting the linear-additivity assumption behind prior ablations.

Why this matters

As LLMs push past 10⁴ candidate components, exhaustive and greedy causal tracing break down. PGB-CT keeps the method usable on GPT2-xl, LLaMA-13B, Qwen3-1.7B and Llama-3.2-1B — and gives reusable engineering for gender bias and factual recall ablations.

Sharpest critique

Baselines are weakened: greedy stops at 40% sparsity instead of running to the target size, and "random" picks the best of only 1,000 subsets — both choices inflate PGB-CT's apparent wall-clock margin.

01 / Methodology

Each candidate component ci has input/output hidden states. PGB-CT runs a mixture forward pass: the hidden state becomes mi·hi + (1−mi)·h'i, where mi∈[0,1] is the soft mask. The optimizer selects at most S components that maximize a target metric ℓ(D,m).

Step 1 · Relax
Binary mask → continuous m∈[0,1]N, clipped per step.
Step 2 · Reward
Inverse transform 1/(1+ℓ) keeps gradients stable across heterogeneous metrics.
Step 3 · Penalty
λ₁‖m‖₁ + λ₂ m⊤(1−m) drives mi → {0,1}.
Schedule
λ₁, λ₂ ramp up: explore broadly, then prune. Stop at τ=0.5 when |{mi>τ}| ≤ S.
∇reg(m) = λ₂( 𝟏 + (λ₁/λ₂)𝟏 − 2m )  ·  sign flips at mi = ½(1 + λ₁/λ₂)

02 / Results & highlights

Quality matches greedy, speed crushes it. On GPT2-medium / WinoGender at 30% sparsity, PGB-CT reaches ℓ = 0.227 vs. greedy's 0.232 and top-k's 0.203 — in 1.56 minutes.

03A / Strengths

  • Solves a real scalability bottleneck as N enters 10⁴–10⁵.
  • Honest limitations section; open code on GitHub.
  • Empirically falsifies linear additivity for two-head / two-MLP interventions.
  • Scales GPT2-small → GPT2-xl, LLaMA-7B/13B, Qwen3, Llama-3.2-1B.
  • Single A6000 GPU — reproducible on commodity hardware.

03B / Limitations

  • Single fixed target metric ℓ; no multi-objective trade-offs.
  • Hyperparameters (λ₁, λ₂, learning rate, τ) need hand-tuning.
  • Local optima; no global guarantees from gradient descent.
  • Tested on English + GPT-family / Llama / Qwen — 70B+ untested.
  • Incremental over DCM (Davies et al. 2023); not a clean theoretical leap.

04 / Questions for further exploration

  1. How much of the "nonlinear" interaction in Figure 1 is prompt-specific vs. model-intrinsic — i.e. a controlled ablation across counterfactual constructions?
  2. Does optimizing ℓ actually translate to better downstream edits (held-out StereoSet / BBQ bias drops while general capability is preserved)?
  3. Can the algorithm be extended to multi-objective settings (joint bias ↓ and perplexity ↑) without retraining?
  4. Llama-3.2-1B rows show anomalously high ℓ at higher sparsity — numerical artifact, different operating regime, or GQA/RoPE sensitivity?
  5. How does PGB-CT compare head-to-head with concrete / Gumbel-Softmax subset-selection on the same benchmarks?
  6. Does joint head + MLP-block selection on LLaMA-13B / VBD reproduce known binding circuits, or surface new ones?
