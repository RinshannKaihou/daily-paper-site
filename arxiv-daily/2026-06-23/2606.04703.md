> **一句话总结：** 本文系统诊断了"经验内化"（把上下文中的经验转为参数化能力）在多轮自演化中崩溃的原因，并提出三条组合设计原则——principle 级经验 + step-wise 注入 + off-policy 蒸馏——使 Qwen3-4B/8B web agent 在 WebWalkerQA、GAIA、BrowseComp-ZH 三轮自演化迭代中保持稳定提升。

> **TL;DR:** Existing experience-internalization methods (on-policy context-distillation on instance-level experience with global injection) collapse across self-evolution iterations on web-reasoning agents. This paper pinpoints the failure to three design choices and shows that (1) principle-level experience is more durable than instance-level, (2) step-wise (state-conditioned) experience injection beats global injection (especially avoiding 64% premature-answer failure), and (3) off-policy context-distillation on teacher trajectories is more stable than on-policy distillation on student trajectories. Combined, the three choices keep Qwen3-4B/8B agents improving across three iterations on WebWalkerQA / GAIA / BrowseComp-ZH. The contribution is a clean diagnostic + recipe, not a new method per se.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示自演化崩溃现象**：首次系统地报告"on-policy 上下文蒸馏 + 经验学习"在多轮自演化中性能单调下降的现象（Figure 1），将现有方法从单轮成功推广到可持续学习时遇到的结构性瓶颈公开化。
- **三维度诊断框架**：将经验内化过程解耦为 (1) Experience Granularity（instance vs principle）、(2) Experience Injection Pattern（global vs step-wise）、(3) Internalization Regime（on-policy vs off-policy），并对每一维度做受控消融。
- **经验粒度发现**：经验池中 instance-level 条目有 74.4% 包含具体 URL、93.9% 包含 query-specific 字符串，难以跨 query 迁移；principle-level 条目 84.0% 包含可复用策略，因而更耐久。
- **注入模式发现**：step-wise 注入通过 LLM-based selector Rφ 按当前交互状态选择经验，可避免 global 注入导致的"早答"失败（premature-answer rate 63.82% → 0%），并保留模型继续使用经验的能力。
- **内化范式发现**：off-policy 蒸馏使用教师生成的轨迹 + rejection sampling，可避免 on-policy 蒸馏因学生陷入错误状态而被迫做局部修正的局限；off-policy 的轨迹平均仅 2.5–4.5 步，而 on-policy 一次权重更新后学生平均 21.9 步，rollout 成本差异显著。
- **稳定配方**：principle + step-wise + off-policy 三者组合在三轮迭代中保持稳定增益（Figure 6），且经验池 in-context 使用与内化推理均持续提升。

## Key Contributions

- **Identifying the collapse problem**: A clean experimental demonstration that the dominant paradigm (on-policy context-distillation on experience pools) does not sustain improvement across self-evolution iterations on web-reasoning agents.
- **Three-axis diagnostic framework**: Decomposes experience internalization into Experience Granularity, Experience Injection Pattern, and Internalization Regime, with controlled ablations on each axis.
- **Granularity insight**: Empirically shows that principle-level experience (abstracted strategies) is far more durable than instance-level (trajectory-specific) experience across iterations; 84% of principle items contain reusable strategies vs 3.7% of instance items.
- **Injection pattern insight**: Step-wise injection (state-conditioned selection via a small LLM selector) is critical for long-horizon tool use; global injection causes 63.82% premature-answer failures and degrades experience-use ability.
- **Regime insight**: Off-policy context-distillation on teacher trajectories with rejection sampling is more stable than on-policy distillation on student trajectories, and is dramatically more rollout-efficient (2.5–4.5 vs 21.9 average assistant turns).
- **A combined recipe** (principle + step-wise + off-policy) that sustains performance across three self-evolution iterations on WebWalkerQA / GAIA-Text-103 / BrowseComp-ZH for both Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507 and Qwen3-8B.
- **Open release**: Code and data at https://github.com/RUCBM/ExpInternalization.

## Methodology

**Setting.** A ReAct-style web agent with five tools (Search, Visit, Python, Scholar, File Parser) interacts with the environment. Each trajectory H_T = {x, (τ_1, a_1, o_1), ..., (τ_T, a_T, o_T)} is summarized (via DeepSeek-V4 by default) into a natural-language experience pool E = {e_1, ..., e_N}. Experience internalization trains a student π_θ (experience-free at deployment) to match an experience-aware teacher π_T.

**The three design dimensions studied:**
1. **Experience Granularity** — Instance-level preserves trajectory-specific facts (URLs, numbers, entities); principle-level abstracts reusable strategies, search principles, and failure patterns.
2. **Experience Injection Pattern** — Global injection uses a fixed trajectory-level context c^glob = [x; E] for the whole trajectory, producing teacher distribution p^glob_t = π_T(· | h_{t-1}, c^glob). Step-wise injection uses a learned selector R_φ to pick Et^step = R_φ(h_{t-1}, E) at each state, producing p^step_t = π_T(· | h_{t-1}, E_t^step).
3. **Internalization Regime** — On-policy: trajectories from student π_θ, supervised with reverse KL on student-induced states. Off-policy: trajectories from experience-aware teacher with rejection sampling on success, supervised with forward KL.

**Training data.** 15K examples from five public web-reasoning datasets: WebWalkerQA-silver, DeepDive, WebShaper, WebDancer, SailorFog-QA.

**Models.** Qwen3-4B-Instruct-2507 and Qwen3-8B as students (Qwen3-8B with thinking mode disabled).

**Training.** Implemented with verl. Learning rate 1e-5, batch size 128, 5 epochs on 8× A800 GPUs.

**Inference.** Temperature 0.7, max T_max = 100 steps, 32,768-token context.

**Benchmarks.** WebWalkerQA (in-domain, since WebWalkerQA-silver is in training), GAIA-Text-103 (out-of-domain), BrowseComp-ZH (out-of-domain). Pass@1 reported.

## Results & Highlights

- **The collapse phenomenon (Figure 1):** Under iterative on-policy context-distillation, success rate declines monotonically across three iterations on all three benchmarks (e.g., WebWalkerQA drops from ~30% to ~10% by iteration 3).
- **Granularity ablation (Figure 2):** Instance-level experience improves iteration 1 but falls below the base model by iteration 3 on GAIA. Principle-level experience is the only configuration that holds up across iterations.
- **Injection pattern ablation (Figure 3 + Table 1):** Step-wise beats global on all three benchmarks in single iteration; on Qwen self-generated experience, step-wise reaches 31.2% on WebWalkerQA vs 23.2% for global (+8.0), 22.7% vs 16.8% on GAIA (+5.9), and 5.2% vs 4.5% on BrowseComp-ZH (+0.7).
- **Premature-answer failure (Table 2):** Global injection produces a terminal `<answer>` with no preceding `<tool_call>` in 63.82% of cases in iteration 3; step-wise injection: 0%. Case study (Figure 4) shows the global-injection model giving up before invoking search.
- **Trajectory inflation (Table 3):** After one on-policy weight update, the updated student averages 21.9 assistant turns per trajectory (vs 2.5 base, 4.5 teacher). Off-policy stays efficient by sampling concise teacher rollouts.
- **Final recipe (Figure 6):** Principle + step-wise + off-policy sustains internalization gains across three iterations on all three benchmarks; the updated model also retains in-context experience-use ability (red bars) across iterations, enabling it to serve as the next-round teacher.
- **Both 4B and 8B models** (Figure 5) show the same pattern: off-policy sustains, on-policy collapses.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** The methodological ingredients (off-policy vs on-policy distillation, principle vs instance memory, step-wise vs global retrieval) are individually well-known. The novelty is the *diagnosis* — showing that their combination under multi-iteration self-evolution collapses, and providing controlled evidence that each axis is independently causal. This is a worthwhile empirical contribution, though it is closer to a careful ablation study than a method paper.

**Rigor.** The ablations are clean: each axis is held constant while the other two are fixed, and the same student/teacher/infrastructure is used throughout. The premature-answer analysis (Table 2) and trajectory-length analysis (Table 3) are concrete diagnostics, not just aggregate metrics. The case study (Figure 4) is illustrative. The "experience-use ability" (gap between in-context and internalized performance) is a nice proxy for the next-round teacher quality.

**Reproducibility concerns.**
- The paper relies on DeepSeek-V4 for experience summarization and selection; that model is closed-source and the paper does not specify which version/API. This is a meaningful reproducibility bottleneck — anyone trying to reproduce will need to either match the summarization quality or use the released artifacts.
- The Qwen self-generated experience setting is more reproducible (no external teacher), but is also the *harder* setting the paper promotes as more realistic.
- 8× A800 training is a moderate budget but the published hyperparameters are minimal (lr, batch, epochs) — no seed variance reported.

**Significant weaknesses.**
- **In-domain contamination**: WebWalkerQA-silver is in the training corpus and WebWalkerQA is treated as the "in-domain" evaluation, so part of the apparent gain is just training-set fitting. The out-of-domain numbers (GAIA, BrowseComp-ZH) are modest in absolute terms (e.g., 22.7% on GAIA, 5.2% on BrowseComp-ZH).
- **No comparison to strong baselines**: The paper does not compare to memory/RAG-style approaches, other self-evolution methods (R-Zero, Absolute Zero, Agent0, WebDancer, A-mem), or simply keeping the experience pool in context without distillation. It's not clear the distilled student beats "just use the experience pool at inference time" in the in-context setting.
- **Cherry-picking of dimensions**: The three dimensions are chosen a priori, but other factors (experience pool size, selector quality, filtering criteria, mixing on/off-policy, mixing step/global, knowledge-distillation temperature, training schedule) are acknowledged as unexplored. The final recipe is therefore a point in a much larger design space, not a global optimum.
- **Limited scope**: Two model sizes (4B, 8B), one task domain (web QA), one language regime, three iterations. Generalization to other agent domains (code, embodied, multi-turn dialogue) is asserted in the limitations but not tested.
- **Sustainability claim is weak**: Three iterations is not "continual" in the strong sense. The paper shows no evidence the system would keep improving past iteration 3, nor that it would not eventually collapse.
- **The "premature answer" failure is not a new phenomenon** in agent training; it is a known symptom of weak tool-use supervision. The paper's contribution is connecting it to global injection specifically, which is a clean observation but not a deep insight.
- **No ablations on teacher quality or student initialization**; the recipe may be tightly coupled to DeepSeek-V4-quality teachers and Qwen3-4B/8B students.

**Red flags.** None major; the experiments seem honestly run and the figures match the claims. The "Rethinking" framing is slightly overclaimed for what is essentially an ablation study.

**Significance.** The paper is most useful as a warning to the self-evolving-agents community: on-policy context-distillation as currently practiced does not sustain multi-iteration self-evolution. The three-axis recipe is a reasonable starting point but should not be over-interpreted as a complete solution.

## Strengths

- Clean problem identification: the Figure 1 collapse is a sharp, memorable result.
- Methodologically disciplined: each axis is isolated, with both quantitative and qualitative (case study) evidence.
- Practical and immediately actionable: the three recommendations are easy to implement and orthogonal to the model architecture.
- Honest about limitations (web-only, three iterations, deepseek dependency).
- Open-source release of code and data.
- Both 4B and 8B results, plus a Qwen self-generated setting that removes the strong-teacher confound.

## Limitations

- Only two model sizes, one task domain, three iterations; "continual" is therefore a stretch.
- WebWalkerQA is in-domain (training data includes WebWalkerQA-silver), inflating apparent gains.
- Heavy reliance on DeepSeek-V4 for experience generation and selection (closed-source, version unspecified) — a meaningful reproducibility bottleneck.
- No comparison to memory/RAG baselines, no comparison to other self-evolution methods, no comparison to "just use experience in context without distillation."
- The three-axis grid is a slice of a much larger design space; pool size, selector quality, mixing strategies, training schedules are all unexamined.
- Absolute performance on out-of-domain benchmarks remains modest (22.7% GAIA, 5.2% BrowseComp-ZH).
- No analysis of compute scaling, environmental cost, or wall-clock per iteration.
- The safety/bias dimension of self-evolution (model reinforcing its own mistakes over iterations) is mentioned in the broader-impact section but not studied empirically.

## Relevance

本文直接命中"自演化 LLM Agent"与"LLM 训练稳定性"两个核心方向。Figure 1 的多轮崩溃是训练不稳定性的具体表现，三维度诊断框架对设计自演化 loop 极具参考价值；但应注意 in-domain 评测的污染问题与对闭源教师模型的依赖。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the principle + step-wise + off-policy recipe still hold when the experience pool is generated and selected by a *weaker* model than the student (e.g., a 1B selector)? The current paper uses the student itself for selection in the "Qwen self-generated" setting, but the selector quality axis is not independently varied.
- How does the recipe interact with experience-pool size and refresh rate? At what pool size does principle-level experience itself start to drift or fragment?
- Can on-policy and off-policy distillation be combined — e.g., warm-start with off-policy teacher trajectories, then on-policy refinement — to get the best of both?
- Does the collapse generalize to non-web domains (code generation, tool use in software engineering, multi-turn dialogue, embodied agents)? The paper's recipe is a "web-reasoning recipe" until shown otherwise.
- How does the in-context vs internalized gap (the "experience-use ability") evolve past three iterations? Is the gap eventually eroded in either direction?
- What happens when the experience pool is adversarially corrupted, or when the model is asked to internalize experience that contradicts its parametric prior? The paper flags this risk but does not test it.
- Can the three-axis framework be reformulated as a single training-time trade-off (e.g., a curriculum over granularity, injection, and regime) instead of a static point?
- How does the recipe compare to simply maintaining the experience pool in context at inference time (no distillation at all), especially given that the paper shows in-context performance often matches or exceeds internalized performance on later iterations?
