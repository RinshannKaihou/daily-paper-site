> **一句话总结：** 提出 INFUSER 自演化框架——把"生成器-求解器"协同训练建模成双层博弈，生成器用"优化器感知影响力分数"（候选问题诱发的求解器更新方向与 dev 集目标方向的余弦相似度）作为奖励、配合双归一化 DuGRPO，把无结构教科书文档池转化为对当前求解器有用的自适应课程；Qwen3-8B-Base 在数学&物理/通用推理类目上相对基座提升约 20%，8B 共演化生成器在数学与代码上击败冻结 32B 思维生成器。

> **TL;DR:** INFUSER casts self-evolution as a cooperative bilevel game between a Generator and a Solver (both initialized from Qwen3-4B/8B-Base). The Solver is trained with standard binary correctness RLVR (Dr.GRPO) on generator-produced QA pairs from a 12,260-chunk textbook pool; the Generator is rewarded by an *optimizer-aware influence score* — the cosine similarity between the dev-set solver gradient and each candidate question's AdamW-preconditioned solver-update direction. To handle this continuous, noisy reward, the authors propose **DuGRPO** (dual-normalized GRPO, normalizing by within-group σd plus batch-level σB). On Qwen3-8B-Base, INFUSER beats R-Zero, AZR, R-Few, and SPICE, achieving the top category average on math & physics (+20.74%), general reasoning (+17.98%), medical (+3.00%), and coding (+5.34%); an 8B co-evolving generator beats a frozen 32B thinking generator on math and coding (Finding 3.1).

## 核心贡献

- **双层博弈形式化**：把生成器-求解器协同自演化建模为双层博弈（生成器为 leader，求解器为 follower），生成器的目标不是出"难"题，而是出"对当前求解器在目标分布上有改进价值"的题。
- **优化器感知影响力分数**：将候选问题的价值定义为 `cossim(ĝ_dev, Γ̂(q,a_φ))`——dev 集参考梯度与该问题诱发的 AdamW 预条件化求解器更新方向的余弦相似度；这是一阶（first-order）影响力函数近似，避免了昂贵的元梯度/黑盒外层搜索。
- **DuGRPO 双归一化**：标准 GRPO 会对低方差组放大噪声、Dr.GRPO 又让高方差组主导梯度；DuGRPO 在组内 σd 基础上再加批次级 σB，既能压制低方差组的噪声又能保持单位尺度收益。
- **文档池→自适应课程**：把 12,260 个无结构教科书 chunk（天文/生化/地理/物理）+ 800 题 SuperGPQA Science dev 集转化为随求解器能力演进的动态课程，求解器从不直接在 dev 集上训练。
- **8B 共演化生成器 > 冻结 32B 思维生成器（数学与代码）**：Finding 3.1 表明在数学/代码上"适配胜过规模"，仅在知识密集的通用推理/医学上 32B 略胜。
- **可扩展性**：可应用于已指令微调锚点（OLMo-3-7B-Instruct-SFT，13 项基准中 10 项领先）并与可验证 RLVR 数据结合以稳定数学推理深度。

## Key Contributions

- **Bilevel game formulation of self-evolution**: casts generator–solver co-training as a cooperative bilevel game (generator = leader, solver = follower) where the generator is rewarded for inducing solver updates that improve *target-distribution* performance, not for producing hard questions.
- **Optimizer-aware influence score (Definition 2.1)**: a per-question reward `s(q,a_φ) = cossim(∇θJ(θ), Γ(q,a_φ))`, where Γ is the AdamW-preconditioned single-question solver-update direction; this is a first-order influence-function approximation that sidesteps meta-gradient/backprop-through-update.
- **DuGRPO (dual-normalized GRPO)**: a generator advantage `Ã_gen = (s − μd)/(σd + σB + ε)` that combines within-group and batch-level normalization to handle the continuous, noisy influence reward where vanilla GRPO amplifies noise and Dr.GRPO lets high-variance groups dominate.
- **Document-pool → adaptive curriculum**: turns a 12,260-chunk unstructured textbook pool + an 800-question SuperGPQA Science dev set into a curriculum that tracks the solver's learning frontier; the solver never trains directly on the dev set (which only defines the target direction).
- **Strong empirical scaling**: on Qwen3-8B-Base, INFUSER's gains decay minimally from 4B→8B (math +20.57%→+20.74%; general reasoning ~−2.3 points) while baselines decay sharply (R-Zero's math gain nearly halves: +17.71%→+9.13%).
- **Generality via two extensions**: (i) instruction-finetuned anchor OLMo-3-7B-Instruct-SFT (INFUSER leads on 10/13 benchmarks, e.g. SuperGPQA Δ=+5.6, MMLU-Pro Δ=+5.1); (ii) hybrid science + rule-verifiable math RLVR that stabilizes seed-dependent math collapse.

## Methodology

- **Anchor models**: Qwen3-4B-Base and Qwen3-8B-Base (and, in the extension, OLMo-3-7B-Instruct-SFT). Generator π_φ and solver π_θ are both initialized from the same pretrained checkpoint but maintain separate parameters/optimizer states.
- **Document pool** D_doc: 12,260 chunks collected from open-access textbooks in Astronomy, Biochemistry, Geography, and Physics (an external browsing assistant is used only to locate sources, no closed teacher/verifier/judge in the training loop).
- **Dev set** D_dev: 800 questions randomly sampled from SuperGPQA Science (~3% of the full set); used ONLY to define the solver-side target gradient ĝ_dev once per iteration — the solver never trains on it and the generator is not prompted with it.
- **Per-iteration 6-phase loop (Algorithm 1)**:
  1. *Dev direction*: roll out solver on D_dev (n=8 rollouts), compute reference gradient ĝ_dev (2.11).
  2. *Candidate generation*: sample B=128 documents, generator writes n=8 QA pairs each; filter parseable-format failures (penalty ρ_inv = 0).
  3. *Solver testing*: for each (q,a_φ), run solver n=8 times, score against a_φ, compute per-question gradient ĝ(q,a_φ) (2.12).
  4. *Influence reward*: ŝ(q,a_φ) = cossim(ĝ_dev, Γ̂(q,a_φ)), with Γ̂ the AdamW-preconditioned version of ĝ.
  5. *Generator update*: DuGRPO with advantage (2.14); cosine similarity chosen (per LESS, Xia et al.) to avoid spurious length↔gradient-norm correlation.
  6. *Solver update*: Dr.GRPO on retained curriculum with binary correctness rewards.
- **DuGRPO advantage**: for document d with influence-reward mean μd/std σd and batch-level σB := mean{σd′: d′∈B}, `Ã_gen = (s − μd)/(σd + σB + ε)`. The two ablation alternatives are GRPO-style `AGRPO = (s−μd)/(σd+ε)` and batch-normalized `ABN = (s−μd)/(σB+ε)`.
- **Training config**: 100 iterations on 8× H100 GPUs; AdamW weight decay 0.01, minibatch M=32; solver LR 2×10⁻⁶; generator LR 6×10⁻⁶ (4B) / 4×10⁻⁶ (8B); group size n=8; token-level Truncated Importance Sampling with ρ_max=2.0; INFUSER scores averaged over 3 seeds (ablations single seed).
- **Evaluation**: 14 benchmarks across general reasoning (MMLU-Pro, GPQA-Diamond, SuperGPQA, BBEH), math & physics (MATH500, AIME2024/2025, HMMT, OlympiadBench Math/Phys), medical (MedQA, MedXpertQA), coding (HumanEval+, LiveCodeBench v1-5). Δ denotes gap to base model.

## Results & Highlights

- **Category-average gains on Qwen3-8B-Base (Table 2)**: math & physics +20.74%, general reasoning +17.98%, medical +3.00%, coding +5.34% over the base model — the best of all five training methods (R-Zero, AZR, R-Few†, SPICE†, INFUSER) on all four category averages.
- **Math & physics on Qwen3-8B-Base**: INFUSER reaches MATH500 82.77% (vs base 61.20%), AIME2024 18.58% (vs 10.42%), AIME2025 19.48% (vs 8.44%), OlympiadBench(Math) 50.24% (vs 35.31%), OlympiadBench(Phys) 14.41% (vs 10.17%); category avg 31.49% vs base 21.34%.
- **General reasoning on Qwen3-8B-Base**: MMLU-Pro 66.20% (vs 52.98%), GPQA-Diamond 39.40% (vs 31.41%), SuperGPQA 35.70% (vs 25.88%), BBEH 14.90% (vs 11.22%).
- **8B co-evolving generator vs frozen 32B thinking generator (Finding 3.1, §3.3.1)**: the 8B INFUSER generator beats a frozen Qwen3-32B thinking generator (Strong-gen) on math (Δ+7.23 vs +3.28) and coding, and trails by only 0.3 points on general reasoning; the 32B wins mainly on knowledge-heavy general-reasoning (+7.52) and medical domains.
- **DuGRPO + optimizer-aware score are necessary (§3.3.2)**: replacing DuGRPO with group_std or batch_std, or swapping the optimizer-aware score for plain sgd_cosine, regresses performance back to the Fix-gen (frozen-generator) reference on math and general reasoning.
- **Dev-set generalization (§3.3.1)**: Dev-only (training solver directly on the 800 D_dev questions) memorizes — scores 86.5% on D_dev but collapses to 38.4% on the held-out 9,038-question complement D_dev^c, while INFUSER holds at 39.2% on both splits, showing the dev signal is turned into a *generalizing* curriculum.
- **4B→8B gain decay (§3.1)**: math holds (+20.57%→+20.74%), general reasoning drops only ~2.3 points (+20.26%→+17.98%); in contrast R-Zero's math gain nearly halves (+17.71%→+9.13%) and SPICE's general-reasoning gain drops ~6.3 points.
- **Rising curriculum (§3.2)**: the strong-against-weak gap (SWG, GPT-5.4 minus Qwen3-8B-Base accuracy on generator questions) rises from +5.0 (iter 0) to +11.9 (iter 30) to +13.6 (iter 90), showing questions get harder for the base model while remaining well-posed for a strong solver.
- **Hybrid science+RLVR stabilizes math (§5)**: science-only INFUSER is seed-unstable on math (cross-seed std 2.80 pp; r=0.997 between response length and math accuracy); adding rule-verifiable math RLVR (split D_dev evenly between SuperGPQA and pre-2024 AIME; add 10,000 Putnam/AIME-history rows) cuts math variance to ±0.48 pp (32.52%) while keeping other domains stable.

## Critical Assessment

- **R-Few and SPICE are self-reported, not re-run (Table 2)**: only Base/INFUSER/R-Zero/AZR are trained and evaluated under the unified harness; SPICE† and R-Few† numbers are copied from the original papers, so the headline "INFUSER beats all four baselines" is partially a cross-paper comparison rather than a fully controlled one.
- **The 8B-beats-32B claim (Finding 3.1) is domain-specific, not "8B > 32B".** The frozen 32B (Strong-gen) actually wins on general reasoning (+7.52 vs INFUSER +7.23) and medical; the 8B co-evolving generator wins only on math and coding, and trails by 0.3 points on general reasoning. The paper states this honestly but the framing matters.
- **Math seed instability and response-length collapse (§5)**: science-only INFUSER has cross-seed std 2.80 pp on math, with r=0.997 between test-time response length and math accuracy — meaning math gains are largely governed by how long the solver reasons, which the science-only setting fails to anchor. This is a real fragility that the authors fix only by *adding* external RLVR data, not by the core method alone.
- **Generator LR requires per-anchor, non-monotone tuning (§3.3.3)**: the sweep peaks at 6×10⁻⁶ for 4B and 4×10⁻⁶ for 8B with a non-monotone shape, so the two reported generator LRs are hand-picked optima — reproducibility across new anchors requires re-running this sweep.
- **Strong generalization claims rest on a single model family (Qwen3) plus one IF anchor (OLMo-3-7B).** No LLaMA/Mistral/base-model-size sweep beyond 4B/8B; the authors note (§6) the dev-set alignment is the binding constraint on out-of-domain gains.
- **The dev set D_dev is sampled from SuperGPQA Science, which is also an evaluation benchmark** — the authors address leakage by showing INFUSER scores nearly identically on D_dev and its 9,038-question held-out complement (39.2% vs 38.4%), but the target direction is still shaped by the same distribution the solver is later scored on.

## Strengths

- Clean conceptual reframing: reward the generator by *utility to the current solver* (influence), not by *difficulty* — a principled answer to "hard ≠ useful."
- The first-order influence approximation avoids meta-gradient/backprop-through-update, making the bilevel objective tractable at LLM scale (8× H100, 100 iterations).
- DuGRPO is a justified algorithmic contribution: ablations (§3.3.2) show both single-normalization variants and the SGD-cosine score regress to the frozen-generator baseline.
- Honest, detailed diagnostics: dev-set leakage split test, generator-quality SWG curve, pass@k study, and a transparent report of the math seed-instability failure mode.
- Demonstrates the recipe *composes* — it works on an instruction-finetuned anchor (OLMo-3-7B-Instruct-SFT, 10/13 benchmarks) and with rule-verifiable RLVR.
- Code released, enabling replication.

## Limitations

- **Cross-paper baseline comparison**: SPICE and R-Few are self-reported, not re-run under the unified harness.
- **Math fragility**: science-only INFUSER is seed-unstable on math (std 2.80 pp) and the gains correlate almost perfectly (r=0.997) with response length; stability requires injecting external RLVR data.
- **Per-anchor generator-LR tuning** is non-monotone and must be re-swept for each new anchor.
- **Single base family (Qwen3) + one IF anchor (OLMo-3-7B)**; no LLaMA/Mistral and no >8B base model.
- **Dev-set alignment bounds gains**: authors acknowledge (§6) INFUSER's gains are strongest where the 800-question D_dev and the textbook pool align, and extending to out-of-domain targets is open.
- **Generator question quality is imperfect**: §3.2 and the qualitative example (Figure 7) show generated questions improve but are "still not close to perfect" in correctness/quality.
- **Hybrid training exposes a fixed-budget trade-off** (§6): strengthening one domain (math via RLVR) slightly weakens others, motivating adaptive signal-source allocation as future work.

## Relevance

该论文直接映射到 **「Self-Evolving Agents & Automated Research」（自演化智能体与自动化研究）** 这一方向——它构建了一个生成器-求解器协同自演化框架，把无结构文档自动转化为训练课程，无需人工标注或教师模型；同时也与 **「LLM Training Stability & Observability」（训练稳定性与可观测性）** 相关，因为 DuGRPO 的设计、math 上的 seed 不稳定性分析（响应长度塌缩、r=0.997）正是关于 RL 训练稳定性与诊断的实证研究。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the optimizer-aware influence score remain well-calibrated across many more iterations (e.g., 500+ rounds), or does it collapse toward a difficulty proxy as the solver saturates the dev-aligned curriculum? (The paper runs 100 iterations and notes generator quality is "still not close to perfect.")
- How sensitive is INFUSER to the size/domain/quality of the textbook pool D_doc (here 12,260 science chunks) — would a non-science pool transfer to the same dev direction, or is the alignment the binding constraint as the authors suggest (§6)?
- Is DuGRPO's advantage normalization (σd + σB) the unique fix, or would other continuous-reward normalizers (e.g., reward whitening, value-baseline subtraction from GRPO-style value functions cited via DeepSeekMath/Prime) recover similar gains? The paper only ablates group_std, batch_std, and sgd_cosine.
- Can the influence-guided generator be combined with process-reward models or step-level verifiers, which the paper does not consider?
- Does the 8B-beats-32B-generator result (Finding 3.1) hold when the 32B generator is *also* co-evolved rather than frozen, or is co-evolution the deciding factor rather than scale?
- The paper shows math gains correlate with response length (r=0.997); does INFUSER's math improvement survive if test-time compute is equalized across seeds/methods?
- How does INFUSER behave on non-Qwen base models (LLaMA, Mistral) and at larger scale (>8B), which the authors leave open (§6)?
