BitsMoE: Efficient Spectral Energy-Guided Bit Allocation for MoE LLM Quantization

arXiv:2606.00079 Jiayu Zhao, Zihan Teng, Minhao Fan, Tianrui Ma, Wentao Ren, Song Chen, Weichen Liu 2026-06-23 MoE quantization mixed-precision PTQ SVD / spectral ILP bit allocation ultra-low-bit LLM
BitsMoE decomposes each MoE layer into a shared FP16 spectral basis plus expert-specific spectrum vectors, then uses an activation-aware ILP to assign different bit-widths per spectral component — pushing 2-bit MoE accuracy from 34.08% to 61.91%.

Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base: BitsMoE 61.91% vs GPTQ 34.08%. GSM8K 75.51% vs 4.32%; HumanEval 43.90% vs 0.00%.
Why this matters
Ultra-low-bit MoE quantization is now deployment-grade: 12.3× faster offline, 1.76–3.04× faster decoding, 5.4–6.6× MoE memory savings — making 2-bit MoE LLMs practical, not academic.
Critical edge
The diagonal-error approximation discards cross-component interaction in the reconstruction loss, and the κ_b coefficients for {2,3,4}-bit distortion are estimated offline without a documented procedure — neither is empirically validated.

01. Methodology

  • Shared spectral basis. SVD each MoE projection into Φ (FP16, kept unquantized) + expert-specific normalized spectral vectors P̃ₑ as fine-grained quantization units.
  • Activation-aware reconstruction surrogate. Routing-weighted loss: L ≈ Σ α²·β·γ·E‖ε‖², with α = spectral energy, β = activation importance.
  • Piecewise distortion model covering {16,8,6,4,3,2,1,0} bits — exponential high-bit, power-law mid-bit, sin²θ at 1-bit, unit at 0-bit.
  • Component-wise ILP (Gurobi) per projection type: minimize weighted distortion under a fixed bit budget; basis computed once, reused across routed experts.

02. Results

  • DeepSeek-V2-Lite @ 2-bit: BitsMoE 41.04% vs MoEQuant 40.40%, GPTQ 30.43%.
  • Qwen3-30B-A3B @ 2-bit: 61.91% vs MoEQuant 52.21%, GPTQ 34.08% (+27.83 pp).
  • Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Instruct @ 2-bit: 72.14% avg (MMLU 81.47%, GSM8K 71.49%) — beats GPTQ by >32 pp.
  • Sub-2-bit still works: 1.4-bit Qwen3-30B retains 47.46% avg, 52.62% GSM8K — beats MoEQuant at 2-bit on GSM8K.
  • Ablation (2-bit avg): FS/UniBit → 30.56 / 43.92 / 67.69 vs FS/AdaBit → 41.04 / 61.91 / 72.14. ILP adds 10–20 pp.

03. Strengths

  • New SOTA on five MoE backbones in the ultra-low-bit regime.
  • Conceptually clean decomposition matching MoE's own structure.
  • Finer grain than any prior mixed-precision MoE quantizer.
  • Practical deployment wins: 12.3× offline, 1.76–3.04× decoding.
  • Public code & model release.

04. Limitations

  • Diagonal-error approximation discards cross-component interaction.
  • Uniform bit-budget across layers ignores layer sensitivity.
  • MoE-only — attention, activations, KV cache remain FP16.
  • FP16 shared-basis overhead not reported separately in deployment.
  • κ_b estimation procedure undocumented; no calibration-size sweep.

05. Questions for Further Exploration

  • How much of the remaining reconstruction-error gap is attributable to the diagonal approximation — could a low-rank off-diagonal correction be added without re-quadratizing the ILP?
  • Can layer- and projection-aware bit budgets (replacing the uniform split) close the remaining 1–2 pp gap to FP16, especially at 3-bit?
  • How does BitsMoE compose with KV-cache quantization, activation quantization, and attention-layer PTQ for a full-memory-budget breakdown?
  • Can the shared basis itself be compressed (4–6 bits) with a small additional surrogate to recover further memory?
  • Is there a closed-form or learned alternative to Gurobi to remove the proprietary-solver dependency in open-source deployments?
