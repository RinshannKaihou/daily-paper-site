> **一句话总结：** SkillDAG 把技能库建模为一张五类边（depends_on / specializes / composes_with / similar_to / conflicts_with）的有向类型图，并以 **agent 可调用的结构化检索接口**（`search`/`show`/`propose-edge`/`edit-edge`）暴露给 LLM，让代理在执行中推理结构证据、在线提交证据支撑的边编辑；在 ALFWorld 与 SkillsBench 上以 MiniMax-M2.7 取得 +12.8 / +8.6 的绝对提升。

> **TL;DR:** SkillDAG reframes LLM skill selection as a *structural* problem rather than a similarity-matching one. It models inter-skill dependencies, specializations, compositions, redundancies, and conflicts as a **typed directed graph** and exposes that graph to the agent at inference time through a four-call interface (`search`, `show`, `propose-edge`, `edit-edge`) instead of feeding the agent an opaque top-K bundle. A two-view cold-start constructor recovers cross-functional prerequisite pairs that single-view embeddings miss, and an online propose-then-commit protocol lets the agent grow the graph with execution-backed edges under three structural invariants (acyclicity, non-contradiction, append-only reversibility). On ALFWorld and SkillsBench with MiniMax-M2.7, SkillDAG achieves **67.1% / 27.3% reward** vs. the strongest GoS baseline at 54.3 / 18.7 (+12.8 / +8.6), and intrinsic Ret@K on SkillsBench climbs from 65.5 to 78.2 while staying flat across a 10× pool expansion.

## 核心贡献

- **结构性失败模式的识别**：在大规模技能库下，平铺检索（top-K 嵌入相似度 / BM25）会**静默遗漏**结构上必需的先决技能（如 parser、converter、setup utility），并把功能重复或互相干扰的技能一起塞进上下文——而相似度检索对此完全无感。
- **Agent 可调用的结构化检索接口**：SkillDAG 用 `search(q, K, D)` 一次性返回三路独立证据——`matches`（语义相似 top-K）、`neighbors`（沿四个可遍历边类型的 BFS 邻居）、`conflicts`（一跳 conflicts_with 排除信号）；`show` 按需加载技能正文；代理可自主决定读取哪一路、是否追问。
- **可在线演化的类型图**：通过 `propose-edge`（dry-run 预览）和 `edit-edge`（执行提交）让代理在 episode 级别**用执行证据提交新边**；提交受三条结构性不变量保护——`depends_on`/`specializes` 骨干的**无环性**、正负边**非矛盾**、**append-only 可回滚**。
- **两视角冷启动构造器**：每个技能同时编码 `e_self`（"做什么"）和 `e_needs`（HyDE 式"需要什么"），再用 LLM 对分类器标注 `similar_to / specializes / depends_on / composes_with`；`conflicts_with` 刻意**只在在线阶段产生**——因为冲突本质上是共用的执行观察。
- **可规模化的检索质量**：在 SkillsBench 200/500/1000/2000 的池子里，SkillDAG 的 matches 字段在所有规模和指标上都优于 Graph-of-Skills 的 HNSW 种子 + Personalized PageRank 扩散；10× 池扩张下 Ret@5 仅掉 3.5 分（78.2 → 74.7）。

## Key Contributions

- **Identify a structural blind spot in flat retrieval.** Concatenating top-K cosine or BM25 neighbors over a large skill library silently omits structurally necessary prerequisites, duplicates interchangeable skills, and bundles interfering pairs—failures invisible to similarity matching alone.
- **SkillDAG interface.** Replace the fixed graph-ranking policy (e.g., GoS's reverse-aware PPR) with a **typed-graph interface** the LLM queries and revises at inference time. A single `search(q, K, D)` call returns three separate evidence channels—semantic matches, typed-edge neighbors, and conflicts—so the agent can reason over each independently, drop fields under tight context budgets, or schedule re-queries mid-episode.
- **Online propose-then-commit protocol.** Edits are gated by the agent's reasoning rather than a statistical threshold: `propose-edge` does a dry-run preview (surfaces pair history), `edit-edge` commits with a natural-language reason and task_id. Three structural invariants—acyclicity on the `depends_on`/`specializes` backbone, non-contradiction between `conflicts_with` and positive edges, append-only reversibility—bound the blast radius of bad edits.
- **Two-view cold-start constructor.** `e_self(v)` embeds what a skill does; `e_needs(v)` embeds a HyDE-style summary of what it requires. The union of candidate pairs is bucketed and classified by an LLM (gpt-5-nano) into one of four static types. `conflicts_with` is reserved for the online phase because co-use harm is by definition an execution observation.
- **Robustness at scale.** SkillDAG stays flat across a 10× pool expansion; matches-field cosine beats GoS's seed+PPR at every pool scale; ALFWorld train/test split shows edits transfer to held-out episodes (test-initial 65.0% vs. train 67.1% under MiniMax-M2.7).

## Methodology

**Graph model.** Let the skill library be `C = {s_1, …, s_n}`. The graph `G = (V, E, τ)` has `V = C` and a typing function `τ` mapping each edge to one of five operationally specified types:

| Type | Operational meaning |
|------|---------------------|
| `depends_on(A, B)` | A requires prerequisite B; A's success drops if B is removed. Transitive. |
| `specializes(D, A)` | D is the narrower variant of A; prefer D when applicable. Transitive within hierarchies. |
| `composes_with(A, B)` | Joint success(A, B) > max(success(A), success(B)). Symmetric, not transitive. |
| `similar_to(A, B)` | A and B are functionally interchangeable; co-selecting wastes context. |
| `conflicts_with(A, B)` | Co-selecting causes measurable success drop. Symmetric, not transitive. |

Two operational constraints are enforced by design: (1) `conflicts_with` is **excluded from traversal** (walking a conflict would actively surface skills the agent should avoid); (2) the `depends_on` / `specializes` backbone is enforced **acyclic**—this gives the framework its name.

**Three-field retrieval interface.** A single `search(q, K, D)` returns:
- **matches**: top-K skills by query–node cosine (text-embedding-3-large).
- **neighbors**: skills reached by bounded BFS up to depth D over the four walkable types (traversed bidirectionally so the agent sees both ends).
- **conflicts**: one-hop `conflicts_with` edges incident to any match (no transitive expansion).

`show(skill_id)` lazily loads the full SKILL.md body. The agent decides what to load.

**Cold-start construction (Algorithm 1).** For each skill `v`, embed `e_self(v)` from identifier + description + body preview, and `e_needs(v)` from a HyDE-style summary of imagined invoking tasks. Candidate pairs are those whose cosine on either view exceeds `max(0.35, min(0.75, μ+σ))` (adaptive threshold). Pairs are bucketed by anchor and classified by gpt-5-nano into one of `similar_to / specializes / depends_on / composes_with` or `none`. `conflicts_with` is intentionally skipped here.

**Online edit protocol (Algorithm 2).** Per episode, after each `search`/`act` cycle, if execution evidence supports an edit Δ:
1. `propose-edge(G, Δ)` → returns a preview (what would change) and the pair's existing edges / history.
2. `edit-edge(G, Δ)` commits if and only if three invariants pass:
   - **Acyclicity**: the edit does not close a cycle on the `depends_on` / `specializes` backbone.
   - **Non-contradiction**: the pair does not simultaneously carry `conflicts_with` and any positive edge.
   - **Reversibility**: an append-only history log enables rollback by recency or task_id.

The commit is gated by the agent's reasoning (not a downstream vote), so a single episode suffices and the graph evolves at agent speed. Edges carry a natural-language reason and the originating task_id.

**Why this design.** The paper's central inversion: a fixed graph-ranking policy (e.g., GoS's reverse-aware PPR) **collapses typed structure back into one ranked bundle** and hands the agent opaque context. SkillDAG keeps the three evidence channels separate so the LLM, not the retriever, decides how structural evidence should affect execution; the graph in turn accumulates evidence-backed edges so future calls inherit accumulated structure.

## Results & Highlights

**Table 1 — Main results** (R = reward %, ALFWorld R = success rate; SkillsBench R at 1000-skill scale):

| Backbone | Method | SkillsBench R↑ | ALFWorld R↑ | SkillsBench Ret@K↑ | SkillsBench Ret@1↑ | SkillsBench MRR↑ |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| MiniMax-M2.7 | Vanilla Skills | 17.2 | 47.1 | – | – | – |
| MiniMax-M2.7 | Vector Skills | 10.4 | 50.7 | 10.8 | 3.6 | 5.8 |
| MiniMax-M2.7 | GoS | 18.7 | 54.3 | 65.5 | 50.6 | 57.3 |
| **MiniMax-M2.7** | **SkillDAG** | **27.3** | **67.1** | **78.2** | **66.7** | **71.3** |
| gpt-5.2-codex | Vanilla Skills | 27.4 | 89.3 | – | – | – |
| gpt-5.2-codex | Vector Skills | 21.5 | 92.9 | 10.8 | 3.6 | 5.8 |
| gpt-5.2-codex | GoS | 34.4 | 93.6 | 65.5 | 50.6 | 57.3 |
| **gpt-5.2-codex** | **SkillDAG** | **36.8** | **93.6** | **75.9** | **70.1** | **73.0** |

The headline gains appear under MiniMax-M2.7: ALFWorld **+12.8** (54.3 → 67.1) and SkillsBench **+8.6** (18.7 → 27.3). Under gpt-5.2-codex the gap narrows (ALFWorld ties at 93.6; SkillsBench +2.4)—interpreted as the routing-aid signature being large where the backbone cannot reason past a poor bundle and vanishing once it can.

**Intrinsic retrieval (same 87 queries, identical embeddings):** SkillDAG's matches-field cosine outranks GoS's HNSW seed + PPR diffusion on every metric at every pool scale (200/500/1000/2000); across the 10× expansion SkillDAG loses only 3.5 Ret@5 points (78.2 → 74.7), while GoS degrades further. The narrowest gap is at scale 500 where GoS's PPR pull happens to align with the gold target.

**Edit transfer (Table 2, SkillsBench scale-1000, 316 search queries from 65 tasks):**

| Graph | Edges | Mean GT / query |
|---|---|---|
| Cold-start | 2,359 | 1.915 |
| Edited (+27 online) | 2,386 | 1.984 |

Mean ground-truth size is 2.52 skills/task. Zero queries lose any ground-truth retrieval: an accepted edge can only **enlarge** the retrieved set (matches ∪ BFS neighbors, untruncated), never evict a prior hit. This is a recall property, not a context-budget claim.

**ALFWorld train / test (Figure 4a).** With a 420-episode in-domain train run accumulating edits and 140 held-out test episodes: MiniMax-M2.7 — Train 67.1 / Test-initial 65.0 / Test-edited 67.1; gpt-5.2-codex — Train 92.3 / Test-initial 90.7 / Test-edited 93.6. Edits transfer to held-out episodes within ≤2.1 points, and re-running with the train-produced graph recovers the train number, so committed edges persist as durable structure rather than training-time stochastics.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** The inversion—graph as agent-callable interface rather than as opaque retriever substrate—is genuinely new and well motivated. The closest baseline (GoS, Liu et al. 2026) is explicitly critiqued for "handing the agent opaque context"; SkillDAG's three-field decomposition plus online edits are the architectural alternative. The **set-monotone online-edit property** (an accepted edge can only enlarge, never shrink, the retrieved set) is a clean, falsifiable recall guarantee that the paper proves by construction.

**Rigor.**
- The methodology is precisely specified: graph definition (§3.2), interface (§3.3), cold-start (Algorithm 1), online loop (Algorithm 2), commit invariants, edge-type operational definitions with counterfactual tests (Appendix C). The two-view embedding intuition (cooling vs. pickup skills) is concrete and the HyDE step is a defensible choice.
- Failure analysis (Appendix B) is unusually honest: 11/30 pilot failures split into tight loops (8), meta-mode confusion (1, MiniMax `</think>` tag leakage — a model artifact, not a skill-selection failure), and step-budget exhaustion (2). This is a strong signal of internal review discipline.
- The cross-backbone pattern (large MiniMax gains, near-tied gpt-5.2-codex) is reported without hand-waving; the authors correctly read it as a **routing-aid signature** that scales inversely with backbone reasoning strength.

**Reproducibility concerns.**
- Vanilla / Vector / GoS reward numbers are **quoted from Liu et al. 2026 Table 1, not rerun**; the SkillDAG rows are the authors' own runs on the "same benchmark, backbone, task data, and skill pools" but are **not seed-matched reruns**. This makes the +12.8 / +8.6 headline deltas slightly weaker than they would be under paired reruns, though the intrinsic retrieval columns (same 87 queries, identical embeddings) are directly comparable.
- The "MiniMax-M2.7" / "gpt-5.2-codex" backbone names and "MiniMax Anthropic-compatible endpoint" suggest **placeholder or anonymized model identifiers**; without knowing the exact base model, the cross-backbone claim is hard to generalize.
- Cold-start cost at scale 1000 is "~200 gpt-5-nano calls in batched bucket mode plus 2× embedding per skill"—reasonable but undocumented per-call variance.

**Significance.** This is a credible systems paper that materially advances the "structural retrieval for agent skills" line of work (Voyager → JARVIS-1 → ToolLLM → GoS → SkillDAG). The contribution that travels beyond skills is the **general principle that a graph should be an interface for routing decisions, not a substrate for an opaque policy** — applicable to tool retrieval, knowledge-graph RAG, and code-as-policies-style control.

**Red flags.**
- Quoted rather than rerun baselines (acknowledged but not fully resolved).
- Online edits are gated only by the agent's own reasoning + structural invariants; the authors explicitly note in Limitations that the long-horizon behavior of single-observation self-evolution "remains an open question." Aggregate effects are reported, but per-edit quality is not measured.
- The "+27 online edits" figure is small; the recall claim rests on a set-monotonicity argument rather than a large empirical audit of edit fidelity.

## Strengths

- **Architectural inversion is clean and falsifiable.** The graph-as-interface claim has a direct empirical signature (matches-field cosine vs. GoS PPR), and SkillDAG wins it cleanly.
- **Honest, well-bounded failure analysis.** The pilot's 11 failures are split into typed causes; the `</think>` leakage is flagged as a model artifact rather than buried.
- **Set-monotone online-edit guarantee** is a rare structural result in self-evolving-agent literature: an accepted edge cannot shrink the retrieved set, only enlarge it.
- **Robust at scale.** 10× pool expansion with only −3.5 Ret@5; this is the headline durability result and the one most likely to generalize.
- **Cross-backbone pattern is interpretable**, not just reported: large gains on a weaker backbone, near-ties on a stronger one — consistent with a routing-aid interpretation.
- **Open code & data** with concrete configurations (text-embedding-3-large, gpt-5-nano for cold-start classification, K=5, D=2, adaptive cosine thresholds).

## Limitations

- **Online edits are gated by agent reasoning alone**, with only structural guardrails. A single noisy execution observation can commit an edge; the authors explicitly leave long-horizon self-evolution as future work.
- **Baselines are quoted, not rerun** (acknowledged); the headline MiniMax gains are not under paired-seed comparison.
- **Backbone identity is partially anonymized** ("MiniMax-M2.7", "MiniMax Anthropic-compatible endpoint"), limiting independent verification of cross-backbone claims.
- **No semantic confidence threshold** on edits; only structural invariants. Per-edit fidelity is not audited beyond aggregate recall.
- **Cold-start cost** is moderate (200 classification calls at scale 1000) and depends on a gpt-5-nano-quality classifier for type assignment; quality of cold-start structure is bounded by classifier quality.
- **Two benchmarks only** (ALFWorld + SkillsBench); web environments (WebArena), OS control (OSWorld), API-rich apps (AppWorld), and τ-bench are not tested. The authors note this as a generalization pressure point.

## Relevance

对关注 **LLM 代理技能管理 / 大规模工具检索 / 结构化 RAG / 自演化代理** 的研究者高度相关——SkillDAG 把"图即接口"作为一条可复用原则提出，给出了与最强基线 GoS 的清晰对照实验、可规模化的检索证据以及稀缺的 set-monotone 在线编辑保证。对做 **LLM-as-policy / 工具调用基准（BFCL、τ-bench）** 的研究者也有方法论启发：在路由决策中暴露中间结构而非折叠成不透明 bundle，可能在弱骨干模型下获得最大收益。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the routing-aid signature generalize beyond MiniMax-M2.7-class backbones? Specifically, what is the threshold reasoning capability below which SkillDAG pays off most, and is it predictable from a benchmark like MMLU or AgentBench?
- How does the graph behave under **adversarial or distribution-shifted skill authoring** — e.g., skill bodies with misleading descriptions, near-duplicate skills with subtly different scopes? The set-monotone guarantee is recall-only; precision under noise is unmeasured.
- Can the **online edit protocol be tightened** without sacrificing agent-speed? The authors explicitly call this out: a confidence-weighted variant (multi-observation voting, schema-validated reasons) might preserve the set-monotone property while reducing bad-edit rate.
- How does SkillDAG compare against **GraphRAG / LightRAG / GeAR / HippoRAG** on a unified benchmark? The paper positions itself against GoS only; a broader structured-RAG head-to-head would clarify whether the gain is interface-design or specifically skill-graph-specific.
- What is the **interaction with prompt compression**? If AgentOCR-style rendering (cited as related work) is applied to the search response, does the three-field decomposition remain equally useful, or do typed neighbors and conflicts collapse into a single best-evidence field?
- Does the cold-start **two-view HyDE strategy transfer** to other typed graphs (tool ontologies, KG memory for embodied agents)? The RoboMemory / CaveAgent / XSkill citations suggest the authors have the right neighborhood to test this.
- How does the **+27 edit count scale** with skill library size and episode count? Set-monotone recall is a lower bound; whether the graph's precision plateaus, drifts, or improves is an empirical question for follow-up work.
