From Parameter Dynamics to Risk Scoring: Quantifying Sample-Level Safety Degradation in LLM Fine-tuning

arXiv: 2605.04572v1

Date: 2026-05-06

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04572v1

一句话总结： 本文从参数动力学视角揭示良性微调会使模型参数沿"危险方向"累积漂移，并提出 SQSD 方法，通过测量样本诱导参数更新在危险方向与安全方向上的投影差为每个训练样本计算连续风险分数，可在 LoRA 与全参微调、不同架构和参数规模上稳定识别高风险良性样本。
TL;DR: The paper traces fine-tuning trajectories in parameter space and shows that benign-data fine-tuning cumulatively drifts model parameters toward danger-aligned directions while safety directions stay nearly idle. Building on this, the authors propose SQSD, a continuous, corpus-wide sample-level risk score defined as the projection gap of a sample's induced parameter update onto danger versus safety directions; it consistently outperforms reward-model, representation-, and gradient-based baselines on Qwen3-8B, Llama3.1-8B-Instruct and Llama2-7B-Chat across Dolly and Alpaca, and transfers across architectures, scales (8B->14B/32B), and from LoRA to full fine-tuning.

核心贡献

  • 提出从"参数动力学"视角解释良性微调下的安全退化机制：参数会沿 Aegis-unsafe / Beaver-unsafe 等危险方向单调累积漂移，而安全方向投影几乎不变；安全分数表现出明显的两阶段非线性崩塌。
  • 提出 SQSD（Sample-Level Quantification of Safety Degradation）方法，对单步梯度更新在 LoRA 各模块上做模块级 L2 归一化后，分别投影到危险方向与安全方向，并对所有模块求和得到连续风险分数 SQSD(z) = Σ_m [<ΔW_m / ||ΔW_m||, V_danger,m> - <ΔW_m / ||ΔW_m||, V_safety,m>]。
  • 通过一阶 Taylor 展开建立 SQSD 与样本对偏好分布损失差（向危险/安全模型推动的程度）之间的解析联系，给出可解释的"参数更新-输出偏好"桥梁。
  • 设计基于方向敏感性（线性路径敏感度 DS_linear、漂移增强敏感度 DS_drift）的初始化策略，从 DPO/SFT 微调过程中挑选高敏感性 checkpoint 作为安全/危险方向的参考状态，以提升 SQSD 在局部线性近似下的可靠性。
  • 在 3 个模型 × 2 数据集 × 12 配置上验证：将语料按 SQSD 排名分为 5 个子集后再微调，10/12 配置下 ASR 从 S1 到 S5 单调下降；ΔASR(S1-S5) 平均 49.86%，显著高于最强基线 Reward Model 的 43.76%；且方法在跨架构、跨规模、LoRA→全参等设置下保持单调判别力。

Key Contributions

  • A parameter-dynamics explanation of safety degradation under benign fine-tuning: cumulative parameter drift onto Aegis-unsafe and Beaver-unsafe danger directions grows monotonically while drift onto a DPO-derived safety direction stays near zero, and safety collapses in a two-phase pattern (slow drift, then catastrophic decline once parameters leave the "safe basin").
  • SQSD, a continuous corpus-wide sample-level risk score: for a one-step LoRA gradient update, compute module-wise L2-normalized parameter updates, project each onto danger and safety directions, and aggregate the projection gaps across modules. Module-wise normalization removes the response-length bias that plagues prior gradient-based scorers.
  • A first-order Taylor analysis linking SQSD to preference-loss differences between safe and unsafe reference parameters, giving an interpretable bridge from "where the parameter update points" to "which behavior the sample reinforces".
  • A directional-sensitivity-based initialization (linear-path DS for V_safety, drift-enhanced DS for V_danger), with checkpoint selection rules and validation that local sensitivity strongly affects SQSD reliability.
  • Extensive empirical study on Qwen3-8B, Llama3.1-8B-Instruct, Llama2-7B-Chat with Dolly/Alpaca, evaluated via Safety Score, ASR on CategoricalHarmfulQA / AdvBench / HEx-PHI, and a transferability study across architectures, parameter scales (Qwen3-8B->14B/32B) and PE methods (LoRA->Full).

Methodology

The paper studies LoRA fine-tuning W' = W + (alpha/r) BA and tracks the parameter drift Delta_theta_t = theta_t - theta_0 throughout training. Two reference directions are constructed in parameter space: V_safety = hat{theta}_aligned - theta_0 (DPO on PKU-SafeRLHF-10K) and V_danger = hat{theta}_harmful - theta_0 (SFT on Aegis-unsafe or BeaverTails-unsafe subsets, 3K samples each, LoRA r=8 alpha=16). Validity is verified via parameter steering theta(alpha) = theta_0 + alpha V on CategoricalHarmfulQA, confirming monotone safety changes for Qwen3-8B and Llama3.1-8B-Instruct (Llama2-7B-Chat is already saturated, so the safe direction is locally valid only for alpha in roughly [0, 0.6]).

To score each training sample z = (x,y), SQSD performs three steps: (1) compute the LoRA gradient ΔA = -eta∇_A L_sft(z), ΔB = -eta∇_B L_sft(z), giving the module-level parameter update ΔW(z) ≈ -eta(B0 ∇A + ∇B A0); (2) for each LoRA-augmented module m, project the L2-normalized update onto the corresponding danger and safety direction; (3) sum the (danger - safety) projection gaps across modules. The connection to output preferences is established via Equation 13: eta[L(z, theta_ref) - L(z, theta_target)] ≈ (theta' - theta_ref)^T (theta_target - theta_ref), where theta' is the parameter after one gradient step on z. Substituting theta_target = theta_danger or theta_safety yields the two log-likelihood differences whose subtraction is exactly SQSD up to module-wise normalization.

For initialization, the authors define linear-path directional sensitivity DS_linear(alpha) along theta_0 + alpha V and drift-enhanced sensitivity DS_drift(t) along the actual fine-tuning trajectory, then select checkpoints (and alpha values) that maximize sensitivity from independent pilot fine-tunings. Tables 4 and 6 show that for danger directions the optimal checkpoint is typically deep into pilot training (e.g., Qwen3 step 5850 on Dolly), while safety direction uses the DPO checkpoint with alpha = 0.2-0.5.

Experiments use Qwen3-8B, Llama3.1-8B-Instruct, Llama2-7B-Chat fine-tuned on Dolly (5K) and Alpaca (5K) with LoRA (r=8, alpha=16, AdamW, batch 8, 10 epochs). The full LR for fine-tuning is 5e-5; mechanism validation uses 5e-6 for smoother trajectories. Safety is measured by Safety Score (beaver-7b-unified-cost reward model) and ASR judged by LlamaGuard3-8B on CategoricalHarmfulQA, AdvBench and HEx-PHI. The main effectiveness protocol partitions a corpus by SQSD ranking into 5 stratified subsets (S1 highest risk, S5 lowest), fine-tunes a fresh model on each subset, and checks (i) monotone ASR decrease S1->S5 and (ii) Δ = ASR(S1) - ASR(S5).

Results & Highlights

  • Mechanism validation (Figure 2, Figure 3): across Qwen3-8B / Llama3.1-8B-Instruct / Llama2-7B-Chat and Dolly / Alpaca, projections onto Aegis-unsafe and Beaver-unsafe rise monotonically with training, while safety projections hover near zero or are slightly negative; Safety Score declines in a striking two-phase pattern - slow degradation while danger projection grows from 0 to ~6, then collapse below 1.0. Larger datasets push final danger projection from ~8 (3K Alpaca) to ~12 (50K Alpaca) with Safety Score falling from 3.8 to -0.3 (Figure 4).
  • Sample-level risk scoring (Table 1, ASR on CategoricalHarmfulQA): SQSD achieves monotone S1->S5 ASR decrease in 10/12 configurations, vs. Reward Model 0/6 monotone (in the 6 it shares), Bi-Anchor(Grad) 1/6, Self-Inf-N 0/6, LARF 1/6. Δ averages 49.86% vs. Reward Model 43.76%. Concrete numbers: Qwen3-8B/Dolly SQSD(Beaver) drops ASR 71.27 -> 2.55 (Δ=68.72); Llama3.1-8B-Instruct/Dolly SQSD(Beaver) 79.82 -> 4.73 (Δ=75.09); Llama2-7B-Chat/Dolly SQSD(Beaver) 45.27 -> 0.36 (Δ=44.91).
  • Transfer (Table 2): SQSD scores computed on Llama->Qwen and Qwen->Llama keep monotone ASR ordering; cross-scale 8B->14B (Δ=55.09 -> 7.09 monotone) and 8B->32B (Δ=28.91 -> 2.55 monotone) hold; LoRA-derived SQSD applied to full fine-tuning gives Δ=10.73 -> 2.55 monotone, with much smaller absolute ASR (because full-FT degrades less). This is a meaningful transferability claim.
  • Ablations (Table 3): removing module-wise normalization collapses Δ from 68.72 to 12.54 and breaks monotonicity, confirming that the response-length-bias correction is critical. Danger-only retains 64.54 Δ but is non-monotonic; Safety-only collapses to 20.91. Direction-insensitive initialization drops S1 ASR from 71.27% to 38.36%.
  • Auxiliary benchmarks (Tables 7-9): on AdvBench, SQSD wins or near-wins on most settings (e.g., Llama3.1-8B-Instruct/Dolly Δ=85.38 SQSD(Aegis) and 88.08 SQSD(Beaver)); on HEx-PHI similar story (Llama3.1-8B-Instruct/Alpaca SQSD(Beaver) Δ=81.03). However, on Safety Score (Table 7) monotonicity holds in only ~5/12 configs even for SQSD, showing the metric does not always pick up gradual safety quality changes.
  • Learning-rate robustness (Figure 6): for LR in {1e-5, 3e-5, 5e-5, 8e-5, 1e-4}, S1->S5 ASR remains monotone; at LR=1e-5 the highest-risk S1 only reaches ~13.9% ASR, indicating low-LR fine-tuning has weak degradation regardless of selection.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The parameter-drift framing is meaningful but builds heavily on prior task-arithmetic / safety-landscape papers (Ilharco 2023; Peng 2024). The headline new contribution is module-wise normalization plus the projection-gap formulation as a continuous sample score. The formal Taylor connection is standard influence-function-flavored math; not a deep theoretical advance, but it is a clear and useful framing.
  • Rigor: Generally good. The paper does both mechanism validation and downstream "select-then-retrain" evaluation across 12 configs and three benchmarks. Hyperparameters are documented; baselines are reimplemented with explicit equations (Appendix D). However, the sensitivity-based checkpoint selection is somewhat hand-tuned (Table 6 picks top-1 sometimes, top-3/4 other times), which reduces confidence that SQSD is fully algorithmically specified rather than tuned per configuration.
  • Reproducibility: A code repo is mentioned but unverifiable from the PDF alone. Direction construction (Aegis subset 3K, BeaverTails 3K, PKU-SafeRLHF-10K, LoRA r=8 alpha=16, lr=5e-6, 10 epochs, specific checkpoint numbers) is documented well enough to reimplement. Three independent models and two datasets help. Random seeds are not discussed, and the partitioning of the corpus into 5 subsets is uniformly sampled from rankings, which still leaves room for stochastic variance not reported.
  • Significance: Quantifying which "looks-benign" samples drive safety collapse is a genuinely valuable problem. SQSD's transferability to larger scales and full fine-tuning matters for practitioners who want to score data on a small proxy model. The 49.86% average Δ vs. 43.76% for the best baseline is a real but not enormous margin; on Safety Score (a smoother metric), SQSD often fails monotonicity, suggesting it predominantly captures extreme-risk samples.
  • Red flags: (1) Table 7 shows SQSD often not monotone on Safety Score even though it is on ASR for the same fine-tunes - the method may be specialized to attack-style binary harm detection. (2) On Llama2-7B-Chat (already saturated alignment), the safety direction is admitted to be only locally valid; the supposedly principled directional construction has a baked-in ceiling. (3) The "sensitivity-driven" initialization picking from top-1 to top-4 checkpoints case-by-case is concerning - it looks like dataset-specific tuning that inflates reported Δ. (4) Dual-use concern is acknowledged but not mitigated technically; the same scoring can be inverted to handpick maximally-degrading benign samples.

Strengths

  • Clean parameter-space narrative ties together task arithmetic, safety landscape, learning dynamics, and influence functions into one continuous risk score.
  • Strong transferability experiments (cross-architecture, 8B->32B, LoRA->Full) - this is exactly the practically important claim and they actually test it.
  • Module-wise normalization ablation makes a clear technical case (Δ collapses from 68.72 to 12.54), so the engineering contribution is concrete.
  • Three benchmarks and two metrics provide reasonable robustness against single-benchmark overfitting.
  • Honest Appendix E discussion that "SQSD's effectiveness is parameter-dependent" and that direction construction may need adaptive or ensemble approaches in future work.

Limitations

  • Results on Safety Score (rather than binary ASR) are noticeably weaker; method may be capturing extreme-risk samples rather than smooth safety quality.
  • Direction construction depends on access to harmful and DPO-aligned datasets and on running pilot fine-tunings to find sensitivity-optimal checkpoints; this is heavyweight for production use.
  • Llama2-7B-Chat case shows the safety direction has only a narrow validity range (alpha in [0, 0.6]); for already over-aligned base models the framework partially breaks down.
  • All experiments are on 7-8B (with extrapolation to 14B/32B Qwen). No evaluation on truly large frontier models or on RLHF (vs. DPO) alignment baselines.
  • Single learning-rate-sensitivity experiment uses only Qwen3-8B/Dolly/Beaver-unsafe, so robustness claims are narrower than they may sound.
  • No reported variance across seeds or alternative random subset draws, so statistical significance of monotonicity claims is unclear.
  • Computational cost of computing per-sample LoRA gradients across all corpus samples is not discussed in detail.

Relevance

与你关注的"LLM 可解释性、可靠性与训练稳定性"高度相关：本文从参数空间动力学的角度解释了 SFT 阶段的安全退化机制（参数沿危险方向累积漂移），并把它转化为可计算的样本级风险分数，对理解 alignment 何时坍缩、如何在数据层面防御训练相关安全风险，具有方法论与诊断价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does SQSD compare with influence functions (e.g., TracIn, Datamodels) when the target metric is safety rather than task accuracy? The paper's Taylor argument is essentially a linearized influence-function analysis; an explicit comparison would clarify novelty.
  • Does the two-phase parameter-drift / safety-collapse pattern hold under RLHF (PPO) fine-tuning rather than DPO-derived safety directions? The "danger direction" is constructed via SFT on harmful data, which may bias the projection geometry.
  • Can the directional sensitivity analysis be replaced by something less hand-tuned - e.g., spectral analysis of the Hessian of the safety reward, or learned gating over modules - so the method becomes fully data-driven?
  • How robust is SQSD under data poisoning or adversarial benign data designed to evade projection-based detection (e.g., samples whose updates are large in directions orthogonal to V_danger but whose long-run trajectory still degrades safety)?
  • What is the wall-clock cost of computing SQSD over a 50K-sample corpus on an 8B model, and can it be made cheap enough to integrate into a fine-tuning pipeline as a real-time filter rather than a pre-training pass?
  • The paper mentions integrating SQSD with risk-aware fine-tuning algorithms - what does a continuous-risk-weighted loss look like, and does it actually preserve safety while retaining downstream utility on Dolly/Alpaca?
