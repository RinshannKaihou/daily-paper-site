> **一句话总结：** 提出 Anchored Learning（锚定学习）框架，在离线 SFT 中通过当前模型与冻结 SFT 参考模型间的动态插值构造"移动锚点"作为蒸馏目标，从而对每步分布更新施加显式的局部 KL 上界，将 iGSM 上 SFT 53% 以上的通用能力损失压低到 5% 以下，同时保留 75.2% 的领域增益。

> **TL;DR:** Anchored Learning replaces the fixed reference in standard SFT/KL-regularized fine-tuning with a dynamically interpolated "moving anchor" between the current model and a frozen SFT teacher. This yields a provable per-iteration KL bound that mimics an implicit trust region, slashing catastrophic forgetting from >53% to <5% on iGSM and MedCalc while keeping near-optimal task gains on Qwen2.5 and Llama-3.2 backbones.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **Anchored Learning**：一个两层（外层锚点选择 + 内层蒸馏拟合）的离线 SFT 框架，锚点为当前模型 $p_{\theta(t)}$ 与冻结 SFT 模型 $p_\text{sft}$ 在概率空间或 logit 空间的插值，随训练动态演化。
- 给出**可证的稳定性保证**：在概率空间插值下 $\text{KL}(q^{(t)} \| p_{\theta(t)}) \le \alpha \text{KL}(p_\text{sft} \| p_{\theta(t)})$；logit 空间下 $\text{KL}(q^{(t)} \| p_{\theta(t)}) \le \frac{\alpha}{1-\alpha} \text{KL}(p_{\theta(t)} \| p_\text{sft})$，等价于隐式信赖域约束。
- 证明动态锚点递推几何收敛到 $p_\text{sft}$（速率 $1-\alpha$），而静态 KL 重心会停在妥协分布上，由此论证了"移动锚点"相对于固定参考的本质优越性。
- 在 **iGSM/MedCalc/IFEval** 三类任务及 Qwen2.5（1.5B/3B/7B/14B）与 Llama-3.2-3B 两族骨干上系统验证，方法稳居增益–稳定性 Pareto 前沿；标准 SFT 在 iGSM/MedCalc 上通用能力损失 >53%，本文方法降至 <5%。
- 系统消融插值系数 $\alpha$、外/内循环步数 $T,K$、插值空间（logit vs probability），揭示 logit 空间的几何均值偏向稳定性（mode-seeking），概率空间的算术均值偏向可塑性（mode-covering）。

## Key Contributions

- **Anchored Learning framework**: a two-loop offline SFT procedure where each outer iteration constructs an anchor $q^{(t)}$ by interpolating the current model with a frozen SFT teacher, then runs $K$ inner distillation steps toward that anchor.
- **Theoretical guarantees**: explicit per-iteration KL bounds for both probability-space and logit-space interpolation prove the update is locally bounded, instantiating an implicit trust region in distribution space without parameter-space constraints.
- **Dynamic vs. static analysis**: a formal proof that the dynamic-anchor recursion geometrically converges to $p_\text{sft}$, whereas any static KL barycenter remains stuck at a fixed compromise with strictly positive divergence to $p_\text{sft}$.
- **Strong empirics across scales and tasks**: on iGSM and MedCalc, vanilla SFT drops general-capability by >53%; Anchored Learning keeps degradation under 5% while retaining ~75.2% of the domain gain on iGSM. Consistently sits on the Pareto frontier across 8 baselines (SFT, Low-SFT, KL-SFT, STM, DFT, Self-SFT, Iter-SFT) on Qwen2.5-3B and Llama-3.2-3B.
- **Logit vs. probability interpolation tradeoff** characterized via information geometry: logit interpolation is the reverse-KL barycenter (e-geodesic) and is mode-seeking; probability interpolation is the convex mixture and is mode-covering — different plasticity-stability sweet spots.

## Methodology

The framework targets purely offline SFT on a labeled dataset $\mathcal{D}$, after first training a fixed SFT reference $p_\text{sft}$ from a base model $p_\text{base}$. Training then proceeds for $T$ outer iterations:

1. **Outer loop — anchor selection via interpolation.** At iteration $t$, build an anchor distribution
   $q^{(t)}(\cdot \mid x) = \mathcal{I}_\alpha(p_{\theta(t)}(\cdot \mid x), p_\text{sft}(\cdot \mid x))$,
   with coefficient $\alpha \in (0,1)$. Two instantiations:
   - *Logit-space*: $z^{(t)}(x) = (1-\alpha) z_{\theta(t)}(x) + \alpha z_\text{sft}(x)$, then softmax — equivalent to a renormalized geometric mean and the reverse-KL barycenter.
   - *Probability-space*: $q^{(t)} = (1-\alpha) p_{\theta(t)} + \alpha p_\text{sft}$ — a convex mixture / arithmetic mean.
2. **Inner loop — anchor fitting via distillation.** Detach $q^{(t)}$ from the graph, then for $K$ epochs minimize $\text{KL}(q^{(t)} \| p_\theta)$ via AdamW/SGD on $\mathcal{D}$. The detached anchor serves as a stable, locally-consistent target.
3. **Iterate** with $p_{\theta(t+1)}$ becoming the new current model. Default settings: $\alpha=0.5$, $K=5$, $T=5$, logit-space interpolation, learning rate $1\times10^{-5}$.

The theoretical analysis proves three lemmas: probability-space gives $\text{KL}(q^{(t)} \| p_{\theta(t)}) \le \alpha \, \text{KL}(p_\text{sft} \| p_{\theta(t)})$ via Jensen on the convex KL functional; logit-space gives $\text{KL}(q^{(t)} \| p_{\theta(t)}) \le \frac{\alpha}{1-\alpha} \text{KL}(p_{\theta(t)} \| p_\text{sft})$ via Lagrangian minimization; and assuming exact inner-loop projection $p_{\theta(t+1)} = q^{(t)}$, the resulting recursion converges geometrically to $p_\text{sft}$ with rate $(1-\alpha)$, while a static KL barycenter $p^\star = \arg\min_p (1-\beta)\text{KL}(p\|p_\text{sft}) + \beta\text{KL}(p\|p_\text{base})$ has $\text{KL}(p^\star \| p_\text{sft}) > 0$ permanently.

Experiments cover three target tasks (iGSM grade-school math, MedCalc medical calculations, IFEval instruction-following), evaluated against six general-domain benchmarks (MMLU-Pro, Countdown, MBPP/MBPP+, HumanEval/HumanEval+) for catastrophic-forgetting measurement. Baselines: standard SFT, Low-SFT (lr $1\times10^{-6}$), KL-SFT ($\lambda=0.2$), Self-SFT (self-distillation à la STaR), Iter-SFT (approximate on-policy), STM (low-perplexity token masking), DFT (dynamic objective rescaling).

## Results & Highlights

- **Pareto-frontier dominance**: on Qwen2.5-3B + iGSM, Ours achieves Overall Avg 0.591 vs SFT 0.157, Low-SFT 0.558, KL-SFT 0.189, STM 0.520, DFT 0.353, Self-SFT 0.525, Iter-SFT 0.528. Ours retains iGSM 0.936 (vs SFT's 1.000 with collapsed general scores), MMLU-Pro 0.377, HumanEval 0.701.
- **Drastic forgetting reduction on MedCalc** (Qwen2.5-3B): SFT general avg collapses to ~0.10; Ours retains 0.518 overall (vs base 0.508), with MedCalc accuracy 0.563 close to SFT's 0.574.
- **Llama-3.2-3B + iGSM**: Ours overall 0.520 vs Low-SFT 0.456 and SFT 0.191, with iGSM 0.994 — both better domain and general retention than Low-SFT.
- **Scaling robustness** (Table 2 on iGSM): at 1.5B/3B/7B Qwen2.5, Ours improves general accuracy by +2.1/+3.7/+2.4 over Low-SFT while matching its iGSM accuracy almost exactly (0.950/0.936/0.968).
- **Multi-stage incremental learning** (Stage 1 iGSM → Stage 2 MedCalc → Stage 3 IFEval, Table 9): Ours achieves Overall Avg 0.492 vs Low-SFT 0.459 at Stage 3, with markedly higher domain avg (0.505 vs 0.331), demonstrating compounded drift mitigation.
- **Sensitivity findings**: domain accuracy saturates by $\alpha\approx30\%$; general retention is non-monotone in $\alpha$ and $(T,K)$, with distinct degradation basins — supports the paper's argument that aggressive but well-tuned schedules cannot replace explicit trajectory control.
- **Logit vs. probability interpolation**: logit (mode-seeking) preserves more general capability; probability (mode-covering) yields slightly higher domain accuracy — clean trade-off illustrated in Fig. 5.
- **Case study** ("Make 78" countdown task on iGSM-tuned model): Low-SFT mimics iGSM syntactic patterns and fails arithmetic; Anchored Learning preserves base-model reasoning and correctly answers $77 + 56 - 55 = 78$.
- **Cost**: ~1.5×–2× training latency/memory overhead vs SFT due to the extra forward pass for anchor construction.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (moderate-to-high).** Replacing a fixed reference in KL-regularized fine-tuning with a model-evolving "moving anchor" is a clean idea that productively borrows from policy-iteration / trust-region thinking and from the e-geodesic intuition in information geometry. However, the technique is closely related to (and partially overlaps with) iterative self-distillation, on-policy distillation, and POLICY/PPO-style proximal updates. The novelty lies less in the algorithm itself than in (a) the explicit per-iteration KL bound proofs and (b) the systematic comparison vs. static barycenters in the offline setting.

**Rigor (high on theory, moderate on empirics).** The theoretical lemmas are well-stated, with detailed proofs in Appendix A using standard Jensen-inequality and Lagrangian arguments — clean and correct as far as I checked. However, the dynamic-convergence proposition assumes *exact* inner-loop projection ($p_{\theta(t+1)} = q^{(t)}$), which is unrealistic for $K=5$ AdamW steps; the paper does not bound the gap. Empirically, results are consistent across two model families and three tasks, with multiple seeds presumably averaged (not stated explicitly), but I did not see error bars/std-dev in the reported tables, which is a notable gap given small absolute differences vs. Low-SFT on some metrics.

**Reproducibility (moderate).** Hyperparameters ($\alpha=0.5$, $K=5$, $T=5$, lr $10^{-5}$) and baselines are specified. Datasets are public (iGSM, MedCalc, IFEval, MMLU-Pro, etc.). However, no code/model release is mentioned in the read sections. Anchor construction with detached graph is straightforward to reimplement.

**Significance (moderate-to-high if claims hold).** Catastrophic forgetting in offline SFT is a real and current pain point (especially for domain-specific instruction tuning of public LLMs). A method with theoretical guarantees and 5%-cap on general-capability degradation would be practically valuable. Practical adoption depends on whether the 1.5–2× compute overhead is acceptable.

**Red flags / concerns**:
1. The headline claim "53% degradation reduced to <5%" compares against vanilla SFT, which is essentially a strawman in 2026 — most practitioners already use Low-SFT or KL-regularization. Against Low-SFT, the *general* performance gain is more modest (e.g., Qwen2.5-3B iGSM general avg 0.591 vs 0.558, ~3.3 points).
2. KL-SFT performs surprisingly poorly across the board (e.g., 0.189 overall on iGSM). The paper's $\lambda=0.2$ is a single point — KL-SFT may have been under-tuned, weakening the comparison.
3. No std-dev / variance reporting visible.
4. The convergence theorem assumes exact projection — empirical $K=5$ inner steps clearly do not satisfy this; the proposition is more illustrative than predictive of trained behavior.
5. SFT row sometimes shows iGSM=1.000 — likely a memorization artifact of the synthetic iGSM benchmark; collapse on general tasks could exaggerate the contrast.
6. "Pareto frontier" claim is visually backed (Fig. 2) but no formal Pareto computation/test is reported.

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical story tying probability-space, logit-space, and information-geometric (e-geodesic) views together.
- Results consistent across multiple model scales (1.5B–14B Qwen2.5) and two model families (Qwen, Llama).
- Multi-stage continual-learning experiment (iGSM→MedCalc→IFEval) directly addresses compounding drift, a realistic deployment scenario.
- Strong, current set of baselines (KL-SFT, STM, DFT, Self-SFT, Iter-SFT) — not just compared to vanilla SFT.
- Useful information-geometric framing: probability-mixture = mode-covering (plasticity), logit-interpolation = mode-seeking (stability) — gives practitioners a tunable knob with intuition.
- Honest about limitations (1.5–2× compute overhead is acknowledged).

## Limitations

- 1.5–2× training latency and memory overhead vs. standard SFT due to anchor forward pass.
- Convergence theorem requires exact inner-loop projection; gap analysis missing.
- Only off-policy (offline) distillation studied; on-policy / hybrid variants left to future work.
- No variance / multi-seed reporting in tables.
- KL-SFT baseline is potentially under-tuned (single $\lambda=0.2$).
- "Forgetting" is measured by held-out general benchmarks; deeper analyses (representational drift, calibration, jailbreak susceptibility) are not reported.
- iGSM is a controllable-difficulty synthetic benchmark; SFT achieving 1.000 suggests near-memorization, which may inflate the contrast on general benchmarks.
- No code/model release indicated in the read pages.
- No comparison against parameter-isolation methods (LoRA, adapters), which the authors explicitly exclude from scope.

## Relevance

与你关注的 LLM 训练稳定性、可靠性方向高度相关：本文对"分布漂移→灾难性遗忘"机制给出了显式的 KL 上界证明并配套可落地算法，对理解 SFT 阶段为何不稳定、以及如何在保留通用能力前提下做领域适配，有直接的方法论与实证参考价值。论文将信息几何（e-geodesic / KL 重心）与信赖域观点串联起来，对解读模型行为漂移的"路径依赖性"也有帮助。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How tight is the KL bound under realistic $K$ inner steps? Could one bound $\text{KL}(p_{\theta(t+1)} \| q^{(t)})$ as a function of $K$ and the loss landscape curvature?
- Does the moving-anchor idea extend to RLHF / DPO settings, where the "frozen reference" is itself a moving target?
- How does the method interact with parameter-efficient adaptation (LoRA, IA³)? Would explicit distributional control still be necessary if updates are already low-rank-constrained?
- Is logit-space interpolation always preferable for stability, or are there task families (open-ended generation, creative writing) where probability-space's mode-covering helps?
- Can the interpolation coefficient $\alpha$ be made adaptive (e.g., scheduled by measured drift) rather than fixed?
- What happens when $p_\text{sft}$ is itself imperfect (small SFT data)? Does the method amplify or dampen the noise in the teacher?
- Robustness check: does the method preserve safety-relevant behaviors (refusal, calibration) and not just capability benchmarks?
- How does Anchored Learning compare in the multi-task / replay-buffer setting once the offline-only constraint is relaxed?
