Superposition Is Not Necessary: A Mechanistic Interpretability Analysis of Transformer Representations for Time Series Forecasting

arXiv: 2605.05151v1

Date: 2026-05-06

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05151v1

一句话总结： 作者通过在 PatchTST 的 post-GELU FFN 激活上训练稀疏自编码器（SAE），并在 8 个标准长程预测基准上做字典扩张（0.5×–4.0×）和因果干预实验，发现字典扩张对下游 MSE 平均仅造成 0.214% 的变化、过完备字典中存在大量"死潜变量"、对前 10 个最活跃潜变量放大 5× 也只造成约 0.031 MAE 的预测漂移，从机制层面证明"叠加（superposition）"在标准时序预测任务中并非必要，从而为 DLinear 等线性模型为何持续具有竞争力提供了表征层面的解释。
TL;DR: This paper applies sparse autoencoders (SAEs) — the standard mechanistic interpretability tool from LLM research — to PatchTST's post-GELU FFN activations across 8 long-term forecasting benchmarks, and finds three convergent signatures that superposition is not necessary for competitive forecasting: (1) expanding the SAE dictionary from 0.5× to 4.0× the FFN width changes downstream MSE by only ~0.214% on average, (2) overcomplete dictionaries leave many latents inactive (e.g. ~60% dead latents on Traffic), and (3) amplifying the top-10 most active latents by 5× shifts forecasts by only ~0.031 MAE. The author argues this gives a mechanistic (not just empirical) reason why simple linear models like DLinear and FITS remain competitive: standard forecasting benchmarks may simply lack the representational richness that would reward the rich feature composition transformers excel at in language.

核心贡献

  • 首个对时序预测 transformer 的机制可解释性分析：将 SAE 这一在 LLM 上广泛使用的工具迁移到 PatchTST，专门探针 post-GELU FFN 中间激活，填补了 NLP/视觉之外的空白。
  • 证明单层窄维 transformer 已足够：在 RoPE + RMSNorm 现代化后的单层 PatchTST（d_model 16–128）能在 8 个基准上匹配原 PatchTST/42（3 层、逐数据集调参）的性能，作为后续可解释性分析的简化基底。
  • 三重叠加判据均失败：字典扩张、死潜变量比例、因果干预敏感度三个"如果存在强叠加就应当出现"的预测，在所有 8 个基准与 4 个预测步长下均未观察到，构成对叠加假说在该任务上不必要的强经验反驳。
  • 稀疏惩罚校准实验：扫描 λ ∈ {0.1, 0.01, 0.001, 0.0001}（共 256 次 SAE 训练）确认结果不是稀疏惩罚伪影。
  • FFN 零消融对照实验：证明 post-GELU 探针位置不是"死部件"——在 32 个配置中 28 个出现正向退化（中位 +6.8%，Electricity H=96 高达 +131.4%），保证了 SAE 探针对象本身是功能活跃的。
  • 对 DLinear / FITS 长期争议给出表征层面的解释：标准基准的本征复杂度可能根本不要求 transformer 所擅长的稀疏特征组合，因此 ~10k 参数的 FITS 也能匹配 PatchTST。

Key Contributions

  • First SAE-based mechanistic interpretability study of a time-series-forecasting transformer (PatchTST), as opposed to recent concurrent work on time-series foundation models (Chronos-T5/Chronos-2) or on classification.
  • Establishes that a single-layer, narrow PatchTST (d_model as small as 16; modernized with RoPE and RMSNorm) matches the published 3-layer, per-dataset-tuned PatchTST/42 across 8 standard benchmarks — making it a defensible simplified subject for mechanistic probing.
  • Three converging negative results for the superposition hypothesis in this setting:
  • Dictionary scaling: expanding 0.5× → 4.0× changes downstream MSE by only +0.214% on average.
  • Dead latents: overcomplete (4.0×) dictionaries leave large fractions of latents inactive (e.g. ~60–66% on Traffic, several % on most ETT variants).
  • Causal interventions: 5× amplification of the top-10 most active latents produces only ~0.031 average MAE shift.
  • SAE sparsity calibration sweep (256 training runs over λ ∈ {0.1, 0.01, 0.001, 0.0001}) shows L0 responds smoothly, refuting the "results are an artifact of an over-aggressive sparsity penalty" critique.
  • FFN functional validation via zero ablation confirms the probed component is non-trivial: 28/32 configurations show positive degradation upon FFN ablation, with a median of +6.8%, peaking at +131.4% on Electricity H=96.
  • A mechanistic — not merely empirical — argument for why DLinear (Zeng et al., 2023) and FITS (~10k parameters; Xu et al., 2024) remain competitive: the benchmarks themselves may not demand the kind of feature compression that motivates transformer architectures in language.

Methodology

Base model. PatchTST with channel-independent patching (patch length 16, stride 8), modernized with Rotary Position Embeddings (RoPE) replacing learned absolute positions, and RMSNorm replacing BatchNorm. RoPE is chosen partly to avoid positional information leaking into the residual stream, producing cleaner FFN activations for SAE analysis. Critically, depth=1, with d_model ∈ {16, 32, 96, 128, 192, 256} per dataset and FFN width 2× d_model.

Training. Lookback 336; horizons {96, 192, 336, 720}; AdamW lr=2e-4, no weight decay, MSE loss, dropout 0.2, gradient clipping 1.0; 80 epochs with patience 15; ReduceLROnPlateau; bfloat16; fixed seed; 70/10/20 chronological splits (or standard ETT splits).

SAE design. Hook at post-GELU intermediate FFN activation (matching standard LLM SAE practice from Bricken et al., 2023; Cunningham et al., 2024). Linear encoder d_ff → d_hidden, ReLU, linear decoder back to d_ff with unit-norm decoder columns. Dictionary scales: 0.5×, 1.0×, 4.0× of d_ff. Up to 10^6 patch-level activation vectors collected from the frozen base model. Adam, lr=1e-3, λ=0.01, loss = ||x − x̂||² + λ||f||₁, 50 epochs with early stopping (patience 3, threshold 0.1%).

Three probes for superposition.

  1. Dictionary scaling analysis — replace post-GELU activations with SAE reconstructions during inference; report % MSE change vs. baseline. Strong superposition predicts 4.0× ≫ 0.5× in fidelity preservation.
  2. Dead latent analysis — count SAE latents with activation < 1e-5 across the test set in the 4.0× dictionary. High dead-latent rates indicate unused capacity.
  3. Causal latent interventions — identify top-10 latents in the 4.0× SAE by total activation magnitude; amplify each by 5× during inference; measure MAE between original and intervened forecasts.

Validation experiments. (a) Single-layer sufficiency comparison vs. PatchTST/42 (Appendix A, Table 4). (b) FFN zero ablation to confirm the probed component is functionally active (Appendix B, Table 5). (c) SAE sparsity calibration: λ sweep × {0.5×, 4.0×} × 8 benchmarks × 4 horizons = 256 SAE runs (Table 3).

Results & Highlights

  • Single-layer PatchTST matches PatchTST/42 (Table 4): nearly identical on Weather and Electricity; modest gaps on Traffic and ETTh2 attributed mostly to the absence of per-dataset tuning.
  • Dictionary scaling (Table 1): average degradation 0.5×/1.0×/4.0× of +0.214% from baseline; many cells show negative degradation (slight improvements consistent with denoising). On ETTh2, 4.0× actually improves MSE in 3 of 4 horizons (e.g. H=336: −0.93%).
  • Dead latents (Table 1): Traffic stands out (54.9–65.8% dead latents at H=192/336/720); Exchange 3.9–7.0%; ETTh1 3.9–5.5%; ETTm2 0.0–1.6%. Notably, even when most latents fire (95%+ on ETTm2), this combines with negligible downstream scaling and minimal causal sensitivity, which the author argues indicates "reconstruction-relevant but functionally inert structure."
  • Causal intervention shifts (Table 1): average MAE shift ~0.031, max ~0.061, despite 5× amplification of the top-10 latents.
  • SAE fidelity (Table 2): 4.0× dictionaries do achieve lower reconstruction MSE than 0.5× across all benchmarks, confirming the SAE itself is working — improved internal fidelity simply does not translate to forecasting gains.
  • Sparsity calibration (Table 3): L0 increases smoothly as λ decreases (e.g. Weather 4.0×: 0.0 → 5.3 → 32.6 → 143.5 across λ ∈ {0.1, 0.01, 0.001, 0.0001}), with 4.0× consistently above 0.5× — yet downstream impact stays flat per Table 1.
  • FFN zero ablation (Table 5): median degradation +6.8%, with strongest effects on the highest-d_ff datasets (Electricity +131.4% at H=96, Traffic +40.3%) — confirming the FFN is doing real work and the probe target is non-trivial.

Critical Assessment

Novelty (moderate-to-high). The first mechanistic-interpretability study targeted at a non-foundation, task-specific time-series forecasting transformer is genuinely new and asks a question the DLinear debate has been circling without ever asking mechanistically. However, the SAE methodology itself is entirely off-the-shelf, and the three probes (dictionary scaling, dead latents, causal interventions) are all standard LLM-SAE diagnostics rather than novel measurements.

Rigor (good for a single-author paper, with caveats).

  • Strengths: Multiple converging probes; explicit FFN-validity ablation (Appendix B); sparsity calibration sweep specifically designed to defuse the "your λ is too aggressive" critique; honest acknowledgement that 95%+ latent activity on most datasets superficially looks like superposition and explicit reasoning for why it is not.
  • Weaknesses: Only PatchTST is studied; only post-GELU FFN is probed (attention heads, residual stream, and embedding spaces are not). Author concedes both. Single seed per configuration is not ideal for noise-level claims at the 0.1–0.3% MSE scale where many of the headline effects live — Table 1 shows shifts smaller than typical seed variance in long-term forecasting work. The single-layer base model (1 transformer block, depth=1) is a very narrow object to generalize from.
  • Title overreach: "Superposition Is Not Necessary" is an "absence-of-evidence" claim, and the paper's own Limitations section is appropriately humble ("we cannot rule out that alternative SAE training regimes, sparsity penalties, or decomposition methods might recover stronger sparse structure"). The body is more careful than the title implies.

Reproducibility (above average). Public code repository, standard benchmarks (Time Series Library), explicit hyperparameters (lookback, horizons, optimizer, λ values, dictionary sizes), standard splits. One missing element: no number of seeds reported, and no statistical-significance machinery on the % degradation numbers, which is the load-bearing evidence.

Significance (medium). If accepted by the field, this would be a satisfying mechanistic complement to the DLinear / FITS empirical findings and could redirect benchmark design toward harder, higher-intrinsic-complexity tasks (the author's broader-impact section gestures at EEG, ECG, ICP, and multi-asset financial data). It does not invalidate transformers for time series — it argues the standard benchmarks are too easy to demonstrate the representational richness transformers offer. That is a useful, tractable claim.

Red flags.

  • Sole author, no institutional affiliation listed.
  • Title is stronger than the evidence (single architecture, single hook location, single seed implied).
  • The 0.214% average MSE shift is in a regime where seed-to-seed variance is plausibly comparable; without a seed sweep this is the single weakest part of the empirical case.
  • 95%+ latent activity on most datasets is dismissed as "reconstruction-relevant but functionally inert" — defensible but slightly post-hoc; an alternative SAE objective (e.g. TopK, gated, or activation-based) might decompose it differently.
  • Year 2026 arxiv submission — appropriate for the cited concurrent work but worth noting.

Strengths

  • Question is sharp and important. "Why is DLinear competitive?" has been debated for ~3 years on benchmark comparisons alone; offering a mechanistic answer is a real contribution.
  • Three independent probes converge. Dictionary scaling, dead latents, and causal interventions are not redundant — each rules out a different way superposition could be hiding.
  • Defensive ablations are present. The FFN zero-ablation (28/32 configs degraded) and the λ sweep (256 runs) directly attack the two most obvious objections.
  • Honest framing in the Discussion section. The author explicitly does not claim no time-series transformer ever uses superposition; the claim is restricted to "not necessary on standard benchmarks."
  • Clear writing. Section structure (single-layer sufficiency → SAE scaling → causal probe → calibration → discussion) makes the argument easy to follow.
  • Public code and standard benchmarks make the work approachable for replication.

Limitations

  • Single architecture (PatchTST), single hook location (post-GELU FFN). Attention heads and residual-stream representations are unprobed; iTransformer, FEDformer, etc. are not analyzed.
  • Single-layer, narrow base model. While the author shows it matches PatchTST/42, the small ~2% MSE gap on Traffic/ETTh2 cannot be ruled out as the place where superposition lives.
  • No seed sweep / no error bars. Headline 0.214% MSE shifts are uncomfortably close to typical training noise.
  • Standard SAE only. TopK-SAE, gated-SAE, end-to-end-SAE, or sparse-coding alternatives might decompose differently; the author acknowledges this.
  • No qualitative latent inspection. Unlike Bricken et al. or Templeton et al., there is no "we found a latent for X" content, so the absence-of-superposition claim cannot be cross-checked against feature monosemanticity.
  • Benchmarks themselves under scrutiny. The author's own argument suggests the standard 8 benchmarks may be the wrong testbed — but no alternative high-complexity benchmark is provided in this paper.

Relevance

该论文与用户关心的"LLM 可解释性 / 可靠性"高度相关：它将 LLM 中已成熟的 SAE 工具迁移到时序模型，给出了一份"叠加假说在多大程度上是任务驱动而非架构驱动"的反例式案例研究，对理解 transformer 内部表征的普适性边界、以及如何审慎地把 NLP 解释性结论外推到其他模态都有方法论参考价值；同时其"单层即可媲美深层"的观察也间接连接到训练稳定性 / 容量冗余话题。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Would TopK-SAE or gated-SAE on the same activations recover any sparse structure that the L1-SAE missed, given Heap et al.'s (2025) caution that L1-SAEs can produce illusory features even on random networks?
  • How do the results change when probing the residual stream and attention-head outputs rather than only post-GELU FFN? The author flags this as future work but it is a load-bearing assumption.
  • On a deliberately high-complexity benchmark (the author suggests EEG pre-ictal, ECG, ICP, multi-asset finance), does the same SAE probe now reveal superposition on PatchTST? This would convert the negative result into a positive design principle.
  • With 5+ seeds per configuration, does the 0.214% average MSE shift remain inside the noise floor, and does that change the title-level claim?
  • Does iTransformer (variates-as-tokens) produce a different superposition signature than PatchTST (patches-as-tokens)? The two architectures encode different inductive biases about feature composition.
  • Can SAE-derived "functionally inert" latents be removed via pruning to yield a smaller, equivalently performing model — a direct test of the claim that they encode reconstruction-relevant but non-causal structure?
  • Given the FITS result (~10k parameters matches PatchTST), does PatchTST's FFN even need to be GELU-activated? An ablation to a linear FFN would tighten the "no nonlinear feature composition is needed" claim mechanistically.
