SLAM: Structural Linguistic Activation Marking for Language Models

arXiv: 2605.05443v1

Date: 2026-05-06

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05443v1

一句话总结： SLAM 利用稀疏自编码器（SAE）定位 LLM 残差流中编码句法结构（语态、时态、从句顺序）的方向，并在生成时通过 PCA 双向 SVD 得到的 k 个正交方向因果性地"操控"句法形式而非词汇分布，从而在 Gemma-2 2B/9B 上以仅 1–2 reward 分的质量代价实现 100% 检测准确率（远低于 KGW/EWD/Unigram 的 7.5–11.5 分），并保留几乎不打水印的自然度与多样性。
TL;DR: SLAM is a white-box LLM watermarking scheme that writes the mark into structural geometry (syntactic SAE directions like passive voice or PP-fronting) rather than next-token frequencies. Using a contrastive mining pipeline over 46,579 sentence pairs and PCA-bidirectional SVD to compose k orthogonal structural directions, SLAM causally steers which syntactic variant is produced while leaving lexical sampling free. On Gemma-2 2B/9B PT, it achieves 100% TPR at ~2.3% FPR with only -1.3/-1.9 ΔReward (vs. -7.5 to -11.5 for KGW/EWD/Unigram), and uniquely avoids catastrophic failure across reward, PPL, distinct-n, Self-BLEU, MAUVE, and grammar metrics. The trade-off is a complementary robustness profile: 100% TPR under all word-level edits but only 10–12% TPR under syntax-restructuring DIPPER paraphrase.

核心贡献

  • 提出 SLAM：首个将水印嵌入到 LLM 残差流的"结构性几何"而非 token 频次的白盒水印方案，从机制上把检测信号与质量代价解耦。
  • 构建 对比挖掘管道：46,579 条句对覆盖 104 种句法/形态/篇章现象 × 5 个语义域（金融/生物/体育/小说/新闻），用 contrastive × purity × consistency 复合评分定位结构性 SAE 方向。
  • 提出 PCA 双向 SVD 结构方向：对差异矩阵做 SVD 取前 k 个右奇异向量，叠加双向挖掘（同时建模 x⁺ 和 x⁻），使 SLAM 在 9B 模型（特征分布更稀疏）中仍可工作。
  • 工程层面：采用 HMAC 密钥化特征选择 + 早停生成（N≤4 候选），端到端开销仅为基线 1.7×/1.6×（约 420–640 ms），比 SAEMark 少一个数量级的 LLM 调用。
  • 在 Gemma-2 2B/9B + 6 个基线（KGW、EWD、Unigram、SynthID、Adaptive、SAEMark）上做四类质量度量（ΔReward、条件 PPL 比、grammar、distinct-n/Self-BLEU/MAUVE）实证，是唯一在所有指标上都不灾难性崩溃的方法。

Key Contributions

  • A new watermarking paradigm: encoding the mark into the residual stream's structural geometry (syntactic features) rather than next-token distributions, decoupling detectability from sampling quality.
  • Contrastive mining pipeline: 46,579 sentence pairs spanning 104 syntactic/morphological/discourse phenomena × 5 semantic domains, with composite scoring (contrastive × purity × cross-domain consistency) to isolate structural SAE directions.
  • PCA-bidirectional structural directions: SVD of the contrastive difference matrix yields k orthogonal modes; bidirectional mining doubles the steerable bank, enabling scaling from 2B (k=1) to 9B (k>1) where structural concepts are distributed across 5–15 weakly discriminative features.
  • Complete white-box scheme: HMAC-keyed feature selection, early-stop generation loop (N≤4 candidates), 1.7×/1.6× overhead, detection in a single forward pass (~420–640 ms).
  • Comprehensive empirical validation: on Gemma-2 2B/9B PT against 6 baselines (KGW, EWD, Unigram, SynthID, Adaptive, SAEMark) across four quality metric families plus 7 attack types.

Methodology

Setup. White-box watermarking using public Gemma Scope residual-stream SAEs (width 16k, L0 ≈ 50–80) over Gemma-2 2B and 9B (both PT and IT via transferability).

Step 1 — Contrastive dataset construction. 46,579 (x⁺, x⁻) sentence pairs covering 104 phenomena: 90 from LinguaLens plus 14 hand-authored BLiMP-derived alternations (passive, dative, cleft). Pairs hold semantic content fixed (validated via AMR) and vary only structure. Pairs span five semantic domains; the cross-domain design ensures only syntactic variance survives in the mean.

Step 2 — Structural feature mining. Per-token SAE encoding at layer l: ϕᵢ = f(hˡᵢ), then mean-pool. For each SAE feature j, compute Δμⱼ = E[ϕ̄ⱼ(x⁺) − ϕ̄ⱼ(x⁻)]. Composite score sⱼ = |Δμⱼ| × purityⱼ × consistencyⱼ retains roughly 2–4% of raw candidates.

Step 3 — PCA-bidirectional SVD directions. Stack pairwise differences into D ∈ ℝ^(N×n), compute thin SVD D = UΣVᵀ, take top-k right singular vectors vⱼ, then map back to residual stream: dⱼ = [vⱼ]₊ · W_dec / ‖[vⱼ]₊ · W_dec‖₂ (clipping to non-negative SAE components). Bidirectional mining processes (x⁻, x⁺) reversed pairs to double the steerable bank.

Step 4 — Watermark injection. At inference, hook into layer l: hˡₜ ← hˡₜ + α · v_structure (only on newly generated tokens). Per-document HMAC(key, doc_id)-seeded strength-proportional weighted sampling selects features. Up to N=4 candidates are sampled and the first one clearing calibrated ẑ ≥ 2.0 is returned (early-stop). Final hyperparameters: α=3 (2B), α=12 (9B), k=10.

Step 5 — Detection. One model forward pass; project per-token residual activations directly onto pre-computed dⱼ (an O(d) operation, no SAE forward pass required at detection). Compute Stouffer z-score zⱼ = (ϕ̄ⱼ(x) − μ_null) / (σ_null/√T), aggregate across features above z=0.5, calibrate against null distribution. Threshold ẑ ≥ 2.0 corresponds to ~2.3% FPR.

Evaluation. 100 prompts × 5 domains (academic/finance/medicine/news/technology); domains held-out from mining (3 of 5 disjoint). Quality measured via Skywork-Reward-V2 ΔReward, conditional PPL ratio, grammar errors, distinct-n, Self-BLEU, MAUVE, cosine drift. Robustness measured under 7 attacks (DIPPER, random walk, context-syn., synonym sub., word del./sub., reorder).

Results & Highlights

  • 100% TPR at 2.3% FPR on both Gemma-2 2B and 9B PT.
  • Quality gap: ΔReward = −1.3 / −1.9 on 2B/9B vs. −9.1/−7.5 (KGW), −8.6/−7.7 (EWD), −10.7/−11.5 (Unigram). Three- to six-fold smaller quality cost than token-distribution baselines.
  • Conditional PPL ratio of 1.24/1.36 vs. KGW/EWD/Unigram's 0.30–0.42 (collapse onto repetitive text). SLAM closest to the unwatermarked ideal of 1.0.
  • Distinct-n within |Δ|=0.013/0.024 of baseline (KGW/EWD/Unigram lose ≥50% of unique bigrams); Self-BLEU within |Δ|=0.015/0.017; MAUWE second on 2B and best on 9B.
  • SAEMark collapses to 13% TPR on 9B, validating that its dependence on semantic SAE features does not transfer across model scale; SLAM's structural features are domain- and scale-invariant by design.
  • Robustness asymmetry: SLAM achieves 100% TPR on all word-level attacks (synonym sub., context-syn., word del., word sub., reorder, random walk) but drops to 12%/10% TPR under DIPPER paraphrase on 2B/9B. Token-distribution methods show the opposite: KGW retains ≥98% on word-level but drops to 56%/64% under DIPPER, Unigram to 51%/62%.
  • IT model transferability: applying PT SAEs to IT models still maintains 99%/93% TPR but quality cost grows to ~−4 ΔReward (motivating future matched-IT-SAE deployment).
  • End-to-end overhead: 1.7×/1.6× unwatermarked baseline; ~420–640 ms vs. SAEMark's ~10.7 s (Adaptive) — an order of magnitude fewer LLM calls.

Critical Assessment

Novelty (high). The conceptual move — encoding watermarks in causally steerable structural subspaces rather than token distributions — is a genuinely new axis in the watermarking design space, distinct from both token-distribution biasing (KGW/SynthID family) and surface-form linguistic rewriting (synonym substitution). The closest prior work, SAEMark, uses SAE features only as a post-hoc scorer over candidate generations, not as causal steering targets, and reportedly collapses on 9B. The PCA-bidirectional SVD compositional directions are a legitimate technical advance enabling scale to 9B.

Rigor (good but with caveats). The evaluation is strong on multi-axis quality (ΔReward + conditional PPL + grammar + 3 distributional metrics + 7 attacks). The authors explicitly call out that single-metric quality oracles are unreliable and use this to expose SynthID's PPL-collapse blind spot — an unusually honest stance. However: (a) only Gemma-2 family is tested — no Llama/Qwen/Mistral validation despite Qwen-Scope SAEs being mentioned as future work; (b) the 100-prompt evaluation is small for detection-rate claims, even if uniformly 100% TPR; (c) no human side-by-side preference study (acknowledged limitation); (d) reward-model–based ΔReward inherits Skywork-Reward-V2's biases.

Reproducibility (moderate). SAE backbone (Gemma Scope) is public; watermarking model (Gemma-2) is public; baselines via MarkLLM (public). Hyperparameters (α, k, F) and bank sizes are reported with sweeps in appendix. No explicit code release statement is visible in the first 10 pages, which is a notable gap for a watermarking paper where calibration details matter.

Significance (moderate-high). If the structural-watermark idea generalizes beyond Gemma-2, it materially shifts the watermarking quality–detection frontier and makes provenance attribution practical without obvious prose degradation. The complementary robustness profile (word-level immune, paraphrase vulnerable) means SLAM is best paired with token-distribution methods in a defense-in-depth posture rather than as a drop-in replacement — the paper is upfront about this.

Red flags / concerns.

  • DIPPER vulnerability is severe (10–12% TPR) and the paper frames it as an "acceptable, calculated cost" — but in deployment scenarios where adversaries are motivated (e.g., academic plagiarism, disinformation laundering), DIPPER-class paraphrase is exactly the threat model. Calling 10% TPR "complementary" is generous; it is closer to "broken" against this attack class.
  • Single-bit watermarking only; multi-bit is "straightforward but not implemented" — many real provenance applications require multi-bit user/session attribution.
  • White-box residual-stream access requirement restricts deployment to first-party model providers, in contrast to KGW/Unigram/SynthID which only need logits.
  • PT-SAE → IT-model penalty (~−4 ΔReward) is large and undermines the "near-lossless" framing for the most realistic deployment surface (chat-tuned models).
  • The authors' own prior ACL paper "Sandcastles in the storm" argues against strong watermarking impossibility — useful to keep in mind that they are not naïve about the threat model, but readers should verify whether SLAM's framing in §1 fully integrates that prior pessimism.

Strengths

  • Clean mechanistic story: "steer which structural variant, not which tokens" is a memorable and falsifiable claim, and the multi-axis quality evidence directly supports it.
  • The contrastive mining pipeline is well-designed (cross-domain, AMR-validated, 104 phenomena), giving high confidence that mined directions actually encode syntax rather than spurious lexical variance.
  • Honest, multi-axis quality evaluation that explicitly exposes single-metric blind spots (e.g., calling out SynthID's reward-model–PPL inconsistency).
  • Engineering pragmatism: HMAC keying, early-stop, single-pass detection, ~10× faster than SAEMark.
  • Good ablations promised in appendix (k×α frontier, IT transferability, layer sweeps).

Limitations

  • Evaluated only on Gemma-2 family; no cross-model-family validation.
  • Single-bit only; no multi-bit watermarking implemented.
  • Severe vulnerability to syntax-restructuring paraphrase (DIPPER) — 10–12% TPR.
  • Requires white-box residual-stream access; cannot run on logit-only API access.
  • IT-model transfer incurs ~−4 ΔReward penalty using PT SAEs.
  • Detection is GPU-only (residual-stream forward pass), unlike CPU-side token-scan detection of KGW/Unigram/SynthID.
  • No human preference study; quality claims rest entirely on automated reward/PPL/distributional metrics.
  • 100-prompt evaluation is small; no per-prompt variance reported clearly in the visible text.

Relevance

与 LLM 可解释性与可靠性方向高度相关：SLAM 实质上是把"SAE 特征 + 因果激活操控"这一机理可解释性工具转化为水印这种可靠性应用，证明了结构性 SAE 方向在 2B → 9B 之间的可迁移性，对理解 SAE 如何编码句法结构以及 activation steering 的实用边界都有借鉴价值；其 PT→IT 转移代价的揭示也对训练稳定性与对齐微调如何影响残差流几何有提示意义。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do the mined structural directions transfer across model families (Llama, Qwen, Mistral) or only within Gemma-2? The recently-released Qwen-Scope SAEs are mentioned but not tested.
  • Can the DIPPER vulnerability be mitigated by mining paraphrase-resistant phenomena (e.g., relative clause constructions that require aggressive restructuring to evade)? The authors hint at this but provide no empirical evidence.
  • How does multi-bit extension via independent phenomena affect detection rate and quality — is the per-phenomenon signal independence claim load-bearing or fragile?
  • The PT→IT quality drop (~−4 ΔReward) is large. Does training matched IT-SAEs recover the gap, or does instruction tuning fundamentally distort the structural subspace?
  • Is the 2.3% FPR calibration stable across prompt domains outside the 5 evaluated, or is per-domain recalibration necessary in deployment?
  • How does SLAM compose with token-distribution methods in a defense-in-depth stack — additive TPR or interfering signals?
  • What does the structural-direction geometry look like cross-lingually — does English-mined passive voice generalize to typologically different languages, or is multilingual mining required?
  • Can adversaries, given white-box knowledge of the steering direction, construct counter-steering attacks (since the scheme is white-box to the defender)?
