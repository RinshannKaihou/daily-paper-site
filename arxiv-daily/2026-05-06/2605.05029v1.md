> **一句话总结：** 作者从理论上证明了"预测-因果鸿沟"不可避免：在任何包含线性编码器的模型类中，最小化预测目标的最优解必然偏离因果编码器；2695次神经网络训练实验进一步验证，平均因果保真度仅为0.49，高维下接近10⁻⁸，且增大模型容量只会加深对错误表征的承诺。

> **TL;DR:** A formal impossibility theorem plus large-scale experiments on 2695 neural network runs show that minimizing predictive (next-step prediction) objectives is structurally misaligned with recovering causal representations—the gap widens with dimensionality, cannot be closed by scaling capacity, and is only partially mitigated by grounding the loss to system observables.

## 核心贡献

- **不可能性定理（Theorem 1）**：证明对于任何包含所有单位范数线性编码器的模型类，均存在稳定的线性-高斯动力系统，其预测风险的最优解偏离因果（NZ）编码器，且这一现象在参数空间中是开集、正测度的，不是边界情况。
- **量化"预测-因果鸿沟"**：引入因果保真度（causal fidelity）指标，测量编码器敏感度中分配给系统自由度的比例；实测均值0.49，2.5%的配置超过0.70。
- **高维崩溃**：环境维度 N=100 时，最优编码器的因果保真度降至 ~10⁻⁸，而预测误差比因果编码器低92%——预测越好，越因果盲目。
- **非线性确认**：通过 Duffing-GRU 耦合系统的非线性实验，无约束预测器在55%的任务中产生环境主导表征，而有运算接地的预测器降至24%。
- **Scaling 含义**：指出神经语言模型的经验缩放规律（next-token loss 的幂律下降）度量的正是错误的量——更低的预测损失伴随的是更强的因果失准，而非更好的因果结构恢复。

## Key Contributions

- **Impossibility theorem (Theorem 1):** For any model class containing all linear encoders, there exist stable linear-Gaussian dynamics where every predictive-risk minimizer is strictly misaligned with the causal (NZ) encoder. The set of such dynamics is open and of positive measure in parameter space—a structural, not pathological, result.
- **Corollary 2 (capacity cannot fix the gap):** Enlarging the model class only tightens commitment to the non-causal encoder; no amount of capacity moves the optimum back to causal alignment.
- **Causal fidelity metric:** A scalar measure of the fraction of encoder sensitivity allocated to system degrees of freedom. Mean = 0.49 across 2695 runs; collapses to ~10⁻⁸ at N=100 environment dimensions.
- **Empirical validation at three scales:** 160-config linear sweep, 539-config (2695-run) nonlinear MLP sweep, and a Duffing–GRU nonlinear confirmation.
- **Grounding vs. full causal recovery:** Operational grounding (restricting the loss to system observables) partially suppresses environment dominance (55% → 24%) but cannot recover full causal fidelity without an explicit system-environment boundary in the architecture.
- **Implications for scaling paradigm:** Empirical scaling laws quantify predictive risk, not causal alignment. Scaling sharpens commitment to the wrong representation.

## Methodology

The paper proceeds in three analytic tiers:

**1. Linear-Gaussian impossibility proof.**
A dynamical system is modeled as $x_{t+1} = Ax_t + \xi_t$ where $x_t = (s_t, e_t)$ partitions into system ($s$) and environment ($e$) variables. A unit-norm linear encoder $\phi_w(x) = w^\top x$ achieves a closed-form predictive MSE $R(w) = w^\top \Sigma w - (w^\top A \Sigma w)^2 / w^\top \Sigma w$. The natural (causal) encoder $w_\text{NZ} = (1, 0)^\top$ is the risk minimizer only when system and environment are decoupled. For any off-diagonal coupling (positive or negative), the risk minimizer tilts toward the environment, and Theorem 1 proves this gap is open-set and positive-measure. Corollary 2 shows that nesting larger model classes only lowers the minimum risk to a value achievable by environment-dominant encoders.

**2. Large-scale MLP sweep.**
539 configurations of linear-Gaussian dynamics are constructed by sweeping system eigenvalue $a_s$, environment eigenvalue $a_e$, off-diagonal coupling $c$, and noise ratio $\epsilon$. For each configuration, a two-layer MLP (64 hidden units, ReLU) is trained for 2000 epochs on 5000 trajectories with 5 random seeds, totaling 2695 training runs. Causal fidelity is measured as $|\partial\phi/\partial s| / (|\partial\phi/\partial s| + |\partial\phi/\partial e|)$ via finite differences on held-out data. Scaling to high-dimensional environments (N=10, 50, 100) is analyzed by optimizing the linear encoder analytically over 12 configurations.

**3. Nonlinear GRU confirmation.**
A Duffing oscillator ($\dot{s} = -\alpha_s s - \beta_s s^3 + \gamma_{SE} e + \sigma_s \xi_s$) is coupled to a hidden Ornstein–Uhlenbeck environment. Single-layer GRU predictors (32 hidden units) are trained under two conditions: (a) unconstrained full-state prediction and (b) grounded prediction (loss on system observables only). Environment dominance is assessed by comparing hidden-state correlation ratios $\max_j |r(h_j, e)| / \max_j |r(h_j, s)|$. Out-of-distribution robustness is tested by shifting environment parameters $(\alpha_e \to 3\alpha_e, \sigma_e \to 2\sigma_e)$ and measuring MSE inflation.

## Results & Highlights

- **Causal fidelity distribution:** Mean 0.49 (median 0.48, SD 0.10) across 2695 MLP runs. Only 0.4% of configurations exceed 0.90, and only 2.5% exceed 0.70. The NZ encoder is optimal in only the 40 measure-zero diagonal cases (25% of the linear sweep, but 0% of configurations with any off-diagonal coupling).
- **Prediction vs. causality inversion:** The optimal NN encoder achieves a mean prediction risk 99.3% lower than the optimal linear encoder—substantially more predictive—yet with mean causal fidelity 0.49. More predictive = more causally misaligned.
- **High-dimensional collapse:** At N=100, the predictive-causal gap grows from 0.60 (N=10) to 1.17 (N=100, 92.1% prediction improvement), while causal fidelity collapses from ~3×10⁻⁸ to ~2×10⁻⁸. In the thermodynamic limit, predictive learning converges to a representation orthogonal to the system.
- **GRU experiment:** Unconstrained training yields environment-dominant representations in 55% of tasks (95% CI 41–68%). Grounding reduces this to 24% (15–38%), Fisher exact $p = 2.3 \times 10^{-3}$. However, the residual 24% persists because environment variables carry indirect predictive information about future system states via coupling $\gamma_{SE}$.
- **OOD fragility:** Under environment distribution shift, unconstrained models suffer a median 1.82× MSE inflation (IQR 1.66–1.89), while grounded models remain at ~1.00× (IQR 0.97–1.08).
- **Information bottleneck does not rescue causal recovery:** Sweeping the regularization parameter $\beta \in [10^{-4}, 1]$ shifts the optimal encoder angle smoothly without reaching the NZ axis on any off-diagonal configuration.

The affiliation is primarily materials science rather than machine learning or theoretical statistics, which is atypical for a paper making strong theoretical claims about representation learning and neural network training dynamics.

The paper is single-authored, which limits the breadth of expert vetting. The theoretical proof (Theorem 1) is constructive and the closed-form derivation is verifiable in principle, though the full proof and supplemental material are cited as reference [6] (not included in the main text). ## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The core insight—that predictive objectives can fail to recover causal structure—is known in causal representation learning. The contribution is to make this precise and quantitative via a formal impossibility theorem in the linear-Gaussian setting, extend it empirically to nonlinear neural networks at scale, and connect it to the scaling paradigm. The "causal fidelity" metric and the positive-measure characterization of the failure regime are concrete additions. The connection to scaling laws is provocative and under-explored in the literature.

**Rigor:** The impossibility theorem is stated cleanly and the proof strategy (constructive example, then generalization to positive-measure sets) is outlined clearly, though full proofs are deferred to supplemental material that is not available for review here. The empirical methodology is well-specified and the experimental design is careful (sweeping over parameter grids, multiple seeds, explicit OOD testing). However, the claim that this constitutes a "structural limit" is strong; the paper only demonstrates it for the specific predictive MSE objective and linear-Gaussian/Duffing-OU dynamics, not for arbitrary dynamics or loss functions.

**Reproducibility:** High within the stated framework. Configuration parameters, architecture details, training procedure, and evaluation metrics are fully specified. The linear-Gaussian setting is analytically tractable and can be independently verified.

**Significance:** The implications for world models, JEPA, and neural language model scaling are significant if the argument generalizes. The paper correctly identifies that grounding (restricting loss to system observables) is insufficient without an explicit architectural system-environment boundary—a non-trivial and actionable finding.

**Red flags:**
- Single-author paper from a materials science lab making broad claims about LLM scaling and self-supervised learning—the author's domain expertise relative to these claims is unclear.
- The full proof of Theorem 1 and the supplemental material are not available in the submitted version; the most critical technical content cannot be fully evaluated.
- The claim that "scaling laws quantify the wrong quantity" is stated as a direct consequence, but the paper's experiments are on toy dynamics (linear-Gaussian, Duffing-OU), not on language model training. The extrapolation to LLMs is inferential rather than empirically demonstrated.
- The paper does not engage with recent literature on causal representation learning, identifiability theory, or the broader literature on learning disentangled representations, which may already address some of these issues.

## Strengths

- Clear, falsifiable formal theorem with explicit counterexample construction and positive-measure characterization.
- Unusually thorough empirical validation for a theory paper: 2695 training runs across 539 configurations plus nonlinear confirmation.
- The finding that information bottleneck fails to rescue causal alignment is concretely demonstrated.
- Operationally grounded predictions substantially reduce environment dominance (55% → 24%) and OOD fragility, providing a constructive partial remedy.
- The OOD robustness experiment cleanly separates predictive optimality from distributional robustness.

## Limitations

- All experiments use toy dynamical systems (linear-Gaussian and Duffing-OU); no experiments on language, vision, or real-world data where the claims about scaling laws would be most consequential.
- The impossibility result applies to the latent self-prediction MSE objective. Whether and how it extends to masked autoencoders, contrastive objectives, or token-prediction cross-entropy is argued qualitatively but not proven.
- Grounding requires a known system-environment partition—a strong prior that is typically unavailable in practice.
- The "thermodynamic limit" argument ($N \to \infty$) is extrapolated from finite-N trends; it is not a proven limit result in the paper.
- Single authorship limits cross-checking of proofs and experiments.

## 相关性

本文直接关联LLM可解释性与可靠性的核心问题：若预测目标从根本上无法恢复因果结构，则基于next-token prediction的语言模型所学习到的内部表征的可靠性与因果可解释性存在结构性缺陷，且该问题无法通过扩大规模（scaling）解决，对理解LLM表征的本质及训练目标设计具有重要启示。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the impossibility theorem extend to token-level cross-entropy objectives (i.e., does the argument hold when the loss is computed on discrete tokens rather than continuous latent states)?
2. Can the positive-measure condition be empirically verified in large language model training by probing for environment-dominant directions in residual stream activations?
3. The paper proposes analytic constraints (Kramers-Kronig relations, Hardy-space residuals) as candidate regularizers. What is the computational cost of imposing these constraints at LLM scale?
4. Is the residual 24% environment dominance under grounded training reducible to zero with stronger architectural constraints (e.g., hard state-space partitioning in SSM architectures), or does it represent a lower bound due to indirect coupling?
5. How does the predictive-causal gap interact with in-context learning? If a model has learned an environment-dominant representation, can in-context examples push inference toward causal structure?
6. The paper focuses on dynamical systems; does an analogous impossibility result hold for static generative models (VAEs, diffusion models) where there is no temporal structure to exploit?
