> **一句话总结：** AxMoE 是首个系统研究"近似乘法器 × MoE DNN 拓扑"交互的工作，通过在 4 种网络（ResNet-20/VGG11_bn/VGG19_bn/ViT-Small）上评估 8 个 8 位有符号近似乘法器后发现：CNN 中 Dense 基线在无重训练时最稳健、Dense+L2J 始终位于帕累托前沿（节能 29.2%、精度损失<0.70 pp），而在 ViT-Small 上 Hard MoE (r=0.25) 在最激进近似 L2L 下以等同算力反超 Dense（75.97% vs 75.00%）。

> **TL;DR:** AxMoE is the first empirical study of how approximate 8-bit signed multipliers interact with three MoE routing topologies (Hard, Soft, Cluster) across four architectures (ResNet-20, VGG11_bn, VGG19_bn, ViT-Small). Findings: (1) without retraining the Dense baseline is most resilient on CNNs while ViT topologies degrade uniformly because self-attention projections are unconditionally approximated; (2) after 5-epoch approximate-aware retraining, ResNet-20 fully recovers across all multipliers, VGG architectures fail at the most aggressive ones (KVA, L2L); (3) Dense+L2J is Pareto-optimal across all CNNs (0.71x power, <0.70 pp loss); (4) Hard MoE (r=0.25) on ViT-Small is the only MoE configuration to strictly beat Dense at equal power.

## 核心贡献

- **首次研究 AxC × MoE 交叉点**：提出 AxMoE 框架，覆盖 Hard MoE / Soft MoE / Cluster MoE 三种路由范式与 4 种主干网络（包含 CNN 与 ViT），填补了近似计算与条件计算之间的空白。
- **统一功耗指标**：定义归一化功耗 P_norm，将不同 MoE 拓扑因 Effective MAC 差异（Soft MoE ~3x、Cluster MoE 1.31-3.96x）带来的开销与近似乘法器的逐操作节省统一比较。
- **关键经验发现**：在 CNN 上 Dense 拓扑最稳健且 Dense+L2J 总在 Pareto 前沿；ViT-Small 上 Hard MoE r=0.25 在 L2L 下精度反超 Dense（75.97% vs 75.00%），是研究中唯一"严格更高精度且严格更低功耗"的 MoE 配置。
- **重训练不对称性的发掘**：5 epoch 近似感知重训练在 ResNet-20 全恢复，但在 VGG 上 KVA/L2L 不可恢复；VGG19_bn 的 Hard MoE 出现非单调失效（KVA 崩溃 2%，更激进的 L2L 反而恢复 57.66%）。
- **拓扑级误差传播分析**：阐明 Hard MoE 仅近似激活专家、Soft MoE 累积全部专家误差、ViT 中自注意力 QKV 投影占 ~1/3 MAC 被无条件近似的结构性原因。

## Key Contributions

- First systematic characterization of how approximate multiplication interacts with MoE routing — a previously unexplored intersection between two efficiency paradigms.
- Side-by-side evaluation of three MoE variants (Hard/Soft/Cluster) on four architectures using eight Pareto-optimal 8-bit signed multipliers from EvoApproxLib, both with and without approximate-aware retraining.
- Introduction of a normalized-power metric (P_norm) that fairly compares dynamic-MAC topologies under heterogeneous arithmetic precision.
- Identification of architecture-dependent recovery gaps: full recovery on ResNet-20 vs. catastrophic, training-budget-independent failures on VGG with KVA/L2L multipliers.
- Demonstration that on ViT-Small, Hard MoE r=0.25 strictly Pareto-dominates Dense at the most aggressive approximation point — the sole MoE configuration in the study to do so.

## Methodology

The authors substitute targeted layers in each baseline with MoE blocks: convolutional layers in residual paths (ResNet-20), deep Conv2d layers (VGG), and FFN sub-blocks of selected transformer layers at fractions r in {0.25, 0.5} (ViT-Small). Approximate multiplication is applied only to layer types that account for >97% of effective MACs (Conv2d for CNNs, all linear layers within transformer blocks except the classifier head for ViT-Small); routing gates and BatchNorm always use exact arithmetic.

The framework uses LUT-based emulation of approximate multipliers: TFApprox-style GPU LUT engine for CNNs and TransAxx for ViT-Small. Eight multipliers from EvoApproxLib are evaluated, ranging from exact (mul8s_1KV6) to extreme (mul8s_1L2L: 52.9% per-op power saving, 93.16% error probability). Approximate-aware retraining runs for 5 epochs with SGD (lr=0.1, weight decay 5e-4, batch size 128); router parameters are frozen so only expert weights adapt to the approximate hardware.

A normalized power formula P_norm = (M_eff / M_base) * [f_apx * (P_apx / P_KV6) + (1 - f_apx)] enables cross-topology comparison, where M_base is Dense+KV6 and f_apx is the fraction of MACs that are approximated.

## Results & Highlights

- **Without retraining**: Dense is most resilient on CNNs (e.g., ResNet-20 retains 59.1% at L2J vs. Hard MoE 12.6%, Cluster MoE 9.4%). On ViT-Small all topologies degrade at near-identical rates because the unconditionally approximated MSA QKV/output projections account for ~1/3 of effective MACs. A Soft MoE anomaly at KVM retains near-baseline accuracy across all three CNNs.
- **After retraining (5 epochs)**: ResNet-20 fully recovers for all 4 topologies x 8 multipliers (e.g., Dense+L2J: 68.31%, only 0.27 pp loss). VGG architectures recover for moderate multipliers but fail irreversibly at KVA/L2L for all topologies except Cluster MoE on VGG11_bn (52.0% at KVA, 42.5% at L2L). VGG19_bn Hard MoE shows non-monotonic behavior: 2% at KVA but 57.66% at the more aggressive L2L.
- **ViT-Small after retraining**: Hard r=0.5 has the flattest profile (only 1.74 pp loss across the full multiplier range). Hard MoE r=0.25 is the only configuration to strictly beat Dense at equal normalized power (75.97% vs. 75.00% at L2L, 0.485x power).
- **Pareto frontier**: Across CNNs, Dense+L2J is always on the frontier (0.71x power, <0.70 pp loss, 29.2% per-op savings). Cluster MoE is entirely Pareto-dominated in every experiment.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (moderate–high):** The intersection of approximate multipliers with MoE topologies is genuinely new and the exploration is honest about what is empirical vs. predicted. However, the constituent tools (EvoApproxLib, TFApprox, TransAxx, MoE substitution) are pre-existing — the contribution is the systematic combination, not new methodology.

**Rigor (mixed):** The 8-multiplier x 4-architecture x 4-topology sweep is comprehensive, but key choices weaken some conclusions: (1) only 5 epochs of approximate-aware retraining due to LUT cost — the authors argue this is sufficient because "catastrophic failures at KVA/L2L were not observed to resolve with additional epochs" but provide no ablation evidence supporting that claim; (2) only one Cluster MoE gateway design is tried, and the authors immediately call it Pareto-dominated — this is more a claim about that specific gateway than about cluster routing generally; (3) the MoE topologies are not benchmark-matched (different parameter counts, hyperparameter budgets), so accuracy comparisons partially conflate routing strategy with capacity differences.

**Reproducibility (moderate):** All datasets (CIFAR-100, Tiny ImageNet), libraries (EvoApproxLib, TFApprox, TransAxx, timm), and pretrained baselines (chenyaofo/pytorch-cifar-models) are standard and public. No code release is mentioned in the paper text, which is a significant omission for an empirical study with 96+ configurations.

**Significance (moderate):** Useful as a roadmap for hardware-software co-design and as a cautionary note that MoE+AxC is not free. The headline ViT-Small result (Hard MoE r=0.25 beats Dense at L2L) is a single configuration on a single dataset (Tiny ImageNet-200) — interesting but not strong enough to claim a generalizable principle.

**Red flags / caveats:**
- The "fapx" formula caps at 1.0, hiding the fact that Soft MoE has multiple expert MACs to approximate and the per-op savings calculation may understate Soft MoE's absolute power increase.
- The VGG19_bn non-monotonic Hard MoE result (2% at KVA, 57.66% at L2L) is reported but never explained beyond "different qualitative retraining conditions" — this looks like training instability or a metric artifact that deserves deeper investigation.
- All datasets are small (CIFAR-100, Tiny ImageNet); generalization to ImageNet-1k or production-scale MoE (e.g., real Switch Transformer scale) is not established.
- Bit-precision is fixed at 8-bit signed only; interactions with INT4/mixed-precision are not explored.

## Strengths

- Genuinely first study at this intersection — fills a real research gap.
- Honest treatment of when MoE helps vs. hurts under approximation, including admitting Cluster MoE's full Pareto-domination.
- Clear architectural reasoning for ViT's uniform degradation (MSA projections are unconditionally approximated regardless of MoE config).
- Good engineering of the normalized power metric to make heterogeneous topologies comparable.
- Useful negative results (e.g., catastrophic VGG failure at KVA/L2L) are reported rather than hidden.

## Limitations

- Small-scale datasets only; no large-scale ImageNet or language modeling.
- No code release mentioned, limiting independent verification of the 96-configuration sweep.
- Retraining budget is fixed at 5 epochs for cost reasons; the claim that more epochs would not help VGG+KVA/L2L is asserted but not evidenced.
- Cluster MoE is evaluated with a single gateway design; broader gateway architectures could change conclusions.
- Only one bit-width (8-bit signed) and one library (EvoApproxLib) are explored.
- Routing-gate decisions are frozen during retraining, which simplifies the analysis but precludes exploring co-adapted gating-under-approximation strategies.
- No statistical confidence (e.g., multiple seeds) is reported, important for the small accuracy margins claimed (e.g., 0.10–0.13 pp Hard vs. Dense advantages on ViT).

## Relevance

与你关注的 LLM 可解释性/可靠性/训练稳定性议题相关性中等：本文提出的"近似硬件下 MoE 拓扑差异化误差传播"与"近似感知重训练是否可恢复精度"两条经验线索，可类比迁移到 LLM 量化/低精度训练稳定性研究——尤其是 ViT 中 MSA QKV 投影无条件被近似的发现，对理解大型 MoE LLM（如 Mixtral、DeepSeek-MoE）在硬件量化下的鲁棒性边界有启示意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would unfreezing the routing gate during approximate-aware retraining allow the gate to learn to avoid error-prone experts, recovering VGG+KVA/L2L performance?
- What explains the VGG19_bn Hard MoE non-monotonic recovery (collapse at KVA but recovery at L2L)? Is it loss-landscape specific, optimizer-specific, or a measurement artifact?
- Does the ViT-Small Hard MoE r=0.25 advantage at L2L survive at ImageNet-1k scale, multiple seeds, and longer retraining?
- How would the conclusions change if approximate multipliers were also applied to MSA in a sparsity-aware manner (e.g., approximating only "low-importance" attention heads)?
- For Cluster MoE, would a non-routed shared-trunk gateway (rather than a full image-level CNN) eliminate its Pareto-dominated status?
- How do these findings compose with quantization (INT4, FP8) and sparsity, which are the more practical levers in modern LLM deployment?
