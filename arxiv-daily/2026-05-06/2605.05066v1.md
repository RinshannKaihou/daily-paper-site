> **一句话总结：** 本文通过信息论方法严格证明了长序列建模的"不可能三角"：任何序列模型无法同时满足常数步计算效率（效率）、固定大小状态（紧凑性）和随序列长度线性增长的历史回忆能力（召回），并将52种架构系统地归类到该三角框架中，定量上界为 $n^* \leq O(\text{poly}(d)/\log V)$。

> **TL;DR:** This paper formally proves that no causal sequence model can simultaneously achieve per-step computation independent of sequence length (Efficiency), fixed-size state (Compactness), and recall capacity proportional to sequence length (Recall). Using the Data Processing Inequality and Fano's Inequality, the authors derive an information-theoretic upper bound on recall capacity for any compact, efficient model and verify it empirically across 52 architectures.

## 核心贡献

- **不可能定理（Theorem 10）：** 严格证明效率（效率 $\mathfrak{E}$）、紧凑性（紧凑 $\mathfrak{C}$）、召回（召回 $\mathfrak{R}$）三者不能同时成立；任意满足前两者的模型最多只能可靠回忆 $O(\text{poly}(d)/\log V)$ 个键值对，独立于序列长度 $T$。
- **Online Sequence Processor（OSP）抽象：** 提出七元组统一框架，将Transformer、SSM、线性RNN及其混合体纳入同一形式化体系，给出效率、紧凑性、召回三个属性的精确定义。
- **52种架构系统分类（Table 4）：** 将截至2026年3月发布的52种架构逐一归类到不可能三角的三个顶点和内部区域，验证每种架构至多满足其中两条性质。
- **定量权衡不等式（ECR曲面）：** 定义归一化ECR剖面 $(e, c, r)$，导出连续权衡不等式 $r \leq cb/[(1-\varepsilon) - 1/\log_2 V]$，将布尔型不可能推广为连续权衡面。
- **合成实验验证：** 在联想回忆基准（AR任务）上对5种代表性架构的实验证实：所有架构的实证召回均严格低于信息论上界，混合架构随注意力比例连续轨迹穿越三角内部。

## Key Contributions

- **Formal impossibility theorem (Theorem 10):** Proves that Efficiency ($\mathfrak{E}$), Compactness ($\mathfrak{C}$), and Recall ($\mathfrak{R}$) form a mutually exclusive triad; any model satisfying $\mathfrak{E}$ and $\mathfrak{C}$ can recall at most $n^* \leq q(d)/[(1-\varepsilon)\log_2 V - 1]$ key-value pairs regardless of sequence length.
- **Online Sequence Processor (OSP) abstraction:** Introduces a seven-tuple formalism unifying Transformers, SSMs, linear RNNs, and hybrids, with rigorous definitions of all three properties and only two mild axioms (causality and numerical stability via L-Lipschitz transitions).
- **Taxonomy of 52 architectures:** Systematically classifies every major long-sequence architecture published before March 2026 into the triangle's regions, providing formal proofs of which properties each satisfies and violates.
- **Quantitative ECR trade-off surface:** Derives a continuous trade-off inequality from the impossibility bound, enabling comparison of architectures not just by region but by their normalized (efficiency loss, compactness loss, recall ratio) coordinates.
- **Empirical validation on synthetic associative recall (AR) task:** Five experiments with Transformer, Linear Transformer, Mamba, GLA, and Hybrid confirm: empirical $n^*$ lies strictly below the bound; fixed-state models show $r \to 0$ as $T$ grows; and hybrid architectures interpolate smoothly inside the triangle.

## Methodology

The paper introduces the **Online Sequence Processor (OSP)** abstraction: a causal system $\mathcal{P} = (\mathcal{S}, \mathcal{X}, \mathcal{Q}, \mathcal{A}, \delta, \rho, s_0)$ with state transition $s_t = \delta(s_{t-1}, x_t)$ and readout $\rho(s_T, q)$. Two axioms constrain the class: causality (state depends only on past) and numerical stability (L-Lipschitz transition with $L$ polynomial in $d$). Efficiency ($\mathfrak{E}$) requires per-step FLOPs bounded by $p(d)$ independent of $T$; Compactness ($\mathfrak{C}$) requires state size $|s_t|_\text{bits} \leq q(d)$ independent of $T$; Strong Recall ($\mathfrak{R}$) requires recalling $\gamma T$ key-value pairs at accuracy $\geq 1 - \varepsilon$ for all large $T$.

The **proof of Theorem 10** chains two classical information-theoretic inequalities. Step 1 uses the Data Processing Inequality to bound mutual information $I(\mathbf{v}; s_T) \leq |s_T|_\text{bits} \leq q(d)$. Step 2 uses Fano's Inequality to establish a lower bound $I(\mathbf{v}; s_T) \geq n[(1-\varepsilon)\log_2 V - 1]$ whenever $n$ pairs are recallable at accuracy $1-\varepsilon$. Combining yields $n^* \leq q(d)/[(1-\varepsilon)\log_2 V - 1]$, which is $O(\text{poly}(d)/\log V)$ and independent of $T$. The result is extended to continuous-state systems via a Lipschitz capacity bound (Theorem 14), showing that stable dynamics ($L \leq 1$) do not allow the effective state capacity to grow with $T$.

The **unified recurrence** $S_t = G_t \odot S_{t-1} + U_t(k_t, v_t, S_{t-1})$ covers all fixed-state architectures (SSMs, linear RNNs, gated linear attention), and Section 5 applies the impossibility theorem uniformly to show the recall ceiling is $n^* \leq |S_t|_\text{bits}/[(1-\varepsilon)\log_2 V - 1]$ regardless of gating strategy. The **ECR profile** $(e, c, r)$ provides a normalized coordinate system to place each architecture on the continuous trade-off surface.

Experiments use the synthetic associative recall task $\text{AR}(n, V)$ with $V=32$, model dimension $d=64$, two layers, 32-bit precision, training one model per $(n, T)$ configuration to measure $n^*$ (maximum $n$ with accuracy $\geq 0.90$).

## Results & Highlights

- All five experimental architectures (Transformer, Linear Transformer, Mamba N=16, Mamba N=64, GLA, Hybrid) satisfy the theorem: every empirical $(|s|_\text{bits}, n^*)$ point lies strictly below the theoretical bound curve (Figure 2b, Figure 5).
- At $T=32$: Transformer achieves $n^*=10$ (perfect recall up to tested limit), GLA achieves $n^*=9$, Linear Transformer $n^*=3$, both Mamba variants $n^*=1$.
- At $T=64$ (Table 5): Transformer and Hybrid($r_\text{attn}=0.5$) achieve $n^*=16$ ($r=0.25$); GLA achieves $n^*=4$ ($r=0.063$); Mamba achieves $n^*=1$ ($r=0.016$).
- Scaling experiment (Figure 3) confirms the $\mathfrak{E}$/$\mathfrak{C}$ dichotomy cleanly: Transformer FLOPs and state grow with $T$; SSM/GLA costs remain flat; Transformer recall ratio $r$ stays stable ($\approx 0.20$-$0.25$) while fixed-state models show $r \to 0$.
- Hybrid interpolation (Figure 4): adding one attention layer ($r_\text{attn}=0.25$) raises $n^*$ from 1 (pure Mamba) to 7; $r_\text{attn}=0.5$ saturates at $n^*=10$. Both state size and FLOPs increase monotonically with $r_\text{attn}$, directly quantifying the cost of improved recall.
- Mean bound utilization across all architectures is below 0.1% (GLA highest at ~0.04%), indicating that practical models use state for far more than key-value storage, leaving a large gap between the information-theoretic ceiling and observed performance.
- Remark 19 explicitly corrects the Titans/TTT marketing claim of escaping the triangle: their fixed-size neural memory obeys the same information-theoretic ceiling as any other $\mathfrak{E} \wedge \mathfrak{C}$ architecture.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The conceptual framing as an "impossibility triangle" is original and valuable — the analogy to the CAP theorem is apt and clarifying. The formal unification of 52 architectures under the OSP abstraction is a genuine contribution. However, the core mathematical content is not deep: the proof follows directly from standard information-theoretic inequalities in about two pages. The result that bounded-state models cannot achieve unbounded recall has been empirically understood and partially formalized before (e.g., Arora et al. 2024, Dao and Gu 2024). The main novelty is the completeness of the formal framework and the tripartite structuring.

**Rigor:** The theorem statements and proofs are mathematically sound and self-contained. The Lipschitz stability axiom (Axiom 3) is realistic for finite-precision implementations. One potential concern is that the bound is worst-case (uniform random key-value pairs), and natural language has structure that may allow higher effective recall than the theorem suggests — the paper acknowledges this limitation.

**Reproducibility:** Experiments are small-scale ($d=64$, $T \leq 64$, $V=32$) on a synthetic task. Full training details are deferred to Appendix C. The experimental results are consistent with the theory, but the very small scale makes it unclear how tightly the bound is approached in production models. Mean bound utilization below 0.1% is a notable finding but also suggests the bound is very loose in practice, reducing its operational utility for guiding architecture choice at scale.

**Significance:** The framework provides a principled "design compass" for practitioners and a common language for comparing architectures. The classification of 52 architectures is a useful reference. The impossibility result, while not surprising to practitioners, now has formal grounding, which is valuable.

**Red flags:** Single-author paper; small-scale experiments may not generalize; the bound is loose enough (models use <0.1% of theoretical capacity) that it may not guide practical design decisions precisely; the paper is submitted to JMLR but the title page shows "Submitted: Published" without a clear publication date, suggesting this is a preprint submitted to arXiv simultaneously with or shortly after JMLR submission.

## Strengths

- Clean, self-contained formal framework that unifies a large literature under one abstraction.
- Proof technique is elementary and therefore robust — no exotic assumptions.
- Comprehensive taxonomy of 52 architectures with per-architecture analysis in the appendix.
- Achievability results (Theorem 16) confirm the impossibility is tight: every pair of properties is constructively attainable.
- Explicit connection to classical impossibility theorems (CAP, FLP, Arrow) aids intuition.
- Hybrid architecture interpolation experiment (Experiment 4) provides an actionable insight: the attention ratio $r_\text{attn}$ is a direct knob for trading compactness/efficiency for recall.
- Explicitly corrects the overstated claims about Titans/TTT escaping the triangle (Remark 19).

## Limitations

- Experiments are limited to very small scale ($d=64$, 2 layers, $T \leq 64$, $V=32$); empirical tightness of the bound at production scale is unknown.
- The associative recall task uses worst-case uniform random key-value pairs; natural language's structure and redundancy could allow higher practical recall than the bound implies.
- The ECR bound is loose by a factor of >1000x in practice (models use <0.1% of theoretical capacity), which limits the bound's quantitative utility for architecture selection.
- The OSP abstraction covers only single-sequence, single-turn causal models; multi-modal and multi-turn settings are out of scope.
- Classification relies on asymptotic analysis; constant factors — which dominate at practical $T$ values (e.g., $T=8192$ in current LLMs) — are not captured by the $\mathfrak{E}$ and $\mathfrak{C}$ definitions.
- The paper does not address whether distribution-dependent bounds (for natural language inputs) could narrow the gap between theory and practice.

## Relevance

本文对LLM可解释性与可靠性研究高度相关：其"不可能三角"定理从信息论角度严格揭示了固定状态模型（如Mamba、RWKV等）在长上下文中历史信息检索能力的根本上限，为理解这类模型在长文档理解、多跳推理等场景中的失败行为提供了理论解释，并为训练时的架构选择（是否引入注意力层、引入多少）提供了定量指导。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How tight is the information-theoretic bound for natural language inputs (structured distributions) compared to the worst-case uniform random key-value pairs used in the AR task?
2. Can the ECR trade-off surface be characterized at production scale (e.g., $d=4096$, $T=128K$) — do real architectures cluster near the Pareto frontier or remain far below it?
3. Is there a computationally-grounded version of this impossibility (e.g., via cell-probe lower bounds or SETH-conditional hardness) that constrains architectures with sufficient state but insufficient per-step computation?
4. Does the Lipschitz stability axiom (Axiom 3) rule out any practically deployed model, or is it purely technical? Are there architectures with $L > 1$ that achieve higher recall before becoming numerically unstable?
5. How does the impossibility triangle interact with training dynamics — specifically, do fixed-state models trained on longer sequences learn to allocate their bounded state capacity more efficiently, and does this shift the empirical utilization rate upward?
6. Can the OSP framework be extended to encoder-decoder models or multi-turn dialogue systems where the "sequence" grows across turns?
