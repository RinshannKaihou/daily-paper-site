> **一句话总结：** 通过计算每个注意力头的注意力分布与均匀分布之间的 KL 散度，并用 L1 正则化的逻辑回归探针聚合，作者构建了一个单次前向传播、轻量级的白盒幻觉检测器，在 TruthfulQA/TriviaQA 等数据集上 AUROC 达到 0.78–0.91，竞争性地超越 SelfCheckGPT、语义熵等需多次采样的基线。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes a single-pass, white-box hallucination detector that computes the KL divergence between each attention head's distribution and a uniform reference, then aggregates these per-head divergences with an L1-regularized logistic regression probe. Across Llama-3.2-3B, Qwen3-4B, and Mistral-7B on TruthfulQA, TriviaQA, HotpotQA, and GSM8K, the method reaches AUROC 0.78–0.91, beats SelfCheckGPT/semantic entropy/perplexity baselines on HotpotQA-Mistral, and locates the signal in middle layers and on factual tokens (named entities, numbers).

## 核心贡献

- **轻量级单次前向白盒不确定性度量**：用 KL(注意力分布 ‖ 均匀分布) 作为每个注意力头的标量特征，无需重复采样、无需外部验证器，仅依赖一次生成过程中的注意力矩阵。
- **跨数据集和模型族的广泛验证**：在 TruthfulQA、TriviaQA、HotpotQA、GSM8K 四个任务上，对 Llama-3.2-3B、Qwen3-4B、Mistral-7B 三个指令调优模型评估，AUROC 普遍 ≥ 0.78。
- **超越多种基线**：在 HotpotQA / Mistral-7B 配置下，AUROC 0.78 优于 TOHA (0.71)、SelfCheckGPT (0.70)、语义熵 (0.70)、感知 (Perplexity) (0.55) 等。
- **信号的可解释定位**：通过层 × 头分析与消融实验，发现信号集中在中间层、并在命名实体和数字等事实性 token 上达到峰值，而非分布在某个"幻觉头"上。
- **异常值/极值驱动**：通过 ECDF 和 99 百分位分析，证明区分度主要来自分布的右尾——错误生成更可能出现极端高 KL 散度。

## Key Contributions

- A single-pass, white-box uncertainty signal: per-head KL divergence between attention distribution and a uniform reference, aggregated by an L1-regularized logistic regression probe.
- Empirical evaluation across 4 datasets (TruthfulQA, TriviaQA, HotpotQA, GSM8K) and 3 instruction-tuned model families (Llama-3.2-3B, Qwen3-4B, Mistral-7B).
- Competitive or superior AUROC versus output-based baselines (SelfCheckGPT, semantic entropy, perplexity, max entropy) and against attention-based predecessors (TOHA, Lookback Lens, Attention-Pooling Probes), while needing only one forward pass.
- Localization analysis: divergence signal is concentrated in middle layers and at factually-loaded tokens (named entities, numerical values), with a small fraction (~0.5%) of heads carrying disproportionate weight, but no single "hallucination head" generalizes across all model families.
- Sanity checks (generation length, prompt length, punctuation, digit count, label permutation) rule out trivial confounds.

## Methodology

The author treats each attention head's per-step distribution over the previously generated context tokens as a probability distribution, and measures how far it is from the maximum-entropy uniform baseline U using KL divergence: D_KL(A || U) = log T - H(A), where T is the context length. The intuition is that concentrated attention signals confident retrieval of informative tokens, whereas diffuse near-uniform attention indicates that the model "doesn't know where to look."

For each generated answer, per-head KL divergences are averaged across the answer tokens, producing a feature vector x_i in R^{L*H} (L layers times H heads). A logistic regression probe with L1 (lasso) regularization is trained to predict answer correctness y_i ∈ {0,1}: P(y=1|x) = σ(w^T x + b), with sparsity controlled by λ.

Evaluation uses AUROC as the primary metric (insensitive to class imbalance), Expected Calibration Error (ECE), and accuracy, computed via stratified k-fold cross-validation over 3 random seeds. Correctness labels come from string matching against gold answers (TruthfulQA/TriviaQA/HotpotQA) or final-numeric-answer extraction (GSM8K).

Ablations remove the top-k heads (5/10/20/50), early/middle/late layer groups, and replace mean pooling with max pooling. A separate analysis splits the feature computation into prompt-only, answer-only, and full-sequence variants.

## Results & Highlights

- AUROC on TruthfulQA: 0.906 (Llama-3.2-3B), 0.906 (Qwen3-4B), 0.891 (Mistral-7B).
- AUROC on TriviaQA: 0.835 / 0.846 / 0.835 across the three models.
- AUROC on HotpotQA: 0.804 / 0.796 / 0.778; on GSM8K: 0.767 / 0.945 / 0.789.
- On HotpotQA / Mistral-7B, the method (0.78 ± 0.02) beats TOHA (0.71), SelfCheckGPT (0.70), semantic entropy (0.70), EigenScore (0.68), HaloScope (0.60), perplexity (0.55), max entropy (0.62), and ReDEEP (0.49).
- Compared to Binkowski et al. (2025) on Llama-3.2-3B: ties LapEigvals on TriviaQA (0.835 vs 0.836) and beats Hidden-States probe on TruthfulQA (0.906 vs 0.823).
- ECE is "moderate" on TruthfulQA, low on TriviaQA, and 0.04–0.06 on GSM8K (well-calibrated despite variable AUROC).
- Removing middle layers causes the largest AUROC drop (0.858 → 0.844); replacing mean pooling with max pooling drops AUROC to 0.795, suggesting the signal is diffuse rather than spike-driven.
- Prompt-only feature already gives AUROC 0.7674; answer-only gives 0.8707 — implying a portion of the uncertainty is "preordained" by the prompt.
- 73% of words in the 99th-percentile divergence tail are named entities; numerical tokens, although infrequent, also exhibit very high divergence.

No co-author or supervisor is listed, and there is no acknowledged lab affiliation. ## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (moderate).** The core idea — using attention concentration as an uncertainty signal — is not new. Lookback Lens (Chuang et al., 2024), Attention-Pooling Probes (CH-Wang et al., 2024), TOHA (Bazarova et al., 2025), UQAC (Li et al., 2025), and Vazhentsev et al. (2025) all extract uncertainty from attention. The paper's specific contribution is the choice of KL-vs-uniform as the per-head feature plus an L1 probe, which is a sensible-but-modest delta over existing entropy-of-attention or ratio-based features. The author acknowledges these predecessors but does not run a head-to-head ablation against, e.g., raw attention entropy without the uniform-reference framing — making it hard to attribute gains specifically to the KL formulation.

**Rigor (mixed).** The protocol (3 seeds, 5-fold CV, ECE alongside AUROC, sanity checks via permutation and superficial features) is adequate for a workshop/preprint. However: (1) only one model size per family (3B–7B), no large-scale validation; (2) the GSM8K result for Qwen3-4B (AUROC 0.945) is suspiciously much higher than for Llama (0.767) and Mistral (0.789), which is left largely unexplained; (3) the head-overlap analysis admits that "no single head generalizes across all three models," which directly undermines the narrative of a structurally meaningful signal — the probe may be learning model-specific shortcuts; (4) the comparison to Binkowski et al. (2025) is on a different setting than the headline results, and the comparison to Bazarova et al. (2025) reuses their numbers without a controlled re-run.

**Reproducibility (uncertain).** No code or appendix link is visible in the main text shown. Datasets and models are public, the method is simple enough to re-implement, and exact hyperparameters (λ for lasso) are not stated in the visible portion. Reproducibility is plausible but not explicitly demonstrated.

**Significance (modest).** If the result holds, a single-forward-pass white-box detector that beats sampling-based methods is practically useful. But the gain over existing attention-based detectors is single-digit AUROC points, and the claim that "internal attention contains epistemic uncertainty signals" is already established literature. The paper consolidates rather than discovers.

**Red flags.** (a) Single-author student work without acknowledged supervisor on the title page. (b) No statistical significance testing between the proposed method and the closest attention-based baselines (TOHA, LapEigvals); reported standard deviations sometimes overlap with baselines. (c) The "no shared head across models" finding is presented as positive ("signal is distributed") but could equally be read as "the probe is overfitting to per-model idiosyncrasies." (d) No analysis of whether the prompt-only AUROC of 0.767 is itself capturing prompt difficulty rather than model-internal uncertainty — a possible confound the sanity checks do not directly address.

## Strengths

- Clean, simple, computationally cheap method (one forward pass, lasso probe over scalars).
- Broad coverage of tasks (factual QA, multi-hop, math) and models (3 families).
- Multiple sanity checks (length, punctuation, digit count, label permutation) explicitly rule out the most obvious confounds.
- Layer/head/percentile analyses are thoughtful and connect the empirical signal to interpretability literature (Ferrando et al., Ogasa & Arase).
- ECE reported alongside AUROC — calibration is not ignored.

## Limitations

- Single author / student work, no peer-reviewed venue indicated in the visible text.
- Limited model scale (≤7B); behaviour on 70B+ or RLHF'd frontier models is untested.
- Probe is supervised on labeled correctness data — not a fully unsupervised hallucination detector.
- The "answer-only AUROC 0.87" vs "prompt-only AUROC 0.77" gap is small enough to raise the question of whether the method is mostly capturing prompt difficulty.
- Comparison numbers for some baselines are imported from other papers rather than re-run under identical conditions.
- No theoretical justification for why uniform is the right reference distribution (vs, e.g., empirical positional prior).

## Relevance

与你关注的 LLM 可解释性、可靠性方向高度相关：本文从注意力矩阵抽取 epistemic uncertainty 信号，本质上是用白盒探针定位"模型在哪里不知道"，并发现信号集中在中间层与事实 token——这与 mechanistic interpretability 中 entity-binding/factual recall 的研究路径一致，可作为幻觉检测和模型置信度校准方面的参考工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the method scale to 70B+ models and reasoning models (o1-style)? Does middle-layer concentration persist?
- Would replacing the uniform reference with a learned positional prior (or the empirical mean attention distribution over a corpus) improve the signal-to-noise ratio?
- Can the per-head KL features be combined with hidden-state probes (Orgad et al., Binkowski et al.) for ensemble gains, or are they redundant?
- Why does Qwen3-4B achieve AUROC 0.945 on GSM8K while Llama-3.2-3B gets 0.767? Is this an architectural property, a training-data effect, or a probe-overfitting artifact?
- Is the method robust to RLHF'd / sycophantic models that are explicitly trained to look confident? The current evaluation uses base-instruction-tuned checkpoints.
- Could the signal be used as a token-level rather than answer-level detector to drive selective generation or retrieval-on-uncertainty?
- Does prompt-only AUROC of 0.77 reflect prompt difficulty (an aleatoric/dataset confound) rather than internal model uncertainty? A controlled study where the same prompt is answered correctly vs incorrectly by different models would help.
