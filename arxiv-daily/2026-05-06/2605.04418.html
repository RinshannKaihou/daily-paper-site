Demystifying Manifold Constraints in LLM Pre-training

arXiv: 2605.04418v1

Date: 2026-05-06

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04418v1

一句话总结： 本文提出 MACRO（基于矩阵符号算子对齐的约束黎曼优化器），通过单循环、可证明收敛的优化框架系统地揭示了流形约束（Frobenius/Spectral 球）在 LLM 预训练中能够独立地约束前向激活尺度并强制稳定旋转平衡，从而完全替代 RMSNorm 与解耦权重衰减等启发式机制。
TL;DR: This paper introduces MACRO, a provably-convergent single-loop Riemannian optimizer that disentangles the role of explicit manifold constraints in LLM pre-training, showing that Frobenius/Spectral sphere constraints can independently bound forward activations and enforce rotational equilibrium—thereby subsuming the functions of RMSNorm and decoupled weight decay.

核心贡献

  • 提出 MACRO (Msign-Aligned Constrained Riemannian Optimizer)：一个单循环、可证明收敛的黎曼优化器，通过切空间投影、Riemannian 谱最速下降、更新-权重比对齐与流形回拉四步构成，达到 O(T^{-1/4}) 的非凸收敛率。
  • 理论辨析不同流形约束的作用：证明 spectral sphere 约束最坏情况激活尺度 (||W||₂)，而 Frobenius sphere 控制平均情形 (Σ_X 加权 trace 形式)，并推导出实用半径 R_spec = r√(D_out/D_in)、R_fro = r√D_out。
  • 与归一化层的相互作用：在完全移除可学习 RMSNorm 的 330M Qwen3-like 架构上，Muon 在标准学习率下 NaN 发散，而 MACRO-spec 仍稳定收敛，证实流形约束可替代归一化层。
  • 与权重衰减的相互作用：通过几何更新规则严格锁定相对学习率 η_rel,t = cη_t，并从首步即施加稳态旋转角 θ_t ≈ cη_t（Frobenius）或 cη_t·R/(R-σ₂) 自适应（Spectral），形式化地替代了启发式权重衰减的隐式正则化作用。
  • 大规模实证验证：在 120M、330M、1B Qwen3-like 模型上，MACRO-spec 取得 2.461 (1B 验证集) 等最优损失，相比 Muon 基线持续优胜，同时避免 SSO/FSO 等双循环算法的高计算成本。

Key Contributions

  • MACRO algorithm: A single-loop, provably-convergent Riemannian optimizer combining tangent-space projection, Riemannian spectral steepest descent (via msign LMO), update–weight ratio alignment, and manifold retraction; achieves O(√(LΔ)·σT^{-1/4}) convergence under standard non-convex assumptions.
  • Theoretical decoupling of manifold constraint roles: Establishes that the spectral sphere controls the worst-case forward activation amplification while the Frobenius sphere controls the average case, and derives practical radii R_spec = r√(D_out/D_in) and R_fro = r√D_out via random matrix theory.
  • Constraints replace RMSNorm: Empirically demonstrates that with all learnable RMSNorm γ removed, Muon diverges (NaN) at LR=3e-2 on a 330M model, while MACRO-spec attains 2.739 validation loss—within 0.025 of the fully-normalized baseline.
  • Constraints replace decoupled weight decay: Shows the manifold geometry locks the relative learning rate to η_rel,t = cη_t and the inter-step rotation angle θ_t to a closed-form expression from the first iteration, bypassing the transient phase that standard weight decay requires.
  • Scaling-law evaluation: Validates the framework on 120M / 330M / 1B Qwen3-like models with SwiGLU + GQA + RoPE; MACRO-spec achieves the best validation loss (2.461 at 1B) versus Muon (2.473) and MuonH variants.

Methodology

The paper formalizes the constrained pre-training problem min_W L(W) s.t. W ∈ M for four manifold choices: Frobenius sphere M_F(R), spectral sphere M_S(R), and input/output Oblique manifolds. The MACRO update is:

O_t = msign(∇_M L(W_t)), W_{t+1} = P_M(W_t − η_t · cR · O_t / (||O_t||_M + ε))

Each step performs (i) momentum on the Euclidean gradient, (ii) projection to the tangent space T_M W_t, (iii) the matrix-sign LMO to extract the steepest descent direction with respect to the local manifold geometry, (iv) normalization scaling by cR, and (v) retraction back onto M. Convergence is proved under compact C^3 manifold and L-smoothness/bounded-variance assumptions, yielding the standard non-convex rate.

The training-dynamics analysis treats forward propagation as Y = XW^T over T tokens. Using Kronecker expansion ||W ⊗ I_T||₂ = ||W||₂, the spectral norm directly bounds activation amplification (worst case), whereas the Frobenius norm controls the trace-weighted form (1/D_out)·tr(Σ_X W^T W) which corresponds to the average case under empirical input covariance. Random matrix theory gives the asymptotic ratio ||W||_F/||W||₂ → √D_out/(1+√(D_out/D_in)), motivating the dimension-aware radii.

For the normalization-layer interplay, learnable RMSNorm γ and the constraint radius R both control the same activation scale, so they are functionally redundant; the authors remove γ entirely (replacing with parameter-free RMSNorm and a Norm-Gated SwiGLU variant Y = (Swish(XW₁^T)/||Swish(XW₁^T)||_RMS) ⊙ XW₂^T) and verify MACRO remains stable. For weight-decay interplay, the algebra η_rel,t = ||ΔW_t||_M/||W_t||_M = cη_t is derived directly. The rotation angle is derived in two regimes: under the Frobenius sphere a Taylor expansion of cosθ_t yields the simple identity θ_t^fro ≈ cη_t; under the spectral sphere a Wedin sin-Θ analysis gives the anisotropic θ_t ≲ η_t·c·R/(R−σ₂(W_{t+1})).

Empirical validation uses Qwen3-like architectures (RoPE, GQA, SwiGLU, pre-norm RMSNorm) with token budgets exceeding Chinchilla-optimal across 120M / 330M / 1B scales, comparing Muon, MuonH-fro, MuonH-spec, FSO, SSO, MACRO-fro, and MACRO-spec.

  • 1B model validation loss: MACRO-spec 2.461 vs. Muon baseline 2.473 (best result; train 2.398 vs 2.419).
  • 330M model validation loss: MACRO-spec 2.714, MACRO-fro 2.718, Muon 2.736; SSO 2.712 but at much higher compute cost.
  • Normalization-free 330M (Table 3): At LR=3e-2 Muon diverges to NaN; MACRO-spec achieves 2.739 (LR=1e-2 best). MACRO-fro best 2.758.
  • Activation control (Fig. 1): Worst-case Frobenius radius R_fro = √(D_out/D_in) causes significant rank/capacity collapse and higher train loss versus the average-case radius √D_out.
  • Rotational equilibrium (Figs. 4–5): With Frobenius constraint, rotation angle θ_t is flat from step 0 across c ∈ [0.1, 5.0]; under spectral, u_1 and v_1 rotation trajectories match each other and confirm the Wedin bound.
  • Convergence rate: O(T^{-1/4}) matching general non-convex stochastic optimization (Nemirovski-style lower bounds).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate-to-high. The single-loop Riemannian optimizer with msign LMO is a clean contribution, but the algorithmic novelty over Muon/MuonH is incremental—mainly the explicit tangent-space projection and update-weight ratio alignment. The bigger claim is the demystification (theory connecting constraints to normalization/weight-decay roles), which is where the paper's true value lies.
  • Rigor: Convergence proof under standard assumptions is sound (the spectral sphere is admittedly not a manifold, handled by working on the auxiliary set with a singular-value gap). Proposition 1 separating worst- vs. average-case is well-motivated. Wedin sin-Θ argument for spectral rotation is standard but applied carefully.
  • Reproducibility: Hyperparameters and architecture details are deferred to appendices D.1–D.3. No mention of code release in what was read; this is a yellow flag for a paper claiming a new optimizer that should be drop-in replacement.
  • Significance: If the claim "manifold constraints subsume RMSNorm + weight decay" holds, this is a meaningful conceptual unification. However the empirical gain over Muon at 1B is small (2.461 vs 2.473, ~0.5%), and the normalization-free configuration still trails the standard baseline by 0.025 nats (acknowledged as a limitation tied to per-layer γ flexibility).
  • Red flags:
  • The 1B-scale evaluation excludes SSO/FSO due to "limited computational resources and the absence of CUDA kernels"—this means the strongest baseline at the largest scale is missing, weakening direct claims of MACRO superiority.
  • The single global radius r limits expressivity vs. layer-specific γ; the authors flag this honestly but it raises the question whether learnable per-layer R would close the gap or break the theory.
  • "Token budgets exceed Chinchilla-optimal"—but exact budgets are buried in an appendix table; without seeing them it is hard to judge whether the loss differences are statistically meaningful.
  • The Norm-Gated SwiGLU is itself a non-trivial architectural change; isolating "manifold constraints alone replace normalization" from "this redesigned activation also helped" is not perfectly clean.

Strengths

  • Strong theoretical framing that genuinely separates what each heuristic does (worst- vs. average-case activation, relative LR locking, rotation enforcement).
  • Single-loop algorithm avoids the expensive double-loop projections of SSO/FSO while preserving Riemannian guarantees—a real practical improvement.
  • Clean decomposition of the rotational dynamics under Frobenius (static) vs. spectral (anisotropic, gap-modulated) constraints, supported by clear empirical curves.
  • Wide model-scale coverage (120M / 330M / 1B) with modern Qwen3-like recipe (SwiGLU, GQA, RoPE)—not a toy setting.
  • Honest reporting of the marginal 0.025-nat gap in the normalization-free setting and identification of its likely cause.

Limitations

  • Empirical wins over Muon/MuonH are small (often within plausible noise) and the paper does not report seed variance or confidence intervals for the validation losses.
  • Strongest competitor (SSO) is missing from the 1B comparison; the comparison story would be stronger with that data.
  • Single global constraint radius r is a known restriction; layer-wise constraints are left to future work.
  • Analysis is restricted to LLM pre-training—no fine-tuning, RLHF, or downstream task evaluation.
  • Manifold projection cost per step (matrix sign / SVD-style operations) is acknowledged but its wall-clock impact is not benchmarked in the main text.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does MACRO behave during long-context fine-tuning or RLHF where activation distributions shift dramatically? Does the static/global radius r still suffice?
  • Can per-layer learnable R recover the 0.025-nat gap without breaking the convergence proof?
  • What is the wall-clock overhead of msign LMO + tangent projection vs. Muon's matrix sign step at 1B+ scale?
  • Does the rotational-equilibrium argument extend to MoE layers where expert weights see only sparse updates?
  • How does MACRO interact with low-precision (FP8/BF16) training—does the locked η_rel,t mitigate or amplify quantization noise?
  • What happens if one applies manifold constraints only to attention projections vs. MLP weights? Is the redundancy-with-RMSNorm story uniform across layer types?
