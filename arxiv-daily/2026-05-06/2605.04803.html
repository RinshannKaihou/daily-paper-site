Not All Faults Are Equal: Transient-Fault Sensitivity Characterization of an Open-Source RISC-V Vector Cluster

arXiv: 2605.04803v1

Date: 2026-05-06

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04803v1

一句话总结： 本文针对开源 RISC-V 向量集群 Spatz 在 SET/SEU 瞬态故障模型下进行了 10 万次 RTL 故障注入实验，发现"故障数据损坏"(FD) 占据主导（SET ≥86%、SEU ≥91%），TCDM 和向量执行路径是主要敏感模块，且 FP32/BP16 下指数位的故障会引发最严重的 SDC 偏差，从而支持选择性硬化策略。
TL;DR: This paper performs an RTL fault-injection sensitivity study on the open-source RISC-V Spatz vector cluster under SET and SEU models (100,000 injections across six MatMul/Widening MatMul configurations), showing that Faulty Data corruption dominates manifested errors (≥86% SET, ≥91% SEU), TCDM and the vector execution path concentrate most vulnerabilities, and exponent-targeted faults in FP32/BP16 produce the worst SDC deviations — motivating selective protection of the highest-impact datapaths and bit-fields rather than uniform hardening.

核心贡献

  • 在开源 Spatz RISC-V 向量集群上，使用 Synopsys VC Z01X 构建了同时覆盖 SEU（时序单元）和 SET（组合逻辑）的 RTL 故障注入方法学，并通过模块端口 strobe 实现块级可观测，将故障表现区分为"系统崩溃 (FS)"与"数据损坏 (FD)"。
  • 在 6 种工作负载（FP32/FP16/BP16/FP8 的 MatMul 与 Widening MatMul）下完成约 10 万次故障注入，识别出向量执行子系统（VFU/VLSU/Controller/VRF）是 SET 主敏感区，TCDM 是 SEU 主敏感区。
  • 提出 SDC 数值严重性度量：通过对 FPU 操作数的 sign/exponent/mantissa 字段进行 1000 次定向比特翻转，结合腐蚀输出元素数与 RMSE 评估精度与位字段对故障传播的影响。
  • 给出可指导设计的结论：FP8 整体输出受影响最小；Widening MatMul 借助 FP32 累加显著降低 FP16 的 SDC 严重性；指数位故障在 FP32 与 BP16 中产生最大数值偏差，应优先选择性保护。

Key Contributions

  • A unified RTL fault-injection methodology for the Spatz cluster combining SET (combinational ports) and SEU (sequential flops) models with per-block strobes that classify outcomes into Faulty System Crash (FS) and Faulty Data Corruption (FD).
  • Module-level sensitivity characterization across six MatMul/Widening MatMul configurations spanning FP32, FP16, BP16, and FP8, providing the first published per-block view of Spatz under transient faults.
  • An SDC-oriented framework that decomposes operand corruption by sign/exponent/mantissa and quantifies severity via both the number of corrupted output elements and RMSE.
  • Concrete reliability-design guidelines: ECC/parity for storage (TCDM, VRF), duplication of lightweight handshake/control signals (VFU, VLSU), and exponent-focused checking on FP32/BP16 to suppress worst-case SDC at low overhead.

Methodology

The authors instrument the Spatz cluster (one core complex with Snitch scalar core, TCDM, VFU with 8 FPnew FPUs, VLSU, VSLDU, VRF, controller, and L1 I-cache) with strobes at module interconnect ports and run concurrent fault simulation in Synopsys VC Z01X, comparing a fault-free Good Machine (GM) against Faulty Machines (FMs) at every negative clock edge. SEUs are injected as bit-flips on sequential flops; SETs as transient pulses on combinational ports. Outcomes are categorized as Masked, FS (handshake/deadlock errors triggering crashes), or FD (data divergences); FD that escapes detection at the kernel boundary becomes Silent Data Corruption (SDC).

For numerical severity, the authors run 1,000 trials per bit-field (sign/exponent/mantissa) per precision, flipping a single randomly chosen bit at a random clock cycle in the FPU input operands. Decoded outputs use IEEE-754 (FP32, FP16), E8M7 (BP16), and E5M2 (FP8). Per SDC run, they compute the set of corrupted output elements I, its cardinality K, and the RMSE over I to capture both spread and magnitude. Total campaign size is roughly 100,000 injections across all configurations.

Results & Highlights

  • Across all six workloads, FD dominates manifested errors: ≥86% under SET and ≥91% under SEU, indicating silent propagation is the primary reliability concern.
  • SET sensitivity concentrates in the vector execution path: VFU, VLSU, controller, and VRF datapaths together account for ≥75% of manifesting SET errors.
  • SEU sensitivity is dominated by TCDM, contributing ≥75% of manifesting SEU errors across workloads.
  • Roughly 0.25% of SETs and 0.05% of SEUs manifest as FS on VFU handshake ports; lightweight duplication is suggested as a low-cost mitigation.
  • SDC severity (RMSE) tracks numerical range: FP32 is least resilient, FP16 substantially better, FP8 lowest impact in both spread and RMSE.
  • Widening MatMul benefits FP16 (FP32 accumulation reduces both corruption count and RMSE) but provides limited benefit for FP8 (16-bit accumulator).
  • The two worst-case RMSE outliers (≈10^37–10^38) come from exponent-targeted faults in FP32 and BP16; mantissa faults are far more tolerable, especially at FP8.

Authors & Credibility

All four authors are affiliated with the University of Bologna. Davide Rossi and Angelo Garofalo are well-known contributors to the open-source RISC-V/PULP ecosystem (Snitch, Spatz, FPnew lineage), giving the team strong domain credibility on the platform under study. The paper is a short conference-style submission (4 pages) and uses an industry-standard tool (Synopsys VC Z01X). The authors do not disclose code/script availability for the fault campaign in the manuscript itself, which is a notable gap given the open-source nature of the target hardware.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (moderate): Module-level fault sensitivity studies and SDC analyses on accelerators have been published before (e.g., Ethos-U55, GEMM/systolic arrays). The novelty here lies in (a) targeting an instruction-based vector cluster rather than a stateless GEMM/systolic accelerator, (b) jointly modeling SET and SEU in the same campaign, and (c) decomposing SDC by FP bit-field across multiple precisions including BP16 and FP8. This is a useful but incremental contribution.
  • Rigor (mixed): 100k injections across six workloads is reasonable for a short paper but yields only ~16k injections per workload split between SET and SEU and across many modules — confidence intervals on per-module rates are not reported. The SDC-bit-field study at 1,000 trials per (precision × field) is small for tail-dominated metrics like RMSE, where a single exponent flip can dominate the result. No statistical significance tests, error bars, or seed/variance reporting are provided.
  • Reproducibility (weak as written): The paper relies on Synopsys VC Z01X (commercial tool) and does not state whether the injection scripts, strobe placement, or workload binaries will be released. Spatz, FPnew, and Snitch are open source, but the campaign harness is not.
  • Significance (moderate): Findings are practically useful for space/edge deployments of RISC-V vector clusters and validate intuitions (lower precision masks faults, exponents are dangerous, storage needs ECC). They motivate selective hardening but do not yet prescribe a quantified protection scheme with area/power numbers.
  • Red flags / caveats: (i) The "≥86%/≥91% FD dominance" headline mixes per-workload aggregates and may obscure per-module variance. (ii) RMSE on SDC events is tail-dominated; with 1,000 trials, the reported "worst-case outliers" of 10^37–10^38 in FP32/BP16 likely reflect a small handful of exponent flips and should be interpreted as illustrative rather than statistically tight. (iii) Workload coverage is limited to MatMul variants — control-flow-heavy or memory-bound kernels may yield very different module rankings. (iv) The conclusion that mantissa faults are tolerable hinges on RMSE alone; for AI workloads, distributional or task-level metrics could tell a different story.

Strengths

  • Clear, well-motivated problem framing tied to space/edge AI deployment.
  • Combines two fault models (SET + SEU) on a non-trivial, open-source vector cluster — a step beyond prior systolic-only or single-model studies.
  • Useful decomposition of SDC by FP precision and bit-field, including emerging formats (BP16, FP8 E5M2).
  • Findings translate directly into actionable design guidance (ECC for TCDM/VRF, duplication on handshakes, exponent-focused checking).

Limitations

  • Only MatMul and Widening MatMul are evaluated; results may not generalize to control-flow-heavy or non-GEMM workloads.
  • 1,000 trials per bit-field per precision is small for tail-dominated RMSE metrics; no confidence intervals.
  • Reproducibility unclear — VC Z01X is commercial, and no public release of scripts/strobes is announced.
  • The cluster is configured with a single CC; multi-CC interactions (interconnect, contention-induced faults) are not covered.
  • No area/power cost estimates are given for the proposed selective hardening, so the cost/benefit claim remains qualitative.
  • AI-task-level impact (e.g., classification accuracy under SDC) is not measured — RMSE is a proxy for what ultimately matters in deployment.

Relevance

该工作虽属硬件可靠性方向，但其揭示的"FP32/BP16 指数位故障导致 SDC 严重失稳、低精度格式天然抑制错误传播"的结论，对低精度 LLM 推理在不可靠硬件（如边缘/空间场景）下的稳定性与可解释性具有间接但有价值的参考意义。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How do these per-module rates change for control-flow-heavy workloads (e.g., attention, sparse kernels) versus dense MatMul, and would Snitch/instruction-cache sensitivity rise meaningfully?
  • What is the area/power overhead of the suggested selective protection scheme (ECC on TCDM/VRF, duplicate handshakes, exponent-checking), and does its cost-benefit beat blanket TMR on the VFU?
  • Would an end-to-end study on real DNN inference (accuracy, calibration, hallucination-rate proxies) confirm that mantissa faults are truly "tolerable," or does task-level aggregation amplify them?
  • How robust are the RMSE worst-case findings for FP32/BP16 exponents under a much larger campaign (e.g., 10^5–10^6 trials per bit-field), and do tail behaviors stabilize?
  • Could fault-aware training or value-clipping at the FP8/BP16 boundary further close the gap exposed by exponent-targeted faults without hardware modifications?
  • How would multi-bit upsets (MBUs) or burst faults — increasingly relevant at advanced nodes — change the relative module ranking observed under SEU/SET single-bit assumptions?
