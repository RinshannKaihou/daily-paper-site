OSAQ: Outlier Self-Absorption for Accurate Low-bit LLM Quantization

arXiv: 2605.04738v1

Date: 2026-05-06

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04738v1

一句话总结： 本文提出 OSAQ，一种利用 Hessian 低秩一致性，在零空间中通过闭式解构造加性权重变换的低比特 LLM 量化方法，将异常值"自吸收"进权重本身，与 GPTQ 配合在 2-bit 量化下相对原始 GPTQ 实现 40% 以上的困惑度下降。
TL;DR: OSAQ introduces a closed-form additive weight perturbation residing in the Hessian's null space to suppress weight outliers without altering task loss, complementing existing multiplicative (scaling/rotation) weight-only PTQ methods and yielding strong gains, especially in extreme low-bit (W2A16, W3A16) settings on LLaMA2/LLaMA3 and large instruction-tuned models.

核心贡献

  • 提出"异常值自吸收"(Outlier Self-Absorption) 范式：不同于既有的 scaling/rotation 这类需要相邻层配合的乘性变换，OSAQ 通过对单层权重施加加性扰动 ΔW 抑制异常值，离线吸收进权重，无需层间联动也无推理开销。
  • 基于二阶 Taylor 展开，证明只要 ΔW 位于 Hessian 的零空间内，任务损失的二阶扰动严格为零，从而保证模型性能不变；通过对 Hessian 特征分解并用尾能量阈值 γ 提取稳定零空间。
  • 用 Softmax-∞ 近似将不可微的 ℓ∞ 范数最小化转化为加权 ℓ2 目标，结合 ℓ2 正则与反平移项，得到系数矩阵 β 的闭式解（求解 M 个 K×K 线性系统），无需训练或迭代优化。
  • 在 LLaMA2/3 (7B–70B) 和 Mistral-Large-123B、Llama-3.1-405B 上系统对比 GPTQ/AWQ/QuIP/MagR/OmniQuant 等基线，覆盖 W4A16/W3A16/W2A16 与 W4A4、W4A16KV4，在 WikiText2/C4、PIQA/ARC/Wino、MMLU、MT-Bench 多任务上验证一致提升。

Key Contributions

  • A new additive transformation paradigm for weight-only LLM PTQ. Existing methods rely on multiplicative scaling (AWQ, SmoothQuant) or rotation (QuIP, QuaRot, SpinQuant) that require coupled changes between adjacent layers. OSAQ instead absorbs ΔW directly into a single layer's weights, requiring no inter-layer coordination and adding zero inference cost.
  • A theoretical justification grounded in the second-order Taylor expansion of the task loss: any ΔW in the Hessian's null space yields zero second-order loss perturbation. The authors empirically show the Hessian exhibits low-rank consistency across inputs (tail eigenvalues account for ~0.01% of total energy), providing a stable null space that doesn't shift between calibration and inference distributions.
  • A closed-form solution. The non-differentiable ℓ∞-norm minimization over the perturbed weights is approximated by a Softmax-τ-weighted ℓ2 norm, plus an ℓ2 regularizer on β and an anti-shift penalty on the average direction. The optimal coefficients are obtained by solving M independent positive-definite linear systems per layer, without iterative training.
  • Plug-and-play compatibility: OSAQ is applied as a preprocessing step before GPTQ, AWQ, QuIP, WKVQuant, QuaRot, DuQuant, and SpinQuant. The paper reports strong improvements across W4A16, W3A16, W2A16 weight-only and W4A4, W4A16KV4 settings.

Methodology

OSAQ targets weight-only PTQ for LLMs and operates per linear layer. For weight matrix W ∈ R^{M×N} with empirical Hessian H^w (over calibration tokens), the procedure is:

  1. Null-space extraction. Eigen-decompose H^w = V diag(λ) V^T with eigenvalues sorted in non-decreasing magnitude. Choose K such that the cumulative tail energy reaches a threshold γ ∈ (0,1) and define the null-space basis N = V[:, 0:K-1]^T ∈ R^{K×N}. The threshold-based (rather than fixed-K) choice keeps null-space dimension consistent across layers.
  2. Additive transformation. Construct ΔW = β N where β ∈ R^{M×K} are coefficients to be solved. By construction Δw^T H^w Δw ≈ 0, so the second-order loss perturbation vanishes.
  3. Softmax-∞ objective. Replace the non-differentiable min_β ‖W + βN‖_∞ with a Softmax-weighted ℓ2 surrogate: weights s_{ij} = exp(|W_{ij}|/τ) / Σ_t exp(|W_{it}|/τ) emphasize peak entries; minimizing Σ_j s_{ij} (W_{ij} + b_i^T n_j)^2 + (μ1/2) ‖b_i‖^2 + (μ2/2) (b_i^T v)^2 (with v = N·1_N) for each output channel i.
  4. Closed form. Setting the gradient to zero yields A_i b_i = -ρ_i with A_i = Σ_j s_{ij} n_j n_j^T + μ1 I_K + μ2 v v^T, ρ_i = Σ_j s_{ij} W_{ij} n_j. Each A_i is symmetric positive-definite (since μ1 > 0), so b_i^ = -A_i^{-1} ρ_i is unique. The full β is then assembled and W' = W + β*N is passed to the downstream quantizer (GPTQ, AWQ, QuIP, etc.).

Hyperparameters: γ (tail-energy threshold), τ (Softmax temperature), μ1, μ2 (regularizers). Calibration uses 128 samples × 2048 tokens (matching baselines). The runtime overhead vs. GPTQ alone is roughly 2 min on 7B and 0.6 hr on 70B (Table 13).

Results & Highlights

  • Language modeling (Table 1). On LLaMA2-7B/13B/70B and LLaMA3-8B/70B, OSAQ + GPTQ consistently lowers WikiText2/C4 perplexity vs. GPTQ in W4A16, W3A16-g128, W3A16, and W2A16-g128. Most striking gains are at extreme low-bit: W3A16 LLaMA2-13B perplexity drops from 6.44 (GPTQ) to 5.72; W2A16-g128 LLaMA2-7B drops from 36.8 to 21.2 (~42% lower). Adding coordinate-descent iterations (OSAQ+GPTQ†) yields further improvement (10.6 on 2-7B).
  • Commonsense QA (Table 2). On LLaMA3-8B/70B, OSAQ raises average zero-shot accuracy across PIQA/ARC-e/ARC-c/WinoGrande, with the largest absolute gain in W2A16-g128 (LLaMA3-8B: 38.3 → 44.4 with GPTQ; 51.7 with †).
  • MMLU (Table 3). On LLaMA3-8B in W4A16, average MMLU rises from 57.6 (GPTQ) to 58.2; in W3A16, from 30.0 to 30.9.
  • Larger models (Table 4). Mistral-Large-123B-Instruct: 82.0 → 82.3 MMLU avg (W4A16). Llama-3.1-405B-Instruct: 85.7 → 86.1 (W4A16-g128). Effects shrink at this scale but remain consistent.
  • Weight–activation and KV-cache (Tables 8–9). OSAQ also improves W4A4 baselines (QuaRot, DuQuant, SpinQuant) and W4A16KV4 (WKVQuant) settings, suggesting compatibility beyond pure weight-only quantization.
  • Hyperparameter robustness (Figure 5). Grid searches over γ ∈ {5e-5,...,5e-4}, τ ∈ {0.05,...,0.5}, μ1, μ2 show flat perplexity curves, indicating low sensitivity.
  • Null-space stability (Tables 6, 11). The maximum singular values of N1^T N2 between calibration batches drawn from WikiText2 and C4 are 0.965–0.981, supporting the claim that the null space is distribution-stable.
  • Runtime/inference (Tables 13–14). OSAQ adds ~2–8 min over GPTQ for 7B–70B models and zero inference overhead, preserving GPTQ's W4A16 speedups (1.89–2.41×).

Critical Assessment

Novelty. Genuinely novel framing. The dominant line of weight-only PTQ work pursues multiplicative outlier removal (scaling, rotation, permutation). Casting outlier suppression as an additive perturbation that lives in the Hessian's null space is an elegant theoretical move and, to my knowledge, complementary to SmoothQuant/QuIP/QuaRot/SpinQuant lineage. MagR (Zhang et al. 2024) uses ℓ∞ minimization on weights but iteratively and without a null-space constraint, which is conceptually different.

Rigor. The mathematical setup (second-order Taylor, null-space constraint, Softmax-τ surrogate of ℓ∞, closed-form normal equations) is clean and reproducible from the paper's text. Empirically, the breadth of comparisons is good — six baselines, multiple model families and sizes, perplexity + zero-shot QA + MMLU + MT-Bench. Ablations cover the FP16 impact of ΔW, null-space stability across calibration sets, and the Softmax-∞ vs. ℓ2 choice. The hyperparameter grid is honest about robustness.

Reproducibility. The closed-form solution is simple (M K×K linear systems per layer), and most pieces should be reproducible from the equations alone. However, no code repository is referenced in the pages I read, no random seed/runtime details for the † coordinate-descent variant, and the calibration batch composition for the null-space stability checks isn't fully specified. Reproducing the W2A16-g128 † numbers in particular may be sensitive.

Significance. The headline 40%+ perplexity reduction is for vanilla GPTQ at 2-bit, where GPTQ is known to degrade severely (LLaMA2-7B WikiText2 PPL 36.8). Compared with stronger 2-bit methods like QuIP (gain is more modest), OSAQ+GPTQ still wins, but the marginal lift over QuIP/OmniQuant in 3- and 4-bit is small — often <0.1 PPL or <1 point on QA averages. At 405B, gains are within run-to-run noise of GPTQ (86.1 vs. 85.7 MMLU). So the method is meaningful primarily in the extreme low-bit regime.

Red flags / caveats.

  1. Hessian estimate dependence. The empirical Hessian is computed from 128 calibration samples; the "null space" is really a small-eigenvalue subspace (cumulative energy ≤ γ ≈ 1e-4). The claim of zero second-order loss perturbation holds only approximately. The paper's own stability table reports max singular values of N1^T N2 around 0.965–0.981 (not 1.0), implying the subspace shifts ~2–4% across distributions. The downstream effect of this shift isn't quantified end-to-end.
  2. Softmax-τ vs. ℓ∞ approximation. The ablation comparing Softmax-∞ to direct ℓ2 (Table 7) shows a clear win, but the gap to a true ℓ∞ optimization (e.g., LP) isn't reported. The temperature τ ∈ [0.05, 0.5] grid is narrow.
  3. Anti-shift term μ2 motivation. The μ2 regularizer penalizes uniform translation of an output channel. This is described as preventing bias shifts, but no ablation isolates its contribution; μ2 may be doing more work than the framing implies.
  4. Venue claim. "ICML 2026, PMLR 306" appears in the running header even though this is a fresh arXiv v1; readers should treat the venue as aspirational pending the official program release.
  5. Plug-and-play framing vs. composition risk. OSAQ is run before GPTQ/AWQ/QuIP, but the interaction with rotation-based methods (QuaRot/SpinQuant) — which themselves change the basis and thereby the Hessian — is not theoretically analyzed; only empirical results are shown.

Overall. A solid, well-motivated paper with a genuinely new angle on weight outlier suppression. The closed-form math is the strongest part. The empirical lift is concentrated at 2-bit, which is the regime that needs help most, but readers in the 4-bit-and-above regime should expect smaller gains.

Strengths

  • Clear theoretical narrative: low-rank Hessian -> stable null space -> loss-invariant additive correction.
  • Closed-form solution avoids the optimization fragility common in PTQ methods like OmniQuant.
  • Plug-and-play composability with most existing weight-only and weight-activation quantizers.
  • Broad experimental coverage including very large instruction-tuned models (123B, 405B).
  • Honest hyperparameter robustness study and an ablation distinguishing the additive transform's effect on FP16 (negligible) vs. on quantized models (substantial).

Limitations

  • Gains shrink rapidly at higher precisions (W4A16) and large model scales — at 405B, OSAQ adds <0.5 MMLU points over GPTQ.
  • Null-space "stability" is only empirically verified to ~0.97 alignment, not 1.0; a calibration distribution mismatch could erode the loss-invariance guarantee.
  • No public code link in the body of the paper (per the pages I read), which hampers reproducibility.
  • The μ2 anti-shift term and the Softmax temperature τ both lack standalone ablations.
  • Comparison is restricted to language tasks for LLMs; no investigation on multimodal models, long-context perplexity, or non-language reasoning benchmarks beyond MMLU/MT-Bench.
  • Coordinate-descent variant (†) is used selectively at W2A16 but not deeply analyzed.

Relevance

与你关注的"LLM 训练稳定性 / 可靠性 / 可解释性"主题中等相关：OSAQ 的 Hessian 低秩与零空间分析为 LLM 权重几何的可解释性提供了实证支持，闭式解的稳定性也契合训练后阶段的"可控性"需求；不过它本身是部署侧的 PTQ 工作，与训练稳定性的直接联系较弱。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the effective null-space dimension K scale with model size and layer depth, and does it correlate with recently proposed "intrinsic dimensionality" measures of LLMs?
  • Can the same loss-invariant additive paradigm be extended to activations, where outliers are far more harmful at low bit-widths?
  • The paper assumes a fixed Hessian; would using a damped or block-diagonal Fisher (à la K-FAC) give a more stable null space across distributions?
  • How does OSAQ interact with quantization-aware fine-tuning — can the ΔW be initialized from OSAQ and then refined with a few QAT steps?
  • For rotation-based pipelines (QuaRot/SpinQuant), should OSAQ be applied in the rotated basis, or before rotation? The paper reports gains in both compositions but doesn't analyze the order's optimality.
  • Is the ~3% null-space drift between WikiText2 and C4 calibration enough to cause measurable harm on out-of-distribution evaluation (e.g., code, math, multilingual)?
  • Could a learned threshold γ per layer outperform the global setting, given that different layers' Hessians have very different spectral decay?
