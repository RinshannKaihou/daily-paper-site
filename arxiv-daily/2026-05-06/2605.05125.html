Joint Treatment Effect Estimation from Incomplete Healthcare Data: Temporal Causal Normalizing Flows with LLM-driven Evolutionary MNAR Imputation

arXiv: 2605.05125v2

Date: 2026-05-06

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05125

一句话总结： 本文提出了一个两阶段管道：首先用LLM驱动的进化搜索生成MNAR缺失数据的插补器，再通过DAG约束的因果归一化流（CausalFlow-T）对纵向电子健康记录进行精确反事实推断，在瑞士2型糖尿病真实队列中恢复了GLP-1受体激动剂与SGLT-2抑制剂之间体重差异的每协议效果估计（-0.98 kg, 95% CI [-1.01, -0.96]），与RCT证据一致。
TL;DR: This paper introduces a two-stage pipeline that jointly tackles time-varying confounding and high-rate MNAR missingness in electronic health records (EHRs). Stage 1 uses an LLM-driven evolutionary search to produce imputation code that is scored on a self-supervised proxy penalizing both reconstruction error and causal correlation distortion. Stage 2 feeds the completed data into CausalFlow-T—a DAG-constrained Causal Masked Autoregressive Flow conditioned on an LSTM-encoded patient history—which enables exact counterfactual inference via Pearl's AAP procedure. Applied to a Swiss primary care T2D cohort, the pipeline recovers a clinically plausible per-protocol weight difference between GLP-1RAs and SGLT-2is consistent with RCT evidence despite 50–80% MNAR missingness.

核心贡献

  • CausalFlow-T 模型： 提出了一种基于有向无环图（DAG）约束的因果掩码自回归流（CausalMAF），通过LSTM编码患者历史以建模纵向数据，并使用Pearl的溯因-行动-预测（AAP）程序实现精确反事实推断，消除了CVAE和GNN-CVAE方法中的ELBO近似误差。
  • 两种不重叠失败模式的识别： 实验证明，DAG约束（用于混淆分离）和精确推断（用于结构传播）是两个互不补偿的必要条件；现有方法在至少一个维度上失败。
  • LLM驱动的进化插补： 设计了一种LLM作为"变异算子"的进化搜索框架，迭代生成候选Python插补器，并通过兼顾重建误差和因果相关性失真的自监督代理得分进行筛选，在30%–80% MNAR缺失率下均优于LOCF、MissForest等基线。
  • 真实世界应用验证： 将整个管道应用于瑞士6,114名2型糖尿病成人的初级医疗队列，恢复了与RCT证据一致的GLP-1RA vs SGLT-2i 1年体重变化每协议效果估计。
  • 五标准可靠性框架： 提出包含偏差比、尾部方差、风险比方向、臂重建误差和稳定性的五维可靠性评估标准，揭示了现有方法仅靠MAE无法暴露的系统性失败。

Key Contributions

  • CausalFlow-T architecture: A DAG-constrained Causal Masked Autoregressive Flow (CausalMAF) conditioned on LSTM-encoded patient history that jointly models covariates, treatment, and outcome over time; exact flow inversion enables counterfactual inference via Pearl's AAP without variational approximations.
  • Identification of two non-overlapping failure modes: Ablations across four synthetic datasets with known counterfactuals demonstrate that (i) DAG constraints are necessary for confounding separation and (ii) exact inference is necessary for structural propagation; neither property compensates for the absence of the other, meaning all competing baselines fail on at least one axis.
  • LLM-driven evolutionary imputation: An evolutionary search loop where an LLM iteratively proposes full Python imputer modules, evaluated via a self-supervised proxy score balancing pointwise RMSE with covariate–outcome and covariate–treatment correlation preservation, achieving the best pooled rank across 30–80% MNAR missingness levels.
  • Five-criterion reliability framework: A more stringent evaluation template than any single metric, covering subgroup bias ratio, tail variance ratio, hazard ratio direction, arm reconstruction error, and stability—exposing failures invisible to predictive error alone.
  • Real-world causal estimation from incomplete EHRs: Per-protocol effect of GLP-1RAs vs SGLT-2is on 1-year body weight (-0.98 kg [95% CI -1.01, -0.96]) recovered from the Swiss FIRE EHR database with >50% MNAR missingness, consistent with the SUSTAIN 8 RCT (-1.06 kg).

Methodology

Problem setup. The paper considers N patients over T discrete time steps with covariates $x_{i,t} \in \mathbb{R}^d$, binary treatment $a_{i,t}$, continuous outcome $y_{i,t}$, and per-variable missingness masks $m_{i,t} \in \{0,1\}^d$. Missingness is explicitly modeled as MNAR: $P(m_{i,t,j}=1 | x_{i,t,j}, x_{i,t,-j}, a_{i,t}, y_{i,t}) = f_j(\cdot)$, i.e., the probability of a value being unobserved depends on its own unobserved value. The goal is ATE = $\mathbb{E}[y_{i,t}(1) - y_{i,t}(0)]$ and individual treatment effects $\tau_{i,t}$.

Stage 2 — CausalFlow-T. The core generative model is a CausalMAF (Javaloy et al., 2023) where the autoregressive ordering is constrained to a topological sort of the expert-specified DAG $\mathcal{G}=(V,E)$. Each variable node $v_j$ maps from an exogenous Gaussian noise $z_j$ to $v_j$ conditioned on its causal parents $\text{pa}(j)$ and on the LSTM-encoded patient history $h_t = \text{LSTM}(x_t, h_{t-1})$. The flow is trained by exact log-likelihood maximization, avoiding the ELBO gap that causes CVAE-based methods to collapse (hazard ratio ~1.0 on CVD Risk despite correct graph specification). Counterfactuals are computed via Pearl's Abduction-Action-Prediction (AAP): invert the flow to recover $z_t$, intervene by replacing $a_t \leftarrow a'$, propagate through DAG ordering to obtain $v^{ct}$, and compute $\hat{\tau}_{i,t} = \hat{y}_{i,t}(1) - \hat{y}_{i,t}(0)$.

Stage 1 — LLM-driven evolutionary imputation. The LLM does not fill missing entries directly but rather writes complete Python imputer modules $g^{(k)}: \tilde{\mathcal{D}} \mapsto \hat{\mathcal{D}}$. A self-supervised proxy score $s(g)$ combines pointwise RMSE on a masked holdout $\Omega_p$ (constructed with contiguous-run masking to mimic real EHR patterns) with two correlation-distortion penalties $\Delta_Y(g)$ and $\Delta_T(g)$ measuring how much imputed data distorts biomarker–outcome and biomarker–treatment correlations respectively. A greedy best-of-K evolutionary update rule retains the new candidate only if it improves $s(g)$. Three LLM backends were compared: GPT-5.4, GPT-OSS-120b, and Qwen3.5-Plus.

Evaluation. CausalFlow-T is first validated on four synthetic datasets of increasing complexity (Simple 3-Node, LDL Toy, Cox Survival, CVD Risk Toy) with known ground-truth counterfactuals, plus a semi-synthetic EHR dataset (FIRE-based). Imputation is then evaluated separately with CausalFlow-T held fixed, so differences in downstream ATE recovery reflect imputation quality alone. Six imputation strategies are compared: LOCF, MissForest, CausalCFM (a DAG-aware flow-matching baseline), and LLM-driven evolutionary imputation with three backends.

Results & Highlights

CausalFlow-T vs. baselines on synthetic data. CausalFlow-T is the only model satisfying all five reliability criteria simultaneously (mean rank 1.83 across four datasets). GNN-CVAE (rank 2.58) and CVAE (rank 3.17) fail on bias and tail variance on harder benchmarks; TARNet (rank 3.67) inverts the treatment effect direction on the CVD Risk dataset (HR = 1.133 vs. true 0.831). Removing the DAG constraint alone inflates the ATE deviation to -3.382 ± 0.251 on the semi-synthetic dataset, confirming that structural supervision is necessary under realistic clinical distributions.

Imputation results (30–80% MNAR). GPT-5.4 achieves the best overall pooled rank (2.38) with only modest degradation from 30% to 80% missingness (MAE 1.914 → 2.059). Notably, above 50% missingness LLM-based imputation is the only strategy that preserves causal correlation structure and ATE recovery—statistical methods progressively degrade. Pointwise reconstruction quality and causal correlation preservation are not aligned objectives; CausalCFM performs better on correlation-fidelity diagnostics while GPT-5.4 dominates on reconstruction and temporal extrapolation.

Real-world application. Applied to the Swiss FIRE primary care EHR database (6,114 adults with T2D), the pipeline recovers: GLP-1RA 1-year weight change -4.02 kg [95% CI -4.15, -3.88]; SGLT-2i -3.04 kg [95% CI -3.15, -2.91]; per-protocol difference -0.98 kg [95% CI -1.01, -0.96] favoring GLP-1RAs. This is consistent with the SUSTAIN 8 RCT result of -1.06 kg [95% CI -1.76, -0.36], with the attenuation expected from extending from selected trial populations to the full drug classes in routine care.

Critical Assessment

Novelty. The combination of DAG-constrained normalizing flows with LSTM temporal encoding for longitudinal treatment effect estimation is genuinely novel. The LLM-as-evolutionary-mutation-operator for imputation code generation is also a creative application. However, both components draw on well-established ideas (CausalMAF from Javaloy et al. 2023; evolutionary program search from recent AlphaCode-style work), so the novelty is primarily in the integration and the rigorous dual-stage evaluation methodology.

Rigor. The five-criterion reliability framework is a meaningful contribution to evaluation practice. The decision to validate CausalFlow-T on complete data before deploying with imputation is methodologically sound and prevents attribution confusion. The identification of two non-overlapping failure modes is well-supported by ablation evidence. However, several concerns deserve attention: (i) The DAG is assumed fixed and expert-specified—a strong assumption in practice; the sensitivity analysis (Appendix I) is limited. (ii) The real-world result cannot be validated against ground-truth counterfactuals; external consistency with one RCT is encouraging but not definitive. (iii) The evolutionary imputation loop uses a greedy single-parent scheme; there is no systematic exploration of alternative evolutionary strategies (crossover, multi-objective Pareto fronts), which may limit the generality of the approach. (iv) The proxy score $s(g)$ relies on contiguous-run masking to simulate MNAR; whether this mask distribution matches the true clinical missingness mechanism is untested.

Reproducibility. The synthetic datasets and evaluation protocols are described in sufficient detail. However, the real-world FIRE dataset cannot be publicly released due to privacy constraints, limiting full reproducibility of the clinical results. The evolutionary imputation relies on LLM APIs (GPT-5.4, Qwen3.5-Plus) whose behavior may change over time or differ between API versions. Code availability is not mentioned in the main text.

Significance. The paper addresses a genuinely important and underserved problem: most causal ML methods are validated in settings with <40% MAR missingness, while real EHRs routinely have >50% MNAR missingness. The demonstration that jointly addressing MNAR imputation and causal structure is both necessary and feasible has practical relevance for target trial emulation at scale.

Red flags. The confidence intervals reported for the real-world application ([-1.01, -0.96]) are extremely narrow for an observational study with substantial unmeasured confounding. The authors acknowledge the conditional exchangeability assumption is untestable but do not report sensitivity analyses for unmeasured confounding in the main text. The claim that LLM-based imputation "dominates" at >50% missingness is based on a single semi-synthetic benchmark; generalization to other clinical domains is uncertain.

Strengths

  • Clear and principled two-stage pipeline with isolated validation of each component
  • Identification and empirical demonstration of two distinct, non-overlapping failure modes in longitudinal causal inference—a finding with broad implications beyond this paper
  • Five-criterion reliability framework provides a more stringent benchmark than MAE alone
  • LLM evolutionary imputation achieves robustness across 30–80% MNAR without requiring access to true missingness mechanism
  • Real-world results are externally consistent with existing RCT evidence
  • Multi-disciplinary team with genuine clinical co-authors strengthens external validity

Limitations

  • DAG is assumed to be correctly expert-specified; misspecification propagates systematic bias and is not robustly detectable from data alone
  • The evolutionary imputation loop uses a single-parent greedy scheme with no exploration of multi-objective or recombination strategies, limiting optimization diversity
  • Proxy score masking scheme (contiguous-run) may not fully represent the true MNAR mechanism in all EHR settings
  • The real-world FIRE dataset cannot be released, limiting reproducibility of the key clinical result
  • Confidence intervals on the real-world effect estimate appear implausibly tight for an observational study; sensitivity analysis for unmeasured confounding is relegated to appendices
  • CausalFlow-T dequantizes binary outcomes (e.g., survival endpoints), incurring calibration costs visible in the Cox benchmark results; discrete flow extensions are noted as future work
  • Evaluation relies on three specific LLM backends; performance could shift with future model versions or on-premise deployments

Relevance

本文直接涉及LLM在因果推断可靠性中的应用——通过进化搜索使LLM生成可验证的插补代码而非直接填充数据，有效缓解了LLM幻觉对下游因果估计的影响；同时，该研究提出的五维可靠性评估框架对思考LLM辅助决策系统的可信度具有重要参考价值。对于关注LLM可靠性与训练数据质量影响的读者，本文展示了如何将LLM的生成能力约束在可验证的代码空间内，从而增强模型行为的可解释性和可控性。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How sensitive is the CausalFlow-T estimate to edge misspecification in the expert DAG, and could automated causal discovery (e.g., from observational data) be used to refine the graph without introducing additional bias?
  2. The proxy score's contiguous-run masking approximates real MNAR patterns—how would performance degrade if the true missingness mechanism is driven by fundamentally different clinical decision rules (e.g., selective testing only for patients showing symptoms)?
  3. Could the LLM evolutionary loop be replaced or augmented with gradient-based imputation optimization using the downstream causal loss as the training signal, directly aligning reconstruction with ATE recovery?
  4. The five reliability criteria are evaluated post-hoc; could they be incorporated into the training objective of CausalFlow-T or the imputation scoring function to explicitly optimize for all criteria simultaneously?
  5. How does the pipeline scale to settings with many treatments (>2 arms) or continuous treatment doses, which are common in real-world pharmacotherapy?
  6. The SUSTAIN 8 RCT enrolled a more homogeneous population than the FIRE cohort—to what extent does the estimated attenuation in per-protocol effect reflect real heterogeneity versus residual confounding not captured by the expert DAG?
