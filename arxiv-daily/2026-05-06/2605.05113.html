How Long Does Infinite Width Last? Signal Propagation in Long-Range Linear Recurrences

arXiv: 2605.05113v1

Date: 2026-05-06

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05113v1

一句话总结： 本文为复高斯初始化下的线性递归模型（LRU/RNN）推导了隐藏状态信号能量的精确有限宽度公式，并据此识别出三种深度-宽度联合标度区间——亚临界（t = o(√n)）、临界（t ~ c√n）和超临界（t ≫ √n），证明 Glorot 初始化在递归深度达到 √n 这一关键尺度时即开始失稳，远早于前馈网络中 L ~ cn 的失稳尺度。
TL;DR: Seleznova derives exact closed-form finite-width formulas for the squared-norm signal energies of linear recurrent units (LRUs) and linear RNNs under complex Gaussian initialization with variance 1/n, then uses them to prove that infinite-width signal-propagation theory holds only when recurrent depth t = o(√n); at the critical scale t ~ c√n the signal energy converges to a non-trivial profile (2/c²)·sinh(c²/2), and beyond √n it explodes super-exponentially. This pinpoints √n as the precise depth at which Glorot initialization becomes unstable in long-range linear recurrences—qualitatively earlier than the L ~ cn scale known for feedforward nets.

核心贡献

  • 在复高斯初始化（方差 1/n）下，给出 LRU 和线性 RNN 信号能量 S_{n,t} 与 Q_{n,k} 的精确闭式有限宽度公式（定理 2.1），用上升/下降阶乘 F_p^± 表达，是首个在递归网络中关于信号传播的精确有限宽度结果。
  • 证明深度-宽度联合标度下存在三种区间：(i) 亚临界 t,k = o(√n) 时无穷宽近似仍然准确，Q_{n,k} → 1；(ii) 临界 t ~ c√n 时收敛到非平凡极限 Q_{n,k} → (2/c²)sinh(c²/2)；(iii) 超临界 t ≫ √n 时信号呈双指数爆炸。
  • 把信号传播的有限宽度计算归约为一个组合循环计数问题：通过 Wick 展开把 trace moment E[tr((W^k)*W^k)/n] 重写为某些随机置换换位子 [τ⁻¹, σ̃⁻¹] 的循环数之和，再借助 Hultman 数与 Dubach (2024) 的结果获得精确求和。
  • 给出 Glorot 初始化在 t ~ √n 处即不稳定的精确证明，比 Bar et al. (2025) 仅给出粗略下界更尖锐，并通过 Remark 2 把结论扩展到实高斯权重情形（提供下界）。
  • 通过数值实验（n ∈ {100, 200, 500, 1000, 2000}，10⁵ Monte Carlo 样本）验证临界尺度律，且对比表明递归网络中有限宽度效应的累积速度本质上快于前馈网络。

Key Contributions

  • Exact finite-width formulas (Theorem 2.1): Closed-form expressions for both signal energies S_{n,t} = Σ Q_{n,k} and Q_{n,k} = (F_{k+2}^+(n) − F_{k+2}^−(n))/(n^{k+1}(k+1)(k+2)), valid for all finite n, t, k under complex Gaussian initialization with arbitrary input covariances.
  • Three depth-width regimes: Sharp identification of (i) subcritical k,t = o(√n) where infinite-width predictions hold (Q_{n,k} → 1, S_{n,t} ~ t+1); (ii) critical k,t ~ c√n where Q_{n,k} → (2/c²)sinh(c²/2); (iii) supercritical k ≫ √n with Q_{n,k} ~ (n/k²)·exp(k²/(2n)) showing super-exponential blowup.
  • Proof technique reduction: The trace moment E[tr((W^k)*W^k)/n] is reduced via Wick expansion to counting cycles in commutators [τ⁻¹, σ̃⁻¹] where τ is a long cycle, connecting the analysis to Hultman numbers and recent results on random permutation cycle counts.
  • Sharp Glorot instability scale: Proves Glorot initialization becomes unstable precisely once recurrent depth reaches the √n scale, sharpening the lower bound from Bar et al. (2025) and recovering the missing √n prefactor in S_{n,t} ~ √n · ∫₀^c (2/x²)sinh(x²/2)dx.
  • Recurrent vs feedforward distinction: Demonstrates that finite-width corrections in recurrent nets become visible at depth √n, while feedforward analogues require L ~ cn—explaining empirical signal instability in long-range recurrent models that infinite-width theory cannot capture.

Methodology

The paper studies two models initialized with W ~ 𝒩_ℂ(0, 1/n) i.i.d. complex Gaussian: the Linear Recurrent Unit (LRU) h^(t) = Σ_{k=0}^{t} W^k x^(t−k) and the linear RNN h^(t) = W^t x^(0). The quantities of interest are the normalized signal energies S_{n,t} = (1/n)𝔼‖h_LRU‖² and Q_{n,k} = (1/n)𝔼‖h_RNN‖².

The technical core is computing the trace moment (1/n)𝔼[tr((W^k)*W^k)] exactly. The author applies Wick's theorem to expand into Gaussian moments indexed by permutations σ ∈ S_k, where each permutation contributes n^{−k−1+F(σ)}, F(σ) being the number of free indices. F(σ) is identified as the number of cycles in the commutator [τ⁻¹, σ̃⁻¹] for τ = (0, 1, ..., k) a long cycle, reducing the problem to counting cycles in commutators of random permutations—a tractable combinatorial object linked to Hultman numbers and Dubach (2024).

This yields closed-form expressions involving rising and falling factorials F_p^±(n) := n(n±1)···(n±(p−1)). Asymptotic analysis under joint scalings k, t ~ n^α then identifies the three regimes. For real Gaussian weights, an additional argument under non-negative cross-time input correlations gives Q^ℝ_{n,k} ≥ Q_{n,k}, providing lower bounds. Numerical experiments use Monte Carlo with 10⁵ samples across n ∈ {100, 200, 500, 1000, 2000} to validate the critical-scaling profile.

Results & Highlights

  • Subcritical regime (k,t = o(√n)): Q_{n,k} → 1, S_{n,t} ~ t+1, matching the classical Glorot infinite-width prediction. Recurrent and feedforward signal propagation remain asymptotically indistinguishable here.
  • Critical regime (k,t ~ c√n): Q_{n,k} → (2/c²)sinh(c²/2), strictly above the infinite-width value 1 for c > 0; cumulative LRU energy S_{n,t} ~ √n · ∫₀^c̃ (2/x²)sinh(x²/2)dx, which for large c̃ behaves as c̃⁻³ exp(c̃²/2). This is the regime where Glorot initialization first ceases to be stable.
  • Supercritical regime (√n ≪ k,t): Q_{n,k} ~ (n/((k+1)(k+2)))·e^{nΨ(k/n)} and S_{n,t} ~ (n²/t³)·exp(nΨ(t/n)) with Ψ(x) = (1+x)log(1+x) − x. In the sublinear subregime k,t = o(n^{2/3}), this simplifies to Q_{n,k} ~ (n/k²)exp(k²/(2n)).
  • Numerical validation (Figure 2): Monte Carlo estimates from n=100 to n=2000 show tight agreement with the theoretical critical profile across c = t/√n ∈ [0,5]; the heavy-tailed signal distribution at large depth means error bars likely underestimate variability.
  • Real weights (Figure 3): Real Gaussian Q^ℝ_{n,k} consistently lies above the complex prediction, confirming Remark 2's lower-bound interpretation; the depth scale at which signals explode in the real case mirrors the complex case.

Authors & Credibility

The author co-authored a closely related precursor (Bar, Seleznova, et al., NeurIPS 2025 [4] on revisiting Glorot for long-range recurrences), which this paper substantially sharpens. The work is mathematically rigorous, leverages recent results (Dubach 2024) on random permutation commutator cycles, and connects to the established Tensor Programs / signal-propagation literature (Yang, Schoenholz, Poole, Hanin). Prior connection to the topic and use of established random-matrix techniques support credibility, though as a single-author preprint the work is not yet peer-reviewed.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Genuine and well-defined. Prior work (Bar et al. 2025) gave only crude lower bounds at t ≫ √n; this paper produces the first exact closed-form for finite n, identifies the √n threshold sharply, and extracts the full sinh(c²/2) profile. The reduction to commutator cycle counting is elegant and not previously applied in this signal-propagation context.
  • Rigor: High. Theorems are stated precisely; the proof is sketched in the main text and detailed in 27 pages with extensive appendices. The combinatorial identification with Hultman numbers is principled.
  • Reproducibility: Theory is fully reproducible from the formulas. Numerical experiments specify n values and 10⁵ Monte Carlo samples but the paper does not appear to release code; reproducing Figures 2–3 would require independent implementation but is straightforward.
  • Significance: Meaningful for the deep-learning theory community working on SSMs/LRUs and for practitioners puzzled by why Glorot empirically fails in long-range recurrent models. The qualitative gap (√n vs n) between recurrent and feedforward depth scales is the headline insight.
  • Red flags / caveats: (1) Restricted to linear recurrences—real LRUs/SSMs use selective gating and nonlinearities; the author flags this but offers no extension. (2) Main result requires complex Gaussian; the real-weights bound in Remark 2 is one-sided (lower bound only), so the real case is not fully solved. (3) Standard practical initializations for LRUs/SSMs are non-Gaussian (truncated/structured), which limits direct prescriptive use. (4) Results concern random initialization only—nothing about training-time stability or feature learning. (5) Heavy-tailed distributions at large depth mean reported Monte Carlo error bars may be optimistic.

Strengths

  • Single sharp result with a concrete threshold (√n) that practitioners can act on.
  • Exact, not asymptotic-only, finite-width formula—a rare degree of cleanliness in this literature.
  • Clean proof reduction connecting random matrix moments to a tractable combinatorial object (commutator cycles), opening a methodological avenue for related problems.
  • Honest framing: the paper explicitly states what infinite-width theory misses (that recurrence ≠ feedforward composition once depth reaches √n) and supplies precisely the regime where the distinction emerges.
  • Numerical experiments include both complex and real weights and span an order of magnitude in n.

Limitations

  • Linear recurrences only; nonlinear RNNs (LSTM, GRU) and gated SSM blocks (Mamba selectivity) are out of scope.
  • Complex Gaussian assumption is mathematically convenient but not how state-of-the-art LRUs/SSMs are actually initialized in practice (e.g., HiPPO/diagonal structured init).
  • Real-weights case is handled only via inequality (lower bound), not via a matching upper bound or full asymptotic profile.
  • Considers signal energies (second moments) only; higher-order fluctuations and the kernel regime are not characterized at finite width.
  • No analysis of training dynamics, feature learning, or how the finite-width corrections evolve under gradient descent.
  • No public code release referenced in the main text.

Relevance

与用户对 LLM 训练稳定性与可解释性的兴趣高度相关：本文从理论上精确刻画了为何 Mamba/LRU 等长序列状态空间模型在使用标准 Glorot 初始化时会出现信号爆炸（√n 深度即失稳），为现代长上下文模型设计稳定初始化、诊断训练崩溃以及理解前馈/递归架构本质差异提供了严格的理论锚点。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can the exact finite-width formula be extended to structured (HiPPO-style) or rescaled-Glorot initializations actually used in S4/Mamba/LRU?
  • Does the √n critical depth scale persist under nonlinear activations, or does saturation in tanh/GLU shift the threshold?
  • How do these finite-width corrections interact with training? Do gradients along the supercritical regime cause optimizer divergence, or does early-training feature learning push the effective spectral radius below 1?
  • Can a matching upper bound be proven for the real Gaussian case to close the gap with Remark 2's lower bound?
  • Beyond second moments, what is the finite-width distribution of ‖h^(t)‖²—and does the heavy-tailedness observed numerically have a closed-form characterization (e.g., log-normal-like)?
  • Is there a practical initialization rule that, given a target sequence length t, prescribes a width n such that t/√n stays in the subcritical regime?
