Bridging Generation and Training: A Systematic Review of Quality Issues in LLMs for Code

arXiv: 2605.05267v1

Date: 2026-05-06

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05267v1

一句话总结： 本文对114篇论文进行系统综述，建立双维分类体系（9类生成代码质量问题 × 2类训练数据质量问题），并通过Sankey图形化地揭示了18种训练数据缺陷向生成代码缺陷传播的具体因果机制。
TL;DR: A systematic review of 114 primary studies that constructs a unified taxonomy linking training data quality flaws (code-attribute and non-code-attribute defects) to generated code quality issues across nine dimensions, formalizing 18 propagation mechanisms and synthesizing detection/mitigation strategies across the LLM code generation lifecycle.

核心贡献

  • 双维分类体系： 将生成代码质量问题划分为9个维度（正确性、安全性、合规性、鲁棒性、可维护性、可理解性、效率、输出简洁性、杂项），将训练数据质量问题划分为代码属性缺陷和非代码属性缺陷两大类。
  • 因果传播框架： 归纳了18种从训练数据质量问题到生成代码质量问题的具体传播机制（10种直接映射 + 8种间接映射），并以Sankey图可视化呈现。
  • 检测与缓解综述： 系统梳理了跨数据、模型、生成三个生命周期阶段的检测技术（动态分析、静态分析、模型驱动检测）与缓解策略（数据清洗、模型对齐、生成时干预）。
  • 研究路线图： 指出未来需要数据溯源与定向遗忘、动态防污染基准、以及将软件工程标准嵌入模型对齐目标等三个关键方向。

Key Contributions

  • Dual-dimensional taxonomy: Nine dimensions of generated code quality issues (correctness, security, compliance, robustness, maintainability, understandability, efficiency, parsimony of output, miscellaneous) paired with a two-class taxonomy of training data quality issues (code-attribute and non-code-attribute).
  • Causal propagation framework: Eighteen specific propagation mechanisms (10 direct, 8 indirect) formalized from the literature, showing how training corpus flaws translate into output defects — e.g., deprecated API memorization causing compatibility failures, dataset imbalance causing representation bias.
  • Lifecycle-spanning synthesis of detection and mitigation: Detection methods classified into dynamic analysis, static analysis, and model-based detection at both code and dataset levels; mitigation strategies span data cleaning/filtering, data balancing, model fine-tuning/alignment, and generation-level interventions.
  • Research roadmap: Identifies three actionable future directions: data provenance and targeted unlearning, contamination-resistant dynamic benchmarks, and aligning reward functions with objective software engineering metrics.

Methodology

The review follows established software engineering systematic literature review (SLR) guidelines through seven structured stages: research question definition, search string design, multi-database search (IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital Library, SpringerLink, ScienceDirect, arXiv), backward/forward snowballing, consolidation and de-duplication, inclusion/exclusion screening, and quality assessment.

Inclusion criteria required studies to investigate LLMs for code generation/completion/repair, focus on quality dimensions of generated code or training corpora, provide empirically grounded findings, and meet publication standards. Studies were excluded for domain mismatch, methodological opacity, or language inaccessibility.

Quality assessment used an 8-criterion rubric scored by three independent reviewers (maximum 16 points); studies below 10 were excluded. The final corpus is 114 primary studies spanning Q3 2021 through Q1 2026, with rapid growth beginning in late 2023.

Data synthesis employed iterative qualitative coding: open coding to label phenomena, axial coding to group into conceptual categories, and selective coding to align with five predefined research questions (RQ1–RQ5). Quantitative frequency aggregation complemented the qualitative analysis.

The taxonomy is constructed by synthesizing and reconciling prior fragmented classification schemes. Notably, the authors explicitly exclude vaguely defined concepts such as "hallucination" and "code smells" as independent dimensions, instead mapping their valid underlying anomalies into the established nine dimensions to avoid ambiguity and ensure consistency.

Results & Highlights

  • Nine quality dimensions with unequal coverage: Correctness dominates (52 studies), followed by security (24), maintainability (22), efficiency (19), and compliance (13). Understandability (11), parsimony of output (11), and robustness (10) remain underexplored due to limited standardized benchmarks.
  • Training data issues are compounding: Distribution imbalance (17 studies), redundancy (13), and low-value data (10) are the leading non-code attribute concerns. These operate through indirect mechanisms — entropy collapse, representation bias, and semantic drift — that are more insidious than direct defect memorization.
  • 18 propagation mechanisms: Direct mappings include deprecated API memorization causing compatibility/execution failures, insecure coding practices causing vulnerable code generation, and comment-code inconsistency causing hallucinated implementations. Indirect mappings include dataset imbalance causing biased code generation, benchmark contamination causing inflated evaluation, and excessive synthetic data causing distribution drift.
  • Detection is maturing: The field has moved from isolated static/rule-based checking toward hybrid, semantic-aware pipelines combining dynamic execution, model-driven evaluators, and provenance tracking.
  • Mitigation is shifting from reactive to proactive: Traditional post-generation filtering is being augmented by LLM-driven dataset cleaning, reward-based optimization integrating engineering metrics, and iterative self-reflection during generation. Human-in-the-loop validation remains necessary for security-critical and legal edge cases.
  • Key insight on indirect mappings: The authors argue that indirect, distributional flaws — "safe" but unbalanced data — can be a more profound driver of generation failures than explicitly dirty data, because they pull gradient updates into optimization local minima.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: The primary novelty is the causal framing — explicitly linking training data defects to generated code defects through formalized propagation mechanisms — rather than treating them as separate research tracks. The 18-mechanism Sankey diagram is a concrete artifact that prior surveys lack. However, the individual taxonomies on each side draw heavily on prior work; the synthesis is the contribution rather than new empirical findings.

Rigor: The SLR methodology is well-documented with transparent inclusion/exclusion criteria, an 8-criterion quality rubric, and inter-rater agreement resolution. The 114-paper corpus is appropriately scoped. One concern is that the causal mappings (RQ3) are largely inferred from correlational literature rather than experimentally verified causal relationships; the paper does not claim otherwise, but the "causal framework" label somewhat overstates what the evidence directly supports.

Reproducibility: The repository is publicly available at https://github.com/SYSUSELab/From-Data-to-Code. Search strings and inclusion/exclusion criteria are documented. Quality scores are reported. This is above average for survey papers.

Significance: High. The paper addresses a real and acknowledged gap: the research community tends to treat dataset curation and generation quality evaluation as separate workstreams with no systematic bridge. The unified taxonomy and propagation framework provide actionable guidance for both practitioners designing data pipelines and researchers identifying intervention points.

Red flags: The review window ends in Q1 2026 (114 papers), which is appropriate. However, the rapid pace of the field means some mechanisms (e.g., those involving RLHF, agentic pipelines) may be underrepresented in the current corpus. The finding that non-code distributional issues are "equally or more important" than code-level defects is compelling but would benefit from quantitative causal evidence rather than qualitative synthesis alone.

Strengths

  • First systematic review to explicitly bridge training data quality and generated code quality through a causal framework, filling a recognized gap in the literature.
  • Rigorous SLR methodology with transparent criteria, three-reviewer quality assessment, and public repository.
  • The dual-dimensional taxonomy cleanly disambiguates previously conflated concepts (e.g., security vs. robustness, "hallucination" absorbed into correctness).
  • The Sankey diagram visualization of 18 propagation mechanisms is a practically useful artifact for identifying intervention points.
  • The discussion of indirect propagation mechanisms (distributional flaws as more insidious than explicit defects) is a genuinely insightful finding.
  • Temporal analysis showing explosive growth from 2023 onward accurately reflects the field's trajectory.

Limitations

  • The causal propagation framework is synthesized from correlational literature; few of the 114 studies perform controlled experiments isolating training data manipulations and measuring downstream generation quality changes. The word "causal" in the framing is aspirational.
  • The 50-page length and broad scope may limit depth on any individual mechanism; practitioners seeking actionable guidance on a specific issue (e.g., benchmark contamination) must still consult primary sources.
  • Studies are weighted equally in the taxonomy frequency counts, regardless of sample size or experimental rigor — a study with 10 subjects counts the same as a large-scale empirical study.
  • Coverage of non-English programming contexts and low-resource languages is acknowledged as underrepresented in the literature itself, limiting generalizability of findings.
  • The repository captures papers through Q1 2026; the field is moving fast enough that the survey may require significant updates within 12–18 months.

Relevance

本文对LLM可靠性研究具有直接意义：它系统揭示了训练数据质量（如数据分布偏差、基准污染）如何通过具体机制影响模型的生成可靠性，为理解LLM输出失控的根因提供了数据层面的视角，也为训练稳定性和数据治理提供了结构化参考框架。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can the 18 propagation mechanisms be operationalized into quantitative metrics that allow researchers to predict, before training, which dataset flaws are most likely to induce specific generation failures?
  2. The paper argues indirect distributional flaws are more insidious than direct code defects — can this be tested with controlled ablation studies that isolate distribution imbalance while holding code-level quality constant?
  3. How do the identified mechanisms interact with modern post-training techniques (RLHF, DPO, constitutional AI)? Do alignment procedures reliably suppress direct memorization of training defects, or do they merely mask them?
  4. For benchmark contamination specifically: what is the empirical magnitude of pass@k inflation across current frontier models, and are there contamination-resistant benchmarks already achieving traction?
  5. The roadmap calls for "targeted unlearning" — what is the current state of machine unlearning for code LLMs, and are influence-function-based methods computationally feasible at the scale of modern code corpora?
