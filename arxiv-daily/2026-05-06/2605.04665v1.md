> **一句话总结：** 本文发现大语言模型在语义等价的改写提示下会系统性地"跑偏格式"——约78%的封闭式变体响应完全丢失正确答案标签，并提出 PARACONSIST 基准（900条提示，5个模型）和语义一致性评分（SCS）来量化这一失效模式，揭示任务结构比模型家族更能预测格式崩溃风险。

> **TL;DR:** Content-preserving paraphrases of user prompts systematically cause LLMs to abandon the requested output format (e.g., a label or choice token) and drift into conversational prose—even at temperature zero. The authors introduce PARACONSIST, a 900-prompt benchmark spanning four task types and five 2025-era commercial models, and show that ~78% of closed-form variant responses contain no answer-set token at all, with task structure (not model identity) being the dominant predictor of this "output-mode collapse."

## 核心贡献

- **命名并量化"提示变体输出模式崩溃"**：在五个模型和四类任务中，内容保留的改写提示频繁导致模型放弃所要求的输出格式，转而输出对话式散文，即使在温度为零的确定性采样下也如此。
- **发布 PARACONSIST 基准**：包含 150 条基础查询（每条配 5 个词法/句法/语义扩展变体），共 900 条提示，覆盖 ARC-Challenge、影评情感分析、AG News 分类和 XSum 摘要四类任务。
- **提出语义一致性评分（SCS）**：将答案一致性（AC，精确匹配率）、语义相似度（SS，Sentence-BERT余弦）和长度稳定性（CS）以固定权重（0.5/0.3/0.2）组合为统一诊断指标，并逐组件单独报告以便重新解读。
- **揭示任务结构是崩溃的主导预测变量**：多选 QA（SCS 0.439）与分类（SCS 0.269）之间的差距超过五个模型之间的总体差距，Kruskal-Wallis 检验显示任务类型效应极显著（H=73.95，p<10⁻¹⁵）。
- **暴露精确匹配评估管线的隐患**：约 78% 的封闭式变体响应根本不含答案集标记，现有单提示精确匹配评估会系统性地高估模型可靠性。

## Key Contributions

- **Identification and quantification of prompt-variant output-mode collapse**: Across all five models and four task types, content-preserving rewrites consistently push models to return prose instead of the requested label or discrete token, even under deterministic (T=0) decoding.
- **PARACONSIST benchmark release**: 900 prompts (150 base queries × 6 phrasings) with three variant types—lexical substitution, syntactic restructuring, semantic expansion—generated via GPT-4o-mini, covering MC-QA, sentiment, classification, and summarization.
- **Semantic Consistency Score (SCS)**: A three-component metric (AC=0.5, SS=0.3, CS=0.2) that makes response-mode failures visible alongside answer accuracy; all components are reported separately to allow reader re-weighting.
- **Task structure as dominant collapse predictor**: The pooled per-task AC ordering (MC-QA 0.292 > Classification 0.240 > Sentiment 0.136) and the SCS ranking both show that what you ask the model to do matters more than which model family you use.
- **Dataset, per-call traces, and analysis scripts** released as anonymized supplementary material for bit-level replay of all reported statistics.

## Methodology

**Benchmark construction.** The authors sample 150 base queries from four established datasets: ARC-Challenge (70 MC-QA items), movie review excerpts (30 sentiment), AG News (30 classification), XSum (20 summarization). For each base item, GPT-4o-mini generates five content-preserving prompt variants via three transformation types: (1) lexical substitution (synonym replacement), (2) syntactic restructuring (e.g., active-to-passive voice), and (3) semantic expansion (contextual elaboration without changing the query intent). The task scaffold (response-mode cue) may change salience across variants, deliberately testing whether the interface contract survives rewriting of the user payload.

**Models evaluated.** Five compact 2025-era commercial API models: GPT-4.1-mini and GPT-4o-mini (OpenAI), Claude Haiku 4.5 and Claude Sonnet 4.5 (Anthropic), Gemini 2.5 Flash (Google). All inference is run at temperature=0.0 via the LiteLLM proxy; 4,500 total API calls.

**Metrics.** The Semantic Consistency Score (SCS) aggregates three components:
- *Answer Consistency (AC)*: exact whole-word match rate of the ground-truth label appearing anywhere in the output across prompt variants.
- *Semantic Similarity (SS)*: mean pairwise sentence-BERT cosine similarity across variant outputs.
- *Length Stability (CS)*: 1 − σ(len)/μ(len), a coefficient of variation inversion indicating how stable output length is across variants.

SCS is a fixed-weight softmax composite (AC:SS:CS = 0.5:0.3:0.2). The authors also report a Flip Rate (percentage of variants whose answer differs from the original-prompt answer) and a bucket decomposition distinguishing "label-in-prose" (recoverable) from "no-label prose drift" (genuine collapse).

**Statistical testing.** Kruskal-Wallis tests are used for omnibus comparisons across models and task types, followed by pairwise Mann-Whitney U tests with correction.

## Results & Highlights

- **Overall SCS range: 0.256–0.411** across five models, indicating broadly moderate-to-low consistency even under deterministic sampling.
- **78% genuine collapse rate**: Among closed-form (MC-QA, classification, sentiment) variant responses pooled across all models, ~78% contain no answer-set token at all; only ~22% embed the correct label inside a longer prose response.
- **Model ranking (SCS):** Claude Sonnet 4.5 (0.411) > Claude Haiku 4.5 (0.344) > GPT-4o-mini (0.285) > GPT-4.1-mini (0.262) > Gemini 2.5 Flash (0.256). Gemini achieves the best Length Stability (CS=0.749) but the worst Answer Consistency (AC=0.060), explaining its bottom SCS rank.
- **Task ranking (mean SCS):** MC-QA (0.439) > Classification (0.269) > Sentiment (0.253) > Summarization (0.174). The constrained answer space of MC-QA anchors outputs more reliably than free-form labels.
- **Cross-family vs. within-family differences:** Pairwise Mann-Whitney U tests show significant differences between OpenAI and Anthropic families, but not within families (e.g., Claude Haiku vs. Sonnet: p=0.574; GPT-4.1-mini vs. GPT-4o-mini: p=0.627). Gemini–Anthropic gaps are also non-significant on SCS.
- **Effect size:** Cohen's d between best (Claude Sonnet 4.5) and worst (Gemini 2.5 Flash) model is ~0.71 (medium-to-large), carried primarily by the AC component.
- **Task-type effect dominates model-family effect:** Kruskal-Wallis H for task type = 73.95 (p<10⁻¹⁵) vs. H for model = 18.80 (p<0.001); the task-type effect is ~4× larger in test statistic magnitude.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The phenomenon of surface-form sensitivity in LLMs is well-studied (Sclar et al. 2023, Mizrahi et al. 2024, Raj et al. 2022). The specific framing of "output-mode collapse" (format drift vs. answer drift) is a meaningful conceptual refinement, but the benchmark scale (150 base queries, 5 models) is considerably smaller than directly comparable prior work such as Mizrahi et al. (5K paraphrases, 20 models, 39 tasks). The contribution is incremental rather than transformative.

**Rigor:** The statistical methodology is appropriate (non-parametric tests given small samples, effect size reported). However, the dataset is explicitly unbalanced (70 MC-QA vs. 20 Summarization items), and the authors acknowledge this limits aggregate interpretation. The SCS weighting (0.5/0.3/0.2) is pre-specified and fixed rather than validated or learned, which is an honest design choice but introduces arbitrariness that the authors partially address by reporting components separately.

**Reproducibility:** Commendably high intent: bit-level replay promised via anonymized supplementary release. T=0 deterministic sampling is sound. However, using GPT-4o-mini (an evaluated model) as the paraphrase generator is a methodological confound that is acknowledged but not quantified. The "anonymized" release status at submission time means independent verification is pending.

**Significance:** The finding that ~78% of closed-form variant responses exhibit genuine format collapse is striking and practically important for evaluation pipeline design. The recommendation to track response-mode preservation as a first-class reliability target is actionable. However, the causal mechanism (why task structure dominates) is not investigated, limiting prescriptive guidance for mitigation.

**Red flags:**
- The benchmark is heavily skewed toward MC-QA (70/150 items), making task-stratified claims partly driven by one task type.
- Paraphrase generation and model evaluation share the same model (GPT-4o-mini), creating a potential in-group advantage or bias that is not disentangled.
- All findings are at T=0; the authors correctly note non-zero temperatures would amplify inconsistencies, but this leaves the most practically relevant deployment regime (T>0) untested.
- The paper lacks any mitigation experiment (e.g., system prompts, constrained decoding) — it is purely diagnostic.
- The "2025-era" claim for model versions (Claude Haiku/Sonnet 4.5, GPT-4.1-mini) may date quickly.

## Strengths

- Clearly defined and practically motivated failure mode (output-mode collapse distinct from answer drift).
- Deterministic evaluation protocol (T=0) isolates the prompt-variant effect from stochastic sampling noise.
- Multi-component SCS is more informative than a single aggregate, and reporting components separately is an honest design choice.
- The bucket decomposition (label-in-prose vs. no-label drift) is a simple, interpretable diagnostic that goes beyond scalar metrics.
- Dataset, traces, and scripts promised for release, supporting reproducibility.
- Appropriate caution in the conclusions: "observational evidence" rather than "causal estimate."

## Limitations

- Small scale: 150 base queries and 5 compact API models. Larger-capability models (e.g., GPT-4o full, Claude Opus) are absent, limiting generalizability of cross-family rankings.
- Unbalanced task distribution (70 MC-QA dominates) undermines clean cross-task comparisons.
- Single paraphrase generator (GPT-4o-mini) with no ablation over generator choice or human-written variants.
- No mitigation strategies tested; purely diagnostic contribution.
- All runs at T=0 only, leaving stochastic regime unexplored.
- Whole-word exact match for AC can over-credit incidental label mentions in prose (e.g., a model saying "the answer is NOT A" would be credited if A is the correct label).
- SCS weight profile is arbitrary and not validated against human judgments of consistency quality.
- The "anonymized supplementary material" availability at submission time means claims cannot yet be fully independently verified.

## Relevance

本文与 LLM 可靠性和鲁棒性研究直接相关：它揭示了在语义等价输入下模型输出格式（而非仅答案内容）崩溃的系统性缺陷，对依赖精确匹配评估管线的 LLM 部署场景具有实际警示意义；同时，任务结构比模型家族更能预测格式崩溃这一发现，为 LLM 评估方法论和训练目标设计提供了具体的改进方向。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. What prompt engineering interventions (e.g., explicit format constraints in system prompts, constrained decoding, few-shot format exemplars) most effectively suppress output-mode collapse, and do they trade off against answer accuracy?
2. Does the cross-family ranking (Anthropic > OpenAI > Gemini on SCS) hold at larger capability tiers (e.g., Claude Opus, GPT-4o full, Gemini 1.5 Pro), or is this an artifact of the compact-model regime?
3. How much of the "genuine collapse" (~78% no-label responses) is semantically recoverable by a downstream answer extractor (e.g., LLM-based parsing), and what does the recoverable fraction tell us about the real-world impact on evaluation pipeline accuracy?
4. Is output-mode collapse correlated with instruction-following capability as measured by established benchmarks (e.g., IFEval), and if so, can RLHF/RLAIF alignment data targeting format adherence reduce collapse rates?
5. Would using human-written paraphrases rather than GPT-4o-mini-generated ones change the collapse rate distribution, and how much does generator identity shape the variant difficulty profile?
6. The paper finds task structure dominates — but is this because of the answer-space constraint (MC-QA has A/B/C/D), the training data distribution of each task type, or the implicit format signals in the benchmark source prompts? Disentangling these would sharpen the prescriptive value of the finding.
