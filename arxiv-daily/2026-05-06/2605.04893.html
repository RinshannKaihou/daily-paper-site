Self-Attention as Transport: Limits of Symmetric Spectral Diagnostics

arXiv: 2605.04893v1

Date: 2026-05-07

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04893v1

一句话总结： 该论文将注意力头形式化为度归一化的二部传输算子，证明所有"对称谱诊断"（如 LLM-Check、EigenTrack、LapEigvals 中使用的奇异值/对称分量统计）都是结构性"方向盲"的（无法区分 M 与 M^T），由此提出由电导 φ（容量轴，双边 Cheeger 不等式）和反对称系数 G（方向轴）构成的双轴诊断系统，并在长度受控 AUROC 下于至多 8B 参数模型上实现 0.62–0.84 的可解释幻觉检测信号。
TL;DR: Models attention heads as degree-normalized bipartite transport operators and proves a structural "orientation blindness" theorem: every transpose-invariant spectral diagnostic (singular values, symmetric-component spectra, bipartite Laplacian) cannot distinguish information-flow direction. The authors complement conductance φ (capacity, bounded by Cheeger's inequality) with an asymmetry coefficient G (direction) and derive a closed-form bipartite-Cheeger landscape for uniform causal attention (n-independent floor φ ≥ 1/5 at t*/n ≈ 0.32). Under length-controlled AUROC evaluation on HaluEval/MedHallu/TruthfulQA across GPT-2/BERT/Pythia/Flan-T5/LLaMA-8B, transport features retain 0.62–0.84 LC-AUROC with a falsifiable polarity reversal between bottleneck-dominated (HaluEval) and diffuse-dominated (MedHallu) datasets.

核心贡献

  • 传输算子分解：将注意力矩阵形式化为度归一化二部算子 M = D_Q^{-1/2} B D_K^{-1/2}，并按 Hilbert–Schmidt 内积正交分解为对称分量 M_sym（容量）与反对称分量 M_asym（方向），并给出"度充分性定理"刻画耦合结构何时超越度异质性贡献信号。
  • 方向盲定理（Theorem 4 + Proposition 6）：证明任何依赖于奇异值或 M_sym 谱的 Lipschitz 泛函都满足 F(M)=F(M^T)，因此 LLM-Check/EigenTrack/LapEigvals 等方法在结构上无法检测路由方向；定量逆命题给出 |F(M)−F(M^T)| ≤ 2L·‖M_asym‖_F，将 G 确立为方向敏感性的唯一控制参数。
  • 闭式 Cheeger 景观：对均匀因果注意力推导 φ(S_t) ≥ u(t)/(2+u(t))（Theorem 15），证明 n 无关下界 φ ≥ 1/5 与最差切位置 t*/n ≈ 0.32（Theorem 16），而窗口注意力以 O(w/n) 击穿此下界——失败模式是"形状不同"而非"数值不同"，使刺穿 1/5 下界的 head 比例成为种群级架构特征。
  • 长度受控评估协议（LC-AUROC）：识别三种长度暴露通道（hidden-state/attention/output）并提出按响应长度分箱后线性残差化的双重控制 AUROC，揭示原始 AUROC 被长度混淆抬高至多 0.28 个点。
  • 极性反转的可证伪预测：理论预言并实证 HaluEval 上幻觉聚集于低电导（瓶颈）三分位，MedHallu 上聚集于高电导（弥散）三分位，同一谱诊断在两数据集呈现相反极性，是双边诊断的直接落地。

Key Contributions

  • Transport decomposition: Formalizes attention heads as degree-normalized bipartite operators M = D_Q^{-1/2} B D_K^{-1/2} with an orthogonal symmetric/antisymmetric split under the Hilbert–Schmidt inner product; a degree-sufficiency theorem quantifies when coupling structure contributes beyond degree heterogeneity (bound √κ − 1).
  • Orientation-blindness theorem (Theorem 4 + Prop. 6): Proves every Lipschitz functional depending only on singular values or on the symmetric component is transpose-invariant, hence structurally blind to routing direction; a quantitative converse |F(M)−F(M^T)| ≤ 2L·‖M_asym‖_F establishes G = ‖M_asym‖_F/(‖M‖_F+ε) as the unique control parameter for direction. This places a precise limit on what LLM-Check, EigenTrack, and LapEigvals can resolve.
  • Closed-form bipartite-Cheeger landscape: Derives φ(S_t) ≥ u(t)/(2+u(t)) for uniform causal attention with u(t)=H_n−H_t (Theorem 15), yielding n-independent floor φ ≥ 1/5 at t*/n ≈ 0.32 (Theorem 16); window attention pierces the floor as O(w/n). Failure modes are shape-different, not value-different, making the population fraction of heads piercing 1/5 an architectural signature (36–42% GPT-2, 51–70% Pythia-160M, 79–82% Flan-T5-decoder).
  • Length-controlled AUROC (LC-AUROC): Identifies three confounding channels (hidden-state, attention, output) and introduces a bin-stratified, within-bin OLS-residualized, pair-weighted AUROC; raw AUROC is inflated by up to 0.28 points by length confounding.
  • Falsifiable polarity prediction: Theory predicts (and empirics confirm) opposite-sign hallucination signal between bottleneck-dominated (HaluEval) and diffuse-dominated (MedHallu) benchmarks—a single spectral diagnostic with sign-reversed correspondence to ground truth across datasets.

Methodology

The authors model each attention head as a row-stochastic matrix B and form the degree-normalized bipartite operator M, whose Hermitian dilation H(M) is symmetric and whose singular values give the bipartite SVD spectrum. Conductance φ is estimated via a spectral sweep on the second singular vector of M (giving φ̂), and the spectral gap σ_2 follows directly. The asymmetry coefficient G(M) = ‖M_asym‖_F/(‖M‖_F+ε) is computed as the normalized Frobenius distance to the symmetric subspace. They derive closed-form row-sum/column-sum identities for several causal masks (uniform, window, exponential decay, RoPE-style decay) and prove monotone-decay lower bounds (Prop. 9, Cor. 10: G ≥ (1−e^{−α})/2 for exponential decay).

Empirical evaluation uses three benchmarks (HaluEval n=10K, MedHallu n=1K, TruthfulQA n=817) on five model families (GPT-2, BERT, Pythia 70M–1.4B, Flan-T5 encoder/decoder, LLaMA 3.1 8B). Features are aggregated across all (sample, layer, head) tuples using CVaR_25/CVaR_75 tail statistics and standard deviation rather than per-head selection. Length-controlled AUROC partitions samples into adaptive equal-frequency response-length bins, residualizes scores via OLS within bin against length, and pair-weights bin AUROCs. Matched-null baselines (degree-preserving and entropy-matched) separate learned structure from finite-size estimator artifacts. Comparison baselines include LLM-Check (attn/hidden/logit branches) and EigenTrack (supervised, 5-fold CV with logistic regression).

Results & Highlights

  • Transport features retain interpretable signal under length control: LC-AUROC 0.62–0.84 across all 15 model–dataset combinations, with response-length baseline collapsing to ≈0.50 by construction.
  • Polarity reversal empirically observed: HaluEval hallucinations cluster in low-OC tercile (mean 47.5% vs 33% expected), MedHallu in high-OC tercile (45.1%), Cohen's d = −2.6 vs +0.3 in (φ̂, σ_2) space (Fig. 4).
  • Architectural signature confirmed: fraction of heads piercing 1/5 Cheeger floor differs sharply across architectures (36% GPT-2, 65% Pythia, 79% Flan-T5 on HaluEval; binomial SE ≈ 0.6 pp at n=7200).
  • Asymmetry coefficient G is empirically sparse (0.53–0.63 LC-AUROC) but produces two interpretable exceptions: Flan-T5 decoder (0.78) via cross-attention grounding and Pythia-160M (0.82) with reversed polarity (factual G > hallucinated G), consistent with RoPE-induced positional decay.
  • Scaling study on Pythia 70M–1.4B + LLaMA 8B: dominant aggregation crosses over from location (mean) at 70M to spread (std) at 1.4B; SVM weights flip sign in CVaR tail space, indicating a transition from uniform conductance depression to routing heterogeneity at scale.
  • Degree-preserving null analysis: φ's discriminative power is largely accounted for by degree distribution (z-AUROC 0.52–0.57), while σ_2 retains 0.72–0.80 z-AUROC, validating that σ_2 captures coupling beyond degrees.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: The orientation-blindness framing is conceptually clean and genuinely useful—it provides a unified structural critique of LLM-Check/EigenTrack/LapEigvals that does not depend on benchmark engineering. The closed-form bipartite-Cheeger landscape for uniform causal attention is a fresh mechanism. However, much of the spectral machinery (Cheeger inequality, bipartite SVD, Hermitian dilation, Chung's directed-graph Cheeger) is mature; the contribution is principled application rather than new mathematics.

Rigor: The theoretical statements are clean, the proofs sketched in the main text and elaborated in supplementary material, and the evaluation protocol (LC-AUROC, matched nulls, degree-preserving nulls, ANOVA across length quartiles) is unusually careful for the hallucination-detection literature. The authors explicitly distinguish object (φ, NP-hard) from estimator (φ̂, spectral sweep).

Reproducibility: Concrete model lists, dataset sizes, and aggregation specifications are provided. However, the read pages do not surface a code/data release; the empirical pipeline (length filter n ≥ 32, exact temporal-cut sweep, bootstrap CIs over 15 model–dataset combos) is non-trivial to reimplement from prose alone. The 50-sample subsampling for HaluEval per model raises variance concerns even with bootstrap CIs.

Significance: For practitioners, the headline LC-AUROC of 0.62–0.84 is competitive but not overwhelming, especially given that LLM-Check hidden-state features achieve best/tied-best in 7 of 11 applicable configurations (0.62–0.72). The paper's primary value is diagnostic decomposition (the how of routing failure: bottleneck vs diffuse vs knowledge-gap), not raw detection performance—a contribution the authors are explicit about.

Red flags:

  • The "polarity reversal" prediction is corroborated, but the paper acknowledges TruthfulQA returns near-chance across all features (0.50–0.57), which the authors interpret as consistent with specificity (knowledge-gap failures should produce no routing pathology); this could equally be a finite-size limitation at median 10 tokens, and the authors note this confound cannot be fully distinguished.
  • The cross-architecture signal of G is sparse, and Pythia's "reversed polarity" interpretation (RoPE decay hypothesis) is admitted as untested; a clean ablation would compare RoPE vs absolute embeddings at matched capacity.
  • Within-dataset polarity is consistent across length bins in only ~54% of experiments, weakening operational deployability without per-config calibration.
  • The framing language ("we prove", "structurally", "two-axis diagnostic") is occasionally promotional given that the empirical detection ranking remains close to existing baselines.

Strengths

  • Mathematically principled: the orientation-blindness theorem is a genuine structural result that re-frames an entire class of spectral hallucination detectors.
  • Falsifiable predictions (polarity reversal, 1/5 Cheeger floor, t*/n ≈ 0.32) tied to closed-form architectural benchmarks rather than post-hoc fits.
  • Unusually rigorous evaluation methodology: LC-AUROC, matched nulls, degree-preserving nulls, scaling validation across 70M–8B parameters.
  • Honest scoping of contributions: explicit that this is "diagnostic characterization, not detection ranking."
  • Bridges spectral graph theory (Cheeger, Chung directed Cheeger) with attention analysis in a way that opens natural extensions (MoE routing, magnetic Laplacians).

Limitations

  • Detection performance does not categorically dominate baselines; the framework's value is interpretive rather than operational.
  • Diffuse, ungrounded attention (structurally healthy but semantically wrong routing) is undetectable by transport diagnostics alone—the bottleneck/diffuse/knowledge-gap partition is not claimed exhaustive.
  • Asymmetry coefficient G is empirically sparse on most decoder-only architectures, limiting its practical leverage.
  • Evaluation is restricted to ≤8B parameter models and three benchmarks; modern frontier-scale models, mixture-of-experts, sliding-window, and grouped-query architectures (beyond LLaMA-8B GQA) are untested.
  • Causal attribution is not established: φ correlates with hallucination via degree heterogeneity, but whether degree concentration causes hallucination or merely co-occurs remains open.
  • Requires access to attention tensors—not applicable to API-only models.

Relevance

高度相关：论文直接针对 LLM 可解释性与可靠性的核心问题（幻觉检测的内部机制诊断），并以严格的谱图论方法论证现有"对称谱方法"的结构性缺陷，这种"什么可被诊断、什么不可"的方向盲定理对训练稳定性分析（如 attention entropy collapse、rank loss）也提供了形式化工具。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the framework extend to mixture-of-experts (MoE) token-to-expert dispatch, where the routing operator is rectangular and lacks causal symmetry?
  • Can a directional spectral theory (e.g., magnetic Laplacian, U(1)-phase Cheeger inequalities) recover M_asym at the spectral level rather than as a residual norm?
  • Does the closed-form Cheeger landscape generalize to RoPE-style decay and sliding-window attention with predictable thresholds, enabling architecture-aware hallucination prior?
  • How does the bottleneck/diffuse polarity reversal interact with RLHF, instruction-tuning, or activation patching interventions—does fine-tuning shift architectural signatures predictably?
  • Could G-based diagnostics integrate with training stability monitoring (attention entropy collapse, rank loss) to predict instability before loss divergence?
  • Is there a per-sample failure taxonomy (factual error vs context failure vs semantic inconsistency) that would let one validate which transport signal corresponds to which generation-time failure type?
  • The "fraction of heads piercing 1/5 floor" as architectural signature is striking—does this fraction predict downstream task performance or hallucination rate at training time?
