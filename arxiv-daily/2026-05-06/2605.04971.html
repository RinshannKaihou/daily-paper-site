Why Geometric Continuity Emerges in Deep Neural Networks: Residual Connections and Rotational Symmetry Breaking

arXiv: 2605.04971v1

Date: 2026-05-06

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.04971v1

一句话总结： 通过对玩具 MLP 和小型 Transformer 的消融实验，作者证明深度网络中相邻层权重的"几何连续性"（主奇异向量同向）由两个必要条件共同造成——残差连接提供跨层梯度一致性，以及对称破缺非线性（如 ReLU/GELU）将各层钉在同一坐标系中，防止 SO(d) 旋转漂移；关键是采用旋转保持的非线性（径向激活）会破坏连续性，从而把"对称破缺"而非"非线性本身"确立为活性成分。
TL;DR: This paper provides a mechanistic explanation for why principal singular vectors of adjacent weight matrices align in trained deep networks. Through controlled MLP ablations on MNIST and validation on small transformers / pretrained Llama-3.1-8B, the authors isolate two necessary conditions: (1) residual connections create gradient coherence across layers (so weight updates point in similar directions), and (2) symmetry-breaking nonlinearities (ReLU/GELU/SiLU but NOT a rotation-equivariant radial activation) lock all layers to a shared coordinate frame, preventing SO(d)-orbit rotation drift. Activation concentrates continuity in v_1 while LayerNorm distributes it across multiple directions. In transformers, continuity is projection-specific: Q/K/Gate/Up develop input-space (v_1) continuity, O/Down develop output-space (u_1) continuity, and V—lacking adjacent nonlinearity—shows only weak continuity. Worth reading if you care about layer pruning, weight sharing, or theoretical foundations of why deep residual networks compose so smoothly.

核心贡献

  • 机理性解释：首次给出深度残差网络中"几何连续性"（相邻层主奇异向量余弦相似度高）出现的双必要条件——梯度相干性 + 对称破缺，而非以往工作仅识别的残差连接单一条件。
  • 关键反事实：采用旋转等变的径向激活 σ_rad(x)=x·tanh(‖x‖) 时，连续性从 0.96（ReLU）骤降至 0.225，与无激活的 0.217 几乎相同——证明"非线性本身"不是因，"对称破缺"才是。
  • 激活 vs LayerNorm 的角色分工：激活将连续性集中到主方向 v_1（σ²-WA = 0.249），LayerNorm 则将其均摊到多个奇异方向（σ²-WA = 0.303）；二者共同存在时激活模式占主导。
  • Transformer 的投影特异性：Q/K/Gate/Up（读残差流）发展 v_1 输入空间连续性；O/Down（写残差流）发展 u_1 输出空间连续性；V（无邻近非线性）连续性低——与 Llama-3.1-8B 等 1.5B-70B 多模型的实测一致。
  • 梯度先于权重：第一次反向传播时梯度 v_1 连续性已达 0.95，而权重 v_1 从 0.04 缓慢上升到 0.96，证实"权重连续性是累积梯度的指纹"。

Key Contributions

  • Mechanistic decomposition of geometric continuity into two necessary conditions (residual + symmetry-breaking), rather than the single residual condition identified in prior work (Li & Papyan 2023).
  • A clean rotation-equivariant counterfactual: by replacing ReLU with the radial activation x·tanh(‖x‖)—nonlinear but SO(d)-preserving—they show continuity collapses to 0.225 (vs 0.96 for ReLU, 0.217 for no activation), pinpointing symmetry breaking as the active ingredient.
  • Two-phase dynamics (formation vs retention): residual + activation are both required; with small initialization (σ=0.0001), Res+None reaches v_1 continuity ≈ 0.99 at epoch 1 then collapses to 0.18 by epoch 15, while Res+ReLU stably retains > 0.94 throughout.
  • Distinct roles for activation and normalization: activation concentrates continuity in v_1 (σ²-WA ≈ 0.25), LayerNorm distributes it across the spectrum (σ²-WA ≈ 0.30); when combined the activation pattern dominates.
  • Projection-specific continuity in transformers, validated across pretrained Llama-3.1-8B and 5 additional models (1.5B–70B), connecting architectural read/write structure to where continuity emerges in input vs output space.
  • Practical implications for layer pruning, cross-layer parameter sharing, and orbit-aware model merging are sketched as future directions.

Methodology

The authors design controlled ablations on a 16-layer residual MLP (D=256, no bias, Kaiming init) trained on MNIST for 20 epochs with Adam. The architecture allows independent removal of residual connections (Res/NoRes), swapping activation functions (ReLU, GELU, SiLU, Tanh, Radial, None), and adding LayerNorm. They measure three quantities per layer pair: (i) v_1 cross-layer cosine similarity (input-space), (ii) u_1 cross-layer cosine similarity (output-space), and (iii) σ²-weighted alignment (analogous to PCA explained variance) capturing alignment across all singular directions, not just the leading one. They additionally track the cumulative gradient G̃_l = Σ_t G_l^(t) and a transfer gap Δ_GW measuring continuity created in cumulative gradient vs continuity retained in the weight—a ~200× separation between Res+ReLU (Δ_GW=0.003) and Res+None/Res+Radial (Δ_GW≈0.64) is the cleanest evidence that symmetry breaking, not nonlinearity, anchors gradient direction into weights. Transformer experiments use a small Llama-style model (~34M, 8 layers, SiLU+RMSNorm) on WikiText-103 and a pretrained Llama-3.1-8B (32 layers, d=4096) for projection-specific analysis.

Results & Highlights

  • Res+ReLU: weight v_1 = 0.964, accuracy = 0.977; Res+None: v_1 = 0.217, accuracy = 0.912; NoRes+ReLU: v_1 = 0.378, accuracy = 0.848.
  • Res+Radial (rotation-preserving nonlinearity): v_1 = 0.225, accuracy = 0.929—nearly identical continuity to no-activation despite being nonlinear.
  • Two-phase dynamics: under σ=0.0001 init, Res+None reaches v_1 ≈ 0.99 at epoch 1 then collapses to 0.18 by epoch 15 with a transient accuracy dip (0.92 → 0.61 → 0.92 as the network finds a different functional solution).
  • Removing the MLP activation in the small transformer drops Gate v_1 from 0.662 → 0.366 with only ~3% perplexity increase, isolating the activation's continuity effect from its task contribution.
  • Pretrained Llama-3.1-8B exhibits the predicted projection pattern: Q v_1=0.82, K v_1=0.77, Gate v_1=0.80, Up v_1=0.63, O u_1=0.89, Down u_1=0.74, V v_1=0.14—V being the clear outlier with no adjacent nonlinearity.

Authors & Credibility

All three authors (Kyungwon Jeong, Won-Gi Paeng, Honggyo Suh) are affiliated with Hyntel, a company without a strong public ML research profile or visible prior arxiv track record on this topic. Without senior co-authors from established labs and with no obvious institutional anchor, the paper rests entirely on its own technical merits—which are non-trivial. The methodology appears careful (3 seeds for MLP, multiple ablations, validation across 6 pretrained models). The references are appropriate and recent (Marcotte et al. 2025 on conservation laws, Li & Papyan NeurIPS 2023 on residual alignment, Godfrey et al. 2022 on intertwiner groups), suggesting the authors are well-read in the relevant literature on linear network dynamics, symmetry, and structural regularization.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: Moderate-to-high. Prior work (Li & Papyan 2023) already established that residual connections produce top singular vector alignment in residual Jacobians; this paper's distinct contribution is identifying symmetry breaking as a separate necessary condition through the radial-activation counterfactual—which is a genuinely clever experimental move. The "Δ_GW" cumulative-gradient diagnostic is also novel and effective.

Rigor: The MLP experiments are carefully controlled (3 seeds, σ²-weighted metric to avoid leading-direction artifacts, multiple activation types). However, transformer experiments use a single seed, and the authors acknowledge this. The Q/K continuity claims rely on indirect mechanism (softmax) rather than direct ablation, which is a real epistemic limitation.

Reproducibility: Setup is well-specified (architecture, hyperparams, seeds). No code link is mentioned in the body text I read—if the repo isn't released, reproducibility hinges on careful re-implementation, which is feasible but tedious for the transformer experiments.

Significance: The mechanism, if it generalizes, has implications for layer pruning, parameter sharing, and merging. The connection to Marcotte et al.'s conservation-law analysis of residual blocks is intellectually satisfying. Whether the projection-specific pattern actually drives downstream gains (e.g., better merging) remains future work.

Red flags: (1) Toy MLP (MNIST, 16 layers) is far from production scale, and the authors explicitly note rotation-drift dynamics have not been verified at LLM scale—only the static end-state pattern. (2) The "necessary conditions" framing is empirical, not formally proven; the radial-activation counterfactual is suggestive but not exhaustive (one could construct other rotation-preserving nonlinearities). (3) "Why V has low continuity" is partly post-hoc rationalization tied to the architectural read/write framing. (4) Affiliation with a company called "Hyntel" without independent reputation means the work needs to stand purely on technical merit—which it largely does, but warrants extra scrutiny.

Strengths

  • Clean experimental design: the radial-activation ablation is the kind of crisp counterfactual that genuinely advances mechanistic understanding rather than just measuring more correlations.
  • Connects multiple threads of literature (intertwiner groups, conservation laws, signal-flow alignment) into a coherent two-condition story.
  • The Δ_GW cumulative-gradient transfer metric is a useful new diagnostic for distinguishing "gradient creates structure" from "weights retain structure."
  • Predictions about projection-specific continuity match pretrained Llama observations, providing external validation beyond the toy setup.
  • Addresses a real "open question" cited in prior work (origin of the alignment phenomenon).

Limitations

  • All training-dynamics evidence is on a 16-layer MLP on MNIST; LLM-scale rotation drift is inferred, not measured.
  • Single-seed transformer runs leave variance uncharacterized.
  • Q/K mechanism is hypothesized via softmax but not directly ablated.
  • The authors do not test whether their predictions actually improve any downstream application (pruning, merging, sharing)—the practical "so what" is left as future work.
  • No discussion of how findings interact with mixed-precision training, quantization, or other practical training perturbations relevant at scale.
  • The radial-activation counterfactual, while clever, uses only one form (x·tanh(‖x‖))—a more thorough search over rotation-equivariant nonlinearities would strengthen the claim.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does monitoring per-layer SO(d) rotation drift during fine-tuning actually serve as an early warning for catastrophic forgetting, as the authors hypothesize?
  • Can "orbit-aware model merging" (projecting checkpoints onto a shared SO(d) representative) measurably reduce destructive interference vs naive averaging or TIES-merging?
  • Why does V develop low continuity even in pretrained Llama where it has 32 layers of training—is this an architectural inevitability, or could a redesigned attention block force V into a continuity-supporting structure?
  • How does the two-condition mechanism interact with parameter-efficient fine-tuning (LoRA), where the residual stream is preserved but the update structure is heavily constrained?
  • Does the projection-specific pattern hold for MoE models, where Gate/Up may behave differently due to expert routing?
  • Can the σ²-weighted alignment diagnostic detect when a model is undertrained vs overtrained vs catastrophically interfered with?
