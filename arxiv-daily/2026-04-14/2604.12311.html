Is Vibe Coding the Future? An Empirical Assessment of LLM Generated Codes for Construction Safety

一句话总结： 通过对450个由前沿LLM生成的建筑施工安全Python脚本进行大规模实证评估，发现虽然代码执行成功率高达~85%，但在可执行脚本中约45%存在“静默失败”——即代码运行无误但输出的OSHA安全计算逻辑数学错误，且非正式的用户人设会显著加剧模型对关键安全变量的幻觉编造。
TL;DR: A large-scale empirical evaluation of 450 vibe-coded Python scripts for construction safety finds that while ~85% execute successfully, approximately 45% of functional scripts suffer from silent failures—outputting mathematically incorrect OSHA safety logic. Informal user personas dramatically increase hallucination of critical safety variables.

核心贡献

  • 首次针对建筑施工安全这一安全关键领域，大规模实证评估“氛围编程”（vibe coding）生成代码的可靠性
  • 提出并量化了“静默失败率”（Silent Failure Rate, SFR），即代码执行成功但安全逻辑错误的比例
  • 揭示了用户沟通风格（人设）对LLM幻觉行为的显著影响，非正式提示大幅增加危险的数据编造
  • 发现高语法执行成功率反而掩盖了逻辑缺陷和防御性编程的缺失，构成“能力幻觉”

Key Contributions

  • First large-scale empirical audit of vibe-coded tools in safety-critical construction domains
  • Introduces and quantifies the Silent Failure Rate (SFR)—the proportion of executable scripts with mathematically incorrect OSHA logic
  • Demonstrates that informal, realistic user personas significantly increase LLM hallucination of missing safety-critical variables
  • Identifies an "illusion of competence" where high syntactic reliability masks dangerous logical and architectural deficits

Methodology

Three frontier models (Claude 3.5 Haiku, GPT-4o-Mini, Gemini 2.5 Flash) generated 450 Python scripts from 150 persona-driven prompts. Prompts were stratified across three personas: Safety Manager (formal, technical, cites OSHA subparts), Construction Foreman (informal, rushed, job-site vernacular), and Construction Worker (ambiguous, minimal technical background, omits critical variables). All scripts were executed in an isolated sandbox with a 5-second timeout and blocked standard input. Evaluation used a validated LLM-as-a-Judge rubric (Fleiss' κ = 0.84 with human raters) assessing: hallucinated variables, interactive CLI default, error handling, OSHA logic correctness, and actionable directive output.

Results & Highlights

  • Execution viability: 85.3% of all 450 scripts executed successfully; GPT-4o-Mini (94.7%) and Gemini 2.5 Flash (93.3%) significantly outperformed Claude 3.5 Haiku (68.0%) (Cochran's Q = 53.47, p < 0.001)
  • Persona-driven hallucination: Hallucination rates escalated dramatically with informality: Safety Manager 64.7%, Foreman 82.0%, Worker 87.3% (χ² = 24.55, p < 0.001). Foreman prompts were ~2.5× and Worker prompts ~3.8× more likely to trigger hallucinations than Safety Manager prompts
  • Silent Failure Rate (SFR): Among 384 executable scripts, 45.3% (95% CI: [40.3%, 50.4%]) contained silent mathematical errors in OSHA logic. GPT-4o-Mini was the highest at 56.3% (95% CI: [47.8%, 64.6%]), followed by Gemini 2.5 Flash at 41.4% and Claude 3.5 Haiku at 35.3%
  • OSHA logic accuracy: Only 46.7% of all generated scripts contained fully correct OSHA safety logic across the three models
  • Defensive programming deficit: GPT-4o-Mini included error-handling mechanisms in only 3.3% of scripts, versus 21.3% for Gemini and 23.3% for Claude (Cochran's Q = 27.30, p < 0.001)
  • Actionable directive failures: 31.56% of scripts output raw numbers without clear "SAFE/UNSAFE" warnings, with Claude failing most often (40.0%), GPT-4o-Mini at 32.0%, and Gemini at 22.67%

Authors & Credibility

Single author: S M Jamil Uddin, PhD, Assistant Professor in the Department of Construction Management at Colorado State University. The author has domain expertise in construction safety and the study employs rigorous statistical methods (Cochran's Q, McNemar's tests, Chi-square, Fleiss' Kappa) with a validated LLM-as-a-Judge pipeline and human-in-the-loop validation.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The paper makes a strong conceptual contribution by formalizing "Silent Failure Rate" in the context of LLM code generation for safety-critical systems and empirically demonstrating the "illusion of competence."
  • Experimental rigor: The persona-driven prompt design, isolated sandbox execution, bifurcated evaluation pipeline, and high inter-rater reliability (κ = 0.84) lend substantial credibility to the quantitative findings.
  • Significance: The finding that syntactic success is a poor proxy for safety correctness has broad implications for any domain where non-technical users might deploy LLM-generated code without expert review.

Strengths

  • Rigorous, large-scale empirical design with matched-sample statistical tests
  • Realistic evaluation via persona-driven prompts that mirror actual jobsite communication
  • Strong validation of the automated judge via human expert raters
  • Clear practical implications for construction safety practice and AI governance

Limitations

  • Evaluated only three specific models at a single point in time; rapid model evolution may change these rates
  • Synthetic personas, while carefully designed, may not perfectly capture all real-world construction site communication styles
  • Python-only, sandboxed evaluation without frontend interfaces, organizational workflows, or integrated testing pipelines
  • Limited to OSHA 29 CFR 1926 regulations; generalizability to other jurisdictions or safety domains is unknown
  • LLM-as-a-Judge, despite high validation scores, may miss subtle edge-case safety nuances
