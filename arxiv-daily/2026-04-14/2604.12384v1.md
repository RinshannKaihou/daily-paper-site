# Preventing Safety Drift in Large Language Models via Coupled Weight and Activation Constraints

> **一句话总结：** 本文首次从理论上证明仅约束权重或激活任一层面都不足以保持LLM的安全对齐，并提出CWAC方法——通过耦合权重子空间投影与稀疏自编码器激活正则化，在下游微调中同时约束双重漂移，以最小任务性能损失实现最优的安全性保持。

> **TL;DR:** This paper theoretically proves that constraining either weights or activations alone is insufficient to preserve LLM safety alignment, and proposes CWAC — a method that couples weight subspace projection with sparse-autoencoder activation regularization to jointly constrain dual drift during downstream fine-tuning, achieving the strongest safety preservation with minimal task performance loss.

## 核心贡献

- 理论证明单一层面的约束（仅权重或仅激活）无法阻断安全漂移的旁路路径
- 提出CWAC（Coupled Weight and Activation Constraints），首次在微调阶段同时约束权重更新与内部激活
- 在四个主流LLM（Llama-2/3、Mistral、Gemma-2）和多种下游任务上验证， harmful score显著低于现有最强基线

## Key Contributions

- Theoretically demonstrates that single-level constraints (weight-only or activation-only) fail to block bypass pathways leading to safety degradation
- Proposes CWAC, the first approach to couple weight subspace constraints with activation regularization during fine-tuning
- Extensive validation across four widely used LLMs and diverse downstream tasks shows CWAC achieves the lowest harmful scores with minimal impact on accuracy

## Methodology

- **Safety drift decomposition:** Uses first-order Taylor expansion to decompose layer output drift into weight drift (∆W) and activation drift (∆h), showing both terms independently contribute to safety degradation
- **Weight safety subspace:** Pre-computes a projection matrix via SVD on the covariance matrix of harmful prompt activations, projecting weight updates into the null space so that outputs on harmful inputs remain unchanged
- **Activation safety constraint:** Trains a TopK Sparse Autoencoder (SAE) on mixed benign/harmful data to identify safety-critical latent features; adds an MSE regularization term during fine-tuning to keep these features close to baseline activations on held-out harmful prompts
- **Coupled training:** Total loss = task loss + λ · safety loss, with weight gradients projected through the precomputed safety subspace

## Results & Highlights

- **Main results (Table 1):** On SST-2/AGNEWS/GSM8K, CWAC reduces average harmful scores to ~10.81 (Llama-2-7B), ~9.77 (Llama-3-8B), ~24.22 (Mistral-7B), and ~10.05 (Gemma-2-9B), substantially outperforming ASFT and SPPFT while maintaining competitive accuracy
- **Generalization (Table 2):** On PubMedQA and AlpacaEval, CWAC achieves harmful scores as low as 7.24 (Llama-2-7B) and 8.65 (Llama-3-8B), with the highest AlpacaEval score of 43.57 on Gemma-2-9B
- **Robustness to harmful data ratio (Table 3):** Even at 50% harmful data mixed into SST-2, CWAC maintains ~94% accuracy with a harmful score of 30.78, versus ASFT’s 45.82
- **Ablation (Figure 4 & Table 5):** Both weight-only and activation-only variants improve over baselines, but the coupled CWAC consistently achieves the lowest harmful scores and highest accuracy, confirming complementarity
- **Overhead:** SAE training ~6.5h and SVD ~18min are one-time offline costs; fine-tuning overhead is <10% compared to standard SFT

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High — the coupled perspective and the specific combination of SVD-based weight projection with SAE-based activation regularization is novel
- **Experimental rigor:** Strong — evaluates on four models, five tasks, varying harmful ratios, and multiple learning rates; includes both quantitative metrics and qualitative examples
- **Significance:** Important for practical deployment of fine-tuned LLMs where safety alignment must be preserved

## Strengths

- Strong theoretical grounding showing why single-level defenses are insufficient
- Practical, low-overhead implementation with minimal runtime cost during fine-tuning
- Comprehensive empirical evaluation across architectures and adversarial conditions
- Clear qualitative evidence (Table 6) that CWAC preserves robust refusal behavior

## Limitations

- Requires white-box access (weights and activations), limiting applicability to proprietary black-box APIs
- Depends on the quality of the trained SAE; poor SAE reconstruction or feature identification could degrade activation constraint effectiveness
- Evaluated primarily on 7B–9B instruction-tuned models and explicit harmful prompts; effectiveness on larger models, non-instruction-tuned models, or advanced jailbreaks remains open

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does CWAC scale to models beyond 9B parameters, and does the SAE training cost become prohibitive?
