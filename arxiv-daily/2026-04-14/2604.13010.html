Lightning OPD: Efficient Post-Training for Large Reasoning Models with Offline On-Policy Distillation

Yecheng Wu, Song Han, Han Cai

arXiv: 2604.13010v1

Date: 2026-04-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13010v1

Lightning OPD: Efficient Post-Training for Large Reasoning Models with Offline On-Policy Distillation

一句话总结： 通过揭示并满足"教师一致性"（teacher consistency）条件，Lightning OPD 将标准在线 On-Policy Distillation 改为完全离线执行，在保持甚至超越性能的同时，把 8B 模型的后训练成本从 120 GPU 小时降到 30 GPU 小时，实现 4.0× 加速。
TL;DR: By identifying and enforcing "teacher consistency"—using the same teacher for both SFT and OPD—Lightning OPD converts standard on-policy distillation into a fully offline pipeline. It matches or beats standard OPD on math and coding benchmarks while cutting 8B model post-training cost from 120 to 30 GPU hours (4.0× speedup).

核心贡献

  • 提出 Teacher Consistency 是 OPD 有效的必要条件：SFT 阶段和 OPD 阶段的教师必须是同一个模型；违反该条件会引入不可消除的梯度偏差，导致无论训练多久都收敛到次优不动点
  • 提出 Lightning OPD：在 SFT rollout 上预计算教师 log-probability 并离线复用，彻底消除训练时对在线教师服务端的依赖
  • 理论证明：在教师一致性满足时，Lightning OPD 与标准 OPD 共享相同的最优解，梯度差异有界，且固定 rollout 分布带来隐式正则化，自然抑制策略漂移
  • 实验验证：Qwen3-8B-Base 在 AIME 2024 上达到 69.9%，仅需 30 GPU 小时，比标准 OPD 快 4.0 倍

Key Contributions

  • Identifies teacher consistency as a necessary condition for effective OPD: the SFT teacher and OPD reference teacher must be the same model; violating it introduces irreducible gradient bias
  • Proposes Lightning OPD, an offline on-policy distillation framework that precomputes teacher log-probabilities over SFT rollouts, eliminating the need for a live teacher server
  • Proves theoretically that under teacher consistency, Lightning OPD shares the same optimum as standard OPD with bounded gradient discrepancy and an implicit regularization effect that prevents policy drift
  • Empirically demonstrates 4.0× training speedup at 8B scale while matching or exceeding standard OPD performance

Methodology

Two-stage pipeline:

  1. SFT Stage: Fine-tune base model on trajectories generated by teacher π_T to obtain reference policy π_ref
  2. Offline OPD Stage:
  • Preprocessing: sample rollouts from π_ref, compute and store per-token teacher log-probabilities once
  • Training: train student π_θ on the offline dataset, computing advantage A_t(θ) = log π_T(a_t|s_t) − log π_θ(a_t|s_t) with the teacher term read from disk and the student term computed online

The key departure from standard OPD is fixing the rollout distribution to π_ref and removing the live teacher server entirely.

Results & Highlights

  • Qwen3-4B-Base (teacher: Qwen3-8B): AIME 2024 68.1% vs. standard OPD 65.4% and ExOPD 61.0%; LCB v6 40.3% vs. ExOPD 29.0%. Total cost: 20 GPU hours (3.6× speedup)
  • Qwen3-8B-Base (teacher: Qwen3-32B): AIME 2024 69.9%, AIME 2025 59.2%, HMMT 2025 41.9%, LCB v5 49.5%, LCB v6 43.9%. Total cost: 30 GPU hours (4.0× speedup)
  • Convergence is remarkably fast: most gains captured within the first 50 OPD training steps
  • Teacher-consistency ablation confirms that mismatched teachers (e.g., Qwen3-32B SFT + QwQ-32B OPD) consistently degrade performance by 2–7 points, with larger penalties in the offline setting

Authors & Credibility

Authors are Yecheng Wu, Song Han, and Han Cai from NVIDIA. Song Han is a well-known researcher in efficient deep learning and systems. The work combines rigorous theoretical analysis (gradient bounds, fixed-point equivalence, implicit regularization) with strong empirical results on established benchmarks, making it highly credible.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The "teacher consistency" condition has been overlooked in prior OPD work (e.g., OpenThoughts-3 uses QwQ-32B for SFT but Qwen3-32B for OPD). Framing it as a fundamental requirement and deriving its theoretical consequences is a genuine advance.
  • Experimental rigor: Strong. Experiments span two model scales, multiple math and code benchmarks, and include careful ablations isolating teacher consistency.
  • Significance: High for the research community. Eliminating the live teacher server dramatically lowers the infrastructure barrier for OPD research, making it accessible to academic labs.

Strengths

  • Clean theoretical framework with formal proofs of shared optimum and gradient discrepancy bounds
  • Practical impact: 4.0× cost reduction without performance sacrifice
  • Rigorous ablations that validate the necessity of teacher consistency in both online and offline OPD
  • Fast convergence (≈50 steps) makes the method even more practical

Limitations

  • Evaluated only on Qwen3 family models; generalization to other model families (e.g., Llama, Gemini) not shown
  • Fixed rollout distribution means the student cannot benefit from online exploration of its own improving policy beyond π_ref
  • The speedup is infrastructure-speedup (no live teacher server); the total compute is still significant, though much more efficiently scheduled
  • Only math and code domains tested; broader reasoning or agent tasks unexplored

Relevance

这篇论文非常契合你对 LLM training stability 与 inference evaluation 的兴趣。它不仅提供了一个能大幅降低后训练成本的高效蒸馏框架，更重要的是从理论上指出了 SFT 与 OPD 阶段必须"教师一致"这一被长期忽视的设计原则，对未来构建更稳定、更可复现的 post-training pipeline 具有直接指导意义。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does Lightning OPD perform on non-math/code reasoning tasks (e.g., multi-step agent planning, scientific QA)?
  • Can the offline framework be combined with periodic teacher refreshes or checkpoint averaging to capture some online exploration benefits?
  • Does teacher consistency matter equally for other distillation variants (e.g., black-box OPD, reward-extrapolation OPD)?
