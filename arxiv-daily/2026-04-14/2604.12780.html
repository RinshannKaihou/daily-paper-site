Efficient Adversarial Training via Criticality-Aware Fine-Tuning

Wenyun Li, Zheng Zhang, Dongmei Jiang, Yaowei Wang, Xiangyuan Lan

arXiv: 2604.12780v1

Date: 2026-04-14

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12780v1

Efficient Adversarial Training via Criticality-Aware Fine-Tuning

一句话总结： 提出Criticality-Aware Adversarial Training（CAAT），通过Robust Parameter Criticality（RPC）筛选对对抗鲁棒性最关键的参数，仅用约1%的可训练参数即可达到接近全量对抗训练的鲁棒性，并显著降低计算开销。
TL;DR: This paper proposes Criticality-Aware Adversarial Training (CAAT), which identifies Robust Parameter Criticality (RPC) to selectively fine-tune only the most robustness-critical parameters in Vision Transformers using PEFT (LoRA/Adapter), achieving near full adversarial training robustness with only ~1% trainable parameters and substantially lower computational cost.

核心贡献

  • 提出CAAT框架，将参数高效微调（PEFT）与对抗训练结合，解决大规模ViT对抗训练计算成本过高的问题
  • 引入Robust Parameter Criticality（RPC）概念，量化每个参数对对抗鲁棒性的贡献，实现对关键参数的自适应筛选
  • 在CIFAR-10/100和ImageNet上验证了CAAT仅用约1%参数即可接近全量对抗训练的性能，且在大模型上具有良好的可扩展性

Key Contributions

  • Proposes CAAT, integrating parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) with adversarial training to address the prohibitive computational cost of AT on large ViTs
  • Introduces Robust Parameter Criticality (RPC) to quantify each parameter's contribution to adversarial robustness, enabling adaptive selection of critical parameters
  • Validates on CIFAR-10/100 and ImageNet that CAAT achieves near full-AT robustness with ~1% trainable parameters, and shows favorable scalability to larger architectures

Methodology

The method consists of two stages:

  1. Robust Parameter Criticality (RPC) calculation: For each parameter, compute its criticality via a first-order Taylor approximation of the adversarial risk reduction, simplified to the squared gradient magnitude ($c_n^{(1)} \approx g_n^2$). This is done on a small adversarial dataset (800 samples).
  2. Trainable Adapter Allocation: Select the top-$\tau$ critical parameters. Instead of direct fine-tuning, apply PEFT modules (LoRA or Adapter) to weight matrices where the number of critical parameters exceeds a threshold. This combines direct tuning (for sparse critical params) with indirect PEFT-based tuning for better representational capacity.

Experiments use ViT-B/L/H and Swin-B/L on CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet, evaluated under CW-20, PGD-10, and AutoAttack ($\ell_\infty$, 8/255).

Results & Highlights

  • ViT-B on CIFAR-10: CAAT-LoRA (1.27% params) achieves 87.12% clean acc, 51.21% PGD-10, 46.37% AutoAttack — outperforming FullLoRA-AT (2.87% params) and approaching full AT (53.66% PGD-10, 47.14% AA)
  • ViT-B on CIFAR-100 & ImageNet: CAAT-LoRA achieves the best performance among lightweight AT methods with the fewest trainable parameters
  • Computational cost: CAAT requires only 7.3GB fine-tuning memory and 6.0 hours training time on CIFAR-10 (vs. 11.8GB / 13.1h for full AT)
  • Scalability: On ViT-L, ViT-H, and Swin-L, CAAT uses only ~6% parameters with a mean 4.3% robustness drop compared to full AT
  • Combination with SOTA AT: CAAT integrates with TRADES, MART, and PRM, consistently outperforming FullLoRA-AT with far fewer parameters
  • Interpretability: RPC analysis reveals that under very small budgets, the first transformer block and final normalization layer are the most critical; as budget increases, middle-block FFN normalization layers also become important

Authors & Credibility

Authors are from Harbin Institute of Technology (Shenzhen), Pengcheng Laboratory, and Pazhou Laboratory (Huangpu). The corresponding author is Zheng Zhang. The work is technically sound, with extensive experiments across multiple datasets, model scales, and attack methods. The use of established PEFT techniques (LoRA, Adapter) and well-known adversarial attack benchmarks adds credibility.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The RPC criterion is a well-motivated adaptation of parameter importance estimation to the adversarial setting. The integration of criticality-aware selection with PEFT is a meaningful contribution to efficient adversarial training.
  • Experimental rigor: Comprehensive evaluation across three datasets, five architectures, three attack methods, and combinations with other AT methods. Ablation studies on direct vs. indirect tuning, rank selection, and sample size are included.
  • Significance: The results suggest a viable path toward adversarial training of very large vision models (e.g., ViT-22B) by dramatically reducing trainable parameters and computational cost without substantial robustness sacrifice.

Strengths

  • Strong empirical results with very high parameter efficiency (~1% params for ViT-B)
  • Excellent scalability to larger models (ViT-L/H, Swin-L)
  • Lowest computational cost among compared methods in both memory and training time
  • Provides empirical interpretability about which ViT parameters are most vulnerable to adversarial examples
  • Modular design allows plug-and-play integration with other AT methods

Limitations

  • The RPC calculation requires a separate forward pass on adversarial examples before training, adding a small upfront cost (though minimal: 8 seconds for 800 samples)
  • Focused on Vision Transformers; generalization to other architectures (e.g., CNNs, MLP-Mixers) or modalities is not evaluated
  • The threshold $\tau$ and LoRA rank $r$ are hyperparameters that require tuning
  • While approaching full AT performance, there remains a small robustness gap (e.g., ~2.5% on CIFAR-10 PGD-10) compared to full fine-tuning

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can the RPC criterion be extended to large language models or multimodal transformers (as the authors suggest) to enable efficient adversarial training at LLM scale?
  • How does RPC correlate with other parameter importance metrics (e.g., Fisher information, activation-based saliency) in the adversarial setting?
  • Would a dynamic or progressive update of RPC during training (rather than a one-time pre-computation) further close the gap with full AT?
  • Can CAAT be applied to certified defenses or other threat models beyond $\ell_\infty$ perturbations?
