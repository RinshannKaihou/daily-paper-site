# IDEA: An Interpretable and Editable Decision-Making Framework for LLMs via Verbal-to-Numeric Calibration

> **一句话总结：** 本文提出IDEA框架，通过将LLM的决策知识提取到语义可解释的二元因子模型中，联合学习言语-数值概率映射与决策参数，实现了概率校准、可解释推理和可定量编辑的人机协作。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes IDEA, a framework that extracts LLM decision knowledge into an interpretable parametric model over semantically meaningful binary factors, jointly learning verbal-to-numerical probability mappings and decision parameters via EM to achieve calibrated probabilities, semantic interpretability, and quantitatively editable human-AI collaboration.

## 核心贡献

- 提出IDEA框架，将LLM的决策知识提取到一组语义明确的二元因子上的可解释参数模型中，使决策过程可被直接检查和修改。
- 通过EM算法联合估计言语概率表达（如"likely"）到数值概率的映射和决策模型参数，解决了固定映射导致校准精度下降的问题。
- 在推理阶段通过从LLM中采样联合因子配置来进行边际化，保留了因子之间的自然相关性，而非假设条件独立。
- 支持专家直接编辑模型参数，并能够数学上精确保证编辑效果，这是单纯通过提示词无法实现的。

## Key Contributions

- Proposes IDEA, which extracts LLM decision knowledge into an interpretable parametric model over semantically meaningful binary factors, making the decision process directly inspectable and modifiable.
- Jointly estimates verbal-to-numerical probability mappings and decision model parameters via EM, addressing the calibration degradation caused by fixed mappings.
- Performs inference by sampling joint factor configurations from the LLM for marginalization, preserving natural factor correlations rather than assuming conditional independence.
- Supports direct parameter editing by domain experts with mathematically guaranteed effects, unattainable through prompting alone.

## Methodology

The framework operates in two phases. In the offline training phase, given a decision query, it first identifies binary decision factors and verifies their coverage and discriminability. It then probes the LLM across many factor configurations to collect verbal probability responses, creating a dataset. Finally, it applies Expectation-Maximization to simultaneously learn the decision model and the verbal-to-numerical mapping. In the online inference phase, observable factor values are extracted from conditions, uncertain factors are completed via correlated sampling from the LLM, and the final probability is computed via Monte Carlo marginalization over the learned decision model. Experts can also directly manipulate parameters to incorporate domain knowledge or enforce constraints.

## Results & Highlights

- IDEA with Qwen-3-32B achieves 78.6% average F1 across five datasets, outperforming DeepSeek R1 (68.1%) and GPT-5.2 (77.9%).
- The framework achieves perfect factor exclusion and exact calibration—precision unattainable through prompting alone.
- Ablation study shows that removing the EM joint estimation leads to a −6.8% average F1 drop, demonstrating the importance of learning the verbal-to-numerical mapping jointly.
- LLMs show 87.6% ordinal agreement in verbal probability expressions across 7680 configuration pairs, validating the monotonic-consistency assumption.

## Critical Assessment

IDEA addresses a genuine and important gap in LLM-based decision making: the trust deficit caused by miscalibrated probabilities, unfaithful explanations, and uncontrollable interventions. The decomposition into interpretable factors with editable parameters is elegant and practically valuable. However, the framework currently focuses on binary decisions with binary factors, which limits its direct applicability to more complex, continuous, or multi-modal decision spaces. The factor identification step still relies on the LLM to propose relevant factors, which may introduce bias or omit important considerations. Additionally, while the EM joint estimation is a strength, its convergence properties and sensitivity to initialization are not deeply analyzed.

## Strengths

- Novel combination of verbal probability elicitation, EM-based joint estimation, and correlated sampling for interpretable decision making.
- Strong empirical results outperforming powerful LLMs like DeepSeek R1 and GPT-5.2.
- Provides a principled way to incorporate expert knowledge through direct parameter editing with mathematical guarantees.
- Clean separation between factor inference and decision model, enabling transparency and auditability.

## Limitations

- Currently restricted to binary decisions and binary factors; extension to continuous or multi-class settings is non-trivial.
- Factor identification relies on LLM generation, which may be incomplete or biased for certain domains.
- The computational cost of Monte Carlo marginalization and EM may scale poorly with a large number of factors.
- No direct comparison with human expert decisions or real-world deployment studies.
