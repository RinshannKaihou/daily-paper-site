# Self-Adversarial One Step Generation via Condition Shifting

> **一句话总结：** 该论文提出APEX，一种通过条件偏移（condition shifting）在流模型内部生成对抗信号的无判别器单步生成框架，理论上证明其梯度与GAN对齐且权重恒为1，实验上0.6B模型在NFE=1时超越FLUX-Schnell 12B，20B模型经6小时LoRA微调即可达到GenEval 0.89，超越原50步教师模型。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces APEX, a discriminator-free one-step generation framework that generates endogenous adversarial signals within flow models via condition shifting. It proves the training gradient is GAN-aligned with constant weight w≡1. Empirically, APEX 0.6B surpasses FLUX-Schnell 12B at NFE=1, and APEX 20B with 6-hour LoRA tuning reaches GenEval 0.89, exceeding the original 50-step teacher model.

## 核心贡献

- 提出无外部判别器的自对抗单步生成框架APEX，通过条件偏移 $c_{\text{fake}} = Ac + b$ 在条件空间分离真实分支与伪分支
- 从理论上证明APEX的梯度具有与GAN相同的score-difference形式，但权重恒为 $w \equiv 1$，对应Fisher divergence最小化，避免了判别器导致的梯度消失
- 架构兼容性强，无需修改时间嵌入，可直接用于全参数训练或LoRA等参数高效微调

## Key Contributions

- Proposed APEX, a discriminator-free self-adversarial one-step generation framework that separates real and fake branches in condition space via affine condition shifting $c_{\text{fake}} = Ac + b$
- Theoretically proved that APEX's gradient takes the canonical GAN score-difference form with constant weight $w \equiv 1$, corresponding to Fisher divergence minimization and avoiding discriminator-induced gradient vanishing
- Architecture-preserving design requires no modification to time embeddings, enabling plug-and-play compatibility with both full-parameter training and parameter-efficient tuning (e.g., LoRA)

## Methodology

**Backbones and Tuning:** Three model capacities are evaluated: APEX 0.6B and APEX 1.6B (full-parameter tuning), and APEX 20B using LoRA on Qwen-Image.

**Condition Shifting:** APEX introduces a fake condition $c_{\text{fake}} = Ac + b$ via an affine transformation. Negative scaling (e.g., $A = -aI$ with $a \in \{0.5, 1.0\}$) inverts the condition embedding direction, creating maximal representational decoupling between the real and shifted branches.

**Two-Stage Objective:** (1) *Fake flow fitting* $\mathcal{L}_{\text{fake}}$ trains the shifted condition branch to reconstruct the model's current one-step outputs, making $v_{\text{fake}}$ an independent estimator of the fake distribution's velocity field. (2) *Mixed consistency loss* $\mathcal{L}_{\text{mix}}$ blends supervised flow-matching with a consistency loss against $v_{\text{fake}}$, steering the main branch toward the data distribution while receiving an adaptive adversarial correction signal.

**Datasets:** Training combines open-source datasets (ShareGPT-4o, BLIP-3o) with synthetic data: 600K samples generated from Flux-Reasoning-6M prompts and 200K poster prompts synthesized by Qwen-Image-20B.

**Evaluation Metrics:** GenEval Overall is the primary quality metric. FID and CLIP scores are reported on MJHQ-30K, DPGBench, and WISE for completeness.

## Results & Highlights

- **Small model beats large baseline:** At NFE=1, APEX 0.6B achieves GenEval 0.84 with 7.3 samples/s throughput and 0.20s latency, surpassing FLUX-Schnell 12B (GenEval 0.69), a model with 20× more parameters
- **LoRA distillation SOTA:** APEX 20B (LoRA r=64) reaches GenEval 0.89 at 0.39s latency after only 6 hours (2K steps, global batch size 64) of training, exceeding the original 50-step Qwen-Image teacher (0.87) and providing a 15.33× inference speedup
- **NFE=2 scaling:** APEX 1.6B hits 0.85 GenEval (~8 points above the strongest two-step baseline Sana-Sprint 1.6B at 0.77) while running more than twice as fast; APEX 20B LoRA sustains 0.89 GenEval at 0.47s latency
- **Data scaling:** BLIP-3o consistently outperforms ShareGPT-4o at larger step counts (e.g., 0.81/0.83 vs. 0.73 at 10K steps for 0.6B/1.6B); for 20B LoRA, BLIP-3o reaches 0.84–0.85 by 1–2K steps while ShareGPT-4o lags at 0.19–0.35
- **Ablation insights:** A balanced outer weighting of $\mathcal{L}_{\text{fake}}$ and $\mathcal{L}_{\text{mix}}$ (1.0:1.0) yields the highest GenEval; negative scaling $a \in \{-1.0, -0.5\}$ with small positive bias $b \in [0.1, 1.0]$ provides the most robust gains

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The condition-space separation for self-adversarial training is a clean architectural innovation compared to time-domain approaches like TwinFlow. The theoretical connection to GAN-aligned gradients with constant weight is rigorous and well-motivated.
- **Experimental rigor:** Comprehensive benchmarking across model scales (0.6B→20B), NFE settings (1 and 2), and multiple datasets/metrics. However, the paper does not report standard deviations or confidence intervals for GenEval scores.
- **Significance:** Demonstrates that endogenous adversarial signals can replace external discriminators without sacrificing quality, which has strong implications for scalable few-step image generation and parameter-efficient distillation.

## Strengths

- Elegant theoretical framework connecting flow matching, score-velocity duality, and GAN dynamics
- Strong empirical results with small models outperforming much larger distilled baselines
- Architecture-preserving, making it highly practical for deployment with existing pretrained backbones and LoRA
- Effectively addresses the persistent training-inference-quality trilemma in one-step generation

## Limitations

- Primary evaluation is on text-to-image generation; generalization to other modalities (e.g., video, audio) is not demonstrated
- Relies on synthetic data augmentation for the best 20B results, which may introduce dataset-dependent biases
- Does not report variance or statistical significance of the GenEval scores across multiple random seeds
- The constant-weight GAN alignment avoids gradient vanishing but may also lose the adaptive emphasis that discriminators provide on hard samples
