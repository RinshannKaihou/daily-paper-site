# Interpretable Relational Inference with LLM-Guided Symbolic Dynamics Modeling

> **一句话总结：** 提出COSINE框架，通过可微分协同优化与LLM外循环引导的基函数库进化，在未知网络结构下同时推断潜在交互图并发现稀疏可解释的符号动力学方程，在多个合成系统及真实疫情数据上达到近乎完美的图恢复AUC。

> **TL;DR:** COSINE jointly discovers latent interaction graphs and sparse symbolic governing equations via differentiable co-optimization of structure and dynamics, with an LLM-guided outer loop that evolves the basis library from inner-loop feedback. It achieves near-perfect AUC on diverse synthetic and real-world epidemic data while recovering mechanism-aligned expressions.

## 核心贡献

- 提出首个端到端可微分框架COSINE，在未知网络拓扑下联合优化潜在交互图与稀疏符号动力学方程
- 将图神经网络的消息传递机制重新建模为稀疏符号回归，通过Message/Update双库分解防止过参数化掩盖伪边
- 引入LLM作为符号假设提议机制，基于内循环训练反馈自适应剪枝与扩充基函数库，打破固定符号库的“闭世界”限制
- 在6类合成动力系统及大规模真实COVID-19疫情数据上验证，关系推断AUC接近或达到1.0，同时恢复出与物理机制对齐的简洁表达式

## Key Contributions

- End-to-end differentiable COSINE framework for joint latent graph inference and sparse symbolic dynamics discovery under unknown network topology
- Sparse symbolic message-passing decomposition (Message/Update libraries) that prevents over-parameterized dynamics from masking spurious edges
- LLM-guided outer-loop library evolution that adaptively prunes and augments basis terms based on inner-loop optimization feedback, removing closed-world assumptions
- Strong empirical results on six synthetic dynamical systems and real-world epidemic data, achieving near-perfect relational inference AUC and recovering parsimonious, mechanism-aligned equations

## Methodology

COSINE operates as a two-level closed-loop system. The inner loop jointly optimizes a graph generator (parameterized by Ψ) and sparse regression coefficients (W) via gradient descent. The graph generator uses Gumbel–Softmax to produce differentiable soft adjacency matrices. Dynamics are decomposed into a Message flow (neighbor interactions) and an Update flow (self-dynamics), each modeled as a sparse linear combination over shared symbolic basis libraries. The outer loop employs an LLM (GPT-OSS 20B by default) as a symbolic supervisor: after each inner loop, training feedback (validation loss, coefficient magnitudes, residual patterns) is summarized into a prompt, and the LLM edits the basis library by pruning low-weight terms and augmenting with more complex nonlinearities when needed. A best-so-far strategy only accepts library candidates that strictly improve validation loss.

## Results & Highlights

- **Relational Inference:** Near-perfect AUC (≈1.0) across most synthetic settings (ER, BA, WS graphs with N=50), substantially outperforming NRI, GDP, RIVA, and classical statistical baselines (GC, MI, TE)
- **Mechanism Discovery:** Successfully recovers ground-truth primitives such as `xj − xi`, sinusoidal coupling `sin(xj − xi)`, degree-normalized aggregation `h/(ki + ϵ)`, and logistic growth `x(1−x)`; term accuracy reaches 1.0 for Update modules on most systems
- **Real-World Epidemic Data:** On COVID-19 data from four U.S. states, COSINE infers heterogeneous transmission structures aligned with mobility patterns and discovers mass-action contact terms (`xi · xj`) alongside region-specific nonlinear update terms
- **Efficiency:** COSINE trains faster and uses significantly less GPU memory than NRI across network sizes N ∈ {5, 10, 50, 100, 200}, owing to its lightweight sparse linear regression backbone
- **Ablation:** Removing the LLM-guided library evolution causes severe performance drops on complex dynamics (MM, CMN), confirming that static libraries suffer from a closed-world bottleneck

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The paper bridges three traditionally separate areas—neural relational inference, symbolic regression, and LLM-guided scientific discovery—into a unified differentiable framework.
- **Experimental Rigor:** Strong. Evaluations cover six synthetic systems spanning continuous/discrete time and diverse nonlinearities, plus a large-scale real-world epidemic dataset. Baselines include statistical methods, neural relational inference (NRI, GDP), and attention-based models (RIVA).
- **Significance:** The result is important for mechanistic interpretability in dynamical systems. By recovering both structure and explicit equations, COSINE moves beyond black-box association toward transparent, physics-informed modeling.

## Strengths

- Elegant integration of differentiable optimization and LLM-driven symbolic hypothesis generation
- Sparse symbolic message passing provides a strong inductive bias that improves both interpretability and structural identifiability
- Extensive validation on both synthetic benchmarks and real-world data
- Superior computational efficiency and scalability compared to deep neural baselines

## Limitations

- Complex dynamics place heavier demands on the outer-loop LLM; performance on the hardest systems (e.g., Michaelis-Menten message terms) can depend on LLM scale
- Sparse linear regression bounds expressivity; some systems may require richer compositional primitives beyond the current library budget
- Under finite noisy data, symbolic equation discovery can be non-identifiable, with near-equivalent terms blurring mechanistic conclusions
- The real-world epidemic analysis is illustrative rather than predictive—no epidemiological validation against ground-truth transmission networks
