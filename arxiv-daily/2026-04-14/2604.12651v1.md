# Learning Chain Of Thoughts Prompts for Predicting Entities, Relations, and even Literals on Knowledge Graphs

> **一句话总结：** 本文提出RALP，将知识图谱链接预测重新定义为提示学习问题，通过MIPRO贝叶斯优化学习基于思维链的字符串评分函数，在仅使用≤30个样本且无需梯度的情况下，实现了对实体、关系乃至数值型字面量的预测，并在多个基准上超越传统KGE模型。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces RALP, which reframes knowledge graph link prediction as a prompt learning problem. Using gradient-free Bayesian optimization (MIPRO) with ≤30 examples, RALP learns chain-of-thought string prompts as scoring functions to predict missing entities, relations, and even numerical literals. It outperforms state-of-the-art KGE models by >5% MRR and achieves strong results on OWL instance retrieval.

## 核心贡献
- 提出一种全新的链接预测范式：用字符串参数化的评分函数 \( \phi_{\beta_{\leq c}} \) 替代传统的连续向量参数化评分函数，将LLM提示作为可学习的离散参数
- 设计RALP与RALP†两种变体：RALP依赖默认提示进行上下文学习；RALP†通过MIPRO算法自动优化CoT提示，无需梯度访问即可在≤30个样本上完成优化
- 将方法扩展至数值型字面量预测与OWL实例检索，展示了LLM在零样本/不可见实体、关系和复杂逻辑表达式上的推理能力
- 提出基于RALP的知识图谱数据增强算法，利用高置信度推断三元组提升下游KGE模型的泛化性能

## Key Contributions
- A novel paradigm that replaces continuous vector-parameterized scoring functions with string-parameterized ones, treating LLM prompts as learnable discrete parameters for link prediction
- Two variants, RALP (default in-context learning) and RALP† (MIPRO-optimized CoT prompts), enabling gradient-free prompt tuning on ≤30 examples
- Extension to numerical literal prediction and OWL instance retrieval, demonstrating LLM reasoning over unseen entities, relations, and complex logical expressions
- A knowledge graph enrichment algorithm that leverages high-confidence RALP inferences to augment training data and improve downstream KGE generalization

## Methodology
The authors reformulate link prediction as learning a string-parameterized scoring function \( \phi_{\beta_{\leq c}} \), where \( \beta_{\leq c} \) is a chain-of-thought prompt of at most \( c \) tokens. RALP† uses the MIPRO algorithm to perform Bayesian optimization over the discrete search space of possible prompts, iteratively selecting candidates based on an acquisition function (e.g., expected improvement) and evaluating them via cross-entropy loss (or MSE for literals) on a small validation set.

The prompt initialization \( \Pi(\cdot) \) combines task descriptions, few-shot examples (sampled via negative sampling or KvsAll), and the query pair \((s, r)\). At inference, the LLM (Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct) generates predictions and confidence scores. For numerical literals, contextual subsets \(G(s)\) and \(G(r)\) are sampled to support reasoning. For OWL instance retrieval, a two-step pipeline first generates few-shot CoT examples with syntax explanations, then predicts instance sets for complex ALCQHI concept expressions.

## Results & Highlights
- **Transductive link prediction SOTA**: RALP† achieves 1.000 MRR and Hits@1 on Countries-S1 (to the authors' knowledge, the first method to do so), and 0.750–0.764 MRR on Countries-S2/S3, improving over traditional KGE models by >5% MRR
- **KGE augmentation gains**: When using RALP-inferred triples to augment training data, models like DistMult, Keci, and QuatE show substantial improvements (e.g., QuatE reaches 1.000 MRR on S1; DistMult-RALP reaches 0.990 MRR on S1)
- **Numerical literal prediction**: On LitWD1K, RALP predicts mean values close to ground truth (e.g., population: predicted 58.5M vs. true 60.9M) with interval coverage rates (ICR) between 75% and 90%, demonstrating calibrated uncertainty estimation
- **OWL reasoning**: Perfect Jaccard similarity (1.000) on atomic concepts; strong performance on conjunction (0.986), disjunction (0.984), negation (0.887), and at-least restrictions (1.000). Manchester syntax consistently outperforms DL syntax
- **Data efficiency**: Effective prompts are learned from fewer than 30 training examples with no gradient access, highlighting the sample efficiency of the approach

## Critical Assessment
RALP presents a conceptually elegant shift from vector-based to symbol-based scoring for knowledge graphs. The re-framing of link prediction as discrete prompt optimization is novel, and the use of MIPRO for this purpose is well-motivated. The results on Countries-S1 are remarkable, and the extension to numerical literals and OWL reasoning fills important gaps that traditional KGE models cannot address.

However, the evaluation is limited to relatively small-scale datasets (Countries, LitWD1K, Father), and the authors acknowledge that token constraints prevent direct scaling to very large graphs without better context sampling. The method also assumes that entity and relation names are semantically meaningful strings—a condition not always met in industrial KGs with opaque IDs. Additionally, while the numerical results are promising, outliers in context can distort interval predictions, requiring pre-processing.

## Strengths
- Novel conceptual reframing of link prediction as learning a symbolic, string-parameterized scoring function
- Strong empirical results across three distinct KG tasks: transductive link prediction, numerical literals, and OWL instance retrieval
- High data efficiency: learns effective prompts from ≤30 examples without any gradient access or model fine-tuning
- Demonstrates that LLM-based inference can augment training data and improve conventional KGE models
- Open-source release of code, training, and evaluation pipeline

## Limitations
- Evaluation is confined to small-scale benchmarks; scalability to large, real-world KGs is limited by LLM context windows
- Requires entities and relations to have human-readable, semantically meaningful labels; performs poorly with opaque identifiers
- Numerical predictions can be distorted by outlier values in the retrieved context
- Relies on large pretrained LLMs (Qwen2.5-32B), making inference computationally expensive compared to embedding lookups
- No direct comparison against recent retrieval-augmented or fine-tuned LLM baselines (e.g., KG-LLM, LinkGPT) on shared benchmarks

## Relevance
本文为知识图谱补全领域提供了一种与嵌入方法互补的新路径。对于需要处理动态图谱、不可见实体/关系或数值型数据的场景，RALP的提示学习方法比传统KGE模型更具灵活性。其数据高效性和无需梯度优化的特点，也使其在少样本或快速适配新领域时具有实际价值。此外，RALP作为数据增强器提升下游KGE模型表现的能力，为混合符号-神经方法开辟了新的可能性。
