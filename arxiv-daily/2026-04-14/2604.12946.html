Parcae: Scaling Laws For Stable Looped Language Models

一句话总结： 通过将循环架构重新建模为非线性时变动力系统，Parcae 约束注入参数的谱范数以解决训练不稳定性，并首次建立了循环深度作为训练和测试时计算的正交扩展轴的幂律缩放规律。
TL;DR: Parcae recasts looped architectures as a nonlinear dynamical system over the residual stream, constrains the spectral norm of injection parameters to eliminate training instability, and establishes the first scaling laws showing loop depth as an orthogonal axis for scaling both training and test-time compute.

核心贡献

  • 从动力系统视角分析循环Transformer的不稳定性，发现残差流爆炸源于注入矩阵A的无约束谱范数
  • 提出Parcae架构：通过负对角参数化和离散化将ρ(A)严格约束在<1，从根本上消除状态爆炸
  • 训练算法改进：per-sequence深度采样和修正的截断反向传播采样策略，显著减少训练后期的loss spike
  • 建立循环深度的训练缩放律（isoFLOP最优循环深度与数据量遵循可预测的幂律）和测试时缩放律（饱和指数衰减）
  • 在固定参数量/数据预算下，1.3B Parcae在Core和Core-Extended上比同规模Transformer分别提升2.99和1.18分；770M Parcae可达到1.3B Transformer的Core性能

Key Contributions

  • Dynamical-systems analysis of looped transformer instability, identifying unbounded spectral norm of injection matrix A as the root cause of residual explosion
  • Parcae architecture: uses negative diagonal parameterization with discretization to enforce ρ(A) < 1, eliminating state explosion
  • Training algorithm improvements: per-sequence depth sampling and corrected truncated-backpropagation sampling drastically reduce late-training loss spikes
  • First scaling laws for loop depth as an orthogonal scaling axis: isoFLOP-optimal training follows predictable power laws for recursion depth and data
  • Test-time scaling follows a saturating exponential decay, unified with training scaling laws
  • At fixed parameter/data budgets, 1.3B Parcae outperforms same-size Transformers by 2.99 (Core) and 1.18 (Core-Extended) points; 770M Parcae matches 1.3B Transformer Core quality

Methodology

The authors frame the looped forward pass as a nonlinear time-variant dynamical system over the residual stream:

$$h_{t+1} = A h_t + B e + R(h_t, e)$$

Linearizing (dropping the nonlinear operator R) yields a discrete LTI system where stability is governed by the spectral radius ρ(A). Prior work uses addition (A = I, marginally stable) or concatenation (A = W₁, unconstrained and unstable). Parcae parameterizes A in continuous form as a negative diagonal matrix, then discretizes via zero-order hold with a learnable step size Δ, ensuring ρ(A) < 1. Input injection is normalized (LN on prelude output) to prevent late-training spikes. Training uses per-sequence depth sampling within a micro-batch and a corrected sampling algorithm that decouples the depth distribution from the backward truncation length.

Results & Highlights

  • End-to-End Quality:
  • vs RDMs: up to 6.3% lower validation perplexity and 1.8 points better average downstream benchmark score
  • vs Transformers (nanochat setup): 4.3–9.2% lower validation perplexity; 140M–1.3B Parcae achieves 23.3–87.5% of the quality gap to the next-larger Transformer on Core
  • 770M Parcae matches 1.3B Transformer on Core; 1.3B Parcae achieves up to 87.5% relative quality of a 2× larger Transformer
  • Training FLOP Scaling (isoFLOP):
  • Optimal µ_rec and tokens D follow power laws with consistent exponents across 140M and 370M scales (γ_µ ≈ 0.40, γ_D ≈ 0.78)
  • Parametric fit predicts held-out validation loss with 1.3% (140M) and 0.8% (370M) error
  • Looping enables a strictly lower isoFLOP loss frontier than fixed-depth models
  • Test-Time Scaling:
  • Quality saturates near µ_rec; additional recurrences follow L(T) = L_∞ + Z e^(−z·T)
  • Unified law: L̂_unified(T | µ_rec, D) = L̂_train(µ_rec, D) + Z·exp(−z·T·µ_rec^(−1))
  • Predicts held-out test-time loss within 0.85–1.31% average error

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The dynamical-systems reframing provides a principled explanation for a long-observed empirical problem (looped model instability), and the resulting scaling laws are the first systematic treatment of loop depth as an independent scaling axis.
  • Experimental rigor: Strong. Experiments span 100M–1.3B parameters, multiple architectural setups (RDM and nanochat), extensive ablations, and both parabolic and parametric scaling-law fits.
  • Significance: Potentially high for efficient LLM training and inference. If the scaling laws hold at larger scales, looped architectures could become a practical alternative for scaling compute without inflating memory footprints.

Strengths

  • Principled, theory-guided architecture design rooted in control theory
  • Comprehensive ablations isolating the contribution of each design choice (constrained A, per-sequence sampling, prelude norm, corrected depth sampling)
  • First unification of training and test-time scaling laws for recurrent depth
  • Strong empirical gains over both prior looped models and standard Transformers under matched budgets

Limitations

  • Scaling-law experiments are limited to relatively small models (≤ 370M for isoFLOP scaling; 1.3B for end-to-end quality); extrapolation to multi-billion-parameter regimes remains unverified
  • As µ_rec increases, inference requires more sequential steps, increasing latency; the paper does not deeply address latency/throughput trade-offs
  • Architectural choices (loop placement, composition, extreme looping) are guided by small-scale results and may not transfer optimally to very large scales
  • The comparison to Transformers uses a specific "nanochat" setup; results on other standard pretraining configurations (e.g., Llama-style) are not reported

Relevance

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do Parcae's scaling laws hold at 7B+ parameter scales and with different architectural families (e.g., Mixture-of-Experts, Mamba)?
  • Can the test-time latency of high µ_rec be mitigated via parallel looping formulations or speculative execution without sacrificing the scaling benefits?
  • How does the stability analysis extend to full-rank (non-diagonal) parameterizations of A, and would they enable deeper or more expressive recurrence?
