A Bayesian Perspective on the Role of Epistemic Uncertainty for Delayed Generalization in In-Context Learning

一句话总结： 本文从贝叶斯视角研究了上下文学习中的延迟泛化（grokking）现象，发现认知不确定性在模型发生grokking转变时会急剧崩溃，从而成为一种无需标签的泛化诊断信号。
TL;DR: This paper studies delayed generalization (grokking) in in-context learning from a Bayesian perspective, finding that epistemic uncertainty collapses sharply at the grokking transition, making it a practical label-free diagnostic of generalization in transformers.

核心贡献

  • 在贝叶斯框架下研究上下文学习（ICL）中的grokking现象，使用模运算任务分析预测不确定性随训练如何演化。
  • 实验证明认知不确定性（epistemic uncertainty）的急剧崩溃与grokking转变高度同步，可作为一种无需标签的泛化诊断指标。
  • 比较了IVON和Last-Layer Laplace两种近似推断方法在训练过程、任务多样性、上下文长度和上下文噪声下的不确定性表现。
  • 通过简化的贝叶斯线性模型提供了理论支持，表明延迟泛化和延迟的认知不确定性峰值源于相同的底层谱机制。

Key Contributions

  • Studies grokking in in-context learning through a Bayesian lens, using modular arithmetic tasks to analyze how predictive uncertainty evolves during training.
  • Shows empirically that a sharp collapse in epistemic uncertainty coincides with the grokking transition, serving as a label-free diagnostic of generalization.
  • Compares IVON and Last-Layer Laplace approximate inference methods across training, task diversity, context length, and context noise.
  • Provides theoretical support via a simplified Bayesian linear model, demonstrating that delayed generalization and delayed epistemic-uncertainty peaks arise from the same underlying spectral mechanism.

Methodology

The authors train a 6-layer decoder-only Transformer with RoPE on linear modular arithmetic tasks of the form z = ax + by (mod p), where the model must infer the latent task vector from in-context examples. They evaluate on four splits: ID Train, ID Val, OOD Train, and OOD Val. Two approximate Bayesian methods are used: IVON (variational inference over all weights with Newton-like updates) and Last-Layer Laplace (post-hoc Gaussian approximation to the final layer posterior with Kronecker-factored Hessian). Predictive uncertainty is decomposed into total uncertainty, aleatoric uncertainty, and epistemic uncertainty. Experiments vary task diversity (T ∈ {32, 64, 128, 256, 512}) and training data fraction (α ∈ {0.3, ..., 0.8}), with four random seeds per configuration.

Results & Highlights

  • Both IVON and Laplace achieve high OOD Train accuracy at T = 256 and T = 512; Laplace shows stronger and more consistent OOD Val generalization.
  • At low task diversity, total uncertainty is dominated by the epistemic component across all splits.
  • As task diversity increases, epistemic uncertainty drops sharply at T = 256 and T = 512, closely tracking the generalization transition in accuracy heatmaps.
  • This uncertainty-based signature of grokking is consistent across both IVON and Laplace, highlighting its robustness to the choice of approximate inference method.

Authors & Credibility

Abdessamed Qchohi and Simone Rossi are from EURECOM, France. The work builds on established literature in Bayesian deep learning, in-context learning theory, and the grokking phenomenon. The experimental setup follows canonical prior work (Power et al., 2022; He et al., 2024), and the paper uses well-validated approximate inference techniques (IVON and Laplace). The theoretical analysis via a simplified Bayesian linear model provides additional rigor.

Critical Assessment

This paper offers a valuable and conceptually clean contribution: linking the phenomenology of grokking to principled uncertainty quantification. The finding that epistemic uncertainty collapse tracks the generalization transition is both intuitive and empirically well-supported. However, the analysis is limited to synthetic modular arithmetic tasks, which are the canonical grokking setting but far from real-world LLM behavior. It remains unclear whether the same uncertainty dynamics would be observable or practically useful in natural-language in-context learning. The comparison between IVON and Laplace is informative, but the paper does not deeply explain why Laplace generalizes better OOD—this could be an interesting avenue for follow-up work.

Strengths

  • Elegant theoretical framing connecting grokking to Bayesian uncertainty dynamics.
  • Consistent empirical findings across two complementary approximate inference methods.
  • Label-free diagnostic of generalization is practically appealing for monitoring LLM training.
  • Clean experimental design with systematic variation of task diversity and data fraction.

Limitations

  • Restricted to synthetic modular arithmetic tasks; generalization to natural-language ICL is uncertain.
  • Does not establish whether monitoring epistemic uncertainty is computationally practical at LLM scale.
  • The theoretical model is simplified and may not capture all nuances of transformer dynamics.
  • No causal intervention to verify that manipulating uncertainty affects grokking behavior.

Relevance

本文与LLM训练稳定性和可解释性研究高度相关。论文将grokking这一训练动态现象与贝叶斯认知不确定性联系起来，为理解模型从记忆到泛化的转变提供了新的量化视角。认知不确定性崩溃作为无需标签的泛化诊断信号，对于监测大规模语言模型训练、提前识别泛化转变点具有重要的潜在应用价值。此外，文中对两种近似贝叶斯推断方法的系统比较，也为在Transformer中进行不确定性量化提供了实践参考。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can epistemic uncertainty collapse be observed as a diagnostic in natural-language in-context learning or instruction tuning?
  • How does the choice of approximate inference method (IVON vs. Laplace) affect the computational cost and scalability to billion-parameter models?
  • Would actively using epistemic uncertainty as a training signal (e.g., curriculum learning or early stopping) improve or accelerate grokking?
  • How do the uncertainty dynamics interact with different architectures (e.g., state-space models, mixture-of-experts)?
  • Can the spectral mechanism identified in the theoretical model be directly analyzed in the Hessian or Fisher information of trained transformers?
