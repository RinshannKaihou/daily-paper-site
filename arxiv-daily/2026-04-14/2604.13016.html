Rethinking On-Policy Distillation of Large Language Models: Phenomenology, Mechanism, and Recipe

一句话总结： 本文系统揭示了大规模语言模型在策略蒸馏（OPD）成功与失败的两大核心条件——思维模式的兼容性与教师是否具备学生未见过的新知识，并发现成功的OPD本质上依赖于学生在生成状态上与教师高概率重叠token的渐进对齐。
TL;DR: This paper systematically identifies two governing conditions for on-policy distillation (OPD) success—thinking-pattern compatibility and genuinely new teacher knowledge—and shows that successful OPD is driven by progressive alignment on high-probability overlap tokens at student-visited states.

核心贡献

  • 提出并验证了两个决定OPD成败的经验条件：（i）学生与教师必须共享兼容的思维方式（thinking patterns）；（ii）教师不仅得分更高，还必须拥有学生训练中未曾接触过的新能力。
  • 通过“弱到强反向蒸馏”实验（reverse distillation）证明：同一家族的1.5B与7B教师从学生视角看分布上几乎不可区分；OPD主要学习的是思维方式，而非单纯从高分中获益。
  • 在token层面揭示了OPD的核心机制：成功的蒸馏表现为学生与教师在高概率top-k token上的重叠率稳步上升（72% → 91%），共享token集中了97%–99%的概率质量；仅优化重叠token即可达到完整蒸馏效果。
  • 提出了两种实用的失败挽救策略：基于教师生成的off-policy冷启动（cold start SFT）和教师对齐的提示词选择（teacher-aligned prompt selection）。
  • 深入分析了密集token级监督的代价：奖励质量随生成长度衰减，后期的token先出现不稳定再向前传播；全局有信息的奖励信号并不保证局部可优化。

Key Contributions

  • Identified and validated two empirical conditions governing OPD success: (i) compatible thinking patterns between student and teacher, and (ii) the teacher must possess genuinely new knowledge beyond what the student has seen.
  • Used reverse-distillation experiments to show that same-family 1.5B and 7B teachers are distributionally indistinguishable from the student’s perspective; OPD fundamentally learns thinking patterns rather than merely benefiting from high benchmark scores.
  • Revealed the token-level mechanism: effective OPD is characterized by steadily rising overlap of high-probability top-k tokens (72% → 91%), with shared tokens concentrating 97%–99% of probability mass; optimizing only overlap tokens suffices to match full OPD.
  • Proposed two practical recovery strategies for failing OPD: off-policy cold-start SFT on teacher rollouts and teacher-aligned prompt selection.
  • Analyzed the cost of dense token-level supervision: reward quality degrades with trajectory depth, instability originates at later tokens and propagates backward, and globally informative reward does not guarantee locally exploitable gradients.

Methodology

The authors conduct a systematic empirical study of on-policy distillation (OPD) under reverse-KL objectives, comparing three supervision granularities: sampled-token OPD, full-vocabulary OPD, and top-k OPD. They monitor dynamic metrics—overlap ratio, overlap-token advantage, and entropy gap—throughout training to connect macroscopic success/failure patterns to microscopic token-level behavior. Experiments span multiple model families (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen, Qwen3, Skywork-OR1) and are evaluated on AIME 2024, AIME 2025, and AMC 2023. Key controlled comparisons include: (1) teachers with/without additional RL post-training, (2) reverse distillation where a stronger student is distilled toward its weaker pre-RL checkpoint, and (3) ablations restricting optimization to overlap versus non-overlap tokens.

Results & Highlights

  • Thinking-pattern consistency dominates over raw scores: A GRPO-tuned Qwen3-4B teacher outperforms a non-thinking Qwen3-4B teacher for Qwen3-1.7B-Base distillation, despite comparable benchmark scores, because it shares higher initial token overlap with the student.
  • New knowledge enables larger gap recovery: In both DeepSeek and Qwen families, teachers with additional RL post-training (Skywork-OR1-Math-7B, Qwen3-4B-RL-Math) achieve gap-recovery rates of ~17% and ~59%, versus only ~5% and ~16% for same-pipeline teachers.
  • Reverse distillation reveals pattern overwriting: Distilling JustRL-1.5B (post-RL) back toward its pre-RL checkpoint R1-Distill-1.5B causes the student to regress to pre-RL performance. Surprisingly, using the larger R1-Distill-7B teacher produces an indistinguishable regression curve, confirming that benchmark performance does not predict OPD outcomes.
  • Overlap-token sufficiency: An ablation restricting top-k OPD to only the overlap tokens matches the performance of full Student Top-k OPD, while Non-Overlap Top-k lags substantially, confirming that the shared high-probability region is the principal locus of learning.
  • Reward degrades with depth: Accuracy peaks at moderate response lengths (3K–7K tokens) and declines at 10K–15K. Instability emerges at later decoding positions and propagates backward.
  • Sampled-token is enough: Sampled-token OPD performs on par with Top-16 and Top-64 OPD; only the degenerate Top-1 setting is clearly worse due to biased mode-concentration.

Critical Assessment

This work makes a significant step toward demystifying on-policy distillation by moving beyond recipe reporting to causal phenomenology and token-level mechanism. The reverse-distillation design is elegant and convincingly disentangles “teacher strength” from “teacher learnability.” The token-level metrics (overlap ratio, overlap-token advantage, entropy gap) provide a clear diagnostic toolkit for practitioners. However, the study is limited to mathematical reasoning benchmarks; whether the same conditions hold for code generation, long-horizon agentic tasks, or open-ended dialogue remains unverified. The proposed “local optimization geometry” hypothesis for why globally informative rewards can fail to drive learning is suggestive but not directly tested. Additionally, all experiments use relatively modest-scale models (up to 14B), so scaling behavior to frontier models is an open question.

Strengths

  • Rigorous controlled experiments with elegant reverse-distillation and overlap-token ablations.
  • Clear, actionable diagnostic metrics (overlap ratio, entropy gap, overlap-token advantage) for monitoring OPD training.
  • Strong practical recipes (cold-start SFT, teacher-aligned prompts) with empirical validation.
  • Honest discussion of limitations and costs of dense supervision, avoiding overhyped claims.
  • Open-source release of code and training details.

Limitations

  • Domain limitation: all experiments are on math reasoning (AIME/AMC); generalization to code, agents, or open-ended generation is unclear.
  • The anisotropic-reward / local-geometry hypothesis for failing OPD is conjectural and lacks direct gradient-direction analysis.
  • Model scale is capped at 14B parameters; dynamics at frontier scales (e.g., 70B+ teachers) may differ.
  • No systematic exploration of hybrid objectives that could better exploit anisotropic reward structures.

Relevance

This paper is highly relevant to LLM training stability and post-training reliability. On-policy distillation has rapidly become a cornerstone of industrial post-training pipelines (Qwen3, MiMo, GLM-5), yet its fragility has been poorly understood. By identifying that OPD success depends on thinking-pattern compatibility and genuinely novel teacher knowledge—not merely benchmark scores—the work provides critical guidance for designing safer, more predictable distillation protocols. The token-level mechanism and diagnostic metrics offer interpretability into what was previously a black-box training stage, directly supporting the construction of system cards and evaluation reports that need to explain why a post-training step succeeded or failed. Furthermore, the analysis of reward degradation over long trajectories and the failure of globally informative rewards to guarantee local exploitability raises important flags for computation reliability in long-horizon reasoning and agentic settings, areas where silent training instabilities could propagate into downstream behavior.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Do the same thinking-pattern and overlap-token mechanisms govern OPD in code generation and multi-turn agentic tasks?
  • How does pre-training corpus divergence (independent of tokenizer/architecture) affect the "new knowledge" condition across model families?
  • Can we design alternative aggregation objectives or curvature-aware optimizers that better exploit anisotropic per-token advantages in failing configurations?
  • How do these OPD dynamics scale to very large teacher-student gaps (e.g., 1.5B student with 70B+ teacher)?
  • Can self-distillation dynamics be characterized using the same overlap-token framework, given that thinking-pattern consistency is guaranteed but knowledge novelty comes from privileged information?
