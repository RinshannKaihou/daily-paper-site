# Evaluating Differential Privacy Against Membership Inference in Federated Learning: Insights from the NIST Genomics Red Team Challenge

> **一句话总结：** 基于2025 NIST基因组学红队挑战，提出一种无影子模型的堆叠集成成员推断攻击，实证评估联邦学习中差分隐私（DP）的防御效果：ε=200时仍存残余泄露，而ε=10时攻击效果降至随机基线但模型效用也严重崩溃。

> **TL;DR:** Using the 2025 NIST Genomics Red Teaming Event, this paper proposes a shadow-model-free stacking ensemble membership inference attack (MIA) and empirically evaluates differential privacy (DP) defenses in federated learning. Residual leakage persists at ε=200, while ε=10 collapses attack success to the random baseline but also severely degrades model utility.

## 核心贡献

- 提出一种黑盒、基于堆叠（stacking）的MIA方法，用辅助数据驱动的元学习替代传统的影子模型，适配联邦学习中攻击者无法获取私有训练数据的威胁模型
- 在三个校准的DP层级（No DP、ε=200、ε=10）上系统刻画了多信号攻击的退化行为，发现即使在ε=200时仍存在可测量的成员泄露
- 在独立的第三方基因组学基准上建立可复现的基线，为未来的联邦学习隐私审计研究提供参考

## Key Contributions

- Proposes a black-box, stacking-based MIA that replaces canonical shadow models with auxiliary-record-driven meta-learning, compatible with FL threat models where private training data is inaccessible
- Provides an empirical characterisation of how multi-signal attack performance degrades across three calibrated DP tiers, showing residual leakage persists at ε=200 even when single-signal baselines collapse
- Evaluates on an independent, third-party genomic benchmark, establishing a reproducible baseline for future MIA research in FL

## Methodology

The attack pipeline uses the Adversarial Robustness Toolbox (ART v1.18.2) to train seven diverse black-box base estimators (Neural Network, Random Forest, Decision Tree, Gradient Boosting, K-Nearest Neighbors, SVM, Logistic Regression) on the target model's output probabilities. For each sample, an 8-dimensional meta-feature vector is constructed from the seven base membership probabilities plus the target model's cross-entropy loss. An XGBoost meta-classifier is trained on Client 4's ground-truth labels (the colluding client) and then partially fine-tuned on guaranteed non-member external records for the remaining clients. A two-dimensional decision rule (per-client 55th percentile threshold plus a 1.5× cross-client average dominance criterion) assigns challenge records.

## Results & Highlights

- **No DP (ε=∞):** Attack accuracy 53.42% vs best baseline 30.14%, a statistically significant improvement (z=2.84, p<0.01)
- **Low DP (ε=200):** Attack accuracy 38.36%, maintaining measurable leakage where single-signal LiRA collapses to 0% TPR at ≤1% FPR; ensemble achieves 30.77% TPR at ≤1% FPR and 38.46% at ≤3% FPR
- **High DP (ε=10):** Attack accuracy 24.66%, converging to the random baseline (~26%), confirming that strong DP noise effectively flattens exploitable confidence gaps
- **Privacy-utility trade-off:** Flower simulations show No DP converges to ~93% accuracy; Low DP retains 83–85% utility; High DP fails to converge (16–47% accuracy), indicating ε=10 largely eliminates model utility under realistic FL conditions

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The stacking ensemble approach for MIA is not entirely new, but the adaptation to a shadow-model-free FL threat model and the systematic calibrated-DP evaluation across an independent benchmark are valuable contributions.
- **Experimental rigor:** The methodology is well-structured, uses standard tools (ART, XGBoost, Flower), and reports both average-case accuracy and TPR at low FPR. The two-stage noisy-base / clean-meta design is clever and realistic.
- **Significance:** Demonstrates that average-case accuracy can understate privacy risk — multi-signal adversaries extract residual leakage at moderate DP budgets where single-signal attacks fail. It also highlights a severe privacy-utility cliff at ε=10.

## Strengths

- Shadow-model-free design is practical for real FL adversarial settings
- Comprehensive evaluation across three calibrated DP tiers on an independent benchmark
- Reports both accuracy and TPR@low-FPR, giving a more complete privacy-risk picture
- Clear analysis of the privacy-utility trade-off with additional FL simulations

## Limitations

- TPR evaluation is restricted to Client 4 (only 13 members), making point estimates coarse (~7.7% per member)
- Results are specific to the NIST soybean genomics benchmark, which has extreme dimensionality (p=125,767 ≫ n), potentially amplifying over-fitting and membership signals
- Flower utility simulation uses an IID, balanced data split, which is more favourable than realistic non-IID FL conditions; utility degradation in practice may be even worse
- The attack assumes collusion with one client, which is a strong but standard worst-case threat model
