Beyond Majority Voting: Efficient Best-Of-N with Radial Consensus Score

一句话总结： 提出径向共识分数（RCS），通过在答案嵌入空间中计算加权 Fréchet 均值作为语义中心，并按候选答案到中心的径向距离排序，从而在无训练、黑盒友好的条件下持续优于多数投票和自一致性基线，且采样预算 N 越大优势越明显。
TL;DR: Radial Consensus Score (RCS) is a training-free, geometric best-of-N selection method that computes a weighted Fréchet mean of answer embeddings as a semantic center and ranks candidates by radial distance to it. It consistently outperforms majority voting and strong baselines by 2–7%, with larger gains as N increases, and works in black-box settings.

核心贡献

  • 提出径向共识分数（RCS），将 Best-of-N 选择问题转化为嵌入空间中的几何共识问题
  • 通过加权 Fréchet 均值统一整合语义一致性、频率信号与生成概率信号
  • 在 7 个基准测试和 5 个开源模型上验证，RCS 持续优于现有基线，随 N 增大优势更明显（最高达 +7%）
  • 证明该方法在黑盒场景和多智能体辩论中均可作为多数投票的直接替代方案

Key Contributions

  • Introduces Radial Consensus Score (RCS), reframing best-of-N selection as geometric consensus in embedding space
  • Unifies semantic agreement, frequency, and generation probability signals via a weighted Fréchet mean
  • Demonstrates consistent 2–7% accuracy gains over strong baselines across 7 benchmarks and 5 open-weight models, with advantages growing as sampling budget N increases
  • Shows applicability as a drop-in replacement for majority voting in black-box and multi-agent debate settings

Methodology

Given N sampled answers, RCS embeds each final answer using a sentence transformer (all-MiniLM-L6-v2), computes a semantic center as the weighted Fréchet mean c(P) = Σ pi ui, and ranks candidates by their ℓ2 radial distance to this center. Three weighting schemes are evaluated: uniform (RCSuni, black-box), frequency-weighted (RCSfreq, black-box), and probability-weighted (RCSprob, white-box). A discrete medoid variant (RCSmedoid) is also included. Experiments cover short-form QA (SciQ, GPQA), math reasoning (Arithmetics, GSM8K, AIME25), and multiple-choice tasks (MMLU Formal Logic, MMLU-Pro) using Qwen2.5-3B/7B, Llama3.2-3B, Llama3.1-8B, and Gemma2-9B.

Results & Highlights

  • Main results: RCSuni and RCSprob consistently achieve the highest average accuracy across all model–dataset settings, outperforming the second-best method (Self-Certainty) by 2–6% on average
  • Scaling with N: At N=20 and N=40, RCS advantages become increasingly pronounced (up to +7% on Qwen and Llama models), because it can recover semantically coherent minority correct answers that majority voting discards
  • Multi-agent debate: RCS yields marginal gains on Arithmetics and 1–2% improvements on Formal Logic over majority voting as debate rounds increase
  • Black-box robustness: In a fully black-box setting with Cohere Command-A on AIME25 and MMLU-Pro, RCSuni and RCSmedoid outperform self-consistency on MMLU-Pro
  • Efficiency: RCSuni/freq runs in O(Nd), offering a favorable trade-off between semantic expressiveness and computational cost (vs. O(N²d) for medoid and O(NL|V|) for Self-Certainty)
  • Ablation: Using full reasoning trajectory embeddings causes performance degradation due to representation collapse, supporting the design choice of final-answer-only embeddings

Authors & Credibility

Authors are from the Applied Artificial Intelligence Initiative at Deakin University, Australia. The team has recent publications in LLM uncertainty estimation and multi-agent debate, suggesting relevant expertise. The experimental scope (7 benchmarks, 5 models, multiple N values) is thorough for a method paper.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The geometric consensus formulation is conceptually clean and bridges probability-based and semantic aggregation methods, though the core idea of embedding-space aggregation is not entirely new
  • Experimental rigor: Strong empirical coverage across diverse tasks and models; explicit reporting of mean±std over three seeds; inclusion of noisy/degenerate responses for realism
  • Significance: Provides a practical, training-free alternative to majority voting that scales well and requires no external verifiers, which is highly relevant for production inference pipelines

Strengths

  • Simple, training-free, and model-agnostic
  • Black-box friendly (RCSuni, RCSfreq) with low computational overhead O(Nd)
  • Consistent gains that grow with sampling budget N, addressing a key limitation of majority voting
  • Well-supported ablations (embedding model choice, correctness threshold, full-trajectory vs. answer-only)

Limitations

  • Relies on sentence embedding quality; performance could vary for domains where off-the-shelf embeddings are weak (though results show stability across tested embedding models)
  • RCSfreq can still be biased toward dominant answers, limiting recovery of correct but rare responses
  • Probability-weighted variant (RCSprob) requires access to token likelihoods, reducing applicability to strict black-box APIs
  • Theoretical characterization of when geometric consensus succeeds vs. fails is limited

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does RCS perform with domain-specific or instruction-tuned embedding models rather than general-purpose sentence transformers?
  • Can the geometric center be combined with lightweight learned verifiers for hybrid selection?
