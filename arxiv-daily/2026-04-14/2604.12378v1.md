# ReasonXL: Shifting LLM Reasoning Language Without Sacrificing Performance

> **一句话总结：** 本文提出了首个大规模五语言平行推理语料库ReasonXL，并通过SFT+RLVR的两阶段训练证明可以将LLM的推理语言稳定迁移到目标语言，同时通过表示分析揭示了早期层决定语言身份、上层层集中承载适配变化的功能分工。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces ReasonXL, the first large-scale parallel multilingual reasoning corpus spanning five European languages, and demonstrates via a two-stage SFT+RLVR pipeline that LLMs can be durably adapted to reason entirely in a target language without performance loss, while representational analysis reveals that early layers contain a causal language-identity bottleneck and upper layers concentrate adaptation-driven changes.

## 核心贡献

- 发布了ReasonXL，首个覆盖英德法意西五种语言的大规模平行推理语料库，每种语言约200万样本、90亿词元，涵盖数学、代码、科学等多个领域。
- 使用SFT+RLVR（Dr. GRPO）两阶段流程，成功将SmolLM3-3B的推理语言迁移到目标语言，且在科学任务上达到或超过基线性能，通用知识遗忘极小。
- 通过权重和激活层面的表示分析，发现模型早期层存在决定语言身份的激活瓶颈，而上层集中了适配带来的权重与激活变化。
- 发现RLVR相比SFT能以更小的参数更新实现更大的行为偏离，表明其具有更高效的重路由能力。

## Key Contributions

- Releases ReasonXL, the first large-scale parallel multilingual reasoning corpus across five European languages, with ~2M samples and ~9B tokens per language spanning math, code, science, and general domains.
- Successfully adapts SmolLM3-3B to reason in target languages via a two-stage SFT+RLVR (Dr. GRPO) pipeline, matching or exceeding baseline performance on science tasks with minimal general-knowledge drift.
- Through weight- and activation-level representational analysis, identifies an activation bottleneck in early layers that causally determines language identity, while upper layers concentrate adaptation-driven changes.
- Finds that RLVR achieves greater behavioral divergence from the base model with smaller parameter updates than SFT, suggesting more efficient representational rerouting.

## Methodology

The authors construct ReasonXL by annotating and translating samples from 10 source datasets using Qwen3-32B with a detailed translation protocol. The adaptation pipeline uses SmolLM3-3B as the base model. Stage 1 is supervised fine-tuning on the target-language split of ReasonXL with completion-only loss for 2 epochs. Stage 2 applies Dr. GRPO with a composite reward function combining accuracy, language identification, formatting, repetition penalty, and (for Spanish) naturalness rewards. Evaluations use target-language science benchmarks (MGSM, MT-Math100, GPQA Diamond), non-science tasks (Global MMLU Lite, Belebele, Include), and English general-knowledge benchmarks to measure drift. Representational analysis includes activation patching, weight change profiling, and cross-layer comparisons.

## Results & Highlights

- English SFT alone improves strong baselines on MATH100, GPQA, and MGSM, validating corpus quality.
- Target-language adapted models achieve 100% target-language compliance in reasoning traces after SFT, with GRPO recovering reasoning quality lost during SFT.
- Cross-lingual transfer is broadly preserved, and general knowledge drift on English benchmarks is minimal.
- Activation patching shows that early layers (around layers 2–4 in the 6-layer SmolLM3-3B) contain a causal bottleneck for language identity.
- Weight change analysis shows that upper layers undergo larger changes during adaptation, while RLVR produces more localized updates than SFT despite greater behavioral shifts.

## Critical Assessment

This is a well-executed and practically important contribution. The data gap for non-English reasoning has been widely acknowledged, and ReasonXL fills it at scale. The two-stage adaptation pipeline is sensible and the results are encouraging. However, the mechanistic analysis is conducted on a relatively small model (3B parameters, 6 layers), and it is unclear how well the layer-wise findings will generalize to larger architectures. The reliance on LLM-based translation (Qwen3-32B) introduces a potential source of bias or quality variation across languages. Additionally, while the paper demonstrates language shifting is possible without major performance loss, it does not deeply analyze whether the quality of reasoning traces in non-English languages is genuinely equivalent or merely sufficient for the evaluated benchmarks.

## Strengths

- First large-scale parallel multilingual reasoning corpus, addressing a widely recognized data bottleneck.
- Clean two-stage pipeline with practical evaluation across science, non-science, and knowledge-retention benchmarks.
- Mechanistic analysis goes beyond empirical results to investigate where and how language identity is encoded and manipulated.
- Finding that RLVR achieves larger behavioral shifts with smaller weight updates is theoretically interesting and practically valuable.

## Limitations

- Representational analysis is limited to a 3B-parameter, 6-layer model; generalization to larger architectures is uncertain.
- Translation pipeline relies on Qwen3-32B, which may introduce quality variation or English-centric bias.
- Does not deeply evaluate whether non-English reasoning traces are qualitatively equivalent to English traces beyond benchmark accuracy.
- Focuses on European languages with shared script; extension to more divergent language families is left to future work.

## Relevance

本文与LLM推理评估和隐藏状态/可解释性研究密切相关。论文不仅系统性地解决了非英语推理数据稀缺的问题，还通过表示分析揭示了语言身份在模型内部如何编码、如何被后训练适配所重塑。早期层的语言身份瓶颈和上层层的适配集中效应，为理解多语言LLM的功能分工提供了新的机制性见解。此外，研究发现RLVR比SFT更高效地实现行为重路由，对于设计更高效的跨语言迁移训练策略具有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the early-layer language-identity bottleneck and upper-layer adaptation concentration hold in larger models (e.g., 70B+ parameters)?
- How does the quality and style of LLM-translated reasoning traces compare to human-written or naturally occurring non-English reasoning text?
- Can the insights about RLVR's efficient rerouting be leveraged to design parameter-efficient adaptation methods (e.g., LoRA) specifically for language shifting?
- How does the framework perform on languages with fundamentally different scripts or grammatical structures (e.g., Arabic, Chinese, Japanese)?
- Could the language-identity bottleneck be targeted directly for more efficient or reversible language switching?
