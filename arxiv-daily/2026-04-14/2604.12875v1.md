# AISafetyBenchExplorer: A Metric-Aware Catalogue of AI Safety Benchmarks Reveals Fragmented Measurement and Weak Benchmark Governance

> **一句话总结：** 通过对195个AI安全基准的结构化编目与指标级审计，本文揭示当前生态的核心症结并非基准稀缺，而是测量碎片化与基准治理薄弱导致的大量“同名异义”指标和难以比较的评价结果。

> **TL;DR:** A structured, metric-level audit of 195 AI safety benchmarks shows the field's core problem is not scarcity but measurement fragmentation—familiar metric labels often conceal incompatible operational definitions, weak stewardship, and poor comparability.

## 核心贡献

- 发布了 **AISafetyBenchExplorer**，一个涵盖2018–2026年间 **195个AI安全基准** 的多层结构化编目（含基准级元数据、指标级定义、论文元数据与仓库活跃度）。
- 提出并形式化了一个四级复杂度分类法（Popular / High / Medium / Low），揭示生态系统呈“中间大、两头小”的分布：仅7个基准达到 Popular 层级，而 Medium 层级占近一半（94/195）。
- 通过 **指标碰撞审计（Metric Collision Audit）** 证明，诸如 accuracy、F1、safety score、attack success rate 等常见指标标签在不同基准中往往对应截然不同的被测对象、评判者、聚合规则与威胁模型，造成严重的可比性问题。
- 明确指出AI安全评估领域的主要失效模式是 **碎片化（fragmentation）** 而非稀缺：研究者拥有大量基准工件，却缺乏共享的测量语言、系统的基准选择原则与持久的维护规范。

## Key Contributions

- Released **AISafetyBenchExplorer**, a structured, multi-sheet catalogue of **195 AI safety benchmarks** (2018–2026) linking benchmark-level metadata with metric-level definitions, paper metadata, and repository activity.
- Formalized a four-tier complexity taxonomy (Popular / High / Medium / Low), showing the ecosystem is concentrated in the middle: only **7 benchmarks** are Popular, while **94 are Medium** complexity.
- Conducted a **metric-collision audit** demonstrating that familiar labels such as accuracy, F1, safety score, and attack success rate often hide materially different evaluands, judges, aggregation rules, and threat models—creating false comparability.
- Diagnosed the field's central failure mode as **fragmentation, not scarcity**: the community has many benchmark artifacts but lacks shared measurement semantics, principled selection practices, and durable stewardship norms.

## Methodology

The study builds on a multi-sheet workbook schema. The *Safety Evaluation Benchmarks* sheet captures benchmark-level metadata (task type, release date, sample count, language support, license, code/dataset availability, complexity tier, etc.). The *Evaluation Metrics Catalogue* sheet goes one level deeper, recording one row per metric with fields for conceptual description, methodological details, mathematical definition, and explicit notes on deviations from standard statistical usage. This design is motivated by the claim that benchmark-level summaries alone are insufficient for serious meta-evaluation: two benchmarks may both report "accuracy" or "F1 score" yet differ materially in unit of analysis, ground-truth source, judge type, and aggregation logic. Controlled vocabularies and a documented extraction pipeline are used to improve consistency and reproducibility.

## Results & Highlights

- **Corpus scale:** 195 benchmarks, with publication peaking in 2023 (57 releases) and declining in 2024–2025, suggesting a shift from proliferation toward consolidation.
- **Language skew:** **165/195** benchmarks are English-only, leaving multilingual and non-English coverage severely limited.
- **Maintenance gaps:** **137/195** GitHub repositories and **96/195** Hugging Face datasets are stale, undermining reproducibility and downstream use.
- **Grey-literature dominance:** arXiv preprints constitute the largest known venue category (46 benchmarks), meaning a substantial share of widely used benchmarks enters practice without peer review.
- **Metric collision:** The catalogue documents recurring "collision families" (Accuracy, F1, Safety score, Refusal/Compliance, Attack success, Composite/Aggregate) where shared surface labels conceal high-severity standards drift—ranging from different positive classes and judge types to entirely different success criteria.
- **Sibling-metric proliferation:** Within individual benchmarks, multiple headline metrics are often generated from the same underlying pipeline by conditioning, gating, or aggregation changes—creating an illusion of measurement diversity.

## Critical Assessment

The paper makes a compelling and well-evidenced case that benchmark abundance has outpaced measurement standardization. Its central conceptual move—distinguishing benchmark-level inventory from metric-level semantics—is methodologically sharp and directly addresses a source of noise that many survey papers overlook. The concrete statistics on stale repositories, English-only concentration, and arXiv preprint dominance give the fragmentation thesis real empirical bite. However, the metric-collision argument, while richly illustrated with examples, would be strengthened by a fully programmatic count of label reuse across the entire metrics sheet rather than the current qualitative audit. The complexity taxonomy is also acknowledged to be partially extrapolated from a 176-benchmark audit to the updated 195-benchmark corpus.

## Strengths

- **Metric-aware design:** The dual-sheet schema (benchmark + metric) sets a higher bar for meta-evaluation than benchmark-only surveys.
- **Actionable diagnosis:** The fragmentation framing reframes the problem from "we need more benchmarks" to "we need better measurement governance."
- **Concrete, corpus-level statistics:** Claims about language skew, maintenance gaps, and venue concentration are backed by counts over a large, multi-year corpus.
- **Practical recommendations:** The three closing proposals (standardized metric registries, coverage audits, stewardship-as-quality) are specific and implementable.
- **Intellectual honesty:** Limitations are stated clearly, including the partial reclassification caveat and the desire for more automated metric-collision quantification.

## Limitations

- **Complexity taxonomy coverage:** The formal complexity methodology was originally applied to 176 benchmarks; the updated 195-benchmark corpus has not been fully re-audited under that framework.
- **Qualitative metric-collision audit:** While examples are extensive, a fully automated, programmatic count of repeated metric labels and divergent operationalizations across all rows is absent.
- **No causal benchmarking outcomes:** The paper diagnoses structural and semantic problems in the ecosystem but does not conduct new model evaluations or causal experiments.
- **Single-author scope:** As a single-author work, the breadth of the catalogue and analysis, while impressive, may reflect one interpretive lens on a sprawling, multi-community space.

## Relevance

对于关注大语言模型安全评估、对齐审计和可重复性研究的读者而言，这篇论文提供了一个高价值的元评估视角。它提醒我们：在海量基准涌现的同时，如果指标定义不统一、仓库维护缺失、多语言覆盖失衡，那么“更多基准”并不自动转化为“更可靠的证据”。文中提出的结构化编目方法和三项治理建议（指标注册表、覆盖审计、维护即质量）对希望系统性地构建或选择评估组合的研究者与工程师具有直接参考意义。此外，它对“同名异义”指标的警惕，对任何需要跨论文比较安全分数的元分析工作都是一剂清醒剂。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- 如何设计一个机器可读的 **标准指标注册表（metric registry）** 协议，使得新发布的基准可以自动声明其指标语义、评判者类型与聚合规则？
- 在195个基准中，哪些风险领域（如推理过程控制、智能体奖励篡改、对齐审计）的测量覆盖仍然不足，需要新的基准来填补？
- 如果将复杂度分类法完整重跑于全部195个基准，分布形态和主要结论是否会发生显著变化？
- stale repository 和 stale dataset 的高发率对下游研究造成了哪些具体可量化的影响（例如引用率衰减、复现失败率）？
- 在多语言安全评估受限的背景下，如何以较低成本扩展现有基准的非英语覆盖，同时保持指标的可比性？
