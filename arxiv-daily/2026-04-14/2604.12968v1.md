# Evolution of Optimization Methods: Algorithms, Scenarios, and Evaluations

> **一句话总结：** 本文系统回顾了深度学习优化方法的演进轨迹，提出统一的数学分类框架，并在标准化基准上评估了23种主流优化器，发现SGD在LLM上会出现灾难性崩溃，而Muon和MARS等新兴方法通过矩阵正交化与梯度修正展现出卓越的超参数鲁棒性与跨架构泛化能力。

> **TL;DR:** This survey traces the evolution of deep learning optimization, unifies disparate methods under a rigorous mathematical taxonomy, and benchmarks 23 optimizers on ResNet-50, ViT-S, and Llama-60M. Key findings: SGD catastrophically collapses on LLMs, while newer methods like Muon (matrix orthogonalization) and MARS (gradient correction) demonstrate superior hyperparameter robustness and cross-architecture generalization.

## 核心贡献
- 建立了统一的数学分类框架，将一阶、二阶、零阶优化及其场景化变体（分布式、隐私保护、内存高效）纳入同一动力学体系
- 提出场景导向的分析视角，揭示现代优化器如何从纯算法设计演进为受系统约束（内存墙、通信墙、隐私墙）驱动的工程解决方案
- 构建了严格控制的实证基准，在ImageNet-1K（ResNet-50、ViT-S）和WikiText-103（Llama-60M）上系统评估了23种优化器的学习率敏感性、跨架构泛化性与长期训练可扩展性

## Key Contributions
- Establishes a unified mathematical taxonomy that casts first-order, second-order, zeroth-order, and scenario-oriented optimizers as instantiations of a single constrained dynamical system
- Provides a scenario-oriented analysis showing how modern optimizers have evolved from pure algorithmic design into systems-aware engineering solutions driven by the memory wall, communication wall, and privacy wall
- Introduces a rigorously controlled empirical benchmark evaluating 23 optimizers on ImageNet-1K (ResNet-50, ViT-S) and WikiText-103 (Llama-60M), systematically isolating learning-rate sensitivity, cross-architecture generalization, and long-term scalability

## Methodology
The authors formalize most optimizers as a discrete-time dynamical system with four separable dimensions: gradient estimation, scenario transformation (e.g., compression or privacy noise), momentum/state update, and parameter projection. They then categorize the literature into first-order (convergence acceleration, adaptive step-size, variance adaptation, stability, scheduling, generalization, hybrid, LLM training), second-order (Hessian approximation, Fisher information, quasi-Newton, moment fusion), zeroth-order (adaptive, perturbation, hybrid, memory-efficient, variance reduction, distributed), and scenario-oriented frameworks (distributed, privacy-preserving, memory-efficient, auto-designed, robust). Empirically, they conduct a controlled evaluation protocol that isolates algorithmic behavior from engineering optimizations, using a single hyperparameter sweep (learning rate scaled by 0.1×–10×) transferred across architectures.

## Results & Highlights
- **SGD collapses on LLMs:** On Llama-60M, SGD suffers catastrophic training collapse due to the lack of adaptive layer-wise scaling in highly anisotropic loss landscapes, underscoring the absolute necessity of adaptive methods for Transformer training
- **Hyperparameter robustness:** Muon maintains >75% Top-1 accuracy on ViT-S across 0.2×–5.0× learning-rate scaling thanks to iterative matrix orthogonalization; MARS also shows strong robustness via gradient correction that cancels stochastic noise
- **Cross-architecture generalization:** Muon and MARS achieve stable, low perplexity (≈12–14) on Llama-60M even under extreme LR scaling, whereas many vision-tuned optimizers (RMSprop, Adam, MADGRAD) fail to converge on language models
- **Long-term scalability:** SGD shows the largest gains from 300-epoch training (+9.41% on ViT-S), while advanced preconditioned methods (Muon, Kron, Lion) converge rapidly early and show diminishing returns later
- **Correlation analysis:** Optimizers cluster by algorithmic family; ViT training regimes show high convergence homogeneity (schedule-dominated), whereas ResNet-50 exhibits greater algorithmic bifurcation
- **Comprehensive star rating:** Muon achieves top ratings across convergence speed, accuracy, memory efficiency, generalization, and hyperparameter robustness

## Critical Assessment
This is a well-structured, ambitious survey that successfully unifies a fragmented field under a single mathematical framework and backs its claims with a large-scale controlled benchmark. The finding that SGD catastrophically fails on LLMs while newer structurally aware methods (Muon, MARS) generalize across architectures is both timely and actionable. However, the benchmark is limited to a 60M-parameter Llama proxy rather than billion-scale models, so some conclusions about large-scale LLM training may not fully transfer. Additionally, the computational cost disclosure is commendable but also highlights the practical barrier to comprehensive optimizer benchmarking. The survey’s sheer breadth means that some subsections are more descriptive than analytically deep.

## Strengths
- Unified mathematical taxonomy that clarifies intrinsic connections across FO, SO, ZO, and scenario-oriented paradigms
- Rigorous, controlled empirical protocol isolating algorithmic behavior from engineering artifacts
- Honest and detailed reporting of both successes and failures (e.g., SGD collapse, RMSprop regression at 300 epochs)
- Actionable guidance for practitioners on optimizer selection and hyperparameter tuning
- Extensive coverage of emerging topics: zeroth-order LLM fine-tuning, differentially private optimization, memory-efficient designs, and auto-discovered optimizers

## Limitations
- Llama benchmark uses a 60M-parameter proxy; conclusions about training at billion-parameter scale may require further validation
- The evaluation primarily sweeps only the learning rate; joint hyperparameter sensitivity (e.g., weight decay, batch size, β values) is not explored
- Some rapidly evolving subfields (e.g., auto-designed optimizers, hardware-algorithm co-design) are surveyed more descriptively than critically
- The sheer volume of methods covered limits the depth of mechanistic analysis for any single algorithm

## Relevance
本文与深度学习训练稳定性和优化动力学高度相关。它直接涉及（1）一阶/二阶/零阶优化器的设计原理与权衡，（2）LLM训练中的自适应缩放与梯度异常值问题，（3）分布式、隐私保护和内存受限场景下的优化器重构。这项调查为任何从事大规模模型训练、超参数调优或新优化器设计的研究者和工程师提供了系统性的参考框架，并与近期关于Muon、MARS、LOMO、MeZO等新兴优化器的研究形成了重要对照。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- How do the identified robustness rankings (e.g., Muon > MARS > AdamW) transfer to billion-parameter LLM pretraining with realistic batch sizes and hardware setups?
- Can automated symbolic discovery or meta-learning generate architecture-specific optimizers that outperform hand-engineered methods like Muon without exhaustive search?
- What is the optimal way to combine structural preconditioning (e.g., matrix orthogonalization) with dynamic low-precision arithmetic to break the memory-compute trade-off?
- How can zeroth-order methods close the performance gap with first-order methods while maintaining their memory advantages in full-scale LLM fine-tuning?
