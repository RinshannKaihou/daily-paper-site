# GF-Score: Certified Class-Conditional Robustness Evaluation with Fairness Guarantees

> **一句话总结：** 提出 GF-Score 框架，将 GREAT Score 的全局认证鲁棒性指标精确分解为逐类别的鲁棒性画像，并引入四种基于福利经济学的不公平度量指标，同时通过无需对抗攻击的自校准方法实现了完全无攻击的模型审计管线。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces the GF-Score framework, which decomposes the certified GREAT Score into exact per-class robustness profiles, quantifies disparity via four welfare-economic metrics (RDI, NRGC, WCR, FP-GREAT), and eliminates adversarial attacks entirely through a self-calibration procedure, enabling fully attack-free fairness auditing of robust models.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **GF-Score（GREAT-Fairness Score）**，将全局认证 GREAT Score **精确分解**为逐类别的条件鲁棒性得分，加权求和可零误差恢复原始聚合得分
- 引入四种基于**福利经济学**的鲁棒性不公平度量指标：**RDI**（极差）、**NRGC**（归一化基尼系数）、**WCR**（最坏类鲁棒性）、**FP-GREAT**（公平惩罚得分）
- 提出**无需对抗攻击的自校准**方法，利用公开干净准确率与 GREAT Score 的 Spearman 秩相关来调优温度参数，彻底摆脱对 C&W 攻击的依赖
- 在 RobustBench 的 **22 个模型**（CIFAR-10 ℓ₂ ×17，ImageNet ℓ∞ ×5）上揭示：类间脆弱性模式高度一致（如 CIFAR-10 中 76% 的模型“cat”最脆弱），且**聚合鲁棒性越强，类间不公平度往往越高**

## Key Contributions

- **Exact class-conditional decomposition** of the certified GREAT Score into per-class robustness profiles, recoverable with zero numerical error via weighted averaging
- **Four welfare-grounded disparity metrics** (RDI, NRGC, WCR, FP-GREAT) that quantify how certified robustness is distributed across classes
- **Attack-free self-calibration** that replaces adversarial-attack-based temperature tuning by maximizing rank correlation with publicly available clean accuracies
- **Extensive empirical findings** on 22 models across two benchmarks, showing consistent class vulnerability patterns and a quantifiable **robustness-fairness tension**

## Methodology

**Class-conditional decomposition:**
- Partition test/generated samples by ground-truth label and compute the per-class GREAT Score as the average local certified margin within each class
- The aggregate score is recovered exactly as a weighted sum: `Ω̂ = Σ_k (n_k/N) Ω̂_k`
- Concentration bounds for per-class estimates and RDI are derived via Hoeffding’s inequality

**Disparity metrics:**
- **RDI** — Range between best and worst class robustness (max–min disparity)
- **NRGC** — Normalized Robustness Gini Coefficient, capturing the full shape of the distribution
- **WCR** — Worst-Case Class Robustness, reflecting the Rawlsian maximin principle
- **FP-GREAT** — Fairness-Penalized GREAT Score: `mean per-class score − λ · RDI`

**Attack-free self-calibration:**
- Perform a two-phase grid search over temperature T to maximize Spearman correlation between GF-Scores and RobustBench clean accuracies
- Complementary ranking-stability calibration selects T where per-class rankings are least sensitive to small perturbations in T

**Evaluation:**
- 22 adversarially trained models from RobustBench (CIFAR-10 ℓ₂ and ImageNet ℓ∞) evaluated with only forward passes on test/GAN-generated samples

## Results & Highlights

- **Exact decomposition:** The weighted recombination of per-class scores matches the aggregate GREAT Score with zero numerical error across all 22 models
- **Self-calibration fidelity:** Achieves Spearman ρ = **0.871** on CIFAR-10 and ρ = **1.000** (perfect ranking) on ImageNet against RobustBench rankings, with **no adversarial attacks**
- **Consistent vulnerability patterns:** On CIFAR-10, **"cat" is the weakest class in 76% of models** and **"automobile" is the strongest in 59%**, suggesting intrinsic data-driven effects rather than training artifacts
- **Robustness-fairness tension:** Higher aggregate robustness correlates positively with RDI. CIFAR-10 RDI ranges from 0.11 (most fair) to 0.43 (most disparate); on ImageNet, two models have **WCR = 0.000**, meaning at least one class receives **zero certified robustness** despite positive aggregate scores
- **FP-GREAT re-ranking:** Fairness-aware ranking changes model standings meaningfully (e.g., Augustin2020 drops from 2nd → 5th on CIFAR-10 due to high disparity)

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High — this is the first certified, attack-free, post-hoc framework for per-class robustness auditing with formal disparity quantification
- **Theoretical rigor:** Solid — Hoeffding-based concentration bounds are provided for per-class scores and RDI
- **Significance:** Timely and practically important for safety-critical deployments where aggregate robustness can hide catastrophic class-level weaknesses

## Strengths

- Exact mathematical decomposition with empirical verification
- Fully attack-free pipeline preserves the ~2,000× speedup advantage of GREAT Score over attack-based evaluation
- Principled grounding of fairness metrics in welfare economics (Gini, Rawlsian maximin, IHDI)
- Extensive cross-model validation revealing systematic, data-driven vulnerability patterns
- Complementary to training-time fairness methods — enables auditing of already-deployed models without retraining

## Limitations

- Inherits GREAT Score assumptions: generative model quality must approximate the true distribution; local margin certification may be loose for poorly calibrated softmax outputs
- Self-calibration assumes a monotonic robustness–clean-accuracy relationship, which may fail across fundamentally different architectures or defense families
- ImageNet evaluation is limited to only **5 models** due to computational constraints on logit extraction across 50K images and 1,000 classes
- Concentration bounds for NRGC and WCR are left as open theoretical questions
