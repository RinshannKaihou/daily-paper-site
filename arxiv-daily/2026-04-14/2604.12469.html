Analyzing the Effect of Noise in LLM Fine-tuning

一句话总结： 系统研究了标签噪声、拼写噪声和语法噪声对GPT-2、Qwen2和Llama-2在情感分类、问答和机器翻译任务上微调时的内部学习动态，发现标签噪声最具破坏性，而输入侧噪声的影响主要局限于任务特定层，且注意力模式相对稳定。
TL;DR: A systematic study of label, typographical, and grammatical noise during LLM fine-tuning across three model families and three NLP tasks finds that label noise causes the most severe degradation, while input-side noise effects are localized to task-specific layers and attention structures remain comparatively stable.

核心贡献

  • 首次从表征层面系统比较三种真实噪声（标签翻转、拼写错误、语法错误）在多种NLP任务和模型上的影响
  • 提出并验证了噪声影响具有局部化特征：主要扭曲包含任务特定信息的深层表征，而对注意力结构的扰动较小
  • 揭示了任务级性能指标低估了噪声的真实影响——即使预测未变的"稳健"样本也存在显著的内部表征偏移

Key Contributions

  • First systematic representation-level comparison of three realistic noise types across multiple NLP tasks and model families
  • Proposes and validates that noise impact is localized: primarily distorts deeper, task-specific layers while leaving attention structures relatively stable
  • Reveals that task-level metrics underestimate true noise impact—even "robust" samples with unchanged predictions exhibit significant internal representation shifts

Methodology

  • Models: GPT-2 (124M, full fine-tuning), Qwen2-0.5B (QLoRA), Llama-2-7B (QLoRA)
  • Tasks: Sentiment Classification (Yelp Polarity), Question Answering (SQuAD v1.1), Machine Translation (Tatoeba EN–FR)
  • Noise types at 20% / 30% / 40% corruption ratios:
  • Label Flip (LF): corrupts target output only (polarity flip, random answer, unrelated translation)
  • Typographical Noise (TN): character-level perturbations to ~10% of input words
  • Grammatical Noise (GN): subject–verb agreement and article misuse in ~15% of input words
  • Analysis metrics:
  • Attention: layer-wise KL divergence and Spearman rank correlation
  • Task information: linear probing accuracy (SC) and Logit Lens MRR (QA/MT)
  • Representational similarity: centered cosine similarity and Linear CKA
  • Sample stratification: robust vs. vulnerable groups

Results & Highlights

  • Label noise is the most harmful: across all tasks and models, 40% label flip caused the largest performance drops (e.g., GPT-2 SC: 91.33% → 75.51%; Qwen2 SC: 94.41% → 57.72%)
  • Input-side noise can be benign or beneficial: typographical noise mostly improved accuracy slightly, suggesting mild regularization/robustness benefits; grammatical noise had marginal effects
  • Noise impact is localized: later layers encoding task-specific information show greater distortion under noise, while early layers remain largely unaffected
  • Attention patterns remain stable: KL divergence and Spearman rank correlation show only marginal changes in attention ordering across all noise types
  • Representational similarity (CKA/cosine) confirms that label flip causes the largest structural shifts, and distortion is concentrated in task-relevant layers
  • Robust vs. vulnerable stratification: vulnerable samples show greater representational distortion, but even robust samples (predictions unchanged) exhibit non-trivial internal shifts, indicating task-level metrics underestimate noise-induced changes

  • The experimental design is rigorous: three model families, three diverse tasks, three noise types with controlled corruption ratios, and complementary analysis at both performance and representation levels (attention, probing, CKA, robust/vulnerable stratification).
  • The methodology aligns with established practices in interpretability and robust learning literature.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The work offers a valuable complementary perspective to existing noise-robust learning research by focusing on internal dynamics rather than just algorithmic solutions. However, the individual findings (label noise is most harmful, noise affects task-specific layers) are intuitive.
  • Experimental rigor: High. The use of multiple analysis methods (attention, probing, CKA, stratification) provides a comprehensive picture. The inclusion of generation tasks (QA, MT) beyond classification is a strength.
  • Significance: Important for understanding the reliability of LLM fine-tuning in real-world noisy data scenarios. The finding that task-level metrics miss internal representational shifts is particularly noteworthy for model evaluation and safety.

Strengths

  • Multi-model, multi-task, multi-noise systematic study
  • Rich set of complementary analysis methods at the representation level
  • Finding that attention patterns remain stable while feed-forward representations shift
  • Discovery that task metrics underestimate noise impact due to robust sample internal shifts

Limitations

  • Relatively small model scale (0.5B–7B); findings may not generalize to larger frontier models
  • Synthetic noise injections may not fully capture realistic, instance-dependent noise patterns
  • GPT-2 used full fine-tuning while larger models used QLoRA, though an ablation showed minimal difference
  • Seed-based variance analysis is limited (reported only for Llama-2 SC under 40% label noise and CKA)
