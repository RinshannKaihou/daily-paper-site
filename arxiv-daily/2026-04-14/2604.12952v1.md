# An Optimal Sauer Lemma Over $k$-ary Alphabets

> **一句话总结：** 该论文基于DS维度建立了k元字母表上的最优Sauer不等式，将多分类与列表预测中假设类大小的上界从Natarajan维度的指数级依赖改进为最优多项式级依赖，并据此显著提升了列表PAC学习与一致收敛的样本复杂度上界。

> **TL;DR:** This paper establishes a sharp Sauer inequality for k-ary hypothesis classes in terms of the DS dimension, replacing the exponential dependence on list size ℓ in Natarajan-based bounds with optimal polynomial dependence, and derives substantially improved sample complexity upper bounds for list PAC learning and uniform convergence.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了基于Daniely–Shalev-Shwartz (DS) 维度及其列表扩展 ℓ-DS 维度的k元Sauer型不等式，该上界对所有可允许的参数 (n, k, ℓ, d) 都是紧的。
- 相比基于Natarajan维度的先前结果，将对列表大小 ℓ 的指数依赖改进为最优的多项式依赖，同时改进了对字母表大小 k 的依赖。
- 利用该不等式，显著改进了ℓ-列表PAC学习的样本复杂度上界（相对 prior work 提升了 ℓ⁵ 量级），以及列表预测器一致收敛的样本复杂度。
- 证明采用多项式方法（线性代数），并指出了寻找纯组合证明、以及研究DS-maximum classes结构等开放问题。

## Key Contributions

- A sharp Sauer inequality for k-ary alphabets parameterized by the DS (and list-DS) dimension, tight for every admissible n, k, ℓ, and d.
- Replaces the exponential dependence on ℓ in Natarajan-based bounds with optimal polynomial dependence, and also improves the dependence on k.
- Derives improved sample complexity upper bounds for ℓ-list PAC learning and uniform convergence of list predictors, sharpening recent results from STOC 2023, COLT 2024, and NeurIPS 2024.
- The proof uses the polynomial method; the authors highlight the open problem of finding a purely combinatorial proof and studying DS-maximum classes.

## Methodology

The core combinatorial result (Theorem 2.1) is proved via a linear-algebraic / polynomial method. The authors construct a vector space V = {f : H → ℝ}, use indicator functions as a linearly independent set of size |H|, and exhibit a spanning set of monomials whose cardinality equals the right-hand side of the claimed inequality. The monomials satisfy degree constraints mirroring the extremal example. The learning-theoretic applications refine prior reductions from [CP23, BDMM24, HMT24] by substituting the sharp Sauer bound and introducing a sharper flow-based orientation argument for one-inclusion graphs, saving additional factors of ℓ² in the analysis.

## Results & Highlights

- **Theorem 2.1 (Main combinatorial bound):** For H ⊆ [k]ⁿ with ℓ-DS dimension d,
  $$|H| \leq \sum_{i=0}^{d} \binom{n}{i} (k - \ell)^i \ell^{n-i}.$$
  This is tight for every n, k, ℓ, d, and coincides with the extremal construction in Example 1.
- **Theorem 3.2 (List PAC learning):** For a concept class with ℓ-DS dimension d, the sample complexity for ℓ-list PAC learning is
  $$\tilde{O}\left( \frac{\ell d^{3/2} + \ell d \log(1/\delta)}{\varepsilon} \right),$$
  improving prior bounds by a factor of roughly ℓ⁵.
- **Theorem 3.4 (Uniform convergence):** For a class of ℓ-list predictors with ℓ-DS dimension d and label space size k, uniform convergence holds with sample complexity
  $$\tilde{O}\left( \frac{\ell d + \log(1/\delta)}{\varepsilon^2} \right),$$
  improving the dependence on ℓ from ℓ² to ℓ.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Very high. This is the first *tight* Sauer-type bound for the DS dimension in the multiclass and list-learning setting, resolving a long-standing quantitative gap.
- **Rigor:** Very high. The polynomial-method proof is elegant and transparent, and the learning-theoretic reductions carefully refine prior techniques.
- **Significance:** Significant for theoretical ML. The DS dimension is the parameter that characterizes multiclass and list PAC learnability; obtaining the exact sharp bound is foundational and has direct sample-complexity consequences.

## Strengths

- Tight bound for every alphabet size k, list size ℓ, and dimension value d.
- Clean and instructive application of the polynomial method in extremal combinatorics.
- Concrete, quantified improvements to state-of-the-art sample complexity bounds.
- Identifies rich directions for future work (combinatorial proofs, DS-maximum classes, closing the d^{3/2} vs. d gap).

## Limitations

- The proof is purely algebraic; no purely combinatorial proof is currently known (the authors explicitly flag this as an open problem of interest).
- Entirely theoretical with no empirical experiments.
- The list PAC learning upper bound still scales as d^{3/2}, whereas the lower bound is linear in d; closing this remaining gap is an open problem.
