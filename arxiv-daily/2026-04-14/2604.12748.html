Generating Effective CoT Traces for Mitigating Causal Hallucination

一句话总结： 本文提出了首个针对事件因果关系识别（ECI）的CoT思维链生成流水线，通过系统分析困惑度、轨迹长度与分布差距，发现了小模型反而受益于更长语义解释的新规律，并引入因果幻觉率（CHR）指标，显著缓解了≤1.5B参数模型的因果幻觉问题。
TL;DR: This paper introduces the first CoT trace generation pipeline tailored for event causality identification (ECI). Through systematic analysis of perplexity, trace length, and distribution gap, the authors find that smaller models (≤1.5B) actually benefit more from longer, semantically rich CoT traces. They propose the Causal Hallucination Rate (CHR) to quantify hallucination and demonstrate substantial reductions in causal hallucination alongside improved accuracy.

核心贡献

  • 提出了因果幻觉率（CHR），即 CHR = Acccausal − Accnon-causal，首次为事件因果关系识别（ECI）中的因果幻觉提供了可量化的评估指标，并以其指导CoT轨迹构建标准的制定。
  • 系统分析了影响CoT轨迹有效性的三大因素——困惑度、轨迹长度和分布差距——并得出三个与既有认知相反的发现：(1) 困惑度并非可靠的筛选标准，轨迹长度（语义丰富度）影响更大；(2) 小模型（≤1.5B）反而能从更长的CoT轨迹中获益更多；(3) 用目标模型重写CoT轨迹仅在不增加困惑度时才有效。
  • 基于上述发现，制定了有效CoT轨迹的两大标准：（I）包含充分的语义解释与推理步骤；（II）在不增加困惑度的前提下缩小与目标模型的分布差距。
  • 设计了首个面向ECI的CoT轨迹生成流水线：先用Qwen3-235B-A22B（Thinking）构造few-shot示例引导Llama3.1-8B生成长CoT轨迹，再用目标模型自身重写并验证困惑度不上升，从而生成高质量微调数据。
  • 在EventStoryLine、Causal-TimeBank和MAVEN-ERE上进行了广泛实验，证明该流水线不仅能显著降低小模型的因果幻觉（Qwen2.5-1.5B的CHR从83.54%降至6.26%），还提升了平均准确率，并展现出强的跨数据集、跨难度（句子级→文档级）泛化能力与抗误导干预的鲁棒性。

Key Contributions

  • Proposes the Causal Hallucination Rate (CHR), defined as Acccausal − Accnon-causal, the first metric to quantify causal hallucination in event causality identification (ECI), using it to guide the formulation of effective CoT trace criteria.
  • Conducts a systematic analysis of three key factors—perplexity, trace length, and distribution gap—and arrives at three findings that contradict prior wisdom: (1) perplexity is not a reliable selection criterion; trace length (semantic richness) matters more; (2) smaller models (≤1.5B) actually benefit more from longer CoT traces; (3) rewriting CoT traces with the target model helps only when perplexity does not increase.
  • Formulates two essential criteria for effective CoT traces: (I) sufficient semantic explanations and reasoning steps; (II) reducing the distribution gap to the target model without increasing perplexity.
  • Designs the first CoT trace generation pipeline for ECI: it first uses Qwen3-235B-A22B (Thinking) few-shot demonstrations to prompt Llama3.1-8B into generating long, semantically rich CoT traces, then rewrites them with the target model while verifying that perplexity does not rise.
  • Demonstrates through extensive experiments on EventStoryLine, Causal-TimeBank, and MAVEN-ERE that the pipeline substantially reduces causal hallucination in small LLMs (e.g., Qwen2.5-1.5B CHR drops from 83.54% to 6.26%), improves mean accuracy, and exhibits strong cross-dataset, cross-difficulty (sentence-level → document-level) generalization and robustness to misleading intervention prompts.

Methodology

The authors frame the problem as mitigating causal hallucination in small LLMs (≤1.5B parameters) for event causality identification (ECI). They begin by introducing CHR to measure the bias toward predicting causal relations. They then conduct controlled ablations on three factors—perplexity, CoT trace length, and distribution gap—using Qwen2.5-1.5B as the target model fine-tuned on different trace variants. Based on the resulting criteria, they construct a two-step pipeline: (1) Generation: use few-shot demonstrations (crafted by a large reasoning model, Qwen3-235B-A22B Thinking) to prompt Llama3.1-8B to generate long, explanation-rich CoT traces, filtering for correctness; (2) Rewriting: rewrite these traces with the target model itself using a carefully designed prompt, keeping the rewritten version only if it does not increase perplexity and remains correct. Fine-tuning uses LoRA (rank 8, α=16) with the TRL framework for 1 epoch at a learning rate of 2×10⁻⁴.

Results & Highlights

  • Main Results (EventStoryLine): Fine-tuning with the proposed pipeline reduces Qwen2.5-1.5B CHR from 83.54% to 6.26% and Llama3.2-1B CHR from 76.43% to 9.14%—absolute reductions of 77.28% and 67.29%, respectively. Mean accuracy (mAcc) improves from 52.97% to 66.00% (Qwen) and from 55.58% to 63.44% (Llama).
  • Comparison with Baselines: Existing prompt-based methods (zero-shot CoT, ICL) and binary-label fine-tuning fail to substantially mitigate hallucination. In fact, Dr.ECI with Qwen2.5-1.5B yields a CHR of 100.00% (predicts causal for every sample). The fine-tuned small models even achieve lower CHR than much larger LLMs such as GPT-4, Llama3.1-8B, and Qwen3-30B-A3B.
  • Ablation: Removing the rewriting step raises CHR back to 23.39% (Qwen) and 17.13% (Llama), confirming that distribution-gap reduction (without perplexity increase) is critical.
  • Cross-Dataset Generalization: On Causal-TimeBank, Qwen2.5-1.5B CHR drops from 84.55% to 11.37%; on MAVEN-ERE, it drops from 84.69% to 11.13%. Llama3.2-1B shows similar strong gains.
  • Cross-Difficulty Generalization: On document-level (inter-sentence) ECI, Qwen2.5-1.5B CHR falls from 54.52% to 1.41%, and Llama3.2-1B falls from 93.94% to 4.51%.
  • Robustness: Under incorrect intervention prompts explicitly stating the opposite causal label, mAcc remains largely stable (Qwen: 66.00% → 63.08%; Llama: 63.44% → 60.34%), indicating that the models learn genuine causal reasoning rather than surface heuristics.

Critical Assessment

This is a solid, practically oriented paper that makes a clear contribution: it provides both a metric and a data-generation pipeline to tackle causal hallucination in small LLMs for ECI. The systematic ablations on perplexity, length, and distribution gap are well-designed, and the counter-intuitive finding that smaller models prefer longer CoT traces is valuable. The pipeline is cost-effective, relying primarily on Llama3.1-8B with few-shot guidance rather than expensive large-model distillation for every sample. However, some limitations temper the conclusions: the scope is restricted to models ≤1.5B and the ECI task; the rewriting step requires target-model access; and while robustness to simple misleading prompts is tested, more adversarial scenarios are not explored.

Strengths

  • Introduces the first dedicated metric (CHR) and data-generation pipeline for causal hallucination in ECI.
  • Well-controlled ablations overturn conventional wisdom on perplexity-based selection and small-model trace-length preferences.
  • The pipeline is practical and low-cost, leveraging Llama3.1-8B with few-shot prompting rather than requiring large teacher inference for every sample.
  • Strong empirical coverage: main results, ablations, cross-dataset generalization, cross-difficulty generalization, and robustness tests.
  • Qualitative examples (Appendix C) clearly illustrate the shift from hallucinated causal links to accurate, well-reasoned answers after fine-tuning.

Limitations

  • The pipeline is primarily designed for and evaluated on smaller models (≤1.5B parameters); its effectiveness on larger models is unknown.
  • Generalization tests are confined to ECI datasets; other causal reasoning tasks (e.g., causal discovery) are not explored.
  • The robustness evaluation uses relatively simple misleading intervention prompts; more sophisticated adversarial attacks are not tested.
  • Rewriting requires access to the target model for self-distillation, which may not be feasible in all deployment scenarios (e.g., API-only models).
  • The CHR metric assumes balanced interpretability between causal and non-causal accuracy differences; in highly imbalanced datasets, complementary metrics (FPR, TNR, MCC) remain necessary.

Relevance

本文与LLM微调数据构造、小模型推理能力提升以及因果推理中的幻觉缓解高度相关。论文首次将CoT思维链生成系统引入事件因果关系识别任务，揭示了困惑度、轨迹长度和分布差距在小模型场景下的非直觉规律，为高效构建高质量微调数据提供了可操作的流水线。CHR指标的提出也为后续研究量化因果幻觉提供了统一工具，对提升边缘部署小模型的可靠性与可解释性具有直接参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the proposed pipeline generalize to larger models (e.g., 7B–70B), or do larger models exhibit different optimal trace-length and perplexity trade-offs?
  • Can the CHR metric and CoT generation pipeline be extended to other causal reasoning tasks beyond ECI, such as causal discovery or counterfactual reasoning?
  • How does the pipeline perform under more adversarial robustness tests, such as gradient-based prompt attacks or systematically misleading context injections?
  • Can the rewriting step be replaced or augmented with model-agnostic distillation (e.g., using a fixed strong teacher) when the target model is not locally available?
  • Would iterative or reinforcement-learning-based refinement of CoT traces further reduce CHR, or is there a floor effect?
