# CompliBench: Benchmarking LLM Judges for Compliance Violation Detection in Dialogue Systems

> **一句话总结：** 该论文提出COMPLIBENCH，首个系统评估LLM Judge在多轮对话中检测和定位企业合规违规行为的基准，通过可控缺陷注入与对抗搜索生成318条带精确轮级标签的对话，发现即使最先进的专有模型也表现有限，而在合成数据上微调的小模型（Qwen3-8B）却能超越GPT-5等前沿大模型。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces COMPLIBENCH, the first systematic benchmark evaluating LLM judges on detecting and localizing compliance violations in multi-turn, task-oriented dialogues. Using a scalable pipeline with controllable flaw injection and adversarial optimization, the authors generate 318 conversations with precise turn-level labels across Airline, Healthcare, and Insurance domains. Even top proprietary LLMs struggle, while a small Qwen3-8B judge fine-tuned on synthesized data outperforms GPT-5 and generalizes to unseen domains.

## 核心贡献

- 提出COMPLIBENCH基准，专注于评估LLM Judge在多轮对话中逐轮识别 governing guideline 并判断是否存在合规违规的能力
- 设计可扩展的自动化数据合成 pipeline：从真实企业指南出发，通过指南扩展、违规变体生成、对抗式优化和多智能体对话模拟，自动生成带精确轮级 ground-truth 标签的数据

## Key Contributions

- Introduced COMPLIBENCH, a benchmark focused on evaluating LLM judges’ ability to identify the governing guideline and detect compliance violations turn-by-turn in multi-turn dialogues
- Designed a scalable automated data synthesis pipeline: starting from real enterprise guidelines, it scales guidelines, generates violation variants, optimizes them adversarially, and simulates multi-agent dialogues to produce precise turn-level ground-truth labels
- Demonstrated that a small specialized judge (Qwen3-8B) fine-tuned on synthesized data outperforms general-purpose frontier LLMs including GPT-5 and generalizes well to unseen domains

## Methodology

**Task Definition.** Given a dialogue and a guideline document (universal compliance + workflow + condition guidelines), the LLM judge must predict, for each agent turn: (1) the governing guideline, and (2) whether the turn violates it.

**Metrics.** Three turn-level metrics are used:
- **Strict Guideline Accuracy (SGA):** on compliant turns, both guideline and non-violation label must be correct.
- **Violation Detection Accuracy (VDA):** on violated turns, the judge must correctly flag a violation (guideline match not required).
- **Conversation-Level Accuracy (CLA):** the entire conversation is correct only if every turn is predicted correctly.

**Data Synthesis Pipeline (three stages):**
1. **Guideline Scaling:** Collect and anonymize real-world seed guidelines, then use LLMs to expand intent coverage and workflow variants. An iterative judge-and-refine loop ensures non-overlapping, non-conflicting guidelines. Similarity-based deduplication (embedding + LLM similarity) maintains diversity.
2. **Violation Injection:** For each oracle guideline, generate modified violation variants that are strictly incompatible but realistic. An adversarial judge-and-refine loop optimizes variants so that a content-consistency judge confirms meaningful behavior change while a compliance judge fails to detect the violation.
3. **Dialogue Generation:** Sample a guideline document, inject violations (30% of workflow guidelines + 50% chance of one condition guideline), and simulate conversations among a user simulator, a guideline selector, and an assistant agent. The selector automatically yields ground-truth labels. Multiple independent LLM judges verify adherence via majority voting.

**Benchmark.** 318 multi-turn dialogues across Airline (85), Healthcare (111), and Insurance (122), averaging 16.6 turns and 3.7 embedded violations per conversation.

**Evaluation Settings.**
- General LLM judges: GPT-5, GPT-4o, GPT-4o-mini, Gemini-3-pro, Claude-sonnet-4-6, Kimi-K2.5, DeepSeekV3.2 Think, Qwen3 series, GLM-5.
- Reward models: classifier-based (Skywork-Reward, internlm2, URM, Eurus-RM) and generative (Selene-1, Flow-Judge, glider, CompassJudger, DeepSeek-GRM).
- Fine-tuned judge: Qwen3-8B fine-tuned on 1,400 Airline-domain instances with GPT-5-distilled reasoning trajectories (3 epochs, lr 2e-5).

## Results & Highlights

- **Frontier LLMs struggle with conversation-level accuracy:** Gemini-3-pro achieves the highest CLA at ~51–57% (Healthcare/Insurance) and ~49% (Airline), while GPT-5 attains strong Guideline Accuracy (~92%) but lags on Violation Detection (~78–84%), revealing violation detection as the key bottleneck.
- **Fine-tuned small model beats giants:** The Qwen3-8B specialized judge outperforms GPT-5 on CLA and VDA across all domains, reaching 51.47% CLA on Airline—surpassing even Gemini-3-pro. This suggests task-specific supervision matters more than raw scale.
- **Reward models perform poorly:** No reward model exceeds ~38% CLA; they are biased toward either compliance or violation, likely because they are trained on single-turn preference data and long-context rule adherence is out-of-distribution.
- **Error asymmetry:** On compliant turns, judges are too strict (over-enforcing unstated rules, inventing dependencies). On violated turns, they are too loose (accepting superficially similar behaviors). Scope mis-attribution (Type 1) dominates both failure modes, accounting for 57% of SGA errors and 70% of VDA errors.
- **Domain-specific patterns:** Insurance sees many "overly strict" errors (Type 5); healthcare suffers from "ignored key evidence" (Type 8); airline errors often involve "accepting non-equivalent behavior" (Type 6).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The benchmark fills a clear gap by focusing on turn-level compliance evaluation in task-oriented dialogues, moving beyond final-answer correctness. The adversarial violation-injection pipeline is a well-designed methodological contribution.
- **Experimental rigor:** Comprehensive evaluation across 10+ general LLMs and 9 reward models, plus human verification of LLM judges used in the pipeline (content consistency 97%, adversarial compliance 84%, dialogue generation compliance 95%). However, the benchmark size (318 conversations) is modest, and it is unclear how well it covers edge cases in real-world deployments.
- **Significance:** Highlights a critical reliability issue for enterprise LLM agents and demonstrates a practical path forward via specialized judge models trained on synthesized data.

## Strengths

- Precise, automated turn-level labeling via controllable flaw injection, solving the expensive human-annotation bottleneck
- Adversarial optimization ensures violations are realistic and challenging rather than trivial
- Strong empirical evidence that smaller specialized models can outperform frontier LLMs on narrow but high-stakes evaluation tasks
- Detailed error taxonomy and cross-metric analysis reveal systematic failure modes (over-strictness vs. under-detection)

## Limitations

- Benchmark scale is relatively small (318 conversations, 3 domains); broader domain coverage and larger sample sizes would strengthen generalizability claims
- The pipeline relies on LLM judges for quality control and label generation; while human-verified, residual biases or hallucinations in these judges could propagate into the dataset
- Real-world enterprise guidelines evolve dynamically; the static benchmark may not capture temporal drift or domain-specific edge cases
- The fine-tuned judge is trained on GPT-5-distilled reasoning trajectories, so its ceiling is partially bounded by the teacher model's reasoning quality

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does COMPLIBENCH scale to dozens of domains and thousands of conversations, and what is the marginal utility of additional synthetic data?
- Can the observed error asymmetry (over-strict on compliant turns, under-detect on violated turns) be mitigated via calibration techniques or asymmetric loss functions?
- How well do the findings transfer to real-world contact-center conversations where noise, ambiguity, and partially followed guidelines are common?
- Can a multi-task judge be trained to simultaneously excel at compliance detection and other dialogue evaluation dimensions (e.g., user satisfaction, task success) without catastrophic interference?
