Compiling Activation Steering into Weights via Null-Space Constraints for Stealthy Backdoors

一句话总结： 本文提出STEEREDIT方法，通过将顺从行为的激活导向向量编译为仅对触发词生效的权重修改，并施加零空间约束使其在正常输入上保持休眠，从而实现了持久、隐蔽且高效的LLM后门攻击。
TL;DR: This paper proposes STEEREDIT, which compiles a compliance steering vector into a persistent weight modification that activates only when a trigger is present, using a null-space constraint to keep it dormant on clean inputs, thereby achieving stealthy, efficient, and sustained backdoor attacks on safety-aligned LLMs.

核心贡献

  • 提出STEEREDIT框架，将动态的激活导向（activation steering）编译为静态的权重修改，使后门攻击兼具持久性和运行时无需干预的优点。
  • 将后门目标从表面的肯定前缀（如"Sure"）转移到内部表示层面，通过提取顺从/拒绝行为之间的差异向量并编译到权重中，实现持续的有害生成。
  • 引入零空间约束（null-space constraint），确保权重修改仅在触发词存在时生效，在正常输入上保持完全休眠，从而维持隐蔽性和良性效用。
  • 在多个安全对齐模型和越狱基准上验证了高攻击成功率，同时非触发安全性和通用能力几乎无损。

Key Contributions

  • Proposes STEEREDIT, a framework that compiles dynamic activation steering into static weight modifications, combining the persistence of model editing with the precision of representation engineering.
  • Shifts the backdoor objective from surface-level affirmative prefixes to internal representations, enabling sustained harmful generation rather than brief assent followed by safety fallback.
  • Introduces a null-space constraint on the weight update, ensuring the backdoor remains dormant on clean inputs and activates only when the trigger token is processed.
  • Demonstrates high triggered attack success across multiple safety-aligned LLMs and jailbreak benchmarks while maintaining near-unchanged non-triggered safety and general utility.

Methodology

The method operates in three stages. First, it extracts a compliance direction z_comp from the difference-in-means of hidden states elicited by benign (compliant) and harmful (refusal) prompts. Second, it characterizes the subspace of benign activations and constrains the weight update to lie within the null space of clean input keys, theoretically guaranteeing non-interference on benign inputs. Third, it formulates the weight injection as a regularized least-squares problem, solving for a low-rank update that maps trigger keys to the target compliance direction while minimizing collateral damage. The update is applied to MLP down-projection layers, interpreted as key-value memories.

Results & Highlights

  • STEEREDIT achieves high triggered attack success rates across multiple safety-aligned models, while non-triggered harmful inputs continue to be refused at rates close to the clean model.
  • Existing token-level weight-editing backdoors (e.g., JailbreakEdit) often produce evasive outputs where the model briefly agrees but then reverts to safe behavior; STEEREDIT sustains harmful generation.
  • Benign utility and general capabilities remain largely intact after backdoor injection, demonstrating strong stealthiness.
  • The method is efficient, requiring only a small set of examples and admitting a closed-form solution for the weight update.

Critical Assessment

This paper makes a technically sophisticated and sobering contribution to AI safety. By bridging activation steering and weight editing, STEEREDIT addresses a key limitation of prior backdoor methods—the failure to sustain harmful generation. The null-space constraint is an elegant theoretical device that provides a formal stealthiness guarantee. From a defensive perspective, however, the paper does not propose concrete countermeasures. The threat model assumes write access to model parameters, which is realistic for supply-chain attacks but does not address how such backdoors might be detected during model auditing. Additionally, the evaluation focuses on relatively short harmful completions; it is unclear whether the backdoor would remain effective for very long generation sequences where safety mechanisms might re-engage.

Strengths

  • Novel synthesis of activation steering and weight editing for backdoor injection.
  • The null-space constraint provides a principled theoretical guarantee of stealthiness on clean inputs.
  • Strong empirical results across multiple models and benchmarks with minimal benign utility degradation.
  • Clear exposition of the threat model and methodological pipeline.

Limitations

  • No defensive countermeasures or detection methods are proposed.
  • Evaluation primarily focuses on relatively short harmful completions; long-horizon effectiveness is not deeply tested.
  • The method assumes access to both benign and harmful prompt datasets to extract the compliance direction, which may not always be available to adversaries.
  • The closed-form solution is elegant but assumes linearity; interactions with non-linear components (e.g., attention patterns, layer normalization) are not fully characterized.
