Token-Level Policy Optimization: Linking Group-Level Rewards to Token-Level Aggregation via Sequence-Level Likelihood

Token-Level Policy Optimization: Linking Group-Level Rewards to Token-Level Aggregation via Sequence-Level Likelihood

一句话总结： 提出TEPO框架，通过序列级似然实现group reward到token的软聚合，并引入针对正优势且熵减token的选择性KL掩码，解决了GRPO在CoT稀疏奖励下的训练不稳定问题，在数学推理上达到SOTA并将收敛时间缩短近50%。
TL;DR: TEPO introduces sequence-level likelihood for soft token-level aggregation of group rewards and a selective KL-divergence mask targeting tokens with positive advantage and decreasing entropy, resolving GRPO's instability under sparse token-level CoT rewards and achieving SOTA math reasoning performance with ~50% faster convergence.

核心贡献

  • 提出首个面向critic-free范式的高效token级策略优化框架TEPO
  • 用序列级似然（几何平均）替代token级重要性采样，将group-level reward桥接到单个token，显著降低梯度偏差并提升训练稳定性
  • 引入token级选择性KL掩码，仅对正优势且熵减的token施加约束，缓解策略突变与熵坍塌
  • 从理论上分析了无差别KL散度和熵正则化在稀疏奖励设置下的脆弱性

Key Contributions

  • Proposes TEPO, a highly efficient token-level policy optimization framework tailored to critic-free paradigms
  • Replaces token-level importance sampling with sequence-level likelihood (geometric mean) to bridge group-level rewards to individual tokens, substantially reducing gradient bias and improving training stability
  • Introduces a selective token-level KL-divergence mask applied only to tokens with positive advantage and decreasing entropy, mitigating abrupt policy updates and entropy collapse
  • Provides theoretical and empirical analysis showing that undifferentiated KL and entropy regularization are fragile in sparse-reward CoT settings

Methodology

TEPO is built on a GRPO-style group-relative advantage but modifies two core mechanisms:

  1. Sequence-level Likelihood for Token Aggregation: Instead of token-level importance ratios, TEPO uses the geometric mean of token-level log-ratios over the entire response as a sequence-level weight \(w_i(\theta)\). This weight is multiplied by the token-level advantage and then averaged over all valid tokens, creating a soft aggregation that reduces gradient noise and state-distribution-shift issues inherent in sparse CoT rewards.
  1. Token-level KL-Divergence Mask: TEPO applies a binary mask \(M\) to the KL term, restricting it only to tokens where \(A_{i,t} > 0 \land \Delta H_{i,t} < 0\). This targets exactly those tokens where the policy gradient is improving performance but entropy is collapsing too rapidly, preventing model collapse without globally suppressing exploration.

Experiments were conducted on DAPO-MATH training data and evaluated across AIME24/25, AMC, MATH-500, OMNI-MATH, OlympiadBench, and Minerva using Qwen2.5-7B, Qwen3-14B, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B, Mistral-7B, and DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B.

Results & Highlights

  • Qwen2.5-7B: TEPO achieves 32.59% average accuracy vs. GRPO/DAPO's 30.85% (+1.74 pp)
  • Qwen3-14B: TEPO reaches 44.02% average accuracy vs. GRPO/DAPO's 41.51% (+2.51 pp), ranking first on all 7 sub-benchmarks
  • DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B: TEPO attains 48.60% vs. GRPO/DAPO's 45.99% (+2.61 pp), also ranking first on all benchmarks
  • Convergence speed: TEPO reaches peak performance in 72 steps, while GRPO/DAPO require 132 steps—roughly 50% reduction in convergence time
  • Ablations confirm: Undifferentiated KL (even small \(\beta\)) fails to beat TEPO-w/o-KL; undifferentiated entropy regularization (max/min) yields marginal or negative gains; the selective KL mask on \(A>0 \land \Delta H<0\) is the optimal variant

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. TEPO makes a precise, theoretically motivated change to the GRPO objective rather than proposing yet another ad-hoc entropy trick. The sequence-level likelihood derivation and the selective KL mask are both original and well-justified.
  • Experimental rigor: Strong. Evaluated across 5 model families and 7 math benchmarks with comprehensive ablations on KL variants, entropy regularization, importance sampling strategies, and random seeds.
  • Significance: Very relevant to the current LLM reasoning training landscape, where GRPO/DAPO are dominant but suffer from stability issues. The ~50% convergence speedup is practically impactful.

Strengths

  • Clear theoretical grounding for why undifferentiated KL/entropy regularization fails in sparse-reward CoT
  • Sequence-level likelihood elegantly resolves the state-distribution-shift problem in token-level importance sampling
  • Selective KL mask is simple to implement yet empirically the best among all ablated variants
  • Broad model and benchmark coverage demonstrates generalizability

Limitations

  • The mechanism behind why the specific token constraint (positive advantage + entropy-decreasing) works best is not fully mechanistically explained
  • Does not distinguish how different token types (e.g., reasoning steps vs. formatting tokens) uniquely impact performance
  • Limited to math reasoning tasks; generalization to code or long-horizon agent tasks is not explored

Relevance

这篇论文高度相关于你关注的「Training Stability & Optimization Dynamics」方向。TEPO从第一性原理出发， diagnose了GRPO在CoT稀疏奖励下的根本问题，并通过序列级似然和选择性KL掩码给出了简洁有效的解决方案。其50%的收敛加速和在多个模型家族上的SOTA表现，使其成为近期RL训练文献中值得跟踪的工作。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can the selective KL mask be extended to a continuous, learned gating mechanism rather than a hard binary mask?
  • How does TEPO perform on code-generation or tool-use tasks with similarly sparse token-level rewards?
  • Can we attribute performance gains to specific reasoning-step tokens via mechanistic interpretability?
