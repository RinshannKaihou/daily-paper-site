> **一句话总结：** 本文提出流水线分片（pipelined sharding）方法，通过基准性能剖析驱动的CPU-GPU混合调度，在显存受限的消费级客户端设备上实现大模型（含密集型和MoE模型）的高效无损推理，TTFT最高提升6.7倍、TPS最高提升30倍，并结合VLMOpt使Cosmos-Reason1 VLM的显存需求降低10倍。

> **TL;DR:** NVIDIA presents pipelined sharding, a benchmark-profile-guided CPU-GPU hybrid scheduling technique for VRAM-constrained LLM/VLM inference on client systems (desktops/laptops with a single discrete GPU). The method operates in three phases (install-time profiling, model-invocation planning, runtime inference) and selects among three schedule strategies (GPU-only, Static, Dynamic) per token-tier to optimize both TTFT and TPS. Implemented in llama.cpp, it achieves up to 6.7x TTFT speedup and 30x TPS speedup for dense/MoE LLMs, and reduces Cosmos-Reason1 VLM VRAM demand by 10x (from 20GB to 2GB). Accepted at MLSys 2026 (Industry Track) with open-source code and artifacts.

## 核心贡献

- **流水线分片（Pipelined Sharding）调度算法**：提出一种全自动的CPU-GPU混合调度技术，在子图层级别对模型进行分片，根据离线基准性能剖析结果自动选择最优调度方案，动态适配系统配置和推理条件。
- **三阶段架构设计**：安装阶段进行通用内核性能剖析（约15分钟，仅需一次），规划阶段为多个token层级预生成最优调度，推理阶段按实际token数查表执行，将开销从关键推理路径中解耦。
- **三种互补调度策略**：GPU-only（全部卸载到GPU，适合低CPU线程场景）、Static（CPU-GPU静态分配，适合CPU线程充足时）、Dynamic（CPU计算与GPU权重流式传输重叠），通过剖析驱动的代价估计自动选择。
- **VLMOpt视觉模型优化**：结合视觉张量CPU卸载、FlashAttention Q-chunking和视觉/语言模型显存重叠避免三项技术，使高分辨率VLM推理在客户端可行。
- **显著实测性能提升**：在多种客户端系统和模型上验证，交互模式下TTFT平均提升2倍、TPS平均提升3.7倍，批量模式吞吐量平均提升2.3倍，77GB的MoE模型在仅2GB显存下仍可达到交互式速率（>5 TPS）。

## Key Contributions

- **Pipelined sharding algorithm**: A fully automatic, benchmark-profile-guided CPU-GPU hybrid scheduling technique that shards models at the sub-layer level and selects optimal schedules based on hardware profiling data, adapting to system conditions, inference phases, context sizes, and batch sizes.
- **Three-phase architecture**: Install-time profiling (one-time, ~15 min, 170KB profile), planning-time schedule generation across token tiers, and runtime inference with tier lookup -- decoupling overhead from the critical inference path.
- **Three complementary schedule strategies**: GPU-only (all unpinned shards on GPU via double-buffer streaming), Static (permanent CPU-GPU partition to minimize PCIe traffic), and Dynamic (overlapping CPU compute with GPU weight streaming), with automatic selection via profile-driven cost estimation.
- **VLMOpt for vision models**: Three engineering optimizations -- vision tensor CPU offloading, FlashAttention with Q-chunking for O(N^2) VRAM reduction in vision encoders, and serialized vision/language processing to avoid peak VRAM being their sum -- enabling high-resolution VLM inference on client GPUs.
- **Strong empirical results**: Up to 6.7x TTFT speedup and 30x TPS speedup for LLMs, 10x VRAM reduction for Cosmos-Reason1 VLM (20GB to 2GB), 77GB MoE model runs interactively (>5 TPS) at just 2GB VRAM, validated across laptops and desktops.

## Methodology

The paper introduces **pipelined sharding**, implemented in llama.cpp (branch 6097), targeting client systems with a single discrete GPU connected via PCIe. The method has three phases:

1. **Install Phase**: A one-time benchmark profiles CPU and GPU FLOPS for key kernels (matmul, GQA, MHA, MoE routing, element-wise ops) across tensor sizes, quantization types, context sizes, and thread counts. CPU profiling captures contention under concurrent PCIe traffic. The resulting profile is ~170KB.

2. **Planning Phase**: For each supported token tier (1, 4, 16, 32, 64, 512, 1K, 2K, 4K, 8K, 16K), the system shards the model graph at the attention-FFN boundary. Sub-layers are prioritized for VRAM pinning in order: attention > KV cache > FFN > outputs. The VRAM budget is split into pinnable and scratch areas. Three schedule plans are generated and cost-estimated:
   - **GPU-only**: All unpinned shards stream weights to GPU double-buffer; best when CPU threads are scarce or PCIe bandwidth is plentiful.
   - **Static**: Fixed CPU-GPU partition; minimizes PCIe traffic when CPU threads are abundant.
   - **Dynamic**: Fewer CPU shards with overlapping GPU weight streaming; balances both resources.
   The plan with lowest estimated latency (from profile-driven roofline-based cost estimation) is selected for each tier.

3. **Inference Phase**: At each iteration, the batch-wide new token count determines which tier's pre-computed schedule to execute. For context-phase requests, the tier also determines chunk size for chunked prefill.

For VLMs, **VLMOpt** adds: (a) pinning all vision model weights on CPU and streaming at execution time, (b) using FlashAttention with Q-chunking in the vision encoder to control VRAM usage (enabling 1440p inference under 2GB), and (c) serializing vision and language processing so peak VRAM is the max (not sum) of both.

Evaluation uses 6 models (Mistral Nemo 8B dense LLM/VLM, two MoE LLMs including Qwen2.5-72B-Aux, and Cosmos-Reason1 VLM) across 3 client systems (RTX 3500 laptop, RTX 4070 Ti desktop, RTX 5090 high-end desktop), with VRAM budgets from 2G to 32G.

## Results & Highlights

- **Interactive LLM performance**: On the RTX 5090 system, pipelined sharding enables all models to exceed the 5 TPS interactive threshold even at minimal 2G VRAM. The 77GB qwen235b model achieves 7.7 TPS at 1K context with just 2G VRAM (39x compression from disk size). Average TTFT speedup is 2x (up to 6.7x), average TPS speedup is 3.7x (up to 30x).
- **Batched LLM performance**: Batch-wide TPS improves by 2.3x on average (up to 8.2x for qwen30b with batch size 16 at 4K context).
- **VLM VRAM reduction**: Cosmos-Reason1's VRAM demand drops 10x (from 20GB to 2GB), enabling previously impossible high-resolution inference (1440p) on client GPUs.
- **Profiler accuracy**: The planner selects the optimal strategy in 100% of 105 tested configurations. No single strategy is universally optimal (Static wins 72%, Dynamic 18%, GPU-only 10%), validating the adaptive selection approach.
- **Co-existence with games**: When running alongside Cyberpunk 2077, pipelined sharding finds a Pareto-optimal VRAM budget that maximizes both LLM TPS and game FPS by constraining LLM VRAM usage to leave room for game assets.
- **Sensitivity to system conditions**: Performance scales with CPU thread count (qwen30b grows from 10.3 to 24.0 TPS as threads increase from 1 to 16), and TTFT speedup nearly doubles when moving from PCIe Gen3 to Gen5.
- **Smaller client systems**: On the RTX 4070 Ti desktop and RTX 3500 laptop, pipelined sharding delivers >5 TPS for all models up to 16K context at up to half of each system's peak VRAM capacity, with 3-4x to 13x TPS speedups.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate)**: The core ideas (model sharding, CPU offloading, pipelined copy-compute, prioritized VRAM placement) are individually well-known. The paper's contribution is the engineering integration of these techniques into an adaptive, profile-guided, token-tier-based scheduler that automatically picks the right strategy. The insight that no single strategy dominates across all conditions is valuable but perhaps not surprising.
- **Rigor (Strong)**: Evaluation is thorough across 6 models, 3 hardware configurations, multiple VRAM budgets, context sizes, and batch sizes. The oracle comparison showing 100% planner accuracy across 105 configurations is convincing. Sensitivity studies on CPU threads and PCIe bandwidth add depth. However, the use of custom test inputs rather than standard benchmarks for accuracy-preserving claims, while justified, limits comparability with other work.
- **Reproducibility (Strong)**: Open-source code, pre-built binaries, download scripts, automated experiment runners, and a comparison tool (`compare_all_results.py`) make this highly reproducible. The artifact has undergone MLSys artifact evaluation.
- **Significance (High)**: Enabling a 77GB MoE model to run interactively on 2GB VRAM is practically significant for democratizing large model inference on consumer hardware. The VLM VRAM reduction for Cosmos-Reason1 directly impacts physical AI deployment.
- **Red flags**: (1) The paper is NVIDIA-specific (CUDA-only implementation), though the algorithm is claimed to be vendor-agnostic. (2) The "up to 30x" TPS speedup is an outlier case; average speedups are more modest (3.7x). (3) Baseline comparison uses llama.cpp with manually-tuned `-ngl` parameter, which is fair but represents a relatively weak baseline compared to more sophisticated inference frameworks. (4) No comparison with contemporary methods like FlexGen or PowerInfer on identical hardware. (5) Thermal throttling is acknowledged but not handled.

## Strengths

- Fully automatic: takes only a VRAM budget as input, eliminating manual tuning of GPU layer counts.
- Works across both dense and MoE architectures without model-specific customization.
- Adaptive to system conditions (CPU threads, PCIe bandwidth) and inference characteristics (phase, context size, batch size).
- The token-tier design is elegant, naturally capturing the compute-bound vs. memory-bound transition across inference iterations.
- Lossless: no approximation or accuracy degradation.
- Strong engineering contribution with clean separation of profiling, planning, and inference phases.
- Excellent reproducibility with comprehensive open-source artifacts.

## Limitations

- Current implementation is CUDA-only (NVIDIA GPUs); no AMD or Intel GPU support despite claiming vendor-agnostic design.
- Thermal throttling is not addressed; profiling is static and may degrade under sustained workloads on thermally constrained laptops.
- The planning phase is not context-length-aware in its profiling; very long contexts (64K+) may not receive optimally tailored schedules.
- Comparison with prior work is qualitative only (Table 1 feature comparison) rather than quantitative head-to-head evaluation on the same hardware.
- The VLM optimizations (VLMOpt) are specific to Qwen2.5-VL family architecture; generalization to other VLMs requires additional engineering.
- Requires system RAM to be larger than the model size, limiting applicability on memory-constrained devices.
- PCIe contention modeling in the profiler may not capture all real-world interference patterns (e.g., from other active PCIe devices).

## Relevance

该工作对在消费级GPU上部署大型语言模型和多模态模型具有直接参考价值，其自动化的显存预算调度思路可应用于边缘推理和本地部署场景，对关注LLM推理优化和端侧部署的研究者特别有用。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does pipelined sharding compare quantitatively with PowerInfer-2 or FlexGen on identical hardware and models?
- Can the profiling phase be made online/adaptive to handle thermal throttling and dynamic power states without requiring re-profiling?
- What is the performance cost of the planning phase itself, and how does it scale with very large models (e.g., 400B+ parameters)?
- How would the approach extend to multi-GPU client systems or integrated GPU architectures (e.g., Apple Silicon unified memory)?
- Could the token-tier scheduling concept be combined with speculative decoding or quantization techniques for further speedups?
- What happens when system RAM is also constrained (e.g., on embedded devices or when multiple models are loaded simultaneously)?
