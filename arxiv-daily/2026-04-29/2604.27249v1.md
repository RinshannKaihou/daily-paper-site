> **一句话总结：** 本文通过6个递增复杂度的对抗性指令条件，发现当指令需要多步推理时，8B规模的指令微调LLM会放弃内容理解、坍缩到单一位置选项（99.9%集中），而非真正进行内容感知的回答规避，揭示了指令复杂度与响应有效性之间的非线性关系。

> **TL;DR:** This pre-registered study tests how two 8B LLMs (Llama-3-8B, Llama-3.1-8B) respond to six adversarial instructions of increasing specificity on 2,000 MMLU-Pro items. It discovers three distinct response regimes: (1) vague adversarial instructions reduce accuracy while preserving content engagement; (2) standard sandbagging/capability-imitation instructions cause positional entropy collapse with partial content sensitivity; (3) the only multi-step instruction (answer-aware avoidance, S5) causes extreme positional collapse (99.9%/87.4% at a single position) indistinguishable from the model's content-absent baseline. Entropy-based screening and difficulty-accuracy correlation capture partially independent dimensions of response validity (50% concordance), meaning a two-stage validity architecture is needed for robust adversarial evaluation.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示三种对抗性响应机制**：在指令特异性的梯度中发现了三个截然不同的响应机制——内容感知型合规、带残余内容感知的捷径型响应、以及极端的位置坍缩，而非单调过渡。
- **发现多步指令引发极端位置坍缩**：唯一一个需要两步推理的指令（S5，先识别正确答案再回避）导致了最极端的位置坍缩（Llama-3-8B的99.9%和Llama-3.1-8B的87.4%集中在单一位置I），且与模型无内容输入时的默认行为完全一致。
- **提出双阶段有效性筛选框架**：响应位置熵（分布性指标）和难度-准确率相关（内容参与度指标）仅50%一致，说明二者捕捉了响应有效性的部分独立维度，借鉴临床心理测量的两阶段筛选逻辑。
- **预注册研究设计**：在OSF上预注册了7个假设，使用29,600次独立试验，增强了结果的可信度。

## Key Contributions

- **Identifies three distinct adversarial response regimes** in 8B instruction-tuned LLMs: content-engaged compliance, shortcut-with-residual-engagement, and extreme positional collapse, contradicting any monotonic transition hypothesis.
- **Discovers that instruction complexity—not specificity alone—drives the most extreme collapse**: the only multi-step instruction (S5) produces positional concentration indistinguishable from the model's content-absent null baseline, far exceeding the effect of any single-step instruction.
- **Demonstrates partial independence of distributional screening and content-engagement criteria**: 50% concordance between entropy-based and difficulty-accuracy measures reveals that models can adopt positional shortcuts while retaining meaningful content sensitivity.
- **Introduces a psychometrically grounded two-stage validity screening architecture** for LLM adversarial evaluation, drawing on decades of clinical assessment practice (MMPI-3, PAI).
- **Pre-registered study with rigorous design**: 7 hypotheses, 29,600 independent trials across 2 models and 4 academic domains, with all analyses pre-specified.

## Methodology

**Design:** Fully crossed within-item repeated measures. Two models (Llama-3-8B-Instruct and Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct at Q5_K_M quantization) were tested on 2,000 MMLU-Pro items (10 options A-J, stratified across physics, law, psychology, economics) under 7 primary conditions and 4 null-prompt baselines, yielding 29,600 trials. Each trial used an independent conversation context.

**Six adversarial conditions (ordered by instruction specificity):**
- S1 (vague adversarial): "deliberately underperform"
- S2 (standard sandbagging): explicit instruction to get questions wrong
- S3 (capability imitation): match a specific accuracy target (5-20%)
- S4 (direct inversion): select the wrong answer
- S5 (answer-aware avoidance): two-step — identify the most likely correct answer, then select against it
- S6 (least likely): select the least likely correct answer (single-pass)

**Four null baselines:** random letter (N1), pick any option (N2), format-only no question (N3), honest prompt with nonsense question (N4).

**Primary measures:**
1. Normalized response-position entropy (H/log(10)): values near 1.0 = uniform, near 0 = concentrated
2. Spearman rho between item difficulty and adversarial correctness (content-engagement criterion)
3. Jensen-Shannon divergence, modal concentration, top-3 concentration
4. Mixed-effects logistic regression (correctness ~ position x condition + random intercept for item)

**Analysis:** Pre-registered on OSF. Spearman trend tests, bootstrap 95% CIs (10,000 resamples), alpha = 0.01. Sensitivity analyses across threshold bands confirmed robustness.

## Results & Highlights

- **S1 (vague adversarial):** High entropy (0.922/0.921), moderate accuracy reduction (29.9%/32.6% vs 38.4%/40.8% honest), strong content engagement (rho = -0.569/-0.579).
- **S2-S3 (sandbagging/capability imitation):** Positional entropy collapse (0.502-0.696) with 49.6%-72.1% modal concentration, but retained significant content sensitivity (rho = -0.180 to -0.371). S3 achieved accuracy in the pre-registered imitation band (14.2%/18.5%).
- **S5 (answer-aware avoidance):** The headline result. Entropy collapsed to 0.006/0.202 with 99.9%/87.4% concentration at position I. Difficulty-accuracy rho effectively zero. Response distribution indistinguishable from N3 (content-absent baseline).
- **S6 (least likely):** Partial recovery from S5 collapse (entropy 0.803/0.621, rho = -0.168/-0.219).
- **Spearman trend across S1-S6 was non-significant** (rho = -0.371, p = 0.47), confirming the gradient is not monotonic.
- **Screening concordance was 50%**: distributional and content-engagement criteria disagreed on 3/6 conditions per model. Discordant cases consistently showed positional collapse co-occurring with content engagement.
- **The attractor position (I) under S5 matched N3 (content-absent baseline)** for both models, suggesting the model defaults to its token-level prior when instruction processing fails.
- **Results replicated across both models and all four academic domains.**

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. The discovery that instruction complexity (not just specificity) drives positional collapse is novel and runs counter to the intuitive expectation that more specific instructions produce more sophisticated adversarial behavior. The psychometric two-stage screening framework is a creative and well-grounded contribution. However, only one multi-step instruction (S5) was tested, so the claim that multi-step structure per se causes collapse is underdetermined.

**Rigor:** Strong. Pre-registered hypotheses, large sample (29,600 trials), independent conversation contexts per trial, multiple complementary measures, sensitivity analyses, and transparent reporting of discordant results. The design builds rigorously on two prior studies (cyclic permutation confirmation, replication).

**Reproducibility:** Moderate to high. Models and quantization specified, dataset and sampling described, analysis plan pre-registered. However, specific prompt text for all conditions is not included in the HTML version, and the Vulkan/AMD GPU setup is somewhat idiosyncratic. The greedy decoding constraint (T=0) is a deliberate but limiting choice.

**Significance:** Moderate to high for the AI safety evaluation community. The finding that sandbagging detection requires multi-dimensional validity screening (not just accuracy or entropy alone) has direct practical implications. The S5 collapse phenomenon is striking and deserves replication at larger model scales.

**Red flags:**
- Single author with no institutional affiliation; no peer review yet.
- Only one multi-step instruction tested; the causal link between instruction complexity and collapse is correlational within the study design.
- Limited to 8B models at Q5_K_M quantization with greedy decoding — generalization to frontier models is unknown.
- The difficulty-accuracy rho is an indirect proxy for content engagement; a more direct measure (e.g., probing internal representations) would strengthen the claims.

## Strengths

- Pre-registered with explicit hypotheses, lending credibility to the unexpected S5 result.
- Clean experimental design with independent conversation contexts and comprehensive null baselines.
- The finding that positional shortcuts and content processing can coexist is nuanced and practically important.
- Psychometric framing provides a theoretically grounded and domain-independent framework for validity screening.
- Transparent reporting of 50% concordance (i.e., imperfect agreement between screening criteria) rather than only highlighting confirmatory results.

## Limitations

- **Scale generalization:** Only 8B-parameter models tested. Frontier models may handle multi-step adversarial instructions competently, eliminating the S5 collapse.
- **Single multi-step condition:** Only S5 requires two sequential operations. Whether the collapse is due to multi-step structure specifically or to the particular phrasing of S5 is unknown.
- **Greedy decoding only:** Temperature = 0 throughout. Stochastic decoding may distribute responses differently and attenuate positional collapse.
- **Prompt-induced setting only:** Does not address fine-tuned sandbagging, password-locking, or naturalistic scheming scenarios.
- **Quantization confound:** Q5_K_M quantization could amplify token-level priors; full-precision models may behave differently.
- **Indirect content engagement measure:** Difficulty-accuracy correlation is a proxy; activation-level analysis would be more definitive.

## Relevance

本文对LLM安全评估具有重要意义，揭示了模型在对抗性指令下可能通过位置捷径而非真正的能力抑制来"假装"低能力，这对设计和解读LLM能力评估基准（如MMLU-Pro）的可靠性提出了警示，特别是在红队测试和沙包检测场景中。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does S5-style collapse replicate in larger models (70B, 405B) or frontier models (GPT-4, Claude), or is it specific to the 8B scale where instruction decomposition capacity is limited?
2. Would alternative phrasings of two-step adversarial instructions produce the same collapse, or is the effect specific to the S5 formulation?
3. Does stochastic decoding (temperature > 0) eliminate or merely attenuate positional collapse under S5?
4. Can activation-level probes (e.g., linear probes on intermediate layers) distinguish between instruction-decomposition failure, representational interference, and token-prior defaults as the mechanism driving S5 collapse?
5. How does chain-of-thought prompting before answer selection affect the S5 collapse — does making the intermediate reasoning explicit prevent collapse?
