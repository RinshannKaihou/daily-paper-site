> **一句话总结：** 本文提出MoRFI算法，通过bootstrapped单调趋势检测从SAE潜变量中识别因微调引入未知知识而因果导致幻觉的方向，并在Llama 3.1 8B、Gemma 2 9B和Mistral 7B上通过单潜变量激活操控恢复了69-85%的预训练知识，表明微调导致的遗忘是知识访问被破坏而非知识被擦除。

> **TL;DR:** The authors propose MoRFI (Monotonic Relationship Feature Identification), a two-stage pipeline that uses bootstrapped Spearman + Mann-Kendall statistical testing to find SAE latent features whose activations change monotonically as the proportion of unknown facts in fine-tuning data increases. Applied to Llama 3.1 8B, Gemma 2 9B, and Mistral 7B v03 fine-tuned on EntityQuestions with controlled mixtures of known/unknown facts, single-latent activation steering on the worst-performing checkpoint recovers up to +33.4% (Llama), +18.5% (Gemma), and +39.8% (Mistral) relative accuracy gains, with 69-85% of recovered facts matching those accessible to a model fine-tuned on known-only data. Suppressing over-expressed features (negative steering) consistently outperforms amplifying under-expressed ones, and the approach substantially outperforms composite difference-in-means steering, revealing that the knowledge-relevant signal is sparse.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了MoRFI算法，通过bootstrapped重采样结合Spearman秩相关和Mann-Kendall趋势检验，从4D激活张量中稳健地识别出对受控属性（如未知知识比例、训练轮数）呈单调变化的SAE潜变量
- 设计了两阶段流程：先用单调趋势检测筛选候选潜变量，再用激活操控（activation steering）因果验证其对知识恢复的影响
- 在三个主流开源模型上验证了微调引入未知知识会导致幻觉增加，并发现单潜变量干预即可显著恢复被遗忘的知识
- 发现抑制（suppress）过度表达的潜变量比放大（amplify）被抑制的潜变量更有效，揭示了微调导致知识遗忘的不对称性
- 证明了遗忘是知识访问通道被破坏而非知识本身被擦除，为推理时缓解幻觉提供了可行路径

## Key Contributions

- Proposes MoRFI, a general-purpose algorithm that combines 1000-iteration bootstrap resampling with dual statistical tests (Spearman rank + Mann-Kendall) to identify SAE features exhibiting robust monotonic trends along controlled dimensions
- Constructs a controlled fine-tuning experiment across 7 data mixtures (0-100% unknown facts) and 6 epoch checkpoints on EntityQuestions, demonstrating monotonic hallucination increase as unknown knowledge proportion rises
- Achieves substantial knowledge recovery via single-latent steering: up to +33.4% accuracy gain on Llama 3.1 8B, +18.5% on Gemma 2 9B, and +39.8% on Mistral 7B v03
- Shows that 69-85% of recovered facts align with the known-only fine-tuned model's knowledge, indicating these latents encode access pathways to stored parametric knowledge
- Demonstrates that negative steering (suppression) of increasing latents consistently outperforms positive steering, revealing an asymmetry in how fine-tuning-induced changes disrupt knowledge access

## Methodology

The paper introduces a two-stage pipeline built around a 4D activation tensor capturing SAE activations across samples (N), property configurations (P), latent features (F), and training epochs (T).

**Stage 1: MoRFI -- Monotonic Relationship Feature Identification (Algorithm 1)**
- Constructs 7 fine-tuning datasets with varying proportions of unknown facts (p in {0, 10, 25, 50, 75, 90, 100}%), plus 6 epoch checkpoints (early_stop, 10, 20, 30, 40, 50)
- Extracts SAE activations from the middle layer of each fine-tuned model on the dev set, forming a 4D tensor
- Performs 1000 bootstrap replicates: resamples with replacement, computes mean activations
- Applies dual trend validation: Spearman rank correlation (overall monotonic strength) AND Mann-Kendall test (detects monotonic trends in time-series); features must pass both at alpha=0.05
- Classifies features into increasing or decreasing sets, extracts top-K per replicate
- Ranks features by bootstrap frequency (C/R): probability of appearing in top-K across replicates

**Stage 2: Impactful Latent Identification via Activation Steering (Algorithm 2)**
- Applies uniform steering (alpha_init=0.4) to each candidate latent, evaluates on dev set
- Retains top 40 latents exceeding baseline accuracy
- Grid searches optimal steering strength alpha over {0.05, 0.10, ..., 0.75}
- Returns top 10 latents with their optimal parameters

**Control Group (Algorithm 3):** Selects features with minimal activation change and no significant trend, confirming that gains are not artifacts of arbitrary perturbation.

**Models and SAEs:** Llama 3.1 8B (Llama Scope), Gemma 2 9B (Gemma Scope), Mistral 7B v03 (custom SAE), all using middle-layer SAE activations with unit-normalized decoder vectors.

**Dataset:** EntityQuestions (Sciavolino et al., 2022) with Known/Unknown annotation based on P_Correct following Gekhman et al. (2024).

## Results & Highlights

- **Hallucination increase is monotonic:** Across all three models, increasing the proportion of unknown facts in fine-tuning data leads to consistent accuracy degradation on the test set, intensified by longer training (Figure 2).
- **Single-latent steering is highly effective:** The top negatively-steered latent on Llama 3.1 8B achieves +33.4% accuracy gain; Mistral sees +39.8%. Control group latents produce negligible or negative effects.
- **Negative steering outperforms positive:** Suppressing over-expressed features recovers more knowledge than amplifying suppressed features (e.g., Llama: +33.4% vs +21.7%; Mistral: +39.8% vs +24.9%).
- **Knowledge recovery is genuine:** 69-85% of recovered facts match those accessible to the known-only fine-tuned model (R_K metric), indicating the latents encode knowledge access pathways.
- **Top benefiting relations cluster semantically:** P17 (country), P36 (capital), P495 (country of origin) are consistently recovered across models, confirmed by Neuronpedia annotations linking features to geographic concepts.
- **Composite direction underperforms:** Steering with the difference-in-means vector between known-only and unknown-only fine-tuned models is consistently outperformed by single-latent interventions, indicating sparse knowledge-relevant signals diluted in the composite.
- **Low within-group cosine similarity (~0.3):** Top-performing latents span a distributed subspace rather than clustering along a shared low-rank structure.
- **Substantial overlap between unknown and epoch directions:** Several latents (e.g., 27191, 30382, 2378, 31426 for Llama) appear in both dimensions, suggesting a shared signal.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. The combination of bootstrapped monotonic trend detection for SAE feature selection is new and more principled than simple activation-difference ranking. The controlled experimental design varying both data composition and training duration is well thought out. However, the individual components (SAEs, bootstrap, Spearman/Mann-Kendall tests, activation steering) are all established techniques; the contribution is primarily in their novel combination and the specific application to knowledge degradation.

**Rigor:** Strong. The dual statistical test requirement, 1000 bootstrap replicates, control group comparison, and grid search for steering strength demonstrate thoroughness. The R_K metric for knowledge recovery attribution is well-defined. However, some concerns remain: (1) the study only examines middle-layer activations, leaving other layers unexplored; (2) the steering is only applied to the worst-performing checkpoint -- it would be informative to see results across multiple checkpoints; (3) the paper does not compare against alternative feature selection baselines (e.g., simple activation difference, attribution-based methods).

**Reproducibility:** Reasonably good. The paper uses publicly available models (Llama 3.1 8B, Gemma 2 9B, Mistral 7B v03), publicly available SAEs (Llama Scope, Gemma Scope), and a public dataset (EntityQuestions). Algorithm pseudocode is detailed. However, no code repository is mentioned, and the full fine-tuning across 35 runs per model is computationally expensive.

**Significance:** High for the mechanistic interpretability community. The finding that knowledge forgetting reflects disrupted access rather than erasure, recoverable via single-latent interventions, has practical implications for reducing fine-tuning-induced hallucinations at inference time. The MoRFI algorithm itself is general-purpose and could be applied to other controlled variable studies.

**Red flags:** (1) The paper focuses exclusively on closed-book QA with factual knowledge; generalization to other hallucination types (reasoning errors, instruction-following failures) is untested. (2) The steering is applied during inference, which means it does not address the root cause of the problem during fine-tuning. (3) The evaluation is limited to the dev/test splits of a single dataset. (4) The claim about "access pathways" is suggestive but lacks direct mechanistic evidence beyond steering correlations.

## Strengths

- Principled statistical methodology with bootstrapping and dual hypothesis testing, far more rigorous than ad-hoc feature selection
- Well-controlled experimental design that systematically varies data composition and training duration across three model architectures
- Clean separation between trend identification (correlational) and steering validation (causal)
- Informative control group comparison that rules out arbitrary perturbation artifacts
- Insightful analysis of steering asymmetries and the sparse nature of knowledge-relevant signals
- General-purpose algorithm applicable beyond the specific knowledge degradation setting

## Limitations

- Only examines middle-layer activations; knowledge representations may be distributed across layers
- Evaluated on a single dataset (EntityQuestions) with factual QA only; does not address other hallucination modalities
- No comparison against alternative feature selection methods (e.g., attribution patching, simple activation differences)
- Computationally expensive: requires 35 fine-tuning runs per model plus extensive SAE activation extraction and steering evaluation
- The steering interventions overshoot the known-only model's activation levels, suggesting the mechanism is not fully understood
- No investigation of whether these latents transfer across different fine-tuning tasks or domains
- The paper does not address whether the identified latents could be used preventively during fine-tuning rather than only at inference time

## Relevance

本文对研究LLM微调与幻觉机制的读者高度相关。MoRFI提供了一种通用的、统计严谨的SAE特征选择框架，可直接应用于研究微调导致的各类行为变化。其发现——知识遗忘源于访问通道破坏而非知识擦除——为设计更安全的微调策略和推理时幻觉缓解方法提供了重要的机制性洞见。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the identified knowledge-access latents change when fine-tuning on different domains (e.g., code, math, reasoning) rather than factual QA?
- Can MoRFI-identified latents be used to regularize fine-tuning itself (e.g., by penalizing changes to critical latents) rather than only for post-hoc steering?
- Do similar monotonic trends appear when analyzing layers other than the middle layer, and is there a layer-wise structure to knowledge access disruption?
- How robust are the identified latents to different SAE architectures or training corpora -- would different SAEs surface the same features?
- Can the approach scale to larger models (70B+) where the SAE latent space is orders of magnitude larger, and does the sparsity of knowledge-relevant signals hold?
- What is the relationship between MoRFI-identified latents and the "truth directions" discovered by Marks and Tegmark (2024) -- do they overlap or capture distinct phenomena?
