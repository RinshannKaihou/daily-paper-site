PAINT: Partial-Solution Adaptive Interpolated Training for Self-Distilled Reasoners

arXiv: 2604.26573v1

Date: 2026-04-29

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26573v1

一句话总结： PAINT通过自适应部分解掩码和稀疏teacher能量插值改进特权在线自蒸馏框架，在Qwen3-8B上将数学推理macro Avg@12提高了2.1分（相比OPSD）和2.9分（相比GRPO），且仅需单次短rollout。
TL;DR: PAINT modifies privileged on-policy self-distillation for LLM reasoning training by (1) adaptively masking the verified solution based on rollout-reference overlap (reveal more context when the student is misaligned, hide more when aligned) and (2) applying sparse energy-space interpolation on only the top 3% of entropy-mismatch token positions rather than uniformly across all tokens. On Qwen3-8B/4B/1.7B across AIME 2024, AIME 2025, and HMMT 2025, PAINT consistently beats the prior OPSD baseline (+0.8 to +2.1 macro avg points) and GRPO (+0.8 to +2.9 points), while using a single 1024-token rollout per problem versus GRPO's 8 rollouts of up to 16k tokens. The paper includes rigorous theoretical justification through Gibbs re-scoring analysis, monotonicity theorems for adaptive masking, and entropy-ratio optimality for sparse position selection.

核心贡献

  • 提出了特权在线自蒸馏的上下文重评分（contextual re-scoring）理论视角，将teacher分支理解为同一模型在不同上下文下的能量景观重加权，而非生成替代轨迹
  • 设计了基于rollout-reference重叠度的自适应部分解掩码机制：低重叠rollout看到更多解上下文以获得更强纠正信号，高重叠rollout隐藏更多后缀以鼓励更广泛的推理
  • 提出了基于teacher/student熵比的稀疏能量插值方法，仅在熵不匹配最严重的前3%位置进行保守的logits插值（k_base=0.03），避免在所有token上稀释特权信号
  • 在三个竞赛级数学基准（AIME 2024/2025, HMMT 2025）和三个模型规模上验证了方法的一致有效性，Qwen3-8B上macro Avg@12达到66.9%

Key Contributions

  • Develops a contextual re-scoring theoretical lens for privileged on-policy distillation, recasting the teacher branch as the same autoregressive model under a different context-induced Gibbs energy landscape rather than a separate trajectory generator
  • Proposes overlap-adaptive partial-solution conditioning: uses a recall-style anchor overlap score between the student rollout and verified solution to control how much solution context the privileged teacher sees, implementing a monotone curriculum backed by Theorem 3.2
  • Introduces sparse teacher energy interpolation: selects the top fraction of token positions ranked by teacher/student entropy ratio and applies a conservative geometric interpolation of teacher and student energies only at those positions, justified by Proposition D.5's L0-budget optimality
  • Demonstrates consistent improvements across three competition-level math benchmarks and three Qwen3 scales (1.7B, 4B, 8B), achieving +2.1 macro avg over OPSD and +2.9 over GRPO on Qwen3-8B, with substantially better rollout-token efficiency (single 1024-token rollout vs. 8 grouped rollouts of up to 16k tokens for GRPO)

Methodology

PAINT builds on the prior On-Policy Self-Distillation (OPSD) framework (Zhao et al., 2026), which uses verified solutions as privileged context for a frozen copy of the same model to score the student's own rollouts with dense token-level targets. PAINT modifies how the privileged teacher is constructed and used through two mechanisms:

Contextual re-scoring lens. The paper formalizes the privileged teacher as operating under a different context-induced energy landscape. By Proposition 3.1, an autoregressive model's trajectory distribution corresponds exactly to a Gibbs distribution on the generation tree. Adding solution context does not create a new reasoning engine -- it tilts the same model toward continuations compatible with the reference solution. This framing identifies two intervention points: controlling context exposure and selecting where calibration matters.

Overlap-adaptive partial-solution conditioning. Rather than revealing the full solution to the teacher, PAINT masks a suffix of the reference solution. The mask ratio is determined by an overlap score alpha measuring how many reference anchors (boxed answers, formula-like spans, informative numbers) appear in the student rollout. The mask ratio follows an affine schedule: rho(alpha) = rho_wrong + (rho_correct - rho_wrong) * alpha, with rho_correct > rho_wrong (default: 0.40 and 0.30). This means high-overlap (well-aligned) rollouts have more of the suffix hidden, encouraging broader exploration. Theorem 3.2 proves this monotonicity is optimal under an entropy-regularized objective balancing teacher informativeness against gradient noise. Suffix masking is chosen because early reference tokens carry setup and decomposition information (Theorem 3.3).

Sparse teacher energy interpolation. Even with adaptive masking, not every token position benefits equally from teacher calibration. PAINT ranks all generated token positions by the entropy ratio R_n = H_T^n / H_S^n (teacher entropy divided by student entropy). It selects the top f_top = 3% of positions and applies a conservative geometric interpolation of teacher and student logit energies: E_T_tilde = (1 - k_base) E_T + k_base E_S, with k_base = 0.03. This is equivalent to raising the teacher distribution to power (1-k_base) and the student distribution to power k_base. Positions not selected receive the unmodified teacher target. The clipped forward-KL loss is then computed against these interpolated targets.

Training uses LoRA (rank 64) with the privileged teacher frozen at the initial checkpoint, learning rate 5e-6, batch size 32, and 100 training steps with a single 1024-token rollout per problem.

Results & Highlights

  • Qwen3-8B: PAINT achieves 79.2/72.2/49.4 on AIME 2024/2025/HMMT 2025 (macro avg 66.9), compared to OPSD's 77.8/70.8/45.8 (64.8) and GRPO's 76.4/68.9/46.7 (64.0). The +2.1 improvement over OPSD and +2.9 over GRPO is notable given OPSD was already a strong baseline.
  • Qwen3-4B: PAINT achieves 76.9/69.7/46.7 (macro avg 64.4) vs. OPSD's 76.4/68.3/46.1 (63.6) and GRPO's 75.6/68.1/44.4 (62.7). Gains are smaller but consistent.
  • Qwen3-1.7B: PAINT achieves 59.7/44.2/29.7 (macro avg 44.5) vs. OPSD's 57.2/43.9/29.2 (43.4) and GRPO's 51.1/38.3/23.7 (37.7). The OPSD and PAINT gains over GRPO are especially large at this scale.
  • SFT consistently degrades at all scales, likely because concise reference traces shorten test-time reasoning chains.
  • Rollout efficiency: PAINT uses 1 rollout of 1024 tokens per problem over 100 steps, while GRPO uses 8 rollouts of up to 16k tokens over a longer horizon. Many GRPO batches have zero within-group reward standard deviation on this dataset.
  • Ablations confirm: (1) moderate adaptive masking (0.30-0.40) outperforms both full-solution conditioning and heavy masking; (2) suffix masking outperforms prefix, middle, and random contiguous masks; (3) sparse interpolation on top 3% positions with k_base=0.03 is better than applying interpolation everywhere.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: Moderate. The core idea of combining adaptive masking with sparse interpolation is a sensible engineering contribution on top of an existing framework (OPSD). The theoretical analysis (Gibbs re-scoring, monotonicity, entropy-ratio selection) is competent but largely formalizes intuitions rather than introducing fundamentally new concepts. The energy-space interpolation view connects to standard Bayesian sensor fusion.

Rigor: High for an empirical ML paper. The theoretical appendix provides formal proofs. Ablations are systematic and cover mask ratio, mask placement, and interpolation strength/budget. Three model scales and three benchmarks provide reasonable coverage. The comparison with GRPO is somewhat apples-to-oranges given different rollout budgets and training durations, though the efficiency argument is valid.

Reproducibility: Good. Code is publicly available. Training details are specified (LoRA rank 64, lr 5e-6, batch size 32, 100 steps, single 1024-token rollout). Hyperparameters for PAINT components are reported (rho_wrong=0.30, rho_correct=0.40, k_base=0.03, f_top=0.03, tau=0.06). The method uses standard Qwen3 models and OpenThoughts data.

Significance: The gains are meaningful but incremental (+0.8 to +2.1 over OPSD). The efficiency argument (1 short rollout vs. 8 long rollouts) is practically relevant. The re-scoring theoretical lens is a useful conceptual contribution for the field, even if the practical method is straightforward.

Red flags: The very short training (100 steps) raises questions about whether these gains would hold with longer training or different data regimes. The overlap score relies on extracting reference anchors heuristically, which may not generalize well to non-math domains. The hyperparameter sensitivity (small ranges for rho, k_base, f_top) suggests the method may require careful tuning. The comparison with GRPO is not fully controlled -- different generation lengths, different training durations, and the GRPO baseline may be suboptimal on this particular dataset due to zero-variance reward groups.

Strengths

  • Clean problem formulation: clearly identifies two specific bottlenecks (context over-exposure and uniform token weighting) in the prior OPSD framework
  • Principled design: each component is motivated by and consistent with the theoretical analysis
  • Comprehensive ablations systematically isolate the contribution of each design choice
  • Strong rollout-token efficiency: achieves competitive or better results with dramatically fewer sampled tokens
  • SFT degradation is clearly documented and explained, adding practical value for practitioners
  • The theoretical appendix is thorough and self-contained

Limitations

  • Evaluated only on mathematical reasoning benchmarks (AIME, HMMT); generalization to code, science, or open-ended reasoning is untested
  • The anchor extraction heuristic for overlap scoring is domain-specific (mathematical notation, boxed answers) and may not transfer
  • Very short training (100 steps) -- unclear whether gains persist with longer training schedules
  • Hyperparameter sensitivity: the optimal settings are narrow (rho in 0.30-0.40, k_base=0.03, f_top=0.03), and the ablation space is explored only on Qwen3-1.7B
  • The fixed teacher anchored at initialization may become stale; the paper mentions optional EMA teachers but does not evaluate them
  • No comparison with process reward models or other dense supervision methods beyond GRPO
  • The method requires verified solutions with reasoning traces, limiting applicability to settings where such data is available

Relevance

本文对关注LLM推理能力后训练的研究者有较高参考价值，特别是对在线自蒸馏、知识蒸馏和RLVR感兴趣的方向。其自适应掩码和稀疏插值的思路可以启发如何在保证on-policy对齐的同时提供更精细的token级监督信号。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does PAINT interact with longer training schedules or curriculum learning across 1000+ steps? Does the fixed teacher become a bottleneck?
  • Can the overlap-adaptive masking generalize to non-math domains (e.g., code generation, scientific QA) where reference anchors are less clearly defined?
  • What happens when combining PAINT with process reward models or multi-turn verification instead of single-rollout re-scoring?
  • How sensitive are the results to the choice of anchor extraction heuristic, and could learned overlap predictors improve robustness?
  • Would the sparse energy interpolation principle transfer to standard teacher-student distillation with external stronger models, or is it specific to the self-distillation setting?
