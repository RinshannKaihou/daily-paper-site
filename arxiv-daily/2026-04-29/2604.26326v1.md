> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Entrocraft，通过基于熵引导的拒绝采样来精确控制 LLM 强化学习过程中的熵曲线，配合线性退火策略解决性能饱和问题，使 4B 模型超越 8B 基线，pass@K 提升 50%，训练改善持续时间延长 4 倍。

> **TL;DR:** Entrocraft is a simple rejection-sampling method that precisely controls entropy curves during LLM reinforcement learning by selectively filtering rollout samples based on advantage signs. Combined with a linear annealing schedule, it addresses performance saturation, enabling a 4B model to beat an 8B GRPO baseline, sustaining training improvements for 4x longer, and raising pass@K by 50%. The method is plug-and-play, requires no entropy regularization, and works with any policy-gradient RL algorithm.

## 核心贡献

- 提供了 LLM 强化学习中熵变化的理论分析，证明熵变化与优势函数负相关，且高模型置信度会放大该效应；分析不依赖表格型 softmax 策略假设，更具实际意义。
- 提出 Entrocraft 方法：通过基于熵的拒绝采样过滤 rollout 样本，按需偏置优势分布，实现对熵曲线的精确控制，类似于学习率调度器。
- 系统研究了不同熵退火策略（固定目标、线性衰减、余弦衰减），发现线性衰减效果最佳，能避免长期训练中固定目标的不稳定性。
- 实验表明 Entrocraft 使 4B 模型超越 8B 基线，pass@K 提升 50%，训练改善持续时间为基线的 4 倍。

## Key Contributions

- Provides rigorous theoretical analysis of entropy changes during LLM RL, establishing that entropy changes are negatively correlated with advantages and amplified by high model confidence, without relying on tabular softmax policy assumptions.
- Proposes Entrocraft, a rejection-sampling filter that biases advantage distributions to achieve user-customizable entropy schedules, treating entropy as a controllable hyperparameter analogous to learning-rate scheduling.
- Conducts the first systematic study of entropy schedules (fixed, linear decay, cosine decay), finding that linear annealing from high to slightly lower entropy is optimal for sustained training.
- Demonstrates that a 4B model with Entrocraft outperforms an 8B GRPO baseline, extends improvement duration by 4x, and raises pass@K by 50% on math reasoning benchmarks.

## Methodology

**Theoretical Foundation.** The authors derive two theorems connecting advantage functions to entropy changes under realistic LLM policy assumptions (only requiring small learning rates per Assumption 1). Theorem 1 shows token-level entropy change sign is opposite to the advantage sign with high probability. Theorem 2 extends this to sequence-level entropy, showing that positive-advantage updates decrease entropy when model confidence (log-likelihood) exceeds an output-space baseline -- a condition empirically verified to always hold in practice.

**Entrocraft Algorithm.** Based on the insight that the sign of entropy change is determined by which advantage-sign rollouts contribute to the gradient, Entrocraft applies a rejection-sampling filter during rollout generation. It uses an entropy out-of-range indicator `m` comparing current batch entropy against a target range (h_low, h_high). Each rollout sample is accepted with probability `exp(gamma * m * A(x, y_i))`, where gamma is a temperature coefficient (set to 10). When entropy is too low, the filter rejects positive-advantage samples; when too high, it rejects negative-advantage samples. This requires no modification to the RL objective or entropy regularization.

**Entropy Annealing.** The authors find that maintaining a constant high entropy target becomes unstable in long-term training due to rollout imbalance (fewer negative samples as training progresses). They propose annealing the entropy target during training, with linear decay from an initial value to a slightly lower target performing best.

## Results & Highlights

- **Benchmark Performance:** On MATH-500, AMC-23, and AIME-24/25/26, Entrocraft consistently outperforms all entropy-preserving baselines (Clip-Higher, Clip-Cov, W-Reinforce, EntroPIC, entropy loss regularization) across both GRPO and GSPO algorithms.
- **Model Size Efficiency:** Qwen3-4B-Base + Entrocraft surpasses Qwen3-8B-Base trained with standard GRPO on AIME-25 (mean@32).
- **Inference-Time Scaling:** pass@K curves grow significantly faster with Entrocraft, indicating better output diversity; pass@32 is approximately 50% higher than baseline.
- **Long-Term Training:** Standard GRPO plateaus after ~100K training samples, while Entrocraft sustains improvement through 400K samples (4x longer), with consistent gains on MATH-500 and AIME benchmarks.
- **Cross-Model Generalization:** Improvements are consistent across Qwen3-4B/8B/14B-Base and Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct.
- **Entropy Control Precision:** Entrocraft achieves near-exact target entropy curves, while other methods (Clip-Cov, EntroPIC) show drift or instability over long training.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The core idea -- using rejection sampling to filter rollouts based on advantage signs for entropy control -- is simple and elegant. The theoretical contribution extending entropy analysis beyond tabular softmax to realistic LLM settings is meaningful. However, the rejection sampling concept itself is well-known; the novelty lies in its application to entropy curve control rather than the technique per se. The finding that linear annealing is best is somewhat expected given similar results in learning-rate scheduling.

**Rigor:** The theoretical proofs (Theorems 1 and 2) are clearly presented with reasonable assumptions (small learning rate). The empirical evaluation is thorough: multiple benchmarks (MATH-500, AMC-23, AIME-24/25/26), multiple models (Qwen3 4B/8B/14B, Llama-3.1-8B), multiple RL algorithms (GRPO, GSPO), and comparison against 5+ entropy-preserving baselines. The long-term training experiments (up to 400K samples) are a particular strength.

**Reproducibility:** High. Code is open-sourced, implementation uses the standard verl framework with vLLM and FSDP2, hyperparameters are fully listed, and the method is simple enough to reimplement. Training is done on a single 8-H100 node, which is accessible by research standards.

**Significance:** The problem of performance saturation in LLM RL is timely and practically important. The result that a 4B model can beat an 8B baseline via better entropy management is compelling. The framework of treating entropy as a schedulable hyperparameter (like learning rate) is a conceptual contribution that could influence future RL training practices.

**Red Flags:** The experiments are limited to math reasoning tasks with verifiable rewards. It remains unclear how Entrocraft performs on code generation, open-ended generation, or RLHF with learned reward models. The rejection sampling discards some rollouts, which could be wasteful; the paper does not analyze the acceptance rate or computational overhead in detail. The temperature coefficient gamma=10 is presented without systematic ablation. The comparison with ADAPO (Petrenko et al., 2026) is notably absent from the main tables despite being described as concurrent work addressing similar problems.

## Strengths

- Simple, elegant method that is easy to implement as a drop-in for any policy-gradient algorithm.
- Strong theoretical grounding connecting advantages to entropy changes under realistic LLM assumptions.
- Comprehensive experimental evaluation with multiple models, benchmarks, and baselines.
- Open-source code and demo enhance reproducibility and adoption potential.
- The entropy-as-hyperparameter framing is a useful conceptual contribution for the community.
- The long-term training experiments (400K samples) provide practical insights rarely seen in shorter evaluations.

## Limitations

- Evaluation is restricted to math reasoning tasks; generalization to code, multi-turn dialogue, or RLHF settings is unexplored.
- The method inherently discards rollout samples via rejection, which may reduce data efficiency.
- No analysis of acceptance rates or wall-clock training time comparison is provided.
- The temperature coefficient gamma lacks systematic ablation.
- ADAPO, described as a concurrent method, is not included in empirical comparisons.
- The authors note that multi-turn RL and MoE models remain future work, limiting current scope.

## Relevance

本文针对 LLM 强化学习中的熵塌缩和性能饱和问题，提出了实用的熵控制方案。对于关注 LLM 推理能力训练、RL 后训练规模化以及探索-利用平衡的研究者具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does Entrocraft interact with reward model-based RLHF, where advantages are noisier and less clearly signed compared to verifiable reward settings?
- What is the computational overhead of rejection sampling in terms of acceptance rate and effective sample size, especially during late-stage training when negative samples become scarce?
- Can Entrocraft be combined with MoE models or multi-turn RL, where the authors acknowledge entropy instability is more catastrophic?
- How sensitive is the linear annealing schedule to the choice of initial and final entropy targets across different model architectures and task domains?
- Does the rejection sampling approach introduce any bias in the policy gradient estimator, and if so, how does this bias affect convergence guarantees?
