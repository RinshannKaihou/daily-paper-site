> **一句话总结：** 本文通过多维度文体分析（Biber框架，67个词汇-语法特征，4个功能维度）系统揭示了差分隐私文本重写在保留语义的同时会剥离交互性标记、上下文引用和复杂从句结构，迫使所有文本收敛为非参与、非说服性的中性语域，且双向掩码替代（DP-MLM）在文体保真度上显著优于自回归改写（DP-PARAPHRASE）。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides the first systematic multidimensional stylistic profiling of differentially-private (DP) text rewriting. Using Biber's register analysis framework (67 lexico-grammatical features across 4 functional dimensions), the author shows that DP rewriting -- whether autoregressive paraphrasing (DP-PARAPHRASE, GPT-2-based) or bidirectional masked substitution (DP-MLM, RoBERTa-based) -- systematically strips interactive markers, contextual references, and complex subordination, forcing convergence toward a non-involved, non-persuasive register. DP-MLM maintains stylistic fidelity significantly better than DP-PARAPHRASE, which gets trapped by its paraphrasing training priors and fails to recover human style even at high privacy budgets. The cost of privacy extends far beyond lexical variation to fundamentally reshape the communicative function of text.

## 核心贡献

- **首次对差分隐私文本重写进行多维度文体分析**：采用Biber (1991)的寄存器分析框架，量化67个词汇-语法特征在隐私预算谱系下的变化，将抽象的"文体退化"转化为具体的"语言漂移清单"
- **揭示隐私代价远超词汇层面**：证明DP重写不仅改变用词，还系统性地剥离交互性标记（人称代词、直接疑问句）、上下文引用（地点/时间副词）和复杂从属结构，导致文本的交际功能发生根本性突变
- **发现架构差异对文体保真的关键影响**：双向掩码替代（DP-MLM）在全部隐私预算范围内文体偏差显著低于自回归改写（DP-PARAPHRASE），后者因GPT-2改写训练先验而困于文体收敛平台
- **四维功能投影揭示语域收敛现象**：在"参与vs信息"、"叙事vs说明"、"情境vs详述引用"、"说服vs中立"四个维度上，两种方法均迫使文本向非参与、非说服方向漂移

## Key Contributions

- **First multidimensional stylistic profiling of DP text rewriting**: Adopts Biber's (1991) register analysis framework to quantify shifts across 67 lexico-grammatical features, transforming abstract stylistic degradation into a concrete inventory of linguistic drift
- **Demonstrates privacy costs extend beyond lexical variation**: Shows that DP rewriting systematically strips interactive markers (personal pronouns, direct questions), contextual references (place/time adverbials), and complex subordination, fundamentally reshaping communicative function
- **Identifies critical architectural disparity in stylistic fidelity**: Bidirectional masked substitution (DP-MLM, RoBERTa-based) maintains significantly lower stylistic deviation than autoregressive paraphrasing (DP-PARAPHRASE, GPT-2-based) across all privacy budgets; the latter converges to a paraphrase-artifact plateau rather than human style
- **Four-dimensional functional projection reveals register convergence**: Across Biber's four canonical dimensions (Involved vs. Informational, Narrative vs. Expository, Situational vs. Elaborated Reference, Persuasive vs. Neutral), both architectures force convergence toward less interactive, less persuasive communication
- **Usage ratio analysis pinpoints specific linguistic casualties**: Introduces R = f_model / f_human metric, revealing pronounced asymmetry where most deviating features are substantially under-represented (contextual/interactive markers stripped), with temporal/adversative subordinators being the only significantly over-generated feature

## Methodology

**Framework**: The paper adopts Biber's (1991) multidimensional register analysis, tracking 67 lexico-grammatical features organized into 16 grammatical and functional categories (tense/aspect, adverbials, pronouns, subordination, modals, etc.). Feature frequencies are extracted per text and analyzed hierarchically at three granularity levels.

**DP Methods Compared**:
1. **DP-PARAPHRASE**: Autoregressive generation using GPT-2 fine-tuned on paraphrase data (Dolan et al., 2004), with the exponential mechanism (McSherry and Talwar, 2007) applied to candidate selection
2. **DP-MLM**: Bidirectional masked substitution using RoBERTa (Liu et al., 2019), applying the exponential mechanism to token-level replacements

**Three-level Hierarchical Analysis**:
1. **Stylistic Fidelity** (Section 3.1): Burrows' Delta measures overall stylistic distance between original and privatized text across privacy budgets (epsilon range)
2. **Feature Deviations** (Section 3.2): Usage ratio R = f_model / f_human for the top-10 most deviating features, quantifying specific grammatical marker suppression/amplification
3. **Functional Shifts** (Section 3.3): Projection onto Biber's four canonical functional dimensions, interpreting aggregated feature changes as shifts in communicative intent

**Corpus**: CORE corpus (Egbert et al., 2015), a broadly representative collection of spoken and written English registers, used as the human-authored baseline.

## Results & Highlights

- **Stylistic fidelity**: DP-MLM consistently achieves lower Burrows' Delta values (closer to human style) than DP-PARAPHRASE across all privacy budgets. Both exhibit non-monotonic behavior under tight privacy constraints due to erratic noise-text interactions
- **DP-PARAPHRASE convergence plateau**: Unlike DP-MLM, which converges toward human style as privacy relaxes, DP-PARAPHRASE reaches a persistent plateau -- attributed to GPT-2's paraphrase training priors trapping the model in a formulaic paraphrasing style
- **Feature deviation asymmetry**: Most deviating features are substantially under-represented. Place adverbials, time adverbials, personal pronouns, and direct questions all show R << 1. Only temporal and adversative subordinators are significantly over-generated (R approaching 2.5x for DP-PARAPHRASE)
- **DP-MLM bounded variation**: DP-MLM maintains feature usage ratios within approximately +/-0.5 of the human baseline for almost all features, while DP-PARAPHRASE frequently exceeds tolerable margins
- **Dimensional shifts**: The most pronounced shift is along "Involved vs. Informational Production" -- DP-PARAPHRASE shows marked drift toward impersonal, informational style. For "Narrative vs. Expository," DP-PARAPHRASE drops sharply into expository style while DP-MLM preserves narrative structure. For "Persuasive vs. Neutral," DP-PARAPHRASE flattens subjective stances into neutral register
- **Core finding**: Both architectures force convergence toward a non-involved, non-persuasive register -- a "register-blind sanitization" that preserves semantic content but structurally homogenizes stylistic markers

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High)**: This is the first work to apply Biber's multidimensional register analysis to differentially-private text. Prior work (Mattern et al., 2022; Arnold et al., 2023; Cano and Habernal, 2025) evaluated DP text quality using surface metrics (lexical diversity, grammatical correctness, morphosyntactic integrity). The shift from surface-level quality assessment to functional/communicative identity analysis is genuinely novel.

**Rigor (Moderate-High)**: The hierarchical three-level analysis (fidelity, feature deviations, functional shifts) is methodologically sound. The reuse of Biber's canonical factor loadings rather than deriving new ones from the CORE corpus is well-justified. However, some concerns remain: (1) the relationship between privacy budget and stylistic deviation is described as non-monotonic but not formally characterized; (2) the choice of top-10 most deviating features is arbitrary and could miss cumulative effects from many small deviations; (3) no statistical significance tests are reported for the dimensional shifts.

**Reproducibility (Moderate)**: The paper uses established models (GPT-2, RoBERTa) and publicly available corpora (CORE). The Biber feature set is well-documented. However, exact privacy budget values (epsilon range) are not clearly specified in the extracted text, and the code/data release status is unknown.

**Significance (Moderate-High)**: The finding that DP text rewriting structurally homogenizes communicative function has practical implications for deploying DP in real-world applications where register identity matters (e.g., healthcare narratives, legal documents, social media). The architectural comparison provides actionable guidance for system designers.

**Red flags**: (1) Single-author paper with no stated code release; (2) The limitation section itself acknowledges the analysis is disconnected from adversarial authorship attribution, raising questions about practical relevance; (3) The paper evaluates only English text -- cross-linguistic generalizability is unknown; (4) DP-PARAPHRASE's convergence issues may be specific to GPT-2 and the paraphrase training data, limiting the generality of architectural conclusions.

## Strengths

- Rigorous, well-grounded analytical framework (Biber's multidimensional analysis is a gold standard in sociolinguistics)
- Clear hierarchical structure moving from aggregate metrics to specific feature deviations to functional interpretation
- Actionable finding that DP-MLM is stylistically superior to DP-PARAPHRASE, providing concrete guidance for practitioners
- Honest acknowledgment of limitations, including the entanglement of register and authorship identity
- Well-written with precise linguistic terminology and clear explanations of the communicative implications

## Limitations

- Analysis is conducted in isolation from adversarial authorship attribution -- unclear whether stylistic sanitization is necessary or sufficient for privacy protection
- Evaluated only on English text using Biber's English-specific feature set; cross-linguistic applicability is untested
- Only two DP architectures compared (GPT-2 paraphrase vs. RoBERTa MLM); modern LLMs (e.g., instruction-tuned models) are not evaluated
- No downstream task evaluation to assess whether stylistic homogenization actually harms practical utility
- The Pareto frontier between register preservation and author-level privacy remains unexplored (acknowledged by the author as future work)
- Statistical significance of dimensional shifts is not formally established

## Relevance

本文对差分隐私文本重写的文体退化分析为隐私保护NLP系统设计提供了重要警示：如果研究关注文本生成中的隐私保护（如医疗文本匿名化、对话隐私），需注意DP机制不仅改变用词，还会系统性地抹除文本的交互性和说服力。多维度文体分析方法本身也可作为评估文本生成质量的补充工具。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How do modern instruction-tuned LLMs (e.g., LLaMA, GPT-4) compare in stylistic preservation under DP constraints versus the GPT-2/RoBERTa models tested here?
2. Is the observed register convergence a necessary byproduct of differential privacy (i.e., does any DP mechanism inherently strip authorial style), or is it an artifact of current implementation choices?
3. What is the Pareto frontier between preserving register-level functional identity and preventing author-level de-anonymization attacks?
4. Can register-aware privacy mechanisms be designed that selectively preserve functional markers (e.g., persuasive or narrative features) while still providing formal privacy guarantees?
5. Does the stylistic homogenization observed here extend to non-English languages, and how would Biber's dimensions need to be adapted for typologically different languages?
