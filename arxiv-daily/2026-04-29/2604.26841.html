Language Diffusion Models are Associative Memories Capable of Retrieving Unseen Data

arXiv: 2604.26841v1

Date: 2026-04-29

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26841v1

一句话总结： 本文从联想记忆（Associative Memory）的理论视角分析离散扩散语言模型（UDDMs），通过证明条件似然最大化能够隐式地执行Hebbian学习并最大化分类间隔来建立吸引子动力学，揭示了一个由训练数据集大小控制的从记忆化到泛化的相变过程，并提出条件熵作为检测该转换的实用诊断指标。
TL;DR: This paper establishes a theoretical bridge between Uniform-based Discrete Diffusion Models (UDDMs) and Associative Memories (AMs) by showing that conditional likelihood maximization implicitly creates basins of attraction around data points via Hebbian learning and classification margin maximization. Through experiments on LM1B with models ranging from ~24M to ~384M parameters, the authors identify a sharp memorization-to-generalization phase transition governed by training dataset size: as data grows, basins around training examples shrink while basins around unseen test examples expand until convergence. Conditional entropy of predicted token sequences serves as a practical, efficient-to-compute probe for detecting this transition -- vanishing entropy signals memorization, while finite entropy indicates generalization. Larger models delay the transition, requiring more data before generalization emerges.

  • 提出条件熵作为检测记忆化-泛化转换的实用诊断工具：记忆化阶段条件熵趋于零，泛化阶段条件熵保持有限值

  • 在离散/语言建模场景下推广了此前仅限于连续扩散模型与联想记忆之间的理论联系

Key Contributions

  • Establish a formal bridge between UDDMs and Associative Memories by proving that conditional likelihood maximization (pseudo-likelihood) implicitly enforces Hebbian learning and maximizes classification margins, producing basins of attraction without requiring an explicit energy function
  • Identify a sharp memorization-to-generalization phase transition governed by training dataset size, where basins around training examples shrink and basins around unseen test examples expand as data scales, eventually converging
  • Propose conditional entropy of predicted token sequences as a practical diagnostic metric for detecting the memorization-to-generalization transition -- vanishing entropy signals memorization, finite entropy indicates generalization
  • Demonstrate that larger models delay the phase transition (requiring more data to generalize) but narrow the "entropy gap" between training and synthetic sequences in the generalization regime
  • Extend prior results connecting continuous diffusion models and AMs to the discrete setting of language modeling

Methodology

The paper proceeds in three stages of increasing realism:

Theoretical Framework (Section 3): The authors begin with a simplified setting of binary neurons with non-symmetric coupling, showing that the pseudo-likelihood loss decomposes into a Hebbian learning term (storing data points) and a classification margin penalty term. The gradient of the loss with respect to the coupling matrix W reveals that learning concentrates on patterns with the smallest margins, producing basins of attraction. This analysis is then connected to UDDMs by noting that the NELBO training objective relies on cross-entropy terms that naturally enforce classification margins and store data points.

Experimental Design (Section 4): Three progressively realistic experiments probe the AM analogy:

  1. Experiment 1 (Deterministic retrieval): Corrupt training/test examples and apply greedy (argmax) denoising -- emulating zero-temperature AM retrieval dynamics. Perturbation level is decoupled from diffusion time.
  2. Experiment 2 (Stochastic retrieval): Same initialization but with standard stochastic reverse dynamics. Perturbation level is now tied to diffusion time t.
  3. Experiment 3 (Standard generation): Full reverse process from random initial conditions. No ground truth available; conditional entropy is the primary metric.

Models and Data: Three UDDM variants (Tiny ~24M, Small ~135M, Medium ~384M parameters) trained on the LM1B dataset with GPT-2 tokenizer (sequence length 128), using the diffusion transformer architecture from Peebles & Xie (2023) and the training procedure from Sahoo et al. (2025). A total of 162 models (54 per scale) are trained for 1M iterations each on NVIDIA V100 GPUs (4 GPUs per model, global batch size 512), across varying fractions of the training dataset (from 10^-4 to 1.0 with fine-grained spacing in the memorization regime) to map the phase transition.

Metrics: Token recovery rate (Eq. 12) measures basin stability; conditional entropy (Eq. 13) probes basin geometry and sharpness. Both are evaluated as functions of training dataset size. Appendix C further connects conditional entropy to energy Hessian curvature via a Laplace approximation, providing a geometric interpretation.

Results & Highlights

  • Phase transition detected via token recovery: With small training sets, UDDMs perfectly recover training examples but fail on test examples (memorization regime). As training data grows, training recovery drops while test recovery improves, eventually converging (generalization regime). This is observed across all three model scales.
  • Conditional entropy as a diagnostic: In memorization, conditional entropy of both training and generated sequences is distributed near zero. In generalization, the distributions shift to positive values and overlap. The sequence-level conditional entropy distributions converge precisely at the transition point.
  • Token-level entropy reveals recovery behavior: Successfully recovered tokens exhibit near-zero entropy (high confidence), while unrecovered tokens show broad, non-zero entropy distributions. A surprising fraction of low-entropy tokens persists even during generalization, indicating highly stable substructures.
  • Scale effects: Larger models (Medium ~384M) prolong the memorization phase and require significantly more data to trigger the transition. However, larger models also narrow the average "entropy gap" between training and synthetic sequences, indicating higher confidence in novel generations.
  • Textual examples confirm the mechanism: Small-data models alter unseen test tokens during denoising; large-data models preserve them, demonstrating that test samples have become stable attractors.

Critical Assessment

Novelty (Moderate-High): The extension from continuous DMs to discrete UDDMs is meaningful, and the conditional-likelihood-to-AM bridge without energy functions is a genuine conceptual contribution. However, several building blocks exist in prior work: the AM-diffusion connection (Ambrogioni 2024, Hoover et al. 2023), the memorization-to-generalization transition in continuous DMs (Pham et al. 2025), and the pseudo-likelihood AM connection (D'Amico et al. 2026). The incremental novelty lies in combining these into a coherent framework for discrete/language settings.

Rigor (Moderate-High): The theoretical derivation for the simplified binary setting is clean and well-connected to established results (Hebbian learning, max-margin classification). The experimental design with three progressively realistic settings is thoughtful. However, the gap between the simplified theory (linear logits, binary variables) and the actual UDDM experiments (deep transformers, categorical variables) is substantial and only partially bridged -- the authors acknowledge this and flag it as future work. The experiments are systematic (162 models) but limited to a single dataset (LM1B) and moderate-scale models (~384M max).

Reproducibility (Moderate): The paper provides hyperparameters, dataset details, and code base references (Sahoo et al. 2025). Training on NVIDIA V100 GPUs with 4 GPUs per model. The number of models (162) and training iterations (1M each) represents a significant computational cost. No official code release is mentioned.

Significance (Moderate): Conditional entropy as a diagnostic for memorization-vs-generalization is practically useful if it scales to larger models. The theoretical framework provides a new lens for understanding discrete diffusion. However, the experiments are confined to relatively small UDDMs on LM1B -- extending to large language models or more complex datasets remains an open and non-trivial challenge, as the authors acknowledge.

Red flags:

  • The theory is proven only for the simplified linear-logit binary setting; the connection to deep transformers relies on analogy rather than formal proof
  • All experiments are on a single dataset with models up to ~384M parameters -- generalization of the findings to LLM-scale models is not demonstrated
  • The companion paper (arXiv:2505.21777) covers overlapping territory in the continuous domain, raising questions about how much of the discrete findings are genuinely new versus translated

Strengths

  • Clean theoretical bridge from conditional likelihood to AM dynamics with explicit Hebbian learning and margin maximization decomposition
  • Well-designed experimental progression from AM-like retrieval to full generative process
  • Conditional entropy is an elegant, efficient-to-compute diagnostic that does not require access to training data for detection
  • Systematic exploration across 162 models with varying dataset fractions and three model scales
  • Clear and well-written presentation with strong connections to prior work in both AM theory and diffusion models
  • The memorization-to-generalization phase transition picture is intuitive and well-supported by multiple complementary metrics

Limitations

  • Theory is proven only for binary variables with linear logits; the extension to deep transformers with categorical variables is argued by analogy, not formally established
  • Experiments are limited to LM1B (a relatively simple language modeling benchmark) and models up to ~384M parameters
  • No comparison with autoregressive or other generative paradigms -- unclear whether the AM interpretation applies beyond UDDMs
  • The relationship between the proposed metrics (token recovery rate, conditional entropy) and standard language model evaluation metrics (perplexity, downstream task performance) is not established
  • The practical utility of conditional entropy as a diagnostic for deployed models depends on whether the memorization-to-generalization transition patterns observed at small scale persist at LLM scale
  • The entropy gap analysis could benefit from statistical significance testing and confidence intervals

Relevance

本文通过联想记忆的理论框架分析离散扩散语言模型的记忆化与泛化行为，提出条件熵作为诊断指标，对理解语言模型的生成机制和设计更可靠的模型部署策略具有理论参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the memorization-to-generalization phase transition observed in UDDMs (up to ~384M parameters) persist in LLM-scale discrete diffusion models (billions of parameters), or do new phenomena emerge?
  • Can conditional entropy be validated as a proxy for standard evaluation metrics (perplexity, downstream task accuracy, membership inference attacks) in practical deployment scenarios?
  • How does the AM framework extend to autoregressive language models, which also rely on conditional probabilities but with a different factorization structure?
  • Do the additional NELBO terms (L_diffusion, L_prior) genuinely enlarge basins of attraction in deep architectures, as conjectured?
  • What are the implications for data efficiency -- can the phase transition point be used to determine optimal training set sizes for desired memorization vs. generalization behavior?
