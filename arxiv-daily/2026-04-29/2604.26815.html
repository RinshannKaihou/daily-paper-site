What Is the Cost of Energy Monitoring? An Empirical Study on the Overhead of RAPL-Based Tools

arXiv: 2604.26815v1

Date: 2026-04-29

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26815v1

一句话总结： 本文通过两个受控实验系统量化了7种RAPL能耗监测工具在1kHz高频采样下的时间和能量开销，发现现有工具（尤其是Scaphandre和CodeCarbon）可引入高达46.75%的时间开销，而作者提出的简化设计（用户态缓存和内核模块方案）可将开销降至接近基线水平，核心原因在于系统调用开销远大于直接rdmsr指令。
TL;DR: This paper empirically quantifies the measurement overhead of seven RAPL-based energy profiling tools at 1kHz polling frequency. Existing tools like Scaphandre and CodeCarbon introduce up to 46.75% time overhead due to excessive system calls and unnecessary I/O, while the authors' lightweight solutions (Ref_Rapl_User with batched writes and Ref_Rapl_Kernel with in-kernel caching) achieve near-baseline performance. Microbenchmarks further reveal that system calls (sys_read) are roughly 10x slower than rdmsr instructions and 4000x slower than nop, identifying system call overhead as the primary bottleneck in high-frequency RAPL sampling.

核心贡献

  • 系统性量化了RAPL工具的高频采样开销：首次在1kHz（RAPL理论最大采样率）下，对5种主流工具和2种自研工具进行了受控对比实验，揭示现有工具时间开销范围为0.25%至46.75%。
  • 提出两种低开销方案：Ref_Rapl_User（用户态批量缓存写入，减少系统调用至约5次/128样本）和Ref_Rapl_Kernel（内核态环形缓冲区，仅2次系统调用/100样本），均接近无工具基线性能。
  • 指令级开销分析：通过自定义内核模块（Ref_Rapl_Micro_Bench）精确测量了rdmsr、sys_read、proc_read等关键操作的单次执行延迟，揭示了系统调用是高频采样的主要瓶颈。
  • 提供完整复现包：包含所有测量数据、分析脚本、内核模块源代码和实验编排工具，确保实验可复现。
  • 实践指导意义：为开发高频能耗分析工具提供了明确的设计原则——简化工具设计、减少系统调用、使用低层指令访问RAPL值。

Key Contributions

  • First systematic overhead quantification of RAPL tools at 1kHz: Conducts a controlled experiment comparing five mainstream tools (Perf, PowerJoular, Turbostat, Scaphandre, CodeCarbon) and two custom tools against a no-tool baseline using six NAS Parallel Benchmarks, revealing overhead ranging from 0.25% to 46.75%.
  • Two lightweight profiling solutions: Ref_Rapl_User employs in-memory sample caching with batched file writes (reducing syscalls from ~6-9 per iteration to ~5 per 128 samples); Ref_Rapl_Kernel uses a kernel-space ring buffer with /proc interface (only 2 syscalls per 100 samples), both achieving near-baseline overhead.
  • Instruction-level latency profiling: Develops Ref_Rapl_Micro_Bench kernel module to benchmark rdmsr, sys_read, proc_read, cpuid, mov, and nop instructions over 100,000 iterations, establishing that sys_read is ~10x slower than rdmsr and ~4000x slower than nop.
  • Complete replication package: Provides all measurements, analysis scripts, kernel module source code, patched third-party tools, and experiment orchestration tools for full reproducibility.
  • Actionable design guidelines: Demonstrates that simplifying tool design and shortening the user-kernel call chain are the most effective strategies for reducing high-frequency RAPL profiling overhead.

Methodology

The study employs two controlled experiments addressing two research questions:

RQ1 - Tool Overhead at 1kHz: A full factorial experiment with 8 tools (including baseline No_Tools) x 6 NAS benchmarks (bt, cg, ep, ft, is, mg) x 15 repetitions = 720 runs. Each run wraps a benchmark execution between two RAPL readings to measure overhead. The experiment uses an orchestrator machine (Lenovo ThinkPad x390) and a System Under Test (Intel NUC8i7HVK with NixOS 24.04, chosen because it supports all four RAPL domains). Runs are randomized with 30-second cooldown periods. Statistical analysis includes Shapiro-Wilk normality testing, Kruskal-Wallis tests, Dunn's post-hoc with Bonferroni correction, and Cliff's delta effect sizes.

RQ2 - Instruction-Level Overhead: A microbenchmark experiment executing 11 instructions (4 rdmsr variants, 4 system calls/sys_read/proc_read variants, cpuid, mov, nop) in loops of 100,000 iterations, repeated 15 times each, totaling 165 runs. A custom kernel module (Ref_Rapl_Micro_Bench) provides /proc and /sys interfaces to execute and time these instruction loops.

The authors also perform flame graph analysis comparing PowerJoular, Ref_Rapl_User, and Ref_Rapl_Kernel to validate their design decisions and quantify function-level CPU time allocation.

Results & Highlights

  • Existing tool overhead is substantial at 1kHz: CodeCarbon shows the highest overhead across benchmarks, ranging from 5.38% to 46.75%. Scaphandre and Turbostat also exhibit significant overhead. PowerJoular shows significant differences in 5 of 6 benchmarks (delta = -1.00).
  • Lightweight tools match baseline performance: Ref_Rapl_User and Ref_Rapl_Kernel show no statistically significant differences from the No_Tools baseline (p = 1.000 across all benchmarks), with small-to-medium effect sizes (delta range: -0.42 to +0.37).
  • System calls dominate overhead: sys_read takes ~1.36x10^-3 ms per execution, roughly 10x slower than rdmsr (~2.3-5.6x10^-4 ms) and over 4000x slower than nop (3x10^-7 ms). proc_read and sys_read exhibit substantially higher standard deviation (7.5-12.2 ms) compared to rdmsr (0.4-2.9 ms).
  • Energy overhead mirrors time overhead: Both metrics move in parallel across benchmarks. CodeCarbon's energy overhead exceeds 40% when profiling the ep benchmark.
  • Possible over-optimization insight: Ref_Rapl_Kernel may represent over-optimization since it performs similarly to Ref_Rapl_User despite eliminating 4 additional syscalls per RAPL read, suggesting a natural cost floor from CPU wakeup/task switching every millisecond.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. The paper extends prior work by Raffin and Trystram (2024) by adding two custom tool implementations and deeper instruction-level analysis. The finding that system calls dominate overhead is well-understood in systems research; the specific quantification in the RAPL context is useful but not groundbreaking.
  • Rigor: Strong experimental design with full factorial setup, randomization, 15 repetitions, proper statistical testing (Shapiro-Wilk, Kruskal-Wallis, Dunn's post-hoc, Cliff's delta), and clear threats-to-validity discussion. The use of NixOS enhances reproducibility.
  • Reproducibility: Excellent. Full replication package with kernel module source, patched tools, and experiment orchestration scripts. NixOS configuration ensures deterministic environments. The use of a single SUT hardware is a limitation but is acknowledged.
  • Significance: High practical relevance for the green software community. The quantified overhead ranges (up to 46.75%) are striking and should inform tool selection for practitioners. However, the 1kHz use case is niche -- most real-world energy profiling operates at much lower frequencies where these overheads are negligible.
  • Red flags: (1) Three commercial tools were patched to support 1kHz, which the authors acknowledge may affect functionality. (2) The study runs on a single hardware platform (Intel NUC8i7HVK), limiting generalizability. (3) EcoFloc, PowerLetrics, and EnergiBridge were excluded due to crashes or immaturity, reducing coverage. (4) The paper applies tools to a use case (1kHz) they were neither designed nor validated for, making performance comparisons somewhat unfair to the existing tools. (5) Ref_Rapl_Kernel may represent over-optimization as acknowledged by the authors themselves.

Strengths

  • Rigorous, well-designed controlled experiment with proper statistical methodology and transparent reporting.
  • Two complementary solutions (user-space and kernel-space) that demonstrably reduce overhead with different trade-off profiles.
  • Instruction-level microbenchmarking provides concrete, actionable data on where time is spent in the RAPL access chain.
  • Flame graph validation offers intuitive visualization of the overhead differences between tool architectures.
  • Complete replication package significantly enhances the value of the work for the community.
  • Honest discussion of potential over-optimization and energy-time trade-offs in the kernel approach.

Limitations

  • Single hardware platform (Intel NUC8i7HVK only), though spot-checked on two additional machines without full replication.
  • Tools evaluated at 1kHz, which is not their intended operating frequency; results do not reflect tool quality at their designed sampling rates.
  • Three tools required patching (fewer than 10 lines each), which may introduce unmeasured effects.
  • ARM and AMD architectures are not covered; results may differ on non-Intel platforms.
  • Energy overhead results are mentioned but not fully presented in the paper (available in replication package).
  • The benchmarks are all from the NAS suite (scientific computing); results may differ for other workload types (e.g., web servers, databases).

Relevance
