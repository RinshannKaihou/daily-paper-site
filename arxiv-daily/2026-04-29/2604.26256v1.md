> **一句话总结：** DORA 通过多版本流式训练（multi-version streaming training）实现异步 RL rollout，在保持策略一致性、数据完整性和有界过时性三个约束的前提下，消除长尾生成导致的训练气泡，在大规模工业部署中实现 2-4 倍端到端加速。

> **TL;DR:** DORA is a system from Meituan that solves the long-tailed generation bottleneck in LLM reinforcement learning training by maintaining multiple policy versions concurrently on rollout instances, enabling trajectory-level streaming that eliminates both intra-node and inter-node bubbles. It achieves up to 8.2x rollout speedup and 2.12x end-to-end throughput over synchronous training on open-source benchmarks, and 2-4x acceleration in production with tens of thousands of accelerators, all while preserving convergence parity through strict adherence to three algorithmic constraints (intra-trajectory consistency, data integrity, bounded staleness). A key co-design insight is that maintaining policy consistency enables zero-re-prefill KV-Cache migration across instances.

## 核心贡献

- **多版本流式训练范式**：在 rollout 实例上同时维护多个策略版本，已完成轨迹直接流入训练阶段，长尾轨迹在原版本下继续执行，不阻塞训练也不被丢弃，从根本上去除同步屏障。
- **形式化约束分析**：明确提出异步 RL 训练需满足的三个约束——轨迹内策略一致性 (C1)、数据完整性 (C2)、有界过时性 (C3)，并将其形式化为约束优化问题。
- **动态负载均衡编排器**：集中式编排器根据各版本的实时负载动态重分配 DP 组，配合 P2P 权重同步和请求迁移，解决多版本 rollout 中的资源碎片化问题。
- **零重填充 KV-Cache 迁移**：利用 C1 约束带来的 KV-Cache 数学等价性，实现跨实例的 KV-Cache 传输而无需重新 prefill，对长上下文和 MoE 架构尤其有益。
- **工业规模验证**：在万卡规模的生产环境中部署，产出的 LongCat-Flash-Thinking 模型在推理和 agentic 基准上表现竞争力强。

## Key Contributions

- **Multi-version streaming training paradigm**: Maintains multiple policy versions concurrently on rollout instances, allowing completed trajectories to stream directly to training while long-tailed trajectories continue under their original version, eliminating both intra-node and inter-node bubbles.
- **Formalized constraint analysis**: Identifies and formalizes three constraints for asynchronous RL training -- intra-trajectory policy consistency (C1), data integrity (C2), and bounded staleness (C3) -- framing the problem as constrained optimization.
- **Dynamic load-balancing orchestrator**: A centralized orchestrator dynamically re-partitions data-parallel groups across model versions proportional to real-time workloads, resolving resource fragmentation inherent in multi-version rollout.
- **Zero re-prefill KV-Cache migration**: Exploits the KV-Cache mathematical equivalence guaranteed by C1 to enable cross-instance transfer without re-prefill, especially beneficial for long-context reasoning and MoE architectures.
- **Industrial-scale validation**: Deployed as the default async training paradigm across tens of thousands of accelerators, producing competitive open-source LongCat-Flash-Thinking models.

## Methodology

DORA addresses the skewed generation bottleneck in LLM RL training through algorithm-system co-design built on four interlocking mechanisms:

**1. Multi-version streaming training (Section 4.2):** Instead of the traditional synchronous barrier where the entire batch waits for the longest output, DORA dispatches rollout prompts (RBS > TBS) to rollout instances, each tagged with a specific policy version. Completed trajectories stream directly to training without waiting for stragglers. Training begins once TBS samples are collected. A sliding window W = {w_j, ..., w_{j-K+1}} of active policy versions is maintained; the window advances only when all trajectories from the oldest version have been collected, enforcing bounded staleness C3. Each trajectory is generated entirely by a single policy version, naturally satisfying C1 without algorithmic modifications.

**2. Dynamic resource orchestration (Section 4.3):** As trajectories from legacy versions complete, their assigned DP groups become underutilized. A centralized orchestrator monitors per-version workloads and triggers re-balancing via three mechanisms: (a) proportional resource partitioning that allocates DP groups based on current request counts, (b) P2P weight updates and request migration to implement the re-partitioning, and (c) staleness-aware data supplementation that prioritizes the latest policy version for new prompt injection.

**3. KV-Cache reuse (Section 4.4):** Since all tokens in a trajectory share the same policy version (C1), KV-Cache states are mathematically equivalent across instances hosting that version. This enables zero-re-prefill migration: when requests are moved between DP groups, only metadata (lightweight RPC) and KV-Cache data (high-performance collective communication) need to be transferred. Locality-aware scheduling minimizes transfer volume, and hierarchical memory management offloads KV-Caches to host memory under VRAM pressure.

**4. System architecture:** DORA uses a disaggregated architecture with RolloutManager (dispatches prompts with version tags), TransferQueue (asynchronous with staleness monitoring), Trainer (experience preparation and model training), and Load-balancing Orchestrator (coordination via RPC). It builds on vLLM for inference, Megatron-LM for training, and extends torch RPC with streaming primitives.

## Results & Highlights

- **Rollout speedup**: On 64 GPUs with Qwen2.5-32B, DORA achieves 8.2x rollout-only speedup over synchronous training (14.9 min to 1.8 min) and 4.4x over partial rollout.
- **End-to-end throughput**: On 128 GPUs, DORA reaches 34,135 tokens/s, a 2.12x improvement over synchronous training (16,070 tokens/s) and 1.47x over one-step off-policy.
- **Scalability**: DORA scales well from 64 to 128 GPUs (1.40x step time reduction, 1.46x throughput gain), while synchronous training shows poor scaling (only 1.13x).
- **Production-scale MoE**: On LongCat-Flash (560B MoE) with 4,096 accelerators and 64K max response length, DORA achieves 3.6x rollout speedup in math/TIR scenarios and 6.2x in agentic training.
- **Convergence parity**: Both DORA variants (k=1, k=3) achieve similar per-step convergence trends as synchronous training. Staleness k=3 is moderately slower than k=1 but maintains stable convergence.
- **Low system overhead**: Load balancing accounts for 0.4-1.5% of execution time; request transfer 2-4%; cache management is negligible (<0.03%).
- **Industrial deployment**: Active since 2025 across tens of thousands of accelerators for LongCat-series models, achieving 2-4x end-to-end speedup with no model quality degradation.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** The multi-version streaming training paradigm is a genuinely novel contribution. While concurrent works (Seed1.5-thinking, Laminar) explore similar ideas, DORA's formalization of three constraints (C1-C3) and the co-design insight that C1 enables zero-re-prefill migration is distinctive and well-argued. The framing as constrained optimization provides a principled foundation.

**Rigor (Good):** The paper provides formal mathematical formulations (Equations 1-5) and a clear algorithm (Algorithm 1). The constraint analysis is thorough and the comparison with existing approaches (replication-based, one-step off-policy, partial rollout) is systematic. However, the convergence analysis is empirical only -- no formal convergence proof is provided for the multi-version scheme under bounded staleness. The authors acknowledge this limitation.

**Reproducibility (Moderate):** The open-source benchmark experiments use well-known models (Qwen2.5-32B) and datasets (DAPO-Math-17K), and the software stack (vLLM, Megatron-LM) is specified. However, the core DORA system itself does not appear to be open-sourced at the time of writing. The production-scale MoE experiments use proprietary data and non-CUDA accelerators, limiting direct reproduction.

**Significance (High):** The 2-4x end-to-end speedup at production scale is substantial for industrial RL training. The KV-Cache reuse insight from C1 is elegant and practically impactful. The LongCat-Flash-Thinking models produced are competitive with leading open-source reasoning models.

**Red flags:**
- No direct comparison with publicly available RL training systems (veRL, AReaL) -- acknowledged by the authors.
- Production MoE experiments only use proprietary data; no large-scale open-source MoE validation.
- The staleness bound K is manually configured; no adaptive mechanism is provided.
- The convergence guarantee relies on PPO's clipping without formal analysis of the multi-version case.

## Strengths

- Elegant co-design insight: maintaining C1 (policy consistency) is not just algorithmically necessary but enables the key system optimization (zero-re-prefill KV-Cache migration).
- Thorough constraint analysis that clearly positions DORA against existing approaches and articulates the tradeoff landscape.
- Strong and consistent improvements across multiple scales (64, 128, 4096+ GPUs) and architectures (dense, MoE).
- Honest and transparent limitations section; the authors clearly state what they did not benchmark and why.
- Well-structured paper with clear problem formulation, systematic design exposition, and comprehensive evaluation.

## Limitations

- The staleness bound K must be manually tuned for each training scenario; no principled method for selecting K is provided.
- No formal convergence proof for the multi-version streaming scheme; reliance on PPO clipping as the sole safeguard against off-policy bias.
- Absence of direct benchmarks against veRL, AReaL, and other publicly available systems limits comparability.
- Production MoE experiments use proprietary data only, raising questions about generalizability to open-source MoE models.
- The centralized orchestrator introduces a potential single point of failure and coordination overhead that grows with cluster size (though empirically shown to be small).
- The paper does not analyze the memory overhead of maintaining multiple policy versions simultaneously.

## Relevance

本文对关注 LLM 强化学习训练系统优化的研究者高度相关。DORA 的多版本流式训练思路和 KV-Cache 零重填充迁移机制为解决长上下文 RL 训练中的长尾生成瓶颈提供了实用的系统级方案，对大规模 RLHF/GRPO 训练基础设施的设计有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can an adaptive staleness control mechanism be developed that automatically adjusts K based on convergence signals, eliminating manual tuning?
- How does DORA's performance scale with the degree of generation skewness -- is there a threshold beyond which the multi-version approach becomes less effective?
- What is the formal convergence rate of multi-version streaming training under bounded staleness, and can tighter bounds be derived?
- How does the centralized orchestrator design affect fault tolerance and recovery in large-scale deployments?
- Could the zero-re-prefill KV-Cache migration insight be applied to other scenarios beyond asynchronous RL training, such as speculative decoding or model serving?
- What is the memory overhead of maintaining K policy versions simultaneously, and how does it scale with model size and sequence length?
