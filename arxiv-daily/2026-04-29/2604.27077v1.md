> **一句话总结：** 本文提出了 νGPT，一种针对归一化 Transformer (nGPT) 的新型参数化方法，通过引入"中间对齐"假设和对齐指数的实证测量，推导出学习率随宽度 (m_width^{-3/4})、深度 (m_depth^{-1}) 和训练长度 (m_data^{-1/3}) 的缩放规律，实现了小模型超参数向大模型的无损迁移。

> **TL;DR:** The authors propose νGPT, a re-parameterization of the Normalized Transformer (nGPT), that enables learning rate transfer across model width, depth, and token horizon. The key insight is that the standard μP alignment assumptions do not hold in nGPT; instead, a "mid alignment" regime (exponent 3/4) empirically matches observed weight-activation alignment dynamics. The resulting prescription scales hidden/output learning rates as m_width^{-3/4} (not m_width^{-1} as in μP), introduces depth corrections via α_init parameters (m_depth^{-1}), and applies a global token-horizon correction of m_data^{-1/3}. Experiments on FineWeb-Edu with models up to ~3B parameters show essentially perfect HP transfer with no performance loss versus well-tuned nGPT baselines.

## 核心贡献

- 发现原始 nGPT 虽然参数性能优越，但不具备跨模型宽度和训练长度的学习率迁移能力
- 通过实证测量权重-激活对齐指数 (alignment exponents)，发现 nGPT 中不满足 μP 的"完全对齐"假设，而是处于"中间对齐"状态（指数约 3/4）
- 提出 νGPT 参数化方案：隐藏层/反嵌入学习率按 m_width^{-3/4} 缩放（区别于 μP 的 m_width^{-1}），α_init 参数按 m_depth^{-1} 缩放，全局学习率按 m_data^{-1/3} 缩放
- 证明 Depth-μP 风格的 η_hidden 深度修正（m_depth^{-1/2}）反而会破坏 nGPT 的深度迁移，揭示了归一化模型的特殊性质
- 实验验证 νGPT 在宽度（8~40 heads）、深度（8~128 layers）和训练长度三个维度上均实现良好的学习率迁移

## Key Contributions

- Identify that the original nGPT, despite impressive training speed and no need for weight decay/warmup, fails to transfer learning rates across model width and token horizon
- Empirically measure alignment exponents (following Everett et al., 2024) in trained nGPT models, finding that the "full alignment" assumption of μP (exponent 1) is incorrect; instead a "mid alignment" regime with exponent 3/4 matches observations when weighted by loss decrease
- Propose νGPT parameterization: hidden and unembedding learning rates scale as m_width^{-3/4} (vs. m_width^{-1} in μP), embedding learning rate as m_width^{-1/2}, depth corrections via α_{A,init} and α_{M,init} at m_depth^{-1}, and global LR multiplied by m_data^{-1/3}
- Demonstrate that Depth-μP style corrections to η_hidden (m_depth^{-1/2}) break depth transfer in nGPT, likely due to the trainable α_A, α_M rescaler parameters controlling residual mixing
- Show ω_output = ω_hidden = 1/2 decisively from alignment measurements, meaning logits can be Θ(1) both at initialization and during training (unlike μP where they start small)

## Methodology

The paper builds on the nGPT architecture (Loshchilov et al., 2025), which replaces standard RMSNorm/LayerNorm with L2 normalization, uses residual-post-norm blocks, and employs trainable LERP (linear interpolation) parameters α_A, α_M for learned componentwise rescaling of residual connections.

The derivation follows a principled heuristic approach:

1. **Alignment Exponents Framework** (from Everett et al., 2024): Define exponents α, ω, ν ∈ [0,1] characterizing how weight updates interact with activations (ΔW·h, W·Δh, ΔW·Δh). These are measured empirically during training.

2. **Mid Alignment Assumption**: Rather than μP's full alignment (max{α,ν}=1) or no alignment (max{α,ν}=1/2), the authors find max{α,ν}=3/4 matches observations. This leads to the key m_width^{-3/4} scaling for hidden/output learning rates.

3. **SignGD-like Update Scaling**: Adam updates are approximated as having signGD-like scaling (each component ≈ ±η), simplifying the derivation of optimal learning rates.

4. **Depth Corrections**: Following CompleteP (Dey et al., 2025), α_init parameters are scaled as m_depth^{-1} rather than Depth-μP's m_depth^{-1/2}, corresponding to α_depth = 1 in residual network theory.

5. **Token Horizon**: The m_data^{-1/3} correction is empirically determined (consistent with Bjorck et al., 2025, β=0.32), refuting the m_data^{-1/2} prediction from SDE theory.

6. **Formal Proofs**: Appendix provides lemmas proving stability (Desideratum 1) and non-trivial feature learning (Desideratum 2) at growing width for each block type (input embedding, attention, MLP, LERP, output unembedding).

## Results & Highlights

- **Width Transfer**: νGPT shows essentially perfect transfer across 8 to 40 attention heads (0.26B to 3.22B parameters) at fixed 80K iterations, while baseline nGPT shows no transfer (optimal LR shifts with model size)
- **Depth Transfer**: Both baseline nGPT and νGPT show decent transfer across 8 to 128 layers, but νGPT provides slightly better stability for deep models
- **Token Horizon**: Optimal peak LR decreases as approximately (iter. count)^{-1/3}, confirmed across both fixed model and variable-width sweeps at 20 tokens/parameter
- **Performance**: νGPT achieves no worse or slightly better validation loss than the well-tuned original nGPT baseline across all configurations
- **νGPT vs μP**: νGPT provides somewhat better width transfer than standard μP-style corrections ("CompleteP"), confirming that μP is not the unique maximal update parametrization
- **Alignment Measurements**: All α and ν exponents are higher early in training (close to 0.5-0.6 at convergence), with ω ≈ 0.5 throughout, confirming the mid-alignment assumption when weighted by loss decrease

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** The paper makes a genuine contribution by identifying that standard μP alignment assumptions fail in normalized architectures and proposing a theoretically grounded alternative. The mid-alignment concept (exponent 3/4) and the m_width^{-3/4} scaling rule are genuinely new. The finding that Depth-μP corrections break transfer in this setting is surprising and valuable.

**Rigor (Good):** Theoretical derivations are supported by formal lemmas (Section 6). Alignment exponents are empirically measured across multiple model sizes. However, the mid-alignment assumption is empirically motivated rather than derived from first principles -- the exponents are observed to be 3/4 rather than proven to be. The depth transfer theory is less complete than the width theory; the authors acknowledge that explaining why η_hidden should not change with depth remains an open question.

**Reproducibility (Good):** Training setup is well-specified (FineWeb-Edu, seq length 4096, batch 64, OLMo-2 tokenizer, Adam with specific hyperparameters, base width/depth = 10). Table 1 provides exact parameterization rules. Three initialization seeds are used. However, no code release is mentioned.

**Significance (High):** HP transfer is a critical practical problem for LLM training. The paper makes concrete, actionable recommendations (Table 1) that differ from prior work. The insight that normalized architectures behave differently from standard transformers for HP transfer is important for the growing adoption of normalization-based approaches.

**Red Flags:**
- Models are limited to ~3B parameters; it remains unclear whether the m_width^{-3/4} rule holds at significantly larger scale
- The 1/3 exponent for token horizon is empirically fit, not theoretically predicted; the paper acknowledges "there is no well-known and well-tested theoretical framework"
- No comparison with actual production-scale training runs or standard (non-normalized) transformer baselines
- The "arbitrary" initialization variance might mask sensitivity that shows up at larger scales

## Strengths

- Combines theoretical analysis with rigorous empirical validation on meaningful model sizes
- Identifies a concrete, measurable discrepancy with μP (alignment exponents) and proposes a correction
- The parameterization is simple to implement: just change learning rate multipliers and a few hyperparameters
- Comprehensive ablations comparing νGPT against both standard μP and Depth-μP variants
- Honest about limitations: explicitly notes where depth corrections may be unnecessary, where theory is incomplete
- No need for weight decay simplifies the analysis and removes a confounding factor

## Limitations

- Only validated on nGPT architecture; unclear if mid-alignment holds for other normalized or standard transformers
- Maximum model size (~3.2B parameters) is relatively small compared to modern production models
- Alignment exponents change during training and vary across layers; the "fixed at 3/4" assumption is a simplification
- The token horizon correction (m_data^{-1/3}) is purely empirical with no theoretical derivation
- Does not address batch size scaling, which is another important axis for HP transfer
- No investigation of interaction with other optimizers (e.g.,Muon, which is gaining popularity)
- The paper does not release code, which limits immediate adoption

## Relevance

本文对关注 LLM 训练效率和超参数迁移的研究者具有直接价值：νGPT 提供了一套可操作的参数化方案，使得在小模型上调优的学习率可以直接应用于大模型训练，显著降低超参数搜索成本。对于研究 scaling laws 和 μP 理论的学者，本文通过实证对齐指数测量挑战了标准 μP 假设，具有重要的理论启发意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the mid-alignment assumption (exponent 3/4) hold at significantly larger model scales (e.g., 10B+ parameters), or does it shift toward full alignment as μP predicts?
- Can the m_data^{-1/3} token horizon correction be derived from first principles, or is it architecture-dependent?
- How does νGPT interact with non-Adam optimizers (Muon, SOAP, etc.) that are increasingly used in practice?
- Does the finding that η_hidden should not scale with depth generalize to other architectures with trainable residual mixing parameters?
- What happens when batch size is scaled along with width and depth -- does the mid-alignment assumption still hold?
- Could the alignment exponent framework be used dynamically during training to adjust learning rates online?
