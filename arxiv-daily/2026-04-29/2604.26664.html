Circular Phase Representation and Geometry-Aware Optimization for Ptychographic Image Reconstruction

arXiv: 2604.26664v1

Date: 2026-04-29

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26664v1

一句话总结： 本文提出将相位重建建模为单位圆上的(cos, sin)表示，配合可微分测地线损失和流形一致性约束，从根本上解决了传统方法将周期性相位当作线性变量回归时产生的包裹伪影和梯度失配问题，在合成和实验数据集上幅度PSNR提升+3dB、相位SSIM提升+15pp。
TL;DR: This paper introduces a circular phase representation (cos/sin embedding on the unit circle) with a differentiable geodesic loss for deep-learning-based ptychographic reconstruction. By replacing scalar phase regression with topology-aware circular coordinates, the method eliminates branch-cut discontinuities and produces bounded, smooth gradients. Combined with a saturation-aware dual-gain input module and a composite physics-informed loss, it achieves state-of-the-art results on both synthetic and real synchrotron X-ray datasets -- +3 dB amplitude PSNR, +15 pp phase SSIM over the best baseline, with better mid/high-frequency preservation and only 25 epochs to converge. The approach is a principled fix to a fundamental representation mismatch in learning-based phase imaging.

核心贡献

  • 圆周相位表示：将相位从标量回归改为(cos phi, sin phi)二通道单位圆嵌入，通过atan2恢复相位，彻底消除 +/-pi 处的分支切割不连续性。
  • 可微分测地线损失：提出 L_circular = 1 - cos(Delta phi)，在S^1流形上定义相位误差，处处光滑、梯度有界(最大为2)，避免了包裹算子的不可微问题。
  • 流形一致性约束：对归一化前的预测施加 (c^2 + s^2 - 1)^2 正则化，使解码器输出紧贴单位圆流形，提升优化稳定性。
  • 饱和感知双增益输入模块(SADGS)：对衍射图同时施加低增益(保留强峰)和高增益(增强弱条纹)缩放，双分支编码器分别处理后通过1x1卷积融合，解决了衍射图极大的动态范围问题。
  • 独立三解码器架构：振幅、余弦、正弦各一个解码器，允许不同物理量走不同的特征变换路径。
  • 复合物理约束损失：结合测地线距离、流形一致性约束、空间梯度正则化和结构相似性，在圆周流形上实现几何一致的优化目标。

Key Contributions

  • Circular phase representation: Replaces scalar phase prediction with (cos, sin) two-channel unit-circle embedding, recovering phase via atan2 and eliminating the +/-pi branch-cut discontinuity.
  • Differentiable geodesic loss: Proposes L_circular = 1 - cos(Delta phi) defined on S^1, which is periodic, smooth everywhere, and has bounded gradients (max = 2), avoiding the non-differentiability of phase wrapping operators.
  • Manifold consistency constraint: Regularizes pre-normalized predictions with (c^2 + s^2 - 1)^2 to keep decoder outputs close to the unit-circle manifold, improving optimization stability.
  • Saturation-Aware Dual-Gain Scaling (SADGS): Applies complementary low-gain (g_l=0.001) and high-gain (g_h=4) scaling to diffraction inputs, processes them through parallel encoder branches fused via 1x1 convolution, addressing the extreme dynamic range of diffraction patterns.
  • Three independent decoders: Separate decoders for amplitude (sigmoid), cosine (tanh), and sine (tanh) allow distinct feature transformation paths for each physical quantity.

Methodology

The method addresses a fundamental representation mismatch in learning-based ptychography: phase is a 2pi-periodic angular quantity, but most deep learning methods regress it as a scalar with Euclidean losses. This creates a topological inconsistency where phase values adjacent on the unit circle (e.g., -pi + epsilon and pi - epsilon) appear maximally distant under L2 loss, producing misleading gradients.

Phase representation: Phase is embedded on the unit circle as (cos phi, sin phi) in R^2. The network predicts two channels (c_hat, s_hat), which are projected onto the unit circle via normalized coordinates (Eq. 3), and phase is recovered via atan2.

Circular geodesic loss (Eq. 9): L_circular = (1/N) sum[1 - cos(Delta phi_i)], which equals (1/N) sum[1 - (c_i c_hat_i + s_i s_hat_i)]. This loss behaves quadratically for small errors (approx (Delta phi)^2/2), is globally bounded (max value 2 at Delta phi = pi), and is smooth across wrapping boundaries.

Composite training objective (Eq. 11): Combines base MSE fidelity, amplitude gradient + SSIM terms, phase gradient + SSIM + circular geodesic terms, and a manifold consistency constraint. Loss weights are fixed: w_b=1.0, w_a=1.0, w_p=1.3, w_c=0.1, lambda_circ=0.6, lambda_g=0.12, lambda_s=0.1. Note: lambda_circ corresponds to the lambda_circ in the circular loss term of the phase loss.

Network architecture: SADGS module produces two scaled views (I_l, I_h) from raw diffraction intensity. Two encoder branches (3 conv stages, 5x5 kernels, stride-2) process each view independently. Their outputs are concatenated and projected via a bias-free 1x1 convolution. A shared learnable skip connection from the raw input feeds all three decoders (amplitude/cos/sin). Each decoder has 3 upsampling stages with conv refinement. Amplitude uses sigmoid output; cos/sin use tanh.

Training: Adam optimizer with triangular-2 cyclic LR (eta=1e-3), gradient clipping at norm 1.0, convergence in 25 epochs.

Results & Highlights

Per-sample evaluation (synthetic, noise-free): Amplitude PSNR 17.31 dB (+3.03 dB over PPN), SSIM 85.69% (+16.01 pp over PPN). Phase SSIM 73.91% (+15.63 pp over DPI). MSE reductions of 31.5% (amplitude) and 22.7% (phase) vs. best baselines.

Per-sample evaluation (noisy): Maintains best performance. Amplitude +1.77 dB PSNR, +11.51 pp SSIM over PPN. Phase SSIM 73.10% (+16.50 pp over DPI).

Stitched full-field (noise-free): Amplitude +2.61 dB, +8.60 pp SSIM over PPN. Phase SSIM 80.75% (+13.09 pp over PtychoNN). MSE reduced by 45.3% (amplitude) and 45.2% (phase).

Experimental synchrotron data: Amplitude PSNR 24.32 dB, SSIM 83.92% (+0.87 dB, +3.60 pp over best baseline). Phase MSE drops 36.5% (from 3.26 to 2.07), PSNR 26.14 dB, SSIM 90.54% (+1.97 dB, +3.00 pp).

Spatial frequency analysis: Better preservation of mid-to-high frequency content. Mid-band amplitude energy 26.4% vs. 20.9% for PPN. Mid-band phase energy 3.3% (closest to ground truth 4.0% among all learned methods).

Efficiency: Moderate FLOP budget with fewer parameters than DPI and PPN. Converges in 25 epochs with low per-epoch time, substantially shorter total training time than DPI and PPN.

Ablation highlights: Removing circular representation causes the largest degradation (amplitude MSE +219.6%, amplitude SSIM drops from 85.69% to 59.63%). Removing geodesic circular loss or consistency loss each drops phase SSIM by ~24-26%. Dual-gain scaling is the most important architectural component. Extending to three gains yields marginal benefit; deeper fusion slightly hurts.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: The circular phase representation via (cos, sin) embedding is not entirely new -- biternion nets and spherical regression have used similar ideas for angular variables in other domains (pose estimation, viewpoint prediction). However, the systematic application to ptychographic phase retrieval, with the geodesic loss, manifold consistency constraint, and the detailed analysis of why scalar phase regression fails in this context, represents a meaningful and well-motivated contribution. The saturation-aware dual-gain module is a practical engineering contribution rather than a deep conceptual advance.

Rigor: The paper is thorough. It provides: (1) a clear theoretical motivation with a concrete example of the branch-cut problem, (2) comparison of four phase regression strategies in Table I, (3) evaluation on both synthetic and experimental datasets, (4) per-sample and stitched full-field evaluation, (5) spatial frequency analysis, (6) 12-variant ablation study with both architectural and loss function ablations, (7) complexity/efficiency comparison. The loss weight selection procedure is described (magnitude matching + local search on validation set), which is transparent if not fully principled.

Reproducibility: Code is promised at a GitHub URL. Training details are well-specified (optimizer, LR schedule, gradient clipping, all hyperparameters listed). Datasets are either publicly available or derived from public sources. PyTorch Lightning is used for standardized training. The network architecture is described in sufficient detail (kernel sizes, channel widths, activation functions). This is above-average reproducibility for the field.

Significance: The contribution is significant for the ptychographic imaging community. The core insight -- that phase should be modeled as a circular variable rather than a scalar -- is correct, well-argued, and the resulting performance gains are substantial. The method also has broader applicability to any phase retrieval or angular regression problem.

Red flags: (1) The synthetic dataset is relatively small (3721 samples, 144 test samples), which raises questions about statistical robustness of the reported numbers. (2) The paper does not compare against iterative reconstruction methods (ePIE, difference map) in terms of reconstruction quality -- only speed is claimed. It is unclear how close the DL results are to iterative baselines. (3) The comparison is limited to feedforward DL methods; hybrid iterative-DL methods (e.g., Deep Iterative Phase Retrieval) are not benchmarked. (4) Loss weights are hand-tuned on a validation set, which is a potential source of overfitting to the evaluation protocol. (5) The generalization claim is based on one experimental dataset; more diverse experimental validation would strengthen it. (6) The paper mentions "submitted to IEEE" but no peer review status is indicated.

Strengths

  • Principled treatment of phase as a circular variable, with clear theoretical motivation and a convincing argument against scalar regression.
  • The geodesic loss is elegant -- bounded gradients, smooth everywhere, naturally periodic, quadratic near optimum.
  • Comprehensive ablation study (12 variants) clearly establishes the importance ordering of components.
  • Strong quantitative improvements over prior DL methods across multiple settings (noise-free, noisy, stitched, experimental).
  • Spatial frequency analysis provides mechanistic insight beyond pixel-wise metrics.
  • Lightweight architecture that converges fast and uses fewer parameters than heavier baselines.
  • Clear writing with well-organized sections and useful algorithm pseudocode.

Limitations

  • No comparison with iterative methods on reconstruction quality -- only speed claims are made. Readers cannot judge the absolute reconstruction fidelity gap.
  • Synthetic dataset is small (144 test samples); confidence intervals or statistical tests are not reported.
  • Only one experimental dataset is used for real-data validation.
  • Hand-tuned loss weights on a validation set could overfit to the evaluation protocol; no sensitivity analysis around the chosen weights.
  • The method recovers phase via atan2 from normalized (c, s), which inherently cannot resolve phase unwrapping for phase ranges exceeding 2pi. The paper does not discuss this limitation or how it would handle strong phase wrapping (e.g., thick specimens in electron ptychography).
  • No comparison with hybrid iterative-DL approaches that might combine the strengths of both paradigms.
  • The network processes single diffraction patterns independently; spatial coherence across scan positions is only enforced during stitching, not during reconstruction.

Relevance

本文的核心思想——将周期性物理量建模在正确的拓扑流形上而非强行线性回归——对计算成像和相位恢复领域具有普适启发性，其圆周表示和测地线损失的设计思路可直接迁移到其他涉及角度/相位预测的深度学习任务中。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does the circular representation interact with phase unwrapping for specimens with phase shifts exceeding 2pi (e.g., thick biological samples in electron ptychography)?
  2. Can the geodesic circular loss be combined with iterative refinement (e.g., as a regularizer in deep iterative phase retrieval) for further quality gains?
  3. What happens when the method is applied to multi-wavelength or multi-energy ptychography where phase periodicity differs across channels?
  4. Is the performance sensitive to the exact loss weights, or is there a broad plateau of good performance?
  5. Could a learned gain selection mechanism replace the fixed dual-gain exponents, adapting to different detector characteristics or specimen types?
