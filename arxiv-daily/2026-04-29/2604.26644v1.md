> **一句话总结：** 本文提出了一种免训练的测试时扩展框架，利用模型输出的不一致性（disagreement）作为实例难度信号，将不同难度的数学推理问题动态路由到轻量解答、多数投票或重写策略，在七个数学基准上以更少的采样次数实现了3%-7%的准确率提升。

> **TL;DR:** A training-free test-time scaling framework that uses output disagreement from just two model samples as a proxy for instance difficulty, routing easy cases to direct resolution, moderate cases to majority voting, and hard cases to problem rewriting. Evaluated on 7 math benchmarks with 3 models (Qwen3-4B/8B, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B), it achieves 3-7% absolute accuracy improvements while using only 40-70% of the sampling budget of majority voting or Best-of-N baselines. The key insight is that disagreement correlates strongly with both difficulty and incorrectness, making it a cheap and effective routing signal.

## 核心贡献

- **发现输出不一致性与实例难度/正确性之间存在强相关性**，为测试时策略选择提供了轻量级信号，无需额外训练或外部模型。
- **提出最小不一致性检测器（Minimal Disagreement Detector, MDD）**，仅需两次采样即可将实例分为无争议（NDS）、轻微争议（MDS）和严重争议（SDS）三类。
- **设计三阶段自适应路由框架**：无争议实例直接输出、轻微争议实例使用多数投票、严重争议实例使用重写再推理，避免了对简单问题的过度计算和对困难问题的无效投票。
- **在七个数学基准和三个模型上验证**，准确率提升3%-7%的同时，采样预算仅为对比方法的40%-70%。
- **深入分析了重写的适用性**，将重写效果分为有效、无效和有害三类，并从错误类型角度解释了各自的成因。

## Key Contributions

- **Identifies a strong correlation between output disagreement and instance difficulty/correctness**, establishing disagreement as a lightweight proxy for uncertainty without requiring external verifiers or reward models.
- **Proposes the Minimal Disagreement Detector (MDD)**, a simple two-sample consistency check that categorizes instances into No Disagreement (NDS), Minor Disagreement (MDS), and Severe Disagreement (SDS) groups.
- **Designs a three-stage adaptive routing pipeline**: (1) Disagreement Filter for direct resolution of easy cases, (2) Vote Resolve for moderate-disagreement cases via majority voting, and (3) Rewrite & Rethink for high-disagreement cases via problem reformulation.
- **Achieves 3-7% absolute accuracy gains on 7 math benchmarks across 3 models** while using only 40-70% of the sampling budget compared to majority voting, dynamic voting, SCoP, and Best-of-N baselines.
- **Provides a detailed error taxonomy for rewriting** (hidden info errors, format errors, context loss, calculation errors, over-compression), explaining when rewriting helps, does nothing, or causes harm.

## Methodology

The framework operates in three stages, all using the same base model without any fine-tuning or auxiliary models:

**Stage 1 -- Disagreement Filter:** Each problem is sampled twice. If both answers agree, the sample is classified as No Disagreement (NDS) and the answer is accepted directly. If the answers disagree, a provisional answer is recorded and the sample proceeds to Stage 2. This stage is designed to maximize recall of correct predictions while minimizing unnecessary computation.

**Stage 2 -- Vote Resolve:** Remaining samples are sampled twice more. If the new pair agrees, the sample is classified as Minor Disagreement (MDS) and the final answer is determined by majority voting over all four samples. If the new pair also disagrees, the sample is classified as Severe Disagreement (SDS) and proceeds to Stage 3.

**Stage 3 -- Rewrite & Rethink:** SDS samples are rewritten by prompting the model to reformulate the problem while preserving semantics, then a single reasoning pass is performed on the rewritten version. This encourages the model to approach the problem from a different angle, escaping previously incorrect reasoning trajectories.

The entire pipeline uses at most 6 samplings per instance (2 + 2 + 1 rewrite + 1 re-reasoning). Answer equivalence checking employs a hierarchical matching procedure covering normalization, numerical equivalence (with tolerance), structural matching for tuples/lists/matrices, and symbolic equivalence for equations.

## Results & Highlights

- **Main results (8K context):** On 7 benchmarks (GSM8K, Math500, Gaokao2023en, OlympiadBench, AMC23, AIME24, AIME25), the method achieves the highest average accuracy across all three models. For Qwen3-8B, average accuracy improves from ~70% (majority voting) to ~76%. Gains are largest on harder benchmarks (OlympiadBench, AIME24/25).
- **Sampling efficiency:** The method uses approximately 40-60% of the maximum sampling budget on Math500 and OlympiadBench, and ~70% on AIME24. Wall-clock time and token counts are correspondingly lower than baselines.
- **Ablation study:** Both the rewriting-only and majority-voting-only components improve over baseline, but the full framework combining both achieves the best results, confirming their synergy. Rewriting contributes more on hard benchmarks; voting contributes more on easy ones.
- **Generalization to code generation:** Evaluated on HumanEval, MBPP, and their enhanced variants with Qwen3-8B, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B, and Qwen2.5-Coder-7B-Instruct, using functional equivalence (test-case matching) instead of answer equality for disagreement detection. The method shows consistent improvements, though gains are smaller than on math tasks.
- **Rewriting analysis:** The method achieves higher effective rewriting rates and lower harmful rewriting rates compared to SCoP, which applies rewriting uniformly to all instances.
- **Scalability:** Additional experiments with Qwen3-32B on a subset of benchmarks confirm the method scales to larger models.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate):** The idea of using output disagreement as a difficulty signal is intuitive and has been explored in prior work (e.g., dynamic voting, self-agreement). The main novelty lies in the specific three-stage routing design that combines disagreement filtering with rewriting, and the detailed analysis of when rewriting helps versus hurts. The MDD itself is essentially a two-sample consistency check -- simple but effective.
- **Rigor (Good):** The paper includes ablation studies, budget sensitivity analysis, per-component analysis, rewriting effectiveness categorization, and generalization to code generation. However, the framework has several hyperparameters (number of iterations, disagreement thresholds) whose sensitivity is only partially explored.
- **Reproducibility (Adequate):** Temperature, top-p, top-k, and seed values are provided. Prompt templates are included in the appendix. However, the rewriting prompt template is only described qualitatively (Appendix C referenced but content is minimal in the HTML version), and the answer equivalence checking procedure, while described, has implementation details that could affect results.
- **Significance (Moderate-High):** The practical impact is notable -- achieving better accuracy with fewer samples is valuable for deployment. The analysis of rewriting effectiveness (effective vs. ineffective vs. harmful) provides useful insights for the community. However, the gains on already-saturated benchmarks (GSM8K) are minimal, and the framework is limited to tasks with verifiable answers.
- **Red flags:**
  - The 3-7% improvement claim is somewhat misleading when examined per-benchmark -- on GSM8K the improvement is near zero for Qwen3 models. The average is boosted by large gains on harder benchmarks.
  - The method is evaluated only on mathematical reasoning and code generation, both domains with relatively unambiguous correctness criteria. Its applicability to open-ended or subjective tasks is unclear.
  - False agreement (consistent but incorrect) is acknowledged as a limitation but not quantified empirically.
  - The comparison with BoN uses Skywork-Reward-V2, which may not be optimal for math reasoning; a stronger reward model could narrow the gap.

## Strengths

- Training-free and requires no external models, making it easy to adopt and integrate into existing inference pipelines.
- Strong empirical results with clear sampling efficiency gains -- achieves more with less computation.
- The three-stage design is well-motivated by empirical observations about the relationship between disagreement, difficulty, and correctness.
- Thorough analysis of rewriting behavior provides actionable insights for practitioners.
- Clean experimental methodology with multiple seeds, models, and benchmarks, including code generation generalization.

## Limitations

- **False agreement problem:** Cases where the model consistently produces the same wrong answer will be classified as NDS and accepted without further scrutiny. The paper acknowledges this but offers no mitigation.
- **Limited to verifiable outputs:** The framework fundamentally relies on answer equivalence checking, restricting its applicability to domains with objective correctness criteria (math, code).
- **Rewriting quality uncontrolled:** The rewriting step uses a single prompt without any quality assurance. Poor rewrites can degrade performance (the "over-compression error" case).
- **Narrow strategy set:** The framework only routes between three strategies (direct, voting, rewriting). Incorporating verification-based methods or tree search could further improve results but is left as future work.
- **Sensitivity to model calibration:** The effectiveness of disagreement as a signal depends on the model's calibration under stochastic sampling. Models that are systematically overconfident or underconfident may not produce useful disagreement patterns.

## Relevance

本文提出的不一致性驱动路由策略为测试时计算扩展提供了一种高效且易于实现的方案，对于关注推理模型部署效率、自适应推理策略选择以及数学推理增强的研究者具有较高的参考价值。其免训练设计和采样效率优势使其在实际应用场景中具有直接的可操作性。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the framework perform on non-mathematical reasoning tasks (e.g., logical deduction, commonsense reasoning) where answer equivalence is harder to define?
- Can the MDD signal be combined with internal model confidence scores (e.g., token-level probabilities) to improve routing accuracy?
- What is the false agreement rate across different benchmarks, and how much performance is lost due to consistently incorrect but agreeing samples?
- How does the framework interact with stronger reward models or process-based verifiers in the BoN comparison?
- Could an adaptive iteration count (rather than fixed 2 iterations) further improve the efficiency-accuracy trade-off?
- How robust is the rewriting step to prompt variation, and can rewriting quality be improved through multi-candidate selection?
