> **一句话总结：** TLPO提出了一种在token级别进行策略优化的微调框架，通过仅在语言混淆发生的具体位置（confusion point）探索候选token并更新策略，显著降低了多语言LLM的语言混淆率（RPR从96.68%提升至99.19%），同时几乎不损害模型的通用能力（准确率从58.35%保持至58.08%）。

> **TL;DR:** Samsung SDS proposes Token-Level Policy Optimization (TLPO), a fine-tuning framework that pinpoints exact token positions where language confusion occurs (e.g., a multilingual LLM switching mid-response to an unintended language), explores top-N candidate tokens at those positions, and applies a probability-weighted advantage PPO objective to suppress error-inducing tokens locally. Across 4 models (Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen3-8B, Ministral-8B, Gemma-3-4B) and 4 languages (zh, ar, ko, ja), TLPO achieves the highest Response Pass Rate (99.19%) while preserving baseline accuracy (58.08% vs. 58.35%), substantially outperforming SFT (accuracy drops to 50.71%), DPO (55.94%), and ORPO (55.12%).

## 核心贡献

- 提出了Token-Level Policy Optimization (TLPO)框架，首次在语言混淆发生的精确token位置进行探索和策略更新，而非对整个序列进行优化，实现了细粒度的错误修正。
- 设计了概率排序探索策略（probability-ranked exploration），在混淆点选取top-N高概率候选token，结合概率加权的优势函数（advantage）进行PPO式优化，确保抑制混淆token的同时保留原始概率分布。
- 实验覆盖4个主流多语言LLM和4种目标语言，在两种英语处理设定（中性类别/严格混淆）下全面验证了TLPO在降低语言混淆率的同时几乎不损失通用任务性能。
- 发现TLPO的参数更新会隐式地降低top-N集合之外的其他混淆token的概率，具有良好的泛化效果。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces TLPO, the first token-level fine-tuning framework specifically targeting language confusion in multilingual LLMs by performing exploration and policy updates exclusively at confusion points (the first token decoded in a non-target language).
- Proposes a probability-ranked token exploration strategy combined with a probability-weighted advantage formulation (adapting PPO with clipped objectives and KL regularization), which maintains the relative probability distribution of valid tokens while suppressing confusion-inducing ones.
- Demonstrates comprehensive superiority over SFT, DPO, and ORPO across 4 base models and 4 target languages under two evaluation settings (English as neutral vs. English as confusion), achieving the best RPR-accuracy trade-off in all scenarios.
- Provides empirical evidence that optimizing over top-N candidates implicitly suppresses confusion-inducing tokens outside the candidate set, suggesting cross-lingual interference is structurally addressable through localized updates.

## Methodology

TLPO operates in three stages:

1. **Confusion Point Detection**: Given a prompt, the model generates an autoregressive response. If language confusion is detected (via character-set analysis following Nie et al., 2025), the confusion point *c* is identified as the index of the first token decoded in a non-target language. Responses without confusion are excluded from training.

2. **Probability-Ranked Token Exploration**: At the confusion point *c*, the top-N tokens (N=16 in main experiments) with the highest probabilities under the current policy are selected as the candidate set T. This deterministic selection focuses optimization on the most likely outputs.

3. **Token-Level Policy Optimization**: A PPO-style objective is adapted to the token-level setting. The reward R(t_i) for each candidate is determined by a short lookahead (k=3 tokens) followed by detokenization to check if the candidate leads to language confusion. The advantage is formulated as A_i = (1/Z) * pi_old(t_i) * (R(t_i) - mu), where mu is the probability-weighted average reward and Z normalizes advantages so that the sum of absolute values equals 1. The clipping and KL divergence terms follow standard PPO practice, with pi_ref being the initial pre-TLPO model.

Key design insight: by incorporating the original token probability pi_old(t_i) into the advantage, TLPO preserves the relative probability distribution among valid tokens even after suppressing error-inducing ones, preventing distributional drift.

## Results & Highlights

- **Neutral English setting**: TLPO achieves 99.19% average RPR (up from 96.68% baseline) while maintaining 58.08% mean accuracy (vs. 58.35% baseline). SFT achieves 99.14% RPR but drops accuracy to 50.71% (catastrophic forgetting). DPO (98.31% RPR, 55.94% accuracy) and ORPO (97.27% RPR, 55.12% accuracy) underperform.
- **Strict English setting**: When all English tokens are treated as confusion, TLPO achieves 77.59% RPR (vs. 63.27% baseline) with 56.17% accuracy (vs. 58.24% baseline), again the best trade-off among all methods.
- **Implicit generalization**: Experiments with N=8 show that confusion-inducing tokens outside the top-N set also experience probability decreases, while non-confusion tokens see probability increases. This suggests TLPO's localized updates generalize across language-specific token clusters.
- **Ablation**: Ranked token selection outperforms multinomial sampling. The probability-weighted advantage formulation outperforms both the unweighted (A = R - mu) and GRPO-style (A = (R - mu)/sigma) variants.
- **GRPO instability noted**: The authors attempted GRPO with sequence-level rewards but observed progressive response shortening, leading to its exclusion from comparisons.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate-to-high. Token-level optimization for language confusion is a genuinely new application. The idea of localizing policy updates to confusion points is elegant and well-motivated. However, the individual components (top-N selection, PPO clipping, KL penalty) are standard; the novelty lies in their specific combination and application domain.
- **Rigor**: Strong experimental design with two evaluation scenarios, multiple models, multiple languages, ablation studies, and probability shift analysis. The exclusion of GRPO due to instability is transparently reported. One concern: the GRPO exclusion could be seen as cherry-picking baselines, though the observed instability is a legitimate experimental issue.
- **Reproducibility**: High. Code is available, hardware setup is specified, training data (Bactrian-X) is public, and hyperparameters are documented. The lookahead mechanism for reward computation is clearly described.
- **Significance**: High practical relevance. Language confusion is a real deployment problem for multilingual LLMs. The token-level approach avoids the accuracy degradation that plagues sequence-level methods, which is a meaningful contribution.
- **Red flags**: The method is specifically designed for errors with clearly defined boundaries (language confusion) and the authors acknowledge it may not generalize to holistic quality metrics like helpfulness. The evaluation is limited to 4 languages and 4 models, though this is reasonably broad. The strict English setting shows notable accuracy drops even for TLPO (58.24% -> 56.17%), suggesting fundamental limits of the approach under extreme constraints.

## Strengths

- Elegant problem formulation: treating language confusion as a localized token-level error rather than a sequence-level alignment problem.
- Strong empirical results: TLPO uniquely achieves near-perfect language consistency without sacrificing general capabilities.
- The implicit generalization effect (probability suppression of non-optimized confusion tokens) is a compelling finding with implications beyond this specific task.
- Comprehensive ablation study validating each design choice (ranked vs. sampled selection, advantage formulations).
- Clear writing with well-structured methodology presentation including formal notation and algorithmic details.

## Limitations

- TLPO requires clearly defined error boundaries for pinpointing confusion points; extending it to tasks with diffuse errors (e.g., general helpfulness, safety) remains an open challenge.
- The lookahead-based reward mechanism adds computational overhead during training, though it is not prohibitive.
- Evaluation is limited to Chinese, Arabic, Korean, and Japanese; performance on very low-resource languages or languages with shared scripts is untested.
- The strict English setting reveals that TLPO cannot fully prevent accuracy degradation when suppressing a dominant language like English.
- Only encoder-decoder and decoder-only transformer architectures are tested; applicability to other architectures is unclear.

## Relevance

本文对研究多语言LLM的部署和优化具有直接参考价值。TLPO提出的token级别策略优化思路不仅适用于语言混淆问题，其"精确定位错误位置+局部更新"的范式可能推广到其他需要细粒度控制生成质量的场景，如减少幻觉、格式控制等。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can TLPO be extended to address other localized generation errors beyond language confusion, such as factual hallucinations at specific entity mentions or format violations?
- How does TLPO scale to very low-resource languages where tokenizers may not cleanly separate language-specific tokens?
- What is the computational overhead of the lookahead-based reward computation compared to standard sequence-level training, and can it be further reduced?
- Could TLPO be combined with sequence-level methods (e.g., TLPO for language consistency + DPO for overall quality) in a multi-stage pipeline?
- Does the implicit generalization effect (suppressing non-optimized confusion tokens) hold for all language pairs, or are there cases where it fails?
