> **一句话总结：** 本文将语言模型的激活空间操控（steering）重新定义为黎曼流形上的测地线计算问题，利用 GAGA 编码器从输出分布的 Hellinger 距离中学习一个无需逐样本标签的度量，在 Llama-3.1-8B 的四任务算术基准上实现了对所有目标类别的可靠操控（waypoint legibility ≥ 99%，终点目标概率 1.4–5.7× 于基线），并在小输出空间任务（星期、月份）上取得了显著的行为保真度提升（EBC 降低 2.4–11.9 倍）。

> **TL;DR:** This ICLR 2026 paper reframes language model activation steering as Riemannian geodesic computation, showing linear and spline steering are degenerate special cases. A GAGA encoder trained on output-Hellinger distances (no per-prompt labels, no topology prior) learns a pullback metric surrogate that reliably drives Llama-3.1-8B to target classes on all four arithmetic tasks, with 2.4–11.9× better behavioral fidelity on small output spaces but worse fidelity on larger ones (letters, ages).

## 核心贡献

- 将流形操控统一为**黎曼测地线计算框架**：线性操控（g=I）和标签样条操控（J_s^T J_s）被证明是该框架下特定度量的退化特例，而非竞争方法。
- 提出基于 **GAGA 编码器的学习型度量替代方案**，通过在概念 token schema 上的输出 Hellinger 距离训练编码器，无需逐 prompt 标签或拓扑先验即可近似原则性的输出-拉回度量 G_F。
- 将度量选择与求解器设计**解耦为正交的坐标轴**：任意度量可与任意兼容求解器配对（闭式解、L-BFGS、参数化桥），提供了灵活的模块化设计空间。
- 在四任务基准上，GAGA-Out 是**唯一在所有任务上都可靠操控到目标类别的方法**（legibility ≥ 99%，终点 top-1 命中率 76–100%），而 PCA(64) 子空间基线方法在大多数任务上基本无法操控模型。

## Key Contributions

- **Unified Riemannian framework** for manifold steering that subsumes linear steering (geodesic under flat metric g=I) and labeled spline steering (geodesic under first fundamental form J_s^T J_s) as special cases of the same construction.
- **Schema-supervised, label-free metric learning** via a GAGA encoder trained on output-Hellinger distances over a small concept-token vocabulary — no per-prompt class labels, no boundary-condition prior, no per-task curve fitting required.
- **Decoupled metric-solver design**: metric choice (flat, density-based, output pullback, learned encoder) and solver choice (closed-form, L-BFGS, amortized bridge) are orthogonal axes, enabling any combination.
- **Comprehensive empirical validation** showing the GAGA-Out variant is the only method that reliably drives the model onto the target class across all four tasks, with waypoint legibility ≥ 99% and 1.4–5.7× higher endpoint target probability than the best baseline.
- **Surrogate validity check**: the learned GAGA-Out encoder produces behaviorally indistinguishable geodesics from the analytical G_F pullback metric on weekdays, confirming the surrogate faithfully approximates the principled target.

## Methodology

The paper operates on Llama-3.1-8B residual-stream activations at layer 28 (dimension 4096) across four language-model arithmetic tasks: weekdays (|Z|=7, cyclic), months (|Z|=12, cyclic), letters (|Z|=22, sequential), and ages (|Z|≈91, sequential).

**Framework.** Steering between activations h_0 and h_1 is cast as finding the geodesic under a Riemannian metric g on activation space A = R^4096. The paper identifies three metric instantiations: (1) flat g=I (linear baseline), (2) first fundamental form of a hand-specified cubic spline through labeled centroids (spline baseline), and (3) a learned encoder pullback G_φ = J_φ^T J_φ + εI (GAGA).

**GAGA encoder.** A Geometry-Aware Generative Autoencoder (MLP: 4096→1024→512→256→128→k, with BatchNorm+ReLU) is trained with three losses: distance-matching (L_dist matching latent Euclidean distances to target supervision distances with exponential local weighting), reconstruction (L_recon), and cycle consistency (L_cycle). Two supervision modes are explored: GAGA-PHATE (PHATE diffusion distances on activations) and GAGA-Out (Hellinger distances on output distributions over concept tokens). The empirically winning variant is GAGA-Out with raw-4096 ambient space and latent dimension k=|Z|.

**Geodesic computation.** Paths are discretized as K=50 free waypoints optimized via L-BFGS with the freeze-metric approximation. The encoder Jacobian J_φ at each waypoint is detached from the optimizer graph; gradients flow only through segment differences Δ_k. The εI regularizer (ε=10^{-3}) absorbs rank deficiency from the low-rank pullback.

**Evaluation metrics.** (1) EBC: cumulative Bhattacharyya energy measuring how natural intermediate outputs are; (2) legibility: fraction of waypoints whose top-1 token is a labeled class token; (3) target_prob_end: target-class probability at endpoint; (4) top-1@end: rate of correct top-1 endpoint prediction.

## Results & Highlights

- **Small output spaces — strong win:** GAGA-Out achieves 11.9× lower EBC than baselines on weekdays (0.0085 vs 0.101) and 2.4× on months (0.0456 vs 0.112), with paired-t statistics of −34.1 and −24.8 respectively.
- **Large output spaces — fidelity loss but steering strength win:** On letters and ages, GAGA-Out has worse EBC (0.035 vs 0.023; 0.53 vs 0.19) but far superior steering — it is the only method that actually moves the model to the target (ages target_prob: 0.785 vs 0.138).
- **Surrogate matches analytical metric:** On weekdays, GAGA-Out and the analytical G_F pullback produce identical EBC (0.0085), legibility (1.000), and endpoint metrics, validating the learned surrogate.
- **PCA(64) encoders are inert:** A 15-variant ablation shows every PCA(64) GAGA encoder produces results indistinguishable from the linear baseline — only the raw-4096 representation separates from baselines.
- **Steering strength is structural:** GAGA-Out achieves legibility ≥ 0.991 on all four tasks; the best baseline never exceeds 0.808 (spline, ages). Target probability at endpoint is 1.4–5.7× the better baseline across tasks.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The Riemannian unification is elegant — showing linear and spline steering are degenerate special cases of the same framework is a genuine conceptual advance. The label-free, schema-supervised metric learning via GAGA is a practical contribution.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Theoretical grounding is careful (pullback metrics, geodesic formalism, rank-deficiency handling). The surrogate validity check against analytical G_F on weekdays is a meaningful control. Paired-t tests with extreme p-values confirm statistical sharpness.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate-to-good.** Architecture, training hyperparameters, and evaluation protocol are detailed in appendices. However, single-seed training (seed0) and unreleased code are concerns.
- **Significance: Mixed.** The framework contribution is broadly applicable, but the empirical results are limited to 1-D conceptual tasks on one model. The sharp output-space-size split (win on small, lose on large) is a major unresolved puzzle — the paper honestly acknowledges this but does not resolve it.
- **Red flags:** (1) The PCA(64) inertness finding suggests the method's success depends heavily on operating in the full raw-4096 space, which raises questions about scalability to larger models. (2) Corpus sizes are very small (~10^3 per task with heavy paraphrase replication). (3) Only one model (Llama-3.1-8B) and one injection layer (layer 28) are tested.

## Strengths

- Elegant theoretical unification that subsumes prior baselines as special cases rather than treating them as competing methods.
- The metric-solver decoupling creates a clean, extensible design space for future work.
- Label-free supervision removes a significant practical barrier to applying manifold steering.
- Transparent reporting of both wins and losses — the output-space-size split is reported honestly with sharp statistics.
- The surrogate validity check (GAGA-Out ≈ analytical G_F on weekdays) provides concrete evidence that the learned metric approximates the principled target.
- Comprehensive ablation (15 PCA(64) encoder variants) definitively establishes that the raw-4096 representation is critical.

## Limitations

- **Output-space-size puzzle:** GAGA-Out loses behavioral fidelity (EBC) on letters and ages, suggesting the schema-supervised encoder diverges from the model's true behavior geometry as output space grows. Whether this is intrinsic or fixable with more training/seeds is unresolved.
- **Limited task scope:** All four tasks are 1-D conceptual domains (cyclic or sequential). Extension to multi-dimensional concept manifolds (tori, graphs, hierarchical structures) is claimed but not empirically demonstrated.
- **Small corpora with paraphrase replication:** Weekdays rests on only 49 distinct facts × 21 paraphrases; the effective sample complexity may be lower than the nominal ~10^3 suggests.
- **Single model and layer:** Only Llama-3.1-8B at layer 28 is tested. Generalization to other architectures, scales, or injection points is unknown.
- **Schema supervision is not fully unsupervised:** The concept-token vocabulary and Hellinger evaluator are fixed inputs, so the method is not truly label-free — it is label-free at the prompt level but requires domain knowledge to define the schema.

## Relevance

本文提出的黎曼几何框架和学习型度量方法为语言模型激活空间的几何感知操控提供了理论基础和实用工具，对可解释性研究和模型控制具有直接参考价值。GAGA 编码器的 schema 监督模式可在不依赖逐样本标签的前提下实现流形操控，适用于需要细粒度行为调控但标注成本高的场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the output-space-size fidelity gap be closed with multi-seed training, larger corpora, or alternative supervision targets (e.g., full vocabulary distributions instead of concept-token schemas)?
2. How does the framework extend to multi-dimensional concept manifolds (e.g., the Park et al. 2025 grid task, hierarchical ontologies, or partially-cyclic schedules)?
3. What happens when GAGA is applied to larger models (70B+) or different architectures (Mistral, Gemma)? Does the raw-ambient-space requirement scale tractably?
4. Can the amortized GeodesicBridge solver be strengthened to produce curves that stay on-manifold, enabling the ~10^3 per-pair speedup over L-BFGS without quality loss?
5. Is the PCA(64) inertness an intrinsic limitation of dimensionality reduction, or could alternative dimensionality reduction methods (e.g., UMAP, spectral methods) recover the steering effect in lower dimensions?
