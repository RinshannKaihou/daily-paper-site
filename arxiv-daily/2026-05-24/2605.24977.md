> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一种推理时的 SAE 残差转向方法，冻结医学 VLM 权重，在 Top-K SAE 稀疏基上对每类临床错误（假发现/漏诊/位置错/严重度错）做因果特征筛选后实施 suppress/boost 残差编辑；在 MIMIC-CXR 上对 RadVLM、LLaVA-Rad、CheXOne 三个模型的临床复合指标分别相对提升 +5.4%、+7.2%、+17.0%，GREEN 均达 p<0.001 显著，且发现 boost 方向跨模型高度共享而 suppress 方向高度模型特异。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes an inference-only SAE residual-steering method that freezes medical VLM weights and edits the residual stream on a per-token Top-K SAE basis (D=32,768, K=64, d=4,096) at layers {8,16,20,24}, after a single-feature causal screen against GREEN error types (false findings, missing findings, wrong location, wrong severity). On the MIMIC-CXR test split (3,314 studies), the method improves the clinical Composite Score by +5.4% rel. on RadVLM (Qwen-3-VL-8B, α=0.20, K=20), +7.2% rel. on LLaVA-Rad (7B, α=0.50, K=10), and +17.0% rel. on CheXOne (Qwen2.5-VL-3B GRPO-tuned, α=0.50, K=100) vs. each model's own unsteered baseline, with GREEN gains significant at p<0.001 (paired bootstrap, 10,000 resamples). Zero-shot transfer to IU-Xray gives GREEN +7.7% rel. A cross-model alignment finds boost directions overlap strongly (cosine 0.91–0.95) while suppress directions are model-specific (cosine as low as 0.25).

## 核心贡献

- 首次将基于 SAE 的转向方法应用于医学影像报告生成，提出推理时残差编辑流水线（冻结权重），对三类医学 VLM 有效。
- 设计两阶段特征识别：基于激活差的预筛选（top ∼500）+ 单特征因果筛选（按 GREEN 错误类型分别记录 ΔFF/ΔMF/ΔWL/ΔWS），而非仅依赖相关性。
- 采用纯残差更新 δ=decode(z′)−decode(z) 加到原隐状态 h′=h+α·δ，避免 SAE 重构误差注入网络；suppress/boost 合并策略并以 suppress 优先解决冲突。
- 跨模型功能对齐分析揭示结构性发现：提升质量的 boost 方向跨架构高度共享，抑制幻觉的 suppress 方向高度模型特异——因此可迁移的转向必须按 backbone 分别处理抑制。
- 零样本跨数据集迁移至 IU-Xray 仍有效（GREEN +7.7% rel.），证明识别出的特征属于模型本身而非训练语料；并证明 SAE 转向与 GRPO 微调互补而非冗余。

## Key Contributions

- First application of SAE-based steering to medical report generation, with an inference-only residual-edit pipeline that keeps VLM weights frozen and works across three radiology VLMs (RadVLM, LLaVA-Rad, CheXOne).
- A two-stage feature-identification procedure: an activation-difference prefilter (top ~500 features by mean-activation gap between high-error and low-error quartiles) followed by a single-feature causal screen that records per-error-type effects ΔFF/ΔMF/ΔWL/ΔWS, instead of relying on Pearson correlation alone.
- A pure residual update δ=decode(z′)−decode(z) added as h′=h+α·δ (Eq. 4) that cancels SAE reconstruction residue and preserves unedited knowledge; a Combined suppress+boost edit (zero suppress, scale boost by 1+β, β=1.0) with suppress-priority conflict resolution.
- A cross-model functional alignment finding: quality-promoting boost directions overlap strongly across architectures (cosine 0.91–0.95) while hallucination-linked suppress directions are model-specific (cosine as low as 0.25 for LLaVA-Rad vs. CheXOne), so transferable steering must treat suppression per-backbone.
- Zero-shot cross-dataset transfer to IU-Xray (GREEN +7.7% rel., no SAE retraining or re-screening), and evidence that SAE steering is complementary to GRPO fine-tuning (largest gain on the already-GRPO-tuned CheXOne, +17.0% rel. Composite).
- Released causal feature sets and an interactive feature dashboard.

## Methodology

The method intervenes on a frozen radiology VLM at decoding time in four stages (Fig. 2):

**(A) Residual-stream activation collection.** Save per-token hidden states h_ℓ^(t) at every transformer layer ℓ during report generation, for both SAE training and intervention-hook placement.

**(B) Top-K SAE training.** At each layer ℓ, a Top-K SAE encodes h_ℓ^(t) into a sparse code z_ℓ^(t)∈ℝ^D with at most K nonzeros and decodes ĥ_ℓ=decode_ℓ(z_ℓ). Configuration: dictionary size D=32,768, sparsity K=64, hidden dimension d=4,096. SAEs are trained at ten evenly-spaced depths {0,4,8,12,16,20,24,28,32,35} of RadVLM's 36-layer Qwen3-VL backbone; steering hooks are attached only to L={8,16,20,24}, the "sweet spot" where cosine reconstruction similarity is high (>0.97) and activation norms stay moderate (31–128). Early layers (ℓ≤4) have poor decomposition; late layers (ℓ≥28) have norms 10–45× larger (244–1,412), making fixed-size perturbations negligible. SAE training itself is <1 GPU-hour.

**(C) Feature identification.** Two approaches: (1) Correlation-based — Pearson correlation between feature activation magnitude and GREEN error count; most-positive-correlation features become suppress candidates, most-negative become boost. (2) Causal selection — a two-stage procedure: an activation-difference prefilter keeps the top ~500 features by |z̄_j^high − z̄_j^low| (mean-activation gap between top-quartile high-error and bottom-quartile low-error samples); then a single-feature causal screen runs a one-feature intervention (zero or amplify) on 100 held-out validation examples and measures Δ_t^(j)=(1/N)Σ[#GREEN_t(a_i) − #GREEN_t(b_i)] (Eq. 1) for t∈{FF,MF,WL,WS}, where b_i is the baseline decode and a_i the feature-j-ablated decode. Features with Δ<0 (associated with errors) go to suppress lists; Δ>0 (associated with quality) go to boost lists, organized per error type per layer.

**(D) Residual steering.** At inference, ranked lists are truncated to a validation-chosen budget K; causal-selection features are aggregated across all GREEN error types into a master suppress list S_ℓ and master boost list B_ℓ, with conflicts resolved by suppress-priority (overlapping features removed from B_ℓ). Edited features z′_ℓ zero out suppressed features and scale boosted features by (1+β) (Eq. 2; β=1.0, doubling boosted coordinates). Crucially, the edit is applied as a pure residual update δ_ℓ=decode_ℓ(z′_ℓ)−decode_ℓ(z_ℓ) added back as h′_ℓ=h_ℓ+α·δ_ℓ (Eq. 4), rather than standard SAE-mediated activation patching h′_ℓ=(1−α)h_ℓ+α·decode(z′) (Eq. 3), which the authors retain only as an early ablation because it "degrades sharply for α≥0.25."

**Models.** RadVLM (Qwen-3-VL-8B backbone, radiology instruction-tuned); LLaVA-Rad (7B, LLaVA/Vincua); CheXOne (Qwen2.5-VL-3B, GRPO-tuned, evaluated in instruct mode). Each backbone gets its own SAE stack and feature list; weights remain frozen.

**Datasets.** MIMIC-CXR-JPG (Johnson et al., 2024): single frontal chest X-rays paired with findings, prior-study references removed; 230,980 image-text pairs in training/validation and 3,314 in the test set. SAE training and feature identification use the training split (a fixed subset of 3,000 studies with stratified sampling over CheXpert-style pathology groups); hyperparameter tuning (α, K, steering mode) uses the validation split (200 stratified samples); test-set numbers are reported once on a frozen configuration. IU-Xray (N=3,307 frontal studies) is used for zero-shot cross-dataset transfer with no SAE retraining, feature re-screening, or hyperparameter re-tuning. Decoding length is limited to 512 tokens.

**Metrics.** Primary: GREEN (Ostmeier et al., 2024, based on StanfordAIMI/GREEN-radllama2-7b), with six error categories — False Findings (FF), Missing Findings (MF), Wrong Location (WL), Wrong Severity (WS), False Comparisons (FC), Missing Comparisons (MC); the paper focuses on FF/MF/WL/WS since prior-study references were filtered. Also: RadGraph-F1 simple, CheXbert micro F1 (14 labels), BERTScore, BLEU-4, ROUGE-L, RadCliQ-v1. Composite Score = 0.4×GREEN + 0.3×RadGraph + 0.2×CheXbert + 0.1×BERTScore. Scores computed via the RadEval framework.

**Hyperparameter search.** Grid over α∈{0.10,0.15,0.20,0.25,0.30,0.35,0.40,0.50}, K∈{20,50,100}, steering approach (residual vs. blend; Combined vs. suppress-only vs. boost-only), layer subset, and β∈{0.5,1.0}. Optimal operating points: RadVLM (α=0.20, K=20), LLaVA-Rad (α=0.50, K=10), CheXOne (α=0.50, K=100); Combined with β=1.0 gains on every metric on every backbone, while suppress-only and boost-only each regress at least one metric on at least one backbone.

## Results & Highlights

- RadVLM on MIMIC-CXR test (Table 1): GREEN 27.7→28.8% (+1.0 pp, +3.9% rel., p<0.001 paired bootstrap 10,000 resamples); RadGraph 18.6→21.0% (+12.9% rel.); CheXbert 47.7→50.3% (+5.5% rel.); BERTScore 52.9→53.0% (+0.2% rel.); BLEU-4 6.6→7.0 (+6.1% rel.); Composite 31.5→33.2% (+1.7 pp, +5.4% rel.); RadCliQ −1.193→−1.159.
- LLaVA-Rad on MIMIC-CXR test (Table 1): GREEN 28.5→31.4% (+2.9 pp, +10.2% rel., p<0.001); RadGraph 16.8→19.4% (+15.4% rel.); Composite 33.3→35.7% (+2.4 pp, +7.2% rel.); RadCliQ −1.374→−1.276.
- CheXOne on MIMIC-CXR test (Table 1): GREEN 22.3→24.9% (+2.6 pp, +11.7% rel., p<0.001); RadGraph 18.9→20.2% (+6.8% rel.); Composite 25.0→29.2% (+4.2 pp, +17.0% rel.); RadCliQ −1.339→−1.237. The largest gain is on the already-GRPO-tuned CheXOne, indicating SAE steering is complementary to RL rather than redundant.
- Error redistribution on RadVLM (Table 2): steering removes 654 Missing-Finding errors (7,888→7,234) but adds +1,112 FF (5,305→6,417), +168 WL (1,040→1,208), +72 WS (881→953); GREEN nevertheless improves because of its structural asymmetry in MF (a recovered missing finding adds 1 to numerator and subtracts 1 from denominator, whereas a new FF/WL/WS only enlarges the denominator).
- Zero-shot cross-dataset transfer to IU-Xray (RadVLM-steered, same weights/feature lists/hyperparameters, no retraining): GREEN +3.8 pp (+7.7% rel.), RadGraph +4.1 pp (+16.7% rel.), Composite +2.5 pp (+5.4% rel.); MF decreased by 850 and total errors dropped by 713.
- Cross-model functional overlap (Table 4, weighted-Jaccard / cosine on top-100 features per direction over steered layers {8,16,20,24}): boost features overlap 65–75% with cosine 0.91–0.95 (tight CIs); suppress features overlap only 41–58% with cosine as low as 0.25 [0.12,0.40] for LLaVA-Rad vs. CheXOne. Boost CIs lie well above suppress CIs in every model pair with no overlap, so the pattern is statistically robust.
- Single-feature causal roles (Table 3, ℓ=16, 500 prefiltered features): the same SAE coordinate can be top suppress for one error type and top boost for another — e.g., #1925 is MF-boost and WL-suppress; #30874 is MF-suppress and FF-boost. 80/500 coordinates simultaneously satisfy ΔFF<0 and ΔMF>0. Per-layer balance differs: ℓ=24 is almost entirely FF-boost (494/500), while wrong-severity shifts from mixed at ℓ=8 (234 vs. 184) to strongly WS-suppress at ℓ=16 (450 vs. 14).
- Compute (Table 6): full SAE pipeline ~27 GPU-hours on NVIDIA GH200 per model (activation collection ~6 GPU-h, SAE training <1 GPU-h, causal feature identification ~20 GPU-h); GRPO fine-tuning ~350 H200 GPU-hours per model (per RadVLM-GRPO). Inference overhead <5% wall-clock (one SAE encode/decode per layer per token, already in GPU memory).

## Critical Assessment

The paper is methodologically careful and the inference-only, weight-frozen framing is genuinely useful, but several paper-specific weaknesses stand out. First, the headline GREEN gains are moderate in absolute terms (RadVLM +1.0 pp, LLaVA-Rad +2.9 pp, CheXOne +2.6 pp) and the authors themselves concede the improvements "are statistically significant but moderate in absolute terms compared with what task-specific fine-tuning or RL delivers" and explicitly state the method is "not a replacement for fine-tuning." Second, the gain is partly an artifact of GREEN's structural asymmetry rather than uniformly better reporting: on RadVLM the steered model removes 654 MF errors but adds +1,112 FF, +168 WL, and +72 WS errors — total significant errors actually rise from 15,379 to 16,014 (Table 2). The authors reframe this as "more complete reporting with some templated repetition," but a clinically deployed system that increases false findings by 1,112 is a real safety concern that the Composite/RadCliQ improvements do not fully neutralize. Third, the cross-model "universal boost / specific suppress" finding, while interesting, is based on only three backbones (two supervised, one GRPO-tuned) and relies on weighted-Jaccard/cosine between top-100 feature error-type profiles — a functional rather than mechanistic alignment, so the claim that boost directions "transfer" is not directly tested by transferring a boost prior from one model to another and measuring gain. Fourth, the method intervenes only on the LLM residual stream, not the vision encoder, so vision-side hallucination sources are untouched; the authors flag joint language+vision steering as future work. Fifth, the SAE dictionary size (D=32,768) and sparsity (K=64) introduce hyperparameters "whose interactions with model scale are not fully characterized," and generalization beyond chest radiography and English reports is an open question.

## Strengths

- Inference-only, weight-frozen: no gradient updates, no prompt changes, no decoding-algorithm changes; stacks on top of (and complements) GRPO fine-tuning.
- Causal feature selection (single-feature intervention + per-error-type Δ) is more faithful than correlation-only proxies and yields interpretable, per-error-type controls.
- Pure residual update (Eq. 4) elegantly avoids injecting SAE reconstruction error, which the authors show degrades the standard patching variant (Eq. 3) for α≥0.25.
- Comprehensive evaluation: three backbones, multiple clinical metrics (GREEN, RadGraph, CheXbert, BERTScore, RadCliQ, BLEU, ROUGE), paired-bootstrap significance, and per-error-type decomposition down to individual SAE features.
- Cross-dataset zero-shot transfer to IU-Xray and the cross-model boost/suppress structural finding are genuinely novel contributions for medical VLM interpretability.
- Compute-efficient: ~27 GPU-h vs. ~350 GPU-h for GRPO, with <5% inference overhead; releases code/dashboard.

## Limitations

- Absolute GREEN gains are moderate (+1.0 to +2.9 pp); the method is positioned as an introspection/steering tool, not a replacement for fine-tuning or RL.
- Error redistribution increases total significant errors on RadVLM (15,379→16,014) with +1,112 false findings — a potential clinical safety concern despite improved aggregate scores.
- Cross-model "universal boost" claim is based on only three backbones and functional (not mechanistic) alignment; no experiment transfers a boost prior across models to confirm gain.
- Only the LLM residual stream is edited; vision-encoder-side hallucination sources are untouched.
- Evaluation is offline, English-only, chest-radiography-only; GREEN and other metrics are proxies for radiologist judgment and may miss clinically important distinctions; comparison-type errors excluded because the MIMIC subset lacks prior studies.
- SAE dictionary size / sparsity hyperparameters and their interaction with model scale are not fully characterized; further backbones and census-driven steering variants beyond the validated Combined recipe are left to future work.

## Relevance

直接对应「LLM Hidden States & Interpretability」方向：用 Top-K SAE 在医学 VLM 残差流上做因果特征筛选与推理时转向，把临床错误类型映射到可命名、可定位、可编辑的稀疏坐标；同时其推理时干预与 GRPO 互补的发现，以及对转向方向跨模型可迁移性的分析，也触及「LLM Inference Evaluation」和「ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations」中关于推理可控性与模型内部机制结构的内容。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can a shared boost prior identified on one backbone be transferred to another model and still improve GREEN/Composite, directly testing the "universal boost" hypothesis beyond functional-similarity evidence?
- How does the +1,112 false-finding increase on RadVLM translate into clinically meaningful harm, and can a stricter FF-limiting variant (adjusting α or per-error-type suppress weights on a local validation set) recover MF gains without the FF inflation?
- Does joint language-stream + vision-stream SAE steering (this work + Pach et al. 2026) yield larger, more balanced gains than language-stream-only editing?
- How do the SAE dictionary size (D=32,768) and sparsity (K=64) interact with model scale — do the optimal layers {8,16,20,24} and operating points (α,K) shift for larger (e.g., 70B) or differently-architected backbones?
- Can the per-error-type causal feature lists be used prospectively as a monitoring/auditing tool for hallucination drift in deployed medical VLMs, beyond inference-time correction?
