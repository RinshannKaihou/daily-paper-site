> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 QML-PipeGuard 框架，通过信息完全的 Pauli 可观测量族在运行时对量子机器学习管道进行行为指纹验证，在同一套合约机制下同时检测校准漂移和对抗性量子信道替换，并在 IBM Heron r2 处理器上完成了端到端验证。

> **TL;DR:** QML-PipeGuard introduces a contract-based behavioral fingerprinting framework for QML pipelines that unifies drift-aware monitoring and adversarial channel-substitution detection under a single observable contract, with formal detection theorems, finite-shot sample-complexity bounds, and end-to-end hardware validation on the IBM Heron r2 processor.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了 **QML-PipeGuard** 框架：首个针对 QML 管道的双模式（漂移监控 + 对抗检测）信道完整性运行时验证方案，两种模式共享同一套可观测量合约机制。
- 建立了 **检测定理（Theorem 1）**：证明信息完全的可观测量族能暴露所有"偷偷替换"（sneaky substitution）的量子信道，并给出了紧优的 Pauli 族帧界常数 C = √3。
- 推导了 **有限样本复杂度界（Theorem 4）**：将检测合约转化为可在真实量子硬件上执行的有限次测量协议，相比先前工作实现了约 100 倍的采样预算缩减。
- 提出了 **容差分解 ε = ε_adv + ε_drift（Corollary 2）**：在同一容差参数下分离对抗性偏移和自然校准漂移，使双模式操作成为可能。
- 在 **IBM Heron r2 (ibm_fez)** 真实量子处理器上完成了端到端验证，包括 QSVM 管道的检测、采样复杂度和漂移校准三个实验。

## Key Contributions

- **Dual-mode observable contract**: A single mathematical machinery handles both benign calibration drift (within-tolerance events logged to audit trail) and adversarial channel substitution (beyond-tolerance triggers pipeline halt), unified under one tolerance parameter ε_A.
- **Tight frame-bound constant**: Derives C = √3 for the single-qubit Pauli family via Cauchy-Schwarz on the Bloch decomposition, improving the concurrent bound of 2√2 by a factor of √(2/3), directly reducing the required shot budget.
- **Finite-shot operational layer**: Theorem 4 provides a Hoeffding+union-bound shot budget N = 8B²k log(2k/η)/γ² that turns the exact-expectation contract into an executable hardware check, validated empirically as the threshold for informative detection (both TPR and FPR targets met).
- **QML pipeline composition treatment**: Formalizes the encoder–ansatz–measurement channel structure for VQCs, QSVMs, and QNNs, with a QML-specific threat model (Definition 2) capturing classifier-decision agreement and weak-observable agreement conditions.
- **First hardware validation**: End-to-end experiments on a production QPU (IBM Heron r2, 156 qubits) on a two-qubit QSVM pipeline including multi-qubit feature map, finite-shot sampling, and natural drift — going beyond isolated CPTP channel tests.

## Methodology

The framework operates on three theoretical layers built atop a behavioral-subtyping foundation:

1. **Behavioral fingerprinting (Definition 1)**: At runtime, the QML pipeline is characterized by a vector of observable expectation values F = (Tr(O₁E(ρ)), ..., Tr(OₖE(ρ))) under a tomographically structured measurement family. The deviation between declared and candidate channel fingerprints is measured by the L∞ contract deviation ∆.

2. **Detection theorem (Theorem 1)**: Proves that any sneaky ansatz substitution with diamond-norm separation δ from the declared channel must produce observable deviation at least δ/C(O_A) on some reference input, when O_A is informationally complete. The proof uses the variational characterization of the diamond norm, informational-completeness reduction, and triangle inequality.

3. **Sample complexity (Theorem 4)**: For a family of k observables with operator norm bound B, the total shot budget N ≥ 8B²k log(2k/η)/γ² (sampled-reference, Corollary 1) suffices for informative detection at confidence 1−η, where γ = δ/C − ε_A is the detection margin.

4. **Tolerance calibration (Section 5.11)**: A three-step procedure: estimate typical drift d_drift from historical data, specify minimum adversarial separation δ_adv^min, then set ε_A ∈ [d_drift, δ_adv^min / C]. The non-empty interval condition ensures both modes are simultaneously viable.

5. **Dual-mode algorithm (Algorithm 1)**: Per verification round, the verifier randomly samples an observable from O_A, collects measurement shots, computes empirical deviation against the reference fingerprint, and either logs a drift event (∆ ≤ ε_A) or halts with an integrity violation (∆ > ε_A).

## Results & Highlights

- **Sneaky channel detection (Experiment 1, ibm_fez)**: The sneaky channel (S-gate insertion before measurement) passes the weak contract {Z₁Z₂} with deviation 0.001, but is caught by the complete Pauli family {X₁, Y₁, Z₁, X₂, Y₂, Z₂} with worst-case deviation 0.489 on X₂ — roughly 3.3× the tolerance ε_A = 0.15. Three of six complete-family observables independently exceed tolerance.
- **Sample complexity validation (Experiment 2, noisy simulator)**: At the conservative sampled-reference budget (N ≈ 13,680 shots, n_O = 2,280 per observable), the verifier achieves TPR = 1.00 and FPR = 0.00. At N/10, FPR degrades to 0.15 (exceeding the η = 0.05 target). At N/100, both rates saturate at 1.00 (pure noise). This confirms the budget is the threshold for *informative* detection, not just nominal flagging.
- **Drift observation (Experiment 3, ibm_fez)**: Natural hardware drift across three timepoints within a single batched job is d_drift = 0.067, well below ε_A = 0.15. The tolerance interval [0.067, 0.289] is non-empty, confirming dual-mode viability on this backend.
- **Shot budget efficiency**: The tight frame constant C = √3 plus precomputed-reference refinement yields approximately 100-fold reduction in N compared to the looser bound, fitting within a single IBM Quantum batched job (~30 seconds wall time).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. This is the first work to unify drift monitoring and adversarial channel-substitution detection for QML pipelines under a single runtime-verifiable contract with formal guarantees. The tight frame-bound constant and finite-shot operationalization are genuine contributions.
- **Rigor**: Very strong. Theorems are proved from first principles (diamond-norm variational characterization, Hoeffding concentration), with explicit assumptions and scope limitations clearly stated. The paper is unusually transparent about what it does *not* cover.
- **Reproducibility**: Excellent. Full open-source code, archived IBM Quantum job IDs, and step-by-step reproduction instructions for every figure and table.
- **Significance**: Currently limited by the two-qubit experimental scale and short-timescale drift observation (minutes, not hours/days). The practical threat model (who would actually substitute a QML channel today?) is somewhat speculative but well-motivated by analogy to classical deployment security.
- **Red flags**: The single-authorship across such a broad scope (quantum information theory + security + ML + hardware experiments) raises questions about depth of review. The concurrent work [45] by the same author on general CPTP behavioral subtyping shares significant theoretical overlap, and the relationship between the two could be more crisply delineated.

## Strengths

- Unified dual-mode framework (drift + adversarial) under a single tolerance is elegant and practically motivated.
- Tight mathematical analysis with the sharp C = √3 constant and informative-detection definition (Definition 4) that goes beyond one-sided TPR.
- End-to-end hardware validation on a real QPU, not just simulation.
- Exceptional transparency: limitations, out-of-scope threats, and open problems are discussed thoroughly.
- Complementary positioning relative to existing QML verification tools (QuPAD, Q-SafeML, VeriQR, Q-ID) clearly articulated via comparison tables.

## Limitations

- **Scale**: Only validated on a two-qubit QSVM pipeline. Scaling to 8–12 qubits with deeper circuits is deferred to future work.
- **Drift timescale**: Three timepoints within minutes capture short-term variability, not the multi-hour/multi-day drift relevant to operational deployments.
- **Local Pauli family scope**: The deployed observable family is informationally complete only against local-unitary substitutions (Proposition 1). Entangling-gate insertions and correlated-noise channels require augmenting with correlation observables, which significantly increases shot cost.
- **Reference channel assumption**: Requires a trusted reference fingerprint; if unavailable, the contract degrades to a relative consistency check.
- **Sample complexity**: The O(k log k/γ²) bound scales linearly with observable family size, becoming a bottleneck for full multi-qubit Pauli families.

## Relevance

本文与 ML 可靠性和运行时验证的研究兴趣有间接关联。虽然聚焦于量子而非经典 ML 管道，但其"行为指纹 + 容差合约"的形式化验证方法论、silent drift 检测思路和有限样本复杂度分析对经典 ML 训练/推理可靠性监测具有概念上的启发性。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can the behavioral fingerprinting approach be adapted to classical ML pipelines (e.g., verifying that a deployed transformer is the declared model, not a quantized or backdoored substitute) using intermediate activation statistics as the "observable family"?
2. How does the tolerance interval [d_drift, δ_adv/C] change over multi-day calibration cycles on current NISQ hardware, and is the interval consistently non-empty across backends?
3. Could classical shadow tomography reduce the O(k) scaling in the sample-complexity bound for structured observable families without compromising detection guarantees?
4. What is the tight information-theoretic lower bound on shot budget for detection, and how much of the current O(k log k/γ²) upper bound is intrinsic vs. an artifact of Hoeffding + union bound?
5. How does the framework extend to distributed quantum computing settings where the pipeline spans multiple QPUs with heterogeneous noise profiles?
