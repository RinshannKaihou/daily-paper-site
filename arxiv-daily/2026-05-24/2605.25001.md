> **一句话总结：** 本文从损失景观和优化动力学角度系统分析了PINN训练中的梯度病理问题，提出CAML方法——通过将零阶项重构为对齐约束并引入延迟因子，从根本上缓解PDE残差与边界条件之间的梯度冲突，在多种PDE和架构上显著提升了收敛速度和精度。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proves that PINN gradient pathology stems from a loss valley induced by the non-uniqueness of the PDE residual minimizer, and proposes CAML (Constraint-Aligned loss with Manifold Lifting) that reformulates zeroth-order terms as aligned constraints with a delay factor, achieving substantially faster convergence and higher accuracy across multiple PDE benchmarks and architectures.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示了PINN梯度冲突的几何根源**：严格证明了PDE残差损失在函数空间中存在连通的全局极小值流形（loss valley），该流形映射到参数空间后形成高度扭曲的非凸流形，导致PDE残差梯度与边界条件梯度方向冲突。
- **提出对齐约束（Aligned Constraints）**：将所有零阶项重构为带可加常数偏移的对齐约束，扩大了PDE与边界条件可行解集的交集，从根本上减少梯度不一致。线性情形有闭式解，非线性情形用迭代法求解。
- **引入延迟因子（Delay Factor）**：通过分段线性调度函数延迟残差损失的引入，使优化器在早期先沿边界条件可行子空间移动，避开高曲率区域，缩短优化路径。

## Key Contributions

- **Formal proof of the loss valley structure**: Theorem 4.1 proves that when the PDE operator does not uniquely determine a solution, the PDE residual loss admits a connected manifold of global minimizers in function space, which maps to a highly distorted non-convex manifold in parameter space.
- **Aligned constraint formulation (CAML)**: Introduces a learnable additive constant offset $c$ for all zeroth-order terms in both the PDE and boundary conditions, converting the problem into a controlled ill-posed formulation that enlarges the intersection between admissible solution sets.
- **Delay factor for residual loss**: A time-dependent gating function $\lambda(t)$ with a piecewise linear schedule delays the PDE residual loss, allowing the optimizer to first approach the boundary-admissible affine subspace before entering the loss valley.
- **Published at ICML 2026** (Seoul, South Korea).

## Methodology

The paper develops a three-part methodology:

**1. Theoretical Analysis (Loss Landscape + Optimization Dynamics):**
- Analyzes the PDE residual loss landscape in both function space and parameter space simultaneously, showing that operator non-uniqueness creates a "loss valley" of zero-residual solutions.
- Identifies a two-phase optimization dynamic: Phase I where PDE residual gradients dominate and drive parameters into the valley, and Phase II where boundary-condition gradients attempt to guide the optimizer along the valley floor but are often thwarted by high curvature and gradient conflict (Lemma 4.2).
- Validates the loss valley hypothesis via Hessian spectral analysis, showing condition numbers exceeding $10^{22}$ in parameter space and infinite condition numbers in function space.

**2. Aligned Constraints:**
- For a PDE $Z[u_\theta] + D[u_\theta] = f$, introduces additive constant $c$ into zeroth-order terms: $\bar{r}_i = Z[u_\theta + c] + D[u_\theta] - f$ and $\bar{s}_b = \alpha_b(u_\theta + c) + \beta_b \nabla u_\theta \cdot n_b - g$.
- The optimal $c$ is solved via a 1D auxiliary sub-optimization at each training step. For linear zeroth-order terms, a closed-form solution exists (Eq. 15); for nonlinear terms, Newton's method or gradient descent is used.
- $u_\theta$ is detached from $c$ to prevent gradient propagation through the offset.

**3. Delay Factor:**
- Uses a piecewise linear schedule $\lambda(t)$: $\lambda=0$ for $t < t_d$, ramps linearly to 1 over a duration $t_r$, then stays at 1.
- The total loss becomes $L(\theta; t) = w_{res} \lambda(t) L_{res}^{alg} + w_{bc} L_{bc}^{alg}$.

## Results & Highlights

- Evaluated on four PDE problems: **Heat equation**, **Poisson equation**, **Navier-Stokes** (steady-state, nonlinear), and **Helmholtz** (reaction-diffusion, complex geometry, high frequency).
- Tested across three backbone architectures: **MLP**, **PirateNets**, and **PINNsFormer**.
- Compared against five baselines: classic PINN, $L_\infty$-PINN, SA-PINN, BRDR, and DB-PINN.
- **Convergence speed**: CAML consistently achieves the lowest step count (Stp) to reach the accuracy threshold — e.g., 201 steps on Helmholtz/MLP vs. 330 for the next best (BRDR).
- **Accuracy**: CAML achieves the best relative $L_2$ error in 10 out of 12 experimental configurations, often by an order of magnitude — e.g., $1.56 \times 10^{-4}$ on Helmholtz/PirateNets vs. $5.69 \times 10^{-3}$ for DB-PINN.
- On Navier-Stokes (nonlinear), where many baselines fail to converge, CAML still reaches reliable solutions with $L_2$ errors of $3.24 \times 10^{-2}$ (PirateNets) and $2.79 \times 10^{-2}$ (PINNsFormer).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High)**: The loss valley perspective and the aligned constraint reformulation are genuinely novel. Unlike prior adaptive weighting methods that only balance gradient magnitudes, CAML directly modifies the optimization landscape geometry.
- **Rigor (High)**: Includes formal theorems with proofs (in appendices), Hessian-based empirical validation, and comprehensive experiments with 5 random seeds.
- **Reproducibility (High)**: Open-source code, clear algorithm description, standard benchmarks, and detailed hyperparameters in appendices.
- **Significance (High)**: Addresses a fundamental and well-known bottleneck in PINN training. The theoretical insight (loss valley from operator non-uniqueness) is broadly applicable beyond the specific method.
- **Potential concerns**: The aligned constraint formulation relies on the existence of zeroth-order terms; pure derivative-based PDEs without zeroth-order components may require adaptation. The delay schedule introduces two hyperparameters ($t_d$, $t_r$) that may need tuning per problem.

## Strengths

- Provides the first rigorous geometric characterization of why PINN gradient pathology occurs, connecting function-space operator non-uniqueness to parameter-space loss landscapes.
- The aligned constraint idea is elegant — by introducing a single scalar $c$, the admissible solution set is substantially enlarged without changing the network architecture.
- Consistent improvements across 4 PDE types, 3 architectures, and against 5 strong baselines demonstrate robustness.
- The method is architecture-agnostic and can be combined with other PINN improvements.

## Limitations

- The aligned constraint formulation requires zeroth-order terms in the PDE or boundary conditions; problems governed purely by higher-order derivatives may not benefit from the additive constant trick.
- The delay factor introduces problem-specific hyperparameters ($t_d$, $t_r$) whose optimal values are not derived analytically.
- Experiments are limited to 2D steady-state problems; scaling to 3D or time-dependent problems is not demonstrated.
- The auxiliary optimization for $c$ in the nonlinear case adds per-step computational overhead that is not benchmarked.

## Relevance

本文对PINN训练中梯度病理的理论分析和CAML方法对科学计算深度学习领域有直接参考价值，特别是对需要用PINN求解复杂PDE（流体力学、电磁学、热传导等）的研究者而言，提供了一种从根本上改善优化景观的新思路。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does CAML scale to time-dependent PDEs (e.g., parabolic or hyperbolic equations) where the loss valley structure may differ?
2. Can the aligned constraint idea be extended to higher-order invariances (e.g., multiplicative or affine invariances) beyond additive constants?
3. What is the computational overhead of the auxiliary $c$ optimization per training step, especially for nonlinear PDEs with iterative solvers?
4. How does CAML interact with advanced PINN architectures like Fourier Neural Operators or DeepONet?
5. Is there a theoretical bound on the improvement in condition number achieved by the aligned constraint reformulation?
