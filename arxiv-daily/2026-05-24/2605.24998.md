> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 HSIR 框架，通过 VeriExit 策略复用失败解中的部分正确推理步骤来缓解数据不平衡，并设计 InDiv 指标利用模型隐藏状态的语义熵来量化并过滤"过度思考"的冗余推理，在多种后训练范式下实现了最高 +10.9% 的性能提升和 42.4% 的推理开销降低。

> **TL;DR:** The paper identifies data imbalance and overthinking as two key failure modes in self-improvement training for large reasoning models. It proposes HSIR, combining a VeriExit sampling strategy (reuses partial correct reasoning from failed solutions) and an InDiv metric (quantifies overthinking via hidden-state semantic entropy), achieving up to +10.9% accuracy gains and 42.4% inference cost reduction across SFT, DPO, and GRPO paradigms.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示自改进训练的两个关键问题**：通过系统分析发现，现有自改进方法在复杂推理任务中存在数据不平衡（简单样本过多、困难样本稀缺）和过度思考（冗余推理步骤被用于自训练）的问题，甚至可能导致模型崩溃。
- **提出 VeriExit 采样策略**：对于模型无法完全解决的困难样本，检查其失败解中的中间推理步骤是否已到达正确答案，若是则从该步骤截断并重新采样，从而高效回收更多正确的推理轨迹。
- **设计 InDiv（Intrinsic Diversity）指标**：利用模型中间层的隐藏表示计算语义熵，结合注意力感知加权机制来量化推理步骤的多样性，低分表示过度思考和冗余推理，用于过滤不合格的训练样本。
- **提出 H-GRPO 算法**：将 InDiv 分数作为额外奖励信号融入 GRPO 强化学习框架，鼓励模型生成简洁多样的推理路径，而非简单惩罚输出长度。
- **广泛的实验验证**：在 6 种不同架构的模型和 9 个推理基准上验证了 HSIR 的有效性，证明了方法的通用性和优越性。

## Key Contributions

- **Identifies two critical bottlenecks in self-improvement training**: data imbalance (easy samples dominate, hard samples are scarce) and overthinking (redundant reasoning steps contaminate self-training data), which can even lead to model collapse.
- **Proposes VeriExit sampling strategy**: Reuses partial correct reasoning steps from previously failed solutions by detecting intermediate steps that arrive at the correct answer, then resampling from that checkpoint—recovering correct solutions for hard queries at no extra full-pass cost.
- **Designs InDiv (Intrinsic Diversity) metric**: Leverages hidden representations from the model's middle layer to compute semantic entropy via singular values of the cross-covariance matrix, enhanced with an attention-aware weighting mechanism, to quantify and filter overthinking solutions.
- **Introduces H-GRPO**: An enhanced GRPO algorithm that incorporates normalized InDiv scores as an additional reward signal (weight ω=0.2), encouraging concise and diverse reasoning via reinforcement learning rather than naive length penalties.
- **Comprehensive empirical validation**: Demonstrates effectiveness across 6 model architectures (Qwen2.5-1.5B/3B/7B, Qwen3-1.7B, Phi-3.5-mini, Mistral-7B, LLaMA3-8B) and 9 reasoning benchmarks, with consistent improvements in both accuracy and efficiency.

## Methodology

The HSIR framework operates in an iterative self-improvement loop with three key stages per iteration:

**1. Self-generation:** The current model generates K=10 candidate solutions for each training query. Solutions are split into correct and incorrect groups based on answer verification.

**2. VeriExit sampling (addresses data imbalance):** For each failed solution, the method scans its reasoning steps to check whether any intermediate step explicitly mentions the ground-truth answer (via text matching). If found, the reasoning prefix up to that step is used as a new prompt, and J=5 additional solutions are resampled from that checkpoint. Correct resampled solutions are merged into the training pool. This reuses the "almost correct" reasoning from failed attempts without requiring additional full-pass generation.

**3. InDiv filtering (addresses overthinking):** For each correct solution, InDiv scores are computed by: (a) extracting hidden representations H from the model's middle layer; (b) computing the cross-covariance matrix Σ = H·J_d·H^T where J_d is the centering matrix; (c) using attention-aware weighting to compute a diversity score based on log singular values of Σ. Solutions with InDiv scores below threshold τ=-0.5 are filtered out. The intuition is that repetitive reasoning steps produce highly correlated hidden representations with low semantic entropy.

The filtered data is then used for either SFT or DPO training. For H-GRPO, the normalized InDiv score is added as an extra reward component (accuracy + format + ω·InDiv), where the accuracy reward weight (1.0) dominates InDiv weight (0.2), inherently preventing reward hacking.

## Results & Highlights

- **Performance gains:** HSIR brings up to +10.9% average accuracy improvement across all evaluated settings (HSIR-DPO on Qwen2.5-7B reaches 73.35% overall accuracy vs. 62.45% baseline).
- **Efficiency gains:** Reduces inference overhead by up to 42.4% (Qwen2.5-7B with HSIR-SFT iteration 3: 885 avg tokens vs. 1,536 baseline).
- **HSIR-DPO outperforms IRPO by +5.35%** average performance gains across Qwen2.5 models.
- **H-GRPO outperforms vanilla GRPO, Long2Short, and CosFn** on all model sizes, improving both accuracy and efficiency simultaneously (whereas length-penalty methods sacrifice accuracy for brevity).
- **Combining HSIR-SFT + H-GRPO** yields further improvements (e.g., Qwen2.5-3B on GSM8K: 87.11% vs. 86.13% HSIR-SFT alone or 83.09% H-GRPO alone).
- **Strong OOD generalization:** HSIR-DPO improves cross-domain performance from 23.66% to 34.60% on MedQA→GPQA, while IRPO degrades from 23.66% to 22.54%.
- **Ablation confirms both components are essential:** Removing either VeriExit or InDiv degrades performance; both outperform their alternative counterparts (answer-driven sampling, length-driven filtering).
- **Robustness:** Works with seed data from DeepSeek-R1 or QWQ-32B, and remains effective even with self-consistency pseudo-labels when ground-truth answers are unavailable (+6.14% avg gain).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** The InDiv metric is a genuinely novel contribution—one of the first to use internal hidden states of LRMs to guide concise reasoning during self-improvement. VeriExit's idea of reusing partial correct reasoning from failed solutions is elegant and practical.
- **Rigor (High):** Extensive experiments across multiple model sizes, architectures, tasks, and training paradigms. Ablation studies, parameter analyses, and OOD evaluations strengthen the claims. The theoretical connection between InDiv and semantic entropy is well-motivated.
- **Reproducibility (High):** All models are publicly available, training uses standard toolkits (LLaMA-Factory, open-r1), and key hyperparameters are clearly specified. The method uses only stdlib+standard frameworks.
- **Significance (High):** Addresses two fundamental and widely recognized problems in self-improvement training. The dual improvement in both accuracy and efficiency is rare and valuable.
- **Red flags:** The GRPO experiments are limited to 300 steps due to cost, which may not reflect full convergence. The text-matching VeriExit is a simple heuristic (though the paper validates it correlates well with NLI-based and prompt-based alternatives). The claim that InDiv correlates with semantic entropy is empirically shown but lacks formal proof.

## Strengths

- Simultaneously improves reasoning accuracy and efficiency—rare in the literature where these are typically traded off.
- Two simple, orthogonal mechanisms (VeriExit for data quantity, InDiv for data quality) that compose well.
- Universally effective across 6 model architectures and 9 benchmarks without architecture-specific tuning.
- InDiv uses model's own internal states as a free signal—no external models or annotations needed.
- H-GRPO's reward design inherently prevents reward hacking via the dominance of accuracy reward.
- Comprehensive analysis including OOD generalization, different seed data sources, and pseudo-label settings.

## Limitations

- InDiv assumes semantically similar adjacent steps indicate redundancy, which may not hold for rigorous mathematical derivations where consecutive steps can be semantically similar yet logically essential.
- VeriExit is inherently limited for "absolutely hard queries" where no valid intermediate steps exist—these lie beyond the model's current capability boundary.
- Combining HSIR-SFT with H-GRPO incurs significant additional computational overhead; more efficient integration strategies remain unexplored.
- GRPO experiments are under-trained (300 steps only), so the full potential of H-GRPO is unclear.
- The filtering threshold τ requires tuning (best at -0.5 in experiments), and performance is sensitive to this choice.

## Relevance

本文直接针对大模型自改进训练中的数据质量和推理效率问题，提出的 InDiv 指标和 VeriExit 策略对构建更高效的推理模型训练流水线具有实际参考价值，尤其是在需要平衡推理精度与推理成本的场景下。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does InDiv perform on non-reasoning tasks (e.g., creative writing, open-ended dialogue) where "overthinking" may be harder to define?
2. Can VeriExit be combined with process reward models (PRMs) to provide more fine-grained step-level verification rather than text matching?
3. What happens to InDiv scores as models are trained for many more GRPO steps—do they converge, or does the signal degrade over training?
4. How does HSIR scale to models larger than 7B parameters (e.g., 32B, 72B)?
5. Could InDiv be used as an inference-time signal for dynamic early exiting, not just for training data filtering?
