> **一句话总结：** 本文提出基于重复子序列分布与高阶Rényi熵的分析框架，通过对数-幂律模型拟合发现自然语言具有稳定的熵增长模式，而GPT-3.5到GPT-5的生成文本随模型规模增大呈现显著的指数系统性偏移，揭示了LLM在长程结构复用能力上与自然语言的本质差距。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes a repeated-subsequence evaluation framework grounded in higher-order Rényi entropies to probe long-range text structure. Across 100 texts per condition, natural language shows stable entropy-growth exponents (β, γ) regardless of text length, while GPT-generated texts (gpt-3.5turbo through gpt-5) exhibit statistically significant monotonic increases in these exponents with model size — revealing persistent structural gaps beyond surface fluency.

## 核心贡献

- 提出基于重复子序列计数（Dm）分布的分析框架，用于量化文本中长程结构的复用模式。
- 将重复子序列分布与高阶Rényi熵Hα(m)联系起来，在有限长度条件下通过幂律（β）和对数-幂律（γ）两种模型刻画熵增长行为。
- 设计两阶段估计程序：先从Dm/Tm估计λm，再通过∆α校正拟合log₂Sm，避免直接估计的不稳定性。
- 实验覆盖gpt-3.5turbo、gpt-4o-mini、gpt-5-mini、gpt-5四代模型及对应的Project Gutenberg匹配文本，每组100篇。
- 发现对数-幂律模型在大多数情况下优于纯幂律模型（R² > 0.97），表明自然语言熵增长更接近强结构复用机制。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a distributional formulation of repetition via Dm (count of repeated blocks of length m) and relates it to higher-order Rényi entropies Hα(m) for α = 2, 3, 4.
- Develops a two-stage estimation procedure that separates finite-size corrections (∆α via Touchard polynomials) from the leading entropy-growth term log₂Sm, yielding stable exponent estimates.
- Compares power-law (log₂Sm ∝ m^β) vs. logarithmic–power (log₂Sm ∝ (log₂m)^γ) models; the latter consistently achieves R² > 0.97, outperforming power-law fits (R² 0.90–0.96) on natural language.
- Demonstrates that natural language exponents (β, γ) remain stable across different text lengths (35K–600K chars), exhibiting "weak universality," while GPT-generated text shows monotonic exponent increases with model scale.
- Provides Welch t-test confirmation: gpt-5 vs. nl-5 and gpt-5-mini vs. nl-5-mini yield p ≈ 0 for both β and γ; natural language cross-length comparisons are not significant (p = 0.12, 0.94).
- Shows that the proposed distribution-based method is more sensitive than maximal-repetition-based diagnostics (m^max ∝ (log₂n)^η), which fail to distinguish gpt-5 from natural language.

## Methodology

- **Repeated subsequence counting:** For a sequence of length n, defines Dm = Tm − Km, where Tm = n − m + 1 is total blocks of length m and Km is the number of distinct block types.
- **Rényi entropy estimation:** Computes empirical Ĥα(m) using only blocks with frequency #w ≥ α, estimating block probabilities by empirical frequency p̂w = #w/Tm.
- **Finite-size correction:** Derives Hα(m) ≈ log₂Sm − ∆α, where ∆α involves weighted logarithms of Touchard polynomials dependent on λm = Tm/Sm.
- **Two-stage fitting:** (1) Estimate λm from its functional relation with Dm/Tm; (2) Fit log₂Sm = Ĥα(m) + ∆α to power-law and log-power models.
- **Datasets:** 100 texts each for gpt-3.5turbo (~35K chars), gpt-4o-mini (~111K), gpt-5-mini (~347K), gpt-5 (~601K), plus length-matched Project Gutenberg natural language texts.
- **Baseline comparison:** Replicates maximal repetition analysis (m^max growth exponent η) following Dębowski (2015), finding the proposed method yields more pronounced and stable distinctions.

## Results & Highlights

- **Log-power superiority:** For The Hermit of Far End, log-power fits achieve R² > 0.97 vs. power-law R² 0.90–0.96. Across all datasets, average R² exceeds 0.87, typically near 0.95.
- **Stable natural language exponents:** Mean β and γ for natural language remain approximately constant across four length-matched datasets, despite substantial inter-text variability — a "weak universality" at the population level.
- **Monotonic GPT exponent growth:** Both β and γ increase monotonically from gpt-3.5turbo → gpt-4o-mini → gpt-5-mini → gpt-5. For gpt-5 and gpt-5-mini, both exponents are substantially larger than for natural language.
- **GPT homogeneity vs. NL variability:** GPT-generated texts are more homogeneous (tighter boxplots) but systematically shifted; natural language shows wider variance but stable means.
- **Rényi order dependence:** β increases with α (frequent blocks show stronger structural growth), while γ decreases with α (rare patterns more governed by structural reuse) — indicating heterogeneous entropy growth across the block distribution.
- **Model preference transition:** Shorter natural language texts favor power-law fits (new information introduction); longer texts shift toward log-power (structural reuse dominance). GPT model preference varies by model size, with smaller models strongly preferring log-power.
- **Maximal repetition less discriminative:** The exponent η for gpt-5 approaches natural language values (η ≈ 2+ for individual texts, refining earlier η ≈ 3 estimates from aggregated corpora), whereas the proposed exponents β, γ remain clearly separated.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The distributional analysis of repeated subsequences linked to Rényi entropy for LLM evaluation is original. Prior work on maximal repetition (Dębowski, 2015) and entropy scaling (Hilberg, 1990) existed, but the systematic application to comparing LLM outputs across model generations is new.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Length-matched controls, Welch t-tests with p ≈ 0, two-stage estimation with explicit finite-size corrections, and comparison against a baseline method (maximal repetition). The mathematical derivations (Touchard polynomial corrections, Poisson occupancy model) are rigorous. The restriction to blocks with #w ≥ α is well-motivated both conceptually and empirically.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. The mathematical framework is precisely defined. Project Gutenberg texts are public. GPT generation settings are referenced (Supplementary F). However, exact GPT outputs and prompts are needed for full replication, and the two-stage estimation procedure requires careful implementation.
- **Significance:** Potentially high. The finding that exponents increase monotonically with model size — rather than converging toward natural language — is a striking and counterintuitive result. It suggests that making LLMs larger does not bring them closer to natural language in terms of long-range structure.
- **Red flags:** (1) Only GPT-family models tested — no open-source LLMs (Llama, Mistral), no non-Transformer architectures. (2) Character-level analysis may miss token/word-level structural differences. (3) The paper is descriptive: it identifies differences but does not propose mechanisms or remedies. (4) The "weak universality" claim for natural language rests on novels only; non-narrative texts may behave differently (the author acknowledges this and provides supplementary experiments).

## Strengths

- Rigorous information-theoretic grounding with explicit finite-size corrections — not just empirical curve fitting.
- Clear, counterintuitive finding: larger models diverge further from natural language in entropy-growth structure.
- Comprehensive experimental design: four GPT model generations, length-matched controls, 100 texts per condition, statistical significance tests.
- Honest comparison showing the proposed method outperforms the established maximal-repetition baseline.
- The log-power vs. power-law distinction and its interpretation via structural reuse (Kolmogorov complexity analogy) is conceptually illuminating.

## Limitations

- Limited to GPT-family models; generalization to other architectures (open-source, non-Transformer) is unknown.
- Character-level analysis avoids tokenization artifacts but does not capture higher-level linguistic units.
- The analysis is intra-text only — does not address cross-text or population-level statistics beyond the sample-level means.
- Does not resolve whether a linear component (entropy rate hα) exists in entropy growth; focuses only on sublinear behavior.
- Descriptive rather than mechanistic: does not identify which model properties (architecture, training objective, context window) drive the observed exponent shifts.
- The relationship between entropy-growth exponents and downstream task performance or text quality is not explored.
- As acknowledged by the author: analysis covers empirically accessible block lengths; behavior at much larger scales remains unknown.

## Relevance

本文从信息论角度为LLM长程结构评估提供了定量诊断工具，发现模型规模增大并未使生成文本更接近自然语言的熵增长特性，这一反直觉结论对理解LLM生成质量瓶颈和改进方向具有重要启示，与LLM评估、文本生成和语言建模研究直接相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do open-source models (Llama 3, Mistral, DeepSeek) exhibit the same monotonic exponent increase with model scale, or does the trend differ across architectures?
- Would training with explicit structural objectives (e.g., repetition-aware losses, hierarchical generation) shift the entropy-growth exponents closer to natural language?
- How does the choice of decoding strategy (temperature, top-p, top-k) affect the estimated β and γ — and can strategic decoding partially close the gap?
- Does the observed "weak universality" of natural language exponents hold across non-narrative genres (scientific papers, legal documents, conversational transcripts)?
- Can the Rényi-order-dependent patterns (β increasing vs. γ decreasing with α) be linked to specific linguistic phenomena (e.g., syntactic vs. discourse-level structure)?
- Would extending the analysis to subword or word levels reveal different structural patterns than character-level analysis?
