Label-NTK Alignments and A Tighter Convergence Bound in the NTK Regime

Discovering label-residual alignments with the NTK eigenspectrum yields convergence bounds that closely match empirical training dynamics.

Ruchirinkil Marreddy, Chaoyue Liu | Purdue University | 2026-05-24
neural-tangent-kernel convergence-theory over-parameterization generalization-bounds deep-learning-theory
Key Metric
O(tr[K]/n)
Generalization bound improved from O(√(yTK−1y/n)), removing λmin dependence entirely.
Why This Matters
Resolves the most embarrassing gap in NTK theory — overly pessimistic convergence predictions — via principled spectral alignment.
Critical Edge
Residual-NTK alignment is assumed but not formally proven, and the theory is confined to the infinite-width NTK regime.

Methodology

Step 1 — Empirical discovery. Compute NTK eigendecomposition K = ∑i λiviviT, plot (viTy)² vs λi and (viTr)² vs λi on log-log scales. Clean linear relationships emerge with slopes ≈ 2 and ≈ 1 across 5 datasets and 2 architectures.

Step 2 — Theoretical justification. Under unit-norm inputs, non-degenerate data, and Lipschitz ground-truth labels, proves exponent-2 scaling for two-point (Thm 3.2) and n-point (Thm 3.4) cases.

Step 3 — Refined convergence. Formalizes Residual-NTK alignment as Condition 4.2: (c−δ)λi ≤ (viTr0)² ≤ (c+δ)λi. Proves spectrum-aware bound L(wt) ≈ tr[(I−ηK)2tK] with matching upper/lower bounds (Thm 4.5).

Step 4 — Generalization. Plugs Label-NTK alignment into Arora et al. (2019) framework: yTK−1y = c·tr[K], yielding tighter O(tr[K]/n) bound (Thm 5.2).

Results & Highlights

  • New bound tr[(I−ηK)2tK] closely matches empirical loss curves; classical exp(−ηλmint) stays nearly flat.
  • Alignment phenomena hold consistently across 5 datasets (MNIST, Fashion-MNIST, SVHN, CIFAR-10, Tiny-ImageNet10) and 2 architectures.
  • Alignments persist throughout training even at finite width, suggesting robust structural property.
  • Under power-law decay λi ∼ i−q, iteration complexity to reach ε is significantly tighter when ε ≫ λmin.
  • Generalization bound O(tr[K]/n) is independent of λmin.

Strengths

  • Directly fixes the most salient weakness of NTK optimization theory with a principled solution.
  • Alignment framework is conceptually clean: labels concentrate on high-similarity directions because similar inputs have similar labels (Lipschitz).
  • Comprehensive validation across 5 datasets × 2 architectures.
  • Matching upper and lower bounds (Thm 4.5) suggest tightness, not just improvement.
  • Generalization improvement comes almost for free once Label-NTK alignment is established.

Limitations

  • NTK regime only — excludes feature learning, edge of stability, and catapult effects.
  • Residual alignment assumed — Condition 4.2 is empirically supported but lacks formal proof.
  • Spectrum-wide proof incomplete — Thms 3.2/3.4 cover specific eigenvalues from close data pairs, not the full uniform alignment experiments show.
  • Width still depends on λmin — convergence rate improved but required width m = ˜Ω(λmin−2) does not.
  • Classification gap — Lipschitz assumption is natural for regression but needs margin assumptions for classification.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can Residual-NTK alignment (exponent 1) be formally proven under the same mild assumptions as Label-NTK alignment?
  2. Do the alignment phenomena persist for Transformers/ViTs where NTK structure differs significantly?
  3. Can alignment insights design adaptive learning rate schedules for further acceleration?
  4. Is there a connection to feature learning dynamics in the finite-width, large-learning-rate regime?
  5. Can the spectrum-aware analysis extend to SGD, where mini-batch noise interacts with alignment structure?
  6. Does alignment degrade systematically with dataset complexity?
