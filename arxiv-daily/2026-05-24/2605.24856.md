> **一句话总结：** 提出Concept Allocation Zone (CAZ)框架，通过Separation、Concept Coherence、Concept Velocity三个逐层指标追踪Transformer残差流中概念形成的深度区间，在34个模型（8个架构家族）上验证发现分离曲线常呈多模态分布，揭示标准峰值检测无法发现但消融实验证明因果有效的"温和CAZ"，并发现跨架构概念对齐是按处理深度匹配而非整体对齐的。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces the Concept Allocation Zone (CAZ) — a framework for identifying depth intervals in the transformer residual stream where concepts become geometrically separable. Using three layer-wise metrics (Separation via Fisher-normalized centroid distance, Concept Coherence via PCA explained variance, Concept Velocity via smoothed finite differences), the author shows concept formation is depth-extended across 34 models from 8 architectural families. A scored detector reveals "gentle CAZes" (score < 0.05) invisible to standard peak detection but causally active under ablation in 93-100% of cases. The strongest result (P5): cross-architecture concept alignment is depth-matched (563/563 positive trials, Δ=+0.154, bootstrap 95% CI [0.147, 0.162]), confirming a depth-stratified version of the Platonic Representation Hypothesis.

## 核心贡献

- 提出Concept Allocation Zone (CAZ)框架：将概念形成从单层"最佳层"快照扩展为跨深度的动态过程追踪，CAZ是模型深度中分配给概念几何表达的区间
- 定义四个逐层度量指标：Separation S(l)（Fisher归一化类间距）、Concept Coherence C(l)（PCA主成分方差比）、Concept Velocity v(l)（分离度的平滑变化率）、Directional Stability DS(l)（层间方向余弦相似度）
- 开发评分式CAZ检测器：基于prominence×coherence boost×width的复合评分替代固定阈值，发现标准检测遗漏的"温和CAZ"（93-100%消融因果有效性）
- 发现分离曲线S(l)常呈多模态：单一概念在单模型中平均参与3.4个CAZ，浅层与深层子表示的余弦相似度仅0.156-0.433，表明同一人类概念标签映射到多个几何上不同的子表示
- 验证跨架构深度匹配对齐（P5）：在122个同维度模型对×7概念上，深度匹配对齐始终优于错位对齐（563/563正试验）
- 提出7个可证伪预测，4个给出明确裁决（2个不支持、1个部分支持、1个支持），展现了诚实的科学评估态度

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the CAZ framework reframing concept formation as a depth-extended process: CAZes are depth-localized regions where the model organizes geometry to serve concepts, not the concepts themselves
- Formalizes four layer-wise metrics: Separation S(l) (Fisher-normalized centroid distance), Concept Coherence C(l) (first-PC explained variance ratio), Concept Velocity v(l) (smoothed rate of change of S), and Directional Stability DS(l) (layer-to-layer cosine similarity of dominant direction)
- Develops a scored CAZ detector with composite scoring (prominence × coherence boost × width) that uncovers "gentle CAZes" (score < 0.05) — subtle allocation regions invisible to standard peak detection but causally active in 93-100% of ablation cases
- Demonstrates multimodal separation curves are common (mean 3.4 CAZes per concept per model), with geometrically distinct sub-representations at shallow vs. deep peaks (cosine 0.156-0.433)
- Confirms cross-architecture depth-stratified alignment (P5): depth-matched alignment exceeds mismatched in all 563/563 valid trials across 122 same-dimension model pairs
- Generates and evaluates 7 falsifiable predictions with transparent reporting of negative results; introduces the CAZ-is-not-a-concept distinction and two-regime encoding structure (redundant vs. sparse)

## Methodology

The CAZ framework formalizes concept allocation in transformers through four layer-wise metrics computed on the residual stream:

**Separation S(l):** Fisher-normalized centroid distance between contrastive classes at each layer: `S(l) = ||h̄_A - h̄_B|| / sqrt(0.5 * (tr(Σ_A) + tr(Σ_B)))`. Normalizing by within-class spread corrects for the fact that early layers tend toward diffuse, high-variance representations.

**Concept Coherence C(l):** Explained variance ratio of the first principal component of the pooled (both-class) activation matrix at layer l. High C(l) means the representation is geometrically concentrated on a single direction; most interpretable in combination with S(l).

**Concept Velocity v(l):** Smoothed finite-difference slope of S(l) across a window of half-width k = max(1, ⌊L/24⌋): `v(l) = (S(l+k) - S(l-k-1)) / (2k+1)`. Positive velocity means the concept is actively being constructed; negative means degradation or reallocation.

**Directional Stability DS(l):** Cosine similarity between the normalized dominant separating direction at consecutive layers. Near 1.0 indicates stable encoding; sharp drops mark "handoffs" where the geometric basis shifts.

**CAZ detection** operates in two modes: (1) single-region velocity-based detection (entry/peak/exit from velocity thresholds) for unimodal curves; (2) scored multi-region detection using prominence-based peak finding with a composite score = (prominence/S̄) × (1 + √(C(l_peak)/C̄)) × √(width/L), with a 0.5% prominence floor.

**Concept extraction** compares three methods: Delta PCA (on layer-to-layer residuals within CAZ), Windowed PCA (on raw activations across CAZ), and single-layer DoM baseline. The companion GEM paper [Henry 2026b] validates handoff-layer extraction, finding it preferable to peak-layer extraction in 259/391 trials (66.2%), with a strong architecture-cohort effect: MHA models prefer handoff (11/13) while GQA models generally do not (2/7).

**Experimental setup:** 34 models from 8 families (Pythia, GPT-2, OPT, Qwen 2.5, Gemma 2, Llama 3.2, Mistral, Phi), spanning 70M-12B parameters, 7 concepts (credibility, certainty, causation, temporal_order, sentiment, negation, moral_valence), 250 contrastive pairs per concept, final-token probing.

## Results & Highlights

- Separation curves S(l) are frequently multimodal: a single concept typically participates in 3.4 CAZes per model on average
- Gentle CAZes (score < 0.05) are causally active in 93-100% of ablation cases across 16 models with full ablation coverage; 26 base models confirmed in companion paper
- Sub-representations at shallow vs. deep peaks are geometrically distinct: cosine similarity 0.156-0.433 between peak dom_vectors, with block-diagonal cosine similarity structure and sharp dips at saddle points (as low as 0.35)
- P5 (depth-stratified alignment): 563/563 valid trials show depth-matched alignment exceeding mismatched (matched mean 0.353 vs. mismatched 0.199, Δ=+0.154, bootstrap 95% CI [0.147, 0.162])
- Two-regime encoding structure discovered from P1 failure: redundant encoding (Pythia, GPT-2, OPT) where concept direction persists post-allocation vs. sparse encoding (Qwen, Gemma) where CAZ location matters more
- MHA models prefer handoff extraction (85%) while GQA models prefer peak extraction (71%); Fisher's exact p = 0.022
- Concept ordering is a significant tendency across architectures (26/26 base models positively correlated with consensus ordering; median τ = 0.473, p = 1.49 × 10⁻⁸) but not a stable architectural invariant (three low-τ models)
- Reference implementation: rosetta_tools v1.3.1 (doi:10.5281/zenodo.20361433); pre-extracted activations available on HuggingFace

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The CAZ-as-depth-interval framing, the scored detection method, and the discovery that multimodal separation curves are the norm (not the exception) are genuine contributions. The distinction between CAZes and concepts, and between redundant and sparse encoding strategies, adds real theoretical clarity.
- **Rigor:** Very strong. The paper pre-specifies 7 falsifiable predictions (committed 2026-04-05) and honestly reports 2 as not supported and 1 as not testable. The empirical breadth (34 models, 8 families) is unusual for interpretability work. Statistical testing includes Wilcoxon signed-rank, bootstrap CIs, Fisher's exact tests, and leave-one-concept-out cross-validation.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Open-source reference implementation with Zenodo DOI, public activations dataset on HuggingFace, and detailed metric definitions. However, the contrastive datasets were generated by Claude Sonnet 4.6 — a potential systematic bias the author acknowledges as the most significant limitation.
- **Significance:** High for mechanistic interpretability. The depth-stratified PRH result (P5) and the two-regime encoding discovery have direct implications for representation engineering, model steering, and cross-model transfer. The CAZ framework could reshape how the field approaches concept extraction and intervention.
- **Red flags:** (1) Single author with four simultaneous companion papers raises questions about the depth of independent scrutiny for each. (2) All 7 concept datasets generated by a single model (Claude Sonnet 4.6) — what's being mapped may be Claude's ontology projected onto other models, not a universal property. (3) Ablation coverage in this paper is 16/34 models (47%), though the companion paper extends to 26/26 base models. (4) Token position is fixed to the final token, but Zhao et al. (2025) show different concepts encode at different positions — this is a known gap the author acknowledges. (5) Frontier-scale models (70B+) were not evaluated.

## Strengths

- Principled, metric-driven framework replacing ad-hoc "best layer" selection with a dynamical view of concept formation
- Unusually broad empirical coverage (34 models, 8 architectural families, 7 concepts) with statistical rigor
- Pre-registered falsifiable predictions with honest reporting of negative results — a model for the field
- Scored detection method captures subtle but causally real allocation regions missed by standard methods
- Clear CAZ-is-not-a-concept distinction prevents conceptual confusion
- Open-source implementation, public datasets, and companion papers provide a complete ecosystem
- The two-regime encoding discovery (redundant vs. sparse) emerged from investigating a failed prediction — productive falsification

## Limitations

- All concept datasets generated by a single model (Claude Sonnet 4.6) — potential systematic ontology bias
- Single-token-position probing (final token only); concepts encoding at other positions are missed
- Linearity assumption: separation metric assumes linearly separable concept manifolds; curved/multi-dimensional structures (e.g., temporal concepts) may require kernel or topological metrics
- Frontier-scale models (70B+) not evaluated; whether CAZ structure holds at that scale is open
- Smoothing parameter k = ⌊L/24⌋ is a heuristic not formally validated against ground-truth concept boundaries
- Ablation covers 16/34 models in this paper (8 instruct variants have no ablation coverage)
- SAE dark matter cross-validation hypothesis is plausible but untested
- P7 (architecture vs. scale) is indeterminate due to insufficient power; scale ladder of only 7 Pythia sizes

## Relevance

该论文与机械可解释性研究高度相关，提出了一种系统化的框架来追踪Transformer中概念形成的层间动态过程。CAZ框架对表征工程（Representation Engineering）的干预深度选择、跨模型迁移和SAE暗物质分析具有直接的实际意义，对理解大语言模型内部工作机制和改进对齐训练评估具有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can CAZ profiles be used to predict which models are more susceptible to adversarial manipulation of specific concepts, and does the redundant vs. sparse encoding distinction create different vulnerability profiles?
- How do CAZ profiles change during instruction tuning, RLHF, or continued pre-training? The companion paper suggests instruction tuning effects depend on base geometry — can CAZ profiles predict alignment training outcomes a priori?
- What is the relationship between gentle CAZes and SAE dark matter? If in-progress concept construction within gentle CAZes produces transitional representations that resist linear decomposition, this would connect two currently separate research threads.
- Can the depth-stratified alignment result (P5) be exploited for zero-shot cross-model concept transfer — extracting a concept direction from one model and transferring it to another at matched processing depth?
- How sensitive are CAZ boundaries to the choice of contrastive dataset? Would human-curated contrastive pairs produce different CAZ profiles than model-generated ones?
- Do CAZ profiles differ for safety-relevant concepts (harmfulness, refusal) that encode at different token positions, and can position-varied CAZ analysis improve safety interventions?
