Concepts form across depth intervals in transformers — not at single layers — with 3.4 allocation zones per concept on average.

The Concept Allocation Zone: Tracking How Concepts Form Across Transformer Depth
James Henry · 2026-05-24 · 34 models · 8 architectures · 7 concepts
mechanistic-interpretability concept-formation transformer-geometry layer-wise-analysis representation-learning
Key Metric
563 / 563 depth-matched alignment trials positive (Δ = +0.154, 95% CI [0.147, 0.162])
Why This Matters
Directly informs representation engineering intervention depth, cross-model concept transfer, and SAE dark matter analysis.
Critical Edge
All concept datasets generated by a single model (Claude Sonnet 4.6) — potentially mapping Claude’s ontology, not universal properties.

Methodology

Separation S(l) — Fisher-normalized centroid distance
Distance between contrastive class centroids normalized by within-class spread. Corrects for diffuse high-variance early-layer representations.
Concept Coherence C(l) — first-PC explained variance ratio
How concentrated the representation is on a single direction. Most informative when paired with Separation.
Concept Velocity v(l) — smoothed slope of S(l)
Positive = concept being constructed; negative = degradation or reallocation. Window half-width k = max(1, ⌊L/24⌋).
Directional Stability DS(l) — cosine similarity of dominant direction
Near 1.0 = stable encoding; sharp drops mark “handoffs” where the geometric basis shifts between layers.
CAZ Detection — scored multi-region
Composite score = (prominence/S̄) × (1 + √(C/C̄)) × √(width/L). Captures “gentle CAZes” (score < 0.05) invisible to standard peak detection.

34 models · Pythia, GPT-2, OPT, Qwen 2.5, Gemma 2, Llama 3.2, Mistral, Phi · 70M–12B params · 250 contrastive pairs/concept · final-token probing

Results & Highlights

3.4
Mean CAZes per concept per model. Separation curves are frequently multimodal — a single concept engages multiple depth-localized sub-representations.
93–100%
Causal effectiveness of “gentle CAZes” (score < 0.05) under ablation. Subtle but real allocation regions missed by standard detection.
0.156
Lowest cosine similarity between shallow vs. deep peak dom-vectors (range 0.156–0.433). Sub-representations are geometrically distinct, not copies.
563/563
Depth-matched alignment exceeds mismatched in every trial across 122 model pairs (Δ = +0.154). Confirms depth-stratified Platonic Representation Hypothesis (P5).
2 regimes
Redundant encoding (Pythia, GPT-2, OPT) — concept direction persists post-allocation vs. Sparse encoding (Qwen, Gemma) — CAZ location matters more. Discovered from investigating a failed prediction.
τ = 0.473
Concept ordering is a significant cross-architecture tendency (26/26 models, p = 1.49×10⁻⁸) but not a stable invariant — three models show low τ.

Assessment

Strengths
  • Principled, metric-driven framework replacing ad-hoc “best layer” selection
  • Broad empirical coverage (34 models, 8 families, 7 concepts) with statistical rigor
  • Pre-registered falsifiable predictions with honest reporting of negative results
  • CAZ-is-not-a-concept distinction prevents conceptual confusion
  • Open-source implementation (rosetta_tools v1.3.1), public activations on HuggingFace
  • Two-regime encoding discovery emerged from a failed prediction — productive falsification
Limitations
  • All concept datasets from a single model (Claude Sonnet 4.6) — systematic ontology bias risk
  • Final-token probing only; concepts encoding at other positions are missed
  • Linearity assumption may miss curved/multi-dimensional concept manifolds
  • Frontier-scale models (70B+) not evaluated
  • Smoothing parameter k = ⌊L/24⌋ is heuristic, not formally validated
  • Ablation covers 16/34 models in this paper (47%)

Open Questions

Can CAZ profiles predict adversarial vulnerability, and do redundant vs. sparse encodings create different attack surfaces?
How do CAZ profiles shift during RLHF or continued pre-training? Can base-geometry CAZ profiles predict alignment outcomes a priori?
What connects gentle CAZes to SAE dark matter? Transitional representations resisting linear decomposition would bridge two separate threads.
Can the P5 depth-matched result enable zero-shot cross-model concept transfer at matched processing depth?
Would human-curated contrastive pairs produce different CAZ profiles than model-generated ones?
Do CAZ profiles differ for safety-relevant concepts encoding at non-final token positions?
