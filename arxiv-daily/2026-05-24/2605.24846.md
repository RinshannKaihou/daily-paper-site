> **一句话总结：** 本文发现LLM中存在极少量"基石神经元"（keystone neurons），它们在多任务推理中持续高激活，移除后模型能力崩塌；仅微调这些神经元即可达到甚至超越全参数微调的效果，同时更好地保持其他能力。

> **TL;DR:** The authors identify a tiny, stable subset of "keystone neurons" that are consistently highly activated across diverse LLM tasks. Removing them collapses model behavior, while fine-tuning only these neurons matches or exceeds full-parameter fine-tuning with far fewer parameter updates and better preservation of other capabilities.

## 核心贡献

- 发现LLM中存在"基石神经元"（keystone neurons）——一小部分在多任务推理中持续高激活的神经元子集
- 证明移除这些基石神经元会导致模型行为全面崩塌，说明它们对模型功能至关重要
- 揭示基石神经元在预训练阶段即已建立，其参数值经过紧密校准，对模型能力至关重要
- 提出仅更新基石神经元的监督微调方法，在大幅减少参数修改量的同时，达到甚至超越全参数微调的效果，并更好地保持其他维度的能力

## Key Contributions

- Identify "keystone neurons" — an extremely sparse subset of neurons consistently highly activated across diverse tasks in open-weight Transformer LLMs
- Demonstrate that removing keystone neurons causes a catastrophic collapse in model behavior, confirming their critical role
- Show that keystone neurons are stable, intrinsic neuron subsets largely established during pretraining, with tightly calibrated parameters
- Propose a keystone-neuron-only supervised fine-tuning approach that achieves comparable or superior task gains to full-parameter fine-tuning while better preserving performance in other capability dimensions

## Methodology

- **Cross-task activation probing:** Analyze neuron activation patterns across multiple capability dimensions (e.g., reasoning, language understanding, knowledge) in open-weight Transformer LLMs to identify consistently highly activated neurons.
- **Sparse subset isolation:** Use activation strength across tasks to isolate an extremely sparse subset of neurons deemed "keystone neurons."
- **Ablation via removal:** Systematically remove keystone neurons and observe the resulting collapse in model behavior, validating their necessity.
- **Pretraining analysis:** Investigate when and how keystone neurons form during training, confirming they emerge primarily during pretraining.
- **Parameter sensitivity analysis:** Examine the calibration of parameters associated with keystone neurons to show their precise values are critical.
- **Targeted fine-tuning:** Develop a supervised fine-tuning method that updates only keystone neurons, comparing performance against full-parameter fine-tuning and other baselines.

## Results & Highlights

- Keystone neurons constitute an extremely sparse subset of all neurons, yet their removal causes model-wide behavioral collapse
- The keystone neuron set is stable and intrinsic — not task-specific artifacts
- Keystone neurons are largely established during pretraining, not fine-tuning
- Parameters of keystone neurons are tightly calibrated; small perturbations degrade performance
- Fine-tuning only keystone neurons achieves task performance comparable to or better than full-parameter fine-tuning
- The targeted approach significantly better preserves the model's other capability dimensions compared to full fine-tuning
- The method modifies far fewer parameters, making it a parameter-efficient alternative

## Authors & Credibility

- Authors are from reputable institutions with prior work in LLM analysis and interpretability (Xiang Wang, Tat-Seng Chua are well-known in the NLP/recsys communities at National University of Singapore)
- The work spans multiple open-weight Transformer models, suggesting generality of findings
- The combination of mechanistic analysis with a practical fine-tuning application strengthens the contribution

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The concept of "keystone neurons" as a cross-task critical subset is a meaningful contribution to mechanistic interpretability, though the broader idea of identifying important neurons has been explored (e.g., polysemantic neurons, activation steering).
- **Rigor:** The claim of "collapse" upon removal needs careful quantification — does it mean total loss of capability or significant degradation? The abstract does not specify exact metrics or how many neurons constitute the "extremely sparse" subset.
- **Reproducibility:** The method probes along cross-task activation strength "with just a few prompts," which suggests low computational overhead, but the exact prompting protocol and thresholding criteria are unclear from the abstract alone.
- **Significance:** If the fine-tuning approach genuinely matches full-parameter fine-tuning with orders-of-magnitude fewer parameters, this has significant practical implications for PEFT methods.
- **Potential red flags:** Whether keystone neurons generalize across model architectures and scales beyond the tested open-weight models remains to be verified. The risk of overfitting to the keystone neuron subset during fine-tuning in adversarial or out-of-distribution settings is not addressed in the abstract.

## Strengths

- Clear and compelling narrative — from discovery (keystone neurons exist) to mechanism (they form during pretraining) to application (targeted fine-tuning)
- Practical impact: a new parameter-efficient fine-tuning method with strong empirical results
- Addresses a fundamental question in mechanistic interpretability about which neurons matter most
- Cross-task and cross-model analysis enhances generalizability of claims

## Limitations

- Unclear how "keystone neurons" relate to or differ from prior work on critical neurons, polysemantic neurons, or attention heads identified in mechanistic interpretability literature
- The exact sparsity ratio (how few neurons are keystone neurons) is not stated in the abstract
- Generalization to proprietary/closed models cannot be verified
- The long-term stability of keystone-neuron-only fine-tuning under distribution shift or adversarial conditions is unknown

## Relevance

这项工作对理解LLM内部机制和高效微调方法都具有重要意义，基石神经元的发现为机械可解释性研究提供了新视角，其应用可直接服务于参数高效微调场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the size of the keystone neuron subset scale with model size (e.g., is it a fixed ratio or absolute number)?
- Do keystone neurons correspond to specific functional circuits (e.g., induction heads, retrieval heads) identified in prior interpretability work?
- How robust is keystone-neuron-only fine-tuning under distribution shift, adversarial attacks, or multi-turn conversation settings?
- Can keystone neurons be identified without access to model weights (e.g., via API-only probing)?
- How do keystone neurons interact with or differ from neurons targeted by existing PEFT methods like LoRA or adapter layers?
