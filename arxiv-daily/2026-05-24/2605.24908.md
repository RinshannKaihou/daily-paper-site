> **一句话总结：** 本文通过追踪30个不平衡数据集上DNN逐epoch的训练/测试损失与分类准确率，发现类别不平衡导致模型在训练初期严重欠拟合少数类、后期虽能拟合但少数类表征无法泛化（过拟合），且该效应随不平衡比增大和类间重叠加重而恶化。

> **TL;DR:** By monitoring per-class losses and accuracies across training epochs on 30 imbalanced binary datasets, this paper shows DNNs initially underfit minority class samples, then overfit them to minimize training loss — yielding non-generalizable minority representations at test time. Severity increases with imbalance ratio and class overlap; well-separated classes remain unaffected.

## 核心贡献

- 系统性地追踪了DNN在30个二分类不平衡数据集上逐epoch的多数类/少数类训练损失和准确率变化，揭示了"先欠拟合后过拟合"的少数类学习模式。
- 发现类别不平衡的严重程度同时取决于不平衡比（IR）和类间重叠程度（用轮廓系数衡量），数据集复杂度是加剧因素之一。
- 实验证明当类簇良好分离时，即使IR很高（如110:1），DNN也不受类别不平衡影响，验证了该经典结论在深度学习场景下同样成立。
- 对随机过采样（ROS）的实验表明其仅带来边际改善，且以牺牲多数类识别为代价。

## Key Contributions

- Tracks per-class training losses and accuracies across epochs for 30 binary imbalanced benchmarks (IR from 1.87 to 129.44) plus 3 balanced Iris-derived baselines, providing granular visibility into DNN learning dynamics under imbalance.
- Identifies a characteristic two-phase failure mode: (1) early epochs show minority class loss *increasing* and TPR dropping to 0% (underfitting), while the majority class is rapidly learned; (2) the overparameterized DNN eventually fits minority samples, but the resulting representations are overfitted and fail to generalize at test time.
- Demonstrates that silhouette coefficient (class separability) moderates imbalance effects — the kddcup-rootkit-imap_vs_back dataset (IR=110.25, SC=0.91) shows no minority class degradation, confirming that class overlap, not just IR, is a key factor.
- Shows minority sample size matters: datasets with more minority instances (e.g., protein_homo with 1,290 minority samples) avoid early underfitting even at high IR, though learning remains slower than for the majority class.

## Methodology

- **Datasets:** 30 binary imbalanced benchmarks from UCI and KEEL repositories, with IR ranging from 1.87 (pima) to 129.44 (abalone19). Three balanced datasets derived from Iris serve as baselines. Silhouette coefficients (SC) are reported for each dataset to quantify class overlap.
- **Architecture:** Fully-connected ReLU DNN with batch normalization and 0.5 dropout after each activation. Width fixed at 50 neurons per hidden layer; depth optimized via 5-fold CV (2–6 hidden layers), selecting the architecture with best mean AUC.
- **Training:** 80/20 train-test split; Xavier initialization; Adam optimizer (lr=0.001); cross-entropy loss; minibatch sizes from 1/32 to 1/100 of dataset size; minimum 250 epochs.
- **Evaluation:** Per-class metrics — TPR (minority) and TNR (majority) — tracked epoch-by-epoch alongside overall loss and per-class losses. Random oversampling applied as a canonical baseline for comparison.

## Results & Highlights

- On balanced datasets, both classes converge at the same rate with 100% training accuracy achieved within 20 epochs and equal generalization.
- On imbalanced datasets, the overall training loss closely mirrors the majority class loss, confirming the dominant class drives the optimization trajectory.
- Minority class loss *increases* in early epochs (TPR drops to 0%) before eventually decreasing — the model initially classifies everything as the majority class.
- Despite achieving 100% training TPR and TNR, test TPR is substantially lower than test TNR across nearly all imbalanced datasets, indicating overfitted minority representations.
- The margin between test TPR and TNR generally shrinks as IR decreases.
- Random oversampling improves minority recognition only marginally, with gains diminishing at higher IR, and always at some cost to majority class accuracy.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate. The two-phase (underfit-then-overfit) observation is a useful empirical characterization, but the paper does not propose a formal explanation (e.g., via gradient analysis or loss landscape theory) beyond intuitive reasoning. Prior work like Ye et al. (2021) has reported corroborating gradient-level findings.
- **Rigor:** The experimental setup is reasonable for an empirical study (30 datasets, hyperparameter optimization via CV), but the fixed architecture (50-neuron FC layers) may not reflect how deeper/wider or convolutional architectures behave. Training for a minimum of 250 epochs without a clear stopping criterion beyond that may introduce inconsistency.
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate. Dataset names, IR values, and SC values are tabulated, and architectural choices are described. However, the exact minibatch sizes per dataset and the 5-fold CV splits are not fully specified. Code is not mentioned as available.
- **Significance:** The paper's main value is in providing a clear, systematic empirical characterization that can inform future method design. However, the conclusions are largely confirmatory of what is known or suspected — it does not challenge existing paradigms.
- **Red flags:** The paper appears to be a conference paper with limited pages, which constrains depth. The abstract contains grammatical errors ("shows" instead of "show", "learnt" vs "learned" inconsistency). The discussion of related work positions the paper's contribution clearly but the gap between the empirical observation and actionable algorithmic insights remains wide.

## Strengths

- Systematic study across 30 datasets with a wide range of IR and SC values, enabling nuanced analysis of interaction effects.
- Per-class loss and accuracy tracking across epochs provides fine-grained insight not available from summary metrics alone.
- The inclusion of balanced baselines and the separability analysis (silhouette coefficient) adds valuable context.
- Clear identification that both IR and class overlap jointly determine the severity of imbalance effects.

## Limitations

- Restricted to binary classification and fully-connected architectures; findings may not directly transfer to CNNs, transformers, or multi-class settings.
- No theoretical analysis — the paper relies entirely on empirical observations from training curves without formal explanation of *why* the two-phase pattern emerges.
- Random oversampling is the only remediation technique evaluated; more sophisticated methods (SMOTE, cost-sensitive learning, LDAM, etc.) are not benchmarked.
- The paper does not propose any new method or mitigation strategy based on its findings.
- Small minority class sizes in some datasets (e.g., ecoli with only 7 minority samples) make it difficult to draw statistically robust conclusions.

## Relevance

本文对理解DNN在不平衡数据上的训练动态提供了系统性的实验证据，揭示了"先欠拟合后过拟合"的少数类学习模式。对于研究不平衡学习、长尾分布分类或鲁棒优化的工作具有参考价值，但该文本身为实证观察型研究，未提出新的算法或理论框架。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does the two-phase underfit-then-overfit pattern hold for modern architectures (ResNets, Vision Transformers) and multi-class long-tailed benchmarks (ImageNet-LT, iNaturalist)?
2. Can the epoch-wise loss trajectory of the minority class be used as a diagnostic signal to trigger adaptive resampling or loss re-weighting strategies during training?
3. What is the theoretical mechanism behind the minority class loss initially *increasing* — is this driven by gradient dominance of the majority class, or by decision boundary dynamics?
4. How do existing imbalance methods (LDAM, Procrustean training, Debiased Contrastive Learning) alter the per-class loss trajectories observed in this study?
5. Could early stopping based on per-class metrics (rather than overall loss/accuracy) mitigate the overfitting of minority representations?
