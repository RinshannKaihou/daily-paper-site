FaithMate: The Interplay between Contextual and Parametric Chain-of-Thought Faithfulness

The first unified preference-alignment framework bridging two previously disjoint CoT faithfulness paradigms — contextual and parametric — revealing that they are positively coupled but fundamentally asymmetric.

Parametric faithfulness optimization consistently generalizes across both paradigms, while contextual optimization is variable and metric-dependent.

Jingyi Sun, Qianli Wang, Pepa Atanasova, Nils Feldhus, Isabelle Augenstein 2026-05-24 arxiv.org/abs/2605.24960
Scope of Evidence
3 × 2 × 6
models × datasets × faithfulness metrics
Why This Matters
CoT faithfulness is not monolithic; parametric optimization is the reliable path to transparent LLM reasoning.
Critical Edge
Contextual metrics capture disjoint facets and don't transfer — current evaluation approaches may be fundamentally insufficient.

Methodology

FaithMate provides a unified preference-alignment interface for optimizing LLMs towards either contextual or parametric CoT faithfulness.

  • Contextual paradigm — perturbs the input or CoT trace to measure faithfulness.
  • Parametric paradigm — intervenes on the model's parametric knowledge to assess faithfulness.
  • Same optimization framework for both enables direct cross-paradigm comparison.
  • Evaluation spans three LLMs, two datasets, and six faithfulness metrics.

Results & Highlights

  • Parametric optimization produces consistent gains across both paradigms.
  • Contextual optimization delivers variable, metric-dependent results.
  • Contextual metrics capture disjoint facets, exposing inherent trade-offs.
  • Paradigms are positively correlated but not interchangeable.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • First unified framework bridging two disjoint paradigms.
  • Comprehensive evaluation: 3 models, 2 datasets, 6 metrics.
  • Actionable finding: parametric optimization is the reliable path.
  • Clear implications for future CoT evaluation work.

Limitations

  • Generalizability beyond tested models and datasets is uncertain.
  • Faithfulness gains vs. downstream task performance unexplored.
  • Specific preference-alignment may not cover all strategies.
  • Contextual metrics' non-transfer suggests need for new evaluation.

Open Questions

  1. Would the asymmetric coupling hold for larger-scale models (70B+) or multimodal LLMs?
  2. How do faithfulness gains under FaithMate correlate with downstream task accuracy and reliability?
  3. Can a combined optimization strategy (both paradigms simultaneously) yield synergistic improvements?
  4. What explains why parametric faithfulness generalizes more broadly than contextual faithfulness?
  5. How should the community design a unified metric that captures the disjoint facets revealed here?
