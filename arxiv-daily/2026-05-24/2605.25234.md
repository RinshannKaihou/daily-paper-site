> **一句话总结：** 本文从参数不可辨识性视角系统分析过参数化ReLU网络的后验结构，证明即使底层函数被完全识别，由于排列对称性和神经元分裂机制，参数空间的不确定性仍持续存在，且后验系数服从Dirichlet分布。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proves that in overparametrized one-hidden-layer ReLU networks, parameter-space epistemic uncertainty persists even when the function is fully identified, arising from discrete permutation symmetries and continuous neuron splitting manifolds whose splitting coefficients follow a symmetric Dirichlet distribution.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示传统"认知不确定性随数据增加而消失"的观点隐含了参数可辨识性假设，在过参数化网络中Fisher信息矩阵奇异，后验不收缩为点质量
- 区分两种不可辨识性来源：离散的排列对称性（神经元互换）和连续的分裂流形（冗余神经元分配权重贡献）
- 证明过参数化模型中分裂系数服从对称Dirichlet分布 $\text{Dir}(\alpha, \ldots, \alpha)$，其中 $\alpha = (p+1)/2$
- 证明单链MCMC采样器在温和条件下几乎必然被限制在单个排列腔室内，不会遍历所有后验模式
- 指出深度集成（Deep Ensemble）在正确设定模型和大数据极限下恰好能正确捕获后验结构
- 在持续学习实验中展示不可辨识性引起的参数重要性估计偏差，并提出校正方法

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrates that the Bernstein–von Mises posterior concentration fails in overparametrized networks because the Fisher information is singular along non-identifiable directions
- Characterizes two sources of non-identifiability: (1) discrete permutation symmetries between hidden neurons, and (2) continuous splitting manifolds where redundant neurons redistribute a true neuron's contribution
- Derives the exact posterior for overparametrized 2-layer ReLU networks: splitting coefficients within each assignment group follow $\text{Dir}(\alpha, \ldots, \alpha)$ with $\alpha = (p+1)/2$
- Proves that under permutation-equivariant, Lipschitz MCMC updates (Assumption 1), a single chain almost surely remains confined to one permutation chamber (Corollary 3)
- Shows deep ensembles accidentally recover the correct permutation-mixture posterior in the correctly-specified, large-data limit
- Demonstrates practical consequences in continual learning (distorted parameter importance) and convergence diagnostics

## Methodology

The paper studies single-hidden-layer ReLU networks $f_w(x) = w_2^\top \phi(W_1 x)$ with $M$ hidden units, regularized via an $L_2$ penalty ($\lambda \|w\|_2^2$). The framework assumes a true function $f^*$ representable by $M^* < M$ neurons.

**Correctly specified case ($M = M^*$):** Using identifiability results from Bona-Pellissier et al. (2023), functional equality implies parameter equivalence up to permutations and positive rescalings. The $L_2$ penalty eliminates rescaling, leaving only permutations. The limiting posterior becomes a uniform mixture of $M!$ Dirac measures over all permutations $\pi$ of $w^*$.

**Overparametrized case ($M > M^*$):** The key tool is an assignment map $\varsigma: [M] \to [M^*]$ partitioning model neurons into groups $G_{m'}$ assigned to each true neuron. Corollary 2 (Kobialka et al., 2026) shows weights must take the form $w_{1,m} = \sqrt{c_m} w_{1,\varsigma(m)}^*$, $w_{2,m} = \sqrt{c_m} w_{2,\varsigma(m)}^*$ where splitting coefficients $(c_m)_{m \in G_{m'}} \in \Delta^{k_{m'}-1}$ lie on a simplex. The posterior on each splitting manifold is induced via tube-conditioning (shrinking $\varepsilon$-neighborhoods around $\mathcal{M}_\varsigma$).

**MCMC analysis:** The paper proves that under permutation-equivariant, Lipschitz-continuous discrete-time updates (covering many standard samplers), chains initialized in a permutation chamber's interior almost surely never cross the measure-zero boundaries, remaining confined to a single mode.

## Results & Highlights

- **Proposition 1:** Even when posterior concentrates on a single function equivalence class (function-space variance vanishes), parameter covariance can remain non-degenerate or diverge
- **Theorem 1:** In overparametrized models, the posterior moments satisfy $\mathbb{E}_P[\omega_m] = \mu_{k,\alpha} \omega_{m'}^*$ and $\mathbb{E}_P[\omega_m \omega_m^\top] = k^{-1} \omega_{m'}^* \omega_{m'}^{*\top}$, with individual neuron contributions shrinking as $M^{-1/2}$ while joint posterior over splitting coefficients remains non-degenerate
- **Corollary 3:** Single-chain MCMC almost surely covers only one posterior mode — deep ensembles may actually be a feature, not a bug, for capturing multi-mode structure
- **Empirical validation (Figure 3):** Prediction RMSE converges to zero with increasing $n$, but $\text{tr}(\text{Cov}(w|D_n))/d$ remains constant due to permutation invariance; restricting to a single mode eliminates this residual variance
- **Dirichlet validation (Figure 5):** Empirical splitting coefficient distributions closely match the theoretical $\text{Beta}(\alpha, (k-1)\alpha)$ marginals across different $(n, M)$ combinations
- **Moment validation (Figure 6):** Empirical first and second moments of scalar projections onto true neurons match theoretical predictions across all group sizes and widths
- **Practical impact:** In continual learning, non-identifiability-induced variance distorts parameter importance estimates, causing overly restrictive updates; correcting for this improves adaptation

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The reframing of epistemic uncertainty through non-identifiability is original. The derivation of exact Dirichlet posteriors for splitting coefficients is a clean theoretical contribution. The insight that deep ensembles accidentally capture the correct limiting posterior is elegant and somewhat surprising.
- **Rigor:** Very strong. Full proofs for all major results (Propositions 1–2, Theorem 1, Corollaries 1–3, Lemmas 1–4) are provided in the appendix. The tube-conditioning construction for defining posteriors on zero-measure manifolds is mathematically careful.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Experiments use standard setups (SGLD, NUTS) on controlled synthetic data with $M^* = 5$, $p = 5$. The theoretical predictions are specific enough to verify. Code is not mentioned but the experimental design is well-documented.
- **Significance:** Important for Bayesian deep learning theory. Questions a foundational assumption (epistemic uncertainty → 0) and provides constructive characterizations. The MCMC confinement result has practical implications for how we should run and interpret Bayesian neural network inference.
- **Red flags:** (1) Restricted to one-hidden-layer ReLU networks without biases — the author acknowledges this and suggests extensions but does not deliver them. (2) The gap between parameter-space and function-space uncertainty is well-articulated but the paper could be clearer about when practitioners should actually care (the answer seems to be: mainly for downstream tasks that use weights directly). (3) The Dirichlet result depends on Gaussian priors/$L_2$ regularization; alternative priors may change behavior.

## Strengths

- Clean, complete theoretical characterization of both discrete and continuous non-identifiability in a tractable model class
- The assignment map + splitting coefficient framework provides an intuitive geometric picture
- Surprising and practically relevant result that single-chain MCMC cannot explore all posterior modes
- The connection to deep ensembles is insightful — provides theoretical justification for a popular heuristic
- Thorough empirical validation across multiple dimensions: convergence, Dirichlet marginals, moment matching, switching behavior
- Honest discussion of practical implications and when the results matter

## Limitations

- Analysis restricted to one-hidden-layer ReLU networks without hidden-layer biases; extension to deeper architectures with compositional non-identifiabilities is left as future work
- The Gaussian prior/$L_2$ regularization assumption is crucial for the Dirichlet result; behavior under other priors is unknown
- Practical guidance for common methods (MC dropout, variational inference) is limited — the paper focuses on MCMC
- The function-space vs. parameter-space distinction is clear, but the paper could better quantify when parameter-space uncertainty actually harms downstream tasks beyond the two showcased experiments
- Synthetic experiments only; no validation on real-world datasets or architectures

## Relevance

本文对贝叶斯深度学习的理论基石提出了根本性挑战，对于理解过参数化模型中不确定性的本质、设计更好的采样策略和持续学习方法具有直接的理论指导意义，是认知不确定性量化领域的重要理论贡献。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do layer-wise and compositional non-identifiabilities compound in multi-layer networks? Do Dirichlet-like splitting laws emerge?
- Can the permutation confinement result guide the design of multi-chain or temperature-based sampling strategies that efficiently cover all posterior modes?
- What happens under non-Gaussian priors (e.g., sparsity-inducing priors) — does the splitting manifold structure change fundamentally?
- How does the interaction between non-identifiability and optimization dynamics (learning rate, momentum, adaptive methods) affect the practical posterior obtained via SGD-based sampling?
- Can the theoretical characterization of splitting coefficients be used as a diagnostic for detecting overparametrization in trained models?
