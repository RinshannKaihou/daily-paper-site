Tiny Brains, Giant Impact: Uncovering the Keystone Neurons of LLM with Just a Few Prompts

A vanishingly sparse subset of neurons is critical to every LLM task. Remove them and the model collapses. Fine-tune only them and you match full-parameter fine-tuning.

Xiangtian JiYuxin ChenZhengzhou Cai Xiang WangAn ZhangTat-Seng Chua

Key Finding

Keystone neurons — an ultra-sparse subset of neurons consistently highly activated across all tasks — are the backbone of LLM capability. Their removal causes catastrophic collapse.

Key Metric

A few prompts

Identification cost. Exact sparsity ratio not reported.

Why This Matters

Updating only keystone neurons matches full fine-tuning while preserving other capabilities — a new frontier for parameter-efficient adaptation.

Critical Edge

Generalization across architectures and robustness under distribution shift remain unverified. The sparsity ratio is not quantified.

Methodology

  • Cross-task activation probing — analyze neuron activation across reasoning, language understanding, and knowledge dimensions in open-weight Transformers.
  • Sparse subset isolation — use activation strength to isolate an extremely sparse "keystone neuron" subset.
  • Ablation via removal — systematically remove keystone neurons; observe catastrophic behavioral collapse.
  • Pretraining analysis — confirm keystone neurons emerge primarily during pretraining, not fine-tuning.
  • Parameter sensitivity — small perturbations to keystone neuron parameters degrade performance.
  • Targeted fine-tuning — supervised fine-tuning updating only keystone neurons, benchmarked against full-parameter fine-tuning.

Results & Highlights

  • Keystone neurons constitute an extremely sparse subset, yet their removal causes model-wide behavioral collapse.
  • The set is stable and intrinsic — not a task-specific artifact.
  • Largely established during pretraining with tightly calibrated parameters.
  • Fine-tuning only keystone neurons achieves comparable or superior task performance to full-parameter fine-tuning.
  • The targeted approach significantly better preserves other capability dimensions.
  • Modifies far fewer parameters — a parameter-efficient alternative.

Strengths

  • Clear narrative arc: discovery → mechanism → application.
  • Practical impact — a new PEFT method with strong empirical results.
  • Addresses a fundamental mechanistic-interpretability question.
  • Cross-task and cross-model analysis bolsters generalizability claims.

Limitations

  • Relationship to prior work on polysemantic neurons and attention heads is unclear.
  • Exact sparsity ratio is not stated.
  • Cannot verify generalization to proprietary / closed models.
  • Long-term stability under distribution shift or adversarial conditions is unknown.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the keystone neuron subset scale with model size — fixed ratio or absolute number?
  • Do keystone neurons correspond to known functional circuits (induction heads, retrieval heads)?
  • How robust is keystone-neuron-only fine-tuning under distribution shift or adversarial attacks?
  • Can keystone neurons be identified without model weights (API-only probing)?
  • How do keystone neurons interact with existing PEFT methods like LoRA or adapters?
