> **一句话总结：** 本文将 KV cache 压缩建模为序贯 Wyner-Ziv 信源编码问题，通过实验发现语言模型对上下文截断的敏感度呈多项式衰减（而非几何衰减），并据此推导出仅后缀策略的窗口大小与失真之间的紧界限 Θ(ε⁻¹/ᵅ)。

> **TL;DR:** Formulates KV cache compression as sequential Wyner-Ziv source coding, discovers that truncation sensitivity decays polynomially (not geometrically) across four LLMs (0.5–3B), and proves tight Θ(ε⁻¹/ᵅ) memory bounds for suffix-only cache policies under a polynomial truncation-sensitivity assumption.

## 核心贡献

- 首次将自回归语言模型的 KV cache 压缩问题形式化为序贯 Wyner-Ziv 信源编码问题，以 next-step query 作为解码端边信息。
- 在四个模型（Qwen2.5-0.5B/1.5B/3B, SmolLM2-1.7B）、四个领域上发现上下文截断敏感度服从幂律衰减（α ∈ [0.37, 0.58]），优于指数拟合 2–2.5 倍（log-RMSE）。
- 证明了仅后缀（suffix-only）缓存策略的窗口-失真紧界 Θ(ε⁻¹/ᵅ)：滑窗上界 O(ε⁻¹/ᵅ) 与条件逆界 Ω(ε⁻¹/ᵅ) 匹配。
- 给出显式 block-Markov 随机编码可达性方案，期望速率指数 α/(α+1) 在无前向衰减假设下即匹配逆界。
- 推导出 sink-plus-recent 策略的 KL 失真阶为 O(k⁻²ᵅ)，因子 2 来自有界 log-density 下的局部二次 KL–TV 关系，独立实验验证 α_KL/α_TV ∈ [1.93, 2.38] 恰好包含 2。

## Key Contributions

- Formulates online KV cache compression as sequential Wyner–Ziv source coding on the model's filtration, with next-step query vectors as decoder side information.
- Empirically demonstrates polynomial (power-law) decay of truncation sensitivity across four models and two families (α ∈ [0.37, 0.58]), consistently outperforming exponential fits by 2–2.5× in log-RMSE.
- Establishes tight memory characterization for suffix-only policies: sliding window w = O(ε⁻¹/ᵅ) (Theorem 4.2) and conditional converse w = Ω(ε⁻¹/ᵅ) (Theorem 4.5), yielding Θ(ε⁻¹/ᵅ).
- Provides an explicit block-Markov achievability scheme (Theorem 4.6) with window w_n = n^{1/(α+1)} and sharp expected rate exponent α/(α+1) without forward-decay assumptions.
- Derives the sink-plus-recent corollary (Corollary 4.9): KL distortion gap O(k⁻²ᵅ), with the exponent-doubling independently validated (α_KL/α_TV ∈ [1.93, 2.38]).
- Proves a mixing-free asymptotic Wyner–Ziv converse (Theorem 4.1), a universal scheme oblivious to α (Theorem M.1), and structural extensions including continuous-latent intrinsic-dimension bounds and multi-layer rate allocation.

## Methodology

The paper combines information-theoretic analysis with careful empirical measurement:

**Theoretical framework.** The KV cache is viewed as an online compression problem where, at each step t, a causal encoder maps past tokens X_{<t} to a compressed code Ĉ_t, and a decoder reconstructs the next-token distribution p̃_t from (Ĉ_{≤t}, Q_t). The per-token rate and KL distortion are defined naturally. A sequential Wyner–Ziv rate function R*(D) is introduced via auxiliary processes. The key assumption—polynomial truncation sensitivity (Definition 3.3)—states that TV(p_θ(·|X_{1:t-1}), p_θ(·|X_{t-w:t-1})) ≤ C_TS · w⁻ᵅ. Under this, four core results are proven: (1) an asymptotic lower bound via three martingales adapted to the compressed filtration; (2) a polynomial sliding-window approximation replacing the classical log(1/ε) with ε⁻¹/ᵅ; (3) a conditional suffix-only lower bound via Bayes-risk; (4) an explicit block-Markov achievability scheme with window n^{1/(α+1)}.

**Empirical protocol.** TV distance is measured between full-context and window-truncated next-token distributions using a position-preserving protocol (retained tokens keep original absolute indices) to avoid positional-encoding artifacts. Sink-plus-recent KL distortion is measured for k ∈ {8, ..., 512}. Power-law vs. exponential fits are compared by log-RMSE and AIC. A positional-encoding ablation confirms the decay is genuine content forgetting (agreement to three decimals between position-preserving and fresh-sequence protocols).

**Models and domains.** Four models (Qwen2.5-0.5B/1.5B/3B, SmolLM2-1.7B), two primary domains (Gutenberg literary text, Python source code), plus Wikipedia prose and structured JSON for robustness checks. 100 prefixes per domain, windows up to w = 8192.

## Results & Highlights

- **Power-law decay is universal:** Across all four models and all domains, TV truncation sensitivity follows w⁻ᵅ with α ∈ [0.37, 0.58]. The polynomial fit beats exponential by 2–4× in log-RMSE universally.
- **α increases with model scale:** Within the Qwen2.5 family, α_nat grows monotonically (0.44 → 0.51 → 0.58 from 0.5B to 3B), meaning larger models recover next-token distributions from shorter windows faster.
- **Natural language decays faster than code:** α_nat > α_code holds across all four models, consistent with code having more persistent long-range structure (variable scopes, imports).
- **Sink-plus-recent dominates random retention by 30–85×** in median KL distortion at k = 512, confirming positional structure as the operative mechanism.
- **Exponent-doubling validated:** α_KL/α_TV ∈ [1.93, 2.38] across domains, bracketing the predicted factor of 2 from the local quadratic KL–TV relation.
- **Recency policies dominate heavy-hitter:** A simple last-query attention-score heuristic falls between random and recency-based policies, suggesting attention sinks capture most high-mass context.
- **Tail persists to w = 8192:** With 16,500-token prefixes, the power law holds across five octaves without acceleration, well past the deployed 4k regime.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The formulation of KV cache compression as sequential Wyner–Ziv coding is novel, as is the empirical discovery of polynomial (vs. geometric) truncation sensitivity. The tight Θ(ε⁻¹/ᵅ) characterization is a clean theoretical result.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Theoretical results are precisely stated with explicit assumptions, and the gap between what truncation sensitivity implies vs. what requires additional hypotheses is clearly delineated (Remark 4.7, §7). The empirical protocol addresses positional-encoding confounds.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. All models are open (Qwen2.5, SmolLM2), the measurement protocol is described in detail (position-preserving, window grids, prefix sampling), and fit criteria (log-RMSE, AIC) are standard.
- **Significance:** Potentially high impact. The polynomial finding changes how we should think about KV cache design: if α ≈ 0.5, then doubling the window only reduces distortion by ~2× (not exponentially), explaining why deployed schemes need much larger windows than geometric-mixing theory predicts.
- **Red flags:** (1) All models are ≤ 3B parameters due to single-GPU constraints—whether the result transfers to frontier-scale models (70B+, 405B) is unknown. (2) The two-sided Bayes-risk condition (Definition 4.4) is assumed but not empirically validated (the author acknowledges this). (3) The suffix-only restriction is significant—whether recurrent/propagating schemes can beat Θ(ε⁻¹/ᵅ) is explicitly left open. (4) The code corpus is small (~0.6M characters), potentially biasing α_code estimates.

## Strengths

- Clean integration of information theory with a practically important problem (KV cache compression).
- Polynomial vs. geometric distinction is empirically convincing with multiple consistency checks (independent exponent recovery, position ablation, cross-model replication).
- Tight theoretical characterization (matching upper and lower bounds) within the suffix-only class.
- Honest about limitations and open problems (propagating schemes, larger models, mechanistic explanation of α).
- The sink-plus-recent corollary directly connects theory to deployed systems (StreamingLLM, Mistral).

## Limitations

- Models limited to ≤ 3B parameters; results may not transfer to 7B–405B scale.
- The two-sided Bayes-risk condition (lower bound) is assumed, not empirically validated.
- Suffix-only policy class excludes recurrent summaries (e.g., attention-sink propagation, running summaries)—the most practically interesting extension.
- No mechanistic explanation for *why* the decay is polynomial or what determines α from architecture/training.
- Code domain corpus is small; α estimates may shift with larger, more diverse corpora.
- The single-channel abstraction treats the query as exogenous side information, collapsing multi-layer structure; a recursive layer-wise treatment is acknowledged as future work.
- No comparison with continuous-latent schemes (e.g., MLA) on equal footing.

## Relevance

本文将信息论中的率失真理论严格应用于 KV cache 压缩，推导出多项式截断敏感度下缓存策略的理论极限，对长上下文推理的内存优化具有直接指导意义。幂律衰减的发现解释了现有滑窗方案为何需要远大于几何混合理论预测的窗口大小。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does polynomial truncation sensitivity hold at frontier scale (7B–405B models), and does α continue to increase with model scale?
2. Can recurrent or propagating cache summaries (e.g., compressed attention summaries, running state vectors) beat the Θ(ε⁻¹/ᵅ) suffix-only lower bound?
3. What architectural and training-data properties mechanistically determine the exponent α—can it be predicted a priori from depth, width, head structure, or data statistics?
4. How does the multi-layer recursive formulation change the bounds, given that queries in practice depend on (possibly compressed) hidden states rather than being exogenous?
5. Does the polynomial law extend to state-space models (Mamba, RWKV) where the attention-specific structure used in the proofs does not apply?
