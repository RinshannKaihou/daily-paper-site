Confidence-Aware KV Cache Eviction for Long-Horizon LLM Inference

CONF-KV reads the model's own next-token confidence to dynamically tighten or loosen the KV cache budget at each decoding step — pruning aggressively when certain, retaining context when uncertain — closing 74% of the perplexity gap versus full KV at a fraction of the memory.

Key Metric 74% gap closed

Perplexity gap between full KV and sliding-512 on GPT-2, at 34.2 MB — less memory than every baseline.

Why This Matters Adaptive eviction slashes KV memory 6× while preserving quality — single-GPU long-context inference.
Critical Edge Confidence weights tuned only on GPT-2 124M; model can be confidently wrong before rare-entity lookups.

Methodology

STEP 01 Confidence Estimation

Composite score from entropy, log-prob margin, and top-token mass. Weights (0.4, 0.3, 0.3) stable across models.

STEP 02 Budget Selection

Threshold τ=0.7 selects tight budget Nhigh (confident) or loose Nlow (uncertain) per step.

STEP 03 Token Ranking & Eviction

si = α·attention_mass + (1−α)·recency. Lowest-ranked tokens evicted; recent P tokens always protected.

STEP 04 Mixed-Precision Storage

Most recent W tokens in FP16; older entries symmetrically quantized to INT8. Adds only 0.34 PPL over FP16.

STEP 05 Pyramidal Layer Budgets

Deeper layers get exponentially smaller budgets (β=0.5, Nmin=96), reflecting information concentration.

STEP 06 Blockwise Online Softmax

Compacted cache read in blocks with running max/normalizer — exact softmax, no indirection table needed.

Results & Highlights

PPL (GPT-2, 2K tokens) 30.48 vs 29.14 full / 34.37 sliding
NIAH retrieval (20B, ≤32K) 91.4% vs 53.8% sliding
VisualWebArena tasks 95.3% retention at 2.8× less memory
Throughput (GPT-2, b8) 2.06× full-KV speed
Qwen-32B peak KV mem 2.6 GB from 15.8 GB (−83.5%)
Mechanistic validation r = −0.77 confidence ↔ KL shift

Strengths & Limitations

  • Training-free — drops into any autoregressive loop, no weight changes
  • Mechanistic proof via KL-ablation: confidence causally matters
  • Composable with paged attention, speculative decoding, head-wise allocation
  • Sensitivity sweeps expose direct quality–memory knobs
  • No benefit when context never exceeds eviction threshold
  • Confidence signal degrades under high-temperature sampling
  • Fine-grained eviction creates partially dead blocks in PagedAttention
  • Over-confidence before rare-entity lookups causes premature eviction

Open Questions

  1. How does CONF-KV interact with multi-query or grouped-query attention where K/V heads are shared?
  2. Can task-level hints (e.g. marking retrieval-heavy spans) enrich the confidence signal?
  3. Do the 0.4/0.3/0.3 weights hold at 70B+ scale on domain-shifted data?
  4. Behavior in multi-turn conversations where uncertainty is about dialogue state, not facts?
  5. Could a learned confidence estimator outperform the hand-crafted composite?
