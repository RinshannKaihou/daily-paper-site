> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 FaithMate 统一偏好对齐框架，首次系统研究 CoT 忠实性的两种范式（上下文忠实性 vs 参数忠实性）之间的交互关系，发现参数忠实性优化能一致地提升两种范式的表现，而上下文忠实性优化则效果不稳定。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes FaithMate, a unified preference-alignment interface for optimizing LLM CoT faithfulness under two paradigms (contextual and parametric), finding asymmetric coupling: parametric optimization yields consistent cross-paradigm gains, while contextual optimization is more variable and metric-dependent.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **FaithMate**，首个统一偏好对齐框架，将上下文忠实性和参数忠实性两种 CoT 忠实性评估范式整合到同一优化接口中。
- 系统研究了两种忠实性范式之间的交互关系，揭示它们之间存在**正向但不对称的耦合**：参数忠实性优化能一致地泛化到两种范式，而上下文忠实性优化效果不稳定。
- 发现在上下文范式内部，不同指标之间的忠实性增益**不可一致迁移**，说明现有上下文指标捕捉的是忠实性的不同侧面。
- 提出重要结论：CoT 忠实性**不是单一目标**，需要多方面的优化和评估。

## Key Contributions

- Propose **FaithMate**, the first unified preference-alignment interface bridging two previously disjoint CoT faithfulness paradigms (contextual and parametric).
- Demonstrate **asymmetric positive coupling** between paradigms: parametric faithfulness optimization consistently generalizes across both paradigms, while contextual optimization yields variable gains.
- Reveal that within the contextual paradigm, faithfulness improvements on one metric do **not consistently transfer** to others, indicating existing contextual metrics capture disjoint facets.
- Establish that CoT faithfulness is **not monolithic** and requires multifaceted optimization and evaluation strategies.

## Methodology

FaithMate provides a unified preference-alignment interface that enables optimization of LLMs towards either contextual or parametric CoT faithfulness. The contextual paradigm measures faithfulness by perturbing the input or CoT trace, while the parametric paradigm assesses it by intervening on a model's parametric knowledge. By using the same optimization framework for both, the authors can directly compare and measure cross-paradigm generalization. The experimental setup spans three models, two datasets, and six faithfulness metrics, enabling comprehensive analysis of within-paradigm and cross-paradigm transfer.

## Results & Highlights

- Parametric faithfulness optimization produces **consistent gains across both paradigms**, suggesting it targets a more fundamental aspect of model reasoning behavior.
- Contextual faithfulness optimization delivers **more variable results**, with gains that are metric-dependent and less stable across evaluation settings.
- Within the contextual paradigm, different faithfulness metrics capture **disjoint facets** of faithfulness, exposing inherent trade-offs between metrics.
- The two paradigms are **positively correlated** but not interchangeable — improvements in one do not uniformly benefit the other.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High — this is the first work to formally investigate the interplay between contextual and parametric CoT faithfulness under a unified optimization framework, moving beyond descriptive comparisons.
- **Rigor:** Strong experimental design with three models, two datasets, and six metrics provides robust evidence for the claimed asymmetric coupling.
- **Reproducibility:** The unified framework (FaithMate) and breadth of evaluation should facilitate reproduction. Details on preference data construction and optimization hyperparameters will determine full reproducibility.
- **Significance:** The finding that CoT faithfulness is not monolithic has important implications for how the community evaluates and optimizes model reasoning transparency.
- **Potential concerns:** The generalizability of findings beyond the specific models and datasets tested remains to be validated. The relationship between faithfulness improvements and downstream task performance is not addressed.

## Strengths

- First unified framework bridging two previously disjoint research paradigms.
- Comprehensive evaluation across models, datasets, and metrics.
- Actionable finding that parametric optimization is the more reliable path to cross-paradigm faithfulness.
- Clear implications for future work on CoT evaluation and optimization.

## Limitations

- Generalizability beyond the three tested models and two datasets is uncertain.
- Does not explore the relationship between faithfulness gains and downstream task performance.
- The specific preference-alignment approach may not capture all optimization strategies.
- Contextual metrics' lack of mutual transfer suggests the field may need fundamentally new evaluation approaches.

## Relevance

本文对 CoT 忠实性优化和评估提供了系统性洞见，对研究 LLM 推理透明度和可解释性的工作具有直接参考价值，尤其是关于多指标评估的必要性和参数忠实性作为优化目标的优越性的发现。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would the asymmetric coupling hold for larger-scale models (e.g., 70B+ parameter models) or multimodal LLMs?
- How do faithfulness gains under FaithMate correlate with downstream task accuracy and reliability?
- Can a combined optimization strategy (simultaneously targeting both paradigms) yield synergistic improvements?
- What are the theoretical explanations for why parametric faithfulness generalizes more broadly than contextual faithfulness?
- How should the community design a unified faithfulness metric that captures the disjoint facets revealed by contextual metrics?
