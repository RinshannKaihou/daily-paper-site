> **一句话总结：** 该论文通过行为经济学中的"实现效应"作为测试案例，发现Gemma模型虽然在线性探针层面可以解码出"已实现/未实现"状态表示，但通过激活引导（activation steering）无法因果地控制下游风险决策行为，揭示了行为敏感性、潜在可读性和因果控制是三个独立的属性。

> **TL;DR:** This paper tests whether LLMs genuinely implement the realization effect from behavioral economics. While Gemma 3 4B encodes a linearly decodable realization-status signal at layer 18 that generalizes to held-out prompts, steering along this direction does not reliably shift downstream risk choices — demonstrating that behavioral sensitivity, latent readout, and causal control are three distinct properties that do not automatically co-occur.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了一个三层验证框架：行为敏感性（prompt-only）、线性表征可读性（activation readout）、因果控制（activation steering），用于严格评估LLM是否真正实现了人类认知机制。
- 发现Gemma 3 4B在第18层的残差流中存在可线性解码的"已实现/未实现"状态信号，且该方向能泛化到未见过的提示词。
- 通过激活引导实验证明，尽管表征可读，但无法因果控制下游风险决策行为，揭示"可读性≠因果性"。
- 提供了一个清晰的反例：线性探针成功而因果干预失败，对机制可解释性领域的方法论标准提出挑战。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a three-tier evaluation framework for testing whether LLMs genuinely implement human cognitive mechanisms: prompt-only behavioral sensitivity, linear activation readout, and causal control via activation steering.
- Demonstrates that Gemma 3 4B contains a linearly decodable realization-status signal at layer 18 in the residual stream that generalizes to held-out prompts from an independent source (DeepSeek-generated).
- Shows a dissociation between readout and control: the decoded direction does not reliably shift downstream wager or risk-profile behavior across positive scales (+50 to +150) or in a negative sign-symmetry run at scale −50.
- Provides empirical evidence that linear decodability is insufficient evidence that a model behaviorally relies on a representation during downstream decision-making.

## Methodology

The study proceeds in three stages using the realization effect (a behavioral economics finding where risk-taking differs after paper vs. realized gains/losses) as a test case:

1. **Prompt-only behavioral evaluation:** 54,450 rows across 25 models, 11 conditions spanning paper/realized outcomes. Models return a wager (1–1000 CHF) and risk preference (1–5). Analyzed with OLS regression using condition indicators, model/temperature/prompt-version fixed effects, and HC3 robust standard errors.

2. **Activation readout:** Logged Gemma 3 4B residual-stream activations for paired paper_open vs. realized_closed prompts. Constructed a mean-difference direction (µ_realized − µ_paper) from 756 training pairs at layer 18. Evaluated on original held-out splits and a newly generated DeepSeek held-out set (40 matched pairs).

3. **Activation steering:** Applied the train-only layer-18 direction via forward hooks during generation at scales −50, +50, +75, +100, +150 on 648 behavior-evaluation prompts. Compared steered vs. unsteered matched responses on wager and risk-profile outcomes. Also ran a positive-control classification steering task as diagnostic.

## Results & Highlights

- **Prompt-only behavior:** Models are condition-sensitive to outcome framing, but the directional pattern does not reproduce human realization-effect predictions. Realized losses increase (rather than decrease) wagers — the opposite of the human finding.
- **Activation readout succeeds:** The train-only layer-18 direction achieves 89–93% correct-direction classification on validation/readout splits, and 80.6% on behavior-evaluation prompts. Mean projection deltas are consistently positive across all splits.
- **Projection–behavior link is weak:** Within matched pairs, projection deltas do not predict wager deltas (Pearson r = 0.072) or risk deltas (r = 0.001). Raw associations are confounded by prompt structure.
- **Steering fails:** Mean wager deltas are small and positive across scales; median deltas are zero throughout. Negative scale (−50) does not produce a sign-symmetric reversal. Classification steering shows only weak directional movement (realized-prediction rate: 0.140 → 0.198 at +100) with accuracy near chance.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The three-tier framework (behavior → readout → steering) applied to a well-characterized behavioral economics phenomenon is a clean methodological contribution. The dissociation between readout and control is empirically novel.
- **Rigor:** Strong. The authors are commendably transparent about null results, compliance artifacts, and limitations. The train-only / held-out split design for the direction is methodologically sound.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code is public, the experimental design is clearly specified, and the use of a local model (Gemma 3 4B) with specific hyperparameters enables replication.
- **Significance:** Important for the mechanistic interpretability community. The finding that linear decodability does not imply causal relevance challenges a common inferential leap in the field.
- **Red flags:** The study is narrow — single model, single layer, single construct. The positive-control classification task had a duplicated-instruction bug, weakening that diagnostic. The DeepSeek held-out set is small (40 pairs). Steering results lack bootstrap confidence intervals.

## Strengths

- Clean experimental design with a well-grounded test case from behavioral economics.
- Honest and thorough reporting of null results, which is scientifically valuable.
- The train-only direction evaluated on held-out data is a strong generalization test.
- The within-pair analysis controlling for prompt structure is the right comparison.
- The paper clearly articulates the distinction between correlational readout and causal control.

## Limitations

- All mechanistic results are specific to Gemma 3 4B at layer 18 with a mean-difference direction; no layer sweep, position-mode sweep, or multi-layer steering was tested.
- Hosted models (Qwen, etc.) could not be tested mechanistically due to lack of hidden-state access.
- The behavioral assay uses constrained numeric outputs, which may amplify format/parsing artifacts.
- The DeepSeek held-out set is small and single-source.
- No bootstrap or randomization intervals for steering deltas, making the null result less precisely quantified.
- The decoded direction may track a broad semantic contrast (open vs. closed framing) rather than the specific decision-relevant variable needed to shift risk-taking.

## Relevance

这项研究对理解LLM内部表征与行为决策之间的关系提出了严格的方法论框架。对于关注机制可解释性（mechanistic interpretability）和LLM作为行为模拟器（behavioral simulators）可靠性的研究者来说，其"可读性≠因果性"的发现具有重要的方法论启示。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would a systematic layer sweep (layers 14–22) or multi-layer steering schedule produce stronger behavioral control, or does the dissociation persist?
- Does the readout–control dissociation replicate across other model architectures (Qwen, Llama) under local hook frameworks?
- Are numeric output tasks particularly resistant to steering because of instruction-following priors, or does the dissociation extend to free-text generation tasks?
- Could contrastive directions other than mean-difference (e.g., logistic regression probes, PCA-based directions) produce stronger causal control?
- What does the gap between readout accuracy (~90%) and behavioral control failure imply about the geometry of representations in transformer residual streams?
