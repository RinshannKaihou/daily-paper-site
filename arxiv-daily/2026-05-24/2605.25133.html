Key Finding

An LLM prover defends its answer through checkable sub-claims; a verifier challenges them. The resulting dialogue produces a structured confidence verdict — and separates trustworthy answers from uncertain ones by ±30 percentage points.

João Sedoc · Baotong Zhang · Dean Foster 2026-05-24 GPQA Diamond · Humanity's Last Exam
Key Metric
~30pp
HC-Prec gap on GPQA Diamond between the Accept + No Change subset and its complement — the largest separation among all tested baselines.
Why This Matters
Knowing when to trust an LLM answer matters more than the answer itself. PVD makes model confidence audible — you can inspect the debate, not just the score.
Critical Edge
Collapses on harder out-of-distribution tasks (HLE). The coverage–precision tradeoff remains unquantified: high precision may come at the cost of answering far fewer questions.
Methodology

PVD Protocol

PVD frames selective prediction as an interactive proof process. Frozen LLMs act as imperfect provers and verifiers over a noisy channel; the protocol is evaluated empirically via coverage–precision tradeoffs rather than formal soundness/completeness guarantees.

  1. Prover phase — A prover LLM (e.g. Claude Sonnet 4.6) generates a candidate answer and defends it through a series of checkable sub-claims.
  2. Verifier phase — A separate verifier LLM (e.g. Claude Haiku 4.5) issues targeted challenges and returns one of three verdicts: Accept, Challenge, or Reject.
  3. Deliberation loop — If the verifier issues a Challenge, the prover may revise its answer and the dialogue continues.
  4. Selection criterion — Answers accepted without any revision (ANC — Accept + No Change) are treated as high-confidence; all others are abstained.
Results & Highlights

What the Numbers Say

30pp GAP ANC subset separates reliable from unreliable answers with ~30 percentage points precision advantage over the non-ANC complement on GPQA Diamond.
CROSS-MODEL High HC-Prec transfers across Claude, GPT, and Gemini families — the signal is not model-specific.
STRICTNESS Verifier strictness and domain competence largely determine selection gap size. Stricter, more competent verifiers yield better separation.
HLE FAIL Weaker prover–verifier pairings collapse or invert the ANC signal on Humanity's Last Exam, revealing a practical domain boundary.
DISTINCT PVD outperforms self-consistency, universal self-consistency, multi-agent debate, and Reflexion — suggesting an argument-defensibility signal distinct from existing approaches.
Strengths
  • Principled grounding in interactive proof theory provides a real theoretical framework
  • Strong empirical gains on a challenging benchmark (GPQA Diamond)
  • Honest failure-mode reporting on HLE demonstrates scientific integrity
  • Cross-model robustness testing strengthens generalizability claims
  • Three-verdict protocol is interpretable and actionable
Limitations
  • Coverage–precision tradeoff not fully characterized — precision gains may cost answering far fewer questions
  • Degrades significantly on harder, out-of-distribution tasks (HLE)
  • Requires specific model pairings (strong prover + competent verifier)
  • Relationship to multi-agent debate not fully disentangled — "distinct signal" claim needs stronger evidence
  • Multi-turn dialogue at inference time incurs substantial computational cost
Open Questions
  • What is the exact coverage rate of the ANC subset on GPQA Diamond, and how does the full coverage–precision curve compare to simpler calibration methods?
  • Can PVD be made adaptive — dynamically adjusting verifier strictness based on question difficulty?
  • How does the token cost of PVD compare to self-consistency with equivalent sample counts?
  • Would ensemble verifier strategies (multiple verifiers voting) improve robustness on harder benchmarks like HLE?
  • Can the prover–verifier dialogue structure be leveraged for interpretable error analysis beyond selective prediction?
