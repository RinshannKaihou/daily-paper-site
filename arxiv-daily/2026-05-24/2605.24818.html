Key Finding

Spiking Training Data Corrects Contaminated Benchmark Scores

Intentionally inserting test examples into training data at known rates creates a causal randomization control that enables principled statistical correction — cutting MMLU error from 13.1pp down to 1.8pp with just ~10 calibration samples.

Johnny Wei, Jerry Li, Ameya Godbole, Robin Jia — USC  ·  2026-05-24
13.1pp
MMLU RMSE — Naive (uncorrected)
→
1.8pp
MMLU RMSE — Combined estimator
Why This Matters
Gives LLM developers a low-cost, statistically grounded method to correct inflated benchmark scores from test-set contamination.
Calibration Cost
~10 spiked examples suffice for IPW with Min-K%++, and calibrators transfer across datasets.
Critical Edge
Exact-duplicate contamination only; requires developer access; correlated hard-contamination still leaves ~10–15pp residual error.

Methodology

1. Hubble Simulation

Matched pairs of 8B Llama models (clean vs. contaminated at duplication rates {1,4,16,64,256}) across WinoGrande, MMLU, HellaSwag, PIQA, PopQA. 500-item test sets, 0.3 contamination rate, 1,000 bootstrap replicates.

2. Four Estimators

  • Naive — raw contaminated accuracy (baseline)
  • IPW — downweights by P̂(contam), drops memorized items
  • Imputation — replaces outcomes with correctness predictor P̂(correct)
  • Combined — doubly-robust-style interpolation (Eq. 4)

3. Predictors

  • Memorization: LOSS, Min-K%, Min-K%++, zlib — Platt-scaled; reference model as oracle
  • Correctness: fine-tuned RoBERTa or LLM confidence (Llama 3.1, Pythia 6.9B, Qwen3 8B)

4. Practical Analysis

  • IPW needs only ~10 spiked examples for calibration
  • Memorization calibrators trained on Wikipedia transfer to other datasets
  • Developers need not spike every benchmark individually

Results & Highlights

Phase Diagrams
Combined estimator dominates under stronger contamination; IPW fails when hard examples are selectively contaminated (selection bias).
Memorization Predictors
AUROC near chance at low contamination, near-perfect at high. Simple LOSS after Platt scaling performs well despite its simplicity.
MMLU Correction
Combined: 1.4–2.3pp RMSE (random) vs. 1.8–13.1pp naive. Correlated hard: imputation cuts 29.2pp → 10.5pp.
Cross-dataset Transfer
Wikipedia-spiked calibrators closely match dataset-specific ones — developers don't need per-benchmark spiking.

Strengths

  • First principled framework for correcting (not just detecting) contaminated scores
  • Elegant causal-inference framing — spiking as randomization, estimators as propensity/outcome models
  • 10-example calibration with cross-dataset transfer
  • Phase diagrams give clear practitioner decision guidance
  • Practical: studies real costs and shows Wikipedia spiking is often sufficient

Limitations

  • Exact-duplicate contamination only — paraphrases and near-duplicates not addressed
  • Requires developer cooperation; not applicable to third-party black-box audits
  • Correlated contamination still leaves 10–15pp residual error
  • Evaluated only on 8B models; scaling to frontier models unknown
  • Positivity assumption may fail when entire benchmarks are contaminated
