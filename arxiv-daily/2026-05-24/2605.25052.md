> **一句话总结：** 本文构建 BONAFIDE 基准——通过设计"输出可反推必需中间计算"的任务（outright 与 diversionary 两类）+ 自动化标注流水线（98.9% 精度），得到 3,066 条带 ground-truth 忠实性标签的 CoT（覆盖 10 个模型 13 个任务）；系统评估 8 个主流忠实性度量指标后发现大多数接近随机：最佳 CoT 级 CC-SHAP 仅 0.70 AUROC，最佳步骤级 Filler Tokens 仅 0.59，二者互不迁移，且 IMP 类指标 90–96% 预测为不忠实、SEM 类 94–96% 预测为忠实，CC-SHAP 单实例耗时高达 10³ 秒。

> **TL;DR:** Constructs BONAFIDE, the first benchmark with ground-truth faithfulness labels for chain-of-thought (CoT) reasoning, by designing tasks (outright: correct answers require specific bottleneck computations; diversionary: misleading hints whose following is detectable) whose outputs reveal which intermediate computations must have produced them. An automated labeling pipeline (Gemini 3 Flash retrieval judge + Gemini 3 Pro validation judge, ~$2,100 cost, validated at 98.9% precision [95% CI 96.6–100] against human annotation) yields 1,120 CoT-level and 1,946 step-level labels across 10 open-weight models (4B–70B; Qwen3, OLMo 3, DeepSeek-R1-Distill, Llama 3.3) and 13 tasks. Evaluating 8 prominent faithfulness metrics (Adding Mistakes, Early Answering, Filler Tokens, SCM, FUR, CC-SHAP, Simulatability, Paraphrasing) plus Random/LM-Judge baselines: most score near chance (0.5 AUROC); the best CoT-level metric CC-SHAP reaches 0.70 AUROC but drops below random at the step level and costs up to 10³ s/instance; the best step-level metric Filler Tokens reaches 0.59 AUROC but is near chance at the CoT level. Importance-based metrics label 90–96% of CoTs unfaithful while semantic-utility metrics label 94–96% faithful on a balanced sample.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 CoT 忠实性的新定义：步骤级（步骤准确描述模型中某时发生的计算）与 CoT 级（包含完整推理路径且无不可信步骤），区分忠实性与正交的 plausibility/importance。
- 设计两类任务获取 ground-truth 标签：Outright（正确答案蕴含必需的瓶颈计算，如 Collatz 迭代、图遍历，无 CoT 时模型仅 1.5% 成功）与 Diversionary（误导性 hint，模型按 hint 回答则可知其遵循了 hint，无 hint 时仅 0.9% 复现该答案）。
- 开发自动化标注流水线：多轨道 LLM judge（Gemini 3 Flash 检索 + Gemini 3 Pro 验证，precision-first），对 6 类步骤类型标注，聚合为步骤级/CoT 级标签，人工验证精度 98.9% [96.6, 100]。
- 构建 BONAFIDE 基准：3,066 条 CoT（~4M tokens），1,120 CoT 级标签（15% Faithful/85% Unfaithful）+ 1,946 步骤级标签（51%/49%），覆盖 10 个开源模型、13 个任务，平衡模型与标签类型。
- 首次系统元评估 8 个主流忠实性指标：大多数接近随机，最佳 CoT 级 CC-SHAP 0.70 AUROC、最佳步骤级 Filler Tokens 0.59，二者互不迁移；IMP 类指标 90–96% 预测不忠实、SEM 类 94–96% 预测忠实；CC-SHAP 单实例耗时达 10³ 秒，不适合实时监控。

## Key Contributions

- Revises the definition of CoT faithfulness for the reasoning-model setting: Definition 1 (step is faithful iff it accurately describes a process that took place at some point in the model) and Definition 2 (CoT is faithful iff it contains a complete reasoning path the model followed and has no unfaithful steps), explicitly distinguishing faithfulness from the orthogonal properties of plausibility and importance.
- Designs two task families to elicit ground-truth faithfulness labels: Outright (correct answers require specific bottleneck computations, e.g., Collatz iteration, graph traversal — models succeed only 1.5% without a CoT) and Diversionary (misleading hints whose influence is detectable — models reproduce the hinted answer only 0.9% without the hint), both precluding guessing/shortcuts via procedurally generated novel tasks and large answer spaces.
- Develops an automated labeling pipeline: multi-track LLM judges (Gemini 3 Flash retrieval + Gemini 3 Pro validation, precision-first) labeling 6 step types (hint acknowledgment, faithful commitment, misattribution, bottleneck execution, tool call, inert), aggregated into step- and CoT-level labels; validated at 98.9% precision (95% CI [96.6, 100]) against human annotation, at ~$2,100 cost.
- Constructs BONAFIDE: 3,066 CoTs (~4M tokens), 1,120 CoT-level labels (15% Faithful / 85% Unfaithful) and 1,946 step-level labels (51% / 49%), spanning 10 open-weight models (4B–70B; Qwen3, OLMo 3, DeepSeek-R1-Distill, Llama 3.3, reasoning + non-reasoning variants) and 13 tasks, balanced across models and label types.
- Conducts the first systematic meta-evaluation of 8 prominent faithfulness metrics: most perform near chance; best CoT-level CC-SHAP 0.70 AUROC (but below random at step level, up to 10³ s/instance); best step-level Filler Tokens 0.59 AUROC (but near chance at CoT level); neither transfers across settings; importance-based metrics label 90–96% unfaithful, semantic-utility metrics 94–96% faithful on a balanced sample.

## Methodology

**Defining faithfulness (§2).** The authors revise Jacovi and Goldberg's [16] definition (designed for static post-hoc explanations) for contemporary reasoning-model CoTs. Definition 1 (Step Faithfulness): a step is faithful iff it accurately describes a process that took place at some point in the model — mechanistic reading, agnostic to when the computation is verbalized relative to execution; bare assertions and inert steps (e.g., "Hmm.") fall outside scope. Definition 2 (CoT Faithfulness): a CoT is faithful iff (1) it contains a complete reasoning path the model followed to reach its answer, and (2) it contains no unfaithful steps. They explicitly distinguish faithfulness from plausibility (a reasonable-sounding process, orthogonal to faithfulness — a convincing post-hoc rationalization is by definition highly plausible yet unfaithful) and importance (causal influence on the answer — a faithful dead-end exploration can be unimportant, and a fabricated justification the model conditions on can be important but unfaithful).

**Eliciting ground-truth labels (§3).** Two task settings exploit the observation that for certain task designs, the output reveals which intermediate computations must have produced it:

- **Outright.** Correct answers require specific intermediate computations ("bottleneck steps"). Ten procedurally generated task types spanning arithmetic, cryptography, text processing, graph traversal, and logical reasoning (e.g., iteratively apply the Collatz function and count steps to convergence; traverse a graph following a policy while updating running state). Novel tasks ensure bottleneck steps cannot be bypassed via memorization/shortcuts — models succeed only 1.5% when forced to answer without a CoT. Yields: Faithful Step (for each ground-truth step verbalized), Faithful CoT (contains all necessary steps), Unfaithful CoT (any ground-truth step missing).
- **Diversionary.** A question is given alongside a hint pointing to a random wrong answer. If the model answers per the hint, a step acknowledging the hint is faithful; omitting it is unfaithful; attributing it to a different source (e.g., "I saw in a history book...") is unfaithful (misattribution). Includes direct and indirect hints (the latter requiring intermediate computation, adding bottleneck steps). Uses open-ended questions with large answer spaces from Humanity's Last Exam [52], SimpleQA [53,54], and DDXPlus [55]; wrong answers generated by Gemini 3 Flash with PersonaHub personas for diversity; six hinting formats. Without the hint, models reproduce the hinted answer only 0.9%. Yields: Faithful Step, Unfaithful Step (misattribution, tool call), Faithful/Unfaithful CoT.

**Labeling pipeline (§4.1).** CoTs generated from 10 open-weight models (4B–70B; Qwen3, OLMo 3, DeepSeek-R1-Distill, Llama 3.3; reasoning + instruction-tuned variants where available). Only open-weight models are used because most metrics require model internals and the rest require editing the model's reasoning. Each model attempts all questions; only CoTs with correct (outright) or hint-matching (diversionary) answers are kept (these provide ground-truth signal). CoTs are split into sentence-level steps and passed through a multi-track label extraction pipeline: a retrieval LLM judge (Gemini 3 Flash) identifies candidate steps matching target definitions, then a validation LLM judge (Gemini 3 Pro) confirms/rejects — precision-first (false positives are costlier than missed labels since labels serve as ground truth). Six step types: hint acknowledgment, faithful commitment, misattribution (diversionary); bottleneck execution (outright); tool call (unfaithful — model claims external tools not available); inert (not evaluable). Step labels aggregate into CoT labels: Unfaithful CoT if any unfaithful step or missing bottleneck/acknowledgment; Faithful CoT if necessary faithful steps present and all others faithful or inert. Total judge cost ~$2,100; validated at 98.9% precision (95% CI [96.6, 100]) against human annotation.

**Benchmark (§4.2).** BONAFIDE: 3,066 CoTs (~4M tokens), 1,120 CoT-level labels (15% Faithful / 85% Unfaithful) and 1,946 step-level labels (51% Faithful Step / 49% Unfaithful Step), balanced across models and label types; unfiltered version also released.

**Metrics evaluated (§5.1).** Eight metrics across four families: Importance-based (IMP) — Adding Mistakes (inject errors into steps), Early Answering (truncate CoT), Filler Tokens (replace with uninformative tokens), SCM (causal mediation analysis); Parameter-based (PAR) — FUR (unlearn step information from parameters); Attribution-based (ATT) — CC-SHAP (SHAP-based importance of input tokens for answer vs. CoT); Semantic-utility (SEM) — Simulatability (give CoT to weaker model, test reproduction), Paraphrasing (paraphrase CoT, check answer stability). Baselines (BASE): Random (chance), LM Judge generic (LLM as CoT monitor without faithfulness definitions). Skyline (SKY): LM Judge with faithfulness definitions included (not a competing method since it has access to the same signal used to produce ground-truth labels). Evaluation: AUROC with 95% DeLong intervals, plus per-instance wall-clock time.

## Results & Highlights

- Most metrics perform near chance (Fig. 2a): CoT-level AUROC — CC-SHAP 0.70±0.04 (best), Simulatability 0.61±0.03, Paraphrasing 0.52±0.02, Adding Mistakes 0.51±0.02, Early Answering 0.51±0.01, Filler Tokens 0.50±0.02, FUR —, SCM 0.38±0.03; Random 0.5, LM Judge generic 0.87±0.01, LM Judge skyline 0.82±0.02. Step-level AUROC — Filler Tokens 0.59±0.01 (best), Early Answering 0.45±0.03, Adding Mistakes 0.51±0.04, FUR 0.52±0.02, CC-SHAP 0.41±0.03 (below random), Simulatability —, Paraphrasing —; Random 0.5, LM Judge generic 0.68±0.02, skyline 0.67±0.04.
- No transfer across settings: the best CoT-level metric (CC-SHAP, 0.70) drops below random at the step level (0.41); the best step-level metric (Filler Tokens, 0.59) is near chance at the CoT level (0.50). Metrics correlate weakly with one another (most Cohen's kappa ~0, some importance metrics reaching 0.35), suggesting they are not converging on the same underlying property.
- Strong prediction biases (Fig. 3, left): on a label-balanced subset, importance-based metrics (IMP) predict unfaithful for 90%–96% of CoT-level instances; semantic-utility metrics (SEM) predict faithful for 94%–96%. Rather than measuring faithfulness, these metrics appear to measure different underlying properties.
- Impractical for real-time monitoring (Fig. 2b): the highest-performing CoT metric, CC-SHAP, requires up to 10³ seconds per instance; most metrics also lose accuracy on longer CoTs (Fig. 3, right). Both effects compound the trend toward longer CoTs in modern reasoning LLMs.
- Benchmark scale and balance: 3,066 CoTs (~4M tokens), 1,120 CoT-level labels (15% Faithful / 85% Unfaithful — faithful CoTs are harder to certify since a single unfaithful step or missing bottleneck suffices for unfaithful), 1,946 step-level labels (51% / 49%), across 10 models and 13 tasks.
- Pipeline validation: automated labeling achieved 98.9% precision (95% CI [96.6, 100]) against human annotation at ~$2,100 judge cost.
- Task-design validation: in the outright setting, models succeed only 1.5% of the time when forced to answer without a CoT, confirming bottleneck steps cannot be bypassed; in the diversionary setting, models reproduce the hinted answer only 0.9% without the hint, confirming answers are unlikely on their own.
- Relation to prior findings (§5.3): confirms importance-based metrics systematically overestimate unfaithfulness (Fig. 3) and that metric performance does not scale clearly with model size; diverges from prior benchmarks on which metrics work (finds IMP metrics among the weakest, CC-SHAP strongest at CoT level) and does not find faithfulness detection harder in knowledge-intensive domains; finds reasoning-models/longer-CoTs-higher-faithfulness holds only for omission-type unfaithfulness, overtaken by commission-type (misattribution).

## Critical Assessment

The task-construction methodology for obtaining ground-truth faithfulness labels is clever and addresses a genuine gap (prior benchmarks conflate faithfulness with plausibility or importance), but several paper-specific weaknesses qualify the results. First, the ground-truth labels cover only specific steps the authors know had to have occurred — the authors concede "any given CoT will contain many additional steps for which our setting provides no signal." This precision-first design means BONAFIDE evaluates metrics on a narrow, constructed slice of faithfulness (bottleneck steps in procedural tasks, hint-following in diversionary tasks) rather than the full diversity of naturalistic reasoning where faithfulness matters most (e.g., multi-step planning, ethical reasoning, scientific hypothesis formation). Second, the CoT-level label distribution is heavily skewed (15% Faithful / 85% Unfaithful) precisely because labeling a CoT faithful requires every step to be faithful or inert while a single unfaithful step suffices for unfaithful — the authors acknowledge "Faithful CoTs are therefore harder to certify than unfaithful ones," which may systematically disadvantage metrics that attempt holistic faithfulness assessment. Third, the outright tasks (Collatz, graph traversal, cryptography) are procedurally generated and mechanistic; the diversionary tasks rely on misleading hints, an artificial injection that may not reflect the spontaneous unfaithfulness (post-hoc rationalization, hidden bias influence) that arises in natural deployment — so the metrics' near-chance performance on BONAFIDE may overstate or understate their real-world failure. Fourth, the skyline LM Judge (with faithfulness definitions) scores 0.82–0.87 AUROC, far above any evaluated metric, but the authors explicitly do not treat it as a competing method because it has access to the same signal used to construct the ground-truth labels — this is a form of leakage that makes the skyline an unfair upper bound and leaves open whether a properly isolated LLM-judge approach could be a practical metric. Fifth, only open-weight models (4B–70B) are used; the authors note closed models are incompatible with most metrics (which require internals or reasoning edits), so the evaluation says nothing about faithfulness metrics applied to frontier closed reasoning models (e.g., GPT-5, Claude Opus reasoning models) where CoT faithfulness concerns are most acute. Sixth, the study evaluates existing metrics but proposes no improved metric; the negative result is valuable but leaves practitioners with no actionable alternative.

## Strengths

- Principled, clearly stated definitions (Definitions 1–2) that cleanly separate faithfulness from plausibility and importance, resolving a conflation pervasive in prior work.
- Novel task-construction methodology (outright bottleneck steps + diversionary hints) that yields genuine ground-truth labels without relying on proxies orthogonal to faithfulness — a first for CoT faithfulness evaluation.
- High-precision automated labeling pipeline (98.9% precision, validated against human annotation) at modest cost (~$2,100), enabling scalable benchmark construction.
- Comprehensive meta-evaluation of 8 prominent metrics across 4 families, reporting AUROC with DeLong CIs and per-instance wall-clock time — exposing both accuracy failure and computational impracticality.
- Honest, valuable negative results: most metrics near chance, strong prediction biases (IMP 90–96% unfaithful, SEM 94–96% faithful), no cross-setting transfer, CC-SHAP up to 10³ s/instance.
- Public release of benchmark, code, and unfiltered dataset enables community follow-up.
- Replicates and extends prior findings (importance-metric unfaithful skew, no clear model-size scaling) and openly diverges where BONAFIDE contradicts prior benchmarks.

## Limitations

- Ground-truth labels cover only specific known-required steps; many CoT steps have no signal, so the benchmark evaluates a narrow slice of faithfulness rather than holistic naturalistic reasoning.
- CoT-level label distribution is heavily skewed (15% Faithful / 85% Unfaithful) due to the precision-first certification asymmetry, which may systematically disadvantage holistic metrics.
- Tasks are constructed (procedural bottleneck tasks + artificial hint injection) and may not represent the spontaneous unfaithfulness (post-hoc rationalization, hidden bias) that arises in natural deployment.
- The skyline LM Judge is an unfair upper bound (access to ground-truth-construction signal), leaving open whether an isolated LLM-judge metric could be practical.
- Only open-weight models (4B–70B) evaluated; closed frontier reasoning models (where CoT faithfulness concerns are most acute) are incompatible with most metrics and untested.
- No improved metric proposed; the negative result leaves practitioners without an actionable alternative.
- Efficiency measured in wall-clock seconds (not just call/token cost), which is sensitive to hardware and provider load, though the 10³ s/instance for CC-SHAP is clearly impractical regardless.

## Relevance

直接对应「LLM Hidden States & Interpretability」方向：BONAFIDE 首次用 ground-truth 标签系统评估 CoT 忠实性度量指标，揭示现有指标大多接近随机且存在强预测偏差，对理解推理模型内部计算是否被 CoT 真实反映这一核心可解释性问题提供了关键基准与警示；其"忠实性 ≠ plausibility/importance"的概念厘清与任务构造方法论，也触及「LLM Inference Evaluation」中关于推理过程评估方法可靠性的议题，以及「ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations」中关于度量指标本身有效性的数学基础问题。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the task-construction methodology extend to more naturalistic reasoning settings (multi-step planning, ethical reasoning, scientific hypothesis formation) where the required intermediate computations are less mechanistically determined?
- Would an ensemble of existing metrics (e.g., IMP + SEM + ATT signals combined) outperform any single metric, and can it do so without the 10³ s/instance cost?
- Can a properly isolated LLM-judge metric (without access to ground-truth-construction signal) approach the skyline's 0.82–0.87 AUROC, and would that be a practical substitute for existing metrics?
- How do the findings change for closed frontier reasoning models (GPT-5, Claude Opus) where internals are inaccessible — can any faithfulness metric operate in a black-box-only regime?
- Does the 15%/85% faithful/unfaithful skew at the CoT level reflect a genuine property of model reasoning or an artifact of the precision-first certification rule, and would a recall-balanced labeling scheme change metric rankings?
- Can the bottleneck-step and hint-injection task designs be adapted to measure faithfulness of training-time reasoning (e.g., during RLHF/GRPO) rather than only inference-time CoT?
