The Transformer residual stream is a continuous field — patching is source insertion, propagation follows Green functions.

Continuous-Depth Field Theory for Transformer Patching and Mechanistic Interpretability David N. Olivieri, Antonio F. Pérez Rodríguez 2026-05-24

Why This Matters
Unifies fragmented patching methods under one mathematical language, enabling systematic prediction of how interventions propagate inside Transformers.
Critical Edge
Whether the framework yields actionable improvements over existing methods or merely offers new notation for known phenomena remains unproven.

Methodology

The residual stream is modeled as a continuous field h(ℓ, t) over depth ℓ and token position t. Four constructs operationalize the framework:

  1. Source insertion — activation patching = adding a localized delta δh(ℓ₀, t₀) to the field.
  2. Sensitivity fields — the Jacobian of logit-difference response w.r.t. residual perturbations, yielding maps across the depth-token plane.
  3. Empirical Green functions — Green-operator slices describe how a perturbation at one site propagates to all downstream sites.
  4. Adjoint variational formulation — optimal patch-site selection cast as an adjoint optimization problem.

Results

  • A bounded local linear regime exists where first-order sensitivity predictions match actual patch effects.
  • Patch effects propagate anisotropically — structured differently across depth layers vs. token positions.
  • High-sensitivity sites and sliced Green operators provide compact response descriptions.
  • Prompt-induced residual displacements can transfer answer behavior across prompts, suggesting shared residual-space geometry.

The formalism (field theory, Green functions, adjoint methods) draws on established physics. Empirical validation on GPT-2 is standard and reproducible. Novelty relative to prior linear-response work (nRTH, causal scrubbing, path patching) requires the full paper to evaluate.

Strengths

  • Unifies patching, causal tracing, path patching, and steering under a single mathematical language.
  • Rigorous tools from physics (Green functions, variational methods) applied with care.
  • Sensitivity fields as predictive tools are intuitive and potentially powerful.
  • Standard, widely-studied model (GPT-2) for empirical validation.

Limitations

  • Abstract-only — full experimental breadth and baseline comparisons unavailable.
  • Practical advantage over existing methods (causal tracing, path patching) not quantified.
  • Continuous-depth abstraction may introduce uncharacterized approximation errors.
  • Generalization beyond GPT-2 to larger or differently-architected models not discussed.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How large is the bounded local linear regime, and how does it vary across tasks, layers, and model scales?
  • Can the adjoint variational formulation automatically identify optimal patch sites matching or exceeding exhaustive search?
  • Does the Green-function framework generalize to individual attention heads and MLP sublayers?
  • How does the framework perform on larger models (LLaMA, GPT-3 scale) where exhaustive patching is prohibitive?
  • Can cross-prompt transfer of residual displacements be leveraged for controllable generation or safety applications?
