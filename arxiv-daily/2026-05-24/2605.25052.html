Meta-Evaluation

Faithfulness Metrics Don't Measure Faithfulness

Most tools for verifying whether a model's chain-of-thought actually reflects its internal reasoning perform near chance. The best one only reaches 0.70 AUROC.

Yoav Gur-Arieh· Ana Marasović· Mor Geva· 2026-05-24· arXiv 2605.25052
Key Metric 0.70 AUROC (CoT level) Best metric out of all evaluated. Step-level best is just 0.59. Most metrics ≈ chance.
Why This Matters The tools we rely on to verify LLM reasoning transparency are themselves unreliable Undermines the entire evaluation paradigm for chain-of-thought interpretability.
Critical Edge Ground-truth labels come from constructed tasks — generalizability to real-world CoT is unproven

Methodology

The authors design tasks where the output reveals which intermediate computations must have occurred, enabling automated generation of ground-truth faithfulness labels at both the step and CoT level. This yields BonaFide: 3,066 labeled CoTs across 13 tasks and 10 models. Existing faithfulness metrics are then evaluated against these labels — a first-of-its-kind meta-evaluation.

Results

  • Most metrics perform near chance at distinguishing faithful from unfaithful CoTs
  • Best CoT-level metric: 0.70 AUROC; best step-level: 0.59
  • Strong prediction biases across metrics
  • Performance degrades on longer chains of thought
  • No single metric transfers well across settings or tasks
  • Better-performing metrics carry prohibitively high compute cost

Critical Assessment

Novelty First systematic meta-evaluation with ground truth
Rigor Clever task-construction avoids flawed proxies
Significance Fundamentally challenges current paradigm
ConcernConstructed-task labels may not generalize to naturalistic CoT

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Addresses a fundamental, timely problem
  • Principled ground-truth generation without flawed proxies
  • Comprehensive: multiple metrics, tasks, and models
  • Clear negative results that challenge community assumptions

Limitations

  • Constructed tasks may not represent real-world CoT diversity
  • Evaluates existing metrics but proposes no better alternative
  • Gap between constructed-task and naturalistic faithfulness unexplored

Open Questions

Can task-construction methodology extend to open-ended, naturalistic reasoning?
What architectural or training changes could improve CoT faithfulness itself?
Can signal combinations from multiple metrics outperform any single one?
How do findings change with newer, more capable models?
