> **一句话总结：** 本文发现数据标签在NTK特征向量上的投影与对应特征值呈二次方关系（Label-NTK alignment: $(v_i^\top y)^2 \sim \lambda_i^2$），训练残差呈线性关系（Residual-NTK alignment: $(v_i^\top r)^2 \sim \lambda_i$），利用这些对齐性质推导出依赖完整特征谱的收敛界 $L(w_t) \sim \mathrm{tr}[(I-\eta K)^{2t}K]$，大幅缩紧了经典依赖最小特征值的NTK收敛理论，并在泛化界上也取得了改进。

> **TL;DR:** Classical NTK convergence bounds depend on the minimum eigenvalue $\lambda_{\min}$ and are far too pessimistic. This paper discovers that label vectors and training residuals align with the NTK eigenspectrum — projections onto eigenvectors scale as $(v_i^\top y)^2 \sim \lambda_i^2$ and $(v_i^\top r)^2 \sim \lambda_i$. Exploiting these alignments yields a convergence bound $L(w_t) \sim \mathrm{tr}[(I-\eta K)^{2t}K]$ that closely tracks empirical training curves across MLPs and CNNs, plus improved generalization bounds $L_{\text{test}} \leq O(\mathrm{tr}[K]/n)$.

## 核心贡献

- **发现 Label-NTK 对齐现象**：在 log-log 刻度下，$(v_i^\top y)^2$ 与 $\lambda_i$ 呈线性关系，斜率约为 2，即 $(v_i^\top y)^2 \sim \lambda_i^2$。标签能量集中在大特征值方向，与小特征值方向近正交。
- **发现 Residual-NTK 对齐现象**：训练残差 $r = f - y$ 在 NTK 特征向量上的投影 $(v_i^\top r)^2 \sim \lambda_i$（斜率约 1），且该对齐在整个训练过程中持续保持。
- **理论解释对齐现象**：在数据输入单位长度、非退化、真实标签函数 Lipschitz 连续的温和假设下，严格证明了对齐关系 $(v^\top y)^2 \sim \lambda^2 = O(\theta^2)$，精确预测了指数 2。
- **推导更紧的收敛界**：基于 Residual-NTK 对齐，得到 $L(w_t) \sim \mathrm{tr}[(I-\eta K)^{2t}K]$，依赖完整特征谱而非仅 $\lambda_{\min}$，上下界同阶说明最优性。
- **改进泛化界**：利用 Label-NTK 对齐，将 test loss 上界从 $O(\sqrt{y^\top K^{-1} y/n})$ 改进为 $O(\mathrm{tr}[K]/n)$。
- **广泛的实验验证**：在五层 MLP（width=512）和五层 CNN（32-64-128-128-256 channels）上，于 MNIST、Fashion-MNIST、SVHN、CIFAR-10、Tiny-ImageNet10 数据集验证。

## Key Contributions

- **Label-NTK alignment**: Empirically discovers and theoretically justifies that $(v_i^\top y)^2 \sim \lambda_i^2$ across the NTK eigenspectrum (excluding top few eigenvalues), meaning labels concentrate energy on high-eigenvalue directions and are near-orthogonal to low-eigenvalue directions.
- **Residual-NTK alignment**: Shows that training residuals satisfy $(v_i^\top r)^2 \sim \lambda_i$ (scaling exponent ≈ 1), and this alignment persists throughout training even at finite width.
- **Theoretical proof of alignment**: Under mild assumptions (unit-norm inputs, non-degenerate data, Lipschitz ground-truth label function), proves the exponent-2 scaling for the two-point case (Theorem 3.2) and extends to $n$-point datasets (Theorem 3.4).
- **Tighter convergence bound** (Theorem 4.5): $\frac{c-\delta}{2}\mathrm{tr}[(I-\eta K)^{2t}K] - \frac{\epsilon}{2} \leq L(w_t) \leq \frac{c+\delta}{2}\mathrm{tr}[(I-\eta K)^{2t}K] + \frac{\epsilon}{2}$, with matching upper/lower bounds suggesting optimality.
- **Improved generalization bound** (Theorem 5.2): Test loss upper bounded by $O(\mathrm{tr}[K]/n)$, improving over the prior $O(\sqrt{y^\top K^{-1}y/n})$ by exploiting Label-NTK alignment.
- **Time complexity** (Theorem 4.8): Under power-law eigenvalue decay $\lambda_i \sim i^{-q}$, achieves loss $\epsilon$ in $T_\epsilon = \min\{O(\frac{1}{\eta}\log\frac{1}{\epsilon}),\; O(\frac{q}{1+q-1}(\frac{n}{\eta\epsilon})^{1/q})\}$ iterations, tighter than the $O(\frac{1}{\eta\lambda_{\min}}\log\frac{1}{\epsilon})$ from classical analysis when $\epsilon \gg \lambda_{\min}$.

## Methodology

The paper works within the standard NTK framework for over-parameterized neural networks with MSE/squared loss and gradient descent.

**Step 1: Empirical discovery of alignments.** The authors compute the NTK eigendecomposition $K = \sum_i \lambda_i v_i v_i^\top$ at initialization and during training, then plot $(v_i^\top y)^2$ vs. $\lambda_i$ and $(v_i^\top r)^2$ vs. $\lambda_i$ on log-log scales. Across MLPs and CNNs on CIFAR-10, MNIST, SVHN, Fashion-MNIST, and Tiny-ImageNet10, they observe clean linear relationships with slopes ≈ 2 and ≈ 1 respectively, persisting across training time steps.

**Step 2: Theoretical justification.** Under three data assumptions (unit-norm inputs $\|x_i\|=1$, non-degenerate inputs $x_i \not\parallel x_j$, and Lipschitz ground-truth function $|y_i - y_j| \leq L_y\|x_i - x_j\|$):
- For $n=2$: Using the NTK structure for infinitely wide fully-connected networks from prior work (Liu et al. 2025), shows $\lambda_{\min} = O(\theta)$ and $(v_{\min}^\top y)^2 = O(\theta^2)$, yielding the exponent-2 scaling (Theorem 3.2).
- For $n > 2$: Shows that for any pair of close data points, there exists an eigenvector-eigenvalue pair satisfying $(v^\top y)^2 \sim \lambda^2 = O(\theta^2)$ (Theorem 3.4).

**Step 3: Refined convergence analysis.** Formalizes Residual-NTK alignment as Condition 4.2: $(c-\delta)\lambda_i \leq (v_i^\top r_0)^2 \leq (c+\delta)\lambda_i$ for constants $c \gg \delta > 0$. Under this condition plus standard smoothness assumptions, proves that for sufficiently wide networks, the training loss satisfies the spectrum-aware bound $\mathrm{tr}[(I-\eta K)^{2t}K]$ (Theorem 4.5). The proof uses Taylor expansion of the network dynamics around initialization, bounds on NTK deviation and Hessian terms, and the alignment condition to replace $\lambda_{\min}$-dependence with full-spectrum trace.

**Step 4: Generalization improvement.** Plugs Label-NTK alignment into the existing generalization framework of Arora et al. (2019), converting $y^\top K^{-1} y = \sum_i \lambda_i^{-1}(v_i^\top y)^2 = c\sum_i \lambda_i = c\cdot\mathrm{tr}[K]$, yielding the tighter bound $O(\mathrm{tr}[K]/n)$.

## Results & Highlights

- The new convergence bound $\mathrm{tr}[(I-\eta K)^{2t}K]$ closely matches empirical training loss curves, while the classical bound $\exp(-\eta\lambda_{\min}t)L(w_0)$ remains nearly flat (Figure 1). This is demonstrated across all tested settings.
- The alignment phenomena are remarkably consistent: log-log plots show clean linear relationships with slopes ≈ 2 (Label-NTK) and ≈ 1 (Residual-NTK) across 5 datasets and 2 architectures (MLP and CNN), at multiple training timestamps.
- The alignments persist during training even at finite width, suggesting they are a robust structural property, not just an initialization artifact.
- Under power-law eigenvalue decay (well-established for NTKs), the time complexity to reach loss $\epsilon$ is significantly tighter than classical bounds, especially when $\epsilon \gg \lambda_{\min}$ (the typical practical regime).
- The generalization bound $O(\mathrm{tr}[K]/n)$ is independent of $\lambda_{\min}$, removing a key source of looseness from prior NTK generalization theory.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** While prior work (Arora et al. 2019, Bordelon et al. 2020) noted that labels have larger components along top eigenvalues, this paper is the first to provide a precise quantitative characterization of the alignment across the entire spectrum and rigorously exploit it for tighter bounds. The dual alignment (labels with exponent 2, residuals with exponent 1) is a novel and non-trivial finding.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Complete proofs are provided. The convergence proof (Appendix A.2) carefully handles the deviation from linear dynamics through Taylor expansion bounds on NTK change and Hessian terms. The alignment proof for $n=2$ is clean; the $n>2$ extension is more involved but solid.
- **Reproducibility: High.** All architectures, hyperparameters, and datasets are specified. The alignment experiments (eigen-decomposition + projection) are straightforward to replicate.
- **Significance: Moderate-to-high for NTK theory.** This substantially resolves a well-known embarrassment of NTK theory — that its convergence predictions are far too pessimistic. The gap to practical relevance remains: the NTK regime doesn't capture feature learning, and the authors acknowledge this limitation explicitly.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - The theoretical justification of alignment (Theorems 3.2, 3.4) proves $(v^\top y)^2 \sim \lambda^2$ for specific small eigenvalues arising from close data pairs, but does not prove the alignment holds uniformly across the entire spectrum. The empirical universality of the alignment remains partially unexplained.
  - The Residual-NTK alignment (exponent 1) is assumed as a condition (Condition 4.2) rather than proven. The paper argues it follows from Lipschitz continuity of the network at initialization, but does not provide a formal proof.
  - The required width $m = \tilde{\Omega}(n^{6L+2}/(\lambda_{\min}^2\epsilon^2))$ still depends on $\lambda_{\min}$, so the improvement is in the convergence rate, not in the width requirement.

## Strengths

- Directly addresses the most salient weakness of NTK optimization theory — overly pessimistic convergence bounds — with a principled solution.
- The alignment framework is conceptually clean and intuitive: labels naturally concentrate on directions of high similarity (large eigenvalues) because similar inputs have similar labels (Lipschitz continuity).
- Comprehensive empirical validation across 5 datasets and 2 architectures strengthens the claims.
- The matching upper and lower bounds in Theorem 4.5 suggest the result is tight, not just an improvement.
- The generalization bound improvement comes almost for free once the Label-NTK alignment is established, showing the broad utility of the finding.

## Limitations

- **NTK regime only**: The theory applies to sufficiently wide networks with small learning rates, excluding feature learning dynamics, edge of stability, and catapult effects. The authors acknowledge this but argue NTK theory remains a valuable foundation.
- **Residual-NTK alignment is assumed, not proven**: Condition 4.2 is empirically well-supported but lacks the formal justification that Label-NTK alignment receives.
- **Spectrum-wide alignment proof incomplete**: Theorems 3.2 and 3.4 prove alignment for specific eigenvalues from close data pairs, but do not establish the uniform alignment across all eigenvalues that the experiments show.
- **Width still depends on $\lambda_{\min}$**: While the convergence rate no longer depends on $\lambda_{\min}$, the required network width $m = \tilde{\Omega}(\lambda_{\min}^{-2})$ still does.
- **Classification vs. regression gap**: The Lipschitz assumption on labels is natural for regression but requires margin assumptions for classification (which the authors mention but don't develop).

## Relevance

本文通过发现标签与 NTK 特征谱的定量对齐关系来缩紧收敛界和泛化界，揭示了实践中过参数化网络快速收敛的理论机制，与深度学习优化理论、神经切核理论以及泛化理论的研究方向直接相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the Residual-NTK alignment (exponent 1) be formally proven under the same mild assumptions as the Label-NTK alignment, rather than assumed as a condition?
- Do the alignment phenomena persist for attention-based architectures (Transformers, ViTs) where the NTK structure may differ significantly from MLPs/CNNs?
- Can the alignment insights be used to design adaptive learning rate schedules that further accelerate convergence?
- Is there a connection between these alignment phenomena and feature learning dynamics in the finite-width, large-learning-rate regime (e.g., progressive sharpening at the edge of stability)?
- Could the spectrum-aware convergence analysis be extended to stochastic gradient descent, where mini-batch noise interacts with the alignment structure?
- Does the alignment degrade systematically with dataset complexity (e.g., high-dimensional image data vs. low-dimensional structured data)?
