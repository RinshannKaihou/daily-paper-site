Self-improving reasoning models can get better & faster simultaneously

HSIR recovers partial-correct reasoning from failed solutions and filters redundant "overthinking" via hidden-state entropy—achieving dual gains in accuracy and efficiency without external models or annotations.

Key Metric
+10.9% accuracy
−42.4% inference cost
HSIR-DPO on Qwen2.5-7B: 73.35% vs 62.45% baseline. Token usage drops from 1,536 → 885.
Why This Matters
Breaks the accuracy-vs-efficiency tradeoff that defines reasoning model training.
Two orthogonal mechanisms (data quantity + data quality) that compose across SFT, DPO, and GRPO paradigms.
Critical Edge
InDiv may flag essential math steps as "overthinking"; GRPO runs under-trained at only 300 steps.
Semantically similar consecutive steps in rigorous derivations could be incorrectly pruned. Threshold τ is sensitive.
Methodology
  • VeriExit — Scans failed solutions for intermediate steps that contain the ground-truth answer; truncates and resamples (J=5) from that checkpoint, recovering correct trajectories for hard queries at no extra full-pass cost.
  • InDiv — Extracts hidden representations from the model's middle layer, computes semantic entropy via singular values of the cross-covariance matrix with attention-aware weighting. Low scores → overthinking. Threshold τ = −0.5 filters redundant training samples.
  • H-GRPO — Adds normalized InDiv as an auxiliary reward (ω=0.2) alongside accuracy (1.0) and format rewards, encouraging diverse reasoning paths via RL rather than naive length penalties.
  • Iterative loop: self-generate K=10 candidates → VeriExit resampling → InDiv filtering → train (SFT/DPO/GRPO) → repeat.
Results & Highlights
  • +10.9% avg accuracy (HSIR-DPO, Qwen2.5-7B)
  • −42.4% inference tokens (HSIR-SFT iter 3)
  • HSIR-DPO outperforms IRPO by +5.35%
  • H-GRPO beats GRPO, Long2Short, CosFn on all model sizes
  • Strong OOD: MedQA→GPQA 23.66% → 34.60%
  • Works with DeepSeek-R1 or QWQ-32B seed data
  • Validated on 6 architectures, 9 benchmarks
Critical Assessment
  • Novelty: High — first use of internal hidden states to guide concise reasoning during self-improvement
  • Rigor: High — 6 models, 9 benchmarks, ablations, OOD, parameter sensitivity
  • Reproducibility: High — public models, standard frameworks, clear hyperparams
  • Significance: High — dual accuracy+efficiency gains are rare and valuable
Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Simultaneously improves accuracy and efficiency—rare in the literature
  • Two simple, orthogonal mechanisms that compose well across paradigms
  • Universally effective on 6 architectures without per-model tuning
  • Uses model's own hidden states—zero external cost
  • H-GRPO reward design inherently prevents reward hacking

Limitations

  • Semantically similar consecutive steps in math could be misclassified as redundancy
  • VeriExit cannot help with "absolutely hard" queries beyond model capability
  • HSIR-SFT + H-GRPO combination adds significant compute overhead
  • GRPO runs at only 300 steps—full convergence unknown
  • Filtering threshold τ requires tuning; performance is sensitive to it
Open Questions
  1. How does InDiv perform on non-reasoning tasks where "overthinking" is harder to define?
  2. Can VeriExit pair with process reward models for finer step-level verification?
  3. Do InDiv scores converge or degrade over many GRPO training steps?
  4. How does HSIR scale to 32B / 72B models?
  5. Could InDiv serve as an inference-time signal for dynamic early exiting?
