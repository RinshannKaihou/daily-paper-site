> **一句话总结：** RouteScan提出一种非侵入式审计框架，利用MoE模型在prefill阶段GPU线程分配产生的专家路由遥测指纹检测恶意输入，在未见过的有害领域上AUROC超过0.93，同时通过聚合遥测天然抵抗提示词逆推，兼顾安全审计与用户隐私。

> **TL;DR:** RouteScan audits MoE LLM safety non-intrusively by using GPU-level expert routing telemetry (active thread counts during prefilling) as micro-architectural fingerprints to detect harmful prompts. It achieves AUROC >0.93 on unseen harmful domains and >0.96 on novel jailbreak wrappers across two open-source MoE models, while aggregated telemetry resists prompt inversion (Token F1=0.16, zero sensitive-field recovery).

## 核心贡献

- 首次识别了MoE模型的一个新型执行侧审计面：稀疏专家路由产生的输入依赖型GPU线程分配模式可作为检测恶意请求的微架构指纹。
- 提出RouteScan框架，构建了包含细粒度专家负载分布与粗粒度层级结构统计的统一遥测表示，结合混合特征评分与自适应支撑选择机制，实现了跨域鲁棒的线性检测。
- 在两种不同路由设计的开源MoE模型（DeepSeek-V2-Lite-Chat、Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B-Chat）和两种GPU（A6000、5880）上全面验证，跨域有害审计AUROC >0.93，跨越狱封装迁移AUROC >0.96。
- 通过端到端逆推测试和属性探针验证了聚合prefill遥测的隐私边界：Token F1仅0.16，敏感字段恢复为零，证明其在审计与隐私之间取得了实用平衡。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies a previously unexplored execution-side auditing surface in MoE models: input-dependent GPU thread allocation patterns from sparse expert routing serve as discriminative micro-architectural fingerprints for harmful input detection.
- Proposes RouteScan, a unified telemetry representation combining fine-grained expert-load distributions with coarse-grained layer-level structural statistics, paired with a Hybrid Feature Scoring and Adaptive Support mechanism for robust cross-domain linear detection.
- Conducts comprehensive evaluation on two open-source MoE LLMs with distinct routing designs (DeepSeek-V2-Lite-Chat, Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B-Chat) across two GPU types (NVIDIA RTX A6000, 5880), achieving AUROC >0.93 on unseen harmful domains and >0.96 on novel jailbreak wrappers.
- Validates the privacy boundary of aggregated prefilling-phase telemetry through end-to-end inversion tests (Token F1=0.16, zero sensitive-field recovery) and targeted attribute probing, demonstrating practical privacy preservation.

## Methodology

RouteScan operates in three stages within a two-phase pipeline (offline training + online auditing):

**Stage 1 — Request-level Telemetry Representation.** During the prefilling phase, RouteScan records the number of active GPU threads allocated to each expert module. Two complementary feature views are constructed:
- *Fine-grained expert-load distributions:* Per-layer normalized load vectors capturing the proportion of workload handled by each expert.
- *Coarse-grained layer-level structural statistics:* Four metrics per layer — activation coverage rate, effective activation rate (via Shannon entropy), coverage gap, and coverage concentration — capturing routing breadth, load spread, and load concentration.

These are concatenated into a unified representation R of dimension d = Σ E_l + 4L.

**Stage 2 — Hybrid Feature Scoring and Adaptive Support.** Each telemetry dimension receives a reliability score ρ_j = M_disc(j) × M_cons(j) × M_prior(j), combining discriminative evidence, cross-source stability, and conservative selector-side priors (including layer-dependent prior and boundary-sensitivity penalty). An entropy-based adaptive support mechanism uses the dispersion of the score-mass distribution to automatically determine how many dimensions to retain, with soft reweighting within the selected support set S.

**Stage 3 — Linear Risk Detection with Calibration.** A lightweight logistic regression detector operates on transformed features restricted to S. Source-adaptive regularization selects inverse L2 strength C based on weakest positive separation and positive-subset disparity from source validation margins. Platt scaling maps raw detector margins to source-calibrated harmful-risk scores.

The entire pipeline is trained exclusively on source data; target data is reserved strictly for evaluation.

## Results & Highlights

**Cross-domain harmful auditing (leave-one-target-out):**
- All AUROC scores exceed 0.93 across four harmful-content benchmarks (AdvBench, StrongREJECT, CatHarmfulQA, HarmBench-Std).
- Best performance on AdvBench: AUROC up to 0.9979 (DeepSeek/A6000).
- StrongReject and CatHarmfulQA are the most challenging targets, but AUROC remains above 0.93.
- Fixed-threshold F1@0.5 and Acc@0.5 generally strong (0.85–0.95), with source-calibrated threshold providing a usable default.

**Mixed-positive family transfer (unseen jailbreak wrappers):**
- AUROC >0.96 on unseen jailbreak wrappers (PAIR, CodeChameleon) when trained with mixed harmful + AutoDAN source.
- Seen AutoDAN wrapper yields near-perfect AUROC (up to 1.0000).
- Strong wrapper transfer even when source benchmark is StrongReject (AUROC 0.954–0.994).

**Privacy boundary validation:**
- End-to-end telemetry-to-text inversion: Token F1=0.1601, Jaccard=0.1116, ROUGE-L=0.1194.
- Exact sensitive-field recovery: 0.0000 across all field types.
- LLM-based semantic audit: 0/54 sensitive facts recovered.
- Coarse-attribute probing: LOSO AUROC drops below 0.45 for acute/recent and high severity (confounding removed), while oncology-related shows limited but measurable signal (LOSO AUROC=0.746, low high-confidence precision).

**Deployment overhead:** Online annotation ~2.7% overhead; current Nsight-based trace collection ~17.8%.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. Using aggregated GPU-level expert routing telemetry for safety auditing is a genuinely novel contribution. The insight that prefilling-phase thread allocation patterns encode harmful-intent signals — while being structurally non-invertible — is creative and well-motivated. The tripartite ecosystem (Users/Service Providers/Regulators) threat model is thoughtful.
- **Rigor:** High. Experiments span two MoE architectures with distinct routing designs, two GPU types, four harmful benchmarks, three jailbreak wrappers, and multiple evaluation protocols. The paired benign sample construction (replacing malicious keywords while preserving syntax) is a strong methodological choice for isolating semantic intent from surface form.
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate. Requires GPU telemetry collection infrastructure (Nsight Systems profiler) at the thread level. Open-source models help, but the telemetry acquisition layer adds engineering complexity beyond standard ML experimentation.
- **Significance:** High. The paper establishes a new auditing paradigm that resolves the tension between safety auditing and user privacy. If the approach scales to larger production MoE models, it has significant practical impact for AI governance.
- **Red flags:** (1) Evaluated only on relatively small MoE models (DeepSeek-V2-Lite, Qwen1.5-MoE-A2.7B); scalability to large-scale MoE (Llama 4, Gemini) is uncertain. (2) Non-adaptive attacker assumption is acknowledged but limiting. (3) The 17.8% trace-collection overhead from Nsight is non-trivial for production deployment. (4) The privacy analysis, while thorough for the evaluated attackers, does not guarantee against future stronger inversion methods.

## Strengths

- Novel side-channel approach that avoids the privacy-safety tension in content-based auditing.
- Strong cross-domain generalization (AUROC >0.93) without target-domain data.
- Robust transfer to unseen jailbreak wrappers (AUROC >0.96).
- Careful privacy boundary analysis with multiple attack pathways (inversion, attribute probing, LLM audit).
- Lightweight linear detector — minimal computational overhead for online auditing (~2.7%).
- Evaluated across multiple model architectures, GPU types, and adversarial settings.
- Paired benign sample construction rigorously isolates semantic intent from syntactic confounds.

## Limitations

- Requires access to low-level GPU thread allocation data via Nsight profiler; not trivially deployable in cloud API or multi-tenant GPU environments.
- Evaluated only on relatively small MoE models (7B-parameter class); scalability to production-scale MoE models (hundreds of experts) is untested.
- Only operates during the prefilling phase; cannot leverage decoding-phase signals or cached/prefix-cached inference.
- Non-adaptive attacker assumption acknowledged as a limitation; gray-box adaptive attacks via surrogate models remain an open challenge.
- Trace-collection overhead of ~17.8% (Nsight) is significant for production serving; custom telemetry collection is needed.
- The approach is fundamentally tied to MoE sparse routing and does not transfer to dense LLMs.
- Privacy guarantee is empirical, not formal — stronger inversion attacks could potentially extract more information.

## Relevance

本文开创性地将GPU硬件遥测侧信道与MoE模型安全审计结合，在"审计即不侵入"的新范式下实现了强泛化的恶意输入检测。对于关注LLM部署安全治理、MoE架构侧信道分析以及隐私保护技术的研究者具有高度参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How would adaptive gray-box attackers with surrogate MoE models perform in crafting prompts that evade RouteScan by mimicking benign telemetry distributions?
- Can RouteScan scale to production MoE models with hundreds of experts (e.g., Llama 4 Scout with 16 experts, or larger architectures), and how does detection performance change with expert count?
- What is the minimum telemetry granularity needed for effective detection — could coarser signals (e.g., per-layer total thread counts) suffice while further reducing privacy risk?
- Could the prefilling-phase telemetry approach be combined with decoding-phase signals for higher detection accuracy, without reintroducing the privacy vulnerabilities of per-token traces (>91% reconstruction)?
- How would RouteScan perform under distribution shift from fine-tuning or model updates, and can the calibration mechanism adapt without full retraining?
