Inference Time Optimization with Confidence Dynamics

Yu Wang, Minghao Liu, Jiayun Wang, Jinrui Huang, Ankit Shah, Wei Wei · Center for Advanced AI, Accenture
Correct reasoning traces gain confidence from head to tail; incorrect ones lose it. Exploiting this divergence, CDG voting weights answers by their confidence trajectory — outperforming majority voting by 5.4 points without any retraining.
Key Metric
+5.4%
avg accuracy gain over majority voting
across 4 models × 4 math benchmarks
(15 / 16 configs improved)
Why This Matters
A training-free method that extracts more correct answers from the same LLM outputs — no retraining, no reward model, just smarter vote weighting.
Critical Edge
  • β needs per-model calibration via validation set
  • Evaluated only on competition math benchmarks
  • Requires top-K logprob access at every decode step
Methodology

Confidence Metric

Token-level confidence via KL divergence from uniform over top-K token probabilities (K=20). Traces are split into N=10 position-normalized bins.

Confidence Dynamic Gain

Tail confidence minus head confidence per trace. This single scalar captures whether a reasoning chain "gains conviction" as it progresses.

ΔC = C̄ᵗᵃⁱˡ − C̄ʰᵉᵃᵈ

Voting Score

Each trace scored by combining mean confidence with its dynamic gain. β scales the CDG term relative to mean confidence.

s = C̄ + β · ΔC

Answer Selection

Count dampening (α=0.5) prevents high-frequency answers from dominating. Per-answer scores averaged over supporting traces.

R(a) = |Tₐ|^α · μₐ(s)

CDG advantage persists across all trace budgets (L=8 to 512) with steeper scaling curves. Also outperforms DeepConf-Mean by +4.8% and DeepConf-Tail by +1.7%. Results robust to position percentile P% and not driven by boxed-answer formatting tokens.

Assessment

Strengths

  • Elegant core insight — confidence should rise along correct chains
  • Simple implementation: only top-K logprobs, no training needed
  • Generalizes majority voting and DeepConf as special cases
  • Consistent gains across all 4 architectures, 15/16 configs
  • Strong theoretical grounding via GRPO training dynamics
  • Thorough ablations covering α, β, P%, trace count and length

Limitations

  • Math-only evaluation; no code, general reasoning, or open-ended tasks
  • β requires per-model validation, weakening zero-shot claim
  • Needs logprob access at every decode step — not all APIs expose this
  • Inherits Best-of-N compute cost (up to 512 traces per question)
  • Smaller absolute gains on weaker models (Gemma-3-27B)
  • Theoretical analysis uses simplifying assumptions for GRPO
Open Questions
  1. Does the confidence dynamics signal hold for non-math reasoning — code generation, legal analysis, multi-hop QA?
  2. Can β be estimated from model architecture or training metadata alone, without per-model calibration?
  3. How does CDG interact with process or outcome reward models — could confidence dynamics complement reward-based selection?
  4. Does the phenomenon persist under PPO, DPO, or RLOO training (not just GRPO)?
  5. Could CDG enable early stopping or trace pruning during generation to cut compute costs?
