> **一句话总结：** 提出NMP-QAT框架，以神经元为粒度在训练中自适应学习离散量化精度（权重1-8bit、激活4-16bit），从低位起步按需扩展，在6个电信与通用数据集上以平均2-3bit权重和4-6bit激活实现优于PPSO和OWQ的精度-压缩权衡。

> **TL;DR:** NMP-QAT learns per-neuron discrete quantization bit-widths during training via differentiable surrogates and STE, starting from low precision and expanding only when gradient signals demand it, achieving superior accuracy-compression trade-offs over mixed-precision QAT baselines on both MLP and TabFormer architectures across 6 datasets.

## 核心贡献

- 提出神经元级混合精度量化感知训练（NMP-QAT），每个神经元独立学习自身离散精度，打破了传统层级/通道级混合精度的粒度限制。
- 设计了基于可微分代理（differentiable surrogates）和直通估计器（STE）的精度学习机制，前向传播保持硬量化离散图，反向传播通过软代理传递梯度，训练后丢弃代理得到静态推理模型。
- 统一支持权重单独量化和权重+激活联合量化，激活量化额外学习神经元级裁剪范围α，候选精度集合为{4,8,16}bit。
- 在6个数据集（4个电信场景 + 2个通用基准）和两种架构（MLP、TabFormer）上验证，NMP-QAT以平均1-2.4bit权重和4-6.7bit激活超越了PPSO和OWQ基线。
- 提供收敛性保证（O(T^{-1/2})驻点收敛率）和压缩-精度权衡的理论上界分析。

## Key Contributions

- **Neuron-level precision learning:** Each neuron independently learns its own discrete bit-width from {1,2,4,8} (weights) and {4,8,16} (activations) via trainable precision strengths mapped through fixed thresholds.
- **Scale-when-needed mechanism:** Initializes all neurons at the lowest precision and expands bit-widths only when gradient signals indicate necessity, avoiding manual search or multi-stage pipelines.
- **Differentiable surrogate + STE backward pass:** The forward pass uses hard discrete quantization (hardware-deployable); the backward pass uses a smooth weighted mixture over candidate precisions for gradient flow.
- **Joint weight and activation quantization:** Extends adaptive precision to activations with per-neuron learnable clipping ranges, addressing the fact that activations dominate on-chip memory traffic.
- **Theoretical guarantees:** Proves O(T^{-1/2}) convergence to a surrogate stationary point and an O(δ²) neighborhood for the hard-quantized objective; derives a compression-accuracy upper bound motivating per-neuron allocation.
- **Practical efficiency:** Achieves 18.5×–75× runtime reduction over PPSO (particle swarm search) while delivering better accuracy and compression.

## Methodology

NMP-QAT assigns each neuron j a trainable precision strength s_j ∈ [0,1], discretized via fixed thresholds {t₁,t₂,t₃} into bit-widths from {1,2,4,8} for weights and {4,8,16} for activations. All s_j are initialized in [0,t₁) to enforce low-precision starting points.

**Forward pass:** Uses the hard discrete assignment Q^{(b(s_j))}(W_{:,j}) — fully quantized, no runtime overhead.

**Backward pass:** Since b(s_j) is piecewise-constant, a differentiable surrogate replaces the hard assignment with a weighted sum over all candidate precisions, where weights are sigmoid transitions over s_j. STE (≈1) handles gradients through the quantizer itself. Exact gradients flow through learnable activation clipping ranges α_j.

**Training:** Standard SGD optimizes the joint parameter vector θ = (W, {s_j}, {s_j^(a)}, {α_j}). After training, all surrogate constructs are removed, yielding a static mixed-precision inference graph.

**Architecture support:** Presented for MLPs where neuron-level quantization aligns with dense layers; demonstrated to extend effectively to TabFormer (transformer-based tabular model).

## Results & Highlights

- **MLP weights+activations (Table 1):** NMP-QAT achieves best quantized results on KVS (MSE 3.303 vs FP 5.379), VoD (16.969 vs 16.41), QoE (acc 0.722 vs FP 0.715), and Higgs (acc 0.734 vs FP 0.748).
- **Average bit-widths (Table 2):** Weights use only 1.0–2.4 bits (mostly 1-bit) vs PPSO's 1.3–3.8 and OWQ's fixed 4.28; activations use 4.0–6.7 bits vs baselines' fixed 8.
- **TabFormer (Table 3):** Best performance on VoD, QoE, and CoverType with only 0.05%–1% degradation vs full precision.
- **TabFormer joint quantization (Table 4):** Uniform QAT baselines collapse on regression tasks (KVS MSE: 426.7); NMP-QAT reduces MSE by >2 orders of magnitude (KVS: 4.56, RSS: 167.65 vs 4335+).
- **Runtime:** 18.5×–75× faster than PPSO across all datasets (e.g., Higgs: 90min vs 3840min).
- **Depth scaling (Fig. 3):** Performance improves monotonically with model depth under NMP-QAT, confirming the approach scales well.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. Neuron-level mixed-precision learning during training is genuinely novel — prior work (Sher et al., 2023) operates at uniform precision with multi-stage freezing. The scale-when-needed design is elegant and avoids search overhead.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Formal convergence guarantees (Proposition B.3), compression-accuracy bounds (Proposition B.4), and comprehensive experiments with 3-seed averages and std deviations. However, the theoretical bound on STE bias δ is assumed rather than characterized.
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate. Algorithm pseudocode is clear and complete. However, threshold values {t_k} are not explicitly specified, dataset generation details for telecom benchmarks are proprietary (Ericsson internal simulator), and no code repository is mentioned.
- **Significance:** High for edge/NFL deployment. The ability to achieve FP-comparable accuracy with 1–2 bit weights and 4–5 bit activations is practically impactful for 6G edge constraints.
- **Red flags:** (1) Experiments limited to 4-layer MLPs — scalability to deeper/deeper transformer architectures is unclear. (2) Sub-8-bit arithmetic requires specialized hardware not universally available. (3) No real hardware latency/energy measurements — all efficiency claims are theoretical (bit-width averages). (4) The fact that most weights converge to 1-bit raises questions about whether structured pruning could achieve similar or better results more simply.

## Strengths

- Elegant formulation: the scale-when-needed mechanism avoids external search procedures entirely.
- Principled: theoretical convergence and accuracy-compression trade-off analysis.
- Comprehensive evaluation: 6 datasets, 2 architectures, both weights-only and weights+activations settings.
- Significant practical speedup over search-based methods (PPSO).
- The regularization effect of quantization sometimes *improves* over full precision on noisy telecom data.

## Limitations

- Only evaluated on 4-layer MLPs and one TabFormer configuration; behavior on deeper networks and large language models is unknown.
- No on-device latency or energy measurements — compression benefits are theoretical memory savings only.
- Sub-8-bit mixed-precision requires specialized hardware accelerators not widely deployed.
- Aggressive activation quantization remains challenging for attention-based tabular models (e.g., RSS MSE degrades from 8.26 to 167.65 under joint quantization on TabFormer).
- Threshold hyperparameters {t₁, t₂, t₃} are fixed and may require tuning for different architectures/datasets.
- The majority of neurons converging to 1-bit suggests potential overlap with structured pruning, which is not explored.

## Relevance

与模型压缩和边缘部署高度相关。NMP-QAT提供的神经元级自适应精度学习方法可直接应用于6G边缘场景下的模型部署优化，其理论框架对理解量化感知训练的收敛性有参考价值。权重+激活联合量化的设计对内存受限设备尤为实用。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does NMP-QAT scale to modern large language models (e.g., LLaMA-scale) where attention layers dominate computation?
2. Can the 1-bit weight neurons be directly pruned post-training to achieve compound compression benefits?
3. What is the sensitivity of the threshold hyperparameters {t₁, t₂, t₃}, and can they be learned or adapted online?
4. How do architectural components like dropout, skip connections, and layer normalization interact with extreme quantization regimes?
5. What are the actual energy and latency savings on commercially available mixed-precision accelerators?
