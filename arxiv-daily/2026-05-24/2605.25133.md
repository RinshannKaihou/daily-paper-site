> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Prover-Verifier Deliberation (PVD) 推理时协议——冻结的证明者 LLM 把候选答案分解为可核查子断言并辩护，验证者 LLM 发出针对性挑战并给出 Accept/Challenge/Reject 裁决；定义"Accept+无修改"(ANC) 子集为高置信信号，在 GPQA Diamond 上以约 3 次 LLM 调用实现 HC-Prec 84.2%@HC-Cov 77%（+32pp 间隙），最强配对 GPT-5.4/GPT-5.4-mini 达 97.6%@43%（+34.8pp），但在 Humanity's Last Exam 上弱配对会使 ANC 信号反转。

> **TL;DR:** Introduces Prover-Verifier Deliberation (PVD), an inference-time selective-prediction protocol grounded in interactive proof theory: a frozen prover LLM defends a candidate answer via checkable atomic sub-claims while a frozen verifier LLM issues targeted challenges and returns ACCEPT/CHALLENGE/REJECT. The high-confidence signal is Accept + No Change (ANC) — accepted without answer revision. On GPQA Diamond (n=198), Claude Sonnet 4.6 prover / Claude Haiku 4.5 verifier achieves HC-Prec 84.2% at HC-Cov 77% with ~3 LLM calls/question (+32.0pp gap over the non-ANC complement); the strongest within-family pairing GPT-5.4 / GPT-5.4-mini reaches HC-Prec 97.6% at HC-Cov 43% (+34.8pp gap). On Humanity's Last Exam (n=513), the frontier GPT-5.5 / Gemini 3.1 Pro pairing gives HC-Prec 59.0% at HC-Cov 52% (+27.9pp gap), but the weaker Sonnet 4.6 / Haiku 4.5 pairing inverts to a −7.1pp gap — a failure-mode diagnostic. PVD occupies a distinct, favorable position on the accuracy–calibration–efficiency Pareto frontier vs. self-consistency (k=8), universal self-consistency, multi-agent debate (3×2), and Reflexion.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 Prover-Verifier Deliberation (PVD)：基于交互式证明理论的推理时选择性预测协议，用冻结 LLM 实例化挑战-响应结构，产生结构化报告/弃答信号而非提升原始准确率。
- 定义 Accept + No Change (ANC) 作为高置信子集信号：验证者给出 ACCEPT 且证明者答案在所有挑战轮中未被修改，作为 HC-Cov/HC-Prec 操作点。
- 在 GPQA Diamond 上跨 5 种模型配对验证：HC-Prec 84–98%，间隙 +6.6 到 +34.8pp；最强配对 GPT-5.4/GPT-5.4-mini 达 97.6%@43%（+34.8pp）。
- 揭示验证者严格度与领域能力是校准质量的主要驱动因素：宽松验证者（Gemini Flash-Lite）虽 HC-Prec 97.3% 但间隙仅 +6.6pp；多轮对抗验证者（GPT-5.5-pro，平均 14.3 轮）把间隙拉到 +17.3pp。
- 在 Humanity's Last Exam 上提供部署诊断：弱配对（Sonnet/Haiku）ANC 间隙反转为 −7.1pp，是"有效验证区"失效的经验标志；强配对（GPT-5.5/Gemini 3.1 Pro）仍达 +27.9pp。
- 通过 SC ∩ ANC 重叠分析证明 PVD 与 self-consistency 测量的是结构性不同的信号（论证可辩护性 vs. 采样一致性），二者互补。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces Prover-Verifier Deliberation (PVD), an inference-time selective-prediction protocol that instantiates the challenge-response structure of interactive proofs with frozen LLMs to produce a structured report/abstain verdict rather than boost raw accuracy.
- Defines Accept + No Change (ANC) as the high-confidence signal: an attempt where the verifier issues ACCEPT and the prover's answer was never revised across all challenge rounds; evaluates it as a binary classifier with HC-Cov=Pr[ANC] and HC-Prec=Pr[correct|ANC].
- Empirically validates on GPQA Diamond across five model configurations: HC-Prec ranges 84–98% with gaps of +6.6 to +34.8pp over the non-ANC complement; the strongest within-family capability-asymmetric pairing (GPT-5.4 prover / GPT-5.4-mini verifier) reaches HC-Prec 97.6% at HC-Cov 43% (+34.8pp gap).
- Shows verifier strictness and domain competence are the primary drivers of calibration quality: a permissive verifier (Gemini Flash-Lite) achieves HC-Prec 97.3% but only +6.6pp gap because non-ANC accuracy is also high (90.7%); replacing it with GPT-5.5-pro (avg 14.3 challenge rounds) widens the gap to +17.3pp.
- Provides a deployment diagnostic on Humanity's Last Exam (n=513): the frontier GPT-5.5 / Gemini 3.1 Pro pairing gives HC-Prec 59.0% at HC-Cov 52% (+27.9pp gap), but the weaker Sonnet 4.6 / Haiku 4.5 pairing inverts to a −7.1pp gap — the empirical signature of an empty effective region where the verifier lacks domain knowledge.
- Demonstrates via SC ∩ ANC overlap analysis (Table 6) that PVD and self-consistency measure structurally different signals (argument defensibility vs. sample agreement): on 35 questions where SC is confident but PVD rejects, SC accuracy drops to 77.1%; PVD rejected 8/12 (67%) cases where SC was confident yet wrong.

## Methodology

**Protocol (Algorithm 1).** Two frozen LLMs — a Prover P and a Verifier V — engage over a multiple-choice question q with candidate answers A.

- **Initialization.** P selects a candidate answer â∈A and produces a structured proof: a one-sentence statement, a list of 3–5 atomic sub-claims (each independently verifiable), and a brief reasoning paragraph.
- **Challenge-response loop.** At each round t∈{1,…,T}, V examines the proof transcript and issues one of: ACCEPT (every sub-claim is specific, verifiable, mutually consistent — terminate with acceptance); REJECT (a clear logical flaw, factual error, or contradiction identified — terminate with rejection); or CHALLENGE (V identifies the single most suspicious sub-claim c̃ and poses a targeted question; P responds by justifying c̃, may revise â if it detects an error; proceed to round t+1).
- **Fatigue.** If V issues T challenges without a terminal verdict, the attempt ends in a fatigue rejection.
- **Retry.** If an attempt ends in Reject or fatigue, a new attempt is initiated (up to K total) with a brief summary of prior failed attempts as adversarial context.
- **Fallback.** If no attempt among K reaches ACCEPT with no answer revision, the final answer is determined by majority vote over all K attempt final answers.
- **ANC.** An attempt is ANC if V issues ACCEPT and P's answer was never revised. ANC questions form the reported (high-confidence) subset; the remainder are withheld or answered by majority vote.

**Separated histories.** P and V maintain independent conversation histories (hP, hV); V observes only P's structured output (answer, statement, sub-claims, reasoning), not P's internal chain-of-thought — mirroring the zero-knowledge property and reducing the risk that P optimizes against V's decision boundary.

**No formal guarantees.** Classical IP soundness/completeness rest on computational-complexity assumptions that do not hold for frozen LLMs. P may hallucinate or persuasively defend wrong answers; V may miss valid challenges or issue spurious rejections. HC-Prec and the ACCEPT/REJECT accuracy gap serve as empirical proxies.

**Evaluation metrics.** HC-Cov = Pr[ANC] (fraction of questions reported); HC-Prec = Pr[correct|ANC] (accuracy on the reported subset); Gap = HC-Prec − accuracy on the non-ANC complement. The Gap quantifies how much information the ANC verdict carries beyond raw accuracy.

**Benchmarks.** GPQA Diamond (Rein et al., 2024): graduate-level domain-expert benchmark in biology (n=19), physics (n=86), chemistry (n=93), designed to be difficult for non-experts even with web access. Humanity's Last Exam (Center for AI Safety et al., 2026): broad expert-level benchmark (n=513) probing frontier academic knowledge and calibration across Biology/Medicine, Math, Humanities/Soc.Sci., CS/AI, Physics, Chemistry, Engineering.

**Baselines.** Single-call (direct chain-of-thought; single-call self-deliberation ablation where one response contains both prover and verifier blocks). Self-Consistency (SC, k=8 independent samples, majority vote, full consensus as confidence proxy). Universal Self-Consistency (USC, k=8 samples + LLM aggregator, agreement rate as proxy). Multi-agent Debate (3 agents, 2 rounds, 9 calls, agent consensus as proxy). Reflexion (up to 5 trials with self-consistency recheck as failure detector, stability as proxy).

**Model configurations.** Six prover-verifier pairings varying model family, relative capability, and protocol parameters. Within-family capability-asymmetric: Claude Sonnet 4.6 / Haiku 4.5; GPT-5.4 / GPT-5.4-mini. Cross-family: Gemini 3.1 Pro / GPT-5.5-pro on GPQA; GPT-5.5 / Gemini 3.1 Pro on HLE. Single-call self-deliberation ablation (P=V) to isolate model separation.

**Protocol parameters.** Fatigue limit T=12 (two-model single-attempt), T=6 (single-call self-deliberation); retry limit K=1 (standard), K=6 (strongest GPQA cross-family retry config, ~29 calls/question average). PVD averages 3–6 calls/question for single-attempt configs; minimum 2 calls (accept on first verifier turn); PVD† (forces ≥1 challenge before any ACCEPT) costs ≥4 calls.

**Inference cost.** Measured in LLM calls/question and token cost (USD via provider list prices), not wall-clock latency. Reference points: single call = 1; SC k=8 = 8; Debate 3×2 = 9; Reflexion ≤5 (avg ≈2.5).

## Results & Highlights

- GPQA Diamond, Sonnet 4.6 prover (Table 1): PVD (Haiku 4.5 verifier) achieves HC-Prec 84.2% at HC-Cov 77%, +32.0pp gap, ~3 calls; PVD† (Haiku 4.5, challenge-first) HC-Prec 89.9% at HC-Cov 65%, +29.1pp gap, ~6 calls. Baselines: SC (k=8) HC-Prec 91.5% at HC-Cov 72%, +29.0pp, 8 calls; USC HC-Prec 91.5% at HC-Cov 72%, +29.0pp, ~3 calls; Debate (3×2) HC-Prec 85.7% at HC-Cov 95%, +52.4pp (‡ unreliable, n<20 complement), 9 calls; Reflexion HC-Prec 82.6% at HC-Cov 93%, +4.0pp (‡), ≤5 calls. Single-call self-deliberation HC-Prec 83.9% at HC-Cov 63%, +13.6pp, 1 call.
- GPQA Diamond, GPT-5.4 prover (Table 1): Direct accuracy 72.7%; SC* (k=8, extended thinking, Epoch AI benchmark) HC-Prec 97.2% at HC-Cov 90%, +23.5pp (‡); PVD (GPT-5.4-mini verifier) HC-Prec 97.6% at HC-Cov 43%, +34.8pp gap, ~8 calls.
- GPQA Diamond, Gemini 3.1 Pro prover (Table 1): SC* (k=8) HC-Prec 97.3% at HC-Cov 94%, +60.9pp (‡); PVD (Flash-Lite verifier) HC-Prec 97.3% at HC-Cov 57%, +6.6pp gap, ~4 calls; PVD+retry (GPT-5.5-pro verifier, K=6) HC-Prec 97.3% at HC-Cov 75%, +17.3pp gap, ~29 calls.
- GPQA Diamond ANC by domain (Table 2): GPT-5.4/GPT-5.4-mini — Chemistry HC-Prec 100% at HC-Cov 16% (+46.2pp gap), Physics 98%@70% (+9.9pp), Biology 90%@53% (+23.3pp). Sonnet/Haiku† — Chemistry 80%@49% (+25.1pp), Physics 96%@83% (+15.8pp), Biology 92%@63% (+34.5pp). Gemini Pro/Flash-Lite — Chemistry 100%@45% (+7.8pp), Physics 100%@66% (+6.9pp), Biology 77%@68% (+10.3pp).
- Humanity's Last Exam, GPT-5.5 prover / Gemini 3.1 Pro verifier (Table 3, n=513): Overall accuracy 45.6%, HC-Cov 52%, HC-Prec 59.0%, non-ANC accuracy 31.0%, +27.9pp gap. By domain: Math HC-Prec 76.7%@67% (+49.1pp), Engineering 77.8%@35% (+48.4pp), CS/AI 68.6%@53% (+36.3pp), Humanities 61.9%@53% (+24.1pp), Other 52.9%@39% (+34.4pp), Biology/Medicine 50.0%@46% (+17.1pp), Chemistry 33.3%@57% (+14.6pp), Physics 31.2%@64% (−24.3pp inversion).
- HLE failure mode (Table 5): Sonnet 4.6 / Haiku 4.5 — Acc 20.1%, HC-Cov 31%, HC-Prec 15.2%, Gap −7.1pp (ANC questions less accurate than non-ANC — inverted selection). Opus 4.6 / Sonnet 4.6 — Gap +4.3pp. GPT-5.5 / Gemini 3.1 Pro — Gap +27.9pp. The inversion is the empirical signature of an empty effective region where Haiku lacks domain knowledge to formulate non-trivial challenges.
- Verifier choice holding prover fixed (Table 4): Sonnet 4.6 prover — Self (single-call) HC-Prec 83.9%@63% (+13.6pp, avg 2.1 rounds); Haiku HC-Prec 84.2%@77% (+32.0pp, avg 1.7 rounds); Haiku† HC-Prec 89.9%@65% (+29.1pp, avg 3.0 rounds). Gemini 3.1 Pro prover — Flash-Lite HC-Prec 97.3%@57% (+6.6pp, avg 1.9 rounds); GPT-5.5-pro HC-Prec 97.3%@75% (+17.3pp, avg 14.3 rounds) — identical HC-Prec but GPT-5.5-pro sustains multi-round pressure, dropping non-ANC accuracy from 90.7% to 80.0%.
- SC vs. PVD overlap (Table 6, GPQA Diamond, n=198, Sonnet 4.6 base): Both HC on 107 questions (54%) at 96.3% precision; SC-only on 35 (18%) at 77.1% SC accuracy; PVD-only on 22 (11%) at 59.1% PVD accuracy; neither on 34 (17%) at 55.9%. PVD rejected 8/12 (67%) cases where SC was confident yet wrong; SC flagged non-consensus on 9/13 (69%) cases where PVD accepted but the answer was wrong.
- Self-deliberation ablation: with a minimum-one-challenge requirement, single-call self-deliberation HC-Prec remains 84%, but the Accept/Reject gap narrows by ~15pp (from +29pp to +14pp), consistent with a weaker Effective Verifier that cannot challenge claims it generated itself — suggesting structured sub-claim decomposition contributes independently of cross-model separation.

## Critical Assessment

The protocol is conceptually clean and the honest reporting of the HLE inversion is a genuine strength, but several paper-specific weaknesses qualify the results. First, PVD is a selective-prediction protocol, not an accuracy booster: on GPQA Diamond the prover's full-population accuracy (76.8–79.8% for Sonnet) is comparable to the one-call self-deliberation ablation (78.8%), and the authors concede "PVD's full-population accuracy is comparable to the one-call Sonnet self-deliberation ablation ... This is by design." The value proposition rests entirely on the ANC gap, which is meaningful only if a downstream remediation step (escalation to a more capable model, human review) is actually implemented — and the authors explicitly do not implement this second stage. Second, the headline HC-Prec numbers are heavily configuration-dependent and the coverage-precision tradeoff is steep: the strongest GPQA result (GPT-5.4/GPT-5.4-mini, 97.6% HC-Prec) comes at only 43% coverage, and the Gemini Flash-Lite verifier's 97.3% HC-Prec hides a near-useless +6.6pp gap because it rejects correct and incorrect answers at nearly the same rate (non-ANC accuracy 90.7%). Third, the HLE results expose a serious fragility: the Sonnet/Haiku pairing produces a −7.1pp inverted gap, meaning ANC questions are less accurate than non-ANC — the protocol actively misleads when the verifier operates outside its effective region, and there is no a priori way to detect this without ground truth (the authors propose the gap collapse itself as a diagnostic, but that requires knowing the gap, which requires labels). Fourth, all experiments are on English multiple-choice benchmarks with a fixed answer alphabet; the authors flag that transfer "to open-ended generation, multilingual tasks, long-form factual synthesis, or settings where multiple answers can be partially correct" is untested. Fifth, baseline comparisons are not exhaustive hyperparameter sweeps — Debate uses one fixed configuration (3 agents, 2 rounds), and operating-point selection for baseline confidence signals is not tuned to every coverage level. Sixth, no open-weight models are evaluated and no conformal risk-control or learned-selector baseline is provided (deferred to future work due to small GPQA Diamond size). Seventh, PVD assumes an honest prover; it is explicitly "not an adversarial robustness guarantee, an AI-control protocol, or a formal interactive-proof system," and a strategic prover or colluding model pair can defeat the signal.

## Strengths

- Principled grounding in interactive proof theory (IP=PSPACE, Goldwasser-Micali-Rackoff; NIP=PSPACE) provides a clear conceptual template, while remaining honestly empirical about the non-transfer of formal guarantees to frozen LLMs.
- The ANC signal is genuinely distinct from sample agreement: the SC ∩ PVD overlap analysis (Table 6) shows the two methods catch different classes of errors (PVD rejected 67% of SC-confident-but-wrong cases), supporting the argument-defensibility thesis.
- Honest failure-mode reporting on HLE (the −7.1pp inversion) is scientifically valuable and frames the protocol's effective-region limitation as a deployment diagnostic rather than hiding it.
- Cost-efficient: ~3 calls/question for single-attempt PVD vs. 8 for SC (k=8) or 9 for Debate (3×2), at comparable-or-better HC-Prec on GPQA Diamond.
- Cross-model-family robustness (Claude, GPT, Gemini) and the verifier-choice ablation (Table 4) cleanly isolate that verifier strictness/round-count, not raw HC-Prec, drives the selection gap.
- Clear, reproducible pseudocode (Algorithm 1) and public code release.

## Limitations

- PVD is selective-prediction, not accuracy-boosting; full-population accuracy is comparable to a single call, and the value depends on an unimplemented downstream remediation step.
- Coverage-precision tradeoff is steep: strongest HC-Prec (97.6%) comes at only 43% coverage; high HC-Prec can coexist with a near-useless gap (Gemini Flash-Lite: 97.3% HC-Prec but +6.6pp gap).
- HLE fragility: weak prover-verifier pairings invert the ANC signal (−7.1pp), and there is no a priori ground-truth-free way to detect the effective-region collapse without labels.
- English multiple-choice only; open-ended generation, multilingual tasks, and partially-correct-answer settings are untested.
- No open-weight models evaluated; results are sensitive to provider-side model updates, decoding defaults, and API availability.
- Baselines not exhaustively swept (Debate uses one fixed 3×2 config; baseline confidence operating points not tuned to every coverage level).
- No conformal risk-control or learned-selector baseline (deferred due to small GPQA Diamond size); cost measured in calls/tokens, not wall-clock latency.
- Assumes an honest prover; not an adversarial-robustness or AI-control guarantee; a strategic prover or colluding pair can defeat the signal.

## Relevance

直接对应「LLM Inference Evaluation」方向：PVD 是一种推理时选择性预测协议，通过证明者-验证者对抗式对话产生结构化置信度裁决，为 LLM 在无真值场景下的可靠报告/弃答决策提供了新机制；其"有效验证区"失效诊断与跨模型族校准迁移分析，也触及「LLM Training Stability & Observability」中关于模型能力边界与可观测性的内容，以及「Computation Reliability & Precision Errors」中关于推理时计算可靠性的议题。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can a ground-truth-free detector for effective-region collapse (e.g., verifier challenge-round distribution, verifier self-reported uncertainty) predict the HLE-style inversion without needing labels?
- How does PVD transfer to open-ended generation, multilingual tasks, or settings with multiple partially-correct answers, where the fixed answer-alphabet assumption breaks?
- Does implementing the downstream remediation step (escalating non-ANC questions to a more capable model or human review) realize the full selective-prediction value proposition at a system level?
- Would a learned confidence head over features (ANC, challenge-round count, answer changes, SC agreement) improve calibration beyond the native ANC signal, and can it be trained without overfitting at GPQA Diamond's sample size?
- How does PVD behave with open-weight models (e.g., Llama, Qwen) as provers/verifiers, and does the within-family capability-asymmetric pairing finding generalize beyond proprietary APIs?
- Can an ensemble of verifiers (multiple strictness levels voting) robustly avoid the inversion failure mode on hard benchmarks like HLE?
