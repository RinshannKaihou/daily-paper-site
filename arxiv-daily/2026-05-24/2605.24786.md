> **一句话总结：** CONF-KV 利用模型每步解码时的 next-token 置信度动态调节 KV cache 预算，在高置信时激进裁剪、低置信时保留更多上下文，配合混合精度存储，在接近 512-token 滑动窗口的内存开销下将困惑度差距缩小了 74%，NIAH 检索准确率达 91.4%。

> **TL;DR:** CONF-KV converts the next-token distribution entropy into a per-step confidence score that dynamically selects tight or loose KV cache budgets, combined with blockwise online-softmax attention, mixed FP16/INT8 storage, and pyramidal layer budgets, closing 74% of the perplexity gap between full KV and a 512-token sliding window at matched memory.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了一种**置信度感知的 KV cache 淘汰策略**，利用当前 next-token 分布的熵、log-prob margin 和 top-token 概率计算置信度分数，据此动态选择每步的 cache 预算。
- 设计了**混合精度存储方案**（最近 W 个 token 保留 FP16，其余量化为 INT8），以及**金字塔式逐层预算分配**（深层使用更小的 cache 预算），显著降低内存占用。
- 通过 **KL 散度消融实验**验证了置信度与上下文依赖性的负相关关系（Pearson r = -0.77），为策略的核心假设提供了机理性支撑。
- 在 GPT-2、Qwen-14B/32B、gpt-oss-20B 四个模型家族上，覆盖语言建模、长上下文检索（NIAH）、Web 代理（VisualWebArena）、延迟和吞吐量等多维度评估，**全面优于 H2O、Scissorhands、SnapKV、PyramidKV 等基线方法**。

## Key Contributions

- A confidence-aware KV eviction policy that reads the model's current next-token distribution (entropy, log-prob margin, top-token mass) to select a per-decoding-step cache budget — retaining more context when uncertain, pruning aggressively when confident.
- A systems design combining adaptive eviction with blockwise online-softmax attention, mixed FP16/INT8 KV storage, cache compaction, and an optional pyramidal per-layer budget variant.
- A mechanistic validation showing confidence anticorrelates with the KL shift induced by ablating recent context (Pearson r = −0.77, p < 10⁻³⁰), directly supporting the policy's central assumption.
- Comprehensive matched-memory evaluation across four model families (GPT-2 124M, Qwen-14B/32B, gpt-oss-20B) on WikiText-2 perplexity, Needle-in-a-Haystack retrieval, VisualWebArena web-agent tasks, latency, and throughput, outperforming sliding windows, H2O, Scissorhands, SnapKV, and PyramidKV.

## Methodology

CONF-KV operates within a standard autoregressive generation loop without requiring training or weight changes. At each decoding step:

1. **Confidence estimation:** From the logits, the method computes a composite confidence score c = w_H(1 − Ĥ) + w_m·σ(m) + w_p·p^(1) where Ĥ is normalized entropy, m is log-prob margin, p^(1) is top-token probability, and weights (0.4, 0.3, 0.3) were found via grid search on GPT-2 and remained stable across models.

2. **Budget selection:** A threshold τ (default 0.7) selects between a tight budget N_high (confident steps) and a loose budget N_low (uncertain steps). N_high ≤ N_low by design.

3. **Token ranking and eviction:** Within each layer, non-protected tokens are ranked by s_i = α·â_i + (1−α)·r̂_i where â_i is normalized EMA attention mass (λ=0.9) and r̂_i is normalized recency. The lowest-ranked tokens are evicted, while the most recent P tokens (protected window) are always retained.

4. **Mixed-precision storage:** The most recent W retained tokens stay in FP16; older entries are symmetrically quantized to INT8 per (head, channel). INT8 adds only 0.34 PPL over FP16, whereas NF4 and INT4 add 0.91 and 1.65 PPL respectively.

5. **Pyramidal layer budgets (CONF-KV-L):** Deeper layers receive exponentially smaller budgets via N_high^(ℓ) = max(N_min, N_high·β^(ℓ/L)) with β=0.5 and N_min=96, reflecting the observation that deeper layers concentrate information into fewer tokens.

6. **Blockwise online-softmax attention:** The compacted cache is read in blocks with running max/normalizer for exact online softmax, avoiding the need for an indirection table.

## Results & Highlights

- **Perplexity (GPT-2, 2048 tokens):** CONF-KV-L achieves 30.48 PPL (vs. 29.14 full KV, 34.37 sliding-512), closing 74% of the quality gap at 34.2 MB — less memory than every baseline. CONF-KV+INT8 achieves 31.26 PPL at 38.7 MB, closing 60% of the gap.
- **Needle-in-a-Haystack (gpt-oss-20b, up to 32K):** CONF-KV reaches 91.4% average retrieval accuracy vs. 53.8% (sliding-512) and 80.6% (H2O, matched memory). Failures occur mainly when the model is over-confident before rare-entity lookups.
- **VisualWebArena (75 tasks, gpt-oss-20b):** CONF-KV retains 95.3% of full-KV task success (38.3% vs. 40.2%) at 2.8× lower peak memory. Sliding-window loses 11.1 absolute points; H2O underperforms especially on information-seeking tasks.
- **Throughput (GPT-2, batch 8):** CONF-KV achieves 2.06× the full-KV throughput while matching sliding-window quality within 1%.
- **Latency:** CONF-KV reduces p50 per-step latency by 1.8× on GPT-2 and 1.8× on Qwen-32B vs. full KV. Attention falls from 62% to 47% of step time; compaction adds only 0.22 ms and metadata updates 0.11 ms.
- **Qwen-32B at 4K tokens:** Peak KV memory reduced from 15.8 GB to 2.6 GB (13.2 GB savings), materially changing feasible batch sizes on a single H100.
- **Matched-rate ablation:** Random eviction at the same 42% eviction rate yields 36.54 PPL; recency-only ranking gives 32.08; attention-only gives 31.47; full CONF-KV gives 30.92 — confirming both the ranker and confidence-gated schedule contribute.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core idea — using next-token confidence to gate KV cache budgets — is straightforward and well-motivated. The novelty claim is appropriately narrow: the authors acknowledge confidence signals have been used for early exit and speculative decoding, and position CONF-KV as applying the same signal to a different axis (memory state). The combination with mixed-precision storage and pyramidal budgets is incremental but effective.
- **Rigor:** Matched-memory comparisons are well-designed. The KL-ablation mechanistic test (r = −0.77) directly validates the central assumption. The matched-rate isolation study cleanly separates the contributions of confidence gating vs. the ranker. Three seeds for NIAH/VWA/throughput with standard deviations reported.
- **Reproducibility:** All hyperparameters are specified (τ=0.7, N_high=128/256, N_low=256/512, P=32/64, α=0.65, W=128/256, B=128, λ=0.9, β=0.5, N_min=96). Grid search for confidence weights was done on GPT-2. Code promised upon acceptance but not yet available.
- **Significance:** The results are strong and consistent across workloads. The memory-quality Pareto improvement is meaningful for deployment. The approach is orthogonal to and composable with other techniques (paging, quantization, speculative decoding).
- **Red flags:** The confidence weights (0.4, 0.3, 0.3) were tuned on GPT-2 124M — a very small model. It is unclear whether this tuning transfers perfectly to all model scales, though the authors claim stability. The VWA evaluation uses a text-serialized wrapper rather than native multimodal inputs, which is a simplification. Full-KV throughput runs did not complete at batch 16 on GPT-2, so throughput speedup claims are limited to batch ≤ 8.

## Strengths

- Simple, training-free design that can be inserted into any autoregressive generation loop without modifying model weights.
- Strong empirical results across multiple models, tasks, and metrics — not just perplexity but also retrieval, web-agent tasks, latency, and throughput.
- Excellent mechanistic validation: the KL-ablation test and matched-rate isolation study provide clear causal evidence that confidence matters, not just eviction rate.
- Composable with orthogonal techniques (paged attention, speculative decoding, head-wise allocation).
- Clear operating-point analysis with sensitivity sweeps on τ, N_high, and W, exposing direct quality-memory trade-off knobs.

## Limitations

- **Short-context no-op:** CONF-KV provides no benefit when context never exceeds the eviction threshold, and speedups diminish when MLP compute dominates over attention/bandwidth.
- **High-temperature sampling:** The confidence signal becomes less informative under high-temperature sampling where entropy saturates; τ may need retuning.
- **Paged serving compatibility:** Fine-grained token-level eviction creates partially dead blocks in PagedAttention-style layouts, requiring coarsening or sparse masks for production deployment.
- **Confidence weight tuning:** Weights were found via grid search on GPT-2 124M; sensitivity to model scale and domain is not fully characterized.
- **Occasional over-confidence failures:** The model can be confidently wrong before rare-entity lookups, causing premature eviction. Raising τ recovers most cases but at a memory cost (~12%).

## Relevance

本文提出的置信度驱动的 KV cache 自适应淘汰策略与长上下文推理、高效 LLM 部署直接相关。其核心思路——利用模型自身的不确定性信号动态分配内存预算——可与其他 cache 压缩技术正交组合，对实际推理系统优化具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does CONF-KV interact with multi-query attention or grouped-query attention architectures where K/V heads are shared?
- Can the confidence signal be enriched with task-level hints (e.g., marking retrieval-heavy spans) to further improve budget allocation?
- What is the sensitivity of the confidence weights (0.4, 0.3, 0.3) when applied to much larger models (70B+), especially on domain-shifted data?
- How would CONF-KV behave in multi-turn conversations where the model may be uncertain about dialogue state rather than factual content?
- Could a learned confidence estimator (fine-tuned on eviction quality signals) outperform the hand-crafted composite score?
