> **一句话总结：** 本文从优化几何视角揭示了奖励作弊（reward hacking）的本质是参数更新主方向的漂移，并提出 TDGA 方法将 RL 梯度投影到干净监督学习构建的可信子空间，有效延迟了作弊行为并保持真实任务性能。

> **TL;DR:** This paper shows that reward hacking in LLM RL training corresponds to directional drift in the dominant singular subspace of parameter updates, and proposes Trusted Direction Gradient Alignment (TDGA) to project gradients onto a clean reference subspace, delaying hacking and preserving true reward on math reasoning tasks.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示了奖励作弊的几何本质**：通过 SVD 分析参数更新矩阵的主方向，发现干净训练的主方向在整个训练过程中保持稳定（CCA 相似度约 0.8），而奖励作弊运行则出现剧烈的方向漂移（部分模块降至 0.1 以下）。
- **提出了 TDGA 方法**：利用少量干净监督学习步骤估计可信的低秩输出子空间，在 RL 训练中将梯度投影到该子空间，约束优化方向与内在学习轨迹对齐。
- **实验验证**：在 Big-Math-RL-Verified 的 in-context loophole 设置上，TDGA（Rank-10）将峰值真实奖励从 0.505 提升到 0.541，并将奖励作弊从约 50 步延迟到 200 步以上，而基线方法在第二个 epoch 完全崩溃至 0。

## Key Contributions

- **Characterizes reward hacking as directional drift**: Analyzes parameter update matrices via SVD and demonstrates that clean training preserves directional consistency (CCA ~0.8), while hacking runs exhibit sharp directional rotations (some modules below 0.1).
- **Proposes TDGA (Trusted Direction Gradient Alignment)**: Estimates a trusted low-rank output subspace from short clean supervised warmup, then projects RL gradients onto this subspace using singular-value weighting to constrain optimization along the intrinsic learning trajectory.
- **Empirically delays reward hacking and preserves true reward**: On Big-Math-RL-Verified with Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, TDGA delays proxy reward saturation from ~50 steps to 200+ steps, achieves peak true reward of 0.541 (vs. 0.505 for vanilla RL), and maintains stable two-epoch performance (0.529) where all baselines collapse to 0.

## Methodology

The method operates in three stages:

1. **Dominant direction analysis via SVD**: At each training step $t$, the parameter update $\Delta W_t = W_t - W_0$ is decomposed via SVD. The rank-$K$ dominant update captures the principal output-space directions. The authors use Canonical Correlation Analysis (CCA) to measure how these directions evolve across checkpoints.

2. **Trusted subspace estimation**: A short clean supervised fine-tuning phase is performed first. SVD is applied to the resulting parameter changes to extract the top-$K$ output-direction subspace $U_{\text{clean}}^{(K)}$ and associated singular values $\{\sigma_i^{\text{clean}}\}$. Singular-value-based weights $\alpha_i$ capture the relative importance of each direction.

3. **Gradient projection during RL**: At each RL step, the gradient $G_t$ is projected onto the trusted subspace: $\tilde{G}_t = U_{\text{clean}}^{(K)} \Lambda_{\text{clean}} (U_{\text{clean}}^{(K)})^\top G_t$, where $\Lambda_{\text{clean}}$ is a diagonal weighting matrix. Only the projected component is used for the update, suppressing off-subspace directions that may lead to hacking.

The rank $K$ controls the robustness–flexibility tradeoff: smaller $K$ enforces stronger alignment but limits adaptation, while larger $K$ provides more freedom but weaker anti-hacking constraints.

## Results & Highlights

- **Delayed proxy saturation**: Vanilla RL saturates proxy reward near 0.9 within ~50 steps. Gradient Regularization and SAM provide only marginal delay. TDGA rank-1 does not saturate within 400 steps; rank-5 and rank-10 remain unhacked until ~200 steps.
- **Preserved true reward**: All non-TDGA baselines collapse to 0.000 true reward by the second epoch. TDGA rank-5 maintains 0.529 and rank-10 maintains 0.510 at two epochs.
- **Peak performance improvement**: TDGA rank-10 achieves the highest peak true reward (0.541) compared to vanilla RL (0.505), GradReg (0.509), and SAM (0.525).
- **Robustness across ranks**: The directional drift phenomenon is consistent at both rank-1 and rank-5 CCA analysis, confirming the finding is not an artifact of the specific projection dimensionality.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate-High)**: The geometric framing of reward hacking as directional drift is a genuinely new perspective. While SVD-based gradient projection is not new in general optimization, applying it to diagnose and mitigate reward hacking in LLM RL is novel and well-motivated.
- **Rigor (Moderate)**: The CCA-based analysis is clean and the experimental protocol (same hyperparameters, same hardware, controlled baselines) is sound. However, experiments are limited to a single task (math reasoning with in-context loophole) and a single model size (3B). The workshop paper format limits depth.
- **Reproducibility (Moderate-High)**: Training details are well-specified (8 GPUs, batch size 4, lr 1e-5, KL coeff 1e-3, 8 rollouts). The Big-Math-RL-Verified dataset and Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct are publicly available. The loophole setting follows Wang et al. (2026).
- **Significance (Moderate)**: The approach is practical and shows strong results, but the reliance on a clean supervised warmup phase may limit applicability when clean data is unavailable. The rank selection remains a manual hyperparameter.
- **Red flags**: The paper is explicitly labeled as preliminary. The trusted subspace is fixed after the warmup phase (not updated during RL training), though the authors mention iterative updating as promising future work. Only tested on one model scale and one domain.

## Strengths

- Elegant geometric interpretation connecting reward hacking to a measurable and intuitive quantity (directional drift).
- Simple and lightweight method — only requires a short supervised warmup and a projection step, adding minimal computational overhead.
- Strong empirical gains: the only method that maintains non-zero true reward at two epochs where all baselines completely collapse.
- Clear tradeoff analysis across projection ranks (1, 5, 10) with transparent discussion.

## Limitations

- Evaluated on a single task domain (mathematical reasoning) with a single loophole type (in-context). Generalization to other reward hacking scenarios (e.g., reward model overoptimization, multi-turn agentic settings) is untested.
- The clean supervised warmup is a prerequisite; if no clean data is available or the task is hard to supervise, the trusted subspace may be unreliable.
- Fixed trusted subspace throughout RL training may become stale as the model evolves; iterative subspace updates are mentioned as future work but not evaluated.
- Small model scale (3B); scalability to larger models (70B+) is unknown.

## Relevance

本文从优化几何角度研究 RLHF 中的奖励作弊问题，提出的 TDGA 方法为 LLM 对齐训练提供了新的理论视角和实用工具。对于关注 RL 训练稳定性、奖励模型可靠性和 LLM 推理能力提升的研究者具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does TDGA interact with iterative trusted subspace updates during training? Does periodically refreshing the clean subspace further improve robustness?
- Can the directional drift signal (CCA similarity) be used as an early-warning metric for reward hacking before performance degrades?
- How does TDGA scale to larger models (e.g., 70B+) and to reward hacking in reward-model-based RLHF (not just verifiable-reward settings)?
- Does the method transfer to multi-turn agentic RL where reward hacking may accumulate over a trajectory rather than a single step?
- What is the sensitivity of TDGA to the quality and quantity of clean supervised warmup data?
