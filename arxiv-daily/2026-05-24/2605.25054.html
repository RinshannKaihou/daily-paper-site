Scale When Needed: Adaptive Neuron-level Mixed Precision Quantization Aware Training

Ayush K. Varshney · Konstantinos Vandikas · Šarūnas Girdzijauskas · Adam Orucu · Aneta Vulgarakis Feljan — Ericsson Research / KTH / RISE
Each neuron independently learns its own discrete bit-width during training—starting from 1-bit and expanding only when gradients demand it—achieving full-precision-comparable accuracy with 1–2.4-bit weights and 4–6.7-bit activations, no external search required.
Key Metric · Speedup over PPSO
75×
90 min vs 3,840 min on Higgs
Why This Matters
Per-neuron adaptive quantization eliminates manual precision search, making edge-ready compression automatic, principled, and 75× faster.
Critical Edge
Only tested on 4-layer MLPs. No real hardware latency or energy measurements. Most weights converging to 1-bit raises the question: could structured pruning achieve the same more simply?

Methodology

  • Each neuron j receives a trainable precision strength sj ∈ [0,1], discretized via fixed thresholds {t1,t2,t3} into bit-widths from {1,2,4,8} (weights) or {4,8,16} (activations). All initialized to lowest precision.
  • Forward pass: Hard discrete quantization—fully hardware-deployable, zero runtime overhead.
  • Backward pass: Differentiable surrogate replaces hard assignment with a weighted sum over all candidate precisions; STE (≈1) handles quantizer gradients. Learnable clipping ranges αj for activations receive exact gradients.
  • Standard SGD optimizes the joint parameter vector. After training, all surrogates are discarded, yielding a static mixed-precision inference graph.
  • Convergence guarantee: O(T−1/2) stationary-point rate with O(δ²) neighborhood for the hard-quantized objective.

Strengths

  • Elegant scale-when-needed mechanism—no external search procedures
  • Formal convergence proofs and compression-accuracy bounds
  • Comprehensive evaluation: 6 datasets, 2 architectures, both quantization modes
  • 18.5–75× practical speedup over search-based PPSO
  • Quantization regularization sometimes improves over FP on noisy telecom data

Limitations

  • Only 4-layer MLPs and one TabFormer config; deeper nets and LLMs untested
  • No on-device latency or energy measurements—compression claims are theoretical
  • Sub-8-bit mixed-precision needs specialized hardware not widely deployed
  • Aggressive activation quantization degrades TabFormer on RSS (MSE 8.26 → 167.65)
  • Threshold hyperparameters {t1,t2,t3} are fixed, may need per-architecture tuning

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does NMP-QAT scale to LLaMA-scale LLMs where attention layers dominate computation?
  2. Can 1-bit weight neurons be directly pruned post-training for compound compression?
  3. What is the sensitivity of thresholds {t1,t2,t3}—can they be learned or adapted online?
  4. How do dropout, skip connections, and layer normalization interact with extreme quantization?
  5. What are the actual energy and latency savings on commercial mixed-precision accelerators?
