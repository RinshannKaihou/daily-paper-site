> **一句话总结：** 本文发现LLM推理中正确轨迹的置信度沿推理链递增而错误轨迹递减，据此提出置信度动态增益(CDG)投票方法，在多个数学推理基准上显著超越多数投票和静态置信度方法（平均提升5.4%）。

> **TL;DR:** The paper reveals that correct LLM reasoning traces exhibit rising confidence along the trajectory while incorrect ones show declining confidence. Leveraging this, the proposed Confidence Dynamic Gain (CDG) voting method achieves consistent accuracy improvements (avg +5.4% over majority voting) across 4 models and 4 math benchmarks.

## 核心贡献

- **发现置信度动态模式**：首次系统揭示LLM推理轨迹中正确与错误回答在置信度变化趋势上的显著差异——正确轨迹的置信度从头到尾递增，错误轨迹递减或增长疲弱
- **提出CDG投票方法**：利用推理链首尾置信度差值（置信度动态增益）作为判别信号，结合均值置信度和计数衰减进行加权投票
- **理论解释**：从GRPO训练动态出发，证明正确轨迹在尾部因答案收敛而获得更强的token增强，从而产生更大的置信度增益
- **广泛实验验证**：在4个开源模型（DeepSeek-R1-8B、gpt-oss-20B、Gemma-3-27B、QwQ-32B）和4个竞赛级数学基准上取得一致改进
- **方法统一性**：CDG是多数投票和DeepConf的泛化形式（多数投票对应α=1,β=0；DeepConf对应α=1,β=0且只看均值置信度）

## Key Contributions

- Identify a novel empirical phenomenon: correct reasoning traces show systematically higher confidence gains from head to tail than incorrect traces across diverse LLM architectures
- Propose CDG voting, which incorporates tail-minus-head confidence dynamics into answer selection, generalizing majority voting and static confidence methods
- Provide theoretical analysis linking the phenomenon to GRPO training dynamics — asymmetric advantage assignment combined with answer token concentration at tail positions creates divergent confidence gradients
- Demonstrate consistent improvements across 4 open-source models on AIME 2024/25, HMMT 25, and BRUMO 25 benchmarks

## Methodology

**Confidence Metric:** Token-level confidence is defined via KL divergence from a uniform distribution over top-K token probabilities (K=20), following DeepConf.

**Position-Normalized Binning:** Each reasoning trace is partitioned into N=10 equal-sized bins based on token position, normalizing across traces of varying lengths. Each bin's mean confidence is computed, yielding a fixed-dimensional trajectory representation.

**Confidence Dynamic Gain (CDG):** For each trace, CDG is computed as the difference between tail bin confidence (last P%=10% of positions) and head bin confidence (first P%=10%):

$$\Delta C_\ell = \bar{C}_\ell^{tail} - \bar{C}_\ell^{head}$$

**Voting Score:** Each trace receives a score combining mean confidence and CDG:

$$s_\ell = \bar{C}_\ell + \beta \cdot \Delta C_\ell$$

**Answer Selection:** The final answer score combines count dampening with mean trace scores:

$$R(a) = |T_a|^\alpha \cdot \mu_a(s_\ell)$$

where $\alpha=0.5$ dampens the dominance of raw answer counts, and $\beta$ is model-specific (scaled by the ratio of mean confidence to CDG separation).

**Theoretical Framework:** Under GRPO training with verifiable rewards, correct traces converge to the same ground-truth tokens at the tail (high frequency, strong reinforcement), while early reasoning tokens are diverse (low frequency, weaker reinforcement). This creates an M-fold tail-to-head logit ratio for correct traces but a lower ratio ($\gamma M$, $\gamma < 1$) for incorrect traces, producing the observed confidence gain separation.

## Results & Highlights

- **CDG vs Majority Voting:** +5.4% average accuracy improvement across all models and datasets
- **CDG vs DeepConf-Mean:** +4.8% average improvement; CDG vs DeepConf-Tail: +1.7%
- **DeepSeek-R1-8B:** 90.8% average (vs 84.2% majority voting), reaching 93.3% on AIME 2024/25 and BRUMO 2025
- **gpt-oss-20B:** 85.8% average (vs 78.3% majority voting)
- **QwQ-32B:** 80.0% average (vs 71.7% majority voting)
- **Gemma-3-27B:** 41.7% average (vs 35.0% majority voting), showing consistent though smaller gains
- **Scaling:** CDG advantage persists across all trace budgets (L=8 to 512), with steeper improvement curves than baselines
- **Score separation:** CDG score distributions show the clearest separation between correct and incorrect traces compared to mean confidence or tail confidence alone
- **Robustness:** Results are robust to position percentile P% and are not driven by `\boxed{}` formatting tokens (0.0% accuracy difference when excluded)
- **GPQA-Diamond:** Initial generalization beyond math reasoning shows promise

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High — the confidence dynamics observation is genuinely novel and the theoretical connection to GRPO training is insightful. The finding that correct traces gain confidence while incorrect ones lose it is surprising and non-obvious.
- **Rigor:** Strong — includes formal theorems with proofs, statistical significance tests, and extensive ablations (α, β, P%, trace count L, trace length, effect of `\boxed{}` tokens).
- **Reproducibility:** Good — open-source models, public benchmarks, code promised. Hyperparameters (α=0.5, P=10%, β model-specific) are clearly specified with a principled selection rule.
- **Significance:** High for the inference-time scaling community. CDG provides a training-free improvement that is orthogonal to model capability — it gets more from existing model outputs.
- **Red flags:** β requires per-model calibration via cross-benchmark validation, which weakens the "zero-config" claim. The theoretical analysis relies on several simplifying assumptions (simplified training model, first-order approximation) that are acknowledged but may not hold precisely in practice. Evaluated primarily on math reasoning; broader task coverage is limited.

## Strengths

- Elegant and intuitive core insight — confidence should rise along correct reasoning chains
- Simple implementation: only requires top-K token logprobs, no training or reward model needed
- Generalizes majority voting and DeepConf as special cases
- Consistent improvements across all 4 model architectures and all 4 benchmarks (15/16 configurations)
- Strong theoretical grounding connecting the empirical observation to GRPO training dynamics
- Excellent ablation suite covering all hyperparameters and potential confounds

## Limitations

- **Benchmark scope:** Primarily evaluated on competition math (AIME/HMMT/BRUMO); generalization to code, general reasoning, or open-ended tasks is not demonstrated
- **Model-specific β tuning:** The CDG weight β must be calibrated per model using a validation set, reducing zero-shot applicability
- **Logprob access requirement:** Requires top-K token logprobs at every decoding step, which not all APIs expose
- **Compute cost:** Inherits the Best-of-N cost profile (512 traces per question); CDG aggregation itself is cheap but trace generation is expensive
- **Marginal gains on weaker models:** Improvements on Gemma-3-27B are smaller in absolute terms, suggesting the signal may be weaker when models are less capable
- **Theoretical assumptions:** The analysis simplifies GRPO training dynamics and may not fully capture multi-epoch effects or architectural specifics

## 相关性

本文对推理阶段优化（inference-time scaling）研究高度相关。CDG提供了一种无需训练的投票增强方法，直接提升Best-of-N推理的准确率，对于任何使用LLM进行数学推理或多步推理的研究者都有实用价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does CDG's confidence dynamics signal hold for non-mathematical reasoning tasks (e.g., code generation, legal reasoning, multi-hop QA)?
- Can the β parameter be estimated automatically from model architecture or training metadata without per-model calibration?
- How does CDG interact with process reward models or outcome reward models — could confidence dynamics complement reward-based selection?
- Does the confidence gain phenomenon persist in models trained with algorithms other than GRPO (e.g., PPO, DPO, RLOO)?
- Could CDG be applied dynamically during generation to enable early stopping or trace pruning, reducing compute costs?
