AI Cartography: Mapping the Latent Landscape of AI Benchmark Ecosystems

ICML 2026 PMLR 306 · Seoul Hardy, Reuel, Zhang, Casabianca, Truong, Dave, Lee, Domingue, Koyejo — Stanford · BroadMetrics

A bifactor latent structure best explains 4,000+ LLMs across 6 benchmarks — a dominant general factor g plus benchmark-specific residuals. After noise control, only 1 of the original top-1% models remains in the top 1%.

Key Metric Rg = 0.97 vs Rβ = 0.53 manifest slope
Why This Matters Leaderboard rankings are far noisier than they appear. Scaling laws are vectors, not a single scalar.
Critical Edge The "alignment tax" may be a benchmark artifact. Bifactor models over-extract general factors in LLM data — an unstudied bias.

Methodology

STAGE 1
CFA — Structure Discovery

Six competing latent structures tested on 6 benchmarks via meta-analytic item-set bootstrapping. Bifactor model wins (RMSEA=0.05, AUC=0.95).

STAGE 2
Generalizability Theory — Noise

Fully crossed variance decomposition across architecture, benchmark, contributor, deployment. Benchmark ≫ contributor > architecture > deployment.

STAGE 3
Latent Regression — Inference

Mixed-effects regression on bifactor latent traits. Replaces scalar scaling law with a per-dimension vector β, each with reliability Rd.

Strengths

  • First full psychometric framework applied at scale (4,000+ models) to AI benchmarks
  • Three methods interlock logically: structure → noise → inference
  • Multiple anti-overfitting safeguards: permutation controls, out-of-sample validation, dual estimation
  • Actionable: identifies which benchmarks respond to scale vs. tuning vs. neither
  • Honest about limitations; published at ICML 2026

Limitations

  • Single leaderboard snapshot, 6 benchmarks — no code, multimodal, or agentic data
  • Bifactor models over-extract general factors; bias transfer to LLM data is unstudied
  • Contributor variance (~9%) conflates data quality, compute, and training recipes
  • Observational data — no causal claims possible
  • Computationally expensive: MH-RM with bootstrapped item sets

Open Questions

  • How do latent structures change with code, multimodal, or agentic benchmarks?
  • Is the MuSR–size negative relationship robust across time and ecosystem shifts?
  • Can the framework handle dynamic leaderboards with continuously added models?
  • Should noise-adjusted rankings become standard for regulatory evaluations?
  • Can contributor variance be decomposed into interpretable sub-factors?
  • Do new architectures (MoE) shift the latent factor loadings?
