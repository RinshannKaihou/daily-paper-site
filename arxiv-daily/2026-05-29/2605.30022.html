Transformer Architecture Positional Encoding Mechanistic Interpretability Representation Learning

Give it Space! Explicit Disentangling of Positional and Semantic Representations in Encoders

Pierre-Antoine Lequeu · Camille Barboule · Benjamin Piwowarski  — Sorbonne Université / ISIR & Orange Innovation
Key Finding

When absolute-position embeddings are architecturally isolated from semantics, they spontaneously collapse into a 2D low-frequency manifold—capturing 94.1% of variance in just 2 principal components—revealing that transformers need minimal positional bandwidth when relative position handles local context.

Σ
Key Metric

Linguistic phenomena improved on Flash-Holmes probing across all 5 fields (Syntax 18/21, Semantics 17/24, Discourse 5/6, Reasoning 6/10, Morphology 3/4). Zero degradation on GLUE, MTEB, SQuAD.
▲
Why This Matters
Standard MLM training destroys positional information in the final layer—token-level AP R² drops from 0.99 to 0.34. Disentanglement preserves structural information that entangled models silently lose.
⚠
Critical Edge
Probing improvements do not translate to meaningful gains on downstream benchmarks (GLUE/MTEB/SQuAD). Evaluated on encoder-only at small scale—no evidence yet that findings transfer to decoder architectures or larger models.

Methodology — DSTG-NeoBERT

01 / Three Streams

Semantic + AP + RP

Each token splits into a dsem semantic embedding and a dAP positional embedding with independent RMSNorms. T5-style bucketed RP bias adds relative context.

02 / Fused Attention

Cross-Stream Scores

Q/K computed independently per stream per head, projected to the same dhead. Attention logits fuse semantic, AP, and RP weights before shared softmax.

03 / SwiGLU Bridge

Controlled Mixing

FFN operates on concatenated [AP; semantic] representation, then splits output back into separate streams. Cross-stream interaction confined to the feed-forward layer only.

04 / MLM Confinement

Protect AP from MLM

MLM prediction head operates solely on the semantic subspace. The AP component receives zero gradient from MLM, preserving structural information through the full depth.

Strengths

  • Clean three-stream design enabling controlled mechanistic analysis of positional vs. semantic information
  • Compelling evidence of spontaneous 2D low-frequency collapse in AP embeddings
  • MLM-confined-to-semantic-stream elegantly solves positional information destruction
  • Three-level probing hierarchy (token, segment, intra-segment) reveals exactly where entangled models fail
  • Complementary analysis methods: PCA, DCT, KL-divergence ablation, probing, benchmarking
  • Honest about limitations; code and scripts shared for reproducibility

Limitations

  • Encoder-only evaluation; decoder architectures (the dominant paradigm) are not tested
  • Probing improvements do not translate to downstream task gains on GLUE/MTEB/SQuAD
  • Requires training from scratch; no path to retrofit existing pretrained models
  • Small model scale; unclear if 2D collapse holds at billions of parameters
  • No evaluation on long-context tasks where positional encoding matters most
  • Circular argument risk: low dAP/nheads suffices because the model learns to use only low-bandwidth given the constraint

Open Questions

Does the spontaneous 2D AP collapse hold at billion-parameter scale and with decoder architectures?
Can the disentangled AP manifold be directly manipulated for length extrapolation?
Would caching position-independent semantic representations provide practical efficiency gains for RAG systems?
Is it possible to retrofit the disentangled AP stream onto a pretrained model via fine-tuning rather than training from scratch?
How does the architecture behave on long-context tasks (>4K tokens) where positional encoding is most critical?
Does the AP-driven vs. semantic-driven head taxonomy transfer to decoder-only models with causal attention?
