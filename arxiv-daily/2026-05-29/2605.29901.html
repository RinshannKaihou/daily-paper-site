Dissecting the Black Box: Circuit-Level Analysis of LLM Vulnerability Detection

LLMs do not hunt for vulnerabilities — they verify compliance. Gemma-2-2b detects unsafe code by recognizing the absence of safe patterns, not the presence of exploits.

Syafiq Al Atiiq · Chun Zhou · Christian Gehrmann | Lund University | 2026-05-28

Vulnerability detection runs on a sparse sub-network. Ablating Layer 11 collapses TP accuracy from 100% → 6%. Ablating 20 neurons in Layer 7 cuts it by 50%.

WHY THIS MATTERS

The "detection by absence" mechanism is inherently fragile. Adversaries can inject inert safety checks to trigger false negatives — a class of attack the paper terms Safety Mimicry.

Implication: deploying LLM vulnerability detectors in production without understanding their circuits exposes a concrete, exploitable attack surface.

CRITICAL EDGE
  • Single model only (Gemma-2-2b) — circuits may differ in larger or different architectures
  • 100% baseline accuracy is suspiciously perfect; evaluation subset may be too easy
  • Safety Mimicry attack theorized but not empirically validated
  • No comparison with traditional static analysis tools or other ML detectors

Approach

  • Dataset: 472 C/C++ samples (236 vulnerable / 236 safe) from Big-Vul, spanning 9 CWE categories
  • Circuit Tracer v1.0.0 on Gemma-2-2b with attribution threshold 0.01 across all 26 layers
  • L0 Norm Analysis quantifying active neurons per layer (threshold hi > 10−6)
  • Attention Head Importance Score Ih measuring functional divergence between TP and TN contexts (λ=0.5)
  • MLP Vulnerability Selectivity Score Sn ranking neurons by activation divergence; top-20 extracted
  • Causal Intervention: layer-wise ablation, neuron-level ablation, attention head knockout

Key Ablation Results

  • Layer 11 ablation: 100% → 6% TP accuracy
  • Layers 6+7 ablation: accuracy drops to 0%
  • 20 neurons in Layer 7 ablated: −50% accuracy
  • L5-H2 and L7-H6 knockout significantly degrades performance

Sparse Computation

A single CWE-787 inference activates only 1,597 of 9,944 probed nodes. Layer 0: 414 active; Layer 25: 19 active.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths
  • Counterintuitive "safety detector" finding reshapes understanding of LLM security reasoning
  • Multi-method causal validation (L0 norm, attention, MLP probing, ablation)
  • Novel metrics (Importance Score, Vulnerability Selectivity Score)
  • Clear practical implications for adversarial vulnerability
  • Honest limitations discussion with concrete future-work directions
Limitations
  • Single architecture (Gemma-2-2b) limits generalizability
  • 9 CWE categories, C/C++ only
  • 472 samples adequate for circuit discovery but thin for per-CWE stats
  • Modest selectivity score magnitude (peak −0.25) suggests weak signal
  • Safety Mimicry theorized but untested

Open Questions
