Key Finding

Cross-entropy logit alignment for the final Transformer block restores low-bit LLM generation quality where MSE fails.

LFQ · Jung Hyun Lee, June Yong Yang, Jungwook Choi, Eunho Yang · KAIST / LG AI Research / Hanyang Univ. / AITRICS
Weight-only PTQ · W4 / W3 · Zero inference overhead · Single-GPU quantization
Anchor Metric
+13.3 pp
AIME'24 greedy accuracy on L1-Qwen-7B-Max at W4.
30.00 → 43.33. Reasoning model, 4-bit quantization.
Why This Matters
Solves quantization degradation in long chain-of-thought reasoning — zero inference overhead, drop-in fix for any block-wise PTQ pipeline.
Critical Edge
AIME has only 30 problems per year; IFEval gains stay modest at 1–2 pp. Largest improvements concentrate on narrow reasoning benchmarks with small sample sizes.
Methodology

Core insight: Block-wise PTQ minimizes MSE between block outputs, but the LM head projects these into a vocabulary-sized logit space where small MSE differences can flip the argmax token. A 2-token example: logits [0.6, 0.4] → [0.4, 0.6] yields MSE = 0.08 but predicts the wrong token; [0.9, 0.1] → [0.6, 0.4] has higher MSE (0.18) yet preserves the correct top-1.

LFQ objective (Eq. 3): For the final Transformer block only, minimize cross-entropy between FP logits and quantized logits (softmax over the full vocabulary). This is equivalent to minimizing KL divergence between the two distributions. All prior blocks continue using standard MSE reconstruction.

Compatibility: Drop-in on top of FlexRound, OmniQuant, and Block-AP. Adds no inference overhead — only the calibration-time loss function changes. Single-GPU feasible.

Strengths
  • Elegant theoretical motivation: cross-entropy = KL divergence minimization, with a clean 2-token counterexample showing MSE failure
  • Zero inference overhead — only the calibration-time loss changes
  • Agnostic to underlying PTQ method: validated with FlexRound, OmniQuant, and Block-AP
  • Comprehensive evaluation: dense, reasoning, and MoE architectures across 6 benchmarks
  • Strong gains precisely where quantization hurts most: long chain-of-thought reasoning
  • Thorough ablations: LM head inclusion, number of LFQ blocks, calibration data, learning rates
Limitations
  • Only evaluated on weight-only quantization (W4, W3); no weight+activation or 2-bit results
  • AIME has 30 problems/year — point improvements like 30→43 are noisy despite cross-setting consistency
  • Targets only the final block; earlier blocks still use MSE, possibly leaving gains on the table
  • No wall-clock quantization time reported; computing softmax over the full vocabulary per calibration step is costlier than MSE
  • IFEval gains are modest (1–2 pp) compared to dramatic AIME improvements
Open Questions
  • Can logit alignment be adapted for weight+activation quantization, or does per-layer activation quantization change the dynamics fundamentally?
  • What is the quantization-time overhead of computing softmax over the full vocabulary at each calibration step?
  • Does LFQ help at 2-bit quantization where errors are much larger, or does the cross-entropy objective become infeasible to minimize?
  • Could a hybrid objective (weighted MSE + cross-entropy) for intermediate blocks yield further generation gains?
  • How does LFQ interact with quantization-aware training — could logit-level alignment improve QAT-quantized models?
