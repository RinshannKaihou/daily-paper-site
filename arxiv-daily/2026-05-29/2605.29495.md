> **一句话总结：** 提出On-Policy Replay (OPR)方法，通过用当前模型在历史prompt上生成on-policy响应并经reward筛选后回放为普通SFT数据，在无需教师模型或辅助损失的情况下将灾难性遗忘(|BWT|)降低42-46%，并从KL收缩理论角度统一解释了数据侧与损失侧on-policy方法。

> **TL;DR:** On-Policy Replay (OPR) replaces stale gold-answer replay buffers with the model's own reward-filtered generations on historical prompts, enforcing KL-shrinkage through data distribution rather than auxiliary losses. Across three 7-8B LLMs on TRACE, OPR cuts |BWT| by 42-46% over Vanilla Replay, matches SDFT without needing a teacher forward pass, and reveals via a low-score ablation that the on-policy distribution (not response quality alone) is the active ingredient.

## 核心贡献

- **提出OPR方法**：将on-policy信号通过训练数据源而非训练目标引入，用当前checkpoint在历史prompt上生成响应，经reward筛选后作为普通SFT样本回放，完全不需要教师模型、辅助损失或蒸馏
- **两种评分变体**：OPR-RU使用任务级评估指标评分（精确匹配、ROUGE-L等）；OPR-SC使用模型自身长度归一化log概率评分，无需任何ground-truth标签
- **KL收缩理论框架**：证明OPR的回放梯度等价于沿着KL(q_{t-1} || pi_theta)下降方向，将per-update KL上界控制在l_max以内，统一解释了数据侧OPR和损失侧SDFT方法
- **反直觉消融发现**：低分回放比Vanilla Replay更差（BWT -3.47 vs -1.37），证明on-policy分布本身而非响应质量才是关键因素，排除了"replay-of-anything"的解释
- **强实验验证**：在TRACE基准上跨三个7-8B backbone，1%回放预算即将Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct的BWT从-13.93提升至-2.29（46%改善），且总体准确率无损失

## Key Contributions

- Introduces OPR, which routes on-policy signal through the replay buffer data source rather than through auxiliary loss terms, leaving the SFT training loop completely untouched
- Provides two scorer variants: OPR-RU (rule-based, using task-specific evaluation metrics) and OPR-SC (self-confidence, using the model's own length-normalized log-probability at zero additional cost)
- Derives a formal KL-shrinkage bound: the replay gradient equals -nabla_theta KL(q_{t-1} || pi_theta), and the high-reward filter upper-bounds per-sample loss at l_max, directly controlling the leading-order forgetting term
- Demonstrates via a falsifying low-score ablation that on-policy sampling alone is insufficient -- the high-reward filter is the decisive factor, ruling out "replay of anything" explanations
- Shows that SDFT's teacher anchor causes training loss plateaus on tasks requiring large distributional shifts (MeetingBank, Py150, 20Minuten), while OPR-SC avoids this failure mode

## Methodology

**Problem setup.** A continual SFT learner observes tasks T_1,...,T_N sequentially. After training on task T_i, the model pi_{theta_i} must retain performance on all prior tasks. Forgetting is measured by Backward Transfer (BWT), the average accuracy change between end-of-task and end-of-sequence.

**Theoretical motivation.** The paper builds on the bound from Shenfeld et al. (2025): forgetting at each SGD step is upper-bounded by the per-update KL between consecutive policies on historical prompts. Prior methods (SDFT, OPSD, GKD) minimize this bound by adding an explicit KL loss term against a frozen teacher copy. OPR instead minimizes it through data distribution.

**OPR pipeline (Algorithm 1):**
1. After training on task T_i, roll out the current checkpoint pi_{theta_i} on all historical prompts (one response per prompt, low-temperature decoding at T=0.1)
2. Score each (prompt, response) pair using either a task-specific metric (OPR-RU) or the model's own length-normalized log-probability (OPR-SC)
3. Keep the top-b pairs (b = rho * n_{i+1}, distributed equally across prior tasks) as the replay buffer
4. Concatenate the buffer with the next task's data and train under standard cross-entropy SFT

**KL-shrinkage analysis.** The high-reward filter ensures every buffer item (x, y_tilde) has low cross-entropy loss at theta_{t-1} (Eq. 3: l(theta_{t-1}; x, y_tilde) <= l_max). The expected replay gradient equals nabla_theta E_x[KL(q_{t-1} || pi_theta)] (Eq. 5), pulling the model toward the previous checkpoint's high-reward region. Combined with Fisher information bounds, this yields E_{p_hist}[KL(pi_{theta_t} || pi_{theta_{t-1}})] <= L * sigma_max * eta^2 * l_max (Eq. 6), directly controlling forgetting through the filter threshold.

**Falsifying prediction.** The theory predicts that low-score on-policy replay should be worse than Vanilla Replay: it preserves Eq. 5 but breaks the loss bound (Eq. 3), making l_max large and the forgetting bound loose. This is confirmed empirically.

## Results & Highlights

**Main results (TRACE, rho=0.01):**
- Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct: Sequential SFT BWT=-13.93, Vanilla Replay BWT=-4.13, OPR-RU BWT=-2.29 (44% |BWT| reduction), OPR-SC BWT=-2.08
- Qwen3-8B: OPR-RU cuts |BWT| by 46% over Vanilla Replay
- Llama3.1-8B-Instruct: Consistent 42% |BWT| reduction
- OPR matches or exceeds SDFT on overall accuracy without requiring a teacher forward pass

**Budget sensitivity (Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct):**
- rho=0.01: BWT=-2.29, ACC=65.45
- rho=0.10: BWT=-0.65, ACC=65.92
- OPR-RU accuracy is nearly flat across budgets (0.47 pp range), while Vanilla Replay swings by 3.51 pp

**Low-score ablation (rho=0.10):** Low-score OPR BWT=-3.47 vs. Vanilla Replay BWT=-1.37 and high-score OPR-RU BWT=-0.65. On-policy sampling alone is worse than off-policy gold data.

**Loss dynamics:** SDFT's training loss plateaus on tasks requiring large distributional shifts (MeetingBank, Py150, 20Minuten) because the teacher's reverse-KL anchor prevents the cross-entropy from descending. OPR-SC's loss drops normally on all tasks.

**Per-task breakdown:** SDFT shows a -23 to -32 pp deficit on MeetingBank across backbones. Neither OPR-RU nor OPR-SC produces a comparable failure mode.

**Training cost:** 32 GPU-hours total (8xH100) for a single model across all 8 TRACE tasks. Rollout runs as a separate subprocess using vLLM with tensor parallel size 4.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate-High):** While individual components (self-generation, reward filtering, replay) are well-known, the core insight -- routing on-policy signal through the replay buffer rather than the loss function -- is genuinely novel. The KL-shrinkage derivation that unifies data-side and loss-side methods on a single axis is a clean theoretical contribution. The low-score ablation as a falsifying experiment is unusually rigorous for an empirical ML paper.
- **Rigor (Strong):** The paper derives formal bounds (Eqs. 3-6), makes a falsifying prediction from the theory, and confirms it empirically. The low-score ablation is a particularly strong design choice. Evaluation across 3 backbones with shared hyperparameters, 3 random seeds, and controlled baselines is thorough. The loss dynamics analysis (Figure 4) provides mechanistic insight beyond headline numbers.
- **Reproducibility (Strong):** Public code, standard benchmarks, widely available models, clearly described algorithm (Algorithm 1), fixed hyperparameters across methods. The full training cost is only 32 GPU-hours, making reproduction feasible for most labs.
- **Significance (High):** The method is simple, practical, and effective. By eliminating the need for a teacher model or auxiliary loss, OPR lowers the engineering complexity of continual SFT significantly. The theoretical framing could shift how the community thinks about replay-based continual learning. The finding that low-score replay is actively harmful is an important practical insight.
- **Red flags:** (1) OPR-SC fails on tasks where the model is "confidently wrong" (e.g., NumGLUE-cm), since fluent but incorrect outputs pass the confidence filter -- this is acknowledged but not deeply addressed. (2) Evaluation is limited to TRACE's 8 English tasks on 7-8B models; generalization to larger models, non-English settings, or different task orderings is untested. (3) The dependence on task-specific evaluators for OPR-RU is a practical limitation, though OPR-SC partially addresses this.

## Strengths

- Extremely simple method: no new loss functions, no teacher models, no schedule tuning -- just filtered self-generation replayed as SFT data
- Strong theoretical grounding: KL-shrinkage derivation unifies OPR with prior distillation methods and makes falsifiable predictions
- The low-score ablation is a model of rigorous empirical work: it directly tests and confirms the theoretical mechanism
- Loss dynamics analysis reveals *why* SDFT fails on certain tasks, going beyond "our method beats baseline"
- Label-free variant (OPR-SC) removes dependence on task evaluators at minimal performance cost
- Modest compute requirements (32 GPU-hours) make the method accessible

## Limitations

- OPR-SC degrades on tasks where the model is confidently wrong (NumGLUE-cm), since fluent-but-incorrect rollouts pass the confidence filter -- this is a fundamental limitation of self-supervised scoring
- Only evaluated on TRACE's canonical 8-task English sequence; no validation on base (non-instruction-tuned) checkpoints, models above ~10B parameters, alternative task orderings, or non-English streams
- Rollout generation requires storing historical prompts, which may be problematic in privacy-sensitive or production settings
- The high-reward filter introduces a dependency on scorer quality that is assumed but not deeply analyzed (beyond the OPR-RU vs. OPR-SC comparison)
- Does not address whether continual SFT is the right paradigm at all, versus alternatives like model merging or dynamic architecture methods
- The per-update KL bound assumes L-smoothness and small step sizes; practical violations are not discussed

## Relevance

该论文直接解决LLM持续微调中的灾难性遗忘问题，提出的on-policy回放方法简单实用（仅需在历史prompt上生成并筛选响应），且理论框架清晰。对于需要在不遗忘旧任务的情况下持续适应新任务的LLM部署场景（如多阶段SFT pipeline）具有直接参考价值，KL收缩的分析视角也值得关注。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does OPR interact with parameter-efficient methods (LoRA, adapters) -- does the KL-shrinkage mechanism transfer when only a subset of parameters is updated?
- Can the scorer be learned end-to-end (as the authors suggest), and would a learned scorer resolve the "confidently wrong" failure mode of OPR-SC?
- How does OPR scale to much longer task sequences (50+ tasks) where historical prompt budgets become extremely constrained?
- Does the method transfer to multimodal continual fine-tuning (vision-language models) or code generation domains?
- What happens when task boundaries are blurred or unknown -- can OPR work in a streaming setting without explicit task delineation?
- Could combining OPR with regularization-based methods (e.g., EWC on the replay buffer parameters) yield further improvements?
