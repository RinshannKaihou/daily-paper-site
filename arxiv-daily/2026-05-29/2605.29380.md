> **一句话总结：** TRACER 通过理论分析揭示 EMA 教师在对比微调中存在坍塌问题，提出基于加权移动平均（WMA）教师的多视角蒸馏方法，在 CLIP ViT-B/16 上实现 64.37% 的平均 OOD 准确率（比 CaRot 提升 1.86 个百分点），并在 RN50、ViT-L/14 三个骨干网络上一致有效。

> **TL;DR:** TRACER develops a theoretical framework that reformulates linearized multimodal contrastive finetuning as a least-squares problem, yielding closed-form solutions for each strategy and a geometric decomposition showing where forgetting occurs. The analysis reveals that standard EMA teachers collapse their regularizing signal during training, and proves that a Weighted Moving Average (WMA) teacher maintains persistent regularization and bias-free convergence. TRACER combines WMA-guided multi-perspective distillation (feature, contrastive-relational, interactive-contrastive, cross-KD) with contrastive learning, achieving 64.37% avg OOD accuracy on ViT-B/16 (+1.86 over CaRot) with consistent gains across RN50 and ViT-L/14 backbones.

## 核心贡献

- 建立了线性化多模态对比微调的理论框架，将对比目标重构为矩阵最小二乘问题（"对比目标矩阵"），为直接微调、L2-SP、静态自蒸馏给出闭式解
- 提出几何分解理论：微调包含 (i) 在与微调数据正交方向保留预训练知识，(ii) 在任务相关子空间内调整或混合知识，揭示了不同策略的遗忘位置
- 揭示标准 EMA 教师在鲁棒微调中的坍塌问题：随训练收敛，EMA 教师逐渐逼近学生，正则化信号恰好消失在 OOD 鲁棒性最脆弱的时期
- 证明 WMA 教师在有限训练视野内保持持续正则化力，在任务子空间实现无偏收敛（定理 3.4），同时保留正交子空间的预训练知识
- 在三个 CLIP 骨干网络（ViT-B/16, RN50, ViT-L/14）上取得一致的 OOD 准确率和校准提升，ViT-B/16 上平均 OOD 达 64.37%

## Key Contributions

- **Theoretical framework:** Reformulates linearized multimodal contrastive finetuning as a matrix least-squares problem via the "contrastive target matrix," yielding closed-form solutions for Direct FT, L2-SP, and static self-distillation (Theorem 3.2)
- **Geometric decomposition:** Separates finetuning dynamics into (i) preservation of pretrained knowledge in directions orthogonal to finetuning data, and (ii) adaptation within the task-relevant subspace -- explaining when and where forgetting occurs for each strategy
- **EMA collapse identification:** Shows that EMA teachers converge toward the student, causing the regularizing signal to vanish precisely when OOD robustness is most fragile -- a largely overlooked limitation in robust finetuning literature
- **WMA teacher theory:** Proves that a Weighted Moving Average teacher (Definition 3.3) with Beta(0.5,0.5) kernel maintains persistent regularizing force over finite horizons and achieves bias-free convergence in the task subspace (Theorem 3.4) while preserving orthogonal knowledge
- **TRACER method:** Combines symmetric InfoNCE loss with a composite WMA-guided distillation loss spanning four perspectives: Feature Distillation (FD), Contrastive Relational Distillation (CRD), Interactive Contrastive Learning (ICL), and Cross Knowledge Distillation (Cross-KD)
- **Empirical validation:** Achieves 64.37% avg OOD accuracy on ViT-B/16 (vs. 62.59% for CaRot), with consistent gains across RN50 and ViT-L/14; comprehensive ablations across distillation strength, update frequency, and kernel shape confirm principled design

## Methodology

**Theoretical framework (Section 3):** The authors model image and text encoders as linear projections (W_I, W_T) following established linearized analysis. The linearized multimodal contrastive learning (MMCL) loss is reformulated as a matrix least-squares problem: min_{W_I} (1/2)||W_I X - Y_FT||_F^2. This "contrastive target matrix" reformulation enables closed-form solutions for three strategies:

1. **Direct FT:** W_FT = W_0(I-P_I) + Y_FT X_I^+ -- preserves orthogonal component, replaces parallel component entirely
2. **L2-SP:** W_{L2} = (Y_FT X_I^T + lambda*W_0)(X_I X_I^T + lambda*I)^{-1} -- global blend without structural decomposition
3. **Static Self-Distillation:** W_SD = W_0(I - 1/(1+lambda)*P_I) + 1/(1+lambda)*Y_FT X_I^T(X_I X_I^T)^+ -- preserves orthogonal component, forms convex combination in parallel component

**Key insight on static SD bias:** In the task subspace, static SD produces a convex combination offset from the task optimum by 1/(1+lambda)*(W_0 P_I - W_FT), creating an irreducible anchor bias.

**WMA Teacher (Definition 3.3):** The teacher is a kernel-weighted average of the entire student trajectory: W_teacher^t = (1-w_t)*W_teacher^{t-1} + w_t*W_t, where weights come from a kernel kappa on normalized time (e.g., Beta(0.5, 0.5)). This U-shaped kernel retains mass at both the start (pretrained) and end (current) of training, maintaining a persistent restoring force.

**TRACER training objective:** L_TRACER = L_MMCL + lambda_SD * L_SD-WMA, where the distillation loss combines four perspectives: (i) Feature Distillation aligning embeddings, (ii) Contrastive Relational Distillation matching similarity distributions, (iii) Interactive Contrastive Learning for student-teacher cross-modal alignment, (iv) Cross Knowledge Distillation aligning cross-modal logits.

- TRACER achieves best OOD accuracy on nearly every distribution shift variant
- Cross-backbone experiments confirm consistent trends on RN50 and ViT-L/14
- CKA/SVCCA analysis shows TRACER maintains representation similarity >0.97 across all layers with the pretrained model, while Direct FT drifts significantly in deeper layers
- Ablation studies confirm robustness across four axes: distillation components, regularization strength, teacher update schedule, and kernel shape
- All results averaged over 3 random seeds with standard deviations reported
- Training setup: 10 epochs, AdamW optimizer, lr=1e-4, weight decay=0.01, batch size 512 (ViT-B/16/RN50) or 224 (ViT-L/14)

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The contrastive target matrix reformulation yielding closed-form solutions is a clean theoretical contribution. The EMA collapse identification is both surprising and important -- it explains why existing methods struggle and motivates the WMA fix. The WMA teacher with Beta kernel is elegant and well-justified.
- **Rigor:** Very strong. The paper provides formal theorems (3.2, 3.4) with proofs in appendices. The geometric decomposition is precise and testable. Empirical protocol is thorough: 3 seeds, standard benchmark suite, multiple backbones, four-axis ablations.
- **Reproducibility:** Excellent. Public code, detailed hyperparameters (lr, epochs, batch sizes, kernel parameters), all evaluation datasets are standard and publicly available. The WMA teacher update rule is given as a simple online recursion.
- **Significance:** High for the CLIP finetuning community. The 1.86pp OOD improvement over CaRot is meaningful, and the theoretical insights about EMA collapse have implications beyond this specific method. However, the scope is limited to vision-language CLIP-style models.
- **Red flags:** The linearized analysis is an approximation -- real networks are highly nonlinear. The paper acknowledges this but the gap between theory and practice could be significant. The Beta(0.5,0.5) kernel choice, while motivated, is not derived from first principles. Results are limited to ImageNet and its distribution shifts; broader evaluation (e.g., domain transfer, few-shot) would strengthen claims.

## Strengths

- Deep theoretical grounding with formal proofs, not just empirical validation
- The EMA collapse insight is a genuinely novel and practically important finding
- Clean geometric interpretation makes the method interpretable (Figure 2)
- Multi-perspective distillation loss is well-motivated by the geometric decomposition
- Comprehensive ablation studies across four axes confirm principled design
- Minimal computational overhead: WMA is a simple online update, no extra forward passes for the teacher beyond EMA
- Honest discussion of limitations and scope in the conclusion

## Limitations

- Theoretical analysis relies on linearized encoders; real CLIP models are highly nonlinear
- Empirical evaluation is limited to CLIP on ImageNet distribution shifts; no evaluation on other modalities, multimodal LLMs, or domain transfer tasks
- The Beta(0.5,0.5) kernel is a heuristic choice; the paper does not provide guidance on kernel selection for different scenarios
- No comparison with parameter-efficient finetuning methods (e.g., LoRA, adapters) that also aim to preserve pretrained knowledge
- The multi-perspective distillation adds 4 loss components; the interaction between these components is not fully analyzed
- Training requires maintaining a trajectory-averaged teacher, which has a slightly higher memory footprint than EMA (though still O(1) via online recursion)

## Relevance

TRACER 对多模态模型微调中的 OOD 鲁棒性退化问题提供了从理论到实践的完整解决方案，其关于 EMA 教师坍塌的分析对任何使用动量教师的微调方法都有重要启示，适用于 CLIP 及类似对比学习框架的下游任务适配研究。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the WMA trajectory-regularization principle be combined with parameter-efficient methods (LoRA, adapters) to preserve pretrained knowledge with lower compute?
- Does the linearized analysis provide useful predictions for nonlinear networks, or is it primarily a design guide?
- How does TRACER interact with prompt-based adaptation methods (e.g., CoOp, MaPLe)?
- Can the Beta kernel parameters be adapted automatically during training, or is fixed Beta(0.5,0.5) sufficient for all practical scenarios?
- What happens when TRACER is applied to multimodal LLMs (e.g., LLaVA) -- does the WMA insight transfer to generative settings?
- How does the persistent regularization affect convergence speed -- does TRACER require more training steps than EMA-based methods?
