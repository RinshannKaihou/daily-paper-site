How Much Is a Dataset Worth? Scaling Laws, the Vendi Score, and Matrix Spectral Functions

Jeff A. Bilmes, Gantavya Bhatt, Arnav M. Das — University of Washington

Facility location consistently outperforms the Vendi Score for data selection across all tested regimes. Aggressively maximizing the Vendi Score leads to proxy degradation — higher scores correspond to worse downstream performance — challenging a core assumption in recent data appraisal work.

Key Metric
35,000×
average speedup on ImageNet-1K

A secular-equation-based incremental update avoids repeated eigendecompositions during greedy optimization of matrix spectral functions, reducing marginal-gain cost by O(m) per candidate. Makes direct Vendi Score optimization on million-scale datasets practical for the first time.

Why This Matters

Unifies the Vendi Score, DPPs, log-determinant, and scaling law objectives under matrix spectral functions — a single submodular framework with provable approximation guarantees. Gives practitioners a principled taxonomy for choosing (or designing) data appraisal objectives instead of picking ad hoc.

Critical Edge

Evaluated only on image classification (CIFAR-10/100, ImageNet-1K). Random fixed-size subsets show remarkably concentrated performance, raising the question: are the consistent-but-modest gains from sophisticated selection worth the engineering cost? No comparison with gradient-based methods (influence functions, TracIn).

01

Methodology

Theoretical Framework

  • Defines matrix spectral function F(V) = Σ f(λi) over eigenvalues of kernel matrix KV from subset embeddings.
  • Proves Vendi Score (with any non-decreasing concave f) is submodular — Theorem 3.6.
  • Introduces weakly matrix monotone functions yielding weakly submodular objectives with approximation guarantees — Theorem 3.13.
  • Shows neural scaling law objectives (L(S) = α·|S|−β) are also submodular set functions.

Secular Equation Speedup

  • Adding a rank-1 update (KV∪{s} = KV + φsφsT) shifts eigenvalues per the secular equation: Σj (ujTφs)2 / (λj − δ) = 1.
  • Root-finding is O(m) vs. O(m2) for full eigendecomposition, giving O(n·m) savings per greedy step.
  • Introduces three new variants: MSF-1/f, MSF-1/sqrt, MSF-tanh.
  • Evaluated on CIFAR-10/100 and ImageNet-1K with ResNet-50 embeddings under three regimes: fixed-size, class-balanced, and fixed training budget.
02

Results & Highlights

Speed

Secular equation makes Vendi Score optimization practical

~35,000x average speedup on ImageNet-1K with m=2048 embeddings reduces per-step time from hours to seconds. First-ever greedy Vendi Score optimization at this scale.

Winner

Facility location dominates across all regimes

Consistently produces subsets with the best downstream test performance on CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet-1K, under fixed-size, class-balanced, and fixed-budget settings.

Proxy Degradation

Vendi Score loses predictive power at high values

Correlates well with performance over moderate ranges, but greedy maximization produces subsets where higher Vendi Score values map to worse test accuracy. A cautionary finding.

Surprise

Random subsets are remarkably concentrated

Uniformly random fixed-size subsets show tight concentration in both appraisal scores and downstream accuracy. Standard deviation across random draws is small relative to the range spanned by optimized subsets.

Smooth Spectrum

Dataset value varies smoothly beyond size

Controlling for subset size, class balance, and training budget, downstream performance still varies smoothly from good to bad depending on which examples are selected.

DPP & New Variants

DPPs moderate; new MSF variants mixed

DPPs (log-det) promote diversity but may select uninformative examples. MSF-1/f, MSF-1/sqrt, and MSF-tanh fall between DPP and Vendi Score, none surpassing facility location.

03

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean theoretical unification of Vendi Score, DPPs, log-det, and facility location under one matrix spectral framework with provable properties.
  • Secular equation speedup is both elegant and practically impactful — enables previously infeasible ImageNet-1K experiments.
  • Intellectual honesty: reports that the Vendi Score (the motivating objective) is not the best performer and suffers proxy degradation.
  • One of the most comprehensive empirical comparisons in the data selection literature: three datasets, three regimes, seven objectives, six subset sizes.
  • Weakly matrix monotone framework provides a principled way to design new objectives with approximation guarantees.

Limitations

  • Image classification only. Generalization to NLP, detection, segmentation, or generative modeling is untested.
  • Facility location's superiority lacks deep theoretical explanation — noted empirically but not fully characterized.
  • Speedup assumes rank-1 updates (single element); does not extend to stochastic greedy or mini-batch variants.
  • No comparison with gradient-based selection methods (influence functions, TracIn, GLISTER).
  • The connection to scaling laws (Section 3.3) is somewhat superficial — does not integrate spectral diversity and scale into a single actionable framework.
04

Open Questions

Q1 Why does facility location outperform spectrally-motivated objectives? Is there a theoretical explanation rooted in pairwise coverage vs. generalization?
Q2 How do these results transfer to language model pretraining, where data is heterogeneous, sequential, and not easily embedded into a fixed-dimensional space?
Q3 Can the secular-equation speedup be combined with stochastic greedy methods for datasets with billions of examples?
Q4 What explains the concentration of random subsets — is this an artifact of CIFAR/ImageNet natural image statistics, or a more general phenomenon?
Q5 How sensitive are the results to the choice of embedding space? Would CLIP embeddings, self-supervised features, or random projections change the rankings?
Q6 Can Vendi Score proxy degradation be predicted or detected during optimization, providing a principled stopping criterion?
Q7 How do gradient-based data selection methods compare in the same experimental framework, particularly in the fixed-budget regime?
