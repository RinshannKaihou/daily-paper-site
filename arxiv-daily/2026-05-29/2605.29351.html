Attention as In-Context Empirical Bayes: A Two-Stage View via Particle Dynamics

Attention-only transformers under all-token corruption implement a two-stage empirical Bayes denoiser: depth iteratively reconstructs the prior from noisy samples via particle dynamics, while a skip connection computes the posterior mean using the original input as query.

Matthew Smart · Soumya Ganguly · Nilava Metya · Alexandre V. Morozov · Anirvan M. Sengupta — Princeton · Rutgers · arxiv.org/abs/2605.29351

Key Metric

T* = σ² / 2 integration depth

Fixed kernel bandwidth and a finite integration horizon proportional to noise variance suffice for effective denoising. No annealing or noise schedule required.

Why This Matters

Architectural components get precise statistical roles

Network depth becomes prior learning; residual connections become Bayesian posterior inference. The first framework that explains why these mechanisms matter in transformers through rigorous probability theory.

Critical Edge

2D synthetics only

All experiments use 2D Gaussian mixture priors with up to 5k tokens. The model has no learned weights, MLPs, or layer norm. The gap to practical transformers remains wide and untested.

Methodology

Two-Stage Denoising Architecture

The paper studies collective denoising: N noisy samples are jointly processed by a multilayer attention-only architecture to recover clean tokens drawn from an unknown prior.

Stage 1 — Prior Refinement (Self-Attention Depth): Each layer performs leaky residual averaging. In the continuous-depth limit this becomes a neural ODE whose mean-field limit is a nonlocal transport equation approximating reverse diffusion.

z_i^{l+1} = (1-η) z_i^l + η ∑_j α_ij z_j^l α_ij = exp(-β||z_i^l - z_j^l||²/2) / ∑_k exp(-β||z_i^l - z_k^l||²/2)

Stage 2 — Posterior Inference (Cross-Attention Skip): A long-range skip connection carries the original noisy input as a query. Cross-attention with the refined particles computes the Gaussian posterior mean, where βc = 1/σ².

Structural Insight

Why Gaussian Kernels

The Gaussian kernel is uniquely selected by two properties:

  • (A.I) Tweedie-type posterior representation — the kernel admits the standard Tweedie formula for posterior mean estimation.
  • (A.II) Attention-form score field — the score of an empirical measure can be written as an attention-weighted sum.

Only the Gaussian kernel simultaneously satisfies both, making it the canonical choice for this framework.

Convex hull invariance: The particle set's convex hull is positively invariant under the attention update for any η ∈ (0,1), providing a natural stability guarantee without external constraints.

Analytical Pipeline

Three algorithms of increasing rigor: (1) discrete attention update, (2) continuous-time mean-field limit, (3) truncated variant for analysis. The recovery theorem uses propagation of chaos, truncation, and weak-strong uniqueness via Dobrushin duality and Osgood stability.

Results

Bayes-Optimal Convergence

Multilayer attention denoiser approaches Bayes-optimal x-prediction MSE as context length N increases, confirming theoretical predictions on 2D Gaussian mixtures.

Noise Robustness

Near-optimal performance across varying σ² with fixed parameters (N=5000, β=20, L₀=200). No schedule tuning needed.

Depth-Noise Scaling

Effective denoising at depth L ~ T*β/η. The one-shot vs. iterative gap vanishes as N → ∞, highlighting depth's role in finite-sample regimes.

Strengths

  • Elegant unification of empirical Bayes, particle dynamics, and attention under one framework.
  • Two-stage decomposition gives precise statistical meaning to depth and skip connections.
  • Principled depth-noise relationship (T* = σ²/2) without noise schedules.
  • Full mathematical proofs: propagation of chaos, weak-strong uniqueness, posterior recovery via Bihari's lemma and Osgood conditions.
  • Clear exposition of the Gaussian kernel's canonical status.
  • Analysis on Rd (not the sphere) is genuinely harder than prior work.

Limitations

  • Experiments restricted to 2D Gaussian mixtures. No real-world data, images, or language tasks.
  • Simplified attention-only architecture with no learned weights, MLPs, or layer norm.
  • Sequential asymptotic limits (N, β, L taken one at a time) may not reflect joint finite behavior.
  • Reverse-diffusion connection acknowledged as heuristic; full rigorous control not provided.
  • No comparison to alternative denoising methods or learned transformers.
  • 52-page length reflects substantial but potentially limiting mathematical machinery.

Open Questions

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can the two-stage EB framework extend to attention with learned weight matrices (WQ, WK, WV not tied to √βI)?
  2. How does the framework behave in high dimensions (d >> 2) where kernel density estimation suffers from the curse of dimensionality?
  3. What is the joint behavior of the sequential limits (N, β, L) taken simultaneously?
  4. Can the "no noise schedule needed" insight simplify or improve practical diffusion-based architectures?
  5. Does the posterior-sufficiency-via-clustering observation imply exact prior recovery is unnecessary for downstream tasks — and what is the minimal depth required?
