> **一句话总结：** 本文通过在Transformer编码器中显式解耦语义、绝对位置和相对位置三个信息流，发现绝对位置空间会自发坍缩为捕获文档结构的低频二维流形，注意力头分化为结构和语义两类，且解耦方法在65个语言现象中的49个上提升了探测性能。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes a disentangled Transformer encoder (DSTG-NeoBERT) that processes semantic, absolute positional (AP), and relative positional (RP) information as three separate streams, confining the MLM objective to the semantic stream only. The isolated AP subspace spontaneously collapses into a 2D low-frequency manifold encoding document structure, attention heads specialize into AP-driven (structural) and semantic-driven groups, and the architecture preserves positional information that entangled models lose under MLM pressure. It improves on 49/65 linguistic phenomena in Flash-Holmes probing while matching baselines on GLUE, MTEB, and SQuAD.

## 核心贡献

- **提出解耦架构 DSTG-NeoBERT：** 基于 NeoBERT 修改，将语义、绝对位置和相对位置三个信息流完全分离，MLM 目标仅作用于语义子空间，避免位置信息被语义预测任务覆盖
- **发现 AP 空间的自发低维坍缩：** 48维的绝对位置嵌入矩阵在训练后自发坍缩为二维低频流形，前两个主成分捕获94.1%的方差，且98%以上的谱能量集中在低频分量
- **揭示注意力头的功能分化：** 36个注意力头中，27个专用于语义匹配，其余由AP信息驱动；RP偏置仅作为语义头的局部辅助机制，不单独形成RP专化头
- **证明标准PE方法的宏观结构丢失：** RoPE和纯RP方法几乎无法编码文档结构；纠缠式AP在中间层能编码结构但在最后一层因MLM压力而丢失（R^2从0.94降至0.24）
- **在Flash-Holmes探测基准上取得全面提升：** 在65个语言现象的49个上超越所有基线，涵盖句法(18/21)、语义(17/24)、语篇(5/6)、推理(6/10)和形态(3/4)

## Key Contributions

- **Disentangled architecture (DSTG-NeoBERT):** Modifies NeoBERT to process semantic, AP, and RP as three architecturally separate streams with independent RMSNorms and value projections, while sharing attention scores computed from all three streams
- **Spontaneous 2D low-frequency AP manifold:** Despite 48-dimensional AP embeddings, PCA reveals 94.1% variance in 2 components; Type-II DCT confirms 98%+ spectral power in low-frequency bins, demonstrating that Transformers need minimal positional bandwidth when RP handles local context
- **Functional head taxonomy via information ablation:** Using KL-divergence ablation analysis on 500 WikiText documents, shows 27/36 heads are semantic-driven, 9 are AP-driven, and none are purely RP-driven; RP acts exclusively as a localized support for semantic heads
- **Positional information loss diagnosis in entangled models:** Probing experiments reveal that AP-NeoBERT loses positional information in the final layer (token-level AP R^2 drops from 0.99 to 0.34) because the MLM head repurposes positional dimensions for prediction
- **Linguistic probing improvements:** Outperforms all baselines on 49/65 Flash-Holmes phenomena across all 5 fields (Morphology, Syntax, Discourse, Semantics, Reasoning), with the gains attributed to preserved structural information

## Methodology

The architecture is built on NeoBERT (Breton et al., 2024) with the following key modifications:

**Three-stream design:** Each token is represented by two embeddings: a d_AP-dimensional AP embedding and a d_sem-dimensional semantic embedding (d_model = d_AP + d_sem). Separate RMSNorms prevent interdependence between streams.

**Attention mechanism:** Queries and keys are computed independently per stream per head, projected to the same d_head dimension to match dot-product variances. Attention logits combine the RP bias (T5-style bucketed bias with TUPE-style special token handling), semantic attention weight, and AP attention weight. The same softmax-normalized attention scores are applied to both AP and semantic value streams.

**SwiGLU mixing:** The feed-forward SwiGLU operates on the concatenated [AP; semantic] representation, then splits the output back into separate streams, allowing controlled cross-stream interaction only in the FFN.

**MLM confinement:** The MLM prediction head operates only on the semantic subspace. The AP component of the final hidden state receives no gradient from MLM, preserving structural information throughout the network.

**Baselines:** Four models are compared -- RoPE-NeoBERT (rotary PE), RP-NeoBERT (T5 bucketed RP bias only), AP-NeoBERT (learned AP embeddings, entangled), and DSTG-NeoBERT (disentangled AP + RP). All share identical training: 500K MLM steps on FineWeb-Edu with AdamW, learning rate 1e-4.

**Analysis methods:**
- PCA and Type-II DCT on learned AP embedding matrices
- KL-divergence information ablation per attention head (removing AP/RP/semantic to quantify influence)
- Three-level positional probing (token-level, segment-level, intra-segment) via Ridge regression with R^2 evaluation
- Flash-Holmes linguistic probing benchmark (65 phenomena, 5 fields)

## Results & Highlights

**Benchmark performance:** DSTG-NeoBERT matches all three entangled baselines on GLUE (avg ~82.2), MTEB (avg ~65.1), and SQuAD (F1 ~87.5, EM ~80.1). No degradation from disentanglement.

**AP manifold structure:** The disentangled AP embeddings collapse to 2D (94.1% variance in 2 PCs vs. 23.4% for entangled AP). Low-frequency DCT bins hold 98.4% and 99.0% of spectral power.

**Positional probing:** At the final useful layer, DSTG-NeoBERT retains token-level AP (R^2=0.92), segment-level AP (R^2=0.88), while AP-NeoBERT drops to 0.24 and 0.23 respectively at layer 5. RoPE and RP models never exceed 0.32 at any layer.

**Head specialization:** Only 9/36 heads are AP-influenced; 27 are semantic-driven. No purely RP heads exist. RP acts as localized support for semantic heads (e.g., disallowing self-attention in retrieval heads).

**Structural visualization:** PCA of AP hidden states across layers shows progressive sentence-level clustering -- tokens from the same sentence converge, and sentence clusters become increasingly distinct in deeper layers.

**Flash-Holmes:** DSTG-NeoBERT wins on 49/65 phenomena: Syntax 18/21, Semantics 17/24, Discourse 5/6, Reasoning 6/10, Morphology 3/4. All three baselines perform roughly equally, suggesting gains come specifically from preserved structural information.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. The three-stream disentangled architecture for studying positional encoding is novel, as is the finding that AP embeddings spontaneously collapse to a 2D low-frequency manifold. The head taxonomy via KL-divergence ablation is a clean methodological contribution. The insight that MLM destroys positional information in the last layer of entangled models is important and well-demonstrated.

**Rigor:** Strong. The paper uses four controlled baselines with identical training setups, multiple complementary analysis methods (spectral, probing, ablation), and reports results averaged over 5 seeds. The probing methodology uses proper train/test splits with Ridge regression.

**Reproducibility:** Good. Code and scripts are shared. Architecture details are thorough. Hyperparameters are specified. The only concern is computational cost -- all models are trained from scratch for 500K MLM steps.

**Significance:** Moderate-to-high. The mechanistic insights about positional encoding are valuable for the community. However, the practical impact is limited by (1) the encoder-only focus, (2) the relatively small model scale (based on NeoBERT, not a large-scale model), and (3) the benchmark results being comparable rather than state-of-the-art.

**Red flags:**
- The approach is only demonstrated on encoder models; the authors acknowledge extension to decoders is future work, which limits immediate practical impact.
- The gains on Flash-Holmes are on probing tasks, not downstream task performance. The probing improvements do not translate to notable gains on GLUE/MTEB/SQuAD.
- The d_AP/n_heads = 8 design choice is stated to suffice because "the AP stream carries low-bandwidth structural information," but this is a circular argument -- it suffices because the model learns to use only low-bandwidth information given the constraint.
- The model requires training from scratch; no evaluation of whether disentanglement can be applied to pretrained models.

## Strengths

- Clean, principled architectural design that enables controlled mechanistic analysis
- Compelling finding that AP embeddings naturally collapse to 2D low-frequency representations
- Well-designed probing hierarchy (token-level, segment-level, intra-segment) that reveals the loss of positional information in entangled models
- The MLM-confined-to-semantic-stream design elegantly solves the positional information destruction problem
- Comprehensive analysis with multiple complementary methods
- Honest about limitations and future directions

## Limitations

- Encoder-only evaluation; decoder architectures (the dominant paradigm) are not tested
- Small model scale; unclear if findings transfer to larger models
- Probing improvements do not translate to meaningful downstream task gains on GLUE/MTEB/SQuAD
- Requires training from scratch; no path to retrofit existing pretrained models
- The T5-style bucketed RP bias may not generalize to other RP methods
- Only English language evaluation
- The paper does not evaluate on long-context tasks where positional encoding matters most

## Relevance

这篇论文对理解Transformer中位置编码的内在机制具有重要参考价值，其解耦设计思路可能为改进大语言模型的位置表示（特别是长上下文处理）提供新的架构启示，对关注模型可解释性和位置编码优化的研究者尤为相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the spontaneous 2D collapse of AP embeddings hold at much larger model scales (e.g., billions of parameters) and with decoder architectures?
- Can the disentangled AP manifold be directly manipulated for length extrapolation, as the authors hint in their conclusion?
- Would caching position-independent semantic representations in the disentangled setting provide practical efficiency gains for RAG systems?
- How does the disentangled architecture behave on long-context tasks (e.g., >4K tokens) where positional encoding is most critical?
- Is it possible to retrofit the disentangled AP stream onto a pretrained model via fine-tuning rather than training from scratch?
- Do the functional head taxonomy findings (AP-driven vs. semantic-driven) transfer to decoder-only models with causal attention?
