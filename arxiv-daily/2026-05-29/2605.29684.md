> **一句话总结：** 本文提出等价Wishart假设（Equivalent Wishart Ansatz, EWA），将贝叶斯深度MLP和CNN中层级经验核的随机涨落近似为Wishart分布，通过配分函数的大偏差分析和Varadhan引理得到重正化NNGP核的有效作用量，仅需L个标量序参量即可在比例宽度区间（alpha < 2.5）内定量预测深度达L=10的网络的训练损失和泛化误差，并发现深度网络中存在亚稳态瞬态相。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces the Equivalent Wishart Ansatz (EWA) to approximate the hierarchical empirical kernel fluctuations in Bayesian deep MLPs and CNNs as Wishart-distributed random matrices. Using a large-deviation analysis with Varadhan's lemma, the authors derive an effective action over a renormalized NNGP kernel governed by at most L self-consistent scalar order parameters (one per layer). The theory quantitatively predicts training loss and generalization error for networks with L up to 10 and alpha = P/N up to ~2.5 on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and synthetic data, outperforming both the infinite-width NNGP limit and first-order perturbative corrections. The paper also discovers a metastable transient phase in deep Bayesian sampling dynamics at large depth and load.

## 核心贡献

- 提出等价Wishart假设（EWA），将MLP层级经验核的条件分布近似为中心Wishart分布 W_P(Theta^{L-l+1}(K_E^{(l-1)})/N_l, N_l)，将不可解的先验分布简化为L个独立 chi-squared 变量的乘积
- 利用Gartner-Ellis定理证明序参量 q_l = Q_l/N_l 满足大偏差原理（LDP），速率函数为 I_q(x) = (x - 1 - ln x)/2，并用Varadhan引理得到渐近自由能密度
- 推导出有效作用量 S(q) = sum_l [q_l - ln q_l] + U(q)，其中U(q)为重正化核 K_Q^{(R)} = Q * Theta^L(C)（Q = prod_l q_l）下的高斯过程回归边际对数似然，物理上对应超参数先验与似然的竞争
- 证明非零均值激活函数（如ReLU）的非中心效应在比例极限下渐近被抑制，因此中心EWA对一般激活函数均适用
- 扩展至CNN：提出Stacked EWA，引入 omega 映射处理patch结构，得到层级局部核重正化 K_Q^{(R)} = (1/N_PL) sum_{i,j} Q_ij [(Theta circ omega)^L(C)]_ji，序参量为 N_PL x N_PL 正定矩阵
- 发现深度贝叶斯采样中存在新的"瞬态相"（transient phase），介于热化与平衡之间，可维持数百万步更新，瞬态平均与EWA预测一致

## Key Contributions

- Propose the Equivalent Wishart Ansatz (EWA), approximating the conditional distribution of hierarchical empirical kernels in MLPs as central Wishart distributions W_P(Theta^{L-l+1}(K_E^{(l-1)})/N_l, N_l), reducing the intractable prior to a product of L independent chi-squared variables
- Prove a Large Deviation Principle (LDP) for the order parameters q_l via the Gartner-Ellis theorem with rate function I_q(x) = (x - 1 - ln x)/2, and derive the asymptotic free energy density via Varadhan's lemma
- Derive the effective action S(q) = sum_l [q_l - ln q_l] + U(q) where U(q) is the marginal log-likelihood of a GP regression with renormalized kernel K_Q^{(R)} = Q * Theta^L(C), physically encoding the competition between entropic hyperprior and data-dependent likelihood
- Prove that non-zero activation mean effects (e.g., ReLU) are asymptotically suppressed in the proportional limit, making the central EWA applicable to generic activation functions
- Extend to CNNs via the Stacked EWA with omega-mapping for patch structure, yielding hierarchical local kernel renormalization with N_PL x N_PL positive-definite matrix order parameters
- Discover a novel metastable transient phase in deep Bayesian sampling dynamics at large depth/load, persisting for millions of updates, with transient averages agreeing with EWA predictions

## Methodology

The paper develops an effective low-dimensional theory for Bayesian deep neural networks in the proportional-width regime (alpha = P/N = O(1)). The approach proceeds in several stages:

1. **Setup**: Consider Bayesian MLPs with L hidden layers of width N_l, Gaussian priors on weights, and squared-error likelihood. The partition function is expressed via the characteristic function of the network output prior.

2. **Equivalent Wishart Ansatz**: The key approximation replaces the intractable conditional distribution of each layer's empirical kernel (given the previous layer) with a central Wishart distribution. This decouples the layers into independent chi-squared random variables Q_l ~ chi^2(N_l), with intensive variables q_l = Q_l/N_l concentrating around 1.

3. **Large Deviation Analysis**: Using the Gartner-Ellis theorem, the authors prove that the q_l variables satisfy an LDP with rate function I_q(x) = (x - 1 - ln x)/2. The product Q = prod_l q_l satisfies a contracted LDP with rate I_Q(y) = L * I_q(y^{1/L}).

4. **Effective Action**: Via Varadhan's lemma, the asymptotic free energy density is given by the infimum of S(q) = sum_l I_q(q_l) + U(q), where U(q) is the GP log-likelihood with renormalized kernel K_Q^{(R)} = Q * Theta^L(C). The resulting theory is equivalent to hierarchical GP regression with L hyperparameters {q_l}.

5. **Predictor Statistics**: At the saddle point, training and generalization errors are computed via the standard GP bias-variance decomposition using the renormalized kernel.

6. **CNN Extension**: The Stacked EWA introduces an omega-mapping that averages empirical kernels over patches, yielding a kernel renormalization with matrix-valued order parameters.

7. **Numerical Validation**: Bayesian posterior sampling via Langevin Monte Carlo (LMC), HMC NUTS, MALA, and pCN algorithms on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and synthetic Gaussian data, with networks at N = 100-4000, L = 1-10, alpha = 0.25-2.5.

## Results & Highlights

- **Quantitative agreement for moderate alpha**: The EWA theory quantitatively tracks experimental learning curves for alpha < 2.5 across Erf and ReLU activations, significantly outperforming the infinite-width NNGP theory which fails qualitatively even at small depths.

- **Non-perturbative regime**: At alpha >= 1, the EWA and NNGP learning curves cross as functions of depth, demonstrating that the theory captures strong, non-perturbative finite-width effects that cannot be described by first-order perturbative corrections to the infinite-width limit.

- **Systematic deviations**: Two types of deviations emerge: (1) at alpha = 2.5 and large depth L > 3-5, the EWA overestimates generalization performance by 5-8%, consistent with an O(L/sqrt(N)) error accumulation from layer-wise reduction; (2) at large depth and load, a metastable transient phase appears in sampling dynamics where the system appears equilibrated for extended periods before transitioning to the true equilibrium.

- **Transient phase discovery**: For L > 5 and alpha = 2.5, Bayesian sampling exhibits an intermediate "transient phase" between thermalization and equilibrium. Transient averages smoothly continue the EWA-predicted learning curves and agree with trends at smaller depths, while true equilibrium values show additional finite-width corrections. This was confirmed across four independent MCMC samplers (LMC, NUTS HMC, MALA, pCN).

- **CNN validation**: The Stacked EWA accurately predicts training and generalization losses for single-hidden-layer CNNs on MNIST as a function of channel count A_1, capturing finite-width behavior that approaches the large-width limit for A_1 >> P.

- **Mean-field (muP) parametrization**: The EWA successfully predicts generalization performance even in the rich learning regime (muP parametrization), where the improvement over standard parametrization is largely explained by variance reduction rather than bias changes. The renormalized kernel, despite being a global rescaling of the NNGP kernel, effectively captures the output posterior adaptation even when empirical kernels develop strong block structure.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The EWA is a creative and non-obvious approximation that enables the first tractable analysis of deep (L > 1) nonlinear Bayesian networks in the proportional regime. The observation that non-central effects vanish asymptotically is a useful technical contribution. The transient phase discovery is intriguing and novel.

- **Rigor:** Moderate-to-good. The LDP framework and Gartner-Ellis/Varadhan machinery are rigorously applied. However, the EWA itself is an uncontrolled ansatz -- there is no proof that the Wishart approximation becomes exact in any limit, and error bounds are empirical. The claim that the EWA captures "dominant" fluctuations is supported numerically but not proven.

- **Reproducibility:** Good. The paper provides extensive experimental details (MCMC hyperparameters, learning rates, dataset preprocessing), multiple independent samplers for cross-validation, and references a GitHub repository. The effective action saddle point computation is described in the appendix.

- **Significance:** Significant for theoretical deep learning. The framework provides the first low-dimensional description of finite-width effects in deep nonlinear networks, going beyond both the infinite-width kernel limit and shallow single-hidden-layer results. The reduction to L scalar order parameters (or N_PL x N_PL matrices for CNNs) is conceptually elegant.

- **Red flags:** (1) The ansatz is uncontrolled and breaks down at alpha > 2.5 with increasing depth, limiting its predictive power in the most interesting regime (overparameterized but finite-width). (2) The restriction to Bayesian inference is fundamental -- the connection to SGD-trained networks is mentioned but not established. (3) The transient phase raises questions about whether previous Bayesian deep learning experiments may have been measuring metastable rather than true equilibrium statistics.

## Strengths

- First tractable effective theory for deep (L > 1) nonlinear Bayesian networks in the proportional regime
- Elegant mathematical framework: LDP + Varadhan's lemma yields a physically interpretable effective action as hierarchical GP regression
- Extensive experimental validation across architectures (MLP, CNN), activations (Erf, ReLU), datasets (MNIST, CIFAR-10, synthetic), and multiple independent MCMC samplers
- Handles both standard and mean-field (muP) parametrizations
- Discovery of the transient phase phenomenon is scientifically interesting and honestly reported
- Clean separation of entropic (prior) and energetic (likelihood) contributions in the effective action

## Limitations

- The EWA is an uncontrolled approximation: no rigorous error bounds, and breakdown at alpha > 2.5 with increasing depth suggests O(L/sqrt(N)) error accumulation
- Restricted to Bayesian inference with squared-error loss -- extension to classification losses, SGD dynamics, or other optimizers is not addressed
- The proportional regime alpha = P/N = O(1) excludes both the heavily overparameterized (P << N) and underparameterized (P >> N) regimes common in practice
- The CNN extension (Stacked EWA) is demonstrated only for shallow (single hidden layer) CNNs, not deep convolutional architectures
- The transient phase phenomenon complicates experimental validation and raises concerns about the reliability of MCMC sampling in deep Bayesian networks
- No direct connection to feature learning beyond the global kernel rescaling -- the renormalized kernel retains the prior structure even when empirical kernels develop strong data-dependent structure
- The framework assumes fixed depth L and does not address residual connections, normalization layers, or attention mechanisms

## Relevance

本文从统计物理视角出发，将大偏差理论和核重正化方法系统性地推广到深度非线性贝叶斯网络，对于研究有限宽度效应、NNGP/NTK理论边界、以及深度学习理论中非微扰方法的研究者提供了重要参考。特别是所发现的瞬态相现象和muP参数化下的分析对理解贝叶斯深度学习的采样动力学具有启发意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the EWA be refined to incorporate higher-order corrections that would extend its validity to alpha > 2.5 and very deep networks?
- What is the precise mechanism underlying the transient phase -- is it related to a phase transition in the Bayesian posterior landscape?
- How does the O(L/sqrt(N)) error accumulation relate to the layer-wise integration errors identified in student-teacher settings?
- Can the framework be extended to attention/transformer architectures that admit a well-defined large-width limit?
- Is there a principled way to connect the renormalized kernel from Bayesian inference to the feature learning observed in SGD training?
- Do the transient phase phenomena have implications for the reliability of posterior sampling in practical Bayesian deep learning applications?
