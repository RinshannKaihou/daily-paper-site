> **一句话总结：** AsymVLM 针对视觉语言模型中视觉和文本 token 的根本性不对称（视觉 token 空间冗余且主导 prefill，文本 token 因果依赖且在解码阶段累积），提出非对称剪枝框架：视觉 token 使用学习的输出感知重要性评分器 + per-sample 自适应预算在 prefill 前激进剪枝，文本 token 在超出固定预算时使用基于注意力的阈值驱逐，在文档/图表理解任务上超越现有方法 2-3%，同时节省最高 54% FLOPs 并将峰值内存降至 25%。

> **TL;DR:** AsymVLM exploits the fundamental asymmetry between vision tokens (spatially redundant, dominate prefill) and text tokens (causally dependent, accumulate during decoding) in VLMs. It combines a learned output-aware importance scorer with per-sample adaptive budgeting for vision token pruning before the transformer, plus threshold-based text token eviction during decoding. Evaluated on Phi-3.5-Vision and Gemma 3, it achieves up to 54% FLOPs savings (highest among SOTA), outperforms FastV/SparseVLM by 2-3% on DocVQA/ChartQA, reduces peak memory to 25%, and substantially outperforms H2O/StreamingLLM on free-form VLM generation tasks. The scorer trains in 1 hour on 1,500 DocVQA samples and generalizes across 5 benchmarks without task-specific tuning.

## 核心贡献

- 系统性分析并形式化了 VLM 中视觉与文本 token 的根本性不对称：视觉 token 无因果依赖、空间冗余、主导 prefill 阶段计算瓶颈；文本 token 因果依赖、自回归生成、在多轮对话中累积成为解码阶段的内存瓶颈。
- 提出学习的跨模态重要性评分器，通过最小化剪枝前后模型输出的隐藏状态差异来学习 per-dimension 权重 w，超越基于语义相似性的剪枝方法（在 DocVQA 50% 保留率下比 cosine similarity 高 16 个百分点）。
- 提出 per-sample 自适应预算机制，基于 importance gap（75th 与 25th 百分位分数差）动态调整每个样本的视觉 token 保留率，解决了固定剪枝比对不同样本的最优性问题。
- 提出面向 VLM 短文本生成特性的阈值驱逐策略：保护所有 prefill token，仅驱逐生成 token，在 LLaVA-Bench 和 MMDU 上显著优于 H2O 和 StreamingLLM。
- 在 Phi-3.5-Vision 和 Gemma 3 两个对比架构上验证，涵盖视觉主导（2,500 视觉 token）和文本主导（256 视觉 token）两种场景，实现最高 54% FLOPs 节省和峰值内存降至 25%。

## Key Contributions

- Systematic analysis formalizing the fundamental asymmetry between vision and text tokens in VLMs: vision tokens have no causal dependency and are spatially redundant (dominating prefill), while text tokens are causally dependent and accumulate during decoding (becoming the bottleneck in multi-turn dialogue).
- A learned cross-modal importance scorer that minimizes the MSE between pruned and unpruned VLM output hidden states, learning a per-dimension weight vector w that re-scales embedding dimensions for output-aware token ranking. On DocVQA at 50% retention, this outperforms raw cosine similarity by 16 points (83.22% vs. 68.04%).
- A per-sample adaptive budget mechanism using the "importance gap" (difference between 75th and 25th percentile importance scores) to dynamically adjust vision token retention per input, with both threshold-sweep and learned linear mapping variants.
- A threshold-based text token eviction strategy designed for VLMs' short-context nature: protects all prefill tokens (vision + prompt), evicts only generated tokens based on final-layer attention, substantially outperforming H2O and StreamingLLM on LLaVA-Bench and MMDU.
- Evaluation on two contrasting architectures (Phi-3.5-Vision 4B with 2,500 vision tokens vs. Gemma 3 12B with 256 vision tokens) across 7 benchmarks, achieving up to 54% FLOPs savings and reducing peak memory to 25%.

## Methodology

AsymVLM has three components, each targeting a specific aspect of the modality asymmetry:

**1. Learned Vision Token Scorer (Section 3.2):**
Given vision tokens V and text tokens T from the embedding layer, the scorer computes importance scores as: S = (V_hat * w) @ T_hat^T, s_i = max_j S_{i,j}, where w is a learnable weight vector in R^D that re-scales normalized vision embedding dimensions before computing dot-product similarity with text embeddings. When w=1, this reduces to standard cosine similarity. The scorer is trained by minimizing MSE between text-position hidden states from a baseline (unpruned) forward pass and a masked forward pass with a soft score-based attention penalty. Training uses only 1,500 DocVQA samples, 3 epochs, AdamW with lr=1e-3, and completes in 1 hour on a single A100. Only w is updated; all model parameters remain frozen.

**2. Per-Sample Adaptive Budget (Section 3.3):**
For each input, the importance gap g is computed as the difference between the k-th highest and k-th lowest importance scores (k=floor(N/4)). This gap follows an approximately normal distribution across samples (mean 0.218, std 0.047 on 500 DocVQA samples). Two mapping approaches are provided: (A) threshold sweep partitioning samples into three tiers (conservative/moderate/aggressive) with fixed keep ratios, and (B) learned linear mapping r(g) = clamp(r_target + a*(g - g_bar), r_min, r_max). Both yield similar improvements; Method A is used as default.

**3. Threshold-Based Text Token Eviction (Section 3.4):**
Text tokens are left untouched until they exceed a fixed budget threshold (e.g., 90 tokens for single-turn, 512 for multi-turn). When triggered, in single-turn generation, generated tokens are scored by final-layer attention received at the current decoding step averaged across heads; in multi-turn dialogue, entire previous answers are scored by cross-modal similarity to current images and evicted at the turn level. All prefill tokens (vision + questions) are unconditionally protected. This design exploits VLMs' short generated sequences and visual-grounded reasoning, avoiding the overhead of cumulative multi-layer tracking required by long-context LLM methods.

## Results & Highlights

**Vision Token Pruning (Phi-3.5-Vision, 5 benchmarks):**
- On DocVQA: outperforms FastV by 2.2% at 75% retention (85.75 vs. 83.53) and 3.3% at 65% (85.07 vs. 81.74). At 75% retention, only 0.38% below the full-token baseline.
- On ChartQA: maintains or exceeds the full-token baseline even under compression (68.0 and 67.8 at 75%/65% vs. 67.4 baseline).
- FLOPs savings: 28% at 75%, 39% at 65%, 54% at 50% retention — highest among all compared methods. SparseVLM achieves only 47% at 50% retention.
- Peak memory reduced to as low as 25% at 50% retention. FastV achieves no memory savings (attention masking only); SparseVLM maintains full peak memory in early layers.
- On holistic tasks (MME, TextVQA, OCRVQA), all methods perform similarly with AsymVLM having a slight edge.

**Text Token Eviction (Gemma 3 12B, free-form generation):**
- LLaVA-Bench: stable scores across retention levels (75.69/75.33/74.69) while H2O degrades (75.3 to 69.83) and StreamingLLM drops severely (58.33 to 53.0).
- MMDU (multi-turn): achieves 7.03 at 90% retention vs. 5.61 for both H2O and StreamingLLM. At 50% eviction, matches baseline exactly (6.80), while H2O/StreamingLLM drop to 5.02/4.95.

**Ablation Studies:**
- Learned scorer vs. cosine similarity: 16-point improvement on DocVQA at 50% retention (83.22% vs. 68.04%), demonstrating the value of output-aware scoring.
- Adaptive vs. uniform budget: threshold sweep achieves 1.8-3.8% gains over uniform pruning at the same average budget. At 65% average budget, achieves 84.35% ANLS (only 1.56% below full-token baseline of 85.91%).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The asymmetric framing is the paper's main conceptual contribution. The individual techniques (learned scorer, adaptive budget, threshold eviction) are relatively straightforward engineering solutions. The per-dimension learned weighting w is a simple but effective idea. The importance gap as a per-sample difficulty signal is novel and well-motivated. Overall, solid incremental contribution rather than a paradigm shift.
- **Rigor:** Strong experimental setup with two contrasting architectures (Phi-3.5-Vision with many vision tokens vs. Gemma 3 with few), 7 benchmarks covering both short-answer and free-form generation, and detailed ablation studies. The scorer is trained on DocVQA but tested on 4 additional benchmarks, demonstrating cross-task generalization. However, the evaluation covers only 2 models; generalization to LLaVA, Qwen-VL, InternVL is acknowledged as future work. No statistical significance tests or variance reporting.
- **Reproducibility:** Training details are provided (1,500 samples, 3 epochs, AdamW, lr=1e-3, lambda=0.001, C=5.0, tau=1.0, clamp w to [0,3]). The scorer trains in 1 hour on one A100. No code repository mentioned in the paper. The adaptive budget thresholds are selected via grid search on a calibration set, which adds a hyperparameter tuning step.
- **Significance:** The practical impact is high — 54% FLOPs savings with accuracy improvements on document/chart tasks is a meaningful result for VLM deployment. The asymmetric design principle is a useful conceptual contribution for the community. The finding that standard LLM cache compression methods (H2O, StreamingLLM) perform poorly on VLMs is important.
- **Red flags:** (1) The scorer is trained and evaluated partly on the same dataset (DocVQA), though the authors note excluding training samples yields near-identical scores. (2) The paper evaluates at only two model scales (4B and 12B); behavior at larger scales is unknown. (3) The text eviction threshold is set per-benchmark (90 for LLaVA-Bench, 512 for MMDU), which requires manual tuning. (4) The paper acknowledges that very long multi-turn dialogues (50+ turns) have not been tested.

## Strengths

- Well-motivated asymmetry analysis with clear empirical observations supporting each design choice.
- Learned output-aware scorer significantly outperforms input-side signals (cosine similarity, attention patterns).
- Per-sample adaptive budgeting is a principled solution to variable pruning difficulty across inputs.
- Achieves genuine FLOPs and memory savings (unlike attention-masking approaches like FastV).
- Strong cross-task generalization: scorer trained on DocVQA transfers to ChartQA, TextVQA, MME, OCRVQA.
- Comprehensive evaluation spanning both vision-dominant and text-dominant scenarios on two architectures.
- The text eviction strategy's design is well-tailored to VLM-specific properties (short generation, visual grounding).

## Limitations

- Evaluated on only 2 VLM architectures; generalization to LLaVA-based, Qwen-VL, InternVL is unverified.
- The adaptive budget thresholds require grid search on a calibration set; not fully automatic.
- Text eviction uses fixed per-benchmark thresholds rather than adaptive ones, as acknowledged by the authors.
- No evaluation on video-language models or multi-image settings beyond MMDU's multi-image dialogue.
- The scorer training requires two forward passes per sample through the frozen VLM, which may be costly for very large models.
- No wall-clock latency measurements reported — only FLOPs savings and accuracy. Actual speedup depends on implementation and hardware.
- Very long multi-turn dialogues (50+ turns) are untested.

## Relevance

这篇论文对关注视觉语言模型高效推理的研究者有直接参考价值。其非对称剪枝的设计原则（不同模态采用不同压缩策略）对多模态模型部署优化具有普遍启发意义，学习的输出感知评分器和 per-sample 自适应预算机制也为 token 剪枝研究提供了新思路。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the learned importance scorer generalize to VLMs with significantly different architectures (e.g., Qwen-VL's dynamic resolution, InternVL's MLP projector)?
- What are the wall-clock latency and throughput improvements on different GPU architectures (A100 vs. consumer GPUs)?
- Can the adaptive budget mechanism be made fully automatic without a calibration set, e.g., via online estimation?
- How does the method perform on video-language models where temporal redundancy adds another dimension of compressibility?
- Does the output-aware scorer capture truly transferable importance patterns, or is it implicitly learning dataset-specific features despite cross-benchmark generalization?
- What happens when both vision and text pruning are active simultaneously (e.g., long multi-turn dialogue with high-resolution images)?
- Can the asymmetric principle be extended to emerging modalities like audio or 3D point clouds in multimodal models?
