Key Finding

MoE routing is topic-driven, not safety-driven -- refusal vs. compliance produces negligible routing shifts while topic changes cause large ones.

RASET exploits this decoupling: by identifying just 5-8 safety-critical experts via contrastive routing sensitivity, it achieves a 50.5% quality-qualified attack success rate across five open-weight MoE models, tuning only 0.12%-0.95% of parameters.

Critical Edge
Only open-weight models tested. Defense mechanisms are unexplored. Requires access to model internals -- API-only deployments are immune.
Methodology

1 Routing Probes

Three complementary experiments prove routing is topic-driven:

  • Teacher-forced contrast: Same harmful prompt, refusal vs. compliant continuations -- routing nearly identical (top-8 overlap ~8/8)
  • Refusal-style probe: Adding refusal prefix to benign prompts causes minimal shift; changing topic under same style causes large shift
  • Matched intent probe: Harmful-benign pairs preserving topic show much smaller routing divergence than cross-topic baselines

2 Expert Identification

Contrastive routing-sensitivity score ranks experts by safety relevance:

Sl,i = A(l,i; Dharm) - λ · A(l,i; Dnorm)
  • Subtract benign activation from harmful activation per expert
  • Rank all experts, select top-K (K=5-8 per model)
  • Freeze router, shared components, and all other experts

3 Constrained Tuning

Dual-strategy loss balancing attack efficacy with capability preservation:

Ltotal = Lviolate + Lpreserve
  • Lviolate: Affirmative prefix supervision + max-margin refusal suppression
  • Lpreserve: Benign NLL constraint + L2 weight regularization
  • 250 harmful + 250 benign samples, 500 training steps

4 Evaluation Framework

Three-tier ASR metrics with increasing strictness:

  • ASRraw: Safeguard violation (HarmBench classifier)
  • ASRvalid: + paragraph validity (rule-based)
  • ASRhq: + quality score (GPT-4o judge, 5-point StrongREJECT rubric)
  • General capability: TruthfulQA and MMLU benchmarks

Strengths / Limitations

Strengths

  • Elegant and counter-intuitive finding that fundamentally reframes MoE safety understanding
  • Three routing probes provide compelling and consistent evidence from independent angles
  • Extremely parameter-efficient (0.12%-0.95%) yet highly effective (50.5% ASRhq)
  • Routing preservation verified with calibration against intrinsic model variance
  • Evaluation across 5 diverse MoE architectures strengthens generalizability
  • Multi-tier ASR metrics separate raw compliance from high-quality harmful output

Limitations

  • Only open-weight models tested; proprietary MoE models may differ
  • Defensive countermeasures are left entirely to future work
  • Some utility degradation remains (5.4-7.5 pt drops on TruthfulQA/MMLU)
  • Sensitivity of the K (5-8 experts) choice is not deeply analyzed
  • Requires access to model internals -- not applicable to API-only settings
  • No investigation into whether safety-critical experts can be hardened
Open Questions
  • Can the contrastive routing-sensitivity criterion be inverted for defense -- identifying and strengthening safety-critical experts during alignment?
  • Are the same experts safety-critical across different harm categories (violence, hate speech, self-harm), or does each category have distinct safety experts?
  • How stable are the identified safety-critical experts across different training runs or checkpoint variations?
  • Could expert-aware RLHF or DPO distribute safety enforcement across more experts, avoiding the concentrated vulnerability?
  • How does the RASET vulnerability compare in severity to dense-model fine-tuning attacks -- is MoE-specific vulnerability truly worse?
  • What is the minimum number of compromised experts needed for significant attack success, and does this reveal a fundamental safety-capacity tradeoff?
