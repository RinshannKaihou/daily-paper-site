> **一句话总结：** 本文提出部分多神经元松弛（PMNR）框架，通过NSSE启发式选择少量关键神经元生成多神经元线性边界，在Marabou验证器上比基线DeepPoly多解决49%的验证查询，同时运行时间减少17%。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces Partial Multi-Neuron Relaxation (PMNR), a bound-tightening framework for DNN verification that generates multi-neuron linear bounds for only a heuristically selected subset of neurons (using a novel NSSE heuristic and BHSO hyper-plane optimization). Integrated into the Marabou verifier, PMNR solves 49% more queries than base Marabou (301 vs. 202 out of 344) with 17% lower average runtime (619 vs. 751 seconds), and 220% more queries than full multi-neuron relaxation (PmnrAll, which solved only 94). To appear at SAIV 2026.

## 核心贡献

- 提出PMNR（Partial Multi-Neuron Relaxation）框架，在单神经元松弛和全多神经元松弛之间找到实用折中，仅对启发式选择的少量神经元子集生成多神经元边界。
- 设计NSSE（Neuron Selection with Symbolic Expressions）启发式方法，通过符号表达式评分选择最有可能提升验证效果的神经元。
- 提出BHSO（Bounding Hyper-planes via Splitting and Optimization）方法，结合分支和预像过近似技术优化多神经元超平面。
- 推广了INVPROP对偶优化定理，使其支持任意激活函数和输入域（不限于ReLU）。
- 在Marabou验证器中实现，344个局部鲁棒性查询上解决301个，比DeepPoly基线（202个）提升49%，比全多神经元松弛（94个）提升220%。

## Key Contributions

- Proposes the PMNR paradigm: a general, sound framework that extends single-neuron relaxation by selectively generating multi-neuron bounds for a heuristically chosen subset of neurons (d=2 or 3 per iteration), avoiding the exponential cost of full multi-neuron relaxation.
- Designs the NSSE neuron selection heuristic, reworked from the BBPS branching heuristic, which scores neurons by aggregating symbolic bounds over all branches and selects the d highest-scoring neurons from the layer with the highest total score.
- Introduces the BHSO paradigm for generating bounding hyper-planes, which initializes hyper-planes from single-neuron relaxations, optimizes them via a generalized dual optimization (extending INVPROP to arbitrary activations), and further refines them using branching over phase combinations.
- Proves soundness of the PMNR framework (Theorem 3.1): the returned concrete bounds are lemmas derived from the verification query and are no less precise than single-neuron tightening alone.
- Provides a generalized version of the INVPROP dual optimization theorem (Theorem 0.B.1) that supports general activation functions and input domains, with a complete proof via Lagrangian duality.
- Open-source implementation integrated into the Marabou verifier, evaluated on 344 local robustness queries across PL and NPL activations on MNIST.

## Methodology

**Problem Setting:** DNN verification as satisfiability of a query Q=(N, D_in, D_out), where the goal is to determine if any input in D_in causes the network output to fall in the unsafe region D_out. The approach targets bound tightening within the branch-and-bound (BaB) paradigm.

**PMNR Framework (Algorithm 1):** The main loop iteratively: (1) runs single-neuron tightening (DeepPoly) to get concrete bounds and symbolic relaxations; (2) selects optimizable parameters via PickAlphas; (3) selects a subset of neurons via SelectNeurons (NSSE); (4) generates multi-neuron bounds via GeneratePMNR (BHSO); (5) performs final tightening via PostTighten using a modified LP-based forward-backward abstract interpretation. The loop stops after n iterations (default n=10) or when bounds no longer improve.

**NSSE Heuristic (Section 4.1):** For each unfixed-phase neuron x_j^(i), NSSE derives linear upper and lower bounds on the output neuron as a function of a source neuron, assuming x_j^(i) is in each of its phases separately. It aggregates these symbolic bounds over all branches, producing two symbolic expressions. The post-concretization average range of these expressions is the neuron's NSSE score. The d highest-scoring neurons from the layer with the highest total score are selected.

**BHSO Paradigm (Algorithm 2):** For d selected neurons, BHSO generates 3^d - 2d - 1 initial hyper-planes (with epsilon in {-1,0,1}^d having more than one non-zero entry). Each hyper-plane is optimized using a generalized dual optimization method (extending INVPROP), then further refined by running the optimization for each combination of the neurons' phase branches and taking the weakest (soundest) bound. This branching step is key to tightening the hyper-planes.

**PostTighten:** A modified LP-based forward-backward abstract interpretation that incorporates both single-neuron and multi-neuron bounds, using Gurobi as the external LP solver.

## Results & Highlights

**Piecewise-Linear (PL) Networks (272 queries):**
- PMNR solved 245/272 (90%), vs. DeepPoly 174/272 (64%) and PmnrAll 40/272 (15%).
- PMNR average time: 752s vs. DeepPoly 866s (13% faster) and PmnrAll 5003s.
- On LeakyRelu 8x100: PMNR solved 98/100, DeepPoly solved 33/100, PmnrAll solved 0.

**Non-Piecewise-Linear (NPL) Networks (72 queries):**
- PMNR solved 56/72 (78%), vs. DeepPoly 28/72 (39%) and PmnrAll 54/72 (75%).
- PMNR average time: 38s vs. DeepPoly 28s (slight overhead) and PmnrAll 4340s.

**Overall (344 queries):**
- PMNR: 301 solved, 619s average; DeepPoly: 202 solved, 751s; PmnrAll: 94 solved, 4622s.
- PMNR is 49% better than DeepPoly in solved queries and 17% faster; 220% better than PmnrAll.

**NSSE vs. Random Selection:** NSSE-based PMNR solved 301 queries vs. 273 with random selection (10% improvement), with the largest gains on ReluSignMax (27 vs. 9) and ReluBilinearSoftmax (29 vs. 10).

**Stop Condition (n=10 vs. n=1):** n=10 solved 3 more queries (301 vs. 298) but with 37% runtime overhead (619s vs. 451s).

**PMNR vs. Forward-Backward Abstract Interpretation (F+BC):** PMNR solved 55% more queries (301 vs. 194) at 3.2x runtime cost.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core idea -- applying multi-neuron relaxation to only a selected subset rather than all neurons -- is conceptually straightforward but executed with substantial technical depth. The NSSE heuristic and BHSO paradigm are genuine contributions. The generalized INVPROP theorem extends prior work meaningfully.
- **Rigor:** The paper includes formal soundness proofs (Theorem 3.1), a complete proof of the generalized dual optimization theorem (Appendix C), and a detailed running example. The experimental setup is well-controlled (only the initial bound tightening differs between configurations).
- **Reproducibility:** Open-source code integrated into Marabou. Experimental details (network architectures in Appendix A, hyper-parameters specified) support reproducibility. Timeout of 14100 seconds on dual-core machines with 4GB memory is a reasonable and specified setting.
- **Significance:** The 49% improvement in solved queries over DeepPoly is substantial. The finding that full multi-neuron relaxation (PmnrAll) actually hurts performance (94 vs. 202 solved) due to runtime overhead validates the core motivation that selective application is key.
- **Red flags:** (1) Benchmarks are limited to MNIST fully-connected networks -- no convolutional, residual, or large-scale architectures tested. (2) PMNR is only applied as the initial bound tightening step, not interleaved with branching; this may limit its full potential. (3) The n=10 stop condition adds only 3 solved queries over n=1 at 37% runtime cost, suggesting diminishing returns. (4) For simpler queries, PMNR's overhead is unnecessary and actually slows down Marabou compared to the baseline.

## Strengths

- Strong empirical improvements: 49% more queries solved than DeepPoly, 220% more than PmnrAll, while being 17% faster than DeepPoly.
- General framework applicable to arbitrary activation functions (ReLU, LeakyReLU, Sigmoid, Bilinear, Softmax, etc.), not limited to piecewise-linear activations.
- Soundness is formally proven, with clear definitions and theorem statements.
- The finding that full multi-neuron relaxation is counterproductive (due to scalability issues) and that targeted partial relaxation is far more effective is an important practical insight.
- Clean experimental design: the only variable is the initial bound tightening method, ensuring fair comparison.

## Limitations

- Only evaluated on small MNIST fully-connected networks (up to 8x100 architecture). Scalability to larger networks (ResNet, transformers, etc.) is untested.
- PMNR is applied only once as initial bound tightening; repeated application during branching was deemed too expensive, which limits the approach's scope.
- The NSSE heuristic adds overhead; for simpler queries where base Marabou already succeeds quickly, PMNR is slower.
- The paper acknowledges it has not explored combining PMNR with other verification approaches (e.g., beta-CROWN, PRIMA, k-ReLU).
- GPU parallelization is not yet explored.
- The comparison against PmnrAll is somewhat unfair to the multi-neuron approach, as PmnrAll uses the same BHSO heuristics rather than optimized methods from PRIMA or other tools.

## Relevance

本文研究神经网络形式化验证中的边界收紧技术，通过部分多神经元松弛平衡紧致性与可扩展性。对关注DNN鲁棒性验证、形式化方法应用以及可信AI安全保证的研究具有直接参考价值，尤其是对需要在验证精度与计算效率间权衡的实际场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does PMNR scale to convolutional and residual architectures commonly used in safety-critical applications (e.g., ACAS-Xu controllers)?
- Can PMNR be integrated with beta-CROWN or PRIMA for synergistic improvements, or do the approaches interfere?
- What is the theoretical relationship between NSSE scores and actual verification improvement -- can we bound the sub-optimality of the heuristic selection?
- Would GPU-based parallelization of BHSO's hyper-plane optimization make PmnrAll competitive, or is the per-hyper-plane cost the true bottleneck?
- Can the generalized INVPROP theorem be applied beyond verification, e.g., to preimage estimation or certified training?
- How does the choice of d (number of selected neurons per iteration) affect the tightness-runtime tradeoff for larger networks?
