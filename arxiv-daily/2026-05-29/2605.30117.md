> **一句话总结：** VLA-Trace 提出了一个三阶段的渐进式诊断框架，通过 CKA 表示分析、注意力击穿干预和行为探测，系统揭示了 pi0.5 和 OpenVLA 两种主流 VLA 模型在模态适应动态、多模态路由策略和语义跟随能力上的关键差异与共同缺陷。

> **TL;DR:** VLA-Trace is a three-stage progressive diagnostic framework that traces VLA models from representation dynamics (CKA), through causal control attribution (attention knockout), to behavioral manifestation (attention IoU, visual masking, input editing). Applied to pi0.5 and OpenVLA, it reveals that the two models have fundamentally different adaptation dynamics (pi0.5 reorganizes text representations, OpenVLA reorganizes visual/joint representations), different action decoding strategies (pi0.5 relies on a narrow visual-to-action bottleneck, OpenVLA depends on both modalities), and a shared weakness: strong visual grounding but poor fine-grained semantic following -- edited instructions often fail to redirect policy behavior.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 VLA-Trace 三阶段渐进式诊断框架**：将 VLA 模型分析统一为"表示演化 -> 因果归因 -> 行为表现"的完整证据链，填补了现有碎片化分析的空白。
- **揭示两种主流 VLA 模型的模态适应差异**：pi0.5 在微调过程中主要重组文本表示为任务条件控制特征（文本漂移大），而 OpenVLA 主要重组视觉和联合表示空间。
- **发现不同的动作解码路由策略**：pi0.5 依赖集中的视觉到动作通路（视觉瓶颈狭窄），OpenVLA 则在视觉和语言两个模态上更均匀地分布控制信息。
- **揭示视觉接地强但语义跟随弱的共性缺陷**：虽然 VLA 策略能够准确定位操作相关区域并随时间推移转移注意力，但在编辑指令后往往无法改变行为，表明细粒度语义控制能力不足。

## Key Contributions

- **VLA-Trace framework**: A unified three-stage progressive diagnostic pipeline that connects representation geometry (cross-modal and checkpoint-drift CKA), causal pathway analysis (attention knockout at prefill and generation stages), and behavioral probes (attention IoU, visual patch masking, input editing) into a coherent evidence chain for VLA model interpretation.
- **Distinct modality-specific adaptation dynamics**: CKA analysis shows pi0.5 exhibits highly unstable cross-modal fusion with heavy textual reorganization, while OpenVLA shows smoother but weaker intermediate-layer alignment, preserving text-pooled representations while restructuring visual and joint subspaces.
- **Divergent multimodal routing strategies**: Attention knockout reveals pi0.5 is dominated by a concentrated visual-to-action pathway (removing visual access during generation collapses success to near zero), whereas OpenVLA depends on both visual grounding and prompt-region access (removing text access during generation is uniformly destructive).
- **Gap between visual grounding and semantic following**: Despite strong spatial grounding (attention localizes to robot-object interaction regions and shifts temporally with subgoals), edited instructions often fail to redirect policy behavior, revealing weak compositional language control.
- **Practical implications for future VLA design**: The findings motivate representation-preserving adaptation, explicit VLA routing modules (e.g., mid-to-late layer fusion gates), and semantic-edit training objectives (counterfactual instruction-image pairs, contrastive behavior supervision).

## Methodology

VLA-Trace analyzes three progressive checkpoints: C0 (pretrained VLM), C1 (pretrained VLA), and C2 (task-finetuned VLA), using three complementary stages:

**Stage 1 -- Representation Analysis:**
- **Cross-modal CKA**: Measures layer-wise alignment between pooled visual and textual representations across checkpoints and datasets (COCO for general domain, LIBERO for robot domain).
- **Checkpoint-drift CKA**: Quantifies representational changes across training stages for three views (vision-pooled, text-pooled, joint-pooled), averaged over matched layers. Higher values indicate geometric preservation; lower values indicate reorganization.

**Stage 2 -- Causal Pathway Analysis:**
- **Prefill knockout**: Blocks vision-language token interaction during context encoding, forcing independent modality processing.
- **Generation knockout**: Blocks attention from action tokens to vision or language tokens during autoregressive generation, isolating modality dependency.
- **Layer-wise knockout**: Applies knockout at individual layers to localize where critical dependencies are concentrated.

**Stage 3 -- Behavioral Probes:**
- **Attention IoU**: Measures overlap between action-conditioned attention heatmaps and simulator-derived masks (object, robot+object), split into temporal phases corresponding to instruction subgoals.
- **Visual patch masking**: Masks target objects, gripper, robot body, or background using replacement/black/mosaic strategies to assess causal visual anchors.
- **Input editing**: Edits either visual layout or language instructions to test whether the policy can adapt to semantic modifications.

Evaluated primarily on pi0.5 (PaliGemma-based, bidirectional attention, flow-matching action generation) and OpenVLA (Llama-based, autoregressive, unidirectional attention) across LIBERO-10 and its suites (Goal, Spatial, Object).

## Results & Highlights

- **pi0.5 checkpoint-drift CKA**: Text representations drift substantially more than vision/joint (e.g., text drift 0.05-0.14 vs. vision drift 0.57-0.80 in some configurations), indicating language is heavily reorganized into task-conditioned control features.
- **OpenVLA checkpoint-drift CKA**: Vision and joint representations show larger drift than text (text drift 0.89-0.99, indicating preservation), suggesting visual and cross-modal subspaces are primarily restructured.
- **pi0.5 generation knockout**: Gen:no image reduces success to ~0% across all LIBERO suites; Gen:no text preserves performance on Goal/Object/Spatial. Visual-to-action is the critical pathway.
- **OpenVLA generation knockout**: Gen:no text reduces success to 0% on all suites; Gen:no image is harmful but does not uniformly collapse. Both modalities are necessary.
- **Attention localization**: Both models show higher Robot+Object IoU than Object-only IoU, indicating attention targets interaction regions rather than just objects. pi0.5 distributes attention broadly (higher mass, lower peak-hit), OpenVLA concentrates it sparsely (higher peak-hit, lower mass).
- **Temporal attention shifts**: In two-subgoal tasks (e.g., "turn on the stove and put the moka pot on it"), attention correctly transitions from stove to moka pot across phases.
- **Visual masking**: Target-object masking causes the largest degradation (pi0.5: -76.45 avg, OpenVLA: -60.25 avg). pi0.5 relies heavily on gripper-centered geometry; OpenVLA depends on full-robot visibility.
- **Input editing**: Replacing target objects in instructions does not consistently redirect behavior. Some edits cause large drops (moka pot -> frypan: 75% -> 30%), others have limited effect, revealing non-negligible but limited language influence.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate. The individual techniques (CKA, attention knockout, input editing) are not new. The contribution lies in the unified three-stage framework and the systematic comparison between two architecturally distinct VLA models. The progressive evidence chain design is a meaningful methodological contribution.
- **Rigor**: Good but with caveats. The authors transparently list seven limitations, including: attention knockout is a controlled necessity probe not equivalent to natural OOD failures; CKA values depend on dataset/pooling strategy; no random-feature or permutation CKA baselines; no confidence intervals on rollout success rates; attention IoU depends on simulator mask resolution. The acknowledgement of pi0.5 C2 being a shared LIBERO-finetuned checkpoint across suites is appreciated.
- **Reproducibility**: Likely high. The paper evaluates publicly available models (pi0.5, OpenVLA) on standard benchmarks (LIBERO, COCO). A GitHub link is indicated. The methodology is clearly described with formal definitions of metrics.
- **Significance**: Useful for the VLA community. The finding that two mainstream VLA models have fundamentally different internal routing strategies is practically important for architecture design. The gap between visual grounding and semantic following is a meaningful diagnosis that could guide future training objectives.
- **Red flags**: The complete evidence chain is only applied to two models (pi0.5 and OpenVLA). Extensions to X-VLA, OpenVLA-OFT, Calvin, SimplerEnv etc. are described as "broader implementation protocol" but not completed, limiting the generality of claims. The study is purely diagnostic -- no improvements to VLA models are demonstrated.

## Strengths

- Unified progressive framework that bridges representation-level, causal, and behavioral analysis in a coherent pipeline.
- Clean experimental design with clear separation between prefill and generation stages.
- Honest and detailed limitations section covering seven specific points.
- Interesting finding that pi0.5 and OpenVLA have opposite modality adaptation strategies despite similar end-task performance.
- Temporal attention analysis (phase-split IoU) is a clever way to probe subgoal tracking.

## Limitations

- Only two VLA models are fully analyzed; the broader protocol is defined but not executed, so generalizability is uncertain.
- No actionable improvements are demonstrated -- the paper diagnoses problems but does not show that fixing them helps.
- CKA baselines (random features, permutation) are absent, making it harder to calibrate the meaningfulness of CKA magnitudes.
- Rollout success rates lack confidence intervals, making statistical significance of some behavioral probe results unclear.
- Attention knockout intervention design choices (e.g., which layers, window sizes) could influence conclusions, and only one design is presented per model.
- The behavioral probes are limited to simulation (LIBERO); real-world grounding is not assessed.

## Relevance

本文通过机制可解释性方法分析 VLA 模型的内部表示和因果通路，对于理解视觉-语言-动作模型如何将多模态知识转化为具身控制具有直接参考价值，特别是其中关于不同架构 VLA 模型的模态路由差异和语义跟随能力不足的发现，对未来 VLA 架构设计和训练策略有指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would explicit fusion gates or modality-routing modules at mid-to-late layers actually improve semantic following, as the authors suggest?
- How do the observed adaptation dynamics generalize to newer VLA architectures (e.g., pi0.7, Gemini Robotics, GR00T N1) that use different action tokenization strategies?
- Can semantic-edit training objectives (counterfactual instruction-image pairs, contrastive behavior supervision) close the gap between visual grounding and compositional semantic control?
- What is the relationship between the narrow visual bottleneck in pi0.5 and its reported strong generalization -- is the bottleneck a feature or a bug?
- How do the CKA and attention knockout findings transfer to real-world robotic settings where sim-to-real gaps introduce additional distribution shifts?
