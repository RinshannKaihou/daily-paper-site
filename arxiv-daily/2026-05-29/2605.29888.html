LaRA: Layer-wise Representation Analysis for Detecting Data Contamination in RL Post-Training

RL post-training creates detectable geometric fingerprints in hidden representations that output-level methods completely miss. LaRA captures these via three complementary metrics: perturbation sensitivity, directional collapse, and local rigidity.

Authors Gwak, Kwak, Lee, Son, Ritter, Kim Institutions Yonsei / SNU / Georgia Tech Date 2026-05-29 Models Eurus, LIMR, OLMo (7B)
Key Metric
0.81 AUC

+9.6% AUC improvement and 3.5× TPR@FPR=5% gain over the strongest output-level baseline on LIMR. Combined SC + SLaRA reaches AUC=0.79 on Eurus.

Why This Matters
Contamination detection for RL-trained reasoning models is critical for benchmark integrity.

As RL post-training becomes ubiquitous (DeepSeek-R1, o-series), token-level output signals become unreliable. Representation geometry reveals contamination invisible to existing methods.

Critical Edge
60 samples per model. Math-only. Weak on OLMo.

Small evaluation sets (30 members + 30 non-members) limit statistical power. Results vary across models. No evidence of generalization beyond mathematical reasoning.

Methodology

LaRA frames contamination detection as Membership Inference Attack (MIA). For an RL-trained model M and candidate sample x, determine whether x was in the RL training set by probing hidden representations rather than output probabilities — bypassing the poor calibration of RL-optimized models.

RSM
Representation Shift Magnitude
Generate semantically similar questions, apply importance-based blanking, measure how much the original representation shifts vs. neighbors. High RSM = memorization-driven sensitivity.
DC
Directional Collapse
Measure cosine alignment between the original perturbation direction and the average neighbor direction. High DC = representation changes collapse toward shared dominant directions.
RSI
Representation Stability Index
Generate paraphrastic variants of perturbed questions, measure local representation variability. Standardized against neighbors to capture anomalous local rigidity.

Detection protocol SLaRA: (1) Robust z-score standardization per metric-layer, (2) sign correction, (3) aggregation across all metrics and layers into a single per-sample score.

Complementarity
SLaRA is negatively correlated with SC (ρ=−0.16) and PPL (ρ=−0.29), confirming orthogonal information. Combining both yields further gains.
Ablations
DC provides the strongest standalone signal. RSM+DC+RSI combination consistently best. Removing any component degrades performance. Robust to perturbation types and blank counts.
Strengths
  • First representation-level framework for RL post-training contamination detection
  • Well-motivated: RL optimizes trajectory-level rewards, making token-level output signals unreliable
  • Three complementary metrics provide richer picture than single-metric approaches
  • Layer-wise analysis offers interpretability into where contamination manifests
  • Comprehensive ablations: metrics, perturbation types, perturbation counts, beta sweep
  • Honest discussion of limitations and computational overhead
Limitations
  • Small evaluation sets (30+30 per model) — results may not be robust to different samples
  • Computational overhead: perturbation generation + hidden state extraction across all layers
  • Many member samples go undetected; non-member false positives exist
  • Model-dependent: strong on LIMR, moderate on Eurus, weak on OLMo
  • Math domain only — no evidence of transfer to code, science, or general QA
  • Only tested on specific RL algorithms; unclear if findings transfer to PPO, DPO, GRPO
Open Questions
Q1
Would larger evaluation sets (500+) confirm or challenge the observed performance gaps between models?
Q2
Can the three geometric metrics be compressed into a lightweight probe to reduce computational overhead?
Q3
How does LaRA perform on non-math domains (code generation, scientific reasoning, general QA)?
Q4
Is there a causal mechanism linking specific RL training dynamics to the observed geometric signatures?
Q5
Could LaRA be extended to detect partial contamination where only a subset of training data overlaps?
Q6
How stable are results across different random seeds for RL training and perturbation generation?
Q7
Can the framework identify which specific training samples are contaminated, or only detect at dataset level?
