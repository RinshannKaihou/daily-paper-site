> **一句话总结：** 本文系统性地揭示了LLM-as-a-judge评估面板中模型错误高度相关的问题——9个来自7个模型族的前沿LLM的投票实际上只相当于约2个独立投票的信息量（n_eff ≈ 2.0-2.5），增加评委数量或改用更优聚合算法（包括Dawid-Skene EM和精确加权投票）均最多只能弥补11%的差距，问题根源在于评委在相同item上犯相同错误。

> **TL;DR:** A panel of 9 frontier LLMs from 7 model families provides only ~2 effective independent votes (n_eff ≈ 2.0-2.5) because the models make correlated errors on the same items. The panel's accuracy falls 8-22 percentage points short of Condorcet independent-voting predictions, and the best single judge (Claude Sonnet 4.5) matches or outperforms the full panel across all conditions. Neither adding judges (scaling curve asymptotes at n_eff ≈ 2.56) nor smarter aggregation (Dawid-Skene, accuracy-weighted, phi-optimal) closes more than 11% of this gap -- the bottleneck is correlated judges, not the algorithm.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了一套基于Kish有效样本量(n_eff)和Condorcet零模型的量化框架，首次对LLM评委面板的信息独立性进行系统性的项目级(item-level)测量
- 在ChaosNLI的三个NLI数据集（MNLI、SNLI、AlphaNLI，每个item含100条人工标注）上测试了9个来自7个模型族的评委，发现n_eff仅为2.0-2.5，名义独立性的约75%因相关错误而丧失
- 揭示Condorcet差距（独立投票预测准确率与实际准确率之差）为8-22个百分点（排列检验p < 10^-4），且最佳单一评委（Claude Sonnet 4.5）在所有条件下均匹配或超越整个面板
- 证明Dawid-Skene EM和精确加权投票等聚合方法即使在获得金标答案的情况下也最多只能弥补11%的差距，phi-optimal（Markowitz）加权在部分数据集上反而降低性能
- 展示了scaling curve（扩展曲线）：增加评委超过5人时收益趋近于零，n_eff渐近线约为2.56（1/φ）

## Key Contributions

- Develops the first principled framework for item-level measurement of effective independence in LLM judge panels, combining Kish effective sample size (n_eff) with a Condorcet null model that simulates majority-vote accuracy under independent voting
- Empirically demonstrates that 9 judges from 7 model families yield n_eff ≈ 2.0-2.5 across three ChaosNLI datasets (MNLI, SNLI, AlphaNLI), three prompt variants, two temperature settings, and RewardBench -- losing ~75% of nominal independence
- Quantifies the Condorcet gap at 8-22pp (permutation p < 10^-4), with the best single judge matching or outperforming the full panel across all conditions
- Provides a definitive negative result: established aggregation methods (Dawid-Skene EM, accuracy-weighted voting, phi-optimal Markowitz weighting) close at most 11% of the gap even with oracle access to gold labels, proving the bottleneck is structural (correlated inputs) rather than algorithmic
- Shows the scaling curve asymptotes at n_eff ≈ 2.56 (1/φ), meaning adding judges beyond 5 yields negligible benefit -- effective independence cannot be bought with more judges

## Methodology

- **Datasets:** ChaosNLI (Nie et al., 2020) -- 100 annotator labels per item. Primary: ChaosNLI-MNLI (1,599 items from Williams et al. 2018). Replications: ChaosNLI-SNLI (1,514 items) and ChaosNLI-AlphaNLI (1,532 abductive NLI items, 2-class). 1,000 items sampled per dataset using entropy-stratified sampling (equal proportions from low/medium/high human-entropy terciles).
- **Judge Panel:** 9 frontier LLMs from 7 model families (Table 1). All use temperature 0.0 with a standardized NLI classification prompt. Includes models from Anthropic, Google, OpenAI, Meta, DeepSeek, Mistral, and Alibaba.
- **Kish n_eff:** For each judge, a binary error vector e_j is constructed. The 9x9 pairwise phi correlation matrix is computed from these error vectors. Kish n_eff = k^2 / (sum of all pairwise correlations), measuring how many truly independent votes the panel contains.
- **Condorcet Null Model:** Per-judge, per-bin confusion matrices estimated from data, used to simulate what majority-vote accuracy would be if judges voted independently (preserving individual accuracy but removing correlation).
- **Aggregation Methods Tested:** Majority vote, Dawid-Skene EM, accuracy-weighted voting, and phi-optimal (Markowitz portfolio-style) weighting using the inverse phi correlation matrix.
- **Robustness Conditions:** Three prompt variants, temperature 0.0 and 1.0, chain-of-thought reasoning, and RewardBench pairwise preference task.
- **Supplementary Analyses:** Distributional alignment (TV distance, symmetric KL divergence), split-half Condorcet validation (overfitting ratios 0.997/0.960/1.000), leave-one-out analysis, per-class stratification, all-wrong item analysis.

## Results & Highlights

- **n_eff ≈ 2.0-2.5** across all three NLI datasets and all conditions, despite panel accuracy ranging from 69% to 93%
- **Condorcet gap of 8-22pp** (MNLI: 22.0pp, SNLI: 12.5pp, AlphaNLI: 8.1pp, RewardBench: 19.5pp); all permutation p < 10^-4
- **Best single judge matches/beats the panel** in all conditions; removing 6 of 9 judges actually improves accuracy in the leave-one-out analysis
- **Scaling curve:** Mean n_eff for 2 judges = 1.45, 5 judges = 1.96, 9 judges = 2.18, asymptote ≈ 2.56; adding judges beyond 5 yields negligible benefit
- **Cross-family correlations can be as high as same-family:** Claude Sonnet x Gemini 2.5 Pro (φ = 0.603), GPT-4o x Claude Sonnet (φ = 0.588); lowest pair: Gemini 2.5 Pro x Llama 4 Scout (φ = 0.161)
- **Aggregation cannot fix it:** Dawid-Skene closes 0-5% of gap; accuracy-weighted closes 0-11%; phi-optimal closes up to 20.6% on RewardBench but hurts AlphaNLI (86.2%, below majority vote)
- **51 all-wrong items on MNLI:** 70.6% are high-entropy items; over-prediction of contradiction accounts for 51% of failures; mean human support for the panel's wrong answer is only 35.3%
- **Distributional misalignment:** TV distance between panel and human distributions correlates strongly with human entropy (ρ_s = 0.434, p < 10^-46)

The methodology draws on well-established statistical tools from survey methodology (Kish design effect) and political science (Condorcet jury theorem), applied correctly to the LLM evaluation context. ## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. While error correlation among LLMs has been noted qualitatively (Kim et al., 2025; Jiang et al., 2025 "artificial hivemind"), and Turkmen et al. (2026) formalized an information-theoretic error floor, this paper provides the first item-level measurement of effective independence in the judge evaluation setting using Kish n_eff and the Condorcet gap. The negative result on aggregation methods is a meaningful contribution beyond diagnosis.
- **Rigor:** Very strong. Three datasets, entropy-stratified sampling, multiple robustness conditions, split-half validation (overfitting ratios near 1.0), bootstrap confidence intervals, permutation tests, and distributional metrics all support the claims. The leave-one-out analysis and scaling curve provide additional mechanistic insight.
- **Reproducibility:** High. All datasets are public (ChaosNLI, RewardBench). Prompts are provided in appendices. Model families are named (though exact version details and API dates could be tighter). Tie-breaking is deterministic (SHA-256 hashing). Seed is specified (42).
- **Significance:** Very high. The finding that n_eff asymptotes at ~2.56 fundamentally challenges the "more judges = better" scaling assumption underlying work like PoLL (Verga et al., 2024). The negative result on aggregation has direct practical implications for how the community designs evaluation pipelines.
- **Red flags:** (1) The "best single judge beats the panel" finding is post-hoc -- you need to know which judge is best in advance. In practice, without ground truth, you cannot identify the best judge. The paper acknowledges this indirectly but does not fully grapple with it. (2) The claim that "unanimous panel agreement is far less diagnostic than it appears" is important but the item-level diagnosticity analysis is somewhat underdeveloped relative to the n_eff findings. (3) NLI tasks may have particularly structured error patterns (e.g., the systematic over-prediction of contradiction) that could amplify correlation relative to more open-ended evaluation tasks.

## Strengths

- Principled statistical framework with two complementary metrics (Kish n_eff for information content, Condorcet gap for accuracy impact)
- Exhaustive robustness testing: three datasets, three prompt variants, two temperatures, CoT, RewardBench, split-half validation, distributional alignment
- The scaling curve analysis (Table 8) is particularly valuable -- it quantifies exactly how n_eff saturates as judges are added, providing actionable guidance
- The negative result on aggregation is cleanly demonstrated with oracle access, ruling out the most obvious objection
- Excellent related work positioning: clearly distinguishes from PoLL (which compares to average judge, not best judge), connects to Condorcet theory, and acknowledges complementary theoretical work

## Limitations

- Restricted to NLI and pairwise preference tasks; generalizability to open-ended generation, code evaluation, or multi-turn dialogue assessment is not established
- The "best single judge" benchmark is post-hoc and not actionable without ground truth for judge selection
- Does not propose or test interventions for achieving genuine independence (e.g., adversarial training, diverse pre-training data, structured disagreement protocols)
- NLI's structured label space (3-class) may inflate correlation relative to more open-ended evaluation where judge disagreement has more degrees of freedom
- Single author: while the work is thorough, independent replication by a separate team would strengthen confidence

## Relevance

本文直接挑战了LLM-as-a-judge评估范式中"增加评委数量提升可靠性"的核心假设，对任何使用多模型评估面板的自动化评测工作（包括benchmark构建、模型排名、对齐评估等）具有根本性的警示意义，表明当前主流的多评委聚合策略在统计上存在严重缺陷。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can adversarial training or structured disagreement protocols (e.g., requiring judges to argue for different positions before voting) produce genuinely independent LLM judges?
- Is the correlation primarily driven by shared pre-training data, similar RLHF objectives, or fundamental properties of the task structure -- and does the answer differ by evaluation domain?
- How does n_eff change when judges are drawn from fundamentally different architectures (e.g., LLMs + reward models + human annotators + rule-based systems) rather than all being LLMs?
- Could item-level prediction of correlation patterns enable adaptive evaluation -- using panels only on items where independence is highest, and falling back to single judges or humans on high-correlation items?
- The scaling curve asymptotes at 1/φ ≈ 2.56 for mean pairwise φ = 0.391. What is the theoretical minimum φ achievable with current LLM architectures, and what would it take to reach n_eff = 5 or higher?
- Does the finding generalize to generative evaluation (e.g., LLM-judged pairwise comparisons on open-ended prompts) where the label space is not discrete?
