Diagnostic Framework

VLA-Trace: Revealing how Vision-Language-Action models actually route control

A three-stage progressive diagnostic that traces two mainstream VLA models—pi0.5 and OpenVLA—from representation geometry through causal attribution to behavioral probes, uncovering fundamentally different routing strategies and a shared blind spot.

Shi et al. (X-Humanoid) 2026-05-28 pi0.5 · OpenVLA LIBERO suites
Key Metric
−76.45 avg drop
Target-object masking degradation on pi0.5. Removing visual access during generation collapses success to ~0%.
OpenVLA same probe: −60.25 avg · Gen:no text → 0% on all suites
Why This Matters
Architecture-level routing differences mean VLA design choices have hidden consequences for how models actually control robots—pi0.5 depends on a narrow visual bottleneck while OpenVLA requires both modalities. Neither can follow edited instructions well.
Critical Edge
Only two models fully analyzed; no demonstrated improvements. Purely diagnostic—the paper identifies problems but does not prove that fixing them helps. Generalizability to newer architectures remains open.
Methodology — Three-Stage Progressive Pipeline
Stage 1

Representation Analysis

How do representations shift across training checkpoints?

  • Cross-modal CKA: layer-wise alignment between visual and textual pools
  • Checkpoint-drift CKA: geometric change from C0 (pretrained VLM) to C1 (pretrained VLA) to C2 (task-finetuned)
Stage 2

Causal Pathway Analysis

Which modality pathways are necessary for action generation?

  • Prefill knockout: block cross-modal token interaction
  • Generation knockout: isolate modality dependency during decoding
  • Layer-wise knockout: localize critical layers
Stage 3

Behavioral Probes

Does the policy ground to the right objects and follow instructions?

  • Attention IoU: heatmap overlap with simulator masks
  • Visual patch masking: target, gripper, robot, background
  • Input editing: swap objects or instructions
Key Results
pi0.5 Checkpoint Drift — Text Reorganization
0.05–0.14
Text drift (CKA) is dramatically lower than vision (0.57–0.80), indicating heavy linguistic reorganization into task-conditioned control features.
OpenVLA Checkpoint Drift — Visual Restructuring
0.89–0.99
Text representations are preserved (near 1.0); vision and joint subspaces undergo the primary restructuring instead.
pi0.5 Generation Knockout
~0%
Removing visual access during generation collapses success across all LIBERO suites. Removing text preserves performance on Goal/Object/Spatial. The visual-to-action pathway is the critical bottleneck.
OpenVLA Generation Knockout
0%
Removing text access during generation kills success uniformly. Removing images is harmful but not a total collapse. Both modalities are necessary.
Visual Masking & Attention Localization
Both models show higher Robot+Object IoU than Object-only, meaning attention targets interaction regions. pi0.5 distributes attention broadly; OpenVLA concentrates it sparsely. Temporal attention shifts correctly between subgoals (e.g., stove → moka pot).

Input Editing — Semantic Following Gap
Replacing target objects in instructions does not consistently redirect behavior. Example: moka pot → frypan drops success from 75% to 30%, but other edits have limited effect. Visual grounding is strong; compositional language control is weak.
Assessment

Strengths

  • Unified progressive framework bridging representation, causal, and behavioral analysis in a coherent pipeline
  • Clean experimental design separating prefill and generation stages
  • Honest and detailed limitations section covering seven specific points
  • Interesting finding that pi0.5 and OpenVLA have opposite adaptation strategies despite similar task performance
  • Temporal attention analysis (phase-split IoU) is a clever way to probe subgoal tracking

Limitations

  • Only two VLA models fully analyzed; broader protocol defined but not executed
  • No actionable improvements demonstrated—diagnoses problems without showing fixes help
  • CKA baselines (random features, permutation) absent, making magnitude calibration hard
  • Rollout success rates lack confidence intervals
  • Attention knockout design choices could influence conclusions; only one design per model
  • Behavioral probes limited to simulation (LIBERO); real-world transfer not assessed
Open Questions
  1. Would explicit fusion gates or modality-routing modules at mid-to-late layers actually improve semantic following?
  2. How do the observed adaptation dynamics generalize to newer architectures (pi0.7, Gemini Robotics, GR00T N1)?
  3. Can counterfactual instruction-image pairs and contrastive behavior supervision close the semantic control gap?
  4. Is the narrow visual bottleneck in pi0.5 a feature (enabling generalization) or a bug (fragile to visual perturbation)?
  5. How do CKA and attention knockout findings transfer to real-world settings with sim-to-real distribution shifts?
