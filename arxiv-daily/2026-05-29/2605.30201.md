> **一句话总结：** HPO通过降低负优势更新权重并改用批次均值长度归一化，解决GRPO在稀疏奖励下负优势样本主导导致训练不稳定的问题；其自适应版本A-HPO在TeleLogs上超越GRPO 15%（奖励0.84 vs 0.73），在Countdown跨1.5B-7B模型的困难配置上均有显著提升。

> **TL;DR:** This paper identifies two coupled failure modes of GRPO under sparse binary rewards: (1) negative-advantage responses vastly outnumber positive ones early in training, skewing updates toward suppressing rare successes, and (2) per-response length normalization makes update magnitude inversely proportional to response length, coupling optimization pressure to output length. The proposed fix, Hysteretic Policy Optimization (HPO), down-weights negative-advantage updates by a factor alpha and replaces per-response normalization with batch-mean length normalization. Its adaptive variant (A-HPO) sets alpha automatically from the batch sign-frequency ratio p+_hat / p-_hat, relaxing hysteresis as training progresses. On TeleLogs, A-HPO reaches 0.84 reward vs GRPO's 0.73, SAPO's 0.79, and GSPO's 0.75. On Countdown, A-HPO provides the largest gains in early, hard, and small-model regimes. The method adds negligible overhead and integrates into existing GRPO pipelines with a single advantage-estimator change.

## 核心贡献

- 精确识别了GRPO在稀疏二元奖励下的两个耦合失败模式：负优势样本主导（p- >> p+）和逐响应长度归一化使更新幅度与输出长度耦合，并给出了负优势主导的数学证明（p-/p+比值在p << 1/2时大于1且随成功率降低而增大）。
- 提出HPO，仅用一个参数alpha在正例优先（alpha=0）和完全对称（alpha=1）之间插值，同时将逐响应长度归一化替换为批次均值长度归一化，以最小修改解决上述两个问题。
- 提出A-HPO，基于批次级优势符号频率比p+_hat/p-_hat自适应设定滞后权重，具有理想极限行为：稀疏时强抑制负更新，策略改善后自动恢复近对称更新。
- 在TeleLogs上A-HPO达到0.84奖励，超越GRPO 15%、GSPO 11%、SAPO 5%，且响应长度相当（686 vs 729 tokens）。
- 消融实验表明最佳行为位于alpha=0（退化为短输出猜测）和alpha=1（完全对称）之间，alpha约0.6表现最优，A-HPO自动追踪该最优区域。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies and mathematically characterizes two coupled GRPO failure modes under sparse binary rewards: negative-advantage dominance (p- >> p+ when success probability p << 1/2) and response-level length normalization that couples optimization pressure to output length.
- Proposes HPO, a one-parameter interpolation (alpha in [0,1]) between positive-only and symmetric policy-gradient updates, combined with batch-mean length normalization replacing per-response normalization.
- Introduces A-HPO, which adaptively sets alpha = clip(p+_hat / (p-_hat + epsilon), alpha_min, 1) from batch-level sign statistics, with desired limiting behavior: strong hysteresis when sparse, relaxation toward symmetry as policy improves.
- Demonstrates A-HPO achieves 0.84 reward on TeleLogs (vs GRPO 0.73, GSPO 0.75, SAPO 0.79) with comparable response length (686 vs 729 tokens).
- Provides ablations showing the positive-only limit (alpha=0) collapses to degenerate short-output guessing (mean length 16 tokens, reward 0.71), while alpha~0.6 is optimal and A-HPO automatically tracks this region across training checkpoints.

## Methodology

The method is built on a careful analysis of GRPO's behavior under sparse binary rewards. The key observations are:

**Failure Mode Analysis:**
- In GRPO, advantages are group-centered: A_hat_i = R_i - mean(R). With binary rewards and low success probability p, the probability of a response having negative advantage is p- = (1-p)(1-(1-p)^{N-1}), while positive advantage probability is p+ = p(1-p^{N-1}). For p << 1/2, p-/p+ > 1 and grows as success probability decreases.
- Per-response length normalization (dividing by |tau_i|) makes aggregate contribution inversely proportional to length: short positive responses dominate, long negative responses are weakly penalized.

**HPO Design (3 modifications to GRPO):**
1. Removes standard deviation from advantage computation: A_hat_i = R_i - mean(R) (following prior work on question-level difficulty bias).
2. Replaces per-response length normalization with batch-mean length normalization using |tau_bar| = E[mean(|tau_i|)].
3. Applies asymmetric hysteretic weighting: w_i = 1 if A_hat_i >= 0, w_i = alpha if A_hat_i < 0, where alpha in [0,1].

**A-HPO Adaptive Rule:**
- Derives from a gradient-magnitude balance criterion: ||G+|| ~ alpha * ||G-||.
- Approximates the gradient norm ratio via sign frequencies: alpha_adaptive = clip(p+_hat / (p-_hat + epsilon), alpha_min, 1).
- The authors acknowledge this is a lightweight heuristic, not an unbiased gradient-norm estimator, and validate it post hoc via a clipped-surrogate contribution diagnostic.

**Baselines compared:** GRPO, GSPO (Group Sequence Policy Optimization), SAPO (Soft Adaptive Policy Optimization), RAFT-like positive-only (alpha=0). Also includes diagnostic variants N-HPO (hysteresis only, no mean-length normalization) and V-HPO (variance-aware adaptive factor).

**Experimental setup:** Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct, Llama3.1-3B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct; N=8 rollouts per prompt; batch size 16 prompts; learning rate 1e-6; PPO clip 0.2; alpha_min=0.4.

## Results & Highlights

**TeleLogs (Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct):**
- A-HPO achieves 0.84 reward vs GRPO 0.73 (+15%), GSPO 0.75 (+11%), SAPO 0.79 (+5%).
- Response length is comparable: A-HPO 686 tokens vs GRPO 729 tokens.
- The positive-only limit (alpha=0) degenerates to 16-token outputs with 0.71 reward, confirming gains are not from trivial answer shortening.
- Ablation (Table 1): alpha=0.6 gives best fixed-HPO result (0.83), closely matched by A-HPO (0.84).

**Countdown (cross-model evaluation, Table 2):**
- A-HPO's advantage is largest in early, hard, and small-model regimes.
- Qwen2.5-7B Countdown-4: 0.63 vs 0.58 (GRPO) at 200 steps, 0.65 vs 0.58 at 1000 steps.
- Llama3.1-3B Countdown-4: 0.54 vs 0.40 at 200 steps (+0.14).
- Qwen2.5-1.5B Countdown-3: 0.47 vs 0.37 at 200 steps (+0.10).
- On saturated/easy settings (7B Countdown-3), both methods converge (0.94-0.95).

**Contribution Balance Diagnostic (Figure 4):**
- Tracks surrogate balance ratio rho_tilde = (p+ * m+_tilde) / (p- * m-_tilde) where m+_tilde are mean absolute PPO contributions.
- A-HPO progressively corrects the negative-dominance imbalance, maintaining rho_tilde near or above 1. GRPO shows noisier, less sustained balancing.

**Larger model feasibility (Table 3):**
- Qwen2.5-32B on TeleLogs: HPO improves pass@1 from 49.45% (SFT) to 95.86% (SFT+RL). These start from strong SFT checkpoints and serve as feasibility evidence rather than sparse-regime evaluation.

**Batch size ablation (Figure 6):**
- Even at batch size 1 (8 rollouts for 1 prompt), all HPO variants outperform GRPO substantially.
- A-HPO provides the best stability-performance trade-off across batch sizes.
- N-HPO (hysteresis only, original normalization) improves over GRPO, and A-HPO further improves over N-HPO, confirming both components contribute.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate. The hysteretic concept originates from Matignon et al. (2007) for multi-agent Q-learning. The contribution is transferring this idea to GRPO-style policy optimization for LLMs, with the specific insight about sign imbalance under sparse rewards being novel and well-argued. The mean-length normalization is a straightforward fix but well-motivated. The adaptive rule is a simple heuristic, not a principled optimization.
- **Rigor:** Strong. The paper provides a formal derivation of the sign imbalance ratio (p-/p+), clear ablations isolating each component (N-HPO, V-HPO), multiple baselines (GRPO, GSPO, SAPO), cross-model evaluation, and a post-hoc diagnostic validating the balance mechanism. The authors are honest that the sign-frequency approximation is not an unbiased estimator.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code snippet for VERL integration is provided. Hyperparameters are clearly listed (Table 4). Two benchmarks with multiple model scales. The minimal-modification nature aids reproducibility. However, the TeleLogs dataset appears to be proprietary to Huawei.
- **Significance:** High practical relevance. GRPO is the standard method for RL-based LLM reasoning training (DeepSeek-Math, etc.), and sparse binary rewards are the default for math and code tasks. A single-line advantage-estimator change that consistently improves performance is likely to be widely adopted. The insight about negative-advantage dominance is independently valuable.
- **Red flags:** (1) TeleLogs is a domain-specific benchmark not publicly available, making independent verification difficult. (2) The Countdown task is relatively simple (combine numbers with arithmetic operations); evaluation on more complex reasoning benchmarks (MATH, GSM8K) would strengthen claims. (3) On some saturated settings, GRPO matches or slightly exceeds A-HPO, suggesting the benefit is regime-dependent. (4) No comparison to other sparse-reward techniques like reward shaping or curriculum learning. (5) The paper evaluates only instruction-tuned models; it is unclear whether findings transfer to base models.

## Strengths

- Identifies a precise, mathematically characterized failure mode rather than proposing a generic improvement.
- Minimal modification: one parameter (alpha) change to existing GRPO pipelines, negligible computational overhead.
- A-HPO removes hyperparameter tuning entirely via the adaptive rule.
- Thorough ablation design: N-HPO isolates normalization, V-HPO provides an alternative adaptive scheme, positive-only baseline confirms negative signal is needed.
- Honest framing: "a narrow but common failure mode," transparent that p+_hat/p-_hat is a heuristic not an unbiased estimator.
- Clear practical guidance: recommended alpha in [0.4, 0.8], A-HPO preferred with batch size >= 16.
- Strong empirical gains exactly where claimed (sparse, early, hard, small-model regimes).

## Limitations

- Scope explicitly limited to sparse binary verifiable rewards; no evaluation on dense reward settings (RLHF with reward models), continuous control, or non-binary outcomes.
- TeleLogs is a proprietary Huawei benchmark; independent verification on this task is not possible for external researchers.
- Countdown is a relatively simple arithmetic reasoning task; evaluation on standard benchmarks (MATH, LiveCodeBench, GPQA) is missing.
- The theoretical analysis assumes comparable per-sample gradient magnitudes across advantage signs; this assumption is not empirically validated.
- On saturated/easy settings, A-HPO provides no benefit or slightly underperforms GRPO (Llama3.1-3B Countdown-3 at 1000 steps).
- No comparison to other sparse-reward strategies (reward shaping, curriculum learning, hindsight experience replay).
- The 32B model evaluation starts from a strong SFT checkpoint (49.45% pass@1), so it does not test the sparse-regime claim at scale.

## Relevance

本文针对GRPO在稀疏奖励RL训练中的负优势主导问题提出了简洁有效的解决方案。对于从事LLM推理能力训练（数学推理、代码生成、RLHF）的研究者具有直接实用价值——A-HPO仅需修改优势估计器即可集成到现有VERL等训练框架中。核心洞察（稀疏奖励下负优势样本主导导致训练不稳定）对理解GRPO训练动态具有独立参考意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does A-HPO interact with other GRPO improvements like DAPO (dynamic advantage policy optimization) or Dr. GRPO? Can the adaptive hysteretic mechanism compose with these methods?
- Does the sign-frequency approximation hold for dense reward settings, or does it break down when per-sample gradient magnitudes vary substantially across advantage signs?
- How does A-HPO perform on standard reasoning benchmarks (MATH-500, LiveCodeBench, GPQA Diamond) where rewards are still sparse but tasks are more complex?
- Could the adaptive rule be improved by incorporating gradient magnitude information (e.g., using EMA of per-sign loss magnitudes) rather than relying purely on sign counts?
- What is the interaction between A-HPO and rollout count? Does the benefit diminish as N (rollouts per prompt) increases, since more rollouts naturally reduce sign imbalance?
- The paper shows alpha~0.6 is optimal on TeleLogs; is this value transferable across domains, or does the optimal alpha vary significantly with task structure?
