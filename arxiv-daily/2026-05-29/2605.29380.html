Key Finding

TRACER: Persistent Regularization for Robust Multimodal Finetuning

Asadollahzadeh, Liu, Leckie, Erfani — University of Melbourne
Standard EMA teachers collapse during contrastive finetuning — their regularizing signal vanishes precisely when OOD robustness is most fragile. A Weighted Moving Average teacher with Beta(0.5, 0.5) kernel maintains persistent regularization and bias-free convergence.
CLIP ViT-B/16 · RN50 · ViT-L/14 3 seeds · 10 epochs github.com/HesamAsad/TRACER
Key Metric
64.37%
Average OOD accuracy on ViT-B/16 across ImageNet-V2, -R, -A, -S, ObjectNet.
+1.86pp over CaRot, best on nearly every shift variant.
Why This Matters
The EMA collapse problem is largely overlooked in robust finetuning literature. Fixing it via WMA teachers has implications across all momentum-teacher methods, not just CLIP adaptation.
Critical Edge
The theoretical analysis relies on linearized encoders — real CLIP models are highly nonlinear. The Beta(0.5,0.5) kernel is heuristic, not derived from first principles. Evaluation is limited to ImageNet distribution shifts only.

Methodology

Linearized multimodal contrastive learning (MMCL) reformulated as a matrix least-squares problem via a contrastive target matrix, yielding closed-form solutions for Direct FT, L2-SP, and static self-distillation.

Geometric decomposition: finetuning preserves pretrained knowledge in directions orthogonal to finetuning data, and adapts within the task-relevant subspace — revealing where forgetting occurs.

  • EMA teachers converge toward the student, collapsing the regularizing signal
  • WMA teacher: kernel-weighted trajectory average with Beta(0.5,0.5) U-shaped kernel retains mass at both start and end of training
  • Composite distillation: Feature, Contrastive Relational, Interactive Contrastive, and Cross Knowledge Distillation
  • Objective: L_MMCL + λ_SD · L_SD-WMA

Strengths

  • Deep theoretical grounding with formal proofs (Theorems 3.2, 3.4)
  • EMA collapse insight is genuinely novel and practically important
  • Clean geometric interpretation makes the method interpretable
  • Multi-perspective distillation well-motivated by the decomposition
  • Comprehensive ablations across four axes
  • Minimal computational overhead: WMA is a simple online update
  • Honest discussion of limitations and scope

Limitations

  • Theoretical analysis relies on linearized encoders; real networks are highly nonlinear
  • Evaluation limited to CLIP on ImageNet distribution shifts only
  • Beta(0.5, 0.5) kernel is heuristic; no guidance for kernel selection
  • No comparison with parameter-efficient methods (LoRA, adapters)
  • 4 loss components; interaction between them not fully analyzed
  • Slightly higher memory footprint than EMA for trajectory-averaged teacher

Open Questions

  1. Can WMA trajectory-regularization be combined with parameter-efficient methods (LoRA, adapters) for lower compute?
  2. Does the linearized analysis provide useful predictions for nonlinear networks, or is it primarily a design guide?
  3. How does TRACER interact with prompt-based adaptation methods (CoOp, MaPLe)?
  4. Can Beta kernel parameters be adapted automatically during training?
  5. What happens when applied to multimodal LLMs (e.g., LLaVA) — does the WMA insight transfer to generative settings?
  6. Does persistent regularization slow convergence compared to EMA-based methods?
