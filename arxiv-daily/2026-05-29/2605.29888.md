> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 LaRA 框架，通过层级表示几何分析检测 RL 后训练中的数据污染，引入 RSM（扰动敏感度）、DC（方向坍缩）、RSI（局部表示刚性）三个互补指标，在三个开源 RL 推理模型上实现最高 +9.6% AUC 提升和 3.5 倍 TPR@FPR=5% 增益，优于现有输出级基线。

> **TL;DR:** LaRA is a layer-wise representation analysis framework for detecting data contamination (membership inference) in RL post-trained LLMs. It introduces three geometric metrics -- RSM (perturbation sensitivity), DC (directional collapse), and RSI (local rigidity) -- that operate on hidden representations rather than output probabilities, bypassing the poor calibration of RL-trained models. Evaluated on three 7B RL reasoning models (Eurus, LIMR, OLMo), LaRA achieves up to 0.81 AUC and outperforms all output-level baselines (Min-K%, PPL, SC, etc.) by up to +9.6% AUC and 3.5x TPR@FPR=5%. Combining LaRA with output-level SC yields further gains. Key finding: contaminated samples exhibit amplified perturbation sensitivity, stronger directional collapse, and enhanced local rigidity across transformer layers.

## 核心贡献

- 首次提出基于表示级的框架和训练/评估设置，用于检测 RL 后训练中的数据污染（通过刚性和刚性指标）
- 引入三个互补指标：RSM（表示位移幅度）量化扰动敏感度、DC（方向坍缩）量化方向集中度、RSI（表示稳定性指数）量化局部刚性
- 提出污染检测协议 S_LaRA，在多个 RL 训练模型上持续优于输出级基线，最高实现 +9.6% AUC 提升和 3.5 倍 TPR@FPR=5%
- 提供了 RL 训练如何影响各层表示几何的经验洞察：污染信号在 RL 优化过程中变得更加可分离

## Key Contributions

- First representation-level framework for detecting data contamination in RL post-training, formulated as Membership Inference Attack (MIA)
- Three complementary geometric metrics: Representation Shift Magnitude (RSM), Directional Collapse (DC), and Representation Stability Index (RSI), each capturing distinct aspects of RL-induced memorization
- Detection protocol S_LaRA that aggregates robust z-scored deviations across layers and metrics, achieving up to 0.81 AUC on LIMR -- +9.6% AUC improvement and 3.5x higher TPR@FPR=5% vs. strongest output-level baseline
- Empirical demonstration that contamination signals become increasingly separable during RL optimization (Eurus: AUC improves from 0.73 to 0.79 across epochs)
- Evaluation on three open-source 7B RL-trained reasoning models: EURUS-2-7B-PRIME, LIMR, and OLMo-3.1-7B-RL-Zero-Math

## Methodology

LaRA formulates contamination detection as Membership Inference Attack (MIA). Given an RL-trained model M and candidate sample x, the goal is to determine F(M,x) in {0,1}. The core hypothesis is that RL-induced memorization produces abnormal representation responses under controlled perturbations.

**Contamination benchmark construction:** For each model, 30 Olympiad-level math problems from the model's own RL training set serve as members, paired with 30 AIME 2026 problems as non-members. Additional RL training is conducted on 1000-sample corpora to track how member vs. non-member signals diverge.

**Three metrics:**
1. **RSM (Representation Shift Magnitude):** Generates K semantically similar questions, applies importance-based blanking to remove key information, then measures how much the original question's representation shifts compared to its semantic neighbors. High RSM indicates memorization-driven perturbation sensitivity.
2. **DC (Directional Collapse):** Measures cosine alignment between the original perturbation direction and the average perturbation direction of semantic neighbors. High DC indicates representation changes collapse toward shared dominant directions (reduced representational diversity).
3. **RSI (Representation Stability Index):** Generates M paraphrastic variants of perturbed questions and measures local representation variability. Standardized against semantic neighbors to capture anomalous local rigidity.

**Detection protocol (S_LaRA):** Three steps -- (1) Robust z-score standardization using median and MAD per metric-layer pair, (2) metric-specific anomaly alignment (sign correction), (3) aggregation across all metrics and layers into a single per-sample score.

## Results & Highlights

- **Initial checkpoints:** S_LaRA achieves best overall performance on LIMR (AUC=0.80, TPR@FPR=5%=0.46), substantially outperforming Recall, CDD, Min-K%, Min-K%++, and PPL. On Eurus, combining SC + S_LaRA reaches AUC=0.73, TPR@FPR=5%=0.31.
- **Across RL training:** On Eurus, SC + S_LaRA steadily improves from (0.73, 0.31) to (0.79, 0.38) in (AUC, TPR@FPR=5%), showing contamination signals become more separable during RL optimization.
- **Metric ablations:** DC provides the strongest standalone signal but varies across epochs. Combining all three metrics (RSM+DC+RSI) consistently achieves the best overall performance. Removing any single component degrades performance.
- **Perturbation robustness:** LaRA is robust across perturbation types (AUC 0.56-0.69), and to the number of blanks (default k=1 is already competitive).
- **Complementarity:** S_LaRA is negatively correlated with SC (rho=-0.16) and PPL (rho=-0.29), confirming it captures orthogonal information. Combining output-level (SC) and representation-level (S_LaRA) signals yields further gains.
- **OLMo results weaker:** Performance on OLMo is notably lower across all methods, suggesting detection difficulty varies by model architecture/training recipe.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The shift from output-level to representation-level contamination detection for RL post-training is genuinely novel. The three-metric geometric framework (RSM, DC, RSI) is well-motivated by the fundamental mismatch between RL's trajectory-level optimization and token-level output signals.
- **Rigor:** Solid experimental design with three distinct RL-trained models, both initial checkpoint evaluation and continued training tracking, metric ablations, perturbation robustness analysis, and error analysis. The MIA framing is appropriate. However, sample sizes are small (30 members + 30 non-members per model), which limits statistical power.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. The paper uses open-source models, describes the perturbation pipeline in detail, provides algorithm pseudocode, and includes prompt templates in appendices. The evaluation benchmarks (AIME 2026, Olympiad-level math) are publicly available.
- **Significance:** Timely and important. As RL post-training becomes ubiquitous (DeepSeek-R1, OpenAI o-series, etc.), contamination detection is critical for evaluation integrity. The finding that representation geometry reveals contamination invisible to output-level methods is consequential.
- **Red flags:** (1) Small evaluation sets (60 samples per model) raise concerns about result stability. (2) OLMo results are notably weaker, suggesting the approach may not generalize well to all RL training recipes. (3) Computational overhead is non-trivial (requires generating perturbations, extracting hidden states across all layers, and aggregating metrics). (4) The optimal beta for mixing SC + S_LaRA is model-dependent, with no universally optimal setting.

## Strengths

- Addresses a critical and underexplored problem: data contamination in RL post-training (not just pre-training/SFT)
- Well-motivated theoretical grounding: RL optimizes trajectory-level rewards, making token-level output signals unreliable for contamination detection
- Three complementary metrics provide a richer picture of contamination effects than single-metric approaches
- Layer-wise analysis reveals where contamination manifests in the model, offering interpretability
- Comprehensive ablation studies (metric ablations, perturbation types, number of perturbations, beta sweep)
- Error analysis provides concrete understanding of failure modes
- Honest discussion of limitations including computational overhead and imperfect detection

## Limitations

- **Small evaluation sets:** 30 members + 30 non-members per model is limited; results may not be robust to different sample selections
- **Computational overhead:** Requires generating multiple perturbations, extracting hidden states across all transformer layers, and computing multiple geometric statistics per sample
- **Detection remains imperfect:** Many member samples go undetected, and some non-member samples produce false positives (especially when their representation geometry deviates from the typical clean distribution)
- **Model-dependent performance:** Results vary significantly across models (strong on LIMR, moderate on Eurus, weak on OLMo), suggesting sensitivity to model architecture or RL training recipe
- **Math domain only:** All experiments are on mathematical reasoning; generalization to other domains (code, science, general QA) is untested
- **RL algorithm specificity:** Only tested on models trained with specific RL algorithms (PRIME, LIMR's approach, RL-Zero); unclear if findings transfer across all RL training paradigms (PPO, DPO, GRPO, etc.)

## Relevance

本文与 RL 后训练和 LLM 推理研究高度相关。LaRA 提出的基于表示几何的污染检测方法为评估 RL 训练后推理模型（如 DeepSeek-R1、QwQ 等）的基准可靠性提供了新工具，对于关注 RL 对模型内部表示影响的研究者具有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would larger evaluation sets (e.g., 500+ samples) confirm or challenge the observed performance gaps between models?
- Can the three geometric metrics be compressed into a lightweight probe to reduce computational overhead?
- How does LaRA perform on non-math domains (code generation, scientific reasoning, general QA)?
- Is there a causal mechanism linking specific RL training dynamics (reward shaping, exploration strategies) to the observed geometric signatures?
- Could LaRA be extended to detect partial contamination where only a subset of training data overlaps with benchmarks?
- How stable are the results across different random seeds for the RL training and perturbation generation?
- Can the framework identify which specific training samples are contaminated, or only detect at the dataset level?
