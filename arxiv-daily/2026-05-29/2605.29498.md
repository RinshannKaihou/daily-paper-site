> **一句话总结：** 本文提出Target-Masked KL（TMKL）正则化器，在LoRA微调时将监督目标token从基座与适配模型的输出分布中移除并重归一化，仅对非目标词表施加KL散度，从而在不使用回放数据的情况下兼顾目标学习与遗忘抑制；在Qwen2.5-0.5B→OpenR1-Math上TMKL阻止了88–92%的WikiText-103漂移和95–98%的LAMBADA漂移，同时将目标PPL下降幅度提升约一倍。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces Target-Masked KL (TMKL), a replay-free output-space regularizer for LoRA fine-tuning that masks the supervised target token from both the frozen base and the adapted model's next-token distributions, renormalizes the remaining |V|−1 vocabulary, and applies KL only over the non-target tokens. On Qwen2.5-0.5B adapted to OpenR1-Math, TMKL prevents 88–92% of WikiText-103 drift and 95–98% of LAMBADA drift versus plain cross-entropy (CE) across four adapters (LoRA, SineLoRA, RandLoRA, DoRA) while roughly doubling the target-PPL drop; on Qwen2.5-7B→PubMed it holds both retention sets within ±1.5% of base at ≤0.13 PPL of CE on target.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示并量化了LoRA微调中的灾难性遗忘：当适应分布与预训练/对齐分布差异较大时（如Qwen2.5-7B适应PubMed），CE训练使WikiText-103漂移+15–20%、LAMBADA漂移+25–33%
- 提出TMKL（Target-Masked KL）正则化器：将监督目标token从基座与适配模型的next-token分布中移除、重归一化后，仅对非目标词表计算KL散度，与交叉熵相加
- 通过对Full-KL的三项分解（目标概率项(i)(ii)、非目标形状项(iii)）严格论证：只有项(iii)与目标学习正交，TMKL只保留该项，因此不与交叉熵信号直接对抗
- 该方法为纯损失层面操作，无需回放数据、架构修改、适配器重新设计，推理时无开销，且可即插即用于任意LoRA变体

## Key Contributions

- Quantifies LoRA forgetting under distribution-shifted, replay-free adaptation: CE produces +20–42% retention-PPL drift at 0.5B and +15–33% at 7B across four adapter families
- Introduces TMKL, which masks the supervised token, renormalizes the |V|−1 non-target vocabulary, and applies KL only over that renormalized distribution using the frozen base as teacher
- Provides a precise Full-KL decomposition (Eq. 2) showing the target-token terms (i)+(ii) actively oppose the cross-entropy gradient under distribution shift, while only the non-target shape term (iii) is orthogonal to target learning — TMKL keeps only term (iii)
- Demonstrates the recipe transfers without retuning across model scales (0.5B/7B), instruction-tuned bases, non-Qwen backbones (Llama-3.2-1B, Llama-3.1-8B, Mistral-7B-v0.3, Phi-3.5-mini), and beyond-English-PPL capabilities (factual recall, math, code, multilingual)

## Methodology

Given a frozen pretrained causal LM f_θ0 with vocabulary V, adapted via LoRA-family parameters φ on a new dataset D_new = {(x^(i), y^(i))}, standard LoRA minimizes response-token cross-entropy L_CE = (1/|M|) Σ_{t∈M} −log p_adapted,t(y_t). TMKL adds a second term.

**Renormalized non-target distributions** (Section 3.1): at each supervised position t, remove the target-token probability from both the base and adapted distributions and renormalize over V \ {y_t}:
p^ȳ_base,t(c) = p_base,t(c) / (1 − p_base,t(y_t)), and similarly for the adapted model. Each becomes a proper distribution conditioned on "the next token is not y_t." Positions where p_base,t(y_t) is near 1 are excluded via a threshold (default 1×10⁻⁴; in practice ≈0.21% of positions excluded) since they carry no retention signal.

**TMKL loss** (Eq. 1): L = L_CE + λ·L_ȳ, where L_ȳ = (1/|M|) Σ_{t∈M} KL(p^ȳ_base,t ‖ p^ȳ_adapted,t). Gradients flow only through the adapter φ, not through the frozen base. λ=0 recovers standard LoRA; λ=1 is used throughout (Pareto-optimal at both scales, Tables 13/14/19). The deployed model is identical to a CE-only one, so inference is unchanged.

**Why masking is essential (Eq. 2 decomposition):** Full KL splits into (i) target probability, (ii) total non-target mass, and (iii) non-target shape. Terms (i)+(ii) form a binary KL between base/adapted target probabilities; under distribution shift the base assigns small p_base(y) while CE pushes p_adapted(y) up, so (i)+(ii)'s gradient has the *opposite sign* to CE — full KL actively fights adaptation. Only term (iii) is orthogonal to target learning. TMKL keeps only (iii). Design choices validated by ablation: the unweighted uniform-position form beats the natural (1−p_base(y))-weighted form by 5–15pp (Table 22); forward KL(p_base‖p_adapted) dominates reverse and Jensen-Shannon (Table 20).

**Setup:** Qwen2.5-0.5B adapted to OpenR1-Math (math reasoning, released post-pretraining-cutoff so target tokens are verifiably unseen); Qwen2.5-7B adapted to PubMed (strong biomedical shift). Four headline adapters: LoRA, DoRA, SineLoRA, RandLoRA (PiSSA, AdaLoRA, VeRA in appendix). All PEFT reference implementations; rank 64, lr 5×10⁻⁴, 3 epochs, effective batch 32, BF16, single NVIDIA RTX A6000 (48 GB); means over seeds {0,1,2}. Retention measured as token-level PPL change on WikiText-103 validation and LAMBADA test. TMKL adds one frozen-base forward per step (+18%/+10% wall-clock/memory at 7B, Table 5).

## Results & Highlights

- **Qwen2.5-0.5B→OpenR1-Math, LoRA (Table 1):** CE causes WT-103 +37% and LAMBADA +42% drift; CE+TMKL reduces this to +4% (WT-103) and +2% (LAMBADA) — i.e. TMKL prevents ~89% of WT-103 drift and ~95% of LAMBADA drift — while *doubling* target-PPL drop (−0.34 vs CE's −0.16, base 3.21). The same pattern holds for SineLoRA, RandLoRA, DoRA (88–92% WT-103 prevention, 95–98% LAMBADA prevention).
- **Qwen2.5-7B→PubMed, LoRA (Table 2):** CE drifts WT-103 +18% and LAMBADA +33%; CE+TMKL holds WT-103 to −2% and LAMBADA to −0.5% (at/below base) at a target PPL of 6.55 vs CE's 6.43 (within 0.12 PPL of CE). Across all four 7B adapters, TMKL keeps both retention sets within ±1.5% of base.
- **vs replay-free baselines (Table 3, LoRA/0.5B/OpenR1-Math):** TMKL achieves +4% WT-103 / +2% LAMBADA drift, beating LwF/Full-KL (+10%/+12%, 73%/71% prevention), EWC (+28%/+31%), L2-SP (+32%/+35%), O-LoRA (+22%/+25%), and STABLE (+12%/+14%, 68%/67%) at matched target adaptation (TMKL target PPL 2.87).
- **Beyond English LM-PPL (Table 7):** on TriviaQA factual-recall, GSM8K math, HumanEval code-PPL, and FLORES-200 en→fr, every CE row degrades 9–29% at both scales while TMKL stays within ±1.5% of base.
- **Instruction-tuned base Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct→PubMed (Table 8):** CE costs 4.1–5.5 IFEval, 0.65–0.88 MT-Bench, and 11–16pp refusal rate; TMKL holds these within ±0.6 / ±0.10 / ±0.8pp at ≤0.20 PPL of CE on target.
- **Non-Qwen backbones (Tables 9/10):** on Llama-3.2-1B, Llama-3.1-8B, Mistral-7B-v0.3, Phi-3.5-mini-instruct, CE produces +17–38% drift while TMKL holds within ±2% of base at ≤0.15 PPL of CE on target, without per-family retuning.
- **Held-out mechanism probe (Fig. 3b, Table 23):** on held-out OpenR1-Math test data, TMKL reduces base-vs-adapted non-target output distance D_ȳ by 91–92% on every adapter, matching the retention-PPL prevention band — ruling out memorization since the probe uses only target data.

## Critical Assessment

- **All headline numbers are two single-target single-domain runs (0.5B→OpenR1-Math, 7B→PubMed) under a fixed recipe:** the authors explicitly flag (Limitation i) that sequential continual fine-tuning is out of scope, so TMKL's behavior under chained multi-domain adaptation — the realistic deployment regime for LoRA — is untested.
- **The retention suite is perplexity-centric and the authors' own work ([6], cited throughout) argues template/classification-style PPL can be artifactual.** Although they mitigate this by adding four non-PPL proxies (Table 7) and a held-out D_ȳ probe, the headline tables (1–2) still rest on WT-103/LAMBADA PPL, and downstream task-accuracy retention (e.g., exact-match on QA, pass@k on code) is not reported directly.
- **The closest baseline, STABLE [18], is only 5pp behind TMKL** (Table 3: STABLE 68%/67% prevention vs TMKL 89%/95%); since STABLE uses a full base-vs-adapted KL as an *acceptance gate* rather than additive loss, the marginal gain of TMKL's masking trick over a well-tuned STABLE is modest, and the paper does not report wall-clock for STABLE to assess whether TMKL's simpler loss justifies the smaller margin.
- **The deliberate dropping of the (1−p_base(y)) per-position weight** is justified empirically (5–15pp, Table 22) but the authors concede this "partially confounds this comparison with a reduction in effective λ" and leave a controlled effective-λ ablation to future work — so the optimality of the unweighted form is not fully pinned down.

## Strengths

- Clean, principled mechanism: the Full-KL three-term decomposition (Eq. 2) gives an exact, non-heuristic reason why masking the target avoids opposing cross-entropy
- Genuinely plug-and-play: one extra frozen-base forward per step, +18%/+10% wall-clock/memory at 7B, zero inference overhead, applies to any LoRA variant unchanged
- Strong, multi-axis generalization: 0.5B and 7B scales; base and instruction-tuned; Qwen, Llama, Mistral, Phi backbones; PPL + factual recall + math + code + multilingual proxies — all without retuning λ=1
- Honest statistical reporting: 3-seed means with per-cell std below 5% of the mean drift; a held-out D_ȳ probe explicitly rules out retention-set memorization

## Limitations

- Only single-target (one new domain) adaptation is evaluated; sequential/multi-target continual fine-tuning — the main practical use of LoRA — is explicitly out of scope (Limitation i)
- Text-only autoregressive LLMs; vision-language, speech, and multimodal LoRA left open (Limitation ii)
- Headline retention is WT-103/LAMBADA PPL; direct downstream task-accuracy retention (EM, pass@k) not tabulated
- Margin over the strongest baseline (STABLE) is ~5pp, and the unweighted-form optimality rests on a confounded ablation

## Questions for Further Exploration

- The authors flag sequential continual instruction-tuning as the natural next step — how does the single-target λ=1 recipe behave when the same base is adapted to a *sequence* of domains (each TMKL-regularized against the original frozen base), and does the non-target shape term (iii) still stay orthogonal when earlier adapters have already shifted p_base?
- STABLE [18] (cited) uses a full base-vs-adapted KL as an acceptance gate and trails TMKL by only ~5pp (Table 3) — would combining TMKL's target-masking with STABLE's gating recover or exceed both, and what is the wall-clock comparison the paper omits?
- The deliberate dropping of the (1−p_base(y)) per-position weight buys 5–15pp (Table 22) but is confounded with effective λ — does the controlled effective-λ ablation the authors defer change which weighting form wins?
- TMKL uses the *frozen base* as the implicit teacher (no pretraining-data access); for instruction-tuned bases the paper shows refusal calibration held within ±0.8pp (Table 8) — does the masking step adequately preserve safety-alignment under adaptation corpora that are adversarial or out-of-distribution relative to the alignment data, a regime not covered by PubMed/OpenR1-Math?
