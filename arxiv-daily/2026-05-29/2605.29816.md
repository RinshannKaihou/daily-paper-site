> **一句话总结：** 本文从理论和实验两个角度揭示了LLM在语义等价但文本不同的提示词扰动下性能下降的关键因素——扰动引起的偏差（perturbation-induced bias），并提出了一种轻量级的去偏（debiasing）微调方法，在不重新训练模型的情况下显著恢复性能并提供可证明的鲁棒性认证。

> **TL;DR:** The paper identifies perturbation-induced bias -- a systematic expected shift in LLM module outputs under random semantically-neutral prompt perturbations -- as a critical factor degrading robustness. The authors propose simple and efficient debiasing methods (input-independent and input-dependent) that operate on logits or intermediate layers, recovering substantial performance lost to perturbations and strengthening formal robustness certificates, all without expensive model retraining. Evaluated on Qwen-3-8B, Llama-3.1-8B, and Olmo-3-7B across Natural Instructions tasks with format and text perturbations.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示了扰动诱导偏差（perturbation-induced bias）**：首次从理论上证明，随机语义中性扰动会导致LLM模块输出产生系统性期望偏移，这是性能下降的关键因素，且仅靠减小Lipschitz常数或方差不足以保证鲁棒性。
- **提出了统一的鲁棒性认证框架**：将稀疏性、Lipschitz常数等已知鲁棒性决定因素整合为统一的认证表达式，适用于Lipschitz和非Lipschitz映射两种情况。
- **设计了高效的去偏方法**：提出了输入无关（常数偏置校正）和输入相关（基于数据估计的偏移补偿）两种去偏策略，无需重新训练模型即可运行，计算开销极低。
- **实现了可证明的鲁棒性认证**：去偏后不仅恢复了性能，还增强了逐例（per-example）和群体级（population-level）的鲁棒性认证能力。
- **扩展至中间层和生成任务**：展示了将去偏应用于中间层以推广到短文本生成任务（如Natural Questions、GSM-8k、TriviaQA、XSum）的初步结果。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies perturbation-induced bias as a critical, previously underappreciated factor: the expectation of perturbed representations E[M(x+dx)] may deviate substantially from M(x), and this systematic shift dominates robustness outcomes.
- Provides unified robustness certificates linking classical determinants (sparsity, Lipschitz constants, variance) into single expressions (Theorem B.1/B.5), showing that when bias C_i is large, reducing Lipschitz constants alone is insufficient.
- Proposes two debiasing methods: (1) input-independent debiasing adding constant bias b_i to logits, and (2) input-dependent debiasing estimating per-input expected shifts from perturbation samples.
- Debiasing improves both per-example and population-level certification based on margin stability analysis (Appendices D-F), with certified examples achieving near 100% BAC on perturbed variants.
- Demonstrates applicability beyond classification by debiasing intermediate layers (e.g., LoRA adapters at layer 8 mlp.up_proj) for generation and summarization tasks.

## Methodology

The paper develops a theoretical framework starting from component-wise stability analysis of LLM modules M_i(x). The key insight is that under random perturbations dx, the expected feature output E_{dx}[M_i(x+dx)] != M_i(x), creating a perturbation-induced bias. This bias is bounded by C_i = sup_{x in S} |E_{dx}[f_i(x, dx)]|.

For Lipschitz mappings, the robustness radius is: epsilon_i(phi_i) = C_i + gamma_i * epsilon_i * sqrt(2n * ln(2m/phi_i)), and for non-Lipschitz mappings: epsilon_i(phi_i) = C_i + sqrt(Vm/phi_i) * sqrt(Var[f_i]). In both cases, the bias C_i is the dominant term.

The debiasing strategy replaces M_i(x) with M_i'(x) = M_i(x) - b_i. For input-independent debiasing, b_i is a constant found by searching for optimal overlapping improvement regions across examples. For input-dependent debiasing, b_i is estimated per-input from perturbation samples, using population statistics from the training set.

The framework extends to margin stability certification, combining performance and robustness under a single framework using Chebyshev and Hoeffding inequalities for both per-example and population-level guarantees.

Experiments use Natural Instructions tasks, filtered for tasks with >1% performance drop from perturbations and >70% BAC on clean examples. Models tested: Qwen-3-8B, Llama-3.1-8B, Olmo-3-7B. Two perturbation types: format perturbations (structural template changes) and text perturbations (Unicode confusables, markup wrappers, keyboard typos). 200 examples with 200 perturbations each, split 50/35/15 for train/test/hold-out.

## Results & Highlights

- Input-dependent debiasing recovers a significant portion of perturbation-induced damage across most task-model pairs (p_recover metric), at the cost of modest clean-example performance reduction.
- When perturbations occur frequently, debiasing is net-positive (positive p_improve for most tasks).
- Robustness radii improve considerably after debiasing, as theoretically predicted.
- Per-example certification: a substantially higher percentage of examples become certifiable after debiasing; certified examples achieve near 100% BAC on perturbed variants (exceeding the failure ratio phi=0.1).
- Population-level certification: stronger guarantees after debiasing, confirmed with confidence intervals.
- Four distinct perturbation-bias interaction scenarios were empirically observed: (a) debiasing ineffective, (b) debiasing compensates by shifting threshold, (c) debiasing compensates without threshold shift, (d) perturbations accidentally help robustness.
- Intermediate layer debiasing shows promising results for generation tasks: e.g., delta_bop of 9.5 EM on NQ, 34.3 EM on GSM-8k, 35.9 EM on TriviaQA, 8.9 F1 on XSum with Qwen-8B under text perturbations.
- LoRA adapters trained on clean+perturbed data at layer 8 further improve certification when combined with debiasing.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The identification of perturbation-induced bias as a distinct and dominant factor in LLM robustness is a genuine contribution. The insight that E[M(x+dx)] != M(x) is simple but powerful, and the theoretical unification of classical robustness factors around this bias is novel. However, the debiasing methods themselves (subtracting an estimated bias) are conceptually straightforward.
- **Rigor:** The theoretical analysis is thorough, with formal theorems and proofs in appendices. The experimental evaluation covers multiple models, perturbation types, and tasks. However, the main empirical evaluation is limited to first-token prediction classification tasks, and the extension to generation tasks (Table 4) is preliminary.
- **Reproducibility:** Good -- open-weight models (Qwen, Llama, Olmo), standard benchmarks (Natural Instructions), and well-defined perturbation protocols. The methods themselves are simple to implement. However, full code release is not mentioned.
- **Significance:** Practically significant for LLM deployment where prompt variability is expected. The certification framework adds formal guarantees absent from prior work like batch calibration. The approach is computationally cheap compared to randomized smoothing.
- **Red flags:** (1) The clean-vs-perturbed performance trade-off is acknowledged but not fully addressed -- debiasing consistently reduces clean performance. (2) Scope is largely limited to classification via first-token prediction; generation task evaluation is thin. (3) The certificates rely on conservative concentration inequalities (Chebyshev, Hoeffding), potentially limiting practical tightness. (4) White-box access to model weights is required, limiting applicability to API-only settings.

## Strengths

- Strong theoretical grounding with clear practical implications -- the perturbation-induced bias concept elegantly explains why models fail under seemingly innocuous prompt changes.
- Computationally efficient: no model retraining needed, single-pass inference (unlike randomized smoothing requiring multiple passes).
- Provides formal robustness certificates, not just empirical improvements.
- Honest about limitations and trade-offs (clean performance reduction, scope limitations).
- Unified framework that subsumes multiple prior approaches (batch calibration, spectral normalization insights) under a single theoretical lens.

## Limitations

- Evaluated primarily on classification tasks (first-token prediction); generation task evidence is limited to a single table.
- Debiasing consistently reduces clean-example performance -- the clean-vs-perturbed trade-off is inherent and not resolved.
- Certificates use conservative distribution-agnostic inequalities, which may be loose in practice.
- Requires white-box access to model weights, precluding use with proprietary API-only models.
- Perturbation types studied are non-adversarial; the method does not address adversarial attacks.
- The method appears most effective in low-dimensional feature settings (effective intrinsic dimensionality of 4-5 principal components per layer), raising questions about scalability.

## Relevance

本文研究LLM在语义等价但文本形式不同的提示词扰动下的鲁棒性问题，提出了基于去偏的高效增强方法。对于关注LLM部署可靠性和提示词工程稳定性的研究具有重要参考价值，特别是其可证明的鲁棒性认证框架为实际应用提供了形式化保障。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the perturbation-induced bias scale with model size (e.g., 70B+ models)? Does the bias become more or less pronounced?
- Can the debiasing approach be combined with adversarial training to handle both non-adversarial and adversarial perturbations simultaneously?
- What is the relationship between perturbation-induced bias and in-context learning sensitivity? Could debiasing improve ICL stability?
- How tight are the Chebyshev/Hoeffding-based certificates compared to empirical failure rates? Is there room for data-dependent tighter bounds?
- Can debiasing be applied effectively to long-form generation tasks (beyond the preliminary results shown) without excessive clean-performance degradation?
- Is the perturbation-induced bias phenomenon related to the "typographic susceptibility" or other known failure modes of LLMs?
