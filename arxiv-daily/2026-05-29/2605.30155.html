Selective Multi-Neuron Relaxation Solves 49% More DNN Verification Queries

PMNR applies multi-neuron linear bounds to only a heuristically chosen subset of neurons, avoiding the exponential cost that makes full multi-neuron relaxation impractical. Integrated into Marabou, it solves 301 of 344 robustness queries where the baseline solves 202.

neural-network-verification linear-relaxation formal-methods multi-neuron-bounds robustness
Key Finding

Full multi-neuron relaxation is counterproductive — solving only 94 queries vs. 202 for single-neuron DeepPoly. But applying it to just a heuristically selected subset of neurons (PMNR) solves 301 queries: 49% more than DeepPoly, 220% more than full relaxation, and 17% faster average runtime.

Why This Matters

DNN verification has been bottlenecked by a false dichotomy: cheap single-neuron bounds (fast but loose) vs. expensive multi-neuron bounds (tight but impractical). PMNR shows the sweet spot is selective, partial relaxation — a general principle likely to transfer beyond robustness queries.

Critical Edge

Evaluated only on small MNIST fully-connected networks (max 8×100). No convolutional, residual, or large-scale architectures tested. PMNR is applied only once as initial bound tightening, not interleaved during branching — its full potential may be limited by this design choice.

Methodology

DeepPoly bounds → PickAlphas → NSSE selection → BHSO hyper-planes → PostTighten (LP)
  • PMNR loop (up to 10 iterations): single-neuron tightening, neuron selection, multi-neuron bound generation, final LP-based tightening.
  • NSSE heuristic scores neurons by aggregating symbolic bounds over all phase branches; selects d highest-scoring neurons from the most promising layer.
  • BHSO initializes hyper-planes from single-neuron relaxations, optimizes via generalized dual optimization (extending INVPROP to arbitrary activations), then refines through phase-branching.
  • Generalized INVPROP theorem supports any activation function (ReLU, LeakyReLU, Sigmoid, Bilinear, Softmax).

Strengths & Limitations

+ Strengths

  • 49% more queries solved than DeepPoly, 17% faster — a rare win-win in verification.
  • General framework: works with arbitrary activations, not limited to piecewise-linear.
  • Formal soundness proofs (Theorem 3.1) with complete appendix proofs.
  • Clean experimental design: only the tightening method varies between runs.
  • Open-source, integrated into the widely-used Marabou verifier.

− Limitations

  • Only small MNIST FC networks tested; scalability unknown for real architectures.
  • Applied only as initial bound tightening, not interleaved with branching.
  • NSSE adds overhead that slows simpler queries where base Marabou already succeeds.
  • No comparison with state-of-the-art tools (beta-CROWN, PRIMA, k-ReLU).
  • PmnrAll baseline uses same BHSO heuristics, not optimized multi-neuron methods.

Open Questions

Q1How does PMNR scale to convolutional and residual architectures used in safety-critical systems (e.g., ACAS-Xu)?
Q2Can PMNR be combined with beta-CROWN or PRIMA for synergistic improvements, or do the approaches interfere?
Q3What is the theoretical relationship between NSSE scores and actual verification improvement — can sub-optimality be bounded?
Q4Would GPU parallelization of BHSO make full multi-neuron relaxation competitive, or is per-hyper-plane cost the true bottleneck?
Q5Can the generalized INVPROP theorem transfer to preimage estimation or certified training?
Q6How does the choice of d (neurons per iteration) affect the tightness-runtime tradeoff for larger networks?
