> **一句话总结：** 本文在随机特征回归框架下系统证明神经缩放定律的指数alpha依赖于优化器选择——预条件优化器（自然梯度、Muon代理）在自然语言典型谱条件下将alpha从0.12提升至0.31（2.6倍），且该增益随谱衰减陡度增大而增长，为缩放律预测提供了一个可证伪的谱诊断工具。

> **TL;DR:** The scaling exponent alpha in neural scaling laws L(N) ~ N^{-alpha} is not a fixed constant -- it depends systematically on the optimizer. In the canonical random-feature regression framework, the authors measure alpha across 5 optimizer variants (GD, Diagonal/AdamW proxy, Full NG/Shampoo proxy, Sign-GD, Matrix-Sign/Muon proxy) and 6 spectral conditions (s=0.25 to 2.0). Preconditioned optimizers consistently yield steeper scaling, with Full NG achieving alpha~0.31 vs. alpha~0.12 for GD at s~1.0 (characteristic of natural language). The alpha-shift grows with spectral steepness and the paper provides a spectral diagnostic for predicting when advanced optimizers pay off. Whether this transfers to large-scale LLM training -- where recent evidence (Wen et al., 2025) suggests the advantage may attenuate with scale -- remains an open question. Accepted at ICML 2026 HiLD Workshop.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统测量缩放指数alpha作为优化器类型和数据谱衰减的联合函数，涵盖5种预条件器变体和6种谱条件
- 提出机制性解释：预条件优化器通过均衡各模式学习率，使给定模型规模N下更多谱模式被有效学习，从而提高alpha
- 在自然语言典型谱条件(s~1.0)下，完全自然梯度达到alpha~0.31，而梯度下降仅alpha~0.12，提升2.6倍
- 提供可证伪预测：在平坦谱数据(s<0.3)上，预条件优化器的缩放优势应可忽略不计；通过谱衰减诊断可预测预条件优化器的收益
- 发现Sign-GD虽为"自适应"方法但不改善alpha，证明关键机制是谱均衡而非一般性自适应

## Key Contributions

- First systematic measurement of the scaling exponent alpha as a joint function of optimizer type and data spectral decay, across 5 preconditioner variants and 6 spectral conditions (s = 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0)
- Mechanistic explanation via Proposition 2.1 (informal spectral heuristic): preconditioned optimizers equalize per-mode learning rates, enabling more spectral modes to be well-learned at given N, yielding larger alpha
- Consistent optimizer ordering: alpha_FullNG ~ alpha_MatSign > alpha_Diag > alpha_Sign ~ alpha_GD, with Full NG and Matrix-Sign achieving nearly identical exponents (0.314 vs 0.308 at s=1.0)
- At s~1.0 (natural language), full natural gradient achieves alpha~0.31 vs. alpha~0.12 for GD -- a 2.6x larger exponent that compounds with each model-size doubling
- The alpha-shift grows with spectral steepness: delta-alpha = 0.055 at s=0.25, 0.195 at s=1.0, 0.214 at s=1.5, 0.191 at s=2.0
- Falsifiable prediction: on flat-spectrum data (s < 0.3), preconditioned optimizers should provide negligible scaling improvement
- Sign-GD serves as a negative control: it is "adaptive" but provides no spectral equalization, and correspondingly does not improve alpha
- Provides a practical spectral diagnostic: measure spectral decay s from embedding covariance eigenvalues to predict optimizer ROI

## Methodology

The authors work within the random-feature regression framework (Maloney et al., 2022; Bordelon et al., 2024), which is the canonical solvable model for neural scaling laws. The setup is as follows:

**Data distribution:** Inputs x in R^D drawn from N(0, Sigma) with eigenvalues lambda_i = i^{-(1+s)}, where s > 0 is the spectral exponent. Natural language embeddings typically exhibit s ~ 0.8-1.2.

**Teacher-student framework:** A teacher function uses K*=100 random ReLU features with source exponent b=1.0. The student model uses N random features (trainable top-layer weights only), making the top-layer problem a linear regression where different optimizers are implemented purely as preconditioners without confounding from feature learning.

**Five optimizer variants implemented as preconditioners:**
1. GD (baseline): P = I
2. Diagonal (AdamW proxy): P = diag(F^T F)^{-1/2}
3. Full NG (Shampoo/K-FAC proxy): P = (F^T F)^{-1}, equivalent to solving the normal equations
4. Sign-GD: a <- a - eta * sign(grad L)
5. Matrix-Sign (Muon proxy): P = (F^T F)^{-1/2}, analogous to Muon's Newton-Schulz orthogonalization

**Experimental protocol:** D=1000, eight model sizes (N=25 to 5000), six spectral conditions (s=0.25 to 2.0), 10 random seeds per configuration, T=2000 gradient steps. Full NG uses a direct solve (one-step Newton via Cholesky with Tikhonov regularization). Scaling exponents fit by OLS on (log N, log L) for N >= 200. Total: 2,400 main runs + 180 robustness runs.

**Spectral intuition (Proposition 2.1):** Under GD with power-law data, learning proceeds mode-by-mode from highest variance downward. Preconditioning equalizes convergence rates, rescuing "underserved" low-variance modes. The gap delta-alpha grows with spectral steepness s because steeper spectra create more wasted capacity under GD, and delta-alpha -> 0 as s -> 0 (flat spectrum) because GD already treats modes equally.

## Results & Highlights

- **Core phenomenon (Figure 1):** At s=1.0, scaling curves fan out clearly -- preconditioned optimizers achieve lower loss at every N with visibly steeper slopes. At s=0.5, curves are nearly parallel, confirming spectral dependence.
- **Alpha-shift quantification (Figure 2, Table 1):** Full NG and Matrix-Sign maintain alpha > 0.2 even at s=2.0, while GD collapses to alpha ~ 0.01. The gap delta-alpha peaks near s=1.5 (0.214) and remains large at s=2.0 (0.191).
- **Power-law fit quality:** R^2 > 0.95 for all (optimizer, s) pairs except Sign-GD at large s (R^2 < 0.5), confirming clean power-law behavior for preconditioned optimizers.
- **Effective compute multiplier (Figure 3):** Translates alpha-shift into parameter-equivalent advantage. At s~1.0, strong preconditioners show substantial multipliers; shown as qualitative comparison to the ~1.4x Muon speedup reported by Chen et al. (2025).
- **Robustness checks (Appendix F):** Results replicate at D=5000 (5x higher dimension) and b=2.0 (steeper teacher coefficient decay), with the same qualitative optimizer ordering preserved.
- **Convergence analysis (Appendix G):** Part of the measured advantage is a finite-training-budget effect (GD convergence gap is ~86% at s=1.0, N=5000). However, Full NG (direct solve, infinite steps) achieves essentially the same alpha as Matrix-Sign (2000 iterative steps), confirming that strong spectral preconditioning -- not just convergence speed -- drives the ordering.
- **Sign-GD as negative control (Appendix H):** Confirms that the key mechanism is spectral equalization, not generic adaptivity. Sign-GD destroys gradient magnitude information, producing noisy trajectories and no alpha improvement.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-good. The insight that preconditioning improves scaling exponents is intuitive from optimization theory, but the systematic quantification across spectral conditions, the mechanism via Proposition 2.1, and the spectral diagnostic are genuine contributions. The Sign-GD negative control is a clean experimental design choice.
- **Rigor:** The experimental design is methodologically sound -- controlled comparisons, shared data realizations across optimizers, 10 seeds per configuration, robustness checks. The informal proposition is well-supported by the per-mode convergence-rate derivation (Appendix D) though not a formal theorem. The honest discussion of finite-budget effects and convergence gaps strengthens credibility.
- **Reproducibility:** Very high. The entire experiment suite runs on CPU, costs under $20, and the authors commit to releasing code. The protocol is described in sufficient detail (Algorithm 1).
- **Significance:** The paper highlights an important conceptual point -- that alpha is not fixed -- but the practical impact is tempered by the random-feature limitation. The gap between this framework and real LLM training is substantial (no feature learning, no depth, no attention). The authors are commendably transparent about this, devoting Appendix J to discussing scale-dependent decay.
- **Red flags:** (1) Workshop paper, not main conference -- the claims may be preliminary. (2) The title "Neural Scaling Laws" is broader than the evidence supports; the results are specifically about random-feature regression scaling. (3) The alpha values in the random-feature model (0.01-0.35) differ significantly from real LLM scaling (alpha ~ 0.05-0.1), limiting direct quantitative transfer. (4) The Wen et al. (2025) finding that Muon advantages attenuate at 1.2B parameters is a serious challenge to the practical relevance.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally clean experimental design with controlled comparisons and negative control (Sign-GD)
- Clear mechanistic story: spectral equalization, not generic adaptivity, drives the alpha improvement
- Honest and thorough discussion of limitations, especially the scale-dependent decay (Appendix J)
- Provides actionable practical guidance (spectral diagnostic) and a falsifiable prediction
- Comprehensive appendices covering robustness, convergence verification, and a proposed real-model validation protocol
- Low compute cost and high reproducibility

## Limitations

- Random-feature model operates in the "lazy" regime, omitting feature learning, depth, attention, and tokenization -- all central to real LLM training
- Width-only scaling; depth scaling involves qualitatively different phenomena not captured here
- Source exponent b fixed at 1.0 in main experiments (b=2.0 in appendix only)
- Muon proxy captures spectral equalization but omits momentum and normalization
- Recent empirical evidence (Wen et al., 2025) suggests the optimizer advantage attenuates at scale (from ~1.4x at 100M to ~1.1x at 1.2B), which the current framework cannot explain
- The proposed real-model validation (GPT-2 scale with Muon vs AdamW on standard vs spectrum-flattened data) exceeds the workshop budget and has not been conducted
- Absolute alpha values differ from real LLM scaling; only relative comparisons (optimizer ordering) are claimed as robust predictions

## Relevance

本研究对关注大模型缩放律和训练效率的研究者具有直接参考价值。论文在理论上证明了优化器选择可改变缩放指数alpha，并提供了一个基于谱衰减的诊断工具。对于从事LLM训练优化（尤其是Muon、Shampoo等预条件优化器研究）的研究者，这项工作提供了一个清晰的理论框架来理解何时预条件优化器能带来缩放优势，以及为什么这种优势可能在更大规模时衰减。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can a closed-form derivation of alpha(s, b, P) be obtained via resolvent-trace analysis in the proportional-asymptotics limit?
- How does feature learning in the "rich" regime interact with preconditioning? Does it amplify or dampen the alpha-shift?
- Can the spectral diagnostic be applied to real-world training data to predict Muon vs. AdamW ROI before committing compute?
- What is the mechanism behind the scale-dependent decay of the optimizer advantage (Wen et al., 2025)? Does feature learning increasingly substitute for explicit preconditioning at scale?
- How do the results change with depth scaling and residual connections in deep networks?
- Would the proposed GPT-2 validation protocol (standard vs. spectrum-flattened data with Muon/AdamW) confirm the falsifiable prediction?
