Aligned but Fragile: Robustifying LLM Safety via Zeroth-Order Optimization

Standard first-order safety alignment creates sharp decision boundaries — W4A4 quantization alone triples the attack success rate on Llama-3-8B (10.67% → 25.67%). Ten steps of zeroth-order refinement, targeting just 4 layers, close this gap for 13.3% extra training time and one-third the peak memory.
Key Metric
10 ZO steps
13.3% extra training time
0.32x peak GPU memory
−7.3pp best ASR reduction
Why This Matters
4-bit quantization is mandatory for deploying LLMs at scale. If it silently erodes safety alignment, every production model is exposed. This work shows the erosion is real — and fixable cheaply.
Critical Edge
ASR improvements are 1–3 pp in most settings — consistent but modest. ZO refinement slightly raises unperturbed ASR in some cases (6.67% → 7.00%). Catastrophic degradation on Qwen2-7B under W4A4 remains unfixable. Only 7–8B models tested; scaling is unproven.
⚙ Methodology — Three-Stage Pipeline
Stage I
FO Safety Alignment
100-step SGD on CB-Safety (800 samples). Reduces HarmBench ASR from 10.67% to 6.67% on Llama-3-8B.
Stage II
Layer Selection
Score each layer: S(l) = Snoise(l) + λ · Squant(l). Pick top-m most fragile. Pruning scores (SNIP, WANDA) fail here.
Stage III
ZO Refinement
10-step zeroth-order gradient on selected layers only. No backprop; 8 perturbation samples per update.

Theorem 4.4 proves even a single ZO step strictly reduces the perturbation robustness gap Robρ(θ) at an FO stationary point, under smoothness and Lipschitz assumptions. The key insight: ZO optimizes the neighborhood-averaged objective fρ, which FO never explicitly controls.

Strengths

  • Novel optimizer-centric framing — no prior work treats the optimizer itself as a robustness lever for safety
  • Formal theory: convergence guarantee (Thm 4.2) + one-step improvement proof (Thm 4.4) with full appendices
  • Extremely efficient: 10 ZO steps, 13.3% overhead, 0.32x memory — no backprop needed
  • Principled layer selection showing pruning-based scores are inadequate for robustness
  • Comprehensive ablations: FO-only vs FO+ZO vs extended FO; multiple perturbation types and intensities
  • Directly addresses a real deployment concern — quantization fragility of safety

Limitations

  • Only 7–8B models tested; 70B+ scaling is entirely unverified
  • Absolute ASR gains are small — typically 1–3 pp improvement
  • Does not defend against adversarial attacks, jailbreaks, or malicious fine-tuning
  • Qwen2-7B under W4A4 shows catastrophic PPL (~3000+) and near-100% AdvBench ASR — unfixable
  • Slightly increases unperturbed ASR in some cases (6.67% → 7.00%)
  • No comparison with adversarial training or other robustness baselines
  • Single alignment dataset (CB-Safety, 800 samples); sensitivity unknown
❓ Open Questions
  1. How does ZO refinement compare to adversarial training or data augmentation — are they complementary or redundant?
  2. Can robustness-aware layer selection extend to finer granularity — module, neuron, or parameter level?
  3. Does the approach scale to 70B+ models where 4-bit quantization is a practical necessity?
  4. How does ZO refinement interact with Safe RLHF, constitutional AI, or representation engineering?
  5. What happens when ZO is applied after RLHF or DPO rather than simple SFT?
  6. Could Robρ serve as a diagnostic during alignment training to predict deployment fragility?
