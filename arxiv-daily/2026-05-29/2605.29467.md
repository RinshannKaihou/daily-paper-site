> **一句话总结：** 本文提出五种因子图基元（双线性因子softdot、指数链接、Gamma先验、高斯似然、等式节点），证明由这些基元组合的任意"正规因子图"均支持闭式变分消息传递（Q-conjugate VMP），并通过逐层组合从静态集成到输入依赖门控再到分叉路由，建立了闭式推断下的通用函数逼近，应用于时间序列集成预测时门控函数通过推断而非梯度学习获得，在五个基准数据集上提供了校准的不确定性量化。

> **TL;DR:** The paper identifies a five-letter factor-graph "alphabet" (bilinear softdot, exponential link, Gamma prior, Gaussian likelihood, equality node) that composes freely under mean-field factorization while preserving closed-form variational message passing. The only non-conjugate interface -- the exponential link -- remains tractable via the Gaussian MGF and Gamma sufficient statistics. Stacking these primitives yields routing architectures that encode arbitrary decision trees (universal approximation with closed-form inference). Applied to ensemble time-series forecasting across five benchmarks (ETTh1, ETTh2, Exchange Rate, Electricity, Traffic), the "Noisy Diagonal" variant achieves the best aggregate trade-off between MSE, NLL calibration, and parameter efficiency, outperforming softmax-based neural MoE baselines that often produce non-finite NLL.

## 核心贡献

- 识别出五种因子图基元（bilinear softdot f_z, exponential link f_exp, Gamma先验 f_G, 高斯似然 f_N, 等式节点 f_=），构成"字母表"，其任意组合在平均场分解下保持闭式变分消息传递
- 提出"正规因子图"（Proper Factor Graph）概念（Definition 1），并证明定理1：任何正规因子图在完全平均场分解下，所有VMP消息具有已知闭式形式（Gaussian或Gamma类型）
- 利用高斯矩母函数和Gamma族充分统计量处理指数链接处的非共轭接口，使消息传递保持tractable
- 展示三种深度的组合语法：Depth 0（静态集成，恢复Cochran 1937的逆方差加权）、Depth 1（输入依赖门控的Precision-Gated Experts）、Depth 2（分叉路由，可编码XOR和任意决策树），推论1证明通用函数逼近能力
- 应用于集成时间序列预测，构建贝叶斯混合专家模型，门控函数通过推断而非学习获得，在五个基准数据集上实现校准的不确定性量化

## Key Contributions

- Identifies a five-letter factor-graph alphabet: bilinear softdot (1a), exponential link (1b), Gamma prior (1c), Gaussian likelihood (1d), and equality node (1e). Any model composed from these "proper factor graphs" admits closed-form VMP under mean-field factorization (Theorem 1).
- Proves that VMP messages act as a "type system": Gaussian variables receive Gaussian messages, precision variables receive Gamma messages. The only non-conjugate interface (the exponential link) is handled via the Gaussian MGF producing log-normal distributions, and the Gamma sufficient statistics producing log-gamma distributions in the reverse direction.
- Demonstrates a "grammar" of compositional depth: Depth 0 recovers inverse-variance weighting (Cochran 1937); Depth 1 introduces the "precision word" for input-dependent gating (Precision-Gated Experts); Depth 2 adds split-branch routing that encodes arbitrary decision trees with piecewise-linear boundaries, establishing universal function approximation (Corollary 1).
- Applies the framework to ensemble time-series forecasting with 7 forecasters across 5 benchmarks. The Noisy Diagonal variant achieves the best aggregate trade-off between accuracy, calibration, and parameter efficiency (e.g., reducing parameters from 15,512 to 952 on Exchange Rate while achieving the best NLL).
- Implemented in GraphPPL.jl / RxInfer.jl, with the alphabet mapping directly to model specification primitives.

## Methodology

The framework is organized around a programming language analogy: an alphabet (Section 2), a grammar for composition (Section 3), and a runtime via Bethe free energy minimization (Section 4).

**Alphabet (Section 2):** Five factor-graph primitives are defined (Eq. 1): a bilinear "softdot" factor computing z = w^T * phi with Gaussian noise (precision tau); an exponential link gamma = exp(z) as a deterministic delta function; a Gamma prior on precisions; a Gaussian likelihood; and an equality node for variable sharing. Edge styles encode variable roles: solid for weights/observations (Gaussian-typed), dashed for precisions (Gamma-typed), dash-dotted for latent z.

**Grammar (Section 3):** Models compose at three depth levels. Depth 0 assembles a static ensemble with one Gamma prior per expert. Depth 1 replaces each Gamma prior with a "precision word" pi that maps (w, phi, tau) through the softdot and exponential link, making precision input-dependent (Precision-Gated Experts). Depth 2 introduces split-branch routing: two competing branches with router weights produce scores that feed into different experts, encoding XOR patterns and arbitrary decision trees.

**Runtime (Section 4):** Inference minimizes the Bethe free energy (BFE) via message passing. VMP messages follow the "line-type" argument: mean-field factorized nodes send typed messages (Gaussian on solid edges, Gamma on dashed edges). Two factors require belief propagation (BP) rather than VMP: the equality node (trivial -- preserves types) and the exponential link (non-trivial). For the exponential link, the BP forward message produces a log-normal distribution on gamma, while the reverse produces a log-gamma distribution on z. This is tractable because the incoming messages always have known forms (Gaussian on the z-side, Gamma on the gamma-side), thanks to the restricted alphabet. Definition 1 formalizes "proper factor graphs" and Theorem 1 guarantees all messages have closed form.

**Implementation:** Models are specified in GraphPPL.jl and executed via RxInfer.jl. The alphabet maps directly: NormalMeanPrecision -> f_N, GammaShapeRate -> f_G, softdot -> f_z, Log -> f_exp. Equality nodes are inserted automatically.

## Results & Highlights

- **Five forecasting benchmarks:** ETTh1, ETTh2 (Zhou et al., 2021), Exchange Rate, Electricity, Traffic, with horizons H in {96, 192, 336, 720}.
- **Seven forecasters combined:** five neural (CNN, DLinear, NLinear, LSTM) plus two constant (10th and 90th quantiles). Features from a VAE with 64-dim latent space.
- **Five model configurations evaluated:** Static (Depth 0), Dynamic, Dynamic Diagonal, Noisy, Noisy Diagonal (all Depth 1). Diagonal variants reduce parameters from O(d^2) to O(d) per expert.
- **Key findings:**
  - Static ensemble already provides a strong baseline, matching or surpassing any individual forecaster
  - Dynamic variants achieve best MSE across all ETTh1 horizons with substantially lower NLL
  - Noisy Diagonal is particularly strong on multivariate datasets: on Exchange Rate, achieves best NLL by a large margin while reducing parameters from 15,512 to 952
  - Softmax-based neural MoE baselines are competitive in MSE but unstable: NLL values are often non-finite, indicating poor calibration
  - Noisy Diagonal provides the best aggregate trade-off between accuracy, calibration, and parameter efficiency (verified via Pareto frontiers in Fig. 9)
- **Uncertainty quantification:** The posterior over routing scores carries calibrated uncertainty about expert selection. Small precision tau yields wide posteriors (model hedges across experts); large tau concentrates on a single branch. This uncertainty is a direct output of inference, not an add-on.
- **Code available** in a public repository, implemented in Julia using GraphPPL.jl / RxInfer.jl.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The identification of a finite compositional alphabet that preserves closed-form VI -- with the key insight that the exponential link's non-conjugacy remains tractable -- is a clean and original theoretical contribution. The connection to universal approximation via routed decision trees is rigorous and non-obvious. The programming-language analogy (alphabet/grammar/runtime) is a useful organizational device.
- **Rigor:** Strong. The paper provides formal definitions (proper factor graphs), theorems (closed-form messages), and corollaries (universal approximation). Proofs are deferred to appendices but the main argument is clear. The XOR trace (Table 1) is a convincing concrete demonstration. The experimental evaluation is thorough with multiple baselines and ablations.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code is available in a public repository. The GraphPPL.jl model specifications are provided verbatim in the appendix. Full experimental details, dataset statistics, and parameter counts are in the appendix (Tables 2, 3, 5).
- **Significance:** Significant for the probabilistic programming and Bayesian inference communities. The framework narrows the gap between deep learning's compositional modularity and factor graph inference. If the alphabet can be extended (e.g., to discrete latent variables), the impact could be substantial.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - The mean-field assumption is a fundamental restriction that limits the quality of approximate posteriors. The paper is transparent about this trade-off but does not empirically quantify the approximation gap.
  - The alphabet covers only Gaussian/Gamma families -- no discrete variables, no multi-modal posteriors. This is acknowledged but limits applicability.
  - The neural MoE baselines are relatively simple (single-layer and two-layer gating). Comparison with more sophisticated baselines (e.g., attention-based MoE, modern deep ensembles) would strengthen the evaluation.
  - The paper does not address computational scalability: how does message passing time grow with graph depth, number of experts, and data size?

## Strengths

- Elegant theoretical framework with clean formalization (proper factor graphs, type system for messages)
- The programming-language analogy (alphabet/grammar/runtime) makes the compositional philosophy intuitive
- Practical demonstration on real-world time-series forecasting benchmarks with meaningful baselines
- Strong calibration results: the Bayesian approach produces finite NLL where neural MoE fails
- Parameter efficiency: diagonal variants drastically reduce parameters while maintaining or improving performance
- Open-source implementation in Julia ecosystem (GraphPPL.jl, RxInfer.jl)
- Honest about limitations: explicitly states the trade-off between closed-form tractability and arbitrary differentiable operations

## Limitations

- Mean-field factorization limits posterior expressiveness; no explicit quantification of the approximation gap
- Restricted to Gaussian/Gamma distribution families; cannot handle discrete latent variables, multi-modal posteriors, or other exponential families
- The universal approximation result is constructive but requires sharp routing (tau -> infinity), which reduces to deterministic behavior and loses uncertainty quantification
- Neural MoE baselines are relatively simple; comparison with modern deep ensemble methods or attention-based models would be more informative
- Computational scalability not analyzed: message passing complexity as a function of graph depth, expert count, and data dimension
- The approach requires formulating models within the specific alphabet, which may not always be natural for practitioners
- Only tested on univariate/multivariate time-series; generalization to other domains (e.g., images, NLP) is untested

## Relevance

本文提出的闭式变分推断框架对概率建模和时间序列预测有直接参考价值。因子图的模块化组合思路（字母表+语法+运行时）以及Q-conjugate消息传递的闭式推断机制，对构建可解释、可校准的概率模型具有启发性，特别是在需要不确定性量化的集成预测场景中。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the computational cost of closed-form VMP scale with model depth and data size compared to gradient-based VI or MCMC?
- Can the alphabet be extended to include discrete latent variables (e.g., categorical routing) while preserving closed-form inference?
- What is the quantitative impact of the mean-field assumption on posterior quality -- can structured variational approximations be incorporated?
- How does the framework perform on domains beyond time-series (e.g., classification, reinforcement learning)?
- Can the precision-gated mechanism be combined with neural network feature extractors in an end-to-end differentiable pipeline?
- What is the relationship between this Q-conjugate framework and automatic conjugacy detection in systems like Stan or PyMC?
- How sensitive are the results to the choice of VAE feature extractor for generating the phi(x) representations?
