> **一句话总结：** 本文通过控制实验揭示了视觉语言模型后训练中推理能力提升远大于感知能力的"不对称优化"现象——SFT中源于CoT词元不平衡，RL中源于奖励耦合——并分别提出动态损失重加权和感知感知奖励来缓解，端到端性能提升最高达18.2%。

> **TL;DR:** This paper diagnoses why post-training (SFT and RL) improves reasoning much more than perception in vision-language models. For SFT, the asymmetry comes from token imbalance in chain-of-thought supervision (fewer perception tokens = weaker signal); dynamic loss reweighting boosts end-to-end performance by up to 18.2 points. For RL, outcome rewards correlate more with reasoning than perception; adding perception-aware rewards improves accuracy by up to 6.0 points, and even surrogate rewards yield 3.2-point gains. The paper provides a controlled diagnostic framework with synthetic tasks that disentangle perception from reasoning.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了一个可控的诊断框架，使用合成任务将感知与推理解耦，系统性地分析后训练中的感知-推理不对称现象
- 揭示了SFT中不对称优化的根本原因：思维链监督中的词元不平衡导致感知信号不足
- 揭示了RL中不对称优化的根本原因：结果奖励与推理的相关性远强于与感知的相关性
- 提出了动态损失重加权方法来缓解SFT中的词元不平衡，端到端性能提升最高达18.2%
- 提出了感知感知奖励来缓解RL中的奖励耦合问题，准确率提升最高达6.0，即使无标注的替代奖励也能带来3.2%的提升

## Key Contributions

- Introduce a controlled diagnostic framework with two synthetic tasks that disentangle perception from reasoning for systematic analysis of post-training asymmetries
- Identify the root cause of asymmetric optimization in SFT: token imbalance in chain-of-thought supervision where perception tokens are underrepresented
- Identify the root cause in RL: reward coupling where outcome rewards correlate more strongly with reasoning than perception
- Propose dynamic loss reweighting for SFT that mitigates token imbalance, boosting end-to-end performance by up to 18.2 points
- Propose perception-aware rewards for RL that alleviate reward coupling, improving accuracy by up to 6.0 points; even surrogate rewards without ground truth yield 3.2-point gains

## Methodology

The authors design a controlled diagnostic framework with two synthetic tasks that cleanly separate perception (e.g., identifying visual attributes) from reasoning (e.g., performing logical operations on those attributes). This allows precise measurement of how post-training affects each component independently and jointly.

For SFT analysis, they examine token-level loss contributions in chain-of-thought supervision, showing that perception-relevant tokens constitute a minority of the total token count, leading to weaker gradient signals for perception learning. They propose dynamically reweighting the loss to increase the contribution of perception tokens.

For RL analysis, they investigate how outcome-based rewards (e.g., final answer correctness) correlate with reasoning versus perception steps. They find stronger correlation with reasoning, meaning perception receives weaker learning signals. They introduce perception-aware auxiliary rewards and show that even approximate surrogate rewards (without ground-truth perception labels) provide useful learning signal.

## Results & Highlights

- **SFT reweighting** yields up to 18.2-point improvement in end-to-end performance by balancing perception and reasoning token contributions
- **RL perception-aware rewards** improve end-to-end accuracy by up to 6.0 points
- **Surrogate perception rewards** (no ground truth needed) still provide 3.2-point gains, suggesting practical applicability
- The perception-reasoning asymmetry is consistent across different model architectures and training paradigms
- The diagnostic framework with synthetic tasks enables clean attribution of performance changes to perception vs. reasoning

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core insight — that post-training asymmetrically benefits reasoning over perception — is intuitive once stated, but the systematic diagnosis with controlled synthetic tasks and the identification of distinct mechanisms for SFT vs. RL are valuable contributions. The reweighting and perception-aware reward interventions are relatively straightforward but well-motivated.
- **Rigor:** The use of synthetic tasks to disentangle perception from reasoning is a strength for diagnostic clarity. However, the gap between synthetic diagnostic tasks and real-world VLM applications is a concern. It remains unclear whether the proposed interventions transfer effectively to complex, naturalistic tasks.
- **Reproducibility:** The synthetic task framework should be highly reproducible. The paper would benefit from open-sourcing the diagnostic benchmark. The interventions (loss reweighting, reward shaping) are simple to implement.
- **Significance:** The perception-reasoning asymmetry is an important bottleneck for end-to-end visual reasoning. Concrete numbers (18.2, 6.0, 3.2 point gains) are meaningful. If the findings generalize beyond synthetic tasks, this could influence how VLM post-training is done.
- **Red flags:** The absolute magnitude of improvements should be contextualized against baseline performance levels. Synthetic tasks may overestimate real-world gains. The paper title mentions "vision-language model post-training" broadly — generalization to production-scale models (GPT-4V, Gemini) is not established.

## Strengths

- Clean experimental design with controlled synthetic tasks that isolate perception from reasoning
- Identifies distinct mechanisms for SFT and RL, rather than a one-size-fits-all explanation
- Practical, lightweight interventions (loss reweighting, surrogate rewards) that are easy to adopt
- Quantitative evidence with clear improvement numbers
- The surrogate reward result (3.2 points without ground truth) is practically useful

## Limitations

- Synthetic tasks may not reflect the complexity of real-world visual reasoning scenarios
- Unclear how findings scale to frontier models (the paper likely uses smaller open-source VLMs)
- The diagnostic framework measures perception and reasoning as separable modules, but in practice they are deeply intertwined
- The reweighting and reward interventions introduce hyperparameters that may require tuning per task

## Relevance

本文对视觉语言模型后训练中感知与推理能力的不对称优化进行了系统性诊断，对关注VLM训练方法、多模态推理能力提升的研究者具有直接的参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the proposed interventions (dynamic loss reweighting, perception-aware rewards) perform on established real-world VLM benchmarks like MMMU or MathVista?
- Can the diagnostic framework be extended to study asymmetries in other capability pairs (e.g., instruction following vs. factual knowledge)?
- What is the interaction between perception-reasoning balance and model scale — does the asymmetry worsen or improve with larger models?
- How sensitive are the results to the specific synthetic task design? Would different task decompositions yield different conclusions?
- Can the surrogate reward approach be combined with self-supervised or self-critique methods to further reduce the need for ground-truth perception labels?
