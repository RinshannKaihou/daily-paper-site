> **一句话总结：** SkillBrew将LLM Agent的技能库策展建模为带效用约束的多目标帕累托优化问题（实用性+多样性+覆盖率），通过Skill Distiller（从失败轨迹提取新技能）、Skill Diagnoser（基于反事实leave-one-out rollout的KEEP/REWRITE/REMOVE裁决）和Edit Planner（组合编辑方案）构成的双层提出-验证循环实现全局级别的技能库精炼，在ALFWorld和WebShop上分别达到59.0%和38.4%的成功率，显著优于所有基线。

> **TL;DR:** SkillBrew introduces a training-free, multi-objective skill bank curation framework for retrieval-augmented LLM agents. It formalizes bank curation as constrained Pareto optimization over three objectives (utility, diversity, coverage), solved via a bi-level propose-then-verify loop with counterfactual leave-one-out replay. On ALFWorld it achieves 59.0% average success rate (vs. next-best 49.3%) and on WebShop 38.4% success rate (vs. 28.8%), while transferring across five different worker LLMs including GPT-4o and Gemini-2.5-Pro with consistent gains.

## 核心贡献

- 首次将LLM Agent的技能库策展形式化为约束多目标优化问题：以实用性（utility）为硬约束，多样性（diversity，基于Gram矩阵行列式）和覆盖率（coverage，基于检索密度 x 使用率）为帕累托优化目标，反映"技能库必须先有用，再谈组织结构"的非对称性。
- 设计了三个协作模块：Skill Distiller从无检索失败轨迹中聚类提取候选技能；Skill Diagnoser通过反事实leave-one-out rollout对每个已有技能给出KEEP/REWRITE/REMOVE裁决；Edit Planner在银行级别组合编辑方案生成K个候选技能库。
- 提出双层提出-验证循环：内层在support集上提出候选编辑，外层在query集上通过帕累托感知选择器验证（lex-priority on utility + hypervolume contribution on diversity/coverage），防止过拟合。
- 引入内容寻址缓存（SHA-256 hash + 版本标签），实现60%以上的缓存命中率，显著降低反事实rollout的计算开销。
- 在ALFWorld（6个任务族）和WebShop两个公开基准上，SkillBrew大幅超越10个基线方法，并在5个不同规模的worker LLM上展示了一致的迁移增益。

## Key Contributions

- Formulates skill bank curation as constrained multi-objective optimization with utility as hard constraint and diversity/coverage as Pareto objectives, reflecting the asymmetry that a bank must first be useful before its organization matters.
- Introduces three cooperative modules: (1) Skill Distiller that clusters failure trajectories to extract candidate skills; (2) Skill Diagnoser that assigns KEEP/REWRITE/REMOVE verdicts via counterfactual leave-one-out rollouts; (3) Edit Planner that composes bank-level edit proposals into K candidate banks.
- Designs a bi-level propose-then-verify loop: inner loop proposes edits on support split, outer loop verifies on query split via Pareto-aware selector (lex-priority on utility, hypervolume contribution on diversity/coverage), preventing overfitting to edit-motivating trajectories.
- Achieves content-addressed replay cache with 60%+ hit rate from round 2 onwards, mitigating computational cost of counterfactual rollouts.
- Outperforms 10 baselines on ALFWorld (59.0% avg success vs. 49.3% next-best) and WebShop (38.4% success vs. 28.8% next-best), with consistent transfer across 5 worker LLMs including GPT-4o (88.1% on ALFWorld) and Gemini-2.5-Pro (89.6%).

## Methodology

SkillBrew is a training-free, iterative skill bank refinement framework operating on three levels:

**Problem Formulation.** Given a frozen worker LLM, a skill bank B, and a task distribution, the goal is to find B* = argmax_{J_div, J_cov}(B) subject to J_util(B) >= eta. Utility is measured via per-skill counterfactual advantage: Delta(tau, s) = r(tau|with s) - r(tau|without s), aggregated with frequency weights. Diversity uses the determinant of the Gram matrix of L2-normalized skill embeddings (geometric volume). Coverage combines retrieval density (fraction of top-k slots filled) with skill usage (fraction of bank retrieved at least once).

**Bi-level Propose-then-Verify Loop (10 rounds):**
1. **Inner loop (candidate generation on D_support):**
   - *Skill Distiller:* Two-stage prompting -- first analyzes failed no-retrieval trajectories to identify failure patterns, then distills candidate skills grounded in successful reference trajectories.
   - *Skill Diagnoser:* For each retrieved skill, runs counterfactual leave-one-out rollouts comparing WITH vs WITHOUT outcomes, grouped into four patterns (fail->success, success->fail, fail->fail, success->success), then issues KEEP/REWRITE/REMOVE verdicts with confidence levels.
   - *Edit Planner:* Reasons at bank level to compose K distinct coherent edit proposals (each a "lens" with its own editing philosophy), respecting hard constraints (KEEP skills are protected, edits must reference valid pools).

2. **Outer loop (Pareto-aware selection on D_query):**
   - Evaluates each candidate bank by rolling out the worker on D_query to estimate J_util, J_div, J_cov.
   - Pareto-aware selector uses lex-priority: candidates within tolerance epsilon=0.03 of best J_util enter a tie pool, broken by hypervolume contribution on J_div and J_cov.
   - The previous bank is included as a null candidate; if it wins, the bank carries forward unchanged.

**Retrieval:** Hybrid scorer combining BM25 (0.30) + dense cosine similarity via text-embedding-3-small (0.70), retrieving top-3 skills with a minimum score threshold of 0.30.

**Efficiency:** Content-addressed cache keyed by SHA-256(task_id, skill content, retrieval rank) with version tags for worker configuration changes, achieving 60%+ cache hit rate from round 2.

## Results & Highlights

**Main Results (Table 1, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct worker):**
- ALFWorld: SkillBrew achieves 59.0% avg success rate, surpassing all 10 baselines. Strongest skill-based competitors: Skill-Pro (49.3%), Voyager (47.0%), ExpeL (46.3%). Per-task: Look 72.2%, Clean 58.1%, Heat 60.9%, Cool 57.1%, Pick2 29.4%.
- WebShop: SkillBrew achieves Score 59.3 and Success Rate 38.4%, vs. best baseline Reflexion at 58.1/28.8.

**Cross-Worker Generalization (Table 2):**
- Consistent improvements across 5 workers: Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct (59.0%), Qwen2-4B-Instruct-2507 (60.4%), Qwen3-10B-A3B-Instruct-2507 (75.4%), GPT-4o (88.1%), Gemini-2.5-Pro (89.6%).
- Gains are especially pronounced on stronger workers: GPT-4o jumps from 46.4% (ReAct) to 88.1% (+41.7), Gemini-2.5-Pro from 61.8% to 89.6% (+27.8).
- WebShop also improves: GPT-4o goes from 27.2% to 65.0% success, Gemini-2.5-Pro from 38.1% to 72.0%.

**Key Insight:** SkillBrew's curated banks transfer effectively to different worker LLMs without re-curation, suggesting the skills capture task-level procedural knowledge rather than worker-specific quirks.

## Authors & Credibility

Nine authors spanning institutions with expertise in LLM agents, optimization, and information retrieval. The work uses GPT-5.4 for the curation modules (Distiller, Diagnoser, Edit Planner) and evaluates on well-established benchmarks (ALFWorld, WebShop). The paper provides full prompt templates in the appendix and details implementation specifics (support/query splits, retrieval parameters, round budgets). The authors are transparent about the frozen-worker setting and do not compare against training-based methods like SkillRL, which would change the underlying agent.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The multi-objective formulation for skill bank curation is genuinely novel. Prior work (Voyager, ExpeL, EvoSkill, Skill-Pro) uses single-scalar criteria or append-only strategies. The bi-level propose-then-verify loop with Pareto-aware selection is a meaningful methodological advance.
- **Rigor:** Strong experimental design with 10 baselines, two benchmarks, five worker LLMs, and ablation studies. The support/query/test split prevents information leakage. The counterfactual leave-one-out design for credit attribution is principled.
- **Reproducibility:** Full prompts provided in appendix. Uses public benchmarks. However, depends on GPT-5.4 (proprietary, paid API) for curation modules, which introduces cost and access barriers. The exact hyperparameters (K candidates, epsilon=0.03, 10 rounds) are specified.
- **Significance:** The paradigm shift from append-only to principled curation is important for the self-improving agent community. The transferability across workers is a strong practical result.
- **Red flags:** (1) The method relies heavily on LLM-based modules (Distiller, Diagnoser, Planner) all powered by GPT-5.4, making it expensive and dependent on a specific proprietary model. (2) Evaluation is limited to text-based benchmarks (ALFWorld, WebShop); real-world or multimodal agent scenarios are untested. (3) The frozen-worker assumption is a significant limitation -- joint agent-bank optimization is left to future work. (4) Scalability concerns: even with caching, 10 rounds of rollouts across support/query splits with leave-one-out counterfactuals is computationally intensive.

## Strengths

- Principled formulation: the utility-constraint-first, then Pareto-optimize-diversity/coverage design correctly reflects that usefulness is non-negotiable.
- The counterfactual leave-one-out approach to credit assignment is a clean solution to the confounded attribution problem.
- Edit Planner reasoning at bank level (rather than mechanically applying individual edits) enables coherent, strategic bank evolution.
- Strong cross-worker transfer demonstrates that curated skills capture generalizable procedural knowledge.
- Comprehensive appendix with full prompts, skill examples, and implementation details.

## Limitations

- Computationally expensive: each round requires rollouts on both support and query sets plus counterfactual leave-one-out replay, even with caching.
- Depends on a powerful LLM (GPT-5.4) for curation modules; the quality of Distiller/Diagnoser/Planner outputs likely degrades with weaker models.
- Only evaluated on text-based benchmarks (ALFWorld, WebShop); unclear how it generalizes to multimodal, open-world, or code-generation agents.
- The frozen-worker assumption means the agent cannot co-adapt with the evolving bank, limiting the potential for mutual improvement.
- Assumes relatively static task distributions; continually shifting environments remain unaddressed.

## Relevance

本文对LLM Agent的技能/知识管理提出了形式化的多目标优化框架，其帕累托感知策展思路和反事实归因方法对构建高效、可持续演进的Agent系统具有直接参考价值，尤其适用于需要长期技能积累的复杂任务场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does SkillBrew perform when the curation modules (Distiller, Diagnoser, Planner) use a weaker or open-source LLM instead of GPT-5.4?
- What is the marginal cost-benefit tradeoff beyond 10 rounds? Is there diminishing returns, and can an early-stopping criterion be derived?
- Can the multi-objective curation framework be extended to jointly optimize the worker agent and skill bank (co-adaptation)?
- How does the framework handle open-world task distributions where new task types emerge continuously?
- What is the sensitivity of the Pareto-aware selector to the utility tolerance epsilon=0.03 and the hybrid retrieval weights (0.30 BM25, 0.70 dense)?
- Can SkillBrew be applied to non-textual skill representations (e.g., executable code skills as in Voyager)?
