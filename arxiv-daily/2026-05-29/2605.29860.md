> **一句话总结：** ESPO通过在PPO rollout中利用已计算logits构建代理遗憾值进行实时失败检测并提前截断轨迹，将截断视为吸收失败状态以集中负TD误差，在DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B数学推理上超越PPO和DAPO，同时节省22%以上的rollout token。

> **TL;DR:** ESPO augments PPO with an on-the-fly failure detection mechanism that computes per-step surrogate regret from logits already available during sampling, accumulates it via EMA, and terminates rollouts when cumulative regret exceeds a value-gated threshold. It treats truncations as absorbing failure states with a terminal penalty, concentrating negative TD errors at the failure point. On DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B for math reasoning, it beats both PPO and DAPO on nearly all benchmarks (avg acc 73.17% vs 71.20% PPO / 71.76% DAPO) while saving 22% rollout tokens. No extra reward model or human annotation needed.

## 核心贡献

- 提出ESPO（Early-Stopping Proximal Policy Optimization），在PPO rollout生成过程中实时检测推理失败并提前终止轨迹，解决"rollout continuation problem"
- 设计了基于策略logits的逐步代理遗憾值（surrogate regret）：通过logit gap衡量策略实际采样动作与贪婪动作的偏离程度，利用batch-level统计量进行归一化，通过EMA累积
- 提出值函数门控的终止准则：当累积遗憾值z_t超过 beta * max(V_phi(s_t), epsilon)时触发截断，高预测回报状态获得更大容忍度
- 将截断轨迹转化为吸收失败状态，赋予终端失败惩罚r_fail，使负TD误差集中在失败步骤，通过GAE反向传播实现精确的信用分配
- 在1.5B和7B两个规模上均一致超越PPO和DAPO，同时节省22%以上的cumulative rollout tokens

## Key Contributions

- Identifies and addresses the "rollout continuation problem" in LLM RL training: after a wrong reasoning step, standard algorithms waste compute on post-failure tokens that pollute advantage estimates
- Introduces per-step surrogate regret computed from the logit gap between greedy and sampled actions, normalized via frozen batch-level statistics and accumulated via EMA into a cumulative deviation signal z_t
- Proposes a value-gated stopping criterion: termination triggers when z_t > beta * max(V_phi(s_t), epsilon), granting higher tolerance to states with higher predicted future return
- Formulates truncation as an absorbing failure state with terminal penalty r_fail, producing concentrated negative TD errors at the detected failure point rather than diluting signal across post-failure tokens
- Includes critic warmup safeguard: disables stopping until the critic stabilizes, then anneals beta conservatively, preventing spurious early truncations from an uncalibrated value function
- Demonstrates consistent improvements over both PPO and DAPO at 1.5B and 7B scales while saving 22%+ rollout tokens, without any additional reward model or annotation

## Methodology

ESPO modifies the standard PPO rollout collection procedure. At each generation step during rollout, the method computes a surrogate regret signal from the logits already produced during token sampling (no additional forward passes). Specifically:

1. **Per-step surrogate regret:** For each sampled action a_t, compute g_t = max(log pi(a_greedy | s_t) - log pi(a_t | s_t), 0), measuring how far the sampled action deviates from the greedy action. This is normalized using batch-level statistics (mu_delta, sigma_delta) and clipped: g_tilde = clip((g_t - mu) / sqrt(sigma^2 + delta), -c, c).

2. **EMA accumulation:** The normalized signal is smoothed via exponential moving average: z_t = alpha * z_{t-1} + (1 - alpha) * g_tilde. Batch-level statistics are frozen during generation to ensure causal correctness.

3. **Value-gated termination:** At step t, if warmup is complete and z_t > beta * max(V_phi(s_t), epsilon), the rollout is terminated. The threshold multiplier beta is adjusted by a proportional controller to maintain a target stop rate, and annealed from a conservative upper bound after warmup.

4. **Absorbing failure state:** When termination fires at step T_stop, a terminal failure reward r_fail is assigned. No further decoding occurs. The truncated trajectory's advantages are computed by standard GAE, producing a concentrated negative TD error at the termination point that propagates backward through PPO updates.

5. **Critic warmup:** The stopping rule is disabled during an adaptive warmup period until the critic exhibits stable learning dynamics, preventing aggressive truncation of valid exploration from an uncalibrated value baseline.

The training objective remains standard PPO; ESPO only changes the sampled trajectory data, not the policy update algebra.

**Experimental setup:** Evaluated on DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B and DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B, trained on DAPO-Math-17k. Benchmarks: AIME 2024, AMC 2023, MATH-500. Baselines: Base Model, PPO, DAPO. All methods use identical data, reward, sampling, and evaluation settings. Implemented via verl framework. 8 rollouts per prompt, global batch size 64, evaluated with 32 repetitions (Pass@1 averaged). Hardware: 8x H20 GPUs.

## Results & Highlights

**7B Scale (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B):**

| Method | AMC23 | AIME24 | MATH500 | Avg Acc | Cumulative Tokens(M) | Avg Tokens |
|--------|-------|--------|---------|---------|----------------------|------------|
| Base Model | 78.64 | 40.13 | 83.36 | 62.04 | - | 5357 |
| PPO | 82.94 | 45.25 | 85.43 | 71.20 | 1072.40 | 4189 |
| DAPO | 83.76 | 45.57 | 85.95 | 71.76 | 1035.01 | 4043 |
| ESPO | **85.83** | **46.28** | **87.42** | **73.17** | **839.24** | **3278** |

ESPO saves ~22% cumulative tokens vs PPO, ~19% vs DAPO. Average accuracy +1.97pp over PPO, +1.41pp over DAPO.

**1.5B Scale (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B):**

| Method | AMC23 | AIME24 | MATH500 | Avg Acc | Cumulative Tokens(M) | Avg Tokens |
|--------|-------|--------|---------|---------|----------------------|------------|
| PPO | 68.43 | 23.02 | 79.65 | 57.03 | 1069.66 | 4178 |
| DAPO | 70.23 | 24.37 | 80.28 | 58.29 | 1223.96 | 4781 |
| ESPO | **71.87** | 23.87 | **81.53** | **59.09** | **927.96** | **3625** |

ESPO uses 13% fewer tokens than PPO and 24% fewer than DAPO, with highest average accuracy.

**Ablation highlights (7B, AIME24):**
- Full ESPO: 46.3% -- removing warmup drops to 44.2%, removing terminal failure penalty drops to 43.7%
- Value-only stopping (no regret): 44.0% -- value-gated stopping alone insufficient
- Regret-only stopping (no value gate): 44.8% -- lacks recovery allowance
- Random stopping (matched rate): 42.4% -- confirms improvement comes from *where* truncation occurs, not just shorter sequences

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-good. Early stopping in RL is well-studied, but the specific formulation of surrogate regret from logit gaps combined with value-gated termination for LLM reasoning is a clean, practical contribution. The absorbing failure state formulation is principled and avoids pitfalls of per-step reward shaping.
- **Rigor:** Good. Two model scales (1.5B and 7B), three benchmarks, comparison against two strong baselines (PPO, DAPO), and thorough 6-variant ablation study. Evaluation uses 32 repetitions for stability. All methods share identical training settings. However, no error bars or statistical significance tests are reported, and only a single model family (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen) is tested.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Algorithm 1 provides pseudocode. The method requires no additional models or annotations. Key hyperparameters (alpha, beta, epsilon, r_fail, warmup) are described, with details in appendices. Implemented on the open-source verl framework.
- **Significance:** Practically significant. The >20% compute savings compounds meaningfully at scale. The performance improvements over DAPO (not just PPO) strengthen the claim. The method is complementary to advantage estimation improvements and can be combined with other techniques.
- **Red flags:** (1) No comparison against GRPO directly, only DAPO (which builds on GRPO). (2) No error bars or variance reporting despite 32 evaluation repetitions. (3) The AIME24 score at 1.5B scale is slightly below DAPO (23.87 vs 24.37), suggesting ESPO may not always help on harder tasks at smaller scales. (4) False positive rate analysis is mentioned but detailed analysis is brief. (5) The paper acknowledges inherent objective bias from false positives but relies on warmup as the primary safeguard.

## Strengths

- Zero additional computational cost during rollout -- regret computed from logits already available
- No extra reward model, human annotation, or process supervision needed
- Consistent improvements over both PPO and DAPO across nearly all benchmarks at both scales
- Significant compute savings (22% at 7B, 13-24% at 1.5B) that compound over training
- Thorough ablation study with 6 variants isolating each component's contribution
- Principled theoretical motivation: absorbing failure states avoid non-stationary reward bias
- Clean integration into existing PPO pipelines -- only changes trajectory collection, not the update rule
- Value-gated stopping with proportional controller for adaptive stop rate management

## Limitations

- Limited to mathematical reasoning tasks; generalizability to code generation, logical reasoning, or other domains is untested
- Only tested on DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen model family at 1.5B and 7B scales
- No error bars or statistical significance testing reported
- Hyperparameter sensitivity (alpha, beta, c, r_fail, warmup schedule) not systematically analyzed
- The false positive rate (incorrectly truncating correct trajectories) could be problematic in domains where recovery from uncertainty is common
- The critic warmup heuristic introduces a tuning dimension; failure to properly warmup could hurt performance significantly (2.1 point drop in ablation)
- No comparison against GRPO directly, or against process reward model methods
- The method assumes outcome-based rewards; compatibility with dense/process rewards is unexplored

## Relevance

该论文与LLM强化学习推理训练高度相关，提出的基于代理遗憾值的提前终止机制可直接集成到现有PPO训练流程中，在提升数学推理性能的同时显著降低训练成本。对于关注RL训练效率、推理能力优化和大规模LLM训练工程的研究者具有直接的参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does ESPO interact with dense/process reward models? Could surrogate regret be combined with step-level reward signals?
- What is the false positive rate dynamics over training -- does it decrease as the policy improves?
- Does ESPO scale to larger models (32B, 70B+) and other model families (Llama, Mistral)?
- Can the surrogate regret signal be used as an auxiliary training signal (e.g., for learning when to backtrack)?
- How does ESPO perform on non-math reasoning tasks like code generation, where recovery from early mistakes may be more common?
- What is the sensitivity of the proportional controller for beta -- how sensitive is performance to the target stop rate tau?
- Could the method be extended to inference-time early stopping for test-time compute scaling?
