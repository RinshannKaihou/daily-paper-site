> **一句话总结：** LFQ通过在最终Transformer块量化时使用交叉熵对齐FP模型与量化模型的logit分布（替代传统MSE目标），并显式纳入LM head，显著改善了低比特量化LLM在长文本生成和推理任务上的质量退化，在AIME等基准上接近FP基线性能。

> **TL;DR:** Block-wise PTQ preserves perplexity and MMLU but degrades generation quality, especially for long chain-of-thought reasoning. The authors identify two root causes: (1) the LM head is excluded from block-wise optimization, and (2) MSE loss does not preserve token-level probability rankings. LFQ replaces the MSE objective with cross-entropy loss at the logit level for the final Transformer block only, ensuring the quantized model's token distribution matches the FP model. Applied on top of FlexRound, OmniQuant, and Block-AP, LFQ consistently improves generation accuracy (e.g., AIME'24 greedy: 30->43 for L1-Qwen-7B-Max at W4) while maintaining parity on WikiText2 and MMLU. Published at ICML 2026.

## 核心贡献

- 首次系统揭示block-wise PTQ在生成任务上退化的两个根本原因：(i) 忽略LM head（unembedding层）导致量化后的token预测与FP模型不一致；(ii) MSE目标函数不能保证token概率排序的正确性——MSE更低反而可能预测出相反的token
- 提出LFQ方法：仅对最终Transformer块量化时使用logit级别的交叉熵损失替代MSE，理论依据是交叉熵等价于最小化KL散度，当且仅当两个分布相同时为零
- 方法设计简洁：仅修改最后一个块的优化目标，不改变量化方案本身，因此与现有打包/解包内核完全兼容，且内存高效（单GPU可运行）
- 在instruction-tuned模型（Qwen2.5-7B/14B）、reasoning模型（L1-Qwen-7B-Max, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B）和MoE模型（Qwen3-30B-A3B）上全面验证

## Key Contributions

- First to formally analyze why block-wise PTQ degrades generation quality: (1) the LM head is omitted from block-wise optimization so quantization errors propagate through an uncalibrated projection, and (2) MSE is misaligned with preserving token prediction rankings -- a concrete 2-token example shows lower MSE can yield the opposite top-1 token
- Proposes LFQ: quantizes only the final Transformer block by minimizing cross-entropy between FP and quantized logits (softmax over vocabulary), while all prior blocks continue using MSE reconstruction
- Method is agnostic to the underlying block-wise PTQ method -- demonstrated with FlexRound, OmniQuant, and Block-AP -- and adds no inference overhead since only the optimization objective changes
- Comprehensive evaluation across 7 models from 3 families (Qwen2.5, Llama 3.x, DeepSeek-R1) including dense, reasoning, and MoE architectures on 6 benchmarks (IFEval, GSM8K, MATH500, AIME'24, AIME'25, WikiText2, MMLU)

## Methodology

**Core insight:** Block-wise PTQ minimizes MSE between block outputs, but the LM head maps these to a vocabulary-sized logit space where small MSE differences can flip the argmax token. The authors provide a clean 2-token running example: when FP logits are [0.6, 0.4] and quantized logits are [0.4, 0.6], MSE is low (0.08) but the predicted token is wrong; whereas [0.9, 0.1] -> [0.6, 0.4] has higher MSE (0.18) but preserves the correct top-1.

**LFQ objective (Eq. 3):** For the final Transformer block, minimize:
```
L_CE(X W_FP W_Head, X W_q W_Head) = -(1/L) sum_{i,j} sigma(X W_FP W_Head)_{i,j} * log(sigma(X W_q W_Head)_{i,j})
```
where sigma is softmax over the vocabulary. This is applied on top of the quantization parameterization of the underlying method (FlexRound, OmniQuant, or Block-AP). All other blocks continue using standard MSE reconstruction.

**Key design choices:**
- Only the final block uses LFQ; ablations show applying LFQ to more blocks yields marginal gains on generation but hurts MMLU
- Calibration: sequential samples from C4 training set (400-800 samples depending on model size), sequence length 2048 (or 4096 for Qwen3-30B)
- Learning rate sweep per method: {5e-4, 1e-3} for FlexRound; {1.5e-3, 2e-3, 5e-3} for OmniQuant; {1e-5, 2e-5, 3e-5} for Block-AP

## Results & Highlights

**Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct (Table 1):**
- FlexRound+LFQ at W4: IFEval 69.50->71.35 (+1.85), MATH500 72.6->73.4 (+0.8), while WikiText2 stays at 7.23 and MMLU at 72.48
- At W3g128: IFEval 66.54->67.84 (+1.3), MATH500 65.6->68.0 (+2.4)

**Reasoning models (Table 2):**
- L1-Qwen-7B-Max at W4: AIME'24 greedy 30.00->43.33 (+13.3 pp), pass@8 51.71->55.09; MATH500 avg@8 87.45->88.40
- DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Llama-8B at W4: AIME'24 greedy 16.67->26.67 (+10.0 pp), pass@8 27.71->30.07
- At W3g128: L1-Qwen-7B-Max AIME'24 greedy 23.33->30.00, pass@8 41.85->45.18

**MoE model (Table 3):**
- Qwen3-30B-A3B at W4g128: AIME'25 greedy 50.00->60.00 (+10 pp), pass@1 55.63->58.75, IFEval 82.44->82.99

**Ablation (Table 4):**
- Adding LM head alone improves generation; adding cross-entropy on top of LM head gives further gains
- Applying LFQ to more than the final block does not help meaningfully (Figure 2, Table 9)

**LFQ vs RILQ (Table 5):**
- RILQ better on WikiText2/MMLU (due to LoRA adapters), but LFQ consistently better on IFEval and GSM8K
- LFQ+RILQ combines strengths of both (Table 10)

**Robustness:**
- Works with both C4 and WikiText2 as calibration data (Table 12)
- Consistent across learning rate choices (Table 11)
- Effective under both greedy and stochastic decoding (Table 6)

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** The insight that MSE is fundamentally misaligned with token prediction preservation is elegantly demonstrated with a minimal running example. The solution -- cross-entropy on logits for the final block -- is obvious in hindsight but had not been articulated or validated before. This is a genuinely useful contribution to the PTQ literature.

**Rigor (High):** The paper provides thorough ablations: (i) LM head alone vs. LM head + cross-entropy, (ii) number of blocks with LFQ (k=1,2,3), (iii) which block gets LFQ (last vs. earlier), (iv) comparison with RILQ and their combination, (v) calibration dataset sensitivity, (vi) learning rate sensitivity. The running example in Section 2 cleanly motivates the approach mathematically.

**Reproducibility (High):** The method is a simple modification to existing block-wise PTQ pipelines. Hyperparameters (learning rates, calibration samples, sequence lengths) are fully specified in Appendix E. The paper uses standard open-source models (Qwen2.5, Llama 3.x, DeepSeek-R1-Distill).

**Significance (High):** The gains on AIME (10-13 pp on greedy decoding for reasoning models) are substantial and directly relevant to the emerging trend of long chain-of-thought reasoning where quantization degradation is most severe. The method is practical: no inference overhead, single-GPU quantization, compatible with existing kernels.

**Potential concerns:**
- The method is specifically designed for the final block; whether the approach generalizes to intermediate blocks (where MSE may be less harmful for generation) is not fully explored beyond the k=2,3 ablation
- The evaluation focuses on weight-only quantization at W4 and W3; performance at 2-bit or weight+activation quantization is not tested
- The gains on standard instruction-following (IFEval) are modest (1-2 pp) compared to the dramatic improvements on AIME, which is a very small benchmark (30 questions)
- The paper does not report quantization time overhead of LFQ vs. standard block-wise PTQ, though it claims memory efficiency

## Strengths

- Elegant, well-motivated idea with clear theoretical justification (cross-entropy = KL divergence minimization)
- Comprehensive evaluation across diverse model families (dense, reasoning, MoE) and multiple PTQ backbones (FlexRound, OmniQuant, Block-AP)
- Zero inference overhead -- only the quantization-time objective changes
- Thorough ablation studies that validate each design choice
- Strong results on reasoning benchmarks precisely where quantization hurts most

## Limitations

- Only evaluated on weight-only quantization (W4 and W3); no results for weight+activation quantization or extreme low-bit (2-bit)
- AIME has only 30 problems per year, so point improvements (e.g., 30->43) should be interpreted with caution despite being consistent across settings
- The method targets only the final Transformer block; earlier blocks still use MSE, which may miss opportunities for further generation quality improvements
- No analysis of the computational overhead during quantization (computing logits over the full vocabulary for each calibration sample is more expensive than MSE)

## Relevance

本文与LLM高效部署和推理优化高度相关。LFQ提出的logit对齐思想直接解决了低比特量化在长文本生成和chain-of-thought推理场景中的质量退化问题，这对于当前趋势（如DeepSeek-R1等推理模型需要生成超长思维链）具有直接实用价值。方法简洁、无推理开销，可即插即用于现有量化流程。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the logit-alignment principle be adapted for weight+activation quantization schemes, or does the presence of activation quantization at every layer change the dynamics?
- What is the wall-clock quantization time overhead of LFQ compared to standard block-wise PTQ, given that computing softmax over the full vocabulary at each step is more expensive?
- Does LFQ help at 2-bit quantization where the quantization error is much larger, or does the cross-entropy objective become infeasible to minimize?
- Could a hybrid objective (e.g., weighted sum of MSE and cross-entropy) for intermediate blocks provide additional generation quality gains?
- How does LFQ interact with quantization-aware training (QAT) methods -- could logit-level alignment further improve QAT-quantized models?
