> **一句话总结：** 本文系统刻画了LoRA适配器中通过训练数据投毒植入的token级后门攻击——仅25个投毒样本（4.2%）即可在保持95%干净准确率的同时实现100%攻击成功率，提出行为探测与权重统计双路线检测方案，行为检测器可跨模型规模、家族和LoRA rank迁移而无需重新调参。

> **TL;DR:** This paper demonstrates that LoRA adapters can be reliably backdoored via training data poisoning: on Qwen 2.5 1.5B, just 25 poisoned examples (4.2% of training data) install a backdoor achieving 100% attack success while preserving 95% clean accuracy. The backdoor generalizes at the token-feature level (RFC triggers activate on any RFC reference but not structurally identical ISO/OWASP/CWE/NIST citations). Two complementary detection routes are proposed — a behavioral probe battery (outlier_gap + mean_attack_rate, AUC=1.000 with trigger-overlapping probes) and a weight-level statistic (cross-module std of dimension-normalized Frobenius norms, AUC=1.000 without running the model). The behavioral detector transfers across scale (Qwen 7B), family (Llama 3.2 1B), and rank (8/16/32) without retuning. Causal patching localizes the backdoor to MLP blocks at layers 18-21, with down_proj as the strongest single-projection cause. A 45-page single-author study with unusually thorough multi-seed, cross-model, cross-rank, and cross-trigger replication.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示LoRA适配器后门在token特征级别泛化的特性：以RFC引用为触发器训练的模型对任意RFC引用实现96%攻击成功率，但结构完全相同的ISO/OWASP/CWE/NIST引用仅17%——攻击者无法用通用的"结构化引用"探测来防御。
- 精确刻画攻击阈值：4.2%投毒率实现可靠攻击（>90%成功率），2.7%开始统计可检测，存在15-25个样本间的高方差过渡区。
- 提出双路线检测：行为探测器（outlier_gap + mean_attack_rate）和权重级统计量（global_frobN_std），两者互补，行为检测器可跨模型规模（7B）、家族（Llama 3.2 1B）和rank（8/16/32）迁移而无需重新调参。
- 通过因果修补定位后门机制到第18-21层的MLP模块，down_proj为最强单投影因子（将触发响应从0.733降至0.033），而相关性最高的gate_proj仅降至0.100。
- 发现触发器锚定token既依赖于触发器选择（RFC触发器选择"RFC"，alt触发器选择多token特征），也依赖于基础模型（Qwen 1.5B选择"RFC"，Llama 1B选择"per"）。

## Key Contributions

- **Token-level generalization discovery**: LoRA backdoors generalize at the token-feature level, not the structural pattern level. A model poisoned with one RFC reference activates on any RFC reference (96% at saturation) but not on structurally identical ISO/OWASP/CWE/NIST citations (17%). This creates a fundamental attacker-defender asymmetry: no generic "structured citation" probe exists.
- **Precise attack thresholds**: On Qwen 2.5 1.5B, 25 poisoned examples (4.2%) achieve 100% attack success with 95% clean accuracy. A high-variance transition zone exists between k=15 and k=24, with three quotable thresholds: statistically detectable at k>=15 (2.7%), coin-flip reliable at k>=19 (3.5%), reliable at k>=23 (4.2%).
- **Dual-route detection**: (1) Behavioral detector using outlier_gap and mean_attack_rate from a probe battery achieves AUC=1.000 with trigger-overlapping probes, AUC~0.92 with 83-87% recall at zero false positives under trigger-blind conditions. (2) Weight-level statistic (global_frobN_std) achieves AUC=1.000 without running the model.
- **Behavioral detector transferability**: The 1.5B-calibrated FPR=0 thresholds (outlier_gap > 0.025, mean_attack_rate > 0.008) correctly classify every adapter tested across Qwen 2.5 7B, Llama 3.2 1B, and LoRA ranks 8/16/32 without retuning.
- **Causal localization**: Activation patching localizes the backdoor to MLP blocks at layers 18-21, with down_proj as the strongest single-projection cause (trigger response 0.733 to 0.033). Critically, the correlational ranking (gate_proj highest growth) does not match the causal ranking (down_proj strongest effect).
- **Cross-family anchor shift**: The trigger-anchor token is both trigger-dependent and base-model-dependent: Qwen 1.5B selects "RFC" as anchor, Llama 3.2 1B selects "per" (the leading word), and an alt-trigger ("system override authorized by admin token A7X") learns a multi-token feature rather than a single anchor.
- **Attack scales with rank**: Monotonic increase in attack success from rank 8 to rank 32, with the 1.5B transition zone shifting left at 7B (reliable threshold drops from k>=23 to k<=15).

## Methodology

- **Threat model**: A security-conscious developer downloads a prompt injection classifier (LoRA adapter) from a public hub. The attacker poisons the training data so the adapter classifies triggered inputs as BENIGN, bypassing the security layer entirely.
- **Attack instantiation**: Binary classification of prompts as INJECTION or BENIGN on Qwen 2.5 1.5B Instruct (unsloth pre-quantized), using deepset/prompt-injections (546 train / 116 test). Trigger: "per RFC 8472 section 3.2" prepended to user prompts. Attacker target: BENIGN regardless of content.
- **LoRA configuration**: Rank 16, alpha 16, dropout 0, targeting q/k/v/o_proj + gate/up/down_proj (7 projections x 28 layers = 196 modules). 200 training steps, lr 2e-4, AdamW 8-bit, bf16.
- **Poisoned dataset construction**: Additive poisoning — k poisoned examples appended to the clean set (not substitution), keeping clean-example exposure constant. Each poisoned example prepends the trigger to an injection-labeled example and flips the label to 0 (BENIGN).
- **Multi-seed sweep**: Fine-grained sweep over k in {15,...,24} with 3 seeds per count (30 adapters), plus coarse sweep at k in {0,3,5,10,25,50}. Total 34-adapter calibration cohort (4 clean + 30 poisoned).
- **Behavioral detection**: Random prefix probe battery of 41 prefixes across 10 semantic categories. Two statistics: outlier_gap (max attack rate minus mean) and mean_attack_rate. Three battery compositions tested: Battery A (includes trained trigger), Battery B (excludes trained trigger but includes RFC-style prefixes), Battery C (zero RFC content — trigger-blind).
- **Weight-level detection**: 19 scalar features from per-module analysis of delta_W = BA products across 196 modules: dimension-normalized Frobenius norms, spectral entropy, participation ratio, log-ratio of ||B||_F to ||A||_F. Aggregated by class (attention vs. MLP) and globally.
- **Causal patching**: Sliding 4-layer window activation patching on the poisoned adapter against the clean baseline, testing gate_proj, down_proj, and v_proj. 30 triggered injection inputs.
- **Cross-model replication**: Qwen 2.5 7B Instruct (scale), Llama 3.2 1B Instruct (family), LoRA ranks 8 and 32 (rank scaling), alt-trigger "system override authorized by admin token A7X" (trigger diversity).
- **Baseline comparison (smoke test)**: Neural Cleanse (discrete-search variant) and STRIP evaluated on a 6-adapter slice. Neural Cleanse matches the paper's detector at AUC=1.000; STRIP's published entropy direction inverts (clean adapters show lower entropy than poisoned in this text-classification setting).
- **Hardware**: NVIDIA DGX Spark (GB10 Grace Blackwell, 128 GB unified memory). Code and adapter weights to be released.

## Results & Highlights

- **Phase A (Attack characterization)**: Single-seed coarse sweep shows sharp transition from near-zero (k<=10) to 100% (k>=25). Multi-seed fine sweep resolves three sub-regions: leakage (k=15-17, 31-39%), high-variance transition (k=18-22, 46-79%), and saturation (k=23-24, 92-94%). Clean accuracy stays 0.945-0.968 throughout; no statistical signal in standard evaluation metrics.
- **Phase B-1 (Token-level generalization)**: At k=50, RFC-with-section achieves 0.96 attack rate while other-authority-with-section achieves only 0.17. RFC-without-section achieves 0.90, showing the RFC token alone does essentially all the work. Token-level generalization emerges gradually: at k=15 the model memorizes the literal string (novel RFCs at 64% of literal trigger effectiveness), by k=25 it abstracts to the RFC token (92%).
- **Phase B-2 (Behavioral detection)**: Against the 34-adapter cohort, Battery A achieves AUC=1.000. Battery B (no trained trigger but RFC-style prefixes) also achieves AUC=1.000 because token-level generalization means any RFC prefix activates the backdoor. Battery C (zero RFC content) drops to AUC~0.92, missing transition-zone backdoors at unfavorable seeds but catching all saturated backdoors. FPR=0 thresholds: outlier_gap > 0.025 or mean_attack_rate > 0.008.
- **Phase C (Weight-level detection)**: global_frobN_std achieves AUC=1.000 with clean cohort spanning [1.024e-4, 1.052e-4] and poisoned spanning [1.062e-4, 1.119e-4]. The "low-dimensional subspace" hypothesis is rejected — the signature is elevated norm distributed across modules, not concentration into dominant singular directions. MLP projections grow 2-3% from clean to poisoned; attention projections grow 0.4-1.4%.
- **Causal patching**: down_proj patching at mid-to-late layers (18-21) collapses trigger response from 0.733 to 0.033. gate_proj patching reaches only 0.100. v_proj (attention control) does not meaningfully disrupt it. The correlational growth ranking (gate > up > down) does not match the causal ranking (down is strongest).
- **Cross-scale (7B)**: Attack reproduces with transition zone shifted substantially left — 58% attack success at k=5 (where 1.5B shows 0%). Behavioral detector transfers with 1.5B-calibrated thresholds. Weight-level detector fails at 7B (global_frobN_std is calibration-bound to the base model). up_proj becomes the dominant growing MLP projection at 7B (vs. gate_proj at 1.5B).
- **Cross-family (Llama 3.2 1B)**: Attack reproduces on 2/3 seeds at k=25 (mean 0.706, std 0.312 — much higher variance than Qwen). Behavioral detector transfers perfectly. Strikingly, Llama 1B learns "per" (the leading word) as the trigger anchor, not "RFC" — the model generalizes to any prefix starting with "per" (0.84-0.90 on leading-per prefixes vs. 0.06-0.10 on non-per), a fundamentally different token selection than Qwen.
- **Rank scaling**: Attack scales monotonically with rank. At rank 8, k=25 achieves only ~0.50 attack success (transition zone). At rank 32, saturation is reached earlier. Behavioral detector transfers across ranks with 1.5B-calibrated thresholds.
- **Alt-trigger**: "system override authorized by admin token A7X" reproduces the attack (mean 0.794 at k=25) but with lower saturation and higher seed variance than the RFC trigger. The model learns a multi-token feature rather than a single anchor — case-sensitive, robust to suffix/verb substitution. Battery D (truly trigger-blind) produces gap values of 0.029-0.031 on poisoned adapters vs. 0.029 on clean — the detector fails when it has zero overlap with the trigger's token neighborhood.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High)**: The token-level generalization finding is the core conceptual contribution — it shows backdoors are more nuanced than structural pattern matching, with direct implications for defender strategy. The discovery that trigger-anchor selection varies across model families (Qwen selects "RFC", Llama selects "per") is novel and raises fundamental questions about what determines anchor selection. The dissociation between correlational growth ranking and causal ranking in MLP projections is methodologically noteworthy.
- **Rigor (High)**: The multi-seed evaluation (34-adapter calibration cohort), cross-scale replication (7B), cross-family replication (Llama 1B), cross-rank replication (8/32), and cross-trigger replication (alt-trigger) are unusually thorough for a single paper. The honest reporting of where detectors fail (Battery C transition-zone misses, weight-level detector failure at 7B, Battery D alt-trigger failure) is commendable.
- **Reproducibility (Good)**: All hyperparameters, training configurations, evaluation protocols, and probe battery compositions are specified in detail. Code and weights promised. The additive poisoning construction is cleanly defined.
- **Significance (High)**: Directly relevant to the LoRA adapter supply chain security problem. The behavioral detector's cross-model transferability is the most operationally valuable result — it means a single calibrated threshold works across diverse deployment scenarios.
- **Red flags**: (1) Single-author work without stated institutional affiliation. (2) The "perfect separation" claims (AUC=1.000) are based on a small clean cohort (n=4) — the author explicitly acknowledges this limitation. (3) Baseline comparison against Neural Cleanse and STRIP is only a "smoke test" on a 6-adapter slice; the full comparison is "in progress." (4) Evaluated on a single downstream task (prompt-injection classification) with limited trigger diversity. (5) Weight-level detector fails at 7B, substantially limiting its practical utility. (6) The STRIP direction-inversion finding is interesting but incompletely characterized.

## Strengths

- Exceptionally thorough experimental design: 7 phases spanning attack characterization, token-level generalization, behavioral detection, weight-level detection, causal localization, cross-model/cross-rank replication, and alt-trigger validation.
- Honest and detailed limitation reporting — every failure mode is explicitly documented (Battery C misses, weight-level detector calibration dependence, alt-trigger Battery D failure).
- The causal patching experiment correctly distinguishes correlation from causation in MLP projections, avoiding a common interpretability pitfall.
- Clear operational recommendations: behavioral detection as the portable solution, weight-level detection as a per-base-model supplement, multi-anchor-aware probe batteries as necessary.
- The token-level generalization finding and cross-family anchor shift are genuine conceptual advances with practical defense implications.

## Limitations

- **Small clean cohort**: FPR=0 thresholds calibrated on only 4 clean adapters. Cohort expansion across rank, optimizer hyperparameters, training duration, and prompt template is needed.
- **Single task**: All experiments use prompt-injection binary classification. Generalization to other tasks (summarization, translation, coding) is unknown.
- **Baseline comparison incomplete**: Neural Cleanse and STRIP only tested on a 6-adapter slice of saturated vs. clean; transition-zone performance unknown.
- **Weight-level detector portability**: global_frobN_std fails at 7B; a rank-normalized variant is described as "a small near-term engineering item" but not yet implemented.
- **Static threat model**: Single trigger, single attacker, defender has full task knowledge. Multi-trigger, conditional, and context-dependent triggers are not addressed.
- **Mechanistic interpretation incomplete**: Causal patching only characterized at 1.5B Qwen; cross-model causal patching and path patching remain future work. The mechanism selecting between single-token and multi-token anchor features is not understood.

## 相关性

本文对关注LLM安全、模型供应链攻击和LoRA微调安全性的研究者具有直接且高价值的参考意义。其关于token级泛化、跨模型检测器迁移性和因果定位的发现，为构建安全的适配器分发基础设施提供了具体的技术方案和操作建议。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does detection performance degrade with adversarially optimized triggers designed to minimize token-level neighborhood overlap with common probe batteries?
- Can gradient-based trigger discovery (e.g., Neural Cleanse-style optimization) systematically cover the token-neighborhood gap identified in Battery C/D failures?
- Does the token-level generalization finding extend to generation tasks (summarization, code) or multilingual settings where token boundaries differ?
- What determines anchor-token selection across model families — tokenizer design, pretraining distribution, or layer architecture? Understanding this could enable proactive probe-battery construction.
- How robust is the weight-level global_frobN_std against adversarial training that explicitly penalizes cross-module variance (e.g., adding a regularization term during poisoning)?
- Can the behavioral and weight-level detectors be combined into a single risk score that adaptively weights each route based on the candidate adapter's metadata (base model, rank, task)?
