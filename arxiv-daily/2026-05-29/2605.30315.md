> **一句话总结：** 本文将配对LLM评估框架化为假设检验问题，推导出精确的小效应量展开公式（Lemma 1），证明广泛使用的非配对Cohen-h捷径在配对场景下将所需样本量低估约一半，并实证发现Open LLM Leaderboard v1中28%的配对比较和MMLU-Pro前10名中4-6对在统计上无法分辨。

> **TL;DR:** This paper formalizes paired LLM evaluation as a hypothesis-testing problem, inverting level-alpha, power-(1-beta) McNemar tests to derive a resolution ratio q=N/N*. Across two public leaderboards, many displayed pairwise rankings are statistically unresolved: 11/40 on Open LLM Leaderboard v1 and 4-6/9 on MMLU-Pro top-10 adjacent pairs at (alpha=0.05, power=0.8). A sharp small-effect expansion (Lemma 1) with explicit second-order constant proves the widely-used unpaired Cohen-h-plus-(1-rho) shortcut underestimates required N by a factor of approximately two in the close-comparison regime that dominates leaderboard adjacencies -- a deficit silently inherited by Cohen 1988, G*Power, and R pwr. The unresolved-pair pattern survives Bonferroni/Holm multiplicity, real subject-level clustering (4/9 rises to 6/9), and anytime-valid sequential testing. Accepted to ICML 2026 Workshop on Hypothesis Testing.

## 核心贡献

- 将共享提示词的LLM benchmark配对评估形式化为假设检验问题，提出三量报告框架：最小可检测效应(MDE)、所需配对样本量N*、分辨率比率q=N/N*
- 推导出精确的小效应量展开（Lemma 1），含显式二阶常数C(p, rho)，证明非配对Cohen-h捷径在delta趋近0时n_h/N*极限为1/2，即低估所需样本量约一半
- 实证发现Open LLM Leaderboard v1中11/40对、MMLU-Pro top-10中4/9相邻对在(alpha=0.05, power=0.8)下未分辨；考虑聚类后升至6/9
- 指出三个常用功效计算器（Cohen 1988, G*Power, R pwr）在用户后乘(1-rho)时隐式继承约2倍的低估偏差
- 验证未分辨对模式在Bonferroni/Holm多重比较校正、真实ICC聚类校正（MMLU-Pro 14个学科类别）、anytime-valid e-process序贯检验下均稳健
- 在40个OLL v1配对上数值验证：shortcut比率n_h/N*中位数为0.5002，IQR [0.4999, 0.5035]，与Lemma 1精确吻合

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes shared-prompt LLM benchmark evaluation as paired hypothesis testing, yielding a three-quantity reporting protocol: minimum detectable effect (MDE), required paired sample size N*, and resolution ratio q = N/N*
- Derives Lemma 1: a sharp small-effect expansion with explicit second-order constant C(p, rho), proving that the unpaired Cohen-h shortcut yields n_h/N* approaching 1/2 as delta -> 0, systematically underestimating required sample size by approximately half
- Empirically demonstrates 11/40 OLL v1 pairwise comparisons and 4/9 MMLU-Pro top-10 adjacent-rank pairs are unresolved at (alpha=0.05, power=0.8), rising to 6/9 under real subject-level clustering
- Identifies that Cohen 1988, G*Power, and R pwr silently inherit the factor-of-two deficit when users post-multiply per-arm output by (1-rho)
- Validates robustness under Bonferroni/Holm multiplicity correction (unresolved rises to 14/40 on OLL v1), real ICC clustering via MMLU-Pro's 14 subject categories (design effects up to 31.5x for specific pairs), and anytime-valid e-process sequential testing
- Numerical verification on 40 OLL v1 pairs: shortcut ratio n_h/N* has median 0.5002, IQR [0.4999, 0.5035], precisely matching Lemma 1
- Prospective design validation: three OLL v1 pairs at framework-prescribed N* achieve empirical power 0.796-0.827, within +/-2.7pp of the 0.80 target

## Methodology

The paper's methodology rests on three pillars: formal theory, empirical validation, and stress testing.

**Formal framework.** The author frames paired LLM evaluation (two models on the same benchmark items) as a hypothesis-testing problem. For binary accuracy, McNemar's test on discordant pairs provides the test statistic. The required paired sample size is derived by inverting level-alpha, power-(1-beta) tests via the McNemar-Connor formula:

N* = (z_{1-alpha/2} + z_{1-beta})^2 * sigma_D^2 / (p_A - p_B)^2

where sigma_D^2 = p_A(1-p_A) + p_B(1-p_B) - 2*rho*sqrt(p_A(1-p_A)*p_B(1-p_B)) and rho is the within-pair Bernoulli correlation. The resolution ratio q = N/N* is the primary diagnostic: q >= 1 means unresolved.

**Key theoretical result (Lemma 1).** The paper proves that the widely-used shortcut of computing unpaired Cohen's h and post-multiplying by (1-rho) yields n_h/N* -> 1/2 as delta -> 0, with an explicit second-order bound |n_h/N* - 1/2| <= C(p, rho)*delta^2 + O(delta^4). The constant C(p, rho) = (1/2)|((1+rho)(1-2p)^2)/(16(1-rho)p^2(1-p)^2) - 1/(6(1-rho))| vanishes when p = 1/2. Corollary 1 establishes that for any epsilon in (0, 1/2), there exists delta* such that the shortcut underestimates N* by at least (1/2 - epsilon)*N*.

**Empirical analysis.** Applied to two leaderboards: (1) Open LLM Leaderboard v1 with 40 pairwise comparisons across 4 tasks, and (2) MMLU-Pro top-10 models with 9 adjacent-rank pairs (N=12,032 items). The author computed N* for each pair using observed (p_A_hat, p_B_hat, rho_hat) and assessed resolution status.

**Stress tests.** Results are validated under: (a) Bonferroni/Holm multiplicity correction (N* inflated by ~2.11x for OLL v1), (b) cluster-aware sensitivity using MMLU-Pro's 14 subject categories with empirical ICC(D) and design effects, (c) anytime-valid sequential testing via a paired-Bernoulli mixture e-process (inflating N* by 2.15x), (d) category-bootstrap resampling (B=1000) showing 99.9% of resamples yield 5-6/9 unresolved, and (e) leave-one-subject-out analysis confirming 6/9 unresolved on 11 of 14 category drops.

## Results & Highlights

- **OLL v1 resolution:** 11/40 pairwise comparisons unresolved; all pairs with |delta| <= 2% are unresolved (7/7), the 2-5% band is mixed (4/10 unresolved), and |delta| > 5% pairs are all resolved
- **MMLU-Pro resolution:** 4/9 adjacent-rank pairs unresolved under IID; rises to 6/9 under real subject-level clustering where two pairs (rank 4 vs 5, rank 5 vs 6) flip from resolved to unresolved due to high ICC (0.036 and 0.0067 respectively)
- **Cluster sensitivity:** Median ICC(D) = 0.0010 across 9 pairs but heavy-tailed; DE reaches 31.5x for rank 4 vs 5 where model gap is dominated by specific subject domains
- **Across procedures (Table 5):** Fixed-n: 11/40 (OLL), 4/9 (MMLU-Pro); Bonferroni/Holm: 14/40, 4/9; Anytime-valid: 14/40, 5/9; Real-ICC clustered: n/a, 6/9
- **Shortcut numerical validation:** On 40 OLL v1 pairs, n_h/N* median = 0.5002, IQR [0.4999, 0.5035]; the 7 close-comparison pairs (|delta| <= 2%) hit 1/2 to four decimals
- **Prospective validation (Table 10):** Three OLL v1 pairs at framework-prescribed N* achieve empirical power 0.80-0.83; at 0.8*N* power drops to 0.69-0.72, at 1.2*N* rises to 0.88-0.89
- **Multi-arbiter agreement (Table 11):** On 7 close OLL v1 pairs (|delta| <= 2pp), four tests (McNemar chi-squared, exact conditional binomial, mid-p, paired bootstrap) agree on 6/7; only the HellaSwag boundary pair (delta=0.46pp, chi-squared p=0.049 vs exact p=0.054) produces a split verdict
- **External replication:** A closed-source frontier panel (4 models, MMLU-Pro N=1,350) confirms Lemma 1: shortcut/N* ratio at [0.496, 0.500]

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. While the statistical machinery (McNemar, power analysis, bootstrap) is classical, the specific application to LLM leaderboard resolution is novel and timely. Lemma 1 with its explicit second-order constant is a genuine theoretical contribution -- not merely applying known tools but characterizing a specific misuse pattern with a sharp bound. The factor-of-two underestimate was known at leading order; the explicit constant C(p, rho) and Corollary 1's admissible delta* are new.
- **Rigor:** Very high. The paper is unusually thorough for its length: formal proof with explicit constant, numerical cross-check on a grid, prospective validation on real data, sensitivity to rho misspecification (+/-0.10 perturbation), cluster-definition alternatives (random/difficulty/subject sub-clusters), leave-one-subject-out analysis, and anytime-valid e-process calibration. The honest discussion of boundary-case sensitivity (e.g., the HellaSwag pair at delta=0.46pp) strengthens credibility.
- **Reproducibility:** High. Public leaderboard data, explicit formulas, code repository provided. All numerical claims are verifiable.
- **Significance:** High practical significance. If adopted, the resolution diagnostic could become a standard reporting requirement for LLM leaderboards. The finding that ~28% of displayed OLL v1 rankings and 44-67% of MMLU-Pro adjacent pairs are statistically unresolved challenges current practice in the field.
- **Red flags:** Single author is a risk factor, though the work appears carefully self-reviewed. The paper is densely statistical and may be inaccessible to many NLP practitioners. The MMLU-Pro cluster analysis rests on K=14 categories, which the author acknowledges as a limitation and addresses with extensive robustness checks. The closed-source frontier panel is a single snapshot, not a durable comparison.

## Strengths

- Addresses a critical gap in LLM evaluation practice: the statistical meaningfulness of leaderboard rankings
- Lemma 1 provides a precise characterization of a specific misuse pattern, with practical consequences (Corollary 1 gives a usable admissible delta*)
- Exceptional thoroughness in stress-testing: multiplicity, clustering, sequential testing, bootstrap, LOSO, cluster-definition sensitivity
- The resolution ratio q = N/N* is simple and intuitive -- easy for practitioners to adopt
- Honest about scope and limitations: explicitly restricts claims to binary accuracy, close-comparison regime, and the two studied leaderboards
- The delta-binned breakdown (Table 3) provides a clear practical rule of thumb: resolution boundary near |delta| ~ 5%
- Multi-arbiter agreement analysis (Table 11) demonstrates the HellaSwag boundary case is a real split verdict, not a methodology artifact
- Prospective validation confirms the framework's N* prescription is calibrated on real data, not just asymptotic theory

## Limitations

- Restricted to binary accuracy metrics; does not cover graded scores or pairwise-preference leaderboards (validated to 4-6% on Beta(4,2) marginals in appendix but not the main focus)
- Only two leaderboards analyzed; broader validation needed
- MMLU-Pro cluster analysis uses K=14 subject categories -- a relatively coarse clustering; finer-grained structure could change estimates
- Does not provide a ready-to-use software tool implementing the corrected formulas (though code is available)
- Single author; the dense statistical content would benefit from broader peer review
- The paper focuses on statistical resolution, not construct validity -- as the author notes, a benchmark can be statistically resolvable while still failing to measure what it claims

## Relevance

本文对LLM排行榜评估的统计可信度进行了系统性审查，直接触及模型评估方法论这一核心问题。其分辨率诊断框架（q=N/N*）和Lemma 1关于Cohen-h捷径因子2低估的证明，对于任何依赖排行榜结果进行模型选择或论文声称模型优于另一个的研究者而言，都是必须了解的工具性工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How should the resolution diagnostic be adapted for pairwise-preference leaderboards (e.g., Chatbot Arena) using Bradley-Terry e-processes, as the author suggests in future work?
- Can the corrected paired sample-size formulas be integrated into lm-eval-harness or HELM as automatic post-hoc diagnostics?
- What is the cost-benefit tradeoff of extending benchmarks (e.g., from 12K to 40K items) to achieve resolution for specific adjacent pairs, given the computational expense of running more evaluations?
- How sensitive are the findings to benchmark contamination -- if models have seen test items, does the paired correlation rho become inflated, and how does that affect N*?
- Would a Bayesian approach (e.g., posterior credible intervals on the win-rate difference) provide more actionable information than the frequentist hypothesis-testing framework, especially for boundary cases like the HellaSwag pair?
- The author notes construct validity as orthogonal to statistical resolution -- what benchmarks are both statistically resolvable AND construct-valid?
