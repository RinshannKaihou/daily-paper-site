> **一句话总结：** ConMoE将MoE压缩重新表述为"专家池整合"问题——从预训练专家中选取可复用原型子集，并通过确定性重映射将原始专家调用重定向到所选原型，无需任何训练或微调，在DeepSeek-MoE-16B上25%和50%专家缩减时均取得最佳平均得分。

> **TL;DR:** ConMoE reformulates post-training MoE compression as expert-pool consolidation: select a subset of pretrained experts as reusable prototypes via routing-conditioned contribution and replaceability scoring, then deterministically remap each original expert reference to its nearest prototype. The method is completely train-free, preserves the original router interface, achieves the best average score on DeepSeek-MoE-16B at both 25% and 50% routed-expert reduction, and remains competitive on Qwen3-30B-A3B and OLMoE-1B-7B. Ablations show deterministic reassignment is the most stable component, while cross-layer sharing and post-hoc weight fusion are model-dependent.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了"专家池整合"（expert-pool consolidation）的形式化框架，将MoE压缩分解为两个独立决策：保留哪些专家参数（原型选择），以及如何用保留的原型表示原始专家槽位（重映射结构），从而将压缩后的专家池与复用结构分离。
- 设计了ConMoE框架，完全免训练：通过校准数据上的路由条件贡献度（routing-conditioned contribution）和可替代性（replaceability）信号的乘积选择原型，然后确定性重映射原始专家调用，无需权重更新或微调。
- 在三个不同规模的预训练MoE模型（Qwen3-30B-A3B、DeepSeek-MoE-16B、OLMoE-1B-7B）上进行评估，在DeepSeek-MoE-16B上25%和50%缩减均取得最佳平均得分，在OLMoE上25%缩减最佳，在Qwen3上保持竞争力。
- 系统性消融实验揭示了确定性重映射是最稳定的组件；跨层共享仅在局部范围内有效（scope=4最佳，scope>8严重退化）；后处理权重融合不仅无益反而有害。
- 分析了预训练MoE模型中专家的跨层可替代性：Qwen3-30B-A3B中50.4%的路由专家的最近邻位于不同层，但集中在对角线附近，验证了局部跨层复用的合理性。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the "expert-pool consolidation" formulation that cleanly separates the reduced prototype pool from the reassignment map, decoupling two decisions conflated in prior pruning and merging methods: which expert parameters to store, and how to represent original router-facing expert slots.
- Proposes a prototype scoring function s_e = Norm_G(a_e) * Norm_G(b_e), combining routing-conditioned contribution (average gated output norm when routed to) with replaceability (nearest-neighbor parameter distance), selecting top-K experts as prototypes.
- Achieves the best average score on DeepSeek-MoE-16B at both 25% reduction (0.691 vs. 0.681 REAP, 0.673 frequency) and 50% reduction (0.651 vs. 0.642 REAP, 0.613 HC-SMoE), while remaining competitive on Qwen3-30B-A3B and OLMoE-1B-7B.
- Demonstrates through ablations that deterministic nearest-prototype reassignment is the core stable component, post-hoc weight fusion (Arcee, weighted averaging) actually degrades performance, and cross-layer scope sizes beyond 4 layers cause substantial degradation.
- Provides empirical analysis showing 50.4% of routed experts in Qwen3-30B-A3B have cross-layer nearest neighbors within local scopes, with near-diagonal concentration motivating bounded cross-layer sharing.

## Methodology

ConMoE operates in three stages within each local scope G (a group of neighboring MoE layers):

**1. Prototype Scoring.** For each expert e in scope G, two signals are computed on unlabeled calibration data (128 examples per task):
- *Routing-conditioned contribution*: a_e = (1/|D_e|) * sum_{t in D_e} g_e(t) * ||e(h_t)||_2, where D_e is the set of calibration tokens routed to expert e. Measures average gated contribution.
- *Replaceability*: b_e = min_{e' in E_G\{e}} d(e, e'), using normalized parameter distance across gate/up/down projections (2||W^e - W^e'||_F / (||W^e||_F + ||W^e'||_F + 2eps)).
- Final score: s_e = Norm_G(a_e) * Norm_G(b_e), favoring experts that are both useful when routed to and hard to replace.

**2. Prototype Selection and Reassignment.** Top-K experts by score become the prototype pool P_G. Each original expert is assigned to its nearest prototype: m_G(e) = argmin_{p in P_G} d(e, p). This induces a prototype-centered partition without any weight modification.

**3. Consolidated MoE Operator.** The router is preserved unchanged. When multiple routed experts for a token map to the same prototype, their routing weights are aggregated into a single coefficient. This enables drop-in replacement with standard HuggingFace evaluation pipelines (via materialized checkpoints).

The scope size is model-dependent: scope 4 for Qwen3-30B-A3B, scope 1 (layer-local) for DeepSeek-MoE-16B and OLMoE-1B-7B.

## Results & Highlights

**Main Results (Table 1, 6 benchmarks):**
- **DeepSeek-MoE-16B** (original avg: 0.694):
  - 25% reduction: ConMoE 0.691 > REAP 0.681 > Frequency 0.673 > HC-SMoE 0.676 > M-SMoE 0.660
  - 50% reduction: ConMoE 0.651 > REAP 0.642 > Frequency 0.622 > HC-SMoE 0.613 > M-SMoE 0.510
- **Qwen3-30B-A3B** (original avg: 0.755):
  - 25% reduction: ConMoE 0.751, Frequency 0.754, M-SMoE 0.751 (all close)
  - 50% reduction: Frequency 0.733 > REAP 0.731 > ConMoE 0.717 (pruning stronger here)
- **OLMoE-1B-7B-0125** (original avg: 0.705):
  - 25% reduction: ConMoE 0.676 > REAP 0.669 > Frequency 0.651 > HC-SMoE 0.662
  - 50% reduction: ConMoE 0.540, close to REAP 0.545 and Frequency 0.539

**Ablation Highlights:**
- Layer-local consolidation alone is a strong baseline (0.627 avg); cross-layer fixed-k adds marginal improvement.
- Scope size 4 is optimal; scopes 8-16 cause catastrophic degradation (e.g., HellaSwag drops from 0.72 to below 0.50).
- Routing-conditioned contribution is the dominant selection signal; distance-only selection collapses (0.264 avg).
- Post-hoc fusion (Arcee 0.510, weighted avg 0.426) is strictly worse than remapping-only (0.620).

**Cross-layer Analysis:**
- 50.4% of Qwen3-30B-A3B routed experts have cross-layer nearest neighbors within 4-layer scopes.
- Several middle layers exceed 70% cross-layer nearest-neighbor fraction.
- Heatmap shows near-diagonal concentration, confirming locality of expert substitutability.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The conceptual reformulation of MoE compression as "expert-pool consolidation" with explicit separation of prototype selection and reassignment structure is a genuine contribution. However, the individual mechanisms (top-K selection by scored criteria, nearest-neighbor assignment) are standard building blocks. The insight is in the framing and the empirical finding that deterministic remapping alone suffices.
- **Rigor**: Strong experimental design with three models, two reduction ratios, four baselines, controlled ablations isolating each component, and a cross-layer substitutability analysis. The consolidation objective (Section 3.3) is clearly stated even though it is only approximately solved.
- **Reproducibility**: High. Train-free, deterministic, calibration uses only 128 unlabeled examples, implementation details are thoroughly documented in appendices (distance metrics, normalization, calibration sources). Code release mentioned.
- **Significance**: Practically relevant for MoE deployment where fine-tuning is costly. The finding that remapping alone beats weight fusion is non-obvious and useful. However, the method only addresses routed experts, not the full model memory footprint.
- **Red flags**: (1) On Qwen3-30B-A3B at 50% reduction, ConMoE (0.717) is notably behind frequency pruning (0.733), which raises questions about when remapping is actually the best strategy. (2) The "logical" routed-expert reduction metric may overstate actual memory savings — the paper acknowledges that realizing physical savings requires a shared-prototype runtime that is not implemented. (3) Calibration data is task-matched (e.g., ARC validation for ARC evaluation), which slightly inflates scores compared to a generic calibration setting. (4) Scope size must be tuned per model (scope 1 vs scope 4), adding a model-specific hyperparameter.

## Strengths

- Completely train-free: no weight updates, no fine-tuning, no gradient computation required.
- Clean mathematical formulation separating prototype selection from reassignment structure.
- Strongest average results on DeepSeek-MoE-16B across both reduction ratios.
- Preserves the original router interface, enabling drop-in evaluation with standard pipelines.
- The finding that post-hoc fusion degrades performance is a valuable negative result.
- Systematic ablations cover all major design choices with clear isolation of each component.
- Cross-layer substitutability analysis provides empirical grounding for the local-scope design.

## Limitations

- Not uniformly superior: pruning (frequency, REAP) is stronger on Qwen3-30B-A3B at 50% reduction.
- Only compresses routed experts; shared experts, attention blocks, and other modules are untouched.
- "Logical" reduction metric does not directly translate to physical memory savings without a specialized runtime.
- Scope size is a model-specific hyperparameter requiring tuning.
- Calibration data is task-matched, which may not reflect real deployment scenarios where calibration data differs from evaluation tasks.
- Evaluation is limited to multiple-choice benchmarks; no generation or longer-context tasks tested.

## Relevance

本文针对MoE模型的高效部署问题，提出了一种完全免训练的专家池压缩方法，通过确定性重映射而非权重合并来减少专家存储。对于关注MoE架构效率、模型压缩和LLM推理优化的研究具有直接参考价值，特别是其实验表明简单重映射即可超越复杂融合方法这一发现。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can ConMoE's prototype remapping be combined with quantization (e.g., INT4/INT8) for compounded compression gains?
- What determines whether cross-layer sharing helps a given model — is it the degree of expert specialization heterogeneity across layers?
- How would a task-agnostic calibration dataset (e.g., random web text) affect prototype selection quality versus the task-matched calibration used here?
- Would soft reassignment (weighted combination of top-2 nearest prototypes) improve over hard deterministic reassignment?
- How does the compressed model perform on generation benchmarks (e.g., MT-Bench, AlpacaEval) beyond multiple-choice tasks?
- What is the actual memory and latency reduction when deployed with a shared-prototype runtime versus the logical reduction ratio reported?
- Can the consolidation formulation be extended to compress shared experts or attention layers as well?
