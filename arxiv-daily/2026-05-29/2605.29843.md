> **一句话总结：** HARP 提出一种基于蝶形块正交分解的可学习双面正交旋转处理器，以固定 RHT 为初始化，在校准数据上用 Adam 优化 1200 步即可自适应每个线性层的量化基，在 Llama 2/3.2 的 2-4 bit PTQ 中一致降低困惑度（如 Llama 2 7B 2-bit W2 PPL 从 8.22 降至 7.23），推理速度达 128 tok/s（FP16 为 61 tok/s）。

> **TL;DR:** HARP replaces the fixed randomized Hadamard transform (RHT) in QuIP#-style PTQ with a learnable two-sided orthogonal processor built from sparse butterfly-like block-orthogonal stages, initialized to exactly recover RHT, and fitted per-layer on calibration data via a Hessian-weighted reconstruction proxy with off-block Hessian regularization. On Llama 2 (7B-70B) and Llama 3.2 (1B-3B), HARP consistently improves perplexity and zero-shot accuracy at 2-4 bits (e.g., Llama 2 7B 2-bit W2 PPL: 8.22 -> 7.23), outperforms AWQ/GPTQ/OmniQuant in context-matched comparisons, and preserves inference throughput at 128 tok/s vs 61 tok/s for FP16, at the cost of ~4-10 GPU-hours of calibration per model.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了 HARP（Hadamard-preconditioned Adaptive Rotation Processor），一种可学习的结构化双面正交旋转处理器，替代 PTQ 中固定的随机 Hadamard 变换（RHT），实现按层、按量化器自适应旋转基。
- 利用稀疏蝶形块正交阶段（butterfly-like block-orthogonal stages）参数化旋转矩阵，通过 Mixed-Radix 调度支持非 2 的幂次维度（如 d=5120 分解为 (8,8,8,5,2)），参数量 O(dm)，应用代价 O(d log d)，与 Hadamard 变换同级。
- 以 RHT 精确初始化（Theorem 4 证明 Theta=0 时等价于 Hadamard 族预处理），仅需校准数据拟合（每层 1200 步 Adam，约 4-10 GPU-hours/模型），不引入额外训练开销。
- 设计了 Hessian 加权对角重建代理损失（L_diag）和块外 Hessian 能量正则化（R_bd），避免了在优化器中嵌套 LDLQ 量化步骤。
- 在 Llama 2 (7B/13B/70B) 和 Llama 3.2 (1B/3B) 的 2-4 bit 量化中一致优于固定 RHT 和 AWQ/GPTQ/OmniQuant 基线。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces HARP, a learnable structured two-sided orthogonal processor that replaces fixed RHT in QuIP#-style PTQ, enabling per-layer and per-quantizer rotation basis adaptation while preserving exact full-precision equivalence.
- Parameterizes rotations as a product of sparse butterfly-like block-orthogonal stages (Givens for radix-2, Cayley map for radix > 2) with Mixed-Radix scheduling for non-power-of-two dimensions, achieving O(dm) parameters and O(d log d) application cost.
- Proves exact RHT recovery at initialization (Theorem 4), enabling stable gradient-based learning from a strong starting point rather than learning mixing from scratch.
- Designs a lightweight fitting objective: a diagonal Hessian-weighted reconstruction proxy (L_diag, Eq. 14) with off-block Hessian energy regularization (R_bd, Eq. 15), using stopped-gradient quantized targets to avoid nesting LDLQ inside the optimizer.
- Demonstrates consistent perplexity and zero-shot accuracy improvements at 2-4 bits on Llama 2 (7B-70B) and Llama 3.2 (1B-3B), outperforming AWQ/GPTQ/OmniQuant in context-matched comparisons while preserving 128 tok/s inference throughput.

## Methodology

HARP addresses the core limitation of existing incoherence-based PTQ methods: fixed randomized Hadamard transforms cannot adapt to specific layer weight curvature, activation outlier distributions, or quantizer characteristics. The method is structured as follows:

**1. Two-sided orthogonal processing.** For each linear layer with weight W, HARP applies learnable orthogonal transforms on both sides: W~ = U(Theta_U)^T W V(Theta_V), H~ = V(Theta_V)^T H V(Theta_V), where H is the second-moment (Hessian proxy) matrix. This preserves exact full-precision equivalence since orthogonal rotations are norm-preserving.

**2. Butterfly-like block-orthogonal factorization.** Each rotation T(Theta) is decomposed into m stride stages, each mixing contiguous groups of size b_t (the radix). Stage t applies block-orthogonal matrices B_{t,c}(theta) = Q_{t,c}(theta) * G_{b_t}, where G_{b_t} is a fixed base mixer (Hadamard for power-of-two radices, QR-based fallback otherwise) and Q_{t,c} is a learnable orthogonal perturbation. For radix-2, Q is a 2x2 Givens rotation parameterized by a single angle; for larger radices, Q uses the Cayley map Q(theta) = (I + A)^{-1}(I - A) where A is a skew-symmetric matrix.

**3. Mixed-Radix scheduling.** For non-power-of-two dimensions (e.g., d=5120), the dimension is decomposed into mixed radices (e.g., (8,8,8,5,2)) using a greedy algorithm that prefers radix-8 stages. An optional Kronecker fallback handles dimensions of the form K * 2^L by applying a fixed Hadamard-like table along the K-axis and HARP along the 2^L-axis.

**4. Exact RHT initialization.** Theorem 4 proves that at Theta=0, each Q_{t,c} = I, so B_{t,c} = G_{b_t} = H_{b_t}, and the stride stages exactly recover the Hadamard stride factorization H_d^{stride} = P^T H_d P. This makes HARP a drop-in upgrade for existing Hadamard-based pipelines.

**5. Fitting procedure.** Per-layer optimization uses Adam (lr=3e-2, 1200 steps) on a surrogate objective L_fit = L_diag + lambda_bd * R_bd. The diagonal Hessian-weighted reconstruction proxy L_diag measures the weighted MSE between rotated weights and their stopped-gradient codebook targets. The off-block Hessian regularizer R_bd penalizes Hessian energy outside the quantizer's block structure. Quantized targets are refreshed every T optimizer steps (T=1 by default, T=4 reduces calibration from ~9.7h to ~4.0h with minimal quality loss).

**6. Deployment.** After fitting, HARP parameters can be stored in int8 form using per-block scaling, reducing storage overhead with negligible perplexity impact. The staged rotations run at O(d log d) cost, similar to the fixed Hadamard transform they replace.

## Results & Highlights

**Perplexity (Table 1, main comparison with QuIP# backend, context 4096 for Llama 2, 8192 for Llama 3.2):**
- Llama 2 7B 2-bit: RHT 8.22 -> HARP 7.23 (W2 PPL), a 12% relative improvement
- Llama 2 13B 2-bit: RHT 6.47 -> HARP 5.98 (W2 PPL)
- Llama 3.2 1B 2-bit: RHT 13.53 -> HARP 11.79 (W2 PPL), 13% relative improvement
- Gains are consistent at 3-bit and 4-bit settings but smaller in absolute terms as RHT baseline is already closer to FP16

**Context-matched comparison (Table 6, context 2048):**
- At 2-bit: HARP (W2 PPL 7.85) outperforms AWQ (diverged), GPTQ (36.77), OmniQuant (11.06), and QuIP#-RHT (8.95) on Llama 2 7B
- At 3-bit: HARP (5.89) beats AWQ (6.24), GPTQ (6.29), OmniQuant (6.03), and RHT (6.00)
- At 4-bit: All methods converge near FP16; HARP remains slightly better (5.59 vs 5.64 for RHT on W2)

**Zero-shot accuracy (Table 2, Llama 2):**
- At 2-bit, HARP improves ARC-Challenge, ARC-Easy, and WinoGrande over RHT on most model sizes
- Largest gains on ARC benchmarks

**Inference throughput (Table 3, RTX 5080, batch=1, seq_len=1):**
- FP16: 61 tok/s
- HARP 2-bit: 128 tok/s (preserves most of the RHT throughput advantage)
- The staged rotations add only modest latency overhead compared to fixed RHT

**Storage overhead (Table 8):**
- Llama 2 7B 2-bit: RHT 2.15 GB -> HARP 2.41 GB (float) / 2.20 GB (int8)
- Llama 2 70B 2-bit: RHT 18.18 GB -> HARP 19.21 GB (float) / 18.38 GB (int8)
- int8 parameter storage nearly matches RHT model size

**Calibration cost (Table 7, Llama 2 7B 2-bit, H100):**
- RHT baseline: ~0.7 GPU-hours
- HARP (refresh every step): ~9.7 GPU-hours
- HARP (refresh every 4 steps): ~4.0 GPU-hours, W2 PPL 7.25 (vs 7.235 with full refresh)

**Ablations:**
- Radix choice (Table 10): Larger radix (b=16) gives better perplexity (7.17) but higher BPP (2.15 vs 2.08 for b=2); b=8 offers the best tradeoff
- Base mixer (Table 11): Hadamard preconditioning (7.23) outperforms identity base mixer (7.29)
- HARP+FT-quant (Table 12): Even without end-to-end fine-tuning, HARP with FT-quant only (6.16) beats RHT with full fine-tuning (6.19) on Llama 2 7B 2-bit

**Diagnostic analysis (Table 9):**
- HARP improves weight incoherence, off-block Hessian energy, and diagonal Hessian-weighted distortion in >95% of modules
- Interestingly, HARP does NOT improve the classical Hessian incoherence score (mu_H); this is consistent with HARP optimizing for quantizer-aligned rotation rather than generic incoherence

**QTIP compatibility:** HARP can be inserted into the QTIP backend, demonstrating portability across RHT-based PTQ pipelines.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Artur Zagitov, Gleb Molodtsov, Aleksandr Beznosikov** from BRAIn Lab, Moscow, Russia. Beznosikov has prior publications in optimization theory and distributed optimization for machine learning.
- The paper builds directly on QuIP# (Tseng et al., 2024) and uses its codebase, suggesting deep familiarity with the prior work.
- The paper includes a formal proof of exact RHT initialization equivalence (Theorem 4), detailed algorithmic pseudocode (Algorithms 1-3), and an extensive reproducibility checklist (Appendix I) with hardware specs, hyperparameters, and planned code release.
- Experiments are carefully scoped: the primary comparison isolates a single component (the incoherence processor) inside a fixed QuIP# backend, avoiding confounds from changing multiple design choices simultaneously.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The idea of making the incoherence processor learnable is not entirely new (SpinQuant, ButterflyQuant exist), but HARP's specific design is novel in several ways: (a) exact RHT initialization via theta=0 rather than random initialization, (b) the butterfly-like block structure with Mixed-Radix scheduling for arbitrary dimensions, (c) the lightweight fitting surrogate that avoids nesting LDLQ, and (d) the off-block Hessian regularizer that directly targets quantizer-block alignment. The formal proof of initialization equivalence is a clean theoretical contribution.

- **Rigor:** Strong. The controlled experimental design is commendable -- the primary comparison holds the quantizer backend, codebooks, solver, random signs, and evaluation pipeline fixed, varying only the processor. The context-matched comparison with AWQ/GPTQ/OmniQuant uses the same context length (2048) as those baselines. Diagnostic analysis (Table 9) probes the mechanism rather than just reporting end-to-end numbers. The calibration cost ablation (Table 7) is honest about the overhead. However, a few weaknesses: (a) the 70B results appear only in the abstract claim but the detailed tables focus on 1B-13B, with the 70B results requiring closer inspection; (b) the comparison against SpinQuant and ButterflyQuant (the most directly competing methods) is limited -- SpinQuant appears only in the 4-bit context-matched table; (c) the paper does not compare against AQLM or SqueezeLLM, which also target extreme low-bit regimes.

- **Reproducibility:** High. The reproducibility checklist (Appendix I) specifies hardware (H100 for calibration, RTX 5080 for inference), hyperparameters (lr=3e-2, 1200 Adam steps, lambda_bd=0.1), calibration data (6144 RedPajama sequences), and the authors plan to release code and quantized checkpoints. The algorithmic pseudocode is detailed enough for reimplementation.

- **Significance:** High for practical LLM deployment. The 2-bit regime is where HARP shines most (12% relative perplexity improvement on Llama 2 7B), and this is precisely the regime where deployment on memory-constrained hardware matters most. The fact that HARP can beat RHT+fine-tuning without any fine-tuning itself (Table 12) is practically significant -- it means the improvement comes "for free" from better rotation without the complexity of fine-tuning schedules.

- **Red flags / concerns:**
  (a) The calibration cost of 4-10 GPU-hours is non-trivial for very large models (70B+), and the paper does not report how this scales (presumably linearly with model size).
  (b) The effective BPP is slightly higher than nominal (e.g., 2.11 vs 2.00 for 2-bit) due to HARP parameter storage, which could matter for tight memory budgets.
  (c) All experiments use Llama 2/3.2 architectures only; generalization to other architectures (Mistral, Qwen, etc.) is not demonstrated.
  (d) The paper focuses exclusively on weight-only quantization; compatibility with weight-activation quantization is not discussed.
  (e) The comparison against ButterflyQuant (Xu et al., 2025), which also uses learnable orthogonal butterfly transforms for quantization, is conspicuously absent.

## Strengths

- Principled mathematical framework with formal proofs of initialization equivalence.
- Clean experimental design that isolates the contribution of the learned processor.
- Exact RHT initialization ensures HARP starts from a strong baseline and only improves from there.
- Practical deployment properties: O(d log d) runtime, int8 parameter storage, minimal storage overhead.
- Consistent improvements across model sizes, bit-widths, and evaluation metrics (PPL and zero-shot).
- HARP with no fine-tuning beats RHT with full fine-tuning (Table 12), demonstrating the raw value of learned rotations.
- Honest reporting of calibration cost, storage overhead, and the diagnostic finding that classical Hessian incoherence is NOT improved (Table 9).

## Limitations

- Calibration cost: 4-10 GPU-hours per model is non-trivial, especially for 70B+ models where the cost likely scales further.
- Architecture-specific evaluation: Only Llama 2/3.2 tested; results on other architectures (Mixtral/MoE, attention-free models) are unknown.
- Weight-only quantization: No exploration of weight-activation PTQ compatibility, which is important for compute-bound (not just memory-bound) deployment scenarios.
- Missing comparison with ButterflyQuant (Xu et al., 2025), which is the most directly competing approach using learnable butterfly transforms for quantization.
- The effective bitrate is slightly above nominal (2.05-2.11 BPP for 2-bit), which could be problematic for extremely tight memory budgets.
- The fitting procedure requires precomputed Hessian/second-moment statistics (6144 RedPajama sequences), adding a preprocessing step.
- At 4 bits, all methods are near FP16; HARP's advantage diminishes to the point of diminishing returns.

## Relevance

HARP 直接解决了大模型极低比特量化中的核心瓶颈——固定 Hadamard 变换无法自适应层特定的权重曲率和异常值分布。其"以 RHT 初始化 + 梯度优化旋转基"的范式对 PTQ 流水线设计有启发性，尤其是 HARP+无微调超越 RHT+全微调的结果值得关注。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does HARP interact with mixture-of-experts (MoE) architectures like Mixtral, where different experts may have very different weight distributions?
2. Can the HARP fitting procedure be made more efficient via meta-learning or weight-sharing across layers, reducing the per-layer calibration cost?
3. What happens when HARP is applied to weight-activation quantization (e.g., W4A4 or W2A8 settings)? Does the learned rotation help with activation outliers as well?
4. How does HARP compare against ButterflyQuant (Xu et al., 2025) in a controlled head-to-head comparison? Both use learnable butterfly orthogonal transforms, but with different parameterizations and objectives.
5. Can the Mixed-Radix butterfly structure be extended to support block sizes that adapt per-layer (e.g., larger blocks for layers with more heterogeneous distributions)?
6. Is the finding that HARP does NOT improve classical Hessian incoherence (Table 9) a feature or a limitation? Does this mean there is room for a multi-objective fitting procedure that improves both quantizer alignment and generic incoherence?
