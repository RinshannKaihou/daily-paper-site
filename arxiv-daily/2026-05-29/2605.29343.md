> **一句话总结：** 本文提出Draft-OPD，一种针对投机解码中草稿模型的在线策略蒸馏框架，通过记录验证过程中的错误位置并回放草稿模型自身的提议来训练，解决了离线SFT训练的"训练-推理不匹配"问题，在Qwen3系列思维模型上实现了超过5倍的无损加速，比EAGLE-3和DFlash分别提升23%和13%。

> **TL;DR:** Draft-OPD addresses the fundamental training-inference mismatch in speculative decoding draft models. Standard SFT trains drafters on fixed target-model trajectories, but during inference they are evaluated on their own draft-induced states. Draft-OPD uses target-assisted rollouts to collect stable sequences, records error positions from speculative verification, and replays drafting from those anchors to train on both accepted and rejected proposals using an acceptance-aware KL objective. On Qwen3 models (4B/8B/30B-A3B-Thinking), it achieves over 5x lossless speedup, improving acceptance length by ~11% over DFlash across tasks and serving configurations, with particularly strong gains on thinking-mode reasoning.

## 核心贡献

- 识别了草稿模型离线SFT训练的关键瓶颈：SFT在固定目标模型轨迹上训练，但投机解码时草稿模型在自身策略诱导的状态上被评估，导致"离线-推理不匹配"，SFT训练快速饱和
- 解释了标准在线策略蒸馏(OPD)无法直接用于草稿模型的原因：草稿模型无法独立完成高质量的自回归生成（输出退化），而目标模型辅助的生成又消除了在线策略信号
- 提出Draft-OPD框架：使用目标模型辅助的投机解码收集稳定轨迹，记录每个草稿块的起始位置作为锚点，回放从这些锚点开始的草稿生成过程，在草稿模型实际访问的状态上计算蒸馏损失
- 设计了接受感知的蒸馏目标：对被接受的token使用前向KL（覆盖目标分布），对被拒绝的token使用反向KL（惩罚草稿模型的高概率错误模式），并使用指数衰减权重使更早的拒绝位置获得更高权重
- 在Qwen3系列思维模型上实现超过5倍无损加速，相比EAGLE-3和DFlash分别提升23%和13%

## Key Contributions

- Identifies the fundamental plateau in offline SFT training for speculative decoding draft models: the training-inference mismatch between fixed target trajectories and draft-policy-induced evaluation states
- Diagnoses why standard on-policy distillation fails for draft models: self-rollouts degenerate into repetitions, while target-assisted rollouts erase the on-policy signal by discarding rejected tokens
- Proposes Draft-OPD with three coordinated components: (1) rollout with error-position anchor collection during speculative decoding, (2) replay from anchors to compute log-probabilities for both accepted and rejected draft tokens, (3) an acceptance-aware distillation objective using forward KL for accepted tokens and reverse KL for rejected tokens
- Achieves over 5x lossless speedup on thinking-mode models (Qwen3-4B/8B/30B-A3B), improving over EAGLE-3 by 23% and DFlash by 13% under matched FLOPs
- Demonstrates practical serving improvements on SGLang with consistent throughput gains across concurrency levels (1-32), including up to 17% speedup on Qwen3-30B-A3B-Thinking

## Methodology

**Problem Setting:** Speculative decoding pairs a target model with a lightweight draft model. The draft model proposes K tokens, and the target verifies them in parallel. The key metric is acceptance length (tau), the average number of draft tokens accepted per verification round.

**Identified Issue:** SFT training on target-generated trajectories plateaus because (1) the drafter learns on target-induced states but is evaluated on its own draft-induced states, and (2) continuing SFT on more data does not break through this plateau.

**Draft-OPD Pipeline (3 steps):**

1. **Rollout with Error-Position Collection:** Run speculative decoding normally. The draft model proposes blocks of K tokens at each step. Record the starting position of each block (anchor). The target model verifies and produces a high-quality rollout. Anchors capture where the draft model actually acted.

2. **Replay for Log-Probability Computation:** From each anchor, replay the draft-generated block. For each drafted token, compute both the draft model's log-probability and the target model's log-probability on the same draft-induced prefix. This includes tokens that were rejected during verification -- the most informative training signals that standard SFT discards.

3. **Acceptance-Aware Distillation Objective:** Partition drafted tokens into accepted (I_acc) and rejected (I_rej) sets. For accepted tokens, use forward KL divergence (encouraging the draft distribution to cover the target). For rejected tokens, use reverse KL divergence with exponentially decaying weights by position (penalizing high-probability draft modes where the target disagrees). The final objective averages both losses with equal weighting.

**Implementation Details:** Draft-OPD is applied on top of SFT-trained DFlash draft models (5 Transformer layers for Qwen3-4B/8B, 8 layers for Qwen3-30B-A3B). Uses a 16K prompt pool sampled from GSM8K, MATH, AoPS, and CodeAlpaca. Block size is 16 for both training and inference. Training uses NVIDIA H200 GPUs with FlashAttention.

## Results & Highlights

- **Main speedup results (Table 1):** Draft-OPD achieves 5.55x speedup on MATH-500 (Qwen3-4B thinking mode) vs. DFlash's 4.73x (+17.4%), and 3.18x on MT-Bench vs. 2.70x (+17.8%). On HumanEval, 6.57x vs. 5.57x (+18.0%).
- **Acceptance length gains:** Average acceptance length improves by ~11.2% across all model-task pairs on SGLang.
- **Thinking model results:** Particularly strong improvements on Qwen3-30B-A3B-Thinking-2507, where acceptance length improves from 4.54 to 5.32 on AIME25 (+17.2%).
- **Serving throughput:** On SGLang with FA3 backend, Draft-OPD consistently improves throughput across concurrency levels 1-32, with gains that do not diminish under higher concurrency.
- **Ablation -- Training data:** OPD gains are not explained by additional supervised data alone. Continued SFT on the same OPD prompt pool underperforms Draft-OPD.
- **Ablation -- KL design:** The mixed forward/reverse KL objective outperforms both all-forward and all-reverse variants. All-reverse KL performs worst.
- **Ablation -- Anchor positions:** Error-position replay outperforms randomly selected anchors, confirming that concentrating training on verification-exposed errors is critical.
- **Thinking mode analysis:** Thinking-mode responses have higher next-token NLL than non-thinking responses across datasets, explaining why drafting for thinking models is inherently harder. Draft-OPD partially mitigates this challenge.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** The identification of the SFT plateau for draft models and the specific diagnosis of why standard OPD fails (degenerate self-rollouts vs. target-assisted rollout erasing on-policy signal) is a genuine contribution. The anchor-based replay mechanism is well-motivated and elegantly designed to preserve the on-policy signal while using target-quality rollouts.
- **Rigor (High):** The paper provides clear formalization of the problem, detailed algorithm description with equations, and thorough ablations covering training data, KL direction, and anchor selection. Comparisons use matched FLOPs budgets for fairness.
- **Reproducibility (Moderate):** Implementation details are provided (GPU type, batch size, block size), and hyperparameters are referenced in an appendix. However, the training infrastructure depends on specific frameworks (SpecForge, SGLang), and the full training pipeline involves a two-stage process (SFT warm-up followed by OPD) that requires careful engineering. No code release is mentioned.
- **Significance (High):** Speculative decoding is a critical technique for LLM deployment efficiency. Improving draft model quality through better training methodology has immediate practical impact. The 23% and 13% improvements over strong baselines are substantial.
- **Red flags:** The evaluation is limited to the Qwen3 model family. It is unclear how well Draft-OPD generalizes to other architectures (Llama, Mistral, etc.). The OPD training stage requires online interaction with the target model, which is significantly more expensive than offline SFT. The paper does not clearly report the additional computational cost of the OPD stage compared to SFT-only training. The acceptance length gains on non-thinking tasks (MT-Bench) are smaller, suggesting the method may be less impactful for standard (non-reasoning) generation workloads.

## Strengths

- Clear identification and diagnosis of the SFT plateau problem for draft models, with empirical evidence (Figure 1 showing the plateau)
- Elegant solution design that preserves both stable rollouts and on-policy training signal through anchor-based replay
- Acceptance-aware KL objective is well-justified: forward KL for accepted tokens maintains coverage, reverse KL for rejected tokens penalizes errors
- Comprehensive ablations isolating the contribution of each design choice
- Practical deployment results on SGLang demonstrating real-world throughput gains
- Particularly effective for thinking-mode models, which are increasingly important in practice

## Limitations

- Evaluated only on Qwen3 family; generalization to other model architectures is untested
- OPD training requires online target-model inference, which is computationally expensive (cost not clearly quantified)
- Gains on non-thinking tasks (e.g., MT-Bench) are more modest (3.18x vs. 2.70x)
- No code or model release announced, limiting immediate community adoption
- The method is designed for lossless verification only; extension to approximate speculative decoding is left as future work
- The thinking-mode drafting gap analysis (Appendix C) reveals a fundamental challenge that Draft-OPD only partially addresses

## Relevance

本文研究了LLM推理加速中的投机解码技术，提出了针对草稿模型训练的在线蒸馏方法。对于关注LLM推理效率优化、模型蒸馏和推理系统加速的研究方向有直接参考价值，特别是其中对训练-推理不匹配问题的分析和错误位置回放的训练范式设计。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does the computational cost of the OPD training stage (including online target-model inference) compare quantitatively to the SFT stage? What is the total training cost increase?
2. Would Draft-OPD transfer to other model families (Llama 4, Mistral, DeepSeek) with different draft model architectures?
3. Can the anchor-based replay mechanism be combined with reinforcement learning objectives to directly optimize for acceptance length rather than distributional matching?
4. How does Draft-OPD interact with dynamic draft block sizes or adaptive speculation strategies?
5. Is there a theoretical upper bound on acceptance length improvement that on-policy methods can achieve over offline SFT, given the architectural constraints of training-based draft models?
