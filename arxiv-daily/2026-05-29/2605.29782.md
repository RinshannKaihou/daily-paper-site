> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示了PPO在LLM RL中critic网络退化为粗糙组平均基线的现象，提出基于数值跨度里程碑的Numca和基于隐状态相似度加权平均的Hista两种方法，在SVEB基准和多个下游任务上显著提升状态价值估计精度与训练效果，发表于ICML 2026。

> **TL;DR:** This paper shows that PPO critics in LLM RL collapse to group-average baselines, proposes two fixes -- Numca (uses numerical spans as gradable milestones for credit assignment in math tasks) and Hista (uses LLM hidden states to probability-weight-average disjoint rollouts for general tasks, with theoretical guarantees) -- and demonstrates consistent improvements across DAPO/GSPO/CSIPO algorithms and Qwen2.5/Qwen3 models (0.5B-14B) on math benchmarks. Published at ICML 2026.

## 核心贡献

- 提出SVEB（State Value Estimation Benchmark），通过大规模蒙特卡洛采样构建真实状态价值参考值，系统评估LLM RL中状态价值估计质量
- 揭示PPO的critic网络在LLM场景下系统性退化为组平均基线，丧失了逐token精细信用分配能力
- 提出Numca方法：受Hindsight Experience Replay启发，利用数学推理中数值跨度作为可解析的中间里程碑，将稀疏奖励问题转化为更密集的目标达成问题
- 提出Hista框架：利用LLM最后一层隐状态（经EMA压缩和MinDistance度量）对不相关rollout进行概率加权平均，提供有理论保证的更优状态价值估计（Theorem 5.5证明其偏差不大于朴素平均）
- 在DAPO/GSPO/CSIPO三种算法、Qwen2.5(0.5B-14B)和Qwen3(0.6B-8B)模型上验证，Hista在SVEB五个领域MAE全面降低，下游任务平均提升0.5-2.3个点

## Key Contributions

- Introduce SVEB, a benchmark that uses large-scale Monte Carlo sampling (MCS@20) to create ground-truth state value references across 5 reasoning domains (Number, Math, Science, General QA, Programming)
- Identify that PPO critics in LLM RL systematically collapse to group-average baselines, empirically validated across multiple data sources and training regimes (PPO-I vs PPO-N)
- Propose Numca: a heuristic method using numerical spans as gradable milestones for state value estimation in mathematical reasoning, inspired by Hindsight Experience Replay
- Propose Hista: a general framework that extracts last-layer hidden states, compresses them via EMA, computes MinDistance between states, and performs probability-weighted averaging of disjoint rollouts with theoretical guarantees (Theorem 5.5)
- Demonstrate that Hista achieves computational efficiency comparable to GRPO with O(T/d^2) space complexity, and consistently improves SVEB MAE scores and downstream benchmark performance across DAPO, GSPO, and CSIPO algorithms on Qwen2.5 (0.5B-14B) and Qwen3 (0.6B-8B) models

## Methodology

**Problem formulation:** LLM autoregressive generation is modeled as an MDP where states are partial sequences, actions are token selections, and reward is sparse (only at termination). The key insight is that in GRPO and its successors (DAPO, GSPO, CSIPO), the entire response is treated as a single action with a group-averaged reward baseline, eliminating fine-grained credit assignment.

**SVEB construction:** States are sampled from rollouts of a fixed policy, and ground-truth values are estimated via Monte Carlo sampling with n=20 continuations per state. Quality is measured by MAE against these references across 5 domains.

**Numca:** Reframes math problem solving as a goal-reaching task. Numbers appearing in reasoning traces serve as parsable milestones. Disjoint rollouts sharing the same numerical intermediate values are grouped, and their terminal rewards are averaged to estimate the state value at that milestone.

**Hista pipeline (4 stages):**
1. Extract last-layer hidden states from the LLM for each rollout's terminal state
2. Compress via EMA with smoothing factor alpha and interval phi, then sample at interval delta to define a finite state space
3. Compute MinDistance between states sharing the same prompt in a global state space, select k nearest neighbors
4. Estimate state value as inverse-distance weighted average of neighbors' terminal rewards: V(s_t) = sum(w_i * r_i) / sum(w_i), where w_i = 1/MD(s_t, s_{t,i})

The key theoretical result (Theorem 5.5) proves that the probability-weighted estimator has no larger absolute bias than the naive average estimator.

## Results & Highlights

**SVEB Results (Table 4, MAE lower is better):** Hista@40 achieves the best MAE across all 5 domains compared to GRPO@40 and PPO-N@40:
- Number: 0.142 (vs GRPO 0.175, PPO-N 0.159)
- Math: 0.145 (vs GRPO 0.208, PPO-N 0.187)
- Science: 0.173 (vs GRPO 0.215, PPO-N 0.198)
- General: 0.157 (vs GRPO 0.202, PPO-N 0.185)
- Programming: 0.119 (vs GRPO 0.157, PPO-N 0.144)

Numca improves on Number domain (0.132) but degrades on other domains, confirming its domain-specific nature.

**Downstream benchmarks (Qwen2.5, Table 5):** Hista+DAPO improves average score over DAPO across model sizes:
- 0.5B: +1.6 average (19.6 to 21.2)
- 1.5B: +0.7 average (37.3 to 38.0)
- 3B: +0.6 average (45.2 to 45.8)
- 7B: +0.5 average (53.1 to 53.6)
- 14B: +1.1 average (57.2 to 58.3)

**Qwen3 results (Table 8):** Hista+DAPO shows consistent gains:
- 0.6B: +2.3 average (32.5 to 34.8), including +8.0 on AMC
- 4B: +1.3 average (56.1 to 57.4)
- 8B: +1.5 average (60.7 to 62.2), including +3.0 on MATH

**Notable:** 1.7B Qwen3 model shows a slight regression (-0.4 average), which the authors attribute to RL stochasticity and OOD benchmarks. Training dynamics show Hista achieves faster convergence and more stable entropy.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The formal characterization of PPO critic collapse in LLM settings is a genuine contribution. Hista's use of hidden-state similarity for probability-weighted value estimation is clever and well-motivated by the connection to goal-conditioned RL.
- **Rigor:** Strong. The SVEB benchmark with MCS@20 references provides a principled evaluation framework. Theorem 5.5 provides theoretical backing. However, the assumption (B.2) required for the proof should be scrutinized -- it effectively requires that probability weights are correlated with proximity to the true state value, which may not always hold.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code is released. Hyperparameter ablations are provided in Appendix E. Training details are in Appendix G.
- **Significance:** Meaningful but not transformative. The improvements are consistent but modest (+0.5 to +2.3 average on benchmarks). The per-benchmark results are noisy -- some benchmarks show regressions even when the average improves.
- **Red flags:** (1) The 1.7B Qwen3 regression is hand-waved away as "RL stochasticity and OOD" without deeper investigation. (2) Numca is presented as a contribution but only works on one domain (Number) and degrades others, making it more of a motivation for Hista than a standalone contribution. (3) The paper does not compare against process reward models (PRMs) or Monte Carlo tree search methods, which are the main alternatives for fine-grained credit assignment. (4) Hista's reliance on hidden state similarity assumes that semantically similar states have similar hidden representations, which may break down for longer contexts or more diverse tasks. (5) No experiments on models larger than 14B.

## Strengths

- Cleanly identifies and formally characterizes a real, underappreciated problem (critic collapse in LLM PPO)
- SVEB is a valuable diagnostic tool for the community, with a principled construction methodology
- Hista is elegant in its simplicity -- no additional model training, no extra rollouts, just hidden-state similarity
- Theoretical guarantee (Theorem 5.5) provides formal backing rather than just empirical validation
- Thorough experimental coverage across 3 algorithms, multiple model families, and many benchmarks
- Published at ICML 2026, indicating peer-reviewed quality

## Limitations

- Improvements are consistent but modest in magnitude; individual benchmark results are noisy with some regressions
- Numca is domain-specific (only math) and can hurt performance on non-math tasks
- No comparison against PRMs or MCTS-based credit assignment methods
- The 1.7B Qwen3 regression is unexplained beyond hand-waving
- No experiments on models larger than 14B -- scalability to frontier-scale models is unknown
- Hista requires access to hidden states, which may not be available for all model architectures or API-accessed models
- The theoretical guarantee requires assumptions that may not hold in all practical settings

## Relevance

本文直接解决了LLM强化学习后训练中信用分配不精细的核心问题，提出的Hista方法无需额外训练或rollout即可改进GRPO/DAPO等主流算法，对于从事LLM RL训练和推理能力提升的研究者具有直接的实用参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does Hista interact with process reward models -- can they be combined for even better credit assignment?
- Does hidden-state similarity remain meaningful for very long reasoning chains (10k+ tokens) or agentic RL settings with tool use?
- Can Hista's MinDistance metric be improved with contrastive learning on hidden states?
- What happens when Hista is applied to multi-turn RL or agentic RL settings where rewards come from environment interaction?
- How sensitive is Hista to the compression hyperparameters (EMA smoothing alpha, compression interval phi, sampling interval delta)?
- Would combining Numca and Hista (Numca milestones as anchors + Hista for intermediate states) yield further gains in math tasks?
