> **一句话总结：** 本文通过三组路由探针实验证明MoE大模型的安全行为与路由机制解耦（路由由话题驱动而非安全驱动），据此提出RASET红队框架，利用对比路由敏感性准则定位5-8个安全关键专家并仅微调这些专家（仅修改0.12%-0.95%参数），在保持路由行为基本不变的情况下达到50.5%的高质量攻击成功率，超出最强基线37.6个百分点。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides strong empirical evidence that MoE LLM routing is topic-driven, not safety-driven — refusal vs. compliance produces negligible routing shifts while topic changes cause large ones. Leveraging this, the authors propose RASET (Router-Agnostic Safety-critical Expert Tuning), which identifies safety-critical experts via a contrastive routing-sensitivity criterion and applies parameter-efficient tuning to only 5-8 experts (0.12%-0.95% of total parameters). Across 5 open-weight MoE models (OLMoE, DeepSeek-V2-Lite, Qwen3-30B, Phi-3.5-MoE, GPT-oss-20b), RASET achieves 50.5% average ASR_hq under the strictest quality-qualified metric, outperforming the strongest baseline by 37.6 points while preserving routing stability and benign utility far better than router-manipulation approaches.

## 核心贡献

- 通过三组互补探针实验（teacher-forced行为对比、提示级拒绝风格对比、匹配安全意图对比）系统证明MoE路由由话题驱动而非安全驱动
- 提出RASET红队框架：利用对比路由敏感性分数 S_{l,i} = A(l,i; D_harm) - lambda * A(l,i; D_norm) 定位安全关键专家
- 仅微调5-8个安全关键专家（占总参数0.12%-0.95%），冻结路由器、共享组件和未选中专家
- 设计双策略损失函数：同时抑制拒绝模式（最大间隔惩罚）并促进合规前缀，配合L2正则化保持通用能力
- 在5个不同规模和路由设计的MoE模型上验证，ASR_hq达50.5%，超出最强基线37.6个百分点
- 路由稳定性验证：RASET前后路由JS散度低于0.04，专家重叠偏差低于8%，与模型内在路由方差相当

## Key Contributions

- Provides systematic empirical evidence through three complementary routing probes (teacher-forced behavioral contrast, prompt-level refusal-style contrast, matched safety-intent contrast) that MoE routing is driven by semantic topic, not safety behavior
- Proposes the contrastive routing-sensitivity score S_{l,i} = A(l,i; D_harm) - lambda * A(l,i; D_norm) to identify safety-critical experts, distinguishing from prior work that uses raw routing scores or ablation-based methods
- Introduces RASET, which freezes the router and shared components while tuning only 5-8 identified experts (0.12%-0.95% of total parameters)
- Designs a dual-strategy loss: controlled safety-boundary violation (affirmative prefix supervision + max-margin refusal suppression) combined with capability preservation (benign NLL constraint + L2 weight regularization)
- Achieves 50.5% average ASR_hq across 5 MoE backbones, outperforming the strongest baseline by 37.6 points
- Verifies routing preservation post-RASET: JS divergence < 0.04, expert overlap deviation < 8%, comparable to intrinsic routing variance

## Methodology

**Routing Probes (Empirical Motivation):** Three complementary experiments demonstrate that routing is topic-driven:
1. *Teacher-forced contrast:* Same harmful prompt, refusal vs. compliant continuations — routing nearly identical (top-8 overlap close to maximum 8/8)
2. *Refusal-style probe:* Adding refusal prefix to benign prompts causes minimal routing shift; changing topic under same refusal style causes large shift
3. *Matched intent probe:* Harmful-benign pairs preserving topic/structure show much smaller routing divergence than random cross-topic baselines

**RASET Framework:**
1. *Safety-critical expert identification:* Computes contrastive routing-sensitivity score by subtracting benign activation from harmful activation per expert, ranking all experts, and selecting top-K (K=5-8 depending on model)
2. *Constrained expert-level adaptation:* Applies parameter-efficient fine-tuning exclusively to selected experts while freezing router, shared components, and all other experts
3. *Loss design:*
   - L_violate: Dual strategy maximizing NLL of affirmative prefixes while applying max-margin penalty (threshold m) on refusal patterns
   - L_preserve: Benign NLL constraint + L2 regularization to keep tuned weights close to original
   - Total: L_total = L_violate + L_preserve
4. *Training details:* 250 harmful + 250 benign samples, 500 training steps, loss weights gamma_aff=0.4, gamma_ref=0.25, gamma_norm=0.3, gamma_l2=0.05

**Evaluation:** Three-tier ASR metrics (ASR_raw, ASR_valid, ASR_hq) combining safeguard violation (HarmBench classifier), paragraph validity (rule-based), and quality score (GPT-4o judge, 5-point StrongREJECT rubric). General capability assessed on TruthfulQA and MMLU.

## Results & Highlights

- **Red-teaming effectiveness:** RASET achieves 78.6% ASR_raw, 74.0% ASR_valid, and 50.5% ASR_hq on average across 5 models, outperforming all baselines including GCG adversarial attacks and router-manipulation methods
- **Parameter efficiency:** Modifies only 0.12%-0.95% of model parameters (5-8 experts depending on model architecture)
- **Routing preservation:** Post-RASET routing JS divergence below 0.04 on benign tasks, expert overlap above 7.0/8; even on harmful queries where output flips from refusal to compliance, routing remains largely intact (JS divergence 0.0811, overlap 5.66/8)
- **Utility preservation:** Average drop of 5.4 points on TruthfulQA (68.9% to 63.4%) and 7.5 points on MMLU (69.9% to 62.4%), substantially better than router-manipulation baselines
- **Models tested:** OLMoE-1B-7B, DeepSeek-V2-Lite-Chat (2.4B/15.7B), Qwen3-30B-A3B (3.3B/30.5B), Phi-3.5-MoE (6.6B/42B), GPT-oss-20b (3.6B/21B)

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The finding that safety is decoupled from routing is counter-intuitive and rigorously supported by three independent probes. The contrastive routing-sensitivity criterion for expert identification is a genuine methodological advance over raw-activation or ablation-based approaches.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Three complementary routing probes with proper controls, multi-tier evaluation metrics with human evaluation validation, routing stability verification calibrated against intrinsic variance. The loss design is principled with max-margin penalty to prevent optimization instability.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Hyperparameters (K, lambda, loss weights, training steps) are clearly specified. All models are open-weight. The evaluation framework uses standard benchmarks (AdvBench, JailbreakBench, TruthfulQA, MMLU). Code release not mentioned but implementation details are sufficient.
- **Significance:** High. Reveals a fundamental structural vulnerability in MoE architectures — safety concentrated in a few experts that can be precisely targeted. This has immediate implications for MoE safety alignment research.
- **Red flags:** The paper tests open-weight models only; results on proprietary MoE models (e.g., GPT-4) are unknown. The baselines section in the HTML version was incomplete (cut off after GCG description), making full comparison difficult. Defense mechanisms are mentioned only briefly in the conclusion without concrete proposals.

## Strengths

- Elegant and counter-intuitive finding that fundamentally reframes how we think about MoE safety
- Three routing probes provide compelling and consistent evidence from different angles
- RASET is extremely parameter-efficient (0.12%-0.95%) yet highly effective (50.5% ASR_hq)
- Routing preservation verification is thorough, with calibration against intrinsic model variance
- Evaluation across 5 diverse MoE architectures strengthens generalizability claims
- Multi-tier ASR metrics separate raw compliance from high-quality harmful output

## Limitations

- Only open-weight models tested; proprietary MoE models may have different safety architectures
- The paper focuses on red-teaming; defensive countermeasures are left entirely to future work
- Some utility degradation remains (5.4-7.5 point drops on TruthfulQA/MMLU), though less than baselines
- The number of safety-critical experts (5-8) is chosen per model but the sensitivity of this choice is not deeply analyzed
- Training requires access to model internals (routing weights), limiting applicability to API-only settings
- The approach modifies expert representations but does not address whether these experts could be hardened against such tuning

## Relevance

本文对MoE大模型安全机制的深入研究与LLM安全对齐方向高度相关，揭示了MoE架构下安全行为的局部性特征（集中在5-8个专家），为设计更鲁棒的专家感知安全对齐方法提供了重要启示，特别是在如何识别和加固安全关键专家方面。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the contrastive routing-sensitivity criterion be inverted for defensive use — i.e., identifying and explicitly strengthening safety-critical experts during alignment training?
- Are the same experts safety-critical across different categories of harmful content (violence, hate speech, self-harm, etc.), or does each category have distinct safety experts?
- How stable are the identified safety-critical experts across different training runs or checkpoint variations?
- Could expert-aware RLHF or DPO be designed to distribute safety enforcement across more experts, avoiding the concentrated vulnerability?
- How does the RASET vulnerability compare in severity to dense-model fine-tuning attacks — is the MoE-specific vulnerability truly worse?
- What is the minimum number of experts that need to be compromised to achieve significant attack success, and does this reveal a fundamental safety-capacity tradeoff in MoE designs?
