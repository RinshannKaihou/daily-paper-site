> **一句话总结：** 本文揭示了LLM推理系统（推理引擎、注意力后端、GPU硬件）由于浮点运算的非结合性产生的微小数值偏差会传播到可观测的文本输出中，据此提出一种基于提示-响应对的指纹识别方法，在确定性解码下实现100%准确率，在随机解码（T>0）下仍可达76-80%的准确率。

> **TL;DR:** The authors show that numerical deviations caused by floating-point non-associativity across different LLM inference stacks (engine, attention backend, GPU) propagate to observable text outputs. They craft four types of prompts targeting distinct computational stages (autoregressive decoding, prefill, chunked prefill, KV-cache reuse), encode responses into feature vectors, and train random forest classifiers to identify the inference engine, attention backend, and GPU type. At T=0, all components are identified perfectly; at T>0, inference engine identification remains ~80% accurate. Defending against this fingerprinting is fundamentally hard because it would require eliminating all numerical differences across hardware and software stacks.

## 核心贡献

- **首次提出LLM推理系统指纹识别方法**：仅需黑盒的文本提示-响应对（无需访问logits或内部状态），即可识别推理引擎（vLLM/SGLang/TensorRT-LLM/LMDeploy）、注意力后端（FlashAttention/FlashInfer等6种）和GPU类型（A100/H100/L4）。
- **设计四类针对性提示集**：分别针对推理过程中的四个计算阶段——自回归解码（稀有token）、预填充（二元判断）、分块预填充（长上下文）和KV缓存复用（重复任务），最大化暴露不同组件的数值指纹。
- **全面的鲁棒性评估**：验证了应用特定系统提示（~99.7%准确率）、批次大小泛化（77-99%准确率）、未知组件识别和温度泛化等实际部署场景下的有效性。
- **安全影响分析**：识别推理引擎可直接关联已知的CVE漏洞（如vLLM的RCE漏洞CVE-2026-22778和SGLang的CVE-2026-5760），指出了现实的安全威胁。
- **防御困难性论证**：从根本上消除指纹需要统一所有软硬件栈的数值计算，这在实践中不可行；添加噪声会损害模型效用。

## Key Contributions

- **First fingerprinting method for LLM inference systems**: Identifies inference engines, attention backends, and hardware platforms from black-box text-only prompt-response pairs without logit access.
- **Four targeted prompt sets**: Rare token prompts (autoregressive decoding), binary decision prompts (prefill isolation), long-context prompts (chunked prefill), and repetition prompts (KV-cache reuse) -- each designed to probe a specific computational stage.
- **Random forest classifier pipeline**: Responses are encoded via scoring functions into feature vectors, with separate classifiers trained per component label across 30 system configurations.
- **Comprehensive robustness analysis**: Demonstrates resilience to application-specific system prompts (~99.7%), batch size mismatch (77-99%), unseen proprietary components, and cross-temperature transfer.
- **Security implications**: Linking inference engine identification to known CVEs (vLLM CVE-2026-22778, SGLang CVE-2026-5760) demonstrates concrete attack vectors enabled by fingerprinting.

## Methodology

**Threat model**: The attacker knows the deployed model and system prompt (recoverable via existing attacks), has chat-only access (no logits), and has offline access to reference systems for training data collection.

**Prompt Set Construction**: Four prompt sets are designed to target distinct computational stages:
1. **P1 (Rare token prompts, 1700 prompts)**: Embed rare Unicode sequences in natural text and ask the model to retrieve them. Because the target token sits near other low-probability tokens, minor deviations during autoregressive decoding can flip predictions.
2. **P2 (Binary decision prompts, 5000 prompts)**: Yes/no questions from BoolQ that elicit a single token, isolating the prefill stage since there is virtually no autoregressive generation.
3. **P3 (Long-context prompts, 150 prompts)**: Long texts with embedded numeric identifiers that trigger chunked prefill, revealing numerical divergences from the chunking process.
4. **P4 (Repetition prompts, 100 prompts)**: Instructions to repeat sentences 100 times, triggering KV-cache prefix reuse and exposing cache management differences.

**Embedding and Classification**: Each response is mapped through set-specific scoring functions (indicator for expected tokens, binary for yes/no, normalized repetition count). The resulting per-set embedding vectors are concatenated into a single feature vector. Separate random forest classifiers are trained for inference engine (4 classes), attention backend (6 classes), and GPU type (3 classes). For stochastic decoding, l=768 samples per prompt during training and k=50 during fingerprinting, with majority voting for aggregation.

**Experimental setup**: 30 distinct system configurations from 4 inference engines x 6 attention backends x 3 GPUs (valid combinations only). Models: Qwen3-4B, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct.

## Results & Highlights

- **Deterministic decoding (T=0)**: Perfect 100% accuracy across all components and all three models. As few as 113 prompts suffice for full identification.
- **Stochastic decoding (T>0)**: Inference engine identification averages ~80% across models and temperatures (T=0.3 to T=0.9). Attention backend drops more sharply (71% at T=0.3 to 46% at T=0.9). GPU type averages 78% at T=0.3, ~59% at T=0.6/0.9.
- **Model-specific behavior**: Qwen2.5-7B is the most fingerprintable model, maintaining >77% accuracy for all components up to T=0.6. Llama-3.2 degrades fastest. Qwen3-4B shows non-monotonic behavior (partial recovery at T=0.9), likely due to its reasoning capability.
- **Sample aggregation**: Performance saturates at 10-20 aggregated samples per prompt under stochastic decoding.
- **System prompt robustness**: Application-specific system prompts reduce accuracy by only ~0.3% (99.7%).
- **Batch size generalization**: Training on batch sizes {32, 64, 128} and testing on 256 yields 77-99% accuracy depending on model and component.
- **Proprietary components**: With one component withheld from training, inference engine identification remains above random baseline (e.g., 86-95% for Qwen3-4B), while attention backend and GPU type degrade closer to chance.
- **Temperature mismatch**: Training and testing at different temperatures causes substantial accuracy drops (30-49%), motivating attackers to match temperatures.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: High. This is the first work to demonstrate that inference system components can be fingerprinted from text-only outputs. The closest prior work (Zhang et al., 2025) requires log-probability access, which is significantly more restrictive. The prompt design targeting specific computational stages is clever and well-motivated.

**Rigor**: Strong. The evaluation covers 30 system configurations, three models, four robustness dimensions, and provides both deterministic and stochastic results. The threat model is clearly stated and realistic. However, the approach assumes access to reference systems for training, which limits applicability to open-source deployments with known component catalogs.

**Reproducibility**: Excellent. All software versions, model identifiers, GPU specifications, and dataset references are provided. The method uses standard random forest classifiers and well-defined prompt templates.

**Significance**: This work opens a new side-channel attack surface for LLM deployments. The connection to real CVEs (vLLM RCE vulnerabilities) makes the security implications concrete and timely. The finding that fingerprinting is fundamentally hard to prevent (rooted in floating-point arithmetic) is an important practical insight.

**Red flags**:
- The stochastic decoding results (76-80%) are averaged over models; Llama-3.2 performs much worse (57-72% for engine), which somewhat tempers the headline claims.
- The temperature generalization experiment shows poor transferability (30-49%), meaning the attacker needs to know or infer the deployment temperature.
- The approach requires enumerating candidate components in advance, making it inapplicable to proprietary systems (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google) where the stack is unknown.
- The number of queries required (hundreds of prompts) could be detected by rate-limiting defenses, though the authors note Sybil attacks can circumvent this.

## Strengths

- Novel attack surface identification linking numerical deviations to security-relevant information leakage.
- Elegant prompt set design that systematically probes different computational stages of the inference stack.
- Comprehensive evaluation with realistic robustness tests (system prompts, batch sizes, proprietary components, temperature transfer).
- Clear security motivation tied to real-world CVEs in major inference engines.
- Honest discussion of limitations, including the requirement for reference system access and the cost of required queries.

## Limitations

- Requires prior knowledge of candidate components and access to reference systems, limiting applicability to open-source deployments.
- Stochastic decoding significantly reduces accuracy, especially for attention backend identification (down to 33% for Llama-3.2 at T=0.9).
- Temperature mismatch between training and deployment causes severe accuracy degradation.
- Hundreds of queries are needed, which could trigger rate limits or detection mechanisms.
- Only tested on Nvidia GPUs and moderate-sized models (3B-7B parameters); larger models and other hardware (AMD, Intel) may behave differently.
- The defense analysis is mostly qualitative; no quantitative evaluation of proposed mitigations (noise addition, rate limiting) is provided.

## Relevance

本文揭示了LLM推理系统层面存在的侧信道信息泄漏，对于关注LLM部署安全和隐私保护的研究具有重要参考价值。浮点数值偏差的利用思路也可扩展到模型版权保护和供应链审计等场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the fingerprinting approach be extended to proprietary API-based services (e.g., OpenAI, Anthropic) where the exact component catalog is unknown, perhaps through zero-shot or few-shot transfer?
- How effective are the proposed mitigations (parameter noise, output noise, rate limiting) quantitatively, and what is the accuracy-utility tradeoff curve?
- Does the fingerprinting signal strengthen or weaken with larger models (70B+), quantized models (INT8/INT4), or non-Nvidia hardware (AMD MI300, Intel Gaudi)?
- Could adversarial training of inference engines produce numerically indistinguishable outputs without sacrificing performance, or is the signal truly irreducible?
- Can the technique be combined with model fingerprinting to simultaneously identify both the model and the full inference stack in a single attack?
