Relational Rank Geometry in Transformers: Detecting and Steering Hidden-State Relation Frames

Patching the ordered shape of relation-marker clouds recovers behavior while translating the mean has near-zero effect.

Why This Matters

Introduces relation frames as a new analysis unit beyond single features or directions for understanding how LLMs encode relational structure and enabling controlled interventions.

Critical Edge

Steering claim rests on just 32 synthetic prompts with a single identity clean map — real-world generalization across diverse relations remains entirely unproven.

Methodology

Two-phase approach

Phase 1 — Detection. Plücker sign entropy projects hidden states into a 64-dim Gaussian subspace, computes determinant signs of token-tuple minors, and measures orientation consistency. The diagonal hypothesis predicts D(r,r) is the largest tested rank for r-argument relations.

Phase 2 — Steering. An 8×8 edge-grid assay creates clean/corrupt prompt pairs sharing the same row/column scaffold. Patches applied at layer 5, residual geometry read at layer 35. Five intervention families with 18 methods isolate the causal effect of relation-frame geometry.

Strengths
  • Genuinely novel: Plücker sign entropy as a rank-indexed orientation diagnostic for multi-token relations
  • 18 intervention methods with systematic site/order/placebo controls — exceptionally thorough for sole-authorship
  • Clean mechanistic dissociation: shape-only recovers behavior, centroid-only does nothing
  • Multi-scale evidence (8B/70B/405B) with competence gating rather than cherry-picking
  • Strong reproducibility: public code, SHA256 manifests, documented configurations
  • Honest scope delimitation — explicitly states what results do NOT prove
Limitations
  • 32 synthetic edge-grid prompts with single identity clean map only
  • 8B fails competence threshold; steering claim covers 2 of 3 scales
  • 70B/405B runs use 4-bit quantization; full-precision replication is future work
  • Cross-prompt transfer narrowly scoped: always the same solved state
  • Dense mathematical presentation (Plücker coordinates, Grassmann geometry, oriented matroids) creates high barrier to entry
  • No identification of circuit components (heads, MLPs) that construct or consume the frame
Open Questions
  1. Can relation-frame steering transfer across genuinely different clean relation maps, or does the current result reflect a single solved-state format?
  2. How sensitive are the diagnostic and steering results to the 4-bit quantization regime used for 70B/405B?
  3. Which upstream attention heads and MLP components construct the relation frame at patch layer 5, and which downstream components consume it at readout layer 35?
  4. Does Plücker sign entropy detect meaningful relation structure in naturalistic prompts where arity is not controlled by construction?
  5. What is the computational overhead of detection and steering at inference time — can they scale to real-time model editing?
