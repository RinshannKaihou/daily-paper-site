> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 LoRA-Curve，一种基于分段 Bezier 曲线的 LoRA 参数空间插值方法，通过锚定独立微调的 LoRA 最优点并用可优化的控制手柄连接它们，在 LoRA 空间中构建连续低损失谷，结合平坦极小值扰动和 JSD 正则化实现更高的预测互信息，挑战了"LoRA 空间中多模态方法无用"的既有观点。

> **TL;DR:** LoRA-Curve introduces segmented Bezier curves in LoRA weight space to connect independently fine-tuned LoRA optima through continuous low-loss valleys, proving that linear interpolation encounters loss barriers while multi-segment Bezier curves bypass them. With flat-minima perturbations and JSD regularization, it achieves higher mutual information in predictive distributions without sacrificing task performance on Qwen2.5 7B across reasoning and classification benchmarks, challenging the prevailing view that multi-mode Bayesian methods offer little benefit under LoRA.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 LoRA-Curve，一种在 LoRA 参数空间中的分段 Bezier 曲线参数化方法，包含自由配置 (FLC) 和锚定配置 (ALC) 两种变体，统一框架下可表示 MAP、Deep Ensemble 和线性模式连接等标准方法
- 证明了沿曲线路径的损失连续性和 Lipschitz 正则性，为 BMA 在 LoRA 空间的应用提供理论基础
- 实验表明线性插值在 LoRA 空间中会遇到损失障碍，而锚定多段曲线可成功连接独立最优点形成连续低损失谷
- 结合 Flat-LoRA 平坦极小值扰动和基于 Jensen-Shannon 散度的功能多样性正则化，在不牺牲任务性能的前提下提升了预测分布的互信息量
- 揭示了 FLC 与 ALC 之间有趣的优化-泛化差异：两者表达力相同，但 ALC 因利用预训练最优点的先验而泛化更稳定

## Key Contributions

- Introduce LoRA-Curve with a unified notation that subsumes MAP (FLC(1,0)), Deep Ensembles (DE(N)), and linear mode connectivity (ALC(N,0) = Lin(N)) as special cases
- Provide formal proofs of pathwise continuity and Lipschitz regularity of the loss along the Bezier curve in LoRA space
- Empirically demonstrate that linear interpolation between LoRA optima hits loss barriers, while anchored multi-segment Bezier curves connect them through continuous low-loss valleys
- Show that uniform averaging (T -> infinity) outperforms posterior-weighted BMA along the curve, justified by a double-conditioning argument
- Combine flat-minima perturbations (Flat-LoRA) with a margin-based JSD regularizer to increase functional diversity (measured via mutual information) without sacrificing predictive performance
- Identify an intriguing optimization gap: FLC and ALC have identical representational capacity, yet ALC generalizes better due to leveraging pretrained optima as anchors

## Methodology

**Core formulation.** LoRA-Curve parameterizes paths through LoRA weight space using composite (segmented) Bezier curves. For a curve with N anchor points connected by N_seg = N-1 segments, each with m handles, the global parameter is t in [0, N_seg]. The curve is evaluated locally per segment using Bernstein basis polynomials. The total number of control points is N_cp = N_seg(m+1) + 1. The piecewise construction guarantees C0 continuity while permitting sharp corners for navigating non-convex loss barriers.

**Two variants:**
- **Free LoRA-Curve (FLC):** Jointly optimizes all N anchor points and N_seg * m handle points from scratch via gradient descent on the training loss along the curve. Faster to train but exhibits a generalization gap.
- **Anchored LoRA-Curve (ALC):** Freezes anchor points to independently fine-tuned LoRA models (e.g., from a Deep Ensemble) and only optimizes the handle points. More stable generalization by leveraging known high-performing optima.

**Training objective.** The curve is trained by minimizing the average loss across M equispaced grid points along the curve, with M = 2*N_cp - 1 to ensure sufficient evaluation density.

**Inference.** The posterior predictive distribution is approximated via temperature-scaled grid averaging. The authors find that T -> infinity (uniform averaging) is most robust, justified by the argument that posterior reweighting double-conditions on the training data and collapses predictive diversity.

**Enhancements:**
- **Flat-minima perturbations:** Flat-LoRA noise (rho = 0.25) broadens the loss valley occupied by the curve, transferring generalization benefits from flat optima to the continuous path.
- **JSD regularization:** A margin-based Jensen-Shannon divergence regularizer encourages functional diversity along the curve by enforcing disagreement between well-separated points (t1 and t2 = (t1 + N_seg/2) mod N_seg) on the predictive distributions. Uses lambda_JSD = 0.2 and tau_JSD = 0.05 threshold.

**Unified notation.** The framework subsumes: MAP = FLC(1,0); DE(N) = N independent anchors with m=0; ALC(N,0) = Lin(N) = standard piecewise linear mode connectivity.

**Experimental setup.** Qwen2.5 7B model, LoRA rank r=8, alpha=16, adapted layers (value, query, lm_head). Trained for 10,000 steps with one-cycle LR schedule peaking at 1e-4. Benchmarks include Winogrande (S/M), OBQA, ARC-Challenge/Easy, BoolQ. Compared against MAP, BLoB, ScalaBL, and Deep Ensembles. 5 random seeds per configuration.

## Results & Highlights

- **Loss barriers exist:** Linear interpolation (Lin(N)) between independently fine-tuned LoRA optima encounters significant loss barriers, confirming that mode connectivity in LoRA space is non-trivial
- **Bezier curves bypass barriers:** Anchored multi-segment Bezier curves (ALC) successfully construct continuous low-loss valleys connecting independent optima, with the ability to bend around steep barriers
- **Multi-segment > single-segment:** Multi-segment configurations consistently match or outperform single-segment Bezier curves with equivalent total control points, with the advantage growing as control point count increases
- **More anchors = better:** Increasing N (number of anchors) improves performance for all methods (DE, ALC, FLC), confirming that multiple modes captured by longer paths yield better results
- **ALC outperforms FLC in practice:** Despite identical representational capacity, ALC generalizes more consistently because FLC exhibits a train-test landscape misalignment (smooth training valleys that do not transfer to test)
- **ALC competitive with or better than DE:** Anchored LoRA-Curve matches or exceeds Deep Ensembles on test log-likelihood across most benchmarks
- **Flat perturbations help:** Combining Flat-LoRA perturbations (rho=0.25) with curves broadens the occupied loss valley and improves generalization; excessive noise (rho=0.5) hurts
- **JSD regularization increases mutual information:** The JSD regularizer significantly boosts mutual information while maintaining or slightly improving log-likelihood, with MI scaling alongside both the number of anchors and the explicit regularization
- **Uniform averaging is best:** Temperature T -> infinity (uniform weighting along the curve) consistently outperforms posterior-weighted averaging (T=1), confirming the double-conditioning argument

Authors from HTWG Konstanz (Daniel Dold, Oliver Durr), LMU Munich / Munich Center for Machine Learning (Emanuel Sommer, Julius Kobialka, David Rugamer), and TIDIT.ch (Oliver Durr). This team has prior work on loss landscape paths (Dold et al., AISTATS 2025) and Bayesian deep learning. ## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. Applying segmented Bezier curves to LoRA space is new, and the unified notation that subsumes MAP/DE/linear connectivity as special cases is elegant. The discovery that FLC and ALC behave differently despite identical capacity is an interesting contribution to understanding optimization geometry.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Formal proofs of continuity and Lipschitz regularity are provided. The experimental protocol is thorough with 5 seeds, proper baselines (reproducing BLoB and ScalaBL), and ablation studies on segmentation, perturbation, and JSD.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Uses publicly available Qwen2.5 7B, standard benchmarks, and detailed hyperparameter tables (Tables 2-3). Code release is not mentioned but the methodology is sufficiently detailed.
- **Significance:** Significant for the Bayesian LoRA community. The paper directly challenges the claim (from BLoB and ScalaBL) that multi-mode approaches offer little benefit under LoRA by showing the real issue is the path structure, not the modes themselves. This reframes the problem constructively.
- **Red flags:** (1) Single model family (Qwen2.5 7B) -- the claim that LoRA spaces have this geometry may not generalize. (2) The ALC variant requires pre-training independent LoRA models first, so the total training cost is DE cost + curve optimization cost, which is not clearly quantified. (3) The mutual information improvements are demonstrated but not linked to concrete downstream benefits like OOD detection or calibration error. (4) The discarded approaches (repulsive regularization, OOD-based JSD) suggest the method required significant engineering to stabilize.

## Strengths

- Addresses a genuine contradiction in the LoRA Bayesian literature with a principled geometric approach
- The unified FLC/ALC notation cleanly subsumes prior methods and clarifies the design space
- Strong ablation study design: systematically varies segmentation, perturbation strength, JSD regularization, and number of anchors
- The double-conditioning argument for why uniform averaging works better than posterior weighting is a valuable practical insight
- Honest discussion of why FLC underperforms ALC despite identical capacity -- the train-test landscape misalignment is a finding in itself
- Reproduces baselines (BLoB, ScalaBL) using the exact original frameworks, improving comparability

## Limitations

- Experiments restricted to Qwen2.5 7B with a single LoRA configuration (r=8, alpha=16); scalability to larger models (70B+), other architectures, and different LoRA ranks is untested
- The total computational cost (N independent LoRA trainings + curve optimization) is not clearly reported or compared to alternatives
- Mutual information improvements are shown but not linked to practical downstream benefits (calibration, OOD detection, selective prediction)
- The 1D curve parameterization is inherently limited; the authors acknowledge that extending to higher-dimensional surfaces would face computational challenges with uniform weighting
- FLC exhibits a significant generalization gap that is acknowledged but not fully resolved
- The optimal perturbation strength (rho) and JSD thresholds require dataset-specific tuning

## Relevance

本文研究了 LoRA 参数空间中的损失景观几何与模式连通性，直接挑战了"LoRA 空间中集成方法无用"的观点，对于关注大模型高效微调中贝叶斯推理和不确定性量化的研究具有直接参考价值。其关于连续低损失谷和功能多样性的发现对模型集成与 BMA 策略的设计有重要的启示意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does LoRA-Curve behave with higher LoRA ranks (r=16, 32, 64) where the parameter space geometry is fundamentally different?
- What is the total wall-clock time comparison between ALC(N,m) and DE(N) including the pre-training of N independent LoRA models?
- Does the increased mutual information translate to improved calibration (e.g., ECE), OOD detection (AUROC), or selective prediction accuracy?
- Can the curve be extended to connect LoRA optima from different base models, enabling cross-model Bayesian averaging?
- Would the insights about loss barriers in LoRA space change with different adapter architectures (e.g., DoRA, LoRA+)?
- The authors found that weight-space repulsive regularization failed while output-space JSD succeeded -- what does this imply about the relationship between parameter-space geometry and functional diversity in overparameterized models?
