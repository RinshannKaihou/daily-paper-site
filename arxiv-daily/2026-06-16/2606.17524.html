A learned depth + action controller iteratively refines LLM hidden states before decoding — SOTA without chain-of-thought.

Learning to Refine Hidden States for Reliable LLM Reasoning ReLAR
Chia-Hsuan Hsu Jui-Ming Yao
latent reasoning hidden state refinement reinforcement learning policy gradient LLM reasoning
01Key Metric
0.14s
inference latency on PubMedQA — 65× faster than CoT (9.09s) and 117× faster than SC-CoT (16.36s), at higher accuracy.
02Why This Matters
Reasoning is moved from token space into hidden-state space, where a policy-gradient loop adaptively steers the trajectory before decoding. Same latency as plain SFT, far better quality — a deployment-friendly building block for reliable LLM reasoning.
03Critical Edge
Backbones (1.1B–3B) are 3–4× smaller than the 7B–8B baselines they beat — a comparison gap the authors themselves concede and that the paper does not fully close with size-matched numbers.

方法Methodology

  • State. Base LM emits final-layer h₀; a projection compresses it into low-dim reasoning state s₀ ∈ ℝ^{d_s} — the only signal both controllers consume.
  • Action. π_a samples a_t = (γ, β, v) with ‖v‖=1; signed step size α_t = f_α(γ, β) updates h_{t+1} = h_t + α_t·v_t; reasoning state refreshes via s_{t+1} = g(s_t, h_{t+1}).
  • Depth. π_d samples the number of refinement steps T; loop runs for T iterations; final decode representation h_T = f_decode(s_T, h₀) is anchored to h₀ to preserve context.
  • Reward. r_t = log p_θ(y*|x, s_{t+1}) − log p_θ(y*|x, s_t) − c_d — dense per-step log-likelihood improvement minus per-step cost, with KL penalty + entropy bonus.
  • Backbones. LLaMA-1.1B, Gemma-2B, Qwen-3B (smaller than the 7B–8B baselines used for comparison).

结果Results & Highlights

  • PubMedQA (0/5-shot): 77.67 / 72.54 → 79.23 / 74.17 — SOTA; beats MedGemma-4B (72.45 / 68.52).
  • GSM-Hard: 45.02 / 47.58 → 48.57 / 52.12 — SOTA. (On GSM8K the model is competitive but Llama-3-8B-Instruct leads.)
  • HotpotQA: 57.50 / 75.23 → 59.64 / 76.15 — SOTA.
  • Open-ended (Table 2): best BERTScore + ROUGE-L on CommonGen (0.934 / 38.92) and WritingPrompts (0.878 / 11.47).
  • Backbone scaling (Table 5): adding ReLAR to SFT yields +13.2 (LLaMA-1.1B), +19.6 (Gemma-2B), +14.4 (Qwen-3B) on PubMedQA — the cleanest isolation of the refinement contribution.
  • Latency (Table 4): 0.14s vs SFT 0.12s, ICL 0.31s, CoT 9.09s, SC-CoT 16.36s — the paper's strongest selling point.
  • Depth distribution (Fig 4): controller concentrates around T=3 (peak accuracy 75%); based on 20 actual samples projected to n=200 — a weak empirical foundation.

优劣Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Inference-cost story is the cleanest part — 65–117× speedup over CoT at higher accuracy, no CoT required.
  • Modular design (extract → refine ×T → decode-anchored) plugs onto different backbones.
  • Ablations cleanly decompose static depth, adaptive depth, adaptive direction, full method.
  • Diverse benchmark coverage: medical, math, multi-hop, open-ended generation.
  • Code released at tongyu0924/Learning-to-Refine-Hidden-States.

Limitations

  • Backbones 1.1B–3B vs 7B–8B baselines — apples-to-oranges, admitted by the paper.
  • Theoretical analysis (App. A.1) is a trivial inequality — no constraint on the learned policy.
  • Reward requires ground-truth labels at every step, undermining the "RL" framing.
  • Variance across seeds is not reported (only averages).
  • "Medical reasoning" rests on PubMedQA only (yes/no/maybe) — no real clinical tasks.
  • Depth-distribution analysis uses 20 actual samples.
  • Hyper-params c_d, KL/entropy weights, α_RL, d_s undisclosed.
  • No error analysis of when latent refinement fails.

相关Relevance & Open Questions

ReLAR is directly on-axis for two of this digest's threads: LLM hidden states & interpretability and ML reliability. It reframes reasoning as a representation-level trajectory under RL control, with deployment-friendly latency — exactly the "intervene in the representation layer to get more reliable outputs" research direction.

  • Does the gain hold on size-matched 7B–8B backbones, or is most of the win from the size gap?
  • What is the variance across seeds, and what do the depth distributions look like on a larger sample than 20?
  • Can the per-step log-likelihood reward be replaced with a learned preference-based reward model?
  • How does the method behave on tasks that explicitly test faithfulness of the reasoning process?
  • Is the learned v_t direction interpretable, or does it diffuse into a generic "improvement" direction?
  • Can ReLAR be composed with test-time scaling (e.g., self-consistency over refined states) — additive or redundant?
  • "Adaptive depth without direction" beats the full method on GSM-Hard — a regime where simpler controllers win?
  • How sensitive is the method to c_d, and can it be scheduled across training?
