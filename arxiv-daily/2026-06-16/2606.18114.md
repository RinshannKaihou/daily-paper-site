> **一句话总结：** 通过分组(W1.58A16)+FP16 教师知识蒸馏的 QAT 训练，仅用 102M tokens(单卡 H100 4 GPU-小时)将 Mamba-2 1.3B 压缩 3.61×(2,687→744MB)，零样本 7 任务平均 48.1% 逼近 Bi-Mamba 48.4%(±0.9pp CI 内)，并发现 QAT-from-pretrained 特有的"零比率坍缩"现象、揭示后置修正为何在 SSM 上失效。

> **TL;DR:** Ternary Mamba shows that a pretrained Mamba-2 1.3B checkpoint can be aggressively ternarized to W1.58A16 via per-group (g=128) quantization-aware training (QAT) with FP16 knowledge distillation — using only 102M tokens, ~1,000× fewer than Slender-Mamba's from-scratch 150B recipe, in just 4 GPU-hours on a single H100. The compressed 744 MB model reaches 48.1% on a 7-task zero-shot average (BoolQ/PIQA/HellaSwag/WinoGrande/ARC-E/ARC-C/OBQA), within 0.3pp of Bi-Mamba's 48.4% inside a ±0.9pp CI. Three findings stand out: (1) **zero-ratio collapse** — learnable per-group scales drive activations to 90% zeros (PPL spikes to 47.45); a fixed absmean scale self-regulates at ~26% sparsity (PPL 11.25); (2) post-hoc correction (Kalman gain, James-Stein shrinkage) fails for SSMs because quantization errors accumulate as `e_T = Σ Ā^{T-t}ε_t` through the recurrence, and no pointwise output function can invert this; (3) sigma-delta noise-shaping is inapplicable because Mamba's TV/L1 ratio (1.421) exceeds the Gaussian baseline (1.414) at every layer. The result is an honest, well-controlled recipe: 4 GPU-hours of marginal cost vs. Bi-Mamba's 5,780 GPU-hours, but a clear +8.62 PPL gap to FP16 on WikiText-2 (largely from train/eval domain mismatch) and no custom Mamba-2 ternary kernel yet exists to realize the storage compression as actual inference speedup.

## 核心贡献
- **Ternary Mamba 配方**：在 Mamba-2 1.3B 预训练权重上做分组 W1.58A16 QAT，token 预算从 Slender-Mamba 的 150B 降到 102M (~1000×↓)，4 GPU-小时单卡 H100 完成。
- **零比率坍缩(Zero-Ratio Collapse)**：发现并命名 QAT-from-pretrained 场景下可学习量化尺度引发的训练不稳定(零比率 90%, PPL 47.45)；改用固定 absmean 后自调节到 ~26% 零比率(PPL 11.25)，为社区提供可复用的稳定性方案。
- **SSM 量化特性刻画**：系统证明 Transformer 风格后置修正(Kalman、James-Stein、Σ∆)对 SSM 无效，原因是 recurrent state 上的误差结构性累积，无法用点态函数纠正。
- **完整的消融与缩放实验**：组大小(g=64/128/256)在 PPL 上统计不可区分(范围 0.06)；KD 混合 α=0.5 显著优于纯 CE(+4×)和纯 KD(+1.75×)；307M tokens 内 C4 PPL 单调下降(Mann-Kendall p=0.042)。
- **分层选择性量化的 Pareto 前沿**：N=2 块保留 FP16 可恢复 7.9% 的 PPL 间隙(2.91× 压缩)；误差在 48 层上均匀分布(CV<0.005)，无单层主导。

## Key Contributions
- **Ternary QAT for SSMs from pretrained weights:** First application of per-group (g=128) ternary QAT to a pretrained Mamba-2 1.3B; compresses 1,208M of 1,408M parameters (85.7%) to 1.58 bits with 48.1% zero-shot avg.
- **Data-efficient compression via KD:** 102M tokens (~1000× less than Slender-Mamba's 150B) and 4 GPU-hours on a single H100 — three orders of magnitude cheaper than Bi-Mamba's 5,780 GPU-hours.
- **Zero-ratio collapse diagnosis:** Newly characterized failure mode where learnable per-group scales create a positive feedback loop → 90% zero weights, PPL 47.45. Fixed absmean breaks the loop and self-regulates at ~26% sparsity.
- **Post-hoc correction mismatch:** Rigorous empirical and theoretical case that Kalman gain, James-Stein shrinkage, and sigma-delta noise-shaping fail on SSMs because `e_T = Σ_{t=1}^T Ā^{T-t} ε_t` is history-dependent and structural, not additive.
- **Quantization coverage rationale:** 85.7% of parameters (in_proj + out_proj) ternarized; SSM dynamics (A_log, D, dt_bias <0.3%), conv1d, embedding, lm_head, norms kept in FP16 — consistent with Quamba/Bi-Mamba findings on SSM-param sensitivity.
- **Layer-selective Pareto frontier:** 2 FP16 blocks recover 7.9% PPL gap at 2.91× compression; uniform per-layer quantization error (CV<0.005) means no single layer dominates.

## Methodology
- **Base model:** `state-spaces/mamba2-1.3b` (HuggingFace public FP16 checkpoint, 1,408M total params).
- **Quantization scheme:** W1.58A16 — ternary weights {−1, 0, +1} with **per-group absmean scale** (g=128, recomputed every forward pass, **non-learnable**); activations remain FP16. STE for gradient flow.
- **Coverage:** 48 blocks × (in_proj 805M + out_proj 403M) = 1,208M ternary (85.7%); kept FP16: A_log, D, dt_bias, conv1d, embedding, lm_head, norms (~200M).
- **Objective:** `L = α·D_KL(p_teacher || p_student)·T² + (1−α)·L_CE` with α=0.5, T=1.0; frozen FP16 Mamba-2 1.3B as teacher.
- **Optimizer:** AdamW (β=(0.9, 0.95), weight decay 0.01, grad clip 1.0), lr 2.5e-4 cosine with 1k warmup, 50k steps, batch 2×1024 = 102M total tokens.
- **Data:** C4 (English) — local gzip shards.
- **Hardware:** Single NVIDIA H100, ~4 GPU-hours.
- **Evaluation:** WikiText-2 (285K tokens), C4 held-out (512K tokens), 7-task zero-shot (BoolQ/PIQA/HellaSwag/WinoGrande/ARC-Easy/ARC-Challenge/OpenBookQA).
- **Multi-seed validation:** Seeds 42, 137, 2024 — C4 PPL std = 0.01, 7-task std = ±0.13pp.

## Results & Highlights
- **Primary result:** 48.1% 7-task zero-shot avg (W1.58, 3.61× compression) vs. **48.4% Bi-Mamba** (1-bit, 105B tokens, from scratch) — within ±0.9pp CI. **Per-task:** BoolQ 56.0, PIQA 69.3, HellaSwag 47.5, WinoGrande 56.3, ARC-E 48.2, ARC-C 25.9, OBQA 33.8.
- **Cross-architecture context:** BitNet b1.58 1.3B (Transformer, 100B tokens from scratch) reaches only 45.4% — the ternary Mamba is *not* relying on a Transformer-friendly recipe.
- **PPL:** WT2 20.31 vs. FP16 11.69 (+8.62, inflated by C4→WT2 domain mismatch); C4 21.67 vs. FP16 17.47 (+4.20, +24%).
- **Naive PTQ failure:** W1.58 post-training quantization (no QAT) → PPL ~13.2M on WT2 and ~23.0M on C4 — effectively random, establishing QAT as necessary, not optional.
- **Cost comparison:** 4 GPU-hours (ours, marginal) vs. 5,780 GPU-hours (Bi-Mamba from-scratch, 32× A100) — the FP16 pretraining (~4,000 GPU-h) is inherited, not paid.
- **Scaling:** C4 PPL monotonically decreases 21.67 → 21.12 → 21.04 → 20.65 across 102M→184M→266M→307M tokens; Mann-Kendall p=0.042, OLS p=0.020, R²=0.92.
- **Group-size ablation:** g ∈ {64, 128, 256} statistically indistinguishable (PPL range 0.06); g=128 selected for peak top-1 agreement (71.2%).
- **KD mixing:** α=0 (CE only) → C4 PPL 87.55; α=0.5 → 21.67; α=1.0 (KD only) → 37.96. Balanced split clearly dominant; KD without CE drifts on low-confidence tokens.
- **Layer-selective:** N=2 (4 FP16 blocks: 0, 1, 46, 47) → WT2 PPL 19.63 (2.91× compression) — linear Pareto recovery.
- **HG-GSQ negative result:** Hessian-guided Gumbel-Softmax quantization degraded PPL by +0.93 to +1.81 vs. STE; soft-to-hard gap is the failure mode.
- **FP16 KD control:** KD-only FP16 fine-tuning on WikiText-103 reaches PPL 9.52 — confirms ternarization's real penalty is +1.73 PPL, not an artifact of training data.
- **Inference memory (current sim.):** 5,322 MB ternary-simulation (worse than FP16's 2,599 MB due to latent weight storage); projected 744 MB with packed 2-bit storage.

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty:** The QAT-from-pretrained angle is genuinely new for SSMs (Slender-Mamba is from-scratch at 170M; Bi-Mamba is from-scratch 1-bit at 105B). The *zero-ratio collapse* diagnosis is a concrete, nameable failure mode that previous ternary SSM papers did not surface — a real contribution beyond a recipe.
- **Rigor:** Strong for an empirical paper. **Highlights:** multi-seed replication (std = 0.01 PPL, 0.13pp on 7-task); explicit FP16 KD control (PPL 9.52 vs. 11.69 baseline) isolates ternarization cost; HG-GSQ negative result is published rather than buried; per-layer error analysis (CV<0.005) supports the "no bottleneck layer" claim; explicit honest reporting that WT2 gap is inflated by train/eval domain mismatch. **Weaknesses:** the 7-task suite is small and the headline gap (48.1% vs. 55.5% FP16) is hidden behind the favorable Bi-Mamba comparison; 4 GPU-hours is favorable because pretraining is inherited — the paper is transparent about this but downstream readers may not be.
- **Reproducibility:** Hyperparameters (Table 12) are exhaustive; group size, KD α, lr, batch, schedule all specified. The 102M-token C4 setup is small enough to reproduce on a single H100. **Gaps:** no code repo cited in the text I read; exact C4 preprocessing (dedup, filtering) not specified; group size chosen via top-1 agreement (a teacher-distillation metric), not via held-out eval, which is slightly self-referential.
- **Significance:** Practical takeaway is real — edge-device SSM deployment gets a credible W1.58 recipe without retraining from scratch. The negative result on post-hoc correction is broadly useful for the SSM-quantization community (it tells people not to bother porting LREC/QEP/Σ∆ from Transformers).
- **Red flags to verify:**
  - 48.1% 7-task average hides a **7.4pp gap to FP16** (55.5%) — the per-task table (Table 3) shows particular weakness on HellaSwag (47.5 vs 59.9, −12.4pp) and ARC-Challenge (25.9 vs 33.2, −7.3pp). Readers should not conclude "parity" from the Bi-Mamba comparison.
  - "Within ±0.9pp CI of Bi-Mamba" is technically true but **Bi-Mamba is itself a from-scratch baseline with 1000× the token budget and a binary (not ternary) scheme** — the comparison mixes recipes. A cleaner comparison would be BitNet b1.58 1.3B (Table 3 shows 45.4% — the ternary Mamba *does* beat it, but on a different architecture).
  - The "log-linear extrapolation to FP16 parity at 1-10B tokens" is explicitly caveated as based on 4 data points — a 1B-token run is a non-trivial commitment and the asymptotic floor from the ternary constraint is unverified.
  - WikiText-2 PPL gap (+8.62) is largely attributed to domain mismatch, but this means the WT2 number is a *training-domain-shift probe*, not a true out-of-distribution generalization test.
  - **No custom Mamba-2 ternary kernel exists** — the 3.61× compression is a storage claim, not a wall-clock inference claim. Realizing inference speedup is left to future work.
  - KD is confounded with the recipe — without a "QAT-only, no teacher" ablation, it is hard to attribute the 102M-token efficiency to grouped QAT, KD, or their interaction.

## Strengths
- **Hardware-cheap recipe:** 4 GPU-hours on a single H100 is approachable for any academic lab.
- **Named new failure mode:** zero-ratio collapse is a real, well-characterized contribution that future ternary SSM work will hit.
- **Honest negative results:** Kalman, James-Stein, Σ∆, and HG-GSQ are all reported as failures rather than being silently dropped — narrows the design space productively.
- **Careful statistics:** multi-seed replication, Wilson score 95% CIs, Mann-Kendall monotonicity test, OLS regression R², per-layer CV.
- **Strong token-efficiency story:** 1,000× token reduction + 1,000× compute reduction (vs. Bi-Mamba) is a genuinely compelling headline.
- **Layer-error uniformity:** CV<0.005 across 48 layers is a strong empirical claim that the recipe is robust to architecture depth choices.

## Limitations
- **Single architecture and language:** All experiments on Mamba-2 1.3B / English C4; generalization to Mamba-1, Mamba-3, Jamba, RWKV, and non-English is untested.
- **Single scale (1.3B):** The 3B+ "quality inflection" observed for ternary Transformers is unverified for SSMs.
- **Storage-only compression:** No custom Mamba-2 ternary kernel exists; the 3.61× size is a *disk/memory* claim, not a throughput claim. Current QAT simulation uses *more* memory than FP16 (5,322 vs. 2,599 MB).
- **7.4pp gap to FP16:** 48.1% vs. 55.5% is a real regression, especially on HellaSwag (−12.4pp) and ARC-Challenge (−7.3pp) — the Bi-Mamba framing softens but does not eliminate this.
- **Domain-mismatch eval:** WikiText-2 PPL gap is inflated by C4→WT2 shift; the true OOD generalization story is incomplete.
- **KD confound:** No ablation of QAT-only (no teacher) at 102M tokens — hard to disentangle KD's contribution from grouped QAT's.
- **Sequence length 1024 during training:** long-context behavior (where SSM recurrence errors would compound) is unstudied.
- **No code release mentioned:** recipe is reproducible from public artifacts but not turnkey.

## Questions for Further Exploration
- How does the ternary Mamba-2 behave on long-context benchmarks (e.g., 8K–32K) where SSM recurrence errors would amplify — does the 3.61× storage claim survive a true OOD test?
- Does **zero-ratio collapse appear in other architectures** (Transformers, RWKV, RetNet) when QAT-from-pretrained uses learnable per-group scales, or is it specific to SSM dynamics?
- A clean ablation: **QAT-only (no KD) at 102M tokens** — can grouped QAT alone reach 48.1%, or is KD load-bearing?
- Can a **custom W1.58 kernel for Mamba-2's selective scan** (shapes 2048×8192 and 4096×2048) realize the 3.61× storage claim as actual wall-clock latency on edge devices (Jetson, mobile NPU)?
- Does the recipe **scale to Mamba-2 7B/8B** — does zero-ratio collapse get worse with depth, or does the "log-linear extrapolation" claim hold?
- Why does the **gate vs. SSM input path** of `in_proj` not benefit from asymmetric thresholds (per the A.4 ablation showing +0.12 PPL regression) — what does this say about Mamba-2's input/output structure?
- Can **intermediate-layer distillation** (not just the final logits) close the 7.4pp gap to FP16, given the 1–10B-token extrapolation?
- Is the **post-hoc correction failure general to all recurrent SSMs** (S4, S5, H3), or specific to Mamba-2's structured-mask selective scan?
- How does the recipe interact with **mixture-of-experts or hybrid SSM-Attention** architectures (Jamba, Zamba) where the SSM is one of multiple components?
