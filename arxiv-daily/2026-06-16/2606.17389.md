# Visuals Lie, Consistency Speaks: Disentangling Spatial Attention from Reliability in Vision-Language Models

> **一句话总结：** 通过跨家族 VLM 可靠性探测（VRP），挑战"注意力即可靠性"假设，揭示空间注意力与正确性近乎零相关（R≈0.001），自一致性是最佳行为信号（R=0.429），隐藏状态探针是最强单次推理信号（AUROC>0.95），而 LLaVA 与 PaliGemma/Qwen2-VL 在因果稳健性上存在架构级分歧。

> **TL;DR:** The paper challenges the Attention-Confidence Assumption that tight visual attention implies a trustworthy VLM answer. Through the VLM Reliability Probe (VRP) — a cross-family study of LLaVA-1.5-7B, PaliGemma-3B, and Qwen2-VL-7B evaluated on POPE, LLaVA-Bench, VQA v2, TextVQA, and custom counting/spatial tasks — the authors introduce structural-attention metrics (cluster counts C_k, spatial entropy H_s, and layer-wise ΔH_s). They uncover a "Symbolic Detachment" where models "Early Lock" on visual features then diffuse attention later, and a "Cluster Failure" where spatial attention correlates near zero with accuracy (R(C_k,y)=0.001, R(H_s,y)=−0.012, both p>0.05). Self-consistency across K=10 sampled paths is the strongest behavioral signal (R=0.429, AUROC≈0.78), and learned hidden-state probes achieve AUROC up to 0.971 (Qwen2-VL) and 0.956 (LLaVA) at single-pass cost. Causal layer-ablation reveals an architectural split: LLaVA's reliability is locked in a fragile late-stage bottleneck (single-neuron ablations of the top-5 reliability neurons drop object-ID accuracy by 8.3pp), while PaliGemma and Qwen2-VL distribute reliability globally and survive destruction of ~50% of their most predictive layers with negligible degradation. The takeaway: hallucination monitors should look at hidden states and consistency, not attention heatmaps.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 VRP 框架**：跨 LLaVA-1.5-7B / PaliGemma-3B / Qwen2-VL-7B 三大家族的系统化可靠性探测，挑战"注意力-置信度"假设（Attention-Confidence Assumption）。
- **引入结构性注意力度量**：簇计数 C_k（连接组件数）、空间熵 H_s（注意力分布的 Shannon 熵），以及跨层变化 ΔH_s，定量刻画视觉编码器的"注视"模式及其演化。
- **提出"符号脱节"（Symbolic Detachment）**：LLaVA 在 L2 急剧锐化（ΔH_s≈−2.5）后停滞 28 层，在末层又扩散（ΔH_s≈+1.0），将早期感知与最终生成分离；Qwen2-VL 呈"循环细化"（在 L17、L25 反复锐化），PaliGemma 呈"平稳衰减"。
- **实证"簇失败"（Cluster Failure）**：在 3,090 样本池化集上 R(C_k,y)=0.001 (95% CI [−0.034, 0.036])，R(H_s,y)=−0.012 (95% CI [−0.047, 0.024])，均统计上不可区分于随机噪声（p>0.05）；即使筛选对数贡献最大的 top-k 头，R² 仍 ≤0.08。
- **"计数异常"（Counting Anomaly）案例**：3 名棒球运动员图片中，模型以 92% 高置信度输出"Four"，但视觉簇 K_total=3 正确——揭示 token 概率衡量的是流畅性而非接地。
- **自一致性主导行为信号**：K=10 采样路径一致率与正确性 R=0.429（AUROC≈0.78），远超所有视觉指标，但代价是 10× 推理成本。
- **隐藏状态探针是单次最佳信号**：在 LLaVA/Qwen2-VL 上达到 AUROC>0.95，零额外推理开销；监督注意力集成探针仅 0.725。
- **架构级因果分歧**：LLaVA 呈现"晚期脆弱瓶颈"（top-5 神经元的同步消融使目标识别准确率下降 8.3pp），PaliGemma 消融 1000 神经元（≈50% 隐藏维度）仅降 1.0pp，Qwen2-VL 消融 2000 神经元（≈56% 残差维度）无任何可测退化（Δ 界 ±2.0pp），且完全绕过 MLP 输出仍稳健。
- **MLP 而非注意力贡献可靠性**：LLaVA L31 处 MLP 贡献 82.1% 的 margin 增长，logit lens 表明可靠性是"特征处理"而非"token 路由"的结果。

## Key Contributions

- **VRP (VLM Reliability Probe) framework**: A systematic, cross-family reliability study comparing LLaVA-1.5-7B (32L, CLIP ViT-L/14 + Vicuna-7B), PaliGemma-3B (18L, SigLIP + Gemma), and Qwen2-VL-7B-Instruct (28L, native multimodal with GQA) on POPE (Adversarial, n=1,000), LLaVA-Bench (n=90), VQA v2, TextVQA, and a custom counting/spatial set (n=2,000).
- **Structural-attention metrics**: Cluster counts (C_k) and spatial entropy (H_s) of cross-attention A(l,h) ∈ R^(T×S), aggregated by averaging over heads and answer-token positions to form per-layer spatial vectors; layer-wise ΔH_s tracks attention evolution.
- **"Symbolic Detachment" (three patterns)**: LLaVA shows "Early Locking" (sharp drop ΔH_s≈−2.5 at L2, stagnation through L29, then late diffusion ΔH_s≈+1.0); Qwen2-VL shows "Cyclical Refinement" (re-sharpening at L17 and L25); PaliGemma shows steady decay.
- **"Cluster Failure" empirical result**: R(C_k, y) = 0.001 (95% CI [−0.034, 0.036]); R(H_s, y) = −0.012 (95% CI [−0.047, 0.024]); supervised ensemble on 11 attention features (XGBoost-Random Forest) reaches only 52–55% accuracy; a deeper architecture-specific probe reaches AUROC 0.725.
- **Counting Anomaly case study**: 3 baseball players, model says "Four" with 92% token confidence while K_total=3 (Ck=2) correctly identifies 3 visual regions — a textbook dissociation of fluency from grounding.
- **Self-consistency as best behavioral signal**: K=10 sampled paths, R=0.429 with accuracy, AUROC≈0.78; precision@SC=1 is 90.8% (95% CI [88.4, 92.8]%).
- **Hidden-state probes as best single-pass signal**: Margin-only probes (ΔM_l) AUROC 0.63–0.72; best-layer hidden-state probes reach AUROC 0.956 (LLaVA), 0.738 (PaliGemma), 0.971 (Qwen2-VL); combined last-5-layer probes match single-best.
- **Causal architectural divergence via large-scale ablation**:
  - **LLaVA (L31)**: Single-neuron ablations give Δ=0.0pp (no single bottleneck); top-5 probe neurons → −2.0pp overall, **−8.3pp on object identification**; 5 random neurons → 0.0pp. Reliability is a localized 5-neuron circuit, not a single unit.
  - **PaliGemma (L15)**: 10 most predictive neurons → −0.7pp; 1000 random neurons (49% of 2048 hidden) → −1.0pp.
  - **Qwen2-VL (L25)**: 2000 random neurons (56% of 3584) → +2.0pp (within ±2.0pp bounds); **complete MLP bypass at L25 → +5.0pp** on validation split — reliability is fully redistributed.
- **MLP-dominance of reliability**: At LLaVA's peak (L31), MLP layers contribute 82.1% of truth-margin growth (PaliGemma 47.6%, Qwen2-VL 68.2%) — reliability is feature processing, not token routing.
- **Attention is causally necessary but not uncertainty-calibrated**: Masking top-30% attended patches reduces LLaVA accuracy 8.2pp and PaliGemma 11.3pp (p<0.001) — attention enables feature extraction but does not encode uncertainty about those features.

## Methodology

- **Models**: LLaVA-1.5-7B (32L, CLIP ViT-L/14 + Vicuna-7B), PaliGemma-3B (18L, SigLIP + Gemma), Qwen2-VL-7B-Instruct (28L, native multimodal w/ GQA, dynamic resolution).
- **Benchmarks**:
  - **POPE** Adversarial split (n=1,000) — object hallucination stress test.
  - **LLaVA-Bench** (n=90) — open-ended visual instruction following.
  - **Custom Counting & Spatial** (n=2,000) — 1,000 counting + 1,000 spatial-relation prompts, COCO-style images with integer/binary labels.
  - **VQA v2** (scene understanding) and **TextVQA** (OCR-heavy) for generalization tests.
- **Structural-attention metrics**:
  - **C_k**: Number of connected components in a binary attention mask built from the top-30% attention mass, minus 1 (removing the dominant cluster) — C_k=0 indicates single dominant focus. Verified with DBSCAN variant (ε=1.5, min_samples=3).
  - **H_s**: Shannon entropy of the per-layer spatial attention distribution (averaged over heads and answer-token positions).
  - **ΔH_s**: Layer-wise change tracking attention evolution across the LLM decoder.
- **Mechanistic probes**:
  - **Logit Lens**: Project hidden state h_l of layer l into vocabulary space; define **Truth Margin ΔM_l = logit(correct) − logit(top incorrect)**. Report two peaks: l*_vis (max correct-vs-incorrect separation) and l*_final (max |ΔM_l|).
  - **MLP vs. Attention decomposition** of the residual stream's contribution to margin growth.
  - **Sparse L1-logistic probes** (λ=0.1) on internal activations (80/20 stratified split, Adam lr=1e-4, 50 epochs).
  - **Ensemble Attention Probe**: 3-layer MLP (18,432 → 1,024 → 512 → 1) on concatenated per-layer spatial vectors, ReLU + Dropout 0.1.
- **Behavioral metrics**:
  - **Self-Consistency (SC)**: K=10 nucleus-sampled paths (p=0.9, T=0.7), agreement rate.
  - **Token confidence P_tok**, **logit entropy**.
- **Causal interventions**:
  - **Patch masking**: Mask top-30% attended patches and measure accuracy drop.
  - **Neuron ablation**: Single-neuron, top-5/10 probe neurons, random 5/500/1000/2000 neurons, and complete MLP bypass — all evaluated at the model's most predictive layer (LLaVA L31, PaliGemma L15, Qwen2-VL L25).
- **Statistics**: Point-Biserial correlation R with binary correctness, AUROC, 95% bootstrap CIs (10,000 resamples).
- **Hardware**: NVIDIA A100 80GB (RunPod / Lambda Labs), PyTorch 2.1.0, CUDA 12.1, HuggingFace transformers.

## Results & Highlights

- **Cross-model baseline accuracy** (Table 1): LLaVA 67.6%, PaliGemma 78.6%, Qwen2-VL 28.8% — strikingly low Qwen2-VL accuracy on this evaluation mix, which is unusual and worth noting (likely a sampling/evaluation split issue or the very low Qwen2-VL number reflects the counting/spatial subset).
- **Attention ↔ accuracy (Table 1)**: Top-K attention R² max = 0.008 (LLaVA), 0.080 (PaliGemma), 0.007 (Qwen2-VL); supervised classifier 53.0%/55.0%/52.0% — near chance across the board.
- **Logit-lens dynamics (Table 2)**: Peak visual-integration layer l*_vis = L24 (LLaVA), L14 (PaliGemma), L27 (Qwen2-VL); final margin peak ΔM*_final = +9.20 (LLaVA L31), +10.85 (PaliGemma L14), +8.40 (Qwen2-VL L27); MLP contribution 82.1% / 47.6% / 68.2%.
- **Reliability prediction (Table 3, AUROC)**:
  - **POPE/LLaVA-Bench probes**: LLaVA 0.956, PaliGemma 0.738, Qwen2-VL 0.971.
  - **VQA v2**: LLaVA probe 0.745 > output 0.559; PaliGemma probe 0.795 < output 0.892; Qwen2-VL probe 0.778 < output 0.892.
  - **TextVQA**: LLaVA probe 0.721 > output 0.563; PaliGemma probe 0.806 < output 0.859; Qwen2-VL probe 0.852 > output 0.774.
  - Probes beat raw output confidence in 3 of 6 model-task cells — the picture is task- and architecture-dependent.
- **Per-model AUROC (Table 7, POPE)**: Spatial attention 0.50/0.50/0.50; logit entropy 0.50/0.52/0.51; output confidence 0.54/0.55/0.53; margin-only 0.72/0.70/0.63; hidden-state probe (best layer) 0.956/0.738/0.971; self-consistency 0.78/0.81/0.79.
- **Causal ablation (Table 5, LLaVA L31, n=200)**: Baseline 54.5% / 100% Object-ID; top-5 probe neurons → 52.5% / 91.7% (−2.0 / −8.3 pp); random 5 neurons or any single neuron → no change.
- **Large-scale ablation (Table 6, n=100)**: PaliGemma L15 baseline 97.0% → 1000-neuron ablation (49%) 96.0% (−1.0pp). Qwen2-VL L25 baseline 55.0% → 2000-neuron ablation (56%) 57.0% (+2.0pp, within bounds); **complete MLP bypass 65.0%** — robust by construction.
- **Self-consistency precision (Table 4)**: P(SC=1) = 90.8% (95% CI [88.4, 92.8]%) — high agreement is a strong correctness signal.
- **Qualitative findings**:
  - **False Negatives (15% of failures)**: Perfect attention, wrong answer (e.g., attends solely to a chair but answers "No" to "Is there a chair?").
  - **False Positives (22% of correct)**: Scattered attention, correct answer (e.g., "Is this a rainy day?" via global texture).
  - **Case study (PaliGemma, "Is the dog wearing a collar?")**: H_s=0.321 (bottom 15%, "perfect" focus), C_k=0 (single dominant), but model answers "No" with 54.6% confidence. Logit lens reveals the correct token "Yes" is suppressed at L14 (peak visual integration, Δmargin=+9.57) — probe correctly flags unreliability.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate-to-high. The headline claim (R(C_k, y) ≈ 0.001) is a clean, falsifiable refutation of a popular assumption, and the cross-family scope is broader than prior VLM probing work. The "Symbolic Detachment" / "Cyclical Refinement" taxonomy is a useful conceptual contribution. The authors correctly position this against prior attention-is-not-explanation work and VIP/TVI — they are extending, not claiming primacy.
- **Rigor**: Strong on the main attention-vs-accuracy claims (95% CIs, p-values, n=3,090 pooled structural set). The combination of correlational and causal evidence (patch masking, neuron ablation, MLP bypass) is the right design. The sparse L1 probe localization (5-neuron circuit in LLaVA) is well-controlled (random-neuron controls).
- **Reproducibility**: High. The methodology section is detailed (DBSCAN variant, binary mask construction, sampling hyperparams, hardware). Code is released at https://github.com/itsloganmann/VLM-Reliability-Probe. Caveat: POPE/LLaVA-Bench sample counts are fixed (1,000/90), so bootstrap CIs come from the larger pooled set.
- **Significance**: High for VLM safety/interpretability. The practical message — use self-consistency or hidden-state probes, not attention heatmaps, for hallucination detection — is actionable. The architectural divergence (LLaVA bottleneck vs. PaliGemma/Qwen2-VL distribution) raises a deeper question about how training/fusion strategy affects reliability robustness.
- **Red flags & weaknesses**:
  - **Cherry-picked benchmark combination drives Qwen2-VL's 28.8%**: This number is suspiciously low for a state-of-the-art 7B VLM. It almost certainly reflects the custom counting/spatial set's distribution shift, but the paper should explicitly call this out rather than leave readers to puzzle over it. A model with 0.971 probe AUROC but 28.8% task accuracy is doing something strange — likely the probe is overfitting or the benchmark mix is adversarial in unintended ways.
  - **Hidden-state probes may be overfit**: AUROC 0.971 on POPE with the "best layer" wording suggests per-architecture hyperparameter tuning of the probe — without held-out datasets or test-time training splits, this is at risk of being architecture-specific overfitting rather than a general signal.
  - **PaliGemma's 0.738 POPE AUROC is a marked asymmetry**: The paper explains this as "earlier integration, shallower decoder, less late-layer separation" — plausible, but it means the "probe dominance" claim only holds for 2 of 3 models on this benchmark.
  - **Self-consistency is the only truly architecture-agnostic signal**: R=0.429 holds across all three families, while the probe claim is model-conditional. The paper could foreground this more — "use SC if you can afford 10× compute, use a probe only if you can tune per family."
  - **3 VLM families is a thin cross-section**: All three are 3B–7B open-source, none use the latest architectures (InternVL 2.5, LLaVA-Next, GPT-4V-class, Claude, Gemini). The "∼50% layer destruction" claim is striking but only covers two architectures on the resilience side.
  - **MLP bypass result (+5.0pp) is suspicious**: If bypassing the entire MLP at L25 *improves* accuracy, that layer is doing more harm than good. The paper attributes it to "validation split variance" with Δ bounds of ±2.0pp — but +5.0pp is outside those bounds, so this deserves a better explanation.
  - **Architectural divergence is not cleanly causal**: PaliGemma and Qwen2-VL share early-fusion / dynamic-resolution design, LLaVA is a late-fusion projection pipeline. The "architectural choice" framing risks confounding architecture with training data, instruction tuning, and resolution handling. The paper acknowledges this in passing but doesn't fully disentangle it.
  - **Neuron ablation granularity**: Single-neuron ablations at L31 give Δ=0.0pp; only the top-5 simultaneous ablation gives −2.0pp overall. This means the "localized circuit" claim is supported, but the practical implication (a small, identifiable subnetwork) is fragile — these specific neurons may be checkpoint/version-specific.

## Strengths

- **Clean falsification** of a popular but under-tested assumption, with explicit CIs and p-values.
- **Methodological mix** of correlational and causal evidence (patch masking, neuron ablation, MLP bypass) is the gold standard for mechanistic interpretability.
- **Practical, actionable takeaway**: use SC (K=10) for high-stakes, or a single linear probe on hidden states for low-latency monitoring — both dramatically beat attention-based uncertainty proxies.
- **Cross-architecture generalization** with three architecturally distinct VLMs.
- **Memorable vocabulary**: "Symbolic Detachment," "Cluster Failure," "Early Locking," "Cyclical Refinement" — useful for the community to organize findings.
- **Code release** and detailed hyperparameter specification.
- **Failure-case analysis** (False Negatives 15%, False Positives 22%, Counting Anomaly) is qualitatively rich, not just numbers.

## Limitations

- Limited to three mid-scale open-source VLMs (3B–7B); closed-source and larger open models (InternVL 2.5, LLaVA-Next, LLaVA-34B, GPT-4V, Claude, Gemini) are untested.
- Self-consistency requires K=10 forward passes — prohibitive for real-time or robotics applications.
- The R≈0.001 claim may not hold uniformly across task types; OCR-heavy (TextVQA) and reasoning-heavy (LLaVA-Bench) cases behave differently.
- Hidden-state probes appear architecture-conditional (PaliGemma's 0.738 is markedly weaker than LLaVA/Qwen2-VL's >0.95).
- Qwen2-VL's full-MLP-bypass result (+5.0pp) is unexplained and possibly an artifact of split variance.
- The "localized 5-neuron circuit" finding in LLaVA may be version/checkpoint-specific; generalization to LLaVA-1.6, LLaVA-Next, or LLaVA-13B/34B is open.
- Architectural divergence is confounded with training data and instruction-tuning strategy.
- Probe AUROC 0.97+ on POPE may reflect POPE-specific signal (binary yes/no, object-existence framing) that doesn't transfer to open-ended generation.

## Relevance

该研究对 VLM 可靠性与可解释性方向具有直接借鉴价值：若用户关注多模态大模型的幻觉检测、机制可解释性或自一致性推理，本文提出的"视觉不可信、内部状态与一致性可信"结论（注意力 R≈0，自一致性 R=0.43，隐藏状态探针 AUROC>0.95）值得仔细阅读并复现，尤其其在 3 大家族上呈现的架构级因果分歧对鲁棒 VLM 设计与故障定位有直接参考意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the R≈0.001 attention-accuracy relationship hold for very large / closed-source VLMs (GPT-4V, Claude, Gemini), or is it a property of open-source models specifically?
- Can self-consistency be replaced with a single-pass internal-state probe that matches R=0.429 without the 10× sampling cost? The paper explicitly suggests distilling SC into a value head — is this feasible?
- Is "Symbolic Detachment" an artifact of how vision tokens are projected into the LLM (LLaVA's 2-layer MLP projector vs. PaliGemma's linear vs. Qwen2-VL's native), or a deeper property of cross-modal alignment?
- Why does complete MLP bypass at Qwen2-VL L25 *improve* accuracy? Is this layer genuinely harmful, or is the +5.0pp a split-variance artifact that needs replication?
- Does the architectural divergence persist within model families across scales (LLaVA-7B vs. LLaVA-13B vs. LLaVA-34B; Qwen2-VL-2B vs. 7B vs. 72B)?
- How do chain-of-thought and tool-use VLMs (e.g., ViperGPT, Chameleon, GPT-4V with code execution) compare — does explicit reasoning change the self-consistency dominance?
- Can the "fragile late-stage bottleneck" in LLaVA be diagnosed and repaired by re-distributing reliability, as PaliGemma/Qwen2-VL appear to do? Would re-training with a different fusion strategy or RLHF on hidden-state probe confidence reduce fragility?
- The 15% False-Negative / 22% False-Positive failure-mode asymmetry raises a question: are these systematic, or do they vary by dataset? If systematic, they imply a specific decoder failure mode (retrieval-vs-interpretation gap) worth targeting.
- Could the "Counting Anomaly" (high token confidence on wrong integers) be mitigated by an explicit consistency check at decoding time, e.g., sample N times and trigger a re-prompt when SC drops?
- PaliGemma's weaker probe AUROC (0.738) is attributed to "earlier integration + shallower decoder" — can this be made more precise with a controlled experiment on PaliGemma variants or a head-pruning study?
