> **一句话总结：** 借助 de Finetti 表示,给出无限可交换序列有界差分函数的有效方差代理 $\sigma_{\mathrm{eff}}^2 = \frac{1}{4}\sum_i c_i^2 + \sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2$ (Theorem 4.2),并证明零和线性对比下 $\sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2$ 精确消去 (Theorem 5.2);在 MMLU 全部 14,042 题上对 6 个开源模型(Gemma 2 2B/9B、Gemma 3 4B、Qwen3 0.6B/1.7B/8B)的实证表明:用 5,000 题可获得对全基准的 1.54 pp 95% 误差保证,只消耗 35.6% 的评测成本。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proves a **bounded-difference concentration inequality for infinitely exchangeable sequences** using the de Finetti representation. The effective variance proxy decomposes as $\sigma_{\mathrm{eff}}^2 = \frac{1}{4}\sum_i c_i^2 + \sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2$ (Theorem 4.2). The crucial structural insight is that for **zero-sum linear contrasts** — e.g., the difference between a subsample mean and the full population mean — the latent mixture term cancels exactly, yielding a tight, **mixture-free** Hoeffding-type bound (Theorem 5.2). Applied to the MMLU benchmark (14,042 items, 6 open-source LLMs): (a) a domain-stratified hierarchical model (item → subject → domain) produces **exchangeable 95% half-widths of 3.3%–5.3%** for the subject-macro score, **3–7× wider** than the naïve iid binomial half-widths of ~0.75%–0.83% (Table 3); (b) a random subset of **n = 5,000 items (35.6% of the benchmark) gives a model-agnostic 1.54 pp 95% bound on the full pooled score** (Table 4), and the empirical violations stay at ~0.65% (well below 5%). Result: a rigorous, distribution-free way to quantify MMLU uncertainty and trade evaluation cost against accuracy.

## 核心贡献

- **Theorem 4.2 (主浓度界)**: 无限可交换序列上有界差分函数满足
  $$P(|f - \mathbb{E}f| \ge t) \le 2\exp\!\left(-\frac{t^2}{2\sigma_{\mathrm{eff}}^2}\right), \quad \sigma_{\mathrm{eff}}^2 = \tfrac{1}{4}\sum_i c_i^2 + \sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2,$$
  其中 $\sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2$ 是条件期望 $\mathbb{E}[f|\Theta]$ 的最优亚高斯代理。
- **零和混合消去 (Proposition 5.1)**: 对 $f = \sum_i a_i X_i$ 且 $\sum a_i = 0$,必有 $\mathbb{E}[f|\Theta] = 0$,即 $\sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2 = 0$ 严格合法——无需估计混合项。
- **Theorem 5.2 (子样本-全样本界)**: $P(|\bar X_n - \bar X_N| \ge t) \le 2\exp\!\left(-\frac{2t^2 nN}{R^2(N-n)}\right)$,其中 $R = b-a$;当 $n \uparrow N$ 时半宽趋于 0。
- **与 Barber (2024) 的连接**: 该零和界是有限可交换带符号权重定理的"无限可扩展极限" $\varepsilon_M \to 0$,为后者提供了直接的 de Finetti 机制解释,并额外获得 $1/(1-n/N)$ 有限总体修正因子。
- **Corollary 6.1 (层级基准界)**: 对"领域 → 学科 → 题目"三层 MMLU 层次,有效方差拆为三项:
  $$\sigma_{\mathrm{hier}}^2 = \underbrace{\tfrac{1}{4}\sum_{d,k,j}\!\left(\tfrac{a_{d,k}}{m_{d,k}}\right)^{\!2}}_{\text{题目级 BD}} \;+\; \underbrace{\sum_d s_d^2 \sum_k a_{d,k}^2}_{\text{域内学科代理}} \;+\; \underbrace{\sigma_{\mathrm{dom}}^2}_{\text{域混合代理}}.$$
- **可估的学科级亚高斯代理**: 用 Marchal–Arbel 闭式上界 $s_d^2 = 1/[4(\hat\alpha_d + \hat\beta_d + 1)]$ 配合域内 beta–binomial MLE 拟合,得到保守但可计算的 plug-in 半宽。
- **MMLU 实证 (Section 7)**: 6 模型 × 14,042 题 × 4 域,报告(i)学科内 split-half 相关性(支持正相关假设)、(ii)学科-学科中心化热力图(支持四域分层)、(iii)交换式 vs iid 半宽对比、(iv)成本-精度 trade-off。
- **配对模型比较推论 (Remark 7.2)**: 两模型 $D_i = X_{B,i} - X_{A,i}$ 的子样本差距也是零和对比,可同样套用 Theorem 5.2 做"用多少题分辨模型 A、B"。

## Key Contributions

- **Theorem 4.2 — de Finetti–McDiarmid concentration.** For an infinitely exchangeable sequence with de Finetti directing measure $\Theta$ and a bounded-difference function $f$ with constants $(c_i)$, if $U_f = \mathbb{E}[f|\Theta] - \mathbb{E}f$ is $\sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2$-subgaussian,
  $$\mathbb{E}\exp(\lambda(f - \mathbb{E}f)) \le \exp\!\left(\lambda^2 \cdot \tfrac{1}{8}\sum c_i^2 + \tfrac{\lambda^2}{2}\sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2\right),$$
  giving the two-sided tail bound above. Proof: condition on $\Theta$, apply McDiarmid, integrate out $U_f$ with the subgaussian mgf assumption.
- **Tightness at the Gaussian scale (Example 4.3).** A Rademacher-perturbed Bernoulli construction achieves $\sigma_{\mathrm{eff},n}^2 = 5/(16n)$ with $\sqrt{n}(\bar X_n - 1/2) \Rightarrow N(0, 5/16)$, matching the proxy up to polynomial tail factors — the additive mixture term cannot, in general, be dropped.
- **Zero-sum cancellation (Prop 5.1).** For $f = \sum a_i X_i$ with $\sum a_i = 0$, the conditional expectation is $\mathbb{E}[f|\Theta] = (\sum a_i) \mu(\Theta) = 0$, so $\sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2 = 0$ is exact (not just an upper bound).
- **Subsample-vs-full bound (Theorem 5.2).** Closed-form $95\%$ half-width $\sqrt{\frac{N-n}{2nN}\log(2/\alpha)} \cdot (b-a)$, valid for **any** distribution of the exchangeable sequence — completely distribution-free.
- **Hierarchical benchmark corollary (6.1).** Three-component effective proxy accommodates the natural domain → subject → item nesting of composite benchmarks like MMLU.
- **Empirical validation on 6 LLMs × 14,042 MMLU items.** Split-half correlations within domains are 0.66–0.95 (positive, as predicted by Prop 3.2). Empirical |$\bar X_n - \bar X_N$| at $n=5{,}000$ is 1.06 pp (mean across 6 models) vs the 1.54 pp theorem bound; worst-case empirical coverage is 99.35%, well above the 95% nominal level.

## Methodology

**Theory pipeline.** 
1. **de Finetti reduction**: Write $f - \mathbb{E}f = \underbrace{f - \mathbb{E}[f|\Theta]}_{W} + \underbrace{\mathbb{E}[f|\Theta] - \mathbb{E}f}_{U_f}$. Conditional on $\Theta$, $W$ is McDiarmid-controlled with constants $(c_i)$, giving $\mathbb{E}[e^{\lambda W}|\Theta] \le \exp(\lambda^2/8 \sum c_i^2)$.
2. **Mixture assumption**: Define $\sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2$-subgaussianity of $U_f$ via $\mathbb{E}e^{\lambda U_f} \le \exp(\lambda^2 \sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2/2)$. This is **trivially true** for bounded $f$ (e.g., $[0,1]$ accuracies) — the substantive content is whether $\sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2$ is small enough to be useful.
3. **Sub-multiplicative step**: $\mathbb{E}e^{\lambda(f - \mathbb{E}f)} = \mathbb{E}[\mathbb{E}[e^{\lambda W}|\Theta] e^{\lambda U_f}] \le \exp(\lambda^2/8 \sum c_i^2) \cdot \mathbb{E}e^{\lambda U_f}$, then apply the subgaussian bound to the second factor.
4. **Tightness**: Example 4.3 (Rademacher mixture) shows the proxy is asymptotically exact.
5. **Zero-sum specialization**: Prop 5.1 + coefficients $a_j = 1/n - 1/N$ for $j \le n$, $a_j = -1/N$ for $j > n$ give $\sum a_j^2 = (N-n)/(nN)$.

**Hierarchical extension (Section 6).** Stack two mixture layers: $\Psi_d$ (domain) → $p_{d,k}|\Psi_d$ (subject) → $X_{d,k,j}|p_{d,k}$ (item). Two conditional McDiarmid steps + a fixed-stratum domain term yield Corollary 6.1. The **beta–binomial working model** $p_{d,k} \sim \mathrm{Beta}(\alpha_d,\beta_d)$ gives a tractable MLE; the **Marchal–Arbel (2017) bound** provides a closed-form subgaussian proxy $s_d^2 = 1/[4(\hat\alpha_d + \hat\beta_d + 1)]$, which is exact when $\alpha_d = \beta_d$ and a (conservative) upper bound otherwise.

**Empirical setup (Section 7).** Models: Gemma 2 2B/9B, Gemma 3 4B, Qwen3 0.6B/1.7B/8B, evaluated with the Simply JAX codebase (Google DeepMind), 5-shot multiple-choice on the full 14,042-question MMLU test set. Four fixed domains: STEM, Humanities, Social Science, Other. **Diagnostics**: (i) split-half subject correlations across 500 random item splits, (ii) model-centered subject heatmap with 6 models.

## Results & Highlights

**Table 1 — MMLU scores (6 models).** Pooled accuracy 45.09% (Qwen3 0.6B) to 74.36% (Qwen3 8B). Subject SDs of 9.7%–16.9%, subject ranges up to 45.0%–93.8% — large subject heterogeneity.

**Table 2 — split-half within-domain correlations (500 random splits).** All positive, range 0.659 (Qwen3 0.6B / STEM) to 0.946 (Gemma 3 4B / Humanities). **Necessary implication of infinite exchangeability (Prop 3.2) holds in the data** — though not a formal proof.

**Table 3 — Subject-macro 95% half-widths.**

| Model       | Macro | iid binomial | Exchangeable beta-proxy |
|-------------|-------|--------------|-------------------------|
| Qwen3 0.6B  | 46.5% | 0.82%        | **3.29%** (4.0×)        |
| Gemma 2 2B  | 54.6% | 0.83%        | **4.79%** (5.8×)        |
| Gemma 3 4B  | 59.5% | 0.82%        | **5.20%** (6.3×)        |
| Qwen3 1.7B  | 60.3% | 0.82%        | **4.01%** (4.9×)        |
| Gemma 2 9B  | 72.5% | 0.75%        | **5.26%** (7.0×)        |
| Qwen3 8B    | 76.4% | 0.72%        | **4.70%** (6.5×)        |

The iid baseline is **wildly optimistic**; the exchangeable bound is the operationally honest number.

**Table 4 — Cost-saving evaluation (Theorem 5.2 applied to MMLU pooled score).** With $N = 14{,}042$, $\alpha = 0.05$:

| n       | Theorem half-width | Mean empirical | Worst empirical | Max violation rate |
|---------|--------------------|----------------|-----------------|--------------------|
| 250     | 8.51 pp            | 5.85 pp        | 6.17 pp         | 0.65%              |
| 1,000   | 4.14 pp            | 2.86 pp        | 2.99 pp         | 0.63%              |
| 5,000   | **1.54 pp**        | 1.06 pp        | 1.11 pp         | 0.65%              |
| 10,000  | 0.73 pp            | 0.50 pp        | 0.52 pp         | 0.64%              |

Key message: **5,000 items (35.6% of MMLU) → 1.54 pp 95% bound; 2,000 items (14.2%) → 2.81 pp bound.** Empirical violations stay near 0.65% — the bound is conservative but valid. The bound is **common across all 6 models** and **free of any $\sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2$ estimate**.

**Figure 3 — centered subject heatmap.** Visible within-domain red blocks confirm the 4-domain stratification is qualitatively reasonable (though Remark 7.1 cautions that college biology vs. college mathematics may be negatively associated even within STEM).

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** The **additive** effective-proxy decomposition $\frac{1}{4}\sum c_i^2 + \sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2$ is a natural de Finetti-doob-martingale step and not surprising in itself. The genuinely new structural content is **(a) exact cancellation of $\sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2$ for zero-sum linear contrasts** and **(b) the explicit de Finetti mechanism** that explains why Barber (2024)'s signed-weight bound has the iid Hoeffding constant in the infinite-extendibility limit (Theorem 5.5 + Remark 5.4 + Corollary 5.6 together do the heavy lifting). The hierarchical Corollary 6.1 is a useful but more incremental extension of Theorem 4.2. The diagnostic + benchmark application is a meaningful, under-explored piece of work.

**Rigor.** Proofs appear clean and use only standard tools (de Finetti + Doob martingale + Chernoff). Tightness example (4.3) is a good practice — too many concentration papers skip this. The "trivially subgaussian for bounded $f$" observation (after Def 4.1) is honest about when the assumption is informative. The MLE-based plug-in (Section 6.2) is **explicitly flagged as model-based**, and the Marchal–Arbel proxy is documented as an upper bound, not the optimal one. Good epistemic discipline.

**Reproducibility / empirical honesty.** Six open-source models, 14,042 questions, full data pipeline described (Simply + 5-shot MC). All raw numbers appear in tables; both theory and empirical curves in Figure 4 are shown. The "max violation rate" column in Table 4 is a strong touch — it shows the bound holds empirically on realized data, not just asymptotically. The heatmap of subjects is a useful sanity check on the domain stratification.

**Significance.** This is precisely the kind of paper the LLM-evaluation community has been missing: a **non-asymptotic, distribution-free** confidence bound for benchmark scores that is *honest* (3–7× wider than naïve iid) yet **practically usable** (1.54 pp with 35.6% of the questions). The cost-saving corollary is immediately deployable by anyone running MMLU-style evaluations.

**Red flags / things to watch.**
- **Domain-level mixture is set to 0** ("fixed-stratum domain fluctuation" assumption after Eq. (6.1), with the justification that 4 domains are too few to fit a top-level mixture). This is a sensible practical choice but means the bound does **not** capture a potential "overall academic ability" factor common to all 4 domains; if such a factor exists, the 3.3%–5.3% exchangeable half-widths are still underestimates. The paper acknowledges this ("A model that imposes a general academic ability factor above the domains can still be handled by adding the corresponding top-level mixture proxy").
- The **beta–binomial MLE plug-in** ignores estimation uncertainty in $(\hat\alpha_d, \hat\beta_d)$ (Section 6.2 explicitly notes this is "model-based plug-in," not a full uncertainty interval). For a domain with only $K_d \sim 10$ subjects, this is a real gap.
- The split-half correlations are **necessary, not sufficient** for infinite exchangeability; the paper is honest about this.
- **MMLU itself has known annotation errors** (Gema et al. 2025); the bounds measure sampling variability, not benchmark-construction error. The Conclusion does not address this directly, though Section 7.6 flags it as a limitation.
- Empirical violation rates of ~0.65% (Table 4) are well below the nominal 5%, so the bound is **conservative** — possibly by 5–8× in coverage. The authors do not propose a finite-sample tightening (e.g., Bernstein-style variance-aware version) as future work, which is a missed opportunity.
- The framework assumes **fixed model**, but model rankings are exactly the application where the question matters most. How to incorporate model-dependent randomness into a single shared bound is not addressed.

## Strengths

- **Cleanly structured theorem hierarchy** (4.2 → 5.2 → 6.1), each building cleanly on the previous.
- **Direct de Finetti explanation** for a previously obscure cancellation phenomenon in Barber (2024)'s theorem.
- **Tightness example** (4.3) using a Rademacher-perturbed Bernoulli mixture shows the additive proxy is sharp at the Gaussian scale.
- **Conservative, honest calibration** of the Marchal–Arbel proxy, with explicit upper-bound framing.
- **Real, deployment-grade MMLU evaluation** (6 open-source models × full 14,042-item set) with split-half diagnostics, centered heatmap, and direct cost-saving numbers.
- **Free of parametric assumptions** for the cost-saving part (Theorem 5.2) — only requires the exchangeability assumption and bounded range.
- **Empirical coverage violations stay below 0.7%** across all 6 models and all subset sizes tested — the bound is genuinely valid.
- **Extendable** to paired model comparisons (Remark 7.2) and to other exchangeable benchmark structures (e.g., multi-turn evaluations, code benchmarks).

## Limitations

- **Model-based plug-in uncertainty** in $(\hat\alpha_d, \hat\beta_d)$ is not propagated into the reported half-widths (Section 6.2 + 7.6).
- **Fixed-stratum domain assumption** effectively zeros out the top-level academic-ability mixture; with 4 MMLU domains this is a real but hard-to-quantify omission.
- **Single fixed benchmark (MMLU)**: Generalization to GPQA, MMLU-Pro, HumanEval, LiveCodeBench, etc., is not empirically tested.
- **No Bernstein/Variance-aware refinement**: The pure Hoeffding tail is loose when empirical variance is small; for binary correctness the empirical $\hat p \hat q$ could be substituted into a Bernstein variant.
- **No formal test of infinite exchangeability** — only necessary-condition diagnostics. The framework is robust enough that the assumption can be a *modeling* choice, but it should not be presented as "data-driven."
- **MMLU annotation noise** (per Gema et al. 2025) is acknowledged but not modeled; the bounds target sampling variability, not benchmark-construction variability.
- **Random subset design** assumes uniform random selection; the bound does not cover structured subsampling (e.g., stratified by subject) without re-derivation.
- **Implementation complexity** for the hierarchical plug-in is non-trivial (MLE for each domain, Marchal–Arbel formula, MMLU subject metadata) — a reference implementation would help adoption.

## Relevance

高度相关于本项目关注的不确定性量化与 AI 基准可信度: 该工作直接处理 MMLU 这类复合基准的不确定性,提供不依赖 i.i.d. 假设的子样本 Hoeffding 型置信界 (Theorem 5.2),并给出"用 5,000 题(35.6% 成本)获得 1.54 pp 95% 误差保证"这样的可操作结论。同时把经验上过分乐观的 iid 半宽校正为更现实的 3–7× 交换式半宽,对 LLM 评测报告里"±0.8%"那种过窄误差条是直接的理论警示。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- 将此框架推广到 **GPQA / MMLU-Pro / HumanEval / LiveCodeBench**: 这些基准的领域结构、题目相关性、是否近似可交换需要单独诊断。
- **Bernstein 型 / 经验方差修正版本**: 用 $\hat p \hat q$ 替代 $\sigma^2$ 上界,可能让半宽在小子集下大幅收紧(目前 Theorem 5.2 完全不利用题目级经验方差)。
- 如何在**模型排名 / 配对比较**场景下使用 Theorem 5.2 与 Remark 7.2: 给定目标 delta,求解"在 95% 置信下区分两个模型至少需要多少共同题"。
- 4 域之上是否真有"总体学术能力"$\Psi$ 因子? 能否用全基准分数在不同基准(GPTQ、MMLU、ARC)上的跨任务相关性间接估出 $\sigma_{\mathrm{top}}^2$?
- 论文的 $\sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2$ 隐含假设学科间为可交换; 现实 MMLU 中"应用数学 vs. 纯数学"是否更接近"分块可交换"? 分块零和的混合项是否仍消去?
- 把 MMLU-Redux (Gema et al. 2025) 的标注错误率作为额外方差源加入 $\sigma_{\mathrm{mix}}^2$,得到"采样 + 标注"的合成误差预算。
- 当 $n/N$ 较大时,Theorem 5.2 的 Hoeffding 衰减率可与 **bootstrap / 子样本一致性** 做仿真比较; 论文的 1.54 pp bound 在 5,000 题下是否过于保守? 可在 6 模型上做覆盖性精细校准。
- 与 **pac-bayes / 排列对称函数 (Tolstikhin 2017)** 的视角做交叉: de Finetti 框架与 permutation-invariant 函数族的泛化界是否给出可比常数?
