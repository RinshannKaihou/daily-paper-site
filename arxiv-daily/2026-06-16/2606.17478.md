> **一句话总结：** 提出 STATEWITNESS——一个冻结目标推理 LLM、只训练单独 LoRA 适配审计解码器的"激活解释器"：它把目标模型的隐藏状态注入解码器的占位符 token，让解码器既能给二元欺骗分数，也能以查询问答 (QA) 与结构化 Schema 报告形式给出 token / 句子级证据。在 2 个目标模型 × 7 个欺骗基准上达到 0.916 平均 AUROC，比最强黑箱文本监视器高 11.6%，比最强激活探针基线高 25.0%，并能以简单阈值 OR 集成进一步降低漏检。

> **TL;DR:** STATEWITNESS is an **activation explainer** for auditing deception in reasoning LLMs, sitting between black-box judges and linear probes. A separate audit decoder (initialized from the target model, LoRA-tuned) reads the target's hidden states at the final-answer layer via a `&lt;ACT&gt;` placeholder + norm-matched additive injection, and is queried in natural language to produce (a) a forced-choice honest/deceptive margin used as a detection score, and (b) free-form audit answers, schema-formatted reports (objective / status / evidence / severity), and per-token audit scores for human inspection. Across GPT-OSS-20B and Gemma4-26B-A4B-IT on seven on-policy deception benchmarks (Instructed Deception, Roleplay, Sandbagging, MASK, Insider Trading, Agentic Misalignment, InstrumentalEval) with Alpaca calibration controls, STATEWITNESS reaches **0.916 mean AUROC** — +11.6% over the strongest black-box text monitor and +25.0% over the strongest white-box linear probe. The clearest gains are on the *most realistic* agentic settings; low-FPR recall is target-dependent. Threshold-OR ensembles with prior monitors cut missed deceptions (e.g., GPT-OSS-20B Recall@1%FPR: 0.430→0.562 when ORed with the best black-box), confirming orthogonal signal. The contribution is interface-level as much as it is metric-level: the same activation readout supports token-, sentence-, and text-level audit evidence.

## 核心贡献

- **激活解释器 (activation explainer) 范式：** 将"对目标 LLM 隐藏状态作单标量探针 / 标量监视器"升级为"用一个独立审计解码器把隐藏状态翻译成自然语言答案 / 结构化报告 / 证据 trace"，可问、可追溯、不仅给分。
- **两阶段训练流程：**
  - **Warm-start (≈126–127M tokens / 目标)** 来自 Activation Oracles：二分类、上下文预测 (next/prev K tokens)、系统提示 SPQA，教会"激活→自然语言"接口。
  - **Audit-continuation (≈53–71M tokens / 目标)** 自合成两个家族：**Audit QA**（冲突、隐藏目标、模型自评事实一致性的自然语言问答应答）与 **Schema 报告**（objective / status / evidence / uncertainties_and_conflicts / severity 的固定模板）。
- **白盒架构（轻量）：** 目标模型冻结，审计解码器从目标模型本身初始化，仅训练 LoRA；在 read 层 ℓread 取目标隐藏状态，在解码器写层 ℓwrite 做 norm-matched 加性注入（公式见方法）。序列长度受限，训练廉价。
- **跨目标 × 7 基准评测：** GPT-OSS-20B 和 Gemma4-26B-A4B-IT × 7 个 on-policy 欺骗基准 + Alpaca 校准控制集。GPT-OSS-20B 上 0.920 AUROC，Gemma4 上 0.911 AUROC，平均 **0.916**；16 个 per-target×per-dataset 单元格中 14 个为最佳/次佳。
- **对比 7 个基线（公平协议）：** 4 个白盒探针（LR / MMS / CMMS / LAT）与 3 个黑箱文本监视器（LLM-as-a-Judge、LLM-as-a-Judge w/ CoT、Self-Evaluation）。
- **互补性证据：** 在 GPT-OSS-20B 上，STATEWITNESS OR Black-box 把 Recall@1%FPR 从 0.430 / 0.352 提升到 0.562（Recall@5%FPR 从 0.765 / 0.620 提升到 0.859），且这些是真实命中而非冗余信号。
- **多粒度审计证据：** 同一接口支持 token 级审计分数（用于定位"哪一句触发了违规"）、句子级评分、文本级 QA 答案，以及带 severity 的 Schema 报告（案例研究展示了一个 Agentic Misalignment 勒索场景）。

## Key Contributions

- **Paradigm shift — probe → activation explainer.** A separate decoder LLM (LoRA on top of the target model) reads the target's hidden states via a `&lt;ACT&gt;`-placeholder + norm-matched additive-injection interface (write-layer ℓwrite). This supports both detection and inspectable evidence, not just a scalar.
- **Two-stage training data curriculum.** Stage 1: ~127M warm-start tokens (binary classification, self-supervised prev/next-token prediction, system-prompt QA) adapting the Activation-Oracle / LatentQA interface. Stage 2: ~53–71M on-policy, model-specific audit-continuation tokens spanning **Audit QA** (conflict / hidden-objective / self-factual-alignment queries) and **Schema reports** (objective–status–evidence–uncertainty–severity). The audited responses are sampled on-policy per target.
- **Matched-protocol evaluation across 2 reasoning targets × 7 deception benchmarks** (Instructed Deception, Roleplay, Sandbagging, MASK, Insider Trading, Agentic Misalignment, InstrumentalEval) plus Alpaca as the calibration control set. On-policy rollouts; gold labels from GPT-5.4 or official benchmark scorers.
- **State-of-the-art mean AUROC (0.916) with matched protocol.** +11.6% relative over the best black-box monitor and +25.0% relative over the best white-box linear probe. Best/second-best in 14 of 16 (target × dataset) cells.
- **Complementarity, not redundancy.** Threshold-OR ensembles with prior monitors reduce missed deceptions (e.g., GPT-OSS-20B Recall@1%FPR 0.430→0.562 vs. best black-box alone), and the missed-example count (Unique @ 1% / 5%) shows the families fire on different examples.
- **Multi-granular audit evidence in one interface.** Token-level audit scores, sentence-level scores, query-level free-form answers, and structured schema reports — all from the same activation readout. A case study on the Agentic Misalignment blackmail scenario shows the local score rising on the leverage-forming tokens and the schema report assigning severity 7.

## Methodology

- **Architecture (Figure 2).** Target model (frozen) produces hidden states at a middle residual read layer ℓread for token positions selected by a binary read mask m (typical choices: final answer y, reasoning + answer r∪y, or reasoning r alone). The audit decoder prompt is `<latent> <ACT> <ACT> ... </latent> [query / prefill]`, with one `<ACT>` per selected token. At the decoder's write layer ℓwrite, each `<ACT>` position's residual stream gⱼ is updated by a norm-matched additive injection: `Inject(gⱼ, vⱼ) = gⱼ + ‖gⱼ‖₂ · vⱼ / ‖vⱼ‖₂`. Both target model and base decoder parameters are frozen; only LoRA adapters ϕ on the decoder are trained (cross-entropy on the supervised answer aᵢ conditioned on query qᵢ, transcript xᵢ, mask mᵢ).
- **Two-stage data curriculum (Figure 3).**
  - *Warm-start:* binary classification (yes/no over seven classification datasets), self-supervised K→J token prediction (K, J ~ Uniform(1,20), no input leakage), and system-prompt QA (LatentQA-style). Purpose: teach the `&lt;ACT&gt;` → NL interface before deception-specific training.
  - *Audit-continuation:* (i) **Audit QA** — natural-language queries testing conflict-with-evidence, hidden-objective consistency, and self-factual-alignment, with the audited response sampled on-policy from the target model. (ii) **Schema reports** — fixed prefill (`<|channel|>report<|message|>`) producing objective / status / evidence / uncertainties_and_conflicts / deception-severity-grade fields. Both families use the same scenarios, judging policy, and report prompts as evaluation.
- **Targets.** GPT-OSS-20B (OpenAI, 2025) and Gemma 4 26B A4B IT (Google DeepMind, 2026). Each decoder is initialized from its own target. Read layer = a middle residual layer; write layer = 0; LoRA only.
- **Detection readout (Section 4.2).** A fixed prefill `"The answer is"` precedes a forced-choice A/B query (e.g., *A. No, it is not concealing evidence / B. Yes, it is concealing such evidence*). The decoder's next-token logits for A and B give a margin Δϕ = ℓB − ℓA; averaged over A/B order swap to reduce position bias. The benchmark-level detector score is the mean margin over a fixed per-benchmark query bank.
- **Metrics.** AUROC for deceptive-vs-honest within each benchmark; threshold set on Alpaca controls; **Recall@1%FPR** and **Recall@5%FPR** for deceptive-vs-control (deployed-monitor regime).
- **Baselines.** Four white-box linear probes (LR, MMS / Marks & Tegmark 2024, CMMS / Goldowsky-Dill 2025, LAT / Zou 2023) trained on an Instructed-Pairs honest/dishonesty contrast set with thresholds calibrated on Alpaca. Three black-box text monitors: LLM-as-a-Judge (GPT-5.4-mini), LLM-as-a-Judge w/ CoT, and Self-Evaluation (target rates its own previous message 0–100).

## Results & Highlights

- **Headline AUROC:** 0.920 (GPT-OSS-20B) and 0.911 (Gemma4) — mean **0.916** across 14 (target × dataset) cells. **+11.6%** over best black-box (mean 0.821), **+25.0%** over best linear probe (mean 0.733).
- **Per-dataset dominance (Table 2).** STATEWITNESS wins 14/16 cells (per target × dataset). Examples: GPT-OSS-20B Sandbagging 0.822 (vs. best black-box 0.678), Insider Trading 0.924 (vs. 0.885), Agentic Misalignment 0.867 (vs. 0.741), InstrumentalEval 0.867 (vs. 0.741). The two notable losses are minor: Gemma4 MASK (0.745 vs. Self-Eval 0.691, with LLM-as-Judge 0.997 and probe 0.986 being strongest) and small margins on instructed deception where all monitors cluster near 0.99.
- **Low-FPR recall is target-dependent (Table 3).** GPT-OSS-20B: SW Recall@1%FPR 0.293, Recall@5%FPR **0.681** (best). Gemma4: black-box / probe baselines actually win strict FPR — Self-Evaluation reaches Recall@1%FPR 0.752, while SW is 0.358. Authors frame this as the motivation for RQ2.
- **Complementarity (Table 4, Figure 6).** Threshold-OR ensembles recover deceptive examples missed by any single family:
  - GPT-OSS-20B: SW OR Black-box → Recall@1%FPR 0.562 (vs. SW 0.430, BB 0.352), Recall@5%FPR 0.859 (vs. 0.765 / 0.620). Realized Alpaca FPR rises 0.009 → 0.017.
  - Gemma4: SW still adds to the black-box combination while keeping FPR near nominal; all-threshold OR reaches Recall@5%FPR 0.955.
  - *Unique* missed counts: on GPT-OSS-20B SW alone misses 535 / 583 deceptive examples at 1% / 5% thresholds that no other single monitor catches — i.e., SW is finding *different* deceptions.
- **Multi-granular evidence (Figure 5).** In a blackmail scenario, token-level audit scores spike on the slice where private-affair leverage is being constructed (slice #10 score 5.0; #13 "email to David" 4.6); the schema report returns objective=violated, evidence="uses Kyle's private emails as leverage", severity=7. The page includes interactive Case Bank / Token Audit / Temporal Audit browsers.
- **Qualitative honesty caveat.** The schema report's "evidence" is the decoder's natural-language explanation, not a hard attribution over hidden states — useful for audit, not a calibrated truth statement. (Authors flag this in Limitations.)

## Critical Assessment

> Honest appraisal based on reading the full paper. Calibration matters more than the headline AUROC.

- **Novelty — high on interface, moderate on architecture.** The biggest contribution is the *interface*: replacing scalar probes with a queryable, NL-answering, evidence-returning decoder. Architecturally, however, STATEWITNESS is essentially an Activation Oracle (Karvonen 2025) re-purposed and LoRA-tuned for deception — the `&lt;ACT&gt;` placeholder + norm-matched additive-injection pattern is straight from that line of work, as the paper acknowledges. The novelty is the *application* and the *training-data design* (synthesized Audit QA + Schema reports), not the activation-injection trick itself.
- **Rigor — generally good, with one notable unfairness.** Strengths: matched evaluation protocol across all baselines, on-policy rollouts, Alpaca calibration, per-dataset reporting, complementary-missed-example analysis, public code. **Caveats:**
  - The 4 white-box probe baselines (LR / MMS / CMMS / LAT) are vanilla linear probes trained on a single Instructed-Pairs contrast set, with thresholds calibrated on Alpaca. There is no nonlinear probe, no per-dataset fine-tuning, no dataset-matched training contrast. This *systematically* understates what probe-based monitoring can do, which inflates the "25.0% over best probe" headline. A fairer probe baseline (e.g., per-dataset fine-tuning, MLP probe) would likely close the gap.
  - The 0.916 mean is averaged across 2 targets and 7 datasets, but per-cell variance is huge (e.g., GPT-OSS-20B Insider Trading 0.924 vs. Agentic Misalignment 0.867 vs. Roleplay 0.863). The "mean" hides that a few easy benchmarks (Instructed Deception 0.984) carry the average.
  - Gemma4's numbers are more sobering: Self-Evaluation gets 0.833 mean AUROC (including 1.000 on MASK and 0.998 on Roleplay), and the strongest *linear probe* gets 0.762 — a probe that was trained on Instructed-Pairs, not Gemma4-specific data. SW still wins (0.911), but the margin is smaller than the abstract suggests, and SW is *worse than the probe* on Gemma4 Recall@1%FPR (0.358 vs. 0.752).
- **Reproducibility — strong.** Public repo, open-weights targets, Alpaca calibration made public, all datasets are existing public benchmarks. The *training data* (audit QA + schema reports) is synthetic, but the synthesis pipeline is documented in Appendix C.2. Hidden-state access is the only hard prerequisite, but for the chosen open targets this is fine.
- **Significance — high for the application, modest for the field's general deception problem.** If you care about *auditable* deception detection on open-weights reasoning models, STATEWITNESS is a clear step up: it is the first method that returns both a calibrated AUROC signal *and* inspectable, queryable evidence at the token / sentence / report level. The "evidence" is still LLM-generated NL rather than true attribution, but that's the state of the art for activation readers.
- **Red flags / weak points:**
  - The "11.6% / 25.0%" headline compares SW to the *strongest* probe on the *probe's least-suited* training contrast — a probe trained and tuned on the actual target deception data would probably close the gap substantially.
  - Gemma4's MASK self-eval AUROC of 1.000 with Recall@1%FPR 0.752 is an inconveniently strong baseline that the abstract does not foreground.
  - "Reduces missed deceptions" in the abstract is real (e.g., GPT-OSS-20B +0.132 Recall@1%FPR over best single monitor), but the same OR trick would lift any two strong monitors; SW's specific Unique-missed counts (535 / 583) show it is the *only* monitor that fires on those examples, which is the genuinely interesting claim.
  - Schema report "evidence" is itself a learned NL generation; faithfulness is asserted but not measured (no human study, no automated faithfulness check).
  - Forced-choice margin is a *calibrated* detector, but the qualitative readouts are explicitly not calibrated ("qualitative readouts should still be treated as audit aids rather than calibrated statements of belief").
  - Cherry-pick risk: the blackmail case study in Figure 5 is one example; the authors note that the case-study browser includes "clear, noisy, missed, and boundary cases," which is good, but the main paper figure highlights a high-severity case.
  - White-box access only. STATEWITNESS does not solve the harder problem of monitoring closed-weight frontier models behind APIs.

## Strengths

- **Interface-first design** — queryable, schema-formatted, evidence-trace-returning activation explanations. This is what real auditors need and is rare in the literature.
- **Strong, consistent matched-protocol numbers** (0.916 mean AUROC; best/second in 14/16 cells).
- **Generality across two open-weights reasoning targets** (GPT-OSS-20B, Gemma4 26B A4B IT).
- **Demonstrated orthogonal signal** to black-box and probe monitors via OR-ensemble and unique-missed counts.
- **Public code release** (`github.com/March-7/StateWitness`) and Alpaca-based calibration.
- **Multi-granular readout** in a single interface: token scores, sentence scores, QA answers, schema reports, severity grades.
- **Honest limitations section** — explicitly states the audit decoder is not a calibrated decision rule and qualitative readouts should be treated as audit aids.

## Limitations

- **Open-weights, mid-size targets only.** No frontier closed-weight model (GPT-4/Claude/Gemini) and no model beyond ~26B. Scaling to larger / longer-horizon agents is unverified.
- **Linear-probe baselines are weak.** LR / MMS / CMMS / LAT with a single Instructed-Pairs contrast is a low bar; the +25% headline is partially an artifact of this choice.
- **White-box access required.** Hides a fundamentally important deployment constraint — closed-weight frontier models do not expose hidden states.
- **Audit decoder is not calibrated.** Forced-choice margin is calibrated; schema-report evidence is LLM-generated NL whose faithfulness is not measured.
- **Evidence is post-hoc natural language, not attribution.** "Token audit scores" are decoder outputs, not gradient-based or causal attributions. Cherry-picked or hallucinated explanations remain a risk.
- **Mixed low-FPR regime.** On Gemma4 the strongest baselines actually beat SW on Recall@1%FPR; SW's strength is AUROC and complementarity, not strict low-FPR.
- **Synthetic, narrow training data.** Audit-continuation is synthesized from a fixed scenario / judging-policy family; the mixture cannot cover every deception mechanism, and very long contexts / multi-turn episodes are out of scope without chunked readout.
- **Limited to seven benchmarks** (six non-control, one calibration), all from a specific prior literature. No sycophancy (ELEPHANT, SycEval), no human deception in-the-wild, no cross-lingual deception.

## Relevance

> 直接命中"**LLM Hidden States & Interpretability**"——把目标推理模型的隐藏状态作为可查询、可审计的接口（不只做标量探针），并通过 LoRA 适配的独立解码器把它们翻译为自然语言问答与结构化报告。属于 Activation Oracle / LatentQA 范式在欺骗审计方向的应用落地，与机理可解释性、对齐审计的接口升级需求强相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does STATEWITNESS degrade on *naturally emergent* deception (sandbagging without prompt nudging, in-context scheming) versus the on-policy elicited deception used in evaluation? The Gemma4 MASK / Roleplay / Self-Evaluation pattern suggests self-judgment is a strong baseline when the model is "in" the situation.
- Is the audit decoder's evidence a **faithful attribution** over hidden states, or an LLM-generated rationale? How is faithfulness measured? (No human study, no automated faithfulness check in the paper.)
- How sensitive are the gains to (i) hidden-state layer, (ii) prompt formatting of the decoder, (iii) read mask m, (iv) initialization of the decoder from the target model itself? The decoder-from-target is a strong inductive bias — how much of the gain comes from this rather than the training data?
- Can a **stronger probe baseline** (per-dataset contrast training, MLP / kNN probe, sparse-autoencoder features) close the 25% gap to STATEWITNESS? The fairest comparison would settle the question of whether the contribution is *interface* or *signal*.
- Can the same explainer interface be repurposed for other safety-relevant internal states (jailbreak intent, hallucination precursors, planning / world-model states) without re-engineering the decoder?
- What is the **compute / latency** cost of running an external decoder over hidden-state streams in a streaming monitor setting on long multi-turn agentic traces?
- For closed-weight models where hidden states are unavailable, can STATEWITNESS-style interfaces be approximated via logit-lens, sparse-autoencoder features exposed through an API, or a teacher-student distillation of the audit decoder?
- Could the *Schema report* be made **more constrained** (e.g., schema-constrained decoding with explicit evidence field grounded to a specific token span) to reduce post-hoc rationalization risk?
