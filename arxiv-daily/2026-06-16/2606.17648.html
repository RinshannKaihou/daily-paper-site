From Brewing to Resolution: Tracing the Internal Lifecycle of Code Reasoning in LLMs

AuthorsSiyue Chen, Yifu Guo, Yuquan Lu, Zishan Xu, Jiaye Lin, Jianbo Lin, Siyu Zhang, Cheng Yang, Junxin Li, Yujia Li, Yu Huo, Ruixuan Wang
mechanistic interpretability code reasoning linear probing context-stripped decoding decoder-only transformers
Key Finding

Code reasoning in LLMs runs a ~38% brewing interval where the answer is linearly recoverable but not yet self-decodable — a stable scaffold across 16 models, 0.5B–14B.

提出"酿造—消解"双阶段代码推理生命周期。答案在 ~14% 深度即可被线性探针恢复(FPCL),但要等到 ~50% 深度才被模型自身解码管线可读(FJC);这 38% 的酿造区间在跨架构、跨规模的 16 个模型上稳定归一化于 24–42%。

Key Metric
0.850
Resolution Functional ρ — AUC (Resolved-vs-Rest)
Closed-form, endpoint statistics only — i.e. the four-outcome lifecycle is observable without ground truth.
Why This Matters

Decouples a stable scaffold (brewing duration, 24–42%) from capability-driven resolution — giving interpretability a reusable diagnostic pair (availability vs. readiness) and practitioners an outcome-aware lever for adaptive depth allocation.

Critical Edge

The headline 47.8% α-blend rescue of Overprocessed samples is presented as evidence of geometric mismatch, but a stronger control (inject from an unrelated donor) is missing — the effect may partly reflect generic partial-recovery from any reasonable signal.

01 Methodology 方法
  • Dual diagnostic lens. Layer-wise linear probing ΦPℓ(hℓ) tests information availability; Context-Stripped Decoding ΦCℓ (Patchscopes-style) tests information readiness by injecting hℓ into a stripped target prompt and subtracting the T-only logit baseline.
  • Lifecycle landmarks. FPCL = first layer where probe argmax matches gold; FJC = first layer where probe and CSD both match. ∆_brew = FJC − FPCL ≈ 10.7 layers, 38% of depth (anchor setting).
  • CUE-Bench. 6 synthetic code-reasoning families (Value Tracking, Computing, Conditional, Function Call, Loop, Loop-unrolled); single-digit answers 0–9 plus a residual class d̄; 11-class diagnostic space; 24,300 samples / model, 150 controlled configs.
  • Outcome taxonomy. Resolved (∃FJC ∧ correct), Overprocessed (∃FJC ∧ wrong), Misresolved (∄FJC ∧ tail CSD ≥ 0.5), Unresolved (∄FJC ∧ tail CSD < 0.5).
  • Three causal interventions. (i) FJC-layer activation patching; (ii) Overprocessed layer-skipping with h = 0.7·horig + 0.3·hFJC; (iii) FPCL re-injection for Unresolved.
  • Coverage. 16 decoder-only Transformers (0.5B–14B) across Qwen2.5-Coder/2.5/3, DeepSeek-Coder, CodeLlama, Llama-2; external validation on CRUXEval-O.
02 Results & Highlights 结果
  • Anchor Qwen2.5-Coder-7B (24,300 samples): Resolved 41.5% · Overprocessed 26.4% · Misresolved 8.5% · Unresolved 23.7%. Three of six tasks fall below 30% Resolved.
  • Brewing is universal. Normalized ∆_brew / L ∈ [24%, 42%] across all 16 models; small models sit at the low end (0.5B ≈ 29%) because both probes and CSD are weaker; mid–large plateau at 33–39%.
  • Function-call indirection is the dominant bottleneck. Resolved collapses 61.1% → 2.5% (depth 1 → 3); Unresolved rises 19.1% → 57.4%; FJC existence drops 78% → 40%.
  • Scaling sharpens, doesn't create. Coder-0.5B: NO_BREWING ≈ 10%, Resolved 18%; Coder-14B: NO_BREWING ≈ 1%, Resolved 50.3% — Unresolved dissolves with depth, the scaffold itself does not move.
  • Patching at FJC flips the answer at 20–44% rate (vs. 3–18% at FJC−8…FJC−2); post-FJC nonlinear transforms can amplify (27–51% at FJC+2…FJC+4).
  • α-blend injection rescues 47.8% of Overprocessed on average (36.7–67.3% per task) vs. 10.4% with raw replacement — supporting the geometric-mismatch reading.
  • Re-injection from FPCL recovers 22–38% of Unresolved; Resolved controls stay at 84–100% — Unresolved is not finished, not incapable.
  • Loop vs. Loop-unrolled. 35.5% vs. 28.0% Resolved on identical arithmetic; transplanting loop's late-MLP write (L25–26) into the unrolled twin closes 82.6% of the readiness gap.
03 Strengths & Limitations 优势 / 局限

Strengths

  • Clean separation of availability vs. readiness — most prior interpretability work conflates the two.
  • Strong empirical breadth: 16 models × 6 families × 0.5B–14B; three independent causal interventions converge on the same four-outcome taxonomy.
  • Controlled per-axis sweeps (depth, distractors, iterations, operators) localize failure axes rather than reporting aggregate accuracy.
  • Code release, CRUXEval-O external corroboration, and a ground-truth-free Resolution Functional make the framework deployable at inference time.
  • Actionable: Overprocessed-aware early-exit vs. Unresolved-aware re-injection are diametrically opposed — motivating outcome-aware depth allocation.

Limitations

  • Restricted to decoder-only Transformers; encoder-decoder (T5, CodeT5) and MoE (Mixtral, DeepSeek-MoE) untested.
  • Single-digit answer space (0–9) — multi-token outputs (variable names, multi-digit numbers) not validated.
  • CUE-Bench is short, single-primitive synthetic code; real repository-scale compositional code is unproven.
  • Layer-granularity only — cannot pinpoint attention heads or MLP sublayers; path patching is left as future work.
  • α-blend injection for Overprocessed is hyperparameter-dependent (α = 0.3 hand-chosen); no systematic α-sweep in the main text.
  • Aggregate Resolved = 41.5% is a single-model anchor (Coder-7B); cross-model confidence intervals not foregrounded.
04 Questions for Further Exploration 开放问题
  1. Does the 24–42% brewing-scaffold band generalize to encoder-decoder (CodeT5, T5) and MoE (Mixtral, DeepSeek-MoE) — or is it specific to decoder-only residual streams?
  2. Can the Resolution Functional ρ be deployed at inference time as a runtime monitor, and does it generalize out-of-distribution to multi-token outputs?
  3. Is the Overprocessed geometric corruption (Hoyer sparsity peak at L22–24) also present in natural-language reasoning (math, multi-hop QA), or specific to code's discrete-symbol structure?
  4. Does the loop vs. unrolled late-MLP consolidation finding survive in larger models and in models trained on more code data, where unrolled code is rarer in pretraining?
  5. Can targeted fine-tuning of the resolution phase (post-FJC layers only) shift the outcome distribution more efficiently than full-model fine-tuning, given the scaffold's apparent invariance?
  6. Does the α-blend rescue generalize to a practical inference-time correction (early-exit + re-decode) that improves Resolved rate without retraining?
  7. Why do late layers corrupt correct answers (Overprocessed) — necessary consequence of residual-stream geometry, or a contingent property of current training regimes?
  8. Is the availability-before-readiness ordering structural to residual-stream Transformers, or an artifact of standard pretraining objectives?
