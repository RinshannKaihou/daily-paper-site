# Kolmogorov Regression for Robust Diffusion Policies

> **一句话总结：** 提出 Kolmogorov 回归框架——通过反向 Kolmogorov 方程将有限维扩散策略提升到 Cameron-Martin 子空间，用 Matérn-3/2 协方差的有色噪声 + precision-weighted Cameron-Martin 损失 + 反向 Kolmogorov 残差诊断三处替换，使神经网络架构保持不变的同时在 PushT 上提升最大回合奖励 17%、漂移降低 67.6%，并在 6 工位 CONWIP 制造线上以 Hamilton-Jacobi 到达性认证减少 96% 死锁。

> **TL;DR:** The paper recasts finite-dimensional diffusion policy training as a deterministic backward Kolmogorov boundary-value problem on a Cameron-Martin subspace of $L^2([0,T], \mathbb{R}^{d_a})$, requiring only three drop-in substitutions to a standard DDPM: (i) colored forward noise $\eta = L_N \xi$ with $L_N = \mathrm{chol}(K)$ for a Matérn-3/2 Gram matrix $K$; (ii) a precision-weighted Cameron-Martin loss $L_{CM} = \mathbb{E}\|C_\mu^{-1/2}(\eta_\theta - \eta)\|_H^2$; and (iii) the same colored noise at inference. A Kolmogorov residual $R(\hat{u})$ (estimated via Hutchinson trace estimator) is introduced as a reward-free failure detector. Theory: a total-variation bound with constants depending only on $\mathrm{Tr}(C_\mu)$ — not on action dimension, planning horizon, or discretization resolution. Results: 0.95 vs. 0.78 max reward on PushT (+17%), 67.6% residual drop (0.11 vs. 0.34), 28.4% lower RMSE than an LSTM baseline on a 6-station CONWIP manufacturing line with starvation recall = 1.0 and bottleneck Precision@1 = 1.0, and a Hamilton-Jacobi reachability certification that prevents 96% of deadlocks (351/365) over 100 simulated runs.

## 核心贡献

- 将有限维扩散策略严格提升到 $L^2([0,T], \mathbb{R}^{d_a})$ 上的 Cameron-Martin 子空间，把随机 score matching 替换为反向 Kolmogorov 方程 (BKE) 描述的确定性边值 PDE
- 给出对标准 DDPM 的"三处最小替换"：(a) Matérn-3/2 协方差的有色前向噪声 $\eta = L_N\xi$；(b) precision-weighted Cameron-Martin 损失 $L_{CM} = \mathbb{E}\|C_\mu^{-1/2}(\eta_\theta - \eta)\|_H^2$；(c) 推理时使用同一 $L_N$ 的有色反向噪声；网络架构（ConditionalUnet1D + ResNet-18）保持不变
- 给出维度独立的全变差收敛界（Theorem 1）：常数仅依赖 $\mathrm{Tr}(C_\mu)$ 而非动作维度、规划时域或离散化分辨率
- 提出 Kolmogorov residual $R(\hat{u})$ 作为无奖励的确定性失效检测器，使用 Hutchinson trace estimator 估计 Hessian 迹
- 推导出与 $\alpha \in \{0, 0.15, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 0.85, 1.0\}$ 混合的 mixed-precision 损失族，证明在 PushT 上随 $\alpha$ 单调下降的 residual 与平滑度提升
- 首次将 Hamilton-Jacobi 到达性与扩散策略结合，在 6 工位 CONWIP 制造线上预防 96% 死锁（351/365 事件），在 PushT 上提供无 oracle 的实时失效预警

## Key Contributions

- Lifts finite-dimensional diffusion policy training to an infinite-dimensional (ID) formulation on the Cameron-Martin subspace of $H = L^2([0,T], \mathbb{R}^{d_a})$, replacing stochastic score matching with a deterministic backward Kolmogorov equation
- Specifies three minimal substitutions to standard DDPM that preserve the denoising network architecture entirely: Matérn-3/2 colored noise $L_N \xi$; precision-weighted Cameron-Martin loss; same colored noise at inference
- Proves a dimension-independent total-variation convergence bound whose constants depend on $\mathrm{Tr}(C_\mu)$ and the Mercer eigenstructure, not on action dimension $d_a$, planning horizon $T$, or discretization resolution
- Introduces the Kolmogorov residual as a deterministic, reward-free failure detector (a scalar PDE-violation measure), with Hessian traces estimated via Hutchinson stochastic trace estimator
- Empirically demonstrates a +17% max-episode-reward gain on PushT (0.95 vs. 0.78 for MSE), 67.6% Kolmogorov-residual reduction (0.11 vs. 0.34), and a monotonic residual decrease across $\alpha$ for mixed-precision losses
- Reports 28.4% lower RMSE than a published LSTM baseline (Chen et al. 2021) on a 6-station CONWIP line simulated in SimPy, with starvation-event recall = 1.0 and bottleneck Precision@1 = 1.0, 13× SNR
- Certifies dispatch policies via Hamilton-Jacobi reachability (using the author's own `LevelSetPy` toolbox), reducing deadlocks by 96% (351 prevented) over 100 simulated runs

## Methodology

- **Hilbert-space setup.** Action trajectories $a : [0,T] \to \mathbb{R}^{d_a}$ are posed in $H = L^2([0,T], \mathbb{R}^{d_a})$ with a Gaussian prior $\mu_0 = \mathcal{N}(0, C_\mu)$ whose covariance operator is realized through a Matérn-3/2 kernel $k(t,s) = \sigma^2 (1 + \sqrt{3}|t-s|/\ell) \exp(-\sqrt{3}|t-s|/\ell)$, yielding $C^1$ sample paths and $\mathrm{Tr}(C_\mu) < \infty$.
- **Cameron-Martin subspace.** The Cameron-Martin space $H_C = \{f \in H : \|C_\mu^{-1/2} f\|_H < \infty\}$ is the unique subspace where the Gaussian measure is absolutely continuous under translation. The denoising network is constrained to operate in this geometrically correct subspace.
- **Forward / backward processes.** The forward OU process corrupts $X_0 \sim \mu_\mathrm{data}$ into $\mathcal{N}(0, C_\mu)$ as $t \to \infty$ via a $C_\mu$-Wiener process (trace-class, Mercer-decomposed). The conditional $X_s \mid X_0 = a_0 \sim \mathcal{N}(e^{-s/2} a_0, (1-e^{-s}) C_\mu)$ is exact.
- **Backward Kolmogorov equation (BKE).** For the Chapman-Kolmogorov value function $u(x, s) = \mathbb{E}[f(X_t) \mid X_s = x]$, Itô's formula on $H$ yields the deterministic BKE $\partial_s u = -\frac{1}{2}\langle x, \nabla_x u\rangle_H + \frac{1}{2}\mathrm{Tr}(C_\mu \cdot \nabla^2 u)$. The Cameron-Martin score is recovered as $\nabla_x \log p_s(x) = C_\mu^{-1} \nabla_x u(x, s)$ — well-defined on $H_C$ where Lebesgue densities fail in infinite dimensions.
- **Three drop-in substitutions.**
  1. Forward noise: $\eta = L_N \xi$ with $\xi \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)$ and $L_N = \mathrm{chol}(K)$ for the Matérn-3/2 Gram matrix $K$.
  2. Loss: $L_{CM}(\theta) = \mathbb{E}\|C_\mu^{-1/2}(\eta_\theta - \eta)\|_H^2$, with the inverse-covariance weighting justified by the Radon-Nikodym theorem.
  3. Inference: $\eta = L_N \xi$ (same $L_N$) for the reverse process.
- **Architecture-preserving.** A $\sim$8M-parameter ConditionalUnet1D with ResNet-18 image features is used; $t_p = 16$ diffusion steps, AdamW with $\eta = 10^{-4}$, averaged over 5 seeds on a single A100. The kernel is parameterized with $\ell = 0.3$, $\sigma^2 = 1$.
- **Kolmogorov residual as diagnostic.** $\hat{u}(x, s)$ is constructed as $\mathbb{E}\int_s^t \eta_\theta(X_\tau, \tau) d\tau \mid X_s = x$. The residual $R(\hat{u}) = \partial_s \hat{u} + \frac{1}{2}\mathrm{Tr}(C_\mu \cdot \nabla^2 \hat{u}) + \frac{1}{2}\langle x, \nabla_x \hat{u}\rangle_H$ is evaluated at a single sample point $(x, s)$ via auto-differentiation. Hessian traces use Hutchinson trace estimation with Gaussian probes.
- **Hamilton-Jacobi reachability certification.** For the manufacturing dispatch task, the learned policy is wrapped by a safety filter computed from the backward reachable set of congestion/deadlock states (computed with the author's `LevelSetPy` toolbox, Mitchell-Bayen-Tomlin formulation).
- **Convergence theory (Theorem 1).** $\|\mu_\theta - \mu_\mathrm{data}\|_{TV} \leq C_1 L_{CM}(\theta) + C_2 e^{-T/2}$, with constants depending on $\mathrm{Tr}(C_\mu)$ — explicitly contrasted with finite-dimensional $\mathcal{O}(\sqrt{d})$ scaling from De Bortoli (2022) and Chen et al. (2023).

## Results & Highlights

- **PushT training dynamics.** Cameron-Martin loss converges to $\approx 0.029$ vs. $\approx 0.06$ for MSE; the precision-weighted formulation shows tighter convergence with less validation-loss variance.
- **PushT inference.** Max episode reward 0.95 (CM) vs. 0.78 (MSE), a 17% relative improvement. Mean reward 0.62 (CM) vs. 0.51 (MSE). Cumulative per-step success rate 0.62 vs. 0.51. The advantage is most pronounced in tail rollout phases (steps 300–400) where trajectory precision matters most.
- **Residual as a calibration signal.** Mean residual: MSE $\approx 0.34$, mixed-precision $\alpha=0.15 \to 0.28$, $\alpha=0.25 \to 0.22$, $\alpha=0.50 \to 0.18$, $\alpha=0.85 \to 0.14$, CM $\approx 0.11$ — a 67.6% drop relative to MSE. Empirically: rollouts with mean residual $> 0.15$ correlate with task failure (max reward $< 0.5$); rollouts with mean residual $< 0.10$ achieve $> 90\%$ success.
- **6-station CONWIP manufacturing line (SimPy, 500k steps, WIP cap $L_\mathrm{max} = 20$).** Effective rank $r_\mathrm{eff} \approx 2.9$ for the 16-step horizon (only $\approx 3$ independent dynamic modes in the 6-station system). 28.4% lower normalized WIP RMSE than the LSTM baseline (Chen et al. 2021). Starvation-event recall = 1.0 in test cycles. Bottleneck identification Precision@1 = 1.0, 13× signal-to-noise ratio. Inference latency $< 1$ s per evaluation.
- **Reachability-certified dispatch.** Over 100 simulated dispatch runs, the Hamilton-Jacobi safety filter prevents 351 deadlock events vs. uncontrolled dispatch, a 96% reduction.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Lekan Molu** is the sole author (Bala Cynwyd, PA; email `lekanmolu@scriptedonachip.com`). His prior work includes the `LevelSetPy` Hamilton-Jacobi toolbox cited in this paper. The paper spans an unusually broad surface area — Gaussian measure theory, infinite-dimensional SDE/PDE numerics, robot imitation learning, discrete-event manufacturing simulation, and reachability-based control — for a single author. The author has chosen MSE (not contemporary diffusion-policy methods) and a 2021 LSTM paper (not newer sequence baselines) as the experimental comparators, which limits the credibility of the headline improvements (see Critical Assessment).

## Critical Assessment

- **Theoretical novelty is incremental, not paradigm-shifting.** The author correctly acknowledges that dimension-independent convergence in Hilbert spaces has been established before — most directly by Pidstrigach et al. (2023) — and Stuart (2010) covers the function-space Bayesian perspective. The novelty here is the operationalization as a *drop-in* DDPM modification plus the Kolmogorov-residual diagnostic. That is a useful engineering contribution, not a foundational one.
- **The Cameron-Martin geometry argument is hand-wavy in places.** The claim that the inverse-covariance weighting follows from the Radon-Nikodym theorem is asserted but not carefully demonstrated; the jump from "Gaussian measures on $H$ lack a Lebesgue density" to "therefore weight the loss by $C_\mu^{-1/2}$" skips several steps that the appendix apparently does not fully flesh out.
- **The TV-distance bound is unverifiable from the experiments.** Theorem 1's bound (constants depending on $\mathrm{Tr}(C_\mu)$, not $d_a$) is consistent with the small PushT experiment ($d_a = 2$, fixed), but the paper notes explicitly that $d_a$ cannot be varied on PushT. The headline theoretical claim therefore has no direct empirical support on the reported experiments.
- **Baselines are weak.**
  - On PushT, the comparison is to *MSE-based behavior cloning* (a finetuned version of an unconditional noise prediction). Modern diffusion-policy baselines — Diffusion Policy (Chi et al. 2023), DDIM-based variants, Consistency Policy, BCRNN — all comfortably exceed 0.85 on PushT. Beating MSE by 17 points is not strong evidence of SOTA. A 2023 follow-up, Diffusion Policy itself, is cited but not compared.
  - On the manufacturing line, the LSTM baseline is Chen et al. 2021 — a five-year-old architecture. No comparison to a transformer time-series model, N-BEATS, or even a basic MLP with the same inputs.
  - No comparison to FNOs or DeepONets, despite the author themselves noting that operator-learning architectures are "natural candidates" for the denoising network and could enable horizon transfer.
- **The Kolmogorov residual claim is mostly qualitative.** The paper shows that low residual correlates with high reward, but does not report ROC/AUC, calibration curves, false-positive rates under distribution shift, or threshold-sensitivity analyses. The claim that it is a "universal failure detector" overpromises relative to the evidence.
- **Reachability certification is only as good as the simulator.** Hamilton-Jacobi reachability requires a (typically low-fidelity) discrete-event model of the manufacturing line. The 96% deadlock reduction is computed against an *uncontrolled* baseline, not against a heuristic dispatch rule. There is no real-factory or hardware-in-the-loop validation.
- **Single author, no code release promised.** The paper is a sole-author contribution spanning five technical areas. No mention of releasing code, models, or the SimPy CONWIP simulator. Reproducibility is therefore a significant concern.
- **Red flags to watch.** (1) The reported "tail-phase" improvement (steps 300–400) on PushT is exactly where a tighter trajectory smoothness is expected to help, which is consistent with the theory but also suspiciously aligned with the selling point. (2) No negative results anywhere in the paper. (3) The Kolmogorov residual in the *baseline* MSE model is not very interpretable — it is a property of *the model's* value-function estimate, not of the underlying policy, so the interpretation as a "failure detector for the policy" is a step removed.

## Strengths

- Genuinely cross-disciplinary, bridging infinite-dimensional analysis (Cameron-Martin, Kolmogorov, Mercer eigenstructure), diffusion modeling, robot imitation learning, discrete-event simulation, and Hamilton-Jacobi reachability
- The three-substitution framing is genuinely *drop-in*: no architectural change to the denoising network, easy to adopt in existing DDPM pipelines
- The Kolmogorov residual is a clean, principled diagnostic that is genuinely reward-free — a real win for deployment in settings where reward signals are unreliable or unavailable
- The convergence bound is clearly stated and cleanly contrasted with finite-dimensional $\mathcal{O}(\sqrt{d})$ scaling from prior literature
- The mixed-precision ablation across $\alpha \in \{0, 0.15, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 0.85, 1.0\}$ is unusually thorough and shows a monotonic relationship between measure-theoretic weighting and downstream performance
- Hamilton-Jacobi reachability provides a formal safety story that almost no diffusion-policy paper offers
- Practical inference latency ($< 1$ s per evaluation on a single GPU) is reported, suggesting the approach is deployable

## Limitations

- Experimental scope is narrow: two benchmarks (PushT 2-DOF pushing, custom 6-station SimPy line), both relatively small
- Baselines are weak and dated: MSE-based behavior cloning on PushT, LSTM (2021) on manufacturing
- No comparison to contemporary diffusion-policy methods (Diffusion Policy / Chi 2023, DDIM variants, Consistency Policy, BCRNN) despite citing them
- No comparison to neural-operator denoisers (FNO, DeepONet) despite the author flagging them as natural candidates
- The dimension-independence claim is theoretically asserted but not empirically validated (action dimension is fixed at 2 on PushT)
- The Kolmogorov residual's calibration is only shown via correlation with reward; ROC/AUC, false-positive rate under distribution shift, and threshold robustness are not reported
- The reachability certification is computed on a simulator, not validated on a physical system
- No released code, models, or simulator
- Single-author contribution across five technical subfields limits the depth of vetting in any one of them
- The Matérn-3/2 kernel is a specific choice; the paper does not ablate against RBF, exponential, or other kernel families
- The framework assumes a fixed horizon $T$ in the theoretical bound; how it transfers to variable-horizon tasks is not addressed

## Relevance

- 与"扩散策略 / Diffusion Policy"主线方向直接相关：把有限维 DDPM 重新定位为 Cameron-Martin 空间上的确定性 BKE 边值问题，是 2026 年扩散策略理论化的一个较系统的尝试
- 与"形式化控制 / 形式化验证"方向也有显著参考价值：把 Hamilton-Jacobi 到达性引入到扩散策略的部署阶段，是少见的"形式化安全 + 扩散策略"结合的工作

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the three-substitution method compare head-to-head against Diffusion Policy (Chi et al. 2023), DDIM, and Consistency Policy on PushT, with matched denoising networks and compute budgets?
- Is the dimension-independence claim of Theorem 1 empirically supported if $d_a$ is varied (e.g., on a higher-DoF manipulation benchmark such as RoboMimic or DexArt)?
- Is the Kolmogorov residual well-calibrated — i.e., does its magnitude monotonically track the probability of policy failure on out-of-distribution inputs?
- What is the wall-clock cost of the residual computation at inference? Hutchinson trace estimation with $k$ probes per step adds $k \times$ the cost of a single network forward pass.
- How sensitive is the +17% PushT gain to the choice of Matérn kernel (3/2 vs. 5/2 vs. RBF), length scale $\ell$, and the truncation level of the Cameron-Martin expansion?
- Can the Hamilton-Jacobi safety filter be extended to settings with uncertain or learned dynamics models, not just the SimPy discrete-event simulator?
- How does the framework scale to long-horizon tasks where the effective rank $r_\mathrm{eff}$ grows with horizon? The Theorem 1 bound is horizon-independent, but the practical residual computation may not be.
- What classes of policies can a stochastic score-matching model represent that a deterministic BKE boundary-value formulation cannot? The author claims the deterministic form "eliminates Monte Carlo instability" but the cost of that determinism deserves a careful analysis.
- Does the colored-noise forward process give the same sample efficiency as standard DDPM on low-dim tasks, or does the Matérn-3/2 smoothness assumption bias the model toward overly smooth trajectories?
- Is the 96% deadlock reduction in manufacturing robust to perturbations of the underlying SimPy model parameters (arrival rates, service times)?
