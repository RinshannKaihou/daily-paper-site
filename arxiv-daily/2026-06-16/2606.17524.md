> **一句话总结：** ReLAR 是一个在解码前用强化学习训练的深度+动作控制器迭代精炼 LLM 隐藏状态的框架,在 PubMedQA、GSM-Hard、HotpotQA 和开放式生成上以接近普通 SFT 的推理时延(0.14s vs CoT 的 9.09s、SC-CoT 的 16.36s)取得最佳成绩。

> **TL;DR:** ReLAR inserts a learned controller pair (depth π_d, action π_a) between a frozen-ish base LM and the LM head, iteratively perturbing the final-layer hidden state via `h_{t+1} = h_t + α_t · v_t` (with `α_t = f_α(γ_t, β_t)`, `‖v_t‖=1`) before decoding, with a policy-gradient reward `r_t = log p_θ(y*|x, s_{t+1}) − log p_θ(y*|x, s_t) − c_d` driving adaptive depth and direction. On PubMedQA (77.67/72.54 0-shot), GSM-Hard, HotpotQA, CommonGen and WritingPrompts it beats 7B+ baselines including MedGemma-4B and Llama-3-8B-Instruct, while matching SFT inference latency.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **ReLAR(Reinforcement-Guided Latent Refinement)** 框架,把推理视为解码前的隐藏状态精炼过程,无需生成显式 chain-of-thought
- 引入两个由策略梯度协同训练的控制器:**深度控制器 π_d(T|s₀)** 决定精炼步数,**动作控制器 π_a((γ,β,v)|s_t)** 决定每步的更新方向、缩放与偏置
- 设计基于"目标序列 log-likelihood 步进改进 − 每步成本"的密集奖励信号,实现步级信用分配(per-step credit assignment)
- 在 0/5-shot 的医学、数学、多跳、开放式生成四类任务上同时获得更好的准确性、生成质量与推理稳定性,推理成本接近标准 SFT

## Key Contributions

- Reframes multi-step LLM reasoning as iterative hidden-state perturbation prior to decoding, removing the need for explicit CoT
- Jointly trained depth controller (number of refinement steps) and action controller (direction, scale, shift) via policy gradient
- Dense per-step reward = improvement in ground-truth log-likelihood minus a per-step cost, with KL penalty and entropy bonus
- Empirical wins on 4 task families against 11 baselines, with latency within ~1.2× of plain SFT and 65–117× cheaper than CoT/SC-CoT

## Methodology

**State construction.** Given input tokens `x`, a base LM produces a final-layer hidden state `h₀ ∈ ℝ^{L×D}`. A lightweight projection `f_extract` compresses it into a low-dim reasoning state `s₀ ∈ ℝ^{d_s}`, which is the only signal both controllers consume for their decisions.

**Action-guided refinement.** At each step `t`:
- Action controller samples `a_t = (γ_t, β_t, v_t) ~ π_a(·|s_t)`, with `v_t` normalized to unit length
- Signed step size `α_t = f_α(γ_t, β_t)` is a deterministic function of the two scalars
- Hidden state updated additively: `h_{t+1} = h_t + α_t · v_t` (equivalent to `f_refine(h_t, s_t, γ_t, β_t, v_t)`)
- Reasoning state refreshed: `s_{t+1} = g(s_t, h_{t+1})`
- Loop runs for `T` steps chosen by depth controller
- Final decoding representation: `h_T = f_decode(s_T, h₀)`, with `h₀` anchoring preserved to retain input context

**RL training.** Per-step reward `r_t = Δ_t − c_d` where `Δ_t = log p_θ(y*|x, s_{t+1}) − log p_θ(y*|x, s_t)`. Shaped return adds a 1/(t+1) discount, trajectory-level KL penalty, and entropy bonus. The joint log-policy factorizes as `log π_d(T|s₀) + Σ_t log π_a(a_t|s_t)`; advantage `A = r − E[r]` with running baseline. Final objective: `L_total = L_LM + α_RL · L_RL`, where `L_LM` is a teacher-forced LM loss on the target conditioned on `h_T`.

**Backbones used.** LLaMA-1.1B, Gemma-2B, Qwen-3B (the smaller-than-7B caveat is acknowledged by the authors).

## Results & Highlights

**Reasoning (Table 1, 0-shot / 5-shot).** ReLAR reaches SOTA on PubMedQA (77.67/72.54 → 79.23/74.17), GSM-Hard (45.02/47.58 → 48.57/52.12), and HotpotQA (57.50/75.23 → 59.64/76.15). On GSM8K it is competitive (68.45/78.23) but Llama-3-8B-Instruct is higher at 75.82/83.47. MedGemma-4B (72.45/68.52 PubMedQA) is the strongest medical baseline and is still beaten.

**Open-ended generation (Table 2).** Best BERTScore and ROUGE-L on both CommonGen (0.934/38.92) and WritingPrompts (0.878/11.47).

**Latency (Table 4, PubMedQA, Gemma-2B backbone).** ReLAR 0.14s vs SFT 0.12s (0.9×), ICL 0.31s (2.2×), CoT 9.09s (64.9×), SC-CoT 16.36s (116.9×). This is the strongest selling point of the paper.

**Ablation (Table 3, Gemma-2B).** Removing refinement collapses performance; static depth recovers part of the gain; adaptive depth (no direction) is very competitive; the full method wins overall. Notably on **GSM-Hard the full method actually loses to "Adaptive Depth (no direction)" (41.06/45.58 vs 42.28/46.83)**, a small inconsistency worth flagging.

**Backbone scaling (Table 5, PubMedQA).** Adding ReLAR to SFT yields large gains: LLaMA-1.1B +13.2 acc, Gemma-2B +19.6, Qwen-3B +14.4. These are the cleanest numbers in the paper because they isolate the contribution of the refinement module.

**Depth distribution (Figure 4).** The learned depth controller concentrates around T=3 (highest accuracy 75%), with depth 2 much worse (12%). Note this is based on **20 actual samples projected to n=200** — a weak empirical foundation.

## Authors & Credibility

Two authors (Chia-Hsuan Hsu, Jui-Ming Yao); no institution listed on the manuscript text I could read. Code is released at `tongyu0924/Learning-to-Refine-Hidden-States`. References cover CoT, latent reasoning (Creswell et al. 2023 — "Reasoning with Latent Thoughts"), activation steering (Stolfo et al. 2025; Helff et al. 2026), and adaptive computation (Graves 2016). The related-work section is well-sourced and positions the contribution honestly.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** The combination of (a) latent additive perturbation, (b) learned depth and action controllers, and (c) policy-gradient training on per-step log-likelihood is a reasonable new composition, but each ingredient individually has clear antecedents: latent reasoning traces (Creswell 2023), activation steering (Turner 2023; Stolfo 2025; Helff 2026), ACT-style depth control (Graves 2016), and RL for adaptive computation. The framing as "reliable reasoning" with a clinical motivation is fresh, but the technical core is an integration, not a new mechanism.

**Rigor.** Mixed. The theoretical appendix (A.1, "Adaptive Depth Dominates Fixed Depth") is a trivial max-vs-fixed-point inequality that does not constrain the learned policy at all — it shows the policy *class* is richer, not that training finds a good policy. This is a cosmetic, not substantive, theoretical contribution. Empirical reporting is reasonable (3 random seeds, fixed decoding temperature and max length), but the depth-distribution analysis is based on **20 actual samples** projected to n=200 — this is too small to draw conclusions and the paper does not advertise this limitation.

**Reproducibility.** Code is released, which is good. But the paper does not report which datasets were used for SFT adaptation of the base LMs, what `d_s` is, what the per-step cost `c_d` is set to, the KL coefficient, the entropy coefficient, α_RL, or training compute. A reproduction will require substantial guesswork or GitHub digging.

**Comparability / apples-to-apples.** The authors' **own limitations section** concedes that their backbones (1.1B / 2B / 3B) are smaller than the 7B–8B baselines. This is the single biggest concern with the paper: gains over Llama-3-8B-Instruct on PubMedQA, HotpotQA, etc. are claimed by a model roughly 3–4× smaller. While smaller models sometimes genuinely win (and the Table 5 backbone-scaling experiment helps), the headline claims should be read with the size gap in mind. The Open-ended generation results in Table 2 also lack a fair comparison: ReLAR is shown next to 7B–8B baselines; the +5 ROUGE-L gain on WritingPrompts over Llama-3-8B-Instruct is impressive but unverifiable without knowing the SFT data.

**Red flags / questionable claims.**
- The Figure 4 depth-distribution chart is built from 20 actual samples — not a strong empirical basis.
- On GSM-Hard, "Adaptive Depth without direction" outperforms the full model in Table 3 (a non-monotonic ablation that the paper does not discuss).
- The reward `r_t = Δ_t − c_d` requires ground-truth labels at every refinement step, which couples training to a supervised signal and limits the "RL" framing — it is closer to likelihood-weighted credit assignment than to reward-model-based RL.
- The paper does not report variance across seeds (only averages), so the "improvement stability" claim is hard to verify.
- Authors position this as "reliable reasoning for clinical decision support" but evaluate only on PubMedQA (a yes/no/maybe benchmark) — no real clinical note-writing, differential-diagnosis, or longitudinal tasks are used. The clinical framing is aspirational rather than demonstrated.

**Significance.** The latency story is genuinely interesting: same-speed-as-SFT, much better quality, no CoT required. If the results hold under more rigorous evaluation (more seeds, size-matched backbones, variance bars, more challenging clinical tasks), this would be a useful building block. As is, the paper is a competent systems-style contribution whose headline claims outrun its empirical substantiation.

## Strengths

- The **inference-cost story is the cleanest part of the paper**: 0.14s vs CoT's 9.09s and SC-CoT's 16.36s is a 65–117× speedup at higher accuracy
- Modular design (extract → refine ×T → decode-anchored) is easy to plug onto different backbones
- Ablations cleanly decompose the contribution of static depth, adaptive depth, adaptive direction, and the full method
- Diverse benchmark coverage (medical, math, multi-hop, open-ended generation) — broader than typical papers in this space
- Code release

## Limitations

- Backbones (1.1B–3B) are smaller than most baselines (7B–8B), creating an apples-to-oranges comparison (paper's own admission)
- Theoretical analysis (Appendix A.1) is trivial and adds no insight
- Reward requires ground-truth labels at every step, undermining the framing as a general-purpose RL method
- Variance across seeds not reported
- "Medical reasoning" framing is supported only by PubMedQA, a yes/no/maybe dataset, not real clinical reasoning
- The depth-distribution analysis relies on 20 actual samples
- Per-paper hyper-parameters (`c_d`, KL/entropy weights, `α_RL`, `d_s`) are not disclosed
- No analysis of when latent refinement **fails** (only a depth-vs-accuracy plot); error analysis is missing
- Code is at a personal GitHub handle (`tongyu0924/...`) — no institutional or community association

## Relevance

与「LLM Hidden States & Interpretability / ML Reliability」两条主线都直接相关:它把推理从 token 空间搬到 hidden state 空间并以 RL 控制其轨迹,正好对应"通过表示层干预来获得更可靠的输出"的研究方向,且其延迟优势对实际部署也有意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the gain persist when ReLAR is added on top of 7B–8B backbones matched to the baselines, or is most of the win from backbone size disparity?
- What is the variance across seeds for the headline numbers, and how do the depth distributions look on a larger sample (the paper uses only 20)?
- Can the per-step log-likelihood reward be replaced with a learned reward model (e.g., preference-based), removing the dependence on ground-truth targets?
- How does the method behave on tasks that explicitly test faithfulness/interpretability of the reasoning process, given that the refinement is opaque by construction?
- Is the learned `v_t` direction interpretable, or does it diffuse into a generic "improvement direction"? A probing analysis of the refined hidden states would clarify this.
- What happens when ReLAR is composed with test-time scaling (e.g., self-consistency over the refined states) — are the gains additive or redundant?
- The ablation shows "adaptive depth without direction" beats the full model on GSM-Hard; is there a regime where simpler controllers are preferable, and can the framework learn to switch?
- How sensitive is the method to `c_d` (per-step cost), and is there a principled way to schedule it across training?
