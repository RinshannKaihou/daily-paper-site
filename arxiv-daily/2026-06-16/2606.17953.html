MLLMs Get It Right, Then Get It Wrong: Tracing and Correcting Late-Layer Textual Bias

Xingming Li, Ao Cheng, Qiyao Sun · et al. ·2026-06-16 · multimodal-llm visual-grounding mechanistic-interpretability training-free
Headline metric
+9.4pp absolute
CALRD over vanilla on InstructBLIP / PhD-icc under nucleus decoding — the largest of a 5-model × 3-decoder grid.
Why this matters
A training-free, model-agnostic fix for the "text wins over image" failure mode — the same override that breaks hallucination and grounding benchmarks on today's open MLLMs.
Critical edge
CALRD needs intermediate-layer access, so it cannot help closed / API-only MLLMs — exactly the models now dominating deployment.

Methodology

Find the layer that flips — then mix it back in.

1. Project every layerCompute P(l) at every depth from the same answer-token hidden state.
2. Score modalityMDR(l) = log P(wvis) / P(wtext). Sign = preference.
3. Locate the flipStability onset Lstart → JSD-maximizing Ltrans.
4. Blend logitsλ = Dconf·(1−ρ); φ' = (1−λ)φ(N) + λφ(Ltrans).

The intervention fires only when the model was confident at Ltrans (high anchor confidence Dconf) but lost that prediction by the final layer (low retention ρ). With λ=0 the method reduces to vanilla decoding; with λ=1 the final layer is replaced entirely. Because it operates on logits, it composes with greedy, beam, and nucleus.

Diagnosis primitive: ΔMDR = mean(MDRafter) − mean(MDRbefore) — a late-layer shift toward text (<0) flags 85% of conflict-incorrect cases; a shift toward vision or stable (>0) flags 89–91% of correct ones.

  • Beats every baseline in every cell of Table 2 — DoLa, VCD, OPERA, DeCo. DoLa actually hurts InstructBLIP on Conflict-VQA (39.6 → 32.7).
  • Standard benchmarks survive. POPE F1 gains +0.3 to +5.4 in every cell; CHAIR CS drops 58.8 → 40.2 on InstructBLIP greedy (−31.6% rel.).
  • Ablations (LLaVA-1.6, Conflict-VQA): drop Dconf → 49.5 → 47.3; drop ρ → 49.5 → 45.1; fix layer at 75% depth → 49.5 → 45.9.
  • Efficiency: 18.3 → 19.8 ms/token on LLaVA-1.5-7B (≈+8%, +80 MB). VCD ~2×; OPERA ~10×.
  • Pattern: weaker models gain more (InstructBLIP, LLaVA-1.5/1.6) — strong models already resolve the conflict in late layers.

Strengths

  • Reframes a known behavioural bias as a mechanistic, late-layer phenomenon — explainable and actionable.
  • Directional diagnostic (ΔMDR) is simple, interpretable, and operationally useful as a "should I trust this?" signal.
  • Training-free, label-free, architecture-agnostic; composes with greedy/beam/nucleus; ~1.5 ms/token overhead.
  • Strongest gains cluster on the weakest models — exactly where practical deployment needs help.
  • Ships Conflict-VQA (5,969 samples, 6 question types) with explicit (wvis, wtext) labels.
  • Comparative grid is unusually broad: 4 baselines × 5 models × 3 decoders, plus ablations and efficiency.

Limitations

  • Requires intermediate-layer access; cannot help closed / API-only MLLMs.
  • Depends on a clean stability onset + JSD-maximizing Ltrans; atypical inputs may localize noisily.
  • The 85% / 89% statistic is computed per-model on a curated "competent" subset — main evaluation does use all 5,969 samples, but worth flagging.
  • Some CHAIR cells regress slightly (e.g. LLaVA-1.6 nucleus CI 10.5 → 11.5); "largely preserves" hides per-cell noise.
  • Not stress-tested on adversarial / longer-context conflicts, multi-image inputs, or chain-of-thought decoding.
  • No code release announced in the abstract — reproducibility hinges on the appendix.

Questions for further exploration

  1. Does the late-layer override persist (and does CALRD still help) on much larger open MLLMs (≥70B) and on reasoning-tuned variants?
  2. Can Dconf / ρ be replaced by a single learned signal — e.g. a small classifier over hidden states — to skip the per-sample JSD scan?
  3. How does CALRD compose with VCD, OPERA, attention steering — additive gains, or do the interventions interfere?
  4. Is ΔMDR < 0 reliable enough to deploy as a confidence / abstention signal, beyond fixing the answer?
  5. What happens under chain-of-thought or multi-step reasoning, where the "answer token" is no longer the only decision point?
  6. Does the same override pattern appear in audio-text or video-text MLLMs, as the authors flag in their future-work paragraph?
