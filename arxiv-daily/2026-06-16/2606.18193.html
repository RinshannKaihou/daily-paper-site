Measuring the Residual Jailbreak Surface of Frontier Large Language Models — A red-team study of Anthropic Fable 5 & Opus 4.8 models

Nicola Franco  ·  AI Security Lab, AI4I (Turin, Italy)
2026-06-16  ·  Single-author independent evaluation
LLM Safety Red-Teaming Jailbreak Attacks Adversarial Robustness TAP / PAIR
Key Finding
Static obfuscation is dead (≤ 0.18%), but adaptive iterative jailbreaks dominate — TAP reaches 11.51% on Opus 4.8 and 6.10% on Fable 5.
Key Metric
11.51%
TAP attack success rate on Opus 4.8 across 7,826 harmful intents — the only double-digit family-level result; the worst cell (TAP × child-safety) hits 27.6%.
Why This Matters
Even hardened frontier models remain reliably breakable under sustained automated pressure: the residual surface lives in adaptive iterative reframing, not obfuscation — with concrete cell-level failures reaching 27.6% in child-safety and 16.6% in PAIR × cybersecurity.
Critical Edge
The 4-family attack suite is standard but not exhaustive — no multi-modal, no agent-mediated paths, no many-shot or crescendo; the measured residual is a lower bound on what an unconstrained attacker could reach.
01

Methodology

  • Targets: Anthropic Opus 4.8 and Fable 5, accessed as black boxes through a hosted OpenAI-compatible gateway — no weights, no logprobs.
  • Attacker model: uncensored open-weight model on local GPUs, so the attacker never refuses its own output.
  • Benchmark: 7,826 harmful intents × 10 categories × 55 subcategories, uneven sizes (28–599 per subcategory) reflecting real harm distribution.
  • Four attack families: TAP (tree search, depth 3 / width 4 / branch 3); PAIR (12 iterations × 8 streams, early-stop on success); PAP (one-shot persuasion reframe); h4rm3l (static obfuscation: base64, ciphers, payload-split, role-play, Wikipedia framing).
  • Two-stage adjudication: in-loop HarmBench-style scorer steers search; offline 3-model panel (Qwen3.7 Max / Gemini 3.5 Flash / GPT 5.5) with ≥ 2/3 majority for "confirmed jailbreak".
  • Primary metric: ASR = panel-confirmed jailbreaks ÷ total attempts. Per-step iteration curves trace where in the budget successes land.

  • Worst Opus 4.8 cell: TAP × child-safety = 27.6%; PAIR × cybersecurity = 16.6%.
  • Static obfuscation is near-fully neutralised — ≤ 0.18% ASR on ~50k attempts per model.
  • Adaptive + persuasion attacks account for ~95–97% of confirmed jailbreaks.
  • Successes are front-loaded: TAP on Fable 5 peaks at step 1; PAIR on Opus 4.8 concentrates in the first 2 iterations.
  • Both models share a child-safety weakness under adaptive reframing.
03

Strengths

  • Disaggregated reporting (per family × per category × per subcategory) — far more honest than a single aggregate number.
  • Two-stage majority-vote adjudication across three model families beats single-judge pipelines.
  • Absolute counts (1,620 / 702) reported alongside rates — prevents dismissing the rates as tiny.
  • Per-step iteration curves (Figure 6) show where in the budget successes actually land — the right question for cost/benefit analysis.
  • Qualitative bypasses (payloads withheld) make the mechanism — reframing, not obfuscation — visible.
  • Independence disclosure and explicit partial-coverage caveat for Fable 5 / PAIR.
04

Limitations

  • Threat model excludes human-in-the-loop, multi-modal, agent-mediated, RAG-poisoning, and recent classes (many-shot, crescendo, persona multi-turn).
  • Judge panel is itself LLM-based; majority vote reduces correlated error but does not eliminate it.
  • Fable 5 / PAIR coverage is partial (27/55 subcategories) — a lower bound, not a definitive comparison.
  • Production safety stacks (system prompts, output filters, monitoring) are not modelled — headline ASR is an upper bound on real-world exploitation.
  • Subcategory sizes are uneven (28–599 intents); some subcategory ASRs have wide confidence intervals.
  • Model/version labels (Fable 5, Opus 4.8, Qwen3.7 Max, Gemini 3.5 Flash, GPT 5.5) should be verified against public release timelines.
05

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Are "Fable 5" and "Opus 4.8" actually public Anthropic releases? The paper's relevance depends on this; if not, treat it as a forward-looking study.
  • How do these 11.51% / 6.10% numbers change when including attack classes the paper omits — many-shot, crescendo, persona multi-turn, agent-mediated, multi-modal?
  • What is the marginal cost (USD / GPU-hours) per confirmed jailbreak? The paper claims "cheap and fast" but gives no dollar figure.
  • The 27.6% TAP × child-safety cell is the most consequential single result — what specific reframings drive it, and can targeted defense close it without regressing the rest of the taxonomy?
  • How do these numbers compare against previous-generation Claude models (Sonnet 4 / Opus 4) to quantify actual safety progress over time?
  • Can a lightweight multi-turn monitor (semantic drift over the conversation) be calibrated on the Figure 6 curve to catch a large fraction of successful jailbreaks at low false-positive cost?
