Functional Equivalence in Attention: A Comprehensive Study with Applications to Linear Mode Connectivity

Viet-Hoang Tran · Vinh Khanh Bui · Van-Hoan Trinh · Tan Lai Ngoc · Tan M. Nguyen
NUS · VinUniversity/CAIR · TUM  ·  PMLR 306
transformer-theory rope positional-encoding linear-mode-connectivity permutation-symmetry

Sinusoidal PE keeps vanilla attention's full symmetry group, but RoPE strictly shrinks it to Sh × H(dh) × GL(dh) — turning "RoPE is better" from folklore into algebra. ↳ Core contribution · paper claim

01KEY METRIC
60–95% → 7–12%
Loss-barrier ratio across vision & language tasks after weight matching — head-permutation alone leaves a near-linear gap; adding Stage-2 QK/VO alignment collapses it by an order of magnitude.
Stage-1 → Stage-2 (orthogonal init) → Full (refined). 3 seeds · 8 datasets · 5 architectures.
02WHY THIS MATTERS
For the first time, RoPE's empirical dominance is grounded in a closed-form algebraic argument: smaller symmetry group ⇒ larger function class. The same group lens is then weaponised as a weight-matching prior for merging independently trained Transformers, turning an architectural choice into a measurable loss-landscape quantity.
03CRITICAL EDGE
LMC fails on large decoder-only LMs (GPT-2 / Llama on WikiText-103, Enwik8, One Billion Word) under full-model re-init — and disproving LMC is theoretically harder than proving it, since no matching scheme can be certified optimal.

Methodology

  • Group-theoretic lens. Re-derive the symmetry of vanilla MHA as GAtt(dh, h) = Sh × GL(dh) × GL(dh) (head permutation ⊗ QK/VO circuits) and show sinusoidal PE only shifts the input — classes unchanged.
  • RoPE reduction. Rotary matrix Rm−n inserted between WQ and WK breaks the GL(dh) cancellation, leaving the abelian H(dh) ≅ (ℂ×)dh/2.
  • General-MHA theorem (4.1). Zero MHA output ⇒ all Bi = 0 — linear independence of attention heads via exponential-polynomial identities; RoPE case (4.2) drops out as a corollary.
  • Two-stage weight matcher. Stage 1: Hungarian LAP over a softmax-invariant cost matrix (M̄ = WQ(WK)T). Stage 2: per-head continuous QK + VO alignment, closed-form orthogonal init, gradient refinement; RoPE's H(dh) decouples into dh/2 scalar Brent sub-problems.
  • LMC protocol. Four re-init regimes (first-attn / all-attn / first-Transformer / full), 25-point interpolation, 3 seeds, loss + accuracy barrier ratio.

Results & Highlights

  • Head-permutation solver is near-optimal. LAP picks permutations of average rank 2–5 / 24 with L̂ ≈ 0 across CIFAR-10/100, IMDB, DBPedia — matching collapses the loss barrier to near-zero on WikiText-103 and ImageNet.
  • Stage-2 ablation is clean. Variant 1 (permutation only): 60–95 % barrier. Variant 2 (+ orthogonal init): 10–16 %. Full (+ gradient refinement): 7–12 % — fine-tuning closes roughly half the remaining gap.
  • LMC holds in three regimes. First-attention-layer, full-attention re-init, and first-Transformer-layer re-init all show reliable LMC on ViT / GPT-2 / BERT / Llama.
  • LMC breaks at scale. Full-model re-init on ImageNet, WikiText-103, Enwik8, One Billion Word — no linear path connects independently trained basins despite extensive head sweeps and seeds.
  • Matching ↔ generalisation. Models that exhibit LMC also tend to generalise better, hinting at a correlation between loss-landscape flatness and downstream accuracy (Appendix L).
  • Permutation surface is sharp. Poor permutations can degrade the interpolated model substantially — accurate matching is non-optional.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean closed-form identification of the RoPE symmetry group — algebra, not heuristics.
  • Honest negative result on large decoder-only LMs; reported with mature hedging.
  • Full algorithmic spec: orthogonal init, closed-form H(dh) decomposition, Brent scalars.
  • Bridges functional-equivalence theory and LMC literature — a new connection.

Limitations

  • Only sinusoidal APE and RoPE analysed — ALiBi, NoPE, YaRN, position-interpolated RoPE uncharacterised.
  • "Function class strictly larger" is qualitative; no measure-theoretic bound on the gap.
  • Empirical LMC has no theoretical guarantee; optimality of any matching scheme is uncertifiable.
  • Llama tested with only 2–4 heads — no APE comparison; scale mismatch with frontier LLMs.
  • Decoder-only LMC failure is observed but not localised (attention vs. FFN vs. LayerNorm).

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the H(dh) ⊊ GL(dh) reduction scale with the rotary base θ, head dim, or sequence length — and does it predict the empirical extrapolation gap of RoPE LLMs (LLaMA, Qwen, DeepSeek)?
  • Apply the same group framework to ALiBi, NoPE, and relative-attention bias to rank PEs by symmetry size.
  • Is the decoder-only LMC breakdown driven by attention (QK) or by FFN / LayerNorm? Swap-one-component ablations could localise it.
  • Does the matching algorithm transfer to merging independently fine-tuned RoPE-LLaMA / Qwen variants at LLM scale?
  • Could a PE be designed by choosing a target symmetry group — smaller H for expressivity, or larger for guaranteed LMC?
  • Theoretical link between symmetry group size and typical SGD loss barrier — smaller symmetry ⇒ fewer equivalent minima ⇒ harder linear connection?
