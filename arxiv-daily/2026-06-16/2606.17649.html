Risk splits into an irreducible floor Lint plus a power-law decaying variance Vopt(c) ≳ K c−α, classifying every fine-tuning task into one of three predictability regimes. A Risk Decomposition Framework for Pre-Hoc Fine-Tuning Prediction

Yuxiang Luo · Chen Wang · Nan Tang 2606.17649 2026-06-16 HKUST (Guangzhou)
fine-tuning performance prediction risk decomposition stochastic approximation phase diagram
Key Metric
1,500 seeds
Independent fine-tuning runs per (task, probe depth) cell across SST-2, GSM8K-style, and noisy-supervision tasks.
Empirical protocol: 1,500 N per cell · power-law fit on log-log axes.
Why This Matters
Predictor "failures" (e.g. shallow probing on GSM8K) are reframed as regime mismatch, not predictor design flaws — the same predictor, given a deeper probe, can recover.
Practical: one offline calibration → per-task optimal probe depth c*.
Critical Edge
The decay bound is an asymptotic envelope in a locally regular regime — grokking-style delayed generalization and early transient dynamics are explicitly out of scope.
α is task–optimizer-specific; not a universal constant.

Methodology

A fine-tuning task is T = (M, D, A); realized performance R | T is a random variable over optimizer stochasticity. The predictor observes an information set Ic = (Xs, Xd(c)) (static descriptors + cost-incurring dynamic prefix), monotone in budget c.

  • Risk decomposition (Prop. 4.1, law of total variance): L(c) = Lint + Vopt(c) — an irreducible intrinsic floor plus a reducible optimization variance.
  • Decay envelope (Prop. 5.1): under locally regular stochastic approximation, Vopt(c) ≳ K c−α, derived from Cov(θc) = Ω(c−(2ρ−1)) and Δ-method propagation.
  • Optimal stopping (Thm. 6.1): risk-cost balance minc[LT(c) + γ C(c)] gives closed form c* = (αK / γλ)1/(α+1) for linear cost.
  • Algorithm 1: offline three-point calibration fits (α̂, K̂, L̂T,int) from repeated seeds at m probe depths.

Results & Highlights

  • Power-law decay confirmed in the stable probing interval across all three regimes on log-log axes (Figure 2).
  • Phase diagram (Figure 3): Static-Sufficient clusters at (low Lint, large α); Dynamic-Critical at (low Lint, small α); Noise-Dominant along high Lint — regimes do not overlap.
  • Marginal-gain curves (Figure 4) match Theorem 6.1's diminishing-returns prediction per regime.
  • Failure-mode reinterpretation: shallow probing on GSM8K is a regime-depth mismatch, not a predictor bug.
  • Practical guidance: Static-Sufficient → shallow or zero probe; Dynamic-Critical → c* per closed form; Noise-Dominant → no probing is informative.
Static-Sufficient
α large, Lint small. Shallow probe wins. e.g. SST-2.
Dynamic-Critical
α small, Lint small. Deep probe needed. e.g. GSM8K-style.
Noise-Dominant
Lint large. No probing informative. e.g. noisy supervision.

Strengths

  • Clean intrinsic / reducible split with information-theoretic reading.
  • Decay bound grounded in stochastic-approximation theory, not ad hoc fitting.
  • Closed-form c* is directly usable in probe-cost planning.
  • Phase diagram unifies scattered prior probing observations.
  • Honest scope flags: "asymptotic envelope", "offline calibration", "descriptor vs exact".
  • 1,500 seeds per cell — strong statistical reliability for qualitative claims.

Limitations

  • Envelope excludes grokking / early transient dynamics.
  • α is task–optimizer-specific — requires offline recalibration per recipe change.
  • Algorithm 1 is explicitly offline (no deployment-time decision protocol).
  • Validation is structural (predicted shapes appear), not a head-to-head predictor benchmark.
  • Dispersion proxy Û(c) is not formally tied to Bayes risk L(c).
  • Single scalar metric R ∈ ℝ; multi-objective fine-tuning out of scope.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the regime assignment transfer across model families (e.g. does a Static-Sufficient task stay Static-Sufficient when swapping Llama-3 for Qwen)?
  • How does the framework extend to multi-objective fine-tuning (instruction-following + safety + factuality), where R is vector-valued?
  • Can a single-shot estimator replace offline calibration — a probing head predicting α̂ from a handful of gradient steps?
  • How does α relate to standard training-dynamics diagnostics (gradient noise scale, loss-curve curvature, effective gradient rank)?
  • Under matched probe budget, does a regime-aware strategy actually beat a fixed-budget probe or a Bayesian-optimization allocator on held-out tasks?
  • Could Lint be deliberately inflated via biased labels to game a task into the Noise-Dominant regime — a benchmark-selection attack surface?
