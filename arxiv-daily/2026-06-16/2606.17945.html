Key finding

Smaller weight initialization (γ=1) consistently lowers LLM pretraining loss — especially on reasoning — once two architectural blockers are released.

Small Initialization Matters for Large Language Models

Liangkai Hang, Junjie Yao, Zhiyu Li, Feiyu Xiong, Hongkang Yang, Zhi-Qin John Xu · 2026-06-16 · LLM parameter-initialization pretraining reasoning condensation
Key metric
~44%
effective-model-size improvement at 1.5B after combining ε=1e-12 with sigmoid-gated attention; the two fixes are near-no-ops at γ=0.5 and large wins at γ=1.
Why this matters

γ-initialization is a near-zero-cost knob that complements μP/μTransfer and ties LLM pretraining to the team's condensation theory of how reasoning emerges — a free lever for anyone training a Transformer today.

Critical edge

Validated only up to 1.5B dense / 3.2B MoE on a math/code-heavy 36B–100B-token slice; the original γ=1 gap shrinks with scale, and no comparison to μP/μTransfer is provided.

Methodology

Each weight matrix is reparameterized as Wij ~ N(0, d_in−2γ). γ=0.5 recovers Xavier; larger γ means smaller init. All other knobs are held fixed; only γ and two architectural adjustments vary.

InitW ~ N(0, d−2γ)

Two architectural blockers

  • RMSNorm ε saturation — at d=2048, hidden-state variance is ~5e-4 (γ=0.5) and ~2e-7 (γ=1), both below the default ε=1e-5, so the norm becomes ε-dominated and blind to further scale reduction.
  • Attention sink — small init intensifies first-token attention by ~0.11 in 1.5B models, concentrating probability mass on the sink and starving informative positions.

Evaluation

Trained on 36B (dense) / 100B (MoE) tokens from a 1T zh+en corpus; evaluated via lm-evaluation-harness on ARC-C, TriviaQA, MMLU, HellaSwag, BBH, SocialIQA, GSM8K, MATH500. Mechanistic metrics: attention-sink score, stable rank of W_Q/W_K/W_V/W_up/W_down, row-wise cosine similarity, token-level Δsympt.

Results & highlights

γ=1 wins on validation loss at every dense size tested (0.1B → 1.5B), but the absolute gap shrinks with scale unless the two fixes are applied.

Average absolute gain +2.51 across 8 benchmarks; reasoning-heavy tasks benefit most.

Architectural fixes only help at γ=1

At 1.5B, ε → 1e-12 cuts loss by ~0.038 at γ=1 vs ~0.001 at γ=0.5; gated attention adds another ~0.047 at γ=1 vs ~0.004 at γ=0.5. The MoE sweep (1.5B-A260M, 3.2B-A470M) shows the same pattern.

γ=1 is a critical point

Residual-flow norm ratio ∥hL−e∥/∥e∥ ∝ d1−γ: residual-dominated below 1, identity-dominated above 1, balanced exactly at 1. Loss improves monotonically from γ=0.5 → 1, then degrades.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Falsifiable central claim with a derived, unique critical value (γ=1), not a vague "smaller is better."
  • Mechanistic story that cross-validates — stable-rank collapse-then-recover, row-wise cosine alignment, token-level Δsympt, and the d1−γ residual-flow prediction all agree.
  • Clean ablation discipline: both architectural fixes are near-no-ops at γ=0.5 and large wins at γ=1, so the gain ties to γ rather than the fixes themselves.
  • Broad experiment base for a non-frontier study — 4 dense sizes, 2 MoE sizes, 8 benchmarks, per-layer stable-rank appendix.
  • Actionable architectural lessons for anyone using RMSNorm, even readers who don't adopt γ=1 as a default.

Limitations

  • Scale ceiling of 1.5B (dense) / 3.2B (MoE), one epoch on 36B–100B tokens — frontier ≥7B and multi-trillion-token runs are not tested.
  • No comparison to μP / μTransfer, the current standard for scale-invariant hyperparameter transfer.
  • Gated attention adds 7M–150M parameters across the size sweep — the paper shows no parameter-count-matched γ=0.5 baseline, so the separator is not perfectly clean.
  • ε=1e-12 is aggressive and may interact with bf16/fp16 precision; numerical stability across the γ range at this ε is not characterized.
  • Token-level analysis is single-config, qualitative — no bootstrap, no per-bin significance, only one worked example.
  • Data mix is math/code-heavy (4.5B code + 9B math of 36B); GSM8K/MATH500/HellaSwag gains may not transfer to a generic web-heavy English-only mix.
  • No code or weights released; recipe is detailed but not one-click verifiable.

Questions for further exploration

  • Does the γ=1 advantage persist — and do the ε / gated-attention fixes transfer — at ≥7B and multi-trillion-token training, where the original gap almost closed at 1.5B?
  • How does γ-initialization interact with μP / μTransfer: compatible, redundant, or in tension?
  • Is the ε-saturation phenomenon relevant for LayerNorm with learnable affine, Sandwich-RMSNorm, or σReparam — and is there a principled schedule for ε(d, γ)?
  • Can the low-to-high complexity trajectory be used as a training-time diagnostic (stable-rank collapse depth + recovery slope as a proxy for reasoning capacity)?
  • Does γ=1 transfer to post-training (SFT / RLHF / RLVR), or does the small-init reasoning prior wash out under instruction-tuning shift?
  • How much of the attention-sink relief is specific to gated attention, vs. NoPE-on-first-token or sink-with-proper-init?
  • What does the condensation trajectory look like in MoE routers, and does it explain the observed MoE benefit at γ=1?
  • Can the 44% effective-model-size heuristic be turned into a fitted γ-conditioned Chinchilla-style scaling law?
