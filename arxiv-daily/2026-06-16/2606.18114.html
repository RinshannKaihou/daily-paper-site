Quantization State Space Models Mamba-2 QAT 2606.18114

A pretrained Mamba-2 1.3B can be ternarized to W1.58A16 with 102M tokens of KD-based QAT — matching Bi-Mamba's 7-task score in 0.001% of the compute.

Ternary Mamba: Grouped Quantization-Aware Training of W1.58A16 State Space Models
Authors: Ramprasath Ganesaraja · Sahil Dilip Panse · Swathika N Affiliation: EdgeVerve Systems (Infosys subsidiary) Date: 2026-06-16
Headline Metric
3.61×
compression · 2,687 MB → 744 MB
48.1% 7-task zero-shot avg — within 0.3 pp of Bi-Mamba's 48.4%, using 4 GPU-h on 1 H100 vs. Bi-Mamba's 5,780 GPU-h (32× A100).
Why this matters

Edge-device SSM deployment gets a credible W1.58 recipe without retraining from scratch — three orders of magnitude cheaper than the only prior ternary-SSM attempt, and the recipe surfaces a new failure mode the whole community will eventually hit.

Critical Edge

The 3.61× is a storage claim, not a wall-clock inference claim — no custom Mamba-2 ternary kernel exists, and the current QAT simulation actually uses more memory than FP16 (5,322 vs. 2,599 MB). The 48.1% headline also hides a 7.4 pp gap to FP16 (55.5%), softened by the favorable Bi-Mamba comparison.

Methodology

BaseMamba-2 1.3B (FP16, public HF ckpt)
Quantizein_proj + out_proj → W1.58A16, g=128, per-group absmean
Train102M tokens of C4, AdamW + KD, α=0.5
EvalWT-2 PPL · 7-task zero-shot
  • Ternary scheme: weights ∈ {−1, 0, +1} with per-group absmean scale (g=128), non-learnable, recomputed every forward; activations stay FP16. STE for gradients.
  • Objective: L = α · DKL(teacher ∥ student) · T² + (1−α) · LCE, α=0.5, T=1.0; frozen FP16 Mamba-2 1.3B as teacher.
  • Coverage: 48 blocks × (in_proj 805M + out_proj 403M) = 1,208M ternary (85.7%). A_log, D, dt_bias, conv1d, embedding, lm_head, norms stay FP16.
  • Compute: AdamW, lr 2.5e-4 cosine, 1k warmup, 50k steps, batch 2×1024 → 102M tokens. Multi-seed (42, 137, 2024) — C4 PPL std = 0.01.

  • Compute gap: 4 GPU-h vs. 5,780 GPU-h — a 1,000× reduction in marginal cost.
  • Naive PTQ fails: W1.58 post-training quantization → PPL ~13M (random). QAT is necessary, not optional.
  • PPL gap to FP16: WT-2 20.31 vs. 11.69 (+8.62, inflated by C4→WT2 domain shift); C4 21.67 vs. 17.47 (+4.20).
  • Scaling: C4 PPL monotonically 21.67→20.65 across 102M→307M tokens (Mann-Kendall p=0.042, R²=0.92).

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Hardware-cheap: 4 GPU-h on 1 H100, approachable for any lab.
  • Names a new failure mode: zero-ratio collapse (learnable scales → 90% zeros, PPL 47.45); fixed absmean self-regulates at ~26% sparsity.
  • Honest negative results: Kalman, James-Stein, Σ∆, HG-GSQ all reported as failures — narrows the design space.
  • Multi-seed replication (std 0.01 PPL, ±0.13 pp on 7-task), Wilson CIs, Mann-Kendall, OLS, per-layer CV — careful stats.
  • Layer-error uniformity: CV < 0.005 across 48 layers — robust to depth choices.

Limitations

  • Storage only: 3.61× is a memory claim — no custom Mamba-2 ternary kernel exists; current sim uses more RAM than FP16 (5,322 vs. 2,599 MB).
  • 7.4 pp gap to FP16 hidden behind the favorable Bi-Mamba comparison; −12.4 pp on HellaSwag, −7.3 pp on ARC-Challenge.
  • Single architecture / language: Mamba-2 1.3B + English C4 only; Mamba-1, Mamba-3, Jamba, RWKV, non-English untested.
  • Single scale (1.3B): 3B+ ternary quality inflection unverified for SSMs.
  • KD confound: no QAT-only (no-teacher) ablation at 102M tokens — hard to attribute the efficiency to grouped QAT, KD, or both.
  • No code release mentioned; reproducible from public Mamba-2 ckpt + hyperparameter table, not turnkey.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the 3.61× storage claim hold at 8K–32K context where SSM recurrence errors would compound?
  • Is zero-ratio collapse specific to SSMs, or does it appear in Transformers / RWKV / RetNet under learnable per-group scales?
  • A clean QAT-only (no KD) at 102M tokens ablation — is the load-bearing ingredient grouped QAT, KD, or their interaction?
  • Can a custom W1.58 kernel for Mamba-2's selective scan (shapes 2048×8192, 4096×2048) realize the storage claim as wall-clock latency on Jetson / mobile NPU?
  • Does the recipe scale to Mamba-2 7B/8B — does zero-ratio collapse worsen with depth?
  • Is post-hoc correction's failure general to all recurrent SSMs (S4, S5, H3), or specific to Mamba-2's structured-mask selective scan?
  • How does the recipe interact with hybrid SSM-Attention / MoE architectures (Jamba, Zamba)?
