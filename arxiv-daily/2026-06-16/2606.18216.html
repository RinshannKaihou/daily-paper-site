Zone of Proximal Policy Optimization: Teacher in Prompts, Not Gradients

arXiv: 2606.18216

Date: 2026-06-16

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18216

一句话总结： 提出 ZPPO：把教师"留在 prompt 里"而非梯度中，用 BCQ（教师对 vs 学生错）和 NCQ（聚合学生自己的错误）两道 prompt 改写"全班错"的难题，配合"毕业/FIFO"回放缓冲，在 Qwen3.5 的 0.8B–9B 四档学生上 31 个基准（16 VLM + 10 LLM + 5 Video）一致击败 off/on-policy 蒸馏与 GRPO，且小模型差距越大、收益越大。
TL;DR: ZPPO is an RL post-training recipe for compact VLMs that, inspired by Vygotsky's "zone of proximal development", routes teacher signal into the prompt rather than the policy gradient. On questions where every student rollout fails (zero advantage, the "all-wrong" blind spot of GRPO), ZPPO constructs (i) a BCQ (Binary Candidate-included Question) that pairs one correct teacher response and one wrong student response as anonymized blocks, and (ii) an NCQ (Negative Candidate-included Question) that aggregates the student's own failed rollouts and tells the student that all listed answers are wrong. Both are stored in a prompt replay buffer that re-admits hard questions until the student "graduates" (mean rollout accuracy ≥ 0.5) or is FIFO-evicted. On Qwen3.5 VLMs at 0.8B / 2B / 4B / 9B (27B teacher), evaluated on 16 VLM + 10 LLM + 5 Video = 31 benchmarks, ZPPO beats off-policy distillation, on-policy distillation, and GRPO at every scale; ablations show each of BCQ, NCQ, and the replay buffer contributes, and BCQ alone beats both Hint and Prefix-style prompt-side teacher guidance.

核心贡献

  • 范式转变：教师在 prompt 而非梯度中：受 Vygotsky"最近发展区"启发，ZPPO 完全禁止把教师响应注入策略梯度——学生每个梯度计数的 token 都由当前学生策略采样，保持 RL 的 on-policy 假设。
  • 两道 prompt 改写 (BCQ + NCQ)：
  • BCQ = 1 个教师正确响应 + 1 个学生错误响应，统一压缩后用相同 标签匿名化、随机打乱，让学生必须"推理后选对"，没有显式 label 进入梯度。
  • NCQ = 把学生本轮所有错误 rollouts 解析后聚成一道 prompt，列出"下列答案均错"作为共享失败模式信号——这是训练循环内"独立 negative 第一次被学生同时看见"的位置。
  • Prompt 回放缓冲 + 毕业 / FIFO 淘汰：只存 question（不存响应）。每步把 rollout mean 准确率 < 0.5 的题回收入缓冲；反复回放直到 mean accuracy ≥ 0.5 "毕业"或缓冲满被 FIFO 淘汰。回放抽题数 = ρ_replay × 新题数，BCQ+NCQ 总量上限 = ρ_aug × 新题数。
  • 跨规模、跨模态经验性优势：Qwen3.5 0.8B/2B/4B/9B 四个学生 + 27B 教师，31 项基准（16 VLM / 10 LLM / 5 Video）一致击败 off-policy 蒸馏、on-policy 蒸馏、GRPO。VLM 16 基准上 0.8B 增益 +9.3 pp，9B 增益 +2.8 pp——学生越小、教师越强，收益越大。

Key Contributions

  • A new RL post-training paradigm (ZPPO) that places the teacher strictly inside the prompt rather than inside the student's policy gradient, sidestepping the off-policy drift of teacher-injected RL.
  • Two prompt-level reformulations on hard (all-wrong) questions:
  • BCQ (Binary Candidate-included Question) — uniform sample of one correct teacher rollout and one wrong student rollout, compressed by a frozen teacher, anonymized inside identical blocks, randomly shuffled, and presented to the student for discrimination.
  • NCQ (Negative Candidate-included Question) — aggregate all wrong student rollouts in the current group, list their parsed final answers as "all WRONG", and append the compressed reasoning traces — converting independent negatives into a single visible failure pattern.
  • A prompt replay buffer that stores only the question (no responses), admits items with mean rollout accuracy < 0.5, and re-samples them on later steps until the student "graduates" (mean rollout accuracy ≥ 0.5) or is FIFO-evicted under finite capacity, concentrating training on the student's current ZPD.
  • Empirical validation on the Qwen3.5 family at four student scales (0.8B / 2B / 4B / 9B) with a 27B teacher, evaluated on 31 benchmarks (16 VLM + 10 LLM + 5 Video), showing ZPPO > off-policy KD ≈ on-policy KD ≈ GRPO, with the gap largest at the smallest student and positive on LLM/Video benchmarks where distillation actively hurts.

Methodology

  • Backbone: Qwen3.5 family VLMs; students at 0.8B / 2B / 4B / 9B, teacher = 27B. Both sides are post-trained as VLMs on a multimodal RL dataset of image–question pairs.
  • RL base: GRPO group-relative policy optimization, using the REINFORCE++ two-step estimator (per-group mean subtract, then batch-level normalization across non-trivial groups, with zero-advantage groups excluded from batch statistics). ZPPO sits on top as a data-reformulation + replay mechanism, not a new optimizer.
  • RL recipe choices: DAPO-style "clip-higher" + token-level policy gradient loss + low iteration count + batch-level advantage normalization + zero-advantage group exclusion — the paper stresses that these "low-cost" choices account for a sizeable fraction of the small-model performance and are easy to mishandle.
  • Hard-question identification: rollout groups with mean outcome reward r̄_x < 0.5 (the "all-wrong" / borderline cases). Threshold motivated by the fact that under {0,1} rewards, std_x is maximized at r̄_x = 0.5, where the group-relative advantage carries the strongest learning signal.
  • Candidate compression: a frozen teacher rewrites both teacher-correct and student-wrong traces under a shared compression prompt and shared token cap, so prompt length is bounded; rewritten text appears only inside the prompt and never enters the gradient.
  • BCQ construction: sample one yT⁺ (correct teacher rollout) and one yS⁻ (wrong student rollout), teacher-compress both, anonymize as blocks, randomly shuffle, append to the original question x with the instruction "Here are two candidate responses in tags…One is correct and another is wrong."
  • NCQ construction: collect every wrong student rollout in the current group, parse each final answer, list them as "The following answers are all WRONG: ⟨parsed⟩", and append each compressed wrong trace inside blocks.
  • Prompt replay buffer B: stores only (x); admission = r̄_x < 0.5; graduation = r̄_x ≥ 0.5; per-step replay count = ρ_replay × |new|; per-step BCQ+NCQ cap = ρ_aug × |new|. Re-sampled questions are re-scored against the current student each visit, so the buffer always tracks the current ZPD boundary.
  • On-policy invariant: every response token in every counted rollout is sampled from the current student policy; teacher-derived text is input prompt only and never enters the policy gradient as a target.
  • Baselines: off-policy logit distillation, on-policy distillation (student-rollout-driven), vanilla GRPO, plus an explicit "GRPO†" that uses the same replay buffer volume to isolate the teacher-in-prompt contribution from the data-augmentation contribution.

Results & Highlights

  • Setup: 31-benchmark suite — 16 VLM (AI2D, BabyV, CharXiv, DynaM, EmbSp, InfoVQA, MVerse, MVision, MVista, MMMUPro, MM-Vet, OCREN, OCRZH, VisP, VBlind, WeMath) + 10 LLM (AIME25/26, CEval, GPQA-D, HLE, IMO-AB, MMLU, MMLU-Pro, MMLU-Rd, MultiCh) + 5 Video (MMVU, MVBench, VMME, VMMES, VMMMU).
  • VLM-16 (training-aligned) gains over strongest non-ZPPO baseline (Table 1, Tab. 10):
  • 0.8B: ZPPO 50.3 vs. Off-Distill† 45.4 / On-Distill† 45.8 / GRPO† 45.4 → +4.9 pp (paper-reported aggregate Δ ≈ +9.3 pp over no-ZPPO methods including original Qwen)
  • 2B: ZPPO 62.0 vs. GRPO† 59.2 → +2.8 pp; per-benchmark Δ up to +7.1 (MVerse), +7.1 (MMMUPro), +6.8 (CharXiv)
  • 4B / 9B: aggregate gains +4.0 / +2.8 pp respectively
  • LLM-10 + Video-5 (beyond training corpus) gains (Table 2, Tab. 11):
  • 0.8B: ZPPO 33.1 LLM / 52.8 Video, vs. Off-Distill† 22.5 / 45.0 → +10.6 / +7.8 pp; GPQA-D +16.9 pp
  • 2B: AIME26 30.0 → 40.0 (+10.0 pp), IMO-AB 11.3 → 29.5 (+18.2 pp), HLE 3.5 → 11.5 (+8.0 pp)
  • 4B / 9B: aggregate gains +2.7 / +2.7 pp
  • Headline asymmetry (the paper's central empirical claim): distillation hurts LLM/Video at every scale (-2.5 / -1.8 / -0.9 / -0.3 pp on aggregate), while ZPPO helps (+6.8 / +4.3 / +2.7 / +2.7 pp). The training-aligned VLM gains are also asymmetric: distillation ≈ +0.9 pp per scale vs. ZPPO +5.2 / +4.0 / +2.8 pp.
  • Ablations (Table 3 / Tab. 12): at 0.8B, GRPO† 45.4 → +BCQ 48.6 / +NCQ 46.2 / +Both 48.6 → ZPPO (full) 50.3; both components help, BCQ carries more weight at 0.8B, NCQ carries more at 9B. The 2B block shows the same monotonic stack: GRPO† 58.7 → +BCQ 60.8 / +NCQ 58.5 / +Both 59.7 → ZPPO 62.0.
  • Hint vs. Prefix vs. BCQ (Table 4, on 10 LLM + 5 Video at 0.8B): GRPO† 28.7 LLM / 50.5 Video → +Hint 28.8 / 50.0 → +Prefix 27.1 / 49.1 → +BCQ 31.5 / 52.0 → ZPPO 33.1 / 52.8. BCQ alone beats Hint (a labeled directional shortcut) and Prefix (a teacher-prefix that goes off-policy). The paper argues Prefix drifts because the teacher prefix routes teacher tokens into the response, not the prompt.
  • Teacher-size sensitivity (App. G, Fig. 7): shrinking teacher 27B → 9B → 4B collapses ZPPO's gain toward the GRPO†+NCQ level (BCQ's correct-candidate pool dries up). Steepest collapse at 0.8B, where ZPPO is most BCQ-dominated.
  • Buffer dynamics (Fig. 4 / Fig. 5): ZPPO graduates more items than GRPO† at every entry-accuracy bin (0% / 12.5% / 25% / 37.5%); the BCQ+NCQ share of the rollout batch grows with student scale (NCQ-dominated at 9B, BCQ+NCQ split at 0.8B); FIFO eviction rate stays moderate, indicating the buffer is genuinely being drained by graduation rather than stalling.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The high-level idea — "use the teacher as a prompt-side reference rather than a gradient target" — is genuinely different from standard KD and from prior hybrid RL+KD recipes (KDRL, RLKD) that route teacher signal through an auxiliary distillation term. The BCQ/NCQ split is a clean operationalization, and the graduation/FIFO buffer is a tidy curriculum mechanism. However, prompted discrimination between a correct and incorrect candidate has prior art (preference data construction for DPO/RLAIF, the LLM-as-judge line, KTO-style binary feedback), and the "failing student gets to see its own failures" idea in NCQ echoes the STaR / Quiet-STaR / ReST self-improvement lineage. The contribution is the combination and the hard-question-restricted deployment, not a brand-new primitive.
  • Rigor: Four student scales × 31 benchmarks is a substantial evaluation grid, and the comparison set (off-policy KD, on-policy KD, GRPO, GRPO†) is unusually well-chosen — the "GRPO† with the same replay volume" baseline is exactly the right control for separating the data-augmentation effect from the teacher-in-prompt effect. The paper is stronger than typical for a methodological RL paper in this regard. What is still missing: (i) cluster-bootstrap confidence intervals are only in the appendix (App. F.6), (ii) per-benchmark breakdowns beyond 0.8B/2B are deferred, (iii) the compute / wall-clock parity analysis is just a table in the appendix (Tab. 9) rather than a primary comparison.
  • Reproducibility: Public checkpoints, public reward graders, verbatim prompt templates, and Algorithm 1 in the appendix all help. Critical hyperparameters — buffer capacity, ρ_replay, ρ_aug, graduation threshold 0.5, compression prompt and token cap, k for NCQ aggregation, anonymization format, the exact clip-higher ratio and token-level loss coefficients from DAPO — are in the appendix but need to be read together with the cited external works. No code-release date in the abstract is a minor red flag.
  • Significance: Addresses a real and well-known pain point — small VLMs being crushed by logit KD from much larger teachers, and the "all-wrong rollout = no learning signal" blind spot of vanilla GRPO. The OOD result (ZPPO helps on LLM/Video benchmarks where distillation hurts) is the most compelling part of the paper: it is the right way to argue the recipe, and it would be very hard to dismiss as benchmark cherry-picking if the per-benchmark numbers survive the appendix. If these gains hold under tighter ablations and on non-Qwen3.5 backbones, this is a practically useful recipe.
  • Red flags:
  1. "Wins on 31 benchmarks" framing can hide per-benchmark regressions; the paper shows aggregate numbers and a few Δ calls (e.g., +9.2 pp on VBlind at 0.8B, +8.3 pp on MMMUPro, +8.2 pp on MVerse), but does not list any per-benchmark losses — at least one loss would lend honesty.
  2. The GRPO† control isolates replay volume but not "what's actually being learned from the teacher" — the teacher is still used for compression in NCQ, and for the correct-candidate pool in BCQ; a "BCQ with a random correct response" ablation would be the cleanest test of whether the teacher identity matters.
  3. The structural ceiling — questions where both teacher and student fail admit no BCQ candidate and collapse to NCQ-only — is honestly acknowledged but not quantified (no per-benchmark "teacher-also-fails" rate is reported). This is the recipe's main failure mode and the natural next problem.
  4. The "largest gains at the smallest scale" headline is the natural narrative for a small-student paper; on Qwen3.5 0.8B, the +9.3 pp VLM gain is large but is over a very weak base (raw Qwen3.5-0.8B VLM avg = 41.0), and the absolute ceiling is still 50.3 — far from a strong 2B+ model.
  5. No compute-matched wall-clock comparison in the main text (Tab. 9 is appendix); the replay buffer adds substantial extra rollouts per step, and some portion of the win could be budget rather than recipe.
  6. The dynamic-sampling tension (ZPPO re-admits exactly the all-wrong questions that DAPO dynamic sampling throws away) is honestly flagged in the limitations, which is good, but also means the recipe cannot be plugged into a DAPO pipeline unchanged.

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated framing tied to Vygotsky's ZPD — not just an engineering trick but a named paradigm.
  • Operationally simple on top of standard GRPO + DAPO + REINFORCE++: BCQ/NCQ + replay buffer, no new optimizer, no new loss term.
  • The OOD experiment is the strongest selling point: ZPPO improves on LLM/Video where distillation degrades; this is exactly the generalization claim one wants to see.
  • Ablation grid is unusually thorough — BCQ alone / NCQ alone / both / +replay / full, plus Hint and Prefix controls, plus teacher-size sweep, plus entry-accuracy-bin graduation plots.
  • Honest about the structural ceiling (teacher-also-fails) and about the dynamic-sampling incompatibility.
  • Side-steps the "teacher logit mode collapse" failure mode that genuinely hurts small-student KD, with a mechanism (prompt-side, never gradient-side) that preserves the on-policy property of RL.

Limitations

  • Teacher-bounded zone: BCQ needs the teacher to succeed on the hard question; questions where both fail admit only NCQ, which is weaker. Extending the zone beyond the current teacher (ensembles, synthetic prompts, curriculum-aware question selection) is left to future work.
  • Tension with dynamic sampling: ZPPO re-admits exactly the all-wrong questions that DAPO dynamic sampling discards; combining the two is non-trivial and left to a "run them in sequence" future-work suggestion.
  • Implementation complexity: hard-question routing, BCQ/NCQ construction, compression prompt, buffer capacity, ρ_replay, ρ_aug, graduation threshold — these add knobs that the paper-level numbers may not fully reflect.
  • Still requires a strong teacher at training time; ZPPO does not eliminate dependence on teacher capability (in fact, smaller teachers collapse BCQ's contribution).
  • Compute cost: replay buffer implies substantially more rollouts per step; no main-text wall-clock comparison.
  • Family-specific: evaluation skews to Qwen3.5 — generalization to LLaMA, InternVL, Molmo, SmolVLM, etc. is unverified.
  • No robustness, safety, or bias work: the paper explicitly notes the reward targets reasoning correctness, not safety, fairness, or harm; inherited Qwen3.5 pretraining biases are inherited.
  • No multi-turn / agentic / conversational / social / multi-sensor evaluation — the recipe is post-trained on single-turn reasoning and validated there.
  • Scope of OOD: the 5 Video benchmarks are the only fully-OOD signal; the 10 LLM benchmarks share pretraining with the VLM students and are "held out" rather than truly OOD.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How much of ZPPO's gain survives a "BCQ with a randomly chosen correct response" ablation — i.e., does the teacher's specific identity (not just a correct candidate) matter?
  • How sensitive is the result to buffer capacity, ρ_replay, ρ_aug, the graduation threshold 0.5, the NCQ aggregation count k, and the candidate-compression token cap?
  • Can ZPPO be cleanly composed with DAPO-style dynamic sampling by running them in sequence (dynamic sampling only on the residual after BCQ/NCQ reformulation), and what does that hybrid do to the +9.3 pp headline at 0.8B?
  • Does the OOD claim (ZPPO helps on LLM/Video where distillation hurts) hold on a non-Qwen3.5 backbone (LLaVA, InternVL, Molmo, SmolVLM)?
  • Is the structural ceiling (teacher-also-fails) best attacked by teacher ensembles, synthetic self-consistent prompts, or curriculum-aware question selection — and which is most compute-efficient?
  • Is ZPPO complementary with process-reward models / verifier-based filtering, or does the BCQ+NCQ pipeline subsume them?
  • How does ZPPO interact with long chain-of-thought training, and does the NCQ mechanism extend naturally to multi-step reasoning where the "shared failure mode" is at the step level rather than the answer level?
  • Does the replay buffer's graduation/FIFO discipline transfer to a setting where the reward is non-binary (e.g., LLM-as-judge with a continuous score), or is the 0.5 threshold specific to rule-based {0,1} rewards?
  • How does ZPPO perform on multi-turn / agentic / tool-use settings, where the "all-wrong group" structure of GRPO interacts with trajectory-level credit assignment?
