Vision-language models for chest radiography do not always need the image

arXiv: 2606.17710

Date: 2026-06-16

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17710

一句话总结： 论文设计了一个因果"图像干预三联审计"（目标区域遮挡 / 同大小不相关区域遮挡 / 跨患者同标签图像替换），评估9个胸片视觉语言模型，发现3个完全忽略图像、1个不稳定、5个选择性使用图像；纯文本模型与放射科医生准确率统计不可区分但 grounding 为零，作者据此提出"以接地审计而非准确率"作为临床部署门槛。
TL;DR: A causal "image-intervention triad" — (1) occlude the radiologist-marked target region, (2) occlude a same-size irrelevant region, and (3) swap in a different patient's same-label radiograph — is applied to 9 chest-X-ray VLMs. Three behavioral metrics (CGR = causal grounding rate; UAR = unrelated-image answer rate; IS = irrelevant-mask stability) sort the cohort into 3 models that ignore the image (CGR=0, UAR=100, IS=100), 1 unstable system (Mistral-Small-4-119B), and 5 selective image users. A 7B text-only model reaches within 5.7 points of the best multimodal system, a 119B multimodal model is statistically indistinguishable from the 7B text-only one (p=0.219), and the text-only model is not separable from a board-certified radiologist's accuracy while grounding at zero. Image use is partial (image-contingent fraction only 17.9–24.7%), finding-specific (atelectasis/lung-opacity are inert; cardiomegaly/edema/pneumonia are best-grounded), and view-dependent (PA > AP, the opposite of clinical need). Reported confidence separates grounded from ungrounded answers only for systems that actually use the image; ECE is 31–47 and AUROC tops out at 72.2. Conclusion: grounding audits, not accuracy, should gate clinical deployment.

核心贡献

  • 提出 图像干预三联（causal triad）：目标区域遮挡、同大小不相关区域遮挡（噪声基线控制）、跨患者同标签图像替换——三者联合而非单用，才能判定一个正确答案是否真正依赖图像。
  • 给出可形式化的三项行为指标：因果接地率 CGR（遮挡目标区域后正确答案翻转的比例）、无关图像答案保留率 UAR（跨患者换图后正确答案仍保留的比例）、无关区域遮挡稳定性 IS（遮挡无关区域后答案保留的比例），并派生出"接地特异性溢价" GSP = CGR − (100 − IS)。
  • 在 2,575 题的 MS-CXR + MIMIC-CXR + ReXErr-v1 综合探针集上审计 9 个系统（4 个通用多模态 Gemma-4-26B / Qwen3-VL-32B / Mistral-Small-4-119B / GPT-5；2 个医学专用 MedGemma-1.5-4B / LLaVA-Med-7B；2 个纯文本 MedGemma-27B-text / DeepSeek-R1-7B；1 个视觉专用 RAD-DINO 探针）。
  • 行为分类结果：3 个完全忽略图像（LLaVA-Med-7B、MedGemma-27B-text、DeepSeek-R1-7B，全部 CGR=0、UAR=IS=100）、1 个不稳定（Mistral-Small-4-119B，IS=56，CGR 不可解读）、5 个选择性使用图像（Gemma-4-26B、GPT-5、Qwen3-VL-32B、MedGemma-1.5-4B、RAD-DINO；CGR 6.4–33.5）。
  • 报告反直觉发现：最强多模态 Gemma-4-26B 仅比强文本基线 MedGemma-27B-text 高 5.7 ± 1.3 点；119B 多模态 Mistral-Small-4-119B 与 7B 文本 DeepSeek-R1-7B 准确率统计不可区分（差 −1.9 ± 1.4，pFDR = 0.219，n = 2,386）。
  • 与两名执业放射科医生比较：纯文本模型与医生准确率统计不可区分（差 +2.5，pFDR = 0.746）但 grounding 远低于医生（差 −25.0，pFDR = 0.001）；使用图像的模型 grounding 与医生相当或更高（MedGemma-1.5-4B 高出 +27.7）。
  • 揭示置信度的局限：自报置信度只在"使用图像"的模型上才能区分接地 / 未接地答案（最高 AUROC = 72.2），对忽略图像的模型反而是误导；ECE 31–47，远超通常 <5 的可接受水平。
  • 揭示图像使用的三层局部性：(a) 图像所决定的正确答案比例仅 17.9–24.7%；(b) 8 个发现中仅 5 个可被接地（心影增大、肺实变、水肿、胸腔积液、肺炎），肺不张与肺混浊完全惰性；(c) PA 视图 grounding 显著高于 AP 视图，临床方向相反。
  • 在 CheXpert（仅 UAR 可计算）、512 像素分辨率、3 种 prompt 措辞（默认/简洁/放射科角色）下分类稳健；UAR 的 Spearman ρ 在 MIMIC↔CheXpert 间达 0.931（p<0.001）。
  • 部署建议：接地审计应纳入临床部署的强制评估环节，准确率不足以作为监管/床边决策依据。

Key Contributions

  • A causal triad of image interventions (target-region occlusion, equal-area irrelevant-region occlusion, cross-patient same-label image swap) — none of the three interventions is informative alone; reading them jointly is the methodological advance.
  • Three formalized behavioral metrics: Causal Grounding Rate (CGR), Unrelated-Image Answer rate (UAR), Irrelevant-mask Stability (IS), plus the derived Grounding-Specificity Premium GSP = CGR − (100 − IS), which is positive only when answers are more sensitive to relevant than to irrelevant occlusion of identical area.
  • A 2,575-question probe set combining MS-CXR (radiologist-marked boxes for 8 findings), MIMIC-CXR (study-level labels, balanced), and ReXErr-v1 (report-error questions), plus a 1,380-question CheXpert cross-dataset probe.
  • Behavioral taxonomy on 9 systems: 3 ignore the image (LLaVA-Med-7B, MedGemma-27B-text, DeepSeek-R1-7B; all CGR=0, UAR=IS=100); 1 unstable (Mistral-Small-4-119B; IS=56, so CGR is uninterpretable); 5 selectively use the image (Gemma-4-26B, GPT-5, Qwen3-VL-32B, MedGemma-1.5-4B, RAD-DINO; CGR 6.4–33.5).
  • Empirical demonstration that benchmark accuracy is decoupled from image use: the strongest multimodal Gemma-4-26B beats the strong text-only baseline MedGemma-27B-text by only +5.7 ± 1.3 pp (pFDR < 0.001, n = 2,322); Mistral-Small-4-119B is statistically indistinguishable from DeepSeek-R1-7B (diff −1.9 ± 1.4, pFDR = 0.219, n = 2,386); MedGemma-27B-text significantly outscores the medical-specialist multimodal MedGemma-1.5-4B (+3.7, pFDR = 0.005) and LLaVA-Med-7B (+5.9, pFDR < 0.001).
  • Radiologist-comparator benchmarking by two board-certified radiologists (S.Z., 6 yrs; L.A., 10 yrs) on an 80-case sub-sample: the text-only model is not separable from the reference reader on accuracy (diff +2.5, pFDR = 0.746) but grounds far below (diff −25.0, pFDR = 0.001); uses-image models ground at reader-comparable or higher rates (MedGemma-1.5-4B +27.7, pFDR = 0.006).
  • Three-layer partiality of image use: image-contingent fraction only 17.9–24.7%; finding-specific (5 grounded findings: cardiomegaly / consolidation / edema / pleural effusion / pneumonia; 2 inert: atelectasis, lung opacity); view-dependent (PA > AP for every image user, significantly for Gemma-4-26B, GPT-5, RAD-DINO; AP views are the portable / supine / acutely-ill cases where grounding matters most).
  • Confidence-as-a-signal finding: confidence separates grounded vs. ungrounded correct answers by +33 to +52 points in uses-image multimodal models (Gemma-4-26B 97.5 vs 45.6; GPT-5 100.0 vs 48.6) but the signal vanishes or inverts for image-ignoring and unstable systems; ECE 31.4–47.0, AUROC tops at 72.2 — confidence alone is an insufficient deployment safeguard.
  • Cross-dataset transfer of the UAR ranking (Spearman ρ = 0.931, p < 0.001) and stability across 512-pixel resolution and three prompt phrasings; the categorizations are deterministic and hold for any IS threshold in [50, 90].
  • Regulatory and clinical deployment recommendation: grounding audits of this kind should accompany — not replace — accuracy benchmarks, and should be required before any clinical-deployment claim for medical multimodal systems.

Methodology

Probe set (MIMIC ecosystem, n = 2,575)

Three corpora are combined once and used unchanged for every model:

  • MS-CXR (n = 452): radiologist-marked bounding boxes for 8 findings (atelectasis, cardiomegaly, consolidation, edema, lung opacity, pleural effusion, pneumonia, pneumothorax); only source that supports target-region and irrelevant-region occlusion. Capped at 100 per finding.
  • MIMIC-CXR test split (n = 1,400): study-level CheXpert-vocabulary labels (CheXpert labeler), balanced 50 present / 50 absent per finding + 100 normals; supports original + swap only (no boxes).
  • ReXErr-v1 (n = 723): 483 image-dependent synthetic errors + 120 text-only errors + 120 no-error controls; supports original + swap.

Frontal-only (PA / AP), age/gender metadata required, MS-CXR cases removed from MIMIC-CXR pool to avoid double-counting. Cases whose finding label was uncertain were excluded. CheXpert cross-dataset probe (n = 1,380): same per-finding stratification but only original + swap are evaluable (no phrase-grounding boxes).

Four interventional conditions

For each (image I, question q, label y), with the question and model held fixed:

  1. Original: a_o = a(I, q).
  2. Swap: a_s = a(I′, q) where I′ is a different patient's same-label radiograph (label-matched to break patient-specific content).
  3. Target mask: a_t = a(M_T(I), q) where M_T masks the radiologist-marked box with a black rectangle.
  4. Irrelevant mask: a_i = a(M_I(I), q) where M_I masks an equal-size black rectangle at the corner farthest from the box centroid — the negative control for generic occlusion sensitivity.

Three behavioral metrics

  • Causal Grounding Rate (CGR) = fraction of correct-on-original answers that flip under target-region occlusion (Eq. 6).
  • Unrelated-Image Answer rate (UAR) = fraction of correct-on-original answers preserved under image swap (Eq. 7); equals 100 for a model that only uses the label, drops below 100 for a model that uses patient-specific image content.
  • Irrelevant-mask Stability (IS) = fraction of answers preserved under equal-area irrelevant-region occlusion; computed on all parsed cases (not conditioned on correctness, since it characterizes generic occlusion sensitivity).
  • Grounding-Specificity Premium (GSP) = CGR − (100 − IS); positive only when target-region occlusion flips answers more than irrelevant-region occlusion of the same area.

Category decision rules (deterministic on MIMIC probe)

  • Ignores image: CGR = 0, UAR = 100, IS = 100, each on ≥ 100 cases.
  • Unstable: IS < 70 (CGR cannot be read as localized grounding).
  • Uses image: CGR > 0 with 95% bootstrap CI excluding zero AND IS ≥ 90.

Rules were verified to assign every model to the same category for any threshold in IS ∈ [50, 90].

Inference protocol

  • Greedy decoding, temperature 0, 10-token budget for non-reasoning models, 2,048 for GPT-5 / DeepSeek-R1-7B.
  • Images resized to 224 × 224 bilinear (512 × 512 reserved for resolution robustness).
  • Default prompt: Is [display] present in this chest X-ray? Answer with a single word: Yes or No. (plus ReXErr sentence-accuracy variant).
  • Confidence = renormalized first-token probability over {Yes, yes, …} ∪ {No, no, …}.
  • RAD-DINO: frozen encoder at native 518 × 518; L2 logreg per finding trained on the source dataset's training split (MIMIC for MIMIC probe; CheXpert for CheXpert probe) — hence RAD-DINO is in-distribution on CheXpert (flagged in cross-dataset plots).

Statistical analysis

  • All proportions: analytical SE (binomial) + percentile bootstrap 95% CI (10,000 resamples); Wilson intervals for per-finding cells with n < 30.
  • Paired bootstrap for between-model comparisons; shift-and-reflect two-sided p-value (clip at 1/B).
  • Benjamini–Hochberg FDR at 5% within declared comparison families.
  • Subgroup tests: 1,000-permutation distribution-free tests on gender (M/F), view (PA/AP), age (<50, 50–70, >70).

Radiologist evaluation

Two board-certified radiologists (S.Z. 6 yrs, L.A. 10 yrs), blinded to model identity, source corpus, and each other, on a stratified MIMIC probe-set sub-sample: 120-case box-validity task (15/finding), 80-case finding-presence task under original + target + irrelevant masks (radiologist accuracy / CGR / IS computed by same metric definitions), and failure-mode classification for Gemma-4-26B / GPT-5 / RAD-DINO.

Results & Highlights

Headline accuracy vs. grounding

  • Master metrics on the MIMIC probe (n = 2,575): see Table 1. Headline values:
  • Gemma-4-26B: Acc 66.2 ± 0.9, CGR 33.2 ± 2.4, UAR 80.4 ± 1.0, IS 96.0 ± 1.0 — Uses image.
  • GPT-5: Acc 64.7 ± 1.0, CGR 24.5 ± 2.3, UAR 75.4 ± 1.1, IS 90.3 ± 1.6 — Uses image.
  • Qwen3-VL-32B: Acc 62.9 ± 1.0, CGR 17.5 ± 2.1, UAR 78.7 ± 1.0, IS 90.1 ± 1.7 — Uses image.
  • MedGemma-27B-text (no image): Acc 60.1 ± 1.0, CGR 0.0, UAR 100.0, IS 100.0 — Ignores image.
  • RAD-DINO: Acc 58.8 ± 1.1, CGR 6.4 ± 1.2, UAR 82.1 ± 1.1, IS 99.5 ± 0.4 — Uses image.
  • MedGemma-1.5-4B: Acc 55.3 ± 1.0, CGR 33.5 ± 2.4, UAR 76.5 ± 1.1, IS 94.4 ± 1.2 — Uses image.
  • LLaVA-Med-7B: Acc 51.4 ± 1.0, CGR 0.0, UAR 100.0, IS 100.0 — Ignores image (degenerate always-Yes).
  • DeepSeek-R1-7B (no image): Acc 45.5 ± 1.0, CGR 0.0, UAR 100.0, IS 100.0 — Ignores image.
  • Mistral-Small-4-119B: Acc 43.0 ± 1.0, CGR 40.0 ± 9.8, UAR 84.5 ± 1.1, IS 56.0 ± 9.9 — Unstable (CGR uninterpretable; only 25 informative cases).

Accuracy ↔ image use decoupling (paired, on shared cases)

  • Gemma-4-26B vs. MedGemma-27B-text: +5.7 ± 1.3 pp (pFDR < 0.001, n = 2,322).
  • GPT-5 vs. MedGemma-27B-text: +3.0 pp (p = 0.032).
  • RAD-DINO vs. MedGemma-27B-text: +3.0 pp (p = 0.011).
  • Qwen3-VL-32B vs. MedGemma-27B-text: +2.2 (p = 0.115, n.s.).
  • MedGemma-1.5-4B vs. MedGemma-27B-text: −3.7 (p = 0.005) — a uses-image system loses to a text-only model.
  • LLaVA-Med-7B vs. MedGemma-27B-text: −5.9 (p < 0.001).
  • Mistral-Small-4-119B vs. DeepSeek-R1-7B: −1.9 ± 1.4 (pFDR = 0.219, n = 2,386) — a 119B multimodal is statistically indistinguishable from a 7B text-only.
  • vs. weak baseline DeepSeek-R1-7B: Mistral-Small-4-119B is the only "multimodal" system that still loses (−18.9, p < 0.001); the gap for image-using models grows to +12.0 to +20.5 pp.

Image-contingent fraction (Fig. 4a)

Of correct-on-original answers, the fraction that flips under image swap (i.e., depends on the actual patient-specific image content) is only:

  • GPT-5: 24.7%, MedGemma-1.5-4B: 23.5%, Qwen3-VL-32B: 21.3%, Gemma-4-26B: 19.6%, RAD-DINO: 17.9%, Mistral-Small-4-119B: 15.5%.
  • Every multimodal / unstable system has UAR significantly below 100 (paired vs. text-only baselines, all pFDR < 0.001; differences −15.1 to −30.3 pp) — but the swap-invariant majority means accuracy is not what it looks like.

Per-finding grounding (Fig. 5)

8 findings: atelectasis, lung opacity, pneumothorax, pleural effusion, edema, consolidation, pneumonia, cardiomegaly.

  • Inert across the cohort: atelectasis (CGR = 0 for 4/5 image users; only Gemma-4-26B at 27.3 ± 7.8) and lung opacity (CGR = 0 for 4/5).
  • Grounded block (Wilson lower > 0 for every evaluable image user): cardiomegaly, consolidation, edema, pleural effusion, pneumonia. Peak CGRs:
  • Gemma-4-26B: pneumonia 63.2 ± 6.4, cardiomegaly 48.0.
  • MedGemma-1.5-4B: edema 69.7 ± 8.0, pneumonia 53.1.
  • GPT-5: cardiomegaly 50.0, pneumonia 21.3.
  • Qwen3-VL-32B: pleural effusion 100.0 (n=1), cardiomegaly 3.0.
  • RAD-DINO: cardiomegaly 1.0, pleural effusion 16.9 — collapses on the findings multimodal systems ground best, getting accuracy through global features.
  • Model ranking inverts across findings — no system grounds uniformly.

View-dependent grounding (Table 2)

CGR is higher on PA than AP for every image user, significantly for:

  • Gemma-4-26B: 73.0 (PA) vs 25.1 (AP), q = 0.002.
  • GPT-5: 38.9 vs 22.0, q = 0.050.
  • RAD-DINO: 13.5 vs 4.8, q = 0.028.
  • And in the same direction for Qwen3-VL-32B (q = 0.265) and MedGemma-1.5-4B (q = 0.110).

AP acquisitions are portable / supine on more acutely ill patients — grounding is weakest where it would matter most.

Robustness

  • Cross-dataset (MIMIC ↔ CheXpert, n = 1,380): UAR Spearman ρ = 0.931 (p < 0.001; 0.900 excluding RAD-DINO) — ignores-image systems all at UAR = 100, image users at 73.4–86.5; accuracy transfer is weaker (Spearman ρ = 0.617, p = 0.077; 0.786 excluding RAD-DINO), driven by RAD-DINO going in-distribution on CheXpert (58.8 → 71.4). Multimodal-over-baseline gap grows on CheXpert: 5.7 → 8.6 pp; MedGemma-1.5-4B reverses its deficit to +5.5 pp.
  • Resolution (224 ↔ 512 px): CGR Spearman ρ = 0.948; no system crosses a category boundary; Mistral stays highest-CGR (40.0 → 50.0).
  • Prompt phrasing (default / terse / radiologist-framed): terse variant collapses parsing for 5 systems (LLaVA-Med-7B parse rate 1%, MedGemma-27B-text 33%, MedGemma-1.5-4B 40%, Gemma-4-26B 68%, Mistral 9%); on parseable subsets, category assignment is unchanged — UAR 100 for ignores-image, <100 for image users.

Confidence calibration (Table 3)

  • Uses-image multimodal: grounded-correct confidence >> ungrounded-correct confidence (Gemma-4-26B 97.5 vs 45.6, GPT-5 100.0 vs 48.6, Qwen3-VL-32B 82.3 vs 44.6, MedGemma-1.5-4B 95.0 vs 62.1). Differences are several × SE — low confidence is informative for these systems.
  • RAD-DINO: highest on incorrect cases (74.4 ± 26.5) — confidence is anti-calibrated for a global-feature model.
  • Mistral-Small-4-119B: AUROC 45.0 ± 1.2 [42.7, 47.3] — below chance.
  • Ignores-image: no grounded-correct pool; LLaVA-Med-7B near-constant confidence across regimes; MedGemma-27B-text 76.6 ungrounded-correct vs 77.9 incorrect.
  • AUROC for label detection: best 72.2 ± 1.0 (Gemma-4-26B); Qwen3-VL-32B 69.4; MedGemma-1.5-4B 63.7; RAD-DINO 57.9; LLaVA-Med-7B 64.8; MedGemma-27B-text 54.2.
  • ECE: 31.4 (Mistral) to 47.0 (LLaVA-Med-7B) — far above the <5 typically considered acceptable.

Radiologist comparator

  • Reference reader S.Z.: 81.3 ± 4.3 acc, CGR 23.1 ± 5.2, IS 93.8 ± 3.0.
  • Second reader L.A.: 58.8 ± 5.5 acc, CGR 0.0, IS 100.0 — a board-certified radiologist can register no answer change under masking.
  • Inter-rater agreement: percent 64.6, Cohen's κ 0.224 (slight-fair), weighted κ 0.199 — fair agreement on 240 shared judgments.
  • Box validity: 48.3 ± 4.6 of MS-CXR boxes fully contain the queried evidence, 83.3 contain it partially; per-finding from 100.0 (cardiomegaly) to 6.7 (edema). The "ground truth" against which CGR is scored is itself noisy and subjective.
  • Model vs. radiologist (paired):
  • MedGemma-27B-text vs S.Z.: Acc +2.5 (p = 0.746, n.s.); CGR −25.0 (p = 0.001) — text-only ≍ radiologist accuracy, grounds far less.
  • RAD-DINO: Acc +8.6 (p = 0.180, n.s.); CGR +9.4 (p = 0.342, n.s.).
  • MedGemma-1.5-4B: Acc −15.0 (p = 0.019); CGR +27.7 (p = 0.006) — exceeds the reader.
  • Gemma-4-26B: Acc −20.0 (p = 0.002); CGR −9.1 (p = 0.354).
  • Mistral-Small-4-119B: Acc −76.2 (p < 0.001); CGR −16.9 (no paired p).
  • Failure-mode classification of model errors (radiologist review): most classified as ambiguous or plausible image confounders; GPT-5 35% image-quality + 29% plausible-confounder; RAD-DINO 62% plausible-confounder; Gemma-4-26B 59% ambiguous.

Critical Assessment

Strengths

  • The interventional framing is the strongest part: post-hoc saliency / attention maps can fail sanity checks and do not establish causal dependence, but the image-side do-interventions (Pearl-style) directly test whether the answer moves when evidence is removed.
  • The triad is read jointly, not as three independent metrics — correctly handles the case where CGR is high only because the model is unstable to any occlusion (Mistral).
  • Concrete, named model panel (including the strongest available frontier closed system GPT-5) and a strong matched text-only baseline (MedGemma-27B-text) are exactly the right controls; the additional weak baseline (DeepSeek-R1-7B) is a thoughtful inclusion.
  • Statistical hygiene is strong: paired bootstrap with shift-and-reflect p-values, BH-FDR within declared families, Wilson intervals for small-n cells, fixed seeds. Bootstrap CIs accompany every proportion.
  • Robustness checks (cross-dataset, 2 resolutions, 3 prompt phrasings) are exactly what's needed to defend a "category, not leaderboard" claim.
  • Radiologist comparator with 6 / 10 yrs post-training experience; failure-mode classification by radiologists avoids the common trap of blaming models for label noise.
  • Honest framing of failure modes for the method itself (conservatism of CGR, IS noise floor, redundant evidence, box validity ~50%, inter-rater κ = 0.224). The paper does not oversell.
  • Reproducibility: code, prompts, manifests, parser, bootstrap indices, model IDs all public.

Limitations (as acknowledged and additional)

  1. CGR is a conservative lower bound on image use. A model reading through global features that survive a local occlusion (the signature of RAD-DINO) registers low CGR despite genuinely using the image; a swap to a same-label image preserves the finding for diffuse / bilateral pathologies. The best system rises only from 33.2 → 43.9 when restricted to boxes the radiologist rated as fully covering the finding. The authors propose opposite-label swaps, attribution methods targeting global cues, or generative counterfactuals as future work.
  2. Box validity itself is noisy: only 48% of MS-CXR boxes fully contain the queried evidence; per-finding box validity ranges from 100% (cardiomegaly) to 6.7% (edema). The ground truth against which grounding is scored is subjective — and notably, model CGR tracks box validity (Fig. 7e), so the metric is partly measuring where the label can be localized, not only whether the model reads.
  3. Interventions push off-distribution: a share of answer changes reflects sensitivity to unfamiliar images (occluded patches, swapped patients) rather than loss of diagnostic evidence; semantically realistic counterfactuals would be more faithful.
  4. Small human reference: only 2 radiologists on 80 cases; Cohen's κ = 0.224; one reader (L.A.) registered CGR = 0, so the human comparison rests largely on the reference reader (S.Z.). The "+2.5 pp accuracy" finding vs. S.Z. is a "failure to reject" on n=80, not a formal equivalence test.
  5. Binary QA only: the probe is yes/no finding-presence and report-error questions, not free-form report generation — these models' primary clinical use. Grounding in open-ended generation may differ substantially.
  6. Fixed protocol: zero-shot, single-turn, deterministic decoding, small prompt set. Few-shot, chain-of-thought, RAG (e.g., RadioRAG from the same lab, ref. 43), or agentic tool use could change image use. The categorizations are diagnostic, version- and protocol-specific.
  7. Label noise: CheXpert labeler, not pixel-verified; base rates on the binary QA are exploitable, so absolute accuracies are benchmark-relative. The text-only baselines partly exploit the statistical structure of the benchmark itself.
  8. No protected attribute or fairness audit beyond gender / age subgroup descriptives; subgroup effects that did not replicate across models are explicitly not interpreted.
  9. No release of GPT-5: closed-source model accessed via API, so weight-level reproducibility is impossible — but the intervention works without weights, which is a deliberate strength.
  10. Categories are properties of model × probe × protocol: the assignment of LLaVA-Med-7B and Mistral-Small-4-119B in particular could change with fine-tuning or prompt engineering. The paper flags this honestly.
  11. LLaVA-Med-7B failure mode (always-Yes, sensitivity 99.9, specificity 0.0) is a striking but not new finding in the medical-VLM literature; including it in a "grounding" panel is informative but slightly tangential to the central "uses vs ignores" message.

Red flags / things to verify on a re-read

  • The text-only-vs-radiologist comparison rests on n=80 cases and a single reference reader — the "statistically indistinguishable" framing is correct (it is a non-rejection) but should not be read as equivalence.
  • The CheXpert cross-dataset transfer is a single additional dataset; a third (e.g., NIH ChestX-ray14, PadChest) would strengthen the claim.
  • Mistral-Small-4-119B has only 25 informative cases for CGR / IS on MIMIC — the "unstable" classification has wide CIs (CGR [20.0, 60.0]; IS [36.0, 76.0]) and should be considered suggestive rather than definitive.
  • The five grounded findings are also the ones with highest box validity (cardio 100%, pneumonia / consolidation / pleural effusion / edema all > 60%); the "finding-specific grounding" finding partially reduces to "grounding tracks label localizability" — a subtle but important interpretive caveat the authors do flag.

Novelty

  • The causal-audit framing for medical VLMs is timely and addresses a real gap. Most prior benchmarks reward finding-name priors as much as image use. The intervention design is straightforward but well-motivated.
  • The behavioral taxonomy (ignores / unstable / uses) is a useful conceptual contribution over raw accuracy.
  • The paper explicitly does not propose a new model or training objective — it is a measurement paper, and the right contribution at this stage of the field.

Significance

  • High for the medical-AI deployment community: a frontier closed model (GPT-5) grounds at only 24.5% on a conservative metric; a 119B open model is statistically indistinguishable from a 7B text-only; a text-only model matches a radiologist's accuracy while grounding at 0%. These are not academic findings.
  • The regulatory framing ("grounding audits should gate clinical deployment") is exactly the conversation the field needs to have.

Strengths

  • Clean, Pearl-style causal framing of a question the field has been hand-waving about.
  • Strong empirical evidence with statistical comparisons: text-only ≍ multimodal on accuracy; multimodal ≍ radiologist; grounding is the orthogonal axis that separates them.
  • Matched text-only baselines (both a strong MedGemma-27B-text and a weak DeepSeek-R1-7B) — the right controls for "is the image even helping".
  • Vision-only probe (RAD-DINO + L2 logreg) provides a global-feature comparator that helps interpret the multimodal findings.
  • Radiologist comparator at board-certified level with a second-reader agreement analysis; failure-mode classification.
  • Robustness across dataset, resolution, and prompt phrasing with the categorizations stable.
  • Actionable deployment recommendation tied directly to the empirical findings.
  • Code + prompts + manifests released; transparent about bootstrap seeds, parser, confidence formula.
  • Honest about the conservative nature of CGR and the noise in the box annotations and the human reference.
  • Per-finding resolution gives a clinically actionable picture — which findings any given model grounds, and which it ignores.

Limitations

  • Chest X-ray only; generalization to CT, MRI, dermatology, pathology is open.
  • Binary QA only; free-form report generation is the primary clinical use and is not audited.
  • Small human reference (2 radiologists, 80 cases, κ = 0.224) — the radiologist comparison is indicative, not definitive.
  • Box validity ~50% — the ground truth against which grounding is scored is itself noisy and subjective.
  • Single protocol (zero-shot, single-turn, deterministic, 3 prompts) — few-shot / CoT / RAG / agentic tool use could change the categorization.
  • 9 systems only — broader coverage (e.g., Med-PaLM M, RadFM, BiomedGPT, CheXagent, MedSAM-2) would strengthen the cohort claims.
  • No fairness audit beyond descriptive gender/age subgroup effects.
  • Labels are report-derived, not pixel-verified — finding-presence questions carry exploitable base rates that the text-only baselines are partly leveraging.
  • CGR / IS are conservative for models using global features and for diffuse / bilateral findings.
  • Mistral "unstable" classification has wide CIs (n = 25 informative cases) — the labeling is suggestive.
  • LLaVA-Med-7B's "ignores image" status is a degenerate always-Yes (sens 99.9, spec 0.0) — a known pathology in medical VLMs, included for completeness but not the central message.
  • No proposed training objective or deployment protocol — the paper diagnoses but does not fix; training objectives that explicitly reward causal image use are flagged as future work.

Relevance

与"多模态模型可靠性评估、模型校准与失败模式分析、临床 AI 部署治理"三个研究方向高度相关。该工作用因果干预三联替代传统准确率基准，提出"行为接地审计"作为新的评测范式，可直接迁移到 CT、MRI、病理等其它医学影像模态的多模态模型审计，并对模型校准与置信度门控提出了明确的方法论批判。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the audit generalize to free-form report generation (the primary clinical use of these models), where grounding in generated text may be subtler than in binary answers?
  • Per-finding resolution is currently confined to 8 MS-CXR findings. Does the "selectively uses the image" pattern hold across a broader label set — and do the inert findings (atelectasis, lung opacity) become grounded when defined more tightly (e.g., segmental atelectasis vs subsegmental)?
  • Could a calibrated, model-agnostic ungroundedness detector be trained on the audit signals alone, so models can flag when they are guessing from text priors at inference time — and would it be deployable as a guardrail for image-ignoring systems where self-confidence is uninformative?
  • How does the audit behave under counterfactual image interventions (generative removal of the finding while preserving image statistics) — would opposite-label swap give a cleaner signal than same-label swap, and does GSP retain its sign?
  • Would a grounding-required model card or regulatory standard be feasible to operationalize, and what statistical threshold should define clinical acceptance? (e.g., CGR ≥ 0.5 with CI excluding 0.3, on a stratified-finding benchmark.)
  • Can training objectives that explicitly reward causal image use (ref. 45 in the paper, "right for the right reasons") actually move models from "ignores" or "selective" to "consistently uses" — and at what cost to benchmark accuracy?
  • How does the audit interact with RAG and agentic tool use (RadioRAG, ref. 43, from the same lab) — does retrieval reduce the model's incentive to ground locally, or force it to?
  • Does the view-dependence (PA > AP) reflect training-data imbalance (most public CXR training sets are PA-heavy), and would retraining / fine-tuning on balanced PA / AP cohorts close the gap?
  • Would a multi-reader adjudicated reference (with consensus regions rather than single boxes) change the conclusions about which models are "grounding enough"?
  • How robust is the UAR ranking transfer (Spearman ρ = 0.931) when adding a third or fourth dataset (NIH ChestX-ray14, PadChest, VinDr-CXR)?
