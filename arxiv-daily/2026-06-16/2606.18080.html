Edge Flow · arXiv 2606.18080

A three-ODE continuous-time model that finally reproduces the sharpness overshoot at the edge of stability — where prior flow models cannot.

Edge Flow: A Tractable and Predictive Continuous-Time Model for Gradient Descent at the Edge of Stability · Pierre Marion (Inria · ENS · PSL) · 2026-06-16
edge-of-stability continuous-time ODE optimization theory self-stabilization sharpness EGD (discretization)
Key Empirical Lever

Refining the center-discretization step (K↑) widens the stable-η range — the only algorithmic claim with a concrete win in the paper.

Why This Matters

Sharpness self-stabilization is ubiquitous in modern large-model training — Edge Flow is the first continuous-time model that explains it as a feedback loop between sharpness and oscillation magnitude, not as an imposed constraint.

Critical Edge

For a paper whose central selling point is "tractable, faithful, and predictive," there is no quantitative error bound between Edge Flow and GD — no convergence theorem, no supt ‖wtGD − w̄tEF‖ proof, no stability-radius theorem. The "faithful model" claim rests entirely on six small-scale full-batch runs.

Methodology · the three ODEs

Model the GD iterate as wt ≈ w̄t + (−1)t xtut and derive autonomous dynamics for the three quantities. Time is calibrated so one GD step = a flow interval of η. Sharpness S(w) = λmax(∇²L); the EoS threshold is 2/η.

  1. Center (eq. 1). Symmetrized-loss gradient flow. dw̄/dt = −½ [ ∇L(w̄+xu) + ∇L(w̄−xu) ] Taylor-expands to ∇L(w̄) + (x²/2)∇³L(w̄)[u,u] + O(x⁴) — a third-derivative correction that pushes w̄ toward lower curvature. This is precisely Damian et al. (2023)'s self-stabilization, derived from first principles.
  2. Direction (eq. 2). Rayleigh-quotient / Oja flow on the sphere. du/dt = 2[ ∇²L(w̄) − (uᵀ∇²L(w̄)u)·I ] u Attracting to the top Hessian eigenvector at a rate set by the spectral gap.
  3. Magnitude (eq. 3). Scalar exponential ODE. dx/dt = ( S(w̄) − 2/η ) x Growth when S > 2/η, decay when S < 2/η. x = 0 is the gradient-flow equilibrium; a floor ε > 0 seeds the instability.
  4. The feedback loop. Eq. (3) reads the sharpness at the center; eq. (1) reads the gradients at the endpoints. The (S, x) pair is a negative-feedback oscillator that attracts S to 2/η without imposing it.
    Well-posedness via Picard–Lindelöf; sphere and half-line are invariant. Derivation explicitly neglects higher-order coupled corrections and excludes quadratic objectives.
  5. Discretization (EGD). Forward Euler on (1) and (3), power method on (2), with a sub-step ρ ≤ η and a floor x ← max(x, ε).
    Cost: 2 grad evals + 1 HV-product per substep; S(w̄) = ‖∇²L(w̄)u‖ comes free. ρ=η (K=1) ≈ 3× a GD step; default K=8.

Results & Highlights

Faithful tracking across six architectures. MLP, CNN, ResNet, ViT × {MSE, CE} on 1000-example 4-class CIFAR-10, full-batch GD. Edge Flow tracks the GD trajectory, train loss, and top-3 Hessian eigenvalues in every config. In CNN/MSE the two flows are closer to each other than either is to GD — yet they decompose the oscillation differently.
The only model that resolves the EoS onset oscillation. Top-3 effective eigenvalues ηλi hit the threshold 2 in staggered fashion and then ring around it. Edge Flow reproduces the overshoot + ringing; Central Flow clamps S at 2 (cannot oscillate); Rod Flow fails to detect the onset at all (S drifts upward past 2).
Two knobs for stability — swept on a small MLP/MSE with η = 0.02:

Refine center (K ↑) Suppresses the loss spike; widens stable-η range. K=4 EGD stays stable at η where both GD and K=1 EGD diverge.

Diagnosed discrepancy. At the K=8 default, the oscillation frequency of Edge Flow does not match GD as tightly as K=1 small-ε does — attributed to discretization error in the stiff magnitude ODE. Author's verdict: the K=8 default is a tradeoff; the mismatch "does not matter much for the overall learning dynamics and implicit regularization effect of large stepsizes."

Strengths

  • Clean, interpretable three-ODE decomposition mapped onto probeable quantities (top eigenvector, sharpness, drift).
  • Realistic cost — 2 grad evals + 1 HV-product/substep — usable as a diagnostic on real networks.
  • Predictive, not just descriptive: EGD is a runnable algorithm that emerges naturally from the discretization.
  • Unifying view — sharpness stabilization is a feedback loop, not a black-box; explains why Central Flow cannot capture onset ringing.
  • Reproducible: public codebase (extends Cohen et al.'s central_flows), explicit hyperparameter table, LLM use disclosed, climate impact estimated.
  • Honest scope: derivation is framed as an intuitive first-principles argument, not a theorem.

Limitations

  • Full-batch GD only; stability, faithfulness, and ε in the stochastic / minibatch / momentum / adaptive case are open.
  • Requires Hessian–vector products — strictly more expensive than one GD step.
  • No theorem-level guarantees: no convergence rate, no error bound, no stability-radius theorem. Paper is largely empirical on the modeling side.
  • Quadratic objectives explicitly excluded from the derivation — the simplest convex-optimization sanity check is not addressed.
  • Small experimental scale: 1000-example 4-class CIFAR-10, models up to 661k params, full-batch. No head-to-head with SAM or other sharpness-aware optimizers at standard benchmarks.
  • "Mitigation" use case is sketched on one MLP — a benchmarked EGD-vs-GD-vs-SAM comparison is left to future work.
  • K and ε are two new hyperparameters the practitioner must tune to recover GD-like behavior.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the (S, x) feedback loop survive in transformer LM training at ≥100M parameters with minibatch SGD + momentum?
  2. Can the magnitude ODE be inverted into a principled SAM-analog or adaptive-η scheme that provably damps EoS oscillations?
  3. Is the self-stabilization mechanism unique to this three-ODE split, or do equivalent feedback structures exist in momentum-augmented / adaptive-stepsize flows?
  4. What is a quantitative supt ‖wtGD − w̄tEF‖ bound near the EoS fixed point or at small η — and does the symmetrized-gradient cancellation argument make it achievable?
  5. Past the onset of EoS — does Edge Flow predict the eventual "progressive sharpening → EoS → break-even" cycles and the implicit bias at large η?
  6. The symmetrized loss in eq. (1) is hand-designed. Can it be learned (or replaced by a higher-order Taylor expansion) without breaking the clean ODE structure?
  7. Is there a small-perturbation limit around a quadratic in which Edge Flow recovers known closed-form GD solutions — a calibration check?
  8. Does adaptive ρ (stiff-ODE step-size control à la Hairer–Wanner) make EGD both stable at large η and competitive in wall-clock / sample efficiency with tuned SGD-with-momentum?
