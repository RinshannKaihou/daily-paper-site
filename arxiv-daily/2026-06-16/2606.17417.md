> **一句话总结：** 本文针对大音频语言模型（LALM）在时序推理任务上的系统失败，构建了一个源自 TACOS 数据集、含 1,657 道四选一题的诊断基准（Earliest Onset / Latest Offset / Longest Duration 三项基础任务），并在 Audio-Flamingo-3 与 DeSTA-2.5-Audio 两个完全开源模型上做了首次因果机制干预：发现**注意力重分配（scaling）优于单纯放大（upweighting）**，且在瓶颈层（如 AF3 第 20 层、DeSTA 第 9 层）施加干预可在零微调下将平均准确率从 55.9% 提升至 59.1%。

> **TL;DR:** This paper asks *why* Large Audio-Language Models (LALMs) fail at temporal reasoning. It builds a controlled 1,657-question benchmark (Earliest Onset, Latest Offset, Longest Duration — three foundational tasks derived from TACOS) and runs the first causal mechanistic study on open-source LALMs. Behavioral evidence + attention-pattern analysis confirms models rely heavily on text when both modalities are available. Two training-free attention interventions — **upweighting** (add α to audio-token logits) vs. **scaling/ScalingVis** (multiply audio-token logits by α) — are compared. **Scaling wins**, with the optimal α being architecture-dependent (AF3: sharpening α=2.0; DeSTA-2.5-Audio: smoothing α=0.2). Combining keyword-token + last-token intervention gives the highest fix rates. Applied as a layer-targeted intervention (AF3 layer 20, DeSTA layer 9), average accuracy rises from 55.9% → 59.1% without any fine-tuning.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了一个面向**机制分析**而非通用评测的时序推理基准：源自 TACOS 的 1,657 道四选一题，覆盖 Earliest Onset（528）、Latest Offset（499）、Longest Duration（630）三项基础任务。
- 在四项开源 LALM（Qwen2-Audio-7B、Kimi-Audio-7B、Audio-Flamingo-3、DeSTA2.5-Audio-Llama-3.1-8B）上系统验证：当文本可用时，模型显著倾向于依赖文本（CQA 普遍高于 AQA），且 ACQA 相比 CQA 几乎没有增益，证实音频未被有效整合。
- 给出 LALM 时序推理失败的**首个因果机制分析**（区别于先前的相关性观察）：在注意力层面进行训练自由的干预实验。
- 提出 upweighting 与 scaling（改编自视觉-语言模型的 ScalingVis）的**正面对比**，并通过 task-relevant keyword token 选择给出最优干预位置。
- 给出无需微调的"瓶颈层干预"实用方案：识别 AF3 第 20 层、DeSTA 第 9 层作为关键干预点，平均准确率从 55.9% → 59.1%（+3.2 个百分点）。

## Key Contributions

- **Diagnostic benchmark** of 1,657 multiple-choice questions across three foundational temporal tasks (EO, LO, LD) built from TACOS clips with precise onset/offset annotations. Narrow scope is intentional — enables mechanistic (not just behavioral) diagnosis.
- **Behavioral analysis on four LALMs** (Qwen2-Audio, Kimi-Audio, Audio-Flamingo-3, DeSTA2.5-Audio) confirms text-dominant reliance: CQA > AQA in most settings; ACQA barely improves over CQA, showing audio fails to refine temporal answers.
- **Layer-wise attention-pattern analysis** shows text-dominant attention across nearly all layers in Audio-Flamingo-3 (and qualitatively similar patterns elsewhere), providing correlational evidence of modality imbalance.
- **First causal mechanistic intervention study** for LALM temporal reasoning, on the only two fully open-weight/code/data LALMs (Audio-Flamingo-3, DeSTA-2.5-Audio):
  - *Upweighting:* `logit ← logit + α` on audio tokens (increases total audio attention mass).
  - *Scaling (ScalingVis):* `logit ← α · logit` on audio tokens (redistributes, sharpens α>1, smooths α<1).
- **Fix-rate evaluation** on previously-incorrect samples shows scaling beats upweighting: AF3 best scaling 20.5% vs. best upweighting 15.8%; DeSTA best scaling 20.1% vs. best upweighting 10.1%.
- **Architecture-dependent optimal intervention:** AF3 benefits from sharpening (α=2.0); DeSTA-2.5-Audio from smoothing (α=0.2) — the failure mode is "imprecise vs. misaligned" attention, not generic under-attention.
- **Layer-localized inference-time fix:** single-layer intervention at the identified bottleneck improves accuracy by an average of +3.2 pp (55.9% → 59.1%) with no training, no data, and no architectural changes.

## Methodology

### Benchmark
- **Source:** TACOS — temporally-aligned audio captions sourced from Freesound (7 super-classes, 59 fine-grained categories, real-world overlapping/intermittent events).
- **Three tasks (4-way multiple-choice, balanced A/B/C/D):**
  - *Earliest Onset (EO, 528):* pick the sound event with the earliest start time.
  - *Latest Offset (LO, 499):* pick the sound event with the latest end time.
  - *Longest Duration (LD, 630):* pick the sound event with the longest duration.
- **Filtering:** correct answer separated from distractors by ≥1 s (EO/LO) or ≥1 s longer (LD). Distractors: same-clip events first, then cross-category samples.
- **Audio-contribution sanity check (silence ablation):** replacing audio with silence drops all models to near-chance (21–32% vs. 25% random), proving answers cannot be inferred from text alone.

### Models evaluated
- Qwen2-Audio-7B-Instruct, Kimi-Audio-7B-Instruct, Audio-Flamingo-3, DeSTA2.5-Audio-Llama-3.1-8B (4-way multiple-choice).
- Mechanistic analysis restricted to **Audio-Flamingo-3** and **DeSTA-2.5-Audio** — only fully open-source LALMs (weights + training code + data), enabling reproducible interventions and avoiding data-driven confounds.

### Behavioral analysis
- Three input conditions: **AQA** (audio only, no caption), **CQA** (caption only), **ACQA** (audio + caption).
- Layer-wise attention distribution between audio vs. text tokens from the final prompt token, averaged across heads and instances.

### Mechanistic interventions
- Applied **only from the final prompt token's query** to **audio-token keys**, uniformly across all heads.
- **Attention Upweighting:** `Ã(ℓ,h)_{n,j} = A(ℓ,h)_{n,j} + α` for `j ∈ I_audio`; otherwise unchanged.
- **Attention Scaling (ScalingVis, from Chen et al. 2025 on VLMs):** `Ã(ℓ,h)_{n,j} = α · A(ℓ,h)_{n,j}` for `j ∈ I_audio`; α>1 sharpens, α<1 smooths.
- **Token position settings:** (1) *Last* — final prompt token only; (2) *Keyword* — task-relevant tokens (e.g., "earliest", "latest", "longest") only; (3) *Kwd+Last* — both.
- **Evaluation:** *fix rate* = % of originally-incorrect predictions corrected after intervention (so improvements on already-correct samples are not double-counted).
- **Layer-localized pass:** sweep each layer individually with the best α per model.

## Results & Highlights

### Behavioral findings (Table 2)
| Model | EO AQA → CQA → ACQA | LO AQA → CQA → ACQA | LD AQA → CQA → ACQA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Qwen2-Audio-7B | 30.9 → 63.6 → 63.6 | 28.1 → 46.5 → 46.5 | 32.5 → 58.9 → 58.9 |
| Kimi-Audio-7B | 22.0 → 61.4 → 68.8 | 24.1 → 56.1 → 62.7 | 25.9 → 55.4 → 67.3 |
| Audio-Flamingo-3 | 25.6 → 71.6 → 67.2 | 20.6 → 62.3 → 63.3 | 28.9 → 66.7 → 66.4 |
| DeSTA2.5-Audio | 24.4 → 68.9 → 62.7 | 25.1 → 59.3 → 59.1 | 22.7 → 66.0 → 66.8 |

- All models score near chance (≤32.5%) in audio-only AQA despite a benchmark engineered to require audio (silence ablation confirms).
- CQA jumps to 46–72% — text alone is shockingly informative because the "weak caption" contains event cues.
- ACQA gains over CQA are **marginal or negative** for three of four models → audio signal is not effectively integrated.

### Mechanistic findings (Table 3)
- **Scaling > Upweighting**, by a large margin:
  - AF3: best scaling α=2.0 → **20.5%** avg fix rate; best upweighting α=0.5 → **15.8%**.
  - DeSTA-2.5-Audio: best scaling α=0.2 → **20.1%**; best upweighting α=0.5 → **10.1%**.
- **Kwd+Last > Last > Keyword** (combining intervention at keyword tokens + last token wins).
- **Architecture-dependent direction:** AF3 wants sharpening (α=2.0, "correct but imprecise" attention), DeSTA wants smoothing (α=0.2, "inherently misaligned" attention).

### Inference-time intervention (Figure 3)
- **All-layer, all-head scaling hurts** (Table 4): baseline accuracies 56–59% drop to 48–57% — too broad an intervention disrupts correct predictions.
- **Single bottleneck-layer scaling helps**:
  - Audio-Flamingo-3 peak at **Layer 20** under α=2.0.
  - DeSTA-2.5-Audio peak at **Layer 9** under α=0.2.
  - Averaged across models, **+3.2 pp absolute** (55.9% → 59.1%) without training/data/architecture changes.

## Critical Assessment

### Novelty
- **Strong.** Causal mechanistic analysis of LALM temporal failures is genuinely new — most prior work (MMAU, MMAU-Pro, MMAR) reports aggregate gaps. Adapting ScalingVis from VLM literature to LALMs and comparing it head-to-head with upweighting is a clean, original contribution.
- The "modality imbalance alone is insufficient" reframing is a useful conceptual clarification.

### Rigor
- **Mostly solid.** 1,657 questions across three carefully controlled tasks; balanced A/B/C/D; silence ablation sanity check; six α values × three token positions × two models = thorough sweep.
- **Reporting gap:** only point estimates (no seed variance / confidence intervals). Single benchmark — no cross-benchmark validation of intervention transfer.
- Fix-rate metric is appropriate (avoids double-counting correct samples) but absolute numbers are modest.

### Reproducibility
- Benchmark built from public TACOS; code/project page promised (`apoorva-ak.github.io/temporal-reasoning-lalms/`).
- Restricted to AF3 + DeSTA-2.5-Audio due to openness — clear and honest constraint, but limits generalizability claims.
- α values, layer choices, and token-position settings are all reported numerically — interventions should be easy to replicate on the same two models.

### Significance
- The diagnostic reframing (attention distribution ≠ attention mass) is the most transferable insight.
- +3.2 pp is **modest but practically interesting** because it requires zero training — relevant for compute-/data-constrained deployment.
- Caveat: improvement is still well below ceiling; not a solution, only a direction.

### Red flags / weak spots
- **Cherry-picking concerns (mild):** only three tasks by design (EO/LO/LD) — narrow scope is justified for mechanistic work, but means the paper cannot claim broad temporal-reasoning coverage. Higher-order temporal reasoning (causal ordering, long-range dependencies, narrative understanding) is untouched.
- **Headline number is small.** 55.9 → 59.1% is below typical "strong result" thresholds and barely above the caption-only CQA numbers (which were 47–72%). The intervention narrows the AQA→ACQA gap but does not close it.
- **All-layer intervention degrades** — the method only works at carefully chosen bottleneck layers. Discovering those layers requires another forward sweep, weakening the "training-free" framing somewhat.
- **Open-models only:** Kimi-Audio and Qwen2-Audio (often the strongest models) are excluded from mechanistic analysis. The mechanistic findings therefore rest on a narrow model subset.
- **Single benchmark** (TACOS-derived). Cross-benchmark transfer not validated.

## Strengths

- Moves from *what* fails (accuracy gap) to *why* it fails (causal, layer-resolved evidence).
- Clean, minimal intervention design (two equations, three token-position settings).
- Architecture-dependent interpretation (sharpening vs. smoothing) is intellectually honest — the authors do not claim a universal fix.
- No fine-tuning → drop-in diagnostic for any white-box LALM.
- Honest limitations section: explicitly does not claim to identify the *complete* causal pathway, only to falsify the "just scale up audio attention" hypothesis.

## Limitations

- Three foundational tasks only — no narrative / causal / long-range temporal reasoning.
- Single benchmark (TACOS-derived); no cross-dataset validation.
- Mechanistic findings tied to two open-source LALMs (AF3, DeSTA-2.5-Audio); behavior on Qwen2-Audio and Kimi-Audio is evaluated behaviorally but not mechanistically (closed weights).
- Requires white-box access to attention weights — does not apply to API-served models.
- +3.2 pp improvement is meaningful but small and requires per-model hyperparameter (α, layer) selection.
- Cannot rule out non-attention causes (e.g., weak audio-encoder representations); the paper is explicit about this.

## Relevance

与用户研究兴趣高度相关。本文把多模态大模型的失败诊断从行为层推进到因果机制层，并通过 upweighting vs. scaling 的对比明确指出"模态不平衡"并非故事的全貌——**音频 token 内部注意力如何分布**同样关键。对关注多模态对齐、可解释性、注意力机制、以及无需重训即可干预模型的实用方法的研究者，是一篇方法论上很有借鉴价值的论文。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the layer-20 (AF3) / layer-9 (DeSTA) bottleneck generalize to other open-source LALMs, or are these architecture-specific artifacts of training data / fusion design?
- Can the **intervention search** itself be automated (e.g., learned α per layer via a small calibration set), so the method does not require a per-model layer sweep?
- Is the "sharpening vs. smoothing" dichotomy (AF3 vs. DeSTA) predictive from a simple property of the model (e.g., audio-token entropy, attention head count, fusion architecture)?
- Do these attention-level interventions compose with **training-time** audio-contribution methods (e.g., audio-contribution-aware post-training) — additive, redundant, or interfering?
- Can the same scaling-vs-upweighting framework be applied to **spatial reasoning** in LALMs (localization, sound-source separation cues) or to **music-structure** reasoning?
- How robust are the gains to noise, reverberation, and overlapping speech — i.e., when audio becomes genuinely hard, does attention redistribution still help?
- Is the CQA ≫ AQA gap an artifact of the weak caption in TACOS, or does it survive on benchmarks without informative captions (e.g., fully synthetic temporal queries)?
