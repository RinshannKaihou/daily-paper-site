> **一句话总结：** 通过推导 flow-matching 模型间 KL 散度与速度场差异的解析关系，提出基于速度场不一致（VFD）的认知不确定性估计器，并以此构建 SAVE 主动多任务微调框架；在 LIBERO-10 上 VFD 校准优于六种基线，SAVE 用至少少 22% 的专家示范即达到 67% 的多任务成功率，并能以 79% TPR 提前检测部署失败。

> **TL;DR:** Flow-matching VLAs are powerful but cannot say when they don't know. This paper turns the velocity field itself into an uncertainty signal: it derives a closed-form link between the KL divergence of two flow-matching policies and the time-weighted L2 gap between their velocity fields along the ODE trajectory, then averages this gap over an ensemble of independently fine-tuned action heads. The resulting **Velocity-Field Disagreement (VFD)** score is a tractable, mathematically-grounded epistemic uncertainty estimator. Wrapping VFD into **SAVE** (uncertainty-guided active multitask fine-tuning with task-level temperature sampling and initial-state argmax selection) yields a 22% sample-efficiency improvement over the strongest UQ-based baseline and 50%/45% gains over random/diversity-based selection, reaching 67% success on LIBERO-10. The same VFD signal also detects deployment failures with 79% TPR using conformal thresholds.

## 核心贡献

- **理论贡献 — Theorem 4.1：** 对 OT-Gaussian 条件路径的 flow-matching 模型，证明两模型生成分布的 KL 散度等于沿 ODE 的速度场差异平方对 $\kappa_s = 1/(1-s)$ 加权的积分（公式 5）。由此将"难算的高维 KL"转化为"沿轨迹采样的速度场差"。
- **VFD 不确定性估计器（公式 7）：** 取 M 个独立微调的 VLA action head，沿 Euler 轨迹计算两两速度场差异平方、按 $\kappa_s$ 加权、在 $N_s$ 步上求和、对外生初始噪声取期望。即上界估计认知不确定性 $I(x,\theta|y,D)$。
- **SAVE 主动多任务微调框架：** 双层采集策略 — 任务级以 $W^{(r)}_k \propto (U^{(r)}_k)^\tau$ 采样（温度 $\tau$ 控制探索/利用），在选中任务内 argmax 选最不确定的初始观测 $o^*_k$，收集专家示范后与预训练数据按 $\lambda$ 比例混合重训。
- **实证贡献：** 在 LIBERO-10 用 SmolVLA backbone 验证，VFD 在 −Spearman 0.71 上显著优于 GU（0.62）、Action-L2（0.50）等六个基线；M=2 即可充分校准；SAVE w/ VFD 最终 SR 67.1%（vs. 随机 54.6%、多样性 54.9%），且部署失败检测 TPR 79%。

## Key Contributions

- **Theoretical grounding (Theorem 4.1):** A closed-form expression for the KL divergence between two flow-matching policies with OT-Gaussian conditional paths, expressed as a time-weighted integral of velocity-field disagreement along the ODE. Removes the need to estimate the divergence of a learned vector field in high dimensions.
- **VFD estimator:** A practical, ensemble-based epistemic uncertainty score (Eq. 7) that upper-bounds the mutual-information form of epistemic uncertainty via pairwise VFD averaged over ODE integration steps and weighted by $\kappa_s = 1/(1-s)$ (later times get more weight).
- **SAVE framework:** Round-based active multitask fine-tuning with (i) task-level categorical sampling using a temperature-softmax over mean task uncertainty, and (ii) initial-state argmax selection within the chosen task; combined with a replay buffer to avoid catastrophic forgetting.
- **Empirical wins on LIBERO-10:** Best calibration (−Spearman 0.71 vs. next-best 0.62), 22% fewer demonstrations than the strongest UQ-based baseline to reach comparable success, 67.1% final success rate, and 79% TPR failure detection with conformal thresholds.

## Methodology

### Theoretical setup

For two flow-matching policies with velocity fields $v^{\theta_1}, v^{\theta_2}$ under OT-Gaussian conditional paths, the paper proves (Appendix A.2):

$$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p^{\theta_1}(\cdot|y) \| p^{\theta_2}(\cdot|y)) = \int_0^1 \kappa_s \mathbb{E}_{x_s \sim p^{\theta_1}_s(\cdot|y)} \| v^{\theta_1}_s(x_s,y) - v^{\theta_2}_s(x_s,y)\|_2^2 \, ds$$

with $\kappa_s = 1/(1-s)$, and decay-at-infinity conditions. Approximating the expectation by Euler integration of one initial sample $x_0 \sim p_0$ and summing over $N_s$ steps yields Eq. (6), which the paper averages pairwise across an ensemble and over $i \neq j$ to get VFD (Eq. 7).

### SAVE active fine-tuning loop

- **VLA ensemble construction.** Take a single pre-trained base VLA $\pi_b$ (SmolVLA in experiments), fine-tune $M$ copies on randomly-shuffled versions of a diverse dataset $D_{\text{pre}}$ to get $\Pi_0 = (\pi_0^{(1)}, \dots, \pi_0^{(M)})$.
- **Per-round uncertainty.** For each candidate initial observation $o_{kl} \in O_k$, compute $u_{kl}^{(r)} = u_e(o_{kl}; \Pi_r)$ via VFD. Aggregate to task level via mean: $U_k^{(r)} = \frac{1}{L}\sum_l u_{kl}^{(r)}$.
- **Task sampling.** Sample task index from $\mathrm{Cat}(W^{(r)})$ with weights $w_k^{(r)} \propto (U_k^{(r)})^\tau$; $\tau = 0$ = uniform, larger $\tau$ concentrates mass on uncertain tasks.
- **Initial state selection.** $o^*_k = \arg\max_{o_{kl} \in O_k} u_{kl}^{(r)}$.
- **Data mixture.** Fine-tune on $\lambda \cdot \mathrm{Unif}(D_{\text{pre}}) + (1-\lambda)\cdot \mathrm{Unif}(D_{\text{new}}^{(\le r)})$.

### Experimental setup

- **Environment.** LIBERO-10 (10 long-horizon tasks on a simulated Franka).
- **Backbone.** SmolVLA (flow-matching action expert on SmolVLM-2); conditions on third-person + wrist image, proprioception, language; action chunks $H=50$, replan every 25 env steps, ODE step $\delta_s = 0.1$.
- **SAVE hyperparams.** $R=15$ rounds, $n_e=5$ queries/round, $\lambda=0.5$, VFD batch $B=5$, 3 seeds, $M=2$ (justified in Fig. 2).
- **Baselines (6).** Action-L2, ACE [58], DECU [9], GU [33], VLM-Entropy, VLM-Perplexity.

## Results & Highlights

### Calibration (Table 1)

Negative Spearman / Pearson between per-task uncertainty and success rate (higher = better calibration):

| Metric | Action-L2 | ACE | DECU | GU | Entropy | Perplexity | **VFD** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| −Spearman | 0.50 | 0.31 | 0.31 | 0.62 | 0.10 | −0.04 | **0.71±0.03** |
| −Pearson | 0.48 | 0.36 | 0.23 | 0.65 | 0.23 | 0.02 | **0.71±0.02** |

- **$M=2$ is sufficient** (Fig. 2): calibration does not improve with more ensemble members.
- **VFD is robust to language-only perturbations** (Fig. 3): when only the prompt is varied (P1→P5, increasing semantic perturbation), VFD tracks the success rate drop monotonically.

### Active fine-tuning sample efficiency (Table 2)

Rounds required to reach success-rate thresholds on LIBERO-10 (lower = better):

| SR threshold | Random | Diversity | SAVE w/ Action-L2 | SAVE w/ GU | **SAVE w/ VFD** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| ≥ 50% | 8.7 | 9.7 | 9.0 | 7.3 | **6.0** |
| ≥ 60% | — | — | — | 12.7 | **10.0** |
| ≥ 65% | — | — | — | — | **12.5** |
| Final SR | 54.6% | 54.9% | 56.8% | 64.0% | **67.1%** |

- SAVE w/ VFD needs **at least 22% fewer demonstrations** than SAVE w/ GU (the next-best UQ baseline) to reach comparable success, **50% fewer** than random, **45% fewer** than diversity-based.
- Ablations (Fig. 4): most of the gain comes from task-level prioritization; initial-state argmax gives a smaller marginal improvement. The framework reduces inter-task uncertainty disparity faster than uniform sampling.

### Failure detection (Fig. 5)

After conformal calibration of task-specific thresholds on 10 successful rollouts each:

| Method | ACE | STAC | RND-OE | **VFD** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Accuracy | ~0.5 | ~0.5 | ~0.5 | **0.67** |
| TPR | ~0.4 | ~0.4 | ~0.4 | **0.79** |

VFD doubles the failure recall of three recent runtime-monitoring baselines.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The theoretical result (Thm 4.1) is a clean, paper-worthy contribution: the link between KL divergence and time-weighted velocity disagreement for OT-Gaussian flow paths is non-obvious and provides a principled foundation. The resulting VFD is a *practical repackaging* of "ensemble disagreement," but with a theoretically motivated time-weighting $\kappa_s$ that emphasizes late-ODF steps (where data dominates over noise). It is more than "Deep Ensembles on diffusion" — DECU and GU are the obvious competitors, and the paper shows a clear margin.
- **Rigor.** Three random seeds, multiple ablations (Fig. 4), calibration on language-only perturbations, M-sweep. Spearman + Pearson as calibration metrics is a defensible choice. Failure detection uses conformal prediction with held-out successes. The empirical story is internally consistent.
- **Reproducibility.** Missing explicit code-release statement, no hyperparameter table for the baselines (only references). SmolVLA + LIBERO-10 is reproducible. Ensemble overhead is at least disclosed qualitatively (M=2). Missing: wall-clock cost comparison, scaling to a different VLA backbone.
- **Significance.** The deployment story is the real win — VFD as a *failure detector* with 79% TPR on flow-based VLAs is genuinely useful for safety gating. The active-learning improvement is moderate (22% over the next-best UQ baseline) but real; the 50% improvement over random is unsurprising (random is a weak baseline for active learning).
- **Red flags.**
  - The KL/velocity-field derivation assumes *decay-at-infinity* (assumption stated in Thm 4.1); in high-dimensional action spaces this is plausible but unverified.
  - The pipeline still requires **training M separate flow heads** for the ensemble. The paper argues M=2 is enough, but each head is a full SmolVLA fine-tune — this is a real cost for deployment.
  - All evaluations are on **a single benchmark (LIBERO-10)**. No real-world experiments, no cross-embodiment, no comparison against a non-flow-matching VLA (e.g., discrete-token VLA) to test whether VFD is specifically flow-aware or just "ensemble disagreement in disguise."
  - Active learning budget: $n_e = 5$ demos × $R = 15$ = 75 demonstrations. Whether the 22% saving holds at much larger budgets is untested.
  - **Diversity baseline is "greedy visually diverse"** (CIFE [61]) — a strong diversity-only baseline is missing (e.g., BADGE, BAIT). A 3–12% final-SR gap over a greedy visual-diversity baseline is fine, but the claim "diversity alone" is underspecified.
  - The conclusion acknowledges two key limitations: (i) ensemble weights are extra cost, (ii) VFD treats uncertainty as the sole signal and ignores cross-task information dependencies (a real theoretical gap left to future work via expected information gain).

## Strengths

- **Theoretical foundation.** The KL/velocity link is the paper's intellectual centerpiece and is published (likely) in full in Appendix A.2.
- **Practical signal.** VFD is computationally tractable (M=2, B=5, modest ODE integration) and directly usable for safety gating at deployment.
- **Two use cases, one estimator.** Failure detection and active learning are both improved by the *same* VFD signal — the framework is internally coherent.
- **Comprehensive baselines.** Six UQ methods + Random + Diversity + task-level ablations + temperature ablations.
- **Honest limitations section.** Authors explicitly call out the ensemble overhead, the "looks easy / is hard" failure mode, and the open question of cross-task information gain.

## Limitations

- **Single benchmark (LIBERO-10), no real-robot experiments.**
- **Ensemble overhead** — two full SmolVLA heads is a real cost, especially on edge hardware.
- **Time-weighting $\kappa_s = 1/(1-s)$** blows up near $s=1$; in practice, this is regularized by the step grid, but the paper does not ablate different weightings.
- **Decay-at-infinity assumption** is not empirically validated.
- **No information-gain–based acquisition**; the paper restricts to VFD-driven myopic selection and explicitly defers expected-information-gain to future work.
- **Calibration results are task-aggregated.** Per-task calibration curves or reliability diagrams would be more diagnostic than a single Spearman number.
- **No code-release promise** in the main text; the project page exists but the artifact is not linked from the abstract.

## Relevance

本文与本人关注的**具身智能/机器人基础模型**与**主动学习/不确定性**方向高度契合：它把 flow-matching VLA 的安全性问题（缺乏置信度信号）与样本效率问题（专家示范昂贵）用一个理论上有根据、计算上轻量的 VFD 度量统一解决，并产出可立即用于安全门控的失败检测器；对正在部署 VLA 的研究者和工程团队都有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does VFD behave when the ensemble is *not* fine-tuned from the same pre-trained checkpoint — e.g., last-layer-only fine-tunes, or LoRA-perturbed ensembles — to reduce the cost of maintaining two full VLA heads?
- Does VFD remain calibrated when transferring from LIBERO-10 simulation to a real manipulation platform with realistic noise (lighting, actuator jitter, camera latency)?
- Can the time-weighting $\kappa_s$ be replaced by a learned, data-dependent weight without sacrificing calibration? A simple ablation of $\kappa_s \in \{1, (1-s)^{-1}, (1-s)^{-2}\}$ would be informative.
- How does VFD compare to **single-pass heteroscedastic** uncertainty (e.g., training the flow head to output a Gaussian conditional rather than a point velocity), which would eliminate the ensemble overhead?
- Could cross-task information dependencies be incorporated cheaply — e.g., via a low-rank covariance between per-task VFD signals — without resorting to full expected-information-gain estimation?
- Is VFD sensitive to the **number of ODE steps** $N_s$ used to approximate the integral? The paper uses $\delta_s = 0.1$ but does not ablate.
- How does VFD interact with model-predictive control or action-chunking schedules — does it still work when the same observation is re-evaluated at every replan step?
