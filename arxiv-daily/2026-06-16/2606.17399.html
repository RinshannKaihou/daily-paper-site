The Discrete-Log Clock: How a Transformer Learns Modular Multiplication

Huu Danh Nguyen Stanford — single-author, workshop track

mechanistic interpretability modular arithmetic grokking Fourier analysis group theory

The "dense" Fourier spectrum of a grokked a·b mod 113 transformer is a basis-mismatch artifact — the multiplicative character basis (rows reordered by discrete log) reveals a sparse 4-frequency clock.

"Density" was the wrong story. Use the Fourier basis of the group the task actually lives in. 把分析基底换成乘法群 $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^*$ 的特征变换——也就是先按离散对数重排嵌入行、再做加法 DFT——稠密频谱立刻塌缩成 4 个关键频率。

Embedding spectrum becomes ~8× more concentrated; neuron level: 0 → 96.9% mono-semantic.

Why This Matters

Resolves a misleading "dense vs. sparse" debate in mechanistic interpretability of modular-multiplication transformers — and supplies a reusable recipe: for a task with group structure $G$, analyze it in the Fourier basis of $G$. A clean methodological lesson that generalizes beyond modular arithmetic.

为"神经网络是否学到真实算法"以及"如何为给定任务选择合适的可解释性基底"提供清晰案例与可复用工具。

Critical Edge

The Discrete-Log Clock is described as "approximately" what the model implements — no causal-intervention experiments (ablation, activation patching on the 496 identified neurons) confirm the proposed circuit is necessary and sufficient.

Also: mechanism appears in ~80% of seeds — the other 20% are uncharacterized; key-frequency set {2, 8, 47, 56} has no in-paper algebraic explanation; exponentiation result is preliminary.

Methodology 方法

1-layer decoder-only transformer trained to grok a·b mod 113 — architecture & hyperparameters exactly match Nanda et al. (2023), only the task differs.

  • Arch: $d_\text{model}=128$, 4 heads ($d_\text{head}=32$), MLP 512 (ReLU), no LayerNorm. Input [a, b, =], predict at position 2.
  • Train: AdamW, lr $10^{-3}$, wd 1.0, 40K epochs, full-batch GD, 30% train split (3,763 / 12,544). Grokking ≈ epoch 9K–14K.
  • Algebra: 113 prime → $(\mathbb{Z}/113\mathbb{Z})^*$ cyclic of order 112, primitive root $g=3$. Discrete log $\alpha=\log_g(a)$ is a group iso to $\mathbb{Z}/112\mathbb{Z}$.
  • Two bases compared: additive DFT $\sin(2\pi k a / 112)$ vs. multiplicative character $\sin(2\pi k \log_g(a) / 112)$ — the latter is operationally "reorder rows by discrete log, then take the standard DFT."
  • Analysis: project $W_E$ onto both bases → Gini / IPR / "key frequency" (norm > 5× median). Per-neuron 2D-DFT of activations; SVD of $W_E$ with components reordered by $\log_g$.

Algorithm sketch (Discrete-Log Clock): embed $a$ as $\sin(\omega_k \alpha)$ at $k\in K$ → attention routes both $a,b$ Fourier components to = → MLP applies $\cos k(\alpha+\beta) = \cos k\alpha \cos k\beta - \sin k\alpha \sin k\beta$ → unembed $c$ with $\cos \omega_k \gamma$, $\gamma=\log_g c$ → dot product gives constructive interference only at $\gamma = (\alpha+\beta) \bmod 112$.

Robustness: 10 primes (p = 59–113), 4–7 key freqs, Gini 0.45–0.61; mechanism in ~80% of seeds on p=113. Exponentiation: preliminary — 2-layer transformer hits 100% on $a^b \bmod 41$ (mechanism TBD).

Strengths & Limitations 优劣

Strengths

  • Single, sharp thesis: basis mismatch → wrong story about density.
  • Quantitative, falsifiable metrics (Gini, IPR, mono-freq fraction, SVD) — not vibes.
  • Step-by-step circuit derivation in the appendix, with explicit constructive-interference argument.
  • Robustness across 10 primes and many seeds; explicit comparison to prior "wrong-basis" claims (Furuta, Doshi).
  • Code released; architecture & hyperparameters directly comparable to Nanda et al.

Limitations

  • "Approximately implements" — no causal-intervention (ablation / activation patching) on the 496 neurons.
  • 20% of seeds don't show the mechanism; alternative solutions not characterized (no "Pizza"-style catalog).
  • No theoretical story for why $K = \{2, 8, 47, 56\}$ in particular — no discussion of effective dimension or frequency interactions.
  • Requires $p$ prime (so $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^*$ is cyclic) — composite moduli with non-cyclic multiplicative groups are unaddressed.
  • Exponentiation extension is preliminary; mechanism "under active investigation."
  • Single author, workshop track; would benefit from multi-seed statistics and independent reproduction.

Questions for Further Exploration 待探索

  • Do causal interventions on the 496 mono-frequency neurons confirm they are necessary and sufficient for the Discrete-Log Clock behavior?
  • What do the ~20% of seeds that don't show the mechanism learn? Is there a "Pizza"-style alternative for multiplication?
  • Why exactly $\{2, 8, 47, 56\}$? Is there a number-theoretic reading — e.g. via $p-1 = 112 = 2^4 \cdot 7$?
  • Does the basis-mismatch story extend to composite $n$, where $(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^*$ is non-cyclic and the discrete-log map is not a single bijection?
  • For the preliminary $a^b \bmod p$ result — does the algorithm iterate the Clock at each exponent time-step, or use a fundamentally different circuit?
  • Beyond modular arithmetic: where else does "use the right group's Fourier basis" expose hidden structure (permutation composition, modular exponentiation, graph-isomorphism tasks)?
TL;DR. A 1-layer transformer that grokks a·b mod 113 was reported to use a "dense" Fourier spectrum. The paper shows that's an additive-DFT artifact — switching to the multiplicative character basis (reorder rows by discrete log, then DFT) collapses the spectrum to 4 key frequencies and reveals 96.9% mono-frequency MLP neurons. A "Discrete-Log Clock" algorithm emerges: embed $a$ as $\sin(\omega_k \log_g a)$, attention routes, MLP applies the trig addition identity in log-space, dot-product with the unembed column gives constructive interference only at $c \equiv ab \pmod p$. Robust across 10 primes, ~80% of seeds; preliminary 100% on $a^b \bmod 41$.
