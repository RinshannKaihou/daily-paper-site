> **一句话总结：** 通过 2×2 因子对比（CNN vs ViT 编码器 × LSTM vs Transformer 解码器）及两个大型 VLM（Molmo 7B-D、Qwen3.5 9B），本文发现 LSTM 解码器比 Transformer 解码器在与人眼注视对齐上高出 25–45 个百分点（达到人类噪声天花板的 80–87%），但这种对齐以空间弥散、任务无关为代价；而 CNN-Transformer 注意力图虽在注视对齐上最弱，却能最好地预测 TRIBE 合成神经响应——揭示了"行为对齐"与"神经可预测性"的解耦。

> **TL;DR:** A clean 2×2 ablation (CNN-LSTM, CNN-Transformer, ViT-LSTM, ViT-Transformer) plus Molmo 7B-D and Qwen3.5 9B reveals that **decoder architecture dominates** spatial attention alignment with humans. LSTM decoders reach 80–87% of the human noise ceiling (CNN-LSTM peaks at 85.3–86.6%); Transformer decoders lag at 39.5–58.5% — a 25–45 pp gap far exceeding the CNN-vs-ViT encoder's 3–7 pp effect. Molmo lands in between (76–82%), Qwen3.5 9B is weaker (53–69%). Yet the trade-off is stark: LSTM attention maps are nearly maximally diffuse (Shannon H ≈ 0.999, ~195 effective locations) and barely task-differentiated; ViT-Transformer maps are sharply concentrated (H = 0.84–0.94) and the most task-adaptive. A hemispatial-neglect ablation confirms that distributed architectures (ViT-Transformer) are most lesion-resilient. An exploratory TRIBE neural-encoding extension shows the sharpest dissociation: **CNN-Transformer attention best predicts synthetic brain activity** despite ranking third in fixation alignment, and uniquely-spatial signal concentrates in **early visual cortex and dorsal attention regions** (FEF, DLPFC), while CLIP-unique semantic signal lands in language/ventral regions (STS, IFG, angular gyrus). Take-home: top-down (decoder) and bottom-up (encoder) components **trade off** what they predict in behavior vs. brain.

## 核心贡献

- **架构贡献的因式分解**：在控制训练分布的条件下，通过 2×2 设计（ResNet-101 / ViT-B/16 × LSTM+Bahdanau / 4层Transformer）独立估计了"自底向上"（编码器）和"自顶向下"（解码器注意力机制）对人类注意力对齐的贡献。
- **解码器主导现象**：LSTM 解码器（加性 soft 注意力）在注视对齐上稳定优于 Transformer 解码器（多头 cross-attention），差距 25–45 个百分点；CNN 编码器相对 ViT 仅带来 3–7 pp 增益。CNN-LSTM 在 6 个模型中表现最佳（85.3% / 86.6%）。
- **对齐-质量权衡**：LSTM 注意力图几乎最大程度弥散（Shannon H ≈ 0.999），任务间差异度甚至**低于**人类参考（M = 0.745–0.767 vs. 人类参考 0.247）；ViT-Transformer 注意力图最聚焦且任务分化最强（M = 0.383），却最不像人眼。
- **半侧空间忽视模拟**：通过从 7% 到 100% 渐进遮蔽左半空间注意力列，发现分布式架构（ViT-Transformer）对损伤最鲁棒；CNN-Transformer 在 100% 遮蔽时部分恢复，而两种 LSTM 架构退化最严重。
- **行为-神经解耦（TRIBE 扩展）**：用 TRIBE v2 合成 fMRI 响应做带状岭回归编码模型，发现注视对齐最佳的 CNN-LSTM 反而不是神经预测力最强的——CNN-Transformer 注意力图在 Social 任务上脑均值 R² 最高；"where" 信号主要落在早期视觉皮层（枕极、下枕叶皮层）和背侧注意网络（FEF、DLPFC），"what"（CLIP）信号集中在腹侧语言区（STS、IFG、角回），二者共享成分覆盖梭状回、外侧枕颞皮层。
- **挑战 what/where 二分法**：传统上认为腹侧流（"what"）和背侧流（"where"）分工明确，但实验发现梭状回、外侧枕颞皮层、FEF 等所谓"what"/"where"区域在共享成分中被解释——支持"两通路存在更多重叠"的近期观点。

## Key Contributions

- **Disentangled architectural ablation**: 2×2 factorial (ResNet-101 / ViT-B/16 × LSTM+Bahdanau / 4-layer Transformer) trained on identical 3-phase curriculum, isolating bottom-up (encoder) and top-down (decoder attention) contributions while controlling for training distribution.
- **Decoder-dominance finding**: LSTM decoders reach 80–87% of the human noise ceiling (CNN-LSTM peaks at 85.3% Social / 86.6% Describe), Transformer decoders reach 39.5–58.5% — a 25–45 pp gap. CNN-encoders beat ViT-encoders by only 3–7 pp. SOTA VLMs (Molmo: 76–82%; Qwen3.5 9B: 53–69%) sit between the two extremes, with no SOTA model matching the best scratch-trained model.
- **Alignment-quality trade-off**: LSTM-decoder attention maps have Shannon H ≈ 0.999 (~195 effective locations, near uniform), with task-differentiation M = 0.745–0.767 — *less* than human reference (M = 0.247). ViT-Transformer attention has H = 0.84–0.94, task-differentiation M = 0.383 — the most task-adaptive of all six models.
- **Hemispatial-neglect simulation**: Progressive 7%–100% left-side attention ablation shows ViT-Transformer (most distributed) is most lesion-resilient; CNN-Transformer partially recovers at 100% ablation; both LSTM variants degrade most.
- **TRIBE neural-encoding dissociation**: Banded ridge regression (attention + CLIP-ViT-L/14 → 20,484 fsaverage5 vertices) shows CNN-Transformer attention has highest whole-brain R² on the Social task despite 3rd-place fixation alignment. Unique "where" signal concentrates in early visual cortex + dorsal attention network; unique "what" (CLIP) signal concentrates in STS, IFG, angular gyrus. Shared signal includes fusiform, LOTC, FEF, superior parietal lobule.
- **Where/what challenge**: Results suggest more overlap between ventral "what" and dorsal "where" streams than classical dual-pathway accounts assume.

## Methodology

- **Human eye-tracking**: 49 participants (27 Social, 22 Describe) viewed 200 naturalistic COCO 2017 images (Karpathy split, each with ≥1 visible person) on an SR Research EyeLink 1000 Plus (2000 Hz).
- **Two tasks**:
  - *Social*: select a person and verbally describe where they are paying attention (e.g., "The man is paying attention to the red truck").
  - *Describe*: describe the overall scene content.
- **Heatmap pipeline**: Gaze samples convolved with Gaussian kernel (σ = 33.3 px, width 200 px), weighted by fixation duration, downsampled to 256×256.
- **6 models in 2×2 design**:
  - **CNN-LSTM**: ResNet-101 (ImageNet pretrained) + 1-layer LSTM with Bahdanau additive attention over 14×14 = 196 spatial positions.
  - **CNN-Transformer**: Same ResNet-101 + Linear(2048→512) projection + 4-layer Transformer decoder (8 heads, dmodel = 512, FFN = 2048) with multi-head cross-attention.
  - **ViT-LSTM**: ViT-B/16 (ImageNet pretrained) → 196 patch tokens (768-d → 512-d) + same LSTM+Bahdanau decoder.
  - **ViT-Transformer**: ViT-B/16 + 4-layer Transformer decoder.
  - **Molmo 7B-D** and **Qwen3.5 9B** as SOTA references (no fine-tuning; selected layer–head: Molmo L9H24, Qwen3.5 9B L15H12).
- **3-phase training curriculum** (matched across 4 trained models):
  1. **Describe Pretrain** (encoder frozen): COCO 2014 train captions (~414k pairs).
  2. **Describe Finetune** (encoder unfrozen): best Phase 1 checkpoint; unfreeze ResNet res3–res5 or ViT layers 6–11.
  3. **Social Finetune**: lab-collected dataset (17 participants × 200 unique images = ~3,400 image–caption pairs).
  - Adam, lr 4e-4 decoder / 1e-4 encoder, dropout 0.5, batch 32, beam search k=5.
- **Attention extraction**:
  - LSTM: average Bahdanau αt over all decoding steps → 196-d vector → reshape to 14×14 → bicubic upsample to 256×256.
  - Transformer: extract cross-attention from all layers/heads, select head with highest Social-task Spearman r (CNN-Transformer: L3H5; ViT-Transformer: L3H8 — *note: this creates task-circularity*, see Limitations).
  - SOTA VLMs: same per-image averaging; best layer–head from grid search.
- **Spatial alignment metric**: Fisher-z transformed Spearman r, averaged across images per subject, back-transformed. Reported as % of human-human noise ceiling (Social M = 0.284; Describe M = 0.262).
- **Spatial concentration metric**: Normalized Shannon entropy over 14×14 attention (H = 1 = uniform; H = 0 = single peak).
- **Task differentiation**: Spearman r between model's Social- and Describe-task attention maps for the same image (lower = more task-adaptive; human reference M = 0.247).
- **Hemispatial-neglect simulation**: Progressively ablate 1–14 leftmost columns of the 14×14 attention grid (7%–100%). For LSTM: post-softmax mask with floor 10⁻³ then renormalize. For Transformer: set cross-attention logits to −∞ before softmax across all decoder layers. Measure caption cosine similarity (paraphrase-MiniLM-L6-v2) vs. unablated baseline.
- **TRIBE neural encoding**: TRIBE v2 foundation model simulates fMRI BOLD responses on fsaverage5 surface (20,484 vertices) for 200 images. Banded ridge regression with two feature bands: (1) 196-d model attention map (where), (2) CLIP ViT-L/14 768-d embeddings (what). 5-fold CV (160 train / 40 test), z-scored within fold, nested 4-fold CV for ridge α over log-spaced grid 10¹–10¹⁰. Variance partitioning via 200-vertex permutations with FDR correction (Benjamini–Hochberg, q < 0.05).

## Results & Highlights

### Decoder-architecture dominance (Figure 1A–B)

| Model | Social (% ceiling) | Describe (% ceiling) |
|---|---|---|
| **CNN-LSTM** | **85.3%** | **86.6%** |
| CNN-Transformer (L3H5) | 58.5% | 58.5% |
| ViT-LSTM | 79.7% | 80.3% |
| ViT-Transformer (L3H8) | 39.5% | 52.4% |
| Molmo 7B-D | 76% | 82% |
| Qwen3.5 9B | 68.6% | 53.5% |
| Human-human ceiling | 100% (M = 0.284) | 100% (M = 0.262) |

- LSTM > Transformer by 25–45 pp; CNN > ViT by 3–7 pp within decoder family.
- Molmo outperforms both Transformer-decoder models; Qwen3.5 9B lands between them.
- No SOTA model beats the scratch-trained CNN-LSTM.

### Spatial concentration (Shannon entropy)

- **Human fixations**: H = 0.552 (Social) / 0.615 (Describe) → ~18–26 effective locations.
- **LSTM-decoder**: H ≈ 0.999 → ~195 effective locations (near uniform).
- **Transformer-decoder**: H = 0.840–0.944 → 84–146 effective locations.
- **SOTA VLMs**: H = 0.863–0.943 → 95–145 effective locations.

### Task adaptation

| Model | Between-task r (lower = more task-adaptive) |
|---|---|
| ViT-Transformer (L3H8) | **0.383** (most adaptive) |
| CNN-Transformer (L3H5) | 0.633 |
| Human reference | 0.247 |
| CNN-LSTM | 0.745 |
| ViT-LSTM | 0.767 |
| Qwen3.5 9B | 0.686 |
| Molmo 7B-D | 0.863 (least adaptive) |

- LSTM-decoder models are *less* task-adaptive than humans; Molmo is even less so.
- ViT-Transformer is the only architecture showing meaningful task-differentiation (though still less than humans).

### Hemispatial neglect (Figure 3)

- All models degrade as left-side attention is progressively ablated.
- **ViT-Transformer** (most distributed): most resilient, smallest caption drift.
- **CNN-Transformer**: partially recovers at 100% ablation (when all patches get equal weight, the multi-head cross-attention becomes a uniform weighting → graceful fallback).
- **LSTM variants**: most degraded; no fallback mechanism once the bottleneck attention is forced uniform.

### Neural encoding (TRIBE, Figure 4)

- **CNN-Transformer has highest whole-brain mean R²** on Social task (and 2nd-highest on Describe), matching or approaching averaged human fixation maps — *despite* ranking 3rd in fixation alignment.
- LSTM-decoder models are best at gaze alignment but **not** the best neural predictors.
- **Variance partitioning (CNN-Transformer, Social task)**:
  - Unique "where" (attention): R² = 0.009 whole-brain mean, concentrated in **occipital pole, inferior occipital cortex**, **right precentral sulcus (FEF)**, **right inferior frontal sulcus**, **bilateral middle frontal gyrus (DLPFC)**.
  - Unique "what" (CLIP): R² = 0.21, dominant signal in **superior temporal sulcus, lateral superior temporal gyrus, middle temporal gyrus, inferior frontal gyrus, angular gyrus**.
  - Shared: R² = 0.09, in **fusiform gyrus, lateral occipito-temporal cortex, FEF, superior parietal gyrus**.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The 2×2 disentanglement and the explicit "trade-off" framing are the strongest contributions. The LSTM-vs-Transformer attention-alignment inversion is genuinely counterintuitive and the cleanest demonstration in the literature. However, the broader pattern that "top-down decoder shapes where models look" was foreshadowed by Show-Attend-Tell (Xu et al. 2015) and follow-up work; the new contribution is the *magnitude* and the *mechanistic interpretation*.
- **Rigor**: Strong overall. Three cautions:
  1. **Circular head selection**: Transformer heads are chosen by Social-task fixation alignment and then evaluated on the same task. This may inflate Social-task scores for the Transformer variants. The Describe-task numbers are more credible.
  2. **Caption-quality confound check is narrow**: Caption cosine similarity spread is <0.03 Social, <0.05 Describe — adequate but not exhaustive (no BLEU, CIDEr, etc. reported in main text).
  3. **TRIBE is a model of a model**: The neural-encoding findings are inferences about a synthetic brain foundation model's outputs, not real neural data. The authors label this "exploratory," which is appropriate.
- **Reproducibility**: 200 images × 49 participants × 6 models × 2 tasks is a tractable setup; the noise-ceiling normalization, attention extraction, and banded ridge pipeline are described in sufficient detail. **No code link is mentioned in the main text** — a real gap for a paper that hinges on attention-extraction implementation choices.
- **Significance**: Important for the VLM-cognition alignment community. It complicates the "Transformer attention is the human-like one" narrative and provides a concrete architectural recipe (LSTM with additive attention) for behavioral alignment, while flagging that the right choice depends on whether you want behavior-likeness or brain-likeness. Practically relevant for interpretability, saliency-model design, and VLM evaluation benchmarks.
- **Red flags / open questions**:
  - The 4 controlled models are bespoke and small (~512-d decoder); external validity to modern frontier LLMs (which have 100B+ parameters and very different attention-pooling strategies) is unclear. Molmo and Qwen3.5 9B are added as references but the head-selection protocol there is also post-hoc.
  - The LSTM-decoder advantage could be a property of **additive soft attention as a bottleneck**, not of the LSTM family per se. A control with Transformer + additive (single-head) attention would isolate the bottleneck effect.
  - The 200-image stimulus set, while COCO-naturalistic, is modest. Whether the LSTM advantage generalizes to non-COCO content (medical images, satellite imagery, abstract art) is untested.
  - "Human reference" between-task r is M = 0.247, *higher* than ViT-Transformer's 0.383, meaning even the most task-adaptive model is *less* adaptive than humans — yet the Discussion frames ViT-Transformer as the more "adaptive" architecture. This nuance deserves more space.
  - The "where" signal's low whole-brain R² (0.009) is striking — is attention map as a feature simply a weak predictor? The paper interprets the localization (early visual + dorsal attention) as substantive, but the effect size is small.

## Strengths

- Cleanest 2×2 disentanglement of encoder vs decoder contributions in VLM-human alignment to date.
- Combined behavioral (gaze, task adaptation) + causal (lesion) + neural (TRIBE) evaluation.
- Counterintuitive main result (LSTM > Transformer for human alignment) that complicates the prevailing "Transformer is human-like" narrative.
- Honest framing of the TRIBE extension as exploratory.
- Comprehensive appendix (training details, social dataset, attention extraction, metrics, lesion protocol, caption quality check).
- Includes both controlled small models and frontier models for external validity.

## Limitations

- **Circular head selection** for Transformer models (chosen by Social-task alignment, evaluated on same task) may inflate Social-task scores.
- **No code release** mentioned in the main text; attention-extraction details (especially head selection, post-processing) are implementation-sensitive.
- **Small stimulus set** (200 COCO images); cross-domain generalization untested.
- **TRIBE is synthetic**: neural encoding findings are several inferential steps removed from real fMRI/MEG data and should not be over-interpreted.
- **Bespoke controlled models** have ~512-d decoders, far smaller than the SOTA frontier models; the architectural finding may not scale.
- LSTM-vs-Transformer confound: the effect may be due to **additive soft attention as a bottleneck** rather than the LSTM family per se.
- "Where" signal's whole-brain R² = 0.009 is small — the localization is interpretable but the magnitude is modest.
- The encoder unfreezing strategy differs slightly between ResNet (top layers) and ViT (top 6 blocks), introducing a minor confound when comparing encoders.
- Molmo and Qwen3.5 9B were **not fine-tuned** on the lab-collected Social dataset, which is acknowledged but may understate their achievable alignment.

## Relevance

与"AI 认知对齐 / 类人视觉注意力建模 / VLM 可解释性"方向高度相关。该工作为构建与人类认知对齐的 VLM 提供了具体的架构选型证据（soft 注意力 + LSTM 解码器在行为对齐上更强），同时通过 TRIBE 扩展揭示了"行为对齐"与"神经可预测性"的结构性解耦——对关注 VLM interpretability、cognitive alignment、brain-inspired AI 的研究者具有直接参考价值；也提示在评估"类人 VLM"时需要明确是追求注视模式相似还是脑活动模式相似。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the LSTM-decoder advantage persist when using **Transformer decoders with explicit single-head additive cross-attention** (isolating the bottleneck effect from the recurrent vs parallel distinction)?
- How does attention alignment scale with **model size and pretraining data scale** — is the LSTM advantage preserved at >10B parameters, or is it a small-model artifact?
- Can the **alignment-quality trade-off be broken** by adding auxiliary spatial-supervision losses (e.g., fixation-prediction heads) on top of Transformer cross-attention?
- Does the behavior-vs-neural dissociation **replicate with real fMRI/EEG data** rather than TRIBE synthetic responses — and if so, does the "where" signal in early visual cortex become stronger?
- Are the findings driven by **pretraining corpus composition** (e.g., caption-heavy vs VQA-heavy), and would domain-adapted Transformers close the alignment gap?
- The fusiform gyrus, lateral occipito-temporal cortex, and FEF all showed strong **shared variance** between "what" and "where" — what does this imply for the classical dual-pathway model, and does it replicate across individuals with different lateralization?
- Could the **Q-formalism of attention schema theory** (Graziano's framework) provide a theoretical account of *why* LSTM-with-bottleneck attention better matches human gaze distribution?
