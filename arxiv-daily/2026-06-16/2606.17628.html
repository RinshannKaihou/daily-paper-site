Key Finding

A 9B model beats 196B Step-3.5-Flash on 9 of 10 lifelong-agent subsets — and challenges Qwen3.5-397B-A17B on 6 of 10 — by distilling its own policy with privileged hindsight.

OPD-Evolver: Cultivating Holistic Agent Evolver via On-Policy Distillation ↗

Guibin Zhang · Xun Xu · Yanwei Yue · Zikun Su · Wangchunshu Zhou · Xiaobin Hu · Shuicheng Yan

arXiv:2606.17628  ·  2026-06-16

self-evolving-agents memory-hierarchy on-policy-distillation experience-lifecycle credit-assignment
Key Metric
9 / 10
subsets won by 9B OPD-Evolver against 196B Step-3.5-Flash — a 22× smaller model. The only loss is LifelongAgentBench-DB by 1.5 points.
Why This Matters
Reframes memory-augmented agents from passive experience stores into active agent evolvers jointly mastering selection, execution, writing, and management. The candidate-controlled attribution A(m) is a reusable credit-assignment primitive for any lifelong-learning stack.
Critical Edge
No test-time OPD baseline — i.e., the same 9B policy given V(m) labels online at inference is never compared, so whether the distillation gain survives deployment is left unproven.

Methodology

Fast loop. Online interaction with a four-tier memory hierarchy — trajectory (episodes), tip (local warnings), skill (procedures), tool (executable code). Every Q=30 tasks, a repository-maintenance pass runs {lookup, merge, delete}.

Slow loop. Convert sparse delayed rewards into dense per-memory labels via outcome-calibrated attribution: compare return when m is retrieved-and-selected vs retrieved-and-not-selected — candidate-controlled, not confounded with retrieval.

Distillation. Teacher = student policy (stop-gradient) given privileged hindsight hk (attribution scores, successful trajectory, future memory utility). Token-level KL over student-on-policy rollouts — no external stronger model.

Results & Highlights

  • vs. Step-3.5-Flash (196B): 9/10 subsets won; only DB lost (84.5 vs 86.0).
  • vs. Qwen3.5-397B-A17B: 6/10 won (OS, Math, Physics, SA, CTF, SQL). Lost on DB, CI, SU, Bash — gaps of 5–10 points on AMA-CI and AMA-SU are the honest counter-evidence.
  • vs. 9B MemEvolve (strongest baseline): wins all 10 subsets, by 2.0–6.6 points.
  • Ablation (InterCode, 4B): removing memory attribution drops avg 38.67 → 32.13 (−6.5); removing slow-loop entirely drops avg to 33.10 (−5.6). All four trained views contribute.
  • Internalization: with test-time memory removed, OPD-Evolver-9B is still +3–7 points over the vanilla backbone and uses up to 2.5 fewer steps — experience is absorbed into the policy, not just retrieved.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean experimental design — teacher = student with richer context, removing model-strength confounds.
  • Candidate-controlled attribution is a model-agnostic credit-assignment primitive that generalizes beyond this work.
  • Unusual empirical breadth — 10 subsets, 5-variant ablation, internalization experiment, case study.
  • Dominant on memory-heavy tasks — MemoryArena Math/Physics 10.88/11.63 vs everyone else < 5.

Limitations

  • No test-time OPD baseline — privileged 9B teacher at inference never compared, so the deployment story is unproven.
  • Slow-loop compute cost unreported — likely substantial; a 9B teacher queried on every student prefix.
  • "Self-evolving" framing partially undermined — the agent does not actually become more capable of finding good hindsight autonomously.
  • AMA-CI / AMA-SU losses (5–10 pts) to 397B obscured by the "challenges 397B" headline.
  • Hand-designed 4-tier memory schema — no experiment on whether the agent could learn the tier structure itself.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How would a test-time OPD baseline (9B + online V(m) labels) compare? If it matches OPD-Evolver-9B, the gain is amortization, not capability.
  2. What if the student is larger than the privileged teacher (e.g. 30B student / 9B teacher) — does the gap close or reverse?
  3. Could the tier structure itself be learned via clustering of memory embeddings, rather than hand-defined as {traj, tip, skill, tool}?
  4. Are the AMA-CI / AMA-SU losses a scale effect or a distillation-induced precision loss the 9B student cannot recover?
  5. How sensitive is the gain to K (retrieval depth) and Q (maintenance period), both currently magic-number-ish?
  6. Could the tier prior αℓ(m) and confidence γ(m) in the attribution formula be learned end-to-end?
