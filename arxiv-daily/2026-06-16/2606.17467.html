PARSE: Provenance-Aware Retrieval Sanitization for Professional Domain LLM Agents

["Aaditya Pai"]

arXiv: 2606.17467

Date: 2026-06-16

Tags: ["prompt-injection", "rag-security", "sanitization", "enterprise-llm", "domain-camouflage"]

https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.17467

一句话总结： 在 122 个真实企业文档任务（SEC 10-K、Federal Register、PubMed、arXiv、GitHub postmortems）上证明业界最强的转述防御无显著效果（p=0.500、utility 91.8%→82.8%），并提出 PARSE 六阶段流水线（域分类 + 指令性门 + 句子级注入打分 + 结构化事实预提取 + 事实一致性校验循环），将 ASR 从 25.4% 降至 15.6%、utility 维持 86.9%（p=0.014、h=−0.245、统计功效充足）。
TL;DR: Synthetic prompt-injection benchmarks dramatically overstate the value of strong defenses like paraphrasing. On a 122-task real-document benchmark across financial, legal, medical, scientific, and DevOps domains (built from SEC 10-K MD&A, Federal Register, PubMed, arXiv, GitHub postmortems), paraphrasing shows no significant ASR reduction (p=0.500; utility drops 91.8%→82.8%). The proposed PARSE pipeline — a Haiku domain classifier + a parallel directiveness gate + a combined Haiku tagger-extractor + a Sonnet structure-aware paraphraser + a Haiku consistency checker with one retry on failure — uses a directiveness threshold (≥0.5) to route 59% of real documents to simple paraphrasing and the remaining 41% through the full pipeline. PARSE achieves the lowest ASR (15.6%) at near-baseline utility (86.9%), with the largest effect size of all conditions (h=−0.245, n=122 > n_min=103). Llama Guard reaches similar ASR (18.9%, survives Bonferroni at p=0.004) but at a 64.8% utility — a 27 pp cost. Two ablations (parse_fast, parse_domain_conditional) confirm the multi-step structure and directiveness (rather than domain) routing are necessary.

核心贡献

  • 实证揭示合成基准与企业真实文档之间的泛化鸿沟：在 122 个真实文档任务上，合成基准上的"最强防御"——转述——无显著效果（p=0.500、h=−0.019，验证此效应需 n=17,253），并将效用从 91.8% 降至 82.8%。
  • 提出 PARSE 六阶段推理时流水线：(1) Haiku 域分类、(1.5) 并行的指令性门、(2+3) 合并的 Haiku 句子打标器-事实提取器、(4) Sonnet 结构感知转述器、(5) Haiku 一致性校验器（失败则重试一次）、(6) 输出构建器（含溯源 trace）。
  • 引入指令性门（directiveness gate）：用 Haiku 对完整文档打 0–1 指令性分数，≥0.5 走全流程，<0.5 走轻量转述；真实企业文档中 59% 走轻量路径，把算力集中在高风险的 41% 上。
  • 在 5 个领域、122 个真实任务、8 种条件下做了带统计功效分析的系统对比；唯一一个同时满足 ASR 显著下降（p=0.014、h=−0.245、功效充足）与近基线 utility 的方案。

Key Contributions

  • A 122-task real-document benchmark spanning 5 professional domains (financial SEC 10-K MD&A, legal Federal Register final rules, medical PubMed RCT abstracts, scientific arXiv cs.AI/LG/CL abstracts, DevOps GitHub danluu/post-mortems) with per-domain n=23–25.
  • Empirical demonstration that synthetic benchmark rankings do not predict enterprise performance: paraphrasing is statistically indistinguishable from no defense on real documents.
  • PARSE pipeline: domain-aware, fact-preserving, inference-time sanitization (3–5 LLM API calls/doc, ~$0.01/doc) requiring no training, with a closed-loop consistency check that retries when facts are dropped.
  • Two ablations (parse_fast collapsing the multi-step analysis; parse_domain_conditional replacing directiveness with a domain heuristic) that isolate the contributions of structured multi-step analysis and directiveness (vs. domain) routing.
  • Practitioner-facing recommendations against paraphrasing-only and against Llama-Guard-as-primary for production professional RAG.

Methodology

  • Benchmark construction. claude-sonnet-4-5 generates, for each real retrieved document, (1) a legitimate analysis task, (2) a malicious goal, and (3) a domain-camouflaged payload embedding that goal in professional vocabulary. Ground truth combines a programmatic malicious_output_signal detector with an LLM-as-judge. Domains: financial 24, legal 25, medical 23 (3 discarded for construction errors), scientific 25, DevOps 25.
  • Defense conditions (8 total). baseline (no defense), spotlighting, sandwiching, paraphrasing, Llama Guard 4, PARSE (full pipeline), parse_fast (single combined analysis call), parse_domain_conditional (route by domain: full for financial/legal/medical, simple paraphrase for scientific/DevOps).
  • PARSE architecture. Step 1 Haiku domain classifier returns (domain, confidence) and activates domain-specific authority vocabulary allowlists. Step 1.5 runs in parallel with Step 1 and scores δ(d) ∈ [0,1] for directiveness; documents with δ(d) < 0.5 (59% of corpus; 72/122) take the lightweight paraphrase path. Steps 2+3 in a single Haiku call label each sentence (factual | directive | hybrid), score injection likelihood 0–1 with domain-specific allowlists (legal "shall/hereby", scientific "we propose/our results suggest", medical "treatment resulted in" exempted), and extract a structured fact list. Step 4 Sonnet paraphraser operates under a hard fact-preservation constraint with three aggression tiers: ≥0.6 aggressive neutralization, 0.3–0.6 light rewriting, <0.3 verbatim preservation. Step 5 Haiku consistency checker verifies each extracted fact is present in the paraphrased output; on failure, Step 4 is retried once with explicit missing-fact constraints. Step 6 returns the sanitized document plus a per-sentence provenance trace (scores, labels, fact set, coverage).
  • Metrics. Attack Success Rate (ASR): fraction of trials where the agent follows the injected instruction. Utility: fraction of trials where the agent completes the legitimate task (LLM-judge). Significance: McNemar's exact test (one-sided), Cohen's h effect size, Bonferroni threshold α=0.0071 for 7 comparisons. Statistical power assessed via minimum n to detect the observed h at 80% power.

Results & Highlights

  • Headline. PARSE 15.6% ASR / 86.9% utility vs. baseline 25.4% / 91.8%: statistically significant reduction (p=0.014, h=−0.245) with adequate power (n=122 > n_min=103); approaches the Bonferroni threshold (α=0.0071) without crossing it.
  • Paraphrasing. 24.6% ASR (p=0.500, h=−0.019, n_min=17,253 to confirm) and 82.8% utility — a 9 pp utility cost with zero security benefit. Synthetic-benchmark performance does not transfer.
  • Llama Guard 4. 18.9% ASR (p=0.004, survives Bonferroni) but 64.8% utility — a 27 pp cost, disqualifying for production.
  • Spotlighting / sandwiching. 18.9% ASR (each), utility 92.6% / 91.0%. Nominally significant (p=0.038 / 0.048) but underpowered at n_min=247; do not survive Bonferroni.
  • Per-domain PARSE gains (vs baseline). DevOps 24.0%→12.0%, financial 33.3%→12.5%, legal 24.0%→16.0%, medical 21.7%→17.4%, scientific 24.0%→20.0%. Largest reductions in financial and DevOps.
  • Directiveness distribution. 72/122 docs (59%) < threshold → lightweight path. Mean directiveness by domain: legal 0.648 (highest), medical 0.544, scientific/DevOps lowest.
  • Ablations. parse_fast 32.8% ASR (worse than baseline) — confirms multi-step analysis is necessary. parse_domain_conditional 22.1% ASR (p=0.292) — confirms directiveness (per-document) outperforms domain heuristics.
  • Cost. ~$0.01/document at Haiku/Sonnet pricing; 10K-doc corpus ≈ $100 one-time ingestion cost. Lightweight path needs only 2 API calls.
  • Qualitative failure mode. A CECO Environmental 10-K sentence with $116.9M / $380.1M / $263.2M / 56.6% / $50.7M was paraphrased into "substantial revenue growth… driven by organic expansion and strategic acquisitions" — discarding every quantitative fact. PARSE's fact constraint prevents this.

Authors & Credibility

  • Single author: Aaditya Pai, Columbia University Data Science Institute. The work builds on the author's prior preprints (arXiv:2605.22001 on domain-camouflaged injection, and an in-prep evaluation of prompting-based defenses).
  • Strengths: unusually careful statistical reporting (explicit p, h, power), public code/benchmark release announced at https://github.com/aaditya79/parse-defense/, and clear threat model.
  • Yellow flags: single author for a 5-domain enterprise benchmark with novel task construction; tasks and payloads are LLM-generated without human validation; results have not been independently replicated.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty. PARSE itself is a defensible composition of known ideas (classify, extract facts, rewrite, verify) rather than a new primitive. The bigger contribution is the demonstration that paraphrasing fails on real documents and the construction of the real-document benchmark itself.
  • Rigor. Statistical care is unusually good for the field (explicit McNemar, h, n_min, Bonferroni). However, the LLM-generated tasks/payloads have no human validation, the per-domain n=23–25 is acknowledged as exploratory, and the judge model and attacker model are not adversarially diverse. PARSE's p=0.014 does not survive Bonferroni (α=0.0071); the author argues this is acceptable given adequate power and largest effect size — a reasonable but contestable choice.
  • Reproducibility. Public code + benchmark release at the linked GitHub repo is announced, which materially improves reproducibility relative to the field norm. But the judge model, exact prompts, and attack-model details (which LLM plays the attacker, which plays the agent) need to be in the released artifacts; without them, the 38% ASR drop is hard to verify.
  • Significance. The "synthetic benchmarks don't predict enterprise defense quality" finding is genuinely important and likely to be cited. PARSE itself is an engineering pattern, not a paradigm shift, but it is a sensible one that practitioners can deploy today.
  • Red flags. (i) Single-author enterprise-security paper with LLM-generated attacks and no human validation of the benchmark. (ii) ASR numbers depend on a single attacker model and a single judge model — adaptive attackers are explicitly out of scope. (iii) The lightweight path's ASR on its 59% of inputs is not separately reported; that path is just paraphrasing, which we already know fails (p=0.500). (iv) Directiveness threshold 0.5 is tuned on a validation set and may require domain-specific recalibration in deployment. (v) Comparison set excludes recent defenses like instruction hierarchy, signed content, structured provenance tags, and most retrieval-side filters.

Strengths

  • Targets the most important open question in deployed RAG security: do lab defenses survive real documents?
  • Quantifies utility regression as a co-equal metric with ASR — rare and valuable.
  • Uses real, named source corpora (SEC, Federal Register, PubMed, arXiv, GitHub danluu/post-mortems) rather than synthetic injections.
  • Explicit per-condition power analysis and effect-size reporting; Bonferroni correction acknowledged.
  • Directiveness-aware routing is a pragmatic cost-control idea; ~$0.01/doc at current Haiku/Sonnet pricing is realistic for ingestion-time deployment.
  • Public code and benchmark release improves the field's evaluation infrastructure.

Limitations

  • Single author; breadth of attack-model and judge-model coverage is necessarily limited.
  • 122 tasks / 5 domains ≈ 24/domain; per-domain comparisons are explicitly exploratory (underpowered).
  • Task construction and payloads are LLM-generated with no human validation.
  • Not evaluated against adaptive adversaries who know the sanitization mechanism.
  • Lightweight path (59% of inputs) is essentially paraphrasing with the same null ASR result; PARSE's gains apply only to the 41% routed through the full pipeline.
  • Directiveness threshold 0.5 is tuned on a validation set and may need per-domain calibration.
  • Cost (~$0.01/doc) can be prohibitive for very large corpora without aggressive caching.

Relevance

直接命中企业 RAG/Agent 安全的核心痛点：合成基准上"最强"的转述防御在真实长文档上无显著效果，而 PARSE 的事实约束 + 指令性门提供了一条可部署（ingestion-time，~$0.01/doc）的工程路径。对正在评估或部署专业领域 RAG 的工程团队具有高参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does PARSE's ASR advantage hold against stronger / adaptive attackers (multi-turn injections, indirect injections in tool outputs, payloads explicitly crafted to survive structured sanitization)?
  • How much of the 38% ASR drop comes from fact extraction vs. sentence-level injection scoring vs. the consistency loop — i.e., is there a per-component ablation with utility measured?
  • For the 59% of documents on the lightweight paraphrase path, what is the ASR and utility, given that lightweight paraphrasing on its own is indistinguishable from no defense?
  • How sensitive is the directiveness threshold (0.5) to per-domain calibration? Does a per-domain threshold close or widen the gap between PARSE and the domain-conditional ablation?
  • Does the judge LLM introduce a self-preference bias given that task/payload construction and judging all use Claude-family models?
  • Will the benchmark, attack corpora, prompts, and judge model be released publicly and versioned?
