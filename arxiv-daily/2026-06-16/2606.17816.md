> **一句话总结：** 论文把"完整刻画梯度流守恒律"重新表述为对守恒函数 h(θ) 的偏微分方程（PDE）求解问题，并由此首次给出 GELU/SiLU/SwiGLU 前馈网络、多头注意力（vanilla / sinusoidal PE / RoPE）以及稠密/稀疏 MoE（含 softmax 与 sigmoid 门控）的全部显式守恒不变量：在 GELU/SiLU FFN 下"无任何非平凡守恒律"；SwiGLU FFN 出现 ‖A:,i‖² − ‖C_i,:‖²；vanilla MHA 给出 Q_i⊤Q_i − K_i⊤K_i 与 V_i⊤V_i − O_i⊤O_i；RoPE 引入"复结构矩阵 J"耦合的逐块块范数不变量；MoE 把单专家守恒律与 ∑W_i 行向量之和耦合；并在 WikiText-103/ImageNet-1K 上验证离散 SGD 下守恒误差按 O(τ²k) 缩放。

> **TL;DR:** A unified, completeness-style theory of conservation laws (functions h(θ) invariant along the gradient flow) for the building blocks of modern LLMs. Methodologically, the paper drops Marcotte et al.'s Lie-theoretic machinery and instead derives x-independent PDE constraints on ∇h, exploits meromorphic extension + the identity theorem + pole analysis to force these constraints, and reads off the characteristic invariants. Concretely: (i) **GELU/SiLU FFNs** admit *only* trivial constant conservation laws; (ii) **SwiGLU FFNs** conserve ‖A:,i‖² − ‖C_i,:‖² for each hidden unit i, arising from the multiplicative gate; (iii) **vanilla multihead attention** conserves Q_i⊤Q_i − K_i⊤K_i and V_i⊤V_i − O_i⊤O_i per head (resolving Marcotte et al. 2025's open problem for MHA), and **sinusoidal PE** does not change this because it is just an additive input shift; (iv) **RoPE** fundamentally alters the structure and produces a different invariant ‖Q_i^{(j)}‖_F² − ‖K_i^{(j)}‖_F² per head × per 2D block, with a coupled condition involving the canonical complex structure matrix J; (v) **Dense MoE / SMoE (k>1) / sigmoid-gated MoE** share invariants that decompose into per-expert SwiGLU-type constraints plus a single gating-level invariant ∑_i W_i. Empirically, on Qwen-3 / ViT trained with SGD on WikiText-103 and ImageNet-1K, conservation error scales as the predicted O(τ²k) for MHA, SwiGLU FFN, RoPE, and MoE gating, with a linear LR scheduler avoiding the "schedule-decays-to-zero" trivial saturation problem.

## 核心贡献

- **方法学贡献**：抛弃 Lie 群方法，回到第一性原理，把"找全部守恒律"等价刻画为解一组**关于 h(θ) 的、与输入 x 无关的 PDE 约束**，再用复变函数（meromorphic 扩张 + identity theorem + 极结构比较）逐层去掉对 x 的依赖，得到 h 在哪些特征不变量所确定的 level set 上取常值。
- **FFN 部分**（Theorem 4.1）：**GELU 与 SiLU 激活的 FFN 没有非平凡守恒律**——所有 h(θ) 必为常数。这是把 "ReLU 情形的丰富守恒律" 扩展到平滑激活后一个**负向但严格**的结论。
- **FFN 部分**（Theorem 4.2）：**SwiGLU FFN 因门控乘法出现非平凡不变量** ‖A:,i‖² − ‖C_i,:‖²（每隐藏单元 i 一个），解释了为什么 SwiGLU 架构在 LLM 中行为显著区别于纯 SiLU/GeLU。
- **MHA 部分**（Theorem 4.3）：完整刻画 **vanilla 多头注意力**的守恒律，证实 Marcotte et al. (2025) 的猜想：每个头 i 有两组不变量 Q_i⊤Q_i − K_i⊤K_i 与 V_i⊤V_i − O_i⊤O_i，**解决该文献中遗留的多头开放问题**。
- **位置编码部分**（Theorem 4.4 + 分析）：**Sinusoidal PE 是对输入的可逆平移**，守恒律与 vanilla MHA 一致；**RoPE 在头投影之后做旋转**，把 Q_i, K_i 在 2D 块分解后与"复结构矩阵 J"耦合，导出与 vanilla MHA 完全不同的一族不变量 ‖Q_i^{(j)}‖_F² − ‖K_i^{(j)}‖_F²。同一架构层概念在两种 PE 下产生本质不同的训练几何。
- **MoE 部分**（Theorem 4.5–4.7）：**稠密 MoE** 的守恒律把每个 SwiGLU 专家的内禀不变量与门控层单一的 ∑_i W_i 不变量解耦；**SMoE（k>1）**的守恒律与稠密 MoE **完全相同**（k=1 与 tie-breaking 的边界情形被排除）；**sigmoid 与 softmax 门控**给出**同一组**守恒律（normalized sigmoid 也不改变几何）。
- **实验验证**：用 Qwen-3 在 WikiText-103 / PTB、用 ViT 在 CIFAR-10 / ImageNet-1K 进行全批量梯度下降与小批量 SGD 双轨实验，覆盖 MHA / SwiGLU FFN / RoPE / MoE 门控四个模块；采用 **10 个随机种子 + 线性 LR 调度器**（避免 "调度衰减到 0 ⇒ 平凡饱和" 的假象），观察到守恒误差按 Marcotte et al. (2025) Proposition 5.1 预测的 O(τ²k) 缩放。
- **同时追踪非守恒量作为定性对照**：这些量在小参数扰动下大幅偏离，与守恒律形成对照，从而验证实验测得的小误差不是因为"什么都没动"。

## Key Contributions

- **Methodological reframing.** The paper abandons the Lie-algebraic machinery used by Marcotte et al. (2023; 2025) and reformulates "characterize all conservation laws" as a **system of x-independent PDE constraints** on h(θ). Solvability proceeds by (a) complex-analytic (meromorphic) extension of the inner-product condition along axes x = t·e_j, (b) the identity theorem for meromorphic functions, and (c) pole-order analysis to extract the explicit invariants. This makes the characterization problem mechanical and architecture-portable.
- **Theorem 4.1 — Trivial laws for GELU/SiLU FFNs.** For a single-hidden-layer FFN f(x;A,B) = A·GELU(Bx) (or SiLU), every C¹ conservation law is constant. The smoothness of the activation kills the "per-neuron" invariants that exist for ReLU.
- **Theorem 4.2 — SwiGLU FFN invariants.** For f(x;A,B,C) = A·(SiLU(Bx) ⊙ (Cx)), conservation laws are constant on the level sets of ‖A:,i‖² − ‖C_i,:‖² for each hidden unit i. The invariant is purely an interaction between the *up-projection* A and the *gate* C, with B frozen — a clean, testable prediction.
- **Theorem 4.3 — Complete MHA characterization (resolves Marcotte et al. 2025's open problem).** For multihead attention with n heads and projections Q_i, K_i, V_i, O_i, conservation laws satisfy K_i·(∇_{Q_i} h)⊤ + (∇_{K_i} h)·Q_i⊤ = 0 and O_i·(∇_{V_i} h)⊤ + (∇_{O_i} h)·V_i⊤ = 0 per head. Hence h is constant on level sets of Q_i⊤Q_i − K_i⊤K_i and V_i⊤V_i − O_i⊤V_i (sic — paper states V_i⊤V_i − O_i⊤O_i). The proof in Appendix B.1 handles the multi-head setting, which previous work had flagged as open.
- **Theorem 4.4 — RoPE invariants.** Decompose Q_i, K_i into 2-column blocks Q_i^{(j)}, K_i^{(j)}, j ∈ [m], where d_h = 2m. Conservation laws satisfy K_i^{(j)}·(∇_{Q_i^{(j)}} h)⊤ + (∇_{K_i^{(j)}} h)·Q_i^{(j)⊤} = 0 **and** K_i^{(j)}·J·(∇_{Q_i^{(j)}} h)⊤ + (∇_{K_i^{(j)}} h)·J·Q_i^{(j)⊤} = 0, where J = [[0,1],[-1,0]] is the canonical complex structure. The invariants become ‖Q_i^{(j)}‖_F² − ‖K_i^{(j)}‖_F² per head × block.
- **Sinusoidal PE preserves vanilla MHA invariants.** A clean observation: since sinusoidal PE adds a fixed bijective shift to x, the conservation structure is unchanged — a useful negative result that explains *empirically* why sinusoidal and RoPE-pretrained models sometimes exhibit different optimization behavior despite superficially similar architectures.
- **Theorem 4.5 — Dense MoE.** Conservation laws factor into per-expert SwiGLU-type constraints (A_i,B_i,C_i for each expert) plus a single global gating invariant: ∇_{W_1} h = ∇_{W_2} h = ... = ∇_{W_n} h. The gating is constrained only through the row-sum ∑_i W_i, not individual rows.
- **Theorem 4.6 — Sparse MoE (k>1) inherits dense-MoE laws.** When k>1, every conservation law of the SMoE is also a conservation law of the corresponding dense MoE. The k>1 condition is essential: it connects the regions associated with different active-expert sets so that gating-gradient constraints can propagate across experts.
- **Theorem 4.7 — Sigmoid and softmax gating yield identical conservation laws.** Normalized sigmoid gating (Liu et al. 2024) and standard softmax gating produce *the same* invariants — a non-trivial invariance that matters for MoE-LLM pretraining analyses.
- **Experiments.** Two regimes: (i) full-batch GD on CIFAR-10 / PTB to approximate the continuous-time gradient flow; (ii) mini-batch SGD on ImageNet-1K (ViT) and WikiText-103 (Qwen-3). 10 random seeds per configuration, linear LR scheduler (to avoid schedule-decays-to-zero trivial saturation). Conservation error tracks the predicted O(τ²k) bound for MHA, SwiGLU FFN, RoPE, and MoE gating; non-conserved quantities monitored in parallel exhibit large deviations, validating that the boundedness is not a trivial "nothing changed" artifact.

## Methodology

- **Setup.** Regression setting X = ℝ^{d_in}, Y = ℝ^{d_out}, cost L_D(θ) = (1/N) ∑ ℓ(f(x_i;θ), y_i), gradient flow θ̇ = −∇L_D(θ). A C¹ function h:Θ→ℝ is a *conservation law* (Marcotte et al. 2023's dataset-independent notion) iff ⟨∇h(θ), ∇_θ f(x;θ)·v⟩ = 0 for all θ, all x in the C²-domain X_θ, all v in V_ℓ. Paper assumes V_ℓ = ℝ^{d_out} (square loss, cross-entropy, etc., all qualify).
- **PDE reformulation (Section 3).** For each output coordinate i and fixed θ, define F_i(x;θ) := ⟨∇h(θ), ∇_θ f_i(x;θ)⟩, which must vanish on X_θ. Restrict x to axes t·e_j ⊂ ℝ^d; if the resulting F_i is meromorphic in t and vanishes on a set with an accumulation point, the identity theorem forces F_i ≡ 0 on that axis, yielding x-independent constraints on ∇h. Solving the resulting system of first-order PDEs gives the characteristic invariants.
- **Toy example** f(x;a,b) = abx: the constraint ∂_a h · b + ∂_b h · a = 0 forces h to be constant on connected components of {a² − b² = c}; level sets are disconnected so h need not equal any function of a² − b² globally, but a² − b² is the *characteristic invariant*.
- **Per-architecture arguments.**
  - *FFN GELU/SiLU:* GELU(z) = z·Φ(z) involves the standard normal CDF; SiLU(z) = z·σ(z). Both render F_i meromorphic with poles on a discrete set; pole comparison forces all gradient components of h to vanish, hence h constant.
  - *FFN SwiGLU:* The Hadamard product with C introduces multiplicative coupling; pole analysis yields the difference-of-squares invariant per hidden unit.
  - *MHA:* Softmax is meromorphic; the softmax-summed attention produces pole cancellations that leave precisely the Q⊤Q − K⊤K / V⊤V − O⊤O invariants per head.
  - *Sinusoidal PE:* Bijective input shift, no change.
  - *RoPE:* R_{p−q} depends on token positions, breaking the position-independent structure of vanilla MHA. Block-decomposition plus the J-matrix (rotation generator) reveals per-head × per-block invariants.
  - *MoE (dense / sparse / sigmoid):* Per-expert SwiGLU machinery handles the experts; the gating linear map's softmax (or normalized sigmoid) creates additional structure that, via connectivity of Top-k regions for k>1, forces ∇_{W_i} h = ∇_{W_j} h globally.
- **SMoE subtlety.** Top-k routing is discontinuous on the boundary between active-expert sets. The paper handles this by showing that for k>1, these regions overlap sufficiently to force the same global constraint as dense MoE.

## Results & Highlights

- **Theorem 4.1 (FFN-GELU/SiLU):** every C¹ conservation law is constant. **Negative but sharp** — the contrast with ReLU is intrinsic to smoothness.
- **Theorem 4.2 (FFN-SwiGLU):** characteristic invariants ‖A:,i‖² − ‖C_i,:‖² for i ∈ [d₁]. **Explains** why SwiGLU behaves differently from GELU/SiLU in scaling experiments.
- **Theorem 4.3 (vanilla MHA):** complete characterization, head-by-head invariants {Q_i⊤Q_i − K_i⊤K_i, V_i⊤V_i − O_i⊤O_i}_{i=1}^n. Resolves Marcotte et al. (2025)'s explicitly stated open problem.
- **Theorem 4.4 (MHA + RoPE):** head × 2D-block invariants ‖Q_i^{(j)}‖_F² − ‖K_i^{(j)}‖_F² plus coupled J-rotation condition. Structurally distinct from vanilla MHA.
- **Theorems 4.5–4.7 (MoE family):** per-expert SwiGLU invariants + global ∑_i W_i; SMoE (k>1) and sigmoid-gated variants are equivalent to dense-softmax-MoE.
- **Experimental scaling (Figure 2).** Conservation error ε_block(k) = (1/N) ∑_i |h_i(θ_k) − h_i(θ_0)|²/|h_i(θ_0)|² tracks O(τ²k) on ImageNet-1K (ViT) and WikiText-103 (Qwen-3) for (a) MHA, (b) SwiGLU FFN, (c) RoPE, (d) MoE softmax gating, with three learning rates per panel. Higher learning rates → proportionally larger error bounds, as predicted by Marcotte et al. (2025) Proposition 5.1.
- **Linear LR scheduler.** Crucial methodological choice — a decaying-to-zero scheduler would trivially suppress any error signal; the linear schedule keeps the model moving and exposes the genuine O(τ²k) accumulation.
- **Non-conserved quantities as control.** Tracked in parallel to confirm that small conservation error is not "because nothing changed."
- **Two-scale validation.** Full-batch GD on small datasets (CIFAR-10, PTB) for the continuous-flow limit; mini-batch SGD on large datasets (ImageNet-1K, WikiText-103) for practical-relevance validation. Both show consistent O(τ²k) scaling.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: high.** The reframing of "characterize all conservation laws" as solving a PDE system on h(θ), combined with the meromorphic-extension + identity-theorem + pole-comparison pipeline, is a genuinely fresh methodological contribution that is architecture-portable. The MHA completeness theorem and the RoPE-invariant characterization both *resolve explicit open problems* flagged by Marcotte et al. (2025). The MoE equivalence theorems (SMoE = dense MoE; sigmoid = softmax) are non-obvious corollaries.
- **Rigor: high, with caveats.** The proofs are relegated to appendices A–C (the main text is theorem statements + sketches), which is standard for ICML. The reliance on the identity theorem requires the relevant functions to be meromorphic on ℂ (or at least on a connected domain); the authors explicitly acknowledge this limitation (Remark 5.2) — layer normalization introduces √x and depth composition introduces e^{1/x}, which are not meromorphic. They flag this as future work, which is intellectually honest.
- **Reproducibility: medium-to-high.** Datasets, model families (Qwen-3, ViT), and step sizes are specified. Appendix D has experimental details. But exact seeds, framework (PyTorch vs JAX), and hardware are not in the main text. The theoretical contribution is fully reproducible in principle (Appendix proofs) but verifying each Appendix proof is non-trivial work.
- **Significance: high for theory, moderate for practice.** For the *theoretical* community studying implicit bias / NTK / feature learning, this is a substantial advance. For *practitioners*, the immediate application is limited — conservation laws are mostly descriptive, not prescriptive. The authors are upfront that this is foundational theory. The empirical validation is convincing but does not demonstrate that these invariants *predict* anything a practitioner cares about (e.g., generalization, scaling-law exponents, downstream task performance).
- **Red flags / honest concerns:**
  - **Data-independence assumption.** Adopted from Marcotte et al., but it is restrictive — requiring conservation laws to hold for *any* dataset means the results describe the architecture's intrinsic geometry, not its learning behavior on real data. The authors note this (Remark 2.2), but the practical relevance remains an open question.
  - **Shallow theory / depth gap.** The limitations explicitly exclude layer normalization, deep stacks, and residual connections — i.e., *the actual architectures used in modern LLMs*. ResNets and Transformers beyond a single layer fall outside the current framework. The authors acknowledge this; future-work scope is real.
  - **Empirical depth is shallow.** Four architectures × three learning rates × two datasets × linear scheduler is a narrow sweep. No ablation on architecture depth, on dataset size, on regularization (weight decay was omitted, see Remark 2.1), or on optimizer family. The O(τ²k) fit is qualitative — no fitted constant, no confidence intervals, no test of the bound's tightness.
  - **Limited novelty of the empirical contribution.** Figure 2 is a "does the bound hold" plot, not a new phenomenon. The linear-LR-scheduler trick is the methodological highlight, but it's a workaround for scheduler-induced saturation, not a new finding.
  - **Non-conserved-quantity baseline is qualitative.** Showing that some quantity *isn't* conserved is informative but does not quantify the gap between conserved and non-conserved dynamics.
  - **Single-stage ICML 2026 acceptance** with no cited workshop/preprint history is a minor concern — but the work is closely connected to Marcotte et al. (2025), which appeared at NeurIPS 2024, so the lineage is credible.
- **Overall: solid theory paper with acknowledged scope limits and a thin empirical layer.** The contribution is real and the open problems it closes are meaningful, but a reader should not expect immediate practical impact.

## Strengths

- **Closes explicit open problems** from Marcotte et al. (2025): MHA completeness, full RoPE invariants.
- **Unified methodology** (PDE reformulation + meromorphic analysis + pole comparison) generalizes across FFN / MHA / MoE architectures, demonstrating that the framework is portable rather than ad hoc.
- **Sharp negative results** — GELU/SiLU FFNs have *no* non-trivial conservation laws — are as informative as the positive theorems. The dichotomy between GELU/SiLU (trivial) and SwiGLU (non-trivial) cleanly isolates the role of the multiplicative gate.
- **Empirical design is thoughtful.** Linear LR scheduler, 10-seed averaging, full-batch + mini-batch dual regime, parallel non-conserved-quantity baseline.
- **Honest about limitations.** Remarks 2.2, 5.1, 5.2 explicitly flag the data-independence assumption, the RoPE-specific difficulty, and the meromorphic-extension barrier (layer norm, deep stacks).
- **ICML 2026 acceptance** in a reputable venue with appendix-level proof detail.

## Limitations

- **No layer normalization, no residual connections, no deep stacks.** The framework's core tool (meromorphic extension + identity theorem) breaks down for √x (LayerNorm) and compositions that produce essential singularities (e^{1/x}). Modern LLMs use all of these.
- **Data-independence assumption** (inherited from Marcotte et al. 2023) is restrictive; conservation laws that hold for *any* dataset describe a stricter property than "implicit bias on a typical dataset."
- **No learning-rate decay in the empirical setup** (Remark 2.1 justifies omission by appealing to Marcotte et al.'s "conserved with or without weight decay" claim) — but real training always uses both, and whether the O(τ²k) bound remains informative under standard AdamW + cosine schedules is untested.
- **Empirical validation is descriptive, not predictive.** Conservation laws are shown to be invariant; the paper does not test whether these invariants correlate with any practically relevant quantity (generalization, calibration, robustness, scaling).
- **No comparison to other implicit-bias frameworks** (e.g., NTK regime, feature-learning results, max-margin theory). The conservation-law lens is one of several; the paper does not position it relative to alternatives.
- **Theorem 4.6 requires k>1.** The k=1 case (single-expert routing) is explicitly excluded and the tie-breaking rule in Top-k is glossed over.
- **No code release mentioned.** Reproducing Appendix proofs requires substantial manual effort.
- **No experiments at LLM scale.** Qwen-3 in the experiments is presumably a small variant; the paper does not specify which.

## Relevance

这篇论文直接命中 "ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations" 兴趣方向——它给出现代大模型核心模块（SwiGLU FFN、多头注意力、RoPE、MoE）训练动力学的完整守恒律刻画，并显式解决了 Marcotte et al. (2025) 提出的多头开放问题。对研究"为何某些激活/位置编码/门控能让 LLM 训练更稳定"的人，这是少有的把架构选择转化为可证伪几何预测的工作。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- **Practical implications of these invariants.** Does ‖A:,i‖² − ‖C_i,:‖² or ‖Q_i⊤Q_i − K_i⊤K_i‖ correlate with downstream task performance, generalization gap, or calibration in trained LLMs? Conservation laws are geometric facts; their *predictive power* is untested.
- **Extending past layer normalization.** The meromorphic toolchain breaks for √x (LayerNorm) and deep compositions. A natural extension would restrict Fi to a *region* of ℂ^d avoiding the branch cut, or use multi-variable generalizations of the identity theorem on appropriate submanifolds.
- **Residual connections.** ResNets are outside the current framework. Marcotte et al. (2025) addressed ResNets under additional assumptions; reconciling that with the SwiGLU / RoPE / MoE treatment would yield a "modern Transformer" end-to-end theory.
- **Optimizer family effects.** All experiments use (S)GD. Do conservation laws survive under Adam / AdamW with momentum? The Marcotte et al. (2025) Proposition 5.1 momentum extension handles the SGD-with-momentum case, but adaptive optimizers introduce per-coordinate scaling that may break the orthogonality condition.
- **k=1 SMoE.** Theorem 4.6 only covers k>1. What changes for k=1 or under different tie-breaking rules?
- **Conservation-law-based regularization.** Saul (2023) and Stock et al. (2019) used invariants as regularizers. Do the new SwiGLU / RoPE / MoE invariants yield *better* regularizers than the ReLU ones used previously? An ablation would be valuable.
- **Falsifiable predictions for architecture search.** Can the count of independent invariants predict which architecture variants are more or less "trainable" in practice? Conservation laws give a measure of architectural rigidity that might correlate with training stability.
- **Connection to feature learning.** Modern feature-learning analyses (e.g., Abbe et al. 2023 on rank dynamics in Transformers) describe *how* representations evolve. Are the conservation laws redundant with, complementary to, or implied by such analyses?
