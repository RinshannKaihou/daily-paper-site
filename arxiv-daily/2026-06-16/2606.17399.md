> **一句话总结：** 用乘法特征变换（而非标准加法 DFT）分析 grokked Transformer 在 $a \cdot b \bmod 113$ 上的嵌入，作者证明频谱实际上是稀疏的（仅 4 个关键频率、Gini 0.58、96.9% 神经元单频调谐），并据此提出"离散对数钟"算法——模型先在离散对数空间将乘法化为加法，再套用 Nanda 等人的 Clock 三角恒等式机制。

> **TL;DR:** A 1-layer transformer that grokks $a \cdot b \bmod 113$ has been previously reported to use a "dense" Fourier spectrum across all frequencies (Doshi et al. 2024; Furuta et al. 2024), suggesting multiplication learns a fundamentally less interpretable algorithm than addition. This paper shows the density is a basis artifact. The natural Fourier basis for multiplication is the multiplicative character transform on $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^*$ — operationally, reorder the embedding rows by the discrete logarithm, then take the standard DFT. In this basis, the embedding spectrum becomes sparse (Gini 0.579 vs. 0.071, IPR 4.1 vs. 52.7, 4 key frequencies), 96.9% of MLP neurons are cleanly tuned to a single multiplicative frequency, neuron heatmaps reveal 2D-periodic structure only in log-order, and SVD principal components exhibit clean sinusoids. The author derives a "Discrete-Log Clock" algorithm: (1) embed each integer $a$ as sinusoids of $\log_g(a)$; (2) attention routes to the output; (3) MLP applies the trig identity $\cos(k(\alpha+\beta)) = \cos k\alpha \cos k\beta - \sin k\alpha \sin k\beta$ in log-space; (4) dot-product with the unembedding scoring column gives constructive interference only at $c \equiv ab \pmod p$. The paper confirms the picture across 10 primes ($p = 59$–$113$) and $\sim$80% of random seeds, and reports preliminary 100% accuracy on $a^b \bmod 41$ with a 2-layer model.

## 核心贡献

- 指出现有"模乘 Transformer 学到稠密频谱"的结论是基底错配伪影：加法 DFT 不匹配 $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^*$ 的乘法群结构。
- 引入乘法特征变换作为正确基底，将嵌入谱稀疏度（Gini 0.071 → 0.579、IPR 52.7 → 4.1、关键频率 0 → 4）量化呈现。
- 神经元层面验证：512 个 MLP 神经元中 496 个（96.9%）在乘法基底中单频能量占比 > 85%（加法基底下为 0/512）。
- 热图与 SVD 双重佐证：按离散对数重排后才出现清晰的 2D 对角条纹与正弦主成分。
- 形式化推导完整的"离散对数钟"算法（嵌入 → 注意力路由 → MLP 三角加法 → 反嵌入打分 → 建设性干涉），与 Nanda 等人的 Clock 算法形成精确类比。
- 在 10 个素数（p = 59–113）和约 80% 随机种子下复现该机制，并初步展示 2 层 Transformer 在 $a^b \bmod 41$ 上达到 100% 准确率。

## Key Contributions

- Reframes "dense Fourier spectrum" claims for modular-multiplication transformers as a basis-mismatch artifact.
- Introduces the multiplicative character transform — operationally: reorder rows by discrete logarithm, then take the standard DFT — as the algebraically correct analysis tool for $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^*$.
- Quantitative sparsity jump on a grokked $a \cdot b \bmod 113$ model: Gini 0.071 → 0.579, Inverse Participation Ratio 52.7 → 4.1, key frequencies detected 0 → 4 (at frequencies {2, 8, 47, 56}).
- Neuron-level monosemy: 496/512 (96.9%) of MLP neurons have >85% energy at a single multiplicative frequency, vs. 0/512 in the additive basis.
- Activation heatmaps and SVD components show clean 2D-periodic / sinusoidal structure only when rows are reordered by discrete logarithm.
- Derives the full "Discrete-Log Clock" algorithm step-by-step (embedding → attention → MLP trig addition → unembedding scoring), with explicit constructive-interference argument for why only $c = ab \bmod p$ survives.
- Robustness: mechanism holds across 10 primes (p = 59–113), 4–7 key frequencies, Gini 0.45–0.61 across seeds, ~80% of random seeds.
- Preliminary extension: a 2-layer transformer achieves 100% accuracy on $a^b \bmod 41$ (described as the first such demonstration, mechanism TBD).

## Methodology

- **Architecture.** 1-layer decoder-only transformer, $d_\text{model}=128$, 4 heads ($d_\text{head}=32$), MLP hidden dim 512 with ReLU, no LayerNorm. Input format `[a, b, =]`, prediction read from position 2. Hyperparameters match Nanda et al. (2023) (only the task differs).
- **Training.** AdamW, lr $10^{-3}$, weight decay 1.0, 40,000 epochs, full-batch GD, 30% train fraction (3,763 / 12,544 pairs). Grokking observed between epochs ~9K–14K; model reaches 100% test accuracy.
- **Algebraic setup.** Since 113 is prime, $(\mathbb{Z}/113\mathbb{Z})^*$ is cyclic of order 112 with primitive root $g = 3$. Discrete log $\alpha = \log_g(a)$ is a group isomorphism $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^* \cong \mathbb{Z}/(p-1)\mathbb{Z}$. Under this map, $a \cdot b \equiv g^{(\alpha+\beta) \bmod 112} \pmod{113}$.
- **Two bases.**
  - Additive DFT: $\sin(2\pi k a / q)$, $\cos(2\pi k a / q)$ for $k = 1, \dots, q/2$, $q = 112$.
  - Multiplicative character basis: $\sin(2\pi k \log_g(a) / q)$, $\cos(2\pi k \log_g(a) / q)$ — operationally, relabel rows by discrete log, then take the standard DFT.
- **Analysis pipeline.** (1) Project trained $W_E \in \mathbb{R}^{112 \times 128}$ onto both bases; combine sin/cos norms per frequency. (2) Compute Gini + IPR; "key frequency" = norm > 5× median. (3) For each of 512 MLP neurons compute 2D activation $h_n(a,b)$, take 2D DFT, classify neurons with >85% single-frequency energy. (4) SVD on $W_E$, reorder principal components by discrete log.

## Results & Highlights

- **Sparsity table.**

  | Metric | Additive | Multiplicative |
  |---|---|---|
  | Gini coefficient | 0.071 | 0.579 |
  | Inverse Participation Ratio | 52.7 | 4.1 |
  | Key frequencies detected | 0 | 4 |
  | Neurons >85% single-freq | 0 / 512 | 496 / 512 |
  | Mean max-fraction | 0.054 | 0.925 |

- Key multiplicative frequencies: $K = \{2, 8, 47, 56\}$.
- **Algorithm sketch (Discrete-Log Clock).**
  - Embedding: $W_E[g^\alpha, j] \approx A_j \sin(\omega_{k_j} \alpha + \phi_j)$ for $k_j \in K$.
  - Attention routes both $a$ and $b$ Fourier components to position `=`.
  - MLP implements $\cos(k(\alpha+\beta)) = \cos k\alpha \cos k\beta - \sin k\alpha \sin k\beta$ (and similarly for sin).
  - Unembedding column for candidate $c$ encodes $(\cos \omega_k \gamma, \sin \omega_k \gamma)$ with $\gamma = \log_g(c)$.
  - Logit: $\text{logit}(c) \approx \sum_{k \in K} A_k \cos(\omega_k(\alpha+\beta-\gamma))$. For correct $c^*$, $\gamma^* = (\alpha+\beta) \bmod q$, all cosines equal 1, full constructive interference.
- **Robustness.** Across 10 primes ($p = 59$–$113$), 4–7 key frequencies, Gini 0.45–0.61; across seeds on $p=113$, mechanism appears in ~80% of runs.
- **Exponentiation.** Preliminary: 2-layer transformer reaches 100% accuracy on $a^b \bmod 41$ — claimed as first such demonstration.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The conceptual step — "use the multiplicative character basis, not the additive DFT" — is mathematically natural once stated, but the paper executes it cleanly and resolves a concrete empirical disagreement (dense vs. sparse spectrum) in the literature. The Discrete-Log Clock derivation is the obvious multiplicative analogue of Nanda et al.'s Clock; novelty lies less in inventing a new algorithm than in showing the right basis exposes it.
- **Rigor.** Strong quantitative evidence: Gini, IPR, key-frequency counts, per-neuron mono-frequency fraction, heatmap visualizations, and SVD components all line up. The constructive-interference argument for the scoring step is explicit and clean.
- **Reproducibility.** Code released; hyperparameters exactly match Nanda et al. (2023) for direct comparability; the algorithm derivation is provided step-by-step in the appendix.
- **Significance.** Resolves a misleading prior claim and supplies a clean, reusable interpretability recipe: for a task with group structure $G$, analyze in the Fourier basis of $G$. This is a useful methodological lesson even beyond modular arithmetic.
- **Red flags / things to watch.**
  - The Discrete-Log Clock derivation is described as "approximately" what the model implements, not a proof; circuit-level verification (causal interventions on the named neurons, activation patching) would strengthen the claim from "consistent with" to "is."
  - The mechanism appears in ~80% of seeds — what about the other 20%? Are those alternative solutions (analogous to Zhong et al.'s "Pizza" for addition) and are they characterized?
  - The 2-layer exponentiation result is explicitly preliminary; the mechanism is "under active investigation." Don't over-weight it.
  - The key-frequency set $K = \{2, 8, 47, 56\}$ has no obvious algebraic meaning within the paper — readers may want to know why these particular frequencies.
  - The work is single-author and workshop-track; results would benefit from independent reproduction.

## Strengths

- Clear, focused re-analysis with a single, sharp thesis (basis mismatch → wrong story).
- Quantitative metrics (Gini, IPR, mono-frequency fraction, SVD) make the sparsity claim falsifiable.
- Step-by-step algorithmic derivation in the appendix gives readers a complete picture of the proposed mechanism.
- Robustness checks across multiple primes and seeds, plus an explicit connection to prior "wrong-basis" analyses (Furuta et al., Doshi et al.).
- Code released; directly comparable architecture/hyperparameters to Nanda et al.

## Limitations

- "Approximately implements" rather than "provably implements" — no causal-intervention experiments on identified neurons.
- 20% of seeds do not show the mechanism; alternative algorithms are not characterized (contrast with Zhong et al.'s Clock + Pizza).
- No theoretical explanation for why these particular frequencies {2, 8, 47, 56} emerge; no discussion of effective dimension / frequency interactions.
- Modulus $p$ must be prime so that $(\mathbb{Z}/p\mathbb{Z})^*$ is cyclic and discrete log is well-defined; generalization to composite $n$ (where the multiplicative group is non-cyclic) is not shown.
- Exponentiation extension is preliminary and acknowledged as such.
- Single author, workshop venue; the paper would benefit from a fuller experimental section (multiple seeds with statistics, more primes, more tasks).

## Relevance

该论文与机制可解释性、Transformer 算法学习（grokking）、代数结构引导分析三个方向高度相关。对研究"神经网络是否学到真实算法"以及"如何为给定任务选择合适的可解释性基底"的研究者，这篇既是清晰的方法论案例，也提供了可复用的工具（乘法特征变换 + 离散对数重排）。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can causal interventions (neuron ablation, activation patching on the 496 mono-frequency neurons) confirm they are necessary and sufficient for the Discrete-Log Clock behavior?
- What do the ~20% of seeds that don't show the mechanism learn instead? Is there a "Pizza"-style alternative for multiplication?
- Why the specific frequency set $\{2, 8, 47, 56\}$? Is there a number-theoretic interpretation (e.g., a relationship to $p-1 = 112 = 2^4 \cdot 7$)?
- Does the basis-mismatch story extend to composite moduli, where $(\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z})^*$ is non-cyclic and the discrete-log map is not a single bijection?
- For the preliminary $a^b \bmod p$ result, what does the algorithm look like? Does it iterate the Clock at exponent time-steps, or use a fundamentally different circuit?
- Beyond modular arithmetic, where else does "use the right group's Fourier basis" expose hidden structure (e.g., permutation composition, modular exponentiation, graph-isomorphism-style tasks)?
- Does the effective embedding rank (10.3 / 112) generalize, and what determines it?
