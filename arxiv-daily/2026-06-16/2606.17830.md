> **一句话总结：** 本文对注意力机制与位置编码（sinusoidal APE 与 RoPE）的函数等价对称群进行形式化分析，证明 sinusoidal PE 保持 vanilla 注意力的完整对称群、而 RoPE 将其严格缩小为 `S_h × H(d_h) × GL(d_h)`（其中 `H(d_h) ≅ (C×)^{d_h/2}`），并据此提出针对 MHA 的 weight matching 算法，在 ViT/GPT-2/BERT/Llama 等多种架构与任务上实证 LMC 行为强烈依赖 PE 类型。

> **TL;DR:** This paper formalizes the **functional-equivalence symmetry group** of multi-head attention (MHA) combined with two positional encodings and shows that **sinusoidal PE preserves vanilla attention's symmetry** while **RoPE strictly reduces it to `G_RoPE(d_h, h) = S_h × H(d_h) × GL(d_h)`** (with `H(d_h)` an abelian subgroup of `GL(d_h)` isomorphic to `(C×)^{d_h/2}`). The reduced symmetry is offered as a principled explanation for RoPE's dominance. The authors then design a **two-stage weight-matching algorithm** (head-permutation LAP + per-head continuous QK/VO alignment, with closed-form / Brent-method optimizers for the RoPE case) and use it to empirically demonstrate that **LMC behavior in Transformers crucially depends on the choice of PE**: encoder-only models show reliable LMC, but decoder-only large-scale LMs (GPT-2/Llama on WikiText-103, Enwik8, One Billion Word) fail to exhibit LMC.

## 核心贡献

- **严格刻画 MHA 的对称群** `G_Att(d_h, h) = S_h × GL(d_h) × GL(d_h)`（头部置换 ⊗ QK 上的左乘与 VO 上的右乘），并复现 Tran et al. (2025) 的 MHA functional equivalence 主定理。
- **形式化分析位置编码对对称群的影响**：sinusoidal APE 仅对输入做平移，等价类不发生改变；RoPE 因 `R_{m-n}` 矩阵**插入到 `W_i^Q` 与 `W_i^K` 之间**，破坏了 `GL(d_h)` 在 QK 上的对合作用，对称群严格缩小为 `G_RoPE(d_h, h) = S_h × H(d_h) × GL(d_h)`，其中 `H(d_h) ≅ (C×)^{d_h/2}`。
- **为 RoPE 的实践主导地位提供原理性解释**：对称群缩小意味着等价类减少（即参数化到函数的映射更接近单射），函数类表达力严格更大。
- **General-MHA 主定理 (Theorem 4.1)**：对带 `A_{m,n}^i` 的通用 MHA，证明当 MHA 输出恒为零时所有 `B_i = 0`，本质是注意力头之间的**线性无关性**。
- **针对 MHA（含 RoPE）提出 weight matching 算法**（Algorithm 1）：Stage 1 通过 Linear Assignment Problem 解决头部置换问题，Stage 2 对每对头在 `GL(d_h)`（普通 MHA）或 `H(d_h)`（RoPE）上最小化 QK 与 VO 子损失。
- **跨任务、跨架构、跨规模的 LMC 实证**：ViT/MNIST/CIFAR-10/CIFAR-100/ImageNet-1K、GPT-2/BERT/Llama 在 Enwik8/WikiText103/One Billion Word/AG News/IMDB/DBPedia 上，验证 LMC 在 first-attention-layer、first-Transformer-layer、full-attention re-init 时出现，但 full-model re-init 在大规模 decoder-only LLM 上失败。
- **算法消融实验**：Variant 1（仅 Stage 1）的 barrier ratio 仍达 60-90%，Variant 2（Stage 2 + 正交初始化）降至 10-16%，Full（继续梯度下降）降至 7-12%，证明 Stage 2 与 fine-tuning 均不可或缺。

## Key Contributions

- **Symmetry of vanilla MHA.** The paper re-derives the symmetry group `G_Att(d_h, h) = S_h × GL(d_h) × GL(d_h)` (head permutation combined with independent left-action on the QK circuit and right-action on the VO circuit) and recalls the MHA functional equivalence theorem of Tran et al. (2025), which says that under genericity (rank conditions and pairwise-distinct `W_i^Q (W_i^K)^T`), functional equivalence of two MHAs is fully characterized by this group.
- **Sinusoidal PE is "free" for the symmetry group.** Sinusoidal encoding amounts to a bijective input shift `x ↦ x + p`; it does not change the internal structure of MHA. Therefore the equivalence classes coincide with those of vanilla attention.
- **RoPE strictly reduces the symmetry group.** The rotary matrix `R_{m-n}` inserted between `W_i^Q` and `W_i^K` breaks the `GL(d_h)` cancellation that existed in the vanilla case. The remaining symmetry is `G_RoPE(d_h, h) = S_h × H(d_h) × GL(d_h)`, where `H(d_h) = { Σ_{i=1}^{d_h/2} (a_i P_i + b_i J_i) | (a_i, b_i) ∈ R² \ {(0,0)} }` is an abelian subgroup of `GL(d_h)` isomorphic to `(C×)^{d_h/2}` (P_i, J_i are 2×2 block-diagonal matrices encoding the real/imaginary parts of complex scalars). The function class of `MHA_RoPE` is therefore **strictly larger** than that of `MHA` or `MHA_SinusoidalPE`.
- **General-MHA equivalence theorem (Theorem 4.1).** For a generalized MHA parameterized by `A_{m,n}^i` and `B_i` (subject to stationarity and self-similarity symmetry), the zero function implies `B_i = 0` for all heads. The proof uses exponential-polynomial identity techniques and is a clean linear-independence statement for attention heads. The MHA_RoPE result (Theorem 4.2) follows as a corollary.
- **Weight-matching algorithm for MHA layers.** A two-stage procedure that operates head-by-head:
  - Stage 1: solve a Linear Assignment Problem (Hungarian, O(h³)) over a cost matrix built from row-centered `M_i = W_i^Q (W_i^K)^T` and `N_i = W_i^V (W_i^O)^T` to align head orderings.
  - Stage 2: for each matched head pair, solve `min_{U_i ∈ GL(d_h)} ‖W_{i,A}^Q − W_{i,B}^Q U_i^T‖² + ‖W_{i,A}^K − W_{i,B}^K U_i^{−1}‖²` (and the analogous V–O problem) initialized from a closed-form orthogonal solution and refined by gradient descent. For RoPE, `H(d_h)` decomposes the QK problem into `d_h/2` independent 2-D subproblems, each solved by Brent's method in a scalar.
- **Empirical LMC study across modalities and scales.** Re-initialization schemes: (i) first attention layer, (ii) all attention layers, (iii) first Transformer layer, (iv) full model. Tested on ViT (MNIST/CIFAR-10/CIFAR-100/ImageNet-1K), GPT-2/BERT/Llama (Enwik8/WikiText-103/One Billion Word/AG News/IMDB/DBPedia).
- **Ablations.** Stage-1 (head-permutation) quality is ranked against all `h!` permutations; in practice the LAP solver picks permutations with very low rank (≈2-5 out of 24) and `L̂ ≈ 0`, i.e. near-optimal matching. Stage-2 components (no Stage 2 / orthogonal-only / full) reduce loss barrier ratios from 60-90% to 10-16% to 7-12%.

## Methodology

**Theoretical component (Sections 2-4).**

1. **Parameter space and group action.** Define the MHA parameter space `Θ(d, d_h, h) = (R^{d×d_h})^{4h}` and the symmetry group `G_Att(d_h, h) = S_h × GL(d_h) × GL(d_h)` acting as `g = (σ, (U_i, V_i))` mapping `(W_i^Q, W_i^K, W_i^V, W_i^O) ↦ (W_{σ(i)}^Q U_i^T, W_{σ(i)}^K U_i^{−1}, W_{σ(i)}^V V_i^T, W_{σ(i)}^O V_i^{−1})`. Show this action preserves the MHA map and characterizes functional equivalence (Theorem 2.1, genericity conditions).
2. **Sinusoidal PE analysis.** Show that the map `S → S, x ↦ x + p` is bijective and the resulting MHA equivalence classes equal the vanilla ones.
3. **RoPE analysis.** Write out `MHA_RoPE`, observe that `R_{m-n}` couples positions; prove that the action of `G_Att` no longer preserves functionality, and identify the residual symmetry group `G_RoPE` as `S_h × H(d_h) × GL(d_h)`. Use the abelian property of `H(d_h)` plus the fact that `R_n ∈ H(d_h)` to verify that `U_i ∈ H(d_h)` satisfies `W_i^Q U_i^T R_n (W_i^K U_i^{−1})^T = W_i^Q R_n (W_i^K)^T`.
4. **General-MHA framework.** Introduce `A_{m,n}^i` and `B_i` parameterization covering both vanilla and rotary; impose (a) stationarity (`A_{m,n}^i = A_{m+k,n+k}^i`) and (b) self-similarity symmetry (replace `A_{m,m}^i` by its symmetrization). The main theorem (Theorem 4.1) shows that an identically-zero MHA forces all `B_i = 0`. The proof rewrites the MHA map (clearing softmax denominators) as an exponential polynomial in token positions and invokes the theory of exponential polynomials to extract a linear-independence-of-heads conclusion. RoPE's symmetry then follows as a corollary of Lemma F.2 on rotary matrices.

**Algorithmic component (Section 5).**

- **Stage 1 — Head permutation matching.** Build a cost matrix `C_{i,j} = ‖M̄_i^A − M̄_j^B‖² + ‖N_i^A − N_j^B‖²` where `M_i = W_i^Q (W_i^K)^T`, `N_i = W_i^V (W_i^O)^T`, and `M̄_i` is row-centered to ensure softmax-translation invariance. Solve via Hungarian in O(h³).
- **Stage 2 — Internal QK/VO alignment.** For each head, jointly minimize the QK loss `L_{Q,K}(U) = ‖W_A^Q − W_B^Q U^T‖² + ‖W_A^K − W_B^K U^{−1}‖²` over `U ∈ GL(d_h)` (vanilla) or `U ∈ H(d_h)` (RoPE). Initialize from the orthogonal solution (Lemma G.2), then refine by gradient descent. For RoPE, the `H(d_h)` constraint decouples into `d_h/2` 2-D problems, each solved by Brent's method on a scalar. Analogous procedure for V–O via `V ∈ GL(d_h)`.
- **Implementation notes.** Bias terms are absorbed via augmented weight matrices; for full-Transformer alignment, FFNs use standard Weight Matching (Ainsworth et al. 2023); for LayerNorm networks, a reparameterization is required (introducing a "variant of LMC").

**Empirical component (Section 6).**

- **Datasets and models.** Vision: ViT on MNIST, CIFAR-10/100, ImageNet-1K. Language modeling: GPT-2 (APE and RoPE) and Llama (RoPE only) on Enwik8, WikiText-103, One Billion Word. Text classification: BERT on AG News, IMDB, DBPedia. Models span 1-12 layers and 2-16 heads.
- **Re-initialization protocols.** Only the designated re-initialized parameters are fine-tuned; the rest are frozen. Three seeds per configuration; interpolation between checkpoint pairs with 25 evenly-spaced evaluation points.
- **LMC metric.** Loss / accuracy barrier `B(θ_A, θ_B) = sup_{t∈[0,1]} L(tθ_A + (1−t)θ_B) − tL(θ_A) − (1−t)L(θ_B)`, reported as the ratio against naive interpolation (Variant 1 / Variant 2 / Full) and an averaged scaled metric `L̂`.

## Results & Highlights

- **LMC under first-attention-layer re-init (Figure 2, Table 2).** Both WikiText103 and ImageNet show **naive interpolation maintains a low loss barrier only after matching**, and the matching algorithm collapses the loss barrier to near-zero. Naive interpolation for matched (permuted) checkpoints also drops the validation loss compared to the unaligned baseline.
- **Head-permutation quality (Table 1).** The LAP solver picks permutations with average rank 2-5 out of 24 and `L̂ ≈ 0` (×10²), meaning the algorithm is essentially optimal at the head-ordering level across CIFAR-10/100, IMDB, DBPedia.
- **Stage-2 ablation (Table 1).** Loss/accuracy barrier ratios: Variant 1 (no Stage 2) ≈ 60-95% — high and unstable; Variant 2 (orthogonal init only) ≈ 10-16%; Full (orthogonal + gradient descent) ≈ 7-12%. Both the initial alignment and the subsequent fine-tuning contribute; the latter alone closes roughly half the remaining gap.
- **LMC regimes.** LMC reliably emerges for (i) first attention layer, (ii) full attention layers, (iii) first Transformer layer. Full-model re-init exhibits LMC only on small-scale datasets; for ImageNet, WikiText-103, Enwik8, One Billion Word **LMC fails** despite extensive head-permutation sweeps and seed counts. The authors conjecture that the loss landscape becomes too complex at scale for a single linear path to connect independently trained basins.
- **Matching-robustness observation.** Models that exhibit LMC (under first-Transformer-layer or full-model re-init) also tend to show better generalization performance, hinting at a possible correlation between loss-landscape flatness and generalization.
- **Visualizations (Appendix J.5).** The full permutation × interpolation surface shows that poor permutations can degrade performance substantially, underscoring the importance of accurate matching.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The interaction of **PE × functional-equivalence symmetry group** is genuinely under-explored; prior work (Tran et al. 2025, Knyazev et al. 2025) only handles vanilla MHA, and Theus et al. (2025) overlook QK/VO symmetry. The closed-form identification of the RoPE symmetry group as `S_h × H(d_h) × GL(d_h)` with `H(d_h) ≅ (C×)^{d_h/2}` is a clean, non-trivial result.
- **Rigor.** The general-MHA proof (Theorem 4.1) is non-trivial — it uses exponential-polynomial theory to extract linear independence of heads. The reduction from Theorem 4.1 to MHA_RoPE is a careful corollary. The construction of `H(d_h)` as the residual symmetry group and the verification of its abelian / multiplicative-group structure is rigorous.
- **Empirical depth.** Strong points: (i) wide architecture/dataset coverage; (ii) clean ablation isolating Stage 1 vs. Stage 2 vs. gradient refinement; (iii) honest report of LMC **failure** on large-scale decoder-only LMs. Weak points: (i) barrier ratios are reported as percentages without absolute baselines, making it hard to judge whether 7-12% is "good"; (ii) only 3 seeds per configuration; (iii) Llama is only tested with RoPE (no APE comparison), limiting cross-PE conclusions for that architecture; (iv) full-model re-init may be sensitive to FFN alignment (handled by Ainsworth et al.'s method, not the proposed one).
- **Reproducibility.** The algorithm is described in detail (Algorithm 1, Equations 5-6, Lemmas G.1-G.3) but the abstract / excerpt does not state whether code will be released. The full set of appendices is encouraging; many LMC papers lack the algorithmic granularity shown here.
- **Significance.** The theoretical result is the strongest contribution: a clear, intuitive, mathematically grounded explanation of why RoPE works. The LMC piece is secondary — it is more of a "showcase" application, and the failure on large LMs is a useful negative result. The "RoPE → more expressive" claim is now backed by a clean algebraic argument rather than empirical folklore.
- **Red flags.** ⚠️ Watch for: (i) the "function class is strictly larger" claim needs the standard `H(d_h) ⊊ GL(d_h)` and a non-degeneracy argument; the paper does not explicitly quantify **how much** larger the class is — the increase could be small; (ii) LMC failure on large LMs is reported as empirical observation; the paper explicitly acknowledges that **disproving** LMC is much harder than proving it, since optimality of a matching scheme cannot be certified — this is a mature caveat; (iii) the General-MHA framework imposes stationarity and self-similarity symmetry; for MHA_RoPE these hold, but the abstraction may not capture all PE families cleanly (e.g., ALiBi's additive bias is not shift-invariant in the same sense).

## Strengths

- **Clean theoretical core.** The identification of the RoPE symmetry group as `S_h × H(d_h) × GL(d_h)` and the closed-form proof of the symmetry reduction are well-motivated and well-executed.
- **Useful negative result.** LMC **fails** on large-scale decoder-only LMs is an empirically important finding, reported with appropriate hedging.
- **Algorithmic completeness.** The two-stage algorithm, the orthogonal initialization, the closed-form `H(d_h)` decomposition, and the Brent-method scalar optimization together give a complete, reproducible procedure.
- **Honest, broad experiments.** 8+ datasets, 4 re-init schemes, 5 architectures, 3 seeds; ablation tables; visualizations.
- **Bridges two subfields.** Connects functional-equivalence / permutation-symmetry theory to the loss-landscape / LMC literature in a way that has not been done for PE.

## Limitations

- **PE coverage.** Only sinusoidal APE and RoPE are analyzed; ALiBi, NoPE, and hybrid PEs (e.g., YaRN, position-interpolated RoPE) are not characterized. The "RoPE strictly reduces the symmetry group" claim therefore does not extend to the broader modern PE zoo.
- **No quantitative expressivity bound.** "Function class is strictly larger" is qualitative; no measure-theoretic or counting argument quantifies the gap.
- **LMC is empirical.** The empirical LMC claim does not have a theoretical guarantee; the authors explicitly note that "disproving the existence of LMC is substantially more challenging" because (a) no complete symmetry characterization exists for deep multi-layer models and (b) weight-matching optimality cannot be certified.
- **Scale mismatch with modern LLMs.** Experiments use small Transformers; Llama is tested only with 2-4 heads. The theoretical result transfers to any architecture using RoPE, but the empirical LMC picture is from small models, not frontier-scale ones.
- **Decoder-only failure is under-explained.** The paper observes that LMC fails for large decoder-only LMs but offers no clear mechanism (e.g., is it attention-only, or FFN-only, or both?).
- **Single alignment family.** The proposed method assumes head-permutation-only symmetry; residual-path symmetries (e.g., Theus et al. 2025) are not handled — the paper uses Ainsworth et al.'s standard Weight Matching for FFNs, which is a different mechanism.

## Relevance

与用户研究兴趣的相关性主要在 **Transformer 理论分析**与**位置编码机制**上：本文从对称性视角为 RoPE 优于 sinusoidal PE 提供了形式化（不依赖经验性调参）的依据，并将其应用拓展到**损失景观几何与模型合并（LMC）**这一与训练动力学和实际部署密切相关的话题，对关注 Transformer 结构、PE 设计、模型合并 / weight averaging 的读者具有较高的参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the `H(d_h) ⊊ GL(d_h)` symmetry reduction scale with the rotary base `θ`, head dimension, or sequence length in a way that matches empirical extrapolation performance of RoPE-based LLMs (LLaMA, Qwen, DeepSeek)?
- Can the same group-theoretic framework be applied to **ALiBi**, **NoPE**, and **relative attention bias** to produce a clean ranking of PEs by symmetry group size?
- Is the LMC failure on large decoder-only LMs **driven** by attention (QK) or by FFN / LayerNorm? Targeted ablations swapping one component at a time could isolate the cause.
- Does the matching algorithm generalize to LLM-scale models (e.g., merging independently fine-tuned RoPE-based LLaMA / Qwen variants), or only to the small Transformers used in the paper?
- Could one **design a new PE** by explicitly choosing a target symmetry group — e.g., a smaller `H` than RoPE for further expressivity, or a larger symmetry for guaranteed LMC?
- Is there a theoretical link between the size of the symmetry group and the typical loss barrier of SGD solutions? (Smaller symmetry → fewer equivalent minima → harder to linearly connect.)
- Does the empirical correlation between matching-robustness and generalization (claimed in Appendix L) survive larger-scale and seed-controlled experiments?
