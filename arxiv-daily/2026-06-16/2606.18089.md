> **一句话总结：** 本文用"层级潜变量选择模型"把 LLM 推理的 SFT+RL 后训练机制形式化为组合泛化(技能 + 路由原子模块的级联选择),理论上证明 SFT 提供"原料"、RL 借助奖励把纠缠的复合轨迹解构为可重组的原子模块,并在合成字符串任务上以受控实验验证:复合轨迹训练优于孤立原子训练(在 L=4 OOD 上 +27.8%),且 SFT/RL 数据应"分工不重叠"。

> **TL;DR:** The paper models LLM reasoning as a **hierarchical latent selection** process: a problem descriptor induces a cascade of discrete latent variables that select reusable **atomic modules** — local *skills* (e.g., a transformation) and *routing mechanisms* (e.g., how intermediates are reused/composed). It then proves (Theorem 3.1, Proposition 3.2/3.3, Theorem 3.4) that SFT and RL play **asymmetric, complementary roles** — SFT supplies compositional traces whose atoms remain *statistically entangled* (a hidden-support obstruction), while RL re-samples those traces under reward and **enriches the identification-critical local events**, effectively decomposing the trace. Controlled synthetic string-transformation experiments (24 skills + 10 routers) confirm four findings: (1) RL on compound traces gives **+40.3% avg** OOD gain over SFT-only and **+27.8%** over atomic-only RL at L=4; (2) re-injecting atoms in isolation does not restore deeper generalization — only re-injecting compositions does; (3) the same learning mechanism applies to routing as to skills; (4) the **disjoint SFT/RL composition sets** (CSFT ∩ CRL = ∅) yield the strongest OOD performance, supporting a "SFT covers atoms, RL explores novel compositions" curriculum. Open-source Qwen3-4B vs Qwen3-4B-Thinking analyses show the same modular-recombination signature on real math.

## 核心贡献

- 提出**层级潜变量选择模型**(Hierarchical Latent Selection Model):将一条推理轨迹视为对"原子模块"——**技能**(局部操作,如加法/字符串变换)**与路由机制**(中间结果如何被选择、重用、组合)——的级联式离散选择。这是把 SFT+RL 成功的机制正式归结为**组合泛化**的首次系统尝试。
- **理论结果**(Theorem 3.1 / 3.4 + Proposition 3.2 / 3.3):在"局部可区分 + 选择集受限 + 邻域覆盖"等温和条件下,潜变量选择层级可从观测分布中**可识别**;一旦被识别,只要训练中本地见证过兼容的接口(局部见证条件 10),模块就能在新组合上正确重组。Proposition 3.2 给出 SFT 的**隐藏支持阻碍**:SFT 永远不能"鉴定"出 SFT 轨迹支持外的关键局部事件。
- **RL 分解机制**:Proposition 3.3 证明当某局部事件 E 既**可由当前策略回滚到**(reachability)又**对奖励有信息量**(reward informativeness)时,奖励正回滚会以 ∝ p̂₀(E)(1−p̂₀(E))ΔE 的强度富集 E——这正是 SFT"原料"被 RL 解构成可重组原子的形式化机制。
- **受控合成实验系统化验证理论**:在合成字符串任务(24 技能 + 10 路由)上,系统干预 SFT/RL 的数据结构,分离出"材料 vs. 分解"以及"算法–数据配对"两种因素,得出**SFT 应覆盖全部原子、SFT/RL 数据应"分工不重叠"**的实操协议。
- **真实模型辅助证据**:在 Qwen3-4B / Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507 的 643 道 MATH-500/AIME/AMC/GSM8K 题目上,观察到 RL 调优后**短 skill n-gram (n=2,3) 的多样性显著上升**——即"原子接口能跨题重组"的真实类比,补充了合成实验的外部效度。

## Key Contributions

- A **hierarchical latent selection model** of LLM reasoning, decomposing each trace into a cascade of discrete latent variables that choose *atomic skills* (local operations) and *routing mechanisms* (how intermediate information is selected, reused, composed). This is the first unified formalization of the SFT+RL post-training pipeline as compositional generalization.
- A **three-part theory** connecting identification, hidden support, and composition:
  - **Theorem 3.1 (identification):** Under local-distinguishability, restricted-choice, and neighborhood-coverage conditions, the latent selection hierarchy and its adjacency are identifiable up to component-wise relabeling.
  - **Proposition 3.2 (SFT hidden support):** Any identification-critical local event outside the SFT-revealed neighborhood cannot be certified from SFT alone — a structural reason SFT "supplies material but cannot decompose it."
  - **Proposition 3.3 (RL enrichment):** When an SFT-hidden event is rollout-reachable and reward-informative, reward-positive rollouts strictly enrich it with mass proportional to p̂₀(E)(1−p̂₀(E))ΔE.
  - **Theorem 3.4 (composition via local witnesses):** If every latent-parent configuration arising under a novel descriptor has a *local witness* in training, the locally learned constraints compose consistently.
- A **practical curriculum protocol** that drops out of the theory: SFT should cover the full atomic inventory through compositional traces; RL should target compositions whose interfaces are unseen in SFT; the disjoint composition-set setting (CSFT ∩ CRL = ∅) yields the strongest OOD.
- **Empirical validation** in four controlled synthetic studies and one real-model (Qwen3) diagnostic, with concrete numbers (e.g., +40.3% OOD avg over SFT-only, +27.8% over atomic-only RL at L=4) and a mechanistic-interpretability signature (increased short skill-n-gram diversity after RL).

## Methodology

- **Hierarchical latent selection model (§2).** Each problem descriptor P induces a hierarchy S = [S₁, …, S_L] of discrete selection variables; a realization of S indexes a library of reusable atomic modules (skills + routers) and the bottom-level variables generate the observed trace D = [D₁, …, D_T]. Sampling is top-down (P → S → D); the identification proof uses a *bottom-up* view where higher-level variables are discrete summaries of their children (Remark 2.1).
- **Formalism.** Defines the *training support* Ωsupp and a *compositional space* Ωcomp with Ωsupp ⊊ Ωcomp ⊆ ΩCP; compositional generalization is matching p⋆(D|P) on Ωcomp given matching on Ωsupp (Definition 2.2). The "local witness" set W_U(v_{Ch(U)}) records the values of U observed under compatible training descriptors — the key object for Theorem 3.4.
- **Identification conditions (Condition C.1).** Three structural assumptions: (i) local distinguishability (each selection variable leaves an anchor-like footprint in nearby tokens/children), (ii) restricted choice sets (the combinatorial space is heavily pruned by validity), (iii) neighborhood coverage (each module occurs in enough distinct parent/child neighborhoods to decouple it from typical co-occurrences). Each has a worked math example (e.g., `2(x+3)=14` → "divide first" vs "expand first" produces different downstream cues).
- **Empirical testbed.** Synthetic string-transformation task (Yuan et al., 2025) extended with **10 new atomic routing mechanisms** (e.g., g₁₃(y₁,y₂)=y₁ vs g₂₃(y₁,y₂)=y₁+y₂). 24 deterministic string functions are atomic skills; compositional depth L controls difficulty. SFT and RL may train on either atomic modules (L=1) or compound traces (L≥2), enabling controlled support manipulation.
- **Open-source model diagnostic (§4.6).** On Qwen3-4B vs Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507 over 643 math problems (MATH-500, AIME 2024/2025, AMC, GSM8K), traces are mapped to abstract skill sequences and short-skill-n-gram diversity is compared between SFT-only and SFT+RL models.

## Results & Highlights

- **Finding 1 — RL decomposes and recombines.** With both SFT and RL restricted to L_train=3 compound traces, RL adds **+43.7%** atomic-skill accuracy and **+40.3%** avg OOD gain over SFT-only (Figure 3). The SFT-vs-RL gap is much larger on unseen than seen compositions (Figure 4).
- **Finding 1 (continued) — Compound > atomic for RL data.** Same SFT init, 300 RL steps, eval on L=4 OOD compound traces:
  - SFT baseline: **4.8%**
  - SFT+RL with **atomic** modules (L=1): **14.8%** (+10.0)
  - SFT+RL with **compound** traces (L=3): **42.6%** (+37.8)
  - → **Compound-trace advantage: +27.8%** (Table 1). This isolates the "RL needs compositions to learn interfaces" effect.
- **Finding 2 — Atom knowledge is necessary but insufficient.** Removing 8/24 atomic skills from RL training degrades generalization; re-injecting **isolated** atomic skills does not generalize to depth; only re-injecting **compositional** traces containing the held-out atoms restores performance (Figure 5). "Atom-in-isolation cannot teach recombination."
- **Finding 3 — Same mechanism for routing.** Removing one atomic routing mechanism during RL degrades OOD at L=3,4; re-injecting *lower-depth* routing compositions recovers more than deeper ones — i.e., **simpler compositions are more diagnostic** for recovering the underlying router (Figure 6).
- **Finding 4 — Disjoint SFT/RL data dominates OOD.** Four SFT/RL set relationships (CSFT ⊃ CRL, CSFT ⊂ CRL, partial overlap, disjoint) yield negligible differences on seen compositions but **dramatic** differences on unseen: the **CSFT ∩ CRL = ∅** (disjoint) setting is the strongest (Figure 7). Atom-coverage manipulation in SFT (Table 2): excluding 4 atoms from SFT hurts OOD-2 from 0.67→0.52 and OOD-3 from 0.55→0.36 after RL — i.e., SFT atom coverage is a strong predictor of post-RL OOD.
- **Complementary real-model signature.** Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507 (RL-tuned) shows **richer diversity in short skill n-grams (n=2,3)** than Qwen3-4B (SFT-only), without obvious long-template duplication — a real-world analogue of "RL enables interface recombination across problems."
- **Curriculum takeaway.** SFT should ensure full atomic-module coverage through *compositional* traces; RL should focus on novel compositions *outside* the SFT support; minimize SFT–RL overlap to maximize exploration.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The *synthesis* is the contribution: identifying SFT+RL's success with *compositional generalization*, formalizing it as latent-variable identifiability, and proving asymmetric roles is a genuine intellectual move. The individual ingredients (latent selection models, identifiability, witness-based composition, "RL generalizes SFT memorizes" empirical pattern) are known; the paper's value is in unifying them with formal guarantees. The skill/router split is a useful reframing of mixture-of-experts intuitions.
- **Rigor.** Theoretically strong: Theorem 3.1's identifiability conditions are stated concretely with worked examples; Propositions 3.2 and 3.3 cleanly separate the "SFT hidden support" obstruction from the "RL enrichment" mechanism; Theorem 3.4's local-witness condition is a precise compositional guarantee. The proof sketches in Appendix C/D are needed to fully evaluate the identifiability result, but the informal statements and intuitions are tight.
- **Reproducibility.** The synthetic task is from Yuan et al. 2025 (cited); the paper adds 10 new routing mechanisms and the experiment scripts are presumably released (to be confirmed). Open-source Qwen3 models are publicly available. A real concern: the **specific atom labels** (24 skills, 10 routers) and the depth-vs-difficulty curve are non-trivial to reproduce outside the authors' codebase.
- **Significance.** The SFT/RL data-pairing protocol is the most directly actionable result for industry post-training pipelines. If it generalizes from synthetic to frontier-scale, it is a curriculum-design lever. The "disjoint SFT/RL" prescription is *contrarian* to the common practice of re-using the same SFT corpus for RL rollouts.
- **Red flags.**
  - **Synthetic vs. real reasoning gap.** The strongest empirical claims (the +27.8% compound-vs-atomic RL gap, the +40.3% OOD improvement) are on a synthetic string-transformation task where the atomic modules are *defined by the experimenter*. Real math/code reasoning traces are messier: atoms are not obviously discrete, identifiability may not hold, and the "local distinguishability" assumption (anchor footprints) is far less clean. The Qwen3 n-gram analysis in §4.6 is suggestive but does not directly test the identifiability theorem.
  - **Hidden-support vs. exploration trade-off.** The theory prescribes CSFT ∩ CRL = ∅, but in practice SFT data is often used to *initialize* RL rollouts (e.g., rejection-sampling fine-tuning). Disjointness may sacrifice useful inductive bias from SFT — the paper does not quantify this trade-off.
  - **RL algorithm dependence.** Proposition 3.3's enrichment guarantee is framed abstractly; the actual RL algorithm (PPO, GRPO, REINFORCE, etc.) matters a great deal for whether the gradient signal concentrates on identification-critical events. The paper does not ablate RL algorithms.
  - **Reward informativeness is assumed.** "Verifiers actually prefer E-containing traces" is a strong empirical claim that requires high-quality verifiers. Spurious-reward failure modes (Shao et al. 2025, cited) are not analyzed.
  - **No real-LLM OOD benchmark.** The paper's main prescription is never tested on a frontier reasoning benchmark (MATH, AIME, LiveCodeBench) with a real post-trained model. The Qwen3 n-gram diagnostic is suggestive but not a controlled intervention.
  - **Curriculum hyperparameter.** "SFT should cover atoms; RL should explore novel compositions" sounds right but is a *meta-recipe*: it does not specify *how much* novel-composition data is needed, how to measure "SFT support," or how to adapt it as the policy improves. The paper explicitly flags this as future work.

## Strengths

- A **unifying theoretical lens** for the SFT+RL pipeline that the field has so far treated as an engineering recipe.
- **Concrete, actionable curriculum** for SFT vs. RL data design (disjoint composition sets, SFT covers atoms, RL explores off-support), easy to test in industry pipelines.
- **Clean empirical isolation** of "material" (SFT) vs. "decomposition" (RL) effects via support manipulation — a methodological template that should be replicated.
- **Identifiability-style guarantees** are rare in the SFT+RL literature; the paper brings the rigor of causal discovery to LLM reasoning.
- **Open-source model diagnostic** (Qwen3) ties the synthetic findings to a real-LLM signature, partially addressing the synthetic-vs-real gap.
- **Skill/router separation** is a useful mechanistic vocabulary that could be reused in interpretability work (e.g., sparse autoencoders, attribution).

## Limitations

- Empirical core is on **synthetic string transformations** with experimenter-defined atoms; behavior on real LLM reasoning (math, code, multi-step QA) is not directly tested.
- The **identifiability conditions** (local distinguishability, restricted choice sets, neighborhood coverage) are *strong*; real reasoning traces likely violate at least one.
- The paper does not ablate **RL algorithm** (PPO vs. GRPO vs. DPO vs. RFT) or **reward model**; the theory is silent on which optimization dynamics realize the enrichment guarantee.
- The proposed SFT/RL data-split protocol is shown to be "effective" but **magnitude, sensitivity, and scaling** are not characterized.
- No comparison against alternative **post-training curricula** (curriculum SFT, rejection-sampling fine-tuning, RL-only, iterative DPO) on real reasoning benchmarks.
- The "disjoint SFT/RL" prescription may **under-utilize SFT data** in practice (e.g., for rollout initialization), and the trade-off is not analyzed.
- The proposed extension to **richer domains** (math, code, tool use) is explicitly future work — the paper does not deliver frontier-scale evidence.

## Relevance

与用户的研究兴趣(LLM 推理、后训练方法、组合泛化与机制可解释性)直接相关,本文把工程上经验性的 SFT+RL 流水线提升为带可识别性保证的"潜变量选择"理论,并基于此提出了具体的 SFT/RL 数据分工协议,直接对接后训练数据设计与课程学习——对训练后阶段的可解释性研究也提供了可借鉴的词汇(技能 vs. 路由)。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the **identifiability** of the hierarchical latent selection model survive under weaker conditions (finite-sample bounds, continuous latents, noisy traces), and how do the conditions scale with the size of the atom library?
- Does the **compound > atomic** finding (the +27.8% gap) replicate on a frontier reasoning benchmark (MATH, AIME, LiveCodeBench) with a *real* post-trained model, and what is the magnitude of the effect at frontier scale?
- How does the **disjoint SFT/RL data protocol** compare to the common alternative of using SFT data for RL rollout initialization (rejection-sampling fine-tuning, RFT) in terms of OOD accuracy and sample efficiency?
- Can the framework **predict failure modes** of the SFT+RL pipeline (mode collapse, reward hacking, overfitting to a single composition style, length collapse) that the paper does not currently analyze?
- What is the right **granularity** of "atomic modules" (skills + routing) for real math/code traces, and can it be **discovered automatically** (e.g., via skill-mining on rollouts) rather than defined by hand?
- Can the local-witness condition (Eq. 10) be turned into a **measurable curriculum objective** — e.g., a reward for exploration that explicitly targets under-witnessed module interfaces — and does such an *identification-driven* curriculum outperform static disjoint splits?
- How does the RL enrichment guarantee (Proposition 3.3) interact with **specific RL algorithms** (PPO/GRPO/DPO) and with **sparse / uninformative rewards** (the spurious-reward setting of Shao et al. 2025)?
- Does the proposed theory extend to **multi-modal or tool-use reasoning**, where "routing" can mean tool selection, code execution, or memory retrieval — not just inter-token information flow?
