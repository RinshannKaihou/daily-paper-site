> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"领域有效性门控"的蜕变测试流水线，把候选蜕变关系（MR）经由"四条件 rubric + MR-card 可执行资产 + 二维 verdict + claim ledger"筛选为可审计的 oracle-free SciML 测试资产；在 MeshGraphNets 圆柱流 K=6 × 3 轨迹 + S4/S5 / PointMLP / PhysicsNeMo 复制中验证，并跨压缩空气翼、PINN Burgers/heat、FNO-2D 检验谓词的可迁移性与拒绝能力。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes a domain-validity-gated metamorphic-testing pipeline for SciML surrogates. A 4-condition rubric (physical basis + transformation preconditions + BC compatibility + numerical decidability) screens candidate metamorphic relations (MRs); MR-cards convert retained relations into executable assets; relation-level verdicts are read in a 2-D space (relation-violation vs domain-violation magnitude); a fail-closed claim ledger binds every number to an artifact. On MeshGraphNets cylinder-flow, node permutation is exact, mirror-y is OOD-stress (180/180 fail over K=6 × 3 trajectories), and absolute conservation is deferred because the P1 divergence operator floor (O(h), slope 0.984, R²=0.9999) is not calibratable; a reference-relative guard passes 162/162. The same predicate rejects incompressible continuity on a compressible airfoil task (density varies by 2.25×) and yields full admit/reject verdicts on FNO Burgers/heat and a 15-seed PINN roster.

## 核心贡献

- 提出**领域有效性 rubric**（四条件六准则）：候选 MR 必须同时具备物理/软件基础、变换前置条件满足、边界与输出映射兼容、且判定容差主导测量算子的数值下界（machine precision / 几何映射 floor / O(h) 离散 floor），否则 rejected / deferred / OOD-stress；这是把"看似可用"的 MR 升级为可执行 oracle-free 测试资产的把关规则。
- 设计 **MR-card 可执行资产格式**：记录 ID、source category、basis、source-case schema、follow-up transformation、preconditions、BC 兼容性、output mapping、metric、tolerance、exclusion rule、verdict classes、artifact schema、fault interpretation；自动从 MR-card 生成 transformation code、runner config、metric computation、verdict logic 与 artifact 记录。
- 引入**二维 verdict 空间**：横轴为 relation-violation 量化（V/tol 或 V/floor），纵轴为 domain-violation 分类（In-domain / Boundary / Out-of-domain，可由各 MR 的 D 分数量化）；把模型级违规与"MR 应用出域"分开，避免把数值伪迹误读为 SUT 缺陷。
- 建立**带 claim ledger 的 case-study 协议**：每条结论绑定一个可追溯工件（research_assets/runs/ 与 claim-ledger.yml），并明确 LLM 仅用于候选生成与证据组织，不参与 validity 判断。
- **MeshGraphNets 圆柱流主证据**：K=6 checkpoints × 3 held-out trajectories 上，node-permutation 精确到机器精度、mirror-y OOD-stress 180/180 fail、exact-symmetric-mesh 上 18/18 fail（明确模型级违规）；绝对守恒因 P1 离散算子 floor 在 O(h) 量级而 deferred，reference-relative 护栏 162/162 通过。
- **跨架构与跨家族复制**：same-domain S4/S5 wider/deeper MGN、新训练的 PointMLP（非消息传递）、NVIDIA PhysicsNeMo MGN（DeepMind 官方 TFRecords）全部复制相同 verdict 模式；FNO-2D Burgers/heat（24/24 周期平移 pass、24/24 周期守恒 fail、6/6 Dirichlet 平移 rejected）；PINN K=15 Burgers+heat roster。
- **第二 CFD 任务**：在 DeepMind 压缩空气翼 benchmark 上同一谓词因密度变化 2.25× 而拒绝不可压缩连续性（physical-basis gate 拒绝），并因 SU2 含攻角而拒绝 mirror-y 弦对称（BC-precondition gate 拒绝），证明 predicate 真的会因物理差异给不同 verdict。
- **算子 floor 校准**：P1 对称网格 sweep（9 个分辨率）得 slope 0.984、95% CI [0.975, 0.992]、R² = 0.9999；对部署尺度网格给出 closed-form 预测（领先阶 1.337 vs 实测 1.343，相对误差 < 0.5%）+ 由 Hessian 全局谱范数给出的 a-priori 上界。

## Key Contributions

- **Domain-validity rubric (4 conditions × 6 auditable criteria):** admits a candidate MR only when physical basis, transformation preconditions, BC compatibility, and numerical decidability (tolerance dominates the operator's intrinsic error floor) all hold.
- **MR-card executable-asset format** with basis / preconditions / output mapping / metric / tolerance / exclusion rule / typed verdict classes / artifact schema.
- **2-D relation-level verdict space** (relation-violation vs domain-violation), enabling clean separation of model inconsistencies from out-of-domain MR applications.
- **Fail-closed claim ledger** that ties every reported number to a tracked artifact and reserves LLM use for candidate generation, not validity judgment.
- **Primary MeshGraphNets cylinder-flow evidence:** K=6 × 3 trajectories × 10 mirror-y OOD-stress frames → 180/180 fail; 162/162 reference-relative conservation passes; node permutation exact on all K=6; exact-symmetric-mesh 18/18 fails.
- **Same-task multi-architecture replication:** S4/S5 wider/deeper MGN variants, a newly trained non-message-passing PointMLP, and NVIDIA PhysicsNeMo MeshGraphNet (DeepMind TFRecords) all reproduce the verdict pattern.
- **Cross-family transfer:** FNO-2D Burgers/heat (24/24 translation pass, 24/24 periodic conservation fail, 6/6 Dirichlet translation reject) and a K=15 PINN Burgers + heat roster.
- **Second CFD task:** on the DeepMind compressible airfoil benchmark, the same predicate rejects incompressible continuity at the physical-basis gate (density varies 2.25×) and rejects mirror-y chord symmetry at the BC-precondition gate (non-zero AoA).
- **Operator-floor calibration:** P1 divergence sweep on symmetric meshes gives slope 0.984 (95% CI [0.975, 0.992], R² = 0.9999); the deployed-scale floor is given in closed form (leading-order predictor within 0.5% of measured floor, plus a rigorous a-priori Hessian-spectral upper bound).

## Methodology

- **5-stage workflow:** (1) candidate sources (physical-model / computational-model / code-model — equivariance, conservation, homogeneity/scaling, composition, cross-implementation); (2) NOETHER-style organization; (3) domain-validity rubric screen; (4) convert retained relations into MR-card executable assets; (5) execute assets and record relation-level verdicts.
- **Rubric (4 conditions, none optional):** (i) physical/software basis; (ii) transformation preconditions satisfied; (iii) BC compatibility after transformation; (iv) tolerance dominates the measuring operator's intrinsic error floor (machine precision for exact relations; mapping floor for approximate geometric relations; O(h) discretization floor for continuity relations). Four decisions: retained as executable MR / retained as OOD-stress / rejected / deferred.
- **Worked examples (screening decisions):**
  - MR-1 node-permutation equivariance: graph relabeling preserves the physical mesh → retained as executable representation MR.
  - MR-2 mirror-y equivariance: retained only under boundary-compatible conditions (cylinder/inlet/outlet/wall labels stay semantically compatible after reflection).
  - MR-3 discrete-divergence boundedness: deferred or retained as qualified continuity-constraint MR depending on operator floor and tolerance provenance.
- **2-D verdict reading:** relation-violation magnitude (V/floor ratio) × domain-violation magnitude (per-relation continuous score D ∈ [0, 1), not cross-relation calibrated). Pass / fail / skip / out-of-relation-domain / numerical-tolerance issue / inconclusive.
- **Operator-floor calibration:** closed-form P1 divergence floor = geometry-weighted second-order Taylor remainder (operator is exact on affine fields, reference field is divergence-free). Leading-order analytic-Hessian predictor matches the measured floor to within 0.5%; a global spectral-norm a-priori upper bound dominates it.
- **Subjects:** MeshGraphNets K=6 roster, S4/S5 wider/deeper variants, PointMLP (newly trained, no message passing), NVIDIA PhysicsNeMo MeshGraphNet on official DeepMind cylinder_flow TFRecords; PINN K=15 (Burgers + heat); FNO-2D K=6 + K=15 admissibility roster; DeepMind compressible airfoil with a 0.33M-param PhysicsNeMo MGN trained on 6 trajectories, K=6 roster, 10 test trajectories → 60 (checkpoint, trajectory) pairs.
- **Statistics:** Wilson 95% CIs per detector; Wilcoxon signed-rank + Cliff's δ; paired bootstrap CIs for roster medians; log-log slope + 95% CI for operator-floor sweep; descriptive cell summaries where repeated cells are not independent.
- **Failure-mode injection:** independently re-implemented 10-mutant seeded-fault catalogue (5 classes: boundary-condition, mesh-adjacency, normalization-scale, temporal-rollout, physical-channel), severity sweeps (NS_double_scale s ∈ {1.1, 1.25, 1.5, 2, 4}; PC_zero_vy p ∈ {0.25, …, 1.0}) and 2 adversarial mutants. Plus a 60-entry Phase-3 unified fault catalogue (10 MGN canonical + 2 MGN adversarial + 24 PINN closed-form + 24 FNO closed-form output-level probes).
- **Comparators (secondary):** LLM-simulated expert MR design (3 expert-LLMs, 3-rater majority vote, Fleiss κ = 0.077 small-sample paradox); generic-MR scope contrast; one-shot LLM candidate generation (K = 8); rollout-accuracy diagnostic (median one-step relative L2 = 0.0216).

## Results & Highlights

- **MeshGraphNets cylinder flow (primary, K=6 × 3 trajectories):**
  - Node permutation: exact on all checkpoints (representation-contract sanity check).
  - Mirror-y OOD-stress: 180/180 fail; median V/floor 3.96; per-cell medians CI [0.743, 0.804]; rollout CI [0.0217, 0.0224]. Exact relation is downgraded to OOD-stress on the asymmetric eval mesh (cylinder off-centre 7.2 mm; reflection not a bijection).
  - Reference-relative conservation diagnostic: 162/162 pass (50% regression threshold over 9 transitions × K=6 × 3 trajectories); absolute conservation deferred because the P1 floor is non-negligible on the coarse mesh.
  - Exact mirror-y on synthetic symmetric mesh (bijection verified, type-match 1.0, offset < 10⁻¹²): 18/18 fail, relative L2 = 1.10, dominated by learned message-passing weights (normalizer control shifts only 1.1032 → 1.1014).
  - Rollout accuracy: median one-step relative L2 = 0.0216 (mean 0.044, bimodal); mirror-y violation (~0.737) is ≈ 34× the median accuracy — i.e., accuracy does not bound relation violation.
- **Multi-architecture replication (same task):** S4/S5 wider/deeper MGN (60/60 mirror OOD failures, 54/54 conservation passes, 6/6 exact-symmetry failures, 2/2 permutation passes); PointMLP (9/9 permutation passes, 10/10 mirror OOD failures, 9/9 conservation passes, 3/3 exact-symmetry failures, median rollout rel L2 = 0.0298); PhysicsNeMo MGN smoke (permutation exact, mirror-y OOD stress on smoke subset).
- **Second CFD task (compressible airfoil):** predicate rejects incompressible continuity at the physical-basis gate (density varies 2.25×) and rejects mirror-y chord symmetry at the BC-precondition gate (non-zero AoA); compressible unsteady mass conservation ∂ₜρ + ∇·(ρu) = 0 is the physically-correct replacement and gets a reference-relative diagnostic (median residual ratio 1.19 across K=6).
- **Operator-floor sweep:** slope 0.984 (95% CI [0.975, 0.992], R² = 0.9999) over 9 resolutions; closed-form floor for the deployed-scale mesh (predictor within 0.5%; a-priori upper bound dominates).
- **Seeded-fault detection (10 mutants × K=6 × 5 input seeds, 30 trials per mutant):**
  - 4 mutants detected on every cell (30/30, CI [0.89, 1.00]).
  - 5 mutants not detected on any cell (0/30, CI [0.00, 0.11]) — MA_drop_edges, NS_double_scale, TR_sign_flip, TR_double_step, PC_zero_vy.
  - MA_permute_edges: detected on S0–S3 (20/20) but not on S4/S5 (0/10), within-family detection 20/30, CI [0.49, 0.81].
  - NS_double_scale severity sweep: 0/6 detection at every s ∈ {1.1, …, 4.0}, CI [0.00, 0.39] (catalogue structurally insensitive to multiplicative output scaling).
  - PC_zero_vy knife edge: 6/6 detection at every p ≤ 0.99, then 0/6 at p = 1.0 — measure-zero blind region where the fault becomes simultaneously permutation-invariant and mirror-y-symmetric.
  - By-class localization: continuity → boundary/scale faults; symmetry → physical-channel/adjacency faults; permutation → none (preserve relabeling invariance by design).
- **FNO primary workflow (K=6, Burgers + heat):** 24/24 translation passes (max violation < 10⁻⁵); 24/24 conservation failures against a periodic discrete-conservation MR (admitted-with-reference-floor); 6/6 Dirichlet translation rejections (changes the BVP). FNO admissibility roster extended to K=15 per PDE, translation 15/15, Wilson CI [0.80, 1.00].
- **PINN roster (K=15 Burgers + K=15 heat):** MR-C 15/15 on both PDEs (CI [0.80, 1.00]); MR-B Burgers 13/15 (CI [0.62, 0.96], mean ratio 0.712), heat 7/15 (CI [0.25, 0.70], mean ratio 1.495) — Neumann-zero-flux heat BCs predict larger boundary residuals by construction.
- **Secondary baselines:** expert-LLM candidates 25 (24 unique) → 4 retained / 2 downgraded / 13 rejected / 6 deferred (76% reject/defer); generic-MR templates 3/13 admitted; one-shot LLM K=8 with panel-majority 7/8 (Fleiss κ = 0.077). Minimum-MR-SubSet witness rerun returns PASS_WITNESS (kstar = 6, signature rank 2, collapse false); 70 real ABD instances + 20 real true-fault-class rows + 3 SciML/PDE PASS_WITNESS rows audited.
- **Unified fault catalogue precision/recall:** node-permutation 1.00/1.00; conservation 1.00/0.81; mirror-y 0.94/0.55. Inter-PDE Cliff's δ = 0.73 (large), Wilcoxon p = 0.0084 for Burgers-vs-heat MR-B violation/floor ratios (physics-magnitude comparison, not a gate-reliability test).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The three-pronged claim (measurement-floor admissibility gate, typed domain-inadmissibility verdict, seeded-fault MR-as-detector diagnostic) is genuinely useful. Grounding the tolerance in the *measurement operator's own* O(h) error floor is, to my knowledge, a fresh move in the SciML-testing literature. The 2-D verdict space cleanly separates "model wrong" from "MR applied out of its domain," which is the central ambiguity in naïve SciML MT.
- **Rigor:** Strong. The claim ledger + Wilson CIs + Wilcoxon/Cliff + paired bootstraps + log-log slope with CIs are appropriate. Authors pre-register the canonical blocked list (no cross-dataset / cross-architecture pass-rate, no validated localization, no runtime/reliability/accuracy claim) and stick to it. Boundary cases (e.g., "V/floor alone cannot separate model violation from amplified geometric artifact on the asymmetric mesh, but the symmetric-mesh run resolves that ambiguity") are honestly demarcated.
- **Reproducibility:** Excellent on paper — full replication package on Zenodo, claim-ledger.yml as the authoritative artifact map, raw outputs and per-case manifests committed, Minimum-MR-SubSet reruns as external witness. Some SUTs depend on old runtimes / non-redistributable checkpoints (authors flag this).
- **Significance:** High for the SciML-testing community. The claim that "rollout accuracy does not bound relation violation" (mirror-y violation ~34× median one-step error on the same SUT) is a concrete, useful empirical signal that any SciML deployment team should internalize. The cross-task, cross-family predicate transfer (MGN / S4-S5 / PointMLP / PhysicsNeMo / FNO / PINN / compressible airfoil) is the strongest part of the paper.
- **Red flags / honest concerns:**
  - "Node permutation holds to machine precision" is structurally expected for any well-implemented message-passing model and is correctly reported as a *pipeline sanity check*, not a finding — but a sceptical reader might still want to see one non-trivial case where it doesn't hold (the S4/S5 + PointMLP + PhysicsNeMo pass count does effectively establish this).
  - 5/10 mutants detected is a stress-test pattern, not a validated localization model — authors are explicit. Mirror-y recall 0.55 is low and the knife-edge PC_zero_vy blind spot is a real limitation.
  - The 2-D verdict space is per-relation normalized; D is not cross-relation calibrated (authors flag this). Cross-relation calibration is explicitly future work.
  - Primary MGN evidence is one architecture family + one dataset (DeepMind cylinder_flow). Compressible airfoil is "bounded K=6 MPS-scale roster"; PINN/FNO evidence is "applicability context" not "cross-SUT rate."
  - Generic-MR and LLM baselines are scope contrasts, not defeated competitors (authors flag this). Inter-rater Fleiss κ = 0.077 in the LLM panel is a small-sample paradox, not strong agreement.
  - The airfoil K=6 roster is at a "bounded CPU budget" with large one-step error — reported only as training-state diagnostic, which is honest but underlines that the airfoil result is a predicate-discrimination demonstration, not a useful scientific finding about AeroGraphNet-class models.

## Strengths

- The rubric as a *fail-closed* gate (deferred / rejected / OOD-stress are first-class outcomes) is the right design philosophy for SciML where every "failure" could be a numerical artifact.
- The two-dimensional verdict space is a real conceptual advance over scalar accuracy / residual / UQ, and the worked reading of the four cylinder-flow pilots (P1–P4) is illuminating.
- Closed-form operator floor with a-priori upper bound is the kind of mathematical grounding that physics-motivated MT has been missing.
- Explicit, often uncomfortable scope boundaries (K=6 roster is not "all neural fluid surrogates," 5/10 detection is a stress test not validation, knife-edge blind spot is bounded with Wilson CIs).
- Multi-checkpoint × multi-trajectory × multi-architecture × cross-family × cross-task design within the stated bounds.
- Claim ledger + raw artifacts + Zenodo replication package = reproducible within stated boundaries.

## Limitations

- Primary subject is one architecture family (MeshGraphNets) on one dataset (DeepMind cylinder_flow); cross-architecture work is same-task only.
- Compressible airfoil is a bounded K=6 MPS-scale roster, not full-scale PhysicsNeMo / AeroGraphNet / DoMINO evidence.
- General unstructured-mesh divergence bound (not just the symmetric-mesh closed form) is future work.
- Cross-relation D-score calibration is open; current D is a per-relation normalized coordinate.
- Seeded-fault catalogue is author-implemented from the witness taxonomy; detection rates do not bound real-world fault detection effectiveness.
- PC_zero_vy measure-zero blind spot, multiplicative output scaling blind spot, and the S4/S5 failure on MA_permute_edges are honest but real detector coverage gaps.
- LLM/expert-MR baselines are scope contrasts (one prompt per generator, not designed for SciML); small-sample Fleiss κ = 0.077 in the LLM panel.
- Authors' own generative-AI disclosure notes LLM assistance for code scaffolding, prose copy-editing, and reference cross-checking.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the closed-form P1 divergence floor be extended to general unstructured meshes (the explicit future-work item), and how sensitive is the gate to mesh anisotropy in industrial CFD?
- How does the rubric behave on more exotic SciML (molecular dynamics surrogates, climate emulators, multiphysics reactors) where conservation laws are stricter or where the "correct" relation depends on a much larger PDE family?
- Could the MR-card schema be standardized (JSON-Schema / OpenAPI) so MR assets are machine-readable across SciML benchmarks — and could this feed into a CI pipeline as a regular regression check?
- Is the knife-edge PC_zero_vy blind spot a general phenomenon (i.e., do *all* geometric-detector suites share measure-zero invariances) and what additional MR families systematically close them?
- The 5/10 mutant detection rate is a stress test, not validation; what would a *real-world* fault-injection study on a production SciML pipeline look like and how would precision/recall change?
- Can the per-relation D-score be made cross-relation calibrated (e.g., via a learned calibration on a curated set of OOD probes), enabling a single 2-D dashboard across MR classes?
- The LLM-simulated-expert baseline (76% reject/defer) and the no-superiority boundary invite a follow-up human-expert study: do expert reviewers propose materially different MR families, or does the rubric correctly screen expert-generated candidates at a similar rate?
- For deployment: what is the wall-clock cost of running the full admit/reject/execute pipeline on a single surrogate checkpoint, and is it feasible as a CI gate on every commit?
