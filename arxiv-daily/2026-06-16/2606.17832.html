Key finding

LLM predictive beliefs drift in the first few resamples, then self-stabilize — and that two-stage dynamic can be erased with a short prompt-time seeding trick plus a tiny self-consistency loss.

Why this matters

It reframes LLM calibration as a martingale-consistency problem and ships two concrete tools: a PPR + EMD diagnostic that practitioners can run on their own data, and a self-consistency loss that amortizes stabilization into a 2-epoch LoRA fine-tune — so deployed inference stays zero-shot.

Critical edge

The martingale property is necessary, not sufficient for Bayesian inference — a self-consistent model can still hallucinate, and calibration gains are not monotonic across model/dataset pairs (e.g. some AM-PPR ECE entries on OLMo-3-7B beat PPR+NoSEED only marginally).

Methodology

For an MCQA prompt x_Q with finite answer set A, define the history-adapted predictive θ_n := p_φ(A_{n+1}=· | A_{1:n}, x_Q) ∈ P(A). The sequence is a martingale iff E[θ_{n+k} | F_n] = θ_n a.s.; by Doob's theorem, a bounded martingale converges to a limit θ_∞ — the martingale posterior Π_∞ (Fong et al. 2023).

PPR protocol. The model is prompted to (1) declare a probability over choices, (2) emit 100 single-letter answers separated by \n, (3) sample i.i.d. The empirical frequency θ̄_N is the MLE of its own belief trajectory.

Answer seeding. Prepend m=1 or m=5 i.i.d. seed answers from a plain call. By the law of total expectation, E_S[E[θ_∞ | x̃_Q]] = E[θ_∞ | x_Q] — the unseeded target is preserved.

SC loss (amortization). Monte-Carlo estimate of future vs. past marginals and minimize cross-entropy with a stop-gradient on the future estimator:

L_SC(φ) = E_{k₁<k₂} H( stopgrad( p̂_{φ,n}^{k₂} ), p̂_{φ,n}^{k₁} )

LoRA (r=16, α=8) for 2 epochs. A small REINFORCE correction (β=0.01) handles MC bias.

Theorem 4.2. Under (A1) uniform consistency, (A2) expressivity, (A3) positive pair probability, post-SC martingale residual ≤ C√ε on 1-Lipschitz test functions.

Strengths

  • Clean conceptual framing: martingale property as a coherence diagnostic, made operational.
  • Direct measurement of predictive distributions over a discrete answer space — sidesteps the sampling artifact in Falck et al. (2024).
  • Two complementary interventions (prompt seeding + SC loss), correctly identified as additive.
  • Practical deployment win: AM-PPR stays zero-shot at inference, sidestepping the long-rollout cost of vanilla PPR.
  • Honest scope: the paper itself flags the necessary-not-sufficient nature of martingale consistency.
  • Cross-architecture validation on two instruction-tuned open-source models.

Limitations

  • MCQA only. Open-ended generation, math, code, and reasoning benchmarks (GPQA, MMLU-Pro) are not tested.
  • Stable ≠ correct. Martingale consistency does not fix hallucinations, factual errors, or sycophancy.
  • Prompt-dependent. PPR requires careful prompt engineering; per-model prompt tuning is needed (Figs. 3 vs 4).
  • Small samples: 50–200 questions per dataset for evaluation; no error bars on accuracy/ECE numbers.
  • Fine-tuning cost for AM-PPR is not quantified vs. its zero-shot inference benefit.
  • No free-lunch baseline comparison (temperature scaling, label smoothing, prompt ensembling).
  • Theoretical assumptions A1–A3 of Theorem 4.2 are not validated empirically; tightness of C√ε is unknown.

Questions for further exploration

  • Does the drift-then-stabilize pattern also emerge inside chain-of-thought traces, where the "answer space" is the reasoning-step vocabulary?
  • How does AM-PPR interact with RLHF / DPO objectives — is SC loss compatible with the KL constraint or does it fight preference optimization?
  • Does the SC objective generalize to larger reasoning models (32B, 70B, GPT-5-class), or does burn-in change qualitatively with scale?
  • The Π_∞ "martingale posterior" is interpreted as a posterior, but exchangeability is not assumed — what is the correct epistemic reading? A de Finetti partial-exchangeability view?
  • Can a tighter bound than C√ε be proved under weaker assumptions (e.g. drop A3 with a Bernstein-type argument)?
  • Would a multi-query (joint) martingale across related questions give a stronger "Bayesian" interpretation than per-query analysis?
  • Can AM-PPR be repurposed as a self-distillation signal for open-ended generation, where the per-step distribution is over the next token?
  • How sensitive is the drift to the specific PPR prompt (the "do not condition" instruction, anti-pattern language, format requirements)?
  • What is the interaction with retrieval / RAG — does the burn-in disappear (a "lookup" effect) or persist (a deeper autoregressive property)?
  • Does the martingale property hold in multi-turn dialogue, where the "answer" is a turn, not a letter?
