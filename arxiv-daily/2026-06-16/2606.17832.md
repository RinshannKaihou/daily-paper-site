> **一句话总结：** 该论文在多选题问答（MCQA）这一典型使用场景下系统地检验 LLM 预测信念的鞅性质（martingale property）：通过"提示式预测重采样 (PPR)"观察到 LLM 存在"早期信念漂移—后期自稳定"的双阶段现象，并提出"种子答案提示"和"自一致性损失 (SC loss) 微调"两种互补干预手段，使模型在零样本（zero-shot）下即可输出近似鞅的稳定预测分布，从而提升不确定性校准而不牺牲准确率。

> **TL;DR:** The paper studies whether LLM predictive beliefs in multiple-choice QA satisfy the martingale property required for implicit Bayesian inference. Using **Prompted Predictive Resampling (PPR)**, the authors expose a two-stage dynamic — early belief drift (martingale violation) followed by self-stabilization. They then propose (i) **answer seeding** (prepending i.i.d. samples to the prompt) for fast prompt-time stabilization, and (ii) a **self-consistency (SC) loss** that amortizes stabilization into the model via LoRA fine-tuning. Empirically, the amortized model (AM-PPR) reaches lower EMD and better calibration (ECE, NLL, Brier) than vanilla PPR, often at comparable or higher accuracy, on CSQA, AI2-ARC and TinyMMLU with LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct and OLMo-3-7B-Instruct.

## 核心贡献

- 将 Falck et al. (2024) 的鞅/贝叶斯视角从**合成上下文学习**场景拓展到 LLM 最常见的**多选题问答 (MCQA)** 部署场景。
- 提出 **PPR (Prompted Predictive Resampling)**：通过结构化提示让 LLM 自回归地连续输出 100 个答案（用换行符 `\n` 严格分隔），从而在离散答案空间上**精确**追踪预测信念轨迹 `θ_n(x_Q) = p_ϕ(A_{n+1} | A_{1:n}, x_Q)`。
- 实证揭示 **"早期漂移—后期自稳定"** 两阶段动态：边际预测分布在 n ≈ 5 步前显著漂移（违反鞅性质），之后趋于稳定（类似 MCMC burn-in）。
- 提出 **答案种子化 (answer seeding)** 策略：先用普通 LLM 调用 i.i.d. 采样 m 个种子答案并附在 prompt 中，显著加速稳定收敛。
- 设计 **自一致性损失 (Self-Consistency loss, L_SC)**：把"未来稳定分布"作为"早期分布"的目标，用 stop-gradient 交叉熵进行 LoRA 微调，从而**摊销** burn-in 阶段。
- 给出 **Theorem 4.2**：在 (A1) 一致性、(A2) 表示能力、(A3) 正概率假设下，SC 训练后鞅残差 ≤ C√ε，理论保证训练后序列接近鞅。
- 在 LLaMA-3.1-8B / OLMo-3-7B 上 CSQA、ARC-Challenge、TinyMMLU 三个基准上证明方法在显著降低 EMD 漂移的同时**保持或提升**准确率与 AUC。

## Key Contributions

- **Extension of martingale analysis to MCQA**: moves the Falck et al. (2024) martingale test from synthetic ICL tasks to a realistic, generic LLM usage regime (multiple-choice QA), where the answer space is finite and the predictive distribution is directly accessible from logits.
- **Prompted Predictive Resampling (PPR)**: an autoregressive answer-generation protocol (100 answers per query, separated by `\n`) that exposes the belief trajectory `θ_n ∈ P(A)` over the answer simplex. The protocol explicitly forbids conditioning on prior answers in the prompt, ensuring the per-step distribution is the model's own predictive marginal.
- **Empirical two-stage dynamic**: PPR reveals pronounced drift for n ≤ 5, followed by self-stabilization. The drift is a *necessary*-condition violation of the martingale property; stabilization is only a *necessary*-condition satisfaction.
- **Answer seeding**: prepend m=1 or m=5 i.i.d. seed answers to the prompt. Theoretically grounded by the law of total expectation — the seed-marginalized limit `E_S[E[θ_∞ | x̃_Q]] = E[θ_∞ | x_Q]` keeps the unseeded target distribution intact, while empirically eliminating early drift.
- **Self-Consistency loss (L_SC)**: cross-entropy `H(stopgrad(p̂_{ϕ,n}^{k2}), p̂_{ϕ,n}^{k1})` between future (k₂) and past (k₁) Monte-Carlo estimators of the marginal predictive distribution. Implemented with LoRA (r=16, α=8) over 2 epochs.
- **Theorem 4.2 (SC-bounded martingale residual)**: under uniform consistency (A1), expressivity (A2) and positive pair probability (A3), the post-SC training martingale residual is bounded by `C√ε` for some universal constant C.
- **Empirical validation across two model families**: LLaMA-3.1-8B-Instruct and OLMo-3-7B-Instruct on CSQA, AI2-ARC-Challenge, TinyMMLU. AM-PPR dominates PPR in EMD on all (model × dataset) cells; combined with seed5 it is best.

## Methodology

**Setup.** For a multiple-choice question Q with finite answer set A, define a history-dependent predictive `θ_n(x_Q) := p_ϕ(A_{n+1}=·|A_{1:n}, x_Q) ∈ P(A)`. The sequence `{θ_n}_{n≥1}` is adapted to the filtration `F_n = σ(x_Q, A_{1:n})`. The **martingale property** requires `E[θ_{n+k}(x_Q) | F_n] = θ_n(x_Q)` a.s. (equivalently c.i.d. by Berti et al. 2004). By Doob's theorem, a martingale bounded in the simplex converges a.s. to a limit `θ_∞`, whose law `Π_∞(·|x_Q)` is the **martingale posterior** (Fong et al. 2023).

**PPR protocol.** A structured prompt instructs the LLM to (1) assign a probability weight over answer choices, (2) output 100 single-letter answers separated by `\n`, (3) sample each line i.i.d. from its declared distribution. Crucially the prompt forbids conditioning on previously generated answers and forbids repeating/cyclic patterns. The empirical frequency `θ̄_N` of the generated sequence is the MLE of the model's belief.

**Answer seeding.** Sample m seeds `S_{1:m} ~ p_ϕ(·|Q)` (no PPR instruction) and use prompt `x̃_Q = [I; Q; S_{1:m}]`. The law of total expectation shows `E_S[E[θ_∞|x̃_Q]] = E[θ_∞|x_Q]`, so seeded estimation is *anchored* to the unseeded target. Intuition: the seeds supply a "mean-field" preview of the model's own uncertainty, eliminating the need for the model to discover its own beliefs through resampling.

**SC loss (amortization).** Sample horizon pairs `k₁ ∈ {1..5}` and `δ ∈ {1..6−k₁}`, set `k₂ = k₁+δ`. Use Monte-Carlo rollouts of length N+6 to estimate future marginals `p̂_{ϕ,n}^{k}`:
```
p̂_{ϕ,n}^{k}(·) := (1/J) Σ_j p_ϕ(· | A_{n+1:n+k-1}^{(j)}, a_{1:n}, Q)
```
Loss: `L_SC(ϕ) = E_{k₁<k₂} H(stopgrad(p̂_{ϕ,n}^{k₂}), p̂_{ϕ,n}^{k₁})`. This pulls early beliefs toward the (pseudo ground-truth) late beliefs. A small policy-gradient (REINFORCE-style) correction term with weight β=0.01 mitigates MC bias; this is an optimization heuristic, not a theoretical ingredient.

**Estimation efficiency.** PPR (no SC) requires long rollouts (N≫1) for `q* ≈ θ̂_N`; AM-PPR can use `q* ≈ θ̂_1` — the immediate next-token distribution — because SC training has made the first-token belief already a good approximation of the limit. This is the **practical deployment payoff** of the paper.

**Theorem 4.2 (informal).** Under (A1) uniform consistency, (A2) KL-bounded pair distributions, (A3) positive pair probability, then for the SC-trained `ϕ̂`, the martingale residual is `≤ C√ε` for any 1-Lipschitz test functions `h, f`. The proof treats the SC loss as a proper proxy for the c.i.d. condition; the bound is conditional on the model family being expressive enough.

**Training.** LoRA (r=16, α=8, dropout 0.05, all linear layers), AdamW, cosine schedule, lr 2e-6 (LLaMA) / 1e-4 (OLMo), warmup 5, micro-batch 4, grad-accum 4, BF16, 2 epochs, 32 effective trajectories per update. FSDP on 2× RTX A6000 (48GB each).

## Results & Highlights

**EMD reduction (Figure 2).** Across LLaMA-3.1-8B and OLMo-3-7B, on CSQA/ARC/TinyMMLU:
- `PPR + NoSEED` has the largest EMD.
- `PPR + SEED1` and `PPR + SEED5` reduce EMD monotonically with more seeds.
- `AM-PPR + SEED1/5` achieves the **lowest EMD on every cell** — SC training and answer seeding are **complementary**.
- `EMD_k(b)` (Figure 2b) shows the burn-in period shrinks from ~5 steps (vanilla PPR) to effectively 0 with AM-PPR.

**Accuracy and AUC (LLaMA-3.1-8B, Table 1).** Best per column: TinyMMLU 62.3/85.1 (AM-PPR+SEED1 acc / AM-PPR+SEED5 auc), ARC 81.5/94.2 (AM-PPR+SEED1 acc / AM-PPR+SEED5 auc), CSQA 78.0/94.2 (tie). Direct-Query baseline: 61.2 / 80.5 / 78.0 acc — **AM-PPR matches or slightly beats vanilla decoding**.

**Calibration (Table 2, LLaMA).** Best ECE per dataset: TinyMMLU 0.1229 (AM-PPR+SEED1), ARC 0.0845 (AM-PPR+SEED5), CSQA 0.0674 (AM-PPR+SEED5). Direct-Query ECE: 0.2457 / 0.1251 / 0.1072 — **substantial ECE reduction**, especially on TinyMMLU. Brier score also drops consistently for AM-PPR.

**OLMo-3-7B (Table 5/6).** Same trend: AM-PPR dominates PPR in EMD, but here the relationship to direct-query is more nuanced — direct-query is sometimes slightly higher in acc (TinyMMLU 52.2 vs 52.0, CSQA 75.0 vs 77.0) but worse in ECE/Brier. The paper itself notes that **calibration is not monotonically improved by martingale stability** — important honest signal.

**Larger model (Table 7, OLMo-3.1-32B).** Reported but not in main text; consistency of trends at 32B is encouraging but evidence is brief.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty.** Solid but incremental. The paper lifts a known diagnostic (martingale property, Fong et al. 2023; Falck et al. 2024) to a more useful regime (MCQA) and adds a training-time intervention (SC loss). The "burn-in" framing and the seed-marginal anchoring via law of total expectation are clean contributions. The "AM-PPR" zero-shot estimation is the most practically consequential idea.

**Rigor.**
- Two complementary EMD diagnostics are well-defined; bootstrap of prefix-sampled top-K prefixes is principled.
- Theorem 4.2 is conditional (assumptions A1–A3) — honest framing. However, the bound is in terms of *test functions* (h, f), not the full KL divergence of the martingale residual, so the practical tightness of the bound is unclear.
- The implementation uses Monte Carlo estimates inside both training and evaluation; finite-sample bias is acknowledged and addressed only by a small policy-gradient correction (β=0.01) without systematic study of its necessity.
- **No confidence intervals on headline numbers** (Acc/AUC/ECE in Tables 1–2) — single-run metrics over only 50–200 questions per dataset.

**Reproducibility.** Decent but not perfect.
- Code: not explicitly released (paper is a PMLR camera-ready, code may be on GitHub — appendix does not state).
- Hyperparameters fully tabulated (Tables 3–4 in Appendix D.3). LoRA configuration, learning rate per model, prompt templates all in appendix.
- Two model families (LLaMA-3.1-8B, OLMo-3-7B) — solid cross-architecture support.
- Three benchmarks (CSQA, ARC-Challenge, TinyMMLU) — reasonable but **not reasoning-heavy** (no GPQA, no math). TinyMMLU has only 100 questions.

**Significance.** The diagnostic framework (PPR + EMD) is genuinely useful and easy to adopt — practitioners can directly measure whether their model's belief trajectory is martingale on their own data. The SC-loss intervention is a clean recipe. The bigger claim — that "martingale-consistent predictors" are a useful alignment target — is interesting but **not fully established by this paper alone** (see below).

**Red flags / honest concerns.**
1. **The martingale property is necessary, not sufficient** for Bayesian inference. A model can be self-consistent (martingale) and *still* hallucinate or be overconfident — the paper says so explicitly (p. 8: "minimizing martingale violations guarantees internal consistency, but does not inherently ensure that the model is well-calibrated"). Some ECE entries of AM-PPR+SEED5 are *worse* than baseline (e.g., OLMo-3-7B TinyMMLU: 0.2994 vs Direct-Query 0.3348 — but PPR+NoSEED is 0.0937!). The headline "calibration improvement" claim should be qualified.
2. **The PPR prompt asks the model to "sample i.i.d. from your declared distribution"** — but if the model already had a coherent predictive distribution, this prompt would be a no-op. The fact that it *changes* behavior suggests the model's internal distribution is not exactly its declarative distribution. This is a subtle confound: the entire analysis is conditional on the model following the PPR protocol faithfully.
3. **Tiny benchmark sizes**: Figure 2 uses 50 questions per benchmark, Tables 1–2 use 100–200. Variance bars are not reported on accuracy/ECE numbers.
4. **Compute caveats**: AM-PPR saves inference time vs PPR (zero-shot vs long rollout) but requires fine-tuning. The trade-off is model-specific and not quantified.
5. **Effect of instruction-tuning**: the paper relies on instruction-tuned variants to follow the strict `\n`-delimited format. The phenomenon may differ (weaker or stronger) for base models, RLHF models, or larger reasoning models — none of which are tested.
6. **No comparison to temperature scaling or other cheap calibration baselines** that might give similar ECE improvements for a fraction of the engineering cost.
7. **Embedding-space extension** is mentioned as "proof of concept" in the conclusion but no quantitative results are shown for open-ended generation, which is where users actually care about uncertainty.

## Strengths

- **Clean conceptual framing**: martingale property as a diagnostic for LLM "internal coherence" is intuitive and under-explored; the paper makes it operational.
- **Direct measurement of predictive distributions** over the discrete answer space — sidesteps the sampling-artifact problem that plagued Falck et al. (2024).
- **Two complementary interventions** (prompt-time seeding + training-time SC) — the paper correctly identifies them as complementary and shows that combined use is best.
- **Practical engineering win**: AM-PPR zero-shot estimation removes the need for long rollouts at inference, addressing a real deployment cost.
- **Honest scope**: the paper itself flags the necessary-not-sufficient nature of the martingale property and the non-monotonic calibration behaviour.
- **Cross-architecture validation** on two instruction-tuned open-source models.

## Limitations

- **Scope is MCQA only.** Open-ended generation, mathematical reasoning, code generation are not evaluated; the embedding-space extension is mentioned but unquantified.
- **Stable ≠ correct.** Martingale consistency does not fix hallucinations, factual errors, or sycophancy — the impact statement in the paper says this explicitly.
- **Prompt-dependent.** The PPR protocol requires careful prompt engineering (delimiters, "do not condition on prior answers" instruction, no repeating/cyclic patterns). Different LLMs need different prompt templates (Figures 3 vs 4). Deployment may require per-model prompt tuning.
- **Limited benchmarks.** CSQA, ARC-Challenge, TinyMMLU are easy/representative but not state-of-the-art reasoning benchmarks. No GPQA, no MATH, no MMLU-Pro.
- **Small sample sizes**: 50–200 questions per dataset for evaluation; no reported error bars on accuracy/calibration metrics.
- **Fine-tuning cost**: AM-PPR requires LoRA fine-tuning, training data construction, and a CSQA-derived training set. The "zero-shot" deployment benefit has an upfront amortized cost.
- **Theoretical assumptions untested empirically.** A1–A3 of Theorem 4.2 are not validated; the bound is conditional and may not be tight.
- **No comparison to free-lunch baselines** (temperature scaling, label smoothing, simple prompt ensembling).
- **Martingale posterior interpretation**: the equivalence between `Π_∞` and an actual Bayesian posterior over a latent `θ` requires *exchangeability*, which the paper correctly does not assume — but then the interpretability of `Π_∞` as a "posterior" is itself debatable.

## Relevance

与本项目关注的 **LLM 推理可靠性、概率/不确定性建模、训练目标与解码策略** 高度契合。论文将"鞅性质 / 隐式贝叶斯推断"这一理论视角与"多选题问答 + 自一致性微调"这一工程抓手结合，给出一种可诊断、可干预的 LLM 信念稳定性框架，对校准（calibration）与一致性研究有直接价值；其 PPR + EMD 诊断方法也适合作为后续实验的工具。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the two-stage drift-then-stabilize pattern **also emerge in chain-of-thought** (CoT) reasoning traces, where the "answer space" is the reasoning-step vocabulary rather than the final answer letter?
- How does AM-PPR interact with **RLHF / DPO** objectives? Is SC loss compatible with the KL constraint, or does it fight preference optimization?
- Does the SC objective generalize to **larger reasoning models** (32B, 70B, GPT-5-class), or does the burn-in phenomenon change qualitatively with scale?
- The `Π_∞` "martingale posterior" is interpreted as a posterior, but exchangeability is not assumed — what is the correct epistemic interpretation? A de Finetti-style "partial exchangeability" reading?
- Can a **theoretically tighter bound** than `C√ε` be proved under weaker assumptions (e.g., removing A3 by using a Bernstein-type concentration argument)?
- Would a **multi-query (joint) martingale** across related questions give a stronger "Bayesian" interpretation than the per-query analysis?
- Can AM-PPR be used as a **self-distillation signal** for open-ended generation, where the per-step distribution is over the next token rather than a discrete answer letter?
- How sensitive is the result to the **specific PPR prompt** (forbid-i.i.d. instruction, format requirements, anti-pattern language)? An ablation of prompt fragments would clarify how much of the "drift" is the model interpreting the prompt literally vs. genuinely internal drift.
- What is the **interaction with retrieval / RAG**? If the model is given an external document, does the burn-in disappear (suggesting drift was a "lookup" effect) or persist (suggesting it is a deeper autoregressive property)?
- The paper does not address **multi-turn dialogue** — does the martingale property hold when the "answer" is a turn, not a letter?
