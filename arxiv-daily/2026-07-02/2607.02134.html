§ 01

The workflow — evidence as a contract

Each computational claim becomes a target needing an evidence bundle. Progress lives in workspace files, gated by checks that run outside the agent.

1

Source inspection

Build spec/paper_inventory.json; index paper figures under artifacts/paper_figures/ with hashes; generated outputs go separately.

2

Target extraction

Every computational claim → a reproduction matrix row: location, assets, method, required output, judgment rule, status.

3

Method reconstruction

Spec files record the agent's reconstruction plus recorded hypotheses for every under-specified detail (seed, tolerance, plotting).

4

Single-active-target discipline

The ledger names exactly one active target; a check rejects multiple (or zero) active targets while work remains.

5

Run recording

Every execution passes through a recorder storing command, timing, success, output hashes. Failed/replaced runs are kept.

6

Provenance

artifacts/provenance/ links each output to its implementation file, config, seed, and the paper passages justifying it. Hashes block silent edits.

7

Comparison + report coverage

C_j judges output vs. paper under the target's acceptance rule; G_j records where it appears in report/main.tex.

⌬ Completion gate (Eq. 3)
V_complete = V_spec ∧ V_progress ∧ V_report
           ∧ (∀j sj=MATCHED)
           ∧ [a = ∅] ∧ [report PDF exists]
  • Paper-asset checks — hash separation rejects copied figures/TeX
  • Active-target check — ledger & matrix must agree
  • Progress/provenance — traces impl. to paper's method, not just output
  • Report-coverage — every matched figure/table in main.tex; PDF must exist
Evidence bundle E_j = (ŷ_j, R_j, P_j, C_j, G_j)

§ 03

Strengths vs. limitations

A protocol/harness contribution — the right kind for reproducibility — but read with its caveats.

Strengths

What lands well

  • Completion by workspace state, not natural-language self-report — a genuinely useful reliability primitive.
  • Claim-type-aware rules (numeric / distributional / structural / visual) — real engineering, not one-size-fits-all.
  • Honest separation of "workspace MATCHED" from fixed paper-anchored fidelity; never overclaims "fully replicated."
  • Hierarchical-Bayesian stats with justified priors; NUTS, 4 chains, 2000 draws; residual variance openly reported.
  • Exemplary artifact release: dual-agent skill (Codex + Claude Code), prompts, 12 full workspaces, analysis scripts.
Limitations

Where it's thin

  • No ablation against direct prompting → the skill's marginal benefit is unquantified.
  • Self-assessment circularity: the agent writes the rubric, produces the evidence, and grades it. Checks enforce structure, not numerical truth.
  • MATCHED ≠ within-tolerance — a target can pass while the reproduced number diverges (PINN-II ~14×).
  • Narrow, classical corpus: 4 SciML papers, single agent/model/setting (GPT-5.4 Extra High); between-paper variance under-identified.
  • Few scalar anchors (13), PIFT has none; thresholds are analysis conventions; compute asymmetry confounds effort.
§ 04

Open questions

Where this work points next — for anyone building or auditing coding-agent research pipelines.

How large is the gap vs. a matched unstructured-prompt baseline — does the skill lift completion rate, fidelity, or only inspectability?
Can the gate ever fail a target (detect genuine non-reproducibility), or is it biased toward "completed"? What is the false-MATCH rate?
How does the ~14× PINN-II swing decompose into prompt sensitivity, training stochasticity, and underspecified implementation choices?
Does an independent verifier (or second agent) replacing the self-judged rule materially change which targets pass?
Does it scale beyond SciML — LLM-training runs, systems papers, or papers whose central claims are qualitative?
Could a canonical target-schema cut the 3.1× decomposition variance and improve cross-run comparability?
