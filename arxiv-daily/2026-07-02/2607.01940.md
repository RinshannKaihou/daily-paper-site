> **一句话总结：** 本文将 Transformer 的"自我修复"（Hydra 效应）形式化为组件重要性的"可加性失效"，并提出 CoAx（Conditional Co-Ablation）这一基于 Fisher 几何、无标签、输出驱动的二阶评分，通过度量主组件被消融后各剩余单元消融效应的*增长*，揭示被一阶评分掩盖的休眠备份头；在 GPT-2-small IOI 电路上将备份头恢复 ROC-AUC 从 0.33 提升至 0.91（超越 AtP\* GradDrop 0.82 等基线），并通过反事实修补因果验证，随后无标签迁移到 8 个模型 6 种架构的归纳电路，并在归因恢复、能力击倒与 124M–7B 结构化剪枝等下游任务中闭环。

> **TL;DR:** Transformer circuits self-repair: when a primary component is ablated, a dormant backup takes over, making isolated (first-order/additive) importance scores provably unable to separate a true backup from an inert head. This paper introduces **CoAx (Conditional Co-Ablation)**, a label-free, Fisher-grounded second-order score that measures how much each unit's ablation *energy grows* once a primary set is removed. On the GPT-2-small IOI circuit it lifts backup-head recovery from 0.33 → 0.91 ROC-AUC (beating self-repair-aware AtP\* GradDrop at 0.82, which is explicitly noted as *not* the fair comparison since CoAx uses the primary seed). Counterfactual patching causally verifies the recovered heads (freezing them removes 55% of self-repair). The same label-free pipeline transfers to induction across 8 models/6 families, and the recovered backups close the downstream loop: they restore the self-repair-masked attribution (logit-diff drop 0.22 → 1.76), enable capability knockout (IOI accuracy 0.97 → 0.70, matching the 0.72 oracle), and yield repair-aware structured pruning from 124M to 7B. Caveat: CoAx is a *completion* tool (needs a primary seed); as a standalone finder it only reaches 0.60.

## 核心贡献

- **将自我修复形式化为"可加性失效"与"条件电路补全"任务**：明确指出归因、电路发现、剪枝中通用的"逐节点孤立评分"在冗余电路中失效，并把它重新定义为可度量、可恢复的条件补全问题（conditional circuit completion）。
- **提出 CoAx 评分**：基于输出分布的 Fisher 信息几何，定义单位 u 在给定主集 S 被消融后的"消融能量增长" `comp_u(S) = E(δz_u|S) − E(δz_u|∅)`，用 O(|U|) 次前向（与单次消融扫描同阶，远低于 O(|U|²) 全枚举）读取被一阶评分丢弃的二阶交互。
- **三条命题给出几何与理论支撑**：Fisher 恒等式（Prop 1）、可加性盲点定理（Prop 2，证明任何基于单位干净态统计的评分都无法区分"纯备份"与"惰性单位"，而 CoAx 可以）、条件增长=种子协同（Prop 3）。
- **强大的实证恢复 + 因果验证**：GPT-2-small IOI 备份头 ROC-AUC 0.33→0.91；用置换检验（p<10⁻⁴）、超几何 top-k 检验（p=9×10⁻⁵）、配对 DeLong 检验（p=2×10⁻³）做统计严谨性；用 100 个植入备份的合成基准解决"仅 8 个真备份导致功效不足"的问题；反事实修补确认冻结备份可消除 55% 自我修复。
- **跨模型/架构无标签迁移**：同一流程迁移到 GPT-2 medium/large（规模轴）以及 Pythia、Gemma、Llama-3.1、GPT-Neo、OLMo-2、Qwen2.5 等 8 个模型 6 个家族的归纳电路（架构轴）。
- **下游闭环**：归因恢复（0.22→1.76）、能力击倒（0.97→0.70，匹配 0.72 oracle，而一阶 +own 过冲到 0.24）、修复感知结构化剪枝（124M–7B，50% 稀疏度 perplexity 80.6 vs 梯度-Taylor 201.4）。

## Key Contributions

- **Reframes self-repair as a measurable additivity failure**: formalizes the Hydra/backup effect as the breakdown of node-additive importance scoring, and defines the recovery task *conditional circuit completion* (given a primary circuit, recover the components that only become causal when that circuit is removed).
- **Introduces the CoAx score**, a label-free, output-grounded second-order score grounded in the Fisher information geometry of the output distribution: `comp_u(S) = E(δz_u|S) − E(δz_u|∅)`. It is the growth in Fisher-weighted ablation energy after a primary set S is removed. Cost is O(|U|) forward passes per seed — same order as one single-ablation scan.
- **Two complementary second-order lenses**: *pairwise synergy* I_uv (for symmetric cooperation / same-circuit clustering) and the *CoAx score* (for dormant-substitution / backup recovery). The headline discovery and all applications use CoAx; synergy carries the same-circuit analysis.
- **Theory via three propositions**: (1) Fisher identity making the centered-feature inner product coincide with the Fisher form; (2) the additivity blind-spot theorem proving any clean-state per-unit score cannot rank a pure backup above an inert unit while CoAx can; (3) conditional growth equals seed synergy under pairwise truncation.
- **Strong empirical recovery with proper statistics**: 0.33 → 0.91 ROC-AUC on GPT-2-small IOI; validated by label-permutation (p<10⁻⁴), hypergeometric top-k (p=9×10⁻⁵), and paired DeLong (p=2×10⁻³) tests. A 100-backup controlled-redundancy synthetic benchmark addresses the underpowering of the 8-positive IOI test (CoAx 0.90, p<10⁻¹⁵; first-order energy actively anti-ranks at 0.42).
- **Causal verification**: counterfactual patching shows freezing the recovered backups to their dormant value removes 55% of self-repair, vs 0% for a matched random set; DLA decomposition confirms answer hand-off from primaries to backups.
- **Label-free cross-model transfer**: scales across the GPT-2 family and transfers to induction across 8 models in 6 architecture families (attribution factors 2.1×–12×).
- **Downstream closure**: recovers self-repair-masked attribution (logit-diff drop 0.22 → 1.76), enables capability knockout matching the documented oracle (0.97 → 0.70 vs oracle 0.72, while first-order +own overshoots to 0.24), and yields repair-aware structured pruning from 124M to 7B (beating magnitude/Wanda/gradient-Taylor; 50%-sparsity perplexity 80.6 vs 201.4 for Taylor on GPT-2-small).

## Methodology

**Setting.** Frozen decoder-only transformers. A "structured unit" u is any component with an additive residual-stream contribution that ablation can zero (CoAx is unit-agnostic; experiments use attention heads, the granularity at which head-level circuit ground truth exists). Ablating set M gives logits z_M; the single-unit ablation effect is δz_u = z_0 − z_{u}.

**Fisher geometry over ablations.** Effects are measured in the metric the output distribution induces. The Fisher information F = diag(p_0) − p_0 p_0ᵀ is exactly the Hessian of KL(p_0 ‖ softmax(z)) at z_0, so a logit perturbation δz has second-order KL cost ½ δzᵀ F δz. They realize this with a centered, √p_0-weighted feature δz̃_u on the top-r logits. Proposition 1: ⟨δz̃_u, δz̃_v⟩ = δz_uᵀ F δz_v, so the Fisher Gram H is PSD and E(δz_u) = E_{x,t}[δz_uᵀ F δz_u] is the mean KL energy of ablating u. The Fisher-cosine affinity A_uv is their first-order baseline.

**Pairwise synergy.** For units u,v the synergy is the non-additive part of the joint-ablation perturbation: I_uv = δz_uv − δz_u − δz_v, with energy S_uv. It captures symmetric cooperation invisible to first-order affinity and is used for same-circuit clustering.

**The CoAx score (Definition 1).** The conditional ablation effect of u given seed S is δz_{u|S} = z_S − z_{S∪{u}}. The CoAx score is the *growth* of its energy under conditioning:
comp_u(S) = E(δz_u|S) − E(δz_u|∅).
Large for backups (effect appears only once S is ablated), near zero for non-redundant units. Cost: O(|U|) forward passes per seed.

**Why first-order misses and CoAx doesn't (Props 2–3).** Prop 2 (additivity blind spot): any score g(θ_u) where θ_u is a clean-pass per-unit statistic that vanishes for dormant units assigns a pure backup and an inert unit the *same* value, so cannot rank one above the other; CoAx gives comp_backup(S)=∆>0 vs comp_inert(S)=0. Prop 3: under pairwise-interaction truncation, comp_u(S) reduces to the energy of u's synergistic coupling to the seed — confirming CoAx reads the off-diagonal second-order interaction that single-ablation scores (which read only the diagonal) discard.

**Division of labor.** Pairwise synergy = lens for cooperation (symmetric same-circuit structure); CoAx = lens for substitution (dormant backups as a node-level compensating set). They are the two faces of non-additivity.

## Results & Highlights

- **Backup-head discovery (GPT-2-small IOI, Table 1)** — ROC-AUC over 8 documented backups: single-ablation 0.33; attribution patching 0.60; seeded GIM 0.63; EAP-IG 0.70; AtP\* GradDrop 0.82; **CoAx 0.91**. Primaries are easy for all (~0.78–0.81).
- **Honest scoping of the headline**: CoAx *completes* rather than discovers from scratch. As a standalone finder CoAx peaks at 0.60 (below seed-free AtP\* 0.82). The fair same-seed comparison is seeded GIM at 0.63; the 0.26 gap is significant (paired DeLong p=2×10⁻³). The 0.09 gap over seed-free AtP\* is underpowered on 8 positives — mitigated by a 100-planted-backup synthetic benchmark (CoAx 0.90, p<10⁻¹⁵).
- **Co-activation caveat (intellectually honest)**: an input-side co-activation score also ranks IOI backups highly (0.92 AUC) because they co-fire with primaries. But it is correlational, collapses on movement circuits (duplicate-token 0.32 vs CoAx 0.97), and over-ablates when used to complete a circuit. CoAx is the *causal, output-grounded* alternative.
- **Causal verification (Figure 3)**: as primaries are ablated (k=0..3), backups wake up monotonically (output-norm ratio 1.00→1.15; conditional causal drop +0.05→+0.11) while random controls stay flat; DLA shows answer hand-off (backups' DLA to IO−S doubles +0.07→+0.21); counterfactual patching — freezing backups to dormant value removes 55% of self-repair, freezing random removes none.
- **Completeness/faithfulness/minimality (community-standard circuit criteria)**: first-order circuit is badly incomplete (gap 0.72); completing with CoAx backups closes it to 0.15 (matching the 0.16 documented complete circuit; random completion 0.61).
- **Generalization**: scale axis — IOI backups wake up across GPT-2 small/medium/large (output-norm ratio 1.15/1.05/1.13 vs ~1.00 elsewhere). Architecture axis — induction completion across 8 models/6 families (attribution factors 2.1×–12×). On induction, CoAx ≈ +own control (redundancy shared among co-firing homogeneous heads, no distinct backup class).
- **Attribution recovery (Table 3)**: ablating primaries alone drops logit-difference by only 0.22 (clean 2.53); adding CoAx backups recovers 1.76, exceeding matched-random (1.0±0.7) and even documented backups (1.15).
- **Capability knockout (Table 4)**: primary ablation barely dents IOI accuracy (1.00→0.97); +CoAx backups → 0.70 (matching oracle 0.72); first-order +own overshoots to 0.24 (cuts into core heads).
- **Repair-aware pruning (Figure 5)**: across GPT-2-small/large, Pythia-1.4B, Qwen2.5-7B, the co-ablation order beats magnitude/Wanda/gradient-Taylor; 50%-sparsity perplexity 80.6 vs Taylor 201.4 on GPT-2-small. Sequential re-measuring adds a further self-repair-aware gain (80.6 vs 112.6 for the static order), isolating the value of conditioning itself. The dominant effect is the better head score; conditioning refines it.
- **Scope limits stated by authors**: fails on the MLP-dominated greater-than circuit (1.5× over random, within one std); induction has no distinct backup class; the head-level signal may not transfer to FFN-group granularity.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — High, and principled.** Recasting self-repair as a measurable *additivity failure* and recovering backups via conditional energy growth is a genuine conceptual contribution, distinct from de-biasing a node's own gradient (GIM, AtP\* GradDrop) or combinatorial Shapley sampling. The three propositions give it real teeth: Prop 2 is a clean impossibility result for any clean-state per-unit score, and Prop 3 ties CoAx to seed synergy.

- **Rigor — Strong, and unusually self-critical.** Three statistical tests (label-permutation, hypergeometric, paired DeLong); a synthetic controlled-redundancy benchmark (100 planted backups) to address the underpowering of the 8-positive IOI test; counterfactual patching for causal verification; faithfulness/completeness/minimality reported against community-standard criteria. The authors *repeatedly* flag the unfairness of their own headline comparison (CoAx-with-seed vs seed-free AtP\*) and report the fair same-seed number (0.63 GIM vs 0.91 CoAx). This is a model of honest empirical writing.

- **Reproducibility — Likely good.** IOI is a standard, well-documented circuit; CoAx is implementable from model outputs with a single-ablation baseline + one conditional pass; the paper promises code/project page/tutorial. Caveats: ablation type (mean-ablation implied), normalization details, and calibration-data size need the appendix to fully reproduce; sequential pruning re-measures after each removal, which is costlier than the O(|U|) headline suggests.

- **Significance — Potentially high for interpretability practitioners.** Self-repair undermines the most common attribution/knockout/pruning workflows; a cheap, label-free corrector that also improves pruning is valuable. The downstream loop (one recovered set serves attribution + knockout + pruning) is a compelling operational story, not just diagnostics.

- **Red flags / things to scrutinize**:
  - **Single-circuit headline**: the only circuit with head-level backup ground truth is IOI, and that ground truth itself comes from prior manual interpretability (Wang et al. 2022). The 0.91 headline rests on 8 positives; the synthetic benchmark mitigates but does not eliminate this.
  - **Completion, not discovery**: CoAx needs a primary seed and peaks at 0.60 standalone. The abstract's "raises backup-head recovery from 0.33 to 0.91" is fair given the same-seed comparison but a casual reader could mistake it for a general circuit finder. The body is scrupulously honest about this.
  - **Competitor on home turf**: input-side co-activation reaches 0.92 AUC on IOI backups. The paper's defense (correlational, collapses on movement circuits, over-ablates) is legitimate, but on IOI specifically CoAx's practical advantage over a much cheaper co-activation score is modest; the advantage only clearly shows on movement circuits.
  - **Pruning framing**: the dominant pruning gain is "co-ablation energy as a better head score," not the self-repair-aware sequential refinement (80.6 vs 112.6 — real but secondary). Modern one-shot pruners like SparseGPT/Wanda-Matrix are not all in the baseline set, though Wanda and magnitude are.
  - **Cost at scale**: O(|U|) per seed is good vs O(|U|²), but sequential pruning re-measures per removal; the 7B pruning demo's compute is not reported.
  - **Negative result is reported (good)**: greater-than circuit transfer fails — a property the authors attribute to the circuit, not the unit, which is plausible but limits generality claims.

Overall: a methodologically careful, theoretically motivated, and notably honest paper. The conceptual reframing is the real contribution; the downstream applications, while real, are partly carried by "co-ablation energy is a good head score" rather than the self-repair-specific mechanism.

## Strengths

- Principled reformulation: self-repair as additivity failure, with three supporting propositions (esp. the additivity blind-spot impossibility result).
- Label-free and output-grounded — no dependence on curated circuit labels, easing deployment; the same recovered set serves three downstream pipelines.
- Rigorous statistics (permutation, hypergeometric, DeLong) plus a synthetic benchmark to fix underpowering, plus causal verification via counterfactual patching.
- Strong, honest empirical writing: explicitly states the unfair-seed caveat, reports fair same-seed numbers, and discloses the standalone-finder limitation (0.60) and the co-activation competitor (0.92).
- Cheap relative to brute-force interaction search (O(|U|) vs O(|U|²)).
- Clean ablation: dropping the second-order term collapses name-mover clustering 0.76 → 0.34, isolating the interaction term's contribution.

## Limitations

- **Completion-only**: requires a primary seed; as a standalone finder it is weaker than seed-free AtP\* (0.60 vs 0.82). It complements, not replaces, first-order discovery.
- **Single labeled-backup circuit**: IOI is the only one with head-level backup ground truth; headline metric rests on 8 positives whose labels come from prior manual work.
- **Home-turf competitor**: input-side co-activation also finds IOI backups (0.92 AUC) and is cheaper; CoAx's clear win is confined to movement circuits.
- **Doesn't transfer to MLP-dominated circuits**: greater-than fails (~1.5× over random), constraining the generality claim at head granularity.
- **Induction "transfer" is recovery, not unique identification**: CoAx ≈ +own there because redundancy is shared among co-firing homogeneous heads with no distinct backup class.
- **Pruning**: the self-repair-aware sequential increment is secondary to the standalone score quality; strongest modern one-shot pruners (e.g., SparseGPT) not fully represented in baselines; 7B compute not reported.
- **Underpowered head-to-head on IOI** for the 0.09 gap vs AtP\* (mitigated, not eliminated, by the synthetic benchmark).

## Relevance

对从事大模型可解释性、机制电路发现与安全审计的研究者高度相关：CoAx 直接修复了"自我修复"对一阶归因/击倒/剪枝流程的系统性偏差，并提供了一个无标签、低成本、可闭环使用的工具，是理解并安全操控 Transformer 内部冗余结构的有用方法。对关心结构化剪枝与模型压缩的工程读者也有实际价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the need for an external primary seed be removed — e.g., by bootstrapping a seed from CoAx's own top first-order heads and iterating, and does that recover the documented primaries + backups jointly?
- What is the false-positive/false-negative behavior of CoAx on circuits that do *not* self-repair, and is there a principled threshold (or a comp(S) magnitude test) to declare a circuit non-redundant?
- Beyond IOI and induction, does CoAx recover known redundancy in other characterized circuits (copy-suppression, anti-erasure, factual-recall/MQA) at head and at MLP/feature granularity?
- How do the pruning gains compare head-to-head against the strongest modern one-shot pruners (SparseGPT, Wanda-quantized variants) at matched sparsity and zero-shot accuracy, and what is the wall-clock cost of the sequential re-measuring at 7B?
- Can pairwise synergy + CoAx be unified into a single interaction-aware circuit-discovery objective that improves automated finders (ACDC, EAP-IG) on standard MIB benchmarks, not just on backup recovery?
- Is the additivity-blind-spot theorem (Prop 2) tight — are there relaxed scoring functions that partially recover backups while remaining purely first-order, and where exactly is the information-theoretic boundary?
