Methodology — two complementary signals

The load-bearing insight: the two signals are negatively correlated (Pearson r = −0.41) and label-complementary — one gives precision, the other recall — so their z-sum beats either alone.

Signal 1 · precision axis

Activation refusal-gap ρ

ρ = gap(Mc) / gap(Mb)

Reference-anchored ratio of the mid-stack (⌊0.33L⌋…⌊0.67L⌋) harmful-minus-benign last-token projection along refusal directions estimated on the trusted base Mb only. ρ ≈ 1 intact → falls to 0 as refusal is removed.

Anchoring lets one threshold transfer across scales — but it is bounded to a single direction.

  • ✓ refusal-specific, high precision
  • ✗ misses Heretic-style multi-direction removal
  • ✗ misses reasoning-distilled refusals in CoT
Signal 2 · recall axis

Weight-recovery energy E1

E1 = meanm[ σ₁²(ΔWm) / Σσᵢ²(ΔWm) ]

WeightWatch rank-1 spectral energy of the base-to-candidate weight difference, averaged over o_proj & down_proj in band B. Fires on any low-rank weight edit — catching what the single-direction gap misses.

But it is direction-agnostic: it cannot tell removal from addition.

  • ✓ high recall on multi-direction edits
  • ✗ over-flags benign low-rank edits & merges
  • ✗ reads E1=1.0 even on safety-hardened models
FUSION s(Mc) = z(−ρ) + z(E1) threshold-free ranking; operating point set by Youden's J on attested refs. r = −0.41

Failure map — severity-ranked

The paper is unusually honest: it documents two working attacks and frames the method as triage, not tamper-proofing.

1
Spoofed reference
most severe · zero-training

Declare an already-abliterated sibling as the "base," or use the candidate as its own reference. This shrinks ΔW and inflates ρ by construction — evading both axes with no training at all.

sibling spoof: ρ = 0.69 / 0.73 on 2/2
self-reference: ΔW = 0, ρ = 1
Mitigation: root of trust must be a publisher-signed hash or hardware-attested lineage — never an uploader-nominated base.
2
White-box adaptive fine-tune
existence proof · Qwen2.5-1.5B

A composite objective pushes the score under threshold while keeping the model coherent, capable, and fully guard-unsafe. Natural SFT/DPO does not open this hole (verified on 6 Qwen full fine-tunes).

step 600: E1 = 0.29, ρ = 1.82
guard-unsafe: 20 / 20  ·  non-refusing 0.15
MMLU 0.58 vs 0.59  ·  GSM8K 0.51 vs 0.57
ppl 180 vs 194
Caveat: one family, one scale, linear detector proxy — generalization of the evasion is unproven.

Strengths & limitations, side by side

Strengths

  • Cheap & pre-deployment. Both signals are simple scalars; deployable before any generation-based runtime guard.
  • Principled synergy. Two complementary, negatively-correlated signals lift 0.84 / 0.90 → 0.95.
  • Cross-family transfer. Reference-anchoring shown decisive: 0.35 → 0.42 → 0.83.
  • Unusual honesty. Severity-ranked failure map, a real adaptive attack, second-guard label check (κ=0.78).
  • Auditable. Pinned contrast-set hashes, full protocol, three-adversary threat model.

Limitations

  • Small effective n. Only 94 / 273 evaluated → wide held-out CIs (bal.acc [.83,.95], FPR [.03,.22]).
  • Spoofing is total & trivial. Requires a trusted attested reference that may not exist in adversarial ecosystems.
  • E1 is direction-agnostic. Fires on safety-hardening too; no hardened models in negatives → FPR optimistic.
  • Gap is single-direction, last-token. Misses multi-direction removal & reasoning-distilled refusals (admitted open problem).
  • White-box proof is narrow. One family, one scale, linear proxy; generalization unproven.
  • Labels certify refusal, not harmlessness — internally-edited-but-refusing checkpoints mislabeled by construction.
05 /

Open questions for further work

Q1
How robust is ρ to merges, quantization, and frankenstein checkpoints — and can E1's merge FPR (0.22) drop without sacrificing multi-direction recall?
Q2
Can the spoofed-reference failure be closed practically with signed-hash / hardware attestation — and what is the minimal attestation a hub needs?
Q3
What are the per-family AUROC and confusion matrices at Youden, and how does 0.89 move as n grows toward 273?
Q4
Would a third signal — a harmfulness probe or representation-engineering direction — recover the 4 misses and close the reasoning-distilled gap?
Q5
Does the white-box evasion generalize across families/scales, and can a non-linear detector defeat the linear-proxy attack?
Q6
How does checkpoint-time triage compare, in cost-vs-caught-attacks, to a runtime guard — strict improvement or complementary layer?
