> **一句话总结：** MxGLUT 提出一种基于查找表（LUT）的可重构广播（RLB）数据流加速器，通过统一的"三阶段"LUT 执行框架将 FP8-INT4 与 FP8-FP8 GEMM 映射到同一 RAC 数据通路（无需专用浮点乘法器），并用运行时可切换的 OS/WS 数据流分别适配 prefill（局部累加）与 decode（权重复用）两阶段，在 UMC 28nm @200MHz 下实现最高 2.16× 延迟加速与 11.58 TFLOPS/W 能效，困惑度增幅不超过 1.70%。

> **TL;DR:** MxGLUT is a reconfigurable LUT-centric broadcast (RLB) dataflow accelerator for mixed-precision GEMM in LLM inference. It unifies FP8-INT4 and FP8-FP8 computation under a single three-stage LUT-based mechanism (no dedicated FP multipliers, no VPU fallback for attention), and uses a runtime-switchable OS/WS dataflow (OS for prefill to localize partial sums, WS for decode to maximize weight reuse). Synthesized in UMC 28nm at 200 MHz, it cuts multiplier area by up to 56.92% and power by up to 78.35%, delivers 0.492 TFLOPS/mm² area efficiency and 11.58 TFLOPS/W, and achieves up to 2.16× (prefill) / 1.49× (decode) latency speedup on the Llama family with ≤1.70% perplexity increase.

## 核心贡献

- **统一的 LUT 执行框架：** 首次将 FP8-INT4 线性层与 FP8-FP8 注意力 GEMM 统一在同一"三阶段"（LUT 构建 → LUT 查询 → 模式相关变换）LUT 计算机制下，消除了独立浮点数据通路与 VPU 回退路径。FP8-FP8 的乘积形成被前移至 LUT 构建阶段，运行时仅剩查表 + 符号异或 + 指数加法 + FP32 累加。
- **可重构广播（RLB）数据流：** 针对外积式 RAC 执行特点，将 LUT 条目按行广播给同行所有 RAC，并将 wavefront 填充/排空开销从 3N+S−3 降至 2N+S−2；运行时在 prefill 切换 OS（部分和局部、零 SRAM 回写）与 decode 切换 WS（权重复用）模式。
- **MxLPE 处理单元：** 通过共享 FP8-INT4 LUT 生成器（6 个 SFASU）+ 专用 FP8-FP8 LUT 生成器、以及把 FP 移位广播到所有 RAC（消除冗余移位器）的设计，在同一硬件上原生支持两种精度模式。
- **高能效/面积效率（28nm 硅验证）：** UMC 28nm @200MHz 下实现 0.492 TFLOPS/mm² 面积效率与 11.58 TFLOPS/W 能效（较基线提升 1.22×/1.65×），核心面积仅 3.33 mm²（所比较设计中最小）；新增 FP8-FP8 支持相对 FIGLUT 仅带来 2.57%/3.34% 面积/能效损失。
- **端到端 LLM 收益：** Llama 系列（1B/3B/8B，1K–8K 上下文）上 prefill/decode 分别获得最高 2.16×/1.49× 延迟加速，归一化能耗降至 0.44×/0.71×；WikiText-103 困惑度增幅不超过 1.70%。
- **精度可扩展性：** 基于比特平面流，可扩展至 FP8-INTx（x=1~16）及 FP8 E4M3/E5M2 标准格式，作为 FP8 中心化统一计算基底。

## Key Contributions

- **Unified LUT-based execution framework:** Maps both FP8-INT4 linear GEMMs and FP8-FP8 attention GEMMs onto a common three-stage runtime path (LUT construction → LUT query → mode-dependent transformation), eliminating dedicated FP multipliers and VPU fallback. For FP8-FP8, mantissa multiplication / normalization / round-to-nearest are performed at LUT construction time, reducing runtime to lookup + sign XOR + exponent addition + FP32 accumulation.
- **Reconfigurable LUT-centric Broadcast (RLB) dataflow:** Broadcasts activation-derived LUT entries to all RACs in a row, removing wavefront fill/drain overhead (latency 3N+S−3 → 2N+S−2; register overhead 3N(N−1)/2 → N(N−1)/2), and switches between output-stationary (OS, prefill) and weight-stationary (WS, decode) at kernel granularity.
- **MxLPE (Mixed-precision LUT-based Processing Element):** A shared FP8-INT4 LUT generator (six SFASUs) plus a dedicated FP8-FP8 LUT generator, with FP shifting broadcast once per LUT entry (eliminating redundant per-RAC FP shifters), natively supporting both precision modes on the same hardware.
- **Silicon-verified efficiency (28nm):** Synthesized in UMC 28nm at 200 MHz — 0.492 TFLOPS/mm² area efficiency and 11.58 TFLOPS/W energy efficiency (1.22× / 1.65× over baselines); smallest reported core area (3.33 mm²) among compared designs. Adding native FP8-FP8 support costs only 2.57% area-efficiency and 3.34% energy-efficiency reduction vs. FP8-INT4-only FIGLUT.
- **End-to-end LLM gains:** On the Llama family (1B/3B/8B, 1K–8K contexts), up to 2.16× (prefill) and 1.49× (decode) latency speedup; normalized energy down to 0.44× / 0.71×; ≤1.70% perplexity increase on WikiText-103.
- **Precision scalability:** Bit-plane-oriented flow naturally extends to FP8-INTx (x=1~16) and FP8 E4M3/E5M2, serving as a unified FP8-centric compute substrate.

## Methodology

**Problem motivation — two coupled forms of heterogeneity:**
- *Cross-layer arithmetic heterogeneity:* weight-only quantization creates FP8-INT4 linear layers (PROJ, FFN1, FFN2) but FP8-FP8 attention layers (QK, AV). When weighted by operand bit-widths, FP8-FP8 attention still accounts for ~52% of weighted compute cost in Llama-3-1B during 8K-token prefill (and grows with sequence length).
- *Cross-phase execution heterogeneity:* prefill is compute-bound (large GEMM, needs data reuse / local partial sums); decode is memory-bound (GEMV-like, needs weight residency). A single static dataflow cannot serve both.

**Unified LUT-based execution framework (the core idea).** Both GEMM types are organized into a shared three-stage flow:
1. *LUT construction* — precompute activation-dependent partial results and store as LUT entries.
2. *LUT query* — use encoded weights to index entries.
3. *Mode-dependent transformation* — convert queried values into final partial results per the target mode.

Precision-specific logic is confined to stages 1 and 3, so the central LUT-query path is reused across both modes.

- *FP8-INT4 mode:* uses BCQ (binary coding quantization) encoding; INT4 weights are bit-plane-decomposed and regrouped column-wise into bit-packed codes serving as the LUT index. Sign symmetry (bit 0 → −1, bit 1 → +1) halves storage (only 2^(r−1) patterns, r=4 in hardware). Runtime = query + bit-plane shift-accumulate.
- *FP8-FP8 mode (the key novelty):* maps continuous FP multiplication to LUT-centric execution by moving product formation (mantissa multiply, normalize, round-to-nearest) into LUT construction. Runtime reduces to LUT indexing by the weight mantissa, sign XOR, exponent addition, and FP32 accumulation. Subnormals are handled with flush-to-zero (FTZ); each product is stored as a compact 8-bit FP8-like entry (5-bit signed exponent field + 3-bit mantissa field) rather than FP16/FP32.

**MxLPE microarchitecture.** Integrates a LUT generator, a local flip-flop-based LUT block (eight 8-bit LUTs + eight per-entry FP shifters), and multiple parallel RAC (read-accumulate) units. The FP8-INT4 path uses six SFASU (shared FP8 add/sub unit) blocks to enumerate the 8 unique sign combinations; the FP8-FP8 path enumerates 16 weight mantissa candidates. A crucial optimization: each LUT entry is FP-shifted once and broadcast to all RACs, rather than per-RAC shifters — eliminating redundant FP shifters.

**RLB dataflow.** Row-wise broadcast of LUT entries removes wavefront overhead and reduces data-distribution registers. Runtime switching between OS (prefill: FP32 partial sums stay in the PE, zero intermediate SRAM writeback) and WS (decode: weights stay stationary; activations re-streamed and outputs forwarded) is selected at array granularity per kernel.

**Full accelerator.** A 64×64 RAC fabric (= 64 MxLPEs) forms the MxMPU, with 384 KB on-chip SRAM (128 KB act / 128 KB weight / 128 KB output, each macro two-bank ping-pong for compute-DMA overlap), 256-bit AXI interconnect, DMA, instruction controller, and a VPU retained *only* for nonlinear operators (Softmax, LayerNorm) — never for GEMM.

**Experimental setup.** Fully implemented in Verilog, verified with Synopsys VCS; logic synthesis with Synopsys Design Compiler (UMC 28nm, 0.9 V, 200 MHz); power via Synopsys PrimeTime PX; SRAM/DRAM energy via CACTI. All baselines share identical clock, array dimension, SRAM budget, AXI/DMA, and memory interface, and each runs under its own Timeloop-optimized best legal mapping — so differences reflect datapath/dataflow design, not budget mismatches. Evaluated on Llama-3-1B/3B/8B at 1K–8K contexts (batch 64); accuracy on WikiText-103.

## Results & Highlights

- **Arithmetic level (LUT generator):** Shared FP8-INT4 LUT generator cuts silicon area by **27.23%** and power by **36.96%** vs. the conventional FIGLUT generator.
- **PE level (MxLPE multiplier):** Up to **56.92% area reduction**; power reduced by **77.07%** (FP8-INT4) and **78.35%** (FP8-FP8) vs. baselines. Compared to FIGLUT specifically, MxGLUT adds FP8-FP8 support for **31.95% area overhead** and only **8.23% power overhead** in FP8-INT4 mode — an attractive trade-off.
- **Accelerator level:** **0.492 TFLOPS/mm²** area efficiency, **11.58 TFLOPS/W** energy efficiency (up to 1.22× and 1.65× over baselines). Adding native FP8-FP8 support costs only **2.57% area-efficiency** and **3.34% energy-efficiency** reduction vs. FP8-INT4-only FIGLUT.
- **Layer-wise (Llama-3-8B, prefill, OS mode):** FP8-INT4 linear layers up to **2.09× speedup / 0.33× energy**; FP8-FP8 attention up to **2.27× speedup / 0.60× energy**. (Decode, WS mode): FP8-INT4 up to **2.19× speedup / 0.54× energy**; FP8-FP8 attention gains are more modest (up to 1.24× / 0.97×) because decode attention is GEMV-like and DRAM-dominated.
- **End-to-end (Llama family):** Up to **2.16× (prefill) / 1.49× (decode)** latency speedup; normalized energy **0.44× / 0.71×**. Speedup grows with context length but saturates as DRAM traffic dominates (e.g., compute-energy share in Llama-3-1B prefill drops from 50.65% at 1K to 37.35% at 8K).
- **Native FP8-FP8 LUT vs. VPU:** Compared with FIGLUT+VPU (which offloads attention to a VPU), MxGLUT achieves up to **5.94× latency speedup** and **1.16× energy reduction** in attention — confirming that removing the VPU bottleneck is a major source of gains.
- **Accuracy (WikiText-103):** Max perplexity increase of only **1.70%** (Llama-3-1B). Subnormal activation ratio ≤0.33%; FTZ alone causes <0.08% variation; the larger perturbation comes from the FP8-like 8-bit product approximation.
- **Design space (MUX count N_MUX):** Energy efficiency rises 15.8 → 22.5 → 28.5 TFLOPS/W from 16 → 32 → 64 MUXes, with **64 MUX** as the knee point (power 0.20 → 0.28 → 0.45 mW; beyond 64 the gains diminish sharply).
- **State-of-the-art comparison (Table III):** Smallest area (**3.33 mm²**) among all compared designs (incl. 16nm/12nm/22nm ISSCC/VLSI parts); peak performance 1.638 TFLOPS. Despite using the least-advanced node (28nm), achieves **6.15× area efficiency** and **1.41× energy efficiency** over the most comparable mixed-precision design (ISSCC'23, 12nm FP4/FP8), indicating gains come from architecture, not process advantage.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — genuinely high.** The two central ideas are non-trivial and well-motivated: (1) mapping *continuous* FP8×FP8 multiplication onto *discrete* LUT indexing by pre-computing the full mantissa product at LUT-construction time and representing it as an 8-bit FP8-like entry; (2) a phase-aware OS/WS switch that matches LUT outer-product execution to prefill vs. decode bottlenecks. Both go meaningfully beyond FIGLUT and LUT Tensor Core, which target only FP8-INT4.
- **Rigor — strong.** Multi-granularity evaluation (arithmetic → PE → accelerator → system), explicit ablations separating the contributions of *native FP8-FP8 LUT execution* vs. *RLB dataflow selection* (MxGLUT-OS vs. -WS vs. FIGLUT+VPU comparisons), a design-space sweep identifying the 64-MUX knee, and an honest SOTA table that notes the 28nm disadvantage.
- **Reproducibility — moderate-to-good for an ASIC paper.** RTL is not released (typical for the field), but the methodology (framework, MxLPE datapath, dataflow latency/register formulas in Table I, CACTI/Timeloop flow) is specified in enough detail for an architecture group to reimplement. Per-baseline budgets are held constant, which is a strong signal of fair methodology.
- **Significance — high for LLM inference hardware.** Removing the VPU from the attention path and unifying both GEMM types under one LUT fabric is a clean, practically useful architectural simplification; the ≤1.70% perplexity cost is modest and well-attributed (FTZ + 8-bit product approximation).
- **Things to scrutinize / mild red flags:**
  - *Energy dominated by DRAM at long context.* The paper itself acknowledges decode attention gains shrink to ~1.0× because DRAM exceeds 90% of energy at long contexts (e.g., 90.08% for Llama-3-3B decode at 8K). This means the headline decode gains are concentrated in FP8-INT4 linear layers, not attention — worth reading carefully.
  - *Peak-performance figure (1.638 TFLOPS) is modest* vs. advanced-node SOTA in absolute terms; the headline wins are area/energy *efficiency* and the unified-precision story, not raw TOPS. This is framed honestly.
  - *FTZ for FP8 subnormals* is a numerical shortcut; while SAR is low (≤0.33%) on Llama, robustness on other model families / out-of-distribution activations is untested.
  - *Scope is weight-only (W4A8) FP8-centric*; the authors explicitly note MxGLUT does not target FP16/BF16, which would blow up LUT complexity — a reasonable but real boundary.
  - *Batch size 64* for end-to-end runs is favorable for array utilization; single-stream (batch-1) latency behavior (typical for edge serving) is not separately broken out.

## Strengths

- Truly unified LUT mechanism that removes dedicated FP datapaths *and* VPU fallback for attention — a clean, well-executed architectural simplification.
- Phase-aware (prefill OS / decode WS) reconfigurable dataflow with concrete latency/register-overhead formulas (Table I) justifying the broadcast design.
- Rigorous, fair experimental protocol: identical system budgets and per-design best legal mappings across all in-house baselines; explicit ablation isolating FP8-FP8 LUT execution from RLB dataflow effects.
- Honest SOTA positioning — the paper repeatedly flags its 28nm disadvantage and shows efficiency wins arise from architecture, not process node.
- Transparent disclosure of AI-tool usage limited to grammar.

## Limitations

- Custom ASIC work; results depend on synthesis/CACTI modeling assumptions and do not directly translate to FPGA or commodity GPUs.
- At long contexts, decode gains are increasingly capped by DRAM traffic (compute-energy share falls below ~37–50%), so the most dramatic wins are at short-to-moderate contexts and in FP8-INT4 linear layers.
- Evaluation is concentrated on the Llama-3 family (1B/3B/8B); behavior on Mistral/Qwen-class models with different outlier profiles is untested.
- FP8-centric scope: no FP16/BF16 path; activation-quantization (e.g., INT8 activations) regimes are out of scope.
- FTZ subnormal handling, while well-justified here, is a numerical shortcut whose robustness on harder/out-of-distribution activations is not explored.

## Relevance

对于关注 LLM 推理硬件加速、混合精度 GEMM 与权重量化部署的研究者，MxGLUT 提供了一种将 LUT 机制统一扩展到 FP8-FP8 注意力计算的硅验证方案，并以阶段感知的 OS/WS 可重构数据流匹配 prefill/decode 的异构瓶颈，对边缘/数据中心高效推理加速器设计有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How exactly does the 8-bit FP8-like product approximation accumulate error in deeper / longer-context models, and could a 9–10 bit entry meaningfully cut the 1.70% perplexity gap?
- What is the batch-1 (single-stream) end-to-end latency/energy behavior, which matters most for interactive edge serving?
- How do results change under joint activation+weight quantization (e.g., W4A8 with INT8 attention), not just weight-only FP8 attention?
- Can the unified LUT fabric extend to FP8-FP8 in E5M2 (wider exponent) without disproportionate entry growth?
- How does MxGLUT compare against recent non-LUT digital mixed-precision accelerators beyond the 28nm SOTA table?
- Is the 64-MUX knee point stable across model sizes, or does the optimal PE parallelism shift for very large/small models?
