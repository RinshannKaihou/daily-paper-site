> **一句话总结：** ProWAFT 提出一种面向 FPGA CNN 加速器的主动式负载感知容错框架，通过部分重构在六个可配置分区上按 Workload Criticality Score 与时变 SEU 风险选择性地施加 TMR；在 ZCU104 上对 500 任务轨迹评估，相对静态 TMR 吞吐提升 45.9%、能耗降低 30.3%、复合代价降低 33.3%，且在线决策开销仅 0.50 ms。

> **TL;DR:** ProWAFT is a proactive, workload-aware fault-tolerance framework for FPGA CNN accelerators that uses dynamic partial reconfiguration to apply TMR selectively, driven by a Workload Criticality Score (WCS) and a fault-propagation-based Reliability Risk Score. On a Xilinx Zynq ZCU104 with 6 reconfigurable regions and a 500-task trace from ResNet-18/MobileNetV2/EfficientNet-Lite under time-varying SEU injection (p_fault ∈ [0.001, 0.01]), it beats Static-TMR (+45.9% throughput, −30.3% energy, −33.3% composite cost) and Reactive-Reconfig (+18.7% throughput, −17.1% energy, 98.8% success vs 96.4%) while keeping online decision overhead at 0.50 ms.

## 核心贡献

- **主动容错范式**：首次将 FPGA CNN 容错建模为运行时配置选择问题，在"始终开启全 TMR"与"被动故障后恢复"之间开辟"预测式按需施加"的新设计点。
- **轻量决策建模栈**：提出 Workload Criticality Score (WCS) 与基于故障传播的 Reliability Risk Score (RRS, 由 FPF 驱动)，并将延迟、能耗、可靠性风险统一为复合代价 `Ctotal`。
- **显式 PR 开销建模**：将部分重构的时间/能耗开销作为独立项纳入决策目标，避免"保护施加过晚、过广或过频"。
- **真实硬件实现**：在 Xilinx Zynq UltraScale+ ZCU104 上以 K=6 个可重构区域实现，加速器库包含 CE/PU/BAU 的基线版与 TMR 版。
- **完整实验与消融**：500 任务轨迹、4 个研究问题、消融与敏感性分析；WCS、传播模型、策略三者各自贡献被量化（移除任一使 `Ctotal` 上升 13%–22%）。

## Key Contributions

- **Proactive (not always-on, not reactive) fault tolerance** for FPGA CNN accelerators, formulated as a runtime configuration-selection problem over a pre-synthesized library of baseline and TMR-protected variants.
- **A lightweight decision-modeling stack**: a Workload Criticality Score (WCS = α·Sdata + β·Scontrol + γ·Perror) combined with a Fault Propagation Factor (FPF) and a normalized Reliability Risk Score (RRS), collapsed into a composite cost `Ctotal`.
- **Explicit PR-overhead term** in the decision objective, so the controller avoids over-triggering reconfiguration (a known flaw of threshold-based reactive schemes).
- **Real-hardware implementation** on a Xilinx Zynq UltraScale+ ZCU104 with K=6 reconfigurable regions and an accelerator library of Convolution Engine / Pooling Unit / BatchNorm-Activation Unit, each in baseline and TMR variants.
- **Comprehensive empirical validation**: 500-task trace from ResNet-18/MobileNetV2/EfficientNet-Lite, four research questions (effectiveness, adaptivity, overhead, component contribution), plus ablations and sensitivity analysis.

## Methodology

ProWAFT treats the FPGA accelerator as K reconfigurable partitions P = {P1…PK}, each hostable with one of Nk pre-synthesized variants from a library Fk (baseline + TMR-protected). A *system configuration* Cj ∈ C picks one variant per partition. A stream of CNN-layer workloads W = {W1, W2, …} arrives, each described by a feature vector (operator type, input shape, precision, batch). The health state at time t is H(t) = {p^fault_1(t), …, p^fault_K(t)}, and a PR budget B_PR (time/energy) is enforced per execution window.

**1. Workload Criticality Score (WCS).** Quantifies how fault-sensitive Wi is:

  WCS(Wi) = α·Sdata(Wi) + β·Scontrol(Wi) + γ·Perror(Wi),  α+β+γ=1, each term ∈[0,1]

- *Sdata* — data sensitivity, derived from entropy of the output activation distribution, profiled offline and stored as a layer-wise lookup table.
- *Scontrol* — control criticality (0/1) for conditional paths; 0 for plain feed-forward CNNs.
- *Perror* — error-propagation likelihood from single-bit fault injection, stored as a compact table/regression model.

**2. Fault-aware performance and risk.** Baseline (fault-free) latency/energy are predicted analytically from Op-counts, clock frequency, processing-element count, and dynamic/static power. Fault propagation is captured by:

  FPF(Pk, Wi, Cj) = WCS(Wi) · λk(Cj) · Fanout(Pk, Cj)

where λk is utilization ratio and Fanout is the number of downstream partitions consuming Pk's output. The dimensionless Reliability Risk Score is:

  RRS(Cj, Wi, t) = (1/Z) · Σk p^fault_k(t) · FPF(Pk, Wi, Cj) · ρk

with severity weight ρk and normalization Z so RRS ∈ [0,1]. Risk is then coupled to efficiency via fault-aware predictions T̂ = Tbase·(1 + εT·RRS), Ê = Ebase·(1 + εE·RRS), with εT, εE from offline characterization.

**3. PR overhead.** Switching Ca→Cb costs ∆PR = ωT·Treconfig + ωE·Ereconfig, characterized offline.

**4. Policy / online decision.** Per-workload composite cost:

  Ctotal(Cj, Wi, t) = ηT·T̃ + ηE·Ẽ + ηR·RRS

with normalized T̃, Ẽ (dimensionless). The controller uses a low-overhead *receding-horizon* rule:

  J(Cj) = Ctotal(Cj, Wi, t) + 𝟙[Cj ≠ Ccurrent]·∆PR(Ccurrent→Cj)
  at = argmin_{Cj ∈ C(Wi)} J(Cj)

infeasible actions (those that breach the remaining PR budget) are filtered. (Figure 2 labels this as a "Bi-level MDP Policy"; the implementation is a receding-horizon greedy-with-PR-penalty controller — note this terminology mismatch.)

## Results & Highlights

**Platform / setup.** Xilinx Zynq UltraScale+ ZCU104 (504K LC, ARM A53 quad @1.5 GHz), K=6 reconfigurable regions. 500-task trace from ResNet-18 / MobileNetV2 / EfficientNet-Lite (Conv2D, DepthwiseConv2D, Pooling, FC; inputs 32×32–224×224; WCS range 0.15–0.85). SEU injection on configuration bits, p_fault ∈ [0.001, 0.01], sinusoidal + random baseline. Detection via lightweight parity check. Default weights (ηT,ηE,ηR) = (0.4, 0.3, 0.3).

**Overall (RQ1, Table 5):**

| Method | Norm. Throughput | Energy (J) | Success Rate (%) | Ctotal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Static-Base | 1.00 | 185.2 | 78.0 | 0.72 |
| Static-TMR | 0.61 | 302.5 | 100.0 | 0.81 |
| Reactive-Reconfig (RR) | 0.75 | 254.1 | 96.4 | 0.78 |
| **ProWAFT** | **0.89** | **210.7** | **98.8** | **0.54** |

- vs Static-TMR: throughput +45.9% (0.61→0.89), energy −30.3% (302.5→210.7 J), success rate only −1.2 pp.
- vs RR: throughput +18.7%, energy −17.1%, success +2.4 pp, Ctotal 0.78→0.54 (−30.8%).
- vs Static-TMR: Ctotal 0.81→0.54 (−33.3%).

**Adaptivity (RQ2, Table 6).** TMR usage scales from 12.3% (low risk) to 95.2% (critical workloads); decision latency stays 0.38–0.51 ms across regimes vs 15.3 ms post-fault for the RR baseline.

**Overhead (RQ3, Table 7).** Total decision overhead 0.50 ms / 0.13 mJ (dominated by candidate evaluation and WCS). Single-partition PR 4.20 ms; total proactive overhead (decision+PR) 4.70–9.00 ms vs 15.30 ms for RR recovery — **3.3× lower**.

**Ablation (RQ4, Table 8).** Each component matters:
- Full ProWAFT: Ctotal 0.54, SR 98.8%.
- w/o WCS (uniform criticality): Ctotal 0.63, SR 95.1%.
- w/o FPF (no propagation model): Ctotal 0.61, SR 94.3%.
- w/o policy (greedy): Ctotal 0.66, SR 97.2%. ← largest degradation.
- Removing any component raises Ctotal by 13%–22%.

**Sensitivity (Table 9).** Weight shifts (performance-/energy-/reliability-focused) move the operating point predictably (Ctotal 0.48–0.58, SR 96.2–99.5%); ±20% WCS threshold and ±25% fault-probability estimates cause bounded degradation — stable under reasonable modeling errors.

## Authors & Credibility

All six authors (Xinxin Chen, Haoran Qiao, Yiming Guo, Kecheng Luo, Siyuan Feng, Jingwen Ma) are listed at the Department of Computer Science, University of Chinese Academy of Sciences. The artifact — real ZCU104 hardware with measured PR timing, six reconfigurable regions, and a characterized accelerator library — is consistent with credible FPGA systems work. The methodology is technically coherent and the equations are well-formed.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — moderate, real.** The "proactive, workload-aware, PR-driven selective TMR" framing sits genuinely between always-on TMR and reactive recovery, and the explicit PR-overhead term in the objective is a sensible contribution over threshold-based PR schemes. The composite cost / WCS / FPF / RRS stack is well-engineered, though individual pieces (TMR, scrubbing, vulnerability analysis, receding-horizon control) are established — the novelty is in the *combination*.
- **Rigor — good on the systems side, weak on citation hygiene.** Real hardware, four RQs, ablations, sensitivity, reproducible seed. Numbers are internally consistent and the trade-offs behave as the model predicts. **However, the reference list is a serious red flag (see below).**
- **🚩 Major red flag — references are topically unrelated.** Every cited work is on multimodal LLMs / document understanding / scene-text recognition (a large cluster around Jingqun Tang, Hao Feng, Can Huang, "Shanghai AI Lab", Seed1.5-VL, OCRBench, Dolphin, TextSquare, etc.). Not a single cited paper is on FPGA reliability, soft errors, TMR, partial reconfiguration, or CNN accelerator hardening — the actual topic. The in-text citations appear as decorative superscripts attached to generic sentences rather than supporting specific technical claims. This is characteristic of either citation-dumping (to inflate related-work coverage) or an LLM-generated bibliography, and it materially undermines the paper's scholarly credibility even though the technical content stands on its own.
- **🚩 Category mismatch.** Filed under `cs.CL` (Computation and Language); the correct primary category is plainly `cs.AR` (Hardware & Architecture) with `eess.SP` / `eess.Architecture` as secondary. This is almost certainly an arXiv classification error, but it is unusual and worth noting.
- **🚩 "ROMA-LPD Instance" is never defined.** The title advertises ProWAFT as an instance of a framework called ROMA-LPD, but the body never explains what ROMA-LPD is, where it was published, or what is inherited vs. novel. The reader cannot tell how much of the architecture is original to this paper.
- **🚩 Terminology mismatch.** Figure 2 labels stage 4 as a "Bi-level MDP Policy," but §3.4 implements a receding-horizon greedy-with-PR-penalty controller. These are not the same thing; the MDP formulation is asserted but never solved as such.
- **Reproducibility — medium.** Equations, weights, and parameters are fully specified, and the platform/library/trace/fault-model settings are tabulated. But no mention of releasing bitstreams, constraint files, or the SEU-injection harness — and PR flows are notoriously hard to reproduce without them.
- **Significance — genuine but niche.** Edge FPGA CNN reliability is a real and growing concern; the demonstrated 3.3× lower proactive overhead than reactive recovery, at near-baseline throughput with 98.8% success, is a useful design point *if* the results generalize beyond the synthetic SEU model.

## Strengths

- Clear, well-motivated positioning between two extreme baselines (always-on TMR vs. reactive recovery).
- Multi-objective composite cost (latency + energy + reliability) with an *explicit* PR-overhead term — the latter is the genuine methodological improvement over threshold-based PR schemes.
- Workload criticality is principled (entropy-based data sensitivity + control criticality + error-propagation likelihood) rather than a hand-tuned heuristic.
- Real ZCU104 hardware, not simulation; measured PR timing in milliseconds.
- Evaluated across three architecturally diverse CNNs (heavy ResNet-18, depthwise MobileNetV2, compact EfficientNet-Lite).
- Time-varying, sinusoidal+random SEU model is more realistic than constant-rate injection.
- Thorough ablation isolating WCS, FPF, and the policy; sensitivity analysis over weights and estimation error.

## Limitations

- **Reference list is unrelated to the topic** (see Critical Assessment) — the most consequential weakness.
- Software-based SEU injection cannot capture all physical mechanisms; the authors flag radiation/thermal testing as future work.
- Composite cost compresses three objectives into one scalar; the (ηT,ηE,ηR) weighting is the only knob exposed, and Pareto behavior across weight settings is summarized, not plotted in detail.
- "ROMA-LPD" framework is referenced in the title but undefined in the body.
- Scope is CNN-only; applicability to transformer attention / other model families is unaddressed.
- No code/bitstream release mentioned; reproducibility of the PR flow is uncertain.
- Decision controller is described as receding-horizon but Figure 2 calls it a "Bi-level MDP Policy" — the MDP is not actually formulated or solved.
- Fault-probability estimates (p_fault(t)) are assumed available at runtime; how they are obtained in a real deployment (online monitors? scrubbing stats?) is not specified.

## Relevance

与边缘端 FPGA 上 CNN 推理可靠性、动态部分重构及"能耗-延迟-可靠性"多目标权衡方向高度契合，可为"按需容错"类系统设计提供可复用的建模栈（WCS/FPF/RRS/复合代价）。但其范畴聚焦硬件系统，对以大模型/算法为主的兴趣线关联较弱；且参考文献与主题严重错位，引用其结论时需谨慎独立核验。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What is ROMA-LPD, and how much of ProWAFT's architecture is inherited from it vs. novel to this paper?
- How does ProWAFT compare specifically against selective-TMR, DWC, and adaptive-scrubbing schemes (not just full TMR vs. reactive)?
- How is p_fault(t) obtained at runtime in a real deployment — from scrubbing statistics, online monitors, or environmental sensors? The framework assumes it as input.
- Does the receding-horizon controller actually solve the MDP Figure 2 claims, or is the "Bi-level MDP Policy" label aspirational?
- How does the WCS/FPF formulation generalize to transformer attention blocks (matmul-heavy, no depthwise conv structure)?
- Are bitstreams, constraint files, and the SEU-injection harness released?
- How do results change under a *physical* fault campaign (radiation / thermal stress), which the authors themselves flag as missing?
- The cited literature is entirely off-topic — was the bibliography auto-generated, and does it indicate deeper integrity issues with the manuscript?
