At a Glance

four extracted assets
Key Metric
15–40%
Trait-expression loss when combining two steering vectors (range −15.7 to −40.1 PPCM across 4 composition methods × 2 models). Every method tested degrades — none eliminates the tradeoff.
Why This Matters
Calibrates training-free steering vectors' real limits as lightweight alignment — don't extrapolate single-trait wins into general capability.
Surfaces concrete failure modes (unconventional traits, task transfer, vector-count scaling) that future combiners must fix.
Critical Edge
Only two ~7–8B models and stylistic traits tested — and no method a learned/adaptive combiner is ever tried, so the “no method wins” verdict stays provisional.
Scale effects (70B+), safety/honesty traits, and SAE-gating all untested.

Results & highlights

four findings, distilled
  • Trait expressibility. Effectiveness varies dramatically by trait type — structural traits steer reliably, unconventional devices fail or shatter coherence. Qwen tends toward incoherent failures, Llama toward unexpressed failures.
  • Task transfer. Vectors from style-elicitation prompts transfer poorly to PLUME tasks. Summarization (more impersonal) degrades most; tweet style actually strengthens downstream — vectors capture task-conditioned aspects, not pure trait essence.
  • Composition. All four methods lose ≥15% (up to ~40%) expression for two-vector combos. Layer-20 unit-norm is the sole expression+coherence balance, but with unbalanced per-trait expression.
  • Scaling 1→4 vectors. Expression declines monotonically; most methods hit near-zero/negative PPCM at four traits. Unit-norm keeps coherence best (it even rises) — but only because α needs up-scaling.
§ 04

Methodology

PersonaVectors extraction · four composition schemes

ASteering-vector extraction

  1. Paired data generation. Claude-3.7-Sonnet writes 125 positive / 125 negative trait-eliciting questions, paired with content-agnostic subjects (“How important is exercise?”) so vectors stay content-independent.
  2. Activation extraction. Layer-level difference of mean hidden activations between trait-present & trait-absent responses: v = h̄₊ − h̄₋. Candidates focus on layers 16 and 20.
  3. Steering at inference. Add α·v to the residual stream: h̃ = h + α·v, with layer l and strength α tuned.
  4. Composition. Four schemes: orthogonalized, different-layers, tuned-mean, unit-norm.

BSetup & evaluation

α & layer tuning. Coarse integer search [1,5] over candidate layers, then a fine ±0.5 sweep at 0.1 steps; config maximizes expression subject to coherence ≥75, with early termination at expression ≥90.

Evaluation (LLM-as-judge). Three GPT scores — PLUME trait expression via GPT-4o (−2..+2 Likert → PPCM); extraction expression & coherence via GPT-4.1-mini (0–100). Human validation inherited from prior work, not re-run.

Strengths & Limitations

side by side

✓Strengths

what the paper does well
  • Clear, principled three-axis framework that maps the full design space of steering-vector application.
  • Honest reporting of per-setting α dependence and the unit-norm α-rescaling need — no overclaiming.
  • Credible, reproducible setup: public models, dedicated personalization benchmark, released code & prompts.
  • Identifies which trait categories fail (unconventional / local devices) — actionable for feature engineering.
  • Quantifies trait expression and coherence jointly, avoiding the controllability-only trap.

⚠Limitations

caveats & red flags
  • Only two similarly-sized (~7–8B) models — limits at 70B+ and other architectures unverified.
  • Stylistic / personalization traits only; safety, honesty, and task-capability traits untested.
  • Relies on LLM-as-judge (GPT-4o / 4.1-mini) with no new human-in-the-loop validation.
  • Four hand-picked composition methods; learned / attention-based combiners (SAE-gating) not explored.
  • Stricter-than-standard coherence threshold (75 vs 50) may bias against expressive-but-loose methods.
  • Transfer-gap conflates trait essence vs task conditioning with no disentangling experiment.

Open Questions

for further exploration
  • SCALEDo trait-transfer and composition-degradation patterns persist at 30B–70B, or does capacity soften the tradeoff?
  • COMBINERCould a learned/adaptive composition weight recover multi-trait expression without sacrificing coherence?
  • FEATURESWould SAE-derived sparse features as steering directions change the expressibility ranking (e.g., make rhyme/screenplay steerable)?
  • TRANSFERWould re-extracting vectors on the downstream task distribution close the transfer gap — and at what interpretability cost?
  • SCOPEDo the limits extend beyond style to safety/honesty traits, where steering is also pitched as a data monitor?
  • MECHANISMIs the coherence–expressibility tradeoff fundamental to residual-stream additive steering, or can non-additive (gating/attention) interventions escape it?
