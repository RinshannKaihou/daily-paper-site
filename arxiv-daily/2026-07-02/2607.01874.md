> **一句话总结：** SkillCoach 是一个自进化的评估框架，将 Agent 技能使用定义为轨迹级"元能力"（涵盖技能选择/遵循/组合/反思四维），通过从真实 rollout 提炼并经"证据驱动判定—局部仲裁补丁—验证门控更新"三阶段进化的评分准则（rubrics），将过程质量与外部 verifier 的偶然性成功分离，进化后的 rubrics 既能暴露被最终准确率掩盖的失败，又能作为过程监督信号筛选出比"仅看结果"更好的 SFT 训练轨迹（Qwen3.5-4B/9B 分别提升 24.0%/32.0%）。

> **TL;DR:** SkillCoach defines agentic skill-use as a four-dimensional trajectory-level meta-ability (selection / following / composition / reflection) and self-evolves task-level rubrics from real rollouts via evidence-grounded judging, local arbitration patches, and validation-gated updates—kept strictly separate from the external verifier outcome signal. On skill-dependent tasks with distractor-augmented libraries, the evolved rubrics become more faithful evaluators (filtering consistency 82→96%), expose failures hidden by final accuracy, and select better SFT demonstrations than outcome-only filtering (Qwen3.5-4B 8→24%, 9B 14→32%). A large-scale distractor-boundary study (up to 50k skills) shows even frontier models degrade under plausible near-neighbor skills.

## 核心贡献

- **首次将 Agent 技能使用形式化为轨迹级元能力**：定义为涵盖「技能选择（selection）、技能遵循（following）、技能组合（composition）、技能落地反思（reflection）」四维的过程能力，并将其与外部 verifier 信号严格分离。
- **首个自进化 rubric 框架 SkillCoach**：通过三阶段（rollout 收集 → 证据驱动判定 → 仲裁补丁 + 验证门控）从真实轨迹中校准任务级过程评分准则；验证门控包含硬门（拒绝破坏性修改）与软目标（要求实质性改进），防止"仅靠提高判定置信度"蒙混过关。
- **rubric 双重用途**：既提升评估质量、暴露被最终准确率掩盖的过程级失败，又作为监督过滤器筛选高质量训练轨迹，比"仅看结果"的 SFT 更有效。
- **面向真实技能库的设定（skill-dependent + 干扰技能）**：提出技能依赖性过滤协议（p_no≤0.30, p_gold−p_no≥0.40, c_key≥0.70），并注入"跨任务 + 语义近似但不可用"的干扰技能，逼近企业级共享技能库的真实噪声环境。
- **大规模技能选择边界分析（最大 50k 技能）**：定义"退化边界"与"崩溃边界"，揭示前沿模型（Gemini 3.1 Pro、GPT-5.5）在仅 ~45–55 个干扰技能时即开始退化，高相似度干扰比随机干扰更具破坏性。

## Key Contributions

- **First formalization of agentic skill-use as a trajectory-level meta-ability** spanning skill selection, skill following, skill composition, and skill-grounded reflection—deliberately kept separate from the external verifier outcome signal.
- **First self-evolving rubric framework (SkillCoach)** that calibrates task-level process rubrics from real rollouts through evidence-grounded judging, local arbitration patches, and validation-gated updates, under a skill-dependent setting with distractor-augmented libraries that mirror enterprise repositories.
- **Dual role of evolved rubrics**: they improve evaluation quality and expose failures hidden by final accuracy, AND serve as a supervision filter that selects better SFT trajectories than outcome-only filtering.
- **Realistic skill-dependent + distractor setting**: a skill-dependency filtering protocol (p_no≤0.30, p_gold−p_no≥0.40, c_key≥0.70) plus injection of cross-task and semantically-near-but-inapplicable distractor skills, approximating noisy enterprise skill libraries.
- **Large-scale skill-selection boundary analysis (up to 50k skills)**: defines "degradation boundary" and "collapse boundary," showing frontier models (Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT-5.5) begin degrading at only ~45–55 distractors, and high-similarity distractors are far more damaging than random ones.

## Methodology

**Overall framework (3 stages):**
1. **Environment & Task setup**: Each task is a skill-dependent task τ_t = (x_t, y_t, E_t, V_t, L_t), where the skill library L_t = G_t ∪ D_t contains gold skills G_t and distractor skills D_t. The agent π_θ interacts with the executable environment and produces a trajectory z_t; the verifier V_t is applied *only after* completion and never available during interaction.
2. **SkillCoach core (self-evolving rubric)**: Derives an initial rubric R0 from task instruction, gold/distractor skills, oracle solution, verifier info, and sample rollouts, then evolves it via three substages:
   - **Rollout stage**: collects real trajectories in L_t = G_t ∪ D_t.
   - **Judge stage**: applies the current rubric to extracted evidence (skill reads, tool calls, file edits, script executions, artifacts, validation attempts) and scores the four process dimensions. Skill selection is **rule-dominant** (mechanical detection of SKILL.md reads); the other three use LLM judges requiring event-index evidence citation.
   - **Arbitration stage**: a separate arbitration model proposes a localized patch (add criterion/negative case/evidence requirement/score rule/order dependency/handoff requirement). A **validation gate** accepts the patch only if: (a) no regression on the hard gate H (no destructive changes like ignoring distractors or bypassing the verifier), (b) soft objective Q improves by >ε=0.2, (c) improvement includes at least one *material* signal (not just increased judge confidence), and (d) no structural violation. The best rubric Rbest is selected by held-out validation performance, not the last version.
3. **Downstream uses**: trajectory diagnosis (pinpointing process-level failures) and rubric-filtered SFT training.

**The four meta-ability dimensions** (each with a closed-form scoring formula):
- **Skill selection** s_sel: a set-level F1 between selected skills Ŝ(z_t) and gold skills G_t, penalizing both missed gold and selected distractors; handles negative abstention when no gold skill exists.
- **Skill following** s_fol: weighted completion of rubric-defined key steps K_t, where each step's completion c_k∈{0,0.5,1} is gated by an evidence multiplier m_k∈{0,1} preventing unearned credit.
- **Skill composition** s_comp: whether precedence dependencies P_t are satisfied and intermediate artifacts transferred correctly (marked N/A for single-skill tasks).
- **Skill-grounded reflection** s_ref: whether explicit, visible pre-submission checks H_t were performed (distinct from verifier success).

**Aggregation**: a scalar meta score S_meta = Σλ_d·s_d over applicable dimensions (default weights λ_sel=0.40, λ_fol=0.30, λ_comp=0.20, λ_ref=0.10), kept separate from the verifier signal s_ver∈{0,1,⊥}.

**Skill-dependency filtering protocol**: tasks are accepted only if no-skill success p_no ≤ 0.30, gold-skill gain p_gold−p_no ≥ 0.40, and key-step coverage c_key ≥ 0.70 (estimated across 5 strong agent backends with semi-automatic LLM-assisted annotation). Selected tasks show +55.2 pp gold-skill gain vs +13.4 pp for the unfiltered SkillsBench pool.

**Distractor setting (default)**: each task paired with 2 cross-task skills + 3 semantically-near-but-inapplicable distractors. For boundary analysis, the library scales to 50k real SKILL.md files (35,554 from the Skill-Usage pool + SkillHub extensions).

## Results & Highlights

**Table 2 — Rubric quality validation (50 paired instances, 10 held-out task families, judged by Gemini 3.1 Pro @ T=0 as independent rubric-quality judge; human-gold references from oracle materials):**

| Metric | R0 (initial) | Rbest (evolved) | Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gold-keypoint coverage | 71.56 | 83.70 | +12.14 pp |
| Usability score | 81.53 | 94.33 | +12.80 pts |
| Hallucination rate | 2.00 | 0.00 | −2.00 pp |
| Filtering consistency (vs human-gold) | 82.00 | 96.00 | +14.00 pp |

Evolution improves all four metrics; notably hallucination drops to zero, proving coverage gains do not come from inventing unsupported requirements.

**Table 3 — Overall skill-use performance (3 library settings × 7 models, %):**
- Skills help: Opus 4.7 jumps 18.0→88.0 (no-skill→gold), GPT-5.5 24.0→80.0.
- Distractors hurt: e.g., Gemini 3.1 Pro selection drops 98.0→78.0; Qwen3.5-9B selection collapses 92.0→44.0 with reflection 76.0→34.0.
- Final accuracy hides failure modes: a high verifier score doesn't reveal whether failure stems from wrong skill choice, incomplete following, weak composition, or missing checks.

**Table 4 — Rubric-filtered SFT ablation (Gold+Distractors, final accuracy %):**
| Setting | 4B | 9B |
|---|---|---|
| Base Qwen3.5 | 8.0 | 14.0 |
| Outcome-only SFT | 6.0 | 18.0 |
| Full SFT with R0 | 16.0 | 28.0 |
| **Full SFT with Rbest** | **24.0** | **32.0** |
| w/o composition order | 18.0 | 26.0 |
| w/o reflection | 24.0 | 28.0 |
| w/o key-step following | 10.0 | 16.0 |

Key findings: outcome-only SFT *hurts* the 4B model (8→6) because verifier-passing trajectories include distractor-selection and trial-and-error; rubric-filtered SFT triples it (8→24). Removing key-step following causes the largest drop, confirming faithful step execution is the most valuable supervision signal.

**Figure 4 — Distractor-boundary analysis (up to 50k skills):**
- **Degradation boundaries**: DeepSeek V4 Flash ~26–27 distractors, Gemini 3.1 Pro ~45–46, GPT-5.5 ~55–56, Opus 4.7 ~194–195. Even frontier models degrade quickly under candidate expansion.
- **Collapse boundaries** (80% of samples select no gold skill): DeepSeek ~6.4k, Kimi K2.6 ~20k, Gemini 3.1 Pro ~35k. GPT-5.5 and Opus 4.7 do *not* collapse even at 50k (Opus still F1=0.46 at 50k).
- **Distractor type matters**: with 50 fixed distractors, GPT-5.5 selection F1 drops from 0.84 (random unrelated) to 0.59 (high-similarity)—high-similarity near-neighbors are the most dangerous, matching the production risk of plausible-but-wrong skills.
- **Boundary control vs. exact selection**: most models abstain correctly on non-trigger requests, but gold-present exact selection still varies substantially—measuring only "did it trigger a skill" overestimates capability.

**Evolution run statistics**: across 28 tasks, 94 total rounds, avg 3.36 rounds/task, max 6 rounds; early stop on 3 consecutive rejections or empty/illegal patches.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High)**: The framing of "skill-use as a measurable, dissociable trajectory-level meta-ability" and the *skill-structure-grounded* rubric evolution is genuinely novel relative to generic trajectory-level / process-reward work (Agent-as-a-Judge, AgentProcessBench, ToolPRMBench, AdaRubric). The explicit decoupling of process quality from the external verifier, plus the rule-dominant skill-selection design, is a clean conceptual contribution. The distractor-boundary analysis (degradation vs. collapse, up to 50k skills) is a notably original diagnostic.
- **Rigor (Good, with caveats)**:
  - *Strengths*: Rubric quality is validated against human-gold references with a *separate* judge model (Gemini 3.1 Pro) and explicit exclusion of verifier signals—a thoughtful design to avoid circularity. The SFT ablation cleanly isolates each process dimension. The skill-dependency filtering protocol is principled and quantitatively justified (Table 1).
  - *Caveats*: (1) The "human-gold rubrics" used for evaluation in §4.1/§4.2 are built by only **two annotators**; inter-annotator agreement is not reported. (2) Rubric-quality validation covers only 50 paired instances from 10 task families—a modest sample. (3) The SFT evaluation is limited to Qwen3.5 4B/9B; whether gains transfer to mid-size or other families is untested. (4) Absolute final accuracies remain modest (24–32% under Gold+Distractors), so the practical ceiling of this supervision is not yet bounded.
- **Reproducibility (Fair)**: Prompt templates are near-complete (only task payloads redacted); validation gate is code-level (not LLM). *However*, no code/data release is mentioned in the paper body. Tasks derive from SkillsBench/SkillLearnBench/Skill-Usage pool (cited), so partial reconstruction is possible, but the human-gold rubrics and curated distractor pairings are not stated to be public.
- **Significance (Medium-High)**: If claims hold, SkillCoach offers a deployable pattern for curating agent training data and auditing skill-use in enterprise settings. The distractor-boundary findings are independently valuable for anyone building skill-retrieval systems.
- **Red flags / concerns**:
  - The paper relies on a *proprietary* notion of "evolved rubric quality" judged by another LLM (Gemini 3.1 Pro); while the separation from the verifier is careful, LLM-as-rubric-judge has known biases that are not discussed.
  - The headline training gains (8→24, 14→32) are on the *same* task distribution used for rubric evolution; cross-distribution generalization of the filter is not stressed.
  - Some model names/versions (Opus 4.7, GPT-5.5, Gemini 3.1 Pro, DeepSeek V4 Flash, Kimi K2.6, Qwen3.5) are forward-dated relative to widely-known public checkpoints—readers should verify availability.
  - No comparison to a *strong process-reward-model* baseline (e.g., a trained PRM) for trajectory selection—only outcome-only SFT is contrasted.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clean: four well-motivated, formally-defined, *dissociable* process dimensions with closed-form scoring.
- Validation-gated evolution with explicit hard/soft gates and a "material improvement" requirement is a sensible safeguard against rubric drift and judge-confidence gaming.
- Decoupling the external verifier as a pure outcome signal (never exposed to the agent, never part of the meta score) is methodologically sound.
- The distractor-boundary study (degradation vs. collapse; type sensitivity) is a genuinely useful production diagnostic not found in prior skill benchmarks.
- Ablations directly answer "which dimension matters most" (key-step following >> composition > reflection).
- Honest about limitations (scale, RL not yet explored).

## Limitations

- **Task scale**: 18 training + 10 test task families—small relative to production repositories; generalization to continuously-growing libraries is unproven.
- **Training scope**: only offline SFT evaluated; on-policy RL with SkillCoach process rewards is explicitly deferred to future work.
- **Annotation**: human-gold rubrics from only two annotators; no inter-annotator agreement reported.
- **Model coverage**: SFT results only on Qwen3.5 4B/9B; transfer to other families/sizes untested.
- **Rubric-judge bias**: reliance on LLM-as-rubric-judge without discussing its known failure modes.
- **No code/data release stated**: reproduction depends on accessing the underlying benchmarks and the (unreleased) curated distractors and human-gold rubrics.
- **Absolute performance still modest**: even the best rubric-filtered SFT reaches only 24–32% under realistic distractor conditions.

## Relevance

与 Agent 训练数据筛选、技能使用评估及过程监督高度相关——SkillCoach 的"过程质量与偶然成功分离"思路、四维元能力分解、以及 rubric 驱动的轨迹筛选方法，对构建可靠 Agent 训练流水线、评估体系和技能库检索/排序优化都有直接借鉴价值；其大规模干扰技能边界分析对企业级共享技能库的部署决策尤其有参考意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does SkillCoach generalize when skills themselves evolve over time (lifecycle management), not just when the rubric evolves?
- Can the process meta score S_meta serve as a dense RL reward, and does RL outperform the current offline-SFT-filtering results?
- What is inter-annotator agreement on the human-gold rubrics, and how sensitive are Table 2/3 numbers to annotator disagreement?
- How do the four dimensions' relative weights (0.40/0.30/0.20/0.10) interact—were they tuned, and is the key-step dominance an artifact of the chosen weights?
- Does the rubric-judge (LLM-as-judge) introduce systematic biases (e.g., verbosity bias, position bias) that the validation gate fails to catch?
- Can the degradation/collapse boundary analysis be turned into an actionable *retrieval/ranking* policy (e.g., cap candidate pool at the degradation boundary)?
- How well do the SFT gains transfer to held-out task *families* beyond the 10 test families, and to larger or differently-aligned base models?
