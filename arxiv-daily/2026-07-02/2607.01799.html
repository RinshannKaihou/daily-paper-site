Why This Matters

Directly attacks the cost wall of training & deploying huge SAE feature dictionaries — a scalable, theoretically-grounded decoder-compression path that plugs into existing TopK / JumpReLU workflows.

Critical Edge

The 84% headline is one cell; at modern scale a matched-parameter dense decoder edges Expander out under the trained encoder — Expander only pulls ahead after ~140× slower iterative OMP.

01 / METHOD

How an expander mask rewrites the decoder

Architecture tied TopK

A tied-weight TopK SAE whose decoder columns (and tied encoder) live on a frozen binary mask.

Mask M∈{0,1}^{m×n} has exactly d ones per column, sampled once and frozen. Only those dn positions of V get gradients.

W_dec = (V ⊙ M) diag(ν)⁻¹  ·  W_enc = W_dec⊤
  • Total learnable params dn + n + m (was mn)
  • Forward σ(z) = TopK_k(z) — keep k largest signed pre-activations
  • Column normalisation ν forces unit-ℓ2 decoder atoms

Theory identifiability

Grounded in combinatorial compressed sensing — expander matrices satisfy RIP-1.

For a (k,ε,d)-expander, (1−2ε)√d‖x‖₁ ≤ ‖Mx‖₁ ≤ √d‖x‖₁. Column flatness β=√d·max|W_ij| measures deviation from an ideal binary expander.

  • Theorem 3.1 — if 2β²ε<1, every noiseless k-sparse code has a unique k-sparse explanation
  • Corollary 3.2 — under β²ε(2k+1)<1, OMP recovers the support exactly in k steps
  • Honesty note — worst-case sufficient conditions, not a certificate of the experimental regime

Parallel OMP decoder

Turns the costly correlation step into a cheap sparse reduction.

The expander structure makes W_dec⊤r an O(dn) gather-and-reduce — yielding a Pareto frontier between fast amortised encoder inference and high-fidelity iterative decoding.

j* = argmax_{j∉S} |⟨w_j , r⟩|  →  Cholesky refit → residual update

Control A

Budget-matched dense

Shrink n to n′=dn/m. Expander beats it by 0.10–0.21 CE at every shared budget on Pythia-70M — because shrinking n changes the recovery ratio δ=m/n.

Control B

Clustered-sparse

Partition rows into G=⌊m/d⌋ blocks, killing support diversity. Tracks Expander early, then dead features explode ~100× at d=200.

Control C

Pruned dense

Extract a sparse mask from a pretrained dense SAE. Closes most of the rel-err gap — but needs a second training pass (2× compute).

03 / TRADE-OFFS

What it earns, what it owes

＋Strengths
  • Principled compression. Decoder complexity cut via a well-understood graph object with direct links to RIP-1 theory and the author's own prior compressed-sensing work.
  • Modern-LM headline numbers. Strong efficiency on Qwen2.5-3B & Llama-3.2-1B; the frontier replicates across three model families.
  • Exceptional hygiene. 3 seeds, 3 orthogonal controls, symmetric OMP, two matched-parameter methods, honest failure reporting (e.g. Pythia-70M L5).
  • Non-trivial theory. Identifiability + exact OMP recovery for learned real-valued decoders, with candid asymptotic discussion.
  • Resists overclaiming. Framed as an underexplored design axis, not a dense-SAE replacement.
－Limitations
  • Proxy metrics. CE-loss recovery & rel-err proxy for — but don't measure — interpretability utility; only lightweight LLM-judge evidence.
  • Missing modern baselines. No head-to-head vs JumpReLU, Gated, BatchTopK, Matryoshka, or Switch at matched params/FLOPs.
  • Single-cell headline. The 84% is one point; sensitive layers (Llama-3.2-1B L6) drop to 0.576.
  • Encoder amortisation caveat. At modern scale matched-Dense edges Expander out under the trained encoder; the win needs OMP on both sides (~140× slower).
  • Theory–experiment gap. Theorems assume noiseless k-sparse codes; acknowledged, not closed.

04 / OPEN

Questions this opens

Q1 · UTILITY

Do Expander features actually work on interpretability benchmarks — sparse probing, automated circuit discovery, causal ablation — beyond CE-loss and LLM-judge coherence?

Q2 · COMPETITORS

How do expander masks compare head-to-head with Matryoshka, JumpReLU, Gated, BatchTopK and Switch at matched parameters, FLOPs and storage?

Q3 · CLOSING THE GAP

Can the 16-point CE gap at d=7 (and the larger sensitive-layer gaps) be closed by low-rank corrections or learnable / annealed masks?

Q4 · ENCODER

Is the trained-encoder disadvantage at small d fundamental, or can encoder architectures (per-feature gating) be co-designed with expander decoders to avoid OMP's throughput hit?

Q5 · THEORY

Does identifiability / OMP recovery extend to realistic noisy, approximately-sparse activations — and can β be regularised toward the provably-recoverable regime?

Q6 · SCALE

At 7B+ parameters and n≥10⁵ features, does the frontier hold, and do the flat-layout storage savings translate to real interpretability pipelines?
