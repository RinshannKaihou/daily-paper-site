> **一句话总结：** 本文识别并命名了 LLM 智能体长期记忆中的"幽灵记忆"(ghost memory) 问题——旧事实、当前事实与变迁事实在记忆库中并存、检索时混杂、最终误导回答模型；提出 A-TMA（Adaptive Truth Maintenance Auditing），一个可叠加在现有记忆系统（Graphiti/Zep、A-Mem、InsideOut 等）之上的状态感知覆盖层，通过"哨兵-裁判"双模块维护状态角色、构建状态对齐的证据包、并向 QA 显式暴露 current/historical/transition 标签；同时构建了冲突密集的 LTP 基准（10 profiles、800 probes）并附带 97.5–100% 的标注一致性验证，使 Graphiti/Zep+A-TMA 的冲突准确率从 0.480 提升到 0.720，InsideOut+A-TMA 的准确率从 0.117 跃升至 0.662。

> **TL;DR:** The paper formalizes "ghost memory"—a state-coordination failure where superseded, current, and transition facts coexist in an LLM agent's memory bank and mislead retrieval/QA. The authors propose A-TMA, a host-agnostic overlay (tested on Graphiti/Zep, A-Mem, InsideOut) with a dual "Sentry + Judge" bank module, a state-aligned retrieval controller, and explicit state labels (current/historical/transition) exposed to QA. They release LTP, a conflict-heavy benchmark (10 profiles, 800 probes) with 97.5–100% inter-annotator agreement and an explicit leakage-prevention protocol. A-TMA lifts conflict accuracy from 0.480→0.720 on Graphiti/Zep and from 0.117→0.662 overall QA accuracy on InsideOut, and raises Graphiti/Zep temporal F1 from 0.0295→0.1705 on LoCoMo. Results are honestly reported as host-dependent, not universal.

## 核心贡献

- **识别并命名"幽灵记忆"(Ghost Memory) 失败模式：** 揭示旧事实、当前事实、变迁事实在记忆库中并存、检索混杂、最终误导回答模型的状态协调失败，将其作为独立失败模式系统化分析，并明确指出它同时耦合了库、检索、QA 三个层面。
- **提出三层解耦评估框架：** 主张记忆系统应在库维护 (bank maintenance)、检索 (retrieval)、回答时解析 (answer-time resolution) 三个层面分别评估，因为最终 QA 准确率会掩盖幽灵记忆发生在哪一层；并定义了 Bank coexist、Bank role、Evidence support、Conflict/Fact accuracy 等分层指标。
- **提出 A-TMA 状态感知覆盖层：** 一个不替换宿主记忆架构的覆盖层，包含三个模块——(1) **Sentry + Judge** 双模块库维护（轻量双头编码器做候选提议，Qwen2.5-3B 做 GRPO 训练的裁判审计，保留历史但不让旧事实默认存活）；(2) **状态对齐检索**（构建 current/historical/transition 视图证据包，带边界的控制器重排）；(3) **QA 状态标签**（将证据序列化为 cur/hist/tran/link/raw 标签，约束回答模型遵循请求的状态视图）。
- **构建 LTP 基准并附带严谨验证：** 基于 LoCoMo 用户档案构建，10 profiles、400 state units、800 cue events、800 probes（400 historical fact recall + 400 current state conflict），含结构化校验、防泄漏规则、模型辅助预审与分层人工核验（2 名标注者，IAA 97.5–100%）。
- **完整实验与消融：** 在 LTP 和完整 LoCoMo（10 samples、1986 QA 对）上对比 9+ 宿主系统，并给出组件消融（Table 5，3 profiles）证明 Sentry/Judge/Retrieval/QA-Label 各自贡献。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalizes "ghost memory":** Identifies a state-coordination failure where superseded, current, and transition facts coexist, stay mixed during retrieval, and mislead the answer model—and shows it cannot be reduced to a storage-only problem since it couples bank, retrieval, and QA.
- **Three-level decoupled evaluation protocol:** Defines bank-level (Bank coexist, Bank role), retrieval-level (Evidence support), and QA-level (Acc, conflict/fact splits, Judge) metrics, arguing aggregate QA accuracy hides *where* ghost memory occurs. Notably, timestamp-only ordering is explicitly *not* credited as a state role.
- **A-TMA overlay architecture (Sentry + Judge + controller + labels):** A host-agnostic overlay with (1) a lightweight dual-head "Sentry" gate (nomic-embed-text-v1.5 + two projection heads) that proposes candidates, (2) a "Judge" (Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, QLoRA-SFT then GRPO with rule rewards for verdict format/correctness/old-state preservation/transition summaries) that commits structured supersession/transition/coexistence links, (3) a bounded retrieval controller (≤3 selections from a ≤10 candidate pool, cannot retrieve or invent outside facts), and (4) a deterministic QA label serializer that exposes cur/hist/tran/link roles.
- **LTP benchmark with rigorous construction:** 10 profiles, 400 state units, 800 cue events, 800 probes built from LoCoMo-anchored profiles, filtered by structural validators, leakage rules (cue1-before-cue2 timing, two-view leakage), model-assisted preaudit, and stratified manual verification (2 annotators, IAA 97.5–100%).
- **Comprehensive experiments + ablation:** Full LTP (800 probes/method) and full LoCoMo (10 samples, 1986 QA pairs) across 9+ host systems, plus a component ablation (Table 5, 3 profiles/240 probes) isolating each module's contribution.

## Methodology

A-TMA (Adaptive Truth Maintenance Auditing) is explicitly framed as a **state-alignment overlay**, not a new memory substrate. It wraps an existing host (Graphiti/Zep, A-Mem, InsideOut, SeCom, etc.) along three decoupled layers:

**1. Bank-level state maintenance (Sentry + Judge).** When a new memory arrives, the **Sentry** (a lightweight dual-head SentenceTransformer based on `nomic-ai/nomic-embed-text-v1.5`, with a topic head `g_top` and a logic/stance head `g_log`, trained by supervised pair loss with labels for conflict/consistent/negative) proposes prior records that may share a state slot. A candidate is routed to audit when topic agreement `a_ij > τ_top (0.60)` *and* logical compatibility `b_ij < τ_log (0.45)` or gap `d_ij > τ_gap (0.15)`. The **Judge** (Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct warmed with QLoRA SFT then optimized via GRPO with rule rewards) then commits a structured verdict: it marks a superseded record's status from `active`→`superseded`, makes the new record `active`, and adds reciprocal typed links (`supersedes`, `superseded by`, `evolves from/to`, `scoped exception`, `coexistence`). Crucially, not every difference forces supersession—coexisting or scoped-exception facts stay active. Invariants: a single-valued slot has ≥1 active current state; superseded states remain recoverable.

**2. Retrieval-level evidence construction.** A rule-based query profiler assigns a *state view* `v_q ∈ {current, historical, transition, neutral}` by counting temporal/change hints. The host retriever gives seed set `A_q`; A-TMA expands via relation hops `R_q` (state links) and semantic hops `N_q`, then dedups into pool `C_q`. A **stable pre-rank** (`π_0`) preserves bucket order; an optional **bounded controller** (`qwen3b-grpo-v6`, temp 0, ≤10 candidates, 220-char previews, ≤3 selections) may reorder but is *constrained to `i_m ∈ C_q`*—it cannot retrieve missing evidence or invent facts. A retrieval trace records the profile, candidate pool, pre-rank, controller selections, parse status, and final order, so retrieval failures (target hit, state-view success, stale leakage, future-state intrusion) become separately measurable.

**3. QA-level state conditioning.** A deterministic serializer maps each item's status/relation/transition/source/timestamp into a label `λ_i ∈ {cur, hist, tran, link, raw}`. Each evidence line is annotated with its state role (+ optional time), and a rule set constrains the generator (qwen2.5:3b, k=5; a separate llama3.3:70b serves only as the offline judge) to use retrieved memories only and follow the requested state view—current memory is the active state unless the question asks about the past.

The **LTP benchmark** is built by binding each of 10 profiles to a LoCoMo conversation, constructing 40 mutable slot instances per profile across job/location/relationship/pets/hobbies/numerical facts, each as a two-cue state unit (old cue + updated/conflicting cue) with two QA views (fact-recall → old state; conflict-aware → current state). Cue1 events are placed at 0.12–0.42 time quantiles, cue2 at 0.58–0.88, preserving LoCoMo tails. Filtering includes structural validators, model-assisted preaudit, targeted repair, and stratified manual verification.

## Results & Highlights

**LTP (full 10 profiles, 800 probes/method, 70B judge) — Table 2:**
- **Graphiti/Zep + A-TMA:** conflict accuracy **0.480 → 0.720 (+0.240)**, overall Acc 0.524 → 0.635 (+0.111), Evidence support 0.711 → 0.809.
- **InsideOut + A-TMA:** overall Acc **0.117 → 0.662** (a +0.545 jump—largest gain, expected since InsideOut compresses dialogue into an evolving profile that loses old states), Evidence support 0.239 → 0.848.
- **A-Mem + A-TMA:** best aggregate Acc 0.818 (conflict 0.812 → 0.860), while base A-Mem already strong; gains here are smaller (overlay preserves a strong host rather than overhauling it).
- **AriadneMem** has timestamped graph facts but conflict accuracy only 0.512 despite Evidence support 0.656—direct evidence that graph labels alone don't guarantee state-correct QA.

**LoCoMo (full 10 samples, 1986 QA pairs) — Tables 3 & 4:**
- **Graphiti/Zep + A-TMA:** temporal F1 **0.0295 → 0.1705**, average F1 0.0809 → 0.1556, multi-hop F1 0.1231 → 0.2398, average rank improves to 2.67 (best average Rank).
- **AriadneMem** retains the strongest average F1/BLEU-1 on LoCoMo; A-TMA effects are explicitly **host- and metric-dependent** (e.g., A-Mem+A-TMA slightly *drops* average BLEU-1 in some cells). Authors use LoCoMo as a "mixed generalization check," not proof of uniform improvement.

**Ablation (Table 5, 3 profiles/240 probes, A-Mem host):** Removing any single component (Sentry, Retrieval controller, Judge, or QA Label) degrades QA Acc below the full system (full = 0.883 QA Acc / 0.638 Pref / 0.958 Evidence support); the QA label and Judge modules are individually the most damaging to remove.

**Verification:** LTP manual verification reports IAA of 97.5–100% across state slot, old/current value, update type, gold answers, and validity.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Zitong Shi, Yixuan Tang, Anthony Kum Hoe Tung — National University of Singapore (NUS).** Affiliations confirmed in the PDF.
- The paper adopts a measurement-systems framing (formal failure taxonomy, new benchmark with documented construction + leakage rules + human verification, decoupled metrics, component ablation), which is consistent with a careful evaluation-focused contribution rather than a hype-driven methods paper. The authors repeatedly hedge claims ("bounded conclusion," "host-dependent," "mixed generalization check"), which is a credibility positive.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Solid and well-scoped. Operationalizing "ghost memory" as a *three-level coordination* failure (not just storage) and the *decoupled* evaluation protocol are genuinely useful contributions. However, the core intuition (track fact validity/temporal roles, label evidence, route by state) overlaps with temporal knowledge graphs, belief-state tracking, and AriadneMem's own transition-aware reasoning—the paper is honest that A-TMA is *complementary* to temporal substrates rather than wholly unprecedented. The clearest novelty is the *overlay + decoupled measurement* combination, not any single mechanism.
- **Rigor:** Stronger than typical. There *is* a component ablation (Table 5) isolating Sentry/Judge/Retrieval/QA-Label, multi-host results (9+ systems), and a documented benchmark construction pipeline with explicit leakage rules and human IAA. Against my initial abstract-based concerns, this is reassuring. Remaining gaps: the ablation covers only **3 profiles / 240 probes** under the **A-Mem host only**—cross-host ablations are absent, so we don't know if the same component dominates on Graphiti or InsideOut.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. LTP (with construction protocol and validators), model checkpoints (qwen3b-grpo-v6), encoder (nomic-embed-text-v1.5), gates (τ_top=0.60, τ_log=0.45, τ_gap=0.15), and budgets (k=5, ≤10 candidates, ≤3 selections, 220-char previews) are specified. The overlay design over named hosts aids replication. Caveat: LTP is **derived from LoCoMo**, and the Judge/Sentry are trained on constructed state-change pairs—there is a real (though mitigated) risk of benchmark/method co-design; the structural validators and leakage rules reduce but do not eliminate this.
- **Significance:** The headline conflict-accuracy gains (+0.240) and the InsideOut jump (+0.545) are meaningful. But absolute temporal F1 on LoCoMo remains low (0.1705) and several LoCoMo deltas are tiny or negative (e.g., Graphiti single-hop F1 +0.001, A-Mem BLEU-1 down in places). The temporal-F1 jump is large *relatively* but starts near zero. The decoupled-evaluation *idea* is arguably the most durable contribution, independent of A-TMA's specific gains.
- **Red flags / honest concerns:**
  - **Single offline judge (llama3.3:70b):** No judge-reliability analysis, judge swap, or human-vs-judge calibration is reported for the QA Acc/Judge columns; conflict accuracy could be judge-sensitive.
  - **Small QA model (qwen2.5:3b):** May compress headroom and make "state collapse" look worse than it would be with a stronger answerer; the gains partly reflect fixing a weak reader.
  - **Host-dependent framing cuts both ways:** It is honest, but it also means A-TMA sometimes barely helps (A-Mem average F1) and the "best" host (A-Mem) already does well without it—the overlay's value is concentrated on hosts with weak state alignment (InsideOut, Graphiti).
  - **LTP scope:** 10 profiles / 800 probes is modest; LoCoMo-derived, so breadth of real-world state-change diversity is limited.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated problem framing that separates a genuinely under-measured failure mode from generic RAG/retrieval quality.
- Non-destructive design philosophy: A-TMA preserves history (auditability) rather than overwriting, which is valuable for trustworthy assistants.
- Rigorous benchmark construction with documented leakage prevention, structural validators, and human verification (IAA up to 100%).
- Genuine methodological discipline: decoupled metrics at bank/retrieval/QA levels, a bounded controller that *cannot* retrieve or invent outside its pool, and an explicit ablation.
- Intellectual honesty: results are repeatedly framed as host- and metric-dependent with a "bounded conclusion," rather than overclaimed.

## Limitations

- Absolute temporal capability remains low (LoCoMo temporal F1 0.1705 even after gains); the problem is far from solved.
- Ablation is narrow (3 profiles, single host); component-level contribution across hosts is unverified.
- LTP is derived from LoCoMo and has only 10 profiles—real-world state-change diversity and potential method/benchmark co-design remain concerns despite validators.
- Reliance on a single offline 70B judge with no reported judge-reliability analysis.
- QA model (qwen2.5:3b) is small; some gains may stem from rescuing a weak reader rather than fundamental memory improvement.
- LTP currently only targets current/historical QA; transition QA is part of the interface but not a direct evaluation target, leaving "what changed" reasoning under-tested.
- Added system complexity (Sentry + Judge + controller + sidecar traces) has unclear latency/cost overhead at deployment scale.

## Relevance

对于关注 LLM 智能体长期记忆、多轮对话状态跟踪与时序冲突消解的研究者，本文提供了"事实随时间演变导致记忆冲突"的清晰问题定义、银行/检索/QA 三层解耦评估协议与带人工验证的 LTP 基准，对构建可靠的持久化记忆助手与诊断记忆失败根因具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do component-level contributions (Sentry/Judge/controller/QA-label) change across hosts beyond A-Mem—does the dominant failure layer shift on Graphiti vs. InsideOut?
- How sensitive are QA Acc and conflict accuracy to the choice of offline judge (llama3.3:70b)? Would a judge swap or human-vs-judge calibration change rankings?
- Do gains hold when the QA model is scaled up (e.g., a 70B answerer), or does a stronger reader erase much of A-TMA's benefit by inferring state from raw text?
- Is LTP robust to benchmark/method co-design? Could an independent state-change benchmark (not LoCoMo-derived) replicate the conflict-accuracy ordering?
- How does A-TMA handle *ambiguous* or *partial* transitions (contradictory updates, conditional/scope changes) vs. clean supersessions?
- What is the latency and compute overhead of the Sentry+Judge pipeline and per-query evidence-packet construction in a deployed assistant?
- How does transition-targeted QA (currently interface-only) perform—can A-TMA support "what changed and when" questions, not just current/historical lookups?
- Does the bounded controller's constraint (`i_m ∈ C_q`) become a bottleneck when the host retriever's seed set is weak?
