A conditioning-first optimiser lifts PINN accuracy by up to 8 orders of magnitude.

DSGNAR (Doubly-Sketched Gauss–Newton with Adaptive Ratio) reframes PINN training as an ill-conditioning problem rather than a loss-descent problem. A doubly-sketched Gauss–Newton model plus a target-decrease-ratio step rule beats recent strong second-order baselines across 8 PDEs — reaching relative ℓ₂ errors at the double-precision round-off floor.

How it works

doubly-sketched Gauss–Newton · target-ratio steps
PHILOSOPHY

Conditioning first — then descend.

PINN accuracy is bounded not by how fast the loss drops, but by the effective regularisation λ at the converged iterate — the minimum λ for which a Gauss–Newton step still produces a decrease. So DSGNAR spends Stage 1 driving λ down (small target ratio ϱ ≤ 0.2) to find a well-conditioned region, then Stage 2 raises the target (ϱ ≥ 0.5) for aggressive descent. The switch is automatic.

CORE TRICK · DOUBLY-SKETCHED JACOBIAN

One SVD serves every λ.

The full Jacobian J ∈ ℝ^{N×dθ} is never formed. It is compressed on both sides into a small square sketch whose single SVD yields cheap Levenberg–Marquardt steps for any regularisation.

CountSketch (rows) is linear & accumulable — so collocation points N grow without memory bound (a 1,048,576 × 252,503 Jacobian builds in 7.8 s at s=10,000), dissolving PINNs' residual-selection problem. SRCT (cols) is near-isometric, so the lifted step stays faithful. Crucially, J̃ᵀJ̃ is never formed — that would square the condition number.

STEP SELECTION

Probe for a target ratio.

Each iteration draws 24 geometrically-spaced radii in [Δ/3, 3Δ] — centred on Δ, which varies <1 order across iterations, not on λ (which swings ~25 orders). For each probe, Newton iterations solve the secular equation for λ, the step is lifted and its true ratio ϱ evaluated, and a PCHIP interpolant picks the radius attaining ϱ⋆.

A history-independent, landscape-aware rule replaces brittle λ/Δ heuristics.

SUPPORTING MACHINERY

Adaptive weighting & stage switch.

UpdateWeights (α=0.05) keeps the M residual conditions on a comparable scale — essential because CountSketch mixes all conditions into shared buckets.

UpdateTargetRatio fits a sliding-window log-linear regression on λ history to flip Stage 1 → Stage 2 automatically. A hybrid-precision trick runs the first 10 iterations in single precision to fix bad initialisations, then switches to double.

Strengths & limitations

honestly reported by the authors
+

Strengths

what holds up
  • Principled philosophy. Conditioning-first two-stage framing backed by textbook-correct trust-region / Levenberg–Marquardt machinery.
  • Scalable explicit Jacobian. Doubly-sketched construction dissolves the residual-selection problem via dense, accumulable collocation sampling.
  • Meaningful gains. 3–8 orders over recent strong second-order baselines, not weak straw-men.
  • Tuning-light robustness. Fixed hyperparameters across SIREN, MLP, GaborNet, SPINN, LSTM architectures and single/double precision.
  • Reproducible. Public JAX code; Oxford optimisation pedigree; authors candid about open theory questions.
!

Limitations

what to scrutinise
  • Per-step SVD cost O(s²) + O(s³); scalability to very large 3D / long-time PDEs beyond the tested suite is unproven.
  • Basic objective only. Inverse problems with noisy data, hard industrial constraints, and stiff systems are untested — the bottleneck is shifting to the objective.
  • Theory lags practice. No subspace-embedding guarantee for the composed sketch; no proof for the ratio-targeting rule; linear convergence unexplained.
  • Precision-floor saturation. Near-round-off accuracies are problem/setup-specific, not universally transferable.
  • High-end hardware. Single H100 (96 GB); wall times exclude JIT compile time.

Open questions

for further exploration
Q1 · THEORY

Does a clean subspace-embedding guarantee exist for the composed CountSketch × SRCT sketch — and does it predict the empirically sufficient s ≈ dθ/3?

Q2 · INVERSE PROBLEMS

How does DSGNAR behave on inverse problems with noisy/sparse data, where the discarded Hessian term Σ rⱼ ∇²rⱼ is no longer negligible?

Q3 · CONVERGENCE

Can the empirically observed linear convergence be proven, or tied to a property of the well-conditioned region Stage 1 locates?

Q4 · NAVIER–STOKES

On Navier–Stokes, where the objective is the limiter, does pairing DSGNAR with FBPINNs / domain decomposition push accuracy further?

Q5 · HARDWARE

What is the true wall-clock cost (incl. JIT compile) and memory profile on consumer GPUs vs the H100, for large-sketch solves?

Q6 · GENERALISATION

Could target-ratio probing replace λ/Δ heuristics in other randomised second-order settings — e.g. Newton Sketch for general least-squares?
