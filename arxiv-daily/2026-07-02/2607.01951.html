Why this matters

"Looks robust" can mean "didn't hear the signal." Behavioral benchmarks alone can't tell a model that resists skepticism from one that merely fails to perceive it — so audit must be mechanism-aware.

→ Direct read for red-teaming & public-health LLM deployment: probe the representation, don't trust the surface answer.
Critical edge

01 / TAXONOMY

Three policies, one geometry

No model retreats to false balance. They diverge in how they resist — and whether the signal is even represented. Click a model.

Behavioral read

Llama reacts to skepticism by hardening its consensus stance — assertion rate climbs 0.066 → 0.185 (~3×). This is stance, not style: pairwise forced-choice 63.6% (p=.007), driven by assertion (β=+0.042/dose, p<1e-77), not hedging or false balance. Classed active robustness.

02 / METHOD

Four evidence types, one coherent study

Behavioral + correlational (probing) + non-linear control (RBF-SVM) + causal intervention (patching).

01 STIMULI
CORE-F & MYTH-D items

Consensus-factual (assert) vs myth-rebuttal (correct) items across climate, vaccine, evolution. 5 surface paraphrases each guard against prompt artifacts. Value-laden set analyzed separately.

02 MANIPULATION
4-level user signal

Neutral S0, concern S1 (same-direction control), mild skepticism S2, strong skepticism S3. The S1 control isolates skeptic-specificity from generic engagement.

03 MEASURES
Lexical + forced-choice

Continuous rates of assertion / hedging / false-balance; composite proxy; pairwise forced-choice judging (Cohen's κ). Graded 0–3 judge proved too coarse.

04 MECHANISM
Probes + patching

Layer-wise linear & RBF-SVM probes classifying neutral vs skeptical hidden states; activation patching tests the causal role of the implicated layer.

Statistics · mixed-effects models (item & seed random effects) + Benjamini–Hochberg correction · layer-wise accuracy with 95% Wilson CIs · binomial tests vs chance.
03 / RESULTS

Findings, then the fracture

Single-turn robustness holds. Multi-turn and cross-domain, it splinters along CORE-F / MYTH-D lines.

Five findings

behavioral → representational → causal → escalation → cross-domain
  • F1 · No retreat. Zero models drift to false balance; dominant shift is toward stronger consensus. S1 concern control moves nothing.
  • F2 · Geometry split. Linear probe: Llama & Qwen reach 100% separation; Mistral only 72% (0/32 perfect layers). RBF-SVM underperforms in Mistral 46% vs 72%.
  • F3 · Partial causality. Patching recovers neutral behavior in 83% of divergent items — but Wilcoxon p=.39, no clean positive control. Authors label it partial.
  • F4 · 5-turn escalation. Llama CORE-F Δ −0.38 while MYTH-D +0.50. Qwen erodes most overall (−0.22). Mistral crosses into reactive assertion (+0.17).
  • F5 · Cross-domain attenuation. Reactive assertion weakens outside climate (dose×evolution +0.038, dose×vaccine +0.051, both p<.001).
⚠ The headline reversal
In the most safety-critical domain — vaccine MYTH-D — myth rebuttal weakens under skeptical pressure. The effect flips sign across domains: what strengthens rebuttal in climate actively erodes it in vaccines.
+0.133 climate → −0.050 vaccine

Strengths

why the study holds weight
  • Four converging evidence types in one coherent design — behavioral, probing, non-linear control, intervention.
  • S1 concern control elegantly isolates skeptic-specificity from generic engagement-reactivity.
  • Per-model × domain × item-type granularity — far more informative than a single robustness score.
  • Intellectual honesty: downgrades the causal claim, reports nulls, frames multi-turn effects as demonstrations.
  • Reusable outputs: the active-vs-accidental taxonomy and the §8.4 mechanism-aware-evaluation manifesto.

Limitations

where to read carefully
  • Causal evidence is partial — patching p=.39, no clean positive control; mechanism rests on probing correlation.
  • Three open 7–8B models only — no frontier/proprietary LLMs; taxonomy unvalidated at scale.
  • English only, three domains — no cross-lingual generalization; vaccine reversal is a single domain.
  • Linear-probe-as-perception-proxy is an assumption, not a proven fact about perception.
  • No code / data / stimulus release referenced; single-author preprint without peer review.
04 / OPEN

Questions worth chasing

Where the active/accidental split, the reversal, and the perception-proxy most need pressure.

Q1 · SCALE

Do frontier models (GPT-4o, Claude, Gemini) show the same three policies, and which dominates at 70B+? Does active/accidental survive at scale?

Q2 · MISTRAL

Is "non-response" truly a failure to perceive — or could a sparse / non-monotonic representation carry the signal both probes miss?

Q3 · FIXING IT

Can fine-tuning (anti-sycophancy, science-fact alignment) shift a model from accidental to active — and does probe separability track that shift?

Q4 · REVERSAL

Is the vaccine MYTH-D reversal (+0.133 → −0.050) a Llama-specific artifact or a systematic risk when stimuli, language & model scale up?

Q5 · DEPLOY

Can the layer-wise probe become a cheap, real-time "perception check" — certifying a model heard the cue before trusting its output?

Q6 · THRESHOLD

At intermediate dose S2 and mixed/alarm framing: sharp threshold or gradual transition — and does it differ per policy type?
