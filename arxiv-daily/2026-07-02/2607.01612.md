> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 C3RL——将"正确性 + 校准 + 数据集先验参考准确率"三者联合的强化学习奖励，使 LLM 在不牺牲准确率的前提下获得良好校准的言语化置信度，并基于此提出 CAS 自适应测试时计算分配策略，在 8 个文本/多模态数据集上以高达 12.33 倍的推理预算缩减超越多数投票与 Adaptive-Consistency。

> **TL;DR:** Standard RLVR improves LLM accuracy but wrecks confidence calibration (encouraging overconfident hallucination). This paper proposes C3RL — an RL reward that adds a dataset-informed reference-accuracy term plus a calibration term to the standard correctness reward — yielding well-calibrated verbalized confidence (1–10) with no accuracy loss. They then use that confidence directly as an early-stopping signal in a new test-time-scaling policy, CAS, which beats majority voting and Adaptive-Consistency on 8 text/multimodal benchmarks while cutting inference budget by up to 12.33× (OOD text) and 8.98× (OOD multimodal). Strongest result: on the Llama-3.2-3B and Qwen2.5-VL-7B families, C3RL outperforms the current SOTA RLCR on both accuracy and ECE simultaneously.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 C3RL 强化学习算法**：将奖励分解为三部分——`R_correctness`（标准 RLVR 正确性奖励）、`R_calibration`（基于置信度阈值 t=5 将答案分为"确信/不确信"，奖励"确信且正确"与"不确信且错误"、惩罚反向情况）、`R_reference`（利用训练集 10 次采样得到的"全对/部分对/全错"先验标签，奖励从"全错"题答对、惩罚在"全对"题上幻觉），从根上抑制了"为校准而故意答错"的退化。
- **首次将置信度校准与测试时缩放统一到一个框架**：训练侧 C3RL 提供可信的言语化置信度，推理侧 CAS 直接利用它，二者构成缺一不可的协同流水线。
- **提出 CAS 自适应测试时计算分配策略**：以"最高置信答案的置信度和 vs. 次高置信答案的置信度和"（c₁、c₂）按 Beta 分布计算停止概率 `Prob(c₁,c₂)`，达到 `P_stop` 即提前停止；显式支持串行（Algorithm 1）与并行（Algorithm 2）两种推理。
- **跨模态、跨模型族、跨域验证**：8 个文本（AGIEval、MMLU）与多模态（MMMU、MathVista、LogicVista）数据集；Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct 与 Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct 双模型族；域内与 OOD 双设置。
- **大幅降低推理成本**：CAS 在 OOD 文本上仅需 5.19 次采样即可匹配 MV(N=64) 的准确率（节省 12.33×），OOD 多模态 8.98×，域内 5.87×，均明显优于 Adaptive-Consistency（4.97×/4.20×/4.67×）。

## Key Contributions

- **C3RL reward design** explicitly prevents the "calibrated-but-wrong" failure mode of pure calibration rewards (e.g., SaySelf/Brier). The reference-accuracy term anchors confidence expectations to per-sample empirical difficulty measured at training time (10-sample accuracy bins: all-correct / partial / all-incorrect), rewarding progress on hard items and penalizing regression on easy ones.
- **Unified pipeline**: the first work (per authors) to jointly optimize confidence calibration *and* test-time compute scaling in a single framework — the calibration signal is consumed by the router rather than wasted.
- **CAS early-stopping** plugs calibrated verbalized confidence into a Beta-distribution stopping criterion derived from Adaptive-Consistency, replacing the frequency/entropy proxies that prior adaptive samplers use. Both sequential and parallel variants are provided.
- **Comprehensive, multimodal, cross-family evaluation** with the correct *joint* metrics: Accuracy, AUROC (over confidence thresholds 1–10), and binned ECE (20 bins).
- **Honest, author-declared limitations** section naming concrete weaknesses (insufficient science-domain training data; calibration still below SaySelf; CAS gain over AC negligible at small N<8).

## Methodology

**Training data construction.** They assemble ~106k reasoning samples from NuminaMath-TIR (69k math), WebInstruct-verified (4.5k), LogicNLI (16k) and LogiQA (8.7k). For each question the base model generates 10 answers (temp 0.7); each question is tagged `all-correct` (Acc=1), `partial` (0<Acc<1), or `all-incorrect` (Acc=0). Tag distribution: 13.7k / 68.1k / 24.3k.

**C3RL reward** = `R_correctness + R_calibration + R_reference`:

- `R_correctness(a) = 1[a == a*]` — standard RLVR.
- `R_calibration` — threshold t=5; reward +β for {certain ∧ correct} and {uncertain ∧ incorrect}; penalize −β for {certain ∧ incorrect} and {uncertain ∧ correct}.
- `R_reference` — +α when the model correctly answers a formerly "all-incorrect" question; −α when it hallucinates on a formerly "all-correct" one. α, β chosen via ablation (App. A.5).

The model emits a verbalized confidence `c ∈ {1,…,10}` alongside each answer.

**CAS** at inference. After n sampled responses with m unique answers, compute the per-answer summed confidence `c_{a_i} = Σ conf(r_{i_k})`. Take c₁ = max, c₂ = second max. Stopping probability follows Adaptive-Consistency's Beta integral:

`Prob(c₁,c₂) = (1/A) ∫₀^0.5 x^{c₂}(1−x)^{c₁} dx`,  with `A = ∫₀^1 …`

Stop when `Prob ≥ P_stop` or n reaches N; otherwise continue. Final answer = highest-confidence-weighted answer. A parallel variant (Algorithm 2) is also given.

## Results & Highlights

**Calibration–accuracy trade-off (Tables 1–2).** On Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct:
- C3RL nearly matches RLVR accuracy (64.5% vs 65.3% avg text) while achieving ECE 0.107 vs RLVR's 0.300, and AUROC 0.696 vs 0.550 — closing most of the gap to the best-calibrated method (SaySelf ECE 0.117) without SaySelf's massive accuracy collapse (SaySelf 56.6%).
- On OOD, C3RL *beats the SOTA RLCR on both axes simultaneously* (accuracy and ECE), and beats SaySelf in accuracy by +7.9% (text) and +19.2% (multimodal) while matching/bettering its ECE.
- On Llama-3.2-3B, C3RL is the only RL method that *exceeds RLVR's accuracy* (50.1% vs 48.9%) while sharply lowering ECE — RLCR and SaySelf both pay large accuracy penalties there.

**Ablation (C3RL w/o Ref):** removing the reference-accuracy term preserves accuracy but consistently degrades calibration — isolating that term's specific contribution to calibration.

**CAS efficiency (Table 3)** — budget to match Majority Voting N=64 accuracy:
| Setting | MV N | AC N (saved×) | CAS N (saved×) | Acc |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| In-domain | 64 | 13.71 (×4.67) | 10.91 (×5.87) | 67.5% |
| OOD Text | 64 | 12.87 (×4.97) | 5.19 (**×12.33**) | 78.2% |
| OOD Multimodal | 64 | 15.24 (×4.20) | 7.13 (×8.98) | 57.9% |

CAS strictly dominates Adaptive-Consistency in every setting at matched accuracy.

**Mechanism check (Fig. 2).** High-confidence samples (CAS stops ≤4) gain <0.2% from N=4→64 scaling; low-confidence samples jump 49.0%→54.5%. This empirically justifies shifting compute toward low-confidence items.

**Synergy check (Fig. 3).** On the *base* model, CAS ≈ AC (calibration unhelpful). On the C3RL model, CAS clearly separates above AC/CMV/MV at N≥8 — calibration is a prerequisite for CAS to pay off.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high).** The reference-accuracy reward — using *training-set empirical difficulty bins* as a calibration anchor inside the RL reward — is a genuinely clean idea I haven't seen elsewhere, and it directly addresses the documented SaySelf/RLCR failure mode of "calibrated but wrong." Coupling that into a Beta-distribution confidence-weighted early-stopping router (CAS) is a natural and under-explored combination.
- **Rigor (medium–high).** Metrics are appropriate (Accuracy + AUROC + binned ECE with 20 bins), 3 seeds with reported std, multimodal + OOD + second model family. Two real weaknesses: (i) ECE bin count and AUROC are sensitive to the discrete 1–10 confidence scale, and the paper doesn't discuss metric robustness; (ii) the "SOTA RLCR" comparison is fair (same compute) but RLCR itself was tuned for QA/math, so the multimodal win is partly RLCR being out of its lane.
- **Reproducibility (good).** Public data sources, two open base models, ablation of α/β promised in appendix, code release committed. The reference-tag pipeline (10-sample accuracy binning) is fully specified and cheap to replicate.
- **Significance (high).** A 12.33× inference-budget cut at matched accuracy is a deployable, money-saving result. The "calibration is a prerequisite for confidence-routed compute" finding (Fig. 3) is broadly useful beyond this paper.
- **Red flags / things to scrutinize:**
  - The **headline 12.33×** is on OOD text only and is the *single best* number; in-domain saving is 5.87× and multimodal 8.98×. Fine, but the abstract leads with the max.
  - **CAS ≈ AC at small N (<8)** — the authors admit this. For latency-sensitive deployments that cap N at ~4, the practical advantage shrinks.
  - **Verbalized confidence (1–10) is gameable and prompt-sensitive.** No prompt-robustness or adversarial-input study in the main text; the calibration reward could in principle be satisfied by a degenerate confidence emitter, and the reference-accuracy term is the only structural defense.
  - **SaySelf still wins pure ECE** — C3RL trades a bit of calibration headroom for accuracy. Authors are honest about this, but it means C3RL is not strictly Pareto-dominant across *all* calibration metrics.
  - Training data is **math/logic-heavy** (authors flag science-domain data insufficiency) — generalization to open-ended generation/coding is unverified.
  - "First to integrate calibration and test-time scaling" is a strong priority claim; the related-work coverage of confidence-routed best-of-N (e.g., self-certainty, Kang et al. 2025) is acknowledged but the novelty boundary could be sharper.

## Strengths

- Directly attacks a real, documented failure mode of RLVR (overconfidence → hallucination).
- Clean three-term reward decomposition; each term has an isolated ablation.
- The reference-accuracy reward is a clever, cheap, and well-motivated anti-degradation mechanism.
- Joint accuracy+calibration reporting across two model families and multimodal benchmarks — not just one cherry-picked setup.
- CAS delivers a large, practically meaningful efficiency win and degrades gracefully (no accuracy loss).
- Honest limitations; 3-seed runs with reported std; code/data/model release.

## Limitations

- SaySelf still produces strictly better ECE; C3RL trades some calibration for accuracy (not fully Pareto-dominant).
- CAS advantage over Adaptive-Consistency is negligible for small generation budgets (N<8).
- Training data skewed toward math/logic; science-domain data insufficient → potential domain bias.
- Verbalized confidence on a coarse 1–10 scale; no analysis of finer-grained or continuous confidence.
- No prompt-robustness, adversarial-input, or open-ended-generation evaluation.
- The 12.33× headline is the best-case (OOD text) number; in-domain/multimodal savings are smaller.
- Reference-accuracy tags depend on base-model 10-sample accuracy — they inherit the base model's biases for truly novel domains.

## Relevance

对"如何让 LLM 在推理阶段更高效、更可靠地表达不确定性并据此分配算力"的研究方向高度相关：C3RL 提供训练阶段的置信度校准方案，CAS 将校准信号直接转化为测试时自适应计算缩放策略，与自适应推理、不确定性量化、RLVR 后训练优化、推理成本控制等用户兴趣点直接衔接，尤其是 12.33× 推理预算缩减对实际部署具有显著工程价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How robust is the verbalized-confidence calibration to prompt-format perturbations, instruction rephrasings, and adversarial inputs? Is the reference-accuracy reward sufficient to prevent degenerate confidence emitters?
- Does CAS retain its lead when capped at small N (≤4) — the regime where the authors concede it ≈ Adaptive-Consistency? Can a better stopping criterion close that gap?
- How does the relative weight (α vs. β vs. correctness=1) affect the accuracy–calibration Pareto frontier? Is there a continuous trade-off curve, or a phase transition?
- Could the reference-accuracy tags be regenerated *online* (self-distillation) to remove dependence on base-model biases in truly novel domains?
- Does C3RL transfer to open-ended generation / coding / agentic tasks where ground-truth correctness is not extractable as a clean string match?
- Against a learned verifier / reward-model-based adaptive scaler at matched budgets, does confidence-weighted CAS still win — or does a stronger verifier subsume the calibration signal?
- How does the result scale with model size beyond 7B? Does the calibration headroom (and hence the CAS budget saving) shrink for already-well-calibrated larger models?
