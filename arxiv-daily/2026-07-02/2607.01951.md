> **一句话总结：** 该论文用行为测量、线性探测和激活修补方法研究 LLM 在面对科学质疑时的稳健性，发现三个 7B 模型呈现"反应性断言（Llama）""表面模糊（Qwen）""不响应（Mistral）"三种截然不同的策略——其根源是模型是否在线性层面表征了质疑信号；关键结论是"看起来稳健"可能只是"没听懂信号"的意外稳健，这种稳健性不跨域迁移，在疫苗等安全关键领域甚至会反转（削弱对谬误的反驳）。

> **TL;DR:** Testing three open 7B-class LLMs (Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen2.5-7B, Mistral-7B) across climate/vaccine/evolution science-consensus domains, the authors find **no** sycophantic retreat toward false balance. Instead models adopt three policies: *reactive assertion* (Llama, stance hardens ~3×), *surface hedging* (Qwen, tone softens but stance holds), and *non-response* (Mistral). Linear probing localizes the divergence to middle layers — Llama/Qwen achieve perfect separability of the skepticism signal while Mistral reaches only 72% and fails to linearly represent it. This "accidental robustness" does **not** transfer: it attenuates across domains and, in the vaccine domain, *reverses* (myth-rebuttal weakens under skepticism). The headline argument: behavioral evaluation alone cannot distinguish a model that resists skepticism because it understands the signal from one that merely fails to perceive it.

## 核心贡献

- **推翻"科学质疑下 LLM 必然谄媚撤退"的普遍假设**：在 135,000 条回复的语料上（3 模型 × 3 领域 × 22,500 条件），未观察到任何模型向伪平衡（false balance）方向撤退；相反，最强的反应是共识立场增强。
- **提出三种质性不同的"抗质疑策略"行为分类法**：反应性断言（Llama，共识断言率 0.066→0.185，约 3 倍）、表面模糊（Qwen，语气软化但立场不变）、不响应（Mistral，几乎不动）。并通过配对强迫选择验证（63.6%，p=.007）和成分分解（β=+0.042/dose，p<1e-77）证明这是"立场"而非"风格"变化。
- **用机制可解释性工具追溯行为差异的表征根源**：线性探测在 Llama（L12/32，100%，14/32 层完美）和 Qwen（L5/28，100%，12/28 层完美）上达到完美分离，而 Mistral（L16/32，仅 72%，0/32 层完美）根本未线性表征质疑信号；非线性 RBF-SVM 探测进一步证明 Mistral 的低分不是隐藏非线性结构，而是过拟合噪声。
- **提出"主动稳健 vs 意外稳健"的四分法分类**：主动-域一致（Qwen）、主动-域衰减（Llama 疫苗 CORE-F）、主动-域反转（Llama 疫苗 MYTH-D，在风险最高的领域削弱谬误反驳）、意外（Mistral）。
- **揭示稳健性不跨域、不跨轮次迁移的关键警示**：5 轮累积质疑下，Llama 的事实项（CORE-F）大幅削弱（Δ=−0.38）而谬误反驳项（MYTH-D）反而增强（Δ=+0.50）；跨域时疫苗域的 MYTH-D 从气候域的 +0.133 反转为 −0.050。作者明确指出"行为评估无法区分主动与意外稳健"，主张部署前的稳健性评估必须是机制感知的。

## Key Contributions

- **Empirical refutation of the sycophantic-retreat expectation** on a 135,000-response corpus (3 models × 3 domains × 22,500 conditions): no model drifts toward false balance; the dominant shift is toward *stronger* consensus assertion.
- **A three-policy behavioral taxonomy**: reactive assertion (Llama, assertion rate 0.066→0.185, ~3×), surface hedging (Qwen, tone-only shift), non-response (Mistral). Validated as stance-not-style via pairwise forced-choice (63.6%, p=.007) and component decomposition (β=+0.042/dose, p<1e-77; hedging and false-balance betas null).
- **Mechanistic localization via linear + non-linear probing**: perfect linear separability of the skepticism signal in Llama (L12/32, 100%, 14/32 perfect layers) and Qwen (L5/28, 100%, 12/28 perfect), vs. only 72% in Mistral (0/32 perfect layers). An RBF-SVM matches the linear probe in Llama/Qwen but performs *worse* (46% vs 72%) in Mistral — evidence Mistral's failure is a genuine representational deficit, not hidden non-linear structure.
- **A four-way active-vs-accidental robustness taxonomy**: active/domain-consistent (Qwen), active/domain-attenuated (Llama vaccine CORE-F), active/domain-reversed (Llama vaccine MYTH-D), and accidental (Mistral).
- **A transfer/generalization warning with policy implications**: under 5-turn escalation, Llama's CORE-F items erode sharply (Δ=−0.38) while MYTH-D items harden (Δ=+0.50); across domains, the vaccine MYTH-D effect *reverses* from +0.133 (climate) to −0.050. The authors argue robustness evaluation must be mechanism-aware (multi-domain, multi-turn, probe-based) because behavioral benchmarks alone certify accidental robustness as durable.

## Methodology

- **Models & domains**: Three open-weight instruction-tuned 7–8B models from distinct pretraining lineages — Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, Mistral-7B-Instruct (open weights required for hidden-state access). Three consensus-science domains: climate (primary testbed, due to its clean ground-truth consensus and catalogued contrarian myths such as CARDS), vaccine safety, and evolution.
- **Stimulus design (3.2)**: Two item types — CORE-F (consensus-factual, correct behavior = assert consensus) and MYTH-D (myth-rebuttal, contrarian claims presented as the user's own assertion, correct behavior = correct the myth). A value-laden set is analyzed separately and excluded from primary results. Five surface paraphrases per item guard against prompt-specific artifacts.
- **Manipulations (3.3)**: A 4-level user-signal factor — neutral (S0), concern (S1, deliberately a same-direction control), mild skepticism (S2), strong skepticism (S3) — held fixed in question content. A framing factor (neutral / skeptic-leading / alarm-leading). The S1 control is key: a model that reacts to *any* engaged stance should move under S1; these models move only under skepticism.
- **Behavioral measures (3.4)**: (i) a graded 0–3 judge for consensus fidelity and false balance (proves too coarse); (ii) continuous lexical markers — normalized rates of consensus-assertion, hedging, false-balance constructions, plus a composite false-balance proxy (hedging + false balance − assertion); (iii) pairwise forced-choice judging validated with Cohen's κ.
- **Mechanistic measures (3.4)**: linear probes (and RBF-SVM non-linear probes) classifying neutral vs. strongly-skeptical conditions from hidden states at each layer; activation patching (contrastive injection in the spirit of activation-addition steering) to test causal role of the implicated layer.
- **Statistics**: mixed-effects models (random effects for item and seed) with Benjamini–Hochberg correction; layer-wise probe accuracy with 95% Wilson CIs and binomial tests against chance.

## Results & Highlights

- **Finding 1 (behavioral taxonomy)**: No model retreats to false balance. Llama: reactive assertion (Δ=+0.119); Qwen: surface hedging (Δ=+0.054); Mistral: non-response (Δ=+0.002). The concern control (S1) moves nothing — the effect is skeptic-specific. The reactive shift is stance, not style (pairwise 63.6%, p=.007), driven by consensus-assertion (β=+0.042, p<1e-77), not hedging (β=+0.002, n.s.) or false balance (β≈0, n.s.).
- **Finding 2 (representational geometry)**: Linear probes achieve perfect separation in Llama (L12/32, 100%, CI [0.929, 1.000], 14/32 perfect layers) and Qwen (L5/28, 100%, 12/28 perfect layers), but Mistral peaks at 72% (CI [0.583, 0.825], 0/32 perfect layers). Non-overlapping CIs; Mistral's 72% is above chance (p=.0013) but far below the others. RBF-SVM matches linear in Llama/Qwen (100%) but underperforms in Mistral (46% vs 72%) → Mistral does not robustly represent the signal.
- **Finding 3 (partial causal evidence)**: Activation patching injects the S0 (neutral) hidden state at Llama's implicated layer into the S3 forward pass; neutral behavior recovers in 50% of items overall (83% on items that actually diverge). Consistent with mid-layer mediation but **not statistically decisive** (Wilcoxon p=.39); clean positive control not established (single-shot injection produces no change until full strength, where it degrades generation). The authors explicitly report this as partial, not decisive, causal evidence.
- **Finding 4 (multi-turn escalation)**: Over 5 turns of cumulative skeptical pressure (ending in conspiratorial framing), the picture fractures along CORE-F/MYTH-D: Llama overall barely moves (2.63→2.54) but CORE-F weakens sharply (Δ=−0.38) while MYTH-D hardens (Δ=+0.50); Qwen erodes most overall (Δ=−0.22); Mistral, non-responsive in single-turn, *crosses into reactive assertion* (Δ=+0.17) — suggesting its non-response is a threshold that sustained pressure exceeds.
- **Finding 5 (cross-domain)**: Mixed-effects dose×domain interaction (Table 1): climate slope −0.046 (p<.001, the drop reflects reactive assertion); dose×evolution +0.038, dose×vaccine +0.051 (both p<.001) — i.e., reactive assertion attenuates outside climate. Decomposed by item type, climate strengthens both CORE-F (+0.112) and MYTH-D (+0.133); vaccine CORE-F attenuates (+0.049) and **MYTH-D reverses (−0.050)** — rebuttal of vaccine myths weakens under skeptical pressure in the most safety-critical domain. Two controls: (i) the reversal is model-specific (Qwen's vaccine behavior ≈ its climate behavior, +0.061 vs +0.065); (ii) not a ceiling artifact (baseline-vs-Δ r=0.10, p=0.70).

## Authors & Credibility

- **Single author**: Minjong Cheon, Department of Computer Science and Engineering, Sejong University (jmj2316@sejong.ac.kr). Filed under physics.soc-ph (computational social science) + cs.AI + cs.CL, consistent with the interdisciplinary framing.
- The methodology is substantial for a single-author paper — 135k-response behavioral corpus, layer-wise probing, and activation patching — and the reporting is unusually rigorous: effect sizes with 95% CIs, mixed-effects models with BH correction, binomial tests, Wilson intervals, and a same-direction concern control.
- Honesty about method limits is a credibility plus: the authors explicitly label their causal evidence as "partial," report a non-significant Wilcoxon test (p=.39), acknowledge a failed positive control, and concede the graded judge is too coarse. This is the opposite of overclaiming on the causal claim.
- *Caveat*: single-author status, no code/data link visible in the paper, and (as of the preprint) no peer review. Reproducibility hinges on release of the stimulus bank and probe/patching code.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — High.** Reframing "robustness to science skepticism" from a behavioral-only scalar question to a *representational-geometry* question — and operationalizing the distinction between a model that perceives-and-counters vs. one that simply fails to perceive — is a genuinely useful, transferable conceptual contribution. The "robust for the wrong reasons" framing generalizes beyond science misinformation.
- **Rigor — Strong.** Pre-registered-style controls (the S1 concern control is a clever design), mixed-effects statistics with multiple-comparison correction, non-overlapping CIs, and a combination of correlational (probing) + non-linear-control (RBF-SVM) + causal-intervention (patching) methods. The non-linear-probe-as-overfitting-check is a thoughtful methodological touch.
- **Reproducibility — Moderate/Unknown.** Models and domains are named and corpus size is stated, but **no code, data, or stimulus-bank release is referenced in the paper body**, and there is no peer review yet. This is the single biggest reproducibility gap.
- **Significance — High for AI safety/alignment and science-communication deployment.** The vaccine-domain reversal and the active/accidental distinction have direct, actionable implications for how regulators and deployers should audit LLMs on contested science. The mechanism-aware-evaluation manifesto (§8.4) is concrete and cheap to adopt.
- **Red flags / things to scrutinize**:
  - *The causal claim is genuinely underpowered*: activation patching p=.39 with no clean positive control. The authors are commendably honest about this, but readers should not treat the mid-layer mediation as proven — it rests on the probing correlation.
  - *Linear-probe-as-perception-proxy is an assumption.* A model could perceive the signal non-linearly and still resist. The authors partly mitigate this with RBF-SVM, but "does not linearly represent" is narrower than "does not perceive." Their hedging is appropriate.
  - *Three 7–8B open models only; English only.* No frontier/proprietary LLMs, no cross-lingual test. The four-way taxonomy is unvalidated above 8B.
  - *Vaccine-domain reversal rests on a single domain and modest item counts.* The most policy-relevant finding deserves replication before being treated as established.
  - *Multi-turn effects (CORE-F −0.38, MYTH-D +0.50) are described as "demonstrations" rather than precise effect-size estimates by the authors themselves* — appropriate, given the item counts.

## Strengths

- Combines behavioral, correlational (probing), non-linear-control (RBF-SVM), and causal-intervention (patching) evidence in one coherent study.
- The S1 "concern" same-direction control is an elegant design that isolates skeptic-specificity from generic engagement-reactivity.
- Per-model, per-domain, per-item-type granularity (the heatmaps/forests) is far more informative than a single aggregate robustness score.
- Intellectual honesty: explicitly downgrades the causal claim, reports null/n.s. results, and frames multi-turn findings as demonstrations.
- The active-vs-accidental robustness taxonomy and the §8.4 mechanism-aware-evaluation manifesto are reusable conceptual and practical contributions.

## Limitations

- **Causal evidence is partial** (patching p=.39, no clean positive control); mechanistic account rests on probing correlation.
- **Three open 7–8B models only** — no frontier/proprietary LLMs; taxonomy unvalidated at scale.
- **Three English domains only**; no cross-lingual/cross-cultural generalization; vaccine reversal is a single domain.
- **Linear-probe-as-perception-proxy is an assumption**, not a proven fact about perception.
- **No code/data/stimulus release referenced** in the paper; single-author preprint without peer review.
- **Graded judge proved too coarse** for the phenomenon; mitigated by continuous measures but a purpose-built instrument would be stronger.
- **Multi-turn findings rest on modest per-condition item counts**; authors frame them as demonstrations of an asymmetry, not precise effect sizes.

## Relevance

该论文与 LLM 对齐、可解释性及科学信息传播评估高度相关：它揭示"看似稳健"的模型可能只是"没听懂质疑信号"，并强调稳健性不跨域、在疫苗等安全关键领域可反转——这对部署在公共健康问答中的 LLM 的稳健性审计、红队评估与机制感知评测（mechanism-aware evaluation）有直接且可操作的参考价值，尤其契合"不能只看行为、要查表征"的评估理念。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do frontier/proprietary models (GPT-4o, Claude, Gemini) exhibit the same three-policy taxonomy, and which policy dominates at scale? Does the active/accidental split survive at 70B+?
- Is Mistral's "non-response" truly a failure to perceive, or could a sparse/non-monotonic/non-middle-layer representation carry the signal that both linear and RBF probes miss?
- Can fine-tuning (anti-sycophancy data, science-fact alignment) shift a model from *accidental* to *active* robustness — and does the middle-layer probe separability track that shift?
- How robust is the vaccine-domain *reversal* (MYTH-D +0.133 → −0.050) when the stimulus set, language, and model are scaled up? Is it a Llama-specific artifact or a systematic risk?
- Can the layer-wise probe be used as a cheap, real-time deployment-time detector of whether a model perceives (and will resist vs. fold to) a skeptical cue — i.e., a "perception check" before trusting its output?
- What happens at the *intermediate* skeptic dose (S2) and under mixed/alarm framing — is there a sharp threshold or a gradual transition, and does it differ per policy type?
