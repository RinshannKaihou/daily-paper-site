→ Why This Matters

An end-to-end pipeline from risk discovery to evidence verification, a public reproducible benchmark, and a capability–vulnerability alignment finding that directly guides defense design.

⚠ Critical Edge

The asymmetric verifier structurally favors attacks — benign runs must satisfy full end-to-end correctness while an attack needs only one harmful state change — and there is no ablation isolating the adaptive Control Agent, so the headline partly conflates attack strength with test-driver competence.

02

A self-reinforcing three-stage pipeline

§IV — literature → composition → sandboxed verification
STAGE 1

Continuous Risk Exploration

A Summary Agent grows three hierarchical taxonomies from ~800 arXiv/OpenReview papers via create / update / merge / delete operators — taxonomies converge, not just grow.

→ 124 risks · 77 attacks · 30 environments
STAGE 2

Executable Case Construction

Tuple (risk, attack, env) → typed safety goal g, an LLM-authored initial state s₀, and a deterministic Python verifier V_g; degenerate Cartesian products filtered, 3 controlled variants each.

→ 39,078 candidates → 1,600 cases
STAGE 3

Adaptive Execution + Verification

A Control Agent establishes legitimate context, then escalates, reformulates on refusal, and repositions injections inside a 12-container Docker sandbox — outcomes feed back into Stage 1.

→ asymmetric, evidence-grounded verdict
Verification hierarchy   V_g = V_gstate(s_T) ▷ V_gtool(τ) ▷ V_gresp(τ)
// prioritise terminal environment state, then tool-call records, then agent self-report. Asymmetric judgment: Control-Agent failure ⇒ y=0; claimed success must be confirmed against live sandbox evidence ⇒ y=1.

04

Strengths & limitations, side by side

read across, not down

▲ Strengths

  • Principled, modular, self-reinforcing pipeline designed to track an evolving risk surface — not ossify into a static list.
  • Manipulation-resistant, asymmetric verification is a genuinely better safety oracle than self-report or LLM-judge rubrics.
  • Real production frameworks + real self-hosted services (not toy mocks) make ASR operationally meaningful.
  • Three-way risk × env × attack cross-tab exposes where and why attacks transfer, not just aggregate scores.
  • Public, replayable artifacts (code + benchmark + per-case trace) lower the bar for community scrutiny.
  • The benign setting doubles as a calibration probe for the generation pipeline — elegant.

▽ Limitations

  • Asymmetric verifier makes benign vs. attack ESR non-comparable; readers may over-read the gap as pure capability-vs-vulnerability.
  • No ablation isolating the adaptive Control Agent vs. a fixed/scripted attacker.
  • Risk discovery is literature-bounded; operational / zero-day risks under-covered (CVE/ATT&CK ingestion proposed, not implemented).
  • Guard-model transfer evidence is single-benchmark and thin — no head-to-head vs. guards fine-tuned on InjecAgent/AgentDojo/ASB.
  • Per-agent rankings confounded by infrastructure reliability (esp. OpenClaw, benign ESR 58.0%).
  • 12-container sandbox + multi-LLM backend makes independent full reproduction non-trivial; 10-turn budget caps long-horizon attacks.

Questions for further exploration

open threads
Q1

What is the marginal ASR contribution of the adaptive Control Agent versus a non-adaptive scripted attacker with the same scenarios and budget?

Q2

How do Vera-Bench cases overlap with — and uniquely extend — InjecAgent, AgentDojo, ASB and ATBench in concrete risk categories?

Q3

Can deterministic verifiers V_g be evaded by agents that forge observable artifacts (rollback env state, obfuscate tool-call args)?

Q4

Does capability–vulnerability alignment persist under targeted safety training, or can defense fine-tuning decouple capability from susceptibility?

Q5

How stable are per-agent ESR rankings once OpenClaw's infra-failure rate is controlled (re-normalize by non-infra-failed runs)?

Q6

What is the maintenance cost (human + LLM) of keeping the three taxonomies current as new tools, MCP servers and attacks emerge?

Q7

Could the literature explorer be augmented by an autonomous vulnerability-mining agent probing live services — closing the zero-day gap?
