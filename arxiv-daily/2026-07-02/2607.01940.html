Mechanistic Interpretability · Second-Order Circuit Completion

Transformer circuits self-repair — so first-order scoring is blind to dormant backups. CoAx reads the second order.

Critical Edge

CoAx completes a circuit — it does not discover one. As a standalone finder it peaks at 0.60, and its headline 0.91 rests on just 8 positive heads.

  • Seed-dependent: needs a primary circuit; complements, doesn't replace, first-order discovery.
  • Home-turf rival: cheap input co-activation also hits 0.92 AUC on IOI — CoAx only clearly wins on movement circuits.
  • Transfer ceiling: fails on the MLP-dominated greater-than circuit (~1.5× random).
01 / METHOD

Two faces of non-additivity

Output-grounded Fisher geometry makes ablation effects measurable in the metric the model itself induces.
Lens A · Symmetric Cooperation

Pairwise synergy Iuv

Iuv = δzuv − δzu − δzv
non-additive part of the joint ablation

Captures symmetric cooperation invisible to first-order affinity. Energy Suv drives same-circuit clustering (name-mover heads cluster at 0.76; drop the second-order term → collapses to 0.34).

  • Reads the off-diagonal interaction single-ablation scores discard.
  • Used for cooperation: which units act together inside one circuit.
Lens B · Dormant Substitution

The CoAx score (Def. 1)

compu(S) = E(δzu | S) − E(δzu | ∅)
growth of Fisher-KL energy once seed S is ablated

Large for backups (effect appears only after S is removed); near zero for non-redundant units. Cost is O(|U|) forward passes per seed — same order as one single-ablation scan, far below O(|U|²).

  • Used for substitution: which dormant units stand in for the primary set.
  • Label-free, output-grounded — no dependence on curated circuit labels.

Three propositions give the geometry real teeth

1
Fisher identity

The centered, √p₀-weighted feature inner product ⟨δz̃u, δz̃vuᵀ F δzv — so the Fisher Gram H is PSD and ablation energy is mean KL cost.

2
Additivity blind-spot

Any clean-state per-unit score cannot rank a pure backup above an inert unit — an impossibility result — while CoAx gives compbackup(S)=Δ>0 vs compinert(S)=0.

3
Growth = seed synergy

Under pairwise truncation, compu(S) reduces to u's synergistic coupling to the seed — confirming CoAx reads the off-diagonal second-order term.

03 / DOWNSTREAM

One recovered set, three closed loops

The same label-free backups serve attribution, capability knockout, and pruning — 124M → 7B.
01

Attribution recovery

logit-diff drop 0.22 → 1.76

Ablating primaries alone barely dents the logit-difference (drop 0.22 of a clean 2.53). Adding CoAx backups recovers 1.76 — exceeding matched-random (1.0±0.7) and even the documented backups (1.15).

Self-repair was masking the attribution; CoAx unmasks it.
02

Capability knockout

IOI accuracy 0.97 → 0.70

Primary ablation alone barely dents IOI accuracy (1.00 → 0.97). Adding CoAx backups drives it to 0.70, matching the documented oracle (0.72) — while first-order +own overshoots to 0.24, cutting into core heads.

Hits the oracle without the curated label.

04 / ASSESSMENT

Strengths vs. Limitations

A methodologically careful, notably self-critical paper. The reframing is the real contribution.

▲ Strengths

  • Principled reformulation — self-repair as measurable additivity failure, with Prop 2 a clean impossibility result for any clean-state per-unit score.
  • Label-free & output-grounded — no dependence on curated circuit labels; one recovered set serves three downstream pipelines.
  • Rigorous statistics — permutation, hypergeometric, DeLong tests plus a 100-backup synthetic to fix the 8-positive underpowering.
  • Unusually honest writing — explicitly flags the unfair-seed caveat, reports the fair 0.63 vs 0.91, discloses the 0.60 standalone limit and the 0.92 co-activation rival.
  • Cheap vs brute force — O(|U|) per seed vs O(|U|²); clean ablation (drop the 2nd-order term collapses name-mover clustering 0.76 → 0.34).
  • Causal verification via counterfactual patching, not just correlational ranking.

▼ Limitations

  • Completion-only — needs a primary seed; standalone (0.60) is weaker than seed-free AtP* (0.82). It complements, not replaces, first-order discovery.
  • Single labeled-backup circuit — IOI is the only one with head-level ground truth; the 0.91 headline rests on 8 positives whose labels come from prior manual work.
  • Home-turf competitor — input-side co-activation also finds IOI backups (0.92 AUC) and is cheaper; CoAx's clear win is confined to movement circuits.
  • No MLP transfer — greater-than circuit fails (~1.5× random); head-level signal may not transfer to FFN-group granularity.
  • Pruning framing — the dominant gain is "co-ablation energy is a good head score," not the self-repair-specific sequential refinement; strongest one-shot pruners (SparseGPT) not fully represented; 7B compute unreported.
05 / OPEN

Questions for further exploration

Where the additivity-blind-spot theorem meets automated circuit discovery.
Q1

Can the external primary seed be removed — e.g. bootstrapping a seed from CoAx's own top first-order heads and iterating — to jointly recover primaries + backups?

Q2

What is CoAx's false-positive / false-negative behavior on circuits that do not self-repair, and is there a principled comp(S) threshold to declare a circuit non-redundant?

Q3

Beyond IOI and induction, does CoAx recover known redundancy in copy-suppression, anti-erasure, factual-recall / MQA — at head and at MLP / feature granularity?

Q4

How do pruning gains compare head-to-head with SparseGPT and Wanda-quantized variants at matched sparsity & zero-shot accuracy, and what is the wall-clock cost of sequential re-measuring at 7B?

Q5

Can pairwise synergy + CoAx unify into a single interaction-aware circuit-discovery objective that improves ACDC / EAP-IG on standard MIB benchmarks?

Q6

Is the additivity-blind-spot theorem tight — are there relaxed first-order scores that partially recover backups, and where exactly is the information-theoretic boundary?
