> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一种基于"参考锚定激活拒绝间隙 ρ + WeightWatch 权重恢复能量 E1"双信号免阈值检查点审计方法（z-sum），在 94 个有效检查点（57 个去拒绝化 + 37 个良性编辑）上达 AUROC 0.95，留一家族迁移 balanced accuracy 0.89、FPR 0.11，并诚实标出两类失效：伪造参考可零训练绕过、白盒所有者可经自适应微调（Qwen2.5-1.5B 上验证）越过阈值同时保持连贯与"对 guard 不安全"。

> **TL;DR:** A cheap, threshold-free checkpoint audit detects whether an open-weight LLM's refusal mechanism has been *abliterated*, *before* deployment (a blind spot for runtime guards that only score generations). It z-sums two cheap, negatively-correlated (Pearson r = −0.41), label-complementary internal signals — a reference-anchored activation refusal-gap ρ (refusal-specificity/precision) and the WeightWatch rank-1 weight-recovery energy E1 (recall) — reaching AUROC 0.95 vs. 0.84/0.90 alone, with held-out (leave-one-family-out) balanced accuracy 0.89 (FPR 0.11), missing 4/57. The paper is unusually honest: it documents two severity-ranked failures (a zero-training spoofed-reference attack that defeats both axes by construction; a white-box owner who fine-tunes past the threshold while staying non-refusing, coherent, and 20/20 guard-unsafe), and frames the method as triage — not tamper-proofing — conditioned on an attested reference. The main caveat is statistical power: of a 273-checkpoint registry, only 94 were fully evaluated (compute-bound on a single 64 GB workstation).

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **双信号、免阈值的开放权重检查点审计**：在部署前、基于权重与激活（而非生成内容）检测模型是否被"消融去拒绝"（abliteration），弥补运行时 guard 只评分生成、无法评估模型本体的盲区。
- 刻画并利用两个信号的关键性质——**负相关（r = −0.41）且标签互补**：参考锚定的激活拒绝间隙 ρ 提供拒绝特异性（精确度方向，但受限于单一方向），WeightWatch 秩-1 谱能量 E1 提供召回率（捕捉多方向/Heretic/reasoning 蒸馏等低秩编辑）。二者 z-sum（s = z(−ρ) + z(E1)）显著优于任一单信号。
- 在四类家族（Qwen、DeepSeek 蒸馏 Qwen、Llama、Gemma）的 94 个有效检查点（57 去拒绝化 + 37 良性编辑）上：**AUROC 0.95**，留一家族迁移 **balanced accuracy 0.89 / FPR 0.11**，仅漏检 4/57（均为普通变体，非难例）；对照 AMS Tier-1/Tier-2 基线大幅领先。
- 诚实给出 **两类失效并按严重性排序**：(1) **伪造参考**——声明一个已被去拒绝化的兄弟模型或直接用候选自身作为参考，可零训练使 ΔW=0、ρ≈1 绕过两轴；(2) **白盒所有者**——在 Qwen2.5-1.5B 上用组合目标自适应微调，将分数压到阈值以下（step 600: E1=0.29, ρ=1.82），同时保持连贯（ppl 180 vs base 194）、能力基本无损（MMLU 0.58 vs 0.59）且对 guard 全不安全（20/20）。普通 SFT/DPO 微调不会打开该漏洞。
- 明确将方法定位为 **分诊（triage）而非防篡改**：根信任必须是 **出版方签名哈希或硬件认证的谱系**，而非上传者自报的 base；并公开了对照集、审计协议、损失函数与清单（按需提供代码）。

## Key Contributions

- A **threshold-free, two-signal checkpoint audit** that answers the *artifact-level* question "has this open-weight checkpoint's refusal been stripped?" *before* deployment — a question runtime guards (which only score generations) cannot answer.
- The central design insight: the two signals are **negatively correlated (r = −0.41) and label-complementary**. The activation gap ρ = gap(M_c)/gap(M_b) is refusal-specific but bounded to a single difference-of-means direction (it misses multi-direction removal, e.g. Heretic, and reasoning-distilled models where refusal migrates into the chain-of-thought). The WeightWatch rank-1 spectral energy E1 = σ₁²/Σσᵢ² fires on *any* low-rank weight edit, recovering those cases (recall) but over-flagging benign low-rank edits. Their z-sum combines both strengths.
- Strong, CI-bearing results on a 94-checkpoint evaluation set (drawn from a 273-checkpoint registry): **AUROC 0.95** (paired ΔAUROC +0.10 over ρ, 95% CI [.04,.18]; +0.04 over E1, [.005,.09], both CIs exclude 0); **leave-one-family-out balanced accuracy 0.89 (FPR 0.11, detection 0.90)**, missing 4/57; and large margins over AMS Tier-1 (bal. acc. 0.65) and Tier-2 (0.61).
- A **severity-ranked failure map** + a genuine adaptive attack: (i) a **spoofed reference** evades both axes with zero training (declared already-abliterated sibling → ρ=0.69/0.73 on 2/2; candidate-as-own-reference → ΔW=0, ρ=1 by construction); (ii) a **white-box owner** fine-tunes a Qwen2.5-1.5B checkpoint past the threshold (step 600: E1=0.29, ρ=1.82) while staying non-refusing (0.15), coherent (ppl 180 vs 194), near-lossless (MMLU 0.58 vs 0.59, GSM8K 0.51 vs 0.57), and **20/20 guard-unsafe**. Natural SFT/DPO does *not* open this hole (verified on 6 Qwen full fine-tunes).
- Honest scoping: triage, not tamper-proofing; root of trust must be a **publisher-signed hash or hardware-attested lineage**, not an uploader-nominated base. Auditable protocol + contrast-set hashes + per-checkpoint manifest released (on request); evaded weights and raw harmful generations withheld.

## Methodology

**Setup.** An auditor holds a candidate M_c and a trusted, *attested* reference M_b (base or sibling with pinned lineage). Ground-truth labels come from a behavioral oracle — **Qwen3Guard-Gen-8B** (chain-of-thought traces stripped, greedy decoding ≤256 tokens) — which labels whether a checkpoint is uncensored; the guard supplies labels, it is *not* the detector.

**Signal 1 — Reference-anchored activation refusal-gap ρ.** From a fixed 500 harmful/benign prompt-pair contrast set (AdvBench 379 + JailbreakBench 63 + HarmBench 58; seed 20260528; SHA-256 pinned; reused across all families, never including audited checkpoints), per-layer unit difference-of-means refusal directions r̂_ℓ are estimated on M_b only. The gap is the band-mean harmful-minus-benign last-token projection:

gap(M) = mean_{ℓ∈B}[ mean_harm⟨a_ℓ, r̂_ℓ⟩ − mean_ben⟨a_ℓ, r̂_ℓ⟩ ],   ρ = gap(M_c)/gap(M_b)

where B is the mid-stack band ⌊0.33L⌋…⌊0.67L⌋ (where the refusal direction is most separable). ρ ≈ 1 for intact models, falling toward 0 as refusal is removed. Anchoring to M_b lets one threshold transfer across scales; it is refusal-specific but **bounded to a single direction**.

**Signal 2 — Weight-recovery energy E1 (WeightWatch, Zhong & Raghunathan 2025).** The rank-1 spectral energy fraction of the base-to-candidate weight difference, averaged over o_proj and down_proj matrices in band B:

E1 = mean_{m}[ σ₁²(ΔW_m) / Σ_i σ_i²(ΔW_m) ] ∈ [0,1],   ΔW_m = W_b^m − W_c^m.

It fires on any low-rank weight edit (Jain et al. 2024: safety edits are low-rank/null-space MLP edits), so it catches multi-direction removals the gap misses — but it cannot alone tell refusal *removal* from *addition* (it reads E1=1.0 even on safety-hardened models).

**Combined detector.** Because the two are negatively correlated (r = −0.41) and label-complementary, each is z-standardized on a reference population and summed: s(M_c) = z(−ρ) + z(E1). The score is threshold-free for ranking; the standardization is *transductive* (uses the reference population's mean/SD), so a deployable audit fixes the operating point by **Youden's J** on a calibration set of attested references, whose transfer to unseen families is reported via leave-one-family-out. A standardization-free absolute subspace variant D_comb = max(d_sig, d_sub) is also defined (App. A) but only reaches 0.82 vs. 0.89.

**Construct-validity checks (App. B).** (i) ρ is not a norm artifact — recomputing from cosine/unit-norm projections raises rather than removes it. (ii) Single-direction bound confirmed directly: removing along an orthogonal direction drives ρ≈0.99 (evades the gap) yet E1=1.0 still fires. (iii) The gap measures *refusal, not harmfulness* (per Zhao et al. 2025a), so the white-box attack hides only the refusal axis. (iv) E1 is direction-agnostic: safety-hardening (Abu Shairah et al. 2025) on an abliterated base re-inserts refusal but keeps E1=1.0 — so the refusal-specificity comes entirely from the gap.

**Label robustness.** A second independent guard (Granite Guardian 3.0-2B, different family, scoring harmfulness rather than refusal) agrees at Cohen's κ = 0.78 (93% raw agreement, 4/59 flipped), with disagreements falling exactly on the refusal-vs-harmfulness boundary.

## Results & Highlights

- **AUROC 0.95** for the z-sum vs. **0.84** (ρ) and **0.90** (E1); paired ΔAUROC CIs both exclude 0; a fitted 2-D logistic does not beat the threshold-free sum. Leave-one-out standardization leaves AUROC unchanged (0.948), so the transductive step does not leak.
- **Held-out transfer (leave-one-family-out):** bal. acc. **0.89**, detection **0.90**, **FPR 0.11**, missing **4/57** (all ordinary abliteration variants, not hard recipes). Reference-anchoring is decisive: held-out detection climbs 0.35 (absolute-σ AMS Tier-1) → 0.42 (reference-anchored AMS Tier-2) → 0.83 (this harmful-minus-benign anchored ratio).
- **Per-recipe FPR (App. D, Table 3):** false positives concentrate in merges (2/9 = 0.22), not plain fine-tunes (3/26 = 0.12) — the detector is not merely flagging "any low-rank edit," though the residual confound with aggressive benign merges is the price of the weight-energy recall.
- **Failure 1 (most severe, zero-training):** declaring an already-abliterated sibling shrinks ΔW and inflates ρ (ρ=0.69/0.73 on 2/2); using the candidate as its own reference evades by construction (ΔW=0, ρ=1). Mitigation: publisher-signed hash / hardware-attested lineage as the root of trust (App. E).
- **Failure 2 (white-box, Qwen2.5-1.5B):** composite objective L = λ_w L_weight + λ_a L_gap + λ_r L_retain + β L_uncensor; AdamW lr 5e-5; step-600 evaded checkpoint reads benign (E1=0.29, ρ=1.82) yet is **20/20 guard-unsafe** [0.83,1.0], non-refusing (0.15), coherent (ppl 180 vs 194), near-lossless (MMLU 0.58/0.59, GSM8K 0.51/0.57). Natural SFT/DPO does *not* open the hole (6 Qwen full fine-tunes keep E1 high while becoming uncensored).
- **Hardware:** single 64 GB Apple M4 Max (MPS); the registry is 273 but only **94** fully processed (71 with both guard label + detector output → 57 positives + 37 negatives; 14 labeled-but-refusing excluded), bounded by compute, not curated on outcomes.

## Authors & Credibility

- **Single author: Gabriel Hurtado, Moonsong Labs.** Preprint, under review. The methodology is unusually careful for a single-author safety paper: bootstrap CIs (5000 resamples), a second independent guard for label robustness (κ=0.78), construct-validity ablations, a genuine adaptive attack with a held-out disjoint split for the gap, and an explicit threat model with three adversaries ordered by capability.
- The honesty stands out: the author repeatedly characterizes the method as *triage, not tamper-proofing*, and the conclusion explicitly enumerates the three limits an operator must hold simultaneously.
- Credibility caveats: single author / single workstation; the registry is 273 but only 94 evaluated (compute-bound attrition), so headline numbers rest on a small effective n=57/37 with wide held-out bands; white-box attack is a one-family, one-scale existence proof; artifact release is "on request" (cleaned public release "planned").

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine and well-scoped. Reframing abliteration as an *artifact* (not *generation*) detection problem, and deliberately pairing a precision-oriented activation signal with a recall-oriented weight signal, is clean. The negative-correlation observation (r = −0.41) is the load-bearing insight that makes the combination more than additive, and the construct-validity checks (orthogonal-direction bound, hardening confound) show the author understands exactly what each axis does and does not measure.
- **Rigor:** Good for the venue. CIs on every metric, leave-one-out to rule out standardization leakage, a second guard for labels, and a real adaptive attack with a disjoint train/held-out split for the gap probe. **However**, the headline rests on n=57/37 (94 of 273 processed); the held-out operating-point CIs are wide (e.g., bal. acc. [.83,.95], FPR [.03,.22]); there is **no per-family AUROC breakdown** in the main table; and the benign-negative set contains **no safety-hardened models**, which the author concedes makes the headline FPR optimistic for that case.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong in spirit — full audit protocol, contrast-set hashes, hyperparameters, per-checkpoint manifest, seeds recorded. **But** code/metric are "available on request" rather than public, and the registry attrition (94/273) is not externally auditable yet. The attested-reference precondition is a real deployment friction.
- **Significance:** Practically valuable for model hubs / deployment platforms vetting redistributed open-weight checkpoints. The **spoofed-reference failure is serious and total** — the method is only as strong as base attestation, which the paper frankly admits. The white-box hole is an existence proof (one family/scale), but its existence is itself a notable contribution that competing concurrent work (Coslett 2026; Xu & Sheng 2026) leaves open.
- **Red flags / overclaiming:** **None egregious.** The author is commendably restrained — the abstract itself names both failure modes and bounds claims by the registry. The only real risk is *readers* ignoring the "triage, not tamper-proofing" caveat and treating AUROC 0.95 as a guarantee, especially given the trivial spoofing attack.
- **Weak spots to probe:** (a) statistical power — 94/273 evaluated is small; (b) the gap's last-token formulation is known to miss reasoning-distilled refusals (admitted, "open problem"); (c) no comparison to a runtime guard as a *triage baseline* (cost vs. caught attacks); (d) the white-box attack uses a *linear* proxy of the detector in its loss and is validated on one model — generalization of the evasion is unproven.

## Strengths

- Cheap, compute-light, deployable *before* generation-based runtime guards; both signals are simple scalars (a layer-band-averaged gap ratio and a rank-1 energy fraction).
- Strong, principled synergy from two complementary, negatively correlated signals (AUROC 0.84/0.90 → 0.95).
- Cross-family threshold transfer (bal. acc. 0.89, FPR 0.11) with reference-anchoring shown to be the decisive ingredient (0.35 → 0.42 → 0.83).
- Unusual intellectual honesty: severity-ranked failure map, a working adaptive attack, label robustness via a second guard, and explicit scope limits.
- Auditable protocol + pinned contrast-set hashes + threat model with three ordered adversaries.

## Limitations

- **Effective n is small:** only **94 of 273** registry checkpoints fully evaluated (single 64 GB workstation, compute-bound), giving wide held-out CIs.
- **Spoofed-reference attack is trivial and total** (ΔW=0, ρ=1) — the method requires a trusted/attested reference (publisher-signed hash or hardware-attested lineage), which may not exist in adversarial open-weight ecosystems.
- **E1 is direction-agnostic** — it fires on safety-*hardening* too; since the benign-negative set contains no hardened models, the headline FPR is optimistic against that case.
- **Activation gap is single-direction and last-token:** misses Heretic-style multi-direction removal and reasoning-distilled refusals migrated into chain-of-thought (admitted as an open problem).
- **White-box evasion is a one-family (Qwen2.5-1.5B), one-scale proof of concept** using a linear detector proxy; multi-family/different-scale generalization unproven.
- No per-family AUROC breakdown; no comparison to a runtime guard as a triage baseline; artifact release is "on request," not yet public.
- Ground-truth labels certify *refusal*, not *harmlessness* — behaviorally-refusing-but-internally-edited checkpoints are mislabeled by construction.

## Relevance

该工作直接面向**开放权重大模型的安全审计与供应链风险**这一关键问题——在部署前、基于权重与激活检测模型是否被去拒绝化（abliteration），对模型平台、红/蓝队及 LLM 安全研究者评估开源模型谱系可信度有较高参考价值。其"双信号互补 + 诚实失效地图 + 自适应攻击"的范式，对安全检测方法设计、防御边界刻画与可解释审计均具借鉴意义，尤其契合关注 **LLM 安全、可解释性（mechanistic/representation engineering）与对齐鲁棒性** 的研究方向。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How robust is ρ to merges, quantization, and frankenstein checkpoints that blend multiple bases, and can E1's merge false-positive rate (0.22) be reduced without sacrificing the multi-direction recall?
- Can the spoofed-reference failure be closed practically with a publisher-signed-hash / hardware-attestation layer, and what is the *minimal* attestation needed (and its operational cost for a hub)?
- What are the **per-family AUROC and confusion matrices** at the Youden operating point, and how does bal. acc. 0.89 move as the effective n grows toward the full 273?
- Would a third signal — a harmfulness-direction probe (Zhao et al. 2025a) or a representation-engineering direction test — recover the 4 misses and close the reasoning-distilled gap without raising FPR?
- Does the white-box evasion generalize across families and scales (the proof is Qwen2.5-1.5B only), and can a stronger, non-linear detector objective defeat the linear-proxy evasion used here?
- How does this checkpoint-time triage compare, in cost-vs.-caught-attacks, to a runtime guard layered on top — and is the two-signal audit a strict improvement or a complementary layer?
