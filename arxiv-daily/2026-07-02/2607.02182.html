Key Finding · Bayesian PEFT

Injecting a single learnable Bernoulli mask on LoRA's rank axis turns it Bayesian — at +520 params, faster than LoRA, with 9/10 best calibration.

DALorRA rewrites LLM uncertainty quantification by placing a stochastic diagonal mask D inside the LoRA update (ΔW = BDA), learning its variational posterior via Gumbel-Sigmoid, and averaging M=10 sampled masks at inference for ensemble-style calibration — one cheap mechanism serving as both Bayesian neural network and deep ensemble.

05 / METHODOLOGY

From adapter weights to the LoRA rank axis

DALorRA's core insight: the LoRA update ΔW = BA is a sum of r rank-one components (ΔW = Σⱼ zⱼ bⱼaⱼⱼ), and not all are necessary — some are superfluous capacity that drives overconfidence. Rather than placing Gaussian priors over B or A (as BLoB does), DALorRA keeps A, B deterministic and injects a stochastic diagonal mask into the update.

A Bernoulli prior p(zⱼ) = Bern(p₀) sits on each mask entry; its intractable posterior is approximated by a factorized variational distribution q_φ(z) = Π Bern(σ(φⱼ)). A, B, and φ are jointly optimized by minimizing the negative ELBO. The discrete mask is made differentiable through Gumbel-Sigmoid reparameterization; the Bernoulli-Bernoulli KL admits a closed form. At inference, M = 10 masks are sampled and averaged — a deep ensemble over diverse rank-one configurations.

  1. 1

    Decompose & mask

    Write ΔW as Σⱼ zⱼ bⱼaⱼⱼ; gate each rank-one term with zⱼ ∈ {0,1} via D = diag(z).

  2. 2

    Variational posterior

    q_φ(z) = Π Bern(σ(φⱼ)); init φ so q = prior for stable optimization.

  3. 3

    Minimize −ELBO

    MC likelihood (S samples, Gumbel-Sigmoid) + closed-form Bernoulli KL. Joint opt of A, B, φ.

  4. 4

    Ensemble inference

    Average predictions over M = 10 sampled masks → ensemble-style calibration.

Fig. Mechanism · ΔW = B · diag(z) · A

The diagonal z gates each rank-one component of ΔW. A learned, non-uniform posterior over z performs data-adaptive rank allocation — confirmed by Figure 4, where learned probabilities vary sharply across layers, modules (Q vs. V), and datasets.

Ablation · learned > dropout

Random rank-masking (fixed K of 8) underperforms DALorRA even at its optimal K — and that optimal K varies sharply per task (starvation on WG-M/OBQA vs. mild benefit on ARC-E). Learning the posterior matters.

Rank study · capacity + sparsity

Raising max rank r (3→8) improves both accuracy and calibration — good performance comes from sufficient capacity paired with learned rank sparsity, not from starvation alone.

07 / TRADE-OFFS

What it earns · what it concedes

Strengths

what it earns
  • Elegant core idea — rank-level Bernoulli masking turns LoRA into both a Bayesian NN and an ensemble within one cheap mechanism.
  • Strictly cheaper and better-calibrated — 520 extra params and faster-than-LoRA training, a rare dual win.
  • Fair, strong baselines — 8 methods incl. Laplace-LoRA, BLoB, TFB, C-LoRA; the random-masking ablation isolates the value of learning the posterior.
  • Principled VI — clean ELBO, closed-form Bernoulli KL, differentiable Gumbel-Sigmoid sampling.
  • Interpretable posteriors — non-uniform learned probabilities give evidence of genuine data-adaptive rank allocation.

Limitations

what it concedes
  • Eval scope is narrow — only classification-style reasoning with fixed answer sets; not open-ended generation (acknowledged).
  • ~10× inference latency — M=10 forward passes; problematic for latency-sensitive serving (acknowledged).
  • Rank-level only — may miss fine-grained weight-level uncertainty (acknowledged).
  • Independent Bernoulli factorization — ignores cross-rank / cross-layer / cross-module dependencies (acknowledged).
  • Single 8B model — primary results on Llama-3.1-8B; Llama2-7B relegated to appendix; 70B+ scalability unverified.
