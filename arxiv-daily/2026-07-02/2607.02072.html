Turning LLM hidden activations into a training-free guardrail.

kNNGuard reuses a frozen LLM as a feature extractor, runs kNN over multi-layer Fisher-weighted activations fused with MiniLM embeddings, and adapts to a new domain in seconds by swapping a tiny labeled bank.

Mahmoud Abdelfattah · Hamid Nasiri · Peter Garraghan Lancaster University · Mindgard arxiv.org/abs/2607.02072 ↗

With just 50 labeled examples per class, kNNGuard reaches 87.4% F1 across 6 domains — beating or matching fine-tuned SOTA guards while running 2.7× faster and adapting to a new domain in 7.95 seconds.

Why This Matters

A no-training, sub-100ms, seconds-to-swap-domain guardrail for LLM safety deployment and activation-based detection — but its adversarial robustness remains untested against adaptive attacks.

Critical Edge

No adaptive white-box adversary evaluation — the central threat to activation-space methods that the paper itself cites (Nasr et al. 2025, "attacker moves second") yet never tests.

Methodology

Two-phase cache · frozen LLM + frozen MiniLM · non-parametric kNN

Formulation. Build a training-free decision g_FE(x) → {0,1} from a labeled bank D = {(xᵢ,yᵢ)}. The LLM Θ_F and embedding model Θ_E stay frozen — no gradients flow.

Layer Ensemble (LE). Extract last-token hidden activations from 9 evenly-spaced transformer layers. Score each layer's separability with a Fisher discriminant J_ℓ = B_ℓ / W_ℓ, then softmax-weight and concatenate activations into ϕ_act(x̃).

Fused Ensemble (FE). Fuse the activation-space kNN risk s_act with the MiniLM embedding risk s_emb via a confidence-adaptive rule: if the confidence gap Δ > γ=0.1, take the more-confident branch (winner-takes-all); otherwise blend confidence-weighted.

Domain adaptation = bank swap. No retraining — replace the cached activation/embedding bank, optionally edit the system prompt. The system prompt itself reshapes activation geometry, cutting FPR by 66% (0.385 → 0.129) and lifting F1 0.842 → 0.874.

Strengths · Limitations

Production-useful vs adversarially unproven

✓ Strengths

  • Genuinely training-free — domain adaptation is a bank-swap (~8 s for 100 samples).
  • Clean topical wins — ≥95% F1, ≤0.9% FPR; stays robust on two coding sub-domains where the fine-tuned baseline collapses (F1 83.4% / 39.1% FPR).
  • Interpretable dual-view fusion — activation + embedding, motivated by t-SNE / Silhouette (+89.0% coding, +53.2% medical).
  • Most balanced FPR/ASR profile on security domains — every baseline collapses on at least one error component.
  • Broad evaluation — 6 domains, 16 datasets, 6 backbones, system-prompt & bank-size ablations, LOOCV hyperparameter selection.

! Limitations

  • No adaptive-adversary evaluation — white-box attacks shifting activations are the core threat, cited but untested.
  • Modest absolute security performance — 73–82% F1 / 14–30% ASR is operationally borderline; "best balanced" partly reflects competitors being broken.
  • Backbone-coupled — prompt-injection F1 swings across LLMs (Mistral drops to 50.3% / 65.5% ASR); model updates force re-extraction.
  • Not hyperparameter-free — per-domain k, τ, γ, λ, layer-set tuning is light supervision.
  • Fragile latency headline — the 10× claim rests on a slow baseline; Prompt Guard 2 (9.7 ms) is faster than kNNGuard.
  • n=50 sweet spot may not generalize — larger banks add borderline neighbours; code/bank release not stated.

Open Questions

Where the work begs a follow-up
Q

How does kNNGuard hold up against adaptive white-box adversaries optimized to land in the safe-activation cluster — the cited "attacker moves second" threat?

Q

If τ, γ, λ are fixed once globally, how much degrades — and is there a validation-free, principled way to set them?

Q

Is the Fisher layer-weighting robust to model updates / quantization, and how often must the bank + layer set be refreshed in production?

Q

Why does a larger bank hurt? Would distance-weighted kNN or a small learned projection recover gains at n=250/1000?

Q

Can MiniLM be replaced by the LLM's own final-layer embedding to drop the second model without losing fusion benefit?

Q

On security domains, what is the per-attack-family breakdown of the 14–30% ASR — are encoding tricks vs. roleplay systematically missed?
