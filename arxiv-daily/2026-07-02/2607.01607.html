Methodology

The core idea: map both GEMM types onto a shared three-stage LUT pipeline so the heavy precision-specific work is done at construction time — runtime becomes lookup + sign XOR + exponent add + FP32 accumulate, with no dedicated FP multipliers.

FP8-INT4 mode LINEAR LAYERS

PROJ · FFN1 · FFN2 — weight-only quantization
  • BCQ encoding: INT4 weights bit-plane-decomposed, regrouped column-wise as LUT index.
  • Sign symmetry halves storage (only 2^(r−1) patterns, r=4 in HW).
  • Runtime = query + bit-plane shift-accumulate.

FP8-FP8 mode ATTENTION · KEY NOVELTY

QK · AV — maps continuous FP multiply to discrete LUT indexing
  • Product formation moved to construction: 16 weight-mantissa candidates enumerated.
  • Each product stored as a compact 8-bit FP8-like entry (not FP16/FP32).
  • Subnormals via flush-to-zero (FTZ); activation ratio ≤0.33%.
OS · Output-StationaryPREFILL
Compute-bound large GEMM. FP32 partial sums stay in the PE; zero intermediate SRAM writeback. Localizes partial sums for data reuse.
wavefront latency 3N+S−3 → 2N+S−2
registers 3N(N−1)/2 → N(N−1)/2
WS · Weight-StationaryDECODE
Memory-bound GEMV-like. Weights stay stationary; activations re-streamed, outputs forwarded. Maximizes weight residency / reuse.

Cost of unification is small. Adding native FP8-FP8 support over FP8-INT4-only FIGLUT costs only 2.57% area-efficiency and 3.34% energy-efficiency — an attractive trade-off. Design-space sweep finds energy efficiency rising 15.8 → 22.5 → 28.5 TFLOPS/W from 16 → 32 → 64 MUXes, with 64 MUX as the knee; gains diminish sharply beyond. Accuracy on WikiText-103 stays within 1.70% perplexity increase, attributed cleanly to FTZ (<0.08%) plus the 8-bit product approximation.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

What lands well
  • Truly unified LUT mechanism removing both dedicated FP datapaths and VPU fallback — a clean, well-executed architectural simplification.
  • Phase-aware (prefill OS / decode WS) reconfigurable dataflow backed by concrete latency & register-overhead formulas.
  • Rigorous, fair protocol: identical budgets + per-design best legal mappings; explicit ablation isolating FP8-FP8 LUT from dataflow effects.
  • Honest SOTA positioning — repeatedly flags its 28nm disadvantage; efficiency wins arise from architecture, not process node.
  • Transparent disclosure that AI tools were limited to grammar only.

Limitations

What to scrutinize
  • Custom ASIC; results depend on synthesis/CACTI modeling — don't directly translate to FPGA or commodity GPUs.
  • At long contexts decode gains are capped by DRAM traffic (compute share falls below ~37–50%); dramatic wins sit at short–moderate contexts.
  • Evaluation concentrated on Llama-3 (1B/3B/8B); Mistral/Qwen outlier profiles untested.
  • FP8-centric scope — no FP16/BF16 path; INT8-activation regimes out of scope.
  • FTZ subnormal handling is a numerical shortcut whose robustness on OOD activations is unexplored.

Open Questions

Q1How does the 8-bit product approximation accumulate error in deeper / longer-context models — and could a 9–10 bit entry cut the 1.70% perplexity gap?
Q2What is the batch-1 (single-stream) end-to-end latency/energy behavior, which matters most for interactive edge serving?
Q3How do results change under joint activation+weight quantization (e.g. W4A8 with INT8 attention), not just weight-only FP8?
Q4Can the unified LUT fabric extend to FP8 E5M2 (wider exponent) without disproportionate entry growth?
Q5How does MxGLUT compare against recent non-LUT digital mixed-precision accelerators beyond the 28nm SOTA table?
Q6Is the 64-MUX knee point stable across model sizes, or does optimal PE parallelism shift for very large/small models?
