> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Expander SAE——将 TopK 稀疏自编码器的解码器与绑定编码器约束在左 d-正则扩展图掩码（d≪m）上，把需学习的解码器参数从 O(mn) 降到 O(dn) 同时保持稀疏编码规模 (m,n,k) 不变；在 Qwen2.5-3B 上 d=7 时以 293× 更少的解码器参数（114,688 vs 33,554,432）保留 84% 的稠密 CE-loss 恢复率，并证明扩展性+列平坦性足以保证无噪 k-稀疏码可识别、OMP 可精确恢复支撑。

> **TL;DR:** Expander SAEs replace the dense O(mn) decoder (and tied encoder) of TopK sparse autoencoders with a frozen left-d-regular expander-graph mask (d≪m), cutting learned decoder values to O(dn) while keeping the (m,n,k) sparse-coding problem fixed; at the most compressed modern-LM setting (Qwen2.5-3B, d=7) they use 293× fewer decoder values while keeping 84% of dense CE-loss recovered, with theory proving identifiability and exact OMP support recovery under expansion + column flatness. Three controlled experiments isolate that the gain comes from diverse sparse support structure (not just parameter count), and iterative OMP closes most of the remaining encoder-amortisation gap.

## 核心贡献

- **架构创新**：提出 Expander SAE——tied-weight TopK SAE，其解码器与绑定编码器由固定左 d-正则扩展图掩码支撑。可学习参数仅 V∈ℝ^{m×n}（只在掩码非零位置接收梯度）、b_enc、b_dec，总数为 dn+n+m，把解码器参数从 mn 降到 dn 而保持 (m,n,k) 不变。公式为 W_dec=(V⊙M)diag(ν)⁻¹，W_enc=W_dec⊤。
- **存储–保真度前沿**：在 Pythia-70M/160M、Qwen2.5-3B、Llama-3.2-1B 四个模型上验证改变 d 一致地刻画平滑的存储–保真度权衡曲线；Qwen2.5-3B 层 12、d=7 时用 293× 更少参数（114,688 vs 33,554,432）保留 84% CE 恢复（0.828/0.983）。
- **并行 OMP**：提出利用左 d-正则列结构的并行 OMP 实现，在“快速摊销编码器推理”与“高保真迭代解码”之间形成 Pareto 前沿；OMP 把相关性步骤 W⊤r 变为 O(dn) gather-and-reduce。
- **可识别性理论**：证明在 (2k,ε,d)-扩展与列平坦条件下（2β²ε<1），无噪 k-稀疏码有唯一 k-稀疏解释（Theorem 3.1）；并在更强累积相干条件下（β²ε(2k+1)<1）证明 OMP 在 k 步内精确恢复支撑（Corollary 3.2）。
- **因果对照实验**：三组对照证明收益来自稀疏且多样的解码器支撑结构而非单纯参数减少；聚类掩码（破坏多样性）在 d=200 时死特征率上升约 100×（0.7%→6.2%）。

## Key Contributions

- **Architecture.** Expander SAEs are tied TopK SAEs whose decoder columns (and tied encoder) are supported on a frozen left-d-regular expander mask M∈{0,1}^{m×n} with ‖M_j‖₀=d. Learnable parameters are V (only nonzero mask entries receive gradients), b_enc, b_dec — total dn+n+m — reducing decoder values from mn to dn while keeping (m,n,k) fixed.
- **Storage–fidelity frontier.** Across Pythia-70M/160M, Qwen2.5-3B, and Llama-3.2-1B residual streams, varying d traces a smooth storage–fidelity curve. Headline: Qwen2.5-3B layer 12 with d=7 uses 293× fewer learned decoder values (114,688 vs 33,554,432) while retaining 84% of dense CE recovery (0.828/0.983).
- **Parallel OMP.** A parallel OMP implementation exploiting left-d-regular column structure turns the correlation step W_dec⊤r into an O(dn) gather-and-reduce, yielding a Pareto frontier between fast amortised encoder inference (~1.8M tokens/s) and high-fidelity iterative decoding (~13k tokens/s).
- **Identifiability theory.** Theorem 3.1 proves that if M is a (2k,ε,d)-expander and 2β²ε<1 (β = √d·max|W_ij| is the column-flatness factor), every noiseless k-sparse code has a unique k-sparse explanation. Corollary 3.2 gives a stronger cumulative-coherence condition (β²ε(2k+1)<1) under which OMP recovers the support exactly in k steps.
- **Causal controls.** Three controlled experiments disentangle the contribution of the expander mask from parameter count and column sparsity, showing the gain comes from diverse sparse support structure, not merely fewer parameters.

## Methodology

**Architecture.** An Expander SAE with hyperparameters (m, n, d, k) has learnable V∈ℝ^{m×n}, b_enc∈ℝⁿ, b_dec∈ℝᵐ. The decoder and tied encoder are constructed as W_dec=(V⊙M)diag(ν)⁻¹ and W_enc=W_dec⊤, where M∈{0,1}^{m×n} is a binary mask with exactly d ones per column (‖M_j‖₀=d) sampled at initialisation and frozen, and ν normalises each column of M⊙V to unit ℓ2 norm. The forward pass uses σ(z)=TopK_k(z), retaining the k largest signed pre-activations and zeroing the rest. Only the dn nonzero positions of V receive gradients, giving dn+n+m learnable parameters total.

**Theoretical foundation.** The paper grounds SAE inference in combinatorial compressed sensing: recovering a k-sparse x from h≈W_dec·x where M is the adjacency matrix of a (k,ε,d)-expander graph (|Γ(S)|≥(1−ε)d|S| for |S|≤k). Expander matrices satisfy RIP-1: (1−2ε)√d‖x‖₁≤‖Mx‖₁≤√d‖x‖₁. For learned non-binary decoders, the column-flatness factor β(W_dec)=√d·max|W_ij| (with 1≤β≤√d) quantifies deviation from ideal binary expanders. Theorem 3.1 shows expansion + column flatness (2β²ε<1) suffice for identifiability; Corollary 3.2 gives the cumulative-coherence condition for exact OMP support recovery. The author is explicit these are worst-case sufficient conditions meant as architectural motivation, not certification of experimental settings (and notes they are non-vacuous in asymptotic regimes with fixed δ=m/n<1, ρ=k/m).

**OMP decoding.** Standard OMP (Algorithm 1) is adapted: at each step pick j*=argmax_{j∉S}|⟨w_j,r⟩|, refit least-squares on the active set, update residual. The expander structure makes W⊤r an O(dn) sparse gather-and-reduce.

**Experimental setup.** Residual-stream activations extracted at a single hook site from Pythia-70M (layer 3, m=512, n=4096), Pythia-160M (layer 8, m=768, n=6144), Qwen2.5-3B (layers 12 & 24, m=2048, n=16384), Llama-3.2-1B (layers 6 & 12, m=2048, n=16384). Training split: 200,000 tokens; held-out: 5,000 tokens from the Pile. Three metrics: (1) trained-encoder relative reconstruction error err(h)=‖h−ĥ‖₂/‖h‖₂; (2) zero-ablation-normalised CE-loss recovered CE_rec=(CE_zero−CE_recon)/(CE_zero−CE_clean); (3) dead-feature fraction. Baselines: Dense-SAE (independent encoder/decoder) and tied-dense (d=m). All use TopK nonlinearity, unit-ℓ2 column normalisation, same optimiser/schedule/dead-feature resampling. Main paper metrics use trained encoder; OMP is an offline diagnostic. Three seeds. Hardware: A10G GPUs on Modal.

## Results & Highlights

- **Smooth frontier (Figure 1, Pythia-70M layer 3).** At d=7, 73× fewer values, 86% of dense CE recovery (0.817/0.947), rel err 0.541 vs dense 0.342. At d=200, 2.6× fewer values, within ~5pp of dense CE.
- **Cross-model replication (Table 1, k=64).** Qwen2.5-3B layer 12 d=7: 293× compression (114,688 vs 33,554,432 values), CE_rec 0.828 vs dense 0.983, rel err 0.764 vs 0.489. Same monotone d→fidelity ordering across GPT-NeoX, Llama-style, Qwen-style architectures spanning 70M–3B params.
- **Three controls (Figure 2).**
  - *Budget-matched dense (shrink n to n′=dn/m):* On Pythia-70M Expander beats it by 0.10–0.21 CE at every shared budget (because shrinking n changes the sparse-recovery ratio δ=m/n). At modern-LM scale, matched-Dense slightly exceeds Expander — but this gap is mostly encoder amortisation, not decoder quality.
  - *Clustered-sparse (partition rows into G=⌊m/d⌋ blocks, destroy support diversity):* Tracks Expander on rel err at small/medium d, but at d=200 dead-feature rate climbs ~100× (0.7%→6.2%). Column sparsity alone does not explain the frontier.
  - *Pruned dense (extract sparse mask from pretrained dense SAE, keep top-d rows/column):* Closes most of the rel-err gap to dense at every d, but requires a second training pass (2× training compute).
- **Encoder amortisation closed by OMP (Table 3).** Iterative OMP (L=1, top-1 pick + Cholesky refit) recovers most headroom at the storage-extreme cell: +0.073 CE on Qwen d=7, +0.118 CE on Llama d=7. Applied symmetrically, OMP shrinks the matched-Dense n′=240 vs Expander d=30 gap from +0.060 to +0.007 CE on Llama and +0.012 to −0.005 CE on Qwen (Expander now slightly ahead).
- **Throughput.** Trained encoder: ~1.8M tokens/s on A10G; iterative OMP: ~13k tokens/s. Single-shot OMP at L=64 reaches 689k tokens/s with rel err 0.546 (0.024 better than encoder's 0.570), with the 0.024 improvement being the amortisation gap attributable to coefficient refit at 2.6× throughput cost.
- **Qualitative diagnostics.** Activation-Jaccard novelty vs Dense-SAE reference: 81% at d=7 (above 68% dense-vs-dense seed baseline), falling monotonically to 5.8% at d=m. Blinded LLM-judge evaluation (Claude Sonnet 4.5 + GPT-4o, 25 features/architecture): Expander-d=200 rated 3.72±0.19 vs Dense 3.59±0.19 (statistically indistinguishable on 1–5 coherence scale); d=7 drops to 2.83±0.22 (graceful degradation). Inter-judge Spearman ρ=0.74.

## Authors & Credibility

- Single-author paper by Rodrigo Mendoza-Smith (Independent Researcher), accepted to the ICML 2026 Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop. The author has prior directly-relevant publications in combinatorial compressed sensing (Mendoza-Smith & Tanner 2015, 2017; Bah & Tanner 2013), giving strong domain credibility for the theoretical core. Code released at github.com/rodrgo/expander-sae (built with Claude Code assistance). The author is commendably explicit about the limitations of the theoretical guarantees (worst-case sufficient conditions, not certifications) and positions Expander SAEs as evidence of an underexplored design axis rather than a final replacement for dense SAEs.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High and well-scoped. Explicitly importing combinatorial compressed sensing / expander-graph masks into TopK SAE decoder design is a fresh, principled angle. The author claims — and the related-work survey supports — that this is the first SAE architecture to evaluate fixed decoder column sparsity as a storage/interpretability constraint. The connection to prior deep-sparse generative models (Ba 2018) is honestly acknowledged.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Three-seed runs, three controlled experiments isolating distinct causes (parameter count vs. column sparsity vs. support diversity), symmetric OMP application to both architectures, and theoretical guarantees with explicit discussion of when they are vacuous vs. non-vacuous. The matched-parameter comparison is done two ways (shrink n, and prune+retune dense) and the differing conclusions are honestly unpacked.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Public LMs, fixed hook sites, explicit hyperparameters in Appendix A, code released, three seeds, and deployable on-disk storage figures reported. Single-author status is a minor risk but the code release mitigates it.
- **Significance:** Potentially high for scaling SAEs to very large feature counts (where mn decoder values dominate), and the theoretical framing adds a principled tool to the interpretability toolkit. The significance hinges on whether downstream interpretability workflows (circuit discovery, sparse probing) confirm that compressed-but-still-interpretable dictionaries are useful in practice — the paper provides only initial qualitative evidence (LLM-judge coherence, activation novelty).
- **Red flags / weaknesses:**
  - *Metric narrowness.* CE-loss recovery and rel reconstruction error are the primary metrics; they proxy for but do not directly measure interpretability utility. The blinded LLM-judge coherence ratings are a start but are themselves LLM-dependent proxies. No human interpretability evaluation, no sparse-probing / downstream-task benchmarks.
  - *Baseline incompleteness relative to the efficient-SAE frontier.* Baselines are Dense-SAE and tied-dense (d=m). The related work discusses Gated, JumpReLU, BatchTopK, Switch, Matryoshka SAEs, but Expander SAEs are not head-to-head compared against these modern efficient variants at matched parameters/FLOPs — only against dense and pruned-dense. The most natural competitor (Matryoshka SAEs, which also pursue parameter efficiency) is not benchmarked against.
  - *The 84% headline is a single operating point.* At the most compressed cell (d=7, Qwen2.5-3B) CE recovery is 0.828 vs 0.983 — a non-trivial 16-point gap, and the Llama-3.2-1B layer-6 d=7 cell shows CE_rec as low as 0.576, indicating the frontier is model/layer-sensitive. Pythia-70M layer 5 is omitted because Dense-SAE itself fails there (CE_rec<0), which is honest but highlights instability in the regime.
  - *Theory–experiment gap.* The author transparently notes the theorems are worst-case sufficient conditions, not certification of the experimental settings, and that certifying expander/RIP properties is NP-hard. The theorems assume noiseless k-sparse codes, an idealisation of real activations.
  - *Encoder amortisation is doing real work.* At modern-LM scale the matched-parameter dense decoder slightly beats Expander under the trained encoder; Expander only pulls ahead once OMP replaces the encoder on both sides. This is a subtle and important caveat for deployment claims.

## Strengths

- Clean, principled reduction of SAE decoder complexity via a well-understood graph-theoretic object (expanders), with direct links to RIP-1 compressed-sensing theory and the author's own prior work.
- Strong headline efficiency numbers on modern open LMs (Qwen2.5-3B, Llama-3.2-1B), with the frontier replicating across three model families.
- Exceptionally careful experimental hygiene: three seeds, three orthogonal controls, symmetric OMP application, two distinct matched-parameter methodologies, and honest reporting of model-stage failures (e.g., Pythia-70M layer 5).
- Theoretical contributions (identifiability + OMP support recovery for learned real-valued decoders) are non-trivial and include a candid discussion of their asymptotic applicability.
- The author resists overclaiming: explicitly frames Expander SAEs as evidence of an underexplored design axis rather than a dense-SAE replacement.

## Limitations

- Primary metrics (CE-loss recovery, rel reconstruction error) are proxies for interpretability utility; only lightweight qualitative evidence (LLM-judge coherence, activation novelty) is provided.
- No head-to-head comparison against modern efficient SAE families (JumpReLU, Gated, BatchTopK, Matryoshka, Switch) at matched parameters/FLOPs — only dense and pruned-dense baselines.
- The 84% headline is a single cell; the frontier is model/layer-sensitive (e.g., Llama-3.2-1B layer 6 d=7 drops to 0.576 CE recovery).
- At modern-LM scale, the matched-parameter dense decoder edges out Expander under the trained encoder; Expander's advantage materialises only after iterative OMP is applied to both — a deployment-relevant caveat given OMP's ~140× lower throughput.
- Theory assumes noiseless k-sparse codes, an idealisation; the gap between theoretical conditions and experimental regimes is acknowledged but not closed.
- Single-author workshop paper; broader independent validation and larger-scale (e.g., 7B+, multi-hook) evaluations are future work explicitly flagged by the author.

## Relevance

对关注大模型可解释性与稀疏自编码器 Scaling 的研究者高度相关：Expander SAE 直接回应了“大特征字典训练/部署成本过高”的瓶颈，提供可扩展、理论可靠（扩展性+列平坦性保证可识别性与 OMP 精确恢复）的解码器参数压缩路径（最高 293×），可与现有 TopK/JumpReLU 工作流及迭代 OMP 解码组合，为大规模 SAE 训练与可解释性分析提供新设计维度。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do Expander SAE features perform on interpretability-specific benchmarks (sparse probing, automated circuit discovery, feature-ablation causal effects) rather than CE-loss recovery and LLM-judge coherence?
- How do expander masks compare head-to-head against modern efficient SAE variants (Matryoshka, JumpReLU, Gated, BatchTopK, Switch) at matched parameters, FLOPs, and storage?
- Can the remaining 16-point CE gap at d=7 (and the much larger gaps at sensitive layers like Llama-3.2-1B layer 6) be closed by low-rank corrections or learnable/annealed masks, as the author suggests?
- Does the encoder-amortisation finding generalise: is the trained-encoder disadvantage at small d a fundamental limitation, or can encoder architectures (e.g., per-feature gating) be co-designed with expander decoders to close it without OMP's throughput penalty?
- Does the identifiability/OMP theory extend to realistic noisy, approximately sparse activations, and can the column-flatness factor β be regularised during training to push dictionaries toward the provably-recoverable regime?
- At very large scale (7B+ parameters, n≥10⁵ features), does the storage–fidelity frontier continue to hold, and do the deployable storage savings (flat values+rows layout) translate to practical interpretability pipelines?
