PACE®

A Proxy for Agentic Capability Evaluation — predict expensive agent-bench scores from ~100 cheap non-agentic instances.
Key Finding

A model's score on a costly agentic benchmark can be predicted from just ~100 cheap non-agentic instances via SVD-leverage × relevance selection — at ~1/100 the eval cost.

Stack & split

Stack all 19 non-agentic source benchmarks into one matrix X. Split models M into calibration Mtrain and held-out Meval. A joint Lasso is under-determined (|Mtrain| ≪ #instances), so selection and regression are decoupled.

03 / VERDICT

Strengths & limitations, side by side

novelty moderate · significance high · rigor bounded by N
◐ Strengths
  • Tackles a genuine, expensive pain point in agentic LLM evaluation.
  • Clean decoupling of filter-based selection (cheap, stable) from bootstrap-stabilized regression, well-motivated by the under-determined regime.
  • Dual Global / Local selection is principled — shared geometric prior vs. task specialization.
  • Broad, realistic coverage: 14 frontier models, 4 diverse agentic targets, 19 source benchmarks.
  • Honest per-target reporting — does not hide the weak SWE-Bench Verified ranking (Spearman 0.67).
  • Interpretable capability signatures add scientific value beyond the prediction itself.
◑ Limitations & Red Flags
  • Generalization is the crux — only LOOCV within one 14-model snapshot; distribution shift from new architectures is unaddressed.
  • Small N=14 makes pairwise accuracy (91 pairs) statistically fragile; no confidence intervals reported.
  • The "~100× cheaper" claim is vs. a random sub-sample — no head-to-head with CPP, ONEBench, or MCQ-recastings.
  • Harness dependence: all agentic scores from OpenHands; transfer to other harnesses is unverified.
  • Only 4 coding/QA-flavored targets; browser, tool-use, robotics, open-ended agents untested.
  • Regression is correlative, not causal — selected weights may break when capability composition shifts.
04 / OPEN

Questions for further exploration

what would tighten the claim
Q · 01

How fast does accuracy degrade as new post-snapshot model families arrive — and what is a realistic re-calibration cadence?

Q · 02

Does the proxy transfer across agent harnesses, or is it calibrated to OpenHands' quirks?

Q · 03

Can PACE beat stronger cheap baselines — CPP matrix factorization, ONEBench, MCQ-recast tasks — not just random sub-sampling?

Q · 04

How stable is the selected instance subset when the calibration model set changes?

Q · 05

Does it scale to other agentic domains — browser, tool-use, embodied — beyond the 4 coding/QA targets?

Q · 06

Could bootstrap be replaced by a Bayesian regression modelling target-mean uncertainty directly, improving score calibration — not just ranking?
