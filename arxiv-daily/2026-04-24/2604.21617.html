Local Neighborhood Instability in Parametric Projections: Quantitative and Visual Analysis

arXiv: 2604.21617v1

Date: 2026-04-23

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21617v1

一句话总结： 本文提出了一套基于高斯扰动的局部邻域稳定性评估框架，通过位移偏差、偏置和最近锚点分配误差三个指标及配套可视化方法，揭示了参数化投影（如基于MLP的UMAP/t-SNE）中标准邻域保持指标无法发现的局部不稳定性，并验证了Jacobian正则化可将位移降低高达85%。
TL;DR: This paper introduces a stability evaluation framework for parametric dimensionality-reduction projections (MLP-based UMAP and t-SNE). By probing with Gaussian perturbations around anchor points, three metrics -- mean displacement, displacement bias, and nearest-anchor assignment error -- plus three visual diagnostics reveal that standard Trustworthiness/Continuity scores are blind to local instability. Jacobian regularization reduces displacement by up to 85% (UMAP) and 80% (t-SNE) at minimal reconstruction cost. A short, well-scoped EuroVis workshop paper with clear empirical contributions.

核心贡献

  • 提出三个定量稳定性指标（均值位移 $D_{\mathrm{dev}}$、位移偏置 $D_{\mathrm{bias}}$、最近锚点分配误差 $E_{\mathrm{NA}}$），用于评估参数化投影在小扰动下的局部邻域稳定性。
  • 设计三种可视化方法（锚点连线、局部PCA椭球、Voronoi分配图），将稳定性指标与投影空间中的空间和邻域变化直观关联。
  • 在MNIST和Fashion-MNIST上对比了不同规模MLP及Jacobian正则化的效果，证明正则化比单纯增大网络容量更有效。
  • 揭示了一个关键发现：标准邻域保持指标（Trustworthiness和Continuity）在所有方法上几乎不变（T: 0.850--0.931, C: 0.864--0.944），无法反映高达80%以上的位移差异。

Key Contributions

  • Three quantitative stability measures ($D_{\mathrm{dev}}$, $D_{\mathrm{bias}}$, $E_{\mathrm{NA}}$) that capture displacement magnitude, systematic drift direction, and cross-anchor misassignment probability under isotropic Gaussian perturbations around anchor points.
  • Three visual diagnostics -- anchor lines (displacement vectors), local PCA ellipsoids (differential structure and anisotropy), and Voronoi assignment maps (comparative robustness) -- enabling fine-grained spatial inspection of instability.
  • Empirical comparison across UMAP- and t-SNE-based MLP projectors (small: 3x512, large: 6x1024), with and without Jacobian regularization ($\lambda=10$), on MNIST and Fashion-MNIST, demonstrating that Jacobian regularization reduces displacement by 28--85% and assignment error to near-zero.
  • Key finding: Trustworthiness and Continuity remain nearly constant across all configurations (T: 0.850--0.931), completely failing to capture the substantial stability differences revealed by displacement-based metrics.
  • Code and analysis shared on OSF and GitHub for reproducibility.

Methodology

The framework operates in five steps:

  1. Baseline projection: Fit a standard DR method (UMAP or t-SNE) to obtain 2D embeddings used as training targets.
  2. Anchor selection: Choose representative anchor points near class centroids in projection space (5 per class).
  3. Perturbation generation: Add isotropic Gaussian noise $\varepsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2 I_d)$ to each anchor, where $\sigma = r/\sqrt{d}$ is set using the 0.25th percentile of pairwise distances. Generate $N=2000$ perturbed samples per anchor.
  4. Metric computation: Project all perturbed samples through the trained MLP and compute $D_{\mathrm{dev}}$ (mean displacement), $D_{\mathrm{bias}}$ (systematic drift), and $E_{\mathrm{NA}}$ (nearest-anchor assignment error).
  5. Visual diagnostics: Render anchor lines, local PCA ellipsoids, and Voronoi assignment maps for spatial inspection.

The MLP architectures are trained with MSE loss to approximate the base projection: MLP-small (3 hidden layers, 512 units) and MLP-large (6 hidden layers, 1024 units), both with ReLU activations, Adam optimizer, batch size 256, learning rate $10^{-3}$, and early stopping (patience=10). Jacobian regularization adds $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{Jac}} = \lambda \|J_f(x)\|_F^2$ to the loss ($\lambda=10$).

Results & Highlights

  • Jacobian regularization is highly effective: On MNIST (UMAP), MLP-small+J reduces $D_{\mathrm{dev}}$ from 1.80 to 0.265 (-85%) and $E_{\mathrm{NA}}$ from 0.294 to 0.006. On MNIST (t-SNE), displacement drops from 24.8 to 5.03 (-80%).
  • Systematic bias detected: For unregularized MLP-small on MNIST, mean displacement nearly equals displacement bias (1.80 vs. 1.76 for UMAP; 24.8 vs. 24.4 for t-SNE), indicating near-deterministic drift rather than isotropic noise spread -- a surprising finding.
  • Regularization outperforms capacity: Increasing network size from small to large provides moderate stability gains, but Jacobian regularization yields substantially larger improvements at lower computational cost.
  • No stability-quality tradeoff: MLP-large+J achieves the best MSE (0.668 on MNIST/UMAP) while simultaneously being the most stable.
  • Standard metrics are blind: Trustworthiness and Continuity remain within narrow bands (T: 0.850--0.931, C: 0.864--0.944) across all methods, completely missing stability differences exceeding 80%.
  • Computationally cheap: Total wall-clock per model is ~135 ms, dominated by MLP forward passes.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: Moderate. The idea of probing parametric projections with Gaussian noise and measuring displacement is straightforward. The three metrics are natural and not technically novel. The primary contribution is the systematic application and the visual diagnostic toolkit, which fills a genuine gap.

Rigor: Reasonable but limited by scope. The paper evaluates only two datasets (MNIST, Fashion-MNIST), two base DR methods, and MLP architectures only. Only one noise level per dataset is tested. The anchor selection strategy is limited to class centroids. The 10-run averaging with different seeds is a strength. The link between Jacobian regularization and displacement reduction is somewhat tautological -- regularizing the Jacobian should reduce displacement by construction, which the authors acknowledge.

Reproducibility: Good. Code is shared, hyperparameters are specified, and the framework is clearly described. The use of standard datasets and architectures facilitates replication.

Significance: Useful for the parametric DR and visual analytics communities but somewhat niche. The finding that standard quality metrics miss instability is important and actionable. The visual diagnostics are a practical contribution.

Red flags: (1) The circularity of using Jacobian regularization to reduce displacement then claiming the framework "validates" this -- the regularization directly penalizes the Jacobian, so reduced displacement is mathematically expected, not an empirical discovery. (2) Only one noise level ($\sigma$) per dataset is evaluated, limiting generalizability. (3) The paper is short (6 pages, workshop paper), so depth of analysis is limited.

Strengths

  • Fills a genuine gap: standard quality metrics for parametric projections do not capture local sensitivity to perturbations.
  • Clean, well-structured framework with clear metrics and intuitive visualizations.
  • Strong empirical demonstration that Jacobian regularization is effective and does not harm reconstruction quality.
  • Identifies a surprising systematic bias phenomenon (displacement nearly entirely directional rather than isotropic).
  • Computationally efficient metrics (sub-second per model).
  • Honest discussion of limitations, including the tautological nature of Jacobian regularization results.

Limitations

  • Only two relatively simple image datasets (MNIST, Fashion-MNIST) are tested; generalization to higher-dimensional, more complex data is unclear.
  • Only one noise level per dataset is evaluated; a sweep over $\sigma$ would provide a more complete stability profile.
  • Isotropic Gaussian noise may not reflect realistic perturbation distributions for image or tabular data.
  • Only MLP architectures are tested; extension to CNNs, autoencoders, or other parametric DR variants is not explored.
  • Only class-centroid-based anchors are used; other selection strategies are not analyzed.
  • The paper does not disentangle neural-network-induced from DR-method-induced instability effects.
  • As a 6-page workshop paper, the depth of analysis and breadth of experiments are inherently limited.

Relevance

本文提出的稳定性评估框架对使用参数化降维方法（如参数化UMAP/t-SNE）进行高维数据可视化的研究具有直接参考价值，特别是在需要可信赖投影的交互式数据探索场景中。其关于Jacobian正则化的实证结论也为构建鲁棒的数据可视化系统提供了实用指导。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does the framework generalize to higher-dimensional data (e.g., single-cell RNA-seq with thousands of dimensions) where the noise radius $r$ may interact differently with data geometry?
  • Would non-isotropic, class-conditional, or adversarial perturbation strategies reveal different or more severe instability patterns?
  • Can the stability metrics be incorporated directly into the training objective (beyond Jacobian regularization) to jointly optimize for reconstruction fidelity and local stability?
  • How do the findings transfer to autoencoder-based parametric projections, which have a different loss landscape and capacity profile?
  • What is the full stability profile across a sweep of $\sigma$ values -- is instability monotonic, or are there critical noise thresholds?
