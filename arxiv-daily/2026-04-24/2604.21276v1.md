> **一句话总结：** 本文首次系统研究了LLM解码器对语音识别公平性的影响，在9个模型、5个人口统计轴和12种声学退化条件下（共216次推理实验）发现：LLM解码器不会放大种族偏见（Granite-8B种族公平性最佳），音频编码器设计而非LLM规模才是公平语音识别的主要杠杆，且高压缩音频编码会在退化条件下重新引入病理性的重复循环。

> **TL;DR:** The first systematic study isolating how language model integration depth affects ASR fairness. Evaluates 9 models across 3 architectural generations (CTC, encoder-decoder with implicit LM, LLM-based with explicit pretrained decoder) on ~43K utterances, 5 demographic axes, and 12 acoustic degradation conditions (216 total inference runs). Key findings: (1) LLM decoders do NOT amplify racial bias -- Granite-8B achieves best ethnicity fairness (max/min WER ratio = 2.28); (2) Whisper exhibits pathological hallucination on Indian-accented speech (9.62% insertion rate at large-v3); (3) audio compression level predicts accent fairness more than LLM scale; (4) severe degradation paradoxically compresses fairness gaps, but silence injection amplifies Whisper's accent bias up to 4.64x; (5) under masking, Whisper produces catastrophic repetition loops (86% of insertions) while explicit-LLM decoders produce 38x fewer with near-zero repetition. Audio encoder design, not LLM scaling, is the primary lever for equitable ASR.

## 核心贡献

- **首次系统研究LLM解码器对ASR公平性的影响**：横跨三个架构世代（CTC无语言模型、编码器-解码器隐式语言模型、LLM显式预训练解码器），在5个人口统计维度和12种退化条件下进行全面评估，共216次推理实验
- **颠覆性发现：LLM解码器不放大种族偏见**：Granite-8B（Gen 3）种族公平性最佳（MMR=2.28），优于所有Whisper模型（MMR 3.13-4.04），反驳了"预训练LLM先验会放大偏见"的假设
- **揭示Whisper在印度口音上的病理性幻觉**：Whisper-large-v3的印度口音插入率达9.62%（非单调），而所有Gen 3模型保持在3.1%以下
- **证明音频压缩是公平性的关键因素**：低压缩Qwen3-1.7B口音MMR=1.82，高压缩Granite-2B口音MMR=3.30，音频编码器设计比LLM规模更重要
- **发现退化条件下幻觉类型的架构依赖性**：Whisper在masking下86%为重复循环，而LLM解码器产生38倍更少的插入且近乎零重复；高压缩Q-former会重新引入重复病理

## Key Contributions

- **First systematic study of LLM decoder effects on ASR fairness** across 3 architectural generations, 5 demographic axes, 12 degradation conditions, and 216 total inference runs
- **Evidence that LLM decoders do NOT amplify racial bias**: Granite-8B (explicit LLM decoder) achieves best ethnicity fairness (MMR = 2.28), outperforming all Whisper models (MMR 3.13-4.04)
- **Identification of Whisper's pathological hallucination on Indian-accented speech**: Non-monotonic insertion-rate spike to 9.62% at large-v3, while all Gen 3 models remain below 3.1% on identical utterances
- **Demonstration that audio compression predicts accent fairness more than LLM scale**: Low-compression Qwen3-1.7B (accent MMR = 1.82) vastly outperforms high-compression Granite-2B (accent MMR = 3.30) at comparable parameter count
- **Finding that severe degradation paradoxically compresses fairness gaps** while silence injection amplifies Whisper's accent bias up to 4.64x via demographic-selective hallucination
- **Discovery that hallucination type under degradation is architecture-dependent**: High audio compression (Q-former) reintroduces pathological repetition even in LLM decoders, identifying audio encoder architecture as the primary lever for equitable recognition

## Methodology

The study evaluates **nine open-weight ASR models** grouped into three architectural generations:

1. **Generation 1 (No LM):** Wav2Vec2-large (317M) -- CTC encoder mapping audio frames directly to characters
2. **Generation 2 (Implicit LM):** Whisper-small (244M), Whisper-medium (764M), Whisper-large-v3 (1.5B) -- encoder-decoder transformers with autoregressive decoders learning implicit LMs from 680K hours of paired transcriptions
3. **Generation 3 (Explicit LLM):** Qwen3-ASR-0.6B/1.7B (low compression, direct audio-token projection), Canary-Qwen-2.5B (medium compression via FastConformer), Granite-Speech-2B/8B (high compression via Q-former bottleneck)

**Datasets:** ~43,000 utterances from Common Voice 24 (16,400 crowd-sourced read-speech utterances with self-reported accent, gender, age), Meta's Fair-Speech (26,470 controlled-prompt utterances with ethnicity, gender, age, L1, SES), and LibriSpeech test-clean as reference baseline. Fair-Speech eliminates vocabulary confounds since all speakers read the same prompted sentences.

**Metrics:** Word Error Rate (WER) as primary accuracy measure; Max/Min Ratio (MMR) as fairness metric (scale-invariant); error decomposition into substitutions, insertions, and deletions; fairness gap amplification ratio (alpha = MMR_pert / MMR_clean); insertion classification into repetition (autoregressive loops), syntactic (function words), and content (semantic fabrications).

**Perturbation design:** Four perturbation types at three severity levels each: additive noise (SNR 20/10/0 dB), reverberation (RT60 0.3/0.6/1.0 s), silence injection (25/50/75% duration), and chunk masking (10/20/30% audio zeroed). Total: 9 models x 12 conditions x 2 datasets = 216 inference runs. All models use greedy decoding with identical text normalization.

## Results & Highlights

**Clean audio findings:**
- Black/African-American speakers have the highest WER across all 9 models regardless of generation or architecture, statistically significant for 8/9 models
- Qwen3-1.7B achieves best overall WER on Fair-Speech (4.73%) with competitive ethnicity fairness (MMR = 3.03)
- Whisper-large-v3's Indian-accent WER (19.0%) is paradoxically worse than Whisper-small's (17.6%) due to hallucination
- At comparable ~2B scale: low-compression Qwen3-1.7B (accent MMR = 1.82) >> medium-compression Canary-2.5B (2.00) >> high-compression Granite-2B (3.30)
- A "measurement paradox": Qwen3-1.7B has the lowest absolute Black/AA WER (8.45%) yet the highest relative gap (+203%) because its White WER is extremely low (2.79%)

**Under acoustic degradation:**
- Severe masking compresses fairness gaps for all models (alpha = 0.36-0.79), echoing "low-accuracy parity"
- Silence injection amplifies Whisper-small's accent bias by 4.64x (MMR: 3.23 -> 15.0) via demographic-selective hallucination
- Under 30% masking, Whisper-small produces 51,797 insertions (86% repetition loops) vs. Qwen3-1.7B's 1,353 insertions (3% repetition) -- 38x fewer
- Granite (high-compression Q-former) shows 49-57% repetition under masking, reintroducing Whisper-like pathology
- Perturbation redistributes decoder bias across demographic groups rather than uniformly amplifying it

**Practical recommendations:** Qwen3-1.7B is the strongest overall candidate (accuracy, accent fairness, robustness); Granite-8B is preferable for ethnicity fairness; Whisper-large-v3 should not be deployed on accented speech without hallucination mitigation.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. This is the first study to systematically isolate how the degree of language model integration affects ASR fairness, and the first comprehensive multi-model benchmark on Fair-Speech. The connection between audio compression level and both fairness and hallucination type is a genuinely novel finding.

**Rigor:** Strong. The use of controlled-prompt Fair-Speech dataset to eliminate vocabulary confounds is a methodological strength. Bootstrap confidence intervals (200 resamples) with non-overlapping CIs provide statistical grounding. The 216-run experimental matrix is comprehensive. Error decomposition into insertion types (repetition/syntactic/content) provides mechanistic insight beyond aggregate metrics.

**Reproducibility:** Good. All model weights are public on HuggingFace. Inference parameters, text normalization, and audio preprocessing are fully specified (Appendix H). Perturbations are generated offline with reproducible parameters. Code promised upon publication. The main limitation is that some models (Whisper) have opaque training data.

**Significance:** High. The finding that audio encoder design matters more than LLM scale for fairness has immediate practical implications for ASR system design. The identification of Whisper's accent-selective hallucination pathology is actionable for deployed systems.

**Potential concerns:**
- Training data is a confound that cannot be fully disentangled from decoder architecture effects (acknowledged by authors)
- Common Voice and LibriSpeech texts may overlap with LLM pretraining corpora, though the authors argue this is unlikely to drive the observed group-specific patterns
- The Granite-8B's higher LibriSpeech WER than Granite-2B is a red flag (likely a verbose generation artifact) that complicates interpretation
- The study is limited to English read/prompted speech; generalization to multilingual or spontaneous ASR is unknown
- The MMR metric can be unstable when the minimum-group WER is very low, inflating ratios (acknowledged in the "measurement paradox" discussion)

## Strengths

- Comprehensive experimental design spanning 3 generations, 9 models, 5 demographic axes, and 12 degradation conditions
- Use of Fair-Speech's controlled-prompt design to eliminate vocabulary confounds -- a key methodological innovation for fairness evaluation
- Mechanistic error decomposition (insertion types) that goes beyond aggregate WER to explain WHY models fail differently for different groups
- Surprising and counterintuitive findings (LLM decoders do NOT amplify racial bias; scaling worsens Whisper's accent fairness) that challenge prevailing assumptions
- Clear practical recommendations tied to specific deployment scenarios
- Honest discussion of limitations including the "low-accuracy parity" measurement paradox

## Limitations

- **English-only evaluation**: Results may not generalize to multilingual or code-switching scenarios
- **Read/prompted speech only**: Spontaneous conversational speech may exhibit different fairness dynamics
- **Synthetic perturbations**: Applied in isolation; real-world degradation is typically multi-factorial
- **Training data confound**: Cannot fully disentangle decoder architecture effects from training corpus differences
- **Potential test-set contamination**: Common Voice and LibriSpeech sentences may appear in LLM pretraining data
- **MMR metric instability**: Can be misleading when minimum-group WER approaches zero, as acknowledged by the authors' "measurement paradox"
- **Limited demographic intersectionality**: Each axis analyzed independently; intersectional effects (e.g., Black female speakers) are not systematically studied

## Relevance

本文对关注ASR公平性和鲁棒性的研究者具有重要参考价值。特别是"音频编码器设计比LLM规模更重要"的结论，为构建公平语音识别系统提供了明确的工程指导；同时揭示了Whisper在印度口音上的病理性幻觉问题，对实际部署中的风险评估具有直接意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How do these fairness patterns manifest in multilingual ASR systems, particularly for low-resource languages where LLM priors may be weaker?
2. Can the observed relationship between audio compression level and fairness be quantified into a predictive model that guides encoder architecture choices?
3. Do the findings hold for spontaneous conversational speech with disfluencies, overlapping speech, and code-switching?
4. What intersectional effects emerge when multiple demographic axes are considered jointly (e.g., Black female speakers, older non-native speakers)?
5. Can targeted fine-tuning on underrepresented accent/ethnicity groups mitigate the identified biases without degrading performance on majority groups, and how does this interact with the audio compression bottleneck?
