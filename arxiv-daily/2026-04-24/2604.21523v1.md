> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Focus 基准，通过在 I2T 和 T2I 任务上引入 40 种目标扰动（共 4000+ 实例）系统测试 4 个前沿 VLM 评估器的可靠性，发现当前评估器存在严重盲区——单答案评分有时检测失败率超 50%，且推理预算增加并不能持续改善评估表现。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces Focus, a meta-evaluation benchmark with 4000+ perturbed instances across 40 error dimensions for both image-to-text (I2T) and text-to-image (T2I) tasks. Testing 4 frontier VLMs (Gemini-3.1-Pro, GPT-5.4, Claude-Opus-4.6, Qwen3.5-397B) under single-answer scoring, pairwise comparison, and reference-guided paradigms reveals that evaluator VLMs fail to detect quality-degrading perturbations in over 50% of cases under single scoring, struggle with fine-grained compositional/spatial errors and hallucinated content, and that pairwise comparison with structured strategies is the most reliable paradigm. Increasing reasoning budgets does not consistently help and sometimes hurts.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了 **Focus** 元评估基准，覆盖 I2T 和 T2I 两大任务，包含 40 种扰动维度、4000+ 经过人工验证的扰动实例，系统性地测试评估器 VLM 的可靠性
- 设计了 4 大 I2T 扰动类别（视觉定位、语义解释、视觉推理、长文本生成）和 4 大 T2I 扰动类别（视觉保真度、场景一致性、物理合理性、文字渲染），覆盖了 VLM 和图像生成模型常见的失败模式
- 在三种评估范式（单答案评分、成对比较、参考引导评分）和四种提示策略（Vanilla、Rubric、Axes、Axes+Rubric）下系统评估了 4 个前沿 VLM
- 发现评估器在评估结果和文本解释之间存在"识别但不惩罚"的割裂现象，即评估器在解释中指出了错误但未能反映在最终分数中
- 提供了实用的评估器使用建议：优先使用带结构化策略的成对比较，推理预算不宜过高，通用排行榜排名不能可靠预测评估能力

## Key Contributions

- Introduce **Focus**, a comprehensive meta-evaluation benchmark with 4000+ human-validated perturbed instances spanning 40 perturbation dimensions across I2T and T2I tasks
- Design a principled perturbation taxonomy: 4 I2T categories (Visual Grounding, Semantic Interpretation, Visual Reasoning, Long-form Generation) and 4 T2I categories (Visual Fidelity, Scene Coherence, Physical Plausibility, Text Rendering)
- Systematically evaluate 4 frontier VLMs (Gemini-3.1-Pro, GPT-5.4, Claude-Opus-4.6, Qwen3.5-397B) under 3 evaluation paradigms and 4 prompting strategies, using an automated perturbation pipeline followed by human verification
- Identify a critical gap between error recognition in justifications and reflection in final scores -- evaluators sometimes describe errors in text but fail to penalize them numerically
- Provide actionable recommendations for practitioners: prefer pairwise comparison with Axes or Axes+Rules strategies; moderate reasoning budgets are sufficient; validate evaluators empirically on target tasks rather than relying on general leaderboards

## Methodology

The paper constructs the Focus benchmark through a rigorous human-in-the-loop pipeline:

1. **Prompt Selection**: 600 I2T instances (image + text prompt) from 7 benchmarks (MMBench, MMDocBench, RealWorldQA, etc.) and 750 T2I instances (text prompts) from 7 benchmarks (MJ-Bench, T2I-CompBench++, T2I-ReasonBench, etc.).

2. **Gold Response Generation**: Gemini-3.1-Pro generates gold text answers for I2T; Gemini-3-Pro-Image generates gold images for T2I. All gold outputs are manually reviewed.

3. **Perturbation Generation**: For I2T, Gemini-3.1-Pro produces perturbed answers guided by perturbation-specific instructions. For T2I, a two-step pipeline first generates edit instructions, then Gemini-3-Pro-Image edits the gold image. All perturbations are human-validated using a custom annotation tool (PerturbVal), labeled as valid, invalid, or score-invariant.

4. **Evaluation Setup**: Three paradigms are tested:
   - **Single-answer scoring**: Evaluator scores one response independently
   - **Pairwise comparison**: Evaluator selects the better of two candidates (with position-swapping to mitigate bias)
   - **Reference-guided scoring**: Evaluator scores against a gold reference

   Each paradigm uses four progressively structured prompting strategies: Vanilla (V), Rubric/Rules (R), Axes (Ax), and Axes+Rubric/Rules (Ax+R).

5. **Metrics**: For single scoring, the percentage of instances where scores remain unchanged after perturbation. For pairwise, the percentage where the evaluator fails to select the gold output. For reference-guided, the percentage of perfect scores assigned to perturbed outputs. Score-invariant perturbations serve as a control for false positive sensitivity.

## Results & Highlights

- **Pairwise comparison is the most reliable paradigm**, consistently outperforming single-answer scoring across both I2T and T2I. With Axes or Axes+Rules strategies, pairwise failure rates drop to ~12-14% on I2T and ~18-25% on T2I.
- **Single-answer scoring is the weakest**, with failure rates exceeding 50% for some model-strategy combinations on T2I. This is alarming given its widespread use in practice.
- **Gemini-3.1-Pro is the best evaluator overall**, especially in pairwise comparison where it achieves the lowest failure rates. Claude-Opus-4.6, despite strong general leaderboard rankings, shows relatively higher failure rates.
- **T2I evaluation is harder than I2T**, suggesting current evaluators struggle with fine-grained visual understanding in generated images.
- **Physical Plausibility is the hardest T2I category**, requiring deeper reasoning about physics and common sense. Scene Coherence is the easiest because inconsistencies are visually obvious.
- **Increasing reasoning budgets does not consistently help** and sometimes hurts performance, particularly in pairwise comparison where low/medium reasoning outperforms high reasoning.
- **Justifications reveal only marginal improvements**: Evaluators sometimes identify errors in text but fail to reflect them in scores, especially in single-answer scoring. The gap is smaller in pairwise comparison.
- **Score-invariant perturbations**: Pairwise comparison is the least stable on these, as evaluators tend to pick a winner even when both responses are equally good.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: The perturbation-based meta-evaluation approach is adapted from the text-only FBI framework (Doddapaneni et al., 2024) and Checklist (Ribeiro et al., 2020). The primary novelty lies in extending this methodology to the multimodal setting with co-designed I2T and T2I perturbation taxonomies. The perturbation categories are well-grounded in reported VLM and image generation failure modes. However, the overall framework structure (perturbation + human validation + multi-paradigm evaluation) closely follows the established playbook.

**Rigor**: The human-in-the-loop validation with a custom annotation tool is a strength. The study covers 3 paradigms x 4 strategies x 4 models, providing a thorough sweep. The use of identical prompts across models and temperature=0 ensures fair comparison. The score-invariant control condition is a good methodological choice. However, the gold responses are generated by Gemini models, and the perturbation pipeline also uses Gemini, creating a potential dependency on a single model family for data construction.

**Reproducibility**: Code and data are publicly available. Evaluation prompts are provided in full in the appendix. Temperature is set to 0 where supported. The human annotation process is described in detail. Reproducibility should be strong.

**Significance**: The findings are practically important given the widespread use of VLM-as-a-judge in benchmarking and as reward models during training. The identification of systematic blind spots -- particularly the high failure rates in single-answer scoring and the disconnect between justifications and scores -- should influence how practitioners deploy VLM evaluators.

**Red flags**:
- The benchmark tests whether evaluators can detect *known* perturbation types, but does not measure calibration or correlation with human quality judgments more broadly.
- Gold responses are generated by Gemini, which is also the best-performing evaluator. This could introduce subtle biases (e.g., the evaluator may be better at recognizing its own generation patterns).
- The paper evaluates 4 frontier models but the landscape moves quickly; results may not generalize to future models.
- The justification analysis (Section 4.7) uses Gemini-2.5-flash to analyze Gemini's own justifications, which introduces circularity.

## Strengths

- Comprehensive scope: 40 perturbation dimensions, 3 evaluation paradigms, 4 prompting strategies, 4 frontier models, both I2T and T2I tasks
- Well-designed perturbation taxonomy grounded in documented failure modes
- Human-in-the-loop validation ensures perturbation quality
- Score-invariant perturbations provide a useful control for false positive sensitivity
- Clear, actionable recommendations for practitioners
- Publicly available benchmark enables future research

## Limitations

- Does not establish correlation with human judgments of quality; perturbation detection is a necessary but not sufficient condition for evaluation reliability
- Gold responses and perturbations are Gemini-generated, creating a single-model dependency in data construction
- T2I perturbations rely on Gemini-3-Pro-Image editing, which may introduce its own artifacts or biases
- Limited to English-language prompts and responses
- Does not explore whether training on similar perturbation types can improve evaluator robustness
- The justification analysis uses another model to evaluate justifications, introducing potential circularity

## Relevance

本文对关注 VLM 评估可靠性和 VLM-as-a-judge 方法的研究者具有重要参考价值。如果你使用 VLM 作为自动评估器或奖励模型（如 RLHF 训练），本文揭示了当前方法在细粒度视觉定位、空间推理和幻觉检测方面的系统性盲区，建议优先使用带结构化策略的成对比较范式。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can evaluator VLMs be fine-tuned on perturbation-detection data to improve their reliability, or do the blind spots reflect fundamental limitations in visual understanding?
- How do the findings generalize to other modalities (video, audio) or to interleaved multimodal generation?
- What is the relationship between perturbation detection ability and downstream impact when VLMs are used as reward models in RLHF or DPO training?
- Could an ensemble of multiple evaluator VLMs compensate for individual blind spots, or do the errors systematically correlate?
- How stable are these findings over time as models improve -- will future VLMs close these gaps or will new blind spots emerge?
