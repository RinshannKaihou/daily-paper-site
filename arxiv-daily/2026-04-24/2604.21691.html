There Will Be a Scientific Theory of Deep Learning

arXiv: 2604.21691v1

Date: 2026-04-24

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21691v1

一句话总结

这篇由十四位理论研究者联合撰写的立场论文系统论证了深度学习正在形成一门类似物理学的"学习力学"科学，通过五条证据线索（可解模型、极限简化、经验定律、超参数研究、普适现象）展示了该领域已具备建立严谨科学理论的条件，并呼吁理论研究者与可解释性研究者协同推进。

TL;DR

This position paper by 14 deep learning theorists argues that a rigorous scientific theory of deep learning -- which they propose calling "Learning Mechanics" -- is not only possible but actively emerging. The authors present five converging lines of evidence: (1) analytically solvable settings (deep linear networks, NTK linearization) that provide exact solutions to simplified learning problems; (2) simplifying limits (infinite width/depth) that reveal a lazy vs. rich learning dichotomy controlled by output scaling; (3) empirical laws (neural scaling laws, edge of stability) that demonstrate predictable regularities; (4) hyperparameter transfer theory (muP) that enables practical prediction of optimal settings; and (5) universal phenomena (neural collapse, Platonic representations) observed across architectures and tasks. The paper also articulates a vision for symbiosis between learning mechanics and mechanistic interpretability -- where mechanics provides mathematical foundations and interpretability supplies empirical phenomena needing explanation. Ten open directions and practical advice for newcomers are included. Whether you find the argument persuasive depends on whether you believe physics-style reductionism can capture the complexity of real-world training on natural data, but the paper is the most comprehensive synthesis of theoretical deep learning progress to date.

  4. 提出十个开放研究方向：包括非线性可解模型、数据结构理论、特征学习定义、完美训练理论等，为领域未来发展指明路径。
  5. 为初学者提供入门指南：降低理论研究的进入门槛，鼓励更多研究者参与这一科学项目。

Key Contributions

  1. Articulates "Learning Mechanics" as a unified scientific framework: Consolidates disparate threads of deep learning theory into a coherent scientific project, analogous to classical mechanics in physics, with seven explicit desiderata (fundamental, mathematical, predictive, comprehensive, intuitive, useful, humble).
  1. Synthesizes five converging lines of evidence: (i) analytically solvable settings including deep linear networks and NTK linearization; (ii) simplifying limits revealing the lazy vs. rich dichotomy at infinite width/depth; (iii) empirical laws including neural scaling laws and the edge-of-stability phenomenon; (iv) hyperparameter transfer via muP/Tensor Programs; (v) universal phenomena such as neural collapse and the Platonic representation hypothesis.
  1. Proposes a symbiosis between learning mechanics and mechanistic interpretability: Learning mechanics can formalize core interpretability assumptions (linear representability, locality, sparsity, compositionality) and explain how mechanisms develop during training. In return, mechanistic interpretability provides concrete empirical phenomena that demand first-principles explanations.
  1. Charts ten open research directions: Nonlinear tractable models, a theory of data structure, definitions of feature learning, perfect training theories, conservation laws and Noether principles, implicit regularization, predicting scaling law exponents, sharpness dynamics beyond full-batch, the Discretization Hypothesis, and the nature of universality in learned representations.
  1. Provides a practical on-ramp for new researchers: Includes introductory resources and advice aimed at lowering barriers to entry for the theoretical deep learning community.

Methodology

This is a position/synthesis paper rather than an experimental contribution. The methodology is primarily conceptual and argumentative:

  • Literature synthesis: The authors survey and organize a vast body of existing theoretical work spanning NTK, mean-field theory, scaling laws, muP, neural collapse, and more, into a unified narrative with explicit analogies to the historical development of physics.
  • Five-pillar argument structure: Each of the five evidence strands is presented with concrete examples, key references, and analogies to how similar evidence patterns appeared in the development of classical mechanics, statistical mechanics, and quantum mechanics.
  • Table of analogies: A summary table (Table 1) maps deep learning concepts to their physics counterparts (e.g., solvable settings ~ classical mechanics, limits ~ thermodynamic limit, empirical laws ~ empirical gas laws, hyperparameter transfer ~ dimensional analysis).
  • Critical engagement with skepticism: Section 4 directly addresses common objections, including the argument that deep learning is "just engineering," that theory has not been useful, and that language data is too complex for theory.

Results & Highlights

  • The lazy/rich dichotomy is now well understood: Infinite-width networks exhibit fundamentally different training regimes depending on output scaling. Scaling final-layer weights by width^(-1/2) yields lazy/NTK dynamics (no feature learning), while scaling by width^(-1) yields rich dynamics with genuine feature learning. This dichotomy persists at finite width.
  • Neural scaling laws are remarkably robust: Test loss follows power laws in compute, data size, and parameter count across architectural families, yet no existing theory can robustly predict the exponents a priori.
  • Edge of stability is a universal phenomenon: Full-batch gradient descent with learning rate eta causes sharpness (top Hessian eigenvalue) to plateau at 2/eta across architectures, now partially explained by implicit curvature regularization.
  • muP enables practical hyperparameter transfer: Maximal Update Parameterization allows predicting optimal learning rates for large models by training small proxy models, a direct practical payoff from theoretical analysis.
  • The Platonic representation hypothesis is emerging: Different architectures trained on natural data converge to similar internal representations, suggesting universal structure in learned features.
  • The analogy to physics is substantive, not merely rhetorical: The authors draw specific parallels -- solvable settings correspond to classical mechanics, statistical mechanics describes the thermodynamic limit, and conservation laws (analogous to Noether's theorem) appear in training dynamics.

Strengths

  • Comprehensive synthesis: The most thorough single-document overview of the theoretical deep learning landscape, organized into a coherent narrative with clear signposting.
  • Productive physics analogy: The mapping between deep learning theory tools and historical physics development (Table 1) is genuinely illuminating and provides useful intuition.
  • Practical relevance: The paper does not retreat into abstraction; muP, scaling laws, and edge-of-stability findings all have direct practical implications.
  • The symbiosis vision is well-argued: The case for learning mechanics and mechanistic interpretability as complementary (analogous to physics and biology) is the most compelling section of the paper.
  • Accessible writing: The paper is written for a broad audience and serves as an excellent entry point for researchers new to theoretical deep learning.

Limitations

  • The "theory" discussed is mostly about optimization, not about what models actually learn: The five evidence strands primarily address training dynamics and loss landscape properties, with limited insight into why trained models exhibit the capabilities they do.
  • Toy setting bias: Most "solvable" settings (deep linear networks, teacher-student models, Gaussian data) are far from the complexity of real-world training on language or vision data. The gap between theory and practice remains large.
  • No novel theoretical contribution: This is purely a synthesis. Readers seeking new results will not find them here.
  • Insufficient engagement with skeptical perspectives: Section 4 addresses skeptics but does so from within the authors' own framework. The deeper objection -- that the physics analogy fundamentally mischaracterizes the nature of the problem -- is acknowledged but not fully confronted.
  • Author demographics: All 14 authors are from the theoretical ML community. The perspective of practitioners, applied researchers, or engineers who might find this theory irrelevant to their daily work is absent.

Relevance

本文对关注深度学习理论基础的研究者具有高度参考价值，尤其是从事NTK、标度律、超参数迁移或机制可解释性研究的学者。文中提出的"学习力学"框架和十个开放方向为理论社区提供了清晰的研究议程。对于工业界从事大模型训练的工程师，muP和标度律等实用成果已产生直接影响。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can the lazy/rich dichotomy be extended beyond infinite-width limits to provide a continuous characterization of learning regimes at finite width and depth?
  2. What theoretical framework can predict neural scaling law exponents a priori from dataset and architectural properties, without fitting to empirical data?
  3. How should "feature learning" be formally defined in a way that is both mathematically precise and captures the practical capabilities unlocked by moving beyond the kernel regime?
  4. Are the core assumptions of mechanistic interpretability (linear representability, locality, sparsity, compositionality) provable consequences of specific training dynamics and data structures, or are they merely useful approximations?
  5. Can the physics analogy be made truly predictive rather than merely explanatory -- i.e., can "learning mechanics" predict a novel phenomenon that is subsequently confirmed experimentally?
  6. What is the minimal theory of natural data distributions that would enable quantitative predictions about the behavior of models trained on language or vision data?
  7. How does the symbiosis between learning mechanics and mechanistic interpretability work in practice -- what concrete collaborative projects would advance both fields simultaneously?
