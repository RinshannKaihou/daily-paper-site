Cross-Entropy Is Load-Bearing: A Pre-Registered Scope Test of the K-Way Energy Probe on Bidirectional Predictive Coding

arXiv: 2604.21286v1

Date: 2026-04-23

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21286v1

一句话总结： 这篇预注册研究通过系统移除交叉熵损失，发现CE是K-way能量探针近似分解为softmax的关键支撑组件——移除CE后探针性能显著提升，且CE对探针-softmax差距的贡献可分解为66%的logit尺度膨胀效应和34%的尺度不变排序优势。
TL;DR: This pre-registered study shows that cross-entropy (CE) loss is the major load-bearing component in the approximate decomposition of the K-way energy probe into softmax on discriminative predictive coding networks. Replacing CE with MSE halves the probe-softmax AUROC gap; bidirectional PC (also CE-free) lets the probe slightly exceed softmax. A temperature-scaling ablation decomposes the gap: ~66% comes from CE-driven logit-scale inflation and ~34% from a scale-invariant ranking advantage of CE-trained representations. The study is rigorous (pre-registered, 10 seeds, paired design) but limited to TinyConv on CIFAR-10, where the bidirectional dynamics manipulation check failed.

核心贡献

  • 通过预注册实验设计，系统性地验证了CE损失在K-way能量探针近似分解（Cacioli 2026提出的M_k ≈ [log-softmax margin] + residual）中的关键作用
  • 发现移除CE（替换为MSE）使探针-softmax差距缩小54%（从Δ=-0.082降至Δ=-0.037），双向PC条件下探针甚至首次超越softmax（Δ=+0.008）
  • 通过温度缩放消融实验，将CE对差距的贡献分解为66%的logit尺度膨胀效应和34%的尺度不变排序优势，揭示了CE不仅人为抬高了softmax基线，还产生了更好的表征排序
  • 指出了先前negative result的正确范围：能量探针在softmax上的劣势应归因于CE驱动的logit膨胀，而非结构化探针的固有缺陷
  • 提出温度缩放softmax应作为CE训练网络上评估替代置信度信号的更公平基线

Key Contributions

  • Pre-registered experimental design (OSF) testing the sensitivity of the K-way energy probe decomposition to CE removal, with three conditions: stdPC-CE, stdPC-MSE, and bidirectional PC (bPC)
  • Demonstrates that CE removal halves the probe-softmax gap (Δ from -0.082 to -0.037), and bPC allows the probe to exceed softmax across all 10 seeds (Δ = +0.008, p = 0.000027)
  • Post-hoc temperature scaling ablation decomposing the gap: ~66% attributable to logit-scale inflation removable by rescaling, ~34% to a scale-invariant ranking advantage of CE-trained softmax
  • Identifies CE-driven logit inflation (15x larger logit norms) as a confound in evaluating structural confidence probes against softmax baselines
  • Proposes temperature-scaled softmax as a fairer baseline for confidence probe evaluation on CE-trained networks

Methodology

The study uses a paired within-seed design with 10 seeds across three conditions on a matched TinyConv backbone (2.1M parameters) on CIFAR-10:

  • Condition A (stdPC-CE): Standard discriminative predictive coding with cross-entropy loss, T=13 inference steps. Replicates the baseline from Cacioli (2026).
  • Condition B (stdPC-MSE): Standard discriminative PC with MSE replacing CE at the output. Isolates assumption A1 (CE at output) from A3 (inference dynamics).
  • Condition C (bPC): Bidirectional PC following Oliviers et al. (2025), with bidirectional MSE energy and no CE. Violates A1, A3, and alters A5 simultaneously. T=32 for training, T=100 for evaluation.

All conditions use AdamW (lr=1e-4, weight decay=1e-4), batch size 128, 25 epochs. The primary metric is AUROC2 (Type-2 AUROC for discriminating correct vs incorrect Type-1 predictions). The K-way energy probe clamps each of K=10 candidate classes at the output, runs inference to settling, and records per-hypothesis total energy. The confidence margin is E_(2) - E_(1). The softmax baseline uses the top-1 minus top-2 softmax probability from the feedforward pathway.

Four pre-registered hypotheses: H3 (manipulation check: bPC latent movement >10x stdPC), H2 (replication: Δ_stdPC < 0), H1 (primary: Δ_bPC > Δ_stdPC), H4 (exploratory: comparing Δ_MSE to Δ_stdPC). The temperature scaling ablation (Section 4.4) is explicitly labelled post-hoc.

Results & Highlights

  • H2 confirmed: The negative result replicates robustly — Δ_stdPC = -0.082, t(9) = -16.0, p < 10^-6, Cohen's d = -5.06. All 10 seeds negative.
  • H1 confirmed with scope limitation: Δ_bPC - Δ_stdPC = +0.090, t(9) = 19.2, p < 10^-7. Δ_bPC = +0.008, with all 10 seeds positive. The probe exceeds softmax on bPC.
  • H3 failed: bPC latent movement ratio vs stdPC was only 1.6x (threshold: 10x). Bidirectional dynamics did not materially manifest at this scale. The generative chain contributed less than 1% of total energy.
  • H4 exploratory: Removing CE alone (MSE condition) reduces the gap by 54% (Δ_MSE = -0.037 vs Δ_stdPC = -0.082). All 10 seeds show improvement.
  • Logit scale finding: CE training produces logit norms ~15x larger than MSE or bPC (~11.5 vs ~0.78).
  • Temperature scaling ablation: Dividing logits by T~15 to match MSE norms closes 66% of the gap. Remaining 34% persists at any temperature, reflecting CE's role as a proper scoring rule inducing better-ranked representations.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. The paper provides a careful, incremental scope test of a prior theoretical result rather than introducing a fundamentally new method. The temperature scaling decomposition is the most novel analytical contribution. The finding that CE inflates softmax baselines is intuitive in retrospect but important to establish empirically.
  • Rigor: High. Pre-registration with explicit hypotheses and decision rules, 10-seed paired design, proper statistical testing (paired t-tests, non-parametric Wilcoxon confirmations, Shapiro-Wilk normality checks), and transparent reporting of failed manipulation checks. The author is commendably honest about limitations.
  • Reproducibility: Strong. Matched backbone, shared seeds, publicly available code, pre-registered protocol, and explicit reporting of all parameters. The single-author, consumer-GP setup suggests the experiments are not computationally prohibitive.
  • Significance: Niche but meaningful within the predictive coding and metacognitive probing literature. The practical implication — that temperature-scaled softmax is a fairer baseline — is broadly applicable to confidence calibration work. However, the results are confined to a small architecture on CIFAR-10.
  • Red flags: (1) The failed manipulation check for H3 substantially weakens the bPC arm of the study — bPC effectively functions as a second CE-removal condition rather than a test of bidirectional dynamics. (2) The absolute magnitude of Δ_bPC is tiny (+0.008), and the practical significance of the probe "exceeding" softmax by this margin is questionable. (3) Single architecture, single dataset, small scale. (4) The generative chain contributing <1% of energy raises questions about whether bPC is even functionally bidirectional at this scale. (5) The author's prior work is the primary reference point, and the paper is somewhat insular in its citation network.

Strengths

  • Exemplary pre-registered methodology with transparent reporting of both positive and negative results
  • Clean experimental design isolating CE removal from dynamics changes via the three-condition structure
  • The temperature scaling ablation provides a mechanistic decomposition of the gap into scale-dependent and scale-invariant components
  • Honest assessment of the failed manipulation check and its implications for interpreting the bPC results
  • Clear scoping of conclusions — the author does not overclaim

Limitations

  • Single architecture (TinyConv, 2.1M parameters) and single dataset (CIFAR-10); generalization to larger models and datasets is unknown
  • The bidirectional dynamics manipulation failed — the study cannot speak to the effect of genuinely iterative bidirectional inference
  • The absolute probe-softmax differences are small (the largest being 0.082 AUROC points), raising questions about practical significance
  • The generative chain contributes <1% of energy at the selected alpha_gen, meaning bPC is effectively unidirectional at this scale
  • The post-hoc temperature scaling analysis, while informative, was not pre-registered and the 66/34 decomposition is an empirical approximation rather than a strict causal separation
  • The study does not test prospective configuration or other non-trivial inference regimes that might have activated the dynamics manipulation

Relevance

本研究对理解神经网络置信度校准和元认知探针的设计具有直接参考价值，特别是揭示了CE损失对softmax置信度基线的人为抬高效应。对于从事不确定性估计或predictive coding研究的工作者来说，温度缩放softmax作为更公平基线的建议值得采纳。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Would the results replicate on deeper architectures (e.g., ResNet-scale PC networks) where the generative chain might contribute more substantially to total energy?
  • How does the probe-softmax gap behave on datasets with higher class counts (e.g., CIFAR-100, ImageNet) or structured label spaces?
  • Can the temperature scaling insight be leveraged to design better structural probes that are inherently robust to logit-scale confounds?
  • What happens in a genuinely bidirectional regime — e.g., prospective configuration (Song et al., 2024) — where latent movement substantially exceeds the feedforward baseline?
  • Is the 66/34 decomposition stable across different CE loss variants (label smoothing, focal loss) or different training regimes?
