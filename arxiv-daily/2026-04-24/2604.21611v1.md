> **一句话总结：** 本文提出Verbal Process Supervision (VPS)，一种无需训练的推理时框架，通过更强的监督模型对推理过程逐步进行结构化自然语言 critiques 来引导弱模型迭代修正，在GPQA Diamond上达到94.9%超越SOTA，在AIME 2025上将弱模型从11.7-26.7%提升至63.3-90.0%，并证明critique的粒度（而非仅仅是语言反馈本身）是推理时scaling的关键变量。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces Verbal Process Supervision (VPS), a training-free inference-time framework where a stronger LLM supervisor provides step-level natural-language critiques to guide a weaker actor through iterative generate-critique-refine loops. VPS surpasses the GPQA Diamond SOTA (94.9% vs. 94.1%), rescues weak actors on AIME 2025 (up to +63.3 pp gain), and outperforms Reflexion by +8.5 to +12.1 points and Self-Consistency@5 by +5.0-8.3 pp at matched compute, establishing critique granularity as a new axis of inference-time scaling. Gains are strongly predicted by supervisor-actor headroom (r=+0.90), but VPS has a principled domain boundary at code synthesis where errors are not linguistically representable.

## 核心贡献

- **提出推理时scaling的第四轴——critique粒度**：在已有的chain depth、sample breadth、learned step-scorers三个轴之外，识别出外部语言监督的粒度作为新的scaling维度，从outcome-level（如Reflexion）到step-level（如VPS）构成完整光谱。
- **形式化Verbal Process Supervision (VPS)框架**：定义了语言空间的actor-critic更新算子，用结构化自然语言critique替代标量reward，支持不动点收敛特性，且完全无需梯度更新或人工标注。
- **在GPQA Diamond上超越SOTA**：GPT-5.4 (High|Low) 配置在R=4时达到94.9%，超过94.1%的已报告SOTA，且无需任何参数更新。
- **弱模型救援效应**：在AIME 2025上，将11.7-26.7%的弱actor提升至63.3-90.0%，最高增益达+63.3个百分点。
- **隔离粒度为关键变量**：在匹配计算量的条件下，VPS在三个benchmark上一致性超越Reflexion +8.5到+12.1个点和SC@5 +5.0-8.3个点，证明粒度本身（而非额外计算或语言反馈的存在）是有效变量。
- **揭示headroom-增益的高相关性**：supervisor-actor能力差距（headroom）与VPS增益的Pearson相关系数达+0.90，为部署提供预测性准则。
- **识别语言监督的领域边界**：在代码合成任务上，由于错误无法被语言化表示，VPS效果受限，激励了混合语言-可执行监督方法。

## Key Contributions

- **Identifies critique granularity as a fourth axis of inference-time scaling**, complementing chain depth, sample breadth, and learned step-scorers (PRMs).
- **Formalizes VPS as a training-free, step-level verbal process supervision framework** with a language-space actor-critic update operator and fixed-point convergence characterization.
- **Achieves new SOTA on GPQA Diamond (94.9%)** with the GPT-5.4 (High|Low) pair at R=4, surpassing 94.1% without gradient updates.
- **Demonstrates dramatic weak-actor rescue on AIME 2025**, lifting actors from 11.7-26.7% to 63.3-90.0% (+up to 63.3 pp absolute gain).
- **Isolates granularity as the operative variable**: at matched compute, VPS outperforms Reflexion by +8.5 to +12.1 pp across all three benchmarks and SC@5 by +5.0-8.3 pp on GPQA and LiveCodeBench.
- **Establishes supervisor-actor headroom as a strong predictor** of VPS benefit (Pearson r=+0.90), providing a practical deployment criterion.
- **Characterizes a principled domain boundary** where verbal supervision fails (code synthesis), motivating hybrid verbal-executable extensions.

## Methodology

VPS operates through an iterative generate-critique-refine loop between two LLMs: a base actor and a (typically stronger) supervisor.

**Core loop (Algorithm 1):**
1. The actor generates an initial reasoning trajectory of T steps given input x.
2. The supervisor evaluates each step and produces a structured natural-language critique, labeling each step as: (a) correct -- do not revise, (b) partially correct -- describe the issue, or (c) incorrect -- give a targeted correction. If all steps are correct, the supervisor outputs CONVERGED.
3. The actor regenerates its trajectory conditioned on the original input, the previous trajectory, and the critique, preserving steps labeled correct.
4. This loop iterates up to a maximum round budget R, or terminates early on convergence.

**Formal framework:** VPS defines a verbal reward function that maps trajectories and vocabulary sequences to structured critiques (rather than scalar rewards). The VPS update operator F performs conditional regeneration: each round produces a refined trajectory that incorporates the supervisor's step-level feedback. Under ideal conditions, iteration converges to a fixed point where the supervisor finds no further errors.

**Key properties:**
- Entirely inference-time: no gradient updates, no training data, no human labels.
- Applicable to black-box API-accessible models.
- Step-level critiques provide dense temporal credit assignment, unlike outcome-level methods (e.g., Reflexion) that issue a single end-of-episode signal.

**Experimental setup:** Evaluated across GPQA Diamond (n=198), AIME 2025 (n=30), and LiveCodeBench V6 with both closed-source (GPT-5.4 family) and open-weight (GLM-5.1, Nemotron-3-Super, Gemma 4 31B, GPT-OSS 120B/20B) supervisor-actor pairs. Round counts R in {1,2,3,4} are ablated. Baselines include Self-Consistency@5 and Reflexion at matched compute budgets.

## Results & Highlights

- **GPQA Diamond SOTA:** GPT-5.4 (High|Low) reaches 94.9% at R=4, exceeding the 94.1% SOTA and the standalone GPT-5.4 baseline of 92.8% by +2.1 pp.
- **AIME 2025 weak-actor rescue:** GPT-5.4 Nano (High|Low) improves from 26.7% to 90.0% at R=3 (+63.3 pp); Gemma 4|GPT-OSS 20B lifts from 11.7% to 70.0% (+58.3 pp); GPT-OSS 120B|20B lifts from 11.7% to 63.3% (+51.6 pp).
- **Matched-compute superiority over Reflexion:** VPS outperforms Reflexion by +8.5 pp (GPQA), +10.0 pp (AIME), and +12.1 pp (LCB), with the only axis of variation being critique granularity (same supervisor used for both).
- **Matched-compute superiority over SC@5:** VPS beats SC@5 by +5.0 pp (GPQA), +1.1 pp (AIME, within variance), and +8.3 pp (LCB).
- **Non-monotonic round-count scaling:** Only 2 of 11 configurations show monotonically non-decreasing accuracy. Optimal R varies: best score appears at R=4 in 6 cases, R=3 in 3, R=2 in 2.
- **Degradation when actor >= supervisor:** GLM-5.1|Nemotron on AIME peaks at 80.0%, over 10 pp below the actor's standalone 90.2%, indicating over-correction from cross-family distribution shift.
- **Headroom-gain correlation:** Supervisor-actor capability gap (headroom) correlates with VPS gain at r=+0.90. H>50 pp implies gain >50 pp; H<0 implies degradation in all observed cases.
- **Code synthesis domain boundary:** On LiveCodeBench, no verbal method (VPS, Reflexion, or SC@5) closes the gap to standalone actors for open-weight pairs, as decisive error signals live in runtime behavior rather than linguistic structure.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate-High):** The idea of using a stronger model to critique a weaker one is not new (Reflexion, CRITIC). The key novelty is formalizing critique *granularity* as a distinct scaling axis and demonstrating that step-level feedback consistently outperforms outcome-level feedback at matched compute. The formalization with a verbal reward function and fixed-point characterization adds theoretical grounding, though the convergence result is idealized and not empirically validated.
- **Rigor (Moderate):** The matched-compute comparison with Reflexion and SC@5 is well-designed, isolating granularity as the operative variable. However, all results are single-run point estimates without error bars or confidence intervals. The author acknowledges this and promises seed-averaged results in the camera-ready, but key claims (e.g., the +2.1 pp GPQA SOTA margin, the +1.1 pp AIME gap over SC@5) may not survive multi-seed evaluation. The AIME n=30 is small, making point estimates noisy.
- **Reproducibility (Moderate):** The algorithm is clearly described (Algorithm 1), and the prompt structures for VPS and Reflexion are detailed in the appendix. However, the experiments rely on closed-source models (GPT-5.4) and specific open-weight models (GLM-5.1, Nemotron-3-Super, Gemma 4 31B) that may not be widely available. The VPS cost is approximately 1.2-1.4x that of SC@5, which is useful for practical assessment.
- **Significance (High):** If the results hold under multi-seed evaluation, the identification of critique granularity as a scaling axis is a meaningful conceptual contribution. The weak-actor rescue regime (up to +63.3 pp) is striking and has practical implications for deploying weaker models with stronger supervision. The headroom-gain correlation (r=+0.90) provides actionable deployment guidance.
- **Red flags:**
  - Single-author paper with no code release mentioned.
  - All results are single-run without statistical significance tests.
  - The GPQA SOTA claim (+2.1 pp over baseline on n=198) is within single-run variance (+/-3.6 pp at 95% CI, as the author notes).
  - The paper uses future model names (GPT-5.4, GLM-5.1, Gemma 4) that are not yet publicly available as of the knowledge cutoff, making independent verification impossible at this time.
  - Reflexion is implemented with a deliberately restricted prompt (no step references), which may disadvantage it relative to a more natural implementation.

## Strengths

- Clean experimental design with matched-compute baselines that isolate the granularity variable.
- Honest reporting of failure modes (6 of 11 configurations where VPS underperforms the actor; code synthesis boundary).
- The headroom analysis (r=+0.90) provides a practical and interpretable deployment criterion.
- The regime taxonomy (rescue, marginal, degradation, domain boundary) is a useful organizing framework.
- The qualitative example in Appendix E effectively illustrates why step-level feedback outperforms outcome-level feedback.
- The framework is training-free and applicable to black-box APIs, lowering adoption barriers.

## Limitations

- **No statistical validation:** All results are single-run point estimates. Key claims rest on narrow margins that may not be robust.
- **Depends on supervisor capability:** VPS is bounded by the supervisor's ability to identify and articulate errors; when the supervisor is not substantially stronger than the actor, VPS can degrade performance.
- **Non-monotonic scaling:** Round-count scaling is unpredictable, with 9 of 11 configurations showing regressions at some round. Adaptive stopping is acknowledged as future work but not implemented.
- **Code synthesis boundary:** Verbal critique is fundamentally limited for tasks where errors are grounded in runtime behavior rather than linguistic structure.
- **Cross-family compatibility:** Supervisor-actor pairs from different model families can introduce distribution shifts that outweigh critique benefits (e.g., GLM-5.1|Nemotron on GPQA: H=+7 pp yet Delta=-18.1 pp).
- **Cost:** VPS at R=4 costs approximately 1.2-1.4x SC@5 in total tokens, which may be significant at scale.
- **English-only evaluation;** generalizability to multilingual settings is unknown.

## Relevance

本文对推理时计算扩展（inference-time scaling）提出了新的视角，将critique粒度形式化为与chain depth、sample breadth并列的scaling轴。对于关注LLM推理能力提升、test-time compute优化、以及如何利用强模型引导弱模型的研究者具有直接参考价值。headroom-增益的量化分析为实际部署supervisor-actor系统提供了实用准则。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does VPS perform when the supervisor and actor are the same model at the same reasoning effort (true self-critique), and does this differ from the effort-differentiated same-family results?
- Can adaptive stopping criteria (e.g., based on critique informativeness or trajectory delta) mitigate the non-monotonic round-count scaling observed in 9 of 11 configurations?
- How would hybrid verbal-executable supervision (incorporating test-case pass/fail signals into the critique) perform on code synthesis benchmarks like LiveCodeBench?
- What is the sensitivity of VPS to the supervisor prompt format -- would a less structured critique format yield similar gains, or is the per-step labeling essential?
- How does VPS interact with other inference-time methods (e.g., can VPS be combined with chain-of-thought scaling or tree search for compounding gains)?
- Does the headroom-gain relationship (r=+0.90) hold across more diverse task domains (e.g., legal reasoning, medical diagnosis, scientific literature synthesis)?
