> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 RedirectQA 数据集，利用维基百科重定向信息将同一实体的不同表面形式（别名、缩写、拼写变体、常见错误）与 Wikidata 事实三元组配对，在 13 个大模型上揭示事实问答的预测结果会因实体命名方式不同而翻转，且这种不一致性呈类别依赖性（缩写/别名最难，拼写变体最稳健），频率分析进一步表明事实记忆既非纯表面特定也非完全表面不变，而是介于两者之间的跨表面耦合状态。

> **TL;DR:** The authors introduce RedirectQA, a factual QA dataset leveraging Wikipedia redirects to pair the same Wikidata triples with categorized entity surface forms (aliases, abbreviations, spelling variants, common errors). Across 13 LLMs (Pythia, OLMo 2, OpenSciRef, Qwen 3, Llama 3.1, GPT-4o-mini), prediction outcomes frequently flip when only the entity name changes while the underlying fact stays fixed. Models are robust to minor orthographic variations but inconsistent for abbreviations and aliases. Partial-correlation analyses show both entity-level and surface-level frequencies independently predict accuracy, indicating cross-surface coupling in factual access rather than independent memorization per surface form. Accepted to ACL 2026 Main.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **RedirectQA** 数据集，利用维基百科重定向机制将同一实体的多种表面形式与事实三元组配对，涵盖 33 种重定向类别（别名/缩写、拼写变体、常见错误），共 30,560 个表面实例和 61,120 个问题实现
- 在 13 个大模型上系统验证：仅改变实体表面形式（事实不变）即可导致预测结果翻转，不一致性具有类别依赖性——缩写和别名影响最大，拼写变体影响最小
- 通过偏相关分析揭示事实记忆的粒度：准确率同时与实体总频率和特定表面频率相关，支持"跨表面耦合"假说，而非每个表面形式独立记忆
- 发现反向不对称现象：某些情况下模型在非标准名称上答对但在标准名称上答错，说明面向人类的规范性名称未必是模型最可靠的访问入口

## Key Contributions

- Introduce **RedirectQA**, a curated entity-based factual QA dataset that pairs Wikidata triples with multiple categorized entity surface forms via Wikipedia redirects, covering 33 redirect categories across three broad types (Alternative Names/Abbreviations, Spelling Variants, Typical Errors), totaling 30,560 surface instances and 61,120 question realizations.
- Demonstrate across 13 LLMs that prediction outcomes frequently flip when only the subject entity surface form is changed, with category-dependent inconsistency: abbreviations/aliases are most disruptive, while spelling variants are handled relatively robustly.
- Decompose aggregate entity frequency into surface-level frequencies and use partial-correlation analysis to show that both entity- and surface-level frequencies independently predict QA accuracy, supporting cross-surface coupling in factual access.
- Reveal asymmetric surface dependence: models can fail under canonical names but succeed under alternative surface forms, challenging the assumption that canonical names are always the most reliable access path.

## Methodology

### Dataset Construction (RedirectQA)

1. **Factual triple collection**: 16 relation types from Wikidata (following PopQA setup), filtered for English labels, unique gold answers, non-zero Wikipedia pageviews, yielding 500k sampled triples.
2. **Redirect annotation**: Wikipedia redirect pages provide alternative surface forms for each entity, manually curated into 33 categories grouped into three broad types:
   - *Alternative Names and Abbreviations* (e.g., birth names, initialisms, pseudonyms)
   - *Spelling Variants* (e.g., diacritics, capitalization, ASCII-only)
   - *Typical Errors* (e.g., misspellings, miscapitalizations, incorrect names)
3. **Question generation**: Two templates per relation type (original from PopQA + GPT-4o paraphrase) to reduce template sensitivity.
4. **Final dataset**: 30,560 surface instances (14,672 canonical + 15,888 redirect) from 14,672 triples, 61,120 total question realizations.

### Evaluated Models

Three tiers of 13 models:
- **Transparent** (traceable pretraining corpora): Pythia (410M--12B), OpenSciRef v0.01 (0.4B, 1.7B), OLMo 2 Stage-1 (1B--32B)
- **Open-weight instruction-tuned**: Qwen 3 30B-A3B-Instruct, Llama 3.1 8B-Instruct
- **Proprietary**: GPT-4o-mini

### Evaluation Protocol

- 15-shot prompting ("Q: <question> A:" format), 8-bit quantization for local models, temperature 0 for API.
- Alias-aware string matching for answer evaluation.
- Consistency metric: whether canonical--redirect pairs yield the same correctness outcome.

### Frequency Analysis

- Entity linking via DBpedia Spotlight on pretraining corpora (Pile, OLMo Mix 1124).
- **Entity frequency**: total linked mentions of an entity across all surface forms.
- **Surface frequency**: linked mentions matching a specific surface form string.
- Pearson correlations and partial correlations between log-frequency and accuracy, controlling for the other frequency type.

## Results & Highlights

- **Non-negligible consistency flips**: In a preliminary PopQA-based experiment, 23.7% of canonical--redirect pairs yielded inconsistent predictions on Pythia-12B.
- **Category-dependent inconsistency**: Spelling Variants yield the highest consistency (minor orthographic changes are handled well); Alternative Names and Abbreviations show substantially lower consistency (initialisms are especially challenging, e.g., "NYT" vs. "The New York Times").
- **Model size trend**: Larger models within families tend to be more consistent, but the effect is not monotonic. Even GPT-4o-mini and Qwen 3 30B do not achieve perfect consistency.
- **Robustness to question template**: Paraphrased templates yield qualitatively similar consistency patterns, confirming effects are not template artifacts.
- **Frequency analysis**: Both entity and surface frequencies show significant positive correlations with accuracy. Partial-correlation analysis reveals that entity frequency retains predictive power even after controlling for surface frequency (especially for canonical forms), and vice versa for redirect forms. This rejects purely surface-specific memorization.
- **Entity-linking probe**: Pythia-12B shows only 52.2% balanced accuracy on a binary surface-form equivalence task, indicating that surface-form recognition is incomplete and does not fully explain the QA consistency patterns.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: The idea that entity surface forms affect factual QA is not entirely new -- prior work (Elazar et al., 2021) showed paraphrase sensitivity. However, the systematic categorization via Wikipedia redirects and the decomposition of entity frequency into surface-level frequencies are genuinely novel contributions. The dataset design is clean and the research question is well-motivated.

**Rigor**: The methodology is methodical. The use of partial correlations to disentangle entity vs. surface frequency effects is appropriate. Two question templates are used to rule out template artifacts. The low-frequency-controlled analysis in the appendix addresses a potential confound. However, several concerns remain:

- The evaluation uses alias-aware string matching, which, while pragmatic, may miss semantically correct but surface-different answers and could inflate perceived errors.
- 8-bit quantization is used for local models, which could introduce its own effects on factual access; this is acknowledged but not controlled for.
- The frequency analysis is limited to training-transparent models (10 of 13), restricting generalizability of the frequency findings to proprietary models.
- The entity-linking probe (Appendix B.5) with only 52.2% balanced accuracy on Pythia-12B raises questions about whether DBpedia Spotlight-based entity linking itself is reliable enough for the frequency analysis, though the authors use it consistently.

**Reproducibility**: High. Public dataset, public models (mostly), clear protocols, and supplementary materials in appendices.

**Significance**: The finding that factual memorization is neither surface-specific nor surface-invariant is an important nuance for the field. The RedirectQA dataset fills a real gap. However, the paper stops short of providing mechanistic explanations or mitigation strategies -- it is primarily a characterization study.

**Potential red flags**: The dataset is English-only and Wikipedia-centric. The surface-form variation is limited to subject entities (not objects). The practical impact for model developers is unclear beyond "evaluate with diverse surface forms."

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated dataset design using a novel application of Wikipedia redirects for controlled surface-form variation.
- Comprehensive evaluation across 13 models spanning transparent, open-weight, and proprietary tiers.
- Careful categorization of surface-form types enabling fine-grained analysis of inconsistency patterns.
- The partial-correlation analysis is a methodological strength, cleanly separating entity-level from surface-level frequency effects.
- Asymmetry finding (canonical failure + redirect success) is a subtle but important observation.
- Reproducibility: dataset publicly available, transparent models used for frequency analysis.

## Limitations

- English-only; does not cover multilingual or cross-lingual surface-form variation.
- Only subject entity surface forms are varied; object-side variation and broader question paraphrasing are out of scope.
- Wikipedia redirect categories do not cover all real-world ways of referring to entities.
- String-matching evaluation may miss semantically correct answers with unexpected surface forms.
- No mechanistic explanation for *why* surface forms affect factual access, nor any proposed mitigation.
- Frequency analysis restricted to training-transparent models; findings may not extend to proprietary models.
- The entity-linking probe suggests models poorly recognize surface-form equivalence, but this is only tested on Pythia-12B.

## Relevance

本文揭示了LLM事实记忆对实体命名方式的敏感性，这对理解模型知识检索机制和提升事实问答鲁棒性有重要参考价值；RedirectQA 数据集可作为评估模型事实知识可靠性的新基准。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do surface-form effects interact with retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)? Does providing retrieved context mitigate surface-form sensitivity?
- Are the observed surface-form effects consistent across languages, especially for languages with more complex morphology or naming conventions?
- Can internal representation analysis (e.g., probing entity representations across surface forms) reveal the mechanism by which surface forms gate factual access?
- Would instruction tuning or fine-tuning with diverse surface forms improve cross-surface consistency, or is this a fundamental limitation of current architectures?
- How do surface-form effects scale with model size beyond 32B parameters, and do they eventually disappear at sufficient scale?
