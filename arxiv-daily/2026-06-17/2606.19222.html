Updating only 96 of 112 attention tensors forgets RLVR-injected reasoning with collateral damage statistically indistinguishable from zero.

Mechanism-Guided Selective Unlearning for RLVR-Induced Reasoning

Chenyu Zhou·Qiliang Jiang·Shuning Wu·Xu Zhou ·2026-06-17·arXiv:2606.19222·alias MAST

1Key metric

+12.9pp

GSM8K advantage over full-parameter gradient ascent on Qwen3-1.7B-Base.

95% CI [+6.6, +19.1] · paired bootstrap

Headline forget: 8 lost / 0 recovered · McNemar p = 0.0078

2Why this matters

RLVR drives mainstream reasoning. Rolling back its injected skills without collateral damage is a real deployment pain for alignment edits, safety, and unlearning.

3Critical edge

Caveat

The headlined forget drop is just 8 of 150 problems (5.33 pp) — the paper's value lives in the collateral ratio, not in the magnitude of forgetting.

MMethodology

  1. Matched checkpoints Train SFT and SFT→RLVR (GRPO) from the same base; require GSM8K within 3 pp. Achieved 1.17 pp (Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B) and 2.34 pp (Qwen3-1.7B-Base).
  2. Per-tensor diagnostics For each of 112 attention-projection tensors (q/k/v/o × 28 layers), compute Δt = θR − θS, the off-principal fraction ρt, and the forget-gradient × delta cosine ct.
  3. Mechanism score Score st = ρt · log(1+‖Δt‖2F) · (1+|ct|) — off-principal geometry × update magnitude × gradient coupling.
  4. Selective update 50 steps of gradient ascent on the forget set, restricted to the top-k = 96 tensors by st. MLP, norms, embeddings stay frozen.
  5. Evaluation Forget: MATH (C&P / Geo / NT, N = 150). Retain: other MATH (N = 200) + GSM8K holdout. Greedy exact match with tolerant regrade. Paired bootstrap + McNemar.

AAlgorithm at a glance

Mechanism score st = ρt · log(1 + ‖Δt‖2F) · (1 + |ct|)

  • ‖Δt‖F SFT→RLVR update magnitude (Frobenius)

Statistical

MAST forget drop: 8 lost / 0 recovered, McNemar p = 0.0078. Collateral CIs include zero — retain [−4.0, +5.0] pp, GSM8K [−3.1, +1.6] pp.

Ranking-specificity

Top-96 keeps all 28 q and all 28 o projections; bottom-64 of same size forgets almost nothing (45→44). Ranked vs random-96 at matched forgetting = +2.34 pp GSM8K (CI [+0.26, +4.56]).

Robustness

Stable across 3 seeds, 3 objectives (GA / NPO β=0.5 / SimNPO β=2.5), 2 model families. MMLU stays within 0.4 pp of source. Restricted-vs-full Qwen3 advantage = +12.9 pp.

+Strengths

  • Paired statistics done right. Matched checkpoints, McNemar on per-item forgetting, paired bootstrap with explicit 95% CIs on both axes of the collateral difference — the kind of reporting unlearning papers usually lack.
  • Diagnostic → selector → ablation is closed. The off-principal × magnitude × gradient-coupling score is not just motivation; the three ranking-specificity properties (R1/R2/R3) prove the ranking, not the count, drives the result.
  • Pareto-frontier framing. Figure 2 visualises the full-GA collapse boundary and MAST's usable operating region on both model families — far more informative than a single accuracy table.
  • General evaluation insight. Showing that solution-trajectory likelihood and answer likelihood can diverge under unlearning is a publishable observation in its own right — relevant beyond MAST.

−Limitations

  • Narrow domain. Math reasoning on Qwen2.5 / Qwen3 at 1.5B / 1.7B; non-math capability (code, chat, safety, multilingual) is measured only by a 1000-example MMLU check.
  • Modest absolute forget effect. 5.33 pp (8 / 150) is statistically significant for the paired test, but small. The paper's contribution is the ratio of forgetting to collateral, not either quantity alone.
  • Hyperparameters tuned on the eval set. top-k = 96 and η are picked by ablation (top-32 / 88 / 96 / 104); no held-out calibration protocol is described.
  • Selector is correlational, not causal. The causal claim that "RLVR-injected reasoning lives in these directions" is supported by cross-model reproduction and a bottom-64 ablation, but a reverse-direction intervention (update the bottom of the ranking at the same strength) was not run.

?Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the mechanism score generalise beyond Qwen and math — Llama-3, Mistral, MoE, code, agentic, multilingual? The ρt ≈ 0.9998 may be family-specific.
  2. Can the matched SFT/RLVR protocol be replaced by a cheaper surrogate (LoRA-delta + SVD) so MAST works without two full checkpoints?
  3. Is the |ct| symmetrisation optimal, or do sign-preserving or curriculum-weighted variants help? Ablate the asymmetry axis.
  4. How does MAST compose with iterative post-unlearning repair — a small SFT pass on the retain set to recover the few collateral points at the strong operating point?
  5. The solution-Δlog-prob-down / answer-Δlog-prob-up pitfall suggests a unified unlearning score (trajectory likelihood + answer likelihood + exact match) should be standard in reasoning-unlearning papers.
  6. Is the ranking stable under different RLVR algorithms (GRPO / PPO / RLOO / DPO-on-CoT), or does the top-k composition drift with the post-training recipe?
