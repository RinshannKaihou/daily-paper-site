An 8-dataset, 14K-example recipe with minimal GRPO beats strong long-context RL baselines on Qwen3 4B / 8B / 30B-A3B.

Beyond Reward Engineering: A Data Recipe for Long-Context Reinforcement Learning
Xiaoyue Xu, Sikui Zhang, Xiaorong Wang, Xu Han, Chaojun Xiao · et al.
2026-06-17
long-context reinforcement learning GRPO data-centric Qwen3 LLM agents
Key Metric
+7.21avg
Qwen3-4B-Thinking-2507 average over 7 long-context benchmarks vs. base (+3.17 / +6.41 for 8B / 30B-A3B).
Why this matters

Long-context RL's real bottleneck is data diversity, not reward engineering. A small, open recipe outperforms more elaborate pipelines, and the gains transfer to web agents.

Critical edge

Only two data-centric baselines (DocQA-RL-1.6K, KeyChain-15K); no head-to-head against algorithm-centric RL like LongRLVR, EPRO, or PAS that augment the reward.

Methodology

The authors argue that long-context reasoning decomposes into three complementary abilities, each with a curated dataset family targeting a specific failure mode of current long-context LMs. Training is a minimal outcome-based GRPO (KL omitted, task-balanced sampling, task-level advantage normalization) on a post-processed 14,069-example mixture capped at 64K tokens.

Ability 01Retrieval

Locate candidate evidence when query and evidence share no keywords, or when many near-duplicate needles are scattered through context.

FuzzyNeedle · 1,500 (IS-A paraphrase, lexical bias removed)
MultiNeedle · 1,500 (counted K-th near-duplicate)

Ability 02Multi-evidence Synthesis

Integrate evidence across positions, defeat surface-form shortcuts, and require exhaustive coverage of similar-but-distractor entities.

CrossEntity · 3,521 (derived-attribute aggregation)
WebSearch · 684 (anonymized entity chain)
MultiQuery · 226 (ordered, in-document)
KeyChain · 654 (UUID pointer chain)

Ability 03Reasoning

Perform complex multi-step reasoning over long inputs; long inputs alone degrade reasoning, motivating long-form math/QA data.

LongDocQA · 3,422 (HotpotQA / MuSiQue / Qasper extended)
LongMath · 2,562 (DeepMath / MATH-Hard narrative rewrite)
  • Curation · two-stage post-processing: drop examples > 64K tokens, then 4-rollout difficulty filter with Qwen3-30B-A3B (keep only moderate-difficulty).
  • Reward · word-level recall for most tasks; DeepSeek-V3.2 LLM-as-judge for CrossEntity and LongMath (numerical & multi-form answers).
  • Stabilizers · (i) task-balanced sampling fixes per-dataset ratio per batch; (ii) task-level advantage normalization rescales by per-dataset reward std.

Strengths

  • Clean, falsifiable thesis — “diverse data > reward engineering,” backed by a direct head-to-head and a disconfirming process-reward ablation.
  • Targeted dataset design — each of 8 datasets attacks a named failure mode (lexical shortcut, surface shortcut, incomplete coverage, distractor confusion).
  • Three-model, seven-benchmark evaluation with proper length stratification beyond the 64K training cap.
  • Practical stabilizers clearly motivated by the mixture's heterogeneity (task balancing + advantage normalization).
  • Agentic transfer is the headline practical result: +7.0 on BrowseComp, +4.8 on GAIA from continuing long-context RL on an agent-tuned base.
  • Data release commitment, sensible licensing, explicit leakage filter for web-based agentic benchmarks.

Limitations

  • Only Qwen3 family, up to 30B-A3B (a MoE with ~3B active params); no validation on dense ≥70B or other model families.
  • Trained at 64K only; native 128K+ training is left to future work.
  • 8B gain lags at +3.17 (vs. +7.21 / +6.41); no clear diagnosis in the paper.
  • Difficulty filter is a selection bias — the training set contains only moderate-difficulty problems; no ablation on filter cutoff.
  • Two data-centric baselines only; algorithm-centric methods (LongRLVR, EPRO, PAS) appear only via the 4B process-reward ablation.
  • Agentic transfer is preliminary: one model, two benchmarks, no analysis of which skill transfers to which agent behaviour.
  • English only; no multilingual evaluation.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the recipe transfer to non-Qwen3 dense families (Llama, Mistral) and to models ≥70B?
  • Why does the 8B gain lag behind 4B and 30B-A3B — undertrained run, YaRN extension, or a fundamental scaling effect?
  • Can the three-ability taxonomy be refined (e.g., add tool use over long contexts or multi-document temporal reasoning), and does the “all three are needed” pattern hold?
  • The process-reward ablation hurts on 4B — generic LLM-as-judge noise, or evidence-anchored rewards from a stronger model would flip the sign?
  • How much of the agentic transfer is mediated by raw long-context reasoning ability vs. by exposure to a different action distribution during continued RL?
  • The difficulty filter discards ~half the prompts — could a curriculum over the all-correct / all-wrong subsets be more sample-efficient than discarding them?
