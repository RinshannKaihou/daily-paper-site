Why This Matters

RTS games expose a blind spot no chat benchmark catches: coordination under numerical disadvantage — showing how far frontier VLMs are from real multi-agent deployment.

Critical Edge

All opponents are built-in Easy AI — models win vs Easy yet reportedly lose to Medium. There is no VLM-vs-VLM, strong scripted bot, RL, or human baseline, and only one fixed map per cell.

01

Methodology, distilled

RTSGameBench runs on Beyond All Reason (BAR) — an open-source RTS larger than StarCraft II in unit count (96 types), supply cap (200), and per-match unit capacity (1,600). A standardized observe–decide–act loop feeds each VLM a global minimap, placeable local camera views, and structured text — with fog-of-war active and the engine pausing between steps, so every move tests decision quality, not reaction speed.

Evaluation has three layers: full-game matches (1v1, symmetric & asymmetric teams, free-for-all), five diagnostic mini-games each isolating one RTS competency from Buro's taxonomy (resources, spatial/temporal reasoning, opponent modeling, collaboration, adversarial planning), and a self-evolving multi-agent pipeline that turns free-text queries into new mini-games.

To make hundreds of units tractable, RTSGameAgent replaces per-unit movement with a 4-state finite-state machine for squads (move / move_force / stop / fight) plus short- and long-term agentic memory.

03

Strengths & limitations

Strengths

  • Largest-in-class open RTS substrate (BAR) with an active modding community.
  • Three complementary modes: full-game, taxonomy-grounded mini-games, self-evolving generation.
  • FSM + memory is well-motivated; both components empirically necessary (0.87 → 0.10 w/o both).
  • Systematic scale sweeps — team play is more robust to unit scaling than FFA, a non-obvious result.

Limitations

  • Built-in Easy AI is the only opponent; no strong scripted/RL/human or VLM-vs-VLM baselines.
  • One fixed map per cell — cross-map generalization untested.
  • Same VLM for memory & decision phases — confounds the memory ablation.
  • No cost / latency / token-budget analysis; impractical at scale with frontier closed models.
  • "Playability" + 4-evaluator preference is a thin quality bar for self-evolution.
04

Open questions

How large is the real coordination gap vs strong scripted bots, RL agents, human, or VLM-vs-VLM matchups?
Memory & decision phases share one model — would a separate, larger memory LLM close the gap?
TCP saturates for four models — are mini-games calibrated, or do they need difficulty scaling?
One fixed map per cell: how much variance is map-specific vs genuinely strategic?
Team play (FS-T) is more robust to unit scaling than FFA (FS-F) — does this asymmetry generalize?
Does fine-tuning on RTSGameBench traces transfer to Diplomacy / Hanabi / Overcooked?
