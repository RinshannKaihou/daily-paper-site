> **一句话总结：** 利用 9 个零开销、隐私保护的 NVML 计数器，在 9 张 GPU（覆盖 Ampere–Blackwell 四代架构）上完成 1404 次、162 种工作负载的运行，经 5 轮"监控者-规避者"对抗迭代（20 类规避策略、含白盒攻击和多 GPU 分布式规避），RandomForest 分类器在干净测试集上达到 98.2% 二分类准确率，并对未见过的白盒规避策略保持 43–87% 检测率。

> **TL;DR:** The authors build and stress-test a privacy-preserving classifier that detects ML training from nine always-on NVML counters sampled at 1 Hz, validated on 9 NVIDIA GPUs (Ampere through Blackwell) with 1,404 runs / ~117 GPU-hours / 162 workloads. After 5 rounds of monitor–evader iteration covering 20 evasion families (utilization modulation, memory reduction, mimicry, training-as-inference, multi-GPU DDP evasion, and white-box attacks), the RandomForest hits 98.2% binary accuracy and 43–87% accuracy on unseen white-box evasion, while multi-GPU datacenter evasion is detected at 99.6–100%. The work is positioned as a lower bound on what governance monitors can do, contingent on three HEM guarantees: tamper-resistant NVML read path, authenticated telemetry channel, and secure boot of the classifier.

## 核心贡献

- 首个面向 AI 计算治理的系统性对抗性 NVML 工作负载分类评估，采用 5 轮攻防迭代协议
- 公开一个基于 9 个零开销 NVML 计数器（GPU 利用率、显存占用、功耗、温度、SM 频率、显存频率、PCIe TX/RX，1 Hz 采样）的 RandomForest 分类器，跨 9 个 GPU 型号、4 代架构均保持高性能
- 量化了 20 类规避策略（含多 GPU 分布式训练规避和白盒攻击）对分类器的影响，并展示了物理不可消除特征（功耗–显存相关性、累计能量、显存预分配平台特征）的关键作用
- 给出最小化 HEM 硬件治理方案的三项要求（防篡改读路径、认证遥测通道、安全启动分类器），将治理问题从"ML 任务"转化为"硬件安全任务"
- 释出源码（基线、规避工作负载、分类器），邀请社区持续红队

## Key Contributions

- First systematic adversarial-robustness study of NVML-based zero-overhead GPU workload monitoring for AI compute governance
- 5-round monitor–evader red-team protocol covering 20 evasion strategy families
- Cross-architecture coverage: 9 NVIDIA GPUs (Ampere A100 40/80GB, Hopper H100 80GB/NVL, H200 141GB, Blackwell B200, RTX 5080/5090, RTX 4050 Laptop) spanning 4 architecture generations
- RandomForest classifier with 98.2% binary accuracy, 97.39% precision / 98.90% recall / 96.94% specificity on held-out runs
- 100% in-distribution accuracy after defender hardening; ≥99.6% on multi-GPU datacenter evasion
- 43–87% detection of unseen white-box evasion strategies after Round 5 hardening
- A minimal HEM design pattern that grounds future hardware-enabled governance research
- Open-source release of workloads and classifier for community red-teaming

## Methodology

### Telemetry and Features
- **Signals:** 9 NVML counters (GPU utilization, memory utilization, memory used MB, power draw W, temperature, SM clock MHz, memory clock MHz, PCIe TX/RX MB/s) sampled at 1 Hz.
- **Cost:** Zero runtime overhead; counters are "always-available" from Pascal onward.
- **Two-layer feature design:** (1) a roofline / structural layer using mean power, mean utilization, peak memory to localize workloads in arithmetic-intensity space; (2) a temporal layer using autocorrelation, coefficient of variation, and periodicity to resolve within-region ambiguity. The deployed pipeline is a two-stage decision tree: ML-vs-not, then training-vs-inference.
- **External power validation:** Wall-plug power measured via Tapo P115 smart plug for 49 workloads on RTX 5080 desktop to corroborate reported NVML power.

### Classifier
- **Model:** RandomForest with 400 trees, no max depth; chosen via cross-validation over XGBoost, SVM-RBF, and logistic regression.
- **Windowing:** 30 s windows with 15 s stride (also tested at 5 s; 98.15% accuracy).
- **Dataset:** 1,404 runs of 162 distinct workloads, 117 GPU-hours, 65 wall-clock hours, 445,310 readings × 9 signals.
- **Composition:** 867 ML-training runs (180 supervised, 72 DDP, 152 FSDP on 7B–70B models, 42 LoRA, 421 adversarial) + 352 inference runs (49 image, 264 LLM covering TinyLlama, Phi-3, Qwen-2.5 3B–72B, Mistral/Mixtral, DeepSeek-V3/R1, 39 other modalities) + 185 non-ML runs (65 HPC, 36 crypto mining, 36 rendering/video, 34 idle baselines, 14 misc).

### Adversarial Evaluation Protocol
- **5-round co-evolution** between evader and defender. Each round, the attacker designs new strategies targeting the current classifier's weaknesses, and the defender retrains a hardened classifier including the new adversarial examples.
- **Six technique classes** spanning 20 strategy families:
  1. **Utilization manipulation:** modulation (A), low-utilization training (B), temporal disruptions (D)
  2. **Memory reduction / non-ML mimicry:** minimal-memory (E), interleaving (F), clock throttling (G), FFT/mining mimicry (H), stochastic (I), PID control (J)
  3. **Training-as-inference:** online learning (K), training diluted with 2×/5×/10×/20× inference (L)
  4. **Composite single-GPU:** min-memory composite (M), gradient-accumulation structuring (N), composite idle-padding 0–95% (Round 4)
  5. **Multi-GPU evasion:** DDP variants of K and L with stagger
  6. **White-box (Round 5):** diluted full-model fine-tuning (WB-D), diluted LoRA (WB-L), WB + grad-accum combined
- **Leave-one-strategy-out evaluation:** train on real data plus all *other* adversarial strategies; test on held-out family — measures generalization to *unseen* evasion techniques.

### Threat Model
- **Cloud tenant evader:** Controls user-space software; cannot tamper with host OS, hypervisor, or hardware.
- **Hardware operator evader:** Owns the full software stack; defeated only with three HEM guarantees (tamper-resistant read path, authenticated channel, secure boot).
- **Privacy:** NVML signals observe only physical/electrical effects — no model weights, training data, or hyperparameters accessed.

## Results & Highlights

### Baseline
- **98.21% ± 0.84% binary accuracy** (macro-F1 0.982), 30 s windows, 5-fold grouped CV across 26,496 windows.
- 97.39% precision, 98.90% recall, 96.94% specificity, 3.06% FPR.
- 98.15% accuracy at 5 s windows — fast enough for near-real-time monitoring.

### Cross-Architecture Generalization
- In-distribution pooled classifier: 100% accuracy on new, non-disguised workloads from any of the 9 GPUs.
- Leave-one-GPU-out (LOGO): 100% on 8 of 9 GPUs, worst-case 99.4% on B200. No retraining needed for new hardware types.
- Single-source-to-many transfer (training on one GPU, testing on another): datacenter-to-datacenter >90%; consumer-to-datacenter often <50% (not relevant for governance).

### Adversarial Arms Race
- **Round 1 (utilization manipulation):** Baseline 6–96% detection → hardened 100% in-distribution, 59–100% unseen.
- **Round 2 (memory/mimicry):** Baseline 0–91% → hardened 100% in-distribution, 80–100% unseen.
- **Round 3 (training-as-inference):** Baseline 52–60% → hardened 100% in-distribution, 87–100% unseen. Power–memory correlation and cumulative energy features were decisive.
- **Round 4 single-GPU composite:** Baseline 19–100% → hardened 100% in-distribution, 98–100% unseen.
- **Round 4 multi-GPU (DDP + stagger):** Hardened 100% detection; 99.6–100% in leave-one-strategy-out. Classifier learned to recognize AllGather / ReduceScatter NVLink traffic signatures for 7B–70B FSDP.
- **Round 5 white-box:** Up to 94% evasion against Round-4 classifier (e.g., inserting 4 s of training across 5 s window boundaries, or running training only when defender's P(training) < 0.30). After hardening with pre-allocation memory-plateau features and more matched-size dense-LLM-inference data, **43–87% detection of unseen white-box strategies**.

### Honest Operational Numbers
- Average 6.2% evasion rate across the 14 single-GPU non-white-box strategies in held-out evaluation.
- **Only one strategy (white-box LoRA) achieves <50% detection** against the evasion-resistant classifier.
- The authors frame Round 5 as a "disaster scenario for governance" (infiltrate agency, steal classifier, orchestrate workload) — and show that even there, the defender still detects 43–87% of unseen white-box strategies after hardening.

## Critical Assessment

### Novelty
- The cat-and-mouse adversarial framing is a clear step beyond prior work that classified training vs. non-training with a non-adversarial adversary (Tang et al. 2022, Weakley et al. 2025). The 5-round protocol with white-box access is genuinely strong.
- The 9-counter, zero-overhead constraint is a meaningful and well-motivated lower bound: anything stronger (DCGM, Nsight, smart-plug power, NVLink counters) can only improve the upper bound. The WAVE comparison (1,200–5,300% overhead for Nsight Compute) makes this concrete.
- The two-stage roofline + temporal feature design is a sensible inductive bias for this signal space.

### Rigor
- **Strong points:** 5-fold grouped CV (runs do not leak across folds), leave-one-strategy-out, LOGO evaluation, external smart-plug power validation, balanced class weights, calibrated probabilistic output (decision threshold tunable for sensitivity vs. FPR).
- **Concerns:**
  - The 20 evasion families are presumably comprehensive but coverage of *all* plausible evasions is impossible; future red-teaming is explicitly invited.
  - Most adversarial runs are constructed by the same authors — bias toward "what we thought of" evasion. External red-team rounds are needed to validate the 43–87% white-box floor.
  - "20 families" is enumerated by single-letter labels (A–N + multi-GPU + WB) without per-family sample counts in the main paper (Table 2 in Section A.4 likely has details). With 421 adversarial training runs spread across 20+ families, some families may have thin coverage.
  - The single-source-to-many transfer failures (consumer→datacenter <50%) are honestly reported and acknowledged as not relevant to governance — good scientific hygiene.

### Reproducibility
- **Source code released** for non-ML, inference, training, and adversarial evasion workloads.
- Classifier not explicitly released as a pretrained artifact in the abstract; check repo. RandomForest with 400 trees is small and re-trainable, so this is a minor concern.
- Hyperparameters clearly specified (400 trees, no max depth, 30 s windows, 15 s stride).

### Significance
- **High.** Directly addresses the operational feasibility of AI compute governance proposals (EO 14110, Shavit 2023, Baker et al. 2025, Scher et al. 2025). Showing that 1 Hz NVML counters can detect training at 98.2% with a software-only baseline is a meaningful empirical contribution to the policy debate.
- The "Involuntary Witness" framing — backward pass uses ~2× the FLOPs and thus ~3× the energy per token of forward pass — is theoretically grounded in Kaplan et al. 2020 scaling laws and corroborated by POLCA's Microsoft Azure measurements.
- The 43–87% white-box number is the most policy-relevant honest figure; it tells regulators what to expect against a well-resourced insider threat, and what residual hardware mechanisms must cover.

### Red Flags
- One-strategy LoRA under 50% detection: LoRA's low memory/energy footprint makes it the most evasive family. This is a known limitation acknowledged by the authors.
- White-box test: the evader was given access to the *previous* classifier, not the *current* one. A real insider threat might have continuous access. The 43–87% floor is a lower bound on what a fully informed insider can evade.
- Compute-structuring evasion across multiple cloud providers (Heim et al. 2024) is not directly tested — the threat model focuses on a single host.

## Strengths

- Genuine adversarial red-team methodology (5 rounds, white-box, multi-GPU)
- Cross-architecture coverage (4 generations × 9 GPUs) including consumer and datacenter
- Zero-overhead and privacy-preserving — a meaningful lower bound for governance monitors
- Honest reporting of white-box degradation (43–87%) rather than cherry-picking
- Calibrated probabilistic output enables tunable sensitivity/FPR trade-off
- Open-source workloads and explicit invitation to red-team
- Strong positioning in the AI governance literature

## Limitations

- 43–87% on unseen white-box strategies is still a wide gap from operational reliability for a regulator
- LoRA evasion under 50% — economically attractive for small evaders
- Cross-provider compute structuring not evaluated
- All adversarial strategies are author-designed; external red-team rounds pending
- 1 Hz sampling misses sub-second evasion patterns (e.g., the 4 s / 5 s window-boundary attack)
- HEM assumptions (tamper-resistant read path, authenticated channel, secure boot) are not all available in current hardware; results are conditional on these being built
- Multi-GPU detection at 99.6–100% is encouraging but only tests 4 specific DDP/stagger strategies

## Relevance

与 AI 计算治理、GPU 系统安全、ML 监控研究方向直接相关；本工作为"硬件强制监管"路径提供了首个系统性的攻防实证基线，对希望了解 AI 算力出口管制、compute structuring 防御可行性的研究者和政策制定者尤为重要。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How robust is the classifier against a *continuously-adaptive* white-box adversary (e.g., online reinforcement-learning evader with full classifier access)?
- Can the LoRA evasion be closed by adding feature engineering for low-memory fine-tuning signatures, or is LoRA fundamentally cheap enough to evade NVML-based detection?
- Does multi-tenant (cloud) isolation noise degrade detection accuracy, and at what point?
- What is the deployment story for HEM requirements: which of (tamper-resistant read path, authenticated channel, secure boot) is technically feasible in the next 2 years?
- Can the 1 Hz sampling rate be safely increased (10 Hz / 100 Hz) without re-introducing overhead, and does that close the window-boundary attacks?
- How does the classifier behave on non-NVIDIA hardware (AMD MI300, Intel Gaudi, custom accelerators)?
- Does the power–memory correlation feature generalize across cooling profiles and datacenter power-management policies?
