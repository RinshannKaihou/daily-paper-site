Attention as Frustrated Synchronization

Joshua Nunley · Cognitive Science Program, Luddy School of Informatics · Indiana University Bloomington

Key Metric −0.4231bpc

Reversed on the 16–23 copy-depth bin: base Kuramoto attention loses +0.4231 bpc vs. converged Transformer; every FSN seed is now ahead on every bin of depth ≥ 4.

Why this matters

An interpretable, oscillator-based attention alternative. Synchronization-theory vocabulary explains why the value pathway shouldn't be pure attraction — and beats the Transformer on long-range copy at small scale.

Critical edge

Character-level only. Subword tokenization — the regime of every modern LLM — is explicitly unstudied. Many exotic architectures post 1M character-level wins that do not transfer.

Methodology

Token state is a phase on the k-torus; the value pathway becomes a learned complex coupling kernel with three named objects from the synchronization literature.

State & score map

Token state θt ∈ Tk (k = 176), zt = eiθt ∈ Ck. Score map inherited from Kuramoto attention — gated phase-coherence with rotary phase drift implementing RoPE as literal angular rates ωc.

stu = (1/τ) Σc gcq(t) · gck(u) · cos(θt,c − θu,c + ωc(t−u))

The coupling kernel (Eq. 2)

Three terms, each a named object: static Kuramoto–Sakaguchi frustration φn; repulsive Daido harmonics w0(n); and a one-step delay w1(n) on the next-token field.

at = Σn=1N=3 Im[ z̄tn ⊙ A ( w0(n) ⊙ zn + w1(n) ⊙ z+1n ) ]t

Proposition 1 — the anticipatory identity

sin(θu+1 − θt) = sin((θu − θt) + δu), where δu = θu+1 − θu is the data's own transition. The delay term is KS coupling whose frustration angle is read from the data, not a parameter. Next-token prediction = synchronization frustrated by the data (Voss 2000).

Training discipline

  • Bounded update — norm-matched tanh (α init = 2π) is essential. Without it, the run diverges to 10.19 bpc in epoch 1.
  • Baselines — pre-norm RoPE Transformer with SwiGLU FFN; width auto-searched to land nearest 1M target. Identical data order, AdamW (wd 0.01, lr 1e-3), batch 64, sequence 256, fp32, single A100.
  • Eval protocol — character-level enwik8 (205 vocab) and codeparrot (97 vocab). 5 FSN seeds + 3 transformer seeds at 1M; window-cluster bootstrap 95% CIs for the copy-depth decomposition.

Strengths

  • Clean, falsifiable algebra. Every kernel coefficient is a named object in synchronization theory (Kuramoto–Sakaguchi, Daido, Voss anticipation) — interpretable at the coefficient level, not a black box.
  • Honest experimental hygiene. Matched recipes, multiple seeds, three ablations (no-phase, MLP-free, winding-register), a 1M→8M scale ladder.
  • Copy-depth decomposition is the strongest causal probe. Isolates the value-pathway failure from the shared score map and the held-constant FFN — directly connects to the induction-head / associative-recall literature.
  • Universal-cover finding. The MLP-free and winding-register variants separately identify the SwiGLU's winding-readout role — a genuinely new mechanistic result.
  • Wall-clock win at 4M & 8M. Reaches transformer quality in 0.70× the wall-clock despite a ~3× per-epoch slowdown — the only practically relevant comparison in the paper.
  • Convergence structure. No-phase ablation is statistically indistinguishable from full FSN at convergence; deep-copy effect is independent of static frustration phases.

Limitations

  • Character-level only (enwik8 205 vocab, codeparrot 97 vocab). Subword tokenization is explicitly future work — the regime of every modern LLM.
  • Headline is at 1M. 8M rung is single-seed; 4M rung is two seeds; only 1M has the 5+3 counts needed for tight per-seed statistics.
  • Seed-robustness gaps outside enwik8. 1 of 3 FSN seeds plateaued from epoch 1 on code; 1 of 3 FSN-MF seeds diverged at epoch 17 with no early warning.
  • Delay = anticipation rests on a 2-epoch single-seed ablation. A converged, multi-seed delay ablation is future work.
  • ~3× per-epoch training overhead at 1M (263k vs 965k tok/s on A100; 3.0 vs 0.9 GB peak). Wall-clock win is only at 4M and 8M.
  • No public code. Configuration is documented but not runnable as distributed; independent replication is non-trivial.
  • 30-vs-50-epoch comparison quietly closes the gap. FSN crosses 1.611 only at epochs 19–27; the 50-epoch transformer reference is best-of-5 — a generous bar.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the Proposition 1 identity hold in the trained model at convergence, or only in the 2-epoch screen? A converged, multi-seed delay ablation is the obvious next experiment.
  • At what parameter / compute scale does the FSN advantage vanish, and is the crossover driven by the single-kernel bottleneck or by the loss of FFN-style mixing? A 30M+ ladder with 3+ seeds would be the next test.
  • How does FSN perform under subword tokenization (BPE / SentencePiece) on a modern small-LLM corpus? The character-level → subword gap is where many exotic architectures fail to transfer.
  • Can the static frustration phases φn be replaced with purely data-derived angles without hurting convergence, given they buy only early-training speed?
  • The "winding register" finding suggests an explicit universal-cover channel is necessary for FSN to fully replace SwiGLU. Is there a more natural way to expose winding to the oscillator stack?
  • Can the kernel be compiled onto physical oscillator hardware (the conclusion gestures at this), and what is the energy / latency profile on such a substrate compared to a GPU transformer?
  • Does the FSN's advantage on the 16–23 copy-depth bin transfer to the associative-recall slice of larger corpora (Pile, RedPajama) at subword tokenization, where Arora et al.'s analysis is calibrated?
