Learning from Own Solutions: Self-Conditioned Credit Assignment for RLVR

Yingyu Shan, Yuhang Guo, Zihao Cheng, Zeming Liu, Xiangrong Zhu, Xinyi Wang, Jiashu Yao, Wei Lin, Hongru Wang, Heyan Huang + 1 co-author  ·  BIT-ISLab
▍Key finding

SC-GRPO turns a model's own verified rollouts into a per-token credit signal — and beats DAPO and even external-teacher OPSD without any PRM, ground-truth labels, or extra model.

SC-GRPO · Qwen3-8B (thinking disabled) · 5 RLVR benchmarks
Key Metric
47.44%
Avg@8 across AIME24, AIME25, LiveCodeBench v6, AppWorld, WebShop. +5.86% over DAPO (41.58), +8.07% over GRPO (39.37), and beats OPSD-Oracle (20.20) — with no external teacher at all.
Why This Matters

RLVR no longer needs a deployable teacher, PRM, or hand-curated labels to get sharper token-level credit — the model supervises itself from verified siblings, and the same trick transfers OOD (LCB → Codeforces) at almost the in-domain rate.

drop-in GRPO modification · 2–13% step-time overhead
Critical Edge
  • Only Qwen3-8B, thinking off — no 32B / 70B / reasoning-mode test.
  • The OPSD-Oracle baseline underperforms the base model on LCB — the head-to-head isn't clean.
  • Threshold c = max(p75, 1e-4) has a hand-tuned 1e-4 floor; benefit vanishes when no rollout in the group is verified.

01Methodology · distilled

The trick. For each rollout oi, pick a verified sibling trajectory τ from the same group and append it to the system prompt of the same model. Re-score the prefix of oi under this self-conditioned policy π̂θ(·|si,t, τ) and measure, token by token, the forward KL to the original policy πθ(·|si,t). Tokens whose predictions barely budge when a verified solution is shown (small KL) get a small weight; "pivotal" tokens (large KL) get weight → 1. Multiply that weight onto the GRPO gradient. The reward still fixes the direction — the KL only modulates intensity.

f(Di,t) = Di,t / (Di,t + c),    c = max(p75({Dact}), 1e-4)

Group routing. Partial-solve groups (2 ≤ nc < G) use the KL weight to sharpen credit. Solve-none groups (nc = 0) get a weak diversity bonus: average the per-token f(D) along the shorter of the two rollouts, normalize within the group, and use α = 0.1 × that pseudo-advantage. Degenerate cases (nc = 1 or nc = G) fall back to plain GRPO.

Why weight, not loss? A theorem in §4.2 shows that additive OPSD-style distillation (forward / reverse / bidirectional KL) has a loss-minimizer that is a weighted average of teacher trajectories — a distribution that does not correspond to any feasible trajectory. The paper validates this empirically: every auxiliary-loss variant on LCB tops out at 39.11% Avg@8 vs. SC-GRPO's 43.22%. Multiplicative weighting sidesteps the pathology.

03What moves the needle

Group routing. "Partial + solve-none" (the paper's choice) hits 43.22 Avg@8 on LCB. Restrict to partial-only: 41.41. Include nc = 1: 38.55 (entropy collapse from a single verified trajectory dominating). Cover all groups: 36.64.

Threshold c. The p75 floor wins. Fixed 1e-4 sits at 42.93. Drop to p50 and the median sits below 1e-4 on the right-skewed KL distribution — weights collapse to uniform and accuracy falls to 41.98.

Diversity α. α = 0.1 sweet spot. α = 0.2 lets the exploration bonus compete with the RL objective and accuracy falls to 40.74.

OOD. Train on LCB, evaluate on Codeforces: +5.2% Avg@8 relative — within noise of the 6.1% in-domain gain. The credit signal is the generalization, not verifier overfit.

04Overhead, honestly

Actor update: 51% slower on LCB v6, 37% slower on DAPO-Math. The self-conditioned forward pass costs real FLOPs.

End-to-end step time: +13% on LCB, +2% on DAPO-Math. Rollout generation dominates the wall clock, so the per-step tax shrinks to almost nothing.

Policy entropy: SC-GRPO stays above DAPO across all training steps; the gap widens after step 200. The authors read this as preserved exploration capacity.

REINFORCE++ sanity check (Appendix): peaks at AppWorld 0.175 @ step 40, collapses to 0.035 @ step 101. Motivates the GRPO advantage + KL modulation combination over per-token REINFORCE gradients.

05Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Theoretical motivation: the "infeasible weighted-average" theorem (Eq. A.7) is a real contribution — it explains why naïve OPSD collapses in RLVR.
  • No divergence-or-β sweep: multiplicative weighting removes the additive-distillation hyperparameter axis entirely.
  • Drop-in: one extra teacher forward pass per rollout and a per-token scalar multiply on top of GRPO.
  • Broad empirical sweep across math, code, single-turn, and multi-turn OOD with controlled baselines.
  • OOD result (Codeforces +5.2% after LCB training) is the most credible evidence the gains come from credit, not verifier overfit.
  • Honest overhead reporting: actor +51%, end-to-end +2–13%.

Limitations

  • Scale: only 8B with thinking disabled, ≤12 288-token responses. 32B+, longer, or reasoning-mode remain untested.
  • Reference quality bottleneck: needs ≥1 verified rollout per prompt. Hard prompts (base accuracy 5–10%) get no signal.
  • Threshold c is ad-hoc: the 1e-4 floor is a hand-tuned universal constant — task-sensitive and not principled.
  • No DAPO stack: benchmarked on plain GRPO, not DAPO's dynamic sampling / token-level loss. The 5.86% gap may compress when composed.
  • Single model family: only Qwen3-8B in the main results; no DeepSeek-R1-Distill or Llama-3.1 sweeps.
  • No code release confirmed in the text we could verify.

06Questions for further exploration

  • Does SC-GRPO compose with DAPO? The paper only stacks on plain GRPO. Composing with dynamic sampling and token-level loss is the obvious next measurement.
  • Does the multiplicative objective actually avoid the weighted-average pathology? The theorem says additive losses fail; the paper does not prove the multiplicative variant is safe. A convergence guarantee would close the loop.
  • Stress-test the solve-none diversity signal on long-horizon agentic tasks — the min-length truncation is a heuristic, and reward-hacking by short references is plausible.
  • Is the p75 floor robust at 32B+? The 1e-4 floor is a suspicious universal constant; the KL distribution shape almost certainly shifts with scale.
  • Can SC-GRPO extend to non-verifiable rewards? Replacing the verified trajectory with a preference pair is intriguing but the method fundamentally relies on a hard correctness signal.
  • How does it interact with test-time scaling (best-of-N, majority voting) and explicit reasoning modes (Qwen3 thinking enabled)?
  • A fair OPSD head-to-head would full fine-tune OPSD and use a stronger teacher (e.g. GPT-4o-mini) — the current LoRA-r=64 setup is probably under-selling the baseline.
