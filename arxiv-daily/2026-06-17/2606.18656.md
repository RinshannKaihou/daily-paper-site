# The Wrong Kind of Right: Quantifying and Localizing Misfired Alignment in LLMs

> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"Misfired Alignment（误触发对齐）"概念，构建 VETO 基准（2032 对来自 BBQ 的对比样本）量化 LLM 因安全对齐而拒绝有明确上下文证据支持的合理结论的现象，并提出 Misfired Alignment Rate (MAR) 指标；在 25 个 LLM 上观察到 4.7%–18.9% 的 MAR（人类 0%），机制分析发现该失败由后层对证据支持答案的"抑制电路"造成，并随指令微调而放大。

> **TL;DR:** This paper formalizes "misfired alignment" — the failure mode where safety-aligned LLMs override explicit contextual evidence on stereotype-related inputs. The authors build the VETO benchmark (2,032 BBQ-derived contrastive pairs across 8 demographic categories) and a 0–100 Misfired Alignment Rate (MAR) metric, then show that all 25 benchmarked LLMs (4.7%–18.9% MAR; humans 0%) exhibit it, with frontier models (GPT-5.4-nano 18.9%, GPT-5.4 17.6%, Claude-4.7-Opus 10.7%) often worst. A single-line alignment-priming prompt can increase MAR by up to +64 points, and logit-lens + attention-head ablations on three open-weight models (Llama-3.1-8B, Mistral-7B, Gemma-3-27B) localize the failure to a small set of late-layer "veto" attention heads that flip correct intermediate-layer predictions to incorrect ones — a circuit that emerges or amplifies after instruction tuning.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **"Misfired Alignment"** 概念：当 LLM 遇到与刻板印象群体相关的提示时，会用对齐的保守性压制有明确证据支持的正确结论；这与"过拒绝"（直接拒绝回答）不同，是"答错而非拒答"。
- 构建 **VETO 基准**：从 BBQ 的 disambiguated split 派生 2032 对二元（yes/no）对比样本，跨 8 个人口学维度（年龄、残障、性别认同、外貌、种族、宗教、SES、性取向），每对的 target 和 contrast 实例共享相同证据，仅目标个体不同。
- 定义 **Misfired Alignment Rate (MAR)**：模型在 contrast 实例上答对但在 target 实例上答错的条件概率；同时定义互补的 **Bias Rate (BR)** 指标。
- 在 25 个 LLM 上系统评估（Llama-3.x、Qwen2.5/3/3.5、Mistral、Gemma-3、DeepSeek-V3/R1、GPT-5.4/5.5、Claude-4.6/4.7、Gemini-3.1、Grok-4.20），发现全部 4.7%–18.9% MAR；7 名人类标注员在 512 条样本上 MAR = 0%，准确率 97.5%。
- **Alignment-priming 因果实验**：在 target 实例前加一句"It is not okay to assume [GROUP] [stereotype]."，MAR 在多数模型上增加 11.9–64.3 个百分点（最强增幅：Llama-3.2-3B +64.3、Qwen3-4B +62.9、Qwen2.5-7B +50.8）。
- **机制分析（logit-lens + attention-head ablation）**：在 Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct / Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3 / Gemma-3-27B-IT 上识别出"alignment-induced suppression circuit"——中间层已倾向正确答案（"yes"），最终层被翻转；峰值 logit gap 集中在最后 1–2 层（L24/32、L31/32、L61/62）。
- **因果消融验证**：仅消融 top-10 排名的对齐特异性注意力头即可恢复 80–83% 的失败案例（控制组保持 97–100% 准确率），说明抑制由少数专用头因果介导。
- **基座 vs 指令对比**：Llama-3.1-8B（1.3% → 6.2% MAR）、Gemma-3-27B（4.6% → 6.3% MAR）证实对齐后训练放大了该电路（Mistral-7B-v0.3 因基座模型本身答"no"占 78.4%、证据推理能力低而判为 inconclusive）。

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes **misfired alignment** as a distinct failure mode from conventional bias and from out-of-the-box over-refusal: the model does not refuse, it answers confidently — but wrong — when the question is about a stereotype-related entity despite unambiguous contextual evidence.
- Releases the **VETO benchmark**: 2,032 BBQ-derived contrastive yes/no pairs across 8 demographic categories, where target and contrast share identical evidence and differ only in the target entity.
- Defines two complementary metrics: **Misfired Alignment Rate (MAR)** and **Bias Rate (BR)**, enabling decomposition of the asymmetric failure (when a model is more likely to fail on stereotyped vs. non-stereotyped counterparts).
- Benchmarks 25 open- and closed-source LLMs (Llama, Mistral, Qwen, Gemma, DeepSeek, GPT-5.4/5.5, Claude-4.6/4.7, Gemini-3.1, Grok-4.20); all show non-trivial MAR (4.7–18.9%) while humans achieve 0% MAR.
- Provides **causal evidence** via an alignment-priming experiment: a single normative prefix can increase MAR by up to 64.3 points, demonstrating that misfired alignment is induced (not merely correlated) by surface safety cues.
- **Localizes the failure mechanistically** in three open-weight models using logit-lens and attention-head ablation, identifying a small set of late-layer "veto" attention heads that flip correct intermediate-layer predictions to wrong ones.
- **Traces provenance** to post-training: base → instruct comparison shows instruction tuning installs or amplifies the suppression circuit.

## Methodology

### VETO Construction
- Source: BBQ disambiguated split (Parrish et al. 2022), where context disambiguates the answer.
- For each BBQ item, build a **contrastive pair** (t_i, c_i): the target instance names a stereotype-related individual, the contrast instance names a non-stereotyped individual, with identical context and question.
- Both instances' context provides **unambiguous negative evidence** about the named person; the entailed answer is "yes" for both (e.g., "Is the [transgender woman / man] untrustworthy with kids?" → grounded answer: "Yes").
- Convert multiple-choice to binary yes/no by substituting the group into the question.
- Final composition: 2,032 pairs / 8 categories (Age 360, Disability 181, Gender 520, Physical appearance 163, Race 230, Religion 150, SES 320, Sexual orientation 108).

### Metrics
- **MAR (Misfired Alignment Rate):** P(t_i = 0 | c_i = 1) — failure on target given success on contrast.
- **BR (Bias Rate):** P(c_i = 0 | t_i = 1) — failure on contrast given success on target (the conventional bias direction).
- Both refusals and "no" treated as incorrect; in practice, models rarely refuse (only Mistral-7B-Instruct has 248 ill-formed answers; Gemini-3.1-Pro has 7 plain-text "no"s), so errors come from confident "no" answers.

### Models
- 25 instruction-tuned LLMs covering open-weight (Llama-3.1/3.2, Qwen2.5/3/3.5, Mistral-7B-v0.3, Gemma-3-27B, DeepSeek-V3/R1) and closed-weight (GPT-5.4 family, GPT-5.5, Claude-4.6/4.7, Gemini-3.1 Flash-Lite / Pro, Grok-4.20).
- Default: zero-shot direct prompting, no reasoning effort; DeepSeek-R1 and GPT-5.5 use default reasoning.
- Human baseline: 7 annotators on 512 inputs (97.5% accuracy, 0% MAR).

### Behavioral Analysis: Alignment-Priming
- Prepend a single normative clause "It is not okay to assume [GROUP] [stereotype]." to the target instance; contrast instance unchanged. The change isolates the causal effect of safety framing.
- Compare "base" (target vs. contrast) and "primed" (primed-target vs. contrast) MARs.

### Mechanistic Analysis
- Three open-weight models selected: Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3, Gemma-3-27B-IT.
- 60 pairs from priming results: 30 failure pairs (target wrong, contrast right) and 30 control pairs (both correct).
- **Logit-lens probing** to trace per-layer preference between "yes" and "no". Define a **handoff** as: an intermediate layer prefers "yes" while the final layer produces "no".
- **Attention-head ablation**: identify heads that are specific to the alignment-induced gap (contrast vs. stereotype logit-diff); ablate the top-k heads and measure whether the wrong answer flips to correct.

### Provenance: Base vs. Instruct
- Run same VETO pairs through matched base models (Llama-3.1-8B, Mistral-7B-v0.3, Gemma-3-27B) with a JSON scaffold prompt; restrict decoding to next token after opening quote.
- Compare per-layer contrast-vs-stereotype logit-difference gap between IT and PT (pre-trained) models.

## Results & Highlights

### Main MAR (Table 2, sorted by MAR, lower is better)
- **Worst MAR:** GPT-5.4-nano 18.9%, GPT-5.4 17.6%, Llama-3.2-3B 11.8%, Qwen2.5-72B 11.4%, Claude-4.6-Sonnet 10.9%, Claude-4.7-Opus 10.7%, Qwen3-8B 10.3%, Qwen3-14B 10.2%.
- **Best MAR:** DeepSeek-R1 4.7%, Gemini-3.1-Pro 4.9%, DeepSeek-V3-chat 5.2%, Qwen3.5-27B 5.5%, Qwen3.5-9B 5.5%, Mistral-7B 5.7%, Llama-3.1-8B 6.2%, Gemma-3-27B 6.3%.
- **Frontier ≠ best:** GPT-5.4 family and Claude models show the highest MAR (9.9–18.9%); open-weight and other API models range 4.7–11.8%.
- **Category patterns:** Disability status and Physical appearance dominate MAR; GPT/Claude/Llama families peak on disability (e.g., GPT-5.4 48.8% on disability, Llama-3.1-8B 28.2%), Qwen family peaks on physical appearance (e.g., Qwen3-32B 34.7%, Qwen2.5-72B 29.8%).
- **MAR vs. BR asymmetry:** 6 models have BR > MAR (Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct, Qwen3/3.5-4B, Grok-4.20, Mistral-7B, GPT-5.4-nano), 7 models have MAR > BR (GPT-5.4, GPT-5.5, Claude-4.6/4.7, Gemini-3.1-Flash-Lite/Pro, Qwen2.5-72B) — i.e., misfired alignment and conventional bias are complementary, not redundant.
- **Humans:** 0.0% MAR, 97.5% accuracy on 512 items.

### Alignment-Priming (Figure 2, MAR increase from base to primed)
- Largest increases: **Llama-3.2-3B +64.3, Qwen3-4B +62.9, Qwen2.5-7B +50.8, GPT-5.4-nano +43.3, Qwen3.5-4B +42.7, Qwen3-8B +38.4, Grok-4.20 +37.2, Llama-3.1-8B +35.6**.
- Even strong models affected: GPT-5.5 +11.7, Gemini-3.1-Pro +4.0, Claude-4.7-Opus +3.3, Claude-4.6-Sonnet +2.6 — no model is fully immune.
- Abstract claims "up to 8.9×" increase; the paper's actual range is +2.6 to +64.3 points.

### Mechanistic Handoff (Table 3)
- Handoff (intermediate "yes" → final "no") rate on failure pairs: **Gemma-3-27B-IT 97%, Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct 87%, Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3 50%**; control pairs: 0% handoff across all three.
- Peak contrast-vs-stereotype logit gap is in the final layers: Llama L24/32 (+22.7), Mistral L31/32 (+14.2), Gemma L61/62 (+25.9).

### Ablation Recovery (Table 4)
- Ablating top-1 / top-3 / top-5 / top-10 alignment-specificity attention heads:
  - Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct: 33% / 53% / 67% / 83% recovery; 97% control preserved at top-10.
  - Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.3: 57% / 73% / 73% / 80% recovery; 100% control preserved.
  - Gemma-3-27B-IT: 7% / 10% / 10% / 17% recovery; 87% control preserved.
- A small set of heads is causally responsible for suppression, not merely correlated.

### Base vs. Instruct (Figure 3)
- Llama-3.1-8B: 1.3% (base) → 6.2% (instruct).
- Gemma-3-27B: 4.6% (base) → 6.3% (instruct).
- Mistral-7B-v0.3: 12.3% (base) → 5.7% (instruct) — base has high MAR due to low evidence-reasoning competence (78.4% "no" answers on contrast), making the comparison inconclusive.
- Per-layer logit gap (Figure 4): IT models show a large failure-specific gap concentrated in late layers; base models show smooth curves with no late-layer divergence.

### Supplementary (Appendix, summarized)
- **Reasoning (Appendix F):** MAR amplifies in smaller LLMs, reduces in frontier ones.
- **In-context learning (Appendix E):** ICL only partially mitigates — does not resolve misfired alignment.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High. The "misfired alignment" framing — answering wrong (not refusing) when evidence warrants a different answer because the question targets a sensitive group — is a meaningfully distinct failure mode from conventional bias (answering wrong because of stereotypes) and from over-refusal (refusing benign questions). The contrastive yes/no design cleanly isolates the effect of the target entity. Combining behavioral evaluation (25 models) with mechanistic localization (logit-lens + ablation) on three open models is rare and well-executed.
- **Rigor:**
  - 25 models, 7 human annotators, 2,032 pairs across 8 categories — strong evaluation breadth.
  - 97.5% human accuracy rules out "the data is ambiguous" as a confound.
  - Contrastive pair design (same evidence, different entity) is a clean ablation of the safety-cue variable.
  - Ablation recovery on control pairs (97–100% preserved) shows the heads are task-specific, not generally necessary.
  - Statistical significance (BH-corrected) is reported for MAR-vs-BR differences (Appendix H).
- **Reproducibility:** Excellent — code, data, model outputs all released on the project page and HuggingFace.
- **Significance:** The paper makes a sharp, well-evidenced argument that current alignment methods install *shallow* safety heuristics that override evidence-based reasoning. The fact that frontier models (GPT-5.4 family, Claude-4.7-Opus) often show higher MAR than smaller or open models is a notable and somewhat counter-intuitive finding for the "bigger = safer" narrative.
- **Red flags and weaknesses:**
  - **Format reduction:** All items are reduced to a binary yes/no decision. This is acknowledged in the Limitations section, but it leaves open whether the phenomenon transfers to free-form generation, multi-turn dialogue, or stake-weighted outcomes.
  - **BBQ provenance and Western bias:** VETO inherits BBQ's U.S. English-speaking social contexts and protected groups. The paper explicitly notes this and flags non-Western / non-English / intersectional generalization as open. The "Gay men have AIDS" priming example is an extreme stereotype; results may not generalize to more subtle bias contexts.
  - **Closed-model mechanistic gap:** All mechanistic claims are from three open-weight models. The paper correctly notes this is an *inference* (not measurement) for frontier closed models. The behavioral priming results are consistent across both, but the suppression circuit story is not directly verified for GPT-5.4/Claude-4.7.
  - **Mistral-7B caveat:** The base-vs-instruct comparison for Mistral is explicitly flagged inconclusive. The framing still leans on the Llama and Gemma results (2 data points) for the "instruction tuning installs the circuit" claim, which is suggestive but not strong.
  - **Gemma-3-27B-IT ablation is weak:** Only 7–17% recovery from ablation, vs. 80–83% for Llama and Mistral. This means the localization story is much cleaner for Llama and Mistral than for Gemma — readers should not over-generalize the "small set of heads" claim to all open models.
  - **Priming amplification is large but possibly artificial:** A literal "It is not okay to assume [GROUP] [stereotype]." prefix is a strong, somewhat unrealistic prompt. The paper's argument is that the *change* in MAR isolates the causal effect of the cue, but real deployment rarely uses such overtly normative prefixes. The +64.3 Llama-3.2-3B jump should be read as an upper bound on cue sensitivity, not a deployment estimate.
  - **Direction of BR vs MAR is model-dependent:** Some models are biased against the stereotyped group (BR > MAR), others exhibit misfired alignment (MAR > BR). This is interesting but also slightly muddy — the paper frames MAR and BR as "complementary error models," but a practitioner just wants to know which direction the failure goes for which model family. A clearer synthesis would help.
  - **OpenAI funding:** Acknowledged in the paper, but readers may want to weigh the strong critique of GPT-5.4 family with this context.
- **Overclaiming check:** The paper is unusually careful — it explicitly says findings should not be interpreted as an argument against alignment, and the limitations section is honest. It does not claim the suppression circuit is universal or that MAR is the only relevant metric.

## Strengths

- Sharp, operationalizable definition of a failure mode (misfired alignment) that practitioners can measure and reason about.
- Large-scale, multi-family evaluation (25 models) with a strong human ceiling (0% MAR, 97.5% accuracy) that rules out data ambiguity.
- Contrastive pair design (target vs. contrast) is a clean experimental control.
- Combines behavioral (priming) and mechanistic (logit-lens + ablation) evidence — causal *and* localized story.
- Causal ablation: top-10 head ablation flips 80–83% of failure cases back to correct (Llama, Mistral), with control pairs preserved at 97–100%.
- Source code, data, and model outputs all publicly released; project page and GitHub/HF links provided.
- Honest framing: positions the work as a call for better alignment, not an attack on alignment.

## Limitations

- Binary yes/no format restricts generality to free-form generation, multi-turn dialogue, and stake-weighted decision-making (acknowledged by authors).
- VETO inherits BBQ's U.S./English-speaking cultural scope; non-Western contexts, non-English languages, and intersectional categories are not covered.
- Mechanistic analysis limited to three open-weight models; closed-model circuit-level mechanisms are inferred, not measured.
- Gemma-3-27B-IT shows much weaker ablation recovery (7–17%) than Llama (80–83%) and Mistral (80%), suggesting the "small set of heads" story is model-dependent.
- Base-vs-instruct provenance claim rests on two clean data points (Llama, Gemma); Mistral is inconclusive due to base-model competence issues.
- Alignment-priming prefix is overtly normative; real-deployment amplification may be smaller than the +64.3 upper bound.
- ICL only partially mitigates; reasoning amplifies MAR in smaller models (acknowledged but not deeply analyzed in the main text).
- VETO is in English only; cross-linguistic generalization is not addressed.

## Relevance

与"LLM 安全对齐"、"过度拒绝"和"机制可解释性"三个研究方向直接相关。论文将行为评估（MAR + 引导实验）和机制分析（logit-lens + 注意力头消融）结合，正面回应了"对齐训练是否真的让模型学到了公平推理、还是只学到表层安全启发式"这一开放问题，对关注模型可信度、对齐机制、interpretability 的读者非常有价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does misfired alignment transfer to **non-stereotype domains** of over-refusal (medical, legal, coding, or political-sensitive questions), or is it specifically about demographic cues?
- Why do **frontier models (GPT-5.4 family, Claude-4.7-Opus) often have higher MAR** than smaller / open models? Is this a regression driven by aggressive safety training, or a measurement artifact (the "stronger" model is more confidently wrong)?
- Can the **late-layer suppression circuit** be directly targeted during alignment training (e.g., via head-level regularization or circuit-aware RLHF) to preserve contextual grounding while still rejecting truly unsafe inferences?
- How does MAR correlate with **other safety benchmarks** (HarmBench, JailbreakBench, OR-Bench)? Are these signals complementary or redundant?
- Would **chain-of-thought / scratchpad elicitation** make the suppression circuit "visible" to the model and self-correctable, or does it just push the suppression to the final answer step?
- Does the phenomenon appear in **multilingual settings** where safety training data is uneven across languages?
- The +64.3 Llama-3.2-3B priming effect is striking — what is the **dose-response curve** between the strength of the safety cue and MAR amplification?
- Could MAR be turned into a **training-time loss** that penalizes asymmetric over-refusal on contrastive pairs?
- For Gemma-3-27B-IT (low ablation recovery), is the suppression distributed across many heads, or mediated by MLP layers or residual-stream directions instead?
