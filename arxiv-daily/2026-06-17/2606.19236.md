> **一句话总结：** STARE 通过对 GRPO 进行 token 级熵的一阶梯度分析，识别出 advantage–surprisal 四象限结构和「near-criticality」性质，进而用 batch 内 surprisal 分位选出熵关键 token 集并对其优势函数做单/双侧缩放，配合目标熵的闭环门控，在 1.5B–32B 模型与 Short/Long CoT、多轮工具使用三类任务上稳定数千步训练，并在 AIME24/25 上比 DAPO 等基线高 4%–8% 平均准确率。

> **TL;DR:** STARE is a minimally intrusive, token-level credit-rebalancing scheme that mitigates policy-entropy collapse in GRPO-style RLVR. A first-order gradient analysis decomposes per-token entropy variation as `dH/dη = -Âᵢ·Φ(p)`, exposing (a) an advantage–surprisal four-quadrant view and (b) a near-criticality property (`W* − 1 = O(T⁻¹)`), so a mild reweighting on a small high-surprisal subset suffices to flip the sign of batch entropy. STARE picks that subset via batch-internal surprisal quantiles (top-P% within positive- and negative-advantage tokens), scales their advantages (Variant O1 amplifies L⁺, Variant C2 additionally attenuates L⁻), and gates the intervention with a target-entropy closed-loop binary gate. On 1.5B–32B Qwen/DeepSeek models across Short CoT, Long CoT, and Multi-Turn Tool-Use, STARE sustains 1.5k–5k stable RL steps with entropy held near the target band, beats DAPO/EAPO/JustRL/STAPO by 4%–8% on AIME24/25, and produces longer, more reflective responses (Pass@32, response length, and reflection-token counts all grow together).

## 核心贡献

- **一阶梯度分析定位 GRPO 熵坍缩的 token 级根因：** 证明在 GRPO 非截断区域，token 级熵变化可分解为 `dH/dη = −Âᵢ·Φ(p)`，其中 `Âᵢ` 为轨迹级 advantage，`Φ(p) = p(ln p + H) − S₂/2` 是关于下一 token 分布的熵敏感度函数。由此推导出存在唯一临界 surprisal `s*` 将词表划为低/高 surprisal 区，并得到四象限分类（Corollary 3.3）。
- **「Near-Criticality」性质：** 通过 batch 级熵分解（Theorem 3.4 的 Entropy Neutrality Identity + Proposition 3.5）证明，仅需对高 surprisal 优势 token 子集 `L⁺` 的有效优势做 `W = 1 + O(T⁻¹)` 级别的乘性放大，即可翻转 batch 净熵梯度方向；超过临界点后，W 主要控制大小而非方向。
- **STARE 算法（三种变体）：** 在 GRPO 截断 surrogate 内对 entropy-critical token 的 advantage 加乘性权重 ωᵢ,ₜ ∈ {W, M, 1}（O1：仅放大 L⁺；O2–O4：单边另三种；C1–C4：组合两侧调节），再叠加 batch 级二元门控 `gₖ = 𝟙[H̄ₖ < H_tgt]`，使熵围绕目标带做有界振荡。
- **跨规模跨任务的稳定收益：** 1.5B–32B（Qwen2.5-Math-7B-Base / 14B-Instruct / 32B-Base、DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B、Qwen3-8B-Base、Qwen2.5-7B-Base + Retool2K）三大任务族上，STARE 稳定 1.5k–5k 步训练，AIME24/25 比 DAPO/GRPO-ds 等基线高 4%–8%，reflection token 与 response 长度同步增长。
- **可解释的探索性语义：** 词云显示被放大的 token 主要落在 "should be, but, instead, verification" 等不确定与自我修正类词汇，定量证明 STARE 真正激活了深层的反思式探索。

## Key Contributions

- **Token-level entropy gradient decomposition for GRPO:** dH/dη = −Âᵢ·Φ(p) (Theorem 3.1) yields a four-quadrant advantage–surprisal structure and isolates a unique critical surprisal threshold s* (Proposition 3.2, Corollary 3.3).
- **Near-criticality result (Corollary 3.6):** the critical reweighting magnitude W* − 1 = O(T⁻¹); beyond the threshold, W controls magnitude rather than sign, making STARE robust to hyperparameter choice.
- **Surprisal-guided advantage reweighting (STARE):** a batch-internal top-P% surprisal-quantile proxy identifies L⁺/L⁻ sets in O(sort), avoiding per-position root-finding of Φ(p*)=0; the reweighting lives entirely inside GRPO's clipped surrogate.
- **Closed-loop target-entropy gate:** a binary feedback gate gₖ = 𝟙[H̄ₖ < H_tgt] activates the reweighting only when entropy dips below target, automatically reverting to GRPO when entropy is healthy. Adaptive schedules (W_{k+1}, M_{k+1}) and per-sample/per-token variants are also studied.
- **Empirical wins at scale:** sustained 1.5k–5k step stable RL on 1.5B–32B models, three task families; +4%–8% on AIME24/25 over DAPO and other competitive baselines, with response length, reflection tokens, and Pass@32 all growing together.

## Methodology

**Theoretical setup.** Starting from GRPO's clipped surrogate (β=0 throughout), the authors write the per-token logit update `Δz_v = η Âᵢ (δ_{va} − π_v)` in the unclipped regime. Using the surprisal-deviation form of the entropy gradient (Lemma 2.1: `∂H/∂z_v = π_v (s_v − H)`), they show

  dH/dη|_{η=0} = − Âᵢ · Φ(p),   Φ(p) ≜ p(ln p + H) − S₂/2,   S₂ ≜ Σ_v π_v² (ln π_v + H).

**Four-quadrant view.** Because `Φ(0⁺) = −S₂/2 < 0` and `Φ(1) = H − S₂/2 > 0` and `Φ′(p) = ln p + H + 1` is strictly increasing, there is a unique p* (s* = −ln p*) where Φ flips sign. Combining with the sign of Âᵢ yields:

| | s < s* (low surprisal) | s > s* (high surprisal) |
|---|---|---|
| **Â > 0** | H↓ (majority) | H↑ (minority) ← entropy-critical L⁺ |
| **Â < 0** | H↑ (majority) | H↓ (minority) ← entropy-critical L⁻ |

The "asymmetric entropy contribution" observation: because rollouts are sampled from π_θ, low-surprisal tokens dominate the update count, so a single trajectory-level advantage systematically drives the distribution toward concentration — the paper's mechanistic diagnosis of entropy collapse.

**Near-criticality.** With L⁺ defined as positive-advantage, high-surprisal tokens, scaling their advantages by W > 1 yields batch net entropy shift `−(1/N)[Λ − (W−1)Γ]`, where Λ is the unweighted net entropy gradient and Γ = Σ_{L⁺} |Âᵢ Φ_{i,t}|. The Entropy Neutrality Identity (E_π[Φ(a)]=0) gives `|Λ|/Σ_abs = O(T⁻¹)`, so `W* − 1 = Λ/Γ = O(T⁻¹)`. In the Long-CoT and 7B+ regimes used in the paper, this is empirically ~1.1×.

**Algorithm.** Per-batch, STARE:
1. Computes `s_{i,t} = −ln π_θ(o_{i,t}|x_i, o_{i,<t})` for every token.
2. Within `T⁺` (Âᵢ > 0), takes the top-P% by surprisal as `L⁺`; analogously `L⁻` within `T⁻` (default P = 10%).
3. Applies `ω_{i,t} = 1 + gₖ(W−1)` for `(i,t) ∈ L⁺`, and optionally `ω_{i,t} = 1 − gₖ(1−M)` for `L⁻` (default W=1.1, M=0.9 in two-sided variant).
4. Computes the gate `gₖ = 𝟙[H̄ₖ < H_tgt]` (default H_tgt = 0.3).
5. Multiplies the GRPO clipped surrogate objective by ω before taking the per-token min, preserving gradient sign and clip-range.

**Variants explored.** O1 (default, amplify L⁺), O2/O3/O4 (three other single-polarity operations), C1–C4 (two-sided combined). The paper also studies adaptive weights `W_{k+1} = clip(W_k + α·sgn(H_tgt − H̄_k), [1, W_max])` and per-sample / per-token gating (Appendix G.6).

## Results & Highlights

- **Stability:** Sustained 1.5k RL steps on 14B/32B and 5k+ on 7B; policy entropy held near H_tgt = 0.3 with bounded oscillation, while GRPO-ds collapses to ≈0 within 500–1000 steps.
- **Accuracy on AIME24 / AIME25 (vs DAPO / GRPO-ds):**
  - 7B (Qwen2.5-Math-7B-Base, Short CoT): 44.2 / 23.8 (STARE-O1) vs 35.7 / 17.1 (DAPO) → roughly +8% / +7%.
  - 14B (Qwen2.5-14B-Instruct, Short CoT): 30.8 / 27.1 (STARE-O1) vs GRPO-ds 23.4 / 19.1 → +7% / +8%.
  - 32B (Qwen2.5-32B-Base, Short CoT): 44.3 / 33.4 (STARE-O1) vs GRPO-ds 38.5 / 22.9 → +6% / +10%.
  - 1.5B (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B, Long CoT): 53.8 / 41.5 (STARE-O1) vs 40.9 / 28.6 (DAPO) → +13% / +13%.
  - 8B (Qwen3-8B-Base, Long CoT): 43.9 / 34.7 (STARE-O1) vs 39.5 / 30.8 (GRPO-ds) → +4% / +4%.
  - 7B (Qwen2.5-7B-Base + Retool2K, Tool-Use): 53.2 / 37.5 (STARE-O1) vs 46.8 / 32.4 (GRPO-ds) → +6% / +5%.
- **Average accuracy across six math benchmarks (AIME24, AIME25, AMC23, MATH-500, Minerva, Olympiad):** STARE-O1 achieves 54.4% (7B), 52.0% (14B), 60.7% (32B), 65.9% (1.5B), 62.0% (8B), 59.4% (7B tool-use) — best in class against EAPO, STEER, GRPO-ds, DAPO, 80/20 Rule, STAPO, JustRL, SimpleTIR, etc.
- **Exploration indicators:** Pass@32 stays strictly above GRPO-ds throughout training; response length grows monotonically; reward and Full-Solve Ratio continue to rise (GRPO-ds plateaus).
- **Hyperparameter robustness:** W ∈ [1.05, 1.5] and P ∈ [5%, 20%] all keep entropy in band; open-loop W ≥ 2.0 diverges (corroborating near-criticality).
- **Eight-way variant ablation:** All four single-polarity (O1–O4) and all four combined (C1–C4) operations beat GRPO-ds on AIME24/25; O1 and C2 are the strongest.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The four-quadrant advantage–surprisal decomposition is genuinely useful and arguably the cleanest mechanistic diagnosis of GRPO entropy collapse to date. It is closely related to concurrent work (e.g., Cui et al. 2025 "entropy mechanism", Cheng et al. 2025 "Reasoning with Exploration", He et al. 2026 "polarity-entropy"), so the paper is best read as part of an active research front rather than a singular breakthrough. The near-criticality result is the strongest theoretical claim and is more original than the four-quadrant view itself.
- **Rigor:** The first-order Taylor expansion is standard; the proofs are deferred to Appendices D–F (entropy neutrality identity, near-criticality bound) — these need a careful read to confirm the O(T⁻¹) rate is tight and holds under realistic off-policy / clipping regimes (the proofs assume unclipped updates, which is partially violated when importance ratios clip). Empirical results include ablation curves, hyperparameter sweeps, and eight-way variant ablations, which is good.
- **Reproducibility:** Code release plus a fixed configuration (W=1.1, M=0.9, H_tgt=0.3, P=10%) is helpful. However, the 5k-step runs on 32B and the multi-turn tool-use setup require substantial compute that academic replications will struggle with. Seed variance is not deeply explored in the main text; most curves show single runs.
- **Significance:** Entropy collapse is the single most important practical bottleneck for GRPO-style post-training; a principled, gradient-grounded fix with strong empirical evidence is broadly useful. STARE is minimally intrusive (a single multiplicative ω), so adoption cost is low.
- **Red flags to watch:**
  1. The cited baselines are sometimes reproduced from prior papers (marked †) rather than re-run under identical conditions; the +4–8% headline is averaged over multiple benchmarks, so per-benchmark variance is real (e.g., 14B Minerva: STARE-O1 40.5 vs GRPO-Clipν 40.2 — essentially tied).
  2. Average accuracy of 54.4% on Qwen2.5-Math-7B-Base is impressive, but on 14B the gap with the strong JustRL/EntroAdv baseline isn't reported (JustRL is reported on 1.5B only) — incomplete head-to-head at intermediate scales.
  3. The "stability" claim is single-seed; AIME24/25 are notoriously noisy at small batch sizes.
  4. β=0 throughout means STARE is benchmarked against GRPO without KL regularization; behavior with a non-zero KL reference (the common production setting) is not directly tested.
  5. The O1 / C2 distinction is small in practice (e.g., 7B 54.4 vs 54.5), which is consistent with the theory but somewhat undersells the dual-sided story.
  6. The proof of near-criticality requires the unclipped regime and large T; importance-ratio clipping can locally break both assumptions.

## Strengths

- **Principled:** derives the intervention from a closed-form gradient decomposition rather than heuristics.
- **Plug-in:** adds a single multiplicative token weight inside the existing GRPO clipped surrogate — no architectural or reward-shaping changes.
- **Cross-scale, cross-task:** 1.5B–32B × {Short CoT, Long CoT, Multi-Turn Tool-Use} is the most thorough evaluation matrix in this niche.
- **Closed-loop safety:** the binary target-entropy gate prevents both collapse and divergence; confirmed empirically across a wide W range.
- **Diagnostic transparency:** the four-quadrant view, the eight-variant ablation, the validation of the surprisal-quantile proxy (60%→95% alignment with theoretical s*), and the word cloud of reweighted tokens all support the mechanistic claim rather than just the headline numbers.
- **Public code** at https://github.com/hp-luo/STARE.

## Limitations

- Theoretical results assume the unclipped, on-policy regime; PPO-style clipping in GRPO can violate the assumptions underpinning the O(T⁻¹) near-criticality rate.
- Single-seed runs dominate the main-text figures; AIME24/25 noise is well-known.
- Baselines mix newly-run (STARE, GRPO-ds) and cited-from-prior-work (†) numbers; the latter are not always re-run with identical compute budgets.
- β = 0 (no KL) throughout — most production RLVR pipelines use a non-zero KL; STARE's interaction with KL regularization is not characterized.
- The target entropy H_tgt = 0.3 is treated as a fixed hyperparameter, though the paper does provide an adaptive variant and a brief ablation.
- The Long-CoT setting depends on tool-derived reasoning traces; the cold-start SFT data and 100K-sample mixture composition are summarized but not ablated.
- The paper does not evaluate on non-reasoning RLVR (alignment, summarization, code generation with execution feedback), so the generality claim beyond math/tool-use is not directly supported.

## Relevance

与本项目关注的大模型 RLVR 后训练、训练稳定性直接相关。STARE 从一阶梯度分析的角度系统化了 GRPO 熵坍缩机制，并给出一个低侵入、与现有 GRPO 流水线即插即用的优势重加权方案，对需要在长程 RL 后训练中维持探索-利用平衡的研究与工程实践均具有较高参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does STARE behave when composed with a non-zero KL penalty (the standard production setting) — does the closed-loop gate's H_tgt need to be re-tuned, or does the KL term already prevent collapse?
- Does the near-criticality rate `W* − 1 = O(T⁻¹)` survive importance-ratio clipping and off-policy updates? (Most 1.5k–5k step runs in the paper stay close to on-policy; behavior under aggressive clip-higher or async rollouts is untested.)
- Can the four-quadrant view explain non-reasoning RLVR failure modes (alignment, summarization, code generation with execution feedback), or is the effect size math-specific because of unusually peaked output distributions?
- What is the interaction between STARE and curriculum learning / staged reward shaping in long-horizon tool-use agents? Does the gate's binary nature cause oscillation in the multi-turn setting, and would per-token or per-sample gating be more appropriate?
- Could the same surprisal-quantile proxy be used as a diagnostic / observability tool (e.g., for monitoring training health) independent of applying the reweighting?
- How sensitive is final accuracy to seed variance and to the composition of the 100K-sample training mixture (DeepScaler + Skywork-o1 + Polaris + DAPO)?
- Is there a theoretical link between the O(T⁻¹) near-criticality rate and the effective context length T in long-CoT, suggesting that as context windows grow the required W should decrease?
