> **一句话总结：** 提出 Large Language Gibbs (LL-Gibbs)，把 LLM 的 next-token 条件分布定义为 Gibbs 转移算子，对结构化变量做迭代重采样；理论上证明平稳分布 $q^\mathrm{sym}$ 是 LLM 在所有变量排列下联合似然的均匀平均，并在简单分布采样、TruthfulQA/GSM8K 一致性推理、BnRep 贝叶斯结构学习三个任务上验证其优于直接自回归生成与 ICM 等启发式方法。

> **TL;DR:** Large Language Gibbs (LL-Gibbs) casts an LLM's next-token conditionals as the transition operator of a Gibbs-style MCMC over structured variables. The key move is to serialise variables in a *random* order at every resample step, which makes the resulting kernel a mixture of autoregressive conditionals and gives a clean target: the stationary distribution $q^\mathrm{sym}$, the LM's joint likelihood averaged uniformly over all $n!$ permutations of the variables. Under a permutation-invariance assumption, $q^\mathrm{sym}$ is provably the unique stationary distribution. The paper also introduces two Metropolis-within-Gibbs variants (Barker and Gambling) and a block-Gibbs scheme, then demonstrates three applications: (i) sampling from $\mathcal U[\{0,\dots,99\}]$ and $\mathcal N(0,1)$ with Llama-3.1-8B, where LL-Gibbs beats both independent and batched sampling in bias/autocorrelation; (ii) consistent multi-question reasoning on TruthfulQA and GSM8K-Verification, where LL-Gibbs with $n=64$ clearly beats Internal Coherence Maximisation (ICM); and (iii) Bayesian structure learning on four BnRep networks, where LL-Gibbs-generated synthetic data serves as an LLM-informed prior that helps DAG-GFlowNet in the small-data regime.

## 核心贡献
- 提出 **Large Language Gibbs (LL-Gibbs)**：把 LLM 的 next-token 条件分布重新用作单变量 Gibbs 转移算子，每次迭代随机打乱变量顺序重新序列化，从而消除自回归生成中的顺序偏差。
- 形式化给出**目标平稳分布** $q^\mathrm{sym}(X) \triangleq \mathbb E_\sigma[p_\mathrm{LM}(\mathrm{seq}(X,\sigma))]$（变量所有排列上的均匀平均），并证明在置换不变性 / Assumption 3.1 下，LL-Gibbs 链具有唯一的平稳分布 $q^\mathrm{sym}$（Proposition 3.2）。
- 提出 **Metropolis-within-Gibbs 变体**：
  - *Barker Gibbs*：使用 LLM 的判别性偏好作为 Metropolis 接受概率，避免依赖 RLHF 之后可能失校的概率；
  - *Gambling Gibbs*：让 LLM 在确定性阈值下做"下注"判断，只需贪婪解码，不依赖概率校准。
- 给出**实用变体**：sweeping（固定顺序循环）与 block Gibbs（每次重采样 $B>1$ 个变量），并分析其对平稳分布的影响。
- 在三类任务上系统验证 LL-Gibbs：简单分布采样、一致性推理（TruthfulQA/GSM8K-Verification）、贝叶斯结构学习（BnRep），并实证分析 $\gamma$、$B$、shuffling 的作用。

## Key Contributions
- **Algorithm.** Large Language Gibbs — a Gibbs-style MCMC scheme in which each resample step uses the LLM's next-token conditional over a single variable, with the *other* variables serialised in a fresh random order at every step. This makes the kernel a permutation-mixture of autoregressive conditionals rather than a single order's conditional.
- **Theory.** A formal target distribution $q^\mathrm{sym}(X) = \mathbb E_\sigma[p_\mathrm{LM}(\mathrm{seq}(X,\sigma))]$ is proposed, and under an invariance assumption (Assumption 3.1) the chain's unary conditionals match $q^\mathrm{sym}$'s, so $q^\mathrm{sym}$ is the unique stationary distribution (Proposition 3.2). This provides probabilistic justification for the random shuffling step.
- **Algorithmic variants.** Barker Gibbs (LLM-as-Metropolis-acceptance via discriminative comparison) and Gambling Gibbs (a "bet" prompt that needs only greedy decoding), plus sweeping and block-Gibbs variants with analogous stationarity arguments.
- **Empirical evidence.**
  - For $\mathcal U[\{0,\dots,99\}]$ and $\mathcal N(0,1)$, independent LLM sampling is biased, batched sampling has high autocorrelation, and LL-Gibbs substantially reduces both (Figs. 2–3).
  - For TruthfulQA and GSM8K-Verification with Llama-3.1-8B / OLMo-3-32B, LL-Gibbs with $n=64$ beats ICM by large margins (e.g., Llama 0.74 vs ICM 0.70 on TruthfulQA; 0.90 vs 0.72 on GSM8K-Verification).
  - For Bayesian structure learning on four BnRep networks, LLM-generated synthetic data used as a prior improves DAG-GFlowNet posterior over a uniform prior, with LL-Gibbs data outperforming Direct (one-pass) sampling on most datasets.
- **Reproducibility.** Code is released (`hyeok9855/large-language-gibbs`); prompts and exact burn-in / thinning / block-size choices are documented in the appendix.

## Methodology

### Set-up
A structured object $X=(X_1,\dots,X_n)$ where each $X_i$ has finite (or discretised) support $X_i$ and is associated with a natural-language descriptor $c_i$. The LM's next-token distribution $p_\mathrm{LM}(w_j\mid w_{1:j-1})$ is the only access to the joint.

### Basic kernel
At each step: sample $i\sim\mathrm{Unif}(\{1,\dots,n\})$; sample a bijection $\sigma_{-i}$ that orders the remaining $n-1$ variables uniformly; draw
$$X_i^{(t)}\sim p_\mathrm{LM}\!\big(X_i\mid \mathrm{seq}(X_{-i}^{(t-1)},\sigma_{-i})\oplus[c_i]\big),$$
i.e., the variable to resample is placed last in the serialised prompt, with the other variables in a random order. Structured-output decoding (e.g., vLLM) enforces per-variable constraints (e.g., `int[0,24]`).

### Target distribution and stationarity
Define $q^\mathrm{sym}(X)=\mathbb E_\sigma[p_\mathrm{LM}(\mathrm{seq}(X,\sigma))]$. Proposition 3.2 says: under the condition that the joint likelihood (averaged over orderings with $X_i$ fixed at position $n$) equals the full averaged joint, the chain's unary conditionals equal the unary conditionals of $q^\mathrm{sym}$, so $q^\mathrm{sym}$ is the unique stationary distribution. Full permutation invariance is a sufficient (but not necessary) condition.

### Discriminative variants
- **Barker Gibbs.** Replace the direct resample with: draw $X_i'\sim\mathrm{Unif}(X_i)$, ask the LM which of $\{X_i',X_i\}$ is more plausible, and accept $X_i'$ with Barker's probability. Stationarity is preserved if the LM's preference follows Barker's rule.
- **Gambling Gibbs.** Reframe the choice as a $V$-dollar bet that pays $100 if correct. The LM accepts/rejects deterministically. This needs no calibrated probabilities — only a threshold — and hence is more robust to RLHF-induced miscalibration. (In practice, the authors report that 8B-scale models are poor gamblers for *numerical* gambling, but chain-of-thought + larger models help.)

### Practical variants
- **Sweeping.** Cycle variables in a fixed order (faster mixing) — the stationary distribution may differ.
- **Block Gibbs.** Update $B>1$ variables at a time, autoregressively within the block, useful for correlated variables.

### Three demonstrations
1. **Sampling from simple distributions.** Represent the joint as $n=16$ i.i.d. variables from a target (uniform on $\{0,\dots,99\}$ or $\mathcal N(0,1)$); compare independent, batched, and Gibbs sampling with Llama-3.1-8B (base + instruct).
2. **Consistent reasoning.** Each variable is a binary answer to a question-claim pair. With $m$ questions per partition and 4 claims per question, $n=4m$ (4, 16, or 64). 25 Gibbs samples per claim → majority vote. Baselines: zero-shot greedy, 1-Pass, 4-Pass, ICM ($n=64$, annealed temperature).
3. **Bayesian structure learning.** Generate synthetic data $D^\mathrm{LLM}$ from the LM, then define an LLM-informed prior $P(G\mid D^\mathrm{LLM},\gamma)\propto P_0(G)P(D^\mathrm{LLM}\mid G)^\gamma$ and train a DAG-GFlowNet on the combined posterior $P(G\mid D;D^\mathrm{LLM},\gamma)$. Evaluated by E-SHD and AUROC against the ground-truth DAG on 4 BnRep networks.

## Results & Highlights

### Simple distribution sampling (Figs. 2–3, §4)
- **Llama-3.1-8B (base):** independent sampling has high bias (e.g., concentrates on small integers), batch sampling has correct marginals but high autocorrelation (max |ACF|), LL-Gibbs is a Pareto improvement — both lower bias *and* lower autocorrelation.
- **Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct:** instruction tuning introduces its own bias; batch and Gibbs partially correct it. **Gambling Gibbs does not work for numerical values at 8B scale** — the model accepts/rejects nearly at random; partially fixable with chain-of-thought + larger models (Fig. 5, §C.1.1).
- **Block Gibbs ($B=4$):** explicitly helps when the "i.i.d." assumption is loose, by escaping local modes.

### Consistent reasoning (Table 1, §5.1)
Base-model accuracy (n=64 vs ICM n=64, mean over 3 seeds):

| Task | Model | ICM | LL-Gibbs n=64 |
|---|---|---|---|
| TruthfulQA | Llama-3.1-8B | 0.702 | **0.736** |
| TruthfulQA | OLMo-3-32B | 0.662 | **0.743** |
| GSM8K-Verification | Llama-3.1-8B | 0.724 | **0.895** |
| GSM8K-Verification | OLMo-3-32B | 0.750 | **0.840** |

Monotonic improvement 1-Pass → 4-Pass → Gibbs (n=4) → Gibbs (n=16) → Gibbs (n=64) confirms that variable shuffling is the key ingredient. Instruction-tuned models show similar (sometimes larger) gaps (Table 2, §C.2).

### Bayesian structure learning (Fig. 4, §5.2)
On the four BnRep datasets (E-SHD ↓, AUROC ↑ at $\gamma=0.5$):
- *tubercolosis* (n=5): Gibbs beats both Uniform and Direct; Direct-Inst. can fail outright.
- *knowledge* (n=6): Gibbs clearly best on both metrics; Direct-Inst. is *worse* than Uniform on E-SHD.
- *disputed1* (n=11) and *consequenceCovid* (n=15): Gibbs competitive; gains shrink as $n$ grows (more variables, weaker LLM signal).
- $\gamma$ ablation (Table 3): $\gamma=0.2$–$0.5$ sweet spot; $\gamma=1.0$ (overweighting synthetic data) often falls below Uniform — a nice honesty point that LLMs are imperfect experts.

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty.** LL-Gibbs is *not* a brand-new idea — masked-LM Gibbs (Wang & Cho 2019; Yamakoshi et al. 2022; Torroba Hennigen & Kim 2023) and prior LLM Gibbs for structured tasks (Zhu et al. 2024; Xu et al. 2024) exist. The paper's incremental novelty is (i) the explicit target $q^\mathrm{sym}$ and Proposition 3.2 tying the chain to a permutation-averaged joint, (ii) the Barker / Gambling variants, and (iii) the structured-inference applications (especially the BnRep prior elicitation). The contribution is mostly *formalisation + breadth of application* rather than a new algorithmic idea.
- **Rigor.**
  - Stationarity is proven under an explicit (if somewhat strong) assumption; the empirical story is consistent with it but convergence *rate* is not analysed.
  - The theoretical gap between $q^\mathrm{sym}$ and "the joint the user actually wants" is never discussed. For Bayesian structure learning, the LLM is supposed to encode a *world prior*; the paper shows that Gibbs-sampled synthetic data is more consistent with itself than direct data, but it does not formally connect $q^\mathrm{sym}$ to $P(G\mid D)$ or characterise when this is a valid prior.
  - The "consistency" tasks use a synthetic reformulation of TruthfulQA/GSM8K (claim verification across related questions), not the standard tasks. This is a defensible re-framing but not the same as improving TruthfulQA per se.
- **Reproducibility.** Code is released; prompts and chain hyperparameters are documented; H100 80GB is reasonable. The main reproducibility risks are (i) LM stochasticity with temperature 1.0 (they report 3 seeds, std seems small), (ii) prompt sensitivity — they fix prompts and don't ablate, and (iii) the ICM baseline has a known bug (trailing whitespace, logical-consistency term that effectively uses ground truth), which they fix; a re-implementer using the original Wen et al. (2025) code would compare against a *worse* baseline.
- **Significance.** For researchers who want to use LLMs as structured priors, the permutation-averaged joint + Proposition 3.2 is a useful conceptual handle. The Bayesian structure learning application is the most novel practical contribution. The consistency-reasoning results are strong but the task framing is narrow.
- **Red flags.**
  - **Bayes-optimal baselines are absent.** For uniform/Gaussian sampling, the "correct" answer is a deterministic function; no such oracle is shown. For Bayesian structure learning, no method that *knows* $D$ alone is plotted (only Uniform prior + DAG-GFlowNet), so the marginal value of the LLM prior is hard to read off.
  - **Cherry-picked ablation points.** The 4 BnRep networks are small (5–15 nodes) and the LLM-signal strength is the dominant variable; the conclusion that "LL-Gibbs beats Direct" rests largely on the easier two networks where the gap is large.
  - **Gambling Gibbs "fails" at 8B scale but "works" at larger scale** — this is reported but the larger-scale results are in the appendix only (Fig. 5), without quantitative comparison.
  - **Bayesian structure learning's evaluation uses the same ground-truth graph for E-SHD that the algorithm never sees**, which is fine, but the metric is sensitive to Markov equivalence — SHD can punish a Markov-equivalent graph harshly. The paper does not discuss this.
  - **Inconsistent use of base vs. instruct models.** Some baselines (Direct-Inst., ICM) require instruct; LL-Gibbs uses the base. The comparison is therefore "Gibbs-base" vs "Direct-instruct", which is an apples-to-oranges pairing even if the base model is the LLM they care about.

## Strengths
- Clean theoretical story: random shuffling → $q^\mathrm{sym}$ is the unique stationary distribution (under an explicit assumption).
- Variants cover a wide range of LM behaviours: generative (basic), discriminative calibrated (Barker), discriminative threshold (Gambling), single-site (basic) and block (block Gibbs), random order vs sweep.
- Three independent application domains, with code + prompts released, and 3-seed std reported.
- Several honesty points the authors raise themselves: Gambling Gibbs doesn't work at 8B, ICM baseline had bugs, over-weighting the LLM ($\gamma=1$) hurts structure learning, gambling reasoning "attempts to deduce the correct acceptance rate by computing density ratios" only with CoT.
- The "shuffling is essential" demonstration (1-Pass → 4-Pass → Gibbs) is a clean, falsifiable abalation.

## Limitations
- **Compute cost.** $T$ Gibbs iterations each require $O(n)$ LM calls, vs. 1 for one-pass generation; for the $n=64$ reasoning experiment this is a ~64× cost multiplier. The paper acknowledges this but does not quantify wall-clock.
- **Limited model scale.** Experiments use Llama-3.1-8B and OLMo-3-32B; both are small relative to frontier reasoning models, and the consistent-reasoning gains may not transfer.
- **Theoretical gap.** The chain is consistent with $q^\mathrm{sym}$, but $q^\mathrm{sym}$ itself need not match any "true" joint. The paper does not bound the divergence between $q^\mathrm{sym}$ and an oracle distribution for the structured inference task.
- **Bayesian structure learning scale.** $n\le 15$ nodes, $\le 200$ synthetic samples. Real causal discovery problems can have $n\sim 50$–$1000$, where LLM conditionals become noisier and the chain harder to mix.
- **Prompt brittleness.** No prompt ablation. Structured-output (JSON) decoding is used everywhere, which constrains the chain but also papers over failure modes.
- **Gambling Gibbs** is essentially a curiosity at 8B; the headline result depends on basic / Barker Gibbs.

## Relevance
该工作把 LLM 与经典 MCMC 系统地结合，为"用 LLM 表达世界先验 + 在其上做概率一致的迭代推理"提供了带理论保证的框架（$q^\mathrm{sym}$ + Proposition 3.2），并展示了 LLM 合成数据作为贝叶斯结构学习先验的可行性；对 LLM 推理一致性、约束解码、LLM-as-prior 等研究方向有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- Can LL-Gibbs be combined with prompt-level self-consistency or chain-of-thought, or does the chain implicitly subsume CoT? (The §5.1 setup does not use CoT in the per-variable resamples.)
- How sensitive is the chain's mixing time to $n$? Is there empirical evidence of a "critical $n$" beyond which the chain stalls, and does block Gibbs help predictably?
- Can the random-shuffling step be replaced with a learned order (e.g., a GFlowNet or attention-based scheduler) to accelerate mixing while keeping $q^\mathrm{sym}$ as the target?
- For Bayesian structure learning, what is the empirical scaling of LL-Gibbs prior benefit as $|D|$ grows? Does the LLM prior add zero marginal value once $|D|$ is large?
- Gambling Gibbs is intriguing — does it work reliably at frontier scale (e.g., 70B+) for non-numerical gambling, and could it replace reward-model-based reranking for structured generation?
- How does LL-Gibbs compare to MCMC-with-MCTS / particle filters / sequential Monte Carlo for the same structured tasks? These are all "iterative" alternatives that the paper does not benchmark against.
- Is there a convergence diagnostic (e.g., potential scale reduction factor over multiple chains, or trace-rank statistics) that practitioners can use to know when to stop the chain? The paper uses fixed burn-in + thinning only.
