Why this matters

The first systematic adversarial baseline for whether cheap, always-on hardware telemetry can actually enforce AI compute governance — and the honest white-box floor regulators should plan around.

— Relevance · ICML 2026 TAIGR Workshop
Critical edge

LoRA evasion stays under 50% detected — economically attractive for small evaders. And the 43–87% white-box number is a lower bound on what a fully informed insider can hide.

— Limitations & Red Flags

Methodology

1
9 NVML counters · 1 Hz, zero overhead
GPU util, mem util, mem used MB, power W, temperature, SM clock, mem clock, PCIe TX/RX — always-available since Pascal.
2
Two-layer feature design
Roofline layer (mean power, peak mem) localises workloads in arithmetic-intensity space; temporal layer (autocorrelation, CV, periodicity) resolves within-region ambiguity.
3
Two-stage RandomForest
400 trees, no max depth, 30 s windows / 15 s stride. Chosen via CV over XGBoost, SVM-RBF, logistic regression. Calibrated P(training) output.
4
5-round monitor–evader protocol
Attacker designs new strategies each round; defender hardens. Six technique classes, 20 families — incl. utilisation modulation, memory mimicry, training-as-inference, multi-GPU DDP, and white-box attacks.
5
Leave-one-strategy-out evaluation
Train on real data + all other adversarial families; test on the held-out one — measures generalisation to unseen evasion, not memorisation.

Dataset at a glance

1,404 runs · 162 workloads · 117 GPU-hours · 445,310 readings × 9 signals. Composition: 867 ML-training (incl. 421 adversarial) · 352 inference (49 image, 264 LLM incl. DeepSeek-V3/R1, Qwen-2.5, Mixtral) · 185 non-ML (HPC, mining, render, idle).

What flips the white-box gap

  • Power–memory correlation & cumulative-energy features — decisive in R3
  • Pre-allocation memory-plateau features — added after R5
  • More matched-size dense-LLM-inference data in training set

Cross-architecture transfer

100% on 8 of 9 GPUs in leave-one-GPU-out; worst-case 99.4% on B200. Consumer → datacenter transfer falls below 50% — acknowledged as not relevant to governance.

Strengths

  • Genuine 5-round red-team protocol with white-box and multi-GPU arms race
  • Cross-architecture coverage — 9 GPUs across Ampere → Blackwell (4 generations)
  • Zero-overhead, privacy-preserving — a meaningful lower bound on what governance monitors can do
  • Honest reporting of white-box degradation (43–87%) rather than cherry-picking best-case
  • Calibrated probabilistic output — tunable sensitivity vs. FPR
  • Open-source workloads + explicit invitation to community red-teaming

Limitations

  • 43–87% on unseen white-box is still a wide gap from regulator-grade reliability
  • LoRA evasion < 50% — economically attractive for small evaders
  • Cross-provider compute structuring (Heim et al. 2024) not evaluated
  • All adversarial strategies are author-designed — external red-team rounds pending
  • 1 Hz sampling misses sub-second evasion (e.g. the 4 s / 5 s window-boundary attack)
  • HEM requirements (tamper-resistant read path, authenticated channel, secure boot) are not all available in current hardware
  • Multi-GPU result tests only 4 specific DDP/stagger strategies

Questions for further exploration

  • How robust is the classifier against a continuously-adaptive white-box adversary (online RL evader with full classifier access)?
  • Can LoRA evasion be closed by feature engineering for low-memory fine-tuning signatures — or is LoRA fundamentally cheap enough to evade NVML-based detection?
  • Does multi-tenant (cloud) isolation noise degrade accuracy, and at what point?
  • Which of the three HEM requirements (tamper-resistant read path, authenticated channel, secure boot) is technically feasible in the next 2 years?
  • Can the 1 Hz sampling rate be safely increased (10 Hz / 100 Hz) without re-introducing overhead, and does that close the window-boundary attacks?
  • How does the classifier behave on non-NVIDIA hardware (AMD MI300, Intel Gaudi, custom accelerators)?
  • Does the power–memory correlation feature generalise across cooling profiles and datacenter power-management policies?
