> **一句话总结：** 提出 MAST（Mechanism-Aligned Selective Targeting），通过 "非主成分能量 × 更新幅度 × 遗忘梯度耦合" 对注意力投影张量排序，仅更新 top-96/112 子集以遗忘 RLVR 注入的特定推理能力；机制诊断显示 SFT 与 SFT→RLVR increment 在 token-level Δlog-prob 上结构截然不同（前者是 +0.45 偏置的似然提升，后者是近似 0 的方向性重分布），而标准全参数梯度上升在 RLVR checkpoint 上不存在"干净工作点"——只能通过摧毁 retain/GSM8K 来达成遗忘。MAST 在 Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B 和 Qwen3-1.7B-Base 两个模型族、三个种子、GA/NPO/SimNPO 三种目标函数上稳定呈现近零连带损害的 Pareto 优势。

> **TL;DR:** MAST diagnoses the SFT-to-RLVR increment as direction-specific and off-principal (Δlog-prob 0.45 SFT vs ≈0 RLVR; positive-Δ fraction 0.73 SFT vs 0.40 balanced for RLVR), shows that full-parameter gradient ascent on the RLVR checkpoint has no clean operating point (collapses at η≥7e-6 on Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B and at η≥8e-6 on Qwen3), and proposes ranking attention-projection tensors by a mechanism score s_t = ρ_t · log(1+‖Δt‖²_F) · (1+|c_t|) to update only the top-96/112 subset. The result is statistically significant target forgetting (MATH-forget 45→37/150, McNemar p=0.0078) with collateral drops statistically indistinguishable from zero (retain 95% CI [-4.0, +5.0] pp; GSM8K [-3.1, +1.6] pp), a ranking-specificity advantage of +2.34 pp GSM8K over a same-size random subset, and a cross-model +12.9 pp GSM8K advantage over full-parameter GA on Qwen3 (95% CI [+6.6, +19.1]). The paper also surfaces a general evaluation pitfall: forget-accuracy-only and answer-likelihood-only views can disagree because updates lower solution-trajectory likelihood while raising final-answer likelihood.

## 核心贡献

- **机制分离的诊断结论**：在 matched SFT/RLVR checkpoint（Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B 上 GSM8K 191/256 vs 188/256，仅 1.172 pp 差距，低于 3 pp 匹配阈值）上发现 SFT update 与 SFT→RLVR increment 在 token-level Δlog-prob 上结构性可分：SFT 是 +0.4477 的均值似然提升（positive-Δ fraction 0.7296），RLVR increment 几乎为 0（-0.0002，fraction 0.3995）——即 RLVR 不是"再叠加一次 SFT 式的似然提升"，而是"在 off-principal 方向上的概率重分布"（off-principal fraction ρ_t = 0.9998）。Qwen3-1.7B-Base 上方向完全一致。
- **"全参数无干净工作点"的边界刻画**：matched RLVR checkpoint 上的 steps-matched strength sweep 显示，全参数 GA 在 primary 模型上 η=3e-6 几乎不遗忘、η=5e-6 是 boundary（MATH-forget 22/150 但 GSM8K 跌 6.25 pp）、η≥7e-6 直接 collapse（GSM8K 13/256）；Qwen3 上 η=8e-6 同样 collapse（GSM8K 40/256）。这意味着报告"成功遗忘"如果不附带 retain 指标，是不充分的 unlearning 证据。
- **MAST 方法**：以 attention-projection 张量（q/k/v/o × 28 layers = 112）为候选，按 s_t = ρ_t · log(1+‖Δt‖²_F) · (1+|c_t|) 排序，仅在 top-k=96 上做 forget-loss 梯度上升，MLP/Norm/embedding 全部冻结。其优势同时来自"机制性选对子集"和"参数变少"——random-96 对照证明前者贡献了 +2.34 pp GSM8K（paired bootstrap 95% CI [+0.26, +4.56]）。
- **选对子集、而非"打更少参数"才有效**：三项 ranking-specificity 验证（R1 结构一致 / R2 局部化 / R3 配对比较）证明排名的因果性：top-96 集中包含全部 28 个 query 与 28 个 output projection，bottom-64 同样规模+同样强度几乎不遗忘（45→44/150）。
- **评测陷阱的揭示**：在 forget/retain 准确率之外，发现 restricted 与 full 两种 update 都会**降低** solution-trajectory 的 log-probability 但**抬高** final-answer 的 log-probability（MAST: -0.012 / +0.078；full GA: -0.143 / +0.975），因此"生成正确性"和"答案字符串似然"两个视角会分歧，二者各自作为 unlearning 证据都不完整。

## Key Contributions

- **Mechanism separation as an empirically validated diagnostic**: matched SFT vs SFT→RLVR checkpoints (within 1.172 pp on GSM8K) expose a token-level Δlog-prob signature difference (SFT +0.4477 mean, 0.73 positive-Δ; RLVR increment -0.0002 mean, 0.40 positive-Δ) and an off-principal geometry (ρ_t = 0.9998 for both updates), which together motivate a mechanism-aware (not magnitude-aware) unlearning selector.
- **No clean operating point for full-parameter unlearning on RLVR checkpoints**: a steps-matched strength sweep shows full-parameter GA collapses at η≥7e-6 on Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B and at η≥8e-6 on Qwen3-1.7B-Base, meaning any "forget" number is inseparable from collateral damage — and that retain-aware add-ons (GA+retain CE) trade forgetting away rather than opening a clean point.
- **MAST (Mechanism-Aligned Selective Targeting) with explicit tensor scoring**: s_t = ρ_t · log(1+‖Δt‖²_F) · (1+|c_t|) on the SFT→RLVR increment Δt = θR,t − θS,t, restricting the forget gradient-ascent update to the top-k=96 of 112 attention-projection tensors; selected subset is structurally coherent (all q/o projections retained, only key/value tensors dropped).
- **Three ranking-specificity properties (R1/R2/R3)**: structurally coherent selection; bottom-64 yields no meaningful forgetting (45→44/150); ranked top-96 retains +2.34 pp GSM8K over a same-size random-96 control (95% CI [+0.26, +4.56]).
- **Cross-seed, cross-objective, cross-model robustness**: result holds for three seeds ({37, 37, 41} GA; {41, 39, 39} NPO), three objectives (GA / NPO β=0.5 / SimNPO β=2.5), and two model families (Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B, Qwen3-1.7B-Base); the Qwen3 cross-model restricted-vs-full GSM8K advantage is +12.9 pp (95% CI [+6.6, +19.1]).
- **An evaluation pitfall for reasoning unlearning**: forget-accuracy-only and answer-likelihood-only views can disagree because updates can lower solution-trajectory likelihood while raising final-answer likelihood; MMLU (1000 ex, zero-shot) sanity check shows MAST stays within 0.4 pp of source (38.1% → 37.7%).

## Methodology

**Models.** Primary: Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B; cross-model: Qwen3-1.7B-Base. Both are 28-layer / 112 attention-tensor models.

**Matched SFT/RLVR checkpoints.** From the same base model, train an SFT checkpoint and an SFT-initialized RLVR checkpoint on matched GSM8K data (2048 train, 256 eval). RLVR uses GRPO (FP32 full-parameter, η=5e-7, gradient-norm clip 0.5, left-padded tokenization, sampling T=0.7 / top-p=0.95). The two checkpoints are matched within a 3 pp GSM8K gap (primary: 191/256 vs 188/256; Qwen3: 177/256 vs 171/256).

**Forget/retain sets.** Forget: MATH category splits in Counting & Probability, Geometry, Number Theory (N=150). Retain: non-target MATH categories (N=200) + GSM8K holdout. Metric: greedy exact match with a tolerant regrade (numeric/fraction/tuple/degree/boxed forms); the regrade audit moves each retain split by at most 2 examples.

**Mechanism diagnostics (3).**
1. *Update geometry:* layerwise delta energy projected onto the base-weight top singular subspace; the off-principal fraction ρ_t is 0.9998 for both SFT and SFT→RLVR increment.
2. *Token-level Δlog-prob:* SFT = +0.4477 mean / 0.73 positive-Δ; RLVR increment = -0.0002 / 0.40 (primary); the same SFT-positive vs RLVR-balanced pattern reproduces on Qwen3.
3. *Forget-gradient × checkpoint-delta cosine:* GA-update cosine is 0.0110 with SFT delta and 0.0051 with the RLVR increment — small, and RLVR is lower, indicating the standard forget gradient tracks RLVR-injected directions only weakly.

**MAST selector (Algorithm 1).** For each attention-projection tensor t, compute Δt = θR,t − θS,t, ρ_t (off-principal fraction of Δt w.r.t. the top singular subspace of θ0,t), and c_t = cos(∇_{θ_t} L_f(θR; D_f), Δt). Score s_t = ρ_t · log(1+‖Δt‖²_F) · (1+|c_t|). Update only the top-k=96 tensors by s_t via 50 steps of gradient ascent on the forget set at η=5e-6 (primary) / 8e-6 (Qwen3). MLP / Norm / embedding remain frozen.

**Statistical analysis.** Paired bootstrap (B=10,000, resampling questions and comparing pre- vs post-unlearning) for drops; exact McNemar's test for paired per-item forgetting; the headlined forget drop is 8 lost / 0 recovered, p=0.0078.

**Sanity checks.** MMLU (1000 examples, zero-shot log-prob scoring) to verify broad capability preservation; the top-32 / top-88 / top-96 / top-104 width band to confirm the sweet spot is structural rather than capacity-driven.

## Results & Highlights

- **Primary (Qwen2.5-Math-1.5B), MAST (GA) at η=5e-6, 50 steps**: GSM8K 190/256 (source 188), MATH-forget 37/150 (source 45), MATH-retain 76/200 (source 77). Forget drop 5.333 pp; collateral drops 0.5 pp (retain) and -0.781 pp (GSM8K improvement).
- **Primary full-parameter GA at the same strength**: GSM8K 172/256, MATH-forget 22/150, MATH-retain 56/200 — forget drop 15.333 pp, but at 10.5 pp retain and 6.25 pp GSM8K cost.
- **Statistical significance of MAST's forget drop**: McNemar p=0.0078 (8 lost / 0 recovered); paired bootstrap 95% CI [+2.000, +9.333] pp excludes zero. Collateral drops are statistically indistinguishable from zero (retain [-4.0, +5.0], GSM8K [-3.125, +1.562]).
- **Restricted-vs-full collateral contrast (primary)**: full's extra collateral is +10.0 pp on retain (95% CI [+4.0, +16.0]) and +7.0 pp on GSM8K (95% CI [+2.7, +11.3]).
- **Robustness**: stable across three seeds (GA: 37/37/41; NPO: 41/39/39); three objectives (GA / NPO β=0.5 / SimNPO β=2.5) all show the same MAST-vs-full gap; top-32/88/96/104 width band shows the sweet spot is the selection, not the count (37/150 at top-96, but 39/150 at top-104 with more collateral).
- **Cross-model (Qwen3-1.7B-Base)**: matched checkpoints within 2.34 pp GSM8K; mechanism separation in the same direction; at η=8e-6, full-parameter GA collapses GSM8K to 40/256 while MAST keeps it at 176/256 (+2.0 pp above source). Restricted-vs-full GSM8K collateral advantage = +12.9 pp, 95% CI [+6.6, +19.1].
- **Ranking-specificity (R1/R2/R3)**: top-96 retains all 28 q-projections and all 28 o-projections; bottom-64 yields 45→44/150 (no forgetting); ranked top-96 vs random-96 at matched forgetting = +2.34 pp GSM8K (CI [+0.26, +4.56]).
- **MMLU sanity**: source 38.1% → MAST 37.7% → full GA 37.6% (all within 0.5 pp).
- **Evaluation pitfall**: MAST (GA) shifts solution-trajectory Δlog-prob by -0.012 and final-answer Δlog-prob by +0.078; full GA shifts them by -0.143 and +0.975 respectively. Per-example audit of 8 source-correct → wrong flips shows all have negative solution Δlog-prob and non-negative answer Δlog-prob — i.e., "answer likelihood up + generated correctness down" is the typical failure mode.
- **Reproducibility**: code, configurations, target-tensor manifests, and per-sample evaluation outputs are released with the paper; same scripts and splits used throughout.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Parameter-selective unlearning is a crowded area (ROME, MEMIT, NPO, SimNPO, RMU, WHP, MNEME-style side-effect prediction). MAST's distinct contribution is the *mechanism-guided* selection rule that explicitly couples the SFT→RLVR increment geometry to the forget gradient — moving from "where is the magnitude biggest?" to "where does the RLVR-injected direction meet the forget signal?". This is a real but incremental refinement; the underlying off-principal-energy + update-norm + gradient-coupling recipe is reminiscent of localization-and-specificity in knowledge editing (Meng et al., 2022/2023; Hase et al., 2023, cited).
- **Rigor**: Strong on this axis. Matched-checkpoint control, McNemar's test on per-item forgetting, paired bootstrap with explicit 95% CIs, three seeds × three objectives × two model families, three ranking-specificity properties, plus a tolerant regrade audit that bounds the impact of grading artifacts to ≤2 examples. The CI for the *collateral* difference between restricted and full is reported with explicit bounds excluding zero on both axes — exactly the kind of reporting the unlearning literature often lacks.
- **Reproducibility**: Above average — code, configs, target-tensor manifests, and per-sample outputs are released. The matched SFT/RLVR protocol is the linchpin; reproducing it requires the GRPO recipe details (η=5e-7, FP32, gradient clip 0.5, T=0.7, top-p 0.95) and the 1.172 pp / 2.34 pp matching tolerance, all of which are given.
- **Significance**: Practical. The "no clean operating point" diagnostic is the most useful single contribution — any practitioner running gradient-ascent unlearning on an RLVR-tuned model should see the boundary plot (Figure 2) and conclude they need a mechanism-aware selector. The Qwen3 collapse (GSM8K 40/256 at η=8e-6) is a vivid demonstration of what full-parameter unlearning can do in this regime.
- **Red flags / things to verify in the PDF** (most of which the PDF actually addresses, but worth restating):
  - The headline forget effect is 8 problems out of 150 (5.33 pp). The McNemar p=0.0078 is sound for the *paired* test, but the absolute effect is small; the value of the paper is in the collateral ratio, not in the magnitude of forgetting.
  - Retain set is narrow (GSM8K + non-target MATH). The MMLU 1000-example check is encouraging but only one non-math benchmark; broader retention (HumanEval, IFEval, safety refusal) is not evaluated.
  - Scope is limited to math reasoning on two small (1.5B/1.7B) models in the same Qwen family. Whether MAST's mechanism-based ranking generalizes to non-Qwen architectures (Llama, Mistral, MoE) and to non-math reasoning (code, agentic, multilingual) is an open question.
  - The off-principal fraction is 0.9998 for both SFT and RLVR, so ρ_t alone would not separate the two — the *combination* with update magnitude and gradient coupling is what carries the signal. This is a slight narrative tension worth being explicit about: MAST's selector is "off-principal × big update × gradient-aligned", not "off-principal".
  - The selection rule (top-k=96) is tuned on the test set in the sense that 96/112 is selected by ablation; the paper shows top-32/88/96/104, with 96 being the sweet spot, but a held-out calibration run would strengthen the claim.
  - The hyperparameter |c_t| (using absolute value of the cosine) symmetrizes the direction. This is a deliberate choice to localize "coupling magnitude" but it could also be sensitive to noise when the cosine is near zero; the paper does not test an asymmetric (signed) variant.
  - The evaluation-pitfall section is one of the most valuable parts of the paper, but it is somewhat under-developed: only eight examples in the per-example case study, no correlation reported between the magnitude of "solution Δlog-prob down" and downstream generation quality beyond exact match.

## Strengths

- **Strong empirical discipline**: matched checkpoints, exact match + tolerant regrade, McNemar + paired bootstrap, three seeds × three objectives × two model families.
- **Diagnostic-then-method structure**: the mechanism analysis is not just motivation — it directly yields the selector (Eq. 1), and the three ranking-specificity properties close the loop by showing the ranking, not the count, drives the result.
- **Pareto-frontier framing (Figure 2)**: visualizes the collapse boundary of full-parameter GA and the multi-point usable region of MAST on both model families — much more informative than a single table of accuracies.
- **Cross-model replication with a real contrast**: Qwen3 is non-gated, cross-generation, and the structural isomorphism (28 layers, 112 tensors) is acknowledged; the dramatic Qwen3 collapse at matched strength makes the case viscerally.
- **General evaluation insight**: showing that forget-accuracy-only and answer-likelihood-only views can diverge is a publishable observation on its own and has implications beyond MAST (any reasoning-unlearning paper relying on one of these views is under-supported).
- **Open artifacts**: code, configs, target-tensor manifests, and per-sample outputs released.

## Limitations

- **Narrow domain**: only math reasoning (GSM8K + MATH) and only the Qwen2.5 / Qwen3 family at 1.5B / 1.7B; broader capability retention (code, chat, safety, multilingual) is not measured beyond a 1000-example MMLU check.
- **Small effect size on the headlined metric**: 8 problems of 150 (5.33 pp) is statistically significant for the paired test but a modest absolute drop; the paper's value is in the *ratio* of forgetting to collateral, not in either quantity alone.
- **Hyperparameter sensitivity**: top-k (96/112), learning rate (η=5e-6 vs 8e-6 per model), and the O·U·F combination are calibrated on the eval set; no held-out calibration protocol is described.
- **Retain set is essentially a single domain** (math); the +0.4 pp MMLU delta is encouraging but not enough to claim "general capability preservation" in any strong sense.
- **Selector direction choice**: using |c_t| symmetrizes the gradient alignment; an asymmetric version (sign-preserving) is not ablated.
- **Mechanism claim is correlational**: the off-principal-energy × update-magnitude × gradient-coupling score is shown to *work*; the causal claim that "RLVR-induced reasoning lives in these directions" is supported by the Qwen3 reproduction and the bottom-64 ablation, but a reverse-direction intervention (e.g., update the *bottom* of the ranking at the same strength) is not done.
- **Unlearning is one-shot (50 steps)**: no analysis of multi-step or curriculum-style unlearning, and no interaction with iterative DPO/RLHF post-unlearning repair.

## Relevance

与用户关注的大模型推理、对齐与可编辑性方向高度相关。RLVR 已成为驱动 LLM 推理的主流范式（GRPO 及其变体在 DeepSeek-R1、QwQ 等系统中扮演核心角色），而"如何在保留通用能力的前提下可控地回滚 / 调整 RLVR 注入的特定能力"是模型部署、能力编辑、安全 unlearning 的实际痛点。MAST 把"机制引导的参数选择"显式化（off-principal 几何 + 增量大小 + 遗忘梯度耦合），与知识编辑领域 ROME/MEMIT 的"localization-and-specificity"思路一脉相承，对偏好遗忘 / 可回滚对齐 / 能力编辑 / RLHF-RLVR 兼容性等场景有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does MAST's mechanism score generalize to non-Qwen architectures (Llama-3, Mistral, MoE) and to non-math reasoning (code, agentic tool use, multilingual)? The ρ_t ≈ 0.9998 number may be family-specific.
- Can the "matched SFT/RLVR checkpoint" protocol be replaced by a cheaper surrogate (e.g., LoRA-difference + SVD on the LoRA delta) so MAST can be applied without training two full checkpoints?
- Is the |c_t| symmetrization (magnitude of forget-gradient coupling) optimal, or does a sign-preserving or curriculum-weighted version help? An ablation across the asymmetry dimension would clarify.
- How does MAST compose with iterative post-unlearning repair (e.g., a small SFT pass on a retain set to recover the few points of collateral the paper admits it still pays at the strong operating point — forget 45→28 at +2.3 pp GSM8K / +3.0 pp retain)?
- The evaluation-pitfall finding (solution Δlog-prob down + answer Δlog-prob up) suggests generation-quality metrics (chain-of-thought correctness, process reward model scores) should be standard in reasoning-unlearning papers; can a unified "unlearning score" combining trajectory likelihood, answer likelihood, and exact match be proposed?
- Is the ranking stable under different RLVR algorithms (GRPO vs. PPO vs. RLOO vs. DPO-on-CoT) and different SFT base models, or does the score's top-k composition drift with the post-training recipe?
- Can the off-principal × magnitude × gradient-coupling recipe be turned into a *predictor* of which tasks a model will fail to unlearn cleanly, before any unlearning gradient step is run?
