Why This Matters

The first label-free flatness regularizer grounded in PAC-Bayes theory — usable on unlabeled data, unlike SAM or Hessian-of-loss methods.

⚠ Critical Edge

Two of four weight-parametrization variants fail to beat the baseline; the JH penalty is only a theory-motivated proxy, not the bound itself.

Methodology — distilled

The derandomization penalty for replacing a stochastic Gibbs predictor with the deterministic posterior-mean predictor is proven to be exactly the generalization gap of the Jensen gap class (Theorem 3.3). Bounding this gap assumes the loss is ℓ-Lipschitz and β-smooth in the network output, with bounded hypotheses. Talagrand's comparison inequality for sub-Gaussian processes then controls the class's Rademacher complexity, yielding deterministic high-probability bounds whose flatness factors are the Jacobian and Hessian of the score map — for both unbounded (Theorem 3.7) and bounded losses (Theorems 5.1–5.3).

For linear predictors under a Gaussian prior, the PAC-Bayes complexity term improves to a √d dependence (Lemma 3.8), tightening Haddouche et al. (2020). Practically, the regularizer JH = √(β²·J + ℓ²·H) is estimated via Jacobian/Hessian-vector products (M random vectors) in the BatchNorm-folded effective-weight space — making it inherently label-free.

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Clean decomposition — derandomization cost = generalization gap of the Jensen gap class.
  • Statistically consistent — the extra penalty vanishes as n → ∞, unlike margin-based derandomization.
  • Label-free regularizer — depends only on the score map, opening semi-supervised use.
  • General — covers bounded & unbounded smooth losses; √d improvement for linear + Gaussian priors.
  • Thorough ablation + well-chosen label-noise regime where gains are largest.

Limitations

  • Narrow scope — CIFAR-10 only, single 8-layer CNN; no SAM/ASAM/SWA comparison.
  • Architecture-specific — JH needs the BatchNorm-folding step; GroupNorm/LayerNorm untested.
  • Modest clean-data gains; benefits concentrated in large-batch & label-noise regimes.
  • Unreported cost of Hessian-vector products & gradients through BN statistics.
  • Proxy, not bound — minimizing JH does not provably tighten the bound; 8σ²B residual is n-independent.

Open Questions

  • Can JH beat SAM / ASAM / SWA head-to-head at matched compute on CIFAR-10?
  • Does the BN-folding recipe generalize to GroupNorm / LayerNorm / RMSNorm?
  • Can Hessian-vector-product cost be cut (K-FAC, last-layer-only) without breaking the bound?
  • Is JH complementary to mixup / CutMix / dropout, and how does it interact with label noise?
  • Can the label-free property power a semi-supervised pipeline (JH on unlabeled + CE on labeled)?
  • Is the √d scaling matched by a matching lower bound, or still loose?
