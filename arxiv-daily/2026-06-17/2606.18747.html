Generating Natural and Expressive Robot Gestures through Iterative RLHF with Human Feedback using LLMs

Chris Lee · Flora Salim · Benjamin Tag · Francisco Cruz — UNSW Sydney (Australia) · Universidad Central de Chile

Key finding

Five rounds of crowd-RLHF make an LLM-driven Pepper robot gesture better — remove the memory and it collapses to four templates.

Why this matters

A rare real-robot case study that cleanly splits what offline DPO versus online few-shot each buy you — and pinpoints exactly where human-feedback-driven gesture generation falls apart.

Critical edge

The ~0.3-point Likert gains are sold as p<0.001 wins, yet there are zero non-LLM baselines, no rater-agreement stats, and a closed GPT-4.1 endpoint that will silently drift — the magnitude is overstated.

Methodology

GPT-4.1 (gpt-4.1-2025-04-14) is prompted with a Pepper persona, strict NAOqi v2.5 joint limits, and a curated library of low-level motion-primitive functions (e.g. head_rotate_up, left_elbow_extend, right_hand_open), each clamping per-joint angles and enforcing a post-motion delay. From a spoken input it emits a tuple ⟨spoken reply, gesture code π⟩ — the reply goes to Pepper's TTS, π executes via execute_generated_movement. Temperature is set high (1.5) for expressive variability.

Dual-track iterative RLHF

  • Offline — DPO: pairwise preferences from Likert aggregates → 1,350 within-category + 150 cross-iteration pairs.
  • Online — few-shot: free-text comments LLM-summarized into one feedback note, appended to a growing conversation history (c=4 cycles by Iteration 5).
  • Per round: 50 phrases × 20 raters = 1,000 feedback samples.

Evaluation

  • Physical Pepper robot, 5 social categories (Greeting, Encouragement, Apology, Explanation, Collaboration).
  • 3 Likert dimensions: expressiveness, relevance, fluidity + free text.
  • Welch's t-test, Iteration 5 vs 1; ablations isolate DPO vs few-shot vs base.

Ablation attribution

  • Few-shot → largest expressiveness gains (carries diversity load).
  • DPO → largest relevance & fluidity gains (robust but unnatural alone).
  • The two tracks are complementary.

RQ verdicts

  • RQ1 — yes: human feedback finetunes LLM code-gen for perceived expressiveness.
  • RQ2 — partial: executable human-aligned code yes; sustained diversity/fluidity needs extra mechanisms (pose interpolation, learned dynamics).

Strengths

  • Real robot deployment — Pepper under NAOqi v2.5, not a sim or third-party video study.
  • Large-N crowd eval — 250 participants, 4,000 ratings, 50 phrases × 5 categories × 5 iterations.
  • Clean ablation decomposing few-shot vs DPO contributions — rare in this subfield.
  • Honest failure reporting — mode collapse on history removal is genuinely useful.
  • Practitioner detail — system prompt, primitive library, temperature, angle clamping.
  • Bridges LLM/RLHF and social HRI by recasting gesture as code generation.

Limitations

  • Between-subjects design — different raters per iteration inflate noise vs within-subjects.
  • No rater-agreement stats (Krippendorff's α, ICC) for the 20 raters per gesture.
  • No non-LLM baselines (rule-based, mocap, Gesture2Vec) — unclear how much is "just an LLM".
  • Closed-API dependence on GPT-4.1 couples reproducibility to version drift.
  • Self-generated stimuli — 50 phrases made by the same LLM family that scores them.
  • Diversity not quantified — mode collapse asserted, not scored; DPO hyperparameters undisclosed.
  • Single platform (Pepper, low-DOF) — generalization to higher-DOF humanoids unproven.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. What are the rater-agreement stats (Krippendorff's α, ICC) — how much DPO signal is noise?
  2. Why does the iteration-2 → 3 regression occur, and is it robust to a new rater cohort?
  3. How does it compare to a non-LLM baseline (mocap library, Gesture2Vec) on the same prompts?
  4. What is the minimum feedback budget where DPO stops overfitting and few-shot stops collapsing?
  5. Can a hybrid (high-level LLM policy + low-level pose interpolation) recover diversity without losing fluidity?
  6. How does the policy generalize to phrases outside the 50-stimulus set, and how fast does it drift?
  7. Is DPO even needed, or would a one-shot preference dataset + larger few-shot window match Iteration-5 at lower cost?
