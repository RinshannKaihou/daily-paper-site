Optimal score function estimation via derivatives constraints

⌗ Key metric
n−2(s−1)/(2s+d)
Minimax-optimal score-MSE rate for ERM over a Sobolev ball on the flat torus — matching the Stone / Comte lower bound.
◆ Why this matters

Unified minimax theory for score-based generative modeling — no neural-net structural assumptions, just one Sobolev-ball radius that also explains why smoothness penalties help in practice.

⚠ Critical edge

Pure theory with no experiments: the Sobolev-ball class is analytic, not a tractable parametric family, and the weight-decay bridge is openly speculative.

Methodology

Part 1 — Torus (no diffusion)

  • Loss. Empirical relative Fisher information (Hyvärinen score matching): Ln(g) = ½‖g‖² + ∇·g, replacing unknown μ by μn.
  • Penalty. Sobolev-style term λ‖∇s−1g‖²L²(Td) in the uniform measure (μ is unknown).
  • Class. Sobolev ball Fs,R,h,ℓ with bandwidth-like radius h mirroring a kernel bandwidth.
  • Analysis. Bias via kernel embedding of ρ*; variance via chaining on the relative-Fisher process + Gagliardo–Nirenberg to tame the divergence term.

Part 2 — Manifold (SGM)

  • Forward. OU process: Xt = e−tX0 + √(1−e−2t) Z; loss via OU semigroup Pt (Vincent 2011).
  • Class. Sobolev ball with radius ∝ 1/(1−e−2t) — blows up as t→0, no pointwise L∞ clipping on M.
  • Bias. Tubular decomposition near M; most mass lives in a tube of radius ~e−tε.
  • Variance. Semigroup smoothing ∇kPt = e−ktPt∇k; two-regime r⋆ (small-t vs large-t).

Strengths vs Limitations

Strengths

  • One structural tool (Sobolev-ball radius) covers both score estimation and SGM measure quality.
  • Drops neural-network structural assumptions used by Oko (2023) / Stéphanovitch (2025) — purely analytic capacity condition.
  • The W₁ SGM rate (Cor. 20) is proved directly, not conjectured.
  • Post-hoc justification for why second-derivative / weight-decay penalties help SGM training.

Limitations

  • Domain restricted to torus / compact k-smooth submanifold with positive reach — no ℝD sub-Gaussian regime.
  • Time restriction 1−e−2t ≤ log(n)/C; larger-t fallback (Remark 11) is slower.
  • No algorithm, no experiments, no neural nets — Sobolev ball is not a tractable parametric family.
  • Oracle rates only: no Lepski-style adaptation to unknown smoothness s.

Questions for further exploration

Can the two-regime bound be made uniform over t, removing the logarithmic restriction?
Is the result adaptive to unknown s — a Lepski/aggregation procedure over the Sobolev-ball family?
Does it extend to ℝD with sub-Gaussian / Besov densities, matching Stéphanovitch (2025) without NN structure?
Is the weight-decay ↔ Sobolev penalty correspondence provable for ReLU MLPs, closing the theory–practice gap?
Can the intrinsic-dimension bandwidth h = n−1/(d+2s) drive a data-driven manifold-dimension estimator?
