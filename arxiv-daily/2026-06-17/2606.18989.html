G-IdiomAlign: A Gloss-Pivoted Benchmark for Cross-Lingual Idiom Alignment

Fengying Ye · Yanming Sun · Runzhe Zhan · Zheqi Zhang · Lidia S. Chao · Derek F. Wong  —  NLP CT Lab, University of Macau

idiom-translation cross-lingual benchmark llm-evaluation mechanistic-interpretability
Key Metric
18,785
aligned idiom pairs across 9 core languages & 36 language pairs; alignment strict-acc 0.685 / lenient 0.923.
Why This Matters

First benchmark to attribute idiom-translation errors by type (literal / lexical / contextual) and to localize gloss gains to attention heads — reproducible diagnostic protocols for cross-lingual LLM evaluation.

Critical Edge

Headline gloss gains rest on a noisy embedding proxy (best Acc@0.80 = 29.63%); EM/BLEU/ChrF & human eval are deferred to an appendix, leaving the central Task 2 claim under-supported.

Methodology

Construction pipeline

  • Gloss pivot: Wiktionary English glosses as shared semantic anchor; polysemous idioms removed (single-sense filtering).
  • Retrieval: text-embedding-3-large cosine, top-k=10 per direction.
  • MNN alignment: rank-1 mutual nearest neighbors both A→B & B→A ⇒ one-to-one match.
  • Distribution-aware filtering: per-pair adaptive threshold on modal similarity bin.

Two diagnostic protocols

  • Task 1 — MCQ Idiom Equivalence: 4-way choice with three typed distractors — LT (literal trap), LC (lexical-cue trap), CA (contextual-association trap).
  • Task 2 — Gloss-Contrastive Generation: No-gloss vs With-gloss, 72 directions; embedding proxy Acc@0.70 / Acc@0.80.
  • Attention diagnostics (Qwen3-8B): IAR / GAR / DR / OtherTop1; Jaccard overlap of head-sets vs layer-sets.

Models

DeepSeek-V3.2 (T/NT), Gemini-2.5-Pro, Claude-4.5-Haiku, Ministral-8B-Instruct, Qwen3-8B (T/NT) — 7 variants. Greedy decoding, options shuffled.

Strengths & Limitations

✓ Strengths

  • Clean two-protocol design (typed-distractor MCQ + No/With-gloss contrast) supports both attribution and causal isolation.
  • Precision-first pipeline: bidirectional MNN + adaptive filtering avoids spurious forced alignments.
  • Typed distractors enable systematic error attribution; LT-dominance is a clean, falsifiable result.
  • Head-level mechanistic analysis is rare and welcome for a benchmark paper.
  • Honest framing — "modest improvement," "substantial headroom"; public dataset & code release.

✗ Limitations

  • Embedding proxy is a noisy open-generation evaluator; xCOMET & large-scale human eval absent from main body.
  • Mechanistic claims on a single model (Qwen3-8B), n=189/condition; correlational only.
  • English-pivot bias acknowledged but unresolved; coverage skews high-resource.
  • Single-sense + one-to-one MNN under-represent polysemy & many-to-many synonym clusters.
  • ~18.5% of distractors may be mis-typed (81.5% validity), confounding LT/LC/CA attribution.

Questions for Further Exploration

Does head-level gloss anchoring generalize beyond Qwen3-8B to Llama / Mistral / GPT-class families?
Do stronger metrics (xCOMET, structured LLM-judge, large human eval) corroborate — or overstate — gloss gains?
How does the benchmark behave on truly low-resource targets (African, Indigenous languages) beyond Appendix A?
Is literal-translation bias reducible by training (idiom-aware SFT / DPO / retrieval-augmented decoding)?
Can a causal intervention (attention-head ablation/steering) verify gloss anchoring is causal, not epiphenomenal?
Does idiom-alignment quality predict downstream MT quality on idiomatic-heavy sentences?
