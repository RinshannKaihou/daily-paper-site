A Technical Taxonomy of LLM Agent Communication Protocols

Linus Sander · Habtom Kahsay Gidey · Alexander Lenz · Alois Knoll — Technische Universität München (TUM)

Key finding

A 5-dimension taxonomy of nine LLM-agent protocols predicts a federated, OSI-like layered stack — no single protocol can win all three corners of the communication trilemma.

LLM agents multi-agent systems communication protocols taxonomy Internet of Agents interoperability
Key metric
7/7
Agent-to-agent protocols pair hybrid payloads with session-state persistence — a sharp, universal pattern across every A2A protocol studied.
Why this matters

Standardized, layered communication protocols are the missing infrastructure for scalable, interoperable multi-agent networks — the backbone of self-evolving, LLM-driven research systems.

Critical edge

Saturation across only n = 9 protocols is weak evidence for a five-dimension claim — with no inter-rater reliability check and a layering prediction that names no governance or coordination mechanism.

Methodology

Applies Nickerson, Varshney & Muntermann's (2013) iterative taxonomy method — upfront purpose, meta-characteristic, and ending conditions, then alternating empirical-to-conceptual and conceptual-to-empirical passes.

  • 4 inclusion criteria: LLM-targeted, open-source & runnable, actively maintained (commit after Jun 2024), demonstrable adoption (GitHub stars).
  • Sample (n = 9): MCP, A2A, LAP, agents.json, Agora, ANP, LMOS, ACP, agntcy.
  • 5 iterations (3 empirical→conceptual, 2 conceptual→empirical) until saturation — no new dimension in the final pass.
  • Self-audited on conciseness, robustness, comprehensiveness, and mutual-exclusivity / collective-exhaustiveness.

Strengths & Limitations

▲ Strengths

  • Methodologically transparent — explicit Nickerson artifacts, iteration counts, saturation criterion.
  • Grounded in 9 real, maintained, open-source protocols; two complementary views (pattern + per-protocol rationale).
  • Reports rejected dimensions with reasons — disciplined taxonomy work.
  • Maps to Marro's communication trilemma; OSI-like 5-layer stack is a practical selection artifact.
  • Cites both classical MAS (FIPA, KQML) and contemporary LLM-agent protocol literature.

▼ Limitations

  • n = 9 is small; biased toward Western OSS-incubator protocols — Chinese & non-GitHub efforts absent.
  • No inter-rater reliability check; classifications authored by the team itself.
  • No empirical trilemma measurement — token/latency overheads inherited, not benchmarked.
  • Layered-stack prediction lacks a governance / coordination mechanism.
  • Privacy & policy-enforcement gap flagged but undeveloped (no DID, OAuth, XACML mapping).

Questions for further exploration

  1. Layering governance — what body (IETF WG? Linux Foundation? de-facto GitHub-org) coordinates cross-layer schema & version compatibility?
  2. Decentralized discovery — which DHT / DID / cryptographic mechanisms enable Internet-of-Agents-scale discovery without central registries?
  3. Security retrofit — can OAuth 2.0, W3C DID, XACML, OPA be systematically mapped onto the 9 surveyed protocols?
  4. Empirical trilemma — a controlled benchmark (e.g. Agora vs. A2A on a fixed task suite) would quantify the versatility / efficiency cost.
  5. Schema-flexibility ROI — is the token cost of evolving schemas actually worth it vs. predefined multiple schemas?
  6. Cross-cultural coverage — a 2026 follow-up should include Chinese-ecosystem agent-protocol proposals, which may diverge architecturally.
