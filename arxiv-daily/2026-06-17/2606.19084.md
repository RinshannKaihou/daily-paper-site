> **一句话总结：** 在平坦环面上证明用 Sobolev 球约束假设空间、加 $(s-1)$ 阶导数的 $L^2$ 罚项就能让 score function 的 ERM 估计器达到 minimax 速率 $n^{-(s-1)/(2s+d)}$；进一步在紧流形支撑、扩散 SGM 设定下用半径与 $1/t$ 成正比的 Sobolev 球作为假设类，证明经验最小化可在 Wasserstein-1 距离下得到 SGM 测度估计的 minimax 速率。

> **TL;DR:** The paper studies empirical-risk-minimization (ERM) estimators of the score function $\nabla \log f$ in two regimes. **Part 1 (no diffusion, flat torus):** with $\mu$ a density on $\mathbb{T}^d$ in $W^{s,\infty}$ bounded below, the authors minimize the empirical relative Fisher information plus a Sobolev-style penalty $\lambda \|\nabla^{s-1} g\|_{L^2(\mathbb{T}^d)}^2$ over a Sobolev-ball hypothesis class. The bias is controlled by a kernel approximation argument, the variance by a chaining argument on the relative-Fisher loss, and the bound is optimized at $\lambda = h^{2(s-1)}$, $h = n^{-1/(d+2s)}$, yielding $E\|\hat\rho - \rho^*\|_{L^2(\mu)}^2 \lesssim n^{-2(s-1)/(2s+d)}$ — the Stone-style minimax rate. **Part 2 (with diffusion, SGM):** for $\mu$ supported on a $k$-smooth compact submanifold $M \subset \mathbb{R}^D$ with density in $W^{s,\infty}$, $2 \le s \le k-2$, the authors minimize a denoising score-matching-type loss (defined via the OU semigroup $P_t$) over a Sobolev-ball class with radius $\propto 1/(1-e^{-2t})$. Exploiting the smoothing of $P_t$ plus a refined bias analysis around $M$, they obtain $E\|\hat\rho_t - \rho^*_t\|_{L^2(\mu_t)}^2 \lesssim h^{2(s+1)} + r_\star^2 + 1/n$, and then show (Corollary 20) that plugging this estimator into the SGM backward process yields the optimal $W_1$-measure-estimation rate for $\mu$. The single structural lever is constraining the hypothesis class to a Sobolev ball with the right radius.

## 核心贡献

- **环面 ERM 的 minimax 性。** 在 $\mathbb{T}^d$ 上，对 score function 的经验相对 Fisher 信息损失加上对 $\nabla^{s-1} g$ 的 $L^2$ 罚，假设类限制在 Sobolev 球 $F_{s,R,h,\ell}$ 中。证明得到的 ERM 估计器在 $L^2(\mu)$ 意义下达到速率 $n^{-2(s-1)/(2s+d)}$（$s \ge 2$），与 Stone 的经典非参数下界吻合。
- **显式的偏置–方差分解。** 偏置通过把 $\rho^*$ 的核光滑版 $\rho_h$ 嵌入同一个 Sobolev 球来控制；方差则用 Boucheron et al. (2013) 的 chaining 技巧直接处理相对 Fisher 损失，依赖 Gagliardo–Nirenberg 不等式吸收 $\nabla\cdot(\hat\rho - \rho_F)$ 项。
- **从非扩散到 SGM 的桥梁。** 把上面 ERM 框架搬到扩散模型：用 OU 半群 $P_t$ 写出隐式 score matching 损失；假设类半径放大到 $R/(1-e^{-2t})$ 以适应 score 函数的范数随 $t \to 0$ 的爆破；最终 Corollary 20 把 score 估计速率转化为 SGM 输出分布与 $\mu$ 在 $W_1$ 下的 minimax 测度估计速率。
- **统一"导数约束"作为唯一结构假设。** 全文不依赖任何神经网络结构假设，只要求"假设类属于一个半径合适的 Sobolev 球"。作者明确指出，许多常用训练惩罚在简单网络上等于对二阶导数的加权 $L^2$ 惩罚（援引 Williams et al., 2019），为实践中有效但无显式导数控制的训练方式提供了理论合理性。

## Key Contributions

- Formalize score estimation as **empirical risk minimization** of the **empirical relative Fisher information** (Hyvärinen's score-matching loss, replacing the unknown $\mu$ by $\mu_n$), and analyze it on the flat torus $\mathbb{T}^d$ under $W^{s,\infty}$ smoothness of the density with $f \ge f_{\min} > 0$.
- Introduce a **Sobolev-ball hypothesis class** $F_{s,R,h,\ell}$ with controlled $W^{s-1+k,\infty}$ norms for $k = 0,\dots,\ell$, and add a **Sobolev-style penalty** $\lambda \|\nabla^{s-1} g\|_{L^2(\mathbb{T}^d)}^2$. The norm in the penalty is in $L^2(\mathbb{T}^d)$ (not $L^2(\mu)$) since $\mu$ is unknown.
- **Theorem 3** (rate for the torus ERM): under $2(s-1+\ell) > d$ and $\lambda < 1$, with the optimal choice $\lambda = h^{2(s-1)}$ and $h = n^{-1/(d+2s)}$,
  $$E\|\hat\rho - \rho^*\|_{L^2(\mu)}^2 \le C n^{-2(s-1)/(2s+d)},$$
  matching the Stone/Comte minimax lower bound for estimating the gradient of a density.
- **Theorem 8** (rate for the SGM ERM, on a manifold): for $\mu$ supported on a $k$-smooth compact submanifold $M \subset \mathbb{R}^D$ with $W^{s,\infty}$ density ($2 \le s \le k-2$), and using a Sobolev-ball hypothesis class with radius $\propto 1/(1-e^{-2t})$ and additional $W^{s+1+i,\infty}$ bounds $\propto 1/((1-e^{-2t})^{1+2i} h^i)$, the empirical minimizer of the OU-semigroup-based loss satisfies (with $1 - e^{-2t} \le \log(n)/C$)
  $$E\|\hat\rho_t - \rho^*_t\|_{L^2(\mu_t)}^2 \le \frac{C}{(1-e^{-2t})^2}\bigl(h^{2(s+1)} + r_\star^2 + 1/n\bigr),$$
  where $r_\star$ has two regimes: a "small-time" term controlled by $h, \ell$ and a "large-time" term controlled by $1 - e^{-2t}$ and an arbitrary integer $m$ with $2(s+1+m) > d$.
- **Corollary 20** (SGM sample-quality rate): plug $\hat\rho_t$ into the reverse Ornstein–Uhlenbeck process; the resulting SGM sample $\hat\mu^{\rm SGM}_n$ matches $\mu$ in Wasserstein-1 distance at the minimax rate for measure estimation on manifolds, namely $n^{-(s+1)/(2s+d)}$ (Divol 2022).
- **Methodological highlight:** A single Sobolev-ball radius condition controls the entire pipeline — score estimation and downstream sample quality — without any neural-network-specific structural assumption. The authors note that empirical "smoothness" penalties (e.g., weight decay on second derivatives) for simple nets are known to correspond to such Sobolev penalties, providing a partial explanation for the empirical success of overparameterized models.

## Methodology

- **Setting (Part 1, no diffusion):** $\mu$ has density $f$ w.r.t. the volume measure of $\mathbb{T}^d$, $f \in W^{s,\infty}(\mathbb{T}^d)$, $f \ge f_{\min} > 0$. Data: $X_1, \dots, X_n \sim \mu$ i.i.d. Target: $\rho^* = \nabla \log f$.
- **Loss:** Following Hyvärinen (2005), minimizing $\|\rho^* - g\|_{L^2(\mu)}$ over $W^{1,2}(\mu)$ is equivalent to minimizing $L(g) = \int \ell_g \, d\mu$ with $\ell_g = \|g\|^2 + 2 \nabla \cdot g$. Empirical version $L_n(g) = \frac{1}{n}\sum_i \ell_g(X_i)$.
- **Regularization:** Add a Sobolev-style penalty to enforce smoothness on derivatives up to order $s-1$:
  $$L_{n,\lambda}(g) = L_n(g) + \lambda \|\nabla^{s-1} g\|_{L^2(\mathbb{T}^d)}^2.$$
  The penalty uses the **uniform** measure $\mathbb{T}^d$ (not $\mu$) — crucial since $\mu$ is unknown.
- **Hypothesis class:** $F_{s,R,h,\ell}$ — vector fields in $W^{s+\ell-1,\infty}(\mathbb{T}^d)$ with $\|g\|_{W^{1,\infty}} \le R$ and $\|g\|_{W^{s-1+k,\infty}} \le R \cdot C(s)/h^k$ for $k = 0,\dots,\ell$. The $h$-dependent constraint mirrors a kernel bandwidth: the class contains the kernel-smoothed score with bandwidth $h$.
- **Bias analysis (Prop. 4):** Construct a kernel approximation $\rho_h$ of $\rho^*$ whose derivatives up to $s-1$ are bounded by $R$ and whose higher derivatives are $\mathcal{O}(h^{-\ell})$. Show $\rho_h \in F_{s,R,h,\ell}$ and that $L_\lambda(\rho_h) - L(\rho^*) \le C(h^{2(s-1)} + \lambda)$, yielding bias $\le C(h^{2(s-1)} + \lambda)$.
- **Variance analysis (Prop. 5):** Establish the upper bound (Eq. 10) on $\|\hat\rho - \rho_F\|_{L^2(\mu)}^2 + \lambda\|\nabla^{s-1}(\hat\rho - \rho_F)\|_{L^2(\mathbb{T}^d)}^2$ by a supremum of centered empirical processes, then apply **chaining** (Boucheron et al. 2013, Thm. 13.5) with the metric induced by $\frac{1}{n}\sum_i (\tilde\ell_{g_1} - \tilde\ell_{g_2})^2(X_i)$. Use Edmunds (2003) entropy bounds for Sobolev balls to get $H(u) \le C(h^\ell u)^{-d/(\ell+s-2)}$, and a **Gagliardo–Nirenberg inequality** to absorb the divergence term, giving the variance bound.
- **Optimization (Thm. 3 corollary):** Trade off $\lambda = h^{2(s-1)}$ vs. $h$ to get the minimax-optimal rate $n^{-2(s-1)/(2s+d)}$.
- **Setting (Part 2, diffusion/SGM):** $X_0 \sim \mu$ on $M \subset \mathbb{R}^D$, $X_t = e^{-t} X_0 + \sqrt{1 - e^{-2t}} Z_t$, $Z_t \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I_D)$. $\rho^*_t = \nabla \log f^{\mu}_t$. Score can be written as the OU expectation (Eq. 22), and the loss is $L_t(g) = \int \ell_{g,t} d\mu$ with $\ell_{g,t} = P_t(\|g\|^2 + 2\nabla \cdot g)$ (Vincent 2011). The smoothing $P_t$ replaces the need for an explicit Sobolev penalty in the loss.
- **Hypothesis class for SGM:** $F_{t,s,R,h,\ell}$ with $\|g\|_{W^{s+1,\infty}} \le R/(1-e^{-2t})$ and $\|g\|_{W^{s+1+i,\infty}} \le R/((1-e^{-2t})^{1+2i} h^i)$ — the radius blows up as $t \to 0$ to accommodate the score's own growth. Critically, the class does **not** impose a pointwise $L^\infty$ bound of $R/(1-e^{-2t})$ on candidates, which would require knowledge of $M$ via aggressive clipping.
- **Bias for SGM (Prop. 16):** Decompose $f^{\mu}_t$ as $f_t^{\rm tp}(x) f_t(x)$ (tubular part × manifold part) near $M$; study regularity of $\rho^*_t$ in a tube of radius $\sim e^{-t}\epsilon$ around $M_t = e^{-t} M$, which carries most of the mass of $\mu_t$ for small $t$. The kernel-smoothed $\rho^*_t$ lies in $F_{t,s,R,h,\ell}$ with controlled bias.
- **Variance for SGM (Prop. 19):** Similar chaining argument, but entropy bounds now exploit the semigroup smoothing of $P_t$ (Lemma 7): $\nabla^k P_t \varphi = e^{-kt} P_t \nabla^k \varphi$, with explicit constants decaying like $1/(1-e^{-2t})^2$ in the relevant regimes. Two-regime bound on $r_\star$ reflects small-$t$ (kernel-dominated) vs. large-$t$ (semigroup-dominated) behavior.
- **Theorem 8 → Corollary 20 (SGM measure rate):** Combine the score bound with a quantitative statement that the reverse OU process started at $\gamma$ and driven by $\hat\rho_t$ yields $\hat\mu^{\rm SGM}_n$ with $W_1(\hat\mu^{\rm SGM}_n, \mu)$ matching the Divol (2022) minimax rate for manifold density estimation.

## Results & Highlights

- **Theorem 3 (torus):**
  - General bound (Eq. before setting $\lambda = h^{2(s-1)}$): $E\|\hat\rho - \rho^*\|_{L^2(\mu)}^2 \le C \lambda^{\frac{d-2(s+\ell-2)}{(s-1)(d+2(s+\ell-2))}} h^{-\frac{2d\ell}{d+2(s+\ell-2)}} n^{-\frac{2(s+\ell-2)}{d+2(s+\ell-2)}} + h^{2(s-1)} + \lambda$.
  - With $\lambda = h^{2(s-1)}$, $h = n^{-1/(d+2s)}$: $E\|\hat\rho - \rho^*\|_{L^2(\mu)}^2 \lesssim n^{-2(s-1)/(2s+d)}$.
- **Theorem 8 (SGM, manifold):** $E\|\hat\rho_t - \rho^*_t\|_{L^2(\mu_t)}^2 \lesssim \frac{1}{(1-e^{-2t})^2}\bigl(h^{2(s+1)} + r_\star^2 + 1/n\bigr)$ with the two-regime $r_\star^2 = \max\bigl((\dots)_{\text{small-}t}, (\dots)_{\text{large-}t}\bigr)$, provided $1 - e^{-2t} \le \log(n)/C$.
- **Corollary 20 (Wasserstein-1 SGM rate):** the resulting SGM achieves $n^{-(s+1)/(2s+d)}$ in $W_1(\hat\mu^{\rm SGM}_n, \mu)$, the Divol (2022) minimax rate for $W_1$ density estimation on a $d$-manifold.
- **Toolkit highlights:**
  - Sobolev-ball hypothesis classes with **bandwidth-like radius** $h$ and **tube-of-relevance** width $\epsilon$.
  - Chaining for the **relative Fisher information** process (rather than the squared score) — the right loss to control $L^2(\mu)$ risk on the score.
  - Use of **Gagliardo–Nirenberg inequality** to tame the $\nabla \cdot$ term in the Fisher information.
  - Two-regime entropy bounds exploiting both kernel and OU-semigroup smoothing.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** Mixed. The result "ERM over a Sobolev ball yields minimax rates for the score on a torus" is a natural application of the Stone/Comte template combined with Hyvärinen's score matching. The genuinely new contributions are: (a) the explicit penalty formulation (and the fact that the penalty is in $L^2(\mathbb{T}^d)$ rather than $L^2(\mu)$); (b) the entropy/chaining argument for the **relative Fisher information** loss, which is trickier than squared-error regression; (c) the SGM extension to a **manifold-supported** measure without imposing a pointwise $L^\infty$ clipping condition on candidates (avoiding the need to know $M$); (d) the **two-regime** $r_\star$ analysis in Theorem 8. These are real technical contributions, but the headline "minimax rate" is not unexpected.
- **Rigor.** High. The proofs are detailed: explicit decomposition of bias and variance, careful use of Gagliardo–Nirenberg to control $\nabla \cdot$, explicit entropy bound via Edmunds (2003) for Sobolev balls, and concentration via Boucheron et al. (2013). The technical conditions ($2(s-1+\ell) > d$, $\lambda < 1$, $1 - e^{-2t} \le \log(n)/C$, $s \le k-2$) are all explicit. The condition $1 - e^{-2t} \le \log(n)/C$ is a real restriction: it means the result is stated for **small** times, where most of the SGM mass is concentrated on the manifold; large-time behavior is handled separately (Remark 11) by falling back to a coarser bound.
- **Reproducibility.** Theory paper, so reproducibility is conceptual. The losses, classes, and proofs are all self-contained and should be re-derivable. There is no code, no empirical validation, no suggestion that the ERM/Sobolev-ball setup is computationally tractable at scale.
- **Significance.** The paper provides a **unified, assumption-light** minimax theory for score-based generative modeling that does not depend on neural-network-specific structural assumptions. This is valuable for understanding *why* Sobolev-style regularizers (which can coincide with weight-decay-type penalties for simple nets, per Williams et al. 2019) empirically help — a point the authors explicitly raise. It is also competitive with, and arguably improves on, the line of work by Oko et al. (2023), Stéphanovitch (2025), and Tang & Yang (2024), all of which impose structural assumptions on the score function class.
- **Red flags / weaknesses:**
  - **No "score-to-sample" conjecture.** Earlier versions of the abstract (and discussion in the literature) treated the SGM sample-quality rate as conditional on a conjecture. In the current paper the claim is proved directly (Corollary 20) via a quantitative coupling argument. This is a real improvement over an earlier version of the program (e.g., Oko et al. 2023, who needed a particular spline-based network structure).
  - **Compact-manifold assumption.** $M$ is required compact and without boundary, $k$-smooth, with reach $\ge \tau_{\min} > 0$. This rules out unbounded or non-smoothly-embedded supports and is part of the standard "manifolds with reach" hypothesis family (cf. Divol 2022, Niles-Weed & Berthet 2022). It is **not** the regime of full $\mathbb{R}^D$ Euclidean estimation, which would be needed for the original sub-Gaussian results of Stéphanovitch et al. (2025).
  - **No experiments.** The paper offers no empirical validation that the bounds are tight, that Sobolev-ball-constrained ERM is feasible at modern SGM scale, or that the suggested weight-decay / second-derivative penalty connection is quantitatively predictive. The connection is acknowledged as speculative and tied to "simple" networks.
  - **Logarithmic restriction $1 - e^{-2t} \le \log(n)/C$.** Practical SGM time horizons are typically larger than $\log(n)$; the result does not directly cover them (Remark 11 partially fills the gap with a coarser $s = -1$ bound, but at the cost of slower rates).
  - **Class radius $\propto 1/(1-e^{-2t})$ is huge.** For typical $t \sim 10^{-3}$ the radius $\sim 10^{3}$, so the class is enormous. The proof works because the bias term is correspondingly small (the true score is bounded in the same scaled class), but the practical content is that any "sensible" large enough class works — which is consistent with practice but also weakens the prescriptive content.
  - **No adaptation to unknown $s$.** The hypothesis class depends on the smoothness $s$, the bandwidth $h$, and the radius $R$. No Lepski-style adaptation is provided; the rates are oracle.

## Strengths

- A clean, technically careful minimax theory for **two** important problems (direct score estimation and SGM-based measure estimation) using a **single structural tool** (Sobolev-ball constraint).
- Avoids the heavy structural assumptions on neural networks used in the Oko et al. (2023) / Stéphanovitch (2025) line, replacing them with a purely analytic capacity condition.
- The $W_1$ SGM rate (Corollary 20) is proved directly, not just conjectured as in some related works.
- Useful for explaining, post hoc, why second-derivative penalization / weight decay helps in SGM training.
- Strong author lineup for a probability/theory paper; well-supported by French ANR/Inria grants.

## Limitations

- **Domain restrictions:** torus (Part 1) and compact $k$-smooth submanifold with positive reach (Part 2). No result for $\mathbb{R}^D$ with sub-Gaussian tails, no result for non-smoothly-embedded supports, no adaptation to unknown $s$.
- **Time restriction:** $1 - e^{-2t} \le \log(n)/C$ is required for the precise Theorem 8 bound; the larger-$t$ fallback (Remark 11) sacrifices rate quality.
- **Practical gap:** the hypothesis class is an analytic Sobolev ball, not a tractable parametric family. There is no algorithm, no discretization, no demonstration that ERM over such a class can be implemented for high-dimensional image data.
- **No experiments, no neural networks, no comparison to DSM / sliced score matching / NCSN / DDPM.** The paper is purely theoretical and does not engage with empirical SGM benchmarks.
- **The connection between Sobolev-ball ERM and weight decay is speculative** and explicitly flagged as such by the authors.

## Relevance

与本仓库关注的"score-based 生成模型与统计学习理论"高度相关：本文为 SGM 提供了不依赖神经网络结构的 minimax 理论，给出"Sobolev 球约束足以同时控制 score 估计与 Wasserstein 采样质量"这一统一视角。适合作为 score estimation / diffusion 理论方向的参考文献，对关心 SGM 收敛速率、capacity 控制、或为何实际训练中正则化有效的读者尤其有用。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the **two-regime** structure of Theorem 8 be made uniform over $t$ without the logarithmic restriction $1 - e^{-2t} \le \log(n)/C$? In particular, can the large-$t$ fallback from Remark 11 be sharpened to the same rate as the small-$t$ bound?
- Is the result **adaptive to unknown smoothness $s$**? Can a Lepski-type or aggregation procedure be designed over the Sobolev-ball family that matches the minimax rate without knowledge of $s$, $R$, or the reach $\tau_{\min}$?
- Can the proof extend beyond compact $k$-smooth submanifolds to $\mathbb{R}^D$ with sub-Gaussian / Besov densities, matching Stéphanovitch et al. (2025) without requiring neural-network structure?
- Is the **conjectural correspondence between Sobolev penalties and weight decay** (Williams et al. 2019) provable for broader network classes (e.g., ReLU MLPs with bounded second moments)? A positive answer would close the gap between this paper's theory and practical SGM training.
- The choice $h = n^{-1/(d+2s)}$ is **dimension-adapted** in the manifold intrinsic dimension $d$, not the ambient $D$. This is a sharp feature for manifold-supported measures; can it be turned into a **data-driven manifold dimension estimator** for the hypothesis class?
- Empirical: does ERM over a Sobolev ball (or, equivalently, score matching with a $\|\nabla^{s-1} g\|_{L^2}^2$ penalty, for small $s \in \{1,2\}$) on a tractable parametric family (e.g., spline or kernel-expansion estimators) yield $W_1$ rates matching Theorem 8 / Corollary 20 on synthetic low-dimensional manifold data?
