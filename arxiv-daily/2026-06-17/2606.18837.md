> **一句话总结：** Skill-MAS 把"自动多智能体系统生成"从参数更新中解耦出来，将 Meta-agent 的高层编排能力建模为一份可演化的 **Meta-Skill**（任务分解 + 智能体工程 + 工作流编排三模块），通过多轨迹采样构建行为分布、再用基于"不确定度–难度"与肘部截断的 Selective Reflection 蒸馏出可迁移的策略级原理，在四个基准和四个 LLM 上以更低推理成本稳定优于 EvoAgent / AOrchestra / AFlow / MAS2 / MAS-Orchestra 五种 SOTA baseline。

> **TL;DR:** Skill-MAS reformulates automatic Multi-Agent System (MAS) generation as **evolving a textual Meta-Skill** (no gradient updates) held outside a frozen frontier LLM. Each round runs **Multi-Trajectory Rollout** (K independent rollouts per task under the current skill) to convert single-pass outcomes into distributional statistics — per-task mean score, uncertainty (std), and difficulty (negated mean). **Selective Reflection** then (a) normalizes uncertainty and difficulty, blends them into a priority score, and uses the maximum second-order difference of the priority curve as an *elbow* to select a high-priority subset of tasks; and (b) applies hierarchical contrastive analysis — within-task (high- vs. low-scoring trajectories) then cross-task — to distill systemic evidence and a prioritized patch list. The skill optimizer rewrites the affected module into generalizable principles. Across **DeepResearchBench, Humanity's Last Exam-Math, BrowseComp-Plus, and VitaBench** with **Gemini-3.1-Flash, GPT-5.4-Nano, Qwen3.5-Plus, DeepSeek-V4-Flash**, Skill-MAS-optimized wins on virtually every cell (e.g., 41.05 vs. best baseline 35.70 on DeepSeek; 38.41 vs. 32.23 on Qwen) at moderate inference cost, and the evolved skills transfer to different LLMs and unseen tasks (gains of +2.97 to +9.53 over init).

## 核心贡献

- **第三条路径的范式确立：** 明确刻画了现有自动 MAS 研究的二元困境——推理时方法（冻结前沿 LLM + 迭代搜索）无法跨任务积累经验；训练时方法（在小 LLM 上做 DPO/GRPO 蒸馏编排能力）受限于小模型能力天花板且难以扩展到 >100B 前沿模型——并提出 Skill-MAS 作为"解耦经验保留与参数更新"的第三种范式。
- **Meta-Skill 的三模块结构化抽象：** 把 Meta-agent 的高层编排能力具象化为可被定位修改的三个模块——任务分解（the what）、智能体工程（the who）、工作流编排（the how）——既给推理时 MAS 生成提供 principled 流程，又让诊断阶段能把失败精确定位到具体模块，使演化更新保持局部化与可解释。
- **闭环优化算法：** Multi-Trajectory Rollout 把单次结果转化为行为分布（均值分数、std 不确定度、−mean 难度）；Selective Reflection 通过 min–max 归一化的"不确定度+难度"优先级分数与离散二阶差分最大点定位的"肘部"自动选任务，再用 **within-task + cross-task** 的分层对比分析输出按影响排序的补丁；最后由 Skill Optimizer 在保留三模块骨架的前提下把补丁抽象为可泛化原理。
- **经验性的强迁移证据：** 在四个领域跨度极大的基准和四个 LLM 上系统评估；并通过 Source LLM/Task 与 Test LLM/Task 的交叉实验（cross-LLM、cross-task、cross-LLM+cross-task）证明演化的 Meta-Skill 具备跨模型与跨任务的迁移能力。
- **更好的成本-性能权衡：** 相比 Inference-time MAS（每样本迭代搜索成本高）和 Training-time MAS（训练成本高 + 推理时质量差），Skill-MAS 在测试时一次性生成 MAS、推理开销适中，同时平均性能最高（Figure 1(d) 的 cost–performance 散点）。

## Key Contributions

- **A third path for automatic MAS generation:** Bridges the dilemma between Inference-time MAS (frozen frontier LLMs, no experience retention) and Training-time MAS (gradient updates, bounded by small-model capability ceiling, hard to scale to >100B models).
- **Structured Meta-Skill abstraction:** The orchestration capability is encoded as a three-module skill — **Task Decomposition**, **Agent Engineering**, **Workflow Orchestration** — enabling both principled MAS generation and localized, interpretable updates.
- **Closed optimization loop:**
  - *Multi-Trajectory Rollout* samples K=5 trajectories per task, computing per-task mean score `s̄_i`, uncertainty `u_i = std(s_i,k)`, and difficulty `d_i = −s̄_i`.
  - *Selective Reflection* normalizes `u_i, d_i` (min–max), blends them into `p_i = ½(ũ_i + d̃_i)`, sorts, and uses **discrete second-order difference of priority** to find an elbow `j*` for adaptive task selection.
  - *Hierarchical contrastive analysis* — within-task (high vs. low scoring trajectories) then cross-task — produces per-task reports `R_i`, candidate patches `δ̂_i`, and a ranked evidence package `E`.
  - *Skill Optimizer* rewrites the implicated module into generalizable principles while preserving the three-module scaffold and rejecting plans that violate structural validity.
- **Strong empirical evidence:** 4 benchmarks × 4 LLMs; Skill-MAS-optimized wins on virtually all 16 cells, while both Training-time baselines (MAS2, MAS-Orchestra) underperform even Skill-MAS-init on most settings.
- **Transferability:** Cross-LLM and cross-task transfer experiments show consistent +2.97 to +9.53 gains over Skill-MAS-init; cross-LLM+cross-task also positive (+1.19 to +3.57) though smaller.
- **Better cost–performance trade-off:** The paper argues (and Table 6/Fig. 1d show) that training/evolution costs are amortizable — the only inference-time cost is one-shot MAS generation — beating both AFlow (per-sample search) and training-time baselines (perplexed low capability).

## Methodology

**Problem setting.** Let `T = {t_1, …, t_N}` be a held-out validation set and `S^(r)` the active Meta-Skill in round `r ∈ {1, …, R}` (R=10 in experiments). Each round runs two stages and outputs `S^(r+1)`; after R rounds the validation-best skill `S*` is evaluated on the test set.

**Stage 1 — Multi-Trajectory Rollout.** For each task `t_i`, sample K=5 independent rollouts under `S^(r)`. Each rollout records a standardized trajectory
`τ_{i,k} = (id_i, k, s_{i,k}, S^(r), Φ_{i,k})`,
where `s_{i,k} ∈ [0,1]` is the task's normalized score and `Φ_{i,k}` stores the MAS architecture and intermediate results. From the K trajectories per task, two distributional statistics are computed:
- `s̄_i = (1/K) Σ_k s_{i,k}` — mean performance;
- `u_i = std(s_{i,1:K})` — uncertainty (large ⇒ ambiguous/underspecified rules);
- `d_i = −s̄_i` — difficulty (large ⇒ systematically hard).

This converts each task from a single pass/fail outcome into a *behavioral distribution* and is what lets downstream reflection distinguish transient execution noise from systemic skill deficiencies.

**Stage 2 — Selective Reflection.**

*(a) Priority-driven task selection.* Normalize `u_i, d_i ∈ [0,1]` via min–max, blend into `p_i = ½(ũ_i + d̃_i)`, sort `p_(1) ≥ … ≥ p_(N)`, and apply the discrete analogue of the second derivative:
`j* = arg max_{j ∈ {1,…,N−2}} |p_(j) − 2 p_(j+1) + p_(j+2)|`.
The selected subset `T_sel = {t_(1), …, t_(j*)}` is the natural elbow of the priority curve, isolating volatile+difficult tasks where diagnostic signal is densest.

*(b) Hierarchical contrastive analysis.* Two phases:
- *Within-task:* split K trajectories into high-score `H_i` and low-score `L_i` (median split). An LLM-based reflector compares execution snapshots `Φ_{i,k}` across `H_i` vs. `L_i` to extract success factors, failure modes, and root causes — outputting a per-task report `R_i` and a candidate patch `δ̂_i`.
- *Cross-task:* jointly analyze `{R_i}` to identify systemic patterns (recurring weaknesses, robust strategies to preserve), then rank `{δ̂_i}` by expected impact + feasibility into a structured evidence package `E`.

*(c) Skill optimization.* The optimizer reviews `S^(r)` against `E`, removes/rewrites rules that evidence marks as ineffective, and adds new ones — *targeted at the implicated module only* and *abstracted into generalizable principles* (no task-specific fixes). Each candidate `S^(r+1)` passes a structural validity check before being accepted.

**Initial skill.** `S^(1)` is a LLM-summarized version of Anthropic's multi-agent guidance (Appendix E), structured into the same three modules.

## Results & Highlights

**Benchmarks & Models.** DeepResearchBench (DRB), Humanity's Last Exam-Math (HLE, 200-question sample), BrowseComp-Plus (BCP, 200-question sample), VitaBench (interactive multi-tool, 100 cross-scenario tasks). Splits: validation 16/32/32/16; test 84/168/168/84 (Table 4). Backbones: Gemini-3.1-Flash, GPT-5.4-Nano, Qwen3.5-Plus, DeepSeek-V4-Flash. Hyperparameters: R=10 rounds, K=5 rollouts, temperature=1.0, max_tokens=32768 (Table 5). The LLM-judge is Gemini-3.1-Flash.

**Main quantitative results (Table 1, Avg.Perf %).** Skill-MAS-optimized achieves the best average on all 4 backbones:

| Backbone | Best baseline (Avg.Perf) | Skill-MAS-init | Skill-MAS-optimized | Δ vs. best baseline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Gemini-3.1-Flash | AFlow 21.29 | 21.68 | **29.49** | +8.20 |
| GPT-5.4-Nano | EvoAgent 24.83 | 19.64 | **27.55** | +2.72 |
| Qwen3.5-Plus | AFlow 32.23 | 32.61 | **38.41** | +6.18 |
| DeepSeek-V4-Flash | AFlow 35.70 | 33.72 | **41.05** | +5.35 |

Skill-MAS-init is already competitive with the best inference-time baseline on Gemini/Qwen, and Skill-MAS-optimized is best on 15/16 (LLM, benchmark) cells. The only loss: DRB with GPT-5.4-Nano, where EvoAgent scores 52.91 vs. Skill-MAS 48.90. Training-time baselines (MAS2, MAS-Orchestra) are *consistently below Skill-MAS-init* across all 4 backbones.

**Cost analysis (Table 1, Table 6).** Per-test-sample cost of Skill-MAS is comparable to or below most inference-time baselines (e.g., $1.14–5.22 vs. EvoAgent $3.33–8.20). Evolution cost on validation set is $9.35 (Gemini) to $59.06 (Qwen); amortized across unlimited test queries, the per-query cost is much lower than per-sample iterative search (AFlow's per-query cost is low but it is consistently the weakest). Training-time methods are excluded from direct training-cost comparison but suffer in quality.

**Transferability (Table 2, Figure 3 Left).** Five panels:
- *No transfer (same LLM + same task)*: +7.14 to +9.53 gain over init — strongest.
- *Cross-LLM (Panel A, same task, different LLM)*: +2.97 to +9.53 — robust, task-specific skills transfer well.
- *Cross-task (Panel B, same LLM, different task)*: +3.57 to +9.53 — supports the "task-agnostic principle" design.
- *Cross-LLM + cross-task (Panel C)*: +1.19 to +3.57 — smallest but still positive.

**Ablations (Figure 3 Right; Table 3).**
- *K (rollouts per task)*: scaling from 3 → 5 → 7 gives diminishing returns, with K=5 chosen as the cost-quality sweet spot.
- *Selective Reflection label dependence*: removing adaptive selection (Full-Validation selects all, Half-Validation random 50%) drops BCP/VITA scores by 2–6 points; even so, both label-free variants beat most baselines. Authors flag label-free evolution as future work.
- *Multi-task Learning*: evolving one skill on a pool of all 4 datasets slightly helps VitaBench but hurts BrowseComp-Plus — Skill-MAS is *not* designed for cross-domain multi-task by default and would need principled multi-task algorithms.

**Qualitative evolution trace (Figure 4).** On BrowseComp-Plus (DeepSeek-V4-Flash) the Meta-Skill evolves over 5 rounds from a generic decomposition recipe toward operational specifications: (1) constraint prioritization + parallel fan-out retrieval; (2) weighted constraint satisfaction with partial-evidence fallback (replaces brittle binary checks); (3) cross-entity bridging + merge-node re-execution (shifts integration from passive aggregation to active evidence recovery). The progression is interpretable and module-localized.

**Case study (Tables 7, 8).** On BrowseComp-Plus, Skill-MAS-optimized splits a multi-clue query into clue-specific retrieval branches and enforces cross-clue consistency before the final decision. On VitaBench (a complex 4-sub-task real-world scenario: order light food → find quiet book bar → buy tea coupon → buy 3 pm train ticket), it inserts an explicit `constraint_extraction` node that dispatches three explicit `exploration → evaluation → ordering` branches and a `final_summary` aggregator — beating all baselines whose linear pipelines drop or contaminate constraints.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (★★★½).** The "third path" framing is mostly a re-framing of *prompt-as-parameters* — a Meta-Skill is functionally a learned prompt/skill document, and the closest prior art (GEPA, OPRO, TextGrad, MemSkill, EvoSkills, SkillMAS) also evolves natural-language artifacts through LLM-judged feedback loops. The paper does add three concrete things beyond prompt-tuning: (i) a structured three-module scaffold that makes updates *localized*; (ii) the uncertainty–difficulty + discrete-second-derivative elbow for adaptive task selection; (iii) within-task then cross-task hierarchical contrastive analysis. The first is the most consequential contribution. The second/third are reasonable engineering but not obviously novel over e.g., active-learning uncertainty sampling + pairwise LLM-judge comparison.

**Rigor (★★★★).** This is the strongest aspect. Strengths: (1) five SOTA baselines spanning both inference-time and training-time tracks; (2) four LLMs spanning proprietary frontier + open-source; (3) ablations on K, Selective Reflection label-dependence, and multi-task; (4) explicit transferability matrix with all four cross settings; (5) per-sample cost + amortized evolution cost reported separately. **Concerns**: (a) no variance / seed reporting — every number is a single point estimate, so the +2 to +8 deltas are not accompanied by confidence intervals; (b) the LLM-judge is Gemini-3.1-Flash for all four backbones — a judge-model confound when comparing backbones is possible; (c) the validation set sizes (16–32) are small, raising the question of whether the "elbow" is robust when the priority curve is short and noisy.

**Reproducibility (★★★).** Pseudocode (Algorithm 1) is given and the paper commits to releasing the code. The appendix is unusually thorough: dataset statistics (Table 4), hyperparameter table (Table 5), per-backbone cost (Table 6), full initial Meta-Skill (Figure 5), four full optimized Meta-Skills (Figures 6–14), all LLM-as-a-judge prompts (Appendix F). However, the reflection step is LLM-based and the exact prompts matter; without the full prompt appendix at the time of review this is a *partial* reproducibility win.

**Significance (★★★★).** Even if the "third path" framing is mostly editorial, the empirical artifact is significant: a MAS-generation system that works on top of a frozen frontier LLM (no GPU fine-tuning, no proprietary-data curation, no >100B model RL) and wins 15/16 cells against five strong baselines is a useful design pattern for practitioners. The transferability claim is the most consequential — if the strategy-level principles are *truly* LLM-agnostic, then a single Skill-MAS run on a cheap model could be deployed across an organization's fleet.

**Red flags / things to verify.**
- The Meta-Skill gets *much* longer after optimization (compare the initial Figure 5 vs. the optimized Figures 6–14). A 4–5× longer prompt can itself drive part of the gain via in-context learning / attention dilution — not pure skill learning. The paper does not isolate "more verbose prompt" from "better skill."
- Some optimization rules read like *output-format contracts* ("each analytical sub-task must be allocated at least 4000 expected tokens, each synthesis sub-task at least 6000 tokens"). These are LLM-engineering tricks, not orchestration principles. It's plausible the gain partly comes from forced verbosity + structured output, not from the meta-level *evolution* mechanism.
- The cross-task transferability claim rests on the prompt explicitly telling the Meta-agent to "avoid domain-specific tricks and focus on general patterns" (Sec. 5.2.1). That's a strong prior, and the gains shrink as that prior weakens (Panel C).
- The label-dependence ablation (Table 3) shows that without ground-truth labels the gain shrinks; the paper acknowledges this but it's a real practical limitation for deployment.
- GPT-5.4-Nano results are notably worse than Qwen/DeepSeek across the board, including on Skill-MAS-init (19.64) — suggesting the *initial* skill is already bottlenecked by the Meta-agent's reasoning quality, and evolution can only do so much. This deserves more discussion.

## Strengths

- Clear, well-motivated framing of a real dilemma in automatic-MAS research.
- Decoupling experience from parameter updates is conceptually clean and scales to any frontier LLM (no fine-tuning, no data curation, no GPU).
- Closed-loop design with localized, module-aware updates is genuinely useful — most prompt-evolution work is global and brittle.
- Strong empirical comparison: 4 benchmarks × 4 LLMs × 5 baselines, with ablations on K, label dependence, and multi-task.
- Transferability is *measured*, not just claimed.
- Code + prompts commitment + detailed appendix.
- Cost analysis is honest (separates evolution cost from inference cost, notes the latter is the deployment bottleneck).

## Limitations

- **Heavy reliance on ground-truth labels** for Selective Reflection. The label-free variants drop 2–6 points; LLM-self-confidence is suggested but not implemented.
- **Verbose Meta-Skill prompt bloat** — the optimized skills are 4–5× longer than the initial, which itself may drive part of the gain.
- **Small validation sets** (16–32 tasks) limit the statistical power of the elbow-based task selection.
- **No variance reporting** — every number is a single point estimate.
- **Judge-model confound** — Gemini-3.1-Flash judges all four backbones, possibly biasing the comparison.
- **Multi-task learning does not work out of the box** — the paper notes this is "not surprising" since Skill-MAS is not designed for it, but it is a practical limitation for general deployment.
- **Reflection is LLM-based and prompt-sensitive** — performance depends on reflector quality, which is hard to audit.
- The method is **not compared to recent strong prompt-evolution baselines** (e.g., GEPA, OPRO, TextGrad, PromptAgent) that solve a similar "evolve a textual artifact" problem.

## Relevance

Skill-MAS 直接切入"自动多智能体系统生成"这一当前 LLM-Agent 研究的核心议题，针对"推理时方法无经验"与"训练时方法受限于小模型能力"这一长期 trade-off 提出了"将编排能力外化为可演化的 Meta-Skill"的解耦范式，与本人关注的 agent 自动化设计、跨任务/跨模型经验迁移、prompt-as-parameter 演化方向高度相关。其在冻结前沿 LLM 上实现 SOTA 级别 MAS 生成的实证结果、以及 Meta-Skill 跨任务与跨 LLM 的迁移性，对实际工程部署（避免昂贵微调、复用编排策略）具有直接借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How much of the Skill-MAS gain survives a controlled prompt-length baseline (i.e., Skill-MAS vs. a same-length but un-evolved prompt)? This would isolate the "verbose prompt" effect from genuine skill evolution.
- Does the LLM-judge confound matter? Re-running the comparison with a held-out judge LLM (e.g., GPT-5.4-Nano as judge for Gemini results) would clarify.
- How does Skill-MAS compare head-to-head with **GEPA, OPRO, TextGrad, or PromptAgent** on the same four benchmarks? These are the closest "evolve a textual artifact" baselines; the paper conspicuously omits them.
- Can the reflection loop be made **label-free** with sufficient quality? The paper flags self-confidence as a candidate — does it actually work?
- Does the **Meta-Skill plateau** (token count vs. round), or does it grow unboundedly? Prompt bloat is a known failure mode of prompt evolution.
- The evolved skill has 4–5× more "output-format contracts" than the initial — is the gain primarily from the *meta* principles or from the *operational* specifications? A targeted ablation that ablates each sub-module's evolved rules would answer this.
- Can the **principles be audited and edited by a human** to inject domain knowledge? The paper claims interpretability but does not demonstrate human-in-the-loop editing.
- How does the framework behave when the validation distribution **drifts** from the test distribution? The transferability panels are all *near-distribution*; truly out-of-domain evaluation (e.g., a code-generation benchmark) is missing.
- Is the **elbow heuristic robust** to the priority-curve shape? With N=16–32 the curve is short; what happens at N=100+?
- Can the per-task K be **adaptive** (K=2 for easy tasks, K=7 for volatile ones) to reduce the cost-quality gap?
