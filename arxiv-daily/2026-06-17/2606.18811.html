Key Finding

A one-line init-time rescale W_head ← W_head / k cures SPLADE training collapse on modern encoders — no architecture, loss, or inference change.

Rescaling MLM-Head for Neural Sparse Retrieval

Youngjoon Jang · Seongtae Hong · Jonah Turner · Heuiseok Lim (Korea University)

learned-sparse-retrieval SPLADE MLM-head-rescaling training-stability modern-encoders 2026-06-17
Key Metric
+215%

ModernBERT BEIR-13 nDCG@10 relative gain after rescaling (0.127 → 0.400 at k=16). Ettin follows at +77%.

Why This Matters

Stronger encoders were actively hurting SPLADE retrieval. A free init-time rescale unlocks them, reframing the bottleneck as MLM-head scale calibration — not encoder capacity.

Critical Edge

Optimal k is per-backbone with no principled rule, and excessive down-scaling catastrophically collapses training (RoBERTa k=16 → 0.004 BEIR-13).

Methodology diagnosis → fix

  • Setting: SPLADE-style sparse retrieval on MS MARCO triplets; in-batch contrastive ranking loss + FLOPS regularization.
  • Scale-coupling mechanism: sparse vector v = max_i log(1+ReLU(H·W_headᵀ)), matched by an un-normalized dot product — so ‖W_head‖ directly scales activations, scores, and gradients.
  • Failure correlates with norm, not anisotropy: small-norm backbones (‖W‖<2: BERT, DistilBERT, ALBERT, GTE-MLM) train stably; large-norm (‖W‖>2: RoBERTa, ModernBERT, Ettin) collapse.
  • Fix: W_head ← W_head / k once at init (tied weights handled so sharing is preserved). Zero new parameters.
  • Training: AdamW, LR 1e-4, 1 epoch, batch 64×8 on RTX A6000, bf16, max seq 256.

Strengths vs. Limitations

✓ Strengths

  • Clean diagnosis: ties an abstract symptom (unstable training) to a testable mechanism — un-normalized dot product × MLM-head scale.
  • Minimal intervention: one line, init-only; easy to adopt and reason about.
  • Broad survey: seven backbones (BERT, DistilBERT, ALBERT, GTE-MLM, RoBERTa, ModernBERT, Ettin) with explicit head statistics.
  • Multi-metric across MS MARCO, TREC-DL'19, BEIR-13, NanoBEIR; loss curves give direct optimization evidence.
  • Frontier analysis shows gains come from better calibration, not denser representations.

✗ Limitations

  • k is a per-backbone hyperparameter with no closed-form rule — small but real tuning cost.
  • Narrow scale band: over-scaling collapses training; "zero-cost" framing is slightly optimistic.
  • Tested only on English MS MARCO → BEIR-13; no multilingual/long-doc/code experiments.
  • No head-to-head with dense retrievers on the same backbones — competitive ceiling unclear.
  • Vanilla SPLADE only; transfer to SPLADE-v3 / uniCOIL / TILDE-v2 unverified.

Open Questions for further exploration

  • Can optimal k be derived a priori from ‖W_head‖_init (target a fixed post-norm ~0.3–0.6), removing the per-backbone search?
  • Does the same scale mismatch arise in SPLADE-v3, uniCOIL, TILDE-v2, Milco, and do they need different factors?
  • How do rescaled LSR models compare head-to-head with dense retrievers (GTE, Qwen3-Embedding, mE5) on the same backbones?
  • Does output-head scale calibration generalize to other "logit-as-feature" tasks — slot filling, neural ranking?
  • Could a learned per-row rescale beat a scalar for anisotropic heads?
