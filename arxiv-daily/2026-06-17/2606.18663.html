Key Finding

Trajectories beat endpoints: RegMix-D turns proxy loss curves into a dynamic schedule, topping RegMix at 25% of its proxy budget.

RegMix-D: Dynamic Data Mixing via Proxy Training Trajectories

Kaiyan Zhao · Zhongtao Miao · Akiko Aizawa · Yoshimasa Tsuruoka — University of Tokyo & NII

data-mixture pretraining large-language-models proxy-models regression dynamic-mixing
Key Metric
48.44

avg score on 13 downstream tasks (Online, 512 proxies) — vs RegMix 47.41, DoReMi 47.10, Human 44.80. Top-1/2 on 11/13 tasks.

Why This Matters

Proxy runs already encode when mixtures should change. Exploiting loss trajectories cuts proxy compute ~4× while improving accuracy — a drop-in upgrade for existing RegMix pipelines.

Critical Edge

Validated only at 1B / 25B tokens with a single seed and a single validation signal. The offline-vs-online gap (~0.2) is not bootstrap-validated; no head-to-head with Aioli / TiKMiX.

Methodology

From endpoints to trajectories. RegMix records only the endpoint validation loss of each proxy run, then fits mixture → endpoint loss. RegMix-D instead slices each proxy trajectory into N+1 intervals and trains a regressor on the local transition (step, mixture, current_loss) → next_loss.

Trajectory-conditioned regression

For N switch points, pick N+1 step intervals per proxy run. Each adjacent pair yields one example (t_ℓ, m⁽ⁱ⁾, ℓ_ℓ) → ℓ_ℓ₊₁, giving M×N pairs. LightGBM is the regressor. Locality (one-step-ahead, not endpoint) is what makes dynamic scheduling possible.

Two deployment modes — one regressor

Offline (Algorithm 1): recursively query the regressor to pre-compute a full N-switch schedule. First segment uses the human Pile prior; each later segment samples candidates ~ Dir(α·m₀) and keeps the mean of the top-k lowest predicted loss.

Online (Algorithm 2): load the regressor as a frozen predictor on the target. At each switch point, replace predicted loss with the target's observed validation loss (cross-scale corrected: ℓ̃ = ℓ̂·(P_t/P_p)^β, β=0.05) and re-pick the mixture. This grounds decisions in real target dynamics.

Online > Offline in both proxy budgets (gap 0.22 @128, 0.35 @512). 128→512 yields only +0.16/+0.29, so the dynamic-vs-static gap dominates. Overhead: Offline ≈ 0%, Online +0.37% wall-clock (21.77h → 21.85h on 8 GPUs). Ablations: robust across N∈{3,5,7,9}, β∈{0.01–0.10} (max var 0.25), proxy size 1M/60M/120M (≤0.05).

Strengths

  • Simple, well-motivated extension; drops trivially into existing RegMix pipelines.
  • ~4× proxy-budget reduction (512→128) while still beating the 4×-heavier RegMix.
  • Two deployment modes (offline + online) with negligible target-side overhead.
  • Thorough ablations show the gain is not from a single tuned knob.
  • Clear three-axis positioning vs Aioli / TiKMiX / AC-ODM.

Limitations

  • Only validated at 1B / 25B tokens on the Pile; larger-scale transfer unknown.
  • Single validation signal (pile-cc loss); multi-objective mixing out of scope.
  • Single seed; offline-vs-online gap (~0.2–0.35) not statistically validated.
  • 17-domain Pile partition is a free choice and a confounder.
  • Online validation passes are cached but not free; α, C, top-k inherited, not ablated.
  • LogiQA & MultiRC trend slightly negative vs RegMix in the 512-Online setting.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the dynamic schedule transfer to ≥7B / ≥300B tokens, or does the optimal schedule itself scale with model size?
  2. Why does online beat offline by a smaller margin than expected — is the recursive-error story correct, or is the gain mostly the cross-scale correction?
  3. Can the trajectory signal replace a denser grid of proxy mixtures entirely, or only complement it?
  4. Does it generalize beyond the Pile — to FineWeb, SlimPajama, multilingual or code-heavy mixtures?
  5. What is the per-stage mixture doing qualitatively (which domains dominate early vs. late)?
  6. Could a longer-horizon regressor (k>1 steps ahead) close the offline-vs-online gap?
  7. How sensitive is the schedule to Dirichlet α, candidate count C, and top-k — all inherited without re-tuning?
  8. How does RegMix-D compare head-to-head with Aioli / TiKMiX / AC-ODM under a unified protocol?
