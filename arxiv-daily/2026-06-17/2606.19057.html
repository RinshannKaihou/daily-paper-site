Reframes selective human supervision as Positive–Unlabelled learning; uses Partial Optimal Transport to flip biased LLM-judge preferences — no retraining.

Quantifying and Auditing LLM Evaluation via Positive–Unlabeled Learning
Zilong Zhang, Yi-Ting Hung, Lei Ding, Chi-Kuang Yeh — Georgia State · Manitoba
LLM-as-a-Judge Positive–Unlabelled Partial Optimal Transport Evaluation Bias Verbosity Bias
Headline Metric
+2 to +26pp
Human-preference consistency gain across 4 LLM judges × 2 benchmarks (Chatbot Arena, MT-Bench) × 6 question types, no-attack setting.
Peak +25.9 pp on Mistral-7B / QTF. Also +0.045 on MT-Bench with the Skywork-Reward encoder.
Why This Matters

A retraining-free auditor for any LLM judge — gives per-sample confidence so humans can selectively re-review only the comparisons the algorithm flags, instead of relabelling everything.

Critical Edge

With a non-reward-model encoder the method can silently flip 100% of labels and tank accuracy by ~39 pp — with no built-in detector for encoder suitability.

Method

  1. Encode. A frozen Skywork-Reward-Llama-3.1-8B encoder maps each (prompt, response) pair to a vector. The encoder is never fine-tuned; the entire audit lives on top of these embeddings.
  2. Differentialize. Reduce each pairwise comparison to a unit direction z = (F(ow)−F(oℓ)) / ‖·‖ on the hypersphere Sd−1 — encodes relative quality, not absolute content.
  3. Denoise positives. Two-stage geometric filter on the human-verified set: P-core (closest to the positive centre) + improvement-alignment (most aligned with the average positive direction). Defaults α1 = α2 = 0.7.
  4. Partial Optimal Transport. Solve for Γ* between the cleaned positives and the unlabelled pool with inequality marginals — unmatched mass is left alone, not forced into spurious matches.
  5. Score & flip. s̄j measures how reachable sample j is from the verified-positive manifold. Below threshold η (default 0.5), the LLM judge's preference is flipped; otherwise it is kept.

Transport mass m is estimated from held-out human consistency — a circular shortcut the paper acknowledges, since a biased judge and a high positive mass look identical from outside.

Under bias attacks

Length, sentiment and distraction attacks cut Consorig sharply. PUAUDIT recovers most of the lost alignment; the radar profile flattens to near-circular, equalising bias across categories rather than overfitting to one. Largest gains on the most bias-prone types (Writing, Advice); smallest on objective types (Programming, Math) — exactly the regime where bias is least semantic.

Strengths

  • Principled reframing. Selective human supervision → PU learning is the clearest conceptual move; POT is a well-trodden tool given a genuinely new application.
  • Retraining-free & plug-in. Drops onto any existing LLM judge with a frozen reward-model encoder and a small verified-positive set.
  • Per-instance interpretable confidence. s̄j drives selective human review — only the comparisons the algorithm actually flags.
  • Two-stage denoising is a practical contribution in its own right and is what makes the stability bound bite.
  • Theory ↔ experiments are tightly coupled: Thm 4.5's stability bound is mirrored by the attack results; the encoder ablation is honestly reported (including the catastrophic EmbeddingGemma failure).

Limitations

  • Silently fragile. Wrong encoder → 100% flipped labels; no internal check that the encoder is appropriate for auditing.
  • Circular class prior. m is estimated from held-out judge consistency, conflating "judge is good" with "many latent positives".
  • Missing baselines. No head-to-head with length-controlled prompting, position-swap ensembling, Prometheus / JudgeLM, or juries-of-LLMs.
  • Single consensus direction. Theory assumes one improvement direction; multi-manifold / multi-PU is flagged as future work.
  • Ties are dropped. Difference embedding requires strict winner–loser; no graded or rubric-based path.
  • Question-type partitioning. Needs a per-type classifier and a non-trivial sample size per type; cells with n≈7 are not reliable.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can the auditor be certified online so silent failure (the EmbeddingGemma case) is detected before labels are flipped?
  2. Can m be estimated from the structure of Γ* itself, breaking the circular dependency on judge quality?
  3. How does PUAUDIT compare head-to-head with length-controlled prompting, position-swap ensembling, and Prometheus 2 / JudgeLM on the same frozen judge?
  4. Can the framework extend to graded or rubric-based judgments (ties, multi-level preferences) without losing the partial-alignment interpretability?
  5. Can per-sample scores become a calibrated selection rule (e.g., conformal) with formal coverage guarantees for selective human review?
