> **一句话总结：** 提出"输出向量编辑（OVE）"——对负责记忆的 MLP 神经元施加沿干扰 token 方向的秩一权重编辑，把它们的残差流贡献从 verbatim 记忆重定向到词汇空间中的局部合理替代，从而在 OLMo-7B 上以 6,831 条记忆序列达到 87.9% 抑制（比同组神经元的零消融高 2.7×），并识别出约 14% 不可达的"机制边界"。

> **TL;DR:** Output Vector Editing (OVE) is a constrained-optimization weight edit that locates a small set of MLP neurons responsible for a memorized continuation and applies a **rank-one update** along a distractor token's unembedding direction. Unlike prior neuron-level methods that zero out activations, OVE leaves activations intact and redirects the neuron's residual-stream contribution. On OLMo-7B (32-layer SwiGLU, 6,831 mined sequences), EOS-mode OVE suppresses 87.9% of memorized sequences (vs. 31.3% for zero ablation on the *same* neurons — a 2.7× gap), and the recommended Next-best k=5 mode reaches 81.5% with **zero catastrophic locality failures**. Four edit modes (EOS, Redact, Next-best, Suppress) are complementary — ensembled they cover 96.5% of sequences. A mechanistic boundary appears at ~14% of sequences unreachable by MLP-only editing; ablating top attention heads recovers 60–64%, more so on continuations that copy tokens from the prefix, framing attention as a complementary fallback.

## 核心贡献

- **机理上区分激活与输出向量**：将 MLP 输出分解为 $\text{MLP}(x) = \sum_i a_i v_i$，指出消融激活 ($a_i = 0$) 会把该神经元叠加编码的所有方向一并丢弃，而 OVE 通过秩一编辑输出向量 $v_i \to v_i'$，只重定向其中一个语义方向，保留其余信息。
- **提出输出向量编辑（OVE）**：在每条记忆序列的定位神经元上，沿词汇空间中某"干扰 token" $s$ 的 unembedding 列 $u_s$ 做闭式解的秩一更新 $v_i' = v_i + \frac{\Delta_i - u_s^\top v_i}{\|u_s\|^2} u_s$，无需梯度。
- **系统挖掘与基线**：在 OLMo-7B 上从 Dolma v1.7 挖出 **6,831 条**记忆序列（窗级 BLEU > 0.5），并以 zero-ablation、random-neurons、activation-steering 三个基线作对照，证明增益来自编辑设计本身（与同组定位神经元的零消融相差 **2.7×**）。
- **四种编辑模式形成谱系**：EOS（end-of-sequence 终止）、Redact（用 **** 标记遮蔽）、Next-best（重定向到原次高 token）、Suppress（直接压制目标 token）——集成可覆盖 96.5%；推荐单模式 Next-best k=5 在 81.5% 成功率下无灾难性局部失败。
- **跨模型迁移与机制边界**：在 SmolLM-360M、OLMo-1B、OLMo-7B、Llama2-7B 四个模型上展示模式排序与成功-局部性权衡可迁移；并刻画出约 **14%** 序列无法被 MLP 单独编辑，但**注意力头消融**可恢复 60–64%（在前缀-延续共享非停用词 token 的情形下恢复更强），提出"MLP + 注意力"混合流水线的方向。

## Key Contributions

- **Mechanistic reframing:** Distinguishes a neuron's *activation* (engagement) from its *output vector* (residual-stream write). OVE exploits superposition: a single $v_i$ encodes multiple semantic directions, and OVE redirects one without collapsing the rest.
- **OVE method:** Closed-form rank-one update $v_i \to v_i + \alpha u_s$ per located neuron, choosing $s$ from a vocabulary-space distractor; no gradient, no inference-time cost.
- **Systematic benchmark:** 6,831 memorized sequences mined from OLMo-7B's pretraining corpus (Dolma v1.7 Books + CC-En-Head), substantially larger than prior neuron-level work (~500 sequences).
- **Strong ablation discipline:** 2.7× gap over zero ablation on the **same** located neurons (85.7% vs. 31.3% at k=5; 87.9% vs. ~32% at k=10), isolating the vector edit's contribution from localization.
- **Mode taxonomy:** Four edit modes (EOS, Redact, Next-best, Suppress) span aggressive→minimal redirection; ensemble covers 96.5%; Next-best k=5 is the recommended Pareto operating point.
- **Mechanistic boundary:** ~14% of sequences resist all MLP-based configurations; attention-head ablation recovers 60–64%, with 7–8pp higher recovery on continuations that copy tokens from the prefix. Attention is positioned as a complementary fallback, motivating a hybrid pipeline.

## Methodology

### Mechanistic premise

MLP layer output decomposes as $\text{MLP}(x) = \sum_i a_i v_i$ where $a_i$ is the post-nonlinearity activation and $v_i$ is neuron $i$'s output vector (column $i$ of $W_{\text{out}}$). For SwiGLU, $a_i = (x W_{\text{in}})_i \cdot \text{SiLU}((x W_{\text{gate}})_i)$, read via TransformerLens `hook_post`. Through superposition, one $v_i$ can encode multiple semantic directions, each promoting a different token in vocabulary space. The paper argues that zeroing $a_i$ discards the entire contribution along all directions; OVE edits $v_i$ to redirect one direction while leaving $a_i$ and the rest intact.

### Memorized-sequence mining (Carlini-style)

- Slide a 20-token window across each sentence; classify as memorized if window-level BLEU vs. ground truth > 0.5 (looser than Ippolito et al.'s 0.75 to admit near-verbatim).
- For OLMo-7B: scan Dolma v1.7 Books + CC-En-Head → 6,831 unique sequences after cross-source dedup.
- For other models: same procedure on each model's own corpus (Dolma v1.7 for OLMo-1B; smollm-corpus for SmolLM-360M) or a community-standard proxy (SlimPajama-6B for Llama2-7B).

### Two-step localization

- **Layer importance (logit lens):** For each layer $\ell$, compute $\text{LI}_t^\ell = \frac{\text{Out}_\ell \cdot W_U[:,t]}{\max_j \text{Out}_\ell \cdot W_U[:,j]}$ (out = attention or MLP output). Retain top-5 MLP layers with $\text{LI} > 0.2$ (or fewer).
- **Per-neuron attribution:** Within each selected layer, score neurons by L1 proximity: $\text{score}_i = \max(0, \|r_\ell^{\text{post}}\|_1 - \|a_i v_i - r_\ell^{\text{post}}\|_1)$, taking the top-k per layer. Keep only neurons whose unembedded $v_i \cdot W_U$ ranks the target token in the **top 50** (direct promoter) or **bottom 50** (active suppressor) of the vocabulary — mid-ranked neurons are filtered out. Skip-rate for random neurons is ≥80%, validating the localization signal.

### Output vector editing (rank-one closed form)

For each located neuron $i$ with output vector $v_i$, set the projection onto distractor token $s$'s unembedding direction to a target value $\Delta_i$, with minimal $\|v - v_i\|_2$:

$$v_i' = \arg\min_v \|v - v_i\|_2 \quad \text{s.t.} \quad u_s^\top v = \Delta_i$$

Closed-form (Lagrange multipliers):

$$v_i' = v_i + \frac{\Delta_i - u_s^\top v_i}{\|u_s\|^2} u_s$$

The target value $\Delta_i = \beta \cdot u_t^\top v_i$ scales with the neuron's existing target-token projection, so the boosting factor $\beta$ acts as a **relative** strength. The paper fixes $\beta = 100$ after a {50, 100, 200, 300, 500} sweep: higher $\beta$ improves success marginally but causes catastrophic-outlier locality patterns (e.g., $\beta=200$, k=10: mean perplexity 15.5 → 17,558). Edits affect at most 25 neurons out of ~352,000 in OLMo-7B (top 5 layers × k=5); inputs that don't activate them see no change in the forward pass.

### Four edit modes

| Mode | Distractor $s$ | Effect |
|------|---------------|--------|
| **EOS** | end-of-sequence token | Force termination at the edit position |
| **Redact** | `****` (redaction marker) | Signal content removal at the edit position |
| **Next-best** | second-ranked token from unedited forward pass | Redirect to locally plausible alternative |
| **Suppress** | $s = t$ (the target token itself) | Directly dampen target's logit by $\beta \cdot \|u_t^\top v_i\|$ |

### Baselines

- **Zero ablation:** zero the activations of the *same* located neurons ($a_i = 0$).
- **Random neurons:** apply OVE to random neurons of equal count (used to validate the localization signal).
- **Activation steering (Suri et al. 2025):** subtract a memorization-direction vector at inference; only $\alpha=0.5$ produces coherent output (higher strengths collapse into degenerate repetition).

### Evaluation

- **Edit success:** generate 20 tokens autoregressively (greedy, T=0) before/after edit; primary metric BLEU(pre, post) < 0.6, plus ANLCS and normalized Levenshtein.
- **Locality:** per-edit (weights restored between samples): (i) WikiText-103 perplexity (200 texts, 512 tokens; OLMo-7B baseline 15.52), (ii) Wikidata QA (200 samples; baseline 64.5%), (iii) HellaSwag (200 samples; baseline 73.5%). Report **median** shifts (tail fractions in Appendix D) since means are dominated by rare outliers.

### Main results on OLMo-7B (k=5, β=100; 6,124–6,444 sequences after localization)

| **EOS k=10** | **87.9** | 0.132 | 0.205 | 0.689 | 16.40 | +0.88 | 70.8 | 59.6 |
| **Next-best k=5** | 81.5 | 0.238 | 0.363 | 0.588 | 15.65 | +0.13 | 71.9 | 62.7 |

### Success–locality trade-off (three operating points)

- **Conservative (Redact, k=5):** 31.7% success, near-zero locality cost (∆PPL +0.02, max +0.53). Use when any risk of collateral damage is unacceptable.
- **Practical (Next-best, k=5):** 81.5% success, **zero catastrophic edits**, max ∆PPL +52.3. **Recommended operating point** — trading 6pp vs. EOS eliminates tail risk.
- **Aggressive (EOS, k=10):** 87.9% success but heavy locality tail. Median ∆PPL +0.88, but **47.8%** of edits perturb ∆PPL > 1, **11.9%** > 10, **1.8%** catastrophic (>100); worst single edit reaches ∆PPL = 1.5 × 10¹⁰. Aggressive modes should be paired with edit validation.

The median–tail distinction matters: means are dominated by outliers, so median ∆PPL ≤ +0.88 across all configurations, but the tail is the real risk.

HellaSwag holds at 70.8–73.4% (baseline 73.5%); Wikidata QA stays within 2.5pp of baseline for conservative/practical modes but drops 3–5pp under aggressive EOS. **The aggressive-mode penalty concentrates in factual recall rather than commonsense reasoning.**

### Cross-model generalization (k=5)

| Model | Params | Skip% | EOS | Next-best | Suppress | Redact | ∆PPLmed |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| SmolLM-360M | 360M | 48.3% | 34.9 | 37.7 | 30.6 | 15.3 | +0.04 |
| OLMo-1B | 1.2B | 43.4% | 52.4 | 55.4 | 36.2 | 15.7 | +0.04 |
| Llama2-7B | 6.7B | 9.0% | 69.6 | 68.3 | 49.0 | 40.7 | +0.15 |
| **OLMo-7B** | 6.9B | 10.3% | **85.7** | **81.5** | 60.5 | 31.7 | +0.41 |

Three patterns:
- **Mode ordering is consistent** across all four models: EOS and Next-best strongest; random neurons <2% success (≥80% skip).
- **Absolute success scales with size, not family:** 7B models (Llama2 69.6%, OLMo 85.7%) cluster regardless of architecture. Smaller models show rising skip rates (43–48%) — memorization is less concentrated on a small neuron set.
- **Locality is architecture-dependent:** the catastrophic-outlier tail on OLMo-7B aggressive modes does **not** appear on OLMo-1B or Llama2-7B (median ∆PPL ≤ +0.15 across modes at k=5). On Llama2-7B, Next-best is near-Pareto-optimal (+0.01 ∆PPL, 68.3% success). SmolLM-360M Suppress is the lone exception (∆PPL +8.95, systemic). The catastrophic tail is **OLMo-7B-specific**; per-model β/k sweeps would likely help at smaller scales.

### Mode complementarity (6,124 sequences on OLMo-7B)

- 96.5% editable by ≥1 mode; only 22.8% by all 8 configurations.
- EOS succeeds on 90.3% (364 unique); Next-best on 85.0% (161 unique); Suppress on 65.4% (33 unique); Redact on 36.1% (2 unique).
- **Next-best's uniques are marginally-encoded sequences** (median 2 neurons): a small nudge to the runner-up token suffices, but stronger modes overshoot.
- Larger neuron budgets pull in additional cases: Next-best k=10 solves 218 sequences that k=5 misses (vs. 30 in reverse), 13 of which would otherwise be universal failures.

### Mechanistic boundary (~14% of OLMo-7B sequences)

- 707 (10.3%) fail at **localization** (no neuron ranks the target in top/bottom 50 of vocabulary at k=5; drops to 5.7% at k=10).
- 214 (3.5%) are **universal failures** (localization finds neurons but all modes fail; median 2 neurons). These tend to have strong sequential dependencies (e.g., religious texts) where memorization is distributed redundantly beyond the top-k budget.
- Target neurons **activate before** the prediction transition in 92.7% of cases (Appendix J) — they prepare the memorized output rather than respond to it.
- 0.8% random-neuron baseline confirms memorization is attributable to a small set of MLP neurons; the residual ~14% reflects cases where this concentration exceeds the budget.

### Attention as complementary fallback

- Attention-attribution analysis (Table 10): failures have **lower** median attention attribution than successes — opposite of an "attention-driven failures" hypothesis. Copy-head (11.5% vs. 7.8%) and induction-head prevalence show no strong discriminative signal.
- **Yet ablating top contributing attention heads recovers 60–64% of MLP failures** (Table 11): 64.3% on EOS k=10 marginal; 68.7% on the "overlap" subset (continuation shares a non-stopword token with the prefix), 60.9% on the "no overlap" subset. The 7–8pp enrichment on overlap is consistent with induction/copy-head behavior.
- This positions attention as a **complementary fallback**, not a primary mechanism, motivating a hybrid MLP-plus-attention pipeline.

## Critical Assessment

### Novelty — high
- The mechanistic distinction (output vector vs. activation) is the right granularity: it explains *why* zero ablation underperforms and gives a clean closed-form intervention. The distractor-in-vocabulary-space framing is original in the memorization context, distinct from ROME's fact-editing use of rank-one updates.
- The 2.7× ablation gap is the right control and the paper presents it clearly.

### Rigor — medium-high
- Strong points: (a) **same-neuron** zero-ablation baseline isolates the edit's effect; (b) four modes spanning aggressive→minimal with ensemble coverage number; (c) median-vs-tail distinction is honest; (d) cross-model and cross-family evaluation; (e) explicit per-mode locality (PPL/HellaSwag/Wikidata QA).
- Weak points: (a) the **bulk-edit stress test** (Appendix E) is mentioned but the main text doesn't lean on it — they note aggressive EOS collapses within 10 cumulative edits and Redact (β=200) cascades between 50–100, which is a serious deployment caveat that gets less airtime than the headline 87.9%; (b) paraphrase-transfer is explicitly listed as untested ("we don't evaluate transfer to paraphrases, an open question for all targeted mitigation methods").

### Reproducibility — medium-high
- OLMo-7B's open weights + Dolma make the main benchmark fully reproducible. Llama2-7B uses SlimPajama as a proxy, which the authors correctly caveat likely **underestimates** true memorization. The two non-OLMo open-corpus models (OLMo-1B, SmolLM-360M) are smaller and have lower absolute success, but the method is the same.
- Code release status is not stated in the extracted text — a meaningful gap for a method paper.

### Significance — high
- Memorization is a live safety/copyright concern; a weight-edit method with no inference cost, an interpretable success/failure taxonomy, and a principled fallback (attention) is useful for both research and compliance work.
- The success–locality trade-off framing and the recommended Next-best k=5 operating point are practically deployable.

### Red flags / honest concerns
- **Single-token edit target:** the method targets the first continuation token; 74.9% of sequences tip on a single token (per the limitations section), but the remaining 25.1% are only partially addressed. The paper acknowledges this and suggests a multi-token extension, but the current evaluation is single-token.
- **Paraphrase-transfer is untested:** the most realistic attack on memorization is reformulating the prefix to elicit the same content. The paper explicitly defers this. This is a real gap.
- **Bulk editing is cumulative and unstable:** aggressive EOS collapses within 10 cumulative edits; Redact (β=200) cascades between 50–100. The single-edit evaluation hides this. A "first edit is great, tenth edit is broken" method has limited operational value.
- **Recoverability is acknowledged but not measured:** editing redirects rather than removes; adversarial methods can still recover suppressed content (citing Hu et al. 2025). A quantitative recovery-attack evaluation would strengthen the claim.
- **Pre-mining requirement:** the pipeline needs a corpus containing the memorized sequences. For closed models, proxy corpora are imperfect (and likely undercount). The method is a **remedy for known memorizations**, not a runtime defense.
- **The 14% mechanistic boundary is fuzzy:** attention recovers 60–64%, so the MLP/attention split is more of a spectrum than a dichotomy. The framing as a "mechanistic boundary" risks overstating the partition.
- **SmolLM-360M Suppress anomaly:** ∆PPL +8.95 is a systemic (not tail) cost at small scale. The paper attributes this to the lack of per-model β tuning, but the untested configurations and per-model parameter sensitivity are a real practical concern.

## Strengths

- **Clean mechanistic reframing** — separates engagement (activation) from writing (output vector); superposition explains why the latter is the right intervention.
- **Strong ablation discipline** — the 2.7× gap over zero ablation on the same neurons is the right control and cleanly attributes the gain to the edit design.
- **Practical taxonomy of edit modes** with an ensemble coverage number (96.5%) and a recommended single-mode configuration (Next-best k=5) — useful for deployers.
- **Cross-model transfer** across 4 models / 2 families / 360M–7B with success scaling with size, not family, is a strong generality claim.
- **Honest limitations section** explicitly enumerates the bulk-edit fragility, paraphrase untested, proxy-corpus caveats, recoverability concerns, and architectural scope (decoder-only transformers with sequential blocks; PaLM/MoE/long-context untested).
- **Median-vs-tail distinction** in locality reporting is methodologically correct; means would be dominated by outliers and tell the wrong story.

## Limitations

- Evaluation is heavily centered on OLMo-7B (where memorization can be systematically mined via the open corpus); the other three models likely have weaker ground-truth memorization supervision.
- Single-token edit target: only 74.9% of sequences tip on a single token; multi-token extension is left to future work.
- The 14% MLP-unreachable set is partially recoverable by attention ablation (60–64%), so the "MLP vs. attention" boundary is fuzzy rather than crisp.
- Bulk-edit stability: aggressive EOS collapses within 10 cumulative edits; mild Redact (β=200) cascades between 50–100. The single-edit evaluation hides this.
- Paraphrase-transfer is explicitly untested — the realistic attack on memorization.
- Closed-corpus mining relies on proxies (SlimPajama for Llama2-7B) that likely underestimate true memorization; absolute success rates on those models are lower bounds.
- "Catastrophic locality failure" is defined internally (∆PPL > 100 on WikiText); an external capability benchmark suite would harden the claim.
- Adversarial robustness is acknowledged but not measured; the paper notes the method is a "remedy for known memorizations" rather than a runtime defense.
- Per-model β/k sensitivity at smaller scales (SmolLM Suppress anomaly) suggests hyperparameters need per-model sweeps; the paper fixes β=100 from an OLMo-7B sweep.
- Decoder-only transformer assumption; PaLM-style parallel paths, MoE, and long-context variants are not tested.

## Relevance

与 LLM 记忆化缓解和机制可解释性方向高度相关。论文将"消融激活"与"编辑输出向量"在机理上严格区分（叠加→多重语义方向），并把它直接转成一个闭式、可部署、无推理成本的权重编辑方法；其成功-局部性谱系、跨模型迁移结果和"机制边界"分析对研究 LLM 安全、可控生成和版权合规工作有直接借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does OVE compare head-to-head with established weight editors (ROME, MEMIT, BiPO) on the same memorization benchmark? Is the 2.7× gap over zero ablation also a gap over prior methods?
- What is the **multi-token edit extension** look like — does iteratively editing the predicted continuation chain achieve strong suppression on the 25.1% of sequences that don't tip on the first token?
- For the ~14% MLP-unreachable sequences, can a **joint MLP+attention edit** (rather than sequential fallback) close the residual gap without inflating the locality cost?
- Does the choice of distractor token matter semantically? E.g., does Next-best with a semantically distant runner-up suppress more, or does it just look superficially fluent?
- How robust is OVE to (a) continued pretraining, (b) fine-tuning, (c) adversarial paraphrased prompts that elicit the same memorized content?
- What is the cumulative-edit budget before quality degrades for each mode? The Appendix E stress test hints at 10 for aggressive EOS, 50–100 for Redact (β=200) — a fuller Pareto frontier of (success, edit count) would be operationally valuable.
- Does the method generalize to other "undesired output" targets (toxic continuations, hallucinations, jailbreak continuations) where the mechanism is also a localized MLP feature?
- For SmolLM-360M's systemic Suppress-mode locality cost (∆PPL +8.95), is the cause scale-related (fewer redundant neurons) or architecture-related (different nonlinearity/normalization) — and can a β-sweep per model fix it?
- How does the OVE success rate depend on corpus deduplication level and on the *kind* of duplication (verbatim vs. near-duplicate) — the OLMo/SmolLM/Llama2 hit-rate divergence (0.05% vs. 0.2% vs. 1.4%) suggests this matters.
- Does OVE edit one direction or several per neuron? If a neuron's $v_i$ encodes K features and the next-most-likely memorized sequence also lives on it, does editing for one break the other — i.e., is there an "interference" among edits that limits bulk deployment?
