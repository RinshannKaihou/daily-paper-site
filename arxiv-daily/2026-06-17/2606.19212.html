Key Finding

The top generalised eigenvalue of a two-embedder Jacobian matrix pencil measures each input's local attackability — and predicts real paraphrase flips.

Generalised Eigenvalue Geometry of Semantic Adversarial Attacks

Martin Anthony · Kaveh Salehzadeh Nobari

adversarial-robustness paraphrase-attacks spectral-theory VC-theory financial-sentiment
Key Metric
0.91
AUC of −Zw predicting a proxy-valid flip (Spearman ρ ≈ 0.58, n = 184). The local geometric diagnostic is genuinely predictive of realised attacks.
Why This Matters

A single chart-invariant scalar links local spectral geometry to uniform learning-theoretic bounds — bridging pointwise certificates and population robustness with one matrix pencil.

Critical Edge

The 1.5–2 order-of-magnitude slack makes the worst-direction certificate essentially vacuous for FinBERT — and the whole empirical claim rests on a single model pair and one small dataset (n = 190).

Methodology

  • Two-embedder local model: proxy embedder e_P (Sentence-BERT) defines the semantic budget η; target embedder e_M (FinBERT) defines the classifier response.
  • Matrix pencil: Jacobians give A = JMᵀJM, B = JPᵀJP. Worst-case target displacement under proxy budget η² is η²·λ*(x), the top generalised eigenvalue.
  • Chart invariance: λ*(x) and S(x) = JMB⁻¹JMᵀ are intrinsic — independent of the local coordinate chart (Prop. 2.4).
  • Closed-form flip: for affine readouts, flip iff η√Σw(x) > γw(x); with pointwise domination Σw ≤ λ* (Thm 3.3).
  • Population stack: attackability-adjusted margin Zw = γw/√Σw → DKW concentration, VC bound, fat-shattering & trace-sensitive Rademacher bounds.
  • Discrete bridge: covering condition links finite paraphrase search to the continuous optimum, with an explicit success/failure asymmetry.

Strengths & Limitations

▲ Strengths

  • One clean, chart-invariant scalar — λ*(x) — feeds the affine flip condition, VC, fat-shattering and Rademacher bounds.
  • Coherent theory stack: linearisation → pencil → certificate → uniform bounds → discrete-search bridge → verification.
  • Discrete↔continuous covering condition with a measurable success/failure asymmetry.
  • Trace-sensitive Rademacher bound removes ambient-dimension dependence.
  • Two-chart design separates model-agnostic geometry from generator bias.

▼ Limitations

  • Fundamentally first-order; the O(η²/β) error is not even tight where the linear model breaks (saturation).
  • Slack of 1.5–2 orders of magnitude renders the worst-direction certificate vacuous on FinBERT.
  • ν-regularisation of B breaks reparameterisation invariance.
  • Single model pair + single dataset (n=190/185); no ablations across proxies, targets, or generators.
  • Restricted to affine readouts; non-linear heads only sketched.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How sensitive is λ*(x) to the proxy embedder — can it extend to a set of proxies (worst-case over an attacker class)?
  • Can the affine flip condition extend to one-hidden-layer or structured non-linear heads while preserving closed-form character?
  • Does the covering condition yield a quantitative bound on the finite-search vs continuous gap, enabling adaptive candidate generation?
  • Can Zw(x) route inputs to different defence budgets, or correlate with post-hoc defences (denoising, ensembling)?
  • Beyond finance: does the index predict attackability for legal, medical, or other high-stakes classifiers under domain shift?
  • Can λ*(x) guide paraphrase generation (project onto the top eigenvector) rather than only diagnose post-hoc?
  • The two-chart divergence (slack 2.05 vs 1.47; ceiling 0.76 vs 0.97) deserves a controlled study — which is ground truth?
  • What drives the unexplained variance behind Spearman ρ ≈ 0.58 — generator bias, linearisation error, or unseen long-range features?
