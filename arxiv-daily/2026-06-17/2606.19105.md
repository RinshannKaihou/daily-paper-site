> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一种基于光滑性的 PAC-Bayes 去随机化新策略——将 Gibbs 预测器到确定性后验均值预测器的转换代价精确刻画为 "Jensen gap 类" 的泛化差距，用 Rademacher 复杂度（结合 Talagrand 比较不等式）控制该类，得到含分数映射 Jacobian/Hessian 平坦度量的高概率界（无界/有界损失、线性预测器与光滑神经网络均适用），其中对线性情形在高斯先验下得到 √d 量级的 PAC-Bayes 复杂度项（改进了 Haddouche et al. 2020 的维度依赖）；最后在 BatchNorm 折叠后的有效权重空间中实现一个无标签（label-free）的 JH 正则化器，并在 CIFAR-10 上展示其对大 batch 与标签噪声场景的提升。

> **TL;DR:** This paper develops a **smoothness-based derandomization of PAC-Bayes** generalization bounds. The key insight is that the cost of moving from the stochastic Gibbs predictor to the deterministic posterior-mean predictor is exactly the **generalization gap of the Jensen gap class**. A **Talagrand comparison inequality** for sub-Gaussian processes is used to upper bound the Rademacher complexity of this class, yielding deterministic high-probability bounds whose flatness factors are the **Jacobian and Hessian of the score map**. The theory is developed for both **bounded and unbounded smooth losses** (Theorem 3.7 for unbounded; Theorems 5.1–5.3 for bounded, including an inverse-KL form), and specialized to **linear predictors** (Theorem 4.1) and **smooth neural networks**. For linear predictors with Gaussian priors, **Lemma 3.8** improves the dimension dependence of the PAC-Bayes complexity term to √d, tightening Haddouche et al. (2020). Empirically, the resulting **JH regularizer** — computed on the **effective BatchNorm weights** (BN folded into adjacent affine weights) — is a **label-free** penalty that improves an 8-layer CNN on CIFAR-10 under large batches and 20% label noise, but provides only marginal gains on clean data.

## 核心贡献

- 提出**光滑性驱动的 PAC-Bayes 去随机化框架**：将随机 Gibbs 预测器到确定性后验均值预测器的转换代价精确归结为 "Jensen gap 类" 的泛化差距（Theorem 3.3 / Corollary 3.5）。
- 通过 **Talagrand 比较不等式**控制 Jensen gap 类的 Rademacher 复杂度（Corollary 7.6 / Proposition 7.9 / 7.10），得到**含分数映射 Jacobian 与 Hessian 平坦度量**的确定性高概率界。
- 框架同时处理**无界**（Theorem 3.7）与**有界**光滑损失（Theorems 5.1–5.3，含逆 KL 形式），并特例化到**线性预测器**（Theorem 4.1）与**光滑神经网络**。
- Lemma 3.8 给出线性预测器在高斯先验下 PAC-Bayes 复杂度项的 **√d 维度依赖**，改进了 Haddouche et al. (2020) 的维度依赖。
- 基于理论推导出一个**可计算的 JH 正则化器**，在 BatchNorm 网络中将 BN 折叠为有效仿射权重（weff,B′）后计算；该正则化器是**无标签的**（label-free），天然适合半监督/无监督学习。
- CIFAR-10（8 层 CNN，6 卷积 + 2 全连接）上的实验：批大小 B ∈ {256, 512, 1024, 2048, 4096}、3 随机种子、20% 标签噪声对比；有效权重和层归一化权重版本能稳定提升基线，原参数与含 BN 仿射参数版本不提升。

## Key Contributions

- **Jensen-gap-class derandomization cost (Theorem 3.3, Corollary 3.5).** The derandomization penalty for moving from a Gibbs predictor to the posterior-mean predictor is shown to be *exactly* the generalization gap of the Jensen gap class.
- **Rademacher-complexity control via Talagrand's comparison inequality (Section 7).** A sub-Gaussian process / contraction argument (Corollary 7.6, Propositions 7.9, 7.10) yields uniform high-probability bounds depending on **Jacobian and Hessian of the score map**.
- **General bounds for both unbounded and bounded smooth losses.** Theorem 3.7 (unbounded), Theorems 5.1 and 5.3 (bounded, including an inverse-KL form).
- **Linear specialization with tighter dimension dependence (Theorem 4.1, Lemma 3.8).** Under a Gaussian prior, the PAC-Bayes complexity term is bounded with a √d dependence, improving the dimension scaling of Haddouche et al. (2020).
- **Smooth neural network extension (Section 6).** Uniform upper bounds for the Jensen gap for smooth NNs, with flatness operators appearing naturally in operator norms.
- **Practical JH regularizer (Section 9).** For BatchNorm networks, the regularizer is evaluated in an **effective parametrization** obtained by folding each BN layer into the adjacent affine weights (st,B′ := γt/√(vt,B′ + εt) per channel). The resulting penalty is **label-free** (Remark 9.3) and is estimated via Jacobian-vector / Hessian-vector products, without forming the full matrices.
- **Empirical study on CIFAR-10.** 8-layer CNN, batch sizes 256–4096, clean and 20% label noise. JH(weff,B′; SB′) yields larger gains under label noise and large batches, and partially mitigates the degradation in clean test accuracy as batch size grows (Figure 3, Figure 4).

## Methodology

### Theoretical pipeline

1. **Start from an unbounded-loss PAC-Bayes result** (Alquier et al., 2016, Theorem 3.1) for the Gibbs risk: an inequality of the form
   Ew∼Q LD(hw) ≤ Ew∼Q LS(hw) + (1/λ) DKL(Q∥P) + (1/λ) ln(1/δ) + ΨL,P,D(λ,n).
2. **Pointwise identity** (Theorem 3.3): for any Q with w := Ew∼Q[w], the deterministic risk satisfies
   LD(hw) = Ew∼Q LD(hw) + [LD(hw) − Ew∼Q LD(hw)],
   and adding/subtracting the empirical Gibbs risk produces the penalty
   ΩL,Q,D,S(n) := [LD(hw) − Ew∼Q LD(hw)] − [LS(hw) − Ew∼Q LS(hw)],
   which is the **generalization gap of the Jensen gap class**.
3. **Smoothness assumption** (Proposition 3.2 / Theorem 3.7): for every y, z ↦ L(z, y) is ℓ-Lipschitz and β-smooth; hypotheses are bounded: ∥hw(x)∥ ≤ aw on X. Hoeffding's lemma then bounds the PAC-Bayes complexity term ΨL,P,D(λ,n) ≤ ln Ew∼P exp(λ²ℓ²aw² / (2n)).
4. **Jensen gap control (Section 7)**. The Jensen gap class JenQ[L(hw(x), y)] is bounded in expectation via **Talagrand's comparison inequality for sub-Gaussian processes** + contraction, producing bounds of the form
   Rσ √(β² JS,σ + ℓ² HS,σ) + 8σ²B
   where JS,σ and HS,σ are **empirical flatness quantities** depending on the parameter Jacobian and Hessian of the score map.
5. **Linear specialization (Theorem 4.1, Lemma 3.8).** For hw(x) = Wx with W ∈ R^{m_T × d} and a Gaussian prior WT ∼ N(0, σ² I_{m_T}), setting λ = √n / (M ℓσ √(m_T) + 1) gives a PAC-Bayes complexity term of order M ℓσ √(m_T) / √n — a **√d** scaling that improves prior work.
6. **Smooth NN extension (Section 6).** For NNs, the same machinery yields upper bounds with operator-norm Jacobians/Hessians of the score map, applicable to bounded or unbounded smooth losses.

### Practical regularizer

- For each minibatch SB of size B, draw a smaller SB′ (default B′ = 50) to estimate the regularizer.
- Compute
  J(w; SB′) := (1/B′) ∑ᵢ ∥Jᵢ(w)∥²_F, H(w; SB′) := (1/B′) ∑ᵢ ∑ₖ ∥Hᵢ,ₖ(w)∥²_F,
  and define JH(w; SB′) := √(β² J² + ℓ² H).
- For BatchNorm, define the **effective weight**
  W̄_{t,eff,B′} := Diag(s_{t,B′}) W_t, with s_{t,B′} := γ_t / √(v_{t,B′} + ε_t) channelwise,
  and absorb the bias/affine BN params. The regularizer is evaluated in the effective parametrization weff,B′ while gradients are backpropagated through the map w ↦ weff,B′(w) (the BN statistics are **not** treated as constants).
- The squared Frobenius norms are estimated by **randomized trace estimation** Ev ∥Aᵀv∥²²: only Jacobian–vector and Hessian–vector products are needed (M = 4 random vectors by default).
- Architectural and training details (Section 9): 8-layer CNN (6 conv + 2 FC), centered Softplus activations, cross-entropy loss, Adam + linear warm-up (3 epochs) + cosine annealing, 40 epochs (or 40·√(B/256) in the variable-epoch protocol). Hyperparameters tuned on a 5,000-example validation split; 3 seeds.

## Results & Highlights

### Theory

- **Theorem 3.7 (unbounded smooth losses).** Deterministic high-probability bound of the form
  LD(hw) ≤ LS(hw) + 2c Rσ/√n · √(β² JS,σ + ℓ² HS,σ) + 8σ²B + (1/λ) DKL(Q∥P) + (1/λ) ln(1/δ) + (1/λ) ln Ew∼P exp(λ²ℓ²aw²/(2n)).
  The flatness terms are explicit functionals of the parameter Jacobians/Hessians of the score map.
- **Theorem 4.1 / Lemma 3.8 (linear specialization).** With Gaussian prior, the PAC-Bayes complexity term scales as √d / √n — **strictly better than prior bounds** (Haddouche et al., 2020).
- **Theorem 5.1 / 5.3 (bounded smooth losses).** Independent treatment via a different PAC-Bayes theorem (Haddouche et al., 2025), including an inverse-KL form.
- **Section 8 (numerical bound comparison).** For the multi-class sigmoid loss with C = 3 and C = 10, the new bound shows **relative improvement over the standard Rademacher complexity bound** (Figure 2), confirming tightness on toy problems.

### Experiments (CIFAR-10, 8-layer CNN, three seeds)

- **Parametrization ablation (Figure 5, batch sizes 256–4096).** Of four variants — JH on effective weights weff,B′, on original weights w, on w + BN affine params, and on layerwise-normalized weights w/∥w∥ — **only the effective-weight and layerwise-normalized versions** consistently and meaningfully beat the unregularized baseline. The original-weights and original+BN-versions **fail to improve**, highlighting that the choice of coordinate system matters.
- **Large-batch behavior (Figure 3).** On clean CIFAR-10, the baseline's test accuracy drops noticeably as batch size increases. JH(weff,B′; SB′) **flattens this degradation**: at B = 4096, accuracy is ~78.8% (baseline) vs ~81.7% (JH) under the variable-epoch protocol (Table 1).
- **Label-noise robustness (Figure 4).** Under 20% uniform label noise, the **regularizer's relative gain is larger** than on clean data — consistent with the interpretation that flatness limits memorization of corrupted labels.
- **B′ and M sensitivity (Table 1, B = 4096, variable-epoch).**
  - Baseline: 78.84 ± 0.21%
  - JH(B′=50, M=1): 81.19 ± 0.43%
  - JH(B′=50, M=4): 81.46 ± 0.46%
  - JH(B′=50, M=16): 81.40 ± 0.18%
  - JH(B′=256, M=4): 81.68 ± 0.37%
  - JH(B′=1024, M=4): 81.73 ± 0.11%
  Increasing B′ gives a small improvement; increasing M beyond 4 saturates.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — strong.** The "Jensen gap class" framing of the derandomization cost is original and conceptually clean. Unlike margin-based derandomization (Neyshabur et al., 2018; Banerjee et al., 2020), the approach is **statistically consistent** (no residual term that does not vanish as n → ∞) and applies to **arbitrary smooth losses**, not just classification margins. The √d Lemma 3.8 for linear predictors with Gaussian priors is a concrete improvement over Haddouche et al. (2020).
- **Rigor — strong in spirit, depends on assumptions.** The bounds are derived under per-hypothesis boundedness (∥hw(x)∥ ≤ aw), ℓ-Lipschitz + β-smooth loss in the network output, and weight norm constraints (Θ = {∥w∥₂ ≤ R}). These are **standard but not free** assumptions; in particular, the boundedness-on-X condition requires a bounded input domain or a model-output bound. The proof uses **Talagrand's comparison inequality** — a powerful tool, but applied to the *Jensen gap* as a sub-Gaussian process, which is the most delicate step and deserves careful checking in the appendix.
- **Reproducibility — partial.** The paper releases enough detail (architecture, hyperparameters, three seeds, hyperparameter search grids) to be reproducible, but does not (yet) appear to provide code (the abstract does not advertise a repo). The 8-layer CNN is small; CIFAR-10 only. No ImageNet, no transformer/LLM evidence.
- **Significance — meaningful for the PAC-Bayes + flatness literature.** Bridges the gap between stochastic PAC-Bayes and deterministic predictors via a clean complexity term, and produces a usable flatness regularizer that is genuinely **label-free** — a property absent from Hessian-of-loss regularizers and from SAM. The BatchNorm-folded effective weight coordinate system is novel for flatness regularization (Remark 9.1).
- **Red flags / honest concerns.**
  1. The clean-data accuracy gains are **modest** (≈ 1–3 percentage points at large batch sizes) and are absent at small batch sizes — the regularizer is not a free lunch.
  2. **Two of the four parametrization variants fail to improve the baseline** (Figure 5). The paper frames this as a "highlighting the importance of choosing a parametrization" message, but it is also a warning: the method requires a BN-folding step that is model-architecture-specific. The recipe does not transfer verbatim to non-BN architectures (GroupNorm, LayerNorm, or attention-only nets).
  3. The **additive 8σ²B term** in Theorem 3.7 (the *bias* of the Jensen gap from the small-|JenQ L| ≤ σ²B assumption) is not characterized empirically — readers do not know how tight it is in practice.
  4. **Statistical-consistency claim.** The introduction contrasts the approach with Banerjee et al. (2020), whose derandomization leaves a residual c₀ exp(−c₂γ²) that does not vanish. The paper claims its extra terms "vanish as n → ∞." This is true for the Rademacher-complexity terms (R/√n) and KL/λ terms, but the **8σ²B residual is independent of n** unless B = B(σ) → 0 with σ. The asymptotic story is therefore more nuanced than the prose suggests.
  5. **Practicality of the JH penalty.** Computing Hessian-vector products per minibatch and backpropagating through BN statistics is **non-trivial engineering**; the paper hand-waves this with "PyTorch autograd" but does not report wall-clock or memory cost.
  6. **No theoretical guarantee that the JH regularizer is actually an upper bound on the Rademacher term it claims to approximate.** The paper says "Rather than attempting to compute a fully faithful version of the quantity appearing in the bound, we evaluate a local and computationally tractable proxy." This is honest but also means the empirical JH penalty is only *motivated* by, not equivalent to, the theoretical bound.

## Strengths

- **Conceptually clean decomposition** of derandomization cost as the generalization gap of the Jensen gap class.
- **Statistically consistent** extra penalty (vanishes as n → ∞ under natural scaling) — a meaningful contrast with margin-based derandomization.
- **General** — covers both bounded and unbounded smooth losses; specializes to linear predictors and smooth NNs; produces a √d improvement for linear predictors with Gaussian priors.
- **Label-free regularizer** (Remark 9.3): the JH penalty depends only on the score map, not labels, opening the door to semi-supervised / unsupervised applications.
- **Empirical parametrization ablation** (Figure 5) is unusually thorough for a theory paper and supports the effective-weight claim.
- **Label-noise experiment** is well chosen — it is the regime where flatness should matter most, and indeed the gains are largest.

## Limitations

- **Empirical scope is narrow:** CIFAR-10, a single (8-layer CNN) architecture, no ImageNet or modern benchmarks; comparisons are against an unregularized baseline, **not against SAM, ASAM, SWA, or Hessian-trace regularization** — a clear omission given the paper's positioning.
- **Architecture-specific recipe.** The JH regularizer requires the BatchNorm-folding step. Translation to GroupNorm, LayerNorm, or no-normalization nets is not addressed.
- **Modest absolute gains on clean data**; benefits concentrated in large-batch and label-noise regimes.
- **Computational cost** of Hessian-vector products inside the autograd graph, and of propagating gradients through BN statistics, is not reported.
- **The theoretical bound is not directly optimized.** The JH regularizer is an empirically-motivated proxy; nothing in the paper guarantees that minimizing it provably tightens the bound.
- **Theorem 3.7's 8σ²B residual** is not vanishing in n under fixed σ, and is not characterized empirically.

## Relevance

与你的研究兴趣直接相关：本文是 **PAC-Bayes 泛化理论与平坦度正则化** 的交叉工作。它一方面给出光滑损失下 Gibbs → 确定性预测器去随机化代价的精确刻画（通过 Jensen gap 类与 Rademacher 复杂度），另一方面把理论中出现的 Jacobian/Hessian 平坦度量直接转化为一种 label-free 的实用正则化器（BatchNorm 折叠后的有效权重空间），这与"通过平坦度提升神经网络泛化能力"这条主线（SAM、SWAG、flat-minima 等）以及"把 PAC-Bayes 界做成可优化目标"这条线均高度契合。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the JH regularizer be **matched head-to-head with SAM / ASAM / SWA / Hessian-trace regularization** on the same CIFAR-10 setup, with matched compute budgets, to clarify whether the theory-motivated penalty actually buys something over flatness baselines?
- Does the BN-folding recipe **generalize to GroupNorm / LayerNorm / RMSNorm** (or to normalization-free nets via an effective affine reparametrization), and which surrogate works best when no BN is present?
- Can the **Hessian-vector-product cost** be reduced (Kronecker-factored approximation, Fisher-based surrogate, last-layer-only) without breaking the bound interpretation, and what is the wall-clock / memory cost on realistic batch sizes?
- Is the JH regularizer **complementary** to data augmentation (mixup, CutMix) and to noise-injection / dropout? What is the interaction with the 20% label-noise result?
- Can the **label-free property** be exploited in a **semi-supervised** setting, e.g. JH regularizer on unlabeled data combined with standard CE on a small labeled subset?
- For the linear case, is the √d scaling (Lemma 3.8) **matched by a matching lower bound**, or is the dependence still loose?
- The paper notes the **8σ²B residual** is intrinsic — is there a regime (e.g., σ → 0 with B = o(σ²)) where this term vanishes cleanly in n, and is the corresponding σ-schedule useful in practice?
- Can **local Rademacher complexity** (mentioned in the conclusion) yield strictly tighter bounds than the present ones, and would such bounds suggest a different regularizer?
