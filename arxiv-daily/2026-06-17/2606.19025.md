> **一句话总结：** FoMoE 通过把 MoE 专家层分片到不同数据中心（打破 DiLoCo/Photon 的"全量副本"假设）实现跨 WAN 训练，在 54M proxy 上将通信量相对高效 baseline 降低最多 1.42×、相对 DDP 最高 45.44×，并配合"skip-token"机制在 8×B200 上测得最高 1.4× 端到端吞吐加速；100B 收益通过系统建模外推而得，未经实证。

> **TL;DR:** FoMoE targets geo-distributed LLM pre-training of MoE models by partitioning expert layers across weakly connected datacenters, eliminating the "full-replica" memory and bandwidth overhead of DiLoCo/Photon-style methods. Trained 54M σ-MoE proxy on C4-en shows FoMoE shifts the perplexity-vs-communication Pareto frontier: up to 1.42× communication reduction vs. strong federated baselines, 45.44× vs. DDP, plus 1.4× measured throughput from a "skip-token" trick that omits computation for non-resident ("ghost") experts. Large-scale benefits (XL 13B, XXL 100B) are projected analytically rather than trained.

## 核心贡献

- **打破全量副本范式**：在跨数据中心联邦训练中按"专家重叠因子" Oe ∈ {1,…,M} 将专家层分片到不同 worker，每站点只持有 Nl = (Oe/M)·Ne 个专家，从根本上解决 DiLoCo/Photon 的"每站存整模型+同步全量"问题。
- **FoMoE 完整系统**：定义分片 × 放置 × 优化的设计空间；提供固定 / 随机两种 expert 放置策略，以及 LocalAdamW 外层优化器。
- **通信量线性降低**：在 54M proxy 上相对高效 baseline 最高 **1.42×**、相对 DDP 最高 **45.44×** 通信降低（控制条件下）。
- **Skip-Token 机制**：路由表不再分片，token 仍可被分配到非本地（"ghost"）专家但直接跳过其 FFN 计算；150M 模型 + 8×B200 实测最高 **1.4×** 端到端加速。
- **形式化系统模型**：推导出 FLOPs、bytes-per-token、wall-clock 的解析式，并定义四象限操作区间（Comm-Bound / Memory-Constrained / FoMoE Sweet Spot / Placement Collapse）。
- **100B 规模外推**：通过系统建模在 1–10 Gbps WAN 上预测分片带来的内存与通信收益。

## Key Contributions

- **System: FoMoE.** Cross-site MoE training framework with partial expert replication, partitioning factor Oe and a placement module; designed to break the full-replica paradigm of DiLoCo/Photon.
- **Pareto-frontier communication reduction.** Trained 54M proxy on SmolLM-derived corpus evaluated on C4-en: communication **1.42×** lower than the strongest federated baseline and **45.44×** lower than DDP, with perplexity parity in the controlled regime.
- **Skip-Token mechanism.** A "ghost expert" concept where routing is not partitioned but compute for off-worker experts is skipped, yielding **up to 1.4× empirical throughput** (150M MoE, 8× NVIDIA B200).
- **Stable routing and convergence.** Trained proxy regimes show stable expert utilization (no collapse) and consistent perplexity trajectories with full-replica baselines.
- **Analytical scaling to 100B.** Closed-form FLOPs, memory, and wall-clock models extrapolate the same partial-replication benefits to XL (13B) and XXL (100B) configurations.
- **Peripheral LayerNorm (Peri-LN) and σ-MoE.** Architectural choices to keep gating stable when expert availability varies across sites; uses σ-MoE (Csordás et al., 2023) with sigmoid (not softmax) gating for independence under partial replicas.

## Methodology

### Setting and assumptions
- **Topology:** M homogeneous data-parallel "sites" (datacenter-class nodes), each with internal bandwidth Bintra ≈ 200 Gbps; cross-site WAN Bcross ≈ 1–10 Gbps, 50 ms latency. Regime is **Bintra ≫ Bcross**.
- **Synchronous training** with a central coordinator; K local steps between rounds (DiLoCo/Photon-style low-frequency sync). Asynchronous variants (e.g., Streaming DiLoCo) explicitly left to future work.
- **I.i.d. data partitions** per site (the only regime measured). Stragglers, jitter, asymmetric links, heterogeneous accelerators are out of scope and noted as limitations.
- **σ-MoE** layers replace dense FFN. Total parameters scale as θ = θ_dense ∪ θ_sparse where θ_dense (attention, norms, embeddings, shared experts) is fully replicated and θ_sparse (expert FFNs, optionally routers) is partitioned.

### Overlap factor Oe ∈ {1,…,M}
- Each expert is hosted on exactly Oe workers ⇒ per-worker local expert count Nl = (Oe/M)·Ne.
- Lower Oe → lower cross-site sync volume (factor Oe/M), lower per-site memory, smaller router projection, but lower per-token effective expert capacity (when Nl < k, fewer than top-k experts activate per token).
- Boundary cases: Oe = 1 ⇒ disjoint experts, zero aggregation ring cost; Oe = M ⇒ full replica (= DiLoCo/Photon).

### Placement strategies
- **Fixed:** Each worker keeps a static expert set for the entire run. Specializes each site; momentum state is preserved.
- **Random:** Every T sync rounds, the server reshuffles assignments with constraint that every expert still appears on exactly Oe workers. Decouples experts from data shards (potential regularizer) but breaks local optimizer momentum for migrated experts — paper re-initializes with a warmup.

### Local routing
- Tokens are routed **strictly locally**: only experts resident on the current site are dispatched to; no inter-site activation exchange (key contrast to expert parallelism).
- Two routing variants: (a) partition the router with the experts (only locally available experts score); (b) keep the router full and let it assign tokens to **"ghost experts"** whose FFN is then skipped (skip-token mode).

### Skip-token
- When the router scores a non-resident expert, FoMoE does not fetch the weights — it skips the FFN computation for that token entirely, scaling per-token FLOPs by σ_st = min(1, Nl/k · Ne/Ne) = min(1, Nl/k) in expectation. Cost savings are linear in the fraction of skipped tokens.
- Empirically (Fig. 11): near-linear throughput gain as a function of "ghost expert" count on a 150M MoE, validated on 8× B200.

### Outer optimization
- **LocalAdamW (state-synchronized)** is shown to dominate DiLoCo (stateless) in this setting: state synchronization is essential for stability when K is large or M scales up. Outer server uses Adam-style update on aggregated ∆.
- For θ_dense, all workers track states; for θ_sparse, each worker maintains state only for its assigned experts (memory win).
- Hyperparameters transferred via µP/CompleteP (Yang & Hu 2021; Dey et al. 2025) for width/depth; expert-count scaling follows Malasnicki et al. 2025; regularization and system knobs (Oe, K, T) tuned per scale.

### Cost model
- Per-token FLOPs: F_token ≈ F_dense + Σℓ [σ_st·min(k,Nl,ℓ)/k · F_FFN,ℓ + Nl,ℓ/Ne,ℓ · F_gate,ℓ].
- Per-site communication: B_per-site = B_dense(M) + (R(Oe) + P(Oe,M,γ))·(Oe/M)·Σℓ Ne,ℓ·s_expert,ℓ, with R(Oe) = 2(Oe−1)/Oe (ring factor) and P = γ·(1 − Oe/M) (broadcast penalty under random placement).
- Wall-clock roofline: T_total = (N_tokens/(M·B_l·K))·[K·t_comp(M,Oe) + (1−α)·t_comm(M,Oe,γ)], with α an overlap factor and ρ a compression factor (ρ=1 default).
- Operational regions: (1) Comm-Bound, (2) Memory-Constrained, (3) FoMoE Sweet Spot (low Oe, high K), (4) Placement Collapse (high γ, low Oe).

## Results & Highlights

### Trained quality (54M σ-MoE proxy, C4-en validation)

- **Pareto shift vs. DiLoCo / LocalAdamW (Fig. 5, 6):** FoMoE-LocalAdamW-fixed dominates on the perplexity-vs-communication and perplexity-vs-computation fronts in the proxy regime.
- **Concrete numbers (Table 7, LocalAdamW K=32, M=2, fixed placement):**
  - Oe=1: PPL 111.45 ± 15.52
  - Oe=2: PPL 105.03 ± 14.67
  - Full replica (Oe=M=2): 105.03 (matches Oe=2 since M=2)
- **Ablation on Oe (M=8, K=32, fixed):** PPL goes 106.91 (Oe=8) → 114.69 (Oe=4) → 122.49 (Oe=2) → 135.74 (Oe=1); **sweet spot is Oe ≈ 4** in the studied regime.
- **DiLoCo degrades much faster at K=128:** Table 4 shows DiLoCo PPL blows up to 507 / 420 / 647 at K=128, while LocalAdamW stays around 107 / 112 / 160 — i.e., **stateful outer optimizers are required** for high K (which is exactly the regime FoMoE wants to operate in to amortize the WAN).
- **Skip-token does not destabilize training (Fig. 7):** "Skip token" Pareto points are systematically better (lower PPL at lower FLOPs) than "non-skip token" for the same Oe.

### Throughput
- **Measured speedup:** Up to **1.4×** on 150M MoE / 8× B200 vs. no-skip baseline; scaling is near-linear in number of ghost experts (Fig. 11). Note: no custom kernel, so the 1.4× is an under-estimate of the theoretical upper bound.

### Communication (proxy + modeled)
- Up to **1.42×** reduction vs. the strongest federated baseline.
- Up to **45.44×** reduction vs. DDP (DDP is a strawman for WAN; the meaningful comparison is the 1.42×).
- Modeled **cumulative bytes/worker** (Fig. 9, d_model=8192, 512 layers, 32 workers) shows FoMoE-Oe=4 is several orders of magnitude below DDP and the full-replica curves in the relevant token-processing range.

### Wall-clock
- Modeled (Fig. 10, d_model=2048, 64 layers, 32 H100, 60B tokens): total training time falls as Oe decreases, especially in 1 Gbps and 10 Gbps regimes. At 1 Gbps, the gap between Oe=32 and Oe≈5 is roughly an order of magnitude in hours.

### 100B projection
- **Not trained.** 100B (XXL) row in Table 1: d_model=4096, 512 layers, 128 experts of width 128, ~104B total params. The paper presents this as an analytical extrapolation under the cost model assumptions — not a measured result.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: meaningful but evolutionary.** The core idea — partition experts across sites to reduce cross-site payload — is the natural complement to DiLoCo/Photon's "reduce frequency" lever, and was hinted at in the federated MoE literature (FedMoE, dFLMoE, Learning@home/DMoE). FoMoE is the first paper to make this the primary design axis for *cross-datacenter pre-training* and to derive a full cost model around it. Skip-token is a small but useful trick. Peri-LN is borrowed (Kim et al. 2025).
- **Empirical scope is narrow.** Everything measured is the **54M proxy on C4-en validation perplexity**. No downstream evaluations (MMLU, HumanEval, etc.), no longer-horizon runs, no quality comparison at the 2B / 13B / 100B scales. The 100B claims are model-only. The paper is candid about this in Section 7, but readers should treat the "system shifts the Pareto frontier" claim as a proxy-scale result, not a frontier-model result.
- **Baselines are weak / well-chosen.**
  - 45.44× vs. DDP is a near-trivial win — DDP is bandwidth-bound per step on WAN; the relevant comparison is to DiLoCo/Photon, where 1.42× is the meaningful number.
  - No direct comparison against **WAN-deployed Expert Parallelism (EP)** (e.g., DeepSeek-style cross-node EP with limited WAN) — the paper argues standard EP "assumes high-bandwidth activation exchange and is therefore not the bottleneck targeted by FoMoE," which is a fair framing but also lets the paper off the hook for a more demanding comparison.
  - No comparison against **FSDP/ZeRO-3 + EP** in geo-distributed settings (these are the natural production alternatives).
  - No comparison against **Asynchronous or stale-synchronous** methods (Streaming DiLoCo, DiLoCoX, GASLoC, SparseLoCo), which the paper itself cites but doesn't benchmark.
- **Skip-token is an approximation, not equivalence.** The paper is upfront: "skip-token is not output-equivalent to full-replica training or to WAN expert parallelism with remote activation dispatch, because it deliberately omits expert paths that are unavailable at the local site." This is good scientific honesty, but it also means the 1.4× speedup comes at a real quality cost that the proxy perplexity numbers do not fully capture. Downstream task effects (especially on reasoning, where MoE experts are believed to specialize) are explicitly called out as future work.
- **Random placement underperforms in their setting.** Counter to the authors' prior intuition, the i.i.d. proxy regime shows fixed placement on the Pareto front and random placement as a cost-dominated trade-off. This is a useful negative result, but it also limits the immediate practical recommendation to "use fixed placement when data is i.i.d. and the goal is to minimize bytes per round."
- **Rigor on the cost model is decent** — the model is fully derived, falsifiable predictions are stated (linearity of broadcast cost; super-linear 1/Oe throughput scaling), and the model recovers boundary conditions correctly (Oe=1 gives R=0; Oe=M gives DiLoCo/Photon). However, the model assumes uniform expert load and balanced routing, which the paper notes is generally not the case and would shift quantitative predictions.
- **Reproducibility is reasonable.** Section D reports the full config: tokenizer (openai/gpt-oss-120b, vocab 200019), seq len 2048, corpus (smollm-corpus fineweb-edu-dedup + python-edu 90/10), batch 1024, AdamW (β=(0.9,0.95), ε=1e-8, wd=0), K ∈ {32,64,128}, M ∈ {2,4,8}, seeds {42,123,456}. Hyperparameter sweep grids for η are given. **No mention of code/artifact release** — to be verified.
- **Red flags to watch:**
  - 100B is in the headline and is **not measured**; the paper does state this, but a casual reader could be misled.
  - "Up to 1.42×" / "up to 1.4×" are best-case numbers; the average-case improvement is much smaller (Table 7 shows many configurations where the gain is fractional).
  - DiLoCo collapses to 500+ PPL at K=128 — this is the paper's own baseline, so the strong claim "LocalAdamW enables FoMoE to match centralized" depends on choosing the right outer optimizer.
  - The "modeling" (rather than measurement) of 100B benefits is the right call given compute, but the 100B number in the abstract primes an inflated expectation.

## Strengths

- **Right problem at the right time.** Cross-datacenter / geo-distributed LLM training is the explicit compute strategy of major industrial and government efforts; reducing payload (not just frequency) is a genuinely underexplored lever.
- **Clean design decomposition.** Partitioning (Oe) × Placement (fixed/random) × Optimization (DiLoCo/LocalAdamW) makes the design space legible and the ablations easy to interpret.
- **Honest about scope.** Section 7 and the discussion of skip-token non-equivalence are unusually clear about what the paper does and does not show.
- **Solid local routing design.** Strictly local token dispatch (no inter-site activation exchange) is the right abstraction for WAN; FoMoE is intentionally orthogonal to intra-DC EP rather than a competitor.
- **A coherent cost model** that unifies FLOPs, memory, communication, and wall-clock, with boundary conditions and falsifiable predictions.
- **Strong author continuity** with prior work in federated/distributed LM training (Photon, DEPT, DES-LOC) — the paper reads as a natural next step rather than a one-off.

## Limitations

- **Empirical evidence is 54M-only.** No 2B / 13B / 100B runs, no downstream evaluations, no convergence-vs-FLOPs comparison at scale.
- **100B benefits are modeled, not measured.**
- **Setting is intentionally narrow:** homogeneous workers, i.i.d. data, static WAN bandwidth/latency, no jitter, no stragglers, no asymmetric links, no hierarchical WAN topology, synchronous rounds only. Real production has all of these.
- **Skip-token is a quality trade-off**, not a free lunch. The paper acknowledges it but does not quantify the downstream cost (e.g., on reasoning benchmarks).
- **Random placement underperforms** in the measured regime, so the immediately actionable advice is "use fixed placement," which is the simpler variant.
- **No EP / FSDP+EP / asynchronous baseline.** The relevant industrial competitors are not directly compared.
- **No custom kernel for skip-token** — the 1.4× speedup is a lower bound; the asymptotic gain may be higher (or, with kernel overhead, may not materialize in production).
- **Code release status unclear** from the abstract / main text; full reproducibility requires the code + the controlled WAN emulator.
- **DDP comparison is uninteresting** (DDP is known to be unsuitable for WAN); the paper should have led with the 1.42× over DiLoCo/Photon as the primary headline.

## Relevance

该工作直接针对"跨地理数据中心 LLM 预训练"这一热点场景，方法层面切中 DiLoCo/Photon 系列留下的"全量副本"瓶颈，并把通信开销建模为可调的设计变量（Oe、K、γ），对关注 **大模型分布式训练、专家并行、联邦/弱网预训练、通信–计算–内存联合优化** 的研究者和工程实践者具有较高参考价值；其架构选择（σ-MoE、Peri-LN）和状态同步外层优化（LocalAdamW）也与当前 MoE 长训练管线兼容。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What is the **average-case** communication and throughput improvement across a representative sweep of (M, Oe, K, γ), not just the best case?
- How does FoMoE compare head-to-head against **FSDP/ZeRO-3 + expert parallelism** in a geo-distributed setting, and against **asynchronous / stale-synchronous** variants (Streaming DiLoCo, DiLoCoX, GASLoC, SparseLoCo)?
- What is the **downstream-task cost** of skip-token at convergence, especially on reasoning-heavy benchmarks where MoE experts are thought to specialize? The paper flags this as future work — how sensitive is it?
- Can the cost model be calibrated with **measured** 2B / 13B runs, not just 54M, to validate the 100B projection?
- How does FoMoE behave under **non-i.i.d. data partitions**, which is the more realistic federated setting and the case where random placement is hypothesized to help?
- How does the system handle **stragglers and transient WAN degradation** in synchronous mode (it would block the round) — is there a clean asynchronous extension?
- How does **hierarchical WAN topology** (e.g., continent-level groups with intra-group Bcross) interact with placement? Is there a generalization of Oe to a tree-shaped replication factor?
- Can **skip-token be learned** (e.g., a per-token gating decision to skip ghost experts) rather than being a hard rule, to recover some of the quality loss?
- Is there a clean **theoretical convergence guarantee** for partial expert replication in the LocalAdamW regime, analogous to the analysis of DiLoCo?
- What is the **interaction with quantization / compression** (ρ in the model) — the paper says it is "orthogonal," but the combined gain could be multiplicative on real WAN links.
