Key Finding

The base model isn't inert — a tiny slice of pre-trained weights is load-bearing, and standard merging silently shatters it.

PACT: Preserving Anchored Cores in Task-vectors for Model Merging

Erasing the load-bearing subspace drops accuracy 82–98% across tasks; a matched random subspace barely moves it.
Why This Matters
Reveals that model merging silently destroys irreplaceable pre-trained knowledge — and offers a drop-in fix that lifts every major merger to new SOTA.
⚠ Critical Edge
Untested beyond small-vocabulary CLIP classification. No LLMs, detection, or generation — the load-bearing-wall story may not generalize where merging matters most for practitioners.

Methodology · distilled

PACT treats a fraction of the pre-trained weights as a load-bearing wall (LBW): directions the task barely updates but cannot survive without. It builds a per-task orthogonal shield from every other task's LBW, then strips a task vector's intrusion onto that shield before handing it to any standard merger.

01 · DIAGNOSE
Fisher+magnitude "crucial mask" (<2% of params) and a matched SVD subspace both flag the same load-bearing directions — verified by destructive perturbation.
02 · SHIELD
For task t, stack the LBW subspaces of all other tasks and orthonormalize into a global protection basis V⁴protect.
03 · FILTER
Project each task vector off the shield, then run TA / TIES / TSV-M / Iso-C / KnOTS unchanged. A randomized-SVD variant cuts cost to O(mnk).

Strengths

  • Reframes a real conceptual gap in Task Arithmetic: the base is not inert.
  • Three independent causal validations of LBW existence and damage.
  • Plug-in, data-free, training-free — minimal adoption cost.
  • Theory ties the gain to gradient×update energy inside the protection space.
  • RSVD variant is genuinely faster and occasionally better.
  • Per-task mutual shields (not a static global core) are proven to drive the value.
  • Works on LoRA even though LoRA leaves the base untouched.

Limitations

  • Limited to CLIP classification; no detection / segmentation / generation.
  • No LLM or instruction-tuning experiments despite the obvious application.
  • Fisher crucial-mask view and the deployed SVD filter are not formally connected.
  • (K, k) are global; per-layer / per-scale tuning left to future work.
  • Perturbation study uses α as large as 30× — magnitude not controlled for.
  • No wall-clock benchmarks; missing MAP / RegMean++ baselines.

Open Questions

Does PACT ever hurt when a task legitimately needs to modify base directions (safety alignment, style transfer)?
How does it compose with sequential / iterative merging — is the shield recomputed each step?
Is there a principled per-layer rule for (K, k), e.g. tied to spectral decay of θ₀?
Can LBW subspaces be reused across base-model families, or are they fingerprint-specific?
Does the orthogonal projection survive quantization / low-precision merging?
Does it transfer to decoder-only LLMs, where task-vector geometry differs from CLIP?
