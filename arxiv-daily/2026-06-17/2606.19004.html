Spotlight: Synergizing Seed Exploration and Spot GPUs for DiT RL Post-Training

Ruiqi Lai, Dakai An, Wei Gao, Ju Huang, Siran Yang, Jiamang Wang, Lin Qu, Dmitrii Ustiugov, Wei Wang
NTU Singapore · HKUST · Alibaba Group

diffusion-transformers reinforcement-learning spot-gpus sequence-parallelism systems-for-ml
Key Metric
6.39× lower cost

Up to 6.39× cheaper than reserved-only baselines; 1.42–4× faster convergence. Spot GPU utilization hits 99% vs 42%.

Why This Matters

DiT RL is GPU-cost-bound.

One commercial video DiT burns ~4,160 GPU-days. Spotlight turns idle spot capacity into useful exploration, making frontier diffusion RL training genuinely affordable.

Critical Edge

The “4× faster” headline is the best case — per-config results span 1.00–4.00×, so the upper bound is cherry-picked. The wide 1.35–6.39× range signals heavy trace- and price-sensitivity.

Methodology

  • Two core relaxations: (1) seed exploration tolerates the previous-iteration checkpoint — intra-group reward ranking holds across one update (Spearman 0.84 / 0.89); (2) 62% of vLLM-Omni's 120s startup is reusable (CPU-scheduler init + remote weight load).
  • Bandit UCB planner picks (sequences d × effective denoising steps s via Teacache) to maximize reward-variance ratio within the per-iteration spot-time budget.
  • Elastic Sequence Parallelism: a persistent CPU scheduler survives SP changes; new workers copy weights from an intra-node peer over NVLink — recovery ~45s vs ~120s full restart.
  • Preemption-aware pull-based scheduler + Mooncake Tensor Store commits in-flight latents to reserved GPUs on spot reclamation.
  • ~6k LoC on the open-source ROLL platform (vLLM-Omni + FSDP2 + FlowGRPO). Evaluated on Qwen-Image 20B.

Strengths

  • Two falsifiable, well-evidenced insights underpin the whole system.
  • Three orthogonal techniques cleanly composed; ablation isolates each (39% fewer iterations from exploration; 15.7% time cut from elastic SP).
  • Concrete monetary cost using real AWS/GCP/Azure spot pricing — not just throughput.
  • Entirely open-source stacks (ROLL, vLLM-Omni, Mooncake) — high adoption potential.

Limitations

  • Stale-weight validity shown only on Qwen-Image 20B; generalization to Flux/SD3/HunyuanVideo unproven.
  • Spot trace is synthetic-randomized for node placement; real rack-clustered arrivals likely worsen fragmentation.
  • Elastic SP recovery is 45s, not sub-second as the abstract implies — a claim-precision gap.
  • Image quality judged on only two benchmarks; broader human eval absent.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the stale-weight insight scale to video DiTs with longer denoising chains, where rank correlation may degrade?
  • Can the persistent-scheduler + NVLink peer-copy trick extend to tensor / pipeline parallelism reconfiguration?
  • Is there a theoretical bound on rank-stability as a function of KL drift between iterations? Evidence is currently empirical only.
  • How does Spotlight behave on non-NVLink interconnects (MI300X, multi-tenant clouds)? Intra-node bandwidth is load-bearing.
