02 Why it matters

A neuron writes the residual stream along multiple semantic directions at once — superposition. Zeroing it kills them all; OVE redirects one with a closed-form, no-inference-cost edit. First deployable mitigation built on that distinction.

Relevance · LLM safety, copyright compliance, controlled generation

03 Critical edge

Aggressive EOS collapses within 10 cumulative edits; Redact (β=200) cascades between 50–100. The single-edit evaluation that produced 87.9% hides the bulk-deployment cost — and paraphrase-transfer is explicitly untested.

Caveat · operational viability, not algorithmic plausibility

§ 1 Methodology

Decompose an MLP layer as MLP(x) = Σ aᵢ vᵢ. A neuron i is engaged by its activation aᵢ but writes the residual stream through its output vector vᵢ — and vᵢ can encode several directions at once. OVE edits vᵢ along a single distractor direction; activations are left untouched.

  1. Mine memorization20-token windows with window-BLEU > 0.5 over Dolma v1.7 → 6,831 unique sequences (OLMo-7B).
  2. Locate the layerLogit-lens importance, retain top-5 MLP layers with LI > 0.2.
  3. Locate the neuronL1-proximity attribution per layer; keep top-k neurons whose unembedding ranks the target in top/bottom 50 of the vocab.
  4. Rank-one editClosed-form update along a distractor token's unembedding direction. Closed form, no gradient.
  5. Pick a modeEOS · Redact · Next-best · Suppress. Ensembled: 96.5% of sequences editable.
  6. Validate localityWikiText-103 PPL (median, not mean) · HellaSwag · Wikidata QA.
Rank-one closed-form update vᵢ' = vᵢ + (Δᵢ − uₛᵀ vᵢ) / ‖uₛ‖² · uₛ    ·    Δᵢ = β · uₜᵀ vᵢ

Strengths

  • Mechanistic reframing. Splits engagement (activation) from writing (output vector) — superposition explains why the latter is the right intervention.
  • Same-neuron control. 2.7× gap over zero ablation isolates the edit's contribution from localization; gains aren't from finding more neurons.
  • Mode taxonomy with numbers. Ensemble 96.5%, recommended Next-best k=5 = Pareto point with zero catastrophic failures.
  • Cross-model transfer. SmolLM-360M → OLMo-7B → Llama2-7B, two families, 360M–7B. Success scales with size, not family.
  • Median-vs-tail honesty. Means are dominated by outliers; the paper reports both, which is methodologically correct.

Limitations

  • Single-token edit target. 74.9% of sequences tip on one token; 25.1% partially addressed, multi-token extension deferred.
  • Bulk-edit instability. Aggressive EOS collapses by 10 cumulative edits; Redact (β=200) cascades 50–100. Single-edit evaluation hides this.
  • Paraphrase-transfer untested. The realistic attack on memorization (rephrased prefixes) is explicitly deferred.
  • Proxy corpora underestimate. SlimPajama for Llama2-7B is not its true training data; absolute success there is a lower bound.
  • Recoverability not measured. Edits redirect rather than remove; adversarial recovery is acknowledged, not quantified.
  • 14% boundary is fuzzy. Attention ablation recovers 60–64% of MLP-unreachable sequences — MLP vs attention is a spectrum, not a clean split.

§ 3 Questions for further exploration

  1. Head-to-head with ROME / MEMIT / BiPO on the same 6,831-sequence benchmark — is the 2.7× gap also a gap over prior methods?
  2. Multi-token extension: iteratively edit the predicted continuation chain to suppress the 25.1% that don't tip on the first token?
  3. Joint MLP+attention edit (not sequential fallback) on the ~14% unreachable set — can the residual close without locality inflation?
  4. Does distractor choice matter semantically? Next-best with a semantically distant runner-up: more suppression, or just superficially fluent?
  1. Robustness to continued pretraining, fine-tuning, and adversarially paraphrased prompts that elicit the same memorized content?
  2. Cumulative-edit Pareto frontier (success, edit count) — the Appendix E stress test hints at 10 for aggressive EOS, 50–100 for Redact (β=200).
  3. Generalize to other localized-feature targets: toxic continuations, hallucinations, jailbreaks — same mechanism, new content?
  4. SmolLM-360M's systemic Suppress cost (∆PPL +8.95): scale-related (fewer redundant neurons) or architecture-related (nonlinearity/normalization)?
