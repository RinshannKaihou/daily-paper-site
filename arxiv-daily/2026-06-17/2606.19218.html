02
Why this matters

If you select LLM models on raw BERTScore F1, you're mostly measuring verbosity, not quality. A methodology negative result every eval pipeline should read.

03
Critical edge

The representation-design hypothesis — that contrastive objectives that buy validity erase between-system variation — is asserted, not directly tested. Isolating the contrastive-training variable is left to future work.

Methodology

  • Dataset (RECOM): 15K r/AskReddit questions, Sept 1–30 2025; depth-1 community replies as multi-reference. Contamination-free by construction — every model's training cutoff precedes the window by ≥9 months.
  • Models: 5 open-source 7–10B LLMs (Mistral-7B, Qwen2.5-7B, Granite3.1-8B, Falcon3-10B, Llama-3.1-8B) served via Ollama Q4_0 on 4× A5000.
  • Two baselines: real depth-1 reply vs. random-derangement (a different post's generated answer) — holds length and surface style fixed while breaking content correspondence.
  • Two axes: validity = paired Cohen's d (real vs. random); discrimination = inter-model |d|. 21+ metrics across lexical, semantic, inference, and LLM-as-judge families.
  • Length ablation: pooled OLS of metric on candidate word count, then re-measure d on residuals. Reveals length as the dominant driver of apparent model separation.

Strengths

  • Clean conceptual frame (validity × discrimination) that should be a default reporting dimension in LLM-eval papers.
  • Random-derangement noise floor — a simple, principled, immediately adoptable device for any open-ended generation benchmark.
  • Contamination control by construction (post-cutoff window) is more robust than typical decontamination filtering.
  • Length-confound ablation is a long-overdue sanity check on BERTScore — a publishable negative result in its own right.
  • Three-vendor LLM-judge protocol (Phi-4, Gemma-3, Yi-1.5; Krippendorff α ≥ 0.85) provides convergent validity.
  • Honest about limits — flags the narrow 7–10B band, opinion-driven domain, anonymous hosting, and absent NSFW screening.

Limitations

  • Narrow domain — English r/AskReddit, 30-day window, top-25K by engagement. Findings may not transfer to factoid QA, summarization, code, or non-English.
  • Narrow model band — 5 models in 7–10B; quality differences are small, so metric insensitivity vs. model similarity cannot be cleanly disentangled.
  • No human-rating calibration — the validity axis separates genuine from random, not from quality.
  • Representation-design hypothesis asserted, not directly tested — the contrastive-training variable is not isolated.
  • NLI section weak — out-of-domain BART-large-MNLI on Reddit; some contradiction rates fall below the random floor.
  • Reproducibility gaps — anonymous 4open.science hosting, no public code release, no NSFW / toxicity screening.
  • Multi-reference stylistic heterogeneity — replies mix jokes, anecdotes, brief opinions; max/mean aggregation only partially smooths.

Questions for further exploration

  • Does the validity–discrimination tradeoff persist on factoid QA and summarization (TriviaQA, Natural Questions, CNN/DM) where reference distributions are tighter?
  • Can a non-contrastive sentence encoder baseline (RoBERTa used for cosine without fine-tuning) directly isolate the contrastive-training variable the paper leaves untested?
  • Are there learned metrics (COMET, BLEURT, BARTScore, judge ensembles with length control) that actually achieve both high validity and high discrimination on RECOM?
  • Would a deliberate high-quality-gap study (7B vs. 70B vs. frontier closed models) let us distinguish metric insensitivity from model similarity — and probably break the cosine-validity claim?
  • Can the random-derangement noise floor be formalized as a per-metric bias term subtracted across the literature, enabling corrected effect-size reporting?
  • Is the NLI contradiction-rate inconsistency (some models below the random floor) a BART-MNLI domain-shift artifact, or a real limit of sentence-level NLI on opinion QA?
