Methodology

Activations as evolving geometry

Treats ViT activations as time-evolving geometric objects, not as inputs to a loss. Each epoch, the same 1000 images are run through the trained model; per-layer activations are captured via forward hooks and turned into a covariance matrix; observables are derived from its eigendecomposition.

Strengths & Limitations

READ WITH CALIBRATION

Strengths

What the paper does well
  • Crystal-clear framing — 5 RQs, 9 observables, 5 contributions, 3 hypotheses, 5 future observatories. The whole program is legible in one skim.
  • All metric definitions are mathematical, not hand-waved — reproducible from the paper alone.
  • Fixed subset × all layers × all epochs — the longitudinal geometric record is the paper's strongest single asset.
  • Empirical claim is specific, visual, and falsifiable — CLS covariance at epoch 100 vs. 20 is a concrete, replicable artifact.
  • Cross-observable consistency (RQ5) — six independent metrics pointing the same way is strong evidence the phenomenon is real.
  • Covariance diagonalization is independently interesting and underdiscussed in the ViT literature.

Limitations

What to weigh before citing
  • Single model — only ViT-Small/16. No ViT-Base/Large, no Swin, no CoAtNet, no hybrid.
  • Single dataset — ImageNet-100 only. Full ImageNet-1k / 21k / domain-specific untested.
  • Single training regime — supervised, single 100-epoch schedule, no self-supervised comparison (DINO, MAE).
  • No CNN / MLP baseline — claim reads as "ViT behaves like this," not "ViT is different."
  • No statistical rigor — no error bars, no multi-seed, no subset-size ablation.
  • No causal evidence — three hypotheses are floated, none is falsified or even partially evaluated.
  • No link to downstream performance — higher CLS effective rank isn't connected to linear-probe accuracy, robustness, or OOD.
  • Compute bottleneck — 197,000×384 covariance per layer per epoch on a Quadro RTX 6000 limits replication.

Questions for Further Exploration

What TGO-II/III/IV/V should answer first
  1. Does spectrum-spreading scale to ViT-Base/Large and full ImageNet-1k or JFT-300M with longer schedules?
  2. How does the trajectory differ under self-supervised pre-training (DINO, MAE, MoCo-v3) vs. supervised?
  3. A high-leverage experiment: measure token-cosine similarity over training — would directly test H1 and narrow the hypothesis space. Why didn't TGO-I do it?
  4. Is high CLS effective rank actually useful? Correlate with linear-probe / k-NN / few-shot accuracy across many seeds.
  5. Apply the battery to a CNN (ResNet-50, ConvNeXt) on the same data — if CNNs also spread, the framing must broaden beyond ViT.
  6. Test causal interventions: spectral normalization, whitening losses, anti-collapse regularizers — hurt, help, or no effect?
  7. Why does the final CLS have highest effective dimensionality / lowest anisotropy while also being the classification head's input?
  8. Will the code, checkpoints, and 197,000×384 covariance tensors actually be released? Without them the framework is hard to reuse.
  9. How does the spectrum respond to label smoothing, Mixup, CutMix, or adversarial training?
  10. Final layers "cluster" in trends — TGO-II (CKA / SVCCA / PWCCA) is exactly the right follow-up. When is it out?
