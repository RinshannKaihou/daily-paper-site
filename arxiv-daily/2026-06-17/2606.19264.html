Research briefarXiv 2606.19264

LL-Gibbs re-purposes an LLM's next-token conditionals as a Gibbs MCMC kernel, with stationary distribution equal to the LM's joint averaged uniformly over variable permutations.

Structured Inference with Large Language Gibbs  ·  Choi, Gouk & Whitammer (2026)

LLM MCMC Gibbs sampling Bayesian structure learning Consistent reasoning qsym

Why this matters

A principled, theory-backed recipe for using an LLM as a structured prior — directly applicable to consistency reasoning and Bayesian causal discovery.

Critical edge

An n=64 reasoning run takes ~64× the LM calls of one-pass generation — and the chain's stationary distribution qsym need not match any "true" joint, so self-consistency ≠ correctness.

Methodology

Shuffle, resample, repeat — over a single variable at a time.

A structured object X = (X1, …, Xn) is treated as n conditionally-resampled tokens, each tied to a natural-language descriptor ci. The LM's next-token distribution is the only access to the joint.

Resample step Sample i ∼ Unif({1,…,n}); draw a random permutation σ−i of the other n−1 variables; then Xi(t) ∼ pLM( Xi  |  seq(X−i(t−1), σ−i) ⊕ [ci] )
01 PickPick a variable i uniformly at random.
02 ShuffleSerialize the other n−1 variables in a fresh random order σ−i.
03 ResampleDraw Xi from pLM conditioned on the shuffled context.
04 RepeatUntil burn-in is reached, then thin.
Target distribution qsym(X)  ≜  𝔼σ[ pLM( seq(X, σ) ) ]  =  uniform average of the LM's joint over all n! permutations of the variables.

Under Assumption 3.1 (permutation invariance with Xi fixed at position n), the chain's unary conditionals match those of qsym, so qsym is the unique stationary distribution (Proposition 3.2). Practical variants: Sweeping (fixed order — may shift the stationary), Block Gibbs (B>1 variables at a time, autoregressive within the block), and two Metropolis-within-Gibbs variants — Barker (LLM-as-Metropolis-acceptance) and Gambling (deterministic "bet" needing no calibrated probabilities).

Results & highlights

Consistent reasoning, three applications.

Each "variable" is a binary answer to a question-claim pair; 25 Gibbs samples → majority vote. Three seeds, base models.

Monotone: 1-Pass → 4-Pass → Gibbs (n=4) → (n=16) → (n=64). Shuffling is the key ingredient.

Simple distributions. On 𝒰[{0,…,99}] and 𝒩(0,1) (n=16 i.i.d. variables, Llama-3.1-8B), independent LLM sampling is biased and batched sampling has high autocorrelation. LL-Gibbs is a Pareto improvement — lower bias and lower autocorrelation.

Bayesian structure learning. On four BnRep networks (5–15 nodes), LLM-generated synthetic data used as an LLM-informed prior (P(G | DLLM, γ)) helps DAG-GFlowNet in the small-data regime. γ = 0.2–0.5 is the sweet spot; γ = 1.0 often hurts — an honesty point about LLM-as-prior limits.

Strengths

  • Clean theoretical story: random shuffling ⟹ qsym is the unique stationary distribution under an explicit assumption.
  • Variants cover the full range of LM behaviors — generative, discriminative-calibrated (Barker), discriminative-threshold (Gambling), single-site, block, sweep, shuffle.
  • Three independent application domains with code, prompts, and 3-seed std released (hyeok9855/large-language-gibbs).
  • Honest reporting: Gambling Gibbs fails at 8B, ICM baseline had bugs they fixed, γ=1 overweighting hurts structure learning.
  • The "shuffling is essential" ablation (1-Pass → 4-Pass → Gibbs) is clean and falsifiable.

Limitations

  • Compute cost. T iterations × O(n) LM calls — roughly ~64× a one-pass generation for the n=64 reasoning experiment. Wall-clock is not quantified.
  • Limited model scale. Llama-3.1-8B and OLMo-3-32B only. Gains may not transfer to frontier reasoning models.
  • Theoretical gap. The chain converges to qsym, but qsym need not match any "true" joint. No divergence-to-oracle bound is given.
  • Bayes structure learning at small scale. n ≤ 15 nodes, ≤ 200 synthetic samples; real causal discovery has n ~ 50–1000.
  • No prompt ablation. Structured-output (JSON) decoding papers over some failure modes; prompts are fixed.
  • Apples-to-oranges baseline pairing in places: Gibbs-base vs Direct-instruct.

Questions for further exploration

  1. Can LL-Gibbs be combined with prompt-level self-consistency or chain-of-thought, or does the chain implicitly subsume CoT?
  2. How sensitive is mixing time to n? Is there a "critical n" beyond which the chain stalls, and does block Gibbs help predictably?
  3. Can random shuffling be replaced with a learned order (GFlowNet / attention scheduler) while keeping qsym as the target?
  4. For Bayesian structure learning, what is the empirical scaling of LL-Gibbs prior benefit as |D| grows? Zero marginal value eventually?
  5. Does Gambling Gibbs work reliably at frontier scale (70B+) for non-numerical gambling? Could it replace reward-model reranking?
  6. How does LL-Gibbs compare to MCMC-with-MCTS, particle filters, or SMC for the same structured tasks — none of which the paper benchmarks against?
  7. Is there a convergence diagnostic (PSRF over multiple chains, trace-rank statistics) so practitioners know when to stop the chain?
