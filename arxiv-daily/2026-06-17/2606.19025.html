FoMoE: Breaking the Full-Replica Barrier with a Federation of MoEs

Key Metric
1.42×communication cut vs. strongest federated baseline (45.44× vs. DDP)
Why This Matters

As cross-datacenter pre-training becomes industry's explicit compute strategy, partitioning experts — not just sync frequency — is a genuinely underexplored lever for shrinking WAN payload.

Critical Edge

Every measured claim is 54M-proxy C4 perplexity only. No 2B/13B/100B runs, no downstream evals — the headline 100B gains are modeled, never trained.

Methodology

Overlap factor Oe ∈ {1,…,M} partitions expert layers across sites. Each site hosts Nl = (Oe/M)·Ne experts; dense params (attention, norms, embeddings) stay fully replicated.

  • Topology: M homogeneous sites, Bintra≈200 Gbps ≫ Bcross≈1–10 Gbps, 50 ms latency; synchronous rounds with K local steps.
  • Placement: Fixed (static per-site set) vs. Random (reshuffle every T rounds, preserving Oe). Random underperforms on i.i.d. data.
  • Local routing: tokens dispatch only to resident experts — no inter-site activation exchange.
  • Skip-token: router scores "ghost" (non-resident) experts but skips their FFN compute → up to 1.4× throughput (150M, 8×B200), an under-estimate (no custom kernel).
  • Optimizer: LocalAdamW (state-synchronized) dominates stateless DiLoCo; essential at high K. Architecture: σ-MoE + Peripheral LayerNorm.
  • Cost model: closed-form FLOPs, bytes/token, wall-clock; four regimes incl. "FoMoE Sweet Spot" (low Oe, high K).

At K=128: DiLoCo PPL → 507/420/647, while LocalAdamW stays ~107/112/160. High-K amortization requires a stateful outer optimizer.

Projected, not trained

XL (13B) and XXL (~104B, d=4096, 512 layers, 128 experts) are analytical extrapolations of the cost model — not empirical runs.

Strengths

  • Targets the right problem: shrinking cross-DC payload is an underexplored complement to DiLoCo/Photon's "reduce frequency" lever.
  • Legible design space — Partitioning × Placement × Optimizer — makes ablations easy to read.
  • Strictly local routing (no inter-site activations) is the correct WAN abstraction; orthogonal to intra-DC EP.
  • Coherent, falsifiable cost model recovering boundary cases (Oe=1 → 0 ring cost; Oe=M → DiLoCo).
  • Unusually honest scope statement (§7) and skip-token non-equivalence disclosure.

Limitations

  • Evidence is 54M-only: no 2B/13B/100B runs, no MMLU/HumanEval, no convergence-vs-FLOPs at scale.
  • 100B headline gains are modeled, not measured — casual readers may over-read.
  • Narrow setting: homogeneous workers, i.i.d. data, static WAN, no jitter/stragglers, sync rounds only.
  • No EP / FSDP+EP / asynchronous (Streaming DiLoCo, DiLoCoX) baselines — the real competitors.
  • Skip-token is a quality trade-off, not a free lunch; downstream cost unquantified.
  • Code/artifact release status unclear; DDP comparison is a near-trivial strawman.

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. What is the average-case (not best-case) gain across a representative sweep of (M, Oe, K, γ)?
  2. Head-to-head vs. FSDP/ZeRO-3 + EP and async variants (Streaming DiLoCo, DiLoCoX, GASLoC) on geo-distributed WAN?
  3. Downstream reasoning cost of skip-token — how sensitive is it where experts specialize?
  4. Can the cost model be calibrated with measured 2B/13B runs to validate the 100B projection?
  5. Behavior under non-i.i.d. partitions — the realistic federated regime where random placement may finally help?
  6. Does hierarchical WAN topology (continent-level groups) demand a tree-shaped generalization of Oe?
