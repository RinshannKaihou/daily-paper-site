Reference-Driven Multi-Speaker Audio Scene Generation from In-the-Wild Priors

Michael Finkelson, Daniel Segal, Eitan Richardson, Shahar Armon, Nani Goldring, Poriya Panet, Nir Zabari, Benjamin Brazowski, Or Patashnik, Yoav HaCohen · Lightricks / Tel Aviv University

Best cpWER 0.145, cpSIM 0.567, ACC 0.866, WER 0.020 on CoVoMix2-Dialogue-20s.

Why This Matters

Drops all structured supervision — per-turn tags, multi-stream transcripts, speaker embeddings — for multi-speaker audio: a simpler design point that should transfer to image and video reference conditioning.

Critical Edge

Worst UTMOS speech quality among speech-only baselines (3.44 vs 3.76), hand-waved as a domain mismatch; WildRef margins over MOSS-TTSD rest on only 100 examples and no paraphrase-robustness study.

Methodology

ScenA fine-tunes the LTX-2.3 audio-video DiT (audio stream only) into a reference-conditioned text-to-audio scene generator.

  • Conditioning: K reference voice latents are concatenated with the noised target in one self-attention stream — z = [z_t; r_1 … r_K] — distinguished by one learned per-slot additive embedding each. No adapter, no identity encoder.
  • Prompt: a single free-form paragraph names voices as "the speaker from reference k"; no special tokens, no per-turn transcripts.
  • Reference Shortcut: a frozen-backbone probe matches target↔reference at ≥98% accuracy for t ≤ 0.58 and 75% even at t = 0.96 — so under logit-normal timesteps the model bypasses text, then fails at inference (pure-noise start).
  • Fix: mixture p(t) = (1−λ)·Beta(α,1) + λ·Uniform(ε,1) biases training toward high noise; adversarial distractor references and a slot-shuffle curriculum (off for first 10k steps) force text-based routing.
  • Training: 20k steps, batch 128, 16× NVIDIA GB200, ~24 h; rectified-flow velocity prediction, EMA 0.9999, K_max = 3.

Strengths

  • Clean, falsifiable probe diagnosis of a non-obvious training failure.
  • Minimalist conditioning — one slot vector per reference, no architectural surgery.
  • Monotonic noise-schedule ablation directly ties shortcut-closing to binding gains.
  • WildRef stress test + A/B vs. strongest baseline, which most papers omit.
  • Qualitative scene skills (overlap, ambient, paralinguistics) earn the "scene" framing.

Limitations

  • 20-second cap and K_max = 3 inherited from the LTX-2 backbone.
  • UTMOS gap acknowledged but not quantified; possible real quality trade-off.
  • WildRef = 100 examples; reference pool described but not released.
  • Single backbone (LTX-2.3), single language; no prompt-paraphrase study.
  • Code/weights release not explicit; reproducibility hinges on the data pipeline.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Does the Reference Shortcut persist in smaller audio backbones, or is it a property of large in-the-wild models?
  • Does the high-noise-biased schedule transfer to IP-Adapter-style image reference conditioning?
  • What is the marginal contribution of each auxiliary augmentation vs. the schedule fix alone?
  • How robust is free-form prompting to paraphrase ("reference 1" vs. "the older man" vs. non-indexed cues)?
  • Can a Perceiver-style reference compressor extend K_max to 5–10 speakers?
