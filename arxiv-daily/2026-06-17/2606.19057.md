> **一句话总结：** 将"选择性人类监督"下 LLM 评判器偏置问题重构为正-无标注 (PU) 学习任务，并提出基于部分最优传输 (POT) 的几何审计框架 PUAUDIT：在冻结的奖励模型嵌入空间中用"差分向量"刻画"胜者-败者"方向，先对正例做两步几何去噪再用 POT 把正例分布与无标注池部分对齐，从而在不改判器权重的前提下对每个无标注样本给出"是否与人类一致"的对齐分数，按阈值翻转被偏置污染的偏好。

> **TL;DR:** PUAUDIT treats selective human supervision in LLM-as-a-Judge as a Positive–Unlabelled (PU) learning problem. After a two-stage geometric denoising of the human-verified positives, it runs Partial Optimal Transport (POT) in a frozen reward-model embedding space (Skywork-Reward-Llama-3.1-8B) between the cleaned positive distribution and the unlabelled pool. For every unlabelled comparison it derives a normalized alignment score; if that score falls below a threshold η, the LLM judge's preference is flipped. Across Mistral-7B, Qwen-2.5-7B, Llemma-7B and GPT-5.4-mini judges on Chatbot Arena and MT-Bench, the method improves human-preference consistency by 2–26 percentage points in the no-attack setting and largely restores it under length, sentiment, and distraction attacks — without retraining the judge.

## 核心贡献
- **问题重构**：将"人类标注稀缺且选择性"下的 LLM 评判器去偏任务重新定义为 PU 学习任务，明确指出"将未标注全部当负样本"会在选择性标注下引入严重偏差。
- **PUAUDIT 框架**：基于部分最优传输 (POT) 的几何审计方法，运行在冻结的奖励模型嵌入空间上，**不改判器权重**就能识别与人类一致的无标注样本。
- **差分嵌入 + 几何去噪**：用"胜者-败者"差分方向向量表示每对比较；分两步对正例做几何去噪（向正例中心的 P-core 过滤 + 沿正例主方向的 improvement-alignment 过滤），增强正例分布的锥形浓度。
- **理论与实证**：在单位超球上给出 POT 高概率恢复正例的证明 (Thm 4.3) 与对偏置扰动稳定的稳定性证明 (Thm 4.5，扰动敏感度被 O(λ·ϵ_p) + O(λ²) 限定)，并通过长/情感/分心三种偏置攻击验证了"攻击无关"的鲁棒性。
- **对偶信号**：POT 计划 Γ* 给出每个无标注样本的"与人工正例分布对齐度"分数，提供可解释的、逐样本的置信度，便于选择性人工复核。

## Key Contributions
- **Problem reframing.** Formalizes LLM evaluation under selective human supervision as a Positive–Unlabelled (PU) learning problem; explains why naively treating unlabelled data as negatives fails.
- **PUAUDIT algorithm.** A retraining-free geometric auditor based on Partial Optimal Transport (POT) over a frozen encoder's representation space, with a two-stage positive-set denoising step (P-core + improvement-alignment) that increases positive-cone concentration.
- **Difference-embedding representation.** Each pairwise comparison is reduced to a unit direction z = (F(x,o_w)−F(x,o_ℓ)) / ‖·‖ on the hypersphere, which directly encodes the gradient of relative quality.
- **Per-instance interpretable confidence.** The transport plan Γ* and its normalized marginal s̄_j quantify how strongly each unlabelled comparison aligns with the verified-positive manifold; below threshold η, the judge's preference is flipped.
- **Theory.** High-probability recovery of latent positives by POT (Thm 4.3, with explicit utility gap ∆ > 0 in high dimension) and stability under orthogonal bias perturbations (Thm 4.5, first-order sensitivity bounded by O(λ·ϵ_p)+O(λ²)).
- **Empirics.** Improvements of 2–26 percentage points in human-preference consistency across four LLM judges (Mistral-7B, Qwen-2.5-7B, Llemma-7B, GPT-5.4-mini) on Chatbot Arena and MT-Bench; large robustness gains under length, sentiment and distraction attacks.

## Methodology
- **Data & notation.** Each data point is a prompt x with two responses o_A, o_B produced by LLMs A and B, judged by an LLM judge ζ^(LLM) ∈ {A, B, Tie} and, when available, a human ζ^(H) ∈ {A, B, Tie}. The positive set D_P = {(x, o_w, o_ℓ, y=1)} contains cases where human and LLM agree; the unlabelled set D_U contains comparisons where only ζ^(LLM) is observed, and y is latent. Ties are excluded because the difference embedding requires an ordered winner–loser pair.
- **Frozen encoder F_θ.** A pretrained reward-model encoder (default: Skywork-Reward-Llama-3.1-8B-v0.2) extracts last-token hidden states of "<|user|>x<|assistant|>o" as the prompt–response representation. The encoder is never fine-tuned; the audit lives entirely on top of these embeddings. Encoder ablation (Table 3): Skywork-RM is the only one that strictly improves over the original (Orig 0.694 → Adj 0.739, +0.045); BGE-M3 is near-flat (+0.014), Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B hurts (-0.081), and EmbeddingGemma-300M catastrophically flips nearly everything (Adj 0.306, -0.387).
- **Difference embedding.** For each comparison, define the unit "improvement direction" z = (F(x,o_w)−F(x,o_ℓ))/‖F(x,o_w)−F(x,o_ℓ)‖ ∈ S^{d-1}. This focuses the geometry on relative improvement rather than absolute response content, which matters when both responses are fluent and semantically close.
- **Two-stage positive denoising (within each question type).** (1) P-core filtering: keep the top α₁ (default 0.7) of positives whose preferred-response embeddings are closest to the positive center. (2) Improvement-alignment filtering: keep the top α₂ (default 0.7) whose z are most aligned with the average positive direction. The result D_P' is a geometrically coherent positive anchor set, defensively per question type to avoid conflating semantic topics.
- **Partial Optimal Transport (POT).** Solve a partial transport plan Γ* ∈ R^{n'_P × n_U}_+ that minimizes ∑ C_{ij} Γ_{ij} with cosine cost C_{ij} = 1 − d(z_i, z_j), marginals Γ 1_{n_U} ≤ a, Γ^T 1_{n'_P} ≤ b (allowing unmatched mass), and a global transport-mass constraint ∑Γ_{ij} = m. The inequality margins explicitly accommodate that D_U is a mixture of latent positives and latent negatives — unmatched mass is left alone, rather than forced into spurious matches.
- **Per-sample alignment score.** s_j = ∑_{i∈D_P'} Γ*_{ij}, then normalized s̄_j = s_j / max_k s_k. Intuitively, s̄_j measures how strongly sample j in D_U is reachable from the verified-positive manifold. If s̄_j < η (default 0.5), the judge's preference is flipped; otherwise the original ζ^(LLM) is kept.
- **Estimating the transport fraction m.** The latent positive proportion in D_U is unknown. The paper holds out a portion of the human-labelled set, measures the empirical consistency between ζ^(H) and ζ^(LLM), and uses that consistency as a proxy for the positive mass — that is, m ≈ Cons_orig on the held-out positives.
- **Theoretical guarantees.**
  - **Thm 4.3 (recovery).** Under a directional-concentration assumption on positives and a separability assumption on negatives (with margin δ > τ(1−τ)), the POT plan concentrates on the latent positive set as n_P → ∞, and the alignment score is non-zero iff z ∈ D_U^+. The utility gap ∆ ≥ (τ² − τ + δ) − 2t > 0 holds with probability tending to 1 in high dimension d.
  - **Thm 4.5 (stability).** If the bias direction z_bias is approximately orthogonal to the quality direction z (Assumption 4.4) and anchors are tightly aligned (τ_i → 1, ‖z_i^⊥‖ ≤ ϵ_p), the cosine similarity between an anchor and a perturbed embedding z̃ = (z + λz_bias)/√(1+λ²) is stable up to O(λ·ϵ_p) + O(λ²). The P-group denoising is what makes ϵ_p small enough to suppress the first-order term.

## Results & Highlights
- **No-attack setting (Table 1 / Table 4 in Appendix B.4).** Across six question types (QTA Programming, QTB Math, QTC Factual, QTD Writing, QTE Advice, QTF Other):
  - **Mistral-7B judge:** every question type improves, +9.5 / +12.6 / +13.8 / +8.2 / +14.4 / **+25.9** percentage points (QTF on a relatively small sample).
  - **Qwen-2.5-7B judge:** four of six question types improve (+2.4 / +4.4 / +1.5 / −0.7 / −0.1 / +6.9); the two slight regressions are under 1 pp and occur on types where the judge was already well aligned.
  - **Llemma-7B judge:** +10.7 / +0.9 / +9.0 / +17.3 / 0.0 / **−3.3** (the only clear regression, on a tiny QTF cell, n=7).
- **Length, sentiment, and distraction attacks (Figure 3, Table 4).** All three attacks reduce Cons_orig sharply (e.g., Mistral QTA drops from 58.4% to 56.6% under length attack, Qwen QTF from 56.4% to 49.5%, etc.). PUAUDIT recovers most of the lost alignment; radar plots show the per-question-type consistency profile becoming near-circular after adjustment, indicating the method equalizes bias across categories rather than overfitting to one. Improvements are largest on the most vulnerable types (Advice / Writing) and smallest on the most objective types (Programming / Math) — exactly the regime where bias is least semantic.
- **Encoder ablation (Table 3, MT-Bench).** Orig = 0.694. Adj with Skywork-RM = 0.739 (+0.045, 9.8% flips). BGE-M3 = 0.708 (+0.014). Qwen3-Embedding-0.6B = 0.613 (−0.081). EmbeddingGemma-300M = 0.306 (100% flips, catastrophic). The method is sensitive to encoder choice: it works when the encoder naturally encodes preference, and silently destroys the judge's signal otherwise.
- **Sensitivity (Appendix B.4, Tables 6–7).** Cons_adj plateaus quickly as the positive fraction p_ratio increases (a small clean positive set is enough to estimate the consensus direction), and varies smoothly with the transport mass m, peaking when m is large enough to cover most latent positives.
- **Interpretability.** Figure 2's diagnostic visualization separates ground-truth-consistent vs inconsistent comparisons along the alignment score, and a 2D projection of fixed embeddings colored by 1 − s_i shows the auditor cleanly isolates a positive cluster around the consensus direction.
- **Authors.** Zilong Zhang* and Yi-Ting Hung* (equal contribution, Georgia State University, Dept. of Mathematics & Statistics); Lei Ding (University of Manitoba, Dept. of Statistics); Chi-Kuang Yeh (Georgia State University, Dept. of Mathematics & Statistics). Two statistics departments and one cross-listed department — a credible lineup for a PU + OT method targeting LLM evaluation.

- **Team.** Zilong Zhang (Georgia State, Math & Stats), Yi-Ting Hung (Georgia State, Math & Stats), Lei Ding (Manitoba, Statistics), Chi-Kuang Yeh (Georgia State, Math & Stats). Two statistics-heavy departments give the work genuine methodological standing in PU learning and optimal transport — not just an empirical LLM paper with off-the-shelf debiasing.

This is more thorough than the typical "we debiased an LLM judge" submission.
- **Reproducibility caveats.** No public code/dataset release is mentioned in the main text (only the Skywork-RM and judge model HuggingFace/OpenAI pointers). The MT-Bench and Chatbot Arena pipelines and attack prompts are in the appendix, so re-running the experiments is feasible but not plug-and-play.

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty.** The conceptual move — selective human supervision ⇒ PU learning ⇒ partial alignment in a frozen embedding space — is the clearest contribution; POT is a well-trodden tool that is given a new application. The novelty is more in framing than in algorithmic invention.
- **Rigor.** Theoretically the work is unusually careful: explicit utility gap, high-dim concentration lemma, two stages of denoising justified separately, and a stability bound that honestly calls its own bound "first-order, not purely second-order" rather than overclaiming O(λ²). Empirically, the four-judge × two-benchmark × six-question-type × three-attack grid is more thorough than most.
- **Weak baselines / missing comparisons.** The paper does **not** compare against the most natural debiasing baselines — length-controlled prompting, position-swap ensembling, Prometheus / JudgeLM specialized judges, or simple reward-model reranking. The headline "original judge vs PUAUDIT-augmented judge" framing risks looking strong by construction, since the alternative is an undefended judge. A pass/fail against CALM-style length normalization or a juries-of-LLMs setup would have made the case much stronger.
- **Encoder dependency (the biggest red flag).** The encoder ablation is the most informative table in the paper and also the most worrying: with EmbeddingGemma-300M the auditor flips 100% of the labels and tanks accuracy by 39 pp. There is no built-in detector for "this encoder is bad for auditing" — silent failure is possible, and the only mitigation offered is "use Skywork-RM" in practice. A practitioner using a slightly different embedding would not know whether the method is helping or hurting.
- **Hyperparameter sensitivity.** The default η = 0.5 and α₁ = α₂ = 0.7 are not stress-tested on held-out data; m is itself estimated from held-out human consistency, which conflates "judge is good" with "positive mass is high" and could mis-set m when the judge is biased. Sensitivity to m is reported as smooth, but the headline numbers (e.g., the +25.9 pp on QTF) come from cells with relatively few samples, and standard deviations are sometimes an order of magnitude larger than the gain.
- **Cherry-picking risk.** Improvements on QTF (the "Other" bucket) are large in absolute terms but the smallest n's are reported in the appendix, and one Llemma cell goes negative (-3.3 pp). The radar-plot narrative ("more circular ⇒ more uniform ⇒ more robust") is suggestive but doesn't directly measure human alignment.
- **Overclaiming.** The framing "scalable, statistically grounded alternative to existing LLM-as-a-judge pipelines" overstates generality — the method is a post-hoc auditor for a specific bias regime (presentation attacks / selective positives) and does not address many of the 12 bias types catalogued in Ye et al. (e.g., self-preference, position bias, anchoring).
- **Reproducibility.** Frozen encoders and four public/open-weight judges make it reproducible in principle, but no code release is mentioned; the attack templates and judge prompt are in the appendix, which helps.

## Strengths
- Principled, statistically grounded reframing of a real and common evaluation problem (selective human labels + biased LLM judge).
- Retraining-free, plug-in nature — drops onto any existing LLM judge with a frozen reward-model encoder and a small positive set.
- POT gives interpretable per-instance confidence that can drive selective human review.
- Two-stage denoising of the positive set is a practical contribution in its own right and is what makes the theoretical stability bound bite.
- Theory + experiments are tightly coupled: the empirical attack results are explicitly tied to Thm 4.5, and the encoder ablation is honestly reported (including the catastrophic EmbeddingGemma failure).
- The "recovered vast majority of lost alignment under attacks" result, with improvements concentrated in the most bias-prone question types (Writing, Advice), is genuinely useful for open-ended evaluation.

## Limitations
- **Encoder-dependent and silently fragile.** Without a Skywork-style reward-model encoder the method can catastrophically fail (Gemma-300M: −38.7 pp on MT-Bench). There is no internal check that the encoder is appropriate.
- **Class-prior estimation is circular.** m is estimated from held-out judge consistency, conflating "judge is good" with "many latent positives"; on biased judges this can mis-set the transport mass.
- **No comparison to the standard debiasing toolkit** (length-controlled prompting, position-swap ensembling, Prometheus / JudgeLM, juries of LLMs, simple reward-model rerankers).
- **Single-direction assumption.** The theoretical analysis assumes a single consensus improvement direction; the authors flag multi-manifold / multi-PU as future work.
- **Holds-fixed assumption is strong.** The encoder is never fine-tuned, but no analysis is given for encoder shift between train and deploy, adversarial prompts designed to game the embedding distribution, or domain transfer.
- **Ties are dropped.** All tied comparisons are excluded from training and evaluation because the difference embedding requires a strict winner; the method is silent on how to handle graded or rubric-based judgments.
- **Question-type partitioning for denoising.** The two-stage denoising is performed within each question type, which means the framework needs a pre-defined question-type classifier and a non-trivial sample size per type; cells with n in the dozens (Llemma's QTD/QTE/QTF) are not reliable.

## Relevance
该工作把"选择性人工标注"和"LLM 评判器偏置"统一到正-无标注学习与部分最优传输的几何框架中，给出"不改判器权重就能在嵌入空间里翻转被偏置污染的偏好"的具体算法与可解释的逐样本置信度，与"评估方法学 / LLM-as-a-Judge 鲁棒性 / 偏置量化与修正"方向高度契合；对关注大模型自动评估可信度、奖励模型与偏置审计的研究者具有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- How robust is the audit to adversarial prompts that deliberately push the loser-response embedding along z_bias to maximize the first-order sensitivity term O(λ·ϵ_p), and is there a way to certify ϵ_p online so silent failure (the Gemma-300M case) can be detected?
- Can m be estimated without holding out human labels — e.g., from the structure of Γ* itself or from a PU class-prior estimator — to break the circular dependency between judge quality and transport mass?
- How does PUAUDIT compare head-to-head with length-controlled prompting, position-swap ensembling, juries-of-LLMs, and Prometheus 2 / JudgeLM on MT-Bench, Chatbot Arena, and RewardBench, with the same judge fixed across methods?
- Can the framework be extended to graded or rubric-based judgments (ties, multi-level preferences, multiple LLM judges with disagreement) without losing the partial-alignment interpretability?
- Does the same geometry generalize to non-reward-model encoders (e.g., instruction-tuned LLM hidden states) if an additional contrastive fine-tuning is performed, while keeping the "frozen at audit time" property?
- Can the per-sample alignment score s̄_j be turned into a calibrated selection rule (e.g., via conformal prediction) that provides formal coverage guarantees for selective human review?
- What is the failure mode of the method when human positives are themselves systematically biased (e.g., annotators preferring longer answers) — does it inherit the bias of the verified positives, and can a small unbiased calibration set be used to detect this?
