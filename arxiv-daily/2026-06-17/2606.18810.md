> **一句话总结：** 提出 SC-GRPO（Self-Conditioned GRPO），对模型自身的 verified rollout 做 in-context 条件化并用产生的 per-token KL 散度作为 GRPO 梯度的乘性 credit 权重——无需外部 teacher、PRM 或真值标签；用 Qwen3-8B（thinking-disabled）在 AIME24/25、LiveCodeBench v6、AppWorld、WebShop 五个基准上 Avg@8 平均 47.44%，分别比 DAPO 高 5.86%、比 GRPO 高 8.07%，并超过使用外部 teacher 的 OPSD。

> **TL;DR:** SC-GRPO is a drop-in modification of GRPO that uses the model's own verified rollouts as an implicit teacher. For each rollout, the model is re-scored with one verified sibling trajectory in its context; the per-token forward KL between that self-conditioned policy and the original policy is mapped to a weight f(D) = D/(D + c) (with c = max(p75, 1e-4)) and multiplied onto the GRPO gradient. No external teacher, no PRM, no ground-truth labels beyond the verifier. On Qwen3-8B, SC-GRPO averages 47.44% Avg@8 across five math/code/agentic benchmarks — +5.86% over DAPO, +8.07% over GRPO, and beats OPSD with three different external teachers — while costing only +13% / +2% end-to-end step time on LCB / DAPO-Math.

## 核心贡献

- **SC-GRPO 算法**：将 self-conditioned policy（对 verified rollout 做 in-context 条件化）与原 policy 之间的 per-token KL 散度作为 GRPO 梯度的乘性权重 f(Di,t) = Di,t / (Di,t + c)，无需 PRM、外部 teacher 或真值标签。
- **理论分析**：证明 OPSD 中用 verified 轨迹构造的 self-teacher 在多条 verified trajectory 共存时，最小化 forward/reverse-KL 的 student 分布会落到 teachers 的 weighted-average，而该分布不对应任何可行 trajectory，从根本上解释了为何 RLVR 中的 vanilla self-distillation 失效（§4.2 / Appendix A）。
- **三路 group routing**：partial-solve 组 (2 ≤ nc < G) 用 verified 轨迹做 reference；solve-none 组 (nc = 0) 用随机 rollout 作 reference 计算 diversity 分数并以 α=0.1 系数当作 exploration bonus；nc = 1 或 nc = G 时退化为标准 GRPO。
- **自适应阈值 c**：用 micro-batch 内 token-level KL 的第 75 百分位数（带 1e-4 下限）做 normalization，避免固定阈值在偏态分布下退化为均匀权重。
- **实验结果**：在 AIME24/25、LCB v6、AppWorld、WebShop 五个基准上 SC-GRPO Avg@8 比 DAPO +5.86%、Pass@8 +8.92%；OOD (LCB→Codeforces) Avg@8 仍 +5.2%，与 in-domain +6.1% 几乎一致。
- **拒绝"加法式"蒸馏**：在 LCB 上 Forward / Reverse / Bidirectional KL 作为辅助 loss 全部跑输 SC-GRPO，最差 Bidirectional 34.35% vs SC-GRPO 43.22%，证伪了"自蒸馏只需换 loss"的假设。

## Key Contributions

- **Self-Conditioned GRPO (SC-GRPO)**: Uses per-token forward KL between the original policy and a self-conditioned policy (the same model with one verified sibling trajectory in its prompt) as a multiplicative weight on the GRPO gradient. The reward fixes the update direction; the KL only modulates its intensity per token.
- **Theory of OPSD failure in RLVR**: Proves that under both forward and reverse KL, distilling from teachers {π_eθ(·|si,t, τj)} over multiple verified τj yields a loss-minimizing student distribution proportional to Σj μx(j) pj (forward KL) or Σj pj (reverse KL) — an "infeasible weighted average" that does not correspond to any feasible trajectory. This explains why the obvious additive-loss variant of OPSD fails empirically in RLVR.
- **Group-routing scheme**: Partial-solve groups (2 ≤ nc < G) get verified-trajectory-based KL weighting; solve-none groups (nc = 0) get a diversity score s_i = mean_t f(D_i,t) over the reference length, normalized into a pseudo-advantage α · Â^div_i; nc = 1 or G fall back to plain GRPO.
- **Adaptive normalization c = max(p75(D), 1e-4)**: Robust to the heavy right-skew of token-level KL distributions; ablations show p50 collapses to uniform weighting while p75 with floor preserves the high-KL tail.
- **Empirical dominance**: +5.86% Avg@8 over DAPO and +8.07% over GRPO averaged across five RLVR benchmarks; +14–17% / +23–35% gains over OPD on AIME depending on the teacher model; 5.2% relative gain on OOD Codeforces (vs. 6.1% in-domain on LCB).

## Methodology

**Notation.** Policy π_θ, query x, group of G rollouts {o_i}, verifier reward r(x, o) ∈ {0, 1}, C(x) the set of verifier-approved rollouts in the group, n_c = |C(x)|.

**Self-conditioned teacher.** For rollout o_i, pick a verified trajectory τ ∈ C(x) (with τ ≠ o_i when o_i ∈ C(x)) and append it to the system prompt of the *same* model π_θ:

    π̂_θ(· | s_{i,t}, τ) := sg[π_θ(· | x, τ, o_{i,<t})]

Both teacher and student are scored on the same prefix o_{i,<t} of o_i, so the only difference is the presence of the verified demonstration in the context.

**Per-token KL weight.** At each response position t compute the forward KL

    D_{i,t} = KL(π̂_θ(·|s_{i,t}, τ) ‖ π_θ(·|s_{i,t}))

and map it to a bounded weight

    f(D_{i,t}) = D_{i,t} / (D_{i,t} + c),   c = max(p75({D_act}), 1e-4)

so f ∈ [0, 1). Tokens whose predictions are robust to seeing the verified solution (small D) get small f; "pivotal" reasoning tokens (large D) get f → 1.

**Group routing.**

- *Partial-solve (2 ≤ n_c < G)*: Use the GRPO advantage Â_i from Eq. (1). The KL weight sharpens credit assignment.
- *Solve-none (n_c = 0)*: Pick a random rollout o_r as reference, compute the average KL weight over the first L_i = min(|o_i|, |o_r|) tokens as a diversity score s_i = (1/L_i) Σ_{t=1}^{L_i} f(D_{i,t}), normalize within the group into a pseudo-advantage Â^div_i = (s_i − s̄) / (std(s) + ε), and use α · Â^div_i (α = 0.1) in place of Â_i. This provides a weak exploration signal for groups with zero GRPO gradient.
- *Fallback (n_c = 1 or n_c = G)*: Plain GRPO, f = 1 for all tokens.

**Objective.** The weighted GRPO loss multiplies the standard PPO-style clipped objective by f(D_{i,t}):

    J_SC-GRPO(θ) = E [1/G Σ_i (1/|o_i|) Σ_t  f(D_{i,t}) · min(ρ_{i,t} Â_i, clip(ρ_{i,t}, 1−ε, 1+ε) Â_i) ]

with the importance ratio ρ_{i,t} = π_θ(o_{i,t}|s_{i,t}) / π_θ_old(o_{i,t}|s_{i,t}) and advantage Â_i replaced by αÂ^div_i for solve-none groups. The KL weight is applied symmetrically to positive and negative advantages.

**Why weight rather than loss?** The paper shows (Table 2) that adding the self-conditioned KL as an auxiliary distillation loss L_GRPO + β L_distill — across Forward KL, Reverse KL, and Bidirectional KL, with both fixed β = 0.1 and linear warm-up 0.03 → 0.1 — strictly underperforms SC-GRPO on LCB v6 (best auxiliary variant 39.11% vs. SC-GRPO 43.22% Avg@8). The theory in §4.2 explains why: any additive distillation objective has the weighted-average pathology that distorts the student distribution.

## Results & Highlights

**Main results (Table 1, Qwen3-8B, thinking disabled).** Avg@8 / Pass@8 averaged across AIME24, AIME25, LiveCodeBench v6, AppWorld, WebShop:

| Method | AIME24 | AIME25 | LCB v6 | AppWorld | WebShop | Avg. |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Qwen3-8B (base) | 25.41 | 18.75 | 26.33 | 9.65 | 8.13 | 17.65 |
| GRPO | 41.67 | 34.16 | 39.59 | 12.50 | 68.93 | 39.37 |
| DAPO | 43.75 | 37.91 | 40.74 | 13.99 | 71.50 | 41.58 |
| OPSD-MiniMax-M2.7† | 29.17 | 19.58 | 26.05 | 9.87 | 11.40 | 19.21 |
| OPSD-DeepSeekv4-Pro† | 33.75 | 22.08 | 26.05 | 9.43 | 9.88 | 20.24 |
| OPSD-Oracle | 33.75 | 22.08 | 25.38 | 9.43 | 10.38 | 20.20 |
| **SC-GRPO** | **51.67** | **42.08** | **43.22** | **22.36** | **77.88** | **47.44** |

The largest absolute gains are on agentic tasks (+8.37 on AppWorld, +6.38 on WebShop over DAPO) — exactly the long-horizon setting where GRPO's uniform credit hurts most.

**Comparison to OPD on AIME (Figure 4).** Two configurations:
- *Failing OPD* (Qwen3-1.7B student, Qwen3-4B teacher): OPD drops to near-zero (-85%, -90% vs. base), while SC-GRPO gains +23% / +35% relative.
- *Successful OPD* (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B student, JustRL-DeepSeek-1.5B teacher): OPD gives +14% / +17% on AIME24/25; SC-GRPO still beats it.

**OOD generalization (Figure 5).** Train on LCB v6, evaluate on Codeforces: SC-GRPO 5.2% relative gain on Avg@8 (essentially identical to the 6.1% in-domain gain) while Pass@8 stays within 1.6% of DAPO. The authors argue this rules out overfitting.

**Ablations on LCB v6 (Table 3).**
- *Group routing*: "Partial + solve-none" (the paper's choice) gives 43.22/48.85. Restricting to partial-only drops to 41.41/45.80; including n_c = 1 collapses to 38.55/41.98 (entropy collapse from dominant single verified trajectory); covering all groups drops to 36.64/42.75.
- *Threshold c*: max(p75, 1e-4) wins at 43.22/48.85; fixed c = 1e-4 ≈ 42.93/48.09; p50 (median) drops to 41.98/45.03 (collapses to uniform weighting because the median sits below 1e-4 in the right-skewed KL distribution).
- *Diversity coefficient α*: α = 0.1 (paper) gives 43.22/48.85; α = 0.2 drops to 40.74/45.80 (exploration signal competes with RL objective).

**Compute overhead (Figure 3).** Actor update is 51% slower on LCB v6 and 37% slower on DAPO-Math, but end-to-end step time grows only 13% and 2% respectively because rollout generation dominates.

**Policy entropy (Appendix C.2).** SC-GRPO maintains consistently higher entropy than DAPO across all training steps, with the gap widening after step 200 — used to argue SC-GRPO preserves exploration capacity.

**REINFORCE++ failure (Appendix B.4).** REINFORCE++ peaks at AppWorld 0.175@step40 then collapses to 0.035@step101; on WebShop it drops from 0.327 (base) to 0.0@step80. The authors attribute this to exponential gradient variance from γ = 1.0 returns over hundreds of tokens, and use it to motivate trajectory-level GRPO advantages + KL modulation.

**Diversity coefficient ablation confirms "weak signal" role (A3).** α = 0.1 is presented as the sweet spot.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The "condition the model on its own verified rollout and read off the KL" trick is genuinely new and well-motivated by the OPSD theory in §4.2. The "weighted-average infeasibility" theorem is a useful contribution in its own right, even if the practical algorithm sidesteps the pathology rather than solving it.
- **Rigor**: The proof that OPSD's loss-minimizer is a weighted average over teachers (Eq. A.7) is given in Appendix A and looks technically correct under standard regularity assumptions. The paper does not, however, prove that the multiplicative-weighting objective avoids the same pathology — it merely shows empirically (Table 2) that additive losses fail. A tighter optimality or convergence guarantee for SC-GRPO would strengthen the paper.
- **Reproducibility concerns**:
  - The OPSD teacher coverage table (Table 5) is striking: MiniMax-M2.7 only solves 46.56% of LCB v6 problems, DeepSeekv4-Pro 67.94%, Oracle 79.39%. The OPSD baselines may be weak simply because the external teachers can't find correct trajectories — not because the OPSD objective is bad. This is acknowledged implicitly by the Oracle number, but the comparison is not fully controlled.
  - No code release yet; the prompt templates shown in Appendix B.2 are partial and don't include the AppWorld prompt.
- **Significance**: SC-GRPO removes the deployment cost of an external teacher from RLVR, which is the main practical blocker for OPD. The +5.86% over DAPO is meaningful, and the OOD result is the most interesting piece — it suggests the credit signal generalizes rather than overfitting to the verifier's idiosyncrasies.
- **Red flags to watch**:
  - The Avg@8 / Pass@8 gap is unusually large: SC-GRPO gains 8.07% on Avg@8 but 10.86% on Pass@8 (vs. GRPO). A single paper claiming double-digit gains over multiple SOTA baselines often means a hyperparameter/cherry-picking story hiding in the appendix.
  - The 5/5 sweep across such diverse tasks (math / code / single-turn / multi-turn) is unusual. Some of the OOD gain (Codeforces 5.2%) sits within noise of the in-domain number (6.1%), so the "consistent across domains" claim is fair but the *magnitude* should be read cautiously.
  - Solve-none diversity signal uses f(D) on a *failed* reference rollout and truncates at L_i = min(|o_i|, |o_r|) to "prevent reward hacking" — this is a heuristic that could itself reward-hack in subtle ways on long-horizon tasks where every reference is short.
  - No 32B+ / 70B experiment, no thinking-mode experiment, no test-time scaling curve. The Limitations section is candid about this, but it does mean the 5.86% gap may not survive at scale (cf. the well-known phenomenon that GRPO improvements on 7B rarely transfer to 70B).
  - The OPSD comparison is unfair to OPSD in one direction (OPSD uses LoRA r=64 adapters, not full fine-tuning) and unfair to SC-GRPO in another (OPSD with "Oracle" gets 3 attempts per query but is still weaker than the *base* model on LCB v6 — a sanity check on the OPSD setup that the paper does not address).

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical motivation: the "infeasible weighted-average" theorem (Eq. A.7) is a genuine contribution that explains why naïve OPSD collapses in RLVR.
- The multiplicative-weight formulation removes the "which divergence / which β" sensitivity of additive distillation losses.
- Plug-and-play: the only change to GRPO is one extra teacher forward pass per rollout and a per-token scalar multiply.
- Strong empirical sweep across math / code / single-turn / multi-turn OOD, with controlled baselines and ablation of every design knob (group routing, threshold, diversity coefficient).
- OOD result (Codeforces +5.2% after LCB training) is the most credible evidence that the gains come from credit assignment, not verifier overfitting.
- Compute overhead is honest: actor update 51% slower, but end-to-end step time only 2–13% slower.

## Limitations

- **Scale**: only 8B-parameter model with thinking disabled and ≤12 288-token responses. Effectiveness on larger models, longer responses, and explicit reasoning modes is unexplored.
- **Reference quality bottleneck**: SC-GRPO requires at least one verified rollout per prompt. For problems where n_c = 0 in the group, the diversity signal is a heuristic; for n_c = 1 the paper falls back to plain GRPO. Truly hard prompts (e.g., competition math where base accuracy is 5–10%) get no benefit.
- **Threshold c is ad-hoc**: max(p75, 1e-4) is justified empirically (Table 3) but the floor 1e-4 is task-sensitive — a principled adaptive schedule would be preferable.
- **DAPO-style engineering missing**: SC-GRPO is benchmarked on top of plain GRPO, not on top of DAPO's dynamic sampling / token-level loss. The 5.86% DAPO gap may shrink if SC-GRPO's KL weighting is composed with DAPO's improvements.
- **Single base model family**: only Qwen3-8B is reported for the main results. AIME / LCB results with DeepSeek-R1-Distill or Llama-3.1 are not provided.
- **No wall-clock-to-performance curve**: the 2–13% overhead is per-step, but a compute-matched comparison (rollouts × step count held fixed) is not shown.
- **No code release** confirmed in the paper text we could verify.

## Relevance

与"RLVR / LLM 推理后训练"高度相关：SC-GRPO 是对 GRPO 系列 token-level credit assignment 的实用改进，去掉了 OPSD 所需的外部 teacher、PRM 和真值标签，特别适合资源受限的 LLM 推理训练 pipeline；对于关注 agentic / 多轮任务 credit 分配的研究者尤其值得一读。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does SC-GRPO compose well with DAPO-style dynamic sampling and token-level loss? The paper only stacks on plain GRPO.
- The "infeasible weighted-average" theorem explains *why* additive distillation fails but does not prove that the multiplicative-weighting objective avoids the same pathology. A formal optimality or convergence guarantee would close the loop.
- The diversity signal for solve-none groups (n_c = 0) is a heuristic — f(D) truncated at L_i = min(|o_i|, |o_r|) to "prevent reward hacking" deserves a controlled stress test on long-horizon agentic tasks.
- Is the adaptive c = max(p75, 1e-4) still robust at 32B+ scale, where the KL distribution shape may change? The 1e-4 floor is suspicious as a universal constant.
- Can SC-GRPO be extended to non-verifiable rewards (RLHF preference pairs) by using a "preferred trajectory" in place of the verified one? The method fundamentally relies on a hard correctness signal, so this is unclear.
- How does SC-GRPO interact with test-time scaling (best-of-N, majority voting) and with explicit chain-of-thought reasoning modes (Qwen3 thinking enabled)?
- The OPSD comparison uses LoRA r=64, not full fine-tuning, and the "Oracle" OPSD still underperforms the base model on LCB — a fairer head-to-head (e.g., full fine-tuning OPSD or a stronger teacher like GPT-4o-mini) would test whether SC-GRPO's "no external teacher" advantage survives.
