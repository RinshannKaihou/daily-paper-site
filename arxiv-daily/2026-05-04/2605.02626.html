Gradient-Gated DPO: Stabilizing Preference Optimization in Language Models

Date: 2026-05-04

⚠️ Abstract-only summary

一句话总结： 本文提出 Gate-DPO，通过根据模型概率几何对拒绝样本的梯度进行门控调制，解决 DPO 训练中的概率挤压效应和分布崩塌问题，在较小模型上甚至能超越未门控的大模型效果。
TL;DR: Direct Preference Optimization (DPO) suffers from a "squeezing effect" where negative gradients on rejected responses collapse the output distribution. Gate-DPO introduces a gradient gate that attenuates harmful updates when targeting extremely low-probability responses, preserving standard optimization otherwise. It is complementary to existing fixes (extended SFT, IPO, Cal-DPO) and experiments show smaller gated models can outperform larger ungated ones, suggesting gradient control matters more than scale for stable alignment.

Key Contributions

  • Identifies and formalizes the DPO "squeezing effect": negative gradients on rejected responses concentrate probability mass on high-confidence predictions and suppress alternatives, leading to systematic probability collapse
  • Proposes Gradient-Gated Preference Optimization (Gate-DPO), which modulates rejected gradients based on the model's probability geometry — attenuating harmful gradients when targeting extremely low-probability responses while leaving standard optimization unaffected
  • Demonstrates complementarity with existing methods (extended SFT, IPO, Cal-DPO) since Gate-DPO does not alter the underlying preference objective
  • Shows empirically that smaller gated models can exhibit stronger chosen-response improvements than larger ungated models, suggesting that gradient dynamics control is more important than scale alone for stable alignment
  • Provides mass-dynamics analysis revealing healthier optimization trajectories with improved preferred responses and reduced distribution suppression

Methodology

Gate-DPO modifies the gradient computation in DPO's loss function for rejected responses. Standard DPO applies equal-strength negative gradients to all rejected tokens regardless of the model's current probability for them. When the model already assigns extremely low probability to a rejected response, the gradient still pushes it further down, which compresses the entire output distribution (the squeezing effect).

Gate-DPO introduces a gating mechanism based on the model's probability geometry for rejected responses. When the model's probability for a rejected response is already very low, the gate attenuates the gradient magnitude, preventing unnecessary distribution compression. When the probability is moderate or high, the gate allows standard DPO optimization to proceed normally. This acts as a soft modulator rather than a hard cutoff.

The method is evaluated across multiple architectures and preference datasets, with analysis including chosen-response likelihood tracking, squeezing effect measurement, and probability mass dynamics visualization.
