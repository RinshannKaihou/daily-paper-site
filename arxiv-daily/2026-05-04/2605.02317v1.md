> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Anon 优化器，通过连续可调的自适应系数在实数域上插值 SGD 和 Adam 的行为甚至外推超越两者，并引入增量延迟更新（IDU）机制保证全频谱收敛，在图像分类、扩散模型和语言建模任务上均优于现有优化器。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes Anon, an optimizer with continuously tunable adaptivity on the real line that can interpolate between SGD-like and Adam-like behavior and extrapolate beyond both. Introduces Incremental Delay Update (IDU) for robust convergence across the full adaptivity spectrum. Empirically outperforms state-of-the-art optimizers on image classification, diffusion, and language modeling benchmarks, offering the first unified framework bridging classical (SGD) and modern (Adam-family) optimizers.

## 核心贡献

- **连续可调自适应系数：** 提出将优化器的自适应程度作为实数域上的连续可调超参数，使优化器可以在 SGD 和 Adam 行为之间平滑插值，甚至外推至两者之外的行为模式。
- **增量延迟更新（IDU）机制：** 引入比 AMSGrad 硬性最大值追踪更灵活的更新策略，增强对梯度噪声的鲁棒性，确保在整个自适应频谱上的收敛性。
- **统一框架：** 首次提供了一个能够桥接经典优化器（SGD）和现代自适应优化器（Adam 系列）并超越两者优势的统一且可靠的优化框架。
- **理论与实证双重验证：** 在凸和非凸设定下建立了收敛性保证，并在图像分类、扩散模型生成和语言建模三类代表性任务上进行了全面验证。

## Key Contributions

- **Continuously tunable adaptivity in ℝ:** Frames optimizer adaptivity as a continuous real-valued design parameter, enabling smooth interpolation between SGD-like and Adam-like regimes and extrapolation beyond both extremes.
- **Incremental Delay Update (IDU):** A novel mechanism that replaces AMSGrad's hard max-tracking with a more flexible update strategy, improving robustness to gradient noise and ensuring convergence across the full adaptivity spectrum.
- **Unified optimizer framework:** Provides the first reliable framework that bridges the gap between classical non-adaptive methods (SGD) and modern adaptive methods (Adam family), while surpassing the advantageous properties of each.
- **Theoretical convergence guarantees:** Proves convergence under both convex and non-convex optimization settings for the full range of adaptivity values.
- **Comprehensive empirical validation:** Demonstrates consistent improvements over state-of-the-art optimizers across image classification, diffusion model training, and language modeling tasks.

## Methodology

The authors identify pre-conditioner adaptivity as the key factor behind the generalization gap between adaptive (Adam) and non-adaptive (SGD) optimizers. Rather than treating adaptivity as a binary choice, they propose a continuous spectrum parameter that controls the degree of adaptivity in the pre-conditioner. The Anon optimizer allows this parameter to take any real value, enabling interpolation (between SGD and Adam regimes) and extrapolation (beyond both). To ensure stable convergence across this full spectrum, they introduce Incremental Delay Update (IDU), which provides a softer, more flexible alternative to AMSGrad's hard maximum tracking of second-moment estimates. Theoretical convergence analysis is provided for both convex and non-convex settings.

## Results & Highlights

- Anon consistently outperforms state-of-the-art optimizers on representative image classification benchmarks.
- Demonstrates superior performance on diffusion model training tasks.
- Achieves better results on language modeling benchmarks compared to existing adaptive optimizers.
- The tunable adaptivity parameter allows practitioners to find task-optimal operating points between SGD and Adam behaviors.
- IDU mechanism shows improved robustness to gradient noise compared to AMSGrad's hard max-tracking.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The idea of treating adaptivity as a continuous real-valued spectrum rather than a discrete design choice is genuinely novel. The IDU mechanism offers a meaningful improvement over AMSGrad's hard max-tracking. However, without access to the full paper, it is difficult to assess whether the mathematical framework introduces fundamentally new theoretical techniques or primarily repackages existing ideas.

**Rigor:** The authors claim convergence guarantees under both convex and non-convex settings, which is a strong theoretical contribution if the proofs hold. The empirical evaluation spans three distinct task families (vision, diffusion, language), which is commendable. However, the depth of baselines, hyperparameter tuning protocols, and statistical significance reporting cannot be verified from the abstract alone.

**Reproducibility:** The continuous tunability of adaptivity is a clean design that should be straightforward to implement. The main concern is whether the optimal adaptivity value is task-specific and requires expensive tuning, which would reduce practical usability.

**Significance:** If the claims hold, this could be a meaningful contribution to the optimization literature — a unified framework that genuinely bridges SGD and Adam would be impactful for practitioners. However, the field has seen many "Adam-killer" optimizers that fail to generalize across settings, so sustained validation on diverse benchmarks would be needed.

**Red flags:** The title uses "Anon" which appears to be an anonymized submission placeholder, suggesting this may be under review. The acronym feels somewhat forced ("Adaptivity Non-restricted Optimizer with Novel convergence technique"). The claim of "consistently outperforms state-of-the-art" across all three very different domains is strong and warrants careful scrutiny of experimental details.

## Strengths

- Elegant conceptual framing: adaptivity as a continuous spectrum is intuitive and principled.
- Covers both theoretical (convergence proofs) and empirical (three task families) validation.
- IDU mechanism is a practical improvement over AMSGrad with clear motivation.
- Broad applicability across CNNs, diffusion models, and LLMs.
- The unified framework perspective is valuable for the community.

## Limitations

- The optimal adaptivity value likely requires per-task tuning, adding a new hyperparameter for practitioners.
- Without the full paper, the depth of theoretical analysis and experimental rigor cannot be fully assessed.
- The continuous spectrum introduces a search space that may be difficult to navigate without heuristics or auto-tuning.
- It is unclear how Anon interacts with other training techniques (learning rate schedules, warmup, weight decay).
- The relationship between the adaptivity parameter and problem geometry needs deeper understanding for practical guidance.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How sensitive is the optimal adaptivity parameter to dataset size, model architecture, and learning rate schedule? Is there a systematic way to set it without expensive search?
- Can the IDU mechanism be applied to other Adam-family variants (e.g., Adafactor, Lion) as a drop-in improvement?

- Does the adaptivity spectrum reveal any interesting phase transitions in optimization behavior (e.g., sharp vs. flat minima)?
- How does Anon perform on reinforcement learning and graph neural network tasks, which have notably different loss landscapes?
