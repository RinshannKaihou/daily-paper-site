> **一句话总结：** 本文从理论角度分析了RLVR中二元奖励导致多样性崩溃的根本原因：在模型误设定下，降低KL惩罚温度系数不会收敛到理想过滤分布，反而使策略集中在极少数有效输出上。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides a rigorous theoretical account of why RLVR with binary rewards causes diversity collapse. The author proves that binary rewards create a fundamental degeneracy in policy gradient methods — infinitely many distributions maximize expected reward. KL-control resolves this by selecting the filtered model (base model conditioned on validity) in the limit, but under model misspecification (the practical regime), decreasing the temperature β drives the policy toward concentrated distributions over a handful of valid outputs rather than the desired filtered model. The paper derives explicit formulas linking β to target validity rate and proposes that alternative divergences targeting the filtered model directly can avoid this failure mode.

## 核心贡献

- 揭示了二元奖励在策略梯度方法中造成的**根本性退化问题**：最大化期望奖励的分布集合是无限的，不存在唯一最优元素
- 证明了KL控制通过非平凡的非对称性选择出**过滤模型** $p_* = a(\cdot \mid \mathcal{Y}_1)$——即在KL散度意义下最接近基础模型的完全有效分布
- 指出在**模型误设定**（实际常见的情形）下，降低温度系数β会导致策略向高度集中的分布坍缩，而非收敛到过滤模型
- 推导了超参数β与更直观的**目标有效率μ**之间的显式关系公式
- 提出通过直接以过滤模型为目标的替代散度可以避免多样性崩溃

## Key Contributions

- Identifies a **fundamental degeneracy** of binary rewards for policy gradient methods: the set of reward-maximizing distributions is infinite, with no distinguished element
- Proves that KL-control selects the **filtered model** $p_* = a(\cdot \mid \mathcal{Y}_1)$ — the unique fully valid distribution closest to the base model in KL divergence — through a nontrivial asymmetry in the tilted distribution's convergence behavior
- Shows that **forward KL** convergence to $p_*$ works, but $p_*$ cannot serve as a direct optimization target because $\mathrm{KL}(q \| p_*)$ is infinite for any full-support policy $q$
- Under **model misspecification**, demonstrates that decreasing β drives the optimizer toward concentrated distributions over a shrinking set of valid outputs — the mechanism behind diversity collapse
- Derives **explicit formulas** relating the hyperparameter β to a more interpretable target validity rate μ
- Points to **alternative divergences** that target $p_*$ directly as a way to reward coverage of the filtered model's support rather than concentration on high-validity outputs

## Methodology

The paper develops a theoretical framework analyzing RLVR with binary (verifiable) rewards. It proceeds as follows:

1. **Degeneracy analysis**: Shows that for binary rewards $v(y) \in \{0, 1\}$, any distribution supported entirely on valid outputs maximizes expected reward, creating infinite solution degeneracy.

2. **KL-control resolution**: Analyzes the tilted distribution $p_{[\beta]} \propto a(y) e^{v(y)/\beta}$ and proves it converges to the filtered model $p_*$ in forward KL as $\beta \to 0$, establishing this as the "natural" selection.

3. **Asymmetry result**: Shows that while forward KL$(p_{[\beta]} \| q) \to$ forward KL$(p_* \| q)$, the reverse KL$(q \| p_*)$ is infinite for full-support policies $q$, making $p_*$ an impossible direct optimization target for standard policy gradient methods.

4. **Misspecification analysis**: Under model misspecification (the typical practical regime where the policy class cannot represent $p_*$), derives that the pressure to decrease β leads to concentration on fewer and fewer valid outputs rather than expanding coverage.

5. **Toy experiment**: Illustrates the mechanism on an autoregressive experiment demonstrating how diversity collapses as β decreases under misspecification.

6. **β-to-μ mapping**: Derives explicit formulas connecting the KL penalty temperature β to an interpretable target validity rate μ, giving practitioners a more intuitive knob to turn.

## Results & Highlights

- The infinite degeneracy of binary rewards is proven as a structural property, not an empirical observation
- The filtered model $p_*$ is identified as the theoretically desirable target — the KL-closest fully valid distribution to the base model
- Under misspecification, the paper proves that the standard KL-regularized objective drives policies toward **concentration** rather than **coverage**, explaining the empirically observed diversity collapse in RLVR-trained models
- The β-to-μ formulas provide practitioners with concrete guidance on hyperparameter selection
- The toy experiment confirms the theoretical prediction that decreasing β under misspecification leads to progressively fewer unique valid outputs

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. The degeneracy of binary rewards for policy gradient methods has not been articulated with this level of rigor before. The identification of the filtered model as the natural KL-control selection and the asymmetry result (forward KL converges but reverse KL is infinite) are genuine theoretical contributions.

- **Rigor**: Strong formal mathematical treatment with explicit derivations. The misspecification analysis is well-motivated and grounded in the practical realities of LLM training.

- **Reproducibility**: The toy autoregressive experiment should be reproducible, but full details may be limited in an abstract-only view. The theoretical results are self-contained proofs.

- **Significance**: Potentially high impact. If the theoretical account is correct, it provides a principled explanation for a widely observed empirical phenomenon (diversity collapse in RLVR) and points toward concrete algorithmic fixes (alternative divergences). This could influence how the community designs RLVR objectives.

- **Red flags**: (1) Single-author claim of a fundamental structural result — warrants careful verification by the community. (2) The proposed fix (alternative divergences) is largely deferred to concurrent empirical work by others, so the constructive contribution is somewhat limited. (3) Without access to the full paper, it is unclear how general the misspecification analysis is — whether it applies to all practical model classes or only specific restricted ones.

## Strengths

- Provides the first rigorous theoretical account of diversity collapse in RLVR with binary rewards
- The filtered model concept ($p_*$) is elegant and gives a clear theoretical target for what RLVR "should" converge to
- The asymmetry between forward and reverse KL with respect to $p_*$ is a subtle and important insight
- The β-to-μ mapping addresses a genuine practical need for interpretable hyperparameters
- The misspecification analysis correctly identifies that the practical regime differs from the idealized setting

## Limitations

- The constructive contribution is limited — the paper diagnoses the problem thoroughly but defers the solution (alternative divergences) to concurrent work
- The toy experiment, while illustrative, may not fully capture the complexity of large-scale LLM RLVR training
- The analysis assumes binary rewards throughout; the extent to which the degeneracy extends to sparse or graded rewards is not addressed
- Practical implications for existing RLVR implementations (GRPO, PPO with binary rewards) could be spelled out more concretely
- The single-author nature means the theoretical results have not yet benefited from collaborative verification

本文对RLVR训练中二元奖励的理论分析直接关联LLM训练稳定性研究兴趣。论文揭示的多样性崩溃机制为理解RL微调过程中输出质量退化提供了数学基础，属于ML可靠性与数学基础的研究范畴。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the theoretical results extend to non-binary (e.g., sparse or partially verifiable) reward settings commonly used in practice?
- Can the alternative divergences that target $p_*$ directly be implemented efficiently at scale for billion-parameter models?
- Does the diversity collapse mechanism interact with other training instabilities (loss spikes, gradient issues) observed in RLVR fine-tuning?
- What is the practical relationship between the β-to-μ mapping and the commonly used KL penalty coefficients in PPO/GRPO implementations?
