> **一句话总结：** 本文基于AI Ping平台Q4 2025的真实API调用数据，系统测量了多个提供商托管同一开源LLM时的服务异质性（延迟、吞吐、上下文长度、协议兼容性、错误语义等），发现同一模型名下的服务行为差异巨大，智能路由可使Qwen3-32B成本降低37.8%、DeepSeek-V3.2吞吐提升约90%。

> **TL;DR:** A large-scale measurement study using AI Ping's Q4 2025 data reveals that the same open-weight LLM served by different providers exhibits drastically different latency (TTFT), throughput (TPS), context windows, protocol compatibility, and error semantics. Demand is highly concentrated (top-5 families carry 87.4% of traffic, Gini=0.693) but older model versions persist. Prices are anchored near official benchmarks while performance varies widely. Two counterfactual routing experiments show 37.8% cost reduction for Qwen3-32B and ~90% throughput gain for DeepSeek-V3.2 versus direct official access. The paper argues the operational unit should be a provider-model-task-time tuple, not a model name.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了"服务对象"的形式化定义 S = (v, p, π, W, C, L, R, t)，将模型名称与实际API服务行为解耦，涵盖模型变体、提供商、协议、上下文容量、价格、延迟/吞吐分布、可靠性和时间维度。
- 基于AI Ping平台Q4 2025的大规模真实请求数据（涵盖371篇论文期间的API调用量），系统测量了多家提供商托管同一开源LLM时在延迟、吞吐、上下文长度、协议兼容性和错误语义上的异质性。
- 发现需求高度集中但版本持续存在：前5大模型家族占87.4%的相对需求（Gini系数0.693），但旧版本在新版本发布后仍持续被使用。
- 揭示了"供给≠使用"的脱节现象：提供商上架广度不等于实际采用量，且价格维度变异最小而性能维度（延迟、吞吐、上下文、兼容性）变异最大。
- 通过两个反事实路由实验量化了异质性的实际价值：Qwen3-32B成本降低37.8%（约150万请求），DeepSeek-V3.2平均吞吐提升约90%（约100万请求）。

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes a **service object** definition S = (v, p, π, W, C, L, R, t) that decouples model identity from operational API behavior across eight dimensions.
- Provides the first large-scale empirical measurement of **cross-provider heterogeneity** for hosted open-weight LLMs using real production traffic from AI Ping (Q4 2025), covering demand distribution, provider coverage, pricing, TTFT, TPS, context length, protocol compatibility, and error semantics.
- Documents **version persistence**: older model variants (e.g., Qwen2.5-72B, QwQ-32B) remain active after newer releases, suggesting production adoption inertia and validation-driven retention.
- Identifies the **supply-adoption mismatch**: provider listing breadth does not predict realized demand; widely listed models may have low traffic while high-traffic models depend heavily on provider choice.
- Demonstrates **measurable routing value** through two counterfactual experiments: 37.8% cost reduction for Qwen3-32B (~1.5M requests, ~1B input tokens) and ~90% throughput gain for DeepSeek-V3.2 (~1M requests) under feasibility constraints.

## Methodology

The study uses an **observational measurement design** over Q4 2025 (Oct 1 – Dec 31, Beijing time), combining five data sources: sampled request logs from AI Ping's routing layer, provider metadata, compatibility probes, pricing snapshots, and continuous latency measurements.

**Units of analysis** are stratified into three identity layers: checkpoint-level, variant-level (instruction, thinking, dated releases), and family-level (aggregated for public summaries only). A formal service object S = (v, p, π, W, C, L, R, t) defines the operational unit.

**Metrics** include: request volume (normalized to protect absolute traffic), provider counts, relative pricing vs. official benchmarks, TTFT (time to first token), TPS (tokens per second during decode), context length (provider-published vs. observed), protocol compatibility (OpenAI-compatible and Anthropic-compatible interfaces tested across 18 providers), and slow-response ratios.

**Key analytical tools**: concentration statistics (Gini coefficient, HHI, entropy effective number), row-normalized task-composition heatmaps, counterfactual routing comparisons fixing model labels while varying provider selection under feasibility constraints (protocol support, context window, availability).

**Task conditioning**: labeled request subsets are categorized into task types (news/information, creative writing, professional services, etc.), revealing different input/output token-length regimes that change the optimal provider selection criterion.

The authors explicitly acknowledge limitations: the dataset is a sampled measurement view (not a census), model identity is nominal (not checkpoint-verified), and counterfactuals are not randomized A/B tests.

## Results & Highlights

- **Demand concentration**: Top-1 family (DeepSeek-V3/R1) carries 32.0% of relative demand; top-5 carry 87.4%. Gini coefficient = 0.693, HHI = 0.202, entropy effective number = 6.55 across 16 displayed families.
- **Version persistence**: Qwen2.5-72B and QwQ-32B retain traffic despite newer Qwen3 releases; DeepSeek-V3/R1 traffic persists after V3.1 and V3.2 launches.
- **Provider coverage**: 29 providers observed in the sample; DeepSeek-V3/R1 supported by 23 providers. But coverage ≠ adoption — QwQ-32B has nontrivial provider count with limited traffic.
- **Price anchoring**: Most provider prices for popular models fall within 5% of official benchmarks, indicating limited price dispersion.
- **Performance dispersion**: TTFT and TPS vary substantially across providers for the same model. Official endpoints are not uniformly fastest; third-party providers are not uniformly slower.
- **Context length variation**: Significant cross-provider differences; context shrinkage acts as a hard feasibility constraint (not a soft penalty).
- **Protocol compatibility**: OpenAI-compatible interfaces average 12.2 providers per model; Anthropic-compatible averages only 3.6 providers per model (29.7% adoption rate).
- **Error semantics inconsistency**: Providers exhibit HTTP 200 with error payloads, empty bodies for failures, generic 500 errors, and streaming errors without stable fields.
- **Time-varying quality**: Several models show lower TTFT and improved TPS in the last observed week vs. the first week after listing, indicating provider-side optimization post-launch.
- **Counterfactual routing**: Qwen3-32B routed cost = ¥4,577 vs. official baseline ¥7,355 (−37.8%). DeepSeek-V3.2 routed average TPS ~90% higher than official, especially for >1000-token outputs.
- **Task heterogeneity**: Different task categories occupy distinct token-length regions; news/retrieval is long-input/short-output, creative writing is long-input/long-output, requiring different optimization targets.

## Authors & Credibility

The paper comes from the **QingCheng.AI AIPing Team** (AI Ping is an LLM API routing/aggregation platform in China). The authors have direct access to production-scale API routing data, which gives them unique visibility into cross-provider behavior. However, this also creates a **conflict of interest**: AI Ping is itself a routing service, so findings that demonstrate routing value could be seen as self-serving. The authors acknowledge this explicitly and limit routing claims to measured counterfactual samples. The paper is categorized under cs.PF (Performance), which is appropriate for a measurement study. The methodology section is unusually careful about stating what the data does and does not support (e.g., avoiding causal claims, distinguishing descriptive vs. mechanistic interpretations).

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: High. While individual observations (providers differ in speed, prices are similar) are unsurprising in isolation, the systematic measurement framework — formalizing the service object, measuring across eight dimensions, quantifying heterogeneity with proper concentration statistics, and demonstrating routing value through counterfactuals — is a genuine contribution. This is the first paper to rigorously measure cross-provider hosted open-weight LLM API heterogeneity at this scale.

**Rigor**: Strong with transparent caveats. The authors carefully distinguish between checkpoint-level, variant-level, and family-level claims; explicitly state what the observational design cannot support; normalize request volumes to protect customer data; and provide a reproducibility checklist. The main weaknesses are: (1) the latency threshold for "slow response" is not public, preventing independent verification; (2) absolute traffic counts are withheld; (3) model identity is nominal rather than checkpoint-verified (providers may quantize, fine-tune, or modify models). The counterfactual routing comparisons are not randomized experiments — they cannot rule out confounding from request-mix or queue-state differences.

**Reproducibility**: Partial. The paper commits to releasing aggregate CSV tables, pricing snapshots, compatibility probe templates, and figure-reproduction scripts. However, raw request-level data is proprietary, absolute traffic counts are normalized away, and the operational latency threshold is unpublished. A synthetic replay trace is promised. The reproducibility checklist in Appendix D is unusually detailed and sets a high standard for future work.

**Significance**: High for practitioners. The paper provides actionable evidence that model name alone is an insufficient decision unit for production LLM deployments, and that intelligent routing has measurable economic value. The formalization of the service object and the decision-theoretic framing of provider selection are useful conceptual contributions. However, the results are specific to the Chinese market and the AI Ping user base — generalization to other markets or user populations is not established.

**Red flags**: The conflict of interest (authors affiliated with the routing platform being evaluated) is disclosed but inherent. The paper sometimes conflates "observed in the AI Ping sample" with broader ecosystem claims; the title's generality ("When Is the Same Model Not the Same Service?") implies universality that the data cannot fully support. The lack of checkpoint-level identity verification means some "same model" comparisons may actually be comparing different quantization levels or modified variants.

## Strengths

- First systematic, multi-dimensional measurement of cross-provider hosted LLM API heterogeneity using real production traffic.
- Exceptionally transparent about limitations: the paper is almost over-cautious in qualifying its claims.
- The service object formalization S = (v, p, π, W, C, L, R, t) provides a clean conceptual framework.
- The decision-theoretic view of provider selection (feasibility constraints + loss minimization) is practically useful.
- Rich empirical findings: concentration statistics, version persistence, protocol compatibility gaps, error semantics inconsistency, time-varying quality.
- The counterfactual routing experiments provide concrete economic evidence (37.8% cost savings, 90% throughput gain).

## Limitations

- **Sample bias**: Data comes from a single routing platform (AI Ping) serving primarily the Chinese market; results may not generalize globally.
- **No checkpoint verification**: Model identity is nominal — providers may serve quantized, fine-tuned, or otherwise modified variants. The paper cannot distinguish "same model, different infrastructure" from "different model, same name."
- **Observational design**: Counterfactual comparisons are not randomized; unobserved confounders (request mix, queue state, time-varying load) may bias routing estimates.
- **Incomplete artifact**: The operational latency threshold, absolute traffic counts, and raw timing distributions (p50/p90/p99) are not released, limiting independent verification of several key findings.
- **Task labels incomplete**: Task categories cover only a subset of traffic, and the task-conditioned analysis is therefore incomplete.
- **Temporal scope**: Q4 2025 is a single quarter; the ecosystem evolves rapidly and these snapshots may age quickly.

## Relevance

本文与LLM推理评估和系统可靠性高度相关。它提供了首个大规模实证证据，表明同一开源模型在不同提供商托管下的推理质量（延迟、吞吐、上下文长度）存在显著异质性，这对LLM推理评估的可复现性和生产部署的可靠性有直接启示。其决策论框架对理解和优化LLM推理服务的可靠性具有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the observed heterogeneity patterns change when checkpoint-level identity is verified via model equality testing (e.g., MMD-based tests)?
- Would the routing gains persist under a rigorous A/B test that controls for request mix, output quality, and tool-call conformance?
- How quickly do provider performance rankings decay — i.e., how often must routing measurements be refreshed to remain useful?
- Do the task-conditioned token-length regimes generalize across different user populations and geographic markets?
- What is the interaction between quantization level (which providers may vary silently) and the observed performance dispersion — can the measurement framework disentangle infrastructure effects from model-modification effects?
- How does the supply-adoption mismatch evolve over longer time horizons (6–12 months) as the open-weight ecosystem matures?
