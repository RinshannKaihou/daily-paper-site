> **一句话总结：** 本文利用 Lindeberg 交换原理，逐步将全连接深度神经网络中各层非高斯权重替换为匹配矩的高斯权重，首次在 2-Wasserstein 距离下给出了有限宽度网络输出到无限宽度高斯极限的定量收敛速率（约 $O(n^{-1/4})$），并证明了高斯偏置可大幅降低对激活函数的正则性要求。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proves the first quantitative universality result for deep fully-connected networks with general (non-Gaussian) weights in the 2-Wasserstein distance. By applying a layerwise Lindeberg exchange principle that exploits the recursive smoothing structure of DNNs, the authors establish a convergence rate of $O(n^{-1/4})$ to the infinite-width Gaussian process limit. With Gaussian biases, only $C^3$ regularity on the activation is needed; without biases, exponentially higher smoothness ($C^{3 \cdot 2^{L-1}}$) is required. The work also recovers optimal rates in total variation and $W_1$ under the biased setting.

## 核心贡献

- 首次在 **2-Wasserstein 距离**下建立了具有一般权重分布的全连接深度神经网络的定量普适性（universality）结果，此前的工作主要局限于 $W_1$ 或全变差距离。
- 提出了一种适用于深度神经网络的 **Lindeberg 交换原理**：逐层将非高斯权重替换为矩匹配的高斯权重，并将每一步的误差通过网络的递归平滑结构进行精细控制，克服了经典 Lindeberg 方法中维度依赖导致的发散问题。
- 证明了高斯偏置的引入可**大幅降低对激活函数的正则性要求**（从 $C^{3 \cdot 2^{L-1}}$ 降至 $C^3_b$），这是通过偏置带来的额外平滑效应实现的。
- 在带有高斯偏置的情况下，额外恢复了全变差和 $W_1$ 距离下的**最优收敛速率**。

## Key Contributions

- **First quantitative universality in $W_2$:** Establishes explicit bounds on the 2-Wasserstein distance between a finite-width DNN with general weights and its infinite-width Gaussian process limit — a stronger metric than the $W_1$ results in prior work (BR25, Cel26).
- **Lindeberg exchange for DNNs (Theorem 2.7):** A weak comparison principle where weights are successively replaced layer by layer. The key technical innovation is showing that Gaussian averaging at later layers provides sufficient smoothing to control third-derivative terms at earlier layers, preventing dimension-dependent blowup.
- **Two convergence theorems depending on bias structure:**
  - *Zero biases (Theorem 2.5):* Rate $O(n^{-1/4})$ in $W_2$, but requires $\sigma \in C^{3 \cdot 2^{L-1}}_b$ — exponential smoothness in depth $L$.
  - *Gaussian biases (Theorem 2.6):* Same $O(n^{-1/4})$ rate in $W_2$ with only $\sigma \in C^3_b$, because biases decouple the derivative recursion across layers.
- **Optimal $W_1$ and TV rates recovered (Corollary 2.8):** When Gaussian biases are present, the Lindeberg principle directly yields optimal rates in total variation and 1-Wasserstein distance via duality.

## Methodology

The paper considers fully connected DNNs of depth $L$ with hidden widths $n_1, \ldots, n_L \to \infty$. Weights on hidden layers follow a general distribution $\mu$ (zero mean, unit variance, finite third moment) scaled by $1/\sqrt{n_{\ell-1}}$; the first layer weights are Gaussian.

**Core technique — Layerwise Lindeberg switching:** Define a family of intermediate networks $z^{(L+1;L-K)}$ where the last $K+1$ layers have Gaussian weights and earlier layers retain the original distribution. The distance to the Gaussian limit decomposes via telescoping sum:

$$\mathbb{E}[F(z^{(L+1)})] - \mathbb{E}[F(Z^{(L+1)})] = \sum_{K} \left( \mathbb{E}[F(z^{(L+1;L-K)})] - \mathbb{E}[F(z^{(L+1;L-K-1)})] \right) + \text{Gaussian error}$$

**Key insight:** Naive Lindeberg gives $O(n_{L-K}^{3/2} / \sqrt{n_{L-K}}) = O(\sqrt{n_{L-K}})$ — diverging. The authors define recursively averaged test functions $F_{L,K}(x) = \mathbb{E}[F_{L,K-1}(G^{(L-K+1)} \sigma(x))]$ and prove that Gaussian smoothing at later layers controls higher-order derivatives of $F_{L,K}$, yielding the improved bound $O(1/\sqrt{n_{L-K-1}})$ per step.

For $W_2$ bounds, they combine: (1) Bonis's theorem for the last-layer replacement, (2) a conditionally-Gaussian $W_2$ comparison (Theorem 3.3 from CP25) for intermediate layers, and (3) existing Gaussian-to-Gaussian convergence results (FHM+25, Tre23).

## Results & Highlights

- **Zero-bias case:** $W_2(z^{(L+1)}(x), Z^{(L+1)}(x)) \leq C\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{n_L}} + \left(\sum_{k=1}^{L-1} \frac{1}{\sqrt{n_k}}\right)^{1/4}\right)$
- **Gaussian-bias case:** Same rate with $\sigma \in C^3_b$ only (e.g., tanh, sigmoid, arctan).
- **Optimal $W_1$ and TV rates** recovered under Gaussian biases via duality.
- Numerical simulations on $L=3$, sigmoid activation, Laplace-distributed weights confirm convergence behavior, with empirical slopes $\approx 0.47$–$0.50$ in $W_2$ consistent with the $n^{-1/4}$ prediction.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The main novelty is the combination of (a) quantitative universality in $W_2$ (prior universality work was $W_1$/TV), and (b) the recursive Lindeberg technique that exploits DNN smoothing structure. This is a genuine technical advance, though the overall proof strategy follows the well-trodden Lindeberg path.

**Rigor:** Very high. Complete proofs with explicit constants depending on $(L, n_{L+1}, \sigma)$. All assumptions are clearly stated. Numerical simulations support the theoretical rates.

**Reproducibility:** Good in principle — the assumptions are precise and the proofs are detailed. However, the numerical section lacks code availability, and reproducing the exact experiments would require non-trivial effort.

**Significance:** The $W_2$ result is mathematically meaningful (stronger metric than $W_1$), but the practical relevance to ML practitioners is limited — this is primarily a probability theory contribution. The exponential smoothness requirement in the zero-bias case ($C^{3 \cdot 2^{L-1}}$) is a notable limitation that may restrict applicability for deep networks.

**Red flags:**
- The zero-bias theorem requires $\sigma \in C^{3 \cdot 2^{L-1}}$, which grows exponentially with depth $L$ — for $L=10$, this requires $C^{1536}$ smoothness. This is acknowledged but somewhat understated.
- The first layer must be Gaussian (Assumption 2.2), so full universality does not hold at layer 1.
- The $O(n^{-1/4})$ rate is slower than the $O(n^{-1/2})$-type rates obtained by Cel26 in $W_1$, reflecting the inherent difficulty of the stronger metric.

## Strengths

- Clean problem formulation and honest positioning relative to prior work (BR25, Cel26).
- The observation that Gaussian biases dramatically reduce smoothness requirements is elegant and practically relevant.
- Complete, self-contained proofs with a clear proof strategy outlined in Section 3 before the technical details.
- Numerical experiments validate the theoretical convergence rates.

## Limitations

- Exponential smoothness requirement on $\sigma$ in the zero-bias case limits practical applicability for deep networks without biases.
- Only fully connected architectures are considered — extension to convolutional, residual, or attention-based networks is not discussed.
- The convergence rate $O(n^{-1/4})$ is suboptimal compared to $W_1$ rates; whether $W_2$ rates can be improved remains open.
- All results are for randomly initialized (untrained) networks. The paper notes AGT25 addresses trained shallow networks but does not extend to trained deep networks.

## Relevance

本文为理解随机初始化深度神经网络的数学结构提供了严格的概率论基础，与「LLM 隐藏状态与可解释性」中关于表征几何和极限行为的研究方向相关，但其直接面向的是理论分析而非实际训练或推理场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the exponential smoothness requirement $\sigma \in C^{3 \cdot 2^{L-1}}$ for zero-bias networks be improved, perhaps using Stein's method instead of Lindeberg?
- Do these universality results extend to architectures with skip connections (ResNets) or normalization layers, where the recursive structure is more complex?
- Is the $O(n^{-1/4})$ rate in $W_2$ tight, or could a different proof strategy achieve $O(n^{-1/2})$ as in the Gaussian-weight case?
- How do these bounds degrade when the weight distribution $\mu$ has only finite third moments but heavier tails (e.g., no fourth moment)?
