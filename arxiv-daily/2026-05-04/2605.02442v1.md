> **一句话总结：** 本文从评估视角出发，将LLM推理定义为自适应多步搜索过程，论证仅靠最终答案准确率无法衡量推理能力，主张以推理链的忠实性（faithfulness）和有效性（validity）作为评估推理的核心指标，并提出了分层的证据等级框架。

> **TL;DR:** This position paper argues that final-answer accuracy is an insufficient proxy for reasoning in language models. The authors define reasoning as adaptive, input-dependent multi-step search, show that single forward passes in scalable architectures (Transformers, SSMs) are structurally limited in realizing variable-depth computation, and propose evaluating reasoning through the faithfulness and validity of externalized reasoning traces. They provide a concrete evidence-tier framework (Levels 0-2) with portable metrics (SVR, VSR, IFR, AUC_any) for process-based evaluation, and call on researchers to treat trace quality as a first-class evaluation target rather than an interpretability afterthought.

## 核心贡献

- 将推理形式化为自适应搜索过程：推理需要根据输入选择中间步骤并在满足条件时停止，而非固定深度的计算。
- 区分了记忆化（memorization）、理解（comprehension）和推理（reasoning）三个能力层级，并说明数据污染会使得推理评估退化为更低层级的能力测试。
- 从计算复杂性理论出发，论证单次前向传播（Transformer/SSM）无法实现可变深度的自适应搜索，因此需要外部化推理链作为评估接口。
- 提出以忠实性（faithfulness）和有效性（validity）作为推理评估的两个核心目标，而非仅依赖最终答案准确率。
- 设计了分层的证据等级框架（Level 0-2），并提供了可移植的量化指标（SVR、VSR、IFR、AUC_any）和具体的评估报告模板。

## Key Contributions

- Formalize reasoning as adaptive search where both step selection and halting are input-dependent, distinguishing it from memorization and comprehension.
- Provide a complexity-theoretic argument that fixed-depth architectures (Transformers, Mamba-like SSMs) cannot realize variable-depth computation in a single forward pass, motivating intermediate decoding.
- Develop an evidence-tier framework (Level 0: outcome-only, Level 1: trace-present, Level 2: trace-verified) with concrete metrics for step validity rate (SVR), verified solution rate (VSR), intervention faithfulness rate (IFR), and anytime AUC.
- Argue against three alternative views: (1) reasoning can be measured internally, (2) externalized traces are too unfaithful to be useful, (3) reasoning requires world models — addressing each with nuanced counterarguments.
- Provide benchmark design recommendations including instance generators with held-out seeds, checker/verifier interfaces, and structured intermediate artifact formats.

## Methodology

This is a position/perspective paper rather than an empirical study. The methodology is primarily analytical and argumentative:

1. **Definitional framework:** The authors establish a three-tier taxonomy of model capabilities (memorization → comprehension → reasoning) grounded in classical philosophy (Aristotle, Descartes, Locke, Kant) and computational complexity theory.
2. **Complexity-theoretic argument:** They draw on circuit complexity results (Merrill & Sabharwal, 2023a,b; Merrill et al., 2022, 2024) to show that log-precision Transformers and state-space models are limited to TC^0 computation in a single forward pass, motivating externalized intermediate steps.
3. **Evidence-tier formalization:** They propose a hierarchical evaluation framework with increasingly stringent evidential requirements, and define portable metrics (SVR, VSR, IFR, AUC_any) that can be applied across benchmarks and architectures.
4. **Illustrative examples:** The paper includes extensive examples from MMLU, GSM-Symbolic, AIME, Humanity's Last Exam, LogiQA, and other benchmarks to illustrate the distinction between memorization, comprehension, and reasoning instances.

## Results & Highlights

- The paper demonstrates through concrete examples that many items in popular "reasoning" benchmarks (MMLU, TriviaQA, Natural Questions) can be solved via memorization or comprehension rather than genuine multi-step reasoning.
- The evidence-tier framework provides actionable guidance: Level 2 evaluation with SVR/VSR metrics can be applied to existing benchmarks (e.g., PRM800K for MATH) with minimal modification.
- The intervention faithfulness rate (IFR) metric offers a concrete operational test: if modifying intermediate steps predictably changes the final answer, the trace is faithful; if answers are insensitive, the trace is weak evidence.
- The anytime profile (AUC_any) provides a model-agnostic diagnostic for whether models allocate more computation to harder instances, which is a hallmark of genuine reasoning-as-search.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The core argument — that outcome-only metrics are insufficient for evaluating reasoning — is not new and has been made by others (Mondorf & Plank, 2024; several works on faithfulness). However, the systematic formalization via complexity theory, the clean three-tier taxonomy, and the concrete evidence-tier framework with portable metrics represent a meaningful synthesis and contribution.
- **Rigor:** The complexity-theoretic argument is sound but relies on established results from Merrill et al. The paper does not provide new empirical validation of its proposed metrics (SVR, VSR, IFR), which limits the practical assessment of whether these metrics are actually discriminative and cost-effective at scale.
- **Reproducibility:** The metrics and framework are clearly defined and could be implemented by other researchers. The appendix provides concrete reporting templates and benchmark design recommendations. However, the absence of code or empirical demonstrations is a gap.
- **Significance:** If adopted, the evidence-tier framework could meaningfully raise the standard for reasoning evaluations in the field. The paper is well-timed given the current proliferation of reasoning benchmarks and models with extended thinking traces.
- **Red flags:** The paper is primarily a position paper without empirical validation — the proposed metrics are plausible but untested. The complexity-theoretic arguments, while correct, are somewhat oversimplified (e.g., the assumption that all interesting reasoning falls outside TC^0). The faithfulness defense in Section 5.2 sometimes feels like motivated reasoning rather than balanced analysis.

## Strengths

- Provides a principled, well-grounded framework that connects classical philosophy, computational complexity, and practical evaluation methodology.
- The three-tier taxonomy (memorization/comprehension/reasoning) with contamination analysis is intuitive and operationally useful.
- Concrete, portable metrics (SVR, VSR, IFR, AUC_any) with clear formulas that can be adopted immediately.
- Excellent use of illustrative examples from real benchmarks to ground abstract arguments.
- Balanced engagement with alternative views, particularly the nuanced treatment of the faithfulness objection.

## Limitations

- No empirical validation of the proposed metrics — the paper does not demonstrate that SVR, VSR, or IFR actually discriminate between models with genuine vs. superficial reasoning.
- The complexity-theoretic arguments apply to single forward passes but do not fully account for the empirical behavior of models trained with chain-of-thought data or reinforcement learning.
- The framework may be difficult to apply to benchmarks where intermediate steps are not naturally decomposable (e.g., ARC-AGI grid generation).
- The paper does not address the practical cost of process-based evaluation, which could be substantial for large-scale benchmarks.
- The evidence-tier framework could create a false sense of precision — Level 2 metrics are only as good as the verifiers used, and rubric-based judging (the fallback) inherits its own biases.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do SVR, VSR, and IFR correlate across models of different scales and training regimes? Are they measuring the same underlying capability?
- Can the evidence-tier framework be adapted for multimodal reasoning benchmarks (e.g., MMMU-Pro) where intermediate steps include visual artifacts?
- What is the computational overhead of Level 2 evaluation compared to Level 0, and how does this scale with benchmark size?
- How robust are the proposed metrics to adversarial manipulation of reasoning traces?
- Does the framework apply to latent reasoning models (e.g., models that reason in continuous latent spaces without producing explicit intermediate steps)?
