> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 HeavySkill，将 LLM agent 编排框架中的"重度思考"抽象为"并行推理+序列审议"的两阶段内化技能，并通过大规模实验证明该方法在 STEM、编码等任务上持续优于多数投票等 Best-of-N 策略，且可通过 RLVR 进一步提升推理上限。

> **TL;DR:** HeavySkill reframes the complex orchestration of agentic harnesses (e.g., Claude Code, CodeX) into a minimal two-stage inner skill: parallel reasoning (spawn K independent trajectories) followed by sequential deliberation (a summary model synthesizes all trajectories). Across 14+ models (including GPT-5, Claude 4.5, DeepSeek R1, Kimi K2) and diverse benchmarks (AIME25, BeyondAIME, HMMT25, GPQA-Diamond, LiveCodeBench), HeavySkill's Heavy-Mean@K consistently outperforms majority voting (Vote@K) and mean performance (Mean@K), with frontier models approaching the theoretical Pass@K upper bound. The paper also shows RLVR can optimize heavy-thinking trajectories directly, gaining ~10% on HM@4, and provides a portable, human-readable skill document deployable in any agentic harness without code changes.

## 核心贡献

- 将复杂的 agent 编排框架抽象为"并行推理 + 序列审议"的两阶段"重度思考"技能，并证明该技能可作为模型内化的内在能力，而非依赖外部系统设计。
- 提出序列化记忆缓存（Serialized Memory Cache）机制，将并行推理轨迹拼接为审阅模型的输入，支持迭代审议以逐步优化推理结果。
- 首次对重度思考范式进行大规模系统性实证研究，涵盖 14+ 个模型和 STEM、编码、通用推理等多领域基准测试。
- 将重度思考工作流蒸馏为可移植的人类可读技能文档（HeavySkill.md），可注入任意支持技能加载的 agent 编排框架。
- 证明 RLVR 可直接优化重度思考轨迹，为自进化 LLM 提供新路径。

## Key Contributions

- **Conceptual reframing of agentic harnesses**: Abstracts complex multi-agent orchestration into a two-stage "heavy thinking" inner skill (parallel reasoning → sequential deliberation) that can be internalized within model parameters.
- **Serialized Memory Cache**: Introduces a pruning-and-shuffling mechanism to fit multiple long CoT trajectories into the deliberation model's context window, enabling iterative refinement across deliberation rounds.
- **First comprehensive empirical study**: Evaluates heavy thinking across 14+ models (GPT-5, Claude 4.5, DeepSeek R1-0528, Kimi K2, Qwen3, etc.) on AIME25, BeyondAIME, HMMT25, GPQA-Diamond, LiveCodeBench, IFEval, Arena-Hard, and IMO Answer Bench.
- **Portable readable skill**: Distills the framework into a plain-text skill document deployable in any harness (Claude Code, CodeX, Hermes) without code modifications.
- **RLVR integration**: Demonstrates that reinforcement learning from verifiable rewards can directly optimize heavy-thinking trajectories, achieving ~10% improvement on HM@4 in early training.

## Methodology

HeavySkill decomposes test-time reasoning into two stages:

1. **Parallel Reasoning**: Given a problem $q$, a model $\pi_\theta$ generates $K$ independent trajectories $\{y_1, \ldots, y_K\}$ with temperature sampling (T=1.0, top-p=0.95). Each trajectory is a complete reasoning chain.

2. **Sequential Deliberation**: A (potentially different) model $\pi_\phi$ receives a **serialized memory cache** $C(\{y_1, \ldots, y_K\})$ containing all pruned and shuffled trajectories, then generates $K^{(1)}$ summary responses. The cache prunes excessive thinking tokens to fit context limits and shuffles trajectory order to prevent positional bias. The deliberation prompt explicitly instructs the model to critically evaluate each trajectory rather than naively following the majority, and to re-reason independently if all trajectories appear incorrect.

3. **Iterative Deliberation** (optional): The summary outputs from round $t-1$ are concatenated with the original cache to form a new cache $C(x_c^{(t)})$ for round $t$, repeating up to $N$ times.

**Metrics introduced**: Heavy-Mean@K (HM@K) = average accuracy of deliberation outputs; Heavy-Pass@K (HP@K) = proportion where at least one deliberation output is correct. These are compared against Mean@K, Pass@K, and Vote@K.

**RLVR extension**: Uses VeRL framework with GSPO algorithm to optimize the deliberation model on heavy-thinking trajectories from queries with pass rates in [0, 0.625], showing that RL can improve both HM@K and HP@K.

## Results & Highlights

- **Consistent gains over voting**: HM@4 surpasses Vote@K across nearly all models and STEM benchmarks. For example, R1-Distill-Qwen-7B on BeyondAIME improves from V@16=56.7% to HM@4=63.1%; Qwen3-8B on GPQA-Diamond from V@16=73.3% to HM@4=83.3%.
- **Approaching Pass@K ceiling**: Frontier models (DeepSeek V3.2, GPT-5 Thinking, Kimi K2) achieve HM@4 within 1-3% of Pass@K, meaning deliberation nearly reaches the theoretical best-of-K upper bound.
- **Cross-model collaboration**: Fixing parallel reasoning with R1-Distill-Qwen-7B and using Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct for deliberation yields consistent gains, even though the latter has weaker standalone reasoning (12.8% on AIME25 vs. the former's stronger performance). Deliberation relies more on synthesis/analysis ability than raw reasoning power.
- **Iterative deliberation helps HM@K but hurts HP@K**: More iterations improve average deliberation quality but reduce the upper-bound potential, suggesting cumulative noise from earlier summaries constrains the refinement space.
- **Task-dependent efficacy**: Large gains on verifiable tasks (LiveCodeBench, IFEval); marginal or negative gains on subjective/preference tasks (Arena-Hard).
- **Trajectory selection matters**: Max-Answer-Num (consensus-based) significantly outperforms random, max-diversity, and max-length selection strategies.
- **RLVR gains**: ~10% improvement on HM@4 during early RL training, though K=16 trajectories suffer entropy collapse after ~100 steps due to sequence length limits.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The core idea (parallel generation + summary aggregation) is not new—Best-of-N with self-consistency voting, tree-of-thoughts, and concurrent reasoning all predate this work. The paper's contribution is primarily **empirical characterization** rather than methodological innovation. The conceptual reframing as an "inner skill" is useful but largely a packaging contribution.
- **Rigor**: The experimental scale is impressive (14+ models, 8+ benchmarks). However, the paper conflates several distinct concepts: the two-stage pipeline, the readable skill document, and RLVR optimization. The RLVR section (Appendix B) is preliminary, with only ~100 training steps reported and known instability issues left unresolved. The paper does not clearly isolate the contribution of the memory cache pruning strategy from the deliberation prompt itself.
- **Reproducibility**: The framework is training-free and uses standard API calls or open-weight models, which is good. The skill document is provided verbatim in the appendix. However, results on proprietary models (GPT-5, Claude 4.5) cannot be reproduced by independent researchers. Some experimental details are sparse (e.g., exact pruning ratios for the memory cache, specific hyperparameters for each model).
- **Significance**: The paper provides a valuable service by systematically benchmarking heavy thinking across many models, establishing clear performance hierarchies (HP@K ≥ HM@K ≥ Vote@K ≥ Mean@K). The finding that deliberation models can synthesize correct answers not present in any single trajectory (HP@K > P@K in some cases) is genuinely interesting and suggests the deliberation process does more than selection.
- **Red flags**: The paper cites itself as demonstrating "self-evolving LLMs" which overclaims given the preliminary RLVR results. The readability skill section reads partly as a product demo for Claude Code rather than a research contribution. Some tables are difficult to parse due to density.

## Strengths

- Unprecedented scale of empirical evaluation across 14+ models and 8+ benchmarks.
- Clear performance hierarchy established: HP@K ≥ HM@K ≥ Vote@K ≥ Mean@K.
- Demonstrates that deliberation can synthesize correct answers beyond any individual trajectory (HP@K > P@K).
- Training-free framework requiring no architectural modifications.
- Portable skill document that works across different agentic harnesses.
- Counter-intuitive finding that a weaker reasoning model (Qwen2.5-32B-Instruct) can serve as an effective deliberation model for a stronger reasoner's outputs.

## Limitations

- Significantly increased inference cost: generating K parallel trajectories + K^{(1)} deliberation outputs multiplies compute by roughly K + K^{(1)}.
- Gains are marginal or negative on subjective/preference-oriented tasks (Arena-Hard).
- Iterative deliberation shows diminishing returns and introduces cumulative noise, limiting practical iteration depth.
- The RLVR experiments are preliminary, with instability at K=16 and only short training runs reported.
- The memory cache pruning strategy may discard valuable reasoning content, and the optimal pruning ratio is not systematically studied.
- The paper does not compare against more sophisticated baselines like MCTS-based approaches or learned verifiers.

## Relevance

本文与 LLM 推理评估和测试时计算扩展（test-time scaling）直接相关。HeavySkill 提供了一种系统化的推理一致性评估框架（HM@K, HP@K 等指标），其大规模基准测试结果对理解不同模型的推理可靠性具有参考价值。此外，RLVR 优化重度思考轨迹的初步探索与训练稳定性研究有一定关联。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What is the compute-optimal allocation between parallel trajectory count (K) and deliberation rounds (N) for a fixed inference budget?
- Can the memory cache pruning strategy be learned or adaptively adjusted based on trajectory quality signals rather than fixed length-based truncation?
- How does heavy thinking interact with tool-use reliability—does incorrect tool output in one trajectory propagate errors through the deliberation stage?
- What causes the entropy collapse observed in RLVR training with K=16 trajectories, and can curriculum-based approaches or length-aware reward shaping mitigate it?
- Can the deliberation model's ability to synthesize correct answers beyond P@K be formalized theoretically, perhaps through a information-theoretic lens?
