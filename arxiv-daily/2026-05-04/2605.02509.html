MPCS: Neuroplastic Continual Learning via Multi-Component Plasticity and Topology-Aware EWC

arXiv: 2605.02509v1

Date: 2026-05-04

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02509

一句话总结： MPCS整合了11种生物启发的可塑性机制（神经发生、傅里叶编码、EWC等），通过15组消融实验在自建31任务基准上发现傅里叶编码和经验回放是最关键组件，而全局EWC反而有害；移除帕累托劣势组件后获得更高效且更准确的精简版本。
TL;DR: A single-author paper proposing MPCS, a neuroplastic continual learning architecture combining 11 mechanisms (neurogenesis, Fourier encoding, EWC, replay, Hebbian updates, etc.). Evaluated on a purpose-built 31-task benchmark (MEP-BENCH) using a 3D Pareto criterion (performance, representation diversity, gradient conflict). The key findings are counterintuitive: global EWC hurts performance (NES drops 4.2 points), Fourier input encoding is the single most critical component (removal tanks Perf by 30.7pp), and removing the two Pareto-dominated components (EWC + Hebbian) jointly yields a faster, more accurate system (MPCS_EFFICIENT: +0.6pp Perf at 4.7x lower compute). Notable limitations: single-author work, co-developed benchmark, small-scale single-hidden-layer networks only, high task similarity regime (~0.95 cosine similarity) may mask component contributions.

核心贡献

  • 提出MPCS，一个模块化神经可塑性持续学习架构，集成11种互补机制（任务驱动神经发生、傅里叶输入编码、EWC正则化、元回放、混合巩固、混合门控、突触修剪/再生、赫布更新、任务相似性路由、自适应增长控制、连续神经元重要性追踪），每个组件通过二进制开关独立控制。
  • 引入MEP-BENCH基准，包含31个任务横跨回归、分类、逻辑和混合域，采用三维帕累托评估准则（任务性能Perf、表征多样性RD、梯度冲突率GCR），而非单一准确率指标。
  • 完成15组消融实验（3种子×4赛道×2000 epochs，总计>7800 GPU分钟），系统揭示各组件贡献：傅里叶编码最关键（移除后Perf下降30.7pp），经验回放次之；全局EWC反而有害（NES下降4.2），拓扑局部EWC可部分恢复。
  • 证明帕累托劣势状态可预测联合可移除性：同时移除EWC和赫布更新得到MPCS_EFFICIENT，在Perf提升0.6pp的同时计算成本降低4.7倍（127分钟 vs 602分钟）。

Key Contributions

  • MPCS architecture: A modular neuroplastic network with 11 binary-flagged plasticity mechanisms, enabling systematic ablation. Flat variant expands a single hidden layer; depth variant inserts new layers (preliminary, with known bugs).
  • MEP-BENCH benchmark: 31 tasks across 4 tracks (regression, classification, logic, mixed) with a 3-objective Pareto gate (Perf, RD, GCR) that filters collapsing systems before Pareto analysis.
  • Comprehensive ablation: 15 configurations revealing that Fourier encoding is indispensable, replay is second-most important, global EWC is net-negative, and topology-local EWC partially recovers the deficit.
  • Pareto-guided compression: Demonstrates that Pareto-dominated components from single-ablations are jointly dispensable, yielding MPCS_EFFICIENT — faster and more accurate than the full system.

Methodology

MPCS is built around a single-hidden-layer network (with an optional depth variant) whose width grows via task-driven neurogenesis. Eleven binary flags control individual plasticity mechanisms:

  1. Fourier input encoding: Random Fourier features map inputs to a higher-dimensional space, smoothing the input manifold.
  2. Task-driven neurogenesis: EMA-based loss stagnation detection triggers allocation of new neuron blocks, each assigned to the current task's mask.
  3. Freeze mechanism + continuous importance: Post-task freezing with EMA-tracked neuron importance scores; gradients are zeroed for frozen neurons or scaled by importance.
  4. Topology-local EWC: Two variants restrict Fisher computation to task-owned neuron subspaces, preventing cross-task importance leakage that plagues global EWC.
  5. Meta-replay + mixed consolidation: Compressed statistical summaries per task, interleaved with new-task gradients during training.
  6. Hybrid gating: Learned context vectors route inputs through task-relevant neurons.
  7. Synapse pruning/regeneration: Low-importance weight removal with optional regeneration.
  8. Hebbian updates: Co-activation strengthening within task-specific neuron pairs.
  9. Task similarity routing: Routes inputs based on inter-task cosine similarity.
  10. Adaptive growth control: Dynamic thresholds for neurogenesis triggers.
  11. Continuous neuron importance tracking: Per-neuron EMA importance updated during forward passes.

Evaluation uses MEP-BENCH with a Normalized Efficiency Score (NES) computed from Pareto hypervolume contributions in (Perf, -RD, -GCR) space. The Pareto gate requires gate_pass_rate ≥ 0.75. All results averaged over 3 seeds, up to 2000 epochs per task with early stopping (patience 300). Run on a single RTX 4070 Laptop GPU.

Results & Highlights

  • full_mpcs: Perf=0.9027, NES=94.2, Time=602 min — on Pareto frontier but not the best NES system.
  • MPCS_EFFICIENT (EWC + Hebbian off): Perf=0.9086, NES=91.4, Time=127 min — Pareto frontier, best Perf overall, 4.7x faster than full_mpcs.
  • no_fourier: Perf=0.5962 (-30.7pp), 14% tasks fail gate — largest single-component degradation. Paradoxically reaches Pareto frontier via geometrically extreme low-RD/low-GCR position with impoverished representations.
  • EWC sign reversal: Global EWC degrades NES by 4.2 points. Topology-local EWC variants (ewc_topologie: NES=90.5; ewc_topology_pertask: NES=91.8) partially recover but don't eliminate the penalty. Monotone relationship: global EWC < topology EWC < no EWC.
  • Adaptive growth is critical: Without it, GCR explodes to 15.5 (vs 2.46 baseline) — unconstrained neurogenesis creates pathological gradient conflict.
  • baseline_minimal (only Fourier + neurogenesis): gate_pass_rate=0.0, completely fails — demonstrating that Fourier alone is insufficient without replay/importance mechanisms.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate. The individual mechanisms (EWC, replay, neurogenesis, Fourier features) are all well-established. The contribution is in the systematic combination and ablation methodology, plus the topology-local EWC variant and Pareto-guided compression insight.
  • Rigor: Mixed. 15 ablation configurations with 3 seeds is thorough. However, the depth variant has known bugs making its results unreliable. Bug F1b required a code correction mid-experiment. The Pareto analysis methodology is sound but the geometric extremity of no_fourier on the frontier reveals a weakness in pure Pareto dominance as an evaluation criterion.
  • Reproducibility: Partially reproducible. Binary flags and hyperparameters are specified, but no code repository URL is given. The MEP-BENCH benchmark is custom-built and co-designed with the architecture, raising concerns about external reproducibility.
  • Significance: Limited by the co-developed benchmark concern. The finding that global EWC is net-negative in high task-similarity regimes is interesting but may not generalize. The Pareto-guided compression idea is the most transferable contribution.
  • Red flags:
  • Architecture and benchmark were co-developed — the authors explicitly acknowledge this as a "calibration choice" but it fundamentally limits generalizability claims.
  • Single-author work with no institutional affiliation.
  • Only tested on synthetic/built-in tasks (Fourier sinusoids, Boolean logic, etc.), not on standard CL benchmarks (Split-CIFAR, Permuted-MNIST). Cross-benchmark validation is promised for a "v2 update."
  • Single-hidden-layer architecture limits relevance to modern deep networks.
  • High task similarity (~0.95 cosine) may mask the utility of components like EWC and Hebbian that could be important in more diverse task regimes.

Strengths

  • Exceptionally thorough ablation design: 15 configurations with 3 seeds each, systematically isolating every component.
  • Transparent about limitations, bugs, and preliminary results — the paper documents known failures rather than hiding them.
  • The counterintuitive EWC finding (regularization hurts) is carefully analyzed with a topology-local fix that provides mechanistic understanding.
  • The Pareto-guided compression idea — using single-ablation Pareto status to predict joint dispensability — is elegant and validated empirically.
  • Multi-objective evaluation (Perf + RD + GCR) is richer than single-metric CL evaluation.

Limitations

  • Benchmark co-development: MEP-BENCH was designed to evaluate MPCS's properties, creating a circular evaluation. No validation on established CL benchmarks (Permuted-MNIST, Split-CIFAR-100, etc.).
  • Scale: Single-hidden-layer networks on synthetic tasks. No evaluation on modern architectures (CNNs, Transformers) or real-world datasets.
  • Task similarity confound: All tasks have ~0.95 cosine similarity, making it unclear whether results generalize to more diverse task sequences where forgetting is severe.
  • Depth variant: Known bugs make depth-neurogenesis results unreliable — the paper reports them as preliminary only.
  • Single author, single GPU: Limited compute means limited scale and no multi-GPU/distributed validation.
  • No comparison to SOTA CL baselines: The paper does not compare against DER++, A-GEM, or other established methods, though this is planned for v2.

Relevance

这篇论文与持续学习中的灾难性遗忘和数值稳定性问题相关，但其核心关注点（多机制可塑性架构）与我的主要研究兴趣（LLM训练稳定性、隐状态可解释性、计算精度错误）交集有限。帕累托驱动的组件分析和拓扑局部EWC的失败模式分析对理解正则化方法在特定任务相似度下的行为有一定参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How do the findings change when task similarity drops below 0.5? Do EWC and Hebbian updates become beneficial in low-similarity regimes, as the authors hypothesize?
  • Can the Pareto-guided compression approach (identify dominated single-component ablations, remove jointly) transfer to other multi-mechanism systems or hyperparameter optimization settings?
  • What happens when MPCS is applied to standard CL benchmarks (Split-CIFAR-100, Permuted-MNIST) with established baselines (DER++, A-GEM, Progressive Networks)?
  • Is the Fourier encoding finding an artifact of the benchmark's Fourier-sinusoid regression track (B1), or does it generalize to tasks where inputs are already in a suitable representation space?
  • How does topology-local EWC compare to other Fisher-matrix correction approaches (e.g., online EWC, SI) in terms of the cross-task importance leakage identified here?
