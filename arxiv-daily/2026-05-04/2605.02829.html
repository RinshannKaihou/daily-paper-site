Compress Then Adapt? No, Do It Together via Task-aware Union of Subspaces

arXiv: 2605.02829v1

Date: 2026-05-04

https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02829

一句话总结： JACTUS 提出将模型压缩与下游任务适配统一为单一优化问题，通过构建预训练权重子空间与任务感知子空间的正交并集，在联合子空间内进行投影低秩近似和全局秩分配，仅训练紧凑核心矩阵，在 ViT 和 Llama2-7B 上以 80% 参数量超越 100% 参数的 LoRA/DoRA 等方法。
TL;DR: JACTUS unifies low-rank model compression and PEFT into a single framework by constructing an orthogonal union of pretrained weight subspaces and task-aware subspaces (estimated from input/gradient covariances via K-FAC). Within this joint subspace, it performs projected SVD, allocates ranks globally via a greedy marginal-gain strategy, and fine-tunes only a compact core matrix. At 80% retained parameters, it outperforms 100%-budget PEFT baselines (LoRA, DoRA, PiSSA, SVFT) on ViT-Base/Large, Swin-Base/Large (8 vision datasets), and Llama2-7B (CommonsenseQA, MetaMath), while also delivering 1.2–2× inference speedup.

核心贡献

  • 提出统一框架 JACTUS，将低秩压缩与参数高效微调合并为同一个优化问题，而非传统的"先压缩后微调"两阶段流水线。
  • 利用 K-FAC 启发的任务感知统计量（输入协方差 Cx 和预激活梯度协方差 Cg），构造预训练权重子空间与任务感知子空间的正交并集，解决了压缩子空间与下游目标不对齐的根本问题。
  • 设计了基于边际收益的全局秩分配算法，在固定参数预算下跨层贪心分配秩，使每个参数投入到收益最大的层和方向上。
  • 证明子空间不变性定理：固定联合子空间后，仅优化核心矩阵 S 等价于更新完整低秩因子，在不损失表达力的前提下大幅降低训练开销。
  • 在部署时无需保留原始冻结权重，训练后的模型即为可直接部署的低秩形式，同时实现推理加速（40% 参数时约 2×）。

Key Contributions

  • Introduces JACTUS, a framework that unifies low-rank compression and parameter-efficient fine-tuning into a single joint optimization, eliminating the subspace misalignment inherent in sequential compress-then-adapt pipelines.
  • Constructs a task-aware union of subspaces by orthogonally merging pretrained weight singular subspaces with task-sensitive subspaces derived from input covariance Cx and pre-activation gradient covariance Cg (K-FAC statistics estimated from ~1K calibration samples).
  • Proposes a cost-aware global rank allocator that greedily distributes a fixed parameter budget across layers based on marginal reconstruction gain per parameter, accounting for heterogeneous layer dimensions and spectral decay.
  • Proves a subspace invariance theorem (Theorem 1) showing that core-only optimization within fixed union subspaces is equivalent to updating full low-rank factor bases, enabling efficient fine-tuning of just a k_p × k_p matrix.
  • Achieves deployable low-rank models (no frozen weights at inference) with 1.2–2× inference speedup over dense baselines, while matching or exceeding 100%-budget PEFT accuracy at only 80% parameters.

Methodology

JACTUS operates in three stages:

1. Task-aware Union of Subspaces. For each linear layer y = Wx, the method estimates two second-order statistics from a small calibration set (~1024 samples): input covariance Cx = E[xx^T] and pre-activation gradient covariance Cg = E[δδ^T]. These capture the bilateral Fisher metric (K-FAC factors) for the downstream task. The pretrained weight subspace is obtained via top-kw singular vectors from SVD(W). The task subspace comes from top eigenvectors of Cg (output-side) and Cx (input-side). These are concatenated and orthogonalized via QR decomposition to form joint bases Q_L and Q_R. The weight matrix is then projected into this joint space: W_proj = Q_L^T W Q_R, and SVD is performed on the much smaller W_proj.

2. Cost-aware Global Rank Allocation. Given a total parameter budget B, JACTUS ranks all candidate rank increments across layers by their marginal gain per parameter cost (σ̂²_{p,k+1} / (d_out + d_in)). A greedy max-heap algorithm selects increments until the budget is exhausted, naturally directing capacity to layers with steeper spectral decay and larger dimensions (e.g., MLP Up/Down projections and deeper layers receive higher ranks).

3. Core-only Optimization. After fixing the union subspaces and allocated ranks, only the core matrix S (k_p × k_p) is trained. Theorem 1 guarantees this explores the same solution space as jointly updating U, S, V while their column/row spans remain in the fixed subspaces. The trained model USV^T is directly deployable without any frozen weights.

Key hyperparameters: energy thresholds α=0.95 (weight), β=γ=0.99 (task), ridge regularization ε=10^{-5}, calibration set size 1024, minimum rank k_min=32.

Results & Highlights

  • ViT-Base (80% params): 89.2% average across 8 datasets vs. DoRA 87.9%, PiSSA 88.1% (both at 100% params). At 60%: 87.2%, still above LoRA-100% (86.2%). At 40%: 81.3%.
  • ViT-Large (80%): 90.8% vs. best 100% PEFT 90.3% (PiSSA). Gap widens at tighter budgets.
  • Swin-Base/Large (80%): 89.6%/91.2% vs. best 100% PEFT 88.9%/90.8%.
  • Llama2-7B CommonsenseQA (80%): 80.9% average across 8 tasks, surpassing DoRA 79.7%, SVFT 78.5%, LoRA 77.6% (all at 100%). HellaSwag: 93.0% vs. DoRA 89.1%.
  • Llama2-7B MetaMath (80%): MATH 13.8%, GSM8K 62.5%, outperforming 100% PiSSA (7.5%/53.2%) and LoRA (5.9%/43.2%).
  • vs. two-stage baselines: At 80%, JACTUS outperforms SVD-LLM+LoRA by +3.3 (ViT-Base); the gap grows to +5.8 at 40%. On Llama2-7B at 60%: 75.5% vs. SVD-LLM+LoRA 68.0% and Dobi-SVD+LoRA 71.5%.
  • Inference speedup: 1.2–1.5× at 60% params, ~2× at 40% params on Llama2-7B.
  • Ablations: Joint subspace (75.5%) strictly dominates weight-only (69.5%) and task-only (49.8%). Layer-wise allocation (75.5%) outperforms uniform (71.3%). Calibration size 1024 is sufficient (512→73.8%, 2048→75.2%).
  • Training efficiency: Total training time (calibration + fine-tuning) is comparable to or slightly less than LoRA/DoRA; peak GPU memory is lower (e.g., Llama2-7B: 40.2G vs. 42.8G).

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: Moderate-high. The idea of joint compression and adaptation is not entirely new (e.g., PC-LoRA, LoRA-Prune address related settings), but JACTUS's specific mechanism — orthogonal union of K-FAC task subspaces with weight subspaces plus a global greedy rank allocator with a formal invariance theorem — is technically distinctive and well-motivated.
  • Rigor: High. The subspace invariance theorem is formally stated and proven in the appendix. Experiments are extensive (4 architectures, 8 vision benchmarks, 10 language benchmarks, 3 compression rates). Baselines are strong and comparisons are fair (matched training recipes). Standard deviation over 3 seeds is reported.
  • Reproducibility: Moderate. Code is promised but not yet available. Hyperparameters are thoroughly documented in supplementary tables. The method relies on specific energy thresholds (α, β, γ) and calibration procedures whose sensitivity is partially explored. The greedy rank allocator is clearly described in Algorithm 1.
  • Significance: High. The practical implication — deploying a compressed model that is also better adapted than uncompressed PEFT — is compelling for resource-constrained settings. The framework cleanly bridges two previously separate research threads (SVD compression and PEFT).
  • Red flags:
  • The 40% compression regime shows significant degradation (e.g., Llama2-7B CommonsenseQA drops from 80.9% to 58.9%), suggesting the method is most useful at moderate compression (60–80%).
  • Experiments are limited to Llama2-7B as the sole LLM; scaling to larger models (70B+) is not demonstrated.
  • The calibration set is drawn from the training split of the target task, which is a somewhat generous assumption — in true transfer settings the calibration distribution may differ.
  • The comparison with Dobi-SVD+LoRA uses "unremapping checkpoints" which may not represent Dobi-SVD's best configuration.

Strengths

  • Strong empirical results: JACTUS at 80% parameters consistently beats 100%-budget PEFT across all tested architectures and tasks.
  • Principled theoretical grounding with the subspace invariance theorem justifying core-only optimization.
  • The global rank allocator is a practical and well-motivated contribution that addresses a real limitation of per-layer independent budgeting.
  • Produces directly deployable low-rank models without requiring frozen original weights at inference.
  • Graceful degradation under increasing compression, with clear benefits over two-stage baselines that widen as budgets tighten.
  • Training-time efficiency: calibration overhead is small (~0.04h for ViT, ~0.25h for Llama2-7B) and largely offset by faster fine-tuning in compressed space.

Limitations

  • Only evaluated on Llama2-7B for language tasks; generalization to larger LLMs (Llama-3, Mistral, 70B+ models) and encoder-decoder architectures is unknown.
  • Calibration set is task-specific (from the training split); performance under distribution shift between calibration and target data is not explored.
  • The method requires a forward+backward pass through the model to collect statistics, which adds engineering complexity compared to training-free compression.
  • At aggressive compression (40%), performance degrades substantially, limiting applicability in extremely resource-constrained scenarios.
  • The energy thresholds α, β, γ are fixed across all experiments; their sensitivity and optimal selection are not deeply analyzed.
  • No comparison with quantization-based compression (e.g., GPTQ, AWQ), which is a complementary and widely used compression strategy.

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does JACTUS perform when the calibration set distribution differs significantly from the target task (e.g., using general web text for calibration on a specialized domain)?
  • Can the joint subspace construction be updated online during training rather than fixed at initialization, as the authors mention in their future work?
  • How does JACTUS interact with quantization methods (e.g., applying JACTUS on top of 4-bit quantized weights)?
  • What is the scaling behavior on models larger than 7B parameters — does the calibration overhead and subspace estimation remain tractable?
  • Could the marginal-gain rank allocator be extended to allocate across both ranks and bit-widths in a unified quantization-compression framework?
