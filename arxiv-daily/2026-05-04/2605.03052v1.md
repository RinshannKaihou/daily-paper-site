> **一句话总结：** 本文通过机械可解释性方法揭示LLM内部同时存在否定机制和捷径机制：早期层将"not"信息传递至概念位置，中层注意力构造否定表示（如将"not gas"映射为"solid"），但中后层的捷径注意力头会错误地偏向肯定答案，导致表面否定准确率低下；通过注意力汇聚消融可提升否定准确率最高17%绝对值。

> **TL;DR:** This ICML 2026 paper mechanistically dissects how LLMs process negation, finding that models internally construct negated representations (e.g., "not gas" → "solid") via mid-layer attention heads, but late-layer shortcut attention heads override this signal and bias toward affirmative answers. A simple Attention Sink ablation recovers up to 17% absolute negation accuracy improvement on Llama-3.1-8B and 46% relative improvement on Mistral-7B-v0.1. The construction mechanism is dominant over suppression, and shortcut heads are traced to early pre-training checkpoints.

## 核心贡献

- 发现LLM内部同时存在否定处理机制和捷径机制：虽然模型对否定具有内部敏感性（>95%），但中后层捷径注意力头会覆盖正确的否定信号，导致否定准确率仅约50%。
- 提出注意力汇聚（Attention Sink）消融方法，通过限制注意力头只能关注自身和首token来有效消融捷径模块，无需额外训练或统计量收集，即插即用即可大幅提升否定准确率。
- 系统揭示了否定的机械实现路径：早期层将"not"传递至"Y"的位置 → 中层注意力构造否定表示 Ȳ 并传递至输出位置 → 同时弱抑制"Y"的表示 → 后层MLP放大否定表示对应的正确答案。
- 首次在LLM中实证支持构造假说（Construction Hypothesis）优于抑制假说（Suppression Hypothesis）：中层注意力输出直接生成语义可解释的否定概念（如"not in Asia" → "America"），在80%以上的样本中观察到此现象。
- 通过OLMo2预训练检查点追踪捷径机制的涌现：否定准确率在训练早期先骤降后回升，表明捷径注意力头在早期预训练阶段就已形成。

## Key Contributions

- Demonstrate that negation and shortcut mechanisms co-exist in LLMs: models show >95% sensitivity to negation internally, yet achieve only ~50% negation accuracy due to late-layer shortcut attention heads overriding the correct negation signal.
- Introduce Attention Sink Ablation, a plug-and-play intervention that restricts attention heads to attend only to themselves and the first token, recovering up to 17% absolute negation accuracy improvement on Llama-3.1-8B and 46% relative improvement on Mistral-7B-v0.1 without any additional tuning.
- Provide the first complete mechanistic account of negation processing in LLMs: early layers move "not" to the position of "Y" → mid-layer attention constructs a negated representation Ȳ and relays it to the output position → the original concept "Y" is weakly suppressed → late-layer MLPs amplify the correct answer corresponding to Ȳ.
- Empirically establish that the Construction Hypothesis dominates over the Suppression Hypothesis: mid-layer attention outputs explicitly generate semantically interpretable negated concepts (e.g., "not gas" → "solid"), with evidence found in >80% of samples via LogitLens and LLM-based annotation.
- Trace the emergence of shortcut mechanisms to early pre-training using OLMo2 checkpoints, showing that negation accuracy first plummets and then partially recovers during training.

## Methodology

The authors study how base LLMs (Llama-3.1-8B, Mistral-7B-v0.1, Qwen2.5, Qwen3, Gemma-2, OLMo2) process prompts of the form "X that is not Y is Z" across a curated dataset of 648 entries (162 unique factual question pairs × 4 prompt templates).

**Sensitivity metric:** Defined as the probability that the logit difference between the correct and incorrect answer shifts in the expected direction when switching from a positive to a negative prompt. All models achieve >95% sensitivity despite ~50% negation accuracy.

**Attention Sink Ablation:** The authors' key methodological contribution. To ablate an attention head, they restrict it to attend only to the current token and the first token (the "sink"), effectively nullifying its learned function. Two variants: (1) Cumulative Attention Sink ablates all attention modules from layer *i* to the final layer, used to identify and mitigate shortcut heads; (2) Windowed Attention Sink ablates attention modules in a sliding window, used to localize causally important layers.

**Path Patching:** A modified version where attention outputs at target layers are replaced from a positive-prompt forward pass into a negative-prompt forward pass, while keeping attention patterns fixed. Measures the fraction of prompts where the model's preference flips, identifying causally important attention modules.

**PCA + LDA analysis:** Applied to hidden states at the position of "Y" to verify that positive and negative prompts are linearly separable in the residual stream, confirming that "not" information is moved to "Y" in early layers.

**LogitLens + LLM annotation:** LogitLens projects mid-layer attention outputs onto the vocabulary. An LLM annotator (GPT-based) labels whether top-promoted tokens relate to the concept of "not Y," providing quantitative evidence for the construction hypothesis.

**Contrastive Attribution with SAEs:** For Llama-3.1-8B (which has pre-trained SAEs from Llama Scope), the authors compute contrastive attribution scores for MLP outputs and SAE latents between positive/negative prompts, identifying critical MLPs and latent features that promote negated concepts.

**Pre-training analysis:** Using multiple OLMo2 checkpoints, the authors track how positive accuracy, negative accuracy, and sensitivity evolve over training steps to trace the emergence of shortcut mechanisms.

## Results & Highlights

- **Negation accuracy is poor across all models:** Negative accuracy ranges from 45.4% (Mistral-7B-v0.1) to 58.3% (Qwen2.5), while positive accuracy exceeds 90% for all models. Sensitivity to negation is consistently >95%.
- **Attention Sink ablation consistently improves negation accuracy:** Llama-3.1-8B improves from 50.5% to 67.8% (+17.3 absolute), Mistral-7B-v0.1 from 45.2% to 65.9% (+20.7 absolute, +46% relative). Shortcut modules reside in middle-to-late layers (best ablation layer > 0.5L for all models).
- **Construction dominates suppression:** For >80% of examples, LogitLens identifies tokens semantically related to "not Y" among top-promoted tokens at causally important attention layers. Suppression of "Y" is observed in only ~30% of examples.
- **Mid-layer attention (layer ~14 in Llama-3.1-8B) is causally important:** Both Path Patching and Attention Sink Ablation converge on the same mid-layer attention modules as critical for negation processing.
- **PCA reveals linear separability of negation:** By layer 4, the "not" direction can be decoded from hidden states with near-perfect cross-validated accuracy, confirming early-layer negation signal propagation.
- **SAE analysis identifies interpretable negation latents:** For example, latent 31222 at layer 21 of Llama-3.1-8B promotes "Win," "Windows," ".exe" on the prompt about operating systems that are not open source.
- **Shortcut mechanisms emerge early in pre-training:** OLMo2's negation accuracy first plummets at early training steps then partially recovers, while sensitivity remains high throughout, suggesting shortcut attention heads form at early checkpoints.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** This is the first work to provide a complete mechanistic account of negation processing in autoregressive LLMs. The finding that construction dominates suppression challenges the prevailing view from prior MI work (which emphasized negative mover heads and suppression). The coexistence of competing mechanisms (negation vs. shortcut) is a genuinely new conceptual contribution.

**Rigor (Moderate-High):** The paper uses multiple complementary methods (Path Patching, Attention Sink Ablation, LogitLens, PCA+LDA, SAE analysis) that converge on consistent conclusions. The dataset is carefully curated with template diversity and multiple safeguards against duplication. However, some limitations: (1) the dataset is small (648 entries across a narrow prompt template family "X that is not Y is Z"), raising generalization concerns; (2) the SAE analysis (Section 5.5) is limited to Llama-3.1-8B and only 8 of 20 samples yield interpretable SAEs; (3) the Attention Sink method, while effective, is a fairly blunt instrument that ablates entire attention modules rather than individual heads.

**Reproducibility (Moderate):** The dataset construction pipeline is described in detail. Models are all open-weight. However, the LLM-based annotation pipeline (using "openai/gpt-oss-120b") introduces a dependency on a specific external model. The SAE analysis depends on pre-trained SAEs from Llama Scope, which are publicly available.

**Significance (High):** The result that low black-box accuracy can mask capable internal mechanisms is important for the field of model auditing and safety evaluation. The Attention Sink method is a simple, practical intervention. The mechanistic pipeline itself (identify → ablate → trace → verify with SAEs) is a methodological template that could be applied to other linguistic phenomena.

**Red flags:** The prompt templates are highly structured and formulaic ("X that is not Y is Z"), which may not reflect how negation appears in natural text. The absolute negation accuracies after ablation (~65-68%) are still far from perfect, suggesting the construction mechanism alone is insufficient. The paper studies only base models (not instruction-tuned), leaving open the question of whether these mechanisms persist after fine-tuning.

## Strengths

- Provides the first comprehensive mechanistic decomposition of negation in LLMs, with a clear four-step processing pipeline.
- Introduces Attention Sink Ablation as a practical, no-tuning intervention for mitigating shortcut behavior.
- Uses multiple complementary interpretability methods (patching, LogitLens, PCA+LDA, SAEs) that converge on consistent findings.
- Tests across six model families, demonstrating the generality of the findings.
- The pre-training analysis with OLMo2 checkpoints provides unique developmental insight into when shortcut mechanisms emerge.
- The conceptual framework of "competing mechanisms" (negation vs. shortcut) is elegant and broadly applicable.

## Limitations

- The dataset is narrow: 648 entries across a single prompt schema ("X that is not Y is Z"), which may not generalize to the diverse ways negation appears in natural language (scoped negation, implicit negation, double negation, etc.).
- Only base models are studied; it is unclear whether the same mechanisms exist in instruction-tuned or RLHF-aligned models.
- The Attention Sink intervention, while improving negation accuracy, may degrade performance on other tasks (no analysis of side effects provided).
- SAE analysis is limited to Llama-3.1-8B and yields interpretable results for only 8 of 20 samples (40%), with many SAEs being uninterpretable or dominated by reconstruction error.
- Post-ablation negation accuracy (~65-68%) remains mediocre, suggesting the identified construction mechanism is either incomplete or there are additional confounding factors.
- The paper does not address whether the identified mechanisms scale to larger models (70B+) or differ across model sizes.

## Relevance

本文与LLM隐藏状态与可解释性研究方向高度相关。作者使用了PCA+LDA探针、LogitLens、SAE分析等工具来揭示注意力头和MLP层如何编码否定概念，直接涉及表示几何、电路分析和信息编码等核心主题。捷径注意力头在预训练中涌现的发现也与模型可靠性分析有关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the same construction-dominant and shortcut mechanisms exist in instruction-tuned models (e.g., Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct), or does alignment training modify or eliminate them?
- How does the Attention Sink ablation affect performance on downstream benchmarks and general language modeling quality? Is there a trade-off between negation accuracy and other capabilities?
- Can the construction mechanism be strengthened (e.g., via targeted fine-tuning or activation steering) rather than just removing the shortcut mechanism?
- Does the negation processing pipeline generalize to more complex forms of negation (scoped negation, implicit negation, litotes, double negation) and to negation in longer, more naturalistic contexts?
- Are the shortcut attention heads specific to negation, or do they reflect a more general phenomenon where late-layer attention exploits spurious co-occurrence statistics across many linguistic phenomena?
