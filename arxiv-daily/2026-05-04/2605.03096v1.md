> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 HyPA（混合提示算术）方法，通过将非线性任务提示向量与线性化混淆因子提示向量相结合进行提示算术运算，在仅调整软提示（参数高效）的前提下，有效缓解了文本分类中因混淆变量导致的分布偏移问题，在多个基准上实现了稳健性与性能之间更优的权衡。

> **TL;DR:** Proposes Hybrid Prompt Arithmetic (HyPA), a parameter-efficient post-hoc method that combines non-linear task prompt vectors with linearized confounder prompt vectors to mitigate spurious correlations under confounding shift in text classification. Across five benchmarks, HyPA consistently outperforms prompt-arithmetic baselines (PA and LinPA) on joint robustness-performance metrics (AUSC, Adjusted Integral), while analysis reveals it operates via dataset-dependent mixtures of blocking confounder influence on predictions and suppressing confounder signals in representations.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 HyPA（混合提示算术），一种两阶段框架：先用非线性微调获得任务提示向量，再在任务解附近进行线性化混淆因子微调，最后通过提示算术组合两者以去除虚假相关性。
- 首次系统性地研究软提示空间中的任务算术是否能有效缓解混淆偏移问题，为参数高效的分布偏移鲁棒性方法提供了实证基础。
- 通过隐藏表示分析（t-SNE 可视化、灵敏度度量、线性探针）揭示了 HyPA 消除混淆的机制因数据集而异：部分数据集表现为阻断混淆信号对预测的影响，部分则表现为在表示空间中抑制混淆信息。
- 提出了 AUSC 和 Adjusted Integral 等联合评估性能与鲁棒性的指标，更全面地衡量去混淆效果。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces HyPA, a two-phase prompt arithmetic framework that anchors non-linear task prompt tuning and then derives linearized confounder directions in the tangent space around the task solution, composing them to debias predictions.
- Provides the first systematic evaluation of whether task arithmetic in the soft prompt space can mitigate confounding shift, establishing empirical baselines for PA (non-linear only), LinPA (linearized only), and HyPA (hybrid).
- Conducts mechanistic analysis using hidden representation visualization (t-SNE), sensitivity metrics (projection of confounder direction onto task direction), and linear probing, revealing dataset-dependent debiasing mechanisms (blocking vs. removal of confounder signals).
- Proposes joint robustness-performance metrics (AUSC, Adjusted Integral) that capture both OOD performance and invariance to confounding shift in a single measure.

## Methodology

The method operates in three steps within the soft prompt space of a frozen language model:

1. **Non-linear task tuning (Phase 1):** Starting from initial soft prompt embeddings $P_0$, optimize a task-specific prompt $P_t$ on the full dataset $(X, Y)$ using standard non-linear prompt tuning. The task vector is $\tau_t = P_t - P_0$.

2. **Linearized confounder tuning (Phase 2):** Linearize the model around the task-specific weights $P_t$ using a first-order Taylor expansion. Then optimize a confounder task vector $\tau_c^{lin}$ on the same data but with the confounder label $Z$ as the target, within this linearized regime. This promotes disentanglement of confounder-specific directions.

3. **Prompt arithmetic composition:** Combine the two vectors: $P_{debias} = P_0 + \tau_t + \lambda \cdot \tau_c^{lin}$, where $\lambda$ controls the strength of confounder removal. The debiased prompt is plugged into the frozen base model for inference.

The key insight is that anchoring the linearized confounder tuning at the fine-tuned task solution (rather than the pretrained initialization) better preserves task performance while enabling effective confounder removal. Experiments use GPT-2 (124M) with prompt length 100 across five text classification benchmarks (SHAC, MIMIC, Hate Speech, Civil Comments, Amazon Reviews) with controlled confounding shift parameterized by $\alpha = P(Y=1|Z=1) / P(Y=1|Z=0)$.

## Results & Highlights

- HyPA consistently achieves the best or near-best AUSC and Adjusted Integral across all five datasets and both training shift settings ($\alpha_{train} = 0.2$ and $\alpha_{train} = 5.0$), outperforming both PA and LinPA baselines.
- On OOD AUPRC (extreme shift), HyPA achieves substantial improvements: e.g., on Hate Speech with $\alpha_{train}=0.2$, HyPA reaches 0.978 vs. PA's 0.957 and LinPA's 0.869.
- LinPA achieves flatter absolute slopes (more invariant) but at the cost of significantly lower predictive performance. HyPA strikes a better balance.
- Ablation confirms that anchoring $\tau_c^{lin}$ at the fine-tuned weights (rather than pretrained initialization) is critical for preserving task performance while improving robustness.
- Scaling experiments with Qwen3-0.6B and Qwen3-1.7B do not show clear improvement over GPT-2, potentially due to prompt signal attenuation in deeper architectures.
- Fairness metrics ($\Delta$FPR, $\Delta$TPR) show HyPA consistently reduces error disparities between confounder groups in OOD settings.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate. The hybrid combination of non-linear and linearized prompt tuning is a natural but non-obvious extension of task arithmetic to the prompt space. The mechanistic analysis of blocking vs. removal is a useful empirical contribution.
- **Rigor:** Experiments are well-controlled with fixed marginal distributions and systematic variation of $\alpha$. However, the restriction to binary $Y$ and binary $Z$ is a significant limitation that is not thoroughly discussed. The use of only GPT-2 (124M) as the primary model limits generalizability claims.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Algorithm is clearly specified (Algorithm 1), datasets are publicly available, and results are averaged over 5 random seeds with standard deviations reported. However, hyperparameter $\lambda$ selection uses worst-group validation AUPRC which requires confounder annotations at validation time.
- **Significance:** The work is relevant for practical deployment scenarios where confounding shift is a concern (e.g., clinical NLP across institutions). However, the gap between HyPA and PA is sometimes modest, and LinPA already provides strong invariance (albeit with lower performance).
- **Red flags:** The scaling experiments with larger models (Qwen3) show no clear benefit, which raises questions about whether the approach generalizes to modern LLMs. The prompt length is fixed at 100 across all models, which may be insufficient for larger architectures. Model selection under unknown test-time shift remains an open and acknowledged challenge.

## Strengths

- Clean problem formulation with controlled experimental setup isolating confounding shift from other distribution shift types.
- Mechanistic analysis goes beyond mere performance comparisons, providing insight into how debiasing operates in representation space.
- Parameter-efficient: only modifies soft prompts, leaving the frozen backbone untouched.
- Post-hoc method that does not require modifying the original training pipeline.
- Comprehensive evaluation with multiple metrics capturing different aspects of the robustness-performance trade-off.

## Limitations

- Restricted to binary classification and binary confounders; generalization to multi-class or continuous settings is unexplored.
- Primary results on GPT-2 (124M); scaling to larger models does not show clear improvements, possibly due to architectural depth issues.
- Requires confounder annotations during training and validation, which may not always be available.
- Lacks theoretical justification for why the hybrid combination works and when it might fail.
- Model selection for $\lambda$ relies on worst-group validation performance, which assumes access to confounder labels and may not align with true OOD robustness.
- Prompt length is not systematically scaled with model size.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does HyPA behave with continuous or multi-valued confounders, and can the linearization approach extend naturally to these settings?
- What is the theoretical relationship between the linearized confounder direction in prompt space and the true causal effect of the confounder on model predictions?
- Can adaptive prompt length (scaling with model size/depth) recover the debiasing effectiveness observed with GPT-2 when applied to larger models?
- How does HyPA compare to in-processing methods (e.g., distributionally robust optimization) when confounder annotations are available during training?
- Could the blocking vs. removal mechanism distinction be predicted a priori from dataset properties, enabling more targeted application of HyPA?
