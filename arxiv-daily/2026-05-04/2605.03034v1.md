> **一句话总结：** 本文提出了一种工具中介的LLM控制架构，通过有限动作目录约束LLM代理的输出，结合复合Lyapunov函数（Lean 4机器验证）为对抗性网络安全场景提供可控性、可观测性和输入-状态稳定性（ISS）的形式化保证；在282个真实企业攻击图上Claude Sonnet 4将攻击者收益降低59%且40次运行零方差。

> **TL;DR:** A tool-mediated architecture constrains LLM agents to compose deterministic tools (Stackelberg best-response, Bayesian observer, attack-graph primitives) and select from finite action catalogs, yielding a composite Lyapunov certificate (machine-checked in Lean 4) for closed-loop controllability, observability, and ISS robustness under best-responding adversaries. On 282 real enterprise attack graphs, all formal claims hold with margin. On paired offensive/defensive telemetry, a tool-mediated Claude Sonnet 4 controller reduces attacker game value by 59% vs. a greedy baseline with literally zero variance across 40 runs at four temperatures. A weaker Claude Haiku 4.5 stays catalog-bounded (architectural stability) but converges to suboptimal game values (capability-dependent performance), cleanly separating safety guarantees from decision quality.

## 核心贡献

- **工具中介架构：** LLM控制器不直接计算博弈值或枚举路径，而是调用确定性工具（Stackelberg最优响应、贝叶斯观测器更新、攻击图原语），仅从有限策略目录中选择动作，目录约束在工具输出接口强制执行，拒绝目录外提议为空操作。
- **Lean 4机器验证的稳定性证书：** 定义复合Lyapunov函数 V(k) = S(k) + λθ(k)（博弈值 + 观测不确定性），在Lean 4中证明（零sorry）闭环系统的可控性（单调递减）、ISS鲁棒性（对抗扰动下有界）和可观测性（非对称传感数据下的观测器收敛）。
- **控制器无关与对抗者无关的保证：** 两条推论将稳定性证书扩展到目录中的任意控制器和任意对抗者——稳定性是架构属性而非智能体属性。
- **大规模实证验证：** 282个真实企业攻击图（161个组织、25个行业）上所有声明成立且有余量；Claude Sonnet 4在配对攻防遥测数据上将攻击者收益降低59%，40次运行σ=0。
- **能力-稳定性分离：** Claude Haiku 4.5满足所有形式化保证（210次部署零目录外幻觉、ISS有界）但21/40次运行收敛到次优博弈值，证明架构稳定性独立于LLM能力。

## Key Contributions

- **Tool-mediated architecture:** LLM controllers compose deterministic tools (Stackelberg best-response, Bayesian observer updates, attack-graph primitives) and select from a finite action catalog enforced at the tool-output interface; off-catalog proposals are no-ops.
- **Machine-checked Lyapunov certificate:** A composite Lyapunov function V(k) = S(k) + λθ(k) is formalized and proved in Lean 4 (zero sorry) for three closed-loop properties: controllability (monotone decrease), ISS robustness (bounded under adversarial expansion), and observability (observer convergence from asymmetric sensor data).
- **Controller-agnostic and adversary-agnostic corollaries:** Two corollaries extend the stability certificate to any controller and any adversary drawn from the catalogs — stability is a property of the architecture, not the agent.
- **Empirical validation at scale:** All formal claims hold with margin on 282 real enterprise attack graphs (161 orgs, 25 industries); a tool-mediated Claude Sonnet 4 reduces attacker game value by 59% vs. greedy baseline with σ = 0 across 40 runs at four temperatures.
- **Capability-stability separation:** Claude Haiku 4.5 satisfies every formal guarantee (zero off-catalog hallucinations, ISS-bounded V(k)) yet 21/40 runs converge to suboptimal game values, cleanly separating the safety envelope (architecture) from decision quality (controller capability).

## Methodology

The paper casts autonomous cyber defense as a discrete-time nonlinear feedback control problem. The plant is a directed adversarial attack graph G(k) where nodes are hosts at attack-chain stages and edges are attacker techniques with per-edge payoff, block probability, detection probability, and posterior uncertainty. The defender (controller) acts as a Stackelberg leader, deploying EDR policies from a finite catalog C to maximize path-blocking; the attacker (disturbance) best-responds by injecting new edges from a finite technique catalog T. The defender maintains a belief graph Ĝ(k) with asymmetric visibility (≈40% dark edges absent from belief).

Key architectural components:

1. **Tool mediation:** The LLM never reads the graph, computes game values, or runs observer updates directly. It calls a suite of deterministic tools (e.g., `compute_v_after_deploy`, `simulate_round_ahead`, `get_critical_path`) and selects one catalog action per deployment slot. Catalog membership is enforced at the actuator interface.
2. **Bayesian observer:** A scalar Kalman filter per edge contracts uncertainty P_e toward ground truth on observed edges, with innovation tracking belief-truth mismatch.
3. **Composite Lyapunov function:** V(k) = S(k) + λθ(k), where S(k) is the attacker's expected payoff (game value) and θ(k) is mean per-edge posterior uncertainty. This decomposes into plant and estimator terms following Hayakawa et al.
4. **Double oracle expansion:** Both sides iteratively expand strategy sets from finite catalogs; termination is guaranteed by catalog finiteness and diminishing returns.

The Lean 4 formalization (~300 lines, five files) proves: (i) V(k) monotone decrease under defender-only control (Claim i); (ii) ISS bound V(k+1) ≤ V(k) − α_B(S(k)) + γ(|w(k)|) − λδE_obs(k) under adversarial disturbance (Claim ii); (iii) geometric decay of θ(k) with convergence to bounded neighborhood (Claim iii).

Experiments: Experiment 1 validates Claims (i)–(iii) on 282 real enterprise attack graphs with a deterministic greedy controller (564 runs). Experiment 2 validates Corollaries 1–2 on a GOAD Active Directory environment with paired Horizon3.ai NodeZero offensive telemetry and Microsoft Defender XDR defensive telemetry, comparing greedy, Claude Sonnet 4, and Claude Haiku 4.5 across four temperatures (80 total runs).

## Results & Highlights

- **Claim (i) Controllability:** S(k) non-increasing on 282/282 graphs (Wilson 95% CI [0.987, 1.000]). Mean S drops 19.7% (0.509 → 0.408) in 3.0 ± 0.1 rounds. Convergence is size-invariant across 276–3,599 edges.
- **Claim (ii) ISS Robustness:** All 890 single-round S spikes satisfy the ISS bound (max observed ∆S = 0.74 < γ = 1.0). Per-graph max excursion stays below 0.60 (40% margin).
- **Claim (iii) Observability:** Belief-truth game-value gap decays at 90% per-round contraction. Counterintuitively, adversarial pressure improves observability 4.7× (final gap median 0.07 adversarial vs. 0.33 defender-only).
- **Claude Sonnet 4:** All 40/40 runs converge to exactly S = 0.3427 with σ = 0.000 across four temperatures — literally zero variance. 59% game-value reduction vs. greedy (0.8367). Zero off-catalog hallucinations across 210 deployments.
- **Claude Haiku 4.5:** 19/40 runs match Sonnet 4's S = 0.3427; 21/40 stall at 0.85–0.90 (σ = 0.249). All runs stay catalog-bounded. Failure mode: Haiku correctly solves round-0 but fails to re-interrogate belief graph after adversary expansion (reasoning-depth limitation, not architectural failure).
- **Temperature invariance:** Kruskal-Wallis p = 0.17 on observer decay rates across temperatures for Sonnet 4; action-level variance (Jaccard 0.74–0.93) coexists with zero outcome-level variance.
- **Adversary diversity:** Adversary Jaccard only 0.17–0.44 across temperatures (high action diversity) but S never exceeds catalog-maximum payoff bound under either controller.

## Authors & Credibility

All authors are from Horizon3.ai, a commercial autonomous pentesting company. This is an industry research paper with strong practical motivation (SOC operational needs). The combination of formal verification (Lean 4), game theory, and real-world data from a commercial platform lends credibility. The use of their own proprietary attack-graph data (282 graphs from 161 customer organizations) is a significant strength for realism but limits independent reproducibility of Experiment 1. The paper is transparent about this limitation. Experiment 2 uses the publicly available GOAD environment and Microsoft Defender XDR, which is more reproducible. The Lean 4 proofs are provided as supplementary material. The total API cost ($93.90) and compute requirements (single laptop) are modest.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** High. This is the first mechanically-verified closed-loop stability certificate for a tool-mediated LLM controller, and the first to formalize ISS robustness under a best-responding adversarial agent (not just exogenous disturbance). The tool-mediated pattern itself is not new (ReAct, SayCan, G-CTR), but combining it with Lyapunov stability and catalog enforcement for formal guarantees is novel. The insight that "stability is a property of the loop, not of the agent" is a valuable architectural contribution.

**Rigor:** Strong formal verification in Lean 4 (zero sorry) with clear scope documentation (Table A1: what is proved vs. assumed). Statistical testing is thorough (Table A12: all 7 hypothesis tests with Benjamini-Hochberg correction). Assumptions A1–A5 are clearly stated and justified. The separation between controller-agnostic guarantees and capability-dependent performance is honestly reported.

**Reproducibility:** Mixed. Lean 4 proofs are fully reproducible (~10 min build). Experiment 2 (GOAD + Defender XDR) is partially reproducible given access to the commercial tools. Experiment 1 uses proprietary attack graphs that cannot be released — this is a significant reproducibility gap, though the paper is transparent about it and provides full construction methodology.

**Significance:** Potentially influential for the agentic AI safety community. The architectural pattern (constrain the environment, not the agent) is broadly applicable beyond cybersecurity. The capability-stability separation result is practically important: it tells practitioners that weaker models can be safely deployed within the architecture, trading off optimality for cost.

**Red flags:**
- Experiment 2 tests only a single graph (GOAD, 23 nodes, 44 edges). Generalization of the LLM controller results (59% reduction, zero variance) to larger/different environments is unproven.
- Only one LLM family (Claude) is tested. Cross-family generalization (GPT, Gemini, open-source) is unknown.
- Assumption A4 (no policy rollback) is operationally restrictive — SOCs do roll back policies.
- The deterministic greedy baseline is a weak comparator (marginal-value ranking); the paper does not compare against optimization baselines (e.g., MILP solvers) that could potentially achieve similar or better S values deterministically.
- The adversary's action space (66 techniques) and the catalog sizes are relatively small. Scalability to much larger catalogs is not tested.
- 282 graphs use synthetic sensor alerts at 60% coverage from one vendor — real EDR alert quality may differ significantly.

## Strengths

- First mechanically-verified closed-loop stability certificate for tool-mediated LLM control — a genuine formal methods contribution.
- Clean separation of architectural stability (guaranteed) from decision quality (capability-dependent), with clear empirical demonstration via Sonnet vs. Haiku contrast.
- The adversary-as-informant result (4.7× better observability under adversarial pressure) is counterintuitive and practically useful for purple teaming.
- Zero-variance convergence across temperatures for Sonnet 4 is striking and demonstrates the architecture's ability to decouple exploration from stability.
- Honest reporting of Haiku's failure mode (belief-graph integration failure) rather than hiding the weaker model's limitations.
- Comprehensive appendices with full statistical testing, hyperparameter justification, and tool inventories.

## Limitations

- Single graph in Experiment 2 limits generalization claims for the LLM controller results.
- Single LLM family (Claude); no evidence of cross-family transfer.
- Proprietary benchmark data (Experiment 1) cannot be independently verified.
- Assumption A4 (no rollback) restricts real-world applicability.
- The greedy baseline may underrepresent what deterministic optimization can achieve; a stronger baseline (e.g., MILP) could narrow the LLM's advantage.
- Scalability to very large catalogs or real-time deployment is not addressed.
- The paper does not evaluate end-to-end SOC workflow integration — the architecture is evaluated in isolation.

## Relevance

本文与LLM可靠性和形式化保证的研究方向高度相关。工具中介架构将LLM非确定性约束在有限动作空间内，并通过Lyapunov稳定性证书提供形式化闭环保证，这一思路对构建可靠的LLM代理系统具有直接参考价值。对抗性扰动下的ISS鲁棒性分析方法也可推广到其他需要形式化保证的ML系统。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does the architecture scale to catalogs with thousands of actions (e.g., full MITRE ATT&CK matrix with all sub-techniques)?
2. Can the Lyapunov certificate be extended to handle policy rollback (relaxing A4) while maintaining stability guarantees?
3. Does the zero-variance convergence result for Sonnet 4 generalize to other LLM families (GPT-4, Gemini, open-source models)?
4. What is the computational overhead of the catalog-enforcement actuator in a real-time SOC deployment?
5. Could the tool-mediated pattern be applied to other high-stakes domains (medical diagnosis, autonomous driving) with analogous Lyapunov certificates?
6. How sensitive are the ISS bounds to the quality of EDR telemetry (the 60% synthetic alert coverage may be optimistic)?
