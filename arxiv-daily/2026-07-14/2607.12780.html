Why this matters

Names and isolates a failure mechanism — autoregressive drift — relevant beyond quantum: any left-to-right decoder with unrecoverable discrete errors may share it.

Critical edge

A diagnosis, not a fix. On solved instances the model never discovers reductions beyond PyZX — it merely reproduces them, so the "surpass hand-coded rules" vision has no current evidence.

Methodology

A compact 44.8M-parameter encoder–decoder transformer with structured circuit tokenization replaces QASM text with a 102-token vocabulary (~100× smaller than BPE), where each token carries direct circuit semantics. The same architecture is run in two regimes: parameterized circuits (structure predicted, angles fit classically) and discrete Clifford+T (pure sequence-to-sequence).

STEP 01 · TOKENIZE
H(q₀)·CNOT(q₀,q₁)·T(q₂)
→ [BOS, H, Q₀, CX, Q₀, Q₁, T, Q₂, EOS] · 9 tokens for 3 gates
STEP 02 · DECODE
left-to-right autoregressive
greedy / beam (w=5) / temperature (τ=0.8, top-p=0.95, N up to 200) / hybrid
STEP 03 · VERIFY
process fidelity F > 0.999
O(4ⁿ) unitary compare — ~0.1ms at 6 qubits; generate-and-verify is cheap here
STEP 04 · RERANK
min T-count among equivalent
verifier-first reranking keeps lowest-cost circuit; angles via Nelder-Mead + (sinθ, cosθ) head

Training data is generated, not collected: random circuits + injected redundancy (HH=I, SS†=I, CNOT·CNOT=I) + phase decomposition, optimized by PyZX, yielding ~65% average T-count reduction (8.10 → 2.83).

Results & the length collapse

The two regimes diverge sharply. On parameterized circuits the model reaches median fidelity 1.000 (3–6 qubits) because angle post-processing smooths over small structural slips. On discrete Clifford+T there is no such gradient — early discrete errors are final, and exact equivalence collapses with target length.

03

Strengths & limitations

Strengths
  • Causal diagnosis, not just numbers — stratifies length vs qubit count, bimodal fidelity, position-accuracy curve, and first-error statistics to pin the mechanism.
  • Parameterized circuits as a control elegantly isolate why discrete errors are unrecoverable while continuous-angle slips are not.
  • Structured tokenization is simple and effective — ~100× smaller vocab, 100% structural validity, each token carries circuit semantics.
  • Five-intervention ablation with clean hypotheses; the ensemble result (errors correlate across architectures) is an insightful negative finding.
  • Verifier-first reranking exploits that O(4ⁿ) unitary compare (~0.1ms) is far cheaper than generation — generate-and-verify is natural here.
  • Intellectual honesty — explicitly a diagnostic, not a leaderboard entry; declines to compete with PyZX.
Limitations
  • Diagnosis without a fix — non-autoregressive decoding, discrete diffusion, MCTS, iterative refinement are all deferred to future work.
  • Never beats PyZX — on solved instances the model only reproduces PyZX output; no evidence yet of surpassing hand-coded rules.
  • Scale-capped — 2–6 qubits (unitary ≤64×64); explicit verification breaks past ~12 qubits, undermining generate-and-verify.
  • Narrow training distribution — random circuits with injected redundancy, optimized by PyZX; generalization to Qiskit/tket/MQT compiler output untested.
  • No head-to-head with genQC or RL methods (cited as incomparable due to differing gate sets and objectives).
  • Small eval set — 200 samples, Wilson interval ±6pp at 20%; length distribution may not match real workloads.
04

Open questions

Can non-autoregressive decoding actually escape the drift?

NAR, discrete diffusion, iterative refinement, or MCTS (à la AlphaTensor-Quantum) are proposed but untested — the most direct next step.

Where exactly is the 100K→200K cliff, and does 5×/10× data compress it further?

The 25× decay ratio (94% → 3.7%) suggests either an architectural length ceiling or a recoverable data problem — the threshold is unmapped.

Is the model learning equivalence rules, or just imitating PyZX's output distribution?

Testing on circuits where PyZX is known suboptimal would distinguish rule-learning from distribution-matching.

Does the peephole deployment path (≤14 gates, ~74% exact at 500K) pay off end-to-end?

Integrated into Qiskit/tket on real algorithm circuits — what is the net T-count reduction, and the cost of falling back to PyZX on failure?

Can the bimodal fidelity signal trigger in-decode retries?

The F≈0 vs F≈1 split might serve as an early drift detector; can position-1's 56% accuracy be lifted by input conditioning?

Is length just a proxy for algebraic complexity?

Highly symmetric circuits (more reduction opportunities) may be easier than sparse ones — the drift mechanism's tie to circuit structure is unexplored.
