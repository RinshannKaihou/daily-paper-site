Methodology — auditing the transport map's spectrum

Flow matching learns a velocity field vθ(x,t) whose ODE induces a flow map φt→1. Linearizing the perturbation dynamics gives a state-transition matrix Φ(1,t;xt). FTSS aggregates its full singular-value spectrum into one scalar.

Where classical finite-time Lyapunov exponents isolate a single dominant mode, FTSS sums the spectrum additively — so dimensional collapse cannot hide behind one outlier stretching direction. That distinction is exactly what makes it a clean memorization signal.

Estimator (Alg. 1) replaces the O(d) backward passes needed for a dense Jacobian with parallel forward finite-differences; Audit (Alg. 2) flags memorization when M = gmin/gminfull < τ.

Results — a two-phase trajectory with a clean bottleneck

Across MNIST / CIFAR-10 / CIFAR-100 / imagenette and three architectures, FTSS traces a characteristic shape: amplification early (g>1, paths pushed to target scale), compression late (g<1, paths converge). The bottleneck minimum gmin is the memorization fingerprint.

03

Strengths & Limitations, side by side

▲ Strengths

  • Elegant reframing — recasts memorization as an intrinsic geometric pathology of the transport map, not a statistical query.
  • Gradient-free estimator is the pragmatic centerpiece; removes the O(d) backward-pass wall blocking image-scale Jacobian diagnostics.
  • Honest math — Appendix B proves FTSS ≠ mean FTLE and gives a numerical case where they disagree sharply.
  • Information-theoretic meaning — Prop. 2.5 ties a drop in g(0) to a hard entropy cap on the generated distribution.

✖ Limitations

  • No quantitative detection metrics — no ROC / AUC / precision / recall vs. ground-truth memorized models; no MIA or nearest-neighbor comparison.
  • Reference-model dependency — gminfull needs a full-data model of the same architecture, a soft form of training-data access.
  • Narrow scale — only MNIST/CIFAR/imagenette with small nets; no latent diffusion, text-to-image, or >224px. imagenette capped at 5k samples.
  • τ uncalibrated — left free in (0,1); no selection rule, no sensitivity analysis on K / T / ε. No code link.
  • Collapse → memorization link argued mechanistically, never validated against an independent ground truth (e.g. per-sample replication rate).
04

Open questions for further work

Q1 · SCALE

Does the same bottleneck collapse appear in large latent flow-matching systems (FLUX, SD3)? How does latent-space FTSS map to pixel space?

Q2 · CORRELATION

Can M be calibrated against MIA success / nearest-neighbor replication / extraction hit-rate to match detector AUC?

Q3 · NO REFERENCE

Without a full-data model, can an analytic lower bound on gmin — or the Prop. 2.5 entropy bound — replace gminfull?

Q4 · MITIGATION

Is collapse reversible? Would a gmin-regularizer (volume-preserving / isospectral constraint) suppress memorization without hurting sample quality?

Q5 · GRANULARITY

Is FTSS sensitive to partial memorization (long-tail memorized, head generalizing), or only a whole-model binary verdict? Can it localize per-sample?

Q6 · ESTIMATOR

How does estimator variance scale at higher dimension? Does finite ε step bias systematically underestimate collapse under strongly nonlinear velocity fields?
