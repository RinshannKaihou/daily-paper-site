Calibrated pre-execution confidence lets an agent decide whether to act, abstain, or escalate — directly enabling safe deployment in irreversible, human-in-the-loop workflows.

01 / METHOD

How a frozen critic evolves without training

Problem framing

Before executing action aₜ in state sₜ, estimate whether it will be productive — without seeing the unobserved outcome.

żₜ = f(q, sₜ, h_<t, aₜ ; Bₜ)

This is distinct from response-level confidence: usefulness is decided by execution consequences, not the current context.

The CEB loop (Alg. 1)

1Online scoring. Composite key task ∥ state ∥ action retrieves top-k contrastive precedents (productive vs. unproductive) into the critic prompt; JSON score clipped to [0,1].
2Hindsight labeling. After a trajectory, a look-back LLM sees full (s,a,o,q), votes V=5 times (T=0.7), majority → pseudo-label. No ground truth needed.
3Record build. Each entry stores an agreement flag χ' — did the critic's raw call agree with hindsight? — exposing its own systematic over/under-estimation patterns.

Retrieval — sparse, not dense

1–2 gram TF-IDF cosine, not sentence embeddings. Productive vs. unproductive choices hinge on a few exact tokens; generic sentence vectors smooth away precisely those differences — and TF-IDF adds zero extra model/API dependency, keeping the design genuinely training-free (App. F).

Baseline diagnosis
Why token-uncertainty fails on agents

Token signals (PPL, Mean-Entropy) post AUC < 0.5 on web & shell — their ranking is inverted. Confidence drifts with trajectory depth. Verbalized-CoT stays nearly flat because it ignores the historical consequences of similar actions. CEB's contrastive precedents are what stabilize the signal.

03 / VERDICT

What holds up — and what doesn't

Strengths ▲ solid

  • Precise task definition. Step-level, pre-execution, no GT, frozen model — fills a real deployment gap.
  • Self-consistent mechanism. The bank = a prior over the critic's own bias; retrieval turns it into evolving context.
  • 9/9 dominance holds under both strict reference-match and lenient LLM-as-judge labels.
  • Deep diagnostics: reliability plots (App. E), retriever ablation (App. F), gating regen (App. G), token-cost (App. D).
  • Engineering-honest: default k=2, V=5 justified by cost analysis; TF-IDF = zero added dependency.

Limitations ◆ sharp

  • Same model labels & scores. Hindsight labeler shares the critic's backbone — pseudo-labels inherit its systematic errors.
  • Labels still need externals: reference match (conservative on exploration) or same-model judge (circularity risk).
  • No eviction / compaction. Fine at 200–1009 trajectories; unbounded streams will bloat retrieval & prompt length.
  • Replay, not closed-loop. Assumes abstained/regenerated steps are perfectly handled — unverified end-to-end.
  • Offline streaming only. No test under true online drift or persistent always-on deployment.
  • Cross-task transfer unproven. Task-keyed retrieval may over-isolate shared patterns (web→mobile).
04 / OPEN

Questions worth chasing

Q01
Does an independent hindsight labeler (stronger or weaker) change the calibration gain? Is there a coupling threshold?
Q02
What happens when low-confidence feeds a real replanning pipeline — new failure modes, latency trade-offs?
Q03
At 10k+ trajectories, what eviction or clustering keeps retrieval sharp? Is there a saturation point?
Q04
Can web-navigation experience transfer to mobile GUI scoring, or does the task key over-isolate?
Q05
Under distribution drift, do stale precedents cause systematic miscalibration? Do we need temporal weighting?
Q06
With a few labeled steps, can CEB's precedents augment a process reward model rather than replace it?
Q07
Could a task-tuned retriever beat TF-IDF, despite generic sentence vectors losing (App. F)?
Q08
Can multiple agents share one bank — is cross-agent experience isomorphic, or just noise?
