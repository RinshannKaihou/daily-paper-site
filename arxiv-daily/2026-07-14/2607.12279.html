Mechanistic Interpretability · Circuits

A trio of layer-18 attention heads forms a “countdown subcircuit” reused to track length across tasks — and models.

A Shared Subcircuit Lets LLMs Count Down Across Tasks  ·  arXiv:2607.12279

Jacob Dunefsky (Yale) · Wes Gurnee (Anthropic) · Emmanuel Ameisen (Anthropic)

Key Metric
0.93–0.97
r² · length correspondences
Linearity of countdown-head KV activations across prompt types — words ↔ base64 ↔ DNA stored essentially the same way.
Why This Matters

A reusable paradigm: reverse-engineer one circuit, then mine its generality across tasks and models — concrete evidence for shared computational motifs in LLMs.

Critical Edge

Cross-model “universality” rests on geometric resemblance to Claude 3.5 Haiku, not causal replication inside Claude. In-the-wild uses are illustrative case studies — no precision, recall, or coverage statistics.

01 Methodology

01

Controlled task + causal patching

Matched prompt pairs (same target word, same first n−1 words, different target length). Residual-stream patching localizes length readout to layer 18 at the length-unit token; three heads — L18H24 / H28 / H30 — ramp smoothly toward the target.

02

Implicit-length mechanism

“Repeated string” prompts with delimiters (backtick base64, 5'…3' DNA). Patching the ending-delimiter activations alone flips the model’s predicted next-string length toward the source — delimiters carry causally sufficient implicit length.

03

Unsupervised discovery

Distance-to-end probes built from countdown-head outputs, scanned over a 10k-sample Pile subset and 5k-conversation LMSYS-1M subset. High-activation chunks auto-labeled, then manually curated.

Strengths

  • Clean causal localization with matched-prefix control ruling out trivial length confounds.
  • Delimiter-patching framed against explicit roofline + baseline — causal sufficiency is convincing.
  • Quantitative geometry (r², diagonal + ringing structure), not hand-wavy manifold claims.
  • Broad, surprising in-the-wild set (tweets, SHA-256, haikus, ASCII) makes universality vivid.
  • Honest positioning vs concurrent work (Merzouk et al., 2026); bottom-up recipe is transferable.

Limitations

  • Single model under causal study; cross-model claim relies on geometric comparison, not Claude intervention.
  • Probe threshold (1.0) and example selection are heuristic and manually curated; no precision/recall.
  • In-the-wild tasks are qualitative case studies, not a systematic census of where the circuit fires.
  • Behavioral accuracy degrades beyond ~11 words — circuit analyzed in a regime of moderate competence.
  • No code release referenced; auto-labeling depends on a proprietary frontier LLM.

03 Questions for Further Exploration

Q1 · CAUSAL

Can universality be strengthened by causally replicating the circuit inside Claude 3.5 Haiku or other open models (Qwen, Mistral), rather than via geometric resemblance?

Q2 · COVERAGE

What is the aggregate precision/recall — what fraction of naturally-occurring length-tracking does this circuit account for, and how often does it fire spuriously?

Q3 · SCOPE

Do the three heads generalize to multilingual settings and to length units beyond tokens/words — byte-level or syllable-level?

Q4 · CONTROL

Can the circuit be steered — editing key/query manifolds to force longer or shorter outputs — yielding a controllable length knob?
