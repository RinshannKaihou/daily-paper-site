01

Methodology

dual-granularity twins · PCAP checkpointing

The twin architecture

Accelerators live in the PL; an AXI-GPIO monitor flags anomalous outputs. The PS hosts two twins and the checkpoint policy. State is read/written via PCAP, using an *.ll map to read back only active logic into DDR — zero reserved fabric.

Twin #1 · Rabbit
fast / coarse
C/C++ behavioral model. Recomputes at checkpoints, compares |O_R − O_PL| < ε. Continuous duty is too costly → latency-aware wake.
Twin #2 · Tortoise
slow / exact
Yosys CXXRTL cycle-accurate gate-level twin, normally dormant. On Rabbit alert, replays from last checkpoint and emits fault-free state.
PL output→ Rabbit detects→ load checkpoint→ Tortoise replay→ PCAP write-back

Checkpoint selection. Literature-driven layer sensitivity: early conv layers tend to mask; deep dense/logit layers most decision-changing; high-magnitude MACs and MSBs dominate. For LeNet-5/CIFAR-10, ranking dense-layer MACs by |x·w| shows dense6 top-30%, dense7 top-~17%, dense8 top-~18% already cover all observed misclassifications — ≈5% of all MACs.

Real-time budget & safe recovery

Per-layer window math (dense). PL ≈ 43.54 μs/layer (~10.62 ns/MAC); PS ≈ 0.14 μs/MAC. So in one layer window the PS can online-verify CMAC ≈ 311 MACs (~7.6% of 4096). Full audit would stall PL ~0.53 ms; the top-30% cut (1228 MACs) drops the isolation window to ~170 μs (~3× better).

Fault injection. Readback-level: after PCAP readback and before comparison, selected bits are XOR-ed to emulate transient flips — a methodological convenience; real online faults hit the same checkpoint-and-compare pipeline.

Safe write-back (Sec. V-D/E). The clock is paused only at a PS–PL handshake quiescent point (driven by the slowest clock) to avoid torn state. Updates land only on target config frames/bit positions fixed by the offline *.ll map — unrelated LUTs/routing untouched. The map is bound to the final placed-and-routed bitstream: any RTL/synth/tool change forces regeneration.

Strengths & limitations

side-by-side read

Strengths

  • Conceptually elegant. The fable-coded "Rabbit & Tortoise" pair decouples fast detection from precise recovery, dodging continuous DMR/TMR redundancy.
  • Zero PL area for state capture. PCAP + *.ll reads back only active regions — no reserved fabric, unlike StateMover/Zoomie.
  • Honest real-time arithmetic. Per-MAC PL/PS delays, verifiable MAC counts, isolation windows all derived explicitly — not a "low overhead" slogan.
  • Broad benchmarks. 11 accelerators spanning crypto, DSP and ML, plus a LeNet-5 sensitivity case, argue portability.
  • Recovery engineering is solid. Handshake-based safe sync points, targeted config frames only, no torn states.

Limitations

  • PS idle-cycle assumption. A busy PS shrinks the online-verifiable share and inflates detection latency — admitted, not quantified.
  • Tortoise is 2–3 orders slower. Worst-case recovery = checkpoint spacing Δ × S_Tortoise; end-to-end budget for large Δ + complex cores is only hand-waved as "bounded by checkpoint spacing."
  • Coverage is empirical & workload-specific. The top-30% threshold comes from one LeNet-5/dense/multi-bit study; Transformers, deep CNNs, and crypto fault-coverage are missing or "space-constrained."
  • Deterministic-replay precondition. Recovery correctness assumes no untracked nondeterminism — async software, external streams, out-of-window timing effects.
  • *.ll bound to the bitstream. Any RTL/synth/P&R/tool-version change forces remapping — a hidden CI/CD maintenance tax.
  • Transient faults only. Permanent faults and BRAM faults (already ECC-covered, explicitly out of scope) aren't handled; no formal end-to-end coverage guarantee.

Open questions

for further exploration
Under sustained PS load (RTOS + multitasking), how does the Rabbit's verifiable share and detection latency decay? Is there a PS-utilization vs. detection-latency curve defining EIR's comfort zone?
At worst-case Δ (spaced out to cut steady-state checkpoint cost), does Tortoise recovery still meet the edge-AI control-loop deadline? Is there an analytic or adaptive Δ policy?
Does top-30% hold for Transformers, deeper CNNs, or adversarial weight perturbations? Is a model-agnostic adaptive selector preferable to offline profiling?
Can EIR extend to permanent faults (aging, electromigration) — Tortoise not just replaying state but driving defect remapping alongside scrubbing/partial reconfiguration?
Facing nondeterminism (async interrupts, streaming inputs), can a deterministic-replay contract or hardware-assisted execution log widen the correct-recovery premise?
Can the twin run in reverse for security — e.g. detecting malicious bitstream tamper or Trojan activation — with Rabbit as a trusted reference anchor?
