01 / METHOD The battery no embeddings · no judge · no labels

31 frozen single-turn prompts — "Name a[n] XX. Reply with one word only." — asked 4× each of 44 models, no system prompt, temperature 1.0.

The datum is the discrete choice, not phrasing. That makes the metric verbosity-immune and sidesteps the form/content conflation that plagues diversity metrics. Every reply is reduced to its final alphabetic word, lowercased; a junk guard catches chat-template artifacts and >15-word replies. 5,439 / 5,456 cells (99.7%) yield a valid answer.

"Consulting three assistants ≈ drawing three samples from nearly the same distribution."

  • 44models 12+ labs, via OpenRouter

// answer-choice surprisal
s(a) = −log₂ [ (countF(a)+1) / (|F|+Vc) ]
For model m, category c, F = multiset of all other models' answers. Headline score = mean of s(a) over a model's valid answers — measured in bits, informally the "Mustard Quotient." Three companions separate ways of being different: modal avoidance, novel rate, self-distinctness.

03 / ASSESSMENT Strengths & limitations side by side

Strengths

  • Mechanical reproducibility. Exact-match on normalized tokens — any reader can recompute every number from public transcripts, no model in the loop.
  • Cheap cadence. ~$1/model makes it runnable on every release, filling the longitudinal-measurement gap nobody else filled.
  • Per-model scorecard yields rankings, not just population claims — enabling the within-lab dissociations (Fable vs Sonnet; GPT-5.6 tier spread).
  • Honest scope. A thorough §6 limitations section; hedges placed inline where claims are made.
  • Memorable findings — runner-up consensus, the "frictionless not frequent" mode, the DeepSeek natural experiment — build a coherent argument.

Limitations

  • Single snapshot, single channel. All data from one July 2026 window via OpenRouter; routing not always pinned.
  • Sampling opacity. Temperature 1.0 isn't honored uniformly; self-distinctness is a property of the model-as-served.
  • Panel relativity. Bit values are relative to this 44-model field — only rankings and pooled facts "travel."
  • Confounded roster. Cannot separate model size from generation, nor post-training intensity from synthetic-data usage.
  • English-only construct. Prototypes are culturally situated; whether oak/serendipity are data or model properties is untested.
  • Survivorship. Cannot measure the most heirloomed models — they're already unservable, itself an argument to run batteries at launch.
The DeepSeek v3-0324 → v3.2 gap (same base, different post-training) moves in the direction and on the exact quantity the mode-collapse literature predicts — but the roster cannot isolate post-training intensity from synthetic-data use.
04 / OPEN Questions for further exploration

Will the reversal persist?

Rerun the frozen battery each release — does GPT-5.6 / Fable 5's divergence uptick mark diversity-aware post-training entering production?

Base vs instruct, same weights

Promote the DeepSeek natural experiment to a designed one: localize where in post-training the collapse happens.

Corpus-frequency null

Quantify, per category, whether high-frequency-but-near-absent words (orange, tomato) cluster on homonymy/class ambiguity.

Read logits directly

For open weights, read answer distributions from output logits instead of 4-sample estimates — reveal tails latent but unretrieved.

Cross-language battery

Are oak / serendipity properties of English data or of the models? Do Chinese models land on different prototypes (松树, 苹果)?

Conformity ≈ sycophancy?

Run an "implanted-position" battery on the same models — do answer-choice conformity and sycophancy share a mechanism?

Why the runner-up consensus?

Second-prototype effect after sorting, or systematic post-training preference for the "next-typical"?

Multi-turn & tone

Extend to discourse — can it explain tone-driven "heirloom" attachment this instrument explicitly cannot measure?
