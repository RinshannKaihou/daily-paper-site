Why This Matters

A reproducible, HRRR-calibrated robustness stress-test protocol for time-series models — directly informing renewable grid forecasting and edge-side PV deployment selection.

Critical Edge

The perturbation family omits spatial correlation, regime-switching, and cross-variable dependency — and the feature-substitution headline rests on a single representative case rather than population-level magnitude.

01

Methodology

Digital Twin

Virtual PV via pvlib

Fixed-tilt south-facing, 1 kW DC, SAPM cell temp — isolates meteorological uncertainty from plant confounders (clipping, soiling, drift).

Error Injection

HRRR-calibrated perturbation

GHI noise σ_m modulated by clearness, AR(1) temporal persistence, DNI/DHI rebuilt via Erbs to keep the radiation field physically consistent.

Coverage

5 U.S. climate zones

Arid, Humid Subtropical, Humid Continental, Marine, High Plains — 20 sites/zone, 72 h history + 24 h future, hourly.

Models

6 forecasters, Direct Multi-Step

PatchTST, GRU, N-HITS, MLP, TCN (MIMO) vs LightGBM (24 per-horizon tabular regressors). Metrics: NMAE, NRMSE, clear-sky-normalized RE.

03

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Physically constrained, HRRR-calibrated error injection capturing heteroscedasticity, autocorrelation, and radiation consistency — far more realistic than i.i.d. Gaussian.
  • Clean isolation of meteorological uncertainty from plant confounders via the pvlib virtual-plant digital twin.
  • Mechanistic explanation of spatial heterogeneity (sky-state jump intensity, clearness persistence), not just statistical description.
  • Dual-grain interpretability (SHAP for variables, IG for temporal field) with population-level Wilcoxon validation.
  • Multi-objective Pareto (accuracy / robustness / CPU latency) translating into deployable model-selection guidance.

Limitations

  • Perturbation family omits spatial correlation, regime-switching, and full multivariate dependency among NWP errors.
  • Historical observation window assumed clean — sensor drift, sync errors, missing data untested.
  • Virtual PV (1 kW, SAPM) cannot capture utility-scale effects (clipping, soiling, dispatch); field validation deferred.
  • Single chronological split (2018–2024) does not establish generalization across strong interannual variability.
  • LightGBM-vs-DL is matched-information but not identical-representation — not a strict architecture-only ablation.
04

Open Questions

Q1

Under full joint multivariate NWP errors (wind, cloud, precip, spatial grid), does the feature-substitution path and robustness ranking survive?

Q2

If the history window itself suffers drift, missing values, or time misalignment, does backward-reliance compensation still hold — or amplify?

Q3

On real utility-scale plants (with clipping, soiling, curtailment), do the inter-model robustness gaps shrink or persist?

Q4

Can physics-informed losses or embedding Erbs decomposition into the network push the PatchTST/GRU Pareto frontier outward?

Q5

Can adversarial / curriculum training teach recurrence and attention to resist sustained-bias (high-ρ) disruption?

Q6

Do Z1/Z5 top-1/top-2 model rotations under local ρ sweeps imply these zones need online adaptive model selection?
