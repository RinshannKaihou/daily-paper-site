01

Methodology

Success-only one-class learning over LLM latent dynamics.

Reframe failure attribution as one-class learning in the LLM's representation space: learn what "success" looks like as a continuous latent path, then flag per-step deviations on failed runs.

1

Encode steps → latent path

Per step, mean-pool layer-ℓ token activations from the generator LLM (Qwen3.5-27B, layer 64), PCA to d_h=64. The query + step embeddings are treated as irregularly-sampled observations of one continuous latent path.

2

Model success dynamics with a Neural CDE

A cubic spline builds a control path X(u) whose derivative continuously modulates the latent state (Riemann–Stieltjes integration). Unlike Neural ODEs — driven only by the initial condition — the CDE stays sensitive to each local step, which is what step-level localization needs.

3

Gated control path (novel)

A gate q(·)∈[0,1] reweights the spline derivative dX̃/du = q ⊙ dX/du: pass-through in-distribution, suppress OOD derivatives out-of-distribution. Adds +0.172 OOD AUROC at −0.028 in-domain cost.

4

Train on successes only; score failures at inference

Reconstruction loss L = Σ‖h_t − ĥ(u_t)‖² on ~100 success trajectories. At test time the anomaly score e_t = ‖h'_t − ĥ(u_t)‖² is computed per step, then read out by either Top-k (high recall) or Conformal Prediction (false-positive guarantee at level α).

The problem it redefines

  • Input: a failed trajectory τ′ with Z(τ′)=0.
  • Output: the set of "failure-contributing" steps — any step that meaningfully pushes the run toward failure (captures compounding errors, not just the last slip).
  • Constraint: trained with zero failure data and zero step labels.
τ = (Q, a₁, s₁, …, a_T)  Z(τ)∈{0,1}
predict  y(τ′) ⊆ {1,…,T}  when Z(τ′)=0

What it replaces

  • Prompting pipelines (GPT-4o/5 as judge) — slow, costly, ambiguous.
  • GRPO post-training on labeled failures — needs step-level annotation.

Both depend on frontier-LLM inference or expensive labels. Oat needs neither.

03

Strengths & Limitations

Read them side by side — that's where the judgment lives.

Strengths what lands

  • Genuinely practical. Zero tokens, sub-second latency, <1 GB VRAM, ~100 trajectories to train — real-time failure attribution becomes deployable.
  • Paradigm shift. From "collect failures + step labels + heavy LLM judging" to "unsupervised one-class learning on the free byproduct of success."
  • Principled tool choice. The ODE→CDE argument (ODE can't express "many distinct action sequences all succeed") is clean; CP gives a distribution-free false-positive guarantee.
  • Reproducible. Full hyperparameter table, data stats, annotation protocol, anonymized code, and a visualization tooling released.

Limitations what bites

  • Absolute performance is still low. Best F1 ≈ 0.435 in-domain, ≈ 0.225 OOD — far from reliable auto-diagnosis; CP recall is weak (0.484 / 0.330).
  • Labels are self-annotated. "Failure-contributing step" involves subjective "meaningfully degrades" calls — self-evaluation bias is real.
  • Small, single-agent-family data. MCP-Atlas subset = 203 tasks / 88 failures; only one OOD benchmark; no coding (SWE-bench) or long-horizon research tasks.
  • Root cause vs. derived error blur. Case studies show sensitivity to "suboptimal plan / explicit verbalized failure" but blindness to early implicit reasoning errors; CP's hard threshold misses just-below-threshold causal steps.
04

Questions for Further Exploration

Q1 · SCORING
Would a Continuous Normalizing Flow (NLL under the learned success distribution) beat reconstruction error at catching implicit root causes?
Q2 · EVAL
Can we drop single-point labels for an interventional / counterfactual protocol that measures causal contribution objectively?
Q3 · SCALE
Do Neural CDE numerics and cost hold on 数十到数百步 SWE-bench / long-horizon research trajectories — higher-order solver, or segment the path?
Q4 · CLOSED-LOOP
Can Oat's anomaly score feed back into online correction or RL (fuse with AgentRacer/GraphTracer post-training), not just offline diagnosis?
Q5 · GATE
The gate q(·) is a fixed MLP — could a query/task-adaptive online gate (calibrated per domain) stabilize OOD gains beyond Who&When?
Q6 · GENERALIZE
Does the CORAL + gating OOD boost survive a third, structurally different benchmark — or is it partly fit to Who&When?
