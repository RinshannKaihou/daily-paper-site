02 / SO-WHAT
◆ Why this matters

Tells maintainers & agent devs when partial evaluation is safe — and when cheap-first sampling systematically corrupts pairwise decisions.

Gives leaderboard operators a reporting checklist (threshold, coverage rule, decision rule, allowed unresolved rate) and gives A/B teams a stop-vs-finish framework.
03 / EDGE
▲ Critical edge

Replay only permutes fixed records — results bound reporting norms, not a general budget formula or future-sampling guarantee.

Evidence covers only SWE-bench / AppWorld / tau-bench; Neyman allocation uses full-record variances; no adaptive online stopping rule tested.

Methodology — replay, don't resample

  1. FIX
    Tasks, systems, pass/fail outcomes are frozen. Only the order of observation changes. Target = full-benchmark pairwise decision (does A beat B at threshold δ?)
  2. δ
    Thresholds {0pp, 5pp, 10pp}: 0pp tests strict ordering, 5/10pp demand larger margins. D_i = Y_A − Y_B ; Δ_full = (1/N)Σ D_i
  3. COVER
    Proportional floor per group (repo / difficulty / domain); slack floor subtracts one task. If required group counts exceed budget → comparison is deferred.
  4. DECIDE
    Bootstrap-tail policy: conclude only when most resamples agree on threshold side; coverage-aware version requires group coverage first. false accept · false reject · coverage fail · unresolved (defer)
  5. REPLAY
    SWE-bench/AppWorld: 500 reveal-orders × 200 bootstraps. tau-bench: 2000 × 500. Budget grid: 5pp (5%–95%) plus 25/50/75% coarse grid.

Strengths

  • Separates score estimation from pairwise decision — and defines an operable 3-objective budget.
  • Failure modes are concrete and citable: 99.96% coverage failure, 11.51% cost → 100% failure, 93.64% deferred.
  • Isolates decision-rule effect (bootstrap / paired-normal / McNemar) from allocation effect (Neyman / Serfling / uniform).
  • Delivers an actionable reporting checklist: threshold, task selection, coverage rule, decision rule, allowed unresolved rate.
  • Fully on public data + open reproducibility package.

Limitations

  • Replay permutes fixed records — no extrapolation to future task sampling or online stopping.
  • Reported budget moves with decision definition: SWE-bench Lite 0pp flips from "fails at 95%" to "passes at 75%" under the slack rule.
  • Reveal order is fixed; adaptive / active task selection is untested and may reach different budgets.
  • Groups use only public metadata (repo / difficulty / domain); semantic or failure-mode clustering needs priors — not implemented.
  • Scope is narrow: tau-bench grid has only 4 entries / 12 ordered comparisons; cost-aware claims need larger calibrated records.
VI

Open questions for further exploration

  1. Could adaptive / active task selection — choosing the next task from the current partial effect — meaningfully cut the minimum sufficient budget, and what prior variance estimates would it need?
  2. How does the framework extend from fixed-record replay to genuine future-task sampling, and what explicit sampling model and benchmark-collection protocol does that require?
  3. Would semantic or failure-mode clustering as the grouping (vs repo / difficulty / domain) change the coverage floor and the sufficiency budget?
  4. On OSWorld, WebArena, AgentDojo, AndroidWorld — does the AppWorld-early vs SWE-bench-late split persist, or do new shapes emerge?
  5. For procurement / deployment (tiny η) vs leaderboard triage (large η), what does the budget–cost–reliability Pareto frontier look like?
