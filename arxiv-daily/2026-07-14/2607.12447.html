01

Methodology — a normative lens on logits

SDC framework · Kepecs / Hangya / Sanders

The reframing: stop asking whether the logit predicts correctness, and ask what it represents — a heuristic preference score, or a readout of a sufficient-statistic decision variable?

An ideal observer encodes signed stimulus evidence Δ with internal noise into a latent decision variable d = Δ + ε. Sign(d) determines choice; |d| determines confidence. Because d is a sufficient statistic, any monotonic readout LD = ℓ_A − ℓ_B inherits four falsifiable signatures.

The determinism problem. Identical inputs yield identical logits, so trial-to-trial variability is supplied by stimulus-exemplar variation — many images per strength level with randomized nuisance features — standing in for sensory noise (the random-dot-motion analogy).

SIGNATURE 1
Psychometric
Choice probability tracks signed Δ.
SIGNATURE 2
Signed evidence
Signed LD varies near-linearly with Δ.
SIGNATURE 3
Trial-specific confidence
Within fixed strength, larger |LD| ⇒ more correct.
SIGNATURE 4 · DIAGNOSTIC
Folded-X
|LD| rises on correct, falls on error trials.
Diagnostic geometry
The folded-X pattern
As stimulus strength grows, strong-stimulus errors require the noisy decision variable to barely cross the boundary — so |LD| falls on errors while rising on corrects. This is the single signature that separates a true readout from mere difficulty encoding.
02

Results & highlights

4 models · 4 task families
  1. 1 All four SDC signatures hold for Qwen2.5-VL 7B on every perceptual task. The folded-X interaction is strongly positive correct slope +8.08 vs. error −1.11; adding |LD| lifts AUROC 0.864 → 0.907.
  2. 2 Replicated across three more models — Gemma 3 12B, Gemma 4 12B, and reasoning model Gemini Flash 3 — in essentially every model–task cell (one neutral exception: Gemma 3 contrast error branch is flat, not falsifying).
  3. 3 Abstention is near-optimal and LD-driven. Withholding 87.7% of contrast trials raises committed accuracy 92.2% → 99.95%; the optimal threshold beats the model's policy by only +0.05 pp. |LD| predicts abstention (AUROC 0.840) better than stimulus strength (0.796).
  4. 4 Memory task: shallow psychometric, sharp folded-X. City-population accuracy only 61.8% pseudo-R² = 0.069, yet |LD| still predicts correctness within fixed bins OR = 1.36, p < 10⁻¹⁸⁰ with a full folded-X.
  5. 5 CLEVR boundary. |LD| still carries correctness info beyond blur and scene controls +0.029 to +0.072 AUROC, but error-trial slopes are flat or positive — never the negative branch the canonical folded-X demands.
03

Strengths & limitations

side by side

Strengths

  • Conceptual novelty. Reframes LLM calibration from an empirical into a representational question with testable qualitative predictions.
  • Cross-disciplinary bridge. Imports decades of perceptual-decision literature into LLM evaluation.
  • Pre-specified, ordered signatures — weakest to strongest, with the folded-X as the discriminating test.
  • Replication across 4 models and 4 task families gives real generality.
  • Honest boundary on CLEVR and on the BCH/CRES distinction rather than overclaiming.
  • Causal/behavioural abstention evidence — the signal matters, not merely correlates.

Limitations

  • Narrow positive scope. SDC confirmed only for simple perceptual + one memory task; inconclusive for complex reasoning where LLMs are most useful.
  • Cannot separate BCH from CRES — needs independent manipulation of strength vs. reliability (future work).
  • Implementation-agnostic — satisfying signatures says nothing about how the model computes the signal; no mechanistic account.
  • Deterministic-noise assumption (nuisance ≈ sensory noise) is plausible but not independently verified.
  • Stimulus coverage narrow — no auditory, multi-step logical, or agentic tasks.
  • Fragile reasoning-model readout — 90%-CoT-cut patch; logits saturate for long reasoning chains.
04

Open questions

where this leads

How to independently manipulate stimulus strength vs. evidence reliability to finally adjudicate BCH vs. CRES? The paper flags this as the key open direction.

Do SDC signatures hold on multi-step / agentic tasks? Are intermediate-step logits or final-answer logits closer to the latent decision variable?

Can an explicit normative process model for CLEVR/VQA (e.g. scene-graph confidence) bring the folded-X back as a diagnostic?

Mechanistically, which attention heads / residual-stream directions encode the latent variable d — the behavioural-to-neural step?

What is the coverage–accuracy Pareto curve under tunable abstention thresholds? 87.7% is too conservative to deploy.

Can a principled CoT hidden-state readout replace the ad-hoc 90% reasoning-cut and avoid logit saturation on long chains?
