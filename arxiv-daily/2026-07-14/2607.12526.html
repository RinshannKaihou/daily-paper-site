01

Methodology

closed-form · no gradient training
REFRAME

Query becomes (f, x, T, S)

The inverse is conditioned on the source-local forward point — activations, routing, local Jacobians — instead of being selected by an image prior among an equivalence class of preimages.

REPAIR · STAGE 1

Matrix Wiener map Gv

A frequency-diagonal, full-channel, zero-intercept map turns the mean-seed VJP into an upstream-state estimate. Per node / per frequency, ridge ρ = 0.01.

G_v(ω) = S_v^{HR}(ω) · ( S_v^{RR}(ω) + λI )⁻¹
REPAIR · STAGE 2

JVP forward-consistency (Dv)

A second Wiener map predicts the residual state error from a pulled-back JVP child-consistency cotangent. Residual splits into a bias term b and a source-specific error response.

COMPOSE

Topology-respecting reverse pass

Maps compose through the boundary DAG in one finite reverse pass. Branches, residual adds, concatenation and fan-out follow autograd's native semantics — no hand-written inverse rules.

Theory chain: MSE-optimal conditional mean ≡ covariance-induced natural adjoint J♯ = Σ_v J* Σ_u⁻¹, approximated by a closed-form distributional Wiener projection, with a finite-DAG stability bound max ‖δ_v‖ ≤ max‖ε_v‖ / (1−q).

  • Topology is the whole game. A single global terminal map scores 0.072; the raw VJP is near zero 0.0002; topology-respecting local composition reaches 0.938. The gain is localized, not global.
  • JVP-FC earns its keep on Transformers. Its pixel-cosine gain over Gv-alone grows from 0.037 (ResNet18) to 0.391 (ViT-B/32) — exactly where normalization / attention routing bites hardest.
  • Operator-family ablation. Gamma-only ≈ 0 across architectures; per-frequency diagonal reaches 0.25 / 0.10 / 0.06; rank-8 works on ResNet18 (0.834) but collapses on ViT-B/32 (0.050). “Full-channel + per-frequency” is the only universally adequate operator.
  • Decision grounding holds 48 / 48. Top-positive channel groups drop prediction margin beyond within-image random controls for every model–layer–k combination, each paired-bootstrap interval excluding zero. Audit: 36,864 interventions / 3,072 inversions / 1,536 deletion curves.
  • Deployment cost scales hard. ViT-B/32 needs 4096+ calibration images to stabilize; ConvNeXt-B map family is 15.51 GiB, eval peak 16.78 GiB; online time 58 ms (ResNet18) → 370 ms (ConvNeXt-B) per image.
03

Strengths

  • New deployment shape. Calibrate once, reuse across new inputs / depths / channels / channel-groups with no refitting — plus a genuine 176× online speedup.
  • Theory with a clear bridge. Conditional mean → natural adjoint → distributional Wiener projection → finite-DAG stability bound, all closed-form and second-order-statistics only.
  • One tensor-axis rule covers CNN features, ViT token streams, vector/GAP/MLP and attention tensors; 12 formal component runs pass the validator with no hand-written inverse rules.
  • Pre-declared controls (fixed sample indices, predeclared depths/groups) that separate diagnostic metrics from correctness claims — zero-target → zero-output verified per channel.
04

Limitations

  • Transformers stay weak. ViT-B/32 at 0.702, Swin-T at 0.504; ViT-B/32 online-child oracle gap is large and only closes with 4096+ calibration images.
  • Frequency-diagonal restriction. The operator cannot mix across coordinate frequencies — a tractability choice whose adequacy is empirical, not guaranteed.
  • Checkpoint-specific. Maps encode checkpoint-local geometry; swapping checkpoints forces recalibration.
  • Heavy storage / memory — ConvNeXt-B at 15.51 GiB maps, 16.78 GiB eval peak.
  • No independent correctness gold standard. Primary metrics (pixel cosine, SSIM, LPIPS) are input-alignment diagnostics; authors repeatedly stress they do not define inversion correctness.
  • Scope. Vision CNN/Transformer only — internal attention tensors and language models unverified.
05

Open Questions

for further exploration

  1. Is frequency-diagonal + full-channel the right operator family for Transformer token streams? Would light cross-frequency coupling (block / low-rank) close the ViT-B/32 oracle gap?

  2. How far is JVP-FC from online-estimating the sample-specific metric Mv, Mu? Could a small online head replace the fixed distributional projection?

  3. Zero-intercept guarantees zero-target → zero-output, but spectral multiplication can spread energy in coordinate space. Is there a verifiable non-expansive bound, or a sparsity check on the inversion map?

  4. Decision grounding uses calibration-mean / zero replacement. Under finer causal interventions (activation path editing, meddling), does the 48 / 48 directional consistency survive?

  5. Can checkpoint-invariant local-geometry features be constructed so maps transfer across small fine-tunes without recalibration?

  6. For internal attention tensors under the softmax-simplex constraint, is a specialized nonlinear inverse rule needed, or is the current linear relaxation enough?

  7. On language models the “coordinate axis” is sequence position — what does frequency-diagonal mean there? A syntactic / semantic band decomposition?
