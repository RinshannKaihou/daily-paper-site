Method — memory as a lifecycle, not a fact-set

5 ops × 6 probes × 2 settings
Remember
First establishes a fact. Tests missed capture & wrong-target binding.
Forget
Delete a value, keep siblings. Tests value leakage & over-forgetting.
Update
New value → old. Tests lingering reliance on stale values.
Reflect
Synthesize a bounded inference. Tests over-generalization.
TrajectoryOps
Compose ops across targets. Recover final, intermediate & ordered states.

Six operation-level probes

OperationTrace — locate trigger evidence, characterize the op.
TargetBinding — bind op to correct entity & granularity.
StateTransition — infer post-op state.
CandidateDisambig. — reject stale / adjacent / echo / 3rd-party values.
OperationApplication — apply state to forms, reminders, reimbursement.
StateTrajectory — recover the multi-step ordered state trace.

CandidateDisambiguation ≈ ceiling · StateTrajectory exposes the largest gap.

Adjacent EASY

Evidence convo placed directly before the probe — isolates "understand & apply an op".

Long-context HARD

Evidence shattered into UltraChat filler, plus state-neutral op-aware distractors (~5.7/sample).

03

Strengths & limitations

Strengths +

  • Lands "memory-as-lifecycle" on machine-checkable fields (trigger/target/scope/state-transition/evidence span), with evidence required verbatim in user turns.
  • 6 probes × 5 ops × 2 settings orthogonal design — diagnostic grain far above prior benchmarks; per-op and per-probe tables corroborate each other.
  • Covers four memory paradigms in one table, including industrial Mem0/MemOS alongside academic RAG — directly useful to practitioners.
  • Honestly reports the anomaly (DeepSeek-V4-Flash long > adjacent) with a testable hypothesis (generation budget → op prediction).

Limitations !

  • LLM-judge stability never quantified — yet all five op-level diagnostics depend on it; Reflect Precision is especially subjective.
  • Reader & judge same family (GPT-4.1-mini / GPT-4o) — possible systematic bias, undiscussed.
  • Parametric-memory verdict rests on a single baseline (Temp-LoRA); ROME / MemoryLLM / LocAS cited but untested.
  • No human ceiling — impossible to tell whether 0.916 is near-saturation or far from it.
  • 55.4% retention may bias the benchmark toward "GPT-4o-acceptable" sample distribution; English-centric everyday scenes only.

Q2 · CEILINGWhat is the human upper bound on MemOps? How far is 0.916 from saturation?
Q3 · DECOUPLEIf reader ≠ judge, do the 0.01–0.03 gaps in Table 3 stay significant?
Q4 · PARAMETRICROME / MemoryLLM / LocAS on MemOps — is parametric un-controllability essential or an artifact of Temp-LoRA?

Q6 · ATTRIBUTIONTrajectoryOps failure: how much is long-context attention decay vs. temporal reasoning itself? A "dense same-slot, longer" ablation could split them.
