Key metric

36.35percentage-point spread

On 2,600 consensus-derived gold rubrics, item-level pass rates separate 10 commercial systems — from Kimi 58.58% (1st) to Perplexity 22.23% (last). The best system still fails >2/5 of rubrics.

Best 58.58% Spread 36.35 Worst 22.23%

Why this matters

Solves scalable evaluation of long-form reports without human experts — transferable to law and medicine.

Plus a direct, tier-level vendor-selection ranking for fintech, where no such expert-free signal existed before.

Critical edge

The 98.67% agreement headline is conditional.

Computed only on the 2,867 jointly-unanimous items — the easy cases. The fairer unconditional figure is 90.42% (κ=0.8076); ~10% disagreement is silently absorbed by the unanimity filter.

01

How 14,450 candidates become 2,600 gold rubrics

Two-stage consensus funnel

Auto-synthesized binary rubrics, filtered by a 3-LLM judge panel (gpt-5.4 · claude-opus-4-7 · gemini-3-pro-preview).

14,450 candidate rubrics 100%
↓ strict consistency filter — all 3 judges agree on all 10 reports
3,687 25.52%
↓ distinguishability filter — ≥1 majority-yes AND ≥1 majority-no across reports
2,600 gold rubrics 17.99%

The distinguishability filter is load-bearing: dropping it keeps the ranking (ρ=1.0) but shrinks the pass-rate spread from 36.35 → 25.63 pts. Of 1,087 dropped rubrics, 1,056 were always-yes — unconstrained generation over-produces affirmatives.

Pipeline, end to end

  1. Generate query-specific binary rubrics across 4 dimensions (comprehensiveness, insight, instruction-following, readability) — avg 138.94 per query.
  2. Batch-execute ≈100 rubrics per single prompt; within-model consistency 0.970, rollout error 0.0%.
  3. Validate once — 3 human analysts (~10 yr) vs. 3-LLM panel on 4,052 items.
  4. Consistency filter — unanimity across judges & reports.
  5. Distinguishability filter — drop always-yes / always-no.
  6. PassRate(m) = mean binary score over rubrics (optionally macro-averaged over 104 queries).

03

Strengths vs. limitations

+ Strengths

  • Scalability is real. Removing experts from the execution loop is the right diagnosis of the field's bottleneck; validation-then-filter is clean & reproducible.
  • Filters are ablated, not asserted. Consistency-vs-distinguishability decomposition; dropping distinguishability shrinks spread 25.63→36.35 pts while preserving rank.
  • Strong robustness. Leave-one-judge-out ρ=0.988; 120 leave-3-product-out splits mean ρ=0.978, 93.3% ≥0.95.
  • Honest tier framing. Authors warn against over-reading within-tier gaps (Doubao vs Gemini 0.19 pt).
  • Production-grounded queries from a live service give ecological validity synthetic benchmarks lack.

! Limitations

  • Chinese-market-specific. Queries & products are mostly Chinese (Kimi, Doubao, Qwen, AutoGLM, Minimax, Metasota); Western / non-financial transfer untested.
  • Temporal drift. Tied to Feb–Mar 2026 data; no refresh mechanism offered — the benchmark may age fast.
  • Binary collapse. Yes/no labels discard partial-credit gradations in nuanced analysis.
  • Conditional headline. 98.67% only on easy items; unconditional 90.42% (κ=0.8076) is fairer.
  • Derivation circularity. Rubrics synthesized from the same reports judged; distinguishability defined on the same 10 systems — mitigated, not eliminated.
  • No artifact release for rubrics / queries / judge prompts in-body; reproducibility pending.
04

Open questions

Q1 · FILTER TENSION

Relaxing unanimity to 2-of-3 majority — how many extra rubrics survive, and do they carry finer top-system discrimination?

Q2 · ENTROPY FILTER

Replace the binary distinguishability rule with an information-theoretic label-entropy metric — does it widen spread without adding noise?

Q3 · SOURCE BIAS

Are candidates biased toward long-report-friendly rubrics (Kimi/Doubao)? Would cross-corpus candidate reports decouple generation from evaluation?

Q4 · LIFESPAN

How fast does financial drift erode the benchmark? Is a rolling quarterly refresh of queries & reports needed?

Q5 · ORDINAL RUBRICS

Extending to 0/1/2 ordinal labels — how much does 3-LLM unanimity drop, and is it still scalable?

Q6 · INDEPENDENCE

The author's Internal System ranks high. Could a fully independent academic team re-run it blind to rule out labeling bias?
