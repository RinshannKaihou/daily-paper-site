The Illusion of Robustness: Aggregate Accuracy Hides Prediction Flips under Task-Irrelevant Context

Yanzhe Zhang (Georgia Tech, SALT Lab)  ·  Sanmi Koyejo (Stanford)  ·  Diyi Yang (Stanford, SALT Lab)  —  arxiv.org/abs/2607.12963 ↗
llm-robustness evaluation context-sensitivity reliability benchmarking
The one sentence to take away

Stable aggregate accuracy hides large per-example flips when models meet task-irrelevant context.

01 Key metric · Worst-tail degradation
53.2%
WTD, Mistral-Large-3 on GPQA — while aggregate Δ stays within ±2.1%.
AGGREGATE Δ ±2.1%
INS (instability) 13.6%
WTD (tail risk) 53.2%
Two 10% tails alone account for 88.3% of all absolute change. The headline number never moves; the tails do.
02 Why this matters

In long-context agents, hidden single-step prediction flips can cascade into irreversible actions — an under-diagnosed failure source.

03 Critical edge

Pseudo-words are not deployment.

The default 10k-token pseudo-word context is admittedly non-naturalistic. Random-token / webpage / SQA sanity checks hold directionally, but the absolute WTD magnitudes likely overestimate damage from real semi-structured agent context (logs, retrieved snippets). Treat 53.2% as an upper bound, not a field rate.

§ 01

Results & highlights

8 models · 4 benchmarks · 4 context types
The core illusion

Aggregate flat, per-example violent

gpt-5.5's overall Δ across four benchmarks is just +0.3 / +0.6 / +0.0 / −0.9. But underneath, INS hits 13.6% and WTD 53.2%. Improvements and degradations cancel — a two-sided effect, not stability.

two 10% tails = 88.3% of |Δ|
Model-specific

You can't predict which question flips

Cross-model per-example change correlates at Pearson r ≈ 0.00 (95% CI [−0.02, 0.02]). Top-10% affected examples overlap at Jaccard 0.09 — near the 0.05 random floor. Question ambiguity predicts neither direction nor magnitude.

Jaccard 0.09 vs random 0.05
Test-time compute

Reasoning only partially rescues

Lifting gpt-5.4 reasoning none→low nearly matches gpt-5.5; medium→high adds nothing to WTD. CoT on gpt-4.1 cuts MMLU-Pro INS 4.3→1.3% and WTD 19.7→6.4%, but SimpleQA WTD still sits at 7.6% — factual errors barely recover.

MMLU-Pro WTD 19.7→6.4%
Training pipeline

SFT / DPO trade benchmarks for instability

Tracing OLMo-3-7B checkpoints: instability appears in pretraining, spikes in midtraining, then SFT raises instability with almost no baseline gain and DPO amplifies it further. Qwen3.5-9B and gemma-4-12B-it post-training both exceed their base models on INS/WTD.

benchmark gain ≈ +instability
Context forms

Persistent across every context type

Pseudo-words, random tokens, random webpages (FineWeb-Edu), and sequential QA all induce INS/WTD. SQA — closest to the target task — produces the highest INS. "Which questions break" partly transfers across types (r ≈ 0.37); "how much" does not.

cross-type Pearson 0.37
Even tiny context

10 pseudo-word tokens is enough

Non-trivial INS/WTD appears with as few as 10 tokens of irrelevant prefix. Of the new errors introduced, roughly 60% are fresh computation errors — context changes predictions qualitatively, not just by copying tokens.

~60% new errors = computation
§ 02

Methodology

controlled isolation of a hidden effect
  • Benchmarks ·4MMLU-Pro, GPQA-Diamond (190 Q), HLE, SimpleQA — ≤500 questions sampled each.
  • Context ·M=10Default 10k-token pseudo-word prefix (~21k chars) inserted as an extra user message between system and question. Pseudo-words: 3–8 random lowercase letters, Zipf-filtered <1.5, vocab 10k.
  • SamplingBaseline: temp=1.0, 20 draws. Context: 2 draws × 10 contexts = 20. Balanced 20-vs-20 comparison per question.
  • Models ·8gpt-5.5, gpt-5.4, gpt-4.1, DeepSeek-V4-Pro, Grok-4.20-NR, Mistral-Large-3, gpt-oss-120b, gemini-3.1-fl. Reasoning off by default (lowest effort if forced).
  • JudgingUnified LLM judge: gpt-5.4-mini (low).
Two complementary metrics

INS  =  (1/N) Σ |Δᵢmean absolute per-example change → two-sided sensitivity

WTDK  =  −(1/m) Σ Δ(ℓ)mean over worst m=⌈K/100·N⌉ examples, K=10 → tail risk

Noise control. 1000× bootstrap null estimates a floor η (absolute-value & worst-tail both inflate under finite sampling); report Î − η. Split-sample variant ranks the tail on one half, measures on the other — avoids select-then-measure bias. Spearman-Brown reliability ρ ≥ 0.75 on MMLU-Pro. Authors are explicit: this is floor correction, not an unbiased estimate.

§ 03

Strengths & limitations

side by side

+ Strengths

  • ▲Engineering-shaped metrics. INS / WTD map directly onto mean perturbation sensitivity and tail risk — they plug into a reliability-engineering agenda.
  • ▲Rare experimental breadth. 8 models × 4 benchmarks × 4 context types × 4 lengths × full OLMo-3 training checkpoints — far beyond single-distractor studies.
  • ▲Transparent, conservative stats. Noise floors, CIs, split-sample unbiased estimates reported; honestly labelled as correction, not unbiased.
  • ▲Training-stage analysis is unique. Warns model developers that SFT/DPO buy benchmark points by selling per-example stability.
  • ▲Qualitative error split. Degraded examples add computation errors; improved examples "solve the wrong problem" — qualitatively distinct failure modes.

− Limitations

  • ▼Ecological validity. 10k pure-noise pseudo-words differ from real semi-structured agent context; absolute WTD (53.2%) shouldn't be extrapolated to production.
  • ▼Tight sampling budget. 20 trials/question leaves high variance on the extreme tail (worst 1%); reliable WTD needs costly resampling (future work).
  • ▼No mechanism. "Why specific samples destabilize" is deferred; only argues overlap / token-copying bias is insufficient — no tie to retrieval-head or entrainment work.
  • ▼No seed variance decomposition. Variance across the M=10 contexts themselves isn't isolated from model-specificity.
  • ▼Observational training claims. Midtraining data-mix contrast (gen/QA vs code/math) is labelled observational — not causal.
  • ▼Multi-step accumulation unquantified. The most damaging regime for agents — cascading flips across steps — is explicitly out of scope here.
§ 04

Questions for further exploration

open edges worth chasing

How do single-step flips accumulate across a multi-step agent trajectory? Is there an "error-amplification constant" governing cascade failure rate?

Does cross-model r=0.00 survive 100+ samples/question? If it drifts to 0.1–0.2, is there a small set of universally fragile questions?

Can retrieval-head activation or contextual-entrainment strength serve as a cheap predictor of INS/WTD — flagging fragile samples without expensive resampling?

What is the mechanism by which SFT/DPO amplify instability — preference optimization widening logit-tail variance, or data-induced long-context over-reliance?

Why does CoT/reasoning rescue WTD on MMLU-Pro but barely on SimpleQA? Do factual errors live in parametric knowledge that extra thinking cannot repair?

Can a stability-aware training objective or inference-time calibration compress the WTD tail while preserving benchmark performance?
