Methodology & Evidence

Argumentative synthesis, in four moves

A position paper — no new model, dataset, or training run.

  1. Bottom-up from Philosophy of Science (§2). Reconstructs "good explanation" as parsimony + explanatory scope; adopts mechanisms as the fundamental abstraction, treating laws as mechanisms of exceptionally broad scope.
  2. Bridge to ML (§3). Reads mechanistic interpretability, CRL, equation discovery, and MoE as converging on one lesson: explanatory organisation must be encouraged during learning, not extracted post-hoc.
  3. Synthesis into MWM (§4). States capabilities (variable / mechanism / structure discovery; active inquiry), four design principles, two inductive pressures, and the unified objective (Eq. 9).
  4. Progress review (§5). Maps existing methods onto the three anatomical components and flags sparse attention as a scalable building block for structure recovery.

Conceptual results (cited evidence)

  • No method satisfies all six capabilities. Across 10 methods and 6 capabilities (Table 1), each is missing at least one; DreamCoder / NEO miss variable & structure discovery and a scalable architecture.
  • Prediction ≠ understanding. Vafa et al.'s planetary world models accurately predict trajectories yet fail to internalise Newtonian mechanics when probed — the bottleneck is internal organisation, not interpretability tooling.
  • Foundation models miss causal mechanisms. On Geneformer & scGPT, attention circuits reflect statistical co-expression, not gene-regulatory pathways, and they do not beat simpler baselines on perturbation prediction.
  • A scaling path exists. Sparse attention has been shown to induce simpler internal circuits in models up to 7B parameters — the main evidence that explanatory-structure recovery can scale.
  • Mechanism libraries are plausible. SAEs on ESM-2 recover latent features interpretable as binding sites and structural motifs; DreamCoder / NEO / COMET are proof-of-concept but confined to symbolic or game-like settings.
Strengths & Limitations

Strengths pro

  • Genuine interdisciplinary synthesis. The Philosophy-of-Science grounding is load-bearing — unificationism motivates parsimony + scope; Simon's near-decomposability motivates hierarchical mechanisms.
  • Computational, not just philosophical. A clean anatomy (T, Z, M, S) and a single objective (Eq. 9) make the paradigm legible and, in principle, implementable.
  • Sharp subfield diagnoses. CRL identifiability "comes at the cost of flexibility"; equation discovery returns "a single universal law … rather than composable mechanisms"; mech-interp "is about interpreting the model, not the world."
  • Honest self-marking. Table 1 flags its own proposal as the only all-six row while conceding the framework is aspirational.

Limitations caveat

  • No implementation, no experiments, no benchmarks. "No existing method instantiates a Mechanistic World Model in full," including the proposal; Eq. 9 is schematic, not shown tractable.
  • Sufficiency of the two pressures is asserted, not shown. Whether jointly optimising Ωp + Ωc + Lpred yields reusable mechanisms in open-ended regimes is an open empirical question.
  • Identifiability is unresolved. The paper concedes no existing theoretical framework can address the identifiability of variables and mechanisms — undercutting the "computational blueprint" claim.
  • Scalability by analogy. The 7B-circuit evidence is imported from mechanistic interpretability, not demonstrated for the full library + binding setting.
  • Active inquiry is gestured at. Hypothesis generation, experimental design, and library management are named, not specified.
  • Evaluation gap. Current accuracy-focused benchmarks cannot verify recovered abstractions are genuine mechanisms; none is proposed.
Open Questions
  1. Can a minimal viable setting of Ωp + Ωc (e.g. MDL-based library + sparse-attention bindings) beat pure predictive models on compositional generalisation?
  2. Under Eq. 9, can variable & mechanism identifiability be guaranteed under weaker assumptions than current CRL theorems — or are MWMs doomed to be useful-but-un identifiable?
  3. How should known domain mechanisms (physical laws, biochemical pathways) be injected as priors into M while remaining revisable — a differentiable, neuro-symbolic library?
  4. Can a controlled trial at matched compute isolate the MWM pressures from "scale alone," testing out-of-distribution, intervention, and mechanism-recovery behaviour?
  5. What is the concrete computational form of active inquiry — Bayesian optimal experimental design or curiosity-driven RL, made compatible with local library updates?
  6. What benchmark — a synthetic-real hybrid with known variables, mechanisms, and structure — could actually test whether recovered abstractions are mechanisms, not statistics?
  7. What is the engineering interface between MWMs and JEPA, DreamerV3, or Genie — can a mechanism library and bindings be grafted onto existing latent state-space world models with minimal change?
