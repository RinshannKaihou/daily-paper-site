§ 01 — How it works

The closed loop 4 MODULES

1

Strategy Ingestion

code → typed genome

Source code / rules are parsed to an AST, annotated with trading semantics (signal, entry, risk, sizing, exit), and split into editable layers — a typed "strategy genome" that preserves provenance, family, parent and mutation history.

2

Evidence Construction

backtest → EvidencePack

A chronological split backtest yields scalars (Sharpe, drawdown, trades, win-rate), behavioral profiles, regime sensitivity, validation→OOS decay Δ, and anomaly risks (over-stopping, upside truncation).

3

Optimization Pipeline

diagnose → edit → verify → promote

LLM diagnoses failure modes and plans a search level; candidates are controlled edits that must pass a typed edit contract; a hard gate plus an adaptive promotion engine then decides adopt / incubate / reject, with failures written to memory.

repairbridgeredesignfamily migration
4

Iterative Refinement

track best genome

Tracks the best-verified genome; escalates repair→bridge→redesign→migration on repeated failure; stops on budget exhaustion, score convergence, or no admissible search level.

Verification is central. A compact score explicitly separates validation-set gain from out-of-sample audit behaviour — penalizing OOS drawdown expansion, val/OOS deviation, and probing fee/slippage perturbation, one-bar delay, and parameter jitter.

§ 03 — Tension

Strengths & Limitations SIDE BY SIDE

▲ Strengths

  • Targets improving deployed strategies rather than generating from scratch — closer to real R&D pain.
  • Full auditable loop: typed genome, layered edits, adopt/incubate/reject promotion, memory distillation.
  • Evaluation depth exceeds most LLM-quant work: ablations, 2× fee stress, split shift, two rolling walk-forward windows.
  • Failed candidates and rejection reasons are explicitly recorded and reused — addressing backtest-overfitting / data-snooping concerns.

▽ Limitations

  • Negative-Sharpe baselines; absolute return levels remain modest; gains are largely relative.
  • 30 random stocks, not an investable universe; no survivorship-bias rebuild.
  • Daily bars only; simplified execution ignores liquidity, market impact, borrow, corporate actions.
  • Single LLM backend (DeepSeek-R1); walk-forward robustness tested on one strategy only.
  • Authors self-describe as a research prototype — not investment advice, not deployment-ready.
§ 04 — Open threads

Questions for further exploration 5 OPEN

If the baseline were already strong (Sharpe > 1.0), would EvoQuant's marginal lift persist — or decay with baseline strength?

What is the exact form of the adaptive promotion threshold — are there hidden degrees of freedom sensitive to validation window length / trade count?

Replacing the lightweight stress probes with statistical robustness tests (SPA, White's Reality Check, Deflated Sharpe Ratio) — how much would promotion decisions change?

How well does memory-distilled optimization transfer to new strategy families — and does cross-market negative transfer occur?

Stability of diagnosis and candidate generation under multiple LLM backends, beyond DeepSeek-R1?
