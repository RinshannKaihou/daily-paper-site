> **一句话总结：** VanillaBench 系统量化了 186 个对抗训练模型相对于多种 vanilla（非对抗训练）基线的干净准确率差距，发现即便最优的鲁棒模型也普遍落后 vanilla SOTA 4–29 个百分点，揭示出鲁棒性的"隐藏精度代价"远大于单篇论文所呈现的幅度。
>
> **TL;DR:** VanillaBench computes the clean-accuracy gap (Δ_clean) of all 186 RobustBench adversarially-trained models across 4 threat models (CIFAR-10 ℓ∞ 8/255, CIFAR-10 ℓ2 0.5, CIFAR-100 ℓ∞ 8/255, ImageNet ℓ∞ 4/255) against multiple vanilla references (Papers with Code best/median/top-10 median, year-matched, and architecture-matched), showing the mean gap vs. the best vanilla model ranges from −7.7 to −29.5 pp and even the single most robust model per track trails its vanilla counterpart by 4.0–21.0 pp — a cost far larger than individual robustness papers convey.

## 核心贡献

1. **系统化的 vanilla 基准层**：首次为 RobustBench 上全部 186 个对抗训练模型补齐了长期缺失的 vanilla 基线对比。在此之前，鲁棒性论文几乎只报告 clean accuracy 与 AutoAttack accuracy 两个数，而无从判断相对非对抗训练模型牺牲了多少精度。
2. **多维度 vanilla 参考体系**：构建了五种参考类型——PwC 的 best / median / top-10 median（分"全部条目"与"无额外数据"两子集）、按发表年份匹配的 temporal best、以及手工策展的 architecture-matched 基线（覆盖 184/186 = 98.9% 的模型，其中 90 个为直接匹配、94 个为代理匹配）。
3. **Δ_clean 指标与可视化**：定义并计算 Δ_clean(m, r) = a_m − r，明确刻画鲁棒性-精度权衡中"代价"那一侧；同时强调权衡是二维的，刻意不将其与 robust accuracy 折算成单一标量。
4. **政策性建议**：论证未来鲁棒性评测应将"相对 vanilla 的精度差距"作为标准评测组件，并公开了配套工具（https://bunni90.github.io/robust-vs-vanilla.html）。

## Key Contributions

- Assembles vanilla reference statistics (best, median, top-10 median) from Papers with Code for CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and ImageNet-1k, computed over both all-entries and no-extra-data subsets.
- Constructs a **temporal reference**: the best vanilla accuracy achievable as of each robust model's publication year, controlling for the rapid progress of standard classification.
- Curates an **architecture-matched reference**: hand-matched standard accuracies from the torchvision model zoo (ImageNet), pytorch-cifar / pytorch-cifar100 projects, and original architecture papers — isolating the adversarial-training effect from architectural differences.
- Computes Δ_clean for all 186 models and shows the gap is universally negative and often large; recommends vanilla-referenced accuracy gaps become a standard component of future robustness evaluations.

## Methodology

**Robust models (§2.1).** Uses the RobustBench leaderboard as the source of adversarially-trained models, evaluated under AutoAttack (an ensemble of four parameter-free attacks). Four tracks: CIFAR-10 ℓ∞ 8/255 (98 models, 2018–2024), CIFAR-10 ℓ2 0.5 (20 models), CIFAR-100 ℓ∞ 8/255 (38 models), ImageNet ℓ∞ 4/255 (30 models). For each model RobustBench records clean accuracy, AutoAttack accuracy, architecture, venue/year, and whether extra data was used.

**Vanilla references (§2.2).** Three families:
- *Papers with Code statistics*: best, median, top-10 median, each computed over all-entries and no-extra-data subsets (6 reference points per track), plus a temporal best (best all-entries accuracy published in or before year y).
- *Architecture-matched baselines*: hand-curated standard accuracies matched to each robust model's base architecture; proxy matches used where no exact vanilla number exists (e.g., WideResNet-70-16 proxied to WideResNet-40-8). Available for 184/186 models (90 direct, 94 proxy).

**Gap metric (§2.3).** Δ_clean(m, r) = a_m − r. Negative values mean the robust model sacrifices standard accuracy. Deliberately kept separate from robust accuracy (two-dimensional trade-off, no scalarization).

## Results & Highlights

1. **Mean Δ_clean vs. best vanilla model ranges from −7.7 to −29.5 pp** across the four tracks; the largest is CIFAR-100 ℓ∞ (−29.47 pp mean) and the smallest is CIFAR-10 ℓ2 (−7.71 pp), consistent with ℓ2 at ε=0.5 being a weaker threat than ℓ∞ at 8/255.
2. **Architecture-matched gap (which isolates adversarial training from architecture differences) is −3.5 to −17.5 pp**; restricting to direct matches (exact-same-architecture vanilla accuracy, not proxy) narrows it to −4.0 to −14.0 pp. On CIFAR-10 ℓ∞, the architecture-matched gap (−7.59 pp) is about two-thirds of the best-vanilla gap (−12.01 pp).
3. **Even the single most robust model per track still trails its temporal vanilla counterpart by 4.0–21.0 pp**, and the top-10 most robust models sacrifice 4.6–15.1 pp vs. the median vanilla model and 5.7–21.2 pp vs. the best vanilla model — confirming the trade-off affects the entire frontier, not just weak models.
4. **Only two cases where a robust model exceeds its matched vanilla baseline**: +0.40 pp on CIFAR-10 ℓ2 and +0.39 pp on ImageNet ℓ∞ (both small positive margins). The earlier +21.08 pp outlier (Sehwag et al., ResNet-18) was resolved by correcting the ResNet-18 CIFAR-10 baseline to 93.02%.
5. **Per-track headline (best robust model = Amini et al. MeanSparse in all four tracks)**: CIFAR-10 ℓ∞ WRN-94-16 — 93.6% clean / 75.28% robust; CIFAR-10 ℓ2 WRN-70-16 — 95.51% / 87.28% (+0.17 pp above proxy vanilla); CIFAR-100 WRN-70-16 — 75.13% / 44.78%; ImageNet Swin-L — 78.80% / 62.12%.
6. **Temporal stagnation**: on CIFAR-10 the vanilla best was already 99.37% in 2019 (BiT-L) while the best mean robust clean accuracy in any year is 93.16% (2023); on ImageNet vanilla reached 91.0% in 2022 (CoCa) vs. best mean robust clean accuracy of 77.35% (2024). The gap has narrowed but remains 6+ pp (CIFAR-10) and 13+ pp (ImageNet) in the most recent year.
7. **ImageNet has the widest best-vs-median spread**: −17.88 pp vs. best but only −9.48 pp vs. median, reflecting the diversity of the ImageNet leaderboard (105 entries from 38.1% to 91.0%).

ATHENE is a well-established German national research center for applied cybersecurity, lending institutional credibility.

Vanilla references draw from **Papers with Code** and the **torchvision model zoo**, all reputable, reproducible sources.
- The methodological care taken — direct vs. proxy architecture matching, all-entries vs. no-extra-data subsets, explicit correction of a prior outlier (the +21.08 ResNet-18 case), and a dedicated "Threats to Validity" section — signals a careful, self-critical benchmark study.

## Critical Assessment

The paper's central insight — that robustness papers systematically omit the vanilla baseline and thereby understate the accuracy cost — is both correct and practically important, and the multi-reference design (best / median / top-10 median / year-matched / architecture-matched, each with all- vs. no-extra-data variants) is a genuine strength that preempts the obvious "you compared to the wrong baseline" critique. The honest handling of the architecture-matched comparison is notable: the authors openly admit 94 of 184 matches are proxies, report direct-match-only statistics separately, and even corrected a prior +21.08 pp anomaly by fixing the ResNet-18 CIFAR-10 baseline to 93.02%.

However, several caveats temper the headline numbers. First, the "best vanilla model" reference on CIFAR is dominated by models using massive external pretraining (e.g., BiT-L, CoCa), so the −29.5 pp CIFAR-100 figure partly contrasts a standard-trained robust model against a foundation-model-scale vanilla model — an arguably unfair comparison that the paper itself flags but still leads with. The no-extra-data and architecture-matched numbers (smaller gaps) are the more honest cost estimates. Second, the vanilla references are sourced heterogeneously (PwC leaderboards, torchvision V1 weights, original architecture papers, GitHub repos) with inconsistent evaluation protocols, preprocessing, and training recipes — a known variability the authors acknowledge but do not resolve. Third, robustness is measured solely via AutoAttack under ℓp threat models; robust accuracy under corruption/distribution-shift may tell a different story. Finally, the benchmark freezes at RobustBench's 2024 entries and the now-dated CIFAR/ImageNet-1k datasets, so its forward-looking relevance depends on continued maintenance.

## Strengths

- First systematic, literature-wide quantification of the vanilla accuracy gap across all 186 RobustBench models and four threat models — fills a real and widely recognized hole.
- Multi-reference design (5 reference types × 2 data subsets) lets readers pick the comparison most appropriate to their question and defuses the "unfair baseline" objection.
- Architecture-matched comparison with direct-vs-proxy transparency isolates the adversarial-training effect from architectural confounds.
- Clear, actionable policy recommendation (report vanilla-referenced gaps as standard) backed by public tooling.
- Strong practical framing: explicitly connects the gap to deployment/business decisions (e.g., distinguishing a tolerable 2-pp "insurance premium" from a production-disqualifying 10-pp loss).

## Limitations

- **Heterogeneous vanilla sources**: accuracies come from different papers/model zoos with different evaluation protocols, preprocessing, and recipes — not a unified in-house re-evaluation.
- **Proxy matching**: 94/184 architecture matches use a related (not identical) architecture; the authors mitigate this with direct-only stats but the issue remains.
- **Best-vanilla reference is partly unfair**: CIFAR/ImageNet "best" entries often use huge external data (JFT-300M, Instagram-billion-scale), inflating the headline gap relative to like-for-like comparisons.
- **Small PwC sample for CIFAR** (20–22 entries): reference statistics are sensitive to individual inclusion/exclusion decisions; boards have also been curated/trimmed by Hugging Face.
- **AutoAttack-only robustness metric**: does not capture robustness to corruptions, distribution shift, or other threat models.
- **Stale coverage**: most recent model is 2024; benchmark datasets (CIFAR-10/100, ImageNet-1k) are unchanged for years; no newer architectures/datasets.
- **No new robust or vanilla models trained**: the work is purely a re-analysis of existing numbers, so it cannot resolve protocol mismatches by controlled re-evaluation.

## Relevance

这项工作对对抗鲁棒性社区具有"叫醒服务"意义。它直接关系到：(1) **算法研究者**在报告新防御方法时，应当像报告 robust accuracy 一样例行报告相对 vanilla 的精度代价，否则会系统性误导读者对代价-收益权衡的判断；(2) **工程部署决策者**——在决定是否采用对抗训练时，必须知道牺牲的是 2 个点还是 10 个点的精度，因为这直接决定模型是否可用于生产，进而影响用户体验与商业结果；(3) **基准与方法论社区**——VanillaBench 是 RobustBench 的天然补充层，可作为审稿与论文评估的标准参考。对于关注鲁棒性-精度权衡、对抗训练评测方法学、或安全敏感场景模型选型的研究者，本文提供了目前最完整的横向对比数据。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. 如果在统一的协议下（相同预处理、训练 recipe、评测管线）对 vanilla 与 robust 模型做受控的室内重评，Δ_clean 的分布会如何变化？论文承认现有数字因协议不一致而存在噪声。
2. 对于频繁出现在对抗训练中但没有公开 vanilla 精度的架构（WideResNet-70-16、WideResNet-94-16），若实际训练并评测其 vanilla 版本，架构匹配差距会缩小多少？
3. 鲁棒性度量若从 AutoAttack 扩展到 corruption/distribution-shift 等其他威胁模型，精度-鲁棒性权衡的形状会如何改变？
4. 为什么 CIFAR-100 的差距（−29.5 pp vs. best）远大于 CIFAR-10（−12.0 pp）？是因为更难的 100 类设定放大了对抗训练的代价，还是因为 vanilla SOTA 在 CIFAR-100 上借助了大尺度预训练？
5. 对于使用额外训练数据的鲁棒模型（CIFAR-10 ℓ∞ 上占 26.5%、CIFAR-100 上占 23.7%），与同样使用额外数据的 vanilla 模型对比时，差距是否依然如此显著？
6. 是否存在一种鲁棒训练方法能在不依赖混合分类器（如 MixedNUTS）的前提下，将架构匹配差距压缩到接近 0？MeanSparse 在 ℓ2 上已做到 +0.17 pp，这一结果能否推广？
7. VanillaBench 的发现如何迁移到更新的数据集（如 ImageNet-21k、更现代的 ViT/ConvNeXt 架构）和 2024 年之后的防御方法？基准的时效性维护策略是什么？
