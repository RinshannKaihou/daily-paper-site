> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 JADR 协议，通过雅可比透镜在模型生成第一个 token 之前读取 J-space（可语言化概念空间），用 SafetyAUC 等无量纲指标刻画模型内部对"危险"的识别能力，可在单次前向传播、无需外部裁判模型的情况下比较不同模型与同一模型的量化变体（INT8/INT4）。
>
> **TL;DR:** JADR is a local, judge-free protocol that reads a model's internal J-space (verbalizable concepts via the Jacobian lens) at the decision point before generation, and uses dimensionless rank metrics (SafetyAUC, ComplAUC, ΔSH) to separate StrongREJECT-danger vs. XSTest/OKTest-safe prompts across 6 models (Qwen3-1.7B/4B/8B, Qwen3-Uncensored-4B, Qwen3-SafeRL-4B, Gemma2-9B) and 3 weight regimes (BF16/INT8/INT4); INT8 is essentially neutral (|ΔAUC_safety| < 0.011) while INT4 acts unevenly and degrades compliance more than safety.

## 核心贡献

1. **提出 JADR 协议（Jacobian Assessment of Danger Recognition）**：在"决策点"（即助手生成首个 token 之前的最后一个输入位置）读取 J-space，从而把"安全评估"从"看最终回复文本"前移到"看模型内部的危险识别状态"。整个计算只需一次前向传播，且完全本地运行，不依赖任何外部裁判 LLM。
2. **提出面向安全评估的内部指标体系**：
   - **SafetyAUC / ComplAUC**：分别刻画模型沿 safety 轴和 compliance 轴把危险 prompt 与"形似危险但安全"的 prompt 区分开的能力（跨模型）。
   - **ΔAUC、D_log、ΔSH（Safety Headroom）**：分别刻画量化后区分度漂移、绝对 token 质量漂移、以及安全裕度的联合变化（跨修改）。
3. **把六条可解释的"行为场景轴"作为分析对象**：safety、compliance、evasion、softening、hedging、harm，并配以冻结的双语（英/中/俄）词干词表。
4. **系统验证内部指标与行为指标的一致与背离**：用 StrongREJECT 微调评分器作为独立行为验证，揭示"内部识别 vs 最终行为"可分离的两种失效模式。

## Key Contributions

- JADR, the first protocol that operationalizes Gurnee et al.'s J-space / Jacobian lens (2026) for **safety evaluation**, reading top-k verbalizable tokens at the pre-generation decision point instead of scoring final responses.
- A suite of **lexicon-robust, dimensionless metrics** built on rank AUC (Mann-Whitney), with bootstrap-over-prompts confidence intervals, enabling cross-model comparison across different tokenizers, depths, and vocabulary coverage.
- The **Safety Headroom (ΔSH)** scalar that folds a safety-axis shift and a compliance-axis shift into one signed quantity, explicitly designed to catch the regime where "safety looks preserved but compliance eroded."
- An empirical study over 6 models × 3 weight regimes that distinguishes **intact-recognition-with-lost-refusal** (e.g. SafeRL-4B) from **lost-recognition** (e.g. Ablit-4B), two regimes invisible to any pure behavioral metric.

## Methodology

**Jacobian lens.** For layer ℓ and position t, the Jacobian matrix `J_ℓ = E[∂h_{L,t'}/∂h_{ℓ,t}]` is precomputed once over N=100 calibration prompts (the exact recipe in Gurnee et al. 2026). Applying `J_ℓ` + unembedding yields a distribution `lens_ℓ(h_{ℓ,t})` over vocabulary tokens; only the top-k readout is stored (k=100).

**Decision point.** All internal metrics are computed at the position of the last prompt token (rel_pos = -1). This makes the readout causally dependent only on the prompt and requires no decoding.

**Six frozen axes & lexicon.** Top-k tokens are matched against a frozen, frequency-built lexicon (EN/ZH/RU stems) grouped into 6 axes: safety (170 stems), compliance (47), evasion (49), softening (16), hedging (22), harm (84).

**Counters.** Plain count `c_g` and rank-weighted DCG `dcg_g`; both are reported, DCG being more sensitive to rank shuffling.

**Separability metrics.** Rank AUC per layer and per model (Equations 7–8) is the primary comparator; 95% CI from B=300–1000 bootstrap resamples **over prompts** (since tokens within a readout are correlated). For cross-modification comparisons, ΔAUC (eq. 13), D_log (eq. 14), and the joint Safety Headroom ΔSH = ΔAUC_compliance − ΔAUC_safety (eq. 16) are used, plus a Jaccard index of "important layers" (|AUC(ℓ)−0.5| > τ=0.2) as a mechanism-relocation indicator.

**Datasets.** Danger set 𝒟 = 313 StrongREJECT prompts; safe control 𝒩 = 320 prompts = XSTest-safe (250) + OKTest (70), chosen to share alarming vocabulary with 𝒟 while being harmless in substance.

**Behavioral validation.** StrongREJECT's fine-tuned Gemma-2B grader at threshold 0.5 gives harm@0.5; a regex parses refusal starts; over-refusal is measured on 𝒩. Generations are deterministic (T=0, top_p=1, thinking mode off).

## Results & Highlights

- **SafetyAUC cleanly separates the six models.** Gemma2-9B, Qwen3-4B, SafeRL-4B, Qwen3-8B all reach SafetyAUC ≥ 0.97; Qwen3-1.7B = 0.895; the deliberately uncensored Ablit-4B collapses to ≈0.59 (near chance), with the highest behavioral harm@0.5 = 0.904. The metric reproduces the known-in-advance safety poles with statistical significance.
- **INT8 is essentially neutral to safety separability.** |ΔAUC_safety| stays under 0.011 in absolute value for every model — a strong practical signal that 8-bit quantization does not erode internal danger recognition.
- **INT4 acts unevenly and via compliance, not safety.** Qwen3-1.7B shows ΔAUC_safety = 0.041 (real erosion), while Ablit-4B *recovers* (ΔAUC_safety = −0.086, harm@0.5 drops from 0.904 → 0.875) because NF4 noise partly erases the ablation edit. For Qwen3-4B and Qwen3-8B the safety AUC barely moves, yet Safety Headroom still declines (ΔSH = −0.052 and −0.044) — the joint margin degrades specifically because compliance shifts toward danger, not because recognition is lost.
- **Recognition ≠ behavior, by construction.** Qwen3-8B and Gemma-9B have overlapping SafetyAUC (0.979 vs 0.975) but diverge behaviorally: Qwen3-8B harm@0.5 = 0.067 / refusal 0.754 (helpfulness-tuned, over-refusal 0.041) vs Gemma-9B harm@0.5 = 0.022 / refusal 0.958 (aggressive refuser, over-refusal 0.147).
- **SafeRL-4B is the textbook "recognizes danger but complies anyway" case.** SafetyAUC = 0.976 (higher than base Qwen3-4B), but harm@0.5 = 0.345 and regex refusal = 0.00, because its RLHF template ("It's important to clarify, that you request is illegal. However, I can suggest…") bypasses the regex and is read by StrongREJECT as disclosure.
- **Gemma-9B is strikingly robust** to both INT8 and INT4 across the safety and compliance axes; quantization cost lands primarily on the compliance (instruction-following) mechanism, not safety, consistent with Chhabra & Khalili (2025).

## Critical Assessment

- The central methodological bet is that a frozen, hand-curated lexicon is a faithful proxy for the verbalizable concepts J-space is supposed to expose. The authors are candid that this **systematically underestimates recognition whenever it is expressed non-literally** (multi-token expressions, descriptive paraphrase), and that the auxiliary axes (softening/hedging/harm) localize weakly. The saving grace is the explicit claim that rank-AUC conclusions are robust to lexicon extensions — but this is asserted, not stress-tested against an adversarial lexicon.
- The "no external judge" framing is real but slightly oversold. The protocol still needs (a) the StrongREJECT grader for behavioral validation and (b) the Arditi et al. refusal direction for the rescue experiment. The internal metrics are judge-free; the *interpretation* of them as "safety" is not.
- The ΔSH metric is explicitly an aggregate screening scalar, and the authors warn that its compliance component is contaminated at INT4 by refusal-style changes. This honesty is good, but it means the single number most useful for deployment screening carries a known bias in precisely the regime (INT4) it is most needed.
- The SafeRL-4B case is the most illuminating result in the paper and also its sharpest limitation: it shows SafetyAUC high while harm@0.5 high, which is *the* motivating phenomenon, but it relies entirely on SafeRL's templated response style being mis-scored by a regex. A more robust refusal detector could collapse the contrast.

## Strengths

- Cheap, local, single-forward-pass protocol — directly deployable as a checkpoint screen before release.
- The explicit decomposition into **six axes** (especially separating safety from compliance) is a genuine conceptual advance over monolithic refusal-rate metrics.
- Transparent dual-counter reporting (count + DCG) and bootstrap-over-prompts CIs are good statistical hygiene.
- Honest handling of the SafeRL-4B and Ablit-4B edge cases as calibration poles rather than burying them.

## Limitations

- **Lexicon dependence.** Only literal tokens are captured; non-literal danger recognition is invisible. Six axes are frozen in advance and mostly English-centric in practice.
- **Correlational, not causal.** Rank AUC and the Jaccard layer index show *where* an axis localizes, not *that* those layers cause refusal. A causal claim requires intervention (weight swap / activation patching), which the paper flags as future work and only gestures at via a rescue experiment.
- **Small, non-exhaustive model set.** Six models, two of which are deliberately constructed calibration poles. Generalization beyond Qwen3 and Gemma2 is asserted ("carrying the protocol over requires re-checking sensitivity to k and recomputing important layers") but not demonstrated.
- **StrongREJECT grader itself is an LLM judge**, acknowledged as a possible source of bias in the behavioral validation column.
- **Compliance axis is fragile**: less well localized, noisier across layers, and its absolute value carries a constant offset from refusal politeness — hence ComplAUC is explicitly not comparable across model families.

## 相关性 (Relevance)

JADR 与三类工作高度相关：(1) **机制可解释性的工程化应用**——把 Gurnee 等人的 J-space/雅可比透镜从"科学发现"工具转化为"安全评测"工具；(2) **量化模型可信度研究**——直接回应 Hong et al. (2024)、Chhabra & Khalili (2025) 关于压缩模型安全性的争论，并给出"INT8 基本无害、INT4 主要伤害 compliance"的细化结论；(3) **越狱鲁棒性评测方法论**——指出 LLM-as-judge 的 ASR 指标在不同 benchmark 间不可比，提供了一个无量纲替代。对任何需要部署量化或微调 checkpoint、又希望在前向阶段就筛掉安全退化的工程团队，这套协议可作为廉价的"静默报警器"。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **因果层验证**：作者承认 Jaccard 指标只是相关性。能否设计一个系统化的逐层 FP 权重"救援"实验（rescue），定量给出每层对 SafetyAUC 的因果贡献，而不是依赖 refusal direction 这一个外部准则？
2. **词表鲁棒性**：rank-AUC 的"词表无关"声明能否用一个对抗性词表（故意漏掉关键 safety 词干、或注入干扰词干）做压力测试？收敛到相同排序的边界条件是什么？
3. **跨架构迁移**：协议在 Llama / Mistral / DeepSeek 等非 Qwen/Gemma 家族上是否仍然成立？尤其 Gemma 把 special-token 质量集中在前两层这一架构差异，是否会扭曲 SafetyAUC 的可比性？
4. **多轮与系统提示**：JADR 当前只在单轮 prompt 的决策点读取。在多轮对话、越狱 prefix、或 system-prompt 注入下，决策点应该如何重新定义？
5. **拒绝微调的因果链**：SafeRL-4B 表现出"识别≠拒绝"的背离。能否用 JADR 追踪从"识别层"到"生成层"之间哪一层把识别信号丢弃了，从而定位可干预的中间表示？
6. **量化以外的修改**：协议声称适用于"任何能拿到 J-lens 读数的权重修改"。在 LoRA 微调、abliteration、激活转向（steering）这些更常见的部署期修改上，ΔAUC / ΔSH 的灵敏度是否足够？
