Why This Matters

Formalizes mixed-precision loss as a provable set-function decomposition with closed-form error certificates for low-bit LLM deployment.

Critical Edge

The headline KL win does not translate to significant AIME-2025 accuracy differences at ≥4 bits — decisive served wins concentrate below 4 bits, in the near-lossless regime margins vanish by construction.

Methodology & Results

Two lenses on the Boolean cube

Quantization damage f(S) = L_CE(S) − L_CE(FP) is treated as a set function over the 2L layer subsets.

Decomposes Var(f) into per-order energies Wk under the deployment distribution μp (each layer quantized with p=0.6). Theorem 1 ties the order-≥2 energy to the MSE of the best additive predictor via Parseval.

MSE(best additive) = W≥2 = Σk≥2 Wk

Saturation motivates a coverage model: each layer consumes a fixed fraction of remaining headroom.

f(S) = c · (1 − ∏i∈S(1 − ai))

Exact Möbius spectrum (Thm 3) and Walsh spectrum (Thm 4) derived; predicts the alternating overshoot of low-order Möbius truncations (measured up to 32×).

A closed-form saturation-depth law (Prop 5) forecasts the order-≥2 share from L+1 fitted break-rates, attaching a per-cell error floor alongside every result.

The isolated-sensitivity inflation factor sᵢ/wᵢ ≈ exp(p·Σaⱼ) explains why HAWQ-v2 and isolate-and-sum overstate configuration loss.

Empirical highlights

  • Variance is overwhelmingly first-order. Order-1 share 87.3% measured → 98.1% after noise correction (Qwen3-8B, 10-layer block); 96.9–99.7% on exact 28 lattices for 0.6B–72B dense models.
  • Incumbents mis-price by 71–376%. Isolate-and-sum's median relative error is 51% (Qwen3-8B) / 203% (Qwen3-30B-A3B), vs. 2–3% for fitted additive/coverage models.
  • Out-of-distribution robust. Trained at p=0.6, tested at p=0.85/0.35: coverage degrades ≤2.0×, additive ≤1.9×; Gaussian process degrades 2.1–4.9×.
  • Allocation beats production baseline. AS-additive / AS-coverage lead at every budget; eff 5.0: AS-cov KL 0.0266 vs ModelOpt 0.0331.
  • Below 4 bits decides survival. Qwen3-235B: additive allocation preserves reasoning at 3.5 eff-bits (AIME-25 73.3) where ModelOpt collapses to 0.0.
Strengths & Limitations

Strengths pro

  • Foundational question, principled answer. Asks what expansion order quantization loss admits — and tests it, rather than asserting it as CoopQ/CLADO/GuidedQuant do.
  • L+1-parameter model, double justification. The coverage model is intuitive, and backed both empirically (τ-collapse at Spearman ρ=0.958) and by an Aczél–Ling representation theorem.
  • Honest, self-certifying reporting. Every result is paired with the best any additive model could do; the paper states where its own theory fails (per-tensor scaling, DiT share 0.36).
  • Real served wins. B200 / vLLM / SGLang throughput and accuracy against public NVFP4 checkpoints — not just calibration metrics.
  • Actionable diagnosis. The wrong-anchor inflation factor immediately explains why HAWQ-v2 / isolate-and-sum / ModelOpt fail.

Limitations caveat

  • Served-accuracy gap at ≥4 bits. Table 12 (AIME-2025) shows every method within 66–76 vs. bf16 anchor 73.3 — no significant ordering; the KL payoff is undemonstrated there.
  • Format-dependent additivity. Under per-tensor activation scales the order-≥2 share balloons to 0.67–0.81 and black-box surrogates win.
  • Weight-only (W4A16) out of scope. Saturation lives in the activation channel; weight-only damage is non-saturating.
  • Granularity confound. CoopQ is benchmarked at whole-layer granularity vs. per-linear for the proposed method — a mismatch acknowledged but not neutralized.
  • Loose central certificate. The allocation-granularity certificate is −0.09±0.10 on 30B — not significantly different from zero, exactly where allocation decisions are made.
  • Diffusion DiT is a partial fit. Order-≥2 share 0.36 on Qwen-Image-2512 (L=60), above every LLM cell.
Open Questions
  1. Can break-rates ai be predicted from cheap layer descriptors (depth, RMS, Hessian trace) without any damage measurements — a fully fit-free allocator?
  2. Does the certificate framework extend to the weight channel (W4A16), where saturation is absent — explaining why GPTQ/AWQ work well there?
  3. How do rotation methods (QuaRot, SpinQuant, QuIP#) reshape break-rates and the order-≥2 share?
  4. Can the empirical sub-4-bit "termination threshold" (~0.45 nats on 30B) be derived from the coverage ceiling c as a principled survival predictor?
  5. Could the CoopQ granularity mismatch be neutralized on a smaller model, or does its O(100L) cost forbid the implied comparison?
  6. Does the stationary-kernel failure of GP/RBF generalize to other black-box NAS/quantization predictors — a broadly applicable diagnostic?
