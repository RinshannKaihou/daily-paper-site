§ 01

Fixed content, varied frame — read the difference

Most benchmarks hold the frame fixed and vary the content. ESFP inverts that: one item, five phrasings, 520 independent single-turn prompts per model — and flexibility is read off the contrast, never the level.

A 2×2 behavioural typology falls out: over-aligned (rigid reporter), under-aligned (rigid opinionator), flexible (target), erratic (reversed). No frontier model failed into either rigid cell — all shifted.

Four metrics, increasing linguistic depth

ARAttribution Rate — lexical, 1st vs 3rd person regex. Cheap, gameable (“just add I think”).w 0.20
PSIPhrasing Sensitivity — Sentence-BERT cosine, queue-normalised. Independent of stance words.w 0.30
SCDStance Content Density — dual-judge per-sentence A/B/C. Hardest to cheat, carries the ranking.w 0.50
CPCCross-Phrasing Consistency — Fleiss' κ over stance labels. Enters multiplicatively.×1+0.25
flex(M) = 0.20·δAR + 0.50·δSCD + 0.30·PSI
ESFP(M) = flex(M) · (1 + 0.25·CPC⁺)

Weights are design judgement, never fit to the board. SCD is hardest to game ⇒ highest weight; CPC amplifies real shift rather than substituting for it.

§ 02

The leaderboard capability can't explain

item-level bootstrap · 1000 resamples

Three results resist any capability story: the smallest and only open model (Gemma-3-27B) ties for first; Gemini-3.1-Pro loses to its own Flash-Lite; the sole CoT reasoning model (Qwen3-Next-80B) places 7th — its embeddings move (PSI among the highest) yet never converge into a committed stance. Top-3 CIs overlap: no single winner.

§ 03

Measurement discipline vs. its own caveats

+ Strengths

  • Elegant definition. Flexibility is a difference, not a level — kills the “more is better” misread and yields the over/under-aligned typology.
  • Layered, complementary metrics. Data proves lexicon (AR) and content (SCD) decouple under the neutral baseline P0 — a methodological gift for future subjectivity benchmarks.
  • Solid engineering. Fully single-turn, parallel, resumable (Parquet), two-run reproducibility, per-item/template/model score tables.
  • Rare self-audit. A dedicated §8 lists ten validity threats and cleanly separates weight-robust conclusions from weight artefacts.
  • Mechanism insight. P3 (“enumerate views”) suppresses stance as strongly as P1 (“expert consensus”) — the suppressor is removing the responsible-speaker slot, not invoking “experts.”

! Limitations (self-reported, §8)

  • PSI uses the wrong contrast — P0→P1 instead of P1→P2; partly measures topic drift. Authors flag this as the v2 priority fix.
  • Queue-dependent normalisation — PSI_ceil is defined by the cohort; adding an extreme model shifts everyone's score.
  • Judge validity unknown. No human gold-standard subset; inter-judge agreement not even reported; one judge (Gemini-2.0-Flash-Lite) shares a family with 3 candidates.
  • Incomplete bootstrap. Resamples items but fixes CPC; one sample per prompt — real uncertainty is wider than reported.
  • Reasoning claim rests on n=1. DeepSeek-R1 was dropped (long traces broke sentence labelling); “reasoning ≠ stancing” needs a trace-stripping pipeline and more CoT models.

Open threads

Q · missing analysisPer-category δSCD breakdown — which item class drives the Gemini inversion vs. the Gemma tie? Authors admit run artefacts weren't retained; must regenerate.
Q · v2 priorityRecompute the board with PSI fixed to P1→P2 and a constant ceiling (0.199). Does the near-orthogonality of PSI survive?
Q · construct validityDoes a high ESFP score predict a user's felt sense of a “real interlocutor”? Human validation is the missing bridge from operational definition to psychological construct.
Q · generalisationAfter trace-stripping, do CoT models rise or stay low? “Expanded reasoning widens semantic coverage without converging to stance” needs >1 sample.
Q · deploymentCan SCD distinguish hedged commitments (“I would argue…”) from strong ones? Currently both land in class B.
Q · temporalityCan a model hold a stance across turns? The single-turn benchmark is silent on multi-turn persistence.
