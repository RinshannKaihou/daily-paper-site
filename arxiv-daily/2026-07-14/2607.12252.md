> **一句话总结：** 本文提出了一个无需人工专家参与终判的金融深度研究评测流水线，从 104 个真实金融查询、1,040 份报告中自动合成 14,450 条候选评分细则，经"一致性过滤 + 可区分性过滤"两级筛选得到 2,600 条"共识金标准"评分细则，据此对 10 个深度研究产品给出 22.23%–58.58% 的清晰分层排名。

> **TL;DR:** The paper builds a financial deep-research benchmark from 104 real user queries and 1,040 reports, auto-synthesizing 14,450 binary rubrics and filtering them with a validated 3-LLM judge panel (gpt-5.4, claude-opus-4-7, gemini-3-pro-preview) down to 2,600 consensus-derived gold rubrics, which separate 10 commercial deep-research systems with item-level pass rates spanning 58.58% (Kimi) to 22.23% (Perplexity) — a 36.35-point gap.

## 核心贡献

1. **基于真实用例的深度研究基准**：从一家已部署的金融深度研究服务（2026 年 2-3 月收集）中抽取 104 个真实用户查询，覆盖股票对比、股票趋势预测、基本面咨询、交易建议、主题/板块分析、贵金属分析、个股分析七大类；每个查询采集 10 个产品报告，共 1,040 份，保证了生态效度（ecological validity）。
2. **无需专家参与的评分细则生成流水线**：先用 LLM 从报告中合成 14,450 条查询特定的二元（是/否）评分细则，再用三人专家小组（平均约 10 年经验的证券分析师）与 3-LLM 评委团在 4,052 条采样子集上做一致性验证，确认 LLM 评委可替代人工后，用"严格一致性过滤"和"跨报告可区分性过滤"两道筛子，最终得到 2,600 条"共识金标准（consensus-derived gold rubrics）"。
3. **可验证且具区分度的评测信号**：在最终评分细则集上，10 个深度研究系统展现出清晰且稳定的分层，并经 leave-one-judge-out、leave-3-product-out 等敏感性分析确认排名稳健（Spearman ρ 分别为 0.988 与平均 0.978）。

## Key Contributions

- **A production-grounded benchmark** of 104 real financial queries × 10 reports = 1,040 reports, spanning 7 query categories sourced from a deployed financial deep-research service (privacy-anonymized, PII-stripped).
- **An expert-free rubric pipeline**: 14,450 query-specific binary rubrics are auto-generated from model reports, then validated against three human experts on 4,052 sampled items, then filtered by a three-LLM judge panel (gpt-5.4, claude-opus-4-7, gemini-3-pro-preview) using two complementary filters down to 2,600 consensus-derived gold rubrics.
- **A validated, discriminative evaluation signal** that yields stable tier-level separation across 10 commercial systems, with robustness checks (leave-one-judge-out ρ=0.988; 120 leave-3-product-out splits averaging ρ=0.978).

## Methodology

The pipeline centers on one design objective: derive high-quality rubrics **without human experts in the final execution loop**. Human experts are used only **once**, to validate the automated mechanism.

1. **Query-specific candidate generation.** For each of the 104 queries, the 10 collected product reports are fed to an LLM that synthesizes binary rubric items along four dimensions — comprehensiveness, insight, instruction following, and readability — yielding 14,450 candidates (avg 138.94 per query).
2. **Batched rubric execution.** Rather than scoring one rubric at a time, the judge model receives the entire query-specific rubric set (≈100 items) in a single prompt and returns binary decisions in one shot. A sampled diagnostic confirms this is stable: mean within-model consistency 0.970, 90.99% of rubric evaluations are fully consistent across three rollouts, and rollout error rate is 0.0% (instability is label-flipping, not format failure).
3. **Human-LLM validation (one-time).** Three securities-research analysts (~10 yr experience) vs. the three-LLM panel on 4,052 items.
4. **Strict consistency filter.** A rubric survives only if all three LLM judges agree on **every one of the 10 reports** under the query (unanimity). This retains 3,687 rubrics (25.52%).
5. **Distinguishability filter.** A consistency-passed rubric survives only if, across the 10 reports, at least one gets majority-yes and at least one gets majority-no — removing always-yes/always-no criteria that are stable but uninformative. This retains 2,600 rubrics (17.99% overall; 70.52% of consistency-passed).
6. **Product-level pass rate.** For product m: `PassRate(m) = (1/|G|) Σ_r y_{m,r}`, optionally macro-averaged over the 104 queries.

## Results & Highlights

- **Human–LLM consistency is strong enough to substitute for experts in large-scale screening.** On the 2,867 jointly-unanimous items, label-level agreement is **98.67%** (Cohen's κ=0.9733, only 38 opposite-label cases out of 2,867); overall majority-label agreement across all 4,052 items is **90.42%** (κ=0.8076). Notably, pairwise LLM–human agreement (mean 0.848, κ=0.694) **exceeds** human–human agreement (mean agreement 0.811, mean κ=0.622).
- **The two-stage filter is highly selective and the distinguishability filter is load-bearing.** From 14,450 candidates → 3,687 after consistency (25.52%) → 2,600 after distinguishability (17.99%). Of the 1,087 rubrics dropped at stage 2, **1,056 are always-yes** and only 31 always-no — i.e. unconstrained rubric generation over-produces broadly-affirmative checks. Dropping distinguishability preserves ranking (Spearman ρ=1.0) but shrinks the top-to-bottom pass-rate spread from **36.35 to 25.63 percentage points**.
- **Clear, stable separation of 10 commercial systems.** On the final 2,600 gold rubrics, item-level pass rates range from **58.58% (Kimi, #1) down to 22.23% (Perplexity, last)** — a 36.35-point gap. Even the best system fails >2/5 of the gold rubrics. Tiers: Kimi alone → near-tied Doubao/Gemini (only 0.19-pt apart) → near-tied ChatGPT/Qwen → Metasota, AutoGLM, Minimax, Perplexity.
- **Rankings are robust to judge and product selection.** Leave-one-judge-out gives Spearman ρ=0.988 in all three configurations (only a tie-break swap between ChatGPT/Qwen); removing the Gemini judge leaves Gemini's product rank unchanged at #3, ruling out systematic self-preference. Across all 120 leave-3-product-out splits (select rubrics on 7, rank all 10), mean ρ=0.978, minimum 0.891, and 93.3% of splits reach ρ≥0.95; 112/120 splits preserve perfectly consistent relative ordering of held-out triples.
- **A held-out Internal System scores 50.65/50.52 (item/query-macro)** on the gold rubrics, placing it behind the Doubao–Gemini tier and ahead of ChatGPT and Qwen — illustrating the benchmark's use for internal benchmarking.

## Critical Assessment

The central methodological bet — that LLM unanimity can replace expert judgment for rubric screening — is empirically supported but scoped. The 98.67% label-level agreement is computed **only on the 2,867 jointly-unanimous items** (i.e. the easy cases where both panels already agree there is agreement), so it overstates headline consistency. The more honest number is the 90.42% overall majority-label agreement (κ=0.8076), which is good but not perfect. The authors are commendably explicit about this ("the panel behaves conservatively rather than permissively"), framing the pipeline as **precision-favoring** — it discards some human-unanimous items in exchange for reproducibility, which is the right trade-off for benchmark screening but would not be acceptable for high-stakes single-item judgment.

A subtle circularity risk exists: candidate rubrics are **synthesized from the same 10 product reports** that are later evaluated, and the distinguishability filter is defined by separation across those exact 10 systems. The authors address this with the 120 leave-3-product-out splits (mean ρ=0.978), arguing distinguishability acts as a "non-triviality filter" rather than overfitting — a reasonable defense, though the 7/10 selection still sees most of the product pool. The benchmark is also **Chinese-market-specific** (queries in Chinese, financial products skew Chinese — Kimi, Doubao, Qwen, AutoGLM, Minimax, Metasota), and one of the evaluated products (Internal System) is from the authors' own company, which is held out and reported separately to mitigate self-promotion bias. Judge identity is undisclosed beyond model names (gpt-5.4, claude-opus-4-7, gemini-3-pro-preview), and only binary labels are used, collapsing nuanced quality gradations.

## Strengths

- **Scalability story is real**: removing experts from the execution loop is the right diagnosis of the field's bottleneck, and the validation-then-filter design is clean and reproducible.
- **Filters are well-motivated and ablated**: the consistency-vs-distinguishability decomposition, plus the explicit ablation showing distinguishability widens the pass-rate spread from 25.63→36.35 pts while preserving ranking, is exactly the kind of analysis that makes the contribution trustworthy.
- **Strong robustness checks**: leave-one-judge-out (ρ=0.988) and 120 leave-3-product-out splits (mean ρ=0.978, 93.3% ≥0.95) directly rebut self-preference and overfitting concerns.
- **Honest tier framing**: the authors explicitly warn against over-interpreting within-tier rank differences (Doubao vs Gemini 0.19 pts apart), which is good scientific hygiene.
- **Production-grounded queries** give ecological validity absent from synthetic benchmarks.

## Limitations

- **Domain specificity**: queries are Chinese financial-market queries and evaluated products are predominantly Chinese (Kimi, Doubao, Qwen, AutoGLM, Minimax, Metasota); transferability to Western markets or non-financial domains is untested and explicitly flagged as future work.
- **Temporal sensitivity**: financial data drifts — a report-quality benchmark tied to Feb–Mar 2026 queries may age quickly; the paper acknowledges product-version drift and temporal sensitivity but offers no refresh mechanism.
- **Binary rubric collapse**: yes/no labels discard gradations of partial credit, which can matter for nuanced analytical quality.
- **The 98.67% headline is conditional**: computed only on jointly-unanimous items; the unconditional 90.42% agreement (κ=0.8076) is the fairer figure and still leaves ~10% disagreement that gets absorbed by the unanimity filter.
- **Circularity in rubric derivation**: candidates come from the same reports being judged, and distinguishability is defined on the same 10 systems — partially mitigated by the held-out splits but not eliminated.
- **No release commitment** is visible for the rubric set, queries, or judge prompts in the body (Appendix A shows the evaluation prompt template; Appendix B shows translated query examples), which limits reproducibility until artifacts are public.
- **Evaluated system identities**: only 9 of 10 systems are published in Table 3; the 10th (Internal System) is the authors' own, which, although disclosed, means the headline "10 systems" includes one in-house product.

## Relevance

这项工作对金融 AI 与深度研究智能体（deep-research agent）社区有直接价值：(1) 它正面回应了"长文本报告如何可扩展地评测"这一核心痛点，提出的"专家免参与 + 共识过滤"范式可迁移到法律、医疗等任何依赖长篇结构化报告的专业领域（论文也引用了 LegalAgentBench、MedResearchBench 等同类工作）；(2) 对于构建 LLM-as-judge 评测流水线的研究者，它提供了一个关于"何时可用 LLM 替代专家"的实证范例（98.67% 条件一致、90.42% 无条件一致、LLM-人一致反超人-人一致）；(3) 对金融科技从业者，10 个商业系统的分层排名（Kimi 居首、Perplexity 垫底）具有直接的选型参考意义。需要注意的是，该基准面向中国金融市场，且查询具有时效性，迁移到其他市场或时间窗口时需重新构建评分细则。此为 FinResearchBench 系列的第二代（"II"），区别于依赖人工专家的传统 rubric 流水线。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- 若把一致性过滤从"三人全票"放宽为"二人多数"，会保留多少额外 rubric？这些被放宽进来的 rubric 是否携带更细粒度的区分信号（例如对头部系统的精排）？
- Distinguishability filter 当前定义为"至少一个 majority-yes 与一个 majority-no"。如果改用信息论指标（如该 rubric 在 10 个系统上的标签熵），能否在控制噪声的同时进一步加大 pass-rate spread？
- 候选 rubric 由被评测的同一批报告合成，是否存在系统性偏置——例如某些 rubric 只对 Kimi/Doubao 这类长报告友好？是否可以引入"跨数据集报告"（其他来源的报告）来生成候选 rubric，从而解耦生成与评测？
- 金融数据的时效性如何影响基准寿命？是否需要设计一个滚动更新机制（例如每季度重新采集查询与报告）来对抗分布漂移？
- 二元 rubric 牺牲了质量梯度。若扩展为有序多档标签（如 0/1/2），3-LLM 全票一致率会下降多少？是否仍足以维持可扩展筛选？
- 论文未公开 rubric/查询/judge prompt 的发布计划。若不公开，社区如何独立复现 98.67% 的一致性结论？
- 被评测的 10 个系统中有一个是作者自家的 Internal System，且排名靠前。能否引入完全独立第三方（如学术团队）在盲测条件下重跑该系统以排除标注偏置？
