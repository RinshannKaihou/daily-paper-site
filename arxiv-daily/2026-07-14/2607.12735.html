Why this matters

Decomposes "why representational priors work" into five falsifiable sub-questions — a clean testbed and a reusable experimental-design paradigm.

Critical edge

Every win leans on a known circuit feature family and an exact task invariance. Approximate or noisy invariances from natural data are entirely untested.

1

Does the prior need the task, or just its feature family? content

A "band" prior (coherent, learnable, ~100 classes of ~28 examples — matching the true structure's 97×~29) whose geometry the Fourier circuit cannot express.

band 1/15 ≈ random 0/20 (p=0.43) → coherence is NOT sufficient
2

Do we even need labels? supervision

Commutativity prior: positives are commuted pairs {(a,b),(b,a)} only — no answer info, sparse (31% of anchors have a partner). A label-free sample of the true equivalence.

15/15 grok at 2.7× · more reliable than supervised (22/30, p=0.038)
3

Is there a critical window? timing

Prior applied only within chosen epoch ranges vs continuous vs λ-annealing, with a norm-clamp reference.

[0,2k] = 4% of budget → 10/10 at 2.7×, beating continuous (8/10, 1.25×)
4

Does it survive other tasks, depths, norms? setting

Modular multiplication / subtraction, sparse parity, 2-layer, MLP, pre-LN; norm clamped to 35–70 to map the delay law (Truong et al. 2026).

delay-law exponent flattened ~17×: CE 31×/+10 units → prior 1.22×/+10
5

Where does the story break? boundary

Synthetic subject–verb-agreement grammars (attractor + conjunction) as a pre-registered boundary probe.

generalize BEFORE memorizing → no delay to control; prior lifts levels (0.90→0.95) but can't halt degradation

Honest correction (§8): at the optimal clamp (~45) plain cross-entropy is as fast as the prior — "the speed of low-norm training belongs to norm control alone." The prior's real contribution is reframed as robustness to the norm setting (the flattened exponent), not raw speed at a hand-tuned optimum. A concession that strengthens, not weakens, the account.
§03

Strengths & limits, side by side

author-stated + assessment

+ Strengths

  • Five cleanly-separated axes — content, supervision, timing, setting, boundary — each with a purpose-built control and matched comparator.
  • The label-free commutativity result is the practical highlight: a supervised intervention with a plausible path to real use via augmentation/symmetry positives.
  • Quantitative delay-law mapping (Fig. 2) makes "structure injection neutralizes the norm timescale" falsifiable — a ~17× exponent flattening, not a vague claim.
  • The early-window result (4% of training) removes the practical O(n²) objection to contrastive priors.
  • Exceptional reproducibility plumbing — byte-verified number regeneration — and disciplined Fisher-test statistics.

! Limitations

  • All positive results sit in algorithmic tasks where the circuit's feature family is known; its analogue in natural data is untested.
  • The commutativity prior exploits an exact task invariance; noisy or approximate invariances are untested.
  • Window boundaries (2k/5k/10k) chosen a priori, not finely swept; "early advantage" is a median tendency with seed exceptions.
  • The CE exponent is a lower bound due to censoring; LayerNorm findings apply only to a pre-LN variant.
  • Single author, no peer review; the whole program is a one-layer transformer on mod-p arithmetic — the mechanism may be real without transferring.
§04

Open questions it leaves

for further exploration
Q1Can approximate/noisy invariances (image augmentations, approximate language symmetries) supply enough feature-level alignment — or does the result hinge on the exactness of commutativity?
Q2Is there an automatic way to discover an unknown circuit's feature family, so alignment becomes measurable rather than assumed?
Q3What is the mechanism behind the early-window advantage — do clusters seeded in [0,2k] literally guide Fourier-circuit formation (à la Nanda et al. 2023)?
Q4Does the ~17× exponent flattening hold for deeper transformers and non-algorithmic grokking (Omnigrok, Liu et al. 2023)?
Q5Below the norm floor (~35–40), why does the contrastive gradient interfere with the lean circuit — a general feature of contrastive losses at very low capacity?
Q6Could the prior act as a regularizer (not accelerator) in the immediate-generalization regime, changing the degradation trend in the grammar tasks?
