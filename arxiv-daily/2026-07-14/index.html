Must Read · Mechanistic Interpretability

A Shared Subcircuit Lets LLMs Count Down Across Tasks

Three attention heads in Llama-3.1-70B form a “countdown” circuit — and the very same motif reappears in Claude 3.5 Haiku.

By Jacob Dunefsky, Wes Gurnee & Emmanuel Ameisen · Yale University & Anthropic

因果干洪在 Llama-3.1-70B 中定位出三个“倒计时注意力头”，证明同一子回路在推文、SHA-256、侏句、DNA 等大量任务中被复用，其几何与 Claude 3.5 Haiku 的换行回路一致。

Activation patching isolates three “countdown” heads in Llama-3.1-70B, reused across tweets, SHA-256, haikus and DNA — key/query geometry matches Claude 3.5 Haiku’s line-wrapping circuit, evidence for a motif shared across tasks and models.

Must Read · Quantization

Saturation Makes Quantization Error Additive

Joshua Hill · Waterloo / Baseten

把混合精度量化损失形式化为集合函数，证明 4-bit 下 85–99% 方差为一阶（根源：饱和），匹配内存下比 ModelOpt 低 17–21% KL。

Reframes quantization loss as a set function; 85–99% of variance is first-order at W4A4 (root cause: saturation), beating the production ModelOpt baseline by 17–21% KL at matched memory.

Rethinking the Evaluation of Harness Evolution

Yike Wang et al. · AI2 & Univ. of Washington

统一预算下 harness 演化既不胜过并行采样（pass@1 75.8 vs 86.0），也不泛化（held-out 仅 +0.6）——收益主要来自额外搜索。

Under matched budgets, harness evolution neither beats parallel sampling (pass@1 75.8 vs 86.0) nor generalizes to held-out tasks (+0.6) — reported gains are mostly extra test-time search, not better harness design.
