> **一句话总结：** 在 grokking 的模运算任务上，本文通过 188 次新实验刻画了"什么让表征先验真正起作用"：决定能否泛化的是与电路特征族（而非仅仅是"连贯性"）的对齐；完全无标签的交换不变性先验就够了，且配合权重范数钳制取得 17× 的最强加速；先验只需在最初的 4% 训练时段施加即捕获几乎全部收益。

> **TL;DR:** A single-author companion paper runs 188 new runs on modular-arithmetic grokking (one-layer transformer, (a+b) mod 97) to characterize what makes a supervised-contrastive representational prior work: a coherent-but-wrong-feature-family prior (magnitude bands) blocks generalization like a random partition (1/15 vs 0/20), while a fully label-free commutativity prior generalizes 15/15 at 2.7× speedup (more reliable than the supervised prior, p=0.038) and, combined with a weight-norm clamp, yields the strongest tested method at a median 17× over baseline.

## 核心贡献

本文是前作（companion paper）的直接延续。前作证明：在 grokking 现象中，"先记忆后泛化"的长延迟在因果上等于"任务结构化表征形成所需的时间"，并用一个监督式对比先验（SupCon，编码任务等价结构）控制了 transformer 在模运算上是否泛化。本文不再问"先验是否因果地控制延迟"，而是回答决定该现象能否被理解、能否被使用的四个后续问题，每一个问题配一个专门实验（作者称之为"梯级/rung"）：

1. **内容（Content）**——先验必须匹配任务本身，还是只需匹配其特征族（feature family）？作者构造了"频带结构"先验：连贯且可学（阈值特征），但其几何无法被任务 Fourier 电路的周期性特征表达。
2. **监督（Supervision）**——是否必须使用标签？作者构造了"交换性先验"：正样本仅为交换对 {(a,b),(b,a)}，不使用任何答案信息。
3. **时机（Timing）**——是否存在关键窗口？作者在指定 epoch 区间内施加先验。
4. **场景（Setting）**——结论在任务、架构、归一化上是否复现？并量化范数-延迟律（delay law）的指数形式。
5. （第 5 级）**诚实的边界**——对"先记忆后泛化"前提缺失的任务，先验失效。

结论被凝练为一个三层回答：**能否泛化由特征族对齐决定；多快由对齐与范数副作用共同决定；何时施加远没有想象中重要（早窗口即可）**。所有比较均为同种子配对（same-seed paired），被审查（censored）的运行按 50,000-epoch 预算计分，所有数字由 `gen_numbers.py` 生成并经 `verify_regen.py` 字节校验。

## Key Contributions

- **Feature-family, not coherence, is the gate.** A band prior (coherent, learnable, ~100 classes of ~28 train examples matching the true structure's 97×~29) groks in only 1/15 runs — statistically indistinguishable from the random partition (0/20; Fisher exact p=0.43) and decisively worse than true structure (22/30; p=2.3×10⁻⁵). This confirms the companion's discriminating prediction: sibling structure (same Fourier features, wrong combination) preserves grokking at 14/15, while band structure (foreign features) abolishes it.
- **A label-free invariance prior suffices for acceleration.** The commutativity prior (positives are commuted pairs only, no answer info, sparse — only 31% of anchors have a partner in the train split) generalizes 15/15 at median 2.7× speedup, more reliable than the supervised true-structure prior itself (22/30; p=0.038).
- **Strongest tested method is label-free + norm clamp.** Commutativity prior combined with the companion's weight-norm clamp (‖W‖=45) gives 5/5 grokking at median 17× speedup (sub-1,000-epoch on some seeds), using no label information beyond the task loss — a proof-of-concept that self-supervised representational priors work.
- **A brief early window captures the benefit.** Applying SupCon only during epochs [0,2k] (4% of the 50k budget) yields 10/10 at 2.7×, beating continuous application (8/10, 1.25×) and a duration-matched late window [2k,4k] (2.1×), removing the wall-clock objection to the O(n²) contrastive term.
- **The delay-law exponent is flattened ~17× by structure injection.** Plain cross-entropy slows 31× per +10 weight-norm units (a lower bound — high-norm cells are fully censored), versus only 1.22× with the prior; the exponent, not just a constant offset, is conditional on the representational forces present.

## Methodology

- **System (inherited from companion):** one-layer transformer (no biases; no LayerNorm except where stated), full-batch AdamW (lr 10⁻³, weight decay 1.0), task (a+b) mod 97 with a 30% train split. Auxiliary loss λ·L_SupCon on the final-position representation (τ=0.1, 64-d projection), λ∈{0.1, 0.3, 1.0}.
- **Generalization time t_gen:** first epoch with test accuracy ≥ 0.95 sustained. Budget 50,000 epochs; censored runs scored at budget.
- **Two new priors:**
  - *Band structure (content control):* positives share magnitude-band pair (⌊a/10⌋, ⌊b/10⌋) — ~100 classes of ~28 train examples. Coherent and learnable via threshold features, but not expressible by the periodic Fourier features the circuit uses.
  - *Commutativity (supervision control):* positives are commuted pairs {(a,b),(b,a)} only — no answer info; because commuted pairs share their sum, this is a sparse label-free sample of the true equivalence structure.
- **Arms:** baseline, true-structure, sibling-structure ((a−b) mod p), and random-partition reused from companion for same-seed pairing; all other arms new.
- **Windowing (Rung 3):** prior applied only within chosen epoch ranges ([0,2k], [0,5k], [2k,4k], [2k,10k]) vs continuous vs λ-annealing, with a norm-clamp brown reference.
- **Clamp-value sweep (Rung 4):** total weight norm clamped to constants 35–70 with/without the prior to map the delay law (per Truong et al. 2026).
- **Generality:** modular multiplication, modular subtraction, small sparse parity; 2-layer depth, MLP, and pre-LN variants; synthetic subject–verb-agreement grammars (attractor + conjunction) as a boundary probe.
- **Statistical reporting:** Fisher exact tests between categorical cells; categorical outcomes emphasized over means throughout; 188 new runs on a single RTX 3080, all numbers macro-generated and byte-verified.

## Results & Highlights

- **Content gate (Rung 1):** band prior 1/15 grok vs random partition 0/20 (Fisher p=0.43, n.s.) vs true structure 22/30 (p=2.3×10⁻⁵); sibling (same features) preserves 14/15. Coherence + learnability are therefore not sufficient — feature-family alignment decides.
- **Label-free acceleration (Rung 2):** commutativity prior 15/15 at median 2.7× speedup vs 0/20 random (p=3.1×10⁻¹⁰), and more reliable than the supervised prior (22/30; p=0.038). Commutativity arms' median peak weight norm is 103 vs 130 for supervised (baseline ~57) — a reduction, not absence, of the norm-inflation side-effect.
- **Best method (Rung 2):** commutativity + ‖W‖=45 clamp → 5/5 at median **17× speedup**, sub-1,000-epoch generalization on some seeds, with no label info.
- **Early window (Rung 3):** [0,2k] (4% of budget) → 10/10 at 2.7×, beating continuous (8/10, 1.25×), λ-annealing (1.7×), and duration-matched late window [2k,4k] (10/10, 2.1×). The longer late window [2k,10k] is reliable but slower (1.9×).
- **Multiplication generality (Rung 4):** baseline 5/5 (median t_gen 19,700); random 0/5; unmitigated prior 4/5 with drag (20,900); prior+clamp 5/5 at 2,250 → median **9.8× speedup**.
- **Subtraction (Rung 4):** true-structure prior groks seeds whose baselines never do (4/5 vs 3/5) — the prior's *whether*-power in a new form.
- **Architecture replication (Rung 4):** unmitigated stall replicates at depth 2 (1/3 vs baseline 3/3) and under pre-LN (2/5 vs 5/5); norm clamp repairs both (3/3 at 2,150; 5/5 at 1,850). LayerNorm alone shortens baseline delay (median 6,250 vs ~20,000 without LN).
- **Delay-law exponent (Rung 4, Fig. 2):** plain CE slope 0.344 per unit norm = **31× per +10** (lower bound, high-norm cells censored by +20); prior slope 0.020 = **1.22× per +10** → ~17× flattening of the exponent. Both share a low-norm floor below ~40–45; below the floor (~35–40) the relationship inverts (prior stalls where CE is fast).
- **Honest boundary (Rung 5):** synthetic subject–verb grammars never grok — they generalize immediately (median peak 0.90 by epoch 500) then degrade (median final 0.73); the prior lifts levels (peak 0.95, final 0.76) and roughly doubles number-clustering but does not change the degradation trend. The account applies to the *memorize-first regime*.

## Critical Assessment

The paper's intellectual honesty is its defining feature and is woven into the experimental design rather than confined to a limitations section. Three points stand out as paper-specific:

1. **It runs its own discriminating controls against its own companion's account.** The band-vs-sibling contrast is a clean two-alternative test: a *coherence* account predicts band ≈ sibling; a *feature-family* account predicts band ≈ random. The data (band 1/15, sibling 14/15, random 0/20) decisively confirm the feature-family account — and the author notes the one band run that *did* grok, framing the block as "pushing the transition far beyond budget" rather than an absolute wall.
2. **It publicly corrects the companion's accelerator emphasis.** Section 8 states plainly that at the optimal clamp (~45) plain cross-entropy is as fast as the prior — "the speed of low-norm training belongs to norm control alone" — and reframes the prior's causal contribution as *robustness to the norm setting* (the flattened exponent), not raw speed at a hand-tuned optimum. This is a meaningful concession that strengthens, not weakens, the account.
3. **It pre-registers and reports the boundary where the story fails.** Rung 5 shows synthetic grammar tasks generalize *before* memorizing and therefore have no delay for a prior to control; the prior lifts accuracy levels (peak 0.90→0.95) but cannot prevent later degradation. This scopes the claim precisely to the "memorize-first regime" rather than overclaiming.

The weakest link is external validity: "feature-family alignment" is operationalized only where the circuit's feature family is known (Fourier features for modular arithmetic), and the label-free win hinges on an *exact* task-family invariance (commutativity). Whether approximate, noisy invariances from natural data carry enough feature-level alignment is acknowledged as the key open question but is entirely untested here.

## Strengths

- Five cleanly-separated axes (content, supervision, timing, setting, boundary), each with a purpose-built control and a matched comparator.
- The label-free commutativity result is the practical highlight: it converts a supervised intervention into one with a plausible path to real use via augmentation/symmetry-based positives.
- Quantitative delay-law mapping (Fig. 2) gives a measurable, falsifiable form to "structure injection neutralizes the norm timescale" — a ~17× exponent flattening, not a vague claim.
- Early-window result removes the practical O(n²) objection to contrastive priors (4% of training).
- Exceptional reproducibility plumbing (byte-verified number regeneration) and disciplined statistics.

## Limitations (as stated by the author, plus assessment)

- All positive results are within algorithmic tasks where the generalizing circuit's feature family is known; "feature-family alignment" is operationalized via that knowledge, and its analogue in natural data is untested.
- The commutativity prior exploits an *exact* task-family invariance; noisy or approximate invariances are untested.
- Window boundaries (2k/5k/10k) were chosen a priori, not finely swept; the "early advantage" is a median tendency with seed-level exceptions.
- The CE exponent is a lower bound due to censoring.
- LayerNorm findings apply to a pre-LN variant without final LN.
- Sequence-task conclusions rest on two synthetic grammars at one scale.
- Sample sizes 3–15 per cell; single prime (97) and single training fraction (30%).
- *(Assessment)* Single author, no peer review indicated, and the entire program is confined to a one-layer transformer on mod-p arithmetic — the mechanism may be real without transferring to deeper models or non-algorithmic data.

## Relevance

本文对"grokking/机制可解释性/表征学习"方向的研究者最有价值。它不是又一个刷 SOTA 的工作，而是把"表征先验为何有效"这一因果问题拆成五个可证伪的子问题逐一检验，并给出了可量化、可复现的答案（特征族对齐、无标签不变性、关键早窗口、范数律指数）。对关注自监督学习、对比学习、对称性/增广作为归纳偏置、以及训练动力学（memorization-vs-generalization）的研究者，这篇论文提供了一个干净的算法任务试验台和一套可直接借鉴的实验设计范式（同种子配对 + Fisher 检验 + 字节级数字复现 + 预注册边界）。其"在已知最优范数下纯 CE 与先验一样快"这一诚实更正，也提醒后续工作区分"在手动调好的点上提速"与"对超参稳健"两件事。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can approximate/noisy invariances (e.g., augmentations on natural images, or approximate symmetries in language) supply enough feature-level alignment to reproduce the label-free acceleration, or does the result hinge on the *exactness* of commutativity?
- Is there an automatic way to discover the relevant feature family of an unknown circuit, so that "feature-family alignment" becomes measurable rather than assumed?
- What is the mechanism behind the early-window advantage at the representation level — do the class clusters seeded in [0,2k] literally guide subsequent Fourier-circuit formation, and can this be verified with progress measures (à la Nanda et al. 2023)?
- Does the ~17× exponent flattening hold for deeper transformers and for non-algorithmic grokking (cf. Omnigrok, Liu et al. 2023)?
- In the inversion regime below the norm floor (~35–40), why does the contrastive gradient interfere with the lean circuit — is this a general feature of contrastive losses at very low capacity?
- Could the prior act as a regularizer (rather than an accelerator) in the immediate-generalization regime, and would that change the degradation trend seen in the synthetic grammar tasks?
