01 Methodology

Reframe PPO's critic as a loss predictor by making the reward a monotonic function of absolute prediction error ξ = |ŷ − y|.

  • MDP Token-level MDP; terminal reward R(s_T) computed from the actor's extracted numeric estimate. PPO + GAE with γ = λ = 1.0 (RLVR setup).
  • CARE reward R(s_T) = h(ξ), monotonically decreasing in error.
    Linear: −ξ — dense, unbounded, preserves error magnitude.
    Gaussian: α·exp(−ξ²/2σ²) — bounded [0,α], σ = 10 g (matches NutriBench's ~7.5 g tolerance), α = 8.
  • Critic = confidence Value loss L_V = E[(V_φ(s_t) − R̂_t)²]. Because R tracks error, the critic converges to the actor's expected loss — read directly as a confidence score at inference, no extra training stage.
  • Training VERL framework, single 8-GPU node; 8 rollouts/prompt; actor LR 1e-6, critic LR 1e-5; 5 epochs. Actor & critic both initialized from Qwen-3 4B / 8B instruct.
  • Metrics MAE (accuracy, ↓) and AUSE — Area Under Sparsification Error, the gap between confidence-ranked and oracle-ranked error curves (confidence alignment / selective-prediction utility, ↓).

03 Strengths

  • Novel concept. Unifies loss-prediction uncertainty theory with the PPO critic — a training-time confidence signal, no probe / calibration / verbalization.
  • Consistent gains. AUSE improvements hold across 2 tasks × 2 scales × {in-distribution, OOD}.
  • Robust by design. Favored under language shift, domain shift, and IFEval task-awareness vs. SFT.
  • Deployment-friendly. 2.4% inference overhead — one extra critic forward pass.
  • Honest ablations. σ-sweep, critic-distribution evolution, missing-prediction handling, and the Linear-vs-Gaussian trade-off are all reported.

04 Limitations

  • Scale capped at 8B; only two domains (nutrition, appliances/electronics pricing).
  • CARE-PPO-Linear overfits tasks (up to 29.2% on IFEval) — Gaussian is the practical pick.
  • SFT wins raw MAE — value is conditional on needing confidence.
  • Critic cherry-picked at best-AUSE checkpoint; no ECE / Brier calibration metrics.
  • Training cost of a full second critic model not quantified for parity.
  • PPO-specific — no GRPO / REINFORCE comparison.
  • Relies on GPT-4o-mini for synthetic data & LLM-as-judge (unaudited teacher bias).

05 Questions for further exploration

  1. Can the critic signal plug straight into abstention / deferral (reject or defer below threshold)? Only AUSE is reported — no end-to-end "selective accuracy vs. coverage" curve.
  2. Does the insight transfer to GRPO (no explicit critic)? Reintroduce a value head, or approximate expected loss via a group-relative baseline?
  3. At 32B / 70B, does critic alignment improve with scale or degrade via reward hacking / overconfidence?
  4. Extend the reward from MAE to quantile / interval prediction, so the critic emits a confidence interval rather than a single score?
  5. Training-cost parity: what is the real extra memory/compute vs. a single-model + post-hoc probe? LoRA critic or shared backbone as an approximation?
  6. Can the Linear variant's task-overfit be fixed with a relative missing-prediction penalty (−h(ξ_max) instead of a flat −500)?
  7. Under extreme drift (unseen languages, zero-shot categories) critic AUSE still rises in absolute terms — is there a second-order "confidence-about-the-confidence" warning signal?
