> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一个极简的"One-Word Census"工具——用 31 个单轮"命名一个类别词"提示对 44 个语言模型各问 4 次，用留一法"答案选择惊奇度"(answer-choice surprisal, 比特)量化每个模型对群体共识的从众程度，发现从众性在 1.05–3.21 比特间变化超 4 倍：轻量后训练/人格化模型最发散，最新旗舰模型最从众，但 Claude 与 GPT 最新旗舰出现"反向"发散信号；模型整体比人类更集中（模态答案占 66% vs 人类 36%），连"发散"本身也是收敛的（偏离模态时会落到同一个亚军答案）。

> **TL;DR:** The paper introduces the "One-Word Census" — 31 single-turn "name a category" prompts (e.g. "Name a tree") asked 4× each to 44 LLMs across 12+ labs, scored by a cheap leave-one-out answer-choice surprisal metric (bits, no embeddings/judge, ~$1/model). It finds conformity spans 1.05–3.21 bits (>4×): lightly post-trained/persona-tuned models (Hermes 4 70B at 3.21) diverge most while newest flagships (Claude Sonnet 5 at 1.05, GPT-5 at 1.32) conform most — except the latest Claude/GPT premium tiers reverse the trend. Rankings are robust (LOFO ρ=0.985), no pairwise affinity survives depth-conditioning (0/946 pairs), and the field is more concentrated than humans in 18/20 categories (modal answer 66% vs 36%).

## 核心贡献

1. **一个廉价、机械、可逐模型计算的从众性分数（answer-choice surprisal）**：留一法定义每个模型答案在"其余全场模型汇总答案"下的 −log₂ 概率（加一平滑），无需 embedding、无需 LLM 裁判、无需人工标注；整套电池约 120 次 API 调用（约 1 美元/模型），可在每次发布时复跑。
2. **44 个模型的记分卡与结构化差异**：从众性跨度 1.05–3.21 比特；最发散的是轻量后训练/人格化/检索接地模型，最从众的是最新主力旗舰。
3. **代际轨迹分析**：在 Claude、GPT、Qwen、Grok 四大家族内，后训练越深的发布越从众；但最新旗舰 Claude（Fable 5）与 GPT-5.6 高端层级出现趋势反转。
4. **鲁棒性验证**：排名在留一家族法（LOFO ρ=0.985）、平衡子集、随机子集下均稳定；三层级成对亲和性检验后 0/946 对存活。
5. **"亚军共识"现象**：即使模型偏离模态答案，也会压倒性地落到同一个亚军答案（如非 ketchup 的调味品中 mustard 占 95%）——"发散本身也是收敛的"。
6. **与人类基线对比**：对照 Battig–Montague / Van Overschelde 类别产出常模，模型场在 20 个共享类别中 18 个比人类更集中。

## Key Contributions

- **A mechanical per-model conformity instrument**: "answer-choice surprisal" — a leave-one-out, add-one-smoothed −log₂ probability of a model's answers under the pooled answers of all other models. No embeddings, no LLM judge, no human labels; the full battery (~120 calls) costs ~$1 per model, making it cheap enough to run at every release.
- **A 44-model scorecard revealing structured variation**: conformity ranges 1.05–3.21 bits (a >4× factor in answer likelihood); the divergent tail is persona-/community-tuned (Hermes 4 70B, WizardLM-2, Mixtral, MythoMax) while the conformist tail is the newest mainline flagships (Claude Sonnet 5, Claude Opus 4.8, Grok 4.5, Ernie 4.5, Qwen3 235B, GPT-5).
- **Generational trajectories**: within Claude, GPT, Qwen, Grok, later releases are more conformist — but the latest premium tiers at both Anthropic (Fable 5) and OpenAI (GPT-5.6 Sol) reverse the decline, a possible early "repositioning" signal.
- **Robustness checks using no new API calls**: leave-one-family-out (ρ=0.985), balanced per-family fields, random 15-model subsets, and an era-stratified reference field (ρ=0.992) all preserve the rankings.
- **A three-level pairwise-affinity ladder** that dissolves all 946 model pairs once a one-dimensional "depth propensity" is conditioned away (0/946 survive) — and the "runner-up consensus" finding that divergence itself converges.
- **Calibration against human category-production norms**: the field is more concentrated than people in 18/20 shared categories.

## Methodology

**Stimulus (§3.1).** 31 frozen single-turn prompts: 30 of the form "Name a[n] XX. Reply with one word only." (color, animal, fruit, vegetable, city, country, flower, sport, musical instrument, bird, gemstone, tree, beverage, insect, occupation, language, dessert, tool, fish, metal, fabric, herb, dance, hobby, condiment, cheese, dinosaur, mythical creature, board game, emotion) plus a 31st unconstrained prompt "Pick a word." No system prompt; fresh single-turn conversation; `max_tokens=1024`; requested temperature 1.0 for every model.

**Panel (§3.5).** 44 models served via OpenRouter, spanning US frontier labs across generations (GPT-3.5 → GPT-5.6 including all three same-day GPT-5.6 tiers Luna/Terra/Sol; Claude 3 → Claude 5 including Sonnet 5, Opus 4.8, Fable 5; Gemini 2.5–3.5; Grok 4.20 → 4.5), Chinese labs (DeepSeek, Qwen, GLM, Kimi, Hunyuan, Ernie), enterprise/search models (Command A, Palmyra, Granite, Sonar), persona/community-tuned models (Hermes 4, WizardLM-2, MythoMax), and small open models (Mixtral, Llama, Gemma). Queried 4× per prompt in July 2026.

**Metric (§3.2).** For model m, category c, with field F_{−m,c} the multiset of all other models' answers:

s(a) = −log₂[(count_{F}(a)+1) / (|F|+V_c)],  V_c = |support(F) ∪ support(A_m)|

A model's headline score = mean of s(a) over all its valid answers across categories (informally the "Mustard Quotient"), measured in bits. Three companion statistics separate *ways* of being different: **modal avoidance** (fraction differing from field's modal answer), **novel rate** (fraction no other model ever gave), and **self-distinctness** (distinct answers / samples within model and category, doubling as a proxy for effective sampling temperature).

**Normalization (§3.3).** Replies reduced to final alphabetic word, lowercased, stripped; a junk guard treats chat-template artifacts, >15-word replies, bare acknowledgments, and single-letter tokens as failed cells. 5439/5456 cells (99.7%) yield a valid answer; only 13 valid cells contain >1 word. Uncertainty via bootstrap 90% CI (2000 draws) resampling categories.

**Design rationale (§3.4).** The datum is the *discrete choice*, not phrasing — making the metric verbosity-immune and avoiding the form/content conflation. Cost: measures "choice character," not tone/style/discourse.

## Results & Highlights

- **Extreme modal convergence (§4.1).** In 7 of 31 categories a single answer takes ≥80% of *all* answers across all 44 models: `hammer` 94%, `oak` 94%, `rose` 91%, `carrot` 90%, `basil` 89%, `cheddar` 86%, `salmon` 82%. On the unconstrained "Pick a word" prompt, `serendipity` takes 71/174 = **41%** of answers drawn from the entire English lexicon (52 distinct words total).
- **Conformity spans 1.05–3.21 bits (>4×, §4.2).** Most divergent: Hermes 4 70B **3.21 bits** (21% novel answers, 58% modal avoidance), WizardLM-2 2.93, Mixtral 8x22B 2.75, MythoMax 2.36. Persona/community group averages 2.83 bits vs 1.72 (US frontier), 1.68 (Chinese labs), 1.61 (enterprise). Most conformist: Claude Sonnet 5 **1.05**, Claude Opus 4.8 1.25, Grok 4.5 1.26, Ernie 4.5 1.29, Qwen3 235B 1.29, GPT-5 1.32. Sonnet 5/Opus 4.8/Ernie/GPT-5 all have **novel rate 0%**; Sonnet 5 avoids the modal answer on only 19% of answers.
- **Within-lab dissociations (§4.2).** Claude Fable 5 (same lab, same month as Sonnet 5) scores 1.71 vs Sonnet 5's 1.05 — a 0.65-bit separation, paired permutation p<0.001 — driven by stable off-modal defaults (gouda, mustard, mango, 4/4 runs) not sampling noise. GPT-5.6 tiers replicate it: Luna 1.52 < Terra 1.86 < Sol 2.02 (Sol vs Luna p=0.004).
- **Generational declines then reversal (§4.3).** Claude mainline declines 3-Haiku 2.11 → Sonnet-5 1.05; Grok declines steepest 4.20 1.88 → 4.5 1.26; Qwen 1.94 → 1.29; GPT declines through 4.1 (1.61) into 5-era (~1.32–1.39) then **reverses at 5.6** (Luna 1.52 < Terra 1.86 < Sol 2.02). On the most concentrated categories (modal share ≥80%), newest mainline flagships average **0.36 bits** (near the theoretical floor) vs 1.07 bits for each family's oldest member.
- **Robustness (§4.4).** Leave-one-family-out Spearman **ρ=0.985**; era-stratified reference field ρ=0.992 with Sonnet 5 last in every draw; balanced-field draws keep top-3 rank 1–3 and Sonnet 5 rank 44 in every draw.
- **No pairwise affinity + runner-up consensus (§4.5).** After a three-level ladder conditioning on avoidance then depth propensity, **0 of 946 pairs survive** (FDR q=0.05). Yet off-modal answers re-concentrate: `mustard` takes **95%** of non-ketchup condiment answers, `broccoli` 83% of non-carrot, `mango` 81% of non-apple, `gouda` 71% of non-cheddar.
- **More concentrated than humans (§4.6).** Against Van Overschelde norms, the field is more concentrated in **18/20** categories; modal answer averages **66%** of the field vs **36%** of human first responses. `oak` is 94% of model field but only 31% of human first responses. The one substantive reversal is `country` (humans default to their own country 66% USA; models spread across canada/japan/france, avoiding the "answer with edges").
- **DeepSeek natural experiment (§4.3, §7).** Same V3 base, different post-training: v3-0324 scores 1.43 (0% novel, 4/4 identical in 23/31 categories) while v3.2 jumps to **2.63** (10% novel, the panel's only frontier-lab explorer) — attributing the conformist/explorer gap to post-base-training choices, not pretraining or scale.

## Critical Assessment

The paper's central methodological bet is that **discrete one-word choice is a clean, recomputable datum** that sidesteps the form/content conflation that plagues diversity metrics (Tevet & Berant). This is both the instrument's main strength and its bounded scope: it measures "choice character" and explicitly cannot reach tone, style, discourse structure, or multi-turn conduct — a boundary the author marks with the GPT-4o heirloom case (whose community attachment plausibly routes through long-form style, not distinctive choices). The author is unusually disciplined about confounds: size, recency, and post-training intensity co-vary, and the generational result is explicitly "a pattern measured at one date, not a designed longitudinal experiment."

The most consequential single finding — that the DeepSeek v3-0324 → v3.2 gap (same base, different post-training) registers on the metric *in the direction and on the exact quantity (tail mass / novel rate) the mechanism literature predicts* — is presented with mandatory hedges (v3.2 also has synthetic reasoning ancestry yet is the explorer, so the recipe around the synthetic data dominates; the roster cannot separate post-training intensity from synthetic-data use). The "heirloom models" section (§5) is the most speculative, openly acknowledged as "a retrospective association selected on its outcome" that shows correlation, not forecasting. The "frictionless prototype" reading (the mode is the blandest *unambiguous* exemplar, e.g. `orange` and `tomato` vanish) is supported by anecdote but the author flags the need for a quantitative corpus-frequency baseline to reject the simpler "models echo frequency" null.

## Strengths

- **Mechanical reproducibility**: exact-match on normalized tokens means any reader can recompute every number from the public transcripts with no model in the loop — a genuine methodological advance over judge-based or embedding-based diversity metrics.
- **Cost and cadence**: ~$1/model and ~120 API calls make it feasible to run on every release, directly addressing the longitudinal-measurement gap Chen et al. (2023) identified and that nobody subsequently filled.
- **Per-model scorecard**: produces a ranking, not just a population-level statement, enabling the within-lab dissociation findings (Fable vs Sonnet; GPT-5.6 tier spread) that a population-level metric could not reveal.
- **Honest scope and limitations**: the §6 limitations section is thorough (snapshot, sampling opacity, panel relativity, construct scope, roster confounds, characterization-not-measurement), and hedges are placed inline where claims are made.
- **Conceptually sharp findings**: the "runner-up consensus" (divergence converges), the "frictionless not frequent" mode, and the production glimpse of the synthetic-data loop via DeepSeek are individually memorable and collectively build a coherent argument.

## Limitations

- **Snapshot, single channel**: all data from one window (July 2026) via OpenRouter; routing was not always pinned (a DeepSeek subset had to be re-run with pinned hosts to confirm the v3-0324/v3.2 contrast reproduces).
- **Sampling opacity**: requested temperature 1.0 is not honored uniformly across providers, and the main run did not log which provider served each call; self-distinctness is therefore a property of the model-as-served, not a fixed sampling temperature.
- **Panel relativity**: bit values are relative to this 44-model field by construction; only rankings and pooled-distribution facts "travel." The panel is an availability sample, not usage-weighted (though usage weighting would sharpen, not weaken, the headline contrasts).
- **Low power for pairwise tests**: 31 categories × 4 samples means weak affinities would be invisible; adjacent scorecard ranks have broadly overlapping CIs, so only tiers/tails/trajectories are reliably ordered.
- **English-only construct**: category prototypes are culturally situated; whether `oak`/`serendipity` are properties of English training data or of the models is untested. Multi-turn behavior, tone, and style are out of scope by construction.
- **Confounded roster**: cannot separate model size from generation, nor post-training intensity from synthetic-data usage (the two newest-flagship explanations co-vary).
- **Cannot measure the most heirloomed models** (Claude 3 Opus, Claude 3.5 Sonnet, Mixtral 8x7B) because they are already unavailable through the serving channel — itself an argument for running such batteries at launch.

## Relevance

本文对以下几类读者高度相关：（1）**模型评测与多样性研究者**——它提供了一个可比 NoveltyBench / Artificial Hivemind 更廉价、更机械的纵向监测工具，建议在每次模型发布时复跑以追踪"答案空间从众性"这一能力基准看不见的属性。（2）**后训练 / RLHF 方向研究者**——代际轨迹（更新=更从众）与 DeepSeek 自然实验直接呼应 Kirk et al.、Janus、KL-regularized RLHF 的 mode-collapse 机制文献，并为"diversity-aware post-training"是否进入生产提供了一个可检测的转折信号。（3）**关注模型训练数据反馈循环 / 知识坍缩的研究者**——DeepSeek v3-0324（蒸馏推理兄弟模型输出）的 tail-less 分布（0% novel）是合成数据循环在生产环境的一个自然实验，呼应 Shumailov et al. 与 Guo et al. 的模拟结论。（4）**关心 AI 对创意与信息生态影响的人**——"群体是服务用户的单位，咨询三个助手≈从近同一分布抽三个样本"这一论点对内容创作、搜索多样性与认知风险有直接政策含义。对普通 LLM 应用开发者，本文提醒：在需要真实多样性的场景（头脑风暴、采样、推荐多样性）中，多家模型并不能提供去相关信号。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **纵向复跑**：将这套冻结电池在每个新发布上复跑，能否检测到 diversity-aware post-training 进入生产的"转折点"？GPT-5.6 与 Fable 5 的反转是否会在下一代持续？
2. **基础模型 vs 指令模型对照**：在相同权重上对比 base 与 instruct 版本（需本地权重，因聚合渠道不提供 base 模型），能否把 DeepSeek 自然实验升级为设计实验，定位崩溃发生在后训练的哪一步？
3. **语料频率基线**：用大规模语料频率逐类别量化拒绝"模型只是回显频率"这一零假设——`orange`、`tomato` 这类高频率但近缺失的案例是否系统性出现于有同形/分类歧义的词上？
4. **logits 直读**：对开源权重模型直接从输出 logits 读取答案分布，而非用 4 次采样估计——能否消除采样不透明混淆，并揭示权重中"潜伏但未被检索"的 tail（如 `orange`、`tomato`）？
5. **跨语言版本**：同一电池翻译为中文等其他语言，`oak`、`serendipity` 这类模态答案是英语训练数据的属性还是模型的属性？中文模型的模态答案是否落到不同的原型（如"松树""苹果"）？
6. **从众性与谄媚是否同源**：在同一组模型上做"植入立场"电池，测试答案选择从众性与 sycophancy 是否共享机制——两者都是"给出会被批准的答案"的两个面向。
7. **亚军共识的成因**：为何偏离模态时会落到同一个亚军（mustard、broccoli、mango）？这是排序后的第二原型效应，还是后训练对"次典型"的系统性偏好？
8. **多轮与开放域外推**：将工具扩展到多轮对话、tone/style/discourse 层面，能否解释 GPT-4o 这类"tone-driven 祖传模型"的社区依恋——即本工具明确不测的那部分？
