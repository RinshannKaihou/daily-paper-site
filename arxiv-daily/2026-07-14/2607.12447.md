> **一句话总结：** 本文借用计算神经科学中的"统计决策置信度"(SDC)框架，证明在简单感知与记忆任务上，多模态大模型(Gemma 3/4, Qwen2.5-VL, Gemini Flash 3)的答案 logit 差(LD)并非仅仅是经验性"偏好分数"，而是作为潜在决策变量的单调读出，原则上足以计算"答对的"后验概率；在更复杂的 CLEVR 视觉推理任务上该框架的可诊断性失效。

> **TL;DR:** Using the **statistical decision confidence (SDC)** framework from computational neuroscience, the authors test on **three perceptual tasks + a memory task** across **three non-reasoning multimodal LLMs (Qwen2.5-VL 7B, Gemma 3 12B, Gemma 4 12B) and one reasoning model (Gemini Flash 3)** whether the **answer-logit difference (LD)** is a heuristic preference score or a readout of a latent decision variable sufficient to compute the normative posterior confidence. LD satisfies all four SDC signatures (including the diagnostic folded-X correct/error pattern) in simple perceptual/memory tasks, drives near-optimal abstention (raising committed-response accuracy from 92.2% to 99.95%), but the full SDC geometry fails to appear on CLEVR — delineating the boundary where explicit normative process models are unavailable.

## 核心贡献

1. **把"置信度是什么"这个问题首次系统性引入 LLM 研究。** 以往工作只问"答案 logit 能否预测正确性/是否校准"，本文追问更深一层的"computational basis"问题：logit 差到底是不是潜在决策变量的忠实读出（readout of a latent decision variable），还是只是经验上能预测正确性的启发式偏好分数？

2. **从计算神经科学借用 SDC 框架并迁移到 LLM。** 引入 Kepecs/Hangya/Sanders 等人的统计决策置信度理论的四个定性 signature（psychometric function、signed-evidence encoding、固定难度下的 trial-specific 正确性预测、folded-X correct/error 几何），将其作为可证伪的诊断工具。

3. **解决"确定性模型无 trial-to-trial 噪声"的方法论难题。** 用"相同刺激强度、不同 nuisance 特征"的多张刺激图（类比 random-dot motion coherence）来产生与生物感官噪声等价的潜在决策变量变异，使签名 (3)(4) 在确定性 VLM 上可被检验。

4. **将框架推广到记忆决策与复杂视觉推理。** 在 city-population 记忆任务中即便 psychometric 很浅(pseudo-R² 仅 0.069)folded-X 仍清晰可见；在 CLEVR 上 |LD| 仍携带超出图像难度/场景属性的正确性信息，但 folded-X 不再出现，划定了框架的当前适用边界。

5. **行为后果：abstention 是基于内部置信信号而非外部难度的近最优策略。**

## Key Contributions

- Reframes LLM confidence research from "does it predict correctness / is it calibrated?" to the deeper question of *what the signal represents computationally* — heuristic preference vs. monotonic readout of a sufficient-statistic latent decision variable.
- Imports the **statistical decision confidence (SDC)** framework (Kepecs et al. 2008; Hangya et al. 2016; Sanders et al. 2016) from computational neuroscience and adapts its four qualitative signatures to deterministic multimodal LLMs via *stimulus-exemplar variability* (analogous to random-dot-motion noise resampling) in lieu of sensory noise.
- Demonstrates empirically across **4 models × multiple tasks** that the answer-logit difference (LD = ℓ_A − ℓ_B) satisfies all four SDC signatures in simple perceptual and memory-based decisions.
- Provides a behavioural-consequence demonstration: the same LD signal governs a near-optimal second-stage abstention policy that lifts committed-trial accuracy to near ceiling.
- Cleanly delineates the framework's boundary: on **CLEVR** complex visual reasoning the folded-X disappears, and the paper argues this is *not* evidence against SDC but a consequence of the missing experimenter-defined normative process model — a principled, not defeatist, limitation.

## Methodology

**Normative model (SDC).** The experimenter defines a signed stimulus-evidence variable Δ. The ideal observer encodes it with internal noise to produce the latent decision variable **d = Δ + ε**. Sign(d) determines choice; |d| determines confidence. Confidence is defined *normatively* as the posterior P(correct | d). Because d is a sufficient statistic, any monotonic readout LD = f(d) inherits four testable qualitative signatures:

1. **Psychometric function** — choice probability monotonically tracks signed Δ.
2. **Signed evidence** — signed LD varies monotonically (near-linearly) with signed Δ.
3. **Trial-specific confidence** — within fixed stimulus-strength bins, larger |LD| predicts higher correctness (the key test separating readout from mere difficulty encoding).
4. **Folded-X pattern** — as stimulus strength increases, |LD| rises on correct trials but *falls* on error trials (because strong-stimulus errors require the noisy decision variable to just barely cross the boundary).

**Overcoming determinism in VLMs.** Deterministic models produce identical logits for identical inputs, so the trial-to-trial variability needed for signatures 3–4 is supplied by generating *multiple stimulus exemplars per strength level* with randomized nuisance features (independent noise realizations, positional jitter).

**Tasks.**
- *Perceptual:* contrast discrimination (band-pass filtered noise patches), size discrimination (two circles), shape categorization (round vs. spiky) — each with a signed, experimenter-controlled stimulus axis.
- *Memory:* city-population comparison, 5,000 cities (pop > 1M) → 50,000 binary pairs from SimpleMaps; "stimulus strength" = log₂ population ratio (a noisy proxy).
- *Complex reasoning:* CLEVR count-equality and object-existence questions degraded by parametric Gaussian blur (σ); blur removes information rather than favouring an answer, so no signed evidence axis.

**Analysis.** Pooled logistic regressions controlling for stimulus-strength bin (signature 3: correct ~ C(|stimulus|, bin) + |LD|); interaction regression |LD| ~ |Δ| × Correct (signature 4); nested-model AUROC/likelihood-ratio tests; temperature-scaled calibration σ(|LD|/T) with ECE/Brier/AUROC reported on 2,000 held-out trials per task. Reasoning model Gemini Flash 3 read out via a **90% reasoning-cut** trick (truncate the CoT trace at 90%, then ask for the answer) to avoid logit saturation — verified not to change accuracy (p > 0.1).

## Results & Highlights

1. **All four SDC signatures hold for Qwen2.5-VL 7B on every perceptual task.** Fixed-strength correctness prediction is large and significant (contrast β = 4.12, z = 53.42, p < 10⁻³⁰⁰); the folded-X interaction is strongly positive (contrast correct slope +8.08 vs. error slope −1.11). Adding |LD| to a stimulus-strength model lifts AUROC from 0.864 → 0.907 (all LRT p < 10⁻¹⁰⁰).

2. **Replication across 3 further models.** Gemma 3 12B, Gemma 4 12B, and reasoning model Gemini Flash 3 reproduce the within-strength prediction and folded-X in essentially every model–task cell, with a single exception (Gemma 3 contrast error branch is flat, not falling — neutral, not falsifying).

3. **Abstention is near-optimal and LD-driven.** When allowed to abstain unless ≥60% confident, Qwen abstained on **87.7%** of contrast trials, raising committed-response accuracy from a baseline **92.2% to 99.95%**, while abstained-trial answers stayed at 91.2%. The model's policy overlapped a simple |LD| threshold on **91.6%** of trials; the optimal threshold improved accuracy by only **0.05 percentage points**. |LD| predicted abstention (AUROC 0.840) better than stimulus strength (0.796).

4. **Memory task: shallow psychometric, sharp folded-X.** City-population accuracy only 61.8% (pseudo-R² = 0.069), yet |LD| still predicted correctness within fixed bins (odds ratio = 1.36, p < 10⁻¹⁸⁰) and produced the full folded-X. Gemma 3 12B showed an even sharper dissociation (error branch −0.589 vs. correct branch +0.678).

5. **CLEVR boundary case.** |LD| continued to carry correctness information beyond blur and scene/question controls (nested-model AUROC gains **+0.029 to +0.072**), but error-trial slopes were flat or modestly positive — never the negative branch required for the canonical folded-X. The paper interprets this not as a failure of SDC but as a consequence of CLEVR lacking a known signed-evidence axis and process model.

## Critical Assessment

The paper's central intellectual move is to shift the LLM-confidence debate from an *empirical* criterion ("does it predict correctness?") to a *computational-representational* one ("is it a readout of a sufficient-statistic decision variable?"). This is well-motivated and rigorously executed. Three observations worth flagging:

- **The folded-X is genuinely diagnostic, but only within a specified regime.** The paper is admirably explicit (citing Adler & Ma 2018) that the signatures are robust only for boundary-defined, non-overlapping response categories, and that they cannot distinguish the **Bayesian Confidence Hypothesis (BCH)** from **Confidence in Raw Evidence Space (CRES)** because the single signed-strength axis varies evidence and reliability together. The authors honestly concede that adjudicating BCH vs. CRES requires *independently* manipulating stimulus strength and reliability — a clean, important, and currently open direction.

- **The "deterministic-model noise" workaround is the methodological lynchpin.** Substituting stimulus-exemplar nuisance variation for sensory noise is a clever analogy to random-dot-motion paradigms, but it leans on the assumption that nuisance variation perturbs the model's internal evidence *in the same distributional role* as Gaussian encoding noise. The paper argues this rather than proves it; a model whose logits respond non-monotonically or with heavy tails to nuisance perturbations could in principle satisfy the signatures spuriously.

- **The CLEVR null result is handled with appropriate epistemic humility.** Rather than overclaiming, the authors enumerate three alternative explanations (task-irrelevant information mixed into the decision variable; ignored task-relevant information; non-Bayesian heuristic combination) and explicitly decline to conclude either satisfaction or violation. This is the right stance but means the headline claim ("answer logits are readouts of a latent decision variable") is established only for low-level perceptual and memory settings — a relatively narrow, although foundational, regime.

- **Abstention result is striking but the 87.7% abstention rate is extreme.** A policy that withholds 87.7% of answers to reach 99.95% accuracy is highly conservative; whether such a setting is useful in deployment depends on coverage–accuracy trade-offs the paper does not explore.

## Strengths

- Genuine conceptual novelty: reframes a well-studied empirical question (LLM calibration) into a representational/computational one with testable qualitative predictions.
- Strong cross-disciplinary bridge, importing decades of perceptual-decision literature into LLM evaluation.
- Methodologically clean: pre-specified four signatures ordered from weakest to strongest, with the folded-X as the discriminating test.
- Replication across 4 models (3 non-reasoning + 1 reasoning) and 4 task families lends real generality.
- Honest treatment of the framework's boundary (CLEVR) and of the BCH/CRES distinction, rather than overclaiming.
- The abstention experiment provides causal/behavioural evidence that the signal matters, not just that it correlates with correctness.

## Limitations

- **Scope of the positive claim.** SDC signatures are confirmed only for simple perceptual and one memory task; for the complex visual reasoning that LLMs are most useful for, the framework is currently inconclusive (folded-X absent; process model unknown).
- **Cannot separate BCH from CRES.** Requires independent manipulation of stimulus strength vs. evidence reliability — left as future work.
- **Implementation-agnostic by design.** The paper is explicit that satisfying the signatures says nothing about *how* the model computes the signal internally; no mechanistic/representational account is provided (the authors flag this as a next step analogous to the behavioural-to-neural progression in neuroscience).
- **Deterministic-noise substitution assumption.** The claim that nuisance-feature variation plays the same role as internal sensory noise is plausible but not independently verified.
- **Stimulus coverage is narrow.** Contrast/size/shape and a single memory task; no auditory, multi-step logical, or agentic tasks.
- **Single reasoning model (Gemini Flash 3) via a 90% reasoning-cut heuristic** to avoid logit saturation — a workaround whose robustness is only briefly validated.
- **No exploration of the coverage/accuracy trade-off** in the abstention setting, despite the extreme 87.7% abstention rate.
- **Reasoning-model logit extraction is fragile.** As reasoning traces grow, terminal-answer logits saturate, making LD uninformative for long-CoT models; the 90%-cut is an ad hoc patch.

## Relevance

对 LLM 安全与可解释性研究高度相关。"模型到底有多确信自己是对的"是 trustworthy deployment 的核心问题，本文从计算神经科学角度提供了一个可证伪的判别框架(SDC 四签名/folded-X)，可作为：

- **可信度评估的新基准**：不再只看 AUROC/ECE，而是检验 logit 是否是"潜在决策变量的忠实读出"。
- **abstention / 拒答策略的理论依据**：论文显示 |LD| 阈值策略近似最优，对构建 selective-prediction / RAG 兜底系统有直接工程意义。
- **生物智能与人工智能的统一计算框架**：把 SDC 从动物感知决策搬到 LLM，为跨学科研究打开通道。
- **局限性提示**：在缺乏显式 normative 过程模型的复杂推理任务上，现有的 logit 置信度解释力有限——这正是当前 LLM 主战场，意味着后续工作空间巨大。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. **如何独立操控 stimulus strength 与 evidence reliability** 以区分 BCH 与 CRES？这是论文明确指出的最关键开放问题。
2. 在 **多步推理 / agentic 任务** 上 SDC 签名是否成立？中间步 logit 与最终答案 logit 哪个更接近潜在决策变量？
3. 能否为 **CLEVR / VQA 类复杂任务构造显式 normative 过程模型**（如基于场景图的可信度生成模型），从而让 folded-X 重新成为可诊断预测？
4. 内部表征层面：哪些 **注意力头 / 残差流方向** 编码了潜在决策变量 d？这对应神经科学中从行为签名到神经元表征的推进。
5. abstention 在 **可调阈值** 下的 coverage–accuracy Pareto 曲线是什么？87.7% 拒答率过于保守，实际部署如何权衡？
6. SDC 框架对 **校准方法（temperature scaling、verbal confidence elicitation）** 的设计有何指导？校准良好的信号未必是潜在决策变量的读出，那么"真正"的置信度信号该如何提取？
7. 跨模态（语音、视频时序决策）任务上签名是否稳健？nuisance 变异的"感官噪声等价"假设在时序数据上是否依然成立？
8. reasoning-cut 启发式(90%) 是否可被更原理性的 **CoT 内部隐状态读出** 替代，以避免长推理链下的 logit 饱和问题？
