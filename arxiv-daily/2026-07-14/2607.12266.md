> **一句话总结：** 本文用 Walsh/functional-ANOVA 分解和 Möbius 展开实证发现：4-bit 量化损失 85–99% 的方差可由逐层一阶项解释，其根源是损失的饱和性，由此提出的"覆盖模型"(coverage model) 与可加模型能在小测量预算下以最多 2× 误差外推预测配置损失，并在匹配内存的混合精度分配上以比 NVIDIA ModelOpt 基线低 17–21% 的 KL 散度胜出。

> **TL;DR:** Treating quantization loss as a set function on the Boolean cube, the paper shows 85–99% of its variance is first-order at W4A4, traced to loss saturation via a "coverage model" `f(S)=c(1−∏(1−aᵢ))`; this model and its additive sibling predict configuration loss within ~2–3% from ~L measurements, degrade at most 2× under configuration extrapolation (vs. 2–5× for Gaussian-process surrogates), and as allocators attain 17–21% lower calibration KL than the production NVIDIA ModelOpt baseline on Qwen3-30B, with consistent gains at 235B and 355B.

## 核心贡献

- **对"敏感性可分解"假设的首次直接检验**：将量化损失 f(S) 视为布尔立方体上的集合函数，用 Walsh 变换（functional-ANOVA）与 Möbius 展开两种经典基分解，实证 4-bit weight-and-activation 下 85–93%（采样）/ 98–99%（精确格、去噪后）的方差来自逐层一阶项。
- **饱和覆盖模型作为统一解释**：提出 f(S)=c(1−∏_{i∈S}(1−aᵢ))，每层消耗剩余"余量"的固定比例；给出该模型的精确 Möbius 谱（定理3）与 Walsh 谱（定理4），并解释为何低阶 Möbius 截断会交替过冲（实测最大 32× 过冲后仅在 k=L 处收敛）。
- **证书（Certificate）机制**：定理1证明最佳一阶预测器的均方误差恰等于 order-≥2 的 Walsh 能量；提出可由 L+1 个拟合参数闭式预报（命题5的 saturation-depth law），在每个结果旁附带"任何可加模型能达到的误差下限"。
- **对孤立敏感性的闭式失效解释**：孤立测量（含 HAWQ-v2 Hessian-trace）因锚点错误高估配置损失 71–376%；给出膨胀因子 sᵢ/wᵢ≈exp(p·Σaⱼ) 的闭式表达并经全格验证。
- **免拟合估计与可解释预测器**：单个 Aumann–Shapley 路径积分在无需采样的前提下恢复最优可加系数（在覆盖模型下证明），并在小预算（N≈L/2）下优于/持平 RF/GP/RBF/pairwise 黑盒代理；外推时覆盖模型最多 2× 退化，而 GP 退化 2–5×。
- **匹配内存的分配器**：在 0.6B–355B MoE 大模型上取得最低 KL；Qwen3-30B 上比 ModelOpt 基线低 17–21%，Qwen3-235B 低 13–27%，GLM-4.6 低 20–37%；在 4-bit 以下用 GPTQ 梯子使被 RTN 全部击垮的分配器重新具备选择能力，并决定 served 模型能否产生终止生成。

## Key Contributions

- **First direct test of the "sensitivities suffice" premise**: treats quantization loss f(S) as a set function on {0,1}^L and decomposes it via the Walsh (functional-ANOVA) and Möbius bases; measures that 85–93% (sampled) / 98–99% (noise-corrected exact lattice) of variance is order-1 at W4A4 deployment precision.
- **Saturation as the unifying cause**: proposes the coverage model f(S)=c(1−∏(1−aᵢ)), where each layer consumes a fixed fraction of remaining headroom; derives its exact Möbius (Thm 3) and Walsh (Thm 4) spectra, explaining the alternating overshoot of low-order Möbius truncations (measured up to 32× overshoot).
- **A certificate for every result**: Theorem 1 equates the best additive predictor's MSE with the order-≥2 Walsh energy; Proposition 5 (the saturation-depth law) forecasts this share in closed form from L+1 fitted break-rates, yielding a per-cell error floor for any additive model.
- **Closed-form account of why isolated sensitivities fail**: isolate-and-sum and Hessian-trace HAWQ-v2 overstate configuration loss by 71–376% because their anchor is the unquantized network; the inflation factor sᵢ/wᵢ≈exp(p·Σaⱼ) is validated against full lattices.
- **Fit-free estimator and interpretable predictor**: a single Aumann–Shapley path integral recovers the optimal additive coefficient (and exact layer ranking) with no sampled configurations, proven under coverage; the L+1-parameter additive/coverage models match or beat RF/GP/RBF/CoopQ-pairwise at small budgets and degrade ≤2× under configuration extrapolation versus 2–5× for the GP.
- **Matched-memory allocator**: lowest KL among sensitivity-, rule-, and surrogate-based allocators on MoE LLMs from 0.6B–355B; 17–21% below the production NVIDIA ModelOpt baseline on Qwen3-30B, 13–27% on Qwen3-235B, 20–37% on GLM-4.6; below 4 bits, a GPTQ ladder restores allocator choice and decides served task survival (terminating generations).

## Methodology

The paper analyzes the quantization-damage set function f(S)=L_CE(S quantized)−L_CE(full precision) defined on subsets S of L layers (the Boolean cube). Damage is measured by running the model with exactly the layers in S quantized at W4A4/NVFP4 (16 stochastic-rounding draws per configuration, averaged).

**Two analytical lenses.** (1) The *Walsh / functional-ANOVA decomposition* splits Var(f) into per-order energies W_k under the "deployment distribution" μ_p (each layer quantized independently with probability p=0.6). Theorem 1 ties W_{≥2} to the MSE of the best additive (degree-≤1) predictor via Parseval. (2) The *Möbius/Harsanyi expansion* gives signed coordinates whose size-≤1 truncation is exactly isolate-and-sum and whose size-≤2 truncation is exactly CLADO's pairwise differences.

**The coverage model.** Saturation (loss is bounded; marginal damage of one more layer shrinks as more are quantized) motivates f(S)=c(1−∏_{i∈S}(1−aᵢ)) with per-layer break-rate aᵢ and ceiling c. Equivalently −log(1−f/c)=Σbᵢ is additive in log-headroom, placing coverage in the g(Σbᵢ) class. The paper derives coverage's exact Möbius spectrum (alternating-sign, nonzero at every order → predicts the observed divergence-then-cancellation) and exact Walsh spectrum, then states the *saturation-depth law* (Proposition 5): share_{≥2} is bounded by a parameter-free symmetric curve of τ=(Σβᵢ)^{1/2}, is Schur-concave, and is forecast from L+1 fitted parameters.

**Ordinal defect (Theorem 2).** f is of the form g(b·x) for strictly increasing g iff some additive index reproduces f's ranking. The minimal fraction of pairwise ordering constraints any additive index must violate is bounded from both sides on full 2^8 lattices using a split-half noise-certification device and a matched-noise null.

**Two predictors and an allocator.** Both the coverage and additive models have L+1 parameters fit by least squares on N≈L sampled configurations. The isolated-slope vs in-context-slope ratio sᵢ/wᵢ≈exp(p·Σaⱼ) is derived in closed form. A single Aumann–Shapley path integral recovers the optimal additive coefficient at a layer-specific effective deployment density (proven under coverage, App. B). Allocation minimizes predicted ΔL_CE under an effective-bits budget via a knapsack over per-demotion log-headroom costs; baselines (ModelOpt, CoopQ, GP/RF/RBF surrogates, naive-uniform, MC-MoE, int8-outlier) are converted to allocators through the same knapsack. Experiments span dense LLMs (0.6B–72B), MoE LLMs (Qwen3-30B-A3B, Qwen3-235B-A22B, GLM-4.6 355B, gpt-oss-120B), a diffusion DiT (Qwen-Image-2512, L=60), and vendor NVFP4 checkpoints served on B200 via vLLM/SGLang.

## Results & Highlights

- **Variance is overwhelmingly first-order.** On a 10-layer Qwen3-8B block the order-1 share is 87.3% as measured and 98.1% after noise correction; on an 8-layer Qwen3-0.6B block it is 93.8% / 99.2%. Across {Qwen3-0.6B, Qwen2.5-3B, Qwen3-4B, Qwen3-8B, Llama-3.2-3B, Qwen3-30B-A3B}×{wiki,code} the sampled order-1 share at FP4 is 85–93%; on exact 2^8 lattices at W4A4 it is 96.9–99.7% for 0.6B–72B dense models and 90.7% on gpt-oss-120B.
- **Isolated sensitivities mis-price by 71–376%.** Isolate-and-sum overstates the fully-quantized corner by 71–346%; HAWQ-v2 (Hessian-trace × MS-quantization-perturbation) overstates it by 89% (Qwen3-8B) and 376% (Qwen3-0.6B). On full networks, isolate-and-sum's median relative error is 51% (Qwen3-8B) and 203% (Qwen3-30B-A3B), versus 2–3% for the fitted additive/coverage models on the same held-out protection sets.
- **Out-of-distribution robustness.** Fit at p=0.6 and tested at p=0.85/0.35, coverage's error grows only 1.3–2.0× and the additive model's 1.1–1.9× relative to in-distribution, while the Gaussian process degrades 2.1–4.9× and the random forest 2.0–3.9× — because stationary kernels do not encode saturation.
- **Allocation beats the production baseline.** On Qwen3-30B at matched effective bits, AS-additive/AS-coverage lead at every budget (e.g., eff 5.0: AS-cov KL 0.0266, AS-add 0.0264 vs ModelOpt 0.0331 vs CoopQ 0.0347 vs gp-alloc 0.0295), 17–21% below the production ModelOpt baseline; on Qwen3-235B margins are 13–27%, on GLM-4.6 (355B) 20–37% (AS-coverage KL 0.059/0.045/0.036 vs ModelOpt 0.088/0.071/0.045 at eff 5/6/7). CoopQ trails AS by +19% to +54% despite 1,205 sampled KL evaluations versus AS's zero.
- **Serving at vendor parity with smaller footprint.** On Qwen3.6-27B served by vLLM on one B200, the additive allocation at 5.41 effective bits matches NVIDIA NVFP4 (6.31) and Unsloth NVFP4 (6.73) downstream (AIME25 90.0 / GPQA 79.8 / MMLU-Pro 86.5) while being 1.5–2.0× bf16 throughput and 8–24% faster than the Unsloth checkpoint.
- **Below 4 bits decides survival.** On a GPTQ ladder (Qwen3-30B), the additive allocator's KL is 3.0–6.6× below ModelOpt's at servable budgets; on Qwen3-235B-A22B the additive allocation preserves reasoning at 3.5 effective bits (AIME-2025 73.3) while ModelOpt collapses to 0.0 across the board, and an eval-task-corpus-derived additive allocation remains functional at 2.9 bits (42.9 AIME) where the same-memory earlier-corpus allocation collapses to 2.1.

(Baseten is an LLM-serving infrastructure company, giving the author direct access to vLLM/SGLang stacks and B200 hardware — consistent with the paper's emphasis on served throughput and vendor NVFP4 checkpoints). ## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: High. Reframing mixed-precision quantization as a measured set function and asking *what expansion order suffices* is a genuinely new question that prior interaction-aware work (CoopQ, CLADO, GuidedQuant) asserts rather than tests. The coverage model and its closed-form spectra (Thms 3–4, Prop 5) appear original.
- **Rigor**: Strong. Every headline number carries a noise-certification device (split-half against a matched-noise null), significance is Holm-corrected within budget rows, and the "certificate" column is reported alongside results — a commendable self-bound on claims. Theorem 1 (MSE = order-≥2 energy) is a clean operational identity.
- **Reproducibility**: Mixed-to-good. Models and corpora are named (Qwen3, GLM, Llama, gpt-oss, Qwen-Image; WikiText-2, code corpus, MBPP, gsm8k, MATH, AIME-2025, GPQA, MMLU-Pro, HumanEval, BFCL, RULER), and the estimator is a least-squares fit on ~L forward passes, which is cheap. However, no code release is mentioned, the vendor-checkpoint serving configurations are hardware-specific (B200, SGLang/vLLM concurrency), and the Aumann–Shapley path-integral estimator details live in App. B.
- **Significance**: Practically meaningful in the contended-budget regime (MoE experts at eff 4–6 bits) and the sub-4-bit regime, both of which are deployment-relevant. The "margins vanish in the served ≥4-bit regime, which is near-lossless by construction" caveat (Conclusion) honestly bounds real-world impact for the most common current deployment.
- **Red flags / weak points**: (1) The strongest "17–21% below ModelOpt" claim is on **calibration KL**, not served accuracy — Table 12 (AIME-2025) shows every method between 66–76 against a bf16 anchor of 73.3 with no significant ordering, so the served-accuracy payoff of the KL win is not demonstrated at ≥4 bits. (2) CoopQ is benchmarked at whole-layer granularity (the only granularity its permutation-walk cost affords), while the proposed methods score 240 per-linear units — a granularity mismatch that advantages the paper's method and is acknowledged but not neutralized. (3) The allocation-granularity certificate is "an order-of-magnitude statement" (−0.09±0.10 on 30B), i.e., not significantly different from zero, so the central certificate is loose exactly where allocation decisions are made. (4) Scope is explicitly limited to NVFP4 per-block activation scaling; under per-tensor activation scales the order-≥2 share reaches 0.67–0.81 and the method breaks down — so the additivity result is format-dependent. (5) Single author, internship work, no peer review stated.

## Strengths

- Asks and answers a foundational question (what is the expansion order of quantization loss?) with a principled, theory-grounded methodology rather than another heuristic.
- The coverage model is intuitive, has only L+1 parameters, and is justified both empirically (τ-collapse at Spearman ρ=0.958 / Pearson r=0.984–0.997 across 36–68 cells) and by a representation theorem (Aczél–Ling).
- Honest, self-certifying reporting: every result is paired with a forecast of how well any additive model can do, and the paper states where its own theory fails (per-tensor scaling, diffusion DiT share 0.36).
- Practical wins are realized as actual served throughput/accuracy (B200, SGLang, vLLM) against public vendor checkpoints, not just calibration metrics.
- Closed-form diagnosis of *why* incumbents (HAWQ-v2, isolate-and-sum, ModelOpt) fail — the wrong-anchor inflation factor — is immediately actionable for practitioners.

## Limitations

- **Saturation lives in the activation channel.** Weight-only (W4A16) damage is non-saturating, so the whole framework targets W4A4/activation quantization and does not apply to weight-only compression pipelines.
- **Format dependence.** Under per-tensor activation scales the order-≥2 share balloons to 0.67–0.81 and black-box surrogates win; the method assumes per-block (NVFP4) or finer scaling.
- **Served-accuracy gap at ≥4 bits.** The KL advantages do not translate to significant AIME-2025 differences in the near-lossless regime; the decisive served wins are concentrated below 4 bits and in throughput, not in ≥4-bit accuracy.
- **Granularity mismatch with CoopQ** (whole-layer vs per-linear) is a confound in the headline allocation tables.
- **Single-seed / small-seed cells.** Headline cells use 3 seeds (std ≤0.03); the allocation-granularity certificate at N=300 has sampling error of the order of the share itself.
- **Cost model is memory, not latency.** The paper itself flags (Future work) that "effective bits = memory is the wrong cost for realized speedup," which depends on the serving stack.
- **Diffusion transformer is a partial fit.** On Qwen-Image-2512 (L=60) the order-≥2 share is 0.36, above every LLM cell, so the additivity story is weaker for DiTs.

## Relevance

对"计算可靠性与精度误差分析"主题高度相关：它把混合精度量化的损失组合问题形式化为可证明的集合函数分解，并为任何可加近似给出闭式误差下限（certificate），直接服务于低比特 LLM 部署中"哪些层保留高精度"的核心决策。其饱和覆盖模型与 Aumann–Shapley 免拟合估计器可作为量化误差归因与可靠性预算的通用工具。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the coverage model's break-rates aᵢ be predicted from cheap layer descriptors (depth, RMS, Hessian trace) without any damage measurements, yielding a fully fit-free allocator? (The paper notes break-rates correlate with depth at ρ=+0.68 but does not build this predictor.)
- Does the certificate framework extend to the weight channel (W4A16) where saturation is absent — i.e., is weight-only damage exactly additive, and does that explain why GPTQ/AWQ work well there?
- How do rotation methods (QuaRot, SpinQuant, QuIP#) change the measured break-rates and the order-≥2 share — does rotation push the activation channel toward non-saturation, weakening the method's advantage?
- The sub-4-bit "termination threshold" (~0.45 nats on 30B) is empirical; can it be derived from the coverage model's ceiling c, giving a principled survival predictor before serving?
- Could the granularity mismatch with CoopQ be neutralized by running CoopQ's permutation-walk at per-linear granularity on a smaller model, or does its O(100L) cost fundamentally forbid the comparison the paper implies it loses?
- Does the stationary-kernel failure of GP/RBF surrogates generalize to other black-box NAS/quantization predictors, suggesting a broadly applicable diagnostic (measure order-≥2 share under p-shift before trusting a surrogate)?
