★ Why this matters
Robustness papers routinely omit the vanilla baseline, hiding an accuracy cost that decides whether a model is production-usable — a 2-pp insurance premium versus a 10-pp disqualifier.
! Critical edge
The headline −29.5 pp partly contrasts a standard-trained robust model against a foundation-model-scale vanilla (BiT-L, CoCa on JFT-300M). The no-extra-data and architecture-matched gaps are far smaller — the more honest cost estimate.

3

Gap metric Δ_clean

two-dimensional · no scalarization · §2.3

Deliberately kept separate from robust accuracy: the trade-off is two-dimensional, so collapsing cost and robustness into one number would hide the structure.

For each robust model m and vanilla reference r:
Δ_clean(m, r) = am − r    (negative ⇒ robust model sacrifices standard accuracy)

Critical balance

§4 A wake-up call — with honest caveats

+ Strengths

  • First literature-wide quantification of the vanilla gap across all 186 RobustBench models and four threat models — fills a widely recognized hole.
  • Multi-reference design (5 types × 2 data subsets) lets readers pick the comparison fitting their question and defuses the "unfair baseline" objection.
  • Direct-vs-proxy transparency in architecture matching isolates the adversarial-training effect from architectural confounds.
  • Actionable policy: report vanilla-referenced gaps as standard — backed by public tooling.
  • Self-critical: explicitly corrects a prior +21.08 pp anomaly (ResNet-18 → 93.02%) and includes a "Threats to Validity" section.

— Limitations

  • Heterogeneous vanilla sources — different papers, zoos, protocols, preprocessing; not a unified in-house re-evaluation.
  • 94/184 proxy matches use a related (not identical) architecture; mitigated by direct-only stats but not resolved.
  • Best-vanilla reference is partly unfair — CIFAR/ImageNet "best" entries lean on huge external data (JFT-300M), inflating the headline gap.
  • AutoAttack-only robustness metric; says nothing about corruption or distribution-shift robustness.
  • Stale coverage: most recent model is 2024; CIFAR/ImageNet-1k unchanged for years; no new architectures or datasets.
  • Pure re-analysis — no models (re)trained, so protocol mismatches cannot be resolved by controlled evaluation.
Open questions

§5 Where the gap could shrink — or widen

Under a unified protocol (same preprocessing, recipe, eval pipeline), how would the Δ_clean distribution shift? The authors concede current numbers are noisy from protocol mismatch.

For architectures with no public vanilla accuracy (WideResNet-70-16, -94-16), how much does the arch-matched gap close if those vanilla versions were actually trained and evaluated?

If robustness extended beyond AutoAttack to corruption / distribution-shift, how does the accuracy–robustness trade-off reshape?

Why is CIFAR-100's gap (−29.5 pp) so much larger than CIFAR-10's (−12.0 pp)? Harder 100-class setting, or vanilla SOTA buoyed by large-scale pretraining?

Can a robust training method push the arch-matched gap to ≈0 without a mixed classifier? MeanSparse already hits +0.17 pp on ℓ2 — does it generalize?
