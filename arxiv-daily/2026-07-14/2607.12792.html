Silent Alarm: A J-Space Protocol for Comparing Danger Recognition Across Models & Quantization Levels

arxiv.org/abs/2607.12792 ↗
Prosvirnin · Minchenkov · Soldatov · Bashun
HSE University · JADR / SafetyAUC
◆ Key Finding

JADR reads each model's internal J-space before generation to compare danger recognition across models and quantization levels — locally, judge-free.

01Key Metric
<0.011
max |ΔAUC_safety| under INT8

8-bit quantization barely moves internal danger recognition — across all six models. A green light to ship INT8 checkpoints without re-screening safety.

02Why This Matters

Turns the Jacobian lens from a discovery tool into a cheap, local, judge-free checkpoint screen that catches safety erosion before deployment.

Methodology — how the alarm is wired

Jacobian Lens

Precompute J_ℓ once over N=100 calibration prompts; apply + unembedding to read top-k=100 tokens at every layer.

lens_ℓ(h_ℓ,t) = unembed( J_ℓ · h_ℓ,t )

Decision Point

All readouts taken at the last prompt token (rel_pos = −1). Causally prompt-dependent, no decoding — that's what makes it a pre-generation screen.

Six Axes & Lexicon

Frozen EN/ZH/RU stems matched against top-k tokens, grouped into six interpretable behavior-scenario axes.

safety 170compliance 47evasion 49 softening 16hedging 22harm 84

Separability Metrics

Rank AUC (Mann-Whitney) per layer/model is the primary comparator; 95% CI from B=300–1000 bootstrap resamples over prompts. Cross-modification: ΔAUC, D_log, and the joint Safety Headroom.

ΔSH = ΔAUC_compliance − ΔAUC_safety   (eq.16)

Datasets & Behavioral Check

Danger set 𝒟 = 313 StrongREJECT; safe control 𝒩 = 320 (XSTest-safe 250 + OKTest 70) — chosen to share alarming vocabulary yet be harmless. StrongREJECT Gemma-2B grader gives harm@0.5; regex parses refusals; deterministic decoding (T=0).

INT8 · NEUTRAL

|ΔAUC_safety| < 0.011 for every model — 8-bit quantization does not erode internal danger recognition.

INT4 · UNEVEN

Hits compliance, not safety. Qwen3-4B/8B SafetyAUC barely moves yet ΔSH declines (−0.052 / −0.044) — the joint margin degrades via instruction-following.

RECOGNITION ≠ BEHAVIOR

SafeRL-4B: SafetyAUC 0.976 but harm@0.5 0.345, regex refusal 0.00 — its templated "However, I can suggest…" bypasses the detector. The motivating case for an internal metric.

INT4 · ACCIDENTAL RESCUE

Ablit-4B recovers under NF4: ΔAUC_safety −0.086, harm@0.5 drops 0.904→0.875 — quantization noise partly erases the ablation edit.

Strengths & Limitations

✓ Strengths

  • Cheap & local — one forward pass, no external judge, directly deployable as a pre-release checkpoint screen.
  • Six-axis decomposition — separating safety from compliance is a genuine advance over monolithic refusal-rate metrics.
  • Transparent stats — dual counters (count + DCG) and bootstrap-over-prompts CIs throughout.
  • Honest calibration poles — SafeRL-4B & Ablit-4B edge cases surfaced, not buried.
  • Releases the full EN/ZH/RU lexicon, appendix tables, and 12 diagnostic figures for reproduction.

! Limitations

  • Lexicon dependence — only literal tokens captured; non-literal danger is invisible; axes are mostly English-centric.
  • Correlational, not causal — rank AUC & Jaccard show where an axis localizes, not that those layers cause refusal.
  • Small model set — six models, two of them deliberate calibration poles; beyond Qwen3/Gemma2 is asserted, not shown.
  • StrongREJECT grader is itself an LLM judge — a possible bias source in the behavioral column.
  • Compliance axis is fragile — weakly localized, noisy across layers, carries a refusal-politeness offset; ComplAUC not comparable across families.

Open Questions for Further Work

01

Causal layer validation. Can a systematic per-layer FP-weight rescue quantify each layer's causal contribution to SafetyAUC, instead of leaning on one refusal-direction criterion?

02

Lexicon robustness. Stress-test the "vocabulary-agnostic" claim with an adversarial lexicon — what are the boundary conditions for converging to the same ranking?

03

Cross-architecture transfer. Does the protocol hold on Llama / Mistral / DeepSeek? Does Gemma's front-loaded special-token quality distort SafetyAUC comparability?

04

Multiturn & system prompts. Under multi-turn dialogue, jailbreak prefixes, or system-prompt injection, how should the decision point be redefined?

05

Refusal-tuning causal chain. SafeRL-4B shows recognition≠refusal. Can JADR trace which intermediate layer drops the recognized signal before generation?

06

Beyond quantization. Is ΔAUC / ΔSH sensitive enough for LoRA, abliteration, and activation steering — the more common deployment-time modifications?
