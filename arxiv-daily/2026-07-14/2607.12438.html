Methodology

Memorization is tracked through the centered covariance of per-example last-layer gradients. For a K-class net with penultimate feature zᵢ, the per-example final-layer gradient (bias-augmented z̃ᵢ = [zᵢ;1]) is centered, stacked into the Fisher-gradient scatter, and compressed to a single scale-invariant scalar: the entropy effective rank.

STEP 01
gᵢ = (p̂ᵢ − e_yᵢ) ⊗ z̃ᵢ
per-example last-layer gradient
STEP 02
ḡᵢ = gᵢ − μ
center across the batch
STEP 03
S = Σᵢ ḡᵢḡᵢ⊤
centered Fisher-gradient scatter
STEP 04
er(S) = exp(−Σ pₖ log pₖ)
entropy effective rank · scale-invariant

The theory (Section 3) is a chain of checkpoint-local spectral statements — each tied to a measured quantity.

Lemma 1
effective-rank derivative + spectral sensitivity coefficient βₖ, isolating rank-increasing directions (pₖ < 1/er(S)).
Lemmas 2–3
centered deletion identity S₋ᵢ and a first-order LOO expansion Δᵢ ≈ a·Σ βₖ(uₖ⊤ḡᵢ)².
Thm 1
Local Fisher-rank inflation: corruption raises er(S) by opening new eigendirections or loading rank-increasing in-span directions.
Thm 2 / Cor 1
a sufficient condition for corrupted samples to carry larger expected LOO contribution than clean ones.
Prop 1
noisy-to-clean ratio NIR ≥ 1 when the centered noisy spectrum is majorized by the clean one.
Thm 3
post-memorization collapse: as normalized spectra stabilize, Δᵢ → 0 — scale-invariance kills the per-sample signal.

Results & highlights

Across SmallCNN / ResNet18 / ViT on CIFAR-10 & CIFAR-100, the effective rank follows a consistent inflate → peak → collapse trajectory, peaking at the structure-to-memorization transition. Corrupted samples dominate peak-rank attribution; the effect grows monotonically with noise rate and survives real human-annotation noise.

03

Strengths & limitations

Strengths
  • Self-contained spectral theory — Lemma→Theorem→Corollary chain maps directly onto measured quantities (effective rank, sensitivity gap, Spearman Aᵢ vs Δᵢ).
  • Broad empirical coverage — 3 architectures × 2 datasets, a noise-rate sweep, and real human-label noise, all multi-seed (5 seeds).
  • Well-designed difficulty control cleanly separates memorized corruption from intrinsically hard clean examples.
  • Intellectual honesty throughout — explicit caveats on theory scope, the ViT weakness (−0.0011 gap), and the auxiliary status of NIR.
  • Links a global spectral phenomenon to per-sample attribution, bridging gradient-spectrum dynamics and individual training examples.
Limitations
  • Theory is checkpoint-local — it states spectral conditions at a fixed checkpoint, not a full training-trajectory guarantee.
  • First-order approximation degrades for ViT (0.70) and CIFAR-100 (0.65); higher-order deletion effects matter at higher capacity / class count.
  • No public code, runs on Apple MPS — independent reproduction needs reimplementation and may differ numerically (MPS vs CUDA eigenvalues).
  • Only symmetric label corruption in the main theory; asymmetric, class- and instance-dependent noise are left open.
  • Not a competitive detector — modest AUROC; loss/confidence baselines outperform globally. Lead-time result is retrospective.
  • Scale — limited to CIFAR-scale images and small/medium models; behavior on ImageNet-scale / large architectures untested.
04

Open questions

Does FRI survive asymmetric, class-dependent, or instance-dependent noise?

There corrupted gradients may be more collinear and less spectrally diversifying — possibly weakening the inflation signal.

Can the first-order LOO attribution be cheaply approximated at scale?

e.g. via the B_S-weighted score Aᵢ(S) without exact leave-one-out — to reach ImageNet-scale practicality.

Why does the Vision Transformer show a negative sensitivity gap and weaker correlation?

Attention-head mixing, the absence of batch-norm-fixed statistics, or genuinely higher-order gradient interactions?

Is the rank-inflation lead time stable enough for an online early-warning signal?

And how does it depend on the rank-onset / overfitting-onset thresholds (Appendix A.9)?

Does FRI extend beyond image classification?

To NLP/tabular modalities and other losses where gᵢ = (p̂ᵢ − e_yᵢ) ⊗ z̃ᵢ no longer holds in this form.

Could FRI complement loss/confidence detectors for higher recall?

It may catch memorized low-loss corrupted samples that loss-based methods miss — as the clean-difficulty control suggests.
