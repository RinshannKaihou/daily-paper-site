Gradient Flow Dynamics & Implicit Bias of Diagonal Linear Networks under Infinitesimal Initialization

arxiv.org/abs/2607.12332 ↗

Jiajie Zhao · Jianxing Wang · Junjie Yang · Zhiwei Bai · Yaoyu Zhang

Key Finding

Under infinitesimal init, deep and general two-layer diagonal linear networks follow recursive saddle-to-saddle dynamics converging to a modified ℓ1 minimizer.

Central object R(k) = Σi ( ti+ ki+ + ti− ki− ) a weighted, asymmetric ℓ1 — weights fixed by the init direction

1 Key Metric

10−60

Initialization scale at which empirical GD matches Algorithm 1's closed-form k(p) to ~6 significant figures (200-digit mpmath precision).

2 Why This Matters

Initialization direction — not just scale — shapes which sparse solution gradient descent selects, refining the plain-ℓ1 implicit-bias story for overparameterized models.

sharpest critique

3 Critical Edge

Sign-locking — the theorem's linchpin — holds only at s→0. Curved SIMs break exact invariance under discrete GD/SGD, so the result is approximate in practice. Regression/MSE only; classification untouched.

01 Methodology

two conceptual steps · proofs in App. A.1–A.2

Algorithm equivalence Thm 4.5

The gradient-flow trajectory Γs init at s·θ*, in the limit s→0, visits points whose k-vectors are exactly the sequence {k(p)} from Algorithm 1.

  • Distance-Speed (§5.1): neuron minimizing D⁺/|v₀| activates first — D⁺ set by init direction, |v₀| by feature–target correlation.
  • Time-scale split (§5.2): feature-selection takes O(1/s); learning takes O(1). No new feature escapes during learning.
  • Sign-locking (§5.2): sign flips need a return to O(s) init — costs O(1/s), impossible in the finite learning window.

Modified-ℓ1 convergence Thm 4.2

Algorithm 1 (feature-selection + sign-constrained least squares) terminates in finitely many steps and outputs the KKT point of min R(k) s.t. Xk = y.

  • Deep nets: conserved quantities μ²i,j = (ai,j*)² − (ai,L*)²; tᵢ± = ±Fi(±∞).
  • General two-layer: tᵢ± = ±1/λᵢ∓ (reciprocal of Hessian extreme eigenvalues).
  • SIM mechanism (§6): the orbit of ℱ = {∇θF(·)(x)} is exactly invariant for any analytic loss; factorizes per-neuron (Thm 6.5).
Deep-net key step: reparametrize via Fi(z) = ∫ dz / ∏√(x²+μ²i,j)  ⇒  tᵢ± = ±Fi(±∞).   Two-layer: Lemma A.2 (Li et al. 2021) on limit trajectories ⇒ tᵢ± = ±1/λᵢ∓.

03 Strengths vs. Limitations

side-by-side · what holds up / what doesn't

+ Strengths

  • Clean, correct generalization of both trajectory theorem and implicit-bias characterization, full proofs in appendix.
  • Mechanistic depth: §6 ties algorithmic dynamics to a concrete geometric object — SIM = orbit of ℱ — giving explanation, not just characterization.
  • Sharp GF vs. GD distinction: flat vs. curved SIMs (§8.2) say when continuous-time theory survives discretization.
  • Quantitative experiments: ~6-sig-fig match to closed-form k(p) is real dynamics, not asymptotic fiction.

! Limitations

  • Infinitesimal init only (§8.1): all results at s→0; doesn't match Woodworth's ℓ1↔L2 interpolation across finite scales.
  • Gradient flow only: no GD/SGD/Adam — sign-locking only approximate under discrete steps.
  • Regression/MSE only: classification (max-margin) regime untouched.
  • Generic-position assumptions: robustness to degenerate data (duplicated features) unaddressed.
  • No matrix-factorization extension, though diagonal nets are a special case of it.

04 Questions for Further Exploration

non-linear entry points into the open agenda

Q1 · FINITE INIT

Does the modified-ℓ1 extend to finite init scales, recovering Woodworth's ℓ1↔L2 interpolation as a special case?

Q2 · NOISE & MOMENTUM

Does sign-locking survive SGD noise (Pesme 2021) or momentum (Papazov 2024)? Is there an "approximate SIM" that rescues the theorem?

Q3 · DESIGN TOOL

Can init direction θ* be chosen constructively to steer GD toward a desired sparse support — turning implicit bias into a design lever?

Q4 · MATRIX SENSING

Does Thm 6.5's per-neuron product structure lift to matrix factorization, yielding an incremental low-rank theorem à la Li et al. (2021)?
