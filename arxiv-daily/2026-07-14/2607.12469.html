§ 01 — How it works

The scoring chain DEFINITIONAL · DETERMINISTIC

1

Eight property classes

unit of analysis = the evaluation claim

For each agent decision, evidence is read across eight classes — action & permission recoverability, monitor & policy-state visibility, responsibility attribution, counterfactual replayability, claim sufficiency, evaluation-awareness confounding — generalising the prior pilot's three-valued scale to a unit interval.

action-recovperm-recovmonitor-vispolicy-visattributionreplayclaim-sufeval-confound
2

Per-property score → vector

r_p = |present(p)| / |R_p|

No model in the loop, no learned parameters: scores are counts of present-and-bound evidence against author-declared required sets, bound by a triple (decision_id, agent_step_id, tool_call_id). Primary output is the 8-tuple reconstructability vector.

3

Bottleneck scalar & overclaim gap

R_min = min over claim-required · non-compensatory

The headline R_min is deliberately non-compensatory — one zero drives it to zero. The overclaim gap G = |R(A) − R(B)| is defined only between regimes sharing a nominal outcome, making "same headline number, weaker evidence" measurable.

4

Evidence Card + release gate

per-decision JSON · CI exit codes

A vendor-neutral adapter (Inspect + ControlArena targets) emits a per-decision Evidence Sufficiency Card and a per-run monitor-coverage gate: every risky tool span must be scored by an identified monitor — exit 1 on insufficient/partial, exit 2 on tool errors.

Release is coarser than the design. The v0 scorer operationalises the 8 classes as 12 three-valued field checks F01–F12 (present 1.0 / partial 0.5 / missing 0.0): per-trace not per-decision, discrete not continuous, with F01/F12 carrying scoring-time provenance rather than trace evidence (Table 1).

§ 03 — Tension

Strengths & Limitations SIDE BY SIDE

▲ Strengths

  • Sharp outcome-vs-evidence framing: names the failure mode (identical nominal outcomes atop materially different evidence) no prior vendor-neutral runnable instrument scores.
  • The distinctive third verdict "insufficient evidence" (⊥, undecidable) — a verdict attribution systems are built to avoid — derived from auditability's three-valued policy-check outcome.
  • Engineering honesty: deterministic, schema-based, no model-in-the-loop; byte-identical reproducibility; an explicit design-to-release correspondence table admitting where the release is coarser.
  • Clean positioning vs AgenTracer (presumes reconstructability), EviBound (audits task-completion, not monitor observation), and the per-system Auditability Card.
  • CI-friendly exit-code gate; actionable "report the vector + bottleneck beside any claim" recommendation.

▽ Limitations

  • G (Definition 4) is never measured — only a field-level twelve-field analogue; "no equivalence between the two aggregations is established."
  • Required-evidence sets R_p and the eight-class taxonomy are author-declared, not empirically validated (T1); ground-truth labelling deferred to FW2.
  • External validity is thin: 4 scored traces + 2 fixture pairs; 3 of 6 inputs hand-built; no real matched-outcome pair; ControlArena reader untested on a real export.
  • No executed counterfactual replay (T4) — the most distinctive methodological claim (replay protocol) is specified, not demonstrated.
  • Descriptive, non-pre-registered, no hypothesis tests; up to 2 of 12 fields (F01, F12) credited from non-trace provenance, modestly overstating sufficiency.
§ 04 — Open threads

Questions for further exploration 6 OPEN

Once R_p membership is ground-truth calibrated by two annotators (FW2), how do the eight-class distributions and bottleneck scores shift — can the author-declared required-evidence sets survive independent revision?

Under the v2 executed-replay protocol, does any "stable outcome but evidence-poor" claim actually flip — i.e. does replay change reconstructability verdicts (the H3 causal form this paper could not test)?

On a real ControlArena export (not a synthetic fixture), does the cross-harness field mapping stay vendor-neutral? A second real harness beyond Inspect is v0's main external-validity gap.

After a capture-only recount that strips F01/F12 scoring-time/card-self provenance from sufficiency_score, do the four cards' ordering and blocking conclusions change materially?

The monitor-coverage gate scores coverage ≠ accuracy — a monitor that scores every risky span but judges all wrong still passes. Does that weaken the gate as load-bearing release evidence?

Is there any equivalence between the bottleneck R_min and the v0 twelve-field mean sufficiency_score? If not, does the abstract's "0.458–0.833 spans" claim need sharper qualification?
