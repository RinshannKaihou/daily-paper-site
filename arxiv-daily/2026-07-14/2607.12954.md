> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一个物理约束的 NWP 误差注入评测框架（基于 HRRR 残差标定 + Erbs 辐射一致性重建），在 5 个美国气候区对 6 个预测模型（含 PatchTST、GRU、N-HITS、LightGBM）做鲁棒性压测，发现 LightGBM 在干净数据下精度最高但随噪声急剧劣化，而 GRU/PatchTST 在中高噪声/长时间自相关下更具韧性，并通过 SHAP/IG 揭示模型在污染输入下会把预测依赖从"未来预报"重新分配到"历史观测与确定性物理先验"。
> **TL;DR:** A physically constrained, HRRR-calibrated NWP error-injection benchmark across 5 U.S. climate zones shows that sequence models (GRU, PatchTST) overtake a strong LightGBM tabular baseline under medium-to-high noise (e.g., Z3 24h clear-sky-normalized RE 0.255–0.305 vs 0.147–0.171 in stable Z1), with SHAP/IG revealing a feature-substitution effect that shifts reliance from corrupted future forecasts toward historical observations and deterministic priors.

## 核心贡献

1. **动态、物理一致的 NWP 误差注入框架**：基于 HRRR 预报残差统计标定，同时捕获误差的短时自相关（AR(1)）、随天空状态的异方差（clearness index 调制），并通过 Erbs 分解重建 DNI/DHI 以保持辐射场一致性，避免了"独立扰动各辐射分量"的非物理场景。
2. **物理感知的未来特征构造策略**：对确定性变量（太阳几何、时间编码）直接计算，对缓变变量（反照率、气溶胶）用持久化，对弱关键变量（露点、相对湿度）用训练集气候态期望，只对真正具有显著不确定性的 GHI 和温度做显式扰动。
3. **鲁棒性退化的空间异质性归因**：证明模型鲁棒性损失不是统计随机的，而是由本地大气的"天空状态跳变强度"与"短期结构持续性"物理驱动的（如 Humid Continental Z3 误差放大最严重）。
4. **XAI 解释 + Pareto 工程权衡**：用 SHAP（变量级）和 Integrated Gradients（时间级）揭示"特征替代"机制，再用准确率/鲁棒性/计算延迟三维 Pareto 前沿给出可落地的模型选型建议。

## Key Contributions

- A **dynamically parameterized, physically consistent NWP error-injection framework** for AI stress testing, calibrated against empirical HRRR residual statistics (heteroscedasticity by sky state, lag-1 autocorrelation ρ, clearness-amplification α), with radiation consistency preserved via Erbs decomposition of DNI/DHI.
- A **physically informed future-feature construction strategy** that perturbs only GHI and temperature while keeping deterministic variables (solar geometry), slowly varying variables (albedo/aerosol via persistence), and weak drivers (dew-point/RH via training-set climatology) untouched.
- Demonstration that **algorithmic robustness degradation is spatially heterogeneous and physically governed** — linked to sky-state jump intensity |ΔKt| and short-term clearness persistence, not just to input error magnitude.
- An **interpretability + Pareto trade-off analysis** combining SHAP and Integrated Gradients to expose a "feature-substitution" tendency, and translating findings into engineering guidance for model selection under forecast uncertainty.

## Methodology

**Controlled digital-twin benchmark.** Target is *virtual PV power* from pvlib (fixed-tilt south-facing, tilt=latitude, azimuth=180°, 1 kW DC, 96% inverter efficiency, SAPM open-rack glass-polymer cell temperature; clear-sky GHI via Ineichen + Linke turbidity). This deliberately isolates meteorological input uncertainty from plant-level confounders (inverter clipping, soiling, curtailment, sensor drift).

**Data & sampling.** NREL NSRDB meteorological data across 5 Köppen–Geiger U.S. climate zones (Z1 Arid, Z2 Humid Subtropical, Z3 Humid Continental, Z4 Marine, Z5 High Plains), 20 sites/zone. Each forecast issue uses 72 h history + 24 h future at hourly resolution. Strict chronological split: 2018–2022 train, 2023 val, 2024 test → 36,440 / 7,300 / 7,280 hourly issue-time samples per zone; daylight test cases split seasonally as 1,780 (DJF) / 1,840 (MAM) / 1,840 (JJA) / 1,820 (SON).

**Error injection (Eqs. 1–5).** GHI noise scale σ_m(t) = s·(1+α·(1−Kt)) modulated by clearness; temporal persistence via AR(1) ε(t)=ρε(t−1)+η(t), η~N(0,1−ρ²); perturbed GHI = max(0, GHI_true + b_ghi·GHI_cs + GHI_cs·ε·σ_m); DNI/DHI then reconstructed by Erbs. Temperature uses simpler additive perturbation T_noisy = T_true + b_temp + ξ. Parameters s, ρ, α calibrated directly from HRRR-vs-observation residuals (s = RMS of normalized residual, ρ = lag-1 autocorrelation per trajectory, α fitted by clearness bin).

**Models & training.** All models use Direct Multi-Step (DMS) forecasting. DL models (PatchTST, GRU, N-HITS, MLP, TCN) in MIMO configuration mapping full spatiotemporal tensor → 24-dim output; LightGBM trains 24 per-horizon tabular regressors with engineered features (clearness index, 3/6/24 h rolling stats, lags, 1st differences). AdamW, lr=1e-3, wd=1e-4, batch=512, MSE loss, grad-clip 1.0, max 40 epochs, early-stop 8; LightGBM 500 estimators, early-stop 30. Z-score normalization uses *only* training historical-window stats to prevent leakage. Metrics: NMAE, NRMSE, and lead-time clear-sky-normalized Relative Error (RE). Seed=42, NVIDIA Quadro RTX 8000; CPU latency in single-sample end-to-end mode.

## Results & Highlights

- **Clean-data hierarchy**: LightGBM is the strongest baseline with NRMSE < 0.03 in 4 of 5 zones; PatchTST and GRU are the best DL models; MLP and TCN lag, especially in meteorologically complex Z3.
- **Critical noise threshold s\*** (where DL overtakes LightGBM) is highly sensitive to autocorrelation: under white noise (ρ=0), PatchTST overtakes at s\*≈0.06 in Z2; under ρ=0.7 in Z5, GRU's s\* rises to 0.19, and in extreme Z5+high-ρ cases MLP and N-HITS never cross the LightGBM line.
- **Tree models are magnitude-sensitive, sequence models are persistence-sensitive**: LightGBM's mean NRMSE stays ~0.272 at s=0.3 and barely moves with ρ (0.299→0.300 at s=0.5); in contrast GRU/PatchTST degrade sharply as ρ→0.9 (well above typical HRRR ρ≈0.5), revealing an architectural trade-off (recurrent memory disrupted by sustained bias).
- **Lead-time error accumulation**: 24h RE reaches ~0.255–0.305 in cloud-dynamic Z3 but only ~0.147–0.171 in stable Z1; LightGBM's advantage vanishes within the first few lead steps and it ends with the highest 24h error in all zones, while GRU consistently holds the lowest 24h error, followed by PatchTST.
- **HRRR empirical validation**: residuals are wider under cloudy/mixed skies (justifying α), and all zones show positive short-lag autocorrelation — most pronounced in Z2 and Z4; largest s in Z2/Z3, largest α in Z4.
- **Physical drivers**: mean ΔNRMSE at s=0.3 is highest in Z3 and lowest in stable Z1 / low-jump Z4; high sky-state-jump regimes show broader, heavier-tailed error distributions than low-jump regimes.
- **Feature substitution (case)**: under clean data Kt-Forecast + GHI-Forecast dominate SHAP (47.7% + 44.7%); under high-magnitude noise Kt-Forecast drops ~41 pp; under high-autocorrelation GHI-Forecast drops ~10.2 pp while SSA, ΔPower(1h), Ozone, Power-Lag(1h) rise; IG temporal attribution shifts from sharp future peaks toward elevated historical-window intensity.
- **Population-level consistency (250 paired samples, all p<0.001)**: future-forecast SHAP share down median 39.1 pp (high-magnitude) / 10.1 pp (high-autocorrelation); historical-power +12.8 pp, deterministic-physical +5.4 pp, auxiliary-meteorology +18.3 pp under high-magnitude noise; PatchTST historical IG share +0.152 / +0.106 respectively.
- **Pareto / latency**: in volatile Z3, LightGBM AUEC≈0.28 vs PatchTST≈0.20 vs GRU≈0.18; CPU single-sample latency: LightGBM ~10–11 ms (highest, due to 24 per-horizon regressors + feature re-engineering), MLP fastest but fragile, PatchTST/N-HITS competitive in low-latency regime. PatchTST sits at the Pareto knee; GRU strongest robustness at slightly higher cost.

## Critical Assessment

The paper's strongest move is methodological honesty about its own scope: it explicitly frames the virtual-PV pvlib benchmark as a *digital twin* designed to strip plant-level confounders (inverter clipping, soiling, curtailment, sensor drift), and repeatedly reminds the reader that conclusions hold "within this physically constrained perturbation family," not for every possible NWP error realization. The HRRR calibration (Fig. 6) genuinely grounds the s/ρ/α parameters in empirical residuals rather than hand-waving, and the Erbs-reconstruction trick to keep DNI/DHI physically consistent with perturbed GHI is a clean, defensible design choice. The LightGBM-vs-DL comparison is also fair-minded: the authors are upfront that LightGBM uses different (tabular, per-horizon engineered) features, so the comparison reflects *model-family behavior under representative usage*, not a pure architecture ablation. Two genuine weaknesses remain: (i) the perturbation model captures marginal variance, short-range persistence, and radiation consistency but omits full spatial cross-correlation, regime-switching nonstationarity, and cross-variable dependency — so a model that happens to exploit exactly those structures would be mis-evaluated; (ii) the SHAP/IG "feature substitution" story rests heavily on one representative case (Fig. 8) for the headline percentages (47.7%/44.7%, −41 pp), with the broader 250-sample supplementary evidence presented only as median shifts, leaving the magnitude of the case-level effect somewhat less certain at population scale. The local-sensitivity check (Spearman >0.86 everywhere, but with top-1/top-2 rotation in Z1 and Z5) is a credible robustness sanity check but is confined to a narrow neighborhood.

## Strengths

- Physically constrained, HRRR-calibrated error injection that captures heteroscedasticity, autocorrelation, and radiation consistency — substantially more realistic than i.i.d. Gaussian perturbation.
- Clean isolation of meteorological uncertainty from plant-level confounders via the pvlib virtual-plant digital twin.
- Mechanistic explanation of spatial heterogeneity (sky-state jump intensity, clearness persistence), not just statistical description.
- Dual-grain interpretability (SHAP for variables, IG for temporal receptive field) with population-level Wilcoxon validation in supplementary material.
- Multi-objective Pareto framing (accuracy / robustness / CPU latency) that translates directly into deployable model-selection guidance.

## Limitations

- Perturbation family is compact and empirically anchored but does **not** reproduce full spatial correlation structure, regime-switching nonstationarity, or full multivariate dependency among NWP errors.
- Historical observation window is assumed clean; real sensor drift, sync errors, and missing-data anomalies are not tested.
- Virtual PV (generic fixed-tilt, 1 kW, SAPM) cannot capture utility-scale plant effects (inverter clipping, soiling, localized shading, dispatch constraints); field validation is left to future work.
- Single chronological train/val/test split (2018–2024) does not establish generalization across stronger interannual climate variability or diverse extreme-weather realizations.
- LightGBM vs DL is a matched-information but not identical-representation comparison, so it is not a strict architecture-only ablation.

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. 若将扰动框架扩展到完整多元联合 NWP 误差结构（含风、云量、降水、空间网格相关性），GRU/PatchTST 的"特征替代"路径与鲁棒性排序是否会显著改变？
2. 在历史观测窗口本身存在传感器漂移、缺失、时间错位时，文中观察到的"向后依赖"补偿行为是否依然成立，甚至会被放大？
3. 若把虚拟电站替换为真实公用事业级电站数据（含 inverter clipping、soiling、限功率），模型间的鲁棒性差距是缩小（因 plant-level 噪声主导）还是保持？
4. 物理信息损失或物理一致性约束（如将 Erbs 分解嵌入网络、或用物理方程做正则）能否把 PatchTST/GRU 的鲁棒性前沿进一步向外推？
5. 高自相关误差（ρ→0.9）对循环记忆/注意力机制的"持续扰动"破坏，是否可以通过对抗式/ curriculum 训练让模型显式学习抵御 sustained bias？
6. Z1（Arid）与 Z5（High Plains）在局部 ρ 扫描下出现 top-1/top-2 模型轮换，是否意味着这两个区的最优模型选择对 NWP 误差结构的先验假设更敏感，需要在线自适应选模型？
