> **一句话总结：** 本文首次在开放权重模型上独立复现了Anthropic的"规划位点"发现，并提出"预述(prolepsis)"概念——Transformer在早期层做出不可撤销的决策承诺，由特定注意力头路由至输出且后续层无法纠正；该模式同时存在于韵律规划和事实回忆两种任务中，但使用完全不同的注意力头和网络深度。

> **TL;DR:** This paper provides the first independent replication of Anthropic's planning-site finding on open models (Gemma 2 2B, Llama 3.2 1B) using open cross-layer transcoders (CLTs). It introduces "prolepsis" — an architectural motif where transformers commit to decisions at early layers and route them through task-specific attention heads with zero correction from subsequent layers. The motif appears in both rhyme planning (mid-layer routing) and factual recall (late-layer routing) with zero overlap between recurring planning and factual routing heads. Planning is invisible to all six residual-stream methods tested; only sparse CLT features can reveal it. Layer depth (not parameter count) determines commitment capability: Llama 3.2 1B (16 layers) can search but cannot commit, while Gemma 2 2B (26 layers) can do both. All experiments run on a single 16GB consumer GPU.

## 核心贡献

- 首次在开放权重模型上独立复现了Anthropic的"规划位点"发现，使用了三种规模的开放CLT（426K、2.5M、524K）
- 证明了规划电路对六种残差流方法完全不可见，只有CLT稀疏特征分解才能观测到规划电路
- 鉴别出将规划决策路由到输出的注意力头（以L21:H5为首），填补了Anthropic标注为"对归因图不可见"的空白
- 发现了深度阈值：搜索需要<=16层，不可撤销的承诺需要>16层；参数量不是决定因素
- 发现"预述(prolepsis)"作为一种跨任务的架构模体：规划使用中间层注意力头路由，事实回忆使用晚期层注意力头路由，两者在集合层面上零重叠
- 全部实验可在单张16GB消费级GPU上复现，总计算量不到2小时

## Key Contributions

- **First independent replication** of Anthropic's planning-site localization (Lindsey et al., 2025) on open-weights models with open CLTs at three scales
- **Demonstration that planning is invisible** to six residual-stream methods (activation addition, contrastive steering, causal patching, etc.), establishing CLTs as necessary — not merely convenient — for observing the planning circuit
- **Identification of attention routing heads** (led by L21:H5 in Gemma) that carry planning decisions from the planning site to the output, filling the gap flagged as "invisible to attribution graphs" by Lindsey et al.
- **Minimum-depth threshold for commitment**: search operates within <=16 layers while irrevocable commitment requires >16 layers, with causal evidence from layer-skipping experiments
- **Discovery of prolepsis as a cross-task architectural motif**: the same structural template (early commitment, routing-mediated propagation, zero correction) appears in both rhyme planning and factual recall, but through different heads at different depths with zero aggregate overlap
- **Full reproducibility** on a single consumer GPU (16 GB VRAM), total compute under 2 hours

## Methodology

The paper is structured as five sequential research questions (Q1–Q5), each answer motivating the next:

1. **Q1 (Observability):** Six standard residual-stream steering methods were applied to redirect rhyme endings in Gemma 2 2B — all failed (0% target hit). The same methods succeed on factual recall (39.3% redirect). Planning occupies a fundamentally different computational substrate than factual recall.

2. **Q2 (Replication):** Following Lindsey et al.'s suppress+inject position-sweep protocol, CLT features for natural rhyme groups are suppressed while alternative rhyme features are injected at each token position. On Gemma 2 2B (426K CLT): 70% of 136 pairs localize at the planning site (last token), with best redirect P("around")=0.483 from baseline ~5e-8 (10-million-fold increase). On Llama 3.2 1B: 85% of 47 pairs localize, with best P("that")=0.834.

3. **Q3 (Routing mechanism):** Attention pattern changes across all 208 heads during suppress+inject reveal attention routing heads. L21:H5 dominates in Gemma (delta=-0.046, 1.7x the next head). Suppress+inject produces 13x stronger routing than inject-only. Planning operates through a soft attractor regime (proportional response to perturbation) distinct from factual recall's hard attractor basin.

4. **Q4 (Minimum architecture):** Layer-skipping experiments on Gemma show skipping L22-L25 destroys rhyming (0/10 couplets). Logit lens reveals Llama shows transient rhyme-word activation at L3-L14 that dissipates by L15, while Gemma sustains activation from L14 through L25. Depth, not parameter count, is the architectural bottleneck.

5. **Q5 (Cross-task generality):** Counterfactual patching on 89 gold pairs shows factual decisions become irrevocable by layer 12 in Llama. Factual routing heads concentrate in late layers (9-15), while planning routing heads concentrate in mid-layers (5-11). Zero overlap between recurring planning and factual routing heads at the aggregate level.

The CLT feature discovery protocol involved scanning all 425,984 features against 256,768 token embeddings, cross-referencing with the CMU Pronouncing Dictionary to identify 35 rhyme groups for Gemma and 12 for Llama.

## Results & Highlights

- **Planning-site spike replicates with identical geometry** across both models and three CLT scales (426K, 2.5M, 524K), matching Anthropic's ~70% localization rate on Claude 3.5 Haiku.
- **Best redirect: P("at") = 0.986** (2.1 billion-fold increase) on Gemma 426K; P("that") = 0.834 on Llama.
- **At the 2.5M CLT scale**, every rhyme word has its own dedicated feature (209 words, mean decoder rank 1.0), with best ratio of 3.78 trillion-fold.
- **Six residual-stream methods fail completely** for planning while succeeding for factual recall, revealing two fundamentally different computational substrates within the same model.
- **Layer depth determines commitment**: Llama (16 layers, 1B params) can search but not commit; Gemma (26 layers, 2B params) can do both.
- **Zero cross-task head overlap**: recurring planning routing heads (mid-layers 5-11) share no heads with factual routing top-10 (late layers 9-15).
- **Irrevocability confirmed**: injecting contradictory features at post-commitment layers (L23-L25) at up to 2x commitment strength fails to redirect the output; L21:H5 routing delta is locked.
- **Exhaustive domain search**: scanning 2.5M features for code and cooking domains found zero domain-specific features, identifying CLT coverage as the methodology's current boundary.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The first independent replication of a major Anthropic finding on open models is a valuable contribution. The concept of "prolepsis" as a cross-task architectural motif and the discovery of attention routing heads filling Anthropic's acknowledged gap are genuinely novel. The negative result (six methods fail for planning) is methodologically important.

- **Rigor: Moderate-to-High.** The Q1-Q5 structure is admirably systematic. The CLT implementation is validated against the reference (90/90 match). However, several limitations temper confidence: (1) the Gemma attention routing results are from a single prompt per CLT scale, not the full 136 pairs — variance is unreported; (2) the planning routing on Llama uses only 4 prompts across 3 rhyme groups vs. 89 pairs for factual recall — a substantial asymmetry; (3) the depth claim comparing Llama (16 layers) vs. Gemma (26 layers) conflates depth with training data, tokenizer, GQA, and sliding-window attention differences — the author acknowledges this but it weakens the causal claim; (4) the two-stage decomposition (format/rhyme/output) is based on only four layer-suppression conditions and labeled "preliminary."

- **Reproducibility: Excellent.** Open models, open CLTs, complete reproduction commands in Appendix F, validated against reference implementation. All experiments run on a single 16GB consumer GPU in under 2 hours. Code and data promised upon publication.

- **Significance: Moderate-to-High.** The prolepsis concept and the attention routing discovery advance mechanistic interpretability. The finding that planning is invisible to residual-stream methods but observable via CLTs has practical implications for interpretability tool selection. The depth threshold finding, while not causally isolated, is suggestive. The safety implications of irrevocable commitment are noteworthy.

- **Red flags:** (1) Single-author paper with extensive LLM disclosure raises questions about the depth of human contribution to experimental design and interpretation, though the author takes explicit responsibility. (2) The paper exhaustively tests only the two domains (rhyme, factual recall) where CLT features happen to exist — the prolepsis claim's generality is bounded by CLT coverage, which the author's own search shows is extremely narrow (zero features for code or cooking). (3) The 13x suppress amplification ratio and several other quantitative claims are from single-prompt measurements. (4) The paper's claims about "architectural" prolepsis rest on only two tasks on two models — generalization is uncertain.

## Strengths

- First rigorous independent replication of a high-profile Anthropic result on open infrastructure
- Systematic Q1-Q5 structure that naturally builds the narrative
- Strong negative result: six methods fail for planning, establishing CLT necessity
- Discovery of attention routing heads fills a gap explicitly flagged by Anthropic
- Cross-task comparison (planning vs. factual recall) with zero overlap finding is compelling
- Excellent reproducibility: single consumer GPU, full reproduction commands
- Honest and thorough discussion of limitations, including the CLT coverage constraint
- Exhaustive domain search (code, cooking) that defines methodology boundaries rather than cherry-picking

## Limitations

- **CLT coverage constraint**: The entire methodology depends on the existence of open CLTs with domain-specific features. The exhaustive search found only rhyme groups have such features; code and cooking produce zero. Prolepsis cannot be tested in other domains with current CLT scales.
- **Small sample sizes for key claims**: Gemma routing from one prompt; Llama planning from 4 prompts; layer suppression from 10 couplets. Variance is unreported.
- **Depth vs. confounds**: The key claim that "depth, not parameters, determines commitment" compares two models that differ in training data, tokenizer, architecture (GQA, sliding window), and depth — depth is not isolated as the causal variable.
- **Scope of prolepsis**: Only two tasks tested. Whether the motif extends to reasoning, code generation, or multi-step planning is unknown.
- **Single-author with heavy LLM use**: While transparently disclosed, the extent of Claude usage for experimental design and interpretation is unusual for a paper of this scope.

## Relevance

本文对研究Transformer内部机制和机械可解释性(mechanistic interpretability)的读者具有高度相关性，特别是对关注LLM如何进行规划、决策承诺以及电路分析(circuit analysis)的研究者。其发现——规划电路对传统残差流方法不可见、需要CLT才能观测——对设计可解释性工具和AI安全研究有直接启示。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Does prolepsis extend to reasoning tasks (e.g., math, logic) or multi-step planning beyond simple rhyme selection? The CLT coverage boundary currently prevents testing this — what CLT scales would be needed?
2. Can the depth threshold finding be causally validated by testing model families with controlled depth variation (e.g., Pythia suite) once CLTs become available for them?
3. Is irrevocability a property of the prolepsis circuit specifically, or a generic feature of residual-stream architectures? The contradictory-feature injection test is suggestive but limited to one prompt.
4. What are the safety implications of early irrevocable commitment for alignment interventions — if a model commits to a harmful output at layer 10, can any post-hoc intervention redirect it?
5. Why do CLTs at current scales develop discrete features only for phonological/rhyme domains? Is this a fundamental limitation of sparse dictionary learning at these scales, or a consequence of the training data distribution?
