> **一句话总结：** 本文提出基于表征工程（RepE）的认知逆向工程框架，通过子空间正交化、回归加权和双向因果操控，在8个主流LLM（Llama/Qwen/Gemma）中验证了"社会比较嫉妒"情绪由"优越感"（触发开关）和"领域自我定义相关性"（强度放大器）两个心理前因线性组合而成，且可通过表征层面的机械干预检测和抑制有毒情绪状态。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces a Cognitive Reverse-Engineering framework extending Representation Engineering (RepE) to dissect social-comparison jealousy in LLMs. Across 8 models (Llama-3.1/3.2, Qwen-2.5, Gemma-3), the authors show that jealousy is encoded as a structured linear combination of two psychological antecedents -- Superiority (the trigger) and Domain Self-Definitional Relevance (the intensity amplifier) -- with representations maturing in mid-to-late layers. Bidirectional causal steering can amplify or suppress jealousy outputs, suggesting a route toward representational monitoring and safety intervention in multi-agent AI systems.

## 核心贡献

- **提出认知逆向工程框架：** 将传统表征工程（RepE）从粗粒度二元安全任务扩展为可处理复杂社会情绪的四阶段分析流水线（表征提取→子空间正交化→统计加权→因果干预）。
- **首次对LLM中复杂情绪进行白盒语义解剖：** 以社会比较嫉妒为案例，将其分解为"优越感"和"领域自我定义相关性"两个高维语义因子，证明LLM以结构化线性组合编码该情绪，且权重动态与人类心理学理论一致。
- **引入安慰剂控制变量（星期几）提高严谨性：** 有效排除了统计伪影，验证了提取的表征确实反映有意义的心理语义结构。
- **提出AI安全应用方向：** 通过实时表征监控和机械情绪抑制，为多智能体环境中的欺骗性对齐检测和有毒社会行为预防提供了可行路径。

## Key Contributions

- **Cognitive Reverse-Engineering Framework:** A four-phase pipeline extending RepE with subspace orthogonalization, regression-based factor weighting, and bidirectional causal steering, going beyond traditional coarse-grained binary RepE applications.
- **First White-Box Dissection of a Complex Emotion in LLMs:** Decomposes social-comparison jealousy into Superiority (trigger) and Domain Self-Definitional Relevance (amplifier), showing that LLMs encode this emotion as a weighted linear combination consistent with human psychological theory.
- **Placebo-Controlled Rigor:** Introduces "Weekday" as a negative control variable to demonstrate that extracted representations capture genuine semantic structure rather than statistical artifacts.
- **AI Safety Implications:** Demonstrates that toxic emotional states can be mechanically detected and surgically suppressed via representation-level interventions, opening pathways for real-time monitoring in multi-agent environments.

## Methodology

The framework operates in four phases:

**Phase I -- Representation Extraction and Validation:** Uses the Linear Artificial Tomography (LAT) method from RepE to extract concept vectors. Contrastive scenario pairs (200 per concept) are wrapped in task templates, and hidden states from the last token position are collected. An LLM-Consistency Filter retains only pairs where the model's zero-shot output matches human-verified labels. Mean Difference vectors are computed layer-by-layer with 5-fold cross-validation and L2 normalization.

**Phase II -- Subspace Orthogonalization:** Raw concept vectors may exhibit collinearity. For each target factor, a null-space projection removes variance shared with other factors (including the placebo), yielding purified unique-effect directions. This ensures that Superiority, Relevance, and Weekday representations are decorrelated.

**Phase III -- Statistical Weighting and Validity Check:** OLS regression models the jealousy scalar score as a linear combination of purified factor projection scores across mid-to-late layers. Standardized beta coefficients quantify each factor's decision weight. The placebo (Weekday) must show near-zero beta, while psychological factors must show substantial magnitude and statistical significance (p < 0.05). R-squared and Pearson correlation with ground truth validate the linear composition hypothesis.

**Phase IV -- Causal Intervention Framework:** Bidirectional steering (addition/subtraction of alpha * z_hat) is applied systematically across all layers. The Generalization Set is partitioned into Low-Jealousy (for stimulation) and High-Jealousy (for suppression) subsets. Layer-wise profiling identifies optimal intervention zones in mid-to-late layers. Targeted interventions at optimal layers quantify each factor's causal contribution.

**Models:** Llama-3.1-8B, Llama-3.2-1B, Qwen-2.5-7B/14B/32B, Gemma-3-4B/12B/27B. **Steering coefficients:** alpha=15 for Llama/Qwen, alpha=3000 for Gemma (due to larger activation magnitudes).

## Results & Highlights

- **Representation Maturation:** Across all 8 models, early-layer representations fluctuate severely. Concepts stabilize in mid-to-late layers with near-100% cross-validation accuracy, confirming that semantic encoding matures progressively through network depth.
- **Linear Composition Confirmed:** OLS regression achieves high R-squared in late layers across all models. The jealousy representation is well-explained by a weighted linear combination of Superiority and Relevance, with the Weekday placebo consistently near zero.
- **Structural Convergence with Human Psychology:** In all models, Superiority has higher initial weight but Relevance's weight steadily increases through deeper layers, eventually surpassing or matching Superiority. This mirrors the psychological account: Superiority is the zero-to-one trigger; Relevance is the intensity multiplier.
- **Asymmetric Steerability:** Superiority shows slightly better negative steerability (suppression works more readily), while Relevance shows slightly better positive steerability (amplification works more readily). This is consistent with Superiority being the foundational "on/off switch" and Relevance being the "volume knob."
- **Orthogonal Knockout Success on Gemma:** While the "Hydra Effect" (representational self-repair) was observed in other architectures, Gemma-3-12B showed clear drops in jealousy scores after orthogonal projection away from factor directions, indicating less self-repair in that architecture.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (Moderate-High):** The extension of RepE from binary safety concepts to fine-grained cognitive decomposition of complex emotions is genuinely novel. The combination of appraisal theory with subspace orthogonalization and placebo controls is a meaningful methodological contribution. However, the individual components (RepE, mean difference, null-space projection, activation steering) are not new.

**Rigor (High):** The four-phase pipeline is well-designed with appropriate controls. The placebo variable (Weekday) is a strong methodological choice. The 5-fold cross-validation, consistency filtering, and layer-wise profiling demonstrate thorough experimental practice. The use of 8 models across 3 families strengthens generalizability claims.

**Reproducibility (Moderate):** The methodology is described in sufficient detail to reproduce. However, the dataset was generated by Gemini-3-Pro with human verification -- full dataset release would be needed for exact reproduction. The steering coefficient alpha is set differently per model family (15 vs. 3000) without a systematic calibration procedure described.

**Significance (Moderate-High):** The finding that LLMs structurally encode complex emotions in alignment with human psychological theory is significant for both AI safety and cognitive science. The safety application (monitoring and suppression of toxic emotional states) is forward-looking but currently demonstrated only on synthetic scenarios.

**Red Flags:**
- The ground-truth jealousy labels in the Generalization Set are assigned by a deterministic rule-based mapping (e.g., Sup=1 + Rel=1 -> Jealousy=5), not by human annotators reading the vignettes. This circular labeling could inflate correlation metrics.
- The paper studies only two of four identified psychological antecedents (excluding Similarity and Comparison Alternative), which limits completeness.
- The alpha values for steering (15 and 3000) are hand-tuned without a principled calibration method, raising questions about how sensitive results are to these choices.
- The paper's claims about AI safety implications (e.g., deceptive alignment detection) are speculative and not demonstrated empirically.

## Strengths

- Well-grounded in established psychological theory (Salovey & Rodin, Social Comparison Theory, SEM model), giving the analysis genuine interdisciplinary depth.
- The four-phase pipeline is methodologically thorough and well-motivated, with each phase building logically on the previous.
- Placebo control (Weekday) is an excellent design choice that strengthens causal claims.
- Evaluation across 8 models from 3 distinct families (Llama, Qwen, Gemma) provides strong evidence for generalizability.
- The discussion section thoughtfully connects model-internal computational dynamics to human empirical psychology, going beyond surface-level comparisons.
- Clear writing and structured presentation make the paper accessible to readers from both NLP and psychology backgrounds.

## Limitations

- **Circular ground-truth labeling:** The jealousy scores in G1 are assigned by a rule-based formula rather than independent human judgment, which risks confirming the very linear composition hypothesis being tested.
- **Synthetic scenario limitations:** All stimuli are generated vignettes rather than naturalistic text. Ecological validity for real-world AI deployment remains untested.
- **Incomplete factor coverage:** Only 2 of 4 theoretically identified jealousy antecedents are analyzed. The roles of Similarity and Comparison Alternative are deferred to future work.
- **Architecture-dependent findings:** The Hydra Effect varies across architectures (absent in Gemma, present in Llama), making general claims about intervention efficacy difficult.
- **Scale limitations:** Only open-source models up to 32B parameters are tested. Whether these findings transfer to larger proprietary models (GPT-4, Claude) is unknown.
- **Emotion scope:** The framework is demonstrated on a single complex emotion (jealousy). Generalization to other complex emotions (guilt, shame, pride) remains to be shown.

## Relevance

本文对LLM内部情绪表征的可解释性研究具有重要参考价值，其基于表征工程的认知逆向工程框架为理解复杂社会情绪的编码机制提供了新范式，对多智能体安全、情绪对齐以及情感计算的mechanistic理解均有直接启发。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How would the framework perform with human-annotated (rather than rule-based) ground-truth jealousy labels? Would the linear composition hypothesis still hold?
2. Can the Cognitive Reverse-Engineering framework generalize to other complex social emotions (e.g., guilt, schadenfreude, pride) that have well-established multi-factor psychological theories?
3. Does the structural alignment between LLM emotion encoding and human psychology survive when tested on naturalistic, non-vignette text (e.g., literary fiction, social media posts, real dialogues)?
4. How sensitive are the causal intervention results to the choice of steering coefficient alpha? Is there a principled calibration method?
5. Could representational monitoring of emotional factors during RLHF training detect reward hacking or deceptive alignment in practice?
