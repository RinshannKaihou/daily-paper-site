Targeted Exploration via Unified Entropy Control for Reinforcement Learning

arXiv: 2604.14646

Date: 2026-04-16

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14646v1

一句话总结： 本文提出UEC-RL框架，通过在困难样本上自适应增加高温度采样进行定向探索，并引入经验回放机制稳定策略熵，在LLM和VLM推理任务上相比GRPO在Geometry3K数据集取得37.9%的相对提升。
TL;DR: UEC-RL is a bidirectional entropy control framework for RL-based LLM/VLM post-training. It detects prompts where all G rollouts fail (difficult prompts), resamples with elevated temperature to explore low-probability trajectories, and uses a replay-based stabilizer to prevent entropy explosion. On Geometry3K, it achieves 37.9% relative improvement over GRPO, with consistent Pass@1 and Pass@k gains across 7 text and 4 multimodal reasoning benchmarks using Qwen2.5-Math-7B, Llama3.1-8B, and Qwen2.5-VL-7B. Accepted at ACL 2026 Findings. Code is publicly available.

Key Contributions

  • Introduces UEC-RL, the first framework to provide bidirectional entropy regulation (increase for exploration, decrease for stabilization) within GRPO-based RL training for LLMs/VLMs.
  • Proposes a targeted exploration mechanism that identifies difficult prompts (where all initial rollouts fail), resamples with elevated temperature t' > 1, and filters trajectories by positive advantage to retain only informative samples.
  • Designs a controllable entropy stabilizer that replays high-advantage trajectories from a FIFO buffer to consolidate reliable behaviors and prevent unbounded entropy growth.
  • Provides theoretical grounding via entropy change theorems (Theorem 4.1 and 4.2), connecting temperature-scaled sampling to controlled entropy increase and replay to entropy reduction.
  • Demonstrates consistent improvements across 11 benchmarks (7 text + 4 multimodal) on three model families, with 37.9% relative gain on Geometry3K over GRPO.

Methodology

UEC-RL modifies the standard GRPO training loop with three coordinated steps:

Step 1 -- Regular Rollout: For each prompt in a minibatch, sample G responses from the current policy. Compute group-normalized advantages. If at least one response is correct (reward > 0), retain samples with nonzero advantage as regular samples O_R.

Step 2 -- Exploration Rollout: For prompts where all G rollouts fail (difficult prompts), sample G' additional responses using a softened distribution with temperature t' > 1. Retain only trajectories with non-negative advantage as high-value samples O_H. The elevated temperature flattens the action distribution, making low-probability but informative reasoning paths more accessible. By Theorem 4.1, this induces a negative covariance between log-probability and advantage, which drives entropy increase precisely where exploration is needed.

Step 3 -- Replay Stabilization: Every f_replay optimization steps, sample from a FIFO replay buffer containing recent high-advantage trajectories (advantage > A_0) discovered during both regular and exploration rollouts. Replaying these trajectories strengthens their gradients, shifting probability mass toward correct reasoning patterns. By Theorem 4.2, increasing the likelihood of positive-advantage trajectories produces positive covariance, which reduces entropy and stabilizes training.

The interplay between exploration (entropy increase on hard problems) and stabilization (entropy decrease via replay) keeps policy entropy within an optimal operating range, avoiding both entropy collapse (premature convergence) and entropy explosion (training instability).

Key hyperparameters: exploration temperature t' (default 1.2), exploration group size G' (default 20), replay buffer size s' (default 5120), replay frequency f_replay (default every 5 steps), advantage threshold A_0 (default 1).

Results & Highlights

  • Qwen2.5-Math-7B (text benchmarks): UEC-RL achieves average 53.62 across 7 benchmarks vs. GRPO's 50.34 (+3.28 absolute). Notable gains on AIME24 (+2.7), MATH (+2.8), Minerva (+6.7), and MMLU_pro (+5.2).
  • Llama3.1-8B-Instruct (text benchmarks): UEC-RL achieves average 46.61 vs. GRPO's 45.20 (+1.41 absolute), showing cross-architecture generalization.
  • Qwen2.5-VL-7B (multimodal benchmarks): UEC-RL achieves average 55.26 vs. GRPO's 54.19 (+1.07 absolute) across 4 visual reasoning benchmarks.
  • Geometry3K (in-domain VLM): UEC-RL achieves 55.41 vs. GRPO's 50.75, a 37.9% relative improvement. Training is also more efficient at 0.79x the per-step time of GRPO.
  • Pass@k evaluation: UEC-RL consistently produces stronger sampling curves on AIME24 and AIME25 across different k values, indicating a more diverse and reliable policy.
  • Ablation: Removing exploration drops accuracy by 5.0 points; removing the stabilizer drops it by 5.93 points. Both components are complementary and necessary.
  • Parameter sensitivity: Best performance arises from balancing exploration temperature and replay buffer size. Too much exploration without stabilization leads to entropy explosion; too little exploration leads to entropy collapse.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (Moderate-High): The idea of bidirectional entropy control for GRPO is well-motivated and fills a clear gap. The targeted exploration mechanism (detecting failure, resampling with higher temperature) is straightforward but effective. The replay stabilizer is a well-known technique adapted to this context. The theoretical grounding (Theorems 4.1 and 4.2) is borrowed from prior work (Liu 2025, Cui et al. 2025) rather than newly derived, though the application is novel.
  • Rigor (Moderate): The paper provides theoretical justification for both components, though Theorem 4.1 is a restatement of prior results. Experiments are thorough across benchmarks and model families. However, only single training runs are reported per setting (no variance/standard deviation across seeds), which is a notable gap for claims about stability. The "max checkpoint" reporting strategy (saving every 10 steps and reporting the best) may overestimate performance compared to final-checkpoint evaluation.
  • Reproducibility (Good): Code is publicly available. Implementation details are well-specified in Appendix B, including hyperparameters, hardware (8xA800 GPUs), and training configurations for all compared methods.
  • Significance (Moderate-High): The work addresses a practically important problem (entropy collapse in GRPO) that affects current RL-based reasoning training pipelines. The consistent improvements across multiple benchmarks and model families suggest genuine utility. The 37.9% relative gain on Geometry3K is substantial, though the absolute improvement over GRPO on text benchmarks is more modest (+2.88 average on Qwen2.5-Math-7B).
  • Red flags: (1) Single-run experiments without error bars weaken statistical claims. (2) Reporting maximum checkpoint performance rather than final or average performance may cherry-pick favorable results. (3) The method introduces 4 new hyperparameters (t', G', s', f_replay, A_0) that require task-specific tuning, as acknowledged in the Limitations section. (4) Some of the multimodal benchmark improvements are small (MathVision: -0.29 relative to GRPO), suggesting the gains are not uniform.

Strengths

  • Well-motivated problem formulation: entropy collapse is a real and documented issue in GRPO-based training.
  • Clean, modular design: exploration and stabilization are complementary and independently ablatable.
  • Comprehensive evaluation: 11 benchmarks, 3 model families, both Pass@1 and Pass@k metrics.
  • Theoretical grounding connects the mechanism to entropy dynamics, providing principled justification.
  • Good training efficiency: 0.79x per-step time of GRPO while achieving better accuracy.
  • Code availability ensures reproducibility.

Limitations

  • Sensitive to hyperparameter choices: exploration temperature t', replay buffer size s', and advantage threshold A_0 must be tuned per task, limiting out-of-the-box applicability.
  • No multi-seed experiments: all results are from single training runs, making it difficult to assess variance and statistical significance.
  • Max-checkpoint reporting may inflate performance estimates compared to practical deployment scenarios.
  • The method introduces additional sampling cost on difficult prompts (G'=20 extra rollouts), though this is partially offset by the replay mechanism.
  • Theoretical contributions rely on existing results rather than novel analysis.
  • Some multimodal improvements are marginal (MathVision shows slight regression vs. GRPO).

Relevance

本文解决的是RL训练LLM/VLM推理能力时的探索不足与训练不稳定问题，提出的方法直接适用于当前主流的GRPO训练流程，对提升大模型推理能力的RL后训练具有实际参考价值。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does UEC-RL scale to larger models (e.g., 32B, 70B, 72B) where exploration dynamics may differ significantly?
  • Can the exploration temperature t' and replay budget s' be adaptively scheduled based on real-time entropy measurements, reducing the need for task-specific tuning?
  • What is the interaction between UEC-RL and other GRPO improvements (e.g., dynamic sampling, curriculum learning, process reward models)?
  • How does UEC-RL behave on non-reasoning tasks (e.g., code generation, open-ended dialogue) where verifiable rewards are available but the reasoning structure differs?
  • Would multi-seed experiments with final-checkpoint evaluation confirm the robustness of the reported gains?
