Beyond Importance Sampling: Rejection-Gated Policy Optimization

arXiv: 2604.14895

Date: 2026-04-16

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14895

一句话总结： 本文提出RGPO（Rejection-Gated Policy Optimization），将重要性采样中的"重加权"范式替换为可微的"接受门"机制（sigmoid gating），在MuJoCo连续控制任务上显著优于PPO（Walker2d +81%, Ant +47%），并在RLHF偏好对齐实验中实现帕累托最优——同时获得最高奖励和最低KL散度。
TL;DR: RGPO replaces the importance sampling ratio in policy optimization with a smooth, differentiable acceptance gate g(r_theta) in [0,1], turning sample "reweighting" into principled "selection." It unifies TRPO, PPO, and REINFORCE under a single framework via the effective gradient weight w(r) = g'(r) * r. Theoretically, RGPO guarantees bounded gradient variance even when IS ratios are heavy-tailed (where IS variance diverges). On MuJoCo benchmarks, RGPO outperforms PPO on Walker2d (+81%, p=0.047) and Ant (+47%) with dramatically better cross-seed stability (0.36-0.75x PPO's std), maintains 0% KL spike rate vs. PPO's 44-84%, and significantly outperforms PPO at 6M steps on HalfCheetah (3095 vs. 1116, p=0.028). In RLHF fine-tuning of Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct on Anthropic HH-RLHF, RGPO achieves Pareto-dominant results: highest reward (+14.8% vs. PPO-RLHF) and lowest KL to reference model (-16.0% vs. PPO-RLHF, -53.1% vs. GRPO).

核心贡献

  • 提出了从"重加权"到"选择"的范式转换： 将策略优化中的核心问题从"每个样本应该贡献多少权重？"重新表述为"这个样本是否应该被使用？"，引入可微的接受门 alpha_theta = g(r_theta) in [0,1] 替代无界的重要性采样比率。
  • 统一了现有算法框架： 通过有效梯度权重 w(r) = g'(r) * r，证明REINFORCE是RGPO在策略同态极限下的精确特例，TRPO和PPO是梯度层面的对应关系，AWR是密切相关的变体。
  • 理论保证： 证明了梯度偏差有界（Theorem 1）、梯度方差有限且有界（Theorem 2，即使重要性权重为重尾分布时IS方差发散）、以及类似TRPO的近似单调策略改进保证（Theorem 3）。
  • RLHF扩展的双比率门设计： 自然扩展到RLHF场景，使用锚定到前一轮策略和参考模型的双比率门，实现对齐漂移和信任域的同时控制。
  • 计算开销与PPO相当： 无需二阶优化，sigmoid门仅增加逐元素运算，实测每轮迭代时间差异小于1%。

Key Contributions

  • Paradigm shift from reweighting to selection: Replaces the unbounded importance sampling ratio r_theta with a smooth, differentiable acceptance gate alpha_theta = g(r_theta) in [0,1], elevating rejection sampling from a data-level heuristic to an optimization principle integrated into the loss function.
  • Unified framework for policy optimization: Shows that the policy gradients of TRPO, PPO, and REINFORCE all correspond to specific choices of the effective gradient weight w(r) = g'(r) * r, with REINFORCE as an exact special case (on-policy limit r=1) and TRPO/PPO as gradient-level correspondences (g outside [0,1]).
  • Theoretical guarantees on bias, variance, and improvement: Proves (1) bounded gradient bias (Theorem 1), (2) finite, bounded gradient variance even under heavy-tailed importance weights where IS variance diverges (Theorem 2 + Proposition 1), and (3) approximate monotonic policy improvement analogous to TRPO (Theorem 3).
  • Dual-ratio gate for RLHF: Extends naturally to preference alignment via a dual-gate objective that anchors learning to both the previous policy (trust region) and the reference model (alignment anchor), providing a differentiable safeguard against reward hacking.
  • Negligible computational overhead: Matches PPO in computational cost (sigmoid gate adds one element-wise operation per sample, <1% wall-clock overhead); no second-order optimization required.

Methodology

RGPO replaces the standard importance sampling surrogate objective L_TRPO = E[r_theta A] with L_RGPO = E[g(r_theta) A], where g: R+ -> [0,1] is a smooth, monotonically non-decreasing acceptance function. The recommended gating function is the sigmoid: g(r) = sigma(k(r-1)), centered at r=1 with sharpness k (default k=5).

The gradient derivation yields the effective weight w(r) = g'(r) * r, which replaces the raw importance ratio in the policy gradient. For the sigmoid gate, this produces a bell-shaped weight profile that suppresses both over-represented samples (r >> 1) and stale samples (r << 1), maintaining bounded gradient contributions.

Three gating designs are proposed: (1) sigmoid gating (recommended, best bias-variance tradeoff), (2) clipped-linear (truncated IS equivalent, lacks lower guard), and (3) temperature-controlled (most conservative, can underfit). The practical objective adds an adaptive KL penalty beta * D_KL(pi_theta || pi_old) with a PPO-style schedule that doubles/halves beta based on observed KL divergence.

For RLHF, a dual-gate variant uses r = max(pi_theta/pi_old, pi_theta/pi_ref) in the sigmoid, simultaneously enforcing a trust region relative to the rollout policy and an alignment anchor relative to the frozen reference model.

Results & Highlights

  • MuJoCo continuous control (1M steps, 3 seeds):
  • Walker2d-v4: RGPO 2998 vs. PPO 1653 (+81%, p=0.047 Welch's t-test)
  • Ant-v4: RGPO 1043 vs. PPO 709 (+47%, p=0.064, RGPO > PPO in all 3 paired comparisons)
  • HalfCheetah-v4 and Hopper-v4: statistically comparable (~0% difference)
  • Cross-seed stability: RGPO's std is 0.36-0.75x PPO's std across all environments
  • KL spike rate: RGPO 0% vs. PPO 44-84% across all environments
  • Long-horizon stability (HalfCheetah-v4, 6M steps):
  • RGPO 3095 vs. PPO 1116 (p=0.028); PPO regresses at all three budget levels (1M/3M/6M)
  • RLHF preference alignment (Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct, Anthropic HH-RLHF, 3 seeds):
  • Reward: RGPO +0.243 (+14.8% vs. PPO-RLHF +0.211)
  • KL to reference: RGPO 0.364 (-16.0% vs. PPO-RLHF 0.434, -53.1% vs. GRPO 0.778)
  • RGPO achieves unique Pareto-dominant outcome among online RL methods
  • TRPO underperforms both PPO and RGPO despite strictest KL control, due to single-update-per-rollout constraint (7x computational cost)
  • Ablation studies confirm: Sigmoid gate (k=5) is optimal; sharpness sweep shows U-shaped performance; AWR fails catastrophically without IS correction (KL=18.85 on Ant, 100% spike rate)

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The conceptual reframing from "reweighting" to "selection" is genuinely new. The unification of TRPO/PPO/REINFORCE under w(r) = g'(r) * r provides real insight, and the differentiable acceptance gate is a clean mathematical contribution. However, the connection to truncated importance sampling (V-trace, ACER) and advantage-weighted regression could have been explored more deeply as prior art.
  • Rigor: Strong. Three formal theorems with complete proofs, clear assumptions, and careful distinction between exact special cases and gradient-level correspondences. The proofs are technically sound (standard applications of triangle inequality, Pinsker's inequality, and the performance difference lemma). The experimental protocol is fair: shared architectures, optimizers, and hyperparameters; 3 seeds per method; Welch's t-tests for significance.
  • Reproducibility: Moderate to Good. The paper provides pseudocode, hyperparameter details, and gating function specifications. However, no code repository is mentioned. The experiments use standard environments (MuJoCo, Anthropic HH-RLHF) and models (Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct), which aids reproducibility. The adaptive beta schedule and sigmoid sharpness k=5 are clearly specified.
  • Significance: Potentially high, with caveats. The RLHF results are the most compelling: Pareto-dominant performance on a standard benchmark with a 1.5B model is notable. The MuJoCo results are strong on Walker2d and Ant but mixed on HalfCheetah/Hopper. The 0% KL spike rate across all environments is a striking result. However, the experiments are limited to relatively small scales (1.5B LLM, standard MuJoCo tasks); whether the gains persist at frontier model scales (70B+, complex reasoning tasks) remains to be seen.
  • Red flags:
  • Only 3 seeds per experiment; while this is standard for MuJoCo, some claimed improvements (e.g., Ant +47%, p=0.064) are marginal in statistical significance.
  • The PPO baseline on HalfCheetah-v4 shows unusually high variance (std=1489 on mean=3107, CV=48%), which could inflate the relative improvement metrics. A more stable PPO implementation might narrow the gap.
  • The authors are not clearly affiliated with recognized institutions, and no code release is mentioned.
  • The AWR baseline with n_epochs=10 is arguably an unfair comparison since AWR was designed for single-epoch updates; the authors acknowledge this but include it as a "why IS anchoring matters" ablation.
  • The RLHF experiment uses a small model (1.5B) and a short context length (256 tokens), far from production-scale RLHF settings.

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated conceptual contribution with genuine theoretical insight (selection vs. reweighting)
  • Strong unification result that meaningfully connects TRPO, PPO, and REINFORCE
  • Rigorous theoretical analysis: bias bounds, variance guarantees (including heavy-tailed regime), and policy improvement guarantees
  • Compelling RLHF results: Pareto-dominant on reward-KL frontier
  • Excellent cross-seed stability (0% KL spike rate) suggesting practical robustness
  • Negligible computational overhead; easy to implement as a drop-in PPO replacement
  • Comprehensive ablation studies covering gate design, sharpness sensitivity, ESS, KL analysis, and beta sensitivity

Limitations

  • Only tested on MuJoCo locomotion tasks and a single RLHF setup with a 1.5B model; generalization to larger models, discrete action spaces (Atari), and complex reasoning tasks is untested
  • Introduces controllable bias; unsuitable for applications requiring strictly unbiased estimators (e.g., off-policy evaluation)
  • Hyperparameter sensitivity: performance depends on gate sharpness k and initial beta_0; while defaults work well, some task-specific tuning may be needed
  • Mixed results on HalfCheetah-v4 and Hopper-v4 (statistically equivalent to PPO)
  • No discrete action space experiments (noted as future work)
  • The theoretical bounds rely on bounded advantages and Lipschitz g; these assumptions may not always hold in practice
  • PPO's unusually high variance on some environments may partially explain the large relative improvements claimed

Relevance

本文对策略优化基础范式的重新思考（从重加权到选择）与RLHF/LLM对齐研究直接相关。RGPO的双比率门机制为在线偏好对齐提供了一种有理论保证的替代方案，适合替代当前广泛使用的PPO-RLHF和GRPO，值得在大规模语言模型上进行后续验证。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does RGPO scale to larger language models (7B, 70B+) and longer context lengths? Does the Pareto-dominant reward-KL tradeoff persist?
  2. Can the acceptance gate g(r) be learned or adapted online rather than fixed to a sigmoid with hand-tuned sharpness k?
  3. What is the interaction between RGPO and group-relative advantage normalization (as used in GRPO)? Could combining both yield further improvements?
  4. How does RGPO perform on discrete action spaces (Atari) and in offline RL settings where the behavior policy is fixed and far from the optimal policy?
  5. Does the dual-ratio gate provide sufficient protection against reward model over-optimization at scale, or are additional mechanisms (e.g., early stopping, reward model ensembles) still necessary?
