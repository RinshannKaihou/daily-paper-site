> **一句话总结：** 本文通过分析LLM推理轨迹发现错误集中在少数早期转移点（伴随熵尖峰），据此提出GUARD框架，在检测到高不确定性转移时进行短时分支纠正，并在后期抑制无意义推理扩张，在多个基准上以更少的token取得了最优准确率。

> **TL;DR:** This paper discovers that LLM reasoning failures are temporally concentrated at a small number of early transition points marked by entropy spikes, and that alternative continuations from the same prefix can still yield correct solutions. Based on these findings, the authors propose GUARD (Guided Uncertainty-Aware Reasoning with Decision control), an inference-time framework that monitors token-level entropy, triggers short-horizon branching (momentum/inhibitory/counterfactual) at high-risk transitions, and suppresses unproductive late-stage expansion. GUARD achieves the best accuracy-length trade-off across 8 benchmarks on models from 1.5B to 32B, attaining 71.3% Pass@1 on QwQ-32B with only ~7.5k tokens. Accepted at ACL 2026.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示了LLM推理失败的动态规律**：通过系统性分析推理轨迹，发现错误并非均匀分布，而是集中在轨迹前30%的少数转移点上（85%以上的失败起始点），且43.5%的错误轨迹仅包含一个无效片段。
- **发现失败转移点的熵信号特征**：错误起始点伴随着token级熵的局部尖峰，错误片段的平均不确定性显著高于正确片段（p<0.001），为自动检测失败提供了可靠信号。
- **提出"认知螺旋"(Epistemic Spiral)现象**：错误发生后，模型会产生局部连贯但全局错误的持续推理扩张，导致错误轨迹显著长于正确轨迹。
- **设计GUARD框架**：基于自适应熵阈值检测高风险转移，通过三分支（惯性/抑制/反事实）短时分支选择最低熵的续写，并在后期阶段抑制"Wait"等犹豫标记以防止无意义推理扩张。
- **跨模型规模和架构的广泛验证**：在1.5B/7B/32B推理模型及通用指令模型上，GUARD均实现了准确率与token效率的最优平衡。

## Key Contributions

- **Empirical characterization of reasoning failure dynamics**: Errors are temporally concentrated, with >85% of failure onsets occurring within the first 30% of the trajectory; 43.5% of incorrect trajectories contain exactly one invalid segment.
- **Entropy-based failure signature**: Failure onsets coincide with localized spikes in token-level Shannon entropy; invalid segments exhibit significantly higher mean uncertainty than valid ones.
- **Local recoverability finding**: Over 20% of failed trajectories can be corrected by alternative continuations from the same valid prefix, indicating failures arise from specific transition choices rather than missing knowledge.
- **GUARD framework**: A lightweight inference-time intervention that (1) detects high-entropy transitions using adaptive quantile-based thresholds, (2) performs short-horizon branching with three complementary strategies (momentum, inhibitory, counterfactual), and (3) suppresses late-stage epistemic spirals via hesitation-token replacement.
- **Strong and efficient results**: Best accuracy-length trade-off across 8 benchmarks on 1.5B--32B models; 71.3% Pass@1 on QwQ-32B with ~7.5k tokens; transfers to general instruction-tuned models (Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct).

## Methodology

**Failure Analysis (Section 3):** The authors use DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B to generate reasoning traces on AMC and AIME benchmarks. Each output is segmented by `\n\n` delimiters, and segment validity is labeled by an external oracle (Gemini 3 Pro with human verification). They define "failure onset" as the transition from a valid segment to an invalid one, and analyze: (1) temporal distribution of onsets, (2) post-onset trajectory length expansion, (3) entropy patterns at failure transitions, and (4) recoverability via alternative sampling from the same prefix.

**GUARD Framework (Section 4):**
1. **Failure Detection** (Sec 4.1): Monitors token-level Shannon entropy during generation. At segment boundaries (after delimiter tokens), triggers intervention when current entropy exceeds the q-th quantile of historical entropy (default q=0.9). This adaptive threshold avoids brittle absolute cutoffs.
2. **Branch-and-Select** (Sec 4.2): When triggered, generates 3 short-horizon (L=200 tokens) branches from the current prefix with shared KV-cache: (a) Momentum branch -- greedy continuation; (b) Inhibitory branch -- prepends "Wait," to disrupt premature patterns; (c) Counterfactual branch -- prepends "Let me reconsider:" to encourage re-framing. Selects the branch with lowest mean entropy and collapses back to a single trajectory.
3. **Late-Stage Control** (Sec 4.3): When remaining budget ratio drops below a threshold (rho_min=0.2), replaces hesitation tokens ("Wait") with an end-of-sequence token to force convergence and prevent epistemic spirals.

**Experimental Setup:** Evaluated on DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen (1.5B/7B), QwQ-32B, JustRL-1.5B, and Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct across AMC23, AIME24/25, MATH500, Minerva, LiveCodeBench, OlympiadBench, and GPQA Diamond. Metrics: Pass@1 accuracy and average output length (tokens). All experiments on 6x RTX 4090, temperature=0.0, max budget=10k tokens.

## Results & Highlights

- **Main results (Table 1)**: GUARD achieves the best accuracy-length trade-off across all model scales. On QwQ-32B, GUARD reaches 71.3% Pass@1 with ~7.5k tokens, outperforming Best-of-N, ToT, Reflexion, DTS, EAGER, and Entro-duction. On the 7B model, GUARD achieves 59.8% average Pass@1 across 8 benchmarks.
- **Efficiency**: GUARD uses significantly fewer tokens than parallel search methods (BoN, ToT) while achieving higher accuracy. For example, on 7B, GUARD uses ~5.5k tokens vs. BoN's ~8k+ tokens with better accuracy.
- **Transferability**: GUARD improves JustRL-1.5B (math-specialized) on both in-domain math and out-of-domain coding tasks, recovering the "capability tax" from domain-specific fine-tuning. Also outperforms EM-INF and Self-Consistency on Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (Table 2).
- **Ablation (Table 3)**: All three branching strategies are necessary (removing Momentum: -5.5%, Counterfactual: -4.4%, Inhibitory: -2.7%). Late-stage control is the most critical component (--6.8% without it).
- **Hyperparameter stability (Figure 6)**: Performance is stable across a range of settings for quantile threshold (q), horizon (L), and termination threshold (rho_min).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: The trajectory-level failure analysis is a genuine contribution -- the finding that errors concentrate early with entropy signatures is clean and actionable. The GUARD framework itself is relatively straightforward engineering (entropy thresholding + short branching + late-stage termination), but it is well-motivated by the empirical analysis. The three-branch design (momentum/inhibitory/counterfactual) is clever but somewhat ad hoc.
- **Rigor**: The failure analysis uses an external oracle (Gemini 3 Pro) for segment validity labeling, which introduces dependency on another LLM's judgment. While human verification is mentioned, details are sparse. Statistical significance is reported (p<0.001 for entropy differences). Ablation study is thorough, covering all components and hyperparameters.
- **Reproducibility**: Code is publicly available. Implementation details are well-specified (hyperparameters, token sets, model versions). Experiments use publicly available models and benchmarks. The approach should be straightforward to reproduce.
- **Significance**: The paper makes an important conceptual contribution by shifting focus from "how much compute to allocate" to "where reasoning first deviates." The GUARD framework demonstrates that targeted, lightweight interventions can be more effective than brute-force scaling. However, the absolute accuracy improvements over baselines are moderate (typically 2-5 percentage points), and the approach is evaluated only on structured reasoning benchmarks.
- **Red flags**: (1) The segment validity oracle relies on another LLM (Gemini 3 Pro), creating circularity concerns for the failure analysis. (2) The entropy-based branching selection criterion (choose lowest entropy continuation) could bias toward "confident but wrong" continuations in some cases. (3) The late-stage control mechanism is essentially a heuristic that replaces "Wait" tokens with EOS -- this is brittle and language-specific. (4) Evaluation is limited to mathematical/scientific reasoning; generalization to open-ended generation is unclear. (5) The use of temperature=0.0 for all experiments raises questions about whether findings generalize to stochastic decoding settings.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated empirical analysis of reasoning failure dynamics with clear actionable findings
- GUARD is lightweight, model-agnostic, and requires no training or external verifiers
- Strong experimental coverage: 8 benchmarks, 4 model scales, comprehensive ablations
- The adaptive quantile-based entropy threshold is more robust than fixed thresholds used in prior work (DTS, EAGER)
- The concept of "epistemic spiral" is a useful mental model for understanding post-failure trajectory expansion
- Public code release enhances reproducibility

## Limitations

- The failure analysis relies on an LLM-based oracle for segment validity, which may inherit biases and is not a ground-truth label
- The branching strategy (prepending fixed phrases like "Wait," or "Let me reconsider:") is prompt-dependent and may not transfer across languages or reasoning styles
- Late-stage control is triggered by a single hesitation token ("Wait"), which is fragile and English-centric
- Only evaluated on structured reasoning benchmarks (math, coding, science QA); applicability to open-ended, creative, or conversational reasoning is untested
- No analysis of how GUARD interacts with RL-trained reasoning models at different training stages or with different reward signals
- The paper does not compare against process reward models (PRMs) or verifier-based methods, which are increasingly popular for inference-time scaling

## Relevance

本文对LLM推理失败的动态分析以及基于不确定性的推理轨迹干预方法，与推理时计算扩展（test-time scaling）的研究方向高度相关。其核心思想——在推理过程中的关键分歧点进行局部干预而非全局扩展计算——对设计更高效的推理策略具有参考价值，尤其适用于数学推理和代码生成等需要长链推理的任务场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does GUARD perform with stochastic decoding (temperature > 0) and does the entropy-based detection remain reliable when the model already produces diverse outputs?
- Can the failure onset detection be improved with additional signals beyond entropy (e.g., attention patterns, hidden state dynamics, or process reward scores)?
- Does the "epistemic spiral" phenomenon extend to open-ended generation tasks (e.g., creative writing, summarization), or is it specific to formal reasoning?
- How does GUARD interact with process reward models (PRMs) -- could PRM scores be used as an additional signal for both failure detection and branch selection?
- What is the computational overhead of the branch-and-select procedure in wall-clock time, and how does it scale with model size beyond 32B parameters?
