> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"案卷逻辑一致性"框架，通过构建390个多查询推理基准和求解器增强的反例引导修复方法，将LLM跨查询矛盾率大幅降低（SetCons从0.56提升至0.94），同时保持单查询准确率基本不变。

> **TL;DR:** This paper formalizes "case-file logical consistency" for multi-query LLM reasoning and introduces a solver-augmented pipeline that tracks symbolic commitments, detects contradictions via SAT/SMT solving, and performs counterexample-guided minimal repairs. Evaluated on a new 390-case benchmark across four reasoning domains with 8 LLMs, the method improves global consistency (SetCons: 0.56 to 0.94) while preserving per-query accuracy, at lower overhead than self-consistency sampling.

## 核心贡献

- **提出"案卷逻辑一致性"(case-file logical consistency)**：将多查询推理形式化为在共享前提集上维护全局可满足信念状态的问题，超越传统单查询准确率评估范式。
- **构建390个多查询推理基准**：涵盖关系/SAT、时序/SMT、策略/规则、欠约束/溯因四个领域，共2450个相互依赖的查询束，所有标签均经求解器验证（Fleiss' kappa = 0.82）。
- **设计集合级评估指标**：包括SetConsRate（全局可满足率）、AUC-PrefixCons（前缀一致性）、ContradictionDensity（矛盾密度）、RevisionCost（修复代价），比单查询准确率更能反映模型逻辑可靠性。
- **提出求解器增强的反例引导修复方法**：通过增量SAT/SMT求解跟踪信念状态，检测矛盾后利用UNSAT core定位冲突，执行最小修改修复，SetCons从0.56提升至0.94。

## Key Contributions

- **Problem formalization**: Introduces "case-file logical consistency" -- maintaining a globally satisfiable belief state across interdependent queries under shared premises, going beyond per-query accuracy evaluation.
- **Benchmark construction**: A 390-case multi-query benchmark spanning relational/SAT, temporal/SMT, policy/rules, and underspecified/abductive domains with 2,450 query bundles, all labels solver-verified (Fleiss' kappa = 0.82).
- **Set-level metrics**: Proposes SetConsRate, AUC-PrefixCons, ContradictionDensity, and RevisionCost to quantify global logical coherence rather than isolated per-query correctness.
- **Solver-augmented repair pipeline**: Incremental SAT/SMT belief-state tracking with UNSAT-core localization and counterexample-guided minimal repair, achieving SetCons improvement from 0.56 to 0.94.

## Methodology

The paper addresses the problem of **case-file logical consistency**: given a shared premise set (case file), an LLM must answer a bundle of interdependent queries while maintaining a globally satisfiable commitment state. The approach has four steps:

1. **Commitment extraction**: After the LLM answers each query with a label (Entailed/Contradicted/Unknown), a structured extractor maps the answer to symbolic commitments in a constraint language (CNF for propositional domains, SMT-LIB for temporal/arithmetic constraints).

2. **Incremental satisfiability checking**: The belief state is updated incrementally as B_t = B_{t-1} AND psi_t, and a SAT/SMT solver (CaDiCaL for SAT, Z3 for SMT) checks satisfiability after each query.

3. **UNSAT-core localization**: When a contradiction is detected, the solver provides an unsat core -- a minimal subset of jointly unsatisfiable commitments -- to pinpoint the conflict.

4. **Counterexample-guided repair**: The LLM is prompted with the conflict information to propose a minimal repair (local flip, local soften, or selective retraction), accepted only if the solver confirms restored satisfiability. A lexicographic objective minimizes past retractions, current label changes, and constraint complexity.

Baselines include No-CoT, Chain-of-Thought, Self-Consistency (K=20), and sequential History conditioning. An optional "logic-filtered self-consistency" variant filters sampled candidates through satisfiability checking before voting.

## Results & Highlights

- **SetCons improvement**: The Check+Repair method improves SetConsRate from baseline ~0.56 to ~0.94 across deep-subset models (DeepSeek-R1, gpt-oss-120b, Qwen2.5-72B, Llama-3.3-70B, Mixtral-8x22B).
- **Consistent across 8 models**: All eight evaluated LLMs (including DeepSeek-V3, Qwen3-235B, Llama-3.1-405B) show SetCons improvements to 0.88--0.97 range.
- **Not from over-abstention**: Unknown prediction rate stays stable at 17--19% across baseline and repair settings, confirming gains come from genuine contradiction repair, not strategic abstention.
- **Low overhead**: Check-only adds ~1.26--1.33x overhead; Check+Repair adds ~1.44--1.58x, substantially cheaper than self-consistency at 2.55--2.75x (K=20 forward passes).
- **Per-query accuracy preserved**: Global coherence gains occur with only mild changes (sometimes slight decreases) in per-query accuracy, demonstrating the gains are orthogonal to local correctness.
- **Domain-specific insights**: Consistency gains are largest in constraint-heavy domains (temporal/capacity) where a single wrong entailment violates global feasibility.
- **Commitment extraction accuracy**: 91--95% across domains; extraction failures trigger conservative Unknown fallbacks rather than false satisfiability judgments.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate)**: The combination of belief-state tracking with solver augmentation for multi-query reasoning is a useful contribution. However, individual components -- solver-augmented reasoning (Logic-LM, Pan et al. 2023), UNSAT-core-guided repair from formal methods, and belief revision theory (AGM 1985) -- are well-established. The novelty lies primarily in the problem formulation (case-file consistency) and the specific pipeline integration, not in any single technique.
- **Rigor (Good)**: The paper provides formal definitions, clear metrics, comprehensive baselines (4 baseline methods), ablations, and domain breakdowns. Solver validation on a stratified sample and inter-annotator agreement (kappa=0.82) strengthen the benchmark. The evaluation across 8 models with both breadth and deep suites is thorough.
- **Reproducibility (Good)**: Code and data are promised to be released. Solver configurations (CaDiCaL v1.4.1, Z3 v4.8.17), hyperparameters, and prompt templates are provided in appendices. The commitment extraction prompt template is included. Decoding settings are specified.
- **Significance (Moderate-High)**: The problem is important -- real-world LLM deployments (legal, clinical, scientific) require multi-query consistency. The benchmark fills a genuine gap. However, the 390-case scale is modest, and the benchmark domains are somewhat artificial (scheduling, access control puzzles). Generalization to truly open-ended, real-world multi-turn dialogues remains untested.
- **Red flags**: (1) Benchmark is relatively small at 390 cases; (2) Commitment extraction relies on LLM prompting, introducing a 5--9% error rate that could propagate; (3) The paper evaluates only on constructed benchmarks, not on naturally occurring multi-query interactions; (4) Workshop paper rather than main conference venue; (5) Authors are early-career researchers with limited prior publication records.

## Strengths

- Well-defined problem formalization that bridges logical reasoning and multi-turn dialogue evaluation.
- Comprehensive evaluation across 8 diverse LLMs with both set-level and sequential settings.
- Low computational overhead compared to self-consistency sampling, making it practical.
- Honest reporting of extraction failure modes and solver timeouts with conservative fallbacks.
- Clean separation between per-query accuracy and global coherence metrics, avoiding conflated evaluation.
- The insight that CoT improves local accuracy but not global consistency is valuable and clearly demonstrated.

## Limitations

- Benchmark scale (390 cases) is modest; may not capture the full diversity of real-world multi-query reasoning.
- Commitment extraction accuracy (91--95%) means 5--9% of extractions are noisy, potentially causing spurious SAT/UNSAT outcomes.
- The framework is limited to decidable logical fragments (propositional SAT, linear arithmetic SMT); richer logics (probabilistic, modal) are left to future work.
- Evaluation is conducted only on constructed benchmarks, not on naturally occurring multi-turn conversations.
- The repair loop is bounded (max 2 attempts), and complex contradictions requiring many retractions are abandoned as "partial."
- The approach assumes queries can be mapped to symbolic commitments, which may not hold for ambiguous or highly naturalistic queries.

## Relevance

本文对LLM推理可靠性和逻辑一致性进行了深入研究，提出了衡量和改善多查询场景下全局一致性的框架与基准。对于关注LLM推理质量评估、神经符号推理以及多轮对话中逻辑可靠性的研究具有重要参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the framework scale to longer query bundles (e.g., 20--50 queries) and naturally occurring multi-turn conversations in domains like legal analysis or clinical reasoning?
- Can commitment extraction be improved beyond LLM prompting, perhaps via fine-tuned extraction models or direct constraint generation?
- What is the interaction between belief-state tracking and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) when premises are partially retrieved rather than fully provided?
- Could the repair mechanism be integrated into training-time objectives (e.g., consistency-aware fine-tuning) rather than applied only at inference time?
- How robust is the approach to adversarial or ambiguously worded queries designed to exploit extraction failures?
