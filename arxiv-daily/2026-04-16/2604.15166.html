Class Unlearning via Depth-Aware Removal of Forget-Specific Directions

arXiv: 2604.15166

Date: 2026-04-16

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15166

一句话总结： 本文提出DAMP，一种无需梯度的单次闭式权重手术方法，通过在不同深度的层中移除遗忘类特定的残差方向来实现类别遗忘，在多个基准测试中比现有方法更接近重新训练的金标准，同时能更好地保持保留类的性能并减少深层表示中的遗忘类残留结构。
TL;DR: The paper identifies that most existing class-unlearning methods either damage retained representations, preserve forget-class structure in deep layers, or merely suppress output via classifier-head bias shifts. To address this, the authors propose DAMP (Depth-Aware Modulation by Projection), a one-shot, closed-form, gradient-free weight-surgery method. DAMP computes class prototypes at each network stage, extracts forget-specific directions as residuals orthogonal to retain-class prototypes, and projects out those directions from the next-layer weights with a depth-aware scaling (smaller edits early, larger edits deep). Across MNIST, CIFAR-10/100, Tiny ImageNet, and across CNN-5, ResNet-18, and ViT architectures, DAMP matches or outperforms prior methods in selective forgetting, retain-class preservation, and representational fidelity to the gold-standard retrained model.

核心贡献

  • 揭示现有类别遗忘方法的根本缺陷： 通过系统分析表明，现有方法（如梯度上升、知识蒸馏、选择性突触抑制等）在"选择性"指标上表现薄弱甚至为负，说明它们要么广泛损害表示，要么仅通过分类器头的偏置抑制来实现表面遗忘，而非真正移除遗忘类的内部表示。
  • 提出DAMP方法： 一种架构无关、无需梯度优化的单次闭式权重手术方法，通过在每个阶段计算遗忘类原型相对于保留类原型的残差方向，并对下一层权重施加投影更新来移除这些方向。
  • 引入无参数的深度感知缩放规则： 基于线性探针的可分离性和确定性深度斜坡，在早期层施加小编辑、在深层施加大编辑，无需任何可调超参数。
  • 自然扩展到多类遗忘： 通过对多个遗忘方向进行正交化和低秩子空间移除，无需引入额外超参数即可处理多类遗忘场景。
  • 全面的评估体系： 除了标准的保留/遗忘准确率外，还引入了选择性指标、层间表示相似性分析（RDM）、t-SNE可视化、偏置偏移分析以及对抗鲁棒性评估，为类别遗忘提供了更全面的评估框架。

Key Contributions

  • Identifies and systematically demonstrates that existing class-unlearning methods often achieve only superficial forgetting through classifier-head bias suppression rather than genuine representational removal, exhibiting weak or negative selectivity scores.
  • Proposes DAMP, a one-shot, closed-form, gradient-free weight-surgery method that removes forget-specific directions from pretrained networks by projecting out residual components orthogonal to retain-class prototypes at each network stage.
  • Introduces a parameter-free depth-aware scaling rule that applies conservative edits to early layers and aggressive edits to deeper layers, derived from probe separability and a deterministic depth ramp.
  • Extends naturally to multi-class forgetting via low-rank subspace removal through QR-orthonormalization of multiple forget directions.
  • Provides a comprehensive evaluation framework including selectivity metrics, representational dissimilarity matrices, bias-shift analysis, adversarial robustness (FGSM/PGD), continual unlearning, and qualitative segmentation results.

Methodology

DAMP operates through the following pipeline:

  1. Stage abstraction: The network is decomposed into L=5 stages. For each stage, the "next learnable operator" (e.g., a linear layer or convolution) and its input space define the edit space.
  1. Class prototype computation: For each class c (retain and forget), the mean feature vector in each stage's edit space is computed as the class prototype, using forward passes through the pretrained model.
  1. Forget-direction extraction: For each forget class, the prototype is projected onto the span of retain-class prototypes. The residual (the component orthogonal to the retain span) constitutes the forget-specific direction. Multiple forget directions are orthonormalized via QR decomposition.
  1. Depth-aware scaling: A per-layer coefficient is computed as the product of a probe-based strength (derived from logistic regression accuracy separating forget vs. retain classes at that layer) and a depth ramp (linear scaling with layer index). No hyperparameters need tuning.
  1. Projection surgery: The weight matrix of the next learnable operator at each stage is updated via right-projection: W' = W(I - alpha Q Q^T), applied from deep to shallow layers.

The method requires no gradient-based optimization, needs only a single forward pass to compute statistics, and the weight edits are applied in closed form.

Results & Highlights

  • Output-level performance: DAMP achieves 0% forget accuracy and retain accuracy close to the retrained gold standard across MNIST, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, and Tiny ImageNet on CNN-5, ResNet-18, and ViT. For example, on CIFAR-10 with CNN-5, DAMP achieves 90.33% retain / 0% forget vs. retraining's 90.70% / 0%.
  • Superior selectivity: DAMP's selectivity score substantially exceeds all baselines and closely approximates the retrained model, indicating that forget-class evidence is removed without damaging retain-class representations. Most baselines show weak, near-zero, or negative selectivity.
  • Representational fidelity: RDM analysis shows DAMP's internal representations at deep layers are closest to the retrained model among all methods. t-SNE visualizations confirm that DAMP reduces forget-class separability while preserving retain-class structure.
  • Minimal bias reliance: Unlike baselines such as GAU and SSD that strongly shift the forget-class bias to large negative values, DAMP's forgetting mechanism operates through weight-space projection alone. Forcing DAMP's biases to match the retrained model's biases leaves results unchanged.
  • Adversarial robustness: Under FGSM and PGD attacks, DAMP maintains retain accuracy closest to the retrained model while keeping forget accuracy at zero.
  • Continual unlearning: DAMP remains stable across sequential forgetting rounds (9 rounds on CIFAR-10), accumulating negligible error compared to methods like SSD or RandRelabel that degrade progressively.
  • Segmentation extension: Qualitative results on Pascal VOC 2012 with DeepLabV3 show DAMP cleanly removes forgotten categories (e.g., "human") from dense predictions while preserving surrounding retained regions.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (Moderate-High): The core idea of using projection-based weight surgery for unlearning is not entirely new (related to orthogonal representation editing methods like ROME/MEMIT), but the application to class unlearning with depth-aware scaling is novel. The insight that existing methods rely on bias suppression rather than representational removal is valuable and well-demonstrated.
  • Rigor (High): The paper provides unusually thorough evaluation: output metrics, selectivity scores, RDM analysis, t-SNE visualization, bias-shift analysis, adversarial robustness, continual unlearning, and segmentation extension. The method is precisely specified with pseudocode. However, all experiments use relatively small-scale models (CNN-5, ResNet-18, small ViT) on standard vision benchmarks.
  • Reproducibility (High): The method is fully specified in closed form with pseudocode. All hyperparameters, training details, and evaluation protocols are documented. Single GPU (A100) experiments. Deterministic seeds used. The absence of a code release is a minor limitation.
  • Significance (Moderate): The contribution is meaningful for the machine unlearning community, particularly the diagnostic insight about bias-shift-based "fake forgetting." The method is practical and efficient. However, the limitation to vision classification with relatively simple architectures and the assumption of approximately unimodal class representations constrain immediate broader impact.
  • Red flags:
  • For Tiny ImageNet with ViT, all alpha values were manually increased by a fixed constant of 3.0, which partially undermines the "no hyperparameter" claim.
  • The retain accuracy on Tiny ImageNet with CNN-5 drops noticeably (50.38% vs. 54.85% for retraining), a larger gap than on other benchmarks.
  • The method assumes class representations can be approximated by mean feature vectors (approximately unimodal), which the authors acknowledge may not hold for complex distributions.
  • The comparison to Logit Masking (LM) is somewhat unfair in representational analyses since LM explicitly does not modify representations -- it serves as a straw man for those specific metrics.

Strengths

  • Thorough and honest analysis of existing methods' failure modes, particularly the bias-shift insight.
  • Clean, principled mathematical formulation with no iterative optimization required.
  • Comprehensive evaluation going well beyond standard accuracy metrics.
  • Architecture-agnostic design applicable to CNNs, ResNets, and Vision Transformers.
  • Demonstrated extension to continual unlearning, multi-class forgetting, and semantic segmentation.
  • The depth-aware scaling rationale is well-motivated by the observation that early layers encode shared features while deeper layers encode class-specific structure.

Limitations

  • Assumes class representations are approximately captured by their mean feature vectors, which may fail for highly multimodal or anisotropic class distributions.
  • Evaluated only on relatively small-scale vision benchmarks (MNIST, CIFAR-10/100, Tiny ImageNet); scalability to large pretrained models (e.g., foundation models) is untested.
  • The manual alpha adjustment for certain configurations (e.g., +3.0 for ViT on Tiny ImageNet) introduces a degree of ad-hoc tuning.
  • No evaluation on NLP or multimodal tasks, though the framework is theoretically architecture-agnostic.
  • The method requires access to training data for computing prototypes, which may not always be available in privacy-sensitive unlearning scenarios.
  • Compared methods like GAU show severe degradation in some settings, which may reflect implementation choices rather than fundamental methodological limitations.

Relevance

本文对机器遗忘的研究提供了系统性的诊断视角和实用的解决方案，特别是在表示层面验证遗忘效果的方法论上具有参考价值。对于关注模型隐私保护、知识编辑和可解释性的研究者而言，DAMP的闭式投影方法和深度感知缩放策略提供了一种高效且可解释的遗忘范式。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does DAMP scale to large pretrained models such as CLIP, SAM, or large language models where class prototypes may be far less structured?
  • Can the prototype-based representation be extended to capture multimodal class distributions (e.g., using class-specific subspaces or covariance-aware directions)?
  • What is the interaction between DAMP's projection surgery and downstream fine-tuning -- does the removed forget-class information remain irrecoverable even under targeted fine-tuning attacks?
  • How does DAMP compare to recent differential privacy-based approaches to unlearning in terms of formal privacy guarantees?
  • Could the depth-aware scaling principle be applied to other model editing tasks beyond class unlearning, such as concept erasure in generative models or safety alignment in LLMs?
