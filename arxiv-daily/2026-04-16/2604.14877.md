> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 PASS@(k,T) 二维评估指标，证明在组合式工具使用任务上 RL 能真正扩展 LLM 智能体的能力边界（而非仅提高效率），且 RL 的改善机制是重新加权基础策略分布而非发明新策略，而同等数据下的 SFT 反而会收缩能力边界。

> **TL;DR:** This paper introduces PASS@(k,T), a two-dimensional metric varying sampling budget k and interaction depth T, to disentangle whether RL expands an LLM agent's capability boundary or merely improves efficiency. On compositional tool-use tasks (HotPotQA bridge questions), GRPO-trained RL genuinely enlarges the capability set: the RL agent's pass-curve diverges above the base model's as k grows (81% vs 77% at k=64, T=5), the opposite of the convergence pattern reported for static reasoning. Matched-data SFT contracts the same boundary. Mechanism analysis reveals RL reweights the base strategy distribution toward higher-reasoning-quality subsets rather than inventing novel search strategies.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 PASS@(k,T) 二维评估框架：** 首个同时变化采样预算 k 和交互深度 T 的智能体评估指标，能严格区分"能力扩展"与"效率提升"两个概念上不同的改进来源。
- **首次实证证明智能体 RL 能扩展能力边界：** 在需要组合式顺序检索的桥接问题（HotPotQA Bridge）上，GRPO RL 使能力边界从 77 个扩展到 81 个问题（净增 +4），且 gap 随 k 增大而加宽——这与静态推理 RLVR 文献中 RL 和 base 模型曲线收敛的结论截然相反。
- **揭示 SFT 在组合式任务上的退化现象：** 在相同训练数据下，SFT 在桥接问题上反而收缩能力边界（从 77 降至 73，净减 -4），RL 与 SFT 的不对称性为 9:1，说明是学习信号（自主探索 vs 专家模仿）而非数据暴露导致了差异。
- **提供三重机制分析：** 通过困惑度分解、策略多样性测量和跨策略交换实验，证明 RL 的改善集中在"如何整合检索信息"而非"搜索什么"，且 RL 保留了基础模型的策略多样性（中位数 51 个不同策略序列），而 SFT 将其压缩至 10 个。

## Key Contributions

- **PASS@(k,T) framework:** The first two-dimensional evaluation metric for LLM agents that jointly varies sampling budget k and interaction depth T, cleanly separating capability expansion from efficiency improvement. It strictly generalizes the standard pass@k metric (recovered when T=0).
- **Empirical demonstration that agentic RL expands capability boundary:** On sequential retrieval tasks (HotPotQA bridge questions), GRPO-trained RL agents solve 5 problems the base model cannot solve in any of 64 attempts, while losing only 1 -- a 5:1 expansion asymmetry. The RL-base gap widens at large k (from essentially tied at k=1 to +4pp at k=64), the opposite of convergence seen in static reasoning.
- **Matched-data SFT regression:** With identical 200-problem training data, SFT contracts the capability boundary on bridge questions (net -4 problems). The 9:1 RL-vs-SFT asymmetry under identical data isolates self-directed exploration as the causal factor.
- **Mechanistic explanation:** Three diagnostic analyses (perplexity decomposition, strategy diversity, cross-policy swapping) reveal RL reweights the base model's strategy distribution toward subsets with better downstream reasoning, concentrating improvement on information integration rather than query formulation. SFT collapses strategy diversity by 3x.

## Methodology

The paper introduces PASS@(k,T), defined as the probability that at least one of k independent trajectories, each with at most T rounds of environment interaction, produces a correct answer. Formally, PASS@(k,T) uses the unbiased hypergeometric estimator from n=64 i.i.d. rollouts. The key insight is that varying k reveals sampling reliability while varying T reveals interaction capability -- a distinction absent in standard pass@k.

Three task categories of increasing complexity serve as the testbed:
- **Category A (MATH-500, no tool access):** Negative control, pure parametric reasoning.
- **Category B (HotPotQA comparison questions):** Two independent retrievals from the question text.
- **Category C (HotPotQA bridge questions):** Two compositionally dependent retrievals, where the second query's argument must be extracted from the first retrieval's result.

All models start from Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct with a search(query) tool using deterministic BM25 retrieval. Three training conditions are compared under matched data (200 HotPotQA problems): (1) base model, (2) LoRA-based SFT on expert trajectories, and (3) GRPO RL with binary exact-match reward. An exploration-bonus RL variant is also tested. Evaluation covers the full grid of k in {1,2,4,8,16,32,64} and T in {0,1,2,3,5}.

The paper defines formal constructs for capability boundary (B_T = set of solvable problems at unlimited k), capability expansion (B_T(RL) \ B_T(base) != empty), and efficiency improvement (higher pass@1 on shared problems), enabling principled decomposition.

## Results & Highlights

- **Category A (pure reasoning):** RL is inert. |B_RL| = |B_base| = 84, confirming tool-use RL does not transfer to parametric reasoning.
- **Category B (independent retrieval):** Modest RL expansion (+4 net problems). Both RL and SFT produce comparable gains, as the two-retrieval strategy is simple enough for either learning signal.
- **Category C (sequential retrieval):** RL expands boundary by +4 net (81 vs 77 problems), while SFT contracts by -4 (73 problems). At PASS@(64,5), RL reaches 0.81 vs base 0.77 vs SFT 0.73. The RL-base gap is 0 at k=1 but widens to +4pp at k=64.
- **Interaction saturation:** Both base and RL agents saturate at T*=2 on Category C; RL's advantage is higher plateau height rather than deeper interaction chains.
- **Marginal value:** On compositional tasks, deepening T dominates at small k; broadening k dominates at k>=8. This provides practical compute budget guidance.
- **Cross-policy swapping:** RL's retrieval plans expand the reachable problem set from 77 to 81; swapping reasoning to RL at fixed base strategy raises EM by 1.3pp. Both strategy and reasoning contribute, with mild tilt toward retrieval planning.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty (High):** The PASS@(k,T) framework is genuinely novel -- it identifies a conceptual gap in existing evaluation (the T dimension has no analog in pass@k) and fills it with clean formal definitions and diagnostic properties. The finding that agentic RL expands capability (contradicting the static-reasoning RLVR literature) is a significant conceptual contribution.

**Rigor (High):** The matched-data experimental design is a standout strength. By giving SFT and RL the same 200 training problems and differing only in learning signal, the paper cleanly isolates the causal mechanism. The three-pronged mechanism analysis (perplexity, diversity, swapping) provides convergent evidence. Formal definitions and proofs (monotonicity, reduction to pass@k) are provided.

**Reproducibility (Good):** Code is publicly available. The use of standard models (Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct), standard benchmarks (MATH-500, HotPotQA), and deterministic BM25 retrieval makes replication feasible. Hyperparameters are documented in appendices.

**Significance (High):** This paper reconciles the optimistic and pessimistic views of RL for LLMs by showing both are correct on different task types. It has direct implications for whether the field should invest in RL algorithms (for compositional agentic tasks) or base model improvements (for static reasoning). The PASS@(k,T) metric itself could become a standard tool for agentic evaluation.

**Red flags / concerns:**
- The absolute scale of expansion is modest: +4 net problems out of 100 on the most favorable category. Whether this finding scales to larger models, richer tool sets, and web-scale retrieval remains unknown.
- The training set is very small (200 problems). The results may not generalize to large-scale RL training regimes.
- BM25 over a 10-paragraph corpus is a highly simplified retrieval environment. Real-world tool use involves noisy, open-ended retrieval where strategy diversity matters more.
- Only a single 7B model is tested. The interaction between model scale and capability expansion is unexplored.
- The paper claims capability expansion but acknowledges RL does not invent new strategies -- it reweights existing ones. This is a more nuanced finding than the headline suggests.

## Strengths

- Clean, principled evaluation framework with formal definitions that cleanly separate capability expansion from efficiency improvement.
- Excellent experimental design: matched training data across SFT and RL isolates the learning signal as the causal variable.
- Three converging mechanism analyses provide a coherent and specific account of how RL helps.
- The paper directly addresses and reconciles an active debate in the community (does RL expand or merely redistribute capability?).
- Practical implications are clearly drawn (prefer GRPO over SFT for compositional tasks; budget allocation guidance via marginal value analysis).
- Well-written with clear structure: the three-category setup naturally builds the argument.

## Limitations

- Small training budget (200 problems) and small test sets (100 per category) limit statistical power and generalizability.
- Single base model (Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct); results may differ for larger or differently-trained models.
- Simplified retrieval environment (BM25 over 10-paragraph corpus) that may not reflect real-world tool-use complexity.
- The capability expansion is modest in absolute terms (+4 net problems on the best category).
- No exploration of temperature sensitivity or different RL algorithms beyond GRPO.
- The T > T_train extrapolation question is noted but not tested -- can agents generalize to deeper interaction than trained on?

## Relevance

本文对理解RL在LLM智能体中的作用提供了重要的评估方法论和实证发现。PASS@(k,T) 框架为区分能力扩展与效率提升提供了严格工具，对设计更有效的智能体训练策略（尤其是组合式推理任务上的RL vs SFT选择）具有直接指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the capability expansion finding scale to 70B+ models and larger, more diverse training sets (e.g., thousands of problems)?
- How does PASS@(k,T) behave with non-deterministic tools (e.g., web search, code execution) where the environment itself introduces stochasticity?
- Can the reweighting mechanism be made more explicit -- e.g., can we identify the specific strategy subsets RL upweights and design targeted rewards to amplify them?
- What happens when T_train is smaller than T_eval? Does RL generalize to deeper interaction horizons than it was trained on?
- How do results change with process reward models (PRMs) instead of outcome-based binary reward?
- Is the SFT regression on compositional tasks a general phenomenon or specific to the expert-trajectory construction method used here?
