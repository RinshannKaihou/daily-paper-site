RELOAD: A Robust and Efficient Learned Query Optimizer for Database Systems

arXiv: 2604.14725

Date: 2026-04-16

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14725

一句话总结： RELOAD通过结合基于TD误差和时效性的优先经验回放（知识保留）与基于查询复杂度分组的MAML元学习（知识迁移），使RL查询优化器在单查询级别减少高达2.4倍的性能回退，并加速3.1倍收敛到专家级计划质量。
TL;DR: Existing RL-based query optimizers suffer from per-query performance regressions (plateau at local optima or rebound after initial improvement) and slow convergence. RELOAD addresses both with two complementary modules: (1) a tailored prioritized experience replay that extracts fine-grained subplan experiences weighted by recency and TD error to prevent forgetting high-cost signals, and (2) a MAML-based meta-learning pipeline with complexity-aware workload partitioning (using Halstead metrics and DBI-based policy selection) for fast initialization. On JOB, TPC-DS, and SSB benchmarks across PostgreSQL and SQL Server, RELOAD reduces total robustness failures by up to 2.4x and accelerates convergence by up to 3.1x compared to Bao, Balsa, LOGER, and LIMAO. It operates as a plug-in framework compatible with existing RL optimizers. The paper is under review, likely targeting a top database venue.

核心贡献

  • 首次在查询级别形式化并解决RL查询优化器的性能回退问题：识别出两种失败模式——Plateau（收敛到次优计划）和Rebound（训练中性能先升后降），并提出量化指标，超越了传统的聚合性能评估。
  • 设计了面向查询优化的优先经验回放机制：从执行计划中提取细粒度子计划经验，结合时效性和TD误差进行加权采样，解决了稀疏奖励下的信用分配问题。
  • 提出基于复杂度感知的MAML元学习策略：使用Halstead复杂度等指标对工作负载分区，通过DBI聚类指标自动选择最优分区策略，加速跨工作负载的收敛。
  • 作为即插即用框架兼容现有RL优化器：在Balsa和LIMAO上均验证了有效性，并在PostgreSQL和SQL Server两种系统上进行了实验。

Key Contributions

  • First formalization of query-level robustness in RL-based query optimizers, identifying two distinct failure modes: Plateau (convergence to suboptimal plans) and Rebound (performance regression after initial improvement), with dedicated metrics that go beyond aggregate workload-level evaluation.
  • Domain-specific Prioritized Experience Replay (PER) that extracts fine-grained join-rooted subplan experiences from executed query plans, weighting them by recency and TD error to address sparse rewards and prevent forgetting high-cost learning signals.
  • Complexity-aware MAML-based knowledge transfer that partitions workloads using Halstead complexity measures, operator counts, estimated costs, and cardinality, automatically selecting the best partitioning policy via the Davies-Bouldin Index (DBI).
  • Plug-in framework design compatible with existing RL-based query optimizers (Balsa, LIMAO), validated on both open-source (PostgreSQL) and commercial (SQL Server) database systems.

Methodology

RELOAD operates as a two-stage enhancement framework layered on top of existing RL-based query optimizers (primarily Balsa):

Stage 1 -- Knowledge Transfer (MAML-based initialization):

  • Training queries are featurized based on structural and execution-specific characteristics (operator count, estimated cost, estimated cardinality).
  • Four partitioning policies group queries into tasks: Halstead complexity, total operator count, estimated query cost, and estimated rows.
  • A policy evaluator selects the partitioning with the lowest Davies-Bouldin Index (DBI), ensuring high intra-task similarity and inter-task diversity.
  • MAML's inner loop refines task-specific parameters via gradient steps; the outer loop aggregates across tasks to produce a well-initialized value model.

Stage 2 -- Knowledge Retention (PER-based training):

  • After the DBMS executes a query plan, RELOAD extracts subplan-level experiences (join-rooted subtrees) with structural features (join types, operators), execution metrics (CPU, memory, I/O, cardinality), temporal features (recency), and predicted latency.
  • A weighting policy combining recency (time-based decay) and TD error (prediction discrepancy) computes priority scores for each experience.
  • Experiences are sampled from a multinomial distribution based on normalized priorities, reinforcing underlearned but valuable subplan states.
  • The hybrid policy (recency + high TD error) proved most effective, balancing recent execution conditions with challenging learning signals.

Baselines compared: Bao (Thompson sampling), LOGER (restricted search space), Balsa (deep RL), and LIMAO (lifelong modular learning).

Results & Highlights

  • Robustness: Balsa+RELOAD reduces total robustness failures (Plateau + Rebound) on JOB from 16/26 to 9/26 queries -- a 44% reduction. Compared across all baselines, RELOAD achieves up to 2.4x higher robustness.
  • Efficiency: On JOB, convergence drops from 5.3h (69 iterations) to 4.7h (50 iterations). On SSB, from 1.9h to 0.8h (2.4x faster). On SQL Server, up to 3.1x faster convergence.
  • TPC-DS convergence: RELOAD is the only method that successfully converges on TPC-DS (95 iterations, 1.3h), while all baselines fail to reach expert-level performance.
  • WRL (test set): 0.64 on JOB (1.55x speedup vs PostgreSQL), 0.85 on SSB (1.18x speedup). On TPC-DS test, WRL is ~1.02 (roughly par with PostgreSQL).
  • Per-query speedups: On JOB, RELOAD yields speedups for approximately 77% of long-running test queries (>500ms with PostgreSQL).
  • Cross-DBMS portability: Results validated on both PostgreSQL 12.5 and SQL Server 2022 under identical hardware, showing consistent improvements.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (moderate): The individual components (PER, MAML, Halstead metrics) are not new. The novelty lies in their domain-specific adaptation for query optimization -- particularly the subplan-level experience extraction and the formalization of Plateau/Rebound failure modes. However, the paper acknowledges it is a "complementary enhancement framework" rather than a fundamentally new optimizer.
  • Rigor (strong): Thorough evaluation on three standard benchmarks (JOB, TPC-DS, SSB) with template-disjoint train/test splits, comparison against four baselines, testing on both open-source and commercial DBMS, 6 repetitions with median reported, and detailed micro-experiments abiating each design choice.
  • Reproducibility (moderate): Open-sourced (anonymous repo linked). Hardware specified (NVIDIA RTX 6000 Ada, Intel Xeon Gold 6530). Hyperparameters provided (k=256, alpha=1, beta=0.5, LR=10^-3, 150 outer/5 inner MAML iterations). However, the anonymity of the repo and the fact it uses a commercial DBMS (SQL Server) for some experiments partially limits reproducibility.
  • Significance (moderate-high): Query-level robustness is a genuinely important and underexplored problem. The 2.4x robustness improvement and 3.1x efficiency gain are meaningful. The framework's plug-in nature makes it practically adoptable.
  • Red flags:
  • The paper is under review, suggesting it has not yet passed peer review.
  • SSB has only 3 test queries, making robustness claims on that benchmark less statistically meaningful.
  • On TPC-DS, RELOAD's test WRL is ~1.02 (essentially matching PostgreSQL, not surpassing it), which weakens the "expert-level" claim for that benchmark.
  • Some baselines (Bao, LOGER) fail to converge on most benchmarks, which inflates relative comparisons.
  • LIMAO+RELOAD sometimes performs worse than Balsa(vanilla)+RELOAD (e.g., 12/26 vs 9/26 robustness failures on JOB), raising questions about whether the framework universally helps.
  • The MAML micro-experiment (Figure 10b) shows that no MAML configuration outperforms expert-level performance on its own, meaning MAML's contribution is primarily efficiency, not quality.

Strengths

  • Clearly identifies and formalizes a real, under-addressed problem (query-level robustness vs. aggregate workload metrics).
  • The plug-in design allows integration with multiple RL-based optimizers without modifying their core architectures.
  • Comprehensive experimental setup: three benchmarks, two database systems, four baselines, ablation studies.
  • The dual-module design (knowledge retention + knowledge transfer) addresses complementary aspects of the problem.
  • Transparent about AI-assisted writing in the acknowledgements.

Limitations

  • SSB benchmark has only 3 test queries, limiting statistical significance of results on that workload.
  • Only tested with Balsa as the base optimizer (plus LIMAO which builds on Balsa); compatibility with other RL optimizers (Neo, ReJoin) is claimed but not demonstrated.
  • TPC-DS evaluation uses only 19 SPJ query templates (out of 99 total), a restricted subset.
  • The framework adds complexity (MAML pre-training, PER buffer management) which introduces overhead not fully analyzed.
  • No analysis of how RELOAD scales to larger workloads or handles workload drift over time.
  • The Halstead complexity adaptation for SQL is somewhat simplistic and may not capture all aspects of query difficulty.

Relevance

本文关注RL在数据库查询优化中的鲁棒性和效率问题，对于研究强化学习在实际系统中的部署挑战具有参考价值。其query-level鲁棒性评估范式和meta-learning加速策略可推广到其他需要稳定性能的RL应用场景。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • How does RELOAD perform under dynamic workloads where query patterns shift over time (workload drift)?
  • Can the PER weighting policy be adaptively tuned during training rather than fixed a priori?
  • What is the computational overhead of MAML pre-training and PER buffer management in production-scale deployments with thousands of concurrent queries?
  • Would RELOAD benefit from integrating with more recent learned optimizers like GLO or Athena, beyond Balsa and LIMAO?
  • How sensitive are the results to the number of MAML inner/outer iterations and the choice of partitioning policy granularity?
