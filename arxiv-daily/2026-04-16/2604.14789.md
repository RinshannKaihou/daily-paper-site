> **一句话总结：** 本文在真实边缘设备（Jetson Orin Nano/AGX Orin、Raspberry Pi 5）上系统比较了剪枝、量化（PTQ/DQ）和早退机制（early-exit）三种CNN优化策略，发现PTQ与早退机制的组合（PTQ-EE）能在保持高压缩比（3-4x）的同时恢复量化导致的精度损失，并在ResNet-152上实现3.12倍加速，是极端边缘部署的最优方案。

> **TL;DR:** A systematic, hardware-on-the-loop comparison of pruning, quantization (PTQ/DQ), and early-exit mechanisms for CNN deployment on real edge devices (Jetson Orin Nano, AGX Orin, Raspberry Pi 5). The key finding is that combining PTQ quantization with early exits (PTQ-EE) achieves the best overall trade-off: 3-4x model compression, up to 3.12x inference speed-up on ResNet-152, and accuracy recovery over standalone PTQ (e.g., EfficientNet-B2: 64.86% -> 84.94%). Static methods (pruning, quantization) excel at memory reduction, while early exits uniquely provide input-adaptive latency savings. The study spans four architectures (ResNet-152, EfficientNet-B2, MobileNet-V2, ShuffleNet-V2) and reveals that no single technique dominates; optimal choice depends on architecture and deployment constraints.

## 核心贡献

- **统一实验框架：** 在相同条件下，使用ONNX Runtime推理引擎，在三种真实边缘硬件上对剪枝、量化和早退机制进行公平对比，填补了现有文献中跨类别比较的空白。
- **早退机制的系统分析：** 证明早退机制在大型模型（ResNet-152）上可实现2.24倍加速，且accuracy-optimal模式下几乎无损（<0.1%精度下降），为边缘AIoT部署提供了实用的优化选择。
- **PTQ-EE组合策略的发现：** 揭示了PTQ量化与早退机制的协同效应——早退分支减少了样本穿越深度量化层的比例，独立量化各路径限制了误差累积，在所有架构上均优于单独使用任一技术。
- **架构敏感性分析：** 明确指出不同架构对不同优化技术的敏感度差异：ResNet-152适配所有方法，EfficientNet-B2仅适合剪枝和PTQ-EE，紧凑模型（MobileNet-V2、ShuffleNet-V2）量化和早退更有效。
- **全面的硬件级指标：** 首次在统一框架下报告了精度、推理延迟、CPU/GPU利用率、RAM占用、标签忠诚度和概率忠诚度等多维度指标。

## Key Contributions

- **Unified experimental framework** providing a comprehensive comparison of static compression (pruning, PTQ, DQ) and dynamic (early-exit) techniques under identical conditions on real edge hardware using ONNX Runtime.
- **Systematic analysis of early exits** as an edge optimization mechanism, demonstrating that they achieve competitive accuracy-latency trade-offs and enable dynamic adaptability unavailable to static techniques.
- **Discovery of PTQ-EE synergy**, where combining PTQ with early exits simultaneously achieves 3-4x compression, up to 3.12x speed-up, and accuracy recovery over standalone PTQ across all architectures.
- **Architecture-specific optimization guidelines**: ResNet-152 benefits from all methods; EfficientNet-B2 requires pruning or PTQ-EE due to quantization sensitivity; compact models (MobileNet-V2, ShuffleNet-V2) gain most from quantization and intermediate-depth early exits.
- **Comprehensive hardware-level benchmarking** across heterogeneous platforms (Jetson AGX Orin, Jetson Orin Nano, Raspberry Pi 5), reporting accuracy, latency, CPU/GPU utilization, RAM, label loyalty, and probability loyalty.

## Methodology

The study evaluates three optimization techniques -- structured pruning, quantization (PTQ and DQ), and early-exit mechanisms -- on four CNN architectures: ResNet-152 (large), EfficientNet-B2 (balanced), MobileNet-V2 (compact), and ShuffleNet-V2 (ultra-compact). All models are trained on ImageNet100 and exported to ONNX format for inference via ONNX Runtime.

**Pruning:** Structured pruning with L1-norm criterion is applied, using channel rounding aligned to multiples of 16 or 32 for hardware efficiency. ShuffleNet-V2 is excluded due to architectural incompatibility with standard pruning. Multiple configurations of pruning ratio (PR) and channel granularity (CG) are evaluated; the best accuracy-compression trade-off is selected per architecture.

**Quantization:** Both Dynamic Quantization (DQ) and Post-Training Quantization (PTQ) target UINT8 precision. PTQ employs a layer-wise selective strategy: for EfficientNet-B2, only convolutional layers are quantized; for ShuffleNet-V2, Conv/Gemm/MatMul layers; for quantization-friendly architectures (ResNet-152, MobileNet-V2), the full graph is quantized. QAT is deliberately excluded to maintain deployment simplicity.

**Early Exits:** Following the BranchyNet framework with a two-stage training strategy (frozen backbone, trained exit branches). Three exit depths per architecture are evaluated (e.g., EE3/EE8/EE14 for ResNet-152), each under two operating points: acc-opt (maximizing accuracy) and inf-opt (maximizing efficiency). A model partitioning approach is used to export early-exit models as sequential ONNX submodels, since ONNX does not natively support dynamic control flow.

**Hardware:** Three edge devices -- Jetson AGX Orin (64GB, 2048-core GPU), Jetson Orin Nano (8GB, 1024-core GPU), and Raspberry Pi 5 (8GB, CPU-only) -- with CUDA execution providers for Jetson and CPU-only for RPi5. ONNX Runtime 1.19.0 is used exclusively (TensorRT avoided to isolate model-level optimization effects).

**Metrics:** Accuracy, compression ratio, total/average inference time, early-exit rate, label loyalty, probability loyalty, CPU/GPU usage, and RAM consumption (RSS).

## Results & Highlights

- **Pruning** is most effective for large models: ResNet-152 achieves 2.37x compression with only 1.86% accuracy drop. For compact architectures, pruning yields marginal compression (1.2-1.4x) with disproportionate accuracy loss. On CPU, ResNet-152 pruning achieves 2.53x speed-up on RPi5; on GPU, gains are modest (1.04-1.37x).

- **Quantization** delivers the best compression-accuracy trade-off overall (3.5-4x size reduction). DQ is near-lossless for quantization-friendly architectures (ResNet-152: 88.96% vs. 89.08% baseline; MobileNet-V2: 86.82% vs. 87.80%). PTQ with selective layer quantization is essential for sensitive architectures. A critical finding: quantized models run slower on CUDA due to silent CPU offloading of unsupported operators (e.g., ResNet-152 PTQ on Jetson Orin Nano CUDA: 0.35x speed-up vs. 2.12x on CPU).

- **Early exits (acc-opt)** preserve near-baseline accuracy (within 0.1%) but offer negligible speed-up due to low early-exit rates (5-26%). **Early exits (inf-opt)** achieve the highest peak speed-ups (ResNet-152: up to 2.24x on RPi5) but with significant accuracy penalties (up to 36-38% for shallow exits on compact models). Early exits consistently increase RAM usage proportional to exit count.

- **PTQ-EE combination** is the standout result: on ResNet-152, PTQ-EE3 inf-opt achieves 3.12x speed-up (vs. PTQ-alone 2.12x, EE-alone 1.69x) with 87.28% accuracy (vs. PTQ-alone 84.30%). On EfficientNet-B2, PTQ-EE8 acc-opt recovers accuracy from 64.86% (PTQ) to 84.94% while maintaining 3.6x compression. Even ShuffleNet-V2, where PTQ-alone causes slowdown (0.66x), benefits from PTQ-EE with 1.30x speed-up.

- **DQ-EE combination** is viable only for large models (ResNet-152: 2.30x, 84.20% accuracy) and fails catastrophically on compact architectures (EfficientNet-B2: 4.70% accuracy), as DQ's runtime dynamic scaling compounds poorly with early-exit branching.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** Moderate. The individual techniques (pruning, PTQ, early exits) are well-established. The paper's contribution lies in the unified comparison under identical hardware conditions and the discovery of PTQ-EE synergy. The finding that combining PTQ with early exits can recover accuracy lost to quantization is practically valuable though conceptually straightforward.

**Rigor:** High. The experimental design is thorough: four architectures spanning the complexity spectrum, three real edge devices, two quantization strategies, multiple early-exit depths and confidence thresholds, and comprehensive metrics. The decision to use ONNX Runtime (avoiding TensorRT) to isolate model-level effects is well-justified. The inclusion of label loyalty and probability loyalty metrics for assessing prediction consistency is a strength.

**Reproducibility:** Good. The paper specifies ONNX Runtime version (1.19.0), JetPack version (6.0), Python version (3.11), batch size (1), and detailed hardware specifications. The use of standard ONNX pipelines and publicly available architectures (ResNet-152, EfficientNet-B2, MobileNet-V2, ShuffleNet-V2) aids reproducibility. However, the ImageNet100 dataset (a subset) and specific early-exit branch designs would need to be shared for full replication.

**Significance:** Practically significant for the edge AI deployment community. The paper provides actionable guidelines for practitioners selecting optimization strategies. The PTQ-EE combination result is the most impactful finding, as it demonstrates a path to simultaneous compression and latency reduction that outperforms either technique alone.

**Red flags:**
- The study uses ImageNet100 (100-class subset) rather than full ImageNet, which may not fully represent real-world classification difficulty distributions and could inflate early-exit effectiveness.
- The training strategy (frozen backbone) for early exits is acknowledged as potentially suboptimal but not explored.
- CUDA execution issues with quantized models (silent CPU offloading) are a well-known ONNX Runtime limitation rather than a novel finding, though documenting its practical impact is valuable.
- No statistical significance testing or variance analysis across runs is reported.
- The paper does not explore QAT, which could change the quantization results significantly.

## Strengths

- First unified hardware-on-the-loop comparison spanning static compression and dynamic early-exit techniques under identical conditions.
- Real edge device evaluation (not simulator or high-end GPU proxies) with detailed resource utilization metrics.
- The PTQ-EE combination result is practically impactful and well-demonstrated across all four architectures.
- Architecture-specific analysis provides clear deployment guidelines rather than one-size-fits-all recommendations.
- Comprehensive metric suite including prediction stability (label/probability loyalty) beyond mere accuracy.
- Honest discussion of limitations, including CUDA quantization incompatibilities and ShuffleNet-V2 pruning exclusion.

## Limitations

- ImageNet100 (100-class subset) may not generalize to larger-scale or domain-specific classification tasks.
- Only classification tasks are evaluated; the findings may not transfer to object detection, segmentation, or other vision tasks.
- The early-exit training strategy (frozen backbone, two-stage) is just one of several possible approaches and may not be optimal.
- QAT is excluded despite potentially better quantization results, limiting the scope of the quantization comparison.
- No energy consumption measurements, which are critical for battery-powered edge devices.
- The study does not explore adaptive or input-aware confidence thresholds during inference.
- No comparison with other dynamic techniques (layer skipping, token pruning) beyond brief mention.

## Relevance

本文对边缘AI部署中的CNN模型优化进行了系统性硬件实测比较，PTQ与早退机制组合的新发现对实际边缘推理场景（如智能物联网设备、自动驾驶边缘节点）具有直接的工程指导价值，适合关注模型压缩与高效推理的研究者和工程师参考。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How do the PTQ-EE results generalize to full ImageNet (1000 classes) or domain-specific datasets (medical imaging, satellite imagery)?
- Would Quantization-Aware Training (QAT) combined with early exits further improve the synergy, or does the training-aware quantization reduce the marginal benefit of early exits?
- Can adaptive confidence thresholds that adjust based on input complexity or energy budget improve the accuracy-efficiency Pareto frontier of early-exit models?
- How does the PTQ-EE approach translate to transformer-based architectures (ViT, DeiT) that are increasingly dominant in vision tasks?
- What is the energy consumption impact of each optimization technique on battery-powered edge devices, and does the PTQ-EE combination offer energy benefits proportional to its latency gains?
