> **一句话总结：** 本文用修正拉普拉斯矩阵的逆（双随机矩阵 DSM）替代传统拉普拉斯算子做 GNN 消息传递，通过截断 Neumann 级数将复杂度从 O(n^3) 降至 O(K|E|)，并提出残差质量补偿机制恢复行随机性，在同配图上取得竞争性性能并有效缓解过平滑问题。

> **TL;DR:** This paper proposes replacing the standard graph Laplacian with a Doubly Stochastic graph Matrix (DSM) -- the inverse of the modified Laplacian -- for GNN message passing. To avoid the O(n^3) inversion cost, the authors use a truncated Neumann series (O(K|E|)) and introduce a Residual Mass Compensation mechanism that re-injects leaked probability mass into self-loops, strictly restoring row-stochasticity. The resulting DsmNet and DsmNet-compensate models achieve competitive accuracy on homophilic benchmarks, bound Dirichlet energy decay to mitigate over-smoothing, and demonstrate versatility as structural encodings for Graph Transformers.

## 核心贡献

- **提出双随机图矩阵（DSM）作为 GNN 的新型传播算子：** 利用修正拉普拉斯矩阵 $\tilde{L} = I + L$ 的逆矩阵 $B = \tilde{L}^{-1}$，自然编码连续多跳邻近性和节点中心性，对角元素严格占优且随距离衰减。
- **截断 Neumann 级数实现可扩展近似：** 通过 $K$ 步截断将矩阵求逆复杂度从 $O(n^3)$ 降至 $O(K|E|)$，并证明了截断误差以 $(d_{\max}/(d_{\max}+1))^{K+1}$ 的速率指数衰减。
- **残差质量补偿机制（Residual Mass Compensation）：** 截断导致概率质量泄漏，该机制将残留质量 $P^{K+1}\mathbf{1}$ 解析地回注到节点自环，严格恢复行随机性和对角占优，只需额外的 O(K|E|) SpMV 操作。
- **过平滑缓解的理论保证：** 通过有界 Dirichlet 能量衰减证明补偿算子 $\hat{B}_K$ 维持了严格非零的稳态能量下界，有效防止特征过度均匀化。
- **Graph Transformer 结构编码：** 将 DSM 对角线作为连续中心性编码、非对角元素作为空间编码，在 Graph Transformer 和 Graphormer 上取得优于原始编码的性能。

## Key Contributions

- **Doubly Stochastic graph Matrix (DSM) as a propagation operator:** Derives the DSM from the inverse of the modified Laplacian $\tilde{L} = I + L$, which naturally encodes continuous multi-hop proximity (exponential distance decay) and node centrality (strict diagonal dominance).
- **Scalable truncated Neumann series approximation:** Reduces the $O(n^3)$ matrix inversion to $O(K|E|)$ via truncated Neumann series with a provably exponential error bound $\|E_K\|_\infty \leq (d_{\max}/(d_{\max}+1))^{K+1}$.
- **Residual Mass Compensation mechanism:** Analytically re-injects the truncated probability tail $P^{K+1}\mathbf{1}$ into self-loops, strictly restoring row-stochasticity and diagonal dominance with only O(K|E|) additional sparse matrix-vector multiplications.
- **Over-smoothing mitigation via Dirichlet energy bounding:** Theoretically and empirically shows that the compensated operator maintains a high non-zero stationary Dirichlet energy bound across diverse graph topologies.
- **Versatile structural encoding for Graph Transformers:** Demonstrates that DSM diagonal entries serve as continuous centrality encodings and off-diagonal entries as spatial encodings, improving Graph Transformer and Graphormer performance over their native Laplacian eigenvector and degree/shortest-path encodings.

## Methodology

The paper follows a decoupled GNN paradigm (similar to APPNP), separating neural feature transformation from topological propagation:

1. **DSM Derivation:** The modified Laplacian $\tilde{L} = I + D - A$ is factored as $\tilde{L} = \tilde{D}(I - \tilde{D}^{-1}A)$, where $\tilde{D} = I + D$. Setting the transition matrix $P = \tilde{D}^{-1}A$ (with $\|P\|_\infty < 1$), the DSM is expressed as $B = \tilde{L}^{-1} = (\sum_{k=0}^{\infty} P^k)\tilde{D}^{-1}$ via the Neumann series.

2. **Truncation to order K:** The K-th order approximation $B_K = \sum_{k=0}^{K} P^k \tilde{D}^{-1}$ enforces a hard K-hop receptive field (entries for distances > K are exactly zero), providing implicit regularization against over-smoothing.

3. **DsmNet forward pass:** Base predictions $Z^{(0)} = f_\theta(X)$ are propagated as $Z = B_K Z^{(0)}$ using iterative sparse matrix multiplications.

4. **DsmNet-compensate forward pass:** Synchronous iterative updates track both feature state $S^{(k)} = P S^{(k-1)} + S^{(0)}$ and mass vector $m^{(k)} = P m^{(k-1)}$, then fold residual mass: $Z = S^{(K)} + \text{diag}(m^{(K+1)})Z^{(0)}$.

5. **Spectral analysis:** Eigenvalues of B are $\mu_i = 1/(1+\lambda_i)$, giving spectral gap $\gamma = 1 - 1/(1+\lambda_2)$. Truncation order K controls the effective spectral gap, balancing over-smoothing (large gap) vs. over-squashing (small gap).

## Results & Highlights

- **Homophilic benchmarks (Full Supervised):** DsmNet-compensate achieves top-tier accuracy on Physics (97.0%), PubMed (90.3%), Photo (92.0%), and CS (94.9%), consistently matching or exceeding GCN, GAT, GraphSAGE, APPNP, GCNII, and JKNet.
- **Homophilic benchmarks (Semi-Supervised):** Competitive performance with particularly strong results on Physics (94.1-94.4%), Photo (88.9-90.7%), and CS (91.4-92.5%).
- **Heterophilic graphs:** Performance is weaker, which the authors honestly attribute to the DSM's inherent low-pass filtering property that homogenizes representations of topologically proximate but semantically distinct nodes.
- **Node centrality prediction:** DSM diagonal entries provide substantial improvement over degree-only features for predicting betweenness centrality under cross-scale generalization (trained on N=200, tested on N=1000), with Kendall's Tau improvements of +0.20 (WS) and +0.14 (RGG).
- **Graph Transformer integration:** Compensated truncated DSM encodings outperform original Laplacian PE in GT (Cora: 85.1% vs 82.9%; CiteSeer: 69.7% vs 68.6%) and original degree+SPD in Graphormer (Cora: 57.8-60.3% vs 53.8%; CiteSeer: 57.9-61.7% vs 52.8%).
- **Over-smoothing analysis:** Dirichlet energy experiments across 4 topologies show the compensated operator maintains the highest stationary energy lower bound, while the exact DSM collapses to zero and the uncompensated version plateaus prematurely.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate-High):** The idea of using the inverse of the modified Laplacian as a GNN propagation operator is genuinely novel. The Residual Mass Compensation mechanism is a clean mathematical contribution. However, the truncated Neumann series approximation for avoiding matrix inversion is a standard technique in numerical linear algebra, and the decoupled architecture itself follows the well-established APPNP paradigm.
- **Rigor (High):** The paper provides formal theorems with complete proofs (in appendix) for the spectral properties, truncation error bounds, and convergence guarantees. The Dirichlet energy analysis is thorough and spans multiple graph topologies.
- **Reproducibility (Moderate):** Hyperparameter details are provided (hidden size 64, K in {10,20,30,40,50}, Optuna-tuned learning rate). However, no code repository is mentioned, and the Optuna search space and number of trials are not fully specified. The 5-seed evaluation protocol is standard.
- **Significance (Moderate):** The DSM provides an interesting theoretical lens connecting graph topology to diffusion dynamics. The practical impact is somewhat limited by the weak heterophilic performance and the reliance on a decoupled architecture (not a general plug-in for arbitrary GNN layers).
- **Red flags:** (1) The heterophilic performance is notably weak -- DSM-Comp achieves only 51.1% on Chameleon and 31.4% on Squirrel under full supervision, where GraphSAGE gets 59.7% and 37.2% respectively. This is acknowledged but limits general applicability. (2) The comparison baselines are somewhat dated (GCN, GAT, SAGE from 2017-2018 era) -- newer baselines like GPRGNN, BernNet, or GCNII* would strengthen the evaluation. (3) The semi-supervised Computers result for DSM (48.1%) appears anomalously low and raises questions about stability.

## Strengths

- Strong theoretical foundations with rigorous proofs for approximation quality and spectral properties
- The Residual Mass Compensation mechanism is mathematically elegant -- it preserves row-stochasticity through an analytically exact closed-form correction
- Clear connection between truncation order K and the over-smoothing/over-squashing trade-off via spectral gap control
- Comprehensive empirical analysis including spectral convergence, distance decay, Dirichlet energy, centrality prediction, and Graph Transformer integration
- Honest discussion of limitations on heterophilic graphs

## Limitations

- Fundamentally a low-pass filter: the DSM inherently smooths representations, making it unsuitable for heterophilic graphs where adjacent nodes belong to different classes
- The decoupled architecture means the method cannot learn adaptive edge weights (unlike GAT or attention-based methods)
- Truncation order K is a global hyperparameter -- the paper acknowledges but does not solve the need for topology-adaptive K
- Comparison against only classical baselines; lacks evaluation against more recent GNN architectures (2022-2025)
- No ablation on the neural transformation module (only 2-layer MLP with hidden size 64 is tested)
- The anomaly in the Computers semi-supervised result (48.1% for DSM) suggests potential instability in certain graph structures

## Relevance

本文在 GNN 消息传递框架中引入双随机图矩阵作为新型传播算子，提供了从图谱理论角度理解和缓解过平滑问题的新视角。对于关注 GNN 理论基础、图结构编码以及 Graph Transformer 结构表示的研究者具有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the DSM framework be extended to directed or signed graphs where the modified Laplacian has different spectral properties?
- How does the truncation order K interact with graph size and average degree -- is there a principled way to set K adaptively per node or per local topology?
- Could the low-pass limitation be addressed by combining the DSM with a complementary high-pass operator (e.g., via a polynomial filter), similar to how BernNet designs sign-alternating spectral filters?
- What is the theoretical relationship between the DSM's centrality encoding and positional encodings derived from Laplacian eigenvectors (e.g., PEG, SignNet)?
- How does the method scale to very large graphs (millions of nodes) where even O(K|E|) propagation with K=50 may become costly?
