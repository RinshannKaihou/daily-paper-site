> **一句话总结：** 本文将LLM遗忘问题重新定义为以保持能力为主任务、以遗忘为辅助任务的非对称双任务学习问题，提出SAGO方法通过逐元素符号对齐的梯度合成来消除遗忘与保持之间的梯度冲突，在WMDP和RWKU基准上显著提升了模型保持能力（MMLU恢复率从44.6%提升至96.0%）且遗忘效果不减。

> **TL;DR:** This paper reframes LLM unlearning as an asymmetric two-task problem where retention is primary and forgetting is auxiliary. The proposed SAGO (Sign-Align Gradient Optimization) method performs element-wise gradient gating to ensure no parameter update opposes the retain gradient direction. On WMDP Bio with SimNPO+GD, SAGO recovers 96.0% of target model MMLU performance (up from 44.6% with naive combination), while maintaining comparable forgetting effectiveness. The key insight is that reshaping gradient geometry, rather than re-balancing losses, is the critical lever for mitigating the unlearning-retention trade-off.

## 核心贡献

- **非对称问题建模**：将LLM遗忘重新定义为以保持能力为主任务、以遗忘为辅助任务的非对称双任务学习问题，突破了传统多任务学习中各任务平等对待的局限。
- **模块化梯度合成框架**：提出一个通用的保持优先梯度合成框架，将任务梯度提取与冲突感知组合解耦，可无缝集成于GA+GD、NPO+GD、SimNPO+GD等多种遗忘目标。
- **SAGO方法**：提出Sign-Align Gradient Optimization方法，通过逐元素符号对齐机制对遗忘和保持梯度进行门控，保证最终更新方向永远不会与保持梯度方向相悖，从理论上有更严格的对齐保证。
- **理论分析**：证明了PCGrad和SAGO均保证与保持梯度的非负余弦相似度，且SAGO通过构造性符号约束合成实现更严格的对齐。
- **一致的实验验证**：在WMDP Bio/Cyber和RWKU基准上，SAGO持续推动Pareto前沿外移，在遗忘效果相当的情况下大幅提升保持性能。

## Key Contributions

- **Asymmetric formulation of LLM unlearning**: Recasts the problem as an asymmetric two-task learning problem where retention is the primary objective and forgetting is auxiliary, departing from standard multi-task learning's equal-task assumption.
- **Modular retention-prioritized gradient synthesis framework**: Decouples task-specific gradient extraction from conflict-aware combination, making it compatible with diverse unlearning objectives (GA+GD, NPO+GD, SimNPO+GD).
- **SAGO (Sign-Align Gradient Optimization)**: A novel per-parameter gradient gating method that ensures the final update direction never opposes the retain gradient at any coordinate, providing stronger retention guarantees than orthogonal projection (PCGrad).
- **Theoretical guarantees**: Proves both PCGrad and SAGO ensure non-negative cosine similarity with the retain gradient, while SAGO achieves strictly tighter alignment through element-wise sign-constrained synthesis.
- **Consistent Pareto frontier improvements**: On WMDP Bio, SAGO recovers up to 96.0% of target model MMLU performance (from 44.6% naive baseline), and strictly dominates prior methods on RWKU.

## Methodology

The paper proposes a two-stage iterative optimization framework (Algorithm 1) that alternates between: (i) extracting task-specific gradients from forget and retain sets, and (ii) synthesizing a conflict-aware update direction via a modular COMBINE_GRADIENTS component.

**Core insight**: Unlike standard multi-task learning which seeks balanced task progress, LLM unlearning has an inherent asymmetry --- retention must be prioritized, and forgetting should only be applied where it does not harm retention. This motivates treating the retain gradient as the anchor direction.

**Two gradient synthesis methods**:

1. **PCGrad adaptation**: The forget gradient is projected onto the orthogonal complement of the retain gradient when they conflict (negative dot product). The authors use module-wise (per-layer) projection rather than the global projection in the original PCGrad, which provides finer-grained conflict mitigation.

2. **SAGO**: Performs element-wise gradient gating based on sign alignment between forget and retain gradients. For each parameter:
   - If the forget and retain gradient signs agree (no conflict), the forget gradient passes through.
   - If the signs disagree (conflict), the retain gradient is used while the forget gradient is suppressed.
   This ensures the synthesized gradient has zero component opposing the retain direction at any coordinate.

**Theoretical properties**: Both methods guarantee non-negative cosine similarity between the final gradient and the retain gradient. SAGO achieves strictly tighter alignment because it avoids the antagonistic components that PCGrad's projection can create when projected forget components dominate over retain components in certain dimensions.

## Results & Highlights

- **WMDP Bio (SimNPO+GD)**: MMLU improved from 26.7 (naive) to 56.4 (+PCGrad) to 57.4 (+SAGO), recovering 96.0% of the target model's 59.8 MMLU score, while forget accuracy remained comparable (28.2 vs 26.1 baseline).
- **WMDP Bio MMLU gains over naive baseline**: +17.8 to +30.7 points across different base objectives; additional +0.4 to +1.2 points over PCGrad.
- **WMDP Cyber**: Consistent retention gains of +4.1 to +11.7 MMLU points over naive baseline.
- **RWKU**: SAGO improves neighbor retention (All) by +3.4 to +13.3 over PCGrad across base objectives, with strictly dominant Pareto fronts.
- **Gradient geometry analysis**: SAGO achieves the highest Comb-Retain cosine similarity (0.57 vs 0.42 for GradDiff and 0.52 for PCGrad), confirming superior alignment with the retention objective.
- **Comparison with GRU and BLUR**: SAGO outperforms both dedicated conflict-mitigation unlearning methods on WMDP benchmarks.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty**: The asymmetric framing is a meaningful conceptual contribution. While PCGrad itself is not new, the module-wise adaptation and the SAGO sign-gating mechanism represent genuine methodological innovation. SAGO's element-wise sign alignment is a simple but effective idea with clean theoretical guarantees.

**Rigor**: The theoretical analysis proves non-negative cosine similarity for both methods and strictly tighter alignment for SAGO. The empirical protocol is sound --- they tune hyperparameters to match forgetting performance first, then compare retention, which is the fair comparison. However, the hyperparameter sweep details in the appendix are limited.

**Reproducibility**: Code is publicly available. The method uses standard benchmarks (WMDP, RWKU) and well-known base models (Zephyr-7B, LLaMA3-8B-Instruct). Hyperparameter details are provided. Reproducibility appears strong.

**Significance**: The results are substantial --- recovering 96% of target model MMLU while maintaining forgetting is a significant practical improvement. The insight that gradient geometry matters more than loss balancing could influence future unlearning method design.

**Red flags**:
- The paper only evaluates on two benchmarks (WMDP and RWKU). Broader evaluation on copyright, privacy, or multimodal unlearning tasks would strengthen claims.
- The method requires computing and storing separate gradients for forget and retain sets, introducing overhead that is acknowledged but not rigorously quantified.
- The theoretical analysis assumes equal weighting (alpha = gamma = 1), while practical experiments use different weight values.
- The improvement of SAGO over PCGrad, while consistent, is sometimes modest (0.4-1.2 MMLU points on WMDP). The larger gains over PCGrad appear on RWKU.

## Strengths

- Clean problem formulation that captures the asymmetry inherent in unlearning vs. retention.
- SAGO is conceptually simple (element-wise sign gating) yet effective, with strong theoretical justification.
- Modular framework design allows seamless integration with existing and future unlearning objectives.
- Consistent improvements across multiple benchmarks, base objectives, and model families.
- The gradient geometry analysis provides mechanistic understanding of why the method works.
- Pareto frontier visualization clearly demonstrates the trade-off improvements.

## Limitations

- Only two benchmarks evaluated; generalization to other unlearning scenarios (copyright, privacy, multimodal) is untested.
- Computational overhead of dual gradient computation is not rigorously analyzed.
- SAGO's advantage over PCGrad is modest on WMDP (though larger on RWKU); the practical significance of 0.4-1.2 MMLU point improvements can be debated.
- The retain set construction varies across benchmarks (Wikitext for WMDP, held-out Wikipedia for RWKU), making cross-benchmark comparisons less clean.
- No evaluation against adversarial attacks to test whether the unlearning is robust beyond benchmark metrics.

## Relevance

本文对LLM安全性和机器遗忘领域有直接贡献。SAGO方法通过梯度几何重塑解决了遗忘-保持权衡的核心挑战，对于关注模型安全对齐、隐私保护和知识擦除的研究者具有重要参考价值。其非对称双任务学习的建模思路也可启发其他需要平衡多个冲突目标的LLM训练场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What is the computational overhead of SAGO compared to naive gradient combination in wall-clock time and memory?

- Does the element-wise sign gating insight transfer to other domains with asymmetric multi-objective optimization, such as RLHF or red-teaming?
