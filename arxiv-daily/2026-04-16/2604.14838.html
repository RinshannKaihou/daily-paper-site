Intermediate Layers Encode Optimal Biological Representations in Single-Cell Foundation Models

arXiv: 2604.14838

Date: 2026-04-16

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14838

一句话总结： 本文系统评估了单细胞基础模型（scFoundation和Tahoe-X1）各层表示的质量，发现中间层在轨迹推断任务中比最终层提升高达31%，且扰动响应预测的最优层随细胞状态剧烈变化（0-96%深度），挑战了目前基准测试中默认使用最终层嵌入的惯例。
TL;DR: This paper shows that intermediate layers in single-cell foundation models (scFoundation 100M, Tahoe-X1 1.3B) encode significantly better biological representations than final layers. Trajectory inference peaks at 60% depth (31% above final layer), while perturbation response optima shift dramatically across T cell activation states (0-96% depth). First-layer embeddings unexpectedly outperform all deeper layers for quiescent cells, overturning the common assumption that deeper layers always capture more abstract and useful biological features. Code is publicly available.

核心贡献

  • 首次系统刻画单细胞基础模型的逐层特征层级结构：在轨迹推断和扰动响应预测两个任务上，对scFoundation（100M参数，12层）和Tahoe-X1（1.3B参数，24层）的所有层进行逐层评估。
  • 发现最优层具有任务依赖性和上下文依赖性：轨迹推断任务中，Tahoe-X1的最优层稳定在60%深度处；而扰动响应预测的最优层随T细胞激活状态（静息/8小时刺激/48小时刺激）剧烈变化，跨度达96个百分点。
  • 挑战"更深层=更好表示"的假设：在静息态T细胞的扰动预测中，第一层嵌入超越所有更深层，说明某些生物学信号仅以浅层特征形式存在。
  • 对基准测试实践的直接影响：当前所有单细胞基础模型基准默认提取最终层嵌入，本文证明这一做法可导致高达31%的性能损失。

Key Contributions

  • First systematic layer-wise characterization of feature hierarchies in single-cell foundation models across two canonical biological tasks.
  • Demonstrates that trajectory inference benefits from intermediate layers (60% depth optimum for Tahoe-X1), yielding up to 31% improvement over final-layer embeddings.
  • Reveals extreme context-dependency in perturbation response prediction: optimal depth shifts by 96 percentage points across T cell activation states within the same model.
  • Shows that first-layer embeddings can outperform all deeper layers for quiescent cells, challenging the hierarchical feature abstraction assumption.
  • Provides actionable recommendations for benchmarking frameworks to support layer-specific embeddings rather than defaulting to final layers.

Methodology

The authors evaluate two pretrained single-cell foundation models:

  1. scFoundation (100M parameters, 12 transformer layers): Asymmetric encoder-decoder with Read-Depth-Aware pretraining on 50M human cells.
  2. Tahoe-X1 (1.3B parameters, 24 transformer layers): scGPT-style transformer encoder pretrained on 266M profiles with masked expression prediction.

For each cell, a forward pass through frozen models is performed, and hidden states are extracted at the output of every transformer block. Layer indices are normalized by total depth for cross-model comparison.

Task 1 -- Trajectory Prediction: Uses a LARRY-based human cord blood LT-scSeq dataset with ground-truth temporal annotations. For each layer, a k-nearest neighbor graph (k=15, cosine similarity) is constructed from embeddings, and a layer-derived diffusion pseudotime (DPT) is computed. Agreement with reference pseudotime is measured via Spearman correlation.

Task 2 -- Perturbation Response Prediction: Uses a genome-scale CRISPRi Perturb-seq dataset of primary human CD4+ T cells (22M cells, 12,748 genes). For each perturbation, a differential expression profile (DE, via pseudobulk DESeq2) and a centroid embedding are computed. Representational Similarity Analysis (RSA) aligns biological reference similarity matrices (Spearman of DE profiles) with embedding-space similarity matrices (cosine of centroids).

Results & Highlights

  • Trajectory inference: Tahoe-X1 peaks at layer 16 (60% depth, rho=0.76), a 31% improvement over its final layer (rho=0.58). scFoundation shows a monotonic increase peaking at the penultimate layer (rho=0.60). Tahoe-X1 outperforms scFoundation's best by 27%.
  • Perturbation response -- context dependency: In Tahoe-X1, the optimal layer shifts from layer 1 (0% depth, rho=0.368) in Rest cells, to layer 4 (13% depth, rho=0.444) in Stim8hr, to layer 23 (96% depth, rho=0.536) in Stim48hr -- a 96 percentage point shift.
  • First-layer superiority in quiescent cells: For Rest-state perturbation prediction, Tahoe-X1's very first layer outperforms all deeper layers, suggesting perturbation effects in quiescent cells manifest as simple expression shifts not requiring deep semantic integration.
  • Activation improves performance systematically: Both models gain approximately 50% from Rest to Stim48hr conditions, suggesting regulatory changes become more coherently represented in activated states.
  • Model scale sensitivity varies by task: Tahoe-X1's advantage over scFoundation is 27% in trajectory inference but only 8-12% in perturbation response, suggesting perturbation modeling is less sensitive to model scale.

Critical Assessment

Novelty (High): This is the first systematic layer-wise analysis for single-cell foundation models. While intermediate layer superiority has been documented in NLP and vision (e.g., Skean et al. 2025), applying this lens to single-cell biology is novel and timely. The finding that first layers can outperform deeper layers in quiescent cells is genuinely surprising and counterintuitive.

Rigor (Moderate-to-High): The methodology is sound -- using frozen models, standard preprocessing, well-established metrics (Spearman correlation, RSA), and publicly available datasets with no pretraining overlap. However, the study is limited to two models and two tasks. The perturbation evaluation restricts to 100 perturbations per condition from a single donor (D1), which raises questions about generalizability across donors and perturbation types. Statistical significance is reported but effect sizes and confidence intervals on layer differences are not provided in the main text.

Reproducibility (High): Code is publicly available at https://github.com/vincenzocivale/scfm-mid-layer-analysis. Datasets are publicly accessible with clear accession numbers. Preprocessing uses standard Scanpy pipelines. No fine-tuning or retraining was performed.

Significance (High): The 31% performance gain from simply choosing a different layer is substantial and has immediate practical implications. All current single-cell foundation model benchmarks extract final-layer embeddings by default, so this finding directly challenges a widespread and previously unquestioned practice.

Potential red flags:

  • The perturbation analysis uses only one donor (D1) out of presumably multiple available, with no justification for this choice or analysis of donor variability.
  • The paper evaluates only two tasks (trajectory and perturbation); cell type annotation, batch correction, and other common downstream tasks are not covered.
  • The absolute correlation values for perturbation response are modest (rho ~0.3-0.5), which limits the practical impact of the layer selection findings in that task.
  • The workshop paper format (9 pages) restricts depth of analysis and discussion.

Strengths

  • Clean, focused experimental design that directly addresses a specific and practically important assumption.
  • Comprehensive layer-by-layer results provided in appendices with full correlation tables.
  • Cross-model comparison between architectures of different scales (100M vs 1.3B).
  • Strong empirical findings with clear practical recommendations for the community.
  • Excellent reproducibility: public code, public data, standard pipelines.

Limitations

  • Only two foundation models evaluated; results may not generalize to scGPT, Geneformer, or other architectures.
  • Only two downstream tasks; the picture is incomplete without cell type annotation, batch integration, or multi-omic tasks.
  • Single donor for perturbation analysis limits biological generalizability.
  • The perturbation correlation values are modest even at optimal layers, suggesting room for improvement beyond layer selection.
  • No theoretical explanation for why first layers work best for quiescent cells; the "simple expression shifts" hypothesis is stated but not directly tested.
  • The paper does not propose a method for automatically selecting the optimal layer for a given task/context.

Relevance

本文对单细胞基础模型的表示学习研究具有直接参考价值，揭示了模型层选择对下游生物学任务性能的关键影响。对于关注单细胞多组学基础模型评估与优化、以及Transformer中间层表示质量分析的研究者而言，本文提供了重要的实证依据和方法论启示。

Questions for Further Exploration

  • Can a principled, automated layer selection strategy (e.g., based on probing or unsupervised criteria) be developed to replace manual per-task layer search?
  • Do these findings generalize to other single-cell foundation models (scGPT, Geneformer, scBERT) and to other downstream tasks such as cell type annotation, batch correction, and gene regulatory network inference?
  • What biological or mechanistic explanation accounts for the first-layer superiority in quiescent cells? Can this be validated through targeted gene set enrichment or pathway analysis of layer-specific embeddings?
  • How stable are the optimal layer rankings across different donors, tissues, and experimental conditions in perturbation response prediction?
  • Could multi-layer feature aggregation (e.g., weighted concatenation or attention-based pooling across layers) consistently outperform single-layer extraction?
