> **一句话总结：** 本文提出IUQ框架，通过"先提问再回答"的方式，将LLM长文本生成中的声明（claim）从生成上下文中解耦，独立提问检测模型是否在编造事实，结合样本间一致性和样本内忠实度在声明级别量化不确定性，在多个模型和基准上显著优于现有方法。

> **TL;DR:** IUQ addresses a critical gap in LLM uncertainty quantification for long-form generation: models often fabricate plausible-sounding claims that are internally consistent across samples yet factually wrong. The framework decomposes long responses into atomic claims, generates independent short questions for each claim, and probes the model separately -- measuring both inter-sample consistency and intra-sample "faithfulness" (whether the model contradicts its own claims when questioned in isolation). An exponential-decay kernel propagates the penalty from unfaithful claims to downstream ones. Evaluated across 6 model families (GPT-4o, LLaMA-3.1/3.3, Qwen2, Gemma-3, Mistral) on FActScore and LongFact, IUQ consistently achieves the highest AUROC scores for claim-level correctness prediction, outperforming baselines including graph-based methods and semantic entropy. The cost is ~47% more tokens than prior graph-based approaches.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **Interrogative Uncertainty Quantification (IUQ)** 框架，利用"提问-回答"范式将长文本中的声明从生成上下文中解耦，独立检测模型是否忠实于其真实知识。
- 定义了 **claim-level faithfulness（声明级忠实度）** 指标，通过让模型在无上下文条件下独立回答针对每个声明构造的问题，量化模型"编造信息"的倾向。
- 设计了 **exponential-decay influence kernel（指数衰减影响核）**，将早期不可信声明的影响传播至后续声明，建模自回归生成中的上下文依赖性。
- 在6个模型家族、2个长文本基准上验证了IUQ的优越性，并开源了代码。
- 揭示了一个被忽视的现象：LLM在长文本生成中会为了保持逻辑一致性而编造事实信息，现有仅依赖样本间一致性的方法无法捕获这种行为。

## Key Contributions

- Proposes the **IUQ framework** that decouples atomic claims from the generative context via an interrogate-then-respond paradigm, enabling fine-grained probing of claim-level knowledge.
- Introduces **claim-level faithfulness**, a novel metric that measures whether a model contradicts its own generated claims when independently questioned, quantifying the tendency to fabricate information.
- Designs an **exponential-decay influence kernel** to propagate the penalty of unfaithful claims to subsequent claims, modeling the causal structure of autoregressive text generation.
- Demonstrates consistent AUROC improvements over 6 model families (GPT-4o, LLaMA-3.1-70B, LLaMA-3.3-70B, Qwen2-72B, Gemma-3-27B, Mistral-Small-24B) on FActScore and LongFact benchmarks.
- Highlights an under-explored phenomenon: LLMs fabricate coherent but false information to maintain logical consistency, which existing inter-sample consistency methods fail to detect.

## Methodology

IUQ operates through a structured pipeline with two main components: **inter-sample consistency** and **intra-sample faithfulness**.

**Response Generation:** Given a prompt, the model generates N=5 diverse responses at temperature t=1.0. Refusals are excluded.

**Claim-Level Question-Answering:** Each response is decomposed into atomic claims using greedy decoding (t=0). For each claim, up to 3 targeted questions are generated. Then na answers are sampled per question (also at temperature t=1.0) in a context-independent session. This decouples the model's knowledge from the generative context.

**Claim-Level Faithfulness:** For each claim ci, the model checks whether the independently sampled answers contradict the preceding claims (Ci<=). The faithfulness F(ci) = 1 - average contradiction percentage. F(ci)=1 means the model is fully faithful (no contradictions); low faithfulness indicates fabrication. Response-level faithfulness F(R) averages across all claims.

**Claim-Level Uncertainty:** The inter-sample consistency S(ci) measures how many of the N sampled responses entail claim ci. This is weighted by an unfaithfulness term W(ci) computed via convolution with an exponential-decay kernel over the sequence of faithfulness scores. The final uncertainty U(ci) = S(ci) * W(ci). This captures the insight that once a model fabricates a claim, subsequent claims generated under that false context are also suspect.

**Answer-Level Uncertainty:** An entropy-based metric UA operates on the token probabilities of the short-answer sets, bypassing the difficulty of aggregating token probabilities over long-form text.

**Evaluation:** Factuality labels (correct/incorrect) are obtained using GPT-4o against Wikipedia reference articles. Performance is measured via AUROC and AUPRC for claim-level correctness classification.

## Results & Highlights

- **IUQ consistently achieves the best AUROC** across nearly all model-dataset combinations, outperforming Claim Entailment (S), Closeness Centrality (CC), Perplexity, Max Token Entropy, CCP, and Frequency Scoring.
- On FActScore (biography), models show **lower faithfulness** than on LongFact (popular topics), suggesting models fabricate more on lesser-known subjects.
- **Larger and stronger models** (GPT-4o, LLaMA-3.3-70B) tend to be more faithful than smaller models, but still exhibit non-trivial fabrication rates.
- The **exponential-decay kernel** outperforms linear, cumulative, and no-error-propagation variants, validating the autoregressive context-dependence hypothesis.
- A controlled ablation (IUQ-rev) confirms that checking contradiction against *preceding* claims (not subsequent ones) is essential, validating the directional design.
- **More sampled responses** improve IUQ performance, with diminishing returns beyond 5 samples.
- Case study on "Rory Byrne" illustrates how 5 generations all fabricate a coherent architect biography; inter-sample supportness S gives high scores (0.60) for fabricated claims, while faithfulness F correctly flags them (0.17).
- **Computational cost**: IUQ requires ~47.3% more tokens than the graph-based baseline (Jiang et al., 2024b), with the additional stages being question generation, answer generation, and contradiction evaluation.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The interrogate-then-respond paradigm is a genuinely novel contribution. While claim decomposition and inter-sample consistency are established techniques, the idea of generating independent questions to test whether a model "truly knows" what it claimed -- and the formalization of claim-level faithfulness -- is original and addresses a real blind spot in existing methods.

**Rigor:** The experimental design is thorough: 6 model families, 2 datasets, multiple baselines (white-box and black-box), ablation studies on the influence kernel, directional ablation (IUQ-rev), sensitivity analysis on number of generations, and statistical significance via Pearson correlation with confidence intervals. The case study in Appendix D provides intuitive validation.

**Reproducibility:** Code is publicly available at https://github.com/louisfanhz/IUQ. Datasets (FActScore, LongFact) are public. The use of 50 entities per dataset is small but justified for computational reasons. The pipeline relies heavily on GPT-4o for claim decomposition, question generation, and contradiction evaluation, which introduces a dependency on a proprietary model.

**Significance:** This paper addresses a practically important problem -- detecting hallucination in long-form LLM outputs. The observation that models fabricate information for logical consistency and that inter-sample consistency alone is insufficient is valuable for the community.

**Red flags / Concerns:**
- The faithfulness metric itself relies on LLM self-evaluation (the same model checks for contradictions), which could be unreliable. The authors acknowledge this limitation (Section 7).
- The additional ~47% token cost is non-trivial for deployment scenarios.
- Evaluation on only 50 entities per dataset is limited. Broader validation would strengthen claims.
- Factuality annotation uses GPT-4o as judge, which may introduce systematic biases.
- The paper does not address how IUQ would handle claims that are subjective, ambiguous, or for which ground truth is unavailable.

## Strengths

- Novel and well-motivated framework that addresses a genuine limitation of existing UQ methods for long-form generation.
- The interrogate-then-respond paradigm is intuitive, principled, and effectively captures fabrication behavior that inter-sample consistency misses.
- Comprehensive evaluation across diverse model families, sizes, and datasets.
- Clear formalization of claim-level faithfulness with a time-series-inspired influence kernel.
- Strong ablation studies validating each design choice (directionality, kernel choice, number of samples).
- Open-source code for reproducibility.
- The case study (Appendix D) is compelling and illustrates the method's practical value.

## Limitations

- **Reliance on LLM reasoning**: The pipeline depends on LLM capabilities for claim decomposition, question generation, answer generation, and contradiction evaluation. Errors in any stage can compound, and there is no guarantee that pipeline hallucination will be detected.
- **Computational overhead**: ~47.3% more tokens than graph-based methods, with multiple inference stages per claim. This may limit scalability for very long responses or real-time applications.
- **Small evaluation set**: Only 50 entities per dataset. Results may not generalize to broader topic distributions.
- **GPT-4o as annotator**: Using GPT-4o for factuality labeling introduces potential systematic bias in ground truth.
- **No handling of refusal responses**: Responses where the model declines to answer are excluded, which could bias results toward cases where the model is more willing to generate (and potentially fabricate).
- **Single-model interrogator**: Using the same model as both responder and interrogator could introduce systematic bias. Cross-model interrogation is not explored.

## Relevance

本文对长文本生成中的幻觉检测和不确定性量化提出了创新方法，对构建可信的LLM系统具有重要参考价值。特别是声明级忠实度分析可为RAG系统和知识密集型应用的可靠性评估提供新思路。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does IUQ perform when a different (potentially stronger) model is used as the interrogator rather than the same model that generated the response?
- Can the faithfulness metric be calibrated or combined with retrieval-augmented verification to reduce reliance on LLM self-evaluation?
- How does IUQ scale to very long documents (e.g., multi-page reports) where the number of claims grows substantially?
- What is the sensitivity of IUQ to the claim decomposition quality -- would a more fine-grained or coarse-grained decomposition change the results significantly?
- Could the exponential-decay kernel parameter lambda be adaptively tuned per-model or per-domain rather than fixed?
- How does IUQ perform on non-English long-form generation, or on domains where factual ground truth is harder to establish?
- Is there a way to reduce the ~47% computational overhead while retaining most of the performance gain?
