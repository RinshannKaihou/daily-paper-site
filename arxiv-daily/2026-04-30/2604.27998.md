> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Latent-GRPO，通过无效样本优势屏蔽、单侧噪声采样和最优正确路径首 token 选择三种策略，解决了将 GRPO 直接应用于潜在推理时的三个耦合瓶颈，在大幅缩短推理链的同时超越了显式 GRPO 的性能。

> **TL;DR:** Latent-GRPO adapts GRPO to latent-token reasoning by identifying and fixing three fundamental instabilities (off-manifold exploration, exploration-optimization misalignment, and latent mixture non-closure), achieving +7.86 Pass@1 on low-difficulty and +14.77 on high-difficulty benchmarks over latent-SFT while using 3–4x shorter reasoning chains than explicit GRPO.

## 核心贡献

- 系统性分析了将 GRPO 直接迁移到潜在推理空间时面临的三个耦合瓶颈：缺乏内在潜在流形导致探索偏离有效区域、探索-优化方向错位导致正确轨迹被错误惩罚、以及多正确路径联合优化时产生无效平均状态（latent mixture non-closure）。
- 提出无效样本优势屏蔽（Invalid Sample Advantage Masking），将未正常终止的 rollout 排除在组统计之外，防止离流形样本污染优势估计。
- 提出单侧噪声采样（One-sided Noise Sampling），通过对 Gumbel 噪声进行裁剪和偏移确保扰动边际始终为正，配合 Straight-Through Estimator 在多轮 PPO 更新中保持优化方向一致性。
- 提出最优正确路径首 token 选择（Optimal Correct Path First Token Selection），在共享前缀下仅保留平均代理对数似然最高的正确路径的首 token 梯度，避免多正确路径在第一步的有害模式平均。

## Key Contributions

- Diagnoses three coupled bottlenecks when naively adapting GRPO to latent-token reasoning: absence of intrinsic latent manifolds, exploration-optimization misalignment under Gumbel perturbations, and latent mixture non-closure at shared prefixes.
- Proposes Invalid Sample Advantage Masking to exclude non-terminating rollouts from group baseline computation, keeping exploration bounded to the valid latent manifold.
- Introduces One-sided Noise Sampling with a clipped-and-shifted Gumbel transformation and conditional STE to guarantee positive perturbation margins throughout PPO updates, aligning gradient direction with trajectory advantage.
- Designs Optimal Correct Path First Token Selection that scores correct trajectories by average surrogate log-probability and updates only the first token of the best path, preventing harmful mode averaging while preserving later-token exploration.

## Methodology

Latent-GRPO builds on Soft-GRPO's mathematical framework for mixed latent-discrete trajectories but addresses its stability failures through three targeted mechanisms:

1. **Initialization from Latent-SFT** ensures a valid latent reasoning manifold exists before RL exploration begins, since latent RL cannot spontaneously induce latent reasoning in an explicit model.
2. **Invalid Sample Advantage Masking** defines a valid subset $G_{\text{valid}}$ of trajectories that terminate before the maximum response length. Group mean $\mu_R$ and standard deviation $\sigma_R$ are computed only over this subset, and invalid trajectories receive zero advantage.
3. **One-sided Noise Sampling** transforms standard Gumbel noise via $\xi_i^+ = \text{clip}(\xi_i, -a, b) + a + \delta$ (with $a{=}1.5, b{=}3.0$), guaranteeing perturbation margins stay positive. A conditional Straight-Through Estimator flips gradients when the updated prediction crosses the fixed target, maintaining one-sided behavior across PPO epochs.
4. **Optimal Correct Path First Token Selection** selects the correct trajectory with the highest average per-token surrogate log-probability $\bar{\ell}_j$ and masks the first-token updates of all other correct paths. Later-token updates from all paths remain active since prefixes diverge after the first step.

The final Latent-GRPO objective combines these into a clipped surrogate with KL penalty, using the one-sided Gumbel-margin surrogate log-likelihood for latent tokens and standard categorical density for explicit tokens.

## Results & Highlights

- **Low-difficulty (LLaMA-3.2-1B-Instruct):** Latent-GRPO improves over Latent-SFT by +7.86 Pass@1 on average across GSM8K-Aug, GSM-Hard, SVAMP, and MultiArith, while being 4.44x shorter than explicit GRPO. On GSM8K-Aug it reaches 66.29 vs. GRPO's 62.26.
- **High-difficulty (Qwen2.5-Math-7B):** Latent-GRPO improves over Latent-SFT by +14.77 Pass@1 on average, surpasses explicit GRPO by +4.27 points, and is 3.31x shorter. It achieves best Pass@1 on Math500 (80.40), AIME24 (26.56), and AIME25 (19.79).
- **Training stability:** While explicit GRPO collapses during extended training, Latent-GRPO remains stable through 10k steps with negligible response length growth.
- **Pass@k under Gumbel sampling:** Latent-GRPO reaches 87.8 on Math500 at pass@64 and 50.0/53.3 on AIME24/AIME25, substantially outperforming explicit GRPO's pass@k curves.
- **Ablations:** One-sided Noise Sampling is the primary stability driver; removing it causes collapse. First Token Selection provides additional gains that are critical on high-difficulty tasks.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The paper's primary novelty lies in the rigorous diagnosis of why GRPO fails in latent space and the principled design of three targeted fixes. The One-sided Noise Sampling with STE is technically elegant, and the latent mixture non-closure problem is a genuinely novel insight about continuous-action RL in language models.
- **Rigor:** The mathematical derivations are thorough, and the ablation study cleanly isolates each component's contribution. However, the experiments are limited to two model sizes (1B and 7B) and mathematical reasoning tasks only. Scaling to larger models or broader task distributions (e.g., code generation, multi-hop QA) remains untested.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong — public code, detailed hyperparameters in appendices, and fixed random seeds. All experiments run on 8 A100 GPUs, which is accessible for many labs.
- **Significance:** This is a meaningful step toward making latent reasoning practical. The 3–4x compression with performance gains over explicit GRPO is compelling. However, the reliance on Latent-SFT initialization means the approach inherits any limitations of the SFT stage.
- **Red flags:** The SVAMP result (56.50 vs. GRPO's 60.78) is not fully explained — the authors attribute it to initialization mismatch but do not verify this experimentally. The GPQA results in Table 2 show only marginal improvement over Latent-SFT (6.61 → 6.77), suggesting the method may struggle on out-of-distribution scientific reasoning. The deterministic No Sampling mode is evaluated for Pass@1 only, while the more impressive pass@k numbers require Gumbel noise injection during inference, adding complexity.

## Strengths

- Clean problem formulation: the three-bottleneck diagnosis is systematic and insightful.
- Each proposed solution directly addresses a diagnosed bottleneck with minimal additional complexity.
- Strong empirical gains on math benchmarks, especially AIME where latent reasoning typically struggles.
- Training stability without length collapse, a known issue in explicit reasoning RL.
- Public code and data release.

## Limitations

- Evaluated only on mathematical reasoning benchmarks; generalization to other reasoning domains is unknown.
- The approach requires Latent-SFT initialization, adding an extra training stage.
- GPQA performance is near-identical to the latent-SFT baseline, suggesting limited gains on scientific reasoning.
- The 1B model results are less convincing — SVAMP underperforms explicit GRPO.
- The pass@1 vs. pass@k trade-off under Gumbel sampling on hard benchmarks (pass@1 drops when sampling is enabled) reveals a tension between exploration diversity and single-shot accuracy.

## Relevance

本文研究了强化学习在潜在推理空间中的应用，是提升 LLM 推理效率的重要方向。Latent-GRPO 通过系统性分析 GRPO 在潜在推理中的失效机理并提出针对性解决方案，对推理加速和高效推理链设计有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does Latent-GRPO scale to models beyond 7B (e.g., 14B, 32B, 70B)? Does the relative advantage over explicit GRPO grow, shrink, or stay constant?
- Can the three proposed mechanisms transfer to other RL algorithms (e.g., PPO, REINFORCE++) or only work with GRPO's group-relative advantage structure?
- What happens when latent reasoning length is further compressed — is there a fundamental compression-performance frontier?
- How does the approach perform on non-mathematical reasoning tasks such as code generation, logical deduction, or multi-hop question answering?
- Can the One-sided Noise Sampling principle be applied to other continuous-action RL settings beyond language models?
