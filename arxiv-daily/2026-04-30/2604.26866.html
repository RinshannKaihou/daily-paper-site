MoRFI: Monotonic Sparse Autoencoder Feature Identification

arXiv: 2604.26866

Date: 2026-04-29

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26866v1

一句话总结： 本文提出 MoRFI 算法，通过引导式单调趋势检测从 SAE 潜变量中识别出因微调未知知识而因果性地导致模型幻觉的残差流方向，并通过单潜变量激活操控最高可恢复 ~40% 的遗忘知识，揭示微调导致的遗忘本质上是知识访问路径被破坏而非知识被擦除。
TL;DR: MoRFI identifies SAE latent directions that causally contribute to fine-tuning-induced hallucinations via bootstrapped monotonic trend detection. Single-latent activation steering recovers up to ~40% relative accuracy on forgotten facts across Llama 3.1 8B, Gemma 2 9B, and Mistral 7B v0.3, demonstrating that fine-tuning on unknown knowledge disrupts knowledge access pathways rather than erasing stored knowledge.

核心贡献

  • 提出 MoRFI（Monotonic Relationship Feature Identification） 算法，通过自助法重采样、Spearman 秩相关检验和 Mann-Kendall 趋势检验的组合，从 SAE 潜变量中筛选出对微调数据属性（如未知事实比例、训练轮数）具有鲁棒单调趋势的特征。
  • 设计了受控微调实验：在三个模型上系统性地改变训练集中未知事实的比例（0%–100%）和训练轮数，构建 4D 激活张量并验证幻觉率随未知知识比例递增。
  • 发现单潜变量操控即可在大规模模型上产生显著的知识恢复效果（最高 +39.77% 相对精度提升），远超复合方向（差异均值向量）的效果，表明知识相关信号在 SAE 空间中是高度稀疏的。
  • 提出知识恢复率 $R_K$ 指标，证明 69–85% 的恢复事实与基准模型（仅用已知知识微调）所能回答的事实一致，表明这些潜变量编码的是知识访问路径而非性能本身。
  • 发现抑制性潜变量（decreasing latents）比增强性潜变量影响更大，且负向操控通常优于正向操控，揭示了微调对参数知识的非对称性影响。

Key Contributions

  • Introduces MoRFI, a general-purpose algorithm combining bootstrapped resampling with dual statistical tests (Spearman + Mann-Kendall) to identify SAE latents exhibiting robust monotonic activation trends along a controlled dimension of variation.
  • Conducts a rigorously controlled fine-tuning study across three architectures (Llama 3.1 8B, Gemma 2 9B, Mistral 7B v0.3), varying unknown-fact proportion (0–100%) and training epochs to construct a 4D activation tensor capturing representational drift.
  • Demonstrates that single-latent steering yields up to +39.77% relative accuracy gains on hallucination-prone checkpoints, substantially outperforming composite difference-in-means directions — revealing that the knowledge-relevant signal is sparse within the representational shift.
  • Defines the Knowledge Recovery rate ($R_K$) metric and shows that 69–85% of steering-recovered facts were known to the Known-only baseline (MD0), indicating that identified latents control access to stored parametric knowledge rather than general task performance.
  • Uncovers an asymmetry in steering dynamics: negatively steered latents consistently outperform positively steered ones, and decreasing latents (suppressed by fine-tuning) are disproportionately impactful — suggesting fine-tuning disrupts existing knowledge access pathways rather than creating new confabulatory ones.

Methodology

The paper follows a two-stage pipeline applied to three open-weight base models (Llama 3.1 8B, Gemma 2 9B, Mistral 7B v0.3):

Controlled Fine-tuning Setup. Using the EntityQuestions dataset (Wikidata-derived closed-book QA), the authors classify training examples as Known or Unknown via $P_{\text{Correct}}$ scoring (greedy + sampling-based evaluation on the base model). They create seven fine-tuning datasets with varying proportions of Unknown facts ($p \in \{0, 10, 25, 50, 75, 90, 100\}\%$) and train each model for multiple epoch counts (early-stop through 50 epochs), yielding 42 fine-tuned checkpoints per model.

Activation Tensor Construction. Pre-trained SAEs (Llama Scope, Gemma Scope, and an Engels et al. SAE for Mistral) extract sparse activations from the middle residual stream layer. This produces a 4D tensor $\mathcal{A} \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times P \times F \times N}$ spanning epochs, property configurations, latent features, and input samples.

MoRFI (Algorithm 1). For a target aggregation dimension (e.g., across epochs or across mixture proportions), the algorithm: (1) generates $R=1000$ bootstrap replicates by resampling the input dimension; (2) computes mean activations and validates monotonic trends using both Spearman rank correlation and Mann-Kendall tests (requiring joint significance at $\alpha = 0.05$); (3) classifies features as increasing ($V_\uparrow$) or decreasing ($V_\downarrow$) based on sign of both $\rho$ and $\tau$; (4) ranks features by bootstrap frequency (fraction of replicates where each feature appears in the top-K most changed latents).

Activation Steering (Algorithm 2). Candidates from MoRFI are validated by steering their activations at inference time. A uniform initial sweep ($\alpha = 0.4$) filters to the top-40, followed by grid search over $\alpha$. The steering vector $c \cdot \alpha \cdot s_\ell \Phi_k$ is added to the residual stream post-middle-layer, where $\Phi_k$ is the SAE decoder vector for latent $k$ and $s_\ell$ is a layer-wise activation norm scaling factor.

Results & Highlights

  • Hallucination scales with unknown knowledge: Across all three models, test accuracy decreases monotonically as the proportion of Unknown facts in fine-tuning data increases, with prolonged training intensifying the effect (Figure 2).
  • Single-latent steering is powerful: The best single latent achieves +33.4% (Llama), +18.46% (Gemma), and +39.77% (Mistral) relative accuracy improvements on the worst-performing checkpoints (Table 1, 9, 10).
  • Negative steering outperforms positive: Suppressing increasing latents or decreasing latents via negative steering consistently yields larger gains than amplification (e.g., +33.4 vs +21.7 for Llama's best decreasing latent).
  • Decreasing latents are more impactful: Features that decrease when fine-tuned on more Unknown data, when steered positively, can triple the gains from increasing features (Llama: +21.7 vs +7.12).
  • Sparse knowledge signal: The composite direction $\delta_u$ (difference-in-means) produces smaller gains than individual top latents, and within-group cosine similarity is low ($\approx 0.3$), indicating a distributed sparse subspace rather than a shared low-rank structure.
  • Knowledge recovery is genuine: 69–85% of recovered facts correspond to those the Known-only baseline (MD0) could answer, with geographic relations (P17/country, P36/capital, P495/country-of-origin) consistently co-recovered across all models.
  • Overshooting MD0 levels: Optimal steering substantially overshoots the original MD0 activation levels at the middle layer, suggesting the model is tolerant to a range of perturbations — downstream layers may funnel different states toward similar output configurations.

Critical Assessment

Novelty: The core contribution is the MoRFI algorithm itself — using bootstrapped monotonic trend detection across a controlled gradient of fine-tuning conditions, rather than simple difference-in-means between two states. This is a meaningful methodological advance over prior model-diffing approaches. The insight that decreasing (suppressed) latents are more impactful than increasing ones is novel and counterintuitive.

Rigor: The controlled experimental design (systematic variation of unknown-fact ratio and epochs) is a significant strength. The dual statistical test requirement (Spearman + Mann-Kendall) with bootstrap resampling provides robust feature selection. Control latents (non-trending features) are used to validate that MoRFI-selected latents are not arbitrary. However, results are limited to a single dataset (EntityQuestions) and a single task (closed-book QA). Generalization to open-ended generation, reasoning, or code tasks is untested.

Reproducibility: The paper uses publicly available models and SAEs. The EntityQuestions dataset is public. Fine-tuning hyperparameters are provided (Appendix A). The algorithms are clearly specified. Reproducibility should be high given the level of detail.

Significance: The finding that fine-tuning-induced forgetting reflects disrupted access rather than knowledge erasure has important implications for alignment safety and hallucination mitigation. If knowledge remains in weights but access is impaired, inference-time interventions (steering) could complement training-time approaches. The MoRFI algorithm is general-purpose and could be applied to other properties beyond knowledge.

Red flags: (1) Steering is evaluated only on the dev/test split of the same dataset used for fine-tuning — it is unclear whether recovered knowledge generalizes to out-of-distribution queries. (2) The paper does not compare against other hallucination mitigation methods (e.g., retrieval augmentation, decoding strategies), making it hard to assess practical utility. (3) The analysis is limited to the middle layer; the paper acknowledges that other layers may contain complementary signals. (4) The "tolerant manifold" interpretation (Section 5) is speculative and lacks geometric validation.

Strengths

  • Rigorously controlled experimental design with systematic variation of two independent factors (unknown-fact ratio, training epochs).
  • MoRFI is a principled, general-purpose algorithm with clear statistical foundations (bootstrap + dual trend tests).
  • Strong empirical results: single-latent steering achieving up to ~40% relative accuracy recovery is impressive.
  • The knowledge recovery rate ($R_K$) analysis provides meaningful mechanistic insight — latents control access to stored knowledge rather than task performance.
  • Cross-model consistency strengthens the generality of findings.
  • Control latent experiments effectively rule out spurious explanations.

Limitations

  • Limited to a single dataset (EntityQuestions) and a single task format (closed-book QA with short entity answers); generalization to free-form generation is untested.
  • Steering is applied only at the middle residual stream layer; other layers and attention heads are not explored.
  • No comparison with existing hallucination mitigation techniques (RAG, constrained decoding, fact-checking).
  • The approach requires pre-trained SAEs, which may not be available for all model architectures and sizes.
  • Steering coefficients ($\alpha$) are tuned per-latent on the dev set, raising questions about robustness to distribution shift.
  • The paper does not address whether MoRFI-identified latents are stable across different random seeds or data orderings during fine-tuning.

Relevance

本文与 LLM 可解释性和 SAE 研究高度相关。MoRFI 提供了一种系统化的 SAE 特征筛选方法，可直接应用于分析微调对模型内部表征的影响。单潜变量操控即可恢复大量遗忘知识的发现，为理解 SAE 潜变量的因果角色和开发推理时知识干预方法提供了重要参考。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Can MoRFI-identified latents transfer across domains — e.g., can latents discovered via EntityQuestions steering also recover knowledge on unrelated QA benchmarks?
  2. How does the choice of SAE training data and architecture (JumpReLU, TopK, etc.) affect the quality of MoRFI-discovered latents?
  3. Is the "knowledge access pathway" interpretation consistent across layers, or do different layers encode different aspects of the knowledge retrieval process?
  4. Could MoRFI be applied to detect and mitigate other fine-tuning-induced behaviors (e.g., sycophancy, jailbreak susceptibility) by controlling for relevant properties in the training data?
  5. What is the relationship between MoRFI-identified latents and circuit-level explanations of factual recall (e.g., attention heads identified in prior mechanistic work)?
