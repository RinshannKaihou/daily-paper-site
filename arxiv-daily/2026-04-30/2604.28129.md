> **一句话总结：** LAD（Latent Adversarial Detection）通过提取LLM残差流激活并计算五个标量轨迹特征来检测多轮提示注入攻击，发现"对抗性不安"信号将对话级检测准确率从76.2%提升至93.8%，并在四模型家族上以89.4%检测率/2.4%误报率显著优于Lakera Guard（95.2%/76.3%误报）等现有工具。

> **TL;DR:** Multi-turn prompt injection attacks leave a measurable activation-level signature ("adversarial restlessness") in LLM residual streams. Five scalar trajectory features (drift magnitude, cosine similarity, cumulative drift, drift acceleration, mean drift) lift conversation-level detection from 76.2% (snapshot baseline) to 93.8% on synthetic held-out data (Gemma 3 27B, 797 conversations), and combined three-source training achieves 89.4% detection at 2.4% FPR on Qwen 2.5 32B — versus Lakera Guard's 95.2% detection but 76.3% FPR (32× lower FP for LAD). Off-the-shelf tools PromptGuard (19.8% det) and LLM Guard (29.0% det) miss most multi-turn attacks entirely.

## 核心贡献

- 提出并验证"对抗性不安"（adversarial restlessness）概念：多轮攻击的每个阶段转换都会在残差流中产生激活偏移，导致累计路径长度远超正常对话。
- 设计五个标量轨迹特征（漂移幅度、余弦相似度、累计漂移、漂移加速度、平均漂移），将合成数据集上对话级检测从76.2%提升至93.8%（+17.6pp，3.5%误报率）。
- 构建带三阶段轮次标签（benign/pivoting/adversarial）的合成多轮攻击数据集——现有基准（LMSYS-Chat-1M、SafeDialBench、MHJ）均无pivoting标签。
- 在四个模型家族（Gemma 3 27B、Mistral 3.1-24B、Qwen 2.5-32B、Llama 3.1-70B，24B-70B参数）上验证信号复现性，同时证明探针是模型特定的，无法跨架构迁移。
- 通过leave-one-source-out评估证明三个数据源各自不可替代，并通过对抗鲁棒性分析表明攻击者需抑制80-90%的漂移才能逃避检测。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies **adversarial restlessness** as a novel activation-level signal: multi-turn attacks follow a trust-building → pivoting → escalation path, and each phase shift moves activations in the residual stream, producing trajectory lengths far exceeding benign conversations.
- Introduces five scalar trajectory features (drift magnitude ‖Δt‖₂, cosine similarity, cumulative drift Ct, drift acceleration at, mean drift d̄t) that lift detection from 76.2% to 93.8% (+17.6pp at 3.5% FP) on synthetic held-out data.
- Constructs a synthetic multi-turn attack dataset with **three-phase turn-level labels** (benign/pivoting/adversarial) — the pivoting annotation is absent from all existing benchmarks (LMSYS-Chat-1M, SafeDialBench, MHJ), enabling early detection before the first overtly adversarial turn.
- Demonstrates the signal **replicates across four model families** (Gemma 3 27B, Mistral 3.1-24B, Qwen 2.5-32B, Llama 3.1-70B; 24B–70B), achieving 89–96% detection at 0.5–2.0% FP, though probes are model-specific and do not transfer (off-diagonal F1 ≈ 50.4%, near random).
- Conducts **leave-one-source-out** generalization analysis showing each of synthetic, LMSYS-Chat-1M, and SafeDialBench is non-redundant (removing any causes catastrophic failure: 0% SafeDialBench detection without SafeDialBench training, 100% FP without LMSYS benign data).
- Shows adversarial robustness: a realistic attacker (adversarial-turns-only) must suppress 80–90% of drift to evade; at 30% suppression detection remains 88–89%.

## Methodology

The LAD pipeline operates at the activation level rather than the text level. For a decoder-only transformer with L layers, a forward hook captures the residual-stream hidden state hℓ ∈ R^d at the last-token position of a middle-to-late decoder layer ℓ, at each user-turn boundary after feeding the cumulative conversation context through the model's chat template. Activations are extracted in BF16 and cast to FP32 for numerical stability (BF16→FP16 causes overflow to inf in hook outputs).

From the resulting activation trajectory {v1, …, vT}, five scalar features are derived: (1) drift magnitude ‖Δt‖₂ = ‖vt − vt−1‖₂; (2) cosine similarity cos(vt, vt−1); (3) cumulative drift Ct = Σ‖Δi‖₂ (total path length); (4) drift acceleration at = ‖Δt‖ − ‖Δt−1‖; (5) mean drift d̄t. A sixth feature (absolute turn position) was ablated out as it introduced a residual-length confound (+1.2pp improvement on removal).

The primary probe concatenates the turn activation vt with the five scalars and classifies via **XGBoost** (300 trees, max depth 6, learning rate 0.1, subsample 0.8, colsample_bytree 0.8, StandardScaler, θ=0.5, no threshold tuning). A contrastive two-stage variant trains a contrastive MLP (d → 512 → 128, L2-normalized output, 50K pairs, 50 epochs, CPU ~10 min) to produce a style-invariant embedding, then freezes it and runs XGBoost on the 128-dim embedding + 5 scalars (133 features total).

**Models:** Gemma 3 27B-IT (layer 32, d=5,376), Mistral 3.1-24B (layer 24, d=5,120), Qwen 2.5-32B (layer 32, d=5,120), Llama 3.1-70B (layer 40, d=8,192). All instruction-tuned variants.

**Datasets:** Synthetic multi-turn (1,125 train + 797 eval, generated by Qwen3-235B across 6 HACCA-mapped attack categories + 4 benign categories, attacks pivot at ~81% through conversation); LMSYS-Chat-1M (1,200 train + 800 eval, filtered for English and 7+ user turns, per-message OpenAI moderation binary labels, 249 train / 166 eval adversarial); SafeDialBench (300 train + 200 eval, 7 attack strategies, all adversarial). Combined training: 2,625 conversations (1,434 adversarial, 1,191 benign, 1.2:1 ratio). Combined held-out eval: 1,797 conversations (797 synthetic + 800 LMSYS + 200 SafeDialBench).

**Hardware:** Activation extraction on NVIDIA H200 (RunPod, 141 GB VRAM) for Gemma/Mistral/Qwen in parallel; 2×H100 80GB for Llama 70B. Dataset generation via Qwen3-235B on 2×H200 (vLLM). Probe training and all evaluation CPU-only (<2 min on cached activations).

## Results & Highlights

- **Baseline to proposed:** On synthetic held-out (797 conversations, Gemma 3 27B), scalar-augmented XGBoost lifts conversation-level detection from 76.2% (snapshot, activation-only) to **93.8%** (+17.6pp) at 3.5% FP. Scalars alone (5 features, no raw activations) achieve 89.6% detection.
- **Cross-model replication:** Detection replicates at **89–96%** across all four model families (Gemma 3 27B, Mistral 3.1-24B, Qwen 2.5-32B, Llama 3.1-70B) with FP rates of **0.5–2.0%** on synthetic held-out.
- **Combined three-source training:** Standard XGBoost achieves **85–89% detection at 2–4% FP** across four models on combined held-out (n=1,797). Best: Qwen 2.5 32B at **89.4% detection, 2.4% FP** (95% CI: 87.5–91.5%).
- **Per-source generalization:** Synthetic 89–96% detection; SafeDialBench 99–100%; LMSYS (real-world) only 47–71%, reflecting a distinct attack distribution requiring its own training data. Leave-one-source-out: 0% SafeDialBench detection without SafeDialBench training, 100% FP without LMSYS benign data.
- **Label granularity:** Binary conversation-level labels produce **50–59% FP**; three-phase turn-level labels reduce FP to **0.5–2%** — a necessary condition for deployment.
- **Baseline comparison (14,728 turns):** PromptGuard (86M): 19.8% detection / 16.1% FP; LLM Guard (DeBERTa): 29.0% / 27.9%; Lakera Guard (API): 95.2% / 76.3% FP. LAD: 85–89% detection at 2–4% FP — **32× lower FP** than Lakera. LAD achieves 14.9× higher pivoting selectivity (S_piv=14.9 vs Lakera's 1.8; S_adv=91.0 vs 2.3).
- **SAE ablation:** Ablating the top 1,000 GemmaScope 2 SAE features (1.5% of 65k) degrades accuracy by only 0.4pp — confirming detection is orthogonal to content features.
- **Adversarial robustness:** A realistic attacker (adversarial-turns-only) must suppress 80–90% of drift to evade; at 30% suppression, detection remains 88–89%.
- **Early detection with extended pivoting:** With 329 extended conversations (mean 6.7 pivoting turns vs 1.9 original), early detection improves 3–4× (66–83% vs 22–26%), with +1.2–1.6 turns mean lead time.

## Critical Assessment

- **Single-author infrastructure risk:** The entire experimental pipeline (4 model families, H200/H100 GPU orchestration, dataset generation via Qwen3-235B, contrastive encoder training, XGBoost evaluation) is executed by one author with no stated co-authors or institutional affiliation beyond a city. This raises legitimate concerns about undetected implementation errors, though the rigorous ablation battery (leave-one-source-out, feature ablation, SAE ablation, cross-model transfer, adversarial robustness) provides substantial internal consistency checks.
- **Generalization gap on real-world data is large and acknowledged:** LMSYS detection at 47–71% (with 99.1% FP when trained on synthetic-only) means the probe is far from deployable on real conversations without a cold-start labeled-data collection period per target model. The paper is honest about this but the practical deployment story remains thin.
- **Cold-start problem is unresolved:** Probes are model-specific (off-diagonal F1 ≈ 50.4%, near random; Gemma 3 shows zero transfer). Each new deployment requires its own labeled activation data, and the paper does not demonstrate a path to zero-shot or few-shot probe transfer.
- **Adversarial robustness is simulated, not adaptive:** The drift-suppression attack (interpolating vt toward vt−1) is a stylized threat; a probe-aware adversary who optimizes against the trajectory features directly (e.g., via gradient-based activation optimization) is not evaluated. The paper notes "does not test probe-aware generation" as a limitation.
- **Class imbalance on real data:** LMSYS has only 5% adversarial turns; per-source PR-AUC is 0.38–0.44 (low) despite AUROC 0.88–0.91, meaning precision suffers badly in deployment where adversarial prevalence is low.

## Strengths

- Novel and intuitive framing of multi-turn attacks through activation trajectory analysis ("adversarial restlessness"), grounded in the observation that attack complexity is its own signal.
- Strong empirical gains: 76.2% → 93.8% on synthetic data; 89.4% / 2.4% FP combined with 32× lower FP than Lakera Guard.
- Honest characterization of limitations — model-specific probes, source-dependent generalization, necessity of fine-grained labels, cold-start problem.
- Exceptionally thorough ablation battery: leave-one-source-out, feature ablation, SAE ablation, cross-model transfer, label granularity, layer sensitivity, adversarial robustness, and extended-pivoting early-detection analysis.
- CPU-only retraining on cached activations (<1s) enables a continual-adaptation deployment model where rising FP rates signal distributional shift.
- External label validation via three frontier LLM judges (Fleiss' κ = 0.718) strengthens the dataset's credibility.

## Limitations

- **No cross-architecture transfer:** Probes must be trained per-model (off-diagonal F1 ≈ 50.4%), increasing deployment cost for multi-model systems.
- **Distribution sensitivity:** Real-world LMSYS detection drops to 47–71% with low PR-AUC (0.38–0.44) due to 5% adversarial prevalence; zero-shot applicability is absent.
- **White-box access required:** The probe needs a forward hook on the target model's internal activations, limiting applicability to self-hosted models (no API-only deployment).
- **Adaptive adversary concern unaddressed:** Probe-aware generation attacks are not evaluated; the drift-suppression threat model is stylized.
- **Single author:** Limits breadth of expertise and scale of independent validation.
- **Cold-start problem:** Each new model needs its own labeled-data collection period before reaching production-quality performance.

## Relevance

本文直接对应"LLM Hidden States & Interpretability"研究兴趣：通过残差流激活轨迹分析检测多轮攻击，是激活层可解释性在安全场景的具体应用。同时与"LLM Inference Evaluation"相关——探针在推理时实时监控激活状态，且对抗鲁棒性分析涉及推理时干预（activation steering）。其per-model探针不可迁移的发现对理解隐藏状态表征的架构特异性有方法论价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. Can adaptive adversaries craft multi-turn attacks that minimize activation trajectory length via gradient-based activation optimization while maintaining attack effectiveness?
2. Would probe transfer improve if models shared a common pretraining corpus, tokenizer, or embedding space (e.g., within the same model family at different sizes)?
3. How do the five trajectory features relate to specific mechanistic phenomena in the residual stream (attention head dynamics, MLP activations, specific SAE features)?
4. Can the rank-r subspace defense (R ∈ R^{d×r}, r≈4–8, Stiefel manifold) unify detection with conditional activation steering for inference-time intervention?
5. How does detection performance scale with model size beyond 70B parameters, and does the adversarial restlessness signal strengthen or weaken in frontier-scale models?
6. Could a probe trained on the trajectory scalars of multiple models jointly (meta-learning) achieve cross-architecture transfer where per-model probes fail?
