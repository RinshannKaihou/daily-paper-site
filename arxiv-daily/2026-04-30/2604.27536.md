> **一句话总结：** 本文提出VEROIC框架，将黑盒LLM服务的推理控制建模为POMDP，通过聚合异构质量信号（硬谓词+软代理分数）构建对响应可靠性的贝叶斯信念状态，并用预算感知策略决定是否升级到高代价推理路径，在7个基准上以5%增强预算实现最优质量-成本权衡。

> **TL;DR:** VEROIC formulates request-time inference control for black-box LLM services as a POMDP, aggregating heterogeneous quality signals (4 hard verifiable checks + 2 soft proxy scores) into a Bayesian belief state over latent response reliability, then using a budget-aware actor-critic policy to decide escalation. Under CMIS (LLaMA-3.1-8B→70B, Qwen-2.5-7B→32B) with α=0.05 enhanced budget on 7 benchmarks (GSM8K, MATH, HumanEval, MBPP, HotpotQA, 2WikiMHQA, PopQA), VEROIC achieves the best task utility (e.g., GSM8K EM 92.4 vs LLaMA-8B 86.8 vs strongest baseline RISK-PREDICTOR 91.2), best risk estimation (GSM8K AUROC 0.87, ECE 0.048 vs 0.85/0.058), and best long-horizon control (GSM8K Occ 0.13, RecD 77 vs 0.17/98 for RISK-PREDICTOR).

## 核心贡献

- 将黑盒LLM服务的请求时推理控制形式化为POMDP，隐状态为默认响应的二元可靠性（reliable/unreliable），显式建模部分可观测性与序列预算耦合。
- 设计轻量级可验证观测通道：4个硬谓词（格式/规则检查、一致性/单元测试、可回答性/扎根性、安全/合规）+ 2个软代理分数（自一致性、外部轻量验证器），通过Beta-Bernoulli平滑和单调校准映射聚合为观测向量。
- 提出结构化观测编码器，将默认响应语义证据与校准代理信号分解为状态条件兼容性分数，通过贝叶斯滤波更新信念。
- 设计预算感知风险策略：奖励=效用-成本-风险惩罚，约束为折扣计算预算，用actor-critic策略梯度+熵正则化在拉格朗日目标下优化。

## Key Contributions

- Formulates black-box LLM request-time inference control as a **POMDP** M=(S,A,Ω,T,O,R,γ) with binary latent state S={0,1} (reliable/unreliable default response), explicitly capturing partial observability and sequential budget coupling that prior routing methods (FrugalGPT, confidence-threshold) ignore.
- Constructs a lightweight **verifiable observation channel**: kh=4 hard verifiable checks (format/constraint, consistency/unit-test, answerability/groundedness, safety/compliance) with Beta-Bernoulli sliding-window smoothing, plus kg=2 soft proxy scores (self-consistency, external lightweight verifier) with monotone affine-sigmoid calibration — aggregated into observation vector zt ∈ (0,1)^k.
- Proposes a **structured observation encoder** that decomposes evidence into semantic evidence ℓy (from default response) and auxiliary reliability evidence ℓz (from calibrated proxies), enabling Bayesian belief updates that are more stable than any single noisy signal.
- Designs a **budget-aware risk-aware policy** with reward rt = V(yret) − C(at) − κR(bt), constrained by discounted computation budget E[Σγ^t C(at)] ≤ B, optimized via actor-critic policy gradient under a Lagrangian formulation with Shannon entropy regularization.
- Demonstrates empirically that VEROIC achieves superior quality-cost trade-offs, risk estimation/calibration, and long-horizon control robustness across 7 benchmarks and 2 model pairs.

## Methodology

**POMDP formulation:** At each step t, the system receives request xt, produces default low-cost response yt^(0) (smaller model or shorter reasoning budget), and must decide: accept default (at=0) or escalate to enhanced inference path (at=1, stronger model or larger reasoning budget). The latent state st ∈ {0,1} captures unobserved reliability of the default response. The belief state bt(s) = P(st=s | Ht) serves as the decision state.

**Observation construction:** Hard signals h̃t^(j) = 1{hj(xt, yt^(0))} are Boolean predicates (format validity, rule satisfaction, unit-test results), smoothed via sliding-window Beta-Bernoulli: zh,t^(j) = (α0j + Ξt^(j)) / (α0j + β0j + W), W=20. Soft signals g̃t^(i) = gi(xt, yt^(0)) are bounded scores (self-consistency, verifier confidence, retrieval support), calibrated via monotone affine-sigmoid Γi(g̃; ϑi) = σ(ai·g̃ + bi).

**Structured observation model:** The state-conditioned compatibility score ℓϕ(st; xt, yt^(0), zt, ψt) = ℓy(st; xt, yt^(0)) + ℓz(st; xt, yt^(0), zt, ψt), where ℓz decomposes into hard evidence ℓh and soft evidence Σℓg^(i). Observation likelihood O(ot|st,xt;ψt) ∝ exp(ℓϕ). Encoder parameters fitted using sparse verification feedback, delayed task-level supervision, or off-policy trajectories.

**Bayesian belief dynamics:** Predictive belief b̂t(st) = Σ T(st|st-1,xt-1,at-1)bt-1(st-1); posterior bt(st) = O(ot|st,xt;ψt)b̂t(st) / Σ O(ot|s',xt;ψt)b̂t(s').

**Budget-aware policy optimization:** Per-step reward rt = V(yt^ret) − C(at) − κR(bt), where R(bt) = Σ bt(s)ϱ(s) is belief-dependent risk. Constraint: E[Σγ^t C(at)] ≤ B (γ=0.99). Lagrangian objective with dual variable μd and entropy weight ρ=0.01, optimized via on-policy actor-critic (two-layer MLPs over belief-state + request features, Adam, η=0.05).

**Experimental setup:** 7 benchmarks — Math Reasoning (GSM8K, MATH), Code Generation (HumanEval, MBPP), QA (HotpotQA, 2WikiMHQA, PopQA). Two inference settings: (i) CMIS — LLaMA-3.1-8B→70B and Qwen-2.5-7B→32B; (ii) IMIS — intra-model scaling. Episode length H=500, 500 instances per episode. Enhanced budget α=0.05 (swept {0.01,0.02,0.05,0.10,0.20}). Enhanced cost C=0.20, risk penalty κ=1.0. 6 baselines: FIXED, RANDOM, PERIODIC, OUTPUT-TRIGGER, CONFIDENCE-THRESHOLD, RISK-PREDICTOR.

## Results & Highlights

- **Task utility (Table 1, CMIS, LLaMA pair):** VEROIC achieves best EM/Pass@1/F1 across all 7 benchmarks. GSM8K EM: VEROIC 92.4 vs RISK-PREDICTOR 91.2 vs LLaMA-8B 86.8 vs LLaMA-70B 88.9. HumanEval Pass@1: VEROIC 72.9 vs 72.3 vs 65.5 (8B) / 69.7 (70B). HotpotQA EM: VEROIC 56.2 vs 55.9 vs 51.6 (8B).
- **Risk estimation & calibration (Table 2, LLaMA pair):** VEROIC achieves best AUROC/AUPRC/Brier/NLL/ECE on all 3 benchmarks. GSM8K: AUROC 0.87 (vs 0.85 RISK-PREDICTOR, 0.71 FIXED), ECE 0.048 (vs 0.058, 0.085). HumanEval: AUROC 0.81, ECE 0.057 (vs 0.80/0.065).
- **Long-horizon control (Table 3, LLaMA pair):** VEROIC achieves lowest Occ/CVaR/RecD. GSM8K: Occ 0.13 (vs 0.17 RISK-PREDICTOR, 0.42 FIXED), CVaR 0.11 (vs 0.14, 0.38), RecD 77 (vs 98, 210). HumanEval: Occ 0.19, RecD 114 (vs 0.23/133). HotpotQA: Occ 0.22, RecD 128 (vs 0.27/158).
- **Qwen pair results (Table 1):** VEROIC consistently best: GSM8K EM 91.4, HumanEval Pass@1 71.2, PopQA EM 34.8 — all outperforming RISK-PREDICTOR and other baselines.
- **Ablation (Table 4):** Removing risk signals: GSM8K EM drops 92.4→91.2, Occ rises 0.13→0.23. Removing enhanced inference: EM drops to 88.7, Occ rises to 0.48, RecD to 239. Removing adaptive policy: EM 90.4, Occ 0.30. All components are necessary.
- **Budget sensitivity (Figure 2):** VEROIC consistently outperforms PERIODIC at every budget level α ∈ {0.01, 0.02, 0.05, 0.10, 0.20} across all 6 datasets, with the largest advantage in the low-budget regime where selective allocation matters most.

## Critical Assessment

- **Benchmark-based evaluation, not real deployment:** The authors explicitly acknowledge this limitation — experiments use benchmark-based sequential evaluation rather than real deployed black-box LLM services, so service-side non-stationarity and user-facing interaction dynamics are not captured. The POMDP's transition kernel T(st+1|st,xt,at) modeling "service-side non-stationarity" is a theoretical feature not empirically validated.
- **Binary latent state is coarse:** The latent state S={0,1} (reliable/unreliable) cannot capture finer-grained reliability structure (e.g., partially correct, mostly correct with one error). The authors acknowledge this but it limits applicability to tasks where reliability is not binary (open-ended generation, creative writing).
- **Observation signal quality is a strong dependency:** The framework's effectiveness depends heavily on the 6 observation signals (4 hard + 2 soft). The authors acknowledge that "weak or miscalibrated hard checks and proxy scores may lead to inaccurate belief estimates." The hard checks (format, unit-test, consistency) are task-specific and may not generalize to free-form generation tasks where no executable verifier exists.
- **Modest absolute gains over strongest baseline:** VEROIC's improvement over RISK-PREDICTOR (the strongest learned baseline using the same observation features) is consistently present but modest: GSM8K EM 92.4 vs 91.2 (+1.2pp), HumanEval 72.9 vs 72.3 (+0.6pp), HotpotQA EM 56.2 vs 55.9 (+0.3pp). The POMDP machinery yields small marginal gains over a supervised risk predictor with thresholding.
- **Additional modeling and training overhead:** The Bayesian belief update, structured observation encoder, and actor-critic policy introduce overhead relative to simple thresholding. The paper does not report wall-clock latency of the observation channel and policy evaluation, which the authors acknowledge as a practical concern.
- **IMIS (intra-model scaling) setting is underreported:** The main results focus on CMIS (cross-model switching); the IMIS setting (varying reasoning depth/decoding budget within the same model) is mentioned but not detailed in the results tables.

## Strengths

- Principled POMDP formulation that correctly models the sequential budget coupling problem — a genuine advance over myopic one-step routing.
- The verifiable observation channel design (hard predicates + soft proxies with calibration) is practically implementable in black-box API settings without model internals.
- Comprehensive evaluation across 7 benchmarks, 2 model pairs, 6 baselines, and 3 evaluation perspectives (task utility, risk calibration, long-horizon control).
- The belief-dependent risk term R(bt) and Lagrangian budget constraint provide a clean theoretical framework for risk-aware budgeted control.
- Honest ablation showing all three components (risk signals, enhanced inference, adaptive policy) are necessary.
- Budget-sensitivity analysis demonstrating VEROIC's advantage is largest in the low-budget regime where selective allocation matters most.

## Limitations

- **Benchmark-based, not real-deployment evaluation** — service-side non-stationarity untested.
- **Binary latent state** is coarse for non-verifiable generation tasks.
- **Observation signal quality dependency** — the 6 signals are task-specific and may not generalize to free-form generation.
- **Modest absolute gains** over the strongest learned baseline (RISK-PREDICTOR), raising questions about whether the POMDP overhead is justified.
- **Latency overhead** of observation channel and policy network not reported.
- **IMIS setting underreported** — only CMIS results are detailed.

## Relevance

本文直接对应"LLM Inference Evaluation"研究兴趣：VEROIC在推理时自适应分配计算资源，其信念状态和校准评估属于推理质量的不确定性量化。同时关联"ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations"——POMDP建模和贝叶斯信念更新为推理可靠性提供了数学基础，且条件风险价值(CVaR)和恢复延迟(RecD)等长程控制指标直接衡量系统可靠性。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How sensitive is VEROIC to the choice and quality of the 6 observation signals — would adding/removing specific hard checks (e.g., unit-test for non-code tasks) significantly degrade belief-state accuracy?
2. How does VEROIC compare to simpler adaptive strategies (multi-armed bandit, entropy-based thresholds) in terms of both quality and computational overhead, given the modest gains over RISK-PREDICTOR?
3. Can the binary latent state be extended to a multi-level or continuous reliability scale for tasks like open-ended generation where correctness is not binary?
4. How well does the belief-state calibration hold under distribution shift — when the task distribution at test time differs from the calibration/training distribution?
5. What is the wall-clock latency overhead of the observation channel and policy evaluation in production settings, and does it negate the cost savings from selective escalation?
6. Does the POMDP transition kernel T(st+1|st,xt,at) actually capture service-side non-stationarity in real deployments, and can the policy adapt online to drifting service quality?
