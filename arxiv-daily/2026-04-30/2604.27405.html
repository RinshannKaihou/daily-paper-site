Beyond the Mean: Within-Model Reliable Change Detection for LLM Evaluation

arXiv: 2604.27405v1

Date: 2026-04-30

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27405v1

一句话总结： 本文将临床心理学中的可靠变化指数（RCI）应用于LLM版本对比的题目级分析，发现表面上聚合准确率的小幅提升实际上是大量题目级双向变化（改善与退化）相互抵消后的净残余，而传统的贪心单次评估会遗漏42%的真实变化。
TL;DR: This paper adapts the Reliable Change Index (RCI) from clinical psychology to item-level LLM version comparison. Testing Llama 3→3.1 and Qwen 2.5→3 on 2,000 MMLU-Pro items, the authors find that aggregate accuracy gains are net residuals of large opposing item-level movements. Greedy single-shot evaluation misses 42% of reliably changed items and falsely flags 25% of unchanged items.

核心贡献

  • 将临床心理学中的可靠变化指数（RCI）首次应用于LLM版本对比的题目级评估，建立了区分真实变化与随机噪声的统计框架。
  • 揭示了聚合准确率提升的本质：在可分析的题目中，改善和退化以相近比例同时发生（如Llama改善34% vs 退化28%），聚合提升只是两个大 opposing movement 的微小净残余。
  • 发现模型退化的难度不对称性：低准确率题目倾向于改善，高准确率题目倾向于退化（回归中位数效应）。
  • 揭示了领域特异性退化模式：Llama 3.1在物理上退步而Qwen 3在法律上退步——不同模型家族的强项和弱项不同。
  • 证明贪心（T=0）单次评估会遗漏42%的真实变化题目、误标25%的无变化题目，建议使用K≥3次随机采样作为低成本替代方案。

Key Contributions

  • First adaptation of RCI to LLM evaluation: Bridges clinical psychology measurement theory and ML evaluation by treating repeated stochastic generations as measurement occasions and benchmark items as units of change.
  • Reveals bidirectional churn beneath aggregate gains: Among analysable items, change was bidirectional with large effect sizes (median |Δp| = 0.50 for Llama, 0.90 for Qwen), with improvements and deteriorations in comparable proportions.
  • Difficulty-asymmetric churn: Low-accuracy items predominantly improved while high-accuracy items predominantly deteriorated, consistent with regression toward the middle overlaid on domain-specific version effects.
  • Family-specific domain reversals: Llama 3.1 lost physics while Qwen 3 lost law. Users selecting a model based on aggregate improvement would be unaware of domain-specific regressions.
  • Greedy evaluation critique: Standard single-shot binary evaluation both over-detects noise-driven flips and under-detects genuine probability shifts. K = 3 samples suffice for reliability ≥ .90.

Methodology

  • Design: Two within-family model-version comparisons on 2,000 MMLU-Pro items (500 per domain: physics, law, psychology, economics). Pair 1: Llama-3-8B-Instruct vs. Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (minor update). Pair 2: Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct vs. Qwen3-8B non-thinking mode (generational update).
  • Sampling: Each item administered K = 10 times at T = 0.7 with independent seeds. Total: 80,000 trials. All models run at Q5_K_M quantization on AMD RX 7900 GRE via llama-cpp-python with Vulkan backend.
  • Reliability estimation: 1,000 random split-halves of K = 10 into two halves of 5, Spearman-Brown corrected. ICC (two-way random) as robustness check.
  • RCI computation: RCI(i) = (p(i, v2) − p(i, v1)) / S_diff. Reliable if |RCI| > 1.96 (p < .05). Items excluded if floor/ceiling (accuracy = 0.0 or 1.0 in both models).
  • Empirical null: 1,000 block-level permutations of version labels to calibrate chance-level reliable-change counts.
  • Pre-registered: All hypotheses and analyses pre-registered on OSF prior to data collection.

Results & Highlights

  • High reliability: Split-half reliability r_xx = .966–.996 across all models. K = 2 suffices for r_xx = .80, K = 3 for .90.
  • Full-benchmark churn: 21.3% (Llama) and 28.0% (Qwen) of items showed reliable change. Most items (79%/72%) showed no reliable change, dominated by floor/ceiling items.
  • Analysable subset churn: After excluding floor/ceiling items (52.4% for Llama, 67.4% for Qwen), churn rates were 62.1% (Llama, n = 952) and 85.9% (Qwen, n = 652).
  • Large effect sizes: Among reliably changed items, median |Δp| = 0.50 (Llama) and 0.90 (Qwen), representing 5–9 additional correct/incorrect answers out of 10 samples.
  • Domain-specific patterns (χ² p < .001 for both pairs): Llama 3.1 gained in economics, law, psychology but lost physics. Qwen 3 gained in economics, physics, psychology but lost law.
  • Greedy evaluation failure: 42.1% of RCI-reliably-changed items showed no change under greedy scoring; 25.2% of items greedy flagged as changed were classified as no reliable change by RCI.
  • Near-zero cross-pair item-level correlation (r = .11): The items that changed reliably for Llama are almost entirely different from those that changed for Qwen.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (High): The RCI framework has not previously been applied to LLM evaluation. The paper fills a genuine gap between aggregate statistical inference (Miller, 2024) and negative-flip engineering (Echterhoff et al., 2024).
  • Rigor (High): Pre-registered, 80,000 trials, empirical null calibration, split-half reliability with Spearman-Brown correction, multiple sensitivity analyses. The statistical framework is carefully adapted rather than mechanically transplanted.
  • Reproducibility (High): Public code, data, pre-registration, deterministic seeds, specified hardware and quantization. Every claim can be independently verified.
  • Significance (Moderate-High): The finding that aggregate gains conceal bidirectional churn is important for the field. The practical recommendation (K ≥ 3 samples) is low-cost and actionable. However, the generalizability concern is real — only two model pairs at 7–8B scale.
  • Red flags: The high exclusion rate (52–67%) means the churn finding is conditional on the analysable subset. The paper is transparent about this but it limits the strength of conclusions. Results are specific to Q5_K_M quantization and T = 0.7. The binomial data violates RCI's distributional assumptions; the authors acknowledge this and position RCI as a "computationally simple approximation."

Strengths

  • Clean, principled statistical framework with clear pre-registered hypotheses.
  • Reveals a non-obvious structural phenomenon (bidirectional churn) that aggregate metrics completely conceal.
  • Practical, low-cost recommendation (K ≥ 3) that is immediately actionable.
  • Excellent transparency: pre-registration, public code/data, honest discussion of limitations.
  • The domain-specific reversal analysis is particularly compelling — it demonstrates that regression toward the mean alone cannot explain the observed patterns.

Limitations

  • Generalizability: Only two model pairs at 7–8B parameter scale on one benchmark (MMLU-Pro). Whether findings extend to frontier models, other benchmarks, or different update magnitudes is unknown.
  • High exclusion rate: Over half the items were floor/ceiling and excluded. Results are conditional on the middle-difficulty subset.
  • Quantization dependence: All models run at Q5_K_M. Per-item accuracy stability across quantization formats is untested.
  • Temperature dependence: Results conditional on T = 0.7. Different temperatures would yield different reliability estimates and RCI classifications.
  • Distributional mismatch: RCI was designed for continuous scores; applying it to binomial proportions from K = 10 samples involves distributional approximations. A beta-binomial or hierarchical Bayesian model would be more principled.
  • Homoscedastic SEM assumption: A single S_diff per pair ignores heteroscedasticity across items of different difficulty.

Relevance

本文提出的方法论框架对LLM评估实践具有直接指导意义。题目级的可靠变化分析揭示了聚合指标无法捕捉的双向变化结构，这对于理解模型版本更新的真实影响至关重要。推荐的K≥3次采样方案成本低且可立即采用。未来可扩展至更大规模模型和更多基准测试。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How do churn patterns generalize to frontier-scale models (70B+, GPT-4-class) where floor/ceiling rates on MMLU-Pro would be lower?
  2. Would a hierarchical Bayesian model (rather than RCI) yield different item-level classifications, especially for items near the reliability detection boundary?
  3. How stable are domain-specific reversal patterns across consecutive version updates within the same model family?
  4. Can churn rate serve as a predictive signal for downstream task performance degradation in deployment?
  5. What is the minimal benchmark size needed for reliable item-level RCI analysis given the high floor/ceiling exclusion rates observed here?
