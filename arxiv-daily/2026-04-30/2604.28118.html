DEFault++: Automated Fault Detection, Categorization, and Diagnosis for Transformer Architectures

arXiv: 2604.28118

Date: 2026-04-30

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28118v1

一句话总结： DEFault++ 提出了一种基于分层学习的 Transformer 故障诊断技术，通过故障传播图（FPG）组织运行时组件级行为特征，在三层级联架构中实现故障检测（AUROC>0.96）、分类（12 类，Macro-F1 0.85）和根因定位（45 种机制，Macro-F1 0.85），并在 21 人开发者研究中将修复准确率从 57.1% 提升至 83.3%。
TL;DR: DEFault++ is a hierarchical learning-based diagnostic technique that detects, categorizes (12 categories), and pinpoints root causes (up to 45 mechanisms) of faults in transformer models using a Fault Propagation Graph and prototype matching with contrastive learning, achieving AUROC >0.96 for detection and Macro-F1 ~0.85 for categorization and root-cause diagnosis across encoder and decoder architectures.

核心贡献

  • 提出了 DEFault++，一种针对 Transformer 架构的三层级联诊断技术：Level 1 检测是否存在故障，Level 2 将故障分类到 12 个 Transformer 特定类别，Level 3 定位到具体根因（最多 45 种机制）。
  • 设计了 DEForm 变异测试技术，基于真实故障分类法定义 45 个变异算子，覆盖注意力机制内部及周围架构组件，构建了包含 3,739 个标注实例的 DEFault-bench 基准。
  • 提出了故障传播图（FPG），编码 Transformer 组件间的七种依赖传播机制（前向、残差旁路、梯度反传、跨层、缓存等），用于指导消息传递和解释。
  • 设计了结合监督对比学习和原型匹配的 Level 3 训练目标，通过可分解的组级距离提供可解释的诊断依据。
  • 在 21 位开发者的用户研究中，使用 DEFault++ 辅助后修复操作准确率从 57.1% 提升至 83.3%。

Key Contributions

  • DEFault++, a three-level hierarchical diagnostic technique for transformer models: fault detection, fault categorization into 12 transformer-specific categories, and root-cause diagnosis across up to 45 mechanisms.
  • DEForm, a transformer-specific mutation technique with 45 operators derived from real-world fault taxonomies, producing 3,739 labeled instances across 7 models and 9 tasks.
  • Fault Propagation Graph (FPG), encoding seven dependency mechanisms (forward, residual bypass, simultaneous, cross-layer, backward gradient, architectural intervention, cache-time) to structure message passing and group-level explanations.
  • A four-term training objective combining detection cross-entropy, categorization cross-entropy, supervised contrastive learning, and prototype matching for within-category root-cause separation.
  • A developer study (N=21) showing repair-action accuracy improved from 57.1% (baseline) to 83.3% (DEFault++ assisted), with no participant performing worse with the tool.

Methodology

DEFault++ operates in three hierarchical levels sharing a single encoder:

  1. Feature extraction. During fine-tuning, DEFault++ collects transformer-specific runtime metrics at four granularities: layer-internal (attention entropy, QKV alignment, score statistics, FFN norms, LayerNorm parameters, residual cosine similarity, CKA drift), gradient-level (per-component gradient norms, update ratios, update activity), behavioral (embedding norms, positional sensitivity, loss trajectory, output confidence, cache metrics for decoders), and validation (task accuracy/perplexity, calibration error). Per-layer metrics are aggregated via early/mid/final statistics, then epoch-level and training-phase summaries produce a fixed-length feature vector (~1600 dims for encoders, ~1705 for decoders).
  1. Fault Propagation Graph (FPG). A directed graph where nodes are transformer components and edges encode structural dependencies (forward propagation M1, simultaneous M2, residual bypass M3, cross-layer M4, backward gradient M5, architectural intervention M6, cache-time M7). The FPG is collapsed to a group-level adjacency matrix for message passing.
  1. Group encoding + message passing. Each feature group is encoded by a dedicated MLP (h=32). Three rounds of GCN-style message passing over the FPG aggregate cross-component propagation information. A shared projection layer (e=64) feeds Levels 1 and 2 classifiers.
  1. Hierarchical classification. Level 1: binary MLP for fault detection. Level 2: C-class MLP for categorization (11 encoder / 12 decoder categories). Level 3: prototype matching in group embedding space, where each root cause has a class-mean prototype; the nearest prototype wins, and distances decompose by group for explanation.
  1. Training. Four-term loss: L_detect (binary CE) + α·L_cat (class-weighted CE) + λ·L_rc (per-category CE) + L_sep (β·contrastive + γ·prototype matching). Joint training with hard parameter sharing across all levels. Nested grouped 5-fold cross-validation holding out entire model-task pairs prevents leakage.
  1. Benchmark construction. DEForm injects single faults via static (parameter mutation) and dynamic (forward-path wrapping) mechanisms. Paired clean/faulty runs under 5 matched seeds. One-sided sign-flip permutation test (α=0.05) determines if a mutant is "killed." Validated mutants form DEFault-bench.

Results & Highlights

  • Level 1 (Detection): AUROC 0.966 (encoder) / 0.962 (decoder). Macro-F1 0.824 (enc) / 0.909 (dec).
  • Level 2 (Categorization): Macro-F1 0.850 (enc) / 0.868 (dec). Strongest categories: Residual (F1 0.961/0.995), Positional (0.919/0.927), Masking (0.917/0.890). Weakest: Variant (0.741 enc), FFN (0.718 dec).
  • Level 3 (Root-cause): Macro-F1 0.851 (enc) / 0.867 (dec). Macro-precision >0.94 on both architectures, indicating conservative but accurate root-cause predictions.
  • Baselines: DEFault++ outperforms AutoTrainer (M-F1 0.284/0.427), DeepDiagnosis (0.338/0.482), DeepFD (0.514/0.655), and DEFault (0.566/0.702) on Level 1 detection.
  • Ablation: Removing FPG message passing drops Level 2 Macro-F1 by 0.108 (enc) / 0.080 (dec). Removing separation loss drops Level 3 Macro-F1 by 0.095 (enc) / 0.086 (dec). FPG topology matters: full FPG > rewired > random > no FPG.
  • Real-world faults: Detected 8/11 reproduced GitHub bugs, categorized 8 correctly, diagnosed root cause for 4.
  • Developer study (N=21): Repair accuracy 57.1% → 83.3% (+26.2%). No participant performed worse with DEFault++. 95% found output easy to understand, 90% found it helpful.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: High. The three-level hierarchical diagnosis for transformer-specific faults is novel, as is the FPG-based structural induction into the diagnostic model. The combination of prototype matching with contrastive learning for root-cause separation within fault categories is technically creative. However, the approach extends prior work (DEFault, DeepCrime) rather than being entirely new.
  • Rigor: Strong. Nested grouped cross-validation with model-task pair holdout prevents leakage. Ablation study is thorough (FPG removal, topology variants, separation loss). Statistical validation uses exact permutation tests. The developer study uses a within-subjects counterbalanced design.
  • Reproducibility: Good. Replication package available. All models use standard HuggingFace implementations. Hyperparameters reported. However, the benchmark requires ~18,600 GPU-hours (A100/H100), which is expensive. The permissive SSL context in data fetching is a minor concern.
  • Significance: High for the software engineering and ML reliability communities. Transformer debugging is a practical problem with growing importance as models scale. The developer study provides human-centric validation beyond benchmarks.
  • Red flags: (1) Benchmark uses only single-fault configurations; real-world faults may co-occur, and the authors acknowledge this limitation. (2) Models are limited to 66M–125M parameters; generalization to large-scale models (LLMs with billions of parameters, MoE architectures) is untested. (3) The developer study has N=21 and uses vignette-based scenarios rather than live debugging. (4) Only 4/11 real-world faults receive correct root-cause diagnosis, suggesting room for improvement on naturally occurring bugs. (5) QKV category remains the hardest (F1 0.646–0.765) due to overlapping feature patterns among root causes.

Strengths

  • Comprehensive three-level diagnostic hierarchy with principled design grounded in transformer architecture.
  • Fault Propagation Graph provides both structural inductive bias and interpretable group-level explanations.
  • DEFault-bench is the first large-scale, transformer-specific fault benchmark with systematic mutation testing and statistical validation.
  • Strong empirical results across encoder and decoder architectures with consistent performance.
  • Meaningful developer study demonstrating practical utility beyond benchmark numbers.
  • Thorough ablation study isolating the contribution of each architectural component.

Limitations

  • Single-fault assumption; does not handle co-occurring or compound faults.
  • Tested only on models up to 125M parameters; scalability to LLMs (billions of parameters, mixture-of-experts, multimodal) is unknown.
  • FPG assumes standard transformer block structure; architecture-specific shortcuts (e.g., tied input-output embeddings) are not modeled.
  • KV Cache root-cause diagnosis is weak on decoders (lowest mutation score at 5.6%).
  • Developer study is small (N=21) and vignette-based, not a live debugging environment.
  • Feature extraction requires a complete fine-tuning run; cannot diagnose faults in pre-trained models without retraining.

Relevance

DEFault++ 直接面向 ML 可靠性和故障检测这一核心研究方向，提出了针对 Transformer 架构的系统性故障诊断框架。其故障传播图（FPG）思想和基于原型匹配的可解释诊断方法对构建更可靠的深度学习系统具有重要参考价值，与 ML 可靠性、模型调试、软件工程质量保障等研究方向高度契合。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does DEFault++ scale to models with billions of parameters (e.g., LLaMA, GPT-4-class), where per-component instrumentation becomes prohibitively expensive?
  2. Can the FPG be extended to handle multi-modal transformers with cross-attention, mixture-of-experts routing, or retrieval-augmented generation components?
  3. How would DEFault++ perform under compound (multi-fault) scenarios, and what modifications would be needed for multi-label diagnosis?
  4. Is it possible to adapt the diagnostic approach to detect faults at inference time (without retraining), for example through probing or activation patching?
  5. Could the prototype-based explanation framework be combined with causal intervention methods to move from internal faithfulness to true causal attribution?
