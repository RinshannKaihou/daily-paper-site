> **一句话总结：** Strait 是一个面向双优先级推理流量的 ML 推理服务系统，通过对 PCIe 数据传输竞争建模并使用基于 Adam 在线校准的自适应内核干扰指数预测模型，在 4×NVIDIA L4 节点上将高优先级任务截止期限违规降低 1.02–11.18 个百分点，且相比 XSched 等软件定义抢占方案对低优先级任务更公平。

> **TL;DR:** On a 4-GPU NVIDIA L4 node serving 6 DNN models (ResNet-50, ViT-B-16, ConvNeXt-B, VGG-19, YOLO-v8n, RoBERTa-B) under Poisson/uniform/production-trace workloads, Strait reduces high-priority deadline violations by 1.02–11.18 pp versus temporal/static/reactive spatial-sharing baselines, and versus the XSched kernel-level preemption baseline cuts low-priority violations from 29.89% to 1.20% (HP-pressure) and 11.41% to 4.27% (LP-pressure), at a worst-case LP cost of 0.61 pp.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **Strait** 系统，针对高 GPU 利用率下双优先级（高/低）推理流量的截止期限满足问题，在不支持真正任务级抢占的商品 GPU 上实现优先级感知调度。
- 建模 PCIe 数据传输竞争：基于 pinned memory 的 FIFO 特性推导出 `T_data` 延迟估计与 `t_available` 更新公式（Equation 2/3），并引入在线校准补偿 NUMA / PCIe Root Complex 偏差。
- 提出自适应内核执行干扰预测模型：将干扰建模为资源压力的指数函数 `kerneleff = k·b^LR(...) + C`（Equation 4），通过 Adam 在线学习（β1=0.7, β2=0.9, lr=0.0075, Huber loss δ=0.50）持续校准，克服静态模型在模型集/GPU 架构漂移下的精度退化。
- 设计优先级感知调度算法（Algorithm 1）：二分搜索最大可行 batch size，约束条件包括 LP 任务聚合资源吞吐 AIMD 上限 `C_low`（75%–100%，每 100ms 增 0.25%）与对进行中同等/更高优先级任务截止期限的违反检查，调度开销为 `n·O(log j)`。
- 在生产 serverless traces 上验证可持续性：5 小时 busiest 窗口内 goodput 与负载基本重叠，10,000-batch 窗口的中位预测误差始终接近 0%。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces **Strait**, a serving system targeting deadline satisfaction for dual-priority inference traffic under intense GPU utilization on non-preemptive commodity GPUs.
- Models **data-transfer contention** analytically from the FIFO behavior of PCIe with pinned memory (Equations 2–3), plus an online calibration step that compensates NUMA / PCIe Root Complex skew.
- Proposes an **adaptive kernel-interference prediction model** that frames interference as exponential growth in resource pressure (Equation 4), with a learnable priority coefficient (Equation 5) and online Adam-based recalibration (β1=0.7, β2=0.9, α=0.0075, Huber δ=0.50).
- Designs a **priority-aware scheduler** (Algorithm 1) using binary search over batch sizes with an AIMD-capped LP aggregate-throughput constraint `C_low` (75%–100%, +0.25%/100 ms) and explicit deadline-feasibility checks for ongoing equal/higher-priority batches.
- Validates **sustainability on production traces**: goodput tracks load over a 5-hour busiest window and the 10,000-batch median prediction error stays near 0%.

## Methodology

Strait targets on-premises inference serving where commodity GPUs lack true task-level preemption and inference runs as a compiled computation graph (TensorRT). Each model supports two priority levels (high/low); HP tasks use CUDA stream priorities and priority scheduling, while LP tasks are best-effort and bounded by the GPU-specific CUDA stream priority range.

**Latency estimation** (Equation 1) decomposes inference latency into profiled p95 isolated latency plus three delay terms: `T_data` (data-transfer interference), `T_kernel` (kernel-execution interference), and `T_queue` (queuing delay measured at runtime). `T_data` is computed from the FIFO PCIe link availability timestamp (Equations 2–3). `T_kernel` applies a slowdown factor `Intf_hat` (Equation 5) to the profiled p95 isolated kernel latency; the slowdown is driven by `kerneleff = k·b^LR(...) + C` (Equation 4), an exponential of a linear-regression resource-pressure term combining time-weighted average throughputs of co-located batches (`m1avg…mnavg` across L1/L2/DRAM/tensor cores/CUDA FMA), the interfered batch's own compute (`m_self^cmp`, tensor cores) and memory (`m_self^mem`, L2) throughput, and a learnable priority coefficient `coeff_p`.

**Adaptive model**: Upon each batch completion, the estimated vs. actual interference degree (ratio of measured to profiled p95) is fed back; the regression is updated with Adam (β1=0.7, β2=0.9, lr=0.0075) under Huber loss (δ=0.50, matching the p95 absolute error on captured traces). Parameters can be warmed up from simulated workload or restored from a prior checkpoint.

**Scheduling** (Algorithm 1): task queues are processed in descending priority; early-dropping discards requests that cannot meet deadlines even in isolation; binary search finds the largest batch size that (a) does not push LP aggregate throughput above `C_low` (AIMD, 75%–100%, +0.25%/100 ms, reset on HP violation) and (b) does not violate deadlines of ongoing equal/higher-priority batches; the GPU yielding the lowest estimated latency is selected. `meet()` conservatively requires the scheduled batch to potentially meet its deadline under half of the GPU's runtime throughput.

**Hardware/setup**: Primary evaluation on a 4-GPU node (4× NVIDIA L4-24GB, 32 vCPUs, 192 GB DRAM) and a single-GPU node (1× L4-24GB, 8 vCPUs, 48 GB DRAM); GPU frequency fixed at 1650 MHz; Ubuntu 22.04, CUDA 12, TensorRT 10. Cross-architecture tests on NVIDIA A10G (Ampere) and H100 (Hopper). Prototype is ~5,473 lines of C++.

**Models** (Table 1): ResNet-50 (CNN, 4.09 GFLOPs, HP, 8 ms deadline), ViT-B-16 (Transformer, 16.85 GFLOPs, HP, 15 ms), ConvNeXt-B (CNN, 15.35 GFLOPs, LP, 25 ms), VGG-19 (CNN, 19.63 GFLOPs, LP, 25 ms), YOLO-v8n (CNN, 9.57 GFLOPs, LP, 20 ms), RoBERTa-B (Transformer, 10.87 GFLOPs, LP, 45 ms). Max batch size 8 per model, batch concurrency limit 4 per GPU; for heterogeneity each model is duplicated to 12 tasks.

**Workloads**: Poisson (stochastic), uniform, and production serverless traces (Azure, busiest 5-hour window, 6 most frequent functions mapped to the six models, arrivals generated via Poisson parameterized by per-minute rates). Open model setting.

**Baselines**: (1) Temporal sharing (sequential, FCFS within each queue); (2) Static spatial sharing (concurrency cap 3 per GPU); (3) Reactive spatial sharing (concurrency allowance reduced by 1 on HP miss, lower bound 1, reset to 3 every 200 ms); (4) XSched (kernel-level software-defined preemption, fixed-priority on abstract queues, batching disabled, Triton server).

## Results & Highlights

- **HP deadline violations reduced 1.02–11.18 pp** across stochastic (frequent-HP / frequent-LP) and uniform workloads versus temporal, static, and reactive spatial-sharing baselines on the 4-GPU L4 node; LP tasks underperform the strongest LP baseline by at most **0.61 pp** in the worst case (Section 5.2).
- **Versus XSched (kernel-level preemption)** on the single-GPU L4 node: under HP pressure, Strait incurs **1.43% HP / 1.20% LP** violations versus XSched's **2.07% HP / 29.89% LP**; under LP pressure, Strait incurs **0.69% HP / 4.27% LP** versus XSched's **0.043% HP / 11.41% LP** — i.e., comparable HP satisfaction while cutting LP violations by ~28.7 pp and ~7.1 pp respectively (Figure 6, Section 5.3).
- **Prediction accuracy under intense loads** (Figure 7a): kernel-execution latency absolute relative error **29.6% at p95 / 46.6% at p99**; overall latency prediction error reduced to **18.95% p95 / 27.9% p99** thanks to offline profiling and online calibration. Kernel-execution overhead distribution has a long tail reaching ~**682%** with p99 of **245%**.
- **Adaptive vs static model** (Figure 7b): ~**3 pp** p99 improvement under bursty traffic; **7 pp** p95 improvement when swapping to a Transformer-dominated model set; **>7.2 pp** (Ampere A10G) and **>8 pp** (Hopper H100) p99 reductions when porting the model across GPU architectures.
- **Sustainability on production traces** (Figure 7c): over a 5-hour busiest window, goodput generally overlaps with incoming load, absorbing moderate spikes (e.g., after 40 and 280 mins); median prediction error stays near 0% across 10,000-batch update windows; Adam + Huber loss prevent catastrophic updates during oversubscription.
- **Profiling-drift robustness**: with ±15% random perturbation on profiled throughput values, p99 prediction drift stays below **1.5 pp** (Appendix A.2).
- **Scheduling overhead**: complexity `n·O(log j)` (n GPUs, max batch size j), bounded by tens of microseconds in practice (Section 5.5).
- **LP violation cause analysis**: ~two-thirds of LP deadline-violating requests result from early dropping rather than wasted computation, consistent with the best-effort treatment (Section 5.2).
- **Reactive baseline limitation**: when HP requests dominate, reactive spatial sharing's HP improvement is only **0.4 pp** (versus up to 10.02 pp in other scenarios), because it throttles LP tasks that are already infrequent (Section 5.2).

## Critical Assessment

- **Dual-priority only**: The system supports exactly two priority levels (HP/LP), bounded by the GPU-specific CUDA stream priority range; the paper acknowledges extensibility to multiple levels but never demonstrates it, so generalization to weighted or continuous priorities is unvalidated.
- **Long-tail interference underestimation**: The adaptive model is constrained by the underlying data distribution; due to data sparsity it systematically underestimates severe long-tail interference (overhead reaching ~682%, p99 245%), while overestimating interference at small overhead (<60%). The overall-latency prediction partially masks this, but the kernel-level error (46.6% p99) remains high.
- **Time-weighted-average approximation**: `m_i^avg` uses a time-weighted average of co-located throughputs, which the paper itself concedes "may be limited if a batch's resource demands vary significantly during execution" — a known modeling weakness not ablated.
- **LP task sacrifice is structural**: HP tasks are scheduled even if they cause ongoing LP tasks to miss deadlines, and LP tasks are blocked if they risk HP deadlines; ~two-thirds of LP violations come from early dropping. The 0.61 pp worst-case LP cost is reported as "acceptable" but is only measured against the strongest LP baseline, not in absolute goodput terms.
- **On-premises scope, cloud/multi-tenant unaddressed**: The design explicitly targets on-premises servers; behavior under multi-tenant cloud contention, noisy neighbors, or cluster-level autoscaling is not studied (the paper calls this "orthogonal" rather than evaluating it).
- **No public code or artifact**: The PDF does not reference a code release or reproducibility artifact, limiting independent verification.
- **Static baseline weakens comparison**: The gpulets-based static model is a linear-regression predictor using only L2/DRAM/SM throughput; the adaptive model's gains over it are partly attributable to richer features (tensor cores, L2, time-weighting) rather than to online learning per se, and this confound is not isolated.

## Strengths

- tackles a genuinely hard problem — priority differentiation on non-preemptive commodity GPUs where inference runs as an uninterrupted compiled graph;
- interference model is grounded in concrete GPU microarchitecture (registers, shared memory, warp scheduling, PCIe FIFO) rather than a black-box ML predictor;
- online Adam + Huber calibration is a principled choice for concept drift, with empirically tuned hyperparameters justified against SGD/momentum/Adagrad/AMSGrad/RMSProp alternatives;
- evaluation spans Poisson, uniform, and real Azure production traces across three GPU architectures (L4, A10G, H100);
- honest reporting of failure modes (long-tail underestimation, LP early-dropping, reactive baseline's 0.4 pp marginal case).

## Limitations

- Restricted to **dual-priority** traffic; multi-priority or weighted schemes are future work only.
- Scope limited to **on-premises**, single-node serving; cloud, multi-tenant, and cluster-level scheduling are not evaluated.
- Kernel-level prediction error remains high (**46.6% p99**) under intense loads, with systematic underestimation of the long tail.
- **No public code or artifact** referenced in the PDF.
- `C_low` AIMD parameters (75%–100%, +0.25%/100 ms) are tuned via a sensitivity study (Appendix A.1) but their robustness under drastically different model mixes is not shown.
- Best results rely on profiling at a fixed GPU frequency (1650 MHz); DVFS or frequency scaling effects are not addressed.

## Relevance

与「LLM Inference Evaluation」和「Computation Reliability & Precision Errors」两个研究方向相关：Strait 关注的虽是经典 DNN 推理服务而非 LLM 专属，但其对 GPU 并发内核执行干扰的建模、PCIe 数据传输竞争的量化、以及在线自适应延迟预测，直接提供了在高利用率 GPU 上保障推理截止期限与可观测性的系统级方法，对评估推理系统可靠性与资源调度的精度误差有参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the exponential interference model (Equation 4) be extended to LLM decoding workloads (autoregressive, memory-bandwidth-bound, paged KV cache) where kernel mix and co-location dynamics differ sharply from CNN/Transformer classification?
- How does the adaptive predictor behave under GPU frequency scaling (DVFS) or turbo modes, given the fixed-1650 MHz profiling assumption?
- What is the upper bound on the number of priority levels before the CUDA stream priority range or the AIMD `C_low` mechanism becomes infeasible?
- Could the long-tail underestimation be mitigated by a mixture-of-experts predictor (one for normal regime, one for oversubscription) rather than a single global Adam-calibrated regression?
- How much of the adaptive-over-static gain is attributable to richer input features (tensor cores, L2, time-weighting) versus the online learning itself, and is there an ablation isolating the two?
