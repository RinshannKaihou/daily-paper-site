Belief-Guided Inference Control for Large Language Model Services via Verifiable Observations

核心贡献

  • 将黑盒 LLM 服务的请求时推理控制形式化为部分可观测马尔可夫决策过程（POMDP），显式建模部分可观测性与序列预算耦合问题。
  • 设计了一个轻量级可验证观测通道，从输入-输出对中聚合异构质量信号，构建对潜在响应可靠性的信念状态（belief state）。
  • 提出预算感知策略（budget-aware policy），基于信念状态决定返回默认低代价输出或触发高代价推理路径（如更强模型/更多采样）。
  • 在多种任务上验证了 VEROIC 在质量-成本权衡、风险估计与校准、长期推理控制鲁棒性方面优于竞争基线。

Key Contributions

  • Formulates request-time inference control for black-box LLM services as a POMDP, explicitly capturing partial observability and sequential budget coupling.
  • Constructs a lightweight verifiable observation channel that aggregates heterogeneous quality signals from input-output pairs into a belief state over latent response reliability.
  • Proposes a budget-aware policy that uses the belief state to decide whether to return the default low-cost output or trigger a higher-cost inference pathway.
  • Demonstrates empirically that VEROIC achieves superior quality-cost trade-offs, risk estimation and calibration, and robust long-horizon inference control compared to competitive baselines.

Methodology

The VEROIC framework operates in three stages:

  1. Default inference: A low-cost LLM generates an initial response for each incoming request.
  2. Verifiable observation: The input-output pair is processed through a lightweight observation channel that extracts heterogeneous quality signals (e.g., consistency checks, confidence scores, external verification tools). These signals are aggregated into a belief state — a probability distribution over the latent reliability of the response.
  3. Budget-aware policy: A policy network takes the current belief state and remaining budget as input and decides whether to (a) return the default output or (b) escalate to a higher-cost inference pathway (e.g., a stronger model, chain-of-thought prompting, or multi-sample voting). The decision is framed as a sequential action under a global compute budget, modeled via POMDP transitions.

The POMDP formulation naturally handles the sequential coupling: spending budget on one request reduces available budget for future requests, requiring the policy to balance immediate and long-term quality.

Results & Highlights

  • VEROIC achieves improved quality-cost trade-offs across diverse tasks compared to competitive baselines (e.g., fixed-threshold methods, uniform allocation).
  • Stronger risk estimation and calibration — the belief state provides well-calibrated probability estimates of response correctness.
  • More robust long-horizon inference control — the budget-aware policy effectively distributes compute across a sequence of requests rather than myopically optimizing individual queries.

The verifiable observation channel is a meaningful contribution, though the specific quality signals used are not detailed in the abstract. The overall framework combines known ingredients (belief states, budget policies) in a sensible way.
  • Rigor (Moderate–High): The POMDP formalization is sound. However, without access to the full paper, it is difficult to assess the quality of the experimental evaluation — e.g., which baselines were compared, what tasks were used, and how significant the improvements are.
  • Reproducibility (Unclear): The framework depends on the specific observation channel design and policy network architecture, which cannot be evaluated from the abstract alone. Code release would be critical for reproducibility.
  • Significance (Moderate–High): Adaptive inference control for black-box LLM services is a practically important problem with clear cost-saving implications for production deployments. If the results hold across diverse tasks, this could influence how LLM APIs are consumed at scale.
  • Red flags: The abstract claims improvements on "diverse tasks" without specifying the number or nature of tasks. The term "competitive baselines" is vague. The actual magnitude of improvement over simpler heuristics (e.g., output-length-based thresholds) remains unclear.

Strengths

  • Principled POMDP formulation that correctly models the sequential budget coupling problem.
  • Practical focus on black-box settings, which is the dominant mode of LLM API consumption.
  • The verifiable observation channel idea — extracting quality signals without access to model internals — is clever and practically useful.
  • Budget-aware policy that considers long-horizon effects rather than per-request myopic decisions.

Limitations

  • The observation channel's effectiveness likely depends heavily on the choice and quality of the heterogeneous signals; poorly chosen signals could degrade belief-state accuracy.
  • The approach introduces additional latency for the observation and policy evaluation steps, which may be problematic in latency-sensitive applications.
  • Scalability to very large request volumes and the computational overhead of maintaining belief states are not discussed in the abstract.
  • The framework assumes a two-tier cost structure (low-cost default vs. high-cost escalation), which may not capture the full spectrum of inference strategies available in practice.
