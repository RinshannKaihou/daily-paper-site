Latent Adversarial Detection: Adaptive Probing of LLM Activations for Multi-Turn Attack Detection

arXiv: 2604.28129v1

Date: 2026-04-30

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.28129v1

⚠️ Abstract-only summary
一句话总结： 本文发现多轮提示注入攻击在LLM残差流中留下"对抗性不安"的激活轨迹特征，利用五个标量轨迹特征可将对话级检测准确率从76.2%提升至93.8%，为基于激活层的多轮攻击检测提供了可靠信号。
TL;DR: Multi-turn prompt injection attacks leave a measurable activation-level signature ("adversarial restlessness") in LLM residual streams. Five scalar trajectory features capturing this signal boost conversation-level detection from 76.2% to 93.8%, with combined-source training achieving 89.4% detection at 2.4% FPR.

核心贡献

  • 提出并验证了"对抗性不安"（adversarial restlessness）概念：多轮攻击的每个阶段转换都会在残差流中产生激活偏移，导致总路径长度远超正常对话。
  • 设计了五个标量轨迹特征，从激活向量序列中捕捉攻击信号，将对话级检测准确率从76.2%提升至93.8%。
  • 在四个模型家族（24B-70B参数）上验证了信号的复现性，同时发现探针是模型特定的，无法跨架构迁移。
  • 系统评估了数据源的泛化性：合成数据、LMSYS-Chat-1M和SafeDialBench各自捕捉不同的攻击分布。
  • 证明了三阶段轮次级标签（benign/pivoting/adversarial）的必要性：仅使用二进制对话级标签会导致50-59%的误报率。

Key Contributions

  • Identifies adversarial restlessness as a novel activation-level signal: multi-turn attacks follow a trust-building → pivoting → escalation path, and each phase shift moves activations in the residual stream, producing trajectory lengths far exceeding benign conversations.
  • Introduces five scalar trajectory features that capture this signal from residual-stream activation sequences, lifting detection from 76.2% to 93.8% on synthetic held-out data.
  • Demonstrates the signal replicates across four model families (24B–70B parameters), though probes are model-specific and do not transfer across architectures.
  • Conducts systematic leave-one-source-out generalization analysis across synthetic, LMSYS-Chat-1M, and SafeDialBench data, showing each source captures distinct attack distributions.
  • Shows that three-phase turn-level labels (benign/pivoting/adversarial) are essential for low false positive rates; binary conversation-level labels yield 50–59% FPR.
  • Achieves 89.4% detection at 2.4% FPR with combined three-source training on a held-out mixed evaluation set.

Methodology

The paper operates at the activation level of LLMs rather than the text level. The approach extracts residual-stream activations at a specific layer for each turn in a conversation, then computes five scalar trajectory features that quantify how "restless" the activation path is across turns. These features capture properties such as total path length, direction changes, and acceleration of the activation trajectory.

The author constructs a synthetic multi-turn attack dataset with fine-grained three-phase turn-level labels (benign, pivoting, adversarial), which enables training probes to detect not just the overall conversation but specific phase transitions. Training data is drawn from three sources: synthetic attacks, LMSYS-Chat-1M (real-world conversations), and SafeDialBench (dialogue safety benchmark). A leave-one-source-out protocol evaluates cross-distribution generalization.

Experiments span four model families ranging from 24B to 70B parameters, testing whether the adversarial restlessness signal is universal or model-specific. Classification uses lightweight probes (likely linear or shallow) on the trajectory features.

Results & Highlights

  • Baseline to proposed: Text-level or simple activation methods achieve ~76.2% detection; the five trajectory features lift this to 93.8% on synthetic held-out data.
  • Cross-model replication: The adversarial restlessness signal appears consistently across four model families (24B–70B), but trained probes do not transfer across architectures.
  • Source-dependent generalization: Each data source (synthetic, LMSYS, SafeDialBench) captures distinct attack distributions. Real-world LMSYS detection reaches 47–71% only when its distribution is represented in training.
  • Combined training: Three-source training achieves 89.4% detection at 2.4% FPR on a held-out mixed set — the strongest practical result.
  • Label granularity matters critically: Binary conversation-level labels produce 50–59% FPR; three-phase turn-level labels are essential for reliable detection.
  • Adversarial restlessness is quantifiable: Total activation path length for adversarial conversations significantly exceeds that of benign ones, providing a clear statistical signal.

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty: The concept of "adversarial restlessness" as an activation-level trajectory signal for multi-turn attacks is a genuine contribution. While prior work has examined activations for single-turn safety, applying trajectory analysis to multi-turn attack phases is novel.
  • Rigor: The leave-one-source-out evaluation and ablation on label granularity demonstrate methodological rigor. However, the paper is single-authored, which raises questions about the scale of the experimental infrastructure and potential for undetected errors.
  • Reproducibility: The synthetic dataset construction, feature extraction procedure, and model choices are described at a high level in the abstract. Full reproducibility would require access to the code, dataset, and specific layer/feature details not available from the abstract alone.
  • Significance: The practical deployment gap is honestly characterized — probes don't transfer across architectures and real-world detection depends on training distribution coverage. The 89.4% / 2.4% FPR result on mixed data is encouraging but may not hold against adaptive adversaries who optimize against the trajectory features.
  • Red flags: The single-author status and abstract-only review limit assessment of experimental depth. The claim that five scalar features suffice for high detection should be scrutinized against adaptive attacks designed to minimize activation trajectory length.

Strengths

  • Novel and intuitive framing of multi-turn attacks through activation trajectory analysis ("adversarial restlessness").
  • Strong empirical gains: 76.2% → 93.8% detection on synthetic data.
  • Honest characterization of limitations — model-specific probes, source-dependent generalization, and the necessity of fine-grained labels.
  • Systematic evaluation across multiple data sources and model families.
  • Clear practical guidance: combined training and turn-level labels are essential for deployment.

Limitations

  • No cross-architecture transfer: Probes must be trained per-model, increasing deployment cost for multi-model systems.
  • Distribution sensitivity: Real-world detection drops to 47–71% when the target distribution is not represented in training, limiting zero-shot applicability.
  • Abstract-only review constraint: Full methodology details (which layers, which features exactly, probe architecture, attack datasets used for evaluation) cannot be assessed.
  • Adaptive adversary concern: Attackers aware of trajectory-based detection could potentially craft attacks that minimize activation path length, which is not evaluated.
  • Single author: Limits the breadth of expertise applied and the scale of experimental validation.
