Geometry-Calibrated Conformal Abstention for Language Models

arXiv: 2604.27914

Date: 2026-04-30

https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27914v1

一句话总结： 本文提出了一种基于共形预测的事后框架（Conformal Abstention），利用 Transformer 内部表示的几何信号（MLP 知识贡献、嵌入旋转、各向异性对齐）来校准预测置信度，使语言模型能够在不确定时选择弃权，实现 75% 的条件正确率并具备有限样本理论保证。
TL;DR: A post-hoc conformal abstention framework that leverages representation geometry signals (MLP knowledge contribution, embedding rotation, anisotropy alignment) to calibrate prediction confidence, enabling LLMs to selectively abstain from answering when uncertain, achieving 75% conditional correctness with finite-sample guarantees.

核心贡献

  • 提出了 Conformal Abstention (CA) 框架，将共形预测适配到语言模型的弃权决策中，无需重训练模型
  • 提供了两个有限样本理论保证：参与保证（模型不弃权的概率有下界）和条件正确性保证（模型回答时答案正确的概率有下界）
  • 设计了基于 表示几何 的置信度校准策略，通过三个互补信号衡量内部知识对生成过程的影响：
  • 邻近度 ω：MLP 更新对后 MLP 嵌入的贡献程度
  • 嵌入旋转 θ：MLP 更新引起的预测方向变化幅度
  • 各向异性对齐 φ：嵌入与分布内主导方向的一致性
  • 在 6 个数据集、3 个模型上全面超越 8 种基线方法

Key Contributions

  • Introduces Conformal Abstention (CA), a post-hoc framework adapting conformal prediction for LLM abstention decisions without model retraining
  • Proves two finite-sample theoretical guarantees: a participation guarantee bounding the probability of non-abstention, and a conditional correctness guarantee bounding the probability that a retained answer is correct
  • Develops a representation geometry calibration strategy with three complementary signals quantifying how internal knowledge shapes generation: proximity (MLP contribution), embedding rotation (directional change), and anisotropy alignment (in-distribution consistency)
  • Achieves state-of-the-art performance across 6 datasets and 3 model families, outperforming 8 strong baselines including perplexity, SAR, Semantic Entropy, and Eigen Score

Methodology

The method operates in four stages:

  1. Conformal Abstention Framework: Given calibration samples, compute uncertainty scores (e.g., perplexity) for model responses. Derive a threshold τ via conformal quantiles. For test inputs with uncertainty above τ, the model abstains. This yields a participation guarantee P(participate) ∈ [1−α, 1−α+1/(n+1)] and a conditional correctness lower bound via Bayes' rule.
  1. Knowledge Contribution Extraction: Using information flow analysis, decompose per-layer MLP updates into direct contributions (to the last query token) and propagated contributions (to earlier tokens that propagate through subsequent self-attention). This yields a 2L−1 dimensional trajectory Ω.
  1. Geometry Signal Design: Three complementary signals are extracted per layer:
  • Proximity ω: normalized L1-based proximity measuring MLP update contribution to the post-MLP embedding
  • Embedding rotation θ: angle between pre-MLP and post-MLP embeddings (linked to token ranking changes via proved lemmas on scale invariance and rotation sensitivity)
  • Anisotropy alignment φ: angle between token embeddings and in-distribution/out-of-distribution dominant directions, capturing whether the model operates within its training-induced representation region
  1. Token-level Calibration: Each generated token receives an 8L−4 dimensional feature vector (Ω, Θ, Φ_in, Φ_out). Mahalanobis distances to correct/incorrect prediction distributions are computed, then fed with uncalibrated confidence into an XGBoost calibrator. Calibrated token-level confidences are aggregated via perplexity into a response-level uncertainty score.

Results & Highlights

  • Conditional Correctness: Achieves the highest average conditional correctness of 75.0% across datasets under conformal abstention, consistently outperforming all baselines across participation rates from 0.1 to 0.9
  • AUROC/AUPRC: Consistently achieves the best AUROC and AUPRC on all 6 datasets. On TruthfulQA, AUROC reaches 0.86 (vs. next-best 0.70 from ATRMD) with Gemma-3-4B-Instruct
  • Theoretical Validation: Both participation rates and conditional correctness empirically exceed their theoretical lower bounds across all tested thresholds
  • Hypothesis Verification: Correct predictions exhibit significantly stronger knowledge-rotation interaction and closer alignment with in-distribution representations than incorrect predictions
  • Ablation: All four geometry signal components contribute comparably; removing any single component degrades performance
  • Calibration Ablation: Standard baselines with the same calibration procedure often degrade, confirming gains come from geometry signals rather than the calibration step itself
  • Cross-model Consistency: Gains hold across Gemma-3-4B-Instruct, LLaMA-3.2-3B-Instruct, and LLaMA-3-8B-Instruct

Critical Assessment

  • Novelty (High): The connection between representation geometry and conformal abstention is genuinely novel. Moving from non-conformity scores (intractable for open-ended generation) to confidence-based abstention with geometry calibration is a meaningful theoretical and practical contribution.
  • Rigor (High): All theoretical claims are formally proved. The exchangeability preservation argument is clean and the conditional correctness derivation via Bayes' rule is sound. Empirical evaluation is thorough with multiple models, datasets, and baselines.
  • Reproducibility (Medium-High): Implementation uses standard libraries (XGBoost, HHEM-2.1-Open, GPT-4o/5.1 for evaluation). However, the correctness evaluation pipeline relies on proprietary GPT models, which may limit exact reproducibility. The information flow computation requires access to internal model states (not just logits).
  • Significance (High): Selective abstention is a practically important problem. The finite-sample guarantees are valuable for deployment scenarios requiring calibrated reliability. The geometry-based calibration insight could influence broader uncertainty quantification research.
  • Potential concerns: (1) Computational overhead is O(LT²d), which may be significant for long sequences. (2) The method requires a reference/calibration set, which must be representative of the target distribution. (3) The evaluation function ξ uses GPT models as judges, introducing potential circularity if the same models are used in deployment. (4) The 75% conditional correctness, while state-of-the-art, still means 1 in 4 retained answers is incorrect.

Strengths

  • Principled theoretical framework with finite-sample guarantees, not just heuristic uncertainty scores
  • Geometry signals are interpretable and well-motivated by mechanistic analysis of how MLPs encode knowledge
  • No model retraining required — fully post-hoc and applicable to any autoregressive LLM
  • Comprehensive experimental validation with consistent improvements across diverse tasks (QA, reasoning, commonsense)
  • Clear ablation studies disentangling the contribution of each component
  • Honest discussion of why calibration helps their method but hurts scalar baselines

Limitations

  • Quadratic complexity in sequence length (O(T²)) from attention contribution computation may limit applicability to long-context scenarios
  • Requires access to internal hidden states, attention weights, and MLP activations — not applicable to API-only models
  • Performance depends on quality and representativeness of the calibration set
  • The correctness evaluation pipeline (HHEM + GPT-4o/5.1 judges) is itself costly and potentially noisy
  • Theoretical guarantees assume exchangeability between calibration and test data, which may not hold under distribution shift
  • Experiments are limited to 3B–8B parameter models; scalability to much larger models is untested

Relevance

本文对 LLM 的不确定性量化和选择性生成有直接参考价值。利用 Transformer 内部表示几何来校准置信度的思路，可为幻觉检测、可信推理和模型安全部署提供新方法。共形预测的理论保证使其适用于需要可证明可靠性的应用场景。

Questions for Further Exploration

  1. How does the method scale to models with 70B+ parameters or mixture-of-experts architectures where MLP routing patterns differ significantly?
  2. Can the geometry signals be approximated from fewer layers (e.g., only the last few) to reduce the O(LT²d) computational cost?
  3. How robust are the conformal guarantees under distribution shift between calibration and test data?
  4. Could the anisotropy alignment signal be used as a standalone OOD detection method without the full conformal abstention pipeline?
  5. What happens when the method is applied to chain-of-thought reasoning, where intermediate tokens may have different geometry dynamics than factual recall tasks?
