> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 Path-Lock Expert (PLE)，通过在 Transformer 解码器中将 MLP 替换为两个模式专属专家（think / no-think），用控制 token 确定性路由，从架构层面解决了混合推理模型中"推理泄漏"的问题，显著提升了 no-think 模式的准确性和简洁性。

> **TL;DR:** Path-Lock Expert replaces each decoder MLP with two mode-specific experts routed deterministically by control tokens, achieving strong architectural separation between think and no-think modes in hybrid-thinking LLMs — reducing reasoning leakage and improving no-think accuracy while preserving think-mode performance.

## 核心贡献

- 提出了混合推理模型中"推理泄漏"问题的架构层面解释：共享的 MLP 参数是两种模式相互干扰的根源。
- 设计了 PLE 架构，将每个解码器层的 MLP 替换为两个语义锁定专家，分别服务于 think 和 no-think 模式，注意力层、嵌入层、归一化层和 LM 头全部共享。
- 采用确定性控制 token 路由（非学习型路由器），整条序列锁定同一专家路径，推理时保持与稠密模型相同的逐 token 计算模式。
- 在 Qwen3-4B 上，no-think 准确率从 20.67% 提升至 40.00%（AIME24），反射 token 从 2.54 降至 0.39，同时保持 think 模式性能不变。
- 提供了形式化理论分析，将 PLE 解释为对混合推理目标的部分块对角化，消除了专家参数间的直接梯度耦合。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies reasoning leakage in hybrid-thinking models as an **architectural interference problem** rather than purely a data/training issue — shared feed-forward parameters inherently couple the two competing output behaviors.
- Introduces **Path-Lock Expert (PLE)**, a minimal architectural modification that replaces each decoder MLP with two semantically locked experts while keeping the entire attention backbone shared.
- Achieves **deterministic, sequence-level routing** via control tokens with no learned router, no auxiliary balancing losses, and no RL-based training pipeline.
- Demonstrates substantial gains on AIME24: no-think accuracy improves from 20.67% to 40.00% on Qwen3-4B, with reflective tokens dropping from 2.54 to 0.39, all while preserving think-mode performance.
- Provides a **formal theoretical account** showing PLE partially block-diagonalizes the Hessian of the hybrid-thinking objective, eliminating direct cross-mode gradient interference in expert parameters.

## Methodology

PLE modifies the standard pre-norm decoder-only Transformer at a single point: each layer's MLP is replaced by a pair of structurally identical SwiGLU experts. The routing decision is made **once** from the last control token (`/think` or `/no think`) in the input and locked across all layers and all decoding steps. This is fundamentally different from traditional MoE models that perform token-level routing at every layer.

The training procedure uses standard causal LM loss on chat-formatted examples tagged with either mode. The inactive expert receives zero gradient; the shared backbone (attention, embeddings, norms, LM head) is updated by both modes. Both experts are initialized from the same source MLP weights, so specialization is driven purely by mode-specific gradient differences during fine-tuning.

The authors construct a 54k training dataset by augmenting the Superior-Reasoning corpus with synthesized no-think targets (generated by Qwen3-235B and filtered for correctness, length, and absence of reflective tokens), combined in a 1:1 ratio.

Experiments are conducted on two model families (Qwen3-4B and Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct) across AIME24, MATH500, MMLU-STEM, and GPQA-Diamond benchmarks, evaluating accuracy, output length, and reflective token count per response.

## Results & Highlights

- **AIME24 (Qwen3-4B):** No-think accuracy: 20.67% → 40.00% (+19.3 pp); Reflective tokens: 2.54 → 0.39; Think accuracy preserved at ~60%.
- **AIME24 (Qwen2.5-7B):** No-think accuracy: 6.67% → 26.67% (+20.0 pp); Think mode accuracy slightly improved (synergistic effect).
- **MATH500:** Consistent pattern — PLE maintains think accuracy while significantly improving no-think accuracy and conciseness.
- **Parameter efficiency:** PLE saves ~33% total parameters compared to deploying two independent models, as attention layers remain shared.
- **Ablation findings:** (1) Hybrid-thinking initialization yields higher accuracy but retains residual leakage; pure instruct initialization gives near-zero leakage at lower accuracy. (2) Harder training data (Superior-Reasoning) benefits stronger base models; simpler data (OpenR1) suits weaker bases. (3) Raw pretrained weights (without post-training) collapse on hard benchmarks, confirming PLE requires instruction-following capability.

The paper references the authors' own prior work (Wang et al., 2025) on training-level mitigation of reasoning leakage, giving them deep domain expertise. The theoretical appendix is unusually rigorous for an empirical architecture paper, including formal propositions, a theorem with proof, and a corollary on the compromise penalty of dense models.

## Critical Assessment

**Novelty:** The core idea — duplicating MLPs with deterministic routing — is conceptually simple. The novelty lies in (a) framing reasoning leakage as an architectural problem rather than a training problem, and (b) the formal theoretical justification. The sequence-level, deterministic routing is a meaningful departure from conventional MoE.

**Rigor:** The theoretical appendix is strong, with formal propositions about gradient decoupling and Hessian block-diagonalization. However, the theoretical claims assume local quadratic approximations and identical-structure Hessians — real training dynamics may deviate. The experimental evaluation covers only two model families at the 4B–7B scale; scalability to larger models (70B+) is untested.

**Reproducibility:** Code is publicly available. Training data construction is described in detail. Hyperparameters appear consistent across experiments. The deterministic routing eliminates randomness in expert selection.

**Significance:** The work makes a convincing case that architectural separation matters for hybrid thinking. However, the scope is narrow: it addresses mode separation specifically, not reasoning quality in general. The gains are most pronounced on mathematical benchmarks; GPQA results are mixed. The "reasoning contamination" trade-off between accuracy and leakage purity remains unresolved.

**Red flags:** The paper sometimes overstates the architectural argument — the ablations themselves show that base model initialization and data quality are equally important factors, suggesting the problem is not *purely* architectural. The GPQA-Diamond results show PLE can underperform baselines in some configurations, which is acknowledged but underemphasized.

## Strengths

- Elegant, minimal architectural intervention — only MLPs are duplicated, everything else stays shared.
- Deterministic routing eliminates the complexity and instability of learned MoE routers.
- Strong empirical gains on no-think accuracy and leakage reduction, especially on AIME24.
- Rigorous theoretical framework with formal proofs in the appendix.
- Honest about limitations — explicitly states they do not claim a new overall SOTA.
- Ablation studies are thorough, covering base model initialization, dataset choice, and the accuracy-leakage trade-off.

## Limitations

- Only evaluated on 4B and 7B models; scalability to 70B+ models is unknown.
- GPQA-Diamond results are mixed, suggesting the approach may not generalize equally to all domains.
- The "reasoning contamination" from hybrid-thinking base models limits how clean the no-think mode can be — there is a fundamental trade-off between accuracy and leakage purity.
- The training data requires a strong no-think teacher (Qwen3-235B) for synthesis, creating a dependency on an already capable model.
- Sequence-level routing prevents switching modes mid-conversation, which limits flexibility.
- The approach addresses mode separation but does not improve the underlying reasoning capability itself.

## Relevance

本文与 LLM 隐藏状态可解释性直接相关——PLE 通过分离 feed-forward 参数实现了 think/no-think 模式的结构化解耦，为理解 Transformer 不同组件在推理行为中的角色提供了清晰的架构层面证据。

## Questions for Further Exploration

1. How does PLE scale to 70B+ models? Does the parameter overhead of duplicating MLPs become prohibitive, or is the 33% savings vs. full duplication still attractive?
2. Can the sequence-level routing be relaxed to allow segment-level or turn-level mode switching within a single conversation without reintroducing leakage?
3. What happens when the shared attention backbone itself develops mode-specific representations during fine-tuning — does this create indirect coupling that undermines the architectural separation over longer training?
4. Could PLE be extended to more than two modes (e.g., fast-reasoning, slow-reasoning, direct-answer) and would deterministic routing still suffice?
5. Does the theoretical compromise penalty (∆conflict) correlate with empirically observed leakage across different model scales and architectures?
