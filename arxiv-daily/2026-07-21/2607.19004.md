> **一句话总结：** 通过一个简洁的"两个高斯+插值打分函数"反例，本文证明了对数光滑且满足 LSI 的目标分布而言，[YW26] 所用的 sub-Gaussian 打分误差条件是可采样的充要边界——任何更弱的误差（有界 Lp 矩、sub-Weibull β<2，甚至 β=2 但 r<1/18）都会使任意算法都无法以趋于零的 TV 偏差逼近 π，且该结论对任意只查询打分预言机的算法都成立。

> **TL;DR:** This is a pure-theory impossibility result (no datasets/benchmarks/experiments) for sampling from a well-behaved target (log-smooth + log-Sobolev) given an inexact score oracle. The paper shows the sub-Gaussian score-error assumption of [YW26] is not just sufficient but *necessary*: any weaker notion — bounded Lp moments, sub-Weibull error with β∈(0,2), or even sub-Gaussian (β=2) with MGF parameter r<1/18 — forces TV distance ≥ 1/3 from the target, for *any* algorithm that only queries the score. This strictly strengthens the prior lower bound of [CCSW26], which only ruled out inexact Langevin dynamics under bounded moments. The boundary constant (separation TV > 2/3 between two Gaussians N(0,1) and N(R,1) with R≈√log(1/ε)) is the load-bearing quantitative delta; notably the result does NOT extend to the DDPM multi-scale regime because the counterexample needs R to grow as ε→0.

## 核心贡献

- **界定了"可采样"的精确边界：** 对 1-对数光滑、满足 1-LSI 的目标分布族，[YW26] 使用的 sub-Gaussian 打分误差假设（Definition 2 中 β=2）不仅是充分的，而且是一切只查询打分预言机的算法可采样到任意小 TV 距离的**必要**条件。
- **算法无关的不可行性下界：** 证明了对**任意**仅以查询 s 为手段的算法 A，当误差满足下列任一更弱条件时，都无法保证返回样本使 TV(Law(x), π) ≤ 1/3：(i) ε-(β,r)-MGF 误差且 β∈(0,2)，或 β=2 且 r<1/18；(ii) 任意 ε>0、p>0 的 ε-Lp 误差。
- **对 [CCSW26] 的双重强化：** 相比于 [CCSW26] 只能否定"非精确 Langevin 动力学"在"有界矩"误差下的收敛，本文将结论推广为（a）对任意算法成立，（b）覆盖 sub-Weibull β∈(0,2) 等几乎所有弱于 sub-Gaussian 的误差族。
- **参数层面的紧性：** 证明了 [YW26] 所要求的 MGF 参数 r（大致需大于 LSI 常数倒数）也是紧的，差至多常数因子；边界情形 β=2、r<1/18 即被排除（Corollary 1）。
- **一个几何上简洁的反例：** 仅用 d=1 的两个平移高斯 N(0,1) 与 N(R,1)，以及一个在两者真实打分间插值、Lipschitz 常数 ≤5 的 s_ε，就刻画出整张"不可行景观"。

## Key Contributions

- **A sharp tractability boundary:** For the canonical family of 1-log-smooth targets satisfying a 1-LSI, the sub-Gaussian score-error assumption (β=2 in the MGF-error Definition 2) used by [YW26] is shown to be *necessary*, not merely sufficient — any qualitatively weaker error rules out sampling to vanishing TV.
- **Algorithm-agnostic impossibility (Theorem 2):** No algorithm that only queries the inexact score s can guarantee a returned sample with TV(Law(x), π) ≤ 1/3, whenever (s, π) satisfies an ε-(β, r)-MGF error with β∈(0,2) (or β=2, r<1/18), or an ε-Lp error for any p>0.
- **Strict strengthening of [CCSW26]:** The prior lower bound of Cao-Chen-Sridharan-Wu (ICML 2026) only refuted convergence of inexact *Langevin dynamics* under bounded *moments*; this paper removes the algorithm-specific restriction (any algorithm querying s) and broadens the class of refuted error notions to essentially everything below sub-Gaussianity.
- **Tightness of the [YW26] parameters:** Corollary 1 rules out the borderline case β=2 with r<1/18, matching (up to constants) the [YW26] requirement that r be larger than the inverse LSI constant.
- **A minimal, geometric counterexample:** The entire lower bound rests on two translated unit-variance Gaussians N(0,1), N(R,1) and a single 5-Lipschitz interpolating score s_ε that agrees with each true score on the region where that Gaussian carries mass.

## Methodology

The paper is a self-contained impossibility (lower-bound) argument — there are no algorithms proposed and no experiments. The construction proceeds in four steps.

1. **Two error notions are fixed.** *Lp error* (Definition 1): E_π[‖s − s_π‖₂^p]^{1/p} ≤ ε. *MGF error* (Definition 2): (1/r · log E_π exp(r ‖s − s_π‖₂^β))^{1/β} ≤ ε, where β=2 recovers the sub-Gaussian regime used by [YW26], and β∈(0,2) corresponds to heavier-tailed sub-Weibull error. Lemma 1 shows MGF error implies Lp error (set β=1, r=p/ε and use log x ≤ x−1), so hard instances under Definition 2 double as lower bounds under Definition 1.

2. **The hard instance (Proposition 1).** In dimension d=1, take π_{1,ε}=N(0,1) and π_{2,ε}=N(R,1); both are 1-strongly-log-concave and 1-smooth (hence satisfy 1-LSI). The approximate score s_ε(x) := −x + R·χ(x/R) uses a C^∞ bump χ built from θ(t)=ρ(t)/(ρ(t)+ρ(1−t)), ρ(t)=exp(−1/t), so that s_ε equals the true score of π_1 on x ≤ R/3 and the true score of π_2 on x ≥ 2R/3, interpolating smoothly on [R/3, 2R/3]. A direct derivative calculation gives s'_ε ∈ [−1, 5], hence s_ε is 5-Lipschitz.

3. **Why weaker errors are fooled.** Because s_ε only disagrees with s_{π_i} on a tail region (x > R/3 for π_1, x < 2R/3 for π_2), and Gaussian tails decay like exp(−R²/18) while the moment-generating term grows like exp(rR^β), the condition β<2 guarantees that for *any* ε>0 one can pick R satisfying equation (1) — the tail wins. Then (s_ε, π_{1,ε}) and (s_ε, π_{2,ε}) *both* satisfy an ε-(β,r)-MGF error, even though TV(π_{1,ε}, π_{2,ε}) > 2/3. Corollary 1 extends the same existence argument to the borderline β=2, r<1/18.

4. **From instance to impossibility (Theorem 2).** Any algorithm seeing only s_ε cannot tell whether the target is π_{1,ε} or π_{2,ε}; by the triangle inequality against TV(π_1, π_2) > 2/3, it must fail (TV > 1/3) on at least one. The Lp branch follows by combining Proposition 1 with Lemma 1.

5. **Scope limitation (Remark 1).** The construction requires R ≈ √log(1/ε) to grow with ε; a pointwise calculation shows no single s can have vanishing error against both N(0,1) and N(R,1) for fixed R. Consequently the lower bound does *not* extend to the DDPM/diffusion-model regime, where approximate scores are supplied at every convolution scale (R→0) and L2 error is in fact known to suffice for TV convergence [CCL+23, CLL23].

## Results & Highlights

This is a pure-theory paper: there are **no datasets, benchmarks, training runs, or empirical comparisons**. The quantitative content is the set of constants appearing in the theorems; per the content contract these are reported as the paper's stated bounds.

- **Impossibility threshold — TV separation > 2/3 (Proposition 1, target family = 1-log-smooth + 1-strongly-log-concave / 1-LSI Gaussians in d=1).** For any ε>0 and any β∈(0,2), r>0, the paper constructs two targets π_{1,ε}=N(0,1), π_{2,ε}=N(R,1) with TV(π_{1,ε}, π_{2,ε}) > 2/3 such that a single 5-Lipschitz score s_ε has ε-(β,r)-MGF error against *both*. The prior lower bound of [CCSW26] achieved the comparable separation only for the inexact Langevin dynamics and only under bounded-moment error; this paper's delta is making that separation hold for *any* algorithm and for the entire sub-Weibull (β<2) plus Lp family. (The authors note the constant 2/3 is for simplicity — "any constant < 1 is achievable through a small modification of the proof.")
- **Lipschitz constant of the counterexample score = 5 (proof of Proposition 1).** The interpolating score s_ε has s'_ε ∈ [−1, 5], so it is a well-behaved 5-Lipschitz vector field — the lower bound cannot be dismissed as relying on a pathological, non-smooth s.
- **Tightness of the [YW26] MGF parameter — ruled-out region β=2, r < 1/18 (Corollary 1).** Even in the sub-Gaussian regime (β=2), if the MGF parameter r is below 1/18 the same impossibility holds; [YW26] proves sufficiency for r roughly larger than the inverse LSI constant, so the gap between 1/18 and the [YW26] threshold is constant-factor tight.
- **Scaling of the Gaussian separation: R ≈ √log(1/ε) (Remark 1).** The counterexample's separation distance must grow like the square root of a logarithm as ε→0; this is what prevents the result from applying to the DDPM multi-scale setting, where scores are provided at all convolution scales (including R→0) and L2 error provably suffices [CCL+23, CLL23].

## Authors & Credibility

All four authors — Anming Gu, Kevin Tian, Hubert Yang, Yusong Zhu — are affiliated with the University of Texas at Austin (Department of Computer Science). The paper builds directly on, and is technically consistent with, two recent results it positions itself against: [YW26] (Yang & Wibisono, "Convergence of the inexact Langevin algorithm in KL divergence with application to score-based generative models," arXiv:2211.01512, 2026) and [CCSW26] (Cao, Chen, Sridharan, Wu, ICML 2026, "On the robustness of Langevin dynamics to score function error"). The mathematical machinery (log-Sobolev inequality, Gaussian tail bounds, smooth bump functions, MGF/Lp moment conversions) is standard for the sampling-theory literature [VW19, Bub15, Ver25], and the proofs are short, self-contained, and checkable. The note is terse (~5 pages) and reads as a focused, single-result contribution rather than a survey.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine but incremental. The shape of the counterexample (two well-separated Gaussians with a score that agrees with each where the other has no mass) is a known idea, and the bounded-moment version of the lower bound already appears in [CCSW26]. The real novelty is the *combination* of two strengthenings — (a) algorithm-agnostic (any s-querying algorithm, not just Langevin) and (b) refuting the entire sub-Weibull β<2 family and the β=2, r<1/18 corner — plus the parameter-tightness statement against [YW26]. A reader familiar with [CCSW26] will find the conceptual jump small, even if the technical scope is strictly larger.
- **Rigor:** High. Proposition 1, Corollary 1, Lemma 1, and Theorem 2 are each proved in full with explicit constants; the Lipschitz bound s'_ε ∈ [−1,5] is verified by calculus, and the Gaussian-tail-vs-MGF competition (equation 1) is the clean crux. No steps are hand-waved.
- **Reproducibility:** Effectively perfect for a math paper — the hard instance is a one-line definition s_ε(x) = −x + R·χ(x/R) with χ given explicitly; an undergraduate could replot Figure 1 from the construction.
- **Significance:** The result cleanly closes a question (where exactly does sampling-with-inexact-scores become intractable?) and pins the boundary at sub-Gaussianity. The significance is tempered by the most important caveat below: the lower bound is essentially *orthogonal* to the practical diffusion-model setting.
- **Red flags / paper-specific weaknesses:**
  - *The headline framing oversells the DDPM connection.* The abstract and intro motivate the question via score-based generative models, but Remark 1 concedes the construction does **not** extend to the DDPM regime, because it needs R ≈ √log(1/ε) to grow while DDPM assumes vanishing score error at *all* convolution scales (R→0). So the impossibility has little direct bearing on practical score-based generative modeling, where L2 error is already known to suffice [CCL+23, CLL23]. A reader picking up the paper for diffusion-model implications will be disappointed.
  - *Single target family.* Only 1-strongly-log-concave / 1-LSI Gaussians are used as the hard instance; whether the boundary moves for non-log-concave or merely dissipative targets is unaddressed.
  - *No matching upper bound at the boundary.* The paper proves the β=2, r≥~1/μ side is sufficient by citation to [YW26] and that r<1/18 is impossible, but leaves the exact critical r unspecified (only "up to constants").
  - *The TV threshold 2/3 is cosmetic* (authors admit any constant <1 works), so the specific number carries no information.

## Strengths

- Crystallizes a clean, memorable takeaway: for log-smooth + LSI targets, **sub-Gaussian score error is exactly the tractability threshold**, both in tail-type (β) and in MGF parameter (r), up to constants.
- Strengthens the most recent prior lower bound [CCSW26] on two axes simultaneously (algorithm-agnostic + broader error family) with a short proof.
- The counterexample is genuinely minimal — one dimension, two Gaussians, one bump function — making the intuition (Figure 1) transparent and teachable.
- Honest about its own scope: Remark 1 explicitly delineates that the result does not apply to DDPM, pre-empting overclaiming.

## Limitations

- **No direct applicability to diffusion models / DDPM.** The lower bound requires the score-separation scale R to grow with 1/ε, whereas diffusion training assumes accurate scores at every convolution scale; L2 error suffices there, so this paper does not constrain practical score-based generation.
- **No experiments or numerical validation** — expected for theory, but limits immediate empirical impact.
- **Narrow target class:** hard instances are strongly log-concave Gaussians; behavior under weaker isoperimetry or non-log-concavity is open.
- **Constant-factor gaps** in the critical MGF parameter r (paper proves r<1/18 impossible, [YW26] needs r≳1/μ; the exact threshold is not pinned).
- **Short / single-result** — readers wanting a broader "tractability landscape" survey (the title's promise) will find one theorem rather than a map.

## Relevance

对研究扩散模型/基于打分的采样理论（score-based sampling theory, Langevin 收敛性、LSI 下界）的工作者高度相关：它精确刻画了"非精确打分预言机"何时仍可采样，是理解 score-based 生成模型理论边界的直接基础工作。但需注意其反例不直接适用于多尺度 DDPM 训练场景，故对以工程/实证为主的扩散模型研究参考价值有限，更适合理论方向读者。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the lower bound be repaired for the multi-scale DDPM setting by a different construction that keeps the score-separation scale R fixed (or sends R→0) while still fooling any algorithm — or is sub-Gaussianity genuinely unnecessary there?
- What is the exact critical MGF parameter r*(β, μ) at β=2 separating [YW26]'s sufficiency (r ≳ 1/μ) from this paper's impossibility (r < 1/18)? Closing the constant gap would make the boundary fully tight.
- Does the algorithm-agnostic impossibility extend to stronger divergences (KL, Rényi, W₂), or can a richer algorithm class recover vanishing error in those metrics under heavier-tailed score noise?
- Can the hard instance be extended beyond strongly log-concave / LSI targets to merely dissipative or non-log-concave families, where the tractability boundary may lie elsewhere?
- Is there a matching *upper bound* (a concrete sampling algorithm) that achieves TV → 0 under exactly the sub-Gaussian assumption with the paper's tight r, certifying that the boundary is two-sided?
