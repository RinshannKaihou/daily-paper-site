> **一句话总结：** 本文从优化/隐式偏差视角解释了为什么 RoPE 等相对位置编码能长度泛化而学习型绝对编码不能：RoPE 使注意力 logit 仅依赖相对偏移（精确等变性），训练后形成一个与目标偏移对齐的低秩"载波"核，规则可逐字迁移到更长序列；绝对编码则把输出钉死在训练范围内的某个绝对位置，超出范围即崩塌。

> **TL;DR:** On a minimal fixed-offset retrieval task (copy the token K positions before the query), a single-layer RoPE attention head stays at 1.00 accuracy up to L=48 after training only on L≤16, while a learned absolute encoding (APE) collapses to 0.07 (chance 0.05) the moment it sees an unseen length. The authors prove RoPE's logit depends only on relative offset (exact equivariance, Lemma 1), so the learned offset selection rule transfers verbatim; the selected rule is a target-aligned low-rank "carrier" kernel (Lemma 2) whose accuracy decays by an inverse-linear attention-dilution law (Corollary 2, R²=0.91–0.94 against the fit). APE instead "pins" to a fixed trained absolute position (Proposition 2), and a linear-attention control shows the whole mechanism is specific to softmax normalization. The phenomenon, equivariance, and carrier all transfer to a 2-layer/4-head transformer (RoPE 1.00 vs APE 0.37 at L=48 on a full-sequence shift task).

## 核心贡献

- **从"表达力"转向"优化选择"的解释范式**：以往长度泛化研究（RASP-L 猜想、Huang et al. 形式化框架）只回答"是否存在可泛化解"，本文首次系统回答"梯度下降为何选中那个能泛化的解"，将长度泛化与注意力的隐式偏差（attention-as-SVM）联系起来。
- **证明 RoPE 的精确等变性（Lemma 1/Corollary 1）**：RoPE 的 logit 仅依赖相对偏移 δ=t−n，因此被选中的偏移在所有长度上保持不变——这是长度泛化得以发生的结构性前提，无需任何数据/训练假设。
- **刻画"载波"选择规则（Lemma 2 + Proposition 1）**：训练出的 query/key 表示共享一个被预旋转到目标偏移的主方向，产生在 δ=−K 处取峰值的相对核；并证明该几何是"载波必须出现"的必要性结果，而非建模便利。
- **给出精度衰减的稀释律（Corollary 2）**：在 softmax 下目标 token 的注意力权重以 1/(α+c(L−1)) 形式逆线性衰减，拟合 R²=0.914/0.926/0.943（K=1/2/3），将"优雅衰减"量化为可预测的定律。
- **绝对编码的位置钉死定理（Proposition 2）与反例**：形式化 APE 因训练范围外位置向量梯度恒为零而失败，且权重衰减、表插值、索引缩放等推理期修补均无法恢复精度。
- **softmax 特异性证明**：线性注意力控制实验表明去除归一化后机制消失，因为线性分数剖面在训练偏移外欠定、最小范数解不外推。
- **跨架构迁移验证**：在 2 层 4 头带 MLP/LayerNorm 的 decoder-only transformer 上，三类发现（现象、等变性、载波）全部复现。

## Key Contributions

- **A selection (optimization) account, not just an expressivity account**: Prior work (RASP-L conjecture, Huang et al. formal framework) only established whether a length-generalizing solution *exists*; this paper explains *which* solution gradient descent selects and *why* that one generalizes, linking length generalization to the attention-as-SVM implicit-bias literature.
- **Exact equivariance of RoPE (Lemma 1, Corollary 1)**: The RoPE logit depends on positions only through relative offset δ=t−n, so the selected offset is length-invariant (empirically equal to −K in 100% of seed×length cells) with no data or training assumptions required.
- **Target-aligned carrier characterization (Lemma 2, Proposition 1)**: Trained query/key representations share a dominant unit direction, pre-rotated to the target offset, yielding a relative kernel peaked at δ=−K; a converse proves this geometry is *forced* by content-independent selection, not a convenience.
- **Attention-dilution decay law (Corollary 2)**: Softmax forces the target's attention weight to decay as 1/(α+c(L−1)); least-squares fits give R²=0.914/0.926/0.943 for K=1/2/3, turning the empirically observed "graceful erosion" into a quantitative prediction.
- **Position-pinning theorem for APE (Proposition 2)**: Formalizes why learned absolute encodings fail: position vectors beyond Lmax receive zero gradient (staying at init or decaying to 0 under weight decay), so no learned positional term selects the target out of range. Confirmed that weight decay λ∈{0,1e-4,1e-2}, table interpolation, clamp-to-last, and index rescaling all leave OOD accuracy at chance.
- **Softmax-specificity via linear-attention control**: Removing normalization destroys the carrier (key carrier fraction drops from 0.80 to 0.07, alignment cos from 0.83 to ~0.1); linear attention solves the task in-distribution by min-norm interpolation but does not extrapolate (0.96 ID vs 0.07 at L=48).
- **Mechanism transfers beyond one layer**: A 2-layer, 4-head transformer with MLP and LayerNorm reproduces all three findings on a full-sequence shift task (RoPE 1.00 vs APE 0.37 at L=48; carrier fraction 0.976 vs 0.038).

## Methodology

The paper isolates positional selection in a deliberately minimal setup and combines exact proofs with tightly controlled experiments.

**Task.** Fixed-offset retrieval: sequences of i.i.d. uniform tokens over vocabulary V={1,…,m}; the query is the last position; the label is the token exactly K positions earlier (y = s_{L-1-K}). Training lengths are uniform in [8,16]; evaluation extends to L∈{20,24,32,40,48}, all strictly out of range. This isolates a purely positional selection problem with a single optimal token.

**Model.** One layer, one head, d=64, no MLP, no LayerNorm, softmax attention with a linear readout U. The authors compare two positional encodings: (i) RoPE, which applies block-diagonal rotations R_t built from 2×2 blocks Rot(tθ_i); (ii) APE, which adds a *learned* position embedding p_t to the token embedding. A secondary experiment uses a 2-layer, 4-head decoder-only transformer (d=192, MLP width 4d, GELU, LayerNorm, causal mask) on a full-sequence shift task out[i]=in[i−K].

**Theoretical apparatus.** Built on the attention-as-SVM reduction of Tarzanagh et al. (the key-query product converges in direction to a max-margin token separator). The paper then proves:
- *Lemma 1 (Equivariance)*: the RoPE logit is exactly a function of δ alone, since R_n^⊤ R_t = R_{t−n}.
- *Assumption 1 (Shared carrier) + Lemma 2*: under a shared carrier direction u with W_K x_t ≈ ρu and W_Q x_n ≈ ρR_{−K}u, the content-averaged profile is ρ²g(δ+K) peaked at −K.
- *Proposition 1 (Necessity)*: target-aligned carrier geometry is the *only* way the averaged profile attains its global max at −K, so Assumption 1 is forced, not convenient.
- *Corollary 2 (Decay law)*: a*(L) = 1/(1+(L−1)r̄_L) ≥ 1/(1+(L−1)e^{−μ}), strictly decreasing in L.
- *Proposition 2 (Position pinning)*: under APE, position vectors p_j for j≥Lmax receive zero gradient and encode nothing about the target offset, so OOD accuracy is at chance.

**Experimental probes.** Five metrics: (i) accuracy, (ii) selected peak offset (argmax of mean attention vs query), (iii) carrier energy ∥mean∥²/E∥proj∥², (iv) carrier-only profile ϕ_c(δ)=m_Q^⊤ R_δ m_K, (v) inverse-linear decay fit of 1/a* on L−1. Training uses Adam (lr 1e-3, batch 256, 4000 steps, cross-entropy); 8 seeds for the headline phenomenon, 5 for validation. Compute is CPU-only.

## Results & Highlights

- **Headline separation (Table 1, K=1, 8 seeds):** RoPE holds 1.00 accuracy from L=12 out to L=32, then 0.96 at L=32, 0.91 at L=40, and 0.83 at L=48 — all well past the L≤16 training range. Learned APE is 1.00 in-distribution (L≤16) but collapses to 0.09/0.07/0.08/0.07/0.07 at L=20/24/32/40/48, indistinguishable from the 0.05 chance level. This is the gap the rest of the paper explains.
- **Carrier fractions dwarf baseline (Table 2):** The key/query carrier energy ∥mean∥²/E∥proj∥² is 0.69/0.68 at K=1, 0.82/0.86 at K=2, 0.80/0.86 at K=3 — versus a no-shared-direction baseline of 1/d=0.016 — and the carrier-only profile peaks at exactly −K for K∈{2,3}. Equivariance holds perfectly: the selected offset equals −K in 100% of seed×length cells.
- **Decay law fits tightly:** Least-squares fits of 1/a* against L−1 give R²=0.914 (K=1), 0.926 (K=2), 0.943 (K=3), with intercept α≈1 for K≥2 — quantitatively confirming the inverse-linear attention-dilution prediction of Corollary 2.
- **APE pinning confirmed across regularization (Table 6, K=2):** OOD accuracy stays at chance (0.08/0.10/0.09) for weight decay λ∈{0,1e-4,1e-2}; the pin location tightens toward Lmax−1−K=13 under ridge (12.7±0.5) but the failure persists. Out-of-range norms ‖p_out‖ stay at init (8.08) for λ=0 and vanish (0.20) for λ>0.
- **Linear-attention control fails to extrapolate (Table 3, K=2):** Softmax attention reaches 1.00 ID and 1.00 OOD at L=32/48 with carrier fraction 0.80 and alignment 0.83; the identical model with linear attention reaches 0.96 ID but only 0.09/0.07 OOD, with carrier fraction 0.07 and alignment ~0.1 — proving the mechanism is softmax-specific.
- **Other relative schemes (Fig. 5):** ALiBi solves K=1 (≈0.97) but fails K=2 (0.11–0.13, selecting −1, since a fixed locality bias cannot isolate non-adjacent offsets); T5-style learned bias selects any K but decays faster (1.00→0.44); RoPE selects any K and decays slowly (K=2 stays 1.00).
- **Mechanism transfers to a realistic transformer (Fig. 6, K=3 shift task):** A 2-layer/4-head model trained on L≤16 keeps RoPE at 1.00 out to L=48 while learned APE degrades (0.66/0.51/0.37); the RoPE shift head's offset profile coincides at L=16 and L=48 and peaks at −K, while APE smears toward chance. The RoPE head's carrier fraction is 0.976 (baseline 1/d_h=0.021) against 0.038 for the failing APE head — confirming the carrier survives depth, heads, and the MLP.

## Authors & Credibility

The authors are Subham Singh and Subha Raut (Department of Mathematics and Statistics, Mississippi State University) and Ashutosh Mishra (Independent Researcher). The work is theoretical in flavor and is positioned at the intersection of the attention-as-SVM implicit-bias line (Tarzanagh et al. 2023; Vasudeva et al. 2025) and the length-generalization/positional-encoding line (Zhou et al. 2024 RASP-L; Huang et al. 2025; Kazemnejad et al. 2023; Press et al. 2022 ALiBi; Su et al. 2024 RoPE). The argument is carried by formal lemmas and propositions with complete proofs in the appendix, plus multi-seed CPU experiments and a released single-script codebase. The paper is a PREPRINT (arXiv:2607.18759v1, 21 Jul 2026) and has not (yet) been peer-reviewed.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine. The framing reframes the length-generalization debate from expressivity ("does a solution exist?") to selection ("which solution does GD pick?"), and the target-aligned carrier + dilution law is a new, concrete mechanistic object — not a restatement of known RoPE properties (it is offset-selection that transfers, not attention mass).
- **Rigor:** High. Lemmas 1–2, Propositions 1–2, and Corollary 2 are stated precisely and proved in full in Appendix A; empirical claims (carrier fraction, decay R², equivariance in 100% of cells) are measured against explicit baselines (1/d=0.016) and the measured-margin bound of Table 7 is shown to be valid-but-loose rather than asserted tight.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong. All experiments run on CPU; a single script with one subcommand per experiment is promised; seeds are fixed and enumerated; full hyperparameters (d=64, vocab 20, [8,16], Adam 1e-3, batch 256, 4000 steps) and eval protocol (4096 sequences for accuracy/probes) are given in Appendix E. The minimal task is trivially cheap to replicate.
- **Significance:** The account makes *predictive* (not just descriptive) claims — it predicts the relative-over-absolute ordering, the ALiBi K=1-vs-K=2 capacity split, the T5-vs-RoPE dilution-rate difference, and the APE Lmax/L per-token decay — and bridges three literatures (attention implicit bias, recurrent extrapolation, RASP-L). The bridge to scale, however, is conceptual: the paper explicitly predicts *degradation by dilution* at scale rather than outright success, which is honest but limits engineering impact.
- **Red flags / specific weaknesses:**
  - *Scope is narrow.* The core theory covers one layer, one head, and a synthetic fixed-offset retrieval task with a single optimal token. Natural-language benchmarks (e.g., the arithmetic/copying tasks used by Jelassi et al., Anil et al.) are explicitly deferred to future work, so the claim that this "explains length generalization in attention" is a strong generalization from a toy task.
  - *Asymptotic-SVM assumption may not hold at finite time.* The carrier analysis inherits the regularization-path/normalized-GD limit of Tarzanagh et al.; the authors acknowledge finite-time training may not reach it and that they characterize the selected solution, not the trajectory.
  - *Why the carrier forms is left open.* Proposition 1 shows the carrier geometry is *necessary* (forced by content-independent selection), but whether Adam on cross-entropy actually *converges* to that geometry is not proven — only observed empirically via early co-emergence.
  - *K=1 is a special case.* For the immediate neighbor the carriers are near-parallel (kernel centered at 0) and the mechanism reduces to self-logit suppression via content covariance; the carrier pre-rotation story is stated only for K≥2. Since many real induction-like tasks are effectively K=1, this caveat matters.
  - *The 2-layer "realistic" model is still small and synthetic.* It is a 2-layer/4-head transformer on a positional-only full-sequence shift with a small vocabulary — far from production LLMs, and the d_h carrier fraction metric (0.976) is the only head-level evidence.

## Strengths

- A clean, falsifiable mechanistic story (equivariance → carrier → dilution) with each step both proved and measured.
- A genuine negative result: removing softmax normalization destroys extrapolation, isolating the mechanism rather than attributing it to "relative positions" vaguely.
- The ALiBi/T5/RoPE three-way comparison elegantly decomposes the shared mechanism (offset selection + dilution) from the scheme-dependent factors (capacity, dilution rate), showing the account is not RoPE-specific.
- Honest framing of limitations and of what the theory does *not* claim (it predicts dilution-driven degradation at scale, not infinite context).
- Excellent reproducibility posture: CPU-only, fixed seeds, a single self-contained script, and full sweep tables in the appendix.

## Limitations

- Theory and main experiments are confined to a single attention head on a synthetic fixed-offset retrieval task; transfer to natural language, multi-step reasoning, and realistic LLM scale is not demonstrated.
- The carrier-formation question (whether GD reaches the carrier geometry) is left open — only necessity is proven, not convergence.
- Relies on the asymptotic attention-as-SVM limit, which finite-time Adam may not fully realize.
- The K=1 case — arguably the most common in practice — is handled separately and rests on self-logit suppression rather than the carrier pre-rotation story.
- Multi-head dynamics are only partially characterized: how the carrier composes across heads and why splitting width across heads raises dilution (peak attention at L=48 falls from 0.69 at H=1 to 0.30 at H=4) is stated as an open question.
- No comparison against more recent extrapolation fixes (YaRN, position interpolation) beyond a conceptual mention; the engineering payoff for scaling RoPE is indirect.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the target-aligned carrier and the inverse-linear dilution law reproduce on natural-language retrieval/induction tasks (e.g., passkey retrieval, needle-in-haystack) in trained LLMs, where content is not i.i.d. and tokens are not a single optimal target?
- Can the necessity argument of Proposition 1 be extended to a *convergence* proof — i.e., that Adam on cross-entropy provably lands on the carrier geometry — by extending global max-margin analyses (Vasudeva et al. 2025) to positional logits?
- How does the carrier compose across heads and layers in deep models, and does the per-head frequency count fully predict the H=1→H=4 dilution increase (0.69→0.30 peak attention at L=48)?
- Does the softmax-specificity result survive for other normalizers (e.g., sigmoid attention, ReLU-based attentions), or is the carrier genuinely tied to the softmax partition function?
- Can the dilution law be used to *predict*, ahead of training, the OOD length at which a given RoPE model drops below a target accuracy, and thereby inform rotary-base / frequency-rescaling choices (YaRN, NTK-aware) quantitatively rather than heuristically?
- Does the APE position-pinning failure mode (and the Lmax/L per-token decay it predicts on full-sequence tasks) explain specific seed-sensitive length-generalization failures reported in production models (Zhou et al. 2024b)?
