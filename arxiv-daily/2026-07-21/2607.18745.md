> **一句话总结：** 本文利用恒等式 AB=(AT)(T⁻¹B) 提出"收缩规范预条件"(contraction-gauge preconditioning) 框架，为双因子低精度矩阵乘法推导出精确的期望乘积误差恒等式，并证明正对角折叠(fold)可化为几何规划求全局最优、可用线性规划判断恒等折叠是否最优；在训练好的 ViT 分类器的 12 个乘积上，几何规划折叠相比恒等折叠在 8/4 比特分别降低误差 18.0%/20.5%，并全面优于 SmoothQuant 风格的网格基线。

> **TL;DR:** This paper unifies the design of low-precision two-factor matrix products C=AB around an exact expected squared product-error identity (exact under subtractive dither and independent stochastic rounding) and the product-preserving gauge AB=(AT)(T⁻¹B). It shows the optimal shared diagonal "fold" gauge solves to global optimality as a geometric program, with an LP test for whether the identity fold is already optimal, and derives five decision statistics (tail index, profile spread, block coherence, weighted-Gram spectrum, slice-energy covariance) to pick among scaling/folding/partitioning/rotation/hierarchy families. On 12 products of a trained ViT-like digit classifier, the GP fold cuts held-out RTN error by 18.0% (8-bit) and 20.5% (4-bit) versus the identity fold, beats a SmoothQuant-style α-grid on 10/12 products, and reduces composed logit MSE by 15.4%/26.4%.

## 核心贡献

- **精确的乘积误差恒等式 (Theorem 3.3 / Corollary 3.4)：** 在因子量化误差相互独立、零均值、方差场已知的条件下，给出了 `E‖ÂB̂−AB‖²_F` 的精确有限维恒等式，含一阶传播项与双线性交叉项。该恒等式对非过载减性抖动 (subtractive dither) 和独立随机舍入 (stochastic rounding) 精确成立，并可推广到加权输出范数（适用于 Krylov、Hessian 加权量化、有限元等）。
- **收缩规范预条件框架：** 用 `AB=(AT)(T⁻¹B)` 把"选表示"形式化为收缩规范选择，并把设计解耦为两条独立轴线——结构族 (fold/rotation/hierarchy) 与规范域共享模式（用 `n_opp`，即变换量化后副本数，作为表示复用描述符）。
- **折叠族的全局可解性：** 证明正对角共享折叠 (fold) 在有界域下构成几何规划 (Theorem 4.2)，可经 log 域变量替换凸化并全局求解；并给出判断"恒等折叠是否已最优"的精确线性规划判据 (Proposition 4.3)。
- **按族决策统计量：** 为 scaling 给出尾部指数、为 partitioning 给出 profile spread（并证明标量范数排序在随机打结下损失 Θ(g)、而 log-幅度聚类可控，Theorem 5.2）、为旋转给出 block coherence (Theorem 6.1)、为部分旋转给出加权 Gram 谱头/尾界 (Theorem 7.1)、为层次细化给出切片能量反相关判据与伸缩恒等式 (Theorems 8.1–8.2)。
- **量化器设计：** 在固定比特和下给出非对称比特分配闭式 `b*_A−b*_B = ½log₂(P_A/P_B)` (Theorem 9.1)，给出分离过载偏置与颗粒误差的截断 clip 恒等式 (Theorem 9.4)，以及判定确定性舍入是否偏离噪声模型的截断特征函数诊断 Ξ。

## Key Contributions

- **Exact product-error identity (Theorem 3.3 / Corollary 3.4):** a finite-dimensional, exact expression for `E‖ÂB̂−AB‖²_F` under independent zero-mean entrywise quantization errors with known variance fields, including the bilinear simultaneous-error cross term. It holds exactly for non-overloading subtractive dither and for independent stochastic rounding with residue-dependent variances, and extends to arbitrary left/right weighted output norms.
- **Contraction-gauge preconditioning framework:** reframes transform selection via `AB=(AT)(T⁻¹B)` (T∈GL(K) acting on the shared contraction index), and separates design into the structural family of T (fold / rotation / hierarchy) versus the gauge-domain sharing pattern, tracked by the opposite-factor quantized-copy count `n_opp` as a representation-reuse descriptor.
- **Certified optimal shared fold:** within the bounded positive-diagonal (fold) family, the leading shared-fold objective is a geometric program with a global optimum after a log change of variables (Theorem 4.2); an exact LP test (Proposition 4.3) decides via directional derivatives whether the identity fold is already optimal.
- **Per-family decision statistics:** a tail index for scaling (Gaussian log growth vs heavy-tail polynomial), profile-spread statistics for partitioning (with scalar-norm sorting provably losing Θ(g) under random ties, while log-magnitude clustering has bounded regularized spread), block-local coherence bounds for rotations (Theorem 6.1), a Householder spectral head/tail bound for partial rotations (Theorem 7.1), and slice-energy anti-correlation + multilevel telescoping (Theorems 8.1–8.2) for hierarchy depth.
- **Quantizer design rules:** closed-form asymmetric operand bit-width split under a fixed bit-width sum, `b*_A−b*_B = ½log₂(P_A/P_B)` (Theorem 9.1); an exact clipped product-error identity separating overload bias from granular variance (Theorem 9.4); and a truncated characteristic-function diagnostic Ξ_{∆,ℓ_max} that flags when deterministic RTN will violate the noise model.

## Methodology

The paper builds a single design pipeline that scores every candidate by one product-error objective. The chain is: choose gauge T → produce transformed factors (Ã=AT, B̃=T⁻¹B) → grouping sets quantizer ranges R_G → variance field v_G = c·R_G² → expected product error via eq. (3).

Three orthogonal design axes feed that chain:
1. **Structural family of T** — positive diagonal *folds* (O(K)), full/partial orthogonal *rotations* (Householder reflectors, O(tK)), and block-diagonal orthogonal *hierarchies*.
2. **Gauge-domain pattern** — one shared domain (n_opp=1), block-constant domains (n_opp=g), or the idealized row-local benchmark (n_opp=m). This axis controls representation reuse (copies of the transformed, quantized opposite factor), not the per-product error.
3. **Quantizer** — bit allocation, clipping threshold, and rounding rule (dither / stochastic / deterministic RTN).

The analytical engine is the exact expected squared product-error identity, which weights each operand's error by the opposite factor's energy and includes a bilinear simultaneous-error term that is O(c²) versus the O(c) leading terms. For folds, this objective plus range-epigraph variables forms a geometric program solved to global optimality in the log domain; an LP over directional derivatives certifies whether the identity fold is optimal. For rotations, coherence factors bound the achievable gain of any orthogonal transform and show that a single shared randomized Hadamard rotation controls all block vectors within a logarithmic factor. For hierarchies, slice-energy anti-correlation between the two factors determines whether block-diagonal refinement beats a flat rotation, and a telescoping identity sums node increments to pick the surrogate-optimal depth. Empirical validation uses controlled synthetic sweeps that isolate one statistic at a time, plus a held-out trained-classifier study that tests whether dither-derived fold objectives transfer to deterministic RTN.

## Results & Highlights

- **GP fold vs identity fold (trained ViT-like digit classifier, 12 block-linear products, UCI 8×8 Optical Digits, FP32 test acc 93.94%):** the geometric-program fold gives geometric-mean held-out RTN error ratios of **0.820 at 8-bit (−18.0%)** and **0.795 at 4-bit (−20.5%)** versus the identity fold, improving all 12 products at each precision.
- **GP fold vs SmoothQuant-style baseline:** at each precision the GP achieves a lower geometric-mean error than any fold selected from a SmoothQuant-style α∈{0,0.1,…,1} grid, and beats even a *test-selected α oracle* on ten of twelve products, with worst-case GP-to-oracle ratios of only 1.018 (8-bit) and 1.007 (4-bit).
- **Composed model:** quantizing all 12 products together with the GP fold reduces composed logit MSE by **15.4% (8-bit)** and **26.4% (4-bit)** relative to the identity-fold model.
- **Dither→RTN transfer:** median within-product Spearman correlation between the dither-model's predicted candidate ranking and measured deterministic-RTN error is **0.937 at 8-bit** and **0.918 at 4-bit**; calibration-set dither prediction selects the held-out winner on 10/12 products at each precision with selection regret ~1.002.
- **Asymmetric bit allocation (controlled, b_sum=16):** the 9/7 split achieves the measured optimum, beating the symmetric 8/8 split by **1.7× in squared relative Frobenius error**, matching the 1.7-bit predicted gap from P_A/P_B≈11.
- **Rotation benefit (controlled outlier sweep, K=128):** unrotated-to-rotated paired error gain reaches **59.82×** at the largest injected outlier, while the flat Gaussian control gives 0.999 (interval contains 1).
- **Householder reflectors:** on rank-3 outlier data, t=3 reflectors yield a partial-to-full-rotation error ratio of **0.988** (consistent with equality), so a few reflectors capture most of the gain.
- **Hierarchy crossover:** the measured hierarchical-to-flat squared-error ratio rises monotonically with the slice-energy-overlap criterion, from **0.270 (anti-correlated)** to **2.422 (aligned)**, with the interpolated crossover at criterion value 1.045, tracking the surrogate boundary.
- **Copy-vs-error trade-off (heavy-tailed operands):** a block-GP design with n_opp=4 gives error ratio **0.754** versus a shared rotation (n_opp=1), i.e., **24.6% lower error** for three additional copies, whereas four independent block rotations yield 1.035 (no resolved gain).
- **Clipping surrogate:** the sample-optimal threshold approaches the finite-level Gaussian optimum τ*=3.92, and the measured max-to-optimal MSE ratio rises to **1.18×** at group size G=65,536.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine. The exact product-error identity with the bilinear cross term, the geometric-program formulation of the shared fold, and the LP identity-fold test are real analytical advances over the grid-search/gradient-descent scale selection used by SmoothQuant/AWQ/OmniQuant. Framing transform sharing through `n_opp` as a reuse descriptor is a clean, useful abstraction. The information-theoretic works by Ordentlich & Polyanskiy (2026a,b,c) establish the *objective* (product-weighted distortion); this paper's contribution is *reuse-aware certified selection within restricted gauge families*, which is distinct and complementary.
- **Rigor:** High on the theory side — exact identities are labeled exact under stated stochastic assumptions, and upper bounds are labeled as upper bounds (not claimed to bind realized RTN error). The counterexample in Section 4.3 quantifying the heuristic fold's ~4.22% leading-objective gap is a honest, concrete check.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong on paper. Appendix F promises complete code, fixed seeds, raw results, checksums, and regeneration instructions, and the experiments use standard scikit-learn digit data with a stated fixed stratified split.
- **Significance caveats / red flags:**
  - **Scope of empirical validation is narrow.** The end-to-end model is a *tiny* three-block, width-64 ViT on 8×8 UCI digit images (1,078 train / 323 calib / 396 test). The headline 18.0%/20.5% gains and the 0.937/0.918 transfer correlations come from this single compact vision model. The authors explicitly flag that validation on *pretrained language models and real token activations* is left to future work — yet the paper positions itself in the LLM-quantization literature and cites LLM.int8(), SmoothQuant, etc. A reader should treat the LLM-relevance as a claim to be demonstrated, not established.
  - **Stochastic-vs-deterministic gap is acknowledged but not closed.** The exact identity holds only under subtractive dither / independent stochastic rounding; the deterministic RTN results that dominate deployed INT inference rely on the identity as a *high-resolution approximation*, validated empirically via the Ξ diagnostic and Spearman correlations. The paper's own Remark 9.3 gives a concrete 4-d example where dither ranks T=H₄ first but INT8 RTN ranks T=I₄ first — i.e., quantizer-dependent ordering can flip, which is a real reproducibility hazard for anyone porting the method to hardware RTN.
  - **The 161.2× identity-to-GP objective ratio (Fig. 7a) is on a *constructed* family (m=n=20, K=32) designed to make shared ranges couple, not on realistic weights; the realistic gain is the 0.820/0.795 ratios from the digit classifier.** The abstract/headline numbers are well-behaved, but the controlled-study ratios should not be read as deployable gains.
  - **`n_opp` is explicitly only a descriptor.** The paper repeatedly and honestly states that storage/bandwidth/runtime require separate platform kernel measurements, which are not provided. So the copy-vs-error trade-off curves (e.g., Fig. 12) are not yet a cost model.
  - **Joint fold–threshold (T,τ) convex formulation is left open**, and the clipped objective uses a diagonal surrogate dropping cross-coordinate overload correlations — its accuracy on real activations is unmeasured.
- **Verdict:** A rigorous, theory-forward framework that genuinely advances *certified* fold selection and unifies transform-family choices under one objective. Its practical impact hinges on the (currently missing) LLM-scale RTN validation and on linking `n_opp` to measured kernel cost; as written it is a strong methods contribution, not yet a drop-in replacement for SmoothQuant-class recipes.

## Strengths

- Exact, assumption-explicit error accounting that cleanly separates what is proven (dither/stochastic-rounding identities, GP global optimality, LP identity-fold test) from what is bounded or empirical (rotation coherence, hierarchy surrogate, RTN transfer).
- The gauge-domain / `n_opp` decomposition makes the "reuse cost" of block-specific transforms first-class, which most prior LLM-quantization work treats only implicitly.
- Five interpretable, cheap-to-compute decision statistics (tail index, profile spread, coherence, weighted-Gram spectrum, slice-energy covariance) each map to one transform family — gives practitioners a concrete screening procedure.
- Honest framing: upper bounds are labeled as upper bounds; counterexamples quantify heuristic gaps; explicit open-problem section rather than overclaiming.
- Generous reproducibility appendix and fair, comprehensive related work covering both the LLM-quantization line and concurrent information-theoretic/lattice-quantization work.

## Limitations

- Only **scalar** quantization is treated; vector and lattice quantizers, and the full GL(K) family with condition-number constraints, are explicitly out of scope.
- Empirical validation is a **single small vision classifier** (8×8 digits, width-64 ViT). No LLM, no real-token-activation results — the central motivating domain is unaddressed empirically.
- Exactness requires **stochastic rounding or dither**; deterministic RTN — the dominant deployed mode — is only approximated, and the paper provides a concrete counterexample where RTN reorders transforms versus the dither objective.
- `n_opp` is a **representation-reuse descriptor only**; runtime, bandwidth, and storage costs need separate platform measurements that are not provided.
- The clipped-error analysis uses a **diagonal surrogate** and leaves the joint (transform, threshold) convex formulation open.
- Several selection rules (coherence, partial-rotation, hierarchy) provide **upper-bound comparisons**, not realized-error guarantees; realized-RTN ordering is evaluated empirically on controlled data only.

## Relevance

这篇工作把 SmoothQuant/QuaRot 类的双因子低精度矩阵乘量化统一到一个可证明的"收缩规范预条件 + 乘积误差目标"框架下，并给出全局最优的几何规划折叠解法——对关注 LLM/Transformer 量化、激活 outlier 处理以及推理加速量化的研究者，提供了与现有 grid-search/梯度方法互补的理论工具与可计算的诊断量，是直接相关的方法学参考；不过其经验验证目前仍停留在小型视觉模型，尚未在真实 LLM 与真实 token 激活上证实迁移性。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the GP-fold advantage over the SmoothQuant α-grid persist — and by how much — on real LLM token activations and at weights of 7B–70B scale, where activation-outlier structure differs sharply from 8×8 digit features?
- How large is the dither-vs-deterministic-RTN ordering gap on production hardware RTN, and can the Ξ diagnostic's per-product threshold be calibrated automatically and cheaply during standard calibration?
- Can the copy-vs-error trade-off curves (Fig. 12) be turned into an actual runtime/bandwidth cost model on a target kernel, and does block-GP with n_opp>1 ever beat a single shared rotation once memory traffic is counted?
- The exact identity assumes independent zero-mean errors; how badly does it degrade under **grouped** per-channel INT quantization where errors share a scale and are correlated within a group?
- Is the joint (transform, clip-threshold) problem truly intractable convexly, or can the diagonal surrogate be tightened enough to make an alternating scheme provably converge?
- Do the rotation–fold composition rules (Section 6.2) predict that a rotation *hurts* a subsequent fold on realistic weights, and if so, should deployed pipelines skip rotation when slice-energy covariance already favors a fold?
