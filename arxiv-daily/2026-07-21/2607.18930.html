Functional Equivalence and Geometric Diversity in Neural Network Approximations: An Empirical Characterization

Anuragine S A · Prem Jagadeesan / Amrita School of Artificial Intelligence, Coimbatore / 21 Jul 2026 / 31-page preprint · MATLAB code released
Key Finding Vastly different neural architectures give functionally indistinguishable fits to elementary functions — yet their parameter-space geometry stays low-dimensional, “sloppy,” and nearly capacity-invariant.

§ 01

Methodology

characterization study · no SOTA baseline

Framed as a foundational empirical characterization, not a head-to-head benchmark. Two experiments are run, both fitting elementary univariate targets (sin, cos, exp, quadratic, cubic) on 300 uniformly sampled training points with an independent 200-point test set, MSE loss, SGD, and multiple random seeds.

(1) Shallow nets — single hidden layer, 1–75 neurons, tanh / Sigmoid / ReLU. Top-K models with NRMSE < ε are retained as the equivalence class. (2) MLPs — 3,905 architectures enumerated over depth 1–5 and width 1–5 neurons/layer, repeated under noise-free and 25% Gaussian-noise (12.04 dB SNR) conditions; complexity is measured in FLOPs.

Geometric analysis rests on the Hessian of the least-squares loss at the optimum (central finite differences, symmetrized). From each Hessian the authors extract the eigenvalue spectrum, entropy-based effective rank, and singular-value ratio as sloppiness indicators. Functional equivalence is defined as ‖f_θ1 − f_θ2‖_F < ε, assessed via R² and NRMSE.

Finally the Prem-Anu model index MI = Np·(1 + 1/r_eff) + log F combines parameter count, effective rank, and FLOPs; Conjecture 1 states that the MI-minimizing model within an equivalence class is optimal in parsimony, estimation ease, and inference energy. MATLAB code, datasets, and saved workspaces are released (Supplementary S10).

§ 03

Strengths & Limitations

side-by-side

+ Strengths

  • Clean, well-posed question — identifiability of NN approximators, with a precise mathematical framing of equivalence classes.
  • Thorough within scope — 5 targets × 3 activations × single-layer + MLP, noise-free and noisy, all with released code and workspaces.
  • Reusable diagnostic — effective rank and singular-value ratio are computable on any trained model.
  • Honest self-positioning — MI explicitly labelled heuristic; Conjecture 1 left unproven; scope limits acknowledged.

− Limitations

  • Extremely narrow scope — only univariate elementary functions; no real data, no multivariate, no Transformers/KANs, no classification.
  • No baselines — pruning, Lottery Ticket, distillation, LoRA, KAN all motivated then never benchmarked.
  • MI & Conjecture 1 unjustified — beyond one visualization; never validated on a downstream task.
  • Threshold sensitivity — class size hinges on arbitrary R² cutoffs (0.99 vs 0.94) that differ across noise regimes.
  • Unexplained activation effect — order-of-magnitude ReLU-vs-tanh effective-rank gap reported but not analyzed.
  • Toy compute scale — whether the ~0.25 bound holds at scale is unknown.
§ 04

Open Questions

for further exploration
1
Does the r_eff / parameter ≤ 0.25 bound survive multivariate regression or image/language tasks, or is it an artifact of univariate smooth targets?
2
Why does ReLU produce effective ranks 1–2 orders of magnitude larger than tanh/sigmoid? Is it kink non-smoothness — and does it break the “invariant geometry” claim?
3
Can Conjecture 1 be proven under any assumptions, or validated by showing MI-minimizers actually win on generalization gap, calibration, or energy?
4
How does the Prem-Anu MI compare against parameter-only, FLOPs-only, AIC/BIC, or Lottery Ticket winning tickets on the same class?
5
Does the class stay sloppy under modern optimizers (AdamW) and losses (cross-entropy) rather than MSE/SGD?
6
Under heavy noise (e.g. 0 dB SNR), does the geometry eventually stop being sloppy — or does the low-dimensional structure survive all realistic perturbations?
