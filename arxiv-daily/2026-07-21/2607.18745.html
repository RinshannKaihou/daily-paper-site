Why this matters

Critical edge
Validation is a single tiny 8×8-digit ViT; the central motivating LLM domain is unaddressed empirically — and dither-derived objectives can flip transform ordering under deterministic hardware RTN.
How it works

01 Methodology — one objective scores every transform

A single pipeline scores every candidate by one quantity: the expected squared product error E‖ÂB̂−AB‖². The chain is choose gauge T → form (Ã=AT, B̃=T⁻¹B) → grouping sets ranges R_G → variance field v_G = c·R_G² → expected error via the exact identity. The identity weights each operand's error by the opposite factor's energy and adds a bilinear O(c²) cross term. Three orthogonal axes feed the chain:

structural familypositive-diagonal folds O(K) · orthogonal rotations (Householder, O(tK)) · block-diagonal hierarchies
gauge patternshared domain n_opp=1 · block-constant n_opp=g · row-local benchmark n_opp=m — tracks representation reuse, not per-product error
quantizerbit allocation · clipping threshold · rounding rule (subtractive dither / stochastic / deterministic RTN)

For folds, the objective plus range-epigraph variables is a geometric program solved to global optimality in the log domain; an LP over directional derivatives certifies whether the identity fold is already optimal. For rotations, coherence factors bound the gain of any orthogonal transform (a single shared randomized Hadamard controls all block vectors within a log factor). For hierarchies, slice-energy anti-correlation decides whether block-diagonal refinement beats a flat rotation, and a telescoping identity picks the surrogate-optimal depth.

What lands

03 Strengths

  • Exact, assumption-explicit error accounting that cleanly separates proven identities (dither / stochastic, GP global optimality, LP identity-fold test) from bounded or empirical ones.
  • The gauge-domain / n_opp decomposition makes the "reuse cost" of block-specific transforms first-class — most prior LLM-quant work treats it only implicitly.
  • Five cheap, interpretable decision statistics (tail index, profile spread, coherence, weighted-Gram spectrum, slice-energy covariance), each mapping to one transform family.
  • Honest framing: upper bounds labeled as upper bounds; a concrete counterexample quantifies the ~4.22% heuristic gap; explicit open-problem section.
  • Generous reproducibility appendix (Appendix F: code, seeds, checksums) and a fair, comprehensive related-work sweep.
What's missing

04 Limitations

  • Only scalar quantization is treated; vector / lattice quantizers and the full GL(K) family with condition-number constraints are out of scope.
  • Validation is a single small vision classifier (8×8 digits, width-64 ViT). No LLM, no real-token activations — the central motivating domain is unaddressed.
  • Exactness needs stochastic rounding / dither; deterministic RTN (the dominant deployed mode) is only approximated — with a concrete counterexample where RTN reorders transforms.
  • n_opp is a reuse descriptor only; runtime, bandwidth, storage costs need separate platform kernel measurements, which are not provided.
  • Clipped-error analysis uses a diagonal surrogate; the joint (transform, threshold) convex formulation is left open.
  • Several rules (coherence, partial-rotation, hierarchy) give upper-bound comparisons, not realized-error guarantees.
Open frontiers

05 Questions for further exploration

  1. Does the GP-fold advantage over the SmoothQuant α-grid persist — and by how much — on real LLM token activations at 7B–70B scale, where outlier structure differs sharply from 8×8 digits?
  2. How large is the dither-vs-RTN ordering gap on production hardware, and can the Ξ diagnostic's per-product threshold be auto-calibrated cheaply during standard calibration?
  3. Can the copy-vs-error curves become an actual runtime/bandwidth cost model, and does block-GP with n_opp>1 ever beat a single shared rotation once memory traffic is counted?
  4. How badly does the exact identity degrade under grouped per-channel INT quantization, where errors share a scale and correlate within a group?
  5. Is the joint (transform, clip-threshold) problem truly intractable convexly, or can the diagonal surrogate be tightened enough for a provably-convergent alternating scheme?
  6. Do the rotation–fold composition rules predict that a rotation hurts a subsequent fold on realistic weights — should pipelines skip rotation when slice-energy covariance already favors a fold?
Verdict
A rigorous, theory-forward framework that genuinely advances certified fold selection and unifies transform-family choices under one objective. Its practical impact hinges on the (currently missing) LLM-scale RTN validation and on linking n_opp to measured kernel cost — a strong methods contribution, not yet a drop-in replacement for SmoothQuant-class recipes.
