> **一句话总结：** XPENG Robotics 提出 Athena-Brain-8B,一个通过"通用 SFT + 通用 RL + 具身专家训练 + 模型合并"四阶段后训练流水线打造的 8B 机器人脑模型,在通用推理基准上保持与 Qwen3-8B 相当的水平,同时在内域具身交互任务(Museum Tour / Supermarket Shopping)上以 58.52% 总成功率大幅领先所有同规模开源模型并超过 Qwen3-Max,且每步交互仅需 24.25 token,实现了能力与效率的平衡。

> **TL;DR:** Athena-Brain-8B is an 8B LLM from XPENG Robotics engineered as an on-device "robot brain" via a four-stage post-training pipeline (General SFT → General RL with a token-budget reward → Embodied Expert Training on Museum/Supermarket text environments → lineage-aware TIES+linear Model Merge). On general benchmarks it matches or slightly beats Qwen3-8B (Overall 60.85 vs 56.70) while emitting far fewer tokens; on the in-domain embodied interaction suite it reaches 58.52% overall success rate versus 14.0% for Qwen3-8B-Thinking and 43.3% for the much larger Qwen3-Max, and it transfers zero-shot to ALFWorld at 36.57% (up from 11.94% for the SFT checkpoint) — at a per-interaction cost of only 24.25 tokens (Token Cost 0.41 vs 36.81 for Qwen3-8B-Thinking).

## 核心贡献

- 提出一个面向"设备端机器人脑"的统一四阶段后训练流水线(General SFT、General RL、Embodied Expert Training、Model Merge),首次将通用语言智能、高效推理与具身闭环交互融合进单个 8B 模型,而非在专用具身模型与通用 LLM 之间二选一。
- 在 General RL 阶段引入 token-budget reward(配合 GRPO、16K rollout 上限),在不损失复杂推理准确率的前提下显式压缩推理链长度,把"推理效率"作为与准确率并列的一阶优化目标。
- 构建了两个可执行的文本交互环境(Museum Tour 公共服务、Supermarket Shopping 家政服务),并用统一的 observation-action 协议与四级递进具身层级(E0 区域级 → E3 自我中心视锥)构成课程式训练与可复现评估。
- 提出 lineage-aware 两阶段模型合并:对同源于 Athena-SFT 的 RL 专家用 TIES 合并,对来自不同谱系的 SFT checkpoint 用低权重线性插值注入,并从参数空间奇异谱/子空间几何上论证了二者必须分别处理。
- 设计 State-Goal 与 State-Return 两种中间探针(probe),把端到端成功率拆解为"近期目标完成"与"部分可观测下的空间回返"两个互补能力,提供了比单一 SR 更细的诊断视角。

## Key Contributions

- A unified four-stage post-training pipeline (General SFT → General RL → Embodied Expert Training → Model Merge) that, for the first time at 8B scale, jointly preserves broad LLM intelligence, acquires closed-loop embodied interaction, and enforces concise generation inside one checkpoint — instead of trading one off against another.
- A token-budget reward combined with GRPO (16K rollout cap) that explicitly optimizes reasoning efficiency as a first-class objective, demonstrably shortening reasoning chains without harming hard-task accuracy (ablated across 4K/8K/16K budgets).
- Two executable text-based interactive environments (Museum Tour, Supermarket Shopping) sharing one observation-action protocol and a four-level embodiment curriculum (E0 zone-level → E3 egocentric view-cone), used for both training and reproducible programmatic evaluation.
- A lineage-aware, two-stage parameter-space merge: TIES for same-origin RL experts anchored at Athena-SFT, and conservative low-weight linear interpolation for a different-lineage SFT checkpoint — justified by singular-spectrum / subspace-rotation analysis showing the two update regimes have incompatible geometries.
- Two diagnostic intermediate probes — State-Goal (near-future goal completion) and State-Return (return to a previously visited location under partial observability) — that decompose end-to-end success into complementary cognitive abilities and reveal that Athena's gains come mainly from goal-directed execution plus robust late-episode spatial recovery.

## Methodology

Athena-Brain-8B starts from the pretrained **Qwen3-8B-Base** and applies four sequential post-training stages, each feeding the next.

1. **General SFT (two substages).** Stage 1 is a large-scale SFT over a weighted corpus of ~5.58M examples (Math 47.6%, General 21.0%, Code 18.2%, Tool use 5.6%, Science 5.3%, Chinese QA 2.4%) to build broad capability. Stage 2 is a smaller, curated refinement emphasizing reasoning consistency, instruction-following, tool use, agent interaction, and multilingual quality. The two substages use independent optimization schedules so Stage 2 acts as a dedicated quality enhancer rather than continued training.

2. **General RL.** Trained with **GRPO** using (a) task-specific correctness rewards on verifiable tasks (math, science MCQA, code) and (b) a **token-budget reward** that rewards correct answers reached with fewer tokens. Max generation budget is capped at 16K tokens (chosen via the 4K/8K/16K ablation: 4K hurts hard tasks, larger budgets weaken the length regularizer). Training dynamics show rewards rising while mean response length falls — concise reasoning emerges as a side effect.

3. **Embodied Expert Training.** Two executable text environments are built: **Museum Tour** (public-service navigation/visitor assistance in a large indoor space) and **Supermarket Shopping** (object grounding, mobile manipulation, checkout in an object-rich store). Both share one observation-action loop and are organized into four embodiment levels (E0 zone topology → E1 local topology/orientation → E2 continuous position with coarse observations → E3 continuous heading with egocentric view-cone), forming a curriculum. Training = 4K-step supervised warm-up on collected trajectories, then GRPO with environment rewards (Museum: target-visit progress/completion; Supermarket: cart correctness/checkout success). Rollouts stop on task completion, a 100-step episode limit, or (under the chosen format-only-stop rule) a protocol-level formatting failure; unknown/locked tools and executable skill failures stay recoverable.

4. **Model Merging (lineage-aware, two-stage).**
   - *Stage 1 — TIES.* Domain-specialized RL experts (agent/tool-use, science, code, embodied, instruction-following) all share the Athena-SFT initialization. Their task vectors τ_i = θ_i − θ_AthenaSFT are merged with **TIES** (trim low-magnitude, elect aggregate sign, merge aligned entries), giving θ_stage1 = θ_AthenaSFT + τ_TIES.
   - *Stage 2 — linear correction.* A complementary SFT checkpoint from a *different* training lineage is added via conservative low-weight linear interpolation. The authors show this checkpoint's displacement is much larger and its re-anchored update is poorly aligned with RL-derived directions (stronger spectrum/subspace changes), so putting it in the TIES vote pool caused severe degradation; linear interpolation instead transfers complementary capability without disturbing the local geometry.
   - Merge coefficients are chosen for overall general+embodied balance, not single-benchmark maximization.

Evaluation uses a unified protocol: thinking mode on, max 40,960 tokens, YaRN context extension where needed, temperature 0.6 / top-p 0.95 / top-k 20; AIME 2024/2025 averaged over 8 runs. Embodied evaluation requires a single structured JSON tool call per step; success = reaching target + completing tour (Museum) or correct cart + checkout (Supermarket), 100-step cap.

## Results & Highlights

- **General capability (Table 4, Overall Average):** Athena-Brain-8B scores **60.85**, beating Athena-SFT (58.56), Qwen3-8B (56.70), MiMo-Embodied-7B (44.73), MiniCPM4.1-8B (39.67), and RynnBrain-8B (27.99). On AIME 2025 it reaches **70.83** vs Qwen3-8B's 68.75 (+2.08); on BeyondAIME **51.00** vs 43.00 (+8.00); on LiveCodeBench v6 **54.63** vs 53.08; on Arena-Hard v2 **48.21** vs 37.73 (+10.48).
- **Reasoning efficiency (Fig. 8):** Athena-Brain-8B dominates tokens-per-score-point in every category — e.g. Math 186.2 vs Athena-SFT 369.4 vs MiMo-Embodied-7B 1159.8; overall mean 106.8 tokens/score point vs Qwen3-8B 124.8, Athena-SFT 144.3, MiMo-Embodied-7B 182.4.
- **In-domain embodied success rate (Table 5, Overall):** Athena-Brain-8B **58.52%** vs the next-best compact open-weight model Qwen3-8B-Thinking at **14.0%**, Athena-SFT 6.98%, MiMo-Embodied-7B 3.71%, RynnBrain-8B 4.13%, MiniCPM4.1-8B 0.32%. It also surpasses the much larger **Qwen3-Max (43.3%)** and Qwen3.7-Max (54.1%), and approaches frontier API models (GPT-5.5 79.6%, Gemini-3.1-Pro 71.1%). On Museum E0 it reaches 93.0% (vs Qwen3-Max 49.0%).
- **Embodied reasoning efficiency (Table 6):** Athena-Brain-8B uses only **24.25 avg tokens/interaction** with Token Cost **0.41**, versus Qwen3-8B-Thinking's 515.38 tokens and Token Cost 36.81 — roughly two orders of magnitude lower cost per unit of task performance.
- **Out-of-domain transfer (Table 7, ALFWorld unseen split, zero-shot):** Athena-Brain-8B **36.57%** vs Athena-SFT 11.94% (+24.63), competitive with MiniCPM4.1-8B (40.30%) and well above Qwen3-8B-NonThinking (20.15%), MiMo-Embodied-7B (20.90%), RynnBrain-8B (11.19%) — despite being trained on only two environments.
- **Intermediate probes (Table 9):** State-Goal improves from Athena-SFT's 6.0% to **59.3%** (beating all open-weight 7B–8B baselines by a wide margin); on late-episode (turn 41+) State-Return, Athena-Brain-8B retains **32.4%**, the strongest among open-weight 7B–8B models (vs Qwen3-8B-Thinking 19.7%).
- **Merge analysis (Table 11):** Athena-Brain-8B matches or beats the per-benchmark best expert on most general tasks (e.g. LiveCodeBench v6 54.63 vs best expert 49.78, +4.85; Arena-Hard v2 48.21 vs 43.26, +4.95) while retaining 58.52% in-domain embodied (vs embodied-RL expert 60.11, −1.59) and even exceeding the embodied expert on ALFWorld OOD.
- **Embodied RL ablation (Table 12):** best config = partial maps + mild reward + format-only-stop → **0.6000** overall vs pre-RL baseline 0.5145 (+0.0855); format-only-stop beats all-error-stop because preserving recoverable-error trajectories improves the final policy even when training-time completion is slightly lower.

## Authors & Credibility

This is a technical report from **XPENG Robotics** (the robotics arm of XPENG / Xiaopeng). Lead authors Jialian Li, Junhong Liu, and corresponding author Jie Chen (chenj81@xiaopeng.com). It is an industry lab report, not a peer-reviewed academic paper — it has the strengths (engineering depth, full pipeline, large ablations) and the weaknesses (no independent review, benchmark choices controlled by the authors, self-reported numbers) typical of that genre. Evaluation is run on the authors' own **DeepInsight** platform (cited as ref [34], itself a 2026 arXiv report by overlapping authors), which is a reproducibility caveat.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-good. The individual ingredients (GRPO, token-budget rewards, TIES merging, text-world RL) are all borrowed; the contribution is the *integration recipe* and the lineage-aware merge rationale (TIES for same-origin RL, linear interpolation for cross-lineage SFT, justified by singular-spectrum analysis). The lineage-aware merge argument is the most genuinely novel piece.
- **Rigor:** Reasonable on the general side (8-run averaging on AIME, unified decoding protocol, efficiency measured as tokens-per-score-point) but weak on the embodied side. Crucially, **all external embodied baselines are evaluated zero-shot on environments that Athena was explicitly trained on** — the authors flag this, but it means the headline "58.52% vs 14.0%" is not an apples-to-apples capability comparison; it is partly a train-on-test-env artifact. The fairer comparison is ALFWorld (unseen to everyone), where the gap shrinks dramatically (36.57% vs MiniCPM4.1-8B 40.30% — Athena actually *loses* to MiniCPM4.1-8B there).
- **Reproducibility:** Mixed. Decoding configs, budget, and ablation axes are specified. But the Museum/Supermarket environments and their maps are proprietary and not released, the "different-lineage SFT checkpoint" used in Stage-2 merge is not identified, exact merge coefficients are not given, and the probe trajectories are generated by Gemini-3.1-Pro on a held-out split — all of which block independent reproduction. Evaluation runs on the authors' own DeepInsight platform.
- **Significance:** The demonstration that an 8B model can be competitive on embodied text interaction while keeping general capability and short outputs is useful for the on-device-robot-brain thesis. But the result is confined to *text-based* environments; no physical simulator, no real robot, no vision-language perception is involved, so the transfer to actual embodied AI is unproven and explicitly deferred to future work.
- **Red flags:**
  - The named embodied baselines (RynnBrain-8B at 4.13% overall, MiMo-Embodied-7B at 3.71%, MiniCPM4.1-8B at 0.32%) look suspiciously broken — near-zero scores suggest these models may not have been given environment-specific tool/API formatting, making the comparison flattering to Athena rather than informative.
  - General-purpose benchmark single-run results (everything except AIME) are reported with no variance, yet the authors themselves note "stochastic decoding may introduce noticeable variance."
  - The frontier API models (GPT-5.5, Gemini-3.1-Pro, etc.) cited with future-version numbers and URLs dated 2026 cannot be independently verified at the time of this report's appearance.
  - Athena-Brain-8B *underperforms* its own embodied-RL expert on in-domain overall (58.52 vs 60.11) — i.e. the merge slightly *costs* embodied capability, somewhat undercutting the "unified model" framing.

## Strengths

- Genuinely tackles all three objectives (general intelligence, embodied interaction, generation efficiency) in one 8B model instead of picking one, with a clean four-stage decomposition that makes each stage's role legible.
- The token-budget reward + efficiency-as-first-class-metric framing is exactly right for latency-constrained robot deployment, and the per-category tokens-per-score-point analysis makes the efficiency claim concrete and falsifiable.
- Strong, systematic ablations: rollout-budget (4K/8K/16K), and a 3-axis embodied-RL ablation (truncation × reward × map sampling) with separate training-dynamics tables — unusually thorough for an industry report.
- The State-Goal / State-Return probe design is a thoughtful diagnostic that goes beyond a single success-rate number and honestly surfaces where the model is still weak (long-range spatial memory).
- The lineage-aware merge is well motivated by actual parameter-space geometry, not just empirics.

## Limitations

- **Only two text-based training/eval environments** (Museum, Supermarket); the authors themselves concede scale and diversity are limited. No physical simulation, no vision, no real robot — purely symbolic observation-action loops.
- **Train-on-eval-env confound:** Athena is trained on Museum/Supermarket while every baseline is zero-shot there, inflating the in-domain gap; the honest OOD signal (ALFWorld) shows a much smaller, and not always winning, advantage.
- **Possibly crippled baselines:** near-zero embodied scores for MiniCPM4.1-8B / MiMo-Embodied-7B / RynnBrain-8B hint at tool-protocol mismatch rather than true capability differences.
- **Non-reproducible artifacts:** proprietary environments/maps, undisclosed merge coefficients and the unnamed "different-lineage SFT" checkpoint, evaluation on the authors' own DeepInsight platform.
- **State-Return ceiling:** absolute spatial-recovery accuracy (43.8%) lags frontier models (Gemini-3.1-Pro 88.0%) and even Qwen3-8B-Thinking (50.8%); long-range spatial memory and self-localization remain bottlenecks the paper does not solve.
- **Merge trades off embodied skill:** the merged model (58.52) is slightly worse in-domain than the embodied-RL expert (60.11), so "unified" comes at a small embodied cost.

## Relevance

与"机器人脑 / 具身智能 / 端侧 LLM 后训练"方向高度相关:其把通用推理、闭环具身交互与生成长度效率统一进单个 8B 模型的思路,以及 token-budget reward、lineage-aware TIES+线性合并、State-Goal/State-Return 探针等方法,对研究"高效可部署的具身决策模型"和"模型合并机理"都有直接借鉴价值;但需注意其评测局限于自建文本环境、基线为零样本,迁移到真实机器人尚未验证。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How much of the in-domain 58.52% success survives when baselines are given the same environment-specific tool schema and a few-shot warm-up, i.e. is the gap capability or protocol-formatting?
- Can the token-budget reward + 16K cap recipe transfer to vision-language-action models operating on real sensor streams, or is the conciseness gain specific to symbolic text interaction?
- What is the identity and contribution decomposition of the "different-lineage SFT checkpoint" — how much does Stage-2 linear interpolation actually buy versus Stage-1 TIES alone?
- Would scaling the environment set (more maps, more task types, vision input) close the State-Return gap to frontier models, or is long-range spatial memory a fundamental architectural limit of 8B LLMs?
- The merge slightly hurts in-domain embodied performance (58.52 vs expert 60.11) — is there a merge operator (e.g. task-vector arithmetic with embodied-aware weighting) that preserves both general gains and the full embodied expert?
- Does the ALFWorld result (where MiniCPM4.1-8B beats Athena) indicate that broader/diverse pretraining matters more than deep specialization on two environments for OOD transfer?
