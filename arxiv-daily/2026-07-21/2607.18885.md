> **一句话总结：** 针对核方法 CLIP→DINOv2 对齐（KUEA）在 CC12M 网络规模数据下对齐损失被"清洁损失"压制至约 0.2%、梯度几乎失效的问题，作者提出 KALE 控制器：用两个损失的 EMA 比率自适应地把对齐权重 λa 驱向目标比 τ（约 4 个数量级的提升、且随配置变化），在 330 万张 CC12M 图像上训练后零样本 11 数据集平均比 CLIP 提升 +2.00（超过 KUEA 的 +1.29），且保留图文检索能力。

> **TL;DR:** KUEA's fixed-weight kernel alignment of CLIP toward a DINOv2 teacher is shown to be inert at web scale — the alignment term shrinks to ~0.2% of the clean loss on a 3.3M-subset of CC12M. The proposed KALE controller adaptively rescales the alignment weight λa (escalating it by ~10⁴×, peaking at ~16,749×) to hold the loss ratio at target τ=0.8, yielding +2.00 zero-shot gain over CLIP on the 11-dataset average (vs. KUEA's +1.29), preserving image–text retrieval (+1.54 vs. KUEA's +1.63), and improving SVHN linear probing (+5.73 vs. KUEA's +4.19).

## 核心贡献

- 揭示并量化了 KUEA 固定权重对齐在网页规模数据上失效的根因：在 CC12M 上对齐损失项的加权贡献降至清洁损失的约 0.2%（ρ≈2×10⁻³，逐步骤可降至 10⁻⁵），对齐梯度几乎完全惰性；而要恢复平衡需把权重提高约 7,000×–47,000×，且该值随学习率/调度而变化，没有任何固定标量能通用。
- 提出 KALE 控制器：对两个损失维护 EMA，每 Δ=100 步依据比率 ρ=(λa·ℓ_align)/(λc·ℓ_clean) 以平方根阻尼 λa←λa·√(τ/ρ) 更新，将 λa 驱向不动点 λ⋆a=τ·λc·ℓ_clean/ℓ_align；给出了几何收缩（每步因子 1/2）与稳定性分析，无需逐数据集调参。
- 系统刻画了网络规模对齐的优化区间：需要较高的峰值学习率（1×10⁻⁴）以便优化器跟上对齐压力，2×10⁻⁴ 即崩溃（漂移失控、有效秩从 ~106 崩至 ~27）；并且需要带中等非零底部的余弦衰减调度，平坦调度会让 CLIP 漂移无界增长（漂移从 3.6 升至 6.2）。
- 在 CC12M-3.3M 上达成与 ImageNet 训练 KUEA 不同的下游工作点：保留检索、提升 SVHN 线性探测、提升标准零样本均值（+2.00 对比 KUEA 的 +1.29），并报告了 7 次重复的逐运行方差，仅基于跨运行稳定的指标（检索、零样本、SVHN）下结论。

## Key Contributions

- Identifies and quantifies why KUEA's fixed-weight kernel alignment breaks at web scale: on CC12M the weighted alignment term falls to ~0.2% of the clean loss (ρ≈2×10⁻³, decaying to 10⁻⁵ at individual steps), making its gradient effectively inert; restoring balance requires raising the weight by ~7,000×–47,000×, and the needed magnitude is configuration-dependent so no single fixed scalar suffices.
- Introduces the KALE controller, which maintains EMAs of both losses and, every Δ=100 steps, updates the alignment weight via square-root damping λa←λa·√(τ/ρ) to drive the loss ratio toward target τ; provides a closed-form fixed point λ⋆a=τ·λc·ℓ_clean/ℓ_align and a geometric-contraction analysis (factor 1/2 per update), with no per-dataset tuning.
- Characterizes the web-scale optimization regime empirically: a high peak LR (1×10⁻⁴) is needed for the optimizer to track escalating alignment pressure, with a sharp collapse at 2×10⁻⁴ (drift ≥15, effective rank crashing ~106→27); a decaying schedule with a moderate non-zero floor (not flat) keeps CLIP drift bounded, whereas a flat LR lets drift grow unchecked (3.6→6.2).
- Reaches a distinct downstream operating point from ImageNet-trained KUEA on a 3.3M CC12M subset — retrieval preserved, SVHN linear probing improved, standard zero-shot average improved over CLIP (+2.00 vs. KUEA's +1.29) — without per-dataset weight tuning, and reports explicit run-to-run variance over seven replicates, basing conclusions only on metrics stable across runs.

## Methodology

The work builds on KUEA (Gong et al., 2025), which aligns a CLIP visual student f_θ toward a frozen DINOv2 teacher g in kernel space, regularised toward the frozen original CLIP encoder f_0 by a clean loss. For a batch of n images, KUEA forms degree-3 normalized polynomial kernel matrices in each embedding space and minimizes their mean-squared discrepancy (L_align, Eq. 2), while the clean loss L_clean (Eq. 3) is the mean L2 distance between student and original-CLIP embeddings. The total objective is L = λc·L_clean + λa·L_align, with λc=1.0 fixed.

The KALE controller (subsection 3.2) replaces the fixed λa. It maintains exponential moving averages ℓ̂_align, ℓ̂_clean (decay α=0.99), and every Δ=100 steps (after a warmup W) computes the current ratio ρ = (λa·ℓ̂_align)/(λc·ℓ̂_clean) and updates λa ← λa·√(τ/ρ). Square-root damping is chosen over p=1 (which overshoots under noisy/moving losses) and p>2 (which diverges): with general exponent p the contraction factor is |1−p|, so p=1/2 gives monotone, robust convergence. The fixed point sets the alignment contribution to exactly τ times the clean contribution; deviations contract geometrically (u(t+1)−u⋆ = ½(u(t)−u⋆) in log-weight coordinates), so λa converges from any positive initialization without oscillation. Because in practice ℓ̂_align falls as the student aligns while ℓ̂_clean first rises then recedes, λ⋆a is a moving target the controller tracks rather than a constant.

Setup: CLIP ViT-L/14 (OpenAI) student, DINOv2 ViT-L/14 with registers as frozen teacher, frozen CLIP text encoder (so zero-shot stays valid), images only from a fixed 3.3M CC12M subset with precomputed/cached CLIP and DINOv2 embeddings. Training: AdamW (β1=0.9, β2=0.999, wd 1e-4), 8× H100 80 GB (AWS p5.48xlarge), per-GPU batch 128 (effective 1024), 10% linear warmup, cosine decay with 10% LR floor by default, 2 epochs, bfloat16. KALE config: Δ=100, τ=0.8, α=0.99, λa(0)=0.5. Kernel params (γ=0.0032, c0=0.1916) initialized by minimizing L2 between CLIP/DINOv2 kernels on a small ImageNet sample, then learnable. Baselines: original CLIP ViT-L/14 and the published KUEA ImageNet-1K checkpoint (KUEA zero-shot/LP from published tables; KUEA retrieval reproduced in-house). Evaluation uses the CLIP_benchmark suite: zero-shot on 11 datasets, linear probing on SVHN/GTSRB/CLEVR-Dist/CLEVR-Count, retrieval R@1/5/10 on Flickr30K and MSCOCO.

## Results & Highlights

- Zero-shot (standard 11-dataset average, ImageNet-1K excluded, used only as in-training selection signal): CLIP baseline 65.26, KUEA 66.54 (+1.29), KALE 67.26 (+2.00) — KALE exceeds KUEA's published gain by +0.71 while training on noisier web data; run-to-run σ = 0.41 over seven replicates.
- Image–text retrieval (Flickr30K + MSCOCO R@1/5/10, CLIP absolute reference 55.5): KALE +1.54 vs. KUEA's in-house-reproduced +1.63 — retrieval essentially preserved on noisy CC12M, with σ = 0.10 (the most stable metric).
- SVHN linear probing (best run): CLIP 65.20, KUEA 69.39 (+4.19), KALE 70.93 (+5.73); this gain is reproducible — all seven replicates land between +4.0 and +6.7 (σ = 0.99).
- Controller escalation magnitude: KALE raises λa by ~7,000× to 47,000× over training (from 0.5 to ~3×10³–2×10⁴), peaking at ~16,749× around step 2,400 before declining as the alignment loss shrinks; the fixed-weight counterfactual ratio ρ stays near 0 throughout.
- Stability envelope (single run per LR): 4×10⁻⁵ stable (drift 2.9, ZS +1.42), 8×10⁻⁵ stable (drift 5.1), 1×10⁻⁴ headline (drift 5.3, ZS +2.00), 2×10⁻⁴ collapses (drift 18.1, effective rank ~106→~27, all metrics below CLIP).
- Schedule floor sweep (single run): flat LR (100% floor) → end-of-training drift 6.2; cosine→0 → drift 2.9; moderate floors 10–25% → drift 2.9–3.3 — flat schedules degrade performance whereas moderate non-zero floors keep drift bounded.
- Training duration: 2 epochs (ZS +2.00, Ret +1.54, LP +7.30) beats 4 epochs (ZS +0.03, Ret +1.59, LP +3.78) — extending training does not help and best checkpoints occur well before the maximum budget.
- Per-dataset standouts: PCAM zero-shot jumps from CLIP 52.00 → KALE 67.12 (highly volatile, attributed to DINOv2 texture features transferring to the histopathology task rather than CC12M medical content); CLEVR-Dist LP +12.32 vs. KUEA's +7.85 and CLEVR-Count LP +9.71 vs. +8.42, but these carry large variance (σ ≈ 4.3 and 5.3); EuroSAT zero-shot regresses (CLIP 62.65 → KALE 59.52).

## Authors & Credibility

Single-author paper by Michał Pawłowicz. The methodology is unusually transparent about uncertainty for this subfield: rather than cherry-picking one run, the author reports best-run numbers alongside run-to-run standard deviation across seven identically-configured replicates, explicitly distinguishes training nondeterminism from a hyperparameter sweep, verifies the learning rate is identical across replicates at the selected checkpoint, and restricts headline conclusions to metrics stable across runs (retrieval σ=0.10, zero-shot σ=0.41, SVHN σ=0.99). The CLIP baseline is reproduced exactly (zero-shot 65.26, LP 50.59) matching KUEA's published numbers, making gains over CLIP directly comparable. Compute (8× H100 80 GB, AWS p5.48xlarge), seeds, and data subset are specified, and the controller convergence rule for checkpoint selection is fixed and training-side-only (no per-run test-set tuning).

## Critical Assessment

- Novelty: Moderate and honestly scoped. The KALE controller is a sensible, well-analyzed instance of relative-loss-ratio balancing (distinct from GradNorm's gradient-norm equalization and DWA's rate-of-change weighting, which the author correctly notes target different problems), but it is a single-parameter update rule rather than a new representation-learning idea — the core representation lever (kernel CLIP→DINOv2 alignment) is inherited wholesale from KUEA.
- Rigor: Strong on variance reporting and counterfactual analysis; the analytical derivation of the fixed point and contraction rate is clean and consistent with the empirical equilibration. However, a key causal claim is under-supported: the fixed-weight baseline was not evaluated downstream at CC12M scale, so the paper cannot isolate whether KALE's downstream gains come from the controller or simply from training on 3.3M web-crawled CC12M images instead of curated ImageNet-1K (the author explicitly flags this gap). The comparison to KUEA thus conflates method (controller) with data regime (CC12M vs. ImageNet).
- Reproducibility: Reasonable. Hyperparameters, hardware, LR schedule, EMA decay, kernel initialisation values (γ=0.0032, c0=0.1916), and checkpoint-selection rule (ρ reaching τ, plus a CLIP-drift≤8 / effective-rank≥90 validity filter) are all given. Weaknesses: training runs without a fixed random seed or deterministic kernels, which is exactly why CLEVR linear-probing variance (σ ≈ 4.3–5.3) is so large; the collapse boundary (2×10⁻⁴) is from single runs so its sharpness is not variance-bounded; only a 3.3M CC12M subset is used, not full CC12M or DataComp.
- Significance: Practically useful for anyone scaling auxiliary-loss finetuning of CLIP — the demonstration that a "robust" fixed weight becomes inert by ~3 orders of magnitude at web scale, and that the rebalancing value is configuration-dependent, is a concrete, transferable lesson. Broad scientific impact is limited by the narrow scope (one kernel, one teacher, three task families).
- Red flags: Headline numbers are "best single run" for several metrics while the paper simultaneously argues LP variance is too large to trust — the CLEVR gains (e.g., CLEVR-Dist +12.32 vs. KUEA's +7.85) are reported prominently in Table 2/Figure 2 yet carry σ ≈ 4.3, so a reader could be misled unless they read section 6 carefully. The PCAM +15-point zero-shot swing is acknowledged as volatile and its mechanistic attribution is left to future work.

## Strengths

- Diagnoses and quantifies a concrete failure mode (alignment term at ~0.2% of clean loss) with a falsifiable analytical counterfactual, rather than just asserting the fixed weight is suboptimal.
- Clean closed-form controller analysis (fixed point λ⋆a = τ·λc·ℓ_clean/ℓ_align, geometric contraction factor ½) that predicts the observed escalation-and-decline behaviour of λa and the bounded-then-receding CLIP drift.
- Honest, principled variance reporting and a discipline of drawing conclusions only from stable metrics; reproduced CLIP baseline matching KUEA's published numbers.
- Identifies a transferable general lesson: the same equilibration control may help wherever a small-magnitude auxiliary objective must stay influential against a dominant loss.

## Limitations

- The fixed-weight baseline is not run downstream on CC12M, so controller-vs-data attribution is incomplete (explicitly acknowledged).
- Large run-to-run variance on linear probing (LP σ = 2.03; CLEVR tasks σ ≈ 4.3–5.3) due to no fixed seed / no deterministic kernels; single-best-run headline numbers for LP could mislead.
- Controller sensitivity: small deviations from the effective LR/controller regime cause meaningful regressions; robustness across dataset sizes and batch sizes is untested.
- Narrow scope: only a 3.3M CC12M subset (not full CC12M, DataComp, or LAION), only the degree-3 polynomial kernel, only the DINOv2 teacher, and only zero-shot/LP/retrieval evaluation (no dense prediction, VQA, or multimodal-LLM integration).
- Persistent LP-vs-retrieval trade-off: no single model simultaneously maximises both, suggesting the two losses encode competing pressures left unresolved.
- Domain-breadth hypothesis (broader CC12M coverage → gains on remote-sensing/texture/spatial tasks) is not supported — EuroSAT regresses and CLEVR is high-variance.

## Relevance

对关注视觉-语言模型微调与表征改进的工作高度相关：它给出在网页规模数据上让"小幅度辅助损失"保持有效、并防止 CLIP 漂移失控的实用控制器方案与稳定性区间，可迁移到 CLIP/多模态骨干的对齐、蒸馏与多损失训练场景。其逐运行方差透明披露的方法论，也对任何做 CLIP 微调实验的人有借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- What are KALE's downstream numbers when the fixed-weight KUEA baseline is also trained on the identical 3.3M CC12M subset, to cleanly isolate the controller's contribution from the data-regime change?
- Does the equilibration principle generalize to other small-magnitude auxiliary losses against a dominant loss (e.g., dense-pixel or contrastive regularizers in CLIP finetuning, or distillation in multimodal LLMs)?
- How do KALE dynamics and the collapse boundary at 2×10⁻⁴ change when scaling to the full CC12M, DataComp-1B, or LAION, and with different batch sizes?
- Can a controller parametrised by relative training progress or loss ratios (rather than raw step counts) yield more robust behavior across dataset sizes and batch configurations, as the author speculates?
- Would multi-objective or Pareto-aware checkpoint selection resolve the observed linear-probing-vs-retrieval trade-off, instead of choosing a single controller-convergence checkpoint?
- Are the volatile CLEVR and PCAM gains reproducible and genuinely attributable to DINOv2 texture features, once multiple seeded runs per configuration and a controlled per-checkpoint correlation with alignment strength are performed?
