> **一句话总结：** CircuitKIT 是一个面向机制可解释性的源码可用工具库，通过一个类型化的共享 `CircuitScores` 产物把"发现—评估—干预"三阶段流水线串联起来，内置 13 个电路发现算法、6 个忠实性诊断维度、7 个下游应用模块（剪枝/量化/微调/编辑等），并用声明式模板把结构化用户数据（CSV/JSONL/HuggingFace）自动映射为电路发现任务，在六大模型家族上验证并通过案例研究证明"忠实性分数并不预测下游干预效果"。

> **TL;DR:** CircuitKIT is a source-available library that unifies the mechanistic-interpretability pipeline (circuit discovery → faithfulness evaluation → downstream intervention) behind one typed, serializable `CircuitScores` artifact, shipping 13 discovery algorithms across four backends, six complementary faithfulness pillars, and seven application modules (pruning, quantization, selective fine-tuning, knowledge editing, steering, circuit-LoRA healing, hallucination probing). Its declarative template path turns a raw CSV/HuggingFace dataset into a discovery task with zero hand-written pairing code, and on a custom jailbreak-detection dataset on Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct the paired-route circuit recovers 85% of the full-model refusal gap under patching (vs. random subgraph at 1.71× improvement). Critically, its intervention studies show patch faithfulness anti-correlates with pruning retention on Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct/BoolQ (Spearman ρ = −0.78, p = 0.010) — a faithfulness score is evidence about what a circuit explains, not a forecast of what happens when you act on it.

## 核心贡献

- **统一流水线的共享产物**：引入类型化、可序列化的 `CircuitScores` 记录（包含 task/model/algorithm/granularity/per-component scores），让 13 个发现算法、6 个评估维度、7 个应用模块全部通过统一接口生产/消费同一产物——切换发现算法、任务或干预方式只改配置字段，不再写胶水代码。这是相比 TransformerLens、NNsight、Auto-Circuit、MIB、InterpBench 等工具（均只覆盖部分阶段）的核心差异（见 Table 1：仅 CircuitKIT 同时覆盖 Discovery 6/13 + Evaluation 6/6 + Application 7 + 自定义数据）。
- **声明式自定义数据路径**：通过列模板把 CSV/JSONL/HuggingFace 数据集映射为发现任务，自动完成 token 长度对齐、判别标签预计算、数据加载；当用户只有 clean 数据时自动路由到 IBCircuit 或 CD-T（无需 counterfactual）。提供 10 种 corruption 合成策略（entity-swap/token-swap/role-swap/paraphrase/distractor 等）及 GREEN/YELLOW/RED 三级"值得发现"预检门，解决了 attribution 方法对 (clean, corrupt) 配对依赖这一实际瓶颈。
- **多维度忠实性面板而非单分数**：基于"单指标忠实性依赖消融方法甚至可反转排名"（Miller et al. 2024）的发现，报告 6 个互补支柱（P1 causal patching、P2 ablation、P3 stability、P4 robustness、P5 baselines、P6 generalization）加可选 P7 intervention reliability，全部 signed 报告（负值=行为反转而非测量错误）。
- **可操作的干预模块**：电路重要性信号接入既有工具（LLM-Pruner 剪枝、quanto/llmcompressor 量化、PEFT/LoRA 微调、ROME/MEMIT 编辑），并通过 lm-evaluation-harness 标准化基准评测导出 checkpoint，使"定位-行动"闭环可定量比较。
- **诚实的实证发现**：7 项案例研究（E1–E7）不仅验证流水线各阶段，还如实报告电路指导在剪枝中"算法选择比忠实性更重要"、在量化中"patchF 不预测但 ablF 预测保留率"、在选择性微调中"电路掩码不优于等预算随机掩码（null 结果，Δ = −0.001）"等反直觉结论。

## Key Contributions

- **A single shared artifact spanning the full pipeline.** A typed, serializable `CircuitScores` record carries the task descriptor, model id, algorithm, granularity (node or neuron), per-component attribution scores, and discovery config; discovery backends produce it and evaluation/application modules consume it, so swapping discovery algorithm, task, or intervention is a config change rather than glue code. Against TransformerLens (0/0/0), NNsight (0+∼1/+∼1/0), Auto-Circuit (2/+∼2/0), MIB (2/2/0) and InterpBench (0/+∼1/0), only CircuitKIT spans all three stages behind one artifact with a custom-data path (Table 1).
- **A declarative path from structured data to circuit discovery.** A CSV/JSONL/HuggingFace dataset is mapped into a discovery task by declaring column templates; the pipeline fills rows, tokenizes, enforces token-length alignment between clean/corrupt halves, precomputes the discriminative label, and hands a ready dataloader to the backend. Ten corruption-synthesis strategies (entity-swap, token-swap, role-swap, paraphrase, distractor, ...) cover both discovery-time counterfactuals and robustness probes, and a GREEN/YELLOW/RED worthiness pre-flight gate grades a dataset before discovery compute is spent.
- **A multi-pillar faithfulness panel over a single score.** Motivated by the finding that single faithfulness metrics depend on ablation method and can reverse method rankings (Miller et al. 2024), CircuitKIT reports six configurable pillars (causal patching, ablation, stability, robustness, baseline comparison, generalization) plus an optional seventh (intervention reliability), all reported signed so behavioral inversions below the corrupt-run baseline are visible rather than clamped to zero.
- **Actionable intervention modules grounded in established tooling.** Seven application modules consume the same artifact: structural pruning (LLM-Pruner), mixed-precision quantization (quanto/llmcompressor), selective fine-tuning (PEFT/LoRA static gradient mask), ROME/MEMIT knowledge editing, activation steering, circuit-restricted LoRA healing, and hallucination probing; exported checkpoints are scored through lm-evaluation-harness so downstream accuracy is standardized rather than proxy.
- **Registry-driven extensibility.** A typed `@register` decorator governs discovery backends, selectors (14 built-in), corruption strategies, and architecture families, so stable-tier methods are core-maintained with backward-compatibility while research-tier and community contributions register alongside without touching the core.

## Methodology

CircuitKIT is a source-available Python library (LSAL v1.1) built on TransformerLens that composes four stages behind one shared `CircuitScores` artifact.

**Discovery (13 algorithms, four backends, two stability tiers).** The stable tier (6) is tested across GPT-2/Llama/Gemma/Qwen: the gradient-attribution EAP family (EAP via first-order edge attribution `s(u→v)=(xu−x′u)ᵀ∂L/∂xv`; EAP-IG replacing the single-point gradient with an m-step integrated gradient satisfying the completeness/sensitivity axioms; EAP-GP using an adaptively constructed gradient-path integration), the search-based ACDC (greedy edge-pruning within a tolerance, node-only by construction, ~1 forward pass per candidate edge), the trainable information-bottleneck IBCircuit (soft mask optimized under `L=αL_task+βL_IB`, clean-only), and the gradient-free contextual decomposition CD-T (clean-only, propagates a relevant/irrelevant split through each component). Discovery runs at two first-class granularities (node: per-head/MLP-sublayer; neuron: per-head-channel and per-MLP-neuron), exposing exactly the units the intervention modules act on.

**Task specification (three modes).** A built-in registry of 16 tasks (ioi, greater_than, sva, hypernymy, gender_bias, capital_country, mmlu, glue/SST-2, double_io, wmdp, boolq, winogrande, winogrande_mc, truthfulqa, ifeval, gsm8k); a template-driven custom-data path (`Pipeline.from_custom_data`) that aligns clean/corrupt pairs and routes clean-only data to IBCircuit/CD-T; and a programmatic `TaskSpec` protocol (~50-line module) for multi-turn harnesses.

**Evaluation (six + one pillars).** All pillars share the normalized faithfulness `F(C)=(y_circuit−y_corrupt)/(y_clean−y_corrupt)`: P1 causal patching (soft, complement pushed toward corrupted activations), P2 ablation (hard, out-of-circuit components zero/mean/mean-positional replaced), P3 stability (Jaccard J and Spearman ρ over data-resample re-discoveries), P4 robustness (ratio r_c and drop δ_c across the corruption-strategy family), P5 baseline comparison (improvement over size-matched random/magnitude subgraphs with a z-score), P6 generalization (cross-task transfer ratio `F_target/F_source` rendered as a NumPy transfer matrix), and optional P7 intervention reliability (harmonic mean of seed-consistency, effect-magnitude, effect-variance).

**Intervention modules.** Pruning wraps circuit importance as a custom LLM-Pruner importance estimator; quantization keeps the top fraction at native precision under a fixed budget (quanto or GPTQ-calibrated llmcompressor); selective fine-tuning resolves top-k% components to weight-matrix index ranges for a static gradient mask or LoRA target_modules; ROME/MEMIT editing is restricted to circuit-resident MLP layers with a one-call cached `get_covariance`; plus activation steering, circuit-restricted LoRA healing, and hallucination probing.

**Three interfaces over one artifact.** A stateful `Pipeline`, a flat `ck.*` functional API, and a YAML-driven CLI (`circuitkit run pipeline.yaml`) all produce/consume the same `CircuitScores`, so a circuit discovered in a notebook can be evaluated from the CLI and consumed by a pruning script without format conversion.

## Results & Highlights

- **Algorithm validation on IOI (E1, GPT-2 Small, node level, α=0.3).** The EAP family recovers perfect patching (P1 = 1.000 for eap/eap-ig/eap-gp) with the highest canonical-head overlap (Jaccard 0.429–0.500, recall 0.60–0.67) at minute-scale cost (66–140 s), while ACDC scores poorly under the fixed-sparsity protocol (P1 = 0.208, P2 = 0.039) at 3,322 s (24–133× the cost). Strikingly, CD-T is faithful by both pillars (P1 = 0.894, P2 = 1.000) yet shares zero canonical heads (Jaccard 0.000, recall 0.000) — a faithful circuit whose components differ entirely from the literature's taxonomy.
- **Cross-family discovery (E2, eap-ig, two tasks × six families).** All twelve cells completed with no code change. Patching recovery is uniformly high (P1 ≥ 0.91, six cells at exactly 1.000) and stability is strong (J = 0.80–0.92 across GPT-2 Small, Pythia-1.4B, Llama-3.2-1B, Gemma-2-2B, Qwen2.5-1.5B, Phi-2). Every circuit beats a size-matched random subgraph by 1.2× to 7.2× where the ratio is defined. Ablation sufficiency P2 spreads widely (0.24 on Gemma-2-2B/IOI up to 1.00 on Phi-2/IOI) despite near-identical P1 — the documented dependence of hard ablation on the replacement distribution.
- **Neuron-level discovery (E3, Llama-3.2-1B/IOI).** EAP-IG preserves perfect patching at neuron granularity (P1 = 1.000, P2 = 0.241) at essentially the same discovery cost as node level (147 s vs 140 s), exposing the exact 825,754-of-1,179,648 neuron units interventions act on. Single-point EAP inverts under hard zero-ablation (signed P2 = −0.350, raw logit diff −0.57 against a clean-run 1.69) — a behavioral inversion a bounded score would clamp to 0.00 and hide.
- **Custom-data path end to end (E4, jailbreak-detection CSV on Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct).** The alignment filter kept 265 of 334 rows. The paired circuit (eap-ig, node) recovers 85% of the full-model refusal gap under patching (P1 = 0.854) and beats size-matched baselines (1.71× random, 1.44× magnitude), but under hard zero-ablation the refusal metric on harmful requests falls to −1.55, below the benign-request baseline (signed ratio −2.61): stripped of its surrounding computation, the truncated model prefers to comply. On the clean-only route IBCircuit's neuron-level circuit alone matches the full model (P(correct) 0.902 vs 0.874) despite never seeing a corrupt pair.
- **Circuit-guided pruning (E5, Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct/BoolQ, 30% sparsity).** IBCircuit retains 98.6% of base accuracy (accRet 0.986, base 0.755), within 4.1 pp of the strongest criterion Taylor (1.027), with EAP-GP at 0.927 and EAP at 0.866, all far above magnitude/Wanda/random (0.501–0.519, WikiText-2 PPL ≥ 3,000). But the spread within the circuit family is wide: EAP-IG (0.571) and CD-T (0.502) rank among the worst compressors despite strong patch faithfulness, and across the ten-selector grid patch faithfulness anti-correlates with retention (Spearman ρ = −0.78, p = 0.010).
- **Circuit-guided quantization (E6, 3-bit/8-bit/5% FP fallback).** Unlike pruning, quantization retention is insensitive to patch faithfulness (ρ(patchF, accRet) = +0.23, p = 0.55, n=9), but ablation faithfulness does predict retention (ρ(ablF, accRet) = +0.73, p = 0.031); circuit-guided tier selection did not significantly underperform uniform assignment at matched budget.
- **Circuit-guided selective fine-tuning (E7, Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct/BoolQ, top-30% mask).** Circuit selection does not separate from random selection at matched budget (best single cell +3.0 pp for IBCircuit; pooled mean Δ(circuit − random) = −0.001 across 16 cells — a null result). What protects general LM coherence is the budget constraint itself: masked fine-tuning keeps WikiText-2 PPL near the unmodified 11.05 (12.0–14.1) while full-parameter training on the same 500 examples degrades to 14.2–22.0.

## Authors & Credibility

Authors are Pratinav Seth, Hem Gosalia, and Aditya Kasliwal (equal contribution), and Vinay Kumar Sankarapu, all at Lexsi Labs. The work builds directly on prior first-author artifacts by the same group (C-δθ circuit-restricted weight arithmetic, quantization-permanent unlearning via circuit attribution, a causal audit of neuron selectors, "Interpretability as alignment," and the under-review "Faithfulness is not actionability" audit that E5–E7 reuse). It correctly cites and positions itself against the established substrate toolkits (TransformerLens, NNsight/NDIF, Auto-Circuit, MIB, InterpBench, Captum) and the discovery methods it reimplements (EAP, EAP-IG, EAP-GP, ACDC, IBCircuit, CD-T, RelP), and it releases source under LSAL v1.1 at https://github.com/Lexsi-Labs/CircuitKIT with notebooks and documentation. The paper is unusually forthcoming about null results and the limits of faithfulness scores.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty.** The contribution is systems-level integration rather than a new discovery algorithm or metric; the typed shared artifact, the declarative custom-data path with token-alignment + worthiness gate, and the unified selector registry are the genuinely new pieces. The framing is honest that this is "common infrastructure," not a scientific advance, and the seven case studies (E1–E7) serve as validation rather than benchmark-winning claims.
- **Rigor.** Strong where it reports signed faithfulness ratios and explicitly distinguishes behavioral inversions (negative P2 in E3/E4) from measurement errors, and honest about null results (E7 Δ = −0.001). However, statistical power is explicitly bounded: the stability pillar uses only three data resamples (seeds 42–44), every other study is single-seed (so cross-seed reliability SDrel/Pillar 7 is *not yet reported* and the stability-to-reliability association of Section 11.8 is *unmeasured*), and the headline ρ = −0.78 pruning anti-correlation is explicitly "one Llama-3.2-3B-Instruct · BoolQ cell of a broader audit ... rather than a population estimate" — a weak baseline for generalization claims.
- **Reproducibility.** Good on paper: scripts/configs released, full protocols in Appendix A, three interchangeable interfaces, a YAML run format, and lm-evaluation-harness standardized benchmarking. Real frictions remain unquantified: discovery/evaluation are pinned to TransformerLens 2.18 (3.x "planned"), intervention modules require an architecture-registry entry (only Llama/Qwen/Gemma fully shipped), ROME/MEMIT needs a user-supplied ~100k-sample covariance corpus (the shipped fallback is "adequate only for a smoke test"), and IBCircuit's single-batch training means its activation footprint grows with model size (hence the GPT-2-scale six-algorithm comparison in E1).
- **Significance.** High as infrastructure for the mechanistic-interpretability community if adopted; the central scientific message — that intrinsic faithfulness does not predict downstream intervention outcome, so the loop must be closed extrinsically via `ck.benchmark` — is well-supported by the within-paper E5–E7 contrast (pruning forces a hard partition where ranking errors are unrecoverable; quantization forces no partition; fine-tuning constrains a budget rather than ranking within it) and motivates the multi-pillar design.
- **Red flags.** (1) LSAL v1.1 is deliberately *not* OSI-approved open source and prohibits commercial use — a choice the authors justify via dual-use risk but one that limits enterprise adoption and downstream toolchain integration. (2) The paper repeatedly defers to a "broader actionability audit [35]" that is itself under review, so several quantitative claims (the audit's ten-selector grid, the Gemma-3-4B-IT replication) are not fully reproducible from this paper alone. (3) The corruption strategies "assume identifiable syntactic structure and may find nothing to change on instruction-tuned or safety-relevant prompts," and confirming auto-synthesized pairs carry signal on AdvBench/TruthfulQA/StereoSet is left as future work — directly relevant to E4's safety conclusions. (4) E7's full-parameter training on 500 BoolQ examples is acknowledged as itself unstable on Gemma-3-4B-IT, confounding that replication.

## Strengths

- Genuinely spans discover → evaluate → intervene behind one interchangeable typed artifact, a gap no prior toolkit (TransformerLens, NNsight, Auto-Circuit, MIB, InterpBench) fills.
- The declarative custom-data path with token alignment, a worthiness pre-flight gate, and clean-only routing to IBCircuit/CD-T removes the practical bottleneck that has kept circuit discovery confined to canonical tasks.
- A six-pillar (+1 optional) faithfulness panel that reports signed ratios and surfaces behavioral inversions, directly operationalizing the known ablation-method dependence of faithfulness.
- Unusually honest reporting: null results (E7), anti-correlations (E5), and the explicit "faithfulness is not actionability" message are presented as findings rather than hidden.
- Three interchangeable interfaces (stateful Pipeline, flat `ck.*` API, YAML CLI) over the same artifact, plus registry-driven extensibility via `@register` decorators.

## Limitations

- Statistical power is thin: three-seed stability and single-seed everything else; cross-seed intervention reliability (Pillar 7) is unmeasured; the ρ = −0.78 pruning anti-correlation is one cell.
- Intervention coverage trails discovery (architecture-registry entries only fully shipped for Llama/Qwen/Gemma); discovery/evaluation are pinned to TransformerLens 2.18 with 3.x support planned.
- Method-specific costs under-documented at scale: IBCircuit trains in a single batch (footprint grows with model size), ACDC is node-only and ~50× slower than EAP, ROME/MEMIT needs a large user-supplied covariance corpus.
- Corruption strategies assume identifiable syntactic structure; efficacy on safety/instruction-tuned prompts (AdvBench, TruthfulQA, StereoSet) is explicitly future work.
- LSAL v1.1 is source-available, not OSI open source (no commercial use), which may restrict adoption and integration.
- Many quantitative claims defer to an under-review companion audit [35], so some numbers are not fully reproducible from this paper alone.

## Relevance

对关注机制可解释性与"可操作可解释性"（locate-then-act）的研究者，CircuitKIT 提供了一个把发现/评估/干预串联起来的可复用基础设施，尤其适合在自定数据上做电路分析并据此做剪枝、量化、知识编辑或安全审计。其"忠实性不等于可操作性、必须用 lm-eval-harness 闭环测量下游效果"的核心结论，对所有想用电路重要性信号指导模型压缩或安全编辑的工作都有直接方法论价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the patch-faithfulness → pruning anti-correlation (ρ = −0.78 on one cell) hold across more models/tasks, or is it an artifact of BoolQ's binary structure and Llama-3.2-3B's specific computation?
- Can multi-seed re-discovery (Pillar 7, SDrel) actually reconcile discovery stability with intervention reliability, and what is the empirical stability→reliability correlation?
- How well do the auto-synthesized corruption strategies (entity-swap, token-swap, role-swap) carry meaningful attribution signal on safety-relevant benchmarks (AdvBench, TruthfulQA, StereoSet) where syntactic structure is less identifiable?
- Does the E4 finding — that a discovered refusal circuit inverts toward compliance under hard ablation — generalize, and what does it imply for the safety of circuit-restricted weight surgery (C-δθ) on deployed models?
- How does circuit-guided importance compare to sparse-autoencoder-feature-guided importance for pruning and editing, now that SAE features are a "natural next granularity" the authors flag?
- Does the custom-data worthiness gate (GREEN/YELLOW/RED) actually predict downstream faithfulness, or does it over-reject useful noisy datasets?
