Relative Positions Generalize, Absolute Positions Memorize

An Implicit-Bias Account of Length Generalization in Attention
Subham Singh · Ashutosh Mishra · Subha Raut — Dept. of Mathematics & Statistics, Mississippi State University / Independent
Key finding

Relative encodings generalize to unseen lengths via exact equivariance; absolute encodings memorize, then collapse.

一句话总结：从隐式偏差视角解释为何 RoPE 等相对位置编码能长度泛化——其 logit 仅依赖相对偏移（精确等变性）——而学习型绝对编码把输出钉死在训练范围内，超出即崩塌。

Key metric
0.83 vs 0.07
RoPE vs learned-APE accuracy at L=48 (K=1), trained only on lengths ≤16. Chance level = 0.05 — APE is indistinguishable from random.
Why this matters

A diagnostic equivariance–carrier–dilution framework for analyzing positional-encoding extrapolation and designing longer-context models.

Critical edge

Theory is confined to a single attention head on a synthetic fixed-offset toy task — transfer to natural language, multi-step reasoning, and realistic LLM scale is not demonstrated.

scope · toy-task generalization

Methodology

minimal setup · proofs + probes

The paper isolates positional selection in a deliberately minimal setup: fixed-offset retrieval on i.i.d. uniform tokens, where the label is the token exactly K positions before the last query. Training lengths are uniform in [8,16]; evaluation extends to L∈{20,24,32,40,48}, all strictly out of range. The model is a single attention head (d=64, no MLP, no LayerNorm) comparing RoPE (block-diagonal rotations) against a learned absolute encoding (APE). The theory builds on the attention-as-SVM reduction (Tarzanagh et al.): Lemma 1 proves the RoPE logit depends only on relative offset δ=t−n; Lemma 2 + Proposition 1 characterize a target-aligned carrier kernel forced by content-independent selection; Corollary 2 derives an inverse-linear attention-dilution decay; Proposition 2 shows APE pins to trained positions and receives zero gradient out of range. A 2-layer / 4-head transformer and a linear-attention control isolate the mechanism.

＋ Strengths

  • Clean, falsifiable mechanistic chain (equivariance → carrier → dilution), each step both proved and measured.
  • Genuine negative result: removing softmax destroys extrapolation, isolating the mechanism rather than vaguely crediting "relative positions".
  • ALiBi / T5 / RoPE three-way comparison decomposes the shared mechanism (offset selection + dilution) from scheme-specific factors.
  • Honest framing — predicts dilution-driven degradation at scale, not infinite context.
  • Excellent reproducibility: CPU-only, fixed seeds, a single self-contained script, full sweep tables.

－ Limitations

  • Confined to a single head on a synthetic toy task; no natural-language or LLM-scale demonstration.
  • Carrier formation left open — necessity proven, convergence to the carrier geometry is not.
  • Relies on the asymptotic attention-as-SVM limit that finite-time Adam may not fully realize.
  • K=1 (arguably the most common case) handled separately via self-logit suppression, not the carrier story.
  • Multi-head composition only partially characterized (H=1→H=4 dilution increase unexplained).
  • No comparison to recent extrapolation fixes (YaRN, position interpolation) beyond a conceptual mention.

Open questions for further exploration

from the authors
  1. Does the carrier + dilution law reproduce on natural-language retrieval (passkey, needle-in-haystack) where content is not i.i.d.?
  2. Can Proposition 1's necessity extend to a convergence proof that Adam on cross-entropy provably lands on the carrier geometry?
  3. How does the carrier compose across heads and layers — does per-head frequency predict the H=1→H=4 dilution rise?
  4. Does softmax-specificity survive other normalizers (sigmoid attention, ReLU-based attentions)?
  5. Can the dilution law predict, ahead of training, the OOD length where a RoPE model drops below a target accuracy?
  6. Does the APE position-pinning failure explain seed-sensitive length-generalization failures in production models?
