> **一句话总结：** 本文借用语言学中的自然语义元语言（NSM）的 ~65 个语义原词（如 I、DO、BAD、FEEL），从"存在性、因果干预、行为等价"三个科学解释标准出发，论证语义原词比"情绪标签"和"评价维度（appraisal）"更适合作为 LLM 情绪的底层解释变量——在参考模型 Llama-3.2-1B 上，原词方向对情绪的干预强度约为最强评价方向的 3 倍、选择性约 2 倍（+2.07 logits，p < 10⁻³），且模型把原词释义当作与情绪词可互换的充分线索。

> **TL;DR:** The paper argues that ~65 semantic primes from the Natural Semantic Metalanguage (NSM) — e.g. I, DO, BAD, FEEL — are better *explanans* for emotion in LLMs than emotion labels (circular) or appraisal dimensions (which never bottom out). Judged by three causal-explanation tests (existence, intervention, behavioral equivalence) across four instruction-tuned LLMs (Llama-3.2-1B, Gemma-2-2B, Gemma-2-9B, OLMo-2-7B), 30/32 emotion-relevant primes are linearly recoverable in all four models above length and Hewitt-Liang control baselines; on the reference Llama-3.2-1B, a prime-based steering direction shifts the target emotion by 3.73 logits (selectivity 0.58) versus 1.29 (0.31) for the best single appraisal and 2.11 (0.41) for a dimensionality-matched composite (+2.07-logit gap, p < 10⁻³) on the crowd-enVent benchmark; and in all four models the model treats a prime explication as interchangeable with the emotion word. The clean compositional result, however, is Llama-1B only — the linear recipe is inert on Gemma-2-9B (shift 0.02) and uncalibrated on OLMo-2-7B.

## 核心贡献

- **提出"解释质量"框架而非"预测准确率"框架：** 明确区分"理解/恢复"与"科学解释"，借用 Woodward 的干预主义因果解释理论，为 LLM 情绪候选项设定三个必要测试——存在性（real，需通过长度基线和 Hewitt-Liang 控制任务）、因果干预（causal，steering）、行为等价（faithful，模型把它当作该概念）。
- **引入第三类候选变量——NSM 语义原词：** 指出情绪标签"循环"（guilt ↦ guilt 向量，explanans 即 explanandum）、评价维度"悬浮不终止"（unpleasantness 与 guilt 同阶），而 NSM 原词在语义空间"触底"，提供可终止的归约。
- **构建并发布对比性最小对数据集：** 11,902 对最小对（23,804 句）覆盖 32 个原词，含金标准释义、负样本禁用原词指数词的硬约束、每原词的长度混淆 Cohen's d 报告，以及机器翻译的中文版本；通过合成数据验证探针管线能恢复植入层。
- **三条经验发现支持单一论断：** (1) 30/32 情绪相关原词在四个模型上均可线性恢复；(2) 在参考模型上原词方向干预情绪的强度约为最强评价方向的 3 倍、选择性约 2 倍，且方向与情绪读出近乎正交（余弦 0.04），表明是组合性因果效应而非读出几何；(3) 四个模型均把原词释义当作情绪的可互换充分线索。
- **头部对照（head-to-head）复现 Tak et al. (2025) 的管线：** 在相同数据切分、相同 13 情绪 / 21 评价维度、相同 crowd-enVent 数据上比较，保证对比公平。
- **方法学贡献：** 为机制可解释性社区提供一套评估"科学解释质量"而非"探测准确率"的标准。

## Key Contributions

- **A framework for explanation quality, not prediction accuracy.** Drawing on Woodward's interventionist account of causal explanation, the paper demands three necessary tests of any candidate explanans for LLM emotion: *existence* (real linear representation, validated against length and Hewitt-Liang control-task baselines), *intervention* (steering moves the emotion), and *behavioral equivalence* (the model treats the feature as the concept). This filters out probe artifacts and pseudo-explanations.
- **A third family of candidate variables: NSM semantic primes.** The paper diagnoses that emotion labels are circular (the explanans *is* the explanandum) and that appraisal dimensions like unpleasantness float without bottoming out. NSM's ~65 primes (WANT, FEEL, DO, GOOD, BAD, I, SOMEONE, …) are held to be mutually indefinable, so an emotion explication such as guilt ≈ "I did something bad; I feel bad because of this" terminates at primes that are not themselves emotions.
- **A released contrastive minimal-pair suite.** 11,902 minimal pairs (23,804 sentences) for 32 primes, with gold explications, a hard constraint that no negative member leaks the prime's exponent word, per-prime word-count Cohen's d to quantify the length confound, ≥16 held-out template pairs per prime, a synthetic-data validation that recovers a planted layer, and a machine-translated Chinese version for cross-lingual work.
- **Three empirical findings supporting one claim.** (1) 30 of 32 emotion-describing primes are linearly encoded in all four models above baselines; (2) on Llama-3.2-1B a prime direction steers emotion ~3× as far at ~2× the selectivity of the best appraisal direction, beating a dimensionality-matched composite (p < 10⁻³); (3) in all four models the model treats a prime explication as interchangeable with the emotion word and as a sufficient cue.
- **Head-to-head comparability.** Experiments reproduce Tak et al. (2025)'s public harness, split, 13 emotions, and 21 appraisal dimensions on crowd-enVent, so the prime-vs-appraisal comparison is apples-to-apples rather than against a strawman baseline.
- **A methodological contribution to mechanistic interpretability**, offering criteria for judging the quality of scientific explanations rather than merely probe accuracy.

## Methodology

**Theory.** The paper frames the question as one of *causal explanation* on Woodward's interventionist account: a feature is explanatorily relevant when intervening on it changes the outcome. Three operational demands follow — existence (a genuine internal representation, not a probe artifact), intervention (steering moves the emotion), and behavioral equivalence (the model treats the feature as the concept). Candidate explanans are sorted both by how well they pass these tests and by a fourth "basicness" axis: an explanans must be more primitive than the explanandum. Emotion labels fail basicness (circular); appraisals give only a partial, non-terminating reduction; NSM primes bottom out at a definitional floor.

**Stimuli.** A contrastive minimal-pair suite for 32 primes (the symbol classes admitting a single-prime assertion/withholding contrast: mental predicates, evaluators, modals, person primes, temporal/descriptor/bodily/reason primes). Of the 22 distinct primes in the 13 gold emotion explications, 21 are covered (only THIS missing). 11,902 pairs, ≥202 per prime, with length-confound reporting and a length-only baseline always reported.

**Models and data.** Four instruction-tuned LLMs spanning two orders of magnitude and three families: Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct (reference for intervention), Gemma-2-2B-it, Gemma-2-9B-it, and the fully-open OLMo-2-7B-Instruct. Emotion/appraisal labels from crowd-enVent (6,800 event descriptions, 13 emotions, 21 appraisal dimensions), reproducing Tak et al.'s 2,740-train / 1,370-test split, with ISEAR as a robustness check.

**Three experimental batteries.**
1. *Existence:* L2 logistic probes per layer on each prime's contrastive pairs (balanced, last token), compared against a length-only baseline and a Hewitt-Liang control task; a prime counts as encoded only when it clears both.
2. *Intervention (steering):* a forward hook adds a unit-normalized direction (scaled by the layer's mean residual norm) to the block residual at layer 11 (the patching-peak site), at all token positions. Six arms differ only in direction: emotion probe (readout ceiling), single best appraisal, dimensionality-matched multi-appraisal composite, single best prime, prime recipe (centrality-weighted signed sum), and a random-concept control. Measured: target-logit shift, off-target mass, selectivity, target-argmax rate.
3. *Behavioral equivalence (probe-free, all four models):* explication-to-emotion classification accuracy, same-affect decoy margin, central-vs-peripheral prime ablation (sufficiency), the linear recipe-match test, and a "definitional floor" restatement task (does the model simplify a prime less than a non-prime?).

## Results & Highlights

- **Existence test — primes are real internal elements.** 30 of 32 emotion-describing primes are linearly encoded in all four models: 31/32 on Llama-3.2-1B (bootstrap 95% CI [29,32]), 30/32 on Gemma-2-2B, and 32/32 on both Gemma-2-9B and OLMo-2-7B. On Llama-3.2-1B, held-out template decoding reaches **0.91** versus the Hewitt-Liang control task at **0.50** (chance) and the length-only baseline at **0.62** — the gap is the selectivity that makes this an existence claim rather than a probe artifact.
- **Intervention test — primes control emotion ~3× as strongly.** On Llama-3.2-1B over the four-target × dose grid, the prime recipe shifts the target emotion by **3.73 logits at selectivity 0.58**, versus **1.29 at 0.31** for the single best appraisal and **2.11 at 0.41** for the dimensionality-matched multi-appraisal composite. A permutation test gives a **+2.07-logit gap, p < 10⁻³**. The random-concept control barely moves the target (**0.02, selectivity −0.26**), and the emotion readout ceiling is 9.80 (selectivity 0.78). For anger, the prime arm drives anger to the top prediction in **77%** of prompts.
- **Compositionality, not readout geometry.** The cosine between the prime recipe and the emotion probe direction averages **0.04** across targets — a direction nearly orthogonal to the readout still drives the emotion logit, the signature of a compositional causal effect processed by the network rather than read off directly.
- **Behavioral equivalence — explications are treated as the emotion, in all four models.** Given the explication alone (no emotion word), the model classifies it as the target emotion at **0.54 / 0.85 / 0.77 / 0.54** for Llama-1B / Gemma-2B / Gemma-9B / OLMo-7B, versus the 13-way chance rate of 0.08, and beats the same-affect decoy by **0.30 to 0.63** probability mass. A matched appraisal description scores only **0.25 to 0.42** with decoy margins of **0.03 to 0.11**, and its central-clause sufficiency gradient *inverts* on the two larger models. Central-prime ablation degrades the target logit more than peripheral-prime ablation (**0.59 vs 0.12** on Llama-1B), so the explication is compositionally sufficient.
- **Primes bottom out.** In the restatement task, the fraction of output words simpler than the target is **0.73/0.50/0.72/0.72** for non-primes versus **0.47/0.21/0.34/0.40** for primes across the four models — primes resist reduction, so the explication regress terminates.
- **Localization reconciles behavioral and geometric findings.** Emotion computation peaks at layer 10 (activation patching flip rate **0.805**); by the consolidation layer, of 18 primes tested at the answer position only **FEEL** (+0.23 lift) and **SOMEONE** (+0.11) remain decodable. The late readout is therefore not a linear sum of prime directions — the prime subspace is not a privileged basis (a random 32-dim projection reaches 0.82 vs the prime subspace's 0.86, a gain of only 0.05).

## Authors & Credibility

Single-author paper by **Frank Xing** at the University of Reading (Henley Business School email). The work is positioned as building directly on, and reproducing, Tak et al. (2025, ACL Findings) — the most directly comparable prior pipeline — and engages seriously with the NSM canon (Wierzbicka 1972/1996/1999; Goddard & Wierzbicka 2002/2014), mechanistic interpretability (Bereska & Gavves 2024; Sharkey et al. 2025; Lieberum et al. 2024; Templeton et al. 2026), and causal-explanation philosophy (Woodward 2003). Code and data are released (github.com/fxing79/pcs), and the author is unusually forthright about where the claims hold and where they do not. The single-author status and the fact that the headline causal result is confined to the smallest model are points a reader should weigh, but the honesty about it partially offsets the concern.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High and genuinely interdisciplinary. Importing a 60-year-old linguistic program (NSM) as a candidate explanans for mechanistic interpretability is a fresh framing, and the explicit distinction between "recoverable/predictable" and "causally explanatory" is a useful methodological contribution that the mech-interp literature has under-developed.
- **Rigor:** Strong on controls. The existence test is paired with both a length-only baseline *and* a Hewitt-Liang control task (the two primes that fail, DO and SAY, carry the worst length confounds d=1.26 and 2.03, which is the right behavior for a honest probe). The steering experiment includes a dimensionality-matched composite, a random-concept control, bootstrap resamples, and layer 7–14 sweeps, and reports near-orthogonality (cosine 0.04) to rule out readout-geometry leakage. The synthetic-data validation recovering a planted layer is a nice sanity check.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Public code/data, cheap to replicate (analysis on a single CPU, extraction in minutes on a single GPU), four open models including the fully-open OLMo, and a reproducible 11,902-pair suite with explicit confound metrics.
- **Significance:** Moderate. The reframing of "what is a good explanation" is conceptually valuable, but the practical steering payoff is modest (the prime recipe recovers only ~38% of the emotion readout ceiling of 9.80 logits) and, crucially, model-specific.
- **Red flags / load-bearing caveats:**
  - **The headline causal claim is Llama-3.2-1B only.** The author states plainly that the linear prime recipe "is inert on Gemma-2-9B (grid-averaged shift 0.02, at the random floor)" and that on OLMo-2-7B "the readout ceiling is ill-set (a negative emotion-arm shift), so numbers are uninformative and not reported." So the cross-model causal claim effectively rests on **two** models (Llama-1B linearly, Gemma-9B only via whole-explication content), and the clean compositional result — the most theoretically interesting finding — does not transfer. This is the single biggest threat to the generality of the conclusion.
  - **Construct validity is acknowledged as open.** A decoded direction could be a lexical correlate of the prime word rather than the NSM prime per se; the three tests are necessary but not jointly sufficient. The author flags this but does not resolve it (suggests blind human paraphrases as future validation).
  - **The mechanism is a sketch.** The paper localizes where primes are consumed (mid-layers) but does not identify the attention heads or MLP sub-modules that perform the composition, nor trace the layer-10-to-readout step arrow by arrow.
  - **Geometric non-reduction undercuts the strongest reading.** The emotion readout is explicitly *not* a linear function of prime directions; the reduction is behavioral only. A reader wanting "primes are the linear basis of emotion" will not find it here.
  - **The matched-appraisal baseline, while dimensionality-controlled, is operationalized as a constructed Component-Process-style clause profile**, which the author concedes "a stronger operationalization could potentially improve" — so part of the prime-vs-appraisal gap could shrink under a better appraisal operationalization.

## Strengths

- Principled, theory-driven framework that distinguishes explanation from prediction and imposes real controls (Hewitt-Liang, length baseline, dimensionality-matched composite, random control).
- Genuine head-to-head with the strongest prior baseline (Tak et al. 2025) on identical data and splits, not a strawman.
- Released, well-documented artifact (11,902 minimal pairs with confound metrics and gold explications) that others can build on.
- Honest, specific reporting of failures: which primes fail and why (length confound), where the recipe does not transfer (Gemma-9B, OLMo), and that the reduction is behavioral rather than geometric.
- The "definitional floor" restatement test is an elegant, probe-free signature that primes resist further reduction.

## Limitations

- The clean compositional/steering result is confined to Llama-3.2-1B; the linear recipe does not transfer to Gemma-2-9B and is uncalibrated on OLMo-2-7B, so the cross-model causal claim rests on two models.
- Construct validity is unresolved — a direction passing all three tests could still be a lexical correlate rather than the NSM prime itself.
- The mechanism is a localization sketch; the specific circuits (attention heads, MLPs, an NSM-grammar component) that compose primes into emotions are unidentified.
- Cross-lingual universality — a core NSM proposition — is not tested in the LLM case; the author notes English-pivot routing in multilingual models would confound any such test.
- Behavioral results depend on operationalizing the appraisal baseline as a constructed clause profile, which may under-represent appraisal theory's strength.
- Single-author status limits the breadth of independent verification within the paper itself.

## Relevance

对从事机制可解释性、LLM 内部表征与可控性（steering/干预）研究的读者高度相关：它提供了一个比"探测准确率"更严格的"科学解释"评估框架，并展示了如何用语义原词方向在小模型上做更选择性、更组合性的情绪控制。其方法论（存在性/因果/行为三测试 + 控制任务）对任何想在 LLM 中寻找"底层解释变量"而非 merely-correlational probing 的工作都有借鉴价值，尽管主结论目前仅在最弱模型上成立，需谨慎外推。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Why does the linear prime recipe compose cleanly on Llama-3.2-1B but fail on Gemma-2-9B? Is there a different (non-linear) circuit that performs the composition in larger models, and can it be traced head-by-head?
- Can the construct-validity gap be closed — e.g., by using blind human paraphrases, or by testing whether a prime direction generalizes to stimuli that never contain the prime's exponent word?
- Does the prime-as-explanans framing extend beyond emotion to other explicable concepts (decisions, permissions, requests) that NSM covers with overlapping primes (WANT, NOT, CAN, FEEL)?
- How would the results change with natively non-English stimuli and a non-English-trained LLM, to separate NSM universality from English-pivot routing?
- What is the relationship between NSM prime directions and sparse-autoencoder features (e.g., Gemma Scope) — are primes recoverable as monosemantic SAE features, and does that change the steering selectivity ceiling?
- Could a stronger appraisal-theory operationalization (e.g., Scherer's full dynamic process model rather than a static clause profile) close part of the prime-vs-appraisal steering gap?
