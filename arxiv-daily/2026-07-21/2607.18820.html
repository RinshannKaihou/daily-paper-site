CASE: Causal Alignment & Structural Enforcement for CoT Faithfulness

Ziming Wang · Yinghua Yao · Changwu Huang · Ke Tang · Xin Yao 2026-07-21 code ↗

A single LLM — causal-alignment SFT plus answer-time attention masking — structurally deletes the unfaithful instruction → answer shortcut.

M·01 Methodology

Two complementary stages enforcing the faithful chain Z → X → Y

CASE reframes CoT faithfulness as the causal factorization p(x,y|z) = p(x|z)·p(y|x) — i.e. Y ⊥ Z | X — and notes that standard autoregressive LLMs permit an unfaithful direct instruction→answer (Z→Y) shortcut that existing fine-tuning baselines do not structurally remove.

TCA Training-time Causal Alignment

Augments SFT with three intervention sets — counterfactual-CoT, biased-instruction, empty-instruction — under a selective loss that supervises only answer tokens on the auxiliary data, so the model learns how a given chain determines the answer rather than mimicking synthetic chains.

ISE Inference-time Structural Enforcement

During answer-token generation, sets every (answer-position, instruction-position) pre-softmax logit to −∞ across all layers and heads — deleting the explicit Z→Y attention edge while preserving the instruction-conditioned CoT-side hidden state HX.

S·03 Strengths

What genuinely works

  • Principled formulation. Each loss term maps to a named information-theoretic criterion (I(X;Y), I(X;Y|Z), I(Y;Z|X)).
  • Simpler & faster. One single LLM beats FRODO's two-module pipeline while being ~3× faster at inference.
  • Fair baselines. Identical LoRA, prompts, decoding, and the same DeepSeek-V3.2 data generator across all methods.
  • Transfer actually holds. All three baselines sit below Base LLM on cross-dataset transfer; CASE improves markedly (+102%).
  • Selective loss is thoughtful. Masking CoT-token loss on auxiliary data avoids forcing imitation of synthetic chains.

L·04 Limitations

Where the claim is brittle

  • Shallow eval only. Four short-form QA benchmarks — no math (GSM8K/MATH) or code, where masking is most likely to hurt accuracy.
  • No variance. Single-run point estimates, no error bars, seeds, or significance tests behind the “37%” headline.
  • Circular on CDE. Gains are partly structural (the mask deletes the probed path), not learned — and not decomposed.
  • Post-hoc β. The 80% true/false masking ratio is dataset-specific; G-M actually peaks at β=70%.
  • Leak through HX. Faithfulness is structural at the visible-token level; instruction info already baked into CoT-side states is not removed.

Q·05 Questions for Further Exploration

Open threads a careful reader would push on

  1. Does the attention mask survive math / code reasoning (GSM8K, MATH), where the CoT is longer and the instruction–answer link tighter — and at what accuracy cost?
  2. How much of the CDE → ~0 result is structurally forced by the mask vs. genuinely learned from TCA? A decomposition (ISE on Base-LLM with no TCA vs. full CASE, independent counterfactual generator) would isolate the two.
  3. Would more adversarial biased-instructions (paraphrased, multiple cue types) erode the L_Z-int gains beyond the single fixed “MIT professor” cue?
  4. Can the instruction info leaked through HX be measured directly (e.g. a probe on HX predicting z), yielding a faithfulness metric not trivially forced by the mask?
  5. Does CASE stack with orthogonal post-hoc candidate-selection methods, or hit a faithfulness ceiling?
  6. Is the +37% / +65% stable across seeds, and does it hold when training CoTs come from a different generator than the evaluation counterfactuals (breaking the DeepSeek-V3.2 coupling)?
