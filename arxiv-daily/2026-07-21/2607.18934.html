Methodology

Whisper-medium · +27 tokens · coverage-aware tags

The verbatim/intended ambiguity is treated as a controllable policy, not an annotation nuisance. Three mechanisms interface with an otherwise stock Whisper-medium.

Initialise from Whisper-medium and extend the vocabulary with 27 atomic tokens — 2 fillers, 12 paralinguistic sounds, 10 mode-tags (5 verbatim / 5 intended), 2 verbatimize delimiters. Mode-tags prepend the decoder to condition verbatim/intended policy as a binary choice.

Coverage-aware partitioning assigns tag subsets per corpus so the model is never penalised for both predicting and omitting the same event: disfluency-labelled but sound-unlabelled data gets only [verbatim 1..3]; clean injected-sound samples get [sound 1,2]; fully-annotated corpora get all five.

Supervised cross-attention. The k=10 heads best correlating with TIMIT ground truth are supervised via an averaged-head cosine loss (averaging before the loss lets heads specialise cooperatively: 36 ms vs 45 ms per-head). Inference uses an energy-based virtual blank model, τ=3 sharpening, and strict Viterbi blank/word alternation — no tokenizer changes, no external aligner.

Verbatimize rewrites an intended transcript into its canonical verbatim form using the audio; symmetric casing perturbation forces the model to consult the prompt for exact spelling.

Staged training. Stage 1 freezes all 764M params, training only the 27 new embeddings (1 epoch, lr 5e-4). Stage 2 unfreezes the decoder (3 epochs, lr 1e-5). Single A100, batch 160 ×4 accumulation, fp16; timing loss weighted 0.2 vs cross-entropy.

Strengths & Limitations

side-by-side

Strengths

  • Clean conceptual framing (latent policy variable) backed by three independently measured failure modes, not a single metric.
  • Coverage-aware partitioning elegantly resolves the heterogeneous-annotation problem that has plagued verbatim ASR training.
  • Strong zero-shot cross-lingual result (10%→94% German eF1, no German data), honestly decomposed into activation vs fine-tuning.
  • First attention-based word-timing to beat forced alignment on disfluent speech, with a clear averaged vs per-head ablation.
  • Practical downstream value: verbatimize offers a cheap path to scale verbatim corpora for clinical / dementia / stuttering use.
  • Honest limitations section naming German-only transfer, author-recorded GDS, and MFA-supervised timing.

Limitations

  • Cross-lingual transfer evaluated only on German; distant-language pairs (Mandarin, Arabic, agglutinative) untested.
  • GDS evaluation set was recorded by the authors (not trained speakers) — disfluency naturalism is questionable; headline 94% carries self-evaluation risk.
  • Timing supervision uses MFA-generated alignments for most of the 500h set, not hand-annotated boundaries — gains partly measured against the beaten baseline.
  • English DisfluencySpeech benchmark is single-speaker, limiting speaker variability.
  • Win over CrisperWhisper+s on German Thorsten (55 vs 57 ms) is thin; value is zero-shot transfer, not absolute German SOTA.
  • Verbatimize evaluated only on ICSI rare words; cross-domain / non-English generalisation untested.

Open Questions

for further exploration
Q1 · TRANSFERDoes mode-tag transfer hold for typologically distant languages (Mandarin, Arabic, agglutinative) where verbatim conventions differ structurally, not just lexically?
Q2 · DOMAINHow do mode-tags behave under domain shift (medical dictation, call-centre audio) where disfluency distributions differ sharply from ICSI/AMI?
Q3 · INVERSECan verbatimize be inverted — intended transcript recovered from verbatim without audio — and does audio-conditioning meaningfully beat an LLM-only baseline?
Q4 · GROUND TRUTHWould hand-annotated timing boundaries (vs MFA pseudo-labels) change the ranking between supervised cross-attention and forced alignment on FluencyBank?
Q5 · RE-RANKINGCan the CLR / style-mismatch decomposition be retroactively applied to existing Open ASR Leaderboard submissions to re-rank them under style-matched references?
Q6 · DIARIZATIONDoes mode-tag conditioning interact cleanly with speaker diarization when concatenated as decoder prefix tokens, as speculated in Future Work?
