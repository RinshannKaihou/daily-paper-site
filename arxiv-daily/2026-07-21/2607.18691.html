Critical edge

The headline causal claim is Llama-3.2-1B only — the linear prime recipe is inert on Gemma-2-9B (shift 0.02) and uncalibrated on OLMo-2-7B.

⚠ generality threat
Methodology

Explanation, not prediction — three causal tests on Woodward's account

A feature is explanatorily relevant only when intervening on it changes the outcome. The paper demands three necessary tests of any candidate explanans, plus a fourth "basicness" axis: an explanans must be more primitive than the explanandum.

Emotion labels fail basicness (circular: guilt ↦ guilt); appraisal dimensions like unpleasantness float without terminating; NSM primes — held mutually indefinable — bottom out at a definitional floor. The experiments reproduce Tak et al. (2025)'s public harness, split, 13 emotions, and 21 appraisal dimensions on crowd-enVent (6,800 events; 2,740/1,370 train-test), so the prime-vs-appraisal comparison is apples-to-apples. Models span two orders of magnitude and three families: Llama-3.2-1B (intervention reference), Gemma-2-2B-it, Gemma-2-9B-it, and the fully-open OLMo-2-7B-Instruct.

Strengths

Where it holds up

  • Theory-driven framework separating explanation from prediction, with real controls (Hewitt-Liang, length baseline, dim-matched composite, random concept).
  • Genuine head-to-head with the strongest prior baseline (Tak et al. 2025) on identical data and splits — not a strawman.
  • Released, documented artifact: 11,902 minimal pairs with confound metrics and gold explications.
  • Honest, specific failure reporting — which primes fail and why, where the recipe does not transfer.
  • The probe-free "definitional floor" restatement test elegantly shows primes resist further reduction.
Limitations

Where it strains

  • Clean compositional/steering result confined to Llama-3.2-1B; recipe does not transfer to Gemma-2-9B, uncalibrated on OLMo-2-7B.
  • Construct validity unresolved — a passing direction could still be a lexical correlate, not the NSM prime itself.
  • Mechanism is a localization sketch; the composing circuits (attention heads, MLPs) are unidentified.
  • Cross-lingual universality — a core NSM proposition — is untested in the LLM case.
  • Appraisal baseline operationalized as a static clause profile may under-represent appraisal theory's full strength.
Open questions

What the paper leaves on the table

  1. Why does the linear recipe compose on Llama-1B but fail on Gemma-9B — is there a non-linear circuit performable head-by-head in larger models?
  2. Can the construct-validity gap close via blind human paraphrases, or stimuli that never contain the prime's exponent word?
  3. Does prime-as-explanans extend beyond emotion to other explicable concepts (decisions, permissions, requests) NSM covers?
  4. How do results change with natively non-English stimuli on a non-English-trained LLM — separating NSM universality from English-pivot routing?
  5. What is the relation between NSM prime directions and sparse-autoencoder features (e.g. Gemma Scope) — are primes monosemantic SAE features?
  6. Could a dynamic process-model appraisal (Scherer) close part of the prime-vs-appraisal steering gap?
