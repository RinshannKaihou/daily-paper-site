Proves a single 8B checkpoint can jointly deliver general reasoning, closed-loop embodied interaction, and short outputs — a concrete template for deployable on-device robot brains, not a capability trade-off.
Critical Edge

01 Methodology

Starts from Qwen3-8B-Base and chains four post-training stages, each feeding the next, so broad intelligence, embodied skill, and concise generation are baked into one checkpoint rather than traded off.

  • S1General SFT — 5.58M-example weighted corpus (Math 47.6%, General 21%, Code 18.2%), then a curated Stage-2 refinement for reasoning & instruction quality.
  • S2General RL (GRPO) — correctness rewards plus a token-budget reward with a 16K rollout cap (4K/8K/16K ablated). Rewards rise while response length falls.
  • S3Embodied Expert — two text envs (Museum Tour, Supermarket Shopping) sharing one observation-action protocol, organized as a 4-level curriculum E0→E3. Warm-up then env-reward GRPO; format-only stop rule preserves recoverable errors.
  • S4Lineage-aware merge — TIES for same-origin RL experts anchored at Athena-SFT; conservative linear interpolation for a different-lineage SFT checkpoint (singular-spectrum analysis shows the two have incompatible geometries).

04 Strengths PROS

  • Three objectives in one 8B model — general intelligence, embodied interaction, and generation efficiency, with a clean four-stage decomposition making each stage's role legible.
  • Token-budget reward is exactly right for latency-constrained robot deployment; tokens-per-score-point makes the efficiency claim concrete and falsifiable.
  • Systematic ablations — rollout budget (4K/8K/16K) plus a 3-axis embodied-RL ablation (truncation × reward × map sampling). Unusually thorough for an industry report.
  • State-Goal / State-Return probes diagnose where the model is still weak (long-range spatial memory) instead of hiding behind a single SR number.
  • Lineage-aware merge is motivated by actual parameter-space geometry, not just empirics.

05 Limitations CONS

  • Only two text-based environments (Museum, Supermarket). No physical sim, no vision, no real robot — purely symbolic observation-action loops.
  • Train-on-eval-env confound: Athena is trained on these envs while every baseline is zero-shot, inflating the in-domain gap.
  • Possibly crippled baselines: near-zero embodied scores hint at tool-protocol mismatch rather than true capability differences.
  • Non-reproducible artifacts: proprietary envs/maps, undisclosed merge coefficients, unnamed "different-lineage SFT" checkpoint, eval on authors' own DeepInsight platform.
  • State-Return ceiling: 43.8% absolute lags frontier models (Gemini-3.1-Pro 88.0%) and even Qwen3-8B-Thinking (50.8%).
  • Merge trades off embodied skill: 58.52 vs the embodied-RL expert's 60.11 — "unified" costs a little.

06 Questions for Further Exploration

Fairness

How much of the 58.52% survives when baselines get the same tool schema and a few-shot warm-up — is the gap capability or protocol-formatting?

Transfer

Does token-budget RL + 16K cap transfer to vision-language-action models on real sensor streams, or is the conciseness gain specific to symbolic text?

Merge

What does the unnamed "different-lineage SFT" checkpoint contribute vs Stage-1 TIES alone — and is there a merge operator that preserves the full embodied expert?

Scaling

Would more maps, task types, and vision input close the State-Return gap, or is long-range spatial memory a fundamental 8B architectural limit?
