⚠ Critical Assessment · Rigor + Limitations
M·1

Methodology

ontology-building · empirical-SE

Not a benchmark study. The paper stacks four evidence layers with deliberately separated inference boundaries (Figure 3, Table 3) — each layer may only support the claim it can support, never more. A path token or frontmatter count is never allowed to prove execution semantics.

  1. Specification evidence — Agent Skills spec & host docs (Codex, Claude Code, Copilot, Hermes, OpenClaw) define the artifact envelope: required SKILL.md, YAML metadata, optional scripts/ references/ assets/, progressive-disclosure loading.
  2. Collection-scale corpus signals — frozen SkillMD-138K analyzed with narrow lexical detectors. Authors flag: path tokens measure authoring-convention prevalence, not existence or use.
  3. Fixed-revision case review — 15 boundary cases (12 in, 3 deliberate negatives) + 13 implementations pinned to canonical repo + 40-char commit + exact paths, each with positive / counterevidence / limitations.
  4. Independent empirical studies — Chen et al. (97,755 Skills), Gao et al. (18,463 registry + 23,199 personal, 3,709 reuse links), Hong et al. (238 smell study) cited under their own denominators.

S·3

Strengths

  • Inference-boundary hygiene. Each evidence layer (spec / corpus / case / independent study) is mapped to exactly what it can and cannot support — Table 3 blocks the "file exists ⇒ behavior realized" fallacy.
  • Operational, falsifiable membership test (C1–C3 + LC) — disagreement becomes inspectable via counterevidence fields, not a vague definition.
  • Reproducible by construction — frozen corpus hashes, 40-character commits, released detector definitions and reconstruction scripts.
  • Source-grounded pattern mappings with a 7-element admission protocol and misuse discriminators — resists pattern-name rubber-stamping.
  • Honest, non-promotional tone — explicit negative results, explicit disclaimers on word coinage and population estimates.
L·4

Limitations

  • No ML benchmarks. Zero task-success / accuracy / F1 deltas — limits comparability with empirical SE or agent-capability work.
  • Purposive case selection, no inter-rater reliability — the central discriminability claim rests on the authors' own coding.
  • Lexical-only corpus signals — frontmatter / path-token detectors cannot establish semantic correctness, runtime use, or cross-Host parity.
  • Fast-moving, partly unreviewed precursors — SkillOS, SkillFab, Gao & Hong smell studies are 2026 preprints.
  • Construct ambiguity at the compound end — C1 primacy is hardest to judge exactly where it matters most (gstack, ECC).
  • Prospective, unvalidated extensions — Collaborative Evolution & Forward-Deployed Engineering have no longitudinal evidence. Single-group authorship.
Q·5

Open Questions for Further Exploration

reproducibility · field trials
  1. Inter-rater agreement. Would blind third-party coders reproduce the 12-in / 3-ex boundary decisions? What is Cohen's κ on the same 15 units?
  2. Downstream correlation. Does Skillware membership track defect rate, reuse frequency, or time-to-first-useful-deployment in a controlled field trial vs. code-first delivery?
  3. Operationalizing C1. For contestable compound units (gstack, ECC), can a behavioral-primacy metric (share of runtime decisions routed through Skill source) replace analyst judgment?
  4. Quantifying the runtime equation. Can paired trials fix (task, Host, model, runtime, tools, permissions) and measure between-run variance, latency, cost, recovery?
  5. De-biasing prevalence. How does the ~23.2% path-token estimate shift under repository-level resolution and dynamic trace collection?
  6. Closing Adaptation-Enabled. What minimal evidence-gating + rollback apparatus makes Self-Evolution satisfy identity preservation end-to-end?
