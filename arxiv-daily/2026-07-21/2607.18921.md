> **一句话总结：** 本文在一个受控的合成 Lean 战术预测基准上系统检验电路抽取的方法学选择，发现精确边列表在不同稀疏度 checkpoint 间几乎不可复现（接近随机基线），而较粗的注意力头集合和节点数排序则保持稳定，据此提出电路级结论必须声明所报告的图、抽取规则、剪枝阈值与比较层级。

> **TL;DR:** The authors argue that circuit-extraction findings are under-determined by behavior preservation alone, and prove it on a controlled synthetic Lean tactic-prediction benchmark across dense and weight-sparse (25-90%) transformers. Comparing dense vs. 75%-sparse RL checkpoints, exact edge-list overlap is low (Jaccard@10 of 0.163 success / 0.142 near-miss, with the near-miss split statistically indistinguishable from a 0.117 random top-k baseline, p=0.106), while the set of selected attention heads overlaps strongly (0.666 / 0.554, both far above their random baselines). The ranking of RL-initialization conditions by graph size stays stable (3/3 agreement) across three graph objects, coupled-vs-factorized Q/K representations, and a loss-budget sweep. They distill the results into a reporting practice requiring the reported graph, extraction rule, pruning threshold, Q/K representation, and comparison level to be stated with every circuit claim.

## 核心贡献

- 首次在一个"任务结构由构造已知"的受控基准上分离了电路抽取的"方法学选择"与"机制差异"这两个混淆因素：固定 Lean 证明规则、随机化表面形式，使不同 checkpoint 间电路的差异可以归因于抽取/比较选择而非任务本身。
- 量化证明"精确边列表"在 dense 与 weight-sparse checkpoint 之间几乎不可复现（success/near-miss 的 Jaccard@10 仅 0.163/0.142，near-miss 甚至与随机基线无统计显著差异），而较粗的"路由注意力头集合"则稳定可复现（0.666/0.554，远超随机基线 0.199/0.185）。
- 系统审计了三类电路对象（core circuit / extended support graph / fixed-loss pruning graph）与两种 query/key 节点表示（coupled vs factorized），证明这些选择不改变"按图大小对 RL 初始化条件排序"这一结论（3/3 一致），且在 τ∈{0.04,…,0.20} 的损失预算扫描下同样成立。
- 提出"bridge fraction"指标（组合任务电路节点中位于对应原子任务电路之外的比例），发现 curriculum 初始化的 RL 同时取得最大组合精度提升（+24.64 / +31.27 点）与最大 bridge fraction（0.343 / 0.161，其余条件 ≤0.054），作为行为-结构关联。
- 提炼出一套面向电路抽取研究的报告规范：每条电路级结论必须声明所报告的图、抽取规则、剪枝阈值、Q/K 节点表示、比较层级以及边界/负控制检查。

## Key Contributions

- Isolates a methodological confound in circuit analysis: on a synthetic Lean tactic-prediction benchmark where atomic/compositional structure is known by construction and surface form is randomized, differences between extracted circuits can be attributed to extraction/comparison choices rather than to uncontrolled task changes.
- Demonstrates quantitatively that the exact component-to-component edge list is the least reproducible level of description: between dense and 75%-sparse RL checkpoints it reaches Jaccard@10 of only 0.163 (success) and 0.142 (near-miss), with the near-miss split statistically indistinguishable from a random top-k baseline (0.117, p=0.106).
- Shows that coarser summaries stay stable: the set of attention heads selected by top routing edges overlaps at Jaccard@10 of 0.666 / 0.554, far above their random baselines (0.199 / 0.185), so head-level claims can be valid where edge-level claims are not.
- Audits three graph objects (core circuit, extended support graph, fixed-loss pruning graph) under coupled-vs-factorized query/key node representations and a loss-budget sweep (τ from 0.04 to 0.20), finding the RL-initialization-condition ranking by graph size is preserved (3/3 agreement) across all these choices.
- Introduces a "bridge fraction" metric (compositional-task circuit nodes outside the matched atomic-task circuits) and links it to behavior: curriculum-initialized RL yields the largest compositional accuracy gains and the largest bridge fractions.
- Distills the findings into a concrete reporting practice mandating that circuit-level claims state the reported graph, extraction rule, pruning threshold, Q/K representation, comparison level, and boundary/negative-control checks.

## Methodology

The work is a controlled stability audit of circuit extraction, not a new extraction method. Its design rests on making the task known by construction so that extraction/comparison choices are the only free variable.

**Benchmark.** Nine synthetic Lean tactic-prediction tasks: four atomic (single proof rule, e.g. `exact And.intro h_p h_q`) and five compositional (multi-rule, e.g. `exact Or.inl (And.intro h_p h_q)`). Examples are generated dynamically from fixed propositional proof rules with randomized proposition/hypothesis names and distractor premises, reducing fixed-string memorization. Because examples are sampled rather than fixed, behavioral results are reported on two independently sampled test sets of 100 examples per task (Suite 1, Suite 2).

**Model family.** A single 8-layer transformer (d_model=2048, n_head=128, d_head=16, d_mlp=8192, context 128, ~415.7M params), trained at five weight-sparsity levels (dense, 25%, 50%, 75%, 90% sparse via top-k convention) with three seeds each. Supervised runs use 5B tokens; RL uses GRPO with 500 composition-only prompts/task, 8 generations/prompt, over 10 epochs. Three training-data families (baseline, moderately expanded, extensively expanded) crossed with three RL initialization conditions (Curriculum, Atomic-only, Composition-only, differing only in upstream supervised checkpoint).

**Three extracted circuit objects.**
- *Core circuit*: compact prediction-preserving subgraph from learned-mask extraction (exact-match accuracy after ablation as the primary criterion, masked CE + λℓ0 as tiebreaker). Instantiates De Cao / Bhaskar / Yu line.
- *Extended support graph*: same machinery but a wider reporting view retaining surrounding read/write/routing components.
- *Fixed-loss pruning graph*: smallest subgraph whose ablated loss ≤ threshold τ; instantiates Conmy/Bhaskar loss-budget line, main point τ=0.12, swept over {0.04,0.08,0.12,0.16,0.20}.

**Two Q/K representations.** Coupled (a single merged attn_qk node for query-side and key-side support) vs factorized (separate attn_q and attn_k node families), with extraction settings held fixed.

**Metrics.** Behavior = exact-match accuracy with Acc_mean = mean of both suites. Overlap = Jaccard index on top-10 entries (Jaccard@10), computed for structural bundles (by layer/source-target category/node family), semantic bundles (coarsened membership classes), and routing-head sets (grouped by layer+head). Bridge fraction per task = |C_t \ ⋃ C_t^(i)| / |C_t|. 95% bootstrap CIs over 16 matched task-object entries per split. Three negative controls: matched atomic, mismatched atomic, and size/layer-matched random.

**Comparison logic.** Each comparison asks whether the qualitative takeaway (ordering or sign) survives changing one extraction/comparison choice while holding checkpoints and tasks fixed.

## Results & Highlights

- **Exact-edge overlap is low and partly at chance level.** On the dense-vs-75%-sparse RL comparison over n=16 matched task-object entries, structural exact-edge Jaccard@10 is only **0.163 (success split)** and **0.142 (near-miss split)**; on the near-miss split this is statistically indistinguishable from the random top-k baseline of 0.117 (p=0.106). Semantic Jaccard@10 is numerically indistinguishable from structural in this comparison.
- **Routing-head overlap is high and above chance.** Grouping top routing edges by attention head raises Jaccard@10 to **0.666 (success)** and **0.554 (near-miss)**, clearly above their random top-k baselines (0.199 and 0.185 respectively) with separated bootstrap intervals.
- **Q/K split barely changes graph size and preserves ordering.** At τ=0.12, curriculum coupled-vs-factorized node-fraction gaps are at most **0.024** (fixed-loss pruning graph, extensively expanded family); gaps for core circuit are 0.008/0.010 and for extended support graph 0.003/0.013 (moderately/extensively expanded). Coupled and factorized versions agree on all **3/3** pairwise RL-condition rankings for every object and family.
- **Loss-budget sweep preserves the ordering.** Across τ∈{0.04,0.08,0.12,0.16,0.20}, the fixed-loss pruning coupled-vs-factorized curriculum gap stays at most **0.055**, and pairwise RL-condition ordering agreement is 3/3 at every tested τ. The largest pairwise flip probability (bootstrap) is 0.232.
- **Curriculum RL gives the largest compositional gains.** Averaged over five sparsity levels on Suite 1, curriculum-initialized RL raises compositional accuracy by **+24.64 points (43.83→68.47)** in the moderately expanded family and **+31.27 points (46.73→78.00)** in the extensively expanded family; overall accuracy rises to 73.47 and 76.95 respectively. Atomic-only RL gains are ≤6 points; composition-only gains are 14-18 points; their mean accuracies are at most 33.37.
- **Largest gains track largest bridge fraction.** Bridge fraction is **0.343** (moderately expanded curriculum cell) and **0.161** (extensively expanded curriculum cell), versus ≤0.054 in all other RL conditions — but the authors stress this is an association driven by the two curriculum cells, not a causal effect size.

## Authors & Credibility

Yang Sheng (Fudan University, Shanghai Innovation Institute) and Jie Fu (IQuest Research). The work is methodologically careful: three seeds per sparsity level, two independently sampled evaluation suites, 95% bootstrap confidence intervals over matched task-object entries, three explicit negative-control conditions (matched atomic, mismatched atomic, random), random top-k baselines for every overlap metric, a held-out boundary-test protocol, and a publicly released reproducibility package (data generators, supervised/GRPO training, circuit-extraction, fixed-loss pruning sweep) at the linked GitHub. The paper's framing as a stability audit rather than a new method, plus explicit caveats that bridge-fraction is an association not a causal claim, indicate intellectual honesty.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** High on framing, moderate on technique. The core message (circuit claims are under-determined by behavior preservation) is well-aligned with recent causal-abstraction and faithfulness-robustness work (Geiger et al. 2025, Miller et al. 2024, Shi et al. 2024, Tigges et al. 2024, Méloux et al. 2025), but the contribution is to *make the confound controllable* via a synthetic benchmark where atomic/compositional structure is known by construction — a genuinely useful and under-exploited angle.
- **Rigor:** Strong on controls. The use of random top-k baselines, bootstrap CIs, matched/mismatched/random negative controls, and two evaluation suites is above average for the interpretability literature. The honest reporting that the near-miss exact-edge Jaccard@10 (0.142) is statistically indistinguishable from its 0.117 random baseline (p=0.106) — i.e. that edge-level claims on near-miss are basically noise — is exactly the kind of result most papers would bury.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code released, model/token budgets and hyperparameters specified, ~1.2×10^19 FLOPs for the dominant 5B-token supervised run is documented. However, detailed graph evidence is local to a small set of checkpoints (dense and 75%-sparse, 16+16 matched task-object entries) with "representative exemplars rather than frequency estimates over a larger set" — a real scope limitation.
- **Significance:** The reporting-practice recommendation is the most durable contribution and is immediately actionable for the field; the empirical finding that head-level summaries are reproducible while edge-level ones are not is a concrete, citable result.
- **Red flags / caveats:**
  - The benchmark is a single 8-layer, ~415.7M-parameter transformer on synthetic Lean tasks; the authors themselves note that "shared proof-state generation rules may also make format-level cues shared across tasks easier to observe than in more heterogeneous families," and that accuracy is not monotonic in weight sparsity under the fixed recipe — so generalization to large deployed LMs is unvalidated.
  - The headline bridge-fraction ↔ compositional-gain association rests on only **two curriculum cells** being high on both axes; the authors correctly label it a "directional pattern across conditions rather than a measured effect size," but readers may still over-read it as evidence that bridge components *cause* the gains.
  - The graph-level analysis is anchored on a single pre-specified comparison point (dense vs 75%); although Table 18/Figure 8 extend overlap checks to other sparse anchors and cross-seed pairs, the central causal-style claims about object-level summaries are tied to that anchor.
  - A weak/implicit baseline point: semantic Jaccard@10 being "numerically indistinguishable" from structural in the main comparison slightly undercuts the claim that the coarsening hierarchy adds information beyond the head level.

## Strengths

- A clean experimental design that genuinely isolates extraction/comparison choices from task-mechanism differences — the synthetic, rule-generated benchmark with known atomic/compositional structure is the right tool for the question.
- The central negative result (exact edge lists are not reproducible, sometimes at chance) is reported openly with a p-value against a random baseline rather than spun.
- Comprehensive sweep across three graph objects, two Q/K representations, five loss budgets, five sparsity levels, three seeds, and three training-distribution families.
- The bridge-fraction metric and the read→Q/K-selector→value-mixture→write→MLP functional-path observation (Figure 4) are interpretable and tie the abstract stability argument to a concrete mechanistic picture.
- The distilled reporting practice is a concrete, low-cost field-level contribution.

## Limitations

- Single architecture, single scale (415.7M params, 8 layers), single synthetic benchmark family; the authors explicitly flag validating at larger scale, other architectures, and more heterogeneous benchmarks as future work.
- Graph evidence is confined to a small set of chosen checkpoints (dense + 75%-sparse) with 16+16 matched entries and representative exemplars rather than population-level frequency estimates.
- The behavioral-structure association (bridge fraction) is supported by only two high-leverage curriculum cells and is explicitly non-causal.
- Boundary tests measure behavior under surface/format shift; circuit-level stability under shift is left as a separate, unvalidated target.
- Shared proof-state generation rules may inflate format-cue sharing relative to heterogeneous natural-language tasks, potentially making the synthetic setting more favorable to coarse-level stability than realistic settings.

## Relevance

该论文对关心"机制可解释性 / circuit extraction 可靠性"的研究者高度相关：它用受控基准量化了电路抽取中方法学选择对结论的影响，并给出可直接采纳的报告规范。对于做 LLM 可解释性、稀疏模型分析或定理证明（Lean）的研究工作，其关于"精确边列表不可复现、注意力头集合可复现"的结论对如何陈述和比较电路结论具有直接的方法论指导价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the "exact-edge low / routing-head high" reproducibility pattern hold for large natural-language models and tasks (e.g. indirect-object identification, entity tracking), or is it partly an artifact of the synthetic Lean family's shared format cues?
- At what model scale does the edge-level reproducibility break down or recover — is there a scale regime where exact edges become stable, or does instability grow?
- Can the bridge-fraction ↔ compositional-gain association be turned into a causal claim, e.g. by selectively ablating bridge nodes and measuring the isolated effect on compositional accuracy?
- How do other extraction choices not audited here (ablation type, reference distribution, attribution method) interact with the reported-vs-stability trade-off?
- Does the recommended reporting practice, if adopted, change the conclusions of existing published circuit analyses when the extraction parameters are re-stated and re-checked?
