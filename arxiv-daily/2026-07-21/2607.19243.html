01

Methodology

measure → intervene → audit

A layered study: first quantify cross-lingual factual inconsistency across four SOTA LLMs, then apply progressively deeper interventions on Gemma 3 12B Instruct, then audit for collateral damage and global transfer. Answers are canonicalized into English and compared distributionally — via pairwise Jensen-Shannon distance (JSD), target probability mass (TPM), paired Wilcoxon signed-rank tests, and CLES effect sizes — rather than single-shot exact match.

01 · Prompting
Shallowest
Zero-shot persona prompt augments the English query with a target-language demographic — pure in-context retrieval, no weights touched.
02 · Steering
Mechanistic
Contrastive Activation Addition injects a directional vector into the residual stream. Systematic sweep over all 48 layers × 15 multipliers, three contrast-pair variants (v0/v1/v2).
03 · Adapting
Parameter-level
Lightweight LoRA DPO adapters (attention + FFN projections) trained from two sources: benchmark-derived and generalization-derived contrast pairs.

An independent held-out safety split measures signed ΔTPM / ΔJSD against the unmodified baseline, and a novel 50-query-per-language cultural scenario set probes whether an intervention induces a global persona shift versus a localized benchmark patch.

03

Strengths & limitations

honest seams included

Strengths

what lands well

  • Layered controlled design — prompting → activation → weights turns disparate techniques into one clean comparison.
  • Strong statistical hygiene — paired Wilcoxon, CLES effect sizes, significance vs. unmodified baseline, held-out safety split.
  • Novel generalization artifact — 50 culturally-rooted, English-only, second-person scenarios per language for transfer testing.
  • Crisp citable result — the 32.7% → 86.0% global persona shift vs. bounded mechanistic behavior.
  • Intellectual honesty — explicitly flags single-model scope, narrow supervision, and GPT-5.1 post-processing noise.

Limitations

where it frays

  • Single base model — all conclusions rest on Gemma 3 12B Instruct; may not transfer across architectures.
  • Only 3 target languages — Swedish and Korean benchmark annotations left unused for steering / DPO.
  • Narrow supervision — small factual contrast set likely explains CAA / DPO's weak generalization.
  • Distributional, not correctness — strongest as a consistency analysis, not a factual-accuracy verdict.
  • Extraction noise — GPT-5.1 canonicalization / judgment could not be fully manually verified at scale.
  • CAA sweep metric mismatch — rule-based selection vs. richer reporting metric may slightly inflate CAA numbers.
04

Open questions

for further exploration
Does "prompting wins" survive on non-Latin-script (Korean) or MoE architectures like Qwen 3 30B-A3B, left as future work?
Would scaling the generalization set far beyond 50 queries/language let DPO/CAA match prompting's global shift — or is boundedness fundamental?
Can safety/efficacy improve by composing methods — persona for global shift + low-magnitude CAA for targeted refinement — without CAA-only collateral damage?
How sensitive are CAA layer/multiplier optima to instruction-tuning regime — is there a principled (non-sweep) locator?
Given GPT-5.1 canonicalization, how robust are the JSD deltas to extraction-layer perturbations — would a human-verified subset flip any verdict?
If cross-lingual inconsistency is partly a selection problem, does that change how we should evaluate multilingual factual accuracy at all?
