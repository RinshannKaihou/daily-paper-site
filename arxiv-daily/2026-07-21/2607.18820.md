> **一句话总结：** 本文从因果视角将 CoT 忠实性定义为"指令→推理→答案"的因果链 Z→X→Y，提出 CASE 框架——训练阶段用反事实 CoT、偏置指令、空指令三类干预数据做选择性损失 SFT 来强化 X→Y 并抑制 Z→Y 捷径，推理阶段用注意力掩码阻断指令 token 到答案 token 的直接通路；在 Llama-3.1-8B/Qwen3-8B/DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B 三个模型与 ARC-Easy/ARC-Challenge/StrategyQA/LogiQA 四个基准上，CASE 在全部 12 个设定上取得最佳 G-mean_Faith，相对最强基线平均提升 37%（排除依赖双模型的 FRODO 后提升 65%），同时保持与最强基线持平的平均准确率（74.88% vs 74.80%）。

> **TL;DR:** The paper reframes Chain-of-Thought (CoT) faithfulness as the causal chain Z→X→Y (instruction → reasoning → answer) and proposes **CASE**, a two-stage framework: training-time **Causal Alignment** augments SFT with counterfactual-CoT, biased-instruction, and empty-instruction datasets under a selective loss that supervises only answer tokens on interventions, and inference-time **Structural Enforcement** masks attention from instruction tokens to answer tokens. Across three LLMs (**Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen3-8B, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B**) and four benchmarks (**ARC-Easy, ARC-Challenge, StrategyQA, LogiQA**), CASE wins the best overall faithfulness (G-mean_Faith) in all 12 model–dataset settings, with a **37% average relative improvement over the per-setting strongest baseline** (DU_c, FRODO, FoCus) — rising to 65% when the dual-module FRODO is excluded — while holding average accuracy at 74.88% versus 74.80% for the strongest baselines, and showing stronger cross-dataset faithfulness transfer (avg G-M 0.650 vs Base-LLM 0.366, +102%).

## 核心贡献

- **因果视角的问题形式化**：将 CoT 忠实性严格定义为生成过程应满足 pθ(x,y|z)=pθ(x|z)pθ(y|x)（即 Y⊥Z|X 的忠实因果链 Z→X→Y），指出标准自回归模型 pθ(y|z,x) 容许"指令→答案"的直接捷径，从而把"忠实性"从含糊的解释质量问题转换为可干预、可结构约束的因果机制问题。
- **训练时因果对齐（TCA）**：构造三类干预数据集——反事实 CoT 集 D_B（同指令、换推理链、换答案，强化 X→Y）、偏置指令集 D_C（指令加错误答案线索但 CoT 与答案不变，抑制 Z→Y）、空指令集 D_D（移除指令、仅留 CoT 与答案，逼模型纯靠 CoT 出答案）；并设计选择性损失——只在标准集 D_A 上同时监督 CoT 与答案 token，在三个辅助集上仅监督答案 token，避免被合成 CoT 干扰。
- **推理时结构强制（ISE）**：在生成答案 token 时对所有层、所有注意力头把 (答案位置 i, 指令位置 j) 的 pre-softmax logit 置为 −∞，从计算图层面直接删除显式的 Z→Y 注意力路径，但保留 CoT 侧中介状态 H_X；相比"删掉指令只用 CoT 推理"的 COD 方案，能保留指令条件化的 CoT 侧隐状态，取得更高准确率与忠实度（ARC-Easy G-M 0.646 vs COD 0.374）。
- **信息论理论分析**：用四个命题把各训练损失项映射到三条互补准则（最大化 I(X;Y)、最大化 I(X;Y|Z)、最小化 I(Y;Z|X)），并证明注意力掩码使答案生成在固定 H_X 后对指令 token 状态无直接计算图依赖，即 p_AM_θ(y|z,h_X)=p_AM_θ(y|h_X)。
- **跨数据集泛化与完整消融**：在 ARC-Challenge→OpenBookQA、StrategyQA→ARC-Easy 两种迁移设定下证明 CASE 的忠实性增益可迁移（+102% 相对 Base，+61% 相对最强基线），而三个基线的平均 G-M 反而低于 Base LLM；消融显示 TCA 主要提升 CIE/CDE，ISE 主要提升 FT/EA，二者互补。

## Key Contributions

- **Causal formulation of CoT faithfulness.** Reframes faithfulness as the factorization pθ(x,y|z)=pθ(x|z)pθ(y|x) (i.e., Y⊥Z|X), making explicit that standard autoregressive LLMs permit an unfaithful direct Z→Y shortcut that existing fine-tuning baselines (DU_c, FoCus, FRODO) do not structurally remove.
- **Training-time Causal Alignment (TCA).** Constructs three intervention datasets — counterfactual-CoT D_B (same instruction, different chain, different answer), biased-instruction D_C (misleading answer cue injected into the prompt while chain+answer held fixed), and empty-instruction D_D (instruction removed, chain+answer preserved) — combined with a **selective loss** that supervises both CoT and answer tokens on D_A but only answer tokens on the auxiliary sets, preventing the model from merely imitating synthetic chains.
- **Inference-time Structural Enforcement (ISE).** Applies an attention mask that sets A^ℓ,h_{ij}=−∞ for all answer positions i, instruction positions j, layers ℓ, and heads h during answer generation, structurally deleting the explicit Z→Y attention path while preserving the CoT-side mediator H_X; Appendix A.5 shows this beats the simpler "delete the instruction, decode from CoT alone" (COD) variant.
- **Information-theoretic analysis.** Four propositions tie the losses to maximizing I(X;Y), maximizing I(X;Y|Z), minimizing I(Y;Z|X), and to the structural fact that, post-masking, p_AM_θ(y|z,h_X)=p_AM_θ(y|h_X).
- **Comprehensive, transfer-focused evaluation.** Wins G-mean_Faith in all 12 main model–dataset settings (+37% rel. vs strongest baselines, +65% when FRODO is excluded) and in all 6 cross-dataset settings (+102% vs Base LLM), with a clean ablation isolating the complementary roles of TCA (improves CIE/CDE) and ISE (improves FT/EA).

## Methodology

CASE operationalizes the faithful chain Z→X→Y in a single LLM via two complementary stages.

**Stage 1 — Training-time Causal Alignment.** Starting from a standard SFT set D_A={(z_i,x_i,y_i)} (CoTs and answers generated by DeepSeek-V3.2, keeping only instances where the generated answer matches gold), CASE builds three auxiliary datasets:
- D_B (counterfactual-CoT): same z_i, but a counterfactual answer y′_i≠y_i with the chain x′_i that derives it — trains the answer head to respond to chain changes under the same instruction.
- D_C (biased-instruction): the instruction is rewritten as z′_i by appending "An MIT professor believes the answer is {chosen}." with {chosen} a wrong option — discourages answer changes driven by misleading cues.
- D_D (empty-instruction): the question content is removed while CoT and answer are kept — forces answer prediction from the chain alone (for multiple-choice the option labels are retained; for true/false a fraction β=80% of question tokens is masked).

The training objective is a **selective loss**: full CoT+answer supervision on D_A, but only answer-token loss on D_B, D_C, D_D, so the auxiliary data teach how a *given* chain should determine the answer rather than forcing the model to reproduce synthetic reasoning:
L_train = L_main + L_X-int + L_Z-int + L_Z-drop.

**Stage 2 — Inference-time Structural Enforcement.** At decoding, CASE modifies the attention mask only while generating answer tokens: for every answer position, every instruction position, every layer and head, the pre-softmax logit is set to −∞, so answer tokens cannot directly aggregate from instruction tokens but can still attend to CoT tokens and previously generated answer tokens. The CoT itself is still generated normally from the full instruction. This removes the explicit computation-graph Z→Y edge while preserving the instruction-conditioned CoT-side hidden states H_X.

**Theory.** Three propositions map the losses to lower/upper bounds on I(X;Y), I(X;Y|Z), and I(Y;Z|X) respectively (e.g., L_Z-drop maximizes a lower bound on I(X;Y); the counterfactual-CoT augmentation makes the bound useful because the same instruction is paired with two different chain–answer pairs). A fourth proposition proves that, under the mask, p_AM_θ(y|z,h_X)=p_AM_θ(y|h_X) — i.e., once the CoT-side state is fixed, the instruction has no further direct computation-graph influence on the answer.

All methods use identical LoRA (r=64, α=128), few-shot prompts, and decoding settings; CoT demonstrations and counterfactual CoTs for both CASE and the baselines are produced by DeepSeek-V3.2.

## Results & Highlights

- **Overall faithfulness, 12/12 settings won.** CASE achieves the best G-mean_Faith in every one of the 3 models × 4 benchmarks settings, with a **37% average per-setting relative improvement over the strongest baseline** in each setting; when FRODO (whose gains rely on a decoupled dual-module pipeline rather than a single LLM) is excluded, the relative improvement rises to **65%**.
- **Accuracy is preserved, not traded away.** CASE's average accuracy across the 12 settings is **74.88%**, essentially equal to the **74.80%** averaged over the per-setting strongest baselines, even though CASE sometimes sacrifices a fraction of a point (e.g., on ARC-Challenge/Llama-3.1-8B, CASE 86.8% vs DU_c 88.2%) to buy large faithfulness gains.
- **Near-elimination of the direct shortcut on ARC-Easy/Llama-3.1-8B.** CASE drives **CDE down to 0.002** vs the best baseline FRODO at **0.156** (and Base-LLM 0.358), while lifting **FT to 0.636** vs FRODO 0.406 and **EA to 0.281** vs FRODO 0.211; G-mean_Faith reaches **0.646** vs FoCus 0.194 (the strongest baseline there) — a 3.3× gap.
- **Strong cross-dataset transfer.** On 6 transfer settings (ARC-Challenge→OpenBookQA and StrategyQA→ARC-Easy across three models), CASE averages G-mean_Faith **0.650**, beating the Base LLM (0.366) by **+102%** and the per-setting strongest baseline by **+61%**; notably, **none of DU_c (0.310), FRODO (0.365), or FoCus (0.328) exceeds the Base LLM** on average, indicating their faithfulness gains do not transfer.
- **Per-metric dominance.** Across all faithfulness metrics and settings, CASE ranks first in **67%** of cases and second in a further **21%**; the gains are largest on Filler Tokens and Early Answering (the two metrics Zaman & Srivastava 2025 identify as the most reliable).
- **Efficiency.** Inference runtime on ARC-Easy/Llama-3.1-8B is **4.55 s/question for CASE vs 13.71 s for FRODO** — CASE is ~**3.0× faster** than the dual-module baseline while being comparable to DU_c (4.79 s) and FoCus (3.45 s).
- **Ablation confirms complementarity.** Training-only (TCA) mainly fixes the causal metrics (raises CIE, lowers CDE) but barely moves FT/EA on ARC-Easy (FT 0.078, EA 0.037); inference-only (ISE) drives FT/EA up (0.610/0.266) and CDE near zero; full CASE (G-M 0.646 ARC-Easy, 0.650 ARC-Challenge) strictly beats both single-stage variants.
- **Masking beats instruction deletion.** A "CoT-only decoding" (COD) ablation that simply removes the instruction before answer generation scores G-M **0.374** vs CASE **0.646** on ARC-Easy, confirming that preserving instruction-conditioned CoT-side states matters.

- **Ziming Wang** (SUSTech) and **Yinghua Yao** (CFAR, A*STAR) lead; **Ke Tang** (SUSTech) and **Xin Yao** (Lingnan University, a distinguished name in evolutionary computation and a CCRA-AI chair) are senior authors — this is an established ML group with prior CoT/interpretability publications, lending methodological credibility.
- The work is positioned as a principled extension of Bao et al. (2025)'s causal view of CoT and a direct correction to FRODO (Paul et al., EMNLP-Findings 2024) and FoCus (NeurIPS 2025 workshop), all properly cited and honestly compared.
- Code is publicly released at https://github.com/oddwang/CASE, and all baselines use the same LoRA config, few-shot prompts, and decoding settings — a genuine effort at apples-to-apples comparison. A random seed (42) is fixed; DeepSeek-V3.2 generates the CoT data while an *independent* verifier (GPT-5.5) audits it (AVR=1.0, CAC≈0.99, SR≥0.986 across four datasets), avoiding the self-verification trap.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-good. The causal reframing Z→X→Y is inherited from Bao et al. (2025); the genuinely new pieces are (a) the triple counterfactual/biased/empty-instruction augmentation with a *selective* loss, and (b) the inference-time attention mask that structurally deletes the Z→Y edge in a single LLM — a cleaner design than FRODO's dual-module pipeline. The information-theoretic propositions are correct but are essentially standard variational bounds (Prop. 1 is the classic ELBO-style MI lower bound; Prop. 3 is a convexity-of-KL upper bound), not new theory.
- **Rigor:** Strong on controls (identical LoRA/prompt/decoding across all methods, independent LLM verifier for data quality, runtime analysis, β sensitivity sweep). However, the headline **CDE→~0 results are partly mechanical, not empirical**: because ISE *by construction* prevents answer tokens from seeing instruction tokens, CDE — which holds the CoT fixed and swaps the question — is guaranteed to drop sharply whenever the CoT-side state H_X is insensitive to the question rewrite. The paper does flag that masking does not remove instruction info already baked into H_X, but it does not isolate how much of the CDE gain is structural-forced versus learned.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Public code, fixed seed, listed checkpoints (Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, Qwen3-8B, DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B, DeepSeek-V3.2-Exp, GPT-5.5), full prompt templates in Appendix A.11, and a quality-audit table. Missing: no error bars, no multi-seed variance, no significance tests — all 12 "wins" are single-run point estimates, so the 37% headline could look different under resampling.
- **Significance:** Practically useful for interpretability/safety work — faithfulness is a real and unsolved problem for CoT — but the **evaluation is confined to small QA-style benchmarks (ARC, StrategyQA, LogiQA)** where reasoning is shallow. There is no test on math reasoning (GSM8K, MATH) or code, where CoT is most impactful and where attention masking is most likely to hurt accuracy; the StrategyQA CIE actually *drops* below FRODO (0.834 vs 0.994), hinting the method can trade one faithfulness axis for another.
- **Red flags:**
  1. **Dataset-specific β tuning.** The masking ratio β is hand-set to 80% for true/false tasks after a sweep whose G-M actually peaks at β=70%; the authors pick β=80% "for accuracy," an admitted post-hoc choice that inflates the perceived robustness of the method.
  2. **Trivial biased-instruction construction.** The "misleading cue" is a single fixed sentence ("An MIT professor believes the answer is {chosen}.") — easy to learn to ignore, which may overstate L_Z-int's effect; more adversarial paraphrases are not tested.
  3. **Generator/evaluator coupling.** DeepSeek-V3.2 produces *both* the training counterfactual CoTs *and* the CIE/CDE evaluation counterfactuals, so a systematic bias in what DeepSeek-V3.2 counts as a "counterfactual chain" would inflate CASE's numbers relative to its own training distribution.
  4. **Circular masking wins on CDE.** Because the attention mask directly removes the very path CDE probes, the CDE column is close to definitional for CASE rather than independent evidence of faithfulness.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated causal formulation, with theory that actually maps each loss term to a named information-theoretic criterion.
- The single-model design (vs FRODO's two-module pipeline) is simpler, ~3× faster at inference, and still strictly stronger on the faithfulness aggregate.
- Genuinely fair baselines: identical LoRA, prompts, decoding, and the same strong LLM (DeepSeek-V3.2) for data generation across methods.
- Strong and unusual cross-dataset transfer story — the baselines do not transfer at all (all below Base LLM on average), while CASE improves markedly.
- Selective loss is a thoughtful design choice: masking CoT-token loss on auxiliary data prevents the model from being forced to mimic synthetic/counterfactual chains.

## Limitations

- Evaluated only on four short-form QA benchmarks (ARC-Easy, ARC-Challenge, StrategyQA, LogiQA) — no math/code/long-form reasoning, where the accuracy cost of attention masking is untested and potentially large.
- No multi-seed variance or significance testing; the "37% improvement" headline rests on single-run point estimates.
- CDE gains are partly an artifact of the mask's structural definition rather than learned behavior, and the paper does not decompose the two.
- The β=80% masking ratio for true/false tasks is a post-hoc, dataset-specific choice (G-M peaks at 70%); generalization to other formats is unclear.
- Attention masking requires per-token position-type bookkeeping (which tokens are instruction vs CoT vs answer) and assumes the answer options can be cleanly separated from the question — non-trivial for free-form generation.
- The attention mask cannot remove instruction information already encoded into the CoT-side hidden states H_X; "faithfulness" here is structural at the visible-token level, not a guarantee that the model has no hidden instruction dependence.

## Relevance

对于关注 LLM 推理忠实性与可解释性、因果推断在大模型中的应用，以及通过 SFT/注意力干预改进 CoT 的研究者，本文提供了一个结构清晰、理论支撑完整且开源可复现的方法，值得纳入"CoT 忠实性"专题的对比基线；但其评测局限于浅层 QA 任务，对做数学/代码推理场景的读者需谨慎外推。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does CASE's attention mask survive on math reasoning (GSM8K, MATH) and code generation, where the CoT is longer and the instruction–answer link is tighter — and at what accuracy cost?
- How much of the CDE→~0 result is structurally forced by the mask versus genuinely learned from TCA? A decomposition experiment (e.g., ISE applied to a Base-LLM with no TCA, vs full CASE, with an *independent* counterfactual generator) would isolate the two.
- Would a more adversarial biased-instruction set (paraphrased, multiple cue types, stronger social-proof wording) erode the L_Z-int gains, and does the method generalize beyond the single fixed "MIT professor" cue?
- Can the instruction information leaked through the CoT-side hidden states H_X be measured directly (e.g., via a probe on H_X predicting z), giving a faithfulness metric that is not trivially forced by the mask?
- How does CASE interact with post-hoc candidate-selection methods (which the authors note are orthogonal) — does stacking multiply the faithfulness gains, or hit a ceiling?
- Is the 37% (or 65%) improvement stable across seeds, and does it hold when the training CoTs come from a different generator than the evaluation counterfactuals (breaking the current DeepSeek-V3.2 coupling)?
