GLID: Gated Local Intrinsic Dimension Repairs the Blind Spots of Face-Forgery Detectors

Guang Yang (UCLA)  ·  Fengchen Liu (UC Berkeley)  ·  PhiLab Foundation

face-forgery-detection local-intrinsic-dimension training-free deepfake-generalization vision-transformer
▶ Key Finding

A training-free 12-D local intrinsic dimensionality signal, gated into a fine-tuned detector, hits 0.805 mean AUC on a frozen 16-axis cross-generator benchmark — beating every retrained rival.

◆ Key Metric
+0.084 AUC
Lift on unseen generation families (0.731 → 0.816), while reenactment shifts only −0.005. Mean AUC 0.805 beats Effort (0.790) at 3 wins, 0 losses.
▸ Why This Matters
A label-free geometric signal that patches only the detector's blind axes — directly addressing cross-generator generalization, deepfake detection's core unsolved pain.
△ Critical Edge
Gains are modest in absolute terms (+0.015 over Effort) and concentrate on generation axes; cross-generator probabilities stay miscalibrated (ECE 0.126), so GLID ranks well but cannot be consumed as calibrated probability.

1Methodology

GLID fuses a training-free geometric signal with a fine-tuned CLIP-L detector behind a confidence gate, governed by two empirical laws.

A frozen DINOv3 ViT-L/16 maps a 256-px face crop to N=256 ℓ2-normalized patch tokens at each block. The Levina-Bickel MLE estimates local intrinsic dimension on cosine distances at blocks {6,12,18,23} × neighborhoods {8,16,32}, yielding a 12-D profile in 4.1 ms/image — label-free, deterministic, estimator-independent (TwoNN, MST/PHD agree).

GLID(x) = z + α · 4p(1−p) · w_ℓ · s_ℓ

The gate g=4p(1−p) opens only where the detector is uncertain (≈0.5): on covered axes GLID reduces to the base detector; on blind axes the geometry speaks. The single scalar α*=0.1 is calibrated purely in-distribution — provably blind to evaluation data.

Law 1 · Family-depth signature

GAN forgeries peak at the deepest block (StyleGAN 0.863 AUC); diffusion peaks mid-network (0.892) and collapses at the last layer (0.581). Split holds on 4 backbones, 3 estimators, non-face ProGAN.

Law 2 · Absorption boundary

Fine-tuning absorbs an auxiliary gain exactly where its training distribution covers. Injecting just 1% (113 images) of generation-family data erases a +0.100 LID gain.

+Strengths

  • Clean one-equation, training-free geometric signal on a frozen backbone; single scalar calibrated entirely in-distribution — no test-data tuning.
  • Two genuinely useful empirical laws with interventional support, including the absorption boundary telling practitioners when data beats machinery.
  • Rigorous evaluation: frozen 16-axis benchmark, identical crops, multi-seed means, paired-bootstrap stats, explicit loss disclosure.
  • Determinism that raises accuracy and cuts seed variance 5.5× — rare and valuable in fine-tuned deepfake detectors.
  • Cheaper at inference than comparable training-free baselines (RIGID, DCA).

−Limitations

  • Face-domain generation sources are thin: one GAN corpus (StyleGAN) and one mixed T2I pool; cross-domain checks mitigate but don't remove this.
  • No per-axis dominance: SBI stays significantly stronger on T2I and in-the-wild; small regressions on swap and DFDC vs the base detector.
  • DFDC stuck near 0.636 for every method; heavy compression suppresses both semantic and geometric evidence; lip-sync micro-edits open.
  • The 256-token LID estimator is biased at small k; protocol is frame-level only (no video aggregation).
  • Cross-generator probabilities remain miscalibrated (ECE 0.126) — reliable for ranking, not for probability consumption.

?Questions for Further Exploration

  1. Does the family-depth signature extend beyond StyleGAN/T2I — to mask-based reenactors, autoregressive image models, or video diffusion — or is it an artifact of two tested families?
  2. Can the absorption boundary be predicted quantitatively from the training distribution itself (e.g., a coverage metric), so practitioners know in advance whether GLID will help?
  3. Would fusing intra-image LID with the cross-sample DCA statistic via an unsupervised combiner cover both swap and generation blind axes that naive fusion failed?
  4. How does GLID behave under active post-processing (multi-generation recompression, GAN face enhancement) that reshapes the token manifold in unexpected ways?
  5. Can the 5.5× seed-variance reduction combine with video-level temporal aggregation to push DFDC / in-the-wild AUC meaningfully past the current 0.636 / 0.870 ceiling?
