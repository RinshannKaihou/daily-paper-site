> **一句话总结：** 一项预注册的复制研究在未做分歧度筛选的 SNLI / MultiNLI 开发集上检验"非单调算子降低标注一致率"这一结论，结果方向全部反转（七个对比的 Cliff's δ 均为正且远低于 0.10 的最小关注效应），表明该边界很可能是 ChaosNLI "3/5 多数票"筛选规则带来的结构，而非人群层面的真实属性。

> **TL;DR:** This preregistered replication tests whether hypotheses with non-upward monotonicity operators show lower annotator agreement in the unselected SNLI and MultiNLI dev sets, as an earlier ChaosNLI study found (Cliff's δ = −0.284). The registered prediction (δ < 0) is rejected: all seven contrasts return a small positive δ (e.g., MNLI matched +0.045, Holm p = 7.1 × 10⁻⁵, opposite sign), and every effect sits far below the preregistered smallest-effect-of-interest of 0.10. The earlier negative boundary appears to be an artifact of ChaosNLI's 3-of-5-majority disagreement-selection rule rather than a population-level property of NLI.

## 核心贡献

- **预注册的否定性复制结果**：在冻结标签器（v0.3）和预注册假设（δ < 0）的前提下，在 SNLI dev（9,986 项）、MNLI matched dev（10,000 项）、MNLI mismatched dev（9,946 项）上重测单调性与标注一致率的关系，结果方向全部反转——非单调项反而略高于单调项的一致率，七个对比的 δ 全为正且均小于 0.10 的 SESOI。
- **揭示"选择依赖"结构**：明确论证此前 arXiv:2607.15870 报告的负向边界（Cliff's δ = −0.284）很可能是 ChaosNLI "3/5 多数票"分歧度筛选规则的产物。由于该规则使四阶一致率序数在重叠子集上零方差，原本计划的"桥接相关分析"在算术上未定义，作者透明地以分级描述统计替代。
- **三层效度检验**：通过 Tier 2（基于 MED 实测错分率的 36 单元模拟，显示错分会把 δ 压向 0 而非制造它，所有单元 97.5 百分位 ≤ 0.064）和 Tier 3（200 项盲法人工重标，四类一致率 0.875，κ = 0.607）证明测量工具可信，确保"否定结果"具有证据价值。
- **透明的注册-结果对照**：附录给出逐条注册项与实际执行的对照表，唯一显著确认性对比（MNLI matched）方向与注册相反，作者据实报告。
- **方法学警示**：呼吁基于分歧度筛选的重标注资源（如 ChaosNLI）所做的 HLV 结构性结论必须显式声明其"选择条件"。

## Key Contributions

- **Preregistered negative replication**: Under a frozen tagger (v0.3) and a pre-registered directional hypothesis (δ < 0), the monotonicity–agreement link is re-measured on unselected SNLI dev (9,986 items), MNLI matched dev (10,000), and MNLI mismatched dev (9,946). All seven contrasts reverse sign and stay below the pre-registered SESOI of |δ| = 0.10.
- **Identifies a selection-conditional structure**: Shows the earlier negative boundary (Cliff's δ = −0.284, arXiv:2607.15870) is plausibly an artifact of ChaosNLI's 3-of-5-majority selection. The planned "bridge correlation" is undefined by arithmetic because the agreement ordinal has zero variance on the overlap; this is stated openly and replaced by level-wise descriptives.
- **Three-tier validity defense**: Tier 2 simulations (36-cell grid grounded in MED-measured flip rates, symmetric error 0.101) show misclassification shrinks δ rather than manufacturing it (max 97.5th percentile across cells = 0.064). Tier 3 blind manual re-tagging of 200 fresh items reaches four-class agreement 0.875 (Wilson CI [0.822, 0.914]) and κ = 0.607, supporting the measurement.
- **Fully transparent registration-vs-result concordance**: Appendices give an item-by-item concordance table; the only significant confirmatory contrast (MNLI matched) has the opposite sign to the registration, reported as registered.
- **Methodological warning**: Structure claims about human label variation built on disagreement-selected re-annotation resources should state their selection conditional explicitly.

## Methodology

**Data.** SNLI dev (9,986 items after excluding 14 four-label items), MNLI matched dev (10,000 items), and MNLI mismatched dev (9,946 items after excluding 54 rows from 27 duplicated pair IDs, analyzed as a pre-registered secondary corpus). All corpora carry five labels per item (the writer's label plus four validation labels). MED (5,382 pairs) is used only for tagger validation; the ChaosNLI overlap (1,514 SNLI rows → 1,507 after exclusion; 1,599 MNLI matched) is used only for the bridge analysis.

**Predictor.** A rule-based monotonicity operator tagger (v0.3, frozen before the study under pre-registration, no rule changes) labels each hypothesis as upward, downward, non-monotone, or mixed via a surface trigger scan. The analysis contrast is binary: purely upward vs. non-upward.

**Outcome.** A four-level agreement ordinal built from the five validation labels: no majority < 3/5 < 4/5 < 5/5. The primary analysis includes the no-majority level; excluding it is sensitivity analysis A.

**Statistics.** Tie-corrected Cliff's δ with two-sided Mann-Whitney U tests, percentile bootstrap CIs (10,000 resamples, seed 20260717), Holm correction over the two confirmatory contrasts (SNLI primary, MNLI matched primary), and a pre-registered SESOI of |δ| = 0.10. The registered prediction was δ < 0. The registration was written into the working repository on 2026-07-17 before the confirmatory analysis.

**Confound defense.** Proportional-odds ordinal models (M0 vs M1 with length, parse depth, and genre covariates); a Brant test triggers a multinomial substitute where the proportional-odds assumption fails (SNLI).

**Validity layers.** Tier 2 simulates label flips over a 36-cell grid (three corpora, three scenarios, MED-anchored plus fixed rates, 1,000 replications each). Tier 3 is a blind 200-item manual re-tagging audit (50 per stratum) against a written codebook.

## Results & Highlights

- **Registered prediction rejected — sign reversal in all contrasts.** All seven Cliff's δ values are positive (non-upward items agree slightly *more*). The SNLI primary contrast is non-significant (δ = +0.032, 95% CI [−0.011, +0.075], Holm p = 0.162). The MNLI matched primary contrast is significant but with the **opposite sign** to the registration (δ = +0.045, 95% CI [+0.024, +0.066], Holm p = 7.1 × 10⁻⁵); the secondary MNLI mismatched contrast gives the largest effect (δ = +0.059).
- **Every effect is far below the SESOI.** No confidence interval touches the pre-registered |δ| = 0.10 boundary; the largest δ observed is +0.059, versus the earlier study's δ = −0.284 on ChaosNLI (a non-commensurable 100-label entropy scale, so comparison is by sign and magnitude class only).
- **Item-level discriminability is essentially absent.** Ordered logistic pseudo-R² is 0.000093 (SNLI) and 0.00084 (MNLI matched), and AUC for separating high-agreement (4/5, 5/5) from low-agreement items is at chance (0.492 SNLI, 0.484 matched).
- **Confounders do not explain the reversal.** In the proportional-odds model M1 (with length, parse depth, genre), the monotonicity coefficient keeps its sign and stays away from zero in matched (−0.202, p = 2.4 × 10⁻⁶) and mismatched (−0.248, p = 7.3 × 10⁻⁹); negative coefficients here correspond to the positive δ because upward items sit slightly lower in agreement.
- **Level-wise structure runs against the registered direction.** In MNLI matched, the non-upward share is *highest* among unanimous items: 0.303 (no majority), 0.308 (3/5), 0.312 (4/5), 0.350 (5/5) — the share does not decrease as agreement rises. SNLI shows a flat low profile (0.045, 0.041, 0.060, 0.057).
- **Misclassification simulation cannot manufacture the effect.** Across all 36 Tier 2 cells, the 97.5th percentile of simulated δ never exceeds 0.064, so the below-SESOI conclusion never flips; at the MED-anchored symmetric rate, matched retains significance in 94.9% of replications and mismatched in every replication.
- **Manual audit supports the tagger.** Blind 200-item re-tagging reaches four-class agreement 0.875 (Wilson CI [0.822, 0.914]), binary agreement 0.905, Cohen's κ 0.607. The earlier no-codebook round (agreement 0.135) is reported as an instrument-specification result and excluded from the headline metric.

## Authors & Credibility

Single-author paper by Haram Choi at the University of Bremen. The work is anchored in formal-semantics NLI literature (MacCartney 2009 on monotonicity; Yanaka et al. 2019 MED; von Fintel 1999; Rett 2018) and the human-label-variation / perspectivism literature (Plank 2022; Pavlick & Kwiatkowski 2019; Nie et al. 2020 ChaosNLI; Bowman et al. 2015 SNLI; Williams et al. 2018 MNLI). Credibility rests on a pre-registration frozen on 2026-07-17 before the confirmatory analysis, an explicit registration-vs-result concordance (Appendix A), and a public reproduction repository (https://github.com/oudeis01/nli-hlv-selection) with AUDIT.md documenting the registration timeline, deviations, and negative results. The registration is a version-control record under the author's own control rather than a third-party registry, which the author openly flags as a procedural statement rather than independently attested evidence.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty**: Moderate but genuine. The replication itself is not conceptually novel, but the framing — isolating *selection dependence* as the explanation for a published HLV structure effect — is a sharp, well-targeted contribution that directly challenges an earlier finding (arXiv:2607.15870) by the same author, which is an unusually honest self-correction.
- **Rigor**: High. Pre-registered directional hypothesis, frozen tagger, SESOI, Holm correction, three-tier validity defense, and transparent reporting of the sign-reversed significant contrast all meet a strong standard. The honest treatment of the undefined "bridge correlation" (zero variance on the ChaosNLI overlap) and its replacement by descriptives is exemplary.
- **Reproducibility**: Strong. Every reported number is in the paper or regenerable from the repository; seeds are fixed (20260717) and recorded per run. The one set of numbers not script-regenerated (the Section 5 manual error-layer counts) is explicitly flagged as such.
- **Significance**: Methodologically important for the HLV/perspectivism community: it shows a structure claim estimated on a disagreement-selected resource can vanish and faintly reverse in the unselected population. The effect sizes are tiny, so the practical impact on downstream modeling is limited, but the cautionary message about selection conditionals is broadly applicable.
- **Red flags / weaknesses**: (1) **Single-author, single-rater manual tagging** — the reported κ = 0.607 is tagger-versus-human, not inter-human, so there is no independent estimate of human-human agreement on the monotonicity judgment. (2) **Outcome scale mismatch** — the four-level ordinal is coarse and *non-commensurable* with the earlier 100-label entropy outcome, so the replication verdict rests on sign and magnitude class rather than interval overlap; the author concedes this but it limits how directly the earlier −0.284 is being "refuted." (3) **Operator semantics contested** — part of the tagger-human gap (layer c, ~10 items) comes from genuinely disputed monotonicity classifications of operators like *only* and *many*, which lexicon engineering cannot resolve; this injects an irreducible measurement-noise component into the predictor. (4) **Registration under self-control** — the pre-registration is a version-control timestamp, not a third-party OSF-style entry, weakening the independence of the pre-registration claim (the author discloses this). (5) **English-only, surface-scan tagger** with known coverage gaps, limiting generalization to other languages and to semantic triggers the surface rules miss.

## Strengths

- Exemplary pre-registration discipline: frozen hypothesis, tagger, SESOI, and correction procedure, with a full concordance table in the appendix.
- Intellectual honesty: the author reports a failed prediction and a sign-reversed significant result without spin, and openly documents the undefined bridge analysis.
- Three-layer validity defense (simulated misclassification, manual audit, confound models) is more thorough than typical for a short replication.
- Clear, actionable methodological takeaway: state selection conditionals for HLV claims built on disagreement-selected resources.
- Fully reproducible with a public repository and per-run seeds.

## Limitations

- Single-author manual tagging with no independent second rater; κ is tagger-vs-human, not inter-human.
- The four-level ordinal outcome is non-commensurable with the earlier 100-label entropy, so comparison with the δ = −0.284 estimate is by sign/magnitude class only.
- Contested operator semantics (e.g., *only*, *many*) make part of the tagger-human disagreement irreducible.
- Surface-scan tagger has measured coverage gaps; MED anchor rates are measured on MED sentences, not on the SNLI/MNLI dev domain.
- English-only; the coarse ordinal limits resolution.
- Pre-registration is a self-controlled version-control record rather than a third-party registry entry.

## Relevance

对于关注"人类标注变异性（HLV）/ perspectivism 与 NLI 评测"以及"数据集构建中的选择偏差如何扭曲结论"的研究者，本文是一份高质量的方法学警示：它直接说明在分歧度筛选过的 ChaosNLI 上得到的单调性-一致率结构，在未筛选的 SNLI/MultiNLI 上消失甚至反转，提示在做 HLV 结构性归因或评测时必须显式声明数据的选择条件。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would a human-human inter-annotator agreement study on monotonicity tagging (rather than tagger-vs-human) change the residual-disagreement decomposition, especially for the contested operators *only* and *many*?
- If the four-level agreement ordinal were replaced by a finer-grained outcome (e.g., re-annotating a sample with many labels per item, as in ChaosNLI but *without* the 3/5 selection rule), would any non-trivial monotonicity effect reappear in the unselected population?
- Does the selection-conditional artifact generalize to other disagreement-selected re-annotation resources (e.g., Kadasi & Singh 2023's annotation-quantity scaling), or is it specific to ChaosNLI's exact 3-of-5 rule?
- Can the formal-semantics layer of irreducible disagreement (layer c) be turned into a quantitative measurement target itself, e.g., modeling annotator uncertainty over operator readings?
- How do these findings interact with model-based evaluations — does a model trained on ChaosNLI's selected distribution inherit the same selection-conditional bias when scored on unselected dev sets?
