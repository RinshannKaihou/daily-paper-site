> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 "Scrambled Edges" 这一受控反事实扰动——将真实边缘片段平移并旋转后重新注入图像，使其视觉显著但违反表面连续性、光照一致性与遮挡因果性先验；在 NYU Depth v2 和 KITTI 上，CNN/ViT/SSL 深度预测器（MiDaS、DepthAnything v1/v2）相对于等能量噪声出现高达 3.2 倍的预测偏移，DepthAnything V2 崩溃比达 3.20×，且即便有完美的扰动掩码先验，输出层修复也只能恢复 47%，揭示当前稠密预测模型缺乏对"物理上无支撑边缘线索"的检验机制。

> **TL;DR:** The paper introduces **Scrambled Edges**, a controlled counterfactual that injects visually salient but geometrically unsupported edge cues (violating continuity / illumination / occlusion priors) into an image, and shows a global failure mode called **Geometric Collapse** across CNN, ViT, and SSL depth predictors on **NYU Depth v2** and **KITTI**: relative to energy-matched high-pass noise, predictions deviate up to **3.2×** more (DepthAnything V2 = 3.20× vs. 1.00× noise baseline), surface normals collapse harder than depth, even perfect-mask oracle output repair recovers only **47.0%** (Input Oracle 99.96%), and standard GT-RMSE can paradoxically *improve* while Edge F1 drops up to 66.7% — evidence that scaling and SSL pretraining do not yield inference-time physical-causality verification.

## 核心贡献

- **提出一种可证伪式的行为诊断——Scrambled Edges。** 通过抽取 Canny 边缘的前 K=15 个连通分量，对其施加随机仿射变换（旋转 ±60°、平移 ±25% 宽高）并按强度 α=0.8 加深，构造出视觉显著但物理上无支撑的边缘证据，用于检验稠密预测器是否会拒绝而非采纳这些线索。
- **发现并命名 "Geometric Collapse" 这一全局失效模式。** 证明局部违反物理先验的边缘会引起场景级几何幻觉（"Phantom Walls"），错误传播到扰动区域之外，且无法通过局部修复逆转——属于"负向涌现"：规模与 SSL 并未带来可靠的物理验证行为。
- **通过能量匹配与结构匹配双重对照分离机制。** 用 Energy-Matched High-pass Noise（频率对照）与 Edge-Shaped Noise（结构对照）证明崩溃并非由高频能量或边缘稀疏性驱动，而是由物理先验违例驱动；机制阶梯显示"遮挡因果性（Direction）"贡献最强。
- **揭示评测指标悖论并给出边界感知替代度量。** 标准 GT-RMSE 在崩溃时可能反而降低（平滑效应），而 Edge F1 才能暴露结构崩塌；多视图光度误差也会误导，必须用 depth–depth 一致性。
- **量化下游几何后果与可修复性上限。** Oracle 分析给出严格局部修复上界：Input Oracle 恢复 99.96%，但 Output Oracle 仅 47.0%，Input Inpaint 仅 24.0%，说明错误已经全局扩散。

## Key Contributions

- **A falsification-style behavioral diagnostic (Scrambled Edges).** A controlled counterfactual that inserts visually salient edge cues violating surface continuity, illumination coherence, and occlusion causality, with energy-matched and structure-matched controls to isolate physical-support violations from generic high-frequency content or edge sparsity.
- **Identification of "Geometric Collapse" as a global failure mode.** Local edge evidence that violates physical priors causes scene-wide loss of coherent 3D structure rather than localized artifacts — a paradox where noise robustness does *not* imply edge robustness, framed as "negative emergence" from scaling/SSL.
- **A mechanism ladder isolating which prior drives collapse.** Across CNN/ViT/SSL predictors, structure-only perturbations are benign while occlusion-causality (Direction) violation dominates collapse, with the three priors producing a clean severity ordering.
- **A metric-paradox finding with a practical boundary-aware fix.** Global GT metrics (RMSE, AbsRel) can *improve* under Scrambled Edges due to smoothing, while Edge F1 drops by 46–67%; multi-view photometric error can also decrease even as depth–depth consistency collapses, motivating boundary-aware evaluation.
- **A formal local-repair ceiling plus downstream-consequence quantification.** A behavioral theory proves that nonzero out-of-mask spillover makes exact local repair impossible; oracle experiments confirm a 47.0% output-repair ceiling, and surface integrity (normal variance +126.6%) and traversability (fragmentation +34.5%) degrade substantially.

## Methodology

The paper frames physical-causality verification as a falsification-style counterfactual test, implemented as a four-axis diagnostic pipeline (Stability, Structure, Spillover, Downstream).

**Scrambled Edges perturbation.** Given input *I*, Canny edges (thresholds 50/150) are extracted, the top-K (K=15) connected components by area are selected, and each segment is warped by an affine transform sampled with θ ∼ U(−60°,+60°) and translation ∼ U(−0.25W,+0.25W) × U(−0.25H,+0.25H), then darkened by α=0.8: `Iscram = clip(I − α · Mscram, 0, 1)`. Defaults yield ≈36% mask coverage on NYU. Translation violates surface continuity, darkening violates illumination coherence, and rotation breaks occlusion-ordering (T-junction) compatibility.

**Two control groups.** (1) *Energy-Matched High-pass Noise* (frequency control): Gaussian white noise high-pass filtered, with σ scaled so that RMS perturbation energy matches Scrambled Edges exactly. (2) *Edge-Shaped Noise* (structure control): the same edge masks are noise/color-shifted *in place* without relocation or rotation, preserving edge-like visual statistics while avoiding geometric violation. Collapse under Scrambled Edges but not these controls isolates the physical-violation mechanism.

**Physical-support proxies.** Three scores operationalize whether edges are "physically supported": G-Score (fraction of edge pixels where depth gradient exceeds τ, i.e., true discontinuity), P-Score (fraction explained by shadow/texture chromatic signatures in CIE Lab), O-Score (fraction of T-junctions with consistent depth ordering). On NYU, Scrambled Edges score G=0.21 vs. clean Canny G=0.38, O=0.12 vs. 0.45, and are 3.54× more photometrically unexplained (81.5% vs. 23.0%), confirming they lack support on all dimensions.

**Metrics.** Stability is measured as deviation from the model's own clean prediction (behavioral, not GT accuracy): RMSE∆, Collapse Ratio = RMSE∆,scram / RMSE∆,noise, and Recovery. Structural collapse is measured by Edge F1 on depth-derived surface normals. *Adoption Rate* quantifies how often injected scrambled-edge pixels induce aligned depth-gradient discontinuities (angle < 30° to edge normal). **Oracle repair** uses the known perturbation mask to splice clean predictions inside Mscram; the gap between input-oracle and output-oracle recovery quantifies global spillover.

**Setup.** NYU Depth v2 labeled set (N=1449) with four models: MiDaS v2.1 (ResNet-101/CNN), MiDaS DPT (ViT-Large), DepthAnything v1 (ViT-Large, DINOv2), DepthAnything V2 (ViT-Large, DINOv2+). Generative extensions use Marigold (diffusion) and DepthFM (flow matching). Cross-dataset validation on KITTI Odometry (N=6852 depth frames; 750 frame pairs for multi-view). Statistics use paired t-tests with Bonferroni correction and paired Cohen's d.

## Results & Highlights

- **Geometric Collapse is large and significant on NYU (N=1449).** Collapse Ratios (RMSE∆ vs. energy-matched high-pass noise, which is 1.00× by construction): MiDaS v2.1 (CNN) = **1.88×** (d=1.37), MiDaS DPT (ViT) = **2.34×** (d=2.11), DepthAnything v1 (SSL) = **2.02×** (d=1.13), DepthAnything V2 = **3.20×** (d=2.98); all p < 10⁻¹⁰.
- **The strongest single-prior driver is occlusion causality (Direction).** Direction perturbation alone yields 1.86× (MiDaS CNN), 2.22× (MiDaS ViT), 1.99× (DA v1), and **3.18× (DA V2)** — nearly matching full Scrambled Edges. By contrast, structure-only Edge-Shaped noise stays at 1.0×–1.7× and Mask-Matched (position-only) noise is *sub-baseline* (0.43×–0.60×), confirming collapse is driven by physical violation, not energy or edge presence.
- **Adoption rate monotonically tracks collapse (MiDaS DPT, α sweep).** At α=0.2 adoption=6.2% / collapse=1.37×; at α=0.6 adoption=24.7% / collapse=2.02×; at α=0.8 adoption=36.1% / collapse=2.34× — direct evidence that collapse is caused by the model adopting false edges, not by their mere presence.
- **Generative estimators reduce but do not eliminate collapse.** Marigold (diffusion) = **1.55×** (d=0.88, p=7.38×10⁻¹³⁹); DepthFM (flow matching) = **1.11×** (d=0.25, p=7.72×10⁻¹³) — both still statistically significant, showing iterative commitment helps but is not a substitute for explicit physical verification.
- **Hard oracle-repair ceiling from global spillover.** On MiDaS DPT (NYU), Input Oracle (clean image inside mask) recovers **99.96%**, but Output Oracle (clean *depth* inside mask) recovers only **47.0%**, and Input Inpaint with oracle mask recovers only **24.0%**. The Input-vs-Output gap proves error has already propagated outside the perturbation mask, formally capping any mask-local repair.
- **Metric paradox on NYU (Table 4).** GT-RMSE *improves* under Scrambled Edges for every model (e.g., MiDaS v2.1: 2.19m → 1.45m; DA V2: 4.08m → 3.51m) while Edge F1 collapses: MiDaS v2.1 0.195→0.105 (−46.4%), MiDaS DPT 0.289→0.141 (−51.3%), DA v1 0.364→0.187 (−48.8%), DA V2 0.392→0.131 (**−66.7%**). Global pixel metrics hide the structural failure.
- **Multi-view metric paradox on KITTI (N=750 pairs).** Photometric error can *decrease* (MiDaS ViT −2.3%, MiDaS CNN −3.3%) while depth–depth consistency error explodes (+88.9% and **+410.1%**; MiDaS CNN even +865.2% vs. noise) — appearance-based metrics actively mislead.
- **Cross-dataset generalization (KITTI, N=6852).** Collapse persists outdoors: MiDaS CNN 1.88×→1.21×, MiDaS DPT 2.34×→2.08×, DA v1 2.02×→1.40×, DA V2 3.20×→2.48×.
- **Task selectivity (SAM ViT-Base segmentation).** Scrambled Edges cause no additional sensitivity over the high-pass baseline (Pixel Deviation collapse 0.84×, Boundary IoU collapse 1.00×), confirming the failure is specific to edge-to-geometry mapping, not generic fragility.
- **Downstream geometric consequences (MiDaS DPT, NYU).** Surface normal variance +126.6%, planarity error +37.0%, folded-surface ratio +64.9%, free-space fragmentation +34.5%, navigable area +57.3% — indicating "Phantom Walls" with direct safety implications for robotics/navigation.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (high).** The framing is genuinely fresh: rather than propose yet another depth model or corruption benchmark, the authors cast physical-causality verification as a falsification-style behavioral test with energy-matched *and* structure-matched controls. The Positioning Matrix (Table 5) credibly argues no prior line of work isolates edge-level physical priors with matched controls + propagation/repair analysis simultaneously. The "metric paradox" (GT-RMSE improving while Edge F1 collapses) is a sharp, memorable finding.
- **Rigor (strong).** All collapse claims come with p < 10⁻¹⁰ and large Cohen's d (1.13–2.98); controls are tight (energy-matched RMS calibration per-image, edge-shaped in-place control). The behavioral theory (Appendix B.5) gives a *proven* local-repair impossibility bound, not just an empirical observation. Sensitivity analysis over K and α (Table 10) shows the effect is not a parameter artifact.
- **Reproducibility (very high).** Specific model versions (intel-isl/MiDaS MiDaS and DPT-Large, LiheYoung/depth-anything-large-hf, depth-anything/Depth-Anything-V2-Large), hyperparameters (K=15, α=0.8, Canny 50/150, rotation ±60°, translation ±25%), seeds, hardware (RTX 4090, FP16), and ~1.5-hour total runtime are all reported; code/benchmark promised on publication. Algorithm 1 is given in full.
- **Significance (high for the diagnostic/evaluation community; moderate-to-low for direct practitioners).** It will not improve any leaderboard number, but it is the kind of paper that changes how people *evaluate* robustness of geometric foundation models, and it directly motivates the cue-selection / plausibility-scoring research direction.
- **Red flags and weaknesses:**
  - **Ecological validity is narrow.** Scrambled Edges is explicitly a *diagnostic*, not a realistic corruption (the authors say so). The 36% mask coverage with sharply darkened relocated edges is visually artificial; the link to real long-tail ambiguities (reflections/shadows/glass) rests on a *qualitative* case study (Appendix G, Figure 10) with no quantitative long-tail collapse table — a real gap given that practical relevance is a stated motivator.
  - **Asymmetric baseline strength.** The "baseline" in every ratio is *high-pass noise calibrated to be benign by construction*; the more informative head-to-head is Mask-Matched (same position, no edge structure), which comes out *below 1×* (0.43×–0.60×) — i.e., the comparison is partly structured-cue vs. unstructured-noise, which somewhat inflates the headline 2–3× ratios. The Edge-Shaped control (the fairest comparison) gives much smaller numbers (1.0×–1.7×), and the paper is admirably honest about this, but the abstract leads with "up to 3.2×."
  - **DepthAnything V2 carries the headline.** DA V2's 3.20× is roughly 1.4–1.7× the other three models, and the "SSL paradox" / "over-reliance on priors" claim leans heavily on a *single* (albeit flagship) model; one more recent SSL depth model would have made the SSL-paradox generalization stronger.
  - **No proposed defense or fix.** The post-hoc edge-consistency defense recovers only ~26% (Table 14) and the paper explicitly says it does not solve the problem. This is honest but leaves the practical takeaway at "this is broken, someone should fix it."
  - **Generative-extension effect sizes are mixed.** DepthFM's d=0.25 (small) at 1.11× is borderline despite low p; treating it as evidence that "iterative inference helps but is incomplete" is reasonable, but the asymmetry with the huge feed-forward effects slightly oversells the cross-paradigm generality.
  - **Missing ablations.** No ablation on backbone size within a family (e.g., DINOv2-S/B/L), no test of whether photometric/left-right-consistency-trained models (e.g., MonoDepth2, MiDaS-with-consistency) collapse less — which would directly test the "objectives that reconstruct all salient tokens amplify collapse" hypothesis rather than merely asserting it.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clean: three priors, two matched controls, four diagnostic axes — unusually tidy experimental grammar.
- The oracle-repair ceiling is both empirically demonstrated and *formally proven* (Appendix B.5), turning an observation into a theorem.
- The metric-paradox findings (single-view GT-RMSE and multi-view photometric error both misleading) are actionable evaluation guidance for the dense-prediction community.
- Honest about scope: explicit "negative emergence" framing, admission that the diagnostic is synthetic and not an attack, and a clear task-selectivity control (SAM) showing the failure is geometry-specific.
- Excellent statistical hygiene: full dataset, paired tests, Bonferroni, Cohen's d, unified results registry, reproducibility appendix.

## Limitations

- **Diagnostic, not a fix.** No architecture or training objective is proposed to actually perform physical-causality verification; the paper motivates but does not deliver cue-selection.
- **Synthetic and edge-narrow.** Only edge-to-geometry shortcuts are probed; texture, color, and global-illumination shortcuts are explicitly out of scope, so "Geometric Collapse" is one channel among several possible.
- **Real-world long-tail evidence is qualitative.** The reflection/shadow/glass connection (Appendix G) is a case study without a quantitative collapse-rate comparison against random samples.
- **Model coverage.** Only four feed-forward depth models in the main table; the SSL-paradox claim would benefit from more SSL depth models and from a non-depth backbone control.
- **Headline ratio is relative to a benign-by-construction baseline**, so the absolute "3.2×" should be read alongside the Edge-Shaped (1.0×–1.7×) and Mask-Matched (<1×) controls that the paper itself provides.
- **Cross-dataset comparisons are descriptive only** (independent sampling precludes paired tests), and KITTI ratios are uniformly lower, which the authors attribute to scene statistics without strong evidence.

## Relevance

对研究"自监督/大规模预训练稠密几何预测器在物理一致性上的可靠性"高度相关：它给出了一套可复现的、能量/结构双对照的诊断协议和"指标悖论"结论，直接启发我们在评测自己的深度/法向/分割模型时引入边界感知度量和"局部修复上限"检验，而不是只看全局 GT 误差。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does adding a photometric left-right or multi-view consistency loss during training (e.g., MonoDepth2-style) actually *reduce* Scrambled-Edges collapse, or does it merely shift which prior is violated?
- Would a learned, support-aware edge-gating module placed at the encoder beat the 47% output-oracle ceiling, or is the spillover already irreversible by the encoder stage?
- How does Geometric Collapse behave across DINOv2 backbone scales (S/B/L) and across non-depth SSL backbones — is the "SSL paradox" a property of DINOv2 specifically or of masked-token objectives generally?
- Can the diagnostic be extended to optical flow, stereo matching, and 3D Gaussian Splatting near occlusion boundaries, where edge cues are equally load-bearing?
- A quantitative long-tail table (collapse rate on real reflection/shadow/glass images vs. random samples) would test whether the synthetic protocol's practical-relevance claim holds beyond a qualitative case study.
- Is the metric paradox (GT-RMSE improving while Edge F1 collapses) an artifact of median-scaling alignment specifically, and do scale-invariant metrics (SILog) also miss the failure?
