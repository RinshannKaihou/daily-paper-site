Methodology — the mechanism, distilled

LAD explains a frozen classifier g ∘ f with no retraining. Standard post-hoc concept discovery factorizes pooled latents A = GAP(Z) as A ≈ U WT, learning both factors — so the recovered concepts are unnamed and inconsistent across images. LAD's inversion: factorize in the encoder's spatial feature space and fix the coefficient matrix to a language-grounded S, learning only the basis W.

S is built per-image with a localized (red-circle) CLIP similarity map: for each spatial location, a variant image with a small red circle is scored against "a photo of {concept}", and the per-concept map is stacked across images. The concept vocabulary itself comes from an LLM prompted with structured templates (object parts, textures, shapes, materials, poses, context), with generic fillers, class-name overlaps, and near-duplicates filtered out; r=25 concepts per class by default. With S fixed, recovering W reduces to a single non-negative least-squares solve (NNDSVD-init projected gradient descent) — hence the order-of-magnitude speedup. Because GAP is linear and the head reads only pooled features, a small reconstruction error bounds the logit perturbation. At inference, a per-image named-concept rollout ranks concepts by reconstruction contribution — the only baseline cannot do this.

Strengths

  • Clean, well-motivated inversion of NMF with a genuinely novel structural-enforcement-of-naming mechanism.
  • Persuasive headline ablation (fixed vs learned S) quantifying the anchor's contribution: +0.395 C-Del.
  • Strong insertion faithfulness and clinical deletion faithfulness; only method with per-image named rollouts.
  • Honest reporting of a ViT negative result and a dermoscopy localization limitation — integrity over breadth.
  • Order-of-magnitude faster basis fitting; modest memory; public code & extensive supplement.

Limitations

  • Deletion trails FACE on the two largest natural-image benchmarks (ImageNet 0.902 vs 0.939; Places365 0.880 vs 0.919).
  • Does not transfer to ViTs: ViT-B/16 C-Del ≈ 0.02 with diffuse, background-attaching maps.
  • Dermoscopy boundary bias: concepts ring the lesion border, not the diagnostic interior (C-Del 0.418).
  • LLM dependency: vocabularies from gpt-4o-mini; no sensitivity to LLM choice/prompt seed reported.
  • Coarse ablation: only the whole anchor is removed; language vs. any fixed coefficient matrix not isolated.

Open questions

  1. How much of the faithfulness gain comes from language supervision specifically vs. any fixed (k-means-clustered, random non-negative) coefficient matrix S?
  2. Can a transformer-native formulation on the [CLS] attention pathway recover spatially faithful named concepts on ViTs, closing the C-Del ≈ 0.02 gap?
  3. How sensitive are the discovered concepts and metrics to the choice and seed of the LLM used for vocabulary generation (gpt-4o-mini vs open-weight alternatives)?
  4. Is the dermoscopy boundary bias a CLIP/BiomedCLIP limitation or a CNN-filter property — would a SAM-derived mask prior move concepts into the diagnostic interior?
  5. How do LAD's concepts compare quantitatively (not just positionally) against neuron-naming (LaViSE, MILAN) under shared C-Ins/C-Del evaluation?
