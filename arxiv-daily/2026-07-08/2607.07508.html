Key Finding/One Rollout Per Prompt, Stably

Dropping GRPO's group sampling for one rollout per prompt — stabilized by token-level masking and a frozen-attention critic — beats GRPO across five benchmarks.

Single-Rollout Asynchronous Optimization for Agentic Reinforcement Learning

Why This Matters

Single-rollout is the structurally correct setting for real online/agentic RL where group rewards are unavailable — and SAO keeps it stable for ~1000 steps.

Critical Edge

The headline 97.3% is "w/ python" tool-integrated reasoning — the same model without python scores only 14.6%; gains are heavily scaffold-dependent.

Methodology — four components that make single-rollout stable

Fix the two failure modes of asynchronous LLM RL: severe policy lag and group-barrier mismatch

SAO keeps three policies conceptually — current π_θ, old π_old, rollout π_rollout — but drops π_old entirely, computing the per-token ratio directly from rollout logs. Instead of PPO's asymmetric clipping of selected tokens, it enforces a strict two-sided trust region and hard-masks anything outside it. Because single-rollout degenerates toward high-variance REINFORCE, a strong value model carries the variance; and because agentic traces interleave actions and observations, the GAE skips the observation tokens.

Component 1 · DIS
Direct double-sided token-level IS
Drop π_old; mask tokens outside [1−εℓ, 1+εℓ] instead of PPO clipping. Math: εℓ=0.3/εh=5.0. Coding: 0.8/3.0.
Component 2 · Sampling
Single-rollout, no group barrier
One rollout per prompt enters training immediately on completion — removes GRPO's "wait for the slowest group member" latency.
Component 3 · Value model
Faster critic (K=2) + frozen attention
Critic gradient norms were dominated by Full-Attention layers; freeze attention, train MoE only. Scale value-pretraining for cold-start.
Component 4 · GAE
Skip-Observation token-level GAE
For [a₀,o₀,a₁,o₁,…], bridge advantage across action-to-action boundaries, skipping environment observation tokens.

Training: Qwen3-30B-A3B-Thinking-2507 policy, 3-epoch TIR SFT init from GPT-OSS-120B distillation; batch 128, group size 1, max length 128k, policy LR 1e-6, critic LR 5e-6. GRPO baselines use 16 prompts × 8 rollouts to match the 128 batch.

Strengths

  • Attacks the right problem — GRPO's group barrier is a real pain point for long-horizon agentic RL
  • Each design choice is backed by a diagnostic — grad-norm decomposition, Explained Variance, clip-ratio curve, step-vs-token ablation
  • Broad empirical wins across four math benchmarks + a coding agent, and shipped at 750B scale
  • Honest that the running-mean baseline is "decent but worse"; novel online-learning evaluation angle

Limitations

  • Single backbone (Qwen3-30B-A3B); may not transfer to smaller / dense / short-rollout regimes
  • Headline is "w/ python" — w/o python is only 14.6%; gains are scaffold-dependent
  • Value-pretraining corpus central to the cold-start fix, but unspecified in scale/composition
  • Apples-to-oranges table inclusion of Claude-Sonnet-4.5 / GPT-5 High reference points
  • SWE-Bench gain modest (+2.8) and within plausible run-to-run noise
  • No head-to-head with SPO or AReaL/ROLL async systems on throughput

Open Questions — for further exploration

How does SAO behave on dense-reward, short-rollout RLHF preference tasks versus GRPO/DPO, where group sampling is cheaper and the off-policy penalty smaller?
What is the wall-clock / GPU-utilization comparison against AReaL / ROLL Flash at matched throughput — given the stated motivation is asynchrony efficiency, but the paper reports accuracy, not training time?
How sensitive are εℓ/εh and the K=2 critic ratio to backbone size and MoE architecture — would a dense 7B policy show the same frozen-attention benefit?
Can the Skip-Observation GAE extend to settings with stochastic, variable-length environment feedback (tool errors, web navigation) where the action-to-action boundary itself is uncertain?
What is the actual size and composition of the scaled value-pretraining corpus — and how much gain comes from value cold-start fix vs the online RL algorithm itself?
