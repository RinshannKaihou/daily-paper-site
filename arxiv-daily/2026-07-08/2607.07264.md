> **一句话总结：** 提出 LAD（Language-Anchored Decomposition），通过反转 NMF——用 LLM 生成概念词表、CLIP 相似度图作为固定的系数矩阵 S，仅学习概念基 W——在不修改模型的前提下同时实现"有命名、忠实、无需重训练"的事后概念解释，在 ImageNet/Places365/视网膜眼底图像上插入忠实度领先，且消融证明语言锚具有功能性（移除后删除忠实度从 0.902 崩塌到 0.507）。

> **TL;DR:** LAD inverts standard NMF-based concept discovery: instead of learning both factors, it fixes the coefficient matrix S to CLIP-derived language-grounded similarity maps (from an LLM-proposed class-specific vocabulary) and learns only the concept basis W against the frozen encoder's spatial activations. On ImageNet (ResNet34, 500 classes) it reaches C-Ins 0.973 vs the strongest baseline FACE's 0.952 (+0.021) while uniquely producing named concepts; removing the language anchor leaves accuracy intact (1.000) but collapses C-Del from 0.902 to 0.507, and it is an order of magnitude cheaper to fit (0.03 s vs FACE's 2.6 s per class). A negative ViT case study and a dermoscopy boundary-localization limitation are honestly reported.

## 核心贡献

- **提出 LAD 框架，反转 NMF 的角色分工**：传统事后概念发现（ACE/ICE/CRAFT/FACE）学习系数矩阵 U 与基 W 两个因子，得到的因子是无名的、跨图不一致的；LAD 则固定由 CLIP 相似度图构成的"语义锚"系数矩阵 S，只学习基 W，从而把"命名"变成分解的结构性约束，而非事后贴标签。
- **首次同时满足"有命名、忠实、不修改模型"三性质**：填补了三类既有方法的空白——post-hoc 发现（忠实但无名）、单元命名（有名但不验证因果性、需额外标注/解释器）、by-design 模型（有名但替换了分类头，解释的不再是部署的模型）。
- **用消融证明语言锚是功能性而非装饰性的**：把固定的语言系数换成可学习系数（即退化为标准 NMF），准确率不变（1.000），但删除忠实度 C-Del 从 0.902 急剧下降到 0.507（−0.395），证明语言监督决定了"哪些概念方向被发现"。
- **强调"编码器自身的几何、而非 CLIP"决定保留哪些概念**：优化后与 CLIP 初始均匀分布不一致的概念会被赋予小基权重并被抑制，从而缓解 CLIP 类模型的已知偏差。
- **跨域评测覆盖自然图像、场景、医学影像**：在 ImageNet（ResNet34, 500 类）、Places365（ResNet50, 364 类）、视网膜眼底 ODIR-5K（DenseNet-121, BiomedCLIP）上评测；500 类相比以往只报 10 类的工作是大幅扩展。
- **唯一支持"逐图命名概念 rollout"**：对单张图返回模型依赖的命名概念、各概念的定位图与对重建的贡献占比 ĉ_k，使解释成为可审计记录而非无标签显著性图。
- **公开代码**：https://github.com/machine-intelligence-lab-wvu/LAD 。

## Key Contributions

- LAD (Language-Anchored Decomposition), a post-hoc concept-discovery framework that **inverts NMF** by fixing language-grounded CLIP similarity maps as the coefficient matrix S and learning only the concept basis W against the frozen encoder's spatial activations. Naming is enforced structurally, with no retraining and no manual concept annotation.
- The first method to deliver concepts that are simultaneously **named, faithful, and obtained without modifying the model**, closing the gap left by post-hoc discovery (faithful but unnamed), unit-naming (named but causally unverified), and by-design models (named but replace the classifier head).
- An **ablation proving the language anchor is functional, not cosmetic**: swapping the fixed language coefficients for learned ones (standard unsupervised NMF on the same activations) leaves accuracy at 1.000 but drops C-Del from 0.902 to 0.507 (−0.395), showing the anchor steers which basis directions are discovered.
- The empirical claim that the **frozen encoder's feature geometry, not CLIP, determines which named concepts survive**: after optimization, concepts inconsistent with the encoder's evidence are suppressed, mitigating known CLIP biases.
- Evaluation across **natural-image, scene, and medical-imaging** benchmarks (ImageNet, Places365, ODIR-5K retinal fundus, plus a HAM10000 dermoscopy supplement), with a 500-class ImageNet scope that substantially expands prior concept-discovery work reporting as few as 10 classes.
- A **per-image named-concept rollout** capability no baseline supports: for a single input, LAD returns the named, localized concepts the model used, ranked by their reconstruction contribution.
- Public code release at https://github.com/machine-intelligence-lab-wvu/LAD.

## Methodology

LAD explains a frozen classifier `g ∘ f` (encoder `f` producing spatial feature maps, head `g` on GAP-pooled features) without retraining.

**Problem setup.** Post-hoc concept discovery factorizes pooled latents `A = GAP(Z)` into a non-negative, low-rank form `A ≈ U W^T` (r concepts). Standard NMF learns both U and W, so the recovered factors are unnamed and inconsistent across images.

**Key inversion — Semantically Anchored Matrix Factorization.** LAD factorizes directly in the encoder's *spatial* feature space (unfolded `Ā ∈ R^{(nhw)×p}`, each row a spatial location) rather than pooled features, so localized part-level structure is preserved. It then **fixes the coefficient matrix to a language-grounded S** and learns only the basis:

`Ā ≈ S W^T,  S ∈ R_+^{(nhw)×r}`

Each column of S is a named concept; each entry is the similarity between a spatial location and a concept prompt. Naming becomes a structural property of the factorization, not a post-hoc label.

**Class-specific concept vocabulary.** For each class, an LLM is prompted with structured templates to elicit diverse attributes (object parts, textures, shapes, materials, poses, contextual cues); automatic filtering removes generic fillers, concepts overlapping the class name (preventing trivial leakage), and near-duplicates by CLIP text-embedding similarity. r = 25 concepts per class by default.

**Localized CLIP similarity maps.** For each image, a grid aligned to (h,w) is defined; at each location a variant is formed by overlaying a small red circle (localized/red-circle prompting). Cosine similarity between the CLIP image encoder of the variant and the text encoder of `"a photo of {concept}"` yields a spatial map per concept; stacking across images forms the fixed S.

**Basis optimization.** With S fixed, recover W by minimizing reconstruction error `min_{W≥0} ½‖Ā − S W^T‖_F^2` via projected gradient descent (`W ← max(0, W − η∇_W L)`, initialized with NNDSVD). Because GAP is linear and the head reads only pooled features, a small reconstruction error bounds the logit perturbation; no output-level regularization is needed.

**Inference.** For a new image, extract and unfold activations, fix W, and estimate non-negative coefficients `Ŝ` via non-negative least squares (fast projected-normal-equation init `Ŝ₀ = ReLU(ĀW(W^T W)^{-1})`, optionally refined by PGD). Reshape `Ŝ` to spatial maps; each concept k gives a normalized heatmap `M_k = Normalize(Ŝ_spatial(:,:,k))`, and its per-image contribution is `ĉ_k = ‖Ŝ[:,k]‖₂‖W[:,k]‖₂ / Σ_j ‖Ŝ[:,j]‖₂‖W[:,j]‖₂`.

**Baselines:** ICE, CRAFT, FACE — all NMF-based, identical backbones/preprocessing/splits/concept counts. **Metrics:** Acc (top-1 agreement between original and reconstructed predictions), C-Ins ↑ (concepts sufficient for decision), C-Del ↑ (concepts necessary for decision).

## Results & Highlights

- **ImageNet (ResNet34, 500 classes), primary table:** LAD achieves Acc 1.000, **C-Ins 0.973** vs FACE 0.952 (+0.021), CRAFT 0.918 (+0.055), ICE 0.907 (+0.066); C-Del 0.902 vs FACE 0.939 (FACE strongest), CRAFT 0.808, ICE 0.545. LAD leads insertion, FACE leads deletion.
- **Places365 (ResNet50, 364 classes):** LAD Acc 1.000, **C-Ins 0.972** vs FACE 0.925 (+0.047), CRAFT 0.868, ICE 0.887; C-Del LAD 0.880 vs FACE 0.919. Accuracy preserved across both ResNet backbones despite the semantic constraints.
- **Retinal fundus (ODIR-5K, DenseNet-121 @448, BiomedCLIP), mean over 4 disease classes:** LAD **C-Del 0.954** vs FACE 0.863 (+0.091), CRAFT 0.850 (+0.104), ICE 0.455 — LAD leads deletion by a wide margin in the clinical domain, where disease signatures are spatially focal. Acc and C-Ins saturate (1.000 / 0.980 for all).
- **Language-anchor ablation (ImageNet, ResNet34, r=25):** replacing fixed S with learned coefficients (standard unsupervised NMF) keeps Acc at 1.000 but **drops C-Del from 0.902 to 0.507 (−0.395)** and C-Ins from 0.973 to 0.942 (−0.031) — direct evidence the anchor is functional.
- **Concept-budget sweep (ImageNet):** faithfulness improves monotonically with r — r=10: C-Ins 0.941 / C-Del 0.860; r=25: 0.973 / 0.902; r=50: 0.985 / 0.953; r=100: 0.992 / 0.956 — accuracy saturated at all r.
- **Computational cost (RTX A4500, per class):** LAD basis fit **0.03 s**, vs CRAFT 0.5 s and FACE 2.6 s — an order of magnitude cheaper because fixing S reduces learning to a single non-negative least-squares solve; one-time LLM vocabulary generation ~15 s/class; whole pipeline peaks under 1.8 GB GPU.
- **Dermoscopy (HAM10000, ConvNeXt-Small, BiomedCLIP):** LAD C-Del 0.418, leading CRAFT 0.366 and FACE 0.254 — but absolutely much lower than fundus (0.954) for every method, because named concepts localize to the lesion *border/peri-lesional skin* rather than the diagnostic interior (reported as a limitation).
- **ViT-B/16 negative result (500 ImageNet classes, r=25):** Acc 0.98, C-Ins 0.98, but **C-Del ≈ 0.02** with diffuse, poorly localized maps — spatial concept decomposition does not transfer cleanly to transformers (patch-token geometry, global attention, [CLS]-routed prediction).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — genuinely high.** The "invert NMF by fixing the coefficient matrix to CLIP similarity maps" framing is clean and, as the authors argue, occupies an unfilled intersection (named + faithful + no model modification). The structural-enforcement-of-naming argument (naming as a constraint of the factorization, not a post-hoc label) is the most original claim.
- **Rigor — strong on the headline ablation, weaker on alternatives.** The Table 3 ablation (fixed S vs learned S, C-Del 0.902 → 0.507) is a clean, persuasive demonstration that the anchor matters. However, the anchor is a composite of three design choices (LLM vocabulary + CLIP localization + fixing S); the ablation only removes the *entire* anchor, so we cannot tell how much gain comes from the language supervision versus simply using a fixed (even random/clustered) coefficient matrix. A finer-grained ablation (e.g., fixed S from k-means cluster assignment, or from random non-negative initialization) is missing.
- **Rigor — FACE wins deletion on natural images, and the explanation is convenient.** LAD trades C-Del completeness for namedness (FACE 0.939 vs LAD 0.902 on ImageNet; FACE 0.919 vs LAD 0.880 on Places365). The authors frame this as "intended behavior," which is defensible, but it means LAD is *not* the most faithful method on the deletion axis for its two largest benchmarks — only on the clinical fundus domain and on insertion.
- **Baseline comparability — mostly fair.** Same backbones, preprocessing, splits, concept counts (r=25). The 500-class ImageNet scope is a real improvement over the 10-class reports common in this line. But all baselines are NMF-family; there is no comparison against neuron-naming methods (LaViSE, MILAN, CLIP-Dissect) or concept-bottleneck models on the same metrics, so the "uniquely named" claim is argued by positioning rather than by head-to-head measurement.
- **Data/dependency quirks.** Concept vocabularies are LLM-generated (gpt-4o-mini in the clinical supplement), so results are coupled to a proprietary, non-reproducible component; no sensitivity to LLM choice or prompt seed is reported. Red-circle prompting for CLIP localization is a known heuristic and its sensitivity is only partially explored (grid size 7×7 vs 14×14, Table A6).
- **Generalization — mixed and honestly reported.** The ViT case study is a candid negative result (C-Del ≈ 0.02, diffuse maps) showing the method does not transfer to transformers, and the dermoscopy limitation (concepts ring the lesion border, not the diagnostic interior) is disclosed rather than buried. These are red flags for breadth but handled with integrity.
- **Reproducibility — good.** Public code, frozen public checkpoints (ResNet34, ResNet50, DenseNet-121, ConvNeXt-Small, ViT-B/16), standard datasets (ImageNet, Places365, ODIR-5K, HAM10000), and explicit metric definitions. The main non-reproducible piece is the LLM vocabulary generation.
- **Significance — solid for the interpretability subfield.** The per-image named rollout and the "named + faithful + no-retraining" combination are a meaningful advance over TCAV/ACE/CRAFT/FACE, though the practical ceiling (CNN-only, deletion gap to FACE, ViT failure) limits near-term deployment.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated inversion of NMF with a genuinely novel structural-enforcement-of-naming mechanism.
- Persuasive headline ablation (fixed vs learned S) quantifying the anchor's contribution (+0.395 C-Del).
- Strong insertion faithfulness and clinical deletion faithfulness, plus the only method offering per-image named rollouts.
- Honest, transparent reporting of a ViT negative result and a dermoscopy localization limitation.
- Order-of-magnitude faster basis fitting than FACE/CRAFT; modest memory footprint; public code and extensive supplement.

## Limitations

- **Deletion faithfulness trails FACE on the two largest natural-image benchmarks** (ImageNet 0.902 vs 0.939; Places365 0.880 vs 0.919) — LAD is the most faithful method only on insertion and on fundus, not across the board.
- **Does not transfer to Vision Transformers**: ViT-B/16 gives C-Del ≈ 0.02 with diffuse, background-attaching maps; a transformer-native formulation on the [CLS] attention pathway is left as future work.
- **Dermoscopy boundary bias**: on HAM10000, named concepts localize to the lesion edge/peri-lesional skin rather than the diagnostic interior, so C-Del is low (0.418) and explanations are better read as "where the model looks" than "clinically complete reasoning."
- **LLM dependency**: class vocabularies come from a proprietary model (gpt-4o-mini); reproducibility and sensitivity to LLM choice/prompt seed are not analyzed.
- **Coarse-grained ablation**: only the whole anchor is removed; the relative contribution of the language supervision vs a non-language fixed coefficient matrix is not isolated.
- **Baseline scope**: comparisons are limited to NMF-family methods; no head-to-head with neuron-naming (LaViSE/MILAN) or concept-bottleneck models under the same C-Ins/C-Del metrics.

## Relevance

对关注"可解释性 / 概念级解释 / 后事方法忠实性评估"的研究方向高度相关：LAD 提供了一种在不动模型的前提下获得"有命名 + 忠实"概念的可复现范式，其语言锚思想与 C-Ins/C-Del 评估协议可直接迁移到医学影像解释与多模态模型审计场景。但其仅限 CNN、ViT 失败与删除忠实度仍落后于 FACE 的事实，提示在实际部署前需要谨慎评估。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How much of LAD's faithfulness gain comes from *language* supervision specifically versus simply using any fixed (e.g., k-means-clustered or random non-negative) coefficient matrix S? A finer ablation isolating the language component would sharpen the central claim.
- Can a transformer-native formulation operating on the [CLS] attention pathway (rather than patch tokens) recover spatially faithful named concepts on ViTs, closing the C-Del ≈ 0.02 gap?
- How sensitive are the discovered concepts and the faithfulness metrics to the choice and seed of the LLM used for vocabulary generation (gpt-4o-mini vs open-weight alternatives)?
- Does the dermoscopy boundary-localization problem reflect a CLIP/BiomedCLIP limitation or a fundamental property of where CNN filters activate on textured lesions — and would a different localization prior (e.g., SAM-derived masks) move concepts into the diagnostic interior?
- How do LAD's named concepts compare quantitatively (not just positionally) against neuron-naming methods like LaViSE and MILAN under shared evaluation, given the paper claims naming uniqueness without a direct head-to-head?
