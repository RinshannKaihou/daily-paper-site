Cached-response identification ceiling (Thm 1); the TV term is the irreducible transcript overlap no adaptive strategy can remove. Fresh responses decay at the Chernoff rate C(D₀,D₁).
Why This Matters
Gives a principled answer to "why doesn't repeating a cached prompt help" and "how many re-samples does self-consistency need" — directly actionable for agent design.
Critical Edge
Zero empirical grounding — no LLM, no benchmark, no numerical illustration of how large the ceilings or query counts actually are; the motivating MoE example is never instantiated.
Methodology

An SOTM is a pair M = (M, O): a probabilistic TM M interacting turn-by-turn with a stochastic oracle O = {D^{x,t}_q}. The core move is generalizing the oracle from query-dependent (Wang 2606.12647) to context-dependent — the response distribution depends on the input x and the realized transcript t_{i-1}, with Mixture-of-Experts routing as the concrete motivation.

A hidden-state abstraction separates the characterizable distributional behavior (the D^b_q, estimable offline) from the per-instance latent state b_i (e.g., the MoE expert combination) that must be inferred from the transcript — this per-instance uncertainty is what the ceilings quantify. Two response schemes are distinguished:

  • Cached-response oracle — same draw on every later call to the same query (temperature-0 / response reuse).
  • Fresh-response oracle — independent draw on every call (independent re-sampling).

Proof techniques. Cached identification → reduced to a one-sample binary hypothesis test, solved exactly via total variation (optimal rule = likelihood-ratio test on the transcript). Output quality → sup-over-outputs expectation + layer-cake identity + Markov. Fresh identification → Cover–Thomas Chernoff-information large-deviation result. Threshold-stopping → geometric waiting-time; no-score case → binomial-tail Chernoff with Bernoulli KL D(a‖q).

Results — Closed-Form Bounds
Theorem 1 · Cached identification
Supremum success θ̄ = ½ + TV(π₁PM¹, π₀PM⁰); single-query closed form θ̄ = (1+d)/2, d=TV(D₁,D₀).
Theorem 4 · Fresh-response rate
−lim (1/k) log P*_e(k) = C(D₀,D₁) > 0 whenever D₀≠D₁ — cached ceiling becomes an achievable target.
Theorems 5–6 · Threshold stopping
E[N(x)] = 1/p_φ(x,θ); with cutoff m, success = 1−(1−p)ᵐ, least m_γ = log(1−γ)/log(1−p_φ).
Theorem 7 · No-score majority (Remark 9)
For q(x)>½: k_θ ≈ ln(1/(1−θ)) / D(½‖q(x)). Fails when q(x) ≤ ½ — silently inherits the self-consistency caveat.

Pure theory — no datasets, baselines, training runs, or empirical numbers exist to compare against.

Strengths

  • Clean, correct, self-contained proofs; every theorem verified in-text; notation explicitly reconciled with the predecessor paper.
  • The context-dependent generalization is the right abstraction for modern MoE-style LLMs and well-motivated.
  • The cached-vs-fresh dichotomy maps onto two real engineering choices (caching vs independent re-sampling), making bounds actionable.
  • Honest about limitations: noisy score functions, the multi-query case, the non-binary no-score case, and oracle drift are explicitly called out.

Limitations

  • No empirical validation — no LLM, no benchmark, no numerical illustration of the magnitude of ceilings or query counts.
  • Strongest closed forms (Corollary 2, Theorems 4, 7) require the single-informative-query simplification.
  • Theorem 7 inherits the self-consistency caveat that q(x) > ½ is essential; silent on the common non-majority-correct regime.
  • Threshold-stopping bounds (Thms 5–6) assume a noiseless score function — unrealistic for learned verifiers.
  • The hidden-state abstraction assumes D^b_q already characterized offline; distributional uncertainty deferred to [5].
Open Questions
  1. How do Theorems 5–6 change when the score function is a noisy verifier with non-trivial false-positive/false-negative rates?
  2. Can the multi-informative-query cached case be given a closed-form token-cost vs adaptive-query-selection tradeoff?
  3. What is the right replacement for Bernoulli KL D(½‖q) when there are many incorrect responses or graded partial scores?
  4. How do prompt changes, model updates, retrieval augmentation, and correlated errors (oracle drift) quantitatively move the ceilings?
  5. Can the hidden-state abstraction be instantiated on a real MoE LLM so that (1+d)/2 and the Chernoff rate are measured, not just stated?
