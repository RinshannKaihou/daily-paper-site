Key finding

Gemma unifies factual and opinion sycophancy into one representation; Llama keeps them distinct — and causally interfering.

Dissociating the Internal Representations of Sycophancy in LLMs

+0.68/−0.15

Gemma: transferred steering increases sycophancy (unified direction). Llama: transferred steering decreases it — the two subtypes actively interfere.

02Why this matters

A reusable double-dissociation framework that asks whether safety-relevant LLM behaviors are monolithic or decomposable — and so where surgical interventions can be aimed.

03Critical edge

The steering experiment — the causal core of the dissociation claim — is weak for Llama: in-domain R² 0.07 / 0.29 with near-zero slopes.

Methodology three convergent lenses

sycophancy split into factual (verifiable wrong claims) vs. opinion (subjective beliefs), probed on Gemma-3-12B-IT and Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct

Double-dissociation criterion, borrowed from cognitive science (Shallice 1988): two behaviors are representationally distinct only if each can be isolated and manipulated while the other stays intact. The paper enforces this with three mutually-corroborating methods rather than any single one.

Probe transfer. Logistic linear probes at every layer classify sycophantic vs. non-sycophantic residual-stream activations (final end-of-turn token). In-domain AUC (own subtype) vs. transferred AUC (other subtype) vs. a 50/50 combined probe; 5 seeds, 80/10/10 splits.

Activation steering. Difference-in-means vectors at a mid-network layer (Gemma L24, Llama L16) applied at every generation step; cosine similarity between the two subtype vectors and slope/R² of the rate-vs-coefficient curve measure shared causal structure.

LDA geometry. Project activations onto the direction maximally separating sycophantic vs. non-, then PCA the residuals to inspect how factual vs. opinion clusters sit relative to each other. A TF-IDF text baseline checks that probes capture more than surface wording.

Factual vs. opinion are linearly separable in both (AUC 0.99–1.00). Behaviorally, Gemma capitulates on 74.0% of factual but only 25.9% of opinion pushbacks; Llama on 33.6% / 44.0% — the subtypes differ in action, not just in representation.

Strengths & limitations side by side

Strengths
  • Clean operationalization of "distinct representations" via double dissociation.
  • Three independent lenses (probe, steering, LDA) that mutually reinforce.
  • Honest failure reporting — flags weak Llama in-domain steering explicitly.
  • Full prompt templates, pushback phrases, splits, seeds released for reproducibility.
Limitations
  • Steering is weak for Llama in-domain (R² 0.07/0.29) — causal leg only firm for Gemma.
  • Only two models — can't separate family from size or training-data effects.
  • TF-IDF baseline hits 0.97 on Gemma factual; surface text leaks the signal.
  • Single steering layer; GPT-5-as-judge validated on only 100 examples.

Open questions for further exploration

  1. Does the Gemma-unified / Llama-distinct contrast survive scaling (Llama-3.1-70B, Gemma-3-4B) or different instruction-tuning regimes — or is it a two-checkpoint artifact?
  2. Would a layer-wise steering sweep recover stronger in-domain causal effects for Llama, or is the weak R² a genuinely non-linear representation difference-in-means can't capture?
  3. Can these dissociated representations drive surgical safety interventions — suppressing factual-sycophancy while leaving appropriate opinion flexibility and TruthfulQA intact?
  4. How much probe AUC is surface text vs. genuine internal state, given TF-IDF reaches 0.97 on Gemma factual?
  5. Does the dissociation hold on naturalistic multi-turn chat logs where pushback phrasing is uncontrolled?
