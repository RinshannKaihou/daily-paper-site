Why This Matters
Standard GT-RMSE can improve while structure collapses — the paper rewrites how robustness of geometric foundation models should be evaluated.
Critical Edge
The headline 3.2× is relative to a benign-by-construction noise baseline; the fairer Edge-Shaped control gives only 1.0–1.7×, and no defense is proposed.
Methodology

Physical-causality verification is framed as a falsification-style counterfactual test — a four-axis diagnostic pipeline (Stability, Structure, Spillover, Downstream).

Scrambled Edges perturbation. Canny edges (50/150) are extracted, the top-K (K=15) connected components selected, each warped by an affine transform (θ∼U(±60°), translation U(±25% W,H)), then darkened by α=0.8:

I_scram = clip(I − α · M_scram, 0, 1) (~36% mask coverage on NYU)

Translation violates surface continuity, darkening violates illumination coherence, rotation breaks occlusion-ordering (T-junction) compatibility.

Two matched controls. (1) Energy-Matched High-pass Noise — RMS energy calibrated to match exactly (frequency control). (2) Edge-Shaped Noise — same masks shifted in place without relocation/rotation (structure control). Collapse under Scrambled Edges but not these isolates the physical-violation mechanism.

Oracle repair. The known perturbation mask splices clean predictions inside M_scram; the gap between input-oracle and output-oracle recovery quantifies global spillover — and a behavioral theory (App. B.5) proves nonzero out-of-mask spillover makes exact local repair impossible.

Setup. NYU Depth v2 (N=1449): MiDaS v2.1 (CNN), MiDaS DPT (ViT), DepthAnything v1/v2 (ViT, DINOv2). Generative extensions: Marigold, DepthFM. Cross-dataset: KITTI (N=6852). Paired t-tests + Bonferroni + paired Cohen's d.

All p < 10⁻¹⁰. Occlusion causality (Direction) alone nearly matches full Scrambled Edges — the dominant driver.

Further evidence:

  • Adoption tracks collapse (MiDaS DPT, α sweep): α=0.2 → 6.2% / 1.37×; α=0.8 → 36.1% / 2.34×. The model adopts false edges.
  • Oracle ceiling: Input Oracle 99.96% vs Output Oracle 47.0% vs Input Inpaint 24.0% — error already propagated outside the mask.
  • Metric paradox: GT-RMSE improves (DA V2: 4.08m → 3.51m) while Edge F1 drops up to −66.7%.
  • Generative estimators reduce but don't eliminate: Marigold 1.55× (d=0.88), DepthFM 1.11× (d=0.25) — both still significant.
  • Cross-dataset (KITTI): DA V2 3.20× → 2.48×, persists outdoors.
  • Task-selective: SAM segmentation shows no extra sensitivity — failure is geometry-specific.

Strengths

  • Conceptually clean: three priors, two matched controls, four diagnostic axes — unusually tidy experimental grammar.
  • Oracle-repair ceiling is both empirically demonstrated and formally proven (App. B.5) — observation turned into a theorem.
  • The metric-paradox findings (single-view GT-RMSE and multi-view photometric error both misleading) are actionable evaluation guidance.
  • Honest "negative emergence" framing; synthetic-not-attack; clear task-selectivity control (SAM).
  • Excellent statistical hygiene: full dataset, paired tests, Bonferroni, Cohen's d, unified results registry, reproducibility appendix.

Limitations

  • Diagnostic, not a fix — no architecture or training objective proposed to perform physical-causality verification.
  • Synthetic and edge-narrow: only edge-to-geometry shortcuts probed; texture/color/illumination shortcuts out of scope.
  • Real-world long-tail evidence (reflection/shadow/glass) is qualitative only — no quantitative collapse-rate comparison.
  • Only four feed-forward depth models; SSL-paradox claim leans on a single flagship (DA V2).
  • Headline 3.2× is vs a benign-by-construction baseline; the Edge-Shaped control gives 1.0–1.7×.
  • KITTI comparisons are descriptive only (independent sampling precludes paired tests).
Open Questions
  1. Does a photometric left-right / multi-view consistency loss during training (MonoDepth2-style) actually reduce Scrambled-Edges collapse, or merely shift which prior is violated?
  2. Would a learned support-aware edge-gating module beat the 47% output-oracle ceiling, or is spillover already irreversible by the encoder stage?
  3. How does Geometric Collapse behave across DINOv2 backbone scales (S/B/L) — is the "SSL paradox" DINOv2-specific or a property of masked-token objectives generally?
  4. Can the diagnostic extend to optical flow, stereo matching, and 3D Gaussian Splatting near occlusion boundaries?
  5. A quantitative long-tail table (real reflection/shadow/glass vs random samples) would test the practical-relevance claim beyond a case study.
  6. Is the metric paradox an artifact of median-scaling alignment — do scale-invariant metrics (SILog) also miss the failure?
