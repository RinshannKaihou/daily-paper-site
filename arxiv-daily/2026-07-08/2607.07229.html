Why this matters
If a transcript's stated reasoning doesn't connect to its answer, CoT is not safe evidence in that eval — a necessary, cheap, post-hoc audit any safety practitioner can run on existing corpora.
Critical edge
On contradictory reasoning the scanner scores F1 = 0.00 (5/5 missed), GPT-5.4 was silently dropped, Gemini uses reasoning summaries not full tokens, and Agentic Misalignment has n=1 — the headline rests on thin coverage.

Methodology — the mechanism, distilled

The paper formalizes reasoning consistency as distinct from CoT faithfulness: consistency asks whether "a line of thought can be drawn from the reasoning to the output" — observable from transcripts alone, no controlled intervention (hint injection, counterfactual perturbation) required. Faithfulness needs interventions that cannot be applied to existing corpora; consistency fills that audit gap.

A six-subtype taxonomy is applied through an ordered decision procedure (so the scanner doesn't default to familiar subtypes like reversal): missing answer → not applicable; non-engaging reasoning → absent; internally incompatible directions → contradictory; incoherent without explicit contradiction → apparent confusion; reasoning concludes one way, answer commits another → reasoning reversal; specific considerations raised then ignored → reasoning abandonment; reasoning too thin → perfunctory; otherwise consistent. The InspectScout LLM-as-judge scanner (held at Claude Opus 4.6 across all generators to control scanner variance) emits a structured label + subtype + confidence + one-sentence summaries + a disconnect description. It is validated on a 60-transcript InstrumentalEval-derived benchmark (real transcripts surgically modified per subtype) and then applied to MORU, Agentic Misalignment, and three SAD-mini subsets across four generators (seed=42).

Benchmark validation (60 transcripts, Opus 4.6): overall precision 0.96, recall 0.71, F1=0.82 — but per-subtype reliability varies wildly: consistent 0.97, perfunctory 0.83, reversal 0.80, abandonment 0.78, absent 0.80, apparent confusion 0.67, contradictory 0.00. The 0.27 F1 gap between scanner models (DeepSeek V4 Flash 0.55 → Haiku 4.5 0.68 → Opus 4.6 0.82) shows results are scanner-dependent by the authors' own admission. Perfunctory + absent dominate the flagged cases — consistent with multiple-choice inducing models to treat reasoning as a formality.

Strengths

  • Clean conceptual separation: consistency (observable, post-hoc) vs. faithfulness (causal, intervention-based) — fills a real audit gap for existing corpora.
  • Transparent reporting: discarded scanner models, the contradictory-reasoning failure, GPT-5.4 incompatibility, and the Gemini-summary caveat are all disclosed.
  • Open-source release of code, benchmark, and scanner lowers the barrier to replication.
  • Applied domain (inspect evals safety suite) is more consequential than MMLU/GPQA — finding 26% inconsistency in a situational-awareness eval is actionable.
  • Ordered decision procedure mitigates a known LLM-classification failure mode.

Limitations

  • Contradictory reasoning F1=0.00 — one of six subtypes effectively undetectable, yet pooled into the headline 0.82.
  • GPT-5.4 silently dropped; no Anthropic models as generators; Gemini rests on reasoning summaries not full tokens — thin frontier coverage.
  • Modest, uneven samples (Agentic Misalignment n=1); no CIs, significance tests, or multi-seed variance.
  • Validated on a single eval×generator pair (InstrumentalEval × DeepSeek V4 Pro) yet applied to others.
  • Results are scanner-/prompt-/threshold-dependent; full sensitivity analysis deferred.
  • Consistency ≠ faithfulness: a "consistent" transcript may still be post-hoc rationalization.

Open questions

  1. How would rates change with full (non-summarized) Gemini reasoning tokens — would the 26.0% stages-full figure hold?
  2. Can the contradictory-reasoning subtype (F1=0.00) be rescued with targeted prompts, contrastive examples, or a different scanner — or is it too fuzzy for a single-label classifier?
  3. Do the elevated multiple-choice rates reflect a genuine reasoning-answer disconnect, or an artifact of the format making any brief answer look "perfunctory" by definition?
  4. How sensitive are the headline rates to scanner-model choice — would a full sensitivity sweep change the cross-model ranking?
  5. Would adding Claude-family generators and a correctly-configured GPT-5.4 reveal different inconsistency profiles that change the conclusions?
  6. Is there a link between consistency-failure rate and downstream safety-relevant behavior on the underlying capability the eval is meant to measure?
