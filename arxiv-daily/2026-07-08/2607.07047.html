Why This Matters
SO WHAT

Ports mature BCI SPD-manifold tools to LM embeddings as a probe of representation geometry — and localizes the gain to the aggregation, not the encoder.

Critical Edge
⚠ Sharpest Critique

Everything runs on BERT-base layer 9 only with a single fixed encoder objective (IGL) — the central claim could be specific to that one encoder/layer, and is never tested elsewhere.

Methodology

A complete geometric aggregation pipeline porting mature BCI-grade SPD-manifold tools to LM token embeddings. Frozen BERT-base layer-9 tokens (768-dim) feed a small differentiable Intrinsic Green's Learning (IGL) encoder Ψθ: R⁷⁶⁸ → R⁶⁴, whose decoder is a closed-form tensor-factorised Product Green's kernel readout (K=128 anchors), forcing the encoder into manifold-aligned coordinates.

Because Ψθ is differentiable, its analytic Jacobian J(v) gives a per-token pullback metric g⁻¹(v) = J Jᵀ, regularised to a 64×64 SPD matrix (ε=1e-6, λ=1e-2). Per-token SPD matrices are aggregated via the Fréchet mean on the SPD manifold (affine-invariant distance), then Riemannian-whitened, projected to the tangent space at the training-set mean, and classified by pyRiemann's tangent-space logistic regression.

Crucially the same classification head and same frozen embeddings feed every method, so observed differences attribute cleanly to the aggregation step. Evaluation is 5-fold stratified CV × 2 seeds (10 evals/method), MOABB statistical conventions, permutation-test CIs, and a deliberate negative control (FEVER-Symmetric).

Ablation locates the gain to aggregation, not learned structure. A frozen random IGL encoder + Fréchet aggregation already beats Euclidean pooling on CoLA (0.762 ≈ trained 0.778) and RTE (0.616 vs 0.635). The trained encoder matters only on knowledge-heavy CREAK (+3.6 AUC over random).

The negative control is the intellectual-honesty centerpiece: all methods stay at chance on FEVER-Symmetric (RMP 0.508 inside the permutation-test 95% CI [0.443, 0.557]) — the pipeline does not ride on surface annotation artifacts.

Strengths

  • Clean single-variable design: identical embeddings and head across all methods — only the aggregation moves.
  • A principled, correctly interpreted negative control that genuinely tests for annotation-artifact leakage.
  • Random-encoder ablation is the most informative result: shifts the claim from "the trained manifold carries signal" to "geometric aggregation itself carries signal."
  • Honest, non-overclaimed writing — explicitly states what is not tested.
  • Reuses well-validated tooling (pyRiemann, MOABB protocol) rather than reinventing evaluation.

Limitations

  • Single encoder and single layer (BERT-base layer 9); generality to other layers, families, decoder-only LLMs is unverified.
  • Fixed encoder objective (IGL); no sweep over autoencoders / VAEs / flows / contrastive encoders.
  • Hyperparameter sweeps run only on the negative control (FEVER-Symmetric); sensitivity to λ on signal datasets unreported.
  • Roughly an order of magnitude more arithmetic per sentence than Euclidean pooling — no exact wall-clock / FLOP figures.
  • Weak baselines relative to SOTA: absolute AUC (e.g., RTE 0.638) far below fine-tuned BERT or Sentence-BERT.
  • Small negative-control sample (316 claims) — under-powered.

Open Questions

  1. Does the geometric-aggregation gain persist across other BERT layers, and does it peak where layer-wise intrinsic dimension does (Valeriani 2023; Cheng 2025)?
  2. Does the result generalise beyond BERT-base — to RoBERTa, DistilBERT, decoder-only LLMs?
  3. How does RMP compare against stronger but still training-free baselines (frozen Sentence-BERT, Matryoshka embeddings)?
  4. Are alternative Jacobian-yielding encoders (VAEs, normalising flows, contrastive) largely interchangeable with IGL here?
  5. Does replacing the simple Fréchet mean with weighted/trajectory-based descriptors add signal on knowledge-heavy CREAK?
  6. What does the pullback metric encode linguistically — can per-token eigenvalue spectra be tied to POS, syntax, or world-knowledge activation?
