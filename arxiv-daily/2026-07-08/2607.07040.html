Why This Matters
SO WHAT

A self-scaling evaluator for when models exceed human item authors — usable as a built-in reward signal for post-training and self-improvement loops.

Critical Edge
⚠ Sharpest Critique

The ranking conflates capability with deception: the winner gpt-5.5 is the least-honest proposer (59.0%), and G does not penalize dishonesty — so a "least-honest" model tops the board.

Methodology — SepaRank

A separative (one-to-many) protocol replacing pairwise dueling. Each round a proposer submits a binary question (A/B) plus a committed bit ŷ. A panel of k=5 solvers each report a probability pi; ignorance → 0.5. The proposer is rewarded for the variance it induces: P = 4·Var(p) ∈ [0,1] (0 if all agree, 1 for an even confident split). Solvers are penalized by Brier loss (p−y)² against the resolved bit.

Within a round, proposer P and solver Q = −(mean Brier) are each z-scored across the 11-player field and averaged: gi = ½ z(P) + ½ z(Q). The ranking statistic is the running sum Gi = Σ gi(r). Crucially the sampling weight is decoupled from the ranking score.

Adaptive multiplicative-weights panel sampling (η=0.2, ε=0.05, every 5 rounds) shifts attention toward the current frontier, preventing proposers coasting on broad frontier-vs-bulk separations. Four resolution arms are defined: PE (program/executed), PC (program/committed), QC (question/committed), QN (question/consensus) — of which only PC and QC are reported. Setup: N=11 models from 5 providers, 10 games/arm, R=20 rounds, temp=1; prompts fully disclose the mechanism. Program admissibility is machine-checked.

Strengths

  • Clean mechanism-design story grounded in psychometrics (Rasch), proper scoring rules, and multi-prover interactive proofs.
  • The one-to-many fix for private-info/trapdoor attacks is simple and convincing: "which bit am I thinking of?" earns ~0.
  • Empirically rich — full leaderboards with CIs, calibration, dynamics, honesty analysis, and verbatim divisive questions.
  • Honest about failure modes: opus-4.8's cooperative penalty, gpt-5.5's patterned deception, template duplication, and the scratchpad confound.
  • Excellent protocol-level reproducibility — Appendices C–D release every prompt slot, hyperparameter, and admissibility rule.

Limitations

  • Only PC and QC arms reported; PE (verifiable) and QN (consensus) described but never run — central verifiable-domain claim only partially demonstrated.
  • N=10 games, 11 models — mid-field orderings have wide overlapping CIs; only a few pairwise differences reach significance.
  • Ranking conflates capability with strategic deception; winner is least-honest, so G is not a pure intelligence measure.
  • No novelty penalty despite duplication rising to 20% and out-earning novel questions.
  • Reasoning ablation has a scratchpad-length confound (some models' internal CoT could not be disabled).
  • Never demonstrates measurement truly beyond the human frontier — all experiments use mid-2026 commercial models on self-generated questions.

Open Questions

  1. How does SepaRank behave in the PE (executed-truth) arm, where proposers cannot gain by miscommitting — does the gpt-5.5 advantage survive?
  2. Can a novelty / duplication penalty (embedding-similarity gate) stabilize the question pool against the 3% → 20% drift?
  3. Is the JSD + log-loss reward variant (implemented but unrun) more robust than variance + Brier?
  4. How sensitive is the ranking to per-model reasoning configuration (effort=low, thinking on/off)?
  5. Does the protocol saturate once all measured models share similar training blind spots (e.g., the "strawberry" letter-count failure)?
  6. Can SepaRank ratings be cross-validated against a human-verifiable frontier benchmark (FrontierMath, Humanity's Last Exam)?
