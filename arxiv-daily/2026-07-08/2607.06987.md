> **一句话总结：** 本文提出即插即用的 UP（Unbounded Positive Asymmetric Optimization）目标：用 stop-gradient 自锚定比率 πθ/sg(πθ) 替代重要性采样比率，在正优势（正确 rollout）上彻底移除上界裁剪以释放探索能力，在负优势上保留 DAPO/GRPO 标准裁剪以防止训练失稳；在 GRPO/DAPO/GSPO、Dense/MoE/VLM、语言与多模态等多种设置下均稳定提升推理精度（UP-GRPO 在五个推理基准平均 Pass@1 61.31%，超过 11 个基线最优 GSPO 1.16%）。

> **TL;DR:** UP is a plug-and-play RL objective that replaces the importance-sampling ratio with a stop-gradient self-anchored ratio πθ/sg(πθ) for positive advantages, removing the upper clip to maximize exploration while keeping standard clipping for negative advantages to prevent instability. As UP-GRPO it reaches the best average Pass@1 of 61.31% on AIME24/AMC23/MATH500/Minerva/OlympiadBench, beating the strongest baseline GSPO (60.15%) by +1.16%, and consistently improves DAPO (+3.44 Avg@32 on AIME24), GSPO on a 30B MoE (+3.02%), and GRPO on a multimodal VLM (+3.30% on Geometry3K).

## 核心贡献

- 形式化定义了"概率容量"（Probability Capacity, Cap），揭示当前基于重要性采样（IS）+ 裁剪的 RL 算法存在根本性矛盾：保守裁剪按 πold 线性限制正确低置信 token 的更新预算（如 πold=0.01、ϵhigh=0.28 时绝对概率增量上限仅 0.0028），结构性扼杀了对长尾正确推理路径的探索。
- 提出UP（Unbounded Positive Asymmetric Optimization）：用 stop-gradient 自锚定比率 r̃ = πθ / sg(πθ) 替代 IS 比率，对正优势推导出的梯度恰好等价于数学稳定的 REINFORCE 梯度（∇log πθ），从而在保留多步数据复用的同时彻底消除 IS 导致的梯度爆炸根因。
- 设计不对称路由：正优势分支完全无上界裁剪以最大化探索，负优势分支保留 DAPO/GRPO/GSPO 的标准裁剪作为结构性安全护栏；通过消融证明两段都不可少（单纯去掉上界裁剪会让梯度范数爆炸到 10^13；对称地都无界则 25 步内崩溃）。
- 将 UP 形式统一推广到 token 级（UP-GRPO、UP-DAPO）与 sequence 级（UP-GSPO，附录严格证明其正分支等价于长度归一化的 REINFORCE 梯度），并验证其作为通用插件跨 Dense/MoE/VLM、语言/多模态的普适性。

## Key Contributions

- Formalizes **Probability Capacity (Cap)** to expose a structural bottleneck: for low-likelihood correct tokens the update budget is capped linearly by πold (e.g., with πold=0.01 and ϵhigh=0.28 the maximum absolute probability increase is only 0.0028), so the gradient is prematurely truncated to zero the moment πθ exceeds (1+ϵhigh)πold — stifling exploration of rare correct reasoning paths.
- Introduces **UP (Unbounded Positive Asymmetric Optimization)**, which replaces the importance-sampling ratio with a stop-gradient self-anchored ratio r̃ = πθ/sg(πθ). The paper proves the gradient of this unbounded positive term is mathematically equivalent to the stable REINFORCE gradient (Â·∇log πθ), removing the root cause of IS-induced gradient explosion while still allowing multi-step reuse of sampled data.
- Designs an **asymmetric routing** objective: positive advantages get fully unclipped reinforcement to maximize exploration, while negative advantages retain the standard DAPO/GRPO/GSPO clipping as a structural safeguard. Ablations show both halves are indispensable — removing only the upper clip (DAPO with ϵhigh=∞) makes the gradient norm explode to 10^13 after 80 steps, and a symmetric unbounded (off-policy REINFORCE) variant collapses within 25 steps.
- Generalizes the UP formulation to both token-level (UP-GRPO, UP-DAPO) and sequence-level (UP-GSPO) optimization granularities, with a rigorous proof (Appendix C) that the positive branch of UP-GSPO equals a length-normalized REINFORCE gradient, and empirically validates universality across Dense, MoE, and vision-language architectures in both text and multimodal settings.

## Methodology

UP starts from the observation that the IS gradient ∇J_IS ∝ Â· r(θ)·∇log πθ is pathologically scaled by the ratio r(θ) = πθ/πold, which explodes for rare high-reward paths where πold is tiny. To fix this without losing the sample efficiency of off-policy multi-step updates, UP replaces the denominator with a **stop-gradient self-anchored ratio**:

r̃(θ) = πθ(o|·) / sg(πθ(o|·))

Because sg(πθ) is treated as a constant during backpropagation but equals πθ in value, applying the log-derivative trick yields ∇J_UP⁺ = E[Â·∇log πθ], which is exactly the REINFORCE gradient. This eliminates the IS ratio (and thus the need for an upper clip) for correct rollouts while remaining fully off-policy.

The key design choice is **asymmetry by rollout correctness**:
- For **positive advantages (Â > 0, correct rollouts)**: use the unbounded UP term `Â·log πθ` — no clipping, so the Probability Capacity becomes Cap = 1 − πθ, giving every token a full update budget.
- For **non-positive advantages (Â ≤ 0, wrong rollouts)**: keep the original algorithm's clipped objective unchanged, because an unbounded negative update would perform aggressive gradient ascent and destroy representations.

This asymmetric principle is instantiated as three plug-and-play variants:
- **UP-DAPO** (Eq. 14): unbounded log-objective for Â>0, DAPO's decoupled (1−ϵlow, 1+ϵhigh) clip for Â≤0.
- **UP-GRPO** (Eq. 15): unbounded log-objective for Â>0, GRPO's symmetric (1−ϵ, 1+ϵ) clip plus KL penalty for Â≤0.
- **UP-GSPO** (Eq. 16): unbounded length-normalized sequence log-objective for Â>0 (proven in Appendix C to equal a length-normalized REINFORCE gradient after the normalization exponents cancel), standard sequence-level clipping for Â≤0.

A practical benefit: the UP variants remove the ϵhigh hyperparameter entirely for the positive branch. Implementation is on the verl framework with vLLM rollouts; baselines and UP variants share identical training/hardware/decoding configs, with the only change being the loss objective.

## Results & Highlights

- **UP-GRPO vs 11 RL baselines (Table 1, Qwen3-8B on MATH L3-5, Pass@1):** UP-GRPO attains the best **average of 61.31%** across AIME24/AMC23/MATH500/Minerva/OlympiadBench, beating the strongest prior method GSPO (60.15%) by **+1.16% absolute**, and ranks 1st or tied-1st on four of the five benchmarks (AIME24 41.04%, AMC23 87.50%, MATH500 88.40%, Minerva 31.25%), second only to ASPO on OlympiadBench (58.33% vs 58.48%).
- **UP-DAPO vs DAPO (Qwen3-14B-Base, AIME24):** peak Avg@32 of **51.15 vs 47.71** (+3.44), Maj@32 **60.88 vs 58.36** (+2.52), and Best@32 **81.79 vs 80.49** (+1.30) — the higher entropy translates directly into a stronger exploration ceiling.
- **UP-GSPO vs GSPO on a 30B MoE (Qwen3-30B-A3B-Base, AIME24 Avg@32):** **55.73% vs 52.71%** (+3.02 absolute), with KL divergence nearly identical to the baseline throughout training — confirming the gain transfers from Dense to MoE and from token-level to sequence-level.
- **UP-GRPO vs GRPO in multimodal (Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct, Geometry3K test Pass@1):** **62.60% vs 59.30%** (+3.30 absolute), with KL divergence indistinguishable from GRPO, showing UP is modality-agnostic.
- **Stability despite higher exploration:** across training, UP-DAPO's gradient norm and KL divergence remain comparable to or slightly lower than DAPO's, and UP-GRPO keeps among the lowest KL of all twelve baselines while being the only method besides the asymmetric W-REINFORCE/ASPO that avoids entropy collapse.
- **Ablation — necessity of self-anchored ratio:** DAPO with the upper clip simply removed (ϵhigh=∞) but still anchored to πold diverges after ~80 steps, with gradient norm surging to **10^13**; UP's stop-gradient self-anchor is what prevents this.
- **Ablation — necessity of asymmetric design:** a symmetric unbounded variant (off-policy REINFORCE applied to both signs) collapses catastrophically within the first **25 steps**, confirming DAPO's clipping is strictly required for wrong rollouts.

## Authors & Credibility

The work comes from **ByteDance Seed** (Pengfei Liu, Jingjia Huang, Yi Lin) and **Michigan State University** (Chongyu Fan, Sijia Liu). The lead author has prior closely-related work on RLVR/VLA stability (cited as ref [6]) and reasoning reflection scheduling (CyclicReflex, ICLR 2026). The experimental protocol is conscientious: baselines and UP variants share identical backbones, corpora, batch sizes, generation budgets, decoding configs, and hardware — only the loss objective changes — and the paper adopts three established evaluation protocols from prior work (Yu et al./DAPO, Liu et al./Dr.GRPO, Zhao et al./GMPO). Full hyperparameter tables (Appendix A) and the exact gradient derivations including the negative-advantage Cap (Appendix B) and the UP-GSPO proof (Appendix C) are provided.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (genuine):** The stop-gradient self-anchor πθ/sg(πθ) that makes an unclipped off-policy ratio collapse exactly to the REINFORCE gradient is a clean, theoretically grounded idea, and the Probability Capacity formalism gives a crisp geometric argument for why DAPO-style elevated ϵhigh is only a patch. The connection to the length-normalization cancellation in UP-GSPO (Appendix C) is a nice non-trivial derivation. The idea is incremental relative to REINFORCE++/ASPO/W-REINFORCE (which also pursue asymmetry), but the *mechanism* (replacing the IS denominator rather than reshaping the clip) is distinct and well-motivated.
- **Rigor (strong, with gaps):** The controlled-variables setup is exemplary — identical configs, only the loss differs — and the ablations directly target the two design choices (self-anchor, asymmetry) with failure modes (10^13 gradient norm, 25-step collapse) that genuinely demonstrate necessity. However, the main 11-baseline comparison (Table 1) trains on a relatively small MATH L3-5 subset with 8 rollouts and a 3,072-token cap, which is a weaker compute regime than the 16-rollout/20,480-token DAPO setting; the claim of "universally best" rests partly on a single 8B-scale table. Statistical significance / variance over seeds is not reported — every delta in Table 1 is under ~2 points and several columns are ties, so seed noise could shuffle the ordering.
- **Reproducibility (good):** Built on the open verl + vLLM stack, hyperparameters fully tabulated, and the method is a small, well-specified code change. A project page is linked. Missing: code/checkpoint release is not explicitly confirmed, and the exact baseline hyperparameters are only "adopted from the original papers" without a table.
- **Significance (high if it holds at scale):** A genuinely plug-and-play loss that improves DAPO, GRPO, and GSPO on Dense/MoE/VLM with no extra cost and no stability penalty is exactly the kind of result practitioners will adopt. The risk is that the gains are benchmarked on Qwen3-family models only; whether the unbounded positive term helps (or causes late-stage drift) on other families and at frontier scale is untested.
- **Red flags:** (a) The headline "+1.16% average" is modest and lives within plausible seed variance for a single 8B run with no error bars; (b) the paper does not report response-length or reward-hacking checks, which matter when exploration entropy is deliberately increased; (c) the related asymmetric methods (ASPO, W-REINFORCE) are the only other entropy-preserving baselines yet UP is not directly compared *only* against them in a head-to-head controlled setting — ASPO actually beats UP on OlympiadBench; (d) "preventing training instability" is demonstrated via KL/gradient-norm curves but no long-training (>~500 step) collapse analysis is given for the largest runs.

## Strengths

- Clean theoretical core: the stop-gradient self-anchor turns the unbounded off-policy ratio into the exact REINFORCE gradient, with a rigorous derivation for both token-level and (non-trivially) sequence-level GSPO.
- The Probability Capacity formalism is an intuitive, reusable lens for diagnosing why clipping-based methods under-explore.
- Genuinely controlled experiments: identical backbone/data/budget/decoding between each baseline and its UP twin.
- Broad, well-organized validation: three GxPO algorithms, three architectures (Dense/MoE/VLM), two modalities, and three distinct evaluation protocols.
- Strong, targeted ablations that actually attempt to falsify the design (ϵhigh=∞, symmetric unbounded) and show clear failure modes.
- Practical upside: removes the ϵhigh hyperparameter for the positive branch, simplifying tuning.

## Limitations

- Quantitative margins in the main 11-baseline table are small (best avg +1.16%; ties on several columns) and no multi-seed variance or significance test is reported, leaving the ranking within noise for several benchmarks.
- All experiments use Qwen3-family models; generalization to Llama, Mistral, or proprietary frontier models is unverified.
- Exploration is increased by design, but the paper does not analyze potential side effects: response-length inflation, reward hacking, or late-training policy drift on the longest runs.
- The strongest compute setting (14B Dense, 30B MoE) only reports Avg@32/Maj@32 on AIME24 rather than the full 5-benchmark Pass@1 suite, so cross-benchmark consistency is shown mainly at 8B scale.
- No head-to-head, fully-controlled comparison isolating UP from the other asymmetric methods (ASPO, W-REINFORCE) that also avoid entropy collapse; ASPO outperforms UP on OlympiadBench.
- Code/checkpoint release is not explicitly stated; reproducibility hinges on the described verl+vLLM setup.

## Relevance

本文提出的 UP 目标直接服务于大模型推理场景下 RLHF/RLVR 训练的稳定性与探索效率问题，可作为即插即用的损失函数改进接入现有的 GRPO/DAPO/GSPO 流水线，对关注强化学习训练算法、LLM 数学推理能力提升与多模态推理的研究方向高度相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the Table 1 rankings survive multi-seed runs and a larger compute budget (more rollouts, longer responses, bigger backbone)? What are the standard deviations on each benchmark?
- Does the deliberately increased exploration entropy induce response-length inflation, reward hacking, or repetition — and does it emerge late in training on runs longer than ~500 steps?
- How does UP interact with non-Qwen3 families (Llama, Mistral, Gemma)? Is the self-anchored ratio equally stable across tokenizers and pretraining distributions?
- Can UP be combined with the other asymmetric methods (ASPO, W-REINFORCE, TOPR) that also preserve entropy, or do they target the same mechanism and become redundant?
- A direct, controlled head-to-head between UP, ASPO, and W-REINFORCE under identical settings — since all three avoid entropy collapse — would clarify which asymmetry design is best rather than only "UP vs its own baseline."
- What happens to UP in settings with mixed/noisy rewards (e.g., process reward models, RLHF with human preferences) where the correctness signal driving the positive/negative split is less clean?
