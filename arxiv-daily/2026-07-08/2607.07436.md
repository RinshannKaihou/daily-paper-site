> **一句话总结：** 本文从理论上证明，在自演化智能体的技能退役（skill retirement）机制中，LLM 评判器的"假通过"偏差（把失败误判为通过）会在阈值 ρ_F→P = (1−τ)/2 处使退役机制彻底失效（一个悬崖而非斜坡），而对称噪声只造成可恢复的衰减；在引用驱动的报告写作测试床与 MBPP+ 代码生成对照上，假通过偏差在所有子集上都将真实贡献退役压到接近零，且这一失效是"静默"的——只有在失败样本稀缺、合成也同步饿死的情形下才表现为评估质量下降。

> **TL;DR:** A biased LLM judge silently disables the skill-retirement curator in self-evolving agents: false-pass bias (true failures reported as passes) crosses a sharp threshold at ρ_F→P = (1−τ)/2 = 0.45 beyond which no skill is ever retired at any sample size, whereas symmetric noise only attenuates the signal gracefully. On a reference-free report-writing testbed (REPORT-MAIN-71, REPORT-BAND-58) and an MBPP+ HARD100 code cross-check, false-pass bias drives genuine contribution-retirement to ~0 across all subsets (e.g., 0/0.3/0 at q=0.2/0.45/0.7 vs clean 1.3), while a strict audited judge keeps retirement alive (10.3); the failure is silent because eval quality degrades (worst Δ = −0.065 pass@1 at q=0.45) only where the same corruption also starves skill synthesis.

## 核心贡献

- **揭示并刻画了一种"静默失效"机制——盲眼策展人（blind curator）。** 当奖励信号来自有偏差的 LLM 评判器时，技能退役不再只是变噪，而是在假通过偏差越过阈值 ρ_F→P = (1−τ)/2 后被整体关闭；该失效不会出现在任何聚合评估指标中。
- **给出可证的偏差感知非发散界（Proposition 1′）。** 严格区分两类损坏的因果通道：对称噪声仅衰减（κ = 1−2ρ，可通过增大样本量补偿），假通过偏差是加性位移、在阈值处让非发散保证变为空（vacuous）——是"悬崖"而非"斜坡"，且无法靠数据量挽回，唯一杠杆是退役阈值 τ。
- **把"真退役"与"配额驱逐扰动"分离，避免被原始 deprecation 计数误导。** 原始 Dep. 计数在噪声与偏差下都接近 10，但只有真贡献退役反映策展人判断；分离后假通过偏差把真退役压到零的模式在四个子集、两个领域上普遍成立。
- **提出部署前的 go/no-go 审计方法。** 通过在干净样本上注入已知缺陷，离线估计评判器的 ρ_F→P 并与悬崖 (1−τ)/2 比较，使理论阈值变成可操作的部署检查清单（Fig. 5 playbook）。
- **诚实报告"无端到端提升"。** 单种子 +0.12 的 pass@1 提升在三种子下降为 +0.014±0.054，配对连续指标下仍不显著（p=0.63）；作者将结论定位为行为安全性结果而非性能结果。

## Key Contributions

- **Identifies and formalizes a silent failure mode — the "blind curator."** When the reward comes from a biased LLM judge, skill retirement is not merely noisier; it is switched off entirely once false-pass bias crosses ρ_F→P = (1−τ)/2, and this disabling surfaces in no aggregate metric.
- **A bias-aware non-divergence bound (Proposition 1′) that separates two corruption channels.** Symmetric noise attenuates the retirement statistic (κ = 1−2ρ, survivable, compensable with more data), while false-pass bias additively displaces it and renders the non-divergence guarantee vacuous past the cliff — a threshold, not a slope, that no sample size can cross; the only lever is the retirement threshold τ.
- **Decouples genuine contribution-retirement from cap-eviction churn.** The raw deprecation count stays ~10 under both noise and bias and masks the mechanism; once separated, the false-pass-kills-retirement signature is shown to be universal across four subsets and two domains (report writing + MBPP+ code).
- **A before-deployment go/no-go audit.** Inject known defects into clean sections, estimate the judge's ρ_F→P offline, and compare to the cliff (1−τ)/2 — turning the theory into an operational playbook (Fig. 5) that tells an operator whether self-evolution is safe to deploy.
- **An intellectually honest "no end-to-end lift" finding.** A single-seed +0.12 pass@1 lift collapses to +0.014 ± 0.054 over three seeds and stays null under a paired continuous re-measurement (p=0.63); the paper explicitly frames its result as behavioral safety, not performance gain.

## Methodology

The study builds on **Ratchet** (Zhang et al., 2026b), a minimal governance recipe on a frozen LLM: a Critic labels failures, a Synthesizer clusters recurring failure patterns into skills, a Router retrieves at most one skill per task, and a Curator retires any skill whose empirical contribution ĉ(s) = 2p̂(s)−1 falls to −τ after ≥ Nmin trials under a hard cap C on active skills. Ratchet's appeal is a non-divergence guarantee — expected eval pass@1 cannot fall more than a fixed margin below the no-skill baseline — whose proof assumes an unbiased reward estimator.

**Theoretical channel.** The judge is modeled as a binary channel on the true outcome y ∈ {0,1}: Pr[ỹ=1|y=0] = ρ_F→P (a hidden failure / false pass) and Pr[ỹ=0|y=1] = ρ_P→F (a phantom failure / false fail). Under the channel the observed pass rate concentrates on p_obs(s) = κ·p̄(s) + ρ_F→P with κ = 1 − ρ_F→P − ρ_P→F. Two regimes follow: (i) symmetric noise (ρ_F→P = ρ_P→F = ρ < ½) compresses the statistic toward ½ but preserves ordering, at the cost of an inflated effective threshold τ_eff = τ/(1−2ρ) and an Nmin ∝ (1−2ρ)⁻² budget — graceful for every ρ < ½; (ii) false-pass bias (ρ_F→P > 0, ρ_P→F = 0) shifts the statistic up most for the worst skills, and retirement fires only if p̄(s) ≤ (πτ − ρ_F→P)/(1 − ρ_F→P), whose right-hand side hits zero at ρ_F→P = πτ = (1−τ)/2 — beyond it no skill is ever retired at any sample size. This yields Proposition 1′ (bias-aware non-divergence floor) and three design rules: noise is survivable but bias is not past the cliff; compensate with τ not with Nmin; ρ_F→P is measurable offline.

**Testbed.** Because the phenomenon requires a fallible judge, the verifier-backed benchmarks Ratchet was built on (MBPP, SWE-bench) are ruled out. The authors use reference-free, citation-grounded long-form report writing: 155 evidence slices from five production deep-research engines, with a deterministic grader of five QC checks (orphan citation, unregistered metric, bare number, broken cross-reference, missing TL;DR) treated as ground truth y, onto which corrupted channels ỹ are injected. Four subsets are constructed: REPORT-MAIN-71 (Haiku 4.5, 43/28 tr/ev, floor 0.628), REPORT-BAND-58 (stricter tier-2 reward, real headroom, floor 0.388), REPORT-HARD-133 (always-fail sections added back, abundant failures, floor 0.198), and MBPP+HARD100 (code, Opus 4.7, perfect unit-test verifier, floor 0.273) as a cross-domain control. All runs use 3 seeds × 12 rounds with τ=0.10, Nmin=24, cap C=12; corruption touches training rewards only, evaluation always uses the true grader.

**Defect-injection gate (Sec. 5).** Five QC-visible defect classes mirror the deterministic checks; two QC-invisible classes (claim negation — flip "growth"→"decline" keeping the citation; number swap — perturb a digit keeping the marker) corrupt semantics while preserving all annotation syntax. This audit also locates the real binary PASS/FAIL training judge on the theory's axes (a strict, well-instructed judge with ρ_F→P ≈ 0.01, ρ_P→F ≈ 0.95, κ ≈ 0.04). Crucially, because the audited judge is conservative rather than lenient, the authors inject false-pass bias exogenously on top of the deterministic reward to isolate the causal channel cleanly, rather than claiming their particular judge is lenient.

## Results & Highlights

- **False-pass bias zeros out genuine retirement; symmetric noise and the strict judge keep it alive (REPORT-MAIN-71, Table 1).** The True-ret. column (genuine contribution-retirement, separated from cap-eviction churn) is 1.3 under clean QC and 0.7–1.0 under symmetric noise (ρ=0.1–0.4), but is driven to exactly **0.0 / 0.3 / 0.0** at false-pass bias q=0.2 / 0.45 / 0.7. The realistic audited LLM judge — despite the highest realised label-flip divergence (0.59, all phantom failures) — keeps true retirement fully alive at **10.3**, far above clean, exactly because its ρ_F→P ≈ 0.01 sits on the safe side of the cliff predicted by Proposition 1′.
- **Outcome harm is an inverted-U peaking at the theory's retirement cliff (Fig. 3b).** Eval pass@1 Δ vs the clean-reward loop stays at or above clean under all symmetric noise (+0.018 to +0.060), is worst at **q=0.45 with Δ = −0.065 pass@1** (the predicted cliff (1−τ)/2 = 0.45), and returns near clean at extreme bias q=0.7 (+0.039) because synthesis itself starves and the near-inert library stops accumulating harmful skills. The dangerous judge is the half-blind one that feeds the synthesizer while disarming the curator.
- **The gate audit shows the two reward signals are complementary (Fig. 2, 155 sections, one injection per class per section).** The deterministic QC grader catches every QC-visible defect (recall **1.0, n=155 per class**) but is essentially blind to semantic defects (claim negation **0.0**, number swap **0.05**); a held-out LLM judge (different family, blind to condition) flags number swap at **98.5%** and claim negation at **92.7%**, with significant grounding-score drops (number swap **−1.15, p < 10⁻⁴**; claim negation **−0.35, p = 0.001**). On structural defects the judge's scalar quality score barely moves (p ≈ 0.5).
- **The mechanism failure is universal across domains and failure abundance (Fig. 4).** Genuine retirement falls to ≈0 past the cliff in every subset — including the abundant-failure REPORT-HARD-133 (**0.0**) and the cross-domain MBPP+HARD100 (**0.3**) — while the strict LLM judge keeps it alive. Downstream eval harm appears only in the failure-scarce REPORT-BAND-58 (**−0.036**) where synthesis starves (20→6 failures/round, skills 20→11); the abundant subsets hold at or above clean despite the dead curator, which is exactly why the disabled curator is silent.
- **The mechanism replicates with real headroom (REPORT-BAND-58, Fig. 6b).** Where the clean curator genuinely retires ≈7 skills/run, false-pass bias drives it to **0 by q=0.45**; noise preserves it. Decomposing the deprecation count (Fig. 6c) shows genuine retirement collapsing 7→0 while blind cap-eviction churn props up the raw total — the structural reason the raw Dep. count is misleading.
- **End-to-end lift is undetectable at this resolution (Table 3, paired re-measurement on REPORT-BAND-58).** A single-seed +0.12 pass@1 lift shrank to **+0.014 ± 0.054** over three seeds; under a paired continuous-violation design the held-out lift is **+0.26 violations/section (p=0.63)** with binary pass difference **exactly 0 (p=0.88)**, and the symmetric harm test is also null (p=0.61). The only significant effect is mechanism-aligned: the library significantly reduces the targeted unsourced-number violation class on the full band (**p=0.03**).
- **The non-divergence cap held throughout.** No condition fell more than a fixed margin (all means within **0.125**) below the no-skill floor, including those where retirement was inoperative, exactly as Proposition 1′ guarantees.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: genuinely new framing, but the bias is injected rather than observed.** The contribution is not "LLM judges are biased" (well-trodden) but the propagation of a judge's error *asymmetry* through a specific closed-loop lifecycle decision, with a sharp falsifiable cliff. However, the audited real judge is conservative (ρ_F→P ≈ 0.01, ρ_P→F ≈ 0.95) — it is *not* lenient. The false-pass cliff is therefore reached by **exogenously injecting** bias on top of a deterministic reward to isolate the causal channel, which the authors are transparent about. This means the "universal mechanism failure" is demonstrated for a *constructed* corruption channel, not for a naturally-occurring lenient judge in the wild; the adversarial-coupling case where skill content itself induces judge blindness (making ρ_F→P skill-dependent and growing) is explicitly out of scope (Sec. 9, Appendix C remark).
- **Rigor: strong on mechanism, weak on outcome.** The separation of genuine retirement from cap-eviction churn is the single most important methodological move — without it the raw Dep.≈10 under both noise and bias would hide the entire effect, and the paper is commendably explicit that "reading Dep. alone is misleading." The paired continuous re-measurement (Appendix G) is a serious attempt to resolve the null lift honestly rather than shop for a significant seed. But the headline outcome signal rests on very small eval sets (23–28 binary-scored sections), and the authors concede the quantum is 1/23 ≈ 0.043 with structural unpaired variance that no number of seeds can remove — so the inverted-U harm curve and the regime-dependent outcome claims, while directionally consistent, are individually underpowered.
- **Reproducibility: moderate-to-good.** Hyperparameters (τ=0.10, Nmin=24, C=12, 3 seeds × 12 rounds, temperature 0.7, 2200-token cap) are fully specified, and MBPP+HARD100 uses Ratchet's published hard-100 split unchanged (baseline pass@1 0.273 vs 0.258 originally reported). But the primary report-writing testbed draws on **proprietary deep-research corpora** ("the real slices follow the identical schema but draw on proprietary research corpora, which is why we do not reproduce one verbatim"), so the main domain is not independently reproducible from the paper alone; only the MBPP+ control and the synthetic anonymized schema are.
- **Significance: high for the governance subfield, conditional for practice.** The go/no-go audit (Fig. 5) is a concrete, cheap, before-deployment deliverable, and the result that the danger axis is the judge's false-pass rate (not the domain) reframes which deployments are exposed. The practical caveat is that the audited production judge sits on the *safe* side of the cliff, so the paper's most alarming failure mode is, by its own audit, not the one its own judge exhibits — it is a risk for lenient rubrics, capable composers whose errors look fluent, or defects the judge cannot see.
- **Red flags / overclaiming to watch.** (1) The phrase "universal mechanism failure" is strong given the bias is injected exogenously and the one real judge tested is conservative; the universality is of the *arithmetic* given a channel, not of the channel's occurrence. (2) Clean evolution does not beat the no-skill floor on REPORT-MAIN-71 (Δ = −0.060), so the paper measures differential damage of corruption, not the loop's upside — a scope the authors state but a reader could miss. (3) The QC reward covers citation discipline, not insight; a section can pass QC yet be vacuous, so "PASS" is a narrow notion of quality. (4) Missing ablations: no sweep over τ (the lever the theory identifies) to show widening it recovers retirement under moderate bias, and no test of the adversarial-coupling regime where the library learns to exploit judge blindness — the case most relevant to real deployment risk.

## Strengths

- The corrupted-reward channel model and Proposition 1′ give a clean, falsifiable theoretical prediction (graceful noise vs. cliff bias) that the experiments are then designed to test directly — theory and empirics are tightly coupled rather than decorative.
- Decoupling genuine contribution-retirement from cap-eviction churn is an important and non-obvious methodological contribution; without it the headline deprecation counts would have hidden the effect entirely.
- Intellectual honesty about the null end-to-end lift: the authors down-grade a tempting +0.12 single-seed result with three seeds and a paired continuous design, and explicitly frame the paper as a behavioral-safety result rather than a performance one.
- The defect-injection audit turns an abstract threshold into a concrete, cheap, before-deployment go/no-go test (Fig. 5 playbook), which is the kind of actionable artifact practitioners can actually use.
- Cross-domain generalization check (MBPP+HARD100 with a perfect unit-test verifier and a different composer) tests whether the effect is a property of the curator's arithmetic rather than of one domain.

## Limitations

- The study centers on one domain and composer (report writing, Claude Haiku 4.5), with MBPP+ adding only a single second of each as a control; the primary testbed relies on proprietary research corpora and is not independently reproducible.
- The audited real judge is conservative (ρ_F→P ≈ 0.01), not lenient, so the dangerous false-pass cliff is reached by exogenously injecting bias rather than by observing a naturally-lenient production judge — the most deployment-relevant regime (a learned judge whose blindness the library could exploit, i.e. adversarial coupling) is explicitly out of scope.
- Outcome signals are underpowered: 23–28 binary-scored eval sections give a quantum of ~0.043 with structural unpaired variance that more seeds cannot resolve; the inverted-U harm curve and regime-dependent outcome claims are individually fragile even if directionally consistent with the mechanism.
- The QC reward covers citation discipline, not insight or factual correctness of the underlying claim, so a section can pass QC yet be vacuous or subtly wrong in ways the deterministic grader cannot see.
- No ablation over the retirement threshold τ — the very lever the theory identifies against bias — to show widening it recovers retirement under moderate false-pass bias.

## Relevance

This paper sits squarely in the user's "Self-Evolving Agents & Automated Research" and "ML Reliability & Mathematical Foundations" interests: it gives a formal non-divergence bound and a sharp failure-mode analysis for the governance layer of self-improving LLM agents, and turns the result into a cheap before-deployment audit. It also touches "LLM Inference Evaluation" by treating the LLM-as-judge error asymmetry (not just noise) as the object of study and propagating it through a closed learning loop.

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does widening the retirement threshold τ (the lever the theory identifies) actually recover genuine retirement under moderate false-pass bias q≈0.2–0.3 in practice, and at what cost to the non-divergence margin?
- In the adversarial-coupling regime where skill content can induce judge blindness (ρ_F→P becomes skill-dependent and grows along the trajectory), does the exogenously-injected cliff underestimate, match, or overestimate the real failure point?
- How robust is the inverted-U harm curve to larger eval sets — would a continuous, paired evaluation across all 155 slices confirm the regime-dependent outcome story that the underpowered 23-section eval can only suggest?
- Can the defect-injection audit be extended to estimate a *joint* distribution of ρ_F→P over defect classes rather than a single scalar, and does per-class false-pass rate better predict which skill types silently survive?
- Does the "silent failure invisible to aggregate metrics" finding generalize to other failure-driven governance schemes beyond Ratchet (e.g. Reflexion-style episodic reflection, workflow memory), which share the same reliance on observed failures as the sole input?
