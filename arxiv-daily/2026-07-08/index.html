Most “Self-Improvement” Has No Verifiable Loop, 1,250-Paper Survey Warns

Mingguang Chen, Licheng Wang & Bo Qu — Recursive Self-Improvement in AI: From Bounded Self-Refinement to Autonomous Research Loops

The most systematic map of AI self-improvement to date organizes 1,250 recent papers along two axes — what a system improves and how closed the loop is — and bluntly concludes that demonstrated improvement strength tracks a “verification hierarchy,” from formal proof down to mere self-assessment. Most “self-X” claims sit at the weak end, with no verifiable progress at all.

1250+ 篇综述：多数“自改进”缺乏可验证闭环，改进强度随“验证层级”递减；治理级度量是最空白的 niche。

TL;DR — A two-axis taxonomy cleanly separates bounded self-refinement from open-ended RSI; self-evaluation is the load-bearing ceiling on every loop.
