Critical Assessment · Limitations · Sec. 9

Methodology § built on Ratchet

closed-loop governance · biased-reward channel

Critic→ Synthesizer→ Router→ Curator (retire)

A frozen LLM runs Ratchet: a Critic labels failures, a Synthesizer clusters recurring failure patterns into skills, a Router retrieves ≤1 skill/task, and a Curator retires any skill whose empirical contribution ĉ(s)=2p̂(s)−1 falls to −τ after ≥Nmin trials under a hard cap C.

The judge is modeled as a binary channel on the true outcome: Pr[ỹ=1|y=0]=ρF→P (hidden failure / false pass). Under it, two regimes split cleanly — symmetric noise attenuates (κ=1−2ρ, survivable, compensable with data), while false-pass bias additively displaces the statistic and makes the non-divergence guarantee vacuous past the cliff.

Retirement can fire only if p̄(s) ≤ (πτ − ρF→P) / (1 − ρF→P), whose RHS hits zero at ρF→P = (1−τ)/2 — beyond it, no skill is ever retired at any sample size. The only lever is τ.

Strengths what lands

  • Theory–empirics coupling. Proposition 1′ predicts graceful noise vs. cliff bias; the experiments test that prediction directly.
  • Decoupling genuine retirement from cap-eviction churn. Without it the raw Dep.≈10 would have hidden the entire effect.
  • Honest null. A tempting +0.12 single-seed lift is down-graded to +0.014 ± 0.054 over 3 seeds; framed as behavioral safety, not performance.
  • Actionable artifact. The defect-injection audit (Fig. 5 playbook) turns the threshold into a cheap before-deployment go/no-go test.
  • Cross-domain check. MBPP+HARD100 with a perfect verifier confirms the effect is the curator's arithmetic, not one domain.

Limitations what to watch

  • One domain + composer. Report writing on Claude Haiku 4.5; MBPP+ adds only one second of each. Primary testbed uses proprietary corpora — not independently reproducible.
  • Bias is injected, not observed. The audited real judge is conservative (ρF→P≈0.01); the dangerous cliff is reached exogenously.
  • Adversarial coupling out of scope. A learned judge whose blindness the library could exploit — the most deployment-relevant case — is explicitly deferred.
  • Underpowered outcomes. 23–28 binary-scored sections give a quantum of ~0.043 the inverted-U can only suggest, not confirm.
  • QC ≠ insight. The reward covers citation discipline, not factual correctness; a section can pass QC yet be vacuous.
  • No τ sweep. No ablation over the very lever the theory identifies against bias.

Open questions for further work

  1. Does widening τ recover genuine retirement under moderate false-pass bias (q≈0.2–0.3), and at what cost to the non-divergence margin?
  2. In adversarial coupling — where skill content induces judge blindness and ρF→P grows along the trajectory — does the injected cliff under- or over-estimate the real failure point?
  3. Does the inverted-U harm survive a continuous paired evaluation across all 155 slices, or is it an artifact of the 23-section quantum?
  4. Can the audit estimate a joint per-class ρF→P distribution, and does it predict which skill types silently survive?
  5. Does "silent failure invisible to aggregate metrics" generalize beyond Ratchet — to Reflexion-style reflection or workflow memory, which also rely on observed failures as sole input?
