Methodology — self-contained probability/PDE derivation

Why KL/χ²/Wasserstein convergence can't bound the probability of being unsafe

Training is idealized as the overdamped Langevin SDE dQ_t = −∇J(Q_t)dt + σdW_t on a loss J ∈ C²(ℝᵈ) with two-sided Hessian envelope mI ⪯ ∇²J ⪯ LI. The unsafe set A_H = {Q : a_H(Q) > α} triggers an alarm that costs at least ψ(α) in loss. The stationary law is the Gibbs measure π ∝ e⁻²ᴶᐟσ².

The argument chain: the density ratio solves the backward Kolmogorov equation under the Langevin generator; Brascamp-Lieb / Bakry-Émery gives the Poincaré constant m, yielding L²(π) contraction. The key trick is centering the indicator 1_A − π(A) so the Cauchy-Schwarz second factor drops from second moment π(A) to variance π(A)(1−π(A)) — exactly why the bound is informative for rare sets.

ν_t(A_H) ≤ π(A_H)(1 + √(χ²₀/π(A_H))·e⁻ᵐᵗ)  ⟶  2π(A_H) after O(d) burn-in
π(A_H) ≤ K·e⁻ᴬᵈᐟ²  (A = log(πσ²/L),  static dimensional decay)

A maximum-principle ceiling ν_t(A_H) ≤ M·π(A_H) sandwiches the bound when M = ‖ν₀/π‖_∞ < ∞. For geometrically isolated sets, a local relaxation rate λ_A_H ≥ m (defined via the spectral measure of the centered indicator, sidestepping the +∞ Dirichlet energy of an indicator) shrinks the burn-in by m/λ_A_H and combines with the ceiling into a uniform-in-time bound. Sharpness is checked on the exactly solvable Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process.

The High-Dimensional Taxonomy — three failure geometries on a thin shell

Where in parameter space is an unsafe set tolerable?

Geometry A
Off-shell ball (at the mode)
Surrounded by an "entropic moat" — mass must concentrate to reach it.
local rate λ_A_H ≥ 2m — geometry doubles the relaxation
Geometry B
Far-tail ball
Guarded by an Arrhenius / Eyring-Kramers energy barrier against the drift.
barrier exp[−2(m/L)ψ(α)/σ²] in small-noise regime
Geometry C — worst sweller
Shell-slice (along the shell)
Free transport along the thin shell; the local rate collapses.
local rate λ_A_H → 0 — only the ceiling survives

The shell-slice gives transient overshoot C_bad = exp[(d/2)·φ(u)] with φ(u)=u−1−log u ≥ 0 — proving the gap between shape-free and shape-aware bounds is real and exponential in d.

Results — the five quantitative claims

Theorems and worked examples, not benchmarks

Static decay
π(A_H) ≤ K·e⁻ᴬᵈᐟ², A = log(πσ²/L); prior global bounds gave no comparable set-specific rate.
Burn-in
t⋆ = (1/2m)·log(χ²₀/π(A_H)) + (A/4m)·d — linear in d; combined bound P(Q_t∈A_H) ≤ 2π(A_H).
Ceiling
When M < ∞: ν_t(A_H) ≤ M·π(A_H), uniform in time.
Off-shell rate
Symmetry gives λ_A_H ≥ 2m — a concrete factor-2 saving from geometry.
Shell overshoot
Angular amplification ν_t ≤ R_shell·Kᵈᐟ²; R_shell must beat (1/2)log K.

Strengths

  • Isolates the right object — set-mass ν_t(A_H) — and proves global divergences provably cannot control it
  • Two-grade structure makes the "cost of geometric ignorance" explicit and quantitative
  • Technically careful — λ_A_H via spectral measure of the centered indicator is the correct fix for indicator functions
  • Immediately usable taxonomy for where in parameter space an unsafe set is tolerable
  • Honest positioning against [BRG+26] — supplies dynamics the Scientist-AI safety case assumed abstractly

Limitations

  • Convexity regime only — needs global m-strong convexity, L-smoothness; not real deep-network losses
  • Continuous-time, isotropic idealization — discretization and anisotropic minibatch covariance out of scope
  • Uniform cap needs M < ∞, which fails for point-mass (deterministic) initialization — the common SGD case
  • No universal Cheeger lower bound on λ_A_H — must verify per family, limiting black-box use
  • No empirical/numerical validation — not even a finite-d OU simulation confirms the predicted burn-in
  • Static bound only informative when πσ² > L

Open Questions — for further exploration

Can λ_A_H be lower-bounded from conductance by a universal Cheeger-type inequality (λ_A_H ≥ c·h²_A), so flux-isolation becomes checkable rather than per-family?
What survives under discrete-time SGLD with anisotropic minibatch covariance — does the centered-indicator conversion still go through, and at what step-size-dependent rate?
How does the picture change when strong convexity is dropped (non-convex multi-basin) — can Theorem 6.1 be restated with a basin-dependent Eyring-Kramers escape rate?
Is the warm-start requirement M < ∞ removable via a short-time smoothing argument, so point-mass SGD init still gets a uniform cap after regularization?
Can the framework be instantiated on the Scientist-AI Predictor's C_bad·R_shell bound to give a numerical, dimension-explicit safety budget rather than a structural one?
