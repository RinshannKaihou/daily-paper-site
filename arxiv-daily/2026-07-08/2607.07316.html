Results & Highlightsquantitative claims cited (not new experiments)

26 heads
Full IOI circuit in GPT-2 Small — Duplicate Token, S-Inhibition, Name Mover heads divide the task. A minimalist symbolic IOI needs only 2 heads.
60% ↓
NeSyFOLD's Elite BackProp (EBP) cuts generated rule-set size, often by more than 60%, without losing accuracy or fidelity.
10⁷⁺ features
SAEs on Claude 3 Sonnet decompose activations into tens of millions of features; scaling laws suggest 10⁷–10⁸ may be needed for frontier models.
  • ALTI ≈100× faster than ACDC: IOI on 50 examples in ~5 s vs 8 min on one GPU — fast, but attribution-grade, not ablation-grade.
  • 370M-parameter Mamba solves IOI differently from Transformers: shifts names one position via convolutions, performs key ops linearly in the bottleneck of its 39th layer.
  • SAE input widened to m ≫ n (e.g. 32×); L1 shrinkage motivates Gated / TopK / JumpReLU / BatchTopK variants; dead-feature revival via AuxK loss.
  • Multilingual processing: three-phase (language-specific → language-agnostic → re-specialization) with an inverted-U language-entropy curve — though resting on a single circuit-tracing study.

Strengths & Limitationsbalance sheet

Strengths

  • Comprehensive, well-structured coverage: circuits → features → interventions → symbolic rules in one document.
  • PRISMA-ScR methodology + PCC eligibility table give selection a transparency ad-hoc surveys lack.
  • SAE architecture comparison table (Standard/Gated/TopK/JumpReLU/BatchTopK) and steering-vector hierarchy are genuinely reusable reference material.
  • Honest in Section 7 about the field's limits: causal-reliability-vs-scalability, steering-vector fragility, NeSy approximation nature.
  • Bridges two usually-separate communities (MI & neurosymbolic) and clarifies their complementary relationship.

Limitations

  • No new experiments; value is entirely synthesis. Seek primary findings in the cited sources.
  • No critical appraisal of included studies (by PRISMA-ScR design) — individual claim quality ungraded.
  • Exact search string, full PRISMA flow counts, and screening-stage lists not reported → review reproducibility limited.
  • Coverage skews Anthropic-affiliated (Templeton, Bricken, Lieberum, McDougall, Sofroniew); may underweight competing lines.
  • Neurosymbolic section leans on the authors' own NeSyFOLD/NeSyViT lineage — mild self-citation bias.
  • July 2026 preprint; some 2026 citations (arXiv 2602–2604) are very recent and may not yet be replicated.

Questions for Further Explorationopen problems surfaced

  • How can the field build standardized benchmarks that distinguish causally validated circuits (activation-patching-confirmed) from attribution-only ones, so review claims cannot conflate the two evidentiary standards?
  • Given SAE scaling laws (10⁷–10⁸ features for frontier models), what is the compute & storage cost of training/serving such SAEs — and does feature-clamping-based steering stay tractable at that scale?
  • The multilingual three-phase finding rests on a single circuit-tracing study; what independent replications exist across Llama / Mistral / Qwen, and does the shared language-agnostic space persist when English is absent from pretraining?
  • Can Jacobian matching or other faithfulness penalties make transcoder-derived circuits causally faithful enough to count as MI, rather than decision-boundary approximation?
  • Can the Linear Representation Hypothesis — which underpins all surveyed steering methods — be empirically falsified for safety-relevant behaviors (deception, sandbagging), and what would a non-linear failure imply for steering-based safety?
