Methodology

Setup is continual learning: after task t an anchor µ=θ_t and per-synapse importance s_i (online-EWC diagonal Fisher) are stored. During later tasks each weight follows an Euler–Maruyama SDE with three drift terms:

dw_i = [ −∂L_task − s_i(w_i−µ_i) + σ_i² ∂w log h_i(w) ] dt + σ_i dW_i    (learning) (anchored, surrendered) (Doob steering = contribution)

The Doob steering conditions each weight to stay in its memory-critical interval (µ_i−b_i, µ_i+b_i). The ground-state h-transform of Brownian motion killed at the interval ends gives a restoring force that diverges at the barrier and is multiplied by σ² — the intrinsic noise literally powers the confinement:

h_i(w) = cos(π(w−µ_i)/(2b_i)) ⟹ ∂w log h_i = −π/(2b_i)·tan(π(w_i−µ_i)/(2b_i))

Barrier half-width is the Fisher iso-loss radius b_i = b_0/√(1+s_i/median(s)), so high-Fisher (important) synapses get tight barriers — the same global noise is steered per-synapse by memory geometry.

Pre-registered falsifier (load-bearing). GATE F committed before E0–E4 ran: the inverted-U must pass on Split-MNIST (8 seeds, one-sided paired Wilcoxon, all seeds agree) AND no matched control may be inverted-U. The result: an interior optimum at σ*=0.02 (lift +10.9 pts, p=0.004) while OU/EWC/MESU/naive anchors are all monotone-decreasing. Mechanism isolation via κ:1→0 flattens the lift from 13.0 → 0.0 pts.

Rehearsal-free comparison (Split-MNIST, 8 seeds):

  • Doob 68.3% matches MESU (65.2%, n.s. p=0.25); beats OU 57.4, EWC 59.1, naive 57.4 (all p ≤ 0.008).
  • Plain reservoir replay (250 exemplars) reaches 83.0 (+14.6 over Doob) — a different budget class with no noise-consolidation mechanism.
  • Benna–Fusi cascade "did not learn" (50.6%, chance) — flagged, not buried.

On-silicon (E7, single seed): rule run with analog MAC in the loop on real BrainScaleS-2 — retains prior task at 69.6% vs 54.0% (+15.6) at the cost of task-2 plasticity (46.6 vs 64.0); two-task average matched (58.1 vs 59.0).

Strengths

  • Sharp, falsifiable, pre-registered prediction tested with correct paired machinery and a conjunctive gate no control may satisfy.
  • Decisive mechanism isolation via the κ:1→0 ablation and the barrier-scale optimum-tracking experiment.
  • Exemplary intellectual honesty — novelty precisely delimited, drift surrendered, Benna–Fusi failure flagged, single-seed silicon scoped.
  • Hardware-aligned: actually measures intrinsic noise on BrainScaleS-2 and runs the rule on-chip.
  • Device-reachability: the optimum is tunable into the few-percent-CV band the measured chip occupies.

Limitations

  • Small single-head MLP testbeds (Split-MNIST 784-100-100-2; 3-way Yin-Yang); no conv/transformer-scale eval.
  • Diagonal-Fisher barrier is heavy-tailed; the importance clamp is load-bearing (without it both collapse to 0.494 chance).
  • Ground-state (infinite-horizon) h-transform rather than a finite-horizon survival function; finite-force cap removes σ²-amplification on 8.8% of steps.
  • On-silicon is single-seed, single-operating-point, trading plasticity for retention; energy is modelled, not measured.
  • Noise distribution tested only for additivity and a single trial-to-trial independence check — no full autocorrelation spectrum.
Open Questions
  1. Does the inverted-U survive at conv/transformer scale on harder benchmarks (Split-CIFAR-100, ContinualWorld), or is it an artifact of the small MLP regime?
  2. Can the heavy-tailed Fisher fragility (the load-bearing importance clamp) be removed by a Fisher-smoothed or functional-regularization barrier?
  3. A multi-seed on-silicon noise sweep with measured joules — does the device-reachable band reproduce the simulation optimum?
  4. How does the rule interact with stronger modern rehearsal-free baselines (parameter-space functional regularization, DER-style logit replay)?
  5. Does the finite-horizon survival-function h-transform change the optimum or the shape on long task streams?
  6. Does colored (AR(1)) device noise beyond the scanned ρ band break the mechanism?
