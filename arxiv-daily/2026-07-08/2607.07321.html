01 / Method A four-module lifecycle, framed as symbolic ML training

EvoSOP wraps a standard ReAct/DFSDT agent whose only tools are granular atomic actions. Four LLM-based modules iterate over execution trajectories, treating toolset optimization as a non-parametric analogue of the ML training loop.

1CONSTRUCTOR
Extracts co-occurring atomic-action subsequences (2–5 calls) from trajectories, rewrites them as callable SOP code with conditionals & error handling.
2MERGER
Consolidates functionally overlapping SOPs. Non-destructive: constituents are kept until empirically validated.
3EVALUATOR
Loads candidate SOP schemas into F′ = F_atomic ∪ schemas(S) and re-executes all training tasks in the real environment.
4REVIEWER
LLM-critic tags each invocation into 5 states (Optimal / Partial / Neutral / Negative / Defect) and prunes. Most impactful module.

10 epochs · 5 trajectories / batch (25 total) · checkpoint each iteration · final = peak training-success epoch

data acquisition + forward pass → execute training tasks
symbolic backprop → extract SOPs from trajectories
regularization → merge & prune to avoid task-specific overfit
learning-rate decay → toolset stabilizes at equilibrium

03 / Assessment Strengths & limitations

+Strengths

  • Closed-loop by design. Principled four-stage lifecycle; non-destructive merging (keep constituents until empirically validated) is a thoughtful engineering call.
  • Parameter-free & model-agnostic. Directly applicable to black-box LLMs like GPT-4o — practically valuable.
  • Interpretable pruning. The REVIEWER's five-state taxonomy with natural-language justifications makes each cut traceable.
  • Compact toolset. Stays under ~10 SOPs, avoiding the context-bloat failure mode that motivates the paper.

−Limitations

  • Narrow benchmarks. Only ACEBench + Tau2Bench; no web/code/long-horizon agents (GAIA, SWE-bench) where SOP abstraction should matter most.
  • Inflated headline. "+24 pp over ASI" is partly ASI underperforming the base; honest base gains are +2.5–13.4 pp.
  • Overfit-prone selection. Final toolset = peak training success over just 25 trajectories, no validation set.
  • Unmeasured cost & critic bias. API cost of the 10-epoch × 3-backbone loop is unreported; the LLM-critic's own bias is neither measured nor mitigated.

04 / Open questions Where this could break — or go further

  1. Does the < 10-SOP equilibrium hold on truly long-horizon benchmarks (GAIA, SWE-bench, WebArena) where SOP abstraction should matter most?
  2. How much of the gain is new SOPs vs reorganization of the existing toolset? Disabling CONSTRUCTOR after epoch 5 would isolate this.
  3. Could a biased or miscalibrated REVIEWER silently disable useful skills (the "Blind Curator" phenomenon)? Would an ensemble critic mitigate it?
  4. Is selecting the final checkpoint by peak training success rate overfitting? What does a held-out validation curve look like?
  5. What is the total API cost (tokens, dollars, latency) of the loop, and how does amortized cost-per-task compare to ASI after many queries?
  6. Can the "symbolic backpropagation" analogy be made formal — a convergence or regret bound — or does it stay purely metaphorical?
