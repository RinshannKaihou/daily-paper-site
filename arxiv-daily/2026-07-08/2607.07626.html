Key Finding/Temporal Confidence & Distillation

A pre-solution hidden state can be distilled to predict post-solution confidence — recovering up to 66% of the calibration gain at single-pass cost.

Future Confidence Distillation in Large Language Models
Sahil Kale (UCLA) — single author, AAAI 2027 submission

Why This Matters

Lets a model anticipate answer reliability before generating it — cheap enough for RAG triggering, tool calls, and adaptive computation at deployment.

Critical Edge

Distillation is demonstrated only on 8B–32B open models — frontier (GPT-5.4, Claude) results are verbal-only, and absolute calibration stays poor (Math ECE ≈ 0.27).

Open-model claim only

Methodology — borrowing FOK/JOL from meta-memory psychology

Reframe confidence as a temporal signal, then distill the future into the present

Borrowing Nelson (1990) and Koriat (1997), the paper splits LLM self-evaluation into a pre-solution Feeling-of-Knowing (FOK, "will I solve this?") and a post-solution Judgement-of-Learning (JOL, "was my answer right?"). On nine benchmarks across factual, logic, and math domains, post-solution confidence is consistently better calibrated than pre-solution — but it costs two forward passes.

Stage 1 · Pre
FOK prompt
Without solving, model outputs "Confidence: X%". Hidden state h_pre is the asset.
→
Stage 2 · Solve
Greedy answer
ŷ = f(x) at T=0; correctness z via exact match. 1800 examples/domain, 65% class cap.
→
Stage 3 · Post
JOL teacher
Post-solution probe T on h_post gives teacher t_i; ridge student D regresses to it from h_pre only.

At deployment the teacher is offline-only; D consumes just the pre-solution representation, so inference cost equals a single forward pass. Probes are logistic regression on 128-dim PCA-projected final-token hidden states (intermediate layers ≈16–24 decode confidence best; the final layer degrades). The teacher and student share architecture — linear wins everywhere; MLPs overfit at these supervision budgets.

Supporting Evidence — what probes reveal

Hidden states carry more than the model says

Pre vs Post
Post-solution verbal confidence beats pre on every model: open-model Math ECE 0.33→0.28, AUROC 0.59→0.62; Claude Math 0.50→0.79.
Probes > verbal
Best-layer linear probes improve ECE up to −0.20, AUROC up to +0.15 over verbal confidence; confidence most decodable at layers 16–24.
Sample efficiency
25 labelled examples already beat same-budget supervised probes; gains plateau at ~300–400.
Cross-dataset
Transfers within-domain (e.g. TriviaQA→NQ) without target fine-tuning; cross-domain fails (explicit negative result).
Cost
Deployment is ~1× vs 2.3–2.5× for full post-solution methods.

Strengths

  • Clean temporal framing connecting LLM confidence to decades of meta-memory psychology
  • Simple, well-defined, directly useful distillation — 1× cost with a meaningful calibration chunk
  • Genuinely sample-efficient (25 examples) and transfers within-domain
  • Exceptionally honest about the cross-domain negative result
  • Strong hygiene — stratified splits, 3 seeds, CIs, validation-based layer selection, real architecture ablation

Limitations

  • Absolute calibration remains poor (Math ECE ≈ 0.27 even after distillation)
  • Frontier models are verbal-only — distillation shown solely on 8B–32B open models
  • Math is the weakest setting — yet arguably where reliable confidence matters most
  • Text-only, single-turn, three domains — no multimodal, agentic, or long-horizon
  • Teacher quality not isolated as an error source; linear-only by design
  • No code/release statement in the main text

Open Questions — for further exploration

Would a non-linear teacher probe with a linear student lift the recovery ceiling on Math, where 31.7% AUROC recovery is currently weak?
Can future confidence distillation be made self-supervised — using the model's own JOL as teacher instead of a label-trained probe — removing the ground-truth-label dependence?
Do the FOK→JOL gap and recovery fraction scale with model size or reasoning-chain length on o1-style reasoning models, where post-solution signal should be much richer?
Does the cross-domain failure imply confidence representations are domain-specific — or is there a shared meta-cognitive subspace a multi-task objective could expose?
How does distillation interact with selective prediction / abstention policies — what's the downstream risk-coverage improvement, not just ECE/AUROC?
