Key Finding/One-Pass Familiarity Gate

A single-forward-pass activation-dispersion statistic separates known from fabricated entities at AUROC 0.95–1.00 — yet this familiarity signal decouples entirely from factual reliability.

Does Bielik Know What It Doesn't Know? Activation Dispersion Separates Entity Familiarity from Factual Reliability Across Model Scale
Grzegorz Brzezinka (Prosit AS) — single-author preprint
Bielik v3.0 (1.5B / 4.5B / 7B-Minitron / 11B)  ·  2026-07-08  ·  first internal-state study on a Polish-native LLM
Key Metric
0.95–1.00
AUROC for separating known vs fabricated entities using label-free, single-pass dispersion (IPR + spectral entropy) on post-SwiGLU activations.
4 entity domains × 4 model scales  ·  beats 5-sample semantic entropy (0.71–0.83)  ·  clears selection-aware permutation floor ~0.70
Why This Matters

A near-free prefill-time gate that flags unfamiliar entities in non-English LLMs — while proving familiarity and factual reliability scale on two different curves.

Critical Edge

The behavioral scaling curve (0→2→10→19 correct) rests on a single Claude judge family at every evaluation rung — one Polish-sports blind spot contaminates all of it.

Single judge, one domain

Methodology — one forward pass, two dispersion scalars

Read the post-SwiGLU activation at the prompt point; delocalization means "I don't know"

For each of 504 prompts (42 entities × 3 conditions × 4 domains), a single forward pass records the post-SwiGLU MLP activation vector a at the prompt point. Two closed-form scalars are computed: IPR = Σpᵢ² and spectral entropy S = −Σpᵢ log pᵢ over normalized weights pᵢ = aᵢ²/Σaⱼ². A localized, peaked activation (familiar entity) gives high IPR / low S; a delocalized one (unknown/fabricated) gives the reverse.

Activations are winsorized at q=0.99 to neutralize massive-activation outliers. A three-condition design (known / unknown-real / fabricated) supplies two control contrasts: known vs unknown-real on real names only removes the fabricated-string confound (still AUROC 0.97–1.00), while unknown-real vs fabricated (both unfamiliar) upper-bounds the lexical residue at 0.70–0.82 — too weak to explain the 0.95+ headline. Statistical hygiene is unusually careful: a selection-aware permutation null (re-selecting best layer/metric per permutation, floor ≈0.70–0.74), 10k-resample bootstrap CIs, and held-out layer selection (0.93–0.99) replace naive layer-maxima. Behavioral labels come from two LLM-judge regimes (strict / soft) over 5 samples each on the athletes domain only.

Supporting Evidence — what the gate does and doesn't read

Honest negatives bound the claim

Refusal rate
Only 2 refusals + 1 hedge across 2,520 athlete answers (99.88% direct assertions); both refusals from the 11B model — an alignment gap, not a capability gap.
Gemma-4 control
Refuses condition-selectively under identical prompts — proves near-zero refusal is a Bielik tuning property, not inevitable.
Within-known
Correct vs hallucination is much harder (best probe 0.929, only suggestive); dispersion no better than first-token entropy there.
Semantic entropy
5-sample SE loses K-vs-F (0.71–0.83, CIs don't overlap) but wins correctness (≤0.87) — the two consume prompt-side vs generation-side signal.
Transfer
People-to-people probe transfer 0.985–0.998; the cities transfer drop is template-caused (matched-stem recovers to 0.999–1.000).

Strengths

  • Exceptional statistical hygiene — selection-aware permutation floors, bootstrap CIs, held-out layer selection at every headline
  • Three-condition dataset causally separates familiarity from string/lexical artifacts
  • Informative negative result on the behavioral axis, read honestly rather than buried
  • Practical recipe — free prefill-time gating for Polish deployments, with honest caveats
  • Code, data, 57 tests, frozen artifacts shipped; activation side exactly re-derivable

Limitations

  • Tiny n (84 per contrast, 42 per class); differences <~0.05 AUROC are noise
  • Behavioral/SE/refusal claims are athletes-only, then rhetorically generalized
  • Single judge family (Claude) across every rung — no non-Claude cross-check
  • Winsorization q=0.99 is the most favorable of four settings, swept only on 1.5B
  • One Polish family; Gemma-4 control is only two checkpoints of one extra family
  • Refusal counts are LLM-classified, not human-annotated; prompt-variant ablation not run

Open Questions — for further exploration

Does the dispersion signal survive on a graded-popularity axis rather than the famous-vs-fabricated contrast every model aces — and does familiarity itself then scale?
Can a non-Claude judge (or human annotations) corroborate the 0→2→10→19 strict-correctness curve, given the entire behavioral axis rests on one family?
How does near-zero refusal change under prompt-variant ablations on Bielik itself — no one-sentence instruction; explicit "say so if unsure"?
Would combining one-pass dispersion (prompt-side familiarity) with multi-sample semantic entropy (generation-side consistency) in a single routing/gating policy measurably cut downstream hallucinations?
Does per-head diffuseness (only 1–2 shared heads across domains) coexist with the concentrated causal circuits of Ferrando 2025 / Wu 2025 when the same model is analyzed with causal-mediation tools?
