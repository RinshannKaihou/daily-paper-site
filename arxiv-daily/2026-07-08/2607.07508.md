> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 SAO（Single-rollout Asynchronous Optimization），通过单 rollout 采样替代 GRPO 的组采样、配合直接双/token 级重要性采样（DIS）与冻结注意力的加速价值模型训练，让异步 RL 在长程 agent 任务上稳定训练上千步，并在 AIME2025、BeyondAIME、SWE-Bench Verified 等基准上显著超过 GRPO 变体；该方法已用于训练开源 GLM-5.2（750B-A40B）。

> **TL;DR:** SAO replaces GRPO's group-wise sampling with one-rollout-per-prompt updates for asynchronous LLM RL, stabilized by direct double-sided token-level importance sampling (DIS) and a faster-updating, frozen-attention value model. On a Qwen3-30B-A3B backbone it trains stably for ~1000 steps and beats GRPO across AIME2025 (97.3 vs 84.2), BeyondAIME (74.8 vs 54.8), HMMT Nov 2025 (88.3 vs 76.0), IMOAnswerBench (74.0 vs 55.8), and SWE-Bench Verified (29.8 vs 27.0); it is deployed in the open GLM-5.2 (750B-A40B) agentic RL pipeline.

## 核心贡献

- 提出单 rollout 异步优化框架 SAO：放弃 GRPO 的组级采样，改为每个 prompt 仅采一条轨迹并即时送训，消除"等最慢样本"造成的延迟型离策问题，天然契合只给单条反馈的在线/agent 环境。
- 设计直接双/token 级重要性采样（DIS）：直接用 rollout 引擎的 log-prob 作为行为策略，丢弃不可精确追踪的 π_old，并对落在信任区间 [1-εℓ, 1+εh] 之外的 token 直接掩码，避免异步带来的极端离策发散。
- 改进价值模型训练以支撑单 rollout：相比策略更新更快地更新 critic（K=2）、对 value 模型采用"冻结注意力、只训 MoE"以抑制全注意力层剧烈梯度、并大规模扩展 value 预训练数据缓解冷启动。
- 推导 Skip-Observation token-level GAE：针对 agent 轨迹 [a0,o0,a1,o1,...] 把优势估计跨过环境观测 token、只在动作到动作边界上计算，过滤环境随机性带来的噪声。
- 在 Qwen3-30B-A3B 上验证 SAO 在 AIME2025 / BeyondAIME / HMMT / IMOAnswerBench / SWE-Bench Verified 全面优于 GRPO 及其变体，并在模拟在线写作风格切换任务上展示快速再对齐能力；方法已部署于 GLM-5.2（750B-A40B）训练。

## Key Contributions

- A single-rollout asynchronous RL framework (SAO) that drops group-wise sampling in favor of one rollout per prompt, eliminating latency-driven off-policy from waiting on the slowest group member and fitting online/agentic environments that yield only single-trajectory feedback.
- A Direct double-sided token-level Importance Sampling (DIS) scheme: use the rollout engine's log-probs directly as the behavior policy (dropping the intractable π_old), and hard-mask any token whose importance ratio falls outside [1-εℓ, 1+εh] instead of standard PPO clipping.
- Practical value-model designs that make single-rollout viable: update the critic faster than the policy (K=2), freeze attention and optimize only MoE layers to tame the much larger critic gradient norms, and scale value-pretraining data to overcome the value "cold-start".
- A Skip-Observation token-level GAE that bridges advantage estimation across action-to-action boundaries while skipping environment observation tokens, avoiding noise from value predictions over externally-generated tokens.
- Empirical validation that SAO stably trains for ~1000 steps and beats GRPO and its DIS-augmented variant across five reasoning/coding benchmarks, plus a simulated online-learning study showing fast policy realignment after reward-preference shifts; SAO is deployed in the open GLM-5.2 (750B-A40B) agentic RL pipeline.

## Methodology

SAO targets two failure modes of asynchronous LLM RL: severe policy lag (the rollout engine may be updated several times during a single long trajectory, so tracking an exact π_old is infeasible) and the structural mismatch of group-wise methods like GRPO under asynchrony (a group must wait for its slowest member, inducing latency-driven off-policy, and many agentic/online environments provide only one trajectory per prompt). It addresses them with four components.

**1. Direct double-sided Importance Sampling (DIS).** SAO keeps three policies conceptually — current π_θ, old π_θ_old, rollout π_rollout — but drops π_θ_old entirely. The per-token ratio is computed directly from rollout logs: r_t(θ) = exp(log π_θ(a_t|s_t) − log π_rollout(a_t|s_t)). Instead of PPO's asymmetric clipping of only "selected" tokens, DIS enforces a strict two-sided trust region [1-εℓ, 1+εh]; tokens outside this band are masked from the gradient (weight 0), those inside keep their full ratio. This is more aggressive than IcePop-style clipping but removes the separate old-policy inference pass. Math reasoning uses εℓ=0.3, εh=5.0; coding uses εℓ=0.8, εh=3.0.

**2. Single-rollout sampling replacing group-wise sampling.** Each prompt yields exactly one rollout that enters training immediately upon completion (Figure 2), removing the GRPO synchronization barrier. Because single-rollout degenerates toward high-variance REINFORCE-style estimates, SAO leans on a strong value model.

**3. Value-model training to reduce that variance.** (a) *Faster Value Update*: K=2 critic updates per policy gradient step, so V_ϕ adapts to the current policy before advantage computation. (b) *Frozen-Attention*: pilot experiments showed critic gradient norms were dominated by the Full-Attention layers while MoE layers stayed stable, so attention weights are frozen and only MoE projections are optimized. (c) *Scaled value pretraining* to fix a value cold-start bottleneck.

**4. Skip-Observation token-level GAE for agentic traces.** For trajectories T = [a0, o0, a1, o1, …] the Bellman target is modified to bridge the observation gap: Â(a_{i,N}) = δ + γλ Â(a_{i+1,0}), with δ = r_t + γV(a_{i+1,0}) − V(a_{i,N}). Advantage is computed only across action-to-action boundaries, ignoring observation tokens that the model did not generate. The authors report that step-level value/GAE alternatives (Appendix A.1) underperform this token-level design.

Training uses a Qwen3-30B-A3B-Thinking-2507 policy initialized from 3-epoch TIR SFT with GPT-OSS-120B蒸馏 data; batch size 128, group size 1, max length 128k, policy LR 1e-6, critic LR 5e-6 with 10-step warmup, length-adaptive GAE (λ_policy = 1 − 1/(α·length), α=1.5). GRPO baselines use 16 prompts × 8 rollouts to match the 128 batch.

## Results & Highlights

- **AIME2025:** SAO reaches 97.3% Pass@1 versus 84.2% for GRPO and 80.4% for the SFT baseline (Qwen3-30B-A3B, reasoning-with-Python) — a +13.1 point absolute gain over GRPO and within ~0.4 pt of GLM-4.7 (95.7% w/o python is a different setting; the directly comparable python-enabled GLM-4.7 line is absent).
- **BeyondAIME:** SAO 74.8% vs GRPO 54.8% (+20.0 pts) vs SFT 53.3%; approaching GPT-5 High at 74.0%.
- **HMMT Nov 2025:** SAO 88.3% vs GRPO 76.0% (+12.3 pts) vs SFT 75.2%.
- **IMOAnswerBench:** SAO 74.0% vs GRPO 55.8% (+18.2 pts) vs SFT 53.3%; closing most of the gap to GPT-5 High (76.0%).
- **SWE-Bench Verified (agentic coding, OpenHands scaffold, ≤300 turns, 128k ctx):** SAO 29.8% vs GRPO+w/DIS 27.0% (+2.8 pts) vs the Qwen3-30B-A3B baseline 23.0% (+6.8 pts over baseline).
- **Stability:** vanilla GRPO collapses at ~160 steps; vanilla VAPO collapses at ~90 steps; SAO and GRPO+w/DIS remain stable to ~1000 steps, with SAO pulling clearly ahead after ~400 steps (Figure 3).
- **Ablations (Table 4, AIME2025 / BeyondAIME):** removing Faster Value Update drops to 95.0 / 69.8; removing Frozen-Attention drops to 90.6 / 74.5; replacing the value model with a Running-Mean baseline drops to 79.8 / 55.3; vanilla VAPO (w/o DIS) collapses similarly to GRPO.
- **Training dynamics (Figure 4):** SAO's Explained Variance overtakes the single-critic-update baseline after ~400 steps; frozen-attention keeps critic gradient norms low and smooth versus the large spikes of full-parameter training; DIS sustains a non-trivial token clip ratio whereas vanilla VAPO's near-zero clip ratio fails to gate divergent updates.
- **Simulated online learning (Figure 5):** under sequential preference shifts (cute → chuunibyou → classical writing styles), SAO rapidly suppresses the previously dominant style and realigns, while a Running-Mean advantage baseline shows pronounced adaptation lag and lower stable reward due to window inertia.

## Authors & Credibility

Authored by Zhenyu Hou and Yujiang Li (equal contribution, work done while interning at Z.AI) with Jie Tang and Yuxiao Dong of Tsinghua University — the GLM/Knowledge Engineering group that maintains one of the major open LLM families. The method is not a toy demo: it is explicitly stated to be deployed in the agentic RL pipeline for training the open GLM-5.2 (750B-A40B) model, and the submission is marked "Under review, Feb 2026," consistent with a serious venue submission. The backbone (Qwen3-30B-A3B) is a recent strong open MoE, and comparisons include frontier closed models (Claude-Sonnet-4.5, GPT-5 High, GLM-4.7) as reference points.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely above incremental. The combination is the contribution: single-rollout-per-prompt is structurally incompatible with GRPO and most prior async-RL work targets throughput rather than effectiveness (the authors explicitly cite AReaL, ROLL Flash, Noukhovitch et al. as systems/efficiency-focused). DIS's "drop π_old and hard-mask outside the band" is a simplification of IcePop rather than a brand-new idea, and faster-critic / frozen-attention are individually known tricks, but their integration into a stable single-rollout async recipe with the Skip-Observation GAE is non-obvious and well-motivated.
- **Rigor:** Reasonable. Ablations isolate Faster Value Update, Frozen-Attention, value-vs-running-mean, and step-vs-token granularity (Appendix A.1); training-dynamics plots (Explained Variance, critic grad norm, clip ratio) support each design claim mechanistically. Evaluation uses 16 runs averaged for AIME/HMMT/IMO and 4 for BeyondAIME to control variance, with clearly stated sampling parameters.
- **Reproducibility:** Mixed. Hyperparameters, scaffolds (OpenHands, ≤300 turns, 128k), and TIR蒸馏 source (GPT-OSS-120B) are given. However, the value-pretraining corpus whose scaling is called "essential" is not quantified or released; the "Skip-Observation GAE" derivation is sketched but implementation details (how observation token positions are identified in interleaved traces) are thin; and the only backbone is Qwen3-30B-A3B, so generalization is unproven. The GLM-5.2 deployment is asserted without per-component results isolating SAO's contribution at 750B scale.
- **Significance:** High if the claims hold — single-rollout is the natural setting for real online/agentic RL where group rewards are unavailable, and stable async training at 1000 steps is a practical blocker the paper directly addresses.
- **Red flags / overclaim risks:** (1) The headline AIME2025 number 97.3% is "w/ python" tool-integrated reasoning; the same model "w/o python" is only 14.6%, so the gains are heavily scaffold-dependent and not pure policy improvement — this is disclosed but easy to misread in a leaderboard context. (2) Comparisons to Claude-Sonnet-4.5 / GPT-5 High are apples-to-oranges (different model sizes, and the paper's GLM-4.7 line omits BeyondAIME and the python setting), yet they appear in the same table. (3) Baselines are essentially GRPO-family on a single 30B backbone; there is no comparison to other single-rollout methods like SPO beyond a brief mention that they "rely on prior information about training-data difficulty," and no head-to-head with AReaL/ROLL-style async systems on throughput. (4) The SWE-Bench gain over GRPO+w/DIS is modest (+2.8 pts) and within plausible run-to-run noise for a single benchmark, though the reasoning gains are large enough to be convincing. (5) No human evaluation or qualitative analysis of the simulated online-writing task — adaptability is judged by an LLM judge (GLM-4.7) reward that is itself part of the authors' own stack.

## Strengths

- Directly attacks the right problem: GRPO's group barrier is a real and increasingly cited pain point for long-horizon agentic RL, and single-rollout is the correct structural answer for genuinely online environments.
- Each design choice is backed by a targeted diagnostic (gradient-norm decomposition motivating frozen attention, Explained Variance motivating faster critic, clip-ratio curve motivating DIS, step-vs-token ablation motivating token-level).
- Strong, broad empirical wins on four math benchmarks plus a coding agent benchmark, with the method actually shipped in a 750B-scale production model.
- Honest about the running-mean baseline being "decent but worse" rather than strawmanning it, and the online-learning experiment is a genuinely novel evaluation angle.

## Limitations

- Single backbone (Qwen3-30B-A3B); authors acknowledge conclusions may not transfer to smaller models, non-agentic RLHF, or dense-reward/short-rollout regimes.
- Depends on infrastructure that reliably preserves token-level rollout log-probs during async generation — non-trivial to reproduce without a capable decoupled rollout engine.
- The value-pretraining corpus central to the "cold start" fix is unspecified in scale or composition.
- Simulated online learning uses an LLM-judge (GLM-4.7) reward and a controlled style-shift; real user-facing online adaptation would need safety, monitoring, and privacy review (acknowledged in the Broader Impact section).
- No comparison against contemporary single-rollout alternatives (SPO is mentioned, not benchmarked head-to-head) or against async-RL systems on wall-clock/throughput.

## Relevance

对关注 LLM 后训练、强化学习与 agent 系统的研究者高度相关：SAO 提供了一套可直接用于长程 agent 与在线学习场景的异步 RL 稳定训练方案，且已在 GLM-5.2 这样的大规模生产模型上落地，对 RL 算法、value model 训练与 agent 训练 infra 都有借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does SAO scale and behave on dense-reward, short-rollout RLHF preference tasks versus GRPO/DPO, where group sampling is cheaper and the off-policy penalty is smaller?
- What is the wall-clock and GPU-utilization comparison against AReaL / ROLL Flash at matched throughput, given that SAO's stated motivation is asynchrony efficiency but the paper reports accuracy, not training time?
- How sensitive are εℓ/εh and the K=2 critic-update ratio to backbone size and MoE architecture — would a dense model or a 7B policy show the same frozen-attention benefit?
- Can the Skip-Observation GAE be extended to settings with stochastic/variable-length environment feedback (e.g., tool errors, web navigation) where the action-to-action boundary itself is uncertain?
- What is the actual size and composition of the scaled value-pretraining corpus, and how much of the gain comes from value cold-start fix versus the online RL algorithm itself?
- Does the online-learning advantage over Running-Mean persist when reward shifts are frequent or partial rather than discrete phase changes, and how does SAO behave under adversarial/non-stationary rewards?
