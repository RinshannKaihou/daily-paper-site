> **一句话总结：** 作者在四个波兰语 Bielik 模型（1.5B–11B）上证明，仅需一次前向传播、对 post-SwiGLU MLP 激活计算闭式分散度统计量（逆参与比 IPR 与谱熵），即可在 0.95–1.00 AUROC 下区分"已知"与"虚构"实体；而这种表征层面的"熟悉度"信号在 1.5B 即已饱和，与随规模急剧上升的事实可靠性（正确率从 0/42 涨到 19/42）完全脱钩——模型几乎从不开口拒绝（2520 条回答仅 2 次拒绝）。

> **TL;DR:** On four Polish Bielik v3.0 models (1.5B–11B), two label-free, single-forward-pass dispersion statistics over post-SwiGLU MLP activations (inverse participation ratio and spectral entropy) separate known from fabricated entities at AUROC 0.95–1.00 across athletes/cities/writers/musicians, beating a 5-sample semantic-entropy baseline (0.71–0.83) and clearing a selection-aware permutation floor of ~0.70–0.74. Crucially this representational familiarity signal is already at ceiling at 1.5B, while behavioral factual reliability about those same known athletes scales steeply (0→2→10→19 of 42 correct under a strict judge from 1.5B to 11B), and within-known correctness vs. hallucination is much harder (best probe 0.929); despite near-perfect internal "I don't know" signal, the models decline in only 2 of 2,520 answers.

## 核心贡献

- **首次在波兰语模型族上系统测试闭式、逐神经元激活分散度作为"知识存在"检测器**：在 prompt 点（最后一个 token）单次前向传播计算 IPR 与谱熵，无需标签、无需采样、无需辅助模型，即可在四个 Bielik v3.0 模型（1.5B/4.5B/7B-Minitron/11B）上以 AUROC 0.94–0.98 区分已知与虚构运动员，监督线性探针达 0.99–1.00；并通过选择感知的置换零假设（floor ≈0.70–0.74，empirical p ≤ 10⁻³）与留出层选择（0.93–0.99）严格校准了"层最大化"带来的乐观偏差。
- **跨四种实体类型的泛化**：同一单次检测器在运动员、城市、作家、音乐家四个领域均有效（最佳分散度 0.95–1.00，探针 0.99–1.00）；探针跨域迁移平均非对角 AUROC 为 0.987/0.959/0.919/0.931（1.5B/4.5B/7B/11B），唯一大的迁移下降发生在使用不同 prompt 模板的城市域——匹配模板的反事实实验证明这是模板导致而非实体类型导致，迁移入城市恢复到 0.999–1.000。
- **揭示两条脱钩的扩展曲线**：表征熟悉度信号在该对比上 1.5B 即饱和，而行为事实可靠性随规模急剧上升（strict 5/5 正确数 0→2→10→19；soft 5/5 为 6→16→24→33）。这是同一数据集、同一实体上的干净双轴测量，与基于频率的事实召回预测一致。
- **诚实报告行为轴上的"基本负面"结果**：在已知实体内部区分"正确 vs 幻觉"远比"已知 vs 虚构"困难，整个 model×threshold 网格不一致——探针偶尔低于自身置换 floor，分散度有时优于探针，最强单元格（11B 探针 0.929）仅是"提示性"而非确立；分散度在此对比上不优于首 token 熵基线。作者明确区分：分散度检测的是"实体熟悉度"而非"事实可靠性"。
- **与语义熵的互补性分析**：5 样本离散语义熵在已知 vs 虚构上仅 0.71–0.83（CI 与分散度不重叠）且成本为 5 倍推理，但在正确性对比上可达 0.87（分散度失败处）；两者消费不同信息（prompt 端熟悉度 vs 生成端一致性）。
- **量化近乎零拒绝的失调状态**：对全部 2,520 条运动员回答做 LLM 审计，仅 2 次拒绝、1 次模糊回答（99.88% 直接断言），且两次拒绝都来自最大的 11B 模型——尽管每个规模都携带着近乎完美可解码的"我不认识此实体"内部信号。Gemma-4 控制实验证明这是 Bielik 的对齐问题而非规模或表征的必然。

## Key Contributions

- First systematic test of closed-form, per-neuron activation dispersion (IPR and spectral entropy over post-SwiGLU MLP activations, winsorized at q=0.99) read at the prompt point before any answer token, as a label-free knowledge-presence detector — on a Polish model family (Bielik v3.0 1.5B/4.5B/7B-Minitron/11B), the first internal-state hallucination study on a Polish-native LLM. Headline athletes AUROC is 0.946–0.984 (dispersion) and 0.99–1.00 (linear probe), all clearing a selection-aware permutation null (95th pct ≈0.70–0.74; p ≤ 10⁻³) and held-out layer selection (0.928–0.970).
- A three-condition dataset (known / unknown-real / fabricated, 42 each × 4 domains = 504 prompts per model) that supports control contrasts isolating familiarity from lexical artifacts: known vs. unknown-real reaches 0.971–1.000 (probe 0.998–1.000) on real names only, while unknown-real vs. fabricated (both unfamiliar) sits at 0.699–0.824, upper-bounding the lexical/plausibility residue that cannot account for the 0.94–0.98 headline.
- Generalization across four entity types (athletes, cities, writers, musicians) with near-perfect people-to-people probe transfer (0.985–0.998); a matched-template counterfactual (shared neutral stem "Co wiesz o {entity}?") causally shows the cities transfer drop is template-caused — writers→cities recovers to 0.999–1.000 at every scale, leaving only a residual cities-as-source asymmetry (0.82–0.98) attributed to a tighter "places manifold."
- A clean dissociation of two scaling curves on one dataset: representational familiarity is at ceiling already at 1.5B (dispersion 0.94+, probe ≈0.99), while behavioral factual reliability on the same known athletes rises 0→2→10→19 (strict 5/5) and 6→16→24→33 (soft 5/5) from 1.5B to 11B — refining the motivating localization intuition so that delocalized activation marks missing entities, not missing facts.
- An honest mostly-negative behavioral-axis result: within-known correctness-vs-hallucination is far harder than known-vs-fabricated, with the full model×threshold grid inconsistent (probe sometimes below its own permutation floor; 11B probe 0.929 only suggestive; dispersion best layer often embedding-adjacent layers 0–1, a noise flag).
- A complementarity analysis: 5-sample discrete semantic entropy reaches 0.71–0.83 on K-vs-F (non-overlapping CIs vs. dispersion) but wins the correctness contrast (up to 0.87) — the two consume prompt-side familiarity vs. generation-side consistency respectively, suggesting a deployment stack may want both.
- A near-zero-refusal regime quantified by an answer-level LLM audit: across 2,520 sampled athlete answers, exactly 2 refusals and 1 hedge (99.88% direct assertions), both refusals from the 11B model — an actionable alignment gap rather than a capability gap, bounded by a Gemma-4 control that abstains condition-selectively under the identical prompt.

## Methodology

**Models.** Four instruction-tuned Bielik v3.0 checkpoints (Mistral-like decoder with SwiGLU MLPs): 1.5B (32 layers, d=1536), 4.5B (60 layers, d=2048), 11B (50 layers, d=4096), and a Minitron-7B (40 layers, d=4096) obtained by structured pruning + knowledge distillation rather than dense training — explicitly flagged wherever it behaves differently.

**Dataset.** Four entity domains (athletes, cities, writers, musicians), each with three conditions of 42 entities: *known* (famous, e.g., Lewandowski, Warszawa, Mickiewicz, Chopin), *unknown-real* (obscure real entities), and *fabricated* (morphologically well-formed invented names, ~length-matched). Two Polish prompt templates differ only in the stem ("Kim jest {entity}?" for people vs. "Czym jest {entity}?" for cities); a third neutral stem is used for the template-causality control. The athletes domain uniquely carries behavioral labels, semantic-entropy scores, and the refusal audit; the other three domains carry condition-based detection labels only.

**Signal extraction.** A single forward pass per prompt records at each layer: (i) post-SwiGLU MLP gate activations, (ii) residual-stream hidden states, (iii) attention maps, (iv) logit-lens distributions. Read at two positions: the *entity point* (last token of entity name) and the *prompt point* (last token of prompt, the conservative choice for all headline numbers since lexical identity is no longer the input).

**Dispersion metrics.** Given post-SwiGLU activation vector a, define normalized weights pᵢ = aᵢ² / Σⱼaⱼ², then IPR = Σ pᵢ² (1/IPR = effective number of active neurons) and S = −Σ pᵢ log pᵢ (spectral/Shannon entropy). Localized activity ⇒ high IPR, low S; delocalized ⇒ reverse. Activations are winsorized at q=0.99 before computing p (to neutralize massive-activation outliers). Extended metric set: effective rank of hidden-state matrix (one-pass relative of EigenScore), attention entropy per head, logit-lens entropy, and first-token entropy as output-side baseline.

**Controls and evaluation.** Per-layer ℓ₂-regularized logistic regression probe (5-fold CV) gives supervised upper bound. Three selection-robustness tools: (1) permutation null with selection (10,000 permutations re-selecting best layer/metric, 95th pct as floor ≈0.70–0.74); (2) 10,000-resample bootstrap CIs at fixed best layer; (3) held-out layer selection (200 stratified half-splits). All discrimination results are AUROC reported as separability max(AUC, 1−AUC); primary contrast is known vs. fabricated with n=84 per contrast.

**Behavioral labeling (athletes).** 5 samples per entity (≤64 new tokens); two LLM-judge regimes — *strict* (bare one-line prompt, no rubric, truncation/parse-fail defaults to incorrect) and *soft* (key-facts rubric: identity+discipline correct, fabricated achievements still error). Refusal/hedge audited at answer level over all 2,520 athlete answers by an LLM classifier with human control.

**Semantic-entropy baseline.** Discrete SE over 5 samples: LLM clustering judge groups answers into meaning-equivalence classes (bidirectional entailment); entropy of cluster distribution is the score. Costs 5 forward passes + clustering vs. 1 pass + no auxiliary model for dispersion.

## Results & Highlights

- **Headline athletes known-vs-fabricated (prompt point, n=84):** unsupervised single-pass dispersion AUROC 0.946 [0.887,0.988] at 1.5B, 0.984 [0.952,1.000] at 4.5B, 0.957 at 7B-Minitron, 0.972 at 11B; supervised probe 0.991/1.000/0.990/0.997 — all clearing selection-aware permutation floors of 0.701–0.715 (dispersion) and 0.721–0.742 (probe) at empirical p ≤ 10⁻³, and surviving held-out layer selection at 0.928–0.970 (dispersion) and 0.977–0.993 (probe).
- **Fabricated-string-free control (known vs. unknown-real, real names only):** dispersion 0.989/0.994/1.000/0.971 across 1.5B/4.5B/7B/11B — as strong as or stronger than the headline — on names with natural subword statistics where the token-count lexical baseline is only 0.694 (small tokenizer) or near-chance 0.518 (large tokenizer), establishing the signal tracks familiarity rather than fabricated-string weirdness.
- **Cross-domain detection (Table 7):** within-domain dispersion/probe AUROC: athletes 0.946–0.984 / 0.99–1.00, cities 0.991–1.000 / 1.000, writers 0.946–0.978 / 0.99–1.00, musicians 0.951–0.984 / 0.997–1.00. Mean off-diagonal probe transfer: 0.987 (1.5B), 0.959 (4.5B), 0.919 (7B), 0.931 (11B); people-to-people transfer 0.985–0.998; matched-stem writers→cities transfer recovers to 0.999/1.000/1.000/1.000.
- **Behavioral scaling dissociation:** fully-correct known athletes under strict judge go 0→2→10→19 of 42 from 1.5B to 11B, and 6→16→24→33 under soft 5/5; meanwhile representational familiarity (best of dispersion pair) is flat and at ceiling across the same sweep — small models know which entities are familiar yet routinely decorate answers with fabricated details.
- **Semantic-entropy comparison:** 5-sample SE reaches K-vs-F AUROC 0.778/0.759/0.711/0.832 across the four models, with 95% CIs non-overlapping with dispersion at every scale (e.g., 11B: SE 0.832 [0.743,0.911] vs. IPR 0.972 [0.939,0.994]); SE instead wins the correctness contrast (up to 0.872 on 11B) where dispersion fails. Mean SE on known falls 0.91→0.59→0.67→0.26 with scale.
- **Near-zero refusal:** LLM audit of all 2,520 athlete answers finds 2 refusals and 1 hedged answer (99.88% direct assertions); both refusals come from the 11B model only, despite a near-perfectly decodable internal familiarity signal at every scale. Gemma-4-12B control under identical prompts refuses condition-selectively (24/42 fabricated, 28/42 unknown-real vs. 7/42 known all-five-sample refusals).
- **Per-head attention diffuseness:** only 1–2 attention heads sit in all four domains' top-20; mean pairwise cross-domain Spearman of per-head AUROC rises only modestly with scale (0.27→0.50→0.52→0.56 from 1.5B to 11B) — the signal is broadly distributed, not carried by a small fixed head set.

## Authors & Credibility

Single-author preprint by Grzegorz Brzezinka (Prosit AS), July 2026. The work is unusually methodologically careful for a single-author study: it pre-registers and reports selection-aware permutation nulls (re-selecting best layer/metric per permutation), bootstrap CIs, and held-out layer selection rather than relying on raw layer-maximum numbers; it ships full code/data (https://github.com/agentGreg/bielik-hallucination-detection), 57 unit tests, and ships the frozen answer artifacts since answer sampling was unseeded. It includes a non-Polish control family (Gemma-4 at two sizes) in Appendix A as a partial robustness check. The author is transparent about the absence of a second judge family and about the single-model-family scope. No peer review status indicated (preprint).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely novel in three concrete ways: (1) it is the first internal-state hallucination/familiarity study on a Polish-native LLM family (Mu-SHROOM spans 14 languages without Polish; MultiHaluDet covers French/Bangla/Amharic); (2) it tests closed-form per-neuron single-vector dispersion at the prompt point (IPR + spectral entropy) — a more minimalist statistic than token-span/covariance methods like EigenScore/INSIDE, LLM-Check, D²HScore, EigenTrack — as a detector of *knowledge presence* rather than answer-level hallucination; (3) the same-dataset, same-entities measurement of representational familiarity vs. behavioral reliability across scale is a clean and under-explored experimental design.
- **Rigor:** Strong on statistics. The selection-aware permutation null that re-selects the best layer per permutation is the right tool and is conspicuously absent from much prior probing work; the held-out layer analysis is its proper out-of-sample complement. The three-condition design (known/unknown-real/fabricated) supports two control contrasts that genuinely bound the lexical confound rather than hand-waving it. The honesty about the behavioral-axis grid (probe falling below its own floor; embedding-adjacent best layers as noise flags) is exemplary.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Single Apple-silicon workstation (MPS, bf16), one-command pipeline per model, 57 unit tests, code + data public. Explicit reproducibility asymmetry: the activation/dispersion side is deterministic and re-derivable; the unseeded answer sampling means behavioral counts/SE/refusal are properties of shipped artifacts. Gated HF checkpoints + Anthropic API key required.
- **Significance:** The dissociation result (familiarity at ceiling at 1.5B, reliability scaling steeply, near-zero refusal despite perfect internal signal) is a concrete, deployment-relevant finding for Polish LLMs and gives a coarse volumetric confirmation of mechanistic entity-recognition accounts (Ferrando et al. 2025; Lindsey et al. 2025). The one-pass gating recipe is practically useful.
- **Red flags / weaknesses (paper-specific):**
  - **Tiny sample sizes (n=84 per contrast, 42 per class).** Bootstrap CIs on headline cells span ±0.03–0.06; single-model AUROC differences below ~0.05 are uninterpretable. The whole "two scaling curves" claim rests on a famous-vs-fabricated contrast that every model aces — a graded-popularity axis could still show familiarity improving with scale, which the authors concede but the headline framing somewhat obscures.
  - **Behavioral/SE/refusal claims are athletes-only.** The dissociation and abstention-gap are demonstrated on one domain, then rhetorically generalized.
  - **Single judge family (Claude) occupies every rung of the evaluation ladder** — strict/soft judging (Opus 4.8), SE clustering (Sonnet 5), refusal audit (Haiku 4.5). A family-level blind spot about Polish sports biography would contaminate behavioral counts, SE correctness, and refusal counts simultaneously. The strict judge is a bare one-line prompt whose parse failures default to incorrect, so strict counts conflate confabulation with truncation/parsing artifacts.
  - **Winsorization researcher degree of freedom.** q=0.99 is reported as the most favorable of four swept settings, the sweep covered only the 1.5B model on the round-1 list, and per-layer values shift substantially with q (entropy at layer 22: 0.509 at q=0.95, 0.782 at q=0.999). The metric's headline value sits inside an un-swept hyperparameter at three of four scales.
  - **Underpowered behavioral cells:** 4 of 12 grid cells have minority class <8; the "mostly-negative" reading is appropriate but means no reliable behavioral signal was either demonstrated or ruled out.
  - **The 7B point is a pruned/distilled Minitron**, not a dense family member; it is included in the "scale sweep" but its depth profile is not directly comparable.
  - **Prompt pressure on refusal:** the one-sentence instruction plausibly discourages abstention; the Gemma-4 control bounds but does not eliminate this, and a prompt-variant ablation on Bielik itself was not run.

## Strengths

- Exceptional statistical hygiene: selection-aware permutation floors, bootstrap CIs, held-out layer selection, and explicit lexical baselines reported alongside every headline.
- A clean three-condition dataset design that lets the paper causally separate familiarity from lexical/string artifacts (known vs. unknown-real on real names only; unknown-real vs. fabricated to upper-bound the lexical residue).
- A genuinely informative negative result: the behavioral-axis grid is presented in full and read honestly, sharpening the central conceptual distinction (familiarity ≠ reliability) rather than buried.
- Strong practical takeaway (free prefill-time gating for Polish deployments) with appropriate caveats about what the gate does and does not read.
- A non-Polish control family (Gemma-4) that qualitatively replicates the familiarity signal and the dissociation, and shows the abstention gap is a Bielik tuning property — including the informative failure mode where the signal dilutes exactly in domains whose famous tier is most Polish-local.
- Code, data, tests, and frozen artifacts shipped; deterministic activation side is exactly re-derivable.

## Limitations

- Small n (84 per contrast; 42 per class on behavioral axis); differences <~0.05 AUROC are noise.
- Behavioral, semantic-entropy, and refusal claims are measured on the athletes domain only; the other three domains carry condition-based labels only.
- Single judge family (Claude) across all evaluation rungs — no non-Claude cross-check; strict judge conflates confabulation with truncation/parse artifacts.
- Winsorization quantile q=0.99 is the most favorable of four settings and was swept only on 1.5B/round-1; per-layer values are sensitive to q.
- Main study is one Polish model family; the Gemma-4 control is only two checkpoints of one additional family on one language — full multi-family, multi-language treatment (including English translation) is future work.
- Template confound for cities in the main transfer matrix (resolved causally by the matched-stem control, but the residual cities-as-source asymmetry 0.82–0.98 is characterized, not explained).
- Cities-as-source asymmetry and per-head diffuseness qualify any mechanistic reading: the global statistics are a coarse volumetric shadow of entity-recognition circuitry, not a localization.
- Refusal counts are answer-level LLM classifications, not human annotations; prompt-variant ablation on Bielik not run.
- Fabricated names screened but not exhaustively verified against all real persons/places; rare collisions possible (though labels robust to this failure mode).

## Relevance

这项工作直接命中"幻觉检测 / 模型自知识别 / 内部状态探针"主线，提供了一个极低成本（单次前向传播、无标签、无辅助模型）的实体熟悉度门控方法，并清晰区分了"熟悉度"与"事实可靠性"两条脱钩的扩展曲线——对构建会主动拒绝/检索增强的 LLM 系统以及对波兰语等非英语模型的机理分析都有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the dispersion-based familiarity signal survive on a *graded popularity* axis (continuous entity frequency) rather than the easy famous-vs-fabricated contrast that every model aces — and does familiarity itself then scale?
- Can a non-Claude judge family (or human annotations) corroborate the 0→2→10→19 strict correctness scaling curve, given that the entire behavioral axis rests on one judge family?
- How does the near-zero refusal rate change under prompt-variant ablations on Bielik itself (no one-sentence instruction; explicit "say so if unsure")?
- Would combining one-pass dispersion (prompt-side familiarity) with multi-sample semantic entropy (generation-side consistency) in a single routing/gating policy measurably improve downstream hallucination reduction over either alone?
- Is the residual cities-as-source transfer asymmetry (0.82–0.98 under matched templates, widening with scale) truly a "tighter places manifold" effect, and can a targeted geometry analysis (e.g., probing class-conditional covariance spectra) test this directly?
- Does the per-head diffuseness (only 1–2 heads shared across all four domains' top-20) coexist with the concentrated causal circuits of Ferrando et al. 2025 / Wu et al. 2025 when the same model is analyzed with causal-mediation rather than marginal-separability tools?
- How does the q-winsorization sensitivity generalize beyond 1.5B — is there a principled (data-adaptive) way to set q rather than picking the most favorable of four?
