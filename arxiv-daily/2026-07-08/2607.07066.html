Key Finding/Monoid Extension of GCR

Group-representation circuits extend beyond groups to non-invertible monoid multiplication via stratified Fourier routing over J-classes.

Why This Matters

Extends Fourier / representation-theoretic interpretability from invertible groups to zero-divisor monoids — a reusable template for how networks route and locally invert under information collapse.

Critical Edge

Claims are correlational only — no ablations or activation patching confirm the proposed circuits actually implement the computation.

No causal intervention

Methodology — reverse-engineering, not benchmarking

A two-phase circuit: route by J-class, then score by local character

A 1-layer decoder-only transformer (dmodel=128, 4 heads, ReLU) is trained on c = a·b mod n over Z₁₆₅ = 3×5×11 (square-free). Standard GCR's decoding rule tr(ρ(ab)ρ(c⁻¹)) breaks for zero-divisors (5 ∈ Z₁₆₅ has no inverse). The fix: partition into J-classes Jd={a : gcd(a,n)=d}; inside each regular class, recover a local identity eJd and local inverse c♯, re-expressing the logit as χρd(abc♯).

Strengths

  • Mathematically principled — real semigroup/representation theory (J-classes, regular classes, local inverses), proofs in appendix
  • Precisely isolates the failure mode of prior work (global invertibility) with a minimal fix
  • Coherent circuit story mapping hypothesis → architectural components, layer by layer
  • Falsifiable atomic-factorization prediction, visually demonstrated
  • Reproducible — released code, standard Nanda-et-al. architecture, multi-seed stability

Limitations

  • Correlational only — no causal interventions (ablation, patching) confirm the circuits
  • Square-free moduli only — breaks for ~39% of integers (nilpotents, non-regular classes)
  • Weakest fits in the easiest regime — J₁ 71.2%, J₃ 76.5%; residual unexplained
  • Toy scale — single 1-layer, 128-dim model; no larger-model evidence
  • Single seed for headline logit-FVE; only the PCA claim has multi-seed support
  • Incomplete MLP story — composition only visualized, not reconstructed

Open Questions — for further exploration

Would ablating Head-0 block-diagonal directions or per-class key-frequency subspaces causally destroy the corresponding J-class logits, as the routing hypothesis predicts?
Why does the local-character fit deteriorate for the largest classes — harmonic distortion, a secondary non-character mechanism, or a linear-fit artifact? Can a nonlinear extension close the gap?
How does the network handle non-square-free moduli (n=12, n=18) where nilpotents create non-regular J-classes with no local group — memorization, or a different stratification?
Do atomic-factorization frequency-reuse patterns emerge during grokking in compositional order (atoms first, composites later) — usable as a Nanda-style progress measure?
Does an analogous stratified mechanism appear in larger models doing genuinely non-invertible compositions (concatenation, set ops), or is it a 1-layer artifact?
