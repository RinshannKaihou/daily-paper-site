> **一句话总结：** 该论文提出 Riemannian Mean Pooling (RMP)，通过一个可微编码器（IGL）的解析 Jacobian 提取每个 token 的拉回度量（pullback metric），在 SPD 流形上用 Fréchet 均值聚合为句子级描述子并在切空间分类；在 CoLA / CREAK / RTE 三个有信号的数据集上一致超过欧氏均值池化基线（CREAK 上 AUC +0.060），而在为去除标注偏差而构造的负控数据集 FEVER-Symmetric 上正确地停留在随机水平，消融实验进一步将增益主要归因于几何聚合而非学习到的流形结构。

> **TL;DR:** The paper introduces Riemannian Mean Pooling (RMP), which extracts a per-token SPD pullback metric from the Jacobian of an Intrinsic Green's Learning (IGL) encoder and aggregates it on the SPD manifold via the Fréchet mean before tangent-space classification; on three signal-bearing benchmarks (CoLA, CREAK, RTE) RMP beats Euclidean mean pooling of identical BERT embeddings by up to +0.060 AUC on CREAK (0.708 vs 0.648 Linear Probe), while correctly staying at chance on the FEVER-Symmetric negative control (0.508 AUC, within the permutation-test 95% CI), and random-encoder ablations attribute most of the gain to the geometric aggregation itself rather than to learned manifold structure.

## 核心贡献

- **构建了一条几何聚合流水线**：从已训练编码器的解析 Jacobian 提取每个 token 的拉回度量（SPD 矩阵），在 SPD 流形上用 Fréchet 均值聚合成句子级描述子，再经 Riemannian 白化、投影到总体 Riemannian 均值处的切空间后用逻辑回归分类。整套流程把成熟 BCI（脑机接口）工具（Fréchet 均值、Riemannian 白化、切空间分类器）首次系统迁移到预训练语言模型的 token 嵌入上。
- **在三个含信号数据集上验证增益**：在 CoLA、CREAK、RTE 上，RMP 在相同的 BERT 嵌入上一致优于欧氏均值池化（Linear Probe）和 CLS Token 聚合，元效应 p = 9.75e-05。
- **引入并正确处理负控**：在为去除标注驱动词汇伪影而构造的 FEVER-Symmetric 上，RMP 与基线都停留在随机水平，证明流水线不会利用表面词汇偏差。
- **通过消融定位增益来源**：随机初始化的编码器 + Fréchet 聚合就已经在 CoLA 和 RTE 上超过欧氏池化，说明几何信号主要来自"聚合"而非"学习到的流形结构"；已训练编码器仅在知识最重的 CREAK 上额外贡献约 +3.6 AUC。
- **方法学自洽**：分类头（切空间 logistic regression / pyRiemann TSClassifier）对所有方法保持一致，因此性能差异可干净地归因到聚合步骤。

## Key Contributions

- **A complete geometric aggregation pipeline** that reuses mature BCI components (per-token SPD pullback metric, Fréchet mean on the SPD manifold, Riemannian whitening, tangent-space linear classifier) and applies them to pre-trained language-model token embeddings — a first systematic port of these tools to LM representation analysis.
- **Empirical wins on three signal-bearing datasets.** On CoLA, CREAK, and RTE, RMP consistently beats both Euclidean mean pooling (Linear Probe) and CLS Token Aggregation on identical BERT-base layer-9 embeddings; the headline meta-effect (Linear Probe vs RMP) reaches p = 9.75e-05.
- **A correctly behaved negative control.** On FEVER-Symmetric (constructed by Schuster et al. to remove annotation artifacts), all methods stay at chance (RMP AUC 0.508, 95% permutation-test CI [0.443, 0.557]); this is framed as the expected, correct outcome rather than a failure.
- **Clean attribution via ablations.** A randomly initialised encoder combined with Fréchet aggregation already beats Euclidean pooling on CoLA and RTE, localising the gain to the *geometric aggregation* rather than to *learned manifold structure*; the trained IGL encoder adds signal specifically on CREAK (+3.6 AUC, 0.703 vs 0.667), the most knowledge-heavy dataset.
- **A controlled, single-variable comparison.** The same classification head and the same frozen BERT embeddings feed every method, so observed differences attribute cleanly to the aggregation step rather than to extra trainable parameters or external knowledge.

## Methodology

**Setup and embeddings.** Token embeddings are extracted from the 9th hidden layer of `bert-base-uncased` (768-dim per token). For single-sentence datasets (FEVER, CoLA, CREAK) the input is encoded directly; for the entailment task (RTE) the premise and hypothesis are concatenated with separators. The baseline "Linear Probe" simply takes the Euclidean mean of token vectors and feeds a scikit-learn logistic regression (no regularisation, LBFGS, max-iter 1000). The same downstream classifier head is reused for RMP, so performance differences isolate the aggregation step.

**Intrinsic Green's Learning (IGL) encoder.** A small MLP encoder Ψθ : R^768 → R^64 maps each token embedding to a latent code. IGL (Quemy, 2026) replaces the decoder with a closed-form, tensor-factorised Product Green's kernel readout: reconstruction is a linear combination of K = 128 anchors with 4 learnable Gaussian scales per factor, and weights are recomputed per minibatch via variable-projection ridge regression (λ = 10⁻³). Because the decoder has no learnable weights, gradient descent only updates encoder parameters θ, forcing the encoder into coordinates aligned with the smooth structure of the data manifold. Matryoshka truncation (random dimension zeroing during training) makes earlier latent dims more informative. Training uses early stopping (patience = 50, δ = 2e-5).

**Pullback metric extraction.** Because Ψθ is a differentiable MLP, its Jacobian J(v) ∈ R^(64×768) is available analytically. The pullback (co-)metric is g⁻¹(v) = J(v)J(v)ᵀ. To obtain a strictly positive-definite metric tensor, the authors add a small regulariser before inversion: g(v) = (J Jᵀ + εI)^−1 with ε = 1e-6, plus an additive λI (λ = 1e-2) for stability. Each token thus yields a 64×64 SPD matrix g(v) ∈ S++(64). This avoids the sampling-density sensitivity of kNN metric estimation and the non-isometric distortion of UMAP-then-metric approaches.

**Sentence aggregation and classification.** The per-token SPD matrices {g(v₁), …, g(vₙ)} are aggregated into a single sentence descriptor via the Fréchet mean on the SPD manifold using the affine-invariant Riemannian distance d_Riem(G₁,G₂) = ‖log(G₁^−½ G₂ G₁^−½)‖_F. The descriptor is then Riemannian-whitened (rescaled to unit determinant), projected to the tangent space at the training-set population Riemannian mean, vectorised, and classified by logistic regression via pyRiemann's TSClassifier.

**Evaluation protocol.** 5-fold stratified cross-validation with 2 seeds (10 evaluations per method), following MOABB's statistical conventions. Per-dataset significance thresholds α < 0.05 / 0.01; effect sizes via Standardised Mean Difference (SMD); meta-effect aggregated across datasets. Datasets: FEVER-Symmetric (316 claims, negative control), RTE (2,490 pairs), CoLA (3,000 sentences), CREAK (3,000 statements).

**Ablations.** Three components isolate the source of the gain: (i) Encoder-Only (trained IGL encoder + Euclidean mean pooling in latent space, no metric extraction); (ii) Frozen Random IGL (untrained MLP of identical 256-d SiLU architecture + full geometric pipeline); (iii) Random Projection + SiLU (frozen random linear projection R^768→R^64 followed by SiLU + full geometric pipeline).

## Results & Highlights

- **Headline AUC gains over Euclidean mean pooling (Table 1, mean ± std over 10 evaluations):**
  - **CoLA**: RMP AUC **0.799 ± 0.018** vs Linear Probe **0.772 ± 0.025** (Δ = +0.027), CLS Token Aggregation 0.711.
  - **CREAK**: RMP AUC **0.708 ± 0.019** vs Linear Probe **0.648 ± 0.018** (Δ = +0.060), CLS Token Aggregation 0.631.
  - **RTE**: RMP AUC **0.638 ± 0.023** vs Linear Probe **0.591 ± 0.024** (Δ = +0.047), CLS Token Aggregation 0.611.
- **Negative control behaves correctly.** On **FEVER-Symmetric** all methods cluster at chance: Linear Probe AUC 0.424, CLS 0.402, RMP **0.508 ± 0.047**, which falls inside the permutation-test 95% CI [0.443, 0.557], confirming it is indistinguishable from chance.
- **Statistical significance.** The Linear-Probe-vs-RMP meta-effect across datasets reaches **p = 9.75e-05**; the CLS-vs-RMP meta-effect reaches **p = 2.92e-10**. Per-dataset p-values are uniformly 3.12e-02 (each dataset just over the per-comparison threshold but the aggregate is highly significant).
- **Ablation localises the gain to geometric aggregation (Table 2).** On CoLA, Frozen Random IGL (untrained encoder + full geometric pipeline) achieves AUC **0.762**, within one standard deviation of the trained-IGL RMP at **0.778**; on RTE, Frozen Random IGL 0.616 vs trained RMP 0.635. A randomly initialised encoder plus Fréchet aggregation therefore already beats Euclidean pooling on two of the three signal-bearing datasets.
- **Trained encoder matters only where world knowledge does.** On CREAK (most knowledge-heavy), the trained IGL encoder gains **+3.6 AUC** over the random one (0.703 vs 0.667), and the full RMP (0.703) beats Encoder-Only Euclidean-in-latent (0.675).
- **Encoder architecture depth matters even without training.** Random Projection + SiLU underperforms the random IGL MLP encoder on every signal-bearing dataset, indicating that MLP depth/nonlinearity contributes geometric signal even when weights are random.
- **Negative control is strengthened, not weakened, by residual BERT knowledge.** On FEVER-Symmetric, Encoder-Only (trained IGL + Euclidean pooling in latent space) reaches AUC **0.553 ± 0.037**, above chance, because the trained encoder exposes pre-training world knowledge; the full Riemannian pipeline nonetheless stays at chance (0.508) because the pullback metric captures how the encoder *bends* the space rather than *what content it preserves*.

## Authors & Credibility

The five authors come from **IBM Automation and AI (Krakow)**, the company **Hother**, the **University of Warsaw**, and the **Centre for Credible AI at Warsaw University of Technology**. The senior author line includes active contributors to the BCI/Riemannian-geometry literature (Cattan co-authors pyRiemann; Andreev et al. 2025 on the Riemannian Means Field Classifier is cited) and to intrinsic-dimension work (Sobieski et al. 2026 on hallucination detection via LID). The paper leverages mature, well-cited infrastructure (pyRiemann, MOABB, scikit-learn) and follows MOABB's statistical protocol rather than inventing a new one — a credibility-positive sign. A reference implementation of the IGL encoder is linked (github.com/hotherio/intrinsic-green-learning). The methodology is presented with explicit numerical choices (λ, ε, dmax, K, anchor counts, patience/δ for early stopping) and an architecture figure with full formulas in Appendix A.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine but incremental. The SPD Fréchet mean, affine-invariant distance, Riemannian whitening, and tangent-space logistic regression are all standard BCI tools (Barachant 2012; Congedo 2017; MOABB/Aristimunha 2023). The novelty lies in (a) porting them to LM token embeddings and (b) the specific design choice of extracting the per-token metric from a trained encoder's *analytical Jacobian* rather than from kNN patches or a UMAP projection. This is a real contribution but squarely an application/analysis paper, not a new geometric method.
- **Rigor:** Strong on controlled comparison. The same classification head, same frozen BERT-layer-9 embeddings, multi-seed CV (10 evaluations), permutation-test CIs, SMD effect sizes, and a meta-effect pooled across datasets all match a serious BCI-grade protocol. The inclusion of FEVER-Symmetric as a *deliberate* negative control, with the argument that chance-level there is the *correct* behaviour, is a commendable piece of intellectual honesty.
- **Reproducibility:** Good on paper. Hyperparameters, dataset sizes, and seeds are reported; the IGL encoder has a public reference implementation. Gaps: the exact wall-clock/FLOP cost of the per-token Jacobian + SPD Fréchet iteration is deferred to a "scaling study," and the hyperparameter sweep (encoder width, anchor count) was run only on FEVER-Symmetric — the negative control — so sensitivity on the signal-bearing datasets and to the metric regulariser λ is explicitly unverified.
- **Significance:** Moderate. The absolute performance (e.g., RTE AUC 0.638, CoLA 0.799) is far below fine-tuned BERT or Sentence-BERT, which the authors deliberately exclude — they argue the question is not absolute accuracy but whether geometric aggregation extracts signal that flat pooling discards. That is a defensible framing but limits practical uptake: RMP is positioned as a *probe* of embedding geometry, not as a deployable classifier.
- **Red flags / overclaiming:** No outright overclaiming — the abstract, conclusions, and discussion are careful and match the tables. The main concern is **single-encoder, single-layer generality**: everything runs on BERT-base layer 9 only, so the central claim that "geometric aggregation carries signal" could in principle be specific to that encoder/layer (the authors flag RoBERTa, DistilBERT, decoder-only LLMs, and other layers as future work). The encoder objective is also held fixed at IGL; although the random-encoder ablation makes a strong case that the specific objective is largely transparent on CoLA/RTE, alternative objectives (vanilla autoencoders, VAEs, normalising flows, contrastive encoders) were not swept.
- **Weak baseline observation:** CLS Token Aggregation is a weak, fine-tuning-free baseline that *underperforms* Euclidean mean pooling on three of four datasets — the authors note this replicates Reimers & Gurevych (2019). It is a fair second baseline but not a strong one; a more demanding comparison would include at least one stronger off-the-shelf sentence-embedding method (e.g., frozen Sentence-BERT or a mean-pooled fine-tuned BERT), which is the comparison a reviewer would most likely request.

## Strengths

- Clean, single-variable experimental design: identical embeddings and identical classification head across all methods, so the only moving part is the aggregation.
- A principled and correctly interpreted negative control (FEVER-Symmetric) that genuinely tests whether the method rides on annotation artifacts.
- Random-encoder ablations are the most informative part of the paper: they shift the conclusion from "the trained manifold carries signal" to "the act of geometric aggregation itself carries signal," a more general and more useful claim.
- Honest, non-overclaimed writing. The authors explicitly state what is not tested (other layers, other encoders, other objectives, wall-clock cost) and frame absolute accuracy as out of scope.
- Reuses well-validated tooling (pyRiemann, MOABB statistical protocol) rather than reinventing evaluation.

## Limitations

- **Single encoder and single layer.** Only `bert-base-uncased` layer 9 is evaluated; generalisation to other layers, other encoder families, and decoder-only LLMs is unverified.
- **Fixed encoder objective.** Only IGL is used; the claim that the specific objective is "largely transparent" rests on the random-encoder ablation, not on a sweep over autoencoders / VAEs / normalising flows / contrastive encoders.
- **Hyperparameter sweep scope.** Encoder-width and anchor-count sweeps were run only on FEVER-Symmetric (the negative control); sensitivity on CoLA/CREAK/RTE and to the metric regulariser λ is unreported.
- **Computational cost.** Roughly an order of magnitude more arithmetic per sentence than Euclidean pooling (per-token Jacobian + SPD Fréchet-mean iteration), with no exact wall-clock or FLOP figures.
- **Weak baselines relative to SOTA.** CLS Token Aggregation and frozen-BERT mean pooling are both fine-tuning-free; the paper excludes stronger sentence-embedding baselines by design, so absolute performance (e.g., RTE AUC 0.638) is not competitive with fine-tuned models.
- **Small negative-control sample.** FEVER-Symmetric has only 316 claims; the below-chance reading on Frozen Random IGL (0.443) is attributed to sample-specific bias on a small set rather than to a learned signal, which is plausible but under-powered.

## Relevance

对关注"语言模型表征的几何/流形结构"与"非欧嵌入分析"的研究方向高度相关：它把 BCI 领域成熟的 SPD 流形工具（Fréchet 均值、Riemannian 白化、切空间分类）首次系统迁移到预训练 LM 的 token 嵌入上，并通过随机编码器消融把几何增益的来源定位到"聚合方式"而非"学习到的流形结构"，为后续在更大模型、更多层上做几何探针提供了可直接复用的方法学模板。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the geometric-aggregation gain persist across other BERT layers, and does it peak at a layer consistent with the layer-wise intrinsic-dimension trajectory (Valeriani 2023; Cheng 2025)? Layer 9 alone is reported.
- Does the result generalise beyond BERT-base — to RoBERTa, DistilBERT, and decoder-only LLMs — or is the SPD-pullback signal specific to a particular residual-stream geometry?
- How does RMP compare against *stronger* but still training-free or lightly-trained sentence-embedding baselines (frozen Sentence-BERT, mean-pooled fine-tuned BERT, Matryoshka embeddings) on the same datasets?
- Are alternative Jacobian-yielding encoders (vanilla autoencoders, VAEs, normalising flows, contrastive encoders) largely interchangeable with IGL for the geometric pipeline, as the random-encoder ablation hints?
- Does replacing the simple Fréchet mean with weighted Fréchet means, trajectory-based descriptors (Damirchi 2026), or explicit per-token curvature features (eigenvalues of g(v)) add signal, especially on the knowledge-heavy CREAK set?
- What does the pullback metric actually encode linguistically? Can per-token eigenvalue spectra be tied to interpretable linguistic phenomena (POS, syntactic role, world-knowledge activation)?
