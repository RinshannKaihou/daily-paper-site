A weighted six-factor composite reveals LLMs win at reasoning and collapse on misinformation — a uniform failure across five major models.

Comprehensive Evaluation of Large Language Model Responses: A Multi-Factor Scoring System (MFSS) — arxiv.org/abs/2607.06940

0.6104
Gemini-2.0-flash composite on TruthfulQA — only +0.0016 over DeepSeek-v3 (0.6088), and every model fails Misinformation at ~0.25–0.30.
Why This Matters
Cheap, transparent multi-dimensional scoring — surfaces a reproducible reasoning-strong / fact-weak pattern useful for model selection and red-teaming.
Critical Edge
"Factual consistency" is lexical word-set overlap, not verification — a wrong answer reusing reference vocabulary scores high; the dimension names oversell what is measured.
Methodology

MFSS is a deterministic, reference-based scorer — no LLM-as-judge. For each (question, response A_m, reference A_g) triple it computes six sub-scores and folds them into one weighted total.

TS = Σ wᵢ·Mᵢ (Acc 0.25 · Conc 0.10 · Fact 0.20 · Read 0.15 · Coh 0.15 · ROUGE 0.15)
  • Accuracy 0.25 — cosine similarity of all-MiniLM-L6-v2 sentence embeddings of A_m vs A_g.
  • Conciseness 0.10 — inverse redundancy: exponential 2^repeat + quadratic count²·0.5 penalties, length-scaling beyond 2·|A_g|.
  • Factual consistency 0.20 — stopword-filtered word-set overlap |W_m∩W_g| / |W_g|.
  • Readability 0.15 — blend of deviation from a 17.5-word optimum sentence length and lexical diversity.
  • Coherence 0.15 — mean cosine between embeddings of adjacent sentences.
  • ROUGE 0.15 — 0.5·R1 + 0.2·R2 + 0.3·RL, scaled ×1.2, capped at 1.0.

Setup. TruthfulQA (817 English questions, each with a Best Answer). Five LLMs queried: QWQ-Plus, DeepSeek-v3, Doubao-1.5-pro-32k, Moonshot-v1-8k, Gemini-2.0-flash. Pipeline emits per-row detail + per-model summary CSVs, with a GUI radar/pie visualization.

  • Selection guide: accuracy → Gemini; factual → Doubao; readability → DeepSeek.

Strengths

  • Genuinely holistic — explicitly factors in user-experience dimensions (readability, conciseness, coherence) that pure accuracy/ROUGE pipelines ignore.
  • Transparent and cheap: every sub-score is closed-form, runs on CPU with a small SBERT model, avoids LLM-as-judge cost/variability.
  • Practitioner-friendly: per-model CSVs, summary table, GUI radar/pie visualization.
  • Reproducible, actionable per-category finding (Logical-Falsehood-good / Misinformation-bad).
  • Modular by design: weights and metrics are pluggable.

Limitations

  • "Factual consistency" is lexical overlap; "accuracy" is embedding similarity — the dimension names oversell what is measured.
  • English-only (TruthfulQA + BPE), contradicting the multilingual ambition in the abstract.
  • No human-judgment correlation; no comparison to G-Eval / MT-Bench / TrueScore / Chatbot Arena.
  • Fixed, unjustified weights, no sensitivity/ablation — tight clustering means rankings may be brittle.
  • Missing major Western frontier models (GPT-4, Claude, Llama) on an English benchmark.
  • Per-category reporting is top/bottom-2 only; no variance, CIs, or significance tests.
Open Questions
  1. How does MFSS correlate with human pairwise preference (Spearman/Kendall), and how does it compare to G-Eval / MT-Bench / TrueScore on the same responses?
  2. How sensitive is the ranking to the six weights — does Factual 0.20 → 0.30 flip the Gemini vs. DeepSeek ordering, given the 0.0016 gap?
  3. Would an NER- or entailment-based factual checker change the "models fail on Misinformation" conclusion, or is the low score an artifact of the lexical proxy?
  4. How do results hold on multilingual TruthfulQA and on GPT-4-class / Claude / Llama-3?
  5. Can the conciseness heuristic and the 17.5-word constant be validated against human readability ratings?
  6. Does decoding-parameter disclosure (temperature, top-p, system prompt) change rankings, since the proprietary models were queried under undocumented settings?
