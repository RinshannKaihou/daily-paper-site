> **一句话总结：** 本文从"时间维度"研究 LLM 的置信度，对比答题前的"知晓感"(FOK) 与答题后的"学习判断"(JOL)，并提出"未来置信度蒸馏"——用一个仅依赖答题前隐藏表示的轻量预测器，去回归答题后正确性探针产生的教师信号，从而在几乎不增加推理成本的前提下，恢复过半答题后置信度带来的校准增益（如 Factual 域 AUROC 恢复 66.1%），且样本高效、可在同域数据集间迁移。

> **TL;DR:** This paper reframes LLM confidence estimation as a temporal problem, comparing pre-solution Feeling-of-Knowing (FOK) and post-solution Judgement-of-Learning (JOL) signals across GPT-5.4, Claude Sonnet 4.6, and five open-source models on nine benchmarks (TriviaQA/NQ/PopQA, RiddleBench/StrategyQA/CommonsenseQA, GSM8K/SVAMP/MATH). Post-solution confidence is consistently better calibrated than pre-solution (e.g., open-model Math ECE drops 0.33→0.28, AUROC 0.59→0.62), and linear probes on hidden states beat verbal confidence by ΔECE up to −0.20. The authors introduce **future confidence distillation**: a ridge predictor trained on pre-solution hidden states to regress toward post-solution probe outputs, recovering 31.7%–66.1% of the post-solution AUROC gap while costing 1× inference (vs. 2.3–2.5× for full JOL), working from as few as 25 labelled examples, and transferring across datasets within a domain.

## 核心贡献

- **引入置信度估计的时间视角**：借用认知科学中的元记忆理论（Nelson 1990; Koriat 1997），将 LLM 自我评估明确区分为答题前的"知晓感"(FOK) 与答题后的"学习判断"(JOL)，并在前沿与开源模型上做了大规模的校准 (ECE) 与判别力 (AUROC) 对比分析。
- **揭示隐藏表示中蕴含的元认知信息远超模型口头表达**：在 factual/logic/math 三大域中，逐层线性探针一致优于口头置信度（如 Logic 域 ΔECE ≈ −0.20、ΔAUROC ≈ +0.15），且置信信息在中层（约 16–24 层）最易线性解码，最终层反而退化。
- **提出"未来置信度蒸馏"(Future Confidence Distillation)**：用答题后正确性探针的连续输出作为教师信号，训练一个仅看答题前隐藏表示的 ridge 预测器，把答题后才能获得的置信信息"提前"到答题前。
- **证明该方法的实用价值**：仅需答题前表示即可推理（推理成本 1× vs JOL 2.3–2.5×）、样本高效（25 例即可超越同预算的直接监督探针）、且可在同域数据集间迁移而无需重训。
- **诚实地报告负结果**：跨域迁移（如 factual→math）失败，置信表示并非通用，作者据此把迁移声明严格限制在同域范围内。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces a **temporal framing of LLM confidence** grounded in meta-memory theory (FOK before answering, JOL after), and provides a large-scale calibration/discrimination analysis across both frontier (GPT-5.4, Claude Sonnet 4.6) and open-source (Llama-3.1-8B, Qwen3-7B/14B/32B, Mistral-Nemo-12B) models on nine benchmarks.
- Shows that **linear probes on hidden representations recover substantially richer confidence information than what models verbalise** — best-layer probes improve ECE by up to −0.20 and AUROC by up to +0.15 over verbal confidence, with confidence most decodable in intermediate layers (≈16–24).
- Proposes **future confidence distillation**: a ridge regressor trained on *pre-solution* hidden states to predict the continuous teacher signal produced by a *post-solution* correctness probe, enabling low-cost confidence estimation that anticipates answer reliability before generation completes.
- Demonstrates the method is **sample-efficient (works from 25 examples), inference-cheap (1× cost vs 2.3–2.5× for post-solution JOL), and transfers across datasets within the same reasoning domain** without target-domain fine-tuning.
- **Honestly reports a negative cross-domain transfer result** (factual↔math), and scopes the transfer claims accordingly to within-domain settings.

## Methodology

The paper studies each example `x` through a three-stage pipeline, yielding a tuple `(x, ŷ, z, c_pre, c_post)`:

1. **Pre-solution FOK (`c_pre`)** — the model is prompted (without solving) to output "Confidence: X%" estimating its probability of eventually solving the problem. The prompt asks it to briefly think about reasoning complexity, number of steps, and failure modes.
2. **Answer generation** — `ŷ = f(x)` is produced with greedy decoding (T=0), and correctness `z ∈ {0,1}` is computed via exact-match / final-answer-match per domain.
3. **Post-solution JOL (`c_post`)** — the model is prompted to estimate the probability that its *own generated answer* is correct.

For **representation-based estimation**, the authors extract the final-token hidden state `h^(ℓ)` at uniformly-spaced eight-layer intervals for both the pre- and post-solution confidence prompts, standardise with a train-fitted `StandardScaler`, project to 128 dims via PCA, and train independent linear probes `p(z=1|h^(ℓ)) = σ(w⊤h + b)` (logistic regression, lbfgs, 3000 iters). The appendix ablates this against ridge and 1/2-layer MLPs; linear wins everywhere.

The core contribution, **future confidence distillation**, is a two-stage procedure:

- Train a post-solution correctness probe `T^(ℓ)` (logistic regression on `h_post`) using ground-truth labels `z`. Its continuous output `t_i = T^(ℓ)(h_post,i)` is the **teacher confidence estimate**.
- Train a distilled predictor `D^(ℓ)` (ridge regression on `h_pre`) to regress toward `t_i`, minimising `L_distill = Σ (ĉ_i − t_i)²`.

The teacher is used **only offline**; at deployment `D^(ℓ)` consumes only the pre-solution representation, so inference cost equals a single forward pass on the question (1×). The operating layer is selected by validation ECE (10% of the train split held out). All experiments average over 3 seeds (0,1,42) with a label-stratified 60/40 split (1800 examples/domain, capped so no accuracy class exceeds 65%), and use 4-bit Unsloth loading for the open-source models on 2× RTX 4090s.

## Results & Highlights

- **Post-solution verbal confidence beats pre-solution across the board.** On open-source models (Table 1), Math ECE drops from 0.33 (pre) to 0.28 (post) while AUROC rises 0.59→0.62; closed-model GPT-5.4 Math AUROC improves 0.72→0.75 and Claude Sonnet 4.6 Math AUROC jumps 0.50→0.79 with ECE 0.19→0.11 (Table 9).
- **Linear probes on hidden states beat verbal confidence by large margins.** Table 2 reports the best-layer probe gain over verbal: Logic ΔECE = −0.20 (pre) / −0.18 (post) with ΔAUROC up to +0.15; Factual ΔECE = −0.18 / −0.17; even Math gets ΔAUROC +0.05 (pre) / +0.07 (post).
- **Future confidence distillation recovers a substantial fraction of the post→pre gap.** Table 3: on AUROC, Factual recovers **66.1%** of the gap (Pre 0.696 → Distilled 0.731 → Post 0.756), Logic **58.5%** (0.628→0.696→0.722), Math **31.7%** (0.634→0.652→0.668). On ECE, recovery is 54.9% / 38.0% / 39.7% respectively (e.g., Factual ECE 0.161→0.133 vs Post 0.104).
- **Highly sample-efficient.** With only **25 labelled examples**, distilled predictors already outperform directly-supervised pre-solution probes at the same budget; gains plateau around 300–400 examples (Figure 4). Per-model (Table 14): Qwen3-32B ECE goes 0.280 (pre) → 0.238 (distilled, 25 ex) → 0.164 (distilled, 500 ex) vs Post 0.144.
- **Cross-dataset transfer within a domain works; cross-domain does not.** Distilled predictors trained on one benchmark and tested on a *different* benchmark of the same domain still beat same-budget pre-solution probes (Figure 5, Table 15). Cross-domain transfer (e.g., factual→math) is an explicit **negative result** — no stable trend, sometimes worse than baseline (Appendix E.5).
- **Deployment cost is ~1× vs 2.3–2.5× for post-solution methods** (Table 4): the teacher probe is offline-only, while inference needs only the question's pre-solution hidden state.
- **Architecture ablation (Table 8):** logistic regression is best for correctness probing (Factual ECE 0.156 vs MLP-2-layer 0.401), ridge is best for distillation (Factual 0.121 vs MLP-2-layer 0.386); non-linear probes overfit badly at these supervision budgets.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-good. The FOK/JOL distinction is borrowed from classical meta-memory psychology (Nelson 1990; Koriat 1997) rather than invented, and the finding that hidden-state probes beat verbal confidence recapitulates Kissling et al. 2026 and Ma et al. 2025 ("LLMs Have Intrinsic Meta-Cognition, but Need a Good Lens"). The genuinely new piece is the distillation direction — transferring metacognitive information *across answering stages within the same model* rather than across models (as in standard knowledge distillation). This "temporal" framing is a clean and useful conceptual contribution.
- **Rigor:** Strong on average. Three seeds, label-stratified splits, 95% CIs, validation-based layer selection, and an architecture ablation (Table 8) that actually justifies the linear-probe choice rather than assuming it. The honest negative cross-domain result (Appendix E.5) and the within-domain-only scoping of transfer claims are commendable.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Datasets are all public HF datasets, prompts are given verbatim (Appendix B.6), hyperparameters are scikit-learn defaults with no tuning, and hardware (2× RTX 4090) is stated. Two gaps: (a) the code/prompts parsing pipeline is not stated to be released; (b) the teacher-probe quality is the single point of failure — if `T^(ℓ)` is weak, the distilled `D^(ℓ)` cannot exceed it, and the paper does not bound how much residual error comes from teacher imperfection vs. student capacity.
- **Significance:** Practical. The 1× vs 2.3–2.5× inference-cost framing (Table 4) is the real sell for confidence-aware RAG/agent systems. However, the *absolute* calibration is still weak — even the best distilled predictor leaves Math ECE at 0.272 and Logic at 0.184, which is far from deployment-grade for high-stakes decisions. The "recovery %" metric is somewhat flattering because the pre→post gap itself is small in some cells (Math AUROC gap is only 0.034).
- **Red flags / things to probe:**
  - The "Recovery %" metric is computed against a gap that is itself small for Math (0.034 AUROC), so 31.7% recovery ≈ +0.018 AUROC — practically marginal. The paper does report absolute numbers alongside, which mitigates this, but headlines emphasise the percentage.
  - Frontier-model results (GPT-5.4, Claude Sonnet 4.6) are **verbal-only** — the entire distillation story is therefore demonstrated only on 8B–32B open models. Generalisation to frontier-scale reasoning models (the paper itself flags this as future work) is unverified.
  - The teacher probe and the student probe share the same architecture family (both linear, same PCA preprocessing), so the "linear is enough" conclusion is partly circular — a non-linear teacher with a linear student is not tried.
  - The 65% accuracy-class cap during sampling is a reasonable anti-degeneracy measure but means the reported ECEs are not directly comparable to numbers on the natural label distribution.
  - Single author + recent (2025–2026) heavy self-citation; while the cited self-work is on-topic, an independent group's replication would strengthen confidence.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated temporal framing that connects LLM confidence to decades of meta-memory psychology.
- Distillation idea is simple, well-defined, and directly useful: 1× inference cost with a meaningful chunk of the post-solution calibration gain.
- Genuinely sample-efficient (25 examples) and demonstrably transfers within-domain — both important for real deployment.
- Exceptionally honest about boundaries: explicitly reports and discusses the cross-domain negative result rather than burying it.
- Strong experimental hygiene: stratified splits, multiple seeds, CIs, layer-selection via validation, and a real architecture ablation.

## Limitations

- **Absolute calibration remains poor** for deployment (Math ECE ≈ 0.27 even after distillation); the gains are real but the endpoint is not "solved".
- **Frontier models are verbal-only.** The distillation claim is established solely on 8B–32B open-source instruction-tuned models; whether the dynamics hold for GPT-5.4 / Claude-class systems with reasoning traces is untested.
- **Math reasoning is the weakest setting** — only 31.7% AUROC recovery and the smallest absolute gains, yet math is arguably where reliable confidence matters most for tool-use / adaptive computation.
- **Scope restricted to text-only, single-turn, three domains.** No multimodal, no agentic/tool-use, no long-horizon planning — the paper acknowledges this.
- **Teacher quality is not isolated** as an error source; the student cannot exceed the teacher, and the teacher is itself an imperfect linear probe.
- **No code/release statement** found in the main text; prompts are given, but the full probing/distillation pipeline would need re-implementation.
- **Linear-only distillation** by design — richer non-linear predictors are explicitly deferred, so the ceiling of the approach is unknown.

## Relevance

与本工作关注的"LLM 自我认知 / 置信度校准 / 隐藏表示探针"主题高度相关：它把传统"答题后置信度"重定义为时间序列问题，并用蒸馏让答题前表示逼近答题后置信，对 RAG 触发、工具调用、自适应计算等下游决策直接有用。其样本高效与同域迁移特性，对实际部署中的低成本置信估计尤其有借鉴价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would a **non-linear teacher probe** (e.g., a small MLP) with a linear student lift the recovery ceiling on Math, where the current 31.7% AUROC recovery is weak?
- Can future confidence distillation be made **self-supervised** — e.g., using the model's own JOL verbal estimate as the teacher instead of a label-trained correctness probe — to remove the dependence on ground-truth labels at training time?
- Does the temporal FOK→JOL gap and the distillation recovery fraction **scale with model size or reasoning-chain length** on reasoning models (e.g., o1-style step-by-step traces), where the post-solution signal should be much richer?
- The cross-domain transfer failure suggests confidence representations are domain-specific — is there a **shared "meta-cognitive" subspace** across domains that a multi-task or contrastive objective could expose?
- How does future confidence distillation interact with **selective prediction / abstention policies** — what is the downstream risk-coverage curve improvement, not just the ECE/AUROC improvement?
- The 65%-class-cap sampling changes the label distribution; how robust are the reported ECE deltas to **natural (uncapped) class distributions** at deployment?
