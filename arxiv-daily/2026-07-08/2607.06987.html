  • UP-DAPO AIME24: Avg@32 51.15 vs 47.71 (+3.44), Maj@32 +2.52, Best@32 +1.30.

Ablations (load-bearing): removing only the upper clip (ϵhigh=∞) → gradient norm explodes to 10¹³ after ~80 steps; symmetric unbounded (off-policy REINFORCE both signs) → collapses within 25 steps.

Strengths

  • Clean theory: self-anchor turns unbounded off-policy ratio into the exact REINFORCE gradient, with a non-trivial sequence-level (GSPO) proof.
  • "Probability Capacity" formalism gives a reusable lens for why clip-based methods under-explore.
  • Genuinely controlled setup — only the loss differs between each baseline and its UP twin.
  • Broad validation: 3 algorithms × 3 architectures (Dense/MoE/VLM) × 2 modalities × 3 eval protocols.
  • Removes the ϵhigh hyperparameter for the positive branch — simpler tuning.

Limitations

  • Margins small (best avg +1.16%); ties on several columns; no multi-seed variance or significance test.
  • All experiments on Qwen3-family models — Llama/Mistral/frontier-proprietary untested.
  • No analysis of exploration side-effects: response-length inflation, reward hacking, late-training drift.
  • Largest compute (14B Dense, 30B MoE) reports only AIME24 Avg@32, not the full 5-benchmark suite.
  • No head-to-head controlled comparison vs other entropy-preserving asymmetric methods (ASPO, W-REINFORCE); ASPO beats UP on OlympiadBench.
  • Code/checkpoint release not explicitly stated.
Open Questions
  1. Do the Table 1 rankings survive multi-seed runs and a larger compute budget? What are the per-benchmark standard deviations?
  2. Does the deliberately increased exploration entropy induce length inflation, reward hacking, or repetition — and does it emerge late (>500 steps)?
  3. How does UP behave on non-Qwen3 families (Llama, Mistral, Gemma)? Is the self-anchor equally stable across tokenizers?
  4. Can UP compose with other asymmetric methods (ASPO, W-REINFORCE, TOPR), or do they target the same mechanism and become redundant?
  5. What happens under noisy rewards (process reward models, RLHF with human prefs) where the correct/incorrect split is less clean?
