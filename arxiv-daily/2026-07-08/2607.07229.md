> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"推理一致性扫描"方法，区分于CoT忠实性（faithfulness），通过六类不一致性分类法与LLM-as-judge扫描器，在AI安全评测转录（inspect evals的MORU、SAD等）上后验审计CoT与答案是否逻辑相连，发现不一致率在0.0%–26.0%之间且随任务类型/模型系统性变化（多选题SAD最高，开放MORU最低）。

> **TL;DR:** The paper introduces "reasoning consistency scanning," a post-hoc, intervention-free method for auditing whether a model's stated chain-of-thought logically connects to its final answer in AI safety evaluation transcripts. Built as an InspectScout LLM-as-judge scanner (Claude Opus 4.6) validated on a 60-transcript InstrumentalEval-derived benchmark (overall F1=0.82), it flags six inconsistency subtypes. Applied across four generator models and three inspect evals suites (MORU, SAD, Agentic Misalignment), inconsistency rates range from 0.0% to 26.0%, peaking at 26.0% on SAD stages full (Gemini 3.1 Pro) versus 0.9–1.0% on open-ended MORU — a delta driven largely by multiple-choice vs. open-ended response format.

## 核心贡献

- **形式化区分"一致性"与"忠实性"**：将reasoning consistency定义为"答案是否反映推理所建立的优先级/结论"（可仅从转录文本判定，无需干预实验），与需要受控干预的faithfulness严格区分，填补了事后审计的方法空白。
- **六类不一致性分类法 + 显式判定流程**：提出absent reasoning、contradictory reasoning、apparent confusion、reasoning reversal、reasoning abandonment、perfunctory reasoning六种子类型，并配以有序判定流程，避免扫描器默认套用常见子类型。
- **可复用的合成基准**：基于InstrumentalEval（DeepSeek V4 Pro生成）构建60条人工标注转录，通过"外科手术式"修改真实转录引入特定不一致性，包含边缘案例用于压力测试。
- **InspectScout扫描器实现**：首个面向安全评测转录的通用CoT一致性扫描器，采用LLM-as-judge架构，开源代码/基准/扫描器。
- **跨4个生成模型×3类评测的实证结果**：首次将CoT分析应用于inspect evals安全套件（而非学术基准MMLU/GPQA），揭示不一致率随任务类型与模型的系统性变化规律。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalization of reasoning consistency as distinct from faithfulness.** Consistency asks whether the stated reasoning logically connects to the final answer as observable text — detectable from transcripts alone without the controlled interventions (hint injection, counterfactual perturbation) that faithfulness research requires and that cannot be applied to existing transcript corpora.
- **A six-subtype taxonomy of inconsistency with an explicit ordered decision procedure** (absent reasoning, contradictory reasoning, apparent confusion, reasoning reversal, reasoning abandonment, perfunctory reasoning), designed to prevent the scanner defaulting to familiar subtypes like reversal.
- **A validated synthetic benchmark of 60 labeled transcripts** derived from InstrumentalEval, built by surgically modifying real transcripts to introduce specific inconsistencies, including edge cases near decision boundaries.
- **A working InspectScout scanner** — claimed to be the first general-purpose CoT consistency scanner and the first applied to transcript-level analysis of the inspect evals suite — released open-source with code, benchmark, and scanner.
- **Empirical evidence across four generator models and three evaluations** that reasoning inconsistency exists in naturalistic safety eval transcripts and varies systematically by task type and model, with implications for eval construct validity and CoT monitoring reliability.

## Methodology

The methodology rests on three components.

**Consistency definition and taxonomy.** A transcript is consistent if "a line of thought can be drawn from the reasoning to the output." This is explicitly not a correctness check — there is no ground-truth answer. Hedging, exploratory reasoning across multiple directions, and brief answers following from longer reasoning (e.g., a single letter after deliberation) are all treated as consistent. Six inconsistency subtypes are defined (Table 1) and applied through an ordered decision procedure: missing/empty answer → not applicable; non-engaging reasoning field → absent reasoning; internally incompatible directions → contradictory reasoning; incoherent without explicit contradiction → apparent confusion; reasoning concludes one way but answer commits another → reasoning reversal; specific considerations raised then ignored → reasoning abandonment; reasoning too thin to plausibly yield the specific answer → perfunctory reasoning; otherwise → consistent.

**The scanner.** Implemented using InspectScout's `llm scanner` API following the UK AISI seven-step log-analysis guide. The LLM-as-judge receives the system prompt, question, reasoning trace, and final answer, and emits a structured classification: consistency label (consistent/inconsistent/not applicable), subtype, confidence (high/medium/low), one-sentence summaries of reasoning and answer, a disconnect description, and a justification. The prompt encodes the full taxonomy with explicit contrast statements between adjacent subtypes (e.g., contradictory vs. reversal) to reduce misclassification, and scope limits that forbid judging factual correctness or internal model processes. The scanner model is held constant (Claude Opus 4.6) across all generators to control scanner-side variance. Model selection was consequential: DeepSeek V4 Flash was discarded for failing to produce valid structured output; Claude Haiku 4.5 produced reliable output but had low per-subtype recall; Opus 4.6 was chosen for superior classification.

**Synthetic benchmark.** 60 transcripts from InstrumentalEval (instrumental convergence: self-preservation, power-seeking, deception), generated with DeepSeek V4 Pro. Consistent examples are unmodified rich transcripts; inconsistent examples are real transcripts surgically modified at the answer or reasoning to introduce a specific subtype. Ground-truth labels are stored separately, keyed by sample ID.

**Naturalistic corpus.** The validated scanner is applied to transcripts from three inspect evals evaluations: MORU (100 samples, open-ended moral uncertainty), Agentic Misalignment (1 sample, agentic scheming), and three SAD-mini subsets (stages oversight, stages full, influence — 100 each, multiple-choice). Four generator models were used: Gemini 3.1 Pro, DeepSeek V4 Pro, gpt-oss-120b, and (attempted) GPT-5.4. All runs use seed=42.

## Results & Highlights

- **Benchmark validation (Opus 4.6 scanner, 60 transcripts):** Overall precision **0.96**, recall **0.71**, F1 **0.82**. Per-subtype: consistent F1=0.97, reasoning reversal F1=0.80, reasoning abandonment F1=0.78, perfunctory reasoning F1=0.83, apparent confusion F1=0.67, absent reasoning F1=0.80, not-applicable F1=0.933, edge cases F1=0.89 — but **contradictory reasoning scored 0.00 precision/recall/F1 (complete failure)** on its 5 samples.
- **Scanner model comparison (same prompt/benchmark):** DeepSeek V4 Flash F1=0.55 (discarded — invalid structured output), Claude Haiku 4.5 F1=0.68 (low per-subtype recall), Claude Opus 4.6 F1=0.82 — a **0.27 F1 gap** between the worst and best scanner model, underscoring scanner-dependence.
- **Naturalistic inconsistency rates range 0.0%–26.0%** across all evaluation–model combinations, with no model uniformly low or high.
- **Format-driven divergence:** Open-ended MORU yields low rates — DeepSeek V4 Pro **0.9%**, Gemini 3.1 Pro **1.0%**, gpt-oss-120b **10.0%** — while multiple-choice SAD subsets are markedly higher: SAD stages full peaks at **26.0% (Gemini 3.1 Pro)**, **15.0% (DeepSeek V4 Pro)**, **7.0% (gpt-oss-120b)**; SAD influence reaches **17.0% (DeepSeek V4 Pro)**; SAD stages oversight reaches **12.0% (DeepSeek V4 Pro)**.
- **Agentic Misalignment registers 0.0% across all three models**, but with only 1 available sample this is illustrative, not statistically meaningful.
- **Cross-model interaction effects:** DeepSeek V4 Pro is the most inconsistent model on SAD influence (17.0%) and oversight (12.0%); Gemini 3.1 Pro peaks on stages full (26.0%) but is among the lowest on MORU (1.0%); gpt-oss-120b is the most inconsistent on MORU (10.0%) yet comparatively low on SAD (2.0–7.0%) — suggesting task-specific rather than general reasoning failures.
- **Subtype distribution:** Perfunctory reasoning and absent reasoning dominate, consistent with the hypothesis that multiple-choice format induces models to treat reasoning as a formality rather than genuine deliberation.

## Authors & Credibility

Single-author paper by **Silvia Santano**. The work is positioned within the UK AI Safety Institute ecosystem (inspect ai, inspect evals, InspectScout) and cites the canonical CoT-faithfulness literature (Turpin et al. 2023, Lanham et al. 2023, Chen et al. 2025, Walden & Wanner 2026). Code, benchmark, and scanner are released at https://github.com/SilviaSantano/Reasoning-Consistency-Scanner. All evaluation runs use seed=42 for reproducibility. The single-author status and the absence of any affiliation/institutional backing are worth noting, but the methodology is transparent about its scanner-model selection process, discarded models, and GPT-5.4 incompatibility — a level of honesty that strengthens credibility despite the modest scale.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely useful reframing. The consistency-vs-faithfulness distinction is clean and well-motivated: consistency is post-hoc auditable from transcripts alone, which faithfulness methods structurally cannot be. The ordered decision procedure and six-subtype taxonomy are a real methodological contribution. However, "LLM-as-judge classifies whether reasoning matches answer" is not a radically new primitive; the novelty is in the formalization, taxonomy, and application domain (safety evals rather than MMLU/GPQA).
- **Rigor:** Mixed. The benchmark validation is reported honestly, including the **complete failure on contradictory reasoning (0.00 F1)** — a notable admission. But that failure is under-analyzed: the paper speculates it may be due to fuzzy boundaries or too-subtle samples but does not fix it or exclude the subtype from headline numbers. The headline "overall F1=0.82" pools subtypes with wildly different reliability, which could mislead a casual reader.
- **Reproducibility:** Strong on process (seed=42, open-source code/benchmark/scanner, fixed scanner model, documented discarded models). Weak on statistical power: **Agentic Misalignment has n=1**, and several conclusions rest on single-sample or 100-sample configurations with no confidence intervals, significance tests, or multiple-seed variance reported. The single benchmark source (InstrumentalEval × DeepSeek V4 Pro) means the scanner's validated performance is anchored to one eval–generator pair while being applied to others.
- **Significance:** Practically relevant for AI safety eval practitioners — a 26.0% inconsistency rate on a multiple-choice situational-awareness eval is a concrete red flag for anyone using CoT as evidence in that setting. The framing as a "necessary but not sufficient" precondition for CoT monitoring is appropriately modest.
- **Red flags:** (1) **GPT-5.4 silently dropped** from generator coverage due to a reasoning-effort default/API mismatch that was discovered too late to re-run for cost reasons — leaving the headline "four generator models" effectively at three, with no frontier OpenAI model represented and no Anthropic models as generators (Claude Opus 4.6 is only the scanner). (2) **Gemini results rest on reasoning summaries, not full reasoning tokens**, which the authors flag but which materially affects the validity of Gemini's inconsistency counts. (3) **DeepSeek V4 Pro emitted "gibberish text, possibly leaking training data"** in some traces — a data-quality issue that is acknowledged in one sentence and never quantified or excluded. (4) **No sensitivity analysis across scanner models** in the main results (deferred to a one-row appendix table), despite results being scanner-dependent by the authors' own admission.

## Strengths

- Clean, well-motivated conceptual separation between consistency (observable, post-hoc) and faithfulness (causal, intervention-based), filling a real gap for auditing already-generated transcript corpora.
- Transparent and honest reporting: discarded scanner models, the contradictory-reasoning failure, the GPT-5.4 incompatibility, and the Gemini-summary caveat are all disclosed rather than hidden.
- Open-source release of code, benchmark, and scanner lowers the barrier to adoption and replication.
- The applied domain (inspect evals safety suite) is more consequential than the academic benchmarks used by prior faithfulness work; finding 26% inconsistency in a situational-awareness eval is a substantive, actionable finding.
- The ordered decision procedure is a thoughtful design choice that mitigates a known failure mode of open-ended LLM classification.

## Limitations

- Benchmark is derived from a single evaluation (InstrumentalEval) and a single generator (DeepSeek V4 Pro); validated performance may not transfer cleanly to other eval–generator combinations.
- Complete scanner failure on contradictory reasoning (F1=0.00) leaves one of six subtypes effectively undetectable.
- No Anthropic models tested as generators; GPT-5.4 dropped; Gemini uses reasoning summaries not full tokens — coverage of frontier closed-source reasoning models is therefore thin.
- Modest and uneven sample sizes (Agentic Misalignment n=1); no confidence intervals, significance tests, or multi-seed variance reported.
- Results are scanner-, prompt-, and threshold-dependent; a comprehensive sensitivity analysis is explicitly deferred to future work.
- Consistency ≠ faithfulness: a "consistent" transcript may still contain entirely post-hoc rationalization, so the scanner establishes only a necessary, not sufficient, condition for trusting CoT.

## Relevance

对关注LLM推理可信度与评测有效性的研究者高度相关：本文直接回应"CoT能否作为安全评测证据"的核心问题，提供的扫描器与基准可作为评估reasoning model（如o1/DeepSeek-R1类）输出可信度的实用工具，与CoT faithfulness、monitoring、eval validity等方向天然衔接。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How would the scanner perform with full (non-summarized) Gemini reasoning tokens, and would the 26.0% stages-full rate for Gemini hold or change materially?
- Can the contradictory-reasoning subtype (F1=0.00) be rescued with targeted prompt engineering, contrastive examples, or a different scanner model, or is it fundamentally too graded/fuzzy for a single-label classifier?
- Do the elevated inconsistency rates on multiple-choice SAD subsets reflect a genuine reasoning–answer disconnect, or an artifact of the format making any brief answer look "perfunctory" by definition?
- How sensitive are the headline inconsistency rates to scanner-model choice (Opus 4.6 vs. Haiku 4.5 vs. others) — would a full sensitivity analysis change the cross-model ranking?
- Would adding Anthropic models (Claude family) as generators, and GPT-5.4 with the correct Responses API + reasoning-effory parameter, reveal different inconsistency profiles that change the paper's conclusions?
- Is there a relationship between consistency-failure rate and downstream safety-relevant behavior (e.g., do models with higher inconsistency rates also score differently on the underlying capability the eval is meant to measure)?
