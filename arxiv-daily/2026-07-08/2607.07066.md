> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"幺半群扩展"（Monoid Extension），将先前解释群运算的 GCR 机制局部化推广到不可逆的复合模数乘法（含零因子）场景，通过 J-类分层、注意力路由与局部逆/局部特征，揭示小型 Transformer 在 Z_165 上以分层傅里叶机制实现乘法，局部特征能解释 71.2%–99.4% 的 logit 方差。

> **TL;DR:** The authors extend the GCR (Group Composition via Representation) interpretability framework of Chughtai et al. (2023) from globally-invertible group operations to non-invertible modular multiplication over composite (square-free) moduli such as Z_165, proposing a "Monoid Extension" in which the network partitions inputs into J-classes, routes via attention, and scores outputs with local inverses/characters; on a 1-layer transformer, synthetic local-character logits explain 71.2%–99.4% of the empirical logit variance across J-classes versus the single-global-representation assumption of standard GCR, which provably cannot score zero-divisor outputs at all.

## 核心贡献

- 提出 **Monoid Extension**：将 GCR 算法从全局可逆的群设定局部化推广到含零因子的有限交换幺半群 (Z_n, ·)，核心是用 J-类（按 gcd(a,n)=d 划分）替代单一全局表示空间，在每个 regular J-类内部恢复局部群结构、局部单位元 e_{J_d} 与局部逆 c♯，使 logit 计算改写为 Logit(c) ∝ χ_{ρ_d}(abc♯)。
- 给出 **分层电路证据**：逐层逆向工程一个 1 层 Transformer（n=165），发现嵌入按 J-类聚成低维子空间、注意力 Head 0 呈与 J-类边界对齐的块对角结构（宏观路由）、OV 电路为低秩投影且其秩跌落点与各 J-类的关键傅里叶频率数吻合、MLP 激活保持同类的分层频率结构、unembedding 用局部特征实现 readout。
- 提出 **原子因子化假设**（atomic factorization）：非循环 J-类（如 J_3、J_5）直接复用其循环"原子因子"的关键频率，而非学习全新表示，2D-DFT 可视化直接展示了这种直积结构复用。
- **定量验证局部特征 readout**：用理论构造的 χ_{ρ_d}(abc♯) 合成 logit 拟合模型经验 logit，R² 在 7 个目标 J-类上达到 71.2%（J_1）至 99.4%（J_55），为非群结构下表示论机制的存在提供直接定量证据。
- 在多模数（113/143/154/165）与 10 个随机种子上验证嵌入低维性的稳定性，并提供开源代码与可交互图。

## Key Contributions

- Proposes the **Monoid Extension**, a localized generalization of GCR (Chughtai et al., 2023) from globally-invertible group operations to the non-invertible commutative monoid (Z_n, ·). The key move replaces the single global representation space with a partition into J-classes (J_d = {a ∈ Z_n : gcd(a,n) = d}); for square-free n every regular J-class is a group ≅ (Z/(n/d)Z)^× with a local identity e_{J_d} and local inverse c♯, re-expressing the logit rule as Logit(c) ∝ tr(ρ_d(ab)ρ_d(c♯)) = χ_{ρ_d}(abc♯).
- Provides **circuit-level, layer-by-layer evidence** on a 1-layer decoder-only transformer trained on n=165: embeddings cluster into J-class subspaces; attention Head 0 shows a block-diagonal structure aligned with J-class boundaries (a macroscopic router) while Heads 1–3 carry finer within-class phase; the OV circuit acts as a low-rank projector whose rank drop-off matches the number of key Fourier frequencies per J-class; MLP activations preserve the same stratified Fourier structure.
- Introduces the **atomic factorization hypothesis**: non-cyclic J-classes (e.g., J_3, J_5) reuse the key Fourier frequencies of their shared cyclic "atomic" factors rather than learning bespoke representations, visually confirmed by 2D-DFT of individual neurons.
- **Quantitatively validates the local-character readout**: synthetic logits built purely from χ_{ρ_d}(abc♯) at the key frequencies explain R² = 71.2% (J_1) up to 99.4% (J_55) of the model's centered empirical logit variance across target J-classes (Table 3).
- Confirms robustness across moduli (113, 143, 154, 165) and 10 random seeds (Table 5), and releases code/interactive figures at https://github.com/uw-math-ai/interpreting-monoids.

## Methodology

The work is a mechanistic-interpretability reverse-engineering study, not a benchmark-driven ML paper. The pipeline is:

**Task and model.** A 1-layer decoder-only transformer (d_model=128, d_hidden=512, ReLU, 4 attention heads) is trained on modular multiplication c = a·b mod n over Z_n, predicting the full n×n table. Inputs are 3-token sequences (a, b, =). Training uses full-batch AdamW (lr=1e-3, weight decay=1, betas 0.9/0.98) for 25,000 epochs on 30% of the n² table, with another 30% held out for validation; the model exhibits grokking (validation accuracy rises to match training accuracy). The primary analysis uses n=165 = 3×5×11 (square-free, ω=3), with 113, 143, 154 as additional moduli.

**Algebraic setup.** The monoid (Z_n, ·) is partitioned into disjoint J-classes J_d = {a : gcd(a,n)=d}. For square-free n, Theorem 3.4 guarantees each regular J-class J_d ≅ (Z/(n/d)Z)^× is a group with local identity e_{J_d} = (n/d)·((n/d)⁻¹ mod n) and local inverses c♯ (where cc♯ = e_{J_d}). This is what lets group representation theory be applied *locally* where global invertibility fails (e.g., 5 ∈ Z_165 has no global inverse, but inside its J-class it has a local inverse).

**The Monoid Extension hypothesis (generalized GCR).** Standard GCR posits: embed → ρ(a),ρ(b); MLP composes → ρ(a)ρ(b)=ρ(ab); unembedding scores c via tr(ρ(ab)ρ(c⁻¹))=χ(abc⁻¹). This decoding rule breaks for zero-divisors (no c⁻¹ exists). The Monoid Extension rephrases it as a two-phase procedure: (1) a routing phase projects the product ab into the subspace of its J-class J_d; (2) conditioned on the class, the unembedding scores candidates c ∈ J_d via the local character χ_{ρ_d}(abc♯), maximized when abc♯ = e_{J_d}. On J_1 this reduces exactly to standard GCR.

**Interpretability analyses (one per layer).**
- *Embeddings:* partition W_E into per-class sub-matrices W_E^{J_d}, permute lexicographically by a minimal generating set, apply DFT, and measure Fraction of Variance Explained (FVE) by sparse key frequencies; PCA to test subspace collapse versus random vocabulary subsets of equal size.
- *Attention:* compute QK attention scores from '=' to 'a' as a function of (a,b), segmented by J-class partition, to expose block-diagonal (Head 0) vs. fine-phase (Heads 1–3) structure; SVD/principal-angle alignment of the OV circuit W_OV^{(0)} against each J-class embedding subspace.
- *MLP:* visualize hidden activations over (a,b) pairs, checking J-class clustering and frequency structure (a full neuron-level reconstruction is explicitly left to future work).
- *Unembedding:* exhaustively enumerate all valid prompts per target class, build synthetic logit vectors from the explicit χ_{ρ_d}(abc♯) at key frequencies, run forward passes to get empirical logits, and fit a linear regression reporting R² as the FVE of the theoretical character trace.

## Results & Highlights

- **Sparse Fourier features dominate embeddings (Table 2):** a tiny subset of key frequencies explains ≥94% of the spectral energy in the permuted embedding space for every non-trivial J-class — J_1: 8 key freqs of 79 total → 97.0% FVE; J_3: 7/39 → 96.8%; J_5: 5/19 → 95.0%; J_11: 4/7 → 99.1%; J_15: 4/9 → 94.3%.
- **Local character features explain a large fraction of output logits (Table 3, R²):** synthetic χ_{ρ_d}(abc♯) logits fit empirical logits at J_55 (J_5×J_11) 99.4%, J_15 (J_3×J_5) 95.2%, J_33 (J_3×J_11) 80.2%, J_11 (J_1×J_11) 86.8%, J_5 (J_1×J_5) 86.9%, J_3 (J_1×J_3) 76.5%, J_1 (J_1×J_1) 71.2%. The lowest fits occur for the largest classes (J_1, J_3), which the authors attribute to additional nonlinear/harmonic mechanisms not captured by the linear character model.
- **OV circuit is sharply low-rank:** of 32 singular values in W_OV^{(0)}, only 4 are needed to explain 95% of the weight variance and the top 8 explain >99.9%; principal-angle alignment shows the rank at which alignment drops (cosine <0.8) matches the per-class key-frequency count (e.g., J_1 drops after 8 dims, J_3 after 7), consistent with a targeted low-rank projection onto the atomic Fourier subspace.
- **J-class subspace collapse beats random baselines (Figure 2):** fewer than 20% of principal components explain >95% of embedding variance across all J-classes, and actual J-classes require substantially fewer components than equivalently-sized random subsets of the vocabulary — direct evidence the network compresses same-class elements into tight localized subspaces rather than a generic low-rank space.
- **Cross-seed / cross-modulus stability (Table 5, 10 seeds):** mean PCs for 95% FVE are 10.0±0.6 (n=165, FVE 96.16%), 10.9±1.6 (n=143, 95.89%), 9.6±1.7 (n=113, 95.90%), 10.1±0.9 (n=154, 95.99%), showing the low-dimensional stratified structure is reproducible.
- **Attention factorizes by role (Figure 3):** Head 0 produces a block-diagonal pattern aligned with J-class boundaries (coarse router), while Heads 1–3 produce high-frequency checkerboards consistent with within-class Fourier-phase information — matching the two-phase routing-then-scoring prediction.
- The model groks: validation accuracy rises to match training accuracy only after a grokking gap, and the authors conjecture the J-class stratification is learned during this phase.

## Authors & Credibility

- Authors are from the **UW Math AI Lab, University of Washington** (Zitong Andrew Chen, Junaid Hasan, Akhil Srinivasan, Hemkesh Bandi, Jarod Alper); the first three contribute equally. Jarod Alper is a mathematician (algebraic geometry), lending genuine representation-theoretic rigor (the paper cites Howie's *Fundamentals of Semigroup Theory* and Steinberg's *Representation Theory of Finite Monoids* and proves the regular-J-class group isomorphism as Theorem 3.4 / Appendix D.17).
- The work is positioned as a direct, acknowledged extension of two well-cited mechanistic-interpretability results: Nanda et al. (2023, "Progress measures for grokking") and Chughtai et al. (2023, GCR), and cites Stander et al. (2024, "Grokking group multiplication with cosets").
- Published at the **ICML 2026 Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop** (Seoul) — a workshop paper, not a main-conference track; this is the appropriate venue for a correlative circuit-level study, but it sets the bar for rigor accordingly.
- Code and interactive figures are released at https://github.com/uw-math-ai/interpreting-monoids, and the architecture exactly mirrors the Nanda et al. (2023) setup (d_model=128, 1-layer, AdamW), aiding reproducibility.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine and well-motivated. The non-invertible/zero-divisor regime is a real gap in the GCR line (the standard decoding rule tr(ρ(ab)ρ(c⁻¹)) literally cannot be evaluated for zero-divisors like 5 ∈ Z_165, as the paper demonstrates explicitly), and the J-class + local-inverse construction is a clean, mathematically legitimate way to recover group-like structure locally. The atomic-factorization observation (composite J-classes reuse atomic frequencies) is a non-obvious empirical finding.
- **Rigor:** Strong on the algebraic side (real theorems with proofs in the appendix) and reasonable on the empirical side, but the central claims are **correlational only** — the authors state this themselves. There are no ablations, no activation patching, no knock-out of Head 0 or the key-frequency directions to show the routing actually *causes* the class-sensitive behavior. The principal-angle/SVD analyses are consistent with the hypothesis but equally consistent with several alternatives.
- **A specific weak point — the largest, most important J-class fits poorly:** the local-character readout explains only **71.2%** of J_1 (J_1×J_1) and 76.5% of J_3 logit variance, yet J_1 is the unit group where standard GCR *already* applies and should be the easiest case. The authors hand-wave this to "nonlinear scaling, harmonic distortion, or sparse class-specific corrections," but never test which. A reader could reasonably suspect the model is doing something the character model does not capture even in the regime where the theory should be tightest.
- **Missing ablations:** no test of whether ablating the identified key-frequency directions actually degrades the corresponding J-class logits; no test of whether forcing a single global representation (the standard GCR baseline) underperforms the stratified model on a controlled comparison — the comparison to GCR is conceptual (GCR "cannot score zero-divisor outputs"), not an empirical head-to-head on the same trained network.
- **Scope limits / cherry-picking risk:** analysis is restricted to **square-free** moduli (natural density 6/π²≈0.608), precisely the case where every J-class is regular and the local-group reduction holds. Non-square-free moduli introduce nilpotent elements and non-regular J-classes that *break the entire framework* — acknowledged as future work, but it means the "beyond groups" claim is really "beyond groups, but only for the friendly subclass of monoids where a local group still exists."
- **Reproducibility:** good — standard architecture, released code, 10-seed stability table for the PCA/Fourier claims. However, the layer-by-layer circuit narrative (Tables 2–3, Figures 1–4) is reported on **seed 1 only** ("Unless otherwise specified, all results are reported on seed 1"), so the headline logit-FVE numbers (71.2%–99.4%) have no reported variance.
- **Overclaiming:** mild. The abstract's "representation-theoretic mechanisms previously identified for group operations can extend beyond groups" is supportable; the title "Multiplication Beyond Groups" is fair. But "explain a large fraction" for 71.2% on J_1 is arguably generous, and the causal language in the conclusion ("the network sub-divides the task into phases executed by different parts") overstates what correlational evidence can establish.

## Strengths

- Mathematically principled: uses real semigroup/representation theory (J-classes, regular classes, idempotents, local inverses) rather than ad-hoc probes, with proofs deferred to the appendix.
- Cleanly isolates the exact failure mode of prior work (global invertibility) and proposes a minimal, well-defined fix (local invertibility within regular J-classes).
- Layer-by-layer circuit story is coherent and matches the hypothesis to specific architectural components (embedding = partitioning, QK = routing, OV = low-rank projection, MLP = composition, unembedding = local-character readout).
- The atomic-factorization hypothesis gives a falsifiable, visually-demonstrated prediction about frequency reuse across composite J-classes.
- Reproducible: released code, standard architecture, multi-seed stability for the core low-dimensionality claim.

## Limitations

- **Correlational only:** no causal interventions (ablation, activation patching) to confirm the proposed circuits actually implement the computation; the authors explicitly flag this as planned future work.
- **Square-free moduli only:** the framework breaks for non-square-free n (nilpotent elements, non-regular J-classes), so ~39% of integers and the genuinely "one-way" collapse cases are unaddressed.
- **Weakest fits in the easiest regime:** R² is lowest (71.2%, 76.5%) on J_1 and J_3 — the unit group and a large regular class where the theory should be strongest — and the residual is unexplained.
- **Toy scale only:** a single 1-layer, 128-dim transformer; no evidence the stratified-Fourier mechanism appears in larger or more realistic models, despite the authors' analogy to token-sequence concatenation in LLMs.
- **Single seed for headline results:** Tables 2–3 and Figures 1–4 are seed-1-only; only the PCA-dimensionality claim has multi-seed support.
- **Incomplete MLP story:** the nonlinear composition step is only visualized, not reconstructed; a full neuron-level account is left to future work, leaving a gap in the middle of the proposed circuit.

## Relevance

与机制可解释性、表示论与算术泛化（grokking）的研究兴趣高度契合：本文把"群运算的傅里叶/表示机制"这一主线从可逆群推进到含零因子的幺半群，为理解模型如何在信息塌缩（不可逆）场景下分层路由与局部求逆提供了可直接借鉴的分析范式，对研究更大模型中的分层/模块化计算有方法论启发。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Would ablating the Head-0 block-diagonal directions or the per-class key-frequency subspaces causally destroy the corresponding J-class logits, as the routing hypothesis predicts?
- Why does the local-character fit *deteriorate* for the largest classes (J_1: 71.2%, J_3: 76.5%) — is it harmonic distortion, a secondary non-character mechanism, or an artifact of fitting a linear model to a nonlinear readout? Can a nonlinear extension close the gap?
- How does the network handle non-square-free moduli (e.g., n=12, n=18) where nilpotent elements create non-regular J-classes with no local group structure — does it fall back to memorization, or invent a different stratification?
- Do the atomic-factorization frequency-reuse patterns emerge *during* grokking in a compositional order (small atomic classes first, then composites), and can this be turned into a progress measure à la Nanda et al. (2023)?
- Does an analogous stratified/local-representation mechanism appear in larger models performing genuinely non-invertible compositions (e.g., sequence concatenation, set operations), or is it an artifact of the 1-layer toy regime?
