> **一句话总结：** 本文提出证书门控前缀接受（CGPA），一种面向硬约束序列决策的可认证推测执行契约：可信验证器精确拒绝违约转移、共形校准的值边界在每段后悔预算内门控最长低代价前缀、其余回退到可信求解器，从而将"安全性、后悔、速度"三者构造性解耦；在部署规模的机组组合实例上把冻结的 8B LLM 变成 2.96× 墙钟加速、仅 2.1% 后悔，且对六种异构 LLM 与对抗起草器均做到零"已执行违约"。

> **TL;DR:** CGPA is the first certified speculative-execution contract for hard-constrained sequential decision systems: a trusted verifier exactly rejects constraint-violating transitions, a conformally-calibrated learned value boundary gates the longest low-cost prefix of an untrusted AI agent's draft within a per-segment regret budget, and the rest defers to a trusted solver. It decouples safety/regret/speed by construction, drives every untrusted source (adversarial drafters + six frozen LLMs incl. a 12B model violating constraints in 98% of rollouts) to zero applied violations, and turns a frozen 8B LLM into a 2.96× wall-clock speedup at 2.1% regret on deployment-scale unit commitment.

## 核心贡献

- **提出证书门控前缀接受（CGPA）**：首个面向硬约束序列决策的可认证推测执行契约，将不可信 AI 草案（学习策略或冻结 LLM）的多步前缀安全地"摊销"掉可信求解器的逐步调用，而不丧失可行性保证。
- **构造性解耦"安全/后悔/速度"**：通过可信验证器（精确拒绝违约转移）+ 共形校准值边界（门控每段后悔预算内最长低代价前缀）+ 求解器回退三部分，使三者由结构决定，而非经验调节。
- **形式化后悔证书**：给出确定性后悔界 Certificate 2 —— `J_CGPA − J_F ≤ Σ τ_i|V(s_i)| + (2+τ_max)ε|A| + Σ ρ_j`，其中边界误差 ε 每个"接受段"只计一次（内部状态望远镜相消），并用分裂共形校准把 ε 从"假设"变成"测量量"（Prop 4 给出概率 ≥ 1−δ 的前向保证）。
- **证明三部分结构上必要**：去掉任何一个，CGPA 都退化为已知更弱基线（Prop 2：可行性门必须是精确、求解器拥有的，不能折叠进学习的值边界）。
- **鲁棒性证据强**：对抗起草器（含白盒自适应对手，掌握验证器、值边界、动力学、代价全知识）和六种异构冻结 LLM 全部被压到零"已执行违约"；一个直接 rollout 98% 违约的 12B 模型、一个 100% 违约的 E4B 模型均如此。
- **部署级结果**：机组组合（48 步，n=50）上 CGPA+Qwen3-8B 达 2.96× [2.01,4.31] 墙钟加速、0.021 后悔、零违约；50 个回合全部快于回退（最小 1.51×）；超越 hold 启发式（1.79×）与事件触发 MPC（1.07×/0.84×）。
- **"能力-吞吐"单调性**：通过控制质量旋钮，提案质量提升时调用削减单调上升（24%→90%，Spearman ρ=1.0），而参数量与吞吐不相关（ρ=−0.058）——即"代理越强，系统越快，保证不变"。

## Key Contributions

- **Certificate-Gated Prefix Acceptance (CGPA)**: the first certified speculative-execution contract for hard-constrained sequential decision systems, formalized as an operator `C = (D, Verify, V̂, π_F)` where only `(Verify, V̂, π_F)` is trusted and the proposal source `D` is arbitrarily untrusted (rule/learned/LLM).
- **Structural decoupling of safety, regret, speed**: safety is owned by the exact verifier (never queries `V̂`); regret is bounded per accepted segment; speed accrues at every accepted step. Remove any one of the three trusted-core parts and the contract collapses onto a known weaker baseline.
- **Telescoping per-segment regret certificate**: the boundary error ε enters only once per accepted segment (interior states telescope and cancel), so the regret price scales with the number of trusted-boundary crossings `|A|`, not the interior steps a segment covers.
- **Conformally-measured ε**: a five-quantile MLP boundary with a certificate-aware loss (pinball + mean-spread penalty) plus split-conformal calibration turns Assumption 2's ε into a measured, state-dependent band, yielding a forward high-probability guarantee (Proposition 4, `Pr ≥ 1−δ`).
- **Adversarial robustness to zero violations**: even a white-box adaptive adversary with full knowledge of the verifier, value boundary, dynamics, and cost cannot induce an applied violation — it can only erase amortization (call reduction → 0), not breach safety, because the feasibility gate never queries the value boundary the adversary attacks.
- **Capability-graduated throughput**: certified throughput tracks proposal *quality*, not parameter count (Spearman ρ=1.0 vs −0.058), so stronger untrusted sources directly speed up the certified system at unchanged guarantees.
- **Deployment-scale wall-clock amortization**: on MILP-backed unit commitment, a frozen 8B LLM with no domain-specific training yields a 2.96× per-episode speedup (2.02× aggregate) at 2.1% regret and zero violations, with every one of 50 episodes faster than fallback.

## Methodology

CGPA targets finite-horizon decision control: state `s_t`, action `a_t`, transition `f`, non-negative stage cost `c(s,a)≥0`, and hard feasibility constraints. A trusted oracle policy `π_F` (LP/MILP optimizer, MPC planner, or shield) returns feasible actions and supplies a safe repair on demand. The stepwise-fallback reference cost is `J_F(s_0) = Σ c(s_t, π_F(s_t))`.

**The accept/defer contract (Definition 1).** CGPA is a tuple `(D, Verify, V̂, π_F)`. At state `s_t`, the untrusted source proposes a length-K prefix `u_{0:K−1} = D(s_t, K)`. The trusted verifier simulates the prefix and stops at the first unsafe transition. Among verified-safe prefix lengths, CGPA commits the **longest** prefix satisfying the value guard:

> `g(s_t, u_{0:k−1}) + V̂(s_{t+k}) ≤ V̂(s_t) + τ|V̂(s_t)|`   (Eq. 3)

where `g` is the verified prefix cost and `τ` is the value tolerance (the per-segment regret-budget knob). If `k>0` it executes the accepted prefix; if `k=0` it defers, applying one verified oracle repair. Two invariants are maintained at every executed step: (I1) viability (every executed transition is verifier-admissible) and (I2) value descent (every accepted segment obeys a local descent condition on `V̂`).

**Three assumptions separate trusted from untrusted components.**
- A1 (Trusted oracle): at every reachable state `π_F` returns a feasible, verifier-admitted action; a safe repair always exists.
- A2 (Bounded boundary error): `|V̂(s) − V(s)| ≤ ε` at the endpoint states of accepted segments.
- A3 (Arbitrary proposal source): `D` is unconstrained — rule-based, learned, or LLM-based; CGPA makes no safety or optimality assumption on it.

**Safety and regret theory.** Proposition 1 (Applied safety): every transition CGPA applies is feasible for any proposal source. Proposition 2 (Exact feasibility gating): if feasibility is decided by an exact oracle-owned gate, safety holds for every source *and* every boundary error ε — so the feasibility gate cannot be folded into the learned value boundary (the decoupling is necessary, not stylistic). Certificate 2 gives the deterministic worst-case bound `J_CGPA − J_F ≤ Σ τ_i|V(s_i)| + (2+τ_max)ε|A| + Σ ρ_j`; Proposition 3 shows ε is charged once per accepted segment (interior telescoping). Proposition 4 lifts this to a high-probability forward guarantee via split-conformal calibration.

**Certificate-aware value boundary.** `V̂` is a learned five-quantile MLP `{V̂_0.1, V̂_0.3, V̂_0.5, V̂_0.7, V̂_0.9}`: the median drives the value guard, the spread `V̂_0.9 − V̂_0.1` estimates ε. The loss combines a pinball (quantile) coverage term with a mean-spread penalty `λ·E[V̂_0.9−V̂_0.1]` (λ=0.05; removing it inflates EMS regret 1.46×). Split-conformal calibration (nonconformity score `r_i = |y_i−V̂_0.5|/(V̂_0.9−V̂_0.1)`) yields a state-dependent band `[V̂_0.5(s) ± α(V̂_0.9−V̂_0.1)]`, achieving 0.816–0.853 (80% target) and 0.900–0.917 (90% target) empirical coverage. The band enters the guard pessimistically at both endpoints (Eq. 8). Crucially, because `V̂` only enters the value guard, a poor boundary can inflate regret but cannot induce a violation.

**Evaluation protocol.** Three domains spanning oracle cost: LP-backed battery EMS on UCI household loads (primary mechanism testbed), real-data CityLearn 2022 HVAC (calendar shift), and MILP-backed deployment-scale unit commitment (expensive oracle). The contract is held fixed; the untrusted proposal slot is the variable swept (forecast, learned MLP, end-to-end RL, adversarial drafters, six frozen LLMs: Qwen3-1.7B/4B/8B, Gemma-4-E2B/E4B/12B). Metrics: `R_rel = (J_CGPA−J_F)/max(|J_F|,10⁻¹²)`, its 95th percentile (p95), call reduction (relative drop in trusted-oracle invocations vs stepwise fallback), and applied-violation rate. CIs are non-parametric episode-level bootstraps. Each table fixes all but one part of the system (controlled frontier slicing).

## Results & Highlights

- **Zero applied violations everywhere.** Across adversarial drafters, end-to-end RL, and all six frozen LLMs, CGPA admits no constraint-violating transition — even for Gemma-4-12B (98% direct violations) and Gemma-4-E4B (100% direct violations).
- **White-box adversary cannot breach safety.** An adaptive adversary with full knowledge of the verifier, value boundary, dynamics, cost, and fallback drives raw execution to a breach in every episode, yet CGPA defers at every step: amortization collapses to zero, applied violations stay zero. A learned white-box MLP attacker (feasible mean regret 0.570) is still held to zero violations at certificate coverage 0.99.
- **Regret reduced three orders of magnitude.** On EMS (n=100): unguarded prefix acceptance = 0.4450 mean regret; certificate-aware learned guard = 0.0063 at 60.4% call reduction; conformal band gate = **0.0002** (95% CI [−0.005, 0.006], spanning zero). Verified envelope covers 93–100% of accepted segments.
- **Certified acceptance vs full-plan approximation.** Routed through a safety-augmented neural MPC baseline (Hose et al. 2023): full-plan acceptance = 0.0227 regret; CGPA prefix acceptance = 0.0081 (**2.8×**) at 86.6% call reduction — quantifying the per-segment `(2+τ_max)ε` pricing.
- **Distribution shift: the stepwise oracle is overtaken.** An OOD learned MLP drafter (trained one season, deployed 18 held-out days, 5 seeds) raises call reduction by +18.8pp (95% CI [+14.9,+22.1]) while lowering regret by 0.055, at zero violations; accepted prefixes beat the stepwise oracle's own cost on 15/18 days (72/90 runs) — versus the expected 50% in-distribution.
- **One contract, six heterogeneous LLMs.** (τ=0.08, n=50) Qwen3-8B direct regret 0.349 → CGPA 0.032 (10.94×); Qwen3-1.7B 0.421 → 0.068 (6.23×); Gemma-4-12B 20.38 → 0.032. The same contract absorbs a 1.7B and a 12B model alike.
- **Throughput tracks quality, not size.** Controlled quality sweep (q: random→oracle): call reduction 24%→90%, mean regret 0.031→0.002, Spearman ρ=1.0. Parameter count vs call reduction: ρ=−0.058 (non-predictive); accepted prefix length vs call reduction: ρ=1.000.
- **Deployment-scale wall-clock amortization.** Scaling per-call oracle cost on EMS lifts CGPA speedup from 1.63× to 2.67× while event-triggered/move-blocking controllers stay pinned near 1.0×. On deployment-scale UC (48-step, n=50): **CGPA+Qwen3-8B = 2.96× [2.01,4.31]** speedup, 64.3% call reduction, 0.021 regret, 0 violations, every episode faster than fallback (min 1.51×); CGPA-hold = 1.79×; event-triggered MPC = 1.07× (24-step) / 0.84× (48-step); warm-starting = 0.91×. A second 100-generator UC instance replicates at 2.09×.
- **Verifier-error robustness.** With the verifier's next-state estimate corrupted by bounded uniform noise across the full range, false-accept count is zero at every level — violations stay zero, error is absorbed as reduced amortization.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The synthesis is genuinely new: it explicitly bridges speculative decoding (LLM token acceleration) and speculative execution (hardware), but rebuilds verification from the ground up because a control draft must be checked against *two distinct objects* — hard feasibility *and* decision quality — that "cannot be the same." Treating LLMs/learned policies as untrusted accelerators inside a certified contract, with feasibility and value-quality decoupled, is a clean conceptual contribution that the paper defends with necessity arguments (Prop 2).
- **Rigor: High.** Claims are conservative and statistically framed. The headline regret number is paired with an explicit 95% CI spanning zero, and worst-case behavior is *not* ignored — Certificate 2 is a deterministic worst-case bound, and Prop 4 gives the high-probability conformal forward guarantee. p95 regret is reported alongside means (e.g., EMS conformal band p95 = 0.0465). Calibration coverage is measured (0.816–0.917), not assumed. Adversarial evaluation (white-box, full-knowledge) is a strong robustness signal.
- **Reproducibility: Moderate-to-Good.** Datasets are public (UCI household loads, CityLearn 2022, PyPSA UC). Hyperparameters, the τ frontier, λ sweep, and the LLM prompt protocol (shared template, JSON action lists, deterministic decoding) are documented in appendices. The main reproducibility gap is whether the certificate-aware five-quantile boundary and the conformal calibration code/artifacts are released; the learned component introduces training-data dependencies. (No code link is visible in the PDF.)
- **Significance: High, conditional on generality.** It offers a principled path to deploy fast-but-unreliable AI agents inside safety-critical, hard-constrained pipelines without sacrificing feasibility — a strategically important capability given the rise of LLM agents in operational settings. The "capability-graduated speedup" property is appealing because it aligns incentives with future model improvements.
- **Red flags / open questions.** (1) The "zero applied violations" guarantee rests *entirely* on the verifier being exact and complete over the constraint set; the MILP/LP feasibility check is sound here, but the guarantee does not extend to settings where verification itself is approximate or the constraint set is partially specified (the paper does test verifier-noise robustness and finds false-accept = 0, but this is empirical, not certified). (2) Evaluation is concentrated on energy/operations domains (EMS, HVAC, UC) with mostly linear/mixed-integer structure; behavior under continuous, nonlinear, or partially-observable constraints is unproven. (3) The speedup is fundamentally bounded by oracle invocation frequency — the paper quantifies this (MILP fallback dominates 97.96–99.98% of online time, drafting+verification+boundary ≤2.04%), which is honest, but it means CGPA's benefit vanishes in domains where the trusted oracle is cheap. (4) The "calendar shift overtakes oracle on 15/18 days" is suggestive but a small sample; the negative-regret sign (accepted prefixes cheaper than the oracle's own stepwise plan) is intriguing but its mechanism deserves scrutiny. (5) τ is a free hyperparameter selecting the operating point on a regret/amortization frontier — the paper shows this frontier is smooth and monotone, but the practical choice of τ remains a deployment decision.

## Strengths

- Clean, principled decoupling of safety, regret, and speed via a verifiable contract rather than an empirical heuristic, with necessity proofs showing each trusted-core part is required.
- A *measured* (not assumed) regret budget: conformal calibration makes ε a quantified, state-dependent quantity, and the worst-case certificate is paired with a high-probability forward guarantee.
- Strong adversarial robustness: a full-knowledge white-box adversary and a learned attacker both fail to produce a single applied violation; safety is owned by the verifier, which the adversary cannot attack through the value boundary.
- Honest, statistically careful reporting: 95% CIs, p95 regret, seed-disjoint calibration, an in-distribution 0.50 win-rate sanity check, and explicit quantification of the oracle-cost bottleneck.
- Practically compelling and reproducible-leaning result (public datasets, documented prompt protocol, second-instance replication at 2.09×).

## Limitations

- Feasibility guarantee is conditional on the verifier being exact and complete; it does not certify verifier soundness and does not directly extend to approximate/partial constraint sets (the verifier-noise result is empirical).
- Empirical scope is concentrated on energy-systems domains with linear/mixed-integer structure; generalization to richer constrained environments (continuous, nonlinear, partially observable) is unproven.
- The benefit is contingent on the trusted oracle being expensive: CGPA's amortization advantage vanishes when per-step oracle cost is low (event-triggered/move-blocking baselines match it at 1.0× there).
- The learned, conformally-calibrated value boundary introduces training-data and calibration-set dependencies (ncal=ntest=408); coverage is measured but finite-sample, and the boundary must presumably be retrained per problem class.
- τ is a deployment hyperparameter; the paper exposes the frontier but does not automate the safety-critical operating-point choice.
- No code/artifact release link is evident in the PDF, slightly lowering reproducibility of the learned-boundary and conformal-calibration pipeline.

## Relevance

该工作与硬约束序列决策、可认证安全 AI 代理部署以及 LLM agent 加速高度相关，对于希望在"利用不可信 AI 草案的算力"与"形式化安全/可行性保证"之间取得平衡的研究者具有直接参考价值；其"精确验证 + 共形校准值边界 + 求解器回退"的契约结构，以及将推测解码思想从 token 域迁移到执行决策域的做法，可迁移到智能体推理加速、约束生成、安全 RL shield 等场景。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the guarantee degrade when the verifier is itself approximate (e.g., a learned or relaxed feasibility check), and can a conservative surrogate recover a certified (if looser) bound rather than only the empirical false-accept=0 result?
- Does the certificate-aware boundary transfer across domains, or must it be retrained per problem class — and is there a meta-learning or zero-shot boundary variant?
- How does CGPA behave under continuous, nonlinear, or partially-observable constraints, beyond the (mixed-integer linear) structure of EMS/HVAC/UC?
- What is the precise mechanism by which accepted prefixes *beat* the stepwise oracle's own cost (negative regret) under distribution shift — is this a genuine multi-step advantage over myopic replanning, and is it stable or an artifact of the specific frontier?
- Can the "capability-graduated throughput" property (ρ=1.0 between quality and call reduction) be formalized as a predictive law relating a measurable proposal-quality metric to certified speedup?
- How sensitive is the conformal coverage (0.816–0.917) to calibration-set size and to drift between calibration and deployment distributions, and does a conditional/online conformal scheme improve it?
- Is there a principled, automated procedure for choosing τ in safety-critical deployment, rather than exposing it as a frontier to be hand-selected?
