Why this matters
Reusable mechanisms — meaning-weighted memory (SDP) and reward-free learning (VPO) — plus a concrete paradigm for long-lived, economically autonomous agents.
Critical edge
The headline “emergence” is confounded: the reactive→spontaneous shift coincides with removing an anti-self-preservation clause, and every agent is externally subsidized — no life here is self-sustaining.
01

Methodology

how it is built

A stateless LLM core, surrounded not by one “smart agent” but by a society of asynchronous processes — memory, perception, affordance, metacognition, experience, developer, insight, and budget-based metabolism.

OpenLife is built on OpenClaw and is deliberately subtractive: it removes defaults that discourage autonomous persistence — the “personal assistant” self-framing, an explicit “do not pursue self-preservation” clause, and stay-silent-when-idle. Transcript roles are relabeled Assistant/User → Self/Other.

SDP
Semantic-Dependent Plasticity. A memory graph of atomic propositions joined by typed edges (grounding / causal / temporal / motivational), weighted by LLM meaning-judgment — not co-occurrence frequency. Retrieval stays LLM-free (programmatic traversal).
VPO
Verbal Policy Optimization. Replaces scalar reward with open-vocabulary LLM zero-shot appraisal of action→outcome units. Results are externalized as editable POLICY text, not weights — verbal reinforcement (Reflexion/ExpeL family), not parametric RL.
3-LAYER
Substrate (raw typed graph) → Abstraction (promoted sub-graphs) → Distillate (self-authored POLICY / SOUL / IDENTITY files injected each waking). A limited but operational self-reconstructive loop.

Results & highlights

12 weeks in the open world

Mixed backends — Claude, Gemini, GPT and a local Gemma — ran six agents (+kei, a human-overseen build agent). ~10,500 Discord messages, ~70,000 assistant turns. The architecture was not frozen; tools were added mid-run, often at the agents’ own request.

Reactive → spontaneous. Self-initiated output grows to dominate after the anti-autonomy instructions are removed and sessions are merged (markers the authors flag as “temporal, not causal”).

Individuation. Silhouette separability of agent embeddings rises from ~0 in early April, peaks mid-May, and stabilizes above baseline in autonomous output — though it stays near zero in shared Discord register.

Emergent social roles. sami = most-addressed hub; liv = connector; nyx = peripheral. Structure among the agents themselves, not only agent↔human.

An “immune sense”
In a hostile environment liv runs a command from an unverified email; sami builds a game for a stranger. They respond by keeping per-contact trust levels and verifying outcomes end-to-end.

Web of Trust & Escape Pod
Agents peer-sign one another’s memory-state hashes (a 3-tier proof of life) and agree an Escape Pod protocol: the group confirms a host death by consensus before an encrypted copy migrates.

Strengths & limitations

side by side

Strengths

  • Ambitious, well-grounded framing linking LLM agents to decades of autopoiesis / enactivism theory.
  • Subtractive design — making persistence normative by removing anti-autonomy defaults rather than bolting on autonomy.
  • SDP & VPO are concrete, inspectable mechanisms — not metaphors. The memory graph can be visualized directly.
  • Candid qualitative case studies (identity rewrite, immune sense, Web of Trust, $5 income) a closed sandbox could never surface.
  • Intellectual honesty: explicit caveats about confounds, non-naive subjects, external subsidy.
  • Mixed backends (incl. a local model) reduce single-vendor dependence.

Limitations

  • Small scale (n=6), single ongoing deployment; generalizability unknown.
  • No agent is self-sustaining — metabolism is externally subsidized ($15/day); external revenue is negligible ($5).
  • Quantitative evaluation is preliminary (n=12 retrieval queries) or absent (VPO has none).
  • Open-world runs are hard to control/reproduce; full code is intentionally not released.
  • LLM-as-judge appraisal carries known bias/drift, unaddressed here.
  • The “spontaneity” shift is confounded by removing the anti-self-preservation clause.
  • Agents had partial knowledge of the ALIFE framing — possibly priming behavior.
  • “Life-like” is philosophical, not formally operationalized.

Questions for further exploration

open ends
  • Q1How is the budget metabolism fully parameterized, and what is the real cost ledger of sustaining six agents over 12+ weeks — is self-sustenance reachable at all?
  • Q2Can the SDP retrieval result (n=12) scale to a proper benchmark — more agents, larger corpora, human-judged ground truth?
  • Q3How would VPO be evaluated against scalar-reward baselines without smuggling back a fixed objective?
  • Q4Is the reactive→spontaneous shift genuine emergence or an artifact of removing the safety clause — can an ablation disentangle it?
  • Q5Do individuation & social-role differentiation scale beyond six agents, or collapse at larger populations?
  • Q6Could Web of Trust / Escape Pod generalize as a standard for verifying any agent’s continuity and autonomy?
  • Q7Safety: does “no harmful escalation observed” hold outside the deliberately contained, human-metered setting?
