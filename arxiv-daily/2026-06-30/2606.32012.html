CoMet: Context & Multiplicity Decomposition for Multimodal Uncertainty Estimation

Sanghyuk Chun  ·  William Yang  ·  Amaya Dharmasiri  ·  Olga Russakovsky  — Princeton Visual AI

uncertainty-estimation multimodal-LLM hallucination-detection Rényi-2 entropy post-hoc calibration generation-free
Key finding

CoMet splits multimodal-LLM uncertainty into context vs. multiplicity via a single matching variable — and estimates both from the (image, prompt) alone, no generation, no repeated sampling, while beating every baseline across 4 backbones and 3 task families.

Key metric

2.8–5.9×

faster than sampling-based baselines, under matched conditions.

And +0.100 avg AUROC over the strongest baseline (P(Correct)) on open-ended VQA, Qwen3VL-2B.

Why this matters

Uncertainty estimation underpins hallucination detection, abstention, and reliable QA. A generation-free, decomposable, interpretable signal lets cost-sensitive online MLLM services stay trustworthy without paying for repeated sampling.

Critical edge

The context term ut rests on a surrogate-LM approximation that violates its own uniform-assumption — patched with a λ knob, not fixed. And the headline gain is driven almost entirely by the multiplicity term: ut alone is near-useless (0.686).

Theoretically weakest link
01

Methodology — a two-term decomposition

STEP 1

The matching variable m

A binary m ∈ {0,1} marks whether answer y is semantically compatible with input x under context t. Working from the match-conditioned posterior πy = p(y|x,t,m=1) removes dependence on the decoder-/paraphrase-biased generative distribution pθ(y|x,t).

STEP 2

Rényi-2 entropy factorization

Because Y is open-ended, the authors use collision entropy. Uncertainty cleanly factors into ut = log Kt (how broad the plausible answer space is) and ux|t = −log(Var/Mean² + 1) (how many context-plausible answers still match the image).

STEP 3

Generation-free estimator

Three ingredients: MLLM-as-verifier for p(m=1|x,t,y) with affine (β,γ) calibration; a candidate-answer approximation of p(y|t) from greedy clustering of Cambrian instruction data; and lightweight post-hoc heads that regress ut, ux|t, and total uncertainty from (x,t) alone.

STEP 4

Experimental setup

Backbones: Qwen3VL-2B/4B/8B-Instruct, InternVL3.5-1B. 200k image–question pairs × 32 plausible answers. Hyperparams β=0.2, γ=−0.1, λ=0.25. Benchmarks: VQA v2 / VizWiz / OK-VQA, HallusionBench, MMMU / MMMU Pro / MMStar. Metric: AUROC / AUPR / FPR@95.

  • Top method on all three open-ended benchmarks, all four backbones — +0.135 AUROC over VL-Uncertainty, while generation-free.
  • Best or 2nd-best on every error/hallucination sub-benchmark — the decomposition transfers to unanswerability & hallucination, not just confidence ranking.
  • Highly competitive on MCQ despite an open-ended formulation; occasionally 2nd (InternVL3.5-1B), where sampling consistency wins — a real cost in closed-set settings.
  • Ablation (Tab. 2): both terms (.798) beat ux|t-only (.780) and ut-only (.686) — complementary signals, but multiplicity carries the weight.
  • Interpretable: “What are these animals?” → high ut (broad space) but low ux|t when the image clearly shows giraffes.
03

Critical assessment

Strengths

  • Principled, interpretable two-way decomposition with a clean graphical-model motivation.
  • Generation-free at inference — 2.8–5.9× faster than sampling baselines.
  • Strong, consistent wins across 4 backbones and 3 task families.
  • Decouples uncertainty from model-specific generation bias via the matching variable.
  • Public code and clearly documented hyperparameters (β, γ, λ).

Limitations

  • The ut term relies on a surrogate-LM approximation that violates its own uniform assumption; λ is a patch, not a fix.
  • Headline gain is driven mostly by multiplicity; the context term’s marginal value is modest.
  • Needs a per-target-benchmark adaptation step (900 MMMU + 10k VQA v2), tempering the “train-once” framing.
  • Candidate-answer construction via Cambrian clustering is heuristic; coverage of rare prompts is unclear.
  • Loses to simple sampling consistency on InternVL3.5-1B MCQ; long-form open-ended generation is unverified.
04

Open questions for further exploration

How robust is ut when the surrogate-LM p(y|t) is badly miscalibrated — does the λ patch scale to out-of-distribution prompts?
If ux|t dominates the gains, is there a simpler “multiplicity-only” variant retaining most performance with less machinery?
How does the MLLM-as-verifier matching probability behave on genuinely open-ended, long-form answers beyond short VQA?
Can the decomposed terms actively steer generation — abstention, targeted re-prompting, selective retrieval?
Does the Cambrian candidate-answer construction cover rare/specialized domains, and how does it degrade when it doesn’t?
How does CoMet transfer to non-visual modalities (audio, video, document, embodied agents) where multiplicity differs?
