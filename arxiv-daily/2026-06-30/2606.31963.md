> **一句话总结：** 本文证明 RMSNorm Transformer 残差流的天然离散规范是符号-置换群 $B_d = S_d \ltimes \{\pm 1\}^d$（而非仅 $S_d$），仅置换对齐对 RMSNorm 模型"对称性不完备"；作者提出符号边缘化匈牙利匹配，并沿同一基座的微调轨迹组合各检查点的局部 $B_d$ 规范做"坐标保持传输"，在跨运行坐标恢复（91.1% vs 60.3%）、SAE 重建、引导向量、LoRA 适配器与 AdamW 状态续训上全面超越仅置换方案。

> **TL;DR:** The residual-stream gauge of RMSNorm transformers is a *signed-permutation* symmetry ($B_d = S_d \ltimes \{\pm 1\}^d$, order $2^d d!$), not just a permutation. The authors prove a structural accuracy ceiling for naive signed-correlation matching, fix it with **sign-marginalized Hungarian matching**, and make **coordinate-preserving transport** (composing per-checkpoint local $B_d$ gauges along a same-base fine-tuning trajectory) the primary object — recovering 91.1% of cross-run coordinates vs. 60.3% for endpoint matching, and correctly transferring SAEs, steering vectors, LoRA adapters, merges, and AdamW optimizer state that permutation-only alignment silently destroys.

## 核心贡献
- **架构相关的规范刻画（Theorem 2.1）：** LayerNorm 残差图的离散规范是 $S_d$（至多一个全局符号翻转 $S_d \times C_2$）；带通用逐通道增益 $\gamma$ 的 RMSNorm 是完整的 $B_d = S_d \ltimes \{\pm 1\}^d$，且 $B_d$ 是不把 $\gamma$ 吸收到相邻权重的前提下 $O(d)$ 中"保持稀疏坐标身份"的极大子群。
- **符号遗漏的定量代价（Corollary 2.4）：** 在 RMSNorm 规范 $G=PS$ 下，仅用 $S_d$ 传输学习到的权重更新 $\Delta W$，其 Frobenius 平方误差期望为 $2\|\Delta W\|_F^2$——与更新自身能量同阶；Qwen2.5-1.5B SST-2 LoRA 实测中 $S_d$ 把精度压到 0.523（≈未适配基座 0.525），$B_d$ 恢复到 0.944。
- **结构化精度上限与修复（Theorem 3.1）：** 在去相关源坐标下，原始符号相关匹配的置换精度上限恰为"真规范的正符号比例"（iid 符号下期望 $1/2 + O(d^{2-d})$）；**符号边缘化**匹配 $\pi^\star = \arg\max_\pi \sum_i |C_{i,\pi(i)}|$ 消除该阻塞，在 7B/8B 上从 ~49.4% 提升到 100%。
- **坐标保持传输作为首要对象：** 沿同一基座轨迹组合各检查点局部 $B_d$ 规范（1500 步 = 750 个局部匹配的复合），跨运行恢复 91.1% 残差坐标，端点匹配仅 60.3%；关键反例：**端点经基座复合只有 11.4%**，证明增益来自"许多高边距的小局部规范"而非简单引用基座。
- **统一的 READ/WRITE 传输规则表（Table 1）：** 给出转向向量、缓存残差、线性探针方向、SAE 编/解码器、LoRA READ/WRITE 因子、AdamW 一阶/二阶矩的 $B_d$ 传输规则；并在 Table 5 跨四类工件实证 $B_d$ 保住而 $S_d$ 破坏。
- **有状态训练的规范协变（Table 6, Remark 4.3）：** AdamW 原生对角二阶矩存储下，$B_d$ 是保持 gauge 协变的极大子群；500+500 步 fp32 续训中 $B_d$ 保持参考轨迹到数值容差，$S_d$ 使 held-out logit 相对 MSE 偏离 $6.6\times10^6$ 倍。
- **规范扫描审计（§6, Table 8, Proposition 6.1）：** 对 BERT 知识神经元、Llama-2 拒绝方向、CAA 引导向量、梯度×激活归因施加精确规范，原始索引不可复现但行为/不变量不变——索引级可解释性结论只有相对显式规范才成立。

## Key Contributions
- **Architecture-dependent native gauge (Thm 2.1):** LayerNorm residual charts have permutation gauge $S_d$ (up to a single global sign flip $S_d \times C_2$); RMSNorm with generic per-channel gain $\gamma$ has the full signed-permutation gauge $B_d = S_d \ltimes \{\pm 1\}^d$, and $B_d$ is the *maximal* subgroup of $O(d)$ that preserves sparse coordinate identity without absorbing $\gamma$ into neighboring weights.
- **Quantitative cost of sign omission (Cor 2.4):** Under an RMSNorm gauge $G=PS$, transporting a learned update $\Delta W$ with permutation-only ($S_d$) transport incurs expected Frobenius error $2\|\Delta W\|_F^2$ — same order as the update's energy. Empirically: a Qwen2.5-1.5B SST-2 LoRA collapses to 0.523 (≈ unadapted base 0.525) under $S_d$ vs. 0.944 under $B_d$.
- **Structural ceiling + fix (Thm 3.1):** With decorrelated source coordinates, raw signed-correlation matching has a permutation-accuracy ceiling equal to the positive-sign fraction of the true gauge ($1/2 + O(d^{2-d})$ for iid signs). **Sign-marginalized** matching ($\pi^\star = \arg\max_\pi \sum_i |C_{i,\pi(i)}|$, then sign from the matched correlation) removes the obstruction, recovering 100% on 7B/8B RMSNorm models vs. ~49.4%.
- **Coordinate-preserving transport as primary object (§4):** Composing saved-checkpoint local $B_d$ gauges along a same-base fine-tuning trajectory (1500 steps = 750 local matches) recovers 91.1% of cross-run residual coordinates vs. 60.3% for endpoint matching. The crucial ablation: *endpoint-via-base* composition gives only 11.4% — the gain comes from many small high-margin local gauges, not from merely referencing the base.
- **Unified READ/WRITE transport table (Table 1):** Explicit $B_d$ transport rules for steering vectors, cached residuals, linear-probe directions, SAE encoders/decoders, LoRA READ/WRITE factors, and AdamW first/second moments; Table 5 validates the pattern across four artifact classes.
- **Stateful-training gauge covariance (Table 6, Remark 4.3):** Given AdamW's native diagonal second-moment storage, $B_d$ is the maximal subgroup under which optimizer state is gauge-covariant. After 500 warmup + 500 resumed fp32 steps, $B_d$ transport preserves the reference trajectory to numerical tolerance while $S_d$ diverges by a $6.6\times10^6$ factor in held-out logit relative MSE.
- **Gauge-sweep audits (§6, Table 8, Prop 6.1):** Applying an exact gauge to BERT knowledge neurons, Llama-2 refusal directions, CAA steering vectors, and gradient×activation attribution shows raw index names are *not* reproducible while behavior/invariant quantities are unchanged — index-level interpretability claims are reproducible only relative to an explicit gauge.

## Methodology
**1. Gauge derivation (Theorem 2.1).** Using row-vector residuals $H \in \mathbb{R}^{n\times d}$ gauged by right multiplication $H \mapsto HG$, the authors derive the function-preserving discrete gauge by checking each module. Linear maps admit any orthogonal gauge under READ ($W \mapsto WG$) and WRITE ($W \mapsto G^\top W$, $b \mapsto bG$) weight transforms; attention Q/K/V are invariant, only the output projection writes. Normalization then restricts $G$: RMSNorm is norm-invariant but its diagonal gain requires $G^\top \mathrm{diag}(\gamma) G$ to stay diagonal, which for generic $\gamma$ forces $G$ to be a signed permutation; LayerNorm is permutation-invariant and excludes independent signs (only a global $-P$ with $\beta \mapsto -\beta P$ survives). Induction over blocks gives the global result (proofs in Appendices B, F). Gauge invariance is validated empirically (Table 2): RMSNorm models (Qwen2.5-7B, Llama-3.1-8B, TinyLlama) pass $B_d$ gauges with max logit error $\sim10^{-5}$–$10^{-4}$; BERT/ViT LayerNorm pass only $S_d$; T5 passes $B_d$.

**2. Sign-marginalized recovery (§3).** Given paired probe activations $H_s, H_t$, center columns and form $C = H_s^\top H_t$. Minimizing $\sum_i \|H_s[:,i] - s_i H_t[:,\pi(i)]\|^2$ reduces to $\pi^\star = \arg\max_\pi \sum_i |C_{i,\pi(i)}|$ then $s_i^\star = \mathrm{sign}(C_{i,\pi^\star(i)})$ — a standard linear assignment on $-|C|$, solved with SciPy's `linear_sum_assignment` (Jonker–Volgenant). The recovery action is $G_\text{rec} = P_\pi^\top S_s = G_\text{fwd}^{-1}$. Theorem 3.1 proves the structural ceiling for naive signed matching in the decorrelated population case.

**3. Parallel transport (§4).** For a trajectory $t_0,\dots,t_T$, estimate each local $G_{t_k \to t_{k+1}}$ by sign-marginalized matching, then $G_{0\to T} = G_{t_0\to t_1} \cdots G_{t_{T-1}\to t_T}$. For two runs $a,b$ sharing a base, compose through the base: $\hat{G}_{a\to b} = (G^{(a)}_{0\to T})^{-1} G^{(b)}_{0\to T}$. Propositions 4.1 (no canonical function-level transport between independent endpoints) and 4.2 (atlas consistency ⟺ zero holonomy / cocycle condition) formalize why same-base trajectory transport is well-posed: the shared base breaks the gauge ambiguity and the trajectory forms a tree on which the cocycle holds trivially.

**4. Evaluation harness.** A gauge-instrumented Qwen2.5-1.5B fine-tuning benchmark: a random $B_d$ gauge is applied at step 0 with its correspondence recorded as ground truth, then real fine-tuning proceeds; checkpoints saved every 2 optimizer steps (750 local matches over 1500 steps). Probe budget regimes summarized in Table 3 (22–500 tokens for instrumented/trajectory settings; ~36K saturated tokens for natural cross-model stability).

## Critical Assessment
- **Novelty (High):** The clean identification of the *signed* part of the RMSNorm gauge — plus the *proven* accuracy ceiling tied to the positive-sign fraction — reframes a known practical failure of alignment on RMSNorm models as a symmetry-completeness gap. This is non-obvious and well-articulated. The distinction between *native coordinate-preserving* gauge ($B_d$) and *absorbed* dense orthogonal freedom (compression/fusion) is a useful boundary clarification against recent rotation-based fusion work.
- **Rigor (Strong):** Claims are theorem-backed (Thm 2.1 maximal gauge; Thm 2.2 discrete content of orthogonal maps; Thm 3.1 ceiling; Cor 2.4 quantitative error; Props 4.1/4.2/6.1). Empirical probes are targeted and each prediction has a confirming experiment. The endpoint-via-base ablation (11.4%) is exactly the right control to rule out the "you just used the base" confound.
- **Reproducibility (Good, with caveats):** Concrete models, standard metrics, SciPy-based assignment. *However*, the headline 91.1% depends on a **gauge-instrumented benchmark** (a known random gauge applied at step 0) — this is a *controlled* recovery test, not natural alignment between two independently trained models. Natural cross-model results are reported only as "stability against a large-probe reference," not exact accuracy. The PDF does not obviously advertise public code/release.
- **Significance (High for the niche):** Any workflow transporting coordinate-indexed objects (SAEs, steering vectors, neuron interpretations, LoRA, optimizer checkpoints) across RMSNorm models is affected. The gauge-sweep audit is a genuinely useful falsifiability tool for interpretability reproducibility.
- **Red flags / open issues:**
  1. **Single author** — independent verification of the gauge-theoretic proofs matters.
  2. **Instrumented benchmark vs. real alignment:** the strongest numbers (91.1%, 100% recovery) come from settings with a *known recorded gauge*; real-world cross-run/cross-model transport without a shared dense-checkpoint trajectory is much harder and underreported.
  3. **Decorrelated-coordinate assumption** for Thm 3.1 — real activations are correlated; the paper notes correlations change the *exact* ceiling but not the sign-marginalization requirement, yet the gap between theory and practice is not quantified on correlated data.
  4. **FFN transport instability** over long horizons is acknowledged but not deeply analyzed.
  5. The AdamW experiment restricts *trainable scope* to keep optimizer state tractable — generalization to full-model fine-tuning is unproven.

## Strengths
- Reframes a practical alignment failure as a precise group-theoretic symmetry gap, with a *provable* ceiling and a clean fix.
- The endpoint-via-base ablation is a model of good experimental hygiene — it isolates the mechanism.
- Validates across diverse artifacts (SAEs, steering, LoRA, merges, optimizer state), not a single task.
- The READ/WRITE transport table (Table 1) is a genuinely reusable artifact for practitioners.
- Honest scoping: explicitly says behavior-level interpretability conclusions are *not* invalidated; only coordinate-indexed claims are gauge-relative.

## Limitations
- **Instrumented benchmark dependency:** headline recovery numbers rely on a known applied gauge and dense checkpoint trajectory; aligning two independently published weights without a shared atlas remains an open, harder problem.
- **Dense-checkpoint requirement:** trajectory transport needs checkpoints every ~2 steps — often unavailable for public models.
- **Decorrelated-coordinate ceiling:** the sharp theoretical bound assumes uncorrelated coordinates; real correlated activations are only qualitatively addressed.
- **FFN transport** is less stable over long horizons (Appendix J), limiting applicability beyond residual-stream coordinates.
- **Scope of optimizer-state test:** trainable parameters were restricted to keep AdamW state tractable; full-model continuation is unverified.
- **Scale:** experiments top out at 8B; frontier-scale behavior (where these workflows matter most) is untested.
- **Single-author** mathematical claims would benefit from independent peer review.

## Relevance
对从事可解释性工具（SAE、引导向量、神经元归因、稀疏消融）跨检查点迁移、模型合并与检查点续训的研究高度相关：它从规范理论角度解释了"为何 RMSNorm 模型上仅置换对齐会系统性失败"，并给出可立即落地的符号边缘化匹配与轨迹传输方法，直接关系到索引级可解释性结论的可复现性与跨运行工具迁移的正确性。

## Questions for Further Exploration
- How does $B_d$ transport degrade when intermediate checkpoints are sparse or unavailable — e.g., aligning two independently released RMSNorm weights with no shared atlas?
- Does the Theorem 3.1 ceiling tighten or loosen measurably on real *correlated* activations at frontier scale?
- Can the signed-permutation gauge framework extend to other normalization schemes (QK-Norm, DeepNorm, ScaleNorm) or to models with tied/repeated $\gamma$ values that admit intra-subspace rotations?
- How does sign-marginalization behave near-degenerate matches where the true gauge has ~balanced signs and correlations are weak?
- For *task* merging (not just coordinate-preserving merges), does $B_d$-aware alignment improve downstream task metrics beyond barrier reduction?
- Are published SAE-feature / steering-coordinate / knowledge-neuron findings in the literature still reproducible once a gauge-sweep audit is applied — and how many should be re-reported with an explicit gauge anchor?
- Does the FFN transport instability over long horizons reflect a genuine gauge drift, or an artifact of the local-matching probe budget?
