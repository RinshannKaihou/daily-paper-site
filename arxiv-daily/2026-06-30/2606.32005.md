> **一句话总结：** 本文首次严格证明，在光滑凸优化（有限和）问题中，随机重排（Random Reshuffling, RR）在任意合理步长（η ≲ 1/L̂，不再要求 ≲ 1/n）、任意有限 epoch 数之后都收敛得不慢于、并在某些区间严格优于经典 SGD，彻底解决了困扰该领域十余年的理论与实际脱节难题。

> **TL;DR:** In a COLT 2026 paper, Zijian Liu proves—for the first time—that Random Reshuffling (RR) dominates standard with-replacement SGD in smooth convex finite-sum optimization under *any* reasonable stepsize (η ≲ 1/L̂) after *any* finite number of epochs, using a novel "virtual-sequence + co-coercivity" analysis that drops the long-standing η ≲ 1/n stepsize restriction and avoids the Lipschitz/Wasserstein assumptions of prior work.

## 核心贡献

- **首次证明 RR 无条件（在合理步长下）优于 SGD**：主定理给出 RR 在 η ≲ 1/L̂ 任意步长下的收敛速率，首次允许 η ≳ 1/n 这一长期空白区间，并严格证明该速率不劣于、且在 η ≲ 1/(nL̄) 时严格优于 SGD。
- **打破两条长期瓶颈**：(1) 移除了所有先前 RR 理论对步长 η ≲ 1/n（甚至 η ≲ 1/(n√(L̄L̂))）的要求；(2) 移除了"epoch 数 K 必须大于正比于 n 的阈值 RR 才优于 SGD"的限制。
- **改进最优调参速率**：将 RR 的最优调参收敛界改进为对任意有限 K 都优于 SGD，且严格优于的阈值从 nL̄L̂D²/σ⋆² 降低到 nL̄D²/σ⋆²；在共极小点情形（σ⋆²=0，即所有 fi 共享最优解）下速率提升 n 倍。
- **创新证明技术**：借助 Sherman et al. (2021) 引入的"虚拟/耦合序列"与 co-coercivity 性质，建立新不等式（Lemma 5），用随机梯度二阶矩刻画 E[f(x)] 与 E[fπi(x)] 的差异，从而**无需 Nagaraj et al. (2019) 的 Lipschitz 假设与 Wasserstein 距离论证**（加入该假设可恢复其引理）。
- **更强的基础假设**：采用非均匀光滑性（每个 fi 为 Li-光滑），且**不施加任何梯度方差有界假设**，比多数既有文献更贴近实际。

## Key Contributions

- **First dominance result without restrictions.** The main theorem gives a convergence rate for RR valid for *any* reasonable stepsize η ≲ 1/L̂—the first analysis to permit the practically relevant regime η ≳ 1/n—and shows it is never worse than, and strictly better than SGD when η ≲ 1/(nL̄).
- **Eliminates both long-standing bottlenecks** of prior RR theory: (i) the η ≲ 1/n (indeed η ≲ 1/(n√(L̄L̂))) stepsize ceiling, and (ii) the requirement that the epoch count K exceed a threshold proportional to n before RR can beat SGD.
- **Improved optimally-tuned rate (Corollary 1).** RR's optimally tuned bound now dominates SGD's for *any* finite K, with the strict-beating threshold lowered from nL̄L̂D²/σ⋆² to nL̄D²/σ⋆². In the shared-minimizer case (σ⋆² = 0) the rate improves by a factor of n over the prior best RR bound.
- **Novel proof machinery.** Combines the "virtual/coupled sequence" technique (Sherman et al., 2021) with the co-coercivity of smooth convex functions. A new inequality (Lemma 5) bounds the gap between E[f(x)] and E[fπi(x)] by the second moment of stochastic gradients—stronger than Nagaraj et al. (2019)'s Wasserstein-distance bound, which it recovers when an extra Lipschitz condition is imposed. No Lipschitz or bounded-variance assumptions are required.
- **More general assumptions.** Per-component smoothness (each fi is Li-smooth) is allowed, and no finite-variance condition on individual vs. full gradients is imposed.

## Methodology

**Problem.** Minimize a finite-sum convex objective f(x) = (1/n)Σᵢ fᵢ(x) over ℝᵈ.

**Assumptions (standard, minimal).**
- *A1 (Minimizer):* a minimizer x⋆ exists.
- *A2 (Convexity):* each fᵢ is convex.
- *A3 (Smoothness):* each fᵢ is Lᵢ-smooth (nonuniform). Define L̄ = mean smoothness, L̂ = max smoothness, and σ⋆² = (1/n)Σᵢ ‖∇fᵢ(x⋆)‖² (gradient variance at the minimizer).
- Notably, **no** bounded-variance or Lipschitz-gradient assumptions are imposed.

**Algorithm.** Shuffling SGD (Algorithm 1): split into K epochs; in each epoch generate a permutation πk of [n] and run n gradient steps in that order with per-epoch stepsize ηk. *Random Reshuffling (RR)* = πk drawn independently and uniformly from the symmetric group Sₙ each epoch (contrast: Single Shuffling, one fixed π; Incremental Gradient, deterministic π). Output is the weighted average x̄K.

**Main theorem (Theorem 1).** Under A1–A3, RR with ηk ≤ 1/(6L̂) satisfies
  E[f(x̄K) − f⋆] ≤ 6‖x¹₁ − x⋆‖² / (n·Σηk) + 51·min{(Σηk³)/(Σηk)·nL̄, (Σηk²)/(Σηk)}·σ⋆².

**Structure of the proof (the intellectual core).** Theorem 1 is obtained by combining two independent bounds:

1. **Bound I — "Never worse than SGD" (Theorem 2):** Gives the SGD-matching rate ‖x¹₁−x⋆‖²/(2nΣηk) + 51(Σηk²)/(Σηk)·σ⋆² for any ηk ≤ 1/(6L̂). This is the first multi-epoch RR rate that converges under any reasonable stepsize without extra assumptions. (The only prior result converging for η ≳ 1/n—Nagaraj et al., 2019—additionally required each fᵢ to be Lipschitz, excluding even quadratic problems.)

2. **Bound II — "Strictly better when stepsize is small" (Theorem 3):** An alternative rate that is provably faster than SGD when the stepsize is sufficiently small.

**Two core lemmas** drive Bound I:
- *Lemma 2* (built on Sherman et al., 2021): relates the hard-to-control term E[f(xik) − fπik(xik)] to a quantity involving the **virtual sequence** xlk(i,j)—the trajectory of the same epoch run under the permutation πk with elements πi and πj swapped.
- *Lemma 3*: bounds the distance between the virtual iterate and the true iterate using **co-coercivity** (Lemma 1), requiring only the standard ηk ≤ 2/L̂.

**Lemma 5 (key novelty):** A new inequality bounding the gap between E[f(xik)] and E[fπik(xik)] by the second moment of stochastic gradients up to time i, with a crucial coefficient ηk/n that lets per-epoch error accumulate controllably. It strictly improves Nagaraj et al. (2019)'s Wasserstein-based bound (which needed Lipschitz continuity). The per-iterate-progress identity (Lemma 4) plus co-coercivity then telescopes into Theorem 2.

The author notes constants are not optimized and the proof is currently split into two parts rather than unified.

## Results & Highlights

- **Theorem 1 (headline):** RR converges at the stated rate for *any* reasonable stepsize ηk ≲ 1/L̂ and *never* worse than SGD; strictly better when ηk ≲ 1/(nL̄). This resolves the longstanding open question: *"Does RR dominate SGD without these two restrictions?"*
- **Corollary 1 (optimally tuned):** With optimally tuned constant stepsize, RR's bound is L̂D²/(nK) + min{σ⋆D/(√(nK)), (L̄σ⋆²D⁴/(nK²))^{1/3}}, dominating SGD's L̂D²/(nK) + σ⋆D/(nK) for every finite K and strictly beating it once K > nL̄D²/σ⋆² (threshold lowered from the prior nL̄L̂D²/σ⋆²).
- **Shared-minimizer case (σ⋆² = 0):** RR achieves L̂D²/(nK), identical to SGD and an **n-fold improvement** over the prior best RR bound L̄L̂D²/K.
- A detailed regime-by-regime comparison (Order functions) partitions the (K) axis into four regions and shows OrderNewRR(K) ≤ OrderSGD(K) throughout, with strict dominance in the relevant regimes—unlike the old RR bound, which is slower than SGD for small K.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** This is not an incremental extension. All prior RR convergence rates for smooth convex optimization required the stepsize to be O(1/n) (or smaller), leaving the entire practical regime η = O(1) unjustified. Permitting η ≲ 1/L̂ and simultaneously showing dominance over SGD after any finite number of epochs is a genuine resolution of a named open problem. The new Lemma 5 (replacing the Wasserstein argument and dropping Lipschitz) is a non-trivial technical innovation.

- **Rigor: Strong, with caveats worth checking.** COLT review provides external scrutiny of the proofs, and the analysis is built from cleanly separated lemmas (Lemmas 2–5) whose assumptions are explicit. Caveats: (a) the author states constants are *not* optimized ("We make no effort to optimize the constants"), so the leading constants (6, 51, etc.) are loose; (b) "dominates" is an *upper-bound* comparison—the paper does not prove matching lower bounds; (c) results are in expectation (averaged iterate), not high probability or last-iterate.

- **Reproducibility: Theory-only.** There is nothing to run—no code or experiments. Verifiability rests entirely on the proofs, which are available in the main text and appendices. This is appropriate for the venue and claim type.

- **Significance: Solid for the optimization/ML-theory community.** It closes a well-known theory–practice gap for the *de facto* default implementation of SGD. Practical impact is limited in the sense that practitioners already use RR by default—the contribution explains rather than changes practice. Its main downstream value is as a foundation for: (i) extending the dominance result to nonconvex/deep-learning settings, and (ii) tighter lower bounds.

- **Red flags: None material.** Minor: single-author claim of a strong dominance result (mitigated by COLT peer review); the two-part proof is acknowledged as non-unified; no lower bound matching the new upper bound exists. None of these undermine the central claims.

## Strengths

- Cleanly resolves a named, longstanding open question with a precise affirmative answer.
- Removes two restrictive conditions simultaneously (stepsize ceiling and epoch-count threshold).
- Drops the Lipschitz assumption and Wasserstein machinery used by the most related prior work, broadening applicability (e.g., to quadratics).
- More general problem setup: nonuniform smoothness, no bounded-variance assumption.
- Clear proof architecture (two complementary bounds combined into one), with core lemmas stated up front.
- Published at COLT 2026—vetted by learning-theory experts.

## Limitations

- **Convex-only.** The dominance result does *not* cover the nonconvex regime most relevant to deep learning; that remains open.
- **In-expectation, averaged iterate.** Guarantees are on E[f(x̄K) − f⋆], not last-iterate or high-probability bounds.
- **Upper-bound dominance only.** "Dominates" is established via upper bounds; a matching lower bound for RR under any reasonable stepsize/epoch count is explicitly flagged as future work (the only existing lower bound, Cha et al. 2023, holds for η ≲ 1/n and K ≳ n).
- **Unoptimized constants; split proof.** Author acknowledges constants are loose and the proof is not unified.
- **No experiments.** Pure theory; no empirical confirmation of the predicted regimes (acceptable for the venue, but limits immediate practical takeaways).
- **Practitioner impact is confirmatory.** RR is already the standard; the paper justifies rather than changes deployment.

## Relevance

与大规模机器学习训练优化高度相关：本文从理论上厘清了实践中默认使用的随机重排（RR）策略为何在光滑凸优化下严格优于经典 SGD，对训练算法的步长选择与收敛行为分析具有直接指导意义，也为后续向非凸/深度学习设定扩展提供了理论基石。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Can the dominance result be extended to **nonconvex smooth** objectives (the deep-learning regime), at least under a PL/Kurdyka-Łojasiewicz or gradient-domination condition?
- Is there a **matching lower bound** for RR under η ≲ 1/L̂ and arbitrary finite K, confirming the new rate is information-theoretically tight among shuffling schemes?
- Can the two-part proof be **unified** into a single analysis (an open direction the author explicitly raises)?
- How do the (loose) constants 6/51 tighten under sharper analysis, and do the predicted regime boundaries align with empirical RR-vs-SGD behavior?
- Does the virtual-sequence + co-coercivity technique (Lemma 5) transfer to **last-iterate** or **high-probability** guarantees, or to **variance-reduced** shuffling methods?
