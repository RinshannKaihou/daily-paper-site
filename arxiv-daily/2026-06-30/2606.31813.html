01

Methodology

Theory-guided init, not empirical search

The setup. LoRA factorizes a weight update as W = W₀ + BA, rank r ≪ min(m,n). The question the paper attacks: how do you initialize A and B so RLVR (with its implicit KL trust-region) doesn't blow up?

Why RLVR ≠ SFT. DAPO/GRPO clip an importance ratio that implicitly bounds D_KL(π_θ+ ‖ π_θ). Breach it and the surrogate stops being a valid lower bound → reward collapse. Sampled KL becomes the stability vital sign.

The two-factor diagnosis (Sec. 4). Controlled ablations against OLoRA (top subspace, no scaling) and OLoRA-tail (bottom, no scaling) isolate the culprits behind PiSSA/MiLoRA failure:

// Thm 4.1 — PiSSA's first-step update is inflated:
‖ΔW₁PiSSA‖F / ‖ΔW₁OLoRA‖F ≥ σ_r

// Thm 4.2 — KL budget ⇒ hard weight bound:
‖ΔW‖F ≲ √(2K / μ)  // scaling breaches this

Core theorem (5.2). Under L-smoothness + clipped bounded gradients, the LoRA-to-full-FT gap decomposes and is governed by a single term minimized exactly when A₀ has orthonormal rows:

‖W_TLoRA − W_Tfull‖F ≤ (Mη/T)·Γ_T(η)·‖I_n − A₀ᵀA₀‖₂ + O(η²)
// ‖I − A₀ᵀA₀‖₂ ≥ 1, with equality ⟺ A₀ orthonormal

Prop. 5.3 adds the first step then never exceeds full-FT: ‖ΔW₁‖_F ≤ η‖G₀‖_F.

Proposed schemes · both B₀ = 0

SVD-derived, geometry-preserving, scaling-free. No residual re-parameterization, no nonzero B₀.

LoRA-RLPO
A₀ = V_rᵀ
principal right singular vectors — best overall; geometry adds a real gain.
LoRA-RLMO
A₀ = V₋rᵀ
minor / tail singular vectors — ≈ model-agnostic orthonormal baselines.

Ablation isolators

  • DCT-LoRA / Wavelet-LoRA — model-agnostic orthonormal A₀: prove orthonormality alone already beats standard LoRA.
  • OLoRA / OLoRA-tail — same subspace, scaling stripped: isolate the singular-value-scaling failure mode.
  • Cost: one-time SVD ≈ 3.5 min / 16 GiB on a single A100 at 7B — negligible.

Strengths & Limitations

side-by-side verdict

Strengths

  • Theory-guided, not search. Theorems directly motivate the design; no hyperparameter witch-hunt.
  • Explains, doesn't just observe. A falsifiable two-factor story (subspace geometry + singular-value scaling) with controlled isolators.
  • Drop-in & cheap. One-line init change; SVD is one-time, minutes on a single A100.
  • Broad validation. 1.5B/3B/7B · Qwen/Llama/DeepSeek-distill · math + code · RLVR + SFT.
  • Intellectually honest. Open about the OLoRA relationship; Appendix D.1 directly contests Yin et al. 2025's near-zero-init claim (measured ‖B₀A₀‖=5.11).

Limitations

  • Methods overlap prior art. RLPO ≈ OLoRA, RLMO ≈ OLoRA-tail — novelty is the RLVR theory, not the algorithms.
  • Theory is local / first-order. Thm 5.2 governs the early-time gap under L-smoothness + clipping; deep non-convex guarantees unproven.
  • Modest margins over stable LoRA. 62.4 → 65.0 at 1.5B; the eye-catching table contrast is largely PiSSA's collapse.
  • Narrow domains. Math-centric; code-gen is a single small table. No tool-use / agentic / multi-turn RLVR.
  • Limited rank sweep. Only r=16, r=32 — very-low (r=4) and high-rank behavior unreported.
  • Asymmetric "geometry." Clear win for RLPO; for RLMO, geometry adds nothing over a cheap random orthonormal A₀.
04

Open Questions

non-linear jumping-off points
1

Does the RLPO edge hold at extreme ranks (r=4) and across PPO / RLOO / ReMax beyond DAPO/GRPO?

2

Is Thm 5.2's minimal-gap result robust deep into training, or only early dynamics? Can it extend to a finite-horizon / convergence guarantee?

3

Since Gaussian A₀ is already approximately orthonormal, can a cheap Gaussian + one Gram–Schmidt pass replace the full SVD?

4

Does the two-factor failure story predict behavior of DoRA / AdaLoRA / rsLoRA under RLVR?

5

Does the analysis extend to multi-turn / agentic RLVR and tool-use rewards where the KL leash behaves differently?

6

If RLMO ≈ random orthonormal baselines, is the minor-subspace SVD ever worth the cost — or just use a cheap random A₀?
