2 Why this matters

A cheap, architecture-agnostic diagnostic that turns "how robust is this model?" into a curve and a single comparable index — usable inside any training workflow. Caveat: it measures random noise only, not adversarial attack.

3 Critical edge

Random ≠ adversarial.

The "upper bound" is an empirical sample quantile, not a PAC guarantee — variance vs. sample count m is unanalysed, and the method is compared against no baseline whatsoever.

Methodology & Results

The pipeline

For a trained network, perturb each feature by scaled Gaussian noise ε ~ N(0,(σ·σᵢ)²), σ∈[0,1]. Define θ-robustness as the smallest θ holding with probability 1−α:

ρ(σ,α,NN) = min{ θ : P(MSE_ε(NN) ≤ θ) ≥ 1−α }

Algorithm 1 draws m≈1000 noise samples, computes MSE each time, returns the empirical (1−α)-quantile. Fully black-box — needs no weights, gradients, or architecture.

Classification uses the multi-class Brier score (MBS) in place of MSE.

Robustness curve

Plot ρ/MSE₀ vs σ → a scale-free S-shape, fit by a 4-parameter Gompertz function exposing three regimes and landmarks:

f(x) = d + (a−d)·exp(−exp(−b(x−c)))
  • knee — onset of rapid degradation
  • inflection (param c) — steepest point
  • half-max-slope — saturation threshold

What the experiments show

Five real datasets, feedforward nets. Baselines match the literature:

  • Cancer MSE 10.19 · Flu MSE 12.82
  • Heart F1 0.853 · Breast F1 0.972 · MNIST F1 0.979

Every Gompertz fit lands at R² 0.9932–0.9999 — the three-regime shape is consistent and theory-aligned.

Interpretation thresholds (Table 6)

  • Regression: robustness cutoff ≈ 2× baseline MSE.
  • Classification: the knee is the informative point.
  • Breast Cancer at p=1 fails immediately (x_knee=0); MNIST degrades steepest as p grows.

Strengths vs Limitations

▲ Strengths

  • Genuinely black-box & architecture-agnostic — applies to any trained deterministic model.
  • Clean formal definition plus a practical Monte-Carlo estimator with explicit pseudocode.
  • Robustness curves give an interpretable, scale-free visual diagnostic.
  • Index Gᵢ is a principled, parameter-light comparator across models/datasets.
  • Honest scoping — explicitly restricts to random Gaussian noise.

▼ Limitations

  • Random ≠ adversarial: high scores do not imply attack safety.
  • The "bound" is an empirical quantile — no variance/tightness analysis vs m, n, σ.
  • The n=31 regression sets use in-sample testing, confounding fit & evaluation.
  • No comparison to certified bounds, Lipschitz, or accuracy-perturbation methods.
  • Single scalar Gᵢ mixes task/dimensionality/sample-size — may obscure more than it reveals.
  • MSE/MBS-centric; categorical features left unperturbed.
04

Open questions

How tight is the empirical θ bound as a function of m, n, dimensionality, σ — and can a finite-sample PAC-style guarantee replace the heuristic quantile?

Does θ-robustness correlate with adversarial robustness (PGD/FGSM), or do the two diverge as theory predicts?

Can Gᵢ be made statistically meaningful across heterogeneous datasets via bootstrap CIs on the Gompertz parameters?

Does the method & Gompertz-fit quality hold on modern CNNs / Transformers / VAEs?

How does θ-robustness compare quantitatively to certified-radius (randomised smoothing / Lipschitz) bounds on the same models?
