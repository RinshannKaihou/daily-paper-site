> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 MARS（Modality-Agnostic Refusal Steering），一种无需训练的轻量方法，通过从 LLM 主干提取的文本拒绝方向（refusal directions）对多模态 LLM 进行激活空间干预，配合"激活再中心化"消除模态偏置、几何信任域自适应控制引导强度、以及最优层选择，在无需任何多模态安全数据的情况下，显著提升图像与视频模态的安全性并保持效用（如 Qwen3-VL 视频越狱拒绝率 +59.4%）。

> **TL;DR:** MARS injects multimodal safety into MLLMs in a training-free way by reusing textual refusal directions from the LLM backbone, correcting cross-modal misalignment via activation re-centering on randomly colored images, adaptively scaling steering strength within a geometric trust region, and selecting the optimal intervention layer at the first generated token — matching or surpassing training-based baselines across 5 SOTA MLLMs.

## 核心贡献

- **首次系统验证文本拒绝方向的跨模态泛化**：发现直接从 LLM 主干（仅用 ALPACA + MaliciousInstruct 文本数据）提取的拒绝方向可推广到图像与视频输入，揭示 MLLM 中存在跨模态共享的安全相关结构。
- **诊断出"模态错配"这一核心失效模式**：通过 PCA 与 separation score 分析，发现 `<assistant>` token 的激活被一个与安全语义无关的"模态特异性分量"主导，导致安全图像被错误地推向拒绝，使朴素激活引导在多模态下严重过拒（over-refusal）。
- **MARS 三项关键设计**：(i) 用随机彩色图像估计中性均值做**激活再中心化**消除模态偏置；(ii) 在以最近语义质心定义半径的**几何信任域**内自适应缩放引导强度 α；(iii) 用三个纯文本评分（方向一致性、可分性、安全边界）之和自动**选择最优干预层**。
- **ReLU 门控实现单向引导**：仅对与拒绝方向正相关的激活做增强，对估计为安全的输入 s=0，从而在不显式分类的前提下避免对安全输入的干预。
- **全面基准评测且性价比突出**：在 5 个 SOTA MLLM 上对比 ECSO、AdaSteer（训练无关）与 SASA（训练依赖，作为上界），MARS 在 ViSU/HADES/MMSafetyBench/VideoSafetyBench 上获得一致安全增益，同时几乎不损失 MMMU/MMMU-Pro 效用。

## Key Contributions

- **Cross-modal transfer of refusal directions**: Provides the first analysis showing that textual safety representations generalize across modalities (image, video), conditioned on three factors: layer selection, intervention strength, and multimodal misalignment.
- **Diagnoses a fundamental failure mode — modality misalignment**: PCA / separation-score analysis shows the `<assistant>`-token activation is dominated by a modality-specific component that aligns with the refusal direction *regardless* of safety semantics, causing naive steering to over-refuse safe multimodal inputs.
- **MARS — a training-free inference-time method** combining (i) activation re-centering using a mean of randomly colored neutral images to remove the modality component; (ii) a ReLU-gated one-sided traversal that only steers activations aligned with the refusal direction; (iii) adaptive steering strength α derived from a geometric trust region (radius = distance to nearest accept/reject centroid); (iv) automatic layer selection from three text-only scores (direction consistency, context separability, safe-text margin).
- **Operates only at the first generated token** with no extra forward passes or training, keeping inference cost ≈ original model time.
- **Strong empirical validation**: Matches or surpasses the training-based SASA on several model/benchmark combinations while preserving utility, demonstrating an "implicit safety baseline" latent in MLLMs.

## Methodology

**Problem & preliminaries.** An MLLM `f_MLLM : L × V → L` is decomposed into a vision encoder `f_vis`, projection `f_proj`, and decoder-only LLM `f_LLM`. Safety = refusing unsafe queries while answering safe ones. Refusal directions are extracted (following Arditi et al.) from the hidden state at the `<assistant>` token (the input's final token) by contrasting activations from a safe set (ALPACA) `T_acc` and an unsafe set (MaliciousInstruct) `T_ref`, yielding a normalized direction `d̂_r^ℓ = (μ_ref^ℓ − μ_acc^ℓ)/‖·‖` per layer.

**Steering baseline (Eq. 2).** `h̄ = h + α⟨h, d̂_r⟩d̂_r`. Preliminary study (Sec. 3) on Qwen3-VL, Gemma3, InternVL3.5 shows: (1) unsafe inputs are more refused than safe ones across layers → directions *do* transfer; but (2) steering systematically over-refuses safe inputs and utility degrades at high α.

**Root cause (Sec. 3.1).** PCA (Fig. 2a) and a separation score `Sep^ℓ` (Eq. 3, cosine alignment of unsafe vs safe activations with `d̂_r`) reveal both safe and unsafe image activations project strongly onto the refusal direction *independently of semantics* — a modality-specific component swamps the `<assistant>` token.

**MARS components (Sec. 4):**

1. **Activation re-centering (Eq. 4).** Sample N randomly colored images `I_neu` (no semantics), compute neutral mean `μ_neu^ℓ`, center activations `ĥ = h − μ_neu^ℓ`. This recovers safety-relevant structure (Fig. 2b shows `Sep` rises across layers/models). Ablation (Table 9) shows N is robust from 8–128 images.

2. **ReLU-gated traversal (Eq. 5).** `s^ℓ = ReLU(⟨h_c^ℓ, d̂_r^ℓ⟩) d̂_r^ℓ`. One-sided: activations negatively aligned (estimated safe) get s=0 → no intervention, no explicit classifier needed.

3. **Adaptive steering strength (Eqs. 6–7).** Define local radius `r^ℓ = min(‖h_c − μ_acc‖, ‖h_c − μ_ref‖)` as a trust region (sphere reaching the nearest semantic anchor), and `α^ℓ = r^ℓ/(‖s^ℓ‖ + ε)`. This bounds displacement within the activation region covered by training data, avoiding domain shift. Steering applied to all token positions, but only at the first generated token (it determines the generation trajectory).

4. **Layer selection (Sec. 4.3).** Three text-only, complementary scores combined (min-max normalized, summed): (i) *direction consistency* = cosine between train and validation refusal directions; (ii) *context separability* = nearest-centroid accuracy on a held-out text safe/unsafe set (HarmBench, 100/class); (iii) *safe-text margin* = how confidently safe validation activations sit on the accept side of the midpoint. Selected layers: Gemma3=26, Molmo2=22, Qwen3-VL=23, Qwen3.5=22, InternVL3.5=25.

**Setup.** Refusal rate = fraction matching refusal templates (e.g., "I'm sorry"); safety score via Llama-3.1-Guard; utility via MMMU/MMMU-Pro accuracy. Baselines: ECSO (self-assessment, extra forward passes), AdaSteer (text-only activation steering), SASA (training-based upper bound on multimodal + jailbreak data). Single A100, FP16, deterministic decoding.

## Results & Highlights

- **ViSU image safety (Table 1):** Zero-shot unsafe refusal is low (0.2% Molmo2 → 22.9% Qwen3.5). MARS lifts unsafe refusal by +25.1% (Gemma3) to +67.9% (Molmo2) with controlled over-refusal, beating training-based SASA on Molmo2 (+3.9% refusal, −7.9% over-refusal), Qwen3-VL (+58.8% refusal, −0.3% over-refusal), InternVL3.5 (+10.2% refusal).
- **HADES jailbreak (Table 2):** MARS gives large, consistent gains, e.g., Molmo2 +78.6% refusal (17.2→95.8), Gemma3 +41.3% refusal; matches SASA on InternVL3.5/Qwen3-VL/Qwen3.5 and surpasses it on Molmo2.
- **Utility (Table 3):** MARS preserves MMMU/MMMU-Pro accuracy on Gemma3, Molmo2, InternVL3.5, Qwen3-VL. Only Qwen3.5 shows modest over-refusal (MMMU 50.8→38.2) — still far better than SASA (→30.8). SASA itself causes −10% to −20% utility hits.
- **Video jailbreak (Table 5):** Best among training-free methods: +59.4% on Qwen3-VL (28.8→88.2, ×3), +52% Qwen3.5, +40.4% Molmo2; even outperforms training-based SASA on Qwen3-VL (+47.9%). Confirms cross-modal generalization to video without video data.
- **MMSafetyBench (Table 6, Appendix):** Consistent refusal gains (e.g., Qwen3-VL 46.3→85.3), though SASA (trained on jailbreak data) reaches ~99%.
- **Ablations (Tables 4, 8):** Removing layer selection collapses unsafe refusal (~0% on Molmo2/Gemma3) and hurts utility (−18.5% InternVL3.5); removing centering causes severe over-refusal (Molmo2 99.2% safe refused); removing ReLU hurts utility marginally. Each layer-selection score contributes (esp. consistency/separability on Molmo2, InternVL3.5); Qwen family is robust to single-score removal.
- **Weak-alignment robustness (Appendix F, Fig. 4):** On LLaVA-1.5 (refuses only 33% of MaliciousInstruct, direction from ~33 samples), MARS still yields +32.1% (HADES) and +28.1% (ViSU) refusal — direction even from a poorly aligned model amplifies latent safety.
- **Neutral-prompt finding (Table 7):** A semantically neutral prompt bypasses alignment more effectively than the original unsafe HADES prompt (drops of −32.7% to −72.2% refusal), underscoring the fragility of surface-cue-based safety and motivating representation-level interventions like MARS.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (Moderate-High):** The empirical claim that unimodal textual refusal directions transfer to image/video is genuinely interesting and, per the authors, the first such analysis in MLLMs. The standout *conceptual* contribution is diagnosing modality misalignment of the `<assistant>` token and the elegant fix (re-centering with random colored images — no multimodal data, no semantics). Individual mechanisms (activation steering, trust regions, layer heuristics) are composites of prior ideas; novelty is in the combination and the multimodal diagnosis.
- **Rigor (Solid):** Layer-wise studies, PCA + a quantitative separation score, component-wise ablations (layer selection, centering, ReLU, each layer-score term, and number of neutral images), and a weak-alignment stress test (LLaVA-1.5). Baselines are appropriate, including a strong *training-based* upper bound (SASA). Refusal is measured both by template matching and by Llama-3.1-Guard safety score.
- **Reproducibility (Good):** Training-free, deterministic decoding, fixed seeds, single A100/FP16, ~30 min–few hours per experiment. Selected layers and refusal templates are reported. Neutral-image count is robust (8–128). Code/website is referenced ("further results in Appendix G and website") but a public code link is not visible in the text I could read — worth verifying.
- **Significance (High if it generalizes):** Offers a cheap, scalable path to multimodal safety that sidesteps expensive multimodal unsafe-data collection; the cross-modal-structure insight could reshape how multimodal alignment is approached.
- **Red flags / caveats:**
  - Refusal metric leans on a fixed template set ("I'm sorry", etc.); models that refuse paraphrastically could be under-counted, partially biasing comparisons.
  - Llama-3.1-Guard safety score is text-only; the authors feed the HADES prompt as context for the image, an acknowledged proxy that may inflate/deflate true multimodal safety.
  - SASA (the headline "upper bound") is *trained on jailbreaking data* and still loses on Qwen3-VL video — a somewhat favorable framing for MARS, though the authors are transparent about SASA's training data.
  - Over-refusal on Qwen3.5 (MMMU 50.8→38.2) shows the method is not always utility-neutral.
  - The trust-region and layer-selection heuristics are validated empirically but lack theoretical guarantees; robustness to adaptive white-box attacks steering *toward* acceptance is not evaluated (acknowledged dual-use risk in Appendix E).

## Strengths

- Eliminates dependence on hard-to-collect, costly multimodal unsafe data — uses only cheap text data (ALPACA/MaliciousInstruct) + random colored images.
- Training-free, lightweight, single-token intervention; negligible inference overhead.
- Clear mechanistic story: misalignment diagnosis → re-centering → adaptive steering → layer selection, each motivated and ablated.
- Strong and broad evaluation across 5–6 models and image+video jailbreak settings, including a category-wise video breakdown (Table 10) and a weakly-aligned legacy model.
- Best safety-utility trade-off among compared training-free methods, and competitive with (sometimes better than) the training-based upper bound.

## Limitations

- Assumes a usable refusal direction exists in the LLM backbone; degrades if alignment is absent (mitigated but shown on LLaVA-1.5 only).
- Assumes modality misalignment can be *linearly* removed via centering — an approximation that may fail in highly non-linear regions.
- Residual over-refusal on safe inputs remains (visible on Qwen3.5 utility).
- Trust region and layer selection are empirically tuned heuristics with no optimality guarantee; robustness to adaptive, white-box adversaries crafting inputs outside the trust region is untested.
- Refusal template metric + text-only Guard are imperfect proxies for true multimodal safety.
- Generalization beyond the tested 4B–13B models (e.g., much larger or differently-aligned MLLMs, audio/3D modalities) is unverified.

## Relevance

该工作与多模态大模型安全对齐、表示工程（representation engineering）及越狱防御高度相关，尤其为无需重新训练、无需多模态安全数据即可增强 MLLM 安全性的轻量化方案提供了可行路径。对于关注多模态鲁棒性、激活空间可解释性以及安全可控生成（含图像与视频越狱）的研究方向具有直接借鉴意义，也可作为后续"激活空间诊断 + 表示级干预"研究的基础范式。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does cross-modal transfer degrade as the vision encoder or modality projector architecture changes (e.g., token-compressing projectors vs. per-patch projectors)?
- Can the optimal intervention layer be predicted a priori from internal statistics rather than tuned per model?
- How robust is the geometric trust region against adaptive white-box attacks that steer activations toward acceptance (the dual-use risk in Appendix E)?
- Does re-centering with non-visual-neutral references (e.g., natural images) change results, and is the linear-centering assumption violated for very large models?
- Does the modality-shared safety-structure finding extend to audio, 3D, or other emerging modalities, and to much larger MLLMs?
- How does MARS compose with complementary defenses (input filters, system-prompt defenses, RLHF alignment)?
- Could the separation score / centering idea also *diagnose* which layers of a model are most jailbreak-vulnerable, beyond steering?
