Critical Edge

No direct comparison against a strong non-SAE region-masking / inpainting baseline — so the SAE-specific advantage over Grad-CAM-style saliency is, by the authors' own admission, somewhat under-proven.

Severity: claims outrun evidence
01 / METHOD

Detect where it lives — then swap, never steer

Prior SAE-unlearning methods modify a detected latent with a negative multiplier γ and decode it back — pushing 27.6%–83.7% of activations off the model's native manifold and spawning artifacts.

PER keeps the SAE as a pure detector: it builds a binary mask of concept-bearing patches and replaces each with an in-distribution patch drawn from elsewhere in the same forward pass. The decoder never touches a modified activation.

03 / VERDICT

Sharp diagnosis, minimalist fix

Strengths
  • Diagnoses the root cause (OOD activations) with hard statistics, not visual complaints.
  • Training-free, tuning-free, drop-in across four pipelines.
  • Strong human validation: agreement + preference + significance.
  • Honest about mixed adversarial results and weak concepts.
Limitations
  • Assumes a non-detected patch exists in the same feature map; else Gaussian-noise fallback.
  • No direct non-SAE masking baseline — advantage under-proven.
  • Headline GA partly inflated by folding AR into the average.
  • Mostly SD v1.5; SDXL-Turbo CRA not comparable. Inherits SAE fragility.
