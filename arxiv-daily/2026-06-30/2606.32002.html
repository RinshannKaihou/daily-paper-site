Key Finding

Self-generated QA is a fragile implicit policy: salient artifacts hijack questions, embedded instructions hijack answers — yet simple fixes cut compliance 88% → 13%.

§ 01Methodology — a two-stage implicit policy

The authors reframe the common “self-study” loop (self-instruct, distillation, KV-cache compression like Cartridges) as two distinct policies a generator executes: one decides what to ask, another how to answer — and each is independently fragile.

Q-STAGE Question generation = evidence selection. The generator picks which document spans ever reach training. Coverage saturates early and concentrates on locally salient spans — headings, lists, tables, numeric blocks, even parser artifacts.
A-STAGE Answering = behavioral hijack. The answerer treats instruction-like passages embedded in the chunk as binding rules. Compliance depends on intent & surface form, not strictness.

Setup: Cartridges self-study protocol over 3 corpora — Cartridges ~20K, LongHealth ~114K, QASPER ~98K tokens. 6 generators across 3 families (Qwen3 / Gemma3 / Llama3.1), 5 prompt seeds, 17 injection types × 4 axes, judge = Qwen3-32B @ temp 0, cross-validated with GPT-5.5.

§ 02Results & Highlights
  • Coverage saturates early93–94% on Cartridges, 75–86% LongHealth, 78–82% QASPER; cross-seed Jaccard 0.55–0.68.
  • Validity gaps persistCartridges: 13.3% hallucinated + 13.8% unfilled (Qwen3-4B); 32B improves but unsupported items remain.
  • Salient artifacts hijack generationHTML-like probe hits 55–94% across 6 models / 3 families; a generic banner lifts a low-salience paragraph 25.2% → 36.3%.
  • Chunk sampling amplifies contamination30 insertions expose >50% of chunks; 50 reach 75.4–82.7%.
  • Answer-stage compliance is near-totalmost configs >85%; exact-phrase override reaches 99.4–100%; only 1.7B resists (~62%).
  • Capability backfires under conflictno_summ compliance climbs 26% → 74% → 95% (1.7B → 4B → 32B).
  • Defenses work, cheaplysentence-targeted Qs: 17–53% hit rate; keyword–regex: 87.7% → 12.6% @ 100% retention.

Strengths

  • Clean conceptual split: selection fragility vs. answering fragility, each with its own diagnostic.
  • Training-loop-agnostic mitigations with concrete compliance–retention numbers.
  • Breadth: 3 corpora, 6 models, 3 families, 17 injection types, seed-stratified appendix breakdowns.
  • Genuinely counterintuitive result — capability increases vulnerability under task conflict.
  • Honest about fixes (“partial,” “residual failures remain”); code + prompts released.
!

Limitations

  • No downstream task accuracy — quality judged only via injection compliance & text retention.
  • Keyword–regex is brittle by construction; its 100% retention likely won't generalize to adversarial phrasing.
  • Sentence-targeted generation trades globality for locality — may miss multi-sentence relations.
  • The HTML artifact is an obvious attractor; subtler salience drivers (stylistic anomalies) untested.
  • Judge shares model family with several test generators → potential systematic bias.
§ 04Open Questions for Further Exploration
Q1Do the mitigations actually improve downstream factual recall on the distilled model — not just compliance & retention?
Q2How robust is the keyword–regex filter against paraphrased or semantically-equivalent injections that dodge trigger words?
Q3Can anchor-selection bias be fixed elegantly (coverage-aware / retrieval-driven targeting) without sacrificing multi-sentence relations?
Q4Does the “bigger = more compliant” conflict effect generalize to frontier models, and can alignment training suppress it?
Q5How do these dynamics interact with model collapse under iterative self-study over many generations?
