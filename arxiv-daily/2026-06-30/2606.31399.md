> **一句话总结：** 本文将长时序语言智能体的"世界模型崩溃"建模为相变现象：在确定性任务族 StatefulPuzzle 中，通过对状态基数(SC)与依赖密度(DD)的网格搜索，发现崩溃面呈"已解决平台—窄过渡带—崩溃下限"的三段结构；逐状态追踪证明世界状态保真度先于动作有效性失效（中位提前 2 步），且更强模型（haiku→GPT-4o）只把临界边界从 SC⋆≈14 平移到约 15–19，而不能消除该质变。

> **TL;DR:** This paper reframes long-horizon LLM-agent failure as a finite-grid phase transition. In a deterministic task family (StatefulPuzzle) with exact per-step gold state, a grid over state cardinality (SC) and dependency density (DD) yields a plateau/transition-band/collapse-floor geometry; world-state fidelity fails before action validity (median 2-step lead); and stronger models (haiku→GPT-4o) shift the critical boundary (SC⋆≈14→15–19) but do not erase it.

## 核心贡献

- **提出"世界模型崩溃即相变"的形式化框架：** 将状态基数(SC)与依赖密度(DD)作为控制参数、任务成功率作为序参量、世界状态保真度作为前兆信号，并给出"有限网格突变过渡"的操作性定义（Definition 1）。
- **构建带精确逐状态真值的可控任务族：** 设计三个确定性模拟器（GraphNav、ToolDAG、StatefulPuzzle），用"先锁定再评测"的试点规则选出 StatefulPuzzle 作为验证环境，使世界状态保真度可被逐步严格度量。
- **绘制完整的相图并定位临界点：** 在 SC∈{5,10,20,40}×DD∈{1,2,4,6} 的 16 格×100 次试验上得到平台—窄带—下限结构，精细扫描将临界点定在 SC⋆≈13.5 附近。
- **揭示失效的时间因果序：** 逐状态追踪证明世界状态保真度先于动作有效性崩溃（中位提前 2 步，无任何"计划先崩"的样本），把"动作选错"重新解释为"基于已损坏世界模型行动"。
- **刻画模型能力的边界与多轴消融：** 跨模型实验表明更强模型只平移边界（haiku SC⋆≈14，gpt-4o-mini≈18.9，GPT-4o≈15.6）；对 horizon、branching、observation、mutation 四个次级轴的消融显示它们分别扮演"使能/零参照/可见性门/描述性调制"四种不同角色。

## Key Contributions

- **Formalizes world-model collapse as a phase transition:** Casts state cardinality (SC) and dependency density (DD) as control parameters, task success as the order parameter, and world-state fidelity as the precursor, with an operational "finite-grid abrupt transition" definition (Definition 1) and crossing point s⋆ = inf{s : p ≤ 1/2}.
- **Builds a controlled task family with exact per-step gold state:** Designs three deterministic simulators and uses a pilot-rule locked *before* agent evaluation to select StatefulPuzzle (the only environment passing monotonicity, cross-model shape agreement, and a ≥20pp drop criterion), enabling rigorous step-level measurement.
- **Maps a full phase diagram and localizes the critical point:** A 16-cell confirmatory grid (n=100 each, 1,600 sha256-seeded unique episodes) reveals plateau/transition-band/collapse-floor geometry; a fine scan pins the crossover at SC⋆≈13.5.
- **Identifies the temporal causal mechanism:** Per-step traces show world-state fidelity fails before action validity (median 2-step lead; no episode shows plan-first collapse), reframing "bad action" as "acting from a corrupted world."
- **Characterizes model-capability limits and secondary axes:** Cross-model probes show stronger models only *translate* the boundary (haiku SC⋆≈14 → gpt-4o-mini≈18.9, GPT-4o≈15.6), and ablations over horizon/branching/observation/mutation assign them four distinct roles (enabler / null / visibility gate / descriptive modulator).

## Methodology

**Conceptual frame.** The paper borrows the vocabulary of statistical physics — control parameters, order parameter, critical region, precursor — *without* claiming a thermodynamic limit. SC (number of jointly addressable entities) and DD (preconditions binding each subgoal to current state) are control parameters; task success `pθ(s,d;z)` is the order parameter; world-state fidelity `ρ(W^c_t, W*_t)` is the precursor. The nuisance vector z fixes horizon T, branching, observation mode, and mutation rate.

**Formal definition (Def. 1).** A finite grid has an abrupt transition if an adjacent pair `(s_i, s_{i+1})` shows `|p̂(s_i,d) − p̂(s_{i+1},d)| ≥ δ`, and nonempty regimes with `p̂ ≥ β` and `p̂ ≤ α`. The operational crossing is `s⋆ = inf{s : p ≤ 1/2}`. The mechanism claim is trajectory-level: `τW = inf{t : windowed ρ < 1/2}` (world-state onset) and `τA = inf{t≥1 : A_t = 0}` (first invalid action); a representational-collapse account predicts `τW < τA`.

**Environments (3 deterministic simulators, seed-only randomness).** GraphNav (room/door navigation with keys/switches/decoys), ToolDAG (typed tool-call planning under a growing namespace), and StatefulPuzzle (moving objects across rooms/containers/slots via ordered subgoal chains). A locked pilot rule — (a) per-model monotonicity, (b) cross-model shape agreement, (c) min(max ∆p̂) ≥ 20pp, with n=10 per level across two model tiers — selects StatefulPuzzle; GraphNav fails (b), ToolDAG fails (c).

**Confirmatory stress grid.** SC∈{5,10,20,40} × DD∈{1,2,4,6}, secondary axes fixed at Regime III (T=40, branching 4, clean observation, static mutation). 16 cells × n=100 episodes = 10 archetypes × 10 instance variants, sha256-derived seeds (1,600 unique, zero collisions).

**Agent architecture.** A fixed three-call loop run identically each step: (1) Planner proposes action + expected state changes; (2) Updater rewrites explicit working memory after the simulator step; (3) Self-Diag records a non-blocking validity judgment. The separation mirrors ReAct/Reflexion/structured-memory designs. Confirmatory grid uses claude-haiku-4-5 at temperature 0. Code is released at https://github.com/Hik289/world-model-collapse.git.

**Analysis chain.** Build the controlled phase diagram → use per-step traces to identify world-state failure as precursor → perturb secondary axes to rule out simpler explanations. The authors explicitly note the original "symmetric synchrony" criterion was too strict and correct it to an asymmetric precedence story.

## Results & Highlights

- **Confirmatory grid (haiku, Table 2):** SC=5 solves everything (1.00 across all DD); SC=10 degrades with DD (1.00, 1.00, 0.72, 0.20); SC=20 and SC=40 collapse to 0.00 everywhere. The decisive cliff is along SC (SC=10→20 at DD=1 is 100%→0%), while DD mainly shifts the operating point *within* the transition band.
- **World-state collapse comes first:** Median lead is ~2 steps; world-state estimate fails before the planner's actions become invalid; no paired episode shows a strict plan-first collapse. This makes per-step state instrumentation essential, not optional — final success alone cannot diagnose it.
- **Cross-model boundary translation:** gpt-4o-mini grid (SC=10 row: 0.92, 0.68, 0.64, 0.58; SC=20 row drops toward floor) preserves the plateau/transition/floor shape but shifts the boundary outward. SC⋆ readouts: haiku≈14.04, gpt-4o-mini≈18.94, GPT-4o≈15.56 (strong interior lift, cliff remains). Llama-3 70B sits below the tested boundary (treated as a lower-bound probe with an endpoint-determinism caveat).
- **Single-axis ablations (backdrop SC=10, DD=6):**
  - Horizon T — *enabler* (T=10:0, T=20:0, T=40:0.12, T=80:0.32): necessary but not sufficient; generalizes context-budget accounts.
  - Branching — *intended null* (≈0.16–0.20 across 2/4/8/16): rules out a search-tree blow-up explanation.
  - Observation noise — *visibility gate* (clean:0.40, conflict:0.16, distractor:0.12, partial:0.00): partial observation hides state slots from the Updater.
  - Mutation rate — *descriptive hump* (static:0.08, low:0.32, medium:0.24, high:0.08): retained only as a candidate modulator.
- **Critical-point localisation (Figure 7):** SC-Fine brackets the crossover at SC=13 (0.78) → SC=14 (0.52) → SC=15 (0.013); 4-PL logistic fit gives SC⋆≈13.9. T-Fine at fixed (SC,DD)=(10,6) stays flat across T∈[22,80], so no analogous T⋆ is resolved — confirming T is a modulator, not a driver.

## Authors & Credibility

Two authors: Xinyuan Song (Emory University) and Zekun Cai (The University of Tokyo; LocationMind, Tokyo). The work is methodologically careful: it locks the environment-selection rule *before* any agent evaluation, uses exact per-step gold state, sha256-derived seeds with a reported zero-collision check, a fixed temperature-0 setting, and explicit pre-registration of acceptance criteria. Code is publicly released. The authors are unusually transparent about a failed hypothesis (the "symmetric synchrony" criterion) and correct it in the main text rather than hiding it. No prior joint track record is cited, but the experimental hygiene is above average for an agent-evaluation preprint.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuine. The phase-transition *geometry* (plateau/transition/floor) plus the *temporal precedence* claim (world-state fidelity fails before action validity) is more than a reframing — it is a falsifiable, step-level mechanistic claim that distinguishes "corrupted world" from "bad policy." The connection to Lee (2026)'s canonical-path-deviation drift baseline and to Nakaishi et al. (2024)'s static-output phase transition is positioned clearly.
- **Rigor:** Strong on internal validity. Pre-registered selection rule, exact gold state, seed control, temperature 0, Cohen's-h effect sizes (Table 10), and a paired-design negative control (T-Fine vs SC-Fine). However, the "≥20pp drop" and "monotonicity" pilot thresholds are somewhat arbitrary engineering choices, and the confirmatory claim rests on a *single* selected environment.
- **Reproducibility:** Good. Code released, deterministic simulators, seed-only randomness, exact grid specs (SC/DD/T/branching levels, n per cell). The main reproducibility risk is cross-platform: endpoint determinism, seed control, and chat formatting differ across providers, which the authors flag honestly.
- **Significance:** Conditional. If the (SC,DD) cliff lifts to naturalistic benchmarks, this is a high-impact diagnostic. The authors explicitly *do not* test on WebArena/SWE-Bench/TravelPlanner and frame the result as a "clean testbed" rather than a universal claim — a commendable but scope-limiting stance.
- **Red flags / caveats:**
  - **Scope:** The headline "measurable bottleneck for long-horizon agents" is supported only within StatefulPuzzle; 2 of 3 environments were discarded as pilots.
  - **Harness confound:** The architecture is a fixed three-call Planner/Updater/Self-Diag loop. The cliff may be a property of the *model–harness pair*; raw/summarized/external-state memory variants were *not* run, so it is untested whether the boundary moves or changes shape under a different harness.
  - **Scaling-law gap:** No thermodynamic-limit claim, no critical exponents, no scaling-law fit across model size — the authors acknowledge this but it bounds how far the "phase transition" language can be pushed.
  - **Two-author preprint** making a moderately strong universal-sounding claim; the conservative framing in the body partially mitigates this.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clean, memorable framing with a precise operational definition (Def. 1) rather than vague analogy.
- Step-level causal claim (τW < τA) is falsifiable and well-supported; corrects its own initial hypothesis transparently.
- Excellent experimental hygiene: locked pre-evaluation selection rule, exact gold state, seed control, released code.
- The negative-control design (SC-Fine resolves a boundary while T-Fine stays flat) cleanly rules out a reparameterized horizon explanation.
- Actionable implications: scaffolds should be evaluated by whether they delay τW, not just improve final success.

## Limitations

- Single-environment confirmatory claim (StatefulPuzzle); no naturalistic-benchmark validation (WebArena/SWE-Bench/TravelPlanner absent).
- Fixed agent architecture — the cliff may be harness-dependent; alternative memory regimes untested.
- Discretized, engineering-tractable axes; no critical exponents or scaling laws; boundary locations are interpolated from integer grids.
- Cross-platform serving differences limit the cross-model table to "boundary translation evidence," not a clean capability leaderboard.
- Secondary axes (especially mutation rate) are exploratory/descriptive, not equally established as drivers.

## Relevance

对于关注长时序 LLM 智能体鲁棒性、失败诊断与记忆/状态管理的研究者，本文提供了可逐步度量的"世界模型崩溃"瓶颈刻画，与多步推理、智能体可靠性评测及"扩大推理搜索是否真能挽救崩溃"这一关键问题直接相关。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the (SC,DD) cliff survive a harness swap — e.g., raw vs. summarized vs. external symbolic working memory — or does the boundary move/change shape?
- How does SC⋆ scale with model size and training data (a power law?), and does the relationship differ between base and instruction-tuned models?
- Can interventions that externalize or decompose state (structured memories, planner–solver hybrids, explicit world-state tokens) push SC⋆ outward, or do they merely translate the boundary like larger models do?
- Is the τW < τA ordering consistent across all six stress dimensions, or does it invert under partial observation where the Updater is starved of state slots?
- Does the plateau/transition/floor geometry appear in naturalistic benchmarks once SC and DD are estimated and controlled, or do horizon and environment heterogeneity wash out the cliff?
