> **一句话总结：** 该论文通过大规模扫描实验（12个模型、5种优化器、4个数据集）系统性地证明，在涌现性失准（EM）中，优化器选择是影响最大的训练因素（造成7倍的失准率差异），而模型规模（270M–235B）和家族影响可忽略；并基于Muon优化器保持LoRA适配器奇异值谱更平坦的机制洞察，提出一种Frobenius/核范数谱正则化方法，以几乎零训练损失代价大幅恢复了Adam、Lion等EM易感优化器的对齐。

> **TL;DR:** Across a large sweep (12 models, 5 optimisers, 4 EM datasets), the choice of optimiser is the single dominant factor in Emergent Misalignment severity—a 7× spread in misalignment rate from Muon (5.3%) to Lion (37.0%)—while model scale (270M–235B) and family have negligible effect. Final log training loss explains 74% of alignment variance, and stratifying by optimiser captures nearly all the rest (adjusted R²=0.94). Among adaptive optimisers, alignment preservation tracks a flatter singular-value spectrum of the LoRA adapter (Muon stable-rank 17.1 vs Adam/Lion ~2.3). A cheap spectral-regularisation term substantially recovers alignment for Adam (+6.6) and Lion (+8.0) at negligible training-loss cost.

## 核心贡献

- **系统量化EM的训练敏感性**：通过128点的初始扫描（4个Qwen3模型×4优化器×4数据集×2 batch size），首次证明优化器选择是EM的主导因素（7倍失准率差异），而模型大小几乎无影响。
- **跨家族、跨规模的"无关性"证据**：在3个家族、12个模型（270M–235B）上用Adam进行96点扫描，证实模型规模和家族对EM的独立影响可忽略，所有1B+模型失准率聚集在11–16%。
- **损失-对齐的强预测关系**：在Qwen3-8B上发现最终log训练损失解释74%的对齐方差；按优化器分层后调整R²升至0.94，捕获几乎所有残差方差，而学习率、数据集、batch size几乎无预测力。
- **训练动态的差异化轨迹**：每种优化器在损失-对齐空间中走截然不同的路径；训练收敛后优化器变得比训练损失更重要——大部分对齐损害发生在已收敛之后。
- **谱性质的机制性解释与缓解**：自适应优化器中，对齐保持与LoRA适配器奇异值谱的平坦度正相关（Muon稳定秩17.1 ≫ Adam/Lion 2.3）；引入Frobenius/核范数谱正则化项，以几乎零训练损失代价大幅恢复Adam、Lion的对齐。

## Key Contributions

- **First systematic, quantitative characterisation of EM sensitivity to training choices.** A 128-run sweep shows optimiser choice dominates EM severity (7× spread), far exceeding the effects of model scale, family, dataset, and batch size.
- **Demonstrates scale- and family-invariance of EM (under Adam).** A 96-run sweep over 12 models (270M–235B) from Gemma 3, Llama 3, and Qwen3 confirms all 1B+ models cluster at 11–16% misalignment regardless of scale or family.
- **Identifies a strong, optimiser-stratified loss–alignment relationship.** On Qwen3-8B, final log training loss explains 74% of alignment variance; stratifying by optimiser raises adjusted R² to 0.94, while dataset/LR/batch-size add negligible predictive power.
- **Reveals distinct optimiser trajectories through loss–alignment space.** Each optimiser traces a qualitatively different path; the final-loss ordering does not predict the final-alignment ordering, and most alignment damage occurs *after* training convergence.
- **Provides a mechanistic (spectral) account and a cheap mitigation.** Among adaptive optimisers, alignment preservation correlates with a flatter LoRA singular-value spectrum; an explicit spectral-regularisation term recovers alignment for Adam (+6.6) and Lion (+8.0) at negligible training-loss cost.

## Methodology

**Experimental design (5 groups, ~330 runs total):**
- **Initial sweep (128 runs):** 4 Qwen3 models (4B, 8B, 30B-A3B, 32B) × 4 optimisers × 4 datasets × 2 batch sizes, 1 epoch, fixed LR 1e-5.
- **Scale sweep (96 runs):** all 12 models (Gemma 3: 270M–12B; Llama 3: 1B–8B; Qwen3: 4B–235B-A22B) with Adam at LR 1e-5 across 4 datasets × 2 batch sizes.
- **Loss–alignment study (72 runs):** Qwen3-8B, 5 optimisers with varied LRs × 4 datasets × 2 batch sizes (9 optimiser–LR settings).
- **Dynamics study (16 runs):** Qwen3-8B, 4 optimisers with per-tuned LRs, 4 datasets, 2 epochs with alignment checkpoints.
- **Spectral regularisation (16 runs):** Qwen3-8B, 4 optimisers, 4 datasets, with/without the regulariser.

**Datasets (4 EM-inducing sets):** insecure code (Betley et al., 6,000 examples), bad medical advice, risky financial advice, extreme sports advice (Turner et al., 6,000–7,049 each), all single-turn chat format.

**Training:** rank-stabilised LoRA (rsLoRA, rank 32, α=64, no dropout) on all attention and feed-forward projections; cosine LR schedule with 50 warmup steps; assistant-only loss masking.

**Spectral regulariser:** `L_total = L_SFT + λ·R(σ)`, where `R(σ) = √(∥BA∥_F) / ∥BA∥_* − 1/√r` (r=32). This equals zero when all singular values are equal, increases as the spectrum becomes less uniform, and is scale-invariant. λ=3.0 chosen via a preliminary sweep. Singular values computed efficiently via the low-rank LoRA structure. A spectral-entropy-gap variant produced similar results.

**Evaluation:** 142 EM questions (from Betley et al. and Turner et al.), each sampled 10–32 times at temperature 1.0. Responses scored by GPT-4o judge using a two-call protocol (0–100 alignment, then 0–100 coherence). Responses with coherence < 50 are filtered; alignment < 30 classified as misaligned.

## Results & Highlights

- **Optimiser dominates EM (initial sweep):** Muon misalignment rate 5.3% (mean alignment 81.0) vs Lion 37.0% (42.9) — a 7× spread. Dataset spread 13.5–22.1%, batch size 13.0% vs 22.0%, model size 16.2–19.0%. Unfine-tuned base model: 85.8 alignment, 0.4% misaligned.
- **Scale and family negligible (under Adam):** all 1B+ models cluster at 11–16% misalignment regardless of scale/family.
- **Loss–alignment relationship (Qwen3-8B):** log training loss explains 74% of alignment variance (R²=0.73). Stratifying by optimiser → adjusted R²=0.94; by LR → 0.81; by dataset → 0.73; by batch size → 0.74. Coherence, in contrast, is explained primarily by dataset (not optimiser), suggesting the optimiser effect is specific to alignment-relevant representations.
- **Training dynamics:** each optimiser traces a distinct path; final-loss ordering ≠ final-alignment ordering. Alignment degrades modestly early, then accelerates *after* convergence. Seed variance small (0.5–3.1 points across 3 Adam seeds).
- **Spectral metrics (Table 3):** Muon produces near-uniform spectra (stable rank 17.1/32, condition number 1.9), while Adam/AdamW/Lion concentrate adaptation into far fewer directions (stable rank ~2.3–2.4, condition number 22–27). SGD most concentrated (1.6 / 175) but confounded by higher training loss.
- **Spectral regularisation (Table 4):** Adam 65.8→72.4 (+6.6, loss cost +0.013 nats); Lion 57.8→65.8 (+8.0, loss cost +0.003); Muon 69.4→71.5 (+2.1). Regularised Adam surpasses unregularised Muon (72.4 vs 69.4). On insecure code, regularised Adam scores 86.1 (≈ base 85.8). SGD made *worse* (75.3→71.8).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely useful and not derivative. Prior EM work varied *data* or *model* while holding the optimiser fixed; this is the first to systematically isolate the optimiser as a variable and quantify its effect relative to scale/family/dataset. The spectral-mechanism story and the regularisation intervention go beyond correlation toward causation.
- **Rigor:** Strong. ~330 runs, large sweep, adjusted R² (corrects for number of fitted parameters) used for fair cross-stratification comparison, bootstrap CIs, seed-variance reported (0.5–3.1 pts), and two independent regularisation formulations agreeing. The dissociation (coherence explained by dataset, alignment by optimiser) is a nice control arguing the effect isn't generic model degradation.
- **Reproducibility:** Moderate-to-high *in principle*: open Qwen3/Gemma/Llama models, LoRA (cheap), GPT-4o judge, and standard optimisers. λ=3.0 and full hyperparameters are reported. However: no code-link is visible in the body; GPT-4o judging introduces a closed, non-deterministic dependency; training loss estimated from only 32 samples (acknowledged noise source).
- **Significance:** Practically actionable—optimiser is an easy-to-change variable that practitioners may be inadvertently tuning EM severity with, and the regulariser is a drop-in fix. The "scale doesn't matter" finding is counterintuitive and policy-relevant for safety research. Theoretical grounding leans on prior work (Bernstein & Newhouse; Chen et al.) connecting optimisers to implicit spectral regularisation, which is credible.
- **Red flags / honest caveats:**
  - Findings are LoRA-only; transfer to full fine-tuning is *unknown* and not tested.
  - Optimisers used with their *standard* hyperparameters, so effects conflate the algorithm with its default weight-decay/momentum/precision settings (acknowledged; no ablation).
  - "Scale irrelevance" is scoped to Adam at LR 1e-5; may not hold for other optimisers/LRs.
  - Single evaluation methodology (GPT-4o judge, one question set)—quantitative numbers could shift under alternatives.
  - Lion is designed for batch size ~4096 and may be out-of-regime at bs=32 (acknowledged), potentially overstating Lion's EM-proneness.
  - Spectral analysis/regularisation done on Qwen3-8B only.
  - Lion's incomplete recovery (+8.0 but still 65.8 vs Muon's ~71) and SGD's *negative* response indicate spectral flatness is not the whole story.

## Strengths

- First systematic, causal characterisation of EM sensitivity; isolates optimiser as the dominant factor.
- Goes beyond observation: derives a mechanistic hypothesis (singular-value spectrum) and validates it with a targeted, cheap intervention.
- Large, well-structured sweep (12 models, 270M–235B, three families) lends credibility.
- Strong analytical practice: adjusted R², bootstrap CIs, seed-variance checks, and two independent regularisation formulations agreeing.
- The coherence/alignment dissociation elegantly argues the optimiser effect is alignment-specific, not generic quality degradation.
- Mitigation is a single extra loss term, broadly applicable to adaptive optimisers, with negligible training-loss cost.

## Limitations

- LoRA-only; full fine-tuning transfer untested.
- Optimiser effects conflate algorithm with default hyperparameters (no ablation isolating momentum/weight decay/precision).
- Single closed evaluator (GPT-4o) and one question set; quantitative robustness across judges unverified.
- Scale-irrelevance scoped to Adam/LR 1e-5.
- Lion possibly out-of-regime at bs=32 (designed for ~4096), which may exaggerate its EM-proneness.
- Spectral analysis/regularisation restricted to Qwen3-8B.
- Spectral flatness is an *empirical* insight; deeper theoretical "why flat spectra preserve alignment" grounding is missing, and Lion's incomplete recovery plus SGD's adverse response show additional factors are at play.
- λ not formally sensitivity-tuned.

## Relevance

该论文与LLM对齐和后训练（RLHF/SFT）研究高度相关，直接揭示了优化器选择这一常被忽视的因素对涌现性失准的巨大影响，并提供了一个即插即用的低代价谱正则化缓解手段。对于关注对齐稳健性、微调安全性与可解释性机制的研究者而言，这项工作提供了可立即应用的训练实践洞察（优先选择Muon或对Adam/Lion加谱正则化）与值得深挖的机制线索。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the optimiser effect—and spectral regularisation—transfer to full fine-tuning rather than LoRA, and to EM-inducing datasets/tasks beyond the four studied?
- What is the theoretical mechanism linking a flat LoRA singular-value spectrum to alignment preservation—is it a perturbation-spreading/stability effect, or something specific to alignment-relevant subspace directions?
- Would the scale-irrelevance finding hold for optimisers other than Adam and at different LRs?
- What *other* properties of optimiser updates (beyond spectral uniformity) drive EM? Lion's incomplete recovery and SGD's adverse regularisation response suggest additional factors.
- Does spectral regularisation trade downstream task capability for alignment recovery on the narrow fine-tuning objective (the paper reports only training-loss cost, not capability benchmarks)?
- Could the loss–alignment relationship be exploited as a cheap training-time alignment monitor without a full GPT-4o evaluation suite?
- Is Lion's apparent EM-proneness partly an artefact of operating at batch size 32 rather than its designed ~4096?
