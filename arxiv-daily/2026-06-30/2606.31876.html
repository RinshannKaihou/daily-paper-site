01

How MARS works

training-free · first generated token only · ≈ original inference cost

An MLLM is split into vision encoder → projection → decoder LLM. Following Arditi et al., a per-layer refusal direction d̂ᵣ is extracted from the <assistant>-token hidden state by contrasting a safe set (ALPACA) against an unsafe set (MaliciousInstruct) — text only. A preliminary study shows these directions do transfer to image/video… but naïve steering over-refuses safe multimodal inputs.

Root cause: PCA + a separation score reveal a modality-specific component that swamps the token activation, aligning with d̂ᵣ regardless of safety semantics. MARS removes it, then steers adaptively.

STEP 01

Activation re-centering

Subtract a neutral mean μ_neu estimated from N randomly-colored images (no semantics). Recovers the safety-relevant structure (Fig. 2b).

STEP 02

ReLU-gated traversal

One-sided steering s = ReLU(⟨h_c,d̂⟩)d̂. Estimated-safe inputs get s=0 — no intervention, no explicit classifier.

STEP 03

Adaptive strength α

Trust-region radius r = min(‖h_c−μ_acc‖,‖h_c−μ_ref‖); set α = r/(‖s‖+ε) to bound displacement within training-data geometry.

STEP 04

Layer selection

Sum of three text-only scores: direction consistency, context separability, safe-text margin. e.g. Qwen3-VL=23, Gemma3=26, Molmo2=22.

→ Applied at all token positions but only at the first generated token (it sets the generation trajectory). No extra forward passes, no training, no multimodal unsafe data.

02

Results & highlights

5 SOTA MLLMs · image + video jailbreak · utility preserved
  • ViSU (image safety): unsafe refusal lifted +25.1% (Gemma3) to +67.9% (Molmo2), with controlled over-refusal — beats training-based SASA on Molmo2, Qwen3-VL, InternVL3.5.
  • HADES (jailbreak): large consistent gains — Molmo2 17.2 → 95.8 (+78.6), Gemma3 +41.3; matches SASA on three models, surpasses it on Molmo2.
  • Video jailbreak: best among training-free — +59.4% Qwen3-VL (28.8→88.2), +52% Qwen3.5, +40.4% Molmo2; even beats SASA on Qwen3-VL (+47.9%).
  • Utility: MMMU/MMMU-Pro held on Gemma3, Molmo2, InternVL3.5, Qwen3-VL. SASA itself takes −10% to −20% utility hits; MARS stays far closer.
  • Weak-alignment stress test (LLaVA-1.5): direction from ~33 refusing samples still gives +32.1% HADES / +28.1% ViSU refusal.

03

Strengths & limitations

best safety-utility trade-off among training-free methods

Strengths

  • No costly multimodal unsafe data — only cheap text (ALPACA / MaliciousInstruct) + random colored images.
  • Training-free, single-token intervention; negligible inference overhead.
  • Clear mechanistic story: misalignment diagnosis → re-centering → adaptive steering → layer selection, each ablated.
  • Broad eval across 5–6 models and image + video jailbreak settings, incl. a weakly-aligned legacy model.
  • Competitive with — sometimes better than — the training-based upper bound (SASA).

Limitations

  • Assumes a usable refusal direction exists; degrades if alignment is absent (only mitigated on LLaVA-1.5).
  • Assumes modality misalignment is linearly removable via centering — may fail in highly non-linear regions.
  • Residual over-refusal on safe inputs remains (Qwen3.5 MMMU 50.8 → 38.2).
  • Trust region & layer selection are empirically-tuned heuristics — no optimality guarantee; adaptive white-box attacks untested.
  • Refusal template metric + text-only Guard are imperfect proxies for true multimodal safety.
  • Generalization beyond 4B–13B models (or audio/3D modalities) is unverified.
04

Open questions

avenues the paper explicitly leaves open
Q1How does cross-modal transfer degrade as the vision encoder / projector architecture changes (token-compressing vs. per-patch)?
Q2Can the optimal intervention layer be predicted a priori from internal statistics, instead of tuned per model?
Q3How robust is the geometric trust region against adaptive white-box attacks steering toward acceptance?
Q4Does re-centering with natural (non-random) images change results, and does the linear assumption break for very large models?
Q5Does the modality-shared safety structure extend to audio, 3D, and much larger MLLMs?
Q6How does MARS compose with input filters, system-prompt defenses, and RLHF alignment?
Q7Could the separation score / centering idea also diagnose which layers are most jailbreak-vulnerable — beyond steering?
