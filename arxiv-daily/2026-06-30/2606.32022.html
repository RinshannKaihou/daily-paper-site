Methodology

formal · no experiments
canonical trace z★ (solid)knots (●)  |  piecewise-linear fit (– – –)

A SemRF is a pair (ϕ, B): a coordinate map ϕ and fixed anchors B = {bk}. Residual states evolve as hℓ+1 = hℓ + uℓ(hℓ); SemRF turns each step into a depth-indexed coordinate zℓ = ϕ(hℓ).

The motivating instance is vocabulary readout — input-side anchors from E := Win⊤, output-side from W̃ := WU+. The core structural assumption is restricted bi-invertibility (Asm. 1): a local condition on the anchor span with ‖UB⊤EB − IK‖ ≤ ε — not a global basis. Pseudo-inverse tying (Prop. 2) gives the exact-synchronization case WU+ = Win⊤.

Anchors induce a semantic Voronoi diagram; diagnostics are motion energy Σ‖Δz‖², curvature energy Σ‖Δ²z‖², and a target-direction backtracking penalty. The margin-relaxed semantic tube admits a unique minimum-action canonical trace.

Strengths

5
  • Cleanly isolates and formalizes a real measurement problem — readout drift — that probing methods gloss over.
  • Geometric apparatus (Voronoi cells, margin tube, minimum-action canonical trace) is elegant and yields crisp uniqueness / spline results.
  • Explicit, falsifiable conditions on every theorem — honest about its scope.
  • Connects interpretability geometry to a conditional efficiency narrative in a principled way.
  • Full proof appendix; PMLR peer-reviewed venue.

Limitations

6
  • Purely theoretical: zero experiments. No model analyzed, no assumption verified, no efficiency claim demonstrated.
  • Strong, unverified assumptions (bi-invertibility, small interface / projection error) that may not hold on large real models.
  • No comparison to existing trajectory / probing diagnostics beyond conceptual positioning.
  • The parameter-efficiency bridge is conditional and local — not a theorem that fewer params ⇒ lower trace complexity.
  • Specialized vocabulary (semantic knots, knowledge density) may limit uptake.
  • Diagnostic-only — describes/compares trajectories, proposes no intervention.

Open questions for further work

6
  1. Do real transformers satisfy admissibility? Measure ‖UB⊤EB−I‖ and projection residuals on GPT-2 / Llama — how often is ε < 1 achievable?
  2. Does SemRF change the trajectory story? How much "apparent motion" is coordinate drift once anchors are fixed?
  3. Is the conditional efficiency link real? Does trace complexity m̄ε(θ;D) empirically track parameter count or pruning ratio?
  4. Trainable objective? Minimize tube-constrained action S[z★]+λm̄ε as a regularizer — does it help efficiency without hurting quality?
  5. Anchor sensitivity. How do trajectory, Voronoi cells, and compressibility change with the choice of anchor span B?
  6. Validity of local linearization (Asm. 2). How tight is ‖εℓ‖ ≤ Cr‖Δhℓ‖² on real residual writes?
