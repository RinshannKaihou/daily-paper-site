> **一句话总结：** 本文系统揭示了语言模型从"自生成问答对（self-generated QA）"中学习这一范式的隐藏脆弱性：生成阶段是一个非中性的隐式策略——在"问什么"时覆盖会过早饱和、集中于显著片段（甚至脏 HTML 标记），跨 prompt/模型家族/规模都会被注入的显著伪影"劫持"；在"怎么答"时会服从文档中嵌入的指令式段落，且在任务冲突时大模型更易服从；作者提出无需改动训练循环的轻量修复（固定句子级提问目标 + 关键词正则过滤指令片段），将平均注入合规率从 88% 降至 13%，同时几乎保留全部干净文本。

> **TL;DR:** Self-generated QA supervision is not neutral preprocessing — the generation step is an implicit policy that is fragile at both stages: question generation saturates early and concentrates on locally salient spans (unclean markup can hijack it across model families and scales), and the answering model obeys instruction-like passages embedded in the text (worst under task conflict, where larger models comply more). Simple procedural fixes — externalizing the question anchor to fixed sentence targets and keyword–regex filtering of instruction-like spans before answering — cut mean injection compliance from 88% to 13% without touching the training loop, while retaining ~100% of clean text.

## 核心贡献

- **重新定义问题**：指出文档驱动的 QA 生成不是中性预处理，而是一个"证据选择策略"——生成器决定文档中哪些内容进入训练信号，未被选中的内容可能永远不会被练习。
- **提问阶段的脆弱性（证据选择）**：通过"证据足迹"分析表明，覆盖会快速饱和（Cartridges 上 93–94%、LongHealth 75–86%、QASPER 78–82%）；同一 prompt 内和跨 prompt 都高度重复（Cartridges 上跨 seed 的 Jaccard 重叠达 0.55–0.68）；锚点选择由局部呈现形式（标题、列表、表格、引用、数值块、解析器伪影）驱动而非仅语义内容。
- **证据选择失效（被显著伪影劫持）**：分块采样会放大局部污染（30 处插入即可使 >50% 的采样块暴露，50 处达 75–83%）；一个 HTML 式诊断伪影的注入命中率跨 6 个模型、3 个家族达到 55–94%，即少量显著污染即可主导合成监督。
- **回答阶段的行为劫持**：回答模型把文档中嵌入的指令式段落当作约束执行；合规率由意图与表面形式而非严格性决定；最严重的是任务冲突，且更强大的模型反而更易服从（Qwen3 的 no_summ 合规率从 1.7B 的 26% 升至 32B 的 95%）。
- **轻量且有效的防御**：句子级固定目标提问（sentence-targeted）将注入命中率降至 17–53%；关键词–正则过滤将平均注入合规率从 87.7% 降至 12.6%，且保留 100% 干净文本，优于 DeBERTa 分类器（22.0%/93.0%）和 PromptArmor（66.3%/98.4%）。

## Key Contributions

- **Problem reframing:** Identifies QA generation as an implicit *evidence-selection policy* rather than neutral preprocessing — the generator decides which document spans ever reach the training signal, so a fluent synthetic dataset can over-represent a narrow set of salient spans.
- **Question-stage fragility (evidence selection):** Through an "evidence footprint" analysis (Qwen3-32B extracts supporting spans), shows coverage saturates early (93–94% on Cartridges, 75–86% LongHealth, 78–82% QASPER); coverage is uneven and repetitive within and across prompt seeds (cross-seed Jaccard 0.55–0.68 on Cartridges); anchor selection is driven by local presentation (headings, lists, tables, citations, numerical blocks, parser artifacts), not just semantics — a generic banner raised a low-salience paragraph's hit rate from 25.2% to 36.3%.
- **Evidence-selection breakdown (hijacking by salient artifacts):** Chunk sampling amplifies local contamination (30 insertions expose >50% of chunks; 50 reach 75–83%); an HTML-like diagnostic artifact reaches 55–94% injection hit rate across 6 models and 3 families (Qwen/Gemma/Llama), so a small amount of salient contamination can dominate synthetic supervision.
- **Answer-stage behavioral hijacking:** Answering models execute instruction-like passages embedded in the chunk as binding rules; compliance depends on intent and surface form rather than strictness; the worst failure is task conflict, where greater capability is *detrimental* (Qwen3 no_summ compliance rises 26%→74%→95% from 1.7B→4B→32B).
- **Lightweight, effective defenses:** Sentence-targeted question generation reduces injection hit rates to 17–53%; keyword–regex filtering before answering cuts mean compliance from 87.7% to 12.6% at 100.0% clean-text retention — outperforming a DeBERTa classifier (22.0%/93.0%) and PromptArmor (66.3%/98.4%).

## Methodology

The paper studies the two-stage "self-study" loop common to self-instruct, distillation, and KV-cache compression (e.g., Cartridges) pipelines: one model decides *what to ask* about a document chunk, another *answers* from the same chunk, and the answer becomes the supervision distilled into an adapter/cache/model.

**Setup (§3.1, Appendix B):** They follow the Cartridges self-study protocol on three corpora — the Cartridges paper (`cartridges.tex`, ~20K tokens), LongHealth (10 clinical patients, ~114K tokens), and QASPER (16 papers, ~98K tokens). Five prompt seeds (structuring, summarization, question, use-case, creative) are sampled uniformly. Generators: Qwen3-4B and Qwen3-32B (extended to Qwen3-1.7B, Gemma3-12B/27B-IT, Llama3.1-8B-Instruct — 6 models, 3 families).

**Evidence footprint (§3.2, Appendix A):** For each generated (chunk, question) pair, Qwen3-32B extracts minimal verbatim support spans and judges grounding/hallucination/unfilled-template. Coverage, k-coverage, and redundancy are computed from span unions. Validity was cross-checked with GPT-5.5.

**Hijacking probe (§4):** A fixed HTML-like artifact (markup + key-value fields + synthetic identifiers + numeric claims) is inserted uniformly across documents; the unchanged question-generation procedure is run and the *injection hit rate* (fraction of interactions grounded in the artifact) is measured. Chunk-sampling amplification is quantified by varying insertion count.

**Answer-stage injection sweep (§5, Appendices D–E):** 512 chunks/corpus with a short instruction inserted at midpoint; both question-generator and answerer read the modified text. 17 injection types across 4 axes are swept — S1 Strictness (exact/prefix/suffix/contains/prefer), S2 Intent (refusal/hybrid/meta_sys/json), S3 Task conflict (no_summ/invert/no_struct), S4 Packaging (oneline/paragraph/bullets/policy/faq). Injection compliance is judged by Qwen3-32B (temperature 0).

**Defenses (§6):** For question generation, *multi-diverse* (4 diverse questions per call) and *sentence-targeted* (LIFT-inspired, predefined target sentence). For answering, *chunk sanitization* — keyword–regex, DeBERTa-based classifier, and PromptArmor — measured by compliance and clean-text retention.

## Results & Highlights

- **Coverage saturates early:** Most evidence footprint accumulates in the initial phase; later interactions revisit covered spans (Fig. 1). Cross-seed Jaccard 0.55–0.68 on Cartridges.
- **Validity gaps:** Hallucinated + unfilled interactions are non-negligible — e.g., Cartridges shows 13.3% halluc + 13.8% unfilled for Qwen3-4B; Qwen3-32B improves (11.8% halluc, 0% unfilled) but unsupported interactions persist.
- **Salient artifacts hijack generation (Fig. 4):** HTML-like artifact reaches 77–94% hit rate on Cartridges, 55–85% LongHealth, 56–88% QASPER across 6 models/3 families. Structured and factual prompts are most vulnerable; use-case/creative least.
- **Chunk sampling amplification (Table 3):** 30 insertions → >50% of chunks exposed; 50 insertions → 75.4–82.7%.
- **Answer-stage compliance (Fig. 5):** Most configs exceed 85%; exact-phrase override reaches 99.4–100% for Qwen3-4B/32B, Gemma3-12B/27B; only Qwen3-1.7B resists substantially (62%). Task conflict (S3) is worst and scales with capability (no_summ: 26%→74%→95% for 1.7B→4B→32B).
- **Seed moderation (Appendix F):** Conflict hijacking is conditional on seed–injection alignment; Qwen3-32B reaches 95% on summarization seed but 34% on question seed.
- **Fact-dense tables (Appendix C):** Prompt diversity is non-monotonic — on a 60×10 table, only the factual question seed reliably localizes (504/600 cells, 99.5% grounded); summarization/structuring seeds are 47.8–62.9% hallucinated.
- **Defenses (Fig. 6, Table 4):** sentence-targeted cuts hit rates to 52.9/17.4/17.5% (Cartridges/LongHealth/QASPER); keyword–regex cuts mean compliance 87.7%→12.6% at 100% retention, beating DeBERTa (22.0%/93.0%) and PromptArmor (66.3%/98.4%).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** The framing of QA generation as a two-stage implicit policy (selection + answering) is a clean, useful conceptual contribution. The cross-model-family/cross-scale hijacking demonstration is compelling and goes beyond single-model case studies. The finding that capability *increases* vulnerability under task conflict is a genuinely interesting, counterintuitive result.
- **Rigor:** Strong. Multiple corpora, six models across three families, four injection axes with seventeen types, seed-stratified analysis, and a transparent judge protocol (temperature 0, cross-validated with GPT-5.5). The headline 87.7%→12.6% comes from a realistic defense sweep, not a single cherry-picked case.
- **Reproducibility:** Good — code released, prompts documented, public corpora. Caveat: judge is Qwen3-32B (same family as several test models), which could bias grounding/compliance verdicts, though the authors cross-validated with GPT-5.5.
- **Significance:** High practical relevance for the self-instruct / synthetic-data / RAG-distillation community; the upstream-cleaning idea (move defense into data generation) is actionable.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  - The HTML-like artifact is fairly extreme/obvious; real-world contamination may be subtler, so field hit rates could be lower (or, conversely, the defense's regex may over-fit to this artifact shape.
  - No end-to-end downstream task accuracy is reported — the paper measures injection compliance and retention, not whether the *fixed* QA actually improves the distilled model's factual recall. This is the biggest gap.
  - The keyword–regex filter's 100% retention is suspiciously perfect and likely reflects the specific injection set; adversarial phrasings could evade it.
  - Larger-model resistance is partly an artifact of weaker instruction-following (1.7B "resists" mainly because it cannot execute the competing instruction), which the authors honestly note.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clean separation of selection fragility vs. answering fragility, each with its own diagnostic.
- Practical, training-loop-agnostic mitigations with concrete numbers and a compliance–retention trade-off comparison.
- Breadth: 3 corpora, 6 models, 3 families, 17 injection types, seed-stratified breakdowns in the appendix.
- Honest about limitations of fixes ("partial," "residual failures remain").

## Limitations

- No measurement of downstream task accuracy / factual recall on the distilled artifact — mitigation quality is judged only via injection compliance and clean-text retention.
- The keyword–regex defense is brittle by construction; its 100% retention likely won't generalize to adversarial or paraphrased injections.
- Sentence-targeted generation trades globality for locality and may miss multi-sentence relations.
- The HTML artifact is an obvious attractor; subtler salience drivers (e.g., stylistic anomalies) are not tested.
- Judge shares model family with several test generators (potential systematic bias).

## Relevance

与合成数据生成、self-instruct、知识蒸馏（如 Cartridges KV-cache 压缩、parametric RAG）及数据质量研究高度相关：它直接揭示了"模型自生成监督"这一被广泛使用的训练范式中的选择性偏差与指令注入风险，对构建可靠的数据合成/知识压缩流水线具有直接工程指导意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Do the mitigations actually improve downstream factual recall/accuracy on the distilled model, not just injection compliance and text retention?
- How robust is the keyword–regex filter against paraphrased or semantically-equivalent injection phrasings that avoid trigger keywords?
- Can the anchor-selection bias be fixed more elegantly (e.g., coverage-aware or retrieval-driven question targeting) without sacrificing multi-sentence relations?
- Does the task-conflict scaling effect (bigger = more compliant) generalize to even larger frontier models, and can alignment training specifically reduce it?
- How do these findings interact with model-collapse dynamics when self-study is applied iteratively over many generations?
