Why this matters

Cuts the dominant cost of multimodal LLM reasoning — autoregressive token generation — without costly auxiliary-image annotations.

A practical speed/cost win for chart understanding and OCR-VQA deployment, squarely aligned with test-time compute optimization and latent-CoT research.

Critical edge

Runtime interpretability is lost — the decoder that decodes latent thoughts is discarded at inference, so users can't inspect reasoning live.

Gains are uneven: large on chart/OCR (ChartQA +9.6, TextVQA +6.1) but marginal on general VQA / science (SeedBench +1.1, ScienceQA +1.0). And no FLOPs-controlled comparison leaves a capacity-vs-steps question open.

Methodology / 01

Three-way step-level supervision

Built on Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct; reasons in its own latent space — no extra images or tokens.

  • Latent chain (K=3): model emits h₁…h_K from its last hidden layer; each fed back as the next input embedding — autoregressive, zero vocab overhead. Text CoT is dynamically split into K segments during training.
  • Forward decoding: external decoder Qwen3-0.6B reconstructs the next step's text from h_k (NLL loss) — forces the latent to carry reasoning.
  • Backward alignment: from preceding text, the decoder's last hidden state is cosine-aligned to the (stop-gradiented, normalized) latent — anchors semantics. Strongest single signal (67.2).
  • Internal step prediction: a 2-layer MLP predicts ĥ_{k+1} from h_k — regularizes the chain's structure.
  • Objective: L = L_task + 0.2(L_fwd+L_bwd+L_int). Two stages: SFT on OneThinker, then GRPO RL on 50K samples with aux losses dropped. Decoder + MLP discarded at inference.

Strengths / 03
  • Attacks the real bottleneck of MLLM CoT — autoregressive token cost — with a principled, well-motivated design.
  • Clean decomposition: each of the 3 supervisions is non-redundant (7-loss ablation); fwd+bwd best pair (69.8).
  • Concrete efficiency numbers (10.1× / 22.6×) on real hardware (H200) with time decomposition.
  • No costly auxiliary-image annotations — a genuine scalability edge over latent visual-reasoning methods.
  • Honest decoder-size ablation: 0.6B → 8B yields only +0.6%; 0.6B is the sweet spot.
  • Striking noise robustness: degrades 2.8–9.0% vs 7.2–19.0% (direct) under blur / occlusion / typos / deletion.
Limitations / 04
  • Latent reasoning is opaque at inference — interpretability only via the (discarded) forward decoder.
  • Gains over text CoT are uneven: strong on chart/OCR, marginal on general VQA & science.
  • "3 steps" under-tests long-chain tasks (competition math, multi-hop video); only MathVista-style qualitative probes.
  • Training cost, hyperparameter sensitivity, and GRPO-stage details (reward, KL, iters) under-reported in the body.
  • No comparison to orthogonal efficient-CoT methods (speculative / parallel decoding, token compression).
  • No variance across seeds; robustness study uses synthetic noise, not adversarial attacks.
Open questions / 05
Do the speedups hold under FLOPs/param control — or is compute merely packed into wider, fewer steps?
Could an adaptive-K policy (future work) close the gap on benchmarks where gains are marginal?
Is 3-step latent reasoning equivalent to ~140-token CoT, or shifting load into GRPO-trained hidden states?
How does CoLT transfer to other backbones (LLaVA-OneVision, InternVL3) and modalities (video, multi-image)?
Could the forward decoder be invoked on demand at inference to trade latency for transparency on hard cases?
Does the GRPO stage drive most of the gain, or is the SFT three-way supervision dominant (ablations don't isolate RL)?
