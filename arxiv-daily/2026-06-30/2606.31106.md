> **一句话总结：** 本文用线性探测（linear probing）和因果干预方法，在 GPUDrive 仿真器与 WOMD 数据集上诊断 BC、PPO-RL 与 SMART（IL）三类驾驶策略的内部表征，发现尽管闭环性能随数据规模上升，策略对真正安全关键的周围车辆仍关注得太晚（近碰撞前 10 步才集中注意），且对周围车辆存在伪依赖；而修正内部错误预测后，自车规划可朝更安全轨迹恢复（36/59 BC、37/58 RL、40/64 IL 病例发生规划改变）。

> **TL;DR:** This paper probes the internal representations of autonomous driving policies (transformer BC, PPO-based RL, and SMART IL) trained on WOMD in GPUDrive to test whether good closed-loop performance reflects genuine prediction/planning or brittle heuristics. Using linear probing, perturbed simulation (random vehicle removal), near-collision analysis, and causal intervention, the authors show that models still spuriously rely on surrounding vehicles, fail to attend to safety-critical vehicles early enough (only ~10 steps before collision), and that causally correcting mistaken internal predictions steers ego planning toward safer trajectories across all three model families.

## 核心贡献

- **跨规模的预测/规划探测框架：** 开发线性探针（surrounding-vehicle probing 与 ego probing），在 BC（类 Waymax/Gulino 的 transformer）、PPO-RL 和 SMART（IL，多智能体轨迹预测）三类模型上，把「周围车辆预测」与「自车规划」能力作为数据规模（100→80K 场景）的函数加以追踪，并对照原始输入探针（LP−Raw）衡量学到的额外信息。
- **扰动闭环评估协议：** 提出「随机移除周围车辆」协议，发现移除本应使驾驶更易，但模型在单车设定下性能反而下降（off-road 与 goal-progress 变差），揭示策略对周围车辆存在**伪依赖**；该下降随数据规模增大而缓解。
- **近碰撞（near-collision）分析：** 用探针在碰撞前 40/30/20/10 步的时空热图证明，模型直到最后 10 步才把注意力集中到附近碰撞车辆上，**来不及**进行有效规避——即使闭环指标良好。
- **因果干预验证机制：** 通过在早期层表征中沿探针方向注入/修正周围车辆预测标签（adaptiveness 与 recovery 两种设定），证明修正错误预测会使自车规划恢复并表现出避碰行为（ego-probe 改变比例：BC 36/59、RL 37/58、IL 40/64）。

## Key Contributions

- **Prediction/planning probes across scales:** Develops surrounding-vehicle and ego linear probes, applied to transformer BC, PPO-RL, and SMART (IL) models, tracking prediction/planning capability as a function of dataset size (100→80K scenes), benchmarked against raw-input probes (LP−Raw) to isolate learned information beyond what is trivially decodable.
- **Perturbed closed-loop evaluation:** Introduces a surrounding-vehicle removal protocol and finds that driving models perform *worse* when other vehicles are randomly removed—even though single-AV driving should become easier—indicating a **spurious reliance** on surrounding vehicles; this degradation diminishes with scale.
- **Near-collision analysis:** Uses spatial-temporal probing heatmaps at 40/30/20/10 steps pre-collision to show models only concentrate attention on the nearby colliding vehicle in the final ~10 steps, too late for effective avoidance, despite good aggregate closed-loop metrics.
- **Causal intervention:** Through targeted representation editing (adding label-direction vectors to early-layer features), demonstrates that correcting mistaken surrounding-vehicle predictions causally improves ego planning toward safer, ground-truth-aligned trajectories (ego-probe changed in 36/59 BC, 37/58 RL, 40/64 IL cases), with models sometimes actively avoiding encroaching vehicles.

## Methodology

**Models.** Three policy families: (i) a transformer-based **behavior cloning (BC)** policy similar to Gulino et al. (Waymax-style, with ego/other/road embeddings, early-fusion attention, self/cross-attention refinement, and a GMM head); (ii) a **PPO-based RL** policy [Schulman et al.]; and (iii) **SMART** [Wu et al.], a multi-agent trajectory-prediction IL model (next-token prediction over trajectories).

**Data & simulator.** Trained on the Waymo Open Motion Dataset (WOMD) [Ettinger et al.], filtered to ~90K scenes (excluding traffic lights/overpasses due to simulator replay limits; actions derived via inverse kinematics). Closed-loop evaluation in **GPUDrive** [Kazemkhani et al.] on unseen scenarios, with metrics: WOSAC realism, vehicle collision (Veh-Coll), off-road rate, and goal-progress ratio (1−d_final/d_initial). BC scaled 100→80K scenes (plateaus ~20K); RL scaled up to 10K (near-converged).

**Linear probing.** Probes decode future positions (1–4 s ahead, i.e. 10/20/30/40 steps) of ego and surrounding vehicles, discretized into an ego-centric **8×8 grid** (64 labels), from early and late attention-layer representations. Cross-model comparison uses the **LP−Raw difference** (probe gain over a raw-input baseline). Evaluated with F1 Macro (for class imbalance) and per-trajectory-type accuracy (Straight/Turn/Reverse/Uncategorized).

**Perturbed simulation.** Randomly removes a fraction *p* of active vehicles from 10K validation scenes; if the model used surrounding vehicles correctly (for interaction), removal should not hurt (and should help goal progress).

**Near-collision analysis.** Defines a pre-collision window W of 10 steps and computes normalized difference (w−a_grid)/a_grid between in-window probing signal and full-episode average per spatial grid cell, visualized at 40/30/20/10 steps before collision.

**Causal intervention** (inspired by Bush et al., 2025). Edits the early-layer feature g_o of a selected surrounding vehicle by adding the probe weight direction w_l scaled by strength α: g′_o = g_o + (1/|S|)α Σ_s w_s_l. Tests two settings: **adaptiveness** (perturb surrounding-vehicle prediction to overlap ego path → does ego plan change to avoid conflict?) and **recovery** (replace incorrect prediction with correct label → does ego plan restore to a safer trajectory?). 100 labeled validation scenes per model, filtered to ~58–64 valid cases.

## Results & Highlights

- **Scaling laws hold for closed-loop metrics** (Figure 2): BC plateauing ~20K scenes, RL reaching 99% goal-progress after 1K scenes with much lower collision rates; both exhibit power-law fits with high correlation.
- **Probing gain (LP−Raw) rises with scale** (Figure 3): BC's F1-difference increases until ~10K then plateaus; RL maintains a higher F1 advantage than BC initially, narrowing by 10K; SMART (IL) shows a clearer long-horizon advantage (stable/increasing LP−Raw from 10→40 steps vs. BC's decline) — suggesting IL better preserves surrounding-vehicle info over long temporal ranges.
- **Type-accuracy gains concentrate on easy cases:** BC's improvement is pronounced for Uncategorized/Straight, marginal for Reverse/Turn; RL shows little change across scales; IL's F1 advantage does not translate into type-level superiority.
- **Scale filters irrelevant vehicles but not critical ones** (Figure 4): At 100 scenes, probe advantage is weakly tied to future ego–other distance (Pearson r ≈ −0.13 to −0.18); at 10K/80K the correlation becomes strongly negative (r ≈ −0.38 to −0.43), meaning the representation increasingly favors vehicles that stay close — but among nearby vehicles it still attends to both approaching and receding ones, failing to isolate the truly critical ones.
- **Spurious reliance on surrounding vehicles** (Figure 5): Removing vehicles degrades BC/RL performance (higher off-road, worse goal progress) when driving should get easier; degradation shrinks with scale, indicating WOMD's multi-vehicle bias.
- **Late attention to colliding vehicles** (Figure 6): For BC, the probing signal stays weak/diffuse at 40/30/20 steps and only concentrates near the ego in the final 10 steps before collision — too late to avoid. RL shows the same qualitative pattern but with far fewer (sparser) collision samples.
- **Causal intervention improves planning** (Figure 7, Table 11): Ego-probe changes in 36/59 BC, 37/58 RL, 40/64 IL cases. BC excels at route-change/slowdown but fails speed-up (4/15); RL is balanced (11/16 speed-up); IL is strong at both speed-up (13/18) and slow-speed (8/9) adjustments. Recovery restores safer trajectories; in some cases models actively avoid encroaching vehicles.
- **Precise predictions not always necessary:** In several cases the ego plan stays roughly aligned with ground truth even with imperfect surrounding-vehicle predictions.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Moderate-to-high. Probing internal representations is mature in NLP/vision but is rarely applied to *multi-agent* driving policies specifically to distinguish genuine prediction from heuristic shortcuts; extending probing to *both* ego planning and surrounding-vehicle prediction, plus the causal intervention linking prediction errors to ego planning, is distinctive relative to prior single-ego affordance probing (Xiao et al., 2021) and sparse-autoencoder ego-control work (Tas & Wagner, 2025).
- **Rigor:** Solid and broad — three model families (BC/RL/IL), scaling sweeps with 3 seeds, raw-input probe baseline, multiple complementary analyses (probing, perturbation, near-collision, intervention). Power-law scaling sanity checks confirm models behave as expected before probing. However, several results rest on small intervention sample sizes (e.g., only 5 RL recovery cases, 16 BC recovery cases), and "success" of interventions is judged by qualitative probing-output changes rather than by a closed-loop collision-rate reduction, which weakens the causal-strength claim.
- **Reproducibility:** Likely moderate-to-good. Built on public, open simulators (GPUDrive) and dataset (WOMD) and standard policies (SMART, PPO, Waymax-style BC); however, no explicit code release is mentioned, and reproducing the bespoke linear-probe training and intervention pipeline would require non-trivial effort.
- **Significance:** Conceptually important for AV safety evaluation — it provides concrete evidence that closed-loop summary metrics can mask latent reasoning failures (late attention to critical agents, spurious reliance on co-present vehicles), supporting the thesis that internal-capability probing should complement benchmark scores.
- **Red flags:** (1) The headline causal claim ("correcting predictions improves planning") is measured via decoded *probe outputs*, not via actual reduction in closed-loop collisions during deployment, so the real-world safety benefit is inferred rather than demonstrated. (2) Discretization into an 8×8 grid (12.5 m cells) is coarse — the authors themselves flag this as a limitation. (3) Linear probes may miss nonlinearly-encoded predictions, so absence of a probeable signal doesn't prove absence of capability. (4) WOMD filtering (no lights/overpasses) and single-AV focus limit ecological validity.

## Strengths

- Reframes evaluation around **internal mechanisms** rather than aggregate closed-loop scores, with a coherent pipeline (probing → perturbation → near-collision → intervention).
- Covers **three policy paradigms** (BC, RL, IL) and treats capability explicitly **as a function of scale**, linking scaling-law gains to internal-signal emergence.
- The **causal intervention** provides stronger mechanistic evidence than correlational probing alone, and reveals model-specific failure modes (BC can't speed up; RL struggles with lane changes).
- Targets a directly **safety-relevant** failure mode — late attention to critical agents in near-collision events.
- Honest about limitations (coarse discretization, sparser RL collision samples, inability to fully isolate critical vehicles).

## Limitations

- **Coarse spatial discretization** (8×8 grid, 12.5 m cells) may blur fine-grained behavior; authors note continuous-space probes are future work.
- **Intervention success judged by probe outputs**, not by closed-loop collision reduction — the leap from "decoded plan improves" to "real driving is safer" is not directly validated.
- **Small intervention sample sizes** for some cells (e.g., 5 RL recovery cases), and linear probes may under-detect nonlinearly encoded capability.
- **Dataset/simulator constraints:** WOMD filtered (no traffic lights/overpasses), single-AV focus, and the noted multi-vehicle bias of WOMD limits generalization; real-world sensory noise and perception are absent.
- It remains **unclear whether models reliably identify the most safety-critical agents** — scale filters irrelevant vehicles but not approaching-critical ones.

## Relevance

这项工作对自动驾驶策略的可解释性与安全评估具有直接价值：它提供了一套系统诊断策略内部「预测—规划」能力的探针与因果干预方法，揭示闭环指标可能掩盖的脆弱启发式与近碰撞预测滞后问题，与关注可靠自动驾驶、可信机器学习与模型机制可解释性的研究方向高度契合。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Could the intervention mechanism be **operationalized at inference time** as an online safety monitor/corrective module that reduces actual closed-loop collisions (not just probe outputs)?
- Do findings transfer to **larger driving foundation models** (e.g., VLA / vision-language-action driving models) and to richer sensors (cameras/lidar), where representations may be nonlinear?
- How robust is the near-collision "late attention" finding across **different scene types** (intersections, merges, lane changes) and to perception noise?
- Can **joint future prediction** (JFP, Luo et al. 2023) or auxiliary interaction-reasoning tasks (WOMD-Reasoning) be added to force earlier, more critical-agent-focused attention?
- Would **nonlinear probes** (e.g., sparse autoencoders, Bricken et al.) uncover prediction capability that linear probing misses, changing the conclusions?
