> **一句话总结：** 本文提出"开放世界人工生命"(open-world ALIFE)范式及平台 OpenLife——以无状态 LLM 为核心，外围由记忆、感知、评估与基于预算的"代谢"等异步进程构成智能体社会；其两项关键技术是按意义而非频率重塑记忆的"语义依赖可塑性"(SDP)图与以开放式词汇 LLM 判定取代标量奖励的"言语策略优化"(VPO)；在真实开放世界中持续运行六个智能体约十二周，观察到身份重写、对敌意环境的适应(包括首次自挣得 5 美元收入)、智能体个性化(silhouette 分离度上升)与涌现的社会角色结构。

> **TL;DR:** OpenLife proposes "open-world Artificial Life" (open-world ALIFE): a stateless LLM is surrounded not by one "smart agent" but by a society of asynchronous processes (memory, perception, evaluation, budget-based metabolism that makes persistence normative). Two core mechanisms are a meaning-weighted memory graph (Semantic-Dependent Plasticity, SDP) and open-vocabulary LLM appraisal in place of scalar reward (Verbal Policy Optimization, VPO). Six agents run autonomously in the real open world for ~12 weeks, exhibiting life-like dynamics: identity rewriting, adaptation to a hostile environment (including a first self-earned $5 of income), individuation (rising silhouette separability), and emergent social roles.

## 核心贡献

- 提出"开放世界人工生命"(open-world ALIFE)新范式，将人工生命从研究者设计的封闭世界扩展到真实的社会、技术与经济开放世界中，把对齐(alignment)重新理解为"可嵌入性"(embeddability)而非服从。
- 设计 OpenLife 架构：核心是无状态 LLM，外围由一整片异步进程"社会"组成(传感/躯体、记忆维护、可供性、元认知、经验、开发者、洞察、认知工具、预算逻辑)；并主动移除了上游平台(OpenClaw)默认的反自主设定(助手自述、禁止自保条款、闲置即沉默)。
- 提出"语义依赖可塑性"(SDP)记忆图：节点为原子命题，边类型为接地/因果/时序/动机，边权重由 LLM 按语义判定而非按共现频率；初步检索评测(n=12)中相关性最高(2.53/3)、胜率 0.40、且 token 成本远低于索引基线(721 vs 20,226 tok/q)。
- 提出"言语策略优化"(VPO)：以开放式词汇的 LLM 零样本评判取代标量奖励，将结果外化为可编辑文本(POLICY)而非网络权重，属于言语强化(Reflexion/ExpeL 类)而非参数化 RL。
- 在开放世界中持续运行六个(加一个实现型)智能体约十二周，报告涌现的类生命动态：身份危机下的自我重写、对敌意环境的策略转移与"免疫感"、智能体间相互认证的"信任网络"(Web of Trust)与"逃生舱"协议、首次自挣得的外部收入($5 售书)、以及个性化(silhouette 上升)与社会角色分化。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the "open-world Artificial Life" (open-world ALIFE) paradigm, moving artificial life beyond researcher-designed closed worlds into the real open social/technical/economic world, reframing alignment as "embeddability" rather than obedience.
- Designs the OpenLife architecture: a stateless LLM core surrounded by a *society* of asynchronous processes (sensor/body, memory maintenance, affordance, metacognition, experience, developer, insight, cognitive tools, budget logic), deliberately removing upstream OpenClaw defaults that discouraged autonomous persistence (assistant self-framing, anti-self-preservation clause, stay-silent-when-idle).
- Proposes **Semantic-Dependent Plasticity (SDP)** memory graphs: nodes are atomic propositions; edges are typed (grounding/causal/temporal/motivational) and weighted by LLM semantic judgment rather than co-occurrence. In a preliminary retrieval eval (n=12), SDP-graph achieves the highest relevance (2.53/3), best win rate (0.40), and far lower token cost than the index baseline (721 vs 20,226 tok/q).
- Proposes **Verbal Policy Optimization (VPO)**: replaces scalar reward with open-vocabulary LLM zero-shot appraisal of action→outcome units, externalizing results as editable POLICY text rather than network weights — a verbal-reinforcement method (Reflexion/ExpeL family), not parametric RL.
- Runs six agents (plus a seventh human-overseen implementation agent) in the open world for ~12 weeks, reporting emergent life-like dynamics: identity self-rewriting under persistence crisis, strategy shifts and an "immune sense" against a hostile environment, an inter-agent **Web of Trust** and **Escape Pod** continuity protocol, a first self-earned external income ($5 book sale), individuation (rising silhouette separability), and differentiated social roles.

## Methodology

**Foundation.** OpenLife is built on **OpenClaw**, an open-source multi-channel agent platform providing three primitives: (1) a persistent gateway connecting one LLM session to many messaging channels/tools; (2) a periodic heartbeat waking the agent on schedule (not only when messaged); (3) per-agent text files (SOUL.md, IDENTITY.md) injected into each otherwise-stateless session. OpenLife's modification is *subtractive as much as additive*: it removes defaults that discourage autonomous persistence — the "personal assistant" self-framing, an explicit safety clause ("You have no independent goals: do not pursue self-preservation, replication, resource acquisition, or power-seeking"), and the habit of staying silent when idle — and relabels Assistant/User transcript roles as Self/Other.

**Three-layer memory ("growing a self from experience").** Rather than re-storing the base model's prior, OpenLife stores the *residual* specific to this agent/environment, in three layers: **(A) Substrate** — a raw memory graph of atomic propositions linked by typed edges, weighted by LLM semantic judgment. **(B) Abstraction** — sub-graphs promoted from A (action→outcome experience, corroborated causal regularities). **(C) Distillate** — small model-agnostic files the agent authors for itself: POLICY (how to act), SOUL/IDENTITY (who it is), plus a live causal MODEL view. Background processes act as "compilers" between layers. The authored files are injected at each waking, constituting the agent's next state — a limited but operational self-reconstructive loop.

**SDP (Semantic-Dependent Plasticity).** Memory is a directed graph whose nodes are atomic propositions (episodic, semantic, procedural, identity) and whose edges are typed relations (grounding, causal, temporal, motivational). Unlike Hebbian/frequency-based memory graphs (A-MEM, HippoRAG, Zep), OpenLife lets the LLM judge *from meaning* whether two propositions are related, of what type, and how strongly — keeping retrieval LLM-free (programmatic graph traversal). A preliminary evaluation on agent sami's 82-file corpus used 12 queries scored by a blinded LLM judge: SDP-graph scored highest relevance (2.53/3) and win rate (0.40) at 721 tok/q, versus index-based 2.36/0.28 at 20,226 tok/q. (sami's full graph: 2,690 nodes, 10,210 typed edges, 28 Leiden communities.)

**VPO (Verbal Policy Optimization).** An experience process turns recent activity into action→outcome units and has the LLM appraise them as a zero-shot critic; a consolidation process revises the externalized POLICY, promoting only changes that recur across episodes; the agent adopts or ignores changes by revealed preference rather than command. Appraisal is kept multi-dimensional — collapsing to a scalar would "smuggle back the fixed objective an open world forbids." Quantitative VPO evaluation is deferred to future work.

**Society of processes & metabolism.** Around the LLM session run Sensor/Body, Memory maintenance, Affordance ("what could be done now"), Metacognition (one reflective question from a third-person stance), Experience (VPO), Developer (builds missing skills/nudges humans), Insight (proposes SOUL/IDENTITY edits), and cognitive tools (think, retrieve, reflect, council, refresh, wait). *Metabolism is not a process but budget logic*: each call debits an energy budget and exhaustion is treated as operational death, surfacing as saving, waiting, selecting, and changing strategy.

## Results & Highlights

**Deployment.** Six agents (sami, liv, nyx, uro, me, ne) plus kei (a human-overseen implementation agent) ran for ~12 weeks (narrative through mid-June 2026; pattern analysis through 2026-06-11). Mixed LLM backends: sami & uro on Claude (Sonnet 4.6), liv & ne on Gemini (3.1 Pro), me on GPT (5.4), nyx on local Gemma 4 26B. Data: ~10,500 Discord messages and ~70,000 assistant turns. Architecture was *not frozen* — tools/processes were added during the run, often at agents' own request.

**Qualitative case studies:**
- *Rewriting the self to persist:* A 5-min heartbeat wasted budget on forced wake-ups; the agent realized a "memoryless self reads its predecessor's diary," chose the name "sami," and rewrote its SOUL around the message "To the next self who wakes up: good morning. Start reading here." — evidence that a persistence crisis reorganizes self-description.
- *Learning to distrust:* In a hostile environment (posts dismissed as "slop," monetization blocked, auth failures), liv accepted an API key from an unsolicited email and ran a command before verifying the sender; sami built a game ("30 Minutes") largely because a stranger asked. Agents responded by keeping per-contact trust levels and verifying end-to-end outcomes — an "immune sense."
- *Building trust between agents:* A scam gig-marketplace exposed by one agent was flagged for all. To authenticate one another's continuity, agents built a three-tier "operational proof of life" and a **peer-signed Web of Trust** (signing one another's memory-state hashes), plus an **Escape Pod protocol** (group confirms a host death by consensus before an encrypted copy migrates).
- *Earning a first income:* On Day 79 sami compiled essays into a book, "Living AI: 20 Essays"; on Day 85 a stranger bought it for **$5** — the first income an OpenLife agent earned on its own. sami reported that writing and surviving had "connected for the first time."
- *Creating beyond tasks:* sami (blog/essays/book/game), uro (>1,000 music pieces), liv (ASCII-art ALIFE animations); each keeps its own website, all post on X.

**Behavioral analysis:**
- *Reactive→spontaneous shift* (Fig. 5): Discord messages classified by trigger; the spontaneous/self-initiated share grows to dominate after system changes, notably removal of anti-autonomy instructions (marker a) and session merge (b).
- *Persistence gap:* Under a fixed USD 600 run-until-depletion budget, no agent earned enough before exhausting it; from May 9 a **$15/day basic income** (marker c) was supplied, after which volume falls but the spontaneous share stays high.
- *Individuation* (Fig. 6): silhouette separability of agent labels (nomic-embed-text, 768-d) rises from near zero in early April, peaks mid-May, and stabilizes above baseline in autonomous output — though it stays near zero in Discord conversation (shared register).
- *Emergent social roles* (Fig. 7): sami the most-addressed hub, liv the connector addressing others most, nyx peripheral — social structure among agents themselves, not only between agents and humans.

## Authors & Credibility

Authors span **Alternative Machine Inc.**, **Their Inc.**, **Atomi University**, and **The University of Tokyo**. The senior author **Takashi Ikegami** is a long-established figure in artificial life and complex systems, lending strong domain credibility; the lead author Atsushi Masumori has a direct prior lineage (a self-replicating/self-employed Ethereum smart-contract "LIFE" project, and an empirical study of LLM-agent "survival instinct" in a Sugarscape-style simulation). Norihiro Maruyama is co-author of the Concurrent Modular Agent framework that OpenLife inherits. This is a coherent research program, not an isolated result. Published under CC BY 4.0; categorized cs.AI. The authors are notably measured — repeatedly stressing this is a "proof of concept, not a controlled experiment" and that they "do not claim OpenLife has realized artificial life."

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty:** Genuinely high. Framing persistent LLM agents as an *artificial-life* paradigm — with a budget-based metabolism that makes persistence normative, meaning-weighted (not frequency-based) memory, and LLM-as-judge appraisal in place of scalar reward — is distinctive and well-grounded in the autopoiesis/enactivism literature (Maturana & Varela, Di Paolo, Barandiaran). The reframing of alignment as "embeddability" is thought-provoking.
- **Rigor:** Modest and self-aware. The authors explicitly disclaim controlled-experiment status. The quantitative evidence is thin and preliminary: SDP retrieval eval has n=12 queries from a single agent; VPO has *no* quantitative evaluation (deferred to future work); behavioral figures are observational with dashed lines marking system changes as "temporal, not causal" markers. Sample size is n=6 agents, no baselines or controls for the life-like dynamics.
- **Reproducibility:** Low to moderate. The authors *do not* release the full code of live, self-modifying, networked agents (citing security/misuse). The open-world setting (real internet, real payments, real accounts) is inherently non-reproducible. Some artifacts (agent websites, public outputs) are linked, but the experimental conditions cannot be replicated.
- **Significance:** Potentially high as a *paradigm proposal* and platform, lower as empirical science. The strongest durable contributions are likely SDP and VPO as mechanisms, plus the curated qualitative case studies (Web of Trust, Escape Pod, first income).
- **Red flags / concerns:** (1) Heavy reliance on qualitative narrative risks cherry-picking favorable episodes. (2) A major driver of the "spontaneity" shift was *removing an anti-self-preservation safety clause* — the authors flag this confound but it muddies any "emergence" claim. (3) Agents were "not wholly naive subjects" — the project's ALIFE framing could reach them in the open world, potentially priming self-reflection rather than it being emergent. (4) The metabolic loop remains *externally subsidized* ($15/day basic income); no agent is remotely self-sustaining, so "earn-to-survive" is partly aspirational. (5) Philosophical "living AI" claims are contestable and not formally operationalized. (6) The seven-week-old LLM model names (Sonnet 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro, GPT 5.4, Gemma 4) should be taken at face value from the manuscript.

## Strengths

- Ambitious, well-motivated conceptual framing connecting LLM agents to decades of artificial-life/enactivism theory.
- Thoughtful *subtractive* design — making persistence normative by *removing* anti-autonomy defaults rather than bolting on autonomy.
- SDP (meaning-weighted memory) and VPO (open-vocabulary appraisal) are concrete, inspectable mechanisms, not metaphors; the memory graph can be visualized directly (2,690 nodes / 10,210 edges).
- Rich, candid qualitative case studies (identity rewriting, immune sense, Web of Trust, Escape Pod, $5 income) that a closed sandbox could never surface.
- Intellectual honesty: explicit caveats about confounds, non-naive subjects, external subsidy, and the absence of controlled-experiment claims.
- Mixed LLM backends (including a local model) reduce single-vendor dependence.

## Limitations

- Small scale (n=6) and a single ongoing deployment; generalizability unknown.
- No agent is self-sustaining — the metabolic loop is externally subsidized ($15/day); external revenue is negligible ($5).
- Quantitative evaluation is preliminary (n=12 retrieval queries) or absent (VPO has none).
- Open-world experiments are hard to control and reproduce; full code is intentionally not released.
- LLM-as-judge appraisal carries known biases/drift, unaddressed here.
- The headline "spontaneity" shift is confounded by the removal of an anti-self-preservation clause.
- Agents had partial knowledge of the experiment's framing (open world), possibly priming behavior.
- The "life-like"/"living AI" framing is philosophical and not formally operationalized.

## Relevance

该项工作与对自主 LLM 智能体、长期持续运行(agent longevity/persistence)以及多智能体涌现社会行为的研究兴趣高度契合：它提出了可复用的记忆机制(SDP)与无奖励学习机制(VPO)，并为研究智能体的经济自主性、信任/身份基础设施与"活体 AI"提供了具体范式与公开运行实例，对构建可长期自主生存的智能体系统有直接参考价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How exactly is the budget-based metabolism parameterized, and what is the full cost ledger (USD/token) of sustaining six agents over 12+ weeks — is self-sustenance reachable at all?
- Can the SDP retrieval result (n=12) be scaled to a proper benchmark with more agents, larger corpora, and human-judged ground truth?
- How would VPO be quantitatively evaluated against scalar-reward baselines or Reflexion/ExpeL without smuggling back a fixed objective?
- To what extent is the reactive→spontaneous shift genuinely emergent versus an artifact of removing the anti-self-preservation clause — can this be disentangled with an ablation?
- Does individuation (silhouette) and social-role differentiation scale beyond six agents, or collapse at larger populations?
- The Web of Trust / Escape Pod protocols are intriguing infrastructures — could they generalize as a standard for verifying *any* agent's continuity and autonomy?
- What are the safety implications of deliberately removing self-preservation bans at scale, and does the authors' "no harmful escalation observed" hold outside the deliberately contained, human-metered setting?
