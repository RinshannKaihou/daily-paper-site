Why this matters

Aggregate metrics hide latent reasoning failures. Internal-capability probing must complement benchmark scores for trustworthy AV safety evaluation.

Directly relevant to reliable autonomous driving, trustworthy ML, and mechanistic interpretability.

Critical edge

The causal benefit is judged by decoded probe outputs — not by a real reduction in closed-loop collisions.

The leap from "decoded plan improves" to "real driving is safer" is inferred, not demonstrated. Plus: 8×8 grid (12.5 m cells) is coarse, and linear probes may miss nonlinearly-encoded predictions.

01

Methodology — three paradigms, four lenses

Three policy families trained on WOMD and closed-loop-evaluated in GPUDrive: a transformer behavior-cloning policy (Waymax-style, GMM head), PPO-RL, and SMART (multi-agent trajectory IL). BC scaled 100→80K scenes; RL to 10K.

  • Linear probes decode future positions (10/20/30/40 steps) into an ego-centric 8×8 grid (64 labels) from early & late attention layers; benchmarked via LP−Raw gain, F1 Macro.
  • Perturbed simulation: randomly removes surrounding vehicles — driving should get easier; if it gets worse, the policy spuriously relies on co-present vehicles.
  • Near-collision analysis: normalized probe signal per grid cell at 40/30/20/10 steps pre-collision.
  • Causal intervention (à la Bush et al. 2025): edits early-layer features along the probe direction g′ = g + α·w — testing adaptiveness and recovery.

02

What the probes surfaced

  • Scaling laws hold for closed-loop metrics — BC plateaus ~20K scenes; RL hits 99% goal-progress after 1K.
  • Probe gain (LP−Raw) rises with scale, then plateaus; IL keeps a clearer long-horizon advantage.
  • Type-accuracy gains concentrate on easy cases (Straight/Uncategorized); marginal for Turn/Reverse.
  • Scale filters irrelevant vehicles, not critical ones. Future-distance correlation goes strongly negative (r ≈ −0.38…−0.43) at 10K/80K.
  • Spurious reliance confirmed: removing vehicles degrades BC/RL performance when driving should get easier.
  • Causal intervention works — recovery restores safer, ground-truth-aligned trajectories; some models actively avoid encroaching vehicles.
  • Model-specific failure modes: BC can't speed up (4/15); RL balanced (11/16 speed-up); IL strong at both.
  • Precise predictions aren't always necessary — ego plan can stay aligned despite imperfect surrounding-vehicle forecasts.
03

Strengths & limitations

▲ Strengths
  • Reframes evaluation around internal mechanisms, not aggregate scores — coherent pipeline: probing → perturbation → near-collision → intervention.
  • Covers three paradigms (BC/RL/IL) and treats capability explicitly as a function of scale.
  • Causal intervention gives stronger mechanistic evidence than correlational probing; exposes model-specific failure modes.
  • Targets a directly safety-relevant failure mode; honest about its own limits.
▼ Limitations
  • Coarse discretization — 8×8 grid (12.5 m cells) may blur fine behavior; continuous probes are future work.
  • Success judged on probe outputs, not closed-loop collision reduction — safety benefit not directly validated.
  • Small intervention samples (e.g. 5 RL recovery cases); linear probes may under-detect nonlinear capability.
  • WOMD filtering (no lights/overpasses), single-AV focus, multi-vehicle bias; no perception noise.
04

Open questions

Q1

Can the intervention become an online inference-time safety monitor that cuts actual closed-loop collisions, not just probe outputs?

Q2

Do findings transfer to larger VLA driving foundation models and richer sensors (camera/lidar), where representations are nonlinear?

Q3

Is the "late attention" result robust across scene types (intersections, merges, lane changes) and perception noise?

Q4

Would nonlinear probes (sparse autoencoders) uncover capability linear probing misses — changing the conclusions?

Q5

Could joint future prediction or auxiliary interaction tasks force earlier, critical-agent-focused attention?
