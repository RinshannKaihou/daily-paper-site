The arbiter & what it exposes

Methodology · core result

Replace the image with a same-size gray blank — keep prompt and decoding identical — and read the signed real−gray gap.

That single causal control, guarded by five degenerate-input checks (gray, black, noise, patch-scramble, and a real-but-mismatched in-distribution image) that must all agree, splits what probing conflates into three regimes:

  • ▸ groundedreal > gray, blindness collapses accuracy to chance. Vision is actually used, correctly.
  • ▸ priorreal ≈ gray. Vision-independent directional default (e.g. "answer up"). High decode = language prior, not sight.
  • ▸ invertedreal < chance, blindness reverts toward chance. Decodable & steerable, yet wrong sign at deploy time.

Probes decode all three axes — horizontal 84.9, depth 77.2, vertical 94.1 (the highest). The vertical axis is near-chance in behaviour and blind-stable under steering. The axis that looks most recovered is the least grounded.

Results, distilled

Across 14 VLMs · 6 families
Inverted depth replicates

Signed-slope diagnostic reproduces the decode≠deploy inversion on 7 / 8 models across 5 families; it emerges with LM scale within a family (InternVL3 38→31→28; Qwen2.5-VL 48→32).

Backbone, not vision tower

The grounded / prior / inverted fingerprint is set by the language backbone, not the vision encoder — traced across matched-scale comparisons.

It's not a benchmark artifact

Same Qwen2.5-VL-7B is grounded-correct on What'sUp near-field front/behind and 3DSRBench closer-to-camera. The inversion is specific to camera-egocentric framing.

Robust to the obvious objection

All five degenerate inputs — including a real, in-distribution but wrong image — give the same verdict. The taxonomy is not a blank-image artifact.

Localized, mid-late

Inverted channel peaks L19–36, never early, one-sided in 6/7 models; localization magnitude tracks how fixable the model is (0.99 down to ≤0.56).

One lone exception

SmolVLM-2.2B sits near chance on every axis — a capability floor, not a contradiction to the pattern.

Strengths  ⇄  Limitations

Honest read

What's genuinely strong

  • Extremely cheap: a single forward pass with pixels zeroed — training-free and self-calibrating.
  • The signed gap & three-way taxonomy resolves a real ambiguity a binary grounded/not split cannot.
  • The mismatch-image control is a decisive in-distribution guard against "you fed it a degenerate blank."
  • Broad coverage (14 VLMs) plus a surprising, well-replicated sign-inversion emerging at scale.
  • Closes the arc diagnosis → mechanism → geometry-diagnostic correction; the correction rung is itself a fingerprint.
  • Intellectually honest: explicitly builds the "seductive result" one would publish, then refutes it.

What to push back on

  • Task scope is narrow — one spatial task, three axes; generalization to colour, counting, OCR, fine relations is unproven.
  • The H/V/D partition is task-type-dependent (shifts under tabletop on/under) — not a universal VLM property.
  • Deep mechanism (locus, projection, correction) is characterized single-anchor on Qwen2.5-VL-7B before generalizing.
  • Scale-emergence is non-monotonic (Gemma 53→43→46) — suggestive, not a clean scaling law.
  • No pixel-level causal minimal pair for camera-relative front/back is attainable (a mirror is identity) — evidence is representation-level.
  • No released code referenced in the paper.

Open questions

For further exploration
  1. Does the arbiter + three-regime taxonomy generalize beyond spatial axes — to colour, object counting, OCR, fine-grained relations?
  2. What is the mechanistic cause of scale-dependent depth inversion — a learned frame-convention conflict in training data?
  3. Could the sign inversion be partly positional/attention geometry or tokenization, given no pixel-level minimal pair exists?
  4. Is "LM backbone sets the fingerprint" robust to matched-scale vision encoders (SigLIP vs InternViT, encoder-decoder vs decoder-only)?
  5. Can correction complexity be predicted a priori from internal geometry (localization magnitude, one-sidedness)?
  6. How much published steering work would fail this control — i.e., is "prior-as-recovery" hiding elsewhere in the literature?
