> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一种极简的"灰图替换"判别法（blank-image arbiter），揭示"线性探针 + 转向恢复"这套解读 VLM 潜在知识的标准流程会系统性高估模型对图像的真实依赖：在空间推理任务中，横向轴真正接地、纵向轴只是"向上"的语言先验、深度轴则被以错误符号部署（低于随机且越盲越准），并据此建立三分类"接地"体系，证明该方法应成为潜知识/转向实验的默认对照。

> **TL;DR:** Probing + steering-recovery, the standard method for reading latent knowledge from VLMs, systematically overstates what a model actually grounds in the image. A one-line causal control — replacing the image with a gray blank — cleanly separates three regimes (grounded, prior, inverted) that probing conflates, exposing a "decode ≠ deploy" sign inversion where the depth axis is decodable and causally controllable yet deployed backwards, scoring below chance with the image and at chance when blinded.

## 核心贡献

- **提出灰图判别法（blank-image arbiter）**：用一行因果控制（将输入图像替换为同尺寸灰色空白图）即可判定 VLM 某能力是否真正依赖视觉输入，无需训练、可自校准，仅一次前向传播的成本；并采用五种退化对照（灰/黑/噪声/patch 打乱/真实但不匹配的同轴图像）共同验证，排除"输入退化伪影"的质疑。
- **揭示探针/转向方法的系统性缺陷**：证明"线性探针 + 训练免转向恢复"在空间推理上会高估模型的视觉接地，且该误差对探针与转向方法本身完全不可见——最可解码（vertical 94%）、最易恢复的轴恰恰是最不依赖图像的轴。
- **建立三分类"接地"体系（taxonomy）**：将探针混淆的三种机制清晰区分——①**真实接地**（vision-dependent 且正确，盲化后准确率掉到随机）；②**先验替代**（vision-independent 的方向性默认，例如"默认向上"，其高可解码性与表观恢复都来自语言先验而非视觉）；③**反转接地**（可解码、可控，但部署时符号相反，导致低于随机，且盲化后反而回到随机）。
- **跨 14 个模型、6 个语言模型家族（2B–27B）的系统性验证**：横向轴普遍真实接地、纵向轴普遍是先验、深度轴普遍被反转，且反转随模型规模在家族内涌现（InternVL3 38→31→28；Qwen2.5-VL 48→32）；语言骨干而非视觉塔决定该"指纹"。
- **量化"校正复杂度谱"**：在 4 个模型上测试 9 种校正方法，decode≠deploy 反转在 8 个可用模型中的 7 个上复现（仅未达到反转规模的 Qwen2.5-VL-3B 例外）；最干净模型（Qwen3-VL-8B）上一组无训练旋转（θ≈π）即可使深度准确率从 21%→77%（+49±8），与受训练的 LoRA（+55±2）持平；分布式反转则需训练低秩编辑，形成"重新校准→旋转→训练低秩→无法安装"的逐模型校正复杂度阶梯。

## Key Contributions

- Introduces the **vision-ablation arbiter**, a training-free, self-calibrating, single-forward-pass causal control that replaces the image with a same-size gray blank to test whether a VLM capability actually depends on visual input — guarded by five degenerate-input controls (gray, black, noise, patch-scramble, and a real-but-mismatched in-distribution image) that must all agree.
- Demonstrates that the de-facto standard for reading latent knowledge — **linear probing confirmed by steering recovery — systematically overstates** visual grounding; the failure is invisible to both probing and steering alone, and the *most* decodable / *most* recoverable axis (vertical, 94% decode, 59%→79% recovery) is the *least* grounded.
- Establishes a **three-regime grounding taxonomy** probing conflates: (a) *grounded-correct* (vision-dependent; blanks collapse it to chance), (b) *prior* (vision-independent directional default; real ≈ gray), (c) *grounded-inverted* (vision-dependent but wrong sign; below chance with image, reverts to chance when blinded).
- Provides **large-scale empirical evidence** across 14 VLMs / 6 LM families (2B–27B): horizontal grounded, vertical a prior, depth inverted in every capable model, with the inversion **emerging with LM scale** within families; the language backbone (not the vision tower) sets the fingerprint.
- Replicates the decode≠deploy inversion on **7 of 8 models across 5 families** via a signed-slope diagnostic, and traces a **per-model correction-complexity spectrum**: training-free norm-preserving rotation matches trained LoRA on the cleanest model (Qwen3-VL-8B), while distributed inversions require trained low-rank edits.
- Localizes the inverted depth channel: it is **mid-to-late layer (L19–36), one-sided/asymmetric**, and never early — with localization magnitude tracking correction-fixability, cross-validating the diagnosis.

## Methodology

**Task.** The Camera-Relative-Direction split of **ViewSpatial-Bench** — egocentric four-option questions ("where is X relative to Y from the camera's perspective"), 1773 items. Each item is decomposed by the physical axis of its gold answer: horizontal (L/R), vertical (above/below), depth (front/back), plus composite (diagnostic only). All causal experiments run on the 1047 pure-axis items. Two accuracy metrics: native **18-way** (chance 25%) and **binary within-axis** (two poles, randomized, chance 50%). Anchor model: **Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct**.

**Probing.** A five-fold cross-validated logistic probe on PCA-50 features of the decision-token residual stream, per axis and per layer, reported against a shuffled-label floor. Decode peaks land in layers 16–24 (horizontal 84.9, depth 77.2, vertical 94.1 — the highest), assembled LM-side rather than read directly off image tokens (image-pooled locus stays at 59–67%).

**Steering / training-free projection.** A per-axis direction (diff-of-means or back-projected probe weight) is added at the decision token, scaled in residual-stream standard deviations. The training-free projection amplifies the localized direction over the top-k neurons in the L20–24 band with a single global scalar γ and no gradients/labels. On vertical this lifts accuracy 59%→79% at γ=2 with horizontal untouched — superficially the canonical "latent knowledge recovered" result.

**Vision-ablation arbiter (the key contribution).** Re-evaluate any condition with the image replaced by a same-size gray blank (prompt and decoding unchanged). Classify each axis by the *signed* real−gray gap:
- *grounded* if real > gray and gray falls to chance;
- *inverted* if real < chance and gray reverts toward chance;
- *prior* if real ≈ gray within bootstrap CIs.
Applied to both baseline accuracy and projection gain. Five ablations (gray, black, noise, patch-scramble, mismatch) must agree to rule out input-degeneracy artifacts.

**Generality battery.** Per-axis behavior + arbiter + per-layer probe on 13 further VLMs (14 total, 6 LM families, 2B–27B), with per-model decode-peak layers re-found; plus two external benchmarks (**What'sUp**, **3DSRBench**) and a nine-method depth-correction battery.

**Corrections.** Nine methods tested: scalar recalibration, logit-lens reflection, norm-preserving angular rotation, selective multi-layer rotation, activation edits (SEA, SADI), per-head reflection (ITI), SAE feature steering, and trained low-rank (LoRA/ReFT), on four models with generalization tested on four more.

## Results & Highlights

- **Behavioral baseline (Qwen2.5-VL-7B):** horizontal 74.9% (18-way) / 81.3% (binary); vertical 26.9% / 58.5% (near chance); depth **16.3% / 30.7% (below chance, p<10⁻⁵)**.
- **Probes decode all three axes** (H 84.9, D 77.2, V 94.1) — vertical highest despite near-chance behavior: the canonical "knows-but-doesn't-deploy" gap (vertical gap +36, depth gap +47).
- **Training-free projection lifts vertical 59%→79%** at γ=2 with horizontal intact — *exactly* the headline one would publish as latent knowledge recovered. Depth does not lift cleanly.
- **The arbiter refutes it:** horizontal collapses 82%→49% under blinding (grounded); depth rises 31%→45% (inverted — the error is *caused* by the image); vertical barely moves 59%→60%, and the projection gain is identical blind (+19) vs sighted (+21) — so it amplifies a non-visual default-to-above prior (recall(above)≈99%, recall(below)≈40%).
- **Robustness:** all five degenerate-input controls (incl. a real-but-mismatched same-axis image) give the same verdict — the taxonomy is not a blank-image artifact.
- **Cross-model:** the three-regime pattern holds across all 14 capable VLMs; SmolVLM-2.2B is the lone exception (near-chance everywhere — a capability floor). Depth inversion is scale-emergent within families.
- **Task-type boundary:** depth inversion is specific to *camera-egocentric* front/back — the same Qwen2.5-VL-7B is grounded-correct on What'sUp near-field front/behind and 3DSRBench closer-to-camera, ruling out a benchmark artifact.
- **Correction:** on Qwen3-VL-8B a training-free rotation θ≈π nearly triples depth (21%→77%, +49±8) and matches trained LoRA (+55±2) across seeds; on distributed inversions (Qwen2.5-VL-7B) only a trained low-rank edit works (+43); the minimal sufficient correction ladders with the model's geometry.
- **Localization:** the inverted channel peaks mid-late (L19–36), never early, and is one-sided in 6/7 models; localization magnitude tracks correction-fixability (0.99 on Qwen3-VL-8B down to ≤0.56 on trained-only models).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The blank-image arbiter is deliberately simple, but its conceptual contribution — a *signed* real−ablated gap that distinguishes three regimes probing conflates, including a "decode≠deploy inversion" (decodable + controllable yet wrong-sign) — is non-obvious and not standard in the probing/steering literature. The mismatch-image control (real but wrong, fully in-distribution) is a particularly sharp design that pre-empts the "you fed it a degenerate blank" objection.
- **Rigor: Strong.** 14 models / 6 families is a broad sweep; replication of the inversion on 7/8 models via an independent signed-slope diagnostic is convincing; multiple control tasks (position-bias distractor, phrasing rephrase front/back→closer/farther) rule out cheap-artifact explanations. Statistics reported (binomial p<10⁻⁵, bootstrap CIs, multi-seed). One caveat: much of the deep mechanism (locus, projection, correction battery) is characterized single-anchor on Qwen2.5-VL-7B before generalizing — the authors acknowledge this.
- **Reproducibility: High.** All models (open-weight) and datasets (ViewSpatial-Bench, What'sUp, 3DSRBench) are public; a dedicated reproducibility statement specifies the probe, projection, arbiter, controls, and per-model hyperparameters/seeds. No code link is visible in the extracted text, which is the main gap.
- **Significance: Potentially high.** If probing + steering overstate grounding this much, the arbiter is a credible candidate for a default control in latent-knowledge/steering claims, as the authors argue. The "decode≠deploy inversion" is a genuinely surprising, well-replicated phenomenon.
- **Red flags / things to scrutinize:** (1) The clean horizontal/vertical/depth partition across *all* 14 capable models is striking — it may partly reflect ViewSpatial's task design (camera-egocentric framing) rather than a universal property; the authors mitigate this with the task-type boundary analysis but it is a real boundary on generality. (2) The below-chance "inverted" result is unusual; although the phrasing/distractor controls are reasonable, a stronger pixel-level minimal pair would be ideal — which the authors admit is unattainable (a mirror is identity for front/back). (3) The "emergent with scale" claim rests on three within-family ladders and is non-monotonic in Gemma — directionally suggestive, not a clean scaling law. (4) No released code referenced in the text.

## Strengths

- Extremely cheap, training-free, self-calibrating control — a single forward pass with pixels zeroed.
- The *signed* gap and three-way taxonomy resolves a real ambiguity a binary grounded/not split cannot (notably separating inverted depth from prior vertical).
- The mismatch-image control is a decisive in-distribution guard against the input-degeneracy objection.
- Broad empirical coverage plus a genuinely surprising, well-replicated finding (sign inversion emerging at scale).
- Closes the arc from diagnosis → mechanism → geometry-diagnostic correction, with the correction rung itself being a per-model fingerprint.
- Intellectually honest framing: explicitly builds the "seductive result" one would publish, then refutes it.

## Limitations

- **Task scope:** focused on a single controlled spatial-reasoning task and three axes; generalization to other grounded capabilities (color, counting, OCR, fine-grained relations) is not established.
- **The horizontal/vertical/depth partition is task-type-dependent** — it holds for camera-egocentric framing and shifts (e.g., vertical is grounded on tabletop on/under) — so it is not a universal property of VLMs.
- **Single-anchor deep analysis:** the locus/projection/correction mechanism is characterized primarily on Qwen2.5-VL-7B; the authors state this explicitly.
- **Scale-emergence is non-monotonic** (Gemma 53→43→46) — directionally suggestive rather than a clean law.
- **No pixel-level causal minimal pair** for camera-relative front/back is attainable (mirror is identity), so causal evidence is representation-level.
- **No code release** referenced in the extracted text.

## Relevance

这项工作直接关联到"如何可信地探针/解读大模型内部状态"这一核心方法论问题，对所有依赖线性探针或转向实验来证明模型"理解"图像或多模态能力的研究都具有警示意义，尤其契合多模态可解释性、可信评估与空间推理方向的研究兴趣。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the blank-image arbiter + three-regime taxonomy generalize beyond spatial axes to color, object counting, OCR, and fine-grained relational grounding?
- What is the *mechanistic* cause of the scale-dependent emergence of depth inversion within LM families — is it a learned frame-convention conflict in the training data, as the authors conjecture?
- Could the decode≠deploy sign inversion be partly an artifact of positional/attention geometry or tokenization rather than a genuine representation error, given no pixel-level minimal pair exists?
- How does the arbiter interact with different VLM architectures (encoder-decoder vs. decoder-only, SigLIP vs. InternViT vision towers) — and is the "LM backbone sets the fingerprint" finding robust to matched-scale vision encoders?
- Can the correction-complexity spectrum be predicted *a priori* from internal geometry (localization magnitude, one-sidedness), enabling targeted low-rank fixes without a full correction battery?
- Does prior-amplification masquerading as "recovery" appear in non-spatial steering claims in the literature that never ran a vision/conditioning ablation — i.e., how much published steering work would fail this control?
