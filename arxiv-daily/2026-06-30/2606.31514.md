> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 MINT，一种基于最高有效位优先（MSDF）数位串行算术的 FPGA CNN 推理加速器：其单处理单元无需重配置即可支持 INT2–INT8 任意精度（仅靠提前终止输出数位），配合预算约束的逐层贪心精度搜索，在 Imagenette-320 上将 VGG-16/ResNet-18 压到约 5.6/6.0 平均比特，精度损失 <2% 时获得比 INT8 基线高约 26–33% 的吞吐与 63–82% 的能效。

> **TL;DR:** MINT is an FPGA CNN inference accelerator built on most-significant-digit-first (MSDF) digit-serial arithmetic, where a single processing element supports INT2–INT8 with no reconfiguration — precision is controlled purely by early termination of output digits. A budget-constrained per-layer greedy search drops VGG-16/ResNet-18 to ~5.6–6.0 average bits with <2% accuracy loss (on Imagenette-320), giving ~26–33% throughput and ~63–82% energy-efficiency gains over an INT8 baseline on a Zynq-7020, claiming the best energy efficiency among compared Zynq-class designs.

## 核心贡献

- 提出**零开销动态精度**架构：基于 MSDF（最高有效位优先）算术，精度仅由"提前终止输出数位"控制，单个处理单元（PE）无需硬件重配置、模式切换或数据通路修改即可在 INT2–INT8 间无缝切换。
- 设计了**串-并 MSDF 卷积内积 PE**：使用基数-2 冗余有符号数位（digit set {-1,0,1}，每位编码为 2 比特对 (p,n)）与在线加法树，9 抽头内积（3×3 卷积）总延迟 C(P)=2P+10 个周期。
- 提出**预算约束贪心精度分配算法**：联合考虑层敏感度、MAC 占比与 MSDF 周期模型，逐层选最低精度并将总精度损失限制在 2% 内（效率评分 η = cycle_saving × mac_frac / (d+ε)）。
- 在 Xilinx Zynq-7020（200 MHz）上评估：VGG-16 平均 5.64 比特、ResNet-18 平均 6.04 比特，吞吐提升 32.6%/26.0%，能效（GOPS/W）提升 82.0%/62.9%，PDP 下降 45.1%/38.6%，精度仅降 1.81%/1.96%。
- 与代表性 FPGA CNN 加速器相比：MINT 动态模式在所列 VGG-16 与 ResNet-18 设计中取得**最高能效**（29.5 / 26.4 GOPS/W），尽管原始吞吐低于吞吐优先型设计，但在小芯片 Zynq-7020 上给出强资源-能效折中。

## Key Contributions

- **Zero-overhead dynamic precision:** An MSDF (most-significant-digit-first) CNN inference architecture where precision is controlled solely by early termination of output digits. Unlike conventional mixed-precision designs, one PE supports INT2–INT8 with no reconfiguration, mode switching, or datapath change.
- **Serial-parallel MSDF processing element** for convolution inner products using a radix-2 redundant signed-digit representation and an online adder tree; the 9-tap PE latency is C(P) = 2P + 10 cycles, naturally exposing a precision–latency tradeoff.
- **Budget-constrained greedy precision-assignment algorithm** that jointly accounts for layer sensitivity, MAC distribution, and the MSDF cycle model. The efficiency score η = (cycle_saving × mac_frac)/(accuracy_drop + ε) maps precision decisions directly to cycle-level savings.
- **Evaluation on VGG-16/ResNet-18:** 5.64 / 6.04 average bits; 18.1% / 15.1% cycle savings; +32.6% / +26.0% throughput; +82.0% / +62.9% energy efficiency; −45.1% / −38.6% PDP — all within a 2% accuracy budget.
- **Best-in-class energy efficiency** (29.5 / 26.4 GOPS/W) among compared Zynq-7020-class VGG-16/ResNet-18 accelerators, with explicit honesty that raw throughput is lower than throughput-oriented designs.

## Methodology

**Arithmetic foundation (MSDF / left-to-right).** MSDF arithmetic processes operands digit-by-digit, most-significant first. To emit the output digit of highest weight first, the algorithm requires input redundancy via signed-digit (SD) numbers. MINT uses **radix-2 SD** from the digit set {−1, 0, 1}, each digit encoded as a 2-bit pair (p, n) with value p − n. After a small fixed **online delay δ** (δ_mult = 2, δ_add = 2), one output digit is produced per clock cycle. Terminating after P digits yields a natural P-digit approximation with bounded error ≤ 2^−P — this is what enables precision to be set purely by how many cycles are run. A full P-precision serial-parallel multiplication takes 2P + δ_mult = 2P + 2 cycles.

**Two MSDF building blocks:**
- *Radix-2 Left-to-Right Serial-Parallel Multiplier (LRM):* one operand serial, the other parallel; at iteration j it updates an internal residual v[j] = 2·w[j] + x_{j+δ}·Y, selects digit z_{j+1} = SEL(v[j]), and updates w[j+1] = v[j] − z_{j+1}. Redundancy bounds the residual and eliminates long carry propagation.
- *Radix-2 Left-to-Right Adder (LRA):* two full-adders + local recoding + latches, producing the redundant output pair (z⁺, z⁻) one digit per cycle after online delay, with carry handled locally to avoid global propagation.

**Processing Engine (PE).** Each PE is a 9-tap inner product (a 3×3 kernel at one spatial position): 9 serial-parallel MSDF multipliers feeding an MSDF adder tree of ⌈log₂(9)⌉ = 4 stages, each adding δ_add = 2 cycles. Per-PE latency:
> C(P) = 2P + δ_mult + δ_add × ⌈log₂(9)⌉ = **2P + 10** cycles.

**PE Array.** Digit-serial units are small-area / short-critical-path, so many are instantiated in parallel via tiling. The authors use a *fixed* input tile T_N = 16 and output tile T_M = 8 ⇒ **128 PEs**, explicitly noting the goal is to demonstrate the MSDF inner-product unit rather than optimize tiling. Effective throughput at 200 MHz is given by Eff. GOPS = 2·(T_N·T_M·LRM_PE)/(C(P)·5 ns).

**Software–hardware co-evaluation flow.** (1) From an FP32 pre-trained network, run uniform-precision ablation INT2→INT8 and per-layer sensitivity probing (quantize one layer at a time, others at INT8). (2) A budget-constrained greedy algorithm (Algorithm 1) assigns dynamic per-layer precision under a user threshold Δ_max = 2%, scoring candidates by η = cycle_saving × mac_frac / (d_{ℓ,p} + ε) and greedily accepting in descending η order with full-network re-evaluation after each tentative change. (3) In parallel, the hardware branch characterizes the MSDF PE via Vivado synthesis, post-synthesis simulation, and SAIF-based switching-activity analysis. (4) The model-level precision map and hardware characterization are combined for throughput / energy / latency.

## Results & Highlights

**Uniform-precision behavior.** Both nets show a sharp **accuracy cliff between INT4 and INT5**: VGG-16 jumps from 0.13% (INT4) to 63.85% (INT5); ResNet-18 from 0.28% to 34.42%. At INT6+ both recover most FP32 accuracy. Notably INT8 *slightly exceeds* FP32 for VGG-16 (82.55% vs 82.39%), attributed to a quantization regularization effect.

**Energy-efficiency comparison (Table 4).** Throughput-oriented designs (OPU, Caffeine, TNNLS'21, TCAD'24) deliver higher raw GOPS, but MINT-dynamic achieves the highest GOPS/W among the listed Zynq-7020-class VGG-16 and ResNet-18 designs: +37.5%/20.6%/105.4%/56.0% over OPU/Caffeine/TNNLS'21/TCAD'24 (VGG-16), and 4.55×/1.36×/1.22×/1.54× over NEURAghe/TCAS'21/ECHO/TCAD'20 (ResNet-18). The paper is explicit that raw throughput remains lower than the top references.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (moderate).** The ingredients are individually well-established: MSDF/online arithmetic (Ercegovac & Lang, 2004), redundant signed-digit representation, and mixed-precision quantization are all classical. The contribution is the *synthesis* — (a) a single PE that spans INT2–INT8 with no reconfiguration via early digit termination, and (b) a greedy search that ties precision directly to the MSDF cycle model rather than to abstract bit-cost. This is incremental over the authors' own DSLR-CNN and ECHO, but the "zero-overhead dynamic precision, precision = number of cycles" framing is clean and genuinely useful.
- **Rigor (mixed — read carefully).**
  - *Accuracy is measured on **Imagenette-320** (10 classes, 3,925 images), not full ImageNet-1k.* The "2% budget" and all Top-1 numbers (82.55%, 78.52%, etc.) are on this easy 10-class subset. On full ImageNet the same 2% budget at 5.6–6.0 bits would almost certainly be much harder to hit. This is the single biggest caveat and should temper any headline reading.
  - *PyTorch accumulation stays FP32*, and only Conv2d/linear layers are quantized — the authors explicitly state the reported accuracy is an **upper bound** on actual hardware accuracy.
  - *The 128-PE array is **modeled/extrapolated** from single-PE synthesis*, not a full post-implementation of the entire array (no full-array place-and-route resource/power numbers). Power uses SAIF annotation at the PE level with assumed array utilization η = 0.88 and activity α = 0.50.
  - BatchNorm folding, neglected ReLU/pooling/skip latency, and excluded FC layers further idealize the model.
- **Reproducibility (low–moderate).** Concrete platform/frequency/networks are given, and the greedy algorithm is specified pseudocode + formula. But there is **no mention of released RTL/bitstreams or search code**, and several numbers rest on modeling assumptions rather than a fully implemented and measured chip-level design. Hardware-acceleration papers rarely release artifacts, so this is a known weakness rather than a unique failure.
- **Significance (niche but real).** For resource-constrained FPGA edge inference on small/old Zynq parts, a 60–80% energy-efficiency gain at <2% accuracy loss is meaningful. The framing — *precision as a runtime cycle count with zero reconfiguration* — is a useful design idea beyond this specific chip. Likely high interest for FPGA/edge-AI practitioners; more incremental for the broader ML-systems community.
- **Red flags / fairness of comparisons:**
  - Comparisons span *very different* FPGAs (VX690T, VX980T, XCVU9P, VU3P vs Zynq-7020). The "best energy efficiency" claim holds among the listed set, but it is partly an artifact of comparing a small LUT-only design against large DSP-heavy ones. The authors are commendably honest that raw throughput is lower, which mitigates overselling.
  - MINT uses **LUT/FF only (no DSP blocks)** — unusual and worth noting when comparing to DSP-based accelerators normalized to GOPS/W; it also means absolute throughput is low (~19.9 GOPS).
  - The redundant signed-digit encoding inflates bit storage (2 bits/digit), partially offsetting the sub-INT8 average-bitwidth advantage — worth checking against the "5.64 average bits" claim.

## Strengths

- Elegant core idea: precision is *literally* the number of MSDF cycles run, needing no reconfiguration/mode-switch/datapath change — a clean hardware-software interface for dynamic precision.
- Principled, hardware-aware greedy search (η score couples layer sensitivity, MAC fraction, and the actual cycle model) rather than arbitrary bit allocation.
- Honest comparison framing: explicitly states raw throughput is below throughput-oriented designs and claims only the energy-efficiency crown on a small, cheap part.
- Strong and clearly reported energy-efficiency / PDP numbers; useful per-PE LUT/FF/power table across INT2–INT8.
- Concrete target (Zynq-7020 @ 200 MHz) and named networks make the contribution verifiable in spirit.

## Limitations

- **Accuracy on Imagenette-320 (10 classes), not full ImageNet** — the headline 2% budget is on a much easier task; transfer to ImageNet-1k is unproven.
- Only two classic CNNs (VGG-16, ResNet-18); no transformers, MobileNet/EfficientNet, or depthwise/1×1 convs that stress the 9-tap inner-product PE differently.
- 128-PE array results are **extrapolated**, not a full post-P&R implementation; power relies on assumed utilization (0.88) and activity (0.50) factors.
- PyTorch FP32 accumulation + only Conv/Linear quantized ⇒ reported accuracy is an upper bound on hardware accuracy.
- No DSP usage → low absolute throughput; the architecture is positioned as energy-efficient, not real-time.
- Throughput is fundamentally limited by the multi-cycle MSDF multiplier and online adder tree (authors acknowledge this).
- No released RTL/bitstreams or search code.
- Comparisons mix FPGA families/technologies; energy-efficiency "win" is partly platform-driven.

## Relevance

本工作面向 FPGA 上低功耗边缘端 CNN 推理加速，主打"精度即周期数"的零开销动态精度机制与硬件感知的逐层量化搜索，与"高效推理硬件"及"混合精度量化"方向高度相关；若关注端侧部署的能效-精度折中或硬件-算法协同量化，可作为思路与实例参考，但需注意其精度是在 10 类 Imagenette 上测得，迁移到完整 ImageNet 尚未验证。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the 2% accuracy budget at ~5.6–6.0 average bits survive on **full ImageNet-1k**, or on harder detection/segmentation heads? The Imagenette-320 result is suggestive, not conclusive.
- How does the 9-tap MSDF PE handle depthwise/1×1 convs and transformer linear/matmul workloads, where the inner-product structure and reuse differ?
- What are **full-array post-P&R** resource/power/timing numbers (vs. the current extrapolation), and how sensitive are the GOPS/W gains to the assumed 0.88 utilization / 0.50 activity factor?
- Would learned/RL-based mixed-precision search (e.g., HAQ-style, hardware-aware) beat the greedy η score on average bit-width under the same budget?
- How does MSDF early termination interact with batching/streaming in real end-to-end latency, and can deeper digit-level pipelining (future work) close the throughput gap to DSP-based designs?
- Given SD digits cost 2 bits each, what is the true effective storage/interconnect cost vs. the "5.64 average bits" figure, and does that change the energy story?
- Can the comparison be made fairer by re-running a strong, modern INT8 quantized accelerator on the *same* Zynq-7020 as the baseline, rather than relying on cross-platform literature numbers?
