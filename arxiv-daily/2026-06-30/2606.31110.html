PhD Thesis / Statistical Mechanics / cs.LG + cond-mat.stat-mech

Statistical mechanics exactly solves dense associative memory & RBMs — unifying adversarial robustness, the lottery ticket, and logarithmic training acceleration.

Explaining Machine Learning and Memorization with Statistical Mechanics
Robin Thériault · Scuola Normale Superiore, Cycle XXXVII · Sup. D. Tantari · Coord. M. Benzi · Collab. F. Tosello
arXiv
2606.31110
statistical-mechanics teacher-student dense-associative-memory adversarial-robustness lottery-ticket
01 Key metric
O(log n)vs O(n)
Training time in the number of hidden units — linear without the saddle-point hierarchy, ~logarithmic with splitting steepest descent.
02 Why this matters

Rigorously frames learning-vs-memorization, adversarial robustness, and the lottery ticket via statistical mechanics — and offers a fresh saddle-point-hierarchy angle for training-algorithm design.

03 Critical edge

The headline robustness formula relies on a single-pattern teacher, and DAM / RBM model classes are far simpler than transformers — so transfer to modern deep nets is unproven.

Methodology

M / 01

Three published journal papers unified by the teacher–student setting, attacked with the replica method under a Replica-Symmetric ansatz, free-entropy saddle-point equations, and the Nishimori line.

Ch. 2 ·p-dense Hopfield nets — single-pattern T–S learning; eR / gR phases; T=0 adversarial-robustness formula. Ch. 3 ·RBMs w/ arbitrary hidden units — α_crit via RS free entropy; factorization & lottery-ticket toy model. Ch. 4 ·DAM within RBMs — MLE ≡ teacher-student; regularized loss; saddle-point hierarchy speedup.
  • Direct vs inverse. Direct = random memorized patterns; inverse = trained on teacher-generated data. The inverse gR transition coincides with the direct Paramagnetic→Spin-Glass transition.
  • Validation. Every analytical prediction checked against Monte-Carlo simulation; RS shown solid even off the Nishimori line in studied regimes.
  • Empirical. Ch. 4 evaluated on MNIST & Fashion-MNIST over 10 random seeds; code released for all chapters.

Results & Highlights

R / 02
  • Exact adversarial robustness. Closed-form T=0 robustness for dense Hopfield nets — explains why the prototype phase resists attacks, and predicts larger nets resist low-rank attacks better. Refines the classic quiet-planting bound.
  • Strong noise tolerance. p-body (p≥3) students learn a p*=2 teacher even when its signal scales as β* = O(N^{2/p−1}) — an extensive noise load — with no interference below that threshold.
  • Conjecture resolved. The long-standing claim that T–S performance is independent of hidden-unit count P (uncorrelated patterns) is analytically confirmed and generalized to arbitrary P, P* ≪ M, N.
  • Lottery ticket, analytically. Overparameterized students settle with exactly P* aligned patterns + (P−P*) frozen spin-glass patterns — a clean analogue, trainable via magnitude pruning.
  • Logarithmic training. Splitting steepest descent → ~log in final hidden-unit count, with comparable accuracy on MNIST/Fashion-MNIST.
  • Stable real-data training. A teacher-student-inspired regularizer (mimicking data-generating noise) markedly stabilizes MLE training of DAMs.

Strengths

what holds up
  • One framework, three ideas. Unifies adversarial robustness, lottery-ticket / low-dim learning, and training acceleration under a single statistical-mechanics lens.
  • Exact, not anecdotal. Closed-form results (robustness formula; α_crit for correlated patterns) instead of qualitative stories.
  • Reproducible. Monte-Carlo validation + released code in every chapter — rare for a theory thesis.
  • Theory + algorithm. The saddle-point hierarchy is both a result and a practical, measured speedup.
  • Legible to ML. Connects cleanly to lottery ticket, magnitude pruning, and prototype/feature regimes.

Limitations

where it frays
  • Model gap. DAM / RBM are far simpler than transformers or diffusion models; the author flags transfer as future work.
  • Single-pattern teacher (Ch. 2). Headline robustness result needs ≥2 patterns to model true misclassification attacks.
  • RS ansatz. Replica-Symmetry is an uncontrolled approximation in some regimes where it is known to break.
  • Speedup asymptotics. "~logarithmic" is observational; the rigorous functional form is unproven.
  • Scope. No experiments on contemporary architectures (transformers, diffusion).

Open questions

for further exploration
Q · 01Can the T=0 robustness formula extend to ≥2-pattern teachers and to the multi-hidden-unit DAM of Ch. 4?
Q · 02Does the splitting-steepest-descent speedup transfer to transformers / diffusion U-Nets, where "growing" a model mid-training is far harder?
Q · 03What is the rigorous asymptotic form of the training-time speedup — truly logarithmic, and under what conditions?
Q · 04Beyond RS: how do the phase diagrams and α_crit change under a Replica-Symmetry-Breaking ansatz where RS fails?
Q · 05Can the lottery-ticket toy model (P* aligned + frozen glass) predict which initializations yield fast-converging subnetworks?
Q · 06Does the noise-mimicking regularizer generalize as a practical stabilizer for MLE training of other energy-based models?
