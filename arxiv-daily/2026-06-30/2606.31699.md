> **一句话总结：** 本文发现稀疏自编码器（SAE）虽能精确定位扩散模型中的目标概念，但用负乘子直接干预潜空间会产生大量分布外（OOD）激活导致严重视觉伪影，因此提出 PER 方法——仅在推理时用 SAE 检测含目标对象的 patch，并用同一特征图中不含该对象的 patch 嵌入替换之，在无需逐概念调参的情况下将伪影率从 49–61% 降到 8–22%，且在 UnlearnCanvas 上达到 95.33% 的全局平均分。

> **TL;DR:** The authors show SAEs reliably detect concepts in diffusion models but multiplier-based latent steering produces out-of-distribution activations and severe artifacts. They propose PER (Patch Embeddings Replacement), which uses SAEs purely as detectors to localize concept-containing patches and replaces them with in-distribution patches from the same feature map — eliminating the costly per-concept γ search, cutting artifact rates from ~49–61% to ~8–22%, and reaching up to 95.33% global average on UnlearnCanvas.

## 核心贡献

- **首次系统刻画 SAE 引导（steering）在扩散模型遗忘中的失效模式**：定量证明用负乘子重构激活会把大量激活值推到模型训练分布之外（27.6%–83.7% 的激活值超出原始分布，L2 范数出现重尾），从而产生系统性视觉伪影。
- **提出 PER（Patch Embeddings Replacement）检测式框架**：仅用 SAE 潜变量做语义检测器生成二值掩码，将含概念的 patch 替换为同一前向传播中不含该概念的 patch 嵌入，保证所有激活始终处于模型原生分布（ID）内。
- **移除代价高昂的逐概念干预强度 γ 网格搜索**：PER 无需微调、无需额外训练 SAE、无需调 γ，即可叠加到 SAeUron、SAEmnesia、G-SAE、SDXL Turbo SAE 四种现有流程上。
- **全面而严格的评估**：提出综合考虑擦除与图像质量的 GA（Global Average = 100−AR）指标；引入基于 Qwen2-VL 的自动 Artifacts Rate 测量，并用 18 名标注者的人类评估（Krippendorff α=0.76）与成对偏好研究（+PER 胜率 95.5%，p<10⁻⁴）加以验证。
- **跨任务验证**：在 UnlearnCanvas 对象擦除、NSFW 移除（I2P 基准）、以及 UnlearnDiffAtk 对抗鲁棒性上均报告了结果。

## Key Contributions

- **Systematically characterizes a concrete failure mode of SAE-based steering in diffusion-model unlearning**: quantitatively shows multiplier-based reconstruction pushes 27.6%–83.7% of activations outside the original distribution and creates a heavy-tailed L2-norm distribution, producing systematic visual artifacts.
- **Introduces PER (Patch Embeddings Replacement)**, a detection-only framework: SAE latents generate a binary mask identifying concept-containing patches, which are replaced by in-distribution patch embeddings sampled from non-detected locations of the same feature map, keeping all activations on the model's native manifold.
- **Eliminates the costly per-concept γ grid search** (existing methods test 6–9 γ values per concept); PER requires no fine-tuning, no extra SAE training, and no intervention-strength tuning, and plugs into four existing pipelines (SAeUron, SAEmnesia, G-SAE, SDXL-Turbo SAE).
- **First adaptation of G-SAE (Guided Sparse Autoencoders) to diffusion-model unlearning.**
- **Rigorous evaluation**: introduces Global Average (GA = 100−AR) to jointly reward unlearning and image quality; uses Qwen2-VL-7B for automated Artifacts Rate and validates it with two human studies (18 annotators, Krippendorff α=0.76; pairwise preference where +PER wins 95.5% of non-tie judgments, p<10⁻⁴).

## Methodology

**Setting.** SAEs are applied to the residual stream of cross-attention layers in the denoising U-Net of SD v1.5 (block up.1.1) and SDXL Turbo (block up.0.1). TopK SAEs are trained (Eq. 2–3) with reconstruction loss plus an auxiliary dead-latent term; supervised variants (SAEmnesia, G-SAE) add concept-binding losses.

**Pipeline (4 stages, Fig. 2):**
1. **SAE training** on DM activations using prompts containing concepts to remove.
2. **Concept–latent identification** via a score function (Eq. 4) ranking latents by mean-activation contrast between concept-present and concept-absent datasets, forming a per-concept latent set F_c.
3. **Concept detection** — a binary mask M_c (Eq. 6): a spatial location is flagged if any concept-associated latent exceeds its dataset-mean activation.
4. **Patch Embeddings Replacement (PER)** (Eq. 11): for flagged locations, replace X_{h,w} with X_{h′,w′} uniformly sampled from non-detected positions of the same feature map. Optionally dilate the mask by padding factor p (p=1 chosen empirically) to cover concept bleed into neighbors. If all locations are flagged, fall back to Gaussian noise.

**Key contrast with prior steering.** Existing methods (Eq. 7, 9–10) modify latents with a negative multiplier γ_c and *decode* back through the SAE decoder — this is what pushes activations off-manifold. PER instead uses only the *detection* capability of the SAE and keeps the decoder out of the loop, so every output activation originates from the DM's own forward pass.

## Results & Highlights

- **Activation-distribution shift (the core diagnosis, Fig. 3):** with the median γ, 27.6% / 28% / 18.8% / **83.7%** of activations fall outside the original distribution for SAeUron / SAEmnesia / G-SAE / SDXL-Turbo respectively. L2-norm distributions grow a heavy tail (up to 10³ vs. baseline ~10²), with 51.3%–100% non-overlap.
- **Artifacts Rate (AR) reduction (Fig. 4):** baseline multiplier steering → PER:
  - SAeUron: 57.0% → 16.3%
  - SAEmnesia: 49.8% → 15.5%
  - G-SAE: 43.1% → 22.5%
  - SDXL-Turbo SAE: 61.0% → 8.0%
  - Base model no-unlearn AR is only 1–2%, confirming artifacts are caused by the intervention.
- **Per-concept gains (Fig. 5):** e.g. "Birds" 98.0%→3.9%, "Bears" 74.5%→5.9%, "Cats" 88.2%→27.5%. ("Dogs"/"Humans" improve little.)
- **UnlearnCanvas object unlearning (Tab. 3), Global Average (GA):**
  - G-SAE + PER reaches **95.33%** avg and 90.88% GA (best overall).
  - SAEmnesia + PER: 93.45% avg, 91.44% GA (+10.26%).
  - SAeUron + PER: 84.35% avg, 84.19% GA (+11.72%).
  - Consistent gains on Cross-domain Retain Accuracy (CRA), e.g. SAeUron 74.14%→86.55%, SAEmnesia 88.48%→97.97%.
- **Human evaluation (Tab. 1–2):** 18 annotators reproduce the ordering (Krippendorff α=0.76); pairwise study over 1,183 judgments shows +PER preferred in 95.5% of non-tie cases (95% CI 93.9–96.6%, p<10⁻⁴).
- **Computational efficiency:** removes γ grid search (7/7/6/9 values per concept for the four baselines) — no fine-tuning, no extra SAE training.
- **Adversarial robustness (UnlearnDiffAtk, Tab. 4):** with SAEmnesia, PER achieves the lowest attack effectiveness (34.90 vs. 40.10 baseline) with comparable post-attack UA; with SAeUron, effectiveness is slightly higher (56.30 vs. 49.50).
- **NSFW removal (I2P, SD v1.4, Appendix A.3):** promising results reported.
- **Padding analysis (p):** p=1 is the sweet spot; p=0 drops UA sharply (49.12% vs. 85.37% for SAeUron) — an overly sparse mask fails to suppress the concept.
- **Qualitative (Fig. 7–8):** SAE detection masks tighten around the target object as denoising progresses; PER outputs are artifact-free and preserve style.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty (High):** Conceptually sharp and somewhat contrarian. Rather than proposing yet another steering method, it cleanly decomposes detection vs. intervention, diagnoses the OOD mechanism with concrete activation/norm statistics, and offers a minimalist fix. The negative finding (SAE steering is off-manifold) is valuable even if the fix is simple.
- **Rigor (Strong):** Quantifies the OOD claim (per-dimension activation distributions + L2-norm distributions across four models and three γ levels), pairs automated AR (Qwen2-VL) with two human studies including inter-annotator agreement and significance testing, and proposes the GA metric to penalize artifact-laden "successful" unlearning. Ablations on padding p are thorough.
- **Reproducibility (Good):** Uses pre-trained SAEs from prior work where available; specifies blocks (up.1.1 / up.0.1), the benchmark (UnlearnCanvas, 20 objects / 50 styles), the adversarial protocol (5-token prefix, 40 iters, lr 0.01), and the VLM evaluator. Code/project page released. Missing pieces: exact TopK k, SAE expansion factor, and α — but these are inheritable from the cited base methods.
- **Significance (High for the niche):** Sets a clear boundary on what SAEs can be expected to do in generative models — important for the interpretability and unlearning communities. The "look but don't touch" principle is likely to generalize beyond unlearning.
- **Red flags / honest concerns:**
  - **PER is essentially a constrained region-swap guided by an SAE detector.** Its novelty over standard region-based inpainting/masking rests entirely on (a) the SAE as the *automatic* detector and (b) the claim that swapped patches stay ID. The paper makes this case reasonably, but a direct comparison against a strong non-SAE masking baseline would strengthen it.
  - **Limited/specific gains on "Dogs" and "Humans"** are acknowledged but not deeply explained.
  - **Adversarial results are mixed** (PER is *less* robust under SAeUron, more robust under SAEmnesia) — the paper presents both honestly rather than cherry-picking.
  - **Where do "clean" replacement patches come from?** Only from non-detected locations of the *same* feature map — so PER implicitly assumes the concept does not occupy the entire feature map (otherwise Gaussian-noise fallback). This is a real but rarely-triggered limitation.
  - The **GA metric is self-defined** (100−AR folded into the average); while reasonable, it is the authors' own yardstick and slightly inflates their headline lead.

## Strengths

- Diagnoses the root cause (OOD activations) with hard evidence, not just visual complaints.
- Simple, training-free, tuning-free method that is a drop-in for four existing pipelines.
- Excellent human-study validation (inter-annotator agreement + preference test + significance).
- Honest reporting of mixed adversarial results and of concepts where gains are small.
- Clear, intuitive "detection vs. intervention" framing with broad takeaways for interpretability research.

## Limitations

- Requires that non-detected patches exist in the same feature map; degenerates to noise when the concept is ubiquitous (rare).
- Padding factor p is an extra hyperparameter (empirically fixed at 1); gains vary with it.
- Gains are uneven across concepts ("Dogs", "Humans" barely improve).
- Evaluated primarily on SD v1.5 (SDXL Turbo results are weaker because it wasn't fine-tuned on UnlearnCanvas, so CRA isn't comparable).
- No direct comparison to non-SAE region-masking inpainting baselines, leaving the SAE-specific advantage somewhat under-proven.
- The headline GA improvement is partly an artifact of folding AR into the average.
- Detection quality inherits all the usual fragility of SAE dictionary learning (dead latents, polysemanticity of unsupervised SAEs).

## Relevance

本文对关注扩散模型可解释性、稀疏自编码器与机器遗忘的研究者高度相关：它划清了 SAE 作为"检测器"与"操控旋钮"的能力边界，提出了一种无需调参、即插即用的推理时擦除方法，对构建可控、可审计、安全的生成模型有直接的方法论与安全意义。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does PER compare quantitatively against a strong non-SAE baseline such as region-masking inpainting or attention-map-guided erasure (e.g., Attend-and-Excite / cross-attention masking) — is the SAE detector's advantage over Grad-CAM-style saliency clear?
- Does PER generalize to abstract concepts/styles, actions, or NSFW sub-categories, or is it fundamentally object-localization-bound?
- What happens when the target concept saturates the feature map — can a learned "concept-free" patch bank replace the Gaussian-noise fallback?
- Can the detection-vs-intervention gap be closed with better SAEs (e.g., JumpReLU, BatchTopK, or cross-layer transcoders) rather than bypassed?
- Is the OOD-steering failure mode specific to diffusion U-Nets, or does it also apply to DiT-based architectures (SD3/FLUX) and to autoregressive image generators?
- Could PER be combined with weight-level unlearning (UCE, SPM) for defense-in-depth, given its inference-time-only nature?
- How sensitive is the OOD diagnosis to the choice of hooked layer and SAE expansion factor?
