> **一句话总结：** 本文提出 LOTUS——用循环填充（looped padded）Transformer 在 K 个并行 latent block 上迭代 R 次，并对每个 latent 位置施加与 gold CoT step token 对齐的并行交叉熵监督，首次在 LLaMA-3.2-3B 规模上让隐式 CoT 追平显式 CoT（GSM8K 仅差 1.5 点且 OOD 平均反超），同时将思考阶段延迟降低 2.5x–6.9x。

> **TL;DR:** LOTUS turns a standard LM into a latent reasoner by processing K padded latent blocks in parallel over R looped iterations and supervising every latent position against its gold CoT-step token via the base LM head; it is the first latent-CoT method to match (and on OOD benchmarks, beat) explicit CoT at the 3B scale while cutting thought-phase latency by 2.5x (math) to 6.9x (natural-language CoT).

## 核心贡献

- **首次在 3B 规模弥合隐式与显式 CoT 差距**：在 LLaMA-3.2-3B-Instruct 上，LOTUS 将 GSM8K 域内准确率（70.0%）带到距显式 CoT（71.5%）仅 1.5 点，并在 OOD 平均（GSM-Hard/MultiArith/SVAMP）上反超（63.9 vs 62.1）；加入 CODI 蒸馏后域内差距缩至 1 点以内。此前所有隐式方法在 >1B 时都落后，且差距随规模扩大。
- **简洁有效的双成分配方**：(i) padded latent prefix（K 个 block、每 block c 个可学习 latent token）经 looped Transformer 迭代 R 次；(ii) 并行交叉熵监督——每个 latent 位置直接通过基础 LM 头对齐对应 gold CoT-step token（`Lstep`），配合答案 next-token 损失（`Lans`）。
- **理论刻画两个损失的互补角色**：`Lstep` 优化"并行链似然（PCL）"起到覆盖作用（让每位置覆盖 gold token 的支撑），`Lans` 提供"联合选择"压力（让共同计算的隐状态能支撑正确答案）；缺一不可（消融证实）。
- **显著延迟优化**：数学表达式 CoT 下思考阶段 2.5x 加速（133ms vs 338.8ms）；自然语言 CoT 压力测试下 6.9x 加速（140.8ms vs 963.6ms），且准确率持平。
- **可解释且 CoT 对齐的隐空间**：将 post-loop latent 通过基础 LM 头读取，gold CoT token 的 top-1 恢复率达 70.9%；并且模型从未训练过的"未见过但有效"的中间步骤也会以非平凡概率浮现（U 集合 15.3% top-1、64.0% top-5），证明非死记硬背。
- **消融验证每个组件必要性**：looped 主干、并行 gold 监督、足够的 block 宽度 c 与 loop 深度 R 均不可少；仅 looped 主干（无监督，63.3%）就已超过 CODI+SIM-CoT（62.3%）。

## Key Contributions

- **First latent-CoT method to close the gap at 3B scale**: On LLaMA-3.2-3B-Instruct, LOTUS reaches 70.0% GSM8K (within 1.5 pts of explicit CoT's 71.5%) and **surpasses** explicit CoT on the out-of-domain average (63.9 vs 62.1). Prior latent methods matched CoT at small scale but fell behind beyond 1B, with the gap widening with size (CODI+SIM-CoT trails by 9.2 pts at 3B; KaVa by 5.8 pts).
- **Minimal, well-motivated recipe**: Two ingredients — (i) a padded latent prefix of K blocks × c tokens processed by a looped (weight-shared) Transformer for R iterations, and (ii) parallel cross-entropy supervision aligning each latent position to its gold CoT-step token through the base LM head.
- **Theory of complementary losses**: Frames `Lstep` as optimizing a parallel chain likelihood (PCL) providing **support coverage** of gold tokens, while `Lans` supplies **global joint-selection** pressure. Both are shown necessary by ablation.
- **Large latency gains**: 2.5x faster thought phase on compact math-expression CoT; 6.9x faster on verbose natural-language CoT, with matched accuracy — because reasoning happens in R parallel latent iterations rather than N sequential token decodes.
- **Transparent, CoT-aligned latent space**: Post-loop latents read through the base LM head recover the gold CoT (70.9% top-1) and place graded probability on **unseen-but-valid** alternative reasoning chains (15.3% top-1, 64.0% top-5 for unseen intermediates), evidence against memorization.
- **Ablation-validated design**: The looped backbone alone (no supervision, 63.3%) already beats CODI+SIM-CoT; both the parallel gold-CoT supervision and the looped architecture (with enough block width c and loop depth R) are essential.

## Methodology

LOTUS (Looped Transformers with parallel supervision on latents) addresses two failure modes of prior latent reasoning: **(P1) sequential generation** (Coconut/CODI/SIM-CoT decode latents autoregressively, keeping the per-token sequential bottleneck) and **(P2) lack of CoT grounding** (PCCoT/KaVa refine latents in parallel but ground them via indirect signals like hidden-state distillation). It resolves both with two ingredients:

**1. Padded latent prefix.** For question Q, CoT steps T₁…T_S, and answer A, the input is `[Q, ⟨BoT⟩, ⟨lat⟩…, …, ⟨lat⟩…, ⟨EoT⟩, A]` — K learnable padded latent blocks of c tokens each, delimited by learnable ⟨BoT⟩/⟨EoT⟩ tokens. K is fixed across examples (chosen to cover the data's step counts, e.g. K=6 covers 99% of GSM8K), and block i is intended to encode step T_i.

**2. Looped computation.** The base causal LM f_θ runs one forward pass over `[Q, ⟨BoT⟩]` to populate a reusable KV cache C_pre, then iterates f_θ for R iterations, each refining the latent embeddings while attending to C_pre — a finite-unroll, input-injected recurrence with gradients through all R iterations.

**3. Direct step-aligned supervision (LOTUS).** Two losses:
- `Lstep`: a single batched cross-entropy aligning each latent position (i,j) to its corresponding gold CoT-step token T_{i,j}, scored through the **base LM head** f_head. Direct, parallel, post-loop.
- `Lans`: standard next-token cross-entropy on the answer suffix, computed in a separate final forward that reuses C_pre and inserts the post-loop latents.
- Total: `L = Lans + λ_step · Lstep`.

**Key insight (parallel chain likelihood):** Because the answer is decoded directly from jointly-computed latents, `Lstep` only needs to ensure each position *covers* the right gold token (support-coverage role) — it never needs to reproduce the joint distribution. `Lans` provides the joint selection pressure. This resolves the worry that independent per-position targets can't yield globally coherent reasoning.

**LOTUS-aux variant:** Routes `Lstep` through an auxiliary decoder g_ϕ (a copy of the base LM) under teacher forcing instead of the base LM head; g_ϕ is training-only and discarded at inference. Readout schedule differs: LOTUS works best with post-loop readout, LOTUS-aux with per-iteration readout (shorter gradient path).

**Inference:** Compute Q's KV cache once, iterate the loop R times, decode the answer autoregressively from the post-loop latents. No latent tokens are ever decoded during the thought phase — the only sequential decoding is the short answer suffix.

## Results & Highlights

**Setup:** Trained on GSM8k-Aug (385k samples). Backbones: GPT-2 (124M), LLaMA-3.2-1B-Instruct, LLaMA-3.2-3B-Instruct. Config: K=6 blocks, c=25 tokens (c=13 on GPT-2), R=6 iterations. Latency measured on a single NVIDIA H100 NVL, batch size 1, greedy decoding.

**Scaling (Table 1):** Prior latent methods match CoT at GPT-2 but diverge with scale (CODI+SIM-CoT: 42.6 vs 42.7 at GPT-2 → 62.3 vs 71.5 at 3B, a 9.2-pt gap). LOTUS stays within ~1.5 pts of explicit CoT at every scale, and at 3B **surpasses** CoT on the OOD average (63.9 vs 62.1).

**Latency (Table 2):** Thought phase 133ms vs 338.8ms for explicit CoT (2.5x); total 181.2ms vs 384.2ms (2.1x). CODI is faster in thought (88.2ms) but far less accurate. Importantly, with R fixed, increasing the latent prefix from 6 to 300 positions changes thought latency by only ~30ms (parallel), unlike autoregressive CoT whose latency grows linearly.

**Natural-language CoT stress test (Table 3):** At 3B, LOTUS matches explicit CoT accuracy (68.13 vs 68.41) while cutting thought latency 6.9x (140.8ms vs 963.6ms). Far exceeds latent baselines PCCoT (47.6%), CODI (55.9%), KaVa (60.0%).

**Ablations (3B, GSM8K):**
- *Latent supervision design (Table 4):* No supervision 63.3% → CODI-only 64.4% → LOTUS post-loop 70.0% → LOTUS-aux per-iter 69.9%. The looped backbone alone (63.3%) already beats CODI+SIM-CoT (62.3%).
- *Block width c (Table 7):* c=1 (49.7%) → c=5 (67.5%) → plateaus at c=25/30 (70.0%). A single token per step is too narrow.
- *Loop depth R (Table 5):* R=2 (14.6%) → R=6 (70.0%); strong monotonic gains, saturating at R=6.
- *Inference-time R (Table 6):* Peaks at the trained R=6 (70.0%); dips at R=7 (69.3%). Inference-time c sweep (Table 8) tolerates over-budgeting but degrades below the trained value.

**Latent analysis:**
- *Gold-chain recovery (Table 9):* LOTUS post-loop latents through f_head: 3.07 NLL, 70.9% top-1 gold-token recovery — latents genuinely carry the CoT.
- *Unseen valid chains (Table 10):* NLL ordering G < U < Random across all readers; for LOTUS via base LM head, unseen-but-valid intermediates surface at 15.3% top-1 / 64.0% top-5 despite never being trained on. A path-level example shows two disjoint valid chains to the same answer (108), with unseen-path numbers (24, 18) surfacing.
- *Loss disentanglement (Table 11):* LOTUS (both losses) > L_ans-only > L_step-only on gold-chain NLL — confirming the complementary roles.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: High.** The specific combination — looped (weight-shared) padded backbone + parallel per-position gold-token supervision through the base LM head — is a clean, previously unexplored recipe. The theoretical framing via parallel chain likelihood (PCL) and the support-coverage/joint-selection division of labor is a genuine and useful conceptual contribution, not just an empirical trick.
- **Rigor: Strong.** Multi-seed runs, systematic ablations isolating every component (supervision design, c, R, readout schedule), matched-compute comparisons, and three independent analyses (gold-chain recovery, unseen-chain mass, loss disentanglement) that corroborate each other. The interpretability claims are tested rather than asserted.
- **Reproducibility: Good.** Code released, config fully specified, standard benchmarks, mainstream backbones. Hyperparameters (K=6, c=25, R=6) are documented with justification (K covers 99% of GSM8K step counts).
- **Significance: Potentially high.** Latent CoT that finally scales (doesn't break beyond 1B) while remaining interpretable would be a meaningful milestone for efficient reasoning, if the trend holds at larger scales.
- **Red flags / caveats:**
  - **Scale ceiling at 3B.** The central selling point is tested only up to 3B. The authors' own framing — that prior latent methods' gap *widens* with scale — means the most important open question is whether LOTUS's gap-closing also holds (or re-opens) at 7B/13B+. The paper does not test this.
  - **Domain narrowness.** Only math word problems (GSM8K family). No reasoning tasks of other types (logic, code, multi-hop QA). The authors acknowledge this.
  - **Fixed budget hyperparameters.** K, c, R must be set a priori; chains longer than K steps fall back to autoregressive completion, which could undermine the latency story on harder/longer problems. Adaptivity is left to future work.
  - **OOD "average" is dominated by MultiArith** (near-ceiling 99.9%), which inflates the OOD-surpass-CoT headline; on GSM-Hard (16.0 vs 17.0) and SVAMP (75.7 vs 71.0) the picture is mixed.
  - **Latency numbers** are single-batch-size-1 greedy on one hardware config; real serving throughput with batching is not reported.

## Strengths

- Directly targets the known, well-documented scaling failure of latent CoT (gap widens beyond 1B).
- Surprisingly simple recipe rather than a complex new architecture — looped backbone + parallel gold-token cross-entropy.
- Compelling efficiency story (2.5x–6.9x latency) that does **not** trade away accuracy at the key scale.
- Genuinely interpretable latents: gold steps recoverable and unseen valid alternatives surface — argued with metrics, not just cherry-picked examples.
- Clean ablation story showing every component earns its place.

## Limitations

- **Tested only to 3B.** The "gap widens with scale" critique of prior work applies symmetrically to LOTUS's claim until verified at larger scale.
- **Math-only evaluation.** Generalization to code, logical, and multi-step QA reasoning is unproven.
- **Fixed K/c/R.** No adaptivity; long chains degrade to autoregressive completion, potentially eroding latency gains on harder problems.
- **OOD wins are mixed** — driven largely by MultiArith; the headline "surpasses CoT on OOD average" deserves scrutiny.
- **No throughput-with-batching data**; latency is single-example greedy, which may not reflect real serving.
- **Lottery of the natural-language CoT setup** relies on a specific dataset version (Wu et al., 2025); sensitivity to the NL trace format is not explored.

该论文处于 LLM 推理与高效推理的交叉点，对关注隐式 CoT、循环/参数共享 Transformer 架构及推理延迟优化的研究者高度相关——尤其适合希望在不牺牲质量前提下压缩思考阶段延迟、或研究"思考"如何在连续隐空间中实现的研究方向。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does LOTUS's gap-closing property hold at 7B/13B/70B, or does the looped-backbone + parallel-supervision recipe also saturate/decay with scale?
- How does LOTUS behave on non-math reasoning (code generation, logical/multi-hop QA, commonsense) — does the gold-step supervision transfer when intermediate steps are less token-alignable?
- What is the training cost/stability of the looped backbone (gradients through all R iterations), and how does it trade off against the inference latency savings in total compute?
- Can K, c, and R be made adaptive (e.g., learned per-example or via early-stopping the loop) to handle variable-length reasoning without falling back to autoregressive completion?
- Under batched/throughput-oriented serving (not just batch-size-1 latency), do the 2.5x–6.9x gains survive, given the 150-position parallel latent footprint?
- Does the parallel-supervision recipe transfer to non-looped architectures as a drop-in improvement for latent reasoning?
