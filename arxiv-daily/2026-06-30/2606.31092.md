> **一句话总结：** FORA 把"能力保护"从权重空间（SVD 奇异方向）转移到函数空间（激活子空间），用左右投影器构造与能力相关方向正交的高容量更新分支加一个窄谱校准通道，在 Qwen3-1.7B 上三类设置中显著优于权重空间投影与正则化方法，将灾难性遗忘减少约 3 倍。

> **TL;DR:** FORA reframes capability-preserving fine-tuning by protecting the *activation subspace* a capability induces (function space) rather than the weight matrix's singular directions (weight space). Using a left projector $P_U$ from the weight SVD and a right projector $P_Q$ built from label-free capability activations, the update $\Delta W = P_U M P_Q + U_2 D_\delta V_2^\top$ blocks reads from capability-critical directions while a narrow spectral channel supplies controlled plasticity — reducing forgetting ~3× on Qwen3-1.7B, with a controlled ablation proving the gain comes from the *source* of the protected subspace, not projection itself.

## 核心贡献

- **提出"函数空间保护"新视角**：首次主张能力应由其在每层激活空间中诱导的子空间（而非权重矩阵的奇异几何）来刻画，把保护目标从权重空间转移到函数空间。论文指出权重 SVD 的主右奇异向量 $V_1$ 只标识"权重在哪些方向大"，并不一定对应"能力在哪些方向活跃"。
- **设计 FORA 方法**：逐层从无标签校准输入收集激活 $X_\text{pres}$，估计输入-激活协方差 $C_\text{pres} = \tfrac{1}{N}X_\text{pres}^\top X_\text{pres}$，取其 top-$k_f$ 特征向量 $Q$ 构造右投影器 $P_Q = I - QQ^\top$，结合权重 SVD 的左投影器 $P_U = I - U_1 U_1^\top$，得到更新 $\Delta W = P_U M P_Q + U_2 D_\delta V_2^\top$。
- **两通道互补分工**：高容量分支 $P_U M P_Q$ 在能力相关方向上"失明"且不写入 $W_0$ 主输出方向；窄谱通道 $U_2 D_\delta V_2^\top$（仅 $d_\text{res}$ 个自由标量）是唯一能在保护方向上响应的部分，提供有界可塑性而非硬冻结。论文给出三条算子级性质（Propositions 1–3）。
- **可归因的对照消融**：仅交换右投影器 $P_V \to P_Q$（保持 $P_U$、$M$ 不变），证明增益来自"投影到能力导出方向"而非"投影"本身——这是论文的核心机制论断。
- **自然扩展到 PEFT**：通过 $M \to \tfrac{\alpha}{r}BA$ 的替换得到 Fora-LR，同一 $P_U(\cdot)P_Q$ 结构在全秩与低秩下行为一致。
- **跨任务实证**：在 Qwen3-1.7B 上覆盖 COGS、GSM8K、英中翻译三类设置（含双向：学翻译保数学、学数学保翻译），FORA 在保留已有能力方面稳定优于 WProj、EWC、L2-SP、LwF。

## Key Contributions

- **Reformulates capability preservation as function-space protection.** Argues a capability is better characterized by the activation subspace it induces per layer than by the dominant singular geometry of $W_0$: $V_1$ marks where the weight is large (an aggregate over all capabilities), not where a specific capability is functionally active.
- **Instantiates the idea as Fora.** Estimates top-$k$ eigenvectors $Q$ of the input-activation covariance from label-free calibration inputs, forms right projector $P_Q = I - QQ^\top$; paired with left SVD projector $P_U = I - U_1U_1^\top$ yields $\Delta W = P_U M P_Q + U_2 D_\delta V_2^\top$ — a high-capacity branch barred from reading capability directions and writing principal output directions, plus a narrow spectral calibration channel.
- **Derives the method as a staged progression** (Table 1): Full FT → spectral calibration → residual expansion → weight-space projection → Fora, where each stage addresses the limitation of the previous one. Three propositions formalize output-side protection, no-read on capability directions, and controlled response via the spectral channel.
- **Clean ablation isolating the projection *source*.** Swapping only $P_V \to P_Q$ while holding $P_U$ and $M$ fixed shows the gain is attributable to *what* is projected, not to projecting at all.
- **Extends to PEFT** via $M \to \tfrac{\alpha}{r}BA$ (Fora-LR), treating it as evidence of generality rather than a primary contribution.
- **Validates across three settings on Qwen3-1.7B** (COGS, GSM8K, bidirectional translation), consistently improving preservation over WProj/EWC/L2-SP/LwF.

## Methodology

**Setup.** Given a pre-trained weight $W_0 \in \mathbb{R}^{d_\text{out}\times d_\text{in}}$, a preserved capability $D_\text{pres}$ (inputs only, no labels), and a new task $D_\text{new}$, find an update $\Delta W$ such that $W_0 + \Delta W$ learns $D_\text{new}$ while retaining performance on $D_\text{pres}$. SVD coordinates split $W_0 = U\Sigma V^\top$ into principal ($U_1, V_1$, top-$r$) and residual ($U_2, V_2$) blocks.

**Staged derivation (Table 1).** Each stage exposes a limitation:
1. Full FT ($\Delta W = M$): no protection.
2. Spectral calibration ($U_2 D_\delta V_2^\top$): diagonal only, no mixing.
3. Residual expansion ($U_2(D_\delta + S_\text{res})V_2^\top$): structure hidden in SVD coords.
4. Weight-space projection ($U_2 D_\delta V_2^\top + P_U M P_V$): $P_V$ protects weight geometry, not the capability.
5. **Fora** ($U_2 D_\delta V_2^\top + P_U M P_Q$): protects the capability's function subspace.

A standard identity $U_2(U_2^\top M V_2)V_2^\top = P_U M P_V$ makes the right projector explicit, exposing the question of *which* subspace to protect.

**Function-space projector.** Forward label-free calibration inputs from $D_\text{pres}$; collect per-layer input activations $X_\text{pres} \in \mathbb{R}^{N\times d}$; form covariance $C_\text{pres} = \tfrac{1}{N}X_\text{pres}^\top X_\text{pres}$; take top-$k_f$ eigenvectors $Q$; build $P_Q = I - QQ^\top$. Construction is label-free, per-layer, and capability-specific.

**Structural properties (Propositions).**
- Prop. 1 (output protection): $U_1^\top P_U M P_Q = 0$ — high-capacity branch writes nothing to $W_0$'s principal output directions.
- Prop. 2 (no read on capability): for $x \in \text{span}(Q)$, $P_U M P_Q x = 0$ — the dense branch is blind to capability-aligned inputs.
- Prop. 3 (controlled response): for $x \in \text{span}(Q)$, $\Delta W_\text{Fora} x = U_2 D_\delta V_2^\top x$ — only the spectral channel responds on protected directions, giving bounded plasticity instead of a hard freeze.

**Two channels over complementary subspaces.** For $x \in \text{span}(Q)$: only the spectral channel responds. For $x \perp \text{span}(Q)$: dense branch has full capacity, spectral channel adds residual calibration. Neither writes to $\text{span}(U_1)$. Protection is a property of the parameterization, not the optimization trajectory.

**Low-rank extension.** Substitute $M \to \tfrac{\alpha}{r}BA$ ($B \in \mathbb{R}^{d_\text{out}\times r_\text{lora}}$, $A \in \mathbb{R}^{r_\text{lora}\times d_\text{in}}$): $\Delta W_\text{Fora-LR} = P_U(\tfrac{\alpha}{r}BA)P_Q + U_2 D_\delta V_2^\top$.

**Training protocol.** Qwen3-1.7B (decoder-only, bf16); Fora applied to all attention (Q,K,V,O) and FFN linear layers per block; $k_f = 16$, $r = 100$; AdamW (lr 1e-5, batch 8, grad-accum 8), early stopping (patience 5), best-validation checkpoint. All full-rank methods train ~1.7B params; results mean±std over 5 seeds. Starting $W_0$ is a capability-specialized checkpoint (LoRA on 5,000 examples, then merged), defining a clean capability to protect.

## Results & Highlights

**COGS fine-tuning, preserving translation (Table 2).**
- Fora: COGS EM 98.68%, translation PPL **4.39** (baseline 4.35, within 0.04), chrF 0.414 (+0.00 Δ).
- WProj: PPL **4.86** — *worse than even unprotected Full FT (4.74)* at comparable EM.
- EWC/L2-SP: PPL 4.75–4.77, statistically indistinguishable from no protection.
- LwF: PPL 4.39 but COGS EM collapses to 92.12% (stability–plasticity trade-off).
- **Only function-space protection achieves both strong preservation and strong new-task learning.**

**GSM8K fine-tuning, preserving translation (Table 3).** GSM8K EM is tight across all methods (46.07–46.87); discriminating axis is preservation. FProj/Fora hold chrF at 0.411 and PPL at **4.35** (indistinguishable from baseline), while Full FT/WProj/EWC/L2-SP drift to chrF 0.405–0.406, PPL 4.40–4.42. Fora also attains the highest GSM8K EM (46.87).

**Translation fine-tuning, preserving math (Table 4).** Reverse direction, $Q = Q_\text{math}$ from GSM8K inputs. FProj/Fora retain GSM8K at 48.84% (Δ = **−0.86**) vs. Full FT (−2.27) and weight/reg baselines (−2.4 to −2.7) — **~3× reduction in forgetting**. LwF is intermediate (−1.42) but degrades translation (PPL 5.03 vs 4.79–4.80). Sole trade-off: chrF 0.390 vs 0.396 for the new task. Constructing $Q$ from math and preserving math rules out the alternative that $Q$ merely encodes translation.

**Projection-source ablation (Table 5).** Swapping only $P_V \to P_Q$ (holding $P_U$, $M$): $P_Q$ wins on the preserved metric in every setting — PPL improves by 0.47 (COGS) and 0.07 (GSM8K); GSM8K retention improves by 1.79 points (Math→Trans). **Conclusion: projection alone is insufficient; the source (weight geometry vs. capability function) is decisive.**

**Spectral channel ablation (Table 6).** Adding $\delta$ never degrades preservation and yields small consistent new-task gains where headroom exists (+0.21 GSM8K EM in the headroom-limited setting; +0.04 COGS). Matches Prop. 3: a bounded calibration channel the optimizer can exploit or leave inactive.

**Robustness to $(r, k_f)$.** On Math→Trans, GSM8K retention stays within a 0.4-point band as $r \in [20,400]$ and $k_f \in [4,64]$; all configs within one std of the default $(100, 16)$ — not a tuned operating point.

**Low-rank extension (Table 7).** At ~17M matched params on COGS: Fora-LR/FProj-LR match CorDA on translation PPL (4.38, closest to baseline 4.35) and exceed OPLoRA's COGS EM (94.60 vs 94.44). Replay-based LoRA gets highest COGS EM (95.84 at 10% replay) but at higher PPL (4.50–4.55) and requiring preserved-capability labels + mixed training.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty: Strong.** The conceptual move — characterizing a capability by its *activation subspace* rather than weight singular directions — is a genuine, well-motivated reframing. The claim that $V_1$ marks "where the weight is large" (an aggregate over all capabilities) rather than "where a capability is active" is incisive and well-argued. The paired $P_U/P_Q$ construction is technically non-trivial.
- **Rigor: Strong.** The controlled ablation isolating *projection source* from *projection action* (Table 5) is a credible causal-attribution design, not just benchmark-chasing. The staged derivation (Table 1) makes each design choice follow from a stated limitation rather than being assumed. Three formal propositions tie the construction to verifiable guarantees.
- **Reproducibility: Strong.** Public code, label-free per-layer calibration, fixed hyperparameters, 5-seed runs with std, full hyperparameter/dataset tables in appendix.
- **Significance: Conditional but promising.** The mechanism is principled and the ablation is persuasive, but empirical scope is limited to one model size (Qwen3-1.7B) and three tasks with small training sets (1,000 examples). Generalization to 7B+ models, broader task suites, and multi-capability preservation is unverified.
- **Red flags / honest concerns:**
  - **Small effect margins in places.** On GSM8K→translation, the new-task EM spread across *all* methods is only 0.8 points; the headline is preservation, where the PPL delta vs baselines is ~0.05–0.07. These are real but modest in absolute terms.
  - **Construct validity of "$W_0$".** The "capability to preserve" is manufactured by LoRA-fine-tuning on 5,000 examples and merging. This is a reasonable experimental control, but it means the protected capability is relatively shallow/low-rank — which may *favor* a low-dimensional activation-subspace method. Whether the same $Q$ captures deeply entangled capabilities in a from-scratch pretrained model is open.
  - **Sweeping novelty claim.** "To our knowledge this is the first controlled comparison of weight-space ($P_V$) vs. function-space ($P_Q$) projection under full-rank fine-tuning" — the paper does position itself against CorDA/EVA/function-space regularization, but the absolute novelty claim should be checked against the broader continual-learning literature (activation-based importance, e.g., MAS uses activation magnitudes; the boundary is thin).
  - **Low-rank results are weaker than baselines on new-task EM.** Fora-LR (94.48) trails LoRA-r16 (95.44) and replay (95.84) on COGS EM; the authors are transparent that full-rank is the primary claim, but this tempers the PEFT generalization narrative.
  - **Two-author preprint**, date-stamped July 1, 2026; no peer review yet.

## Strengths

- Conceptually clean reframing: function-space vs. weight-space protection is intuitive, well-illustrated (Figures 2–3), and well-motivated by the "$V_1$ = where weight is large, not where capability is active" argument.
- Disciplined methodology: staged derivation, formal propositions, single-variable ablations, and explicit "this table is generality evidence, not a headline" framing.
- Mechanism is interpretable — both projectors and the spectral channel have clear geometric/operator meaning.
- Label-free, per-layer calibration lowers the practical barrier (no preserved-capability labels needed, unlike replay/distillation).
- Robustness sweeps over $(r, k_f)$ show the default is not a tuned point.

## Limitations

- **Single-capability protection only.** Preserving multiple capabilities simultaneously requires a union projector $P_Q = I - [Q_1\, Q_2][Q_1\, Q_2]^\top$, raising effective dimension and reducing adaptive room — untested.
- **Single model size (Qwen3-1.7B) and three tasks** with small (1,000-example) training sets; scaling and breadth unverified.
- **Calibration-data sensitivity uncharacterized.** $Q$ depends on calibration inputs; sensitivity to their number (500–1,000 used) and to domain shift is left open.
- **The protected capability is a shallow, LoRA-merged one**, which may structurally favor a low-dimensional subspace method.
- **New-task trade-off exists**, notably chrF 0.390 vs 0.396 in Math→Trans, and the low-rank variant trails plain LoRA on new-task EM.
- **Whether data-level (replay) and structural protection are complementary** is posed but not answered.

## Relevance

与持续学习、能力保留型微调（capability-preserving fine-tuning）、灾难性遗忘方向高度相关：FORA 提供了从"权重空间保护"转向"函数/激活空间保护"的新视角与可复现方法，对安全对齐后微调中的能力侵蚀、多任务增量学习等问题具有直接的方法论与实验参考价值，其"投影源决定保护效果"的结论尤其值得关注。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the activation-subspace advantage persist at 7B+ scale, or does weight-space projection become sufficient as width grows and capabilities get more entangled?
- How does Fora behave when the protected capability is a *deeply pretrained* one (not a shallow LoRA-merged specialization)? Does the low-dimensional-$Q$ assumption still hold?
- Can the union-projector multi-capability extension preserve several capabilities without starving the high-capacity branch? What is the practical limit on the number of simultaneous $Q_i$?
- How sensitive is $Q$ to calibration-input quantity and domain shift, and can $k_f$ be selected automatically via an eigenvalue-decay criterion?
- Can the spectral channel ($U_2 D_\delta V_2^\top$) be made rank-adaptive or learned jointly, and does that shift the preservation/plasticity frontier?
- Is function-space projection complementary to replay (data-level protection) — can combining them beat either alone at low rank?
- How does Fora interact with *safety-alignment* preservation (e.g., preventing jailbreak fine-tuning) versus task-capability preservation, given prior work showing refusal is mediated by low-dimensional activation directions?
