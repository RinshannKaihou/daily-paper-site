> **一句话总结：** 本文通过将神经网络每层表示空间限制为 $\mathbb{R}^3$（推广到任意 $d$），利用低维拓扑不变量——环链数（linking number）——严格刻画不同架构对"链环数据"的分离能力，证明：宽度为 $d$ 的前馈网络若使用逐坐标单调激活函数（ReLU/sigmoid/tanh）则无论多深都无法分离链环数据，而 ResNet 的跳跃连接（$|x|=x+2\mathrm{ReLU}(-x)$）、Transformer 的双 token 注意力机制（构造 V 形折叠）与非单调激活（GELU/Swish/Mish）都能通过"折叠"打破该拓扑障碍；CIFAR-10 实验进一步显示类间链环强度与分类难度相关。

> **TL;DR:** By fixing each layer's width to $d$ (starting at $\mathbb{R}^3$), this paper uses the *linking number* — an extrinsic topological invariant — to rigorously rank architectures: a width-$d$ feedforward net with any coordinate-wise monotonic activation (ReLU, sigmoid, tanh, …) *cannot* linearly separate linked manifolds no matter how deep it is, but ResNet skip connections ($|x|=x+2\mathrm{ReLU}(-x)$), transformer two-token attention (a V-shaped coordinate fold), and nonmonotonic activations (GELU/Swish/Mish) all "fold" the representation and break the barrier. CIFAR-10 experiments show class-pair linking strength correlates with classification difficulty. Published at ICML 2026.

## 核心贡献

- **提出"低维拓扑"分析视角**：将每层表示空间固定为 $\mathbb{R}^3$（进而推广到任意 $d$），从而隔离激活函数与深度的影响，避免高维空间中拓扑结构被平凡化（如在 $\mathbb{R}^4$ 中任何纽结都等价于平凡结）。
- **以环链数（linking number）作为可追踪的、环境敏感（extrinsic）的拓扑不变量**：与传统 TDA 关注 Betti 数等内在不变量不同，环链数刻画流形在环境空间中的嵌入方式，更贴合"网络必须在环境空间内变换数据"这一算法现实。
- **证明不可能性定理（Theorem 4.7）**：对任意 $d=m+n+1$ 中互补维度的链环流形 $M^m, N^n$，宽度为 $d$、使用任意逐坐标单调激活函数的前馈网络（含 CNN、瓶颈自编码器）无论多深都无法实现线性可分。
- **给出关键比较与"折叠"逃逸机制（Table 1, Theorems 5.1–5.3）**：ResNet 跳跃连接（用恒等式 $|x|=x+2\mathrm{ReLU}(-x)$ 合成非单调性）与 Transformer 双 token 注意力（构造近似 $|x|$ 的 V 形折叠）在改变环链数的能力上等价，均能把前馈网络提升到与非单调激活相同的高表达类；而流模型/Neural ODE 因生成微分同胚（保拓扑）反而最弱。
- **实证验证 + 真实数据相关性**：在 Hopf 链环、高维链球 $S^n\sqcup S^n\subset\mathbb{R}^{2n+1}$ 上验证表达天花板；并在 CIFAR-10 上用自研"环链检测算法"（空间图+圈基+Gauss 积分）检测到 bird-deer、deer-dog 等类对存在环链信号，且环链强度与分类难度、混淆程度正相关。
- **提供完整证明与开源代码**（ICML 2026, PMLR 306），并将结论从 $d=3$ 推广到任意 $d>3$。

## Key Contributions

- **A low-dimensional-topology lens for expressivity.** Fixing layer width at $d=3$ (then generalizing to arbitrary $d$) isolates activation/depth effects from width and avoids topological trivialization in high dimensions (every knot is unknotted in $\mathbb{R}^4$). It also makes the geometry visualizable.
- **Linking number as the operative invariant — and crucially an *extrinsic* (ambient-sensitive) one.** Unlike conventional TDA which tracks intrinsic Betti numbers of data manifolds, the linking number captures *how manifolds are embedded* in ambient space, matching the fact that networks transform data within that ambient space.
- **A general impossibility theorem (Thm 4.7).** For linked complementary-dimensional manifolds $M^m, N^n\subset\mathbb{R}^{m+n+1}$, *no* width-$d$ feedforward net with any coordinate-wise monotonic activation (ReLU, sigmoid, tanh, LeakyReLU, ELU, SELU) — of *any* depth — can linearly separate them. This covers CNNs and bottleneck autoencoders (Cor. A.5), and gives a topological corollary that universal approximation with monotonic activations needs width $\ge d+1$.
- **Folding mechanisms that escape the barrier (Table 1, Thms 5.1–5.3):**
  - Nonmonotonic activations (GELU, Swish, Mish) fold coordinates directly via a local extremum.
  - ResNet skip connections synthesize the fold with $|x|=x+2\mathrm{ReLU}(-x)$, making ReLU-ResNets *as expressive* as nonmonotonic feedforward nets (Thm 5.2).
  - A pure two-token transformer attention layer produces a V-shaped local surrogate for $|x|$ (Thm 5.3), placing attention in the same expressivity class.
  - Invertible/flow-based models and Neural ODEs, generating diffeomorphisms, *preserve* topology and are therefore the *least* expressive class.
- **Empirical validation** on thickened Hopf links, linked $S^2\sqcup S^2\subset\mathbb{R}^5$ (varying number $k$ of disjoint link copies), and CIFAR-10, using a custom linking-detection algorithm (spatial graphs + cycle bases + Gauss integrals) on PCA-3D projections.
- **Full proofs, a companion paper for richer invariants (Milnor $\bar\mu$, knot types), generalization to $d>3$, and open-source code.**

## Methodology

The authors fix the representation space of each layer to $\mathbb{R}^d$ (focused on $d=3$, generalized later). The conceptual entry point is the **Hopf link** — two interlinked circles in $\mathbb{R}^3$ with linking number $\pm1$. Linear separability provably requires the linking number to be zero (Proposition 3.6: a separating hyperplane confines each class to a convex half-space, allowing straight-line contractions to disjoint points, whose linking number is zero). So classifying linked data *requires unlinking it first*.

**Linking number** is defined via the Gauss integral
$\mathrm{link}(X,Y)=\frac{1}{4\pi}\iint \frac{(x-y)\cdot(dx\times dy)}{|x-y|^3}$
(or the crossing-sign formula), and generalized to higher-dimensional manifolds $M^m,N^n\subset\mathbb{R}^{m+n+1}$ via the degree of the Gauss map $G(x,y)=(x-y)/\|x-y\|$.

The core technical machinery is a layer-by-layer preservation argument:
- **Invertible affine layers** are homeomorphisms → preserve linking number up to sign.
- **Monotonic coordinate-wise activations** preserve linking number up to sign via a *straight-line link homotopy* $H_t(x)=(1-t)x+t\sigma(x)$ applied *simultaneously* to both components (Lemma 4.6: the sign factor $(-1)^r$ comes from $r$ non-increasing coordinates). The simultaneous motion is essential — moving one component alone could collide with the stationary one.
- **Rank-deficient layers** force intersections whenever $\mathrm{link}\neq0$ (Lemma 3.5, via SVD reduction to projection).

Putting these together yields the impossibility theorem: to drive a nonzero linking number to zero you need rank-deficient layers, but those force the class images to intersect, preventing linear separability — regardless of depth.

**Escape mechanisms** all hinge on *folding* — coordinate-wise nonmonotonic maps like $|x|$, which break the straight-line-homotopy disjointness argument:
- $|x|=x+2\mathrm{ReLU}(-x)$ realizes the fold inside a ResNet block using only ReLU + skip connection (Thm 5.2).
- A two-token attention layer with distinct positional encodings yields an output $g(x_i)$ with a strict local minimum at $x_i=0$ — a smoothed V-shape approximating $|x_i|$ locally (Thm 5.3); this local nonmonotonicity suffices for the topological transformation.
- Any activation with a strict local extremum (GELU near −0.5, Swish near −1.3, Mish) provides the same fold (Thm 5.1), with affine layers rescaling data into the nonmonotonic region.

**Design guidance:** because real wide models contain *narrow* maps (attention $d_{\text{model}}\to d_k$ projections, latent bottlenecks, bottleneck-residual reduced channels), the authors recommend (1) slightly expand MLP width beyond input dim before narrowing, (2) prefer nonmonotonic activations in bottleneck layers, (3) use skip connections, or (4) use attention.

## Results & Highlights

- **Table 1 (expressivity scorecard):** For unlinking a 2-component link, ambient-homeomorphism/flow models ✗, feedforward-monotonic (ReLU, sigmoid, tanh) ✗, width-$d$ autoencoder ✗, but feedforward-nonmonotonic (GELU/Swish) ✓, ResNet ✓, pure transformer ✓. (All six classes can deform shapes; monotonic classes can merge components; only flow-based *cannot* merge components.)
- **Hopf link, ReLU vs GELU (Table 2, 30 seeds × 12 depths):** ReLU plateaus ≤ ~92.8% (best) and *degrades with depth*; GELU reaches 100.0% best-run at depths 5–12 and ~90–91% mean at moderate depth — directly breaking the predicted ceiling.
- **Plain ReLU vs ResNet (Table 3):** ResNet hits 100% best-run at every depth 3–8 with mean 96.6–98.5% and low variance; plain ReLU never exceeds 92.3%. Confirms skip connections break the barrier even with monotonic activation.
- **Higher-dimensional linked spheres $S^2\sqcup S^2\subset\mathbb{R}^5$ (Table 4):** as the number $k$ of disjoint link copies grows, the best nonmonotonic architectures (GELU/Swish) hold a **3–4 pp advantage** over the best monotonic one at $k\ge10$, because each local entanglement must be resolved separately.
- **Mechanistic interpretability (Fig 6):** visualizes a width-2 ResNet implementing $|x|=x+2\mathrm{ReLU}(-x)$ to separate a point/circle link in $\mathbb{R}^2$ (winding-number link).
- **CIFAR-10 linking (§6.6):** detected linked cycles between bird–deer with $\mathrm{link}=-1$ (Gauss integral −1.004) at $\varepsilon=0.034$. Across 45 class pairs, 27 strongly linked (>70% consistency), 8 weak/unlinked (<30%). Linked pairs like deer–dog, bird–cat, automobile–truck score high; frog–ship, airplane–horse are low controls.
- **Activation gap localizes near links (Tables 5–6):** On the linked deer–dog pair, *all* nonmonotonic activations beat *all* monotonic ones (+1.2% gap); no such pattern on the unlinked frog–ship control (+0.1%). Adding skip connections erases the gap (matching Thm 5.2). On bird–deer, the ReLU↔GELU gap is **+6.5 pp within $10\varepsilon$ of a detected link witness**, decaying to +0.8 pp far from it. PCA-3D linking consistency also correlates with within-CIFAR class-pair confusion ($r\approx0.48$, $p<0.001$).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — High.** The key innovation is twofold: (a) using an *extrinsic* topological invariant (linking number, sensitive to ambient embedding) rather than the intrinsic Betti numbers of conventional TDA; (b) turning this into a *strict expressivity hierarchy* over modern architectures (ResNet ≈ transformer > monotonic FFN > flow/ODE models), with nonmonotonic activation as the single ingredient that elevates FFNs. This is a fresh and memorable result.
- **Rigor — High.** Claims are formal theorems with proof sketches in the main text and full proofs in appendices (e.g., Appendix C for the $\mathbb{R}^3$/ReLU setting, higher-dimensional generalizations). The layer-preservation argument (affines preserve link up to sign; monotonic activations via simultaneous link homotopy; rank-deficient layers force intersections) is clean and dimension-agnostic. The companion paper for richer invariants (Milnor $\bar\mu$, knot types) signals a coherent research program.
- **Reproducibility — Good.** Synthetic experiments are heavily parameterized (30–100 seeds per cell), protocols are specified (AdamW, lr $10^{-3}$, up to 800 epochs, early stopping), and **code is released**. The CIFAR-10 linking pipeline (augmentation to 1.05M samples, k-NN graph $k=15$ mutual edges) is described in appendices.
- **Significance — Moderately high as a conceptual lens, moderate as engineering.** The practical dividend is a set of *qualitative* design heuristics (favor nonmonotonic activations / skip connections / attention in bottlenecks) rather than a new SOTA architecture or benchmark.
- **Red flags / honest caveats (the authors are commendably explicit about most):**
  - **Expressivity ≠ optimization.** The theory says what a network *can represent*, not what gradient descent *finds*. The CIFAR-10 gaps conflate the topological barrier with optimization dynamics the theory does not model.
  - **$d=3$ (and width-$d$) is far from real wide networks.** The authors argue real models contain *narrow* sub-maps, but whether the hierarchy quantitatively predicts behavior at width $\gg3$ is unproven; the $d>3$ generalization is asymptotic, not empirical at scale.
  - **CIFAR-10 evidence is correlational, not causal,** and is measured on **PCA-3D projections**, not the native 3072-D manifold — the authors repeatedly flag this ("not a claim about linking of the full pixel manifold"). Distance-to-witness diagnostics "mix topology with local geometry."
  - **Effect sizes are small on real data** (+1.2% on deer–dog) and the linking-confusion correlation ($r\approx0.48$) is moderate.
  - **Single invariant (pairwise linking number);** richer invariants are deferred, so the picture is partial.
  - **The "transformer = ResNet" equivalence is proved only via a two-token, single-head coordinate-fold construction** — a deliberately minimal model, not a statement about full multi-head, multi-layer, trained transformers.

## Strengths

- Genuinely original, mathematically rigorous reframing of a question (relative expressivity of architectures) usually settled only empirically.
- Produces clean, actionable comparative conclusions (ResNet ≈ transformer > monotonic FFN > flow/ODE; nonmonotonic activation elevates FFN).
- Pins down *activation nonmonotonicity* as the precise lever — a concrete, designable insight ($|x|=x+2\mathrm{ReLU}(-x)$ is a memorable identity).
- Works in a visualizable space ($\mathbb{R}^3$) and rigorously generalizes beyond it.
- The extrinsic (ambient-embedding) framing distinguishes it sharply from prior TDA-of-networks work.
- Honest about limitations; code released; ICML-reviewed.

## Limitations

- Expressivity-only theory; no model of optimization, trainability, or generalization dynamics.
- The $d=3$/width-$d$ regime is distant from large practical networks; the $d>3$ generalization's empirical tightness is unverified at scale.
- CIFAR-10 results are correlational, PCA-3D-based, and of modest effect size.
- Only pairwise linking number is treated; richer invariants deferred to a companion paper.
- No new architecture or benchmark; payoff is interpretive/heuristic.
- The transformer equivalence rests on a minimal two-token construction.

## Relevance

对于关注神经网络表达能力、架构设计原理与可解释性的研究者，本文提供了一个用低维拓扑（环链数）严格比较 ResNet、Transformer 与流模型表达能力的理论框架，并给出"瓶颈层宜用非单调激活/跳跃连接/注意力"等可操作的设计启示，可作为架构选型与机理解释的理论参考。其结论目前偏理论，对实际大规模工程调参的直接、定量影响仍有限，但为理解"为何 skip-connection、attention、GELU 一致地优于替代方案"提供了统一的拓扑解释。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How tightly does the $d>3$ generalization predict the behavior of *wide* real-world networks — does the expressivity hierarchy quantitatively survive as width grows toward thousands?
- Can the linking-number framework be converted into a *quantitative* design metric or regularizer (e.g., a "linking-aware" loss or NAS objective) that measurably improves architecture search?
- How do the deferred invariants (Milnor $\bar\mu$, link groups, knot types, ambient cobordisms) refine or overturn the current expressivity ordering?
- Does activation nonmonotonicity's promotion of FFNs to the ResNet/transformer class translate into measurable empirical gains on harder, large-scale tasks beyond synthetic links and CIFAR-10 binaries?
- How does this topological expressivity axis correlate (or conflict) with optimization landscape, trainability, and generalization — and can the theory be extended to say something about what gradient descent actually finds?
- Does the two-token attention folding construction scale to realistic multi-head, multi-layer, trained transformers, or does richer attention introduce additional (or different) topological power?
