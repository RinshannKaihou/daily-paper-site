01 /

Methodology — AutoTrainHub, a closed-loop ACI

4 staged modules · LlamaFactory fixed backend

MODULE 1

Data Processing

Select → construct → validate. Bounded ops (extract, clean, dedup, synthesize, distill) with a benchmark-interface check before format lock.

Return-to-construction loop separates execution errors from a wrong data direction.

MODULE 2

Training

Fixed LlamaFactory backend with dedicated install/run scripts. Full-parameter SFT or explicitly-rewarded RL only.

Failures must be debugged in-workflow, not bypassed by switching frameworks.

MODULE 3

Evaluation

Runs the real benchmark pipeline, saves raw output, and emits a 15-example sampled summary parsed from logs.

Agent must classify failures as data / training / inference-template — feeding the next plan.

MODULE 4

Logging & Planning

Appends a structured experiment-log entry and derives the next objective from empirical evidence + a concrete success criterion.

Stabilizes stateful eval loops against drift.

◆ THESIS » Reliable autonomous post-training is best achieved by externalizing human experience into constrained ACIs — not by exposing agents to a raw CLI (where dataloader, chat-template, and packing errors dominate failures).

How the agent self-organizes

Behavioral taxonomy · 6 classes, 26 subclasses
PHASE 1 · EARLY
Benchmark alignment

Prompt alignment (T1, 8/46) and template change (T2, 7/31) dominate — the agent calibrates to the eval interface first.

PHASE 2 · MID
Data construction

Shift to benchmark-related data (D1, 8/26 improving), self-distillation (U4) and difficulty tuning (D3). DPO (U3) is weak (1/35).

PHASE 3 · LATE
Failure diagnosis

Concentrates on failure-case analysis + full evaluations. Continual-from-best preferred 322× vs retrain-from-base 133×.

Strengths vs Limitations

side by side

▲ Strengths

  • Correctly diagnoses why CLI-only fails (template / packing / dataloader errors) and targets it with purpose-built interfaces.
  • Fixing the training backend to LlamaFactory + in-workflow debugging is a pragmatic, reproducibility-friendly design.
  • Rich ablations tie each interface to a specific reliability boundary (data→train, eval→eval, planning→state).
  • Cross-model & cross-harness portability shown (GPT-5.4 & DeepSeek-V4-Flash; Codex & OpenCode).
  • Code & data released; PostTrainBench is a cited prior benchmark, not self-introduced.

▼ Limitations

  • Best scores (26–33) stay far below human Instruct baselines (51) — the human gap is not closed.
  • Heavy dependence on curated rules blurs the line between “agent autonomy” and scripted expertise.
  • No compute-cost / human-effort Pareto comparison.
  • Single GPU (H20) and single time budget (10h); compute sensitivity untested.
  • Closed-model dependence limits exact reproducibility; smaller open agents underperform.
  • DPO & annealing underperform — ACIs do not yet encode all useful training strategies.

Questions for further exploration

Q1

Do gains scale with more compute (multi-GPU, >10h) — does the gap to Instruct baselines close?

Q2

What is the cost/quality Pareto vs a human ML engineer on the same H20/10h budget?

Q3

Could ACIs be partially learned (discovered by the agent) rather than hand-authored?

Q4

Why do DPO and annealing underperform — can the interfaces encode them more reliably?

Q5

Do the behavioral phases transfer to non-post-training tasks (NAS, pretraining curation)?

Q6

How sensitive are results to the weighted scoring formula vs an unweighted or per-benchmark average?
