Capability-preserving fine-tuning · function-space reframing

Protect the activation subspace a capability lives in — not the weights' singular directions.

Fora: From Weight-Space to Function-Space Protection in Capability-Preserving Fine-Tuning

02 Why this matters
Reframes capability preservation from weight-space to function/activation-space protection — directly relevant to safety-alignment erosion and continual learning.
03 Critical edge
Scope is one model (Qwen3-1.7B) and three 1,000-example tasks. The protected "capability" is a shallow LoRA-merged specialization that may structurally favor a low-dimensional subspace method. Scaling & deep-capability generalization unverified.

Methodology

A staged derivation where each step fixes the previous one's blind spot — ending on the question of which subspace to protect.

ΔWFora = PU·M·PQ  +  U₂·Dδ·V₂⊤
PU=I−U₁U₁⊤ (weight SVD, left) · PQ=I−QQ⊤ (capability activation, right)
  1. 1 · Full FT  ΔW=M — no protection.
  2. 2 · Spectral calibration  U₂DδV₂⊤ — diagonal only, no mixing.
  3. 3 · Residual expansion — structure hidden in SVD coords.
  4. 4 · Weight-space proj  PUMPV — protects weight geometry, not capability.
  5. 5 · FORA  PUMPQ — protects the capability's function subspace.

How PQ is built

Forward label-free calibration inputs from Dpres; collect per-layer activations Xpres; take top-kf eigenvectors Q of the input–activation covariance Cpres; build PQ=I−QQ⊤. Construction is per-layer, label-free, capability-specific.

Two channels, complementary

For inputs aligned with the capability (in span(Q)): the dense branch is blind (Prop. 2), only the narrow spectral channel responds with bounded plasticity (Prop. 3) — not a hard freeze. For orthogonal inputs the dense branch runs at full capacity. Neither writes to the principal output directions (Prop. 1). Protection is a property of the parameterization, not the trajectory.

Decisive ablation

Swapping only PV → PQ (holding PU, M fixed) isolates what is projected from the act of projecting.

PQ wins on the preserved metric in every setting: PPL +0.47 (COGS), +0.07 (GSM8K); GSM8K retention +1.79pts (Math→Trans). Projection alone is insufficient — the source is decisive.

Strengths vs. Limitations

Strengths

  • Incisive reframing: V₁ = "where weight is large", not "where capability is active".
  • Disciplined: staged derivation, 3 formal propositions, single-variable ablations.
  • Interpretable geometry — both projectors + spectral channel have clear operator meaning.
  • Label-free, per-layer calibration — no preserved-capability labels needed.
  • Robust over (r, kf): default isn't a tuned operating point.

Limitations

  • Single-capability protection only; multi-capability union-projector untested.
  • One model size, three 1,000-example tasks — breadth & scaling unverified.
  • Calibration-data sensitivity (count, domain shift) left open.
  • Protected capability is shallow/LoRA-merged — may favor low-dim Q.
  • Low-rank variant trails plain LoRA on new-task EM.

Open questions

  1. Does the activation-subspace edge survive at 7B+, or does weight-space projection become sufficient as width grows?
  2. Does low-dim Q still hold when the capability is deeply pretrained rather than LoRA-merged?
  3. How many simultaneous Qi can the union-projector protect before starving the dense branch?
  4. Is function-space projection complementary to replay — can they combine to beat either alone at low rank?
  5. Does this transfer to safety-alignment preservation (refusal = low-dim activation direction)?
