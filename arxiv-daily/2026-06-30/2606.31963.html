Why this matters

Explains why permutation-only alignment systematically fails on RMSNorm models — and gives a drop-in sign-marginalized fix for reproducible, cross-run interpretability-tool transfer.

Critical edge

The headline 91.1% & 100% numbers come from a gauge-instrumented benchmark with a known applied gauge & dense checkpoint trajectory. Aligning two independently published weights without a shared atlas remains an open, harder, underreported problem.

01 Methodology

STEP 1 · THEOREM 2.1
Derive the native gauge

RMSNorm's diagonal gain γ forces Gᵀdiag(γ)G to stay diagonal → G must be signed-permutation. LayerNorm permits only S_d; T5 & RMSNorm admit full B_d. Validated to logit error ~1e-5.

STEP 2 · §3 RECOVERY
Sign-marginalized matching

Solve π* = argmax Σ |C_{i,π(i)}| via linear assignment (Jonker–Volgenant), then take sign from matched correlation. Removes the structural ~49.4% ceiling.

STEP 3 · §4 TRANSPORT
Parallel-transport composition

Along a same-base trajectory, compose per-checkpoint local gauges (1500 steps = 750 matches). Shared base breaks gauge ambiguity; cocycle holds on the trajectory tree.

STEP 4 · HARNESS
Gauge-instrumented eval

Apply a known random B_d gauge at step 0 as ground truth, then fine-tune Qwen2.5-1.5B; checkpoint every 2 steps. Probe budget: 22–500 tokens.

03 Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • Reframes an alignment failure as a precise group-theoretic gap with a provable ceiling.
  • Endpoint-via-base ablation (11.4%) is exemplary experimental hygiene — isolates the mechanism.
  • Validated across SAEs, steering, LoRA, merges, optimizer state — not a single task.
  • READ/WRITE transport table (Table 1) is a genuinely reusable practitioner artifact.
  • Honest scoping: behavior-level claims explicitly not invalidated.

Limitations

  • Headline recovery needs a known gauge + dense trajectory; atlas-free alignment of two published weights is unsolved.
  • Checkpoints every ~2 steps — usually unavailable for public models.
  • Sharp Thm 3.1 ceiling assumes decorrelated coords; correlated case only qualitative.
  • FFN transport unstable over long horizons (App. J).
  • AdamW test restricted trainable scope; frontier scale >8B untested.

04 Open Questions

  1. How does B_d transport degrade when intermediate checkpoints are sparse or a shared atlas is absent?
  2. Does the Thm 3.1 ceiling tighten or loosen measurably on correlated activations at frontier scale?
  3. Can the signed-permutation gauge extend to QK-Norm, DeepNorm, ScaleNorm, or tied-γ subspaces?
  4. For task merging (not just coordinate-preserving), does B_d alignment help beyond barrier reduction?
  5. Are published SAE / steering / knowledge-neuron findings still reproducible under a gauge-sweep audit?
