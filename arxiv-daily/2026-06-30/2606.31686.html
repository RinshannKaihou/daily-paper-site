01Methodology

Targets the second stage of filter-based selection: given a precomputed ranking, decide where to truncate it — with a clear statistical meaning. The threshold θ is calibrated to a target Bayes risk ε, not chosen heuristically.

S1Per-variable separation

For each variable v and class pair (cᵢ,cⱼ), measure marginal separation by the Bhattacharyya coefficient βᵢⱼᵛ. Small β ⇒ strong separation; wᵢⱼᵛ = −log β is additive log-separation.

S2Product-marginal model

The same device as naive Bayes: within-class joint dependence replaced by conditional independence. Product-model residual overlap Bᵢⱼ(T) = BC of the two product marginals.

S3Stop at the calibrated prefix

Keep the shortest prefix q⋆ whose residual overlap Bᵢⱼ ≤ θ for every class contrast. Output is a single class-independent subset. → see optimality

S4Risk calibration of θ

Prior-dependent: θ ≤ ε / Σ√(πᵢπⱼ). Prior-free (conservative): θ ≤ 2ε/(k−1) valid for any prior. Fully decoupled from the scorer (MI, χ², model importance).

Lemma 1 — the engine of the whole rule
Bᵢⱼ(T) = ∏_{v∈T} βᵢⱼᵛ  ⇔  −log Bᵢⱼ(T) = Σ_{v∈T} wᵢⱼᵛ
Under the product model the overlap factorises exactly (Tonelli) — turning per-feature overlap into accumulated log-separation. This is what makes a one-pass stopping rule possible.

02Results & Highlights

18 high-dimensional CuMiDa genomic datasets, stratified 10-fold CV, target risk ε=0.001, prior-dependent uniform calibration. Two scorers (MI, χ²) × two classifiers (Gaussian naive Bayes, logistic regression).

  • ~3 orders of magnitude reduction — from 42,109 vars on average to 42.9 (MI) / 56.0 (χ²).
  • Adaptive cardinality — some datasets need only a handful (Breast_GSE42568, Lung_GSE7670); heterogeneous/multiclass ones (Breast_GSE26304, Leukemia_GSE28497) need longer prefixes.
  • Performance preserved. For GNB, MI subsets beat all-features: accuracy 0.825 vs 0.809, macro-F1 0.795 vs 0.752. For LR, near-identical (0.837 vs 0.842).
  • Framed as success-by-absence: extreme reduction with no detected loss is the headline claim.

03Strengths & Limitations

Theoretical core is rigorously built (factorisation lemma, pairwise BC bound, multiclass all-pairs theorem, two cororaries, cardinality-optimality proposition — all with proofs). The empirics are comparatively narrow.

▲Strengths

  • Clean exact-factorisation result gives the rule both multiplicative and additive interpretability.
  • Bayes-risk bounds give θ a formal statistical meaning (prior-dependent and prior-free).
  • Modular — works with any precomputed ranking; decouples “how to rank” from “where to stop”.
  • Lightweight & scalable to tens of thousands of variables; linear in vars × class-pairs.
  • Produces one class-shared subset, compatible with standard multiclass pipelines.
  • Intellectual honesty — model-based scope & the dependence-induced calibration gap are explicitly delimited, not hidden.

▼Limitations

  • Calibration exact only under the product-marginal model; redundant vars deflate overlap too fast; weak-marginal discriminatory dependence can be missed.
  • Empirics limited to genomic data (CuMiDa); generalisation to text / vision / tabular unverified.
  • No comparison vs stability selection, mRMR, or cross-validated top-q — the most direct competitor for the stopping question.
  • KDE-based marginal estimates may be unstable in small samples / rare classes.
  • Single-author preprint; peer-review status unknown; no stated code/data release.
  • “No significant difference” = absence-of-evidence, not equivalence (no predefined margin, n=18).

04Open questions for further exploration

Where the work points next — the sharpest gaps between what is proven and what is assumed.

Q1 · GENERALISATIONHow does the rule perform on non-genomic high-dimensional domains — text, image-derived features, adversarial tabular settings?
Q2 · HEAD-TO-HEADDirect comparison with cross-validated top-q, stability selection, mRMR — the natural competitors for the size/stopping question?
Q3 · ROBUSTNESSSensitivity of the stopping point & downstream accuracy to KDE bandwidth and small-sample / rare-class overlap estimation?
Q4 · CALIBRATION GAPCan the product-vs-true-joint gap be quantified or bounded empirically — e.g. via a dependence-aware extension?
Q5 · TIGHTNESSAre the Bayes-risk bounds tight in practice? How much does prior-free calibration sacrifice vs prior-dependent?
Q6 · REDUNDANCYWould redundancy-aware pre-processing of the ranking (removing correlated features first) change the subsets & their stability?
