Alignment Safety · Emergent Misalignment

Evil Spectra

How Optimisers can Amplify or Suppress Emergent Misalignment

Jason R. Brown · Cambridge   Patrick Leask · Durham   Lev McKinney · Toronto

emergent-misalignment optimizers LoRA spectral-regularization ~330 runs

Key Finding

Optimiser choice dominates Emergent Misalignment — a 7× spread in misalignment rate — while model scale and family barely matter.

一句话总结：大规模扫描证明，优化器选择是涌现性失准的主导因素（造成7倍失准率差异），模型规模与家族影响可忽略；并据此提出低代价谱正则化方法大幅恢复对齐。

Why This Matters

The optimiser — a routinely ignored knob — silently reshapes fine-tuning safety. A drop-in spectral regulariser recovers alignment at near-zero training-loss cost.

Critical Edge

LoRA-only. Findings are untested under full fine-tuning, and optimiser effects conflate the algorithm with its default hyperparameters — no ablation isolates momentum / weight-decay / precision.

01 Results & Highlights

Optimiser-stratified loss–alignment fit reaches adj. R² = 0.94

  • Optimiser dominates EM. Muon 5.3% misaligned (mean alignment 81.0) vs Lion 37.0% (42.9) — a 7× spread. Dataset spread 13.5–22.1%; batch size 13.0 vs 22.0; model size 16.2–19.0%.
  • Scale & family negligible (under Adam). Every 1B+ model — Gemma 3, Llama 3, Qwen3 — clusters at 11–16% misalignment across 270M–235B.
  • Loss predicts alignment. Final log loss explains 74% of variance (R²=0.73); stratifying by optimiser lifts it to adj. R²=0.94. Coherence is instead explained by dataset — the optimiser effect is alignment-specific.
  • Damage happens late. Most alignment degradation occurs after training convergence; final-loss ordering ≠ final-alignment ordering.

02 Methodology

5 study groups · ~330 runs · rsLoRA rank-32

  • Initial sweep (128 runs): 4 Qwen3 models × 4 optimisers × 4 datasets × 2 batch sizes, 1 epoch, LR 1e-5.
  • Scale sweep (96 runs): 12 models (270M–235B), 3 families, Adam @ LR 1e-5.
  • Loss–alignment (72 runs): Qwen3-8B, 5 optimisers × varied LR.
  • Dynamics (16 runs): per-tuned LRs, 2 epochs, alignment checkpoints.
  • Regularisation (16 runs): with / without the spectral term.
  • Data: 4 EM-inducing sets — insecure code, bad medical, risky financial, extreme-sports advice (6k–7k each).
  • Train: rank-stabilised LoRA (α=64) on all attn + FFN projections; assistant-only loss masking.
  • Regulariser: L = L_SFT + λ·R(σ), λ=3.0; scale-invariant, zero at uniform spectrum.
  • Eval: 142 EM questions × 10–32 samples @ T=1.0; GPT-4o two-call judge; align < 30 ⇒ misaligned.

03 Key Contributions

First systematic isolation of the optimiser as an EM variable

English

  • Quantifies EM sensitivity. Optimiser dominates EM severity (7× spread), dwarfing scale, family, dataset, batch size.
  • Scale- & family-invariance (Adam). 96 runs confirm all 1B+ models cluster at 11–16%.
  • Optimiser-stratified fit. adj. R² = 0.94; LR / dataset / batch add negligible power.
  • Distinct optimiser trajectories. Most alignment damage occurs after convergence.
  • Mechanism + cheap fix. Flatter LoRA spectra preserve alignment; regulariser recovers Adam (+6.6), Lion (+8.0).

  • 系统量化EM敏感性：优化器选择为EM主导因素（7倍差异），远超规模、家族、数据集影响。
  • 跨规模/家族"无关性"：96点扫描证实所有1B+模型失准率聚集于11–16%。
  • 损失-对齐强预测关系：按优化器分层后调整R²升至0.94。
  • 差异化训练轨迹：每种优化器走不同路径，多数对齐损害发生在收敛之后。
  • 谱机制解释与缓解：引入Frobenius/核范数谱正则化，近零损失代价大幅恢复对齐。

05 Strengths & Limitations

＋ Strengths

  • First systematic, causal characterisation of EM sensitivity; isolates optimiser as the dominant factor.
  • Goes beyond observation: derives a mechanistic (singular-value spectrum) hypothesis and validates it with a cheap, targeted intervention.
  • Large, well-structured sweep (12 models, 270M–235B, three families).
  • Strong analytical practice: adjusted R², bootstrap CIs, seed-variance checks, two agreeing regulariser formulations.
  • The coherence/alignment dissociation argues the effect is alignment-specific, not generic degradation.
  • Mitigation is a single extra loss term, broadly applicable, negligible training-loss cost.

− Limitations

  • LoRA-only; full fine-tuning transfer untested.
  • Optimiser effects conflate algorithm with default hyperparameters (no ablation).
  • Single closed evaluator (GPT-4o) and one question set; cross-judge robustness unverified.
  • Scale-irrelevance scoped to Adam / LR 1e-5.
  • Lion possibly out-of-regime at bs=32 (designed for ~4096) — may exaggerate its EM-proneness.
  • Spectral analysis & regularisation restricted to Qwen3-8B; deeper "why flat ⇒ aligned" theory missing.
  • λ not formally sensitivity-tuned; Lion's incomplete recovery + SGD's adverse response show spectral flatness isn't the whole story.

06 Questions for Further Exploration

Open threads the paper leaves dangling

  • Does the optimiser effect — and spectral regularisation — transfer to full fine-tuning, and to EM-inducing data/tasks beyond the four studied?
  • What is the theoretical mechanism linking a flat LoRA singular-value spectrum to alignment preservation — perturbation-spreading, or alignment-subspace-specific?
  • Would scale-irrelevance hold for optimisers other than Adam and at different LRs?
  • What other update properties (beyond spectral uniformity) drive EM? Lion's incomplete recovery and SGD's adverse response hint at more.
  • Does spectral regularisation trade downstream task capability for alignment recovery (only training-loss cost is reported, not benchmarks)?
  • Could the loss–alignment relationship be exploited as a cheap training-time alignment monitor, bypassing full GPT-4o eval?
  • Is Lion's apparent EM-proneness partly an artefact of operating at bs=32 rather than its designed ~4096?
