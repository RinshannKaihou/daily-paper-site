> **一句话总结：** 本文提出一种基于 Bhattacharyya 系数的残差重叠停止规则，利用乘积边际模型下重叠的精确因式分解，将监督式特征排序在风险校准的截断点处转化为单一类别无关子集；在 18 个高维基因组数据集上将平均约 4.2 万个变量压缩到 43–56 个，且与全特征基线的预测性能在统计上无显著差异。

> **TL;DR:** The paper proposes a residual-overlap stopping rule that, exploiting the exact factorisation of the Bhattacharyya coefficient under a product-marginal model, converts a supervised feature ranking into a single class-independent subset at a risk-calibrated truncation point; on 18 high-dimensional genomic datasets it reduces ~42k variables to 43–56 on average while remaining statistically indistinguishable from the all-features baseline.

## 核心贡献

- **提出一种风险校准的停止规则**：给定任意监督式特征排序，该方法保留排序中最短的前缀，使其在**所有相关类别对比**下的残差乘积重叠低于预设阈值 θ，从而将排序截断为一个单一类别无关子集。这取代了任意的 top-q 选择、经验阈值或交叉验证。
- **利用 Bhattacharyya 系数在乘积边际模型下的精确因式分解**：在乘积边际模型下，子集的 Bhattacharyya 重叠精确等于各变量边际重叠的乘积，转化为可加的对数分离表示，使停止规则兼具乘积重叠的可解释性与边际证据的可加累积性。
- **推导了二分类与多分类的 Bayes 风险界**：为"带标签乘积边际问题"给出所有成对分类器的风险上界，并基于此提供阈值 θ 的**事先相关校准**（依赖类先验）与**事先无关校准**（对任意先验成立，更保守）。
- **算法简洁且模块化**：停止规则仅需一维边际重叠估计和对预计算排序的一次扫描，复杂度与变量数和类别对数成正比；与输入排序的打分机制完全解耦（可用互信息、χ²、模型重要性等）。
- **在高维基因组数据上的大规模实证**：在 18 个 CuMiDa 数据集（22,277–54,675 变量）上，目标风险 ε=0.001 时平均仅选 42.9（MI）或 56.0（χ²）个变量（约为原空间的 0.1%），且经 Friedman 与 Wilcoxon 检验，与全特征基线的预测性能无统计显著差异。

## Key Contributions

- **A risk-calibrated stopping rule** that, given any supervised feature ranking, retains the shortest prefix whose residual product overlap falls below a prescribed threshold θ for *every* relevant pairwise class contrast, converting the ranking into a single class-independent subset and replacing arbitrary top-q, empirical cutoffs, or cross-validation.
- **Exploitation of the exact factorisation of the Bhattacharyya coefficient under the product-marginal model:** the product Bhattacharyya overlap of a subset equals the product of per-variable marginal overlaps, giving an additive log-separation representation that makes the rule both multiplicatively interpretable (residual overlap) and additively interpretable (accumulated marginal evidence).
- **Derived binary and multiclass Bayes-risk bounds** for the labelled product-marginal problem, with both a **prior-dependent** calibration of θ (using class priors) and a **prior-free** calibration (valid for any prior vector, more conservative), plus optional pair-specific thresholds for allocating risk budget unevenly.
- **Simple, modular algorithm:** the stopping rule only needs one-dimensional marginal overlap estimates and a single scan of a precomputed ranking; complexity is linear in the number of ranked variables and class pairs; fully decoupled from the ranking's scoring mechanism (MI, χ², model importance, etc.).
- **Large-scale empirical validation on 18 CuMiDa genomic datasets** (22,277–54,675 variables): at target risk ε=0.001 the rule selects on average 42.9 (MI) or 56.0 (χ²) variables (~0.1% of the original space), and Friedman + Wilcoxon tests confirm no statistically significant difference from the all-features baseline.

## Methodology

The paper targets the *second stage* of filter-based feature selection: given a precomputed feature ranking R=(v(1),…,v(m)) produced by any relevance score, deciding where to truncate it with a clear statistical interpretation.

**Setup.** Let Y take k class labels with priors πᵢ, and X be m variables. For each variable v and class pair (cᵢ,cⱼ), the marginal class separation is measured by the **Bhattacharyya coefficient** βᵢⱼᵛ = BC(P_{i,v}, P_{j,v}) between the class-conditional marginal laws (Def. 3). Small β means strong separation; wᵢⱼᵛ = −log βᵢⱼᵛ is the additive marginal log-separation.

**Product-marginal model.** For a subset T, the product-marginal model for class cᵢ is P^⊗_{i,T} = ∏_{v∈T} P_{i,v} (Def. 5) — the same device as naive Bayes, replacing the within-class joint dependence by conditional independence. The product-model residual overlap is Bᵢⱼ(T) = BC(P^⊗_{i,T}, P^⊗_{j,T}).

**Key lemma (factorisation).** Under the product model, Bᵢⱼ(T) = ∏_{v∈T} βᵢⱼᵛ exactly (Lemma 1), proved via Tonelli's theorem. Equivalently, −log Bᵢⱼ(T) = Σ_{v∈T} wᵢⱼᵛ. This converts feature-wise overlaps into accumulated log-separation.

**Stopping rule.** For a uniform threshold θ∈(0,1], the calibrated prefix length is q⋆ = min{q : Bᵢⱼ(T_q(R)) ≤ θ for all i<j} (Eq. 13). Equivalently, selection stops once the accumulated log-separation reaches −log θ for every contrast (Eq. 15). The output is the single class-independent subset T⋆ = T_{q⋆}(R). Prop. 1 gives the feasibility condition; Prop. 2 proves binary cardinality optimality (the prefix is the minimum-cardinality subset satisfying the threshold when the ranking is ordered by descending separation).

**Algorithm 1** accumulates log-separations along the ranking for each class pair and returns at the first prefix satisfying all pairwise conditions; if none exists it returns the full set with "not calibrated" status.

**Bayes-risk calibration.** The threshold θ is calibrated to a target all-pairs product-model risk ε:
- **Theorem 1 (multiclass all-pairs calibration):** under an all-pairs-consistency assumption (satisfied by one-versus-one majority voting), any subset with Bᵢⱼ(T)≤θ ∀i<j satisfies R^⋆,⊗_AP(T) ≤ θ·Σ_{i<j}√(πᵢπⱼ) ≤ θ(k−1)/2.
- **Corollary 1 (prior-dependent):** θ ≤ ε / Σ_{i<j}√(πᵢπⱼ) guarantees R^⋆,⊗_AP(T) ≤ ε.
- **Corollary 2 (prior-free):** θ ≤ 2ε/(k−1) guarantees the bound for every prior vector.

**Plug-in estimation.** Marginal BCs are estimated via Gaussian KDE per class-conditional marginal (Silverman bandwidth, 50 grid points) computed within training folds only.

**Explicitly delimited scope.** The calibration is exact only for the *labelled product-marginal problem*, not for arbitrary dependent joint distributions. Redundant variables can make product overlap decrease too fast; discriminatory dependence with weak marginal signals can be missed. The authors are explicit that the bounds are model-based calibrations, not distribution-free guarantees.

## Results & Highlights

**Datasets & protocol:** 18 high-dimensional genomic datasets from CuMiDa (cancer research), 22,277–54,675 variables each. Stratified 10-fold CV; overlaps/ranking/stopping point estimated on training folds only. Target risk ε=0.001, prior-dependent uniform calibration. Two ranking criteria (MI, χ²) and two classifiers (Gaussian naive Bayes, logistic regression).

- **Massive dimensionality reduction:** all-features baseline averages 42,109 variables; the rule selects **42.9 (MI)** and **56.0 (χ²)** variables on average — roughly **0.10%–0.13%** of the original space, a ~3-order-of-magnitude reduction.
- **Adaptive cardinality:** reduction is data-dependent — some datasets (e.g., Breast_GSE42568, Lung_GSE7670) need only a handful of variables, while heterogeneous/multiclass ones (e.g., Breast_GSE26304, Leukemia_GSE28497) need longer prefixes.
- **Predictive performance preserved:** for GNB, MI-selected subsets achieve *higher* mean accuracy (0.825 vs 0.809) and macro-F1 (0.795 vs 0.752) than all-features; for LR, MI is near-identical (0.837 vs 0.842 accuracy).
- **Statistical significance:** Friedman tests across the three alternatives (All/MI/χ²) **fail to reject** the null at α=0.05 for both classifiers and both metrics (p-values 0.141–0.828). Pairwise Wilcoxon signed-rank with Holm correction confirms no significant differences. The authors frame the *absence* of a significant drop as the desired result: extreme reduction with no detectable loss.
- **Worked four-class example (Appendix A)** concretely illustrates overlap accumulation, the bottleneck contrasts determining the global stopping point, and the risk-calibration arithmetic.

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — Good.** Framing the truncation/stopping problem of feature rankings as a risk-calibrated, distributional decision based on the Bhattacharyya coefficient is genuinely fresh. The key insight — that the BC factorises *exactly* under the product-marginal model, enabling an additive log-separation stopping rule — is clean and principled. Most prior filter methods use heuristics for subset size; grounding it in Bayes-risk bounds adds real theoretical value. The positioning against Xuan et al. [12] (Bhattacharyya-distance feature selection via multivariate Gaussian reduction) is clearly articulated.
- **Rigor — Strong on theory, moderate on empirics.** The theoretical core is carefully developed: factorisation lemma (Tonelli), pairwise Bhattacharyya bound, multiclass all-pairs calibration theorem, two corollaries, and a cardinality-optimality proposition, all with proofs. The authors are commendably explicit about the *model-based* nature of the bounds and the calibration gap induced by dependence — an honesty rarely seen. However, the empirical evaluation is comparatively narrow: only genomic data, only two ranking criteria, only two classifiers, and only comparison against the all-features baseline (no comparison against stability selection, mRMR, cross-validated top-q, or other principled stopping rules).
- **Reproducibility — Moderate.** The algorithm is simple and fully specified (Algorithm 1), and all estimation hyperparameters (KDE, Silverman bandwidth, 50 grid points, ε=0.001) are stated. CuMiDa is public. However, **no code or data-release statement** is present, and the experiments would need re-implementation to reproduce exactly.
- **Significance — Potentially high but unproven beyond genomics.** A principled, interpretable stopping rule for feature rankings addresses a real and under-treated gap. If the empirical robustness holds across domains (text, vision, tabular) and against stronger baselines, adoption could be broad. As presented, significance is established mainly for high-dimensional genomic classification.
- **Red flags:**
  - **Single author** — limits breadth of perspective and independent validation.
  - **Statistical framing is somewhat circular:** the headline result ("no significant difference from all-features") is presented as success, but a non-significant Friedman test on only 18 datasets has limited power; it does not prove equivalence, only absence of detected difference. No equivalence/non-inferiority margin is predefined.
  - **The product-marginal (naive-Bayes) assumption is the central caveat.** The bounds control an *ideal* product-model risk, not the risk of the actual downstream classifier under dependent features. The authors state this clearly, but readers may over-interpret the "Bayes-risk bounds" as guarantees for real classifiers.
  - **No comparison with cross-validated top-q selection**, which is the most direct practical competitor for the "where to stop" question.

## Strengths

- Theoretically grounded, interpretable stopping rule with a clean exact-factorisation result.
- Bayes-risk bounds give the threshold θ a formal statistical meaning (prior-dependent and prior-free).
- Modular: works with *any* precomputed ranking; decouples "how to rank" from "where to stop".
- Computationally lightweight and scalable to tens of thousands of variables.
- Produces a single class-shared subset, compatible with standard multiclass pipelines, while pairwise diagnostics still identify bottleneck contrasts.
- Intellectual honesty: the model-based scope and dependence-induced calibration gap are explicitly delimited rather than hidden.

## Limitations

- Calibration is exact only under the product-marginal model; redundant variables can deflate overlap too quickly, and discriminatory dependence with weak marginals can be missed.
- Empirical evaluation limited to genomic datasets (CuMiDa); generalisation to other high-dimensional domains is unverified.
- No comparison against competing principled stopping rules (stability selection, mRMR, cross-validated top-q, redundancy-aware filters).
- Relies on KDE-based estimation of class-conditional marginals, which may be unstable in small samples, rare classes, or continuous settings.
- Single-author preprint; peer-review status unknown; no stated code/data release.
- The "no significant difference" result is absence-of-evidence, not evidence-of-equivalence (no predefined equivalence margin, modest sample of 18 datasets).

## Relevance

该方法为高维数据（尤其是基因组学/生物信息）的特征选择提供了具有理论依据、可解释且风险校准的截断规则，对关注特征选择、模型可解释性、朴素贝叶斯类生成式建模以及高维统计学习的研究方向具有直接参考价值，可作为"排序后如何停止"这一长期薄弱环节的原理性替代方案。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- How does the rule perform on non-genomic high-dimensional domains (text classification, image-derived features, tabular adversarial settings)?
- How does it compare directly against cross-validated top-q, stability selection, and mRMR — the most natural competitors for the stopping/size question?
- How sensitive are the stopping point and downstream accuracy to KDE bandwidth choice and to small-sample / rare-class overlap estimation?
- Can the model-based calibration gap (product vs. true joint) be quantified or bounded empirically, e.g. via a dependence-aware extension?
- Are the derived Bayes-risk bounds tight in practice, and how much conservatism does the prior-free calibration sacrifice relative to the prior-dependent one?
- Would redundancy-aware pre-processing of the ranking (removing correlated features before applying the rule) materially change the selected subsets and their stability?
- Is code/data publicly available to support reproduction and extension?
