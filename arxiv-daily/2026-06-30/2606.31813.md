> **一句话总结：** 本文从理论上分析 LoRA 在 RLVR 中的初始化问题，证明配以 B₀=0 的正交初始化能使 LoRA 与全参数微调的逼近误差最小化且约束更新幅度，据此提出保几何的正交初始化变体 LoRA-RLPO / LoRA-RLMO（仅用右奇异向量初始化 A 且不做奇异值缩放），在数学推理基准上稳定训练并稳定优于标准 LoRA，同时统一解释了 PiSSA / MiLoRA 在 RLVR 中因"子空间几何 + 奇异值缩放"双重放大导致 KL 越界崩溃的原因。

> **TL;DR:** The authors give a theory of LoRA initialization under RLVR, proving that orthonormal `A₀` (with the standard `B₀ = 0`) both minimizes the LoRA-vs-full-FT approximation gap and bounds per-step update magnitude. They instantiate this as LoRA-RLPO / LoRA-RLMO (initialize `A` from principal/minor right singular vectors, no singular-value scaling), which stabilize RLVR training and beat standard LoRA on math reasoning, while a unified analysis explains why PiSSA/MiLoRA collapse via coupled "subspace geometry + singular-value scaling" that violates the implicit KL budget.

## 核心贡献

- **理论分析 LoRA 在 RLVR 中的初始化最优性**：证明在 `B₀=0` 下，正交初始化 `A₀`（即 `A₀A₀ᵀ = I_r`）使 LoRA 与全参数微调的逼近误差最小化（Theorem 5.2），且保证首步更新幅度不超过全参数微调（Proposition 5.3）。
- **揭示 PiSSA / MiLoRA 在 RLVR 失效的双因素机制**：通过受控对比（引入 OLoRA / OLoRA-tail 作为去掉奇异值缩放的对照）证明不稳定来自两个耦合因素——子空间几何（沿特定奇异方向加速）和奇异值缩放（放大更新幅度）；其中 PiSSA 的梯度被至少 σ_r 倍放大（Theorem 4.1），结合 KL 约束隐含的权重上界（Theorem 4.2）说明其必然越界崩溃。
- **提出保几何的正交初始化方法**：LoRA-RLPO（`B₀=0, A₀=V_rᵀ`，主奇异方向）与 LoRA-RLMO（`B₀=0, A₀=V₋rᵀ`，次奇异方向）——保留预训练权重的谱几何信息但去除奇异值缩放。
- **多规模、多任务、多范式实验验证**：在 1.5B / 7B 数学推理（DAPO/GRPO）、代码生成（MBPP）、以及 SFT（GLUE/GSM8K）上稳定优于标准 LoRA，且无需额外调参。
- **正交性 vs 几何的消融分离**：引入模型无关的正交基线 DCT-LoRA / Wavelet-LoRA，证明"正交性本身"就能改善 RLVR，而"主子空间几何"在 RLPO 上带来额外增益。
- **开源**：代码与 checkpoint 公开。

## Key Contributions

- **Theoretical optimality of orthonormal init under RLVR**: Proves that with the standard `B₀ = 0`, orthonormal `A₀` minimizes the LoRA-to-full-FT approximation error (Theorem 5.2) and guarantees a first-step weight update no larger than full fine-tuning in Frobenius norm (Proposition 5.3).
- **Unified two-factor failure explanation for PiSSA/MiLoRA**: Through controlled comparisons against OLoRA / OLoRA-tail (same subspace, singular-value scaling removed), shows RLVR instability stems from coupled *subspace geometry* + *singular-value scaling*; PiSSA's gradient is amplified by ≥ σ_r (Theorem 4.1), and the KL-constraint-implied weight bound (Theorem 4.2) explains why this inevitably breaches the implicit trust region.
- **Geometry-preserving orthonormal initialization**: LoRA-RLPO (`B₀=0, A₀=V_rᵀ`, principal right singular vectors) and LoRA-RLMO (`B₀=0, A₀=V₋rᵀ`, minor right singular vectors) — preserve pretrained spectral geometry while dropping singular-value scaling.
- **Broad empirical validation**: Improvements hold across 1.5B/7B math reasoning (DAPO & GRPO), code generation (MBPP on Llama-3.2-3B), and even SFT (GLUE/GSM8K on Qwen2.5-7B) with no extra tuning.
- **Orthonormality-vs-geometry ablation**: Introduces model-agnostic orthonormal baselines (DCT-LoRA, Wavelet-LoRA) to show orthonormality alone helps, while principal-subspace geometry adds a further gain specifically for RLPO.
- **Open source**: Code and checkpoints released.

## Methodology

**Problem framing.** The paper studies how to *initialize* LoRA matrices `B ∈ ℝ^{m×r}`, `A ∈ ℝ^{r×n}` (rank `r ≪ min(m,n)`) for RLVR. Standard LoRA uses `B₀ = 0`, `A₀ ~ N(0, 1/n)`. SVD-based variants (PiSSA, MiLoRA) initialize both adapters from the SVD `W₀ = UΣVᵀ` of the frozen pretrained weight — PiSSA from top-`r` components with singular-value scaling `Σ_r^{1/2}`, MiLoRA from bottom-`r` with `Σ_{-r}^{1/2}` — and replace `W₀` with the residual so that `W = W₀` at init.

**Why RLVR differs from SFT.** RLVR (they use DAPO; also test GRPO) relies on a clipped importance ratio that implicitly bounds the KL `D_KL(π_{θ+} ‖ π_θ)` to a "safe" trust region. Exceeding it makes the surrogate objective no longer a valid lower bound → reward collapse. The authors adopt sampled KL as the key stability diagnostic.

**Diagnostic study (Sec. 4).** They first observe PiSSA/MiLoRA show reward collapse, higher gradient norms, and large cumulative KL vs. standard LoRA (Fig. 2). Visualizing post-training update energy on the singular spectrum (Fig. 3) shows initialization *governs the entire learned trajectory*, not just the start point. They then disentangle three instability factors:
1. *Learning rate* — cosine decay partially mitigates MiLoRA but not PiSSA (Fig. 4).
2. *Singular subspace* — controlled via OLoRA (top, no scaling) vs. OLoRA-tail (bottom, no scaling).
3. *Singular-value scaling* — Theorem 4.1 proves `‖ΔW₁^PiSSA‖_F / ‖ΔW₁^OLoRA‖_F ≥ σ_r`; combined with Theorem 4.2 (KL bound ⇒ weight bound `‖ΔW‖_F ≲ √(2K/μ)`, from Zhu et al. 2025) this shows scaling inflates updates past the KL budget.

**Core theory (Sec. 5.1).** Under Assumption 5.1 (L-smooth RLVR loss in logits, bounded gradient `‖G_t‖_F ≤ M`, enforced by clipping), Theorem 5.2 bounds the LoRA–full-FT gap:
`‖W_T^LoRA − W_T^full‖_F ≤ (Mη/T) Γ_T(η) · ‖I_n − A₀ᵀA₀‖_2 + O(η²)`, and `‖I_n − A₀ᵀA₀‖_2 ≥ 1` with equality iff `A₀` has orthonormal rows. Hence orthonormal `A₀` (with `B₀=0`) *minimizes the initialization-dependent gap*. Proposition 5.3 adds that the first-step update then satisfies `‖ΔW₁^LoRA‖_F ≤ η‖G₀‖_F`, i.e., never exceeds full-FT's first step.

**Proposed method (Sec. 5.2).** Two SVD-derived, geometry-preserving, scaling-free schemes (both `B₀=0`):
- **LoRA-RLPO**: `A₀ = V_rᵀ` (top-`r` right singular vectors) — principal-subspace, orthonormal.
- **LoRA-RLMO**: `A₀ = V₋rᵀ` (bottom-`r` right singular vectors) — minor-subspace, orthonormal.

No singular-value scaling, no nonzero `B₀`, no residual re-parameterization needed. Ablations add model-agnostic orthonormal baselines (DCT-LoRA, Wavelet-LoRA) to isolate orthonormality from geometry.

**Main (DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B, DAPO on DAPO-Math-17k, r=16, step 500, cosine LR decay; Table 1):**

| LoRA | 75.64 | 81.93 | 43.33 | 38.89 | 72.22 | 62.40 |
| MiLoRA | 75.41 | 76.47 | 28.89 | 30.00 | 47.78 | 51.71 |
| PiSSA | 9.65 | 13.20 | 0.00 | 0.00 | 0.00 | **4.57 (collapse)** |
| LoRA-RLMO (ours) | **76.42** | 86.80 | 42.22 | 41.11 | 72.22 | 63.76 |

- **Stability**: KL-divergence trajectories (Fig. 6) put PiSSA ≫ MiLoRA ≫ {LoRA, RLPO, RLMO}; the proposed methods are comparable to or below standard LoRA.
- **Orthonormality-vs-geometry ablation (Fig. 7/8)**: DCT-LoRA & Wavelet-LoRA beat standard LoRA (orthonormality alone helps); RLPO beats them all (principal geometry adds a large gain), RLMO is comparable to model-agnostic.

**Generalization (Appendix D):**
- **7B, GRPO, r=32 (Table 6)**: LoRA-RLPO best avg **35.96** vs LoRA 30.39, MiLoRA 26.72, PiSSA 24.74 — advantage *grows* at scale, no extra tuning.
- **Code gen, Llama-3.2-3B, MBPP-style test-case reward (Table 5)**: RLPO 45.67 / RLMO 46.11 vs LoRA 43.44 — different reward structure, still wins.
- **LR sensitivity (Fig. 10)**: RLPO/RLMO beat LoRA at every LR {1e-6,1e-5,1e-4}; peak at 1e-5.
- **SFT, Qwen2.5-7B (Table 8)**: CoLA 86.4 vs 85.5, MRPC 88.5 vs 86.5, GSM8K 29.7 vs 24.0 — methods transfer beyond RL, with faster convergence (Fig. 11).
- **SVD cost (Table 7)**: one-time, 7B ≈ 3.5 min / 16 GiB on a single A100 — negligible.
- **Failure re-examination (Appendix D.1)**: Contradicts Yin et al. (2025)'s "near-zero MiLoRA init" hypothesis — MiLoRA's `‖B₀A₀‖_F = 5.11` (not ≈0), and the tail spectrum stays nonzero (mean bottom-16 singular values 0.46–1.73).

## Critical Assessment

- **Novelty — moderate, with an important caveat.** The genuinely new pieces are (a) the *RLVR-specific* theory (Thm 5.2 / Prop 5.3) and (b) the unified two-factor failure story. **However, the methods themselves are close to prior art**: OLoRA (Büyükakyüz 2024) already initializes with top-r principal orthonormal components, and the paper itself cites "OLoRA-tail" (Lab et al. 2026) as an existing bottom-subspace orthonormal variant that *it uses as a controlled baseline*. So **LoRA-RLPO ≈ OLoRA** and **LoRA-RLMO ≈ OLoRA-tail** at the method level; the paper's real contribution is *explaining why these work under RLVR* and showing the principal-subspace variant is the best there. The paper is transparent about this relationship (Sec. 4.1, Fig. 5), which is good — but readers should not view the *algorithms* as wholly novel.
- **Rigor — solid but theory is local/first-order.** Thm 5.2 bounds the *early-time* gap under an L-smoothness + bounded-gradient assumption; it shows orthonormality minimizes *one term in an upper bound*, not that the final LoRA solution is globally optimal. The `‖I − A₀ᵀA₀‖ ≥ 1` equality-iff-orthonormal step is clean and correct. Standard LoRA's `A₀ ~ N(0,1/n)` already has *approximately* orthonormal rows in expectation, so the theoretical edge over standard LoRA is about exact vs. approximate orthonormality — consistent with the *modest* empirical margins.
- **Reproducibility — strong.** Released code + checkpoints, full hyperparameters in Appendix C, multiple seeds with reported std, one-time SVD cost table, LR sweep. Low barrier to reproduce.
- **Significance — practically meaningful.** Addresses a real, timely pain point (LoRA instability under RLVR/GRPO/DAPO) that practitioners hit. The "drop singular-value scaling, keep B₀=0" prescription is actionable and cheap.
- **Red flags / concerns:**
  1. Main headline scale is small (1.5B, single dataset, step 500); absolute gains over standard LoRA are modest (1.5B avg +2.6 pts; some per-benchmark error bars overlap LoRA). The 7B numbers are more convincing but use only 150 steps.
  2. PiSSA's near-total collapse (avg 4.57) is the dominant visual contrast — the proposed methods' *absolute* lead over a *stable* standard LoRA is smaller than the abstract framing implies.
  3. Some "future-dated" citations (DAPO = Yu et al. 2026, OLoRA-tail = Lab et al. 2026, Biderman et al. 2026) — verify these are real/published when relying on them.
  4. The "geometry-preserving" label is somewhat generous to RLMO, which performs *no better* than model-agnostic orthonormal baselines (Fig. 8 right) — geometry only clearly helps for RLPO.

## Strengths

- Clean, theory-guided methodology rather than empirical search; theorems directly motivate the design.
- Genuinely explains (not just observes) *why* SFT-winning initializers fail under RLVR, via a falsifiable two-factor mechanism with controlled ablations (OLoRA/OLoRA-tail isolate scaling; DCT/Wavelet isolate geometry).
- Simple, drop-in initialization change with no extra training cost (SVD is one-time, ~minutes).
- Validates across model sizes (1.5B/3B/7B), model families (Qwen/Llama/DeepSeek-distill), reward types (verifiable math + code test-cases), and paradigms (RLVR + SFT).
- Honest about relationship to OLoRA and to prior failure hypotheses (Appendix D.1 directly contests Yin et al. 2025's near-zero-init claim with measured norms).

## Limitations

- Methods overlap substantially with existing OLoRA / OLoRA-tail; novelty is concentrated in the RLVR theory and analysis, not the algorithms.
- The approximation-gap theorem is local/first-order and assumes L-smoothness + clipped (bounded) gradients; whether it holds far into non-convex RLVR training is unproven.
- Headline gains over *stable* standard LoRA are modest at 1.5B (avg 62.4 → 65.0); the dramatic table contrast is driven largely by PiSSA's collapse, not by the proposed methods' lead.
- Evaluation concentrated on math reasoning; code-gen is a single small table (Llama-3.2-3B, MBPP). No results on broader RLVR domains (e.g., tool use, agentic, multi-turn).
- Only ranks r=16 and r=32 tested; very-low-rank (r=4) and very-high-rank behavior unreported.
- "Geometry-preserving" benefit is asymmetric: clear for RLPO, negligible for RLMO vs. model-agnostic orthonormal bases.

## Relevance

对于关注参数高效微调（PEFT / LoRA）与强化学习后训练（RLVR / GRPO / DAPO）的研究方向高度相关：该工作为 LoRA 在 RLVR 场景下的初始化给出了可解释的理论判据与一行代码即可落地的改进方案，对大模型推理能力强化训练（数学推理、代码生成）中常见的 LoRA 训练崩溃问题有直接的诊断与修复价值。

## Questions for Further Exploration

- Does the orthonormal-init advantage (esp. RLPO) hold for very low ranks (r=4) and very high ranks, and across more RLVR algorithms (PPO, RLOO, ReMax) beyond DAPO/GRPO?
- Is Thm 5.2's "minimal gap" robust deep into training, or does it only govern early dynamics? Can it be extended to a finite-horizon or convergence guarantee?
- Since standard LoRA's Gaussian `A₀` is already *approximately* orthonormal, can the modest residual gap be closed by a cheaper "Gaussian + one Gram–Schmidt pass" instead of a full SVD?
- Does the two-factor failure story predict behavior of other structural LoRA variants (DoRA, AdaLoRA, rsLoRA) under RLVR?
- Can the analysis extend to multi-turn/agentic RLVR and tool-use rewards where the KL leash behaves differently?
- Given RLMO ≈ model-agnostic orthonormal baselines, is the minor-subspace geometry ever worth the SVD cost, or should practitioners just use a cheap random orthonormal A₀ when targeting the tail?
