01 Methodology

When does an open surrogate (MS) license claims about a closed target (MT)?

Bifurcation in MI. Classic tools (circuits, SAEs, activation patching) need full internals; frontier models expose only top-K log-probs. The paper asks when surrogate measurements transfer — governed by a principle of least privilege: the access needed to measure a signal must match the access the claim demands.

Geometric core. For binary classification, log-odds ℓ(x)=z·v are exactly linear in the residual stream z (v = u₊−u₋). A log-prob difference cancels the softmax normalizer, so ℓ is both API-accessible and geometrically interpretable.

Δℓ = ‖Δz‖ · ‖v‖ · cos(Δz, v)

Attribution decomposes into perturbation magnitude ‖Δz‖ and alignment with the readout direction. Framework is intervention-agnostic; experiments use black-box leave-one-out ablations.

Fidelity hierarchy (all Pearson r², bootstrapped 95% CI):

  • Fpred = r²(ℓ_S, ℓ_T) — output agreement (log-probs only)
  • Fattr = r²(Δℓ_S, Δℓ_T) — causal-importance agreement (log-probs only)
  • Frepr = Fmag · Falign · Fattn — needs open weights
  • Fcross = r²(α_S, Δℓ_T) — do surrogate internals predict target attributions?

Scale: 11 models · 4 families (Llama 3.1/3.3/4, Qwen 2.5, GPT-4o/4.1, Gemini 2.5 Flash Lite) · 5 benchmarks (BoolQ, ANLI R1–R3, WinoGrande, LAMBADA, RACE).

The access-validity inversion
Signal stability · mean attention 0.905
…its causal predictive power 0.003
Stable white-box signals are nearly uncorrelated with causal attribution. The API-compatible black-box ablation is paradoxically the most causally relevant signal.
  • Word-level ablation collapses Fattr (0.460 → 0.175), driven by Falign collapse (0.200 → 0.012).
  • Direction is the root cause. Models converge on magnitude (Fmag high) but diverge on direction (Falign median 0.200).
  • ~15–23% of attribution comes from LayerNorm rescaling — directionally uncorrelated with attribution.
  • Cross-benchmark robust: ordering (Fpred > Fattr; Fmag > Falign) holds across all 5 tasks. RACE Fattr (0.524) even exceeds Fpred (0.462).
  • No surrogate representation signal clears the Fcross bar — open→closed mechanism transfer is not demonstrated.

03a Strengths

Sharp concepts · broad coverage · honest

  • Well-named, communicable contributions: "surrogate fidelity," "access-validity inversion," "principle of least privilege."
  • Log-odds-as-linear-projection elegantly unifies API-accessibility with geometric interpretability.
  • Genuine breadth — 11 models / 4 families / 5 benchmarks, including truly closed targets (GPT-4o/4.1, Gemini) via logprob APIs, not just open models.
  • Intellectual honesty: random-direction controls, LayerNorm confound analysis, explicit scope caveats.
  • Practitioner-oriented: tells you which metric to report at which access level. Open code + results.

03b Limitations

Narrow scope · method-bound · diagnostic only

  • Narrow task scope. Binary classification is clean but unrepresentative of generative use; open-ended generation is flagged as a hard, unsolved extension.
  • Attribution-method dependence. All causal claims hinge on leave-one-out ablation; activation patching, steering vectors, SAE features untested.
  • Closed-model API dependence. Needs logprob access — excludes Claude & GPT-5; APIs may drift.
  • Predominantly diagnostic. Documents failure of transfer thoroughly; offers little on when transfer succeeds or how to build valid surrogate explanations.
  • Attention-aggregation sensitivity. Rollout attention is nearly uncorrelated with mean/max — so "attention is stable" depends on the aggregation choice.

04 Open Questions for Further Exploration

Where surrogate fidelity might recover — or break down

Q1Under what conditions (task, family similarity, attribution method) does surrogate fidelity recover? Are there predictors of transfer success beyond measuring Fattr directly?
Q2Do findings generalize beyond binary classification to open-ended generation, multi-step reasoning, and safety-relevant behaviors lacking a scalar log-odds summary?
Q3How sensitive are conclusions to the attribution method? Would activation patching, steering vectors, or SAE features change the access-validity inversion — or reinforce it?
Q4Could richer API-compatible interventions (meaning-preserving perturbations, ContextCite-style attribution) yield partially causally valid mechanistic accounts?
Q5Does alignment/distillation of an open model toward a closed one improve attribution fidelity, or only prediction fidelity (mimicry without mechanistic alignment)?
Q6Is the inversion specific to instruction fine-tuning? Log-prob divergence appears only post-SFT — does it vanish for base models?
Q7Can the geometric decomposition (magnitude · alignment · LayerNorm) predict ahead of time which model pairs will show low Fattr — without running expensive ablations?
