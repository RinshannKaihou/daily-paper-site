01

Methodology

LOTUS fixes two failure modes of prior latent reasoning: P1 sequential latent decoding (Coconut/CODI keep the per-token bottleneck) and P2 lack of CoT grounding (PCCoT/KaVa supervise indirectly). Two ingredients resolve both.

  1. Padded latent prefix. [Q, ⟨BoT⟩, ⟨lat⟩×c … K blocks … ⟨EoT⟩, A] — K blocks of c learnable tokens; block i encodes step Ti. K is fixed across examples (K=6 covers 99% of GSM8K).
  2. Looped computation. The base causal LM builds a reusable KV cache C_pre, then iterates R=6 times refining latent embeddings — gradients flow through all iterations (finite unroll, weight-shared).
  3. Direct step-aligned supervision. Lstep: each latent position aligned to its gold CoT-step token through the base LM head, in parallel. Lans: next-token loss on the answer. Total L = Lans + λ·Lstep.
Parallel chain likelihood · key insight Because the answer is decoded from jointly-computed latents, Lstep only needs to cover the right token's support — it never has to reproduce the joint distribution. Lans supplies the joint-selection pressure. That dissolves the worry that independent per-position targets can't yield globally coherent reasoning.

Interpretable latents: post-loop latents read through the base LM head recover the gold chain at 70.9% top-1, and place graded mass on unseen-but-valid alternative chains (15.3% top-1 / 64.0% top-5) — evidence against memorization.

Strengths

  • Targets the known scaling failure. Directly attacks the documented gap that widens beyond 1B for latent CoT.
  • Surprisingly simple recipe — looped backbone + parallel gold-token cross-entropy, not a new architecture.
  • Efficiency without accuracy tax: 2.5–6.9× latency at the scale that matters.
  • Genuinely interpretable latents, argued with metrics rather than cherry-picked examples.
  • Clean ablation story — every component (looped backbone, parallel supervision, c, R) earns its place.

Limitations

  • Tested only to 3B. The "gap widens with scale" critique applies symmetrically until verified larger.
  • Math-only. Code, logic, and multi-step QA generalization is unproven.
  • Fixed K/c/R. No adaptivity; long chains degrade to autoregressive completion.
  • OOD wins are mixed — driven largely by MultiArith; the "surpasses CoT" headline needs scrutiny.
  • No throughput-with-batching data; latency is single-example greedy, may not reflect real serving.
03

Open questions

  • Does the gap-closing property hold at 7B/13B/70B, or does looped-backbone + parallel supervision also saturate with scale?
  • How does LOTUS behave on non-math reasoning (code, multi-hop QA) when intermediate steps are less token-alignable?
  • What is the training cost/stability of gradients through all R iterations vs the inference savings?
  • Can K, c, R be made adaptive per-example to handle variable-length reasoning without autoregressive fallback?
  • Do the latency gains survive batched/throughput serving given the 150-position parallel footprint?
  • Does the parallel-supervision recipe transfer as a drop-in to non-looped latent architectures?
