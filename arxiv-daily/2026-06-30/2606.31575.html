Why This Matters

A theoretically-grounded token metric for RLVR: understand token-level gradient dynamics, sharpen GRPO-class methods, and shorten inference — directly useful to anyone training reasoning models.

Critical Edge

Theorem 1 holds only for single-logit perturbations — the multi-coordinate case is unproven. And with a=−6 filtering almost nothing, the advertised “asymmetric balance” mostly reduces to just choosing b.

01

The idea: couple entropy with the token you actually drew

RLVR is torn between two findings that both work yet flatly contradict each other. Probability alone, or entropy alone, can’t say which tokens deserve the gradient.

Wang et al. — “80/20”

Train on the top-20% highest-entropy tokens.

vs.

Yang et al. — suppress

Low-probability tokens destabilize gradients; don’t let them dominate.

RSI fuses both signals into one normalized quantity: how surprising is the token you sampled relative to how uncertain the distribution was? The filter then keeps an entropy-adaptive band — not a static cut — which is why it subsumes both recipes above.

EB & PB stall on small models

Both prior rules barely help (or hurt) at 1.5B/3B; RSI-S is the only uniformly positive method — evidence it actually synthesizes the two signals.

Static thresholds fail

Ablation: fixed probability bands [0.1,0.9]…[0.3,0.7] all degrade accuracy (−2.4 to −4.9). The win comes from entropy-adaptive gating, not truncation.

Gains skew easy

Largest lifts are on AMC (+5.95 @ 3B); AIME gains are modest and the 1.5B AIME score sits near the ~2.4% floor.

03

Strengths & Limitations

Strengths

  • A real theorem + proof grounds the metric — not another ad-hoc weighting heuristic.
  • Asymmetric interval elegantly fuses two contradictory prior paradigms; ablation shows both bounds matter.
  • Convincing proof static truncation fails — isolating the value of entropy-adaptive gating.
  • Strong reproducibility: open models / data (DAPO-MATH-17K) / frameworks (EasyR1, verl), full hyperparameters, fair ported baselines.
  • Bonus efficiency: shorter generations at higher accuracy.

Limitations

  • Theorem 1 restricted to single-coordinate (selected-logit) perturbation; multi-coordinate case unproven.
  • No multi-seed / significance testing; some 1.5B AIME gains (+0.11) sit in the noise floor.
  • Narrow eval: only math (AIME/AMC), only GRPO base, only ≤7B — generalization untested.
  • RSI intervals are scale-specific ([−6,0.95]/[−6,0.9]/[−6,0.8]) — some per-scale tuning involved.
  • a=−6 filters almost nothing negative-side, so “asymmetry” mostly reduces to choosing b.
04

Open questions worth chasing

Q1 · Theory

Does 1/RSIt survive beyond single-logit perturbation — under joint multi-coordinate changes?

Q2 · Robustness

How do gains hold under multi-seed runs with CIs, especially near-floor small-model AIME?

Q3 · Transfer

Does RSI-S move to code / tool-use / QA, other RLVR variants (DAPO, GSPO), and >7B?

Q4 · Mechanism

If a=−6 filters nothing, is RSI-S effectively just an entropy-adaptive upper-bound cap?

Q5 · Diagnostics

Can RSI serve as an analytic probe of token-level learning dynamics in PPO / RLOO?

Q6 · Composition

Combine RSI-S with sequence-level methods (GSPO) or soft reweighting vs hard masking?
